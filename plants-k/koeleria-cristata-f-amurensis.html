Koeleria cristata f. amurensis

Koeleria cristata f. amurensis, commonly known as the Amur hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, compact inflorescence that resembles a crown, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and along riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its unique appearance and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
