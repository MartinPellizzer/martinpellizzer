Knema linifolia var. clarkeana

Knema linifolia var. clarkeana, commonly known as the Clarke's white ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces woody capsules containing aromatic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine. Its hard, heavy wood is valued for its durability and resistance to decay.

This species thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
