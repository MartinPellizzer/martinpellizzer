Knema attenuata var. latifrons

Knema attenuata var. latifrons, commonly known as the broad-leaved knema, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. It typically grows in lowland rainforests and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

The tree can reach heights of up to 15 meters and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.
