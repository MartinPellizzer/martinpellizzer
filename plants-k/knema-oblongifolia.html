Knema oblongifolia

Knema oblongifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf knema, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and elliptical, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by fleshy, red or purple berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
