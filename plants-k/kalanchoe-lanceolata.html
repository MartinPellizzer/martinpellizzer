Kalanchoe lanceolata

Kalanchoe lanceolata, commonly known as the mother of thousands, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It features fleshy, lance-shaped leaves that are often marked with distinctive white or silver veins. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow during the winter months. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves.

Kalanchoe lanceolata is popular as a houseplant due to its low maintenance and long-lasting blooms.
