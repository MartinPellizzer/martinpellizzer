Kalaharia spinescens var. hirsuta

Kalaharia spinescens var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy spiny kalaharia, is a perennial shrub native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and leaves, which are covered in fine, hairy textures. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its flowers are small and often pale green or yellow, appearing in clusters during the warmer months.

This variety is notable for its hardy nature and ability to withstand drought and poor soil conditions.
