Krausella polyneura

Krausella polyneura, commonly known as the narrow-leafed krausella, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy environments, characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often found in coastal dunes and rocky areas. Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Krausella polyneura is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
