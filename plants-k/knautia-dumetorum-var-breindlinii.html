Knautia dumetorum var. breindlinii

Knautia dumetorum var. breindlinii, commonly known as the Breindlin's knautia, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and ecological benefits.
