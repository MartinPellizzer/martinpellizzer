Koeleria pyramidata

Koeleria pyramidata, commonly known as the mountain grass or pyramid grass, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, cylindrical flower spikes. The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically pale green to brown in color. Koeleria pyramidata thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
