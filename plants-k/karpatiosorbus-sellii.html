Karpatiosorbus sellii

Karpatiosorbus sellii, commonly known as the Turkish rowan, is a deciduous shrub native to Turkey and parts of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces red berries that are edible for birds but generally not for humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a hardy, visually appealing shrub.
