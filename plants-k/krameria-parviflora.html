Krameria parviflora

Krameria parviflora, commonly known as the smallflower burrplant, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that enclose numerous seeds, which are dispersed by animals. Krameria parviflora thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to drought conditions.

Its unique structure and ecological role make it an important species in desert ecosystems.
