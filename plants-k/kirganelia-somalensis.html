Kirganelia somalensis

Kirganelia somalensis, commonly known as the Somali tamarind, is a small, deciduous shrub native to the Horn of Africa.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, round pods that contain edible seeds, which are used in traditional cuisine. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to dry conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in local ecosystems and agriculture.
