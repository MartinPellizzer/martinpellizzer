Kerneria helianthoides

Kerneria helianthoides, commonly known as the yellow dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in full sun. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in spring, and the plant produces a capsule fruit that contains numerous small seeds.

Kerneria helianthoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
