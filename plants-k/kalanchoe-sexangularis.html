Kalanchoe sexangularis

Kalanchoe sexangularis, commonly known as the stonecrop kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, often blooming in winter. Its stems are upright and branching, forming a compact, shrub-like structure.

Kalanchoe sexangularis is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental appeal.
