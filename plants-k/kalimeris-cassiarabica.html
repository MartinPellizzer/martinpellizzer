Kalimeris cassiarabica

Kalimeris cassiarabica, commonly known as the Cassiar aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southeastern Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers bloom in late summer, producing yellow disc florets surrounded by white to pale pink ray florets.

Kalimeris cassiarabica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
