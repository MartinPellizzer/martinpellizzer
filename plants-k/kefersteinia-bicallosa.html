Kefersteinia bicallosa

Kefersteinia bicallosa, commonly known as the spiny candelabra cactus, is a unique member of the Cactaceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its branching, spiny stems that resemble a candelabra, with a distinctive appearance that helps it stand out in its natural habitat. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a striking contrast to its rugged structure. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its thick, fleshy stems that store water.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by cactus enthusiasts.
