Karangolum faberi

Karangolum faberi, commonly known as the Indian sandalwood.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Indian subcontinent. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, dark brown heartwood, which is highly valued in perfumery and traditional medicine. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptic in shape, with a leathery texture.

The flowers are small and white, often clustered in inflorescences, and it produces small, round fruits containing one or two seeds.
