Kingstonia guttata

Kingstonia guttata, commonly known as the guttate kingstonia, is a flowering plant native to the island of Mauritius.

It belongs to the family Proteaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, palm-like appearance with compound leaves. The plant features showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. Its leaves are often dotted with small, glandular dots, giving it the common name "guttate.".

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
