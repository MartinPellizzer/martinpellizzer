Kadenia salina

Kadenia salina, commonly known as the salt bush, is a halophyte plant adapted to saline environments.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant exhibits a compact growth habit, often forming dense clusters to reduce water loss. Its small, usually green or red flowers are clustered in inflorescences.

Kadenia salina is native to arid and coastal regions where it thrives in salty soils.
