Koeleria phleoides var. luxurians

Koeleria phleoides var. luxurians, commonly known as the purplegrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowering spikes bear small, purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in arid conditions.
