Knema angustifolia

Knema angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf knema, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by distinctive, fleshy, orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
