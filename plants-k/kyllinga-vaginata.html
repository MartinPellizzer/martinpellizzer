Kyllinga vaginata

Kyllinga vaginata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features erect, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. Its root system is fibrous, and it is often found in moist, disturbed soils.

Kyllinga vaginata is known for its rapid growth and is considered an invasive species in many areas.
