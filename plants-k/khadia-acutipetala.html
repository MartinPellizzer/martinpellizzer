Khadia acutipetala

Khadia acutipetala, commonly known as the sharp-leaved khadia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to dry conditions. Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine, and the plant can also serve as forage for livestock.

The species is of interest for its ecological role and potential agricultural applications.
