Kalanchoe wightianum

Kalanchoe wightianum, commonly known as the silver leaf kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are covered in a silvery-white powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant orange to red flowers during the winter months, adding visual interest to gardens. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for xeriscaping.

Kalanchoe wightianum is also known for its ability to propagate through stem cuttings, which is a common method for propagating succulents.
