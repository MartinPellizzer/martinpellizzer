Kunzia tridentata

Kunzia tridentata, commonly known as the three-toothed knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Kunzia tridentata is typically found in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in full sun.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Polygonaceae family.
