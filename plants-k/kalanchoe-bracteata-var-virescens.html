Kalanchoe bracteata var. virescens

Kalanchoe bracteata var. virescens, commonly known as the green rosette kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, fleshy, dark green leaves that form a compact rosette shape. The plant produces vibrant orange to red flowers during the winter months, which are typically followed by the production of small, leaf-like structures called "pups.". It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for arid environments.

Its striking appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor gardening.
