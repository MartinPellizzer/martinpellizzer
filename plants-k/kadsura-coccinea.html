Kadsura coccinea

Kadsura coccinea, commonly known as the red kadsura, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its striking red-orange berries that contrast with its dark green foliage. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its unique appearance.
