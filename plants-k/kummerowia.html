Kummerowia

Kummerowia, commonly known as the Chinese milk vetch, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It consists of several species, with Kummerowia striata being the most well-known. These plants are characterized by their trifoliate leaves and small, yellow or white flowers that bloom in clusters. They are native to East Asia and are often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

Kummerowia species are also valued for their ability to fix nitrogen, making them useful in soil enrichment.
