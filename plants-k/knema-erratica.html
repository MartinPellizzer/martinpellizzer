Knema erratica

Knema erratica, commonly known as the red-flowered knema, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark-brown bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruits are fleshy, red berries that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by birds.

Knema erratica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
