Kalanchoe panduriformis

Kalanchoe panduriformis, commonly known as the paddle leaf kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red, depending on the variety. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright sunlight, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Kalanchoe panduriformis is also known for its ability to propagate through leaf cuttings, which is a common method of propagation for many succulent species.
