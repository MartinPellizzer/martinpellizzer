Kielmeyera neglecta var. neglecta

Kielmeyera neglecta var. neglecta, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a member of the Myrtaceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil. The plant has smooth, grayish bark and elliptical, glossy leaves with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, red, fleshy fruits.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value but can become invasive in certain environments.
