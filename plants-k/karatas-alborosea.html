Karatas alborosea

Karatas alborosea, commonly known as the white rose tree, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and clusters of fragrant, white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, leathery leaves and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and rocky environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
