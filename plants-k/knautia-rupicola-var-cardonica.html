Knautia rupicola var. cardonica

Knautia rupicola var. cardonica, commonly known as the Cardoon Knautia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It is a perennial member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers arranged in dense, rounded clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically gray-green and covered in fine hairs, adapted to thrive in dry, rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
