Krigia nervosa

Krigia nervosa, commonly known as the narrowleaf krigia, is a herbaceous annual plant native to North America.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves with a distinctive veining pattern, and small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10 to 20 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common sight in the prairie and steppe regions of the United States.
