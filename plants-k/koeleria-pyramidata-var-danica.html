Koeleria pyramidata var. danica

Koeleria pyramidata var. danica, commonly known as the Danica grass, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear small, spike-like inflorescences adorned with delicate blue to purple florets. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings. Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice among gardeners seeking low-maintenance, drought-tolerant plants.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses and cereal crops.
