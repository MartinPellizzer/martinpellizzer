Kalanchoe parviflora

Kalanchoe parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered kalanhoe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming in winter. It is a popular choice for rock gardens and containers due to its low maintenance and ornamental value.

Kalanchoe parviflora is also noted for its ability to propagate through stem cuttings, making it easy to grow and share.
