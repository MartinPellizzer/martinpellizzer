Kibara serrulata

Kibara serrulata, commonly known as the African mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of East Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Kibara serrulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
