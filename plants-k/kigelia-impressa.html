Kigelia impressa

Kigelia impressa, commonly known as the sausage tree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, ridged, bark and large, showy flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces long, woody pods that contain a sticky, milky sap, which has been used traditionally for various purposes. Its botanical features include compound leaves with serrated edges and a unique, trumpet-shaped flower structure.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in some regions.
