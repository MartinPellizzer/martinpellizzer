Kalanchoe gracilipes var. microphylla

Kalanchoe gracilipes var. microphylla, commonly known as the miniature kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features small, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a compact and attractive appearance. The plant produces vibrant, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow during the winter months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its unique foliage and seasonal blooms make it a popular choice for both ornamental and horticultural purposes.
