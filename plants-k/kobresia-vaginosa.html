Kobresia vaginosa

Kobresia vaginosa, commonly known as the leafless grass or desert feather grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often reduced to small sheaths, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is well-adapted to harsh environments, with deep root systems that help it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Kobresia vaginosa plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for small desert organisms.
