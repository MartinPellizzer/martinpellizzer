Koeleria splendens subsp. brutia

Koeleria splendens subsp.

brutia, commonly known as the Sierra grass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Its foliage is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
