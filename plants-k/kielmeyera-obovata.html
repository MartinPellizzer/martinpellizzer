Kielmeyera obovata

Kielmeyera obovata, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruits are fleshy, red berries that contain several seeds.

Kielmeyera obovata is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
