Kobresia curvula

Kobresia curvula, commonly known as the dwarf feather grass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It forms dense, mat-like growths that help stabilize soil and prevent erosion in harsh environments. The plant has narrow, blue-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread across open landscapes.

Kobresia curvula is well adapted to extreme conditions, thriving in areas with limited water and high temperatures.
