Kyllinga erecta var. polyphylla

Kyllinga erecta var. polyphylla, commonly known as the broadleaf spikerush, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in the southeastern United States.

It features upright, grass-like stems that are often reddish in color and bear small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant has narrow, strap-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in two rows along the stem. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, producing tiny, three-lobed flowers that are clustered in dense spikes.

Kyllinga erecta var. polyphylla is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
