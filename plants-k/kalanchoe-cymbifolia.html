Kalanchoe cymbifolia

Kalanchoe cymbifolia, commonly known as the Christmas chandelier, is a succulent native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick, fleshy, and often covered in a powdery white coating. The plant produces clusters of vibrant orange or red flowers during the winter months, typically around Christmas. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light.

Kalanchoe cymbifolia is drought-tolerant and is often used as an ornamental plant in gardens and indoor settings.
