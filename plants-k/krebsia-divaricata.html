Krebsia divaricata

Krebsia divaricata, commonly known as the slender leaf vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes, followed by legume-like pods containing small, round seeds. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for horticultural purposes.
