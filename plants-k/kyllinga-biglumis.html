Kyllinga biglumis

Kyllinga biglumis, commonly known as the broadleaf spikerush, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, grass-like stems with narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. Its inflorescence consists of spike-like clusters of small, three-lobed flowers that are usually greenish or reddish in color.

Kyllinga biglumis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
