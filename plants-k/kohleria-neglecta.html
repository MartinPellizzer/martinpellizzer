Kohleria neglecta

Kohleria neglecta, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spider-like inflorescence that consists of numerous small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial herb, often found in shaded, humid environments. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soil with consistent moisture.

Kohleria neglecta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
