Kunzea spathulata

Kunzea spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leaved kunzea, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow to broad elliptical leaves and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to coastal conditions. It has a compact growth habit and is sometimes used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Kunzea spathulata is also valued for its use in traditional Aboriginal medicine.
