Koeleria phleoides var. anomala

Koeleria phleoides var. anomala, commonly known as the slender wild oat, is a grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its low-growing, tufted habit and narrow, blue-green blades that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often found in disturbed areas and is considered a pioneer species in ecological succession.
