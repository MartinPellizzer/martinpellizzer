Kuhnia eupatorioides var. gracillima

Kuhnia eupatorioides var. gracillima, commonly known as the slender false nettle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, hairy stems that grow upright, often reaching heights of up to 60 cm. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rocky or coastal gardens.
