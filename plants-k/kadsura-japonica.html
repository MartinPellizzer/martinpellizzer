Kadsura japonica

Kadsura japonica, commonly known as Japanese kadsura, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive, pipe-shaped fruits that contain small, black seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
