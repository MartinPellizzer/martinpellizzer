Kentia paradoxa

Kentia paradoxa, commonly known as the Paradox Kentia, is a rare and unique species of cycad native to the island of New Guinea.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that give it a striking, fern-like appearance. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs, typical of cycads. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native environment.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for both horticulturists and botanists.
