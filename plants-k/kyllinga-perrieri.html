Kyllinga perrieri

Kyllinga perrieri, commonly known as the Perrier's spikerush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, spike-like clusters of flowers. Native to Southeast Asia, this species typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swampy areas. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into small, three-lobed seeds.

Kyllinga perrieri is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist conditions.
