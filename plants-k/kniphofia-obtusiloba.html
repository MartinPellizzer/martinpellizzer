Kniphofia obtusiloba

Kniphofia obtusiloba, commonly known as the red hot poker, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, torch-like inflorescences that range in color from red to orange and yellow. The plant features sword-shaped, evergreen leaves that are broad and stiff, providing a bold texture in the garden. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Kniphofia obtusiloba is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
