Knautia glandulosa

Knautia glandulosa, commonly known as the sticky knautia, is a flowering plant native to central and southern Europe.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name. Its flowers are typically pink to purple in color, arranged in showy, clustered inflorescences.

Knautia glandulosa thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.
