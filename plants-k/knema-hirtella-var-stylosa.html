Knema hirtella var. stylosa

Knema hirtella var. stylosa, commonly known as the hairy knema, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, ovate leaves with hairy undersides. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

It is typically found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
