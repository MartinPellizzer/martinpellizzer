Kniphofia marungensis

Kniphofia marungensis, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking member of the Asphodelaceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and dense clusters of vivid red, orange, or yellow tubular flowers that resemble poker chips, hence its common name. The plant produces sword-shaped, grass-like leaves that are typically green or bluish-gray in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Kniphofia marungensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as birds and insects.
