Koeleria cristata var. major

Koeleria cristata var. major, commonly known as the tufted grass or mountain grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for alpine and rocky garden environments.

Koeleria cristata var. major is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
