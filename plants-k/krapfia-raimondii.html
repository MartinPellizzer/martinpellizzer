Krapfia raimondii

Krapfia raimondii, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Namibia and South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with deep root systems and waxy leaves to reduce water loss. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Krapfia raimondii plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing sandy soils.
