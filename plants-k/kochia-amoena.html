Kochia amoena

Kochia amoena, commonly known as burning bush, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red foliage that turns vibrant in the fall. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in late summer, followed by fluffy, seed-bearing capsules. Kochia amoena is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in dry, well-drained soils.

It is also known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
