Koeleria cristata var. densiflora

Koeleria cristata var. densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered tufted grass, is a perennial grass species native to the cold climates of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense flowering spikes that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed sites such as meadows, roadsides, and sandy soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while the flowers are small, spike-like inflorescences.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
