Khaya senegalensis var. spectabilis

Khaya senegalensis var. spectabilis, commonly known as the African shea tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its large, bipinnate leaves and showy yellow flowers. The tree produces elongated pods that contain edible seeds, which are used to make shea butter. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland ecosystems.

The plant is valued for its timber, which is used in furniture and construction, as well as for its economic and cultural significance.
