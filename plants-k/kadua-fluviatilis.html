Kadua fluviatilis

Kadua fluviatilis, commonly known as the river kadua, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, often swampy habitats along rivers and streams. It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters.

Kadua fluviatilis is noted for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
