Koeleria gracilis f. grandiflora

Koeleria gracilis f. grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora form of slender wild oat, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and delicate, blue-green foliage. The plant produces small, fluffy panicles of white to pale blue flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as fields, roadsides, and meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is sometimes grown in gardens as a decorative grass.
