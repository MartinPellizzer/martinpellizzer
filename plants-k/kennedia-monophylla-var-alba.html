Kennedia monophylla var. alba

Kennedia monophylla var. alba, commonly known as the white-flowered gloryflower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its striking white, showy flowers that appear in clusters, often in late winter to early spring. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and public spaces.

Kennedia monophylla var. alba is also valued for its role in ecological restoration due to its ability to attract native pollinators.
