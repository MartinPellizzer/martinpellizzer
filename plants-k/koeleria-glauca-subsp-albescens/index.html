Koeleria Glauca Subsp. Albescens

Koeleria glauca subsp. albescens is a lesser-known yet fascinating member of the grass family, native to the cold, arid regions of northern Eurasia. This plant thrives in harsh environments, often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils where few other species can survive. Its delicate blue-green foliage and small, pale flowers make it a striking yet subtle addition to the landscape. Despite its beauty, it remains underappreciated and rarely cultivated, despite its hardy nature and ornamental potential. Understanding and preserving such species is crucial for maintaining biodiversity in fragile ecosystems.
