Kopsia nana

Kopsia nana, commonly known as the dwarf kopsia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches a height of less than 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Kopsia nana thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.
