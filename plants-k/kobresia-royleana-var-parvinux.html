Kobresia royleana var. parvinux

Kobresia royleana var. parvinux, commonly known as the Tibetan feather grass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and fine, silky-textured leaves. The plant thrives in cold, arid environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically clustered in spikelets, and it plays a crucial role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.

Kobresia royleana var. parvinux is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in mountainous ecosystems.
