Kanahia

Kanahia, commonly known as the Hawaiian tree fern, is a member of the Cyatheaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of large, feathery fronds. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its roots form a dense network that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Kanahia is also valued for its cultural significance in Hawaiian traditions.
