Koeleria gracilis subsp. pseudocristata

Koeleria gracilis subsp.

pseudocristata, commonly known as the slender wild oat, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae. It is characterized by its delicate, slender spikelets and fine, hair-like leaves.

This subspecies is typically found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into dry, three-angled fruits.

The plant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
