Kalmia occidentalis

Kalmia occidentalis, commonly known as the western mountain laurel, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, commonly known as the heath family, and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, and the plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils.

Kalmia occidentalis is often found in forested areas and mountainous regions, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
