Kochia melanoptera

Kochia melanoptera, commonly known as the silverleaf kochia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage that turns red or purple in the fall, making it a striking ornamental plant. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by fluffy, pale tan seed heads. Kochia melanoptera is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.
