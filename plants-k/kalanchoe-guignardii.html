Kalanchoe guignardii

Kalanchoe guignardii, commonly known as the Guignard's kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It features fleshy, thick leaves that store water, typical of its succulent nature. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, often blooming in winter. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Kalanchoe guignardii is also noted for its ability to propagate through stem cuttings, which is a common method for propagating many succulent species.
