Kingiodendron platycarpum

Kingiodendron platycarpum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Pau Brasil*, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark red to black bark, which is highly valued for its durability and historical use in dyeing. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by small, round fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
