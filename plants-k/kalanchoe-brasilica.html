Kalanchoe brasilica

Kalanchoe brasilica, commonly known as the Brazilian windowleaf, is a succulent plant native to Brazil.

It features thick, fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and pink. The plant produces showy, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red during the winter months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Kalanchoe brasilica is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
