Karpatiosorbus andreanszkyana

Karpatiosorbus andreanszkyana, commonly known as the Carpathian rowan, is a deciduous shrub native to the Carpathian Mountains in Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces bright red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It typically grows in rocky or forested areas and is known for its hardy nature and ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity in its native habitat.
