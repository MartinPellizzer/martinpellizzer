Kageneckia crataegifolia

Kageneckia crataegifolia, commonly known as the rosebay willow-herb, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, disturbed soils along roadsides and riverbanks. The plant features upright stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. Its showy, pink to red flowers appear in clusters during late spring to early summer.

Kageneckia crataegifolia is known for its adaptability and can sometimes become invasive in certain regions.
