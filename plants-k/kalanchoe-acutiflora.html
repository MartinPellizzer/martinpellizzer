Kalanchoe acutiflora

Kalanchoe acutiflora, commonly known as the flaming Katy, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine white powdery coating called farina. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in winter. It is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its low maintenance and ability to thrive in various light conditions.

Kalanchoe acutiflora is also known for its ability to propagate through its offsets and leaf cuttings.
