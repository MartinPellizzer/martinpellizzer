Kabuyea

Kabuyea, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a genus of flowering plants in the Campanulaceae family.

It consists of several species native to East Asia, particularly China, Japan, and Korea. The plants are characterized by their bell-shaped, often yellow or orange flowers that bloom in summer. They typically grow in woodland or rocky habitats and are valued for their ornamental appeal.

Kabuyea species are also appreciated in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.
