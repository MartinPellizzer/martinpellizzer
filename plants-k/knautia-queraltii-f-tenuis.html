Knautia queraltii f. tenuis

Knautia queraltii f. tenuis, commonly known as the slender knautia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine regions of the Pyrenees.

It features basal leaves that are narrow and linear, with a hairy texture, and produces clusters of pink to purple flowers in late summer. The plant is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, thriving in full sun conditions. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance in mountain ecosystems.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
