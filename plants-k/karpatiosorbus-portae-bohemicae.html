Karpatiosorbus portae-bohemicae

Karpatiosorbus portae-bohemicae, commonly known as the Bohemian rowan, is a rare and endangered species native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its dense, thorny branches and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is critically endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
