Koeleria cristata f. glabrescens

Koeleria cristata f. glabrescens, commonly known as the glabrous tufted grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial grass characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, with small, wind-pollinated flowers. This plant is native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America and is adapted to thrive in sandy or gravelly soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and habitat restoration in dry environments.
