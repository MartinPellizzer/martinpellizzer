Kalanchoe latisepala

Kalanchoe latisepala, commonly known as the broad-sepal kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in late winter or early spring. Its leaves are also known to produce plantlets along the edges, a characteristic feature of many Kalanchoe species.

This hardy plant is popular in gardens and as a houseplant due to its resilience and ornamental value.
