Kara-angolam

Kara-angolam, commonly known as the Indian rose apple, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that resemble apples, with a pinkish-red skin and a sweet, acidic flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

The tree is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Ayurvedic practices.
