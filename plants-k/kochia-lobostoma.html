Kochia lobostoma

Kochia lobostoma, commonly known as the smooth tumbleweed, is an annual flowering plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and capsule fruits. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in dry, sandy soils, making it well adapted to harsh environmental conditions. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.

The plant's unique morphology and ecological role make it a notable species in xerophytic ecosystems.
