Kurkas quadrisetosum

Kurkas quadrisetosum, commonly known as the four-set grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or riparian habitats.

Kurkas quadrisetosum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
