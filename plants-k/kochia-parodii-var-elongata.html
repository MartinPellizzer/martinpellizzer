Kochia parodii var. elongata

Kochia parodii var. elongata, commonly known as the longleaf kochia, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and its ability to produce showy, feather-like bracts that range in color from pink to red. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping and drought-tolerant gardens. Its foliage is typically silvery-gray, adding visual interest even when not in bloom.

Kochia parodii var. elongata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
