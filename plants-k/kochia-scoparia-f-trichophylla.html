Kochia scoparia f. trichophylla

Kochia scoparia f. trichophylla, commonly known as the silky tumbleweed, is a variant of the common kochia plant.

It is characterized by its dense, silky, silver-gray foliage covered with fine, soft hairs. This feature distinguishes it from the typical form of kochia, which has more rigid, spiny leaves. The plant is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is native to arid and semi-arid regions.

Its botanical traits make it well-adapted to harsh environments, thriving in poor soils and extreme temperatures.
