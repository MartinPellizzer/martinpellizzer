Kalanchoe somaliensis

Kalanchoe somaliensis, commonly known as the Somali Kalanchoe, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Somalia and neighboring areas.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in dry environments. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically during the cooler months. It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its resilience and aesthetic appeal.

Kalanchoe somaliensis is well adapted to full sun and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
