Kixia africana

Kixia africana, commonly known as the African kixia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing, succulent shrub that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. Its small, star-shaped flowers are typically white or pale blue, appearing in clusters during the spring.

Kixia africana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and xeriscapes.
