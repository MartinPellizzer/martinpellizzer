Kyllinga albescens

Kyllinga albescens, commonly known as white kyllinga, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are often white or pale in color, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Kyllinga albescens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
