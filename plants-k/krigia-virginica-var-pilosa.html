Krigia virginica var. pilosa

Krigia virginica var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy knotweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. Its showy yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is characterized by its upright growth habit and is often found in disturbed soils.
