Koeleria gracilis subvar. columbiana

Koeleria gracilis subvar. columbiana, commonly known as the Columbia slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spikelet-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, dry habitats such as gravelly slopes and sagebrush steppe. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
