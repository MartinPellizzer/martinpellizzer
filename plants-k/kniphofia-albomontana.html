Kniphofia albomontana

Kniphofia albomontana, commonly known as the white mountain torch, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the genus Kniphofia, which is part of the grass family, Poaceae. This species is characterized by its tall, upright stem and clusters of bright white to pale yellow flowers that resemble a torch. The leaves are long, narrow, and grass-like, often forming a dense rosette at the base.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
