Kanjarum palghatense

Kanjarum palghatense, commonly known as the Palghat kanjarum, is a rare flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant white flowers that bloom during the monsoon season. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments, preferring well-drained, loamy soils. Its leaves are elliptical and glossy, with a smooth texture, and it has a short, branching stem.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered an endangered species requiring conservation efforts.
