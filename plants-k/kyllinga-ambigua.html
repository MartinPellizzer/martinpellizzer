Kyllinga ambigua

Kyllinga ambigua, commonly known as the swamp morning glory, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often found in low-lying areas with poor drainage. The plant has erect, branched stems and small, showy flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Kyllinga ambigua is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive blooms and adaptability to moist soils.
