Koeleria luerssenii subsp. grossheimiana

Koeleria luerssenii subsp.

grossheimiana, commonly known as the Sierra grass, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in mountainous regions, thriving in full sun. Its inflorescence consists of slender spikelets that produce small, delicate flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
