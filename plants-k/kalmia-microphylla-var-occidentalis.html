Kalmia microphylla var. occidentalis

Kalmia microphylla var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western mountain laurel, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, dark green, elliptical leaves that are typically less than 1 inch in length. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
