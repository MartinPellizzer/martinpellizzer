Kurrimia sinica

Kurrimia sinica, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Kurrimia sinica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
