Kraussia incerta

Kraussia incerta, commonly known as the rock aloes, is a succulent shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces striking yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
