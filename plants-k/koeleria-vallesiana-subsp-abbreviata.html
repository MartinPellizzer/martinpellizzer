Koeleria vallesiana subsp. abbreviata

Koeleria vallesiana subsp.

abbreviata, commonly known as the Sierra grass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States. It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid environments and high altitudes.
