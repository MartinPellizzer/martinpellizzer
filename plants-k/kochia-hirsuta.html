Kochia hirsuta

Kochia hirsuta, commonly known as burning bush, is a flowering plant native to Eurasia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant, showy bracts that turn red in the fall. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in dense clusters, which are typically borne on spindly stalks. Kochia hirsuta is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil conditions.

It is often used as an ornamental plant due to its striking seasonal color changes.
