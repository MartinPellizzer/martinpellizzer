Koeleria glaucovirens

Koeleria glaucovirens, commonly known as the blue grass or blue oat grass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant features narrow, blue-green leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in early summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a hardy ornamental grass.

Koeleria glaucovirens is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
