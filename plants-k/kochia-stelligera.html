Kochia stelligera

Kochia stelligera, commonly known as burning bush, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that turn red or purple in the fall, giving it its common name. The plant has a rosette of blue-gray, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in dense clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Kochia stelligera is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
