Kyllinga imerinensis var. perrieri

Kyllinga imerinensis var. perrieri, commonly known as the Madagascar arrowhead, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along streambanks. It has a compact growth habit with a rosette of leaves that form a dense, low-growing structure.

The botanical features of this species make it an ornamental plant with potential for horticultural use in shaded garden settings.
