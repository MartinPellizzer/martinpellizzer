Knautia lancifolia subsp. turocensis

Knautia lancifolia subsp.

turocensis, commonly known as the Turocian knautia, is a perennial herb native to the Carpathian region. It features upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage and produces clusters of pink to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats at high altitudes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a serrated edge, while its flowers are arranged in dense, conical inflorescences.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in alpine gardens.
