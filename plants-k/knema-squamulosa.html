Knema squamulosa

Knema squamulosa, commonly known as the smooth knema, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody, spherical fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forest areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
