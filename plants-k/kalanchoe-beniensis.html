Kalanchoe beniensis

Kalanchoe beniensis, commonly known as the Beni Kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Kalanchoe beniensis is also noted for its ability to propagate through stem cuttings, which is a common method for propagating succulents.
