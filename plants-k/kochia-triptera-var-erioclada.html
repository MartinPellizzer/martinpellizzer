Kochia triptera var. erioclada

Kochia triptera var. erioclada, commonly known as the silverleaf kochia, is a shrub-like plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, hair-like structures called trichomes. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Kochia triptera var. erioclada is also known for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
