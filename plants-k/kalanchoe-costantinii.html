Kalanchoe costantinii

Kalanchoe costantinii, commonly known as the Christmas chandelier, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that are often covered in a white, powdery coating called farina. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the winter months, typically blooming around Christmas. It is a low-maintenance plant that thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.

Kalanchoe costantinii is popular as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and seasonal flowering.
