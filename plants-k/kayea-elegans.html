Kayea elegans

Kayea elegans, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, silvery-gray bark and glossy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody, edible fruits. Kayea elegans thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
