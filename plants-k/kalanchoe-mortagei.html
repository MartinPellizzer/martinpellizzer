Kalanchoe mortagei

Kalanchoe mortagei, commonly known as the "Mother of Thousands," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and clusters of small, bright green leaves. The plant produces vibrant flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow during the spring and summer seasons. One of its most distinctive features is the numerous tiny plantlets that grow along the edges of its leaves, which can easily propagate new plants.

It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.
