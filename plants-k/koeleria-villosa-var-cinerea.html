Koeleria villosa var. cinerea

Koeleria villosa var. cinerea, commonly known as the gray hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its fine, silky, gray-green foliage that gives it a distinctive, almost silvery appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with brown or purplish hues.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
