Krameria secundiflora

Krameria secundiflora, commonly known as the desert holly, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny, branched stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. It produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically consumed by various desert animals.

Krameria secundiflora is well-suited to xerophytic conditions and plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils in its native habitat.
