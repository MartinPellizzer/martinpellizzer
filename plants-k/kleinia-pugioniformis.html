Kleinia pugioniformis

Kleinia pugioniformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ice plant, is a succulent shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It features small, fleshy, ovate leaves that are typically covered in a white, powdery coating called farina, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or yellow, often blooming during the winter months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal conditions with high salinity and strong winds.

Due to its unique appearance and resilience, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in arid gardens.
