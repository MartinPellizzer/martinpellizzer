Kydia calycina

Kydia calycina, commonly known as the golden gooseberry, is a shrub native to Australia.

It produces small, yellow, berry-like fruits that are edible and have a tart flavor. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered and have five petals, typical of the Rutaceae family.

Kydia calycina is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils.
