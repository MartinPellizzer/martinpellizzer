Koeleria lechleri

Koeleria lechleri, commonly known as the Andean rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes Mountains.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its compact, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, often purple or pink flowers. It is adapted to harsh, cold environments and is often found growing in rocky or alpine soils.

Koeleria lechleri is also known for its use in traditional medicine by indigenous communities in South America.
