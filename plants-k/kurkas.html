Kurkas

Kurkas, commonly known as the "honey plant," is a flowering shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Kurkas is also valued for its edible flowers, which have a sweet, honey-like aroma.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates.
