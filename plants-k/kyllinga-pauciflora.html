Kyllinga pauciflora

Kyllinga pauciflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered spikerush, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its grass-like appearance and slender, erect stems. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, preferring moist to wet soil conditions. Its small, spike-like inflorescences contain numerous tiny flowers, which are usually green or reddish in color.

Kyllinga pauciflora plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
