Kickxia acerbiana var. adenocarpa

Kickxia acerbiana var. adenocarpa, commonly known as the purple globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple flowers and hairy, glandular stems. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grassland and shrubland habitats. Its leaves are alternate and lance-shaped, with a rough texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
