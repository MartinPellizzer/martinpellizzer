Kissodendron australianum var. furfuraceum

Kissodendron australianum var. furfuraceum, commonly known as the Australian she-oak, is a deciduous tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its rough, furrowed bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It is adapted to dry climates and is often found in woodland and heathland environments.

The species is valued for its ecological role and has been used traditionally by Indigenous Australians for various purposes.
