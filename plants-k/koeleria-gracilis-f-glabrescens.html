Koeleria gracilis f. glabrescens

Koeleria gracilis f. glabrescens, commonly known as the glabrous koeleria, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually smooth and hairless, giving it the 'glabrescens' designation.

This variety is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
