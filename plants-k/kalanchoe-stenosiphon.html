Kalanchoe stenosiphon

Kalanchoe stenosiphon, commonly known as the narrow-lobed kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Kalanchoe stenosiphon is also notable for its ability to propagate through stem cuttings, which is a common method used by growers.
