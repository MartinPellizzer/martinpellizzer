Kalopanax ricinifolius

Kalopanax ricinifolius, commonly known as the poison ivy of Japan, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its sap contains urushiol, a substance that can cause severe skin irritation.

Kalopanax ricinifolius is often found in forested areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal despite its hazardous properties.
