Koelreuteria bipinnata var. apiculata

Koelreuteria bipinnata var. apiculata, commonly known as the Chinese lantern tree, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that are twice-pinnate, giving it a finely divided appearance. The tree produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by distinctive, inflated fruit that turns red and resembles lanterns, hence its common name. The bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
