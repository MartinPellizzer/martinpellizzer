Karekandel

Karekandel, commonly known as the "bitter melon," is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other gourds and cucurbits. The plant produces elongated, green fruits that turn orange when ripe, often with a warty surface. Its leaves are heart-shaped and lobed, while the stems are hairy and trailing.

Karekandel is valued for its edible fruits, which are used in various cuisines and traditional medicine.
