Kallstroemia canescens

Kallstroemia canescens, commonly known as the white sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun, exhibiting drought tolerance once established. Its foliage is covered in fine, grayish hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Kallstroemia canescens is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
