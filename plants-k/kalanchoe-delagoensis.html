Kalanchoe delagoensis

Kalanchoe delagoensis, commonly known as the mother of millions, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and clusters of bright orange or yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has thick, waxy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its vibrant blooms and low maintenance requirements.

Kalanchoe delagoensis is also known for its ability to propagate easily through stem cuttings.
