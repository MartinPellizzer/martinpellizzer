Kalmia latifolia f. myrtifolia

Kalmia latifolia f. myrtifolia, commonly known as the Myrtleleaf Mountain Laurel, is a cultivar of the Kalmia latifolia species, native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green and have a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

This cultivar is noted for its compact growth habit and ornamental value, contributing to its appeal in horticultural settings.
