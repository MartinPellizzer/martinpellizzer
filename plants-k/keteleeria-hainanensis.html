Keteleeria hainanensis

Keteleeria hainanensis, commonly known as the Hainan fir, is a coniferous tree native to the mountainous regions of Hainan Island in southern China.

It belongs to the family Pinaceae and is characterized by its slender, conical shape and needle-like leaves. The tree produces cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in subtropical forests.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable species for conservation efforts.
