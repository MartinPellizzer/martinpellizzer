Knema sessiflora

Knema sessiflora, commonly known as the sessile-flower knema, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by fleshy, red or purple berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
