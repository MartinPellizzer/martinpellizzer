Karatas ampullacea

Karatas ampullacea, commonly known as the balloon vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated, balloon-like seed pods that enclose the seeds. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Karatas ampullacea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types with adequate sunlight.
