Kosteletzkya depressa

Kosteletzkya depressa, commonly known as the swamp morning glory, is a flowering plant native to wetland areas in the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in shallow water or moist soils. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple. Its vines can climb or trail, and it is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
