Kutchubaea insignis

Kutchubaea insignis, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble small bells, which bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Kutchubaea insignis prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
