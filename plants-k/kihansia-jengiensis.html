Kihansia jengiensis

Kihansia jengiensis, commonly known as the Jengien sandmat, is a unique plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-like structure with small, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in its harsh environment. The plant produces tiny, white flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a delicate touch to its otherwise desolate habitat. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to anchor firmly in sandy soils and access deep underground water sources.

Kihansia jengiensis plays a vital role in stabilizing desert ecosystems and is adapted to survive extreme temperature fluctuations.
