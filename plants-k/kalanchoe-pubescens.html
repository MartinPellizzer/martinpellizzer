Kalanchoe pubescens

Kalanchoe pubescens, commonly known as the fleshy leaf kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white hair, giving it a woolly appearance. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically during the winter months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it ideal for arid or semi-arid environments.

Kalanchoe pubescens is also noted for its ability to propagate through its leaves, which can develop tiny plantlets along their edges.
