Koanophyllon maestrense

Koanophyllon maestrense, commonly known as the Maestran thistle, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant produces small, spiny leaves and yellowish flower heads that attract various pollinators. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand periods of drought.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
