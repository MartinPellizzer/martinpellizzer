Kalopanax septemlobus

Kalopanax septemlobus, commonly known as the seven-lobed cow-itch, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Araliaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves with seven distinct lobes. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in umbels, followed by red berries that are edible but often considered bitter. Kalopanax septemlobus is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health properties.
