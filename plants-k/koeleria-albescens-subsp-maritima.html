Koeleria albescens subsp. maritima

Koeleria albescens subsp.

maritima, commonly known as the coastal bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to coastal regions of North America. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, blue-green foliage.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear pale blue to white flowers. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dunes and maritime environments. Koeleria albescens subsp.

maritima is adapted to salt spray and can tolerate moderate drought conditions.
