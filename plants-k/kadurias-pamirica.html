Kadurias pamirica

Kadurias pamirica, commonly known as the Pamir bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and erect.
