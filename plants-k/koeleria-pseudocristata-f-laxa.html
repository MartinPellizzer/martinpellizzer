Koeleria pseudocristata f. laxa

Koeleria pseudocristata f. laxa, commonly known as the laxa form of the silky wheatgrass, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the Poaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and tufted growth habit. The plant features slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce delicate blue to purple flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in dryland environments.
