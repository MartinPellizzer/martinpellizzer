Koeleria gracilis subvar. afghanorum

Koeleria gracilis subvar. afghanorum, commonly known as Afghan hairgrass, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia, including regions of Afghanistan and Pakistan.

It is a low-growing perennial grass that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny blue to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Botanically, it is characterized by its fine texture and ability to form dense mats, making it a valuable plant for erosion control in its native habitats.
