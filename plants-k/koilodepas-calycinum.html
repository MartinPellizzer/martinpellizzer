Koilodepas calycinum

Koilodepas calycinum, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Koilodepas calycinum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical landscapes.
