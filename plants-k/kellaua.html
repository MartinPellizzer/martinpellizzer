Kellaua

Kellaua, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are broad and elliptical, with a glossy surface that reflects light.

Kellaua is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
