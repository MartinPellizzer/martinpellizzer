Kickxia chasmophytica

Kickxia chasmophytica, commonly known as the crevice bellflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with small, bell-shaped flowers that typically range in color from white to pale blue. The plant thrives in rocky or crevice habitats, often growing in dry, well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces a basal rosette of foliage.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized species within its native range.
