Kosaria

Kosaria, commonly known as the Indian bellflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Campanulaceae and is characterized by its bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to blue. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 cm and features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Kosaria is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
