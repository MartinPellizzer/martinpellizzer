Krainzia

Krainzia, commonly known as the rock daisy, is a genus of flowering plants native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit. The plants typically produce showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red. Krainzia species are adapted to rocky and arid environments, making them resilient to drought and poor soil conditions.

They are often cultivated in gardens for their ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
