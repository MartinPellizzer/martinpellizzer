Kobus acuminata

Kobus acuminata, commonly known as the slender spurge, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has smooth, waxy leaves that are typically lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.

Kobus acuminata is known for its ability to tolerate partial shade and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
