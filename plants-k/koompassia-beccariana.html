Koompassia beccariana

Koompassia beccariana, commonly known as the yellow koompassia, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale yellow bark and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spikes, and its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds. It thrives in humid, lowland forests and is often found in mixed dipterocarp communities.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
