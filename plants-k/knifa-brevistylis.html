Knifa brevistylis

Knifa brevistylis, commonly known as the short-styled knifegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in loose clusters, which are pollinated by wind. Knifa brevistylis thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
