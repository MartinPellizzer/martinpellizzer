Kixia latifolia

Kixia latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf kixia, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its attractive, palmate leaves that are broad and lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, followed by orange-red berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a suitable choice for ornamental gardens.

Kixia latifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscape design for its seasonal interest.
