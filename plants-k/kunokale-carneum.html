Kunokale carneum

Kunokale carneum, commonly known as the crimson velvet fern, is a rare and striking plant native to the arid regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, velvety leaves that range in color from deep red to burgundy, providing a unique visual contrast in its habitat. The plant's flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in shades of pink and white, and bloom during the wet season. Kunokale carneum thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to its harsh environment.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
