Kamptzia

Kamptzia, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Fabaceae.

It consists of several species, most notably Kamptzia albida, which is native to South America. The plant is characterized by its striking yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

The leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant is often found in moist, well-drained soils in tropical and subtropical regions.
