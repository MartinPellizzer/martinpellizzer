Kalanchoe pseudocampanulata

Kalanchoe pseudocampanulata, commonly known as the bell-shaped kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture, adapted to store water. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or orange, which bloom in clusters during the winter months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect sunlight.

Kalanchoe pseudocampanulata is popular as a houseplant due to its low maintenance and vibrant floral display.
