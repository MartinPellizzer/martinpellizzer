Kalanchoe pyramidalis

Kalanchoe pyramidalis, commonly known as the Christmas chandelier, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features dense, upright clusters of fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that form a rosette structure. The plant produces vibrant orange or red flowers in the winter months, typically around Christmas. It is drought-tolerant and prefers well-draining soil and bright, indirect sunlight.

Kalanchoe pyramidalis is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its striking appearance and ease of care.
