Kerchovea

Kerchovea, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a genus of flowering plants native to the Andes Mountains.

These plants are characterized by their showy, bell-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. Kerchovea species typically grow in high-altitude environments, where they thrive in cool, moist conditions. They have a rosette growth form with thick, fleshy leaves that help store water.

The genus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable climates.
