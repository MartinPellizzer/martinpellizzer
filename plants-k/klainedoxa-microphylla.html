Klainedoxa microphylla

Klainedoxa microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved klainedoxa, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in grassland or shrubland habitats. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and are arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Klainedoxa microphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
