Kickxia × confinis

Kickxia × confinis, commonly known as the hybrid rock cress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many economically important crops and ornamental plants. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
