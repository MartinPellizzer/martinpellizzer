Kraunhia brachybotrys

Kraunhia brachybotrys, commonly known as the narrow-leaved kraunhia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright, branching stems that are covered in fine, hairy hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming from late winter to early spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, which helps it to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils.

Kraunhia brachybotrys is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
