Kibara shungolensis

Kibara shungolensis, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, clustered inflorescences that produce small, yellowish flowers resembling mist. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

Kibara shungolensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
