Kolerma

Kolerma, commonly known as *Rhodiola rosea*, is a perennial plant native to cold climates of the Arctic and alpine regions.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, reddish stems and small, pink to white flowers. The plant has a long history of use in traditional medicine for its adaptogenic properties. Kolerma thrives in well-drained soils and can tolerate extreme temperatures.

Its rhizomes are often harvested for their medicinal compounds, including rosavins and salidrosides.
