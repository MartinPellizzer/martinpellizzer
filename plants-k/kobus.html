Kobus

Kobus, commonly known as the African wild cucumber, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Cucurbitaceae, which includes other plants like cucumbers and pumpkins. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that give way to elongated, spiny fruits. Its botanical name, Kobus, is derived from the ancient Greek word for "to climb," reflecting its vine-like growth habit.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
