Kummeria brasiliensis

Kummeria brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian fireweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and somewhat woody.

Kummeria brasiliensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
