Kalanchoe pruinosa

Kalanchoe pruinosa, commonly known as the silver leaf kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are covered in a waxy, silver-gray coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically during the winter months. It is a perennial shrub that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Kalanchoe pruinosa is popular as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
