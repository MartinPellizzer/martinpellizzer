Kniphofia reflexa

Kniphofia reflexa, commonly known as the red hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that bear dense clusters of bright orange-red tubular flowers. The plant's foliage consists of long, narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Kniphofia reflexa is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
