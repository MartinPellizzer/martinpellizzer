Kokia

Kokia, commonly known as the "mountain apple" or "kōkīa," is a genus of flowering plants native to the Hawaiian Islands.

These shrubs or small trees are characterized by their unique, clustered inflorescences and showy, often pink or white flowers. Kokia species are typically found in coastal and lowland habitats, growing in well-drained soils. The plant's leaves are usually simple, alternate, and have a waxy texture.

Kokia is also notable for its ornamental value and historical significance in Hawaiian culture.
