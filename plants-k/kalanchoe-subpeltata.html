Kalanchoe subpeltata

Kalanchoe subpeltata, commonly known as the silver velvet kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves covered in a fine, silvery-white coating that reflects light and reduces water loss. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of orange, yellow, and red during the winter months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for arid environments.

Kalanchoe subpeltata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and resilience.
