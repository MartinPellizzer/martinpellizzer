Kyllinga cyperina

Kyllinga cyperina, commonly known as spikerush, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with slender, erect stems and small, clustered spikelets. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, wetlands, and along the edges of ponds. Kyllinga cyperina has narrow, linear leaves and produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
