Knightia integrifolia

Knightia integrifolia, commonly known as the desert sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with entire margins, and it has a deep root system to access water.

It is well adapted to harsh desert conditions and is often found in sandy soils.
