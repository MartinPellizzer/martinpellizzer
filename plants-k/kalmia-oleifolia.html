Kalmia oleifolia

Kalmia oleifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf mountain heath, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heaths and heathers, and is characterized by its evergreen or deciduous foliage depending on the climate. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, and it thrives in acidic, well-drained soils.

Kalmia oleifolia is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and wetlands.
