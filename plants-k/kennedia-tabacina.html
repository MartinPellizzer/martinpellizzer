Kennedia tabacina

Kennedia tabacina, commonly known as the Tobacco Bush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with narrow, linear leaves. Its flowers are often visited by birds and insects, contributing to its ecological importance.

Kennedia tabacina is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
