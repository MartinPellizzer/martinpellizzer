Koeleria gracilis var. mukdenensis

Koeleria gracilis var. mukdenensis, commonly known as the Mukden grass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and fine, thread-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers. It is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of grassland ecosystems.
