Koenigia campanulata var. fulvida

Koenigia campanulata var. fulvida, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a reddish or purplish tint. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often silver or gray-green in color.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
