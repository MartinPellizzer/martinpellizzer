Koeleria obtusata

Koeleria obtusata, commonly known as the bluntleaf grass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Koeleria obtusata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid regions.
