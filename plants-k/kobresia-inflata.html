Kobresia inflata

Kobresia inflata, commonly known as the inflated kobresia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family, commonly found in high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, cylindrical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces spikelets that are inflated and segmented, giving it a unique appearance. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky or sandy soils.

Kobresia inflata is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine or subalpine vegetation zones.
