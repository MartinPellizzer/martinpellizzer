Kedrostis nana var. nana

Kedrostis nana var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf African dandelion, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Africa.

It features a rosette of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by distinctive seed pods that split open to release tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
