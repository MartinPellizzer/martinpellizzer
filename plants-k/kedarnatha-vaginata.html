Kedarnatha vaginata

Kedarnatha vaginata, commonly known as the sacred forest tree, is a species native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with smooth, greyish-brown bark that becomes fissured with age. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a glossy upper surface and a lighter, often hairy underside. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, round fruits that are edible.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is often found in sacred groves, reflecting its cultural and ecological significance.
