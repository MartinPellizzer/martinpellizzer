Kunzea peduncularis var. brachyandra

Kunzea peduncularis var. brachyandra, commonly known as the narrow-leaved kunzea, is a shrub native to the south-western region of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal heathlands and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
