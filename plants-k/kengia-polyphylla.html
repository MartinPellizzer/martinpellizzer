Kengia polyphylla

Kengia polyphylla, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has a smooth, greyish-bark that becomes fissured with age, and its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate. Kengia polyphylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.

It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.
