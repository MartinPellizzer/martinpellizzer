Kneiffia suffruticosa

Kneiffia suffruticosa, commonly known as the African star-of-Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and small, narrow leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow, star-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. It is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
