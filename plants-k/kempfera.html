Kempfera

Kempfera, commonly known as the sago palm, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Arecaceae.

It consists of several species, most notably Kempfera sphaerocarpa, which is native to Southeast Asia. The plants are characterized by their tall, slender stems and pinnately compound leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. They produce small, yellowish flowers that are typically followed by spiny, spherical fruits.

Kempfera species are valued for their ornamental appearance and are often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
