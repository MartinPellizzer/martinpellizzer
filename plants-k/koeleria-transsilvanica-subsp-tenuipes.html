Koeleria transsilvanica subsp. tenuipes

Koeleria transsilvanica subsp.

tenuipes, commonly known as the slender leaf koeleria, is a perennial grass species native to the Carpathian region. It typically grows in open, rocky habitats and is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing minute, blue to purple flowers. It is adapted to temperate climates and can thrive in well-drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its native range.
