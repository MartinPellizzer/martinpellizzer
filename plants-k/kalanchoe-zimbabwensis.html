Kalanchoe zimbabwensis

Kalanchoe zimbabwensis, commonly known as the Zimbabwean Kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating, adapted for storing water in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, often blooming in winter. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Kalanchoe zimbabwensis is also noted for its ability to propagate through stem cuttings, which is a common method for propagation in succulents.
