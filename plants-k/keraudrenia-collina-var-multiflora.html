Keraudrenia collina var. multiflora

Keraudrenia collina var. multiflora, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
