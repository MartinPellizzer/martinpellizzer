Karatas carolinae

Karatas carolinae, commonly known as the Carolina karatas, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems that are covered in soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge.

Karatas carolinae is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas.
