Knema lenta

Knema lenta, commonly known as the slow-growing evergreen tree, is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
