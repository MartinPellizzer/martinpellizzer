Knautia arvensis var. subintegerrima

Knautia arvensis var. subintegerrima, commonly known as the smooth knautia, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of pink to purple flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a slightly fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and grasslands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
