Knautia kitaibelii subsp. alpigena

Knautia kitaibelii subsp.

alpigena, commonly known as the alpine knautia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its clustered, pink to purple flower heads that resemble daisies.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it thrives in well-drained, sunny conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
