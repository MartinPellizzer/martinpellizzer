Koeleria aegyptiaca

Koeleria aegyptiaca, commonly known as Egyptian goatgrass, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Africa.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and typically grows in dry, rocky soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Koeleria aegyptiaca is also known for its historical use in traditional medicine and as a forage crop for livestock.
