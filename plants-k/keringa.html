Keringa

Keringa, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, tuberous roots and velvety, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Keringa is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
