Kochia muricata

Kochia muricata, commonly known as the spiny kochia, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the amaranth family (Amaranthaceae) and is characterized by its succulent, often spiny stems and leaves. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Kochia muricata is adapted to harsh environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soils.

It is often used as a ornamental plant due to its distinctive appearance and resilience.
