Kniphofia neumanii var. albiflora

Kniphofia neumanii var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered torch lily, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Hemerocallidaceae and is characterized by its tall, upright stem bearing clusters of tubular, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant features broad, strap-like leaves that form a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its vibrant display and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking low-maintenance, showy plants.
