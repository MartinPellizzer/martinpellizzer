Knautia purpurea var. oligadena

Knautia purpurea var. oligadena, commonly known as the purple knautia, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flower heads that resemble small clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows and dry grasslands, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
