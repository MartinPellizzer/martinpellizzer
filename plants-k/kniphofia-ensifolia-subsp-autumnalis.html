Kniphofia ensifolia subsp. autumnalis

Kniphofia ensifolia subsp.

autumnalis, commonly known as the autumn torch lily, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its tall, strap-like leaves and showy, red-orange inflorescences that bloom in late summer to early autumn.

The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its vibrant flowers attract pollinators such as bees and birds, contributing to local biodiversity.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is a popular choice for both gardeners and conservationists.
