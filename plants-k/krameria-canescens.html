Krameria canescens

Krameria canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf krameria, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to dry, hot environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, and its leaves are covered in a dense layer of white, fuzzy hairs. Krameria canescens has a unique root system that helps it conserve water, and it is often found in desert ecosystems.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for survival in harsh, semi-arid climates.
