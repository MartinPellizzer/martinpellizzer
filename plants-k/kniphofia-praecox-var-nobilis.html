Kniphofia praecox var. nobilis

Kniphofia praecox var. nobilis, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features tall, upright stems that bear dense clusters of tubular, brightly colored flowers, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. The leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often form a basal rosette. This plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its showy blooms attract pollinators such as bees and birds, contributing to its ecological value.
