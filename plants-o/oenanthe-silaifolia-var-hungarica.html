Oenanthe silaifolia var. hungarica

Oenanthe silaifolia var. hungarica, commonly known as the Hungarian water dropwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils along streambanks and wet meadows. Its leaves are pinnately divided and often have a distinct, slightly bitter taste.

The small white flowers appear in late spring to early summer, followed by small, oval berries that are dispersed by water.
