Oxypetalum erectum var. floribundum

Oxypetalum erectum var. floribundum, commonly known as the flowering oxypetalum, is a shrub native to southern China.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, and usually hairy leaves that are elliptical in shape. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is primarily due to its showy flowers and compact growth habit.
