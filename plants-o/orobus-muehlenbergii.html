Orobus muehlenbergii

Orobus muehlenbergii, commonly known as Muhlenberg's catchfly, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Orobus muehlenbergii is often found in the eastern United States and is adapted to thrive in temperate climates.
