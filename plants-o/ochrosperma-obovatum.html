Ochrosperma obovatum

Ochrosperma obovatum, commonly known as the broad-leafed hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, obovate leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Ochrosperma obovatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
