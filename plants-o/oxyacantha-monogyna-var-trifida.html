Oxyacantha monogyna var. trifida

Oxyacantha monogyna var. trifida, commonly known as the three-lobed whitebeam, is a variant of the common whitebeam, native to parts of Europe.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its distinctive trifid (three-lobed) leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, red berries in autumn. Its leaves are typically oval with serrated edges, and it thrives in woodland or hedgerow environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for wildlife.
