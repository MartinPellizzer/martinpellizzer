Ocotea carabobensis

Ocotea carabobensis, commonly known as the Carabobo ocotea, is a small evergreen tree native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its lanceolate, glossy leaves and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a small, dark blue drupe that contains a single seed.

Ocotea carabobensis is valued for its aromatic wood and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.
