Opuntia basilaris var. albiflora

Opuntia basilaris var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in spines and glochids, which provide protection from herbivores. The plant produces clusters of white, tubular flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. Its leaves are modified into stems, a common adaptation in arid environments to reduce water loss.

The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping in desert regions.
