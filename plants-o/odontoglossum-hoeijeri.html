Odontoglossum hoeijeri

Odontoglossum hoeijeri, commonly known as the Mexican violet, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central America.

It is a herbaceous plant with rhizomatous growth, producing pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant features showy, violet-colored flowers with a distinctive lip that attracts pollinators. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Odontoglossum hoeijeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
