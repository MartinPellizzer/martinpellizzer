Ophrys nicotiae

Ophrys nicotiae, commonly known as the tobacco orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female wasp to attract male wasps for pollination. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded habitats. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brown in color, with intricate patterns that aid in its mimicry.

Ophrys nicotiae is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ecological significance in supporting specific pollinator species.
