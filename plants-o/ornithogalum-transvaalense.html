Ornithogalum transvaalense

Ornithogalum transvaalense, commonly known as the Transvaal star-of-Bethleem, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of star-shaped, white or pale pink flowers during the summer months. The plant is characterized by its underground bulb, which stores nutrients and water for survival. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
