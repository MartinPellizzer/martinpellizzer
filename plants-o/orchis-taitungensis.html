Orchis taitungensis

Orchis taitungensis, commonly known as Taitung orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique floral structure and vibrant coloration. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories, where it can access the necessary light for photosynthesis. Its flowers are often adorned with intricate patterns and hues of purple and white, contributing to its ornamental value.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a conservation concern.
