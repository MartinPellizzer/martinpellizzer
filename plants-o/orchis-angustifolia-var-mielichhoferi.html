Orchis angustifolia var. mielichhoferi

Orchis angustifolia var. mielichhoferi, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to specific regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and produces striking, pale pink to purple flowers with a distinctive spurred lip. The plant typically grows in calcareous grasslands and woodland edges, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its reproductive strategy involves a symbiotic relationship with specific mycorrhizal fungi, which is crucial for its germination and growth.

Due to habitat loss and fragmentation, this variety is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
