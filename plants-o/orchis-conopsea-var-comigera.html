Orchis conopsea var. comigera

Orchis conopsea var. comigera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved early purple orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous grasslands and woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers in early spring, often with a distinctive hooded labellum. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it has a rhizomatous root system that helps it survive in its habitat.

This orchid is part of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its specialized pollination mechanisms involving specific insects.
