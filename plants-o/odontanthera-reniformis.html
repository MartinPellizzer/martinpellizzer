Odontanthera reniformis

Odontanthera reniformis, commonly known as the kidney-shaped grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive kidney-shaped leaves, which are often hairy and have a bluish-green color. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptability, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
