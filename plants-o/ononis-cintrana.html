Ononis cintrana

Ononis cintrana, commonly known as rock trefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to rocky and coastal areas of the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant has a deep root system that helps stabilize soil in harsh environments. It is well adapted to poor, sandy soils and can tolerate salt spray.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
