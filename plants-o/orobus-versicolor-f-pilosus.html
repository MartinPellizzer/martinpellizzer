Orobus versicolor f. pilosus

Orobus versicolor f. pilosus, commonly known as the hairy orobus, is a variant of the orobus plant characterized by its distinctive hairy foliage.

This plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is native to certain regions of North America. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with a soft, pubescent texture that distinguishes it from other forms of orobus. The flowers are usually pale blue or white, and the plant produces small, legume-like pods.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
