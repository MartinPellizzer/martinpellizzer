Oxalis glaberrima

Oxalis glaberrima, commonly known as the smooth wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its stems are often hairy, and the leaves are glossy with a waxy coating.

Oxalis glaberrima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
