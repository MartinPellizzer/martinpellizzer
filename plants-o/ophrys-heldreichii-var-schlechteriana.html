Ophrys heldreichii var. schlechteriana

Ophrys heldreichii var. schlechteriana, commonly known as the Heldreich's bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the high-altitude regions of the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments with partial shade. Its flowers are usually greenish-brown with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

This orchid is of particular interest to botanists due to its ecological specificity and limited distribution.
