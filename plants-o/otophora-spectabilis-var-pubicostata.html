Otophora spectabilis var. pubicostata

Otophora spectabilis var. pubicostata, commonly known as the showy otophora, is a striking flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that consist of numerous small, tubular flowers arranged in a dense, spike-like cluster. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy texture, and the flowers display a range of colors from pale pink to deep red.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
