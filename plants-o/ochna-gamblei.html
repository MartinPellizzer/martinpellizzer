Ochna gamblei

Ochna gamblei, commonly known as the Gambler's ochna, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruits are follicles that split open to release seeds, and the plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Ochna gamblei is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
