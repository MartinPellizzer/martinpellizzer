Ocotea globosa

Ocotea globosa, commonly known as the globular ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by spherical, berry-like fruits. Its bark and leaves have been used traditionally for their medicinal properties.

Ocotea globosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
