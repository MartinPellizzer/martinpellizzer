Ocotea glaucophylla

Ocotea glaucophylla, commonly known as the white-leaf ocotea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in countries like Brazil, Colombia, and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. It typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters.

Ocotea glaucophylla is valued for its aromatic wood and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
