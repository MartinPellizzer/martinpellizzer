Orchis papilionacea

Orchis papilionacea, commonly known as the butterfly orchid, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, butterfly-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in woodland and grassland habitats, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a unique structure that aids in pollination by specific insects.

Orchis papilionacea is known for its ornamental value and is often found in conservation efforts due to its declining populations.
