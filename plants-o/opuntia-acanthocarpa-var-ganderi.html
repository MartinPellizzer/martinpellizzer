Opuntia acanthocarpa var. ganderi

Opuntia acanthocarpa var. ganderi, commonly known as Gander's prickly pear, is a succulent cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-like stems covered in clusters of spines and glochids, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers during the spring and summer months, followed by edible, red-fleshed fruits. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it stores water in its thick, fleshy stems, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and hardy nature.
