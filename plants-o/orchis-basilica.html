Orchis basilica

Orchis basilica, commonly known as the cathedral orchid, is a striking member of the orchid family native to Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 centimeters in length. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of violet to purple flowers that resemble a cathedral's stained glass. It thrives in moist, shaded woodlands and requires well-drained soil to flourish.

Orchis basilica is also noted for its medicinal properties, historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
