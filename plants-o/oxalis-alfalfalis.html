Oxalis alfalfalis

Oxalis alfalfalis, commonly known as the spurred oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each leaflet bearing a small spur at the base. The plant produces clusters of delicate, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow and orange. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Oxalis alfalfalis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and blooms.
