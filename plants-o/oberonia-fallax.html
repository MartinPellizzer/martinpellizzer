Oberonia fallax

Oberonia fallax, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pendant-like flowers that bloom in shades of white and green. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, using its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted to retain water in its humid, shaded habitat.

Oberonia fallax is prized in horticulture for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
