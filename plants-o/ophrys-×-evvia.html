Ophrys × evvia

Ophrys × evvia, commonly known as the Euboean bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Greek island of Euboea.

It belongs to the genus Ophrys, which is characterized by its unique floral structures that mimic female insects to attract male pollinators. The plant produces showy, bee-like flowers with intricate patterns and colors that closely resemble female bees, a feature known as pseudocopulation. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in open habitats, and it blooms during the spring and early summer.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting specific pollinator interactions and its conservation significance in its native region.
