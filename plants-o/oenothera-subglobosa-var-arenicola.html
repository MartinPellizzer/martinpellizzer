Oenothera subglobosa var. arenicola

Oenothera subglobosa var. arenicola, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compact, globular growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, helping to reduce water loss.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as moths and beetles.
