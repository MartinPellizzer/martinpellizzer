Orobus variegatus var. hirtellus

Orobus variegatus var. hirtellus, commonly known as the hairy orobus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical legume structure. The plant features trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces, giving it its common name. Its small, pea-like flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces elongated pods containing seeds.

It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.
