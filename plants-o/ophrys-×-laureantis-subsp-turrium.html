Ophrys × laureantis subsp. turrium

Ophrys × laureantis subsp.

turrium, commonly known as the Turrian turban orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its distinctive floral structure, featuring a labellum that mimics the appearance of an insect, likely to attract specific pollinators.

The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments. Its flowers are usually a mix of brown and green hues, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
