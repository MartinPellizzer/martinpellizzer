Oxytropis leucantha var. gaspensis

Oxytropis leucantha var. gaspensis, commonly known as Gaspens wild locoweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as sagebrush steppe and grasslands. The plant features compound leaves with small, stiff leaflets and produces clusters of pale yellow to greenish flowers. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with soil bacteria.

This variety is characterized by its low-growing form and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
