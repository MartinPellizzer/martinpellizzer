Oenothera nuttallii

Oenothera nuttallii, commonly known as Nuttall's evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy yellow or orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Oenothera nuttallii is a perennial herb with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats like grasslands and rocky slopes.
