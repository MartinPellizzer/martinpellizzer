Obolaria

Obolaria, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, often brightly colored flowers. The plants typically grow in rocky or crevice habitats, showcasing their adaptability to harsh environments. Obolaria species are native to various regions in the Northern Hemisphere, including Europe, Asia, and North America.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and are often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and ample sunlight.
