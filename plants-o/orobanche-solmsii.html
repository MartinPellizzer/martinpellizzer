Orobanche solmsii

Orobanche solmsii, commonly known as the yellow witchweed, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is native to parts of Europe, North Africa, and the Middle East. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has a unique reproductive strategy involving the secretion of chemicals to attract specific host species. Its life cycle is closely tied to the presence of its host, often leading to reduced growth and yield in agricultural crops.

Due to its parasitic nature, it is considered a weed in some regions and poses challenges for sustainable farming practices.
