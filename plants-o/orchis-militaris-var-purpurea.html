Orchis militaris var. purpurea

Orchis militaris var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple military orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem that can reach up to 1 meter in height, bearing lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking purple to violet flowers in late spring to early summer, arranged in a loose spike. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
