Oxybaphus cordifolius

Oxybaphus cordifolius, commonly known as the heart-leaved oxeye, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is typically found in grassland and woodland habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Oxybaphus cordifolius is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
