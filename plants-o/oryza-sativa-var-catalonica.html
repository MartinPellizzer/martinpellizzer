Oryza sativa var. catalonica

Oryza sativa var. catalonica, commonly known as the Catalonian rice, is a lesser-known variant of the rice plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its short, compact growth habit and smaller grain size compared to other rice varieties. The plant typically thrives in temperate climates and requires a warm, humid environment for optimal growth. Its botanical features include slender, erect stems and small, lanceolate leaves.

This variety is of interest to botanists and agricultural researchers due to its unique genetic makeup and potential for adaptation to specific regional conditions.
