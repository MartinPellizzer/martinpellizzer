Oxalis europaea f. pallidiflora

Oxalis europaea f. pallidiflora, commonly known as the pale-flowered wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive veining pattern and produces small, pale yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and it is often found in rocky or calcareous soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and flowers.
