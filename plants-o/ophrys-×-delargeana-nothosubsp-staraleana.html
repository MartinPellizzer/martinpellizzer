Ophrys × delargeana nothosubsp.

staraleana, commonly known as the Staraleana Ophrys, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its distinctive, bee-mimicking flowers that play a crucial role in its pollination strategy.

The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, thriving in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its floral structure includes a specialized labellum that enhances its attractiveness to pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
