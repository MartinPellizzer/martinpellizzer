Oenothera polymorpha var. indecora

Oenothera polymorpha var. indecora, commonly known as the showy evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and showy yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in arid and semi-arid regions.
