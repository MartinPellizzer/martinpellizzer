Oxytropis coronaminis

Oxytropis coronaminis, commonly known as the crown vetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, bushy growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Oxytropis coronaminis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and fix nitrogen.
