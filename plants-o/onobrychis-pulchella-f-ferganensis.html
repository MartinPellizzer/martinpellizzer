Onobrychis pulchella f. ferganensis

Onobrychis pulchella f. ferganensis, commonly known as the Fergana clover, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes. The plant is adapted to dry and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species for arid regions. It has a fibrous root system that helps in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion.

This variety is often used in forage and soil conservation due to its resilience and nutritional value.
