Onobrychis grandis lusus purpurea

Onobrychis grandis lusus purpurea, commonly known as the purple vetch, is a leguminous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, purple-pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is native to parts of Europe and is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. It has a deep root system that helps in soil stabilization and nitrogen fixation.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and erosion control.
