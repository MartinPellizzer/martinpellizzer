Opuntia leucostata

Opuntia leucostata, commonly known as the white-flowered prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems, which are segmented and covered in spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow or white flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tunas, is edible and has a sweet, juicy flavor.

Opuntia leucostata is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and habitat for various wildlife.
