Olea perrieri

Olea perrieri, commonly known as the Perrier's olive, is a species of flowering plant in the Oleaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its small, greenish-yellow flowers and small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, edible olives that are typically used for culinary purposes. Olea perrieri thrives in well-drained soil and requires a temperate climate with adequate sunlight.

It is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and its fruit.
