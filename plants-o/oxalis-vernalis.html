Oxalis vernalis

Oxalis vernalis, commonly known as spring wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and small, delicate flowers that bloom in shades of yellow or white. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowering period occurs in early spring, making it a notable early bloom in woodland ecosystems.

Oxalis vernalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
