Osteospermum acanthospermum

Osteospermum acanthospermum, commonly known as the blue oatbrush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features striking blue to purple ray flowers surrounded by yellow disk flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its hairy, upright stems and narrow leaves are adapted to arid environments, making it drought-tolerant.

Osteospermum acanthospermum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and resilience.
