Orchis bifolia var. virens

Orchis bifolia var. virens, commonly known as the green bilberry orchid, is a species of orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-lipped flowers that range in color from green to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and is often found in association with other orchid species. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its unique appearance.

The green bilberry orchid is a perennial herb that relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake, highlighting its ecological complexity.
