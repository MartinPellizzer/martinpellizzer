Ornithogalum algeriense subsp. atlanticum

Ornithogalum algeriense subsp.

atlanticum, commonly known as the Atlantic Algerian star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features a basal rosette of narrow, linear leaves and produces bell-shaped, white or pale yellow flowers in late spring to early summer.

The plant is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats. It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous underground storage organs.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
