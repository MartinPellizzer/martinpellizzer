Origanum × majoricum subvar. acinifolium

Origanum × majoricum subvar. acinifolium, commonly known as lemon thyme, is a hybrid perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, lemon-scented leaves that are both ornamental and aromatic. The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers in summer, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its essential oils, rich in thymol and linalool, are valued in both culinary and medicinal applications.
