Odontosoria clavata

Odontosoria clavata, commonly known as the broad-leafed wall-rue, is a fern native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Pacific Islands.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and produces fertile fronds with sporangia on the underside of the leaves. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with lance-shaped pinnae that are often clustered in a rosette formation.

Odontosoria clavata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
