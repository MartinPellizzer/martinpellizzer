Oxypetalum alpinum

Oxypetalum alpinum, commonly known as the alpine oxypetalum, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine environments, preferring well-drained, rocky soils at high elevations. Its foliage consists of simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white or gray hairs.

Oxypetalum alpinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
