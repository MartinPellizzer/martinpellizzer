Orobus hispanicus

Orobus hispanicus, commonly known as the Spanish orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, spurred orchid flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in sunny, open habitats. Its flowers, which are usually pale pink or white, bloom in early spring.

Orobus hispanicus is an important species for pollinators and is often found in Mediterranean-type climates.
