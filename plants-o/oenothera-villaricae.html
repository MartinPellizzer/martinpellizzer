Oenothera villaricae

Oenothera villaricae, commonly known as the Villarica evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of Chile.

It is characterized by its showy yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has hairy, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Oenothera villaricae is part of the Onagraceae family, which includes other evening primroses and related species.
