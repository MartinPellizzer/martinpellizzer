Orchis patens var. orientalis

Orchis patens var. orientalis, commonly known as the Eastern twayblade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodlands and grasslands. It has a symbiotic relationship with mycorrhizal fungi, which aid in nutrient absorption.

Its flowers are usually arranged in a spike and bloom in late spring to early summer.
