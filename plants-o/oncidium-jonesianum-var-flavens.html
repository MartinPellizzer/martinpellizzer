Oncidium jonesianum var. flavens

Oncidium jonesianum var. flavens, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features vibrant yellow flowers with a distinctive golden-orange lip, making it visually striking. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. It requires bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.

Its flowers bloom in clusters, often lasting several weeks, adding ornamental value to gardens and collections.
