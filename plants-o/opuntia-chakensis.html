Opuntia chakensis

Opuntia chakensis, commonly known as the Chakenson cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems that store water efficiently. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by small, fleshy fruits. Its adaptations to harsh environments include reduced leaves and a thick cuticle to minimize water loss.

Opuntia chakensis is often used in local horticulture due to its resilience and ornamental value.
