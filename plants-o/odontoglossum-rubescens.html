Odontoglossum rubescens

Odontoglossum rubescens, commonly known as the red-tipped orchid, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb and produces striking, showy flowers with a distinctive red tip. The plant typically blooms in the spring and has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in cool, moist environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Odontoglossum rubescens is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its vibrant color and elegant floral structure.
