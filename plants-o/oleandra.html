Oleandra

Oleandra, commonly known as the common oleander, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Apocynaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that come in a variety of colors such as pink, red, and white. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces milky sap that can be toxic if ingested. Oleanders are shrubs or small trees that thrive in warm climates and are often used in landscaping for their ornamental value.

They are also known for their ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
