Omphalea malayana

Omphalea malayana, commonly known as the Malay prickly nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are usually ovate with serrated edges, and the stems are covered in fine, sticky hairs.

Omphalea malayana belongs to the family Solanaceae, which includes other nightshade plants.
