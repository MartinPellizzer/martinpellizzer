Oryza sativa subsp. fatua

Oryza sativa subsp.

fatua, commonly known as wild rice, is a grass species native to temperate regions of Asia. It is characterized by its elongated, awned grains and grows in wetland habitats. The plant typically reaches heights of 1 to 2 meters and has hollow, cylindrical stems.

Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a prominent midrib. The botanical features of Oryza sativa subsp. fatua make it distinct from its cultivated counterpart, Oryza sativa subsp.

sativa.
