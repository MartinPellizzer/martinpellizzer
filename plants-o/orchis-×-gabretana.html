Orchis × gabretana

Orchis × gabretana, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Orchis morio and Orchis anthropophora.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family, characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows across Europe. Its flowers are often arranged in a loose spike and are pollinated by various insects, including bees and moths.

The hybrid nature of Orchis × gabretana makes it a subject of interest in both botanical research and horticulture.
