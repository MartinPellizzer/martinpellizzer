Osteomeles latifolia

Osteomeles latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf hibiscus, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically blooming in late summer to early fall. The plant has alternate, broad, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy surface. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Osteomeles latifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
