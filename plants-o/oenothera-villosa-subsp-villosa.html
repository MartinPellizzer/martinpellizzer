Oenothera villosa subsp. villosa

Oenothera villosa subsp.

villosa, commonly known as the silky evening primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America. It is characterized by its hairy stems and showy yellow flowers that open in the evening.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oily seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as prairies and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and ornamental gardening.
