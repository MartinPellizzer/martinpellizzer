Ophrys × apiculata

Ophrys × apiculata, commonly known as the bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive flower structure, which resembles a female bee, a feature that plays a key role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its flowers are usually a mix of yellow, brown, and black, with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of insects.

Ophrys × apiculata is known for its specialized relationship with specific bee species, which it attracts through its mimicry.
