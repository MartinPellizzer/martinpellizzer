Oldenlandia angustifolia var. filifolia

Oldenlandia angustifolia var. filifolia, commonly known as threadleaf mistletoe, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruits are small, three-lobed capsules that release tiny seeds.

It typically grows as a hemiparasitic plant, attaching itself to the branches of trees and shrubs.
