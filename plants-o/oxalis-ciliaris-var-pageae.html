Oxalis ciliaris var. pageae

Oxalis ciliaris var. pageae, commonly known as the silverleaf wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive silver or white border, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate foliage and long blooming period.
