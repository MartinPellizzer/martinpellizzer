Oenothera serrulata var. nuttallii

Oenothera serrulata var. nuttallii, commonly known as Nuttall's evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, serrated leaves and produces showy yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening. The plant thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its flowering period typically occurs in late summer to early autumn, and it is known for its ability to attract nocturnal pollinators like moths.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
