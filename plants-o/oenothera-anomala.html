Oenothera anomala

Oenothera anomala, commonly known as the desert evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has hairy, linear leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds. It is adapted to thrive in poor, sandy soils and can withstand drought conditions.

Oenothera anomala is also known for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses involving its seeds and oils.
