Oxylobium dilatatum var. trilobum

Oxylobium dilatatum var. trilobum, commonly known as the three-lobed oxylobium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with three distinct lobes. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous and contributes to its ability to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils.

The species is often found in open woods and along streambanks, where it plays a role in local biodiversity.
