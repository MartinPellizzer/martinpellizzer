Opuntia × vaseyi

Opuntia × vaseyi, commonly known as the Mexican prickly pear, is a hybrid cactus species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems, known as cladodes, which are covered in spines and are adapted for water storage. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers, followed by edible, fleshy fruits called tunas. Opuntia × vaseyi is a succulent plant with a shallow root system, making it drought-tolerant and well-suited to arid environments.

It plays an important ecological role by providing food and shelter for various desert wildlife.
