Ophrys iricolor var. mesaritica

Ophrys iricolor var. mesaritica, commonly known as the violet orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the Balkan region.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of female insects to attract male pollinators. The plant produces striking violet-colored flowers with intricate patterns that aid in its deceptive pollination strategy. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in forested areas with rich, moist soil.
