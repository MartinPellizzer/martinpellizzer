Oxera merytifolia

Oxera merytifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf oxera, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to New Zealand.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white layer of hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of delicate, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, oval berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and forested areas.

Oxera merytifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
