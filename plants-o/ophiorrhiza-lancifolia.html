Ophiorrhiza lancifolia

Ophiorrhiza lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed marsh bean, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in wet, marshy habitats. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds.

Ophiorrhiza lancifolia is known for its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils and is often found in coastal areas.
