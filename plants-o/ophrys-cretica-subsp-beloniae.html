Ophrys cretica subsp. beloniae

Ophrys cretica subsp.

beloniae, commonly known as the Belonia bee orchid, is a specialized orchid species native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a pouch-like labellum that mimics the appearance of a female bee, aiding in its pollination strategy.

The plant typically grows in calcareous grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing a striking combination of yellow and brown hues.

This subspecies is notable for its evolutionary adaptation to mimic pollinators, enhancing its reproductive success.
