Orchis × romanallens

Orchis × romanallens, commonly known as the Roman valerian orchid, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Orchis morio and Orchis lutea.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant with a tall, unbranched stem that can reach up to 1 meter in height. The flowers are typically violet to purple in color, arranged in a dense spike, and have a distinctive lip that is often marked with darker pigments. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

This orchid is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
