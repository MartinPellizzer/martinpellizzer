Odontoglossum portmannii

Odontoglossum portmannii, commonly known as the Portmann's orchid, is a tropical orchid native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, showy flowers with a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows in high-altitude cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in its challenging environment.

Odontoglossum portmannii is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and rarity.
