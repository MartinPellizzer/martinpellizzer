Ocimum bengalense

Ocimum bengalense, commonly known as Indian basil or holy basil, is a flowering plant native to South and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and thyme. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, opposite leaves and small, tubular flowers that are typically white or violet in color. Ocimum bengalense is often cultivated for its culinary and medicinal properties.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is commonly used in traditional medicine for its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects.
