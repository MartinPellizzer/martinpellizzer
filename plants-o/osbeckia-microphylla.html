Osbeckia microphylla

Osbeckia microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved osbeckia, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in the spring. Osbeckia microphylla thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
