Orchis morio f. alba

Orchis morio f. alba, commonly known as the white fairy orchid, is a rare and striking variant of the common green-winged orchid.

It is characterized by its pale white flowers, which stand out against the typical greenish-yellow of the species. This orchid is native to parts of Europe and thrives in woodland habitats with moist, well-drained soil. Its flowers are typically arranged in a spike and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant is known for its delicate appearance and is often considered a symbol of rarity and beauty in botanical gardens.
