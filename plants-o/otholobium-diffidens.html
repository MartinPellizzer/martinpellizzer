Otholobium diffidens

Otholobium diffidens, commonly known as the narrow-leaf burr medic, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by distinctive, spiny pods that contain several seeds. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.

Otholobium diffidens is also valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
