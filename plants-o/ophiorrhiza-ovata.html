Ophiorrhiza ovata

Ophiorrhiza ovata, commonly known as the white sand tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth, waxy surface. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by small, round fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas or disturbed habitats.

Ophiorrhiza ovata is known for its ability to tolerate saline conditions, making it an important species in coastal ecosystems.
