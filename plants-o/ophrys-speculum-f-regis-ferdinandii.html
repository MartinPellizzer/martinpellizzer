Ophrys speculum f. regis-ferdinandii

Ophrys speculum f. regis-ferdinandii, commonly known as the royal Ferdinand's mirror orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its intricate, bee-mimicking flowers that feature a distinctive mirror-like reflective pattern on the lower lip. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky soils, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing a unique blend of colors that mimic the appearance of a female bee.

This orchid is highly valued for its ornamental beauty and its role in pollination dynamics within its ecosystem.
