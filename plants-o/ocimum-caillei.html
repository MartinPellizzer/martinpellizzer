Ocimum caillei

Ocimum caillei, commonly known as the Caille's basil, is a species of flowering plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a fragrant, menthol-like scent.

Ocimum caillei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its essential oils.
