Onoseris albicans

Onoseris albicans, commonly known as the whitehead dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and early summer. Its leaves are basal, spoon-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while the stem is usually hairy and erect.

Onoseris albicans is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats, and it has medicinal uses in traditional herbal remedies.
