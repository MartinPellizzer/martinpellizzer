Orchis purpurea f. obtusiloba

Orchis purpurea f. obtusiloba, commonly known as the blunt-lobed purple orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to parts of Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged along the stem, while the flowers are clustered in a spike with a distinctive purple hue.

This orchid is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its beauty and ecological significance.
