Oxalis violacea subsp. latifolia

Oxalis violacea subsp.

latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States. It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped lobes and produces clusters of small, violet or pinkish flowers.

The plant has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread and form dense mats. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
