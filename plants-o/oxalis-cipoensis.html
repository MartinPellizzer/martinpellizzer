Oxalis cipoensis

Oxalis cipoensis, commonly known as the Cipo oxalis, is a perennial flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, five-petaled flowers. The plant produces bright yellow or orange flowers, typically in spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Oxalis cipoensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to add color to shaded garden areas.
