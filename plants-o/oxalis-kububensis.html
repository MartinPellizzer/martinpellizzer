Oxalis kububensis

Oxalis kububensis, commonly known as the Kubub oxalis, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Oxalidaceae and is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, colorful flowers. The plant produces yellowish or orange flowers that bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, and it prefers well-drained soil and partial shade for optimal growth.

Oxalis kububensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
