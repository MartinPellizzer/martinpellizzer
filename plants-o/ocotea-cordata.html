Ocotea cordata

Ocotea cordata, commonly known as the fragrant ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, dark berries that contain seeds.

Ocotea cordata is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
