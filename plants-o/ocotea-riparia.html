Ocotea riparia

Ocotea riparia, commonly known as the river ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurel and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, fragrant flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments along riverbanks and in humid forests.

Ocotea riparia is valued for its timber and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
