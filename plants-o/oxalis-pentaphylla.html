Oxalis pentaphylla

Oxalis pentaphylla, commonly known as the five-leafed wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically heart-shaped and have a bright green color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or woodland settings.

Oxalis pentaphylla is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
