Ocotea cujumary

Ocotea cujumary, commonly known as the Brazilian camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant produces small, white flowers and elliptical, dark green leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its wood is valued for its fragrant properties and is used in the production of essential oils and perfumes.

Ocotea cujumary thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.
