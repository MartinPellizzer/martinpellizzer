Oscularia cremnophila

Oscularia cremnophila, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental value.

Oscularia cremnophila is also known for its ability to store water in its leaves, making it drought-tolerant.
