Ouratea dorata

Ouratea dorata, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like bees and birds. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it can grow up to 10 meters in height under favorable conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in warm climates for its vibrant floral display.
