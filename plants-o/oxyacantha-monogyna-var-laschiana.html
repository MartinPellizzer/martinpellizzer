Oxyacantha monogyna var. laschiana

Oxyacantha monogyna var. laschiana, commonly known as the narrowleaf scarlet gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of bright orange-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for hedging or ornamental planting.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological study.
