Odontoglossum alberti

Odontoglossum alberti, commonly known as Albert's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple and white. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern around the pseudobulbs.

Odontoglossum alberti prefers cool, humid environments and is often found in cloud forests at high altitudes.
