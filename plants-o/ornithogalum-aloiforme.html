Ornithogalum aloiforme

Ornithogalum aloiforme, commonly known as the star of Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers during the summer months. The plant is characterized by its fibrous root system and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
