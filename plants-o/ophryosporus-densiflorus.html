Ophryosporus densiflorus

Ophryosporus densiflorus, commonly known as the denseflower orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit. The plant produces dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
