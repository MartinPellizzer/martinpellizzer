Oxytropis farsi

Oxytropis farsi, commonly known as Persian locoweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces yellow or reddish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with small, rounded leaflets, and it thrives in dry, rocky habitats.

Oxytropis farsi is also known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock.
