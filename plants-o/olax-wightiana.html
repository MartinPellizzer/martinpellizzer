Olax wightiana

Olax wightiana, commonly known as the Wight's olax, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Olacaceae and is characterized by its woody structure and showy, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, clustered inflorescences with white or pale yellow petals. Its leaves are usually simple, alternate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Olax wightiana is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
