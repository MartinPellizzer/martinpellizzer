Ocotea pedunculata

Ocotea pedunculata, commonly known as the yellowwood or white wood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark-colored wood that is highly valued for its durability and scent. The plant typically grows in humid, lowland forests and can reach heights of up to 30 meters. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a smooth surface with a fragrant aroma when crushed.

The flowers are small and white, clustered in spikes, and the fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.
