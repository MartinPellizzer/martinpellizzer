Opuntia clavarioides var. ruiz-lealii

Opuntia clavarioides var. ruiz-lealii, commonly known as the Mexican prickly pear, is a cacti species native to Mexico.

It features a tall, columnar stem with a green, ribbed appearance and spiny areoles. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tunas, is edible and has a sweet, succulent texture.

This variety is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy and drought-resistant plant.
