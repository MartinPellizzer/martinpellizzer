Oedera corymbosa

Oedera corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered milkweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, which are arranged in corymbs, giving it its scientific name. It is a member of the Apocynaceae family, known for its milky sap and often toxic compounds.

Oedera corymbosa is typically found in open woodland or along stream banks, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
