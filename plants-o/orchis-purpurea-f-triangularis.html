Orchis purpurea f. triangularis

Orchis purpurea f. triangularis, commonly known as the triangular orchid, is a fascinating orchid species characterized by its distinctive leaf shape.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of Europe, including the British Isles. The plant typically grows in woodland and grassland habitats, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually purple or pink, with a unique triangular lip that sets it apart from other orchid varieties.

This rare form is often found in shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
