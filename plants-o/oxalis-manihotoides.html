Oxalis manihotoides

Oxalis manihotoides, commonly known as the yellow wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Oxalis manihotoides is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
