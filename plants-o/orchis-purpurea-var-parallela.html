Orchis purpurea var. parallela

Orchis purpurea var. parallela, commonly known as the parallel orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features a tall, erect stem with a single spike of showy, violet to purple flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged along the stem, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The orchid's flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, and it is valued for its ecological and horticultural significance.
