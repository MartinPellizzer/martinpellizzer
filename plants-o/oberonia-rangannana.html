Oberonia rangannana

Oberonia rangannana, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red and green inflorescence, which consists of numerous small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to the bark of trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
