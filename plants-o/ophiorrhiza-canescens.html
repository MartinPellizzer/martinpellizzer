Ophiorrhiza canescens

Ophiorrhiza canescens, commonly known as the silver hair root or white root, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its hairy, grayish stems and small, opposite leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow and are arranged in clusters.

Ophiorrhiza canescens is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
