Orchis palczewskii

Orchis palczewskii, commonly known as the Palczewski's orchid, is a rare and endangered species native to specific regions of Europe.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family, characterized by its showy, often colorful flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers have three sepals and three petals, with the latter often being more pronounced.

The species is notable for its specialized pollination mechanisms, relying on specific insect pollinators for reproduction.
