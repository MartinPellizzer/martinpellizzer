Oligosporus halodendron

Oligosporus halodendron, commonly known as the saltbush, is a halophytic shrub adapted to saline environments.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and yellow flowers. The plant thrives in coastal areas and arid regions with high salt content in the soil. Its succulent stems store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry conditions.

Oligosporus halodendron plays a vital role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various desert organisms.
