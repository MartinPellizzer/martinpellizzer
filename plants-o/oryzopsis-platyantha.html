Oryzopsis platyantha

Oryzopsis platyantha, commonly known as the slender ricegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spikelet-like inflorescences that produce both male and female flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and have a rough texture, while its seeds are small and wind-dispersed.

Oryzopsis platyantha plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
