Oryza meyeriana subsp. tuberculata

Oryza meyeriana subsp.

tuberculata, commonly known as the tuberculate ricegrass, is a perennial grass native to China. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential agricultural applications.
