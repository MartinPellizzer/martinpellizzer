Odontophyllum superbum

Odontophyllum superbum, commonly known as the giant flame bamboo, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in height, forming dense clumps. The plant produces long, narrow leaves with a glossy, dark green surface and bright red or orange veins, adding vibrant color to its habitat. Its flowers, which are small and clustered, are often hidden within the foliage, making them less noticeable.

Odontophyllum superbum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid environments.
