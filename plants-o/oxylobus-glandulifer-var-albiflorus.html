Oxylobus glandulifer var. albiflorus

Oxylobus glandulifer var. albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered oxylobus, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glandular leaves and showy white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are often hairy and have a distinct, aromatic scent.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic features.
