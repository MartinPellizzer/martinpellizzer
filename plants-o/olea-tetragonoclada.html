Olea tetragonoclada

Olea tetragonoclada, commonly known as the quadrilateral olive, is a species of flowering plant in the Oleaceae family.

It is native to the Canary Islands and is characterized by its quadrilateral-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, woody drupe that contains a single seed.

Olea tetragonoclada is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained, sandy soils.
