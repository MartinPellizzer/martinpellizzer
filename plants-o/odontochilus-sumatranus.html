Odontochilus sumatranus

Odontochilus sumatranus, commonly known as the Sumatran toothed orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, tooth-like structures on its petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small and fragrant, with a distinctive coloration that ranges from white to pale yellow.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered an endangered species, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
