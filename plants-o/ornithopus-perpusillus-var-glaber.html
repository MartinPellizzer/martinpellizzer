Ornithopus perpusillus var. glaber

Ornithopus perpusillus var. glaber, commonly known as the glabrous dwarf larkspur, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear small, blue to purple flowers clustered in racemes. The leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs, though the 'glaber' variety is hairless. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open meadows and rocky habitats.

This plant is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy, often toxic flowers.
