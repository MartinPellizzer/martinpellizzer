Oxalis bipartita f. bulbifera

Oxalis bipartita f. bulbifera, commonly known as the bulbous wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of yellow or orange. The plant is characterized by its unique ability to form bulb-like structures, which aid in its propagation and survival. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
