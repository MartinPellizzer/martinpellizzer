Oncidium palilionaceum

Oncidium palilionaceum, commonly known as the pink lady's slipper orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, pinkish-white flowers with a distinctive slipper-shaped pouch. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Oncidium palilionaceum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
