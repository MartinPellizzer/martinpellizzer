Opuntia guanicana

Opuntia guanicana, commonly known as the Brazilian prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems that are covered in spines and glochids, which are small, hair-like structures that can cause irritation. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by fruit that is typically red or orange in color. Opuntia guanicana is adapted to arid environments and has a shallow root system that allows it to thrive in dry, sandy soils.

It is also known for its ability to store water in its thick, fleshy stems, making it a resilient plant in harsh climates.
