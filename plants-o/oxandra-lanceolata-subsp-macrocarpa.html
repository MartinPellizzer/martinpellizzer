Oxandra lanceolata subsp. macrocarpa

Oxandra lanceolata subsp.

macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited oxandra, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America. It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers.

The plant's most distinctive feature is its large, woody fruits, which contain several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests. Oxandra lanceolata subsp.

macrocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
