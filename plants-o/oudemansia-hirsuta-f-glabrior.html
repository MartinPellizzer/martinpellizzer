Oudemansia hirsuta f. glabrior

Oudemansia hirsuta f. glabrior, commonly known as the glabrous hirsuta, is a variant of the hairy hirsuta species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and leaves, distinguishing it from the typical hairy form. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodlands or along stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered, often with a pale green or white coloration.

This form is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
