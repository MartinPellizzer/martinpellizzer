Ononis Repens Subsp. Arvensis

Ononis repens subsp. arvensis is a low-growing perennial plant commonly found in disturbed soils and agricultural fields. It has thin, creeping stems that root at the nodes and form dense mats. The plant produces small white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. This subspecies is often considered a weed but plays a role in soil stabilization and ecological succession.
