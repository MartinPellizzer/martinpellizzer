Ononis arvensis var. intermedia

Ononis arvensis var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate field catchfly, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is a low-growing herbaceous perennial that typically thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

This variant is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides, where it can form dense mats.
