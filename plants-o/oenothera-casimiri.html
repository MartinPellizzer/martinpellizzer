Oenothera casimiri

Oenothera casimiri, commonly known as the Casimir's evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.
