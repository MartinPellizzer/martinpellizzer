Odontocarya convolvulacea

Odontocarya convolvulacea, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its slender, twisting stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Odontocarya convolvulacea is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered a weed in cultivated areas.
