Oxalis riparia

Oxalis riparia, commonly known as the river oxalis, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically bright green with a reddish or purple tint on the underside. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are usually yellow or orange, often with five petals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in wet meadows.

Oxalis riparia is a perennial herb that spreads through rhizomes, making it a common sight in riparian zones.
