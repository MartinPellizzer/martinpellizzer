Oxytropis campestris var. jordalii

Oxytropis campestris var. jordalii, commonly known as the Jordan's field vetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it ecologically valuable in soil enrichment.
