Opuntia stenochila

Opuntia stenochila, commonly known as the narrow-leafed prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems that are typically green and covered in sharp spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tunaf, is edible and contains a sweet, juicy pulp.

Opuntia stenochila is well adapted to arid environments and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife.
