Odontoglossum retusum

Odontoglossum retusum, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and dark green, often with a waxy texture.

Odontoglossum retusum is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts worldwide.
