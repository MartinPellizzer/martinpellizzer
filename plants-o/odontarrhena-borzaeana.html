Odontarrhena borzaeana

Odontarrhena borzaeana, commonly known as the Borzana grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, grassy habitats and is adapted to arid or semi-arid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, while its roots are fibrous and extensive.

Odontarrhena borzaeana plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for livestock in its native range.
