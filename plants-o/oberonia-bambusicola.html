Oberonia bambusicola

Oberonia bambusicola, commonly known as the bamboo orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, often purple or violet-colored flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in the undergrowth of tropical forests. Its leaves are usually long and narrow, adapted to capture light in low-light conditions.

Oberonia bambusicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
