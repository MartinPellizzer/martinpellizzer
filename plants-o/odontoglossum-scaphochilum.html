Odontoglossum scaphochilum

Odontoglossum scaphochilum, commonly known as the violet orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a sympodial growth habit, characterized by its pseudobulbs and strap-like leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers with a distinct lip, typical of the Orchidaceae family. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its beauty and unique floral structure.
