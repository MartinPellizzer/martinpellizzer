Ononis paniculata

Ononis paniculata, commonly known as field scabious, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features slender, ascending stems and produces clusters of small, pink or purple flowers arranged in paniculate inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils.

Ononis paniculata is also valued for its role in supporting pollinators and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
