Ophrys × sancticyrensis

Ophrys × sancticyrensis, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a rare and fascinating plant that results from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is characterized by its striking, bee-like flowers that feature a yellow face with a black beard, mimicking the appearance of a bee to attract pollinators. The plant is a perennial herb with a rhizomatous root system and typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers bloom in early spring, producing a unique combination of color and scent to mimic a female bee.

This botanical mimicry is a key adaptation that enhances its reproductive success through specialized pollination by male bees.
