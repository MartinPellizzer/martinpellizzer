Oldenlandia retrorsa

Oldenlandia retrorsa, commonly known as the backward-facing spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in clusters, often in shades of white, yellow, or red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and a creeping or ascending growth habit, making it adaptable to various soil types. It is often found in moist environments such as stream banks and forest floors.

Oldenlandia retrorsa is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
