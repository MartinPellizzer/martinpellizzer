Oxydectes linearis

Oxydectes linearis, commonly known as the slender marsh orchid, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a dense spike, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Oxydectes linearis is typically found in moist soils along the edges of ponds, lakes, and wet meadows.
