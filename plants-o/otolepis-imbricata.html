Otolepis imbricata

Otolepis imbricata, commonly known as the rock cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are usually narrow and arranged alternately along the stems.

Otolepis imbricata is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing a range of morphological features that aid in water conservation.
