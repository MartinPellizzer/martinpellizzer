Olea parvilimba

Olea parvilimba, commonly known as the small-leaved olive, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by small, round fruits containing a single seed. Olea parvilimba is often found in dry woodlands and savannas, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
