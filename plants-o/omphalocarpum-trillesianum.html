Omphalocarpum trillesianum

Omphalocarpum trillesianum, commonly known as the mountain dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dandelion-like appearance with yellow flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, hard-coated seeds that are dispersed by wind, a common adaptation in its native habitat. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
