Oncidium posadarum

Oncidium posadarum, commonly known as the Posada's orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Oncidium posadarum thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

It is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its vibrant blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
