Ononis thomsonii var. lineata

Ononis thomsonii var. lineata, commonly known as Thomson's ononis, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy, and it produces small, yellowish-white flowers in clusters. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

The varietal form lineata is distinguished by its distinctive leaf patterns and slightly different growth habits compared to the typical form.
