Orchis latifolia var. longibracteata

Orchis latifolia var. longibracteata, commonly known as the broad-leaved orchid with long bracts, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and striking purple or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its flowers are adorned with long, slender bracts that give it its varietal name.

This orchid is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments and is a notable species in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
