Oxytropis ganningensis

Oxytropis ganningensis, commonly known as Ganning locoweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, spiny stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It is adapted to harsh environments, with deep root systems that allow it to survive in poor soils and low moisture conditions.

Oxytropis ganningensis is also known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock.
