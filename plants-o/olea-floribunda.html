Olea floribunda

Olea floribunda, commonly known as the flowering olive, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, edible fruits. Olea floribunda is well adapted to dry climates and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
