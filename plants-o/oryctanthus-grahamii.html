Oryctanthus grahamii

Oryctanthus grahamii, commonly known as the Graham's oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a red or purple throat. The plant produces small, heart-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Oryctanthus grahamii is also valued for its ornamental beauty and drought tolerance.
