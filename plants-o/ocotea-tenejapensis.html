Ocotea tenejapensis

Ocotea tenejapensis, commonly known as Mexican laurel, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to Central America and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows as a tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces dark, oily berries that are an important food source for various wildlife.

Ocotea tenejapensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local cultures.
