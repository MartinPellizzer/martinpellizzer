Omphalodes Lusitanica

Omphalodes lusitanica is a lesser-known yet fascinating flowering plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the bellflower family, Campanulaceae, and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. This plant is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of blue and purple. Despite its modest appearance, it plays an important role in local ecosystems. Its resilience and adaptability make it a valuable subject for botanical study.
