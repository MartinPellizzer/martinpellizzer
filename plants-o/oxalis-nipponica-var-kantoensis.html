Oxalis nipponica var. kantoensis

Oxalis nipponica var. kantoensis, commonly known as the Kanto oxalis, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring and produces distinctive, bead-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a suitable ornamental plant for shaded garden environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for horticultural collections and conservation efforts.
