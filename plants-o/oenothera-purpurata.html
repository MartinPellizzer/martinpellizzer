Oenothera purpurata

Oenothera purpurata, commonly known as the showy evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the United States.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to deep red, often opening in the evening. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as prairies and disturbed areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Oenothera purpurata is known for its ability to thrive in full sun and its resilience to drought conditions.
