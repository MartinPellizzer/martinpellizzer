Oyedaea rusbyi

Oyedaea rusbyi, commonly known as the Rusby's ghost orchid, is a rare and elusive orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, delicate appearance, featuring pale greenish-white flowers with intricate patterns resembling ghostly figures. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in the understory of rainforests. Its roots are specialized for epiphytic growth, allowing it to attach to tree trunks without being parasitic.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Oyedaea rusbyi is considered a conservation concern.
