Orchis angustifolia f. densiuscula

Orchis angustifolia f. densiuscula, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid in a dense form, is a botanical variant of the narrow-leaved orchid.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, pale green to yellowish flowers. This orchid typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. The flowers are arranged in a dense spike, giving it its specific epithet "densiuscula," meaning "somewhat dense.".

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is often found in temperate regions of Europe.
