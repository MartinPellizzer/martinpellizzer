Oxylobium lineare

Oxylobium lineare, commonly known as the narrowleaf oxylobium, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its slender, often arching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pale yellow to greenish-yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Oxylobium lineare is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
