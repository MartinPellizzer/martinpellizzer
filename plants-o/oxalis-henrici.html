Oxalis henrici

Oxalis henrici, commonly known as the Henry's wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive veins, and its flowers are typically pink or white, arranged in clusters. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in woodland habitats and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Oxalis henrici is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, enriching the soil in which it grows.
