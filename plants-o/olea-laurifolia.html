Olea laurifolia

Olea laurifolia, commonly known as the laurel leaf olive, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green, leathery leaves that are oval to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or yellow, followed by small, fleshy fruits. Olea laurifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
