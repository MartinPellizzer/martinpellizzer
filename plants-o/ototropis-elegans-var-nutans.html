Ototropis elegans var. nutans

Ototropis elegans var. nutans, commonly known as the nodding elegant sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, triangular leaves that are typically arranged in three rows. The plant produces delicate, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikelets, with the inflorescence often bending downward, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting wetland biodiversity.
