Oberonia fitzgeraldiana

Oberonia fitzgeraldiana, commonly known as the Fitzgerald's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil. Its unique botanical features include intricate floral structures and specialized root systems adapted for nutrient absorption in low-light conditions.

Oberonia fitzgeraldiana is often found in primary forest habitats and is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
