Oenothera amoena f. vinosa

Oenothera amoena f. vinosa, commonly known as the pink evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often growing in dry, open habitats.

The species is part of the evening primrose family (Onagraceae) and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
