Oncidium silvanoi

Oncidium silvanoi, commonly known as the Silvan's orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, showy inflorescences that bear clusters of fragrant, colorful flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Oncidium silvanoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its long-lasting blooms.
