Orbignya racemosa

Orbignya racemosa, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a large evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and can grow up to 40 meters tall. The tree produces large, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by woody pods containing the edible seeds known as Brazil nuts. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately compound, with long, narrow leaflets.

The plant plays a vital role in the ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
