Ozothamnus cupressoides

Ozothamnus cupressoides, commonly known as the coastal daisy bush, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage that resembles small pine needles, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Its aromatic leaves and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
