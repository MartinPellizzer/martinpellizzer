Ophrys bicornis var. cornuta

Ophrys bicornis var. cornuta, commonly known as the horned bee orchid, is a striking orchid species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its intricate floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee, aiding in its pollination strategy. The plant features a single, showy flower with a distinctive horn-like projection on the lip, which is a key feature of the varietal name "cornuta.". Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically growing in dense clusters.

This orchid thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.
