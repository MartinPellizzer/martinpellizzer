Orchis mascula subsp. acutiflora

Orchis mascula subsp.

acutiflora, commonly known as the sharp-flowered male orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is a terrestrial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

The plant produces tall, slender stems with a single, showy violet or purple flower that has a distinctive spurred lip. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

The species is notable for its specialized pollination mechanism, relying on specific insects for reproduction.
