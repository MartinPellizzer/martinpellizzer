Oxalis namaquana

Oxalis namaquana, commonly known as the Namakwa oxalis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it adapts well to dry and nutrient-poor conditions.

Oxalis namaquana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
