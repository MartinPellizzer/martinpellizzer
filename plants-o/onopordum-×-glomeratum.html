Onopordum × glomeratum

Onopordum × glomeratum, commonly known as the everlasting or Spanish thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its robust, spiny stems and large, silvery-green, lobed leaves. The plant produces showy, purple to pink composite flower heads that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its flowers are composed of both ray and disk florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Onopordum × glomeratum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
