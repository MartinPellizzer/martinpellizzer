Oxalis oligophylla

Oxalis oligophylla, commonly known as the small-leaf wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each leaflet being small and heart-shaped, and its clusters of delicate, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is a perennial herb that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.

Oxalis oligophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
