Odontorrhynchus erosus

Odontorrhynchus erosus, commonly known as the rough-horned orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy, and often reddish-brown pseudobulbs. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange with dark markings. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a unique and sought-after specimen among orchid enthusiasts.
