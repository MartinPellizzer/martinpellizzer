Ornithogalum filibracteatum

Ornithogalum filibracteatum, commonly known as the narrow-bracted star-of-Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a bulbous perennial that produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers during the summer months. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves that are often mistaken for grasses or sedges. Its bulbs are used in traditional medicine and as a source of food in some regions.

The species is characterized by its unique floral structure and adaptation to arid environments.
