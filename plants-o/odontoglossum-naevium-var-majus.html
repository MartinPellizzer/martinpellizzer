Odontoglossum naevium var. majus

Odontoglossum naevium var. majus, commonly known as the showy orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking, showy flowers with a distinctive violet or purple hue. The plant features a pseudobulb structure, which stores water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in its natural habitat. Its flowers are typically borne on lateral shoots and are characterized by their intricate patterns and vibrant colors.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its beauty.
