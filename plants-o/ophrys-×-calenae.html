Ophrys × calenae

Ophrys × calenae, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant that results from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is characterized by its unique floral morphology, with petals that mimic the appearance of female bees to attract male pollinators. This mimicry is a key feature in its reproductive strategy, enhancing its chances of successful pollination. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or grassland habitats across Europe.

Its ornamental value and ecological significance make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
