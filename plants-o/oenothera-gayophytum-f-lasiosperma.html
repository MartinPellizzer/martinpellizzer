Oenothera gayophytum f. lasiosperma

Oenothera gayophytum f. lasiosperma, commonly known as the silky evening primrose, is a botanical variant of the evening primrose species.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It is native to the southwestern United States and is often found in dry, open habitats.

This form is notable for its unique floral structure and adaptability to arid environments.
