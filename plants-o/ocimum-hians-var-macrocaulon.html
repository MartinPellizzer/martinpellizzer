Ocimum hians var. macrocaulon

Ocimum hians var. macrocaulon, commonly known as the giant holy basil, is a variant of the holy basil plant characterized by its elongated, robust stems and large, lance-shaped leaves.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, sharing the aromatic properties typical of other members of this family. The plant is native to tropical regions of Asia and is often cultivated for its fragrant leaves used in culinary and medicinal applications. Its unique morphology, including its extended stem and larger foliage, distinguishes it from the standard Ocimum hians.

This variety is valued for its strong scent and adaptability to various growing conditions.
