Ornithogalum juttae

Ornithogalum juttae, commonly known as the Jutta's star-of-Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a bulbous perennial that produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers during the summer months. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves that emerge from a central bulb. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
