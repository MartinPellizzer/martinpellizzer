Origanum cinereum

Origanum cinereum, commonly known as blue germander, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has aromatic, narrow leaves that are often used in herbal teas and culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Its medicinal properties have been historically valued for their antiseptic and digestive benefits.
