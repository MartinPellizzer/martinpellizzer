Oxalis cespitosa

Oxalis cespitosa, commonly known as the wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which resemble those of clover, and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, round, green berries that contain seeds.

Oxalis cespitosa is a perennial herb that thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
