Odontoglossum pusillum

Odontoglossum pusillum, commonly known as the miniature orchid, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, hairy leaves and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in cool, humid environments with well-drained soil.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
