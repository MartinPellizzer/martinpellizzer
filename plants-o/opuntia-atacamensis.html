Opuntia atacamensis

Opuntia atacamensis, commonly known as the Atacama prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in sharp spines and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

Opuntia atacamensis plays a significant role in the local ecosystem and has been used by indigenous communities for food and traditional medicine.
