Oxalis atroglandulosa

Oxalis atroglandulosa, commonly known as the black gland oxalis, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant features prominent black glands on its stems and leaves, which are a distinguishing trait. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Oxalis atroglandulosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique foliage.
