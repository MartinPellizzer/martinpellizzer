Orchis hirtella

Orchis hirtella, commonly known as the hairy orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with fine hairs, giving it its common name.

Orchis hirtella plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
