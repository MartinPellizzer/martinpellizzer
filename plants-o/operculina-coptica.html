Operculina coptica

Operculina coptica, commonly known as the African buttercup, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive white center and a hairy, spiny seed pod. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy coating.

Operculina coptica is also known for its ability to produce a milky sap that can cause skin irritation.
