Orchis militaris var. lactea

Orchis militaris var. lactea, commonly known as the milk orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while the inflorescence consists of a single spike bearing several flowers.

The species is noted for its unique floral structure and is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
