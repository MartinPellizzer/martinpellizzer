Orchis romana f. obtusifolia

Orchis romana f. obtusifolia, commonly known as the Roman orchid or the broad-leaved orchid, is a botanical variant of the common orchid.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring. The plant features broad, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and is often found in meadows and grasslands. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

The flowers are usually a mix of white, green, and purple, creating a striking visual display.
