Orchis × pseudoanatolica

Orchis × pseudoanatolica, commonly known as the Anatolian orchid, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Orchis moria and Orchis pallida.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing a spike of 5 to 15 flowers. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a range of morphological variations, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
