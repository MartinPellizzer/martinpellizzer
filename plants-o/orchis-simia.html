Orchis simia

Orchis simia, commonly known as the monkey orchid, is a species of orchid native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers with a distinctive yellow and green pattern resembling a monkey's face. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in nutrient-rich soils. Orchis simia has a complex root system and produces a single, erect stem with several flowers at the top.

It is a perennial herb that blooms from late spring to early summer.
