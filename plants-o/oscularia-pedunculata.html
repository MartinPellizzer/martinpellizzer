Oscularia pedunculata

Oscularia pedunculata, commonly known as the clustered ice plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a succulent species characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its ability to store water in its leaves allows it to survive in arid environments.

Oscularia pedunculata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
