Onosma taurica subsp. cinerascens

Onosma taurica subsp.

cinerascens, commonly known as the ash-gray onosma, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and produces small, pinkish-white flowers in clusters.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showing a preference for well-drained soils. Its subspecific name, *cinerascens*, refers to its ashen or grayish appearance.

It is often found in mountainous areas, where it contributes to the local flora's biodiversity.
