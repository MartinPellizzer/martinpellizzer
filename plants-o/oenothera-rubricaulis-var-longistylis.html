Oenothera rubricaulis var. longistylis

Oenothera rubricaulis var. longistylis, commonly known as the long-styled evening primrose, is a species native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, orange-yellow flowers that open in the evening and close by morning. The plant has slender, branching stems and hairy leaves that are typically lance-shaped. Its flowers have a unique structure with long, slender stigmas, which is a distinguishing feature of the varietal name.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
