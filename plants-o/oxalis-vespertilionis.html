Oxalis vespertilionis

Oxalis vespertilionis, commonly known as the bat oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, delicate flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing bright yellow or orange flowers. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens or containers.

Due to its unique appearance and blooming habit, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
