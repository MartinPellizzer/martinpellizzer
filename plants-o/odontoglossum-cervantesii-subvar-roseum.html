Odontoglossum cervantesii subvar. roseum

Odontoglossum cervantesii subvar. roseum, commonly known as the rose-flowered toothed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the high-altitude cloud forests of Central America, particularly in Mexico and Guatemala. The plant features a compact, pseudobulbous structure with showy, rose-pink to lavender-colored flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are dark green, strap-like, and arranged in a rosette pattern around the base.

This subvariety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its striking floral display.
