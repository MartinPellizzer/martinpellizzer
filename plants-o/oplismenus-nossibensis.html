Oplismenus nossibensis

Oplismenus nossibensis, commonly known as the Florida wiregrass, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikelets. The plant thrives in wetland environments and is often found in swampy areas with consistently moist soil. Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion in its native habitat.

Oplismenus nossibensis plays a role in supporting local wildlife, including insects and small vertebrates.
