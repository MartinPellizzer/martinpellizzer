Oxalis canaliculata var. trifoliolata

Oxalis canaliculata var. trifoliolata, commonly known as the three-leaf wood sorrel, is a herbaceous plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, often followed by red or orange seed pods. Its leaves are heart-shaped with three leaflets, and it produces calcium oxalate crystals, which can cause irritation if ingested.

Oxalis canaliculata var. trifoliolata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and delicate flowers.
