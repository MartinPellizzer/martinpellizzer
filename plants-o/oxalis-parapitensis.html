Oxalis parapitensis

Oxalis parapitensis, commonly known as the rock oxalis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive red veins and small, five-petaled yellow flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. It is adapted to full sun and has a compact growth habit, making it suitable for rock gardens.

Oxalis parapitensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
