Oenocarpus minor subsp. intermedius

Oenocarpus minor subsp.

intermedius, commonly known as the red sago palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnately compound leaves that are often used in traditional crafts.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by large, edible fruits that contain a starchy, edible kernel. Its roots are also utilized in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

Due to its ornamental value and utility, it is cultivated in various regions for both practical and decorative purposes.
