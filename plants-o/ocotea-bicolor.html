Ocotea bicolor

Ocotea bicolor, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and fragrant, reddish-brown heartwood. The plant typically grows in humid lowland forests and can reach heights of up to 30 meters. Its bark is smooth and often exudes a pleasant scent, while its flowers are small and clustered.

The species is valued for its durable, aromatic wood, which has been traditionally used in furniture and carving.
