Oryza sativa var. bandica

Oryza sativa var. bandica, commonly known as the Bandica rice.

It is a variety of rice native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its unique grain morphology and aroma. The plant typically grows in aquatic or semi-aquatic environments, requiring flooded conditions for optimal growth. Its botanical features include long, slender leaves and a panicle inflorescence that bears small, fragrant flowers.

This variety is valued for its culinary properties and has been cultivated for centuries in traditional rice-growing regions.
