Oldenlandia schlechteri

Oldenlandia schlechteri, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Schlechter's spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet to blue flowers, which bloom in clusters during the wet season. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
