Odontosoria spathulata

Odontosoria spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leaf huperzia, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its evergreen, leathery fronds that are typically broad and lance-shaped. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including China, India, and Southeast Asia. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Odontosoria spathulata thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found growing on rocks or in forest understories.
