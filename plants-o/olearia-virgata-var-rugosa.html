Olearia virgata var. rugosa

Olearia virgata var. rugosa, commonly known as the rough-haired daisy, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and showy white or pale yellow flower heads. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is well adapted to maritime climates. It produces small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Olearia virgata var. rugosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
