Oxalis tenuiloba

Oxalis tenuiloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive white veins and produces small, five-petaled yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is a perennial herb that thrives in partial to full shade and is often found in the understory of forests.

Oxalis tenuiloba is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and blooms.
