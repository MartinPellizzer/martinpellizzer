Onobrychis aequidentata f. dalmatica

Onobrychis aequidentata f. dalmatica, commonly known as the Dalmatian sainfoin, is a perennial leguminous plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is native to the Balkan Peninsula, particularly found in Serbia and Bosnia. The plant has small, hairy pods that contain several seeds, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

It is often used in agricultural settings for its nitrogen-fixing ability and as a forage crop.
