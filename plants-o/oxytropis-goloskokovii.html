Oxytropis goloskokovii

Oxytropis goloskokovii, commonly known as the Goloskokov's locoweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, particularly in Kazakhstan and southern Russia. The plant is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in steppe and desert environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems.
