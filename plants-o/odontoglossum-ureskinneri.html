Odontoglossum ureskinneri

Odontoglossum ureskinneri, commonly known as the Ureskinner's orchid, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a compact growth habit, featuring a pseudobulb that stores water and nutrients. The plant produces intricate, showy flowers with a distinctive coloration, often in shades of white, pink, and violet. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to the cool, humid conditions of its natural habitat.

Odontoglossum ureskinneri is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
