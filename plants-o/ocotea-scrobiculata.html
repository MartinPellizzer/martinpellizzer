Ocotea scrobiculata

Ocotea scrobiculata, commonly known as the Brazilian camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and fragrant bark. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by oval, dark blue berries. Its essential oils are often extracted for use in perfumes and traditional medicine.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood.
