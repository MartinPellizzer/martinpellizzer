Ouratea guianensis

Ouratea guianensis, commonly known as the Guianan ouratea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by woody, elongated fruits containing several seeds. It is often found in lowland and hill forest habitats, growing up to 20 meters tall.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
