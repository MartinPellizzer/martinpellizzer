Origanum vulgare subsp. glandulosum

Origanum vulgare subsp.

glandulosum, commonly known as lemon thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly lemon-scented leaves and small, pink or white flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and prefers full sun. Its foliage is covered with glandular dots that contribute to its distinctive fragrance.

Lemon thyme is often used in culinary applications and traditional medicine due to its essential oils and antimicrobial properties.
