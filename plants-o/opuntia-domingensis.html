Opuntia domingensis

Opuntia domingensis, commonly known as the Dominican prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the Caribbean islands, including the Dominican Republic.

It is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems that are covered in spines and are often ribbed. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tunas, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

Opuntia domingensis is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in tropical climates.
