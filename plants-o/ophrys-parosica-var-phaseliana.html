Ophrys parosica var. phaseliana

Ophrys parosica var. phaseliana, commonly known as the Phaselian bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its intricate floral morphology, featuring a lip that mimics the appearance of a female bee, which plays a key role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, often in open, sunny habitats. Its flowers are usually pale green to brownish in color, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

This orchid is a member of the Ophrys genus, known for its unique pollination mechanisms involving sexual deception of male bees.
