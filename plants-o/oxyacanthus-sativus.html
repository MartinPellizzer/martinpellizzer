Oxyacanthus sativus

Oxyacanthus sativus, commonly known as the common holly, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, oval-shaped leaves and small, bright red berries that are often used in ornamental gardening. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade conditions. It is valued for its winter interest and can be grown as a hedge or specimen plant.

However, its berries are toxic to humans and animals, requiring caution in cultivation.
