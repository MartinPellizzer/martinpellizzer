Osyris densifolia

Osyris densifolia, commonly known as the African milk tree, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are arranged in opposite pairs along its branches. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by distinctive, fleshy, red berries. Osyris densifolia is adapted to arid environments and can store water in its thick stems.

Its unique appearance and ability to thrive in harsh conditions make it a notable species in its native habitat.
