Oxalis fritillariiformis

Oxalis fritillariiformis, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that resemble small sunflowers. The plant features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped lobes and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Oxalis fritillariiformis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
