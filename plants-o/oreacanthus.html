Oreacanthus

Oreacanthus, commonly known as the mountain crownbeard, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in high-altitude environments. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the summer. Oreacanthus species typically have lance-shaped leaves and are often found in rocky or dry habitats.
