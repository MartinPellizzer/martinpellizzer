Oncosperma cambodianum

Oncosperma cambodianum, commonly known as the Cambodian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The tree produces large, clustered inflorescences that bear yellowish-white flowers, followed by edible, fibrous fruits. Its seeds are encased in a hard shell and are often used for their nutritional value.

The plant is also valued for its timber and has cultural significance in local communities.
