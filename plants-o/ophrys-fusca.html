Ophrys fusca

Ophrys fusca, commonly known as the brown bee orchid, is a species of orchid native to Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its flowers are usually brown or reddish-brown in color, with intricate patterns that aid in attracting male bees for pollination.

Ophrys fusca is a perennial herb that produces a single spike of flowers, often with a single, showy bloom per stem.
