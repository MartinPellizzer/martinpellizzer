Oxalis semitruncata

Oxalis semitruncata, commonly known as the heartleaf wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of small, yellow, star-shaped flowers. The plant is part of the Oxalidaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.

Oxalis semitruncata is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
