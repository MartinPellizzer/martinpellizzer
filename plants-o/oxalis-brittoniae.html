Oxalis brittoniae

Oxalis brittoniae, commonly known as the Britton's wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its delicate appearance makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Oxalis brittoniae is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
