Ophrys aranifera f. hymettii

Ophrys aranifera f. hymettii, commonly known as the Hymettus bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the Hymettus mountain range in Greece.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which closely resembles a bee, a feature evolved to attract specific pollinators. The plant produces a single, showy flower with intricate patterns and a hairy, bee-like appearance. It thrives in calcareous soils and requires specific microclimatic conditions for successful growth.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
