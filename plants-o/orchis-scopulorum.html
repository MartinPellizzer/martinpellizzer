Orchis scopulorum

Orchis scopulorum, commonly known as the limestone orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 60 cm in height. The plant produces a single, showy violet-blue flower with a distinctive hooded labellum, which is a characteristic feature of many orchid species. It typically grows in calcareous soils, often in open woodlands or on rocky slopes.

Orchis scopulorum is known for its limited distribution and is considered a rare and protected species in many regions.
