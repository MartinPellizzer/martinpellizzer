Opuntia mistiensis

Opuntia mistiensis, commonly known as the Mexican prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems that are covered in spines and are often ribbed. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers, which bloom in the spring. Its fruit, known as a tuna, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

Opuntia mistiensis is well adapted to arid environments and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of food.
