Oxytropis burchan-buddae

Oxytropis burchan-buddae, commonly known as the Burchan-Budda milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in arid ecosystems.
