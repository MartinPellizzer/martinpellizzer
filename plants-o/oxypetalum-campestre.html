Oxypetalum campestre

Oxypetalum campestre, commonly known as the meadow oxypetalum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open meadows and woodland edges, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features slender stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture. Its flowers are small, white to pale pink, and arranged in clusters, blooming in early summer.

Oxypetalum campestre is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
