Orchis angustifolia var. acuminata

Orchis angustifolia var. acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features slender, erect stems that bear a single spike of pale pink to white flowers. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy texture.

The species is known for its specialized pollination mechanisms, relying on specific insect species for reproduction.
