Oenothera salicina

Oenothera salicina, commonly known as the silverleaf evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-white, fuzzy leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant belongs to the Onagraceae family and is known for its medicinal and ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and dry open areas.

The seeds of Oenothera salicina contain essential fatty acids, making it useful in both traditional and modern applications.
