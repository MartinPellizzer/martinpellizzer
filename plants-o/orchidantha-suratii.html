Orchidantha suratii

Orchidantha suratii, commonly known as the Surat orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, white to pale purple blooms that are highly ornamental. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to habitat loss, Orchidantha suratii is considered endangered and is protected under various conservation initiatives.
