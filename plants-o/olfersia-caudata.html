Olfersia caudata

Olfersia caudata, commonly known as the chocolate orchid, is a tropical plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its pendant, dark brown flowers that resemble chocolate drops. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and absorbing moisture from the air. Its leaves are long and strap-like, often covered in a waxy coating to retain moisture.

Olfersia caudata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
