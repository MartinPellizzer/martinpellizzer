Oxycoccus pusillus

Oxycoccus pusillus, commonly known as the dwarf cranberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, dark red berries. The plant thrives in cold, acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in tundra and rocky habitats. Its leaves are needle-like, and it produces flowers that are typically white or pink.

Oxycoccus pusillus is an important food source for various wildlife species in its native environment.
