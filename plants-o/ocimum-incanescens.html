Ocimum incanescens

Ocimum incanescens, commonly known as sacred basil or holy basil, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine and culinary practices. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers, and its stems are square in cross-section, a common feature among the Lamiaceae family. Ocimum incanescens thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is also valued for its essential oils, which contain compounds like eugenol and rosmarinic acid.
