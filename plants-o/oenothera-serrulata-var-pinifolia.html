Oenothera serrulata var. pinifolia

Oenothera serrulata var. pinifolia, commonly known as the Sierra nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of yellow to orange flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
