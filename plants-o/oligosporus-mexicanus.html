Oligosporus mexicanus

Oligosporus mexicanus, commonly known as the Mexican spore grass, is a unique plant species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, spore-producing structures that are adapted to thrive in harsh, dry environments. The plant exhibits a compact growth form with narrow, grass-like leaves that help reduce water loss. Its reproductive strategy relies heavily on wind pollination, which is well-suited to its native habitat.

Oligosporus mexicanus plays a minor but ecologically significant role in stabilizing soil in its native ecosystems.
