Oxalis trouiniana

Oxalis trouiniana, commonly known as the yellow wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are heart-shaped and often tinged with red or purple veins. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Oxalis trouiniana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
