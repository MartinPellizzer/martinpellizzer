Oenothera macrocarpa subsp. incana

Oenothera macrocarpa subsp.

incana, commonly known as the woolly evening primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and showy yellow-orange flowers that bloom at dusk.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native range.
