Opuntia magnarenensis

Opuntia magnarenensis, commonly known as the Mexican prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in spines and glochids, which serve as a defense mechanism. The plant produces bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit, known as a tunas, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

Opuntia magnarenensis is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in local ecosystems and traditional uses.
