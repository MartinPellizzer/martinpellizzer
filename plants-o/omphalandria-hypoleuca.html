Omphalandria hypoleuca

Omphalandria hypoleuca, commonly known as the white-flowered orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, white or pale pink flowers that bloom during the rainy season. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, and it produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a spike.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
