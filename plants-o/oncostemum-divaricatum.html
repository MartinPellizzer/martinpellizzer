Oncostemum divaricatum

Oncostemum divaricatum, commonly known as the variable stemmed daisy, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and wetlands. Its stems are often branched and can vary in growth form, which contributes to its common name.

Oncostemum divaricatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
