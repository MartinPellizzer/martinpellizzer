Oncidium cucullatum subvar. olivaceum

Oncidium cucullatum subvar. olivaceum, commonly known as the Olive Orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It features pendant, elongated pseudobulbs that support its arching, gracefully drooping inflorescences. The flowers are typically yellow to orange with dark purple or brown markings, creating a striking visual contrast. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil with consistent moisture.

This species is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its vibrant blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
