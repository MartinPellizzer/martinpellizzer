Olearia floccosa

Olearia floccosa, commonly known as the silver tree, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray, woolly leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Olearia floccosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been introduced to other parts of the world for horticultural use.
