Oxalis lotoides

Oxalis lotoides, commonly known as the golden lotus or false shamrock, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each leaflet resembling a small heart shape, and clusters of delicate, yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests or along stream banks. It produces small, round, green fruits that contain several seeds.

Oxalis lotoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and blooms.
