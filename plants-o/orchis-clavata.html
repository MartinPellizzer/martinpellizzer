Orchis clavata

Orchis clavata, commonly known as the purple marsh-orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair. Its flowers are typically arranged in a loose spike and are pollinated by various insects, including bees and wasps.

Orchis clavata plays an important ecological role in its habitat by supporting biodiversity and contributing to the wetland ecosystem.
