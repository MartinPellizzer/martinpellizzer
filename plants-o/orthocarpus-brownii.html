Orthocarpus brownii

Orthocarpus brownii, commonly known as the New Zealand mountain dandelion, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in spring, while its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Orthocarpus brownii is valued for its ecological role in supporting local insect populations and its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
