Opophytum aquosum

Opophytum aquosum, commonly known as the water bladderwort, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its ability to store water in specialized bladder-like structures.

These bladders, which are modified leaves, allow the plant to float and absorb nutrients from the surrounding water. It is typically found in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats, such as ponds and marshes. The plant's morphology includes long, thin stems and small, scale-like leaves.

Opophytum aquosum plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and small invertebrates.
