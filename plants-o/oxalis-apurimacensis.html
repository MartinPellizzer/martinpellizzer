Oxalis apurimacensis

Oxalis apurimacensis, commonly known as the Andean wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Peru.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces orange or yellow berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests and alpine meadows.

Oxalis apurimacensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
