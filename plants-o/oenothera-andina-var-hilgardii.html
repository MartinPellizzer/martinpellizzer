Oenothera andina var. hilgardii

Oenothera andina var. hilgardii, commonly known as the Andean evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or well-drained soils. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
