Oncidium ornithorhynchum f. albiflorum

Oncidium ornithorhynchum f. albiflorum, commonly known as the Whiteflower Orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to South America.

It is characterized by its elegant, pendulous inflorescences that bear numerous white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its foliage consists of long, strap-like leaves that are often used for propagation.

Due to its striking appearance and ease of cultivation, it is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
