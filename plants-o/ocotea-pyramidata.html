Ocotea pyramidata

Ocotea pyramidata, commonly known as the pyramidate ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by oval, woody fruits containing aromatic seeds. Its leaves are typically elliptical with a smooth texture and a glossy surface.

Ocotea pyramidata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
