Ocellochloa pulchella

Ocellochloa pulchella, commonly known as the velvetleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves with a fine, silky texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Ocellochloa pulchella is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in riparian areas.
