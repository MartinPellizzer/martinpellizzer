Onosma aurantiaca

Onosma aurantiaca, commonly known as the orange sandthrift, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its hairy stems and bright orange to yellow flowers. The plant typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry habitats. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Onosma aurantiaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
