Oxalis griffithii

Oxalis griffithii, commonly known as the Indian wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces edible, sour-tasting berries that contain seeds. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest areas.

Oxalis griffithii is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been cultivated for its unique foliage and floral display.
