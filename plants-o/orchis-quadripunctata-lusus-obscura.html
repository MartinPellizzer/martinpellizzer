Orchis quadripunctata lusus obscura

Orchis quadripunctata lusus obscura, commonly known as the obscure four-spotted orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to specific regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and a striking purple-pink flower spike that bears several small, hooded flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as woodlands and stream banks, where it thrives in nutrient-rich soil. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, this orchid is considered endangered and is protected in many areas.
