Ocotea darcyi

Ocotea darcyi, commonly known as the Darcy's ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the humid forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a dark blue berry containing a single seed, and it often grows in shaded understory environments.

Ocotea darcyi is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional crafts and as a source of essential oils.
