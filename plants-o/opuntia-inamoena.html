Opuntia inamoena

Opuntia inamoena, commonly known as the Mexican prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in sharp spines and clusters of vibrant yellow or orange flowers. The plant produces edible fruits known as tunas, which are rich in nutrients and used in traditional cuisine. Opuntia inamoena is drought-tolerant and thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.

Its succulent stems store water, making it well adapted to survive in harsh desert conditions.
