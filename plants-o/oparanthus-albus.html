Oparanthus albus

Oparanthus albus, commonly known as the white oparanthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in dense clusters that bloom in the spring.

Oparanthus albus is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and drought-resistant properties.
