Ochna serratifolia

Ochna serratifolia, commonly known as the African velvetleaf, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical Africa.

It features compound leaves with serrated margins, giving it a distinctive, feathery appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and savanna habitats.

Ochna serratifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some cultures.
