Oenothera contorta var. strigulosa

Oenothera contorta var. strigulosa, commonly known as the woolly evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, rugged appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairie and woodland habitats.

The species is noted for its resilience and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
