Osbeckia calotricha

Osbeckia calotricha, commonly known as the silky osbeckia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, temperate climates. The plant features alternate, ovate leaves with a hairy texture, and its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in loose inflorescences. Osbeckia calotricha is known for its ornamental value, especially in gardens with well-drained soil.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
