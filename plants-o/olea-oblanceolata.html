Olea oblanceolata

Olea oblanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf olive, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family, which includes other olive species. The plant has simple, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by small, round drupes.

Olea oblanceolata is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
