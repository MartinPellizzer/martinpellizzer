Oenothera hirta f. reedii

Oenothera hirta f. reedii, commonly known as Reed's evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Onagraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas. Its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves are notable botanical features.

The seeds are enclosed in a capsule that splits open to release the seeds when mature.
