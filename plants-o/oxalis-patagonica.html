Oxalis patagonica

Oxalis patagonica, commonly known as the Patagonian wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive red veins and small, five-petaled yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. It has a fibrous root system and can spread through rhizomes.

Oxalis patagonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
