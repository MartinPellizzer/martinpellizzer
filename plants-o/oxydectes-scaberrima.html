Oxydectes scaberrima

Oxydectes scaberrima, commonly known as the roughleaf euphorbia, is a shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, rough hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its latex is milky and can be toxic, a common feature among members of this family.

Oxydectes scaberrima is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.
