Orthosiphon rabaiensis

Orthosiphon rabaiensis, commonly known as the Malabar horsegram or Indian senna, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in whorls along the stems. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Orthosiphon rabaiensis thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas.
