Orchis bosniaca

Orchis bosniaca, commonly known as the Bosnian orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are borne in loose clusters and are pollinated by insects such as bees and flies.

Orchis bosniaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
