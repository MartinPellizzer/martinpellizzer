Oxytropis middendorffii subsp. schmidtii

Oxytropis middendorffii subsp.

schmidtii, commonly known as Schmidt's locoweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae. It is characterized by its erect, spiny stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple.

The plant is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, including parts of Mongolia and Russia. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought and extreme temperatures.

Schmidt's locoweed is often found in steppe and desert environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
