Ophrys muscifera f. dubia

Ophrys muscifera f. dubia, commonly known as the rare orchid with a distinctive mimicry of female wasps, is a botanical curiosity characterized by its unique floral structure.

This orchid belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is native to specific regions in southern Europe, particularly in Spain and Portugal. Its flowers exhibit a specialized adaptation, resembling female wasps to attract male pollinators through a process known as pseudocopulation. The plant thrives in rocky, calcareous soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Ophrys muscifera f. dubia is considered a rare and endangered species.
