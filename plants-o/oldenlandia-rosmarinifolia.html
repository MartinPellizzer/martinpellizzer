Oldenlandia rosmarinifolia

Oldenlandia rosmarinifolia, commonly known as the lemon verbena plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lemon-scented leaves and small, white to yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, with a rough texture and a strong, pleasant fragrance.

The flowers are clustered in spikes and bloom during the summer months, attracting various pollinators.
