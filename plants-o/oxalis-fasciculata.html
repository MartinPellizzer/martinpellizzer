Oxalis fasciculata

Oxalis fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered wood sorrel, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often clustered at the base of the plant, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Oxalis fasciculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
