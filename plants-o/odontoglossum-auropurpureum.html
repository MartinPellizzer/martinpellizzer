Odontoglossum auropurpureum

Odontoglossum auropurpureum, commonly known as the purple-tinged orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet and green foliage, with flowers that range from pink to purple, often featuring intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree branches in its natural habitat. Its flowers bloom in late summer, producing a fragrant, showy display that attracts pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

Odontoglossum auropurpureum is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements in cultivation.
