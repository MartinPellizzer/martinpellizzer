Ocotea lanceolaria

Ocotea lanceolaria, commonly known as the lanceleaf ocotea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in countries like Costa Rica, Panama, and Colombia. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves, which are typically glossy and arranged alternately along the stems. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences, followed by oval-shaped berries.

Ocotea lanceolaria is often found in humid, lowland forests and is valued for its aromatic wood and ecological role in its native habitats.
