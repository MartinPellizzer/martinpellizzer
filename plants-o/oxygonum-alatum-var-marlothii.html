Oxygonum alatum var. marlothii

Oxygonum alatum var. marlothii, commonly known as the Marloth's arrowleaf, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features distinctive arrow-shaped leaves that are fleshy and covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
