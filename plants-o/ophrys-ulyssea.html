Ophrys ulyssea

Ophrys ulyssea, commonly known as the Ulysses' Orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid native to the Mediterranean region.

The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands. Its flowers are usually pale green or brown, with intricate patterns that aid in attracting male bees for pollination.
