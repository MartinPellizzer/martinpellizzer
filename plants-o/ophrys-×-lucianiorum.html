Ophrys × lucianiorum

Ophrys × lucianiorum, commonly known as the greater sheep's beard, is a hybrid orchid species native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bee-mimicking flowers that feature a prominent, fuzzy lip resembling a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brown in color, with intricate patterns that aid in its deceptive pollination strategy.

Ophrys × lucianiorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinator populations.
