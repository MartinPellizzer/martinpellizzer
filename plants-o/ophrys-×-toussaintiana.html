Ophrys × toussaintiana

Ophrys × toussaintiana, commonly known as the Tousaint's bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking floral structure, which closely resembles a female bee, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its flowers are usually a mix of yellow, brown, and black, with a distinctive lip that mimics the appearance of a bee's body.

Ophrys × toussaintiana is a short-lived perennial, with a life cycle that includes a dormant phase during unfavorable conditions.
