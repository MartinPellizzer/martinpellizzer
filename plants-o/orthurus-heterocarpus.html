Orthurus heterocarpus

Orthurus heterocarpus, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet-pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a distinctive conical shape formed by tightly packed petals, with a central disk of tubular flowers. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soil in full sun.

Orthurus heterocarpus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and supports pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
