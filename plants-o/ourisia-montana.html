Ourisia montana

Ourisia montana, commonly known as mountain ourisia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the family Primulaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, hairy leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, yellowish flowers in early spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Ourisia montana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
