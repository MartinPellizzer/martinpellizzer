Oncidium regentianum

Oncidium regentianum, commonly known as the regent orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments with well-draining soil. The plant produces elongated, pendulous inflorescences bearing numerous small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and white. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the surrounding substrate.

Oncidium regentianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in greenhouses and gardens.
