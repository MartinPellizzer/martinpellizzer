Osmanthus sumatranus

Osmanthus sumatranus, commonly known as the Java osmanthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often emitting a sweet, honey-like scent. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

Osmanthus sumatranus is also used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
