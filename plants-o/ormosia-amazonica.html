Ormosia amazonica

Ormosia amazonica, commonly known as the Amazonian ormosia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The tree produces pods that contain hard, dark seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Ormosia amazonica is valued for its durable wood and its role in supporting local biodiversity.

Its ecological importance makes it a significant species in the conservation of tropical rainforest ecosystems.
