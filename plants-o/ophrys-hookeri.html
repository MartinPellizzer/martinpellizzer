Ophrys hookeri

Ophrys hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the genus Ophrys, which is known for its mimicry of female insects to attract male pollinators. The plant produces a single, showy flower with a complex structure that includes a labellum resembling a female bee. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often growing in a rosette form.

Ophrys hookeri is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral characteristics.
