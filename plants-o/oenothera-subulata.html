Oenothera subulata

Oenothera subulata, commonly known as the needle-leaf evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves and yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant produces a sticky resin that has been used historically by indigenous peoples for various purposes. It thrives in arid and rocky environments, often found in desert regions.

Oenothera subulata is a member of the Onagraceae family, which includes other evening primrose species.
