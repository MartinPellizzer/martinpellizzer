Oxygonum dregeanum var. pubescens

Oxygonum dregeanum var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy oxalis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, which are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is often grown in both indoor and outdoor settings.
