Osteospermum asperulum

Osteospermum asperulum, commonly known as the rough osteospermum, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and purple or blue petals. The plant has hairy, rough-textured leaves that help it retain moisture in arid conditions. It produces small, dark brown seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation.

Osteospermum asperulum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
