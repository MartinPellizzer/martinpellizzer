Oxalis monticola

Oxalis monticola, commonly known as the mountain wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the spring and early summer. Oxalis monticola thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and delicate flowers.
