Ocotea sarcodes

Ocotea sarcodes, commonly known as the fragrant wood or sweet wood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests, often reaching heights of up to 15 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

The fruit is a small, dark-colored drupe that contains a single seed, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
