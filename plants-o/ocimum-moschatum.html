Ocimum moschatum

Ocimum moschatum, commonly known as holy basil or tulsi, is a small, aromatic shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and sage. The plant is characterized by its dark green, slightly hairy leaves and clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. Its essential oils, rich in compounds like eugenol and camphor, contribute to its distinctive fragrance and medicinal properties.

Ocimum moschatum is widely cultivated for both ornamental and therapeutic purposes.
