Onopordum × turolense

Onopordum × turolense, commonly known as the Turolense thistle, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and large, showy purple to pink flower heads that bloom in the summer. Native to the Iberian Peninsula, it thrives in dry, sunny environments such as rocky soils and open fields. The plant produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal, aiding in its spread.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
