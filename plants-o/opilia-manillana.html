Opilia manillana

Opilia manillana, commonly known as the Manila orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Opilia manillana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
