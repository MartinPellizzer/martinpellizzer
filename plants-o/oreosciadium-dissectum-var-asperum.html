Oreosciadium dissectum var. asperum

Oreosciadium dissectum var. asperum, commonly known as the roughleaf orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely dissected, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in a spike, typical of many orchid species. It thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories.
