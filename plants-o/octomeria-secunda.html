Octomeria secunda

Octomeria secunda, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf sida, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale green to yellow in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Octomeria secunda is known for its adaptability and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
