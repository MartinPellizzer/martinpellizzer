Oxytropis kokrinensis

Oxytropis kokrinensis, commonly known as Kokrin's locoweed, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions. Its small, pea-like flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces hairy, segmented pods that contain seeds.

Oxytropis kokrinensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations and potential medicinal properties.
