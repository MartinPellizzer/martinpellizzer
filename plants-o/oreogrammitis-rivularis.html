Oreogrammitis rivularis

Oreogrammitis rivularis, commonly known as the river wall moss, is a small, cryptic moss that typically grows in moist, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its dense, carpet-like growth and dark green, often glossy leaves. The plant is usually found along stream banks, on rocks, or in wetlands, where it thrives in consistently damp conditions. Its reproductive structures, called sporophytes, are small and often inconspicuous, typical of many mosses.

Oreogrammitis rivularis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing microhabitats for other organisms in its ecosystem.
