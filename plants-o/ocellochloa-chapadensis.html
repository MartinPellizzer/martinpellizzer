Ocellochloa chapadensis

Ocellochloa chapadensis, commonly known as the Brazilian needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry blades and a dense growth habit, often forming compact tufts. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its botanical features include small, spike-like inflorescences and narrow, pointed leaves.

Ocellochloa chapadensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in native habitats.
