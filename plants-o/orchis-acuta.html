Orchis acuta

Orchis acuta, commonly known as the acute orchid, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its flowers are spurred at the base, a distinctive feature of many orchid species.

Orchis acuta is known for its ability to thrive in shaded environments and is often found in association with other orchid species.
