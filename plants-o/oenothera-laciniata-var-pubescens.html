Oenothera laciniata var. pubescens

Oenothera laciniata var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it its varietal name "pubescens.". The plant produces showy yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It thrives in sandy or well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats like prairies and disturbed areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
