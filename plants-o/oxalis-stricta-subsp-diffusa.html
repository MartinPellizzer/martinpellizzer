Oxalis stricta subsp. diffusa

Oxalis stricta subsp.

diffusa, commonly known as the slender wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to North America. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are usually heart-shaped with a deep green color and reddish veins.

The plant produces small, delicate flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. Oxalis stricta subsp.

diffusa is a perennial herb that is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens with partial shade.
