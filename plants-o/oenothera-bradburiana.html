Oenothera bradburiana

Oenothera bradburiana, commonly known as the Bradburian evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Oenothera bradburiana is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its historical use by indigenous peoples.
