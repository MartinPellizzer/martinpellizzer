Ophrys × tuscanica

Ophrys × tuscanica, commonly known as the Tuscan bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Italy.

It belongs to the genus Ophrys, which is known for its mimicry of female insects to attract male pollinators. The plant produces showy, bee-like flowers with intricate patterns that resemble female bees, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. Ophrys × tuscanica typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Its botanical characteristics include a slender stem with a single or few flowering shoots and leaves that are narrow and grass-like in appearance.
