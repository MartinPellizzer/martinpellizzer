Oryzopsis uruguensis

Oryzopsis uruguensis, commonly known as the Uruguay grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has fibrous roots and is adapted to temperate climates.

Oryzopsis uruguensis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
