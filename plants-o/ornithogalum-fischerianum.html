Ornithogalum fischerianum

Ornithogalum fischerianum, commonly known as Fischer's star of Bethlehem, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, linear leaves and produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers during the summer months. The plant belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous root system. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and rocky habitats.

The flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
