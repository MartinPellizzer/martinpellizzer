Oenothera runcinata var. typica

Oenothera runcinata var. typica, commonly known as the narrow-leaf evening primrose, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its capsule fruit contains numerous small, oily seeds that are rich in essential fatty acids.

Oenothera runcinata var. typica is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats, thriving in full sun.
