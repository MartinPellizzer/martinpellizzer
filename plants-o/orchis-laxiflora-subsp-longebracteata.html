Orchis laxiflora subsp. longebracteata

Orchis laxiflora subsp.

longebracteata, commonly known as the long-bract orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet or purple flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its elongated bracts that enclose the inflorescence. It typically grows in moist, calcareous habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

The plant is perennial and produces a single, terminal spike of flowers.
