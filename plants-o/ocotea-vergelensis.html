Ocotea vergelensis

Ocotea vergelensis, commonly known as the Vergeles's ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a drupe, typically containing a single seed, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Ocotea vergelensis is valued for its wood, which is used in traditional crafts and as a source of essential oils.
