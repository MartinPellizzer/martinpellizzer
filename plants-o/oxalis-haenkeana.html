Oxalis haenkeana

Oxalis haenkeana, commonly known as the Chilean wood sorrel, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive reddish veins and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its flowers are typically short-lived, opening in the morning and closing by midday.

Oxalis haenkeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and floral display.
