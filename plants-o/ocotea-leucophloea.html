Ocotea leucophloea

Ocotea leucophloea, commonly known as white-flowered ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon. The tree is characterized by its smooth, whitish bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in inflorescences, contributing to its ornamental value.

The plant is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is used in horticulture for its shade and foliage.
