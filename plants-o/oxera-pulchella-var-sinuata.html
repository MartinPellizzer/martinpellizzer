Oxera pulchella var. sinuata

Oxera pulchella var. sinuata, commonly known as the Sinuous Oxera, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a distinctive sinuous or wavy margin, giving the plant its specific variety name. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
