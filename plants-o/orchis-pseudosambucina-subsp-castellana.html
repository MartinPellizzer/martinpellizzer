Orchis pseudosambucina subsp. castellana

Orchis pseudosambucina subsp.

castellana, commonly known as the Castellana orchid, is a rare and endemic species found primarily in southern Europe. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive violet to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has a pseudobulb structure and produces a single, showy flower per stem.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
