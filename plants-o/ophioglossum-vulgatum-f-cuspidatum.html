Ophioglossum vulgatum f. cuspidatum

Ophioglossum vulgatum f. cuspidatum, commonly known as the spurred adder's tongue fern, is a distinctive fern species characterized by its unique morphology.

It features a short, erect rhizome with a single, broad, lanceolate leaf that has a long, tapering tip. The leaf is typically green and has a waxy texture, with a prominent midrib and a distinctive, spurred appearance at the base. It produces sporangia in clusters on the underside of the leaf, which are arranged in a linear pattern.

This fern is native to various regions in the Northern Hemisphere and thrives in moist, shaded environments.
