Ophrys × martorinae

Ophrys × martorinae, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera (the bee orchid) and Ophrys insectifera (the wasp orchid).

It exhibits a unique blend of characteristics from both parent species, including a striking flower morphology that mimics the appearance of a wasp or bee. The plant is typically found in Mediterranean climates, thriving in open, sunny habitats with well-drained soils. Its flowers are specialized for pollination by bees, using intricate visual and chemical mimicry to attract male bees.

This hybrid species is of particular interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its genetic complexity and ecological significance.
