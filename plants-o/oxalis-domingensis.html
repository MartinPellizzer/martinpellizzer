Oxalis domingensis

Oxalis domingensis, commonly known as the Easter lily, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in the spring. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of white or pink flowers with a subtle fragrance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Oxalis domingensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
