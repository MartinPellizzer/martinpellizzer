Oxalis javanica

Oxalis javanica, commonly known as the Java wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive reddish veins and small, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink or white. The plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments, making it a common sight in tropical forests. Oxalis javanica is also notable for its edible leaves, which have a tangy, sour flavor.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for both garden and horticultural use.
