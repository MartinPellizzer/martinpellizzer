Oenothera × wienii

Oenothera × wienii, commonly known as the Vienna evening primrose, is a hybrid species belonging to the Onagraceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with lance-shaped leaves and a hairy stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed habitats or along roadsides.

Oenothera × wienii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its unique blooming behavior.
