Oxalis gyrorhiza

Oxalis gyrorhiza, commonly known as the red oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers and trifoliate leaves, which are typical of the Oxalis genus. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its succulent stems and leaves allow it to store water, making it drought-tolerant.

Oxalis gyrorhiza is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
