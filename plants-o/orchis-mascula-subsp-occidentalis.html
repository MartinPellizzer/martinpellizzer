Orchis mascula subsp. occidentalis

Orchis mascula subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the western twayblade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, greenish-white flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are narrow and arranged in a rosette at the base, while its flowers are clustered in a spike.

The western twayblade is known for its ability to form dense colonies and is often found in association with other orchid species in its habitat.
