Oxytropis altaica

Oxytropis altaica, commonly known as Siberian locoweed, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Central Asia and northern Russia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments. It contains toxic alkaloids, which can be harmful to livestock, making it a significant concern in grazing areas.

Its ecological role includes nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in nutrient-poor regions.
