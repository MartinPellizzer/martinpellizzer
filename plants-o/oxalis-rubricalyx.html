Oxalis rubricalyx

Oxalis rubricalyx, commonly known as the red-leaf wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant features small, heart-shaped leaves with a reddish tint, which give it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Oxalis rubricalyx is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant.
