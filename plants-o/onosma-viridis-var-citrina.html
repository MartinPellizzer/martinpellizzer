Onosma viridis var. citrina

Onosma viridis var. citrina, commonly known as the yellow globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft texture. This plant prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
