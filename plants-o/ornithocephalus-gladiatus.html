Ornithocephalus gladiatus

Ornithocephalus gladiatus, commonly known as the swordleaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, sword-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and purple. The plant prefers cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas. Its root system is fibrous, and it reproduces through seeds as well as by vegetative propagation.

Ornithocephalus gladiatus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
