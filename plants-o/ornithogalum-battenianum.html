Ornithogalum battenianum

Ornithogalum battenianum, commonly known as the Batten's star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, linear leaves and produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers during the summer months. The plant belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous underground stem, which stores nutrients. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some regions.
