Oncoba ovata

Oncoba ovata, commonly known as the sweet-scented mahogany, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves and produces fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree bears small, round fruits that turn from green to yellow as they mature. Oncoba ovata is valued for its durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
