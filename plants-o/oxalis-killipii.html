Oxalis killipii

Oxalis killipii, commonly known as the pink oxalis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves that resemble those of shamrocks, and its clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in shaded to partially shaded environments. It is a herbaceous perennial that blooms in spring and early summer.

Oxalis killipii is valued in gardens for its ornamental foliage and modest floral display.
