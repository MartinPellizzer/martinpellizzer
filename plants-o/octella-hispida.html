Octella hispida

Octella hispida, commonly known as the hairy octella, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
