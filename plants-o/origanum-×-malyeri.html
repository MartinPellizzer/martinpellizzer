Origanum × malyeri

Origanum × malyeri, commonly known as Greek oregano, is a hybrid perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, narrow-lanceolate leaves that are dense and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees. Its essential oils, rich in compounds like carvacrol and thymol, contribute to its strong fragrance and medicinal properties.

Origanum × malyeri is widely used in cooking and traditional herbal remedies for its pungent, spicy flavor.
