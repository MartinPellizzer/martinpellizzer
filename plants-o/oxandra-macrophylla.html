Oxandra macrophylla

Oxandra macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf oxandra, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round fruits. Oxandra macrophylla thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and horticultural collections.
