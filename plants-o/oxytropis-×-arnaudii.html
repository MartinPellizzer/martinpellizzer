Oxytropis × arnaudii

Oxytropis × arnaudii, commonly known as the hybrid milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its erect growth habit and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Oxytropis × arnaudii is adapted to dry, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and steppe environments.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
