Opuntia alpicola

Opuntia alpicola, commonly known as the alpine prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the Andes Mountains.

It is characterized by its flattened, fleshy stems that are segmented and covered in spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the summer months. Its fruit, known as a tunas, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

Opuntia alpicola is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or arid regions.
