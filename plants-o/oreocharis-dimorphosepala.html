Oreocharis dimorphosepala

Oreocharis dimorphosepala, commonly known as the two-sepal orchid, is a species of flowering plant in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern China, particularly in Yunnan Province. The plant is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it grows in rocky or forested habitats.

This orchid is notable for its unique floral structure, which includes two distinctly shaped sepals.
