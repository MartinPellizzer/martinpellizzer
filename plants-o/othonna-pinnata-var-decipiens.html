Othonna pinnata var. decipiens

Othonna pinnata var. decipiens, commonly known as the False Dandelion, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It features a compact, upright growth habit with thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by distinctive, fluffy seed heads that resemble dandelion fluff. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping.

The leaves and stems are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
