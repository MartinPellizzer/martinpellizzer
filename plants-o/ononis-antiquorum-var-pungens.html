Ononis antiquorum var. pungens

Ononis antiquorum var. pungens, commonly known as the prickly restharrow, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has hairy, often spiny stems and leaves, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry grasslands.

The species is known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
