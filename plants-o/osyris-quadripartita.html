Osyris quadripartita

Osyris quadripartita, commonly known as the African waterwheel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows near water sources, showcasing its adaptability to moist environments. The plant features palmate leaves with four to six lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its showy yellow flowers are clustered in umbels, adding ornamental value.

Osyris quadripartita is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
