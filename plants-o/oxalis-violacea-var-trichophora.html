Oxalis violacea var. trichophora

Oxalis violacea var. trichophora, commonly known as the velvetleaf wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with a velvety texture and deep green color, often with a reddish or purple tint on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to violet, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and has a fibrous root system.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in ornamental gardening due to their attractive appearance.
