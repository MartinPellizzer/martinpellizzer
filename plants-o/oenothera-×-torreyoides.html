Oenothera × torreyoides

Oenothera × torreyoides, commonly known as the Torrey's evening primrose, is a hybrid plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial with showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Oenothera × torreyoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
