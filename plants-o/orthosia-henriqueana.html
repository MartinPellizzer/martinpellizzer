Orthosia henriqueana

Orthosia henriqueana, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and is known for its striking violet or purple-colored blooms. Orthosia henriqueana is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local biodiversity.

Due to habitat loss, it is also considered a species of conservation concern.
