Oerstedella viridiflora

Oerstedella viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered orchid, is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a loose inflorescence, which is a distinguishing feature of the species. It thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Oerstedella viridiflora is valued for its unique coloration and is occasionally cultivated for ornamental purposes.
