Ornithogalum umbellatum subsp. divergens

Ornithogalum umbellatum subsp.

divergens, commonly known as the star-of-Bethleham, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It features a bulbous stem with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers.

The plant is characterized by its umbellate inflorescence, where flowers are arranged in a flat-topped cluster. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
