Oryza sativa var. melanocarpa

Oryza sativa var. melanocarpa, commonly known as black rice, is a variety of rice characterized by its dark purple to black grain color due to high levels of anthocyanin pigments.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is cultivated for both its nutritional value and aesthetic appeal. The plant is an annual grass with tall, slender stalks and small, clustered panicles of flowers. Its seeds are rich in antioxidants, fiber, and essential nutrients, making it a valuable crop in traditional and modern diets.

The botanical features of black rice contribute to its distinct appearance and health benefits.
