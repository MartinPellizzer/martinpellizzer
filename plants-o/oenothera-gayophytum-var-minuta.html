Oenothera gayophytum var. minuta

Oenothera gayophytum var. minuta, commonly known as the dwarf evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the evening primrose family, known for its ecological and ornamental value.
