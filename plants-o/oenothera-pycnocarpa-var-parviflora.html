Oenothera pycnocarpa var. parviflora

Oenothera pycnocarpa var. parviflora, commonly known as the dwarf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the evening primrose family (Onagraceae) and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that typically bloom at dusk. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance as a food source for nocturnal pollinators.
