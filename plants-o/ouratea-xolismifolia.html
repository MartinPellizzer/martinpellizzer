Ouratea xolismifolia

Ouratea xolismifolia, commonly known as the white starwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy dark green leaves that are often arranged in a whorled pattern around the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red or orange berries. Its ornamental value comes from its striking foliage and unique floral display.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in tropical gardens.
