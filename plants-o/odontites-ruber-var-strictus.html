Odontites ruber var. strictus

Odontites ruber var. strictus, commonly known as the red mountain sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive red-tinged leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and herbal practices.
