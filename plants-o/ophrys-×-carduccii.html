Ophrys × carduccii

Ophrys × carduccii, commonly known as the Carducci's bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the genus Ophrys, which is known for its intricate mimicry of female insects to attract male pollinators. The plant produces showy, bee-like flowers that are highly specialized for pollination by bees. Its botanical characteristics include a sympodial growth habit and pseudobulbs, typical of many orchid species.

The flowers are usually yellow or orange with dark markings, resembling a female bee to facilitate deception pollination.
