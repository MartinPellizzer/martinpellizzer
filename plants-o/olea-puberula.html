Olea puberula

Olea puberula, commonly known as the rough olive, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are hairy on the underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by small, round fruits resembling olives. Olea puberula is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.

It is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive foliage and hardy nature.
