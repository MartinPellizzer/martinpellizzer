Opuntia ottonis

Opuntia ottonis, commonly known as the Mexican prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems that are covered in spines and glochids, which are fine, barbed hairs. The plant produces showy yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tunas, is edible and often consumed fresh or processed into beverages.

Opuntia ottonis is well adapted to arid environments and plays an important role in its ecosystem.
