Oreogrammitis trichopoda

Oreogrammitis trichopoda, commonly known as the silver thread fern, is a small, delicate fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant features a slender, creeping rhizome with narrow, lance-shaped fronds that are covered in fine, silvery hairs. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are located on the underside of the fronds.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
