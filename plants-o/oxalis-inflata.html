Oxalis inflata

Oxalis inflata, commonly known as the inflated wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated, leaf-like structures that resemble small, rounded leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Oxalis inflata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
