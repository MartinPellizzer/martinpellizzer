Ophrys × congedoi

Ophrys × congedoi, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant in the Ophrys genus, known for its intricate floral mimicry.

This species is a natural hybrid between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera, resulting in a unique combination of traits from both parents. The flower's striking resemblance to a female bee is a key adaptation that enhances its pollination success through sexual deception. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in temperate regions of Europe.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a model organism for studying plant hybridization and evolutionary adaptations.
