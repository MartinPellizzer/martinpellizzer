Oenothera pallidula

Oenothera pallidula, commonly known as the pale evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oval seeds. Oenothera pallidula is often found in dry, disturbed soils and is adapted to arid environments.

It is part of the Onagraceae family, which includes other species of evening primroses.
