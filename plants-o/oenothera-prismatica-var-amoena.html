Oenothera prismatica var. amoena

Oenothera prismatica var. amoena, commonly known as the pink evening primrose, is a species native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
