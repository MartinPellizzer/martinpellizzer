Ocimum viride

Ocimum viride, commonly known as Holy Basil, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, lance-shaped leaves and small, pale blue or white flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and lavender. Ocimum viride is known for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional medicine.

Its essential oils contain compounds such as eugenol and rosmarinic acid, which contribute to its therapeutic uses.
