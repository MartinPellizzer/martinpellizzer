Oncidium intermedium var. alborubrum

Oncidium intermedium var. alborubrum, commonly known as the red-tipped orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, often orange or red-brown flower spikes that bear numerous small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial orchid, thriving in warm, humid environments with indirect sunlight. Its leaves are strap-like, often arranged in a fan shape, and can vary in color from green to reddish tones.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its striking floral display.
