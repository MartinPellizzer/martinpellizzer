Otophylla auriculata

Otophylla auriculata, commonly known as the earleaf cactus, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of South America.

It features a short, thick stem with spiny leaves that resemble small ears, adapted for water storage and reducing transpiration. The plant produces yellow flowers during the growing season, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its structure allows it to thrive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water.

Otophylla auriculata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
