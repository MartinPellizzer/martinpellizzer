Oxalis lanata var. rosea

Oxalis lanata var. rosea, commonly known as the roseate wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and bright pink to rose-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Oxalis lanata var. rosea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
