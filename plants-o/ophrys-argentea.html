Ophrys argentea

Ophrys argentea, commonly known as the silver orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, bee-like flowers that play a role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats. Its flowers are often pale yellow to silver in color, with intricate patterns that mimic female bees to attract male pollinators.

Ophrys argentea is also known for its ability to form dense colonies in suitable environments.
