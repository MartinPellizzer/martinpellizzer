Orchis latifolia var. robusta

Orchis latifolia var. robusta, commonly known as the broad-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodland areas and thrives in well-drained, loamy soils. The plant features large, lance-shaped leaves and produces tall spikes of purple or violet flowers. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil efficiently.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
