Odontoglossum pendulum

Odontoglossum pendulum, commonly known as the pendant orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a compact growth habit and produces striking, pendulous inflorescences. The flowers are typically violet or purple, with a spurred lip that is characteristic of the Orchidaceae family. It thrives in cool, moist environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in specialized horticultural settings.
