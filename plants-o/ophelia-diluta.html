Ophelia diluta

Ophelia diluta, commonly known as the water crowfoot, is a flowering plant native to freshwater habitats in Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, floating leaves that are divided into three parts. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are often submerged or floating on the water surface. Its rapid growth and ability to colonize water bodies make it both a useful indicator species and a potential invasive plant in non-native regions.
