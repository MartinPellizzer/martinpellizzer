Oldenlandia pulcherrima

Oldenlandia pulcherrima, commonly known as the "Cape ivy" or "Iron cross," is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a trailing perennial that produces small, white or pale yellow flowers with a distinctive, intricate pattern resembling a cross. The plant features opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and blooms.

Its rapid growth makes it both a useful ground cover and a potential invasive species in certain regions.
