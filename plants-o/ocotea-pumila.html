Ocotea pumila

Ocotea pumila, commonly known as the dwarf ocotea, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within the understory of tropical forests. Its small, dark blue berries are an important food source for various wildlife species.

Ocotea pumila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and ecological significance.
