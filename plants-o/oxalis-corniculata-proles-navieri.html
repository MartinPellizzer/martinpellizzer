Oxalis corniculata proles navieri

Oxalis corniculata proles navieri, commonly known as the Navier's wood sorrel, is a flowering plant belonging to the Oxalidaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, yellow flowers that resemble those of true sorrel. The plant is native to regions of South America and is often found in shaded, moist environments. It produces small, edible berries that turn red when mature.

Oxalis corniculata proles navieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
