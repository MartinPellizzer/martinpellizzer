Oenothera heterantha

Oenothera heterantha, commonly known as the showy evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats like prairies and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often branching.

Oenothera heterantha is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its ecological importance in supporting pollinator populations.
