Oxytropis lapponica var. xanthantha

Oxytropis lapponica var. xanthantha, commonly known as the yellow lapp's locoweed, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of Europe and North America.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features dense clusters of small, yellow to golden flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The species is adapted to cold, dry environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
