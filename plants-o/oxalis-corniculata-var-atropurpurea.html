Oxalis corniculata var. atropurpurea

Oxalis corniculata var. atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to Asia and parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are often heart-shaped and have a reddish or purple tinge. The plant produces small, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in gardens and woodland areas.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
