Ophrys taurica

Ophrys taurica, commonly known as the Tauric Orchid, is a species of orchid native to southern Europe and parts of the Middle East.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which features a specialized lip that mimics the appearance of a female bee, aiding in pollination through mimicry. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, open habitats. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brownish in color, with intricate patterns that enhance its visual appeal.

Ophrys taurica is a perennial herb that produces a single, showy flower per stem, often blooming in spring.
