Oenanthe linearis subsp. linearis

Oenanthe linearis subsp.

linearis, commonly known as the narrowleaf water dropwort, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils along streambanks and wet meadows.

The plant features slender, linear leaves that are often slightly hairy, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbels. Its flowering period occurs from June to August, and it is often found in riparian habitats.

The species is notable for its ecological importance in supporting insect life and maintaining wetland biodiversity.
