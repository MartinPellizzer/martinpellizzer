Oncidium carthagenense var. sanguineum

Oncidium carthagenense var. sanguineum, commonly known as the Red Oncidium, is a hybrid orchid species native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range from red to orange and yellow, often with contrasting dark markings. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its pseudobulbs are cylindrical, and it produces long, arching inflorescences with numerous blooms.

It requires bright, indirect light and moderate humidity to thrive, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
