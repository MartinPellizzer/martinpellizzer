Oxytropis tenuis

Oxytropis tenuis, commonly known as narrowleaf locoweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, compound leaves. The plant produces yellow or greenish-yellow flowers in racemes, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America, particularly in the western United States.

Oxytropis tenuis is adapted to harsh environments, with deep root systems that allow it to survive in poor soils and extreme temperatures.
