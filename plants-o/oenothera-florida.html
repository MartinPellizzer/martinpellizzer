Oenothera florida

Oenothera florida, commonly known as the evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Oenothera florida is known for its ability to produce copious amounts of nectar, attracting various pollinators such as moths and beetles.
