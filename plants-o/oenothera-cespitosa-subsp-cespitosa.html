Oenothera cespitosa subsp. cespitosa

Oenothera cespitosa subsp.

cespitosa, commonly known as prairie smoke, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It is characterized by its distinctive, fluffy, pinkish-purple seed heads that resemble smoke rising from the plant.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and sandy soils. It produces showy yellow flowers in the summer, followed by the characteristic seed plumes. Oenothera cespitosa subsp.

cespitosa is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
