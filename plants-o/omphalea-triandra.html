Omphalea triandra

Omphalea triandra, commonly known as the African arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows up to 2 meters in height, characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by orange-red berries containing toxic seeds. Its roots are used in traditional medicine, and the plant is also known for its ornamental value.

However, it is important to note that all parts of the plant contain toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
