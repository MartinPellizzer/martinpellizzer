Oxytropis albana

Oxytropis albana, commonly known as white locoweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are compound, with small leaflets, and it produces pods that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Oxytropis albana is also known for its toxic properties, which can affect livestock if ingested.
