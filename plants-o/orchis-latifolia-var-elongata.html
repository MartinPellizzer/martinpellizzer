Orchis latifolia var. elongata

Orchis latifolia var. elongata, commonly known as the broad-leaved orchid variant with elongated leaves, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features long, narrow leaves and produces spikes of violet or purple flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows, and is often found in rich, loamy soils.

This variant is notable for its ecological importance, supporting various pollinators and contributing to biodiversity in its native habitats.
