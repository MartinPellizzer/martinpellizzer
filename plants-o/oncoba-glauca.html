Oncoba glauca

Oncoba glauca, commonly known as the white cedar, is a small evergreen tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and its distinctive, smooth, gray bark. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are followed by blue-black berries. Oncoba glauca is often found in moist, acidic soils along stream banks and in swamps.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
