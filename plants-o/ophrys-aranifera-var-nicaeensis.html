Ophrys aranifera var. nicaeensis

Ophrys aranifera var. nicaeensis, commonly known as the Nicaean bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its unique floral morphology, with a labellum that mimics the appearance of a female bee, a trait that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing a delicate, bee-like structure that attracts male bees for pollination.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
