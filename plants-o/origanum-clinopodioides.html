Origanum clinopodioides

Origanum clinopodioides, commonly known as sweet marjoram, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses. The plant produces small, pale purple or white flowers and has hairy, narrow leaves that are often used in cooking and herbal remedies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal cultivation.

Its essential oils contain compounds like carvacrol and thymol, which contribute to its distinctive fragrance and therapeutic benefits.
