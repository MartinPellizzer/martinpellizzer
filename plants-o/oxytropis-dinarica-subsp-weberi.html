Oxytropis dinarica subsp. weberi

Oxytropis dinarica subsp.

weberi, commonly known as the Dinaric oxytropis, is a perennial legume native to the Balkan region. It typically grows in rocky and dry habitats, such as limestone slopes and mountainous areas.

The plant features slender, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers that are usually pale blue or violet in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval to lanceolate leaflets.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
