Oxystelma esculentum var. alpini

Oxystelma esculentum var. alpini, commonly known as the alpine bitter melon, is a variant of the bitter melon species native to high-altitude regions.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its slender stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, green, bitter fruits that mature to a yellowish-orange color. Botanically, it belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and pumpkins.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for horticultural and ethnobotanical studies.
