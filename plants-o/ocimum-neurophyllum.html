Ocimum neurophyllum

Ocimum neurophyllum, commonly known as the sweet basil or holy basil, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are aromatic, containing essential oils that contribute to its distinctive fragrance and medicinal properties.

Ocimum neurophyllum is widely cultivated for culinary, aromatic, and traditional medicinal uses.
