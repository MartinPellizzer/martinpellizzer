Oenothera drummondii nana-albida

Oenothera drummondii nana-albida, commonly known as the white evening primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southwestern United States.

It features showy, white to pale yellow flowers that open in the evening and close by morning. The plant has slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and slightly serrated. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by moths and other nocturnal insects.

The plant is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils.
