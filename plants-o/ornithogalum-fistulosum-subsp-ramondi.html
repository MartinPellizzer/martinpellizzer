Ornithogalum fistulosum subsp. ramondi

Ornithogalum fistulosum subsp.

ramondi, commonly known as the Ramond's onion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It features slender, upright stems that bear narrow, linear leaves arranged in a basal rosette.

The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers in umbels during the summer months. Its bulbs are edible when young but become toxic as they mature.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy or rocky habitats.
