Ototropis rufescens

Ototropis rufescens, commonly known as redstem false brome, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a reddish tinge when young, and it produces small, pale green to brown flowers.

Ototropis rufescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
