Oxalis mapirensis

Oxalis mapirensis, commonly known as the Mapire oxalis, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and undergrowth. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, and the flowers can range in color from white to pink or red.

Oxalis mapirensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
