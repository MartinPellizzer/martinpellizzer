Oxalis reclinata

Oxalis reclinata, commonly known as the weeping wood sorrel, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features delicate, trifoliate leaves that droop gracefully from its stems, giving it its characteristic weeping appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Oxalis reclinata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
