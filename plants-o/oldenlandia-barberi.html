Oldenlandia barberi

Oldenlandia barberi, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Oldenlandia barberi is also known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets.
