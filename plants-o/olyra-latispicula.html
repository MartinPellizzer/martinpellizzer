Olyra latispicula

Olyra latispicula, commonly known as the broad-spined euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are adapted to store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, adding a striking visual element to its appearance. Its spiny structure serves as a defense mechanism against herbivores.

Olyra latispicula is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
