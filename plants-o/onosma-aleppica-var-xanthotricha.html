Onosma aleppica var. xanthotricha

Onosma aleppica var. xanthotricha, commonly known as the yellow-haired onosma, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of small, yellowish or golden flowers. The plant has hairy leaves and stems, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
