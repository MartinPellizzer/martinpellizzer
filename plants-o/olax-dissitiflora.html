Olax dissitiflora

Olax dissitiflora, commonly known as the scattered-flower olax, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Olacaceae and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with four to five petals. Its leaves are usually alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Olax dissitiflora is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in tropical and subtropical regions.
