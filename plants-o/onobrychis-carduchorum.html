Onobrychis carduchorum

Onobrychis carduchorum, commonly known as the Armenian clover, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. Native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus, this plant thrives in rocky and well-drained soils. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species in its native habitat.
