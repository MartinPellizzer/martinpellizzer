Oenothera valdiviana

Oenothera valdiviana, commonly known as the Valdivia evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is well adapted to arid environments.

Oenothera valdiviana is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for its unique floral display.
