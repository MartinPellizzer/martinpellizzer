Oenothera intermedia

Oenothera intermedia, commonly known as the prairie smoke, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its unique, fluffy, pink to purple flower heads that resemble smoke, which are actually composed of many small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in prairie and open woodland habitats, preferring well-drained soils. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces seeds in small capsules.

Oenothera intermedia is known for its ability to thrive in dry conditions and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
