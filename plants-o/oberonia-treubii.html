Oberonia treubii

Oberonia treubii, commonly known as the Treub's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, spiral arrangement of leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are often clustered in a loose inflorescence. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, Oberonia treubii is considered a rare and endangered species.
