Onobrychis crista-galli subsp. eigii

Onobrychis crista-galli subsp.

eigii, commonly known as the lesser crown vetch, is a perennial legume native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant has a creeping habit with stolons and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. It is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen and is used in ecological restoration projects.

Its subspecific name, eigii, honors the botanist Johann Friedrich Eig.
