Ocotea laxa

Ocotea laxa, commonly known as the broadleaf ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and aromatic leaves that emit a subtle spicy scent. Ocotea laxa typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests.

Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.
