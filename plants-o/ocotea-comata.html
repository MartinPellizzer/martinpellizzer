Ocotea comata

Ocotea comata, commonly known as the silky ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark brown bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. Its fragrant flowers are typically white or pale green and are followed by small, dark blue berries.

Ocotea comata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
