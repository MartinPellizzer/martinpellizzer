Orchis mascula subsp. pinetorum

Orchis mascula subsp.

pinetorum, commonly known as the pine woods orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the northeastern United States. It typically grows in moist, acidic soils within pine forests and other woodlands.

The plant features a single, erect stem that can reach up to 30 cm in height, bearing a single, showy, violet-blue flower. Its roots are adapted to nutrient-poor conditions, and it forms symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi.

The species is noted for its delicate appearance and ecological significance in forest ecosystems.
