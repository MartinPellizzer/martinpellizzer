Oreobatus deliciosus

Oreobatus deliciosus, commonly known as the Mexican firecracker cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in bright red, spiny areoles. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its adaptations include succulent stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
