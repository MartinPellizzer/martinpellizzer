Odontoglossum crispum var. reginae

Odontoglossum crispum var. reginae, commonly known as the Queen's orchid, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its upright, leafy stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of violet or purple flowers with a distinctive, crinkled petal structure. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
