Ophrys × capellae-pacis

Ophrys × capellae-pacis, commonly known as the Capella-Pais orchid, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys scaposa.

It is characterized by its intricate, bee-mimicking flowers that play a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in temperate regions with well-drained soil and partial shade. Its floral structure includes a labellum that resembles a female bee, attracting male bees for pollination.

This species is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and ecological significance.
