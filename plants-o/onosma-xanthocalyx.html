Onosma xanthocalyx

Onosma xanthocalyx, commonly known as the yellow-flowered onosma, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

The flowers are tubular and attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
