Oxalis zuccariniana

Oxalis zuccariniana, commonly known as the yellow wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Oxalis zuccariniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
