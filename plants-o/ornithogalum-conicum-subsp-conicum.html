Ornithogalum conicum subsp. conicum

Ornithogalum conicum subsp.

conicum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It features a short, rhizomatous stem and produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
