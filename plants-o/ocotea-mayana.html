Ocotea mayana

Ocotea mayana, commonly known as the Honduras ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America and northern South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurel and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, lance-shaped leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, dark berries that contain seeds, and the tree can grow up to 30 meters tall.

Ocotea mayana is often found in humid lowland forests and is valued for its timber and medicinal properties.
