Oxytropis adamsiana subsp. erecta

Oxytropis adamsiana subsp.

erecta, commonly known as the tall milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its erect growth habit, with slender stems that can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale yellow to cream in color, arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

This species is adapted to harsh environments, exhibiting deep root systems and drought tolerance.
