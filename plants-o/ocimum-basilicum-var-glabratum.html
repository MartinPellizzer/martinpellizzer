Ocimum basilicum var. glabratum

Ocimum basilicum var. glabratum, commonly known as wild basil, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color due to the presence of fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry habitats.

This variety is valued for its aromatic leaves and is used in culinary and medicinal applications.
