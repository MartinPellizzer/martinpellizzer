Ophioglossum vulgatum var. crotalophoroides

Ophioglossum vulgatum var. crotalophoroides, commonly known as the snake fern, is a rare and ancient fern species belonging to the family Ophioglossaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, narrow, and often arching fronds that resemble the shape of a serpent, hence its common name. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are clustered in a unique, tongue-like structure at the tip of the frond. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically in temperate regions with high humidity.

Due to its rarity and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
