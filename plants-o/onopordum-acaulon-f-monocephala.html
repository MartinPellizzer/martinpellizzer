Onopordum acaulon f. monocephala

Onopordum acaulon f. monocephala, commonly known as the single-headed thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and a single, large, spiny flower head at the top of a single stem. The plant produces yellow flowers that eventually develop into purple to reddish-brown bracts. Its leaves are typically gray-green with a woolly texture and are covered in fine hairs.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and hardy nature.
