Oxalis bojeriana

Oxalis bojeriana, commonly known as the Bojer's wood sorrel, is a species of flowering plant in the family Oxalidaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade conditions. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, and the flowers typically bloom in spring.

Oxalis bojeriana is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
