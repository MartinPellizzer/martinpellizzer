Obetia laciniata

Obetia laciniata, commonly known as the laceleaf cycad, is a unique cycad species native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, intricately divided leaves that give it a feathery appearance. The plant produces large, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs. Its slow growth rate and long lifespan make it a valuable addition to botanical collections.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
