Oxalis carminea

Oxalis carminea, commonly known as the crimson wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive red-veined pattern, and its flowers are typically pink to red, resembling those of the common wood sorrel. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight. It is often found in woodland edges and shaded gardens, where it spreads via rhizomes.

Oxalis carminea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
