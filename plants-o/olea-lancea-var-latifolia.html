Olea lancea var. latifolia

Olea lancea var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf olea, is a variant of the Olea lancea species native to specific regions in Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by small, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers a sunny to partially shaded environment.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
