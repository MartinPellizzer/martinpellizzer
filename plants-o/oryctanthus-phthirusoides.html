Oryctanthus phthirusoides

Oryctanthus phthirusoides, commonly known as the narrow-leafed oxalis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive and low-maintenance addition to native plant collections.
