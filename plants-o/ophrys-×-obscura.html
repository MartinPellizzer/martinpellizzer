Ophrys × obscura

Ophrys × obscura, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Ophrys apifera (the bee orchid) and Ophrys insectifera (the wasp orchid), resulting in a unique combination of traits from both parents. The plant is characterized by its intricate, bee-like flowers that feature a prominent lip resembling a female bee, which plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. These flowers are typically greenish-yellow with brown markings, mimicking the appearance of a bee to attract male bees for pollination.

Ophrys × obscura is often found in calcareous grasslands and is a protected species in several European countries due to its rarity and ecological significance.
