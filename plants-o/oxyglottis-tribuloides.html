Oxyglottis tribuloides

Oxyglottis tribuloides, commonly known as the spurred gentian, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodlands and has a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers feature prominent spurred lobes, which are adaptations for attracting specific pollinators.

Oxyglottis tribuloides is often found in shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
