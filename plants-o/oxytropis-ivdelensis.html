Oxytropis ivdelensis

Oxytropis ivdelensis, commonly known as the Ivdel locoweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems and small, compound leaves. The plant produces yellowish to reddish flowers in late spring, which are typically arranged in dense clusters. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is considered a harmful plant to livestock.
