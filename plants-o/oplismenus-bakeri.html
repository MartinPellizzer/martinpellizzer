Oplismenus bakeri

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to waterlogged soils. Its foliage is grass-like, with narrow, grass-green leaves that form dense clumps.

Oplismenus bakeri produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring, which are clustered in spikelets and are often overlooked due to their modest appearance.
