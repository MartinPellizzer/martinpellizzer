Ophiorrhiza laevifolia

Ophiorrhiza laevifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leaved mangrove, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a halophyte, meaning it thrives in saline environments, often found in mangrove ecosystems along tidal mudflats and brackish water areas. The plant has small, simple leaves that are typically smooth and glossy, with a distinctive opposite arrangement. Its flowers are small and white, clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, round berries as fruit.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
