Onoseris ovalifolia

Onoseris ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf goldenhead, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant features yellow ray flowers and a central disk of tubular florets, typical of the genus Onoseris. Its oval-shaped leaves are alternate and often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.
