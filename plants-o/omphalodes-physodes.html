Omphalodes physodes

Omphalodes physodes, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically violet or blue in color. The plant has lanceolate leaves that are usually hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
