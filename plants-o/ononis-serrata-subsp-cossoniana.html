Ononis serrata subsp. cossoniana

Ononis serrata subsp.

cossoniana, commonly known as the serrated sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically thrives in dry, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

The plant features small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer, and its leaves are narrow, serrated, and arranged in a basal rosette. It has a compact growth habit and is well adapted to arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils.
