Oxalis semiloba subsp. semiloba

Oxalis semiloba subsp.

semiloba, commonly known as the heartleaf wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States. It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets, often adorned with reddish veins, and produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its stems are often prostrate or ascending, and it produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds. Oxalis semiloba subsp.

semiloba is characterized by its ornamental foliage and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
