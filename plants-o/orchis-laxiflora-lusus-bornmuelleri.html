Orchis laxiflora lusus bornmuelleri

Orchis laxiflora lusus bornmuelleri, commonly known as the Lusus orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to specific regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring. The plant has a complex root system and relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient absorption, a common trait among orchids. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, this orchid is considered endangered in many areas.
