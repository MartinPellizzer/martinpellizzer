Oxalis organensis

Oxalis organensis, commonly known as the Organ Mountains oxalis, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring, producing vibrant yellow or orange flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky habitats.

Oxalis organensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
