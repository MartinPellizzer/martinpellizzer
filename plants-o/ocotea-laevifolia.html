Ocotea laevifolia

Ocotea laevifolia, commonly known as the white sand ocotea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, clustered white flowers. Its fruit is a dark blue drupe that contains a single seed.

Ocotea laevifolia is often found in humid, lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
