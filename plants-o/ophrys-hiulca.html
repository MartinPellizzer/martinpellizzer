Ophrys hiulca

Ophrys hiulca, commonly known as the orchid bee orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its distinctive floral structure, which mimics the appearance of female bees to attract male bees for pollination. The plant produces showy, bee-like flowers with intricate patterns and colors that enhance its mimicry. It thrives in Mediterranean climates and is often found in rocky or limestone soils.

Ophrys hiulca is a rare and specialized orchid, showcasing the fascinating adaptations seen in orchid pollination strategies.
