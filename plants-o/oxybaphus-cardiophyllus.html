Oxybaphus cardiophyllus

Oxybaphus cardiophyllus, commonly known as the heart-leaved baphus, is a rare and distinctive plant native to specific regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale blue or purple in color. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a valuable species in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
