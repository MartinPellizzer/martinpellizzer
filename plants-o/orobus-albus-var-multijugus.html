Orobus albus var. multijugus

Orobus albus var. multijugus, commonly known as the white orobrush, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and North Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or bean family, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional herbal uses.
