Oxymitra obtusifolia

Oxymitra obtusifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved oxymitra, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features robust, hairy stems and broad, ovate leaves with a waxy surface. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in clusters, and attract various pollinators.

Oxymitra obtusifolia is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands.
