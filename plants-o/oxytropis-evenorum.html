Oxytropis evenorum

Oxytropis evenorum, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada locoweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils at high elevations, often in subalpine or alpine habitats. Oxytropis evenorum is known for its ability to thrive in harsh, nutrient-poor environments, making it an important component of its native ecosystem.
