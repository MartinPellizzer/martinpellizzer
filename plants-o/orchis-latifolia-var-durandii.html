Orchis latifolia var. durandii

Orchis latifolia var. durandii, commonly known as the broad-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks. The plant features a rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces tall, flowering stems with clusters of violet to purple flowers. Its flowers are characterized by a distinctive lip that is often adorned with intricate patterns.

This orchid is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
