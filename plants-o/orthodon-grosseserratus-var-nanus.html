Orthodon grosseserratus var. nanus

Orthodon grosseserratus var. nanus, commonly known as the dwarf orthodon, is a compact, low-growing plant native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, serrated leaves and small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
