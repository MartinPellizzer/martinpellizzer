Oxalis sellowii

Oxalis sellowii, commonly known as the Sellow's wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive red veins and small, five-petaled yellow flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.

Oxalis sellowii is also valued for its ability to thrive in shaded garden environments.
