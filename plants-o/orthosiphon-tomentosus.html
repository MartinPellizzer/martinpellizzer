Orthosiphon tomentosus

Orthosiphon tomentosus, commonly known as hairy horsegram, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which are often covered in fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in spikes, followed by small, woody pods containing several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Orthosiphon tomentosus is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
