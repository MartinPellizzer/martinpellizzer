Oenothera serrulata subsp. pinifolia

Oenothera serrulata subsp.

pinifolia, commonly known as the alpine evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It is characterized by its narrow, serrated leaves and produces yellow flowers that open in the evening.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. It is adapted to cold climates and has a long flowering period during the summer months.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more elongated leaves and a more compact growth habit compared to other members of the species.
