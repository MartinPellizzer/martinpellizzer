Olearia flavescens

Olearia flavescens, commonly known as the yellow flax or coastal flax, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and yellow-orange flower heads. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils and salty air. Olearia flavescens produces small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in spring and summer.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
