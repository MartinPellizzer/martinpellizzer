Oenothera biennis subsp. chicaginensis

Oenothera biennis subsp.

chicaginensis, commonly known as the Chicago evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the Midwestern United States. It features showy yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers are typically 1 to 3 inches in diameter, with five petals that reflex outward.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in prairies and disturbed habitats.
