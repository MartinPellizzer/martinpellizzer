Ophiorrhiza patula

Ophiorrhiza patula, commonly known as the small-leaf marsh mallow, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in wet or marshy habitats. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are usually less than 2 cm in length. Its flowers are pale yellow and are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Ophiorrhiza patula is known for its adaptability to waterlogged soils and plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
