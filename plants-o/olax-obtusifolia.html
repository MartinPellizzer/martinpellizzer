Olax obtusifolia

Olax obtusifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved olax, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually hairy and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, flat seeds.

Olax obtusifolia is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and stream banks.
