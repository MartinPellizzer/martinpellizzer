Opuntia amyclaea

Opuntia amyclaea, commonly known as the red-tipped prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in spines and glochids, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months, followed by edible fruits known as tunas. Its succulent stems store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

Opuntia amyclaea plays an important role in its ecosystem, providing food and shelter for various desert animals.
