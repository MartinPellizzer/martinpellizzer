Osbeckia racemosa

Osbeckia racemosa, commonly known as the common osbeckia, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant features alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with hairy surfaces. Its flowering stems bear dense, terminal clusters of small, yellowish flowers.

Osbeckia racemosa is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in wetland environments.
