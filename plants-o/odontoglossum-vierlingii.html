Odontoglossum vierlingii

Odontoglossum vierlingii, commonly known as the Vierling's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces dark green, strap-like leaves and clusters of violet to purple flowers with intricate patterns. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Odontoglossum vierlingii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a suitable climate.
