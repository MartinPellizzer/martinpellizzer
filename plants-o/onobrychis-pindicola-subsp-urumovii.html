Onobrychis pindicola subsp. urumovii

Onobrychis pindicola subsp.

urumovii, commonly known as the Pindus vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is adapted to dry, temperate climates. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological and agricultural studies in its native range.
