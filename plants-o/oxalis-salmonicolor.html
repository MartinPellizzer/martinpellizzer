Oxalis salmonicolor

Oxalis salmonicolor, commonly known as the salmon-colored wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive salmon-pink coloration, which sets it apart from other oxalis species. The plant produces small, delicate white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique foliage and delicate floral display.
