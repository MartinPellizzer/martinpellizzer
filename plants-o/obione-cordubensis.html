Obione cordubensis

Obione cordubensis, commonly known as the Cordoba orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to southern Spain.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its blooming period occurs between May and July, making it a notable flora in the region's biodiversity.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
