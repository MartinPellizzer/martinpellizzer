Oryza sativa var. allachabadica

Oryza sativa var. allachabadica, commonly known as the Allachabadica rice variety, is a traditional rice cultivar native to India.

This variety is characterized by its slender grains and moderate yield potential. It is typically grown in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring a warm and humid environment for optimal growth. The plant has a short stature with a compact panicle, and it matures relatively quickly compared to other rice varieties.

Its botanical features make it suitable for specific regional agricultural practices and culinary uses.
