Oberonia muriculata

Oberonia muriculata, commonly known as the hairy orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers with prominent, hairy sepals and petals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining potting media.

Oberonia muriculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
