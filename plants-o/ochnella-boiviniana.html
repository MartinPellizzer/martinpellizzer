Ochnella boiviniana

Ochnella boiviniana, commonly known as the Boivin's ochna, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by distinctive orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
