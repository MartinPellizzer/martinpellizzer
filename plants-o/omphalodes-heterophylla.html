Omphalodes heterophylla

Omphalodes heterophylla, commonly known as the variable bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive blue or purple hue. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, violet, or white, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its unique leaf morphology and striking floral display contribute to its ornamental value in horticulture.
