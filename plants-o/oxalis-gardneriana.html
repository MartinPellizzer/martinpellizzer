Oxalis gardneriana

Oxalis gardneriana, commonly known as the pink buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Oxalis gardneriana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
