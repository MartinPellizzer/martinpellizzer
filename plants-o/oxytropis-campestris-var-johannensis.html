Oxytropis campestris var. johannensis

Oxytropis campestris var. johannensis, commonly known as the Johann's field lupine, is a flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, spindly stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-shaped flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It is native to central and eastern Europe and thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

This variety is known for its ornamental value and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
