Ooia grabowskii

Ooia grabowskii, commonly known as the Grabowski's ghost fern, is a unique fern species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale green fronds that resemble ghostly apparitions, hence its common name. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture and high levels of atmospheric humidity. Its reproductive structures, including spore-producing sorus, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Ooia grabowskii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
