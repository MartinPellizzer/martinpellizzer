Oxalis lunulata var. violacea

Oxalis lunulata var. violacea, commonly known as the violet wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each leaflet featuring a distinctive white or pale green marking. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically pink or violet in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
