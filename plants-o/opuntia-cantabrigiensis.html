Opuntia cantabrigiensis

Opuntia cantabrigiensis, commonly known as the Cambridge prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers, which are followed by edible, fleshy fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting well to poor soil conditions.

Opuntia cantabrigiensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
