Odontoglossum psittacinum

Odontoglossum psittacinum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers with a distinctive greenish throat and red markings. The plant typically grows in high-altitude cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its leaves are broad and elliptical, often with a waxy texture to help retain moisture.

Odontoglossum psittacinum is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
