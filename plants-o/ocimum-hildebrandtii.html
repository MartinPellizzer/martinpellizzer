Ocimum hildebrandtii

Ocimum hildebrandtii, commonly known as the Hildebrandt's basil, is a species of flowering plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Ocimum hildebrandtii is valued for its ornamental appeal and aromatic properties.
