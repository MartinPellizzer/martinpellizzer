Orchis sambucina var. candida

Orchis sambucina var. candida, commonly known as the white helleborine, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem that can reach up to 1 meter in height, bearing lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces a cluster of white to pale pink flowers with a distinctive three-lobed lip. It thrives in moist, shaded woodland habitats and is typically found in Europe and parts of Asia.

Orchis sambucina var. candida is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
