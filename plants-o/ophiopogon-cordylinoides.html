Ophiopogon cordylinoides

Ophiopogon cordylinoides, commonly known as the Chinese grass or Japanese sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color, and it produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and its ability to form dense ground covers.

The species is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
