Oreodaphne tristis var. oblongifolia

Oreodaphne tristis var. oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed oreodaphne, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical to oblong in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
