Oncostemum neriifolium

Oncostemum neriifolium, commonly known as the blackleaf oncostemum, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features simple, alternate leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and pale underneath, with a distinctive blackish hue when dried. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its propagation.

Oncostemum neriifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
