Odontoglossum constrictum var. pallens

Odontoglossum constrictum var. pallens, commonly known as the pale-leaf orchid, is a species native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often pale green or bluish in color. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale purple, typically in late spring or early summer. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
