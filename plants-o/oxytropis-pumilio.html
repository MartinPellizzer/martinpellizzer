Oxytropis pumilio

Oxytropis pumilio, commonly known as the dwarf locoweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, and its leaves are typically small and hairy.
