Onosmodium carolinianum

Onosmodium carolinianum, commonly known as Carolina marsh mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils. The plant features slender, trailing stems that produce clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is characterized by its showy, showy inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer.
