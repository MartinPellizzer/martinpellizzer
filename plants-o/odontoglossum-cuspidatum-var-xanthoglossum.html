Odontoglossum cuspidatum var. xanthoglossum

Odontoglossum cuspidatum var. xanthoglossum, commonly known as the golden-lipped orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vivid yellow lips and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in high-altitude cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its flowers are borne on tall, slender stems and are prized for their ornamental value in horticulture.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a challenging but rewarding plant for experienced orchid growers.
