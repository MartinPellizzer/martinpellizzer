Ozothamnus × expansifolius

Ozothamnus × expansifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaf goldenbush, is a hybrid shrub native to the coastal regions of southern California.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has a compact, bushy growth habit. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a silvery or grayish underside.

Ozothamnus × expansifolius is adapted to coastal climates and is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
