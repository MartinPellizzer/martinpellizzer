Oleandra musifolia

Oleandra musifolia, commonly known as the musical leaf oleander, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often described as having a musical or rhythmic appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It is a member of the Apocynaceae family, which includes other ornamental and medicinal plants.

Oleandra musifolia is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and is adapted to thrive in warm, humid climates.
