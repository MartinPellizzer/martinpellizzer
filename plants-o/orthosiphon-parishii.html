Orthosiphon parishii

Orthosiphon parishii, commonly known as the Java mint or mountain mint, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, serrated leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom during the summer months. Orthosiphon parishii is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides.

It is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
