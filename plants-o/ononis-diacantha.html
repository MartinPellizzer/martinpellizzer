Ononis diacantha

Ononis diacantha, commonly known as the two-toothed sand-verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial plant characterized by its hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces yellow or reddish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a rough texture.

Ononis diacantha is adapted to sandy and gravelly soils, making it a common sight in dry, coastal areas.
