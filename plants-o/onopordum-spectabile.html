Onopordum spectabile

Onopordum spectabile, commonly known as the Douglas aster, is a perennial shrub native to western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky flower heads that range in color from purple to pink, appearing in late summer to early fall. The plant has silvery-gray foliage with fuzzy, lance-shaped leaves that are aromatic when crushed. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for drought-tolerant gardens.

Its showy blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.
