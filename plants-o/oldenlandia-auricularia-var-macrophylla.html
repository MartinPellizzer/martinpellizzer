Oldenlandia auricularia var. macrophylla

Oldenlandia auricularia var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved earleaf, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, ear-shaped leaves that are typically larger than those of the standard species. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the rainy season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural collections.
