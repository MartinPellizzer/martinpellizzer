Ornithochilus simondianus

Ornithochilus simondianus, commonly known as the bird's beak orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, elongated flower that resembles a bird's beak. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are strap-like and often covered in a fine, hairy texture.

The orchid produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer.
