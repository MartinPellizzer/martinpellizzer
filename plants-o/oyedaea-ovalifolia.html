Oyedaea ovalifolia

Oyedaea ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical studies.
