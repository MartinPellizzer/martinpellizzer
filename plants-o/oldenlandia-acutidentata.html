Oldenlandia acutidentata

Oldenlandia acutidentata, commonly known as the sharp-toothed spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its erect stems and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom throughout the year. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a hairy texture, while the flowers have five petals and numerous stamens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and abandoned fields.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
