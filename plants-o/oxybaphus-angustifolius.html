Oxybaphus angustifolius

Oxybaphus angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved oxybaphus, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are clustered in terminal inflorescences, and it is known for its aromatic foliage.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
