Oxalis polymorpha var. phaseoloides

Oxalis polymorpha var. phaseoloides, commonly known as the common wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish or purple coloration on the underside, and it produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers. The plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in woodland environments, preferring partial shade and moist, well-drained soil.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
