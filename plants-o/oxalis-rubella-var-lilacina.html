Oxalis rubella var. lilacina

Oxalis rubella var. lilacina, commonly known as the lilac-stem oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish-brown coloration and produces clusters of small, delicate flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a versatile addition to various garden settings.

Oxalis rubella var. lilacina is also noted for its ability to thrive in containers, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
