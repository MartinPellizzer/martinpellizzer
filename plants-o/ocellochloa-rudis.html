Ocellochloa rudis

Ocellochloa rudis, commonly known as the rough stoloniferous grass, is a perennial grass species native to various regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and elongated, narrow leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Ocellochloa rudis is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its ability to form dense mats makes it an important component of certain ecosystems.
