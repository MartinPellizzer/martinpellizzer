Ochnella mauritiana

Ochnella mauritiana, commonly known as the Mauritius ochna, is a small evergreen tree native to the island of Mauritius.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lanceolate leaves with a slightly hairy underside. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal forests and secondary growth areas.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species and is of interest for conservation efforts.
