Onosma targevarensis

Onosma targevarensis, commonly known as the Targevaren's hedge mustard, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with four petals.

Onosma targevarensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
