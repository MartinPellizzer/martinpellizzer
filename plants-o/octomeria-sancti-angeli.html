Octomeria sancti-angeli

Octomeria sancti-angeli, commonly known as the San Angelo octomeria, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from violet to red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
