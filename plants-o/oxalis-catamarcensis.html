Oxalis catamarcensis

Oxalis catamarcensis, commonly known as the Catamarcensis wood sorrel, is a species native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and grasslands. It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is known for its attractive foliage and delicate blossoms.

Oxalis catamarcensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in partial shade.
