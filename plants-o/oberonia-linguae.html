Oberonia linguae

Oberonia linguae, commonly known as the tongue orchid, is a species of flowering plant in the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including the Philippines and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its slender, pendant-like inflorescence and small, showy flowers that often resemble a tongue in shape. Oberonia linguae typically grows as an epiphyte on trees, thriving in humid, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular specimen among orchid enthusiasts and botanists alike.
