Odontoglossum heterosepalum

Odontoglossum heterosepalum, commonly known as the Ecuadorian orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a rhizomatous growth habit and produces erect, branched stems that bear numerous flowers. The flowers are typically violet to purple in color, with sepals and petals that are often marked with contrasting patterns. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil for optimal growth.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
