Orchis purpurea var. gracilis

Orchis purpurea var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender purple orchid, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodlands and meadows. The plant features a tall, slender stem with a single, showy violet or purple flower that blooms in late spring. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor environments.

The orchid is noted for its delicate appearance and is often found in shaded, woodland habitats.
