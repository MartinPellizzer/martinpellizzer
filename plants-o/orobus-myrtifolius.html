Orobus myrtifolius

Orobus myrtifolius, commonly known as the myrtle-leaved orobus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive purple or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid soils and is well adapted to dry climates. Its leaves have a fragrant, myrtle-like scent, contributing to its common name.

Orobus myrtifolius is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
