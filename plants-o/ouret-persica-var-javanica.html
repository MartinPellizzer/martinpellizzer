Ouret persica var. javanica

Ouret persica var. javanica, commonly known as the Javanese peach.

It is a variant of the peach tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly Java. The plant is characterized by its deciduous nature, with small, ovate leaves that turn yellow before falling in autumn. Its flowers are typically pink or white, and it produces small, fuzzy fruits that are edible.

The botanical features of this variety make it a subject of interest in horticultural studies.
