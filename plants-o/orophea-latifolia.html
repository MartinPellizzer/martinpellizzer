Orophea latifolia

Orophea latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf orophea, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its palm-like appearance with large, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces striking yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Orophea latifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
