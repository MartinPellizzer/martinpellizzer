Oxycoca macrocarpa

Oxycoca macrocarpa, commonly known as the Pacific yew, is a coniferous tree native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its reddish-brown bark, needle-like leaves, and distinctive red, fleshy berries. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall and thrives in moist, temperate climates. Its wood is lightweight and has been historically used in various applications, including the production of chemotherapy drugs.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
