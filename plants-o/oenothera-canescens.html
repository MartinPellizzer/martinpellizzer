Oenothera canescens

Oenothera canescens, commonly known as plains evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and sandy soils. The plant produces showy yellow or orange flowers that open in the evening, hence its common name. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, giving them a grayish appearance.

Oenothera canescens is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions and is often used in restoration projects due to its ecological resilience.
