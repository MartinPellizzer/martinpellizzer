Ornithogalum pedunculare

Ornithogalum pedunculare, commonly known as the white bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces tall, unbranched stems with bell-shaped, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant is characterized by its showy inflorescence, which blooms in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Ornithogalum pedunculare is also known for its toxic properties, containing compounds that can be harmful if ingested.
