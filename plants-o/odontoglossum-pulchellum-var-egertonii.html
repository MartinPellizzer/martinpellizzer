Odontoglossum pulchellum var. egertonii

Odontoglossum pulchellum var. egertonii, commonly known as the Egerton's orchid, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of South America, particularly in the Andes.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its mountainous, often humid environment. Its leaves are usually dark green and strap-like, and it prefers well-drained soil with partial shade.

This variety is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and relatively manageable care requirements.
