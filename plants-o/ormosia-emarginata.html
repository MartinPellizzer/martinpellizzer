Ormosia emarginata

Ormosia emarginata, commonly known as the Malayan ormosia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces woody pods that contain several elliptical seeds. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its timber, which is used in furniture and construction.

The tree also plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and contributing to soil fertility through nitrogen fixation.
