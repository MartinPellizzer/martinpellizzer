Onosma rhodopaea

Onosma rhodopaea, commonly known as the Greek ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its drought-resistant characteristics.

Onosma rhodopaea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
