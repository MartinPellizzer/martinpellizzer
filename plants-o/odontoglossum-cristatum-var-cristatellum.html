Odontoglossum cristatum var. cristatellum

Odontoglossum cristatum var. cristatellum, commonly known as the cristate orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flattened, and often ribbed pseudobulbs, which store water and nutrients. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. It thrives in cool, humid environments with indirect light, making it a popular choice for greenhouse cultivation.

Due to its unique appearance and hybrid origin, it is highly valued in horticultural collections.
