Oenothera macrocarpa subsp. oklahomensis

Oenothera macrocarpa subsp.

oklahomensis, commonly known as the Oklahoma evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the central United States. It is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that open in the evening and close by morning.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in sandy or loamy soils and is often found in open habitats such as prairies and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and native plant gardens.
