Ophioglossum lancifolium

Ophioglossum lancifolium, commonly known as the snake fern, is a rare and ancient plant belonging to the family Ophioglossaceae.

It is characterized by its unique, lance-shaped leaves that are often divided into two distinct lobes, resembling a snake's head. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are clustered in a terminal, fleshy structure called a sorus. It is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices.

Due to its limited distribution and specialized habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in many regions.
