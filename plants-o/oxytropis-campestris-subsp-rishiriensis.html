Oxytropis campestris subsp. rishiriensis

Oxytropis campestris subsp.

rishiriensis, commonly known as the Rishiri oxytropis, is a perennial herb native to Japan, specifically the island of Rishiri. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to coastal and alpine environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its native range.
