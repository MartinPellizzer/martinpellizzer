Osteospermum glaberrimum

Osteospermum glaberrimum, commonly known as the woolly osteospermum, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, daisy-like flowers with bright yellow centers and purple-pink petals. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has a compact, bushy habit. Its leaves are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a woolly appearance.

Osteospermum glaberrimum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
