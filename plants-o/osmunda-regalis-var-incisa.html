Osmunda regalis var. incisa

Osmunda regalis var. incisa, commonly known as the cut-leaved royal fern, is a variant of the royal fern characterized by its distinctive, finely serrated fronds.

It is a perennial fern that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread and form dense colonies. Its fronds are bipinnate, with a broad, upright structure that contributes to its ornamental appeal.

The spore-bearing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds, a typical feature of ferns.
