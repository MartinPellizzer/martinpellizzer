Ornithogalum melancholicum

Ornithogalum melancholicum, commonly known as the black lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, pale green or white flowers. The plant is characterized by its bulbous root system, which stores nutrients and water. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or rocky areas.

Due to its striking appearance and ecological significance, it is valued in both horticulture and conservation efforts.
