Orbeopsis lutea subsp. vaga

Orbeopsis lutea subsp.

vaga, commonly known as the yellow orbeopsis, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa. It is characterized by its delicate, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months.

The plant typically grows in shrubland and coastal areas, preferring well-drained soils and sunny conditions. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Orbeopsis lutea subsp.

vaga is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
