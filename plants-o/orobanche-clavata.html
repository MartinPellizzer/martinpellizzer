Orobanche clavata

Orobanche clavata, commonly known as the common broomrape, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is found in various parts of Europe, Asia, and North Africa. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has a taproot system that allows it to attach to the roots of its host. It is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its ability to parasitize a wide range of host species.

Its life cycle is closely tied to the growth patterns of its host plants, making it an interesting subject in ecological studies.
