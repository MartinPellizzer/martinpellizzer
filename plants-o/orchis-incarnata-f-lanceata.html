Orchis incarnata f. lanceata

Orchis incarnata f. lanceata, commonly known as the lance-leaved fairy wand, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

This plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of pale pink to purple flowers. The lance-shaped leaves are arranged in a whorl along the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands and wet meadows.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
