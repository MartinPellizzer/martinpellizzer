Olea europaea var. regalis

Olea europaea var. regalis, commonly known as the royal olive, is a variety of the common olive tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and small, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by edible fruits that are typically harvested when they are fully ripe. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

The royal olive is also valued for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a source of oil.
