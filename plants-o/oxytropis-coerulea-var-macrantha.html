Oxytropis coerulea var. macrantha

Oxytropis coerulea var. macrantha, commonly known as blue locoweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its blue to purple flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has small, compound leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Its botanical features make it an important part of the local ecosystem, supporting pollinators and providing forage for wildlife.
