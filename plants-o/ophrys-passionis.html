Ophrys passionis

Ophrys passionis, commonly known as the Passion Flower Orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, butterfly-like flower structure with a distinctive labellum that mimics the appearance of a female bee, aiding in pollination through sexual deception. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories where it thrives on the nutrients of decaying organic matter. Its flowers are ephemeral, blooming for only a few days before wilting, making it a rare sight in the wild.

Due to habitat loss and overcollection, Ophrys passionis is considered endangered and is protected in many regions.
