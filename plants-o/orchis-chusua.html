Orchis chusua

Orchis chusua, commonly known as the common twayblade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, lance-shaped leaves and its striking, pale pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and meadows, where it thrives in well-drained soil. Orchis chusua has a rhizomatous root system and produces small, greenish flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer.

It plays an important role in its ecosystem by supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
