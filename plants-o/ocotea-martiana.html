Ocotea martiana

Ocotea martiana, commonly known as the white sand tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and fragrant bark. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in lowland rainforests. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces drupes that are an important food source for various wildlife.

The tree is valued for its timber and has traditional uses in local medicine.
