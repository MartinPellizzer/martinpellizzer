Ocotea oligoneura

Ocotea oligoneura, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces berries that are edible for some bird species but toxic to humans and domestic animals. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its aromatic wood has been used historically for various purposes, including as a source of essential oils.
