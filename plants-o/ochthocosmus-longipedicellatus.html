Ochthocosmus longipedicellatus

Ochthocosmus longipedicellatus, commonly known as the long-pedicelled broom.

It is a shrub native to southern Africa, characterized by its dense, branched growth habit and bright yellow flowers. The plant features small, opposite leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a pioneer species in disturbed environments.
