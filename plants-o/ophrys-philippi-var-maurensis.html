Ophrys philippi var. maurensis

Ophrys philippi var. maurensis, commonly known as the Philippi's bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its intricate floral structure, which closely resembles a female bee, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brownish in color, with a distinctively shaped lip that mimics the appearance of a bee's body.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species in many regions.
