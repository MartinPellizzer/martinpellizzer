Otoba acuminata

Otoba acuminata, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible but often have a spicy taste. Otoba acuminata is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
