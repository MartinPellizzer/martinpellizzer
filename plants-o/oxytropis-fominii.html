Oxytropis fominii

Oxytropis fominii, commonly known as the Fomin's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in steppes and rocky soils.

Oxytropis fominii is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.
