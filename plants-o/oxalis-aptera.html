Oxalis aptera

Oxalis aptera, commonly known as the common wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial with heart-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in moist, well-drained soil. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Oxalis aptera is known for its ability to tolerate a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland areas.
