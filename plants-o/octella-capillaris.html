Octella capillaris

Octella capillaris, commonly known as the slender hairgrass, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its fine, grass-like foliage. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in humid conditions. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in dense clusters, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.

Octella capillaris is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to prevent erosion.
