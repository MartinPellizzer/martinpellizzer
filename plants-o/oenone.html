Oenone

Oenone, commonly known as the meadow saffron, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features delicate, trifoliate leaves and is often found in moist meadows and woodland edges. Oenone is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it has been historically used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
