Odontoglossum alvarezii

Odontoglossum alvarezii, commonly known as the Alvarez's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, violet-blue flowers with intricate patterns and a unique, elongated lip. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial orchid, often found in cloud forests at high altitudes. Its botanical features include pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, supporting its growth in cool, moist environments.

Odontoglossum alvarezii is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts worldwide.
