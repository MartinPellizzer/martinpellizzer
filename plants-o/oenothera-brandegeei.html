Oenothera brandegeei

Oenothera brandegeei, commonly known as the evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening, hence its common name. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers produce small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Oenothera brandegeei is adapted to arid environments and is often found in disturbed soils and rocky areas.
