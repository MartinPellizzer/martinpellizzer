Orchis strictiflora

Orchis strictiflora, commonly known as the rigid-flowered orchid, is a species native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features a tall, upright stem that can reach up to 1 meter in height, bearing a cluster of showy, violet-blue flowers. Its root system consists of a dense network of rhizomes and tubers, which help store nutrients and water.

Orchis strictiflora is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
