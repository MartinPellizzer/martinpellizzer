Origanum × pabotii

Origanum × pabotii, commonly known as the Greek oregano, is a hybrid shrub native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, gray-green leaves that are densely covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for Mediterranean climates.

Its essential oils, rich in compounds like carvacrol, are widely used in culinary and medicinal applications.
