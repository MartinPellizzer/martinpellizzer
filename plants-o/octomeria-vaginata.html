Octomeria vaginata

Octomeria vaginata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf morning glory, is a tropical plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is native to Central and South America and is often found in moist, disturbed habitats. Its stems are hairy and tend to climb or trail along the ground.

Octomeria vaginata is also known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
