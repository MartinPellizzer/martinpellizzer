Orchis latifolia var. macrochlamys

Orchis latifolia var. macrochlamys, commonly known as the broad-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features a basal rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces a tall flowering stem with clustered, purple or violet flowers. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
