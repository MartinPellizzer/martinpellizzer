Odontoglossum crispatulum

Odontoglossum crispatulum, commonly known as the curly lip orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andean highlands of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy, and often curly sepals and petals, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has a short, stocky rhizome. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with a showy lip that is often curled or fringed.

Odontoglossum crispatulum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
