Ononis natrix subvar. atlantica

Ononis natrix subvar. atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic sandwort, is a perennial herb native to coastal areas of the Atlantic coast.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with small, fleshy leaves adapted to sandy and saline environments. The plant produces clusters of white or pink flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dunes and rocky shores.

Its botanical features make it an important component of coastal plant communities.
