Oldenlandia echinulosa var. echinulosa

Oldenlandia echinulosa var. echinulosa, commonly known as the hairy spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and covered with fine hairs, giving the plant a slightly fuzzy appearance. The flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn and are often accompanied by small, green bracts.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and along stream banks.
