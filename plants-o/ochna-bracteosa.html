Ochna bracteosa

Ochna bracteosa, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall. The plant is characterized by its vibrant orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
