Odontoglossum insleayi

Odontoglossum insleayi, commonly known as the Insley's orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial with erect, slender stems that bear a single spike of flowers. The flowers are typically white to pale pink, with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its specialized pollination mechanisms.

This plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil for optimal growth.
