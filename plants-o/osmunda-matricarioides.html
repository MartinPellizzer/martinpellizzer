Osmunda matricarioides

Osmunda matricarioides, commonly known as the common water fern, is a member of the Osmundaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive fronds that resemble those of the water clover.

It is a perennial fern that typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and stream banks, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant has a well-developed rhizome system that helps anchor it in aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, which are arranged in a spiral pattern, a feature typical of many ferns.

Osmunda matricarioides plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
