Oryza sativa var. ferganica

Oryza sativa var. ferganica, commonly known as the Fergana rice, is a wild rice species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, elongated grains. This variety is adapted to grow in wetland environments and is often found in the Fergana Valley. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a panicle-like inflorescence.

Oryza sativa var. ferganica is of interest to botanists and agricultural researchers due to its potential for genetic diversity and resilience to environmental stress.
