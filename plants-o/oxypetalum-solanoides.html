Oxypetalum solanoides

Oxypetalum solanoides, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.
