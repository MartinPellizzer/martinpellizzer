Ophrys × calypsus var. pseudoapulica

Ophrys × calypsus var. pseudoapulica, commonly known as the Apulian bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its intricate floral structure, which mimics the appearance of female bees to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its flowers are usually a mix of yellow and brown, with a distinctive bee-like shape and markings.

This orchid is a rare and protected species, often found in specific habitats across southern Europe.
