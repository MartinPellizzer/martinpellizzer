Omphalodes rupestris subsp. lojkae

Omphalodes rupestris subsp.

lojkae, commonly known as the Lojka's rock bell, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe. It thrives in rocky and gravelly soils, typically in alpine or subalpine habitats.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens.
