Oxalis divaricata var. scandens

Oxalis divaricata var. scandens, commonly known as the climbing wood sorrel, is a perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with reddish veins and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a trailing or climbing growth habit, making it suitable for hanging baskets or as a ground cover. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, requiring regular watering during the growing season.

Its ornamental value comes from its delicate foliage and prolonged flowering period.
