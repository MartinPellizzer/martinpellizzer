Ornithogalum × gugliae

Ornithogalum × gugliae, commonly known as the hybrid star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its erect, spindle-shaped bulbs and clusters of white or pale blue star-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and produces small, bell-shaped berries that turn red when mature. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its elegant appearance and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
