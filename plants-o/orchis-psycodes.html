Orchis psycodes

Orchis psycodes, commonly known as the fairy orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often pale pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in woodland or grassland habitats, thriving in shaded, moist environments. Its roots are fibrous, and it produces showy, spurred orchid flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its beauty.
