Olivaea leptocarpa

Olivaea leptocarpa, commonly known as the slender-leaved olive, is a species of flowering plant in the family Oleaceae.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in clusters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Olivaea leptocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
