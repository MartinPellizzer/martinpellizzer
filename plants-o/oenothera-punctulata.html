Oenothera punctulata

Oenothera punctulata, commonly known as the dotted evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers that open in the evening, and its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Oenothera punctulata is also known for its ability to attract nocturnal pollinators like moths.
