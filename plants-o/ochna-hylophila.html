Ochna hylophila

Ochna hylophila, commonly known as the African butter tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by distinctive, orange-red berries containing a single seed. Ochna hylophila is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions for its wood and latex.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
