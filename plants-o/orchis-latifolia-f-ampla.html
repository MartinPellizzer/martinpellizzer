Orchis latifolia f. ampla

Orchis latifolia f. ampla, commonly known as the broad-leaved orchid, is a variant of the broad-leaved orchid species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves and showy, violet or purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers are often clustered in loose racemes, and the leaves are lanceolate with a prominent midrib.

This orchid is part of the Orchidaceae family and is noted for its ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
