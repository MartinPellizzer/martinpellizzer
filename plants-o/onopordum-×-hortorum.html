Onopordum × hortorum

Onopordum × hortorum, commonly known as the garden thistle, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue-green leaves and purple to pink composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is native to southern Europe and is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its architectural form and long-lasting blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
