Oryza sativa var. isochroa

Oryza sativa var. isochroa, commonly known as the early rice variety, is a subspecies of rice characterized by its shorter growth cycle and earlier maturation compared to other rice varieties.

It is typically cultivated in regions with shorter growing seasons, such as parts of Southeast Asia and the Philippines. The plant has a compact stature with narrow leaves and produces small, fragrant grains that are often used in traditional cuisines. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a panicle inflorescence that bears numerous spikelets.

This variety is valued for its adaptability to diverse soil conditions and its role in sustainable rice farming practices.
