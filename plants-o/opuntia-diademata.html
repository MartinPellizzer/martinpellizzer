Opuntia diademata

Opuntia diademata, commonly known as the desert nightshade cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flattened, ribbed stems that are covered in spiny areoles, which are unique to the Cactaceae family. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it stores water in its thick, fleshy stems for survival in arid environments.

Opuntia diademata plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing food and shelter for various desert animals.
