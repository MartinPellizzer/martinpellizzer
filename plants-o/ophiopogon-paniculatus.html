Ophiopogon paniculatus

Ophiopogon paniculatus, commonly known as the Japanese spathiphyllum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features rhizomatous growth and produces dense, grass-like foliage that is often used in ornamental landscaping. The plant blooms with clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that are arranged in paniculate inflorescences. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for both garden and horticultural purposes.
