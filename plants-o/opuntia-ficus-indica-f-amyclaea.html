Opuntia ficus-indica f. amyclaea

Opuntia ficus-indica f. amyclaea, commonly known as the Canary Island prickly pear, is a cultivar of the prickly pear cactus native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its large, flat, paddle-shaped stems that are covered in spines and glochids. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers, followed by edible, red-fleshed fruits known as tunas. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental beauty and its use in traditional cuisine and agriculture.
