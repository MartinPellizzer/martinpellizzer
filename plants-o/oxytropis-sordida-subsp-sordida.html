Oxytropis sordida subsp. sordida

Oxytropis sordida subsp.

sordida, commonly known as the black locoweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and northern Mexico. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils, often in sagebrush steppe and desert environments.

The plant produces small, yellow to green pea-like flowers, which are clustered in dense, upright inflorescences. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The species is noted for its ability to thrive in arid conditions and is often found in association with other drought-tolerant plants.
