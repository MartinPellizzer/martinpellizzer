Oligodorella teretifolia

Oligodorella teretifolia, commonly known as the slender-leaved orchid, is a species of orchid native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its root system is specialized for nutrient absorption, and it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for successful germination.

Oligodorella teretifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
