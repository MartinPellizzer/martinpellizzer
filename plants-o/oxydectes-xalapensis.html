Oxydectes xalapensis

Oxydectes xalapensis, commonly known as the Mexican rock cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded stem that often branches into a shrub-like form. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Oxydectes xalapensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
