Ocotea tsaratananensis

Ocotea tsaratananensis, commonly known as the Madagascar laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, clustered flowers that bloom in white or pale yellow. Its fruits are small, woody drupes that contain a single seed.

Ocotea tsaratananensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat and food for various local wildlife species.
