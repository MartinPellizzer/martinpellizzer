Omphalobium gaudichaudii

Omphalobium gaudichaudii, commonly known as the New Zealand clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant has a fibrous root system and is often found in moist, well-drained soils in temperate regions. It is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it an important plant in ecological restoration projects.

Its ornamental value also makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
