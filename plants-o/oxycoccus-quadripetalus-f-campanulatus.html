Oxycoccus quadripetalus f. campanulatus

Oxycoccus quadripetalus f. campanulatus, commonly known as the bell-shaped cranberry, is a variant of the cranberry plant characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers.

It is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the cold regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically harvested in the fall. Its botanical features include dark green, leathery leaves and a compact, mat-forming growth habit.

This form is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique floral structure.
