Oxydectes origanifolia

Oxydectes origanifolia, commonly known as the lemon verbena, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, finely toothed leaves that emit a strong lemon scent when crushed. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are often used in herbal teas and as a culinary herb due to their distinctive flavor.

Oxydectes origanifolia thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental and medicinal gardens.
