Osyris divaricata

Osyris divaricata, commonly known as the African glorybower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of showy, yellow flowers with red or orange stamens. Osyris divaricata is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.
