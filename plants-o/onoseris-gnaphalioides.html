Onoseris gnaphalioides

Onoseris gnaphalioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring.

It is a perennial herb that plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native environment.
