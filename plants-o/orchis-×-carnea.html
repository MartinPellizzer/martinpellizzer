Orchis × carnea

Orchis × carnea, commonly known as the pink orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Europe.

It is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in woodland habitats and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and grass-like, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to its natural environment.

Orchis × carnea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
