Oxyacanthus uva-crispa

Oxyacanthus uva-crispa, commonly known as the red-fruit hawthorn, is a flowering shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and often used in jams and jellies. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a glossy surface and serrated edges.

Oxyacanthus uva-crispa thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.
