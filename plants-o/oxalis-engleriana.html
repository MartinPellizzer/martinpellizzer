Oxalis engleriana

Oxalis engleriana, commonly known as the Engler's wood sorrel, is a flowering plant belonging to the Oxalidaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Oxalis engleriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
