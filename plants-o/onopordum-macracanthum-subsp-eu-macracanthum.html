Onopordum macracanthum subsp. eu-macracanthum

Onopordum macracanthum subsp.

eu-macracanthum, commonly known as the European thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It features a robust, spiny stem that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, with large, silvery-green, lobed leaves.

The plant produces showy purple to violet flower heads, which are surrounded by bracts that are often mistaken for petals. It is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in grasslands and rocky soils.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
