Oncidium miradorense

Oncidium miradorense, commonly known as the Mirador orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in humid, shaded forest understories. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowering stems. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, with intricate patterns and a strong scent to attract pollinators.

Oncidium miradorense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
