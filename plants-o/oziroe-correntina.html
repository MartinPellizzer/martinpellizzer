Oziroe correntina

Oziroe correntina, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are arranged in terminal inflorescences. Oziroe correntina is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species that thrives in well-drained soils.

It is also noted for its milky sap, which is toxic and can cause skin irritation.
