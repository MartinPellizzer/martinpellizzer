Omphalobium comans

Omphalobium comans, commonly known as the narrow-leafed clover, is a perennial herb native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant has a fibrous root system and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils. It is valued for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, improving soil fertility.

Its growth habit and ecological role make it an important species in both agricultural and natural settings.
