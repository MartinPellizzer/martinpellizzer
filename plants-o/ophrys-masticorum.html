Ophrys masticorum

Ophrys masticorum, commonly known as the Mastic Orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of female wasps to attract male wasps for pollination. The plant produces showy, bee-pollinated flowers with a distinctive coloration and pattern. It typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and is often found in open woodlands or scrublands.

Ophrys masticorum is a rare and protected species, valued for its ecological and botanical significance.
