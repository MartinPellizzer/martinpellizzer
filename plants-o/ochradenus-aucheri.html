Ochradenus aucheri

Ochradenus aucheri, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or arid environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, blue or purple bell-shaped flowers. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

Ochradenus aucheri is also noted for its resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
