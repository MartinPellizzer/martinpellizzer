Oreocnide obovata var. paradoxa

Oreocnide obovata var. paradoxa, commonly known as the Paradoxical Obovate Oreocnide, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves that are often densely covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its growth habit is compact, with multiple branches forming a dense, bushy structure.

It is often found in moist, shaded forest environments, where it thrives in the understory.
