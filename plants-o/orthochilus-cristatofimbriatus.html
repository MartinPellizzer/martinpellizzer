Orthochilus cristatofimbriatus

Orthochilus cristatofimbriatus, commonly known as the crested fringed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers with distinctive fringed petals and a spurred lip. It typically grows in rocky or sandy habitats and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
