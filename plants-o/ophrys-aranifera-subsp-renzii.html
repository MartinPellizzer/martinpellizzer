Ophrys aranifera subsp. renzii

Ophrys aranifera subsp.

renzii, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in sunny, well-drained environments. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish in color, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Due to its specific habitat requirements and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species in many areas.
