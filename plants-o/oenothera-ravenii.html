Oenothera ravenii

Oenothera ravenii, commonly known as the Raven's nightshade, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Oenothera, native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, deep purple to black flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and silvery-green, often covered in fine hairs that help reduce water loss.

Oenothera ravenii is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soils, making it a resilient and hardy species.
