Olea capensis subsp. hochstetteri

Olea capensis subsp.

hochstetteri, commonly known as the Hochstetter's olive, is a subspecies native to South Africa. It is a small evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a leathery texture and a fragrant, small greenish-yellow flower cluster. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that turns dark purple when ripe.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and aesthetic qualities.
