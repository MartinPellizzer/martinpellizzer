Oenothera triloba

Oenothera triloba, commonly known as the three-lobed evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves with three distinct lobes. It thrives in sandy or disturbed soils and is often found in open habitats like prairies and roadsides.

Oenothera triloba is also known for its medicinal properties, with its seeds containing essential fatty acids.
