Oreosyce parvifolia

Oreosyce parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved oreosyce, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. Oreosyce parvifolia is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for botanists studying tropical flora.
