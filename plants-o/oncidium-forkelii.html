Oncidium forkelii

Oncidium forkelii, commonly known as the Yellow Butterfly Orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, yellow and green flowers that resemble butterflies. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. It requires bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.

Oncidium forkelii is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
