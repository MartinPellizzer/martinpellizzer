Oxalis cineracea

Oxalis cineracea, commonly known as the ash-gray wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features trifoliate leaves with a pale green to ash-gray coloration, and its flowers are typically pale yellow with five petals. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often consumed by birds and small mammals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Oxalis cineracea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and flowers.
