Opuntia vitelliniflora subsp. interjecta

Opuntia vitelliniflora subsp.

interjecta, commonly known as the Mexican prickly pear, is a cacti species native to Mexico. It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in spines and glochids.

The plant produces yellow or orange flowers, which are typically followed by red, fleshy fruits known as tunas. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing high adaptability to hot and dry climates.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and use in traditional cuisine and medicine.
