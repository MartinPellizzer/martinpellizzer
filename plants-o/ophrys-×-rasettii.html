Ophrys × rasettii

Ophrys × rasettii, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant that exhibits unique botanical features.

It is a natural hybrid between Ophrys apifera, the bee orchid, and Ophrys rasetta, another species within the Ophrys genus. This plant is characterized by its intricate, bee-like floral structure, which plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The orchid's labellum mimics the appearance of a female bee, attracting male bees for mating behavior.

Ophrys × rasettii typically grows in calcareous soils and is found in various parts of Europe, where it thrives in open, sunny habitats.
