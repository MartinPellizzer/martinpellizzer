Oenothera tenuifolia

Oenothera tenuifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and showy yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Oenothera tenuifolia is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.
