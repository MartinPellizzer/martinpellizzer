Orobus ochroleucus

Orobus ochroleucus, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellowish-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil.

Orobus ochroleucus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
