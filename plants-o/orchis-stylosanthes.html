Orchis stylosanthes

Orchis stylosanthes, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe and is characterized by its tall, slender stem and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its roots are often mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to enhance nutrient uptake.

Orchis stylosanthes is noted for its ecological importance, supporting various pollinators and contributing to biodiversity in its native range.
