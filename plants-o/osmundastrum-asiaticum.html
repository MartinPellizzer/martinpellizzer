Osmundastrum asiaticum

Osmundastrum asiaticum, commonly known as the Asian water fern, is a member of the family Osmundaceae and is characterized by its feathery, dichotomous fronds.

It is a perennial aquatic or semi-aquatic fern that thrives in wetland environments, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that helps it anchor in moist soil and absorb nutrients. Its fronds are typically dark green and can grow up to 30 centimeters in length, with a delicate, lacy appearance.

Osmundastrum asiaticum plays an important ecological role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and small aquatic organisms.
