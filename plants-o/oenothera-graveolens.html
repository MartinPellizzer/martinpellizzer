Oenothera graveolens

Oenothera graveolens, commonly known as the stinking nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-to-orange flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Oenothera graveolens is also known for its strong, unpleasant odor, which is most noticeable in the evening.
