Origanum dictamnifolium

Origanum dictamnifolium, commonly known as mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments. Its essential oils contain compounds like carvacrol and thymol, which contribute to its strong scent and medicinal uses.

It is valued in both culinary and traditional herbal practices for its flavor and potential health benefits.
