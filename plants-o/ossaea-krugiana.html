Ossaea krugiana

Ossaea krugiana, commonly known as the Krug's ice plant, is a succulent species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps it conserve water in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in regions with mild winters.
