Oncidium batemanii

Oncidium batemanii, commonly known as the Bateman's orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It features a sympodial growth habit with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences with showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red. Its foliage consists of long, narrow, dark green leaves that are adapted for photosynthesis in its natural habitat.

Oncidium batemanii is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.
