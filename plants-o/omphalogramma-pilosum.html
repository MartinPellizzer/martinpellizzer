Omphalogramma pilosum

Omphalogramma pilosum, commonly known as the hairy omphalogramma, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, and its pods are typically hairy as well. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
