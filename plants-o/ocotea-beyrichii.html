Ocotea beyrichii

Ocotea beyrichii, commonly known as the yellow sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and fragrant wood. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by spherical, dark purple berries. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils, which have been used in traditional medicine.

Ocotea beyrichii is valued for its durable wood and is often found in moist lowland forests.
