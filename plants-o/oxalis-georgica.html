Oxalis georgica

Oxalis georgica, commonly known as the Georgia wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive red veins, and small, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and along stream banks. It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its ability to thrive in partial to full shade.

Oxalis georgica is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
