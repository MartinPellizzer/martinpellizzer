Oritrophium peruvianum subsp. lineatum

Oritrophium peruvianum subsp.

lineatum, commonly known as the Peruvian plume poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America. It is characterized by its striking, feathery inflorescences that resemble plumes, typically in shades of pink to red.

The plant thrives in high-altitude environments, often growing in rocky or alpine habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are sturdy and upright.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
