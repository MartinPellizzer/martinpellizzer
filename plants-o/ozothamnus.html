Ozothamnus

Ozothamnus, commonly known as the golden everlasting or silverleaf daisy, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

These plants are native to Australia and are characterized by their compact, shrub-like growth habit and small, daisy-like flowers. The leaves are typically silver or gray-green, often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. Ozothamnus species are drought-tolerant and thrive in well-drained soils, making them suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
