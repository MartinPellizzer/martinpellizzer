Ostrya americana

Ostrya americana, commonly known as American hop-hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its small, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, yellowish-green catkins that are borne in clusters, and its bark is smooth and grayish-brown when young. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and can reach heights of up to 20 meters.

Ostrya americana is valued for its hardy nature and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental appeal.
