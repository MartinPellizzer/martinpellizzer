Oxytropis dinarica

Oxytropis dinarica, commonly known as Dinaric oxytropis, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or limestone-rich soils, often in mountainous regions. The plant features slender stems and small, compound leaves that are adapted to arid or semi-arid conditions. Its flowers are usually pale blue or violet, arranged in racemes, and it produces small, pod-like fruits.

Oxytropis dinarica is found primarily in the Dinaric Alps and surrounding areas of Southeast Europe.
