Ophrys × zinsmeisteri

Ophrys × zinsmeisteri, commonly known as the Zinsmeister's bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking, bee-shaped flower that mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and thrives in sunny, open habitats. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brown, with intricate patterns that enhance the mimicry.

Ophrys × zinsmeisteri is a rare and protected species, often found in specific microhabitats across the Alps and adjacent regions.
