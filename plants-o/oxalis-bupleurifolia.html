Oxalis bupleurifolia

Oxalis bupleurifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a touch of color to its foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Oxalis bupleurifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.
