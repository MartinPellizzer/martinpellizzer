Obetia aldabrensis

Obetia aldabrensis, commonly known as Aldabra prickly pear, is a succulent plant native to the Aldabra Atoll in the Seychelles.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, paddle-shaped stems that store water efficiently. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, followed by edible red fruits. Its adaptations to arid environments make it a resilient species in coastal and sandy habitats.

Obetia aldabrensis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife.
