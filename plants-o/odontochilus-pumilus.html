Odontochilus pumilus

Odontochilus pumilus, commonly known as the dwarf toothed orchid, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces delicate, fragrant flowers with a unique toothed lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually elliptical and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
