Origanum majoranoides

Origanum majoranoides, commonly known as sweet marjoram, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are fragrant and used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Sweet marjoram thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal cultivation.
