Oncidium saltabundum

Oncidium saltabundum, commonly known as the Shining Orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange, often with intricate patterns. The plant has pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in varying environmental conditions. Its leaves are usually broad and strap-like, providing structural support and facilitating photosynthesis.

Oncidium saltabundum is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in greenhouse settings.
