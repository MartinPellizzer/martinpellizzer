Oxytropis davisii

Oxytropis davisii, commonly known as Davis' locoweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as sagebrush steppe and desert areas. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Oxytropis davisii is also known for its toxic properties, making it potentially harmful to livestock.
