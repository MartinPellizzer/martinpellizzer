Ochna natalitia

Ochna natalitia, commonly known as Natal ochna, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal umbels, followed by distinctive orange to red berries. Ochna natalitia is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and fruit.
