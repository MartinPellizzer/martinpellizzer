Oxalis glabra

Oxalis glabra, commonly known as white wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with three heart-shaped leaflets, often adorned with a white or pale green bloom. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pink, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Oxalis glabra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in woodland gardens.
