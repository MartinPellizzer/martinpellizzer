Oryza sativa var. paracastroi

Oryza sativa var. paracastroi, commonly known as the Javanese rice, is a wild rice species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated grains and is typically found in wetland environments. This variety is notable for its genetic diversity and potential value in rice breeding programs. It has a shorter growth cycle compared to domesticated rice varieties.

The plant exhibits traits that may contribute to stress tolerance, making it an important resource for agricultural research.
