Odontoglossum facetum

Odontoglossum facetum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces striking, violet to blue-violet flowers with a distinctive three-lobed lip. The plant typically grows in high-altitude cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to anchor in rocky or humus-rich soil.

Odontoglossum facetum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
