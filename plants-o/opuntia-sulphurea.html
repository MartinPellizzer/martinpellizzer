Opuntia sulphurea

Opuntia sulphurea, commonly known as the yellow prickly pear, is a cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flattened, fleshy stems that are often covered in bright yellow spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tunas, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

Opuntia sulphurea is well adapted to arid environments and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife.
