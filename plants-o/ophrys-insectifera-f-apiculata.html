Ophrys insectifera f. apiculata

Ophrys insectifera f. apiculata, commonly known as the bee orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive flower shape that closely resembles a bee, a feature evolved to mimic female bees and attract male bees for pollination. The plant produces a single, erect spike bearing several showy, greenish-brown flowers with intricate patterns. It thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

Due to its specialized pollination mechanism and habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in many regions.
