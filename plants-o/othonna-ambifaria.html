Othonna ambifaria

Othonna ambifaria, commonly known as the two-sided othonna, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and fleshy, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Othonna ambifaria is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
