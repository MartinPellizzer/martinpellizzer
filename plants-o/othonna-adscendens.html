Othonna adscendens

Othonna adscendens, commonly known as the climbing othonna, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its ascending growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are often surrounded by bracts. Its leaves are typically narrow and may have a woolly texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Othonna adscendens is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid conditions.
