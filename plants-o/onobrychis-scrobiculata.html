Onobrychis scrobiculata

Onobrychis scrobiculata, commonly known as the yellow needle grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features slender, upright stems with small, needle-like leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, blooming in early spring.

Onobrychis scrobiculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
