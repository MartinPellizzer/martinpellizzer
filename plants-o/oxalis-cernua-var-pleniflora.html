Oxalis cernua var. pleniflora

Oxalis cernua var. pleniflora, commonly known as the clustered wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and prefers partial to full shade. Its foliage and flowers are often adorned with subtle pink or red markings, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Oxalis cernua var. pleniflora is valued in gardens for its charming appearance and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
