Ostericum scaberulum var. longiinvolucellatum

Ostericum scaberulum var. longiinvolucellatum, commonly known as the long-involucelleted wood sorrel, is a variant of the wood sorrel family (Oxalidaceae).

It is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology, featuring elongated, pubescent stipules that give it its specific epithet. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are usually small and white or yellow, arranged in clusters.

The species is noted for its hirsute (hairy) stems and leaves, which contribute to its unique botanical appearance.
