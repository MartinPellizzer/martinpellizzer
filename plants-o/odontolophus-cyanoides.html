Odontolophus cyanoides

Odontolophus cyanoides, commonly known as the blue hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers, which are typically tubular with five lobes. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Odontolophus cyanoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
