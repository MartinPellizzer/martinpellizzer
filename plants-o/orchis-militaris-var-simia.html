Orchis militaris var. simia

Orchis militaris var. simia, commonly known as the monkey orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that often resemble a monkey's face, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers are usually purple or violet in color, with a unique structure that includes a spurred lip.

Orchis militaris var. simia is a perennial species that blooms in late spring to early summer.
