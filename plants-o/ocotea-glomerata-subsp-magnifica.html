Ocotea glomerata subsp. magnifica

Ocotea glomerata subsp.

magnifica, commonly known as the magnolia-leafed ocotea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family. It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in countries like Brazil and Colombia.

The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves that resemble those of magnolia trees, giving it its common name. It produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the summer months.

The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall and is often found in humid, lowland forests.
