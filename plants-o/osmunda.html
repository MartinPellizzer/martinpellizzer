Osmunda

Osmunda, commonly known as the royal fern, is a genus of ferns characterized by its distinctive, tall, and often branched fronds.

It belongs to the family Osmundaceae and is known for its primitive features, including the presence of true roots and a well-developed vascular system. Osmunda species are typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. They have a unique spore-producing structure called a sorus, which is located on the underside of their fronds.

These plants play an important ecological role in their habitats by contributing to soil stability and providing shelter for various organisms.
