Ononis procurrens var. fallax

Ononis procurrens var. fallax, commonly known as the fallax variety of everlasting or sweet everlasting, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features dense clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, creeping stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and along roadsides.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
