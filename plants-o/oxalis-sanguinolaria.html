Oxalis sanguinolaria

Oxalis sanguinolaria, commonly known as the bloodleaf oxalis, is a species of flowering plant in the family Oxalidaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its distinctive red-veined leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Oxalis sanguinolaria is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
