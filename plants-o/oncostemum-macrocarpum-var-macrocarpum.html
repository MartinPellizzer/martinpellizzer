Oncostemum macrocarpum var. macrocarpum

Oncostemum macrocarpum var. macrocarpum, commonly known as the giant false hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often reaching up to 10 cm in diameter. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. Its fruit is a distinctive, fleshy, three-lobed capsule that turns red when mature.

Oncostemum macrocarpum var. macrocarpum thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found growing in coastal areas and disturbed soils.
