Ocotea paradoxa

Ocotea paradoxa, commonly known as the Paradoxical Ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elliptical, dark blue fruits. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils, making it valuable in traditional medicine and as a source of aromatic compounds.

Ocotea paradoxa is often found in humid, lowland forests and is valued for its ecological and potential commercial significance.
