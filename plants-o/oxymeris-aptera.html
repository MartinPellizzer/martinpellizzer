Oxymeris aptera

Oxymeris aptera, commonly known as the slender tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and distinctive pod fruits. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered in racemes, typically appearing in yellow or greenish-yellow hues.

The fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, and the tree is often valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
