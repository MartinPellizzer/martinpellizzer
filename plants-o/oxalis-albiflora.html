Oxalis albiflora

Oxalis albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive heart-shaped base and produces small, white, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its stems are often prostrate or ascending, and it has a fibrous root system.

Oxalis albiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and flowers.
