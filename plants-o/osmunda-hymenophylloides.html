Osmunda hymenophylloides

Osmunda hymenophylloides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are typically submerged or floating in freshwater environments. The plant has a complex life cycle involving both spore-producing and vegetative growth stages. Its rhizomes form dense mats that help stabilize aquatic sediments.

Osmunda hymenophylloides plays a role in maintaining water quality by filtering nutrients and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
