Ophiorrhiza mycetiifolia

Ophiorrhiza mycetiifolia, commonly known as the "mushroom leaf" or "fungus leaf" plant, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, velvety, and often dark green leaves that resemble the texture and appearance of mushrooms. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and swampy areas. Its flowers are small and clustered, often white or pale yellow, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Ophiorrhiza mycetiifolia is also noted for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils, making it an important species in certain ecological contexts.
