Olea chrysophylla var. cuspidata

Olea chrysophylla var. cuspidata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved olive, is a variant of the olive tree native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are typically gray-green in color and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into small, edible drupes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the subtropical climate of its native habitat.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in the region.
