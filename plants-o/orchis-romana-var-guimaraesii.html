Orchis romana var. guimaraesii

Orchis romana var. guimaraesii, commonly known as the Guimaraes orchid, is a rare and endemic orchid species native to Portugal.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive violet to purple flowers with a greenish-yellow lip. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands. It has a short, hairy stem and produces a single or few flowers during the spring season.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern.
