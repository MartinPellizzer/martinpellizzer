Olearia telmatica

Olearia telmatica, commonly known as the coastal daisybush, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit. The plant features silver-grey foliage and produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Olearia telmatica thrives in coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and strong winds.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
