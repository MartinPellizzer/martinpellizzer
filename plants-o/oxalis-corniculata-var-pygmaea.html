Oxalis corniculata var. pygmaea

Oxalis corniculata var. pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf wood sorrel, is a small, herbaceous perennial plant native to Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish or purple tinge, and small, five-petaled flowers that are typically yellow or orange. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

However, it can be invasive in certain regions if not managed properly.
