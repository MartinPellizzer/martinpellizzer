Ophrys ariadnae

Ophrys ariadnae, commonly known as the Ariadne's Orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which closely resembles the appearance of female wasps, a trait known as pseudocopulation, which aids in pollination by male wasps. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone-rich habitats, often in high-altitude environments. Its flowers are usually pale green or brown, with intricate patterns that mimic the wasp's body and wings.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Ophrys ariadnae is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
