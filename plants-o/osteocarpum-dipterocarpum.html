Osteocarpum dipterocarpum

Osteocarpum dipterocarpum, commonly known as the black ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark wood and is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family, which includes many valuable timber species. Its leaves are typically opposite and ovate, with a rough texture.

The tree produces small, clustered flowers and fruits that are adapted for animal dispersal.
