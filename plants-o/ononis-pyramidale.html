Ononis pyramidale

Ononis pyramidale, commonly known as the pyramid speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit with slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, often with a slightly rough texture.

Ononis pyramidale thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, woodland edges, and disturbed sites.
