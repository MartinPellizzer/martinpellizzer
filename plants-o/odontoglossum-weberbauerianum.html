Odontoglossum weberbauerianum

Odontoglossum weberbauerianum, commonly known as the Weberbauer's orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulbous structure that stores water and nutrients. The plant features striking, showy flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
