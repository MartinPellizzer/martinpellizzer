Oxalis lyonii

Oxalis lyonii, commonly known as Lyon's wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial with trifoliate leaves resembling those of true sorrels. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Oxalis lyonii is often found in open woodlands and rocky areas, where it contributes to the local flora diversity.
