Oxalis pringlei

Oxalis pringlei, commonly known as the Pringle's wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces distinctive seed pods that split open to release seeds, a feature typical of the genus Oxalis. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Oxalis pringlei is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
