Ocotea ucayalensis

Ocotea ucayalensis, commonly known as the Amazonian lauraceae, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, small flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, berry-like structures that contain aromatic seeds.

Ocotea ucayalensis plays a significant role in the ecological balance of the rainforest, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
