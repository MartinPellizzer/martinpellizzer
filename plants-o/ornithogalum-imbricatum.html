Ornithogalum imbricatum

Ornithogalum imbricatum, commonly known as the star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is characterized by its bulbous underground stem, which stores nutrients and water. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Ornithogalum imbricatum is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral display.
