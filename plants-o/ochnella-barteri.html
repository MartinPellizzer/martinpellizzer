Ochnella barteri

Ochnella barteri, commonly known as the Barter's ochna, is a small, deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
