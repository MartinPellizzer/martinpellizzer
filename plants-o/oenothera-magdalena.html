Oenothera magdalena

Oenothera magdalena, commonly known as the Magdalena evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Oenothera magdalena is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinator populations.
