Osmundastrum

Osmundastrum, commonly known as the royal fern, is a genus of ferns belonging to the family Osmundaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, robust fronds that can reach up to 2 meters in height, featuring a distinctive central vein and a broad, feather-like structure. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizome is thick and fleshy, which helps it survive in wet conditions.

Osmundastrum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
