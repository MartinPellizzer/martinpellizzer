Ocotea tunquiensis

Ocotea tunquiensis, commonly known as the Tunqui laurel, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are usually glossy and arranged alternately along the stems. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, dark berries.

Ocotea tunquiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
