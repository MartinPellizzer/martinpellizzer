Ophrys pallida

Ophrys pallida, commonly known as the pale bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which closely resembles a female bee, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant produces a single, showy flower with a pale greenish-yellow coloration and intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of a bee. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes, often in areas with well-drained soils.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, Ophrys pallida is considered a vulnerable species and is protected in several European countries.
