Onosma pseudoarenaria subsp. lucana

Onosma pseudoarenaria subsp.

lucana, commonly known as the Lucan onosma, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of pale blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its flowering period occurs mainly in spring, and it is often found in coastal areas and inland cliffs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
