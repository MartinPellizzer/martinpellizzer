Odontoglossum lindleyi

Odontoglossum lindleyi, commonly known as the Lindley's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant produces pendulous, violet or purple flowers with a distinctive, spurred lip. It prefers cool, humid environments and is often found growing in mountainous areas with well-drained soil.

Odontoglossum lindleyi is a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts due to its ornamental value and relatively manageable care requirements.
