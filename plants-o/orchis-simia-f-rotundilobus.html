Orchis simia f. rotundilobus

Orchis simia f. rotundilobus, commonly known as the Monkey Orchid, is a rare and distinctive orchid species characterized by its unique floral structure.

It features showy, violet-blue flowers with a prominent lip that resembles a monkey's face, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodland areas, preferring well-drained, calcareous soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, aiding in water retention.

This orchid is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is noted for its specialized pollination mechanisms involving specific insect species.
