Odontoglossum crispum var. latranum

Odontoglossum crispum var. latranum, commonly known as the Latranum orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and purple. Its inflorescence typically consists of several blooms arranged in a raceme.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
