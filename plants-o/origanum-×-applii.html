Origanum × applii

Origanum × applii, commonly known as Greek oregano, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly fuzzy leaves that are rich in essential oils, giving it a strong, pungent scent. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its medicinal and culinary uses have made it a valued plant in both traditional and modern applications.
