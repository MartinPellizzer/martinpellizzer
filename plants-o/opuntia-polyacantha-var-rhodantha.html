Opuntia polyacantha var. rhodantha

Opuntia polyacantha var. rhodantha, commonly known as the red-flowered prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, fleshy pads and vibrant red flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and full sun exposure. Its pads are edible and can be used in culinary applications, while the flowers are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in harsh landscapes.
