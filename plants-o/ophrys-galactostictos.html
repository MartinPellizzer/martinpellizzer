Ophrys galactostictos

Ophrys galactostictos, commonly known as the milk-stained bee orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its flowers are predominantly yellow with distinctive white markings, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing its seeds.

Ophrys galactostictos is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its specialized pollination mechanism.
