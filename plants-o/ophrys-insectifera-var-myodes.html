Ophrys insectifera var. myodes

Ophrys insectifera var. myodes, commonly known as the bee orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its intricate flower structure that mimics the appearance of a female bee, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, thriving in calcareous soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while the flowers are borne in dense spikes.

This variant is particularly noted for its pale yellow to greenish flowers, which are less showy than the typical bee orchid but equally fascinating in their evolutionary adaptation.
