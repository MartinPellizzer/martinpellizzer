Ophrys × palladinoi

Ophrys × palladinoi, commonly known as the Palladino's bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is characterized by its intricate floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is found in the Mediterranean region, particularly in Italy. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brown, with intricate patterns that aid in mimicry.

Ophrys × palladinoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems as a pollinator attractant.
