Olea

Olea, commonly known as the olive tree, is a flowering plant in the Oleaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, green fruit called olives, which are harvested for culinary and oil production. Olea europaea is the most well-known species, valued for its economic and cultural significance.

The plant is also known for its longevity, with some trees living over 1,000 years.
