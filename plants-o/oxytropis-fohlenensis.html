Oxytropis fohlenensis

Oxytropis fohlenensis, commonly known as the Fohlen's locoweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, spiny stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Oxytropis fohlenensis is also noted for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
