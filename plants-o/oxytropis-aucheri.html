Oxytropis aucheri

Oxytropis aucheri, commonly known as the Armenian locoweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers in globular clusters, which are typically visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Oxytropis aucheri is adapted to grow in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

It is also known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock.
