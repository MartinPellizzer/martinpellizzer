Orchis laxiflora var. intermedia

Orchis laxiflora var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate looseflower orchid, is a species native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, woodland habitats. The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces tall, slender flowering stems. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish, with a distinctive hooded lip.

This orchid is known for its ability to thrive in shaded environments and is often found in association with other orchid species.
