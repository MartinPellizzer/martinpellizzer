Oplismenus flavicomus

Oplismenus flavicomus, commonly known as the golden thread, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae).

It is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its fine, delicate leaves that are typically yellowish-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Native to the southeastern United States, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

Its ornamental value comes from its soft, flowing foliage, making it a popular choice for ground cover in landscaping.
