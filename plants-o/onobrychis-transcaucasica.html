Onobrychis transcaucasica

Onobrychis transcaucasica, commonly known as Caucasian clover, is a perennial leguminous plant native to the Transcaucasus region.

It features trifoliate leaves with oval to lanceolate leaflets and produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes. The plant is characterized by its robust root system and is often found in dry, rocky or sandy soils. It belongs to the genus Onobrychis within the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
