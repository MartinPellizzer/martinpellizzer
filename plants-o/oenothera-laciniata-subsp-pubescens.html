Oenothera laciniata subsp. pubescens

Oenothera laciniata subsp.

pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its subspecific name "pubescens.".

The plant produces showy yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
