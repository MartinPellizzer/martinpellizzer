Orchipedum plantaginifolium

Orchipedum plantaginifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved orchid, is a herbaceous plant characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is native to certain regions of Europe and Asia. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption in poor soils.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
