Oberonia subnavicularis

Oberonia subnavicularis, commonly known as the narrow-boat orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping rhizomes and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive three-lobed lip. It thrives in shaded, moist habitats such as mountain forests.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
