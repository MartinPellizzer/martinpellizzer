Onobrychis araxina

Onobrychis araxina, commonly known as the Armenian clover, is a perennial legume native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Onobrychis araxina is also valued for its potential in forage and soil stabilization due to its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
