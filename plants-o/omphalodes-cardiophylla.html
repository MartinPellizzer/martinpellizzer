Omphalodes cardiophylla

Omphalodes cardiophylla, commonly known as the heart-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
