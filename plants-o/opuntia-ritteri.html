Opuntia ritteri

Opuntia ritteri, commonly known as the Ritter's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems that are covered in spines and are often ribbed. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tunas, is edible and contains a sweet, juicy pulp.

Opuntia ritteri is adapted to arid environments and plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing food and shelter for various wildlife.
