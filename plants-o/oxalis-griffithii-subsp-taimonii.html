Oxalis griffithii subsp. taimonii

Oxalis griffithii subsp.

taimonii, commonly known as the Taimon oxalis, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically heart-shaped and have a distinctive reddish or purplish hue.

The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of yellow or orange, often clustered in umbels. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
