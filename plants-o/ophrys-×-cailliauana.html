Ophrys × cailliauana

Ophrys × cailliauana, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is native to southern Europe, particularly in regions like Spain and Portugal. The plant is characterized by its unique floral morphology, with the labellum resembling a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. Its flowers are typically yellow with a dark brown or black pattern, and it thrives in calcareous soils.

This hybrid is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
