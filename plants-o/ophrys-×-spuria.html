Ophrys × spuria

Ophrys × spuria, commonly known as the bee orchid hybrid, is a fascinating plant with intricate botanical features.

It is a natural hybrid between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera, exhibiting a blend of characteristics from both parent species. The plant produces showy, bee-like flowers that mimic the appearance of female bees, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. Its flowers are typically yellow with brown and white markings, and they emit a mild scent to further attract pollinators.

Ophrys × spuria is a perennial herbaceous plant that thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas across Europe.
