Oxalis reticulata

Oxalis reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial with trifoliate leaves that have a distinctive net-like venation pattern. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Oxalis reticulata is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
