Oryza sativa var. bicolorata

Oryza sativa var. bicolorata, commonly known as the colored rice or variegated rice, is a variant of the rice species characterized by its unique coloration.

This plant exhibits a range of color patterns on its grains, including red, brown, and black, which are due to the presence of anthocyanin pigments. The botanical features of Oryza sativa var. bicolorata include slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically green with a reddish or purplish tinge. It is primarily cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions for its ornamental and culinary value.

The plant's genetic diversity contributes to its adaptability and potential use in breeding programs for improved rice varieties.
