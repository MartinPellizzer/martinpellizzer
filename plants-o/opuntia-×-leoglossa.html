Opuntia × leoglossa

Opuntia × leoglossa, commonly known as the hybrid prickly pear, is a species resulting from the cross between Opuntia ficus-indica and Opuntia robusta.

It is a succulent cactus characterized by its flattened, fleshy stems called cladodes, which are often used for ornamental purposes. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible fruits known as tunas. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in arid and semi-arid climates.

Opuntia × leoglossa is also valued for its ability to store water and its role in traditional medicine and agriculture.
