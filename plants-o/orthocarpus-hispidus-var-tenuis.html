Orthocarpus hispidus var. tenuis

Orthocarpus hispidus var. tenuis, commonly known as the slender thornapple, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is part of the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other plants with similar botanical features.
