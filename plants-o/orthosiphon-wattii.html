Orthosiphon wattii

Orthosiphon wattii, commonly known as Malabar tea or Katjang, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in whorls along the stems. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Orthosiphon wattii thrives in tropical climates and is typically found in moist, shaded environments.
