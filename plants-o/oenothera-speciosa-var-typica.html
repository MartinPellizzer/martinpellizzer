Oenothera speciosa var. typica

Oenothera speciosa var. typica, commonly known as the showy evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, showy yellow flowers that open in the late afternoon and close by morning. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical seed pods that contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Oenothera speciosa var. typica is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
