Oncidium hecatanthum

Oncidium hecatanthum, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can reach up to 3 meters in length, making it one of the longest flowering stems in the orchid family. The plant produces vibrant, colorful flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom sequentially along the spike. Oncidium hecatanthum thrives in warm, humid environments with bright indirect light and well-draining soil.

It is a popular choice for both ornamental gardens and indoor cultivation due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
