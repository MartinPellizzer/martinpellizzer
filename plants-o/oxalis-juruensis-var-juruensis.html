Oxalis juruensis var. juruensis

Oxalis juruensis var. juruensis, commonly known as the Juruensis wood sorrel, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rainforests and undergrowth. It produces edible leaves and flowers, though they are often consumed sparingly due to their oxalic acid content.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
