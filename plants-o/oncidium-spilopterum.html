Oncidium spilopterum

Oncidium spilopterum, commonly known as the Golden Cattleya, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow and green flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Oncidium spilopterum is prized in horticulture for its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
