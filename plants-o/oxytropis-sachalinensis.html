Oxytropis sachalinensis

Oxytropis sachalinensis, commonly known as Sachalin locoweed, is a perennial herb native to the Russian Far East and northeastern China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to cold climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions. Its foliage consists of palmately compound leaves, and it produces cylindrical seed pods that contain small, kidney-shaped seeds.

Oxytropis sachalinensis is also known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock.
