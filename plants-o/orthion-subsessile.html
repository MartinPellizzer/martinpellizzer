Orthion subsessile

Orthion subsessile, commonly known as the slender orthion, is a small, herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or grassland habitats. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring. Its flowering stems are usually short and often appear to be nearly leafless.

Orthion subsessile is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbellate inflorescence and aromatic properties.
