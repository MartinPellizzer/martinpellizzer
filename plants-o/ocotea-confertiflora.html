Ocotea confertiflora

Ocotea confertiflora, commonly known as the Brazilian camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are followed by elliptical, dark blue-black berries. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils, which have been used in traditional medicine.

Ocotea confertiflora is also valued for its timber, which is durable and resistant to decay.
