Ocotea martinicensis

Ocotea martinicensis, commonly known as the Martinique ocotea, is a member of the Lauraceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Caribbean, particularly found in the Lesser Antilles. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, clustered flowers. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed surrounded by a fleshy outer layer.

The tree is valued for its wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making, and for its essential oils used in perfumery.
