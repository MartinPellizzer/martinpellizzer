Obione cinerea

Obione cinerea, commonly known as the ash-gray sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually a bluish-gray in color, giving it its common name. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in spikes and bloom in the summer months.

Obione cinerea is often found in wetland habitats and is valued for its ecological role in supporting insect populations.
