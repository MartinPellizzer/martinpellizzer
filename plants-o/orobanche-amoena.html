Orobanche amoena

Orobanche amoena, commonly known as the common broomrape, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is found in various regions across Europe, Asia, and North Africa. The plant produces small, often inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale in color. Its life cycle involves germinating near the roots of host plants and establishing a parasitic connection.

Orobanche amoena is known for its ability to cause significant damage to agricultural crops by reducing their growth and yield.
