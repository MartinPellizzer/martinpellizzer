Oenothera editicaulis

Oenothera editicaulis, commonly known as the prairie evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in open prairies and disturbed soils. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its stems are hairy and often branch near the base, while its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture.

Oenothera editicaulis is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
