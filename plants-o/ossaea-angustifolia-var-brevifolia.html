Ossaea angustifolia var. brevifolia

Ossaea angustifolia var. brevifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf ossaea, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 1 centimeter wide. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid climates.

This variety is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential ecological significance in its native habitat.
