Orchis biflora

Orchis biflora, commonly known as the two-flowered orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem that bears two distinct flowers, each with a lip that is often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and is found in parts of Europe and Asia. Its flowers are usually a mix of white, green, and brown, creating a unique visual contrast.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a protected species in many regions.
