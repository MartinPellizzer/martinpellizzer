Orchis mixta var. incarnata

Orchis mixta var. incarnata, commonly known as the pink fairy orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces striking, showy flowers in shades of pink and white. The flowers are typically arranged in a loose spike and are characterized by their spurred structure, which is a distinctive botanical feature. This orchid thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows.

Its blooming period usually occurs in late spring to early summer, adding a delicate floral display to its habitat.
