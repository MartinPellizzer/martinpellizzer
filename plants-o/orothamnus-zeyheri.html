Orothamnus zeyheri

Orothamnus zeyheri, commonly known as the Zeyher's goldenbush, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, wiry stems and small, yellowish-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a bright touch to its habitat. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Orothamnus zeyheri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in local horticulture.
