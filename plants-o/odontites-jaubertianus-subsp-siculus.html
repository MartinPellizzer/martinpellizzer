Odontites jaubertianus subsp. siculus

Odontites jaubertianus subsp.

siculus, commonly known as the Sicilian toothed grass, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Sicily. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, daisy-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
