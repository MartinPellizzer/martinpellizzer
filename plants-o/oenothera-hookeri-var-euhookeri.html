Oenothera hookeri var. euhookeri

Oenothera hookeri var. euhookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in supporting pollinator populations.
