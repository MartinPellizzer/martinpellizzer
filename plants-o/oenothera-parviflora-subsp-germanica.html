Oenothera parviflora subsp. germanica

Oenothera parviflora subsp.

germanica, commonly known as the German evening primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features showy yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are borne in clusters at the ends of the stems, and the plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. Oenothera parviflora subsp.

germanica is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in full sun to partial shade.
