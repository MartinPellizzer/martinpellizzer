Ophrys × integra

Ophrys × integra, commonly known as the bee orchid hybrid, is a fascinating plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique floral morphology. The plant produces showy, bee-like flowers that mimic the appearance and scent of female bees, a strategy used to attract male bees for pollination. Its hybrid origin results from crosses between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera, leading to a wide range of morphological variations.

Ophrys × integra thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.
