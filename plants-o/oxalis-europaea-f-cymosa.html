Oxalis europaea f. cymosa

Oxalis europaea f. cymosa, commonly known as the European wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped lobes and small, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its stems are often prostrate or ascending, and it produces small, edible berries that contain seeds.

Oxalis europaea f. cymosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
