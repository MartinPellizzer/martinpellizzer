Oncidium cycnicolle

Oncidium cycnicolle, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a distinctive spurred lip that aids in pollination.

Oncidium cycnicolle is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
