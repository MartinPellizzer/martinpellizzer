Ophrys × gembardtii

Ophrys × gembardtii, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a rare hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is characterized by its intricate floral structure, which mimics the appearance of female bees to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils in woodland or grassland habitats across Europe. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brownish in color, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often protected and requires careful conservation efforts.
