Ononis hircina var. indica

Ononis hircina var. indica, commonly known as Indian bedstraw, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with slender, hairy stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, and have a slightly hairy surface.

Native to regions of Asia, it is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
