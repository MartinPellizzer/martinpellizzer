Ornithogalum orthophyllum var. bosniacum

Ornithogalum orthophyllum var. bosniacum, commonly known as Bosnian star-of-Bethleham, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan region.

It features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers during late spring to early summer. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous underground stem.

The flowers are often visited by pollinators like bees and moths, contributing to its ecological significance.
