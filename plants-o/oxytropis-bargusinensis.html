Oxytropis bargusinensis

Oxytropis bargusinensis, commonly known as the Bargusin legume, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. Its small, pea-like flowers are usually pale blue or purple, and it produces cylindrical seed pods that release seeds through a mechanism called explosive dehiscence.

Oxytropis bargusinensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
