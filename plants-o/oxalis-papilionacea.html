Oxalis papilionacea

Oxalis papilionacea, commonly known as the false shamrock, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which resemble those of the true shamrock, and its delicate, spurred flowers that bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant. It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, thriving in temperate climates.

Oxalis papilionacea is also valued for its ability to spread through runners, making it a versatile addition to various landscapes.
