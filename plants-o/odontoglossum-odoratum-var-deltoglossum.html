Odontoglossum odoratum var. deltoglossum

Odontoglossum odoratum var. deltoglossum, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, and its flowers have a distinctive, sweet scent that attracts pollinators. It prefers cool, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and greenhouses.

The variety deltoglossum is noted for its enhanced fragrance and more robust growth compared to the parent species.
