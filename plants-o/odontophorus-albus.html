Odontophorus albus

Odontophorus albus, commonly known as the white orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and large, showy white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. Its flowers emit a faint fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths.

Odontophorus albus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses and botanical gardens.
