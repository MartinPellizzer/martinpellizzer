Operculina alata

Operculina alata, commonly known as the winged morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves and striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the morning. The plant produces a unique seed pod that is spiral-shaped and often covered in a fibrous, woody coating. Operculina alata thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found growing along riverbanks or in moist, shaded areas.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens and horticultural collections.
