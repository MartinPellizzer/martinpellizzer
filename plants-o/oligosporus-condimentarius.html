Oligosporus condimentarius

Oligosporus condimentarius, commonly known as the peppery weed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and serrated, aromatic leaves that emit a pungent odor. The plant is native to temperate regions and thrives in disturbed soils, often found along roadsides and in agricultural fields. Its leaves are used in some traditional cuisines for their sharp, spicy flavor.

However, it is considered a noxious weed due to its rapid growth and invasive nature.
