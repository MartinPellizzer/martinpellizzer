It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white, powdery wax. These plants are drought-tolerant and thrive in well-draining soil, making them suitable for rock gardens or as container plants.

Orthophytum species produce small, colorful flowers that emerge from the center of the rosette, adding a vibrant touch to their appearance.
