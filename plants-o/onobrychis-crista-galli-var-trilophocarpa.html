Onobrychis crista-galli var. trilophocarpa

Onobrychis crista-galli var. trilophocarpa, commonly known as the three-ridged goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods with three prominent ridges, giving it its specific epithet "trilophocarpa.". It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
