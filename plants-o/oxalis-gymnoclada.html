Oxalis gymnoclada

Oxalis gymnoclada, commonly known as the naked-stem oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Oxalidaceae and is characterized by its distinctive leaf structure, which consists of three leaflets arranged in a trifoliate pattern. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or containers.

Oxalis gymnoclada is also noted for its ability to spread through rhizomes, forming dense mats in suitable conditions.
