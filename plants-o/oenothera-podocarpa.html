Oenothera podocarpa

Oenothera podocarpa, commonly known as the rock evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Oenothera podocarpa is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
