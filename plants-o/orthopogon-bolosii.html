Orthopogon bolosii

Orthopogon bolosii, commonly known as the Bolosii's grass, is a perennial grass species native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands or open habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
