Onopordum syriacum

Onopordum syriacum, commonly known as Syrian thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flowers. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads. It has been used traditionally in herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant's hardy nature makes it a common weed in disturbed areas across its native range.
