Ophioxylon densiflorum

Ophioxylon densiflorum, commonly known as the denseflower wood, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom throughout the wet season. Its leaves are thick and leathery, providing a sturdy structure for the plant.

Ophioxylon densiflorum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
