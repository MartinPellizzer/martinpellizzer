Oenothera syrticola

Oenothera syrticola, commonly known as the Syrticola evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to hot, dry climates.

Oenothera syrticola is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, showcasing its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
