Osmunda longifolia

Osmunda longifolia, commonly known as the slender water fern, is a species of fern native to North America.

It is characterized by its long, slender fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant thrives in wet, swampy environments and is often found in freshwater habitats. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds, a typical feature of ferns.

Osmunda longifolia plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and stabilizing wetland soils.
