Ornithogalum osmynellum

Ornithogalum osmynellum, commonly known as the sweet flag or white lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a rhizomatous root system and produces tall, narrow leaves that are often glaucous in appearance. The plant is noted for its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in summer, arranged in umbels. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water sources.

Ornithogalum osmynellum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.
