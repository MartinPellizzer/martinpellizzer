Ocotea basicordatifolia

Ocotea basicordatifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its large, elliptical, glossy leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in dense inflorescences, while the fruit is a dark blue drupe.

The tree is valued for its aromatic wood and is often found in humid lowland forests.
