Octomeris macrodon

Octomeris macrodon, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its large, floating leaves that can reach up to three meters in diameter, providing a unique habitat for various aquatic life. The plant has a short, thick stem that rises from the water's surface, supporting its expansive foliage. Its flowers are typically white or pink, appearing in the early morning and closing by midday.

Octomeris macrodon plays an important ecological role in its native environment by offering shelter and food for numerous species.
