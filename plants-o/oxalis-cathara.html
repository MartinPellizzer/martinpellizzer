Oxalis cathara

Oxalis cathara, commonly known as the silverleaf wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive silvery underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its stems are hairy and often creeping along the ground, forming dense mats.

Oxalis cathara is adapted to moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade.
