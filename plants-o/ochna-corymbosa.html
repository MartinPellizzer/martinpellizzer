Ochna corymbosa

Ochna corymbosa, commonly known as the African star apple or African cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by bright red or orange berries.

Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
