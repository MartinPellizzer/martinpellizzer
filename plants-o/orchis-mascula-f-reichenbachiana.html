Orchis mascula f. reichenbachiana

Orchis mascula f. reichenbachiana, commonly known as the Reichenbach's male orchid, is a rare and distinctive variant of the male orchid.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure and coloration. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in woodlands or along stream banks. Its flowers are usually a striking combination of green and purple, with a prominent lip that aids in pollination.

This subspecies is of particular interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and specialized ecological requirements.
