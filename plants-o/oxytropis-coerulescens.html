Oxytropis coerulescens

Oxytropis coerulescens, commonly known as blue locoweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its blue to purple flowers and finely divided leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. It has a deep root system that allows it to survive in arid conditions.

Oxytropis coerulescens is also known for its toxic properties, making it potentially harmful to livestock.
