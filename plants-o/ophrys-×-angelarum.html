Ophrys × angelarum

Ophrys × angelarum, commonly known as the angel's orchid, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera (bee orchid) and Ophrys insectifera (waspy orchid).

It is characterized by its intricate floral structure, which mimics the appearance of female wasps to attract male wasps for pollination. The plant produces showy, bee-like flowers with a distinctive yellow and brown coloration, often featuring a prominent "face" that enhances its mimicry. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grassy areas or woodland edges.

This hybrid is notable for its unique pollination strategy and is a subject of interest in both botanical research and horticulture.
