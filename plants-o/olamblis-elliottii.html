Olamblis elliottii

Olamblis elliottii, commonly known as the Elliott's rockbriar, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils, often found in forested areas. The plant features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a rough, textured surface.

Olamblis elliottii is adapted to full sun to partial shade and is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
