Orchis romana var. lutea

Orchis romana var. lutea, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or meadow habitats. It has a tuberous root system and linear, grass-like leaves.

The flowers are usually arranged in a spike and are pollinated by insects, particularly bees.
