Oenothera montevidensis

Oenothera montevidensis, commonly known as the Montevideo evening primrose, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats, including grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often branched.

Oenothera montevidensis is part of the Onagraceae family, which includes other evening primroses known for their ecological and medicinal significance.
