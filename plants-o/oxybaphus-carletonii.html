Oxybaphus carletonii

Oxybaphus carletonii, commonly known as the Carleton's oxeye, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often in open grasslands. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellow flowers with a distinctive dark center. It blooms during the cooler months, usually from late spring to early summer.

Oxybaphus carletonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
