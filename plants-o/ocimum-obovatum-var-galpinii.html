Ocimum obovatum var. galpinii

Ocimum obovatum var. galpinii, commonly known as Galpin's basil, is a variant of the oregano family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly minty leaves and small, pale blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows as a compact shrub, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are oval-shaped, with a slightly serrated edge, and are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its aromatic properties.
