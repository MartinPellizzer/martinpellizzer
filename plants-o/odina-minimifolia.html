Odina minimifolia

Odina minimifolia, commonly known as the dwarf odina, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, narrow leaves that are typically less than 2 centimeters in length. The plant produces tiny, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the warm season. Odina minimifolia thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in dense undergrowth.

Its botanical features make it a unique subject for horticultural study and conservation efforts.
