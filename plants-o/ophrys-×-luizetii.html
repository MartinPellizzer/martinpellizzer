Ophrys × luizetii

Ophrys × luizetii, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant belonging to the Ophrys genus, which is part of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera (the bee orchid) and Ophrys insectifera (the wasp orchid). This plant is characterized by its intricate, bee-like flower structure, which features a prominent lip that mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. Ophrys × luizetii typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils in temperate regions, and it is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a hybrid species that exemplifies the complex relationships between orchids and their pollinators.
