Ophrys × montana

Ophrys × montana, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a hybrid species belonging to the genus Ophrys in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its intricate, bee-mimicking flowers that play a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions with cool, moist climates, often in rocky or woodland soils. Its floral structure includes a labellum that resembles a female bee, enticing male bees for pollination.

Ophrys × montana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitats.
