Ocotea domatiata

Ocotea domatiata, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark reddish-brown heartwood. The plant typically grows in humid, lowland forests and can reach heights of up to 30 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a glossy surface.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.
