Ophrys × sienaertiana

Ophrys × sienaertiana, commonly known as the Sienaert's bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the genus Ophrys, which is known for its mimicry of female insects to attract male pollinators. The plant produces showy, bee-like flowers that are primarily pollinated by male bees through a deceptive mating behavior. It typically grows in calcareous soils and is found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

The species is valued for its unique floral morphology and ecological role in pollination systems.
