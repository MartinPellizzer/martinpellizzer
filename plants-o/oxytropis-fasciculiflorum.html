Oxytropis fasciculiflorum

Oxytropis fasciculiflorum, commonly known as the clustered flaxvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes, where it plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for wildlife.
