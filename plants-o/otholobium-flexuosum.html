Otholobium flexuosum

Otholobium flexuosum, commonly known as the weeping everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant is typically found in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in coastal or inland regions with a Mediterranean climate.

Its seeds are enclosed in small, hairy pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind, contributing to its ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils.
