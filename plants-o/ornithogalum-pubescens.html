Ornithogalum pubescens

Ornithogalum pubescens, commonly known as the hairy star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers during the spring and summer months. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and foliage, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ornithogalum pubescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
