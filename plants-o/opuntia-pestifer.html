Opuntia pestifer

Opuntia pestifer, commonly known as the flat-stemmed prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems that are covered in spines and are often segmented. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible fruits known as tunas. Opuntia pestifer is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system to access water.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
