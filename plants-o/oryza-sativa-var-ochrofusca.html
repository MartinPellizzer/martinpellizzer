Oryza sativa var. ochrofusca

Oryza sativa var. ochrofusca, commonly known as the black rice variety, is a type of rice characterized by its dark-colored grains.

This variety is native to Southeast Asia and is cultivated for its nutritional value and aesthetic appeal. The grains of Oryza sativa var. ochrofusca contain high levels of antioxidants, particularly anthocyanins, which give them their distinctive color. It is typically grown in tropical and subtropical climates with warm temperatures and ample rainfall.

The plant has a short growth cycle and is often used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
