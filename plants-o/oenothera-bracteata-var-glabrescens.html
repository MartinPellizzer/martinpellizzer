Oenothera bracteata var. glabrescens

Oenothera bracteata var. glabrescens, commonly known as the glabrous evening primrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in open woods, prairies, and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers emit a mild scent at night.

The species is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
