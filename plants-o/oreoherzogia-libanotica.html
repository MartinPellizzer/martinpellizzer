Oreoherzogia libanotica

Oreoherzogia libanotica, commonly known as the Lebanon sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage. The plant features small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs.

Oreoherzogia libanotica is adapted to rocky and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments.
