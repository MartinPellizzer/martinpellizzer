Orchis quadripunctata

Orchis quadripunctata, commonly known as the four-spotted orchid, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces a tall, slender stem topped with a single spike of flowers. Its flowers are usually purple or violet with distinctive black spots, which are most prominent on the lower petals.

The four-spotted orchid is often found in woodland edges and meadows, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.
