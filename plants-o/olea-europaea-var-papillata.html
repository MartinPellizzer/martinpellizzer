Olea europaea var. papillata

Olea europaea var. papillata, commonly known as the wrinkled olive, is a variant of the common olive tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, deeply furrowed bark and small, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into drupes, which are the olives harvested for culinary and oil production. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, dry climate.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in traditional agriculture.
