Ornithogalum croceum

Ornithogalum croceum, commonly known as the yellow star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, linear leaves and produces striking yellow flowers in late summer. The flowers are star-shaped, with six petals and are borne on tall, slender stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

The plant is also known for its toxic properties, containing compounds that can be harmful if ingested.
