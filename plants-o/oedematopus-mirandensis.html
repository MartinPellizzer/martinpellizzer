Oedematopus mirandensis

Oedematopus mirandensis, commonly known as the Mirandense water plant, is a rare aquatic plant native to the wetlands of Angola.

It is characterized by its fleshy, swollen leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in shallow, stagnant water bodies. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. Its root system helps stabilize the sediment in its habitat, contributing to the ecological balance of the environment.

Due to habitat loss and pollution, Oedematopus mirandensis is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
