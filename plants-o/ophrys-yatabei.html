Ophrys yatabei

Ophrys yatabei, commonly known as the Yatabe's bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which closely resembles a female bee, a mimicry strategy that aids in pollination by male bees. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments, where it thrives in the cool, humid conditions. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of a bee's body.

Ophrys yatabei is a protected species due to its limited distribution and the threats posed by habitat destruction.
