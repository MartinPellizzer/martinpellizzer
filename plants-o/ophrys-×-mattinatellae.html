Ophrys × mattinatellae

Ophrys × mattinatellae, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a rare and fascinating plant that results from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is characterized by its intricate, bee-like floral structure, which plays a crucial role in its unique pollination mechanism. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is found in specific regions of Europe, particularly in southern Italy. Its flowers exhibit a sophisticated mimicry of female bees, attracting male bees for pollination.

This botanical feature highlights the evolutionary adaptations in orchids for successful reproduction.
