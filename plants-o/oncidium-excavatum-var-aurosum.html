Oncidium excavatum var. aurosum

Oncidium excavatum var. aurosum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical orchid species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow and green foliage and its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are long-lasting and often used in floral arrangements due to their durability and aesthetic appeal.

The variety 'aurosum' is distinguished by its golden coloration, which sets it apart from other Oncidium species.
