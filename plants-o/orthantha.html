Orthantha

Orthantha, commonly known as the orchid vine, is a tropical plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and the presence of showy, fragrant flowers. The plant features strap-like leaves and produces clusters of small, delicate blooms. Orthantha thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is popular in horticultural gardens.
