Oxytropis aven-nelsonii

Oxytropis aven-nelsonii, commonly known as Nelson's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to harsh, semi-desert environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.

Oxytropis aven-nelsonii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
