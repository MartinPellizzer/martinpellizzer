Orthoclada rariflora var. sesquitertia

Orthoclada rariflora var. sesquitertia, commonly known as the rare-flowered orthoclada, is a unique member of the Orthocladaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, thread-like leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
