Oenothera heterochroma var. monoensis

Oenothera heterochroma var. monoensis, commonly known as the Mono Lake evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking yellow to orange flowers that bloom at dusk. The plant typically grows in saline or alkaline soils near the shores of Mono Lake. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

This variant is distinguished by its unique coloration and habitat specificity.
