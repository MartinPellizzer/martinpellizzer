Orthophytum atalaiense

Orthophytum atalaiense, commonly known as the Atalaia grass.

It is a perennial grass species native to Brazil, particularly in the state of Minas Gerais. The plant is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Orthophytum atalaiense is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
