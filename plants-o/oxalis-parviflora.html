Oxalis parviflora

Oxalis parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 10 to 15 centimeters. The plant features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers with five petals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Oxalis parviflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
