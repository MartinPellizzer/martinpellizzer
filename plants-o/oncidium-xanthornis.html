Oncidium xanthornis

Oncidium xanthornis, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It features a pseudobulb that stores water and nutrients, supporting its growth in shaded, humid environments. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences with showy, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that are dark green and often have a waxy texture.

Oncidium xanthornis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is popular in horticulture for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
