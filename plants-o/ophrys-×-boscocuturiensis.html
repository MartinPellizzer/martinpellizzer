Ophrys × boscocuturiensis

Ophrys × boscocuturiensis, commonly known as the Bosco Cuturi orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the genus Ophrys, which is characterized by its intricate floral structures that mimic female insects to attract male pollinators. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is found in alpine and subalpine habitats. Its flowers are highly specialized, featuring a labellum that resembles a wasp or bee, enhancing its mimicry for effective pollination.

This orchid is relatively rare and is of interest to botanists due to its unique evolutionary adaptations.
