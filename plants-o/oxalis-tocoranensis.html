Oxalis tocoranensis

Oxalis tocoranensis, commonly known as the Brazilian wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant is often found in shaded, moist environments such as forests and stream banks. It produces small, edible berries that are sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Oxalis tocoranensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
