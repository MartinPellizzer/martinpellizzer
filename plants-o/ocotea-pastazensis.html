Ocotea pastazensis

Ocotea pastazensis, commonly known as the Pastaza ocotea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the cloud forests of Ecuador and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a distinctive glossy appearance.

Ocotea pastazensis plays a role in the ecological diversity of its habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
