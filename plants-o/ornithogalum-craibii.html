Ornithogalum craibii

Ornithogalum craibii, commonly known as the Craib's star-of-Bethlehem, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features strap-shaped, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers during the summer months. The plant belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its underground bulbs that store nutrients. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
