Oxalis rhombifolia var. pubescens

Oxalis rhombifolia var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its botanical features include trifoliate leaves and a rhizomatous root system.

It is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
