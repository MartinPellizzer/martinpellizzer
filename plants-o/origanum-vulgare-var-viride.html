Origanum vulgare var. viride

Origanum vulgare var. viride, commonly known as green oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, dark green, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces tiny white to pale purple flowers in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates.

Its aromatic leaves are used in cooking and traditional medicine, similar to other members of the Lamiaceae family.
