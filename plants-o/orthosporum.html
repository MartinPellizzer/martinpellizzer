Orthosporum

Orthosporum, commonly known as the goldenrod, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow or golden inflorescences that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in a variety of habitats, including meadows, roadsides, and disturbed soils. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Orthosporum species are often used in gardens for their ornamental value and ecological benefits, providing food for pollinators.
