Orobanche aegyptiaca var. aemula

Orobanche aegyptiaca var. aemula, commonly known as the Egyptian barrenwort, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is characterized by its tubular, often pink or purple flowers. This variety is typically found in arid and semi-arid regions, often growing on the roots of leguminous plants. Its life cycle involves germinating in response to chemical signals released by host roots.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can significantly impact the growth and productivity of its host plants.
