Orchis × pauliana

Orchis × pauliana, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a cross between Orchis morio and Orchis mascula.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland areas. The plant produces tall, upright stems with broad, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often found in gardens and natural habitats.
