Ophrys × angelensis

Ophrys × angelensis, commonly known as the angel's orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its intricate floral structure, which mimics the appearance of female insects to attract male pollinators. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its flowers exhibit a complex morphology with specialized structures that enhance its mimicry and pollination success.

Ophrys × angelensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological significance in its native habitats.
