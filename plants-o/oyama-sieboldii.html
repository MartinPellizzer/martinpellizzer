Oyama sieboldii

Oyama sieboldii, commonly known as the Japanese snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn, adding to its seasonal appeal. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

Oyama sieboldii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in cooler environments.
