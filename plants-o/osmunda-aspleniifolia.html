Osmunda aspleniifolia

Osmunda aspleniifolia, commonly known as the mouse-ear fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Osmundaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble the ears of a mouse, giving it its common name. The plant is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its rhizomes are extensively branched and produce both sterile and fertile fronds.

Osmunda aspleniifolia is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can be found growing in woodlands and along stream banks.
