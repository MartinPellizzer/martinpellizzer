Ophrys fusca f. omegaifera

Ophrys fusca f. omegaifera, commonly known as the omega orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which includes a distinctive lip that mimics the appearance of a female bee, aiding in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats. Its flowers are usually pale green to brown in color, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
