Oxypetalum coeruleum

Oxypetalum coeruleum, commonly known as the blue oxypetalum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically borne on slender, branched stems. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
