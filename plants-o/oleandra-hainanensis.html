Oleandra hainanensis

Oleandra hainanensis, commonly known as the Hainan oleander, is a flowering plant native to the southern Chinese province of Hainan.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and yellow. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and can grow as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Oleandra hainanensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
