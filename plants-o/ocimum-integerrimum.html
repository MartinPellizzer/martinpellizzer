Ocimum integerrimum

Ocimum integerrimum, commonly known as holy basil or tulsi, is a species of flowering plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to the Indian subcontinent and is characterized by its aromatic, opposite leaves and small, tubular flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Ayurvedic medicine. Its leaves are rich in essential oils, which contribute to its distinct scent and therapeutic uses.

Ocimum integerrimum thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
