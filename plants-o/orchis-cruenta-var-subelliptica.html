Orchis cruenta var. subelliptica

Orchis cruenta var. subelliptica, commonly known as the crimson helleborine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist woodlands and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its roots are tuberous, and it produces a single, showy flower per stem.

This orchid is notable for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and its role in supporting pollinators.
