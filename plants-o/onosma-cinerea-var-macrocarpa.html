Onosma cinerea var. macrocarpa

Onosma cinerea var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited hoary everlasting, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features silver-gray, woolly leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its stems are usually erect and branched, often growing up to 30 cm in height.

This variety is noted for its drought tolerance and is often used in rock gardens or as a decorative plant in dry landscapes.
