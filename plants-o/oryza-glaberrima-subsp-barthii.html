Oryza glaberrima subsp. barthii

Oryza glaberrima subsp.

barthii, commonly known as the African rice, is a wild rice species native to West Africa. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance and small, brownish grains.

This subspecies is known for its adaptability to flooded conditions, making it a valuable resource for sustainable agriculture. It has been traditionally cultivated by local communities for its nutritional value and resilience.

Genetic studies suggest it may hold important traits for improving modern rice varieties.
