Oxalis prorepens

Oxalis prorepens, commonly known as the false shamrock, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves that resemble those of the true shamrock, hence its common name. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of yellow and pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Oxalis prorepens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
