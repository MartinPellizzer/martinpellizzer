Onobrychis viciifolia f. glabrescens

Onobrychis viciifolia f. glabrescens, commonly known as the glabrous goat's rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers.

It is a variant of the common goat's rue, distinguished by its reduced pubescence, giving it a smoother appearance. The plant is native to parts of Europe and is often found in dry, rocky soils. It has a compact growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical studies.
