Oritrophium marahuacense

Oritrophium marahuacense, commonly known as the Mexican goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of central Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, with bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and slopes.

Oritrophium marahuacense is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local herbal medicine.
