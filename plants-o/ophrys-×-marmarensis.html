Ophrys × marmarensis

Ophrys × marmarensis, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique floral morphology that mimics female bees, a feature known as sexual deception. The plant produces showy, bee-like flowers with intricate patterns and a distinct three-lobed lip that enhances its pollination strategy. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.
