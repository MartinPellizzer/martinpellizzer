Oxalis stricta var. villicaulis

Oxalis stricta var. villicaulis, commonly known as the slender wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves with distinctive veins and produces clusters of five-petaled yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its stems are slender and often prostrate, and it has a fibrous root system.

Oxalis stricta var. villicaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
