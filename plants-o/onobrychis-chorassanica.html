Onobrychis chorassanica

Onobrychis chorassanica, commonly known as Persian clover, is a perennial leguminous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Its flowers are often pink or purple, and it is used in traditional medicine and as forage for livestock.
