Otanthera scaberrima

Otanthera scaberrima, commonly known as the roughleaf aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring narrow, hairy leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. Its foliage is often covered in fine, rough hairs, which contribute to its common name.

Otanthera scaberrima thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.
