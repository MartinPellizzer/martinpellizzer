Orchis anatolica var. troodi

Orchis anatolica var. troodi, commonly known as the Anatolian orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to parts of Turkey and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet or purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, often in mountainous regions with high altitudes. Its flowers are adapted for pollination by specific insects, such as bees and flies, which are attracted by its scent and color.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
