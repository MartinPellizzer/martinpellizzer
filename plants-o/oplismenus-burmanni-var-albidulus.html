Oplismenus burmanni var. albidulus

Oplismenus burmanni var. albidulus, commonly known as the white-flowered sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water garden landscapes.
