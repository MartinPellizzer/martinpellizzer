Ochna rovumensis

Ochna rovumensis, commonly known as the Rovuma ochna, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of East Africa.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its woody structure and showy, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and along riverbanks. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture.

Ochna rovumensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some local communities.
