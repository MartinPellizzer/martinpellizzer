Oxalis pilosa

Oxalis pilosa, commonly known as wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which resemble those of clover, and small, delicate flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands or along streambanks. Oxalis pilosa is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters, with hairy stems and small, heart-shaped leaves.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
