Octomeles sumatrana

Octomeles sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran octomeles, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in Indonesia.

It belongs to the family Bombacaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces striking orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It is often valued for its timber, which is durable and used in construction and furniture-making.

Additionally, the plant plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
