Oenothera filipes

Oenothera filipes, commonly known as the slender evening primrose, is a herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and showy yellow flowers that open in the evening. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oval seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and open woodlands.

Oenothera filipes is part of the Onagraceae family, which includes other species known for their medicinal and ornamental value.
