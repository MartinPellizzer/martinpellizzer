Olearia boorabbinensis

Olearia boorabbinensis, commonly known as the Boorabbin Daisy-bush, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers and silver-green foliage. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is found in dry, open habitats. It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

Olearia boorabbinensis is a hardy shrub that is well adapted to the arid conditions of its native environment.
