Onopordum macracanthum var. tomentosum

Onopordum macracanthum var. tomentosum, commonly known as the woolly thistle, is a perennial plant characterized by its dense, fuzzy gray foliage and striking purple to pink flower heads.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rough, hairy texture, while the stems are often spiny.

The flower heads are composed of numerous small florets and are visited by a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
