Oxalis diamantinae

Oxalis diamantinae, commonly known as the diamond leaf oxalis, is a striking plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive diamond pattern on the underside, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink or white, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Oxalis diamantinae is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and graceful appearance.
