Orthopenthea Schizodioides

Common ice plant, scientifically known as Orthopenthea schizodioides, is a striking succulent native to the arid regions of South Africa. This plant is renowned for its ability to store water in its fleshy, translucent leaves, allowing it to thrive in harsh, dry environments. Its unique appearance, with tiny, ice-like crystals on the leaf surface, gives it its common name and makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Despite its delicate look, the Common ice plant is highly resilient, capable of surviving extreme temperatures and minimal water. It is not only a fascinating specimen for plant enthusiasts but also an important part of its native ecosystem.
