Oriastrum minutum

Oriastrum minutum, commonly known as the small-fruited oriastrum, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are finely divided and aromatic, and the fruit is a small, dry schizocarp with two mericarps.

Oriastrum minutum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
