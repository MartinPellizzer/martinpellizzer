Oxalis oculata

Oxalis oculata, commonly known as the "false shamrock," is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, delicate flowers. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow blooms with five petals, often accompanied by orange-tipped stamens. Oxalis oculata thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and brief flowering period.
