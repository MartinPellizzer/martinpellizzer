Onobrychis pyrenaica

Onobrychis pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean burclover, is a perennial legume native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and trifoliate leaves, typical of the Fabaceae family. The plant thrives in rocky and calcareous soils, often found in open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. It has a creeping growth habit with a deep root system, allowing it to survive in arid conditions.

Its ecological importance includes soil stabilization and providing forage for wildlife.
