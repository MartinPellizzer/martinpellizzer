Ophioglossum gomezianum

Ophioglossum gomezianum, commonly known as the Gomez's adder's mouth, is a rare and ancient fern-like plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Ophioglossaceae, which is considered one of the most primitive groups of ferns. The plant has a unique structure with a short, upright stem and a single, large, lance-shaped leaf that is often divided into two lobes. Its spore-producing structures, called sporangia, are located on the underside of the leaf, a characteristic feature of its primitive morphology.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
