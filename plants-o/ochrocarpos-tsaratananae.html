Ochrocarpos tsaratananae

Ochrocarpos tsaratananae, commonly known as the Tsaratanana orchid, is a unique and rare plant species native to the rainforests of Madagascar.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its striking, elongated flowers with a distinctive coloration. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on the forest floor or as an epiphyte on tree trunks. Its botanical features include fleshy roots and specialized structures called pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
