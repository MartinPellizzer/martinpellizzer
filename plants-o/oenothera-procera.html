Oenothera procera

Oenothera procera, commonly known as the tall evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in sandy or loamy soils.

Oenothera procera is also known for its medicinal properties, with its seeds used in traditional herbal remedies.
