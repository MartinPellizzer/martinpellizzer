Oxydectes rottlerifolia

Oxydectes rottlerifolia, commonly known as the Rottler's laurel, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces elliptical, leathery leaves with a glossy surface and a strong, aromatic scent. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The tree is relatively hardy and thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-drained soil.
