Oenothera albicaulis f. californica

Oenothera albicaulis f. californica, commonly known as the California evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its pale yellow to white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and the stems are slender and often branched.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its ornamental value in gardens.
