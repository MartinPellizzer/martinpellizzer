Orthosiphon teucriifolius var. galpinianus

Orthosiphon teucriifolius var. galpinianus, commonly known as Galpin's horseleaf, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Lamiaceae family.
