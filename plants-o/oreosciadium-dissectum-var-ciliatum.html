Oreosciadium dissectum var. ciliatum

Oreosciadium dissectum var. ciliatum, commonly known as the fringed rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pinnately compound leaves. The variety ciliatum is distinguished by its finely ciliate (fringed) margins on the leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This plant is noted for its resilience to cold climates and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
