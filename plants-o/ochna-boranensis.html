Ochna boranensis

Ochna boranensis, commonly known as the East African ochna, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Africa.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. Ochna boranensis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

It is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and flowers.
