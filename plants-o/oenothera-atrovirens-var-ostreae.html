Oenothera atrovirens var. ostreae

Oenothera atrovirens var. ostreae, commonly known as the coastal evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western coast of the United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and showy yellow flowers that open in the evening. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal dunes and dry habitats.

Oenothera atrovirens var. ostreae is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds, making it a resilient species in its native environment.
