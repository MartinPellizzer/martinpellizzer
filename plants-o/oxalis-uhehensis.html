Oxalis uhehensis

Oxalis uhehensis, commonly known as the purpleleaf wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically heart-shaped with a distinctive purple hue on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

Oxalis uhehensis is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
