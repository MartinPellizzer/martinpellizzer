Orchis fuchsii f. maxima

Orchis fuchsii f. maxima, commonly known as the giant marsh orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, making it one of the tallest orchids in Europe. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of purple and white, arranged in a dense spike. It thrives in wet, calcareous soils, typically found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

This rare and protected species is an important indicator of high-quality wetland habitats.
