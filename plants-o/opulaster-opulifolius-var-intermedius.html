Opulaster opulifolius var. intermedius

Opulaster opulifolius var. intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate willow-leaf opulaster, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely toothed, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often reddish-brown beneath. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by capsule fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
