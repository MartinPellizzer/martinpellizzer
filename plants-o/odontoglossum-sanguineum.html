Odontoglossum sanguineum

Odontoglossum sanguineum, commonly known as the blood orchid, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, dark red to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in cool, humid environments at high altitudes, often in rocky or forested areas. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique and vibrant floral display.
