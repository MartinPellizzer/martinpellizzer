Oenothera renneri f. mollis

Oenothera renneri f. mollis, commonly known as the soft-hairy evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some historical use in traditional medicine.
