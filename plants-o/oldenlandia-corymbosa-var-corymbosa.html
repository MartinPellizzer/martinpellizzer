Oldenlandia corymbosa var. corymbosa

Oldenlandia corymbosa var. corymbosa, commonly known as the coral vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is a trailing or climbing vine with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, showy white or pink flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid forests or along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
