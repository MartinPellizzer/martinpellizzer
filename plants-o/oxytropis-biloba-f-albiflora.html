Oxytropis biloba f. albiflora

Oxytropis biloba f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered balsamroot, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves and produces tall, erect stems topped with clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant is native to the arid regions of western North America, including parts of California and Nevada. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.

The white-flowered form is distinguished by its more pronounced floral coloration compared to the typical variety.
