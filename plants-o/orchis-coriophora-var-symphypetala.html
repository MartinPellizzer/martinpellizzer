Orchis coriophora var. symphypetala

Orchis coriophora var. symphypetala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces a spike of small, greenish-white flowers with a distinctive lip that is often marked with purple or brown spots. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or grassland habitats.

This variety is known for its subtle appearance and is often found in temperate regions of Europe.
