Oenothera cespitosa var. montana

Oenothera cespitosa var. montana, commonly known as mountain evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as meadows and slopes. The plant features showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often branched and upright.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
