Onosma mertensioides

Onosma mertensioides, commonly known as the Persian everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant produces slender, hairy stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Onosma mertensioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its resilience in arid conditions.
