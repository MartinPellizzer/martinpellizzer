Ophrys × arachnitiformis f. specularia

Ophrys × arachnitiformis f. specularia, commonly known as the "Mirror Orchid," is a rare hybrid orchid species characterized by its intricate floral structure.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is noted for its specialized morphology, including a labellum that mimics the appearance of an insect. The plant exhibits a unique combination of traits from its parent species, Ophrys arachnitiformis and Ophrys speculum, resulting in a distinct floral form. It is typically found in specific habitats with well-drained soils and partial shade.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a hybrid example of adaptive evolution and mimicry in orchids.
