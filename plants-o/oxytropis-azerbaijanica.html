Oxytropis azerbaijanica

Oxytropis azerbaijanica, commonly known as Azerbaijani locoweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Azerbaijan.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is adapted to grow in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine and steppe environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its range.
