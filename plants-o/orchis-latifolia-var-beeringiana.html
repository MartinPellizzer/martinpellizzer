Orchis latifolia var. beeringiana

Orchis latifolia var. beeringiana, commonly known as the Broad-leaved Orchis variant Beeringiana, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and striking purple or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its flowers are usually arranged in a raceme and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
