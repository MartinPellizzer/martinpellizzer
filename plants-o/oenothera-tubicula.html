Oenothera tubicula

Oenothera tubicula, commonly known as the narrowleaf evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It features slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its flowers have four petals and are known for their fragrant scent.

Oenothera tubicula is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils.
