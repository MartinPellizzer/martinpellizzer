Osteospermum cuspidatum

Osteospermum cuspidatum, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features striking, showy yellow to orange central disks surrounded by white to pink ray florets. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Osteospermum cuspidatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
