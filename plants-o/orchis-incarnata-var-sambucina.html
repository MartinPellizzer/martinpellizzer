Orchis incarnata var. sambucina

Orchis incarnata var. sambucina, commonly known as the purple twayblade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, pale purple to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows. It has a short rootstock and lacks true leaves, relying on its flowering stems for photosynthesis.

The species is noted for its ecological importance as a nectar source for various pollinators.
