Opuntia basilaris var. brachyclada

Opuntia basilaris var. brachyclada, commonly known as the narrow-leaf prickly pear, is a succulent cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features flattened, paddle-shaped stems that are typically narrow and covered in spiny areoles. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its foliage is adapted to store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

The plant's structure and growth habits make it well-suited for xeriscaping and desert gardens.
