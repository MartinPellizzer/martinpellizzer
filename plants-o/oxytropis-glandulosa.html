Oxytropis glandulosa

Oxytropis glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Oxytropis glandulosa is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in open grasslands and rocky soils.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for wildlife and livestock.
