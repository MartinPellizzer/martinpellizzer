Ochroma pyramidale var. concolor

Ochroma pyramidale var. concolor, commonly known as the balsa tree, is a fast-growing tropical hardwood species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its light, porous wood that is exceptionally lightweight yet strong, making it highly valued for various industrial applications. The tree has simple, alternate, ovate leaves that are usually smooth and dark green in color. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by winged fruits that disperse its seeds by wind.

Due to its rapid growth and ecological importance, it plays a significant role in tropical forest ecosystems.
