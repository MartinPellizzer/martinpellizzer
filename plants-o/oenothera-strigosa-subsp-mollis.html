Oenothera strigosa subsp. mollis

Oenothera strigosa subsp.

mollis, commonly known as the soft-stemmed evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its hairy, slender stems and yellow flowers that bloom in the evening.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oily seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as prairies and disturbed areas.

The species is valued for its ecological role and has been studied for its potential in biofuel production.
