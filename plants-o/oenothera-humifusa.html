Oenothera humifusa

Oenothera humifusa, commonly known as the showy evening primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, orange to yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has hairy, glandular stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and open fields.

Oenothera humifusa is known for its ability to produce a large number of seeds, contributing to its widespread distribution.
