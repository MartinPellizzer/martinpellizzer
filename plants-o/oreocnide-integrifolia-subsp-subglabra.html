Oreocnide integrifolia subsp. subglabra

Oreocnide integrifolia subsp.

subglabra, commonly known as the smooth-leafed oreocnide, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its smooth, elliptical leaves that are typically glabrous or nearly so.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually borne in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and streamside areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the study of understory vegetation in temperate rainforests.
