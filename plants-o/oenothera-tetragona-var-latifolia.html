Oenothera tetragona var. latifolia

Oenothera tetragona var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf four-angle evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that open in the evening, and its linear, often hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its scientific name reflects its four-angled stem and the broad foliage of the variety.

It is a member of the Onagraceae family, which includes other evening primroses and related species.
