Odontoglossum seringa

Odontoglossum seringa, commonly known as the Seringa orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, sympodial growth habit. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. Its leaves are typically elliptical and arranged in two rows along the stem.

Odontoglossum seringa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
