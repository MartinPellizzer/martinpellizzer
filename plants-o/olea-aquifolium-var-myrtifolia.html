Olea aquifolium var. myrtifolia

Olea aquifolium var. myrtifolia, commonly known as the Myrtle-leaved Olive, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic leaves that are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, with serrated edges resembling those of myrtle. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by edible, black berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
