Ornithogalum teretifolium

Ornithogalum teretifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf star-of-Bethleham, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a slender, upright stem with narrow, linear leaves that are often slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers with six petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
