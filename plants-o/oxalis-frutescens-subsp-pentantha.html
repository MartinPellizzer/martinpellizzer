Oxalis frutescens subsp. pentantha

Oxalis frutescens subsp.

pentantha, commonly known as the five-petaled wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Southeast Asia. It features small, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive red or purple coloration on the underside.

The plant produces clusters of five-petaled, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for shady garden settings.
