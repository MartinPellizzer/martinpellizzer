Oenothera strigosa var. albinervis

Oenothera strigosa var. albinervis, commonly known as the white-veined evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its flowers have four petals and are typically borne in clusters at the ends of the stems.

The variety albinervis is distinguished by its white veins on the leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance.
