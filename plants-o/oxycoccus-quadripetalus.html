Oxycoccus quadripetalus

Oxycoccus quadripetalus, commonly known as the four-petaled oxeye, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces tiny, white, four-petaled flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are leathery and evergreen, adapted to withstand harsh cold climates.

Oxycoccus quadripetalus is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is a hardy plant suited to extreme environmental conditions.
