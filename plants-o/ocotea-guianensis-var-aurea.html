Ocotea guianensis var. aurea

Ocotea guianensis var. aurea, commonly known as golden ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, leathery leaves that emit a distinctive scent. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark blue berries. Its bark is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitats.
