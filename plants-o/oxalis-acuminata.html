Oxalis acuminata

Oxalis acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-lobed wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped lobes and bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in woodland and wetland habitats, where it forms dense mats.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive addition to native plant gardens.
