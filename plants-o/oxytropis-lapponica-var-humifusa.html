Oxytropis lapponica var. humifusa

Oxytropis lapponica var. humifusa, commonly known as the Lapland milkvetch, is a low-growing perennial herb native to arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It features small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates.

This variety is often found in tundra and mountainous habitats where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
