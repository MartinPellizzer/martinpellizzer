Ononis natrix var. fallacina

Ononis natrix var. fallacina, commonly known as the narrowleaf bedstraw, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant has alternate, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils along stream banks, roadsides, and in open woodlands.

This variety is distinguished by its slightly smaller leaves and flowers compared to the typical form of Ononis natrix.
