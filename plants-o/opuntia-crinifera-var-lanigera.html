Opuntia crinifera var. lanigera

Opuntia crinifera var. lanigera, commonly known as the woolly prickly pear, is a cacti species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems covered in dense, white, woolly hairs. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tuna, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

This variety is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water to thrive.
