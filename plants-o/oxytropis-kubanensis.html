Oxytropis kubanensis

Oxytropis kubanensis, commonly known as Kuban milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Russia and parts of Ukraine.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in dry, sandy or loamy soils and is often found in steppe and semi-desert environments.

Kuban milkvetch is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in soil enrichment.
