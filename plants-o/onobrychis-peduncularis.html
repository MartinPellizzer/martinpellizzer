Onobrychis peduncularis

Onobrychis peduncularis, commonly known as narrow-leafed clover, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are narrow and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Onobrychis peduncularis is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it a useful plant in agricultural and ecological contexts.
