Oxalis crispa

Oxalis crispa, commonly known as the crinkled wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive crinkled texture and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 6 to 12 inches and prefers moist, well-drained soil in partial to full shade. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening for its delicate appearance.

Oxalis crispa is also known for its ability to thrive in shaded woodland environments.
