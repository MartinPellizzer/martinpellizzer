Orchis lingua

Orchis lingua, commonly known as the tongue orchid, is a species of orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated flower spike that often resembles a tongue, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and prefers moist, well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually violet or purple in color and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Orchis lingua is a perennial herb with a fibrous root system and lance-shaped leaves.
