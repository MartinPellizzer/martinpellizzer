Ocimum sebrabergensis

Ocimum sebrabergensis, commonly known as the Ethiopian basil, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant leaves and purple or white flowers. It thrives in temperate climates and is often grown for its culinary and medicinal properties.

Its essential oils are used in traditional medicine for their antimicrobial and antioxidant benefits.
