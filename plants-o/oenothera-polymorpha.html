Oenothera polymorpha

Oenothera polymorpha, commonly known as the evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow or orange flowers that open in the evening. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oily seeds. Its leaves are typically alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Oenothera polymorpha is known for its ability to thrive in sandy or disturbed soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.
