Oplismenus bromoides

Oplismenus bromoides, commonly known as littleleaf ironweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically less than 2 inches long, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters. It thrives in moist, rich soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Oplismenus bromoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
