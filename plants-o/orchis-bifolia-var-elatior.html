Orchis bifolia var. elatior

Orchis bifolia var. elatior, commonly known as the greater butterfly orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, butterfly-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, aiding in water retention.

This variety is known for its vibrant purple and white floral display, which attracts pollinators like bees and moths.
