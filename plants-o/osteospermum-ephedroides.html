Osteospermum ephedroides

Osteospermum ephedroides, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to blue and purple. The plant has hairy, silver-green leaves and produces a distinctive blue or purple central disc in its composite flower heads. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Osteospermum ephedroides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
