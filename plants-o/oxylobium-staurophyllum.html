Oxylobium staurophyllum

Oxylobium staurophyllum, commonly known as the mountain lily, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors. The plant produces showy, white to pinkish flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive waxy coating, aiding in water retention.

Oxylobium staurophyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
