Oncidium cucullatum var. flavidum

Oncidium cucullatum var. flavidum, commonly known as the yellow-lip orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, showy flower spikes that produce fragrant, yellowish-orange blooms with a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are long-lasting, often blooming in the spring or early summer.

The variety flavidum is noted for its vibrant coloration and is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value.
