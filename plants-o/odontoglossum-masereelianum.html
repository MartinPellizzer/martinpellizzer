Odontoglossum masereelianum

Odontoglossum masereelianum, commonly known as the Masereelian orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, typical of many orchid species. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and violet, often with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts but is also threatened by habitat loss in the wild.
