Onobrychis fallax var. nabelekii

Onobrychis fallax var. nabelekii, commonly known as the Nabelek's vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial legume characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a creeping growth habit and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
