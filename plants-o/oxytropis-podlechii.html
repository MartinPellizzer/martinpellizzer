Oxytropis podlechii

Oxytropis podlechii, commonly known as the Podlech's locoweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Oxytropis podlechii is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
