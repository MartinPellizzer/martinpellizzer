Oxalis polyphylla

Oxalis polyphylla, commonly known as the mountain wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically heart-shaped and have a distinctive reddish or purple tinge. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a delicate touch to woodland gardens. Oxalis polyphylla prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a versatile choice for shaded landscapes.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to thrive in a variety of garden conditions.
