Oryzopsis leucosperma

Oryzopsis leucosperma, commonly known as white-awned grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and feathery, white-tipped inflorescences. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often in rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Oryzopsis leucosperma plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small wildlife in its native range.
