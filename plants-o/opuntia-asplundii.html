Opuntia asplundii

Opuntia asplundii, commonly known as the Asplund's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, spineless pads and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its pads are edible and can be used in culinary applications, while its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Opuntia asplundii plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
