Orobanche bovei

Orobanche bovei, commonly known as the Bove's broomrape, is a parasitic plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is characterized by its lack of leaves and its specialized, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, often parasitizing members of the Chenopodiaceae family. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to reddish in color and are pollinated by insects.

Due to its parasitic nature, it plays a role in shaping the ecological balance of its host environment.
