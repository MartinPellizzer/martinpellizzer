Ocimum caryophyllinum

Ocimum caryophyllinum, commonly known as Greek oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are broad, wrinkled, and have a strong, pungent aroma due to high concentrations of essential oils.

It is often used in cooking and traditional medicine for its antimicrobial and antioxidant properties.
