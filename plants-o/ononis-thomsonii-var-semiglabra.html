Ononis thomsonii var. semiglabra

Ononis thomsonii var. semiglabra, commonly known as Thomson's ononise, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features slender, wiry stems that are covered with small, glandular hairs, giving it a slightly rough texture. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers clustered in racemes, typically blooming in early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a bluish-green hue.

This variety is adapted to rocky and dry environments, making it a hardy member of the Fabaceae family.
