Osmanthus aquifolium var. japonicus

Osmanthus aquifolium var. japonicus, commonly known as Japanese osmanthus, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that are opposite and often have a spiny margin. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow-green flowers in late summer, which are followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental fruits make it a valuable addition to both traditional and modern horticultural settings.
