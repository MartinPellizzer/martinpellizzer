Oryzopsis holciformis

Oryzopsis holciformis, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, silver-gray blades that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and wet meadows, and is often found in riparian zones. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle with spikelets that are often bracted and can be either male or female.

Oryzopsis holciformis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
