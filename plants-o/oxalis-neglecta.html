Oxalis neglecta

Oxalis neglecta, commonly known as the common wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to western North America.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets, often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically appearing in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Oxalis neglecta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and flowers.
