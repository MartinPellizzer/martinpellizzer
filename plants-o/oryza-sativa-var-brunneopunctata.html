Oryza sativa var. brunneopunctata

Oryza sativa var. brunneopunctata, commonly known as the brown-spotted rice.

This variety is characterized by its unique brown spots on the grain, which distinguish it from other rice types. It is a member of the grass family, Poaceae, and is native to Asia. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with long, narrow leaves and panicles of small, greenish-white flowers.

It is primarily cultivated for its grains, which are used in various culinary and industrial applications.
