Ozotrix helvetica

Ozotrix helvetica, commonly known as the Swiss pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the Alps.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure, typical of the Pinaceae family. The tree has a slender, upright growth habit with a conical shape, making it a striking feature in mountainous regions. Its bark is thin and reddish-brown, becoming rougher with age.

Ozotrix helvetica plays an important ecological role in alpine ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
