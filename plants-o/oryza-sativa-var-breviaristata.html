Oryza sativa var. breviaristata

Oryza sativa var. breviaristata, commonly known as the short-awned rice, is a botanical variant of rice characterized by its reduced awn length.

This variety typically exhibits slender, elongated grains with a pale to straw-colored hull. It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant has a robust root system and narrow, grass-like leaves that contribute to its adaptability in wetland environments.

Its cultivation is primarily focused on its high yield and grain quality, making it an important crop in certain regions.
