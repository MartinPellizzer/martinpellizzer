Oldenlandia comata

Oldenlandia comata, commonly known as the silky sandveld or silky oldenlandia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom throughout the warmer months. The plant has hairy, slender stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Oldenlandia comata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in garden settings for its delicate appearance.
