Obione chanicowii

Obione chanicowii, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate to cold climates. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and bloom in the spring.

Obione chanicowii is often found in alpine environments and is adapted to withstand harsh weather conditions.
