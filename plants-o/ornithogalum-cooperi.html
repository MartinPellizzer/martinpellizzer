Ornithogalum cooperi

Ornithogalum cooperi, commonly known as Cooper's star-of-Bethlehem, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asparagaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. Native to southern Africa, this plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade. Its bulbs are toxic and should be handled with care.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
