Oxalis erythropoda

Oxalis erythropoda, commonly known as the bloodroot oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers and trifoliate leaves that resemble those of clover. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its flowers bloom in early spring and are followed by small, capsule-like fruits.

Oxalis erythropoda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant foliage and blooms.
