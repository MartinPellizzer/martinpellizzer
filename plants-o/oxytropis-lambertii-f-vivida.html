Oxytropis lambertii f. vivida

Oxytropis lambertii f. vivida, commonly known as the vivida oxytropis, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually yellow or pale green. It is characterized by its erect growth habit and is often found in mountainous or steppe environments.

This subspecies is noted for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh, nutrient-poor conditions.
