Oenothera communis var. cruciata

Oenothera communis var. cruciata, commonly known as evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening, hence its common name. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

Oenothera communis var. cruciata is a member of the Onagraceae family and is known for its ecological importance as a food source for various pollinators.
