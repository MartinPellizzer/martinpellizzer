Ocotea rohweri

Ocotea rohweri, commonly known as the African ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Central and East Africa.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed surrounded by a fleshy, edible pulp.

Ocotea rohweri is valued for its timber, which is used in construction and furniture-making due to its durability and fine grain.
