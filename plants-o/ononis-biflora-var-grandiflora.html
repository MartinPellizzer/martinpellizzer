Ononis biflora var. grandiflora

Ononis biflora var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered sandrocket, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or inland dunes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
