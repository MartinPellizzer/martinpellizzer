Oxypetalum arachnoideum

Oxypetalum arachnoideum, commonly known as the spider-tailed orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique inflorescence, which features elongated, spider-like structures that support its showy, white or pale pink flowers. The plant has a compact growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, fragrant blossoms that bloom during the cooler months. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a threatened species in its native range.
