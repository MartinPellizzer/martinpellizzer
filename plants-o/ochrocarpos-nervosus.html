Ochrocarpos nervosus

Ochrocarpos nervosus, commonly known as the veined orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with intricate veining patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its leaves are usually long and narrow, with a waxy surface that helps retain moisture.

Ochrocarpos nervosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
