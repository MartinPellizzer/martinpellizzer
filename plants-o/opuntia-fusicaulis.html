Opuntia fusicaulis

Opuntia fusicaulis, commonly known as the narrow-armed prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are often covered in clusters of spines and glochids. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tunas, is edible and commonly used in traditional cuisine.

Opuntia fusicaulis is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.
