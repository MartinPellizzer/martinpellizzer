Opuntia hirschii

Opuntia hirschii, commonly known as the Hirsch's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in clusters of spines and glochids, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months, followed by edible, fleshy fruits known as tunas. Its succulent leaves store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments with minimal rainfall.

Opuntia hirschii plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing food and shelter for various desert wildlife species.
