Orchidantha lengguanii

Orchidantha lengguanii, commonly known as the Guangdong orchid, is a rare and endangered species native to China.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, fragrant flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as mountain forests, where it thrives on decaying organic matter. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the forest floor, and it has a unique ability to form symbiotic relationships with specific fungi.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
