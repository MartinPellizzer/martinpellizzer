Oxytropis fragiliphylla

Oxytropis fragiliphylla, commonly known as the fragileleaf milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the arid regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, often fragile leaves and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes many nitrogen-fixing plants, contributing to soil fertility.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
