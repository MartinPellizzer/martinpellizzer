Opuntia glaucophylla

Opuntia glaucophylla, commonly known as the blue leaf prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems that are typically blue-green in color, covered in spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible, red-fleshed fruits. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to arid environments.

Opuntia glaucophylla is well-suited for xeriscaping and is often used in desert landscaping due to its drought tolerance.
