Olax benthamiana

Olax benthamiana, commonly known as the Benthams' olax, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the family Olacaceae and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant features simple, alternate leaves with a smooth surface and a slightly waxy texture. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically in greenish-yellow or white hues, and are borne on short pedicels.

The species is notable for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in local ecosystems.
