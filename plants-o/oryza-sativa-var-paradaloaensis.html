Oryza sativa var. paradaloaensis

Oryza sativa var. paradaloaensis, commonly known as the Paradaloaensis rice, is a rare and less-studied variant of rice native to Southeast Asia.

This variety is characterized by its unique grain morphology, featuring a distinct coloration and texture compared to common rice types. Botanically, it possesses a fibrous root system and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a waxy surface. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 1.5 meters, with panicles that produce small, white flowers.

Its genetic diversity makes it an important subject for research in agricultural science and plant breeding.
