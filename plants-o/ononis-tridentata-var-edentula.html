Ononis tridentata var. edentula

Ononis tridentata var. edentula, commonly known as the toothed restharrow, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that produce small, yellowish flowers in early summer. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with three lobes that are often toothed, though the variety edentula has reduced or absent teeth. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
