Orthocarpus purpurascens var. latifolius

Orthocarpus purpurascens var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf orthocarpus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange composite flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
