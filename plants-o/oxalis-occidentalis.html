Oxalis occidentalis

Oxalis occidentalis, commonly known as wood sorrel, is a herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive reddish veins and small, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, edible fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Oxalis occidentalis is often used in ornamental gardening due to its delicate appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
