Orchis spitzelii f. teschneriana

Orchis spitzelii f. teschneriana, commonly known as the Teschner's orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to parts of Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that typically display a mix of purple and white hues. The plant features long, narrow leaves and produces spikes of blooms during the late spring to early summer season. It thrives in moist, woodland environments and is often found in shaded, humus-rich soils.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in many regions.
