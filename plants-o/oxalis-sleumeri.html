Oxalis sleumeri

Oxalis sleumeri, commonly known as the Sleumer's wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded, moist environments. It produces distinctive seed pods that burst open to disperse seeds, a feature common in many members of its family.

Oxalis sleumeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
