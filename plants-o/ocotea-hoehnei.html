Ocotea hoehnei

Ocotea hoehnei, commonly known as the Hoehne's ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elliptical, glossy leaves. It produces small, white flowers followed by dark blue berries that are dispersed by birds.

Ocotea hoehnei is valued for its aromatic wood and plays a role in the ecological balance of its habitat.
