Orchis albertii

Orchis albertii, commonly known as Albert's orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stem that can reach up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained, calcareous soils.

Orchis albertii is typically found in open woodland and meadow habitats, blooming from June to August.
