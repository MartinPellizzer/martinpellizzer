Oxalis pallida

Oxalis pallida, commonly known as the pale wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive white veins and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink or white. The plant is often found in moist, shady environments such as forests and stream sides. It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its ability to thrive in partial to full shade.

Oxalis pallida is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and as a ground cover.
