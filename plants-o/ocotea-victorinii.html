Ocotea victorinii

Ocotea victorinii, commonly known as the Guadeloupe laurier, is a small evergreen tree native to the Caribbean islands of Guadeloupe and Martinique.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurel, cinnamon, and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, glossy, dark green leaves and small, clustered white flowers. Its fruit is a small, dark purple drupe that contains a single seed.

Ocotea victorinii is often found in moist, shaded forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
