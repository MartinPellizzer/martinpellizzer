Oxalis melanograpta

Oxalis melanograpta, commonly known as the black-veined wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with dark purple veins and small, five-petaled flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas. Its foliage and flowers are attractive, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Oxalis melanograpta is also valued for its ability to naturalize and spread through rhizomes.
