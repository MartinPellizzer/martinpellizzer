Orobus ramuliflorus

Orobus ramuliflorus, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, slender stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often clustered at the base, while its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption.

Orobus ramuliflorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
