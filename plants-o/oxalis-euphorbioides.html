Oxalis euphorbioides

Oxalis euphorbioides, commonly known as the false wood sorrel, is a flowering plant belonging to the Oxalidaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Oxalis euphorbioides is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and blooms.
