Ochna suberosa

Ochna suberosa, commonly known as the African mahogany, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, flaky bark that peels off in strips, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange-red berries containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Ochna suberosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses in local communities.
