Oxylobium procumbens

Oxylobium procumbens, commonly known as the lowland oxylobium, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. The plant features small, delicate flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Oxylobium procumbens is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
