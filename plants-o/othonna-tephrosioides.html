Othonna tephrosioides

Othonna tephrosioides, commonly known as the blue oat grass, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features wiry, grass-like stems and produces small, blue to purple flowers in dense spikes. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy and adapted to arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value comes from its striking flower color and compact growth habit.
