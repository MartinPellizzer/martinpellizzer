Oreophilus pseudocaulescens

Oreophilus pseudocaulescens, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, often leafless stems and clusters of small, fragrant orchid-like flowers. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments with high humidity and well-drained, nutrient-rich soil. Its unique morphology and ecological niche make it a subject of interest for botanists studying alpine flora.

Due to habitat loss and climate change, it faces significant conservation challenges.
