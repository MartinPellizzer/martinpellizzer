Ophrys × chatenieri var. bakonyensis

Ophrys × chatenieri var. bakonyensis, commonly known as the Bakony Ophrys, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Carpathian Basin.

It is characterized by its intricate, bee-mimicking flowers that play a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is found in woodland and meadow habitats. Its floral morphology includes a well-developed labellum that mimics the appearance of a female bee, attracting male bees for pollination.

This variety is of particular interest in botanical studies due to its unique hybrid origin and specialized ecological interactions.
