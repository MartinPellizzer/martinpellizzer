Orthosiphon argenteus

Orthosiphon argenteus, commonly known as silver siphon or silver horse plant, is a member of the Lamiaceae family.

It is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its silver-gray leaves, which are covered in fine hairs, and its spikes of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often used in traditional medicine for its diuretic properties.

The leaves and flowers are typically harvested for use in herbal remedies and teas.
