Ossaea cinerea

Ossaea cinerea, commonly known as the pale lichen, is a member of the Lecanorales order and belongs to the family Lecanoraceae.

It is a foliose lichen, characterized by its leaf-like thallus that is typically pale green to grey in color. The plant forms a symbiotic relationship with algae and cyanobacteria, which contribute to its photosynthetic capabilities. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found on tree bark or soil surfaces in temperate regions.

Ossaea cinerea is known for its slow growth and is often used as an indicator species for air quality due to its sensitivity to pollution.
