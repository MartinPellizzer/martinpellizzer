Oxytropis zaquensis

Oxytropis zaquensis, commonly known as the Zaquen's locoweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, and it is adapted to grow in dry, rocky environments.

Oxytropis zaquensis is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, which helps improve soil fertility in its habitat.
