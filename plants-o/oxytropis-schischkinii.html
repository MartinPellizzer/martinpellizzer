Oxytropis schischkinii

Oxytropis schischkinii, commonly known as Schischkin's milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Central Asia and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale purple or white in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in steppes and rocky slopes.

Oxytropis schischkinii is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it ecologically significant in its native habitats.
