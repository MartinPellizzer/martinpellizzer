Olea europaea var. polymorpha

Olea europaea var. polymorpha, commonly known as the Turkish olive, is a variant of the olive tree native to the eastern Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its smaller, more elongated leaves and smaller fruits compared to the typical olive. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, preferring well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its fruit, which can be used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The species is also valued for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
