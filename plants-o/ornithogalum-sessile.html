Ornithogalum sessile

Ornithogalum sessile, commonly known as the sessile star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, linear leaves and produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers during the summer months. The plant has a fibrous root system and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils. It is often found in grasslands and rocky areas, and it is known for its ornamental value.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and moths, contributing to its ecological significance.
