Oxalis tenuifolia

Oxalis tenuifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically narrow and have a heart-shaped base. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Oxalis tenuifolia is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
