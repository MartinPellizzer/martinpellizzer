Odontoglossum duvivierianum

Odontoglossum duvivierianum, commonly known as the Duvivier's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the highlands of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, producing new shoots from the base of the plant. The plant features elongated, narrow leaves and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in greenhouses or specialized gardens.
