Oligolepis amaranthoides

Oligolepis amaranthoides, commonly known as the silverleaf amaranth, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, silver-gray leaves that reflect sunlight. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from green to red, depending on the species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Oligolepis amaranthoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
