Oligosporus tomentellus

Oligosporus tomentellus, commonly known as the woolly spore moss, is a species of liverwort belonging to the family Porellaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth form and is covered in a fine, woolly layer of hairs, giving it its common name. Its reproductive structures, known as sporophytes, are small and club-shaped, bearing spores that are dispersed by wind.

Oligosporus tomentellus plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for microorganisms in its native ecosystems.
