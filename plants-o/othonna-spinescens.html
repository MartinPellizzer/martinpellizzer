Othonna spinescens

Othonna spinescens, commonly known as the spiny othonna, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its sparsely branched stems and small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically followed by small, spherical fruits. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Othonna spinescens is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
