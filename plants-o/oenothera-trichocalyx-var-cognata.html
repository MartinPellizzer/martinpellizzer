Oenothera trichocalyx var. cognata

Oenothera trichocalyx var. cognata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

The species is valued for its ecological role and is also cultivated for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
