Opuntia zacuapanensis

Opuntia zacuapanensis, commonly known as the Zacuapan prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems that are covered in spines and are often ribbed. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the summer months. Its fruit, known as a tunaf, is edible and contains numerous small, sweet seeds.

Opuntia zacuapanensis is well adapted to arid environments and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
