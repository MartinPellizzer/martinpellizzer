Osmunda monticola

Osmunda monticola, commonly known as the mountain shield fern, is a species of fern native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tall, slender fronds that can reach up to 2 meters in height, with a distinctive, shield-like shape. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks, where it forms dense colonies. Its rhizomes are thick and fleshy, allowing it to store water and nutrients efficiently.

Osmunda monticola plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various organisms.
