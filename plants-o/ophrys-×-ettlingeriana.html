Ophrys × ettlingeriana

Ophrys × ettlingeriana, commonly known as the Ettlinger's bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Balkans.

It belongs to the genus Ophrys, which is known for its mimicry of female insects to attract male pollinators. The plant produces striking flowers that resemble female bees, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

The hybrid nature of this species contributes to its genetic diversity and adaptability in different ecological conditions.
