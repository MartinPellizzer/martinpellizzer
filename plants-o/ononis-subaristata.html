Ononis subaristata

Ononis subaristata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are pinkish-white with five petals and are arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

It blooms from late spring to early summer and is adapted to thrive in rocky or sandy soils.
