Origanum vulgare f. semiglaucum

Origanum vulgare f. semiglaucum, commonly known as sweet marjoram, is a cultivar of the common oregano plant.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and aromatic, slightly hairy leaves. The foliage is typically a mix of green and grayish-blue, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators.

This variety is often used in culinary applications and herbal medicine due to its essential oils and aromatic properties.
