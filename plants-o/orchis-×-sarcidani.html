Orchis × sarcidani

Orchis × sarcidani, commonly known as the Sarcidani orchid, is a natural hybrid between Orchis morio and Orchis mascula.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family, characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or grassland habitats. Its flowers are borne in dense spikes and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and flies.

The Sarcidani orchid is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive appearance.
