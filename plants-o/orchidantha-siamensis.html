Orchidantha siamensis

Orchidantha siamensis, commonly known as the Siam orchid, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant blossoms. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by insects.

Orchidantha siamensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
