Oxalis novae-guineensis

Oxalis novae-guineensis, commonly known as the New Guinea wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of New Guinea.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its foliage is notable for its bright green color and the presence of calcium oxalate crystals, which can cause irritation if ingested.

Oxalis novae-guineensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
