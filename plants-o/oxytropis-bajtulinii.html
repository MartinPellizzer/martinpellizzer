Oxytropis bajtulinii

Oxytropis bajtulinii, commonly known as the Bajtulin's locoweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Oxytropis bajtulinii is known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock.
