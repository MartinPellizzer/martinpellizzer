Oenothera pyramidalis var. heterophylla

Oenothera pyramidalis var. heterophylla, commonly known as the variable evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant exhibits variable leaf morphology, with some leaves being lance-shaped and others more rounded, contributing to its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and horticultural interest.
