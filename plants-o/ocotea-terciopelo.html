Ocotea terciopelo

Ocotea terciopelo, commonly known as velvetwood or velvetleaf, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its dark, aromatic bark and glossy, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by elliptical, dark blue berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The tree is valued for its hard, heavy wood, which is used in furniture and construction.
