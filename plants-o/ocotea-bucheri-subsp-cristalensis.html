Ocotea bucheri subsp. cristalensis

Ocotea bucheri subsp.

cristalensis, commonly known as the Cristalense Ocotea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family. It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical conditions.

The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in the spring. Its wood is valued for its fine grain and is used in furniture and cabinetry.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
