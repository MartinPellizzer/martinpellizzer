Oryza sativa var. atra

Oryza sativa var. atra, commonly known as black rice, is a variety of rice characterized by its dark, almost black grain color.

This coloration is due to the high concentration of anthocyanin pigments, which are antioxidants beneficial to human health. The plant is an annual grass that thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring warm temperatures and ample water for optimal growth. Black rice is cultivated primarily for its nutritional value and is used in various cuisines around the world.

It is also valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often used in decorative and ceremonial contexts.
