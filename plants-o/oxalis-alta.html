Oxalis alta

Oxalis alta, commonly known as the tall wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each leaflet resembling a heart shape, and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. It is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.

Oxalis alta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
