Ocotea velloziana

Ocotea velloziana, commonly known as the Brazilian camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by dark blue berries. Its leaves are typically elliptical with a smooth surface and a fragrant aroma.

Ocotea velloziana is valued for its timber and has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of essential oils.
