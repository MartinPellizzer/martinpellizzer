Ochna kirkii subsp. multisetosa

Ochna kirkii subsp.

multisetosa, commonly known as the African wild olive, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green on top and lighter beneath.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by distinctive, orange-red berries. Its bark is smooth and greyish, often exuding a milky sap when wounded.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
