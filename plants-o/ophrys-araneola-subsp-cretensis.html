Ophrys araneola subsp. cretensis

Ophrys araneola subsp.

cretensis, commonly known as the Cretan bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the island of Crete. It is characterized by its unique floral morphology, featuring a strikingly intricate flower that mimics the appearance of a wasp, which aids in pollination by bees.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in the Mediterranean climate of its native habitat. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing a delicate, greenish-yellow color with a distinctive, fuzzy texture.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts in its native region.
