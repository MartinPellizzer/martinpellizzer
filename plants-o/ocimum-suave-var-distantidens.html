Ocimum suave var. distantidens

Ocimum suave var. distantidens, commonly known as the distant-flowered basil, is a variant of the sweet basil species native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and clusters of small, pale purple to white flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. It thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth.

Its aromatic leaves are often used in culinary and medicinal applications, similar to other basil varieties.
