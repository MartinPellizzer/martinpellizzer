Odontoglossum werneri

Odontoglossum werneri, commonly known as the Werneri's orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb that supports its growth during periods of dormancy. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and green, often with intricate patterns on the petals. It prefers cool, humid environments and is typically found in forest understories.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
