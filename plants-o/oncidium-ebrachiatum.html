Oncidium ebrachiatum

Oncidium ebrachiatum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical orchid species native to Central and South America.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or golden in color, often with contrasting red or purple markings. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, adapted to thrive in humid, well-drained environments.

Oncidium ebrachiatum is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
