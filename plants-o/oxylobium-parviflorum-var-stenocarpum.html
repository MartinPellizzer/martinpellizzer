Oxylobium parviflorum var. stenocarpum

Oxylobium parviflorum var. stenocarpum, commonly known as the narrow-fruited oxylobium, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, woody pods that contain several seeds.

It is typically found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to coastal and heathland environments.
