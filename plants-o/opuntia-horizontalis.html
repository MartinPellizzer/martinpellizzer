Opuntia horizontalis

Opuntia horizontalis, commonly known as the flat prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems that are ribbed and covered in spines. The plant produces bright yellow or orange flowers during the summer months, which are followed by edible, red-fleshed fruits. Opuntia horizontalis is drought-tolerant and thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.

Its stems are used for ornamental purposes and in traditional medicine.
