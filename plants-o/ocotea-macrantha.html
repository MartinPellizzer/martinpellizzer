Ocotea macrantha

Ocotea macrantha, commonly known as the large-fruit ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and fragrant wood. The plant typically grows in humid lowland forests and can reach heights of up to 30 meters. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces dark blue, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife.

Ocotea macrantha is valued for its timber and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
