Oldenlandia arborea

Oldenlandia arborea, commonly known as the tree of heaven, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and fruit.
