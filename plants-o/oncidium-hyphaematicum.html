Oncidium hyphaematicum

Oncidium hyphaematicum, commonly known as the blood orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often red or orange-pink flowers that resemble blood droplets, hence its common name. The plant has slender, erect pseudobulbs and long, strap-like leaves that are typically green with a reddish tinge. Its flowers are borne on arching inflorescences and are known for their strong, spicy fragrance.

Oncidium hyphaematicum is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
