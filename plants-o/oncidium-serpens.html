Oncidium serpens

Oncidium serpens, commonly known as the snake orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It features a sympodial growth habit with pseudobulbs and is characterized by its striking, snake-like inflorescence that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. It requires bright, indirect light and well-draining potting media to thrive.

Oncidium serpens is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.
