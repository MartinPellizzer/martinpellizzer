Oxytropis adamsiana subsp. adamsiana

Oxytropis adamsiana subsp.

adamsiana, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit, trifoliate leaves, and showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in dryland gardens.
