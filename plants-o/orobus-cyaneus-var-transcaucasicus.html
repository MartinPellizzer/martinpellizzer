Orobus cyaneus var. transcaucasicus

Orobus cyaneus var. transcaucasicus, commonly known as the Caucasian bluebell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features slender, erect stems that bear clusters of striking blue to violet flowers. The leaves are lanceolate and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained, loamy soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
