Omalotheca sylvatica

Omalotheca sylvatica, commonly known as the swamp milkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland habitats in the southeastern United States.

It features showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. Omalotheca sylvatica thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in swamps, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
