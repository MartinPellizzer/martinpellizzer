Oxalis knuthii

Oxalis knuthii, commonly known as the Knuth's wood sorrel, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. It produces edible leaves and flowers, though they should be consumed in moderation due to their oxalic acid content.

Oxalis knuthii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and floral display.
