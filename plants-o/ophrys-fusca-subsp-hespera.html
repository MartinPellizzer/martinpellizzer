Ophrys fusca subsp. hespera

Ophrys fusca subsp.

hespera, commonly known as the western bee orchid, is a specialized orchid species native to the western United States. It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination.

The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, often in mountainous regions. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with intricate patterns that enhance the mimicry.

The subspecies is distinguished by its geographical distribution and slight morphological differences from the nominate form.
