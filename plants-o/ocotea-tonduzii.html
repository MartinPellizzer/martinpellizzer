Ocotea tonduzii

Ocotea tonduzii, commonly known as the Tonduzii Ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a drupe, typically containing a single seed enclosed in a hard, woody pulp.

Ocotea tonduzii is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional crafts and as a source of essential oils.
