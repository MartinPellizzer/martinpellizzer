Oryzopsis montevidensis

Oryzopsis montevidensis, commonly known as the Montevidense grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly Uruguay and parts of Argentina.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically green or pale yellow in color. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Oryzopsis montevidensis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
