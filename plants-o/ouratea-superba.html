Ouratea superba

Ouratea superba, commonly known as the golden mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant has a symbiotic relationship with its host trees, drawing water and nutrients through its haustoria. Its fruits are small, bright red berries that contain seeds dispersed by birds.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is a symbol of the region's unique biodiversity.
