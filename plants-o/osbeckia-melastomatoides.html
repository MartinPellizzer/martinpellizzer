Osbeckia melastomatoides

Osbeckia melastomatoides, commonly known as the purple-stemmed gooseberry, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit, with hairy, often reddish stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, followed by fleshy, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, and covered in fine hairs.

Osbeckia melastomatoides is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.
