Onobrychis gaertneriana

Onobrychis gaertneriana, commonly known as the Gaertner's burclover, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

It has a taproot system that allows it to thrive in arid conditions, and it is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.
