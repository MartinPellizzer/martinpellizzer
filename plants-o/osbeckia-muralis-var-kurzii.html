Osbeckia muralis var. kurzii

Osbeckia muralis var. kurzii, commonly known as the wall osbeckia, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found growing on slopes or in crevices.

Osbeckia muralis var. kurzii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in marginal environments.
