Ophrys subfusca subsp. numida

Ophrys subfusca subsp.

numida, commonly known as the Numidian bee orchid, is a species of orchid native to parts of North Africa and the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its intricate flower structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee, a feature that aids in its unique pollination strategy.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny, open habitats. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with dark markings, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

This orchid is notable for its specialized relationship with specific pollinators, enhancing its ecological significance in its native environment.
