Oenothera missouriensis var. latifolia

Oenothera missouriensis var. latifolia, commonly known as the Missouri evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the central United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening and close by morning. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as moths and beetles.
