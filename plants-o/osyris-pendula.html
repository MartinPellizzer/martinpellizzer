Osyris pendula

Osyris pendula, commonly known as the hanging osyris, is a tropical plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its drooping, pendulous flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its common name. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with a distinctive tubular shape that attracts pollinators like bees and birds.

Osyris pendula is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
