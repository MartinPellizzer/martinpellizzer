Osmia pulchella

Osmia pulchella, commonly known as the showy mining bee, is a solitary bee species native to North America.

It is a small to medium-sized bee, typically ranging from 8 to 15 millimeters in length. The female Osmia pulchella is known for its distinctive metallic blue or green coloration, which serves as a form of aposematic coloration to deter predators. This species is often found in open habitats such as meadows and gardens, where it forages on a variety of flowering plants.

Osmia pulchella plays an important role in pollination, contributing to the ecological balance of its native environment.
