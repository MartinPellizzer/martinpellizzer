Ophrys × ionica

Ophrys × ionica, commonly known as the bee orchid hybrid, is a fascinating plant that exhibits unique botanical features.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera, two species known for their mimicry of female insects. The plant produces intricate, bee-like flowers that serve as a deceptive strategy to attract male bees for pollination. Its floral morphology includes a prominent lip that mimics the appearance and scent of a female bee, enhancing its pollination success.

Ophrys × ionica typically grows in open, sunny habitats and is found across parts of Europe, particularly in the Mediterranean region.
