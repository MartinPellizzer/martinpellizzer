Ocotea capuronii

Ocotea capuronii, commonly known as the Capuron's ocotea, is a small evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a dark purple drupe, containing a single seed.

Ocotea capuronii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical botanical gardens.
