Onopordum acanthium subsp. araneosotomentosum

Onopordum acanthium subsp.

araneosotomentosum, commonly known as the woolly thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and large, showy purple or pink flower heads.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with a rough, hairy texture, and the flowers are composed of both ray and disk florets.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
