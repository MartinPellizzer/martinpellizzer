Orthosiphon tristis

Orthosiphon tristis, commonly known as Java tea or Malabar mint, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or violet in color. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Orthosiphon tristis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
