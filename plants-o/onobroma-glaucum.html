Onobroma glaucum

Onobroma glaucum, commonly known as the silverleaf globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-green foliage and showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its structure includes a woody stem with opposite leaves and capsule-like fruits that contain seeds.

Onobroma glaucum is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.
