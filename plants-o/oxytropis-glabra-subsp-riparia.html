Oxytropis glabra subsp. riparia

Oxytropis glabra subsp.

riparia, commonly known as the riparian milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It typically grows along riverbanks and moist soils, adapting to riparian habitats.

The plant features slender stems and small, compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pale blue to purple, arranged in small clusters, and bloom in early summer.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and wildlife.
