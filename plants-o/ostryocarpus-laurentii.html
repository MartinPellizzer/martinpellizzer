Ostryocarpus laurentii

Ostryocarpus laurentii, commonly known as the Laurentian thimbleweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

Ostryocarpus laurentii is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
