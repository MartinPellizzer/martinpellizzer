Oxydectes sublyrata

Oxydectes sublyrata, commonly known as the golden cup fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is a small, epiphytic fern that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features delicate, bright yellow fronds that are often curled into a cup-like shape. Its rhizomes are wiry and help it cling to tree trunks or rocks.

The species is native to tropical regions of Central and South America.
