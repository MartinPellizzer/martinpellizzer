Ocotea sauroderma

Ocotea sauroderma, commonly known as the Brazilian camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and fragrant bark. The plant produces small, white flowers and oval-shaped berries, which are typically dispersed by birds. Its wood is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

The tree thrives in humid, shaded environments and plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.
