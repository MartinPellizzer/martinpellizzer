Ononis pendula var. canariensis

Ononis pendula var. canariensis, commonly known as the Canary Island broom.

It is a shrub native to the Canary Islands, characterized by its slender, pendulous stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in coastal or rocky habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
