Orchis scutellata

Orchis scutellata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and striking violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers are often arranged in a spike and are pollinated by insects, particularly bees.

Orchis scutellata is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a role in supporting local biodiversity.
