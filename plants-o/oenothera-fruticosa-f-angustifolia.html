Oenothera fruticosa f. angustifolia

Oenothera fruticosa f. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, woody stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in desert or arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
