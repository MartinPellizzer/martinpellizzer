Oxalis densiflora

Oxalis densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish or purple coloration, and its flowers are small, five-petaled, and typically yellow or orange in color. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. Oxalis densiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.

It has also been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
