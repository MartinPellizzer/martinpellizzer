Osmanthus menglaensis

Osmanthus menglaensis, commonly known as the Mengla osmanthus, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in late autumn. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are opposite in arrangement and have a slightly hairy texture. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that contains several seeds.

Osmanthus menglaensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its aromatic flowers and historical significance in traditional Chinese culture.
