Ocotea nervosa

Ocotea nervosa, commonly known as the narrow-veined ocotea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a small, dark purple drupe that contains a single seed.

Ocotea nervosa is often found in humid, lowland forests and is valued for its essential oils and medicinal properties.
