Onobrychis peduncularis var. eriophora

Onobrychis peduncularis var. eriophora, commonly known as the narrow-leafed burclover, is a perennial legume native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, three-lobed pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

This species is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen and its use in forage and erosion control.
