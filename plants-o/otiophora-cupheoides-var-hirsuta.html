Otiophora cupheoides var. hirsuta

Otiophora cupheoides var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy cuphee, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its foliage is often covered in fine, soft hairs, which give it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
