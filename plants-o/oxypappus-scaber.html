Oxypappus scaber

Oxypappus scaber, commonly known as roughleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in open habitats such as prairies and disturbed areas. Its leaves are covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving them a rough texture.

The small yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
