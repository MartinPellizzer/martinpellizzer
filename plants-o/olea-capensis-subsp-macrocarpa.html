Olea capensis subsp. macrocarpa

Olea capensis subsp.

macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited olive, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant produces large, fleshy fruits that are edible and have a mild, nutty flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
