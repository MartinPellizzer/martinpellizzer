Ophrys × delargeana

Ophrys × delargeana, commonly known as the Delargean bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is characterized by its intricate floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in woodland and meadow habitats across Europe, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually a mix of brown, yellow, and white, with a distinctive bee-like shape.

Ophrys × delargeana is a short-lived perennial, often found in small populations due to its specific ecological requirements.
