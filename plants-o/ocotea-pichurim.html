Ocotea pichurim

Ocotea pichurim, commonly known as the Pichurim-laurel, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the humid forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, dark blue berries. Ocotea pichurim is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.

Its wood is also used in local crafts and traditional medicine.
