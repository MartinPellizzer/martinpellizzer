Oplismenus humbertianus

Oplismenus humbertianus, commonly known as the common sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its triangular, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in three rows. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in dense clusters, which are often surrounded by bracts. Oplismenus humbertianus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
