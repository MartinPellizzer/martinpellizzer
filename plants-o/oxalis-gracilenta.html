Oxalis gracilenta

Oxalis gracilenta, commonly known as the slender wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage.

Oxalis gracilenta is also valued for its ability to thrive in partial shade and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
