Oxytropis coerulea subsp. subfalcata

Oxytropis coerulea subsp.

subfalcata, commonly known as blue locoweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its blue to violet flower clusters.

The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. Oxytropis coerulea subsp.

subfalcata is adapted to arid environments and is often found in areas with well-drained soils.
