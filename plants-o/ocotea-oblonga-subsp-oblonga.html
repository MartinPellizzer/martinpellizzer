Ocotea oblonga subsp. oblonga

Ocotea oblonga subsp.

oblonga, commonly known as the broadleaf ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America. It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees.

The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped, glossy dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed. Ocotea oblonga subsp.

oblonga is valued for its aromatic wood and is often found in humid lowland forests.
