Omphacarpus opacus

Omphacarpus opacus, commonly known as the black nightshade, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are typically white or pale green.

The species is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain regions.
