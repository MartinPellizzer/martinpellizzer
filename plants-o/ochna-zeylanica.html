Ochna zeylanica

Ochna zeylanica, commonly known as the Ceylon ochna, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern and southeastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in cymes, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits.

Ochna zeylanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in folk medicine.
