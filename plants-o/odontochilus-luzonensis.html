Odontochilus luzonensis

Odontochilus luzonensis, commonly known as the Luzon toothed orchid, is a rare and endemic orchid species found exclusively in the Philippines.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers with distinctive tooth-like structures on the lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on tree trunks or rocky substrates in montane forests. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted to conserve moisture in its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat destruction, it is considered critically endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
