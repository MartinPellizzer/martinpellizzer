Oncidium eurycline

Oncidium eurycline, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, showy blooms. The plant typically produces clusters of yellow or golden-orange flowers with a distinctive, elongated shape. Oncidium eurycline is an epiphytic orchid, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic.

It requires bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.
