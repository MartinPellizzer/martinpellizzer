Oenothera bulgarica

Oenothera bulgarica, commonly known as the Bulgarian evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening. The plant has hairy, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and rocky areas.

Oenothera bulgarica is part of the Onagraceae family, which includes other evening primroses known for their nocturnal pollination by moths.
