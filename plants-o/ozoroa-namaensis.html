Ozoroa namaensis

Ozoroa namaensis, commonly known as the Namib Desert lily, is a flowering plant native to the Namib Desert in Namibia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves adapted to arid conditions. The plant produces striking white or pink flowers during the rainy season, typically in late winter to early spring. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep groundwater in the nutrient-poor sandy soils of its habitat.

Ozoroa namaensis plays a vital role in the desert ecosystem, providing food and shelter for various insects and small animals.
