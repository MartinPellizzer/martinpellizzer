Ochna opaca

Ochna opaca, commonly known as the African wild pear, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and opposite, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and paler underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, pear-shaped fruits that are yellow when ripe. Ochna opaca is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a source of food for both wildlife and humans.
