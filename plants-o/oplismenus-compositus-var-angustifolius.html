Oplismenus compositus var. angustifolius

Oplismenus compositus var. angustifolius, commonly known as narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a distinctive pointed tip. The plant produces small, pinkish-white composite flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist to wet soils along stream banks and in wetlands.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and wetland habitats.
