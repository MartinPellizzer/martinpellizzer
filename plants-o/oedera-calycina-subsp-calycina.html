Oedera calycina subsp. calycina

Oedera calycina subsp.

calycina, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a member of the Rutaceae family and is native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its smooth, reddish-bark and lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by spherical, woody fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic and practical properties.
