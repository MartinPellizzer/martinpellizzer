Oreocarya ciliohirsuta

Oreocarya ciliohirsuta, commonly known as the hairy leaf saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, hirsute leaves that are often covered in fine, soft trichomes. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Oreocarya ciliohirsuta is often found in high-altitude meadows and is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family.
