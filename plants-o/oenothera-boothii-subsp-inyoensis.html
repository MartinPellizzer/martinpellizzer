Oenothera boothii subsp. inyoensis

Oenothera boothii subsp.

inyoensis, commonly known as the Inyo evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

The plant has hairy, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in desert ecosystems.
