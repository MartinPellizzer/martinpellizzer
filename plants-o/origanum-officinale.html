Origanum officinale

Origanum officinale, commonly known as oregano, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its small, pale green leaves and pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is known for its aromatic, pungent leaves. Oregano thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for Mediterranean gardens.

Its essential oils, rich in compounds like carvacrol and thymol, contribute to its widespread use in cooking and traditional medicine.
