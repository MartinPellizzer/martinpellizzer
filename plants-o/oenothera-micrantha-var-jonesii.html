Oenothera micrantha var. jonesii

Oenothera micrantha var. jonesii, commonly known as Jones' evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its stems are often hairy, and the leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly hairy surface.

This variety is noted for its ecological importance in supporting local pollinator populations and its adaptability to harsh, dry conditions.
