Ocotea matensis

Ocotea matensis, commonly known as the Guianese ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and fragrant, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, spherical berries that contain a single seed, which is dispersed by animals. Its wood is valued for its fine grain and durability, making it useful in carpentry and furniture making.

Ocotea matensis plays a significant ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
