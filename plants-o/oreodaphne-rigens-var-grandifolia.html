Oreodaphne rigens var. grandifolia

Oreodaphne rigens var. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-leaved oreodaphne, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often leathery in texture and have a distinctive reddish or purple hue when young. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by round, fleshy berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
