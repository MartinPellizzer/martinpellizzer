Oenothera austromontana

Oenothera austromontana, commonly known as the mountain evening primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in mountainous regions, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Oenothera austromontana is part of the evening primrose family (Onagraceae) and is known for its ecological importance in supporting pollinator populations.
