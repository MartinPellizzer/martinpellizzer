Oenothera scapoidea var. claviformis

Oenothera scapoidea var. claviformis, commonly known as the clustered evening primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features slender, branching stems that often form dense clusters, giving it its varietal name "claviformis," meaning club-shaped. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained sandy or loamy soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
