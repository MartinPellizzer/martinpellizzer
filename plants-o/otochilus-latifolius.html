Otochilus latifolius

Otochilus latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf pincushion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, spiny heads of yellow flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Otochilus latifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
