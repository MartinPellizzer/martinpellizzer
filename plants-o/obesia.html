Obesia

Obesia, commonly known as the "fat plant," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Gesneriaceae.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and showy, often brightly colored flowers. These plants are native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas. Obesia species are typically evergreen and can be either shrubs or small trees.

They are popular in horticulture for their ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
