Onopordum boissieri

Onopordum boissieri, commonly known as the Algerian thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowering heads are composed of numerous small florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Onopordum boissieri is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
