Othocallis libanotica

Othocallis libanotica, commonly known as the Lebanon spurge, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its succulent, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, typically blooming in the spring. Othocallis libanotica is adapted to arid and rocky environments, showcasing its resilience to drought.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in xerophytic plant ecology.
