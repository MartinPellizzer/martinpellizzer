Oxalis blastorhiza

Oxalis blastorhiza, commonly known as the zigzag oxalis, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive zigzag-shaped stems and trifoliate leaves that resemble those of true oxalis species. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white, pink, and yellow, typically blooming in the spring. Oxalis blastorhiza is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
