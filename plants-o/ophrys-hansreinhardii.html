Ophrys hansreinhardii

Ophrys hansreinhardii, commonly known as the Hans Reinhard's orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It is characterized by its unique, bee-like flower structure, which plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats, often in forested areas with rich, humusy soil. Its flowers are usually a mix of brown and yellow, mimicking the appearance of female bees to attract male pollinators.
