Orthochilus eustachyus

Orthochilus eustachyus, commonly known as the golden-eyed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking yellow and green flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a short, horizontal rhizome and produces a single erect stem with several lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are spurred at the base and have a distinctive golden coloration, making it visually appealing.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.
