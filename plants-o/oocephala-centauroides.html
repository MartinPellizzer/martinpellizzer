Oocephala centauroides

Oocephala centauroides, commonly known as the centaur's shield, is a striking plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are adapted to store water, allowing it to thrive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its unique leaf shape resembles a shield, giving it its common name and making it a popular ornamental plant.

Oocephala centauroides is also valued for its resilience and ability to withstand extreme temperatures and drought conditions.
