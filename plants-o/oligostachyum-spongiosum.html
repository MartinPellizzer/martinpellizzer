Oligostachyum spongiosum

Oligostachyum spongiosum, commonly known as the spongy bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that are often covered in a spongy, fibrous sheath, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Oligostachyum spongiosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
