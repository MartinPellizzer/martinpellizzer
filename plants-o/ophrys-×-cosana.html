Ophrys × cosana

Ophrys × cosana, commonly known as the bee orchid hybrid, is a fascinating plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its intricate floral structures that mimic the appearance of female bees. The plant produces showy, bee-like flowers that are highly specialized for pollination through mimicry. Its flowers typically have a dark brown to black coloration with yellow markings, resembling a bee's body.

Ophrys × cosana thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas across its native range.
