Ochna bakeriana

Ochna bakeriana, commonly known as the African glorybower, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical in shape and have a smooth texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ochna bakeriana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
