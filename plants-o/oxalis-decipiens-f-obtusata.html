Oxalis decipiens f. obtusata

Oxalis decipiens f. obtusata, commonly known as the blunt-leaf wood sorrel, is a variant of the wood sorrel species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves with a distinctive reddish or purplish hue. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Oxalis decipiens f. obtusata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
