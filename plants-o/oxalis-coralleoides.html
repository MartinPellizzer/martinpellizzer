Oxalis coralleoides

Oxalis coralleoides, commonly known as the coral-like wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, coral-like inflorescences that resemble tiny bells or flowers, giving it its common name. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive pattern of veins, and its flowers are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Oxalis coralleoides is also noted for its ability to bloom throughout the year under suitable conditions.
