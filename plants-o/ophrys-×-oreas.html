Ophrys × oreas

Ophrys × oreas, commonly known as the orchid bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is characterized by its intricate floral morphology, featuring a lip that mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in Mediterranean regions, thriving in sunny, well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually yellow or brown with intricate patterns, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

Ophrys × oreas plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by supporting pollinator populations.
