Oenothera acutifolia

Oenothera acutifolia, commonly known as sharpleaf evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Oenothera acutifolia is a member of the Onagraceae family and is often found in prairies, open woodlands, and disturbed soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinator populations.
