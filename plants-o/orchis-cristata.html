Orchis cristata

Orchis cristata, commonly known as the crested orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spurred flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. The plant has a single, upright stem that bears several leaves and a spike of flowers, with the unique crest-like structure on the lip of each blossom. Orchis cristata thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
