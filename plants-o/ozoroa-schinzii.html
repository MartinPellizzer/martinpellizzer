Ozoroa schinzii

Ozoroa schinzii, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant is characterized by its large, fragrant white flowers that open at night, emitting a strong scent to attract nocturnal pollinators. Its leaves are usually simple and alternate, with a rough, hairy texture.

Ozoroa schinzii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in warm climates for its unique blooming habit.
