Oxalis griffithii f. rubriflora

Oxalis griffithii f. rubriflora, commonly known as the red-flowered wood sorrel, is a striking ornamental plant characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant red flowers.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is native to regions of southern Asia, particularly India. The plant features clusters of small, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to gardens. Its foliage is often variegated, with a mix of green and white, enhancing its visual appeal.

Oxalis griffithii f. rubriflora thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a versatile choice for both containers and garden beds.
