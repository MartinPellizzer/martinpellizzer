Orobus incurvus

Orobus incurvus, commonly known as the curly orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, curling inflorescence. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, and they bloom during the summer months.

Orobus incurvus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
