Ocotea bifida

Ocotea bifida, commonly known as the fragrant ocotea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in countries like Brazil and Colombia. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves, which emit a pleasant scent when crushed. Its evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers make it a distinctive member of the laurel family.

Ocotea bifida is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests.
