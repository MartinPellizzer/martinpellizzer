Ocotea patens

Ocotea patens, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, aromatic bark and dark green, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Ocotea patens thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture-making.
