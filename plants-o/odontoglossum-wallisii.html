Odontoglossum wallisii

Odontoglossum wallisii, commonly known as the Wallisii orchid, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, spurred flowers. The plant produces intricate, fragrant blooms in shades of white, yellow, and green, often with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Odontoglossum wallisii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
