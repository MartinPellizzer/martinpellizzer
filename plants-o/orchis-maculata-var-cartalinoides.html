Orchis maculata var. cartalinoides

Orchis maculata var. cartalinoides, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive purple and white spotted flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption.

This variety is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
