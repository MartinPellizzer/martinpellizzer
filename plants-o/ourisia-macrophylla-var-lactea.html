Ourisia macrophylla var. lactea

Ourisia macrophylla var. lactea, commonly known as the milkleaf ourisia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, milk-white leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
