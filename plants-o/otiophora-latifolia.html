Otiophora latifolia

Otiophora latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It possesses broad, ovate leaves that are often glossy and arranged alternately along its stems. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers followed by fleshy, berry-like fruits that contain toxic seeds. Its roots form haustoria, specialized structures that penetrate the host's vascular system to extract water and nutrients.

Otiophora latifolia plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for certain birds and insects, despite its toxic properties.
