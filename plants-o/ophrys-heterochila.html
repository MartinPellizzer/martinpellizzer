Ophrys heterochila

Ophrys heterochila, commonly known as the heterochila orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its unique, bee-like appearance, which is an evolutionary adaptation for pollination. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded habitats. Its flowers have a specialized structure with a prominent lip that mimics the appearance of a female bee.

Ophrys heterochila is known for its intricate pollination mechanism, involving mimicry to attract male bees for cross-pollination.
