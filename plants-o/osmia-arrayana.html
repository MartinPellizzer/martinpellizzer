Osmia arrayana

Osmia arrayana, commonly known as the arrayan mason bee, is a solitary bee species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a small to medium-sized bee, typically measuring between 8 to 12 millimeters in length. This species is known for its unique nesting behavior, constructing its nests in hollow stems or cavities. Osmia arrayana plays an important role in pollination, particularly for fruit and nut trees.

Its life cycle includes a winter hibernation stage, with adults emerging in the spring to forage and reproduce.
