Olearia heterolepis

Olearia heterolepis, commonly known as the silver tussock, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and fluffy, white or pale yellow flower heads. The plant typically grows in coastal and subalpine regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Olearia heterolepis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
