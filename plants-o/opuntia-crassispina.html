Opuntia crassispina

Opuntia crassispina, commonly known as the thick-spined prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its flattened, ribbed stems that are covered in dense, sharp spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the summer months, which are followed by edible, red-fleshed fruits. Opuntia crassispina is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick stems and having a shallow root system.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
