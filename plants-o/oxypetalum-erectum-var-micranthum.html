Oxypetalum erectum var. micranthum

Oxypetalum erectum var. micranthum, commonly known as the narrow-flowered oxypetalum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The leaves are simple, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
