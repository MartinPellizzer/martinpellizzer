Oncidium × pauwelsii

Oncidium × pauwelsii, commonly known as the Pauwels orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful flowers that often display shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its inflorescence consists of a raceme of showy, fragrant blooms that attract pollinators.

Oncidium × pauwelsii requires bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive in cultivation.
