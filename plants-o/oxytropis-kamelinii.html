Oxytropis kamelinii

Oxytropis kamelinii, commonly known as Kamelin's locoweed, is a perennial shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, spiny stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers in late spring to early summer, which are typically arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including high temperatures and low precipitation.

Oxytropis kamelinii is also known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to grazing animals.
