Oxytropis arbaevae

Oxytropis arbaevae, commonly known as the Arbaev's oxytropis, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or pink. The plant has a low-growing habit, with hairy stems and compound leaves that help it conserve water in its harsh environment.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for local wildlife in its native habitat.
