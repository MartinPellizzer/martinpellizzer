Oxydectes asperrima

Oxydectes asperrima, commonly known as the roughleaf dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its leaves are often arranged in a basal rosette, and the stem is usually unbranched.

The species is known for its adaptability to dry and disturbed environments.
