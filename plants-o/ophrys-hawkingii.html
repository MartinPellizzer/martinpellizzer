Ophrys hawkingii

Ophrys hawkingii, commonly known as the Hawking's bee orchid, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant produces a single spike of flowers, each with a specialized labellum that attracts male bees through mimicry. It thrives in calcareous soils and is typically found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Due to its specialized ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
