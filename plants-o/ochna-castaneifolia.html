Ochna castaneifolia

Ochna castaneifolia, commonly known as the African coffee tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often palmately lobed and resemble the leaves of chestnut trees. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by red, berry-like fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Ochna castaneifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions.
