Oenothera clypeata

Oenothera clypeata, commonly known as the cupplant, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive cup-shaped flower heads that are often filled with a yellow or orange-colored liquid. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy coating.

Oenothera clypeata is also known for its showy, evening-blooming flowers that attract nocturnal pollinators like moths.
