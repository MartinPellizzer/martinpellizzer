Orobus tenuifolius

Orobus tenuifolius, commonly known as the slender-leaved orchid, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers, which are usually pink or purple, bloom in spring and are often clustered in a loose inflorescence.

Orobus tenuifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
