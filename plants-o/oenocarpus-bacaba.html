Oenocarpus bacaba

Oenocarpus bacaba, commonly known as the bacaba palm, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces clustered, yellowish flowers followed by large, woody fruits containing a single, starchy seed. The seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of starch.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
