Orthophytum catingae

Orthophytum catingae, commonly known as the Catinga grass or Catinga plant, is a species of flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of trichomes. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing succulent leaves that store water. It produces small, clustered flowers that are often yellow or orange in color.

Orthophytum catingae plays a role in the ecological dynamics of the Catinga biome, contributing to local biodiversity.
