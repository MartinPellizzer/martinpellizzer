Onosma coerulea

Onosma coerulea, commonly known as the blue bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Onosma coerulea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
