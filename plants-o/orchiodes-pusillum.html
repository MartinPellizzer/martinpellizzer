Orchiodes pusillum

Orchiodes pusillum, commonly known as the dwarf orchid, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features a slender stem with a few narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, usually white or pale yellow, and are clustered in a loose inflorescence.

Orchiodes pusillum is known for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
