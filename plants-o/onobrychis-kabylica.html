Onobrychis kabylica

Onobrychis kabylica, commonly known as the Kabylic mountain clover, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous areas. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Onobrychis kabylica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local herbal medicine.
