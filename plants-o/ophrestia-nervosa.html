Ophrestia nervosa

Ophrestia nervosa, commonly known as the slender waxyplant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, waxy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, sandy soils.

Ophrestia nervosa is typically found in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps.
