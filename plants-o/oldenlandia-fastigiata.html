Oldenlandia fastigiata

Oldenlandia fastigiata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit and clusters of small, showy white or pinkish flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in a fine, velvety texture, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The plant is also notable for its ability to spread rapidly through both seeds and rhizomes.
