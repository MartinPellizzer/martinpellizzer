Oxalis simplex

Oxalis simplex, commonly known as the wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive veins and small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pink. The plant belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils. It is often grown as a ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.

Oxalis simplex is also known for its edible leaves, though they should be consumed in moderation.
