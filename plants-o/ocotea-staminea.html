Ocotea staminea

Ocotea staminea, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree or *Pimenta do Brasil*, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces dark blue to black berries that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its leaves are often used in perfumery due to their strong, spicy scent.

Ocotea staminea thrives in humid, tropical climates and is commonly found in rainforests and coastal areas.
