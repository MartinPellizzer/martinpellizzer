Ophrys × biagiolii

Ophrys × biagiolii, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant that belongs to the Orchidaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Ophrys apifera, the bee orchid, and Ophrys insectifera, the wasp orchid. This species is characterized by its intricate floral morphology, which closely resembles female wasps or bees, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is found in Mediterranean regions.

Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
