Oldenlandia angustifolia var. pedicellata

Oldenlandia angustifolia var. pedicellata, commonly known as the slenderleaf spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, due to their nectar-rich structure.

The species is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
