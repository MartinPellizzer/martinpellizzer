Ophrys andracnitis

Ophrys andracnitis, commonly known as the Greek bee orchid, is a species of orchid native to southern Europe, particularly Greece and Turkey.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant produces showy, bee-like flowers that are typically yellow to brown in color, with intricate patterns that aid in attracting male bees. Ophrys andracnitis is a perennial herb with a rhizomatous root system and grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical significance lies in its specialized pollination mechanism and its role in the ecological diversity of its native regions.
