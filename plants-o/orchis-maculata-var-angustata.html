Orchis maculata var. angustata

Orchis maculata var. angustata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved purple orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, dark green leaves and its striking purple or violet flowers with a distinctive yellowish-green lip. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodlands and meadows. It blooms from late spring to early summer, producing showy, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and flies.

This variety is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments, thriving in areas with moderate to high humidity.
