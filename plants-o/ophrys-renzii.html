Ophrys renzii

Ophrys renzii, commonly known as the Balkan bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant produces a single, showy flower with a dark, bee-like labellum that attracts male bees through visual and chemical cues. Its habitat is typically in moist, shaded woodland areas, where it thrives in nutrient-rich soils.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, Ophrys renzii is considered a vulnerable species.
