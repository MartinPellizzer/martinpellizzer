Ornithogalum maharashtrense

Ornithogalum maharashtrense, commonly known as the Maharashtra star-of-Bethlehem, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of white, star-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. It is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, and its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens.

The flowering period usually occurs in spring, adding a delicate beauty to the landscape.
