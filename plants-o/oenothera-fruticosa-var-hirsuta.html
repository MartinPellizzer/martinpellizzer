Oenothera fruticosa var. hirsuta

Oenothera fruticosa var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hirsute evening primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, which are often covered in fine, glandular hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers that open in the evening, hence its common name, and blooms throughout the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields, prairies, and disturbed sites.

This species is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
