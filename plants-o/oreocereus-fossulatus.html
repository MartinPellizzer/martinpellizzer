Oreocereus fossulatus

Oreocereus fossulatus, commonly known as the fissured moon cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family and is native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its unique appearance, featuring a swollen, segmented stem with deep fissures that give it a distinctive look. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils in arid environments. It produces small, white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators.

Due to its striking appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
