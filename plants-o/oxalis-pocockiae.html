Oxalis pocockiae

Oxalis pocockiae, commonly known as the Natal oxalis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features delicate, trifoliate leaves with a distinctive reddish or pinkish hue, and small, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

Oxalis pocockiae is also valued for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and its resilience to drought conditions.
