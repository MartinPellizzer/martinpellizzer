Orchis punctulata subsp. adenocheila

Orchis punctulata subsp.

adenocheila, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive pink or purple flowers with a yellowish-green lip.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption.

This subspecies is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
