Opuntia × rooneyi

Opuntia × rooneyi, commonly known as the Rooney prickly pear, is a hybrid cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems that are typically green to blue-green in color. The plant produces showy yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tunaf, is edible and contains small, sweet seeds.

Opuntia × rooneyi is well adapted to arid environments and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
