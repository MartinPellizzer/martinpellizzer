Ocotea minarum var. canescens

Ocotea minarum var. canescens, commonly known as the white-leafed ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped, white-velvet coated leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically white or pale yellow, and it produces dark, oily berries.

The tree is valued for its aromatic wood and is often found in humid, lowland forests.
