Oxytropis paysoniana

Oxytropis paysoniana, commonly known as Payson milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as sagebrush steppe and grasslands. The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Oxytropis paysoniana is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native range.
