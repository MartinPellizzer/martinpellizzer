Oxycoccus quadripetalus f. elongato-ovoidaeus

Oxycoccus quadripetalus f. elongato-ovoidaeus, commonly known as the elongated ovate cranberry, is a botanical variant of the cranberry plant.

It is characterized by its elongated, ovate berries that are typically smaller than the standard cranberry. The plant thrives in acidic, wet soils and is often found in bogs and swampy areas. Its flowers are small and white, with four petals, and it produces fruit that is used in various culinary and medicinal applications.

This subspecies is valued for its unique fruit morphology and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
