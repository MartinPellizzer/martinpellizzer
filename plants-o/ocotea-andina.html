Ocotea andina

Ocotea andina, commonly known as the Andean ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the cloud forests of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments at elevations between 1,000 and 2,500 meters. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils, which have been used in traditional medicine.

The species is also valued for its wood, which is durable and used in local crafts and construction.
