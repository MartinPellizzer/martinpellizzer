Ochna angolensis

Ochna angolensis, commonly known as the African firethorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, red-orange flowers. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. Ochna angolensis is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens in arid regions.
