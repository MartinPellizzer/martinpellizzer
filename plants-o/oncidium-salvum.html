Oncidium salvum

Oncidium salvum, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns and a distinctive greenish throat. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in conditions with seasonal dry periods.

Oncidium salvum is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
