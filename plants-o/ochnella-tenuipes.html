Ochnella tenuipes

Ochnella tenuipes, commonly known as the slender-stemmed ochna, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by distinctive berries that turn red when mature.

Ochnella tenuipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and seasonal floral display.
