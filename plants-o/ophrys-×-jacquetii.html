Ophrys × jacquetii

Ophrys × jacquetii, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant that results from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is characterized by its intricate, bee-like flowers that mimic the appearance and scent of female bees, a trait evolved to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows across Europe. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, and it produces a tall flowering stem with multiple flower spikes.

This hybrid species is prized for its unique floral morphology and ecological significance in supporting pollinator behavior.
