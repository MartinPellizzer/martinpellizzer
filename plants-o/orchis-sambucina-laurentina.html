Orchis sambucina laurentina

Orchis sambucina laurentina, commonly known as the Laurentian marsh orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wet meadows and marshy habitats in Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stem that can reach up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces a single, showy spike of violet or purple flowers with a distinctive hooded lip. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette at the base.

This species is adapted to moist, nutrient-rich soils and plays a role in supporting local insect populations.
