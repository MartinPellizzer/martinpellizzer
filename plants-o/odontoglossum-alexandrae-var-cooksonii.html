Odontoglossum alexandrae var. cooksonii

Odontoglossum alexandrae var. cooksonii, commonly known as the Cookson's orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It features delicate, fragrant flowers with a unique coloration pattern, typically in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are elongated and fleshy, adapted to the cool, moist environments it inhabits.

This variety is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts due to its beauty and rarity.
