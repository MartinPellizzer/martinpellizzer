Ocotea poeppigiana

Ocotea poeppigiana, commonly known as the Cuban laurel or palo de tinta, is a member of the Lauraceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of the Americas, particularly Cuba, the Bahamas, and parts of Central America. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, aromatic foliage. It produces small, white flowers followed by dark blue berries, which are an important food source for various wildlife.

Ocotea poeppigiana is also valued for its wood, which is used in construction and furniture due to its durability and aesthetic appeal.
