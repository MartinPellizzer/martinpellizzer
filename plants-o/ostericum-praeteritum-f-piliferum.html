Ostericum praeteritum f. piliferum

Ostericum praeteritum f. piliferum, commonly known as the hairy woodrush, is a botanical variant of the woodrush genus Ostericum.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and the presence of hairy foliage, which distinguishes it from other members of the species. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are linear and often bear short, stiff hairs, contributing to its unique appearance.

This fern-like plant is part of the family Ericaceae and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
