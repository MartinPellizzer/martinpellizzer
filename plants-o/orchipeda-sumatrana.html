Orchipeda sumatrana

Orchipeda sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran orchid, is a rare and endangered orchid species native to the rainforests of Sumatra.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and purple. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in the humid, shaded understory of its natural habitat. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by specific insects, and it plays a crucial role in the ecological balance of its ecosystem.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, conservation efforts are essential to ensure its survival.
