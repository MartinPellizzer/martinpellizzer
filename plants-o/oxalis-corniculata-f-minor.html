Oxalis corniculata f. minor

Oxalis corniculata f. minor, commonly known as the small-leaf wood sorrel, is a flowering plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is native to regions of Asia, including China and Japan. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and woodland edges. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a distinctive reddish or purple coloration on the underside.

Oxalis corniculata f. minor is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in partial shade.
