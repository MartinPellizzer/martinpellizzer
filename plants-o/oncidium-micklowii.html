Oncidium micklowii

Oncidium micklowii, commonly known as the Micklow's orchid, is a tropical orchid species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous inflorescences that bear clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Oncidium micklowii is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.
