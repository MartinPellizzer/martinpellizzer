Ohigginsia aggregata

Ohigginsia aggregata, commonly known as the clustered ohigginsia, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features small, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in clusters along the stems. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Ohigginsia aggregata thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
