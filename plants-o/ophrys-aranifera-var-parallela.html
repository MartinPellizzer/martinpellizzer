Ophrys aranifera var. parallela

Ophrys aranifera var. parallela, commonly known as the parallel orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to specific regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its unique floral morphology, featuring a complex, bee-mimicking labellum that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in calcareous grasslands and open woodlands, where it thrives in well-drained, nutrient-poor soils. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with intricate patterns that aid in attracting specific pollinators.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, this variant is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
