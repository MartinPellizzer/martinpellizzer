Oxytropis inopinata

Oxytropis inopinata, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often in desert environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several narrow, thread-like leaflets.

Oxytropis inopinata is adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions with deep root systems and a low, sprawling growth habit.
