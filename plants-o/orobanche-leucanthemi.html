Orobanche leucanthemi

Orobanche leucanthemi, commonly known as the white broomweed, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies on host plants for nutrients.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers and has a highly reduced structure with no true leaves. It parasitizes a variety of host plants, including those in the Asteraceae and Fabaceae families.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can cause significant damage to agricultural crops.
