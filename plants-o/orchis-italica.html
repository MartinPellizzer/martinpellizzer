Orchis italica

Orchis italica, commonly known as the Italian orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and thrives in woodland or meadow habitats. Its flowers are often arranged in a raceme and are pollinated by insects, particularly bees.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
