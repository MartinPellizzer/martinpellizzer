Oenothera boothii

Oenothera boothii, commonly known as the desert evening primrose, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Oenothera boothii is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient plant in desert ecosystems.
