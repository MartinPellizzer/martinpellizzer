Oenothera biloba

Oenothera biloba, commonly known as the evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening. The plant has hairy, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its flowers are typically borne in clusters at the tips of the stems.

Oenothera biloba is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and is often found in dry, open habitats.
