Ophrys × abdita

Ophrys × abdita, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive floral morphology, which mimics the appearance of female wasps to attract male wasps for pollination. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodlands and meadows, across parts of Europe. Its flowers exhibit a complex structure with a labellum that plays a crucial role in the mimicry strategy.

Ophrys × abdita is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in supporting pollinator interactions.
