Ocotea caesia

Ocotea caesia, commonly known as the red sandalwood or *Ocotea caesia*, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark-colored wood, which is highly valued for its durability and scent. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, and it produces elliptical, dark blue-black berries.

The tree is often found in humid, lowland forests and is known for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
