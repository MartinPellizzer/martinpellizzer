Oxalis megapotamica

Oxalis megapotamica, commonly known as the Brazilian wood sorrel, is a species of flowering plant in the family Oxalidaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant has trifoliate leaves with three leaflets, and it produces small, bead-like fruits that contain several seeds. Oxalis megapotamica is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and delicate blossoms.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes in temperate climates.
