Origanum bracteosum

Origanum bracteosum, commonly known as the bracted oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage is often used in culinary applications, similar to other members of the Lamiaceae family.

Origanum bracteosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
