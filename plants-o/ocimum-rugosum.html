Ocimum rugosum

Ocimum rugosum, commonly known as rough basil, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a rough, textured surface, and it produces small, pale blue or purple flowers. Ocimum rugosum is often cultivated for its fragrant leaves used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Its growth habit is typically bushy, with multiple stems arising from the base.
