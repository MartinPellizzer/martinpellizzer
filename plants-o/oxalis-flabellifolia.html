Oxalis flabellifolia

Oxalis flabellifolia, commonly known as the fanleaf wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive fan-like arrangement, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, delicate yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Oxalis flabellifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to spread through runners.
