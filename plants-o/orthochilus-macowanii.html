Orthochilus macowanii

Orthochilus macowanii, commonly known as the Macowan's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in grassland and rocky habitats, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with rhizomes and multiple pseudobulbs that support its flowering structure. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from yellow to orange, often with intricate patterns.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is appreciated by horticulturists for its unique appearance.
