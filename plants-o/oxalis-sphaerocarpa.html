Oxalis sphaerocarpa

Oxalis sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the globe wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, globe-shaped seed pods and heart-shaped leaves with distinctive veins. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Oxalis sphaerocarpa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ease of care.
