Oenothera torulosa f. mixta

Oenothera torulosa f. mixta, commonly known as the mixed-leaf evening primrose, is a variant of the evening primrose species native to North America.

This plant is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The foliage of Oenothera torulosa f. mixta is notable for its varied leaf shapes, which range from lanceolate to ovate, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping.

The plant is also valued for its potential in herbal medicine due to the presence of essential fatty acids in its seeds.
