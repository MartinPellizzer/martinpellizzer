Oxalis tristis

Oxalis tristis, commonly known as the weeping woodsorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers. Its stems are often reddish and may hang downward, giving it a weeping appearance.

Oxalis tristis is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and blooms.
