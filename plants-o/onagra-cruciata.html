Onagra cruciata

Onagra cruciata, commonly known as the common evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Onagraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, with hairy surfaces.

The seeds are enclosed in a capsule and are a food source for various wildlife species.
