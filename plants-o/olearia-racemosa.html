Olearia racemosa

Olearia racemosa, commonly known as the clustered flax or silverweed, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring silver-gray leaves covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in coastal and subalpine environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Olearia racemosa is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and flowers.
