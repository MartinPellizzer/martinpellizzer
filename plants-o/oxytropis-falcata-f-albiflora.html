Oxytropis falcata f. albiflora

Oxytropis falcata f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered scorpionvetch, is a variant of the scorpionvetch species native to the western United States.

It is a perennial legume characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are typically clustered in racemes, and the flowers are notable for their white coloration in this particular form. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for ecological studies and potential medicinal research.
