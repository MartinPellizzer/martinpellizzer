Oreochloa blanka

Oreochloa blanka, commonly known as the blank's grass, is a species of grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along stream banks. Oreochloa blanka is a perennial species that produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers.
