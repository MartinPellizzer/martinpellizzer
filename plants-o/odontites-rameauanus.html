Odontites rameauanus

Odontites rameauanus, commonly known as the Rameau's toothplant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of linear, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Odontites rameauanus is noted for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies.
