Oxalis psilotricha

Oxalis psilotricha, commonly known as the slender wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are often covered in fine, hair-like structures, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Oxalis psilotricha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
