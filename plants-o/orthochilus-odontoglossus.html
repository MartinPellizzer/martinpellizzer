Orthochilus odontoglossus

Orthochilus odontoglossus, commonly known as the toothed lip orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, hairy leaves and a distinctive flower with a lip that has a toothed edge, giving it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in cloud forests or high-altitude grasslands. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellow, with a contrasting purple or brown lip.

Orthochilus odontoglossus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated by enthusiasts of rare orchids.
