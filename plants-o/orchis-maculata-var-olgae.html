Orchis maculata var. olgae

Orchis maculata var. olgae, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces tall, erect stems bearing clusters of showy, purple to pink flowers. Its flowers are characterized by their distinctive greenish-brown markings and spurred petals.

The species is known for its ecological importance, supporting various pollinators and serving as an indicator of undisturbed habitats.
