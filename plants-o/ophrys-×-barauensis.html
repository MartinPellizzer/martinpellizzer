Ophrys × barauensis

Ophrys × barauensis, commonly known as the Barau's orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the genus Ophrys, which is characterized by its intricate, bee-mimicking flowers that play a key role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its flowers are highly specialized, featuring a complex structure that mimics female bees to attract male bees for pollination.

Ophrys × barauensis is a rare and endangered species, with its population declining due to habitat loss and over-collection.
