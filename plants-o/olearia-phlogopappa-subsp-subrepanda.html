Olearia phlogopappa subsp. subrepanda

Olearia phlogopappa subsp.

subrepanda, commonly known as the silver tussock or coastal daisy, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and showy yellow daisy-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to salty, windy conditions. It has a mat-forming growth habit with dense, woolly leaves that help retain moisture.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils.
