Ornithidium huancabambae

Ornithidium huancabambae, commonly known as the Huancabamba orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to the high-altitude regions of Peru.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure and striking coloration. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous terrain, where it thrives in cool and humid conditions. Its flowers are small but intricate, often displaying a combination of white, purple, and green hues.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts in its native environment.
