Oenothera mollissima var. paraguayensis

Oenothera mollissima var. paraguayensis, commonly known as Paraguayan evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and showy yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by moths and other nocturnal insects.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
