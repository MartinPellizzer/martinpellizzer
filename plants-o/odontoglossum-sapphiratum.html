Odontoglossum sapphiratum

Odontoglossum sapphiratum, commonly known as the Sapphire Orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns and a distinctive spurred lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually elongated and somewhat fleshy, adapted to store moisture in the dry mountain environments where it thrives.

Odontoglossum sapphiratum is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and rarity in cultivation.
