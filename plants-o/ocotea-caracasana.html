Ocotea caracasana

Ocotea caracasana, commonly known as the Caracas sandwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a small, woody drupe that contains a single seed.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in moist, well-drained soils in tropical and subtropical regions.
