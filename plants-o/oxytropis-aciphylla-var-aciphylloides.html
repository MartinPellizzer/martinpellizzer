Oxytropis aciphylla var. aciphylloides

Oxytropis aciphylla var. aciphylloides, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the arid regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, palmately divided leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

This variety is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert scrub habitats.
