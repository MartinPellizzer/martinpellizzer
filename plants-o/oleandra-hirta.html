Oleandra hirta

Oleandra hirta, commonly known as the hairy oleander, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its simple, opposite, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically blooming in summer. Oleandra hirta is known for its showy appearance and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

However, it is important to note that all parts of the plant are toxic and should be handled with care.
