Oxalis obliquifolia

Oxalis obliquifolia, commonly known as the zigzag wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, trifoliate leaves that have a zigzag pattern along the edges. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

Oxalis obliquifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
