Oncidium odontopterygium

Oncidium odontopterygium, commonly known as the toothed orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, long-lasting flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive red or purple lip. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in seasonal environments. Its leaves are strap-like and arranged in a fan shape, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Oncidium odontopterygium is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms and ease of care in suitable growing conditions.
