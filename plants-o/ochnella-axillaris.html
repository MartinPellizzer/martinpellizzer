Ochnella axillaris

Ochnella axillaris, commonly known as the narrowleaf ochna, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical or lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences, followed by distinctive, winged fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in montane forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
