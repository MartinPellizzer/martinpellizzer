Oxalis procumbens subsp. bathieana

Oxalis procumbens subsp.

bathieana, commonly known as the creeping wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to South Africa. It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive white veins and produces clusters of small, yellow, five-petaled flowers.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers. Its succulent stems allow it to store water, enabling it to survive in dry conditions. Oxalis procumbens subsp.

bathieana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to add a delicate touch of color to gardens.
