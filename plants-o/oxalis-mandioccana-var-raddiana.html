Oxalis mandioccana var. raddiana

Oxalis mandioccana var. raddiana, commonly known as the Brazilian wood sorrel, is a species native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky areas. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The plant's botanical features make it an interesting subject for horticultural and ecological studies.
