Orchis coriophora f. alba

Orchis coriophora f. alba, commonly known as the white marsh orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to parts of Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers. This subspecies is distinguished by its predominantly white floral coloration, which sets it apart from the typical greenish-yellow flowers of the main species. It thrives in wet, calcareous habitats such as marshes and stream banks.
