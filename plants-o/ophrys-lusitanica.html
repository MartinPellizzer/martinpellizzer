Ophrys lusitanica

Ophrys lusitanica, commonly known as the Portuguese bee orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking, bee-like flowers that feature a prominent lip resembling a bee's face. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its flowers are specialized for pollination by bees, with the lip acting as a visual lure.

Ophrys lusitanica is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is noted for its unique morphology and ecological significance.
