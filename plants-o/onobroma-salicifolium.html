Onobroma salicifolium

Onobroma salicifolium, commonly known as the willow-leaf trumpet flower, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its willow-like leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, hairy seeds.

Onobroma salicifolium is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
