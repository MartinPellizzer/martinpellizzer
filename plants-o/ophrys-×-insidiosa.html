Ophrys × insidiosa

Ophrys × insidiosa, commonly known as the mimic orchid, is a hybrid species within the genus Ophrys, which is part of the orchid family Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its intricate floral structure, which closely resembles the female wasp, a trait evolved to facilitate pollination through sexual deception. The plant typically grows in temperate regions, particularly in southern Europe, and thrives in moist, calcareous soils. Its flowers are usually pale brown to yellow, with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of wasps.

This species is notable for its specialized pollination strategy, making it a fascinating subject in the study of plant-animal interactions.
