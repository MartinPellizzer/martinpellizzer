Onopordum myriacanthum subsp. myriacanthum

Onopordum myriacanthum subsp.

myriacanthum, commonly known as the many-toothed thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces a large number of small, barbed seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

This species is often found in fynbos vegetation and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
