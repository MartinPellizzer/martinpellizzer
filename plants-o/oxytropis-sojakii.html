Oxytropis sojakii

Oxytropis sojakii, commonly known as the desert locoweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of small, pea-like flowers. The plant has adapted to harsh environments with its deep root system and ability to fix nitrogen, enhancing soil fertility. Its leaves are typically small and hairy, reducing water loss through transpiration.

Oxytropis sojakii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
