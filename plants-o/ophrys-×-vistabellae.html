Ophrys × vistabellae

Ophrys × vistabellae, commonly known as the Vistabellae bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Balkans.

It is characterized by its distinctive floral structure, featuring a labellum that mimics the appearance of a female bee, a feature that plays a key role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and thrives in shaded woodland environments. Its flowers are usually a mix of yellow and brown, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Ophrys × vistabellae is a rare and endangered species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts in preserving its natural habitat.
