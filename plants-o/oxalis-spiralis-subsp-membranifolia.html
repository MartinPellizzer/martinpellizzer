Oxalis spiralis subsp. membranifolia

Oxalis spiralis subsp.

membranifolia, commonly known as the silky wood sorrel, is a species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its delicate, spiraling leaves that give it a unique and intricate appearance.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a subtle ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland settings.

Its foliage is often described as soft and velvety, contributing to its popularity among horticulturists.
