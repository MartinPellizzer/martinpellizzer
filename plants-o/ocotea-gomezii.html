Ocotea gomezii

Ocotea gomezii, commonly known as the Mexican laurel, is a member of the Lauraceae family and is native to Central America and northern South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in humid, tropical forests. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, aromatic bark. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces oval, woody fruits that contain one or two seeds.

Ocotea gomezii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
