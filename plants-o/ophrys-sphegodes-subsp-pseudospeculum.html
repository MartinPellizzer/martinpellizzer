Ophrys sphegodes subsp. pseudospeculum

Ophrys sphegodes subsp.

pseudospeculum, commonly known as the bee orchid, is a species of orchid native to Europe. It is characterized by its striking floral morphology, with a lip that mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination.

The plant typically grows in calcareous grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer.

This orchid is notable for its specialized pollination mechanism and its role in supporting local insect populations.
