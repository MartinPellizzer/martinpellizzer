Olyra podachne

Olyra podachne, commonly known as the narrow-leafed grass.

It is a perennial grass species native to Africa, characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Poaceae family. Its seeds are enclosed in small, cylindrical pods, which contribute to its common name.

Olyra podachne is often found in dry, open habitats and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils.
