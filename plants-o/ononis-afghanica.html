Ononis afghanica

Ononis afghanica, commonly known as Afghan sandspurry, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia, including Afghanistan and Pakistan.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along mountain slopes. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in the spring. Its leaves are succulent and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in dry conditions.

Ononis afghanica is part of the Caryophyllaceae family, known for its characteristic flowers and often used in traditional herbal medicine.
