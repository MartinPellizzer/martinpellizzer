Ocotea imazensis

Ocotea imazensis, commonly known as the Yucatán ocotea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Yucatán Peninsula in Mexico.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurel trees and other aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark-brown bark and lance-shaped, glossy green leaves. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue berries that are often used in traditional medicine.

Ocotea imazensis is valued for its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
