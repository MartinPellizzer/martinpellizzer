Oenothera deflexa var. bracteata

Oenothera deflexa var. bracteata, commonly known as the bracteate evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial with slender, branching stems that often grow along the ground. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, and it thrives in sandy or well-drained soils.

The species is noted for its ecological value and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental appeal.
