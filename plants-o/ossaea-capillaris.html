Ossaea capillaris

Ossaea capillaris, commonly known as the slender mossy grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of tiny, thread-like structures called trichomes. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its inflorescence consists of a single, slender spike that bears small, spikelet-like flowers.

Ossaea capillaris plays a minor role in local ecosystems but is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphological features.
