Ornithosperma serotina

Ornithosperma serotina, commonly known as the late-flowering orchid, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to certain regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its elegant, pendulous flowers that typically bloom in the late season, hence its common name. Its inflorescence consists of multiple racemes, each bearing small, greenish-white to pale yellow flowers.

Ornithosperma serotina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens for its unique floral display.
