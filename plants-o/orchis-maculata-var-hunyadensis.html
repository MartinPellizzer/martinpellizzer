Orchis maculata var. hunyadensis

Orchis maculata var. hunyadensis, commonly known as the Hungarian spotted orchid, is a rare and protected orchid species native to Hungary and parts of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive spotted leaves and showy, pale pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodlands and meadows. It is a perennial herb with a short, stout rhizome and is known for its specialized pollination mechanisms.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered in many regions.
