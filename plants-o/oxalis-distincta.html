Oxalis distincta

Oxalis distincta, commonly known as the redwood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with three heart-shaped leaflets, often tinged with red or purple veins. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental species in native plant gardens.
