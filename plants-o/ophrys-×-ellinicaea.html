Ophrys × ellinicaea

Ophrys × ellinicaea, commonly known as the Greek bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Greece.

It belongs to the genus Ophrys, which is known for its mimicry of female insects to attract male pollinators. The plant produces showy, bee-like flowers with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by bees. It typically grows in calcareous soils and thrives in sunny, open habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in its native range.
