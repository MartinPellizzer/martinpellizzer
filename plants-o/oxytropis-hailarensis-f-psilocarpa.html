Oxytropis hailarensis f. psilocarpa

Oxytropis hailarensis f. psilocarpa, commonly known as the Psilocarpic Locoweed, is a variant of the Oxytropis genus native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect, spindly stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers in early summer, which are typically clustered in racemes. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh, semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is notable for its unique chemical composition, which includes certain alkaloids that have attracted scientific interest.
