Origanum capitellatum

Origanum capitellatum, commonly known as narrowleaf oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, compact leaves that are aromatic and often used in culinary applications. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale blue to purple flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for both gardeners and herbalists.
