Oxalis crassipes

Oxalis crassipes, commonly known as the broadleaf wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish or purple tinge on the underside, and it produces small, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas. It is known for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.

Oxalis crassipes is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
