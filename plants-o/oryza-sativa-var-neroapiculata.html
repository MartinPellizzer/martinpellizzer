Oryza sativa var. neroapiculata

Oryza sativa var. neroapiculata, commonly known as the black-awned rice, is a variant of rice characterized by its unique dark-colored awns.

This variety is native to Southeast Asia and is typically cultivated for its grain, which is used in both culinary and traditional medicinal applications. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, oval-shaped grains that are often darker in color than those of common rice varieties. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring warm temperatures and ample water for optimal growth.

The botanical features of this rice variety make it distinct from other Oryza species, particularly in its morphological characteristics and genetic makeup.
