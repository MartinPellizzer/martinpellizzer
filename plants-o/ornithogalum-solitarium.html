Ornithogalum solitarium

Ornithogalum solitarium, commonly known as the star of Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a tall, unbranched stem topped with a cluster of white, star-shaped flowers. The plant is characterized by its fleshy, often bulbous underground storage structures known as bulbs. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ornithogalum solitarium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical significance in traditional medicine.
