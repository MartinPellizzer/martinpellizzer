Oxytropis sericea var. spicata

Oxytropis sericea var. spicata, commonly known as the silky locoweed, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats. It has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in arid conditions.

This species is also known for its toxic alkaloids, which can be harmful to grazing animals.
