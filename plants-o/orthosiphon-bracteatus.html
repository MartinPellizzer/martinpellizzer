Orthosiphon bracteatus

Orthosiphon bracteatus, commonly known as the bracteate holy basil, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or pink in color. It is often cultivated for its medicinal properties and ornamental value.

The leaves are frequently used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
