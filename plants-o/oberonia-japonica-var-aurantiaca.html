Oberonia japonica var. aurantiaca

Oberonia japonica var. aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a striking tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange-red flowers, which are clustered in loose inflorescences. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are dark green and elliptical, often with a glossy surface.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or terrariums.
