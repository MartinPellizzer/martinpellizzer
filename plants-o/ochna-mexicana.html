Ochna mexicana

Ochna mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican Ochna, is a deciduous shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by distinctive, woody, spherical fruits. Ochna mexicana is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in semi-arid conditions.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
