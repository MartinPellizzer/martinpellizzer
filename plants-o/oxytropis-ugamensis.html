Oxytropis ugamensis

Oxytropis ugamensis, commonly known as the Ugam milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Oxytropis ugamensis is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
