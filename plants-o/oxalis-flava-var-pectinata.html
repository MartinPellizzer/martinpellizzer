Oxalis flava var. pectinata

Oxalis flava var. pectinata, commonly known as the yellow wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are bright yellow with a waxy texture, and its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be found in rock gardens and woodland settings.

Oxalis flava var. pectinata is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
