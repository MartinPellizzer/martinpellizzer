Ophrys × pohornyi

Ophrys × pohornyi, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera, two species known for their mimicry of female wasps. The plant produces intricate flower structures that closely resemble female insects, a trait evolved to attract male pollinators. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, adapting to its habitat in open, sunny areas.

The flowers bloom in early spring, adding a striking visual element to its natural environment.
