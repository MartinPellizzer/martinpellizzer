Ocotea cissiflora

Ocotea cissiflora, commonly known as the sweet-scented ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocado, and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, dark purple drupe that contains a single seed.

Ocotea cissiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates for its aesthetic and aromatic qualities.
