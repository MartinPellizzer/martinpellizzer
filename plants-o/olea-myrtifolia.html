Olea myrtifolia

Olea myrtifolia, commonly known as the myrtleleaf olive, is a small evergreen tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible, wrinkled drupes. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Olea myrtifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long history of use in traditional medicine.
