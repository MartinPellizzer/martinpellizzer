Oldenlandia uniflora var. fasciculata

Oldenlandia uniflora var. fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that often form dense clusters, giving it its varietal name "fasciculata.". The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
