Osmia laevigata

Osmia laevigata, commonly known as the smooth carpenter bee, is a species of bee native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a solitary bee that plays an important role in pollination, particularly for fruit and vegetable crops. The female constructs its nest in hollow stems or wood, laying eggs and provisioning them with pollen and nectar. Its body is typically black with a metallic blue or green sheen, and it has a distinctive smooth, shiny appearance.

Osmia laevigata is known for its efficient foraging behavior and is often found visiting a variety of flowering plants.
