Oenothera grandiflora subsp. erythrocephala

Oenothera grandiflora subsp.

erythrocephala, commonly known as the red-headed evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening and close by morning.

The plant has hairy, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by nocturnal insects such as moths.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.
