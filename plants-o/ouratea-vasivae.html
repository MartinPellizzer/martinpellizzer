Ouratea vasivae

Ouratea vasivae, commonly known as the Brazilian beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 2 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, violet-blue berries that are edible for some bird species. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a smooth texture.

The flowers are white and fragrant, appearing in the axils of the leaves, contributing to its ornamental value.
