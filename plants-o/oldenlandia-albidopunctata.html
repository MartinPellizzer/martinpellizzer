Oldenlandia albidopunctata

Oldenlandia albidopunctata, commonly known as the white-punctured spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers with prominent green veins. The leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, three-lobed fruits that contain minute seeds.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.
