Oxalis minima

Oxalis minima, commonly known as the miniature wood sorrel, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and produces delicate, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow or white. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens. Its growth habit is typically low and spreading, with stems that often root at the nodes.

Oxalis minima is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to add a touch of delicate beauty to shaded garden spaces.
