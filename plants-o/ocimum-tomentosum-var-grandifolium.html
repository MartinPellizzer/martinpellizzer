Ocimum tomentosum var. grandifolium

Ocimum tomentosum var. grandifolium, commonly known as wild basil, is a variant of the sacred basil plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The leaves are typically larger and more robust compared to the standard Ocimum tomentosum. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

This plant is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb due to its aromatic properties.
