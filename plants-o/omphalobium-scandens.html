Omphalobium scandens

Omphalobium scandens, commonly known as the climbing everlasting pea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by small, pod-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

Omphalobium scandens is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
