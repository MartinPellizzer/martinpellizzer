Odontoglossum luteopurpureum var. mulus

Odontoglossum luteopurpureum var. mulus, commonly known as the Purple-tinged Orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb that supports its growth during periods of dormancy. The plant produces showy, violet to purple flowers with yellow markings, which attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a fan-like pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is valued for its vibrant coloration and is often cultivated in greenhouses for its aesthetic and horticultural significance.
