Ocotea jelskii

Ocotea jelskii, commonly known as the Jelskii's ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed enclosed in a fleshy, orange-colored pulp.

Ocotea jelskii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates for its aesthetic appeal.
