Opulaster amurensis

Opulaster amurensis, commonly known as the Amur opulaster, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

Opulaster amurensis is often used in ornamental gardening due to its hardy nature and attractive appearance.
