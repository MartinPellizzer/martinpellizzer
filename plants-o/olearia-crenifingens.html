Olearia crenifingens

Olearia crenifingens, commonly known as the silverweed tree or silverleaf tree, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant has small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

Olearia crenifingens is often found in coastal and forested areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
