Ononis tridentata var. dentata

Ononis tridentata var. dentata, commonly known as the three-toothed cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. It has a creeping growth habit with woody stems and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in native plant landscaping.
