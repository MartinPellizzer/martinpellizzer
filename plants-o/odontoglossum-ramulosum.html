Odontoglossum ramulosum

Odontoglossum ramulosum, commonly known as the ramosa orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in humid, shaded environments at high altitudes. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals.

Odontoglossum ramulosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
