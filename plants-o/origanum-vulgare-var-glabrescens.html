Origanum vulgare var. glabrescens

Origanum vulgare var. glabrescens, commonly known as Greek oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, tubular, purple or pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has aromatic, dark green, slightly hairy leaves that are used in cooking and traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry habitats.

Its essential oils contain compounds like carvacrol and thymol, which contribute to its strong scent and medicinal properties.
