Oncidium cepula

Oncidium cepula, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features a slender, upright stem with a few leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, yellow flowers. Its flowers have a distinctive shape with a spurred lip and are often used in floral arrangements.

Oncidium cepula is valued for its ornamental beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
