Oxalis mutabilis var. alba

Oxalis mutabilis var. alba, commonly known as the white wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive white or pale green coloration. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pink, blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

Oxalis mutabilis var. alba is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
