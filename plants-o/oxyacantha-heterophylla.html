Oxyacantha heterophylla

Oxyacantha heterophylla, commonly known as the variable rosebay, is a shrub native to parts of eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its variable leaf shapes, which can range from ovate to lanceolate, and its clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal heathlands and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. It produces small, red berries that are an important food source for local wildlife.

Oxyacantha heterophylla is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to different environments.
