Odontites maroccanus

Odontites maroccanus, commonly known as Moroccan toothplant, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Atlas Mountains in Morocco.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, adapted to the arid and rocky conditions of its habitat. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Odontites maroccanus is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native region.
