Oxalis sellowiana f. alba

Oxalis sellowiana f. alba, commonly known as the white wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its leaves are heart-shaped with prominent veins, and the flowers consist of five petals arranged in a star-like pattern.

Oxalis sellowiana f. alba is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
