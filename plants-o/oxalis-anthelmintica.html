Oxalis anthelmintica

Oxalis anthelmintica, commonly known as the medicinal wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its preference for partial shade. Oxalis anthelmintica contains compounds that have been traditionally used for their antiparasitic properties.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both ecological and medicinal research.
