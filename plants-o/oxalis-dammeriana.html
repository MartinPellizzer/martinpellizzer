Oxalis dammeriana

Oxalis dammeriana, commonly known as the false shamrock, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which resemble those of the true shamrock, and clusters of small, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories. It has a short stem with hairy leaves and produces red or orange berries that contain the seeds.

Oxalis dammeriana is a popular ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
