Ocotea mandioccana

Ocotea mandioccana, commonly known as Brazilian cinnamon or Brazilian camphor, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and fragrant bark. The plant produces small, white flowers and is known for its essential oils, which are used in traditional medicine and perfumery. Its ecological importance includes providing habitat for various species and contributing to forest biodiversity.

The tree is also valued for its timber, which is durable and has a pleasant scent.
