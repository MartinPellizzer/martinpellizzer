Olea paniculata

Olea paniculata, commonly known as the panicle olive, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in panicles, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape and have a leathery texture. It produces small, fleshy drupes that are typically yellow or orange when mature.

Olea panicultata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
