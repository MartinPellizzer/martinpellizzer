Orobanche picridiphyta

Orobanche picridiphyta, commonly known as the common broomrape, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is characterized by its lack of leaves and its distinctive yellow or reddish flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in association with legumes and other herbaceous plants. Its life cycle involves germinating in response to chemical signals released by host roots, after which it establishes a parasitic relationship.

Orobanche picridiphyta can be a problematic weed in agricultural settings due to its ability to reduce crop yields.
