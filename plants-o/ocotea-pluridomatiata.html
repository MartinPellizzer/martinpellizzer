Ocotea pluridomatiata

Ocotea pluridomatiata, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its aromatic wood is valued for its use in furniture and as a source of essential oils.

Ocotea pluridomatiata thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.
