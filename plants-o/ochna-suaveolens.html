Ochna suaveolens

Ochna suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-smelling ochna, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a velvety texture.

Ochna suaveolens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its pleasant scent.
