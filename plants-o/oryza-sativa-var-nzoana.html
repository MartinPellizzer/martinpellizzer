Oryza sativa var. nzoana

Oryza sativa var. nzoana, commonly known as the Nzoana rice, is a less commonly cultivated variety of rice native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its short, slender grains and typically grows in lowland areas with seasonal flooding. The plant has a robust root system that helps it thrive in waterlogged soils. It is well adapted to tropical climates and requires a warm, humid environment for optimal growth.

This variety is of interest for its potential in improving food security in regions with challenging agricultural conditions.
