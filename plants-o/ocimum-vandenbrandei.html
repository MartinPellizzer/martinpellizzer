Ocimum vandenbrandei

Ocimum vandenbrandei, commonly known as the African basil, is a perennial herb native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and lavender. The plant is characterized by its dark green, ovate leaves and small, pale purple flowers that bloom in clusters. It is often cultivated for its fragrant leaves used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Ocimum vandenbrandei thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
