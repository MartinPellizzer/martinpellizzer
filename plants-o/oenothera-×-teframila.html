Oenothera × teframila

Oenothera × teframila, commonly known as the hybrid evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a cross between Oenothera biennis and Oenothera glabriuscula, resulting in a species with unique botanical characteristics. The plant produces showy, yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening, hence its common name. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and its flowers are typically arranged in clusters at the ends of branches.

Oenothera × teframila is often found in disturbed soils and is adaptable to various environmental conditions.
