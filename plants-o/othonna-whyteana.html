Othonna whyteana

Othonna whyteana, commonly known as the Whyte's othonna, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant features narrow, silver-blue leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which bloom in spring and attract pollinators.

Othonna whyteana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
