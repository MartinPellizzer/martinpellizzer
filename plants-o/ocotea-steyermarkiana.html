Ocotea steyermarkiana

Ocotea steyermarkiana, commonly known as the Steyermark's ocotea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the humid lowland forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests. Its leaves are dark green and glossy, with a distinctive fragrance when crushed, and it produces small, dark berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ecological significance.
