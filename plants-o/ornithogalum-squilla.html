Ornithogalum squilla

Ornithogalum squilla, commonly known as the star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and northern Africa.

It features a bulbous stem with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers in late spring. The plant belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to form dense colonies. Its flowers are often visited by insects, making it an important plant for pollinators.

However, it can become invasive in some regions, posing a challenge in ecological management.
