Opuntia bonaerensis

Opuntia bonaerensis, commonly known as the Buenos Aires prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Argentina.

It is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems that are covered in spines and are often ribbed. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tuna, is edible and has a sweet, slightly tart flavor.

Opuntia bonaerensis is well adapted to arid environments and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of food in its native region.
