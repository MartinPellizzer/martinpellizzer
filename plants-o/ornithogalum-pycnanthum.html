Ornithogalum pycnanthum

Ornithogalum pycnanthum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a bulbous underground stem and linear, grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and grass-green in color. The plant produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
