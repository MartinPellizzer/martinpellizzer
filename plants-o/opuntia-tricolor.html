Opuntia tricolor

Opuntia tricolor, commonly known as the rainbow prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant, multicolored pads that range from green to red, yellow, and orange. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits called tunas. Opuntia tricolor is a succulent plant with thick, fleshy stems adapted to store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

Its structure and coloration make it a distinctive and visually striking member of the Cactaceae family.
