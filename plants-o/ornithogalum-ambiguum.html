Ornithogalum ambiguum

Ornithogalum ambiguum, commonly known as the ambiguous belladonna, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces clusters of white or pale green flowers that are bell-shaped and emit a mild fragrance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ornithogalum ambiguum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
