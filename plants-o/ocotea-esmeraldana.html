Ocotea esmeraldana

Ocotea esmeraldana, commonly known as Emeraldane, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the humid lowland forests of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage and aromatic leaves, which emit a distinctive scent when crushed. It typically grows as a medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 20 meters.

Ocotea esmeraldana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its fragrant properties.
