Osmanthus gracilinervis

Osmanthus gracilinervis, commonly known as the slender-veined osmanthus, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Vietnam and China.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are narrow and have delicate, fine veins, giving it its scientific name.

Osmanthus gracilinervis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
