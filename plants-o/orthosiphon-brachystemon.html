Orthosiphon brachystemon

Orthosiphon brachystemon, commonly known as Java tea or Malabar tea, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is often grown for its aromatic leaves, which are used in traditional medicine and as a tea substitute.

Orthosiphon brachystemon thrives in tropical climates and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
