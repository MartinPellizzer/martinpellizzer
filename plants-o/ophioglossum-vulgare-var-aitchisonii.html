Ophioglossum vulgare var. aitchisonii

Ophioglossum vulgare var. aitchisonii, commonly known as the Aitchison's adder's tongue, is a rare and ancient fern species native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tongue-shaped sterile leaf and a spore-bearing, club-like structure called a sporangium. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are usually long and narrow, with a unique, primitive morphology that reflects its evolutionary history.
