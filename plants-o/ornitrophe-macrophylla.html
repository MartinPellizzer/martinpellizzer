Ornitrophe macrophylla

Ornitrophe macrophylla, commonly known as the broad-leaf orchid, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that often display vibrant colors such as red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture from the air. Its leaves are broad and waxy, helping to retain water in humid environments.

The botanical structure of Ornitrophe macrophylla includes a pseudobulb that stores nutrients and water, supporting its growth in diverse ecological settings.
