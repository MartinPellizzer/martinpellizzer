Oncidium bennettii

Oncidium bennettii, commonly known as the Bennett's orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous flower spikes that bear numerous fragrant, yellowish-green to brownish flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the humid environment, and it thrives in bright, indirect light.

Oncidium bennettii is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.
