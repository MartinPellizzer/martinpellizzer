Ophrys × sulcitana

Ophrys × sulcitana, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant that results from the cross between Ophrys apifera (the bee orchid) and Ophrys insectifera (the wasp orchid).

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its unique floral morphology that closely resembles a female bee or wasp. The plant produces showy, bee-like flowers with intricate patterns and colors designed to mimic pollinators, a strategy known as sexual deception. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow with brown markings, and it thrives in calcareous soils in open habitats.

Ophrys × sulcitana is primarily pollinated by male bees, which attempt to mate with the flower's pseudocopulation structure, facilitating its reproduction.
