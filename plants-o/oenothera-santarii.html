Oenothera santarii

Oenothera santarii, commonly known as the San Antonio nightshade, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the evening primrose family (Onagraceae) and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.
