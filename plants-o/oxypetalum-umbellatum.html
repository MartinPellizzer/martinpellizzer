Oxypetalum umbellatum

Oxypetalum umbellatum, commonly known as the sweetshrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pink flowers that bloom in dense, flat-topped clusters called umbels. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 6 feet and has opposite, elliptic leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its botanical features include showy inflorescences and a preference for moist, well-drained soils in partial to full shade.

Oxypetalum umbellatum is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
