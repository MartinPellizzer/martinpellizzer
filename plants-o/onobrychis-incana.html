Onobrychis incana

Onobrychis incana, commonly known as goat's rue, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. It has a creeping or bushy growth habit and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Onobrychis incana is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable plant in certain ecological contexts.
