Onobrychis oxyodonta var. armena

Onobrychis oxyodonta var. armena, commonly known as Armenian vetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments. It has a deep root system that helps it tolerate drought conditions.

This species is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in soil enrichment.
