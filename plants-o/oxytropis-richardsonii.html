Oxytropis richardsonii

Oxytropis richardsonii, commonly known as the Richardson's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, and it has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Oxytropis richardsonii is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system to access water.

It is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.
