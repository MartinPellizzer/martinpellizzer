Ophrys × arachnitiformis

Ophrys × arachnitiformis, commonly known as the spider orchid hybrid, is a fascinating plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys scrobiculata, exhibiting a unique combination of traits from both parents. The plant is characterized by its intricate, insect-mimicking flowers that closely resemble female wasps, a feature that aids in its pollination strategy. Its floral structure includes a specialized labellum that plays a key role in attracting pollinators.

This hybrid is often found in calcareous soils across Europe, thriving in specific microhabitats with particular environmental conditions.
