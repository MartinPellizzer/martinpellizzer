Oxalis novae-zelandiae

Oxalis novae-zelandiae, commonly known as New Zealand wood sorrel, is a perennial herb native to New Zealand.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive veins and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along streambanks. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in the undergrowth of native forests.

Oxalis novae-zelandiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
