Olax triplinervia

Olax triplinervia, commonly known as the three-veined olax, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Olacaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with three distinct veins. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Olax triplinervia is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
