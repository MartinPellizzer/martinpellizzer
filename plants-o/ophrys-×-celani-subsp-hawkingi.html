Ophrys × celani subsp. hawkingi

Ophrys × celani subsp.

hawkingi, commonly known as the Hawking's orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It is characterized by its intricate floral structure, which closely resembles the female wasp, a key feature in its pollination strategy.

The plant produces showy, bee-pollinated flowers with a complex blend of colors and patterns that mimic the appearance of its pollinators. It thrives in calcareous soils and requires specific microclimatic conditions for successful growth.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
