Oxyacantha azarolus

Oxyacantha azarolus, commonly known as the Persian honeysuckle, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often blooming in early summer. Its fruits are small, red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Oxyacantha azarolus is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
