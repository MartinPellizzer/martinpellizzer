Oenothera cheiranthifolia f. delicatula

Oenothera cheiranthifolia f. delicatula, commonly known as the delicate evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender stems and showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

The flowers emit a mild scent, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
