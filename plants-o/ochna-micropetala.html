Ochna micropetala

Ochna micropetala, commonly known as the small-petalled ochna, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, followed by orange to red berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and showy blooms.

Ochna micropetala prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for gardens in tropical and subtropical climates.
