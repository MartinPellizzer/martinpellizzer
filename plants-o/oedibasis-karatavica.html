Oedibasis karatavica

Oedibasis karatavica, commonly known as the Karatavica grasshopperweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish or pale yellow in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

Oedibasis karatavica is adapted to withstand drought conditions and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
