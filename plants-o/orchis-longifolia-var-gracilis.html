Orchis longifolia var. gracilis

Orchis longifolia var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and delicate, pale purple to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its flowers are borne on a single, erect spike.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
