Ochna macrocarpa

Ochna macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited ochna, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, elongated, woody fruits. Its bark is rough and fissured, and it is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Ochna macrocarpa is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
