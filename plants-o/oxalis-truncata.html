Oxalis truncata

Oxalis truncata, commonly known as the heartleaf wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale yellow with five petals. The plant produces small, rounded fruits that contain several seeds. Oxalis truncata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable environments.
