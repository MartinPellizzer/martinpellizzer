Ophrys lunulata

Ophrys lunulata, commonly known as the moon orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its unique flower structure, which closely resembles a female bee, a feature that plays a key role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellow with intricate markings that mimic the appearance of a bee's abdomen.

Ophrys lunulata is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its specialized relationship with specific pollinators.
