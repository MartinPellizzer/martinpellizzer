Ophioglossum obovatum

Ophioglossum obovatum, commonly known as the broad-ovate adder's tongue fern, is a unique fern species characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves.

It belongs to the family Ophioglossaceae and is native to various regions in the Northern Hemisphere. The plant has a short, underground rhizome and produces sporangia on the underside of its fertile leaves. Its reproductive structures are specialized, with the sporangia borne on short stalks called sporophylls.

Ophioglossum obovatum is often found in moist, shaded environments, such as woodlands and stream banks.
