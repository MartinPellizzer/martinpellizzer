Olea brevipes

Olea brevipes, commonly known as the dwarf olive, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It features small, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a glossy green surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by small, round fruits containing a single seed. Olea brevipes is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its compact size and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
