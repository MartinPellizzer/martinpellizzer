Onopordum canum

Onopordum canum, commonly known as tarragon weed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features silver-gray, woolly leaves and produces yellow composite flowers in late summer. Its botanical characteristics include a robust root system and a branching structure that allows it to thrive in dry, disturbed soils.

Onopordum canum is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats, where it exhibits drought tolerance and adaptability to harsh conditions.
