Orthophytum schulzianum

Orthophytum schulzianum, commonly known as the Schultze's orthophytum, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect water. The plant produces vibrant, showy inflorescences that can last for several months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
