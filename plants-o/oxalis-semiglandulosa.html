Oxalis semiglandulosa

Oxalis semiglandulosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features trifoliate leaves with a velvety texture and reddish veins, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Oxalis semiglandulosa is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and ease of cultivation.
