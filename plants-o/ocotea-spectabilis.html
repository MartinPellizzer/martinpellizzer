Ocotea spectabilis

Ocotea spectabilis, commonly known as the fragrant ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurel and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a spherical drupe, containing a single seed, and it is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.

The plant's aromatic properties make it valuable in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
