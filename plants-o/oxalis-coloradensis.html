Oxalis coloradensis

Oxalis coloradensis, commonly known as the Colorado wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish or purple tinge, and small, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, edible seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Oxalis coloradensis is adapted to arid conditions and is often found in regions with well-drained soils.
