Ourisia macrophylla subsp. lactea

Ourisia macrophylla subsp.

lactea, commonly known as the milkleaf ourisia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southern regions of South America. It is characterized by its distinctive, milky-white leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy textures.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
