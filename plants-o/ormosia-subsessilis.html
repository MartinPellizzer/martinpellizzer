Ormosia subsessilis

Ormosia subsessilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ormosia, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with a narrow, lanceolate shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemate inflorescences, followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its hard, durable wood.

The species is also noted for its potential in ecological restoration due to its adaptability and nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
