Orchis hatagirea var. schlaginweitii

Orchis hatagirea var. schlaginweitii, commonly known as the Himalayan tawny orchid, is a rare and endangered orchid species native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, tawny-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats at elevations ranging from 2,500 to 4,500 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss in its arid environment.

Due to habitat destruction and over-collection, it is critically threatened and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
