Oscularia vernicolor

Oscularia vernicolor, commonly known as the spring-eyed dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white wool. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy, white seed heads that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and drought-tolerant landscapes.
