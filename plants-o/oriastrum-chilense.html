Oriastrum chilense

Oriastrum chilense, commonly known as Chilean thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Andes region of Chile.

It is characterized by its spiny, gray-green foliage and produces showy purple flowers in the spring. The plant is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in well-drained soils. It has a rosette growth habit and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Oriastrum chilense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
