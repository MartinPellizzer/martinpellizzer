Oxalis valdiviensis

Oxalis valdiviensis, commonly known as the Valdivia wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the family Oxalidaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its foliage contains oxalic acid, which can be toxic if ingested in large quantities.

Oxalis valdiviensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
