Odontoglossum vexillarium var. purpureum

Odontoglossum vexillarium var. purpureum, commonly known as the purple orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to the Andes.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple and white petals, which are adorned with intricate patterns that enhance its ornamental appeal. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in late summer, producing a fragrant, showy display that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic beauty and is often cultivated for its unique coloration and delicate structure.
