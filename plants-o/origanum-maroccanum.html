Origanum maroccanum

Origanum maroccanum, commonly known as Moroccan oregano, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of North Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small, bright green leaves and clusters of tiny, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it well-suited for Mediterranean climates. Its essential oils, rich in compounds like thymol and carvacrol, contribute to its strong medicinal and culinary properties.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and digestive benefits.
