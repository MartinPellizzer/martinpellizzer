Olea laeta

Olea laeta, commonly known as the silver olive, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows to a height of about 3 to 5 meters. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery or greyish pubescence. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually white or pale yellow and appear in clusters.

The fruit is a small, spherical drupe that turns from green to yellow or orange when mature.
