Oenothera × hoelscheri var. rubricalyx

Oenothera × hoelscheri var. rubricalyx, commonly known as the red-leaf evening primrose, is a hybrid species native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its striking red-veined leaves and yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils. Its botanical features include showy, tubular flowers and a branching stem structure.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has adapted to thrive in harsh, dry conditions.
