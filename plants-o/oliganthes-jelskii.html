Oliganthes jelskii

Oliganthes jelskii, commonly known as the Jelskii's cholla, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its structure is adapted to conserve water, with thick, fleshy stems that store moisture.

Oliganthes jelskii is typically found in rocky soils and is well-suited to harsh desert environments.
