Oxalis berningeri

Oxalis berningeri, commonly known as the Berninger's wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves contain oxalic acid, which can be toxic if ingested in large quantities.

Oxalis berningeri is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
