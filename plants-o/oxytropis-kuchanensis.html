Oxytropis kuchanensis

Oxytropis kuchanensis, commonly known as Kuchan's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers in spikes, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Oxytropis kuchanensis is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in soil enrichment and ecological restoration projects.
