Onagra chrysantha var. cruciata

Onagra chrysantha var. cruciata, commonly known as the yellow evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in prairies and disturbed areas.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly the oil extracted from its seeds.
