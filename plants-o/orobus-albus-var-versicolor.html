Orobus albus var. versicolor

Orobus albus var. versicolor, commonly known as the white orchid with variegated coloration, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to certain regions of South America, particularly in Brazil and Peru, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its elegant, white flowers that often display subtle variations in color, giving it its varietal name. Its leaves are typically broad and lance-shaped, with a glossy surface that reflects light, enhancing its ornamental appeal.

This species is valued in horticulture for its unique floral display and adaptability to controlled growing environments.
