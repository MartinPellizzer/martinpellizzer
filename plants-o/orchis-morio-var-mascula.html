Orchis morio var. mascula

Orchis morio var. mascula, commonly known as the male orchid, is a rare and distinctive variety of the common green orchid.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which includes a prominent, erect spur that distinguishes it from other orchid species. This variety is native to parts of Europe and is often found in woodland habitats. The plant typically blooms in late spring, producing showy, violet-blue flowers with a sweet fragrance.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a protected species in many regions.
