Orchis globosa var. major

Orchis globosa var. major, commonly known as the large globular orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in a globular or clustered shape. The plant grows from a rhizomatous root system and has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

The orchid is pollinated primarily by bees and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.
