Oenothera tubicula subsp. tubicula

Oenothera tubicula subsp.

tubicula, commonly known as the slender evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It features slender, branching stems and produces showy, yellowish-orange flowers that open in the evening.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are typically borne in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

The species is known for its ability to self-pollinate and its role in supporting nocturnal pollinators.
