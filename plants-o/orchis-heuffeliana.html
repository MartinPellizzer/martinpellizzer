Orchis heuffeliana

Orchis heuffeliana, commonly known as the Heuffel's orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear a single spike of flowers. The flowers are typically violet or purple in color, with a distinctive pouch-like structure called a labellum. This species thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded areas with rich, well-drained soil.

Orchis heuffeliana is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
