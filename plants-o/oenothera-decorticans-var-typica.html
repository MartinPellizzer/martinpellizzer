Oenothera decorticans var. typica

Oenothera decorticans var. typica, commonly known as the plains evening primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the central and western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and showy yellow flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and covered in fine hairs.

The species is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairie and disturbed habitats.
