Oxalis anthyllidifolia var. obtusa

Oxalis anthyllidifolia var. obtusa, commonly known as the broad-leaf wood sorrel, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish or purple tint on the underside, and it produces small, yellowish flowers with five petals. The plant is characterized by its succulent stems and leaves, which help it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable ornamental plant in gardens.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to collections of succulent and woodland plants.
