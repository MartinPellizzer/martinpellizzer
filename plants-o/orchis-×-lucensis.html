Orchis × lucensis

Orchis × lucensis, commonly known as the hybrid green-winged orchid, is a natural hybrid between Orchis morio and Orchis anthropophora.

It is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive greenish-blue flowers with prominent veining. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats across Europe. It produces a single spike of flowers, each with a three-lobed lip that is often greenish in color.

Orchis × lucensis is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated by enthusiasts.
