Opilia celtidifolia

Opilia celtidifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf spurge.

It is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States, characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, alternate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by capsules containing numerous seeds. Opilia celtidifolia is often found in open woods, fields, and along roadsides, thriving in moist to mesic soils.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Euphorbiaceae family.
