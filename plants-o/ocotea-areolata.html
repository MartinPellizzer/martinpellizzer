Ocotea areolata

Ocotea areolata, commonly known as the areolate ocotea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in countries like Mexico, Costa Rica, and Panama. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed surrounded by a fleshy pulp.

Ocotea areolata is often found in moist, shaded habitats within lowland and montane forests.
