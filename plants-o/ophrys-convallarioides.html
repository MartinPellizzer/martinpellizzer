Ophrys convallarioides

Ophrys convallarioides, commonly known as the lady's tresses, is a perennial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like leaves and a striking inflorescence of fragrant, labellum-like flowers that resemble female insects, aiding in its unique pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers are often pale green or yellowish with intricate patterns, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

Ophrys convallarioides is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
