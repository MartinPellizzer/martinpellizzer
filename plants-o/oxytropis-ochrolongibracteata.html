Oxytropis ochrolongibracteata

Oxytropis ochrolongibracteata, commonly known as the yellow bract legume, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features upright stems with pinnately compound leaves, and its flowers are typically yellow or pale yellow. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapes and restoration projects.
