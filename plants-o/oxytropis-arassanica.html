Oxytropis arassanica

Oxytropis arassanica, commonly known as the Armenian locoweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, and it is adapted to thrive in dry, rocky environments.

Oxytropis arassanica is also known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock.
