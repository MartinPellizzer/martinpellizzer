Oenothera victorinii

Oenothera victorinii, commonly known as the Victorin's evening primrose, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and wet meadows. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

The plant is characterized by its long, slender stems and is known for its ability to thrive in shaded environments.
