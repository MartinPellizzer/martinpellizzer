Opuntia margaritana

Opuntia margaritana, commonly known as the blue agave, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces striking blue-green flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are modified into spiny structures called cladodes, which help reduce water loss.

Opuntia margaritana is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of agave nectar.
