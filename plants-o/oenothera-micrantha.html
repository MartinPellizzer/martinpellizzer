Oenothera micrantha

Oenothera micrantha, commonly known as the plains evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its yellow, tubular flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats like prairies and disturbed soils. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence.

Oenothera micrantha is also known for its medicinal properties, with its seeds containing high levels of essential fatty acids.
