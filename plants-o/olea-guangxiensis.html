Olea guangxiensis

Olea guangxiensis, commonly known as Guangxi olive, is a deciduous tree native to southern China.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that are typically green when immature and turn black when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
