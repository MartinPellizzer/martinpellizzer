Ophrys holosericea subsp. mesopotamica

Ophrys holosericea subsp.

mesopotamica, commonly known as the Mesopotamian bee orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of the Middle East. This species is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brownish in color, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

The subspecies is relatively rare, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
