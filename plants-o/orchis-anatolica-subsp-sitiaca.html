Orchis anatolica subsp. sitiaca

Orchis anatolica subsp.

sitiaca, commonly known as the Anatolian orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet-blue flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands. Its leaves are lanceolate and arranged alternately along the stem.

It plays a significant role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to floral biodiversity.
