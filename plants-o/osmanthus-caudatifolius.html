Osmanthus caudatifolius

Osmanthus caudatifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved osmanthus, is a species of flowering plant native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, small yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has slender, elongated leaves that give it its common name, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for garden landscapes.

The essential oils extracted from its flowers are used in perfumery and traditional Chinese medicine.
