Oreodoxa granatensis

Oreodoxa granatensis, commonly known as the granate orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns and a distinctive red or orange lip. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool, moist conditions, often in rocky or forested habitats. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Oreodoxa granatensis is valued for its beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
