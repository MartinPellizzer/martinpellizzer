Opuntia anacantha

Opuntia anacantha, commonly known as the spiny prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, spine-covered pads and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems. Its fruit, known as a tunafly, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

Opuntia anacantha plays an important role in its ecosystem, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
