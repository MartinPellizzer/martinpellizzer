Oncidium guianense f. aureorubrum

Oncidium guianense f. aureorubrum, commonly known as the golden red orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, long-lasting flowers that range from yellow to orange-red in color. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its pseudobulbs are cylindrical, and it produces multiple racemes of fragrant blooms.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
