Orchis incarnata var. albiflora

Orchis incarnata var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered pink orchid, is a species of orchid native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and clusters of pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and wet meadows. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

The species is noted for its delicate appearance and is often found in association with other orchid species in its native range.
