Ophrys monorchis

Ophrys monorchis, commonly known as the one-testicle orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species found primarily in the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a male bee, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant produces a single, showy flower that emits a scent similar to that of a bee, attracting male bees for pollination. Its habitat is typically dry, rocky areas, and it thrives in specific microclimates with limited moisture.

Due to its limited distribution and specialized ecological requirements, Ophrys monorchis is considered a vulnerable species.
