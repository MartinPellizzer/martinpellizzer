Orobanche scolymi

Orobanche scolymi, commonly known as the common broomrape, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is found in various regions across Europe, Asia, and North Africa. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has a taproot system that attaches to the roots of its host. It is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its ability to parasitize crops like tomatoes and beans.

Its life cycle is closely tied to the growth and death of its host, making it a challenging plant to manage in cultivated fields.
