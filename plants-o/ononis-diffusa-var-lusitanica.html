Ononis diffusa var. lusitanica

Ononis diffusa var. lusitanica, commonly known as the Lusitanian whitlow grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils, often found in open woodlands and grasslands. The plant features slender, wiry stems with small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are pale yellow to white, arranged in clusters and blooming from late spring to early summer.

This variety is noted for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in nutrient-poor environments.
