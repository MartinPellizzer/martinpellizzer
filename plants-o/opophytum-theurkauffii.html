Opophytum theurkauffii

Opophytum theurkauffii, commonly known as the dwarf cycad, is a unique plant belonging to the Cycadaceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit. The plant features a short, woody trunk and pinnately compound leaves that are dark green and leathery in texture. Opophytum theurkauffii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its slow growth and ornamental appeal, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in tropical and subtropical regions.
