Ocotea nigrescens

Ocotea nigrescens, commonly known as black ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its dark, aromatic wood and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by spherical, black berries containing the seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and montane rainforests.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
