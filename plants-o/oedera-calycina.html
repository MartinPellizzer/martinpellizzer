Oedera calycina

Oedera calycina, commonly known as the velvetleaf or false holly, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Oedera calycina thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
