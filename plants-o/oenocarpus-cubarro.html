Oenocarpus cubarro

Oenocarpus cubarro, commonly known as the Cuban palm or Cuban coconut palm, is a species of palm native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces large, oval fruits that are covered in a fibrous husk, similar to coconuts. The fruit contains a single seed, which is often used for propagation.

Oenocarpus cubarro thrives in tropical climates and is often found in coastal areas and wetlands.
