Orchis angustifolia f. acuminata

Orchis angustifolia f. acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved twayblade, is a rare and specialized orchid species found in specific habitats across Europe.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color, often with a faint purple or brownish hue. It thrives in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and meadows, and is known for its mycorrhizal associations that aid in nutrient uptake.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, it is considered a vulnerable species in many regions.
