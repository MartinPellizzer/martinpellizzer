Osmanthus angustifolius

Osmanthus angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sweet osmanthus, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in autumn. The plant is valued for its aromatic flowers, which are used in traditional Chinese medicine and as a flavoring in teas and confections. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its unique scent and ornamental value contribute to its cultural significance in East Asian horticulture.
