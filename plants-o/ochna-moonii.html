Ochna moonii

Ochna moonii, commonly known as the Natal yellowwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in terminal umbels, followed by orange-red berries. Ochna moonii thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
