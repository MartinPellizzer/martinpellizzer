Ocotea longipes

Ocotea longipes, commonly known as the long-leaf ocotea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. Its flowers are small and clustered in umbels, and it produces dark blue to black berries that are edible for some bird species.

The tree is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
