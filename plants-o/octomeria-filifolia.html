Octomeria filifolia

Octomeria filifolia, commonly known as the thread-leaved octomeria, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, thread-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some botanical gardens and private collections.
