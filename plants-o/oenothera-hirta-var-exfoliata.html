Oenothera hirta var. exfoliata

Oenothera hirta var. exfoliata, commonly known as the exfoliating evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and showy yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening. The leaves are usually hairy and have a distinctive texture, while the bark of the stem exfoliates, revealing a reddish inner layer. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or disturbed areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
