Oligochaetochilus sargentii

Oligochaetochilus sargentii, commonly known as the Sargent's hairroot orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, intricately patterned flowers and its preference for humid, shaded environments. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or rocks, forming a symbiotic relationship with specific mycorrhizal fungi. Its roots are finely divided and adapted for absorbing moisture from the air, a feature common among epiphytic orchids.

Due to habitat destruction, Oligochaetochilus sargentii is considered endangered and is often found in conservation programs aimed at preserving its unique botanical traits.
