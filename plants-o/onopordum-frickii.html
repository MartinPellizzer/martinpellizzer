Onopordum frickii

Onopordum frickii, commonly known as the Frick's thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowering stems are often branched and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

Onopordum frickii is known for its hardy nature and is sometimes considered an invasive species in non-native regions.
