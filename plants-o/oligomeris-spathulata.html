Oligomeris spathulata

Oligomeris spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leaved dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, spoon-shaped leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties. It thrives in grasslands and disturbed soils, often found in open habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional use in herbal medicine.
