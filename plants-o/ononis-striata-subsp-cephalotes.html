Ononis striata subsp. cephalotes

Ononis striata subsp.

cephalotes, commonly known as the purple sandwort, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is a low-growing perennial that typically thrives in sandy or rocky soils.

The plant features small, clustered flowers that are often pink or purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
