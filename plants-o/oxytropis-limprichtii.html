Oxytropis limprichtii

Oxytropis limprichtii, commonly known as the Limpricht's locoweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, spindly stems and small, compound leaves. The plant produces yellow or greenish-yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland environments.

Oxytropis limprichtii is also known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock.
