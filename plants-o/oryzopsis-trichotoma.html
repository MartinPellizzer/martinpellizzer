Oryzopsis trichotoma

Oryzopsis trichotoma, commonly known as the hairy barleygrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, typical of the Poaceae family. It is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient plant in harsh environments.

Oryzopsis trichotoma is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
