Oedochloa cordata

Oedochloa cordata, commonly known as sicklegrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are often curved at the tips, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences, and its flowers are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

Oedochloa cordata is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
