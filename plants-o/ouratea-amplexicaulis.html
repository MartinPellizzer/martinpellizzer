Ouratea amplexicaulis

Ouratea amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping ouratea, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage with elliptical leaves that often wrap around the stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
