Osmanthus marginatus var. longissimus

Osmanthus marginatus var. longissimus, commonly known as the long-leaf osmanthus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Oleaceae.

It is native to southern China and is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant yellow flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its aromatic blossoms, which are used in traditional Chinese medicine and as a flavoring agent. Its ornamental value is also appreciated in gardens due to its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.

The varietal name "longissimus" refers to the exceptionally long leaves that distinguish this variety from other osmanthus species.
