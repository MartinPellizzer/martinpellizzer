Oxalis mallobolba

Oxalis mallobolba, commonly known as the yellow wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in woodland environments. Its leaves contain oxalic acid, which can be toxic in large quantities.

Oxalis mallobolba is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and delicate flowers.
