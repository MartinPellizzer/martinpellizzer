Onobrychis mutensis

Onobrychis mutensis, commonly known as the Turkish clover, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a deep root system, allowing it to thrive in poor, dry soils. It is often used in forage agriculture due to its high nutritional value for livestock.

Additionally, it plays a role in soil stabilization and nitrogen fixation.
