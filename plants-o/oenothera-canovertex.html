Oenothera canovertex

Oenothera canovertex, commonly known as the yellow evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its yellow, tubular flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Oenothera canovertex thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and native plant restoration.
