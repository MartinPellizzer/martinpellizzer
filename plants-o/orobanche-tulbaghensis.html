Orobanche tulbaghensis

Orobanche tulbaghensis, commonly known as the Tulbagh broom.

It is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on its host for nutrients. The plant produces striking violet or purple flowers, typically in clusters, and is native to southern Africa. It is often found growing on the roots of various grasses and shrubs.

Due to its parasitic nature, it plays a unique role in its ecosystem, influencing host plant populations and contributing to biodiversity.
