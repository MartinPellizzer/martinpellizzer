Oxalis imbricata

Oxalis imbricata, commonly known as the rock oxalis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is a perennial species that produces tubers, allowing it to survive in dry conditions.

Oxalis imbricata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
