Oedipachne

Oedipachne, commonly known as the "sicklegrass," is a genus of grasses native to Africa, Asia, and Australia.

These plants typically grow in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing their adaptability to harsh conditions. Oedipachne species are characterized by their narrow, grass-like leaves and compact growth habit. They often form dense mats or low shrubs, which help in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion.

The genus is of interest in ecological studies due to its role in desert ecosystems and potential for use in land rehabilitation projects.
