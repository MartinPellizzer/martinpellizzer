Ophrys sphegodes subsp. argensonensis

Ophrys sphegodes subsp.

argensonensis, commonly known as the Argenson orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species found in specific alpine habitats. It belongs to the genus Ophrys, which is known for its intricate mimicry of female insects to attract male pollinators.

This subspecies is characterized by its distinctive, bee-like floral structure, which plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it faces harsh environmental conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in some regions.
