Ozanonia uberta

Ozanonia uberta, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in spikes. Ozanonia uberta is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils, making it an invasive species in many areas.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and challenging to control in agricultural settings.
