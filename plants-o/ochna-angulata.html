Ochna angulata

Ochna angulata, commonly known as the angular ochna, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including woodlands and rocky areas, and is often found in regions with a Mediterranean climate.

Ochna angulata is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is used in traditional medicine in some parts of its native range.
