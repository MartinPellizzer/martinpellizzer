Opisthopappus longilobus

Opisthopappus longilobus, commonly known as the long-lobed pappus plant, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its yellowish to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, with a hairy surface.

The fruit is a small, bristly achene that is adapted for wind dispersal.
