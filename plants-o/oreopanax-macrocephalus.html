Oreopanax macrocephalus

Oreopanax macrocephalus, commonly known as the giant arrowhead plant, is a striking member of the Araceae family native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, arrowhead-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length, often with a glossy, dark green surface. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers enclosed in a spadix, which is surrounded by a colorful spathe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its unique appearance and growth habits, it is a popular specimen plant in botanical gardens and conservatories.
