Oxytenanthera auriculata

Oxytenanthera auriculata, commonly known as the golden cup fern, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped fronds that resemble small cups, giving it its common name. The plant belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor gardens.
