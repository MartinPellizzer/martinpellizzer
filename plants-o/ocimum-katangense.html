Ocimum katangense

Ocimum katangense, commonly known as Katanga basil, is a flowering plant native to the Democratic Republic of the Congo.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and spices. The plant features opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are often aromatic and used in traditional medicine. Its small, tubular flowers are typically clustered in whorls along the stems.

Ocimum katangense is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in local remedies for various ailments.
