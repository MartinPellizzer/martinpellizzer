Oenothera neomexicana

Oenothera neomexicana, commonly known as the New Mexico evening primrose, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Oenothera neomexicana is part of the evening primrose family (Onagraceae) and is known for its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
