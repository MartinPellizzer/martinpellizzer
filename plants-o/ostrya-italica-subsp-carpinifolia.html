Ostrya italica subsp. carpinifolia

Ostrya italica subsp.

carpinifolia, commonly known as the Italian hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its small, ovate leaves with serrated edges.

The tree produces small, yellowish catkins in early spring, followed by small, winged nuts in the fall. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mixed forests.

The bark is smooth and gray, becoming fissured with age, and the wood is hard and durable.
