Oplismenus pseudocolonus

Oplismenus pseudocolonus, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense, cylindrical heads, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and marshes, often growing in dense colonies.

Oplismenus pseudocolonus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
