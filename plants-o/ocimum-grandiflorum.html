Ocimum grandiflorum

Ocimum grandiflorum, commonly known as holy basil or tulsi, is a perennial herb native to India.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and lavender. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their therapeutic properties.

Ocimum grandiflorum thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
