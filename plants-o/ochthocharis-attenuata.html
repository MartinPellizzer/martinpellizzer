Ochthocharis attenuata

Ochthocharis attenuata, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a species of orchid native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in a spike. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Due to its delicate appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
