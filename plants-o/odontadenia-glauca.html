Odontadenia glauca

Odontadenia glauca, commonly known as the blue-flowered rock daisy, is a perennial herb native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of grey-green, woolly leaves and produces striking blue or purple flowers in spring. The plant thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats. It is adapted to withstand drought and has a deep root system for water storage.

Odontadenia glauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
