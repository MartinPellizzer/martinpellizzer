Obione longitrichoma

Obione longitrichoma, commonly known as the long-hair fern, is a species of fern characterized by its unique foliage.

It features elongated, lance-shaped fronds covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate regions. Its reproductive structures include sporangia that release spores for propagation.

The botanical study of Obione longitrichoma provides insights into the evolutionary adaptations of ferns in diverse ecosystems.
