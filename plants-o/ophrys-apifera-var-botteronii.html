Ophrys apifera var. botteronii

Ophrys apifera var. botteronii, commonly known as the bee orchid, is a species of orchid native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure that closely resembles a bee, which plays a key role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in grassy habitats and has a short, erect stem with a single spike of flowers. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette at the base of the stem.

The orchid's ability to mimic bees is a remarkable example of evolutionary adaptation in plant pollination.
