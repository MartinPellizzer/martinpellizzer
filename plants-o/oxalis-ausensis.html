Oxalis ausensis

Oxalis ausensis, commonly known as the yellow wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Oxalis ausensis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
