Ochna acuminata

Ochna acuminata, commonly known as the African star glory, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces striking yellow flowers in clusters, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits that are edible for some animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Ochna acuminata is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
