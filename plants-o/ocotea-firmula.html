Ocotea firmula

Ocotea firmula, commonly known as the Brazilian camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and wood. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy texture.

The tree is valued for its fragrant wood, which is used in various traditional and commercial applications.
