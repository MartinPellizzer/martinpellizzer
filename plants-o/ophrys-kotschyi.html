Ophrys kotschyi

Ophrys kotschyi, commonly known as the Kotschyi bee orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its unique flower structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments. Its flowers are usually a mix of brown and yellow, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Ophrys kotschyi is a perennial herb with a short stem and a single, showy blossom.
