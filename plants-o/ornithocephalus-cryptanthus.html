Ornithocephalus cryptanthus

Ornithocephalus cryptanthus, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a unique plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in winter, often appearing as if they are floating in the air. The plant has a compact, mat-like growth habit with dense, silver-green foliage that helps it conserve water in harsh conditions. Its flowers are short-lived, opening in the early morning and closing by midday, a trait that may aid in pollination by specific insects.

Ornithocephalus cryptanthus is also notable for its ability to survive in extreme drought, making it a resilient and fascinating example of botanical adaptation.
