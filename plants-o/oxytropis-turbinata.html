Oxytropis turbinata

Oxytropis turbinata, commonly known as the Turban Vetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, turban-shaped seed pods. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, usually yellow or purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Oxytropis turbinata is also known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
