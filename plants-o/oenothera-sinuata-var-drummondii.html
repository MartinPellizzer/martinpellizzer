Oenothera sinuata var. drummondii

Oenothera sinuata var. drummondii, commonly known as the Drummond's evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening and close by morning. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive sinuate margin. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
