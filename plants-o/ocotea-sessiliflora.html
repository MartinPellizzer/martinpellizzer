Ocotea sessiliflora

Ocotea sessiliflora, commonly known as the Brazilian camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and showy white flowers. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that contain the seeds. Its wood is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine.

Ocotea sessiliflora plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
