Ononis cenisia var. mairei

Ononis cenisia var. mairei, commonly known as the Maire's bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial with slender stems and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has alternate, narrow leaves that are often hairy, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it a characteristic species of open, sunny habitats.
