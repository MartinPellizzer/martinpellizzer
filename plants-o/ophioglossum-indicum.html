Ophioglossum indicum

It is characterized by its distinctive, tongue-shaped spore-bearing structure called a sporophyll, which is unique to the Ophioglossaceae family. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands, where it thrives in humid conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and lanceolate, with a single, prominent sporophyll at the base.
