Oxalis tubistipula

Oxalis tubistipula, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features trifoliate leaves with three oval, pointed leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its stems are often hairy, and it has a fibrous root system.

Oxalis tubistipula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
