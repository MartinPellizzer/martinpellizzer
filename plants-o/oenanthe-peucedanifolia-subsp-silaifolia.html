Oenanthe peucedanifolia subsp. silaifolia

Oenanthe peucedanifolia subsp.

silaifolia, commonly known as the Silaifolia angel's trumpet, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
