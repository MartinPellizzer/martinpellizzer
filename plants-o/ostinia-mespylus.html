Ostinia mespylus

Ostinia mespylus, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, daisy-like yellow blooms that appear in late summer to early fall. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ostinia mespylus is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
