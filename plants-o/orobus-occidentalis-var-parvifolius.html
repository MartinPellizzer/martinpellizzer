Orobus occidentalis var. parvifolius

Orobus occidentalis var. parvifolius, commonly known as the small-leaved western orobus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features small, elliptical leaves and produces clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Its ecological role includes supporting pollinators and providing habitat for various small animals.
