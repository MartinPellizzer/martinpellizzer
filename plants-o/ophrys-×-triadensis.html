Ophrys × triadensis

Ophrys × triadensis, commonly known as the Triad Orchid, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys citrina.

It is characterized by its intricate, bee-mimicking flowers that play a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is found in the Mediterranean region, particularly in southern Europe. Its flowers feature a complex structure with a prominent lip that resembles a female bee, attracting male bees for pollination.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ecological significance in supporting pollinator populations.
