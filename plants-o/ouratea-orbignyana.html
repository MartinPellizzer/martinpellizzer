Ouratea orbignyana

Ouratea orbignyana, commonly known as the orchid tree, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, orchid-like flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has a slender, often branched stem and large, palmate leaves that are deeply lobed. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in tropical gardens for its striking floral display.
