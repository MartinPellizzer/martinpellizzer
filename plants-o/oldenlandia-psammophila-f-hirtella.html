Oldenlandia psammophila f. hirtella

Oldenlandia psammophila f. hirtella, commonly known as the hairy sand-plain morning glory, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, showy blue or violet flowers. The leaves are typically hairy, giving the plant its specific epithet "hirtella," which refers to the hairy texture. It thrives in sandy soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

This plant is adapted to survive in harsh environments with low rainfall and high temperatures.
