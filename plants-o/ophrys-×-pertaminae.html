Ophrys × pertaminae

Ophrys × pertaminae, commonly known as the Pertamina bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is native to southern Europe and is characterized by its distinctive flower structure that mimics the appearance of a female wasp, a trait known as sexual deception. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats. Its flowers are usually yellow to orange in color, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by male wasps.

Ophrys × pertaminae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
