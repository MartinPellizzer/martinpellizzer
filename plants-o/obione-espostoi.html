Obione espostoi

Obione espostoi, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage and delicate, fragrant blooms that appear in late spring. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and specialty collections.

Due to its limited habitat, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
