Ouratea denudata

Ouratea denudata, commonly known as the naked ouratea, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by orange-red berries containing seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and montane forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
