Oenothera mollissima var. valdiviana

Oenothera mollissima var. valdiviana, commonly known as the Valdivian evening primrose, is a species native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. It is a perennial herb that produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, oily seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
