Origanum × paniculatum

Origanum × paniculatum, commonly known as autumn sweet, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in gardens for its fragrance and visual appeal. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, though it can tolerate partial shade.

Origanum × paniculatum is also known for its culinary uses, as its leaves can be used in cooking, similar to oregano.
