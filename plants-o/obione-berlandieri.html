Obione berlandieri

Obione berlandieri, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a garden plant.
