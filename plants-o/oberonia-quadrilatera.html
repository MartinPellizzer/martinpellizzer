Oberonia quadrilatera

Oberonia quadrilatera, commonly known as the four-sided orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive four-sided, fleshy rhizomes and its small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are ovate and arranged in a rosette, and it produces elongated, pendulous inflorescences.

Oberonia quadrilatera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
