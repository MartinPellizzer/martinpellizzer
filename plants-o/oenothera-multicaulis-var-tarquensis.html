Oenothera multicaulis var. tarquensis

Oenothera multicaulis var. tarquensis, commonly known as the Tarquenian evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has a hairy stem and lance-shaped leaves, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local wildlife.
