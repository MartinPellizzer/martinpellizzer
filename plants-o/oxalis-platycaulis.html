Oxalis platycaulis

Oxalis platycaulis, commonly known as the broad-leaf wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped lobes and produces clusters of small, yellow, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its stems are often hairy, and it has a fibrous root system.

Oxalis platycaulis is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
