Oenothera biennis var. sulphurea

Oenothera biennis var. sulphurea, commonly known as sulphur evening primrose, is a variant of the common evening primrose.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America, characterized by its yellow flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in prairies and disturbed areas.

Its botanical interest lies in its unique yellow floral color and its role in ecological and medicinal contexts.
