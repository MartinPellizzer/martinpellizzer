Octolepis ratovosonii

Octolepis ratovosonii, commonly known as the Madagascar grass.

It is a flowering plant native to Madagascar, characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded areas.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
