Operculicarya hyphaenoides

Operculicarya hyphaenoides, commonly known as the ghost fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its intricate, lace-like fronds that give it a ghostly appearance, hence its common name. The plant has a unique reproductive structure called an operculum, which protects the spore-producing organs until they are ready to release spores. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in dense tropical forests.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Operculicarya hyphaenoides is considered a conservation concern.
