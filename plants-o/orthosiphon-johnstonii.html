Orthosiphon johnstonii

Orthosiphon johnstonii, commonly known as Johnston's horsegram, is a member of the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue to violet in color. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant's botanical features make it distinct within its genus, with its unique floral structure and aromatic foliage.
