Oligactis pastoensis

Oligactis pastoensis, commonly known as the Pasto aster, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Colombia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open areas, thriving in temperate to subtropical climates. Its flowers are usually blue to violet in color, and it produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Oligactis pastoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
