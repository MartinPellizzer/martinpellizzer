Oxytropis hissarica

Oxytropis hissarica, commonly known as the Crimean locoweed, is a perennial legume native to the steppes of southern Russia and the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit, hairy stems, and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant produces pods containing several seeds, which are typically dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, temperate climates.

Oxytropis hissarica is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
