Oreophilus ricii

Oreophilus ricii, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to high-altitude regions.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. Its flowers are small, clustered in inflorescences, and display a range of colors from white to pale purple.

Oreophilus ricii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in specialized gardens.
