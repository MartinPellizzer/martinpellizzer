Oxyceros longiflorus

Oxyceros longiflorus, commonly known as the long-flowered oxyceros, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. Its flowering period usually occurs during the summer months.

Oxyceros longiflorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
