Osteospermum lanceolatum

Osteospermum lanceolatum, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the sunflower family, Asteraceae, and is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple daisy-like flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It produces showy composite flowers with a central disk surrounded by ray florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Osteospermum lanceolatum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in full sun.
