Oldenlandia eualata

Oldenlandia eualata, commonly known as the silverleaf spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its delicate, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling small spiderwebs, hence its common name. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in coastal areas or near freshwater sources.

Oldenlandia eualata is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
