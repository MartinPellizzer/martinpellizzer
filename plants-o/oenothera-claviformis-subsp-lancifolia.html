Oenothera claviformis subsp. lancifolia

Oenothera claviformis subsp.

lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and showy yellow flowers that open in the evening.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oily seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential medicinal applications.
