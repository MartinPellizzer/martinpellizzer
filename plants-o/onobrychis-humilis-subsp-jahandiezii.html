Onobrychis humilis subsp. jahandiezii

Onobrychis humilis subsp.

jahandiezii, commonly known as the Persian vetch, is a low-growing leguminous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers, typical of the Fabaceae family.

The plant has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in poor soils and withstand drought conditions. It is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats, contributing to soil stabilization.

Its ecological importance includes providing forage for livestock and supporting local insect populations.
