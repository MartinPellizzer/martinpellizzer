Oxalis stricta var. navieri

Oxalis stricta var. navieri, commonly known as the Navier's wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. It is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to native plant gardens.
