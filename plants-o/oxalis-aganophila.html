Oxalis aganophila

Oxalis aganophila, commonly known as the velvetleaf wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant has a short, hairy stem and produces small, bead-like fruits that contain seeds. Oxalis aganophila thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
