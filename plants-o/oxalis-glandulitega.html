Oxalis glandulitega

Oxalis glandulitega, commonly known as the sticky oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive, glandular hairs on its stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in temperate regions.

Oxalis glandulitega is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
