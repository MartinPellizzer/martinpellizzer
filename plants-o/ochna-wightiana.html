Ochna wightiana

Ochna wightiana, commonly known as the African star apple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its palmate leaves with five to seven lobes. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that are typically clustered in terminal panicles. Its fruit is a rough, spiny capsule that splits open to reveal a sweet, starchy pulp.

Ochna wightiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
