Oplismenus compositus f. glabratus

Oplismenus compositus f. glabratus, commonly known as the smoothleaf false dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves that are typically lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a dense, spherical inflorescence. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species within its native habitat.
