Ortholoma serratum

Ortholoma serratum, commonly known as the California thimbleweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaf margins. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in a spherical shape, and it has a short, creeping stem with alternate leaves.

Ortholoma serratum is adapted to arid environments and is often found in regions with low annual precipitation.
