Oxytropis dumbedanica

It is native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Central Asia, where it thrives in rocky and arid environments. The plant is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems and small, compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically pale blue to purple, arranged in clusters, and bloom in early summer.

Oxytropis dumbedanica is known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock.
