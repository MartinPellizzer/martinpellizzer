Oryza sativa var. graeca

Oryza sativa var. graeca, commonly known as Greek rice, is a cultivated variant of rice native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its short, slender grains and typically grows in temperate climates. This variety is adapted to cooler temperatures and requires less water compared to other rice types. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a grass-like structure with narrow leaves.

It is primarily grown for its edible grains, which are used in various traditional cuisines.
