Orchis latifolia var. traunsteineri

Orchis latifolia var. traunsteineri, commonly known as the Traunsteiner's broad-leaved orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to specific regions of Central Europe.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces striking, pale purple to white flowers with a distinctive, intricate structure. This variety thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in shaded woodland environments. Its floral morphology is adapted for pollination by specific insect species, contributing to its ecological niche.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
