Ornithogalum inconspicuum

Ornithogalum inconspicuum, commonly known as the small white bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces delicate, bell-shaped white flowers on slender stems. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths. It prefers well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its compact size and elegant appearance make it a desirable addition to botanical collections and native gardens.
