Oenothera pyramidalis

Oenothera pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramid evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and showy yellow flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by moths and other nocturnal insects.

Oenothera pyramidalis is known for its ability to thrive in arid and rocky environments.
