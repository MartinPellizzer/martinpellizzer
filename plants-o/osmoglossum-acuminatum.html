Osmoglossum acuminatum

Osmoglossum acuminatum, commonly known as the sharp-pointed orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom during the wet season. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are elongated and have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its moist habitat.

Osmoglossum acuminatum is notable for its specialized pollination mechanism, relying on specific insect species for reproduction.
