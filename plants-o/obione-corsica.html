Obione corsica

Obione corsica, commonly known as Corsican thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Corsica.

It is characterized by its robust, spiny leaves and purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss.

Obione corsica plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing food and shelter for various insects and small animals.
