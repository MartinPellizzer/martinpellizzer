Opuntia wagenknechtii

Opuntia wagenknechtii, commonly known as the Wagenknecht's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of Baja California, Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems that are covered in spines and glochids, which provide protection from herbivores and excessive sunlight. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers in the spring, followed by edible fruits that are typically consumed fresh or processed into beverages. It is well adapted to hot, dry climates and requires minimal water, making it a resilient plant in desert environments.

Opuntia wagenknechtii plays an important ecological role by providing food and shelter for various desert animals.
