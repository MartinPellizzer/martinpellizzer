Olea laxiflora

Olea laxiflora, commonly known as the loose-flower olive, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically green when immature and turn black when ripe. Olea laxiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and adaptability.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
