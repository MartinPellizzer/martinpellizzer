Origanum vulgare subsp. virens

Origanum vulgare subsp.

virens, commonly known as green oregano, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green.

The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in summer, which are clustered in rounded inflorescences. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, giving it a distinct minty and slightly citrusy fragrance.

It is often used in Mediterranean cuisine and traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
