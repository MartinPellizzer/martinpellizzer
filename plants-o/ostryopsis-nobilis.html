Ostryopsis nobilis

Ostryopsis nobilis, commonly known as the noble osier, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars, and is characterized by its slender, flexible branches. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in catkins, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

Ostryopsis nobilis is often used in hedgerows and wetland restoration due to its adaptability and ecological benefits.
