Orchis incarnata f. stenophylla

Orchis incarnata f. stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lady's tresses, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and a dense spike of pale pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and wet meadows. Its flowers are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem, a feature common in many orchid species.

This orchid is often found in the eastern United States and is known for its delicate appearance and ecological significance.
