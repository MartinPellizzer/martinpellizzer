Ornithogalum umbellatum var. angustifolium

Ornithogalum umbellatum var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a tall, leafless stem bearing umbel-like clusters of white, star-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

The species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant appearance and early blooming habit.
