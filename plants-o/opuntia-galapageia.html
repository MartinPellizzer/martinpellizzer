Opuntia galapageia

Opuntia galapageia, commonly known as the Galápagos prickly pear, is a cactus species native to the Galápagos Islands.

It is characterized by its flattened, paddle-shaped stems that are covered in spines and glochids, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the dry season, which are followed by edible, fleshy fruits. Opuntia galapageia thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to the unique climate of the Galápagos.

Its ecological role includes providing food and shelter for various native wildlife species.
