Oxalis sericea var. pilosa

Oxalis sericea var. pilosa, commonly known as the woolly wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and modest floral display.
