Orthosiphon xylorrhizus

Orthosiphon xylorrhizus, commonly known as Java tea or Malabar tea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is often grown for its aromatic leaves, which are used in traditional medicine and as a herbal tea.

The roots and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, contributing to its medicinal properties.
