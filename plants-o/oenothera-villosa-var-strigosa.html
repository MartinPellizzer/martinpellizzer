Oenothera villosa var. strigosa

Oenothera villosa var. strigosa, commonly known as the hairy evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces showy yellow or orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed areas.
