Ophrys aegirtica

Ophrys aegirtica, commonly known as the Greek bee orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its intricate, bee-like flower structure, which features a vividly colored lip that mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male pollinators. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of fragrant, greenish-yellow blooms.

Ophrys aegirtica is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinator populations and its aesthetic appeal in horticultural settings.
