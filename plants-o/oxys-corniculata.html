Oxys corniculata

Oxys corniculata, commonly known as the horned vetch, is a leguminous plant native to various regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, curved pods and trifoliate leaves, which contribute to its ornamental appeal. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Oxys corniculata is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in soil enrichment.
