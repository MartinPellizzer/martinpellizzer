Oxynepeta parviflora

Oxynepeta parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered oxynepeta, is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves.

It is native to southern Africa, where it thrives in rocky and sandy soils. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stems. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Oxynepeta parviflora is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
