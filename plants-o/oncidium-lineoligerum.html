Oncidium lineoligerum

Oncidium lineoligerum, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a tropical orchid species native to Central and South America.

It features vibrant, showy flowers with a distinctive pattern of dark markings on the petals and sepals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrate.

Oncidium lineoligerum is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its long-lasting blooms.
