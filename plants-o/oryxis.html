Oryxis

Oryxis, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant has a thick, fleshy stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in drought conditions. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, adapted to reduce water loss.

Oryxis typically blooms during the rainy season, making it a symbol of resilience in harsh environments.
