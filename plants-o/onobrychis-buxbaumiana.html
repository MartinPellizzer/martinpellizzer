Onobrychis buxbaumiana

Onobrychis buxbaumiana, commonly known as the Buxbaum's goat's rue, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria in its root nodules. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
