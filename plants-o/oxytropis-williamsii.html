Oxytropis williamsii

Oxytropis williamsii, commonly known as Williams' locoweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Oxytropis williamsii is also known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock.
