Oxytropis holdereri

Oxytropis holdereri, commonly known as the Tibetan locoweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in high-altitude regions of the Tibetan Plateau and adjacent areas. The plant is characterized by its slender stems, compound leaves, and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer.
