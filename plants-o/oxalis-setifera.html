Oxalis setifera

Oxalis setifera, commonly known as the slender wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It features trifoliate leaves with slender, thread-like leaflets and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of yellow or white. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. It is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Oxalis setifera is also known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent groundcover in suitable habitats.
