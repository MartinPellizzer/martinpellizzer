Ochroma limonensis

Ochroma limonensis, commonly known as the yellow balsa, is a fast-growing tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its light, porous wood, which is among the lightest in the world. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are small and greenish-yellow, arranged in panicles, and it produces small, round fruits that contain numerous seeds.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its unique wood and ornamental value.
