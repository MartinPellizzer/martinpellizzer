Oenocarpus bolivianus

Oenocarpus bolivianus, commonly known as the Bolivian palm, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellowish flowers followed by elongated, woody fruits. The fruits contain a single large seed surrounded by a fibrous, spongy layer.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in humid, tropical climates.
