Oncostemum vacciniifolium

Oncostemum vacciniifolium, commonly known as the waxleaf oncostemum, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
