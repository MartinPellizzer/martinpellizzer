Ocimum flexuosum

Ocimum flexuosum, commonly known as creeping basil, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that emit a strong mint-like fragrance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or light purple, arranged in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Ocimum flexuosum is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb due to its aromatic properties.
