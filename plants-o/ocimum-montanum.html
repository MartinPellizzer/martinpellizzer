Ocimum montanum

Ocimum montanum, commonly known as mountain basil, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like mint and oregano. The plant is characterized by its slender stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. Ocimum montanum is often cultivated for its fragrant leaves, which are used in traditional medicine and cooking.

It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.
