Oxycoccus quadripetalus f. conicus

Oxycoccus quadripetalus f. conicus, commonly known as the conical oxycoccus, is a variant of the four-petaled oxycoccus.

It is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Arctic and subarctic regions. The plant features small, fleshy, blue-black berries that are rich in antioxidants. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted to withstand cold climates.

The conical form of its fruit is a distinguishing characteristic of this botanical variant.
