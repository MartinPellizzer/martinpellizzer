Oloptum miliaceum

Oloptum miliaceum, commonly known as the common millet, is an annual grass species native to warm temperate regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce numerous tiny grains. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy crop for dry climates. Its seeds are rich in carbohydrates and have been cultivated for thousands of years as a staple food in many parts of the world.

Oloptum miliaceum is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its role in sustainable agriculture.
