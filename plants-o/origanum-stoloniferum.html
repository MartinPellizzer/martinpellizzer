Origanum stoloniferum

Origanum stoloniferum, commonly known as creeping thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces small pink or white flowers in summer, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its creeping growth habit makes it ideal for ground cover in gardens and rocky landscapes.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by square stems and aromatic essential oils.
