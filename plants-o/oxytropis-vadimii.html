Oxytropis vadimii

Oxytropis vadimii, commonly known as the Vadim's locoweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Oxytropis vadimii is known for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil in harsh environments.
