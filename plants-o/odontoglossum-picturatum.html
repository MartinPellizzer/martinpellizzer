Odontoglossum picturatum

Odontoglossum picturatum, commonly known as the painted orchid, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, colorful flowers with intricate patterns. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its strap-like leaves and pendulous inflorescences. Its blooms typically feature a combination of white, yellow, and green hues, with darker markings that resemble paint splatters.

Odontoglossum picturatum is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
