Oxalis macachin

Oxalis macachin, commonly known as the red oxalis, is a species of flowering plant in the family Oxalidaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, delicate flowers. The plant produces bright red or orange flowers that bloom in spring and summer, adding vibrant color to gardens. Oxalis macachin prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a versatile addition to many landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to thrive in a variety of growing conditions.
