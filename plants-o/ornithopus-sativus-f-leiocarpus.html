Ornithopus sativus f. leiocarpus

Ornithopus sativus f. leiocarpus, commonly known as the smooth-seeded field rocket, is a variant of the field rocket plant native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is an annual herbaceous plant characterized by its upright, branching stems and dense clusters of small, yellow flowers. The leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Its seeds are small, smooth, and typically dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation.

This plant is often found in disturbed soils and is valued for its nutritional content and use in forage.
