Oncidium triquetrum

Oncidium triquetrum, commonly known as the Triquetrum orchid, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers with three prominent lobes, giving it its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Oncidium triquetrum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
