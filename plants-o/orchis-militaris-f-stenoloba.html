Orchis militaris f. stenoloba

Orchis militaris f. stenoloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaved soldier's orchid, is a rare and distinctive orchid species native to specific regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and striking purple and white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodland floors or along stream banks. Its flowers are often pollinated by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological significance.

This orchid is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
