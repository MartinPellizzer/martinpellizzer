Ornithogalum crenulatum

Ornithogalum crenulatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, linear leaves and produces clusters of star-shaped, white or pale blue flowers during the spring and early summer. The plant is typically found in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

The flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and moths, contributing to its ecological significance.
