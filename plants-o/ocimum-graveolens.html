Ocimum graveolens

Ocimum graveolens, commonly known as holy basil, is an aromatic herb native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or light purple in color. Its leaves are rich in essential oils, which contribute to its distinctive fragrance and medicinal properties.

Ocimum graveolens is widely used in traditional medicine and culinary applications due to its adaptogenic and antioxidant qualities.
