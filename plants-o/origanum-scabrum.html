Origanum scabrum

Origanum scabrum, commonly known as rough sage, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves and small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant is often found growing in rocky or dry soils, thriving in full sun. Its aromatic foliage contains essential oils, which contribute to its use in traditional herbal medicine.

Origanum scabrum is also valued as a culinary herb, though its strong scent may limit its use in some recipes.
