Oldenlandia cicendioides

Oldenlandia cicendioides, commonly known as the false hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, elliptic leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are showy, with five petals that range in color from white to pink, and they bloom in clusters.

The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds.
