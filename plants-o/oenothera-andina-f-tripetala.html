Oenothera andina f. tripetala

Oenothera andina f. tripetala, commonly known as the Andean evening primrose, is a perennial plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, adapted to the cool, arid conditions of its native habitat. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Botanically, it belongs to the Onagraceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.
