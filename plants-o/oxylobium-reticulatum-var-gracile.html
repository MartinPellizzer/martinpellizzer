Oxylobium reticulatum var. gracile

Oxylobium reticulatum var. gracile, commonly known as the slender net-veined oxylobium, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive reticulate venation pattern. It typically grows in moist, acidic soils along streambanks and in woodland areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species for ecological studies and native plant gardens.
