Ozothamnus leptophyllus

Ozothamnus leptophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf chenopod, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of western Australia.

It features small, narrow leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white pubescence, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. It is well adapted to sandy or gravelly soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Ozothamnus leptophyllus plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for local wildlife.
