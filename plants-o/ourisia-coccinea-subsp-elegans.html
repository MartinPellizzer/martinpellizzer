Ourisia coccinea subsp. elegans

Ourisia coccinea subsp.

elegans, commonly known as the red-flowered ourisia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit.

The plant features bright red, tubular flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, soft pubescence.
