Onobrychis atropatana var. grandiflora

Onobrychis atropatana var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered burr medic, is a perennial legume native to parts of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, spiny pods that enclose its seeds. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in agricultural settings for its forage value.

Due to its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, it is also cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
