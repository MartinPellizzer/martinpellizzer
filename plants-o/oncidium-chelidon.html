Oncidium chelidon

Oncidium chelidon, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers and long, slender stems. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are often clustered in racemes and have a distinctive, fragrant aroma.

Oncidium chelidon requires bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.
