Orobus tomentosus

Orobus tomentosus, commonly known as the woolly orobus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, often appearing in the summer months. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, rounded leaflets, and the plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Orobus tomentosus is often found in woodland and grassland habitats, and it plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
