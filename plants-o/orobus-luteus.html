Orobus luteus

Orobus luteus, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange in color, with intricate patterns that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Orobus luteus is valued not only for its aesthetic appeal but also for its potential medicinal properties.
