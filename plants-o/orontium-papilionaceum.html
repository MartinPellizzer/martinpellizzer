Orontium papilionaceum

Orontium papilionaceum, commonly known as the Indian bean or the wild bean plant, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Orontium papilionaceum is typically found in moist, tropical environments and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
