Opuntia roxburghiana

Opuntia roxburghiana, commonly known as the Indian prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in sharp spines and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces edible fruits known as tunas, which are rich in nutrients. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing remarkable drought tolerance.

Opuntia roxburghiana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in various cultures.
