Oligochaetochilus petrosus

Oligochaetochilus petrosus, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, often fragrant flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows on rocky substrates, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. Its leaves are grass-like, and the inflorescence consists of a single spike bearing several showy, lanceolate flowers.

Oligochaetochilus petrosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
