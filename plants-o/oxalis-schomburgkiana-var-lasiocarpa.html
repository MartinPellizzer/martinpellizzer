Oxalis schomburgkiana var. lasiocarpa

Oxalis schomburgkiana var. lasiocarpa, commonly known as the woolly wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to South America.

It features trifoliate leaves with a velvety texture and small, delicate flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers. Its unique foliage and spring-blooming habit make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

It is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects to the garden.
