Obione peruviana

Obione peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian orchid, is a species native to the high Andes of South America.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at elevations between 3,000 and 5,000 meters. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine layer of white wax.

Obione peruviana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral display.
