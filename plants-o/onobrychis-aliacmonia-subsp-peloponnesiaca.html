Onobrychis aliacmonia subsp. peloponnesiaca

Onobrychis aliacmonia subsp.

peloponnesiaca, commonly known as the Peloponnesian burclover, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It has a deep taproot system, which allows it to access water from deeper soil layers.

This subspecies is often found in open habitats such as shrublands and grasslands.
