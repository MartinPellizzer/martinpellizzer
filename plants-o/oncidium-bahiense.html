Oncidium bahiense

Oncidium bahiense, commonly known as the Bahia orchid, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a distinctive three-lobed lip.

Oncidium bahiense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
