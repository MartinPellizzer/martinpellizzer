Ophrys × carpitana

Ophrys × carpitana, commonly known as the bee orchid hybrid, is a fascinating plant belonging to the Ophrys genus in the Orchidaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera, characterized by its intricate, bee-like floral morphology. The plant produces showy, pseudopapillae-covered flowers that mimic the appearance of female bees to attract male bees for pollination. It thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats across Europe.

This orchid is notable for its specialized pollination mechanism and its role in the study of plant evolution and mimicry.
