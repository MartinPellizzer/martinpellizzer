Oreocereus hempelianus

Oreocereus hempelianus, commonly known as the Easter cactus, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its segmented stems that resemble those of other epiphytic cacti. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink, typically blooming in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and seasonal blooms, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
