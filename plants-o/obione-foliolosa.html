Obione foliolosa

Obione foliolosa, commonly known as the leafy orchid, is a unique epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its intricate, leaf-like structures that resemble foliage, which help it blend into its natural environment. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often blooming during the rainy season. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrates.

Obione foliolosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded, humid environments.
