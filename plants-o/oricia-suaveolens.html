Oricia suaveolens

Oricia suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented orchid, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Oricia suaveolens is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or warm climates.
