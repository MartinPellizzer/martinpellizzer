Oligosporus pedatifidus

Oligosporus pedatifidus, commonly known as the leaf-fringed spore plant, is a rare species characterized by its unique morphology.

It possesses finely divided, pedatifid leaves that give it a feathery appearance, aiding in light capture and water retention. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are clustered along the leaf margins. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as an indicator species for specific microhabitats and its potential ecological importance in fungal spore dispersal.
