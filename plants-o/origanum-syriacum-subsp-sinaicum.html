Origanum syriacum subsp. sinaicum

Origanum syriacum subsp.

sinaicum, commonly known as Syrian oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Middle East and parts of North Africa. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and flowering stems.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are used in culinary and medicinal applications, valued for their pungent, aromatic properties.
