Ononis vulgaris proles caduca

Ononis vulgaris proles caduca, commonly known as the common whitlowgrass, is a low-growing annual herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in loose clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.
