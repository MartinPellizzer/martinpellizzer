Oenothera mollissima subsp. odorata

Oenothera mollissima subsp.

odorata, commonly known as the fragrant evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oval seeds. Its flowers emit a sweet, aromatic scent, which attracts nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinator populations.
