Ostericum albiflorum

Ostericum albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered wintergreen, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas or rocky slopes. Its leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its medicinal properties, including antiseptic and analgesic effects.

The species is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its traditional use in herbal medicine.
