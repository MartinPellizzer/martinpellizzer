Orthaea cordata

Orthaea cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf orthaea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along its slender stems. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale pink flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Orthaea cordata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and flowering display.
