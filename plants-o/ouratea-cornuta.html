Ouratea cornuta

Ouratea cornuta, commonly known as the horned ouratea, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its distinctive, sharply pointed leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by dark blue, waxy berries. Its unique appearance and habitat make it a notable species in botanical studies.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.
