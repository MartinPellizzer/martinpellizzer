Opuntia nashii subsp. gibarensis

Opuntia nashii subsp.

gibarensis, commonly known as the Mexican prickly pear, is a succulent cactus native to Mexico. It features flat, fleshy pads called cladodes, which are often covered in spines and glochids.

The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in summer, followed by edible fruits known as tunas. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-drained soil and full sun.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local cuisine and medicine.
