Oxytropis anertii f. alba

Oxytropis anertii f. alba, commonly known as the white field locoweed, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with stipules and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria.

This species is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.
