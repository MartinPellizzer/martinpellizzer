Ocimum cuanzae

Ocimum cuanzae, commonly known as the Cuanza basil, is a perennial herb native to Central Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like mint and oregano. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant leaves and clusters of white or pale blue flowers. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its essential oils are believed to have antimicrobial and antioxidant properties.
