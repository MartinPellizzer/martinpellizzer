Ophrys aranifera subsp. praecox

Ophrys aranifera subsp.

praecox, commonly known as the Early Bee Orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique floral morphology that mimics the appearance of a female bee, aiding in pollination through mimicry.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brownish in color, with intricate patterns that enhance its deceptive appearance.

It blooms early in the spring, often before other orchid species, hence its common name.
