Oxylobium atropurpureum

Oxylobium atropurpureum, commonly known as the purpleleaf oxylobium, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features slender, upright stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are typically reddish-purple in color, especially during the growing season. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its striking foliage and delicate floral display.
