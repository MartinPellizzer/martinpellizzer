Oxytropis langshanica

Oxytropis langshanica, commonly known as the Langshan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Oxytropis langshanica is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
