Oreocharis aurantiaca

Oreocharis aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking orange-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested habitats and prefers cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, velvety texture.

Oreocharis aurantiaca is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
