Orchis variegata var. submilitaris

Orchis variegata var. submilitaris, commonly known as the variegated orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its flowers are adorned with intricate patterns that aid in pollination.

This species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
