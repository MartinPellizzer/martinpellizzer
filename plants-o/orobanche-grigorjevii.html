Orobanche grigorjevii

Orobanche grigorjevii, commonly known as the Georgian broomrape, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on its host for nutrients.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is characterized by its lack of leaves and its flowering stems that bear small, tubular flowers. This species is typically found in the Caucasus region, where it parasitizes various host plants such as legumes and grasses. Its life cycle involves germinating in response to chemical signals released by its host, allowing it to establish a connection through specialized root structures called haustoria.

Due to its specialized parasitic nature, it plays a unique role in its ecosystem and is of interest to botanists studying plant interactions.
