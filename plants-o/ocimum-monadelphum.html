Ocimum monadelphum

Ocimum monadelphum, commonly known as holy basil or tulsi, is a tropical shrub native to India.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, white or purple flowers. The plant is part of the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and sage. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Ocimum monadelphum thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
