Ophrys × chatenieri

Ophrys × chatenieri, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant that results from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is characterized by its intricate, bee-like flowers that mimic the appearance and scent of female bees, a feature evolved to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is found in the Mediterranean region, particularly in southern Europe. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brown, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

This hybrid species is notable for its genetic diversity and is often studied for its role in plant hybridization and evolutionary biology.
