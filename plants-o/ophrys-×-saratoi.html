Ophrys × saratoi

Ophrys × saratoi, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly found in southern Europe. The plant produces striking flowers that mimic the appearance of female bees, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. Its inflorescence consists of a single spike bearing numerous resupinate flowers, each with a complex structure designed to attract male bees.

The botanical characteristics of Ophrys × saratoi make it a unique example of evolutionary adaptation in the orchid family.
