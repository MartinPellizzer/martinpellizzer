Origanum smyrnaeum

Origanum smyrnaeum, commonly known as Syrian oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens. Its essential oils, rich in thymol and carvacrol, contribute to its strong scent and medicinal properties.

Origanum smyrnaeum is often used in cooking and traditional herbal remedies for its digestive and antimicrobial benefits.
