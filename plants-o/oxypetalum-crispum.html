Oxypetalum crispum

Oxypetalum crispum, commonly known as the crinkled bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant has hairy, crinkled leaves that give it its common name, and it typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils. It is a perennial herb that thrives in cool, moist environments.

Oxypetalum crispum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and blooms.
