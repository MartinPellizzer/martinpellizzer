Oxalis geralensis

Oxalis geralensis, commonly known as the Brazilian wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive veins and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and is often found in shaded, moist environments. It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Oxalis geralensis is also valued for its ability to thrive in partial shade and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
