Ononis lusitanica

Ononis lusitanica, commonly known as the Portuguese sainfoin, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open landscapes and along roadsides.

Ononis lusitanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for ecological restoration.
