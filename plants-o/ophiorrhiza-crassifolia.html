Ophiorrhiza crassifolia

Ophiorrhiza crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved Ophiorrhiza, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, tropical environments. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are thick and leathery, often with a waxy coating. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are arranged in clusters and are followed by small, round fruits.

Ophiorrhiza crassifolia is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in stabilizing coastal and wetland ecosystems.
