Ornithogalum limosum

Ornithogalum limosum, commonly known as the swamp onion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a bulbous stem with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas. It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental appearance and mild, onion-like scent.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.
