Oxalis isosepala

Oxalis isosepala, commonly known as the shieldleaf wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive shield-like appearance, and its flowers are typically yellow with five petals. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds. Oxalis isosepala thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the Oxalidaceae family.
