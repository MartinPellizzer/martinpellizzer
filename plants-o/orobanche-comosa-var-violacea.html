Orobanche comosa var. violacea

Orobanche comosa var. violacea, commonly known as the purple witchweed, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is characterized by its lack of leaves and its showy, often violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is found in various regions across Europe and parts of Asia. Its parasitic nature makes it a significant concern for agricultural crops, as it can reduce yields and weaken host plants.

The species is also notable for its rapid growth and short life cycle, which contributes to its invasive potential in certain ecosystems.
