Orobus × tournefortii

Orobus × tournefortii, commonly known as the hybrid orobus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in dense, terminal clusters, and it produces elongated pods that contain several seeds.

Orobus × tournefortii is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
