Ornithogalum rigidifolium

Ornithogalum rigidifolium, commonly known as the rigid-leaved star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a short, underground bulb and produces a basal rosette of rigid, linear leaves. The plant blooms with umbels of white or pale yellow flowers, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is often grown in gardens across temperate regions.
