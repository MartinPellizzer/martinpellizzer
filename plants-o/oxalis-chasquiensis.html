Oxalis chasquiensis

Oxalis chasquiensis, commonly known as the Andean wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at elevations ranging from 2,000 to 4,000 meters. Its botanical features include bright yellow petals and reddish stems, which help it stand out in its alpine environment.

Oxalis chasquiensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to cool, moist conditions.
