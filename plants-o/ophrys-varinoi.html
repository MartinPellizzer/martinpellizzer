Ophrys varinoi

Ophrys varinoi, commonly known as the Varino's bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee, a feature evolved to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, often in areas with well-drained soil and moderate temperatures. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brown in color, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Ophrys varinoi is a protected species due to its limited distribution and vulnerability to habitat loss.
