Oldenlandia tenerrima

Oldenlandia tenerrima, commonly known as the "silver vine," is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in humid environments.

However, it is also known to be invasive in some regions, outcompeting native vegetation.
