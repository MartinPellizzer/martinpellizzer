Ostryocarpus

Ostryocarpus, commonly known as the sea holly, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Asteraceae.

These plants are typically found in coastal areas and are adapted to salt spray and sandy soils. Ostryocarpus species are characterized by their silver-gray foliage and showy, dandelion-like flowers that bloom in the summer. The plants have a rosette growth habit, with leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

They are often used in ornamental gardening due to their striking appearance and hardy nature.
