Ornithogalum imereticum

Ornithogalum imereticum, commonly known as the Caucasian star-of-Bethlem, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It features a bulbous stem with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of star-shaped white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas, preferring well-drained soils. It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths.
