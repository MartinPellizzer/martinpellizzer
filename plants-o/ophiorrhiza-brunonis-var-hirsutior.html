Ophiorrhiza brunonis var. hirsutior

Ophiorrhiza brunonis var. hirsutior, commonly known as the hairy Ophiorrhiza, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and is often found in coastal regions.

The species is notable for its adaptability to humid, nutrient-poor soils.
