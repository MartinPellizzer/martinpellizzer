Orchis mascula f. fimbriata

Orchis mascula f. fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed male orchid, is a rare and distinctive variant of the male orchid.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a prominent, spurred lip with a fringed appearance. This orchid typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers are usually a mix of purple and white, with a strong, spicy fragrance.

The plant is known for its specialized pollination mechanisms, often attracting specific insect pollinators.
