Oedera calycina subsp. lanceolata

Oedera calycina subsp.

lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceolate thornapple, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa. It is characterized by its succulent, spiny stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped, giving it its subspecies name.

The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
