Oenothera tetragona var. velutina

Oenothera tetragona var. velutina, commonly known as the velvet four-angle evening primrose, is a species native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves and showy yellow flowers that open in the evening. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers have four petals and are pollinated primarily by moths.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
