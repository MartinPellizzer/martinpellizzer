Olea amygdalina

Olea amygdalina, commonly known as the African olive, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the African continent.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that are edible and have a nutty flavor. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid climates, making it a hardy species for tropical and subtropical regions.

Olea amygdalina is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in various cultures.
