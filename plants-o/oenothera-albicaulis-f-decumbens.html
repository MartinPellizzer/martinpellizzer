Oenothera albicaulis f. decumbens

Oenothera albicaulis f. decumbens, commonly known as the decumbent evening primrose, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features slender, sprawling stems that lie close to the ground, often rooting at the nodes. The plant produces showy, white to pale yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variant is notable for its unique growth habit and ecological adaptations to arid environments.
