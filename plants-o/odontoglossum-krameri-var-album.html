Odontoglossum krameri var. album

Odontoglossum krameri var. album, commonly known as the White Orchid of the Andes, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of South America, particularly in the Andes. The plant is characterized by its striking white petals and a distinctive, elongated lip. It typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat.

The flowers are often fragrant and bloom during the cooler months, making it a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
