Oenothera albicaulis f. anthoxantha

Oenothera albicaulis f. anthoxantha, commonly known as the whitestem evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Onagraceae family and is characterized by its showy, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in open, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while its stems are slender and often tinged with red or purple.

The species is noted for its nocturnal pollination, attracting moths and other night-active insects.
