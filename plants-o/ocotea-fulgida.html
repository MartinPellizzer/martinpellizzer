Ocotea fulgida

Ocotea fulgida, commonly known as the golden ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates for its aesthetic and aromatic qualities.
