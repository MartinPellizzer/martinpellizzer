Oxalis nigricans var. diversifolia

Oxalis nigricans var. diversifolia, commonly known as the variegated wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive variegation pattern of green, white, and sometimes pink, giving it an ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically yellow with five petals, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

Oxalis nigricans var. diversifolia is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and easy care requirements.
