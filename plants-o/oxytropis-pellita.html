Oxytropis pellita

Oxytropis pellita, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are typically clustered in racemes. Oxytropis pellita is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native range.
