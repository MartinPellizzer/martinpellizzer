Oxytropis popovii

Oxytropis popovii, commonly known as the Altai locoweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, often found in steppes and mountainous areas. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are compound, with multiple small leaflets, and it has a deep root system that helps it survive in poor soil conditions.

Oxytropis popovii is known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock.
