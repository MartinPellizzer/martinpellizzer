Orobus subvillosus

Orobus subvillosus, commonly known as the hairy orobus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind or animals.

Orobus subvillosus is often found in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
