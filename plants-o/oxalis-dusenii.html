Oxalis dusenii

Oxalis dusenii, commonly known as the Dusen's wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets, often adorned with reddish veins, and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white or pale pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family, characterized by its calcium oxalate crystals in the leaves.

Oxalis dusenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
