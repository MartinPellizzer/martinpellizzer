Oxalis acetosella var. coerulea

Oxalis acetosella var. coerulea, commonly known as the blue wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive blue or purple hue, which are often adorned with white or pale veins. The plant produces clusters of five-petaled, violet or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and has a preference for well-drained, acidic soils.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ability to form dense mats in woodland gardens.
