Olfersia peltata

Olfersia peltata, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is known for its aromatic leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Its fruit, resembling a small, round pod, contains edible seeds that are valued for their nutty flavor.

Olfersia peltata thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental and practical uses.
