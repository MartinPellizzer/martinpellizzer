Ophrys × juanae

Ophrys × juanae, commonly known as the Juanita orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the genus Ophrys, which is characterized by its intricate, often bee-mimicking flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers feature a complex structure with a prominent lip that resembles a female bee, aiding in pollination by male bees.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
