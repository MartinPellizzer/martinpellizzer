Oxalis fluminensis f. pilosella

Oxalis fluminensis f. pilosella, commonly known as the hairy wood sorrel, is a variant of the wood sorrel species native to South America.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its trifoliate leaves with a distinctive hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to both wild and cultivated plant environments.
