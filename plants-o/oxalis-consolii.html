Oxalis consolii

Oxalis consolii, commonly known as the yellow wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive yellow markings and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of yellow and white. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance.

Oxalis consolii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
