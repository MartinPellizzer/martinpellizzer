Obione tridentata

Obione tridentata, commonly known as the three-toothed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, three-lobed flowers that are often clustered in spikelets. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands in suitable habitats.

The plant plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and aquatic life.
