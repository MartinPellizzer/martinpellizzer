Ochna comorensis

Ochna comorensis, commonly known as the Comoro ochna, is a flowering plant native to the Comoros archipelago in the Indian Ocean.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are showy, with bright yellow petals and numerous stamens, making it attractive to pollinators.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
