Oplismenus compositus var. setarius

Oplismenus compositus var. setarius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ironweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, clustered purple flowers in late summer, which are arranged in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
