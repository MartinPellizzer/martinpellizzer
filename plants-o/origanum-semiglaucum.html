Origanum semiglaucum

Origanum semiglaucum, commonly known as Turkish oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, narrow leaves that are slightly hairy and have a silvery-blue underside. The plant produces compact clusters of tiny, pale blue to violet flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic foliage and flowers make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and culinary gardens.
