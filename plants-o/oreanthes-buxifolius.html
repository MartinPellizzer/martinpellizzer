Oreanthes buxifolius

Oreanthes buxifolius, commonly known as the boxleaf oreanthes, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that resembles the leaves of boxwood, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
