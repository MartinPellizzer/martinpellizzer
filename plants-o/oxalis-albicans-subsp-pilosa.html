Oxalis albicans subsp. pilosa

Oxalis albicans subsp.

pilosa, commonly known as the woolly wood sorrel, is a perennial herb native to South Africa. It features small, heart-shaped leaves with a dense covering of fine, white hairs, giving it a woolly appearance.

The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Oxalis albicans subsp.

pilosa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
