Oplonia polyece

Oplonia polyece, commonly known as the golden bush, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, yellowish-green leaves that are opposite in arrangement and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. Oplonia polyece is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
