Oncidium johnsonorum

Oncidium johnsonorum, commonly known as the "Rainbow Orchid," is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescences that often display a mix of red, yellow, and green hues. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers bloom sequentially along a branched stem, creating a dramatic display over several weeks.

Oncidium johnsonorum requires bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.
