Oxytropis kopetdagensis

Oxytropis kopetdagensis, commonly known as Kopetdagan locoweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia, particularly found in the Kopet Dag mountain range.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, spiny stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Its seeds are enclosed in pods and can remain viable in the soil for several years, contributing to its persistence in harsh climates.
