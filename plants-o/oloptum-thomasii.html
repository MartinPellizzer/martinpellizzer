Oloptum thomasii

Oloptum thomasii, commonly known as Thomas's lily, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing white or pale pink petals that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soils with ample sunlight.

Oloptum thomasii is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
