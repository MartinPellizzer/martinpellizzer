Odontochilus pectinatus

Odontochilus pectinatus, commonly known as the peacock orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves and intricate, fragrant flowers that resemble peacock feathers. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial herb with a short, underground rhizome. Its flowers are borne on erect, leafless stems and are known for their vivid colors and unique shapes.

Odontochilus pectinatus prefers shady, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.
