Omphalodes luciliae subsp. kurdica

Omphalodes luciliae subsp.

kurdica, commonly known as the Kurdish bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is adapted to temperate to alpine climates. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
