Ophrys sphegodes var. transhyrcana

Ophrys sphegodes var. transhyrcana, commonly known as the Trans-Hyrkanian bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its unique floral morphology, featuring a pouch-like structure that mimics the appearance of a female bee, which plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, often in mountainous regions with a cool, moist climate. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brown, with intricate patterns that aid in attracting male bees for pollination.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
