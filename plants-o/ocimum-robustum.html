Ocimum robustum

Ocimum robustum, commonly known as wild basil, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs such as mint and sage. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. Ocimum robustum is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its medicinal and culinary uses.

Its essential oils contain compounds like linalool and eugenol, which contribute to its aromatic properties.
