Oenothera luciae-julianiae

Oenothera luciae-julianiae, commonly known as the Julianae-Julianae evening primrose, is a species native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening and close by morning. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to hot, dry climates.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and drought-tolerant gardens.
