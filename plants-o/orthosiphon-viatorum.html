Orthosiphon viatorum

Orthosiphon viatorum, commonly known as the mountain mint, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal properties. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The aerial parts of the plant are typically used in traditional medicine for their diuretic and anti-inflammatory effects.
