Ononis pinnata var. schousboei

Ononis pinnata var. schousboei, commonly known as the Schousboe's bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its slender stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and botanical gardens.
