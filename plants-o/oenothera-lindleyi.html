Oenothera lindleyi

Oenothera lindleyi, commonly known as the evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy yellow or orange flowers that open in the evening, hence its common name. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oily seeds. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Oenothera lindleyi is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
