Oophytum oviforme

Oophytum oviforme, commonly known as the oval eggplant, is a unique species within the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, egg-shaped fruits that are typically green when immature and turn yellow or orange when mature. The plant has a compact growth habit with dark green, ovate leaves that are slightly hairy. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in inflorescences, typical of the nightshade family.

Oophytum oviforme is native to tropical regions and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruits.
