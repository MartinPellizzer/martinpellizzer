Olea javanica var. acuminatissima

Olea javanica var. acuminatissima, commonly known as the Javan olive, is a variant of the olive tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, evergreen leaves that are elliptical and have a pointed tip. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by small, fleshy fruits that turn dark purple when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
