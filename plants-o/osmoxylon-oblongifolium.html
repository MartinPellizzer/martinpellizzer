Osmoxylon oblongifolium

Osmoxylon oblongifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf osmoxylon, is a woody plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, ovate leaves that are often glossy and arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish or yellowish in color. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged appearance.

Osmoxylon oblongifolium is often found in humid, tropical forests where it thrives in shaded, moist environments.
