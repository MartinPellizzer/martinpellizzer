Oplismenus jamaicensis

Oplismenus jamaicensis, commonly known as the Jamaican ironweed, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to the Caribbean.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. Its fibrous roots are known for their durability and have been used in traditional medicine.

Oplismenus jamaicensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
