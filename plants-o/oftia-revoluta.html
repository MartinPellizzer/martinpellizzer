Oftia revoluta

Oftia revoluta, commonly known as the curly-leaf water plantain, is a perennial aquatic plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds, marshes, and slow-moving streams. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often rolled or curled, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually greenish-white in color, and appear in clusters.

Oftia revoluta plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and amphibians.
