Oreopoa anatolica

Oreopoa anatolica, commonly known as the Anatolian rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive floral structure. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with a unique, spurred shape typical of orchids.

Oreopoa anatolica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native region.
