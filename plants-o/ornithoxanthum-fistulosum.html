Ornithoxanthum fistulosum

Ornithoxanthum fistulosum, commonly known as the slender false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble dandelions. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Ornithoxanthum fistulosum is often overlooked due to its modest appearance but plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
