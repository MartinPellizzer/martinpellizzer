Omphalobium simonianum

Omphalobium simonianum, commonly known as the slender field vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, which are typically arranged along the stems. It is often found in grassy areas and has adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Omphalobium simonianum plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitats.
