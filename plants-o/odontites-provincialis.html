Odontites provincialis

Odontites provincialis, commonly known as the Alpine toothed grass, is a small herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, often on rocky slopes and meadows. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes. It is characterized by its finely toothed leaves and its ability to thrive in cold, high-altitude environments.

Odontites provincialis is an important component of alpine vegetation and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
