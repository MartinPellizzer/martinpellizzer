Odontoglossum convallarioides

Odontoglossum convallarioides, commonly known as the silver-veined orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers with a distinct, sweet fragrance. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments at high altitudes. Its flowers are often pollinated by specific species of bees and moths.

Odontoglossum convallarioides is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique appearance.
