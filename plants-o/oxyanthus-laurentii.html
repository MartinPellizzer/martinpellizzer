Oxyanthus laurentii

Oxyanthus laurentii, commonly known as the Laurentian oxyanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of white and yellow. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate to flourish.

Oxyanthus laurentii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
