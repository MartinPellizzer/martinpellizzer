Origanum vulgare var. albiflorum

Origanum vulgare var. albiflorum, commonly known as whiteflower marjoram, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, woody stems and narrow, gray-green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers in summer, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its essential oils, rich in compounds like carvacrol and thymol, contribute to its aromatic and medicinal properties.

It is often used in culinary applications and traditional herbal remedies due to its soothing and antiseptic qualities.
