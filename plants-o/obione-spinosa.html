Obione spinosa

Obione spinosa, commonly known as the spiny obione, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and spiny foliage, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
