Orthocarpus purpurascens var. typicus

Orthocarpus purpurascens var. typicus, commonly known as the red-flowered orthocarpus, is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It features upright, branched stems that are covered in soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces showy, bright red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive aromatic scent.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
