Oldenlandia coronata var. glabrescens

Oldenlandia coronata var. glabrescens, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, star-shaped white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. Its stems are hairy, and the leaves are usually covered with fine, soft hairs.

It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
