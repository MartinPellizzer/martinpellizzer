Oxalis rubroflava

Oxalis rubroflava, commonly known as the red-flowered wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive red or orange coloration, and its flowers are typically pink to red, with five petals. The plant grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, yellowish fruits that contain seeds.

Oxalis rubroflava is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
