Oxytropis gebleri

Oxytropis gebleri, commonly known as Gebler's locoweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. The plant produces spiny, upright stems and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets that are often covered in fine hairs.

Oxytropis gebleri is known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock and wildlife.
