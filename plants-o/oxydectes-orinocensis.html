Oxydectes orinocensis

Oxydectes orinocensis, commonly known as the Orinoco oxydectes, is a unique aquatic plant native to the Orinoco River basin in South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water surface, often forming dense mats. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments with soft, acidic water conditions. Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, typically producing minute seeds that are dispersed by water currents.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
