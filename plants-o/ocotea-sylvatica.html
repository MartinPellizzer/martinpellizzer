Ocotea sylvatica

Ocotea sylvatica, commonly known as the sweet pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into red, berry-like fruits. Its essential oils are often used in perfumery and traditional medicine.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction.
