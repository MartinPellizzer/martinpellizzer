Onoseris cumingii

Onoseris cumingii, commonly known as the Cuming's goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant features upright, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Onoseris cumingii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
