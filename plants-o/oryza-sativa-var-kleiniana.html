Oryza sativa var. kleiniana

Oryza sativa var. kleiniana, commonly known as the dwarf rice variety, is a unique variant of rice characterized by its compact growth habit and shorter stature compared to standard rice plants.

This variety is notable for its genetic diversity and is often studied for its potential in breeding programs aimed at improving rice resilience. It exhibits a range of morphological traits, including smaller leaves and reduced panicle size, which contribute to its distinct appearance. The plant is adapted to various environmental conditions, making it a valuable resource for agricultural research.

Its botanical features make it an important subject in understanding rice genetics and crop improvement.
