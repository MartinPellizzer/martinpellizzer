Ocymastrum sibthorpii

Ocymastrum sibthorpii, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through its succulent leaves and deep root system. It is often found in sandy soils and can thrive in areas with limited water availability.

Ocymastrum sibthorpii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing food for local wildlife.
