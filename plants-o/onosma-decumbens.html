Onosma decumbens

Onosma decumbens, commonly known as the creeping globemallow, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and it produces yellow flowers with five petals. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Its botanical characteristics include a mat-forming growth habit and a long blooming period from spring to early summer.

Onosma decumbens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
