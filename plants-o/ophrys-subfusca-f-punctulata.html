Ophrys subfusca f. punctulata

Ophrys subfusca f. punctulata, commonly known as the spotted bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its unique flower structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the flowers are usually a combination of brown and yellow with distinctive markings.

This orchid is listed as vulnerable due to habitat loss and is often found in specific microhabitats with particular soil and climatic conditions.
