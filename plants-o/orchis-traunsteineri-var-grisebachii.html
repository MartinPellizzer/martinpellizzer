Orchis traunsteineri var. grisebachii

Orchis traunsteineri var. grisebachii, commonly known as the Traunsteiner's orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to specific regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral morphology, featuring a single, showy, spurred orchid flower. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats. Its conservation status is threatened due to habitat loss and limited distribution.

This orchid is of particular interest to botanists due to its distinctive genetic and ecological traits.
