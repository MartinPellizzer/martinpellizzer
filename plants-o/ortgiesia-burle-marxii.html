Ortgiesia burle-marxii

Ortgiesia burle-marxii, commonly known as the Burle-Marx orchid, is a striking plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, yellow, and orange. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Ortgiesia burle-marxii is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained, humid conditions.
