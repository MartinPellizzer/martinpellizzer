Oxylobium cuneatum var. dilatatum

Oxylobium cuneatum var. dilatatum, commonly known as the broad-leafed oxylobium, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its botanical features include hairy stems and pods that split open to release seeds.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
