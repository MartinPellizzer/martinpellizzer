Oxalis umbraticola var. macrophylla

Oxalis umbraticola var. macrophylla, commonly known as the giant forest oxalis, is a striking plant native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 15 cm in diameter, giving it a dramatic appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in shady, humid environments and requires well-draining, acidic soil.

Due to its unique foliage and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
