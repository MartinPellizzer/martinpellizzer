Orchis laxiflora subsp. elegans

Orchis laxiflora subsp.

elegans, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and showy, spurred flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination by insects.

The subspecies elegans is distinguished by its more elongated floral structure and slightly different leaf morphology compared to other subspecies of *Orchis laxiflora*.
