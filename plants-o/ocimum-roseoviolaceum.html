Ocimum roseoviolaceum

Ocimum roseoviolaceum, commonly known as the purple viola basil, is a species of flowering plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers. The plant has aromatic leaves that emit a strong, spicy scent, often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

Ocimum roseoviolaceum thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
