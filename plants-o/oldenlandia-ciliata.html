Oldenlandia ciliata

Oldenlandia ciliata, commonly known as the thread-leaf mistflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves and small, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its stems are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance.

Oldenlandia ciliata is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can thrive in partial shade with well-drained soil.
