Orbeopsis matabelensis

Orbeopsis matabelensis, commonly known as the Matabelensis orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a terrestrial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in woodland or savanna habitats. The plant produces striking, showy flowers with a distinctive coloration, usually in shades of yellow and orange. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
