Onobrychis cana f. zederbaueri

Onobrychis cana f. zederbaueri, commonly known as the Zederbauer's vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive pale green color and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant grows in open, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and dry grasslands. It has a deep root system that helps it thrive in poor soils.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
