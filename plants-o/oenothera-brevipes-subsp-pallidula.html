Oenothera brevipes subsp. pallidula

Oenothera brevipes subsp.

pallidula, commonly known as the pallid-flowered evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening.

The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting nocturnal pollinators.
