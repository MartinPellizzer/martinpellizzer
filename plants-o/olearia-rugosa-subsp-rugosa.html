Olearia rugosa subsp. rugosa

Olearia rugosa subsp.

rugosa, commonly known as the rough-leaved daisy, is a shrub native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage with a rough, hairy texture.

The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of white and yellow, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes. Olearia rugosa subsp.

rugosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
