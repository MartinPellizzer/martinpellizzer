Oxalis tropaeoloides

Oxalis tropaeoloides, commonly known as the false nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial herb with heart-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow-orange flowers that resemble those of the nasturtium. The plant belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive leaf structure and vibrant floral display. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Oxalis tropaeoloides is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
