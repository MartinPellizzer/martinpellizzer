Othonna arbuscula

Othonna arbuscula, commonly known as the woolly ox-tongue, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that covers its short, branching stems. The leaves are typically gray-green in color and are covered in fine, soft hairs. It produces yellow flowers in clusters during the warmer months, adding ornamental value to the plant.

Othonna arbuscula is well adapted to arid conditions and requires minimal water once established.
