Oenothera communis var. hookeri

Oenothera communis var. hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats like prairies and rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are slender and branching.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed environments and is often found in sandy or gravelly soils.
