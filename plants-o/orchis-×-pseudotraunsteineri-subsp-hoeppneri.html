Orchis × pseudotraunsteineri subsp. hoeppneri

Orchis × pseudotraunsteineri subsp.

hoeppneri, commonly known as the Hoeppner's orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Central Europe. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodlands and meadows. It has lance-shaped leaves and a complex inflorescence with numerous flowers arranged in a loose spike.

Due to its hybrid origin, it exhibits a range of morphological variations, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
