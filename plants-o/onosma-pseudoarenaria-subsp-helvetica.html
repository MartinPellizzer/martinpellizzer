Onosma pseudoarenaria subsp. helvetica

Onosma pseudoarenaria subsp.

helvetica, commonly known as the Alpine onosma, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, exhibiting a compact growth habit with narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, arranged in dense spikes. It is well adapted to cold climates and has a deep root system to access moisture in arid conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
