Ornithopus sativus nothosubsp. macrorrhynchus

Ornithopus sativus nothosubsp.

macrorrhynchus, commonly known as the giant flax or tall flax, is a cultivated variant of the flax genus native to Europe. It is characterized by its tall, erect stature and distinctive blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant produces long, slender seed pods that contain small, oval seeds, which are used in various agricultural and industrial applications. Its fibrous stalks are valued for their strength and are used in textile production.

This nothosubspecies is often grown for both ornamental and practical purposes due to its hardy nature and adaptability to diverse soil conditions.
