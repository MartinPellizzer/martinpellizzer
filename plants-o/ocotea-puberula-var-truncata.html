Ocotea puberula var. truncata

Ocotea puberula var. truncata, commonly known as the truncated ocotea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in countries like Brazil and Mexico. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are typically elliptical and have a smooth, waxy texture. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually white or pale yellow and are arranged in clusters.

The fruit is a small, dark blue drupe that contains a single seed, contributing to its ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
