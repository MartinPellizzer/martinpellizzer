Ovieda bracteata

Ovieda bracteata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Ovieda bracteata is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread aggressively, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
