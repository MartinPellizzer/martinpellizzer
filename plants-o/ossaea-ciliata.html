Ossaea ciliata

Ossaea ciliata, commonly known as the silky grass or silky sandmat, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically forms dense mats on sandy or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Ossaea ciliata is adapted to arid environments and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature.
