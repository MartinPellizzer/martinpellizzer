Onopordum illyricum var. cardunculus

Onopordum illyricum var. cardunculus, commonly known as the cardoon, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, hairy, and often lobed leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, flat-topped clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, also known as the sunflower family.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used historically for its edible leaves and stems.
