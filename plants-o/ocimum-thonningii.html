Ocimum thonningii

Ocimum thonningii, commonly known as the East African basil, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and sage. The plant is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny, pale purple to white flowers. Ocimum thonningii is known for its strong, spicy aroma and is often used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
