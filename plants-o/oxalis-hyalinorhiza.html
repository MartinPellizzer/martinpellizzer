Oxalis hyalinorhiza

Oxalis hyalinorhiza, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a strikingly beautiful plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its translucent, waxy leaves that resemble ghostly apparitions, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Oxalis hyalinorhiza is a perennial herb that is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for gardeners interested in native plants.
