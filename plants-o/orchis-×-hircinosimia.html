Orchis × hircinosimia

Orchis × hircinosimia, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Orchis mascula and Orchis morio.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet or purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland habitats across Europe. Its flowers have a distinctive lip that is often marked with darker pigmentation.

This hybrid is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique genetic makeup and ornamental value.
