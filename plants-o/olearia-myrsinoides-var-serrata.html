Olearia myrsinoides var. serrata

Olearia myrsinoides var. serrata, commonly known as the serrated daisy bush, is a species native to New Zealand.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, white, daisy-like flowers. The leaves are typically serrated, giving the plant its specific variety name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native pollinators.
