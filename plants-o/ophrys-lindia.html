Ophrys lindia

Ophrys lindia, commonly known as the Lind's bee orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its intricate, bee-like flowers that feature a dark brown lip resembling a bee's face, which plays a key role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, often in alpine or subalpine environments. Its flowers are designed to mimic the appearance and scent of female bees, attracting male bees for pollination.

Ophrys lindia is a protected species due to its limited distribution and vulnerability to habitat loss.
