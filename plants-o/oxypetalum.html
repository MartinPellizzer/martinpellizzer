Oxypetalum

Oxypetalum, commonly known as the Christmas rose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking white or pink flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring, often around Christmas time, hence its common name. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers with five petals that are usually larger than the sepals. Oxypetalum has a compact growth habit and is often grown as an ornamental plant in gardens due to its unique blooming period and attractive foliage.

It prefers cool, moist environments and is relatively low maintenance once established.
