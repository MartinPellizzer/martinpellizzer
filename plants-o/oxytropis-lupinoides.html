Oxytropis lupinoides

Oxytropis lupinoides, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America, particularly in the western United States. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces yellow to orange pea-like flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Oxytropis lupinoides is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
