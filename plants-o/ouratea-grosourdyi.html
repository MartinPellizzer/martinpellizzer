Ouratea grosourdyi

Ouratea grosourdyi, commonly known as the Brazilian beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for some bird species. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a glossy texture, while its bark is smooth and pale.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
