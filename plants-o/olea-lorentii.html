Olea lorentii

Olea lorentii, commonly known as the olive tree, is a evergreen tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silver-green, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in spring. The tree produces drupes, or olives, which are the fruit used for oil production and consumption. Olea lorentii is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in poor soils.

Its botanical features make it both economically and ecologically significant.
