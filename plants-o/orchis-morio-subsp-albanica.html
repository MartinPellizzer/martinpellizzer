Orchis morio subsp. albanica

Orchis morio subsp.

albanica, commonly known as the Albanian early purple orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Balkan Peninsula. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodlands and grasslands.

The plant produces a single, erect stem bearing a cluster of violet to purple flowers with a distinctive lip. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.

It is a protected species in many regions due to habitat loss and over-collection.
