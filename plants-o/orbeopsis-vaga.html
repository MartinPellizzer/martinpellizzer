Orbeopsis vaga

Orbeopsis vaga, commonly known as the wandering orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pendulous flowers that often hang from the stems. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial orchid with slender, branched stems and small, greenish-white or yellowish flowers. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the humid environment.

Orbeopsis vaga is often found in shaded, moist areas such as forest understories and is valued for its ornamental beauty.
