Orbignya longibracteata

Orbignya longibracteata, commonly known as the Brazil nut tree, is a large evergreen tree native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and compound leaves with long, lance-shaped leaflets. The tree produces large, woody pods that contain multiple seeds, which are the edible Brazil nuts. It thrives in tropical rainforest environments and plays a crucial role in the ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

The plant is also economically significant due to its valuable nut crop.
