Orthophytum harleyi

Orthophytum harleyi, commonly known as the Harley's orthophytum, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, specifically Brazil.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of trichomes. The plant produces striking, showy inflorescences that can reach several feet in height, featuring vibrant red and orange bracts. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a popular specimen among bromeliad enthusiasts and botanists.
