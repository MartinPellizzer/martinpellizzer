Oxytropis michelsonii

Oxytropis michelsonii, commonly known as Michelson's locoweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, spindly growth habit. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically purple or pink in color. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Oxytropis michelsonii is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.
