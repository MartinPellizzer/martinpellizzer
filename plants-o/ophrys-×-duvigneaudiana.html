Ophrys × duvigneaudiana

Ophrys × duvigneaudiana, commonly known as the Duvigneaud's bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its intricate, bee-like floral structure, which features a prominent lip resembling a bee's face. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing a striking display of yellow and brown hues.

This species is valued for its unique morphology and is often cultivated for its ornamental appeal.
