Onobrychis viciifolia subsp. altissima

Onobrychis viciifolia subsp.

altissima, commonly known as the tall sainfoin, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its roots have a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, making it valuable for soil enrichment.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in forage and erosion control.
