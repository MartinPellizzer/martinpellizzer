Ocotea dissimilis

Ocotea dissimilis, commonly known as the Brazilian sandarac tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its dark, aromatic wood, which is highly valued for its durability and scent. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a glossy surface.

Ocotea dissimilis plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
