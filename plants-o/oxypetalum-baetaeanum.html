Oxypetalum baetaeanum

Oxypetalum baetaeanum, commonly known as the Baetaean rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and rocky habitats, characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in spring and early summer. Its botanical features include simple, elliptical leaves and a compact growth habit, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a decorative hedge.

Oxypetalum baetaeanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to Mediterranean climates.
