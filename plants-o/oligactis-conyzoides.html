Oligactis conyzoides

Oligactis conyzoides, commonly known as the African blueweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, blue to purple in color, and are arranged in dense clusters, making it visually distinctive.

Oligactis conyzoides is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
