Oncidium albinoi

Oncidium albinoi, commonly known as the White Oncidium, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, arching inflorescence that bears numerous white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding environment.

Oncidium albinoi is valued for its elegant appearance and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
