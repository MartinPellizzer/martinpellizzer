Orphanidesia

Orphanidesia, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its lack of leaves, relying solely on its roots for nutrient absorption. The plant produces striking, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the summer months. Orphanidesia thrives in wet, swampy habitats and is often found growing on the trunks of trees.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species.
