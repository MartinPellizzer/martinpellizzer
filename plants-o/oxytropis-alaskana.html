Oxytropis alaskana

Oxytropis alaskana, commonly known as Alaska bur pea, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically purple or pink in color. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Oxytropis alaskana is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to nutrient cycling in its ecosystem.
