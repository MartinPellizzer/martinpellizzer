Oxytropis daqingshanica

Oxytropis daqingshanica, commonly known as the Daqingshan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the grasslands of northern China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Oxytropis daqingshanica plays a role in stabilizing soil and is of interest for its potential in ecological restoration projects.
