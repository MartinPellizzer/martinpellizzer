Oryza sativa var. utilissima

Oryza sativa var. utilissima, commonly known as the wild rice, is a grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated grains and grows in aquatic or semi-aquatic environments. The plant typically reaches heights of 1 to 3 meters and has narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces seeds that are often used in traditional cuisines.

The botanical features of this variety make it an important species for both ecological and agricultural studies.
