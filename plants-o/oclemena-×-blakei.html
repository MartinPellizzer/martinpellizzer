Oclemena × blakei

Oclemena × blakei, commonly known as the hybrid hibiscus, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Oclemena acuminata and Oclemena tetragona.

It is a shrub native to the Caribbean, particularly found in the Lesser Antilles. The plant features showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically blooming during the wet season. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate, with a rough texture and a strong, aromatic scent.

Oclemena × blakei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
