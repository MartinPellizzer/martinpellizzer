Oxalis carnosa

Oxalis carnosa, commonly known as the woolly wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, succulent leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is known for its ability to store water in its fleshy leaves, making it drought-tolerant. Oxalis carnosa typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
