Ocotea itapirensis

Ocotea itapirensis, commonly known as Itapire rosewood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon Basin.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark-colored wood. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within the dense rainforest canopy. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a glossy surface.

The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by berries, contributing to its ecological importance in the region.
