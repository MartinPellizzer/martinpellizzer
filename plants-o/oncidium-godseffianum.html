Oncidium godseffianum

Oncidium godseffianum, commonly known as the Godseff's orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, pendant-like inflorescences that bear clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green to white flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrate.

Oncidium godseffianum is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its elegant appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
