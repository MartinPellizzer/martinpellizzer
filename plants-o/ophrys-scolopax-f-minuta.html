Ophrys scolopax f. minuta

Ophrys scolopax f. minuta, commonly known as the small bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as woodlands and grasslands. Its flowers are small and have a distinctive coloration with a dark brown to blackish hue.

The species is known for its intricate pollination mechanism and is considered a valuable subject in the study of plant-animal interactions.
