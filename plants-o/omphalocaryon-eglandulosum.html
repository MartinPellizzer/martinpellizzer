Omphalocaryon eglandulosum

Omphalocaryon eglandulosum, commonly known as the smooth globular cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rounded, globular shape and smooth, spineless surface. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is well adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited water availability. Its flowers, which are usually yellow or orange, bloom during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its otherwise muted appearance.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations, it is a subject of interest in both botanical research and horticultural cultivation.
