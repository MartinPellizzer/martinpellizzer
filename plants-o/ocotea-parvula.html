Ocotea parvula

Ocotea parvula, commonly known as the small-leaved ocotea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in countries like Mexico and Brazil. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are typically small and elliptical in shape. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale green, followed by dark blue berries.

Ocotea parvula is often found in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests.
