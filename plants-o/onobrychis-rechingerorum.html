Onobrychis rechingerorum

Onobrychis rechingerorum, commonly known as the Rechinger's vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, often found in Mediterranean climates.

Its root system helps in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.
