Ocimum dambicola

Ocimum dambicola, commonly known as Dambicola basil, is a species of flowering plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its aromatic, slightly minty scent. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue in color. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinctive, spicy fragrance.

Ocimum dambicola is valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
