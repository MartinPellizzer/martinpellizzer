Oxypetalum cipoense

Oxypetalum cipoense, commonly known as the Cipo rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with slender, woody stems and alternate, simple leaves. Its flowers are fragrant and attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Oxypetalum cipoense is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
