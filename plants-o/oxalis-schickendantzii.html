Oxalis schickendantzii

Oxalis schickendantzii, commonly known as the Schickendantzii wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, delicate yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive seed pods that split open to release seeds, a feature common in many members of its family. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
