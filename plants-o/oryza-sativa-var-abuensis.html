Oryza sativa var. abuensis

Oryza sativa var. abuensis, commonly known as Abuensis rice, is a rare and ancient variety of rice native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small grain size and unique genetic makeup, which distinguishes it from other rice species. This variety is notable for its high genetic diversity, which makes it valuable for breeding programs aimed at improving rice resilience. Its botanical features include a short plant stature, slender leaves, and a preference for wetland environments.

Due to its limited distribution and endangered status, conservation efforts are crucial for preserving this unique plant.
