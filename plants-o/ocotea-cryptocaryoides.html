Ocotea cryptocaryoides

Ocotea cryptocaryoides, commonly known as the black pepper tree, is a member of the Lauraceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant bark. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by dark purple berries, which contain the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement, stipules, and a unique fruit structure that aids in seed dispersal.

Ocotea cryptocaryoides is valued for its ecological role and medicinal properties in its native habitats.
