Ocimum paludosum

Ocimum paludosum, commonly known as swamp basil, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white or pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in wet, marshy habitats and has a fibrous root system adapted to waterlogged soils. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Ocimum paludosum is also valued as a ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and fragrant properties.
