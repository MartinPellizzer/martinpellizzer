Orchis × morioides

Orchis × morioides, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a naturally occurring cross between Orchis morio and Orchis moraea.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features tall, erect stems with whorled leaves and produces showy, violet or purple flowers in late spring. Its inflorescence consists of a dense spike of flowers, often with a mix of different morphological traits from its parent species.

This hybrid is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its beauty.
