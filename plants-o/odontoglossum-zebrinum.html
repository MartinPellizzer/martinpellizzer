Odontoglossum zebrinum

Odontoglossum zebrinum, commonly known as the zigzag orchid, is a species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its striking, zigzag-shaped flower spikes that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with intricate patterns. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, forming a rosette at the base of the plant.

Odontoglossum zebrinum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow under the right conditions.
