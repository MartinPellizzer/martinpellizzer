Orchis blephariglottis

Orchis blephariglottis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces tall, slender flowering stems. Its flowers are usually pale purple or white, with a distinctive hooded structure typical of orchids.

The species is noted for its specialized pollination mechanisms and is often found in nutrient-poor soils.
