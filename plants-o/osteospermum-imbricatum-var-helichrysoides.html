Osteospermum imbricatum var. helichrysoides

Osteospermum imbricatum var. helichrysoides, commonly known as the golden osteospermum, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers with a distinctive central disk. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 30 cm. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while the stems are upright and often branched.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
