Onobrychis crista-galli f. ligulifera

Onobrychis crista-galli f. ligulifera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved goat's rue, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant is native to southern Europe and is often found in dry, rocky soils. It has a creeping growth habit and produces pods that contain small, rounded seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
