Otholobium stachyerum

Otholobium stachyerum, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in cylindrical spikes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils.

Otholobium stachyerum is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
