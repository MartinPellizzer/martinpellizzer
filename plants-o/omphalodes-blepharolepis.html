Omphalodes blepharolepis

Omphalodes blepharolepis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features slender, branching stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, with a distinctive narrow, pointed shape. Its leaves are narrow and lanceolate, often with a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
