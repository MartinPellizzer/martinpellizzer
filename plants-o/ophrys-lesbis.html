Ophrys lesbis

Ophrys lesbis, commonly known as the Lesbia orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its intricate, bee-like flower structure, which plays a key role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its flowers are usually a mix of brown and yellow, with a distinctive pouch-like structure at the base.

Ophrys lesbis is noted for its specialized relationship with certain bee species, which it mimics to ensure successful pollination.
