Oxytropis middendorffii subsp. jarovoji

Oxytropis middendorffii subsp.

jarovoji, commonly known as the Jarovojic oxytropis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the steppes of Central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

It is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native environment.
