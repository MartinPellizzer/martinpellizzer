Oldenlandia umbrosa

Oldenlandia umbrosa, commonly known as the shadowplant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and slightly hairy, with a distinctive waxy texture. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
