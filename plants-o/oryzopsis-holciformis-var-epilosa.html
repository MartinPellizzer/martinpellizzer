Oryzopsis holciformis var. epilosa

Oryzopsis holciformis var. epilosa, commonly known as the eastern grass or tall oatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters, and its leaves are narrow with a prominent midrib. Its inflorescence consists of a terminal panicle, which is often branched and produces small, spikelet-like flowers.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
