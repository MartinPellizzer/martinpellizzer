Orchis purpurea var. amediastina

Orchis purpurea var. amediastina, commonly known as the purple orchid, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodlands or grassy meadows. Its flowers have a distinctive structure with three sepals and three petals, one of which is showy and spurred.

The orchid is also noted for its ability to form mycorrhizal associations with fungi, aiding in nutrient uptake.
