Odontoglossum crispum var. flaveolum

Odontoglossum crispum var. flaveolum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, golden-yellow flowers with a distinctive, crinkled lip. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial orchid, often found in humid, shaded environments at high altitudes. Its pseudobulbs are short and thick, supporting its evergreen foliage.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated for its unique floral display.
