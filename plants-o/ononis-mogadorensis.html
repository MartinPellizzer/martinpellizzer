Ononis mogadorensis

Ononis mogadorensis, commonly known as the Canary Island bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from spring to summer. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and often hairy, adapting to the arid conditions of its habitat.

Ononis mogadorensis is typically found in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in sunny, well-drained environments.
