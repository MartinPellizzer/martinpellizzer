Ophrys morrisonicola

Ophrys morrisonicola, commonly known as the Morrison's bee orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its intricate, bee-like flower structure, which features a vividly colored lip that mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats with well-drained soils, often in alpine or subalpine zones. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing a unique blend of visual and chemical cues to enhance pollination success.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Ophrys morrisonicola is considered a species of conservation concern.
