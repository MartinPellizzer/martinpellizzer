Orontium arvense

Orontium arvense, commonly known as the field watercress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating in shallow water. Its small white or pink flowers bloom in clusters, and it produces small, round pods containing several seeds.

Orontium arvense is typically found in wetlands, ditches, and along the edges of ponds and streams.
