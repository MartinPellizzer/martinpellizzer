Ochna thomasiana

Ochna thomasiana, commonly known as the Thomas's ochna, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, followed by distinctive, woody, spherical fruits. Ochna thomasiana is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Its ornamental value and ecological significance make it an important species in its native ecosystems.
