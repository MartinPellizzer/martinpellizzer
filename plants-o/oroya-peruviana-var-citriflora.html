Oroya peruviana var. citriflora

Oroya peruviana var. citriflora, commonly known as the citron tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into citrus-like fruits. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its fruits are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
