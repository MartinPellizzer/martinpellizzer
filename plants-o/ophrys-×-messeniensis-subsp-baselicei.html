Ophrys × messeniensis subsp. baselicei

Ophrys × messeniensis subsp.

baselicei, commonly known as the Baselicei Ophrys, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its striking, bee-mimicking flowers that play a crucial role in its pollination strategy.

The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats. Its floral structure includes a complex labellum that mimics the appearance and scent of female bees.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique hybrid origins and ecological adaptations.
