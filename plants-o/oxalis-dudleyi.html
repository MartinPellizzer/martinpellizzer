Oxalis dudleyi

Oxalis dudleyi, commonly known as Dudley's wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches a height of about 6 inches, with trifoliate leaves resembling those of true wood sorrel. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, often with a distinctive yellow center. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Oxalis dudleyi is often found in woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
