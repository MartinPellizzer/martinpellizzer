Oenothera bertolonii

Oenothera bertolonii, commonly known as the Bertoloni's evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening and close by morning. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces. Its flowers are typically borne in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Oenothera bertolonii is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils.
