Orobanche alba var. longibracteata

Orobanche alba var. longibracteata, commonly known as the white broomweed, is a parasitic plant belonging to the Orobanchaceae family.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients, typically attaching to the roots of other species. The plant is characterized by its pale yellow to white flowers, which bloom in late summer. Its stems are often branched and bear small, lance-shaped leaves.

This variety is native to Europe and parts of Asia, where it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.
