Oenothera biennis subsp. baurii

Oenothera biennis subsp.

baurii, commonly known as the Baur's evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

The seeds are enclosed in a capsule that splits open to release the seeds when mature.
