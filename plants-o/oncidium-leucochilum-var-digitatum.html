Oncidium leucochilum var. digitatum

Oncidium leucochilum var. digitatum, commonly known as the white-lipped orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a sympodial growth habit, producing pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant features showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and white, often with a contrasting lip. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, typically arranged in two rows along the stem.

It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
