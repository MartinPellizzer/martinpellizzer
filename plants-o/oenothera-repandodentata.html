Oenothera repandodentata

Oenothera repandodentata, commonly known as the plains evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the central and western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive toothed margin, contributing to its common name.

Oenothera repandodentata is also valued for its medicinal properties, with its seeds used in traditional remedies.
