Oenothera volkmannii

Oenothera volkmannii, commonly known as the evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening, hence its common name. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oval seeds. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Oenothera volkmannii is adapted to arid environments and is often found in disturbed soils and dry habitats.
