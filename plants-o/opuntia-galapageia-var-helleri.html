Opuntia galapageia var. helleri

Opuntia galapageia var. helleri, commonly known as the Heller's prickly pear, is a cacti species native to the Galápagos Islands.

It is characterized by its flattened, paddle-shaped stems that are covered in spines and glochids. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the dry season, followed by edible, red-fleshed fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting well to the unique climate of the Galápagos.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its resilience to harsh conditions.
