Orchis maculata var. nesogenes

Orchis maculata var. nesogenes, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking purple and white flowers with distinctive spots. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodlands and meadows. It has a tall, slender stem with a single spike of flowers, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a waxy coating.

This variety is often found in shaded areas and is known for its ecological importance as a food source for certain pollinators.
