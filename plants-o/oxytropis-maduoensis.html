Oxytropis maduoensis

Oxytropis maduoensis, commonly known as the Maduo locoweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, compound leaves. The plant produces yellow or pale green flowers in racemes, which bloom in the summer months. Oxytropis maduoensis is adapted to harsh, dry environments and is often found in steppes and semi-deserts.

Its seeds are encased in a leathery pod, and the plant is known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock.
