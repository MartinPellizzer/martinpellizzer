Orchis × argentarii

Orchis × argentarii, commonly known as the silver-root orchid, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Orchis morio and Orchis mascula.

It is characterized by its striking silver-white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial shade. Its roots are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.

The orchid's botanical significance lies in its role as a hybrid species that showcases the genetic diversity within the Orchidaceae family.
