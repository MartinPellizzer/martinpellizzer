Omphalobium indicum

Omphalobium indicum, commonly known as the Indian sowing sickle, is a perennial herb native to South Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces cylindrical, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils, and it has a shallow root system.

Omphalobium indicum is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable in soil enrichment.
