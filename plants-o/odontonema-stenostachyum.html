Odontonema stenostachyum

Odontonema stenostachyum, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed flame vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a fleshy, sticky coating. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its vibrant floral display.
