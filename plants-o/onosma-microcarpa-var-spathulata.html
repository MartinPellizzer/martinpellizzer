Onosma microcarpa var. spathulata

Onosma microcarpa var. spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leaved onosma, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, often hairy, gray-green leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or arid garden environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to harsh climatic conditions.
