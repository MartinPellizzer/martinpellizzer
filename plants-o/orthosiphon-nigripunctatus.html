Orthosiphon nigripunctatus

Orthosiphon nigripunctatus, commonly known as the black-spotted horsegram, is a member of the family Lamiaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, branched stems and opposite leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It is native to tropical regions of Asia and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The leaves and flowers are used in traditional medicine for their purported diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.
