Oxyanthus lepidus subsp. lepidus

Oxyanthus lepidus subsp.

lepidus, commonly known as the silky oxyanthus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology and floral structure compared to other members of the genus.
