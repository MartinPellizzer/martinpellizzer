Osmunda bromeliifolia

Osmunda bromeliifolia, commonly known as the cinnamon fern, is a species of fern that belongs to the Osmundaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, dark brown, hairy fronds that resemble a cinnamon stick, hence its common name. The plant has a short, thick rhizome that supports its upright, spore-bearing fronds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Osmunda bromeliifolia plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
