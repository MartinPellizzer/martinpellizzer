Origanum maru var. sinaicum

Origanum maru var. sinaicum, commonly known as Sinaitic oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its essential oils contain compounds like carvacrol and thymol, which contribute to its strong aroma and medicinal properties.

This variety is valued for both its culinary uses and its role in traditional herbal medicine.
