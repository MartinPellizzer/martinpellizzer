Opuntia nicholii

Opuntia nicholii, commonly known as the Mexican prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems that are covered in spines and are often ribbed. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tuna, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

Opuntia nicholii is well adapted to arid environments and plays an important role in desert ecosystems.
