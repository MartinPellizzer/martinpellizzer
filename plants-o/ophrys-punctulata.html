Ophrys punctulata

Ophrys punctulata, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features a rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces a single, showy flower with a distinctive lip that mimics the appearance of a female bee, a trait used in its unique pollination strategy. It thrives in calcareous soils and is typically found in grasslands, woodland edges, and open habitats.

The spotted orchid is a protected species in many regions due to its ecological significance and declining populations.
