Oenothera albicaulis var. pallida

Oenothera albicaulis var. pallida, commonly known as the pallid evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its pale yellow to white flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in prairie and steppe habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and native plant gardens.
