Oryza officinalis

Oryza officinalis, commonly known as wild rice, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and shallow lakes, and is adapted to waterlogged conditions. Its seeds are often used in culinary and traditional medicinal practices.

Oryza officinalis plays an important ecological role in supporting aquatic ecosystems and is also valued for its nutritional content.
