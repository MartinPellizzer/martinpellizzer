Osteomeles anthyllidifolia var. anthyllidifolia

Osteomeles anthyllidifolia var. anthyllidifolia, commonly known as the Hawaiian hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically blooming in summer. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are woody and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and cultural significance in Hawaiian horticulture.
