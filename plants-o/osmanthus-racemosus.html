Osmanthus racemosus

Osmanthus racemosus, commonly known as the Chinese sweet osmanthus, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the autumn season. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy on top. Its flowers are widely used in traditional Chinese medicine and as a flavoring agent in teas and confections.

Osmanthus racemosus thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
