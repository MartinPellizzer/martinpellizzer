Oncidium × stanleyi

Oncidium × stanleyi, commonly known as the Stanley's orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that often display shades of yellow, orange, and red, arranged in a spiky inflorescence. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its leaves are long, strap-like, and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Oncidium × stanleyi is prized for its ornamental value and is widely cultivated for both indoor and outdoor gardens.
