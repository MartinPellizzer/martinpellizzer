Ophiorrhiza erubescens

Ophiorrhiza erubescens, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a tropical plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, often pendulous inflorescences and showy, red or pink flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are usually opposite and have a leathery texture, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

Ophiorrhiza erubescens plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for various insect species.
