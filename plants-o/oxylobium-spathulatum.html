Oxylobium spathulatum

Oxylobium spathulatum, commonly known as the broadleaf oxylobium, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in swampy areas or along stream banks.
