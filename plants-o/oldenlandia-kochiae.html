Oldenlandia kochiae

Oldenlandia kochiae, commonly known as the silverleaf false musk vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, creeping habit and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and subtle blooms.
