Ophrys × minaventoi

Ophrys × minaventoi, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant that results from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is characterized by its intricate, bee-like floral structures that mimic the appearance and scent of female bees, a trait evolved to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands and along stream banks. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brownish in color, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

This hybrid species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological significance in supporting pollinator interactions.
