Oroya subocculta var. albispina

Oroya subocculta var. albispina, commonly known as the white-spined oroya, is a member of the Rubiaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, opposite leaves. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape and have a glossy, dark green upper surface. Its flowers are small, clustered in cymes, and usually white or pale pink, with four stamens.

The plant is native to South America and is often found in shaded, moist forest understories.
