Ocotea grisebachiana

Ocotea grisebachiana, commonly known as the gray ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The tree typically grows in humid, shaded environments within lowland and montane forests. Its bark is often covered in a distinctive grayish or brownish lichen, contributing to its common name.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
