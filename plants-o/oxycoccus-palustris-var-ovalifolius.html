Oxycoccus palustris var. ovalifolius

Oxycoccus palustris var. ovalifolius, commonly known as the oval-leaved cranberry, is a variant of the bog cranberry native to northern regions of North America.

It is a low-growing evergreen shrub that thrives in acidic, wet soils, often found in bogs and swamps. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and have a tart flavor, similar to other cranberry species. Its leaves are oval-shaped and remain green throughout the year, contributing to its ornamental value.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and maintaining wetland ecosystems.
