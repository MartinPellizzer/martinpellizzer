Oncidium × pulchellum f. withneranum

Oncidium × pulchellum f. withneranum, commonly known as the Withner's Oncidium, is a hybrid orchid species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, long-lasting flowers that come in shades of yellow, orange, and red, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its pseudobulbs are thick and fleshy, storing water to help it survive dry periods.

The orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its showy blooms.
