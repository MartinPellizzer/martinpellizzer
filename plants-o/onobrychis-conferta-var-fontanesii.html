Onobrychis conferta var. fontanesii

Onobrychis conferta var. fontanesii, commonly known as the Fontanesii sweet clover, is a perennial legume native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural settings.

Its ornamental value also makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
