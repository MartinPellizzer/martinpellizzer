Orchis × beyrichii var. grenieri

Orchis × beyrichii var. grenieri, commonly known as the Grenier's hybrid orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Orchis mascula and Orchis morrowii.

It is a herbaceous perennial with a distinctive inflorescence of pale pink to lavender flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland habitats. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a waxy coating.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
