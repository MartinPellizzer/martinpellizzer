Oenothera californica var. avita

Oenothera californica var. avita, commonly known as the San Diego evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of southern California.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening and close by morning. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to coastal climates with mild, wet winters and warm, dry summers.

This variety is often found in open habitats such as dunes, chaparral, and coastal scrub.
