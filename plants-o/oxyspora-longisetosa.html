Oxyspora longisetosa

Oxyspora longisetosa, commonly known as the long-hair dandelion, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces small, yellow composite flowers that develop into parachute-like seeds. The plant is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. It has a taproot system and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

Oxyspora longisetosa is also noted for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies.
