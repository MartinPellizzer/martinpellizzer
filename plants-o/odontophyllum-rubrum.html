Odontophyllum rubrum

Odontophyllum rubrum, commonly known as the red-stemmed orchid, is a tropical plant native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking red stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, often clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
