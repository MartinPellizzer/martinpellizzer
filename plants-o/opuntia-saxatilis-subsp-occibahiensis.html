Opuntia saxatilis subsp. occibahiensis

Opuntia saxatilis subsp.

occibahiensis, commonly known as the Baja Opuntia, is a species of cactus native to the Baja California peninsula. It is characterized by its flattened, ribbed stems that are typically green with a waxy coating.

The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to arid conditions.

This subspecies is well-suited to rocky and sandy soils, making it a resilient plant in desert environments.
