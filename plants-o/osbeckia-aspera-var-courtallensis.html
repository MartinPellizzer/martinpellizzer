Osbeckia aspera var. courtallensis

Osbeckia aspera var. courtallensis, commonly known as the hairy osbeckia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks.

Osbeckia aspera var. courtallensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
