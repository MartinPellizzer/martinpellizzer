Orchiodes decipiens

Orchiodes decipiens, commonly known as the mimic orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous plant with a unique floral structure that closely resembles the appearance of other orchid species, aiding in its pollination strategy. The plant produces showy, colorful flowers that are typically pink or purple in hue. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the surrounding soil, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Orchiodes decipiens is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by supporting pollinators.
