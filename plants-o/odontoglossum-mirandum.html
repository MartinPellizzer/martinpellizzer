Odontoglossum mirandum

Odontoglossum mirandum, commonly known as the miracle orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial herb with a short, upright stem and produces large, showy flowers that are typically violet or blue in color. The plant has a pseudobulb structure, which serves as a storage organ for water and nutrients. Its flowers are highly fragrant and bloom during the cooler months, making it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.

Odontoglossum mirandum is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its striking appearance.
