Oxalis monticola var. sericea

Oxalis monticola var. sericea, commonly known as the silverleaf wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with a silvery-white pubescence that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Oxalis monticola var. sericea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and delicate flowers.
