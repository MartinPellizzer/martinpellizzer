Oxytropis bogdoschanica

Oxytropis bogdoschanica, commonly known as the Mongolian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems. The plant produces small, yellow pea-like flowers and trifoliate leaves with stipules. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in steppes and rocky soils.

Oxytropis bogdoschanica is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitats.
