Orchis kiraishiensis f. leucantha

Orchis kiraishiensis f. leucantha, commonly known as the white-flowered Kiraishi orchid, is a rare and endemic orchid species found in specific regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral morphology, featuring white petals and a prominent lip. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers are often short-lived but attract specific pollinators, contributing to its ecological niche.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
