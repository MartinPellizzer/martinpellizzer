Oenothera gayophytum f. robusta

Oenothera gayophytum f. robusta, commonly known as the robust evening primrose, is a variant of the evening primrose species native to the southwestern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that open in the evening. The plant features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, typical of the Onagraceae family. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by moths, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
