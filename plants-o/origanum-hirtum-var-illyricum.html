Origanum hirtum var. illyricum

Origanum hirtum var. illyricum, commonly known as Croatian oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly fuzzy leaves and small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates. Its essential oils, rich in thymol and carvacrol, contribute to its strong aromatic properties.

This variety is often used in traditional cuisine and herbal medicine for its digestive and antimicrobial benefits.
