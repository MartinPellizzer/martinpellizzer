Oenothera niagarensis

Oenothera niagarensis, commonly known as the Niagara evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Onagraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found along riverbanks and wetlands. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and somewhat branched.

Oenothera niagarensis is also known for its medicinal properties, with some parts of the plant used in traditional herbal remedies.
