Oncidium pictum

Oncidium pictum, commonly known as the Painted Orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that often feature intricate patterns in shades of red, yellow, and green. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, enabling it to thrive in low-light conditions.

Oncidium pictum is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its vibrant blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
