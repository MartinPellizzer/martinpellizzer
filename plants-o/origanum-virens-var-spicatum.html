Origanum virens var. spicatum

Origanum virens var. spicatum, commonly known as green oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems covered in small, oval-shaped leaves that are bright green and slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale blue to purple flowers in the summer, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its distinctive minty and citrusy fragrance.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for herb gardens and culinary use.
