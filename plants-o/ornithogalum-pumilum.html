Ornithogalum pumilum

Ornithogalum pumilum, commonly known as the dwarf star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers during the spring. The plant is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes. Its bulbs are toxic and should be handled with care.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in Mediterranean climates.
