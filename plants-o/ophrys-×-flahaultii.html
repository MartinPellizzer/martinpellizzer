Ophrys × flahaultii

Ophrys × flahaultii, commonly known as the Flahault's bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species that exhibits unique botanical features.

It is characterized by its intricate, bee-like floral structure, which includes a specialized lip that mimics the appearance and scent of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in temperate climates with well-drained conditions. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brownish in color, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Ophrys × flahaultii is a relatively rare species, often found in specific regions of Europe where its ecological niche is maintained.
