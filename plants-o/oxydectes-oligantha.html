Oxydectes oligantha

Oxydectes oligantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved oxydectes, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of pale yellow to white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Oxydectes oligantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
