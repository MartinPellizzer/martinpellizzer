Olax gambecola

Olax gambecola, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Olacaceae and is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in humid, tropical climates. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a leathery texture, while its flowers are small and clustered.

Olax gambecola is also noted for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
