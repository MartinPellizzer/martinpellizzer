Opuntia delaetiana

Opuntia delaetiana, commonly known as the Mexican prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, fleshy pads called cladodes, which are covered in fine spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tunas, is edible and has a sweet, succulent texture.

Opuntia delaetiana is well adapted to arid environments and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of food.
