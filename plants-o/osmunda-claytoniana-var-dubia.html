Osmunda claytoniana var. dubia

Osmunda claytoniana var. dubia, commonly known as the slender fern or the northern water fern, is a variant of the royal fern characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds.

It typically grows in wet, swampy areas and is often found in the eastern United States. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit and produces spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its fronds. Its foliage is usually a vibrant green, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

This fern is an important component of wetland ecosystems, contributing to biodiversity and soil stabilization.
