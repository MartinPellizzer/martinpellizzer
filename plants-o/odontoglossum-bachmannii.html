Odontoglossum bachmannii

Odontoglossum bachmannii, commonly known as the Bachmann's orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in high-altitude cloud forests. The plant features a pseudobulb that stores water and nutrients, supporting its growth in nutrient-poor soils. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish-white with intricate patterns, and it blooms during the cooler months.

Odontoglossum bachmannii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
