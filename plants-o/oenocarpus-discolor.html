Oenocarpus discolor

Oenocarpus discolor, commonly known as the breadfruit tree, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, spiky fruit. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. Its fruit, which resembles a small bread loaf, is rich in carbohydrates and has been a staple food source for many island communities.

The plant also has cultural and economic significance, particularly in traditional agriculture and local cuisines.
