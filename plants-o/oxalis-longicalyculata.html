Oxalis longicalyculata

Oxalis longicalyculata, commonly known as the long-beak wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive seed pods that split open to release its seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Oxalis longicalyculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
