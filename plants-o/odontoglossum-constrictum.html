Odontoglossum constrictum

Odontoglossum constrictum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments at high elevations. The plant features a pseudobulb with a single leaf, and its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish with a distinctive lip. It produces small, fragrant blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and moths.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a rare ornamental plant.
