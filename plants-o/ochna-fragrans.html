Ochna fragrans

Ochna fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant ochna, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its smooth, often grayish bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by distinctive red, fleshy fruits. Ochna fragrans is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it a unique and ecologically important species in its native habitats.
