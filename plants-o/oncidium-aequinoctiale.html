Oncidium aequinoctiale

Oncidium aequinoctiale, commonly known as the Easter Orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, fragrant flower spikes that typically bloom in spring and early summer. The plant has strap-like leaves that are dark green and often have a waxy texture. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Oncidium aequinoctiale is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its long-lasting blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
