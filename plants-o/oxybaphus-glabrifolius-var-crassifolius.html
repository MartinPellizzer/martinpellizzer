Oxybaphus glabrifolius var. crassifolius

Oxybaphus glabrifolius var. crassifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf oxybaphus, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are broader than those of the typical variety. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

Its unique foliage and adaptability make it a notable species in local flora.
