Otophora paradoxa

Otophora paradoxa, commonly known as the paradox orchid, is a rare and enigmatic flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, intricate floral structure that resembles a miniature tree or shrub. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pale green or white, with a distinctively shaped lip. Otophora paradoxa thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to the harsh conditions of its mountainous habitat.
