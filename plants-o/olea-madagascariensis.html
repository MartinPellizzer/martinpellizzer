Olea madagascariensis

Olea madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar olive, is a small evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically consumed by local wildlife. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in humid, lowland areas.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is a notable species in Madagascar's biodiversity.
