Osmanthus matsudae

Osmanthus matsudae, commonly known as the Japanese osmanthus, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late autumn. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that emit a sweet, honey-like fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

Osmanthus matsudae is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and is valued for its aromatic properties in traditional Japanese culture.
