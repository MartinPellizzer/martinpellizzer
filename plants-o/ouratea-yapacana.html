Ouratea yapacana

Ouratea yapacana, commonly known as the silverleaf ouratea, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by follicle fruits containing several seeds. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is typically found in moist, shaded areas of tropical forests and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
