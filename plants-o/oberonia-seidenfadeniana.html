Oberonia seidenfadeniana

Oberonia seidenfadeniana, commonly known as the Philippine orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its roots are mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to aid in nutrient absorption.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
