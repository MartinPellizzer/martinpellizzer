Oxalis fontana var. bushii

Oxalis fontana var. bushii, commonly known as the Bush's forest oxalis, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features delicate, trifoliate leaves that resemble clover, with a glossy, dark green coloration. The plant produces small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in cool, shaded environments and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Oxalis fontana var. bushii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and greenhouses for its attractive foliage and subtle floral display.
