Orchiastrum mutabile

Orchiastrum mutabile, commonly known as the variable orchid, is a unique flowering plant characterized by its ability to alter its floral structure in response to environmental stimuli.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant's leaves are elongated and lance-shaped, with a waxy surface that aids in water retention. Its flowers, which can change color and shape, are a key feature, often attracting a variety of pollinators.

Due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal, it is a popular subject in botanical studies and horticulture.
