Oreinotinus pichinchensis

Oreinotinus pichinchensis, commonly known as the Pichincha orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, yellow, and orange. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments, often growing in cloud forests where humidity and cool temperatures are prevalent. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air, a feature common among epiphytic orchids.

The Pichincha orchid plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
