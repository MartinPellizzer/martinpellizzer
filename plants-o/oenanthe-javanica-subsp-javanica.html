Oenanthe javanica subsp. javanica

Oenanthe javanica subsp.

javanica, commonly known as the Java water dropwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia. It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often near water bodies such as ponds and rice fields.

The plant has slender, erect stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive, slightly hairy texture.

The species is characterized by its aquatic or semi-aquatic habitat preference and its ornamental value in wetland gardens.
