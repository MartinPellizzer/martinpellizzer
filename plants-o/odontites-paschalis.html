Odontites paschalis

Odontites paschalis, commonly known as Paschal's tooth.

It is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in a loose cluster, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Odontites paschalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
