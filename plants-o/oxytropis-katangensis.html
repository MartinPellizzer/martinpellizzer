Oxytropis katangensis

Oxytropis katangensis, commonly known as Katanga burrweed, is a flowering plant native to the Democratic Republic of the Congo.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the spring and early summer. Oxytropis katangensis is adapted to dry and rocky environments, showcasing its resilience to arid conditions.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and potentially supporting pollinators.
