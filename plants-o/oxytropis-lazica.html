Oxytropis lazica

Oxytropis lazica, commonly known as Lazica locoweed, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Oxytropis lazica is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is known for its toxic properties due to the presence of alkaloids.
