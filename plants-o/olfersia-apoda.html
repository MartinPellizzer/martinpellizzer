Olfersia apoda

Olfersia apoda, commonly known as the naked leaf orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, leafless stems and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow with a distinct, sweet aroma.

Olfersia apoda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
