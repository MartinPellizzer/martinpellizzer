Ossaea baracoensis

Ossaea baracoensis, commonly known as the Baracoensis grass, is a perennial grass species native to the grasslands of South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats and is adapted to seasonal rainfall patterns. Its flowering stems produce small, clustered inflorescences, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native environment.

Due to its ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated for erosion control in suitable climates.
