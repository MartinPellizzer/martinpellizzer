Olax gardneriana

Olax gardneriana, commonly known as the Gardner's olax, is a species of flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the family Olacaceae and is characterized by its small, showy flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant features woody stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly rough texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
