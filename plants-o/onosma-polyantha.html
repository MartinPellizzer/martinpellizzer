Onosma polyantha

Onosma polyantha, commonly known as the Persian shield, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its striking, shield-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from violet to white, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is widely cultivated in horticulture.
