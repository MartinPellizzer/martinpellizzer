Ocotea trinitatis

Ocotea trinitatis, commonly known as the Trinidadian ocotea, is a small evergreen tree native to the Caribbean, particularly Trinidad and Tobago.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed enclosed in a fleshy, orange-colored pulp.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in coastal and moist forest habitats.
