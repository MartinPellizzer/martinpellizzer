Ophrys aranifera f. pseudomuscifera

Ophrys aranifera f. pseudomuscifera, commonly known as the pseudomusciferous bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which closely resembles a female bee, a feature evolved to attract male bees for pollination. The plant produces strikingly intricate flowers with a dark, bee-like appearance, often adorned with intricate patterns and a scent that mimics the pheromones of female bees. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically in woodland or heathland habitats.

Due to its specific ecological requirements and habitat fragmentation, it is considered a vulnerable species in many regions.
