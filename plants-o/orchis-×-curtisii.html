Orchis × curtisii

Orchis × curtisii, commonly known as the Curtis's orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Orchis morio and Orchis mascula.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or grassland habitats. It blooms from June to August, producing a cluster of fragrant, lip-shaped flowers.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a protected species in many regions.
