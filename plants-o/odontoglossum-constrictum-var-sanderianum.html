Odontoglossum constrictum var. sanderianum

Odontoglossum constrictum var. sanderianum, commonly known as the Sander's orchid, is a striking terrestrial orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It features upright, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white pubescence. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences bearing numerous showy, violet to purple flowers with intricate patterns. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the cool, humid environments it inhabits.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its vibrant blooms.
