Ochetophila parvifolia

Ochetophila parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved ochetophila, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a whorled pattern around the stem. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a delicate aesthetic to its appearance. It prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and less commonly cultivated species.
