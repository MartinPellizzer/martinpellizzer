Osmanthus aurantiacus var. thunbergii

Osmanthus aurantiacus var. thunbergii, commonly known as the Japanese sweet olive, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, small yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late autumn. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. It is often cultivated for its aromatic properties and ornamental value.

This variety is well-suited to temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.
