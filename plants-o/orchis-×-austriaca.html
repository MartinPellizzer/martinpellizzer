Orchis × austriaca

Orchis × austriaca, commonly known as the Austrian twayblade, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Orchis moria and Orchis mascula.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe, typically found in moist meadows and woodlands. The plant features a slender, leafless stem that bears a single spike of pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. Its flowers are characterized by a prominent lip and a tubular column, typical of many orchid species.

The Austrian twayblade is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
