Onobrychis calcarea

Onobrychis calcarea, commonly known as the limestone clover, is a perennial legume native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer. It is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment.

Its hardy nature makes it a resilient plant in arid and semi-arid environments.
