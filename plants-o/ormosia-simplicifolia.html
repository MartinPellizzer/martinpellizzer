Ormosia simplicifolia

Ormosia simplicifolia, commonly known as the simple-leaved ormosia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces pods that contain flattened, elliptical seeds, typical of the legume family. It is often found in lowland rainforests and thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and potential ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
