Oxalis chrysantha var. pusilla

Oxalis chrysantha var. pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf wood sorrel, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with reddish veins and produces delicate, five-petaled yellow flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil. It is often used in woodland gardens for its ornamental value and ability to naturalize easily.

Its foliage and flowers add a charming, wildflower-like appearance to shaded landscapes.
