Oxymitra excisa

Oxymitra excisa, commonly known as the scarlet pitcher plant, is a carnivorous plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, tubular leaves that form deep, pitcher-like structures. These pitchers secrete a sticky fluid that traps and digests insects, providing the plant with essential nutrients. The plant's bright red coloration helps attract prey, while its specialized leaf morphology facilitates efficient trapping.

Oxymitra excisa thrives in moist, nutrient-poor environments, making it a unique example of adaptive evolution in plant life.
