Oenothera odorata var. media

Oenothera odorata var. media, commonly known as the fragrant evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening and close by morning. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, hairy textures.

The species is known for its medicinal and ornamental value, with the seeds containing essential fatty acids.
