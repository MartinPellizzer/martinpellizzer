Omphalodes esquirolii

Omphalodes esquirolii, commonly known as the Corsican bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features showy, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lanceolate and slightly hairy, while its stems are erect and branched.

Omphalodes esquirolii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
