Ophrys umbilicata subsp. flavomarginata

Ophrys umbilicata subsp.

flavomarginata, commonly known as the yellow-margin hump-nosed orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its distinctive hump-shaped lip, which is often yellow or orange with a distinctive margin.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has a short, compact habit with a single stem bearing a few flowers.

The species is known for its intricate floral morphology and is of interest to botanists and conservationists due to its limited distribution and ecological specificity.
