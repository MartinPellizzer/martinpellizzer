Ophrys fusca f. funerea

Ophrys fusca f. funerea, commonly known as the funeral orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black flowers that resemble human faces, a feature that aids in its unique pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, greenish-brown seed pods after flowering.

This orchid is part of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its specialized relationship with specific pollinators.
