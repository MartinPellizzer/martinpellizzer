Oxytropis middendorffii

Oxytropis middendorffii, commonly known as the Siberian locoweed, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are palmately compound, and it produces legumes that contain toxic alkaloids, which can be harmful to livestock.

Oxytropis middendorffii plays a role in stabilizing soil in arid regions and is of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
