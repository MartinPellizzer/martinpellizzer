Oxalis mandioccana var. rhombifolia

Oxalis mandioccana var. rhombifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive red or purple vein pattern, and its flowers are typically yellow with five petals. The plant produces small, edible berries that are sometimes used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
