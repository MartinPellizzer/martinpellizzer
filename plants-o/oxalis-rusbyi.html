Oxalis rusbyi

Oxalis rusbyi, commonly known as Rusby's wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each leaflet resembling a heart shape, and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is a perennial species that produces capsule fruits containing several seeds.

Oxalis rusbyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
