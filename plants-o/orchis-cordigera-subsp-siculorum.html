Orchis cordigera subsp. siculorum

Orchis cordigera subsp.

siculorum, commonly known as the Sicilian heart-leaf orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Sicily. It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and delicate, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes, often in nutrient-poor soils. Its reproductive strategy relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake, a common feature among orchids.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
