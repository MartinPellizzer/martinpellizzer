Orobanche fasciculata var. fasciculata

Orobanche fasciculata var. fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered broomrape, is a root-parasitic plant belonging to the Orobanchaceae family.

It lacks chlorophyll and is entirely dependent on host plants for its nutrients, typically parasitizing members of the Brassicaceae family. The plant is characterized by its yellowish or reddish stems and small, scale-like leaves. It produces small, pale flowers that are often clustered together, giving it its common name.

This species is found in various regions of Europe and is often associated with disturbed soils and agricultural areas.
