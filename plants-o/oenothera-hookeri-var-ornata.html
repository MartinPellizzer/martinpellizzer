Oenothera hookeri var. ornata

Oenothera hookeri var. ornata, commonly known as the showy evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It features showy, yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a food source for native wildlife and its ornamental value in xeriscaped gardens.
