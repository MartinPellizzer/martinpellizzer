Oxalis falcata var. callosa

Oxalis falcata var. callosa, commonly known as the roughleaf wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with deeply lobed, waxy blades and distinctive, showy flowers that are typically pink or red. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. Its foliage is characterized by a rough, hairy texture, which gives it its common name.

Oxalis falcata var. callosa is often found in woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in shaded gardens.
