Origanum vulgare f. pallidum

Origanum vulgare f. pallidum, commonly known as pale oregano, is a horticultural form of the common oregano plant.

It is characterized by its lighter-colored foliage and smaller, more delicate leaves compared to the standard variety. This plant is native to southern Europe and thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight. It produces small, pinkish-white flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Pale oregano is often used in culinary applications and as an ornamental plant in gardens.
