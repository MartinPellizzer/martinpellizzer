Oldenlandia capitellata

Oldenlandia capitellata, commonly known as the small-headed spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. The plant features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, often blooming during the rainy season. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often trailing.

Oldenlandia capitellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
