Oryctanthus alveolatus

Oryctanthus alveolatus, commonly known as the honeycomb bush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular, honeycomb-like flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant has small, narrow leaves and produces small, spherical fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Oryctanthus alveolatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
