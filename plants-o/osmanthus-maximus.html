Osmanthus maximus

Osmanthus maximus, commonly known as the sweet osmanthus, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is valued for its fragrant flowers, which are used in teas, confections, and traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late autumn, releasing a sweet, honey-like aroma. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a glossy, dark green surface.

Osmanthus maximus thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for ornamental and culinary purposes.
