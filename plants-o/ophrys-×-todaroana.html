Ophrys × todaroana

Ophrys × todaroana, commonly known as the Todaroan bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its intricate floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant produces showy, bee-like flowers with a dark brown to black coloration, often adorned with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry. It thrives in calcareous soils and is typically found in open habitats such as grasslands and heaths.

Ophrys × todaroana is a rare and protected species, reflecting its ecological significance and the need for conservation efforts.
