Osmunda hirta

Osmunda hirta, commonly known as the hairy sword fern, is a species of fern native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender, and often hairy fronds that can reach up to 2 meters in length. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its reproductive structures include sporangia that produce spores for propagation.

Osmunda hirta plays an important ecological role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for various small organisms.
