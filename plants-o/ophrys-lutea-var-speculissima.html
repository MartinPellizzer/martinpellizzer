Ophrys lutea var. speculissima

Ophrys lutea var. speculissima, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its intricate, bee-mimicking flowers that play a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a dense, velvety texture.

The flowers, which bloom in late spring, feature a yellow coloration with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of female bees.
