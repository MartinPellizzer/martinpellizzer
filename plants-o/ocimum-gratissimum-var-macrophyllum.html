Ocimum gratissimum var. macrophyllum

Ocimum gratissimum var. macrophyllum, commonly known as African basil, is a tropical perennial herb native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant purple or white flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its aromatic foliage, which contains essential oils with various medicinal properties. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both traditional medicine and horticulture.
