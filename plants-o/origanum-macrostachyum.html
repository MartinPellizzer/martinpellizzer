Origanum macrostachyum

Origanum macrostachyum, commonly known as the mountain oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes. Its essential oils, rich in thymol and carvacrol, contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.

Origanum macrostachyum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in moderate climates.
