Oxalis imbricata var. violacea

Oxalis imbricata var. violacea, commonly known as violetleaf wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, violet-colored flowers. The plant produces yellow, spurred flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate foliage and vibrant floral display.
