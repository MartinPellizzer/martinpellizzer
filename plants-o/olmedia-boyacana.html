Olmedia boyacana

Olmedia boyacana, commonly known as the Colombian olive tree, is a species native to the Andean region of Colombia.

It is a small evergreen tree that typically grows to a height of about 5 meters. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves and produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a drupe, similar to an olive, and is used in traditional medicine.

The botanical characteristics of Olmedia boyacana make it an important plant in the biodiversity of its native habitat.
