Oxalis humblotii

Oxalis humblotii, commonly known as the Cape oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and long flowering period.

Oxalis humblotii is also valued for its ability to tolerate drought conditions once established.
