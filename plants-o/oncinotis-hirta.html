Oncinotis hirta

Oncinotis hirta, commonly known as the hairy tansy, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Oncinotis hirta has hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
