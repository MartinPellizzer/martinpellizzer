Oxalis nemorosa

Oxalis nemorosa, commonly known as wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white, yellow, or pink. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes.

Oxalis nemorosa is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
