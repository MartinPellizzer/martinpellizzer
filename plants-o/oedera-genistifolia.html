Oedera genistifolia

Oedera genistifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf strewberry, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-white, fuzzy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and seasonal blooms.
