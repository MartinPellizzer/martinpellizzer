Ophioglossum eliminatum

Ophioglossum eliminatum, commonly known as the lesser adder's tongue fern, is a rare and ancient fern species belonging to the family Ophioglossaceae.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring a long, narrow, spore-bearing frond that resembles a tongue, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. Its reproductive structures are specialized, with sporangia arranged in a linear pattern along the frond.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Ophioglossum eliminatum is considered a conservation concern in many areas.
