Oxytropis neglecta subsp. occidentalis

Oxytropis neglecta subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as western burro's tail, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces trifoliate leaves and is adapted to arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open habitats.

Its seeds are enclosed in pods and dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution across dry regions.
