Ornithopodium egoceraton

Ornithopodium egoceraton, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow petals and intricate floral structure typical of orchids. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil efficiently.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
