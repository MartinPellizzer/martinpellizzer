Oldenlandia lancifolia var. longipes

Oldenlandia lancifolia var. longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem. This variant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or wetlands.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Commelinaceae family.
