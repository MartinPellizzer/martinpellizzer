Oxalis bipartita f. majorina

Oxalis bipartita f. majorina, commonly known as the giant oxalis, is a striking ornamental plant characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant yellow flowers.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is native to South America, particularly Brazil. The plant features prominently veined leaves that resemble those of the common wood sorrel, but on a much larger scale. Its flowers are typically borne in clusters and bloom in spring, adding a dramatic visual impact to gardens.

Due to its size and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in warm climates as a decorative plant.
