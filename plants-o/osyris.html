Osyris

Osyris, commonly known as the waterberry or osyris tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Malvaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its showy, often pink or white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are typically alternate, simple, and ovate to lance-shaped.

Osyris species are often found in moist or wet habitats, such as swamps and riverbanks, and some species are used in traditional medicine.
