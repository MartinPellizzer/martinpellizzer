Oxalis bisulca

Oxalis bisulca, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with three heart-shaped leaflets, each adorned with fine, wavy edges. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring. Its stems are slender and often hairy, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Oxalis bisulca is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
