Ochthocharis sylvestris

Ochthocharis sylvestris, commonly known as the forest orchid, is a species of orchid native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant prefers shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and forest understories. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette.

Ochthocharis sylvestris is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens with the right growing conditions.
