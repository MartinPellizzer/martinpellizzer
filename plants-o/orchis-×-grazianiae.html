Orchis × grazianiae

Orchis × grazianiae, commonly known as the Graziani's orchid, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Orchis morio and Orchis purpurea.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of violet to purple flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
