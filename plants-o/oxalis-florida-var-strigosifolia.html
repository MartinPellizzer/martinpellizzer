Oxalis florida var. strigosifolia

Oxalis florida var. strigosifolia, commonly known as the hairy wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are often covered in fine, hairy glands. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow, star-shaped flowers throughout the spring and summer. Its stems are typically hairy and can grow up to several feet in height.

Oxalis florida var. strigosifolia is often found in woodland areas and moist soils, where it thrives in partial shade.
