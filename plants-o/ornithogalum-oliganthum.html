Ornithogalum oliganthum

Ornithogalum oliganthum, commonly known as the lesser star-of-Bethlem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces umbels of small, white, star-shaped flowers. The plant is characterized by its succulent stems and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It blooms during the summer months and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Ornithogalum oliganthum is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
