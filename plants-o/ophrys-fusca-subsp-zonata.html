Ophrys fusca subsp. zonata

Ophrys fusca subsp.

zonata, commonly known as the Zoned Bee Orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to parts of Europe. It is characterized by its unique floral morphology, featuring a lip that mimics the appearance of a female bee, which plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy.

The plant typically grows in calcareous grasslands and heathlands, thriving in well-drained, sunny habitats. Its flowers are usually pale yellow with distinctive dark markings, aiding in the mimicry that attracts male bees.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, this subspecies is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
