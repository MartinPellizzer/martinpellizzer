Orchis laxiflora f. longebracteata

Orchis laxiflora f. longebracteata, commonly known as the long bract orchid, is a variant of the laxiflora orchid native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its elongated bracts that extend along the flower stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or meadow habitats.

This orchid is part of the Orchidaceae family and is noted for its ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
