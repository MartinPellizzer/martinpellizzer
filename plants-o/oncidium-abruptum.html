Oncidium abruptum

Oncidium abruptum, commonly known as the Spurred Oncidium, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, long-lasting inflorescences that feature a variety of colors, including yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are often fragrant and bloom during the summer months.

Oncidium abruptum requires bright, indirect light and moderate humidity to thrive.
