Oxymeris secundiflora

Oxymeris secundiflora, commonly known as the yellow mangrove, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows in saline and brackish water environments. The plant has distinctive yellowish flowers that appear in clusters, often blooming during the rainy season. Its leaves are opposite and elliptical, with a smooth surface and a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Oxymeris secundiflora plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial species.
