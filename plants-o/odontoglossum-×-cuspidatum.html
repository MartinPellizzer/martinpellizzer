Odontoglossum × cuspidatum

Odontoglossum × cuspidatum, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Odontoglossum ochreum and Odontoglossum pusillum.

It is a herbaceous perennial with striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces inflorescences with clustered, fragrant orchid blooms. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.

This hybrid is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation under proper conditions.
