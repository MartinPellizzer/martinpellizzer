Oncidium altissimum var. baueri

Oncidium altissimum var. baueri, commonly known as the Bauer's Oncidium, is a hybrid orchid species native to South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, showy inflorescences that bear numerous fragrant, yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its pseudobulbs are elongated and segmented, supporting the plant's growth and storage of water and nutrients.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
