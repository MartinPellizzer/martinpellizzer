Oryzopsis rechingeri

Oryzopsis rechingeri, commonly known as the desert ricegrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit, slender leaves, and small, spikelet-like inflorescences. The plant is adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to thrive in sandy or rocky soils. Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize disturbed or degraded landscapes.

Oryzopsis rechingeri plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitats.
