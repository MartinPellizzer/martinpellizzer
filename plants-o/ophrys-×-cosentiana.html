Ophrys × cosentiana

Ophrys × cosentiana, commonly known as the Cosentian orchid, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its intricate, bee-mimicking flowers that play a key role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and meadows across Europe. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of flowers, each with a distinctive, fuzzy labellum that resembles a female bee.

Ophrys × cosentiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral structure.
