Onobrychis komarovii

Onobrychis komarovii, commonly known as Komarov's burclover, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in steppe and semi-desert environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
