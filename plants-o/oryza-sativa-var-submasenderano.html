Oryza sativa var. submasenderano

Oryza sativa var. submasenderano, commonly known as the Submasenderano rice variety, is a less commonly cultivated rice species within the Oryza genus.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated grains and typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions with high humidity. The plant exhibits a semi-aquatic growth habit, with roots that can tolerate both flooded and well-drained soil conditions. Its botanical features include long, narrow leaves and a panicle inflorescence that produces small, white flowers.

This variety is of interest to researchers due to its potential for genetic diversity and adaptability to varying environmental conditions.
