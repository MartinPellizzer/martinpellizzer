Oxalis flaviuscula

Oxalis flaviuscula, commonly known as the yellow wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are often a vibrant green with a slight yellowish tinge. The plant produces small, delicate yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

Oxalis flaviuscula is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
