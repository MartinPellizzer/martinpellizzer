Oocephalus silvinae f. coccineus

Oocephalus silvinae f. coccineus, commonly known as the red-flowered silvina, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, bright red ray florets that contrast with its yellow disk florets, making it visually striking. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and its adaptability to well-drained soils.
