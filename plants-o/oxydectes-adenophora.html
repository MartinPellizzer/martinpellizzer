Oxydectes adenophora

Oxydectes adenophora, commonly known as the hairy leaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, oval-shaped leaves and produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is often found in grassy areas and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its scientific name reflects its distinctive features, with "adenophora" referring to the glandular hairs on its leaves.

Oxydectes adenophora is primarily valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
