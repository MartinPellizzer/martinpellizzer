Origanum albicans

Origanum albicans, commonly known as white marjoram, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces aromatic leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. Its essential oil contains compounds like carvacrol and thymol, which contribute to its antimicrobial properties.

Origanum albicans thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for gardens and herbal cultivation.
