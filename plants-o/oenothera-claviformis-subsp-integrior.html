Oenothera claviformis subsp. integrior

Oenothera claviformis subsp.

integrior, commonly known as the smooth-leaf evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that open in the evening, and its linear, smooth leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oval seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Oenothera claviformis subsp.

integrior is a member of the Onagraceae family, which includes other evening primroses and related species.
