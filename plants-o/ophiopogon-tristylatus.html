Ophiopogon tristylatus

Ophiopogon tristylatus, commonly known as the three-styled lily, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Ophiopogon tristylatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional herbal medicine.
