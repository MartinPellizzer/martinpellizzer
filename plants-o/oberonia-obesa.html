Oberonia obesa

Oberonia obesa, commonly known as the fat orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to its humid, shaded habitat. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-yellow with red or purple markings. It thrives in well-draining potting mix and requires indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and rarity, Oberonia obesa is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
