Ocotea fragrantissima

Ocotea fragrantissima, commonly known as the sweet myrtle, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurel and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its bark and leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal uses.
