Ochna jabotapita

Ochna jabotapita, commonly known as the African butter tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits containing several seeds. Ochna jabotapita is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in regions with low rainfall.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local communities.
