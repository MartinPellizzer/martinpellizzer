Oxytropis komei

Oxytropis komei, commonly known as Japanese locoweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to East Asia, particularly Japan, and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It has compound leaves with multiple leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Oxytropis komei is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, which makes it an important plant in certain ecological contexts.
