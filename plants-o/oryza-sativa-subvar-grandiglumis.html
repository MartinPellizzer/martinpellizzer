Oryza sativa subvar. grandiglumis

Oryza sativa subvar. grandiglumis, commonly known as the large glume rice, is a wild rice species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, glabrous grains and robust, erect growth habit. The plant typically grows in wetland environments and has a fibrous root system that helps it thrive in waterlogged soils. Its panicle is usually loosely branched and contains spikelets with two glumes.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists and agricultural researchers due to its potential for genetic diversity and adaptation to various environmental conditions.
