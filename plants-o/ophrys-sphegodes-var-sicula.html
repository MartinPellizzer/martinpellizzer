Ophrys sphegodes var. sicula

Ophrys sphegodes var. sicula, commonly known as the Sicilian bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the island of Sicily.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant produces striking, bee-like flowers with a dark brownish-purple coloration and intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry. It thrives in calcareous soils and is typically found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts to protect its natural environment.
