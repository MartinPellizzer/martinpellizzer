Oldenlandia grayi

Oldenlandia grayi, commonly known as the silverleaf spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, spider-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in a silvery or grayish pubescence.

Oldenlandia grayi is an herbaceous perennial that produces small, white or pale blue flowers, which attract various pollinators.
