Oncidium cheirophorum var. exauriculatum

Oncidium cheirophorum var. exauriculatum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to South America.

It features striking, elongated inflorescences with bright yellow flowers that have a velvety texture. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters, often lasting several weeks, and it requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

The variety is prized for its vibrant color and relatively easy care, making it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.
