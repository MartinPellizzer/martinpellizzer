Oxalis rubra var. patagonica

Oxalis rubra var. patagonica, commonly known as the Patagonian wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins and produces small, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, and it has a fibrous root system.

Oxalis rubra var. patagonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
