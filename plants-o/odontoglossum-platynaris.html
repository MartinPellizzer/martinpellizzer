Odontoglossum platynaris

Odontoglossum platynaris, commonly known as the silver-veined orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and striking, dark green leaves with distinctive silver veins. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and purple, often with a yellow or green throat. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Odontoglossum platynaris is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in shaded gardens or greenhouses.
