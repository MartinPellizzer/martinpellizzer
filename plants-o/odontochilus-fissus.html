Odontochilus fissus

Odontochilus fissus, commonly known as the fissured toothed orchid, is a rare and highly sought-after orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, toothed leaf margins and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and purple. The plant thrives in cool, shaded environments with high humidity, making it a challenging species to cultivate. Its specific habitat requirements and limited distribution contribute to its status as an endangered plant.

Due to its rarity and aesthetic appeal, it is often collected by enthusiasts, further threatening its survival in the wild.
