Orchis bipunctata

Orchis bipunctata, commonly known as the two-spotted orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, typically found in grasslands and woodland edges. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces striking violet or purple flowers with a distinctive two-spotted pattern on the dorsal sepal. It blooms from late spring to early summer, and its roots are often used in traditional herbal medicine.

The species is notable for its specialized pollination mechanism involving specific insect species.
