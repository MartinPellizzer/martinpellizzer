Orchis vaginata

Orchis vaginata, commonly known as the common twayblade, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and meadows. The plant has a distinctive appearance with a slender, leafless stem and a single, showy orchid-like flower. Its roots are fibrous and often form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi.

The flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in late spring to early summer.
