Olea exasperata

Olea exasperata, commonly known as the Syrian olive, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, drupaceous fruits that are typically harvested when fully mature. Olea exasperata is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.

It has historical significance in ancient agriculture and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
