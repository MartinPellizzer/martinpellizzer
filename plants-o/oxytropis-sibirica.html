Oxytropis sibirica

Oxytropis sibirica, commonly known as Siberian locoweed, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Central Asia and Siberia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by pod fruits containing several seeds. It is well adapted to nutrient-poor soils and can thrive in harsh, arid environments.

Oxytropis sibirica is also known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock.
