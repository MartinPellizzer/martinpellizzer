Odontoglossum crispum var. chestertonii

Odontoglossum crispum var. chestertonii, commonly known as the chestertonii orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, often violet or purple flowers with intricate markings. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and often have a waxy texture, adapted to retain moisture.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
