Oxalis glandulitheca

Oxalis glandulitheca, commonly known as the glandular-leaf wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features trifoliate leaves with three heart-shaped leaflets, each adorned with small, glandular dots. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Oxalis glandulitheca is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
