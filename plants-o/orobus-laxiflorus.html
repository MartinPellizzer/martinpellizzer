Orobus laxiflorus

Orobus laxiflorus, commonly known as the slender-flowered orobus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sandy or rocky habitats, often in grassland or shrubland environments. Its flowers, which are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, bloom in spring or early summer.

Orobus laxiflorus is adapted to arid conditions and is often found in regions with seasonal rainfall.
