Olea aquifolium var. rotundifolia

Olea aquifolium var. rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf holm oak, is a variant of the holm oak native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its rounded, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring, followed by small, black, edible fruits. It is a hardy shrub or small tree that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
