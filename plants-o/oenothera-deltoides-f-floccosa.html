Oenothera deltoides f. floccosa

Oenothera deltoides f. floccosa, commonly known as the silky evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its pale yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has silvery, woolly leaves that help reduce water loss, adapting it to arid environments. It produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, black seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
