Onobrychis saxatilis f. virgata

Onobrychis saxatilis f. virgata, commonly known as the pink needlegrass, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has pinnately compound leaves and is adapted to grow in rocky or dry soils. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
