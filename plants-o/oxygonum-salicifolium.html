Oxygonum salicifolium

Oxygonum salicifolium, commonly known as the willow-leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that resemble willow leaves, and it produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple and blue. The plant is a climbing vine that thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Its seeds are encased in a distinctive, spiral-shaped fruit that aids in wind dispersal.

Oxygonum salicifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
