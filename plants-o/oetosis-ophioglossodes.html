Oetosis ophioglossodes

Oetosis ophioglossodes, commonly known as the broad-leafed fern, is a rare and ancient plant species belonging to the family Ophioglossaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often divided into segments, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its leaves, which release spores for reproduction. It thrives in shaded, moist environments, typically found in temperate forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a sensitive indicator of ecological health.
