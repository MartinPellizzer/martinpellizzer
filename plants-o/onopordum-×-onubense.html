Onopordum × onubense

Onopordum × onubense, commonly known as the Spanish thistle, is a hybrid plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flowers. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soils. It produces small, oblong seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.

The plant has been used historically for its medicinal properties and as a source of fiber.
