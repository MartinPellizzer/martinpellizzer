Odontoglossum sanderianum

Odontoglossum sanderianum, commonly known as the Sander's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial herbaceous plant with a pseudobulb that stores water and nutrients, supporting its growth in high-altitude environments. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. Its leaves are typically broad and strap-like, adapted to maximize sunlight absorption in its native habitat.

Odontoglossum sanderianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts worldwide.
