Ocellochloa gardneri

Ocellochloa gardneri, commonly known as the Gardner's feathergrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, wiry stems with fine, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with silver or gray. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are characteristic of the Poaceae family. It thrives in sandy or well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as coastal dunes or sand plains.
