Oryza sativa var. kasakstanica

Oryza sativa var. kasakstanica, commonly known as the Kazakhstan rice, is a lesser-known variant of the rice plant native to Central Asia.

This variety is characterized by its short, slender grains and is typically cultivated in temperate climates with well-drained soils. It exhibits a high degree of genetic diversity, making it a subject of interest for agricultural research. The plant has a short growing season and is adapted to the specific environmental conditions of its native region.

Due to its unique traits, it holds potential for breeding programs aimed at improving rice resilience in changing climates.
