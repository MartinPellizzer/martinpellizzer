Oenothera tubicula subsp. strigulosa

Oenothera tubicula subsp.

strigulosa, commonly known as the woolly evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy yellow flowers that open in the evening.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential medicinal research.
