Olea mildbraedii var. cuspidata

Olea mildbraedii var. cuspidata, commonly known as the Cape Ash or African olive, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often gland-dotted and have a distinctive glossy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions with a Mediterranean climate.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
