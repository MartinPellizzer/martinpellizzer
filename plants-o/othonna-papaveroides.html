Othonna papaveroides

Othonna papaveroides, commonly known as the "Dwarf African daisy," is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It features compact, rounded growth with bright green, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom in spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Its drought tolerance and attractive appearance make it a popular choice for Mediterranean-style gardens.
