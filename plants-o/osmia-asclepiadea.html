Osmia asclepiadea

Osmia asclepiadea, commonly known as the common leafcutter bee, is a solitary bee species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its metallic green body and distinctive black markings on its abdomen. This bee is known for its unique behavior of cutting and transporting leaf fragments to line its nest cells. The plant associations of Osmia asclepiadea are often with flowering plants that provide nectar and pollen, such as thistles and dandelions.

Its role as a pollinator is significant in maintaining the ecological balance of its habitat.
