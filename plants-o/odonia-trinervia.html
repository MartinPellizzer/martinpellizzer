Odonia trinervia

Odonia trinervia, commonly known as the three-veined orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant has a slender, erect stem with few leaves, and its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the humid forest floor. Odonia trinervia thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its unique floral structure and pollination mechanism make it an important species in its ecological niche.
