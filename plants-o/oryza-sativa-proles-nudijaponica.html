Oryza sativa proles nudijaponica

Oryza sativa proles nudijaponica, commonly known as a subspecies of rice, is a variety characterized by its distinct botanical features.

It is a herbaceous annual plant that typically grows to a height of 1 to 1.5 meters. The plant has slender, erect stems covered with long, narrow leaves that are usually a deep green color. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle, which is a branched cluster of small, spikelet-like flowers.

The grain, or seed, is enclosed in a hull and is the edible part of the plant, rich in carbohydrates and nutrients.
