Ophrys souchei

Ophrys souchei, commonly known as the Souchet's bee orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its unique flower structure that closely resembles a female bee, a feature evolved to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brownish in color, with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of insects.

Ophrys souchei is a rare and protected species, often found in specific habitats across southern Europe.
