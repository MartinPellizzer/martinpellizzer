Ocotea pharomachrosorum

Ocotea pharomachrosorum, commonly known as the aromatic ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and laurel. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils, contributing to its use in traditional medicine and as a source of aromatic compounds.

The species is valued for its ecological role and potential economic applications in the fragrance and pharmaceutical industries.
