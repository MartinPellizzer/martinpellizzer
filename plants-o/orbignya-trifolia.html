Orbignya trifolia

Orbignya trifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds. These seeds, known as Brazil nuts, are enclosed in a hard shell and are rich in nutrients.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food for various wildlife species.
