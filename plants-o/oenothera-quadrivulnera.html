Oenothera quadrivulnera

Oenothera quadrivulnera, commonly known as the four-veined evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oily seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Oenothera quadrivulnera is part of the Onagraceae family, which includes other species of evening primroses.
