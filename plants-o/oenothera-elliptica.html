Oenothera elliptica

Oenothera elliptica, commonly known as the elliptic evening primrose, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening. The plant has narrow, elliptical leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Oenothera elliptica is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for nocturnal pollinators like moths.
