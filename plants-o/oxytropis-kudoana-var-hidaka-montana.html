Oxytropis kudoana var. hidaka-montana

Oxytropis kudoana var. hidaka-montana, commonly known as the Hidaka mountain milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to Japan.

It is characterized by its erect, spindly stems and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and is adapted to grow in rocky, alpine environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions with cool, temperate climates.

Oxytropis kudoana var. hidaka-montana plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
