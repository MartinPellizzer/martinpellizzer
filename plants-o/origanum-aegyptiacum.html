Origanum aegyptiacum

Origanum aegyptiacum, commonly known as Egyptian oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its essential oils contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its strong flavor and therapeutic properties.
