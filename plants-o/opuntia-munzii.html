Opuntia munzii

Opuntia munzii, commonly known as the Munz's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems, which are covered in clusters of small, spiny areoles. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tunas, is edible and typically appears in late summer.

Opuntia munzii is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.
