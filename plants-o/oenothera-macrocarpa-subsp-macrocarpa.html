Oenothera macrocarpa subsp. macrocarpa

Oenothera macrocarpa subsp.

macrocarpa, commonly known as the showy milkweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States. It features showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.

The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with toothed margins. Its milky sap is characteristic of the genus Oenothera, and it thrives in moist to wet soils in woodland or swampy areas.

It is an important host plant for the monarch butterfly.
