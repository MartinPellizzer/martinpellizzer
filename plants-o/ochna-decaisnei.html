Ochna decaisnei

Ochna decaisnei, commonly known as the African yellowwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. Ochna decaisnei is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in woodland and savanna ecosystems.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
