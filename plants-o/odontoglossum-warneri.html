Odontoglossum warneri

Odontoglossum warneri, commonly known as the Warner's orchid, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous plant with a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with intricate patterns. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Odontoglossum warneri is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
