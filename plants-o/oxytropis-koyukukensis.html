Oxytropis koyukukensis

Oxytropis koyukukensis, commonly known as Koyukuk milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for herbivores in its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
