Oxalis stricta f. viridiflora

Oxalis stricta f. viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered wood sorrel, is a flowering plant characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves and small, delicate flowers.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is native to North America, particularly in regions with moist, sandy soils. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges, often forming dense mats. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a distinctive V-shaped notch, and the flowers are usually greenish-yellow with five petals.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
