Otiophora angolensis

Otiophora angolensis, commonly known as the Angola dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and linear leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in dry, arid environments. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands.

Otiophora angolensis is noted for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
