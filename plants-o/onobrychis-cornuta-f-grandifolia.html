Onobrychis cornuta f. grandifolia

Onobrychis cornuta f. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered burclover, is a variant of the burclover species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect, branched stems and trifoliate leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by spiny, pod-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for ecological studies and traditional herbal uses.
