Ophioglossum mononeuron

Ophioglossum mononeuron, commonly known as the single-veined adder's mouth, is a rare and ancient fern-like plant belonging to the family Ophioglossaceae.

It is characterized by its unique, single central vein running the length of its leaves, which are typically lance-shaped and have a distinctive, almost prehistoric appearance. The plant produces sporangia in specialized structures called sporangium clusters, which are located at the base of the leaf. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in temperate regions.

Due to its limited distribution and conservation status, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
