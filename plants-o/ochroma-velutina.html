Ochroma velutina

Ochroma velutina, commonly known as the kapok tree, is a large tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and can grow up to 50 meters tall. The tree produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that are followed by long, pendulous pods containing fluffy, cotton-like seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

The plant is notable for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
