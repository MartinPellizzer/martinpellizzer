Omphalogramma tibeticum

Omphalogramma tibeticum, commonly known as the Tibetan starwort, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to extreme climatic conditions. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, which helps reduce water loss, while its flowers are small and clustered.

Omphalogramma tibeticum is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Tibetan medicine.
