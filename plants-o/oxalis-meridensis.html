Oxalis meridensis

Oxalis meridensis, commonly known as the midday oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves and vibrant yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.

Oxalis meridensis is also valued for its ability to tolerate drought conditions once established.
