Oxalis sericea

Oxalis sericea, commonly known as silverleaf wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with a silvery-white underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive and easy-to-grow plant for gardens and natural landscapes.
