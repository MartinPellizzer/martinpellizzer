Osbeckia simsii

Osbeckia simsii, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada fuchsia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in rocky, well-drained soils, often found in alpine and subalpine regions. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a fine, white pubescence, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Osbeckia simsii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
