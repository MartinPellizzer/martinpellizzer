Opuntia llanos-de-huanta

Opuntia llanos-de-huanta, commonly known as the Huanta flatlands prickly pear, is a cactus native to the Andean regions of Peru.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems that are covered in spines and are adapted to store water. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers, which are typically followed by edible fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing typical succulent features for desert survival.

Its structure and growth habits reflect its evolutionary adaptation to the harsh, dry conditions of its native habitat.
