Oxalis fruticula

Oxalis fruticula, commonly known as the creeping wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial with trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a distinctive appearance, while its flowers bloom in spring.

Oxalis fruticula is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to form dense ground covers.
