Oxytropis vermicularis

Oxytropis vermicularis, commonly known as narrowleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, compound leaves. The plant produces yellow or pale purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Oxytropis vermicularis is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it ecologically significant in certain habitats.
