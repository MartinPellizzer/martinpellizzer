Onites

Onites, commonly known as the "sugar bush," is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, oval leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. Onites thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

Its ecological importance includes providing food for wildlife and supporting pollinators.
