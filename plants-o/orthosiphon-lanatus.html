Orthosiphon lanatus

Orthosiphon lanatus, commonly known as the hairy pug or Indian nettle, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its hairy, square stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in spikes.

Orthosiphon lanatus is often used in traditional medicine for its purported diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.
