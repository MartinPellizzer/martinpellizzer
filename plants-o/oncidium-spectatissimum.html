Oncidium spectatissimum

Oncidium spectatissimum, commonly known as the showy orchid, is a tropical orchid species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, long-lasting inflorescences that display a variety of colors, including yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air. Its flowers are spurred and have a distinctive, intricate structure that aids in pollination by specific insects.

Oncidium spectatissimum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
