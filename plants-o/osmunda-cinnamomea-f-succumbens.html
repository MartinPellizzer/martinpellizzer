Osmunda cinnamomea f. succumbens

Osmunda cinnamomea f. succumbens, commonly known as the cinnamon fern, is a fern species characterized by its distinctive cinnamon-colored fiddleheads.

It belongs to the family Osmundaceae and is native to eastern North America. The plant features a tall, slender rhizome with broad, feather-like fronds that unfurl in a spiral pattern. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.

This fern thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
