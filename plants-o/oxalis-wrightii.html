Oxalis wrightii

Oxalis wrightii, commonly known as Wright's wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Oxalis wrightii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
