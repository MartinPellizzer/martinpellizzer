Opuntia martiniana

Opuntia martiniana, commonly known as the Mexican prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in sharp spines and woolly areoles. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit, known as a tuna, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

Opuntia martiniana is drought-tolerant and thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.
