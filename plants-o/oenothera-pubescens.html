Oenothera pubescens

Oenothera pubescens, commonly known as the downy evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow flowers that open in the evening. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oval seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Oenothera pubescens is a member of the Onagraceae family and is known for its ecological importance to pollinators.
