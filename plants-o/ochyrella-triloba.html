Ochyrella triloba

Ochyrella triloba, commonly known as the three-lobed orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive three-lobed leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are often mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to enhance nutrient absorption.

Ochyrella triloba is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
