Ocotea nicaraguensis

Ocotea nicaraguensis, commonly known as the Nicaraguan ocotea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue berries that are often used in traditional medicine.

The tree is valued for its aromatic wood and is often found in humid lowland forests.
