Oxytropis masarensis

Oxytropis masarensis, commonly known as the Madagascar locust bean, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pods containing edible seeds. Oxytropis masarensis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance through its deep root system.

It plays a role in local ecosystems and has traditional uses in some indigenous communities.
