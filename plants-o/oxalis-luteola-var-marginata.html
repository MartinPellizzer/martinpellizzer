Oxalis luteola var. marginata

Oxalis luteola var. marginata, commonly known as the yellow wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It features trifoliate leaves with yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a shallow root system and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in woodland areas and along streambanks.

Its foliage and flowers are adorned with distinctive white or pale yellow margins on the leaves and petals.
