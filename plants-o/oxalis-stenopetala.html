Oxalis stenopetala

Oxalis stenopetala, commonly known as the narrow-petaled wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features trifoliate leaves with three heart-shaped leaflets and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of yellow or orange. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests or woodlands. Its foliage and flowers are often used in ornamental gardening due to their attractive appearance.

Oxalis stenopetala is also valued for its ability to thrive in partial shade and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
