Ononis pubescens

Ononis pubescens, commonly known as hairy bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its leaves are alternate, oval-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its fruits are small, dry achenes.

Ononis pubescens is also known for its historical use in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.
