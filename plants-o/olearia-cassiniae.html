Olearia cassiniae

Olearia cassiniae, commonly known as the Titi Islands daisy, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and woolly, silver-green foliage. The plant typically grows in coastal and subalpine regions, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its flowers are small, white to pale yellow, and appear in clusters, often surrounded by a ring of bracts.

Olearia cassiniae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
