Ophiorrhiza fusca

Ophiorrhiza fusca, commonly known as the red sandalwood or red mangrove, is a flowering plant species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small, shrub-like plant that typically grows in coastal areas and wetlands, often forming dense thickets. The plant has small, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a distinctive reddish color, and it relies on insects for pollination.

Ophiorrhiza fusca plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
