Orthoraphium roylei

Orthoraphium roylei, commonly known as the Kashmir rose, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often branched.

The Kashmir rose is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its cultural significance in the regions where it is found.
