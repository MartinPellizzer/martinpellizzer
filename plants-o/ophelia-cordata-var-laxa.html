Ophelia cordata var. laxa

Ophelia cordata var. laxa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water plantain, is a aquatic perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically submerged or floating, with white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and streams. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, which can help stabilize sediment.

The species is often used in water gardens and wetland restoration projects due to its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
