Oncocyclus helena

Oncocyclus helena, commonly known as the Helen's onion, is a bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces striking, showy flowers with purple and white petals that bloom in late spring. The plant has a short, upright stem and is characterized by its fragrant, globe-shaped blooms. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Oncocyclus helena is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
