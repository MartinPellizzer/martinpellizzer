Ocotea pomaderrioides

Ocotea pomaderrioides, commonly known as the red cedar or *Cedrela* species, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and laurel. The tree is characterized by its dark, aromatic bark and glossy, elliptical leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and fine grain, making it a sought-after material in furniture and construction.

The plant also produces small, fragrant flowers and dark blue-black berries that attract various wildlife.
