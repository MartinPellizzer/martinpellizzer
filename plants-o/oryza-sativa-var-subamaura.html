Oryza sativa var. subamaura

Oryza sativa var. subamaura, commonly known as the subamaura rice, is a variant of rice characterized by its unique botanical features.

It is a grass species belonging to the Poaceae family, and it typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences. Its leaves are usually narrow and have a smooth texture, while the grains are smaller compared to other rice varieties.

This variety is valued for its distinct appearance and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes as well as for its potential in food production.
