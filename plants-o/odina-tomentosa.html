Odina tomentosa

Odina tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly odina, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly covering on its stems and leaves, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical forests.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and plant enthusiasts alike.
