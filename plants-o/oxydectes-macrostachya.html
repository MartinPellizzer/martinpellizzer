Oxydectes macrostachya

Oxydectes macrostachya, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that resemble flames, composed of numerous small, bright red or orange flowers. The plant has compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets and produces woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
