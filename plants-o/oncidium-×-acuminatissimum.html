Oncidium × acuminatissimum

Oncidium × acuminatissimum, commonly known as the yellow butterfly orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow and green striped flowers, which resemble the wings of a butterfly. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in its natural habitat. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Oncidium × acuminatissimum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its long-lasting, showy blooms.
