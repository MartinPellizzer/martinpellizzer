Ottochloa nodosa var. micrantha

Ottochloa nodosa var. micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered nodding grass, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, nodding inflorescences with white to pale blue flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in moist, well-drained soils in open habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in temperate climates with moderate rainfall.
