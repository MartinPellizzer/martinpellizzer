Ocimum laxiflorum

Ocimum laxiflorum, commonly known as the slenderflower basil, is a species of flowering plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, pale blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate in shape and have a mild, peppery scent when crushed.

Ocimum laxiflorum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of essential oils.
