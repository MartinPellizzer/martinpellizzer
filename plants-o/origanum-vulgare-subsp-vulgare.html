Origanum vulgare subsp. vulgare

Origanum vulgare subsp.

vulgare, commonly known as oregano, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly fuzzy, evergreen leaves and small, pale purple flowers.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for Mediterranean climates. Its essential oils, rich in compounds like carvacrol and thymol, contribute to its strong aroma and medicinal properties.

Oregano is widely used in culinary applications and traditional herbal remedies.
