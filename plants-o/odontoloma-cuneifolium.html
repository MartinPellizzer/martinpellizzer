Odontoloma cuneifolium

Odontoloma cuneifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leafed toothed grass, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive toothed margin, giving it its common name. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Odontoloma cuneifolium is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
