Oenothera capillifolia subsp. capillifolia

Oenothera capillifolia subsp.

capillifolia, commonly known as the slender state evening primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and showy yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the evening.

The plant has opposite, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, elliptical seeds.

This species is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.
