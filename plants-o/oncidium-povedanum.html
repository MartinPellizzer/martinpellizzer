Oncidium povedanum

Oncidium povedanum, commonly known as the Poveda's orchid, is a species native to South America, particularly found in Colombia and Ecuador.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a compact, pseudobulbous structure that supports its growth in shaded, humid environments. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires consistent moisture and indirect sunlight for optimal growth.

Oncidium povedanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
