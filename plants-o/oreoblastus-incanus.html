Oreoblastus incanus

Oreoblastus incanus, commonly known as the silver leaf orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Cape Floristic Region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in dense, fine hairs that help reduce water loss in its arid habitat. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Oreoblastus incanus is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its unique appearance and ecological significance in its native environment.
