Ononis antiquorum var. lanata

Ononis antiquorum var. lanata, commonly known as woolly catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, woolly stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
