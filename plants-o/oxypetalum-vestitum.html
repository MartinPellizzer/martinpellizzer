Oxypetalum vestitum

Oxypetalum vestitum, commonly known as the velvetleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along streambanks. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Oxypetalum vestitum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
