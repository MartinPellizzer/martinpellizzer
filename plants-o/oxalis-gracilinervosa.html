Oxalis gracilinervosa

Oxalis gracilinervosa, commonly known as the slender-veined wood sorrel, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It features trifoliate leaves with delicate, light green veins and small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its stems are often hairy, and the leaves are usually arranged alternately along the stem.

Oxalis gracilinervosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
