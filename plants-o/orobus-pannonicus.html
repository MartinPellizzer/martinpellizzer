Orobus pannonicus

Orobus pannonicus, commonly known as the Pannonian bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are erect and branching.

Orobus pannonicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
