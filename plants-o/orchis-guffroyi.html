Orchis guffroyi

Orchis guffroyi, commonly known as the Guffroy's orchid, is a rare and elusive species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodland habitats, preferring well-drained, acidic soils. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and it is known for its delicate, grass-like leaves.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Orchis guffroyi is considered a conservation concern in many areas.
