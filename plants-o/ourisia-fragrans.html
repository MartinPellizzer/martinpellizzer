Ourisia fragrans

Ourisia fragrans, commonly known as the sweet-scented broom, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds, typical of the legume family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Its fragrant flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
