Osmanthus austrocaledonicus

Osmanthus austrocaledonicus, commonly known as the New Caledonian osmanthus, is a flowering plant native to the island of New Caledonia.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, small yellow or white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Osmanthus austrocaledonicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine in some regions.
