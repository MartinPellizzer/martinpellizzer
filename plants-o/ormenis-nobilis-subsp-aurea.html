Ormenis nobilis subsp. aurea

Ormenis nobilis subsp.

aurea, commonly known as the golden ormenis, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe. It features slender, upright stems covered in small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green color.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, oval-shaped fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to Mediterranean climates.
