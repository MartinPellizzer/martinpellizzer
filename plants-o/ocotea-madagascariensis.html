Ocotea madagascariensis

Ocotea madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascan ocotea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments within humid forests, often reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a distinct aromatic scent when crushed.

The fruit is a small, dark drupe that contains a single seed, contributing to its ecological role in forest ecosystems.
