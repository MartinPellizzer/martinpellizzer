Orchidantha foetida

Orchidantha foetida, commonly known as the stinking orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique, often fragrant or foul-smelling flowers, which can vary in color from white to purple. Native to tropical regions, this plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Due to its distinctive odor and appearance, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
