Ottelia acuminata var. crispa

Ottelia acuminata var. crispa, commonly known as the curly waterwheel, is a semi-aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, branching stems that often grow in shallow water or moist soil. The plant's leaves are narrow, elongated, and have a distinctive wavy or curled margin, giving it its common name. Its small, white flowers appear in clusters and are typically submerged or floating.

Ottelia acuminata var. crispa is known for its ability to thrive in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.
