Oxalis cruzeirensis

Oxalis cruzeirensis, commonly known as the Brazilian wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Oxalis cruzeirensis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and indoor plant collections.
