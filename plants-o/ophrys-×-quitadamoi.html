Ophrys × quitadamoi

Ophrys × quitadamoi, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly in southern Europe. The plant is characterized by its striking, bee-like flowers that mimic the appearance of female bees to attract male bees for pollination. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of flowers, each with a complex structure that includes a labellum resembling a bee's body.

Ophrys × quitadamoi is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological studies related to plant-pollinator interactions.
