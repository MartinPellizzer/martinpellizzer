Ocotea morae

Ocotea morae, commonly known as Moras's ocotea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves and small, clustered flowers. Its wood is valued for its durability and fragrance, often used in traditional crafts.

Ocotea morae thrives in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests.
