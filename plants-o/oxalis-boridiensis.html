Oxalis boridiensis

Oxalis boridiensis, commonly known as the Boridi's wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bead-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Oxalis boridiensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to add a delicate touch to garden landscapes.
