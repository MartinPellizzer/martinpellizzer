Odontoglossum festatum

Odontoglossum festatum, commonly known as the festal orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically blooms in the spring and summer, with flowers arranged in racemes along the stem. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Odontoglossum festatum is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts due to its beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
