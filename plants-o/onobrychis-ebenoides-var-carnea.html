Onobrychis ebenoides var. carnea

Onobrychis ebenoides var. carnea, commonly known as red clover, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a legume characterized by its trifoliate leaves and spikes of pink to reddish-purple flowers. The plant has a deep root system that helps in nitrogen fixation, making it valuable for soil enrichment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in forage and as a cover crop.

Its ornamental value also makes it a popular choice in gardens and landscaping.
