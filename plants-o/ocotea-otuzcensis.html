Ocotea otuzcensis

Ocotea otuzcensis, commonly known as the Mexican laurel, is a member of the Lauraceae family and is native to Mexico.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, fragrant flowers. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife.

The tree is also valued for its timber and has been studied for potential medicinal properties.
