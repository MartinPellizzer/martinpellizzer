Osmanthus reticulatus

Osmanthus reticulatus, commonly known as the Chinese sweet osmanthus, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late autumn. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves with a rough texture and a distinct reticulate pattern on the underside. Its flowers are widely used in traditional Chinese medicine and as a flavoring agent in teas and confections.

The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
