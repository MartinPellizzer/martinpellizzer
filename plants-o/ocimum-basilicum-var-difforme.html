Ocimum basilicum var. difforme

Ocimum basilicum var. difforme, commonly known as purple basil, is a variant of the common basil plant characterized by its striking purple foliage.

This plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic herbs and ornamental value. The leaves are typically ovate with a slightly serrated edge, and they emit a strong, sweet scent when crushed. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Purple basil is often used in culinary applications and as a decorative plant in gardens.
