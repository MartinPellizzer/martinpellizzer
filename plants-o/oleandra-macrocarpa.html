Oleandra macrocarpa

Oleandra macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited oleander, is a striking ornamental plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically blooming in summer. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release numerous small, fluffy seeds.

Oleandra macrocarpa is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
