Oplismenus compositus f. vittatus

Oplismenus compositus f. vittatus, commonly known as the variegated horse-tail, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to southern China.

It features distinctive, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often marked with striking yellow or white variegation. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in clusters during the spring. Its foliage is highly ornamental, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, requiring minimal maintenance once established.
