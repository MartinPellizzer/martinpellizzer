Omphalobium micranthum

Omphalobium micranthum, commonly known as the small-flowered oxytropis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces slender, erect stems.

It is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas, where it contributes to soil stabilization through its extensive root system.
