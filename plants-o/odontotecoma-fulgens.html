Odontotecoma fulgens

Odontotecoma fulgens, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a slender, upright stem with lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. Its flowers are highly fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Odontotecoma fulgens thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
