Oxydectes laccifera

Oxydectes laccifera, commonly known as the Indian velvet tamarind, is a tropical plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into elongated, pod-like fruits. The fruits contain several seeds encased in a fibrous, woody pulp. The plant is native to regions of Southeast Asia and is often found in moist, tropical forests.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
