Oxygonum atriplicifolium

Oxygonum atriplicifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowleaf, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southern United States.

It features elongated, arrow-shaped leaves that are often grayish-green in color, with a distinctive silvery underside. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or marshy environments.

Its foliage is commonly used in floral arrangements due to its unique appearance and durability.
