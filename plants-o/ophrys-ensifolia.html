Ophrys ensifolia

Ophrys ensifolia, commonly known as the sword orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, often strikingly mimic-like flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas, and it thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pale green or brown, with a distinctive, sword-shaped lip that plays a key role in its pollination strategy.

Ophrys ensifolia is notable for its specialized relationship with certain pollinators, particularly bees, due to its intricate floral morphology.
