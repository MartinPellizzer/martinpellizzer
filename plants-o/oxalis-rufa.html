Oxalis rufa

Oxalis rufa, commonly known as the redwood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds.

Oxalis rufa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
