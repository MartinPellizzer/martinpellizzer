Ophiocaulon firingalavense

Ophiocaulon firingalavense, commonly known as the Fireleaf Plant, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, flame-like foliage, with leaves that display a vivid mix of red, orange, and yellow hues. Its flowers, which are often overlooked, are small and clustered in dense inflorescences, usually in shades of white or pale pink.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for tropical gardens and indoor cultivation.
