Oenothera hookeri var. semiglabra

Oenothera hookeri var. semiglabra, commonly known as the plains evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the central and western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats like grasslands and sandy soils. Its stems are usually erect and branching, with lance-shaped leaves that are hairy but less so than the typical variety.

The species is known for its adaptability and resilience in arid environments.
