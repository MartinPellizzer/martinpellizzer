Onobrychis viciifolia subsp. arenaria

Onobrychis viciifolia subsp.

arenaria, commonly known as the sand sainfoin, is a perennial legume native to arid and semi-arid regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in racemes.

The plant is adapted to sandy and gravelly soils, making it a hardy species suitable for harsh environments. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in drought conditions.

This subspecies is often used in ecological restoration and as a forage plant for livestock.
