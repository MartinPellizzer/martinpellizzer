Odontarrhena syriaca

Odontarrhena syriaca, commonly known as the Syrian toothbrush, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 60 cm in height. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive rough texture.

Odontarrhena syriaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
