Orchis × penzigiana nothosubsp. jailae

Orchis × penzigiana nothosubsp.

jailae, commonly known as the Penzig orchid, is a hybrid species native to the mountainous regions of the Alps. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers with intricate patterns.

The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its morphology includes a tall, erect stem with a single or few flower spikes.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in its native range.
