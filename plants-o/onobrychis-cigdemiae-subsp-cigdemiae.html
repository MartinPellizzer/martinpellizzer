Onobrychis cigdemiae subsp. cigdemiae

Onobrychis cigdemiae subsp.

cigdemiae, commonly known as the Armenian clover, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is adapted to temperate climates. It is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
