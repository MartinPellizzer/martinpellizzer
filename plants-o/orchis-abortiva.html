Orchis abortiva

Orchis abortiva, commonly known as the aborted orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear lance-shaped leaves and a single, showy spike of flowers. The flowers are typically violet or purple in color, with a distinctive three-lobed lip that aids in pollination. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
