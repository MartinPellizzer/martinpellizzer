Odontoglossum cristatellum f. argus

Odontoglossum cristatellum f. argus, commonly known as the Argus orchid, is a hybrid orchid native to the high altitudes of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, intricate flower structure with a distinctive pattern resembling the eyes of an argus pheasant. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or green with contrasting purple or red markings, and it blooms during the cooler months.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique and striking appearance.
