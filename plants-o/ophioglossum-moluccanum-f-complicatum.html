Ophioglossum moluccanum f. complicatum

Ophioglossum moluccanum f. complicatum, commonly known as the Moluccan adder's mouth fern, is a rare and distinctive fern species characterized by its unique morphology.

It features a short, thick rhizome with a single, large, leathery, ovate leaf that is often deeply divided into complex lobes. The leaf blade is typically dark green and has a rough, textured surface, contributing to its striking appearance. This fern is native to parts of Southeast Asia, particularly the Molucca Islands, and thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
