Oncidium auriferum

Oncidium auriferum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow and green flowers, which resemble golden coins, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Oncidium auriferum is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
