Oenothera albida

Oenothera albida, commonly known as white evening primrose, is a species of flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats like prairies and sandy soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often branched.

Oenothera albida is part of the evening primrose family (Onagraceae) and is known for its ecological importance in supporting pollinator populations.
