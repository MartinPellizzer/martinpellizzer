Otoglyphis pubescens subsp. maroccana

Otoglyphis pubescens subsp.

maroccana, commonly known as the Moroccan velvetleaf, is a perennial herb native to North Africa. It is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in the summer months.

The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along riverbanks. It has a robust root system that allows it to survive in drought conditions.

The species is of interest in botanical studies due to its adaptive features and ecological significance in its native habitat.
