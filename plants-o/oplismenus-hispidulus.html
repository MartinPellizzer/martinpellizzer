Oplismenus hispidulus

Oplismenus hispidulus, commonly known as roughleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and conical flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and marshes. Its flowers are usually purple to pink, and it blooms from late summer into early fall.

Oplismenus hispidulus is often found in the eastern United States and parts of Canada.
