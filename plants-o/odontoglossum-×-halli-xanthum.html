Odontoglossum × halli-xanthum

Odontoglossum × halli-xanthum, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a natural hybrid between Odontoglossum halli and Odontoglossum xanthum.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as an example of natural hybridization in the orchid genus.
