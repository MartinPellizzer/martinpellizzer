Ocimum zeylanicum

Ocimum zeylanicum, commonly known as cinnamon basil, is a flowering plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia, including India, and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and purple flowers. The plant is widely cultivated for its fragrant leaves, which are used in culinary, medicinal, and aromatic applications. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ocimum zeylanicum is also valued for its essential oils, which contain compounds like linalool and eugenol.
