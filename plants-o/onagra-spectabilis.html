Onagra spectabilis

Onagra spectabilis, commonly known as the showy sunflower, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring rough, hairy stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. Its vibrant yellow ray florets are surrounded by a cluster of yellow to orange disk florets, which are rich in pollen.

Onagra spectabilis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in grasslands and rocky soils.
