Oxytropis susamyrensis

Oxytropis susamyrensis, commonly known as the Karakum oxytropis, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow to brown flowers in late spring, which are clustered in racemate inflorescences. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in steppes and deserts.

Oxytropis susamyrensis is known for its deep root system, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
