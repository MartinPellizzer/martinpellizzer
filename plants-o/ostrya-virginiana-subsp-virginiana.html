Ostrya virginiana subsp. virginiana

Ostrya virginiana subsp.

virginiana, commonly known as the American hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America. It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its small, ovate leaves and smooth, gray bark.

The tree produces small, yellowish-green catkins in early spring, followed by winged samaras in the fall. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and can reach heights of 10 to 30 meters.

This subspecies is often found in mixed hardwood forests and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
