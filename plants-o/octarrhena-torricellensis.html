Octarrhena torricellensis

Octarrhena torricellensis, commonly known as the Torricelli mountain lily, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and purple. The plant is a herbaceous perennial with narrow, grass-like leaves that form dense clumps. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
