Oryza sativa var. binamban

Oryza sativa var. binamban, commonly known as the Binamban rice, is a traditional variety of rice native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its short, slender grains and typically exhibits a white to slightly yellowish color when mature. The plant grows in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring warm temperatures and ample water for optimal growth. It is often cultivated in flooded fields, a method known as paddies, which helps control weeds and provides necessary moisture.

This variety is valued for its unique flavor and is used in both local and regional cuisines.
