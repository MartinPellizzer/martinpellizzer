Ossaea brachystachya

Ossaea brachystachya, commonly known as the short-stalked feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces delicate, feathery inflorescences that are typically arranged in clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Ossaea brachystachya plays a role in stabilizing soils in its native habitat and is sometimes used in restoration projects due to its hardy nature.
