Olea ovalis

Olea ovalis, commonly known as the Indian olive, is a small evergreen tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by small, spherical fruits that turn from green to black as they mature. Olea ovalis is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
