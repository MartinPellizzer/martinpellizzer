Orthopogon burmanni

Orthopogon burmanni, commonly known as the Burmann's grass, is a member of the Poaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in moist, shaded environments. The plant features slender stems and elongated, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet arrangement, typical of many grass species.

Orthopogon burmanni is noted for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity and its potential use in traditional medicine.
