Oncidium cruciferum

Oncidium cruciferum, commonly known as the Christmas orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters during the winter months. The plant has strap-like, dark green leaves and produces showy, yellowish-green to white flowers with prominent red or purple lips. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate watering to prevent root rot.

Oncidium cruciferum is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its long-lasting blooms and ease of care.
