Oligostachyum pulchellum

Oligostachyum pulchellum, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often a striking golden-green color. The plant produces panicles of small, inconspicuous flowers, which are typically greenish or brownish in color. It is a perennial herb that thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is cultivated in some horticultural settings.
