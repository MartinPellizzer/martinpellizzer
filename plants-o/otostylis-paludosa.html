Otostylis paludosa

Otostylis paludosa, commonly known as the swampy grass or marsh grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in marshy areas, swamps, and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist to water-saturated soils. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often submerged or floating, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. Otostylis paludosa is adapted to fluctuating water levels and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems.
