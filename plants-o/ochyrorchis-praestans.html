Ochyrorchis praestans

Ochyrorchis praestans, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a striking terrestrial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

Ochyrorchis praestans is a short-lived perennial, often requiring specific conditions to thrive and reproduce successfully.
