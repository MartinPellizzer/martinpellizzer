Oxytropis crassiuscula

Oxytropis crassiuscula, commonly known as the broadleaf locoweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert environments.

Oxytropis crassiuscula is known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock.
