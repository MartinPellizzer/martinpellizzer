Ormosia parvifolia

Ormosia parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved ormosia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. Ormosia parvifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for shade gardens and as a specimen tree in tropical landscapes.
