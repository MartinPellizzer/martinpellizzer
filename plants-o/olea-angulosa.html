Olea angulosa

Olea angulosa, commonly known as the angular olive, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, angular leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by small, spherical fruits. Olea angulosa is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
