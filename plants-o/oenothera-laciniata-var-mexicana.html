Oenothera laciniata var. mexicana

Oenothera laciniata var. mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican willow herb, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a serrated edge, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This species is often found in rocky or sandy soils in arid or semi-arid regions.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine by some indigenous communities.
