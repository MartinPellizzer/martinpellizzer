Oncidium dimorphum

Oncidium dimorphum, commonly known as the yellow butterfly orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow with brown or purple markings, resembling butterflies. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are strap-like and evergreen, providing a lush backdrop for its vibrant blooms.

Oncidium dimorphum thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil with regular watering.
