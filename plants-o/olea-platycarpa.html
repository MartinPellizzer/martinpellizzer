Olea platycarpa

Olea platycarpa, commonly known as the broad-fruited olive, is a species of flowering plant in the Oleaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into spherical, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

Olea platycarpa is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in horticulture.
