Orbea orientalis

Orbea orientalis, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a striking succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are typically thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in arid environments.

Orbea orientalis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
