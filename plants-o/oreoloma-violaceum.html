Oreoloma violaceum

Oreoloma violaceum, commonly known as the violet orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
