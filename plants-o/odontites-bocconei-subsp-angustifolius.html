Odontites bocconei subsp. angustifolius

Odontites bocconei subsp.

angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved toothed violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It features a basal rosette of lanceolate, often hairy leaves that are typically narrower than those of the main species.

The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers with five petals, arranged in a loose raceme. It thrives in moist, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous habitats.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its range.
