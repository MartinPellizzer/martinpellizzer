Ophryosporus anomalus

Ophryosporus anomalus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, wavy-edged leaves. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that develop into puffball-like seed heads. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

Ophryosporus anomalus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
