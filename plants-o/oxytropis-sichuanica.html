Oxytropis sichuanica

Oxytropis sichuanica, commonly known as Sichuan locoweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Sichuan, China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically purple or pink in color. It is adapted to grow in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

Oxytropis sichuanica is also known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock.
