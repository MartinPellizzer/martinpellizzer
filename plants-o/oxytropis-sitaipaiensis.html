Oxytropis sitaipaiensis

Oxytropis sitaipaiensis, commonly known as the Sitaipai locoweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia, particularly in China. The plant is characterized by its erect, spiny stems and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or pink. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often avoided by grazing animals.
