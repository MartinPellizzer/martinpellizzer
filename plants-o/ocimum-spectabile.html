Ocimum spectabile

Ocimum spectabile, commonly known as purple basil, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers and aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to gardens.

Ocimum spectabile is often used in ornamental gardening and can also be harvested for its fragrant leaves.
