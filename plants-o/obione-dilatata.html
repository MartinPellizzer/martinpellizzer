Obione dilatata

Obione dilatata, commonly known as the broadleaf obione, is a perennial plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its fern-like foliage. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.
