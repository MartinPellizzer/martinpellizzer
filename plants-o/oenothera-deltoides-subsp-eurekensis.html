Oenothera deltoides subsp. eurekensis

Oenothera deltoides subsp.

eurekensis, commonly known as the Eureka evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oily seeds. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs. Oenothera deltoides subsp.

eurekensis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.
