Ophiorrhiza bracteolata

Ophiorrhiza bracteolata, commonly known as the bracteolate mangrove, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small shrub that thrives in saline and brackish water environments, often found in mangrove forests. The plant has small, opposite leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, yellowish flowers followed by capsule fruits. Its root system is adapted to tolerate waterlogged and saline soils, making it an important component of coastal ecosystems.

Ophiorrhiza bracteolata plays a role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
