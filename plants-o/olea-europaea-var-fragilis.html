Olea europaea var. fragilis

Olea europaea var. fragilis, commonly known as the Mediterranean olive tree, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its leathery, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by the characteristic olive fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

This variety is known for its hardiness and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance.
