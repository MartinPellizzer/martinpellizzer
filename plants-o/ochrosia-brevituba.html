Ochrosia brevituba

Ochrosia brevituba, commonly known as the short-stemmed ochrosia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are narrow and strap-like, with a glossy green surface.

Ochrosia brevituba is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
