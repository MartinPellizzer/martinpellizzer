Opanea trinervia

Opanea trinervia, commonly known as the slender starling grass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along waterways. Its leaves are narrow and have a distinct three-veined structure, which is a key identifying feature.

Opanea trinervia plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
