Ocimum waterbergense

Ocimum waterbergense, commonly known as Waterberg basil, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are fragrant and often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Ocimum waterbergense is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
