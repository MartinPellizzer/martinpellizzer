Onobrychis cornuta subsp. cornuta

Onobrychis cornuta subsp.

cornuta, commonly known as the horned vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its distinctive, horn-like stipules on the leaves and its trifoliate leaflets.

The plant produces small, yellow to reddish flowers that are clustered in racemes, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
