Oenothera stenopetala

Oenothera stenopetala, commonly known as the narrow-petaled evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that open in the evening and close by morning. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oval seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Oenothera stenopetala is a member of the Onagraceae family and is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
