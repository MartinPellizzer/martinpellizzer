Ocotea oligantha

Ocotea oligantha, commonly known as the white ocotea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and is often found in the understory of dense forests. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, with a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

The fruit is a small, dark blue drupe that contains a single seed, and the tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
