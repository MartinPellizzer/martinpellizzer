Oerstedina suffrutescens

Oerstedina suffrutescens, commonly known as the African buttercup, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial that typically reaches a height of about 10-30 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are lobed or divided, and its flowers are yellow with five petals arranged in a cup-like shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
