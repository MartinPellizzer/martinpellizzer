Oxalis lindaviana

Oxalis lindaviana, commonly known as the Lindaviana wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces edible, sour-tasting berries that are often used in local cuisines. Oxalis lindaviana thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a unique and attractive plant.
