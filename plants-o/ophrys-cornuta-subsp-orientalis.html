Ophrys cornuta subsp. orientalis

Ophrys cornuta subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as the Eastern Horned Orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Eastern Europe and Western Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive, bee-mimicking flower structure, which features a prominent central spur and intricate patterns that attract pollinators.

The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brownish in color, with a complex morphology that enhances its pollination efficiency.

The subspecies is notable for its specialized adaptations and is considered a rare and protected species in many regions.
