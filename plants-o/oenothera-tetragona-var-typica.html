Oenothera tetragona var. typica

Oenothera tetragona var. typica, commonly known as the four-angle evening primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, elliptical seeds. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
