Ophrys aranifera var. pseudospeculum

Ophrys aranifera var. pseudospeculum, commonly known as the mimic orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which closely resembles a female bee, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant produces a single, showy flower with a complex lip that mimics the appearance and scent of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. It thrives in calcareous soils and requires a specific microclimate with high humidity and partial shade.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
