Ocimum somaliense

Ocimum somaliense, commonly known as Somali basil, is a species of flowering plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to the Horn of Africa, particularly Somalia, and thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its essential oils contain compounds like linalool and eugenol, which contribute to its medicinal and aromatic properties.
