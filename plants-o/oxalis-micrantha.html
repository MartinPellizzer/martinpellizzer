Oxalis micrantha

Oxalis micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive light green color and small, delicate white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas and along stream banks. Its foliage and flowers are often adorned with fine, hair-like structures, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Oxalis micrantha is valued in gardens for its aesthetic qualities and ability to thrive in partial shade.
