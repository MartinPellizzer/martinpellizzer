Ornithogalum spathaceum

Ornithogalum spathaceum, commonly known as the white bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a bulbous rootstock and produces tall, slender stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant is notable for its clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Ornithogalum spathaceum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
