Onopyxus nutans

Onopyxus nutans, commonly known as the hanging bellflower, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its pendulous, bell-shaped flowers that hang from the stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.
