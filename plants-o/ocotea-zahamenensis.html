Ocotea zahamenensis

Ocotea zahamenensis, commonly known as the Madagascar ocotea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage and smooth, dark bark. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded forest environments, often in the understory of primary rainforests. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy surface, while its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences.

The fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed, and the plant is valued for its essential oils and medicinal properties.
