Ophrys × semibombyliflora

Ophrys × semibombyliflora, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant that combines traits from its parent species, Ophrys bombyliflora and Ophrys semibombyliflora.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of female bees to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils. Its flowers are usually yellow to brown in color, with intricate patterns that aid in its deceptive pollination strategy.

This hybrid is often found in meadows and grasslands, where it plays a role in the local ecosystem.
