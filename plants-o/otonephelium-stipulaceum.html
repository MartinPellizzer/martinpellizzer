Otonephelium stipulaceum

Otonephelium stipulaceum, commonly known as the white mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for pollinators.
