Osmia glaberrima

Osmia glaberrima, commonly known as the hairy leaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its broad, hairy leaves and showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.

Osmia glaberrima is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
