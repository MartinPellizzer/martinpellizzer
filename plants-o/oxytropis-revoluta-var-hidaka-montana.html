Oxytropis revoluta var. hidaka-montana

Oxytropis revoluta var. hidaka-montana, commonly known as the Hidaka mountain milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the high-altitude regions of Japan.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale purple or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
