Ophrys fuciflora f. elongata

Ophrys fuciflora f. elongata, commonly known as the elongated bee orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its unique, bee-like floral structure, which features a prominent, hairy labellum that mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as woodlands and meadows, where it thrives in nutrient-rich soils. Its flowers are usually pale green to brownish in color, with intricate patterns that aid in its deceptive pollination strategy.

This orchid is of particular interest to botanists due to its specialized morphology and ecological niche.
