Oxalis schaeferi

Oxalis schaeferi, commonly known as the Schaefer's wood sorrel, is a species of flowering plant in the family Oxalidaceae.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive pattern of veins, and it produces small, delicate flowers in shades of yellow or white. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.

Oxalis schaeferi is typically evergreen, though it may shed its leaves during periods of drought or cold.
