Oxalis campylorhiza

Oxalis campylorhiza, commonly known as the hooked wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky areas. Its stems are often woody and can form dense colonies.

Oxalis campylorhiza is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique floral structure.
