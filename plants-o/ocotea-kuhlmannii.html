Ocotea kuhlmannii

Ocotea kuhlmannii, commonly known as the Kuhlmann's ocotea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in countries like Costa Rica and Panama. The plant is characterized by its aromatic evergreen leaves and small, clustered white flowers. Its fruit is a drupe that contains a single seed, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Ocotea kuhlmannii is valued for its wood, which is used in traditional crafts and as a source of essential oils.
