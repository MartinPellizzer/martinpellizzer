Oncidium cucullatum var. andigenum

Oncidium cucullatum var. andigenum, commonly known as the Andean orchid, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces striking, showy flowers with a distinctive greenish-yellow coloration. The plant typically features long, strap-like leaves and is often found in rocky or mountainous environments. It is a pseudobulbous orchid that thrives in cool, bright conditions with good air circulation.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique adaptations to high-altitude ecosystems and its role in local biodiversity.
