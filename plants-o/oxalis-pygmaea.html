Oxalis pygmaea

Oxalis pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to South America.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves with distinctive orange or red veins, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces delicate, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow or orange, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its compact size and ornamental value, Oxalis pygmaea is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and indoor settings.
