Orthosiphon heterophyllus

Orthosiphon heterophyllus, commonly known as Java tea or kaphi, is a member of the Lamiaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or violet in color. The plant is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves, which are used in traditional medicine and as a herbal tea. Its botanical features include hairy stems and a preference for moist, well-drained soils in tropical climates.

Orthosiphon heterophyllus is notable for its medicinal properties, particularly in the treatment of urinary tract infections and kidney-related ailments.
