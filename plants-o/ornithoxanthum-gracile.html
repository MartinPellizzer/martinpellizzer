Ornithoxanthum gracile

Ornithoxanthum gracile, commonly known as slender oxeye daisy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and thistles. The plant is characterized by its small, delicate yellow flowers with white ray florets, typically blooming in early summer. It grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils.

Ornithoxanthum gracile is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also a food source for various pollinators.
