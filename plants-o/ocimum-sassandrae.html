Ocimum sassandrae

Ocimum sassandrae, commonly known as the African basil, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and medicinal plants. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy texture. Its small, tubular flowers are typically purple or violet, arranged in whorled clusters.

Ocimum sassandrae is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
