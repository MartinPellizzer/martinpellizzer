Oncocalamus macrospathus

Oncocalamus macrospathus, commonly known as the giant bamboo or giant leeward bamboo, is a tall, slender grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its elongated, cylindrical stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated.

Oncocalamus macrospathus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance and structural form.
