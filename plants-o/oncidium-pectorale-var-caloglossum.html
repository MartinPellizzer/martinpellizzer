Oncidium pectorale var. caloglossum

Oncidium pectorale var. caloglossum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features showy, golden-yellow flowers with a distinctive red lip, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, exhibiting an epiphytic growth habit. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often arranged in a fan shape, and are capable of photosynthesis.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated in greenhouses and gardens.
