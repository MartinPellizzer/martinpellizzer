Oxytropis gorodkovii

Oxytropis gorodkovii, commonly known as the Siberian locoweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cold climates of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, compound leaves. The plant produces yellow to pale purple flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, arid environments.

Oxytropis gorodkovii is also known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock.
