Oenothera micrantha var. abramsii

Oenothera micrantha var. abramsii, commonly known as the Abrams' evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Onagraceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow to orange flowers that bloom at night. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds. Oenothera micrantha var. abramsii is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for nocturnal pollinators like moths.
