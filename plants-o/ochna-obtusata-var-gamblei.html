Ochna obtusata var. gamblei

Ochna obtusata var. gamblei, commonly known as the Gambler's ochna, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and simple, elliptic leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or wooded areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and unique fruiting structures.
