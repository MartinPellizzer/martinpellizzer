Oreophila meyeniana

Oreophila meyeniana, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant blossoms. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous terrain, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its flowers come in a range of colors, including white, yellow, and pink.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, Oreophila meyeniana is considered a rare and endangered species.
