Opuntia megacarpa

Opuntia megacarpa, commonly known as the giant barrel cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, thick, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 10 feet tall. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for both humans and animals. Its spines are modified leaves, providing protection from herbivores and reducing water loss.

Opuntia megacarpa is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its succulent stems and performing photosynthesis through its stems rather than leaves.
