Ochna obtusifolia

Ochna obtusifolia, commonly known as the African starwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, ovate leaves with rounded tips. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by woody, star-shaped fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Ochna obtusifolia is valued for its ornamental appearance and has traditional uses in some regions.
