Origanum tournefortii

Origanum tournefortii, commonly known as Spanish oregano, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves with a strong, pungent fragrance. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or coastal gardens.

Its essential oils, rich in thymol and carvacrol, are widely used in culinary and medicinal applications.
