Origanum albiflorum var. congestum

Origanum albiflorum var. congestum, commonly known as whiteflower oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has aromatic, narrow leaves that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and useful in traditional herbal medicine.
