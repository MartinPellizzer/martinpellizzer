Oldenlandia rosulata var. scabra

Oldenlandia rosulata var. scabra, commonly known as the roughleaf blushing flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that resemble clusters of small, pink or red flowers. The leaves are typically rough to the touch, giving the plant its varietal name "scabra," which means rough in Latin. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
