Oenothera fruticosa var. phyllopus

Oenothera fruticosa var. phyllopus, commonly known as the golden-rod evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright, shrub-like growth habit and is often found in dry, disturbed soils. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
