Oxalis bowieana

Oxalis bowieana, commonly known as Bowie's wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of small, yellow, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its botanical characteristics include a rhizomatous root system and capsule fruits that contain several seeds.

Oxalis bowieana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
