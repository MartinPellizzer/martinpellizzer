Orchis latifolia var. aggregata

Orchis latifolia var. aggregata, commonly known as the clustered broad-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and tall spikes of violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its flowers are often clustered at the base of the spike, giving it its varietal name.

Orchis latifolia var. aggregata is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
