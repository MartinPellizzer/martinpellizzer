Onobrychis samanganica

Onobrychis samanganica, commonly known as Persian clover, is a perennial legume native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, and its trifoliate leaves that are typically hairy. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, warm climates. It plays a role in soil nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with rhizobia bacteria.

Onobrychis samanganica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping in drought-prone areas.
