Opuntia inaequilateralis

Opuntia inaequilateralis, commonly known as the unequal-flowered prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in spines and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting well to hot and dry climates.

Opuntia inaequilateralis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local cultures.
