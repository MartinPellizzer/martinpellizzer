Onobrychis sojakii

Onobrychis sojakii, commonly known as the Persian clover, is a perennial legume native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in drought conditions.

Additionally, it is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
