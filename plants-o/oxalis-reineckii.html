Oxalis reineckii

Oxalis reineckii, commonly known as the False Shamrock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features trifoliate leaves that resemble clover, with each leaflet having a distinctive heart-shaped base. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red, depending on the variety. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Oxalis reineckii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
