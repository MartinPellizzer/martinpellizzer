Ozomelis trifida

Ozomelis trifida, commonly known as the three-lobed tamarisk, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Ozomelis trifida is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
