Orchis mascula var. fallax

Orchis mascula var. fallax, commonly known as the autumn orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant produces showy, violet or purple flowers with a distinctive spurred lip. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ephemeral blooming period in late summer to early autumn.
