Oxybaphus suffruticosus

Oxybaphus suffruticosus, commonly known as the African rock fig, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and small, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig-like fruits. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to provide food for both wildlife and humans.
