Ocotea quixos

Ocotea quixos, commonly known as Brazilian cinnamon or quixos, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark reddish-brown bark that exudes a fragrant resin. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elliptical, dark blue-black berries. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a glossy surface with a distinct aromatic scent.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in tropical gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
