Opuntia bisetosa

Opuntia bisetosa, commonly known as the two-headed prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems that are covered in spines and are often ribbed. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit, known as a tunas, is edible and contains a sweet, juicy pulp.

Opuntia bisetosa is well adapted to arid environments and is often used in traditional medicine and as a food source.
