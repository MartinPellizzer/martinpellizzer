Olea woodiana subsp. woodiana

Olea woodiana subsp.

woodiana, commonly known as the Chinese olive, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its lanceolate, glossy dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters.

The fruit is a small, yellowish drupe that ripens in late summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

The tree is well adapted to a variety of soil types and is relatively drought-tolerant.
