Oenothera fruticosa var. pilosella

Oenothera fruticosa var. pilosella, commonly known as the silky evening primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered with a dense layer of fine pubescence.

This species is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas, prairies, and along roadsides.
