Oxytropis dutreuilii

Oxytropis dutreuilii, commonly known as the Dureuil's locoweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or gravelly soils.

Oxytropis dutreuilii is also known for its toxic alkaloids, which can be harmful to grazing animals.
