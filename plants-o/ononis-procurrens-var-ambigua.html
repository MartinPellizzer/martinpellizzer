Ononis procurrens var. ambigua

Ononis procurrens var. ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in early summer. The plant has thin, wiry stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to harsh, dry conditions.
