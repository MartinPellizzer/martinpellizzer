Oldenlandia linoides

Oldenlandia linoides, commonly known as the creeping spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit, with slender stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers with five petals, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape, and covered with fine, silky hairs.

Oldenlandia linoides is often found in moist, disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of light conditions.
