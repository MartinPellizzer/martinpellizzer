Ononis stenophylla var. glabriuscula

Ononis stenophylla var. glabriuscula, commonly known as the glabrous rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its slender, often prostrate stems.

It features small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer, typically in clusters at the ends of the stems. The leaves are linear to lanceolate, with a rough texture and often a bluish-green hue. This variety is adapted to rocky or gravelly soils and is commonly found in dry, sunny environments.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, known for its cruciform flowers and nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
