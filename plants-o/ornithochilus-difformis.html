Ornithochilus difformis

Ornithochilus difformis, commonly known as the bird's beak orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated flower spikes that resemble a bird's beak, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments, often on rocks or tree trunks. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a distinctive hooded lip, and it produces small, round seeds.

Ornithochilus difformis is a rare and delicate species, making it a sought-after plant among orchid enthusiasts.
