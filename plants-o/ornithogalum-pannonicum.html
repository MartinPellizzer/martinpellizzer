Ornithogalum pannonicum

Ornithogalum pannonicum, commonly known as the Pannonian star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herb native to Central and Southeast Europe.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers in early summer. The plant belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous underground storage organs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens, though it can be invasive in some regions.
