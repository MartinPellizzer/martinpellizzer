Ophrys × messeniensis

Ophrys × messeniensis, commonly known as the Greek bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive flower structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee, a trait known as sexual deception. The plant produces showy, bee-like flowers that are typically yellow or brown with intricate patterns. It thrives in rocky and limestone soils, often found in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Ophrys × messeniensis is a short-lived perennial, with its life cycle closely tied to specific environmental conditions.
