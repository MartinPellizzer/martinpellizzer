Oxytropis nana var. obnapiformis

Oxytropis nana var. obnapiformis, commonly known as dwarf locoweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, clustered stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and prairie habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
