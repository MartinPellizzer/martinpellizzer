Oxalis cuzcensis

Oxalis cuzcensis, commonly known as the Andean wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. The plant features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped lobes and produces small, delicate yellow flowers. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a stem that often becomes woody with age.

Oxalis cuzcensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
