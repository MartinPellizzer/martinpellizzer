Oncidium macasense

Oncidium macasense, commonly known as the Macas orchid, is a tropical orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers with red markings. The plant typically blooms during the dry season, showcasing its adaptability to high-altitude environments. Its roots are epiphytic, allowing it to grow on trees without being parasitic.

Oncidium macasense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
