Onobrychis balansae var. major

Onobrychis balansae var. major, commonly known as the Greater Persian clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to fix nitrogen and prevent soil erosion.
