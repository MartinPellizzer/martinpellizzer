Oporinia laciniata

Oporinia laciniata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually less than 2 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers, followed by red berries that contain the seeds. It is native to parts of South America and is often found in humid, tropical environments.

Its parasitic nature allows it to draw water and nutrients from its host, while still performing some photosynthesis.
