Onopordum illyricum subsp. gautieri

Onopordum illyricum subsp.

gautieri, commonly known as the Illyrian thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It features robust, spiny stems and broad, lobed leaves with a rough texture.

The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by prickly, spherical seed heads. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
