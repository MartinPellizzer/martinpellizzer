Oxalis purpurata

Oxalis purpurata, commonly known as the purple shamrock, is a perennial flowering plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive purple tinge and produces clusters of five-petaled flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for.

Oxalis purpurata is also known for its ability to bloom throughout the growing season, adding color to the landscape.
