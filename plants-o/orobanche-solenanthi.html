Orobanche solenanthi

Orobanche solenanthi, commonly known as the desert dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has a unique ability to germinate in response to chemical signals released by its host. Its life cycle is closely tied to specific host species, such as various Cactaceae and Fabaceae plants.

Due to its parasitic nature and habitat preferences, it plays a significant role in the ecological dynamics of its native environments.
