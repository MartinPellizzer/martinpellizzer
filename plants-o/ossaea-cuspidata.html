Ossaea cuspidata

Ossaea cuspidata, commonly known as the sharp-pointed mossy orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky crevices or on limestone formations, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are grass-like and covered in a fine layer of moss, which helps retain moisture in its arid habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Ossaea cuspidata is considered endangered and is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
