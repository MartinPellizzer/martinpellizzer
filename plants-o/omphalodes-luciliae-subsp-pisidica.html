Omphalodes luciliae subsp. pisidica

Omphalodes luciliae subsp.

pisidica, commonly known as the Caucasian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region. It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in clusters at the top of its slender stems.

The plant has lanceolate leaves that are often hairy, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

The subspecies is distinguished by its specific geographic distribution and slight morphological differences from the main species.
