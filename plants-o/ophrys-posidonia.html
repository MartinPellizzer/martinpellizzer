Ophrys posidonia

Ophrys posidonia, commonly known as the bee orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive floral structure, which closely resembles a female bee, a feature that plays a key role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, producing a striking display of yellow and brown hues.

Ophrys posidonia is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is known for its specialized mimicry that attracts male bees for pollination.
