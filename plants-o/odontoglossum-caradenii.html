Odontoglossum caradenii

Odontoglossum caradenii, commonly known as the Ecuadorian orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Odontoglossum caradenii is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its role in the biodiversity of its native habitat.
