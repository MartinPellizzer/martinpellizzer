Oenothera primiveris subsp. bufonis

Oenothera primiveris subsp.

bufonis, commonly known as the yellow evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that open in the evening and close by morning.

The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, oily seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and potential medicinal applications.
