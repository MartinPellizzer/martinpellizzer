Olea mackenii

Olea mackenii, commonly known as the Mackenzie olive, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often grayish-green in color and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, fleshy fruits. Olea mackenii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions.

It is often found in rocky areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
