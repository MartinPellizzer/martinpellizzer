Oxalis parvicormus

Oxalis parvicormus, commonly known as the small-lobed wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped lobes. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Oxalis parvicormus is often found in woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
