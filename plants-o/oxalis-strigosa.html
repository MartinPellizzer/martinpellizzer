Oxalis strigosa

Oxalis strigosa, commonly known as the woolly wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive woolly texture, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate foliage and seasonal flowering.
