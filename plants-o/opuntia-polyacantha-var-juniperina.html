Opuntia polyacantha var. juniperina

Opuntia polyacantha var. juniperina, commonly known as the Juniper Opuntia, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems that are often covered in spines and clusters of small, globose flowers. The plant produces fruit that is typically yellow or red, containing edible seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting well to harsh desert conditions.

Its structure and growth patterns make it a notable example of xerophytic adaptation in the Cactaceae family.
