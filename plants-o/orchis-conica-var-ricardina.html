Orchis conica var. ricardina

Orchis conica var. ricardina, commonly known as the Ricard's narrow-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features a slender, erect stem that bears a single, showy spike of violet to purple flowers. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and the flowers are characterized by their intricate, lip-shaped petals.

This orchid is part of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its delicate appearance and ecological significance in its native range.
