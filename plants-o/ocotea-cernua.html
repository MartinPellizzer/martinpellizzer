Ocotea cernua

Ocotea cernua, commonly known as the drooping ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and small, clustered white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed, and it is often found in humid lowland forests.

Ocotea cernua is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
