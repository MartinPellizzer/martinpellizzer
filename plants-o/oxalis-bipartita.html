Oxalis bipartita

Oxalis bipartita, commonly known as the two-part wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive split at the base, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers with five petals, often accompanied by five sepals. Its foliage is typically covered in fine, silky hairs, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Oxalis bipartita is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
