Orobus enervius

Orobus enervius, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa, particularly in regions like Kenya and Tanzania.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, slender stems and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers, which are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, bloom during the dry season, and the plant is often found in association with other grasses and shrubs.

Orobus enervius is a relatively small plant, rarely exceeding 30 cm in height, and is known for its ornamental value in botanical gardens.
