Onobrychis bakuensis

Onobrychis bakuensis, commonly known as the Baku burclover, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Azerbaijan.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves, which are typically hairy and have a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
