Othonna imbricata

Othonna imbricata, commonly known as the silver leaf othonna, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, silver-gray leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple, blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Othonna imbricata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
