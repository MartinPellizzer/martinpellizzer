Otiophora burttii

Otiophora burttii, commonly known as the Burtt's rock daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Otiophora burttii is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
