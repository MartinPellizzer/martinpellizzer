Omphalobium brasiliense

Omphalobium brasiliense, commonly known as the Brazilian kidney vetch, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features small, trifoliate leaves and produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in spring. It typically grows in grasslands and open areas, often as a perennial herb.

Its botanical structure supports its role in soil enrichment and ecological balance in its native habitat.
