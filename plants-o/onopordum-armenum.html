Onopordum armenum

Onopordum armenum, commonly known as Armenian thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features a robust taproot and upright stems that are usually covered in spiny leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically gray-green with a rough texture and have a distinct, prickly feel.

Onopordum armenum is often found in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions.
