Oxalis articulata subsp. nodulosa

Oxalis articulata subsp.

nodulosa, commonly known as the nodular wood sorrel, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers with five petals.

The plant has a creeping rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread along the ground. Its stems and leaves are often covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas or along stream banks.
