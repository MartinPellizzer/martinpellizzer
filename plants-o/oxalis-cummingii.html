Oxalis cummingii

Oxalis cummingii, commonly known as the golden wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive yellow-orange veins, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Oxalis cummingii is also valued for its ability to spread via rhizomes, forming dense, low-growing mats.
