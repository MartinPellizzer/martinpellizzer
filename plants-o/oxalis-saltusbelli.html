Oxalis saltusbelli

Oxalis saltusbelli, commonly known as the mountain oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils, often in mountainous regions. It produces small, edible seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Oxalis saltusbelli is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
