Oncidium leochiloides

Oncidium leochiloides, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow and green flowers, which bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in seasonal environments. Its leaves are typically broad and sword-shaped, providing a sturdy structure for supporting the inflorescence.

Oncidium leochiloides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
