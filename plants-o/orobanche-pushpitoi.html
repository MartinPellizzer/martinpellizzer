Orobanche pushpitoi

Orobanche pushpitoi, commonly known as the Indian broomrape, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is found in various parts of India and Southeast Asia. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is often found growing on the roots of grasses and legumes.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can cause significant damage to agricultural crops.
