Ornithogalum tenuifolium var. monticola

Ornithogalum tenuifolium var. monticola, commonly known as the mountain adder's mouth, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale green flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, preferring well-drained soils. It is often found in open woodlands and along rocky outcrops.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
