Ozanonia

Ozanonia, commonly known as the sea holly, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Asteraceae.

These plants are characterized by their striking blue or purple ray florets and a central disk of yellow flowers, which resemble a composite flower head. Ozanonia species are typically found in coastal areas and saline environments, showcasing their adaptation to harsh, maritime conditions. They have a rosette growth form, with leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The genus includes several species, such as Ozanonia lamarckiana, which is native to the Mediterranean and coastal regions of Europe.
