Oligochaetochilus pictus

Oligochaetochilus pictus, commonly known as the pictured mossy orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, intricately patterned leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically arranged in a raceme. It thrives in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices.

Its unique leaf patterns and specialized habitat make it a notable species within the Orchidaceae family.
