Oxytropis caputoi

Oxytropis caputoi, commonly known as the Caputo's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with stipules and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is adapted to harsh, rocky environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
