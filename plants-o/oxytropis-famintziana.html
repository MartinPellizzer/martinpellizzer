Oxytropis famintziana

Oxytropis famintziana, commonly known as the Armenian locoweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh, arid environments and often grows in rocky or gravelly soils. Its leaves are typically compound, with a deep lobing structure that helps reduce water loss.

Oxytropis famintziana is also known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock.
