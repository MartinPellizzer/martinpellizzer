Omphacarpus hirsutus

Omphacarpus hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy burrplant, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the southeastern United States and is characterized by its hairy, woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by distinctive, spiny pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open habitats such as fields and roadsides.

Omphacarpus hirsutus is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, which makes it an important pioneer species in ecological succession.
