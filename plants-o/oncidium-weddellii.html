Oncidium weddellii

Oncidium weddellii, commonly known as the Weddell's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to South America.

It features a sympodial growth habit with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces racemes of fragrant, yellow-green flowers with intricate patterns. Its leaves are usually broad and grass-like, adapting to its natural habitat.

Oncidium weddellii is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its attractive blooms.
