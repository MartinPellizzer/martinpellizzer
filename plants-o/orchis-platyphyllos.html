Orchis platyphyllos

Orchis platyphyllos, commonly known as the broad-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, flowering stem that can reach up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces showy, violet or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often arranged in a basal rosette.

Orchis platyphyllos thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.
