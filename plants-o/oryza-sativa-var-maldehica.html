Oryza sativa var. maldehica

Oryza sativa var. maldehica, commonly known as the Maldehica rice, is a less commonly cultivated variety of rice native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated grains and typically exhibits a pale yellow to white color when uncooked. This variety is adapted to wetland environments and is often grown in flooded fields, similar to other rice species. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a grass-like structure with hollow stems.

The plant's flowers are small and clustered, producing grains that are harvested for culinary and agricultural use.
