Olostyla nigrescens

Olostyla nigrescens, commonly known as the black-stemmed everlet, is a perennial shrub native to the temperate forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, woody stems and lance-shaped leaves with a glossy, dark green surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its fruits are small, dark berries that contain seeds dispersed by birds.

Olostyla nigrescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its hardy nature and aesthetic qualities.
