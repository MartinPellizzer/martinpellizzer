Oncidium morenoi

Oncidium morenoi, commonly known as the Moreno orchid, is a tropical orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, fragrant flowers that come in a range of colors, including yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Oncidium morenoi is appreciated for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements in cultivation.
