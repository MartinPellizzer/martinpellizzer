Oxyanthera abbreviata

Oxyanthera abbreviata, commonly known as the dwarf flameflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are usually red or orange in color, which attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

Oxyanthera abbreviata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
