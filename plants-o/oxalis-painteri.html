Oxalis painteri

Oxalis painteri, commonly known as the Painted Wood Sorrel, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to South Africa.

It features delicate, heart-shaped leaves with intricate patterns of red, yellow, and green, resembling a painter's palette. The plant produces small, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink, adding to its visual appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Oxalis painteri is often cultivated as a houseplant or in rock gardens due to its compact size and vibrant foliage.
