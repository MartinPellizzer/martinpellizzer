Olax redmondii

Olax redmondii, commonly known as Redmond's olax, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Olacaceae and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit with woody stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often arranged in clusters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture, adapted to the arid environments it inhabits.

Olax redmondii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
