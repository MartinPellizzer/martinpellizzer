Ophrys apifera var. renatae

Ophrys apifera var. renatae, commonly known as the bee orchid, is a species of orchid native to parts of Europe and North Africa.

It is characterized by its striking flower that closely resembles a bee, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its flowers are hermaphroditic, meaning they contain both male and female reproductive organs.

The species is known for its specialized pollination mechanism, where the flower mimics the appearance and scent of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination.
