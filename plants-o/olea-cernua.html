Olea cernua

Olea cernua, commonly known as the weeping olive, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its drooping branches and small, leathery leaves that are dark green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by black, olive-like fruits. Olea cernua is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique appearance.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
