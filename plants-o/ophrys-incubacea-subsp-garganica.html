Ophrys incubacea subsp. garganica

Ophrys incubacea subsp.

garganica, commonly known as the Garganica bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination.

The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in sunny, open habitats. Its flowers are usually pale green or brown, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species in many areas.
