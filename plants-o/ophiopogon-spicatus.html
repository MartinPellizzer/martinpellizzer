Ophiopogon spicatus

Ophiopogon spicatus, commonly known as the spiny lily or Japanese spikenard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features grass-like leaves that are usually bluish-green in color and grows in dense clumps. The plant produces small, white to pale green flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Ophiopogon spicatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.
