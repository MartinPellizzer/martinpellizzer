Oxalis argillacea

Oxalis argillacea, commonly known as the clay plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardy nature.

Oxalis argillacea is known for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
