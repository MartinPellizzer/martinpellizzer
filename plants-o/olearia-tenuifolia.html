Olearia tenuifolia

Olearia tenuifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved daisy bush, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring silver-gray leaves that are narrow and linear. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Olearia tenuifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in horticulture for its hardy and drought-resistant qualities.
