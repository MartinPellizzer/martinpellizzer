Olearia passerinoides

Olearia passerinoides, commonly known as the silver matweed, is a low-growing shrub native to New Zealand.

It features silver-gray foliage that is densely covered with fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine habitats.

Olearia passerinoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
