Ophrys chiosica

Ophrys chiosica, commonly known as the Chios orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Aegean island of Chios.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a lip that mimics the appearance of a female bee, which plays a role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its flowers are usually pale green to brownish in color, with intricate patterns that aid in attracting specific pollinators.

Ophrys chiosica is a rare and protected species, reflecting its ecological and botanical significance in its native habitat.
