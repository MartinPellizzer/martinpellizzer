Oenothera contorta var. typica

Oenothera contorta var. typica, commonly known as prairie evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, and its slender, branching stems. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and sandy soils. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Oenothera contorta var. typica is known for its ecological importance and is often used in restoration projects.
