Oberonia hexaptera

Oberonia hexaptera, commonly known as the six-winged orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, linear leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows on trees in tropical rainforests, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air and decaying organic matter.

Oberonia hexaptera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
