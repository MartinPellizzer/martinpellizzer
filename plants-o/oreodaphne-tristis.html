Oreodaphne tristis

Oreodaphne tristis, commonly known as the black ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by round, fleshy fruits containing smooth, black seeds.

Oreodaphne tristis is often found in lowland evergreen forests and is known for its slow growth and long lifespan.
