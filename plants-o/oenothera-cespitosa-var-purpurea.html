Oenothera cespitosa var. purpurea

Oenothera cespitosa var. purpurea, commonly known as purple evening primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, blooming in the evening. The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

The species is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for nocturnal pollinators like moths.
