Oncidium tsubotae

Oncidium tsubotae, commonly known as the Tsubota's orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, pendulous inflorescences that bear clusters of fragrant, brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are often yellow or orange with contrasting purple or brown markings, making it a popular choice for ornamental cultivation.

Oncidium tsubotae requires bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.
