Otophora pinnata

Otophora pinnata, commonly known as the bird's nest fern, is a tropical plant characterized by its distinctive, cascading fronds that resemble a bird's nest.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to regions in Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant features a thick, wiry rhizome that supports its broad, pinnately compound leaves. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in humid environments.

Otophora pinnata prefers indirect light and consistently moist soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
