Ocotea cristalensis

Ocotea cristalensis, commonly known as the Crystal Ocotea, is a small evergreen tree native to the humid forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded understory conditions, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are glossy and lanceolate, often featuring a distinct white or silvery band along the midrib.

The fruit is a small, dark berry that develops from the flowers and is an important food source for various wildlife species.
