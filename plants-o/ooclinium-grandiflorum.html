Ooclinium grandiflorum

Ooclinium grandiflorum, commonly known as the grandiflorous ooclinium, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a smooth, waxy surface. Its root system is fibrous, aiding in nutrient absorption from the soil.

Ooclinium grandiflorum is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display.
