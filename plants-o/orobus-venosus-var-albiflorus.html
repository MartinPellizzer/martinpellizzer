Orobus venosus var. albiflorus

Orobus venosus var. albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered orobus, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often trifoliate leaves. The plant features showy white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically compound, with three leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
