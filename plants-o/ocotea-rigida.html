Ocotea rigida

Ocotea rigida, commonly known as the Cuban cinnamom, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and bay laurel. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils, contributing to its use in traditional medicine and as a source of aromatic compounds.

Ocotea rigida thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland tropical forests.
