Omphalodes rupestris subsp. rupestris

Omphalodes rupestris subsp.

rupestris, commonly known as the rock scabious, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in dry, sunny habitats such as cliffs and slopes.

The plant features basal leaves that are narrow and linear, with small, pink or purple flowers arranged in a dense, compact inflorescence. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in harsh environments and its distinctive, often fragrant blooms.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
