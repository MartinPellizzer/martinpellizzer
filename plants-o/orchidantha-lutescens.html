Orchidantha lutescens

Orchidantha lutescens, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family, which includes a wide variety of orchids known for their showy flowers. The plant features slender, erect stems and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers with a subtle fragrance. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Orchidantha lutescens thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
