Othonna acutiloba

Othonna acutiloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaved othonna, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in clusters during the cooler months, typically from spring to early summer. Its succulent stems allow it to store water, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for arid environments.

Othonna acutiloba is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its unique foliage and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
