Octomeria anceps

Octomeria anceps, commonly known as the African snowball, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in winter. The plant has woody stems and narrow, leathery leaves that are arranged in whorls. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.

Octomeria anceps is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
