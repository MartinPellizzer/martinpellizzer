Oxytropis chakassiensis

Oxytropis chakassiensis, commonly known as Chakassian locoweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow to green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Oxytropis chakassiensis is part of the legume family and has the ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it ecologically significant in its native habitat.
