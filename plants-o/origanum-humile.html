Origanum humile

Origanum humile, commonly known as dwarf oregano, is a small, perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features tiny, rounded leaves that are aromatic and often used in culinary applications. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its compact size and fragrant foliage make it a popular choice for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
