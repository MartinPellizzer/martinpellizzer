Oryza sativa var. quinanda

Oryza sativa var. quinanda, commonly known as the Brazilian rice, is a less commonly cultivated variety of rice native to South America.

It is characterized by its short, slender grains and is typically grown in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has a robust root system that helps it thrive in flooded conditions, which is a key adaptation for its native habitat. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a waxy surface that reduces water loss.

This variety is valued for its resilience to environmental stress and its unique flavor profile.
