Oxytropis jonesii

Oxytropis jonesii, commonly known as Jones' milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval to lance-shaped leaflets.

Oxytropis jonesii plays a role in soil stabilization and is a valuable forage plant for certain wildlife species.
