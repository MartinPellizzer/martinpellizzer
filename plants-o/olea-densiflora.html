Olea densiflora

Olea densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered olive, is a species of flowering plant in the Oleaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white flowers. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are typically opposite or clustered at the ends of branches. It produces small, oval fruits that contain a single seed.

Olea densiflora is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and fragrant blossoms.
