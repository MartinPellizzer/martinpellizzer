Orchis provincialis var. laeta

Orchis provincialis var. laeta, commonly known as the Happy Orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to specific regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils, often in woodland or meadow habitats. Its morphology includes a single leaf and a raceme of flowers with a distinct lip structure.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
