Odontoglossum ligulatum

Odontoglossum ligulatum, commonly known as the narrow-lip orchid, is a species native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and delicate, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, showy flowers with elongated petals and a narrow lip, which are typically a mix of white, yellow, and green hues. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Odontoglossum ligulatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
