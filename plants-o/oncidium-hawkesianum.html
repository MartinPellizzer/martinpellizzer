Oncidium hawkesianum

Oncidium hawkesianum, commonly known as the Hawkes' orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, showy inflorescences that feature a mix of yellow, green, and brown tones. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are long-lasting and often used in floral arrangements due to their vibrant colors and fragrance.

Oncidium hawkesianum requires bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.
