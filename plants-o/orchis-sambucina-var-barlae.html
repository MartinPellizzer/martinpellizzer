Orchis sambucina var. barlae

Orchis sambucina var. barlae, commonly known as the Barlae orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of violet to purple flowers with a distinctive spurred lip. Native to specific regions of Europe, this orchid thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats. The plant exhibits a complex life cycle, with a long period of dormancy followed by a brief flowering season.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and its role as an indicator of undisturbed ecosystems.
