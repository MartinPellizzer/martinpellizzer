Ottochloa malabarica

Ottochloa malabarica, commonly known as the Malabar grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Indian subcontinent.

It typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in moist, well-drained soils. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread and form dense stands.

Ottochloa malabarica is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive foliage and adaptability.
