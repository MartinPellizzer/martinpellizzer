Opuntia violacea var. santa-rita

Opuntia violacea var. santa-rita, commonly known as Santa Rita prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems that are often ribbed and covered in spines. The plant produces showy, violet-pink flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible fruits known as tunas. Its succulent leaves store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

This variety is particularly noted for its hardiness and ability to adapt to harsh desert conditions.
