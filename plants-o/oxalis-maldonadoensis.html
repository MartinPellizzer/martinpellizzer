Oxalis maldonadoensis

Oxalis maldonadoensis, commonly known as the Maldonado's wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and stems that are often covered in fine hairs.

Oxalis maldonadoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and blooms.
