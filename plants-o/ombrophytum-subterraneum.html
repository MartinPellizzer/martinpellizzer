Ombrophytum subterraneum

Ombrophytum subterraneum, commonly known as the underground bellflower, is a rare and elusive plant native to the humid forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its subterranean growth habit, with most of its structure hidden beneath the soil surface. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers that emerge only briefly during the wet season. Its leaves are typically small and clustered near the base, adapted to low-light conditions.

Due to its secretive nature and limited distribution, it is considered a challenging subject for botanical study.
