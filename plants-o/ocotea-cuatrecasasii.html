Ocotea cuatrecasasii

Ocotea cuatrecasasii, commonly known as the Cuatrecasas's ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and aromatic bark, which emit a subtle fragrance. It typically grows in humid, shaded environments within cloud forests.

Ocotea cuatrecasasii is valued for its ecological role and has potential uses in traditional medicine.
