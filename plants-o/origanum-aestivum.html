Origanum aestivum

Origanum aestivum, commonly known as summer oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly fuzzy leaves and small, pale blue or purple flowers. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates. Its essential oils, rich in compounds like carvacrol and thymol, contribute to its distinctive flavor and medicinal properties.

It is widely used in culinary applications and traditional herbal remedies.
