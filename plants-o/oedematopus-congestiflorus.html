Oedematopus congestiflorus

Oedematopus congestiflorus, commonly known as the dense-flowered bladder grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its swollen, bulbous stems that store water, a feature typical of succulent plants. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers during the rainy season. Its leaves are often fleshy and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Oedematopus congestiflorus thrives in arid or semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.
