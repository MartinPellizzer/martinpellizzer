Ophrys × reichenbachiana

Ophrys × reichenbachiana, commonly known as the Reichenbach's bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats. Its flowers are usually a combination of brown, yellow, and green hues, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Ophrys × reichenbachiana is a fascinating example of evolutionary adaptation in the orchid family.
