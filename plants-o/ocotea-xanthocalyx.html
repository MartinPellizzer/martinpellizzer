Ocotea xanthocalyx

Ocotea xanthocalyx, commonly known as the yellow-leafed ocotea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within lowland and montane forests. Its leaves are elliptical, with a smooth texture and a distinctive yellowish-green coloration on the underside.

Ocotea xanthocalyx is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage.
