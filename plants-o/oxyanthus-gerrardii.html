Oxyanthus gerrardii

Oxyanthus gerrardii, commonly known as the Florida marsh mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps. The plant features showy, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

Oxyanthus gerrardii plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing wetland soils.
