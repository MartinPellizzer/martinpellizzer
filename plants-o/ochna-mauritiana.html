Ochna mauritiana

Ochna mauritiana, commonly known as the Mauritius ochna, is a small evergreen shrub native to the island of Mauritius.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by distinctive, red, fleshy fruits. Ochna mauritiana is adapted to the tropical climate of its native habitat and is often found in coastal and forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
