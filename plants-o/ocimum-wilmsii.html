Ocimum wilmsii

Ocimum wilmsii, commonly known as wild basil, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs such as mint and sage. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. It is often found growing in moist, shaded areas in tropical and subtropical regions.

Ocimum wilmsii is valued for its fragrant leaves and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
