Obione holocarpa

Obione holocarpa, commonly known as the giant burr vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody vines and large, spiny fruits that contain numerous seeds. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically large and heart-shaped, with a rough texture.

Obione holocarpa is often found in moist, shaded environments within rainforests.
