Osmanthus aquifolium var. aureus

Osmanthus aquifolium var. aureus, commonly known as the golden osmanthus, is a hybrid shrub characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and clusters of small, fragrant yellow flowers.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is native to China, where it is cultivated for its aromatic properties. The leaves are typically glossy and dark green, with a striking golden hue on the underside, adding to its ornamental value. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for gardens and as a decorative hedge.

Its unique appearance and pleasant fragrance make it a popular choice in horticulture.
