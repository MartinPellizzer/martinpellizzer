Orthopogon subverticillatus

Orthopogon subverticillatus, commonly known as the zigzag grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive zigzag arrangement of leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its inflorescence consists of a narrow, cylindrical spike with closely packed spikelets.

Orthopogon subverticillatus is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
