Oryzopsis kokanica

Oryzopsis kokanica, commonly known as the Kashmir grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Himalayan region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
