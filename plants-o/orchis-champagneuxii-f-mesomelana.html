Orchis champagneuxii f. mesomelana

Orchis champagneuxii f. mesomelana, commonly known as the champagne orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to specific regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and requires well-drained, humus-rich soil. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces a single, elongated flower spike.

This orchid is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens due to its unique appearance.
