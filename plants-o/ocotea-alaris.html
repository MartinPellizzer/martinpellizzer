Ocotea alaris

Ocotea alaris, commonly known as the sweet cinnamon tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, cinnamon-scented leaves and bark. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by dark blue berries, which are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Ocotea alaris thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal uses.
