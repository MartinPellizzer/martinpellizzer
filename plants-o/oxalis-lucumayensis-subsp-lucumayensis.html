Oxalis lucumayensis subsp. lucumayensis

Oxalis lucumayensis subsp.

lucumayensis, commonly known as the Peruvian wood sorrel, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America. It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or rocky slopes. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that contain seeds. Oxalis lucumayensis subsp.

lucumayensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
