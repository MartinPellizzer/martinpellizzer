Origanum aciculare

Origanum aciculare, commonly known as creeping thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has tiny, needle-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance and contribute to its aromatic properties. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its essential oils contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, which are valued for their medicinal and culinary uses.
