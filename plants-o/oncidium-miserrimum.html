Oncidium miserrimum

Oncidium miserrimum, commonly known as the sad orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and clusters of fragrant, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers bloom in winter, adding a unique ornamental value to gardens.

Oncidium miserrimum is valued for its resilience and ability to thrive in a variety of growing conditions.
