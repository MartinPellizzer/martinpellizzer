Odontoglossum schlimii

Odontoglossum schlimii, commonly known as the Schlim's orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with elongated, grass-like leaves and produces intricate, pendulous flowers in shades of white and purple. The plant typically blooms in the spring and summer months, depending on the local climate. Its flowers are characterized by their spurred lip and showy petals, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Odontoglossum schlimii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
