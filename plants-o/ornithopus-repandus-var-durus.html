Ornithopus repandus var. durus

Ornithopus repandus var. durus, commonly known as the hardstem sow-thistle, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters, typical of the Brassicaceae family. Its leaves are narrow and alternate, often with a slightly serrated edge.

It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.
