Odontites linkii subsp. cyprius

Odontites linkii subsp.

cyprius, commonly known as the Cyprus toothplant, is a rare and endemic species native to Cyprus. It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in a dense cluster at the end of its stem. It thrives in rocky, limestone soils and is adapted to the arid and semi-arid conditions of its native habitat.
