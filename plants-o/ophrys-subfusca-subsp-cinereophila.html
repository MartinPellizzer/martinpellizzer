Ophrys subfusca subsp. cinereophila

Ophrys subfusca subsp.

cinereophila, commonly known as the ash-colored bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination.

The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its flowers are predominantly dark brown to ash-gray in color, with intricate patterns that aid in its deceptive pollination strategy.

Due to habitat loss and its restricted distribution, this subspecies is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
