Ocimum sericeum

Ocimum sericeum, commonly known as silver basil, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray leaves and small, pale purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and basil. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

Ocimum sericeum thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
