Oxalis cognata

Oxalis cognata, commonly known as the common wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive reddish veins and produces small, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow or white. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is part of the Oxalidaceae family, known for its oxalate crystals in the leaves.

Oxalis cognata is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
