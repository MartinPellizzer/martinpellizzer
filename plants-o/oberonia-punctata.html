Oberonia punctata

Oberonia punctata, commonly known as the speckled orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and strikingly patterned flowers with a distinctive yellow and green coloration. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in late spring to early summer.

Oberonia punctata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
