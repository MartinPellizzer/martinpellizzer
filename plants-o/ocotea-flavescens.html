Ocotea flavescens

Ocotea flavescens, commonly known as the yellow ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, leathery leaves and small, clustered white flowers. Its fruit is a small, woody drupe that turns yellow when mature.

Ocotea flavescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates for its aesthetic and aromatic qualities.
