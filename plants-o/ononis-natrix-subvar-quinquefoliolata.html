Ononis natrix subvar. quinquefoliolata

Ononis natrix subvar. quinquefoliolata, commonly known as the five-leafed sand rocket, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, often in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are palmately divided into five or more lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
