Ochna axillaris

Ochna axillaris, commonly known as the African firethorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, bright red berries that are highly attractive to birds. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. It produces yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, adding to its ornamental value.

Ochna axillaris is often cultivated for its striking appearance and ecological benefits in gardens.
