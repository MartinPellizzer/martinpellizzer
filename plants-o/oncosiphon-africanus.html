Oncosiphon africanus

Oncosiphon africanus, commonly known as African onion, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, dry fruits containing numerous seeds. Oncosiphon africanus is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
