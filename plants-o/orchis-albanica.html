Orchis albanica

Orchis albanica, commonly known as the Albanian orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is known for its delicate, spurred orchid flowers.

Orchis albanica is a protected species in many regions due to its limited distribution and ecological significance.
