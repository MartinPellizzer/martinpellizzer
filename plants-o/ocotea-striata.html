Ocotea striata

Ocotea striata, commonly known as the striated ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage and bark. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within lowland and montane rainforests. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

The tree produces small, fragrant flowers and dark, glossy berries, contributing to its ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
