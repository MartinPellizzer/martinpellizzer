Ocotea subsericea

Ocotea subsericea, commonly known as the white sand ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its wood is valued for its fine grain and resistance to decay, making it useful in construction and furniture-making.

The plant also plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
