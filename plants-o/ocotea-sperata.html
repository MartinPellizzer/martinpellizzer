Ocotea sperata

Ocotea sperata, commonly known as the white sand ocotea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged in whorls around the stem. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife.

Ocotea sperata is often found in moist, shaded habitats within tropical rainforests.
