Orbignya luetzelburgii

Orbignya luetzelburgii, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a species native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is a large evergreen tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and yellowish-green flowers. The tree produces large, woody pods that contain the edible seeds, known as Brazil nuts. These seeds are rich in nutrients, including protein, healthy fats, and essential minerals.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting a wide range of wildlife and contributing to the rainforest's biodiversity.
