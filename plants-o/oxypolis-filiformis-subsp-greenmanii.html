Oxypolis filiformis subsp. greenmanii

Oxypolis filiformis subsp.

greenmanii, commonly known as the slender filiform oxypolis, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America. It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive leaf shapes and often edible or medicinal properties.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered white or greenish flowers.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
