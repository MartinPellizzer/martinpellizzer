Ormosia froesii

Ormosia froesii, commonly known as the Brazilian ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability. The plant has bipinnate leaves and produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences.

Its fruit is a legume pod containing several seeds, and it plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.
