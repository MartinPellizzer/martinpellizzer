Oxalis pyrenea var. macrochaeta

Oxalis pyrenea var. macrochaeta, commonly known as the hairy mountain sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, which are often covered in fine, hairy texture, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white or pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and shaded borders.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
