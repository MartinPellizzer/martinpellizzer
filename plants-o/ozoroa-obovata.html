Ozoroa obovata

Ozoroa obovata, commonly known as the broad-leafed sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically obovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the rainy season. Ozoroa obovata is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, with deep roots that help it access water.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
