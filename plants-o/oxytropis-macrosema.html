Oxytropis macrosema

Oxytropis macrosema, commonly known as the large-seeded locoweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow to greenish flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are palmately compound, consisting of several pairs of oval to lance-shaped leaflets.

Oxytropis macrosema is known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock and wildlife.
