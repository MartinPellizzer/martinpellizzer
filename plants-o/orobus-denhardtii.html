Orobus denhardtii

Orobus denhardtii, commonly known as the Denhardt's orobus, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually pale blue to purple and bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Orobus denhardtii is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, and it plays a role in soil stabilization and ecological diversity in its native habitat.
