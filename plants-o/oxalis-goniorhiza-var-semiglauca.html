Oxalis goniorhiza var. semiglauca

Oxalis goniorhiza var. semiglauca, commonly known as the silverleaf wood sorrel, is a species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a flowering plant in the Oxalidaceae family, characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The leaves are typically covered in a fine, silvery or whitish pubescence, giving it its common name. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an attractive ornamental plant, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
