Ophiopogon angustifoliatus

Ophiopogon angustifoliatus, commonly known as narrow-leaved spathiphyllum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a bluish-green coloration. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens.

Its foliage provides aesthetic value, making it a popular choice for landscape design.
