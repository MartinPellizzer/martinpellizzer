Oxymitra glaucifolia

Oxymitra glaucifolia, commonly known as the blueleaf oxymitra, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its glaucous, bluish-green leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, typically blooming in the spring. It is often found in rocky soils and has adapted to thrive in high-altitude environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
