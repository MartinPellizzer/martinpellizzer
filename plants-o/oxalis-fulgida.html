Oxalis fulgida

Oxalis fulgida, commonly known as the golden wood sorrel, is a striking plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive golden-yellow coloration, which is particularly vivid in the spring. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled white flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

Oxalis fulgida is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and easy care requirements.
