Osteomeles rufescens

Osteomeles rufescens, commonly known as the red-leaf honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its attractive, showy flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often reddish in color, giving it its common name. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Osteomeles rufescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
