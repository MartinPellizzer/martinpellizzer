Onopordum tauricum var. angustifolium

Onopordum tauricum var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved thistle, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are composed of both ray and disk florets.

This species is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
