Ocotea catharinensis

Ocotea catharinensis, commonly known as the Brazilian cinnamon tree, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 15 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, dark brown to reddish-brown bark, which is often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a glossy surface with a strong aromatic scent.

The fruit is a small, woody drupe that contains a single seed, and the plant is known for its ornamental and medicinal value.
