Osmoxylon reburrum

Osmoxylon reburrum, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a member of the Rutaceae family and is native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage that reflects sunlight, helping it to retain moisture in harsh environments. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible, citrus-like fruits. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against drought and herbivory.

Osmoxylon reburrum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
