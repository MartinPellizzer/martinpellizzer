Orobanche foetida var. comosa

Orobanche foetida var. comosa, commonly known as the stinking broomrape, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

It is a member of the Orobanchaceae family and is characterized by its yellowish or brownish, often branched stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color. It is native to regions of Europe and Asia and is often found growing on the roots of various grasses and legumes.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can have significant impacts on agricultural crops.
