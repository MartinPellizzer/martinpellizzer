Orchis × bivonae

Orchis × bivonae, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a naturally occurring cross between Orchis morio and Orchis anthropophora.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe, particularly in the British Isles. The plant features tall, erect stems with whorled leaves and produces showy, violet-blue flowers in late spring. Its flowers have a distinctive three-lobed lip and are often pollinated by bees and other insects.

The hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
