Olea europaea var. communis

Olea europaea var. communis, commonly known as the common olive, is a small evergreen tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves, which are glossy and dark green on the upper surface. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by the characteristic olive fruits. The tree is well adapted to dry climates and is often cultivated for its oil-rich fruit.

It has a long history of use in both culinary and medicinal applications.
