Oldenlandia microcoryne

Oldenlandia microcoryne, commonly known as the small-coral vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows as a vine or trailing plant, often found in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters along the stems. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Oldenlandia microcoryne is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
