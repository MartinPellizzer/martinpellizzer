Ormosia stipularis

Ormosia stipularis, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its ornamental value, with its clustered, pendulous flowers resembling a cascade of golden beads. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and parks.

The tree also has a history of use in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
