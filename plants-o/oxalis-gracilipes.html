Oxalis gracilipes

Oxalis gracilipes, commonly known as the slender wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features delicate, trifoliate leaves with a heart-shaped base and produces small, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and woodland edges. Its stems are often hairy, and the leaves are usually arranged in a rosette pattern.

Oxalis gracilipes is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
