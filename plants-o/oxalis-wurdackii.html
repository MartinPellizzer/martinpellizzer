Oxalis wurdackii

Oxalis wurdackii, commonly known as the Wurdack's wood sorrel, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with reddish veins and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight. It is often grown as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.

Oxalis wurdackii is also valued for its ability to tolerate drought conditions.
