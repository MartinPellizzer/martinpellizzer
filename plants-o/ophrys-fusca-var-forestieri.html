Ophrys fusca var. forestieri

Ophrys fusca var. forestieri, commonly known as the forest bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its intricate floral morphology, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist woodland habitats, where it thrives in nutrient-poor soils. Its flowers are usually dark brown or black, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in many regions.
