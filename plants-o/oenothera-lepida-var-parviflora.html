Oenothera lepida var. parviflora

Oenothera lepida var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered evening primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths. Its flowers are followed by capsule fruits that release seeds equipped with silky hairs for wind dispersal.

This species is adapted to arid and rocky soils, making it a resilient member of its ecosystem.
