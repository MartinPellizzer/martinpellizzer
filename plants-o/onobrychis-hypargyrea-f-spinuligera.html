Onobrychis hypargyrea f. spinuligera

Onobrychis hypargyrea f. spinuligera, commonly known as the spiny broom, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a woody stem with thorny branches, and its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, ovate leaflets. The plant produces yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by cylindrical pods containing several seeds. It is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in shrublands and along coastal areas.

Its ecological importance includes nitrogen fixation and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
