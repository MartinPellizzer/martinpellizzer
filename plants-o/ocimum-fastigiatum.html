Ocimum fastigiatum

Ocimum fastigiatum, commonly known as purple basil, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage with a distinctive purple underside on the leaves. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ocimum fastigiatum is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves used in cooking and traditional medicine.
