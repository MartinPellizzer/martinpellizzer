Ophrys pseudoatrata

Ophrys pseudoatrata, commonly known as the false bee orchid, is a species of orchid native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brown in color, with intricate patterns that aid in mimicry.

Ophrys pseudoatrata is a terrestrial orchid that produces a single, erect flowering stem with several showy blooms.
