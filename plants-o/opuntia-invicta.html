Opuntia invicta

Opuntia invicta, commonly known as the Indian fig cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems that are often ribbed and covered in spines. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit, known as a tunafruit, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

Opuntia invicta is adapted to arid environments and is often used in traditional medicine and as a food source.
