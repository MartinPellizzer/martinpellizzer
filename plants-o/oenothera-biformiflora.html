Oenothera biformiflora

Oenothera biformiflora, commonly known as the two-flowered evening primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southwestern United States.

It features showy yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, aiding in moisture retention. Its stems are usually hairy and can grow up to 1 meter in height.

Oenothera biformiflora is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
