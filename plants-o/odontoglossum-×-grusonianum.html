Odontoglossum × grusonianum

Odontoglossum × grusonianum, commonly known as the Gruson's orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers with intricate patterns and a distinctive, elongated lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its botanical features include long, narrow leaves and a complex inflorescence that supports numerous blooms.

This orchid is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its beauty and rarity.
