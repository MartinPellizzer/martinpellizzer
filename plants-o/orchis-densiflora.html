Orchis densiflora

Orchis densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in woodland or grassland habitats, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its flowers are arranged in dense spikes, and it produces small, greenish-brown fruits that contain seeds.

Orchis densiflora is known for its ecological importance, supporting various pollinators and contributing to biodiversity in its native regions.
