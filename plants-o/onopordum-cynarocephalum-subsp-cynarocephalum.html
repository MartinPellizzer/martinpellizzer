Onopordum cynarocephalum subsp. cynarocephalum

Onopordum cynarocephalum subsp.

cynarocephalum, commonly known as the common globemallow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features a robust root system and erect, branching stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height.

The plant produces showy yellow flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are pinnately divided with serrated edges, and the plant is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

It is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
