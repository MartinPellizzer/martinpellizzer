Ophrys × montiscelanensis

Ophrys × montiscelanensis, commonly known as the Montiscelanese bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, bee-like floral structure that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in temperate climates with moderate rainfall. Its flowers feature a specialized labellum that mimics the appearance and scent of female bees, attracting male bees for pollination.

This botanical feature highlights the plant's evolutionary adaptation to its specific ecological niche.
