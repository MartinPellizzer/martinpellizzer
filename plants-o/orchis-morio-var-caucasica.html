Orchis morio var. caucasica

Orchis morio var. caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian early purple orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and clusters of pale purple to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has lance-shaped leaves with a waxy texture. Its flowers are spurred at the base, a distinctive feature of the Orchidaceae family.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems and is valued for its ornamental beauty.
