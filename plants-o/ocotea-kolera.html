Ocotea kolera

Ocotea kolera, commonly known as the Java ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate, and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by spherical berries.

The bark and leaves of Ocotea kolera contain aromatic compounds, making it valuable in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
