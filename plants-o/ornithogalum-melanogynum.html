Ornithogalum melanogynum

Ornithogalum melanogynum, commonly known as black nightshade lily, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces umbels of white or pale yellow flowers with showy, violet-black petals. It blooms in late summer to early autumn, and its berries are toxic to humans and animals.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
