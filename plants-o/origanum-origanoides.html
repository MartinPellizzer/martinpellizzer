Origanum origanoides

Origanum origanoides, commonly known as lemon grass or sweet basil, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems and narrow, aromatic leaves that emit a strong lemony scent. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers in summer, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its essential oils are valued in culinary and medicinal applications for their aromatic and antimicrobial properties.
