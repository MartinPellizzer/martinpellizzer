Oenothera primiveris var. caulescens

Oenothera primiveris var. caulescens, commonly known as the slender evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

The species is noted for its ecological importance in supporting pollinator populations and its adaptability to harsh climates.
