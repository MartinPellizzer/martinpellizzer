Orchis sambucina monstr. subregalis

Orchis sambucina monstr.

subregalis, commonly known as the Giant Lady's Tresses, is a striking orchid species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its long, spirally twisted flower spikes that bear numerous small, pale purple to white flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or grassland habitats. It is a perennial herb with a rhizomatous root system that allows it to thrive in shaded environments.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is often of interest to botanists and conservationists.
