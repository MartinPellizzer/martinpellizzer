Onopordum acanthium var. araneosotomentosum

Onopordum acanthium var. araneosotomentosum, commonly known as the woolly thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair, which provides a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by spiky, bristly seed heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry grasslands and rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
