Oxalis hirsutissima var. oblonga

Oxalis hirsutissima var. oblonga, commonly known as the woolly wood sorrel, is a perennial plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a fuzzy, velvety texture and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and delicate blossoms.

Oxalis hirsutissima var. oblonga is also valued for its ability to tolerate shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.
