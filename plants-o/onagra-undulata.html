Onagra undulata

Onagra undulata, commonly known as the wavy sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have wavy edges, contributing to its common name.

Onagra undulata is also valued for its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil.
