Olea compacta

Olea compacta, commonly known as the dwarf olive tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color and have a distinctive aromatic scent. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by small, oval-shaped fruits known as olives. Olea compacta is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and can thrive in full sun.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
