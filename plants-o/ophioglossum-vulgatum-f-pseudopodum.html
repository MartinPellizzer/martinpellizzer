Ophioglossum vulgatum f. pseudopodum

Ophioglossum vulgatum f. pseudopodum, commonly known as the false sword fern, is a rare and ancient fern species belonging to the family Ophioglossaceae.

It is characterized by its unique, sword-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces sporangia in small, clustered structures called synangia, which are located on the underside of the leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in many regions.
