Ophrys × sorrentinoi

Ophrys × sorrentinoi, commonly known as the Sorrentino's bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the genus Ophrys, which is well-known for its mimicry of female insects to attract male pollinators. The plant produces strikingly intricate flowers that resemble female bees, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. It typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands.

The hybrid nature of Ophrys × sorrentinoi contributes to its genetic diversity and adaptability in its native environment.
