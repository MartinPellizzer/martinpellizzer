Otolepis pubescens

Otolepis pubescens, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, pubescent stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or yellowish in color. Otolepis pubescens thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
