Oryza schlechteri

Oryza schlechteri, commonly known as the Schlechter's ricegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowering period occurs during the dry season, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Oryza schlechteri is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential ecological significance in its native habitat.
