Othonna purpurascens

Othonna purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-stemmed othonna, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems that are often tinged with a striking purple hue. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or summer. Its leaves are usually small and arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps it conserve water in arid conditions.

Othonna purpurascens is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
