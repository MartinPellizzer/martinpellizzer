Ornithocephalus planifolius

Ornithocephalus planifolius, commonly known as the flat-leaved orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stem and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and pale pink, typically blooming during the wet season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
