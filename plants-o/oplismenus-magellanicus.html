Oplismenus magellanicus

Oplismenus magellanicus, commonly known as the creeping wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Americas.

It features a low-growing, sprawling habit with trifoliate leaves composed of three heart-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow, spurred flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical name reflects its geographical origin, with "magellanicus" alluding to its presence in South America.
