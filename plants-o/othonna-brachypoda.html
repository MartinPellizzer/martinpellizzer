Othonna brachypoda

Othonna brachypoda, commonly known as the narrow-leaved othonna, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Othonna brachypoda is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
