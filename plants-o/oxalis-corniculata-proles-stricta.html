Oxalis corniculata proles stricta

Oxalis corniculata proles stricta, commonly known as the common wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive red or orange veins, and small, five-petaled flowers that are typically yellow or orange. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in gardens and woodland areas.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
