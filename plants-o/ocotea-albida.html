Ocotea albida

Ocotea albida, commonly known as white ocotea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in countries like Brazil, Colombia, and Peru. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed.

Ocotea albida is often found in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests.
