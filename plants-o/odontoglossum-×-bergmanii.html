Odontoglossum × bergmanii

Odontoglossum × bergmanii, commonly known as the Bergman's orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

The flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, producing a fragrant display that attracts various pollinators.
