Ochradenus harsusiticus

Ochradenus harsusiticus, commonly known as the desert spurge, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Ochradenus harsusiticus is also known for its chemical compounds, some of which have been studied for potential medicinal uses.
