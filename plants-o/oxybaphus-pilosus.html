Oxybaphus pilosus

Oxybaphus pilosus, commonly known as the hairy oxeye, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with a distinctive black center, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Oxybaphus pilosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
