Oreophilus hippocrepicus

Oreophilus hippocrepicus, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to high-altitude regions.

It typically grows in rocky soils and shaded forest understories, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are densely packed along the stem. Its flowers are small, clustered in inflorescences, and often display shades of pink or purple.

Oreophilus hippocrepicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in specialized gardens.
