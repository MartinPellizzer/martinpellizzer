Oxys lutea

Oxys lutea, commonly known as the yellow oxys, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention.

Oxys lutea is often found in dry, open habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
