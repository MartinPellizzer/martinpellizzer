Ophrys × borakisiana

Ophrys × borakisiana, commonly known as the bee orchid hybrid, is a fascinating plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Ophrys apifera (the bee orchid) and Ophrys borbasii, resulting in a unique combination of traits from both parent species. The plant is notable for its intricate floral morphology, particularly its labellum, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. It typically grows in calcareous soils in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges across Europe.

This orchid is a perennial herb with a short rhizome and is characterized by its striking, bee-like flowers that play a crucial role in its pollination strategy.
