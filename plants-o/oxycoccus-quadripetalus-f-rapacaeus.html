Oxycoccus quadripetalus f. rapacaeus

Oxycoccus quadripetalus f. rapacaeus, commonly known as the purple-capped crowberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, dark purple berries that are edible. The plant thrives in nutrient-poor, acidic soils and is often found in tundra and boreal forest ecosystems. Its leaves are small, elliptical, and covered with a dense layer of fine hairs.

The species is notable for its ability to survive in extreme cold and is an important food source for local wildlife.
