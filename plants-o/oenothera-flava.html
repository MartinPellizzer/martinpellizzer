Oenothera flava

Oenothera flava, commonly known as the yellow evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that open in the evening, hence its common name. The plant has slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically borne in clusters at the ends of the stems, and they emit a mild fragrance.

Oenothera flava is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight.
