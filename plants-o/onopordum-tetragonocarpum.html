Onopordum tetragonocarpum

Onopordum tetragonocarpum, commonly known as the four-cornered thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, gray-green leaves with a distinctive quadrangular shape. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by spherical, bristly fruits. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed sites.

Onopordum tetragonocarpum is adapted to harsh environments and has a long history of use in traditional medicine.
