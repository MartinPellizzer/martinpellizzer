Oxys acetosella

Oxys acetosella, commonly known as mouse-ear chickweed, is a small, low-growing plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its rosette of oval, velvety leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

Oxys acetosella is a perennial herb that is widely distributed across temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.
