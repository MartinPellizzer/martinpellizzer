Orthosiphon tomentosus var. glabratus

Orthosiphon tomentosus var. glabratus, commonly known as hairy lemongrass, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lemon-scented leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant in traditional medicine and horticulture.
