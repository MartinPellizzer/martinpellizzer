Oenothera drummondii nana

Oenothera drummondii nana, commonly known as the dwarf evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a short-lived perennial that produces showy yellow to orange flowers at night. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open plains.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and native plant gardens.
