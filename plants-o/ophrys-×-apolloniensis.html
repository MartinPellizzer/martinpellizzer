Ophrys × apolloniensis

Ophrys × apolloniensis, commonly known as the Apollo orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the genus Ophrys, which is characterized by its intricate, bee-pollinated flowers that mimic the appearance of female insects. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its flowers are notable for their complex structure, with a labellum that resembles a female bee, aiding in its unique pollination strategy.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological significance in its native habitat.
