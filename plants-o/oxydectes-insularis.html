Oxydectes insularis

Oxydectes insularis, commonly known as the island dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing, herbaceous habit with lobed leaves and bright yellow composite flowers. The plant produces small, wind-dispersed seeds that contribute to its spread across various habitats. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open areas.

Oxydectes insularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
