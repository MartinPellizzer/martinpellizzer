Ophrys × barlae

Ophrys × barlae, commonly known as the bee orchid hybrid, is a fascinating plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant is notable for its intricate floral morphology, particularly its labellum, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. It typically grows in calcareous soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats across Europe.

Due to its unique pollination strategy and ornamental value, it is a subject of both scientific interest and horticultural appreciation.
