Oplismenus hirtus

Oplismenus hirtus, commonly known as the hairy dog's-tongue, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its showy yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Oplismenus hirtus is often found in disturbed soils and can spread through rhizomes, making it a common weed in certain regions.
