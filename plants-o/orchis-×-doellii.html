Orchis × doellii

Orchis × doellii, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Orchis mascula and Orchis morio.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or grassland habitats. It blooms from late spring to early summer, producing flowers that are often fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and moths.

This hybrid is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique genetic composition and ornamental value.
