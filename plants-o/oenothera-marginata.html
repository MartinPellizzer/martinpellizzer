Oenothera marginata

Oenothera marginata, commonly known as the narrowleaf evening primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands. Its leaves are linear and have a distinctive margin that is often wavy or notched.

Oenothera marginata is also known for its medicinal properties, with its seeds containing essential fatty acids.
