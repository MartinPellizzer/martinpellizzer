Onoseris mexicana

Onoseris mexicana, commonly known as Mexican goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. Its yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Onoseris mexicana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
