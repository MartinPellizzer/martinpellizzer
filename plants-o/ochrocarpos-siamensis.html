Ochrocarpos siamensis

Ochrocarpos siamensis, commonly known as the Siam orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are long and narrow, often covered in a fine, waxy coating.

The flowers bloom in clusters, displaying a range of hues from yellow to orange, making it a visually appealing species.
