Octomeria rhodoglossa

Octomeria rhodoglossa, commonly known as the red-leaved octomeria, is a striking shrub native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its vibrant red to purple foliage, which becomes more pronounced in cooler weather. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. Octomeria rhodoglossa thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
