Odontites glutinosus f. pilatianus

Odontites glutinosus f. pilatianus, commonly known as the sticky toothed grass, is a variant of the species Odontites glutinosus.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy, often sticky leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, temperate environments and is often found in meadows or along stream banks.

Its unique foliage and ecological preferences make it a notable species in botanical studies.
