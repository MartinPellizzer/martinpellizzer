Osmanthus fragrans

Osmanthus fragrans, commonly known as sweet osmanthus, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, small yellow or golden flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant produces small, inedible berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy, dark green surface.

Osmanthus fragrans is widely cultivated for its aromatic flowers, which are used in teas, perfumes, and traditional Chinese medicine.
