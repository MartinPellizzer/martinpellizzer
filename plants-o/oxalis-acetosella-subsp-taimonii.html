Oxalis acetosella subsp. taimonii

Oxalis acetosella subsp.

taimonii, commonly known as the Taimon's wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, delicate flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky areas.

The plant features a fibrous root system and produces capsules containing numerous tiny seeds. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, and the flowers are usually pink or white. Oxalis acetosella subsp.

taimonii is native to parts of East Asia, including regions of China and Japan.
