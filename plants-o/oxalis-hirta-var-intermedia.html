Oxalis hirta var. intermedia

Oxalis hirta var. intermedia, commonly known as the hairy wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive hairy texture and produces small, yellowish-orange flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. It is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.

Oxalis hirta var. intermedia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and flowers.
