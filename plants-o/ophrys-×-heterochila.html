Ophrys × heterochila

Ophrys × heterochila, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant that results from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe, particularly in the Mediterranean region. The plant is characterized by its striking, bee-like flower morphology, which features a prominent lip that mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Ophrys × heterochila thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
