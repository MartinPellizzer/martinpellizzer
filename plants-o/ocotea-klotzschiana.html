Ocotea klotzschiana

Ocotea klotzschiana, commonly known as the mountain laurel or white ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurel, avocado, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, dark purple drupe that contains a single seed.

Ocotea klotzschiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates for its aesthetic and aromatic qualities.
