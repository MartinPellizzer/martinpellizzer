Ocotea obtusata

Ocotea obtusata, commonly known as the broadleaf ocotea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in countries like Mexico, Guatemala, and Honduras. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with broad, leathery leaves that are typically dark green in color. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are often white or pale yellow, followed by elliptical berries that contain a single seed.

Ocotea obtusata is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional crafts and as a source of essential oils.
