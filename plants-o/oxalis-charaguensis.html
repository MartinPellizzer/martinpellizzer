Oxalis charaguensis

Oxalis charaguensis, commonly known as the charagua wood sorrel, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its leaves contain oxalic acid, which can be toxic if ingested in large quantities.

Oxalis charaguensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
