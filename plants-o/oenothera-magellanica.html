Oenothera magellanica

Oenothera magellanica, commonly known as the Magellan's evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats, including grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often glandular and slightly hairy.

Oenothera magellanica is known for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
