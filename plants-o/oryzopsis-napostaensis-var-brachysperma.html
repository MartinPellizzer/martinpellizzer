Oryzopsis napostaensis var. brachysperma

Oryzopsis napostaensis var. brachysperma, commonly known as the short-seeded grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous short, pale green spikelets. Its seeds are small and have a distinctive, slightly compressed shape.

This variety is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dryland ecosystems.
