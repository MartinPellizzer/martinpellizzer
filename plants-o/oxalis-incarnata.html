Oxalis incarnata

Oxalis incarnata, commonly known as the pinkwood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a reddish-purple underside and produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant is part of the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive leaf structure and delicate blooms. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Oxalis incarnata is a short-lived perennial that adds ornamental value to gardens with its vibrant foliage and flowers.
