Oxytropis pilosa var. purpurea

Oxytropis pilosa var. purpurea, commonly known as purple silky locoweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and purple-pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This variety is distinguished by its more vibrant floral coloration compared to other varieties of Oxytropis pilosa.
