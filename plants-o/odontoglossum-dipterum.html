Odontoglossum dipterum

Odontoglossum dipterum, commonly known as the two-winged orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves and its unique, two-winged flower structure that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in high-altitude cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its flowers, which are usually purple or violet in color, bloom during the spring and summer months.

Odontoglossum dipterum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
