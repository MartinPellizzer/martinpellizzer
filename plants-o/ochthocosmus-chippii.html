Ochthocosmus chippii

Ochthocosmus chippii, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, violet-blue berries that appear in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged opposite each other on the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

The berries are not only ornamental but also attract birds and other wildlife.
