Ochna carvalhoi

Ochna carvalhoi, commonly known as the Carvalho's ochna, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in shrubland and woodland habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Ochna carvalhoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
