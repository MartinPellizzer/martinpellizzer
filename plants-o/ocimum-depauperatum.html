Ocimum depauperatum

Ocimum depauperatum, commonly known as the poor man's basil, is a species of flowering plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of pale blue or white flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its medicinal and culinary uses.

Ocimum depauperatum is known for its ability to adapt to various soil conditions, making it a resilient herb.
