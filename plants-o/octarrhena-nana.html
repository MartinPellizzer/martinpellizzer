Octarrhena nana

Octarrhena nana, commonly known as the dwarf silvergrass, is a small perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows to a height of about 20-30 centimeters and features narrow, silver-gray blades with a soft texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in panicles, which are often hidden among the foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its compact size and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
