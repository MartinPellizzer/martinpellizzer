Oenocarpus macrocalyx

Oenocarpus macrocalyx, commonly known as the breadnut tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, palm-like leaves. The plant produces large, woody fruits that contain a single, starchy seed, which is the primary economic product. The leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and the tree is valued for its ecological and agricultural significance.

Its growth requires warm, humid conditions and is often found in lowland tropical forests.
