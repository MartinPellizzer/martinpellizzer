Odontoglossum maculatum var. duvivierianum

Odontoglossum maculatum var. duvivierianum, commonly known as the Duvivier's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

This variety is characterized by its striking, velvety petals and a unique coloration pattern that includes marbled markings. It typically grows as an epiphytic plant, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. The flowers are usually fragrant and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Its botanical features make it a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts and horticulturists.
