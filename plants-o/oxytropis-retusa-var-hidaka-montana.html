Oxytropis retusa var. hidaka-montana

Oxytropis retusa var. hidaka-montana, commonly known as Japanese milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has bipinnate leaves with numerous small leaflets, and it produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
