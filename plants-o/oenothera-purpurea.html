Oenothera purpurea

Oenothera purpurea, commonly known as the showy evening primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features striking, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often opening in the evening. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by moths and other nocturnal insects.

Oenothera purpurea is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is often found in open woodlands and prairies.
