Ocimum trichodon

Ocimum trichodon, commonly known as the hairy basil or Indian basil, is an aromatic herb native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like mint and lavender. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant leaves and clusters of white or pale blue flowers. Its essential oils, rich in compounds like linalool and eugenol, contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.

Ocimum trichodon thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental and therapeutic properties.
