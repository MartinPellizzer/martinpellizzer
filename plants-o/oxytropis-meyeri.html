Oxytropis meyeri

Oxytropis meyeri, commonly known as Meyer's locoweed, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow to brown flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert habitats.

Oxytropis meyeri is also known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock.
