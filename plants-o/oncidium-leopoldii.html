Oncidium leopoldii

Oncidium leopoldii, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It features a compact, pseudobulbous structure with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green. The plant produces long, pendulous inflorescences bearing numerous fragrant, yellow flowers with a distinctive white throat. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining potting media to prevent root rot.

Oncidium leopoldii is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its vibrant blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
