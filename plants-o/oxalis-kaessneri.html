Oxalis kaessneri

Oxalis kaessneri, commonly known as the golden oxalis, is a species of flowering plant in the family Oxalidaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces small, edible tubers that are sometimes used in local cuisines. Oxalis kaessneri thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique appearance and adaptability contribute to its appeal in both horticultural and ecological contexts.
