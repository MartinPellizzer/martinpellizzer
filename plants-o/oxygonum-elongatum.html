Oxygonum elongatum

Oxygonum elongatum, commonly known as the longleaf oxygonum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in whorled clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or savannah habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
