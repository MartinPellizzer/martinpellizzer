Ocotea camanducaiensis

Ocotea camanducaiensis, commonly known as the Camanduca Island Ocotea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in coastal or mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive fragrant scent, while its fruit is a small, dark drupe.

Ocotea camanducaiensis plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
