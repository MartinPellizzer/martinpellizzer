Oryza leersioides

Oryza leersioides, commonly known as the lesser rice plant, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is closely related to cultivated rice species. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats and has slender, grass-like foliage. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that bear small, rice-like grains.

Due to its genetic similarity to rice, it is of interest in botanical and agricultural research.
