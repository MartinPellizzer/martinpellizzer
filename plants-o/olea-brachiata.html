Olea brachiata

Olea brachiata, commonly known as the African olive, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family, which includes other olive species. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that appear in clusters. Its fruit is a small, yellowish drupe that matures in autumn.

Olea brachiata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
