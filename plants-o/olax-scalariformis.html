Olax scalariformis

Olax scalariformis, commonly known as the scalloped olax, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Olacaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, scalloped-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
