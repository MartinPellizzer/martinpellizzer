Oreopanax boliviensis

Oreopanax boliviensis, commonly known as the Bolivian arrowroot, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its palmate leaves and clustered, spadix-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at moderate elevations. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Oreopanax boliviensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its attractive foliage.
