Oxalis parvifolia subsp. aureoflava

Oxalis parvifolia subsp.

aureoflava, commonly known as the golden-flowered wood sorrel, is a perennial herb native to South America. It features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive yellowish-green color and small, delicate flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its foliage and flowers are often adorned with a subtle golden hue, giving it its common name.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
