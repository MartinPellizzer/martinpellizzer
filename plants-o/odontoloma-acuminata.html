Odontoloma acuminata

Odontoloma acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-pointed orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom during the wet season. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as forest understories, where it can thrive on the forest floor or on tree trunks. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it has a fibrous root system that helps it anchor in moist soil.

Odontoloma acuminata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
