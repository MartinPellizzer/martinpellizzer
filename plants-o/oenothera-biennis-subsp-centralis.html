Oenothera biennis subsp. centralis

Oenothera biennis subsp.

centralis, commonly known as the Central Plains evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the central United States. It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening and close by morning.

The plant has slender, branching stems and heart-shaped, hairy leaves. It produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, oval seeds.

This species is adapted to dry, disturbed soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.
