Ochthocosmus africanus

Ochthocosmus africanus, commonly known as the African sandthorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Ochthocosmus africanus is adapted to withstand drought conditions and plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils.
