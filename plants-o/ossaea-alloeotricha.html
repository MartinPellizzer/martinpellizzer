Ossaea alloeotricha

Ossaea alloeotricha, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sand sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its root system helps stabilize soil, making it ecologically important in its native habitat.

Ossaea alloeotricha is often found in coastal dunes and inland sandy plains, where it plays a role in preventing erosion.
