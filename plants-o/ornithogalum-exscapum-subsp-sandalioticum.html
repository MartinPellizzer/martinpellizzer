Ornithogalum exscapum subsp. sandalioticum

Ornithogalum exscapum subsp.

sandalioticum, commonly known as the sandalwood lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It features a slender, upright stem that bears a single, showy, white to pale pink flower at the apex.

The plant has lance-shaped, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant floral display.
