Oxalis montana

Oxalis montana, commonly known as mountain wood sorrel, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its stems are often hairy, and it is known for its ability to tolerate cold temperatures.

Oxalis montana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
