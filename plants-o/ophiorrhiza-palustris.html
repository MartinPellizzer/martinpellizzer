Ophiorrhiza palustris

Ophiorrhiza palustris, commonly known as the marsh hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swampy areas. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate or elliptical in shape. Its flowers are showy and typically yellow or white, with five petals and stamens.

Ophiorrhiza palustris produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by water or wind.
