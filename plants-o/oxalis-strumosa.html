Oxalis strumosa

Oxalis strumosa, commonly known as the yellow woodsorrel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats. Its flowers bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Oxalis strumosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
