Oileus abscissus

Oileus abscissus, commonly known as the split-leaved oileus, is a unique plant characterized by its distinctive foliage.

Its leaves are deeply lobed and often appear to be split, giving it a striking visual appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as along stream banks or in dense forests. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or white in color.

The botanical features of Oileus abscissus make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
