Odontoglossum vexillarium var. lehmannii

Odontoglossum vexillarium var. lehmannii, commonly known as the Lehmann's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a sympodial orchid with a rhizomatous growth habit, producing pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowering structures. The plant features lanceolate, dark green leaves and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and violet. It thrives in cool, high-altitude environments with moderate light and well-draining soil.

This variety is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
