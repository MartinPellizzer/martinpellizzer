Orchis strictifolia var. elata

Orchis strictifolia var. elata, commonly known as the tall narrow-leaf orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and woodland edges. The plant features upright, narrow leaves and produces tall spikes of violet to purple flowers. Its flowers are usually spurred at the base, a characteristic feature of many orchid species.

It is known for its ornamental value and is often found in botanical gardens and conservation sites.
