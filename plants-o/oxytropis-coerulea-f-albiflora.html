Oxytropis coerulea f. albiflora

Oxytropis coerulea f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered blue locust, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, compound leaves. The plant produces fluffy, pale blue to white flower clusters that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes, often in regions with well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
