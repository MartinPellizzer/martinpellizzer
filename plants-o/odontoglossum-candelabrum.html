Odontoglossum candelabrum

Odontoglossum candelabrum, commonly known as the candlestick orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, candle-like inflorescence composed of multiple flower spikes that rise from a central stem. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals.

Odontoglossum candelabrum thrives in cool, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
