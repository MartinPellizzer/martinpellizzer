Ornithocephalus falcatus

Ornithocephalus falcatus, commonly known as the sickleleaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, sickle-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, intricate flowers that are typically greenish-white with faint purple markings. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
