Ocotea rubrinervis

Ocotea rubrinervis, commonly known as the red-veined ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with prominent reddish veins, giving it its common name. It typically grows in humid, shaded environments within lowland and montane forests.

Ocotea rubrinervis is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional crafts and as a source of essential oils.
