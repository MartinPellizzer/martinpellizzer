Olea chrysophylla var. somaliensis

Olea chrysophylla var. somaliensis, commonly known as the Somali olive, is a variant of the olive tree native to the Horn of Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree with lanceolate, leathery leaves that are typically yellow-green in color. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by drupes that are usually green when immature and turn dark purple or black when ripe. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to poor soils and drought conditions.

The species is of interest for its ornamental value and potential ecological roles in its native habitat.
