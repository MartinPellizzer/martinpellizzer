Oncidium deburghgraeveanum

Oncidium deburghgraeveanum, commonly known as the Burghgraeve's Oncidium, is a flowering orchid native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its pendulous, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange with darker markings. The plant has strap-like leaves that are often arranged in a fan shape, and it thrives in warm, humid environments. It requires bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to flourish.

Oncidium deburghgraeveanum is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
