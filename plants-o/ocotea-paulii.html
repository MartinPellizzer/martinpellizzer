Ocotea paulii

Ocotea paulii, commonly known as the Brazilian lauraceae, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon trees, and other aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. Its bark is smooth and dark brown, often exuding a fragrant resin.

Ocotea paulii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
