Oxycoccoides japonicus var. sinicus

Oxycoccoides japonicus var. sinicus, commonly known as the Chinese waxflower, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to produce clusters of small, waxy, pink or red flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. Its foliage is typically dark green and glossy, adding visual interest throughout the growing season.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural settings for its vibrant floral display.
