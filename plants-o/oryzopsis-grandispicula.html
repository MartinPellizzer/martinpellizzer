Oryzopsis grandispicula

Oryzopsis grandispicula, commonly known as the large-awned grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and long, bristly awns that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its botanical features include narrow, linear leaves and small, spikelet-based flowers.

Oryzopsis grandispicula plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various desert organisms.
