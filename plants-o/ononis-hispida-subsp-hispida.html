Ononis hispida subsp. hispida

Ononis hispida subsp.

hispida, commonly known as the hairy stitchwort, is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. It features small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to May.

The leaves are usually narrow, hairy, and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or coastal environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
