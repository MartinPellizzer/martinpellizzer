Orchis × caesii

Orchis × caesii, commonly known as the purple-tinged orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Orchis morio and Orchis mascula.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet and white flower spikes. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers are adorned with intricate patterns and are pollinated by various insect species.

Orchis × caesii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often found in botanical gardens and natural reserves.
