Olea dentata var. salicifolia

Olea dentata var. salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaf olive, is a variant of the olive tree characterized by its distinctive willow-like leaves.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by edible olives that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and serrated, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

The tree is also valued for its hardy nature and ability to adapt to various environmental conditions.
