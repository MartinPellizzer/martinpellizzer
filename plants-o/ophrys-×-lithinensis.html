Ophrys × lithinensis

Ophrys × lithinensis, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys lutea, two species of bee orchids. This plant is notable for its intricate floral structure, which mimics the appearance of female bees to attract male bees for pollination. Its flowers are typically yellowish-brown with intricate patterns that aid in mimicry.

Ophrys × lithinensis is often found in calcareous soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats.
