Orobus bauhini

Orobus bauhini, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its long, cascading clusters of yellow-orange tubular flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil.

Orobus bauhini is also known for its fragrant blooms and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
