Ophrys sphegodes subsp. arachnitiformis

Ophrys sphegodes subsp.

arachnitiformis, commonly known as the wasp orchid, is a specialized orchid species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its intricate flower structure that closely resembles a wasp, a feature evolved to attract specific pollinators.

The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained habitats. Its flowers are usually pale green with dark markings, mimicking the appearance of a wasp to enhance pollination efficiency.

This subspecies is notable for its rare and delicate beauty, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
