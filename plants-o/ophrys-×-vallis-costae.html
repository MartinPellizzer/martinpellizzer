Ophrys × vallis-costae

Ophrys × vallis-costae, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant that results from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys fucata.

It is characterized by its intricate, bee-like flower structure, which features a prominent lip that mimics the appearance and scent of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, while its flowers are usually pale yellow to brown, with intricate patterns that aid in deception.

This orchid is a prime example of evolutionary adaptation in the plant kingdom, showcasing the complex relationships between plants and their pollinators.
