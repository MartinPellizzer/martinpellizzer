Omphalodes luciliae subsp. luciliae

Omphalodes luciliae subsp.

luciliae, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, upright stems and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
