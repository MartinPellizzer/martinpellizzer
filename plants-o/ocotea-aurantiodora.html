Ocotea aurantiodora

Ocotea aurantiodora, commonly known as golden ocotea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Venezuela. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. Its fruits are drupes, typically reddish when mature, and contain a single seed.

Ocotea aurantiodora is often found in humid, lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.
