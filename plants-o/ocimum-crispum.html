Ocimum crispum

Ocimum crispum, commonly known as curly basil, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its fragrant, small, dark green leaves that are often curled or crinkled, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Ocimum crispum is widely used in culinary and medicinal applications due to its aromatic properties.
