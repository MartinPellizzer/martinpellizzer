Ophrestia pinnata

Ophrestia pinnata, commonly known as the veined mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees.

It has small, opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its berries are typically red or orange and contain a single seed.

Ophrestia pinnata is found in tropical and subtropical regions, often in humid, shaded environments.
