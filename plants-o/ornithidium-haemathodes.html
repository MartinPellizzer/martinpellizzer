Ornithidium haemathodes

Ornithidium haemathodes, commonly known as the bloodroot orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its vivid red and orange floral structures, which are highly specialized for pollination by specific insect species. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in tropical or subtropical regions. Its leaves are usually dark green and broad, supporting the delicate inflorescences.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in many areas.
