Oldenlandia subverticillata

Oldenlandia subverticillata, commonly known as the false spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or yellow. The plant produces small, three-lobed leaves arranged in whorls around the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

The genus Oldenlandia is part of the Acanthaceae family, known for its ornamental and sometimes medicinal uses.
