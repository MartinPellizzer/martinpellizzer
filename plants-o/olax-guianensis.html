Olax guianensis

Olax guianensis, commonly known as the Guianese olax, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Olacaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
