Oenothera × clavifera

Oenothera × clavifera, commonly known as the desert evening primrose, is a hybrid plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant produces striking yellow flowers that open in the evening, hence its common name. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and the stems are often reddish in color.

Oenothera × clavifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in harsh conditions.
