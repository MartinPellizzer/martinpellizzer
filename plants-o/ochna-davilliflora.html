Ochna davilliflora

Ochna davilliflora, commonly known as the yellow ochna, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing in late summer. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Ochna davilliflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
