Ocimum stenoglossum

Ocimum stenoglossum, commonly known as the narrow-lipped basil, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and have a strong, spicy scent. Its small, tubular flowers are typically purple or violet, arranged in whorls along the stem.

Ocimum stenoglossum is valued for its ornamental appeal and use in traditional medicine.
