Olgaea altaica

Olgaea altaica, commonly known as the Altai olga, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Olgaea altaica is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes at high elevations.
