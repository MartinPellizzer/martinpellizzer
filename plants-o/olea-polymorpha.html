Olea polymorpha

Olea polymorpha, commonly known as the common olive, is a species of flowering plant in the family Oleaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and fragrant yellow flowers. The plant produces small, greenish-black fruits called olives, which are widely used in culinary and medicinal applications. Olea polymorpha is known for its resilience to drought and its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Its wood is also valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture-making.
