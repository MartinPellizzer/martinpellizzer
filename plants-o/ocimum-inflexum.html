Ocimum inflexum

Ocimum inflexum, commonly known as the wild basil or Indian borage, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Ocimum inflexum is also valued in culinary applications, particularly in regional cuisines for its distinct flavor.
