Orthosiphon cinereus

Orthosiphon cinereus, commonly known as silverleaf horsegram, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its grayish-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Orthosiphon cinereus is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
