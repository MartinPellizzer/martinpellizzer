Orchis sambucina subvar. luteopurpurea

Orchis sambucina subvar. luteopurpurea, commonly known as the purple-lilac orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from purple to lilac. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of flowers, each with a three-lobed lip that is a key feature for identification.

The leaves are lanceolate and often have a slightly hairy surface, contributing to its distinctive botanical appearance.
