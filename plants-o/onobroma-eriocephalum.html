Onobroma eriocephalum

Onobroma eriocephalum, commonly known as the woolly poinsettia, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, colorful bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by capsule fruits containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along dry streambeds.

The species is known for its adaptability to harsh conditions and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
