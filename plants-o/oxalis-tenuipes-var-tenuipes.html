Oxalis tenuipes var. tenuipes

Oxalis tenuipes var. tenuipes, commonly known as the slender wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It features delicate, trifoliate leaves with a heart-shaped base and small, five-petaled flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories. Its foliage is covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds.

Oxalis tenuipes var. tenuipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
