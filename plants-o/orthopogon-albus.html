Orthopogon albus

Orthopogon albus, commonly known as the white grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family (Poaceae).

It is native to various regions in the Americas and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, white flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, often with a slight sheen, and the flowers are arranged in dense clusters at the tip of the stems.

Orthopogon albus plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems and provides habitat for various insects and small animals.
