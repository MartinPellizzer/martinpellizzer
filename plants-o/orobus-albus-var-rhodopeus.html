Orobus albus var. rhodopeus

Orobus albus var. rhodopeus, commonly known as the Rhodope white orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to the Rhodope Mountains in Bulgaria.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates with moderate rainfall. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths.
