Olyra micrantha var. dioecia

Olyra micrantha var. dioecia, commonly known as the male and female forms of the African arrowroot, is a dioecious plant species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants. Its rhizomes are often harvested for their starchy roots, which are used in local cuisines.
