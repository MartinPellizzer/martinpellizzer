Oxalis magnifolia var. magnifolia

Oxalis magnifolia var. magnifolia, commonly known as the showy wood sorrel, is a species of flowering plant in the family Oxalidaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Oxalis magnifolia var. magnifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to add vibrant color to gardens.
