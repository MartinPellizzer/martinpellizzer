Olea europaea var. curvifolia

Olea europaea var. curvifolia, commonly known as the curly-leaf olive, is a variety of the olive tree characterized by its distinctive, twisted leaves.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by edible olives that can be harvested in late summer. Its unique leaf morphology makes it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.

The botanical features of Olea europaea var. curvifolia contribute to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.
