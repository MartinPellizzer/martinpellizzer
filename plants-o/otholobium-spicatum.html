Otholobium spicatum

Otholobium spicatum, commonly known as the spiny bush veldt, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, rounded leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland and shrubland habitats.

Otholobium spicatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
