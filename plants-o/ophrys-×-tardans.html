Ophrys × tardans

Ophrys × tardans, commonly known as the bee orchid hybrid, is a fascinating plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera, two species known for their mimicry of female insects. The plant is characterized by its striking, bee-like flowers that serve as a visual lure for male bees, facilitating pollination. Its botanical features include a slender stem with a single or few flowers, each resembling a female bee in color and shape.

Ophrys × tardans is typically found in open, sunny habitats across Europe, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
