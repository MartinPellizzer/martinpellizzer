Onosma hookeri var. wardii

Onosma hookeri var. wardii, commonly known as the Ward's narrowleaf sandthrift, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
