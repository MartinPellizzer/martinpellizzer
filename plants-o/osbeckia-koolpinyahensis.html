Osbeckia koolpinyahensis

Osbeckia koolpinyahensis, commonly known as the Koolpinya osbeckia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It is a small, herbaceous annual that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs to reduce water loss.

Osbeckia koolpinyahensis is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, showcasing features typical of xerophytic plants.
