Oenothera trichocalyx f. heterophylla

Oenothera trichocalyx f. heterophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf evening primrose, is a variant of the evening primrose species native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that open in the evening. The plant is an annual herb with a hairy stem and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental plant in native plant gardens.
