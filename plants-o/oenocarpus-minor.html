Oenocarpus minor

Oenocarpus minor, commonly known as the coconut palm, is a tropical plant native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves. The plant produces a distinctive fruit that contains a single large seed surrounded by a fibrous husk. The coconut palm is known for its versatility, providing food, fiber, and oil.

Its botanical structure supports its adaptation to coastal and tropical environments.
