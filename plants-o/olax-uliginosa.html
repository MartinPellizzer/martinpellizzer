Olax uliginosa

Olax uliginosa, commonly known as the swamp olax, is a shrub native to wetland habitats in tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils, often found in swampy areas and along riverbanks. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are clustered in terminal or axillary inflorescences.

Olax uliginosa is characterized by its adaptability to waterlogged conditions and its role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
