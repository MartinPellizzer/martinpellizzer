Ocotea alnifolia

Ocotea alnifolia, commonly known as the yellow sandarac tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The tree produces dark brown, resinous fruits that are used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural resins. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils, which contribute to its use in perfumery and pharmaceutical applications.

Ocotea alnifolia thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and montane forests.
