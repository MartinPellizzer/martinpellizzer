Oxalis barrelieri subsp. paraguayensis

Oxalis barrelieri subsp.

paraguayensis, commonly known as the Paraguay wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America. It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. It produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Oxalis barrelieri subsp.

paraguayensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
