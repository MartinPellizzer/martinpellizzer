Olea chinensis

Olea chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese olive, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically harvested when they are fully ripe and turn a dark, glossy color. Olea chinensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a versatile plant in horticulture and ecological settings.
