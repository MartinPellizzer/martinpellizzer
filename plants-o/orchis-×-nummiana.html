Orchis × nummiana

Orchis × nummiana, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a natural hybrid between Orchis moria and Orchis anthropophora.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family, characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing intricate, lip-shaped petals that are highly attractive to pollinators. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
