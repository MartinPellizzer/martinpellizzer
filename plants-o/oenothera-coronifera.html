Oenothera coronifera

Oenothera coronifera, commonly known as the crownflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and produces achenes as its seeds. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Oenothera coronifera is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
