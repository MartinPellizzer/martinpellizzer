Ononis natrix proles ramosissima

Ononis natrix proles ramosissima, commonly known as the hairy bedstraw, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems covered in fine, hairy pubescence. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems, typically from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This species is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in dry, rocky habitats.
