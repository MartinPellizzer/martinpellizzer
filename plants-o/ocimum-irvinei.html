Ocimum irvinei

Ocimum irvinei, commonly known as the Irvine basil, is a species of flowering plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a strong, spicy fragrance similar to other basil species.

Ocimum irvinei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of essential oils.
