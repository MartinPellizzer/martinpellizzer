Odontochilus brevistylis

Odontochilus brevistylis, commonly known as the short-styled odontochilus, is a rare and highly sought-after orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, narrow leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white to grayish pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale yellow, with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. It thrives in cool, shaded environments with high humidity, making it a challenging species for cultivation.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often collected and traded, leading to concerns about its conservation status in the wild.
