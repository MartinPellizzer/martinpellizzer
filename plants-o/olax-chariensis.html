Olax chariensis

Olax chariensis, commonly known as the African olive, is a small evergreen shrub native to eastern and southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Olacaceae and is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Olax chariensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
