Orchis syriaca

Orchis syriaca, commonly known as the Syrian orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its roots are fibrous and often form dense colonies, contributing to its ability to colonize disturbed or arid environments.

Orchis syriaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential ecological and medicinal properties.
