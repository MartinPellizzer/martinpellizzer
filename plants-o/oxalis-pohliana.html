Oxalis pohliana

Oxalis pohliana, commonly known as the coral bells, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in cooler climates.

Oxalis pohliana is also known for its ability to produce runners, which can help propagate the plant.
