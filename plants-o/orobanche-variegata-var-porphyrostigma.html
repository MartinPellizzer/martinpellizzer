Orobanche variegata var. porphyrostigma

Orobanche variegata var. porphyrostigma, commonly known as the purple-stemmed broomrape, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is characterized by its lack of leaves and its specialized root system for attaching to host roots. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are often pink or purple in color, and it is typically found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its life cycle is closely tied to specific host species, and it plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can have significant impacts on the growth and survival of its host plants.
