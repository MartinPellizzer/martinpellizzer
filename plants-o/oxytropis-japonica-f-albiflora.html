Oxytropis japonica f. albiflora

Oxytropis japonica f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered yellow vetch, is a perennial legume native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers, which are typically borne on elongated pedicels. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

This form is distinguished by its lighter floral coloration compared to the typical variety.
