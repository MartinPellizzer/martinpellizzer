Oenothera pennellii

Oenothera pennellii, commonly known as the Pennell's evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow or orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Oenothera pennellii is a herbaceous perennial with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
