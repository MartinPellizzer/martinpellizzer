Oncidium alexandrae f. hyperxanthum

Oncidium alexandrae f. hyperxanthum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a hybrid orchid species characterized by its vibrant, golden-yellow flowers.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America. The plant typically features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of showy, fragrant blooms. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
