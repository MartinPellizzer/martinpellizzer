Orthosia ramosa

Orthosia ramosa, commonly known as the slender orthosia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pale blue or white, with five petals and are arranged in clusters.

Orthosia ramosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
