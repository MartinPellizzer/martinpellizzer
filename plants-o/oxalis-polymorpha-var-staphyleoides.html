Oxalis polymorpha var. staphyleoides

Oxalis polymorpha var. staphyleoides, commonly known as the weeping wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish or purple tinge and produces small, yellow flowers that resemble those of the common sorrel. The plant thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens. Its stems are often pendulous, giving it a cascading appearance, and it is known for its ability to spread via rhizomes.

Oxalis polymorpha var. staphyleoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
