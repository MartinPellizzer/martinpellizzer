Opuntia kuntzei

Opuntia kuntzei, commonly known as the Kuntze prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in clusters of spines and glochids. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers, followed by edible, fleshy fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing typical adaptations to drought.

Opuntia kuntzei is also noted for its role in local ecosystems and traditional uses by indigenous communities.
