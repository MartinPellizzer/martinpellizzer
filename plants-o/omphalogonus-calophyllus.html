Omphalogonus calophyllus

Omphalogonus calophyllus, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree has a straight, slender trunk and dense, evergreen foliage. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
