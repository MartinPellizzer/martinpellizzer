Oxystophyllum excavatum

Oxystophyllum excavatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rock heath, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun conditions.

Oxystophyllum excavatum is often found in coastal and inland areas with a Mediterranean climate.
