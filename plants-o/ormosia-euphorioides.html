Ormosia euphorioides

Ormosia euphorioides, commonly known as the flame tree or flame of the forest, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The tree is known for its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a vibrant display during the dry season. Its bark is smooth and greyish, while its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant is also valued for its timber and has cultural significance in some regions.
