Odoglossa

Odoglossa, commonly known as the "spear-tongue orchid," is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia. Odoglossa species typically have small, delicate flowers with a distinctive elongated lip that resembles a spear. The leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and the plants often grow as epiphytes or terrestrials.

They are prized by orchid enthusiasts for their unique floral structure and relatively easy care requirements.
