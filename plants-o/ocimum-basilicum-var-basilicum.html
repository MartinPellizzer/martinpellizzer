Ocimum basilicum var. basilicum

Ocimum basilicum var. basilicum, commonly known as sweet basil, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Middle East.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, purple, or pink, which are clustered in whorls along the stems. Its essential oils, rich in compounds like linalool and methyl chavicol, contribute to its distinctive aroma and various medicinal properties.

Sweet basil is widely cultivated for culinary use and is also valued in traditional medicine for its therapeutic benefits.
