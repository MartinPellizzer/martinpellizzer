Oenothera hookeri var. hewettii

Oenothera hookeri var. hewettii, commonly known as Hewett's evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often branching and upright.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
