Oryza sativa var. culinaris

Oryza sativa var. culinaris, commonly known as rice, is a staple cereal crop widely cultivated for its grains.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, cylindrical grains that come in a variety of colors, including white, brown, and red. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with long, narrow leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. Its reproductive structures, called panicles, produce the rice grains that are harvested and processed for food.

The botanical features of this plant make it well-suited for cultivation in a range of climates, contributing to its global importance as a food source.
