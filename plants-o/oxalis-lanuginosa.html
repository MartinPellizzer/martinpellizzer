Oxalis lanuginosa

Oxalis lanuginosa, commonly known as the woolly wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that are often lobed or heart-shaped. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically appearing in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Oxalis lanuginosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
