Oncoba sulcata

Oncoba sulcata, commonly known as the African desert rose, is a shrub native to arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that reduce water loss, adapting to its harsh environment. The plant produces striking, pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep water sources.

Oncoba sulcata is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
