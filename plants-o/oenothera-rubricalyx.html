Oenothera rubricalyx

Oenothera rubricalyx, commonly known as the showy evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking, orange-yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Oenothera rubricalyx is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats, thriving in full sun.
