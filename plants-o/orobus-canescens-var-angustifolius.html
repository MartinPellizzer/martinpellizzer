Orobus canescens var. angustifolius

Orobus canescens var. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orobus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, hairy stems that often grow in dense clusters. The leaves are linear to lanceolate, with a grayish-green color due to the fine pubescence. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and arranged in spikes, typical of the Fabaceae family.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.
