Ostrya Ostrya Var. Japonica

Ostrya japonica, scientifically known as Ostrya ostrya var. japonica, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia. It is commonly found in China, Japan, and Korea, where it thrives in temperate climates. The tree is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and delicate, heart-shaped leaves. It produces small, yellowish catkins in early spring, followed by small, round nuts in the fall. This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in forest ecosystems.
