Orobus tricuspidata

Orobus tricuspidata, commonly known as the three-toothed orobus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive floral structure. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale yellow or greenish in color. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Orobus tricuspidata is often found in alpine meadows and is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological niche.
