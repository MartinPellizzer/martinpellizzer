Orchis × hybrida

Orchis × hybrida, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a cross between species of the Orchis genus, resulting in a plant with varied floral characteristics.

It typically features showy, often violet or purple flowers that are bilaterally symmetrical, a hallmark of the Orchidaceae family. The plant is perennial and can be found in moist, woodland habitats across parts of Europe and Asia. Its roots are fibrous, and it thrives in partial shade with well-drained soil.

Due to its hybrid nature, Orchis × hybrida exhibits a wide range of morphological variations.
