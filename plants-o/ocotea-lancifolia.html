Ocotea lancifolia

Ocotea lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. Its fragrant flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in axillary inflorescences.

The tree produces small, spherical berries that contain the seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
