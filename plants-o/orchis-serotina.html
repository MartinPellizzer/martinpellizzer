Orchis serotina

Orchis serotina, commonly known as the late orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stem and showy, violet to purple flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer to early autumn, hence its common name. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette at the base of the stem.

Orchis serotina thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodlands and meadows.
