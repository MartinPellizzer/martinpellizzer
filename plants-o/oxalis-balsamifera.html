Oxalis balsamifera

Oxalis balsamifera, commonly known as the balsam wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces a distinctive, fragrant, balsam-like resin, which gives it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Oxalis balsamifera is a perennial herb that can grow up to 30 inches tall, with hairy stems and serrated leaf margins.
