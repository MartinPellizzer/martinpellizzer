Onosma aucheriana subsp. stojanoffii

Onosma aucheriana subsp.

stojanoffii, commonly known as the Stojanoff's onosma, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters.

The plant features upright stems covered in glandular hairs, and its leaves are lanceolate with serrated edges. It produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers in late spring to early summer.

The species is characterized by its aromatic foliage and is often found in alpine meadows and dry steppe habitats.
