Ossaea macrophylla

Ossaea macrophylla, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its large, floating leaves that can reach up to two meters in diameter, providing a unique habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant's showy white flowers bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its roots anchor it in the muddy substrates of still or slow-moving waters.

Ossaea macrophylla plays an important role in maintaining water quality and supporting biodiversity in its native ecosystems.
