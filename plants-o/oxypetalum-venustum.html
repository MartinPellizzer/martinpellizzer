Oxypetalum venustum

Oxypetalum venustum, commonly known as the velvetleaf rose, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and a woody stem that becomes more branched with age. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Oxypetalum venustum is often found in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.
