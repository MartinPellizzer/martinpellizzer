Osyricera erosipetala

Osyricera erosipetala, commonly known as the slender-flowered osyricera, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It features slender, upright stems with small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture and a slight waxy coating. Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and limited distribution, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss and climate change, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring careful preservation.
