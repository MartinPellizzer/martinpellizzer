Ocotea congregata

Ocotea congregata, commonly known as the Brazilian cinnamom, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as cinnamon and laurel. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue berries. Its wood is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

The plant plays an important role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
