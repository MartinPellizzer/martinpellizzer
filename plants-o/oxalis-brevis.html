Oxalis brevis

Oxalis brevis, commonly known as the broadleaf wood sorrel, is a flowering plant belonging to the Oxalidaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. Native to parts of South America, it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces edible leaves and flowers, though they should be consumed in moderation.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
