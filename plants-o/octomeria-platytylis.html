Octomeria platytylis

Octomeria platytylis, commonly known as the flatleaf octomeria, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, helping it conserve water in its harsh environment. Its flowers are typically small and yellow, arranged in clusters that bloom during the dry season.

Octomeria platytylis is adapted to thrive in sandy or rocky soils with minimal rainfall.
