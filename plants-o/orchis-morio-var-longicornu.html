Orchis morio var. longicornu

Orchis morio var. longicornu, commonly known as the long-horned green orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive greenish-yellow flowers with a prominent, elongated lip. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodlands and meadows. It has a rhizomatous root system and produces showy, spurred flowers that bloom in early summer.

The variety longicornu is distinguished by its longer, more pronounced floral lip compared to the typical form.
