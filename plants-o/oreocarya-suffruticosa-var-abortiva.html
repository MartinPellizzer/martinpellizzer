Oreocarya suffruticosa var. abortiva

Oreocarya suffruticosa var. abortiva, commonly known as the Sierra buckwheat, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale pink. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are adapted to arid environments, helping it conserve water. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This variety is noted for its ecological importance, providing food for native pollinators and wildlife.
