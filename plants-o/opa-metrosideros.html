Opa metrosideros

Opa metrosideros, commonly known as the Tahitian flame tree, is a flowering plant native to the Society Islands in French Polynesia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. Its fruit is a legume pod that splits open to release its seeds.

Opa metrosideros is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
