Orthocarpus purpurascens var. pallidus

Orthocarpus purpurascens var. pallidus, commonly known as the pale orchid vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its showy, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and produces small, fleshy berries that are typically yellow when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
