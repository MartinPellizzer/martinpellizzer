Ophrys × peteri

Ophrys × peteri, commonly known as the orchid bee orchid, is a hybrid species within the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its intricate floral morphology, featuring a labellum that mimics the appearance of a female bee, a trait used to attract male bees for pollination. The plant produces a single, showy flower with a complex structure that includes a spurred ovary and a column for pollen transfer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate regions of Europe.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in many areas.
