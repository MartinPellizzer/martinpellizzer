Orthopogon flaccidus

Orthopogon flaccidus, commonly known as the flaccid grasshopper orchid, is a flowering plant native to parts of Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils in arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellowish, and it blooms during the cooler months of the year.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
