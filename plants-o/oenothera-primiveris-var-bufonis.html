Oenothera primiveris var. bufonis

Oenothera primiveris var. bufonis, commonly known as the buffalo nightshade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and its role in supporting pollinator populations.
