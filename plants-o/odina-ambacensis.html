Odina ambacensis

Odina ambacensis, commonly known as the Malagasy sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Odina ambacensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
