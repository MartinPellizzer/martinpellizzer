Olinia chimanimani

Olinia chimanimani, commonly known as the Chimanimani olive, is a small evergreen tree native to the Chimanimani Mountains in eastern Zimbabwe.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family, which includes olives and other ornamental trees. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns from green to yellow as it matures.

Olinia chimanimani is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local communities.
