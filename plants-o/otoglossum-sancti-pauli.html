Otoglossum sancti-pauli

Otoglossum sancti-pauli, commonly known as the Saint Paul's orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, showy flowers. The plant typically produces small, fragrant blooms in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with a distinctive lip that is adorned with intricate patterns. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Otoglossum sancti-pauli thrives in humid, well-drained environments and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in botanical gardens and private collections.
