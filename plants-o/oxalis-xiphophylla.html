Oxalis xiphophylla

Oxalis xiphophylla, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a striking plant native to South Africa.

It is a perennial herb that features showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its unique appearance and long blooming period make it a favorite among botanists and gardeners alike.
