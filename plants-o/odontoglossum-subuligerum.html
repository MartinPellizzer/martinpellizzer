Odontoglossum subuligerum

Odontoglossum subuligerum, commonly known as the slender-leaf orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, pale yellow to white flowers with a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows in high-altitude cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. It is a terrestrial orchid, often found in rocky or shaded habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated by enthusiasts, though it remains relatively rare in the wild.
