Oxytropis jurtzevii

Oxytropis jurtzevii, commonly known as the Tien Shan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia, particularly found in the Tien Shan mountain range.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, often at high altitudes. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish to brownish pea-like flowers. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.

Oxytropis jurtzevii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
