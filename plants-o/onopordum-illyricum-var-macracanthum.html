Onopordum illyricum var. macracanthum

Onopordum illyricum var. macracanthum, commonly known as the Illlyrian thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It features striking silver-gray foliage with spiny margins and produces large, showy purple flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant is characterized by its robust growth habit and is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are typically lobed and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual impact.
