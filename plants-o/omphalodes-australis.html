Omphalodes australis

Omphalodes australis, commonly known as the Australian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Omphalodes australis is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
