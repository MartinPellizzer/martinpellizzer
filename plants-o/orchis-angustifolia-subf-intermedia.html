Orchis angustifolia subf. intermedia

Orchis angustifolia subf.

intermedia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved twayblade, is a member of the Orchidaceae family. It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

The plant features a slender, leafless stem that bears a single, slender spike of pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, usually appearing at the base of the plant.

The species is characterized by its reduced floral structure and is often found in temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.
