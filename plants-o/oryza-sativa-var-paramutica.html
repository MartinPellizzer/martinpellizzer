Oryza sativa var. paramutica

Oryza sativa var. paramutica, commonly known as the Paramutica rice, is a less commonly cultivated variety of rice.

It is characterized by its unique genetic makeup, which includes specific mutations that affect its growth patterns and grain development. The plant typically exhibits a shorter stature compared to other rice varieties, with a more compact growth habit. It is adapted to specific environmental conditions, often thriving in regions with particular soil and climate characteristics.

Due to its genetic distinctiveness, it holds potential for research in crop improvement and genetic diversity studies.
