Othonna pinnatifida

Othonna pinnatifida, commonly known as the narrow-leaved othonna, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its sparsely branched stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in spring. It is adapted to arid environments and stores water in its fleshy stems.

Othonna pinnatifida is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
