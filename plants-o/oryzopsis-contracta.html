Oryzopsis contracta

Oryzopsis contracta, commonly known as the narrowleaf ricegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils.

Oryzopsis contracta is adapted to arid conditions and is often found in regions with low precipitation.
