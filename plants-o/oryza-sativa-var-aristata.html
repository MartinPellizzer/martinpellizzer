Oryza sativa var. aristata

Oryza sativa var. aristata, commonly known as the aristate rice, is a wild rice species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, narrow leaves. The plant produces panicles of tiny, greenish-white flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its grains are smaller and less cultivated compared to domesticated rice varieties.

This species is of interest to botanists and agricultural researchers due to its potential for genetic diversity and adaptation to various environmental conditions.
