Oxalis lasiophylla

Oxalis lasiophylla, commonly known as the velvetleaf wood sorrel, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive velvety texture and a deep green color. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Oxalis lasiophylla is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and is sometimes used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance.
