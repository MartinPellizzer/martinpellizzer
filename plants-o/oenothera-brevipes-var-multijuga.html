Oenothera brevipes var. multijuga

Oenothera brevipes var. multijuga, commonly known as the narrowleaf evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny, oval seeds. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it well-adapted to drought conditions and low-nutrient soils.
