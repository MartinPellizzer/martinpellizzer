Onobrychis laxiflora subsp. macrodonta

Onobrychis laxiflora subsp.

macrodonta, commonly known as the large-flowered burr medic, is a perennial legume native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces.

The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, spherical clusters, followed by inflated, spiny pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its forage qualities and ecological role in soil nitrogen fixation.
