Oedina pendens

Oedina pendens, commonly known as the hanging fern, is a species of fern characterized by its delicate, cascading fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees or rocks in its natural habitat. Its fronds are usually slender and arching, creating a lush, hanging appearance.

Oedina pendens prefers high humidity and indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
