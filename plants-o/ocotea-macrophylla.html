Ocotea macrophylla

Ocotea macrophylla, commonly known as the broad-leaved ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its wood is highly valued for its aromatic properties and durability, making it a significant resource in local economies.

The species also plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, supporting various forms of wildlife.
