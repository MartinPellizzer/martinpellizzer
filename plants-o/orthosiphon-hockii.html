Orthosiphon hockii

Orthosiphon hockii, commonly known as Java mint or hanger's weed, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its slender, square stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its small, tubular flowers are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem.

Orthosiphon hockii is often used in traditional medicine for its purported diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.
