Oxalis vulgaris var. caerulea

Oxalis vulgaris var. caerulea, commonly known as the blue oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a striking blue-green coloration and produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to purple. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soil and partial shade. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.

Oxalis vulgaris var. caerulea is also valued for its ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
