Ocimum ellenbeckii

Ocimum ellenbeckii, commonly known as the Ellenbeck basil, is a species of aromatic herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like mint and lavender. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, opposite leaves that are often fragrant. Its flowers are typically white or pale purple, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the branches.

Ocimum ellenbeckii is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, particularly in traditional South American herbal practices.
