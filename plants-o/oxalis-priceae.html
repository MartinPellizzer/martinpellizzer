Oxalis priceae

Oxalis priceae, commonly known as the Price's wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, bead-like fruits that contain seeds.

Oxalis priceae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
