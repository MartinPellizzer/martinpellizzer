Oxalis violacea f. albida

Oxalis violacea f. albida, commonly known as the white wood sorrel, is a variant of the common wood sorrel characterized by its pale white or cream-colored foliage.

This form exhibits reduced pigmentation compared to the typical green leaves of the species, giving it a strikingly different appearance. It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is native to regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are often pink or white, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Its foliage is often used in horticulture for its unique aesthetic qualities in shaded garden settings.
