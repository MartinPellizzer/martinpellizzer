Orobus serotinus

Orobus serotinus, commonly known as the late-flowering bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often in woodland or scrub habitats. Orobus serotinus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
