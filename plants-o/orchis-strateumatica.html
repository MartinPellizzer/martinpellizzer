Orchis strateumatica

Orchis strateumatica, commonly known as the early purple orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in woodland habitats and thrives in shaded, moist environments. The plant produces striking violet or purple flowers in late spring, which are arranged in a spike-like inflorescence. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinctive white or pale midrib.

Orchis strateumatica is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is known for its complex pollination mechanisms involving specific insect species.
