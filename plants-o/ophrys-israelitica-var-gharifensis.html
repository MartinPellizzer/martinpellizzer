Ophrys israelitica var. gharifensis

Ophrys israelitica var. gharifensis, commonly known as the Gharif Valley bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the Gharif Valley in Israel.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure that mimics the appearance of a female bee, a trait known as pseudocopulation, which aids in pollination by male bees. The plant typically grows in rocky and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brownish in color, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts in its native region.
