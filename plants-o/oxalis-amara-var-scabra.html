Oxalis amara var. scabra

Oxalis amara var. scabra, commonly known as the rough wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive rough texture, and its flowers are typically pale yellow with five petals. The plant produces small, edible berries that turn red when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Oxalis amara var. scabra is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
