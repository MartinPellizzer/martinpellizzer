Olostyla corymbosa

Olostyla corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Olostyla corymbosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
