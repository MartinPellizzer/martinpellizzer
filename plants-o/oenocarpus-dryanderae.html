Oenocarpus dryanderae

Oenocarpus dryanderae, commonly known as the breadnut tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, palm-like leaves and distinctive fruit structure. The tree produces a large, fibrous fruit that contains a single, starchy seed, which is the primary edible part. Its growth habits and ecological role make it an important species in tropical ecosystems.
