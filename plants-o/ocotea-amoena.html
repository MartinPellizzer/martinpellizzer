Ocotea amoena

Ocotea amoena, commonly known as the fragrant ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurel and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns from green to red as it matures.

Ocotea amoena is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates for its aesthetic appeal.
