Oryza sativa subsp. brevindica

Oryza sativa subsp.

brevindica, commonly known as the short-grain rice, is a subspecies of rice native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its shorter grain length and higher amylose content compared to other rice varieties.

This subspecies is often used in traditional cuisines for its sticky texture when cooked. Botanically, it has a prostrate growth habit and produces small, white flowers.

It is cultivated for its grains, which are rich in carbohydrates and essential nutrients.
