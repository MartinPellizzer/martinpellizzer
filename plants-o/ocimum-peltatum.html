Ocimum peltatum

Ocimum peltatum, commonly known as the velvet leaf basil, is a herbaceous plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are rich in essential oils, giving it a distinct aromatic profile. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. Ocimum peltatum thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

It is often used in culinary applications and traditional medicine due to its flavorful leaves and potential health benefits.
