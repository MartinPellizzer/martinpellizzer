Opuntia sphaerica var. rauppiana

Opuntia sphaerica var. rauppiana, commonly known as the Raupp's prickly pear, is a succulent cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical stems that are covered in spines and glochids, which provide protection from herbivores. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tuna, is edible and often used in local cuisines.

This variety is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water to thrive.
