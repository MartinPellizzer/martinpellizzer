Ophiopogon compressus

Ophiopogon compressus, commonly known as the Japanese forest grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features dense, dark green foliage that forms a compact, mat-like structure, often carpeting the forest floor. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or pale green flowers in early summer, followed by dark blue berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often used in ornamental landscaping for its lush, ground-covering appearance.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is valued for its resilience and aesthetic appeal in shaded gardens.
