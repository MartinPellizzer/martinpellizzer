Oncidium melanops

Oncidium melanops, commonly known as the black orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and its striking, dark purple to black flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic, often found in tropical rainforests. Oncidium melanops produces fragrant flowers that can last for several weeks, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Its unique coloration and ease of care have made it a favored ornamental plant among orchid enthusiasts.
