Odontites australis

Odontites australis, commonly known as the Southern Toothbrush, is a flowering plant native to southern South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant produces yellow flowers with prominent white ray florets, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in grasslands and scrublands.

Odontites australis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
