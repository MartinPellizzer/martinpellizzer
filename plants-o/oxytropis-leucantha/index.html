Oxytropis Leucantha

Oxytropis leucantha is a rare and striking flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America. Known for its tall, spindly stems and clusters of pale blue to white flowers, it captivates botanists and nature enthusiasts alike. This legume belongs to the Fabaceae family and is often found in dry, rocky habitats where it thrives with minimal water. Its unique appearance and limited distribution make it a subject of both scientific interest and conservation concern. Despite its beauty, Oxytropis leucantha remains underappreciated and vulnerable to habitat loss and climate change.
