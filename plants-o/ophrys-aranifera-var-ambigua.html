Ophrys aranifera var. ambigua

Ophrys aranifera var. ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to specific regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its intricate floral structure, which closely resembles a female bee, a feature evolved to attract male bees for pollination. The plant produces showy, bee-like flowers with a unique coloration and pattern that mimic the appearance of its pollinators. It thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and heathlands.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
