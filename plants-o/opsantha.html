Opsantha

Opsantha, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruits are capsules that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Opsantha is often found in disturbed habitats and is considered an invasive species in some areas.
