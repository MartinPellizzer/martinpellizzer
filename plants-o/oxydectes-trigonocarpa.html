Oxydectes trigonocarpa

Oxydectes trigonocarpa, commonly known as the three-angled fig, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces small, round fig-like fruits that are edible and have a sweet, delicate flavor. Its leaves are thick, glossy, and have a distinctive three-angled shape, which gives it its scientific name.

The plant is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its culinary uses in local cuisines.
