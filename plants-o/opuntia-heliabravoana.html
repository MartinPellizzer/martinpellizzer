Opuntia heliabravoana

Opuntia heliabravoana, commonly known as the Heliabravo cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its flat, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in drought conditions. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are pollinated by various insects and birds. Its foliage is covered in small, hair-like spines that provide protection from herbivores.

Opuntia heliabravoana is also known for its ability to propagate through offsets, making it a resilient and hardy plant in harsh environments.
