Odontarrhena kavadarcensis

Odontarrhena kavadarcensis, commonly known as the Kavadar Valley grass, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of eastern Afghanistan.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, needle-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
