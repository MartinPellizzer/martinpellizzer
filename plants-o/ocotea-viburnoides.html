Ocotea viburnoides

Ocotea viburnoides, commonly known as the Mexican laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America and northern South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves that are glossy and lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers arranged in clusters, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for some bird species. Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in traditional crafts and furniture.

Ocotea viburnoides thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.
