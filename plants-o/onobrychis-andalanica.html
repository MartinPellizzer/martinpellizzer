Onobrychis andalanica

Onobrychis andalanica, commonly known as the Andalusian burclover, is a perennial legume native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spikes. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is well adapted to Mediterranean climates. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Onobrychis andalanica is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
