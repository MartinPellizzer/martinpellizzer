Oncidium wittmannianum

Oncidium wittmannianum, commonly known as the Wittmann's orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, drooping inflorescences that bear numerous fragrant, brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers come in shades of yellow, orange, and red, often with contrasting patterns.

Oncidium wittmannianum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
