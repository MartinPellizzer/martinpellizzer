Othonna herrei subsp. armiana

Othonna herrei subsp.

armiana, commonly known as the Cape Flax, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette growth habit and fleshy, gray-green leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in a compact inflorescence, typically during the summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established. Othonna herrei subsp.

armiana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
