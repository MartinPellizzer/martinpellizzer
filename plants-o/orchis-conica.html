Orchis conica

Orchis conica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stem and linear leaves. The plant produces a single, showy spike of pale yellow to greenish flowers, which are often tinged with red or brown. Orchis conica typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and flies.
