Orchis cucullata

Orchis cucullata, commonly known as the hooded orchid, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, hood-shaped flower that resembles a small helmet. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption.

The hooded orchid is noted for its delicate appearance and ecological significance in supporting pollinators.
