Ozothamnus bilobus

Ozothamnus bilobus, commonly known as the two-lobed burr bush, is a shrub native to the south-western regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by spiny, globular fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Ozothamnus bilobus is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
