Oncidium longicuspe

Oncidium longicuspe, commonly known as the long-spurred orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, slender petals and sepals that give it its distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or green with a contrasting lip, and it blooms during the rainy season.

Oncidium longicuspe is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
