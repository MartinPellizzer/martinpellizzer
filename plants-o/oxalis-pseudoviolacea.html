Oxalis pseudoviolacea

Oxalis pseudoviolacea, commonly known as violetleaf wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, violet-pink flowers. The plant produces distinctive, spurred petals and has a creeping growth habit with tuberous roots. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Oxalis pseudoviolacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
