Ononis laxiflora subsp. grandiflora

Ononis laxiflora subsp.

grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered whitlow grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, ascending stems that bear small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-tolerant nature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
