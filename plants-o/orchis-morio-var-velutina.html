Orchis morio var. velutina

Orchis morio var. velutina, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has a rosette of lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are borne on a tall, unbranched stem and are often adorned with intricate patterns.

The variety velutina is distinguished by its velvety texture on the leaves and petals, giving it a unique appearance.
