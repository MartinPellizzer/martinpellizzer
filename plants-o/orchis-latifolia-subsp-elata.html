Orchis latifolia subsp. elata

Orchis latifolia subsp.

elata, commonly known as the tall broad-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features tall, erect stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with broad, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers in late spring to early summer, arranged in a loose raceme. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological importance as a food source for various pollinators.
