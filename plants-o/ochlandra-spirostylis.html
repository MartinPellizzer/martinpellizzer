Ochlandra spirostylis

Ochlandra spirostylis, commonly known as the spiral-styled orchid, is a unique species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate floral structure, which includes a distinctive spiral arrangement of stamens. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are often fragrant and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Ochlandra spirostylis is considered a conservation concern in its native range.
