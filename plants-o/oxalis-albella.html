Oxalis albella

Oxalis albella, commonly known as the white wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each leaflet being heart-shaped and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with five petals and a distinctive yellow center. Oxalis albella thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

It is a perennial plant that prefers well-drained soil and can be cultivated as an ornamental in gardens.
