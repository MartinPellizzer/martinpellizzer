Octella verticillata

Octella verticillata, commonly known as the whorled morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive whorled arrangement of leaves, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Octella verticillata is a perennial herb that is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
