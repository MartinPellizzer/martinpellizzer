Ochlandra ridleyi

Ochlandra ridleyi, commonly known as the Malayan orchid, is a tropical plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features striking, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, with intricate patterns on the petals. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the rich, organic soil of its habitat.

Ochlandra ridleyi is often found growing on trees or in dense undergrowth, showcasing its epiphytic nature.
