Oxalis beneprotecta

Oxalis beneprotecta, commonly known as the shieldleaf wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive shield-like appearance, often adorned with prominent veins. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Botanically, it is characterized by its basal rosette growth habit and is part of the Oxalidaceae family.
