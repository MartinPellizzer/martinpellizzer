Oxalis gracilicaulis

Oxalis gracilicaulis, commonly known as slender-stemmed wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its stems are slender and often twining, while its leaves are heart-shaped with a distinctive veining pattern.

Oxalis gracilicaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
