Oenothera macrocarpa var. oklahomensis

Oenothera macrocarpa var. oklahomensis, commonly known as the Oklahoma evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the central United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and native plant landscaping.
