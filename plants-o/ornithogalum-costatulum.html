Ornithogalum costatulum

Ornithogalum costatulum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of white, star-shaped flowers, which are typically arranged in umbels. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves that are often marked with distinctive greenish-white stripes. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ornithogalum costatulum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
