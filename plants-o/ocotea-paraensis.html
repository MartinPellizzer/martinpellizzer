Ocotea paraensis

Ocotea paraensis, commonly known as the Brazilian camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The tree produces oval, woody fruits that contain one or two seeds. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils, which have been used in traditional medicine for their medicinal properties.

Ocotea paraensis is also valued for its durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
