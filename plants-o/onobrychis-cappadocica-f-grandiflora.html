Onobrychis cappadocica f. grandiflora

Onobrychis cappadocica f. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered Cappadocian burr vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive burr-like pods that contain several seeds. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
