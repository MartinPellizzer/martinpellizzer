Oenothera biennis subsp. austromontana

Oenothera biennis subsp.

austromontana, commonly known as the Australian evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats like grasslands and rocky outcrops.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
