Osbeckia ciliaris

Osbeckia ciliaris, commonly known as the silky willow or silky oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant has hairy, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Osbeckia ciliaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and blooms.
