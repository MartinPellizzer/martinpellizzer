Oplismenus undulatifolius f. japonicus

Oplismenus undulatifolius f. japonicus, commonly known as the Japanese wavegrass, is a varietal form of the genus Oplismenus, characterized by its distinctive, undulating leaf blades.

Native to East Asia, particularly Japan, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and wetlands. The plant exhibits a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread vegetatively and form dense mats. Its leaves are typically narrow, elongated, and have a wavy, undulating edge, giving it a unique visual appearance.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to stabilize soil in wetland habitats.
