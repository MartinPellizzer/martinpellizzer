Okea

Okea, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Okea thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
