Ophiorrhiza bifida

Ophiorrhiza bifida, commonly known as the Split-leaf Ophiorrhiza, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small, shrub-like species that typically grows in moist, tropical environments. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that often split into two lobes, giving it its common name. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

The plant is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy or saline soils.
