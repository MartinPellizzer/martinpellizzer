Orleanesia maculata

Orleanesia maculata, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spotted leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the rich, organic soil.

Orleanesia maculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by supporting pollinators.
