Oenothera leucocarpa

Oenothera leucocarpa, commonly known as the white-stemmed evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oily seeds. Oenothera leucocarpa is adapted to arid and rocky environments, showcasing its resilience to drought.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
