Orchis biloba

Orchis biloba, commonly known as the nodding orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its flowers are borne in a loose spike.

Orchis biloba is known for its fragrant blooms and is often found in association with other orchid species in its habitat.
