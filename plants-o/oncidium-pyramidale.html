Oncidium pyramidale

Oncidium pyramidale, commonly known as the yellow butterfly orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It features long, arching stems with numerous showy, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow with red or orange markings. The plant has a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in its natural habitat. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air and surrounding environment.

Oncidium pyramidale blooms in the summer and is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its elegant appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
