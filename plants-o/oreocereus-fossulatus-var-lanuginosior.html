Oreocereus fossulatus var. lanuginosior

Oreocereus fossulatus var. lanuginosior, commonly known as the woolly orchid cactus, is a unique cactus species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems covered in dense, woolly white hairs that provide insulation and protection against intense sunlight and drought. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for both horticulturists and botanists.
