Odontoglossum maculatum

Odontoglossum maculatum, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial herb with a short, stocky rhizome and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically blooms in the spring and has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Odontoglossum maculatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
