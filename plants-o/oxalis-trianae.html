Oxalis trianae

Oxalis trianae, commonly known as the Mexican wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the family Oxalidaceae and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. Its leaves contain oxalic acid, which can be toxic if ingested in large quantities.

Oxalis trianae is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and flowers.
