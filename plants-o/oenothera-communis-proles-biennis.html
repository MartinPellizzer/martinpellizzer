Oenothera communis proles biennis

Oenothera communis proles biennis, commonly known as evening primrose, is a biennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy yellow or orange flowers that open in the evening, hence its common name. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsules that contain numerous small, oval seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

The species is known for its medicinal uses, particularly for its oil-rich seeds.
