Ostryoderris brownii

Ostryoderris brownii, commonly known as the New Zealand black pine, is a species of conifer native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dark, fissured bark and long, slender cones. The plant is adapted to the temperate rainforest environments of the South Island. It plays an important role in the local ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
