Orchis × colemanii nothosubsp. colemanii

Orchis × colemanii nothosubsp.

colemanii, commonly known as the Coleman's orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the northeastern United States. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and requires well-drained, loamy soil. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged along the stem, contributing to its ornamental value.

The species is notable for its genetic diversity and is often studied for its role in orchid hybridization and conservation.
