Oxystophyllum atropurpureum

Oxystophyllum atropurpureum, commonly known as the purple fringed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and often have a reddish or purplish hue, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Oxystophyllum atropurpureum is valued in gardens for its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
