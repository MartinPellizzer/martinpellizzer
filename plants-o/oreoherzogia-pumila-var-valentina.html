Oreoherzogia pumila var. valentina

Oreoherzogia pumila var. valentina, commonly known as the Valentine's dwarf sunflower, is a compact, low-growing perennial plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It features small, daisy-like yellow flowers with a distinctive dark center, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, gray-green leaves that help it retain moisture in its arid environment. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its unique appearance and seasonal blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
