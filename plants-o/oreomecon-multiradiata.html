Oreomecon multiradiata

Oreomecon multiradiata, commonly known as the many-rayed poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a stout, erect stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically arranged in a single, terminal head, with numerous petals radiating outward.

Oreomecon multiradiata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant display.
