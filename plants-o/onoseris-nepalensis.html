Onoseris nepalensis

Onoseris nepalensis, commonly known as the Nepal sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and northern India.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its yellow-orange flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Onoseris nepalensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
