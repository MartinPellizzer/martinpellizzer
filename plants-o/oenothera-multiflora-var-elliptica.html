Oenothera multiflora var. elliptica

Oenothera multiflora var. elliptica, commonly known as the elliptic evening primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features lance-shaped, elliptic leaves that are typically arranged alternately along its stem. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its flowering stems are often branched and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
