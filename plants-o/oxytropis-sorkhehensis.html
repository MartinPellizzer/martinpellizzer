Oxytropis sorkhehensis

Oxytropis sorkhehensis, commonly known as the Sorkheh Mountain locoweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its leaves are often compound, and it produces pods that contain several seeds, contributing to its reproductive strategy in its native habitat.
