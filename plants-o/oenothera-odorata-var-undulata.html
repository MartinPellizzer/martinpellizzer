Oenothera odorata var. undulata

Oenothera odorata var. undulata, commonly known as the undulating evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oily seeds. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to native plant gardens.
