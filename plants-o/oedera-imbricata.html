Oedera imbricata

Oedera imbricata, commonly known as the rock wallaby bush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typically blooming in spring. Oedera imbricata is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a hardy plant in harsh conditions.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in difficult landscapes.
