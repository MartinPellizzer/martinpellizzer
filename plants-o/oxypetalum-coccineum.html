Oxypetalum coccineum

Oxypetalum coccineum, commonly known as the scarlet bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking red, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are clustered in racemes, and the plant is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Oxypetalum coccineum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
