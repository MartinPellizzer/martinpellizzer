Ophrys × monachorum subsp. enzae

Ophrys × monachorum subsp.

enzae, commonly known as the Enza orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Balkan region. It is characterized by its intricate, bee-like flower structure, which plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in forested areas. Its flowers are predominantly yellow with brown and white markings, mimicking the appearance of female bees to attract male pollinators.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological significance in its native habitat.
