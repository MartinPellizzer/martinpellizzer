Oxalis hirta var. hirta

Oxalis hirta var. hirta, commonly known as the hairy wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive hairy texture and produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is a perennial herb that thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist soil conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
