Oxalis bifurca

Oxalis bifurca, commonly known as the two-lobed wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each leaflet having a distinct, heart-shaped base. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Oxalis bifurca is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
