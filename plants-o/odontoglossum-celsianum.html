Odontoglossum celsianum

Odontoglossum celsianum, commonly known as the Celsian orchid, is a species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy flowers. The plant typically blooms in the spring and produces inflorescences with numerous violet to purple flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Odontoglossum celsianum is valued in horticulture for its beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses due to its specific environmental requirements.
