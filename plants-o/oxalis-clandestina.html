Oxalis clandestina

Oxalis clandestina, commonly known as the hidden wood sorrel, is a delicate flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with intricate patterns and produces small, yellow flowers in the spring. The plant thrives in shady, moist environments such as woodlands and forest floors. Its botanical name reflects its elusive nature, as it is often difficult to spot in its natural habitat.

Oxalis clandestina is valued for its ornamental beauty and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
