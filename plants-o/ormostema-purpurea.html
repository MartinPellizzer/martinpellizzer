Ormostema purpurea

Ormostema purpurea, commonly known as the purple orchid vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking purple flowers and climbing habit, often found in humid, shaded environments. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces fragrant blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its botanical classification places it in the Orchidaceae family, highlighting its relation to other orchids.

Ormostema purpurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
