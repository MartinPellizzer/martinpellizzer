Oenothera hirta var. jonesii

Oenothera hirta var. jonesii, commonly known as Jones' evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and showy yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is part of the Onagraceae family and is known for its ecological importance to pollinators.
