Oxalis aurea

Oxalis aurea, commonly known as golden wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive yellowish-green color and produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant is characterized by its succulent stems and leaves, which help it store water in its native arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Oxalis aurea is also noted for its ability to bloom throughout the growing season, adding a delicate ornamental value to gardens.
