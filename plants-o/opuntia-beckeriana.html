Opuntia beckeriana

Opuntia beckeriana, commonly known as the Becker's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in clusters of spines and glochids. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers, which are followed by edible, fleshy fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting well to poor soil conditions.

Opuntia beckeriana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local cultures.
