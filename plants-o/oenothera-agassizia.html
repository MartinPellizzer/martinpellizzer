Oenothera agassizia

Oenothera agassizia, commonly known as Agassiz's evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Onagraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Oenothera agassizia is known for its resilience and is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.
