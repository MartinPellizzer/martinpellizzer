Oxalis pedunculata

Oxalis pedunculata, commonly known as the woodland sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped lobes and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers have five petals and are often accompanied by five sepals that resemble petals.

Oxalis pedunculata is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
