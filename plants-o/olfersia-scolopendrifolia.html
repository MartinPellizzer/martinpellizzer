Olfersia scolopendrifolia

Olfersia scolopendrifolia, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, spider-like appearance, with long, thin petals that resemble the legs of a spider. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its flowers are usually green or yellowish with intricate patterns, and it produces small, dark brown fruits.

Olfersia scolopendrifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
