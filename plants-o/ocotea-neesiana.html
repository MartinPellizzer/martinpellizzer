Ocotea neesiana

Ocotea neesiana, commonly known as the black ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its dark, aromatic wood, which is highly valued in the timber industry. The plant typically grows in humid lowland forests and has lance-shaped, glossy leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces dark, spherical berries that contain the seeds.

Ocotea neesiana is also noted for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional remedies.
