Odontonema fuchsioides

Odontonema fuchsioides, commonly known as the pink flame or fuchsia-like plant, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically pink to purple, with two lips, and are borne in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Odontonema fuchsioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
