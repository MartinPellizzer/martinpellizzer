Ocotea sinaiana

Ocotea sinaiana, commonly known as the Madagascar cinnamon or Sinaita cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like laurel and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils, giving it a distinct aromatic quality.

Ocotea sinaiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
