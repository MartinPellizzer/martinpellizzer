Ononis oligophylla

Ononis oligophylla, commonly known as the lesser whitlowgrass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically features wiry stems and small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, dry fruits known as caryopses. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed habitats.

Ononis oligophylla is adapted to dry conditions and is considered a characteristic species of Mediterranean scrub and coastal dunes.
