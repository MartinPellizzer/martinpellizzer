Oxycoccus macrocarpos

Oxycoccus macrocarpos, commonly known as the large-fruited bearberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, leathery leaves and bright red berries that are edible for humans and wildlife. Its berries are rich in antioxidants and have been used in traditional medicine.

Oxycoccus macrocarpos thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in boreal and subarctic environments.
