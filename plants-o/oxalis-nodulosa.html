Oxalis nodulosa

Oxalis nodulosa, commonly known as the nodular wood sorrel, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating and produces clusters of delicate, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its stems are often hairy, and it has a compact growth habit.

Oxalis nodulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
