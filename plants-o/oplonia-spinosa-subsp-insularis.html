Oplonia spinosa subsp. insularis

Oplonia spinosa subsp.

insularis, commonly known as the island spurge, is a succulent shrub native to specific island environments. It is characterized by its fleshy, spiny leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers.

The plant is adapted to arid and rocky habitats, showcasing a range of morphological features for survival in such conditions. Its subspecies status highlights its distinct ecological and geographical distribution.

Due to habitat fragmentation, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
