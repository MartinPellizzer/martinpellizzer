Odontoglossum helgae

Odontoglossum helgae, commonly known as the Ecuadorian orchid, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate floral structure. The plant typically produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often with a distinctive lip petal. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Odontoglossum helgae is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts due to its ornamental value and relatively manageable care requirements.
