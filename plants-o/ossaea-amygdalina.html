Ossaea amygdalina

Ossaea amygdalina, commonly known as the almond lily, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant features strap-like leaves and produces small, edible fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Ossaea amygdalina is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
