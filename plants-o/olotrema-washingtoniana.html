Olotrema washingtoniana

Olotrema washingtoniana, commonly known as the Washington's mistletoe, is a parasitic plant native to the southeastern United States.

It grows on the branches of hardwood trees such as hickory and oak, relying on its host for nutrients and water. The plant produces small, white, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are evergreen, glossy, and typically arranged in pairs along the stems.

Olotrema washingtoniana plays a unique role in its ecosystem, contributing to the biodiversity of forest canopies.
