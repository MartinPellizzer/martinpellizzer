Opuntia longispina var. corrugata

Opuntia longispina var. corrugata, commonly known as the corrugated prickly pear, is a cactus species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its flattened, ribbed stems that are covered in spines and glochids. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tuna, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
