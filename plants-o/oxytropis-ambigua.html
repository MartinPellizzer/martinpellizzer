Oxytropis ambigua

Oxytropis ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous locoweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and erect, sparsely branched stems. The plant produces small, yellow to brown flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Oxytropis ambigua is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe.

It is known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock.
