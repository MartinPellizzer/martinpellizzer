Oenothera strigulosa var. pubens

Oenothera strigulosa var. pubens, commonly known as the pubescent evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the United States.

It belongs to the genus Oenothera, which includes various species of evening primroses known for their showy, yellow to orange flowers. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it the varietal name "pubens.". It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils.

The flowers bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths.
