Orchis mascula var. tricornis

Orchis mascula var. tricornis, commonly known as the Three-cornered Orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or grassland habitats. The plant features a slender, erect stem with a single, showy orchid-like flower that has a distinctive three-lobed lip. Its flowers are usually pink to purple in color and bloom from late spring to early summer.

The species is notable for its specialized pollination mechanism, relying on specific insect pollinators for reproduction.
