Ononis aragonensis

Ononis aragonensis, commonly known as Aragon's whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by small, pod-like fruits. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its root system helps prevent soil erosion, making it ecologically significant in its native habitat.
