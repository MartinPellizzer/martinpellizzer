Orchis majalis f. barlae

Orchis majalis f. barlae, commonly known as the Barlae orchid, is a rare and distinctive variant of the common twayblade orchid.

It is a terrestrial orchid belonging to the Orchidaceae family, characterized by its unique floral morphology and specialized growth habits. The plant typically features a slender, leafless stem with a single, showy, greenish-yellow flower that blooms in late spring. It thrives in shaded, moist woodland habitats and is often found in nutrient-rich, calcareous soils.

Due to its rarity and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in many regions.
