Ophiorrhiza trichoclada

Ophiorrhiza trichoclada, commonly known as the hairy swamp hibiscus, is a flowering plant species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and is typically found in wetland or swampy environments. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it its common name. Its flowers are showy, with five petals and a prominent yellow stamen, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

Ophiorrhiza trichoclada plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats and is of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
