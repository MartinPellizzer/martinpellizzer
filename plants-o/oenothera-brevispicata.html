Oenothera brevispicata

Oenothera brevispicata, commonly known as the short-horned evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and yellow flowers that open in the evening. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and prairies.

Oenothera brevispicata is part of the evening primrose family, which is known for its nocturnal pollination by moths.
