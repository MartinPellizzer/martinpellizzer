Othonna euphorbioides subsp. jarmilae

Othonna euphorbioides subsp.

jarmilae, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry plant, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa. It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange-red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the Asteraceae family.
