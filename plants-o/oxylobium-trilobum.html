Oxylobium trilobum

Oxylobium trilobum, commonly known as the three-lobed oxylobium, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. Its root system is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

Oxylobium trilobum is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
