Onosma helvetica subsp. austriaca

Onosma helvetica subsp.

austriaca, commonly known as the Austrian helvetia. It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Alps, characterized by its compact growth habit and hairy stems.

The leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough texture, and the flowers are typically pink to purple, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine meadows.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in mountain ecosystems.
