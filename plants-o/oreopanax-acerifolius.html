Oreopanax acerifolius

Oreopanax acerifolius, commonly known as the maple-leafed oreopanax, is a tropical plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which resemble those of maple trees, and its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in panicles, followed by red or orange berries. It is often found in cloud forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal due to its striking foliage.

Oreopanax acerifolius is also noted for its adaptability to a range of soil types, making it a resilient choice for horticultural cultivation.
