Osbeckia nepalensis var. nutans

Osbeckia nepalensis var. nutans, commonly known as the hanging willow herb, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and India.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant features slender, branching stems that often droop, giving it a cascading appearance. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are clustered in spikes and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive botanical appearance.
