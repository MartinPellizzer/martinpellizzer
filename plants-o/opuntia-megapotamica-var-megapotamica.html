Opuntia megapotamica var. megapotamica

Opuntia megapotamica var. megapotamica, commonly known as the giant prickly pear, is a cactus native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its large, flat, paddle-shaped stems that can grow up to 2 meters in length. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tuna, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

Opuntia megapotamica var. megapotamica is adapted to arid environments and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of food.
