Oxalis corniculata var. purpurea

Oxalis corniculata var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to Asia and Australia.

It is a low-growing herb with trifoliate leaves and small, delicate flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces yellowish-orange berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in gardens and disturbed soils.

Oxalis corniculata var. purpurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
