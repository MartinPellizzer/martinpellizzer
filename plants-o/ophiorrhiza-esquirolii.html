Ophiorrhiza esquirolii

Ophiorrhiza esquirolii, commonly known as the coastal or mangrove ophiorrhiza, is a small, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in sandy or coastal soils and is often found in mangrove ecosystems.

This species plays a role in stabilizing coastal areas and providing habitat for various small organisms.
