Oberonia cleistogama

Oberonia cleistogama, commonly known as the cleistogamous orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique reproductive strategy, producing closed, self-pollinating flowers. The plant typically grows on tree branches, utilizing its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are adapted for photosynthesis in low-light conditions.

The species is noted for its small, inconspicuous flowers, which are often hidden within leaf axils.
