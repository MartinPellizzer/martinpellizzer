Ophrys tenthredinifera var. grandiflora

Ophrys tenthredinifera var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large bee orchid, is a striking orchid species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its intricate, bee-like flower structure, which plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant produces showy, yellow-green flowers with a distinctive beard-like appendage that mimics the appearance of a male bee. It thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

This orchid is noted for its specialized relationship with specific pollinators, enhancing its ecological significance.
