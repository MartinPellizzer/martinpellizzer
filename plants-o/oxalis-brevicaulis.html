Oxalis brevicaulis

Oxalis brevicaulis, commonly known as the short-stemmed wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped lobes and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a compact growth habit with short, upright stems and is often found in the understory of forests.

Oxalis brevicaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and blooms.
