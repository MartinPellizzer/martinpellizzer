Orchis israelitica

Orchis israelitica, commonly known as the Israeli orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy flowers that are typically purple or violet in color. The plant produces a single, lance-shaped leaf and blooms from late spring to early summer. It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or rocky habitats.

Orchis israelitica is an important species in the Orchidaceae family, showcasing the diverse botanical features of orchids.
