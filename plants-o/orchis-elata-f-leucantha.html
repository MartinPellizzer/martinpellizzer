Orchis elata f. leucantha

Orchis elata f. leucantha, commonly known as the white-tinged orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, showy flowers that often display a pale or white hue. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and is found in parts of Europe and northern Africa. Its flowers are usually arranged in a single spike and feature a distinctive lip that is often marked with contrasting colors.

This orchid is notable for its specialized pollination mechanisms and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
