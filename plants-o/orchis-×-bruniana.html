Orchis × bruniana

Orchis × bruniana, commonly known as the purple orchid, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Orchis morio and Orchis purpurea.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem that bears a dense spike of violet to purple flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, showcasing its striking, spurred flowers that are typical of the Orchidaceae family. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodlands and meadows across Europe.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest in both horticulture and conservation efforts.
