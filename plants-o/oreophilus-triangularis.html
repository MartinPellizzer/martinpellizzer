Oreophilus triangularis

Oreophilus triangularis, commonly known as the triangular orchid, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive triangular-shaped leaves and striking, fragrant orchid-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in cloud forests or mountainous areas. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, Oreophilus triangularis is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
