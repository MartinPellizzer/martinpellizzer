Onopordum insigne

Onopordum insigne, commonly known as the noble thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, spiny stems and is characterized by its large, purple-blue flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmate, lobed leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry grasslands and rocky areas.

Onopordum insigne is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
