Oncidium macropterum

Oncidium macropterum, commonly known as the yellow butterfly orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that resemble butterflies. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn and have a short blooming period.

Oncidium macropterum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
