Ophiopogon pierrei

Ophiopogon pierrei, commonly known as the Chinese spiderwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale blue, arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Ophiopogon pierrei is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to form dense ground covers.
