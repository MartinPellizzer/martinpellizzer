Owenia vernicosa var. pubescens

Owenia vernicosa var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy owl's eye, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy yellow flowers that resemble owl eyes, typically blooming in late spring. The plant has a trailing growth habit and features hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

The varietal form, pubescens, is distinguished by its increased pubescence compared to the typical species.
