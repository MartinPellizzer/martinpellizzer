Oenothera suffrutescens

Oenothera suffrutescens, commonly known as the evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and showy yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oily seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Oenothera suffrutescens is also known for its medicinal properties, particularly due to the oils extracted from its seeds.
