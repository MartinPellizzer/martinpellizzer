Ocotea notata

Ocotea notata, commonly known as the yellow sandalwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic trees like cinnamon and laurel. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves, which are typically dark green and glossy on the upper surface. Its flowers are small and clustered, and the fruit is a round, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Ocotea notata is valued for its fragrant wood, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
