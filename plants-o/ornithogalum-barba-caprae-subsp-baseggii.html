Ornithogalum barba-caprae subsp. baseggii

Ornithogalum barba-caprae subsp.

baseggii, commonly known as the rock onion or mountain garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in a loose umbel, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
