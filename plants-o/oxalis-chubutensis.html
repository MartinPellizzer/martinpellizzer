Oxalis chubutensis

Oxalis chubutensis, commonly known as the Chubut wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its foliage contains oxalic acid, which can be toxic if ingested in large quantities.

Oxalis chubutensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
