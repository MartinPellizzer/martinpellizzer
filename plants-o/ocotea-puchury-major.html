Ocotea puchury-major

Ocotea puchury-major, commonly known as the Puchury Major, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the tropical rainforests of South America, particularly in regions of Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are typically elliptical and glossy. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by dark blue-black berries.

The plant's aromatic properties make it of interest in both traditional medicine and potential commercial applications.
