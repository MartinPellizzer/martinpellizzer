Ottonia apodostachya var. vidalii

Ottonia apodostachya var. vidalii, commonly known as the Vidal's ottonia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or marshy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
