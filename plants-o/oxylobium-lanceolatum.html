Oxylobium lanceolatum

Oxylobium lanceolatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf oxylobium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. Oxylobium lanceolatum produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to purple, usually blooming in late spring to early summer.
