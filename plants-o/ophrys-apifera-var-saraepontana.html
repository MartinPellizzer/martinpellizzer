Ophrys apifera var. saraepontana

Ophrys apifera var. saraepontana, commonly known as the bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which closely resembles a bee, a mimicry strategy used to attract male bees for pollination. The plant produces a single, showy flower with a dark brown to black lip that mimics the appearance of a female bee. It thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species and is protected in several regions.
