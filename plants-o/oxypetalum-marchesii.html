Oxypetalum marchesii

Oxypetalum marchesii, commonly known as the Malayan rose or mountain rose, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a striking appearance. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and is adapted to cool, humid climates. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Oxypetalum marchesii is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its historical use in traditional medicine.
