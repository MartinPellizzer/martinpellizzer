Orobanche glaucantha

Orobanche glaucantha, commonly known as the blue weed or the white broom.

It is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on its host for nutrients. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers and has a taproot system that attaches to the roots of its host. It is typically found in disturbed soils and is often associated with grasses and other herbaceous plants.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and botanical studies.
