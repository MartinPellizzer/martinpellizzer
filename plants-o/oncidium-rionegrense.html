Oncidium rionegrense

Oncidium rionegrense, commonly known as the Rionegro orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Colombia.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and orange, often with contrasting purple or brown markings. The plant has pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, supporting its growth in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted for attachment to trees and can absorb moisture from the air.

Oncidium rionegrense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
