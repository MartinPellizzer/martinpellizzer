Ononis spinosa f. pseudospinosa

Ononis spinosa f. pseudospinosa, commonly known as the hairy bladderwrack, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, and its leaves are hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is known for its ability to tolerate saline and alkaline conditions, making it a hardy species.

This form is a variant of the species Ononis spinosa, distinguished by its more pronounced hairy foliage.
