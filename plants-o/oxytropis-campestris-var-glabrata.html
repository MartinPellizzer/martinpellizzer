Oxytropis campestris var. glabrata

Oxytropis campestris var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth field lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields and along roadsides.

This species is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability and is used in ecological restoration projects.
