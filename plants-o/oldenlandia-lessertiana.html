Oldenlandia lessertiana

Oldenlandia lessertiana, commonly known as the lesser flame vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and clusters of small, bright yellow or orange flowers. The plant produces tiny, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous minute seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain areas.
