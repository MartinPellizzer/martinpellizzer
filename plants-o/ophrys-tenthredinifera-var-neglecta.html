Ophrys tenthredinifera var. neglecta

Ophrys tenthredinifera var. neglecta, commonly known as the bee orchid, is a species of orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its unique flower structure that closely resembles a bee, which plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, while the flowers are arranged in a dense spike.

This orchid is known for its specialized relationship with specific pollinators, enhancing its ecological significance.
