Oenothera decorticans var. desertorum

Oenothera decorticans var. desertorum, commonly known as the desert evening primrose, is a perennial plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, oval seeds. It is adapted to hot, dry environments and has a deep root system to access water.

Its botanical features make it a resilient and ecologically important species in desert ecosystems.
