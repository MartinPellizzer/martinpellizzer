Ophrys × liberationis

Ophrys × liberationis, commonly known as the mimic orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Balkans.

It is characterized by its intricate, bee-mimicking flowers that play a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant's flowers feature a distinctive lip that resembles a female bee, enticing male bees for pollination. Botanically, it has a compact growth habit with fleshy roots and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its hybrid origin contributes to its genetic diversity and adaptability in different habitats.
