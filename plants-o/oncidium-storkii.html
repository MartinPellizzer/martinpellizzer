Oncidium storkii

Oncidium storkii, commonly known as the Stork's Bill orchid, is a tropical orchid species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, racemose inflorescence that bears numerous fragrant, butterfly-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers display a range of colors, including yellow, orange, and red, with a distinctive white lip.

Oncidium storkii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
