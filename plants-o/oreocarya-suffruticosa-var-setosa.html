Oreocarya suffruticosa var. setosa

Oreocarya suffruticosa var. setosa, commonly known as the hairy oreocarya, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its densely hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are small and usually pink or white, appearing in clusters along the stems.

This variety is well adapted to harsh conditions, making it a resilient member of its ecosystem.
