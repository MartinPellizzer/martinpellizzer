Oenothera deltoides subsp. howellii

Oenothera deltoides subsp.

howellii, commonly known as Howell's evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening and close by morning.

The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and its role in supporting local pollinators.
