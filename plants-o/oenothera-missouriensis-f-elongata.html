Oenothera missouriensis f. elongata

Oenothera missouriensis f. elongata, commonly known as the Missouri evening primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the central United States.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender stems and showy yellow flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as prairies and roadsides. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
