Odontoglossum revolutum

Odontoglossum revolutum, commonly known as the curled-lip orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive, curled lip that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial orchid, thriving in cool, humid environments with well-drained soil. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, often forming a rosette at the base.

Odontoglossum revolutum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
