Ophrys × anamariae

Ophrys × anamariae, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant that results from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is characterized by its intricate floral morphology, which mimics the appearance of female wasps to attract male wasps for pollination. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or grassland habitats across Europe. Its flowers are usually pale brown to yellowish in color, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

This orchid is a prime example of evolutionary adaptation in plant-pollinator interactions.
