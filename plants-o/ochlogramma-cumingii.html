Ochlogramma cumingii

Ochlogramma cumingii, commonly known as the Cuming's dandelion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States and is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces a single, erect stem that bears a composite flower head with yellow ray florets and yellow disk florets. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Ochlogramma cumingii is often found in disturbed soils and is considered an early successional species.
