Ophiuros appendiculatus

Ophiuros appendiculatus, commonly known as the appendiculate sea whip, is a marine coral-like organism belonging to the class Anthozoa.

It is characterized by its branching, flexible structure composed of calcium carbonate, which provides structural support. The polyps of this species are equipped with tentacles that capture plankton for sustenance. It typically inhabits shallow coastal waters, where it forms dense colonies on rocky substrates.

Ophiuros appendiculatus plays a vital role in marine ecosystems by offering shelter and food to various marine organisms.
