Odontochilus calcaratus

Odontochilus calcaratus, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, spurred lip and compact, evergreen foliage. The plant typically grows on rocky substrates and is adapted to humid, shaded environments. It produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often blooming during the rainy season.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
