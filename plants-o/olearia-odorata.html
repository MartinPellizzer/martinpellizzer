Olearia odorata

Olearia odorata, commonly known as the sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features aromatic, silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small, white or pale blue, and are borne in dense clusters, often producing a sweet, honey-like scent.

Olearia odorata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal and fragrance.
