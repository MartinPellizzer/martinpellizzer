Oxalis poeppigii

Oxalis poeppigii, commonly known as the pink oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and clusters of pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments and prefers well-drained soil. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and blooms.

Oxalis poeppigii is also known for its ability to thrive in cooler climates and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
