Orthosiphon laurentii

Orthosiphon laurentii, commonly known as Java tea or Malabar tea, is a member of the Lamiaceae family.

It is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or pink. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
