Oxalis oreocharis

Oxalis oreocharis, commonly known as the silver leaf oxalis, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking silver-green leaves, which are often adorned with intricate patterns of darker green veins. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial shade, making it a unique addition to shaded garden beds.

Due to its limited natural range and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
