Oplismenus flaccidus

Oplismenus flaccidus, commonly known as the slender spikegrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wet, open habitats such as marshes and swamps. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers. Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and culms that are often reddish in color.

Oplismenus flaccidus is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife.
