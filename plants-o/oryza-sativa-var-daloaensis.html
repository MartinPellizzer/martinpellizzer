Oryza sativa var. daloaensis

Oryza sativa var. daloaensis, commonly known as the Daloa rice, is a wild rice species native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with small, clustered spikelets. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, showcasing adaptations to aquatic or semi-aquatic conditions. Its genetic diversity contributes to the study of rice evolution and domestication.

This variety is of interest for its potential in breeding programs aimed at improving rice resilience and yield.
