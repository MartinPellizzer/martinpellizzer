Othonna furcata

Othonna furcata, commonly known as the forked othonna, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and fleshy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Othonna furcata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
