Oenothera elongata

Oenothera elongata, commonly known as the long-stemmed evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and showy yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oval seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as prairies and disturbed areas.

Oenothera elongata is part of the Onagraceae family and is known for its ecological importance to pollinators.
