Oxytropis davidi

Oxytropis davidi, commonly known as David's locoweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Oxytropis davidi is adapted to harsh, arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

It is considered a valuable forage plant for livestock in certain regions.
