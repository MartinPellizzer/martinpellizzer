Oreocarya flavoculata

Oreocarya flavoculata, commonly known as the yellow-eyed lily, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a distinctive orange eye, which are typically arranged in a dense cluster. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Oreocarya flavoculata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
