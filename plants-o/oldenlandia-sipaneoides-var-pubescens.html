Oldenlandia sipaneoides var. pubescens

Oldenlandia sipaneoides var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy oldenlandia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with fine, soft hairs, giving the plant its "hairy" appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its delicate floral display.
