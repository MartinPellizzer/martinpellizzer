Oxalis primuloides

Oxalis primuloides, commonly known as the yellow wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that features heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its calcifuge nature, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. It produces small, edible berries that turn red when mature.

Oxalis primuloides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shady garden conditions.
