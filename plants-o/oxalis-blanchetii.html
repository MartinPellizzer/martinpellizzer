Oxalis blanchetii

Oxalis blanchetii, commonly known as the Sierra oxalis, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, often in mountainous regions. It produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Oxalis blanchetii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
