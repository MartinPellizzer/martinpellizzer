Oxalis muscoides

Oxalis muscoides, commonly known as the mossy wood sorrel, is a perennial flowering plant native to South America.

It features trifoliate leaves that resemble those of true sorrels, with three heart-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Oxalis muscoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
