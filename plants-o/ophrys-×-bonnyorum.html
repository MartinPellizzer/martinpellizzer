Ophrys × bonnyorum

Ophrys × bonnyorum, commonly known as the Bonny Ophrys, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is characterized by its intricate, bee-mimicking flowers that feature a brownish-yellow coloration with intricate patterns resembling a bee's body. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in calcareous soils, often found in open grasslands and woodland edges. Its flowers are highly specialized, relying on mimicry to attract male bees for pollination.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
