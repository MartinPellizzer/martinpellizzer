Orchis fragrans

Orchis fragrans, commonly known as the sweet orchid, is a species of orchid native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, with a distinctive lip petal. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil, while its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette.

Orchis fragrans thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
