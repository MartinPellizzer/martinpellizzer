Orchiodes subregulare

Orchiodes subregulare, commonly known as the small regular orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a delicate, grass-like appearance, featuring narrow leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The flowers are typically white or pale purple, with a distinctive lip that is often marked with darker coloration. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
