Ornithopus martini

Ornithopus martini, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its striking, spiky flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant has silver-gray, lance-shaped leaves that are adapted to withstand salty sea air and strong winds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to coastal gardens.

Ornithopus martini is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
