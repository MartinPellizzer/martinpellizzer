Otoglossum weberbauerianum

Otoglossum weberbauerianum, commonly known as the Weberbauer's orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces pendulous inflorescences with flowers that have a distinctive, spurred lip. Its botanical features include strap-like leaves and a sympodial growth pattern, typical of many orchid species.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires specific care to flourish in cultivation.
