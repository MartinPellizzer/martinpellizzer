Onopordum lyratum

Onopordum lyratum, commonly known as the silky goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-green foliage and clusters of small, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairies and open woodlands. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to native gardens.

The plant is also known for its hardiness and ability to withstand drought conditions.
