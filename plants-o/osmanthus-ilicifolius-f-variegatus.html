Osmanthus ilicifolius f. variegatus

Osmanthus ilicifolius f. variegatus, commonly known as the variegated fragrant woodbine, is a hybrid evergreen shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, featuring white and green patterns on its lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers in late summer, which are followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its unique appearance and pleasant aroma, it is often cultivated for both its aesthetic value and its subtle fragrance.
