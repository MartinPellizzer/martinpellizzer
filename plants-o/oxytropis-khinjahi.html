Oxytropis khinjahi

Oxytropis khinjahi, commonly known as the Khinjahi milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow or pale purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and high-altitude areas.

Oxytropis khinjahi is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
