Orchis viridifusca

Orchis viridifusca, commonly known as the greenish twayblade, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces a single spike of pale green to yellowish flowers, which are often overlooked due to their muted color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Orchis viridifusca is a rare and protected species in many regions, highlighting its ecological significance.
