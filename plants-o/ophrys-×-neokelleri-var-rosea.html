Ophrys × neokelleri var. rosea

Ophrys × neokelleri var. rosea, commonly known as the pink bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a specialized lip that mimics the appearance of a female bee, a feature known as pseudocopulation. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats. Its flowers are usually pink or purple in color, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by male bees.

This orchid is a rare and protected species, valued for its ecological and botanical significance.
