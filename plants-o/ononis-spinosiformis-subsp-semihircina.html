Ononis spinosiformis subsp. semihircina

Ononis spinosiformis subsp.

semihircina, commonly known as the Armenian spiny cinquefoil, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus. It is characterized by its spiny stems and leaves with a rough, hairy texture.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Its subspecies status highlights its distinct morphological and ecological traits compared to other members of the Ononis genus.
