Ophrys fuciflora subsp. cornuta

Ophrys fuciflora subsp.

cornuta, commonly known as the horned orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its unique floral morphology, featuring a distinctive horn-like structure on the lip of the flower.

The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, often in open, sunny habitats. Its flowers are primarily pollinated by specific bee species, showcasing an intricate mutualistic relationship.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
