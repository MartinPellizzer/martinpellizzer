Ornithogalum marginatum

Ornithogalum marginatum, commonly known as the white bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is characterized by its underground bulb, which serves as a storage organ for nutrients. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Ornithogalum marginatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
