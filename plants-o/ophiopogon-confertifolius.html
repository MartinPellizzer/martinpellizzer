Ophiopogon confertifolius

Ophiopogon confertifolius, commonly known as the dense-leaved lily or Japanese forest grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features dense, grass-like foliage with narrow, dark green leaves that form a compact rosette. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer, often arranged in clusters. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes and rock gardens.
