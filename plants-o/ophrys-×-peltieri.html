Ophrys × peltieri

Ophrys × peltieri, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a rare and fascinating plant resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys papei.

It is characterized by its intricate floral structures that mimic the appearance of female bees, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is found in the Mediterranean region, particularly in southern Europe. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brown, with a distinctive lip that resembles a bee's abdomen.

This orchid is of interest to botanists and conservationists due to its limited distribution and unique ecological adaptations.
