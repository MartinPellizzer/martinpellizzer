Ocimum basilicum var. pelvifolium

Ocimum basilicum var. pelvifolium, commonly known as purple basil, is a variety of sweet basil characterized by its distinctive purple foliage and flowers.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in warm, sunny conditions. The leaves are often used in culinary applications, adding a unique flavor to dishes. This plant is also valued for its aromatic properties and is commonly grown in gardens.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for container gardening and herb beds.
