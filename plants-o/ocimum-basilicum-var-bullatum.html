Ocimum basilicum var. bullatum

Ocimum basilicum var. bullatum, commonly known as lemon basil, is a cultivar of sweet basil characterized by its distinct lemony aroma and flavor.

It is a perennial herb native to tropical regions and thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil. The plant features small, oval leaves that are typically a vibrant green with a slightly serrated edge. It produces tiny white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This variety is widely used in cooking, particularly in Mediterranean and Asian cuisines, and is also valued for its aromatic properties in herbal remedies.
