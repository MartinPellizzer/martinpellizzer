Ophrys fuciflora var. holubyana

Ophrys fuciflora var. holubyana, commonly known as the Holubyan bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its unique floral morphology, with a labellum that mimics the appearance of a female bee, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with intricate patterns that enhance the mimicry of insect shapes.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
