Oncidium quadripetalum

Oncidium quadripetalum, commonly known as the four-petaled orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a sympodial growth habit, producing pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant features showy, fragrant flowers with four petals, typically in shades of yellow and orange. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Oncidium quadripetalum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
