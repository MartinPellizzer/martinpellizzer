Opuntia lindheimeri var. chisosensis

Opuntia lindheimeri var. chisosensis, commonly known as the Chisos prickly pear, is a cactus species native to the Chisos Mountains in Texas.

It is characterized by its flattened, paddle-shaped stems that are covered in spines and glochids. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tunas, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

This variety is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in rocky soils.
