Ombrophytum guayanense

Ombrophytum guayanense, commonly known as the Guayana shadeplant, is a tropical plant native to the dense rainforests of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in shaded, humid environments, typically found in the understory of the Amazon and Guiana Shield regions. The plant features broad, waxy leaves that help it conserve moisture in its moist, low-light habitat. Its flowers are small and clustered, often in green or reddish hues, and it produces berries that are an important food source for local wildlife.

Ombrophytum guayanense plays a significant role in the ecological balance of its native ecosystems.
