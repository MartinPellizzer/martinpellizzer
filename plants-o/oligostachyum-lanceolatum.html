Oligostachyum lanceolatum

Oligostachyum lanceolatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically lance-shaped, giving it its specific epithet "lanceolatum.". The plant forms dense clumps of stems that are often used in ornamental landscaping due to their aesthetic appeal. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Oligostachyum lanceolatum is also valued for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a source of sustainable materials.
