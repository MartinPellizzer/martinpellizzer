Oxalis rhomboidea

Oxalis rhomboidea, commonly known as the kidney-shaped wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, each leaflet shaped like a kidney, and its clusters of small, yellow, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun. It is a perennial species that often forms dense mats, and its stems are usually hairy.

The flowers bloom in spring and early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
