Orostachys ramosissima

Orostachys ramosissima, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a low-growing, succulent perennial native to arid regions of North America.

It features dense, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, adapted to store water and reduce transpiration. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is highly drought-tolerant.

Its compact form and ability to withstand extreme temperatures make it well-suited for xeriscaping and desert gardens.
