Outea racemigera

Outea racemigera, commonly known as the Andean bamboo or Andean reed, is a member of the Poaceae family.

It is a tall, perennial grass native to the Andean regions of South America. The plant is characterized by its hollow, jointed stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It often grows in wet or marshy habitats, forming dense stands.

Outea racemigera is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
