Ophrys sphegodes lusus bavarica

Ophrys sphegodes lusus bavarica, commonly known as the Bavarian bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the mountainous regions of southern Germany.

It is characterized by its unique floral morphology, featuring a lip that mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, humid microclimates. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brownish in color, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
