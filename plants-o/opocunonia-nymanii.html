Opocunonia nymanii

Opocunonia nymanii, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated stems and small, intricate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale yellow. The plant prefers humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its roots form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi, aiding in nutrient absorption.

Opocunonia nymanii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
