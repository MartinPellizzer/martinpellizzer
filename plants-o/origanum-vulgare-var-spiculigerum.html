Origanum vulgare var. spiculigerum

Origanum vulgare var. spiculigerum, commonly known as fringed oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems and narrow, gray-green leaves with fringed edges. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in summer, which attract pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its aromatic leaves are used in cooking and traditional medicine for their essential oils and antioxidant properties.
