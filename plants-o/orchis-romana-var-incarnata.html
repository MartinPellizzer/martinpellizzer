Orchis romana var. incarnata

Orchis romana var. incarnata, commonly known as the pink lady's tresses, is a perennial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and its distinctive inflorescence of pink or white flowers that resemble a tresses of hair. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It produces small, fleshy pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in variable conditions.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
