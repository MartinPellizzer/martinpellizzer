Ornithopus × macrorrhynchus

Ornithopus × macrorrhynchus, commonly known as the broad-lobed sea rocket, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and pinnately divided, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is typically found in coastal areas, sandy soils, and disturbed sites, thriving in saline and well-drained conditions.

Ornithopus × macrorrhynchus is an important component of coastal ecosystems, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various insects and birds.
