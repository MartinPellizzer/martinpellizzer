Odontochilus elwesii

Odontochilus elwesii, commonly known as the Elwes' toothed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces a unique, toothed lip that is a distinguishing feature in its floral structure. It thrives in shaded, moist environments, typically in forest undergrowth.

Odontochilus elwesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
