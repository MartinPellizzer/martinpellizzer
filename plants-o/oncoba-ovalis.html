Oncoba ovalis

Oncoba ovalis, commonly known as the oval sycamore, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It features elliptical, glossy, dark green leaves that are typically 5 to 10 centimeters long. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by orange-red berries containing seeds. Oncoba ovalis is often found in coastal and sandy habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in its native region.
