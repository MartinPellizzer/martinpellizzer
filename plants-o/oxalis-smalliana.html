Oxalis smalliana

Oxalis smalliana, commonly known as the smallleaf wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves composed of three heart-shaped leaflets, often with a distinctive reddish or purple tint. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant collections and ecological restoration projects.
