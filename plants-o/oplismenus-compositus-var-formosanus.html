Oplismenus compositus var. formosanus

Oplismenus compositus var. formosanus, commonly known as the Formosan iron vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves, which are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
