Oenanthe crocata var. macrosciadia

Oenanthe crocata var. macrosciadia, commonly known as the giant water dropwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features a robust root system and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and wet meadows.

Its botanical significance lies in its adaptability and role in wetland ecosystems.
