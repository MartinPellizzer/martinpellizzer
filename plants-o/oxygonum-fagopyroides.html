Oxygonum fagopyroides

Oxygonum fagopyroides, commonly known as the golden star, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and grows in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in wetlands, along stream banks, and in swampy areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
