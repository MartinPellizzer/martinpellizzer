Oncidium arangoi

Oncidium arangoi, commonly known as the Arango's orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant, and long-lasting flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant has pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in drier conditions. Its leaves are strap-like and arranged in a fan shape, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Oncidium arangoi is often cultivated for its beauty and is relatively easy to care for in a suitable environment.
