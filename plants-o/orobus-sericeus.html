Orobus sericeus

Orobus sericeus, commonly known as the silky orobrush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and distinctive pod fruits. The plant is known for its soft, silky leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Orobus sericeus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
