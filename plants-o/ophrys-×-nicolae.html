Ophrys × nicolae

Ophrys × nicolae, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is native to the Balkan Peninsula and exhibits a unique morphology with its intricate, bee-like flower structure designed for pollination by male bees. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats. Its flowers display a complex pattern of coloration and mimicry, which plays a crucial role in attracting pollinators.

This hybrid species is of particular interest to botanists due to its genetic diversity and ecological significance.
