Oxalis ferruginata

Oxalis ferruginata, commonly known as the rusty wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically heart-shaped with a reddish-brown tint. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch to its foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun exposure.

Oxalis ferruginata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
