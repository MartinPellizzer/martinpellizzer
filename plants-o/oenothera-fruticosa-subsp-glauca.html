Oenothera fruticosa subsp. glauca

Oenothera fruticosa subsp.

glauca, commonly known as the blue ice evening primrose, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its silver-blue foliage and showy yellow flowers that open in the evening.

The plant thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, powdery coating.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to arid environments.
