Olearia quercifolia

Olearia quercifolia, commonly known as the oak-leaf daisy, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and distinctive, oak-like leaves that are deeply lobed. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Olearia quercifolia thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
