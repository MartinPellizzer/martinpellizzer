Olearia fasciculifolia

Olearia fasciculifolia, commonly known as the New Zealand rock daisy, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features silver-gray foliage with tightly packed, narrow leaves that form compact clumps. Its showy yellow-orange flower heads appear in spring and are surrounded by pale blue bracts.

Olearia fasciculifolia thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and alpine regions.
