Oreodaphne florulenta

Oreodaphne florulenta, commonly known as the fragrant flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, glossy dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn red when mature, containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
