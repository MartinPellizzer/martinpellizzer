Ophrys × diez-santosii

Ophrys × diez-santosii, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a rare and fascinating plant resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is characterized by its intricate, bee-like flower structure, which plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. Its flowers produce a unique scent that mimics the pheromones of female bees, attracting male bees for pollination.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
