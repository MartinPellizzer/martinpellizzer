Oxycoccus japonicus

Oxycoccus japonicus, commonly known as the Japanese cranberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to eastern Asia.

It produces small, red, berry-like fruits that are edible and have a tart flavor. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and remain green throughout the year.

Oxycoccus japonicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
