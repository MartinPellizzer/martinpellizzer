Orthosiphon decipiens

Orthosiphon decipiens, commonly known as the false mint or narrow-leaved orthosiphon, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its small, tubular flowers are typically pink or violet, arranged in whorled clusters.
