Osmanthus longicarpus

Osmanthus longicarpus, commonly known as the long-fruited osmanthus, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, often emitting a sweet, honey-like aroma. The plant produces small, elongated fruits that are typically yellow or orange when mature. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Osmanthus longicarpus is valued in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and aromatic properties.
