Ophiorrhiza latifolia

Ophiorrhiza latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed sandalwood, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically broad and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in sandy or coastal soils and is often found in mangrove or brackish water environments.

Ophiorrhiza latifolia is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
