Ophrys lutea var. sicula

Ophrys lutea var. sicula, commonly known as the Sicilian bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the island of Sicily.

It is characterized by its distinctive floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats. Its flowers are yellow in color, with intricate patterns that aid in its unique pollination strategy.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
