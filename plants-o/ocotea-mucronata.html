Ocotea mucronata

Ocotea mucronata, commonly known as the Mexican laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic trees like cinnamon and bay laurel. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that eventually develop into dark blue berries. Ocotea mucronata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in subtropical gardens.

Its wood is also used in traditional crafts and for fuel due to its density and aromatic properties.
