Oxalis sublanata

Oxalis sublanata, commonly known as the velvet leaf wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of yellow or white, typically in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Oxalis sublanata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
