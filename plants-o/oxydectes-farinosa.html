Oxydectes farinosa

Oxydectes farinosa, commonly known as the farina grass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

Oxydectes farinosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping in regions with suitable climates.
