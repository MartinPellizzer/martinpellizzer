Orthophytum pseudovagans

Orthophytum pseudovagans, commonly known as the Brazilian feather plant, is a species of flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a water-holding cup. The plant produces vibrant orange or red flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Orthophytum pseudovagans is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
