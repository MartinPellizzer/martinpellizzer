Oxytropis lipskyi

Oxytropis lipskyi, commonly known as the Lipsky's locoweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Oxytropis lipskyi is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.
