Orchis dentata

Orchis dentata, commonly known as the toothed orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodlands and meadows. The plant features a tall, unbranched stem that can reach up to 1 meter in height. Its flowers are violet-blue in color, with a distinctive tooth-like appearance on the lip petal.

Orchis dentata is known for its showy blooms and is often found in clusters during the late spring and early summer.
