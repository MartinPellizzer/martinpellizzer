Orthosiphon pascuensis

Orthosiphon pascuensis, commonly known as the mountain mint or velvetleaf, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along streambanks.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens and ecological restoration projects.
