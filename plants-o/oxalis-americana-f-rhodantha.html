Oxalis americana f. rhodantha

Oxalis americana f. rhodantha, commonly known as the red-veined oxalis, is a striking plant characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with deep red veins.

It is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in spring. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant color contrast.

Botanically, it belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is noted for its rhizomatous growth habit.
