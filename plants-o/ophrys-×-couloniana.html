Ophrys × couloniana

Ophrys × couloniana, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is characterized by its intricate, bee-like flower structure designed to mimic the appearance and scent of female bees, thereby facilitating pollination through male bees. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands and meadows across Europe. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brownish in color, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

This hybrid species is prized for its unique floral morphology and ecological role in supporting pollinator interactions.
