Olearia adpressa

Olearia adpressa, commonly known as the silverweed or silverleaf daisy, is a low-growing shrub native to New Zealand.

It features dense, silvery-gray foliage that provides a striking visual contrast against its bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and subalpine regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are small and daisy-like, appearing in clusters during the spring and summer months.

Olearia adpressa is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
