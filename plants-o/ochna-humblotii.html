Ochna humblotii

Ochna humblotii, commonly known as the African velvetwood, is a small to medium-sized tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and distinctive, palmate leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by orange-red berries containing seeds. Ochna humblotii is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

It has cultural and medicinal significance in some local communities.
