Ophrys fuciflora subsp. chestermanii

Ophrys fuciflora subsp.

chestermanii, commonly known as the Chesterman's bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species found in the southeastern region of Australia. It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which closely resembles the appearance of a bee, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as rainforests or under dense vegetation. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellow, with intricate patterns that mimic the body of a bee.

This subspecies is of significant botanical interest due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements.
