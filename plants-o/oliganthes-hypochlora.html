Oliganthes hypochlora

Oliganthes hypochlora, commonly known as the pale-green daisy, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, pale green flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a unique appearance. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are adapted to conserve water in its harsh environment. It is often found in rocky soils and is well-suited to dry climates.

Oliganthes hypochlora plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for certain herbivores.
