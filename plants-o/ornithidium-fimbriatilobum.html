Ornithidium fimbriatilobum

Ornithidium fimbriatilobum, commonly known as the fringed-lobe orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, fimbriate (fringed) lobes on the petals and sepals, which give it its distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories where it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to pale yellow.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Ornithidium fimbriatilobum is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
