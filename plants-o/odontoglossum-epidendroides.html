Odontoglossum epidendroides

Odontoglossum epidendroides, commonly known as the Ecuadorian orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often with a distinctive yellow lip. It thrives in high humidity and partial shade, requiring well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Due to its beauty and rarity, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
