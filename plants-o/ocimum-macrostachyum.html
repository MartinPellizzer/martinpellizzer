Ocimum macrostachyum

Ocimum macrostachyum, commonly known as the large-stemmed basil or wild basil, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. Its flowers are clustered in whorled inflorescences, typically producing purple or white blooms.

Ocimum macrostachyum is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
