Oenothera contorta var. epilobioides

Oenothera contorta var. epilobioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf evening primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and lanceolate leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its flowers are followed by elongated seed pods that split open to release seeds.

This species is often found in dry, open habitats such as prairies and sandy soils.
