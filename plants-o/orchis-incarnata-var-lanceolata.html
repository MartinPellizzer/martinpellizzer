Orchis incarnata var. lanceolata

Orchis incarnata var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lance-leaved fairy wand, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its striking, pale violet to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and wet meadows, and it has lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its flowers are designed for pollination by specific insects, and it plays a role in supporting local biodiversity.

The species is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
