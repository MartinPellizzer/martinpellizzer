Orobanche spartii-juncei

Orobanche spartii-juncei, commonly known as the narrow-leaved broomrape, is a parasitic plant that lacks chlorophyll and cannot perform photosynthesis.

It attaches to the roots of host plants, primarily those in the legume family, to derive nutrients. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and is often found in dry, open habitats. Its life cycle is entirely dependent on its host, and it plays a role in ecological interactions within its environment.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can have significant impacts on the growth and survival of its host plants.
