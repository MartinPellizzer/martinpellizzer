Odontoglossum cristatum var. canaria

Odontoglossum cristatum var. canaria, commonly known as the Canary orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, brightly colored flowers that typically display shades of yellow and orange. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are elongated and covered in a dense layer of fine, white pubescence.

This orchid thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
