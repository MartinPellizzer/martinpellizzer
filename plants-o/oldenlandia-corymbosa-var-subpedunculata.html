Oldenlandia corymbosa var. subpedunculata

Oldenlandia corymbosa var. subpedunculata, commonly known as the creeping spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are typically oval-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to spread rapidly in suitable conditions.
