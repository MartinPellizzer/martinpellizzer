Octomeria decumbens

Octomeria decumbens, commonly known as the creeping octomeria, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features hairy, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, allowing it to retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fibrous roots help it establish in rocky or sandy soils, making it well-suited for xerophytic conditions.

Octomeria decumbens is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
