Oxytropis arctica var. barnebyana

Oxytropis arctica var. barnebyana, commonly known as Arctic locoweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant is adapted to cold climates and grows in rocky or sandy soils. It plays a role in stabilizing soil in its harsh environment and is often found in tundra and alpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
