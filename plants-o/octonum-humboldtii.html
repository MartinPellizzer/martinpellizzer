Octonum humboldtii

Octonum humboldtii, commonly known as the Humboldt's octopus plant, is a unique species native to the coastal regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiraling inflorescence that resembles the tentacles of an octopus, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters along the central stem. It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal dunes and rocky shores.

Due to its rare and specialized habitat, it is considered an endangered species with limited distribution.
