Opuntia recondita var. perrita

Opuntia recondita var. perrita, commonly known as the Perrita prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, spineless pads and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a shallow root system adapted to arid environments, allowing it to thrive in sandy or rocky soils. Its pads can be harvested for food, though they must be prepared carefully to remove the spines.

The species is also notable for its role in local ecosystems, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
