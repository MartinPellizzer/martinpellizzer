Orchis incarnata f. praecox

Orchis incarnata f. praecox, commonly known as the early purple orchid, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking purple to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

It blooms early in the spring, making it one of the first orchids to appear in the wild.
