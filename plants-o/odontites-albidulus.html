Odontites albidulus

Odontites albidulus, commonly known as the white toothed cinquefoil, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, toothed leaves. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Odontites albidulus is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
