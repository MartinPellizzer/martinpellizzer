Omphalocarpum letestui

Omphalocarpum letestui, commonly known as the African burr legume, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces dense, spiny clusters of fruits that serve as a means of seed dispersal through attachment to animals or clothing. Its small, yellow flowers are typically clustered in inflorescences, and it thrives in dry, sandy soils.

The species is noted for its ecological importance in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
