Orchis × predaensis

Orchis × predaensis, commonly known as the Preda's orchid, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Orchis moria and Orchis pallida.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe, particularly in the Balkans. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodland habitats, where it thrives in rich, loamy soils. Its flowers are usually pale purple to pink, with a distinctive hooded lip that is characteristic of many orchid species.

Orchis × predaensis is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
