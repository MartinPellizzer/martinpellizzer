Orchis speciosa

Orchis speciosa, commonly known as the showy orchis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and blooms from late spring to early summer. Its flowers are usually violet or purple in color, with a distinctive lip that is often yellow or white.

Orchis speciosa plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
