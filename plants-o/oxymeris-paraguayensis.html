Oxymeris paraguayensis

Oxymeris paraguayensis, commonly known as the Paraguay rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to South America, particularly Brazil, Paraguay, and Argentina.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its straight, dark-colored heartwood and aromatic scent. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters.

Its seeds are encased in hard, pod-like fruits, and the tree is valued for its high-quality timber used in furniture and musical instruments.
