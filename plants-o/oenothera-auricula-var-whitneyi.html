Oenothera auricula var. whitneyi

Oenothera auricula var. whitneyi, commonly known as Whitney's evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It features showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the evening primrose family, Onagraceae.
