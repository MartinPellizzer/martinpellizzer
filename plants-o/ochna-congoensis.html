Ochna congoensis

Ochna congoensis, commonly known as the Congo ochna, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to central and western Africa.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow or orange flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife. Ochna congoensis thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and potential horticultural use.
