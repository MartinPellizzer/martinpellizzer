Onopordum illyricum f. cardunculus

Onopordum illyricum f. cardunculus, commonly known as the thistle cardoon, is a biennial or short-lived perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, spiny leaves and purple or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is often cultivated for its edible leaves and stalks, which are used in cooking, particularly in Mediterranean cuisine. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and agricultural settings.

Its robust growth and ornamental value make it a notable species in botanical studies and horticulture.
