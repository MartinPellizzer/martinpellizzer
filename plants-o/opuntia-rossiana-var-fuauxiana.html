Opuntia rossiana var. fuauxiana

Opuntia rossiana var. fuauxiana, commonly known as the Fuauxian prickly pear, is a cactus species native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems that are ribbed and covered in spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, followed by edible, red-fleshed fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting well to coastal and rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in traditional cuisine.
