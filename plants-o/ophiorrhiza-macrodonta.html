Ophiorrhiza macrodonta

Ophiorrhiza macrodonta, commonly known as the hairy root tamarind, is a flowering plant species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small shrub characterized by its hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical rainforests.

Ophiorrhiza macrodonta is notable for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity and its potential medicinal properties.
