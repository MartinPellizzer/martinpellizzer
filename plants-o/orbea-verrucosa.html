Orbea verrucosa

Orbea verrucosa, commonly known as the velvet plant or orange velvet, is a member of the Apocynaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces striking orange or red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and birds. Its succulent leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in arid environments.

Orbea verrucosa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ease of care.
