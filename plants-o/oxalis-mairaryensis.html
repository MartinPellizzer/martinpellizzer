Oxalis mairaryensis

Oxalis mairaryensis, commonly known as the Mairary's wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive seed pods that split open to release tiny seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Oxalis mairaryensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
