Oldenlandia subviscosa

Oldenlandia subviscosa, commonly known as the sticky false nettle, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its creeping growth habit and hairy, glandular stems that exude a sticky substance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape, and often have a waxy coating.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in disturbed soils.
