Oxytropis ladyginii

Oxytropis ladyginii, commonly known as Ladygin's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, often at high altitudes. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets.

Oxytropis ladyginii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in arid gardens for its hardy nature and unique appearance.
