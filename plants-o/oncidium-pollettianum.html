Oncidium pollettianum

Oncidium pollettianum, commonly known as the Pollett's orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit and pseudobulbs. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often arranged in racemes. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil.

Oncidium pollettianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
