Orobanche mairei

Orobanche mairei, commonly known as the Maire's broomrape, is a rootless parasitic plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on its host for nutrients.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is found primarily in regions of southern Africa. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It parasitizes a variety of host plants, including some grasses and legumes.

Due to its specialized lifestyle, it plays a unique role in its ecosystem as a hemiparasite.
