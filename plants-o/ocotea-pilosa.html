Ocotea pilosa

Ocotea pilosa, commonly known as the velvetwood or silky ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, silvery hair. Ocotea pilosa produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by dark blue berries, which are an important food source for various birds and mammals.

Its aromatic wood is valued for its durability and is used in furniture and construction.
