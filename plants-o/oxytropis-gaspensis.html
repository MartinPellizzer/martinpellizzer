Oxytropis gaspensis

Oxytropis gaspensis, commonly known as Gaspens wild locoweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, spiny stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as sagebrush steppe and desert grasslands. Its leaves are usually compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces legume pods that contain several seeds.

Oxytropis gaspensis is adapted to survive in nutrient-poor soils and can tolerate drought conditions.
