Oncidium methonica

Oncidium methonica, commonly known as the Methonica orchid, is a tropical orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its long, drooping inflorescence and showy, fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and pink. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, warm environments. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Oncidium methonica is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
