Origanum onites

Origanum onites, commonly known as large-fruited oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, round berries that contain the essential oils responsible for its strong, pungent aroma. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for Mediterranean gardens.

Origanum onites is often used in culinary applications and traditional medicine due to its rich essential oil content.
