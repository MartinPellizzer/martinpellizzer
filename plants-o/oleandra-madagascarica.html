Oleandra madagascarica

Oleandra madagascarica, commonly known as the Madagascar oleander, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant has glossy, lance-shaped leaves and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. It is also noted for its toxicity, as all parts of the plant contain cardiac glycosides that can be harmful to humans and animals.

Despite its toxicity, it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
