Oedera obtusifolia

Oedera obtusifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved oedera, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to parts of Africa and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves with a smooth texture. The plant produces small, clustered yellowish flowers that are often surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Oedera obtusifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
