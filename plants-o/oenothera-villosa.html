Oenothera villosa

Oenothera villosa, commonly known as the downy evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds. Oenothera villosa is often found in sandy or disturbed soils and is adapted to arid environments.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
