Ochna inermis

Ochna inermis, commonly known as the African velvet tamarind, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves with lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that give way to elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds. Ochna inermis thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
