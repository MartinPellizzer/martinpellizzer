Opuntia lasiacantha

Opuntia lasiacantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in spines and clusters of small, vibrant flowers. The plant produces edible fruits, known as tunas, which are typically yellow or red in color. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and the stems store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

Opuntia lasiacantha is also noted for its ability to form dense colonies, making it an important component of desert ecosystems.
