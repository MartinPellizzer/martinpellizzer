Opocunonia

Opocunonia, commonly known as the golden cup plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

Opocunonia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
