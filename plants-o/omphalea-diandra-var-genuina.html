Omphalea diandra var. genuina

Omphalea diandra var. genuina, commonly known as the yellow moonseed, is a species native to the Caribbean islands.

It is a climbing plant characterized by its smooth, yellowish stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by distinctive, fleshy, orange berries. Its botanical features include a woody vine structure and a preference for tropical climates with high humidity.

The plant is often found in shaded, moist environments within forest understories.
