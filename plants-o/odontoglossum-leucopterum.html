Odontoglossum leucopterum

Odontoglossum leucopterum, commonly known as the white-winged orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking white petals and green sepals, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in high-altitude cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a distinctive shape that sets it apart from other orchid species.

Odontoglossum leucopterum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
