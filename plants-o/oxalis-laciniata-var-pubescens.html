Oxalis laciniata var. pubescens

Oxalis laciniata var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves.

The leaves are typically heart-shaped with a hairy texture, giving the plant its common name. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a delicate touch to gardens. Native to the eastern United States, it thrives in moist, woodland environments.

The plant is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and easy maintenance.
