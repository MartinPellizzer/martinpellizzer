Ocotea scrobiculifera

Ocotea scrobiculifera, commonly known as the Brazilian camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark-green, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by oval, woody fruits containing a single seed. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils, which have been used traditionally for their medicinal properties.

The tree is valued for its timber and is also cultivated for its ornamental appeal.
