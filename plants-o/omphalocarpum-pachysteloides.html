Omphalocarpum pachysteloides

Omphalocarpum pachysteloides, commonly known as the broad-leafed burr grass, is a perennial forb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its robust, hairy stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences, followed by distinctive, spiny burrs that aid in seed dispersal. Its fibrous root system allows it to thrive in poor, sandy soils and withstand drought conditions.

Omphalocarpum pachysteloides plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock in its native habitats.
