Onobrychis pentelica var. striatula

Onobrychis pentelica var. striatula, commonly known as the striated needlepod, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are trifoliate, with hairy surfaces, and the pods are elongated and often have distinct striations. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
