Ophrys auriculata

Ophrys auriculata, commonly known as the bee orchid, is a striking Mediterranean orchid characterized by its intricate flower structure.

The plant features a single, showy flower with a distinctive bee-like appearance, complete with a yellow face and a brown, fuzzy beard. It has a slender, leafless stem that typically grows to a height of 20–40 cm. The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, often in clusters of 2–5.

This plant thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climate conditions.
