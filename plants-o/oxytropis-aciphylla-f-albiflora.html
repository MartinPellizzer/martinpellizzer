Oxytropis aciphylla f. albiflora

Oxytropis aciphylla f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered burro bush, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, compound leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of the western United States, particularly in California and Nevada.

This subspecies is adapted to dry environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.
