Oxypetalum martii var. nanum

Oxypetalum martii var. nanum, commonly known as the dwarf oxypetalum, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It features small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive, urn-shaped corolla. The plant has slender, woody stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Due to its ornamental value and unique floral structure, it is occasionally cultivated for horticultural purposes.
