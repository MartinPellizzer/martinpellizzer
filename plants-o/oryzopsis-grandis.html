Oryzopsis grandis

Oryzopsis grandis, commonly known as the giant ricegrass, is a tall, perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its robust growth and can reach heights of up to 6 feet. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species.

Oryzopsis grandis is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
