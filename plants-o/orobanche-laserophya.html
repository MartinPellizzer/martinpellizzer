Orobanche laserophya

Orobanche laserophya, commonly known as the slender broomrape, is a parasitic plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is often found growing on the roots of grasses and other herbaceous plants.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can significantly impact the growth and survival of its host species.
