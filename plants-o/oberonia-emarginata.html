Oberonia emarginata

Oberonia emarginata, commonly known as the marginate orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist forest understories where it thrives in humid environments. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to pale pink.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
