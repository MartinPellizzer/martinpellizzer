Oenothera laciniata var. occidentalis

Oenothera laciniata var. occidentalis, commonly known as the Western ragwort, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and hairy, glandular leaves that are often deeply lobed. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It belongs to the evening primrose family (Onagraceae) and is known for its nocturnal blooming habit.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance as a food source for various pollinators.
