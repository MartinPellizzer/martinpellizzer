Orobus lacteus var. versicolor

Orobus lacteus var. versicolor, commonly known as the variable milkweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces a milky sap, which is a common trait among members of its family. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a soft, velvety texture.

The species is known for its variability in flower color, which contributes to its common name.
