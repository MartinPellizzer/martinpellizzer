Ocotea helicterifolia

Ocotea helicterifolia, commonly known as the "false mahogany," is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by distinctive, spiny capsules containing aromatic seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.

Ocotea helicterifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitats.
