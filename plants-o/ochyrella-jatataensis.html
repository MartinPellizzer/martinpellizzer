Ochyrella jatataensis

Ochyrella jatataensis, commonly known as the Jatata grass, is a perennial herb native to the grasslands of South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically reaches a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Ochyrella jatataensis is adapted to seasonal droughts and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
