Ophrys salassia

Ophrys salassia, commonly known as the Salassia bee orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its unique flower structure, which closely resembles a female bee, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brown in color, with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of insect bodies.

Ophrys salassia is a protected species due to its limited distribution and the threats posed by habitat loss and climate change.
