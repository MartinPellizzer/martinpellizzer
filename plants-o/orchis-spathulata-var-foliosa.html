Orchis spathulata var. foliosa

Orchis spathulata var. foliosa, commonly known as the broad-leaf orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features a basal rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. Its flowers are usually pale purple to pink, with a spurred lip that is a distinguishing feature of the species.

This orchid thrives in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows.
