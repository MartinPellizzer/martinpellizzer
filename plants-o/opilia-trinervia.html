Opilia trinervia

Opilia trinervia, commonly known as the three-veined orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, with a distinctive three-veined structure on the petals.

Opilia trinervia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
