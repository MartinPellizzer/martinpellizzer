Ocotea bernoulliana

Ocotea bernoulliana, commonly known as the Brazilian camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant is known for producing a volatile oil that has been used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are alternate, elliptic, and have a glossy surface.

Ocotea bernoulliana is often found in humid lowland forests and is valued for its ecological and economic significance.
