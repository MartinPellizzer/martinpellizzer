Odontoglossum alexandrae var. trianae

Odontoglossum alexandrae var. trianae, commonly known as the Alexandra's orchid, is a species native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking, showy flowers with a distinctive coloration. The plant typically grows in cool, high-altitude environments, often in shaded forest understories. Its flowers are bilaterally symmetrical, with a spurred lip that serves as a nectar source for pollinators.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
