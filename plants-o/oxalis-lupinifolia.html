Oxalis lupinifolia

Oxalis lupinifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive silvery-white underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Oxalis lupinifolia is often found in woodlands and along stream banks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
