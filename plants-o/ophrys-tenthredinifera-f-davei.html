Ophrys tenthredinifera f. davei

Ophrys tenthredinifera f. davei, commonly known as the Dave's bee orchid, is a rare orchid species native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its unique flower structure, which mimics the appearance of a bee, aiding in pollination by male bees. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and prefers open, sunny habitats. Its flowers are usually yellow with a distinctive dark marking resembling a bee's face.

This orchid is listed as endangered due to habitat loss and limited distribution.
