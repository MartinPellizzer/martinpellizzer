Oenothera Cespitosa Var. Primiveris

Showy evening primrose, scientifically known as Oenothera cespitosa var. primiveris, is a striking plant native to the prairies of North America. It is celebrated for its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths. This species thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to native gardens. Its delicate leaves and tall stalks create a graceful appearance, especially during the summer months. Despite its beauty, it remains a relatively overlooked plant in the world of horticulture.
