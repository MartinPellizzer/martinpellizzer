Oncidium andreae

Oncidium andreae, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, showy flowers that typically feature bright yellow petals and a contrasting lip. The plant has pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in varying environmental conditions. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Oncidium andreae is known for its long-lasting blooms, which can persist for several weeks.
