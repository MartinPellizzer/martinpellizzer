Osyridicarpos linearifolius

Osyridicarpos linearifolius, commonly known as the slender-leafed osyrid, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand prolonged droughts.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists studying xerophytic adaptations.
