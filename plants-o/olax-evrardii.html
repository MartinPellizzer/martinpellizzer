Olax evrardii

Olax evrardii, commonly known as the African olive, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Olacaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often leathery and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant white or yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy berries that contain a single seed. It is adapted to dry and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species that can thrive in poor soils.

Olax evrardii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
