Orleanesia amazonica

Orleanesia amazonica, commonly known as the Amazonian orchid, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often feature vibrant colors. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Orleanesia amazonica plays a significant role in the ecological balance of the rainforest by providing habitat and food for various species.
