Ophrys holosericea subsp. halkionis

Ophrys holosericea subsp.

halkionis, commonly known as the Halkidiki bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the Halkidiki region of Greece. It is characterized by its distinctive bee-like flower morphology, which features a hairy, yellowish-brown labellum that mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in open, sunny habitats. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing a unique reproductive strategy through sexual deception.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts in its native range.
