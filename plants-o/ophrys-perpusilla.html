Ophrys perpusilla

Ophrys perpusilla, commonly known as the lesser bee orchid, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its unique flower structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brownish in color, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Ophrys perpusilla is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its specialized pollination mechanism.
