Origanum pseudo-onites

Origanum pseudo-onites, commonly known as Turkish oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small, white to pinkish flowers and its pungent, medicinal-scented leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. Its essential oils, rich in thymol and carvacrol, contribute to its use in both culinary and traditional medicine.

Origanum pseudo-onites is valued for its hardiness and ability to attract beneficial insects to gardens.
