Orchis coriophora f. nana

Orchis coriophora f. nana, commonly known as the dwarf early purple orchid, is a small, herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the orchid family (Orchidaceae).

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with slender stems and small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodland floors and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is notable for its role in supporting pollinators and its ecological significance in temperate regions.
