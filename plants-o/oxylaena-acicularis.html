Oxylaena acicularis

Oxylaena acicularis, commonly known as the needleleaf oxylaena, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Oxylaena acicularis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
