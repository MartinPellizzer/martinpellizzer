Ophrys loeselii

Ophrys loeselii, commonly known as the Loesel's bee orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which closely resembles a female bee, a mimicry strategy used to attract male bees for pollination. The plant produces a single, showy flower with a complex, bee-like appearance, including a dark brown or black lip that mimics the body of a bee. It typically grows in calcareous soils and is found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in many regions.
