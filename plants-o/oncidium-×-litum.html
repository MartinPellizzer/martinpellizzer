Oncidium × litum

Oncidium × litum, commonly known as the yellow butterfly orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, long-lasting yellow flowers that resemble butterflies, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air. Its inflorescence consists of multiple racemes with showy, fragrant blooms that can last several weeks.

Oncidium × litum prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining potting media, making it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
