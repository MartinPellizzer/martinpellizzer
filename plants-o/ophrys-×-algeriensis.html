Ophrys × algeriensis

Ophrys × algeriensis, commonly known as the Algerian bee orchid, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys fucata.

It is characterized by its intricate, bee-like flower structure, which features a prominent lip resembling a bee's face. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Algeria, and thrives in calcareous soils. Its flowers are typically pale yellow to orange in color, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by mimicking the appearance of female bees.

Ophrys × algeriensis is a perennial herb that grows to a height of about 30–50 cm, with lance-shaped leaves and a short flowering period in spring.
