Oxychloris

Oxychloris, commonly known as the yellow star-of-Bethlehem, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun. It is native to parts of Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
