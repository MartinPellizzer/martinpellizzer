Ophrys × maremmae nothosubsp. bankosii

Ophrys × maremmae nothosubsp.

bankosii, commonly known as the Maremma orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the genus Ophrys, which is known for its mimicry of female insects to attract male pollinators.

The plant typically produces striking, bee-mimicking flowers that are rich in color and intricate patterns. It thrives in calcareous soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
