Oxalis lightfootii

Oxalis lightfootii, commonly known as the Lightfoot's wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive white veins and produces small, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow or orange. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers. Its ornamental value comes from its delicate foliage and subtle blossoms.

Oxalis lightfootii is also noted for its ability to tolerate dry conditions once established.
