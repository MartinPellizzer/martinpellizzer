Oligosporus brachyphyllus

Oligosporus brachyphyllus, commonly known as the short-leaf spore plant, is a unique species characterized by its compact growth habit and specialized reproductive structures.

It belongs to the family Oligosporaceae and is distinguished by its small, clustered spores that are released in response to environmental stimuli. The plant exhibits a distinct morphology with short, broad leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy cuticle. Its habitat is typically found in moist, shaded environments, where it thrives in nutrient-rich soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
