Oxydectes caperoniifolia

Oxydectes caperoniifolia, commonly known as the Cape rose or Cape honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy, and it produces small, clustered fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its seasonal blooming and adaptability to various climates.
