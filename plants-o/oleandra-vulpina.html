Oleandra vulpina

Oleandra vulpina, commonly known as the woolly oleander, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is known for its toxicity due to the presence of cardiac glycosides in its sap and all parts. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Oleandra vulpina is drought-tolerant and can grow in a variety of soil types, making it a hardy addition to gardens.
