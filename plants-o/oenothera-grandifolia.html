Oenothera grandifolia

Oenothera grandifolia, commonly known as showy evening primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are typically 2 to 4 inches in diameter, with five petals that open at dusk.

Oenothera grandifolia is often found in dry, open habitats like woodlands and sandy soils.
