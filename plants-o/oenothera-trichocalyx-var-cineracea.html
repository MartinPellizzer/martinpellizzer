Oenothera trichocalyx var. cineracea

Oenothera trichocalyx var. cineracea, commonly known as the cineraria evening primrose, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with gray or silver. The plant produces striking yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to xeriscapes.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
