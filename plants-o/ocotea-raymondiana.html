Ocotea raymondiana

Ocotea raymondiana, commonly known as the Raymond's ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant typically grows in humid, lowland forests and can reach heights of up to 30 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a glossy surface, while its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences.

The fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed, and the tree is valued for its timber and medicinal properties.
