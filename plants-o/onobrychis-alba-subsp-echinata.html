Onobrychis alba subsp. echinata

Onobrychis alba subsp.

echinata, commonly known as the white vetch, is a perennial legume native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in spikes.

The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

This species is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in ecological restoration and as a forage plant.
