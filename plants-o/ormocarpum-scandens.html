Ormocarpum scandens

Ormocarpum scandens, commonly known as the climbing bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its twining stems and clusters of showy, red or orange-red flowers that resemble bottlebrushes. The plant produces small, round fruits called "ormocarps," which are typically covered in a sticky resin. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Ormocarpum scandens thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
