Ocotea eucuneata

Ocotea eucuneata, commonly known as the Guianese ocotea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within lowland and montane forests. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a distinct aromatic scent when crushed.

Ocotea eucuneata is valued for its wood, which is used in local crafts and construction, and for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity.
