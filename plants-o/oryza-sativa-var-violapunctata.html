Oryza sativa var. violapunctata

Oryza sativa var. violapunctata, commonly known as the violet-punctured rice, is a rare and ornamental variant of rice characterized by its striking violet-colored markings on the grains.

This variety is primarily cultivated for its aesthetic appeal rather than for grain production, making it a popular choice in botanical gardens and specialty horticulture. The plant exhibits typical rice growth habits, with tall, slender stalks and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered, typical of the grass family, and it produces panicles that are often more showy than those of common rice varieties.

Due to its unique appearance, it is of interest to botanists and plant enthusiasts alike.
