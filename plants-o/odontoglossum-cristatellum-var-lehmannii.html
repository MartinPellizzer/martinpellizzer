Odontoglossum cristatellum var. lehmannii

Odontoglossum cristatellum var. lehmannii, commonly known as the Lehmann's toothed orchid, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, strap-like leaves and clusters of fragrant, violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in cool, shaded environments, often in cloud forests. Its flowers are notable for their intricate structure and vibrant coloration, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This orchid is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses due to its specific environmental requirements.
