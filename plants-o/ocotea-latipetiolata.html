Ocotea latipetiolata

Ocotea latipetiolata, commonly known as the broad-petioled ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves with broad petioles and a fragrant aroma. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue berries.

Ocotea latipetiolata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
