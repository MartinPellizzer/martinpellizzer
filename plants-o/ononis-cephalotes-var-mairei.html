Ononis cephalotes var. mairei

Ononis cephalotes var. mairei, commonly known as the Maire's kidneyvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats. It has a creeping growth habit with hairy stems and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This variety is noted for its ecological importance in stabilizing soils and supporting pollinators.
