Onobrychis afghanica var. codringtonii

Onobrychis afghanica var. codringtonii, commonly known as Afghan clover, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

This species is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment and ecological restoration projects.
