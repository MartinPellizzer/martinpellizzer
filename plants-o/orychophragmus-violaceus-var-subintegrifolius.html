Orychophragmus violaceus var. subintegrifolius

Orychophragmus violaceus var. subintegrifolius, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, violet-purple flower heads that are surrounded by pale yellow ray florets. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

This variety is known for its robust growth and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
