Oplismenus tsushimensis

Oplismenus tsushimensis, commonly known as the Japanese nettle or Shikoku nettle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Urticaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are borne on short pedicels. Native to Japan, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Oplismenus tsushimensis is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued as an ornamental plant.
