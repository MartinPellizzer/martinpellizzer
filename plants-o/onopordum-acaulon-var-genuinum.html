Onopordum acaulon var. genuinum

Onopordum acaulon var. genuinum, commonly known as the narrowleaf thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and narrow, gray-green leaves with spiny margins. The plant produces pale yellow to greenish-yellow composite flowers, typically arranged in spherical clusters. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and slopes.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
