Orchis olbiensis subsp. ichnusae

Orchis olbiensis subsp.

ichnusae, commonly known as the Ichnusae orchid, is a rare and endemic orchid species found primarily in the Balkans. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant has a specialized mycorrhizal relationship with fungi, which is essential for its nutrient uptake. Its habitat is often restricted to moist, calcareous soils in forested or semi-shaded areas.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
