Ouratea pseudogigantophylla

Ouratea pseudogigantophylla, commonly known as the giant-leaf ouratea, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elongated leaves that can reach lengths of up to 1 meter. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by distinctive orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understorey of dense tropical forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and horticultural interest.
