Orianthera serpyllifolia subsp. angustifolia

Orianthera serpyllifolia subsp.

angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf mouseear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States. It features small, hairy leaves that are typically narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern.

The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

This subspecies is adapted to arid environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
