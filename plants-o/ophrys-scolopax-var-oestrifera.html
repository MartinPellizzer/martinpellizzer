Ophrys scolopax var. oestrifera

Ophrys scolopax var. oestrifera, commonly known as the Hare Orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hare-like flower shape, which features intricate markings resembling a hare's face. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its blooming period occurs in late spring to early summer, producing fragrant, violet-colored flowers.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a protected species in many regions.
