Ocotea acutifolia

Ocotea acutifolia, commonly known as the sharp-leaved ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped, serrated leaves and fragrant, small white flowers. Its fruits are drupes, typically containing a single seed, and it is often found in humid lowland forests.

Ocotea acutifolia is valued for its aromatic wood and is also used in traditional medicine.
