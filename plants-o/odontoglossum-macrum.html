Odontoglossum macrum

Odontoglossum macrum, commonly known as the Macrum orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb that stores water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in its mountainous habitat. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive lip that attracts pollinators. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette, contributing to its ornamental value.

Odontoglossum macrum is appreciated by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and unique floral structure.
