Oplismenus festucaceus

Oplismenus festucaceus, commonly known as flatleaf muhly, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, flat leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer, adding a delicate floral display. It thrives in wet to mesic environments, often found in marshes, swales, and along stream banks.

Oplismenus festucaceus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland habitats.
