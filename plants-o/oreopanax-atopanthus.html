Oreopanax atopanthus

Oreopanax atopanthus, commonly known as the cloud forest giant arum, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to two meters in length, providing a dramatic visual impact in its natural habitat. The plant produces an impressive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a showy purple spathe, which attracts pollinators such as beetles and flies. It thrives in humid, shaded environments with high elevation and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
