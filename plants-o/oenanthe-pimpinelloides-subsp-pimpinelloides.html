Oenanthe pimpinelloides subsp. pimpinelloides

Oenanthe pimpinelloides subsp.

pimpinelloides, commonly known as the common water dropwort, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist environments such as wet meadows, stream banks, and marshes.

The plant has slender, often hairy stems that are usually unbranched. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a distinct, slightly hairy texture.

The small, greenish-white flowers are arranged in umbels and bloom in early summer.
