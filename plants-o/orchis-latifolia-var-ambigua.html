Orchis latifolia var. ambigua

Orchis latifolia var. ambigua, commonly known as the broad-leaved orchid variant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and thrives in woodland or meadow habitats. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and it produces a single spike of flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variant is noted for its ecological importance as a food source for certain pollinators and its role in maintaining biodiversity in its native ecosystems.
