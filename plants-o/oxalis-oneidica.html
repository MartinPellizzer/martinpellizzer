Oxalis oneidica

Oxalis oneidica, commonly known as the Oneida wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and can spread through rhizomes, forming dense mats.

Oxalis oneidica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
