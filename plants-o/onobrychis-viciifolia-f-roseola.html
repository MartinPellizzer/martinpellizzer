Onobrychis viciifolia f. roseola

Onobrychis viciifolia f. roseola, commonly known as the rose-flowered field vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with a distinctive rose-colored hue. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to purple, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
