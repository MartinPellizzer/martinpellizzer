Ophiopogon chingii

Ophiopogon chingii, commonly known as the Chinese grass-leaf plant, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its clumping growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive foliage.

Ophiopogon chingii is also valued for its traditional medicinal uses in Chinese herbal medicine.
