Oenothera rubescens

Oenothera rubescens, commonly known as the showy evening primrose, is a species of flowering plant in the Onagraceae family.

It is native to the western United States and is characterized by its striking pink to reddish-orange flowers that open in the evening. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Oenothera rubescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and also has historical uses in traditional medicine.
