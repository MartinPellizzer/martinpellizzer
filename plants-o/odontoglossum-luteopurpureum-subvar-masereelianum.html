Odontoglossum luteopurpureum subvar. masereelianum

Odontoglossum luteopurpureum subvar. masereelianum, commonly known as the Masereelian Purple-Orange Orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its striking violet and orange flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. It typically grows in high-altitude cloud forests, thriving in cool, moist conditions.

This subvariety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
