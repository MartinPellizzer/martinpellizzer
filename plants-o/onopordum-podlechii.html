Onopordum podlechii

Onopordum podlechii, commonly known as the Balkan thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf structures. The plant features spiny leaves with a rough texture and produces purple to pink composite flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Onopordum podlechii is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
