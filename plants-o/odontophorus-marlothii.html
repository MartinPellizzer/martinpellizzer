Odontophorus marlothii

Odontophorus marlothii, commonly known as the Marloth's orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, intricate floral structure. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually small and colorful, displaying a range of hues from yellow to orange.

Odontophorus marlothii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
