Oberonia mcgregorii

Oberonia mcgregorii, commonly known as the McGregor's orchid, is a rare and delicate epiphytic orchid native to the rainforests of Queensland, Australia.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is characterized by its small, pale green leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant is known for its unique growth habit, often growing on tree trunks or branches. Its flowers bloom in spring and are highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts due to their rarity and beauty.

Conservation efforts are crucial for its survival, as it faces threats from habitat loss and climate change.
