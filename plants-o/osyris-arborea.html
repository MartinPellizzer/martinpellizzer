Osyris arborea

Osyris arborea, commonly known as the African tulip tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, grey bark and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces striking, showy inflorescences of red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. Osyris arborea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its vibrant floral display.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for urban landscaping.
