Oligogyne burchellii

Oligogyne burchellii, commonly known as the Burchell's ironwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Cape Province of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round, black berries that are edible for birds and contribute to its role in local ecosystems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Oligogyne burchellii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
