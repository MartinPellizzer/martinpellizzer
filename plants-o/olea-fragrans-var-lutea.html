Olea fragrans var. lutea

Olea fragrans var. lutea, commonly known as the golden olive, is a variant of the olive tree characterized by its distinctive yellow foliage.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, followed by olive-like fruits. Its leaves are broad and glossy, providing ornamental value in gardens.

This variety is valued for both its aesthetic appeal and its potential use in essential oil production.
