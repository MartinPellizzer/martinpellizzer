Olax aphylla

Olax aphylla, commonly known as the leafless olax, is a unique shrub native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its absence of leaves, with photosynthesis occurring through its green stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its structure is adapted to arid environments, allowing it to conserve water efficiently.

Olax aphylla is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is a member of the Rubiaceae family.
