Oxalis bustillosii var. biflora

Oxalis bustillosii var. biflora, commonly known as the twin-flowered wood sorrel, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with distinctive heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-drained, fertile soils. Its flowers bloom in spring, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Oxalis bustillosii var. biflora is also valued for its ability to thrive in cooler climates.
