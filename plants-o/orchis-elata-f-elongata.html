Orchis elata f. elongata

Orchis elata f. elongata, commonly known as the elongated marsh orchid, is a rare and distinctive orchid species native to wetland habitats in Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring. The plant features elongated leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a unique structure that includes a spurred lip.

This orchid is adapted to moist, nutrient-poor soils and plays an important role in its ecological community.
