Ophrys episcopalis var. saliarisii

Ophrys episcopalis var. saliarisii, commonly known as the Salisara Orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a complex labellum that mimics the appearance of a female insect, which plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone habitats, thriving in nutrient-poor soils. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to orange in color, with intricate patterns that aid in attracting specific pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species, requiring conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
