Oxalis buchtienii

Oxalis buchtienii, commonly known as the mountain oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often found in mountainous regions and is adapted to dry, well-drained environments.

Oxalis buchtienii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
