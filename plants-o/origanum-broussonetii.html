Origanum broussonetii

Origanum broussonetii, commonly known as Turkish oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are widely used in cooking for their pungent, aromatic flavor.

Origanum broussonetii is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.
