Oxalis hookeriana

Oxalis hookeriana, commonly known as the Hooker's wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Oxalidaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive seed pods that split open to release seeds, aiding in its dispersal. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Oxalis hookeriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its attractive foliage and blooms.
