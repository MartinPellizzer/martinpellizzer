Oxalis suborbiculata f. glabra

Oxalis suborbiculata f. glabra, commonly known as the smooth oxalis, is a variant of the species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its foliage resembles that of a shamrock, with heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated.

This ornamental plant is valued for its attractive appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
