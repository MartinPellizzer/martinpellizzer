Ophrys × sundermanniana

Ophrys × sundermanniana, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant in the Orchidaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Ophrys apifera (the bee orchid) and Ophrys insectifera (the wasp orchid). The plant is characterized by its unique floral morphology, with flowers that mimic the appearance of insects, particularly bees and wasps. Its inflorescence typically consists of a dense spike of resupinate flowers, each with a specialized labellum that plays a role in pollination.

This species is often found in calcareous grasslands and heathlands, thriving in sunny, well-drained soils.
