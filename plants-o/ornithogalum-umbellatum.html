Ornithogalum umbellatum

Ornithogalum umbellatum, commonly known as the star of Bethlehem, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features a bulbous root system and produces a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant blooms with a cluster of white, star-shaped flowers that resemble small lanterns, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

The flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and moths, contributing to its ecological significance.
