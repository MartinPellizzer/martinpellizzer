Oryzopsis vicaria

Oryzopsis vicaria, commonly known as the slender false rice, is a grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of the grass family. It thrives in dry, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and rocky areas.

Oryzopsis vicaria is often mistaken for rice due to its similar appearance, but it is not cultivated for agricultural purposes.
