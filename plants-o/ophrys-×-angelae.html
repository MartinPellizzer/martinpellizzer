Ophrys × angelae

Ophrys × angelae, commonly known as the angel's orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Balkans.

It belongs to the genus Ophrys, which is characterized by its intricate mimicry of female insects to attract male pollinators. The plant produces showy, bee-like flowers that are highly specialized for pollination through sexual deception. Its botanical features include a pseudobulb structure and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological studies on plant-pollinator interactions.
