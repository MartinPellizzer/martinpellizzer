Ocotea smithiana

Ocotea smithiana, commonly known as Smith's ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within lowland rainforests. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a glossy surface with a distinct scent when crushed.

The tree produces small, fragrant flowers followed by elliptical, dark blue-black berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species.
