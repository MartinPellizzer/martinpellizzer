Oenothera trichocalyx var. piperi

Oenothera trichocalyx var. piperi, commonly known as the eastern evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats like meadows and wetlands, and it has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its scientific name reflects its classification within the genus Oenothera, which includes several species of evening primroses.

The variety piperi is distinguished by its specific floral and morphological traits compared to other varieties within the species.
