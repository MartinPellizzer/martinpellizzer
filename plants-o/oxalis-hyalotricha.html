Oxalis hyalotricha

Oxalis hyalotricha, commonly known as the ghost leaf oxalis, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves that resemble ghostly shapes, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Oxalis hyalotricha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
