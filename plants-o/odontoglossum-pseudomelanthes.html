Odontoglossum pseudomelanthes

Odontoglossum pseudomelanthes, commonly known as the purple-veined orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru. This plant typically grows at high altitudes, thriving in cool, humid environments. Its flowers are characterized by their vibrant purple and white coloration with intricate veining.

The plant produces pseudobulbs and has linear, grass-like leaves that help it store water in its native habitat.
