Oenothera missouriensis var. incana

Oenothera missouriensis var. incana, commonly known as Missouri evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that open in the evening and close by morning. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies, roadsides, and disturbed soils. It has hairy stems and leaves, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

The species is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for nocturnal pollinators like moths.
