Ossaea hotteana

Ossaea hotteana, commonly known as the hottean lily, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental, showy flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with six petals and a distinct central stamen.

Ossaea hotteana prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine settings.
