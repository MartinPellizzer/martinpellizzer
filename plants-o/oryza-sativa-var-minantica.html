Oryza sativa var. minantica

Oryza sativa var. minantica, commonly known as the dwarf rice variety, is a cultivated rice species characterized by its compact growth habit and reduced plant height compared to standard rice varieties.

It possesses a grass-like appearance with narrow, elongated leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. This variety is primarily valued for its high grain yield and adaptability to various climatic conditions. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a short stature that makes it suitable for intensive agricultural practices.

The grains are typically small and have a high starch content, contributing to its culinary and nutritional significance.
