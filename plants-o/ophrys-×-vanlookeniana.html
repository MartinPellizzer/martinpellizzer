Ophrys × vanlookeniana

Ophrys × vanlookeniana, commonly known as the Van Loock's orchid, is a hybrid species belonging to the genus Ophrys, which includes many species of orchids known for their mimicry.

This plant is characterized by its intricate, often bee-like flower structure, which plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. It typically grows in calcareous soils and is found in regions with a Mediterranean climate. The flowers are usually green or brown, with intricate patterns that resemble female insects to attract male pollinators.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in several European countries.
