Origanum hypericifolium

Origanum hypericifolium, commonly known as sweet basil or wild basil, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with a pale underside. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its essential oils contain compounds such as linalool and carvacrol, which contribute to its distinctive fragrance and medicinal properties.

Origanum hypericifolium thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a versatile addition to gardens and herbal collections.
