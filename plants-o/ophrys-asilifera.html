Ophrys asilifera

Ophrys asilifera, commonly known as the bee orchid, is a fascinating orchid species native to parts of Europe and North Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bee-like appearance, with a pouch-shaped lip that mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its flowers are usually pale green to brown in color, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Ophrys asilifera is a perennial herb that produces a single flower per stem, making it a visually distinctive and ecologically significant species.
