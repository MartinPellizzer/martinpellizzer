Octomeria prostrata

Octomeria prostrata, commonly known as the prostrate octomeria, is a low-growing perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its sprawling habit and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely commercially available.
