Orchis acuminata

Orchis acuminata, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodlands or meadows. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and it produces small, dark brown seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Orchis acuminata is also known for its ability to form mycorrhizal associations with fungi, which aid in nutrient uptake.
