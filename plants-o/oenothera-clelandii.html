Oenothera clelandii

Oenothera clelandii, commonly known as Cleland's evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has hairy, ribbed stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Oenothera clelandii is also noted for its ability to reseed itself, making it a resilient and hardy species.
