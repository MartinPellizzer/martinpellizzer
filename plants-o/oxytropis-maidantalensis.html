Oxytropis maidantalensis

Oxytropis maidantalensis, commonly known as the Mongolian locoweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It is known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock.
