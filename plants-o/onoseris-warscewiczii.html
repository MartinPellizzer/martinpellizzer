Onoseris warscewiczii

Onoseris warscewiczii, commonly known as the Warshaw's goldendisk, is a herbaceous perennial native to China.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy inflorescences. The plant features basal leaves that are lanceolate and hairy, with a height typically reaching up to 60 cm. Its yellow ray florets are surrounded by a dense cluster of tubular disc florets, making it visually striking.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
