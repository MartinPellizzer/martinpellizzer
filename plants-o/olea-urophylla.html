Olea urophylla

Olea urophylla, commonly known as the African olive, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family, which includes other olive species. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns from green to yellow or red as it matures.

Olea urophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
