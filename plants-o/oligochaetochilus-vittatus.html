Oligochaetochilus vittatus

Oligochaetochilus vittatus, commonly known as the spotted mossy orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features a slender rhizome with a dense covering of fine roots, and its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and often have a faintly veined appearance.

The species is notable for its unique morphology and is of interest to botanists and orchid enthusiasts.
