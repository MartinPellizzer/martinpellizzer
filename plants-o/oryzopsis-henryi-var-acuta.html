Oryzopsis henryi var. acuta

Oryzopsis henryi var. acuta, commonly known as the acuteleaf false rice, is a perennial grass species native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves with a pointed tip. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that bear both male and female flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland conservation.
