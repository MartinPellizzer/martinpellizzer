Oxytropis gracilis

Oxytropis gracilis, commonly known as slender locoweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has small, compound leaves and produces pods that contain seeds dispersed by wind.
