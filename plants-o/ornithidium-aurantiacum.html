Ornithidium aurantiacum

Ornithidium aurantiacum, commonly known as the orange orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant orange-colored flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

The flowers are often clustered in inflorescences and bloom during the rainy season.
