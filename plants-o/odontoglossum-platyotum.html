Odontoglossum platyotum

Odontoglossum platyotum, commonly known as the broad-leaf orchid, is a species native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces strikingly large, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in cool, humid environments at high altitudes. Its flowers are usually violet or purple in color and have a unique, spurred shape.

Odontoglossum platyotum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its beauty.
