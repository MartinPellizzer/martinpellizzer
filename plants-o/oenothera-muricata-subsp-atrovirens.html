Oenothera muricata subsp. atrovirens

Oenothera muricata subsp.

atrovirens, commonly known as the blackleaf evening primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States. It features showy, yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

The plant has hairy, linear leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
