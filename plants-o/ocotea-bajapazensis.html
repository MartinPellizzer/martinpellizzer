Ocotea bajapazensis

Ocotea bajapazensis, commonly known as the Bajapaz Ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. It typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential medicinal research.
