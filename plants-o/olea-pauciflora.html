Olea pauciflora

Olea pauciflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered olive, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, which are often hidden among the foliage. Olea pauciflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.
