Orchis holosericea

Orchis holosericea, commonly known as the woolly twayblade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in woodland or grassland habitats, thriving in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually arranged in a spike and are pollinated by insects, particularly bees.

Orchis holosericea is known for its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native ecosystems.
