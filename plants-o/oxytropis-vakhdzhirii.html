Oxytropis vakhdzhirii

Oxytropis vakhdzhirii, commonly known as the Kazakh milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to Central Asia, particularly found in Kazakhstan and parts of Russia. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, with hairy stems and compound leaves that are typically trifoliate. It produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by narrow, pod-like fruits.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.
