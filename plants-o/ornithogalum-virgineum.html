Ornithogalum virgineum

Ornithogalum virgineum, commonly known as the star-of-Bethleham, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking white or pale green flowers arranged in a star-like cluster, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ornithogalum virgineum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
