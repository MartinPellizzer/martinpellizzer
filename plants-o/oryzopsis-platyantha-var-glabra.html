Oryzopsis platyantha var. glabra

Oryzopsis platyantha var. glabra, commonly known as smooth panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered with a smooth, hairless surface. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically green to reddish in color, with inconspicuous flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas, prairies, and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native grassland ecosystems.
