Oxytropis campestris var. roaldii

Oxytropis campestris var. roaldii, commonly known as the Roald's yellow lupine, is a flowering plant native to the steppes of Central Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and its use in traditional medicine.
