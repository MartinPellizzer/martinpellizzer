Oberonia myosurus

Oberonia myosurus, commonly known as the mouse orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that resemble a mouse, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it produces a single spike of flowers.

Oberonia myosurus is valued for its unique appearance and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
