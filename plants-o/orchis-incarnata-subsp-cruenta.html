Orchis incarnata subsp. cruenta

Orchis incarnata subsp.

cruenta, commonly known as the scarlet twayblade, is a flowering plant native to North America. It is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with a single, showy purple or red flower.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and wet meadows. It has a short, erect stem with a single leaf and produces a distinctive, spurred orchid flower.

The species is pollinated by bees and is considered an indicator of healthy, undisturbed ecosystems.
