Orchis crenulata

Orchis crenulata, commonly known as the wavy orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, wavy-edged leaves and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces a single spike of flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Orchis crenulata is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
