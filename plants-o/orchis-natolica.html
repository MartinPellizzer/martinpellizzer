Orchis natolica

Orchis natolica, commonly known as the purple marsh orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas of Europe.

It typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces a tall, erect stem bearing a dense cluster of violet to purple flowers, which are typically 2–3 cm in length. Its flowers have three sepals and three petals, with the lower petal forming a distinctive lip.

Orchis natolica is often found in nutrient-rich soils and is associated with wet or semi-aquatic habitats.
