Ocellochloa andreana

It is native to the high-altitude regions of South America, particularly in the Andes Mountains. The plant is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often clustered in dense, compact formations.

Ocellochloa andreana is adapted to cold, arid environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
