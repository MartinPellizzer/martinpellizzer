Ovieda bracteosa

Ovieda bracteosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along forest edges.

Ovieda bracteosa is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain areas.
