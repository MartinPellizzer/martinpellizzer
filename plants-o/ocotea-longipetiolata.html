Ocotea longipetiolata

Ocotea longipetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that are elongated and have long petioles, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible for some bird species.

Ocotea longipetiolata is often found in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests.
