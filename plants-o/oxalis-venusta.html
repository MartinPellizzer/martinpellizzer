Oxalis venusta

Oxalis venusta, commonly known as the purple shamrock, is a striking plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive purple hue, which become more vibrant in cooler temperatures. The plant produces clusters of small, delicate flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to form dense, compact growth with minimal maintenance.
