Oxalis nipponica

Oxalis nipponica, commonly known as Japanese wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It features heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins, resembling those of the common wood sorrel. The plant produces small, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Oxalis nipponica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
