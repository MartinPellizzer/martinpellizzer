Oleandra tricholepis

Oleandra tricholepis, commonly known as the woolly oleander, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and showy clusters of pink or red flowers. The plant belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is known for its adaptability to arid conditions. Oleandra tricholepis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and resistance to drought.
