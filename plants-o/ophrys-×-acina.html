Ophrys × acina

Ophrys × acina, commonly known as the bee orchid hybrid, is a fascinating plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Ophrys apifera, the early bee orchid, and Ophrys insectifera, the wasp orchid. The plant is characterized by its intricate, bee-like flowers that have evolved to mimic female bees, a strategy to attract male bees for pollination. Its pseudobulbs are short and thick, supporting its evergreen leaves, and it typically grows in moist, shaded habitats across Europe.

This hybrid showcases the remarkable adaptability and evolutionary strategies found in orchids.
