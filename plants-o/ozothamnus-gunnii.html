Ozothamnus gunnii

Ozothamnus gunnii, commonly known as the New Zealand dandelion, is a perennial herb native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and linear, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal and lowland areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It has a creeping growth habit and produces achenes that are dispersed by wind.

Ozothamnus gunnii is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
