Orchis incarnata lusus hybrida

Orchis incarnata lusus hybrida, commonly known as the showy orchis hybrid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers.

This hybrid plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows, where it thrives in well-drained soil. It features tall, erect stems that bear clusters of showy, spurred orchid flowers, which are often pollinated by bees and other insects. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces a single, elongated seed pod containing numerous small, dark seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinator populations.
