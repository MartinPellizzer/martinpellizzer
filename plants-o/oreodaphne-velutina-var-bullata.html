Oreodaphne velutina var. bullata

Oreodaphne velutina var. bullata, commonly known as the velvet tallow tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark brown bark and elliptical, glossy leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
