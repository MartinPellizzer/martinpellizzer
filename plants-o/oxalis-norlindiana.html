Oxalis norlindiana

Oxalis norlindiana, commonly known as the Norwegian oxalis, is a species of flowering plant in the wood sorrel family, Oxalidaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern Norway and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. It produces small, edible berries that are often consumed by birds and other wildlife.

Oxalis norlindiana is a low-growing perennial that is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
