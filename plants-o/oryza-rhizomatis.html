Oryza rhizomatis

Oryza rhizomatis, commonly known as the rhizome rice, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its underground rhizomes, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish flowers. Its rhizomes are rich in starch and have a slightly sweet flavor.

Oryza rhizomatis is also valued for its potential in sustainable agriculture due to its adaptability to various soil conditions.
