Ossaea quinquenervia

Ossaea quinquenervia, commonly known as the five-veined lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Liliaceae and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Ossaea quinquenervia is noted for its delicate structure and is often found in high-altitude meadows, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
