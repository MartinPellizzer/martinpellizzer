Ornithidium sanguinolentum

Ornithidium sanguinolentum, commonly known as the crimson feather fern, is a rare and striking plant characterized by its vivid red fronds that resemble delicate feathers.

It is native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant's unique coloration is due to high concentrations of anthocyanin pigments, which also provide protection against UV radiation. Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, typically found on the underside of its fronds.

Despite its beauty, it is a challenging plant to cultivate outside its natural habitat due to its specific environmental requirements.
