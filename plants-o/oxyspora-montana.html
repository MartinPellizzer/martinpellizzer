Oxyspora montana

Oxyspora montana, commonly known as the mountain oxyspora, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Oxyspora montana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
