Oxytropis middendorffii subsp. albida

Oxytropis middendorffii subsp.

albida, commonly known as the white milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae. It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and small, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant produces small, round pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, including parts of Russia and Mongolia. Oxytropis middendorffii subsp.

albida is adapted to harsh environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.
