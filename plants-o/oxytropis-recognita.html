Oxytropis recognita

Oxytropis recognita, commonly known as the Sierra lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and woodland habitats.
