Oxalis calthifolia

Oxalis calthifolia, commonly known as the white wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with a heart-shaped leaflet and small, delicate white flowers that resemble those of the genus Calothrix. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas. Its botanical characteristics include a short rhizome and a tendency to form dense mats.

Oxalis calthifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
