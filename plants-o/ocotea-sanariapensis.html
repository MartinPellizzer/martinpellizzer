Ocotea sanariapensis

Ocotea sanariapensis, commonly known as the Sanariape Ocotea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite, and often elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. It produces small, white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by dark blue berries.

The plant is valued for its aromatic wood and has potential medicinal uses, though it remains relatively understudied.
