Ottelia brachyphylla

Ottelia brachyphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved water plant, is a species of aquatic plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its short, broad leaves that float on the water surface, with roots submerged in the aquatic environment. The plant typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats such as ponds and lakes. Its flowers are small and white, often appearing in clusters, and it is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich waters.

Ottelia brachyphylla plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms and contributing to water oxygenation.
