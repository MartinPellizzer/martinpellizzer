Oxalis latifolia

Oxalis latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-drained soil. Oxalis latifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
