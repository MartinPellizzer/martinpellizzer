Ocymastrum rubrum

Ocymastrum rubrum, commonly known as the red-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, and it thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ocymastrum rubrum plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
