Oxytropis ixodes

Oxytropis ixodes, commonly known as the redstem locoweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

The plant produces trifoliate leaves and has a deep taproot system that allows it to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. It is often found in sagebrush steppe and other dry, open habitats.

Oxytropis ixodes is known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock.
