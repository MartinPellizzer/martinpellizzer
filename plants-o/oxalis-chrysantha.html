Oxalis chrysantha

Oxalis chrysantha, commonly known as the golden wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to parts of North America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive yellowish-green color and produces small, star-shaped flowers that range from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is a perennial herb that thrives in temperate climates and is often found in the understory of forests.

Oxalis chrysantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and blooms.
