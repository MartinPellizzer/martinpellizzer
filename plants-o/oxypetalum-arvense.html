Oxypetalum arvense

Oxypetalum arvense, commonly known as the field oxypetalum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy. Its flowers are small, white to pale pink, and clustered in dense, terminal inflorescences.

Oxypetalum arvense is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its role in supporting pollinator populations.
