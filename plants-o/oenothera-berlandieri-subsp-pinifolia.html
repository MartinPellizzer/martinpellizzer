Oenothera berlandieri subsp. pinifolia

Oenothera berlandieri subsp.

pinifolia, commonly known as the pine-leaf evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that resemble pine needles, hence its subspecies name.

The plant produces showy yellow or orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and along dry streambeds.

The species is known for its adaptability to harsh conditions and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
