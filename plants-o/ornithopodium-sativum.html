Ornithopodium sativum

Ornithopodium sativum, commonly known as the common sea bean, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in maritime environments. It has small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer, and it is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.

The seeds are edible and can be used in traditional cuisines.
