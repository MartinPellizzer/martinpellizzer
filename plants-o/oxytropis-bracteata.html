Oxytropis bracteata

Oxytropis bracteata, commonly known as the bracteate milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of western North America.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. It has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in poor, sandy soils and withstand drought conditions.

Oxytropis bracteata plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various desert wildlife.
