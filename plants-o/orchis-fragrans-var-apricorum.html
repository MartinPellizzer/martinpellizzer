Orchis fragrans var. apricorum

Orchis fragrans var. apricorum, commonly known as the spring orchid, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has a short, stout rhizome. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, while the flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white with a faintly colored lip.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
