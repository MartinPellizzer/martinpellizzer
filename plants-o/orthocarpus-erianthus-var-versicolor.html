Orthocarpus erianthus var. versicolor

Orthocarpus erianthus var. versicolor, commonly known as the variable orchid vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, colorful flowers that range from white to pink and purple, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds dispersed by animals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its botanical interest lies in its variability in flower color and its role in local ecosystems.
