Oxalis recurva var. texana

Oxalis recurva var. texana, commonly known as the Texas wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with reddish veins and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland settings. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance.

The plant is also noted for its ability to tolerate drought conditions once established.
