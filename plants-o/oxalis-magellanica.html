Oxalis magellanica

Oxalis magellanica, commonly known as the Magellan wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in cooler climates.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking shade-tolerant plants.
