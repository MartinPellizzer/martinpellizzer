Orchis grisebachii

Orchis grisebachii, commonly known as the pale orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of pale to whitish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption.

Orchis grisebachii is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
