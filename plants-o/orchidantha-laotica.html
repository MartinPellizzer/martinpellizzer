Orchidantha laotica

Orchidantha laotica, commonly known as the Laotian orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Laos.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, fragrant blossoms that typically bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant features strap-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that attract various pollinators.

Due to its limited habitat and vulnerability to environmental changes, Orchidantha laotica is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
