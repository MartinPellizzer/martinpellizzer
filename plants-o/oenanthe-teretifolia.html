Oenanthe teretifolia

Oenanthe teretifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water dropwort, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, terete (cylindrical) stems and small, opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and wet meadows.

Oenanthe teretifolia is often found in temperate regions and is part of the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley.
