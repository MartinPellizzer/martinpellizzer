Ocotea eggersiana

Ocotea eggersiana, commonly known as the Eggers's ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within lowland and mountain forests. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy surface, while its bark is smooth and often exhibits a reddish or brownish hue.

The fruit is a small, dark drupe that develops from the flower and is an important food source for various wildlife species.
