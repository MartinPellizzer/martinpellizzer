Oenothera hazeliae var. subterminalis

Oenothera hazeliae var. subterminalis, commonly known as the subterminal evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It features showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Oenothera hazeliae var. subterminalis is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils.
