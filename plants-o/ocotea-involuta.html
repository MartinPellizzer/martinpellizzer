Ocotea involuta

Ocotea involuta, commonly known as the white ocotea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Venezuela. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are glossy and elliptical in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in axillary or terminal inflorescences.

The fruit is a small, smooth, green drupe that turns yellow when mature.
