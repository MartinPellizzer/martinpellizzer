Ortmannia

Ortmannia, commonly known as the "buttercup orchid," is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to the southern United States, particularly Texas, and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plants are characterized by their showy, yellow or orange flowers with a distinctive cup-shaped structure. They are perennial herbs that often grow in open woodlands or along stream banks.

Ortmannia species are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes cultivated in gardens.
