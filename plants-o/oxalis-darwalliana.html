Oxalis darwalliana

Oxalis darwalliana, commonly known as the velvetleaf wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in shaded environments, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens. Its foliage is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive appearance.

Oxalis darwalliana is also known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, forming dense colonies in suitable conditions.
