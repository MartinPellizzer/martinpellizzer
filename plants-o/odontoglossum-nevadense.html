Odontoglossum nevadense

Odontoglossum nevadense, commonly known as the Nevada tooth-leaf orchid, is a rare orchid species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique tooth-like projections on its leaves, which give it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, humid environments. Its flowers are small and often pale green or white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
