Ophrys cornuta var. leptomera

Ophrys cornuta var. leptomera, commonly known as the slender horned orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its unique floral morphology, featuring a labellum that mimics the appearance of a female bee, which plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as woodlands and rocky slopes, where it thrives in nutrient-poor soils. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brownish in color, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, this variety is considered vulnerable, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts for its survival.
