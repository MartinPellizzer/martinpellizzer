Ophrys × chobautii

Ophrys × chobautii, commonly known as the Chobaut's bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant produces showy, bee-like flowers with intricate patterns and a strong scent, attracting male bees for pollination. It typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats.

Ophrys × chobautii is a perennial herb with a short, stout stem and is valued for its ornamental appeal in botanical gardens.
