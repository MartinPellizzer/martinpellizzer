Oncidium divaricatum

Oncidium divaricatum, commonly known as the Orchid of the Andes, is a tropical orchid native to South America.

It features strap-like, dark green leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hair. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences with showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Oncidium divaricatum is prized for its long-lasting blooms and is a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
