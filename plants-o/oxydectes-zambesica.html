Oxydectes zambesica

Oxydectes zambesica, commonly known as the Zambesian sandthorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, round fruits that are often consumed by wildlife. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
