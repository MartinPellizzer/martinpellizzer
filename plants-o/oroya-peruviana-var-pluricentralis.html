Oroya peruviana var. pluricentralis

Oroya peruviana var. pluricentralis, commonly known as the Andean starflower, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at elevations between 3,000 and 5,000 meters. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to the harsh, high-altitude environment.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in alpine gardens.
