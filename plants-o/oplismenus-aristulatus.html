Oplismenus aristulatus

Oplismenus aristulatus, commonly known as the arrowleaf groundcherry, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear alternate, arrow-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly toothed margin. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into fleshy, orange to red berries containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands.

Oplismenus aristulatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting various pollinators and wildlife.
