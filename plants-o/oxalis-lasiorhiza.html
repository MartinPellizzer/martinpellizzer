Oxalis lasiorhiza

Oxalis lasiorhiza, commonly known as the roughleaf wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with three heart-shaped leaflets and distinctive, delicate, five-petaled flowers that are typically yellow or white. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a rugged appearance.

Oxalis lasiorhiza is often found in sandy or loamy soils and is adapted to humid climates.
