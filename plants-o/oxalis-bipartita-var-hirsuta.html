Oxalis bipartita var. hirsuta

Oxalis bipartita var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of North America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive veining pattern and produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and foliage, which give it its common name. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Oxalis bipartita var. hirsuta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
