Ophrys × fucinis

Ophrys × fucinis, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Ophrys fucina and Ophrys apifera, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant is notable for its intricate flower structure, which mimics the appearance of female bees to attract male bees for pollination. Its flowers are typically greenish-brown with intricate patterns that resemble bee bodies.

Ophrys × fucinis is often found in grasslands and woodland edges across Europe, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.
