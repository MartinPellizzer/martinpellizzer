Onobrychis montana f. parviflora

Onobrychis montana f. parviflora, commonly known as the dwarf milkvetch, is a low-growing perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, blooming in early summer. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
