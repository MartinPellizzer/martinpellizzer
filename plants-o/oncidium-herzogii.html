Oncidium herzogii

Oncidium herzogii, commonly known as the Herzog's orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, long-lasting flowers that typically come in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its roots are epiphytic, allowing it to grow on trees or other surfaces in its natural habitat.

Oncidium herzogii requires bright, indirect light and well-draining potting media for optimal growth.
