Ocotea hirtistyla

Ocotea hirtistyla, commonly known as the hairy ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, camphor trees, and other aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are typically hairy, giving it the common name, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife.

Ocotea hirtistyla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance and ecological significance.
