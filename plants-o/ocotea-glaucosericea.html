Ocotea glaucosericea

Ocotea glaucosericea, commonly known as the white-leafed ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped, glaucous (bluish-gray) leaves and small, fragrant flowers. It typically grows in lowland rainforests and is valued for its aromatic wood.

The species is also noted for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
