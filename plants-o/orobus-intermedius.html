Orobus intermedius

Orobus intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate orobus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are often pink or purple, and it produces small, flat pods that contain several seeds.

Orobus intermedius is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
