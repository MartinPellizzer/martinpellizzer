Oxalis perineson

Oxalis perineson, commonly known as the Perineson wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its foliage contains oxalic acid, which can be toxic if ingested in large quantities.

Oxalis perineson is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.
