Oxalis matancillae

Oxalis matancillae, commonly known as the Matancillae wood sorrel, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. It produces edible leaves and flowers, though they should be consumed in moderation due to their oxalic acid content.

Oxalis matancillae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
