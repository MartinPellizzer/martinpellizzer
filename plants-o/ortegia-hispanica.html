Ortegia hispanica

Ortegia hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in late spring. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern.

Ortegia hispanica thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
