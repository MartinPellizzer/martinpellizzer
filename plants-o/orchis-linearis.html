Orchis linearis

Orchis linearis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features a single, slender stem that can reach up to 60 cm in height, bearing a single spike of pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, often arranged along the stem.

Orchis linearis is known for its distinctive floral structure, which includes specialized petals and a labellum adapted for pollination by specific insects.
