Orchis angustifolia subsp. traunsteineri

Orchis angustifolia subsp.

traunsteineri, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves. It produces a single spike of pale yellow to greenish-white flowers with a distinctive hooded lip.

This subspecies is typically found in moist, calcareous grasslands and woodlands across Europe. The plant is perennial and has a rhizomatous root system, which helps it survive in its specific habitat.

Its flowering period usually occurs between June and August, making it a notable species for botanical study and conservation efforts.
