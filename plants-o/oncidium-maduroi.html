Oncidium maduroi

Oncidium maduroi, commonly known as the Maduro orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous inflorescences that bear clusters of fragrant, colorful flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers often display a mix of yellow, orange, and red hues, with intricate patterns.

Oncidium maduroi requires well-drained potting media and moderate to high light conditions for optimal growth.
