Oenocarpus edulis

Oenocarpus edulis, commonly known as the breadfruit tree, is a tropical plant native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is a large, evergreen tree that can grow up to 25 meters tall, with a broad, umbrella-like canopy. The plant produces large, round fruits that are rich in carbohydrates and have a starchy texture when cooked. Its leaves are large, palmate, and often used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

The tree is highly valued for its nutritional content and ecological importance in tropical ecosystems.
