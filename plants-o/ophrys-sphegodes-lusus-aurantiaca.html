Ophrys sphegodes lusus aurantiaca

Ophrys sphegodes lusus aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden bee orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its intricate, bee-like flower structure, which features a vivid yellow coloration and a complex pattern that mimics the appearance of a female bee. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats. Its flowers are specialized for pollination by male bees, which are deceived into attempting to mate with the flower's pseudocopulation mechanism.

This orchid is of particular interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance.
