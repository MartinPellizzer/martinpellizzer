Ophryosporus origanoides var. microcephalus

Ophryosporus origanoides var. microcephalus, commonly known as the small-headed thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves and produces small, pinkish-white flowers in the summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. Its foliage is often used in culinary applications, similar to other thyme species.

The variety is distinguished by its smaller flower heads compared to other varieties of Ophryosporus.
