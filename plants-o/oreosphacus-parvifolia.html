Oreosphacus parvifolia

Oreosphacus parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale pink. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight. Its unique floral structure and ornamental value make it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.

The plant's botanical significance lies in its role as a species of ecological and aesthetic importance in its native habitats.
