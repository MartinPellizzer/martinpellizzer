Ophryosporus marchii

Ophryosporus marchii, commonly known as the Marchii ghost orchid, is a rare and elusive orchid species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, ghost-like appearance, with delicate white petals and a slender, wiry stem. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. It produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in the early morning, often during the rainy season.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Ophryosporus marchii is highly sensitive to environmental changes and is considered a conservation concern.
