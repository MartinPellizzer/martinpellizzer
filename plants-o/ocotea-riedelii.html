Ocotea riedelii

Ocotea riedelii, commonly known as the Riedel's ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree belonging to the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a small, dark berry that contains a single seed.

Ocotea riedelii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance and ecological significance.
