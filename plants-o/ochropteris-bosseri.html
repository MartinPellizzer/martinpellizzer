Ochropteris bosseri

Ochropteris bosseri, commonly known as the Bosser's cliff fern, is a rare and specialized fern species native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender fronds that can reach up to two meters in length, with a distinctive, glossy green coloration. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically growing on rocky slopes or cliffs where it can anchor itself with its rhizomes. Its reproductive structures, including spore-producing sorus, are located on the underside of its leaves, a common feature among ferns.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Ochropteris bosseri is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
