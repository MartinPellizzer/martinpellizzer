Orobus luteus subsp. transsylvanicus

Orobus luteus subsp.

transsylvanicus, commonly known as the Transylvanian yellow orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland and meadow habitats.

The plant features a slender, upright stem with a single, showy yellow flower that blooms in late spring. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The subspecies is characterized by its unique floral structure and is often found in regions with a temperate climate.
