Obione turbinata

Obione turbinata, commonly known as the turbinated orchid, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, spiral-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and it produces small, fragrant blooms that attract various pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
