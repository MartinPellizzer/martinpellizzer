Ophrys × albertiana nothosubsp. petruccii

Ophrys × albertiana nothosubsp.

petruccii, commonly known as the Albertine bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the Ophrys genus, which is known for its mimicry of female insects to attract male pollinators.

The plant produces intricate, bee-like flowers that are highly specialized for pollination by male bees. It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forest floors or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics include a basal rosette of leaves and a single spike of flowers with a distinctive labellum that resembles a female bee.
