Oncidium warmingii

Oncidium warmingii, commonly known as the Warming's orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It features a compact growth habit with a pseudobulb and is known for its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically produces multiple flower spikes, each bearing several blooms that last for several weeks. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil with regular watering.

Oncidium warmingii is a popular choice for indoor cultivation due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
