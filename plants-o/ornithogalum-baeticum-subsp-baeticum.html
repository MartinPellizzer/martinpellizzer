Ornithogalum baeticum subsp. baeticum

Ornithogalum baeticum subsp.

baeticum, commonly known as the Baetican star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its lanceolate, glaucous leaves and bell-shaped, white or pale pink flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. It is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.

The species is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine.
