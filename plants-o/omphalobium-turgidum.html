Omphalobium turgidum

Omphalobium turgidum, commonly known as the woolly burr medic, is a perennial legume native to Australia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, globular inflorescences. Its root system is well-developed, allowing it to thrive in a variety of soil types.

Omphalobium turgidum is often used in agricultural settings for soil improvement and as a forage crop.
