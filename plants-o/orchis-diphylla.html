Orchis diphylla

Orchis diphylla, commonly known as the two-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features a basal rosette of two large, heart-shaped leaves, from which a flowering stem rises. Its flowers are usually purple or violet, arranged in a spike and characterized by their intricate, lip-shaped petals.

Orchis diphylla is known for its long blooming period, often from late spring to early summer.
