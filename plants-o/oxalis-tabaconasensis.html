Oxalis tabaconasensis

Oxalis tabaconasensis, commonly known as the Tabacones oxalis, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and rocky areas. Its leaves are often adorned with distinctive patterns and can change color based on environmental conditions.

Oxalis tabaconasensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
