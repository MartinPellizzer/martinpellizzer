Ocotea stuebelii

Ocotea stuebelii, commonly known as the Caribbean laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests. Its wood is valued for its durability and distinctive scent, often used in traditional craftsmanship.

Ocotea stuebelii plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
