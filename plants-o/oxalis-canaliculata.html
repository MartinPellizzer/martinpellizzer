Oxalis canaliculata

Oxalis canaliculata, commonly known as the buttonweed or wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive pattern of deep green veins and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is often found in moist, shaded areas and thrives in well-drained soil. It is a member of the Oxalidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Oxalis canaliculata is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
