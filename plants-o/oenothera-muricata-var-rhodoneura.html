Oenothera muricata var. rhodoneura

Oenothera muricata var. rhodoneura, commonly known as the red-stemmed evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, reddish stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths. Its flowers have four petals and are followed by capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and ecological benefits.
