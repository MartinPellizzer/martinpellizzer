Onosma wendelboi

Onosma wendelboi, commonly known as Wendelbo's hyssop, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the eastern Mediterranean.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, tubular blue or violet flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Onosma wendelboi is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun.
