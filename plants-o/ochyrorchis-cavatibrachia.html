Ochyrorchis cavatibrachia

Ochyrorchis cavatibrachia, commonly known as the hollow-bract orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the mountainous regions of New Guinea.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a long, slender spur that extends from the base of the flower. The plant produces small, delicate flowers with a distinctive purple or pink coloration, often with intricate patterns on the petals. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is typically found in shaded, forested areas.
