Ophrys holosericea var. aliceae

Ophrys holosericea var. aliceae, commonly known as the Alice's Orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a specialized lip that mimics the appearance of a female bee, a trait known as pseudocopulation. This mimicry plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy, attracting male bees that attempt to mate with the flower. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts.
