Oncidium amazonicum

Oncidium amazonicum, commonly known as the Amazon orchid, is a tropical orchid native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its long, drooping inflorescences that bear numerous fragrant, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the environment, and it thrives in high humidity and indirect light.

Oncidium amazonicum is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its vibrant blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
