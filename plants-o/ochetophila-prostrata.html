Ochetophila prostrata

Ochetophila prostrata, commonly known as the prostrate ochetophila, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged in a dense, mat-like formation along the ground. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in the axils of the leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and habitat restoration projects.
