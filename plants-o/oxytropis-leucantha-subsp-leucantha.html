Oxytropis leucantha subsp. leucantha

Oxytropis leucantha subsp.

leucantha, commonly known as white locoweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. It has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in arid conditions.

The subspecies is distinguished by its pale blue to white flowers and its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
