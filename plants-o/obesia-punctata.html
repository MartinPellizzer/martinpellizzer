Obesia punctata

Obesia punctata, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, often colorful flowers with a spotted pattern. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or wetlands. Its leaves are usually broad and grass-like, and the flowers bloom in late spring or early summer.

Obesia punctata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
