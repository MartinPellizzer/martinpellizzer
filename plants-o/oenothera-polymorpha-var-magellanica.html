Oenothera polymorpha var. magellanica

Oenothera polymorpha var. magellanica, commonly known as the Magellan evening primrose, is a species native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, demonstrating adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and hairy, while the stems are often erect and branched.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
