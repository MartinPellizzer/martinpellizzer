Orumbella

Orumbella, commonly known as the sacred flower of the Yoruba people, is a rare and mystical plant native to the tropical regions of West Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped blooms that range in color from white to pale yellow, often resembling a small, elegant bell. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often associated with spiritual significance in traditional Yoruba culture. Its leaves are broad and glossy, and it produces a fragrant scent that is said to attract beneficial insects.

Due to its cultural and ecological importance, Orumbella is both a symbol of reverence and a subject of conservation efforts.
