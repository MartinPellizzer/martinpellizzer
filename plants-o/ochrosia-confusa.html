Ochrosia confusa

Ochrosia confusa, commonly known as the Florida starflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces star-shaped, white to yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly waxy texture.

Ochrosia confusa is well adapted to coastal and sandy soils, making it a hardy plant in its native habitat.
