Oxytropis campestris var. scotica

Oxytropis campestris var. scotica, commonly known as Scotch broom, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant is characterized by its bright yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. Its trifoliate leaves are compound, with three small leaflets, and it produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Scotch broom is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in ecological restoration projects.
