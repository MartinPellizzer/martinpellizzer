Oxytropis tschatkalensis

Oxytropis tschatkalensis, commonly known as the Karakorum locoweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it is adapted to survive in arid environments.
