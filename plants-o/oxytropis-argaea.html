Oxytropis argaea

Oxytropis argaea, commonly known as the Argaeasweet vetch, is a perennial legume native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish-pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Oxytropis argaea is also known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in soil enrichment.
