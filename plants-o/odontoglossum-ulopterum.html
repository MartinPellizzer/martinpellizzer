Odontoglossum ulopterum

Odontoglossum ulopterum, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple and green foliage and its elegant, fragrant orchid flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in high-altitude cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in its challenging habitat.

Odontoglossum ulopterum is a popular orchid among horticulturists due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
