Onopordum longissimum

Onopordum longissimum, commonly known as the Spanish thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, pale purple to white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has deeply lobed, silvery-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in dry, rocky habitats.

Onopordum longissimum is known for its ecological importance, providing food and shelter for various insects and birds.
