Oberonia fornicata

Oberonia fornicata, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, sympodial growth habit and produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually ovate to elliptical in shape and have a waxy texture.

Oberonia fornicata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical botanical gardens.
