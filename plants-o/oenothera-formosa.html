Oenothera formosa

Oenothera formosa, commonly known as the showy evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking yellow or orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats like grasslands and disturbed areas.

Oenothera formosa is also noted for its medicinal properties, with some parts of the plant used in traditional herbal remedies.
