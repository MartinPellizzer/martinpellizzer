Oenothera auricula

Oenothera auricula, commonly known as the golden cupflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers that resemble small cups, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and pond edges. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Oenothera auricula is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
