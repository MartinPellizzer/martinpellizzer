Ornithogalum tulbaghense

Ornithogalum tulbaghense, commonly known as the Tulbagh star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of star-shaped, white flowers in spring. The plant is adapted to rocky and sandy soils, often growing in mountainous regions. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and moths, contributing to local biodiversity.
