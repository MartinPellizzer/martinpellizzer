Oxalis confusa

Oxalis confusa, commonly known as the purple oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive purple tint and produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant is part of the Oxalidaceae family, known for its calcium oxalate crystals in the leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Oxalis confusa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to spread through rhizomes.
