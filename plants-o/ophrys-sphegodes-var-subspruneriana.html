Ophrys sphegodes var. subspruneriana

Ophrys sphegodes var. subspruneriana, commonly known as the bee orchid, is a species of orchid native to Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive flower structure, which resembles a bee, aiding in pollination by male bees. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is found in grasslands and woodland edges. It has a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a single spike of flowers.

This variety is part of the Ophrys genus, known for its mimicry and specialized pollination mechanisms.
