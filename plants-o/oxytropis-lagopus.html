Oxytropis lagopus

Oxytropis lagopus, commonly known as the snow lotus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, sparsely leafed stems and showy yellow or purple flowers. The plant thrives in cold, arid environments and is adapted to high-altitude conditions. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are dispersed by wind, aiding in its survival in harsh climates.

Oxytropis lagopus is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
