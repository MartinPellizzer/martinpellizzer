Oryza sativa var. tcharinica

Oryza sativa var. tcharinica, commonly known as the Tcharinica rice, is a variant of the rice species native to specific regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated grains and a unique aroma that distinguishes it from other rice varieties. The plant typically grows in shallow water or wetland environments, requiring a tropical or subtropical climate for optimal growth. Its botanical features include long, narrow leaves and a panicle inflorescence that produces small, white flowers.

This variety is valued for its nutritional content and is often used in traditional cuisines.
