Odacmis

Odacmis, commonly known as the desert cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family and is native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant typically features spines for protection and may produce colorful flowers during the rainy season. Odacmis species vary in height and shape, with some growing as shrubs and others as small trees.

Its botanical features make it well-adapted to desert life, with minimal leaf surface to reduce water loss.
