Onobrychis scardica

Onobrychis scardica, commonly known as the Balkan sainfoin, is a perennial legume native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves, which are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Onobrychis scardica is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability and has been used historically as forage for livestock.
