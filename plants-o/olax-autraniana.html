Olax autraniana

Olax autraniana, commonly known as the African olive, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Olacaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It is adapted to a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Olax autraniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
