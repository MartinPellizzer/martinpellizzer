Orchis spectabilis

Orchis spectabilis, commonly known as the showy orchis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of purple and white, typically blooming from June to August. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its root system consists of a thick, fleshy rhizome.

Orchis spectabilis thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodlands and meadows.
