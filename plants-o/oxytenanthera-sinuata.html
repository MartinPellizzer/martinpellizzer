Oxytenanthera sinuata

Oxytenanthera sinuata, commonly known as the sinuous grass, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, delicate flowers arranged in panicles, which are often borne on long, slender stalks. Its leaves are narrow and slightly curled, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Oxytenanthera sinuata is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its graceful form and adaptability to various soil conditions.
