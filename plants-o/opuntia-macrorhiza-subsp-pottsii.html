Opuntia macrorhiza subsp. pottsii

Opuntia macrorhiza subsp.

pottsii, commonly known as the Potts' prickly pear, is a cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems that are often ribbed and covered in spines.

The plant produces large, showy yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tunafilla, is a spherical, fleshy structure that contains numerous seeds.

This subspecies is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and scrublands.
