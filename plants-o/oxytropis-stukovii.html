Oxytropis stukovii

Oxytropis stukovii, commonly known as Stukov's locoweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its spiny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale blue or purple in color. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

Oxytropis stukovii is also known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock.
