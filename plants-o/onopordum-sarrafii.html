Onopordum sarrafii

Onopordum sarrafii, commonly known as the Persian thistle, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Iran and Afghanistan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, often spiny foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in dry and sunny environments. Its yellow-orange flower heads appear in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Onopordum sarrafii is valued for its ornamental potential and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
