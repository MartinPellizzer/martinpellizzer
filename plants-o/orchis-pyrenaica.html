Orchis pyrenaica

Orchis pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula and the Pyrenees.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its life cycle includes a dormant phase during winter, with leaves emerging in early spring.

Orchis pyrenaica is notable for its specialized pollination mechanisms and is considered a rare and protected species in many areas.
