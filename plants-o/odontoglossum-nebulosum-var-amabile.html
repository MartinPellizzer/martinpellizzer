Odontoglossum nebulosum var. amabile

Odontoglossum nebulosum var. amabile, commonly known as the clouded orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that come in a range of colors including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in cool, humid environments at high altitudes, often in shaded forest understories. Its flowers are borne on tall, slender stems and can last for several weeks, making it a popular choice for both cultivation and botanical study.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a cut flower.
