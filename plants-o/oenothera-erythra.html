Oenothera erythra

Oenothera erythra, commonly known as the yellow evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that open in the evening, hence its common name. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oval seeds. Oenothera erythra typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairies and disturbed areas.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
