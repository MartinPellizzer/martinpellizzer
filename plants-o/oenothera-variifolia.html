Oenothera variifolia

Oenothera variifolia, commonly known as the variable evening primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in a variety of habitats, including prairies, open woods, and disturbed soils. Its leaves are alternate and vary in shape, contributing to its common name.

Oenothera variifolia is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its resilience to drought.
