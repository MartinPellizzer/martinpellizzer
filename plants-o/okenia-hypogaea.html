Okenia hypogaea

Okenia hypogaea, commonly known as the African milk tree, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a shrub-like plant native to arid regions of Africa, characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers, typically green or yellow, which are followed by orange or red fruits containing seeds. Its latex sap is milky and can be toxic, making it important to handle with care.

The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, showcasing remarkable resilience to drought.
