Olax laurentii

Olax laurentii, commonly known as the African snowball, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Olacaceae and is characterized by its dense clusters of white, waxy flowers that resemble small balls. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The flowers bloom in winter, making it a valuable addition to gardens in regions with mild winters.
