Oxalis albicans subsp. californica

Oxalis albicans subsp.

californica, commonly known as the California wood sorrel, is a perennial herb native to western North America. It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers with five petals.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and can spread through rhizomes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
