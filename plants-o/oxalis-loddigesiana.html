Oxalis loddigesiana

Oxalis loddigesiana, commonly known as the "Wood sorrel," is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces edible tubers that are sometimes used in traditional cuisine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Oxalis loddigesiana is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
