Ophiorrhiza robbrechtii

Ophiorrhiza robbrechtii, commonly known as the Robbrecht's mangrove, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

The plant typically grows in mangrove forests, where it forms dense thickets that provide habitat for various marine and terrestrial species. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a leathery texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in spikes.

Ophiorrhiza robbrechtii plays an important role in stabilizing coastal soils and preventing erosion.
