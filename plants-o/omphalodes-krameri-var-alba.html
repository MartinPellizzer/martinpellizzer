Omphalodes krameri var. alba

Omphalodes krameri var. alba, commonly known as white horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking white to pale pink flowers that resemble horns, which bloom in early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
