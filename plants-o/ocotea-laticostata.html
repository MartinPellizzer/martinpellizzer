Ocotea laticostata

Ocotea laticostata, commonly known as the broad-costed ocotea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are usually elliptical and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in axillary or terminal inflorescences.

The fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed, and the plant is often found in humid, shaded forest environments.
