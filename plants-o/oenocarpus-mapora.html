Oenocarpus mapora

Oenocarpus mapora, commonly known as the red mangrove, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive red, swollen fruit that contains a single seed, which is adapted for dispersal by water. The plant thrives in saline and brackish water environments, often forming dense mangrove forests in coastal areas. Its roots form a complex network that helps stabilize shorelines and filter water.

The plant plays a vital ecological role in tropical coastal ecosystems.
