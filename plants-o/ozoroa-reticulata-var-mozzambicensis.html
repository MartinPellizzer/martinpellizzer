Ozoroa reticulata var. mozzambicensis

Ozoroa reticulata var. mozzambicensis, commonly known as the Mozambique nettle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes stinging nettles, and is characterized by its stinging hairs on the leaves and stems. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape, and often have a hairy surface.

The flowers are small and clustered, with both male and female flowers present on the same plant, making it monoecious.
