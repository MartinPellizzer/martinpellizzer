Onobrychis galegifolia

Onobrychis galegifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf burr medic, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, trifoliate leaves with a hairy surface and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spikes. The plant is characterized by its cylindrical, spiny pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Onobrychis galegifolia is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability and historical use in livestock forage.
