Odontoglossum egertonii

Odontoglossum egertonii, commonly known as the Egerton's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the high-altitude regions of Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in cool, shaded forest environments. The plant features a pseudobulb with a single leaf and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. Its flowers are characterized by their intricate lip and spurred structures, which are adaptations for pollination by specific insect species.

Odontoglossum egertonii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
