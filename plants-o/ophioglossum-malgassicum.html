Ophioglossum malgassicum

Ophioglossum malgassicum, commonly known as the Madagascar adder's mouth, is a unique fern-like plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Ophioglossaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, tongue-shaped sporangium. The plant has a short, creeping rhizome with a single, large, lanceolate leaf. It produces spores in a specialized structure called a sporangium, which is located at the base of the leaf.

Ophioglossum malgassicum is adapted to the humid, tropical climate of Madagascar and thrives in shaded, moist environments.
