Ocotea compactiflora

Ocotea compactiflora, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It has glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and arranged alternately along the branches. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated in other tropical regions for landscaping purposes.
