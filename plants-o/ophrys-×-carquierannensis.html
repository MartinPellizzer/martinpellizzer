Ophrys × carquierannensis

Ophrys × carquierannensis, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a rare and fascinating plant resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is characterized by its intricate floral structure, which mimics the appearance of female wasps to attract male wasps for pollination. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows across Europe. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, while its flowers are highly specialized with a labellum that resembles a wasp's abdomen.

This orchid is of particular interest to botanists due to its unique pollination strategy and limited distribution.
