Ocotea megacarpa

Ocotea megacarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a woody drupe that contains a single seed, and the tree can grow up to 30 meters tall.

Ocotea megacarpa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine.
