Oreosedum pedicellatum

Oreosedum pedicellatum, commonly known as the desert honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with small, fleshy leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces clusters of yellow or orange tubular flowers, which are adapted for pollination by bees and other insects. Its stems are often covered in a waxy cuticle, providing additional protection against drought.

Oreosedum pedicellatum is typically found in rocky soils and is well-suited for xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
