Oenothera montereyensis

Oenothera montereyensis, commonly known as Monterey evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of central California.

It is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in coastal scrub and grassland habitats.

Oenothera montereyensis is also noted for its ability to tolerate salt spray and coastal winds.
