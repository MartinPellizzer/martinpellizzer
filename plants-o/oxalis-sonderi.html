Oxalis sonderi

Oxalis sonderi, commonly known as the yellow wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is a perennial shrub that can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters.

Oxalis sonderi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
