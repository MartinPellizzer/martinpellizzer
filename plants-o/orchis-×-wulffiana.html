Orchis × wulffiana

Orchis × wulffiana, commonly known as the Wulff's orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Orchis morio and Orchis pallida.

It is a terrestrial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family, characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet to purple flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing intricate flowers with three petals and a distinctive lip. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows, and is often found in regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a range of morphological variations, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
