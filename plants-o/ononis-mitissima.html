Ononis mitissima

Ononis mitissima, commonly known as lesser restharrow, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, pea-like pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Its foliage is trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.
