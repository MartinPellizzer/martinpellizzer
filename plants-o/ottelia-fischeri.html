Ottelia fischeri

Ottelia fischeri, commonly known as the water buttercup, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its floating leaves that remain just above the water surface. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in the summer months. Ottelia fischeri thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats.

Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the water, making it an important species in aquatic ecosystems.
