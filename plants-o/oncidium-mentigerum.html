Oncidium mentigerum

Oncidium mentigerum, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and exotic orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated pseudobulbs and striking, pale green to white flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its flowers are usually fragrant and bloom in clusters, often during the late summer or early fall.

Oncidium mentigerum is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts due to its beauty and rarity in cultivation.
