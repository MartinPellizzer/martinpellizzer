Orchis majalis lusus alborosacea

Orchis majalis lusus alborosacea, commonly known as the white helleborine, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem that bears a single, showy white or pale green flower with a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist woodlands and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces a spadix of small, greenish-white flowers.

The plant is noted for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and is often found in association with other woodland flora.
