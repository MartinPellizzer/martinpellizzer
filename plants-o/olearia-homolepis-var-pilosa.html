Olearia homolepis var. pilosa

Olearia homolepis var. pilosa, commonly known as the woolly olearia, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting native pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
