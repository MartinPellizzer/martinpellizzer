Oryzopsis latifolia

Oryzopsis latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its wide, flat leaves and tufted inflorescence, which consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often in sandy or rocky soils. Its botanical features include fibrous roots and a clump-forming growth habit.

Oryzopsis latifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in low-water conditions.
