Oncidium hartwegii

Oncidium hartwegii, commonly known as the Hartweg's orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, showy inflorescences that bear clusters of fragrant, brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air. Its flowers are often yellow or orange with intricate patterns, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Oncidium hartwegii requires bright, indirect light and consistent humidity to thrive.
