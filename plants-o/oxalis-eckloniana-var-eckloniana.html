Oxalis eckloniana var. eckloniana

Oxalis eckloniana var. eckloniana, commonly known as the forest oxalis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features delicate, trifoliate leaves that resemble clover, with each leaflet having a pointed tip. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for shade gardens and rockeries.
