Odontites vulgaris subsp. salinus

Odontites vulgaris subsp.

salinus, commonly known as the coastal toothwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to coastal regions of Europe. It thrives in saline and calcareous soils, often found in dunes and coastal grasslands.

The plant has small, tooth-like leaves and produces clusters of white or pale blue flowers. It is adapted to withstand salt spray and wind, making it a characteristic species of coastal ecosystems.

Its limited distribution and specialized habitat make it an important indicator of maritime environments.
