Oenothera biennis

Oenothera biennis, commonly known as evening primrose, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow or orange flowers that open in the evening, hence its common name. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies, disturbed areas, and along roadsides.

Oenothera biennis is also noted for its medicinal uses, particularly for its oil-rich seeds.
