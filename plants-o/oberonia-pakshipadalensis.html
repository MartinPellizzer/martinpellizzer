Oberonia pakshipadalensis

Oberonia pakshipadalensis, commonly known as the Pakshipadalensis orchid, is a rare and endemic species found in the high-altitude regions of the Eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique and intricate floral structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in moist, shaded forest environments. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and often display a striking combination of colors, including shades of purple and white.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts in its native range.
