Oxalis repens

Oxalis repens, commonly known as wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and small, delicate flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread and form dense mats.

Oxalis repens is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
