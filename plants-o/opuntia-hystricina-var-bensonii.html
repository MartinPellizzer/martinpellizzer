Opuntia hystricina var. bensonii

Opuntia hystricina var. bensonii, commonly known as the Benson's prickly pear, is a cactus species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flattened, fleshy stems that are segmented and covered in spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tunaf, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
