Opuntia guilanchii

Opuntia guilanchii, commonly known as the Mexican prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems that are covered in spines and glochids, which are fine, hair-like structures. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tunas, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

Opuntia guilanchii is well adapted to arid environments and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of food.
