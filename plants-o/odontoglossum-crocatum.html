Odontoglossum crocatum

Odontoglossum crocatum, commonly known as the crocodile orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

This plant is characterized by its striking, elongated pseudobulbs and showy, dark green to reddish-brown flowers that resemble the scales of a crocodile. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. The flowers typically bloom in late winter to early spring, adding a dramatic visual display to its natural habitat.

Odontoglossum crocatum is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
