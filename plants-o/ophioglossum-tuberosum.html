Ophioglossum tuberosum

Ophioglossum tuberosum, commonly known as the horse tail, is a rare and ancient fern-like plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are clustered in a long, whip-like structure at the end of its stem. The plant has a unique reproductive system, with spores being released from the sporangia rather than flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is considered a conservation concern in many regions.
