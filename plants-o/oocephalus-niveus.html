Oocephalus niveus

Oocephalus niveus, commonly known as the white-headed rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, showcasing its adaptability to moist conditions. The plant features slender, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, giving it its common name. Its small, greenish-white flowers are arranged in spike-like clusters, and it produces small, dark brown seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Oocephalus niveus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
