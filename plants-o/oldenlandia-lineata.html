Oldenlandia lineata

Oldenlandia lineata, commonly known as the threadleaf mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It features slender, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded habitats.

Its unique foliage and delicate flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
