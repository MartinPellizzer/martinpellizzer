Oxalis macrantha

Oxalis macrantha, commonly known as the large-flowered wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and thrives in partial shade to full sun. Its flowers bloom in spring, and it is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Oxalis macrantha is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plants.
