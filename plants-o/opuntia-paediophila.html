Opuntia paediophila

Opuntia paediophila, commonly known as the dwarf prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, flat, paddle-shaped stems that are covered in clusters of spines and glochids. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers during the summer months, which are followed by edible fruits known as tunas. Opuntia paediophila is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

It plays an important role in its ecosystem, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
