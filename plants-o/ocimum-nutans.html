Ocimum nutans

Ocimum nutans, commonly known as the drooping basil, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and oregano. The plant is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. Ocimum nutans is known for its strong, spicy fragrance and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its aromatic compounds make it a popular choice for culinary and therapeutic applications.
