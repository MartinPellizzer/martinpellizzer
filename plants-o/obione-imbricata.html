Obione imbricata

Obione imbricata, commonly known as the overlapping leaf fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its intricate, overlapping foliage that gives it its common name. The plant features fronds that are typically pinnately divided, with a delicate, lace-like appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Obione imbricata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
