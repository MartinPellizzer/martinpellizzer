Ornithogalum clanwilliamae-gloria

Ornithogalum clanwilliamae-gloria, commonly known as the glory lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of white or pale yellow flowers with a distinctive, bell-shaped structure. The plant has lance-shaped, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
