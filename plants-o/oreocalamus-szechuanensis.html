Oreocalamus szechuanensis

Oreocalamus szechuanensis, commonly known as the Sichuan reed, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Sichuan, China.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
