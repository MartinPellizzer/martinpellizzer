Oenothera × drawertii

Oenothera × drawertii, commonly known as the Drawertii evening primrose, is a hybrid species native to the United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats like roadsides and prairies, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually erect and somewhat woody.

Oenothera × drawertii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
