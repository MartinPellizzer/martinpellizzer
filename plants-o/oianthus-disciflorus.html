Oianthus disciflorus

Oianthus disciflorus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces tall, spike-like inflorescences bearing small, white to pale blue flowers with a distinctive, spurred corolla. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Oianthus disciflorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
