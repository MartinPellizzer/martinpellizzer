Orthosiphon salagensis

Orthosiphon salagensis, commonly known as Java tea or Malabar mint, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and small, pale blue or white flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are widely used in traditional medicine for their diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Orthosiphon salagensis is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
