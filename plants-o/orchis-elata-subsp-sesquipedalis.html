Orchis elata subsp. sesquipedalis

Orchis elata subsp.

sesquipedalis, commonly known as the tall orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

The plant features a tall, erect stem with lance-shaped leaves and produces striking, showy flowers in shades of purple and white. Its flowers are characterized by a complex structure with three sepals and three petals, one of which is prominently hooded.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
