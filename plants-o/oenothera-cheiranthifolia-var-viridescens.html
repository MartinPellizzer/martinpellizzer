Oenothera cheiranthifolia var. viridescens

Oenothera cheiranthifolia var. viridescens, commonly known as the green-flowered evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and striking yellow flowers that open in the evening. The plant produces capsules that contain numerous small, oval seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and native plant gardens.
