Ophiorrhiza rugosa var. merguensis

Ophiorrhiza rugosa var. merguensis, commonly known as the Merguens mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountainous regions. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are usually white or pale yellow with five petals. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
