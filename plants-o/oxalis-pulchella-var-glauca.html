Oxalis pulchella var. glauca

Oxalis pulchella var. glauca, commonly known as the blue oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers and trifoliate leaves that resemble those of clover. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-drained soil. Its foliage is often covered in a waxy, bluish coating, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
