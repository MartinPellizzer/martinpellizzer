Orchis militaris f. acuminata

Orchis militaris f. acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-pointed soldier's orchid, is a botanical variant of the soldier's orchid, native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its erect, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The flowers are typically pale green to yellowish, with a distinctive hooded lip that aids in pollination. It thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

This orchid is noted for its unique floral morphology and is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
