Oxalis pulchella

Oxalis pulchella, commonly known as the pretty wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of yellow or white. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its foliage and flowers are often used in ornamental gardening due to their attractive appearance.

Oxalis pulchella is also valued for its ability to thrive in partial shade and its low maintenance requirements.
