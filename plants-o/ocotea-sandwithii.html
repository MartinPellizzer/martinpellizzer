Ocotea sandwithii

Ocotea sandwithii, commonly known as Sandwith's ocotea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the tropical rainforests of Central America, particularly in countries like Costa Rica and Panama. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are typically elliptical and have a glossy surface. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by spherical, dark blue berries.

Ocotea sandwithii is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
