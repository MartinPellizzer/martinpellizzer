Oldenlandia erecta

Oldenlandia erecta, commonly known as the "silver vine," is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, white or pale green flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a waxy texture, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is also known for its ability to produce a sticky, milky sap when damaged.
