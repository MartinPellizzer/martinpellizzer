Odontadenia cordata

Odontadenia cordata, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while the stems are woody and often branched. This species thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking blooms.
