Opuntia albisaetacens

Opuntia albisaetacens, commonly known as the white-hair prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in white, fuzzy spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by edible, red-fleshed fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and full sunlight.

Opuntia albisaetacens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local cultures.
