Opuntia ruthei

Opuntia ruthei, commonly known as the Desert Cactus or the Red Cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its flattened, fleshy stems that are often ribbed and covered in spines. The plant produces bright yellow or orange flowers, which are typically borne on the tips of the stems. Opuntia ruthei is a succulent, capable of storing water in its thick, fleshy tissue to survive in dry environments.

It is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and is often used in traditional medicine.
