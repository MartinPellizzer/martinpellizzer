Ophiopogon spicatus var. kunthianus

Ophiopogon spicatus var. kunthianus, commonly known as the Kunth's spiky lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color and can grow up to 30 centimeters in height. The plant produces dense clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to naturalize in suitable environments.
