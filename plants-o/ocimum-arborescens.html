Ocimum arborescens

Ocimum arborescens, commonly known as the sacred basil or holy basil, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, lavender-colored flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its medicinal and culinary uses, with leaves rich in essential oils. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Ocimum arborescens is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
