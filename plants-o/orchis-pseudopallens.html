Orchis pseudopallens

Orchis pseudopallens, commonly known as the pale orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear showy, pale purple to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its flowers are spurred at the base and have a distinctive lip that aids in pollination by specific insect species.

Orchis pseudopallens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
