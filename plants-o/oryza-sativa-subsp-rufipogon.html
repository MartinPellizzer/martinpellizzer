Oryza sativa subsp. rufipogon

Oryza sativa subsp.

rufipogon, commonly known as wild rice, is a wild relative of cultivated rice that originates from East Asia. It is characterized by its tall, grass-like appearance and produces small, red or brown grains.

This subspecies is notable for its genetic diversity and is considered an important gene pool for improving modern rice varieties. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in flood-prone areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable resource for agricultural research and breeding programs.
