Orchis caucasica var. markowitschii

Orchis caucasica var. markowitschii, commonly known as the Caucasian twayblade, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Caucasus region.

It is a perennial herb with a slender, leafless stem that bears two opposite leaves. The plant produces a single spike of flowers, typically in shades of purple or pink, with a distinctive hooded lip. It thrives in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows.

The species is characterized by its specialized mycorrhizal relationships, which are essential for its nutrient uptake.
