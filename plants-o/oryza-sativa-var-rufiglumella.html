Oryza sativa var. rufiglumella

Oryza sativa var. rufiglumella, commonly known as red rice, is a variety of rice characterized by its reddish hull and bran layer.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is often cultivated for its unique color and nutritional value. The plant has long, slender leaves and produces panicles of small, rice grains. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a grass-like growth habit typical of the Poaceae family.

Red rice is valued not only for its culinary use but also for its potential in agricultural diversity and resilience to certain environmental conditions.
