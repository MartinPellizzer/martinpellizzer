Orobanche oxyloba f. gussoneana

Orobanche oxyloba f. gussoneana, commonly known as the Italian broomrape, is a parasitic plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on its host for nutrients.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is native to southern Europe, particularly Italy. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has a highly reduced structure compared to its non-parasitic relatives. It typically parasitizes plants in the genus Chenopodium, such as goosefoot species.

Due to its specialized lifestyle, it plays a unique role in its ecosystem, though it is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
