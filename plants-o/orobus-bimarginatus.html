Orobus bimarginatus

Orobus bimarginatus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and pale pink. It is part of the Orchidaceae family, which includes many diverse and often showy flowering plants.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some areas.
