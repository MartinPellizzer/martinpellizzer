Oldenlandia macrosepala var. scabra

Oldenlandia macrosepala var. scabra, commonly known as roughleaf moonviolet, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the shade of forests. The leaves are distinctive for their rough, hairy texture, which gives the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in undergrowth areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
