Oxytropis leucantha var. viscida

Oxytropis leucantha var. viscida, commonly known as sticky milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which contribute to its sticky texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert environments.

Its ecological role includes nitrogen fixation and providing forage for wildlife.
