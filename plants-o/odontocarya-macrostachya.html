Odontocarya macrostachya

Odontocarya macrostachya, commonly known as the broad-stalked mountain grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils at high elevations. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Odontocarya macrostachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
