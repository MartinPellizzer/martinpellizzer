Oxalis hirsutissima var. glabrescens

Oxalis hirsutissima var. glabrescens, commonly known as the woolly wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.

The species is known for its ability to tolerate a range of environmental conditions, making it a versatile choice for gardens.
