Orchis latifolia var. inaequiloba

Orchis latifolia var. inaequiloba, commonly known as the broad-leaved orchid variant with unequal lobes, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often violet or purple, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its leaves are broad and lanceolate, with a slightly hairy surface.

The species is noted for its ecological importance as a food source for certain pollinators and as an indicator of undisturbed habitats.
