Ocimum mearnsii

Ocimum mearnsii, commonly known as Mexican oregano, is a herbaceous plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale purple. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates. Its essential oils, rich in compounds like carvacrol and thymol, contribute to its use in culinary and medicinal applications.

Ocimum mearnsii is also valued in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
