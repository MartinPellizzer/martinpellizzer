Oroya peruviana var. neoperuviana

Oroya peruviana var. neoperuviana, commonly known as the Peruvian orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous plant with slender, erect stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive, spurred shape that is characteristic of the Orchidaceae family. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude cloud forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
