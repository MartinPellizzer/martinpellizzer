Oligostachyum lima

Oligostachyum lima, commonly known as the Philippine bamboo or giant bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to the Philippines and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. It produces inflorescences that are clustered in small, compact spikes, which are a distinguishing feature of the genus.

Oligostachyum lima is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also valued for its potential in sustainable forestry and construction.
