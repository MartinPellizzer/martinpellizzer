Oberonia pygmaea

Oberonia pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf orchid, is a small epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows on trees or rocks, thriving in humid, shaded environments. The plant has slender, wiry stems and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Oberonia pygmaea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in terrariums or as a houseplant.
