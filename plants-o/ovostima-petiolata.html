Ovostima petiolata

Ovostima petiolata, commonly known as the leafy spurge, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It is often found in disturbed soils and is considered an invasive species in some regions.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various environmental conditions.
