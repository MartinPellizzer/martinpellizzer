Oenothera rubricapitata

Oenothera rubricapitata, commonly known as the scarlet evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, orange-red flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Oenothera rubricapitata is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils.
