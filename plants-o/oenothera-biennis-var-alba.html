Oenothera biennis var. alba

Oenothera biennis var. alba, commonly known as the white evening primrose, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy, white to pale yellow flowers that open in the evening and close by morning. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with lance-shaped leaves and a taproot system. Its flowers are composed of four petals and are pollinated primarily by moths and other nocturnal insects.

The plant is known for its medicinal uses, particularly for its oil-rich seeds.
