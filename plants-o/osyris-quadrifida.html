Osyris quadrifida

Osyris quadrifida, commonly known as the four-leaf osyris, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its attractive, palmately divided leaves that resemble a four-leaf clover. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Osyris quadrifida thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

It is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
