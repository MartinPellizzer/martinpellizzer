Octarrhena perpusilla

Octarrhena perpusilla, commonly known as the dwarf silvergrass, is a small perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and compact growth habit, typically reaching only a few inches in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
