Oxyceros horridus

Oxyceros horridus, commonly known as the rough oxyceros, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, giving them a rough texture.

Oxyceros horridus is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
