Osmunda spicanthus

Osmunda spicanthus, commonly known as the bottlebrush fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Osmundaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tall, and slender fronds that resemble a bottlebrush due to their dense, spore-bearing structures called sporangia. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, often found in moist, shaded environments. Its reproductive structures are clustered in spore-bearing clusters at the tips of its fronds, which are typically found on the underside.

Osmunda spicanthus is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
