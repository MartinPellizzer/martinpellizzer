Ochna gilletiana

Ochna gilletiana, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its striking, bright red flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its fruits are woody capsules that split open to release small, smooth seeds.

Ochna gilletiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
