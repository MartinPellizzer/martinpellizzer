Oxalis sellowiana var. alba

Oxalis sellowiana var. alba, commonly known as the white wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of white, five-petaled flowers. The plant belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is known for its delicate appearance and ornamental value. It typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has a rhizomatous root system.

The species is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
