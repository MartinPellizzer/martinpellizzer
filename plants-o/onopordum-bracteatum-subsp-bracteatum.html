Onopordum bracteatum subsp. bracteatum

Onopordum bracteatum subsp.

bracteatum, commonly known as the bracted thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to violet composite flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and disturbed areas. Its flowering heads are composed of many small florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
