Ochrocarpos pachyphyllus

Ochrocarpos pachyphyllus, commonly known as the thick-leaved stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its growth habit is low and spreading, forming dense mats that help prevent soil erosion.

Ochrocarpos pachyphyllus is well adapted to extreme temperatures and requires minimal water, making it a hardy species for rocky or sandy soils.
