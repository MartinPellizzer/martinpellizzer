Oncidium insleayi

Oncidium insleayi, commonly known as the Insleay's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in humid, shaded environments within the understory of tropical forests. The plant produces striking, pendulous inflorescences with vibrant, colorful flowers that range from yellow to orange and red. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the forest floor.

Oncidium insleayi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
