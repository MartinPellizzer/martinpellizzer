Orchis elata f. speciosissima

Orchis elata f. speciosissima, commonly known as the showy marsh orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear large, showy flowers in shades of purple and white. The plant typically grows in wet meadows and marshy areas, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its flowers are often arranged in a loose spike and are pollinated by insects such as bees and wasps.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is found in parts of Europe and western Asia.
