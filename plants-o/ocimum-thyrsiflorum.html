Ocimum thyrsiflorum

Ocimum thyrsiflorum, commonly known as wild basil, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to violet, typically blooming in summer. Its leaves are oval-shaped and have a strong, peppery scent when crushed.

Ocimum thyrsiflorum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
