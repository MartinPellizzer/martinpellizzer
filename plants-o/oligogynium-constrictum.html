Oligogynium constrictum

Oligogynium constrictum, commonly known as the narrowleaf water plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are often submerged or floating, adapted to aquatic conditions. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are typically found in the water or on damp soil. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and colonize wetland areas effectively.

Oligogynium constrictum plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
