Oxalis platensis

Oxalis platensis, commonly known as the false shamrock, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves that resemble those of the true shamrock. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers with five petals, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Oxalis platensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and delicate flowers.
