Orthosiphon buchananii

Orthosiphon buchananii, commonly known as the Malabar senna, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Orthosiphon buchananii thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.
