Orophaca caespitosa

Orophaca caespitosa, commonly known as the mountain grass or orophaca, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant has tufted, grass-like foliage and produces small, clustered inflorescences with white or pale blue flowers. Its botanical features include fibrous roots and a robust stem that supports its compact growth habit.

Orophaca caespitosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in alpine gardens.
