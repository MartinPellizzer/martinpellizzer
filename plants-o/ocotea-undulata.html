Ocotea undulata

Ocotea undulata, commonly known as the zigzag ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves that have a distinctive undulating or zigzag pattern along the veins. Its flowers are small and white, and it produces dark blue-black berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Ocotea undulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in the regions where it is found.
