Oberonia rufilabris

Oberonia rufilabris, commonly known as the red-lipped orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red lips on the inner petals, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its flowers are small and clustered, with a complex structure typical of the Orchidaceae family.

Oberonia rufilabris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
