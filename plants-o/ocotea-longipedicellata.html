Ocotea longipedicellata

Ocotea longipedicellata, commonly known as the long-fruited ocotea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Venezuela. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are drupes, typically green when immature and turning dark when mature.

Ocotea longipedicellata is often found in humid, lowland forests and is valued for its aromatic wood and ecological significance.
