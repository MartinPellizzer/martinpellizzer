Oenothera cespitosa var. crinita

Oenothera cespitosa var. crinita, commonly known as the curly willow or curly state willow, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its rosette of narrow, curly leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant belongs to the evening primrose family (Onagraceae) and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils. Its flowers open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
