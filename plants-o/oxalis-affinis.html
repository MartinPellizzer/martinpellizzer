Oxalis affinis

Oxalis affinis, commonly known as the common wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each leaflet resembling a heart shape, and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the family Oxalidaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in partial to full shade.

Oxalis affinis is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
