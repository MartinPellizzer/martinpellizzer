Ozarthris paradoxa

Ozarthris paradoxa, commonly known as the paradoxical orchid, is a rare and enigmatic species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, intricate floral structure. The plant exhibits a symbiotic relationship with specific mycorrhizal fungi, which is essential for its nutrient uptake. Its flowers are typically small, with a complex arrangement of petals and sepals that resemble a spider-like appearance.

Due to its elusive nature and limited habitat, Ozarthris paradoxa is considered a botanical curiosity and a subject of ongoing scientific study.
