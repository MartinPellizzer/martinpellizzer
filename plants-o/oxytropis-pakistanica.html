Oxytropis pakistanica

Oxytropis pakistanica, commonly known as the Pakistan locoweed, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Pakistan.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, demonstrating a high degree of adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Oxytropis pakistanica is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological niche and potential medicinal properties.
