Ornithogalum wiedemannii var. wiedemannii

Ornithogalum wiedemannii var. wiedemannii, commonly known as the silver arrow, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a short, rhizomatous stem with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in umbels. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
