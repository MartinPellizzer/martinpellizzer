Oxalis stricta var. viridiflora

Oxalis stricta var. viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features trifoliate leaves that are typically heart-shaped with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers with five petals, often clustered in umbels. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Oxalis stricta var. viridiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
