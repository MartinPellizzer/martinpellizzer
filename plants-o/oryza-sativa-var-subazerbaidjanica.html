Oryza sativa var. subazerbaidjanica

Oryza sativa var. subazerbaidjanica, commonly known as the Subazebaidjanica rice, is a cultivated rice variety originating from the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stalks and small, rice-like grains that are typically white when mature. This variety is adapted to temperate climates and requires a warm, humid environment for optimal growth. It is primarily grown in regions with well-drained soils and sufficient sunlight.

The plant's botanical features make it a valuable crop for both culinary and agricultural purposes.
