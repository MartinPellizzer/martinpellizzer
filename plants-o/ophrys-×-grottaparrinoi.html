Ophrys × grottaparrinoi

Ophrys × grottaparrinoi, commonly known as the Grottaparrino bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to southern Italy.

It belongs to the genus Ophrys, which is characterized by its intricate floral structures that mimic female insects to attract male pollinators. The plant produces strikingly colored flowers with a unique labellum that resembles a bee, enhancing its mimicry. It typically grows in calcareous soils and is found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
