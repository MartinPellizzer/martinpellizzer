Ormosia bahiensis

Ormosia bahiensis, commonly known as Brazilian wistaria, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Its leaves are alternate, compound, and often have a hairy texture.

Ormosia bahiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
