Ophrys fimbriata

Ophrys fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed bee orchid, is a species of orchid native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which resembles a female bee, aiding in its specialized pollination by male bees. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its flowers are usually pale green to brownish in color, with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of an insect.

Ophrys fimbriata is a perennial herb that produces a single spike of flowers, often blooming in late spring to early summer.
