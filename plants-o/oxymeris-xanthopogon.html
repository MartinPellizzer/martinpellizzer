Oxymeris xanthopogon

Oxymeris xanthopogon, commonly known as the yellow grass or African silver grass, is a member of the Poaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and savannas.

Oxymeris xanthopogon is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its graceful appearance.
