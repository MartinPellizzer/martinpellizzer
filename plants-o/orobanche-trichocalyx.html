Orobanche trichocalyx

Orobanche trichocalyx, commonly known as the slender broomrape, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is characterized by its lack of true leaves and its highly reduced, often yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and is found in regions across Europe, Asia, and North Africa. Its life cycle involves germinating in response to chemical signals released by host roots, after which it attaches to the host and extracts water and nutrients through specialized structures called haustoria.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can have significant impacts on agricultural crops, making it both a subject of botanical study and a potential agricultural concern.
