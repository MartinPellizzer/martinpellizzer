Oryzopsis alpestris

Oryzopsis alpestris, commonly known as the alpine oat, is a grass species native to high-altitude regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Oryzopsis alpestris thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in alpine and subalpine ecosystems.
