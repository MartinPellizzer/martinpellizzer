Oxytropis baschkiriensis subsp. skvortsovii

Oxytropis baschkiriensis subsp.

skvortsovii, commonly known as the Bashkir milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple.

The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it ecologically significant in its native range.
