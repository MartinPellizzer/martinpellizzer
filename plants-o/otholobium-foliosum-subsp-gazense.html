Otholobium foliosum subsp. gazense

Otholobium foliosum subsp.

gazense, commonly known as the Gazeni bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and legume fruits.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its small, clustered flowers are often pink or purple, and it produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in local horticulture.
