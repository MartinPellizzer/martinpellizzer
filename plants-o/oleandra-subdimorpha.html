Oleandra subdimorpha

Oleandra subdimorpha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved oleander, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 6 meters. The plant features opposite, narrow, and leathery leaves that are often grayish-green in color. Its flowers are showy, appearing in clusters of 3 to 6, and are typically pink or red in hue.

Oleandra subdimorpha is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in full sun.
