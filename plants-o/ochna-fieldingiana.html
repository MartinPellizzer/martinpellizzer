Ochna fieldingiana

Ochna fieldingiana, commonly known as the African firethorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and bright yellow flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible for birds and some mammals. Ochna fieldingiana thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

Its ornamental value is due to its vibrant foliage and unique flowering display.
