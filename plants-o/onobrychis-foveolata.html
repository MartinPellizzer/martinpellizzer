Onobrychis foveolata

Onobrychis foveolata, commonly known as the small-cupped vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, hairy pods that contain several elliptical seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Onobrychis foveolata is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability and use in ecological restoration projects.
