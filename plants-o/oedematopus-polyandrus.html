Oedematopus polyandrus

Oedematopus polyandrus, commonly known as the African water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in diameter, providing a habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the early morning and close by midday, a behavior known as nyctinasty. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize the sediment in shallow water bodies.

Oedematopus polyandrus plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by supporting biodiversity and improving water quality.
