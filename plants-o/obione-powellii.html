Obione powellii

Obione powellii, commonly known as Powell's obione, is a species of flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

The flowers bloom in the summer, producing small yellow blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
