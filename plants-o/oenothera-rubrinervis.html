Oenothera rubrinervis

Oenothera rubrinervis, commonly known as the red-veined evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening. The plant has hairy, linear leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Oenothera rubrinervis is part of the Onagraceae family, which includes other evening primroses and related species.
