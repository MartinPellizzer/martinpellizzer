Oldenlandia caespitosa

Oldenlandia caespitosa, commonly known as the prostrate spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that forms dense mats of foliage, often found in disturbed soils and open areas. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, narrow, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.
