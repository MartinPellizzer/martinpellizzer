Ophiorrhiza rarior

Ophiorrhiza rarior, commonly known as the rare mangrove shrub, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. This species thrives in saline and brackish water environments, typically found in mangrove forests. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Ophiorrhiza rarior plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
