Ocotea arenaensis

Ocotea arenaensis, commonly known as the coastal ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant produces elliptical, woody fruits that contain a single seed. Ocotea arenaensis thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found in secondary forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and conservation efforts.
