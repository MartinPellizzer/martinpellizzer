Ononis thomsonii subsp. semiglabra

Ononis thomsonii subsp.

semiglabra, commonly known as Thomson's whitlow grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows, rocky slopes, and woodland edges.

The plant features slender, ascending stems that are often covered with short, soft hairs. Its small, white to pale pink flowers are borne in clusters and bloom in early summer.

The leaves are linear to lanceolate, with a rough texture, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils.
