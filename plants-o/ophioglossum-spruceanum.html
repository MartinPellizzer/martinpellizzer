Ophioglossum spruceanum

Ophioglossum spruceanum, commonly known as the giant snake plant, is a rare and ancient fern species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, strap-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in length, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces sporangia in specialized structures called sporangium clusters, which are typically found at the base of the leaves. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Ophioglossum spruceanum is considered a conservation concern in many regions.
