Oenothera drummondii var. helleriana

Oenothera drummondii var. helleriana, commonly known as Heller's evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening and close by morning. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, particularly in desert and chaparral regions.

The species is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
