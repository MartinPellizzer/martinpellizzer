Ormosia brasseuriana

Ormosia brasseuriana, commonly known as the African tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers. The tree produces woody pods containing edible seeds that are used in traditional medicine and as a food source. Its deep root system helps prevent soil erosion, making it valuable in reforestation efforts.

Ormosia brasseuriana is also noted for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
