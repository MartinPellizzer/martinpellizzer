Oxytheca insignis

Oxytheca insignis, commonly known as the bluebell of the desert, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Its root system is deep and drought-tolerant, allowing it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Oxytheca insignis plays an important role in stabilizing sandy soils and supporting local biodiversity.
