Oxytropis diversifolia

Oxytropis diversifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its tall, branched stems and compound leaves with a broad, ovate shape. The plant produces small, yellow to cream-colored flowers arranged in dense, elongated clusters. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Oxytropis diversifolia is known for its deep root system, which helps it survive in arid environments.
