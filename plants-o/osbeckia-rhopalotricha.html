Osbeckia rhopalotricha

Osbeckia rhopalotricha, commonly known as the woolly willow, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect stem and dense, woolly foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal spikes. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs.

Osbeckia rhopalotricha is often found in moist, sandy soils and is adapted to thrive in temperate climates.
