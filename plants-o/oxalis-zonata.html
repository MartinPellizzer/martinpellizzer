Oxalis zonata

Oxalis zonata, commonly known as the striped wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive white or yellow markings, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Oxalis zonata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a bedding plant.
