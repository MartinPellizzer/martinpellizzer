Ossaea trichopoda

Ossaea trichopoda, commonly known as the mountain rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky and arid environments. The plant features small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, and its leaves are often covered in a fine, white, waxy coating. Ossaea trichopoda is characterized by its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
