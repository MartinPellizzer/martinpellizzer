Oenothera sinuosa

Oenothera sinuosa, commonly known as the zigzagged evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive sinuate margin. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats like prairies and disturbed areas.

Oenothera sinuosa is a member of the Onagraceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological significance.
