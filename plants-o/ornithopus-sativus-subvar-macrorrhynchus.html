Ornithopus sativus subvar. macrorrhynchus

Ornithopus sativus subvar. macrorrhynchus, commonly known as the tall sea rocket, is a perennial flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has feathery, pinnate leaves that are adapted to withstand salty sea air and coastal winds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dunes and rocky shores.

Ornithopus sativus subvar. macrorrhynchus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal habitats.
