Ophrys Garganica Subsp. Majellensis

Ophrys garganica subsp. majellensis is a rare and distinctive orchid native to the southern Apennines of Italy. This orchid is known for its intricate floral structure and mimicry of female wasps, a strategy that aids in pollination. It thrives in limestone-rich soils and is often found in Mediterranean shrublands and rocky areas. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species. Conservation efforts are crucial to protect this unique plant from environmental threats.
