Ophrys × plorae

Ophrys × plorae, commonly known as the orchid bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is characterized by its intricate, bee-mimicking flowers that play a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in Mediterranean regions, thriving in sunny, well-drained soils. Its flowers feature a complex structure with a labellum that resembles a female bee, attracting male bees for pollination.

This species exemplifies the evolutionary adaptations seen in orchids, particularly in the development of mimicry for reproductive success.
