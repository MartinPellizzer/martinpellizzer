Ophrys marzensis

Ophrys marzensis, commonly known as the Marzen orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which includes a lip that mimics the appearance of a female bee, aiding in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive, bee-like labellum.

Ophrys marzensis is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its specialized adaptations in mimicry and pollination.
