Orchis olocheilos var. osmanica

Orchis olocheilos var. osmanica, commonly known as the Osman orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to specific regions in the Balkans.

It is characterized by its distinctive violet to purple flowers with intricate patterns, often featuring a prominent lip. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodland habitats, preferring well-drained, calcareous soils. Its morphology includes tall, erect stems with few leaves, and it blooms during late spring to early summer.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
