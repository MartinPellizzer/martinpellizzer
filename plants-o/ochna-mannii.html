Ochna mannii

Ochna mannii, commonly known as the African yellowwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by orange-red berries containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest margins and rocky areas.

Ochna mannii is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
