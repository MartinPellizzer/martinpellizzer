Oligochaetochilus commutatus

Oligochaetochilus commutatus, commonly known as the commutated oligochaetochilus, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in a raceme. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
