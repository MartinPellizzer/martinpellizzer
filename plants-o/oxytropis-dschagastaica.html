Oxytropis dschagastaica

Oxytropis dschagastaica, commonly known as the Mongolian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in steppes and rocky soils. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow or greenish-yellow flowers in umbel-like clusters. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Oxytropis dschagastaica is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for grazing animals.
