Oryza sativa var. zeravschanica

Oryza sativa var. zeravschanica, commonly known as the Zeravshan rice, is a rare and ancient variety of rice native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small grains and unique genetic makeup, which distinguishes it from other rice species. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and requires a specific combination of soil and water conditions for optimal growth. Its botanical features include a slender stem, lanceolate leaves, and panicles that bear small, spikelet-like flowers.

This variety is of interest to botanists and agricultural researchers due to its potential for genetic diversity and adaptation to changing environmental conditions.
