Osmunda leptophylla

Osmunda leptophylla, commonly known as the slender fern, is a species of royal fern native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender fronds that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant has a complex rhizome system that allows it to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its spores are produced in spherical, clustered structures called sorus, which are located on the underside of the fronds.

Osmunda leptophylla plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for various organisms.
