Ornithogalum princeps

Ornithogalum princeps, commonly known as the white star lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a rhizomatous root system and produces clusters of bell-shaped, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Ornithogalum princeps is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
