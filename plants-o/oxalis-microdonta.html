Oxalis microdonta

Oxalis microdonta, commonly known as the small-flowered wood sorrel, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive pattern of veins and small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Oxalis microdonta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
