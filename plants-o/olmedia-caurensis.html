Olmedia caurensis

Olmedia caurensis, commonly known as the Cauerns Island olive.

It is a small evergreen shrub native to the Canary Islands, characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that ripen to a dark purple color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and rocky environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
