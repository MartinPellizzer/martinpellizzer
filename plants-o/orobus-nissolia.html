Orobus nissolia

Orobus nissolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stem and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, greenish-white flowers with prominent yellow sepals and petals. It thrives in moist, wooded habitats and is often found in shaded, humid environments.

Orobus nissolia is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
