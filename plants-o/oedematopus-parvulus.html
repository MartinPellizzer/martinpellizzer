Oedematopus parvulus

Oedematopus parvulus, commonly known as the small bladder grass, is a small, herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open, grassy areas and is characterized by its compact stature and dense rosette of fleshy, succulent leaves. The leaves are often swollen at the base, giving the plant its common name, and they are covered in fine hairs. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish or reddish in color.

This plant is adapted to arid conditions and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
