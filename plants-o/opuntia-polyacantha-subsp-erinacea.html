Opuntia polyacantha subsp. erinacea

Opuntia polyacantha subsp.

erinacea, commonly known as the teddy bear cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico. It is characterized by its large, rounded, and flattened stems that are often covered in dense clusters of spines.

The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, followed by edible, fleshy fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and succulent collections.
