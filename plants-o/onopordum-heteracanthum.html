Onopordum heteracanthum

Onopordum heteracanthum, commonly known as the rough thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Its structure and appearance make it both a notable ornamental plant and a potential weed in certain environments.
