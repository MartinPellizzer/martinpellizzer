Olea posua

Olea posua, commonly known as the African olive, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Olea posua thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
