Oxalis flava var. fabifolia

Oxalis flava var. fabifolia, commonly known as the yellow wood sorrel, is a species of flowering plant in the family Oxalidaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each consisting of three heart-shaped leaflets, and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is native to parts of North America, particularly the southeastern United States, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. It has a shallow root system and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
