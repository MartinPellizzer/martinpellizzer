Ophrys omegaifera subsp. israelitica

Ophrys omegaifera subsp.

israelitica, commonly known as the Israeli bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to Israel. It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils in arid or semi-arid environments. It has slender, hairy stems and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive bee-like shape.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
