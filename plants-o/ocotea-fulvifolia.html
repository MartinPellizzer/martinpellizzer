Ocotea fulvifolia

Ocotea fulvifolia, commonly known as the yellow-laurel or golden-laurel, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, round, and turn from yellow to dark purple as they mature.

Ocotea fulvifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
