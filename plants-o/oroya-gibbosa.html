Oroya gibbosa

Oroya gibbosa, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments, often growing in rocky or mountainous terrain. Its leaves are typically broad and ovate, with a slightly hairy texture.

Oroya gibbosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
