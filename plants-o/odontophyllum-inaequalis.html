Odontophyllum inaequalis

Odontophyllum inaequalis, commonly known as the unequal-stemmed orchid, is a species of flowering plant in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly found in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically white or pale green, with a distinctive lip that is often notched or irregular in shape.

Odontophyllum inaequalis prefers shaded, humid environments and is often found growing in forest understories.
