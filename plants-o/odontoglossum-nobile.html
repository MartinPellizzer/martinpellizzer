Odontoglossum nobile

Odontoglossum nobile, commonly known as the noble orchid, is a species native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking, showy flowers with a complex structure typical of the Orchidaceae family. The plant features a pseudobulb, which serves as a storage organ for water and nutrients. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish with intricate patterns, and it blooms during the cooler months.

Odontoglossum nobile is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and adaptability to cultivation.
