Oileus albus

Oileus albus, commonly known as the white oil plant, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its showy, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
