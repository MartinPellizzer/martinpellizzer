Ocotea brevipes

Ocotea brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark-brown bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically white or pale yellow, and it produces small, dark blue berries.

Ocotea brevipes is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
