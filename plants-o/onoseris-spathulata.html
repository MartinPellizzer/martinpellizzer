Onoseris spathulata

Onoseris spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leaved goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
