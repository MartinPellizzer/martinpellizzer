Orobanche epithymum var. superba

Orobanche epithymum var. superba, commonly known as the giant broomrape, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is characterized by its lack of leaves and its specialized root system for attaching to host roots. The plant produces showy, often brightly colored flowers that can range from yellow to red or purple. It is typically found in arid or semi-arid regions where it parasitizes various herbaceous plants.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can significantly impact the growth and survival of its host species.
