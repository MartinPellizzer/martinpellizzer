Ocotea trianae

Ocotea trianae, commonly known as the Trianas ocotea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Venezuela and Colombia. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed, and it often grows in humid, lowland forest environments.

Ocotea trianae is valued for its ecological role and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
