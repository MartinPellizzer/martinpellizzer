Orthosiphon albiflorus

Orthosiphon albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered horsegram, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom during the rainy season. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

It is often cultivated for its medicinal properties and as a forage crop.
