Odontoglossum maxillare

Odontoglossum maxillare, commonly known as the violet orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its sympodial growth habit and pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers with a distinctive lip that is often adorned with intricate patterns. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and unique floral structure.
