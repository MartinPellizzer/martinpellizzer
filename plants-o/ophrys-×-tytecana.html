Ophrys × tytecana

Ophrys × tytecana, commonly known as the Tytheca Orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its complex inflorescence, which consists of a dense cluster of flowers arranged in a spike. The plant produces showy, lip-like petals that mimic the appearance of female insects, a feature that plays a key role in its pollination strategy. It thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in woodland and grassland habitats.

The species is notable for its intricate floral morphology and its importance in the study of orchid hybridization.
