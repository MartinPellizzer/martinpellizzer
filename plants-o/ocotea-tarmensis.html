Ocotea tarmensis

Ocotea tarmensis, commonly known as the Tarmesia sandalwood, is a member of the Lauraceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in humid, lowland forests. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in white or pale yellow. Its wood is valued for its fragrant properties and is used in traditional crafts and as a source of essential oils.

The species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting a variety of wildlife and contributing to forest biodiversity.
