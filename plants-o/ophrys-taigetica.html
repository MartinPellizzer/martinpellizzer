Ophrys taigetica

Ophrys taigetica, commonly known as the Taigetian Orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a lip that resembles a female face, a trait known as pseudocopulation, which aids in pollination by bees. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brownish in color, with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of insects.

Ophrys taigetica is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems as a food source for certain insect species.
