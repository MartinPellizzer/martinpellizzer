Obione papillata

Obione papillata, commonly known as the spiky sea anemone, is a marine invertebrate belonging to the order Actinioidea.

It is characterized by its translucent, cylindrical body covered in small, fleshy papillae that give it a distinctive appearance. The organism possesses a central oral disc surrounded by tentacles, which are used for capturing prey. Obione papillata is typically found in shallow coastal waters, where it attaches itself to rocky substrates.

Its vibrant colors and unique morphology make it a notable species in marine botany and ecology.
