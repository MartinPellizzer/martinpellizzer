Ovieda fragrans

Ovieda fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant ovieda, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, aromatic flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface and a woody stem that can reach up to two meters in height. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a sweet, spicy scent that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ovieda fragrans is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and pleasant fragrance.
