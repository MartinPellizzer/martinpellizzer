Oxalis capitata

Oxalis capitata, commonly known as the buttercup oxalis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that resemble miniature buttercups. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters and prefers well-drained soil in a sunny to partially shaded location. Oxalis capitata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and long blooming period.

It is also known for its ability to thrive in containers, making it a popular choice for gardens and indoor spaces.
