Odontoglossum tetraplasium

Odontoglossum tetraplasium, commonly known as the four-leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in high-altitude cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped, with a waxy surface that helps retain moisture.

Due to its rarity and beauty, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and is often cultivated in specialized horticultural settings.
