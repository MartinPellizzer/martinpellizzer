Ophrys × mazzolana

Ophrys × mazzolana, commonly known as the Mazzola bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking floral structure, which closely resembles a female bee, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant produces showy, bee-like flowers with intricate patterns and a strong scent that mimic the appearance and odor of its pollinators. It typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in temperate climates with adequate moisture.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Ophrys × mazzolana is considered a conservation concern in many regions.
