Orchis maculata var. saccifera

Orchis maculata var. saccifera, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces a tall, erect flowering stem with a cluster of pale yellow to greenish flowers. Its roots are often tuberous, and the plant is known for its distinctive spotted sepals.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators and is considered a valuable species in ecological studies.
