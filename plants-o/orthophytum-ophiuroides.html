Orthophytum ophiuroides

Orthophytum ophiuroides, commonly known as the golden crown cactus, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, branching structure and dense clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant features thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, which help minimize water loss.

Orthophytum ophiuroides is well-suited for rock gardens or as a container plant due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
