Oenothera flaemingina

Oenothera flaemingina, commonly known as the Flamingo flower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking pink to red floral displays, which bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are composed of four to five petals and are pollinated primarily by moths and other nocturnal insects.

Oenothera flaemingina is often found in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and woodland edges.
