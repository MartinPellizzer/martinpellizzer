Oenothera cardiophylla var. typica

Oenothera cardiophylla var. typica, commonly known as the heart-leaf evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive heart-shaped base, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
