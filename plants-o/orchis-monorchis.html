Orchis monorchis

Orchis monorchis, commonly known as the singlehood orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in woodland and grassland habitats. The plant produces a single, showy flower with a distinctive violet or purple coloration. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption from the soil.

The species is notable for its unique floral structure and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
