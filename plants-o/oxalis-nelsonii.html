Oxalis nelsonii

Oxalis nelsonii, commonly known as the yellow woodsorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with three heart-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of five-petaled yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers. Its foliage and flowers are often used in ornamental gardening for their attractive, delicate appearance.

Oxalis nelsonii is also known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, forming dense mats in suitable conditions.
