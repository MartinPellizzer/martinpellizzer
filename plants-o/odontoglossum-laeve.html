Odontoglossum laeve

Odontoglossum laeve, commonly known as the smooth-leafed orchid, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its lanceolate, glabrous leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces showy, spurred orchid flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, typically during the spring and early summer. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil for optimal growth.

Odontoglossum laeve is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a greenhouse or shaded garden setting.
