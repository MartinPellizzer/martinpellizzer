Ochna ovata

Ochna ovata, commonly known as the oval ochna, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate in shape and often have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by distinctive, fleshy, orange-red berries. Ochna ovata thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
