Oxalis cratensis

Oxalis cratensis, commonly known as the narrowleaf wood sorrel, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It features trifoliate leaves with three heart-shaped leaflets, and small, delicate flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and produces capsule fruits containing several seeds.

Oxalis cratensis is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
