Ophrys × maremmae nothosubsp. loutanensis

Ophrys × maremmae nothosubsp.

loutanensis, commonly known as the Maremma orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the genus Ophrys, which is known for its mimicry of female insects to attract male pollinators.

This particular subspecies exhibits unique floral morphology with intricate patterns that resemble insect wings, enhancing its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a hybrid, showcasing the genetic diversity and adaptability of orchids in the wild.
