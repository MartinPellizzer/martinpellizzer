Ophrys romolinii

Ophrys romolinii, commonly known as the Romolini bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which closely resembles a female bee, a mimicry strategy used to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brown in color, with intricate patterns that aid in its deceptive pollination mechanism.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Ophrys romolinii is considered a vulnerable species.
