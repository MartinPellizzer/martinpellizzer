Oncidium pittieri

Oncidium pittieri, commonly known as the pink lady orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous inflorescences that bear clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in varying light conditions.

Oncidium pittieri is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its attractive blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
