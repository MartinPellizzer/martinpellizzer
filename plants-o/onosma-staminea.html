Onosma staminea

Onosma staminea, commonly known as the Persian chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and calming properties.

Its essential oils are also used in aromatherapy and as a natural remedy for digestive issues.
