Ocotea gardneri

Ocotea gardneri, commonly known as the Caribbean lauraceae, is a member of the Lauraceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and woody structure.

It is native to the Caribbean, particularly found in regions of Cuba, Hispaniola, and the Bahamas. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, often reaching heights of up to 15 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy surface with a strong, spicy scent.

The flowers are small and clustered, and the fruit is a dark, fleshy drupe that plays a role in seed dispersal by animals.
