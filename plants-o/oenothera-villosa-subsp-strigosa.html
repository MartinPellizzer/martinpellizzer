Oenothera villosa subsp. strigosa

Oenothera villosa subsp.

strigosa, commonly known as the woolly evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its species name "villosa.".

The plant produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in dry, open habitats. Oenothera villosa subsp.
