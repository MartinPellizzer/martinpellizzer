Orchis latifolia var. abbreviata

Orchis latifolia var. abbreviata, commonly known as the short-stemmed broad-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or meadow habitats. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces tall, erect flowering stems with clusters of violet or purple flowers. Its flowers are characterized by a distinctive lip that is shorter than the standard in this variety.

This orchid is part of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
