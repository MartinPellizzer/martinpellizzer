Ornithogalum pentheri

Ornithogalum pentheri, commonly known as the star of Bethlehem, is a bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical significance in various cultures.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
