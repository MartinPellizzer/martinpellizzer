Ocotea calliscypha

Ocotea calliscypha, commonly known as the yellow-leafed ocotea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that have a distinctive yellowish underside, giving it its common name. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by oval, dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Ocotea calliscypha is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional crafts and as a source of essential oils.
