Oenothera cespitosa var. jonesii

Oenothera cespitosa var. jonesii, commonly known as the Jones' evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland and shrubland habitats.

This variant is distinguished by its specific geographic distribution and floral characteristics compared to other forms of Oenothera cespitosa.
