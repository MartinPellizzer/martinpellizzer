Olea oblonga

Olea oblonga, commonly known as the narrow-leaf olive, is a species of flowering plant in the Oleaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and parts of southern Europe. The plant is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Olea oblonga produces small, spherical fruits that are typically green when mature.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long history of use in traditional medicine.
