Orsinia eupatoria

Orsinia eupatoria, commonly known as the common tansy, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. The plant features a branched stem covered in fine hairs and produces yellow flowers arranged in composite heads. Its leaves are pinnately divided and have a distinctive, slightly aromatic scent.

Orsinia eupatoria is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in herbal remedies.
