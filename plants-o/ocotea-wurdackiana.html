Ocotea wurdackiana

Ocotea wurdackiana, commonly known as the Wurdack's ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and can grow up to 30 meters tall. Its flowers are small and white, clustered in panicles, and it produces dark, aromatic berries.

The tree is valued for its essential oils and has ecological significance in its native habitats.
