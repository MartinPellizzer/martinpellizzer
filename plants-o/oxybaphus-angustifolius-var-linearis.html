Oxybaphus angustifolius var. linearis

Oxybaphus angustifolius var. linearis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved oxybaphus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a valuable addition to native plant collections.
