Orchis variegata

Orchis variegata, commonly known as the variable orchid, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and striking, irregularly shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive veined pattern on the petals.

Orchis variegata is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
