Onobrychis esponellae

Onobrychis esponellae, commonly known as the Canary Island burclover, is a perennial legume native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces cylindrical, hairy pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Onobrychis esponellae is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
