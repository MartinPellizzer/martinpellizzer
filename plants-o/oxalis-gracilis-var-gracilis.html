Oxalis gracilis var. gracilis

Oxalis gracilis var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped lobes and small, delicate white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for shade gardens and as a ground cover.
