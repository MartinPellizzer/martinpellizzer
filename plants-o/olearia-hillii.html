Olearia hillii

Olearia hillii, commonly known as the Stewart Island daisy, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal and forested areas, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its showy, white to pale yellow flowers bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Olearia hillii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
