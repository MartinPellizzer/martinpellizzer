Onosma arenaria subsp. arenaria

Onosma arenaria subsp.

arenaria, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a low-growing annual or biennial herb with small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is adapted to sandy or rocky soils. It is often found in coastal areas and dry habitats.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
