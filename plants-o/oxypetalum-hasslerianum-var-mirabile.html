Oxypetalum hasslerianum var. mirabile

Oxypetalum hasslerianum var. mirabile, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its botanical characteristics include a tall, upright growth habit and a distinctive cone-shaped flower cluster.
