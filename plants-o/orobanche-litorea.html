Orobanche litorea

Orobanche litorea, commonly known as the coastal broom.

It is a parasitic plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies on host plants for nutrients. This species typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, adapting to harsh maritime conditions. Its flowers are small and yellowish, often clustered in dense inflorescences.

Orobanche litorea plays a role in coastal ecosystems, though its parasitic nature can impact native plant communities.
