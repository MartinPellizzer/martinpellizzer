Odontoglossum × andersonianum var. splendens

Odontoglossum × andersonianum var. splendens, commonly known as the splendens orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically come in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in high-altitude environments. Its leaves are strap-like and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is prized by orchid growers for its ornamental value and adaptability to cultivated conditions.
