Ophrys tenthredinifera subvar. choffatii

Ophrys tenthredinifera subvar. choffatii, commonly known as the Choffat's bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to specific regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a wasp, a feature evolved to attract male wasps for pollination. The plant produces a single, strikingly colored flower with intricate patterns that serve as a visual mimicry of its pollinators. It thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.
