Ocotea maranguapensis

Ocotea maranguapensis, commonly known as the Brazilian camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by oval-shaped, woody fruits containing a single seed. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils, which have been traditionally used for their medicinal and aromatic properties.

The species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
