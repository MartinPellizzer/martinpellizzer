Oxytropis nigrescens subsp. pygmaea

Oxytropis nigrescens subsp.

pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf locoweed, is a low-growing perennial legume native to the western United States. It is characterized by its small, hairy stems and clusters of small, typically purple or pink flowers.

The plant produces pods that contain several elliptical seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Oxytropis nigrescens subsp.

pygmaea is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland ecosystems.
