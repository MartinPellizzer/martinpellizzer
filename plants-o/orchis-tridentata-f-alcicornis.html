Orchis tridentata f. alcicornis

Orchis tridentata f. alcicornis, commonly known as the three-lobed orchid, is a rare and distinctive orchid species native to specific regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure, which includes three prominent, recurved lobes. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or grassland habitats. Its flowers are usually a mix of white, green, and brown, with a distinctive scent that aids in pollination.

This subspecies is of particular interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and specialized ecological requirements.
