Oberonia heliophila

Oberonia heliophila, commonly known as the sun-loving orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows on trees in humid, shaded forest environments. The plant features slender, wiry stems and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers clustered in spikes. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern.

Oberonia heliophila is noted for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions while still requiring some sunlight for optimal growth.
