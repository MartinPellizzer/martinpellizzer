Orchis pardalina

Orchis pardalina, commonly known as the red-brown orchid, is a species of orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of violet or purple flowers with a distinctive red-brown hue. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodlands and meadows. Its flowers have a complex structure with three sepals and three petals, with the lip being prominently marked.

Orchis pardalina is a perennial herb that blooms in late spring to early summer.
