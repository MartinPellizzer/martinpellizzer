Oenothera tubicula var. demissa

Oenothera tubicula var. demissa, commonly known as the slender evening primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features slender, branched stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its flowers have tubular corollas that split into five lobes, typical of the Onagraceae family.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
