Orobus occidentalis var. grandifolius

Orobus occidentalis var. grandifolius, commonly known as the large-leaf orobus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats. Its botanical features include showy bracts that surround the small, tubular flowers, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
