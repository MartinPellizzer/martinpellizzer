Ocimum centraliafricanum

Ocimum centraliafricanum, commonly known as African basil, is a species of flowering plant in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is native to Central Africa and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with square stems and opposite leaves. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Ocimum centraliafricanum is valued for its essential oils, which contribute to its distinctive scent and potential therapeutic properties.
