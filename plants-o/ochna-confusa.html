Ochna confusa

Ochna confusa, commonly known as the African hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture.

Ochna confusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
