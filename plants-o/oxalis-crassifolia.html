Oxalis crassifolia

Oxalis crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each leaflet being broad and heart-shaped. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Oxalis crassifolia is often used in native plant gardens due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.
