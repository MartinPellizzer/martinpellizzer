Ocotea pittieri

Ocotea pittieri, commonly known as the white ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a glossy surface with a distinct aromatic scent.

The fruit is a small, dark blue drupe that contains a single seed, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
