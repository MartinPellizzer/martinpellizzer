Oxytropis campestris var. cusickii

Oxytropis campestris var. cusickii, commonly known as the Cusick's locoweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink to purple in color.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often avoided by grazing animals.
