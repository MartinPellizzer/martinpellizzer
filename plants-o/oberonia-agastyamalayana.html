Oberonia agastyamalayana

Oberonia agastyamalayana, commonly known as the Malayan orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to the mountainous regions of Peninsular Malaysia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a distinctive appearance, featuring slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow with a faintly fragrant aroma. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in primary lowland rainforests.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
