Orchis angustifolia subf. strictior

Orchis angustifolia subf.

strictior, commonly known as the narrow-leaved twayblade, is a small terrestrial orchid with a distinctive appearance. It features a slender, grass-like stem that typically grows to a height of around 20-40 centimeters.

The plant produces a single, narrow lance-shaped leaf and a spike of pale yellow or greenish-white flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and damp meadows.

This orchid is known for its specialized mycorrhizal relationships, which are essential for its nutrient uptake.
