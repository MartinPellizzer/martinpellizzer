Onobrychis aurantiaca

Onobrychis aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden burr or sweet vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces distinctive, spiny pods that contain small, edible seeds. It is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in dry, rocky soils.

Onobrychis aurantiaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
