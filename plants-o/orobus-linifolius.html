Orobus linifolius

Orobus linifolius, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer, and its seeds are enclosed in pods. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats.

Orobus linifolius plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
