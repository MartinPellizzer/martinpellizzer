Oxyacantha howellii

Oxyacantha howellii, commonly known as Howell's spindle tree, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, spindly growth habit and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in winter, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.

Oxyacantha howellii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
