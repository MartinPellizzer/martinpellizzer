Olea regia

Olea regia, commonly known as the royal olive, is a species of flowering plant in the Oleaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in warm, sunny climates. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a silvery underside, adding to its ornamental appeal.

The fruit, a small drupe, contains a single seed and is used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some cultures.
