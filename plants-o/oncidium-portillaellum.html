Oncidium portillaellum

Oncidium portillaellum, commonly known as the Portilla orchid, is a species native to the humid regions of Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous plant with a sympodial growth habit, characterized by its pseudobulbs and fleshy leaves. The plant produces striking, pendulous inflorescences with numerous flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Oncidium portillaellum thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
