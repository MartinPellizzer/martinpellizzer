Ormosia minor

Ormosia minor, commonly known as the dwarf ormosia, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows to a height of about 3 to 5 meters and has bipinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, and it produces small, woody pods that contain several seeds.

Ormosia minor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its dense foliage and shade-providing qualities.
