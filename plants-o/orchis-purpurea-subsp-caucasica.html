Orchis purpurea subsp. caucasica

Orchis purpurea subsp.

caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian purple orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and a single, showy purple flower with a distinctive lip.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and rocky areas. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, and it produces a single, erect flower spike.

The species is notable for its intricate floral structure and its role in local ecosystems.
