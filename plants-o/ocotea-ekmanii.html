Ocotea ekmanii

Ocotea ekmanii, commonly known as the Ecuadorian ocotea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves with a glossy upper surface and a lighter underside. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by dark blue-black berries.

Ocotea ekmanii is valued for its aromatic wood and is often found in shaded, moist forest environments.
