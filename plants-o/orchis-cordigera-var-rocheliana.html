Orchis cordigera var. rocheliana

Orchis cordigera var. rocheliana, commonly known as the Rochel's narrow-leaf orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers arranged in a loose spike, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in many regions.
