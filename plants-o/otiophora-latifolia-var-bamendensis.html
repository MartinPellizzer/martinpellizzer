Otiophora latifolia var. bamendensis

Otiophora latifolia var. bamendensis, commonly known as the Bamenda broadleaf, is a rare and endemic plant species native to the Cameroon highlands.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy in appearance. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom during the rainy season, followed by the development of capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in montane forests at elevations between 1,500 and 2,500 meters.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species facing threats from deforestation and climate change.
