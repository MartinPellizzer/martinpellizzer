Origanum vetteri

Origanum vetteri, commonly known as Vetter's oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes. Its essential oils, rich in compounds like carvacrol and thymol, contribute to its strong fragrance and medicinal properties.

Origanum vetteri is often used in culinary applications and traditional herbal remedies due to its aromatic and antimicrobial qualities.
