Operculina platyphylla

Operculina platyphylla, commonly known as the African butterbean, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces distinctive, fleshy, yellow-orange fruits that resemble small beans. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its flowers are typically white or pale yellow with a tubular shape. Operculina platyphylla is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
