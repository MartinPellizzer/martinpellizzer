Oxalis oregana f. smalliana

Oxalis oregana f. smalliana, commonly known as the small-leaf wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with small, heart-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of yellow flowers with five petals. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its foliage is often covered in fine, silky hairs, and it has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil.

Oxalis oregana f. smalliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
