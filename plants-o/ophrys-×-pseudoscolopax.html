Ophrys × pseudoscolopax

Ophrys × pseudoscolopax, commonly known as the mimic orchid, is a hybrid species within the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, bee-mimicking flowers that feature intricate patterns and colors resembling female bees. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers are highly specialized, with a labellum that plays a key role in pollination by attracting male bees.

Botanically, it exhibits a complex genetic structure due to its hybrid origin, making it a subject of interest in both horticulture and evolutionary studies.
