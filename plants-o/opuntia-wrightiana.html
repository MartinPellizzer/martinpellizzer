Opuntia wrightiana

Opuntia wrightiana, commonly known as the Wright's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, spineless pads and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems. Its fruit, known as a tuna, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

Opuntia wrightiana plays an important role in its ecosystem, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
