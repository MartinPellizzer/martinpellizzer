Ornithogalum ramosum

Ornithogalum ramosum, commonly known as the star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a short, rhizomatous stem and produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers during the spring and summer months. The plant has lance-shaped, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Ornithogalum ramosum is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its delicate floral display.
