Oryza sativa var. ochracea

Oryza sativa var. ochracea, commonly known as the ochraceous rice, is a variant of rice characterized by its unique ochraceous (rust-colored) grains.

This variety is native to Southeast Asia and is typically cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has a short stature with erect, slender spikes that bear small, pale yellow flowers. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a grass-like structure with narrow, linear leaves.

The grains are generally smaller and have a higher protein content compared to other rice varieties.
