Oncidium macrobulbon

Oncidium macrobulbon, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 inches in diameter, making it a striking ornamental plant. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually yellow with red or orange markings, and they bloom in clusters.

Oncidium macrobulbon requires bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.
