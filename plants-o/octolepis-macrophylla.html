Octolepis macrophylla

Octolepis macrophylla, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad, floating leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, providing a unique habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant's roots are submerged, anchoring it in the muddy bottom of slow-moving or still water bodies. Its flowers, which are typically pink or white, bloom above the water surface and are pollinated by insects.

Octolepis macrophylla plays an important ecological role in maintaining water quality and supporting biodiversity in its native ecosystems.
