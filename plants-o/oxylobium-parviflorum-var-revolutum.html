Oxylobium parviflorum var. revolutum

Oxylobium parviflorum var. revolutum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved oxylobium, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its small, delicate white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has slender, wiry stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils in open woodlands and heathlands.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
