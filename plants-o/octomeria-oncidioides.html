Octomeria oncidioides

Octomeria oncidioides, commonly known as the Oncidioides orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features a pseudobulb with several leaves and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange. Its flowers are often arranged in a raceme and bloom during the warmer months.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and resilience.
