Oenone alcicornis

Oenone alcicornis, commonly known as the yellow gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, white pubescence, while its stems are often hairy.

Oenone alcicornis is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
