Oplismenus affinis var. humboldtianus

Oplismenus affinis var. humboldtianus, commonly known as the Humboldt's grass, is a species of flowering plant in the family Poaceae.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes. It is native to South America, particularly regions of Brazil and Argentina.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens with proper moisture conditions.
