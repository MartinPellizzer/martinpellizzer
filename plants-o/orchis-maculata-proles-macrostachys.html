Orchis maculata proles macrostachys

Orchis maculata proles macrostachys, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive purple and white spotted flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption.

The species is known for its ecological importance, supporting various pollinators and serving as an indicator of healthy, undisturbed habitats.
