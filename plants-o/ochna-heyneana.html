Ochna heyneana

Ochna heyneana, commonly known as the yellow ochna, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and opposite, simple, elliptical leaves that are usually glossy green. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers with five petals, followed by orange-red, fleshy berries that contain several seeds. Ochna heyneana is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and showy flowers.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical gardens.
