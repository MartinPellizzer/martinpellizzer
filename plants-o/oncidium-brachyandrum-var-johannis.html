Oncidium brachyandrum var. johannis

Oncidium brachyandrum var. johannis, commonly known as the Johannis orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers with a distinctive red lip. The plant typically blooms during the summer months and requires bright, indirect light to thrive. It is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for both novice and experienced orchid growers.

Its botanical features include pseudobulbs and strap-like leaves, typical of many orchid species.
