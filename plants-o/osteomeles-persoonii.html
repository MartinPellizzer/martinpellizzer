Osteomeles persoonii

Osteomeles persoonii, commonly known as the Persian honeyflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, which bloom in late summer. These flowers are adapted to attract pollinators such as birds and insects.

Osteomeles persoonii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy blooms.
