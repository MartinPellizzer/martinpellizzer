Oxalis modesta

Oxalis modesta, commonly known as the small wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive leaflets and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white, pink, or yellow. The plant thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil. It is often used in woodland gardens for its ornamental value and ability to naturalize easily.

Oxalis modesta is also noted for its ability to spread through runners, making it a versatile groundcover plant.
