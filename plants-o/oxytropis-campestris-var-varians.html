Oxytropis campestris var. varians

Oxytropis campestris var. varians, commonly known as the variable field locoweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a perennial forb characterized by its erect, often branched stems and small, compound leaves. The plant produces yellow to reddish-purple flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is native to the western United States and is often found in dry, disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its toxic properties, it is considered a noxious weed in some regions.
