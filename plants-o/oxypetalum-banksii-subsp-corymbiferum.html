Oxypetalum banksii subsp. corymbiferum

Oxypetalum banksii subsp.

corymbiferum, commonly known as the mountain bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa. It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense corymbs.

The plant has slender, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
