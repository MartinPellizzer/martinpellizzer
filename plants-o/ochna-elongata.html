Ochna elongata

Ochna elongata, commonly known as the elongated ochna, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces elliptical, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Ochna elongata is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils.
