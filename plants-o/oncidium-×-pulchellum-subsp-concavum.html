Oncidium × pulchellum subsp. concavum

Oncidium × pulchellum subsp.

concavum, commonly known as the Concave Oncidium, is a hybrid orchid species native to South America. It is characterized by its pendulous, showy inflorescences that bear numerous fragrant, yellowish-green to brownish flowers.

The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial species, often found in humid tropical forests. Its pseudobulbs are elongated and cylindrical, supporting the plant's growth and storage of water and nutrients.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
