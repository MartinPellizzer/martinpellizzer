Orchiodes bifidum

Orchiodes bifidum, commonly known as the bifid orchid, is a species of orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The flowers typically have three petals, with the lateral ones being bifid, or split, giving the plant its scientific name. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
