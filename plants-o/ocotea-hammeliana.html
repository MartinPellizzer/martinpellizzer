Ocotea hammeliana

Ocotea hammeliana, commonly known as the white ocotea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves, which are glossy and dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces dark blue berries that are edible for some bird species.

The tree is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional crafts and as a source of essential oils.
