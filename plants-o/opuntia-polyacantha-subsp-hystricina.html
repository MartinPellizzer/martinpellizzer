Opuntia polyacantha subsp. hystricina

Opuntia polyacantha subsp.

hystricina, commonly known as the Mexican spineless cactus, is a species native to Mexico. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that are typically ribbed and covered in small, glochids instead of large spines.

The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the summer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit.
