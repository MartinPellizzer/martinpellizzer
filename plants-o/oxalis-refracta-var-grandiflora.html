Oxalis refracta var. grandiflora

Oxalis refracta var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of small, yellow, five-petaled flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a short, rhizomatous stem.

Oxalis refracta var. grandiflora is known for its ornamental value and ability to spread through vegetative propagation.
