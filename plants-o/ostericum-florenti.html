Ostericum florenti

Ostericum florenti, commonly known as the Florentine ostericum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems covered in small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grayish-green in color. The plant produces tiny, fragrant yellow flowers in late spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its historical use in herbal medicine.
