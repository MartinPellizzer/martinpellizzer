Ocotea coriacea

Ocotea coriacea, commonly known as the rough-leaved ocotea, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Venezuela. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, and somewhat rough-textured leaves. It produces small, white flowers that are often clustered together, followed by small, dark blue berries.

Ocotea coriacea is often found in humid, lowland forests and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
