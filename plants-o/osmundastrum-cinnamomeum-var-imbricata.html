Osmundastrum cinnamomeum var. imbricata

Osmundastrum cinnamomeum var. imbricata, commonly known as the cinnamon fern, is a member of the family Osmundaceae and is characterized by its distinctive fronds.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant features a central fiddlehead-like spore-bearing structure called a sorus, which is covered by a protective indusium. Its fronds are compound, with a bright green color and a wavy, serrated margin.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is often used in horticulture due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
