Orobus pseudoclusii

Orobus pseudoclusii, commonly known as the African orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy flowers that bloom in a range of vibrant colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in grassland or woodland areas, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its unique floral structure includes a lip that is often distinctly colored and shaped, aiding in pollination by attracting specific insect pollinators.

Orobus pseudoclusii is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
