Operculina petaloidea

Operculina petaloidea, commonly known as the "flowering operculina," is a striking member of the Convolvulaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces trumpet-shaped blooms in shades of pink and white. Its unique floral structure attracts pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.

Operculina petaloidea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in warm, humid environments.
