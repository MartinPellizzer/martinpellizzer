Ophrys × Heterochila F. Dodekanensis

Dodecanese ophrys, scientifically known as Ophrys × heterochila f. dodekanensis, is a rare and fascinating orchid endemic to the Dodecanese Islands in the eastern Mediterranean. This orchid exhibits remarkable mimicry, resembling the female wasp to attract male pollinators, a strategy known as sexual deception. Its unique floral structure and specialized pollination mechanism make it a subject of great interest in botanical and ecological studies. Despite its beauty and ecological significance, it faces threats from habitat loss and environmental changes. Conservation efforts are crucial to preserving this extraordinary plant for future generations.
