Osteospermum uvedalia

Osteospermum uvedalia, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has silver-green, fuzzy foliage that adds visual interest to gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Osteospermum uvedalia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
