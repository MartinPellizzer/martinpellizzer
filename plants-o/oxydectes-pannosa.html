Oxydectes pannosa

Oxydectes pannosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Oxydectes pannosa is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain areas.
