Oxycladium semiseptatum

Oxycladium semiseptatum, commonly known as the common marsh grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It typically grows in dense clumps with slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered at the ends of the stems. Oxycladium semiseptatum thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in areas with standing water or high humidity.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various aquatic and semi-aquatic organisms.
