Ouratea croizatii

Ouratea croizatii, commonly known as the Brazilian whiteflower, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by round, red berries.
