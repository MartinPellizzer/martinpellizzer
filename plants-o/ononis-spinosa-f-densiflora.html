Ononis spinosa f. densiflora

Ononis spinosa f. densiflora, commonly known as the denseflowered sand verbena, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in coastal or arid environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soils in challenging environments.
