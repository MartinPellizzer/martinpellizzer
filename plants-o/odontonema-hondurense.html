Odontonema hondurense

Odontonema hondurense, commonly known as the Honduras flame flower, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, lance-shaped leaves with a dark green color. The plant produces striking clusters of red-orange flowers that bloom during the warm season, attracting various pollinators. Its botanical characteristics include opposite leaf arrangement and showy inflorescences that contribute to its ornamental value.

Odontonema hondurense thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
