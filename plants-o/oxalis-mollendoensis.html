Oxalis mollendoensis

Oxalis mollendoensis, commonly known as the velvetleaf wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant belongs to the Oxalidaceae family, known for its calcium oxalate crystals in the leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value is appreciated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
