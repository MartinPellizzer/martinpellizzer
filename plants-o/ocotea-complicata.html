Ocotea complicata

Ocotea complicata, commonly known as the zigzag ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, lowland rainforests and can reach heights of up to 30 meters. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and have a distinctive zigzag venation pattern.

The fruit is a small, dark blue drupe that contains a single seed.
