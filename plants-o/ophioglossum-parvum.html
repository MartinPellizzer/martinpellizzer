Ophioglossum parvum

Ophioglossum parvum, commonly known as the small adder's tongue fern, is a rare and ancient fern species belonging to the family Ophioglossaceae.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring a fleshy, creeping rhizome and a single, erect, tongue-shaped leaf. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its leaf, which are clustered in a structure called a sorus. Ophioglossum parvum is typically found in moist, shaded habitats, often in woodland areas.

Due to its limited distribution and specialized ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
