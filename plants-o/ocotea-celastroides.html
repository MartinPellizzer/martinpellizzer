Ocotea celastroides

Ocotea celastroides, commonly known as the Mexican laurel, is a flowering plant species native to Central America and northern South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic trees and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. Its small, white flowers are typically clustered in axillary inflorescences, and it produces small, dark berries as fruit.

Ocotea celastroides is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
