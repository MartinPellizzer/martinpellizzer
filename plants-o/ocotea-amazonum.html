Ocotea amazonum

Ocotea amazonum, commonly known as the Amazonian ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a dense canopy. Its leaves are typically elliptical, glossy, and have a strong, spicy scent.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
