Opuntia utahensis

Opuntia utahensis, commonly known as the Utah prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in spines and glochids, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers in the spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible and often used in traditional cuisine. Opuntia utahensis is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick stems and having a shallow root system to absorb moisture efficiently.

Its structure and growth habits make it a resilient and visually distinctive member of the Cactaceae family.
