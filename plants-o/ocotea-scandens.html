Ocotea scandens

Ocotea scandens, commonly known as the climbing ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a dark blue drupe, often containing a single seed.

Ocotea scandens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
