Ophrys × bergeri

Ophrys × bergeri, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of Europe.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera (the bee orchid) and Ophrys insectifera (the wasp orchid). The plant is characterized by its intricate, bee-like flowers that feature a prominent lip resembling a bee's face, which plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. It typically grows in calcareous soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats.

Ophrys × bergeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
