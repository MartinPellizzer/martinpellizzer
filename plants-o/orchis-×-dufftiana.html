Orchis × dufftiana

Orchis × dufftiana, commonly known as the Dufft's orchid, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Orchis moria and Orchis anthropophora.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe, particularly in countries like Austria and Switzerland. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats with well-drained soils, often in alpine or subalpine zones. Its flowers are showy, with a distinctive violet or purple coloration and a spurred lip that is characteristic of many orchid species.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
