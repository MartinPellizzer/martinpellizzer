Oenothera cardiophylla subsp. robusta

Oenothera cardiophylla subsp.

robusta, commonly known as the robust evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats like grasslands and rocky areas. Oenothera cardiophylla subsp.

robusta is part of the Onagraceae family, which includes other evening primroses and related species.
