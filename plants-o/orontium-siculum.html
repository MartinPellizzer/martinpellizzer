Orontium siculum

Orontium siculum, commonly known as the Italian watercress, is a aquatic plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It thrives in freshwater environments such as streams, ditches, and ponds. The plant has slender, branching stems that are often submerged, with small, serrated leaves that are typically less than 1 inch in length. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and arranged in clusters at the water surface.

Orontium siculum is known for its peppery, aromatic leaves, which are edible and used in various culinary traditions.
