Onopordum polycephalum

Onopordum polycephalum, commonly known as the many-headed thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a tall, spiny stem that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, topped with a dense cluster of purple to pinkish flowers. The plant has large, deeply lobed leaves that are covered in fine hairs and often have a bluish-green color. Its flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.

Onopordum polycephalum is also known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soils.
