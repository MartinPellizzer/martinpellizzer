Orthosiphon tubiformis

Orthosiphon tubiformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved holy basil, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tubular leaves and small, white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is often found in moist, shady environments and thrives in humid climates. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Orthosiphon tubiformis is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
