Octomeria uberiformis

Octomeria uberiformis, commonly known as the woolly octomeris, is a unique flowering plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, almost fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the cooler months. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soil.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and delicate species.
