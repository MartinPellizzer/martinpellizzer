Olbia triloba

Olbia triloba, commonly known as the three-lobed orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive three-lobed leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often with a yellow or green throat. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
