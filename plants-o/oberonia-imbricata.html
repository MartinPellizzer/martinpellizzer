Oberonia imbricata

Oberonia imbricata, commonly known as the overlapping orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, overlapping leaf structure that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in shades of green and white, with a distinctive lip.

Oberonia imbricata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
