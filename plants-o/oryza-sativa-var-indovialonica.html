Oryza sativa var. indovialonica

Oryza sativa var. indovialonica, commonly known as the Indonesian rice variety, is a subspecies of rice characterized by its unique genetic makeup and adaptability to tropical climates.

This variety is native to Southeast Asia and is known for its high yield and resilience to environmental stressors such as drought and salinity. Its botanical features include long, slender grains and a fibrous husk that protects the edible endosperm. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with tillering and panicle development typical of other rice species.

It is primarily cultivated for its grain, which is a staple food in many Asian countries.
