Ostericum atropurpureum

Ostericum atropurpureum, commonly known as purple wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues, especially along the veins. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental plant in native gardens.
