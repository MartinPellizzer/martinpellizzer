Oxyanthera elata

Oxyanthera elata, commonly known as the tall yellow oxalis, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers throughout the growing season. The plant features alternate, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish tint along the veins. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Oxyanthera elata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
