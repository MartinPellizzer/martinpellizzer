Ononis viscosa var. sicula

Ononis viscosa var. sicula, commonly known as Sicilian sandwort, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are linear to lanceolate, often with a hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or rocky habitats.

This variety is closely related to other species in the Ononis genus, sharing similar ecological preferences and morphological traits.
