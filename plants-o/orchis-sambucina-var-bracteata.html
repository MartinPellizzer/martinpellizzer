Orchis sambucina var. bracteata

Orchis sambucina var. bracteata, commonly known as the bracted orchid or bracteate orchis, is a rare and distinctive orchid species native to parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces violet to purple flowers with a distinctive hooded lip, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows, often growing in nutrient-rich soil.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
