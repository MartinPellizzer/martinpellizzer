Orchis latifolia var. lineare

Orchis latifolia var. lineare, commonly known as the linear orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers arranged in a spike, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in many regions.
