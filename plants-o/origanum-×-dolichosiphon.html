Origanum × dolichosiphon

Origanum × dolichosiphon, commonly known as Greek oregano, is a hybrid perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, fuzzy leaves and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. Its essential oils, rich in compounds like carvacrol and thymol, contribute to its strong scent and medicinal properties.

It is widely used in culinary applications and traditional herbal remedies.
