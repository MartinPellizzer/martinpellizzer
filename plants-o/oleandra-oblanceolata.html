Oleandra oblanceolata

Oleandra oblanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf oleander, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has simple, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. Oleandra oblanceolata is evergreen, with a shrub-like growth habit that can reach heights of up to 6 feet.

It is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in full sun and well-drained soil.
