Oenothera concinna

Oenothera concinna, commonly known as the narrowleaf evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening, hence its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open woods.

Oenothera concinna is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for nocturnal pollinators.
