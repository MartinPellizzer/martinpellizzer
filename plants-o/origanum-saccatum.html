Origanum saccatum

Origanum saccatum, commonly known as sand thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, pink or white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its essential oils, rich in thymol and carvacrol, contribute to its use in herbal medicine and culinary applications.

Origanum saccatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
