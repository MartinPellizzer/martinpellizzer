Ophelia purpurascens

Ophelia purpurascens, commonly known as the purple water crowfoot, is a flowering plant native to freshwater habitats in Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in shallow water or damp soils, often near streams, ponds, and lakes. The plant features delicate, floating leaves that are heart-shaped with a waxy surface, and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its rhizomatous root system helps stabilize the soil and prevent erosion in aquatic environments.

Ophelia purpurascens plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for invertebrates and serving as a food source for waterfowl.
