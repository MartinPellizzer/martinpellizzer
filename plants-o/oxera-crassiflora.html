Oxera crassiflora

Oxera crassiflora, commonly known as the thick-leaved oxera, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Oxalidaceae and is characterized by its succulent, thick leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. Oxera crassiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand drought conditions.
