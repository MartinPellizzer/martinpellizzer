Odontoglossum × schlesingerianum

Odontoglossum × schlesingerianum, commonly known as the Schlesingerian orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically feature vibrant purple and white hues, with a distinctive lip that is often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees rather than in soil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its botanical significance lies in its role as a parent in the development of many modern orchid hybrids.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
