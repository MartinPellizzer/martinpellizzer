Ononis hircina var. krylovii

Ononis hircina var. krylovii, commonly known as Krylov's hairy cinquefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and compound leaves with five leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
