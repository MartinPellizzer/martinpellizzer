Orchis iberica

Orchis iberica, commonly known as the Iberian orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers arranged in a spike, typically blooming from April to June. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged along the stem, and it thrives in calcareous soils.

Orchis iberica is considered a rare and protected species due to its limited distribution and ecological sensitivity.
