Oncidium luridum var. morrenii

Oncidium luridum var. morrenii, commonly known as the Morren's Yellow Oncidium, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow and green flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its pseudobulbs are elongated and segmented, supporting the plant's growth and storage of nutrients.

The flowers are often fragrant and attract various pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.
