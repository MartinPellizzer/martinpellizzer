Ophrys holosericea subsp. oblita

Ophrys holosericea subsp.

oblita, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to parts of southern Europe. It is characterized by its distinctive, bee-like flowers that mimic the appearance and scent of female bees to attract male bees for pollination.

The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its floral structure includes a single, erect flower with a pouch-like labellum that serves as a landing platform for pollinators.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species and is often protected in its natural range.
