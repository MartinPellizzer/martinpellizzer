Odina

Odina, commonly known as the water plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Alismataceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are typically submerged or floating in aquatic environments. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Odina thrives in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds, lakes, and wetlands.
