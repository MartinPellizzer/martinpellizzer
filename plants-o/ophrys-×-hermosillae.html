Ophrys × hermosillae

Ophrys × hermosillae, commonly known as the beautiful bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the genus Ophrys, which is known for its mimicry of female insects to attract male pollinators. The plant produces striking flowers that closely resemble female wasps, a feature that enhances its pollination success. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

The botanical characteristics of Ophrys × hermosillae include its erect stem, lance-shaped leaves, and showy, bee-mimicking flowers.
