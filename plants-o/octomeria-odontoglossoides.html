Octomeria odontoglossoides

Octomeria odontoglossoides, commonly known as the toothed tongue orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, lance-shaped leaves and intricate floral structure. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or humid habitats. Its flowers are small but colorful, with a distinctive lip that resembles a tongue, contributing to its common name.

Octomeria odontoglossoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
