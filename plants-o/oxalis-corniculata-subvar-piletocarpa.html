Oxalis corniculata subvar. piletocarpa

Oxalis corniculata subvar. piletocarpa, commonly known as the crimson wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with reddish veins and produces small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to red. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. It has a creeping growth habit with runners that can root at the nodes.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners, though it can become invasive in certain regions.
