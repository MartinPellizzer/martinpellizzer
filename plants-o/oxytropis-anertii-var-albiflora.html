Oxytropis anertii var. albiflora

Oxytropis anertii var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered locoweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and small, compound leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats.

Oxytropis anertii var. albiflora is adapted to arid environments and is considered a valuable component of native plant communities.
