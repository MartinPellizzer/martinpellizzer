Odontoglossum × mulus

Odontoglossum × mulus, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a naturally occurring cross between Odontoglossum orchids, resulting in a unique blend of characteristics from its parent species.

This plant is native to the Andes Mountains, where it thrives in cool, humid environments with high altitudes. It produces intricate, showy flowers that typically feature a variety of colors, including white, purple, and pink, with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. The plant has a pseudobulb structure, which helps it store water and nutrients, adapting it to its high-altitude habitat.

Odontoglossum × mulus is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and ease of cultivation under proper conditions.
