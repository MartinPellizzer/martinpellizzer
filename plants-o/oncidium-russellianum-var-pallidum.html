Oncidium russellianum var. pallidum

Oncidium russellianum var. pallidum, commonly known as the pale orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, drooping inflorescences and clusters of pale yellow to white flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its pseudobulbs are cylindrical and its leaves are strap-like, often with a waxy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements in cultivation.
