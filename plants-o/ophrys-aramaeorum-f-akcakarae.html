Ophrys aramaeorum f. akcakarae

Ophrys aramaeorum f. akcakarae, commonly known as the Akcakara Orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Ophrys genus, which is known for its mimicry of female insects to attract male pollinators. This particular form exhibits unique floral structures that closely resemble female wasps, enhancing its pollination success. The plant thrives in specific microhabitats, often in calcareous soils within rocky areas.

Its botanical significance lies in its ecological niche and its role in the biodiversity of its native region.
