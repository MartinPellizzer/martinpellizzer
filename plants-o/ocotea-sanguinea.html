Ocotea sanguinea

Ocotea sanguinea, commonly known as red sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown heartwood that is highly valued for its durability and scent. The plant typically grows in humid lowland forests and can reach heights of up to 30 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a glossy surface, while its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
