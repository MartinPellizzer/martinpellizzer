Oxytropis aculeata

Oxytropis aculeata, commonly known as goat's rue, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia, this plant thrives in rocky and well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Oxytropis aculeata is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.
