Oncoba tettensis var. fissistyla

Oncoba tettensis var. fissistyla, commonly known as the fissistylar palm, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by elongated, fibrous fruits. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
