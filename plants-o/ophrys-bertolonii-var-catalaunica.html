Ophrys bertolonii var. catalaunica

Ophrys bertolonii var. catalaunica, commonly known as the Catalan bee orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique floral morphology, including a specialized lip that mimics the appearance of a female bee. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats. Its flowers bloom in spring, producing a striking display of yellow and brown hues.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in several regions.
