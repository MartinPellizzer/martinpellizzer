Oenothera boothii subsp. desertorum

Oenothera boothii subsp.

desertorum, commonly known as the desert evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is well adapted to arid environments. Its stems are often sprawling or prostrate, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a hairy texture.

The species is known for its ecological importance in supporting pollinator populations in desert ecosystems.
