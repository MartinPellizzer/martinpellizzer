Ocotea pentagona

Ocotea pentagona, commonly known as the five-angled ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage. The leaves are typically elliptic to ovate, with a glossy surface and a distinct five-angled base, giving it its common name. It produces small, white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by dark blue, fleshy berries.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates for its aesthetic appeal.
