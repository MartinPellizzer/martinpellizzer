Onobrychis handel-mazzettii

Onobrychis handel-mazzettii, commonly known as the Italian burr clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. It has a creeping growth habit with nodules on its roots that host nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

This species is valued for its ability to thrive in poor soils and for its potential use in ecological restoration projects.
