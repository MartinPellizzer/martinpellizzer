Oxycedrus echinoformis

Oxycedrus echinoformis, commonly known as the echinocarp tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Rutaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Vietnam, and Cambodia. The tree is characterized by its small, spiny fruits that resemble a hedgehog, giving it its common name. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a rough texture, and it produces fragrant flowers that are yellowish-green in color.

The plant is often valued for its timber, which is used in traditional construction and craftsmanship.
