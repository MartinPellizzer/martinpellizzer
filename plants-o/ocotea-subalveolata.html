Ocotea subalveolata

Ocotea subalveolata, commonly known as the "southern ocotea," is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments within lowland and montane forests. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a distinctive glandular structure on the underside.

The species is valued for its timber and has some traditional medicinal uses among indigenous communities.
