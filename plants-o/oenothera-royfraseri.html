Oenothera royfraseri

Oenothera royfraseri, commonly known as the Roy Fraser's evening primrose, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening. The plant has slender, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by moths, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

Oenothera royfraseri is also known for its ability to attract nocturnal pollinators and its role in local ecosystems.
