Onobrychis sativa var. tatarica

Onobrychis sativa var. tatarica, commonly known as Tatarian clover, is a perennial legume native to the steppes of Eastern Europe and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats. It has a deep root system that helps it withstand drought conditions.

Tatarian clover is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural settings.
