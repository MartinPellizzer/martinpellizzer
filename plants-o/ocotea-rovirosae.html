Ocotea rovirosae

Ocotea rovirosae, commonly known as the Rovirosa ocotea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America, particularly in regions of Brazil, Colombia, and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a small, dark purple drupe containing a single seed.

Ocotea rovirosae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
