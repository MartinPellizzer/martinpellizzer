Ocotea raimondii

Ocotea raimondii, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a member of the Lauraceae family and is native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, dark bark and glossy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue berries that are attractive to birds. Its foliage and aromatic properties make it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.

However, it can become invasive in certain environments if not managed properly.
