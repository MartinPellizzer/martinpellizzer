Ophrys spruneri subsp. grigoriana

Ophrys spruneri subsp.

grigoriana, commonly known as the Grigoriana Orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is characterized by its unique, bee-mimicking floral structure, which plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy.

The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded habitats. Its flowers are usually pale green to brownish in color, with intricate patterns that resemble female bees.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its specific ecological requirements and its role in local biodiversity.
