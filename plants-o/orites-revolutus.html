Orites revolutus

Orites revolutus, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has broad, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in the summer months and attract various pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

Orites revolutus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
