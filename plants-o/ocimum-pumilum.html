Ocimum pumilum

Ocimum pumilum, commonly known as dwarf basil, is a small herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and oregano. The plant typically grows to a height of less than 30 centimeters, with small, oval-shaped leaves that have a distinctive minty aroma. Its flowers are small and clustered in whorls, producing nectar that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ocimum pumilum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of essential oils.
