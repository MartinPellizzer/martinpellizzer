Orchidantha chinensis var. longisepala

Orchidantha chinensis var. longisepala, commonly known as the long-sepal orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated sepals and petals that often display vibrant colors such as pink, white, and purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are often fragrant, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its beauty and rarity.
