Ornithogalum corsicum

Ornithogalum corsicum, commonly known as the Corsican star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in the spring. The plant is characterized by its bulbous root system, which is typical of the Liliaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and rocky areas.

Ornithogalum corsicum is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
