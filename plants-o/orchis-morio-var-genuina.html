Orchis morio var. genuina

Orchis morio var. genuina, commonly known as the Common Mouse-Ear Orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive violet to purple flowers with a yellowish throat. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, downy coating.

The species is known for its ecological importance, supporting various pollinators and serving as an indicator of biodiverse habitats.
