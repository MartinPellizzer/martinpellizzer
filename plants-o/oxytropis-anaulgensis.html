Oxytropis anaulgensis

Oxytropis anaulgensis, commonly known as the desert locoweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of western North America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in desert or semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish to greenish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually compound, consisting of several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Oxytropis anaulgensis is adapted to survive in harsh conditions with limited water availability.
