Oxalis robinsonii

Oxalis robinsonii, commonly known as the Robinson's wood sorrel, is a delicate perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets, often tinged with red or purple veins. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to native gardens.
