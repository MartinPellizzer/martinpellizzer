Oxalis schomburgkiana

Oxalis schomburgkiana, commonly known as the Schomburgk's wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, delicate flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and forest floors. Its foliage is often covered in fine, hair-like structures, and it produces edible tuberous roots.

Oxalis schomburgkiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
