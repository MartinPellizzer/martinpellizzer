Ocotea olivacea

Ocotea olivacea, commonly known as the olive-laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces dark blue, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various bird species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its wood is valued for its durability and fine grain, making it useful in furniture and construction.
