Ornithoglossum glaucum var. undulatum

Ornithoglossum glaucum var. undulatum, commonly known as the wavy bluebell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple, with a distinctive, tongue-like spur at the base. The plant has lanceolate, hairy leaves that are often bluish-green in color, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained, moist soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variant is noted for its unique floral morphology and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
