Oxalis spodiophylla

Oxalis spodiophylla, commonly known as the spider-veined wood sorrel, is a species native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves with distinctive, intricate venation patterns. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by heart-shaped seed pods. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Oxalis spodiophylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in garden settings.
