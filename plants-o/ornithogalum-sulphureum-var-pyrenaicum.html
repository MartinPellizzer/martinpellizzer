Ornithogalum sulphureum var. pyrenaicum

Ornithogalum sulphureum var. pyrenaicum, commonly known as the Pyrenean yellow star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pyrenees region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with a distinctive star-shaped appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
