Ophrys fusca subsp. fabrella

Ophrys fusca subsp.

fabrella, commonly known as the brown bee orchid, is a specialized orchid species native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination.

The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its flowers are usually brown or dark green, with intricate patterns that enhance the mimicry.

This orchid is a member of the Ophrys genus, known for its complex relationships with specific pollinators.
