Odontoglossum crispum var. trianae

Odontoglossum crispum var. trianae, commonly known as the Trianas orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

This species is characterized by its striking, velvety purple flowers with intricate patterns and a distinctive, crinkled leaf structure. It typically grows in high-altitude cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions with partial shade. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in its natural habitat.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts and collectors.
