Opuntia verschaffeltii

Opuntia verschaffeltii, commonly known as the Van der Vegt prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems that are covered in sharp spines and glochids. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months, followed by edible fruits that are often consumed by local wildlife. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting well to poor soil conditions.

Opuntia verschaffeltii plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing food and shelter for various animals.
