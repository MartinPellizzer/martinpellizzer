Ocimum hassleri var. acutatum

Ocimum hassleri var. acutatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf basil, is a variant of the Ocimum genus native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers and has a strong, aromatic scent similar to other basil species. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and culinary potential, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and for medicinal purposes.
