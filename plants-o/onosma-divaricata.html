Onosma divaricata

Onosma divaricata, commonly known as the branched hedge mustard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Onosma divaricata is often found in rocky soils and open habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments.
