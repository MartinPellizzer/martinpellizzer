Ophrys meropes

Ophrys meropes, commonly known as the bee orchid, is a species of orchid native to Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bee-like flower that mimics the appearance and scent of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brown, with intricate patterns that aid in deception.

Ophrys meropes is a perennial herb with a rhizomatous root system and is known for its unique reproductive strategy involving mimicry.
