Ophrys mavromata

Ophrys mavromata, commonly known as the black-faced bee orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee, aiding in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats. Its flowers are usually dark brown or black with contrasting yellow markings, and it blooms between April and June.

Ophrys mavromata is a rare and protected species, often found in specific microhabitats across southern Europe.
