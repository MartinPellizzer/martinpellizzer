Oenothera cheiranthifolia

Oenothera cheiranthifolia, commonly known as the desert evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are adapted to arid environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Oenothera cheiranthifolia is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is well-suited to desert conditions.
