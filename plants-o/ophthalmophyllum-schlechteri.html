Ophthalmophyllum schlechteri

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its unique, eye-like floral structures that resemble human eyes, giving it its common name. The plant features showy, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, with intricate patterns that enhance its visual appeal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its rare and specialized habitat, Ophthalmophyllum schlechteri is considered a delicate and endangered species.
