Oenothera cruciata var. sabulonensis

Oenothera cruciata var. sabulonensis, commonly known as the sand verbena or sand evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is well adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinator populations.
