Orchidotypus muscoides

Orchidotypus muscoides, commonly known as the moss orchid, is a unique epiphytic plant that thrives in humid, shaded environments.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that resemble moss. The plant has a compact growth form with thick, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the air and surrounding substrates.

Orchidotypus muscoides is often found growing on trees in tropical and subtropical regions.
