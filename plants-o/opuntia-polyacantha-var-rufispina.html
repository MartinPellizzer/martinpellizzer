Opuntia polyacantha var. rufispina

Opuntia polyacantha var. rufispina, commonly known as the red-spined prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in clusters of sharp spines, with the spines of this variety typically appearing reddish in color. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months, followed by edible, red-fleshed fruits. Its succulent stems store water efficiently, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

The plant's structure and adaptations make it well-suited for desert climates and a popular choice for xeriscaping.
