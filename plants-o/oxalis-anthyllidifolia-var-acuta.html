Oxalis anthyllidifolia var. acuta

Oxalis anthyllidifolia var. acuta, commonly known as the sharp-leaf wood sorrel, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with three heart-shaped leaflets, and its flowers are typically yellow with five petals. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to native plant gardens.
