Ornithopus perpusillus var. nodosus

Ornithopus perpusillus var. nodosus, commonly known as the dwarf flax or nodding flax, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features slender, branching stems that produce small, blue to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is typically found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and native landscaping.
