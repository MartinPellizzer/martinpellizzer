Ononis ramosissima var. gracilis

Ononis ramosissima var. gracilis, commonly known as slender broomweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features slender, branching stems that are often covered with fine hairs, and its leaves are typically narrow and linear. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
