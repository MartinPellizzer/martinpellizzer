Oxalis nigrescens

Oxalis nigrescens, commonly known as black oxalis, is a species of flowering plant in the family Oxalidaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, delicate flowers. The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers with five petals, often accompanied by prominent stamens. Its leaves are typically dark green and may turn reddish or purplish at the edges, especially in response to light or temperature changes.

Oxalis nigrescens is often grown as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and blooms.
