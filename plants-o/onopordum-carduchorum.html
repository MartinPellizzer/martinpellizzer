Onopordum carduchorum

Onopordum carduchorum, commonly known as the Armenian thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features robust, spiny stems and broad, silver-green leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers, which appear in late summer, are typically pink to purple in color and attract various pollinators.

Onopordum carduchorum is adapted to harsh, arid environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.
