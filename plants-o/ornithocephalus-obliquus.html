Ornithocephalus obliquus

Ornithocephalus obliquus, commonly known as the bent-stemmed orchid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, spiral-shaped inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has narrow, strap-like leaves. Its flowers are small and fragrant, often displaying a range of colors from white to pale purple.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
