Oxytropis rishiriensis

Oxytropis rishiriensis, commonly known as Rishiri oxytropis, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Japan, specifically the island of Rishiri, where it thrives in rocky and coastal environments. The plant is characterized by its erect growth habit, compound leaves, and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. It plays a role in soil stabilization and supports local insect populations.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
