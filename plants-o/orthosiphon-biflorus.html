Orthosiphon biflorus

Orthosiphon biflorus, commonly known as the two-flowered orthosiphon, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite leaves that are usually lance-shaped. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Orthosiphon biflorus is valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
