Ornithocephalus grex-anserinus

Ornithocephalus grex-anserinus, commonly known as the goose-headed orchid, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, showy flowers that resemble a goose's head. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as cloud forests and wetlands. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil efficiently.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and plays a role in local ecosystems by supporting pollinators.
