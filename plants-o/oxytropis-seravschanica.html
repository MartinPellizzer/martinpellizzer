Oxytropis seravschanica

Oxytropis seravschanica, commonly known as the Karakum oxytropis, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia, particularly found in the arid regions of Turkmenistan and Uzbekistan.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

Oxytropis seravschanica is adapted to harsh, dry environments and plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils through its extensive root system.
