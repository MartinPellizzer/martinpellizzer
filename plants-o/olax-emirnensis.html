Olax emirnensis

It belongs to the family Olacaceae and is characterized by its woody structure and small, often clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are usually ovate or elliptical, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.
