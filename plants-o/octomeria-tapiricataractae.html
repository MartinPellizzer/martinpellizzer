Octomeria tapiricataractae

Octomeria tapiricataractae, commonly known as the Tapir River orchid, is a rare and endemic orchid species found in the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that display a unique blend of purple and white hues. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or branches, exhibiting an epiphytic growth habit. Its floral structure includes specialized pollination mechanisms that attract specific pollinators, such as certain species of bees and moths.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
