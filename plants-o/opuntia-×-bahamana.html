Opuntia × bahamana

Opuntia × bahamana, commonly known as the Bahamian prickly pear, is a hybrid cactus species native to the Bahamas.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems that are covered in spines and glochids. The plant produces showy yellow or orange flowers, followed by edible fruits known as tunas. Its succulent leaves store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

Opuntia × bahamana plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
