Onobrychis charikarensis

Onobrychis charikarensis, commonly known as the Karikar shrub, is a perennial leguminous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a woody stem and is often found in dry, rocky habitats. It is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in ecological restoration.

Its seeds are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
