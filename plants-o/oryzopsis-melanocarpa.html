Oryzopsis melanocarpa

Oryzopsis melanocarpa, commonly known as black millet, is a grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, dark-colored grains that give it its common name. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in poor soil conditions, making it a hardy crop in arid regions. Its seeds are used for food, animal feed, and traditional medicine in various cultures.

The plant's botanical features include narrow leaves and panicles that produce numerous tiny florets.
