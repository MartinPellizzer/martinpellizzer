Oxalis primavera

Oxalis primavera, commonly known as spring wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10 to 15 centimeters and thrives in moist, well-drained soil. It is a member of the Oxalidaceae family and is known for its edible leaves, though they should be consumed in moderation.

The plant's botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and wildflower habitats.
