Opuntia subferox

Opuntia subferox, commonly known as the Mexican fire cactus, is a species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its flat, ribbed stems that are covered in spiny areoles. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits. Opuntia subferox is drought-tolerant and thrives in arid environments, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a popular choice for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
