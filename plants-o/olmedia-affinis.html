Olmedia affinis

Olmedia affinis, commonly known as the Spanish broom, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring erect, branching stems covered in small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a preference for well-drained soils.

Olmedia affinis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal or dry environments.
