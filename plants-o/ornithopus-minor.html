Ornithopus minor

Ornithopus minor, commonly known as lesser bird's-foot trefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant has a creeping growth habit with hairy stems and is often found in grassy areas and disturbed soils. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable for soil enrichment.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a popular choice for wildflower gardens and ecological restoration projects.
