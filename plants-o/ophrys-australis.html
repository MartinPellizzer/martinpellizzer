Ophrys australis

Ophrys australis, commonly known as the Australian bee orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its unique and intricate floral structure, which closely resembles a female bee, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant produces showy, bee-like flowers with a vivid yellow and brown coloration, often with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of insects. It typically grows in heathlands and open forests, thriving in well-drained soils.

Ophrys australis is a perennial herb that blooms from late spring to early autumn, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
