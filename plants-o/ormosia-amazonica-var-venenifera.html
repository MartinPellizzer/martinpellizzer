Ormosia amazonica var. venenifera

Ormosia amazonica var. venenifera, commonly known as the Amazonian poisonwood, is a species native to the rainforests of South America.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows in the understory of humid tropical forests. Its most notable feature is the presence of toxic compounds in its leaves and bark, which have been used historically by indigenous communities for various purposes.
