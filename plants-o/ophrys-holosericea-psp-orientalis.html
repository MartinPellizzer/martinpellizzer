Ophrys holosericea psp.

orientalis, commonly known as the Eastern Bee Orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to parts of Central Asia. It is characterized by its intricate, bee-like floral structure, which mimics the appearance and scent of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brownish in color, with a distinctively shaped lip that plays a key role in the pollination process.
