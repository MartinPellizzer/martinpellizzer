Odontoglossum youngii

Odontoglossum youngii, commonly known as the young's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its erect, leafy stems and showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in high-altitude environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink with intricate patterns, and it prefers cool, humid conditions with well-draining soil.

Odontoglossum youngii is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts due to its ornamental value and relatively manageable care requirements.
