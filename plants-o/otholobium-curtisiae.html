Otholobium curtisiae

Otholobium curtisiae, commonly known as the narrow-leafed burr bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brown, and it produces hairy, woody pods that contain small, rounded seeds.

Otholobium curtisiae is often used in traditional medicine and for soil stabilization in degraded landscapes.
