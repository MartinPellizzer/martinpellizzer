Ophrys fuciflora subsp. maxima

Ophrys fuciflora subsp.

maxima, commonly known as the large green orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species found in specific habitats across Europe. It is characterized by its distinctive greenish-yellow flowers that mimic the appearance of female insects, a trait known as sexual deception.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

The subspecies maxima is distinguished by its larger flower size and more robust growth compared to other members of the Ophrys genus.
