Oxalis gracilis var. lilacina

Oxalis gracilis var. lilacina, commonly known as the pink buttercup, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It features delicate, trifoliate leaves with a waxy texture and produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodland or forest understories. It has a trailing growth habit and is popular as a decorative plant in gardens and containers.

Oxalis gracilis var. lilacina is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
