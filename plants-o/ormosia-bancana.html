Ormosia bancana

Ormosia bancana, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability. The plant has compound leaves with alternate arrangement and produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences.

Its fruit is a legume pod containing several elliptical seeds, and it plays an important role in local ecosystems and traditional uses.
