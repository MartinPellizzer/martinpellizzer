Opuntia cylindrarticulata

Opuntia cylindrarticulata, commonly known as the cylindrical spine cactus, is a species native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in spines, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting well to poor soil conditions.

Opuntia cylindrarticulata is also valued for its ability to store water, making it a resilient plant in harsh climates.
