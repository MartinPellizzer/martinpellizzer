Ononis cephalotes var. aequivalens

Ononis cephalotes var. aequivalens, commonly known as the lesser whitlow grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats. The plant has slender, erect stems that bear small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its ornamental flowers.
