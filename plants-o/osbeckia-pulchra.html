Osbeckia pulchra

Osbeckia pulchra, commonly known as the pretty willow herb, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant features slender, willow-like stems and small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Osbeckia pulchra is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
