Ocotea teresae

Ocotea teresae, commonly known as the Brazilian camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark-green, lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, white flowers followed by spherical, dark purple berries. Its wood is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

Ocotea teresae plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
