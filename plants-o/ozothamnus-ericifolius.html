Ozothamnus ericifolius

Ozothamnus ericifolius, commonly known as coastal wavyleaf bush, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It features small, narrow, wavy-edged leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, spherical clusters, which bloom from late winter to early spring. It is adapted to sandy soils and can tolerate salt spray, making it a hardy species in coastal environments.

Ozothamnus ericifolius is often used in coastal landscaping due to its resilience and attractive appearance.
