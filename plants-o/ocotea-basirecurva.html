Ocotea basirecurva

Ocotea basirecurva, commonly known as the narrow-leafed ocotea, is a member of the Lauraceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and woody structure.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features simple, alternate leaves with a glossy, dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in axillary or terminal inflorescences.

The fruit is a small, dark drupe that contains a single seed, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
