Ocimum suave

Ocimum suave, commonly known as sweet basil, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and purple or white flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its square stems and opposite leaves. Ocimum suave thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun.

It is widely cultivated for its culinary and medicinal uses.
