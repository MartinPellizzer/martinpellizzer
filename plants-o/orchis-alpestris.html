Orchis alpestris

Orchis alpestris, commonly known as the alpine orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to alpine and subalpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stem and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it produces small, round, greenish-brown seed pods.

Orchis alpestris is noted for its ecological importance as a food source for various pollinators and its role in alpine ecosystems.
