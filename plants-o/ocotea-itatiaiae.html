Ocotea itatiaiae

Ocotea itatiaiae, commonly known as Itatiaia ocotea, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of southeastern Brazil.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within the understory of tropical rainforests. Its leaves are lance-shaped, glossy, and have a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
