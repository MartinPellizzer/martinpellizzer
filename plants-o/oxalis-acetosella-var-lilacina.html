Oxalis acetosella var. lilacina

Oxalis acetosella var. lilacina, commonly known as the purple wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive heart-shaped base and small, delicate flowers that range in color from pink to deep purple. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. Its flowers typically bloom in early spring, and the plant is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes.

Oxalis acetosella var. lilacina is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
