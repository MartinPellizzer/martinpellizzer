Ocotea nitidula

Ocotea nitidula, commonly known as the silky ocotea, is a flowering plant species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves that are glossy and often emit a subtle scent. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in the axils of the leaves.

The fruit is a small, smooth drupe that matures to a dark color, contributing to its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
