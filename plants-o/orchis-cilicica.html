Orchis cilicica

Orchis cilicica, commonly known as the Cilician orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its flowers are usually purple or violet in color, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination.

Orchis cilicica is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
