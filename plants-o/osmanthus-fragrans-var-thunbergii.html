Osmanthus fragrans var. thunbergii

Osmanthus fragrans var. thunbergii, commonly known as the Japanese osmanthus, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

The essential oils extracted from its flowers are widely used in perfumery and traditional Chinese medicine.
