Orchis × jenensis

Orchis × jenensis, commonly known as the Jenense Orchid, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Orchis morrowii and Orchis mascula.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of flowers, with each flower having three sepals and three petals, one of which is often modified into a labellum.

Orchis × jenensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
