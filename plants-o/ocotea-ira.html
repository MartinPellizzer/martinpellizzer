Ocotea ira

Ocotea ira, commonly known as the black pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and aromatic bark. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers followed by dark blue berries that contain the seeds. Ocotea ira is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic effects.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
