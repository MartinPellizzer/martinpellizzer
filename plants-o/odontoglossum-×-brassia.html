Odontoglossum × brassia

Odontoglossum × brassia, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a cross between Odontoglossum and Brassia, resulting in a unique blend of characteristics from both parent species.

It is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andes, where it thrives in cool, humid environments with well-draining soil. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple, often with intricate patterns. It requires specific care, including bright indirect light and consistent moisture, to bloom successfully.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and ease of cultivation.
