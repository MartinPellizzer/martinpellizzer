Oreocallis brachycarpa

Oreocallis brachycarpa, commonly known as the short-fruited orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to certain regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial herb characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive floral structure. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are often pale green or white, with intricate patterns on the petals. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, adapted to its arid or semi-arid habitat.

Oreocallis brachycarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in specialized gardens.
