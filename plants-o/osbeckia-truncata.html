Osbeckia truncata

Osbeckia truncata, commonly known as the roughleaf sandwort, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils, often in open woodlands or along stream banks. The plant features small, alternate leaves that are usually hairy and have a truncate or notched tip. Its flowers are yellow and clustered in dense, terminal inflorescences, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Osbeckia truncata is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
