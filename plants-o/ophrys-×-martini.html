Ophrys × martini

Ophrys × martini, commonly known as the honeybee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, bee-like flowers that mimic the appearance and scent of female bees to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brownish in color, with intricate patterns that aid in mimicry.

Ophrys × martini is valued in horticulture for its unique and ornamental floral display.
