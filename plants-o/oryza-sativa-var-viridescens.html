Oryza sativa var. viridescens

Oryza sativa var. viridescens, commonly known as the green rice variety, is a botanical variant of the rice plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinct greenish hue, which sets it apart from the typical white or brown rice varieties. The plant exhibits a robust growth pattern with long, slender leaves and inflorescences that produce small, fragrant flowers. Its grains are typically smaller and more elongated, contributing to its unique appearance and culinary use.

This variety is valued for its nutritional content and is often cultivated for both traditional and ornamental purposes.
