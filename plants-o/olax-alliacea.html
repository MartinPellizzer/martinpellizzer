Olax alliacea

Olax alliacea, commonly known as garlic lily, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Olacaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant has strap-like leaves and produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Olax alliacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
