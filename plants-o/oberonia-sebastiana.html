Oberonia sebastiana

Oberonia sebastiana, commonly known as the Malayan orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows on trees in humid, shaded forest environments. The plant has slender, wiry stems and produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. Its leaves are elongated and often have a waxy texture, adapted to retain moisture.

Oberonia sebastiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
