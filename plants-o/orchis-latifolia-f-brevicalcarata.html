Orchis latifolia f. brevicalcarata

Orchis latifolia f. brevicalcarata, commonly known as the broad-leaved orchid with short spur, is a botanical variant of the common twayblade orchid.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family, characterized by its distinctive floral structure. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist woodlands and has lanceolate, often hairy leaves. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish, with a short, narrow spur that is shorter than the standard in the typical form.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its presence in specific habitats across Europe.
