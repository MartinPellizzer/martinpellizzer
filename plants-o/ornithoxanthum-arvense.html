Ornithoxanthum arvense

Ornithoxanthum arvense, commonly known as the field oxalis, is a flowering plant belonging to the Oxalidaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Ornithoxanthum arvense is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resilience to environmental stress.
