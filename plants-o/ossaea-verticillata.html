Ossaea verticillata

Ossaea verticillata, commonly known as the spiral rush, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of leaves that spiral around the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. Ossaea verticillata thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal or wetland habitats.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
