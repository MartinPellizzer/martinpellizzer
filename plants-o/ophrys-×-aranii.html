Ophrys × aranii

Ophrys × aranii, commonly known as the Aran orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Aran Islands off the west coast of Ireland.

It is characterized by its intricate, bee-mimicking flowers that play a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous grasslands and has a slender, erect stem with a single or few erect leaves. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish in color, with a distinctive lip that resembles a female bee.

This species is of particular interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique ecological adaptations.
