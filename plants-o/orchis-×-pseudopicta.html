Orchis × pseudopicta

Orchis × pseudopicta, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a natural hybrid between Orchis morio and Orchis purpurea.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, spurred orchid flowers that bloom in late spring.

This hybrid is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique genetic combination and ornamental value.
