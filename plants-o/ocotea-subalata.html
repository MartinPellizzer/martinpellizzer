Ocotea subalata

Ocotea subalata, commonly known as the cloud forest laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves with a velvety texture. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters. Its flowers are small and clustered, followed by elliptical, dark blue-black berries that are an important food source for local wildlife.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
