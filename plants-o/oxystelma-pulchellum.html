Oxystelma pulchellum

Oxystelma pulchellum, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for warm climates.

Oxystelma pulchellum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
