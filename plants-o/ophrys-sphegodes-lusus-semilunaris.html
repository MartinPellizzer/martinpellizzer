Ophrys sphegodes lusus semilunaris

Ophrys sphegodes lusus semilunaris, commonly known as the semilunar bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species found in specific habitats across Europe.

It is characterized by its unique floral morphology, featuring a lip that mimics the appearance of a female bee, which plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant thrives in open, sunny areas with well-drained soils, often in disturbed or semi-natural environments. Its flowers are typically pale yellow to orange in color, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, this orchid is considered vulnerable, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts to protect its delicate ecological niche.
