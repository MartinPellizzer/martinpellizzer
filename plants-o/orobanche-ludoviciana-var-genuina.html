Orobanche ludoviciana var. genuina

Orobanche ludoviciana var. genuina, commonly known as the Indian paintbrush, is a hemiparasitic plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies on host plants for nutrients.

It produces striking, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which attract pollinators. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and grasslands. It forms a symbiotic relationship with various host species, primarily members of the legume family.

Its ecological role includes contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat and serving as a food source for certain insects.
