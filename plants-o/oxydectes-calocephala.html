Oxydectes calocephala

Oxydectes calocephala, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of South America.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
