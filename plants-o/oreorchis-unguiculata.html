Oreorchis unguiculata

Oreorchis unguiculata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved twayblade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It has a symbiotic relationship with mycorrhizal fungi, which aid in nutrient uptake.

Oreorchis unguiculata is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in clusters along the forest floor.
