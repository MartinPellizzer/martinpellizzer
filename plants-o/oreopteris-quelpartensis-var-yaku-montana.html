Oreopteris quelpartensis var. yaku-montana

Oreopteris quelpartensis var. yaku-montana, commonly known as the Yaku-montana wood fern, is a variant of the quelpartensis wood fern native to specific regions of Japan.

It is a perennial fern characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically bipinnate, with small, clustered sporangia on the undersides of the leaves. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories where it can access the necessary humidity and light conditions. Its botanical structure includes a short, creeping rhizome that supports the growth of its compound leaves.

This variant is notable for its unique morphological features and ecological niche within its native habitat.
