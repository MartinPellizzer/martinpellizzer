Operculicarya hirsutissima

Operculicarya hirsutissima, commonly known as the hairy operculicarya, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically opposite and have a rough, textured surface.

Operculicarya hirsutissima is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
