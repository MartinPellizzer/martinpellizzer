Oreodaphne lanata

Oreodaphne lanata, commonly known as the woolly oreodaphne, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed enclosed in a fleshy pulp.

Oreodaphne lanata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
