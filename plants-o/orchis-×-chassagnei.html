Orchis × chassagnei

Orchis × chassagnei, commonly known as the Chassagne orchid, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Orchis morio and Orchis militaris.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe, particularly France and Italy. The plant features showy, spurred orchid flowers that are typically purple or violet in color, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination. Orchis × chassagnei thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a natural hybrid, showcasing the genetic diversity within the Orchidaceae family.
