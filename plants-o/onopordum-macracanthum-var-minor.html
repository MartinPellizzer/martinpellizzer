Onopordum macracanthum var. minor

Onopordum macracanthum var. minor, commonly known as the lesser globemallow, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, spherical seed pods that contain numerous seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for xeriscaping and ecological restoration projects.
