Obione flavescens

Obione flavescens, commonly known as the yellow-flowered orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellowish flowers and slender, erect stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or wetlands. Its leaves are usually linear and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Obione flavescens is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils, making it a common indicator species in certain ecosystems.
