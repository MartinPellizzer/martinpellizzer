Ononis viscosa subsp. breviflora

Ononis viscosa subsp.

breviflora, commonly known as the short-flowered hedgegar, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, typically blooming from April to June.

The plant has a mat-forming habit with hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often glandular. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

This subspecies is distinguished by its shorter floral stems compared to the typical form of Ononis viscosa.
