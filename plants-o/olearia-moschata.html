Olearia moschata

Olearia moschata, commonly known as the sweet-scented daisy, is a perennial shrub native to New Zealand.

It features silver-gray foliage and produces clusters of small, white, daisy-like flowers with a fragrant, honey-scented center. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes. Its aromatic properties make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Olearia moschata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
