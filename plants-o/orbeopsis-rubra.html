Orbeopsis rubra

Orbeopsis rubra, commonly known as the red orbeopsis, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth texture, while its stems are woody and somewhat branched.

Orbeopsis rubra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its vibrant floral display.
