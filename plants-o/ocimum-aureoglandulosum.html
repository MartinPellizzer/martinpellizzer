Ocimum aureoglandulosum

Ocimum aureoglandulosum, commonly known as golden earring, is a species of flowering plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves, which are covered in glandular hairs, giving it a distinct scent. Its small, tubular flowers are typically yellow or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ocimum aureoglandulosum is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and medicinal properties.
