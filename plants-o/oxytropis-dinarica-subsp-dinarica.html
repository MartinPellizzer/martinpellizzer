Oxytropis dinarica subsp. dinarica

Oxytropis dinarica subsp.

dinarica, commonly known as the Dinaric oxytropis, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky or dry habitats and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native range. Oxytropis dinarica subsp.

dinarica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
