Ocimum decumbens

Ocimum decumbens, commonly known as Mexican oregano, is a herbaceous plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly hairy leaves and small, pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and digestive properties.

Ocimum decumbens is also valued in culinary applications, particularly in Mexican cuisine.
