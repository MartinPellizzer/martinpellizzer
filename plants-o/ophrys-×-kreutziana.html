Ophrys × kreutziana

Ophrys × kreutziana, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera (the bee orchid) and Ophrys insectifera (the wasp orchid). The plant is characterized by its intricate, bee-like floral structures that mimic the appearance and scent of female bees, aiding in pollination by male bees. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its unique morphology and pollination strategy make it a subject of interest in both botanical and ecological studies.
