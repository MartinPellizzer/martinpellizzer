Oxalis taiwanensis

Oxalis taiwanensis, commonly known as the Taiwan wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Oxalis taiwanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
