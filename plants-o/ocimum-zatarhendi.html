Ocimum zatarhendi

Ocimum zatarhendi, commonly known as wild basil, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 cm, with opposite, ovate leaves that have a mild, spicy scent. Its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or pink, and it blooms from late spring to early autumn.

Ocimum zatarhendi is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb due to its aromatic properties.
