Ocotea vaginans

Ocotea vaginans, commonly known as the velvetwood or blackwood, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in countries like Mexico, Guatemala, and Honduras. The plant is characterized by its dark, aromatic wood and lanceolate, glossy leaves. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, rounded berries.

Ocotea vaginans is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its durable timber and medicinal properties.
