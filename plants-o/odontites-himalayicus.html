Odontites himalayicus

Odontites himalayicus, commonly known as the Himalayan toothplant, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, basal leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in its natural habitat.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
