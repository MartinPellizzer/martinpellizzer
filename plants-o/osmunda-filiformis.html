Osmunda filiformis

Osmunda filiformis, commonly known as the slender water fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Osmundaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery fronds that are typically less than 1 meter in length. The plant thrives in wet, swampy environments and is often found in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Osmunda filiformis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
