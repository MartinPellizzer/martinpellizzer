Ochna hexasperma

Ochna hexasperma, commonly known as the six-ribbed ochna, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange to red berries containing several seeds. Ochna hexasperma is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential medicinal research.
