Otanthera confusa

Otanthera confusa, commonly known as the confused blazingstar, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. Its showy, yellow-orange flower heads bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Otanthera confusa is often found in prairies, open woodlands, and along roadsides, thriving in full sun to partial shade.
