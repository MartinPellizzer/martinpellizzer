Orchis laxiflora f. zimmermannii

Orchis laxiflora f. zimmermannii, commonly known as the Zimmermann's looseflower orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to specific regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, elongated flowers that bloom in late spring.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern.
