Onopordum pyrenaicum

Onopordum pyrenaicum, commonly known as the Pyrenean thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula and parts of southern France.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, such as slopes and scrublands. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.

Its robust structure and adaptive features allow it to thrive in arid environments.
