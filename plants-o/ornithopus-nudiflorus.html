Ornithopus nudiflorus

Ornithopus nudiflorus, commonly known as the nakedflower flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers that appear in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Ornithopus nudiflorus plays a role in supporting pollinators and is valued for its ecological resilience.
