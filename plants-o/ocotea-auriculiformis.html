Ocotea auriculiformis

Ocotea auriculiformis, commonly known as the golden ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and avocado. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a dark blue drupe with a single seed, and it is often found in humid lowland forests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
