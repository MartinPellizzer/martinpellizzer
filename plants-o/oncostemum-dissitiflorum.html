Oncostemum dissitiflorum

Oncostemum dissitiflorum, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed flowering plant, is a species native to certain regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its hairy stems and clusters of small, often white or yellowish flowers. The leaves are typically arranged in a rosette pattern and are covered with fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or rocky outcrops.
