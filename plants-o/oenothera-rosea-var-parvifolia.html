Oenothera rosea var. parvifolia

Oenothera rosea var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, branching stems that typically grow to a height of 1 to 3 feet. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening and close by morning. Its leaves are lance-shaped, alternate, and often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is characterized by its smaller leaves compared to the typical Oenothera rosea, giving it a distinct appearance.
