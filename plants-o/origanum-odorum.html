Origanum odorum

Origanum odorum, commonly known as sweet marjoram, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are fragrant, containing essential oils that contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.

Origanum odorum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a spice.
