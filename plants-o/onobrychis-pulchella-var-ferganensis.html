Onobrychis pulchella var. ferganensis

Onobrychis pulchella var. ferganensis, commonly known as the Fergana clover, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to semi-arid climates. It is often used for forage and soil stabilization due to its hardy nature.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
