Oxalis anthyllidifolia

Oxalis anthyllidifolia, commonly known as the pink buttercup, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or stream banks. It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family, which is known for its calcium oxalate crystals in the leaves.

Oxalis anthyllidifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
