Ophrys aranifera subsp. litigiosa

Ophrys aranifera subsp.

litigiosa, commonly known as the litigiosa bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to parts of Europe. It is characterized by its striking floral morphology, which closely resembles a female bee, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy.

The plant produces a single, showy flower with intricate patterns and a hairy, bee-like appearance. It thrives in specific habitats such as calcareous grasslands and open woodlands, often in areas with well-drained soil.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, this subspecies is considered vulnerable, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
