Oenothera cespitosa var. marginata

Oenothera cespitosa var. marginata, commonly known as the showy evening primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are often reddish in color. This variety is distinguished by its marginally colored petals, which may have a pink or reddish edge.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in prairies and open woodlands.
