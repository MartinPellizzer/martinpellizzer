Onobrychis aequidentata var. gussonei

Onobrychis aequidentata var. gussonei, commonly known as the Gussone's goat's rue, is a perennial leguminous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers arranged in racemes. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky or limestone habitats and is adapted to dry conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy species with potential for use in ecological restoration projects.
