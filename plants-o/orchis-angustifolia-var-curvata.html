Orchis angustifolia var. curvata

Orchis angustifolia var. curvata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate, pinkish-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodland floors and stream banks. It has a short rhizome and produces a single spike of small, spurred flowers.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often found in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
