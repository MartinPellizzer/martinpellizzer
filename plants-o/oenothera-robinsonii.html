Oenothera robinsonii

Oenothera robinsonii, commonly known as Robinson's evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in arid and rocky habitats, demonstrating adaptability to harsh environments.

Oenothera robinsonii is also known for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses involving its seeds and oils.
