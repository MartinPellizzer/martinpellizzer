Oenothera serotina

Oenothera serotina, commonly known as evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Onagraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands. Its seeds are enclosed in a capsule that splits open to release the seeds, aiding in its dispersal.

Oenothera serotina is also known for its medicinal properties, with its oils used in various health applications.
