Oraoma

Oraoma, commonly known as the sacred lotus of the Amazon, is a striking aquatic plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, providing shade and habitat for various aquatic life. The plant produces strikingly beautiful flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white, often with a yellow center. Its roots are submerged, anchoring it in the mud and absorbing nutrients from the water.

Oraoma is valued not only for its aesthetic appeal but also for its cultural and medicinal significance among indigenous communities.
