Olea acuminata var. longifolia

Olea acuminata var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved olive, is a variant of the olive tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by olive fruits, which are used in various culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a temperate climate with adequate sunlight.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its potential economic importance in olive cultivation.
