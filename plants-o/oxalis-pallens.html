Oxalis pallens

Oxalis pallens, commonly known as the pale wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Oxalis pallens is a perennial species that produces capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
