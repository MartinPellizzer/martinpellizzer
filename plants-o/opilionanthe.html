Opilionanthe

Opilionanthe, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic orchid, meaning it grows on the surfaces of trees without deriving nutrients from them. The plant is characterized by its delicate, pale green leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in the summer. Opilionanthe is highly sensitive to environmental changes, making it a vulnerable species.

Due to its rarity and unique ecological requirements, it is often found in protected areas and is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
