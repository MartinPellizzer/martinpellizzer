Oxytropis latibracteata var. longibracteata

Oxytropis latibracteata var. longibracteata, commonly known as the broad-bract locoweed, is a perennial legume native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It is adapted to arid environments and can tolerate poor soil conditions.

The species is also noted for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock.
