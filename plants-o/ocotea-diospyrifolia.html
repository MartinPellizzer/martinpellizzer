Ocotea diospyrifolia

Ocotea diospyrifolia, commonly known as the purple-leafed ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often display a purplish hue on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue berries, which are an important food source for various bird species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
