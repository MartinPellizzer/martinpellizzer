Orchis × approximata

Orchis × approximata, commonly known as the common twayblade, is a hybrid orchid species native to Europe.

It is a terrestrial herb with a distinctive appearance, featuring a slender, leafless stem and a single, showy spike of flowers. The flowers are typically greenish-yellow with purple markings, and they bloom from late spring to early summer. This plant is often found in grasslands, woodlands, and along paths, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.

It plays a role in supporting pollinators and is of interest to botanists due to its hybrid nature and ecological significance.
