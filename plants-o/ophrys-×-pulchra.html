Ophrys × pulchra

Ophrys × pulchra, commonly known as the showy bee orchid, is a hybrid species within the genus Ophrys, native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its intricate, bee-like flower structure, which features a specialized lower petal resembling a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brown, with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of insects.

Ophrys × pulchra is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by supporting pollinator activity.
