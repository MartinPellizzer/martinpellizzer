Oxydectes costata

Oxydectes costata, commonly known as the serrated spurge, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems that are often ribbed or ridged. The leaves are typically lance-shaped with serrated edges, and the plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species in suitable climates.

Oxydectes costata is also known for its ability to tolerate drought conditions once established.
