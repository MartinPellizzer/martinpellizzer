Odontonema scandens

Odontonema scandens, commonly known as the silver vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a trailing perennial that produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with a silvery underside, giving it its common name. It thrives in humid, tropical conditions and is often grown as a ground cover or hanging plant.

Odontonema scandens is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
