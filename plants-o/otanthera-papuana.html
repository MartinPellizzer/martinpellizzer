Otanthera papuana

Otanthera papuana, commonly known as the Papuan candleplant, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange inflorescences that resemble candles, hence its common name. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in coastal areas.

Otanthera papuana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in some island communities.
