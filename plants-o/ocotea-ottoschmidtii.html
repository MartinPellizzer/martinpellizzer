Ocotea ottoschmidtii

Ocotea ottoschmidtii, commonly known as the Otto Schmidt's ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by spherical, dark blue berries that are edible for some bird species. Its wood is valued for its fine grain and durability, making it a sought-after material in the furniture and construction industries.

Ocotea ottoschmidtii also plays an important ecological role in its native habitats by providing shelter and food for various wildlife.
