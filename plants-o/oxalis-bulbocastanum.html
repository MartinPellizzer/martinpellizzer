Oxalis bulbocastanum

Oxalis bulbocastanum, commonly known as the bulbous wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves with a waxy texture and bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces bulbous structures underground, which serve as storage organs and aid in propagation. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Oxalis bulbocastanum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
