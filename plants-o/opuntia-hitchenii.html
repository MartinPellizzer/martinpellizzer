Opuntia hitchenii

Opuntia hitchenii, commonly known as the Hitchen's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in sharp spines and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces edible fruits, known as tunas, which are typically orange to red in color. Opuntia hitchenii thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to extreme temperatures and low precipitation.

Its succulent structure allows it to store water efficiently, making it well-suited for desert conditions.
