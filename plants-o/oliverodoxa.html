Oliverodoxa

Oliverodoxa, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a species native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, featuring a broad, spreading canopy. The leaves are alternate, simple, and typically have a silvery or grayish underside, giving the plant its common name. The flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually green or yellow, and appear in clusters.

The fruit is a small, round drupe that contains a single seed.
