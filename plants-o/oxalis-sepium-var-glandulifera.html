Oxalis sepium var. glandulifera

Oxalis sepium var. glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish or purple coloration, often adorned with small, glandular dots. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
