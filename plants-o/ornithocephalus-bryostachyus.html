Ornithocephalus bryostachyus

Ornithocephalus bryostachyus, commonly known as the African orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows in grassland habitats and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with a unique structure that resembles a bird's head, giving it its scientific name.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
