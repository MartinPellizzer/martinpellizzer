Onagra hookeri

Onagra hookeri, commonly known as the desert evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Onagraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant has hairy, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are adapted to arid environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Onagra hookeri is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal in dry gardens.
