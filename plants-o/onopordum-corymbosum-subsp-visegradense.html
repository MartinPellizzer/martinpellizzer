Onopordum corymbosum subsp. visegradense

Onopordum corymbosum subsp.

visegradense, commonly known as the Visegrad thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

The plant features a robust stem with spiny leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are usually lobed and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are arranged in a compact head.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
