Ocotea lenticellata

Ocotea lenticellata, commonly known as the fragrant ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and laurel. The tree is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. Its small, white flowers are followed by spherical, dark blue berries that are edible but not widely consumed by humans.

Ocotea lenticellata is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
