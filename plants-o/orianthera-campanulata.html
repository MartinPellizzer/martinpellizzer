Orianthera campanulata

Orianthera campanulata, commonly known as the blue bell orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and the flowers bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

Orianthera campanulata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
