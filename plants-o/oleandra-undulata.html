Oleandra undulata

Oleandra undulata, commonly known as the wavy oleander, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has opposite, simple, and usually ovate leaves that are often slightly wavy in appearance, giving it its common name. Oleandra undulata is evergreen and can grow as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
