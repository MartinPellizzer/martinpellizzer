Obione hypsophila

Obione hypsophila, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, adapting to harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its roots are fibrous and specialized for nutrient absorption.

Due to its habitat, it is considered a rare and endangered species, requiring conservation efforts to protect its natural ecosystem.
