Orobus occidentalis

Orobus occidentalis, commonly known as the western orobus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It produces pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Orobus occidentalis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small wildlife in its native range.
