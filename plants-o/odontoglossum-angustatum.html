Odontoglossum angustatum

Odontoglossum angustatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in cool, shaded environments at high altitudes. The plant features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in a raceme, with pale green to yellowish petals and sepals.

Odontoglossum angustatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its delicate appearance.
