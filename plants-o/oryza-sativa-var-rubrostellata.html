Oryza sativa var. rubrostellata

Oryza sativa var. rubrostellata, commonly known as the red-stalked rice, is a variant of rice characterized by its distinctive reddish stalks and grains.

This plant belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is native to Asia, particularly regions in China and Southeast Asia. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 1.5 meters, with slender, erect stems that turn red as the plant matures. The leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while the inflorescence consists of spikelets that produce small, rice-like grains.

This variety is valued for its unique coloration and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes or as a specialty crop.
