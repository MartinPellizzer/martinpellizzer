Odontoglossum × josephinae

Odontoglossum × josephinae, commonly known as the Josephina orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, large, and showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually violet or purple in color, with intricate patterns and a strong, sweet fragrance.

Odontoglossum × josephinae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
