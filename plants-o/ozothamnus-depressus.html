Ozothamnus depressus

Ozothamnus depressus, commonly known as the low-growing golden everlasting, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, yellowish-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate floral display. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Ozothamnus depressus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in native landscaping and restoration projects.
