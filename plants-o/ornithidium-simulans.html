Ornithidium simulans

Ornithidium simulans, commonly known as the mimic orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that resemble other orchid species, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has a slender, creeping rhizome system. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, with a subtle fragrance that attracts specific pollinators.

Due to habitat loss and overcollection, Ornithidium simulans is considered endangered in its natural range.
