Ocimum albostellatum

Ocimum albostellatum, commonly known as the white-stemmed basil, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs such as mint and oregano. The plant is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, white or pale green leaves. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, producing a mild, spicy fragrance.

Ocimum albostellatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild culinary uses.
