Ozoroa pseudoverticillata

Ozoroa pseudoverticillata, commonly known as the African feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, fluffy inflorescences that resemble a feather, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Ozoroa pseudoverticillata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
