Oenothera cardiophylla var. petiolaris

Oenothera cardiophylla var. petiolaris, commonly known as the narrow-leaf evening primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features showy yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically narrower than the standard variety. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
