Omphalea elaeophoroides

Omphalea elaeophoroides, commonly known as the purple arrowleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves that are often a deep purple or violet color. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by spiny, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
