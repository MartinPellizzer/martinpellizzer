Odontadenia spoliata

Odontadenia spoliata, commonly known as the African rock orchid, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, succulent-like growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are fleshy and often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

Odontadenia spoliata is often found in open, sunny habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
