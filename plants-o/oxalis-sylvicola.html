Oxalis sylvicola

Oxalis sylvicola, commonly known as the forest wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped lobes and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white, pink, or yellow. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves and flowers are often covered in fine hairs, and it has a fibrous root system.

Oxalis sylvicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
