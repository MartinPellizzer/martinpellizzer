Ochna fruticulosa

Ochna fruticulosa, commonly known as the African star-of-Bethlehem, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, yellow, star-shaped flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has elliptic to ovate leaves with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its propagation.

Ochna fruticulosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun.
