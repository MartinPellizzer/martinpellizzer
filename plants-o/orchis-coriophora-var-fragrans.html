Orchis coriophora var. fragrans

Orchis coriophora var. fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant narrow-leaved orchid, is a species of orchid native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that emit a subtle fragrance. This orchid typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant is part of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its delicate, ephemeral blooms.

Its botanical structure reflects adaptations to its specific ecological niche, with specialized roots and flowering patterns.
