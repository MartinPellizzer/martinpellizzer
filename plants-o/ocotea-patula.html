Ocotea patula

Ocotea patula, commonly known as the white sand ocotea, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in the understory of tropical forests. Its leaves are dark green and glossy, with a distinct aromatic scent when crushed.

The fruit is a small, dark blue drupe that contains a single seed, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
