Orobanche ballii

Orobanche ballii, commonly known as the Ball's broomrape, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and cannot perform photosynthesis.

It relies entirely on its host plant for nutrients, typically growing in association with species of the legume family. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has a distinctive appearance with its fleshy, leafless stems. It is native to parts of Australia and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Due to its parasitic nature, it plays a unique role in its ecosystem, influencing the growth and distribution of its host plants.
