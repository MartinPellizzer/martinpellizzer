Ozanthes benzoin

Ozanthes benzoin, commonly known as benjoin or the Indian gum tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its aromatic, resinous leaves and bark, which emit a strong, spicy scent. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Ozanthes benzoin is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and commercial contexts.
