Ocotea benthamiana

Ocotea benthamiana, commonly known as the mountain pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered white flowers that give way to dark purple berries. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils, which have been used in traditional medicine for their antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

Ocotea benthamiana thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests at high elevations.
