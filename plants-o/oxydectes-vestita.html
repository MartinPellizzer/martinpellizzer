Oxydectes vestita

Oxydectes vestita, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Oxydectes vestita is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural areas.

It has been studied for its potential as a bioindicator of soil health due to its sensitivity to environmental changes.
