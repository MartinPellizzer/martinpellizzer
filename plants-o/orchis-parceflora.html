Orchis parceflora

Orchis parceflora, commonly known as the pale orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear a single spike of pale violet to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient uptake.

Orchis parceflora is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
