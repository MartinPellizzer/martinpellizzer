Oreocharis maximowiczii var. maximowiczii

Oreocharis maximowiczii var. maximowiczii, commonly known as the Korean bellflower, is a perennial herb native to Korea.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and lilac. The leaves are lanceolate, with a smooth texture and a slightly hairy underside. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species within the Campanulaceae family.
