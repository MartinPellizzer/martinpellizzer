Oxalis eriocarpa

Oxalis eriocarpa, commonly known as the woolly wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves with a woolly texture, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of five-petaled yellow flowers, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique foliage and delicate floral display.
