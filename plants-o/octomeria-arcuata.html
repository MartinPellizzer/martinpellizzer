Octomeria arcuata

Octomeria arcuata, commonly known as the waxy leaf octomeria, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its succulent, waxy leaves that are often clustered at the base of the plant. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions.

Octomeria arcuata is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
