Origanum × fortuitum

Origanum × fortuitum, commonly known as Turkish oregano, is a hybrid perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small, pale green to yellowish leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and Mediterranean climates. Its essential oils, rich in thymol and carvacrol, contribute to its strong scent and medicinal properties.

It is often used in culinary applications and traditional herbal remedies.
