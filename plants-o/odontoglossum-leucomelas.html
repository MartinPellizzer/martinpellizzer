Odontoglossum leucomelas

Odontoglossum leucomelas, commonly known as the white-streaked orchid, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, pendulous flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in cloud forests. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help protect it from excessive moisture loss.

The flowers feature a distinctive white stripe on the lip, making it easily identifiable among other orchid species.
