Ozothamnus rosmarinifolius var. brevifolius

Ozothamnus rosmarinifolius var. brevifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved golden weed, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow, linear leaves and small yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal remedies, and it has a compact growth habit that makes it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its resilience to heat and cold.
