Oxalis umbrosa

Oxalis umbrosa, commonly known as the shadowy wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with reddish veins and produces small, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink or white. The plant thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks. Its foliage and flowers are often used in ornamental gardening due to their delicate appearance.

Oxalis umbrosa is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plants.
