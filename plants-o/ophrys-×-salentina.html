Ophrys × salentina

Ophrys × salentina, commonly known as the Salentine bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its intricate floral structure, which closely resembles the face of a female bee, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant produces a single, showy flower with a specialized labellum that mimics the appearance of an insect. Its botanical features include a tall, erect stem with a single, recurved leaf, and it thrives in open, sunny habitats.

Ophrys × salentina is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its unique morphology and ecological significance.
