Osteospermum moniliferum subsp. septentrionale

Osteospermum moniliferum subsp.

septentrionale, commonly known as the blue osteospermum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with narrow, gray-green leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its botanical features include a distinct central cone-like structure surrounded by ray florets.
