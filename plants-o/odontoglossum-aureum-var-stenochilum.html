Odontoglossum aureum var. stenochilum

Odontoglossum aureum var. stenochilum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow petals and sepals, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree branches in its natural habitat. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, producing a fragrant, showy display.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
