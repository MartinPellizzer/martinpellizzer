Oenothera serrulata var. maculata

Oenothera serrulata var. maculata, commonly known as the spotted evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that open in the evening and close by morning. The leaves are serrated and often have a bluish-green hue with a slightly hairy texture. This variant is noted for its unique spotted patterns on the petals, which give it its common name.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.
