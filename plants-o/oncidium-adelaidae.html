Oncidium adelaidae

Oncidium adelaidae, commonly known as the Adelaide orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Australia.

It features a rhizomatous growth habit with long, narrow leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences bearing numerous small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green. Its blooms typically appear in spring and summer, and it thrives in well-drained soil with partial shade.

Oncidium adelaidae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
