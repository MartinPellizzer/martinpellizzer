Orchis × ambigua var. claudiopolitana

Orchis × ambigua var. claudiopolitana, commonly known as the Claudia polita orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and thrives in woodland or meadow habitats. Its flowers are often arranged in a loose raceme and are pollinated by insects such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
