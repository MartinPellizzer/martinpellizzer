Oxydectes floribunda

Oxydectes floribunda, commonly known as the flowering dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces showy yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. After flowering, it develops fluffy white seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas and disturbed sites.
