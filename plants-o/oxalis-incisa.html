Oxalis incisa

Oxalis incisa, commonly known as the cutleaf wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, which are deeply lobed and resemble the shape of a heart. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with five petals. Oxalis incisa thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
