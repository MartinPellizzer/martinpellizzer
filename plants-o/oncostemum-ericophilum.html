Oncostemum ericophilum

Oncostemum ericophilum, commonly known as the heath oncosemum, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils in woodland or heathland environments. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, and it produces small, dark berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals.

Oncostemum ericophilum is often found in association with other ericaceous plants in moist, shaded habitats.
