Olearia pannosa subsp. cardiophylla

Olearia pannosa subsp.

cardiophylla, commonly known as the heart-leaf panny, is a shrub native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The leaves are typically heart-shaped, giving the plant its common name, and are covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
