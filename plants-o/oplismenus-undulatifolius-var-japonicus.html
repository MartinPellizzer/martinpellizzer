Oplismenus undulatifolius var. japonicus

Oplismenus undulatifolius var. japonicus, commonly known as the Japanese wave grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features slender, arching stems that produce small, pale green to white flowers in late spring. The plant's most distinctive characteristic is its undulating, wavy-shaped leaves, which give it a unique and ornamental appearance. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic value.

The variety japonicus is noted for its compact growth and adaptability to various soil types.
