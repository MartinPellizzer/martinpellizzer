Olea asiatica

Olea asiatica, commonly known as the Indian olive tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to South and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by small, fleshy fruits containing a single stone. Olea asiatica is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
