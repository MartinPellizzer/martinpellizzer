Ophrys passionis subsp. majellensis

Ophrys passionis subsp.

majellensis, commonly known as the Majelline Bee Orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its intricate floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee, a trait evolved to attract male bees for pollination.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in dry, sunny habitats. Its flowers are usually a mix of brown, yellow, and orange, with a distinctive bee-like shape.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
