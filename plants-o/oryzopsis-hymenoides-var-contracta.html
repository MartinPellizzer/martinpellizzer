Oryzopsis hymenoides var. contracta

Oryzopsis hymenoides var. contracta, commonly known as the narrowleaf beakgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils, and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities in its range.
