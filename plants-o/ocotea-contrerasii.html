Ocotea contrerasii

Ocotea contrerasii, commonly known as the Huayna or Huayna tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark-green, leathery leaves. The tree produces small, white flowers followed by elliptical, dark blue-black fruits. Its bark is thick and often has a rough, fissured texture.

Ocotea contrerasii is valued for its wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making due to its durability and resistance to decay.
