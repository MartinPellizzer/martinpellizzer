Ouratea inconformis

Ouratea inconformis, commonly known as the Brazilian holly or false holly, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter in color underneath. Its small, white to pale yellow flowers are clustered in umbels and bloom in late spring.

The fruit is a small, smooth, yellowish drupe that contains a single seed.
