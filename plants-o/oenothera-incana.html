Oenothera incana

Oenothera incana, commonly known as the woolly nightshade, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-white, woolly foliage and produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom at night. The plant is often found in dry, disturbed soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers emit a mild fragrance and attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

Oenothera incana is also known for its ability to self-seed and is considered a hardy, low-maintenance plant.
