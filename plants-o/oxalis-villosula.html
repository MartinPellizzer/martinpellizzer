Oxalis villosula

Oxalis villosula, commonly known as the hairy wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, yellow, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has a fibrous root system. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Oxalis villosula is also known for its ability to thrive in clay soils and its preference for partial to full shade.
