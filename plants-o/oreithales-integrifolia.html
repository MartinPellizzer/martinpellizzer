Oreithales integrifolia

Oreithales integrifolia, commonly known as the smoothleaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and entire. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers with a distinct, sweet fragrance. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
