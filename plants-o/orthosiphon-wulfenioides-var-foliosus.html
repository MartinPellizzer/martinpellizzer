Orthosiphon wulfenioides var. foliosus

Orthosiphon wulfenioides var. foliosus, commonly known as Java tea or Malabar tea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom during the rainy season. Its aromatic leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is valued for its adaptability to tropical climates and its role in herbal remedies.
