Oxypetalum corymbiferum

Oxypetalum corymbiferum, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are slender and often branched.

Oxypetalum corymbiferum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
