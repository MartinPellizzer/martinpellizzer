Onobrychis ornata subsp. vanensis

Onobrychis ornata subsp.

vanensis, commonly known as the Van ornamental burr, is a perennial legume native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves with a hairy underside.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, racemose inflorescences, followed by distinctive, globular pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
