Onobrychis garinensis

Onobrychis garinensis, commonly known as the Garin's burclover, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its seed pods are hairy and contain several small, rounded seeds.

Onobrychis garinensis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
