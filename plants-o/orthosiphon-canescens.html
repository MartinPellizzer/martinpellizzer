Orthosiphon canescens

Orthosiphon canescens, commonly known as Java tea or Malabar tea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that are clustered in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.
