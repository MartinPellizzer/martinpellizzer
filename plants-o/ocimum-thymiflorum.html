Ocimum thymiflorum

Ocimum thymiflorum, commonly known as lemon balm, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, lemon-scented leaves and small, clustered flowers that range from white to pale purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

Due to its pleasant aroma and medicinal properties, it is often used in herbal remedies and aromatherapy.
