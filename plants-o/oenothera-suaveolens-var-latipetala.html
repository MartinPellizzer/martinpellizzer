Oenothera suaveolens var. latipetala

Oenothera suaveolens var. latipetala, commonly known as the broad-petaled evening primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southwestern United States.

It features showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening and close by morning, a trait known as nyctinasty. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by moths and other nocturnal insects.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique blooming habit.
