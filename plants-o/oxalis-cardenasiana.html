Oxalis cardenasiana

Oxalis cardenasiana, commonly known as the Mexican wood sorrel, is a perennial flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Oxalis cardenasiana is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and as a ground cover.
