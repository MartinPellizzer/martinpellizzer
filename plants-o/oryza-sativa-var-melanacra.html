Oryza sativa var. melanacra

Oryza sativa var. melanacra, commonly known as black rice, is a variety of rice characterized by its dark pigmentation due to high levels of anthocyanins.

This rice species is native to Southeast Asia and is cultivated for its nutritional value and unique aesthetic properties. The grains are typically smaller and harder than common white rice, with a nutty aroma. It is rich in antioxidants, fiber, and essential minerals, making it a valuable addition to a balanced diet.

The plant's botanical features include long, slender leaves and panicles that produce numerous small, white flowers.
