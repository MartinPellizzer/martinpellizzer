Ononis spinosa var. sancti-marini

Ononis spinosa var. sancti-marini, commonly known as the San Marino rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with fleshy, hairy stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, which are borne on short pedicels. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

This variety is noted for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions and is considered a rare and specialized species.
