Onobrychis laxiflora subsp. shahrestanica

Onobrychis laxiflora subsp.

shahrestanica, commonly known as Persian clover, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellow to reddish-brown pea-like flowers.

The plant is adapted to dry and rocky environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and deep root systems. It is often found in alpine and subalpine zones, contributing to soil stabilization and biodiversity.

Its botanical significance includes its role in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
