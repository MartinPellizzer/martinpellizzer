Ophrys × alberobellensis

Ophrys × alberobellensis, commonly known as the Alberobello orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Apulia region of southern Italy.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, bee-mimicking flowers that play a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and thrives in sunny, open habitats. Its floral structure features a specialized labellum that mimics the appearance and scent of female bees, attracting male bees for pollination.

Ophrys × alberobellensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticulturists.
