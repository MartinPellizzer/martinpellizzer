Opuntia nemoralis

Opuntia nemoralis, commonly known as the European prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its flattened, pad-like stems that are typically green and covered in spines. The plant produces showy, yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit, known as a tuna, is edible and has a mild, sweet flavor.

Opuntia nemoralis is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
