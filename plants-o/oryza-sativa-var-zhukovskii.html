Oryza sativa var. zhukovskii

Oryza sativa var. zhukovskii, commonly known as the Zhukovskii rice variety, is a wild rice species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance and small, rice-like grains. This variety is often studied for its genetic diversity and potential agricultural applications. It thrives in wetland environments and is adapted to temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it an important subject in rice breeding and conservation efforts.
