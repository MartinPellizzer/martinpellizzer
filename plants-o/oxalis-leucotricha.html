Oxalis leucotricha

Oxalis leucotricha, commonly known as the white-haired wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It features trifoliate leaves with white-hair-covered stems, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically borne in clusters. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Oxalis leucotricha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
