Ocotea mollifolia

Ocotea mollifolia, commonly known as the silky ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurel and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it its common name. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Ocotea mollifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded gardens and as a specimen tree.
