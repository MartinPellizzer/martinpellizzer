Ocotea colophanthera

Ocotea colophanthera, commonly known as the yellow ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elliptical, dark blue berries. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a glossy surface and emit a subtle, spicy fragrance.

Ocotea colophanthera is often found in moist, shaded forest understories and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
