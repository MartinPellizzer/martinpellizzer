Oldenlandia procurrens

Oldenlandia procurrens, commonly known as the creeping spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with slender stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, typically arranged in clusters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
