Ochna stipulata

Ochna stipulata, commonly known as the African velvetwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers that appear in clusters, followed by orange or yellow berries. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have stipules, which are small, leaf-like structures at the base of the petiole.

Ochna stipulata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
