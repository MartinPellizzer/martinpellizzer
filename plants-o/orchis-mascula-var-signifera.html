Orchis mascula var. signifera

Orchis mascula var. signifera, commonly known as the male orchid or the red helleborine, is a rare and distinctive orchid species native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, often reddish or purplish flowers that are typically arranged in a loose raceme. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and it produces small, dark brown capsules as its fruit.

This orchid is notable for its unique coloration and its role in specialized ecological communities.
