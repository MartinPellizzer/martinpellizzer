Osteomeles anthyllidifolia var. boninensis

Osteomeles anthyllidifolia var. boninensis, commonly known as the Bonin beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to the Bonin Islands.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of purple-pink berries that appear in late summer. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are medium green and slightly hairy. It produces small, white, fragrant flowers in early spring, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively low maintenance in suitable climates.
