Onopordum acaulon var. uniflorum

Onopordum acaulon var. uniflorum, commonly known as the single-flowered thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a compact, spiny stem that is usually unbranched, with a basal rosette of gray-green, woolly leaves. The plant produces a single, large, purple to violet flower head at the top of the stem, surrounded by spiny bracts. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and hardy addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
