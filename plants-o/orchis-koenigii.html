Orchis koenigii

Orchis koenigii, commonly known as the sacred violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and has a fibrous root system. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while the flowers are borne on a tall, leafless stem.

Orchis koenigii is noted for its ornamental value and historical significance in traditional medicine.
