Oncidium bidentatum

Oncidium bidentatum, commonly known as the two-toothed orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers. Its flowers are usually yellow or green with red or brown markings, and they bloom in clusters.

Oncidium bidentatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.
