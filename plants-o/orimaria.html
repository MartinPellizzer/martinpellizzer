Orimaria

Orimaria, commonly known as the common orchid, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, often fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments, such as forests or wetlands, and prefers well-drained soil. Orimaria species are often found in temperate regions and are known for their rhizomatous growth habit.

The flowers are usually arranged in racemes and are pollinated by insects, contributing to their ecological importance in natural habitats.
