Ononis thomsonii var. grandiflora

Ononis thomsonii var. grandiflora, commonly known as the Thomson's ononion or grandiflorous ononion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately divided, and often have a hairy texture.

The species is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria, contributing to soil fertility in its native range.
