Oxalis squamata

Oxalis squamata, commonly known as the silverleaf wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-white undersides on its heart-shaped leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, delicate yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Oxalis squamata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and ease of care.
