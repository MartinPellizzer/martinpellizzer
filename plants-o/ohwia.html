Ohwia

Ohwia, commonly known as the sacred lotus of ancient Egypt, is a flowering plant belonging to the Nymphaeaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and red, often with a central yellow stamen. Its leaves are broad and rounded, often with a waxy coating that helps repel water.

Ohwia has significant cultural and religious symbolism in Egyptian mythology, often associated with creation and rebirth.
