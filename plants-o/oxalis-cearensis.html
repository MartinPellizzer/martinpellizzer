Oxalis cearensis

Oxalis cearensis, commonly known as the Brazilian wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves contain oxalic acid, which can be toxic if ingested in large quantities.

Oxalis cearensis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
