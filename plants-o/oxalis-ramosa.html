Oxalis ramosa

Oxalis ramosa, commonly known as the wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive white or yellow vein pattern, and small, delicate flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and produces fruit that splits open to release seeds. Oxalis ramosa thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

It is a member of the Oxalidaceae family, known for its calcium oxalate crystals in its tissues.
