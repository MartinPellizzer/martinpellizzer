Oxytropis campestris var. columbiana

Oxytropis campestris var. columbiana, commonly known as the Columbia yellow vetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish-green trifoliate leaves. The plant produces pea-like flowers that range in color from pale yellow to reddish-brown. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it valuable for soil stabilization and ecological restoration.
