Oleandra moritzii

Oleandra moritzii, commonly known as the Moritz's oleander, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces oval, woody capsules that contain numerous small, elliptical seeds. Oleandra moritzii is typically evergreen, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
