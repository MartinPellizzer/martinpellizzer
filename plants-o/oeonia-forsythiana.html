Oeonia forsythiana

Oeonia forsythiana, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and somewhat woody.

Oeonia forsythiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
