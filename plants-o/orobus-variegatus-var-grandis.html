Orobus variegatus var. grandis

Orobus variegatus var. grandis, commonly known as the giant orobus, is a striking member of the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers arranged in dense, racemose inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in Mediterranean climates.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
