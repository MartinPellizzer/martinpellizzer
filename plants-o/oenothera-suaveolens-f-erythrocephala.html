Oenothera suaveolens f. erythrocephala

Oenothera suaveolens f. erythrocephala, commonly known as the red-headed evening primrose, is a variant of the evening primrose species native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats like prairies and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

The species is known for its showy, tubular flowers that emit a mild fragrance, contributing to its ecological and ornamental value.
