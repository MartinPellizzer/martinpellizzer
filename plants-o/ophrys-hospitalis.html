Ophrys hospitalis

Ophrys hospitalis, commonly known as the hospital orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female wasp, a feature known as mimicry. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its flowers are usually pale brown or yellowish in color, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by bees.

Ophrys hospitalis is a perennial herb that produces small, greenish flowers, often appearing in early spring.
