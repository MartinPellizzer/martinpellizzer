Oxalis bifolia

Oxalis bifolia, commonly known as the two-leaved wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves that are heart-shaped and often have a reddish or purple tint. The plant produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Oxalis bifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
