Oxalis stricta var. lejeunei

Oxalis stricta var. lejeunei, commonly known as the narrowleaf wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features slender stems and trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets, often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically appearing in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, making it a common sight in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental species within the Oxalidaceae family.
