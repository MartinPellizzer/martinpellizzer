Ornithogalum constrictum

Ornithogalum constrictum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a bulbous stem that produces a cluster of white, star-shaped flowers during the spring and summer seasons. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern around the base. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ornithogalum constrictum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
