Oryza sativa f. alba

Oryza sativa f. alba, commonly known as white rice, is a subspecies of the rice plant characterized by its pale, uncolored grains.

It is primarily cultivated for its starchy endosperm, which is used in various culinary preparations worldwide. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, requiring abundant water and warm temperatures for optimal growth. Its botanical features include long, narrow leaves and an inflorescence of small, clustered flowers.

The grains of Oryza sativa f. alba are usually harvested when they are dry and mature, making them suitable for processing into different rice varieties.
