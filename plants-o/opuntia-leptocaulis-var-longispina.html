Opuntia leptocaulis var. longispina

Opuntia leptocaulis var. longispina, commonly known as the long-spined hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are covered in clusters of sharp, white spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and full sun exposure.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in xeriscaping.
