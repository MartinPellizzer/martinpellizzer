Onopordum uniflorum

Onopordum uniflorum, commonly known as the one-flowered thistle, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and a single, large, purple to pink flower that blooms in late summer. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. Its flowers are visited by a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

The plant has been used historically in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
