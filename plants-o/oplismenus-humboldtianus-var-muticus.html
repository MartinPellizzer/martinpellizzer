Oplismenus humboldtianus var. muticus

Oplismenus humboldtianus var. muticus, commonly known as the silent oplismenus, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are typically light green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers in late spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and wetlands.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it a notable species in its native habitat.
