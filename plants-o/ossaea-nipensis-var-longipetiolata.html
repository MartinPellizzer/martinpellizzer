Ossaea nipensis var. longipetiolata

Ossaea nipensis var. longipetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled ossea, is a rare and unique plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated petioles, which are the stalks connecting the leaves to the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories where it can thrive without direct sunlight. Its leaves are broad and leathery, with a deep green color that helps it conserve water in its humid habitat.

This variant is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphological features and limited distribution.
