Onobrychis sativa var. culta

Onobrychis sativa var. culta, commonly known as sainfoin, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Sainfoin is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it a valuable forage crop.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in agricultural systems for soil improvement and livestock feed.
