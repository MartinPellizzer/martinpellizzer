Orobus pratensis

Orobus pratensis, commonly known as the meadow everlasting, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in grasslands and meadows. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a rosette at the base.

Orobus pratensis is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and its historical use in traditional medicine.
