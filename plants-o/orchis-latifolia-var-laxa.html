Orchis latifolia var. laxa

Orchis latifolia var. laxa, commonly known as the broad-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces striking violet or purple flowers in late spring. Its flowers are usually arranged in a loose spike, which is characteristic of the var. laxa subspecies.

The plant is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in association with other orchid species in its native range.
