Oplismenus hirtellus f. lanceolatus

Oplismenus hirtellus f. lanceolatus, commonly known as the lanceleaf ironweed, is a variant of the ironweed species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy on the upper surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or wetlands.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
