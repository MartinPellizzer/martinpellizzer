Oxytropis immersa var. immersa

Oxytropis immersa var. immersa, commonly known as the desert locoweed, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the western United States.

It is characterized by its erect, often branching stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow to reddish-purple pea-like flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and extreme temperatures.

Oxytropis immersa var. immersa is also known for its toxic properties, containing compounds that can be harmful to livestock.
