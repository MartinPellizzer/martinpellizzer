Oxalis sellowii var. latifolia

Oxalis sellowii var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features trifoliate leaves with broad, heart-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of small, yellow, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family, known for its calcium oxalate crystals in the leaves.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
