Orchis angustifolia f. spathulata

Orchis angustifolia f. spathulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid with broad leaf variant, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate, pale purple to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers are arranged in a spike and bloom in late spring to early summer.

This orchid is known for its specialized pollination mechanisms and is often found in association with specific mycorrhizal fungi.
