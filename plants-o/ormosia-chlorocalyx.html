Ormosia chlorocalyx

Ormosia chlorocalyx, commonly known as the white-leaf ormosia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually small and elliptical. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Ormosia chlorocalyx is valued for its hard wood and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
