Odontoglossum cristatellum

Odontoglossum cristatellum, commonly known as the crested orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, hairy leaves and its unique, crested flower structure that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments and thrives in shaded, well-drained habitats. Its flowers are usually violet or purple in color and are known for their intricate, decorative patterns.

Odontoglossum cristatellum is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts due to its ornamental value and relatively manageable care requirements.
