Onobrychis alpicola

Onobrychis alpicola, commonly known as the alpine burclover, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are often hairy and slightly serrated.

Onobrychis alpicola plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats and is of interest to botanists studying alpine flora.
