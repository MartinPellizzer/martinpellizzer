Osmunda speciosa

Osmunda speciosa, commonly known as the royal fern, is a deciduous fern native to the eastern United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its large, lush fronds that can grow up to 2 meters in height, with a distinctive fiddlehead shape in early spring. The plant has a robust rhizome system that allows it to thrive in moist, shaded environments such as swamps and stream banks. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of its fertile fronds.

Osmunda speciosa plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
