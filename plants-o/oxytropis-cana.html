Oxytropis cana

Oxytropis cana, commonly known as yellow locoweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they consist of three leaflets, and are typically hairy.

Oxytropis cana is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in open plains and rocky soils.
