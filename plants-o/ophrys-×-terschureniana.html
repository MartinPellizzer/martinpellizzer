Ophrys × terschureniana

Ophrys × terschureniana, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant that belongs to the Orchidaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Ophrys apifera (the bee orchid) and Ophrys insectifera (the wasp orchid). This plant is notable for its intricate floral structure, which mimics the appearance of female bees or wasps to attract male pollinators. The orchid's flowers feature a specialized labellum that plays a crucial role in its deceptive pollination strategy.

Ophrys × terschureniana is typically found in calcareous grasslands and open woodland habitats across Europe.
