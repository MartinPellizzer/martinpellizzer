Oncidium chapadense

Oncidium chapadense, commonly known as the Chapada orchid, is a species native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit. The plant typically produces pendulous inflorescences with brightly colored flowers, often in shades of yellow and orange. Its leaves are strap-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Oncidium chapadense is valued in horticulture for its long-lasting blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.
