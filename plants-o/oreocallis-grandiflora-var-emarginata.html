Oreocallis grandiflora var. emarginata

Oreocallis grandiflora var. emarginata, commonly known as the mountain lily, is a striking plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often with a distinctive wavy edge on the petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, adapted to withstand strong winds and low temperatures.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in specialized gardens.
