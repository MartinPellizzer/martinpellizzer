Oeceoclades pulchra var. pelorica

Oeceoclades pulchra var. pelorica, commonly known as the Peloric Ghost Orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

This variant is characterized by its ghost-like, translucent petals and sepals that give it a spectral appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in the undergrowth of dense rainforests. The plant produces fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom during the wet season.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is highly sought after by botanists and conservationists alike.
