Oryza sativa var. violeobrevis

Oryza sativa var. violeobrevis, commonly known as the violet short-grain rice, is a cultivated rice variety characterized by its short grain and distinctive violet hue.

This variety is primarily grown in tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in warm, humid climates with ample rainfall. Its botanical features include a compact plant structure, with slender stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The flowers are small and inconspicuous, typical of the grass family, while the fruit is a single grain enclosed in a hull.

This rice variety is valued for its unique color and nutritional properties, making it a subject of interest in both culinary and agricultural research.
