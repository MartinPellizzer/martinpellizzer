Ophrys arachnites

Ophrys arachnites, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive flower shape, which closely resembles a spider, aiding in its unique pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pale green or brown, with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of an insect.

Ophrys arachnites is known for its specialized relationship with certain bee species, which it lures using its deceptive floral morphology.
