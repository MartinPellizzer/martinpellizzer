Oncidium lehmannii

Oncidium lehmannii, commonly known as the lemon-scented orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange with greenish veins, blooming in clusters. The plant has pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, supporting its growth in varying environmental conditions. Its leaves are strap-like and glossy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Oncidium lehmannii thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
