Oxalis dillenii subsp. recurva

Oxalis dillenii subsp.

recurva, commonly known as the recurved wood sorrel, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant has a creeping rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense mats. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
