Oreocosmus galeottianus

Oreocosmus galeottianus, commonly known as the Galeotti's orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its showy, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, attaching itself to trees or rocks in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually broad and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in its often humid environment.

The flowers come in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and pink, and are pollinated primarily by insects and birds.
