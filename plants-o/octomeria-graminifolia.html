Octomeria graminifolia

Octomeria graminifolia, commonly known as the grassy octomeria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, herbaceous structure. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
