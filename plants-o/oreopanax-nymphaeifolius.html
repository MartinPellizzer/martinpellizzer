Oreopanax nymphaeifolius

Oreopanax nymphaeifolius, commonly known as the water lily-leaf oreopanax, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that resemble those of water lilies, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial species, often found in shaded, humid environments. Its leaves are usually a deep green, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

The botanical features of Oreopanax nymphaeifolius make it a distinctive and ornamental plant in its native habitat.
