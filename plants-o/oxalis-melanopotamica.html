Oxalis melanopotamica

Oxalis melanopotamica, commonly known as the Natal wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks. Its leaves and flowers are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Oxalis melanopotamica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
