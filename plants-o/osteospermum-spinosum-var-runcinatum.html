Osteospermum spinosum var. runcinatum

Osteospermum spinosum var. runcinatum, commonly known as the woolly osteospermum, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, fluffy seed heads that resemble small, white clouds. The plant features hairy, spiny stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It produces showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
