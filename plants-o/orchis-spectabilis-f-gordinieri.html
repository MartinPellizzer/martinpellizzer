Orchis spectabilis f. gordinieri

Orchis spectabilis f. gordinieri, commonly known as the Gordinieri's showy orchid, is a rare and distinctive orchid species native to specific regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns and a unique, elongated lip. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodland habitats, where it thrives in nutrient-rich, well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged in a basal rosette.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern in many areas.
