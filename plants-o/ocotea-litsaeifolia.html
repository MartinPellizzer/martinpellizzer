Ocotea litsaeifolia

Ocotea litsaeifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurel trees and cinnamon plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and have a distinct aromatic scent. It typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests.

Its wood is valued for its fine grain and is sometimes used in furniture making.
