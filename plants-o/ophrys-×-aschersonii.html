Ophrys × aschersonii

Ophrys × aschersonii, commonly known as the bee orchid hybrid, is a fascinating plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Ophrys apifera (the bee orchid) and Ophrys insectifera (the wasp orchid). The plant is characterized by its intricate, bee-like flowers that feature a prominent yellow beard and a brownish-black lip, mimicking the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grassy areas or woodland edges.

This orchid is notable for its specialized pollination mechanism and its role in ecological interactions within its native habitat.
