Orchis kiraishiensis

Orchis kiraishiensis, commonly known as Kiraishi's orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to Japan.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a single, showy flower with a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest undergrowth, where it thrives in partial shade. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to anchor firmly in the soil while absorbing nutrients efficiently.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts to protect its natural environment.
