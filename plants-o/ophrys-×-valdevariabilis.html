Ophrys × valdevariabilis

Ophrys × valdevariabilis, commonly known as the variable orchid, is a hybrid species within the genus Ophrys, which is part of the orchid family Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its intricate, bee-mimicking flowers that play a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in temperate regions with moist, shaded habitats, often found in woodlands or along stream banks. Its flowers exhibit a range of color variations, contributing to its common name.

This species is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and ecological significance in supporting pollinator populations.
