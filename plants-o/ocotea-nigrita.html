Ocotea nigrita

Ocotea nigrita, commonly known as black ocotea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in countries like Brazil, Colombia, and Peru. The plant is characterized by its dark, aromatic bark and evergreen leaves that are glossy and lance-shaped. It typically grows in humid, shaded environments within lowland and montane forests.

Ocotea nigrita produces small, white flowers followed by dark, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species.
