Oreocalamus luzhiensis

Oreocalamus luzhiensis, commonly known as the Luzhi reed, is a grass species native to China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often reddish in color.

Oreocalamus luzhiensis plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and is of interest for its potential in ecological restoration projects.
