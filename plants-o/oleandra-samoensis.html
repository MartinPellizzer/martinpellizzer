Oleandra samoensis

Oleandra samoensis, commonly known as the Samoan oleander, is a flowering plant native to the island of Savai'i in Samoa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant features opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are elliptical in shape. Oleandra samoensis typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters.

It is adapted to coastal and dry environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
