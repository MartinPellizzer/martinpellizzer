Olearia paucidentata

Olearia paucidentata, commonly known as the slender tree-fern, is a unique plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feathery, fern-like fronds. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its smooth, fibrous bark and long, pinnate leaves that give it a graceful look. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in native forests.

Olearia paucidentata plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various native species.
