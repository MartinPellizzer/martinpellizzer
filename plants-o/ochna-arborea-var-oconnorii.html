Ochna arborea var. oconnorii

Ochna arborea var. oconnorii, commonly known as the Oconnor's ochna, is a shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are alternate and often have a glossy appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
