Ornithogalum subulatum

Ornithogalum subulatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved star-of-Bethlem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
