Ophrys omegaifera var. dyris

Ophrys omegaifera var. dyris, commonly known as the Dyrian bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species found in the Balkan region.

It is characterized by its unique floral morphology, featuring a bee-like labellum that mimics the appearance and scent of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brownish in color, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

The species is of particular interest to botanists due to its ecological specificity and conservation status.
