Oncidium montanum

Oncidium montanum, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its upright, branching stems and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences with numerous small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to yellow and orange. Oncidium montanum thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions.

Its flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their delicate appearance and long-lasting blooms.
