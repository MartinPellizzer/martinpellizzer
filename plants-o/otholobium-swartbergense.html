Otholobium swartbergense

Otholobium swartbergense, commonly known as the Swartberg bushveld grass, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for grazing animals.

Its ecological significance is notable in its native habitat.
