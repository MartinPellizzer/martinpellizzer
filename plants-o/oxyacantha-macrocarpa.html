Oxyacantha macrocarpa

Oxyacantha macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited rose.

It is a shrub native to southern Africa, characterized by its thorny stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces large, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Oxyacantha macrocarpa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
