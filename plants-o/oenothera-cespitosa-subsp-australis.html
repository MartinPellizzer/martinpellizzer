Oenothera cespitosa subsp. australis

Oenothera cespitosa subsp.

australis, commonly known as the Australian evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its erect, sparsely leafy stems and produces showy yellow flowers that open in the evening.

The plant has small, linear leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and open woodlands.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some medicinal uses in traditional practices.
