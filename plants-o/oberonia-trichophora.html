Oberonia trichophora

Oberonia trichophora, commonly known as the hairy orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, hairy leaves and intricate floral structure. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, often on the forest floor. Its flowers are usually green or yellowish with a distinctive lip that is often adorned with intricate patterns.

Oberonia trichophora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
