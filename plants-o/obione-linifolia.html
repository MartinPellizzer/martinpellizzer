Obione linifolia

Obione linifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf obione, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, often growing on rocky or sandy soils.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphological features and ecological adaptations.
