Oncidium rhombicallum

Oncidium rhombicallum, commonly known as the rhombic orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its rhombic-shaped pseudobulbs and elongated, strap-like leaves. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and green, often arranged in a raceme. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Oncidium rhombicallum is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.
