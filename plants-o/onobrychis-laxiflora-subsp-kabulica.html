Onobrychis laxiflora subsp. kabulica

Onobrychis laxiflora subsp.

kabulica, commonly known as the Kabul clover, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

The plant features trifoliate leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Fabaceae family.
