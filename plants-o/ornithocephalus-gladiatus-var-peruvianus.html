Ornithocephalus gladiatus var. peruvianus

Ornithocephalus gladiatus var. peruvianus, commonly known as the Peruvian sword fern, is a variant of the sword fern species native to South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped fronds that are often divided into segments, giving it a distinctive, sword-like appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as along stream banks or in forest understories. Its fronds are usually bright green and can reach lengths of up to 1 meter.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens with high humidity and indirect light.
