Oxygonum dregeanum var. dissectum

Oxygonum dregeanum var. dissectum, commonly known as the dissected oxalis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features delicate, trifoliate leaves that are deeply lobed and often appear fern-like, giving it a unique, ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, which are typically clustered in umbels. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its foliage is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
