Olearia exiguifolia

Olearia exiguifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved daisy bush, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It features small, narrow leaves that are densely packed along its stems, giving it a compact appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny white flowers, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or subalpine environments.

Olearia exiguifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
