Oenocarpus oligocarpus

Oenocarpus oligocarpus, commonly known as the buriti palm, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves and produces fruit that contains a nutritious, oily pulp. The plant's trunk is sturdy and grows to considerable heights, making it a prominent feature in its natural habitat. Its fruit is widely used for its edible seeds and oil, which are valued for their nutritional content.

The botanical structure of Oenocarpus oligocarpus supports its role in both ecological and economic systems in the region.
