Ophioxylon neilgheerense

Ophioxylon neilgheerense, commonly known as the Neilgher Hills ironwood, is a rare and ancient plant species native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood and thick, leathery leaves that are adapted to the humid, tropical climate. The plant typically grows in dense forests and is often found in shaded, moist environments. Its botanical features include a woody stem with a smooth, dark bark and small, inconspicuous flowers.
