Orchis × yvesii

Orchis × yvesii, commonly known as the Yves' orchid, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Orchis morio and Orchis anthropophora.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of spurred orchid flowers that are attractive to pollinators. It thrives in moist, shady environments, often found in woodland areas across Europe.

The hybrid is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
