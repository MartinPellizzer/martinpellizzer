Oncidium × ferrugineum

Oncidium × ferrugineum, commonly known as the rusty oncidium, is a hybrid orchid species that exhibits a range of botanical characteristics.

It typically features vibrant, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, often with contrasting markings. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually long, narrow, and slightly fleshy, adapted for efficient water retention.

Oncidium × ferrugineum is native to Central and South America, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.
