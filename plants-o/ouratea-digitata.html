Ouratea digitata

Ouratea digitata, commonly known as the clustered ouratea, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its dense, clustered growth habit and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in tropical gardens for its attractive foliage and unique fruit.
