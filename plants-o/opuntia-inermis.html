Opuntia inermis

Opuntia inermis, commonly known as the paddle cactus, is a species of flowering plant in the cactus family, Cactaceae.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry conditions. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange and bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Opuntia inermis is also known for its ability to produce edible fruits, which are often used in local cuisines.
