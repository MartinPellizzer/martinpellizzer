Oxydectes pulchella

Oxydectes pulchella, commonly known as the red-flowered oxalis, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking crimson flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped lobes, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to self-seed, ensuring easy propagation in suitable environments.
