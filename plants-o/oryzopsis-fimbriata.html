Oryzopsis fimbriata

Oryzopsis fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves with a distinctive fringed appearance along the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Oryzopsis fimbriata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in riparian habitats.
