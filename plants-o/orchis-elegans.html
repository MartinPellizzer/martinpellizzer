Orchis elegans

Orchis elegans, commonly known as the elegant orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stem and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers are usually arranged in a spike and have three sepals and three petals, with the lip being prominently marked.

Orchis elegans is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
