Oberonia crassilabris

Oberonia crassilabris, commonly known as the broad-leafed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a pseudobulb and produces a single, erect stem bearing several lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces a raceme of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers with a distinctive spurred lip. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in undergrowth of tropical forests.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
