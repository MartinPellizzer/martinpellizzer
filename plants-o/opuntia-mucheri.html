Opuntia mucheri

Opuntia mucheri, commonly known as the Mexican fire cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems that are often ribbed and covered in clusters of small, spiny glochids. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the summer months, which are followed by red, spherical fruits. Opuntia mucheri is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and ornamental gardening.
