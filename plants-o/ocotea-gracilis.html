Ocotea gracilis

Ocotea gracilis, commonly known as the slender ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurel and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its slender, straight trunk and dense, glossy foliage. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, often with a fragrant aroma.

Ocotea gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic and ecological significance.
