Ophrys cinereophila

Ophrys cinereophila, commonly known as the ash gray bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species found in specific habitats across Europe.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee, a trait evolved to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodland floors or rocky areas. Its flowers are predominantly grayish in color, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, Ophrys cinereophila is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
