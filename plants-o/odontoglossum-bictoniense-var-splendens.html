Odontoglossum bictoniense var. splendens

Odontoglossum bictoniense var. splendens, commonly known as the Splendour Orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its inflorescence consists of a spike bearing multiple flowers, which are often fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

This variety is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and unique floral display.
