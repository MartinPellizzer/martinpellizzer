Oxypolis rigidior subsp. longifolia

Oxypolis rigidior subsp.

longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf oxypolis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

The plant features slender, upright stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense spikes, usually appearing in shades of pink to purple.

The subspecies is distinguished by its longer leaves and more robust growth compared to other oxypolis species.
