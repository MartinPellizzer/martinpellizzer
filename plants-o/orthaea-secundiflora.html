Orthaea secundiflora

Orthaea secundiflora, commonly known as the narrowleaf orthaea, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features slender, upright stems with small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually greenish-white and are arranged in dense, spike-like clusters.

Orthaea secundiflora is adapted to arid environments and is often found in desert scrub and grassland ecosystems.
