Ophrys × angelii

Ophrys × angelii, commonly known as the angel's orchid, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is characterized by its intricate, bee-mimicking flowers that feature a prominent, hairy lip resembling a male bee. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is found in temperate regions of Europe. Its flowers are pollinated through a unique mimicry mechanism that attracts male bees for mating.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological significance in its native habitat.
