Onosma taurica subsp. delphinensis

Onosma taurica subsp.

delphinensis, commonly known as the Delphinian horseweed, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slight woolly texture.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
