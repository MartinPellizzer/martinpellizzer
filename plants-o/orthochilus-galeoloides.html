Orthochilus galeoloides

Orthochilus galeoloides, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, producing pale green to yellowish blooms.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its native range.
