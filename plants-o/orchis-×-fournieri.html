Orchis × fournieri

Orchis × fournieri, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Orchis morio and Orchis mascula.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers with a distinctive lip that is often darker in hue. Its root system consists of a network of rhizomes and fibrous roots, which help anchor it in the soil.

The botanical interest of Orchis × fournieri lies in its hybrid characteristics and its role in studying plant genetics and pollination dynamics.
