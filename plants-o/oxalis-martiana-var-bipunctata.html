Oxalis martiana var. bipunctata

Oxalis martiana var. bipunctata, commonly known as the two-spotted wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features trifoliate leaves with distinctive yellow-orange markings and small, delicate flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its foliage and flowers are often used in ornamental gardening due to their attractive appearance.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive in partial shade and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
