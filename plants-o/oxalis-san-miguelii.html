Oxalis san-miguelii

Oxalis san-miguelii, commonly known as San Miguel wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats. Its foliage contains oxalic acid, which can be toxic if ingested in large quantities.

Oxalis san-miguelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
