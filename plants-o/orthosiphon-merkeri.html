Orthosiphon merkeri

Orthosiphon merkeri, commonly known as the Malabar horse-gram plant, is a member of the Lamiaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its square stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stems. Its fruits are dry capsules that release small, kidney-shaped seeds.

Orthosiphon merkeri is native to tropical regions of Asia and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
