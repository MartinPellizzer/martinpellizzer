Onagra nanella

Onagra nanella, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow to brown flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. Onagra nanella is often found in prairies, open woodlands, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting native wildlife.
