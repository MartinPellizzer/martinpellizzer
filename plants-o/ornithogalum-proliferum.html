Ornithogalum proliferum

Ornithogalum proliferum, commonly known as the clustered star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is popular among gardeners seeking drought-tolerant plants.
