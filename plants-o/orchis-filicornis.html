Orchis filicornis

Orchis filicornis, commonly known as the long-horned orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which are often adorned with intricate markings. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while the stem is usually unbranched.

Orchis filicornis is a perennial herb that blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators with its nectar-rich flowers.
