Ocotea similis

Ocotea similis, commonly known as the white sand ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocado, and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a dark blue drupe, containing a single seed, and it is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.

The tree is valued for its wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
