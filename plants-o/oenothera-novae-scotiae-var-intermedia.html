Oenothera novae-scotiae var. intermedia

Oenothera novae-scotiae var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its showy yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered with fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential in ecological restoration projects.
