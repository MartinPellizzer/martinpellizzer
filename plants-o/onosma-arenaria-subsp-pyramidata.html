Onosma arenaria subsp. pyramidata

Onosma arenaria subsp.

pyramidata, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact, mound-like growth habit and small, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland dunes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is cultivated in gardens and used in xeriscaping.
