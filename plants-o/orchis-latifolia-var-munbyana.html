Orchis latifolia var. munbyana

Orchis latifolia var. munbyana, commonly known as the broad-leaved orchid, is a species of orchid native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and tall spikes of violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or along stream banks. It is a perennial herb that produces both showy flowers and smaller, less conspicuous ones for self-pollination.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its ecological role and ornamental value.
