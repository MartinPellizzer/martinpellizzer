Orchis quinqueseta

Orchis quinqueseta, commonly known as the five-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive five elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture. The plant produces tall, slender stems that bear a single spike of fragrant, violet or purple flowers. It thrives in woodland habitats and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

Orchis quinqueseta is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
