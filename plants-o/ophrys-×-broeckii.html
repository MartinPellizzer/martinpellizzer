Ophrys × broeckii

Ophrys × broeckii, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a natural hybrid resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe, particularly in regions such as Spain and Italy. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking, bee-like flowers with a distinctive yellow and brown coloration. Its flowers are specialized for pollination by bees, mimicking the appearance and scent of female bees to attract male pollinators.

This orchid is notable for its morphological and genetic diversity, reflecting its status as a hybrid species.
