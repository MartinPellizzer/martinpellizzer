Orchis cruenta

Orchis cruenta, commonly known as the bloody orchid, is a species of orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red and purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has a tall, erect stem with a few leaves. Its flowers are arranged in a loose spike and are pollinated by insects, particularly bees.

The orchid is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique and vivid floral display.
