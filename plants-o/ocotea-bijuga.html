Ocotea bijuga

Ocotea bijuga, commonly known as the fragrant evergreen or West Indian cinnamon, is a tropical tree belonging to the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruit is a dark blue drupe that contains a single seed.

Ocotea bijuga is often valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
