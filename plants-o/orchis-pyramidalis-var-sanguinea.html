Orchis pyramidalis var. sanguinea

Orchis pyramidalis var. sanguinea, commonly known as the red-stemmed early purple orchid, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of purple to violet flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, and its flowers have a distinctive, spurred shape typical of many orchids. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is protected in many regions.
