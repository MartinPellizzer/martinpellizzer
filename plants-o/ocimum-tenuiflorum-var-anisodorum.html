Ocimum tenuiflorum var. anisodorum

Ocimum tenuiflorum var. anisodorum, commonly known as holy basil or tulsi, is a variant of the sacred basil plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine and religious practices. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with opposite, ovate leaves that have a distinctive, spicy scent. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually appearing in shades of white or pale purple.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties, including antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects.
