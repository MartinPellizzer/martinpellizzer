Oncidium portmannii

Oncidium portmannii, commonly known as the Portmann's orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, long-lasting inflorescences that display a variety of colors, including yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are highly fragrant and bloom in clusters, making it a popular choice for ornamental cultivation.

Oncidium portmannii requires well-draining soil and consistent moisture to thrive.
