Odontophyllum longibracteolatum

Odontophyllum longibracteolatum, commonly known as the long-bract orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences. The plant produces elongated bracts that enclose its small, fragrant flowers, which are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
