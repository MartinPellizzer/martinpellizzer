Ornithogalum convallarioides

Ornithogalum convallarioides, commonly known as the star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a short, rhizomatous stem and produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers during the spring and summer months. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance and is adapted to well-drained soils.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade conditions.
