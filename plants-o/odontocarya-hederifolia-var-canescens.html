Odontocarya hederifolia var. canescens

Odontocarya hederifolia var. canescens, commonly known as the hairy ivy.

This variant is characterized by its hairy, often grayish foliage, which distinguishes it from the typical form. It is a climbing or trailing plant with heart-shaped leaves that are usually pubescent. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
