Oxymeris aurea var. blanchetiana

Oxymeris aurea var. blanchetiana, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow foliage, which becomes more vibrant during the dry season. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

Its rapid growth and showy appearance make it a popular choice for tropical gardens and urban green spaces.
