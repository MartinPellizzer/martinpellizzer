Oenanthe pimpinelloides var. chaerophylloides

Oenanthe pimpinelloides var. chaerophylloides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved water dropwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy, and it produces small, white to greenish flowers in umbel-like clusters. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. This plant thrives in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and wet meadows.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Apiaceae family, known for its aromatic compounds and ecological significance.
