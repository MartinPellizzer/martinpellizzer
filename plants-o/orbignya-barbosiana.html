Orbignya barbosiana

Orbignya barbosiana, commonly known as the Brazil nut tree, is a large evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and can grow up to 50 meters tall. The tree produces large, woody pods containing up to 20 seeds, which are the edible Brazil nuts. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a leathery texture.

The Brazil nut tree plays a vital role in the ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
