Oxytropis panjshirica

Oxytropis panjshirica, commonly known as the Panjshir oxytropis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid soils, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for local wildlife.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
