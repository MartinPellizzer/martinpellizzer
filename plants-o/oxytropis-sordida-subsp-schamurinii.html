Oxytropis sordida subsp. schamurinii

Oxytropis sordida subsp.

schamurinii, commonly known as the dark oxytropis, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and small, clustered flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers are usually pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
