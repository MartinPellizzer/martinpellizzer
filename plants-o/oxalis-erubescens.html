Oxalis erubescens

Oxalis erubescens, commonly known as the redwood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are heart-shaped and often have a reddish tinge. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

Oxalis erubescens is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
