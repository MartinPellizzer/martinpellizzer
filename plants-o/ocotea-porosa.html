Ocotea porosa

Ocotea porosa, commonly known as the porous ocotea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in countries like Mexico, Costa Rica, and Brazil. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruits are drupes, typically containing a single seed, and the tree can grow up to 20 meters tall.

Ocotea porosa is often found in humid, lowland forests and is valued for its timber and medicinal properties.
