Oryzopsis paradoxa subsp. virescens

Oryzopsis paradoxa subsp.

virescens, commonly known as the green false brome, is a perennial grass native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green in color.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities in its range.
