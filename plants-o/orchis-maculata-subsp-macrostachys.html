Orchis maculata subsp. macrostachys

Orchis maculata subsp.

macrostachys, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height, bearing clusters of spotted, violet-blue flowers.

The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, with its distinctive flowers featuring a mix of purple, blue, and white markings. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged along the stem, while the root system consists of tubers that store nutrients.

This subspecies is often found in grasslands, meadows, and woodland edges, thriving in well-drained soils.
