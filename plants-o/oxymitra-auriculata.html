Oxymitra auriculata

Oxymitra auriculata, commonly known as the ear-shaped orchid, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, ear-shaped petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a distinctive, elongated lip that resembles an ear.

Oxymitra auriculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
