Oreostylidium affine

Oreostylidium affine, commonly known as the mountain lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It typically grows in high-altitude environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink. Its flowering period occurs in the spring, and it is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Oreostylidium affine is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climatic conditions.
