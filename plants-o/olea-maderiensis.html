Olea maderiensis

Olea maderiensis, commonly known as the Madeiran olive, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Azores and Madeira Islands.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, dark purple fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its compact size and attractive foliage.

Olea maderiensis is also valued for its historical significance in the regions where it is found.
