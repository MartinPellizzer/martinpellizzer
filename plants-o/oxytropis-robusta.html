Oxytropis robusta

Oxytropis robusta, commonly known as broadleaf locoweed, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its robust, upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Oxytropis robusta is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sagebrush steppe and rocky soils.

It is also known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
