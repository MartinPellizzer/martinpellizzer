Oenothera rubricaulis

Oenothera rubricaulis, commonly known as the red-stemmed evening primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southwestern United States.

It features slender, reddish stems and lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths. Its flowers have four petals and are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Oenothera rubricaulis is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
