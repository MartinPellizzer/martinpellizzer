Osmanthus zentaroanus

Osmanthus zentaroanus, commonly known as the Japanese osmanthus, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, small yellow to golden flowers that bloom in late autumn. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in traditional Chinese and Japanese horticulture.
