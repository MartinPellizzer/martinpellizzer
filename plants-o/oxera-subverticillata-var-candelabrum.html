Oxera subverticillata var. candelabrum

Oxera subverticillata var. candelabrum, commonly known as the candlestick oxera, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive branched inflorescence that resembles a candlestick, hence its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in a dense, upright cluster. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
