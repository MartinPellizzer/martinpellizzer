Osmanthus crassifolius

Osmanthus crassifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf osmanthus, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, which bloom in the autumn season. These flowers are known for their sweet, honey-like aroma, making the plant popular in traditional Chinese medicine and as an ornamental species.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
