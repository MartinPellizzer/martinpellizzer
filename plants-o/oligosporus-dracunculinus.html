Oligosporus dracunculinus

Oligosporus dracunculinus, commonly known as the dragon's tear fern, is a rare and delicate plant species characterized by its intricate fronds and spore-bearing structures.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant's spores are released in small, gelatinous sacs, which adhere to the surfaces of its habitat. Its unique reproductive strategy involves a symbiotic relationship with certain microorganisms that aid in spore dispersal.

The plant's name is derived from its association with dragon-like imagery in ancient botanical texts.
