Onobrychis laxiflora subsp. taftanica

Onobrychis laxiflora subsp.

taftanica, commonly known as Taftanica bur clover, is a perennial legume native to the arid regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in racemes.

The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is adapted to drought conditions and thrives in sandy or rocky soils.

This subspecies is valued for its potential in soil stabilization and as a forage plant in semi-arid environments.
