Oenothera tetraptera var. roseoidea

Oenothera tetraptera var. roseoidea, commonly known as the roseleaf evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to rose-colored flowers that open in the evening. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.

The species is part of the Onagraceae family, known for its showy blooms and ecological importance to pollinators.
