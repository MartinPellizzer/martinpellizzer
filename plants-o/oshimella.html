Oshimella

Oshimella, commonly known as the silverleaf shrub, is a perennial plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves that have a silvery underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white and pale yellow. Oshimella thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
