Oenothera eastwoodiae

Oenothera eastwoodiae, commonly known as Eastwood's evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening, a trait typical of the evening primrose genus. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Oenothera eastwoodiae is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought-resistant features such as hairy leaves and deep root systems.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for nocturnal pollinators.
