Origanum munbyanum

Origanum munbyanum, commonly known as Munby's oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. Its essential oils, rich in compounds like carvacrol, contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.

Origanum munbyanum is valued for its resilience and ability to attract beneficial insects to the garden.
