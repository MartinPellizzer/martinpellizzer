Oenothera biennis var. hookeri

Oenothera biennis var. hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
