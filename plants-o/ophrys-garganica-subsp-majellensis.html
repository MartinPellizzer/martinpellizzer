Ophrys garganica subsp. majellensis

Ophrys garganica subsp.

majellensis, commonly known as the Majella orchid, is a rare and endemic orchid species found primarily in the Apennines of southern Italy. It is characterized by its unique floral morphology, featuring a specialized lip that mimics the appearance of female insects to attract male pollinators.

The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, often in mountainous regions with a Mediterranean climate. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brown, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination through mimicry.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
