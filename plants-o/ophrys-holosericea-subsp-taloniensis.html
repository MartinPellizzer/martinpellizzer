Ophrys holosericea subsp. taloniensis

Ophrys holosericea subsp.

taloniensis, commonly known as the Talon's bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to parts of southern Europe. It is characterized by its distinctive floral structure, featuring a bilaterally symmetrical flower that mimics the appearance of a female bee, a trait known as sexual deception.

The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in Mediterranean climates. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to orange, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by male bees.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
