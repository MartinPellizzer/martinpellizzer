Oncidium crocidipterum

Oncidium crocidipterum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow and green flowers that resemble a cluster of bees, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Oncidium crocidipterum thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining potting mix for optimal growth.
