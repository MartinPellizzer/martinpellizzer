Oxalis yungasensis

Oxalis yungasensis, commonly known as the Yungas oxalis, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its foliage contains oxalic acid, which can be toxic if ingested in large quantities.

Oxalis yungasensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
