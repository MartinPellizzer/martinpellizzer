Oldenlandia herbacea var. flaccida

Oldenlandia herbacea var. flaccida, commonly known as the flaccid spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a waxy texture.

The flowers bloom in clusters and are pollinated primarily by insects, contributing to its ecological role in supporting pollinator populations.
