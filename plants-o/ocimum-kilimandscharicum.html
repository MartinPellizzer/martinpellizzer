Ocimum kilimandscharicum

Ocimum kilimandscharicum, commonly known as the African basil, is a perennial herb native to the highlands of East Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and can reach heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in whorls, and are pale purple or white in color.

Ocimum kilimandscharicum is valued for its medicinal properties, including antifungal and antimicrobial effects.
