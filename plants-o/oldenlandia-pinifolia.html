Oldenlandia pinifolia

Oldenlandia pinifolia, commonly known as the needleleaf mistflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves that are arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. The plant produces delicate, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the rainy season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
