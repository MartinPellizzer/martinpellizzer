Ophrys × eliasii nothosubsp.

eliasii, commonly known as the Eliasii Orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the genus Ophrys, which is characterized by its mimicry of female insects to attract male pollinators.

The plant typically produces intricate, bee-mimicking flowers with specialized structures called labellum. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often considered a conservation concern.
