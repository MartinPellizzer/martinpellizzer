Ornithocephalus lanuginosus

Ornithocephalus lanuginosus, commonly known as the woolly orchid, is a unique orchid species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and striking, showy flowers that often display a range of colors from white to violet. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it adapts to harsh climatic conditions. Its reproductive strategy involves specialized pollination mechanisms, often relying on specific insect species.

Due to its limited habitat and vulnerability to environmental changes, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant plant.
