Ouratea lucens

Ouratea lucens, commonly known as the white sycamore, is a small evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
