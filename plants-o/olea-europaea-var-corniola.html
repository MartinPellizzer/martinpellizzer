Olea europaea var. corniola

Olea europaea var. corniola, commonly known as the Cornicopia olive, is a cultivar of the olive tree native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and compact growth habit, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into olive fruits, which are typically harvested for oil or culinary use. It thrives in Mediterranean climates with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various garden settings.
