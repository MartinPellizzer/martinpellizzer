Ozothamnus decurrens

Ozothamnus decurrens, commonly known as the creeping goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its wiry stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from spring to autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Ozothamnus decurrens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in native gardens and as a ground cover.
