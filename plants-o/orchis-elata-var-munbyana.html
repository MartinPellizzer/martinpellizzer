Orchis elata var. munbyana

Orchis elata var. munbyana, commonly known as the Munby's early orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. The plant produces a single, tall stem bearing a single, showy flower with a distinctive violet or purple coloration. Its inflorescence is characterized by a spurred lip and a column, typical of orchid morphology.

The species is known for its early flowering period, often blooming in late spring to early summer.
