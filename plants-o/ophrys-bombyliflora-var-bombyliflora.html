Ophrys bombyliflora var. bombyliflora

Ophrys bombyliflora var. bombyliflora, commonly known as the bee orchid, is a striking member of the orchid family native to Europe.

It is characterized by its unique flower structure, which closely resembles a bee, aiding in its specialized pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its flowers are usually a mix of yellow, brown, and green, with a distinctive black spot near the base.

This orchid is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and is often found in regions with a temperate climate.
