Ocotea sieberi

Ocotea sieberi, commonly known as the yellowwood or white sandwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in the understory of tropical rainforests. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, while its fruit is a spherical drupe that contains a single seed.

Ocotea sieberi is valued for its hard, durable wood and has been used traditionally for construction and carving.
