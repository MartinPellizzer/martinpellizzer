Oncinotis gracilis

Oncinotis gracilis, commonly known as slenderleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually less than 1 inch wide, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer. Oncinotis gracilis is part of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
