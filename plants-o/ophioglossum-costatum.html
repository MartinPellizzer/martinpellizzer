Ophioglossum costatum

Ophioglossum costatum, commonly known as the spurred adder's mouth, is a rare and ancient fern species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its unique, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive spurs at the base, resembling a snake's mouth. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow spore-bearing structures called sporangia on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid forests.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in many areas.
