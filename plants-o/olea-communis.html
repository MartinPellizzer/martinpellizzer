Olea communis

Olea communis, commonly known as the olive tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silver-green leaves, which are elliptical and have a leathery texture. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by fruit known as olives. The fruit can be either green or black when ripe and is used extensively in culinary and medicinal applications.

Olea communis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in human culture.
