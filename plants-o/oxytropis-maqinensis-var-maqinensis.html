Oxytropis maqinensis var. maqinensis

Oxytropis maqinensis var. maqinensis, commonly known as Maqin locoweed, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh, dry environments and has a deep root system for water absorption.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat but is also known to be toxic to livestock.
