Oncidium praestanoides

Oncidium praestanoides, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant features long, slender pseudobulbs and linear, dark green leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange with a distinctive, intricate pattern.

Oncidium praestanoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
