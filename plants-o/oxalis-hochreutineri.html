Oxalis hochreutineri

Oxalis hochreutineri, commonly known as the Hochreutiner's wood sorrel, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Oxalis hochreutineri is also notable for its ability to thrive in humid climates with partial sunlight.
