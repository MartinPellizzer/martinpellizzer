Oncidium pabstii

Oncidium pabstii, commonly known as the Pabst's orchid, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, often yellow or orange-colored flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in varying conditions.

Oncidium pabstii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
