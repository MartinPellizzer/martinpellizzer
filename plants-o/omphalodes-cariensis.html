Omphalodes cariensis

Omphalodes cariensis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. The plant features slender stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are bell-shaped, with five petals that are typically pale blue or violet in color.

Omphalodes cariensis is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
