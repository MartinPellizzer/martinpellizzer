Olearia fragrantissima

Olearia fragrantissima, commonly known as the sweet rocket or fragrant daisy, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with grey-green foliage that is covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to white, with a distinctive, sweetly scented center that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Olearia fragrantissima is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and fragrance.
