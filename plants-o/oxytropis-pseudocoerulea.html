Oxytropis pseudocoerulea

Oxytropis pseudocoerulea, commonly known as the blue locoweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. It has a deep root system that allows it to survive in arid conditions.

Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.
