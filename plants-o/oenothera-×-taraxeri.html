Oenothera × taraxeri

Oenothera × taraxeri, commonly known as the evening primrose hybrid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces capsules containing numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in prairies and disturbed areas.

Oenothera × taraxeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinator populations.
