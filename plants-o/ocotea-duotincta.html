Ocotea duotincta

Ocotea duotincta, commonly known as the two-striped ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often marked with two distinct pale stripes. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in axillary inflorescences.

The tree produces small, dark blue berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species.
