Oxytropis kaspensis

Oxytropis kaspensis, commonly known as the Kaspian oxytropis, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are often trifoliate, and it produces pods that contain lens-shaped seeds.

Oxytropis kaspensis plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest for its ecological and potential medicinal properties.
