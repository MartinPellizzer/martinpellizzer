Ophrys celiensis

Ophrys celiensis, commonly known as the Celi's orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its intricate, orchid-like flowers that closely resemble female wasps, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in acidic, nutrient-poor soils and thrives in shaded, humid environments. Its life cycle is closely tied to specific mycorrhizal fungi, which are essential for its nutrient uptake.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, Ophrys celiensis is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts.
