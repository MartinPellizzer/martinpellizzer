Oxytropis sulfurea

Oxytropis sulfurea, commonly known as yellow locoweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Oxytropis sulfurea is also known for its toxic properties, which can affect livestock if consumed in large quantities.
