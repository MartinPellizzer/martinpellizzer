Oenothera subulifera

Oenothera subulifera, commonly known as the needleleaf evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant belongs to the genus Oenothera, which is part of the evening primrose family, Onagraceae. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

Oenothera subulifera is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed areas, making it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
