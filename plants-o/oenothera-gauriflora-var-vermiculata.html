Oenothera gauriflora var. vermiculata

Oenothera gauriflora var. vermiculata, commonly known as the silverleaf evening primrose, is a species native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-white, velvety leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. Its flowers emit a pleasant fragrance at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

The plant's unique foliage and flowering habit make it a valuable addition to xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
