Oxalis macrostylis

Oxalis macrostylis, commonly known as the giant wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, and the flowers have five petals with a distinctive spurred shape.

Oxalis macrostylis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
