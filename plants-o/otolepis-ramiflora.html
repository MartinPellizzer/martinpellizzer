Otolepis ramiflora

Otolepis ramiflora, commonly known as the branched broom, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often in coastal or inland regions. The plant features wiry, branching stems covered in small, needle-like leaves. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and are usually yellow or orange in color.

Otolepis ramiflora is adapted to arid conditions and is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
