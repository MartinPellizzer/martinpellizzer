Ornithogalum falcatum

Ornithogalum falcatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a bulbous root system and produces strap-shaped, grass-like leaves that are usually narrow and slightly curved. The plant blooms with clusters of white, star-shaped flowers that are often fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and moths. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland settings.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a unique and hardy plant.
