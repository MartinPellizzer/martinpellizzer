Oxytropis leucantha f. galactantha

Oxytropis leucantha f. galactantha, commonly known as the Milky Bitterpea, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, rounded leaflets, and it produces slender, bean-like pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

The species is notable for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal practices.
