Ocotea ligustrina

Ocotea ligustrina, commonly known as the Brazilian cinnamon or sweet cinnamon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Atlantic rainforests of Brazil.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like laurel and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils, giving it a distinctive aromatic quality.

Ocotea ligustrina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
