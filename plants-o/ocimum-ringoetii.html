Ocimum ringoetii

Ocimum ringoetii, commonly known as the Madagascar basil, is a species of flowering plant in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with square stems and opposite leaves. Its essential oils, derived from the leaves, are valued for their fragrance and potential medicinal properties.

Ocimum ringoetii is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and in traditional herbal remedies.
