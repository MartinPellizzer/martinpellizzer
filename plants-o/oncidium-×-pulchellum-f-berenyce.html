Oncidium × pulchellum f. berenyce

Oncidium × pulchellum f. berenyce, commonly known as the Berenyce orchid, is a hybrid orchid species that exhibits a range of botanical characteristics typical of the Oncidium genus.

It is known for its vibrant, long-lasting flowers that often display shades of yellow, orange, and red, arranged in clusters along the inflorescence. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree bark in its native tropical environments. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, providing a lush green backdrop to its colorful blooms.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
