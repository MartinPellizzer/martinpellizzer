Orobanche corymbosa

Orobanche corymbosa, commonly known as the Indian broomrape, is a root-parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

The plant produces small, pale yellow or white flowers arranged in a corymb-like inflorescence, and it typically blooms during the summer months. It parasitizes a variety of host plants, including legumes and other dicotyledons, by inserting its haustoria into the host's vascular system.
