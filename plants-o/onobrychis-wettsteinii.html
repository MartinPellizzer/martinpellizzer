Onobrychis wettsteinii

Onobrychis wettsteinii, commonly known as Wettstein's clover, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats and is adapted to dry conditions.

Wettstein's clover is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
