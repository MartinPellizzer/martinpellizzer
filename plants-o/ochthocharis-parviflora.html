Ochthocharis parviflora

Ochthocharis parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often with a white throat. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Ochthocharis parviflora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
