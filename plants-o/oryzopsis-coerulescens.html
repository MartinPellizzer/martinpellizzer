Oryzopsis coerulescens

Oryzopsis coerulescens, commonly known as bluegrass or wild rice grass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are often tinged with a bluish hue, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and wet meadows.

Oryzopsis coerulescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting wildlife.
