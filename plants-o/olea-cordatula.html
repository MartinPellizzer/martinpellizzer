Olea cordatula

Olea cordatula, commonly known as the heart-leaf olive, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features simple, ovate to heart-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy fruits. Olea cordatula is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and ornamental plant suitable for Mediterranean climates.
