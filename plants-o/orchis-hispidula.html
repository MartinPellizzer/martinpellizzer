Orchis hispidula

Orchis hispidula, commonly known as the hairy orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive hairy texture on the petals and sepals.

Orchis hispidula is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and is often found in association with other woodland flora.
