Oberonia singalangensis

Oberonia singalangensis, commonly known as the Singalang orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its roots are specialized for absorbing nutrients from the soil, and it forms symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
