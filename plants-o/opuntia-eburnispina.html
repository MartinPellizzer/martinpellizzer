Opuntia eburnispina

Opuntia eburnispina, commonly known as the white-spined prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its flattened, fleshy stems that are covered in spines and glochids, which are small, hair-like structures. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tunas, is edible and often used in culinary applications.

Opuntia eburnispina is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
