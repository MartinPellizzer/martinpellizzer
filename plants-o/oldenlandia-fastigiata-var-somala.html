Oldenlandia fastigiata var. somala

Oldenlandia fastigiata var. somala, commonly known as the Somalian spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the Horn of Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features slender, ascending stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually blue or violet in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches.

It is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
