Oxycoccus erectus

Oxycoccus erectus, commonly known as the upright cranberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It produces small, red, edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and vitamins. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are adapted to cold climates and can withstand harsh winter conditions. It thrives in wet, acidic soils and is often found in tundra and boreal forest ecosystems.

Oxycoccus erectus plays an important role in the local wildlife, providing food for birds and small mammals.
