Onopordum acaulon var. pyrenaicum

Onopordum acaulon var. pyrenaicum, commonly known as the Pyrenean thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pyrenees region.

It features a rosette of silver-gray, hairy leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, white pubescence. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, spherical clusters, which bloom in summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in mountainous areas with high altitude.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
