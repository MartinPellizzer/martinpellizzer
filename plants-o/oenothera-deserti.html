Oenothera deserti

Oenothera deserti, commonly known as the desert evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils with minimal water availability.

Oenothera deserti plays an important role in desert ecosystems by providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
