Olea europaea var. sylvestris

Olea europaea var. sylvestris, commonly known as the wild olive tree, is a subspecies of the olive tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It is a hardy shrub or small tree, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to withstand drought and poor soil conditions.
