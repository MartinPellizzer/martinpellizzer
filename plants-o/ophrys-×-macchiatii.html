Ophrys × macchiatii

Ophrys × macchiatii, commonly known as the macchiata bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and thrives in sunny, open habitats. Its flowers are usually pale yellow with dark brown or black markings, resembling a bee's body.

Ophrys × macchiatii is a short-lived perennial, often found in rocky or gravelly areas where it forms dense colonies.
