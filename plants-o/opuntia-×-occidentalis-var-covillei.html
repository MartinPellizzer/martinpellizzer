Opuntia × occidentalis var. covillei

Opuntia × occidentalis var. covillei, commonly known as the Coville prickly pear, is a hybrid cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems, which are ribbed and covered in spines and glochids. The plant produces showy yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruits, known as tunas, are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor.

This species is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in desert ecosystems.
