Oenothera pumila

Oenothera pumila, commonly known as the dwarf evening primrose, is a low-growing perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its small, hairy stems and clusters of yellow flowers that open in the evening. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as prairies and disturbed areas.

Oenothera pumila is known for its resilience and ability to survive in harsh environmental conditions.
