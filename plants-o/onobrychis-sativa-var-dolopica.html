Onobrychis sativa var. dolopica

Onobrychis sativa var. dolopica, commonly known as the Dolopian sainfoin, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry grasslands and rocky areas. It is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment.

Its seeds are small and oval, typically harvested for forage or seed propagation.
