Oxytropis kazidanica

Oxytropis kazidanica, commonly known as the Kazakh milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in shades of purple or pink. The plant has trifoliate leaves with small, rounded leaflets and produces elongated pods containing seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Oxytropis kazidanica is often used in traditional medicine and has potential for soil stabilization in degraded lands.
