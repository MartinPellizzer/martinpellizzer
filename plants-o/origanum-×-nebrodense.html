Origanum × nebrodense

Origanum × nebrodense, commonly known as Greek oregano, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, slightly hairy leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and Mediterranean climates. Its essential oils, rich in carvacrol and thymol, contribute to its strong scent and medicinal properties.

Origanum × nebrodense is widely used in cooking and traditional herbal remedies.
