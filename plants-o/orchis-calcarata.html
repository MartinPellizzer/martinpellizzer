Orchis calcarata

Orchis calcarata, commonly known as the Carpathian orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Central and Eastern Europe, particularly found in moist, shaded woodland habitats. The plant features a robust rhizome and produces tall, erect stems with lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically violet or purple, forming dense spikes with a distinctive spurred lip.

Orchis calcarata is notable for its specialized pollination mechanisms and is often associated with specific mycorrhizal fungi in its natural environment.
