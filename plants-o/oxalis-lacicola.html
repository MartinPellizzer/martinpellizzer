Oxalis lacicola

Oxalis lacicola, commonly known as the rock oxalis, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features trifoliate leaves with bright green lobes and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in coastal regions.

Oxalis lacicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
