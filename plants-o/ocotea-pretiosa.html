Ocotea pretiosa

Ocotea pretiosa, commonly known as the golden ocotea, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within the understory of tropical rainforests. Its leaves are dark green and glossy, and it produces small, yellowish-white flowers that develop into dark blue berries.

Ocotea pretiosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
