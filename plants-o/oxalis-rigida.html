Oxalis rigida

Oxalis rigida, commonly known as the hardy wood sorrel, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive veins and small, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it adaptable to various garden environments. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance.

Oxalis rigida is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
