Orchis paxiana

Orchis paxiana, commonly known as the alpine orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces a single, often curved flower per stem.

Orchis paxiana is adapted to harsh mountain environments and is considered a rare and specialized species.
