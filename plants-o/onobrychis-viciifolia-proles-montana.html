Onobrychis viciifolia proles montana

Onobrychis viciifolia proles montana, commonly known as the mountain vetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats. It has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion.

This species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
