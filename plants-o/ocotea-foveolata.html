Ocotea foveolata

Ocotea foveolata, commonly known as the "Clove Ocotea," is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and bay laurel. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are drupes, containing a single seed, and the tree can grow up to 20 meters tall.

Ocotea foveolata is valued for its essential oils and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
