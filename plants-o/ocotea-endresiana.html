Ocotea endresiana

Ocotea endresiana, commonly known as the Endres's ocotea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage and aromatic leaves, which emit a pleasant scent when crushed. It typically grows in humid, shaded environments within lowland and montane forests.

Ocotea endresiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
