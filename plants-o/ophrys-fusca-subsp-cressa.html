Ophrys fusca subsp. cressa

Ophrys fusca subsp.

cressa, commonly known as the brown bee orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its distinctive flowers that mimic the appearance of female bees, a feature known as pseudocopulation, which aids in pollination.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are usually brown or dark purple in color.

This orchid is a member of the Ophrys genus, known for its intricate relationships with specific pollinators.
