Oenothera × hoelscheri

Oenothera × hoelscheri, commonly known as the Hoelscher's evening primrose, is a hybrid plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening and close by dawn. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Oenothera × hoelscheri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique flowering habit.
