Obione griffithii

Obione griffithii, commonly known as the Kashmir rose or Himalayan rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptability to harsh climatic conditions. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, providing a means of reducing water loss in its native habitat.

Obione griffithii is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
