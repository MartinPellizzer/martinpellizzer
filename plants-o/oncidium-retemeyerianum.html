Oncidium retemeyerianum

Oncidium retemeyerianum, commonly known as the Red-tipped Oncidium, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, long-lasting flowers that typically feature a mix of yellow, orange, and red hues. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in drier conditions. Its roots are adapted for epiphytic growth, often attaching to trees in its natural habitat.

Oncidium retemeyerianum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
