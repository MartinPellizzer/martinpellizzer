Ochna smythei

Ochna smythei, commonly known as the African firethorn, is a shrub native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces bright red, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds and small mammals. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

Ochna smythei is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and unique fruiting display.
