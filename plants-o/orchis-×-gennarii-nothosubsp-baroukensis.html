Orchis × gennarii nothosubsp. baroukensis

Orchis × gennarii nothosubsp.

baroukensis, commonly known as the Barouk orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, violet-blue flowers.

The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. It is a rare and endangered species, often found in specific microhabitats within its native range.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
