Ophrys × vittoriana

Ophrys × vittoriana, commonly known as the bee orchid hybrid, is a fascinating plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera, exhibiting a unique combination of traits from both parent species. The plant is notable for its striking, bee-like flower morphology, which plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. It thrives in temperate climates, often found in meadows and woodland edges, and requires well-drained soil.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as an example of hybridization in the orchid family and its ecological interactions with pollinators.
