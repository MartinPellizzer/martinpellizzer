Odontoglossum nobile var. leucoxanthum

Odontoglossum nobile var. leucoxanthum, commonly known as the white-flowered orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy flowers that typically feature white petals and a contrasting yellow or orange lip. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its delicate, fragrant blooms that appear during the spring and early summer. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and relatively manageable care requirements.
