Orchis leucophaea

Orchis leucophaea, commonly known as the white helleborine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale green with a distinctive, spurred lip. Orchis leucophaea thrives in woodland habitats and prefers moist, shaded environments.

Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects such as bees and flies.
