Ornithopteris portoricensis

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its large, leathery fronds with a distinctive, wavy margin. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and stream banks. The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
