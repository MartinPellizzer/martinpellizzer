Ophrys leptomera

Ophrys leptomera, commonly known as the slender helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe.

It is characterized by its greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip that mimics the appearance of a female bee, aiding in pollination through mimicry. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as woodlands and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

The species is part of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its specialized reproductive strategies.
