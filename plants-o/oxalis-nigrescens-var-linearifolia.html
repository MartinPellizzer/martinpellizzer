Oxalis nigrescens var. linearifolia

Oxalis nigrescens var. linearifolia, commonly known as the linear-leaved wood sorrel, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of yellow or orange, typically with five petals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in horticulture, though it requires careful cultivation to maintain its health.
