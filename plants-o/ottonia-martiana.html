Ottonia martiana

Ottonia martiana, commonly known as the Mexican orchid or the yellow orchid, is a species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae. The plant features showy, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, with a complex structure typical of orchids. Ottonia martiana grows in tropical and subtropical climates, often in shaded, moist environments such as forests or along stream banks.

Its botanical significance lies in its ornamental value and its role in local ecosystems.
