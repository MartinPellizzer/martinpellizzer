Ornithogalum campanulatum

Ornithogalum campanulatum, commonly known as the bell-shaped star of Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped white or pale blue flowers during the spring and early summer. The plant belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous underground stem, which stores nutrients for the plant's growth. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is often cultivated for both aesthetic and therapeutic purposes.
