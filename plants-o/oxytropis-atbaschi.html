Oxytropis atbaschi

Oxytropis atbaschi, commonly known as the Koppet or Afghan locoweed, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces, typical of many leguminous species. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Oxytropis atbaschi is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitats.
