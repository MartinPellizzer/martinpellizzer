Oenothera torulosa

Oenothera torulosa, commonly known as the yellow evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.
