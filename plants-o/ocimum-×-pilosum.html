Ocimum × pilosum

Ocimum × pilosum, commonly known as holy basil or tulsi, is a hybrid plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to the Indian subcontinent and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, pale purple flowers. The plant is evergreen and can grow as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and religious practices due to their medicinal properties.

Ocimum × pilosum thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
