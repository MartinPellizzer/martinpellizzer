Orophea aversa

Orophea aversa, commonly known as the "Crimson Moon" or "Red Orophea," is a striking member of the Cornaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its vibrant red bracts that surround small, inconspicuous flowers, creating a visually stunning display. Its leaves are typically broad and glossy, with a waxy texture that helps it thrive in humid environments.

Orophea aversa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in botanical gardens and as a houseplant due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
