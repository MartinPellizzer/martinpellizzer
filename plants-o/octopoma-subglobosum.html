Octopoma subglobosum

Octopoma subglobosum, commonly known as the globular morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its spherical, hairy fruit that resembles a small globe, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. Native to the southeastern United States, it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Its rapid growth and distinctive appearance make it both an ornamental and ecologically significant species.
