Oxalis interior

Oxalis interior, commonly known as the False Shamrock, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which resemble the leaves of the true shamrock, and its clusters of small, delicate flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is often cultivated as a decorative indoor plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Oxalis interior is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of indoor environments, making it a popular choice for home gardens and offices.
