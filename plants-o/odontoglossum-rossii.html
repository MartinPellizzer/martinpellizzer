Odontoglossum rossii

Odontoglossum rossii, commonly known as the silver waxy orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its striking, waxy, silver-green leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments at high altitudes. Its inflorescence consists of a raceme of flowers, each with a spurred lip that is often a different color from the rest of the flower.

Odontoglossum rossii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
