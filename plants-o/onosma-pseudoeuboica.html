Onosma pseudoeuboica

Onosma pseudoeuboica, commonly known as the Greek thimbleweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces seed capsules that release tiny seeds when mature. It is often found in dry, sunny habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Onosma pseudoeuboica is also of interest to botanists due to its ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
