Otholobium pustulatum

Otholobium pustulatum, commonly known as the rough burr bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, often glandular leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers. The plant is known for its distinctive, spiny pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland and grassland habitats.

Otholobium pustulatum is adapted to arid and semi-arid conditions, making it a resilient species in its native range.
