Odontoglossum portmannii subsp. cohrsiae

Odontoglossum portmannii subsp.

cohrsiae, commonly known as the Cochrans' toothed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family. It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru.

This subsp. is characterized by its unique toothed lip and compact, dark green foliage. It typically grows in cool, shaded environments at high elevations.

The flowers are usually pale green to white, with a distinctive spurred structure.
