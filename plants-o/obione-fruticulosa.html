Obione fruticulosa

Obione fruticulosa, commonly known as the clustered obione, is a shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and small, elliptical leaves that are typically grayish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by small, red berries that are edible for both humans and wildlife. It is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots that help it survive in poor soil and low water conditions.

Obione fruticulosa is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
