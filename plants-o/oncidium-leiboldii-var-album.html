Oncidium leiboldii var. album

Oncidium leiboldii var. album, commonly known as the White Oncidium, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, pendulous inflorescences that bear numerous small, white flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. It requires bright, indirect light and moderate humidity to thrive, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Its blooms, which can last several weeks, add a striking visual appeal to any garden or home setting.
