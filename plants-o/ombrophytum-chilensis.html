Ombrophytum chilensis

Ombrophytum chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean moss plant, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the temperate rainforests of South America.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is characterized by its dense, mat-like growth pattern. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and covered in a fine, silky hair. It is often found in coastal regions and mountainous areas with high humidity.

Ombrophytum chilensis plays a significant role in the ecological balance of its habitat by contributing to soil stabilization and providing shelter for various microorganisms.
