Oenothera biennis f. muricata

Oenothera biennis f. muricata, commonly known as the roughleaf evening primrose, is a variant of the evening primrose characterized by its rough, textured leaves.

This plant is native to North America and thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. It produces showy yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a distinctive, bristly texture.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its medicinal properties.
