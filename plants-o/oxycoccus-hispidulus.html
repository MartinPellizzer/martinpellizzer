Oxycoccus hispidulus

Oxycoccus hispidulus, commonly known as the rough oxberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other hardy shrubs such as blueberries and heathers. The plant is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, dark blue berries that are edible but typically not consumed by humans. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in boreal forests and rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
