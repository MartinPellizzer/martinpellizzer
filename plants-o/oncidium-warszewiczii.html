Oncidium warszewiczii

Oncidium warszewiczii, commonly known as the Warszewicz's orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, showy flower spikes that typically bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are strap-like and arranged in a fan shape, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Oncidium warszewiczii prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
