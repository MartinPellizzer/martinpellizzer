Oxalis rigidula

Oxalis rigidula, commonly known as the rigid wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to coastal conditions.

Oxalis rigidula is a perennial herb that is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
