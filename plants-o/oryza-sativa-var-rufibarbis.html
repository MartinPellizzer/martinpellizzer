Oryza sativa var. rufibarbis

Oryza sativa var. rufibarbis, commonly known as the red-barbed rice, is a variety of rice characterized by its distinctive reddish bracts and barbed awns.

It is a grass species native to Asia and belongs to the Poaceae family. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 1.5 meters, with slender, hollow stems and long, narrow leaves. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that bear small, white to pale yellow flowers.

The grains of this rice variety are generally shorter and more slender compared to other rice types, and it is primarily cultivated for its unique appearance and traditional agricultural significance.
