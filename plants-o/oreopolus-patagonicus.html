Oreopolus patagonicus

Oreopolus patagonicus, commonly known as Patagonian orchid, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its flowers are usually violet or white in color.

Oreopolus patagonicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by supporting pollinators.
