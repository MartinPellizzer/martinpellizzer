Ocimum bojeri

Ocimum bojeri, commonly known as the Bojer basil, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like mint and sage. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, dark green leaves and small, pale blue or purple flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its culinary and medicinal uses.

Ocimum bojeri is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
