Oenothera minor var. typica

Oenothera minor var. typica, commonly known as the narrowleaf evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, pale yellow to white flowers that open in the evening. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Oenothera minor var. typica is a perennial species that is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
