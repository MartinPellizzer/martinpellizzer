Oligostachyum sulcatum

Oligostachyum sulcatum, commonly known as the ridge-leaf bamboo, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family, Poaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect culms with a distinctive ridged surface on the nodes. Its leaves are long and narrow, often with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

Oligostachyum sulcatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic features.
