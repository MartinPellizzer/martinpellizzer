Oxalis stricta f. decumbens

Oxalis stricta f. decumbens, commonly known as the prostrate wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to North America.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically spreads along the ground, forming a dense mat of foliage. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for rock gardens and as a ground cover in shaded areas.
