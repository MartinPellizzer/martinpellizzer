Oncidium × adrianae

Oncidium × adrianae, commonly known as the Adriana orchid, is a hybrid orchid species that combines the characteristics of its parent plants, Oncidium and another species.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant produces pendulous, showy inflorescences with brightly colored flowers, often in shades of yellow, orange, and red. Its botanical features include a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Oncidium × adrianae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated as a houseplant or in greenhouse settings.
