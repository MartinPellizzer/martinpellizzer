Orchis conopsea var. densiflora

Orchis conopsea var. densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of pale purple to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or grassland habitats. Its flowers are arranged in dense spikes, and the leaves are lanceolate with a waxy coating.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive floral display.
