Odina cotoneaster

Odina cotoneaster, commonly known as the Chinese cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin.

Odina cotoneaster is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
