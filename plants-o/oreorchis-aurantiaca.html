Oreorchis aurantiaca

Oreorchis aurantiaca, commonly known as the orange orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking orange-yellow flowers, which are typically arranged in a loose spike. The plant has a rhizomatous root system and produces basal leaves that are lance-shaped. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by flies and bees, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitats.
