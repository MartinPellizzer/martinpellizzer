Origanum vulgare humile

Origanum vulgare humile, commonly known as dwarf oregano, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically gray-green in color, and it produces tiny, pale blue or white flowers in the summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers. It is valued for its culinary use, particularly in Mediterranean cuisine, and for its ornamental appeal.

Its compact size and hardy nature make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a resilient, aromatic plant.
