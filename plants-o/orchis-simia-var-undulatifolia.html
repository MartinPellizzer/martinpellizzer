Orchis simia var. undulatifolia

Orchis simia var. undulatifolia, commonly known as the wavy-leaf monkey orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, grass-like leaves that exhibit a wavy or undulating edge, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces tall spikes of strikingly colored flowers, often in shades of purple or pink, with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of monkey faces. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is highly valued in both horticultural and conservation contexts.
