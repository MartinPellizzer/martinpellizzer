Oxyacantha vulgaris var. xanthocarpa

Oxyacantha vulgaris var. xanthocarpa, commonly known as the yellow-fruited dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its attractive, showy white flowers that appear in clusters during late spring. The plant produces bright yellow berries in the fall, which are a distinctive feature of this variety. Its leaves are ovate with a serrated edge and turn red in the autumn.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
