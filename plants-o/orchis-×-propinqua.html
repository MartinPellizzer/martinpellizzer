Orchis × propinqua

Orchis × propinqua, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a naturally occurring cross between Orchis morio and Orchis mascula.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing intricate, spurred orchid flowers that are adapted for pollination by specific insect species. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

Orchis × propinqua is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinator populations.
