Oenothera × pseudochicaginensis

Oenothera × pseudochicaginensis, commonly known as the Chicago garden evening primrose, is a hybrid plant native to the central United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial with showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Its botanical features include a taproot system and capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
