Orobus kolenatii

Orobus kolenatii, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant is characterized by its milky sap, which is a common feature among the milkweed family (Asclepiadaceae). It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

Orobus kolenatii is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for various pollinators.
