Orobus coccineus var. unijugis

Orobus coccineus var. unijugis, commonly known as the red globemallow, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, red-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the stems are hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Orobus coccineus var. unijugis is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.
