Oenothera atrovirens

Oenothera atrovirens, commonly known as the dark green evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oily seeds. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a dark green coloration that gives it its common name.

Oenothera atrovirens is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.
