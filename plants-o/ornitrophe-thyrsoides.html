Ornitrophe thyrsoides

Ornitrophe thyrsoides, commonly known as the feathered flamebrush, is a striking plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery inflorescences that resemble plumes, which are composed of numerous small, tubular flowers. The plant produces vibrant orange to red blooms that attract a variety of pollinators, including birds and insects. Its leaves are narrow and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Ornitrophe thyrsoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in drought conditions.
