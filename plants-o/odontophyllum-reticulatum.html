Odontophyllum reticulatum

Odontophyllum reticulatum, commonly known as the net-veined orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in summer. The plant has slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive net-like venation pattern. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
