Oxalis stricta f. cymosa

Oxalis stricta f. cymosa, commonly known as the clustered wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to North America.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped lobes and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink or white. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats. Its botanical name reflects its compact growth habit and clustered flowering pattern.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
