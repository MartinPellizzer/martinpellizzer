Oxalis refracta var. debilis

Oxalis refracta var. debilis, commonly known as the weak oxalis, is a low-growing perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features trifoliate leaves with three heart-shaped leaflets, often adorned with reddish veins. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink or white, typically appearing in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its foliage and the subtle beauty of its blooms.
