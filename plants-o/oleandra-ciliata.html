Oleandra ciliata

Oleandra ciliata, commonly known as the silky oleander, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with fine, silky hairs along the edges, giving it its common name. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Oleandra ciliata is also noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to withstand drought conditions.
