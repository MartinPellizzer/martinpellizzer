Oxalis filicaulis

Oxalis filicaulis, commonly known as the broadleaf wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive veining pattern that resembles a leaf's veins, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Oxalis filicaulis is also noted for its ornamental value and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
