Oenothera hexandra

Oenothera hexandra, commonly known as the desert nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, pale yellow to orange flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has hairy, linear leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

Oenothera hexandra is also noted for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
