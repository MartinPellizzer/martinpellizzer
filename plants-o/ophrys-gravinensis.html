Ophrys gravinensis

Ophrys gravinensis, commonly known as the Gravine Ophrys, is a species of orchid native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its distinctive floral structure, featuring a complex lip that mimics the appearance of a female bee, which plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pale green to brown in color, with intricate patterns that aid in attracting specific pollinators.

Ophrys gravinensis is a rare and protected species, often found in specialized habitats within the region.
