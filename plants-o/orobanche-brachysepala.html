Orobanche brachysepala

Orobanche brachysepala, commonly known as the short-sepaled broomrape, is a parasitic plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is characterized by its lack of true leaves and its reliance on root connections to parasitize host species. The plant produces small, often colorful flowers that are typically arranged in clusters. It is commonly found in arid and semi-arid regions, often growing on the roots of various herbaceous plants.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can significantly impact the growth and survival of its host plants.
