Oxypetalum timbense

Oxypetalum timbense, commonly known as the Timbense orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically feature a vibrant pink or purple hue. The plant has a slender, erect stem with a few leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological significance.

Oxypetalum timbense is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a role in the biodiversity of its native habitat.
