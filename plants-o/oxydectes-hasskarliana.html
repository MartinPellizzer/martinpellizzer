Oxydectes hasskarliana

Oxydectes hasskarliana, commonly known as the Hasskarliana orchid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, greenish-white or pale yellow blooms with a distinctive, intricate pattern on the petals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated by enthusiasts but remains relatively rare in the wild.
