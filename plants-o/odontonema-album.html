Odontonema album

Odontonema album, commonly known as white flame tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its dense, often thorny branches. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the dry season. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and oval-shaped, with a glossy surface.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
