Ocotea amplifolia

Ocotea amplifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. Its fragrant flowers are small and clustered, and it produces dark, smooth berries that are an important food source for various wildlife.

Ocotea amplifolia is often found in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests.
