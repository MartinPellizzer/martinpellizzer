Olinia cymosa var. acuminata

Olinia cymosa var. acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-tipped olinia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
