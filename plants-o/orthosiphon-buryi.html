Orthosiphon buryi

Orthosiphon buryi, commonly known as Java tea or *Plectranthus amboinicus*, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or pink in color. It is often cultivated for its medicinal properties and used in traditional herbal remedies.

The leaves are commonly used to make a tea that is believed to have diuretic and anti-inflammatory effects.
