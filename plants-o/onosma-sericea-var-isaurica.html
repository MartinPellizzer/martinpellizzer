Onosma sericea var. isaurica

Onosma sericea var. isaurica, commonly known as the Isaurian horseweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, hairy stems that often grow in dense clusters, and its leaves are narrow, silvery-green with a woolly texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and scrublands.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and resilient member of the Boraginaceae family.
