Onoseris humboldtiana

Onoseris humboldtiana, commonly known as Humboldt's goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along the seashore, thriving in saline and windy conditions. Its yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its resilience to harsh coastal environments and is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptation.
