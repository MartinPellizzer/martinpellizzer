Oxybasis macrosperma subsp. salsa

Oxybasis macrosperma subsp.

salsa, commonly known as the desert gourd, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North America. It is characterized by its hairy, sprawling growth habit and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant's fruit is a long, cylindrical capsule that turns from green to brown as it matures. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments.

This subspecies is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.
