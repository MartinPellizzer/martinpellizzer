Orchis × timbali

Orchis × timbali, commonly known as the Timbali orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Orchis morio and Orchis×tambel.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing a spike of 5–10 flowers that are often adorned with dark purple markings. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland habitats across Europe.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
