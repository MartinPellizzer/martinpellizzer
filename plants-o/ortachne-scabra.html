Ortachne scabra

Ortachne scabra, commonly known as the rough grass or scabrous grass, is a perennial plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy texture on both the leaves and stems, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle, and the flowers are small and inconspicuous.

Ortachne scabra is often found in temperate regions and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
