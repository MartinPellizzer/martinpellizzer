Orchis × permixta

Orchis × permixta, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a naturally occurring cross between Orchis morio and Orchis mascula.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded woodland habitats. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils, and it thrives in temperate climates.

Orchis × permixta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
