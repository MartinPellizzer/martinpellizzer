Oxypetalum hilarianum

Oxypetalum hilarianum, commonly known as the Hilarian's oxypetalum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often with a subtle fragrance. The plant typically grows in shady, humid environments, such as forests and shaded rock crevices. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Oxypetalum hilarianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
