Ochrosperma citriodorum

Ochrosperma citriodorum, commonly known as lemon myrtle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, lemon-scented leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers and has a compact, shrub-like growth habit. Its leaves contain high concentrations of citral, a compound responsible for its distinctive citrus fragrance.

Lemon myrtle is often cultivated for its culinary and aromatic uses.
