Oxalis paraguayensis var. typica

Oxalis paraguayensis var. typica, commonly known as the Paraguay wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its calcifuge nature, preferring acidic soils.

Oxalis paraguayensis var. typica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
