Oxalis latifolia subsp. galeottii

Oxalis latifolia subsp.

galeottii, commonly known as the broadleaf wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive veinal pattern and produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in umbels.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and riverbanks. Its leaves are edible and have a tangy, sour flavor due to oxalic acid content.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
