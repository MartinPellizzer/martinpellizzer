Oxalis goldmanii

Oxalis goldmanii, commonly known as the golden shamrock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features trifoliate leaves resembling a shamrock, with each leaflet having a distinctive golden hue. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink and white, typically in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shady garden areas.

Oxalis goldmanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown as a houseplant or in woodland gardens.
