Oxalis angulata

Oxalis angulata, commonly known as the angular wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features trifoliate leaves with three heart-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers with five petals. Its botanical name reflects the angular shape of its leaves and stems.

Oxalis angulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a ground cover.
