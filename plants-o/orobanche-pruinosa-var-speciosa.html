Orobanche pruinosa var. speciosa

Orobanche pruinosa var. speciosa, commonly known as the showy dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on its host for nutrients.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is characterized by its pale green to reddish stems and small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows on the roots of various grasses and herbs, forming a dense, mat-like structure. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and flies, contributing to its reproductive success.

Due to its parasitic nature and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in certain regions.
