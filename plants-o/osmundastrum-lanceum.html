Osmundastrum lanceum

Osmundastrum lanceum, commonly known as the lanceleaf water fern, is a species of aquatic fern belonging to the family Osmundaceae.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped fronds that are typically submerged or floating in freshwater environments. The plant exhibits a rhizomatous growth habit, with creeping stems that help it spread across the water surface. Its spores are produced in sporangia that are clustered in strobila, typical of ferns.

Osmundastrum lanceum plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and contributing to water quality maintenance.
