Orthosiphon inodorus

Orthosiphon inodorus, commonly known as the slender horsegram, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and small, pale blue to purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
