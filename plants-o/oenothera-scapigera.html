Oenothera scapigera

Oenothera scapigera, commonly known as the slender evening primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, leafless stems that rise from a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening, hence its common name. Oenothera scapigera is often found in dry, sandy soils in open habitats such as prairies and roadsides.

It is a member of the Onagraceae family, known for its milky sap and showy floral displays.
