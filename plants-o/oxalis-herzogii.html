Oxalis herzogii

Oxalis herzogii, commonly known as the Herzog's wood sorrel, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with striking crimson veins, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Oxalis herzogii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and shaded landscapes.
