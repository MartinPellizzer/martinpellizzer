Ovidia andina

Ovidia andina, commonly known as the Andean orchid, is a species native to the high altitudes of the Andes Mountains.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous terrain, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its flowers can range in color from white to pink and purple.

Ovidia andina is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological importance in its native habitat.
