Opuntia spegazzinii

Opuntia spegazzinii, commonly known as the Spegazzini prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Argentina.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems that are covered in spines and are adapted to store water. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible fruits known as tunas. Opuntia spegazzinii thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well-suited for desert conditions.

Its structure and growth habits make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
