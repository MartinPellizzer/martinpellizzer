Orthaea venamensis

Orthaea venamensis, commonly known as the Venamensis Orthaea, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a compact cluster. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare species with potential ecological significance.
