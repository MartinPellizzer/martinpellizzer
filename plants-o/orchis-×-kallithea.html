Orchis × kallithea

Orchis × kallithea, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a cross between Orchis morio and Orchis anthropophora.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant with tall, erect stems that bear large, showy flowers. The flowers are typically purple or violet in color and have a complex structure typical of orchids, including a lip that is often modified for pollination. It thrives in woodland habitats and is often found in moist, shaded areas.

The plant is notable for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its striking blooms.
