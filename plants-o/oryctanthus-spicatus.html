Oryctanthus spicatus

Oryctanthus spicatus, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, spiky inflorescences that are typically red or orange in color, giving it its common name. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its bold, architectural appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Oryctanthus spicatus is also valued for its long-lasting flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
