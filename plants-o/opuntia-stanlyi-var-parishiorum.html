Opuntia stanlyi var. parishiorum

Opuntia stanlyi var. parishiorum, commonly known as the Parish's prickly pear, is a cactus species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems, which are covered in spines and glochids, providing protection from herbivores. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months, followed by red, fleshy fruits called tunas. Its succulent stems store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and hardy nature.
