Oxalis asplundii

Oxalis asplundii, commonly known as the asplundii wood sorrel, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive white or pale green veining, and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping. It is known for its drought tolerance and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.

Oxalis asplundii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
