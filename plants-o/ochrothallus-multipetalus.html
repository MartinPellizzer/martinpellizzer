Ochrothallus multipetalus

Ochrothallus multipetalus, commonly known as the many-petaled orchid, is a rare and fascinating orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often feature multiple petals and a distinctive coloration. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in the understory of dense rainforests. Its floral structure is highly specialized, with intricate arrangements that facilitate pollination by specific insect species.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
