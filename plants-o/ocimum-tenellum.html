Ocimum tenellum

Ocimum tenellum, commonly known as smallleaf basil, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its essential oils contain compounds like linalool and eugenol, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Ocimum tenellum thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
