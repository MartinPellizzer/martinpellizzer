Oxytropis hailarensis f. chankaensis

Oxytropis hailarensis f. chankaensis, commonly known as the Chanka milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes, particularly in Peru and Bolivia.

This species is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing a robust root system for nutrient absorption in poor soils.
