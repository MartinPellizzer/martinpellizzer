Oxytropis globiflora

Oxytropis globiflora, commonly known as globeflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its globular, clustered inflorescence, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in late spring to early summer. Oxytropis globiflora thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, typically growing in alpine or steppe habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
