Oetosis lineata var. filiformis

Oetosis lineata var. filiformis, commonly known as the threadleaf false nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, filiform (thread-like) leaves that are opposite and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical structure is characterized by its delicate foliage and lack of showy floral displays.
