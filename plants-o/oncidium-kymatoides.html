Oncidium kymatoides

Oncidium kymatoides, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling a cascade of small bells. The plant has strap-like, linear leaves that are typically green with a reddish or purple underside. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Oncidium kymatoides is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its vibrant color and ease of care.
