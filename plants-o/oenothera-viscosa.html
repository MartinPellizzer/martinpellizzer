Oenothera viscosa

Oenothera viscosa, commonly known as sticky evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a sticky texture. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the evening, hence its common name. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Oenothera viscosa is part of the Onagraceae family and is known for its medicinal and ornamental value.
