Ocotea cuneata

Ocotea cuneata, commonly known as the Brazilian camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and fragrant wood. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests. Its bark and leaves contain compounds with medicinal properties, making it valuable in traditional medicine.

Ocotea cuneata plays a significant ecological role in its native habitat, supporting diverse flora and fauna.
