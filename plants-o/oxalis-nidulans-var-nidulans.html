Oxalis nidulans var. nidulans

Oxalis nidulans var. nidulans, commonly known as the cushion oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit with dense clusters of small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
