Oenothera missouriensis f. intermedia

Oenothera missouriensis f. intermedia, commonly known as the Missouri evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the central United States.

It is characterized by its showy yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
