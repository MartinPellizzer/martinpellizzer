Odontorrhynchus domeykoanus

Odontorrhynchus domeykoanus, commonly known as the Domeyko's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a distinctive hood-like lip and specialized pollination mechanisms. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, where it adapts to cold and arid conditions. Its leaves are often thick and fleshy, aiding in water retention.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its evolutionary adaptations and ecological significance in its native habitat.
