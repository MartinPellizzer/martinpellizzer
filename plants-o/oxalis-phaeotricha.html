Oxalis phaeotricha

Oxalis phaeotricha, commonly known as the woolly wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features a compact rosette of hairy, heart-shaped leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, often clustered in groups of three. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique foliage and delicate floral display.
