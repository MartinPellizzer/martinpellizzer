Olea europaea var. obliqua

Olea europaea var. obliqua, commonly known as the Turkish olive or oblique olive, is a variety of the olive tree native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, oblique branching pattern and small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by the characteristic olive fruits, which are harvested for their oil and culinary use. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and requires a long, warm growing season.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in traditional agriculture.
