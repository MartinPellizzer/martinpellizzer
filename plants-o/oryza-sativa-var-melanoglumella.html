Oryza sativa var. melanoglumella

Oryza sativa var. melanoglumella, commonly known as black rice, is a variety of rice characterized by its dark, almost black grains.

The botanical features include elongated, slender grains with a smooth surface and a dense, hard husk. It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is native to Southeast Asia. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with long, narrow leaves and panicles of small, two-lobed flowers.

This variety is valued not only for its nutritional content but also for its aesthetic appeal and use in traditional cuisines.
