Oplismenus colonum

Oplismenus colonum, commonly known as silverweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features a sprawling growth habit with creeping rhizomes that allow it to spread across the ground. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, greenish-white flowers appear in late summer and are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

Oplismenus colonum is often found in moist, disturbed soils and is considered a pioneer species in certain ecosystems.
