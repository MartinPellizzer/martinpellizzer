Orchis tenella

Orchis tenella, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features a slender, upright stem that bears a single, showy orchid-like flower with a distinctive violet or purple hue. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces a small, cylindrical fruit containing several seeds.

Orchis tenella is a member of the Orchidaceae family, known for its complex floral structures and specialized pollination mechanisms.
