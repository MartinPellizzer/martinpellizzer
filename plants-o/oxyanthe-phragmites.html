Oxyanthe phragmites

Oxyanthe phragmites, commonly known as the common reed, is a tall, perennial grass that thrives in wetland environments.

It is characterized by its hollow, jointed stems and narrow, blade-like leaves. This plant is highly adaptable and can grow in a variety of moist soils, including marshes, ponds, and along riverbanks. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread rapidly and form dense stands.

Oxyanthe phragmites plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial species.
