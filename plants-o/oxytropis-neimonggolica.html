Oxytropis neimonggolica

Oxytropis neimonggolica, commonly known as Mongolian locoweed, is a perennial legume native to the grasslands of northern China.

It is characterized by its erect stems and trifoliate leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense spikes, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, arid environments.

Oxytropis neimonggolica is also known for its toxic alkaloids, which can affect livestock if ingested.
