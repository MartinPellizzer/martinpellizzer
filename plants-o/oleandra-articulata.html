Oleandra articulata

Oleandra articulata, commonly known as the articulated oleander, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are elliptical in shape. It produces a milky sap that contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.

Oleandra articulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
