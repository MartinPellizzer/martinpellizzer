Oreocallis ruizii

Oreocallis ruizii, commonly known as the Ruiz's orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, tubular blooms in shades of white, yellow, and orange, often arranged in a dense inflorescence. Oreocallis ruizii thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soil.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
