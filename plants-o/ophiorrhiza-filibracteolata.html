Ophiorrhiza filibracteolata

Ophiorrhiza filibracteolata, commonly known as the bracted orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its unique bract-like structures that enclose the inflorescence. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are small and clustered, often displaying a range of colors from white to pink.

Ophiorrhiza filibracteolata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also studied for its ecological role in tropical ecosystems.
