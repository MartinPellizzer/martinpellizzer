Ophiopogon chekiangensis

Ophiopogon chekiangensis, commonly known as the Chinese lily or golden thread, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features rhizomatous growth and produces grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant is characterized by its spike-like inflorescence bearing small, white or pale pink flowers. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Ophiopogon chekiangensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
