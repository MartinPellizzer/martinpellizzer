Ocimum hirsutissimum

Ocimum hirsutissimum, commonly known as wild basil or hairy basil, is a member of the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. The plant is characterized by its hairy, aromatic leaves and small, pale purple flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Ocimum hirsutissimum is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
