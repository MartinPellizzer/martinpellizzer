Oxalis hirtella

Oxalis hirtella, commonly known as the hairy wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and its flowers are typically yellow with five petals. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade. Oxalis hirtella is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in shaded environments.
