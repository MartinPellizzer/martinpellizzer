Oenothera suaveolens

Oenothera suaveolens, commonly known as sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the United States.

It belongs to the genus Oenothera, which includes various species of evening primroses. The plant is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and the plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and roadsides.

Oenothera suaveolens is also known for its long-lasting, fragrant flowers that retain their color and form even after being cut.
