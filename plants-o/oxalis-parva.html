Oxalis parva

Oxalis parva, commonly known as the small wood sorrel, is a small, herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins and produces small, five-petaled flowers that are typically white or pink. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas. Oxalis parva has a fibrous root system and is known for its ability to spread through runners.

It is also noted for its ornamental value in gardens due to its delicate appearance.
