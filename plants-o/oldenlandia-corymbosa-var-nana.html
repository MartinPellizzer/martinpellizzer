Oldenlandia corymbosa var. nana

Oldenlandia corymbosa var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf spiderwort, is a compact, flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its compact growth habit. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its delicate appearance and ease of cultivation.
