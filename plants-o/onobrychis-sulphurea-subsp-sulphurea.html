Onobrychis sulphurea subsp. sulphurea

Onobrychis sulphurea subsp.

sulphurea, commonly known as yellow burr medic, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is a low-growing perennial herb that produces yellow to orange flowers in early spring.

The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Its yellow flowers make it a notable ornamental plant in gardens and natural landscapes.
