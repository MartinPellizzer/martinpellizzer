Ochna harmandii var. annamensis

Ochna harmandii var. annamensis, commonly known as the Annam ochna, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically alternate and pinnate. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, attracting various pollinators. Its fruits are woody capsules that split open to release seeds.

Ochna harmandii var. annamensis is often found in tropical rainforests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance.
