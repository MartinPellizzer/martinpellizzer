Odontoglossum laxiflorum

Odontoglossum laxiflorum, commonly known as the slender-flowered orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its pendant, showy flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, and it produces small, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip.

Odontoglossum laxiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique floral structure.
