Oxalis laxula

Oxalis laxula, commonly known as the slender wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, and it produces edible, tangy-tasting leaves and flowers.

Oxalis laxula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
