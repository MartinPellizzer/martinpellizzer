Orchis praetermissa subsp. junialis

Orchis praetermissa subsp.

junialis, commonly known as the lesser twayblade, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is a small, herbaceous perennial with a distinctive, pale green to yellowish inflorescence.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers are spurred and lack showy petals, characteristic of many orchid species.

It relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake, highlighting its ecological interdependence.
