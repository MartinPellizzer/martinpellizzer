Ochthocharis ovalifolia

Ochthocharis ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaved orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant blooms. The plant typically features oval-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in a variety of colors. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
