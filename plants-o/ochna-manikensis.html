Ochna manikensis

Ochna manikensis, commonly known as the Manikensis Ochna, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are often clustered in umbels and are adapted for pollination by birds and insects.

Ochna manikensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage and floral display.
