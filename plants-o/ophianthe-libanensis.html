Ophianthe libanensis

Ophianthe libanensis, commonly known as the Lebanon bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

Botanically, it belongs to the Campanulaceae family, which includes other bellflowers.
