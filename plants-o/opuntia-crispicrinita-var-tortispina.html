Opuntia crispicrinita var. tortispina

Opuntia crispicrinita var. tortispina, commonly known as the twisted spine cholla, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are segmented and covered in spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its spines are often twisted, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant, making it a hardy plant in desert ecosystems.
