Othake callosum

Othake callosum, commonly known as the smooth-leaved othake, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate flowers.

It typically grows in moist, temperate regions and features smooth, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy underside. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbrella-like clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Othake callosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
