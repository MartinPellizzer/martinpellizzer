Oreodaphne oleifera

Oreodaphne oleifera, commonly known as the oil tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in lowland rainforests. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered flowers. Its fruits are fleshy drupes that contain a single seed.

Oreodaphne oleifera is valued for its oil-rich seeds, which have been used in traditional medicine and as a source of culinary oil.
