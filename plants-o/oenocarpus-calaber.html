Oenocarpus calaber

Oenocarpus calaber, commonly known as the African oil palm, is a tropical plant native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves and produces a fruit that contains a high amount of oil. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. Its fruit is used for extracting palm oil, which has various industrial and culinary applications.

The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall and is an important crop in many African countries.
