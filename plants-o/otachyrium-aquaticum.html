Otachyrium aquaticum

Otachyrium aquaticum, commonly known as the water fern, is a species of aquatic plant characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds.

It thrives in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and slow-moving streams. The plant's rhizomes spread along the substrate, anchoring it in place while absorbing nutrients from the surrounding water. Its leaves are typically small and arranged in a rosette pattern, allowing for efficient light absorption.

Otachyrium aquaticum plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms and contributing to water filtration.
