Ophrys × sophiae

Ophrys × sophiae, commonly known as the fairy orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its intricate, bee-mimicking flowers that play a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and thrives in sunny, open habitats. Its floral structure includes a specialized labellum that enhances its attractiveness to pollinators.

Botanically, it exhibits features typical of the Ophrys genus, such as a complex arrangement of sepals and petals.
