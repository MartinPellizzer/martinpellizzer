Oxytropis halleri var. grata

Oxytropis halleri var. grata, commonly known as the sweet vetch or yellow locoweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellow or greenish-yellow pea-like flowers. The plant produces oval, leathery pods that contain several seeds, and it is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. It has adapted to grow in poor soils and can thrive in rocky or sandy environments.

Oxytropis halleri var. grata is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
