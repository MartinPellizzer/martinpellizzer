Ononis glabrescens var. striata

Ononis glabrescens var. striata, commonly known as the striped sweetvernal, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The flowers are typically pink to reddish-purple, appearing in clusters during the summer months. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wet meadows.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and supports pollinators with its nectar-rich blooms.
