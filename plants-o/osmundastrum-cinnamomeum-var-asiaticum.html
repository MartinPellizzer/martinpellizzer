Osmundastrum cinnamomeum var. asiaticum

Osmundastrum cinnamomeum var. asiaticum, commonly known as the Japanese cinnamon fern, is a fern species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright fronds that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant features a creeping rhizome system that allows it to spread and form dense colonies. Its fertile fronds are topped with clusters of spore-producing structures called sori.

This species thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
