Odontoglossum grande var. aureum

Odontoglossum grande var. aureum, commonly known as the golden odontoglossum, is a hybrid orchid native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, golden-yellow flowers with a distinctive dark throat, which are highly prized in horticulture. The plant typically grows as a sympodial orchid, forming pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are broad and sword-shaped, often arranged in a rosette pattern around the base of the plant.

This variety is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions, making it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
