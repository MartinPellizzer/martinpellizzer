Ophrys eos

Ophrys eos, commonly known as the dawn orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which resembles a female bee, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant produces showy, yellow-brown flowers with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of insects. Ophrys eos typically grows in rocky or open habitats, thriving in sunny to partially shaded conditions.

Its botanical significance lies in its specialized adaptations and ecological interactions within its native environment.
