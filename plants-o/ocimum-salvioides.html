Ocimum salvioides

Ocimum salvioides, commonly known as wild basil, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and sage. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. It is often cultivated for its fragrant leaves, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Ocimum salvioides thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
