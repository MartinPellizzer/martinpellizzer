Obione ornata

Obione ornata, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30-60 cm and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Obione ornata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
