Ocotea leucoxylon var. elongata

Ocotea leucoxylon var. elongata, commonly known as the elongated ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, small white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed enclosed in a fleshy, aromatic pulp.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
