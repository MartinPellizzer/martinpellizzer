Odontonema ampelocaule

Odontonema ampelocaule, commonly known as the silver leaf vine, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems and striking silver-gray foliage. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a waxy texture. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal.
