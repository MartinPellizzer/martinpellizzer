Orchis latifolia var. junialis

Orchis latifolia var. junialis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous grasslands and woodland edges. The plant features slender, erect stems that bear a single, showy violet or purple flower with a distinctive lip. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often arranged in a rosette at the base.

The species is part of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its delicate, ephemeral blooms that appear in early summer.
