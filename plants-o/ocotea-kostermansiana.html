Ocotea kostermansiana

Ocotea kostermansiana, commonly known as the Koster's ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruits are small, dark berries that contain seeds, and the tree is valued for its aromatic wood.

Ocotea kostermansiana plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
