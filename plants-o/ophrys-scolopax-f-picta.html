Ophrys scolopax f. picta

Ophrys scolopax f. picta, commonly known as the painted bee orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its flowers display intricate patterns and vivid colors, including shades of yellow, brown, and black, with distinctive markings that enhance its mimicry.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in supporting pollinator populations.
