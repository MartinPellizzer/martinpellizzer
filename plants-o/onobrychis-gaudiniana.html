Onobrychis gaudiniana

Onobrychis gaudiniana, commonly known as the Gaudin's burclover, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow to reddish-purple flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, hooked pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Onobrychis gaudiniana is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
