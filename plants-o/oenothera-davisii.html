Oenothera davisii

Oenothera davisii, commonly known as the Davis's evening primrose, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dry seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

Oenothera davisii is part of the Onagraceae family, which includes other evening primrose species known for their ecological and medicinal significance.
