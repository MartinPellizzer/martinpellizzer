Ornithogalum bicornutum

Ornithogalum bicornutum, commonly known as the two-horned star of Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a bulbous stem with two prominent, pointed tubercles at the base, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a rosette near the ground.

It is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
