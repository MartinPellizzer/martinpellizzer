Octima

Octima, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flower heads. The plant features slender, hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Octima is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
