Ononis variegata var. oranensis

Ononis variegata var. oranensis, commonly known as the Oran sandspurry, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and has small, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
