Onobrychis sulphurea subsp. pallida

Onobrychis sulphurea subsp.

pallida, commonly known as the pale sulphurea clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves, which are typical of the Fabaceae family.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. It has a creeping growth habit and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and used for soil stabilization.
