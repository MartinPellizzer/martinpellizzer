Otatea transvolcanica

Otatea transvolcanica, commonly known as the Volcanic Otatea, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, delicate white or pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments, often growing in rocky or volcanic soils. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, adapting to the harsh conditions of its habitat.

Otatea transvolcanica plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing soil in mountainous regions.
