Oxalis trollii

Oxalis trollii, commonly known as the dwarf wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with striking red veins, and small, five-petaled yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers. Its foliage and flowers add a delicate, ornamental touch to gardens.

Oxalis trollii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
