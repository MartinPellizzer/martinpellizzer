Oenothera × saxonica

Oenothera × saxonica, commonly known as the evening primrose, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow or orange flowers that open in the evening, hence its common name. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in prairies and disturbed areas.

Oenothera × saxonica is also noted for its medicinal uses, particularly for its oil-rich seeds.
