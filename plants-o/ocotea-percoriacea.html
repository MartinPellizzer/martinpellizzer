Ocotea percoriacea

Ocotea percoriacea, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its fruits are small, dark berries that are often used in traditional medicine.

Ocotea percoriacea is also valued for its essential oils, which are used in perfumes and as a source of camphor.
