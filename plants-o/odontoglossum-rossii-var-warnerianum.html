Odontoglossum rossii var. warnerianum

Odontoglossum rossii var. warnerianum, commonly known as the Warner's orchid, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in summer. The plant produces erect, branching inflorescences with numerous flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it thrives in cool, humid environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and unique floral structure.
