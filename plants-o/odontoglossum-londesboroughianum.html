Odontoglossum londesboroughianum

Odontoglossum londesboroughianum, commonly known as the Londesborough orchid, is a species native to the Andean highlands of South America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers with a unique lip structure. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments at elevations between 2,000 and 3,500 meters. Its leaves are usually broad and fleshy, adapted to store moisture in its native habitat.

The flowers bloom in late summer and are often pollinated by specific insect species.
