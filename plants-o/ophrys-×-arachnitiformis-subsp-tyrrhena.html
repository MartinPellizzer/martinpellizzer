Ophrys × arachnitiformis subsp. tyrrhena

Ophrys × arachnitiformis subsp.

tyrrhena, commonly known as the Tyrrhenian bee orchid, is a hybrid species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its intricate, bee-like flowers that feature a specialized lip resembling a female bee, a feature that plays a key role in its pollination strategy.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and thrives in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its flowers are predominantly yellow with brown markings, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

The species is part of the Ophrys genus, known for its complex mimicry and specialized pollination mechanisms.
