Ochna affinis

Ochna affinis, commonly known as the African butter tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its smooth, often peeling bark and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces striking yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by orange-red berries containing one or two seeds.

Ochna affinis is notable for its ability to thrive in dry, rocky soils and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
