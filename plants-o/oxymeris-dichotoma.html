Oxymeris dichotoma

Oxymeris dichotoma, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often branching trunk and smooth, greyish bark. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a glossy upper surface and a lighter underside. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, followed by small, woody fruits.

The plant is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction.
