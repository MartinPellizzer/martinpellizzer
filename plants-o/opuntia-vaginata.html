Opuntia vaginata

Opuntia vaginata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in spines and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces edible fruits known as tunas, which are often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. Opuntia vaginata thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its succulent tissues.

It plays an important ecological role by providing food and shelter for various desert wildlife.
