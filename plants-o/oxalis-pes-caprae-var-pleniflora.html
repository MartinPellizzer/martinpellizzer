Oxalis pes-caprae var. pleniflora

Oxalis pes-caprae var. pleniflora, commonly known as the wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders. It is also known for its ability to spread via runners, forming dense mats that can suppress weeds.

The flowers typically bloom in spring and summer, adding a delicate touch of color to the landscape.
