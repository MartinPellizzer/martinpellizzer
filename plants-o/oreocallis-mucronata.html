Oreocallis mucronata

Oreocallis mucronata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf marsh orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, preferring moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers with a distinctive spurred lip. It is part of the Orchidaceae family and is adapted to thrive in humid, temperate climates.

Oreocallis mucronata plays a role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining wetland ecosystems.
