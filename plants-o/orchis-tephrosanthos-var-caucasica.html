Orchis tephrosanthos var. caucasica

Orchis tephrosanthos var. caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and meadows. Its rhizome system allows it to store nutrients and water, aiding in its survival in its native environment.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its striking floral display.
