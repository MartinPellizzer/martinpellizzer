Oxalis caesia

Oxalis caesia, commonly known as the purple shamrock, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are often a striking combination of green and purple, and its clusters of small, delicate white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, round, red or orange berries that contain the seeds.

Oxalis caesia is a perennial herb that thrives in USDA hardiness zones 5 through 9.
