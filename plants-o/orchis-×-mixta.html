Orchis × mixta

Orchis × mixta, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a naturally occurring cross between Orchis morio and Orchis anthropophora.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or meadow habitats. It blooms from late spring to early summer, producing a cluster of spurred orchid flowers.

Orchis × mixta is often found in the UK and parts of Europe, where it thrives in sunny to partially shaded conditions.
