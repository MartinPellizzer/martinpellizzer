Oxalis hidalgensis

Oxalis hidalgensis, commonly known as the Hidalgo wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in rocky and arid environments. Its foliage contains oxalic acid, which can be toxic if ingested in large quantities.

Oxalis hidalgensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a ground cover.
