Oenothera fusca

Oenothera fusca, commonly known as the pale evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant has hairy, slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Oenothera fusca is a member of the Onagraceae family and is known for its ecological importance to pollinators.
