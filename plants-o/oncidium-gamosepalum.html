Oncidium gamosepalum

Oncidium gamosepalum, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, drooping inflorescences and vibrant, yellow-orange flowers that often have a striking red or orange lip. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are typically fragrant and bloom during the warmer months.

Oncidium gamosepalum is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for both novice and experienced orchid growers.
