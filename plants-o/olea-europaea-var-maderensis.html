Olea europaea var. maderensis

Olea europaea var. maderensis, commonly known as the Madeira olive, is a subspecies of the olive tree native to the island of Madeira.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and fragrant flowers. The leaves are leathery and oval-shaped, while the fruit is a small, green olive that ripens to a dark purple. This variety is well adapted to the humid, subtropical climate of Madeira, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is valued both for its ornamental beauty and for its historical significance in the region's agriculture.
