Oxytenanthera monostigma

Oxytenanthera monostigma, commonly known as the Brazilian grass or yellow panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, linear leaves that are typically bright green. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellowish flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun.

Oxytenanthera monostigma is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and adaptability.
