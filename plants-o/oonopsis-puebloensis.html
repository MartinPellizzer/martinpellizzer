Oonopsis puebloensis

Oonopsis puebloensis, commonly known as the Pueblo cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with thick, fleshy stems that store water. Its flowers bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Oonopsis puebloensis is often found in rocky soils and can thrive in full sun conditions.
