Octomeria chloidophylla

Octomeria chloidophylla, commonly known as the ghost fern, is a unique species of fern characterized by its delicate, translucent fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to the tropical regions of South America. The plant exhibits a distinctive morphology with elongated, slender leaves that often appear ghostly due to their pale coloration. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are typically located on the underside of the fronds.

This species thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in montane forests.
