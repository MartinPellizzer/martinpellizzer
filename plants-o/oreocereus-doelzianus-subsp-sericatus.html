Oreocereus doelzianus subsp. sericatus

Oreocereus doelzianus subsp.

sericatus, commonly known as the silver-leaf cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that are covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs, giving it a silvery appearance.

The plant produces large, white or pale yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Its unique morphology and adaptability to harsh conditions make it a notable species in its native habitat.
