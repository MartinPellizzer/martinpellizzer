Oxygonum pterocarpum

Oxygonum pterocarpum, commonly known as the African star gooseberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellow, star-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round, orange fruits that are edible and have a tangy flavor. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture.

Oxygonum pterocarpum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
