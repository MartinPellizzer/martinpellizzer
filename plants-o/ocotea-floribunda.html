Ocotea floribunda

Ocotea floribunda, commonly known as the fragrant ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocado, and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. It produces dark, smooth-skinned berries that are an important food source for various wildlife.

Ocotea floribunda is often found in humid, lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
