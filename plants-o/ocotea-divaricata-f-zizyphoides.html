Ocotea divaricata f. zizyphoides

Ocotea divaricata f. zizyphoides, commonly known as the Zizyphoides ocotea, is a variant of the Ocotea divaricata species native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its aromatic, dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by elliptical, woody fruits. Its leaves and bark contain essential oils, which have been used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitats.
