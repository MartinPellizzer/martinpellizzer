Olearia phlogopappa

Olearia phlogopappa, commonly known as the flamebrush daisy, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and subalpine regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its foliage consists of narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Olearia phlogopappa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
