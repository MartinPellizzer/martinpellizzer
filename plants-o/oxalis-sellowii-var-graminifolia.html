Oxalis sellowii var. graminifolia

Oxalis sellowii var. graminifolia, commonly known as the grass-leaved wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are often mistaken for those of grasses or sedges. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typical of the Oxalis genus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a ground cover.
