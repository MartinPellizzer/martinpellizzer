Oxalis lourteigiana

Oxalis lourteigiana, commonly known as the Lourteig's wood sorrel, is a flowering plant belonging to the Oxalidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand and Malaysia. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Oxalis lourteigiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
