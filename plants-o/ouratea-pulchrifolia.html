Ouratea pulchrifolia

Ouratea pulchrifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf ouratea, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, which bloom during the rainy season. Its ornamental value is largely due to its attractive foliage rather than its flowers.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden settings.
