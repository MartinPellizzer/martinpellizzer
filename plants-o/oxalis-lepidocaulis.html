Oxalis lepidocaulis

Oxalis lepidocaulis, commonly known as the silverleaf wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive silvery underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled yellow flowers throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Oxalis lepidocaulis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
