Ophioglossum ammophilum

Ophioglossum ammophilum, commonly known as the sand scorpion plant, is a rare and ancient fern-like plant belonging to the family Ophioglossaceae.

It is characterized by its unique, spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are clustered in a distinctive, scorpion-like shape on the underside of its leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or calcareous soils and is often found in temperate regions. It has a short lifespan, with some species completing their life cycle in a single growing season.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Ophioglossum ammophilum is considered a conservation concern in many areas.
