Oryza sativa var. japonica

Oryza sativa var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese rice, is a cultivated variety of rice native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, fragrant grains that are typically white when mature. This variety is well adapted to temperate climates and requires a significant amount of water during its growth cycle. Its grains are often used in traditional Japanese cuisine, such as in sushi and rice balls.

The plant has a short growing season and is primarily grown in paddies, contributing to its cultural and agricultural significance in the region.
