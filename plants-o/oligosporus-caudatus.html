Oligosporus caudatus

Oligosporus caudatus, commonly known as the feather tailed spore plant, is a unique species within the fungal kingdom.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender spore-bearing structures that resemble feathers, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spores are dispersed by wind, allowing for wide geographic distribution.

Oligosporus caudatus plays a minor but ecologically significant role in nutrient cycling within its habitat.
