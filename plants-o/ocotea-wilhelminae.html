Ocotea wilhelminae

Ocotea wilhelminae, commonly known as the yellow sandarac tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, woody drupes that contain a single seed.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
