Ophrys cretica var. ariadnae

Ophrys cretica var. ariadnae, commonly known as the Greek bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Greece.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant produces a single, showy flower with a distinctive lip that resembles the body of a bee, attracting male bees for pollination. It thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats at higher altitudes.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species.
