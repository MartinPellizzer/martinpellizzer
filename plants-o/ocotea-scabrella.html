Ocotea scabrella

Ocotea scabrella, commonly known as the rough-leafed ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant typically grows in humid, lowland forests and can reach heights of up to 20 meters. Its leaves are usually dark green, glossy, and have a rough texture, while its flowers are small and clustered.

The fruit is a dark, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed.
