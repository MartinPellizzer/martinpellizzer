Ophrys × coulotii

Ophrys × coulotii, commonly known as the bee orchid hybrid, is a fascinating plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera, two species known for their mimicry of female wasps to attract male pollinators. The plant is characterized by its intricate, bee-shaped flowers that play a crucial role in its pollination strategy. It typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This hybrid is of particular interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance.
