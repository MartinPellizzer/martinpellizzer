Ophrys apifera f. chodatii

Ophrys apifera f. chodatii, commonly known as the bee orchid, is a striking orchid species characterized by its intricate floral structure that mimics the appearance of a bee.

This mimicry is a key botanical feature, as it plays a crucial role in the plant's pollination strategy by attracting male bees. The plant is native to Europe and thrives in calcareous soils, often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its flowers are typically pale yellow to brown, with a distinctive bee-like shape and intricate patterns.

The species is notable for its specialized relationship with specific pollinators, making it an important subject in the study of plant-pollinator interactions.
