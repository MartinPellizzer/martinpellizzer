Oenothera verrucosa

Oenothera verrucosa, commonly known as the sticky evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats like prairies and disturbed areas.

Oenothera verrucosa is also known for its medicinal properties, with its seeds used in traditional herbal remedies.
