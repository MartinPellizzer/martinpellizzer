Oxalis subintegra

Oxalis subintegra, commonly known as the white wood sorrel, is a species of flowering plant in the family Oxalidaceae.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive white or pale green coloration. Its flowers are typically small, yellow, and clustered in umbels.

Oxalis subintegra is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
