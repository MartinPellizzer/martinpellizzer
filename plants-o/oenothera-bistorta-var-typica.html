Oenothera bistorta var. typica

Oenothera bistorta var. typica, commonly known as purple coneflower, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features showy, tubular purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees. The plant has a rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves at the base and a tall, branching stem. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often found in wetlands, meadows, and along stream banks.
