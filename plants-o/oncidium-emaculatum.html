Oncidium emaculatum

Oncidium emaculatum, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, gracefully branching stems and its delicate, pale green or white flowers with a distinctive yellow lip. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Its flowers bloom in clusters, often appearing in late spring to early summer.

Oncidium emaculatum is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its unique and elegant appearance.
