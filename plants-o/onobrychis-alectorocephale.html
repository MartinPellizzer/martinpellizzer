Onobrychis alectorocephale

Onobrychis alectorocephale, commonly known as the purple vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial legume that typically grows in rocky or arid environments. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to purple. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional herbal medicine.

The plant is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
