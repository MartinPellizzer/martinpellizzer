Oenothera cespitosa

Oenothera cespitosa, commonly known as swamp evening primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetlands and marshy areas of North America.

It features showy yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to waterlogged soils, allowing it to thrive in moist environments.

Oenothera cespitosa is also known for its medicinal properties, with its seeds containing oils used in traditional remedies.
