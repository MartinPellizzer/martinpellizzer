Ochna fuscescens

Ochna fuscescens, commonly known as the African starwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and elliptical leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that contain seeds. Ochna fuscescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and unique fruit.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.
