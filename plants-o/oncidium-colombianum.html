Oncidium colombianum

Oncidium colombianum, commonly known as the Colombian orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its elegant, pendulous flower spikes. The plant typically produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green to white flowers with prominent, contrasting lips. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Oncidium colombianum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
