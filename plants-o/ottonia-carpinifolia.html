Ottonia carpinifolia

Ottonia carpinifolia, commonly known as the horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, horn-like bracts that surround the flower cluster. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers with a prominent central stigma. Ottonia carpinifolia is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
