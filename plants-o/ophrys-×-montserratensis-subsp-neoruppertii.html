Ophrys × montserratensis subsp. neoruppertii

Ophrys × montserratensis subsp.

neoruppertii, commonly known as the Neoruppert's orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Canary Islands. It belongs to the genus Ophrys, which is known for its intricate mimicry of female insects to attract male pollinators.

The plant produces showy, bee-mimicking flowers that are often adorned with intricate patterns and markings. It typically grows in rocky or coastal habitats, thriving in the unique microclimates of its native region.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized orchid species.
