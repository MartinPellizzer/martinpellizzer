Osmunda bifurcata

Osmunda bifurcata, commonly known as the giant water fern, is a species of fern that thrives in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its large, floating fronds that can reach lengths of up to three meters. The plant has a complex life cycle involving both a sporophyte and a free-living gametophyte stage. Its roots form dense mats on the water surface, providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Osmunda bifurcata plays an important ecological role in nutrient cycling and water filtration in wetland ecosystems.
