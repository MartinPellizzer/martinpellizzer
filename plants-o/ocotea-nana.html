Ocotea nana

Ocotea nana, commonly known as the dwarf ocotea, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant typically grows to a height of less than two meters, with glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Ocotea nana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens for its compact size and attractive foliage.
