Oligosporus campestris subsp. caudatus

Oligosporus campestris subsp.

caudatus, commonly known as the curly dock, is a flowering plant in the family Brassicaceae. It is characterized by its rosette of broad, wavy leaves and distinctive, club-shaped inflorescences.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically arranged in a terminal cluster. It is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a common weed in agricultural fields.

Its botanical features make it easily identifiable and adaptable to various environmental conditions.
