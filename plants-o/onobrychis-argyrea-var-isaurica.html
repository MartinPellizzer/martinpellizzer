Onobrychis argyrea var. isaurica

Onobrychis argyrea var. isaurica, commonly known as the Isaurian vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a creeping growth habit and produces small, elongated pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
