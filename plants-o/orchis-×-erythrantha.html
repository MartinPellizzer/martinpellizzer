Orchis × erythrantha

Orchis × erythrantha, commonly known as the red-lipped orchid, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Orchis morio and Orchis anthropophora.

It is a tall, herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe, particularly the British Isles. The plant features showy, spurred orchid flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, with a distinctive red lip. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for absorbing nutrients from the soil in woodland habitats.

Orchis × erythrantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in damp, shaded areas.
