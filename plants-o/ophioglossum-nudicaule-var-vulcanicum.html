Ophioglossum nudicaule var. vulcanicum

Ophioglossum nudicaule var. vulcanicum, commonly known as the Vulcan's naked-stalk fern, is a rare and ancient fern species characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often spirally twisted sporangia.

It belongs to the order Ophioglossales, which is one of the most primitive groups of ferns. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often near volcanic soils, reflecting its association with geothermal activity. Its leaves are usually narrow and have a unique, almost snake-like appearance, contributing to its common name.

The species is of particular interest to botanists due to its evolutionary significance and limited distribution.
