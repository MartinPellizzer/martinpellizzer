Orchis parreissii

Orchis parreissii, commonly known as the pink orchid, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, flowering stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, woodland habitats.

Orchis parreissii is an important species for pollinators and is often found in shaded, rich soils.
