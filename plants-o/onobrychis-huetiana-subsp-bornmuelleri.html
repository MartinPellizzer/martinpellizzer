Onobrychis huetiana subsp. bornmuelleri

Onobrychis huetiana subsp.

bornmuelleri, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada vetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-pink flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for wildlife.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
