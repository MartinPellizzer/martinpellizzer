Ocotea exaltata

Ocotea exaltata, commonly known as the white sand ocotea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in countries like Costa Rica and Panama. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen foliage and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are typically elliptical and have a smooth, glossy surface.

Ocotea exaltata is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical rainforests.
