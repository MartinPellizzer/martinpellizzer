Oxypetalum multicaule

Oxypetalum multicaule, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are ovate and covered in a fine, silky hair, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Oxypetalum multicaule is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
