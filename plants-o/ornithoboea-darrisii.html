Ornithoboea darrisii

Ornithoboea darrisii, commonly known as the bird's-beak orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Cape Province of South Africa.

It is a small, perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces striking, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. It blooms during the summer months, attracting various pollinators such as bees and birds.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
