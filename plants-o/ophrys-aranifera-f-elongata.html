Ophrys aranifera f. elongata

Ophrys aranifera f. elongata, commonly known as the long-flowered bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive bee-like floral structure, which features a prominent lip mimicking the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, calcareous soils. Its elongated floral bracts and pale yellow to greenish-brown coloration are key botanical features.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
