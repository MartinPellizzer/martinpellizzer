Ophioglossum pinnatum

Ophioglossum pinnatum, commonly known as the bird's nest fern, is a rare and ancient fern species native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, fan-shaped fronds that resemble a bird's nest, with a central stem and a cluster of leaves radiating outward. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has a distinctive reproductive structure called a sporangium, which produces spores. Its leaves are often covered in a fine, fuzzy layer that helps retain moisture.

Ophioglossum pinnatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens due to its rarity and ecological significance.
