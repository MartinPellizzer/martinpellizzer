Ochetophila riparia

Ochetophila riparia, commonly known as the river sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers. This species is typically found in moist or wetland habitats, such as riverbanks and marshes. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, which can stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Ochetophila riparia plays an important ecological role in aquatic and riparian ecosystems.
