Oxalis tetraphylla var. guerreroensis

Oxalis tetraphylla var. guerreroensis, commonly known as the Guerrero wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. It produces distinctive, bead-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a delicate, native plant for partial shade gardens.
