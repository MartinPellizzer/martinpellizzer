Ochrocarpos siamensis var. odoratissimus

Ochrocarpos siamensis var. odoratissimus, commonly known as the Siam orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows in tropical rainforests, where it thrives in humid and shaded environments. Its flowers are often pale yellow or greenish-white, with a strong, sweet scent that attracts pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
