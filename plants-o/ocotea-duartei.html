Ocotea duartei

Ocotea duartei, commonly known as Duarte's ocotea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a small, woody drupe that contains a single seed.

Ocotea duartei is often found in humid lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
