Ochrocarpos humblotii

Ochrocarpos humblotii, commonly known as the Humboldt's orchid, is a rare and unique orchid species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated flowers that display a striking combination of yellow and brown hues. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in cloud forests where it relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its botanical structure includes a slender, wiry stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered an endangered species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts.
