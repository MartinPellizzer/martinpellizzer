Ocotea venosissima

Ocotea venosissima, commonly known as the veined ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by dark blue-black berries. Its wood is valued for its fine grain and is used in furniture and cabinetry.

Ocotea venosissima thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
