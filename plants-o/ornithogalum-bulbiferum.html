Ornithogalum bulbiferum

Ornithogalum bulbiferum, commonly known as the African star of Bethlehem, is a bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of star-shaped white flowers with a subtle fragrance. The plant's bulb is its primary storage organ, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
