Orthochilus hians var. nutans

Orthochilus hians var. nutans, commonly known as the drooping orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features a slender, upright stem with several elongated, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in a raceme, often drooping downward.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
