Oncidium anomalum

Oncidium anomalum, commonly known as the Orchid of the Andes, is a tropical orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, often fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including yellow, green, and red. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Oncidium anomalum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
