Ocotea lobbii

Ocotea lobbii, commonly known as the Lobb's ocotea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the rainforests of western Africa.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers followed by oval-shaped berries. Ocotea lobbii typically grows in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forest habitats.

Its wood is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional craftsmanship and as a source of essential oils.
