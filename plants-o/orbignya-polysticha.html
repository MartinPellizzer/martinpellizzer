Orbignya polysticha

Orbignya polysticha, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a large evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and produces a unique fruit that contains the edible seeds known as Brazil nuts. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall and has a long lifespan, often living for several centuries. Its ecological importance is significant, as it provides habitat and food for various wildlife species.

The plant is also valued for its economic contribution through the harvesting of its seeds.
