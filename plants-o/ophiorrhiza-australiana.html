Ophiorrhiza australiana

Ophiorrhiza australiana, commonly known as the Australian beach morning glory, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Australia.

It is a small, herbaceous shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shorelines. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces small, showy flowers in shades of blue or purple. It is well adapted to salt spray and can thrive in harsh coastal environments.

Ophiorrhiza australiana plays a role in stabilizing dunes and supporting local biodiversity.
