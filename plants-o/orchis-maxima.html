Orchis maxima

Orchis maxima, commonly known as the greater orchid, is a tall, striking orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring long, narrow leaves and a dense spike of showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its complex, labellum-shaped petals, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Orchis maxima is a perennial herb that blooms from June to August, attracting various pollinators with its fragrant flowers.
