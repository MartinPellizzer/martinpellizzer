Ochagavia elegans

Ochagavia elegans, commonly known as the elegant cholla, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are clustered at the ends of the stems. Ochagavia elegans is a low-maintenance plant that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun exposure.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and desert gardens.
