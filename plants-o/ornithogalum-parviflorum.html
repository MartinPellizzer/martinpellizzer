Ornithogalum parviflorum

Ornithogalum parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a bulbous stem with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, star-shaped white flowers. The plant is characterized by its nocturnal blooming habit, with flowers opening in the evening and closing by dawn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
