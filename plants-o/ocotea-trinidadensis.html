Ocotea trinidadensis

Ocotea trinidadensis, commonly known as Trinidadian ocotea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Caribbean region, particularly Trinidad and Tobago.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments within tropical rainforests. Its leaves are usually elliptical, with a glossy surface and a strong, spicy scent.

The fruit is a small, woody drupe that contains a single seed, contributing to its role in local ecosystems and traditional uses.
