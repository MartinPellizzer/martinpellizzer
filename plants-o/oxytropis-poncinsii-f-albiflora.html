Oxytropis poncinsii f. albiflora

Oxytropis poncinsii f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered locoweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and showy white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
