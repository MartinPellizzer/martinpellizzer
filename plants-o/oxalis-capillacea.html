Oxalis capillacea

Oxalis capillacea, commonly known as the slender wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its stems are slender and often hairy, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Oxalis capillacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
