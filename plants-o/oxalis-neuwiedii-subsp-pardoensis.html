Oxalis neuwiedii subsp. pardoensis

Oxalis neuwiedii subsp.

pardoensis, commonly known as the Pardo's wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America. It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped lobes and clusters of small, yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its foliage is often covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a soft, velvety appearance. Oxalis neuwiedii subsp.

pardoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
