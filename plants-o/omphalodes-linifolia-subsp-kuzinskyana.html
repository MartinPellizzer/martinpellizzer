Omphalodes linifolia subsp. kuzinskyana

Omphalodes linifolia subsp.

kuzinskyana, commonly known as the Kuzinsky's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Australia. It features slender, often hairy stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly toothed margin.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
