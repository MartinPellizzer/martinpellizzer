Ocimum fimbriatum

Ocimum fimbriatum, commonly known as Indian basil or lemon basil, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale purple to white flowers that bloom in whorls along the stems. Its aromatic leaves are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Ocimum fimbriatum thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
