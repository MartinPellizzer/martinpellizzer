Odontites vernus subsp. siculus

Odontites vernus subsp.

siculus, commonly known as the Sicilian toothwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its subspecific name, *siculus*, refers to its occurrence in Sicily, highlighting its regional significance.
