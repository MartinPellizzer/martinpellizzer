Omalocline

Omalocline, commonly known as the "butterfly plant," is a striking member of the Araceae family, known for its large, colorful inflorescence.

It features a spadix surrounded by a showy, petal-like spathe that resembles a butterfly's wings. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments and is native to tropical regions of Central and South America. Its botanical structure includes a rhizomatous root system and broad, arrow-shaped leaves.

Omalocline is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be found in both indoor and outdoor gardens.
