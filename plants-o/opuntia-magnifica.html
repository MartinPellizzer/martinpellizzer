Opuntia magnifica

Opuntia magnifica, commonly known as the giant prickly pear cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, flat, paddle-shaped stems that are covered in sharp spines and glochids. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers, which are followed by edible, fleshy fruits called tunas. Opuntia magnifica is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

It plays an important ecological role by providing food and shelter for various desert wildlife.
