Ononis hesperia

Ononis hesperia, commonly known as lesser burnet, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, clustered pink or purple flowers. The plant has a hairy, upright stem and is characterized by its whorled arrangement of narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Ononis hesperia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
