Ocotea elliptica

Ocotea elliptica, commonly known as the oval-leaf ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a dark blue drupe, often containing a single seed.

Ocotea elliptica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its aesthetic qualities.
