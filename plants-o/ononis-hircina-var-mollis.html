Ononis hircina var. mollis

Ononis hircina var. mollis, commonly known as soft hairy cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with numerous creeping stems that root at the nodes. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, palmately lobed, and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance.

It thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
