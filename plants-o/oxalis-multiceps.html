Oxalis multiceps

Oxalis multiceps, commonly known as the clustered wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with three heart-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady woodlands and has a fibrous root system. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in partial shade.

Oxalis multiceps is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be found in gardens and naturalized areas.
