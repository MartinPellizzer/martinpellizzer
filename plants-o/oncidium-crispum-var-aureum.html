Oncidium crispum var. aureum

Oncidium crispum var. aureum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical orchid species native to Central and South America.

It features striking, long-lasting yellow flowers that are often used in floral arrangements. The plant has strap-like, dark green leaves and produces pendulous inflorescences with numerous blooms. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its vibrant color and relatively easy care requirements.
