Oberonia nayarii

Oberonia nayarii, commonly known as the Philippine orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit and produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it thrives in shaded, humid environments.

Oberonia nayarii is often found in forest undergrowth, where it forms symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi.
