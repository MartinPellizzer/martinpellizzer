Onobrychis viciifolia monstr. ramosa

Onobrychis viciifolia monstr.

ramosa, commonly known as the creeping sainfoin, is a perennial legume native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in racemes.

The plant has a vigorous growth habit and often forms dense mats, making it suitable for erosion control. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Its botanical features make it an important forage plant for livestock in arid regions.
