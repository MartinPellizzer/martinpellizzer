Oenothera biennis var. oakesiana

Oenothera biennis var. oakesiana, commonly known as the Oakesiana evening primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the central United States.

It features showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening and close by morning, a characteristic known as nyctinasty. The plant has slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oily seeds. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered with a dense layer of fine, silky hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique flowering behavior and ecological benefits.
