Orobanche scarlatina

Orobanche scarlatina, commonly known as the red broomrape, is a parasitic plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is characterized by its reddish or orange stems and flowers. The plant typically grows on the roots of various host species, including those in the legume family. Its flowers are usually clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences and are often pink to red in color.

Orobanche scarlatina is found in a variety of habitats, including grasslands and disturbed soils, across Europe and parts of Asia.
