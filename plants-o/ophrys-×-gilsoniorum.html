Ophrys × gilsoniorum

Ophrys × gilsoniorum, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is characterized by its intricate, orchid-like flowers that mimic the appearance of female bees, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant is native to southern Europe and thrives in calcareous soils, often found in open woodland or grassland habitats. Its flowers are typically pale yellow to brown, with intricate patterns that resemble bee bodies.

This hybrid species is notable for its ability to produce viable seeds and for its role in the study of orchid hybridization and mimicry in nature.
