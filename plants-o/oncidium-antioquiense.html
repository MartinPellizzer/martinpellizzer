Oncidium antioquiense

Oncidium antioquiense, commonly known as the Antioquian orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, typical of many orchid species. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often arranged in a raceme. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Oncidium antioquiense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
