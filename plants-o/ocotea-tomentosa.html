Ocotea tomentosa

Ocotea tomentosa, commonly known as the wooly ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, leathery leaves that are covered in a dense, woolly pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers followed by oval, dark blue berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Its wood is valued for its durability and aromatic properties, making it an important species in local ecosystems and traditional crafts.
