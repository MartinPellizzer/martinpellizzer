Ouratea fasciculata

Ouratea fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered ouratea, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Annonaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite, and often clustered leaves. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by edible, fleshy fruits containing numerous seeds. Its foliage is typically dark green and glossy, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical gardens.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
