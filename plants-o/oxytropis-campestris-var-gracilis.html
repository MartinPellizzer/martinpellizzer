Oxytropis campestris var. gracilis

Oxytropis campestris var. gracilis, commonly known as slender field locoweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in racemes. It has a deep root system that helps it thrive in dry, disturbed soils.

This species is often found in grasslands and along roadsides, where it plays a role in soil stabilization.
