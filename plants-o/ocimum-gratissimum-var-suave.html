Ocimum gratissimum var. suave

Ocimum gratissimum var. suave, commonly known as African basil, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small white or purple flowers. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. It is often cultivated for its culinary and medicinal uses, particularly in traditional African medicine.

Its essential oils contain compounds like citral and geraniol, which contribute to its distinctive fragrance and therapeutic properties.
