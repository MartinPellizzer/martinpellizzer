Odontoglossum schlieperianum

Odontoglossum schlieperianum, commonly known as the Schlieper's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in cool, humid environments at high altitudes. The plant features a pseudobulb with a single leaf and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. Its flowers are characterized by a spurred lip and are often arranged in a raceme.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and requires specific care to thrive.
