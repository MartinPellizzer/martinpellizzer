Oxalis japonica var. obtriangulata

Oxalis japonica var. obtriangulata, commonly known as the Japanese wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves with three heart-shaped leaflets, often with a reddish or purple tinge. The plant produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value comes from its delicate foliage and seasonal blooms.
