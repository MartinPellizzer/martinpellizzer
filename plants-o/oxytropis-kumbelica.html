Oxytropis kumbelica

Oxytropis kumbelica, commonly known as Kumbel's locoweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces trifoliate leaves and is adapted to grow in arid and semi-arid environments. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, which makes it an important component of certain ecosystems.

However, it can be toxic to livestock if consumed in large quantities.
