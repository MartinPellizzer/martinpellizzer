Oxytropis latibracteata var. latibracteata

Oxytropis latibracteata var. latibracteata, commonly known as broad-bract locoweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink to purple in color. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and its stems are often hairy. It is native to the western United States and is adapted to dry, rocky habitats.

This species is known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock.
