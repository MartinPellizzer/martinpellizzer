Oenothera spiralis f. viridescens

Oenothera spiralis f. viridescens, commonly known as the greenleaf evening primrose, is a variant of the evening primrose species characterized by its distinct foliage.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green, contrasting with the usual gray-green hues of the species. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.

This form is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its unique foliage and floral display.
