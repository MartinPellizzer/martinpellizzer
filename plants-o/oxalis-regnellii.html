Oxalis regnellii

Oxalis regnellii, commonly known as the forest oxalis, is a species of flowering plant in the wood sorrel family.

It is native to regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive red or purple vein pattern, and it produces small, delicate flowers in shades of yellow or white. Oxalis regnellii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in containers or as a ground cover.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
