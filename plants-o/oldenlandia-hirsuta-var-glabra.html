Oldenlandia hirsuta var. glabra

Oldenlandia hirsuta var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth oldenlandia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture, distinguishing it from the hairy form of the species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
