Ornichia trinervis

Ornichia trinervis, commonly known as the three-veined orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers with a complex structure typical of orchids. The plant has a slender, upright stem and produces small, greenish-white blooms that are often fragrant. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have three distinct veins, giving it its scientific name.

Ornichia trinervis is often found in humid, shaded environments such as rainforests and is valued for its ornamental beauty.
