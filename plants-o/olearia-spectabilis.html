Olearia spectabilis

Olearia spectabilis, commonly known as the showy daisy or mountain daisy, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy yellow flower heads. The plant typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its flowers bloom in spring and early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Olearia spectabilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy and long-lasting blooms.
