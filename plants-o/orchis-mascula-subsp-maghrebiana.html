Orchis mascula subsp. maghrebiana

Orchis mascula subsp.

maghrebiana, commonly known as the Maghreb male orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Mediterranean region, particularly in North Africa. It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and striking violet to purple flowers with a distinctive spurred lip.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces a single, showy flower per stem, which blooms in late spring to early summer.

This orchid is adapted to temperate climates and is often found in regions with well-drained soil.
