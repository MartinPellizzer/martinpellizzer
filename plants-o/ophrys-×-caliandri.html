Ophrys × caliandri

Ophrys × caliandri, commonly known as the bee orchid hybrid, is a rare and fascinating plant that belongs to the Ophrys genus within the orchid family.

This hybrid species is the result of crossbreeding between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera, two species known for their mimicry of female insects. The plant is characterized by its striking, bee-shaped flowers that are highly specialized for pollination by male bees. Its botanical features include a slender stem, lance-shaped leaves, and a single or clustered inflorescence of vividly colored flowers.

Ophrys × caliandri is typically found in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or grassland habitats across Europe.
