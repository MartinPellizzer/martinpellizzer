Oncidium varicosum var. insigne

Oncidium varicosum var. insigne, commonly known as the showy orchid, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking, long-lasting inflorescences that bear numerous fragrant, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its leaves are elongated and fleshy, adapted to store water in its natural habitat.

Oncidium varicosum var. insigne is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
