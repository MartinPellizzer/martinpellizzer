Orchis maculata var. minor

Orchis maculata var. minor, commonly known as the lesser spotted orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features a tall, unbranched stem that bears a single spike of flowers, which are usually pale green to yellowish in color. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil.

The species is characterized by its distinctive spotted leaves and is often found in association with other orchid species in its native range.
