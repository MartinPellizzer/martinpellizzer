Orchis incarnata var. sesquipedalis

Orchis incarnata var. sesquipedalis, commonly known as the showy orchis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and wet meadows. The plant features tall, erect stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers clustered in spikes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy texture.

The orchis is notable for its complex pollination mechanisms, often involving specific insect species.
