Oxytropis montana subsp. jacquinii

Oxytropis montana subsp.

jacquinii, commonly known as yellow lappet vetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves with small, rounded leaflets.

The plant produces yellow flowers in dense clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and open woodlands. Oxytropis montana subsp.

jacquinii plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for wildlife.
