Odontoglossum × murrellianum

Odontoglossum × murrellianum, commonly known as the Murrell's orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant has erect, hairy stems and produces lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white or gray pubescence. Its flowers are usually borne in clusters and feature intricate patterns of purple, pink, and white.

Odontoglossum × murrellianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its beauty.
