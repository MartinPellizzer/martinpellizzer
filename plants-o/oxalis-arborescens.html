Oxalis arborescens

Oxalis arborescens, commonly known as the tree oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and trifoliate leaves, which are often bright green with a reddish tinge. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Oxalis arborescens is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
