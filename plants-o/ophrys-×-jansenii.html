Ophrys × jansenii

Ophrys × jansenii, commonly known as the Jansen's bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its intricate floral structure, which closely resembles a female bee, a feature evolved to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brown in color, with intricate patterns mimicking the appearance of a bee's body.

Ophrys × jansenii is a perennial herb that produces a single spike of flowers, often blooming in late spring to early summer.
