Oxytropis campestris var. davisii

Oxytropis campestris var. davisii, commonly known as Davis' field vetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in restoration projects and as a forage plant for livestock.
