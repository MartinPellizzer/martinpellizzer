Ononis serrata var. glaucescens

Ononis serrata var. glaucescens, commonly known as the glaucous bedstraw, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The leaves are alternate, serrated, and covered with a distinctive bluish-gray waxy coating. Small white flowers with five petals appear in clusters during the summer months.

This plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.
