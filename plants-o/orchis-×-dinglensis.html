Orchis × dinglensis

Orchis × dinglensis, commonly known as the Mediterranean orchid, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Orchis moria and Orchis anthropophora.

It is native to the Mediterranean region, including parts of southern Europe and North Africa. The plant produces tall flowering stems with showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in late spring. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

Orchis × dinglensis is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
