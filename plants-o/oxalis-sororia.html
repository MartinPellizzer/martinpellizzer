Oxalis sororia

Oxalis sororia, commonly known as the small-leaf wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive pattern of five to seven lobes, often adorned with a white or pale green veining. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate foliage and early spring bloom.
