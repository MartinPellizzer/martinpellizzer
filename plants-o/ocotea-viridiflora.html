Ocotea viridiflora

Ocotea viridiflora, commonly known as green-flowered ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its wood is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

Ocotea viridiflora plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
