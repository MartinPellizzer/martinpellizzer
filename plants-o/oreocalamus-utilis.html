Oreocalamus utilis

Oreocalamus utilis, commonly known as the useful reed, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges.

It is a perennial grass-like plant characterized by its triangular, hollow stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps. The plant is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.

Its ecological importance lies in its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
