Oenothera scapoidea var. eastwoodiae

Oenothera scapoidea var. eastwoodiae, commonly known as the Eastwood's evening primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and showy yellow flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oval seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native range.
