Orthosiphon ehrenbergii

Orthosiphon ehrenbergii, commonly known as the Java nettle or Java mint, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, elliptic leaves with serrated edges and clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Orthosiphon ehrenbergii is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
