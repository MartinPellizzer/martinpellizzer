Omphalodes diffusa

Omphalodes diffusa, commonly known as the common sand-verbena, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in sandy or gravelly soils.

Omphalodes diffusa is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open fields.
