Onobrychis buxbaumii

Onobrychis buxbaumii, commonly known as the Buxbaum's burclover, is a perennial legume native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is well adapted to arid environments. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in drought conditions.

Onobrychis buxbaumii is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, contributing to soil stabilization and nitrogen fixation.
