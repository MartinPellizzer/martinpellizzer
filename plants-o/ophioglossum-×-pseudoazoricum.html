Ophioglossum × pseudoazoricum

Ophioglossum × pseudoazoricum, commonly known as the giant shield fern, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Ophioglossaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, shield-shaped fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors, and is native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands. Its sporangia are clustered in a distinctive, fleshy structure called a sorus, which is a key feature of its reproductive biology.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is considered a rare and specialized species in its habitat.
