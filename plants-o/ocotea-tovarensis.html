Ocotea tovarensis

Ocotea tovarensis, commonly known as the Tovarensis Ocotea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within cloud forests, where it thrives in cooler temperatures. Its leaves are elliptical, dark green, and have a glossy surface, while its fruits are small, dark berries that mature in the fall.

Ocotea tovarensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for potential medicinal properties.
