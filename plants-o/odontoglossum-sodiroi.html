Odontoglossum sodiroi

Odontoglossum sodiroi, commonly known as the Andean orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, with rhizomes that produce pseudobulbs. The plant features lanceolate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white or gray pubescence. Its flowers are typically arranged in racemes and are known for their intricate, often fragrant structure.

Odontoglossum sodiroi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and resilience.
