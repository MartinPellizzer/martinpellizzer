Ononis diffusa var. sennenii

Ononis diffusa var. sennenii, commonly known as the Japanese sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Japan.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale pink flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and resilient species suitable for rock gardens and dry garden settings.
