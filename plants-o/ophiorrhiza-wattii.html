Ophiorrhiza wattii

Ophiorrhiza wattii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mangrove, is a flowering plant species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in coastal areas, particularly in brackish water environments. The plant has small, ovate leaves with a smooth surface and is adapted to tolerate saline conditions. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Ophiorrhiza wattii plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
