Oxydectes vulneraria

Oxydectes vulneraria, commonly known as the bleeding heart plant, is a species of flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a rhizomatous root system and prefers moist, well-drained soil in partial shade. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a reddish hue when young.

Oxydectes vulneraria is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and woodland settings.
