Ochna piscicelliana

Ochna piscicelliana, commonly known as the fishscale tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine, scale-like structures. The plant produces showy, yellowish-white flowers that are typically clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Ochna piscicelliana is often found in coastal and sandy habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
