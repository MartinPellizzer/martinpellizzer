Oxytropis mandshurica

Oxytropis mandshurica, commonly known as Siberian locoweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cold climates of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to grow in rocky and arid environments, often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets, and it produces pods that contain small, hard seeds.

Oxytropis mandshurica is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility in nutrient-poor areas.
