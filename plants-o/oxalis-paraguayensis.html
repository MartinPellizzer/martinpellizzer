Oxalis paraguayensis

Oxalis paraguayensis, commonly known as the Paraguay wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive reddish veins and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is characterized by its succulent stems and leaves, which help it retain moisture in its native habitat. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

Oxalis paraguayensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
