Odontoglossum brevifolium

Odontoglossum brevifolium, commonly known as the short-leaved tooth-orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The flowers are typically pale green to white, with a distinctive tooth-like spur at the base of the lip. It thrives in cool, shaded environments with high humidity, often growing in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat preferences, it is a sought-after plant among orchid enthusiasts.
