Oxalis fluminensis f. subglabrata

Oxalis fluminensis f. subglabrata, commonly known as the smooth-leaved wood sorrel, is a variant of the species Oxalis fluminensis.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa, characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The leaves are typically smooth and glabrous, distinguishing it from other forms of the species. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
