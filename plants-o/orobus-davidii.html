Orobus davidii

Orobus davidii, commonly known as David's orchid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, adapting to its high-altitude environment.

Orobus davidii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
