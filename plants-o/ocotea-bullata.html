Ocotea bullata

Ocotea bullata, commonly known as the bullate ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant typically grows in humid, lowland forests and can reach heights of up to 30 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a distinctive waxy coating, while its flowers are small and clustered in panicles.

The fruit is a dark blue drupe that contains a single seed, making it an important component of the forest ecosystem.
