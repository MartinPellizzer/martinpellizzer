Opuntia virgata

Opuntia virgata, commonly known as the narrowleaf prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, spineless stems that are often ribbed and covered in a layer of white, waxy bloom. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible, red-fleshed fruits. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to arid environments.

Opuntia virgata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
