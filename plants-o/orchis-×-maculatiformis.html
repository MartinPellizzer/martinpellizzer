Orchis × maculatiformis

Orchis × maculatiformis, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Orchis morio and Orchis mascula.

It is characterized by its distinctive spotted or mottled leaves and flowers that typically range from purple to pink in color. This plant is native to parts of Europe and is often found in grassy habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Orchis × maculatiformis is a perennial herb that thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
