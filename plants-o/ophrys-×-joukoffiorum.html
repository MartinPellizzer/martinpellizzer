Ophrys × joukoffiorum

Ophrys × joukoffiorum, commonly known as the Joukoff's bee orchid, is a hybrid species within the genus Ophrys, known for its intricate mimicry of female wasps.

This plant is characterized by its striking, bee-like flowers that feature a prominent lip resembling a wasp's body. It typically grows in calcareous soils and is found in Mediterranean climates, particularly in southern Europe. The plant is a perennial herb with a rhizomatous root system and produces showy, pseudobulb-like structures.

Its pollination strategy relies on sexual deception, attracting male wasps that attempt to mate with the flower.
