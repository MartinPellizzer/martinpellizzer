Orchis markusii

Orchis markusii, commonly known as the Marcus's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear a single, showy, violet-blue flower with a distinctive yellow-green throat. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, and its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators. Orchis markusii is a perennial herb that prefers well-drained, calcareous soils in open habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Orchidaceae family.
