Ophrys × marsianii

Ophrys × marsianii, commonly known as the Marsian bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its intricate floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky habitats. Its flowers are usually a mix of brown, yellow, and green hues, enhancing the mimicry effect.

Ophrys × marsianii is a short-lived perennial, producing a single spike of flowers during the spring or early summer.
