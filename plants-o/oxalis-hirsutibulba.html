Oxalis hirsutibulba

Oxalis hirsutibulba, commonly known as the hairy bulbous wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features distinctive trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom in spring, and it is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The plant's unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.
