Ophrys × hybrida

Ophrys × hybrida, commonly known as the bee orchid hybrid, is a fascinating plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera, resulting in a unique combination of traits from both parents. The plant is characterized by its intricate, bee-like flowers that have evolved to mimic female bees, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. These flowers are typically yellow or brown with intricate patterns and a fuzzy appearance, enhancing their resemblance to bees.

Ophrys × hybrida thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands across Europe.
