Oxalis acetosella proles parviflora

Oxalis acetosella proles parviflora, commonly known as the woodland wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest floors and along stream banks. Its flowers typically bloom in early spring, and the plant is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes.

Oxalis acetosella proles parviflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
