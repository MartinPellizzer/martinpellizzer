Oxydectes malambo

Oxydectes malambo, commonly known as the Malambo orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly the Democratic Republic of the Congo. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The orchid thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
