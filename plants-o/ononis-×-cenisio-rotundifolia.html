Ononis × cenisio-rotundifolia

Ononis × cenisio-rotundifolia, commonly known as the hybrid rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pungent aroma. The plant typically features a sprawling habit with hairy stems and basal leaves that are often lobed or toothed. Its flowers, which bloom in early spring, are usually pale yellow or white, and it produces small, round pods that contain several seeds.

This species is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh alpine environments.
