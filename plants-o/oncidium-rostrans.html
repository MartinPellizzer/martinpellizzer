Oncidium rostrans

Oncidium rostrans, commonly known as the rainbow orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that often display a gradient of hues, including red, orange, yellow, and green. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its inflorescence consists of a raceme of elongated, showy blooms that can last for several weeks.

Oncidium rostrans requires bright, indirect light and well-draining potting media to thrive.
