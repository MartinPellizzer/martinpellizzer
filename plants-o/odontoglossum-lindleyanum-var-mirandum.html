Odontoglossum lindleyanum var. mirandum

Odontoglossum lindleyanum var. mirandum, commonly known as the Mirandus Orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial with sympodial growth, characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. It thrives in cool, high-altitude environments with high humidity and partial shade.

Its unique floral morphology and ornamental value make it a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
