Orchis maculata f. paluster

Orchis maculata f. paluster, commonly known as the meadow orchid, is a botanical variant of the spotted orchid.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slight hairy texture.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and its role in supporting pollinator biodiversity.
