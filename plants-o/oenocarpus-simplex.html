Oenocarpus simplex

Oenocarpus simplex, commonly known as the sugar palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, green, edible fruits that mature into a sweet, sticky pulp. Its botanical significance lies in its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates and its role in traditional agriculture.

The plant's fibrous trunk is also used for making ropes and thatching materials.
