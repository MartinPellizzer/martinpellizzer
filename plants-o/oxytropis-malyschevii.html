Oxytropis malyschevii

Oxytropis malyschevii, commonly known as the Khabarovsk locoweed, is a flowering plant native to the Russian Far East and parts of northern China.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant produces spiny stems and yellow to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Oxytropis malyschevii is known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock.
