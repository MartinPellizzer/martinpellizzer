Ostericum sieboldii var. hirtulum

Ostericum sieboldii var. hirtulum, commonly known as the hairy ostericum, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Japan.

It features small, rounded leaves that are densely covered with short, stiff hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers in early spring, typically in shades of white or pale green. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique and relatively rare species within the Ericaceae family.
