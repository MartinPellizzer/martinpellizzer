Ocimum sanctum

Ocimum sanctum, commonly known as tulsi, is a sacred plant in Hindu tradition and is widely cultivated for its aromatic leaves.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other herbs like mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and spikes of pale purple or white flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Tulsi is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine to treat various ailments.
