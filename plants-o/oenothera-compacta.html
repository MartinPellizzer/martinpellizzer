Oenothera compacta

Oenothera compacta, commonly known as the compact evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening. The plant has hairy, linear leaves and a stout, branching stem that often grows close to the ground. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Oenothera compacta is also known for its ability to attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
