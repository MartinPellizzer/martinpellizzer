Ouret

Ouret, commonly known as the common buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring dark green, oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early summer, followed by black, berry-like fruits that are rich in anthraquinones. Its roots are known for containing compounds used in traditional medicine, while its bark and leaves have been utilized in herbal remedies.

Ouret is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
