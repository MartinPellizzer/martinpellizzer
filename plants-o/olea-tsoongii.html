Olea tsoongii

Olea tsoongii, commonly known as the Chinese olive, is a small evergreen tree native to China.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and fragrant, yellowish-white flowers. The tree produces small, fleshy fruits that turn from green to yellow or orange as they mature. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other parts of the world for horticultural purposes.

The plant is also valued for its timber and has traditional uses in Chinese medicine.
