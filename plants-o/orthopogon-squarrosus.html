Orthopogon squarrosus

Orthopogon squarrosus, commonly known as the squarrose orthopogon, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale green to brownish flowers. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy appearance.

Orthopogon squarrosus is typically found in dry, open habitats such as prairies and sandy soils.
