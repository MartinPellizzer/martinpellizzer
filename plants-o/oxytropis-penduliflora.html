Oxytropis penduliflora

Oxytropis penduliflora, commonly known as the hanging-flower oxytropis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. The plant features slender, upright stems with small, palmately divided leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Oxytropis penduliflora is adapted to harsh environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil in alpine ecosystems.
