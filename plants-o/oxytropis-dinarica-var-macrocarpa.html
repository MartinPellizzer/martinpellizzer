Oxytropis dinarica var. macrocarpa

Oxytropis dinarica var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the Dinaric oxytropis, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale purple to white in color. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species in its native range.
