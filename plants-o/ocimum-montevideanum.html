Ocimum montevideanum

Ocimum montevideanum, commonly known as the Buenos Aires basil, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and lavender. The plant is characterized by its fragrant leaves and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to violet. It is often cultivated for its culinary and medicinal uses due to its essential oils and antioxidant properties.

Ocimum montevideanum thrives in warm, humid climates and is relatively easy to grow in home gardens.
