Oxytropis wutaiensis

Oxytropis wutaiensis, commonly known as Wutai locoweed, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces trifoliate leaves and is adapted to grow in dry, rocky environments.

It is often found in alpine and arid regions, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
