Ochna leucophloeos subsp. leucophloeos

Ochna leucophloeos subsp.

leucophloeos, commonly known as the white-leaf ochna, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa. It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often white or pale green in color, giving it its common name.

The flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and the plant produces woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often in moist, well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
