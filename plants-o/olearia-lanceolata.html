Olearia lanceolata

Olearia lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved daisybush, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in coastal and subalpine regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Olearia lanceolata is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and resilience to coastal conditions.
