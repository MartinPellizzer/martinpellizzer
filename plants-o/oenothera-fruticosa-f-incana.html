Oenothera fruticosa f. incana

Oenothera fruticosa f. incana, commonly known as the silky willow weed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its silvery-gray, fuzzy leaves and stems, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed areas like roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
