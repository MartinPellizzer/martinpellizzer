Onobrychis sauzakensis

Onobrychis sauzakensis, commonly known as the Armenian clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which makes it valuable for soil enrichment.

However, it is relatively rare and faces threats from habitat loss and overgrazing.
