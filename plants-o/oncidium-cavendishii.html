Oncidium cavendishii

Oncidium cavendishii, commonly known as the Yellow Oncidium, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous inflorescences that bear clusters of bright yellow, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are strap-like and arranged in a fan shape, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Oncidium cavendishii requires bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.
