Ooia havilandii

Ooia havilandii, commonly known as the Haviland's flax, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in native plant gardens.
