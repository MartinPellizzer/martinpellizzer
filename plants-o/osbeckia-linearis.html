Osbeckia linearis

Osbeckia linearis, commonly known as the slender osbeckia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, temperate climates. The plant features linear, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters. Osbeckia linearis is often found in wetlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in damp, well-drained soils.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a food source for certain pollinators and its potential ecological value in wetland restoration projects.
