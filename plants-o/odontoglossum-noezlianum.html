Odontoglossum noezlianum

Odontoglossum noezlianum, commonly known as the Noezlian orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of violet or purple flowers with intricate patterns. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, adapted to the cool, moist conditions of its mountainous habitat.

Odontoglossum noezlianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and rarity.
