Oncidium wheatleyanum

Oncidium wheatleyanum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical orchid species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, showy inflorescences that typically feature a mix of yellow, orange, and green hues. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to thrive in varied environmental conditions. Its flowers are highly fragrant, often attracting pollinators such as bees and moths.

Oncidium wheatleyanum is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
