Odontoglossum constrictum f. castaneum

Odontoglossum constrictum f. castaneum, commonly known as the chestnut-tinged orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, strap-like leaves and produces striking, pendulous flowers with a unique coloration pattern. The flowers typically display a combination of deep red and orange hues, with a contrasting green throat, giving it a distinctive appearance. This orchid thrives in cool, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its foliage and floral structure are adaptations to its high-altitude habitat, making it a notable example of botanical diversity in the Orchidaceae family.
