Oeosporangium chusanum

Oeosporangium chusanum, commonly known as the Chinese cycad, is a unique plant characterized by its distinct morphology and reproductive structures.

It belongs to the family Cycadaceae and is native to southern China. The plant features a stout, unbranched trunk with a smooth, pale green to brownish surface. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with long, narrow leaflets that give it a feathery appearance.

Oeosporangium chusanum produces cones rather than flowers, with male and female cones occurring on separate plants, showcasing its ancient reproductive strategy.
