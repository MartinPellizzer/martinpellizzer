Ocotea pacifica

Ocotea pacifica, commonly known as the Pacific ocotea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in countries like Costa Rica and Panama. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed enclosed in a fleshy pulp.

Ocotea pacifica is often found in moist, shaded areas of tropical forests, contributing to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.
