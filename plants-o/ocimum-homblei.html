Ocimum homblei

Ocimum homblei, commonly known as the Hombli basil, is a species of flowering plant in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright herb with opposite, ovate leaves that are often fragrant. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

Ocimum homblei is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, similar to other basil species.
