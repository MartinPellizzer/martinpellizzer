Orchis × angusticruris var. pseudomilitaris

Orchis × angusticruris var. pseudomilitaris, commonly known as the narrow-leg orchid, is a hybrid species within the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lanceolate leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. The flowers are typically arranged in a loose spike, featuring a distinctive combination of purple and white hues. This variant is known for its robust growth and adaptability to a range of soil conditions.

It is primarily found in moist woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
