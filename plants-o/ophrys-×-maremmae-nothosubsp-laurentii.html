Ophrys × maremmae nothosubsp. laurentii

Ophrys × maremmae nothosubsp.

laurentii, commonly known as the Laurentian bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species that exhibits unique botanical features. It is a member of the Ophrys genus, known for its specialized floral structures that mimic female insects to attract male pollinators.

The plant produces showy, bee-like flowers with intricate patterns and a distinct coloration that enhances its mimicry. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or heathland habitats.

This nothosubspecies is notable for its hybrid origin, combining traits from its parent species, Ophrys maremmae and Ophrys apifera.
