Orchis × kelleriana

Orchis × kelleriana, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a naturally occurring cross between Orchis morio and Orchis anthropophora.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces tall, slender flowering stems with showy, violet to purple flowers. Its inflorescence consists of several flowers arranged in a loose spike, each with a distinctive three-lobed lip.

Orchis × kelleriana is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
