Oncidium pongratzianum

Oncidium pongratzianum, commonly known as the Pongratz orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous inflorescences that bear clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green to reddish-brown flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrate.

Oncidium pongratzianum requires bright, indirect light and consistent humidity to thrive.
