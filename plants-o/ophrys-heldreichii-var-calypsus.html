Ophrys heldreichii var. calypsus

Ophrys heldreichii var. calypsus, commonly known as the Heldreich's bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils at high altitudes, where it forms a compact rosette of broad, waxy leaves. Its flowers are usually a striking combination of brown, yellow, and green, with a distinctive lip that resembles a bee's abdomen.

This orchid is listed as vulnerable due to habitat loss and limited distribution, making it a subject of conservation interest.
