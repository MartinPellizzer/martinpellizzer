Oxalis maracayuensis

Oxalis maracayuensis, commonly known as the Maracayu oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in montane forests. It produces small, edible berries that are sometimes used in local cuisines.

Oxalis maracayuensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
