Orchis conopsea subsp. odoratissima

Orchis conopsea subsp.

odoratissima, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is a perennial herb with a tall, erect stem that bears several lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces a spike of showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers have a distinctive, sweet fragrance, which is particularly noticeable in the evening.

This subspecies is often found in grasslands, woodlands, and along stream banks, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
