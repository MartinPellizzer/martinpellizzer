Oxalis stellata var. gracilior

Oxalis stellata var. gracilior, commonly known as the slender wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a short stem with hairy surfaces and produces small, round, brown seeds.

The leaves and flowers are often used in ornamental gardening due to their attractive appearance.
