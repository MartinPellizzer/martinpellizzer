Oenothera venusta var. grisea

Oenothera venusta var. grisea, commonly known as the gray evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It features striking, showy flowers that open in the evening and emit a mild fragrance. The plant has hairy, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with a grayish-green hue. Its flowers are typically pale yellow to orange, with five petals and prominent stamens.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to xeriscaped gardens.
