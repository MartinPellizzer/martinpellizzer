Orchis morio subsp. vulgaris

Orchis morio subsp.

vulgaris, commonly known as the common twayblade, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family. It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and two opposite, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces a single spike of small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in pairs. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist meadows and woodland edges.

The species is notable for its mycorrhizal association, which is essential for its nutrient uptake and growth.
