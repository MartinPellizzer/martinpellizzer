Oxandra polyantha

Oxandra polyantha, commonly known as the white-flowered oxandra, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom throughout the year in some regions. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Oxandra polyantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
