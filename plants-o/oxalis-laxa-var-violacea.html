Oxalis laxa var. violacea

Oxalis laxa var. violacea, commonly known as violet wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive violet or purple coloration, and small, five-petaled flowers that range from pink to purple. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate foliage and early spring blooming habit.
