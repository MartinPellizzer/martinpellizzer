Ornithogalum brutium

Ornithogalum brutium, commonly known as the white star-of-Bethlehem, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers in the spring. The plant is characterized by its underground bulb, which serves as a storage organ for nutrients. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade conditions.

Ornithogalum brutium is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
