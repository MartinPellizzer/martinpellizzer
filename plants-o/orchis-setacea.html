Orchis setacea

Orchis setacea, commonly known as the tufted orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers arranged in a loose spike, with petals that are often adorned with intricate markings. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Orchis setacea thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
