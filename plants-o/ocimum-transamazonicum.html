Ocimum transamazonicum

Ocimum transamazonicum, commonly known as the Amazon basil, is a perennial herb native to the rainforests of the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments and has a sprawling habit with opposite, ovate leaves. Its flowers are small, clustered in whorls, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

Ocimum transamazonicum is valued for its essential oils, which contain compounds with potential medicinal properties.
