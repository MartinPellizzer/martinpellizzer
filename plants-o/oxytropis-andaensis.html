Oxytropis andaensis

Oxytropis andaensis, commonly known as Andean locoweed, is a perennial herb native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to high-altitude environments, often growing in rocky or alpine conditions. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Oxytropis andaensis is also known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock.
