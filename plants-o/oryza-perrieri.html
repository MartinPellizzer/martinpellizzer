Oryza perrieri

Oryza perrieri, commonly known as the Perrier's rice, is a rare and endangered species of wild rice native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in shallow, freshwater environments with high humidity and nutrient-rich soils. Its seeds are typically dispersed by water and are an important food source for aquatic birds and small mammals.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, Oryza perrieri is under increasing threat of extinction.
