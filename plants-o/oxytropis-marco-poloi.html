Oxytropis marco-poloi

Oxytropis marco-poloi, commonly known as Marco-Polo's locoweed, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant produces pods that contain several elliptical seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and steppe environments.

Oxytropis marco-poloi is known for its ability to tolerate harsh climatic conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
