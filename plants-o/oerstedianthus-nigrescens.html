Oerstedianthus nigrescens

Oerstedianthus nigrescens, commonly known as the blackening ericaceous plant, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces small, leathery fruits that are typically black when mature, hence its common name. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal fynbos vegetation.

Oerstedianthus nigrescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
