Ochna lanceolata

Ochna lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf ochna, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds with tufts of hair, aiding in wind dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or scrub habitats.

Ochna lanceolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
