Oenothera fruticosa var. differta

Oenothera fruticosa var. differta, commonly known as the variable evening primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
