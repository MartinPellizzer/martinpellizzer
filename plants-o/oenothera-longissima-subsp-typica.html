Oenothera longissima subsp. typica

Oenothera longissima subsp.

typica, commonly known as the desert evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy yellow flowers that open in the evening.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, brown seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils. Oenothera longissima subsp.

typica is noted for its adaptability to harsh conditions and its role in supporting local pollinators.
