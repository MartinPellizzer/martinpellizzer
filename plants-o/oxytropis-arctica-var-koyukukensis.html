Oxytropis arctica var. koyukukensis

Oxytropis arctica var. koyukukensis, commonly known as Arctic locoweed, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open, rocky, or sandy soils. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are often hairy and adapted to cold climates. It is well-suited to harsh environments and can thrive in low-nutrient soils.

This variety is part of the legume family and has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.
