Osbeckia tetrandra

Osbeckia tetrandra, commonly known as the four-stamen willow herb, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. Its scientific name reflects its distinctive four stamens per flower, a characteristic feature of the genus Osbeckia.

Osbeckia tetrandra is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
