Orobus filiformis

Orobus filiformis, commonly known as the slender orobus, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its delicate, climbing habit. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale green to yellow in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a fibrous root system.

Orobus filiformis is often found in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests.
