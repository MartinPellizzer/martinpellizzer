Ocotea hemsleyana

Ocotea hemsleyana, commonly known as Hemsley's ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests, often reaching heights of up to 20 meters. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils, which have been used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.

The species is also valued for its wood, which is used in local crafts and construction.
