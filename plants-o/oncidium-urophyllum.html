Oncidium urophyllum

Oncidium urophyllum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow and green foliage and clusters of fragrant, long-lasting flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical and subtropical climates. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding ornamental value to gardens and greenhouses.

Oncidium urophyllum requires bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.
