Ocotea laetevirens

Ocotea laetevirens, commonly known as the yellow ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, clustered flowers that develop into dark blue berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Its wood is valued for its durability and pleasant scent, making it useful in traditional crafts and furniture.
