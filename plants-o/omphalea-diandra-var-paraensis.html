Omphalea diandra var. paraensis

Omphalea diandra var. paraensis, commonly known as the Paraensis Crownvetch, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural studies.
