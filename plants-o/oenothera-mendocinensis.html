Oenothera mendocinensis

Oenothera mendocinensis, commonly known as the Mendocino evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that open in the evening and close by morning. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and woodlands. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and its flowers are pollinated primarily by moths.

Oenothera mendocinensis is valued for its ecological role and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
