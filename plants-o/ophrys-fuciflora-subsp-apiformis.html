Ophrys fuciflora subsp. apiformis

Ophrys fuciflora subsp.

apiformis, commonly known as the bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its unique floral structure that closely resembles a bee, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, open habitats. Its flowers are usually yellow or green with dark markings, mimicking the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination.

Due to its specific habitat requirements and declining populations, it is considered a vulnerable species in many areas.
