Oxalis californica

Oxalis californica, commonly known as the California wood sorrel, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinct veins and small, delicate flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and streambanks. It produces edible leaves and flowers, though they should be consumed in moderation.

Oxalis californica is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
