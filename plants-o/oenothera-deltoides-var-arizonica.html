Oenothera deltoides var. arizonica

Oenothera deltoides var. arizonica, commonly known as the Arizona evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in desert and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and native landscaping.
