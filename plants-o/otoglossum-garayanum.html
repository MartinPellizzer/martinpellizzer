Otoglossum garayanum

Otoglossum garayanum, commonly known as the Ecuadorian orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude cloud forests, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, and the flowers are usually pale green or white with a distinctive lip.

Due to its habitat specificity, Otoglossum garayanum is considered a rare and endangered species.
