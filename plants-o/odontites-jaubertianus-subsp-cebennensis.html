Odontites jaubertianus subsp. cebennensis

Odontites jaubertianus subsp.

cebennensis, commonly known as the Cebenna toothed grass, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, toothed leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a composite head, which are typical of the family. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its restricted distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
