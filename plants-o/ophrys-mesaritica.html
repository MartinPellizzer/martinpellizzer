Ophrys mesaritica

Ophrys mesaritica, commonly known as the Mesara bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Greece.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee, a feature evolved to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils at high elevations, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brownish in color, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Ophrys mesaritica is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts in preserving its natural environment.
