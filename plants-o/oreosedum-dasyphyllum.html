Oreosedum dasyphyllum

Oreosedum dasyphyllum, commonly known as the cushion stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It features dense, compact growth habits, forming low, mat-like structures that help it conserve water. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or white during the summer months.

Oreosedum dasyphyllum is well adapted to harsh, sunny environments and is often used in rock gardens or xeriscaping.
