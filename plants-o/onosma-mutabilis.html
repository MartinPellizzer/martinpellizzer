Onosma mutabilis

Onosma mutabilis, commonly known as the variable sand-verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its blooming period occurs in the spring and early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Onosma mutabilis is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
