Oxalis amoena

Oxalis amoena, commonly known as the purple shamrock, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which often have a striking purple or reddish hue. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale pink. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Oxalis amoena is also valued for its ability to spread through rhizomes, forming dense ground cover in suitable conditions.
