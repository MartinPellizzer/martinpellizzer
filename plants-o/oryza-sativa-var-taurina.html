Oryza sativa var. taurina

Oryza sativa var. taurina, commonly known as the Taurine rice, is a less commonly cultivated variant of the rice species.

It is characterized by its shorter stature and smaller grain size compared to other rice varieties. This plant typically thrives in temperate climates and requires a shorter growing season. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a culm that is often more robust than that of standard rice.

It is primarily of interest in botanical studies due to its genetic diversity and potential for agricultural adaptation.
