Ocotea grayi

Ocotea grayi, commonly known as the gray ocotea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in countries like Brazil, Colombia, and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a dark blue drupe, containing a single seed, and it is often found in humid, lowland forests.

Ocotea grayi is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
