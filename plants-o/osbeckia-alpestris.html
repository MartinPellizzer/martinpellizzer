Osbeckia alpestris

Osbeckia alpestris, commonly known as the alpine goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically alternate and ovate. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. Osbeckia alpestris thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

It is adapted to cold climates and can withstand harsh environmental conditions.
