Oenothera heterophylla var. rhombipetala

Oenothera heterophylla var. rhombipetala, commonly known as the rhombic evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is a perennial species that produces capsules containing numerous seeds.

The botanical features of this plant make it an important species in ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
