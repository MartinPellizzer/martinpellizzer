Orchis angustifolia subf. conferta

Orchis angustifolia subf.

conferta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, linear leaves and produces tall, slender flowering stems. Its flowers are usually violet or purple, arranged in a spike and characterized by a long, slender lip.

The species is notable for its specialized pollination mechanisms and is often found in calcareous soils.
