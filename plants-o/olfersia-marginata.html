Olfersia marginata

Olfersia marginata, commonly known as the Margined Orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often display a combination of white, yellow, and green hues. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are elongated and lance-shaped, often with a waxy texture.

Olfersia marginata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in greenhouses and botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
