Oberonia cimicina

Oberonia cimicina, commonly known as the Cimicina orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with intricate patterns that attract specific pollinators.

Botanically, it is noted for its delicate foliage and specialized root system adapted for nutrient absorption from its host.
