Oncidium guianense var. album

Oncidium guianense var. album, commonly known as the white orchid of Guiana, is a tropical orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its elegant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a subtle fragrance. The plant has strap-like leaves that are typically green and arranged in a fan shape. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
