Oxytropis campestris subsp. tiroliensis

Oxytropis campestris subsp.

tiroliensis, commonly known as the Alpine milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, compound leaves.

The plant produces yellowish to reddish flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Oxytropis campestris subsp.

tiroliensis is adapted to cold climates and is relatively drought-resistant.
