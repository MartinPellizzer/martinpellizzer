Oenothera crassifolia var. leptocarpa

Oenothera crassifolia var. leptocarpa, commonly known as the slenderleaf evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening. The leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in arid and semi-arid regions.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
