Opuntia costigera

Opuntia costigera, commonly known as the narrow-leafed prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, spineless pads and vibrant yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems. Its fruit, known as a tunas, is edible and used in traditional cuisines.

Opuntia costigera plays an important role in desert ecosystems, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
