Oldenlandia pentandra

Oldenlandia pentandra, commonly known as the silverleaf spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its delicate, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling spider-like structures, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Oldenlandia pentandra is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
