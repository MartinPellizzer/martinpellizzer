Oxalis psoraleoides

Oxalis psoraleoides, commonly known as the false shamrock, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial herb with trifoliate leaves that resemble those of the true shamrock. The plant produces small, delicate yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Oxalis psoraleoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
