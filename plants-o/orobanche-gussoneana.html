Orobanche gussoneana

Orobanche gussoneana, commonly known as the Italian broomrape, is a rootless parasitic plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on its host for nutrients.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. This species is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, often found in disturbed soils and agricultural fields. It parasitizes a variety of host plants, including legumes and grasses, and can cause significant damage to crops.

Due to its lack of photosynthesis, it plays a unique role in ecological systems as a specialist parasite.
