Oreoherzogia pumila var. penduliramea

Oreoherzogia pumila var. penduliramea, commonly known as the pendant flax lily, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It features slender, pendulous stems that bear small, narrow leaves arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces delicate, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
