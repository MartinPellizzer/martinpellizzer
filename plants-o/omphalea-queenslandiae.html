Omphalea queenslandiae

Omphalea queenslandiae, commonly known as the Queensland milk vine, is a climbing shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its milky sap, which can be toxic. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in coastal areas and rainforests.

Its growth habit and chemical composition make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional uses.
