Origanum serpyllum

Origanum serpyllum, commonly known as sweet marjoram, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, and its fragrant leaves are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. Origanum serpyllum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for Mediterranean gardens.

Its essential oils contain compounds like carvacrol and thymol, which contribute to its distinctive aroma and therapeutic properties.
