Ochna leucophloeos subsp. ugandensis

Ochna leucophloeos subsp.

ugandensis, commonly known as the Uganda ochna, is a flowering plant native to East Africa. It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, aiding in its dispersal. Ochna leucophloeos subsp.

ugandensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local communities.
