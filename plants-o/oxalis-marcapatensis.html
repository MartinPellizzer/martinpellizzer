Oxalis marcapatensis

Oxalis marcapatensis, commonly known as the Andean wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in mountainous regions. Its foliage contains oxalic acid, which can be toxic if ingested in large quantities.

Oxalis marcapatensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
