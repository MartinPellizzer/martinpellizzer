Obione nana

Obione nana, commonly known as the dwarf obione, is a small, herbaceous plant native to specific regions in Asia.

It typically grows to a height of less than 10 centimeters, featuring delicate, narrow leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and hairy stems, which aid in its survival in humid conditions.

Obione nana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
