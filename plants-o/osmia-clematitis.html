Osmia clematitis

Osmia clematitis, commonly known as the southern sweat bee, is a solitary bee species native to the southern United States.

It is a small, dark-colored bee with metallic green or blue iridescence on its abdomen. The plant-related aspects of Osmia clematitis primarily involve its role as a pollinator, contributing to the pollination of various flowering plants. It prefers habitats with diverse vegetation, including open fields and gardens.

Its foraging behavior supports the growth and reproduction of numerous plant species.
