Oetosis carnosa

Oetosis carnosa, commonly known as the fleshy leaf plant, is a succulent species characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid environments.

Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it features a rosette growth pattern with waxy, often variegated foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of yellow or orange, which bloom during the warmer months. Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and a short, woody stem.

Oetosis carnosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
