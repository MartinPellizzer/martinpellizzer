Oxalis linearis f. graminifolia

Oxalis linearis f. graminifolia, commonly known as the grass-leaved wood sorrel, is a variant of the wood sorrel species characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its foliage and flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in shaded garden beds.
