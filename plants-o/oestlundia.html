Oestlundia

Oestlundia, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to Africa.

These plants are characterized by their dense, velvety foliage and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. Oestlundia species typically thrive in humid, well-drained soil and partial shade, making them popular houseplants. Their ability to bloom continuously throughout the year adds to their ornamental value.

The genus includes several species, with Oestlundia grandiflora being one of the most widely cultivated.
