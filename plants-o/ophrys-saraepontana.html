Ophrys saraepontana

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. Its flowers are usually pale green or brownish in color, with intricate patterns that aid in attracting its specific pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Ophrys saraepontana is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts in preserving its natural habitat.
