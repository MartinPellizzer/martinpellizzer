Oberonia wallichii

Oberonia wallichii, commonly known as the Himalayan orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern Himalayas.

It typically grows in moist, shaded forest understories at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with a short rhizome and a rosette of narrow, leathery leaves. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish, with three sepals and three petals, and are arranged in a spike.

Oberonia wallichii is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
