Olea indica

Olea indica, commonly known as the olive tree, is a deciduous tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silvery-green leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces small, wrinkled fruits called olives, which are harvested when ripe. Olea indica is known for its resilience to drought and its ability to thrive in poor soils.

It has been cultivated for thousands of years for its fruit, oil, and ornamental value.
