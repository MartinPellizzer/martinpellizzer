Oenothera californica subsp. arizonica

Oenothera californica subsp.

arizonica, commonly known as the Arizona evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its showy yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening and close by morning.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its stems are usually hairless and often branching, while its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.
