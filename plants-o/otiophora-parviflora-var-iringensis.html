Otiophora parviflora var. iringensis

Otiophora parviflora var. iringensis, commonly known as the Iringa daisy, is a flowering plant native to the eastern regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, daisy-like clusters. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slight hairy texture.

Otiophora parviflora var. iringensis is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in areas with well-drained soils.
