Oxalis pes-caprae var. pes-caprae

Oxalis pes-caprae var. pes-caprae, commonly known as wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped lobes and small, five-petaled flowers that are typically white or pink. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.
