Ophrys aranifera var. subfucifera

Ophrys aranifera var. subfucifera, commonly known as the Fuscous Bee Orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive floral structure, which closely resembles a bee, a feature evolved to attract specific pollinators. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually a mix of brown and yellow, with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of a bee's body.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in many areas.
