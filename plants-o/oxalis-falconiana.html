Oxalis falconiana

Oxalis falconiana, commonly known as the Falcon's Bill Oxalis, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers that are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Oxalis falconiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
