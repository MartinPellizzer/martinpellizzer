Ophrys aesculapii

Ophrys aesculapii, commonly known as the sacred bee orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to southern Europe.

This plant is characterized by its unique flower structure, which closely resembles a female bee, a mimicry strategy that aids in pollination by male bees. The orchid's flowers typically feature a dark brown or black lip that mimics the body of a bee, along with a slender column that resembles antennae. It thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

Ophrys aesculapii is a rare and protected species, valued for its ecological and botanical significance.
