Orchis lucana

Orchis lucana, commonly known as the Southern Orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, woodlands, and along riverbanks, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually arranged in a raceme and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Orchis lucana is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
