Othlis

Othlis, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a unique and rare species found in specific tropical environments.

It is characterized by its translucent, waxy leaves that appear almost ethereal under sunlight. The plant has no true roots, relying instead on its stems to absorb moisture from the air. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often going unnoticed despite their ecological importance.

Othlis plays a vital role in its ecosystem, supporting various microorganisms and insects.
