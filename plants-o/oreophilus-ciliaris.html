Oreophilus ciliaris

Oreophilus ciliaris, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a silvery, waxy coating. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and pale pink, typically blooming in the spring. Oreophilus ciliaris thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Its unique appearance and habitat make it a subject of interest for botanists and plant enthusiasts alike.
