Oreodaphne martiniana

Oreodaphne martiniana, commonly known as the Martin's daphne, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its ornamental value comes from its glossy foliage and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

Oreodaphne martiniana is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in tropical gardens and as a houseplant.
