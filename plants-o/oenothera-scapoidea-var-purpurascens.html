Oenothera scapoidea var. purpurascens

Oenothera scapoidea var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purplestem evening primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the central and eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that are often tinged with purple, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Its striking appearance and ecological value make it a desirable plant for native gardens and restoration projects.
