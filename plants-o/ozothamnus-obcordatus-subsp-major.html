Ozothamnus obcordatus subsp. major

Ozothamnus obcordatus subsp.

major, commonly known as the large-leaf golden bush, is a perennial shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and bright yellow flowers that bloom in winter.

The plant typically grows in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It is drought-tolerant and well adapted to Mediterranean climates.

The subspecies is distinguished by its larger leaves compared to other forms of Ozothamnus obcordatus.
