Opuntia engelmannii var. laevis

Opuntia engelmannii var. laevis, commonly known as the Engelmann prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems that are often ribbed and covered in spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tuna, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

This plant is well adapted to arid environments and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of food.
