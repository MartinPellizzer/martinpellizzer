Ossaea ovatifolia

Ossaea ovatifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and small, ovate leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, which bloom in spring. Ossaea ovatifolia is well adapted to dry environments, with deep roots that help it survive in sandy soils.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
