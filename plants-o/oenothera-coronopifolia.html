Oenothera coronopifolia

Oenothera coronopifolia, commonly known as the crownleaf evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. Oenothera coronopifolia is a member of the Onagraceae family, which includes other evening primroses and related species.
