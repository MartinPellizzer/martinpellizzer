Orchis brevicornis subsp. fallax

Orchis brevicornis subsp.

fallax, commonly known as the lesser twayblade, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands.

The plant has a slender, leafless stem that bears a single, lance-shaped leaf. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and arranged in a loose spike, often with a distinctive hooded lip.

It is characterized by its reduced floral structure and is pollinated by specific insects, such as bees and wasps.
