Orchis mascula f. tatrensis

Orchis mascula f. tatrensis, commonly known as the Tatran male orchid, is a rare and distinctive orchid species found in the Tatra Mountains.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers with a yellowish-green lip. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils at high elevations, often in mountainous regions with cool climates. Its morphology includes a slender, hairy stem and lanceolate leaves.

This subspecies is of particular interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique ecological adaptations.
