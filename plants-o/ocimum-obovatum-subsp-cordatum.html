Ocimum obovatum subsp. cordatum

Ocimum obovatum subsp.

cordatum, commonly known as the broad-leaf basil, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often wrinkled and have a strong aromatic scent.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or white, arranged in whorled clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its culinary and medicinal uses.

The subspecies is closely related to other basil species but is distinguished by its unique leaf shape and fragrance.
