Orchis militaris var. spathulata

Orchis militaris var. spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leaved soldier's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or grassland habitats. The plant features a single, upright stem with a few leaves and produces striking violet to purple flowers in late spring. Its flowers are bilaterally symmetrical, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination by insects.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
