Orchis laxiflora lusus leucantha

Orchis laxiflora lusus leucantha, commonly known as the white-flowered early orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and requires specific soil conditions to thrive. Its flowers have a distinctive three-lobed structure, and the leaves are usually lance-shaped with a smooth surface.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, it is considered a protected species in many regions.
