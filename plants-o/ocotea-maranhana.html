Ocotea maranhana

Ocotea maranhana, commonly known as Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces dark blue berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture-making.

Ocotea maranhana thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.
