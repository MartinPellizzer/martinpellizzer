Ophrys fusca subsp. creberrima

Ophrys fusca subsp.

creberrima, commonly known as the common bee orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It is characterized by its distinctive floral morphology, with a labellum that resembles a female bee, aiding in its specialized pollination by male bees.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a spike, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like.

The subspecies creberrima is distinguished by its more frequent flowering and dense inflorescence compared to other subspecies.
