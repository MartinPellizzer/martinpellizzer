Oxalis sanguinea

Oxalis sanguinea, commonly known as the blood oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers and trifoliate leaves, which resemble those of clover. The plant belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils. It produces small, bead-like fruits that contain seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Oxalis sanguinea is a perennial herb that prefers full sun to partial shade and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
