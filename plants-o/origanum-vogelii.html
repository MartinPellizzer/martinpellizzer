Origanum vogelii

Origanum vogelii, commonly known as Vogel's oregano, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine and have a strong, spicy aroma similar to other members of the oregano genus.
