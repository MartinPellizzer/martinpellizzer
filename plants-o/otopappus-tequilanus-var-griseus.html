Otopappus tequilanus var. griseus

Otopappus tequilanus var. griseus, commonly known as the gray-haired tarweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant features narrow, grayish-green leaves with a fuzzy texture, and its yellow flowers bloom in clusters during the spring and summer. It is adapted to thrive in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and potential use in traditional herbal medicine.
