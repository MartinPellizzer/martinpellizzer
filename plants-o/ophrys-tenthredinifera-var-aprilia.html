Ophrys tenthredinifera var. aprilia

Ophrys tenthredinifera var. aprilia, commonly known as the April bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which closely resembles a bee, a feature evolved to attract pollinators through mimicry. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and heathlands, often in nutrient-poor soils. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellow, with intricate patterns that enhance the bee mimicry.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its ecological specificity and conservation status.
