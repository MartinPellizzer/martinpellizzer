Oxalis fortipes

Oxalis fortipes, commonly known as the hardy wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins, resembling those of a shamrock, and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in rocky or sandy soils and is drought-tolerant. Oxalis fortipes is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and resilience.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
