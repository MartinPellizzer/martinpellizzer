Onoseris isotypus

Onoseris isotypus, commonly known as the narrowleaf goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

Its yellow flowers bloom in late summer, contributing to its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
