Ophrys × bastianii

Ophrys × bastianii, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the genus Ophrys, which is known for its orchids that mimic the appearance of female insects to attract male pollinators. This particular hybrid exhibits a combination of traits from its parent species, Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera, resulting in unique floral characteristics. The plant produces showy, bee-like flowers with intricate patterns that aid in its pollination strategy.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a hybrid species that highlights the evolutionary adaptations of orchids in their ecological niches.
