Opuntia diguetii

Opuntia diguetii, commonly known as the Mexican prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems that are covered in spines and glochids, which are tiny, barbed bristles. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tunaf, is edible and often used in traditional dishes.

Opuntia diguetii is well adapted to arid environments and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various animals.
