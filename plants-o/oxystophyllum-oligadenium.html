Oxystophyllum oligadenium

Oxystophyllum oligadenium, commonly known as the desert daisy, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and its ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, which bloom during the cooler months. Its foliage consists of narrow, gray-green leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss.

Oxystophyllum oligadenium is well-suited for xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
