Ochrothallus dubius

Ochrothallus dubius, commonly known as the yellow star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Ochrothallus dubius is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
