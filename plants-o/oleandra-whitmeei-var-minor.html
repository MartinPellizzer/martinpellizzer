Oleandra whitmeei var. minor

Oleandra whitmeei var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf oleander, is a compact shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically bright green and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, pinkish-white flowers during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant blooms and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
