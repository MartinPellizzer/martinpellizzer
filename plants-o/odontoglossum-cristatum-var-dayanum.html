Odontoglossum cristatum var. dayanum

Odontoglossum cristatum var. dayanum, commonly known as the Dayanum orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated pseudobulbs and showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences with white to pale pink petals and sepals, often with a contrasting lip. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, loose soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and rarity.
