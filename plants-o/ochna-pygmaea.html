Ochna pygmaea

Ochna pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf ochna, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It features narrow, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ochna pygmaea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its compact size and attractive foliage.
