Ononis pendula subsp. broussonetii

Ononis pendula subsp.

broussonetii, commonly known as the hanging whitlow grass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features slender, often pendulous stems that bear small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
