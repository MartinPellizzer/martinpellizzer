Ophrys minipassionis

Ophrys minipassionis, commonly known as the miniature passion orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of female insects to attract male pollinators through a process called sexual deception. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its flowers are small, with intricate patterns and a strong, often unpleasant odor that further enhances its mimicry.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Ophrys minipassionis is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
