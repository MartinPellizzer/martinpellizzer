Oenothera vrieseana

Oenothera vrieseana, commonly known as the evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening, hence its common name. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. Its flowers are typically borne in clusters at the ends of the stems, and they emit a mild fragrance.

Oenothera vrieseana is a member of the Onagraceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.
