Oxalis frutescens

Oxalis frutescens, commonly known as wood sorrel, is a flowering plant belonging to the Oxalidaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant is native to various regions in the Americas and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It produces edible leaves and flowers, though they should be consumed in moderation due to their oxalic acid content.

Oxalis frutescens is often grown as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
