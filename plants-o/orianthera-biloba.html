Orianthera biloba

Orianthera biloba, commonly known as the biloba orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often display vibrant colors such as red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, and it produces small, fragrant blooms that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Orianthera biloba is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
