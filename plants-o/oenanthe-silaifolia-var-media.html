Oenanthe silaifolia var. media

Oenanthe silaifolia var. media, commonly known as the broad-leaved water dropwort, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, often with a reddish tinge. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its leaves are broadly ovate to lanceolate, with a distinctive wavy margin.

It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wet meadows, streambanks, and marshy areas.
