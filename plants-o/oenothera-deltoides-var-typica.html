Oenothera deltoides var. typica

Oenothera deltoides var. typica, commonly known as the evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening and close by morning. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while its stems are erect and branched.

The evening primrose is also valued for its oil-rich seeds, which are used in various commercial and medicinal applications.
