Ophrys × albertiana nothosubsp. isabellae

Ophrys × albertiana nothosubsp.

isabellae, commonly known as the Isabelline bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Balkans. It is characterized by its intricate floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination.

The plant produces showy, bee-like flowers with a distinctive dark brown and yellow pattern. It thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in open grasslands and woodland edges.

This rare and specialized orchid is of interest to botanists due to its unique pollination mechanism and hybrid origin.
