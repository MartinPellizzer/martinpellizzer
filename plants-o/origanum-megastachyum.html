Origanum megastachyum

Origanum megastachyum, commonly known as the giant oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and showy purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, making it one of the tallest species in the Origanum genus. Its leaves are often used in cooking, though they are less common than other oregano varieties.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sunlight, thriving in temperate climates.
