Odina wodier var. wirtgenii

Odina wodier var. wirtgenii, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial with bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and compact growth habit.
