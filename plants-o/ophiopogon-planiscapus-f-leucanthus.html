Ophiopogon planiscapus f. leucanthus

Ophiopogon planiscapus f. leucanthus, commonly known as the white-flowered Japanese forest grass, is a cultivar of the genus Ophiopogon, native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage that forms a low-growing, mat-forming plant. The plant features striking white to pale lavender flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial to full shade and is often used in ornamental landscaping for its attractive foliage and floral display.

This cultivar is prized for its ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils and its adaptability to various garden conditions.
