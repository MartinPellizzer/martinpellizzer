Osbeckia pauciramosa

Osbeckia pauciramosa, commonly known as the slender osbeckia, is a low-growing perennial herb native to South America.

It typically has a creeping stem that produces numerous small, alternate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, which are often borne on short pedicels. It is adapted to grow in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Osbeckia pauciramosa is characterized by its hairy foliage and is often found in regions with a temperate climate.
