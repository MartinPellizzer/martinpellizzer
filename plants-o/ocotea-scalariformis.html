Ocotea scalariformis

Ocotea scalariformis, commonly known as the scalloped ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves with a distinctive scalloped margin. Its bark is smooth and often has a reddish or brownish hue.

Ocotea scalariformis is valued for its timber, which is used in furniture and cabinetry due to its durability and fine grain.
