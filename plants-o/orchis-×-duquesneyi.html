Orchis × duquesneyi

Orchis × duquesneyi, commonly known as the Duquesne orchid, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Orchis morrowii and Orchis purpurea.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that typically range in color from purple to pink. The plant is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, woodland habitats. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
