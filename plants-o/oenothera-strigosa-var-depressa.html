Oenothera strigosa var. depressa

Oenothera strigosa var. depressa, commonly known as the plains evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the central and western United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairie and steppe habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
