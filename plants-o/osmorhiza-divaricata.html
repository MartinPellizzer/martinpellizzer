Osmorhiza divaricata

Osmorhiza divaricata, commonly known as California boneset, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white, umbellate flowers. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic when crushed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and streamside habitats.

Osmorhiza divaricata is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its historical use in traditional medicine.
