Olgaea altimurana

Olgaea altimurana, commonly known as the highland olgaea, is a rare flowering plant native to the Andean highlands.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep violet. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to high-altitude conditions with strong winds and intense sunlight. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss.

Olgaea altimurana is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations and limited geographic distribution.
