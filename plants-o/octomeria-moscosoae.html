Octomeria moscosoae

Octomeria moscosoae, commonly known as the Mexican star cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its unique, star-shaped spines and clustered growth pattern. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, adding a striking visual contrast to its spiny structure.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
