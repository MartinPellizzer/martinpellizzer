Oligochaetochilus squamatus

Oligochaetochilus squamatus, commonly known as the scaly leathery orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often found in forest undergrowth. The plant has a short, thick rhizome with a dense covering of scale-like structures. Its leaves are fleshy and arranged in a rosette, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers.

The species is characterized by its unique morphology and is of interest to botanists and orchid enthusiasts.
