Oxalis alstonii subsp. lutzii

Oxalis alstonii subsp.

lutzii, commonly known as the Lutz's wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets, often adorned with fine, silver-white hairs.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland settings.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and attractive addition to native plant collections.
