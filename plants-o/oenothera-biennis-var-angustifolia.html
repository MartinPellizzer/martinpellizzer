Oenothera biennis var. angustifolia

Oenothera biennis var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and showy yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, oval seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and dry fields.

Oenothera biennis var. angustifolia is a member of the Onagraceae family and is known for its ecological importance to pollinators.
