Oxytropis glabra f. villosa

Oxytropis glabra f. villosa, commonly known as the hairy milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect stems covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its trifoliate leaves are palmately arranged, with each leaflet being oval and hairy.

Oxytropis glabra f. villosa is adapted to poor soils and is often found in dry grasslands and disturbed areas.
