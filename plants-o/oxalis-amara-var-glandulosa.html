Oxalis amara var. glandulosa

Oxalis amara var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins and produces clusters of small, yellow, five-petaled flowers. The plant is characterized by its glandular structures on the stems and leaves, which give it its specific variety name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
