Olea capensis subsp. capensis

Olea capensis subsp.

capensis, commonly known as the common olive, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, fleshy fruits that contain a single stone.

The plant produces fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by the development of its edible drupes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
