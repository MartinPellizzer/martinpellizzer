Odontites serotinus var. salinus

Odontites serotinus var. salinus, commonly known as the salt marsh scurvygrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to coastal regions.

It thrives in saline and brackish environments, often found in salt marshes and tidal flats. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or greenish flowers in early spring. It is adapted to withstand high salt concentrations and periodic flooding.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
