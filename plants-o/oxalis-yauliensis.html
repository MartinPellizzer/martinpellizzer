Oxalis yauliensis

Oxalis yauliensis, commonly known as the Yauli wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in cloud forests. Its foliage contains oxalic acid, which can be toxic if ingested in large quantities.

Oxalis yauliensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
