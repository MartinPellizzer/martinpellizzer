Ornithocephalus tripterus

Ornithocephalus tripterus, commonly known as the three-lobed orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive three-lobed petals and is native to tropical regions of South America. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rainforests. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption in poor soils.

The flowers are usually fragrant and attract various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
