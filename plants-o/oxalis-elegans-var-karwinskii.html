Oxalis elegans var. karwinskii

Oxalis elegans var. karwinskii, commonly known as the Karwinski's wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins and produces small, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family, characterized by its calcium oxalate crystals in the leaves.

Oxalis elegans var. karwinskii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
