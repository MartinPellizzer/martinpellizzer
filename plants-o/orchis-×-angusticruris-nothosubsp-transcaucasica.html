Orchis × angusticruris nothosubsp.

transcaucasica, commonly known as the Transcaucasian narrow-horned orchid, is a hybrid species native to the Caucasus region. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and exhibits characteristics of its parent species, Orchis angusticruris and Orchis morio.

The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers with a distinctive lip structure typical of orchids. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and hybrid origin, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
