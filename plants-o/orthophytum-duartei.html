Orthophytum duartei

Orthophytum duartei, commonly known as the Duarte's orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a water-holding cup. The plant produces striking inflorescences with colorful, showy flowers that attract pollinators. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
