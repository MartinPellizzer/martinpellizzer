Ophryosporus pubescens

Ophryosporus pubescens, commonly known as the hairy bladderwrack, is a marine brown alga that thrives in intertidal zones.

It is characterized by its dark brown, leathery fronds covered in fine, hairy structures called paraphyses. This species is typically found attached to rocky substrates in coastal areas. Ophryosporus pubescens plays a vital role in marine ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various organisms.

Its reproductive structures, known as conceptacles, are involved in the release of spores for sexual reproduction.
