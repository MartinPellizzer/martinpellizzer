Oenothera latipetala

Oenothera latipetala, commonly known as the broad-petaled evening primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southwestern United States.

It features showy, yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has hairy, ribbed stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are typically borne in clusters at the ends of branches.

Oenothera latipetala is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils.
