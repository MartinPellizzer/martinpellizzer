Oenothera coquimbensis var. grandidentata

Oenothera coquimbensis var. grandidentata, commonly known as the Coquimbo evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to central Chile.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, demonstrating adaptability to dry conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is part of the Onagraceae family, known for its diverse and often showy flowers.
