Oberonia drepanophylla

Oberonia drepanophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. It has a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in less fertile soils.

Oberonia drepanophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated by enthusiasts.
