Opuntia monacanthos subsp. arechavaletae

Opuntia monacanthos subsp.

arechavaletae, commonly known as the Arechavaleta prickly pear, is a cactus species native to the arid regions of central Mexico. It is characterized by its flat, spineless pads and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant is adapted to survive in hot, dry environments with minimal water requirements. Its pads are edible and have been used traditionally for food and medicinal purposes.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
