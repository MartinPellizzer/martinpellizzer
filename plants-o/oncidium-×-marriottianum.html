Oncidium × marriottianum

Oncidium × marriottianum, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which are often accompanied by green or brown markings. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in conditions with intermittent moisture.

Oncidium × marriottianum is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
