Ophrys fuciflora var. grandiflora

Ophrys fuciflora var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered bee orchid, is a striking orchid species native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive floral morphology, featuring a complex lip that mimics the appearance of a female bee, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded habitats. Its flowers are large and showy, with a yellow-green coloration and intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

This orchid is a prime example of evolutionary adaptation in the plant kingdom, showcasing the sophisticated relationships between plants and their pollinators.
