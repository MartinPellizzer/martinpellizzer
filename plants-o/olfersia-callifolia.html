Olfersia callifolia

Olfersia callifolia, commonly known as the white orchid or white lady orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and leathery, providing structural support and aiding in water retention.

Olfersia callifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and delicate floral scent.
