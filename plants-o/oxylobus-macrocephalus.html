Oxylobus macrocephalus

Oxylobus macrocephalus, commonly known as the large-headed oxylobus, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other damp habitats.

Oxylobus macrocephalus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
