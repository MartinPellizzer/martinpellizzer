Origanum vestitum

Origanum vestitum, commonly known as woolly oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, gray-green foliage and small, aromatic flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes. Its leaves are used in culinary applications and traditional medicine due to their essential oil content.

Origanum vestitum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
