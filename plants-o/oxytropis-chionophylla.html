Oxytropis chionophylla

Oxytropis chionophylla, commonly known as the snow-lace bush clover, is a flowering plant native to the high elevations of the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that grows in alpine and subalpine environments, often found in rocky soils and open meadows. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically white or pale blue, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Oxytropis chionophylla plays a role in stabilizing soil in mountainous regions and provides forage for certain wildlife species.
