Orthosiphon menthifolius

Orthosiphon menthifolius, commonly known as Java tea or *Plectranthus barbatus*, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves with serrated edges, and small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple or blue.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and used in traditional medicine for its diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.
