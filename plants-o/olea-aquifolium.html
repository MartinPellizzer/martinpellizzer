Olea aquifolium

Olea aquifolium, commonly known as the common holly, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that are often spiny and arranged in opposite pairs. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries in the fall. Olea aquifolium is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping.

It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.
