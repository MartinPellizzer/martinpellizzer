Ononis confusa

Ononis confusa, commonly known as the common whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features slender, branched stems and produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a hairy surface, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils.

Ononis confusa is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other open habitats where it can establish itself quickly.
