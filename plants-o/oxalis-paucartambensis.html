Oxalis paucartambensis

Oxalis paucartambensis, commonly known as the Andean wood sorrel, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped lobes and small, delicate flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas. Its botanical characteristics include a short, fibrous root system and stems that are often hairy.

Oxalis paucartambensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
