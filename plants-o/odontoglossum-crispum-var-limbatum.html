Odontoglossum crispum var. limbatum

Odontoglossum crispum var. limbatum, commonly known as the limbatum orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the high altitudes of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in cool, shaded environments, often in cloud forests, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soil. Its flowers, which can be white, yellow, or pink, are highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts for their beauty and rarity.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses due to its specific environmental requirements.
