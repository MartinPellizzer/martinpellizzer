Ophiopogon intermedius var. pauciflorus

Ophiopogon intermedius var. pauciflorus, commonly known as the few-flowered spathiphyllum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features dense, grass-like foliage with narrow, dark green leaves that form a compact rosette. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, emerging from a spathe-like structure. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and landscape design.
