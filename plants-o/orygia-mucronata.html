Orygia mucronata

Orygia mucronata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers a sunny to partially shaded environment. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slight waxy coating, and it produces fragrant, greenish-yellow blooms.

Orygia mucronata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
