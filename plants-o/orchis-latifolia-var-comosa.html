Orchis latifolia var. comosa

Orchis latifolia var. comosa, commonly known as the broad-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and tall flowering stems. The plant produces showy, violet or purple flowers arranged in a spike, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
