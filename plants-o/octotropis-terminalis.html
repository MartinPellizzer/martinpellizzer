Octotropis terminalis

Octotropis terminalis, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, blue to purple flowers. The plant has a fibrous root system that helps it survive in dry, sandy soils. Its seeds are adapted for dispersal by wind, allowing it to colonize disturbed and open habitats.

Octotropis terminalis plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife in its native environment.
