Ocotea campechiana

Ocotea campechiana, commonly known as the Mexican laurel, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Central America and northern South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes well-known genera such as Cinnamomum and Persea. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, aromatic bark. It typically grows in humid, tropical lowland forests and is often found in secondary vegetation.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine and as a source of aromatic wood.
