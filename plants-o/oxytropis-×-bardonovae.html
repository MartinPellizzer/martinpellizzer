Oxytropis × bardonovae

Oxytropis × bardonovae, commonly known as the hybrid burr vetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces hairy, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and steppe habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
