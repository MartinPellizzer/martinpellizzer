Odontarrhena serpentina

Odontarrhena serpentina, commonly known as the serpent's root, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pale green leaves. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used traditionally for its purported healing benefits.
