Olea fragrans var. longifolia

Olea fragrans var. longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf olive, is a species of flowering plant in the Oleaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are typically narrow and elongated, giving the plant its common name. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions and thrives in well-drained soils.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aromatic foliage and delicate blossoms.
