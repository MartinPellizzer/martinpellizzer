Oenothera maysillesii

Oenothera maysillesii, commonly known as the Maysille's evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branching.

Oenothera maysillesii is part of the genus Oenothera, which includes many species of evening primroses known for their ecological and medicinal significance.
