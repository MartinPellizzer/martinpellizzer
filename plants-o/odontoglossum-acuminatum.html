Odontoglossum acuminatum

Odontoglossum acuminatum, commonly known as the Acuminatum Orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often forming a rosette shape.

The flowers are usually borne on tall, slender stems and can produce multiple blooms throughout the growing season.
