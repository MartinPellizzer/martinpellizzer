Oncidium aureum var. stenochilum

Oncidium aureum var. stenochilum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical orchid species native to Central and South America.

It features vibrant yellow flowers with a contrasting green throat, showcasing its striking visual appeal. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture from the air. Its inflorescence consists of a raceme of showy, fragrant blooms that appear during the dry season.

The leaves are strap-like, fleshy, and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water storage and photosynthesis.
