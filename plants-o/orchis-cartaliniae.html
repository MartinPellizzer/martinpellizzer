Orchis cartaliniae

Orchis cartaliniae, commonly known as the Cartalina orchid, is a species native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is a perennial herb with a tall, erect stem that bears a single spike of showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged along the stem. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in open woodland or rocky habitats.

Orchis cartaliniae is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
