Osmunda transvaalensis

Osmunda transvaalensis, commonly known as the Transvaal fern, is a species of royal fern native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, upright fronds that can reach up to 2 meters in height, with a distinctive dark green color and a smooth, leathery texture. The plant produces spore-bearing cones at the base of its fronds, which are clustered in compact, spherical structures. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

Osmunda transvaalensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological systems of its native habitat.
