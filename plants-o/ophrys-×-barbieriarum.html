Ophrys × barbieriarum

Ophrys × barbieriarum, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant that results from the cross between Ophrys apifera (the bee orchid) and Ophrys insectifera (the wasp orchid).

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its intricate and mimicry-based floral structures. The plant's flowers closely resemble female bees or wasps, a trait that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy by attracting male insects. Its botanical features include a basal rosette of leaves and a flowering stem that bears a single, showy flower.

This hybrid is valued for its unique appearance and ecological significance in its native habitat.
