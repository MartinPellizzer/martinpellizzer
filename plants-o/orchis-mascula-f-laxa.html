Orchis mascula f. laxa

Orchis mascula f. laxa, commonly known as the loose orchid, is a botanical variant of the male orchid.

It is characterized by its lax, more open growth habit compared to the typical form. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers with a distinctive spurred lip. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

This orchid is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ecological significance in supporting pollinators.
