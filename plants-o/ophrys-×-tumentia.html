Ophrys × tumentia

Ophrys × tumentia, commonly known as the tawny orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the genus Ophrys, which is known for its mimicry of female insects to attract male pollinators. The plant produces striking, bee-mimicking flowers with intricate patterns and a strong, often unpleasant odor. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

The botanical features of Ophrys × tumentia include its tall, erect stems and its ability to form dense colonies in suitable environments.
