Onosma synanthera

Onosma synanthera, commonly known as the Persian burnet, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant features silver-gray leaves covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Onosma synanthera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
