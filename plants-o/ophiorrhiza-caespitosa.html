Ophiorrhiza caespitosa

Ophiorrhiza caespitosa, commonly known as the clustered Ophiorrhiza, is a flowering plant species native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is a small shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. Its leaves are simple, elliptical, and have a slightly waxy texture.

Ophiorrhiza caespitosa is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks.
