Orchis cruenta var. lanceolata

Orchis cruenta var. lanceolata, commonly known as the blood orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red and purple flower spikes, which can reach up to 30 cm in height. The leaves are typically lanceolate, hence the variety name, and are arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces showy, resupinate flowers with a distinctive lip that often has a dark red or purplish hue.

It thrives in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows.
