Ornithogalum umbratile

Ornithogalum umbratile, commonly known as the shadow lily, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces a tall, slender stem bearing a single, bell-shaped flower with white petals and a green throat. It thrives in woodland or shady environments, preferring moist, well-drained soil.

The species is noted for its nocturnal flowering, with the bloom opening at night and closing by morning.
