Octamyrtus lanceolata

Octamyrtus lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myrtle, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by dark blue, spherical berries. It is well adapted to coastal and sandy soils, making it a hardy and resilient species.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable climates.
