Ophrys × sooi

Ophrys × sooi, commonly known as the Soudan bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Balkans.

It is characterized by its unique floral morphology, featuring a labellum that mimics the appearance of a female bee, a trait that plays a key role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers are usually a mix of brown and yellow, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Ophrys × sooi is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ecological significance in supporting pollinator populations.
