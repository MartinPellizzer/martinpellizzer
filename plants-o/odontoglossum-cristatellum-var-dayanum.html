Odontoglossum cristatellum var. dayanum

Odontoglossum cristatellum var. dayanum, commonly known as the Dayanum orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a sympodial orchid with a short rhizome and produces pseudobulbs that bear leaves and flower spikes. The plant typically grows in high-altitude cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its flowers are usually small, fragrant, and come in shades of white, green, and pink, with a distinctive lip.

It is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts due to its ornamental value and relatively manageable care requirements.
