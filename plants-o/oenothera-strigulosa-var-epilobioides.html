Oenothera strigulosa var. epilobioides

Oenothera strigulosa var. epilobioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces showy, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats like prairies and dunes.

The species is valued for its ecological role and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
