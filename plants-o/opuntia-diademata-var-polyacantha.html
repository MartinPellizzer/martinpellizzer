Opuntia diademata var. polyacantha

Opuntia diademata var. polyacantha, commonly known as the blue agave or desert spoon, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that are often ribbed and covered in small, spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by fruit that contains inedible seeds. Its leaves are modified into spines, and the stems store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.
