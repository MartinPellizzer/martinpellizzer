Oenothera cespitosa f. albiflora

Oenothera cespitosa f. albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflower evening primrose, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting nocturnal pollinators.
