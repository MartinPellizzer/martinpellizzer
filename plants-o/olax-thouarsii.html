Olax thouarsii

Olax thouarsii, commonly known as the Madagascar olive, is a shrub or small tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Olacaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Olax thouarsii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
