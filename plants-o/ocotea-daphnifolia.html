Ocotea daphnifolia

Ocotea daphnifolia, commonly known as the fragrant ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree belonging to the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, warm climates. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, dark blue drupe that contains a single seed.

Ocotea daphnifolia is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
