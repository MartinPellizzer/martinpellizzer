Ornithogalum kodjuricum var. pluriflorum

Ornithogalum kodjuricum var. pluriflorum, commonly known as the mountain onion or white bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a basal rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers during the summer months. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, bulbous root system, which is often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
