Onobrychis humilis

Onobrychis humilis, commonly known as the lesser burclover, is a low-growing perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It features trifoliate leaves with a hairy underside and produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in racemes. The plant is characterized by its cylindrical, spiny pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Onobrychis humilis is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil restoration efforts.
