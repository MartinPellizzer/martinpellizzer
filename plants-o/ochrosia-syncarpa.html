Ochrosia syncarpa

Ochrosia syncarpa, commonly known as the red-veined orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with deep red veins. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a waxy texture, and it thrives in humid, well-drained environments.

Ochrosia syncarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
