Olax angulata

Olax angulata, commonly known as the angular olax, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Olacaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often angular in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the study of tropical flora.
