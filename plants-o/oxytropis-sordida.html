Oxytropis sordida

Oxytropis sordida, commonly known as the gloomy locoweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. Its leaves are typically narrow and trifoliate, and it produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Oxytropis sordida is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in certain ecological contexts.
