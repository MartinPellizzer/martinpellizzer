Ophrys apifera var. maculata

Ophrys apifera var. maculata, commonly known as the bee orchid, is a species of orchid native to Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive flower shape that mimics a female bee, a feature that plays a key role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and is adapted to temperate climates. Its flowers are usually yellow with dark markings, and it produces small, greenish-brown pods containing seeds.

This orchid is known for its specialized relationship with specific pollinators, enhancing its ecological significance.
