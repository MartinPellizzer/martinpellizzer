Oxalis candicans

Oxalis candicans, commonly known as the white wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive white veins and produces delicate, five-petaled white flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, preferring cool to temperate climates.

Oxalis candicans is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
