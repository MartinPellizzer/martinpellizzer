Oxalis boreaui

Oxalis boreaui, commonly known as the Sierra oxalis, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. It produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Oxalis boreaui is a perennial herb that thrives in cool climates and is often found in mountainous regions.
