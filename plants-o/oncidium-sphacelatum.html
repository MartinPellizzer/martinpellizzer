Oncidium sphacelatum

Oncidium sphacelatum, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its long, pendulous inflorescences that bear numerous small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air. Its flowers are usually yellow or green with red or brown markings, and they bloom in clusters.

Oncidium sphacelatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
