Olea hainanensis

Olea hainanensis, commonly known as Hainan olive, is a small evergreen tree native to China's Hainan Island.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, elliptical fruits that are typically harvested when mature. Olea hainanensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has limited commercial use compared to other olive species.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.
