Oberonia pachyambon

Oberonia pachyambon, commonly known as the thick-veined orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, sympodial growth habit and produces striking, showy flowers with intricate patterns. The plant features long, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are typically borne on terminal racemes and exhibit a range of colors, including yellow, orange, and red.

This orchid thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil for optimal growth.
