Ophrys × deheyniana

Ophrys × deheyniana, commonly known as the Dehynian orchid, is a hybrid species within the Ophrys genus, known for its intricate and often deceptive floral structures.

This plant is characterized by its highly specialized morphology, with a labellum that mimics the appearance of female insects to attract male pollinators. It typically grows in calcareous soils and is found in regions with a temperate climate, such as parts of Europe. The plant's flowers are usually pale yellow to brown, with intricate patterns that aid in its mimicry.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, Ophrys × deheyniana is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
