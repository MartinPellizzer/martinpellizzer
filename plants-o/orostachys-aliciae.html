Orostachys aliciae

Orostachys aliciae, commonly known as the Alice's ice plant, is a succulent species native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming growth habit and fleshy, bluish-green leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers during the summer months. It is well adapted to rocky soils and full sun exposure, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Orostachys aliciae is also valued for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
