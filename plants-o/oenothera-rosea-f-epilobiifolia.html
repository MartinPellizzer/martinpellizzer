Oenothera rosea f. epilobiifolia

Oenothera rosea f. epilobiifolia, commonly known as the woolly evening primrose, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and showy, pink to reddish-orange flowers that open in the evening. The plant belongs to the Onagraceae family and is known for its nocturnal blooming habit. Its flowers have four petals and are typically borne on long, slender stems.

This variety is distinguished by its broader, epilobium-like leaves, which give it its specific epithet.
