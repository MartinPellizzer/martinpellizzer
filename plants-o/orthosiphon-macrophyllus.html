Orthosiphon macrophyllus

Orthosiphon macrophyllus, commonly known as broadleaf horseleaf, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and spikes of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant is often found in wetlands and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist, tropical environments. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
