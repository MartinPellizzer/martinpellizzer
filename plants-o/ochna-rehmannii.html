Ochna rehmannii

Ochna rehmannii, commonly known as the African coffee tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that turn red when mature, containing a single seed. Ochna rehmannii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some African cultures.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
