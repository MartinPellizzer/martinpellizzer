Oisodix oblongifolia

Oisodix oblongifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf oisodix, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along its stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, which bloom during the wet season. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Oisodix oblongifolia thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in dense undergrowth of rainforests.
