Orchis morio var. albicans

Orchis morio var. albicans, commonly known as the white helleborine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in woodland habitats and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy texture, while its flowers are arranged in a loose spike.

The white helleborine is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
