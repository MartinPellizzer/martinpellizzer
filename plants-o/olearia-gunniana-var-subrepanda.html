Olearia gunniana var. subrepanda

Olearia gunniana var. subrepanda, commonly known as the silver-leaved daisy, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in coastal and subalpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It has a low-growing habit with a mat-forming structure, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
