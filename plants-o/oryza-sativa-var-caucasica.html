Oryza sativa var. caucasica

Oryza sativa var. caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian rice, is a lesser-known subspecies of rice native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and small, elongated grains. This variety typically thrives in temperate climates and requires a moderate amount of water for optimal growth. The plant has a short vegetative period and matures relatively quickly compared to other rice varieties.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for agricultural research and biodiversity studies.
