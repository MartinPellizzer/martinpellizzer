Opuntia spinosibacca

Opuntia spinosibacca, commonly known as the spiny barrel cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in drought conditions. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits known as tunas. Its spines are modified leaves that help protect the plant from herbivores and reduce water loss.

Opuntia spinosibacca is also notable for its ability to propagate through offsets, making it a resilient and widely distributed species.
