Ophrys decembris

Ophrys decembris, commonly known as the twelve-lobed helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its complex, lip-like flower structure. The plant produces showy, greenish-yellow flowers with intricate patterns that mimic female insects, aiding in its pollination strategy. It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and meadows.

Ophrys decembris is known for its ability to form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi, which help in nutrient uptake.
