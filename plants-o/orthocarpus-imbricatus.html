Orthocarpus imbricatus

Orthocarpus imbricatus, commonly known as the mountain daisy, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine layer of white hair.

Orthocarpus imbricatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
