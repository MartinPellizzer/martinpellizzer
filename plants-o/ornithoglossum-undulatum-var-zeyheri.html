Ornithoglossum undulatum var. zeyheri

Ornithoglossum undulatum var. zeyheri, commonly known as the zig-zag gentian, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features striking, violet-blue, tubular flowers with a distinctive zig-zag pattern on the petals, which gives it its common name. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

The leaves are lance-shaped and have a waxy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
