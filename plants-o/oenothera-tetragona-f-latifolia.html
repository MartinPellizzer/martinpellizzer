Oenothera tetragona f. latifolia

Oenothera tetragona f. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf four-angle evening primrose, is a variant of the evening primrose species native to North America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairies and disturbed areas. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
