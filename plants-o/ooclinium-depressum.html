Ooclinium depressum

Ooclinium depressum, commonly known as the flat ooclinium, is a unique aquatic plant belonging to the family Oocystaceae.

It is characterized by its flattened, ribbon-like morphology, which allows it to spread across the surfaces of water bodies. The plant lacks true roots and instead relies on rhizoids for anchorage and nutrient absorption. It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and lakes.

Its simple structure and reproductive strategy make it an important component of aquatic ecosystems.
