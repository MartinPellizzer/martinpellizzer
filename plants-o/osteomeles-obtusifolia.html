Osteomeles obtusifolia

Osteomeles obtusifolia, commonly known as the blunt-leaf coralberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, red-orange berries that persist through winter, providing food for birds and other wildlife. The plant has simple, ovate to elliptic leaves with a smooth margin and a glossy upper surface. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and swamps.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
