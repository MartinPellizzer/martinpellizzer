Origanum viridulum

Origanum viridulum, commonly known as green oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, dark green leaves and small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant. Its essential oils contain compounds like carvacrol and thymol, which contribute to its aromatic and medicinal properties.

Origanum viridulum is often used in culinary applications and traditional herbal remedies.
