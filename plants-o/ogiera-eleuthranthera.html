Ogiera eleuthranthera

Ogiera eleuthranthera, commonly known as the white-flowered orchid, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, white petals and greenish sepals. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a sweet fragrance that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Ogiera eleuthranthera is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
