Ocimum auricula

Ocimum auricula, commonly known as the auricled basil, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and southern Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves, which are typically oval and slightly heart-shaped, with a distinctive auriculate (ear-like) base. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. Ocimum auricula is often cultivated for its fragrant leaves used in culinary and medicinal applications.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a versatile addition to gardens and herbal collections.
