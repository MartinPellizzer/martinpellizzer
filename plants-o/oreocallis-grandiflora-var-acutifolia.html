Oreocallis grandiflora var. acutifolia

Oreocallis grandiflora var. acutifolia, commonly known as the sharpleaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The leaves are narrow and pointed, giving the plant its common name, while the flowers are typically white or pale pink with a yellow lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in tropical gardens for its beauty.
