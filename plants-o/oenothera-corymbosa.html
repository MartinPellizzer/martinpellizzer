Oenothera corymbosa

Oenothera corymbosa, commonly known as the showy evening primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Oenothera corymbosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
