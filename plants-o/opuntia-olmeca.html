Opuntia olmeca

Opuntia olmeca, commonly known as the Olmec prickly pear, is a cacti species native to southern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems that are covered in spines and are often ribbed. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers, which are followed by edible fruits known as tunas. Opuntia olmeca thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting well to poor soil conditions.

It is also valued for its historical and cultural significance among indigenous communities.
