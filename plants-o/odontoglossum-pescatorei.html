Odontoglossum pescatorei

Odontoglossum pescatorei, commonly known as the Pescatore's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, often with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil for optimal growth.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
