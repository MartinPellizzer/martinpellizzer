Onagra purpurea

Onagra purpurea, commonly known as evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Onagraceae and is characterized by its showy, violet or yellow flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and it thrives in sandy or loamy soils. Its seeds are enclosed in a capsule and are often used in traditional medicine for their oil content.

The plant is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators like bees and moths.
