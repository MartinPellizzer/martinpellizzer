Ophrys sicula

Ophrys sicula, commonly known as the Sicilian bee orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Sicily.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which closely resembles a female bee, a feature evolved to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brownish in color, with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of a bee's body.

Ophrys sicula is a perennial herb that produces a single spike of flowers, often with a single or occasionally two blooms per inflorescence.
