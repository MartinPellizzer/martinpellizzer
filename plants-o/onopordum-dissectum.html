Onopordum dissectum

Onopordum dissectum, commonly known as the Spanish thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a robust root system and can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, with silvery-gray, deeply lobed leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from summer to autumn. Its leaves and stems are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and ability to thrive in poor soils.
