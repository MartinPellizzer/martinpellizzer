Onobrychis argentea var. longeaculeata

Onobrychis argentea var. longeaculeata, commonly known as silver vetch, is a perennial legume native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-white foliage and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces elongated, hairy pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Silver vetch is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
