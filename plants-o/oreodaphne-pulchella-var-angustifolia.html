Oreodaphne pulchella var. angustifolia

Oreodaphne pulchella var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved oreodaphne, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. The leaves are elliptical, with a narrow shape, and are usually glossy green on the upper surface. It produces small, white flowers arranged in panicles, followed by small, round fruits containing a single seed.

This plant is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses in local medicine.
