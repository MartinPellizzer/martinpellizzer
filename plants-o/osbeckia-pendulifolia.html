Osbeckia pendulifolia

Osbeckia pendulifolia, commonly known as the hanging willow herb, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a low-growing perennial with slender, often trailing stems that produce small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and forest floors.

Osbeckia pendulifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
