Ophrys × bazzarae

Ophrys × bazzarae, commonly known as the Bazzarae bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its intricate flower structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its flowers are usually a mix of brown, yellow, and green hues, with a prominent lip that resembles a bee's body.

Ophrys × bazzarae is a rare and protected species, often found in specific microhabitats within its native range.
