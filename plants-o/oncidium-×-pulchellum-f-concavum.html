Oncidium × pulchellum f. concavum

Oncidium × pulchellum f. concavum, commonly known as the concave orchid, is a hybrid orchid species characterized by its unique floral structure.

It features pendulous, showy flowers with a distinctive concave lip that sets it apart from other orchid varieties. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical and subtropical climates. Its foliage consists of broad, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating.

This orchid requires bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.
