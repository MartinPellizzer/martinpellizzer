Oncidium incarum

Oncidium incarum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It features a sympodial growth habit with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often arranged in a raceme. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, typically arranged in two rows along the stem.

Oncidium incarum thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining potting media for optimal growth.
