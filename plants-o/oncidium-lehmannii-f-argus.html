Oncidium lehmannii f. argus

Oncidium lehmannii f. argus, commonly known as the Argus Oncidium, is a hybrid orchid species that exhibits striking, intricate patterns on its flowers.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its long, narrow petals and a distinctive, colorful lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Its flowers bloom in clusters, often displaying a mix of yellow, orange, and red hues, making it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
