Osteospermum dichotomum

Osteospermum dichotomum, commonly known as the blue gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, spiky inflorescences. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Osteospermum dichotomum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
