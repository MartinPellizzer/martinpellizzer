Ochthocharis ovata

Ochthocharis ovata, commonly known as the ovate orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its ovate-shaped leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its flowering period usually occurs during the rainy season, making it a notable species in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.

Ochthocharis ovata is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitats.
