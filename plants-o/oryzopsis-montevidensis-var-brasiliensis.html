Oryzopsis montevidensis var. brasiliensis

Oryzopsis montevidensis var. brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and wetlands. It produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
