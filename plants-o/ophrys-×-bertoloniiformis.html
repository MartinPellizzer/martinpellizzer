Ophrys × bertoloniiformis

Ophrys × bertoloniiformis, commonly known as the mimic orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys bertoloni.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Italy. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking, bee-mimicking flowers that are often yellow or brown in color. These flowers are specialized for pollination through mimicry, attracting male bees that attempt to mate with the flower.

The botanical characteristics of Ophrys × bertoloniiformis make it a fascinating subject in the study of plant evolution and pollination strategies.
