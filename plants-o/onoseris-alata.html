Onoseris alata

Onoseris alata, commonly known as the winged coneflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, winged stems and showy yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in prairies and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall.

Onoseris alata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
