Oxalis aureociliata

Oxalis aureociliata, commonly known as the golden-ciliate wood sorrel, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of delicate, yellow flowers. The plant features hairy stems and leaves with a distinctive golden border, which gives it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Oxalis aureociliata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
