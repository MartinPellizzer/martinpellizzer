Oplismenus colonum var. walteri

Oplismenus colonum var. walteri, commonly known as Walter's ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color, arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces tall, upright flowering stems topped with clusters of small, pale purple to pinkish flowers. It blooms from late summer into early autumn, adding ornamental value to gardens.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in native plant gardens for their aesthetic and ecological benefits.
