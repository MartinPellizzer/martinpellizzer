Ophiorrhiza alatiflora var. trichoneura

Ophiorrhiza alatiflora var. trichoneura, commonly known as the hairy-veined marsh mallow, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in wet or swampy areas. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with hairy veins, and it produces small, white flowers clustered in racemes. Its fruits are capsule-like, containing numerous small seeds.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and supporting local biodiversity.
