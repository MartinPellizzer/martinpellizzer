Otolepis cambodiana

Otolepis cambodiana, commonly known as the Cambodian star sedge, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

Otolepis cambodiana plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and small aquatic life.
