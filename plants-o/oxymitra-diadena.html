Oxymitra diadena

Oxymitra diadena, commonly known as the pink ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a unique morphology, featuring a single, slender stem that produces a single flower. The plant has a fibrous root system and is adapted to grow in shaded, moist environments, typically in pine flatwoods. Its flowers are characterized by their pinkish-white color and a distinctive, ghost-like appearance.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, Oxymitra diadena is considered an endangered species.
