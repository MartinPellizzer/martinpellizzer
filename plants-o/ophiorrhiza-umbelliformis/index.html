Ophiorrhiza Umbelliformis

Ophiorrhiza umbelliformis is a unique flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia. It is known for its striking, umbel-like inflorescence that resembles a cluster of tiny umbrellas. This species thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories and along riverbanks. Its delicate appearance and ecological significance make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists. Despite its beauty, Ophiorrhiza umbelliformis remains relatively understudied and endangered due to habitat loss and deforestation.
