Odina obcordata

Odina obcordata, commonly known as the heartleaf arrowhead, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its roots are fibrous and can be used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Odina obcordata thrives in wet, marshy environments and is often found in swamps and along the edges of ponds.
