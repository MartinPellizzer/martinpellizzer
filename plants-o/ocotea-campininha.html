Ocotea campininha

Ocotea campininha, commonly known as the Brazilian cinnamon tree, is a member of the Lauraceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, dark purple berries. Its bark and leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species for ecological and pharmacological research.
