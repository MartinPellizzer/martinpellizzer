Orchis morio var. picta-rosea

Orchis morio var. picta-rosea, commonly known as the Rose-veined Orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem that bears a single, showy flower with a distinctive rose-pink coloration. The flower features a lip that is often marked with intricate patterns, enhancing its ornamental appeal. This variety thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is typically found in woodland habitats.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique coloration and role in pollination by specific insect species.
