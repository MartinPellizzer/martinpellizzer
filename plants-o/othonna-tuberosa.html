Othonna tuberosa

Othonna tuberosa, commonly known as the tuberous othonna, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, tuberous roots that store water, enabling it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are often fleshy and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

Othonna tuberosa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
