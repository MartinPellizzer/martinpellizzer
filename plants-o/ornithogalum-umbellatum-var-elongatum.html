Ornithogalum umbellatum var. elongatum

Ornithogalum umbellatum var. elongatum, commonly known as the long-stalked star of Bethlehem, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of star-shaped, white to pale pink flowers in late spring. The plant is characterized by its elongated flower stems, which give it its varietal name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
