Ophrys holosericea subsp. homeri

Ophrys holosericea subsp.

homeri, commonly known as the Homeric bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its intricate floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee, a feature evolved to attract male bees for pollination.

The plant produces showy, pale yellow to brownish flowers with intricate patterns that serve as visual signals to its pollinators. It thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in many parts of its range.
