Otites polaris var. pohleanus

Otites polaris var. pohleanus, commonly known as the Arctic bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, pale blue to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils and is often found in tundra environments. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, adapted to withstand cold temperatures and strong winds.

Otites polaris var. pohleanus plays a role in pollination and serves as a food source for local wildlife.
