Ophrys scolopax f. viridiflora

Ophrys scolopax f. viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered bee orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to specific regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant produces greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive bee-like shape, making it visually captivating. It thrives in moist, shaded habitats such as woodland floors and meadows.

Due to its specialized requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in many areas.
