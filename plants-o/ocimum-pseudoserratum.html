Ocimum pseudoserratum

Ocimum pseudoserratum, commonly known as the purple basil, is a member of the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its aromatic, purple-tinged leaves. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with square stems and opposite leaves. Its flowers are small, tubular, and clustered in whorls, attracting pollinators like bees.

Ocimum pseudoserratum is valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
