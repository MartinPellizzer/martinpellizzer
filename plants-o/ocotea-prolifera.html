Ocotea prolifera

Ocotea prolifera, commonly known as the tropical laurel, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America, typically growing in tropical rainforests. The plant is characterized by its evergreen, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils, which have been used in traditional medicine.

Ocotea prolifera plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
