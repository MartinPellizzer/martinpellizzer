Oenothera serrulata var. spinulosa

Oenothera serrulata var. spinulosa, commonly known as the spiny evening primrose, is a species native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features narrow, serrated leaves and produces showy yellow or orange flowers at night. Its flowers are pollinated by moths, and it is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
