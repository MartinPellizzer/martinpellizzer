Octomeria pusilla

Octomeria pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf octomeria, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It typically grows in rocky, high-altitude environments, where it thrives in cool and moist conditions. The plant features tiny, clustered leaves and produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale pink. Its compact size and unique floral structure make it a subject of interest in botanical studies.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
