Orchis fuscescens

Orchis fuscescens, commonly known as the purple orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, violet-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and thrives in woodland or meadow habitats. Its flowers are usually arranged in a loose spike and are pollinated by insects such as bees and butterflies.

Orchis fuscescens is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
