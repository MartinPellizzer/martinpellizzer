Orchis leptophylla

Orchis leptophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers that are typically arranged in a loose raceme. Orchis leptophylla thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in many regions.
