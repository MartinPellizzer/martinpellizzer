Ornithogalum obconicum

Ornithogalum obconicum, commonly known as the broad-leaved star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous underground stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens despite being considered invasive in some regions.
