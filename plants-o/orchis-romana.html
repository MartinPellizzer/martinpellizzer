Orchis romana

Orchis romana, commonly known as the Roman orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and thrives in woodland or meadow environments. Its flowers are usually arranged in a spike and have a distinctive, spurred lip that aids in pollination by insects.

Orchis romana is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
