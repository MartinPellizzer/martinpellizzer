Oxalis lanceifolia

Oxalis lanceifolia, commonly known as the lanceleaf wood sorrel, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive lanceolate shape, and its flowers are typically yellow with five petals. The plant produces small, bead-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Oxalis lanceifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to enrich soil through its nitrogen-fixing properties.
