Onosma heterophylla subsp. viridis

Onosma heterophylla subsp.

viridis, commonly known as the greenleaf synanthus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish-yellow, arranged in loose clusters, and bloom from spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
