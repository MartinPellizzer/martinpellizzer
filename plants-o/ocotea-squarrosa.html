Ocotea squarrosa

Ocotea squarrosa, commonly known as the white sand tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, whitish bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by spherical, woody fruits.

Ocotea squarrosa is often found in moist, well-drained soils in lowland and mountainous regions.
