Oncidium obryzatum var. dasystalix

Oncidium obryzatum var. dasystalix, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical orchid native to South America.

It features pendant, yellowish-green flowers with a distinctive red or orange lip, often resembling a golden crown. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are strap-like, medium green, and often covered in fine pubescence.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil with consistent moisture.
