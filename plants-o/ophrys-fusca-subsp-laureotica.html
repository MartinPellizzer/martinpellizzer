Ophrys fusca subsp. laureotica

Ophrys fusca subsp.

laureotica, commonly known as the laureate bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to parts of southern Europe. It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone-rich habitats, preferring well-drained soils and sunny to partially shaded conditions. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brownish in color, with intricate patterns that aid in its deceptive pollination strategy.

Due to its specific habitat requirements and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species in many regions.
