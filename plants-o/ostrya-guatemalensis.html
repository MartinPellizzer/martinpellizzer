Ostrya guatemalensis

Ostrya guatemalensis, commonly known as the Guatemalan hemlock, is a deciduous tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its slender, conical shape and smooth, gray bark. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in catkins, which are wind-pollinated. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, turning yellow in autumn.

Ostrya guatemalensis is often found in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions.
