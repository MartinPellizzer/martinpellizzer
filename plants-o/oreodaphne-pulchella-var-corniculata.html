Oreodaphne pulchella var. corniculata

Oreodaphne pulchella var. corniculata, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow to orange-red inflorescences that resemble flames, which bloom during the summer months. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its vibrant floral display.
