Oxalis mucronulata

Oxalis mucronulata, commonly known as the narrow-pointed wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a short, stout stem.

Oxalis mucronulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
