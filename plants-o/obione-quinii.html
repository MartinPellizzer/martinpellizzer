Obione quinii

Obione quinii, commonly known as the Mexican fire plant, is a unique succulent native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It features striking red and orange flowers that bloom in late summer, adding vibrant color to its environment. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. Its botanical name reflects its geographic origin and distinctive appearance.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry climates.
