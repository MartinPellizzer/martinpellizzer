Ossaea sparrei

Ossaea sparrei, commonly known as the desert sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a small, herbaceous annual with narrow, linear leaves and produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils. It has a compact growth habit and is well adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Ossaea sparrei plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators in its native habitat.
