Oenocarpus pulchellus

Oenocarpus pulchellus, commonly known as the yellow sago palm, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces yellow, spiky fruits that contain a starchy, edible pith. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of sago flour.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
