Odontoglossum × imschootianum

Odontoglossum × imschootianum, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Odontoglossum coccineum and Odontoglossum lindenii.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, where it thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, humid conditions. The plant produces strikingly colorful flowers, often with red, pink, or purple hues, and features intricate patterns on its petals and sepals. It is a rhizomatous orchid with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, typical of many epiphytic orchid species.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated by enthusiasts for its beauty and resilience in controlled growing conditions.
