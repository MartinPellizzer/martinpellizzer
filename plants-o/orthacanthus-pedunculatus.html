Orthacanthus pedunculatus

Orthacanthus pedunculatus, commonly known as the slender spurge, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, characterized by its production of milky sap. The plant features erect, branching stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in cyathia, typical of the spurge family.

The plant is often found in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and wetlands.
