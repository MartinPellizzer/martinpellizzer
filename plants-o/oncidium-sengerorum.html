Oncidium sengerorum

Oncidium sengerorum, commonly known as the Senger's orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically has strap-like leaves and can thrive in bright, indirect light with moderate watering. It is a popular choice for indoor cultivation due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.

Oncidium sengerorum is also known for its ability to bloom multiple times a year under proper conditions.
