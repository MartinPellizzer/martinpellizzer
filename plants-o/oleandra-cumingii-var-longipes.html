Oleandra cumingii var. longipes

Oleandra cumingii var. longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed oleander, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and as a landscape plant in tropical and subtropical regions.
