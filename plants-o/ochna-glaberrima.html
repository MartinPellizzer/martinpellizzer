Ochna glaberrima

Ochna glaberrima, commonly known as the African yellowwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by orange to red berries. Ochna glaberrima is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and unique fruiting structures.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas across its native range.
