Ocotea nobilis

Ocotea nobilis, commonly known as the fragrant evergreen or white cypress, is a member of the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in countries like Mexico, Guatemala, and Costa Rica. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and small, white flowers that appear in clusters. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Ocotea nobilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
