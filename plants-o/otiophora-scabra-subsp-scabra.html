Otiophora scabra subsp. scabra

Otiophora scabra subsp.

scabra, commonly known as the rough-stemmed rock cress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in dry and sunny environments.

The plant has erect, branched stems that are covered in fine, rough hairs, giving it its common name. Its small, white flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in early spring.

The subspecies is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology, with leaves that are usually lance-shaped and have a rough, textured surface.
