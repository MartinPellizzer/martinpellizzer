Ophrys × pseudobertolonii

Ophrys × pseudobertolonii, commonly known as the mimic orchid, is a hybrid species native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its intricate floral structure. The plant produces showy, bee-mimicking flowers that are highly specialized for pollination by male bees. Its inflorescence typically consists of a single spike bearing several resupinate flowers.

The botanical features of this species highlight its evolutionary adaptations for effective pollination strategies.
