Opuntia grosseana

Opuntia grosseana, commonly known as the giant prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, flat, paddle-shaped stems that are covered in spines and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible fruits, known as tunas, which are typically yellow or red in color. Opuntia grosseana is adapted to arid environments and has a shallow root system that allows it to survive in dry conditions.

Its structure and growth habits make it a distinctive and resilient member of the Cactaceae family.
