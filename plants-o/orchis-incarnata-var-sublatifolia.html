Orchis incarnata var. sublatifolia

Orchis incarnata var. sublatifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pink orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and wet meadows. The plant features slender, upright stems that bear a single, showy pink or purple flower with a distinctive hooded lip. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often arranged in a basal rosette.

The species is part of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure and specialized pollination mechanisms.
