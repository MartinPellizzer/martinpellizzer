Orchis hispanica

Orchis hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. Its flowers are adorned with intricate patterns and are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.

Orchis hispanica is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a role in supporting local biodiversity.
