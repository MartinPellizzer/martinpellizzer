Oxytropis campestris var. rydbergii

Oxytropis campestris var. rydbergii, commonly known as Rydberg's field locoweed, is a flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect stems and trifoliate leaves, which are typically hairy and have a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink to purple in color, blooming in early summer. It is native to the western United States and thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

This species is also known for its allelopathic properties, which can inhibit the growth of nearby plants.
