Oxalis thunbergiana

Oxalis thunbergiana, commonly known as the false shamrock, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves resembling those of the true shamrock. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Oxalis thunbergiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
