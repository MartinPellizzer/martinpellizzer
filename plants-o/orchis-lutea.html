Orchis lutea

Orchis lutea, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stem is erect and bears a single spike of flowers.

Orchis lutea is also known for its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
