Oxalis pickeringii

Oxalis pickeringii, commonly known as the Pickering's wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family, known for its calcium oxalate crystals in the leaves.

Oxalis pickeringii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in garden settings.
