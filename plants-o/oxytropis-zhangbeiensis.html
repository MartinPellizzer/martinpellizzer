Oxytropis zhangbeiensis

Oxytropis zhangbeiensis, commonly known as Zhangbei milkvetch, is a perennial leguminous plant native to northern China.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Oxytropis zhangbeiensis is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in ecological restoration projects.
