Oxalis camisaoensis

Oxalis camisaoensis, commonly known as the Camisao wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. It produces distinctive seed pods that split open to release seeds, aiding in its propagation.

Oxalis camisaoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
