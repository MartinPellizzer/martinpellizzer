Osteospermum hirsutum

Osteospermum hirsutum, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy blue or purple daisy-like flowers. The plant has hairy, gray-green leaves and produces fluffy, white seed heads that resemble small clouds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Osteospermum hirsutum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
