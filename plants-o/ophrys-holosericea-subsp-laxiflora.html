Ophrys holosericea subsp. laxiflora

Ophrys holosericea subsp.

laxiflora, commonly known as the laxiflora bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species found in specific habitats across southern Europe. It is characterized by its distinctive floral morphology, with a labellum that mimics the appearance of a female bee, a key adaptation for its pollination strategy.

The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its flowers bloom in early spring, producing a delicate, greenish-yellow coloration that aids in attracting its specific pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species, requiring careful conservation efforts.
