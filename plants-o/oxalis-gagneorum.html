Oxalis gagneorum

Oxalis gagneorum, commonly known as the Gagne's wood sorrel, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive light green veins and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in the spring, and it is known for its delicate, ornamental appearance.

Oxalis gagneorum is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.
