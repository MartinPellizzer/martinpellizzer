Oncidium portmannii subsp. cohrsiae

Oncidium portmannii subsp.

cohrsiae, commonly known as the Cochrane's orchid, is a tropical orchid native to South America, particularly Brazil. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, often yellow or orange flowers with intricate patterns.

The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in seasonal climates.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its striking floral display.
