Olea concolor

Olea concolor, commonly known as the Mexican olive, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like the olive and jasmine. The tree is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, orange-red fruits.

Olea concolor is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
