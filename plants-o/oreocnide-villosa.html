Oreocnide villosa

Oreocnide villosa, commonly known as the hairy oreocnide, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its flowers are often visited by insects, contributing to its pollination.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
