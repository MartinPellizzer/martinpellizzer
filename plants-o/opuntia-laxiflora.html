Opuntia laxiflora

Opuntia laxiflora, commonly known as the laxiflora prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems that are segmented and covered in spines. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit, known as a tunas, is edible and often used in traditional cuisine.

Opuntia laxiflora is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife.
