Oxalis paraguayensis var. pauciflora

Oxalis paraguayensis var. pauciflora, commonly known as the Paraguay wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in horticulture for their aesthetic appeal.
