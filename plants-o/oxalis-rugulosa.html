Oxalis rugulosa

Oxalis rugulosa, commonly known as the rough wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with a slightly rough texture and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy choice for garden landscapes. Its foliage and flowers are often used in ornamental gardening due to their attractive appearance.

Oxalis rugulosa is also known for its ability to tolerate drought conditions once established.
