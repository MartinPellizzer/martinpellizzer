Opuntia lucida

Opuntia lucida, commonly known as the bright prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems that are covered in sharp spines and clusters of small, colorful flowers. The plant produces edible fruits known as tunas, which are often consumed fresh or used in jams and beverages. Opuntia lucida is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick stems and having a shallow root system.

Its unique structure and ecological role make it an important species in desert ecosystems.
