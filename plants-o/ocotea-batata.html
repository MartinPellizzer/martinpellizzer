Ocotea batata

Ocotea batata, commonly known as sweet myrtle, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and laurel. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, dark purple drupe that contains a single seed.

Ocotea batata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.
