Obione confertifolia

Obione confertifolia, commonly known as the dense-leaved obione, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are densely arranged along the stem. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Obione confertifolia thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
