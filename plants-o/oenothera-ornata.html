Oenothera ornata

Oenothera ornata, commonly known as the showy evening primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically 2 to 4 inches in diameter, with five petals that reflex outward.

Oenothera ornata is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
