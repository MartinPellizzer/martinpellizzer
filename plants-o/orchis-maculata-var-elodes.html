Orchis maculata var. elodes

Orchis maculata var. elodes, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in grasslands, woodlands, and along stream banks. The plant features a tall, erect stem with several whorled leaves and a cluster of showy, violet-blue flowers with distinctive dark markings. Its flowers are bilaterally symmetrical, with three sepals and three petals, one of which is often larger and spurred.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
