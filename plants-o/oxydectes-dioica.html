Oxydectes dioica

Oxydectes dioica, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling bells. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland environments and prefers partial to full shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth texture, while its stems are erect and often hairy.

Oxydectes dioica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
