Opuntia floccosa var. canispina

Opuntia floccosa var. canispina, commonly known as the woolly prickly pear, is a cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its flattened, fleshy stems covered in dense, white, woolly spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tuna, is edible and often used in traditional cuisine.

This species is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water to thrive.
