Orchis latifolia var. humilis

Orchis latifolia var. humilis, commonly known as the broad-leaved orchid or the humble orchid, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodlands. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and it produces numerous small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is distinguished by its shorter stature compared to other forms of Orchis latifolia.
