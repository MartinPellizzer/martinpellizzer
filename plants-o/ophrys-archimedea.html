Ophrys archimedea

Ophrys archimedea, commonly known as the Archimedes Orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee, a trait known as pseudocopulation. The plant produces showy, bee-like flowers with intricate patterns and a strong, sweet scent that attracts male bees for pollination. Ophrys archimedea typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, open habitats.

Its botanical features make it a fascinating subject for both ecological study and horticultural appreciation.
