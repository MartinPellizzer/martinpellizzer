Ocotea oblongifolia

Ocotea oblongifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed ocotea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by dark blue or black berries.

Ocotea oblongifolia is often found in humid, lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.
