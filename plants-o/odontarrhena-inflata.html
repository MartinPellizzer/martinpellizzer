Odontarrhena inflata

Odontarrhena inflata, commonly known as the inflated orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, inflated seed pods that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some horticultural gardens.
