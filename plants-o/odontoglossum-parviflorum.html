Odontoglossum parviflorum

Odontoglossum parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in cool, moist environments at high elevations. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with rhizomes that produce a rosette of fleshy, dark green leaves. Its flowers are small, usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color.

Odontoglossum parviflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated by enthusiasts for its delicate appearance.
