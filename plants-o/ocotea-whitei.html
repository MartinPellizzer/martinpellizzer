Ocotea whitei

Ocotea whitei, commonly known as the white ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant produces dark, aromatic berries that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Ocotea whitei thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its wood is valued for its durability and fragrance, making it an important species in local ecosystems and economies.
