Ocotea pseudocorymbosa

Ocotea pseudocorymbosa, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant produces dark blue to black berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its dense canopy and fast growth make it a popular ornamental species, though it can become invasive in certain regions.

The tree's essential oils are also valued for their aromatic and potential medicinal properties.
