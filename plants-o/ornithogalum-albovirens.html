Ornithogalum albovirens

Ornithogalum albovirens, commonly known as the white-veined star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces striking, star-shaped white flowers with green veins, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

However, it is toxic to humans and animals, necessitating caution in cultivation.
