Oenothera coccinea

Oenothera coccinea, commonly known as the Indian blanket flower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the United States.

It is characterized by its showy, bright red to orange flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Oenothera coccinea is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
