Oncidium psittacinum

Oncidium psittacinum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It features pendulous racemes of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers with distinctive red or orange markings. The plant has strap-like, dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil with high humidity.

Oncidium psittacinum is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its vibrant blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
