Oxytropis inschanica

Oxytropis inschanica, commonly known as the Central Asian locoweed, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and trifoliate leaves, typical of the Fabaceae family. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Oxytropis inschanica is known for its toxic alkaloids, which can be harmful to livestock.
