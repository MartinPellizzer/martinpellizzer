Oryzopsis henryi

Oryzopsis henryi, commonly known as Henry's grass, is a perennial grass species native to China.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle that bears small, spikelet-like flowers.

Oryzopsis henryi is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other parts of the world for landscaping purposes.
