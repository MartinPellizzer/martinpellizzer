Oxalis orthopoda

Oxalis orthopoda, commonly known as the upright wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Oxalis orthopoda belongs to the Oxalidaceae family, known for its calcium oxalate crystals in the leaves.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can thrive in USDA hardiness zones 6 to 9.
