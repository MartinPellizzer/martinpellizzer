Opuntia thurberi var. alamosensis

Opuntia thurberi var. alamosensis, commonly known as the Alamos prickly pear, is a cactus species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in spines and glochids, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months, followed by edible, fleshy fruits known as tunas. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

This variety is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of food in local communities.
