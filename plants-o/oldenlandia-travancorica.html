Oldenlandia travancorica

Oldenlandia travancorica, commonly known as the Travancore spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands. The plant features small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Oldenlandia travancorica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
