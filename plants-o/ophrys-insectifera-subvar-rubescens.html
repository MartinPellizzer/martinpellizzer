Ophrys insectifera subvar. rubescens

Ophrys insectifera subvar. rubescens, commonly known as the red-brown bee orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to southern Europe.

This subvariety is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant produces showy, bee-like flowers with a distinctive red-brown coloration, particularly on the lip. It typically grows in calcareous soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats.

The botanical features of this orchid make it a fascinating subject for both ecological and horticultural study.
