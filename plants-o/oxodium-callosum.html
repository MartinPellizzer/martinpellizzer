Oxodium callosum

Oxodium callosum, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are often clustered in panicles. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and agricultural fields.

Oxodium callosum is also known for its ability to spread rapidly, making it a common invasive species in many regions.
