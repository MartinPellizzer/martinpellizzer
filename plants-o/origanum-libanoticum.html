Origanum libanoticum

Origanum libanoticum, commonly known as Lebanese oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves with a strong, pungent scent due to its high concentration of essential oils. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its culinary and medicinal uses are valued in traditional Middle Eastern cultures.
