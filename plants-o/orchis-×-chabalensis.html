Orchis × chabalensis

Orchis × chabalensis, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a natural hybrid between Orchis morio and Orchis mascula.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodland areas across Europe. It produces a single spike of flowers, each with a three-lobed lip that is often marked with darker veins.

Orchis × chabalensis is an important species for studying hybridization in orchids and ecological adaptation.
