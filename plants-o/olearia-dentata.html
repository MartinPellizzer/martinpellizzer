Olearia dentata

Olearia dentata, commonly known as the serrated daisy or coastal daisy, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and silvery-green foliage. The plant features showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Olearia dentata thrives in coastal and subalpine environments, often growing in well-drained soils.

Its leaves are typically serrated, giving it its common name and contributing to its distinctive appearance.
