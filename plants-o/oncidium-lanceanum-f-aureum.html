Oncidium lanceanum f. aureum

Oncidium lanceanum f. aureum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow and green foliage, which often features striking golden markings. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences with bright yellow flowers that have a distinctive, spurred lip. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and indoor settings.
