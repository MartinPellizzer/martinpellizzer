Oenothera hartwegii subsp. filifolia

Oenothera hartwegii subsp.

filifolia, commonly known as the threadleaf evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the evening.

The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are typically visited by nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
