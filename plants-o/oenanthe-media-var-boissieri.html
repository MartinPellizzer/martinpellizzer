Oenanthe media var. boissieri

Oenanthe media var. boissieri, commonly known as the Boissieri's hemlock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
