Ophrys bilunulata subsp. kalirachiensis

Ophrys bilunulata subsp.

kalirachiensis, commonly known as the Kalirachi orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Africa. It is characterized by its unique floral morphology, featuring a complex, bee-mimicking flower that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy.

The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories or rocky outcrops. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, adapted to conserve moisture in its native habitat.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, this subspecies is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
