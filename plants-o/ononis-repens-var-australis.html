Ononis repens var. australis

Ononis repens var. australis, commonly known as the Australian sandhill crowfoot, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Australia.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with slender, creeping stems that root at the nodes. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from spring to summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in sandy or gravelly soils.

It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy and resilient species.
