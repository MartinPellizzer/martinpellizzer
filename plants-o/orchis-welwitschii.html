Orchis welwitschii

Orchis welwitschii, commonly known as the Welwitsch orchid, is a species of orchid native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its reproductive success.

Orchis welwitschii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
