Oxytropis mixta

Oxytropis mixta, commonly known as the mixed milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems and small, palmately compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

Oxytropis mixta plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
