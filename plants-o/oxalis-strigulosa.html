Oxalis strigulosa

Oxalis strigulosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf wood sorrel, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features trifoliate leaves with a velvety texture and bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments within forests or gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and flowers.
