Oxalis eckloniana var. robusta

Oxalis eckloniana var. robusta, commonly known as the forest oxalis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive reddish veins and produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas. It has a fibrous root system and can spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent ground cover.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for shady garden settings.
