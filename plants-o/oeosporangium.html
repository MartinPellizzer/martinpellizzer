Oeosporangium

Oeosporangium, commonly known as the sporangium of certain ferns and mosses, is a specialized structure responsible for spore production.

It is typically located at the tip of a sporophyll, which is a leaf-like structure in these plants. The sporangium contains a large number of spores, which are released into the air to facilitate reproduction. The development of the sporangium is closely associated with the plant's reproductive cycle.

Its structure varies among different species, reflecting adaptations to different environments and spore dispersal strategies.
