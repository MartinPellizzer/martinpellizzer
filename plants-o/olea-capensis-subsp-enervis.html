Olea capensis subsp. enervis

Olea capensis subsp.

enervis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved olive, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Olea capensis subsp.

enervis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
