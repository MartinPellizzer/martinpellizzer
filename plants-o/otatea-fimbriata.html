Otatea fimbriata

Otatea fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed fern, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its delicate, frond-like leaves that are often adorned with fine, hair-like structures along the edges. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spadices, a common feature in the Araceae family.

Otatea fimbriata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in shaded garden environments.
