Orchiodes elongatum

Orchiodes elongatum, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant prefers humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests or along riverbanks. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil.

Orchiodes elongatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
