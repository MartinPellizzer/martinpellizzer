Orchis angustifolia var. nylandri

Orchis angustifolia var. nylandri, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodlands and meadows. The plant features slender, upright stems that bear a single spike of pale purple to white flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a basal rosette.

The species is notable for its specialized pollination mechanisms, often relying on specific insect species for reproduction.
