Ononis sicula subsp. polyphylla

Ononis sicula subsp.

polyphylla, commonly known as the Sicilian bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats. Its leaves are narrow and often hairy, and it produces small, cylindrical fruits.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
