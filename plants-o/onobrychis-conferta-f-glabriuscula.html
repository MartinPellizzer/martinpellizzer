Onobrychis conferta f. glabriuscula

Onobrychis conferta f. glabriuscula, commonly known as the glabrous broom, is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces pod-like fruits containing small, round seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
