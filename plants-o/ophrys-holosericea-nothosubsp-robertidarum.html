Ophrys holosericea nothosubsp.

robertidarum, commonly known as the Robert's bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to specific regions of southern Europe. It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of female bees to attract male bees for pollination.

The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its flowers are predominantly yellow with intricate patterns that aid in its deceptive pollination strategy.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
