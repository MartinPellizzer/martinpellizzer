Ophrys khuzestanica

Ophrys khuzestanica, commonly known as the Khuzestan Orchid, is a rare and endemic orchid species native to southwestern Iran.

It belongs to the genus Ophrys, which is known for its intricate mimicry of female insects to attract male pollinators. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid habitats, showcasing a compact growth form with narrow leaves. Its flowers are characterized by their complex structure and vivid coloration, often resembling insect forms.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Ophrys khuzestanica is considered a vulnerable species.
