Ohigginsia refulgens f. roezlii

Ohigginsia refulgens f. roezlii, commonly known as the golden star cactus, is a hybrid form of the species Ohigginsia refulgens.

It is native to the Andes Mountains in South America, specifically in regions of Peru and Bolivia. This plant is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers that resemble stars. The leaves are modified into spines, and the plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

It is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
