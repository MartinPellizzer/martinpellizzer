Orychophragmus violaceus var. odontopetalus

Orychophragmus violaceus var. odontopetalus, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in mid to late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a distinctive, cone-shaped seed head. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
