Omphalodes luciliae subsp. cilicica

Omphalodes luciliae subsp.

cilicica, commonly known as the Cilician bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of blue to purple flowers.

The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate blossoms and compact growth habit.
