Ophrys chestermanii

Ophrys chestermanii, commonly known as the Chesterman's bee orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate to cool climates. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brown in color, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species.
