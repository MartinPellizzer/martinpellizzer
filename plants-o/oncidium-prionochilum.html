Oncidium prionochilum

Oncidium prionochilum, commonly known as the Orchid of the Andes, is a tropical orchid native to South America.

It features a sympodial growth habit with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, often arranged in a raceme. Its leaves are strap-shaped and grass-like, typically arranged in two rows along the stem.

Oncidium prionochilum thrives in humid, well-drained environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
