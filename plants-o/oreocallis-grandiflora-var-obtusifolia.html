Oreocallis grandiflora var. obtusifolia

Oreocallis grandiflora var. obtusifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved mountain lily, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soil.

Due to its striking appearance, it is cultivated in gardens as an ornamental plant.
