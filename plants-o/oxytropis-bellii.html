Oxytropis bellii

Oxytropis bellii, commonly known as the Bell's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow to reddish-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. It has a deep taproot system that allows it to survive in dry conditions.

Oxytropis bellii is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to nutrient cycling in its ecosystem.
