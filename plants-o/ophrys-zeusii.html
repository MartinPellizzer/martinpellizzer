Ophrys zeusii

Ophrys zeusii, commonly known as the Zeus orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of female insects to attract male pollinators through a process called sexual deception. The plant produces a single, showy flower with intricate patterns and a distinctive lip that resembles an insect's body. It thrives in nutrient-poor soils and requires specific environmental conditions for successful growth and reproduction.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological specificity, Ophrys zeusii is considered a vulnerable species in its native habitat.
