Onoseris brasiliensis

Onoseris brasiliensis, commonly known as Brazilian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and features tall, erect stems covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters.

Onoseris brasiliensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
