Orchis × olida

Orchis × olida, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a natural hybrid between Orchis moria and Orchis mascula.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant with a tall, upright stem that bears a spike of showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers have a distinctive three-lobed structure with a spurred lip, typical of the Orchidaceae family.

Orchis × olida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
