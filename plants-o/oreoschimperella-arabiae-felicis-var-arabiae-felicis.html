Oreoschimperella arabiae-felicis var. arabiae-felicis

Oreoschimperella arabiae-felicis var. arabiae-felicis, commonly known as the Arabian sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid regions of the Middle East.

It features a dense mat of fleshy, succulent leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically yellow or green, adapted for pollination by wind or insects. Its root system is extensive, enabling it to anchor itself in sandy soils and access deep water sources.

This species is well-suited for xeriscaping and is often used in landscaping in desert regions.
