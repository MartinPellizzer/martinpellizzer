Olea ligustrina

Olea ligustrina, commonly known as the small-leaf olive, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant, small white flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color. Olea ligustrina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
