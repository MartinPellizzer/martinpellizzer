Oxalis pusilla

Oxalis pusilla, commonly known as dwarf wood sorrel, is a small, herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with reddish veins and produces delicate, five-petaled yellow flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of only a few inches, making it well-suited for rock gardens or as a ground cover. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Oxalis pusilla is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and blooms.
