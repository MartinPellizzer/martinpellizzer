Ochna hiernii

Ochna hiernii, commonly known as the African firethorn, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that can grow up to 2 meters in height, characterized by its dense, spiny branches. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in winter, followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy coating.

Ochna hiernii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
