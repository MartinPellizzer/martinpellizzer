Ophidion pleurothallopsis

Ophidion pleurothallopsis, commonly known as the pleurothallopsis orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in forest understories, where it thrives in shaded, moist environments. The plant has slender, erect stems that bear several lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and greenish-yellow, often clustered in racemes, and are adapted for pollination by insects.

Ophidion pleurothallopsis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
