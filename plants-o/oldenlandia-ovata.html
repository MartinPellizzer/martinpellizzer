Oldenlandia ovata

Oldenlandia ovata, commonly known as the "Whiteweed," is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in umbels. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Oldenlandia ovata is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes, making it both a useful plant in certain ecological contexts and a potential invasive species.
