Oetosis filiformis

Oetosis filiformis, commonly known as the slender oetos, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, thread-like leaves and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in dense forests or along stream banks. Its root system is highly specialized, allowing it to absorb nutrients from poor soil conditions.

Due to habitat loss, Oetosis filiformis is considered vulnerable and is the subject of conservation efforts.
