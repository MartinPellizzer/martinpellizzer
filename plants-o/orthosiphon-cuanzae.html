Orthosiphon cuanzae

Orthosiphon cuanzae, commonly known as African rosemary, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers that are clustered in whorls along the stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, minty aroma.

Orthosiphon cuanzae is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
