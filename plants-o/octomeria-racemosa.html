Octomeria racemosa

Octomeria racemosa, commonly known as the clustered octomeris, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
