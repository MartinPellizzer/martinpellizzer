Onobroma macrocephalum

Onobroma macrocephalum, commonly known as the giant crownflower, is a striking plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has a woody stem and produces distinctive, spiny fruit capsules that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in desert or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a popular subject for botanical study.
