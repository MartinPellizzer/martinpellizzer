Oxalis alata

Oxalis alata, commonly known as the wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in partial shade and well-drained soil. Oxalis alata produces small, edible berries that are sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its rapid growth and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
