Osmanthus fragrans f. aurantiacus

Osmanthus fragrans f. aurantiacus, commonly known as the golden osmanthus, is a fragrant flowering plant native to southern China.

It is a compact shrub characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that emit a sweet, citrus-like aroma. The flowers are often used in traditional Chinese medicine and as a flavoring in teas and confections. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and aromatic properties.

It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial shade to full sun.
