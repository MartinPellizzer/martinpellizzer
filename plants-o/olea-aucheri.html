Olea aucheri

Olea aucheri, commonly known as the Persian lilac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountains of western Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pale lavender to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth texture. Olea aucheri thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both horticultural and ecological landscapes.
