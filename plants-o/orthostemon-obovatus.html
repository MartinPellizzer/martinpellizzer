Orthostemon obovatus

Orthostemon obovatus, commonly known as the broad-leaf orthostemon, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, obovate leaves that are typically hairy and have a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, often blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting various pollinators.
