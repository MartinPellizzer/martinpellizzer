Ocotea teleiandra

Ocotea teleiandra, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and fragrance. The plant has simple, alternate, elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface and a lighter underside. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by dark blue to black berries.

The species is also noted for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional medicine.
