Opuntia bruchii f. brachyacanthus

Opuntia bruchii f. brachyacanthus, commonly known as the short-spined prickly pear, is a unique variant of the opuntia cactus characterized by its reduced spines and broader, more rounded pads.

It is native to Mexico and thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting well to harsh climatic conditions. The plant's pads are thick and fleshy, serving as water storage structures, while its flowers are typically yellow or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. This form is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and resilience.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
