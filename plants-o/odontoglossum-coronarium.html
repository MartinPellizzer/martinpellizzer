Odontoglossum coronarium

Odontoglossum coronarium, commonly known as the Crowned Orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial herb with a pseudobulb that supports its growth during periods of dormancy. The plant produces showy, violet or purple flowers with a distinctive crown-like structure at the base of the petals. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in cool, humid environments.

Odontoglossum coronarium is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a decorative plant.
