Ononis crotalarioides

Ononis crotalarioides, commonly known as the horned vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and is characterized by its distinctive, horn-like seed pods. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by the development of its unique, spiraled seed pods.

Ononis crotalarioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in soil stabilization in arid environments.
