Oxytropis yunnanensis

Oxytropis yunnanensis, commonly known as Yunnan locoweed, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province in China.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit, with hairy stems and small, compound leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces yellow to reddish-purple flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Oxytropis yunnanensis is also known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock.
