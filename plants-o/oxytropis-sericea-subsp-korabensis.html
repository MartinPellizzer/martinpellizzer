Oxytropis sericea subsp. korabensis

Oxytropis sericea subsp.

korabensis, commonly known as the Korab's silky locoweed, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its finely hairy stems and leaves.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, often found in mountainous regions. Oxytropis sericea subsp.

korabensis is adapted to poor soils and can tolerate harsh climatic conditions.
