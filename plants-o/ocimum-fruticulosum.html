Ocimum fruticulosum

Ocimum fruticulosum, commonly known as the fragrant basil or lemon basil, is a tropical herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, pointed leaves that are often tinged with purple at the edges. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers and has a strong, aromatic scent reminiscent of lemon or lime. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its culinary and medicinal uses.

Ocimum fruticulosum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
