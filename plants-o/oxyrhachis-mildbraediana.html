Oxyrhachis mildbraediana

Oxyrhachis mildbraediana, commonly known as the African dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Oxyrhachis mildbraediana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its drought tolerance and aesthetic qualities.
