Ornithogalum sumbulianum

Ornithogalum sumbulianum, commonly known as the African star of Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a short, underground rhizome and produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers during the summer months. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Ornithogalum sumbulianum is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
