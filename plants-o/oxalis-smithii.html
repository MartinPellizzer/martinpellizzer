Oxalis smithii

Oxalis smithii, commonly known as Smith's wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Oxalis smithii is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

It is also valued for its ability to tolerate drought conditions once established.
