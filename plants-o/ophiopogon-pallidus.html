Ophiopogon pallidus

Ophiopogon pallidus, commonly known as the pale spathiphyllum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Ophiopogon pallidus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.
