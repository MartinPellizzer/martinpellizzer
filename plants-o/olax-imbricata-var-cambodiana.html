Olax imbricata var. cambodiana

Olax imbricata var. cambodiana, commonly known as the Cambodian olax, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Olacaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by spherical, woody fruits. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
