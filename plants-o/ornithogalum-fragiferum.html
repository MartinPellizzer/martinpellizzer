Ornithogalum fragiferum

Ornithogalum fragiferum, commonly known as the fragrant star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers with a fragrant scent. The plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ornithogalum fragiferum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
