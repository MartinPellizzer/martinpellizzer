Oreodoxa princeps

Oreodoxa princeps, commonly known as the royal orchid, is a striking species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that display a vibrant mix of purple and white hues. The plant belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is noted for its intricate floral structure and specialized pollination mechanisms. Its leaves are broad and thick, adapted to conserve moisture in its arid habitat.

Oreodoxa princeps is highly sought after by botanists and horticulturists due to its rarity and ornamental value.
