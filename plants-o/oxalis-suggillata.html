Oxalis suggillata

Oxalis suggillata, commonly known as the yellow wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Oxalis suggillata thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
