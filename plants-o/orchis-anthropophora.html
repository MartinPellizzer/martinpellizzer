Orchis anthropophora

Orchis anthropophora, commonly known as the human orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet-blue flowers that resemble human figures, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist woodlands and is found in parts of Europe and Asia. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils, while its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

Orchis anthropophora is known for its unique floral structure and is of interest to botanists and conservationists due to its limited distribution and ecological significance.
