Origanum syriacum subsp. dubium

Origanum syriacum subsp.

dubium, commonly known as Syrian oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, pale blue or purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in mountainous areas. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs.

Its essential oils contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, contributing to its medicinal and culinary uses.
