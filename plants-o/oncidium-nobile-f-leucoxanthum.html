Oncidium nobile f. leucoxanthum

Oncidium nobile f. leucoxanthum, commonly known as the white-flowered orchid, is a hybrid orchid species characterized by its striking white petals and sepals.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is native to South America, particularly Brazil. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air. Its flowers are fragrant and bloom in clusters, often displaying a subtle yellow or greenish tinge at the base.

This orchid is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its elegant, long-lasting blooms.
