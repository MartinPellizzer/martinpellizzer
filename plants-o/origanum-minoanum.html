Origanum minoanum

Origanum minoanum, commonly known as Minoan oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It is closely related to other members of the Lamiaceae family, sharing similar aromatic properties.

Origanum minoanum is valued both for its culinary uses and its role in traditional herbal medicine.
