Oxalis subvillosa

Oxalis subvillosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It features trifoliate leaves with a velvety texture and small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.

Oxalis subvillosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
