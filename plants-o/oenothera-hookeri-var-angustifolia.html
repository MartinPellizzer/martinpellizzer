Oenothera hookeri var. angustifolia

Oenothera hookeri var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, angular stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
