Omphalocarpum pierreanum

Omphalocarpum pierreanum, commonly known as the African burrbean, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by spiny, spherical fruits containing seeds. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

The species is notable for its unique seed dispersal mechanism, where the fruits detach and roll away, aiding in seed spread.
