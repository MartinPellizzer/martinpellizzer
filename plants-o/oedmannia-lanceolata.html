Oedmannia lanceolata

Oedmannia lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed oxalis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial species that is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers bloom in spring and are followed by capsule fruits containing several seeds.
