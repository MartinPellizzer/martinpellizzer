Oxytropis jordalii subsp. davisii

Oxytropis jordalii subsp.

davisii, commonly known as the Davis' locoweed, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers.

The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to harsh, dry environments and plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils.

Its growth habit and morphology reflect its evolutionary adaptations to drought and nutrient-poor soils.
