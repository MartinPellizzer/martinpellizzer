Ophrys fusca subsp. calocaerina

Ophrys fusca subsp.

calocaerina, commonly known as the dark bee orchid, is a species of orchid native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its dark brown to blackish sepals and petals, which resemble the appearance of a bee, aiding in its unique pollination strategy.

The plant typically grows in dry, calcareous soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and heaths. Its flowers are highly specialized, with a labellum that mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination.

This orchid is a striking example of evolutionary adaptation in the Orchidaceae family.
