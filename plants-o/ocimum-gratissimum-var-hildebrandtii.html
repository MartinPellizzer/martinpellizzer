Ocimum gratissimum var. hildebrandtii

Ocimum gratissimum var. hildebrandtii, commonly known as Hildebrandt's basil, is a variant of the tropical shrub native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with opposite, ovate leaves that have a distinct minty fragrance. Its flowers are small, clustered in whorls, and range in color from white to pale purple.

This variety is valued for its essential oils, which contain compounds like cineole and camphor, contributing to its medicinal properties.
