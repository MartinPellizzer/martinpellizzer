Orchiodes discolor

Orchiodes discolor, commonly known as the pale orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, pale-colored flowers that range from white to pale pink, often with a faintly speckled appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as under forest canopies, where it thrives in humid conditions. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil.

Orchiodes discolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
