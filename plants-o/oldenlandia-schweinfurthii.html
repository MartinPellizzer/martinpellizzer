Oldenlandia schweinfurthii

Oldenlandia schweinfurthii, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking white, night-blooming flowers that emit a sweet fragrance, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release its seeds, often dispersed by wind.
