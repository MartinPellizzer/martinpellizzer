Oenothera biennis var. rubricaulis

Oenothera biennis var. rubricaulis, commonly known as the scarlet evening primrose, is a biennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in prairies and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically significant.
