Osmunda virginica

Osmunda virginica, commonly known as the eastern royal fern, is a deciduous fern native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender fronds that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant has a distinct crown of fiddleheads, which are tightly coiled young fronds that unfurl in the spring. Its rhizomes are thick and fleshy, allowing it to thrive in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Osmunda virginica plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
