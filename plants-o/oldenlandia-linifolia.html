Oldenlandia linifolia

Oldenlandia linifolia, commonly known as threadleaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It possesses slender, linear leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in length, giving it its common name. The plant relies on a network of fine, thread-like roots to attach to host trees, from which it derives some of its nutrients. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and the plant plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat.
