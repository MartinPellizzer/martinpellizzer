Oxera pulchella subsp. grandiflora

Oxera pulchella subsp.

grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered oxera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand. It features a low-growing, mat-forming habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming from late winter to early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest margins and rocky areas. Oxera pulchella subsp.

grandiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native ecosystems.
