Olea grandiflora

Olea grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered olive, is a species of flowering plant in the Oleaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy white or yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its fruits are small drupes that contain a single stone, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance.

Olea grandiflora thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
