Orchis × semichampagneuxii

Orchis × semichampagneuxii, commonly known as the Semichampagne orchid, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Orchis morio and Orchis mascula.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing intricate, spurred orchid flowers with a distinctive lip. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated for its unique floral display.
