Opuntia andicola var. fulvispina

Opuntia andicola var. fulvispina, commonly known as the Andean spineless prickly pear, is a cactus species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems that are typically spineless, making it distinct from other Opuntia species. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers, which are followed by edible, spherical fruits known as tunas. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting well to high-altitude conditions.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in traditional agriculture and cuisine.
