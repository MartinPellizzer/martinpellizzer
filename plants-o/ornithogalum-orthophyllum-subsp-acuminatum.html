Ornithogalum orthophyllum subsp. acuminatum

Ornithogalum orthophyllum subsp.

acuminatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It features a bulbous stem base with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating.

The plant produces umbels of white or pale pink star-shaped flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
