Ornitharium striatulum

Ornitharium striatulum, commonly known as the striped orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pale pink, are adorned with intricate, parallel stripes.

Ornitharium striatulum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
