Oxygonum sinuatum

Oxygonum sinuatum, commonly known as the Sinuous Cucumber, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive, lobed leaves that are often deeply sinuous in appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically clustered in racemes, followed by edible, elongated fruits that resemble cucumbers in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

Oxygonum sinuatum is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
