Ononis pusilla var. parviflora

Ononis pusilla var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, creeping stems that often form dense mats on the ground. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers clustered in dense racemes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, with a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in dry, sunny environments such as rocky soils and open woodlands.
