Olsynium junceum subsp. colchaguense

Olsynium junceum subsp.

colchaguense, commonly known as the Chilean rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern South America. It typically grows in wet, marshy areas and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense spikes, which are often clustered at the tips of the stems. It is well adapted to moist environments and can thrive in a variety of wetland habitats. Olsynium junceum subsp.

colchaguense plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
