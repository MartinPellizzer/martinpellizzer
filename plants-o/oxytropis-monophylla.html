Oxytropis monophylla

Oxytropis monophylla, commonly known as the single-leaf locoweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its unique, single leaf that emerges from the base of the plant. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid and semi-arid environments.

Oxytropis monophylla is also known for its toxic properties, making it a potential hazard to livestock.
