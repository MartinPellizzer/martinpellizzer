Orthosiphon elliottii

Orthosiphon elliottii, commonly known as Elliot's horse-gram, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and spikes of small, pale blue to purple flowers. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Orthosiphon elliottii is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
