Orchis punctulata subsp. stevenii

Orchis punctulata subsp.

stevenii, commonly known as the Steven's spotted orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous grasslands and meadows, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers with a distinctive spotted pattern on the petals and sepals. Its inflorescence consists of a single spike bearing several flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is notable for its ecological importance as a food source for certain pollinators and its role in maintaining biodiversity in its native habitats.
