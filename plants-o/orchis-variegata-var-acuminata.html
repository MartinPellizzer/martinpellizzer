Orchis variegata var. acuminata

Orchis variegata var. acuminata, commonly known as the acuminated green orchid, is a species of orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has a slender, upright stem with few leaves. Its flowers are arranged in a loose spike and are often spotted or streaked with darker hues.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with the right conditions.
