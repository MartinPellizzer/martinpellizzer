Oxybaphus californicus

Oxybaphus californicus, commonly known as the California bighornweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Oxybaphus californicus is adapted to arid conditions and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
