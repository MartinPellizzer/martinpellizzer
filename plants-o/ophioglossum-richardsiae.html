Ophioglossum richardsiae

Ophioglossum richardsiae, commonly known as the giant shield fern, is a rare and ancient fern species belonging to the family Ophioglossaceae.

It is characterized by its large, shield-shaped fronds that can grow up to 3 meters in length, making it one of the largest ferns in the world. The plant has a unique reproductive system, with sporangia clustered in specialized structures called sori, which are located on the underside of the fronds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in many areas.
