Ocotea caudata

Ocotea caudata, commonly known as the long-tailed ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage and woody structure. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests, reaching heights of up to 20 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

The flowers are small and clustered, followed by dark blue berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species.
