Odontoglossum rhynchanthum

Odontoglossum rhynchanthum, commonly known as the pink fairy orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking pink and white flowers, which bloom during the cooler months. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are typically elliptical and covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs.

Odontoglossum rhynchanthum is often grown in cultivation for its ornamental value and unique floral display.
