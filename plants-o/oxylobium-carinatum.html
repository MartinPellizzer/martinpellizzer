Oxylobium carinatum

Oxylobium carinatum, commonly known as the mountain thimbleweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a perennial herb with a slender, upright stem that bears opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink to reddish in color, appearing in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include a distinctive ribbed stem and showy bracts that surround the flower clusters.

Oxylobium carinatum is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.
