Oberonia trullilabris

Oberonia trullilabris, commonly known as the orchid with the peculiarly shaped labellum, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure, particularly the labellum, which is elongated and resembles a tail. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest undergrowth, thriving in humid tropical climates. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with a distinct, often fragrant scent.

Oberonia trullilabris is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts due to its distinctive appearance.
