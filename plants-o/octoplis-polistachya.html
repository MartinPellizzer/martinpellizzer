Octoplis polistachya

Octoplis polistachya, commonly known as the slender bamboo or Chinese bamboo, is a member of the Poaceae family.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist environments, such as wetlands and marshes, and is native to East Asia. Its flowering stems produce small, clustered inflorescences, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

The plant's rapid growth and adaptability make it both ecologically significant and sometimes invasive in non-native regions.
