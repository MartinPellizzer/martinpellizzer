Olea lindleyi

Olea lindleyi, commonly known as the Lindley olive, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically harvested when they turn a dark purple or black color. Olea lindleyi is adapted to coastal and limestone soils, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value and resilience make it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
