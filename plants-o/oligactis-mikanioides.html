Oligactis mikanioides

Oligactis mikanioides, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, pale blue or white flowers. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to climb or spread along the ground. Its seeds are enclosed in a fleshy, red or orange capsule that splits open when mature.

Oligactis mikanioides is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks.
