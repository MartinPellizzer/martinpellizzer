Odontoglossum trilobum

Odontoglossum trilobum, commonly known as the three-lobed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a compact, pseudobulbous structure that supports its growth in high-altitude environments. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers with three petals and a distinctive labellum, which is often adorned with intricate patterns. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette, helping to conserve moisture in its native habitat.

Odontoglossum trilobum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
