Ocimum hockii

Ocimum hockii, commonly known as Hockii basil, is a species of flowering plant in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its culinary and medicinal uses.

Ocimum hockii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
