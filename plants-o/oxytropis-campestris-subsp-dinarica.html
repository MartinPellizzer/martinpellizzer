Oxytropis campestris subsp. dinarica

Oxytropis campestris subsp.

dinarica, commonly known as the Dinaric field locoweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae. It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in racemes. It is native to the Balkan Peninsula and thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This species is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native environment.
