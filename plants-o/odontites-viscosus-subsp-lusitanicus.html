Odontites viscosus subsp. lusitanicus

Odontites viscosus subsp.

lusitanicus, commonly known as the Lusitanian sticky rockcress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southwestern Europe. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous or alpine regions.

The plant features basal leaves that are succulent and covered in a sticky, resinous substance, which is a distinctive botanical trait. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in loose clusters, and it blooms during the spring and early summer.

This subspecies is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient member of its ecosystem.
