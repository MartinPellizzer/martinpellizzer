Oxytropis thionantha

Oxytropis thionantha, commonly known as the sulfurroot, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant produces small, yellowish to greenish flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are compound, with small leaflets arranged in pairs, and the plant has a deep root system adapted to poor soils.

Oxytropis thionantha is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native environment.
