Oenothera cleistantha

Oenothera cleistantha, commonly known as the white evening primrose, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, white to pale yellow flowers that open in the evening and close by dawn. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, round seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Oenothera cleistantha is also noted for its role in supporting nocturnal pollinators like moths.
