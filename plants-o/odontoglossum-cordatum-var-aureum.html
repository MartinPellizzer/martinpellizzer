Odontoglossum cordatum var. aureum

Odontoglossum cordatum var. aureum, commonly known as the golden heart orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with sympodial growth, characterized by its rhizomatous structure and dense foliage. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of yellow and green, often with intricate patterns. Its leaves are typically ovate to cordate in shape, and the plant thrives in humid, shaded environments.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
