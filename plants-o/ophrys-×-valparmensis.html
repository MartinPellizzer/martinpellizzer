Ophrys × valparmensis

Ophrys × valparmensis, commonly known as the Valparmense bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its unique floral morphology, featuring a labellum that mimics the appearance of a female bee, a trait that plays a key role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brownish in color, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Ophrys × valparmensis is a rare and protected species, reflecting its ecological significance and the need for conservation efforts.
