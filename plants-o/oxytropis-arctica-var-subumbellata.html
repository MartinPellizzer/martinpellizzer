Oxytropis arctica var. subumbellata

Oxytropis arctica var. subumbellata, commonly known as Arctic milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in tundra environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in umbellate clusters, which give it its common name. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

This species is adapted to cold climates and is often found in areas with limited soil nutrients.
