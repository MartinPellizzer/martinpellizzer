Ophrys galilaea subsp. murbeckii

Ophrys galilaea subsp.

murbeckii, commonly known as the Murbeck's bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, open habitats. Its flowers are usually a mix of yellow and brown, with intricate patterns that aid in its deceptive pollination strategy.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, this subspecies is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
