Osmanthus marginatus var. pachyphyllus

Osmanthus marginatus var. pachyphyllus, commonly known as the thick-leaved osmanthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that give it its varietal name. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in the autumn. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

The species is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
