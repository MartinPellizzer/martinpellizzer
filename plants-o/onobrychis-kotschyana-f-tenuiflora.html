Onobrychis kotschyana f. tenuiflora

Onobrychis kotschyana f. tenuiflora, commonly known as the slender-flowered sweet clover, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves with small, rounded leaflets and produces small, yellowish-pink flowers arranged in compact racemes. The plant is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing a deep root system for water absorption. It has a hairy stem and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it valuable for soil enrichment.

This subspecies is often found in alpine meadows and is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its unique ecological adaptations.
