Ossaea amygdaloides

Ossaea amygdaloides, commonly known as the almondleaf sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. Its root system is extensive and adapted to survive in arid, sandy environments.

Ossaea amygdaloides is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
