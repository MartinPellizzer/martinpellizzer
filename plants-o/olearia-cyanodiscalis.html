Olearia cyanodiscalis

Olearia cyanodiscalis, commonly known as the blue disk daisy, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue disc-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils and salty air. Its leaves are narrow and grey-green, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant blue flowers.

Olearia cyanodiscalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
