Ononis natrix subvar. parvifolia

Ononis natrix subvar. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-flowered bedstraw, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in dry, sandy or gravelly soils, often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. The plant has slender, branching stems with small, opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are pale yellow and borne in clusters, producing small, dry fruits known as achenes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
