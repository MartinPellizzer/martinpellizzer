Oxytropis kossinskyi

Oxytropis kossinskyi, commonly known as Kossinsky's locoweed, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Oxytropis kossinskyi is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
