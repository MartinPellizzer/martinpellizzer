Onobrychis iranica

Onobrychis iranica, commonly known as Iranian burr clover, is a perennial legume native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers and trifoliate leaves, typical of the Fabaceae family. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to poor soils and drought conditions. Its seed pods are hairy and contain several small, rounded seeds.

Onobrychis iranica is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
