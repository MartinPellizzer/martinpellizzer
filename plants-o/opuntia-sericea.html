Opuntia sericea

Opuntia sericea, commonly known as the silver pricklypear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems covered in a dense layer of white, silky hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by red, fleshy fruits called tunas. Opuntia sericea is drought-tolerant and thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.

Its structure and adaptations make it well-suited for survival in harsh desert conditions.
