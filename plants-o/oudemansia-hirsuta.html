Oudemansia hirsuta

Oudemansia hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy pincushion cactus, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a short, cylindrical stem covered in dense, woolly hairs that provide protection against intense sunlight and dehydration. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its fuzzy foliage. Its adaptations make it well-suited for arid environments, and it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Oudemansia hirsuta is known for its unique appearance and resilience, making it a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
