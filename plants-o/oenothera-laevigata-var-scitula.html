Oenothera laevigata var. scitula

Oenothera laevigata var. scitula, commonly known as the plains evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that open in the evening and close by morning. The plant has hairy, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with toothed margins. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open prairies and disturbed areas.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by moths and other nocturnal insects.
