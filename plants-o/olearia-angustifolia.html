Olearia angustifolia

Olearia angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved daisybush, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine, silvery hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Olearia angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
