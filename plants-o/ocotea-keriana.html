Ocotea keriana

Ocotea keriana, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Ocotea keriana thrives in humid, warm climates and is well-suited for shaded to partially sunny environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
