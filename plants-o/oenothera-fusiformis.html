Oenothera fusiformis

Oenothera fusiformis, commonly known as the fusiform evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and showy yellow flowers that open in the evening. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oily seeds. Oenothera fusiformis is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
