Oestlundorchis falcata

Oestlundorchis falcata, commonly known as the sickle orchid, is a rare and endangered orchid species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, pale green or yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as pine flatwoods and wetlands. Its reproductive strategy relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake, which is crucial for its survival.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, conservation efforts are essential to preserve this unique botanical species.
