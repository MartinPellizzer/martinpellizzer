Ophrys × orettae

Ophrys × orettae, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera (the bee orchid) and Ophrys insectifera (the wasp orchid).

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, bee-like flower structure that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland or meadow habitats. Its flowers exhibit a high degree of morphological variation, which contributes to its adaptability and ecological significance.

Due to its hybrid origin, Ophrys × orettae is often rare and requires specific conditions to flourish.
