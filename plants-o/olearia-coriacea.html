Olearia coriacea

Olearia coriacea, commonly known as the roughleaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and showy yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained soils. It is often found in open habitats such as scrublands and forest margins.

Olearia coriacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
