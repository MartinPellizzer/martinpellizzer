Orchis latifolia var. majalis

Orchis latifolia var. majalis, commonly known as the broad-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy coating.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
