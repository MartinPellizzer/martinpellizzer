Octadesmia monophylla

Octadesmia monophylla, commonly known as the single-leaf waterwheel, is a species of aquatic plant found in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring a single, spirally arranged leaf that emerges from a short, stout stem. The plant typically grows in slow-moving or still water, such as ponds and marshes, where it can anchor itself to submerged substrates. Its leaves are often ribbon-like and can vary in color from green to reddish depending on light conditions.

Octadesmia monophylla plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small invertebrates and contributing to water quality through oxygen production.
