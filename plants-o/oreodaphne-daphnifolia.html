Oreodaphne daphnifolia

Oreodaphne daphnifolia, commonly known as the white-flowered laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces elliptical, dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
