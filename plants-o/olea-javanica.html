Olea javanica

Olea javanica, commonly known as the Java olive, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by small, round fruits resembling olives. Olea javanica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.

It prefers well-drained soil and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
