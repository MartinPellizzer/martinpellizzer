Oenothera prismatica var. quadrivulnera

Oenothera prismatica var. quadrivulnera, commonly known as the four-veined evening primrose, is a herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and showy, yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening. The leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and often have a distinct four-veined pattern, giving the plant its specific epithet. The flowers produce small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

This species is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and open habitats.
