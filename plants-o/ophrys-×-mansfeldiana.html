Ophrys × mansfeldiana

Ophrys × mansfeldiana, commonly known as the bee orchid hybrid, is a fascinating plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Ophrys apifera (the bee orchid) and Ophrys insectifera (the wasp orchid), resulting in a unique combination of traits from both parents. The plant is characterized by its striking, bee-like flower structure, which plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. Its flowers emit a scent reminiscent of bees, attracting male bees that attempt to mate with the flower, thereby facilitating pollination.

This species is typically found in calcareous grasslands and is often associated with specific microhabitats that support its growth.
