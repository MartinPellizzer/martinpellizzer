Ochna keniensis

Ochna keniensis, commonly known as the Kenyan ochna, is a flowering plant native to East Africa, particularly Kenya.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and elliptical leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas of forests and is often found in mountainous regions. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Ochna keniensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
