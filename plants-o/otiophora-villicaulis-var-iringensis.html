Otiophora villicaulis var. iringensis

Otiophora villicaulis var. iringensis, commonly known as the Iringa spurge, is a succulent plant native to the eastern regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically covered in a powdery white coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters, which are often followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun, making it a hardy species in semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and resilience, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
