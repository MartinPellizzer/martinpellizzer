Oplismenus dubius

Oplismenus dubius, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that are often reddish in color and covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and they are arranged alternately along the stem.

Oplismenus dubius is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands.
