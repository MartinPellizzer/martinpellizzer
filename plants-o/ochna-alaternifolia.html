Ochna alaternifolia

Ochna alaternifolia, commonly known as the African snowball or white thorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, often greyish bark and its clusters of small, white, waxy flowers that appear in winter. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, round, yellow fruits that contain a single seed.

Ochna alaternifolia is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species in tropical and subtropical regions.
