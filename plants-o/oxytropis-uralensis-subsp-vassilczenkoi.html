Oxytropis uralensis subsp. vassilczenkoi

Oxytropis uralensis subsp.

vassilczenkoi, commonly known as the Ural milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and compound leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or steppe environments.

This subspecies is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitats.
