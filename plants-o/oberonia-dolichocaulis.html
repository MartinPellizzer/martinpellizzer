Oberonia dolichocaulis

Oberonia dolichocaulis, commonly known as the long-stemmed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated stem and a cluster of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its unique appearance.

Oberonia dolichocaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
