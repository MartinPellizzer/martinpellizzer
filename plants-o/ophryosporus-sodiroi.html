Ophryosporus sodiroi

Ophryosporus sodiroi, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a herbaceous plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red flower heads, which are surrounded by green bracts. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in the summer months and are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ophryosporus sodiroi is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
