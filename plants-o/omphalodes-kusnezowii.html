Omphalodes kusnezowii

Omphalodes kusnezowii, commonly known as the Siberian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features hairy, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to violet in color, blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Omphalodes kusnezowii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
