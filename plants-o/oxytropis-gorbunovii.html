Oxytropis gorbunovii

Oxytropis gorbunovii, commonly known as the Gorbunov's milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its deep root system.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
