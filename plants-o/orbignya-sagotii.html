Orbignya sagotii

Orbignya sagotii, commonly known as the babassu palm, is a tropical plant native to the Amazon rainforest of South America.

It is a tall, slender palm with a smooth, gray trunk and large, feather-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that develop into hard, woody fruits containing a single seed. The seeds are often harvested for their oil, which is used in cosmetics and cooking.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
