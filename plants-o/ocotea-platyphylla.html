Ocotea platyphylla

Ocotea platyphylla, commonly known as the flat-leaf ocotea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically clustered in umbels, and it produces dark, aromatic berries that are a source of food for various wildlife.

Ocotea platyphylla is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
