Oncidium tayleurii

Oncidium tayleurii, commonly known as the Tayleur's orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its long, drooping inflorescences that bear clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, using its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the environment. Its flowers are short-lived but attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Oncidium tayleurii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
