Ophrys fusca subsp. thracica

Ophrys fusca subsp.

thracica, commonly known as the Thrace bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species found primarily in the Balkan region. It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone-rich soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats. Its flowers are usually dark brown to black in color, with intricate patterns that enhance the mimicry.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
