Onobrychis tournefortii subsp. cappadocica

Onobrychis tournefortii subsp.

cappadocica, commonly known as Cappadocian burr vetch, is a perennial leguminous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes. Its pods are elongated and contain several seeds, contributing to its role in soil stabilization.

It is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
