Opuntia stricta var. reitzii

Opuntia stricta var. reitzii, commonly known as the narrowleaf prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, flat, paddle-shaped stems that are typically narrow and elongated. The plant produces bright yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by edible, red-fleshed fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating high drought tolerance.

Its structure and adaptations make it well-suited for xerophytic conditions.
