Oxalis subcorymbosa var. viscosissima

Oxalis subcorymbosa var. viscosissima, commonly known as the sticky oxalis, is a species native to South America.

It is characterized by its succulent, hairy leaves that exude a sticky substance when damaged. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
