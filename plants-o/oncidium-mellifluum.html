Oncidium mellifluum

Oncidium mellifluum, commonly known as the sweet orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, fragrant flowers that often display a range of colors, including white, pink, and yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Oncidium mellifluum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its long-lasting, showy blooms.
