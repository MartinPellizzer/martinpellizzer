Ononis tridentata subsp. mauretanica

Ononis tridentata subsp.

mauretanica, commonly known as Mauretanian bedstraw, is a perennial herb native to North Africa. It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, white or pale pink flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is well adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three narrow, lance-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

This subspecies is often found in Mediterranean and semi-arid regions, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
