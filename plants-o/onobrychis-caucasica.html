Onobrychis caucasica

Onobrychis caucasica, commonly known as Caucasian clover, is a perennial legume native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil fertility.

Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop for livestock.
