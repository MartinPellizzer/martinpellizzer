Oenanthe banatica

Oenanthe banatica, commonly known as the Himalayan water dropwort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Himalayas.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors. The plant features slender, hairy stems and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in umbels. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinct, slightly hairy texture.

Oenanthe banatica is characterized by its aromatic, sometimes bitter-tasting foliage and is part of the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley.
