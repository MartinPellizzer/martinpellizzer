Ophrys × chettae

Ophrys × chettae, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to southern Europe, particularly in regions like southern France and Italy. The plant is notable for its intricate floral structure, which mimics the appearance of female bees to attract male bees for pollination. Its flowers exhibit a range of colors, typically brown and yellow, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

This orchid thrives in open, sunny habitats and is often found in limestone soils, showcasing its adaptability to specific ecological conditions.
