Oenothera claviformis subsp. cruciformis

Oenothera claviformis subsp.

cruciformis, commonly known as the cruciform evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its yellow, cruciform (cross-shaped) flowers that bloom in the evening.

The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and it thrives in sandy or rocky soils. It is part of the Onagraceae family and is known for its nocturnal pollination by moths.
