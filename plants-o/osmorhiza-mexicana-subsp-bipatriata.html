Osmorhiza mexicana subsp. bipatriata

Osmorhiza mexicana subsp.

bipatriata, commonly known as the Mexican sweetroot, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It belongs to theApiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences.

The plant features a thick, branched root system that is often harvested for its medicinal properties. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a pungent odor, while its small white flowers bloom in late spring.

This subspecies is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and open woodlands.
