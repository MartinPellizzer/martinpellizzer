Ononis procurrens

Ononis procurrens, commonly known as sand rocket, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to June. The plant has slender, branching stems that often lie close to the ground, and its leaves are narrow, linear, and covered in fine hairs. Sand rocket thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, disturbed habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in wildflower gardens.
