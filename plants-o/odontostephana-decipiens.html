Odontostephana decipiens

Odontostephana decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique and often misleading floral structures. The plant produces showy, orchid-like flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its name "decipiens" refers to its ability to mimic other plants, which may aid in pollination.

Odontostephana decipiens thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
