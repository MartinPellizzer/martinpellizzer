Ormocarpum sennoides subsp. hispidum

Ormocarpum sennoides subsp.

hispidum, commonly known as the hairy senna, is a shrub native to parts of Africa and the Middle East. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits.

The plant features palmate leaves with hairy surfaces, which give it its common name. Its flowers are typically yellow and arranged in racemes, attracting pollinators such as bees.

The fruit is a legume, containing several seeds that are dispersed by wind or animals.
