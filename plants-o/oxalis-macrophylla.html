Oxalis macrophylla

Oxalis macrophylla, commonly known as the giant wood sorrel, is a striking perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil. Its foliage and flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardens and shaded landscapes.

Oxalis macrophylla is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
