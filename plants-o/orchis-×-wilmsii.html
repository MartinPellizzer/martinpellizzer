Orchis × wilmsii

Orchis × wilmsii, commonly known as the Wilms' orchid, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Orchis mascula and Orchis morio.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, typically found in moist meadows and woodland edges. The plant produces tall, erect stems with lance-shaped leaves and clusters of pale purple to violet flowers. Its flowers are characterized by their spurred lip and are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
