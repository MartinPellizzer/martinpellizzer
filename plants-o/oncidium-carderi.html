Oncidium carderi

Oncidium carderi, commonly known as the Carder's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a sympodial orchid characterized by its pseudobulbs and linear, grass-like leaves. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences with showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red. Oncidium carderi thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining potting media to prevent root rot.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
