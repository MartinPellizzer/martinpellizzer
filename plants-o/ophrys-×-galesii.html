Ophrys × galesii

Ophrys × galesii, commonly known as the bee orchid hybrid, is a rare and fascinating orchid species native to parts of Europe.

It belongs to the Ophrys genus, which is known for its intricate mimicry of female insects to attract male pollinators. The plant produces showy, bee-like flowers that are highly specialized for pollination through sexual deception. Its botanical features include a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs and linear leaves.

The hybrid nature of Ophrys × galesii results from crosses between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera, leading to unique floral characteristics.
