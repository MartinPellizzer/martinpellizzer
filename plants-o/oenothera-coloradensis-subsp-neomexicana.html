Oenothera coloradensis subsp. neomexicana

Oenothera coloradensis subsp.

neomexicana, commonly known as the New Mexico evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom at dusk.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
