Opuntia testudinis-crus

Opuntia testudinis-crus, commonly known as the Mexican prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in sharp spines and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces edible fruits known as tunas, which are rich in nutrients. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water.

Its structure and adaptations make it well-suited for survival in harsh desert conditions.
