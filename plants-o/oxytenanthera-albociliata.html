Oxytenanthera albociliata

Oxytenanthera albociliata, commonly known as the white-ciliate grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered with fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and wetlands.

Oxytenanthera albociliata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
