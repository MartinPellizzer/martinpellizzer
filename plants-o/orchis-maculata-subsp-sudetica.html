Orchis maculata subsp. sudetica

Orchis maculata subsp.

sudetica, commonly known as the Sudetic spotted orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family. It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems that bear whorled leaves and a single, showy flower with a distinctive purple-pink coloration.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous habitats such as meadows and forest edges. It produces a capsule fruit containing numerous small seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is found primarily in Central and Eastern Europe, where it is considered a rare and protected species.
