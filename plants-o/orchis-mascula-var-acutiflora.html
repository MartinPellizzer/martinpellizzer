Orchis mascula var. acutiflora

Orchis mascula var. acutiflora, commonly known as the sharp-flowered male orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem that can reach up to 1 meter in height, bearing a single, showy flower with a distinctive, spurred lip. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing a violet or purple flower with a yellow or greenish throat. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
