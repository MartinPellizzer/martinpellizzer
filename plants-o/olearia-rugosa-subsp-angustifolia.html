Olearia rugosa subsp. angustifolia

Olearia rugosa subsp.

angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved silver bush, is a shrub native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs.

The plant produces small, daisy-like white flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for landscaping.
