Oxalis nipponica subsp. kantoensis

Oxalis nipponica subsp.

kantoensis, commonly known as Japanese wood sorrel, is a perennial herb native to Japan. It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. It produces edible leaves and flowers, though they should be consumed in moderation due to oxalic acid content.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in Japanese cuisine and medicine.
