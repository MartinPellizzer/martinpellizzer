Oplismenus aemulus var. lasiorhachis

Oplismenus aemulus var. lasiorhachis, commonly known as the glossy sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are glossy green and often have a slightly rough texture. Its inflorescence consists of small, spike-like clusters of greenish-white flowers that appear in late spring to early summer.

The variety *lasiorhachis* is distinguished by its coarser, more rigid leaves compared to the typical form of *Oplismenus aemulus*.
