Ocimum elskensii

Ocimum elskensii, commonly known as Eskens' basil, is a species of flowering plant in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ocimum elskensii is often cultivated for its fragrant leaves, which are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
