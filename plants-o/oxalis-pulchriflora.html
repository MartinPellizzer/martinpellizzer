Oxalis pulchriflora

Oxalis pulchriflora, commonly known as the showy wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish or purple tint, and its flowers are typically pale yellow with five petals. The plant produces small, edible berries that contain seeds for propagation. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Oxalis pulchriflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
