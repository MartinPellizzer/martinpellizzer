Ornithogalum pyramidale subsp. narbonense

Ornithogalum pyramidale subsp.

narbonense, commonly known as the narrow-leaved star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features a basal rosette of narrow, linear leaves and produces tall, slender flower stalks topped with umbels of white or pale green star-shaped flowers.

The plant is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and stems, which help it store water in arid environments. It blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths.

This subspecies is often found in rocky soils and open habitats, showcasing its adaptability to Mediterranean climates.
