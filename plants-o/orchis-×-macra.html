Orchis × macra

Orchis × macra, commonly known as the large-flowered twayblade, is a hybrid orchid species native to Europe.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, grass-like stems and showy, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded areas with rich, loamy soil. Its flowers are arranged in a spike and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Orchis × macra is a perennial herb that relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake, reflecting its ecological dependence on symbiotic relationships.
