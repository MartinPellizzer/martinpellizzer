Oldenlandia congesta

Oldenlandia congesta, commonly known as the clustered spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, showy white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing in the summer months. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, with opposite, linear leaves that are often hairy. Its stems are usually prostrate or ascending, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Oldenlandia congesta is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
