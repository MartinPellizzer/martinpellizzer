Opuntia chaffeyi

Opuntia chaffeyi, commonly known as the Chaffey's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in clusters of sharp spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tuna, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

Opuntia chaffeyi is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in supporting local wildlife by providing food and shelter.
