Orchis athensis

Orchis athensis, commonly known as the Greek orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stem and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, with its inflorescence consisting of several flowers arranged in a spike. Orchis athensis has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and can grow up to 30 centimeters in length.

It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in open woodlands or along streambanks.
