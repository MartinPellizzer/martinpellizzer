Onosma lycia

Onosma lycia, commonly known as Lycian thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal and culinary uses. The plant features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Onosma lycia thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
