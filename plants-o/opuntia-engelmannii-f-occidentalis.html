Opuntia engelmannii f. occidentalis

Opuntia engelmannii f. occidentalis, commonly known as the Western prickly pear, is a cactus species native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems, which are covered in sharp spines and glochids. The plant produces large, showy yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the summer. Its fruit, known as a tuna, is edible and contains a sweet, pulpy interior.

This species is adapted to arid environments and plays an important role in local ecosystems.
