Ocotea malcomberi

Ocotea malcomberi, commonly known as the Malcomber's ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and fragrant, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically green when mature and turn red upon ripening. It is often found in humid, lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.

Its growth habit and foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture in warm climates.
