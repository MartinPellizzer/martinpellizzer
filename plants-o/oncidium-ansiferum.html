Oncidium ansiferum

Oncidium ansiferum, commonly known as the "Rainbow Orchid," is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful flowers that often display a mix of red, orange, yellow, and green hues. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and other structures in its natural habitat. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Oncidium ansiferum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its long-lasting blooms.
