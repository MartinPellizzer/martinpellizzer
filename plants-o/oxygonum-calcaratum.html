Oxygonum calcaratum

Oxygonum calcaratum, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. Oxygonum calcaratum is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
