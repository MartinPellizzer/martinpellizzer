Odontoglossum zelenkoanum

Odontoglossum zelenkoanum, commonly known as the Zelenko's orchid, is a species native to the Andean highlands of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a sympodial growth habit, producing a pseudobulb that stores water and nutrients. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. Its flowers are typically arranged in a raceme and bloom during the cooler months.

Odontoglossum zelenkoanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
