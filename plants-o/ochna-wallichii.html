Ochna wallichii

Ochna wallichii, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey-barked trunk and lance-shaped leaves that turn reddish in autumn. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves and bark contain compounds with potential medicinal properties.

Ochna wallichii is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
