Onobrychis cornuta f. pulvillus

Onobrychis cornuta f. pulvillus, commonly known as the hairy goat's rue, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, palmately compound leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early summer. It is native to southern Europe and thrives in dry, rocky soils.

This fern-like plant is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop for livestock.
