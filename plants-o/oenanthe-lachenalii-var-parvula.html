Oenanthe lachenalii var. parvula

Oenanthe lachenalii var. parvula, commonly known as the small-flowered water dropwort, is a herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in moist or wet habitats such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. The plant features slender, aromatic stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a distinctive basal rosette. Its small, white flowers are clustered in umbels and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is characterized by its delicate appearance and its preference for wet, nutrient-rich soils.
