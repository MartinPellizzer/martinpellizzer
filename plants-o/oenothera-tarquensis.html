Oenothera tarquensis

Oenothera tarquensis, commonly known as the Tarquen's evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening and close by morning. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It belongs to the genus Oenothera, which includes many species of evening primroses known for their unique flowering patterns.

Oenothera tarquensis is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators like moths and beetles.
