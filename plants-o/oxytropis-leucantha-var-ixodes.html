Oxytropis leucantha var. ixodes

Oxytropis leucantha var. ixodes, commonly known as the yellow globemallow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod-like fruit and compound leaves. The plant features showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with small, rounded leaflets, and it grows in dry, rocky habitats.

The species is adapted to arid environments, demonstrating resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
