Ophrys × methonensis

Ophrys × methonensis, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant that results from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland or along stream banks. The plant is notable for its intricate flower structure, which mimics the appearance of female bees to attract male pollinators. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brownish in color, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

This species plays an important role in pollination networks and is a subject of interest in both botanical research and conservation efforts.
