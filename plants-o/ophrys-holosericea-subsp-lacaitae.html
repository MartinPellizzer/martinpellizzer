Ophrys holosericea subsp. lacaitae

Ophrys holosericea subsp.

lacaitae, commonly known as the Sicilian bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the island of Sicily. It is characterized by its unique floral morphology, featuring a bee-like labellum that mimics the appearance and scent of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination.

The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and limestone areas. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to orange in color, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts in its native range.
