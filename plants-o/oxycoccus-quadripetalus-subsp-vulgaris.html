Oxycoccus quadripetalus subsp. vulgaris

Oxycoccus quadripetalus subsp.

vulgaris, commonly known as the common cranberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It produces small, red, edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and other nutrients.

The plant thrives in acidic, wet soils and is often found in bogs and swamps. Its leaves are narrow and dark green, and it has a compact growth habit.

The species is widely cultivated for its fruit, which is used in food, beverages, and traditional medicine.
