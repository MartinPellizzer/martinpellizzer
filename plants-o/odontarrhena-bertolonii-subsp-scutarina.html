Odontarrhena bertolonii subsp. scutarina

Odontarrhena bertolonii subsp.

scutarina, commonly known as the Madagascar star grass, is a perennial herb native to Madagascar. It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale blue or white flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture, and it thrives in dry, rocky habitats.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its unique appearance.
