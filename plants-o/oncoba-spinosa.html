Oncoba spinosa

Oncoba spinosa, commonly known as the Brazilian palm or the spinous palm, is a distinctive plant native to South America.

It is a tall, slender palm with a single trunk and long, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into woody, spiny fruits containing seeds. Oncoba spinosa is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
