Osmanthus intermedius

Osmanthus intermedius, commonly known as the Chinese sweet osmanthus, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant produces small, round, aromatic fruits that are often used in traditional Chinese medicine and culinary applications. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a slightly glossy surface.

Osmanthus intermedius is valued for its ornamental beauty and its essential oils, which are widely used in perfumery and aromatherapy.
