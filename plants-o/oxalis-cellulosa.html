Oxalis cellulosa

Oxalis cellulosa, commonly known as the forest oxalis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks. Its botanical features include heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive white or yellowish vein pattern.

Oxalis cellulosa is also known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, forming dense ground covers in suitable habitats.
