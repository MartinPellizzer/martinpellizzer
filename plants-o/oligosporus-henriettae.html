Oligosporus henriettae

Oligosporus henriettae, commonly known as the Henrietta's spore plant, is a rare and unique species characterized by its intricate spore structure.

It belongs to the fungal family and exhibits a complex life cycle involving both sexual and asexual reproduction. The plant's spores are microscopic, with a distinctive ornamentation pattern that aids in identification. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its rarity, it is of particular interest to mycologists and conservationists.
