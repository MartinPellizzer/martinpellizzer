Oxalis rippae

Oxalis rippae, commonly known as the Rippae wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or rocky areas. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, edible berries that turn red when mature.

Oxalis rippae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
