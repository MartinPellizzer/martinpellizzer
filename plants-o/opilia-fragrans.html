Opilia fragrans

Opilia fragrans, commonly known as the sweet shrub, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Opilia fragrans is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
