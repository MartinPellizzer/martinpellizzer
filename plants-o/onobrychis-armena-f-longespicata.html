Onobrychis armena f. longespicata

Onobrychis armena f. longespicata, commonly known as Armenian clover, is a perennial legume native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats. It has a creeping growth habit and produces cylindrical pods containing several seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential as a forage crop.
