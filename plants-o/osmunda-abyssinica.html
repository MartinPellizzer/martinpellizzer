Osmunda abyssinica

Osmunda abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian water fern, is a species of fern that thrives in wet, swampy environments.

It is characterized by its large, floating fronds that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant has a complex life cycle involving both a sporophytic and a gametophytic phase. Its rhizomes are submerged and help in anchoring the plant while absorbing nutrients from the water.

Osmunda abyssinica plays an important ecological role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various microorganisms and small aquatic life.
