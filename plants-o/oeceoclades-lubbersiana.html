Oeceoclades lubbersiana

Oeceoclades lubbersiana, commonly known as the Lubbers' orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features a sympodial growth pattern, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a distinctive coloration that varies among species.

The leaves are broad and strap-like, contributing to its overall appearance and aiding in photosynthesis.
