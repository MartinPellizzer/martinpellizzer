Oplonia puberula

Oplonia puberula, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf sedge, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually green or brown and appear in clusters.

Oplonia puberula is often found in wet or semi-aquatic habitats and can be an indicator species for certain types of wetland ecosystems.
