Ophiostachys virginica

Ophiostachys virginica, commonly known as wiregrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, reddish-brown flowers. Wiregrass is often found in dry, sandy soils and is an important component of longleaf pine ecosystems.

It plays a vital role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife species.
