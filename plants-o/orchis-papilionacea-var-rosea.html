Orchis papilionacea var. rosea

Orchis papilionacea var. rosea, commonly known as the rose-flowered twayblade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive, rose-pink floral structures that resemble butterflies. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist woodlands and has a slender, erect stem with a single, showy flower. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette at the base.

The species is notable for its specialized pollination mechanisms and is often found in association with specific mycorrhizal fungi.
