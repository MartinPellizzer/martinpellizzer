Ocimum odontopetalum

Ocimum odontopetalum, commonly known as the wild basil or Indian basil, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs such as mint and sage. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a strongly aromatic scent, and it produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or pink in color. Ocimum odontopetalum is often cultivated for its culinary and medicinal uses, as well as its ornamental value.

Its essential oils contain compounds like linalool and eugenol, which contribute to its distinctive fragrance and potential therapeutic properties.
