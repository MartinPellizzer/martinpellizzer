Ossaea humilis

Ossaea humilis, commonly known as the dwarf lily of the valley, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves that form a rosette at the base. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland settings.

Ossaea humilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its delicate, fragrant blooms.
