Onosma hookeri var. intermedia

Onosma hookeri var. intermedia, commonly known as the Kashmir Wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, preferring well-drained soils. It has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and is part of the Boraginaceae family.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
