Ocotea gymnoblasta

Ocotea gymnoblasta, commonly known as the smooth-leaved ocotea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon trees, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small drupes that turn from green to yellow or red as they mature.

Ocotea gymnoblasta is valued for its aromatic wood and is often found in shaded understory environments within humid forests.
