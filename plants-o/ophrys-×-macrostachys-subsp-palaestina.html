Ophrys × macrostachys subsp. palaestina

Ophrys × macrostachys subsp.

palaestina, commonly known as the Palestinian bee orchid, is a rare hybrid orchid native to the Levant region. It is characterized by its intricate floral structure, which mimics the appearance of female bees to attract male pollinators.

The plant typically grows in rocky or arid soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brown, with a distinctive bee-like shape and scent.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
