Orchidantha micrantha

Orchidantha micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered orchid, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its compact growth habit and slender, hairy stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with a distinctive lip petal. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Orchidantha micrantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
