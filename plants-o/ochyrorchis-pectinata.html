Ochyrorchis pectinata

Ochyrorchis pectinata, commonly known as the feather-leaf orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like leaves that are finely divided and resemble feathers, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in late winter to early spring, often clustered in a spike. It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in open, sunny habitats.

Ochyrorchis pectinata is a rare and specialized species, often requiring specific environmental conditions for successful growth.
