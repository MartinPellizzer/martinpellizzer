Opuntia megalarthra

Opuntia megalarthra, commonly known as the Mexican prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems, known as cladodes, which are covered in sharp spines. The plant produces bright yellow or orange flowers, typically in the spring or early summer. Its fruits, called tunas, are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor.

Opuntia megalarthra is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife.
