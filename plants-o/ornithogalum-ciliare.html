Ornithogalum ciliare

Ornithogalum ciliare, commonly known as the fringed star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces umbellate inflorescences with white to pale pink flowers. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, star-shaped blooms that appear in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Ornithogalum ciliare is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in horticulture for its elegant appearance.
