Ocotea montana

Ocotea montana, commonly known as mountain ocotea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage and bark. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests, often reaching heights of up to 15 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy, dark green surface with a distinctive aromatic scent.

Ocotea montana is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
