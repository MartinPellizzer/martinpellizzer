Oryza australiensis

Oryza australiensis, commonly known as the Australian ricegrass, is a grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spikelet-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Oryza australiensis is of interest to botanists due to its potential for use in ecological restoration and its unique adaptations to harsh climates.
