Ophrys helios f. taloniensis

Ophrys helios f. taloniensis, commonly known as the Talonius sun orchid, is a rare orchid species native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the genus Ophrys, which is known for its mimicry of female insects to attract male pollinators. The plant features a single, showy flower with a distinctive, bee-like appearance, including a pseudostigma that mimics the female bee's genitalia. It typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and is characterized by its slender, erect stem and narrow leaves.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
