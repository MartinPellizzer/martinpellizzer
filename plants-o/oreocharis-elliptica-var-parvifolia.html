Oreocharis elliptica var. parvifolia

Oreocharis elliptica var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved elliptic orchid, is a species native to China, particularly in Yunnan Province.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, lanceolate leaves that are typically small in size. The plant produces delicate, white or pale yellow flowers with a distinctive, fragrant aroma. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
