Olsynium chrysochromum

Olsynium chrysochromum, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a showy inflorescence resembling a crown. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Olsynium chrysochromum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
