Oenothera serratifolia

Oenothera serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in sandy or loamy soils and is often found in open habitats such as prairies and disturbed areas.

Oenothera serratifolia is part of the Onagraceae family and is known for its ecological importance to pollinators.
