Ophelia bimaculata

Ophelia bimaculata, commonly known as the water clover, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive whorled arrangement of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that resemble the leaves of clover. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Ophelia bimaculata thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds, ditches, and marshes.

It is an important species for aquatic ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and fish.
