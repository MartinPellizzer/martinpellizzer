Oxalis microstachys

Oxalis microstachys, commonly known as the small-stemmed wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each consisting of three heart-shaped leaflets, and produces small, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in shady, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.

Oxalis microstachys is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
