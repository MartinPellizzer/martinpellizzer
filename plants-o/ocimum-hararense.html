Ocimum hararense

Ocimum hararense, commonly known as the Harare basil, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like mint and oregano. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant leaves and clusters of white or pale purple flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its essential oils are valued for their aromatic and potential therapeutic properties.
