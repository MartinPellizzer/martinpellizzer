Oryza filiformis

Oryza filiformis, commonly known as the slender ricegrass, is a grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, thread-like leaves and small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its flowering occurs during the rainy season, and it produces small grains that are not typically cultivated for human consumption.

Oryza filiformis plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
