Oncidium heterosepalum

Oncidium heterosepalum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, golden-yellow flowers that are often adorned with intricate patterns and a pleasant fragrance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Oncidium heterosepalum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
