Oreogrammitis baldwinii

Oreogrammitis baldwinii, commonly known as the Baldwin's wall-rue, is a rare fern species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that typically grow in moist, shaded environments. The plant's spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the undersides of its leaves. Oreogrammitis baldwinii thrives in rocky or limestone habitats, often clinging to vertical surfaces.
