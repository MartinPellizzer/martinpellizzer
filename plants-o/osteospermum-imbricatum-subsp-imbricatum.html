Osteospermum imbricatum subsp. imbricatum

Osteospermum imbricatum subsp.

imbricatum, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white or pale blue petals.

The plant typically grows as a perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 30 cm, and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is adapted to full sun conditions.

Its foliage is usually gray-green, with hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in arid environments.
