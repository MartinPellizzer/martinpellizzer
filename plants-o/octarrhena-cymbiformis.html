Octarrhena cymbiformis

Octarrhena cymbiformis, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, blue-green leaves that help it conserve water. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. Its succulent nature allows it to thrive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water.

Octarrhena cymbiformis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
