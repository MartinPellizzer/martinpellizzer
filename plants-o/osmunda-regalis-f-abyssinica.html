Osmunda regalis f. abyssinica

Osmunda regalis f. abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian royal fern, is a fern species native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, feathery fronds that can reach lengths of up to two meters. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically near streams or in dense forests. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, and it reproduces through sexual reproduction via spore dispersal.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitats.
