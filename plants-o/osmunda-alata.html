Osmunda alata

Osmunda alata, commonly known as the cinnamon fern, is a species of fern characterized by its distinctive, feathery fronds.

It is native to eastern North America and thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has a short, stocky rhizome with a cinnamon-colored root sheath, which gives it its common name. Its fronds are divided into segments that resemble a fan, with the fertile spore-bearing structures located at the tips.

Osmunda alata is a perennial plant that plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
