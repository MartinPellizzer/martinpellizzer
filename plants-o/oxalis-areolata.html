Oxalis areolata

Oxalis areolata, commonly known as the starry wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each leaflet having a distinctive white or pale green spot. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Oxalis areolata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and blooms.
