Odontoglossum anthoxanthum

Odontoglossum anthoxanthum, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

This herbaceous plant features pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, supporting its growth in high-altitude environments. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by bees and other insects. The plant produces showy, fragrant blooms during the spring and early summer.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and requires well-drained soil and bright, indirect light to thrive.
