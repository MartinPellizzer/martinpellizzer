Orobus subrotundus

Orobus subrotundus, commonly known as the small round orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
