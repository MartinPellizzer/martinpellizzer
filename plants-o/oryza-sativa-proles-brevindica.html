Oryza sativa proles brevindica

Oryza sativa proles brevindica, commonly known as short-grain rice, is a variety of rice characterized by its compact grain structure and high amylose content.

It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with abundant water supply. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 1.5 meters, with long, slender leaves and panicles that bear numerous small, white flowers. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a reproductive cycle that involves both vegetative and reproductive stages.

This variety is often used in traditional cuisines for its sticky texture when cooked.
