Ophrys muscifera f. apiculata

Ophrys muscifera f. apiculata, commonly known as the hairy bee orchid, is a rare and fascinating orchid species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, open habitats. Its flowers are predominantly green with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

This orchid is protected in many areas due to its limited distribution and vulnerability to habitat loss.
