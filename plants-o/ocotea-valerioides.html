Ocotea valerioides

Ocotea valerioides, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree or *Pimenta*, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and showy white flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but often have a spicy or peppery flavor. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in coastal and lowland forests.

Its essential oils and medicinal properties have made it valuable in traditional medicine and aromatherapy.
