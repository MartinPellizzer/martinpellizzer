Orchis tridentata var. acuminata

Orchis tridentata var. acuminata, commonly known as the acuminata orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of Europe and Asia.

This perennial herbaceous plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and thrives in woodland or grassland habitats. It features a tall stem with alternate, lanceolate leaves and produces striking, violet or purple flowers in late spring. The flowers are characterized by their three prominent sepals and a spurred lip, which is a distinctive feature of many orchid species.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its beauty.
