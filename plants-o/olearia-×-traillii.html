Olearia × traillii

Olearia × traillii, commonly known as the New Zealand frostflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features silvery-white, woolly foliage that helps it withstand harsh alpine conditions. Its flowers are typically small and yellow, appearing in clusters during the spring and early summer.

Olearia × traillii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
