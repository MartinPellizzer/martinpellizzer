Olearia allomii

Olearia allomii, commonly known as the Stewart Island daisy, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with white petals and yellow centers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to the temperate climate of Stewart Island. Olearia allomii has small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces numerous flower heads that bloom in spring.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological role and limited distribution.
