Odontoglossum cervantesii var. decorum

Odontoglossum cervantesii var. decorum, commonly known as the Mexican orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. The plant features a compact growth habit with a pseudobulb and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. Its leaves are elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The species is valued in horticulture for its beauty and adaptability to cultivation.
