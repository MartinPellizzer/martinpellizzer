Odontoglossum alexandrae var. stevensii

Odontoglossum alexandrae var. stevensii, commonly known as the Alexandra's orchid, is a striking orchid species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple and white. The plant produces long, narrow leaves and has a pseudobulb structure typical of many orchid species. Its flowers are borne on terminal inflorescences and are often fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy cultivation under proper conditions.
