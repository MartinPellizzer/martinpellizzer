Oxalis landbeckii

Oxalis landbeckii, commonly known as the mountain oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces orange or red berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by birds.

Oxalis landbeckii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
