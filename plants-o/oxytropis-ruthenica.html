Oxytropis ruthenica

Oxytropis ruthenica, commonly known as the Siberian locoweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the steppes and forests of Eastern Europe and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Oxytropis ruthenica is adapted to well-drained soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, including dry and semi-arid regions.

It is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to its ecological significance in certain habitats.
