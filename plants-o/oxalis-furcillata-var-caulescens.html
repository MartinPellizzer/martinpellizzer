Oxalis furcillata var. caulescens

Oxalis furcillata var. caulescens, commonly known as the upright wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a short, sturdy stem that supports its trifoliate leaves, each leaflet being heart-shaped with a distinctive reddish or purple midrib. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens or woodland settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate foliage and vibrant floral display.
