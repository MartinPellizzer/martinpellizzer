Oxypetalum argentinum

Oxypetalum argentinum, commonly known as the Argentine rose or Argentine sweet pepper, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, often pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated edge, while the fruit is a small, fleshy drupe.

Oxypetalum argentinum is valued for its ornamental flowers and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
