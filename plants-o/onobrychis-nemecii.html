Onobrychis nemecii

Onobrychis nemecii, commonly known as the Czech clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along forest edges, preferring well-drained soils. It is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a notable species in botanical studies.
