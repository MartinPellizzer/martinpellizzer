Orchis pectinata

Orchis pectinata, commonly known as the white helleborine, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its lanceolate, dark green leaves and tall flowering stems that bear clusters of white to pale green flowers. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in woodlands and meadows. Orchis pectinata has a complex root system and produces a single, elongated seed pod containing numerous small, dry seeds.

It is known for its ability to thrive in shaded environments and is often associated with moist, well-drained soils.
