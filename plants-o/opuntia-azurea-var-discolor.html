Opuntia azurea var. discolor

Opuntia azurea var. discolor, commonly known as the blue prickly pear, is a cactus species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems that are typically blue-green in color, with a distinctive bluish-white bloom. The plant produces yellow flowers that develop into edible, red-fleshed fruits. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to arid environments.

Opuntia azurea var. discolor is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of food in desert regions.
