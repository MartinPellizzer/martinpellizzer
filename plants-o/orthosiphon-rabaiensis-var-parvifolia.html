Orthosiphon rabaiensis var. parvifolia

Orthosiphon rabaiensis var. parvifolia, commonly known as small-leaved horse-herb, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, characterized by square stems and opposite leaves. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of pale blue to purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Its botanical structure supports its use in traditional medicine for its purported diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.
