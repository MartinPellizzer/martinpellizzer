Ophrys mesopotamica

Ophrys mesopotamica, commonly known as the Mesopotamian bee orchid, is a species of orchid native to parts of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which closely resembles the appearance of a female bee, a mimicry strategy used to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptation to harsh conditions. Its flowers are usually a combination of yellow, brown, and black hues, contributing to its striking visual appearance.

Ophrys mesopotamica is a perennial herb with a short flowering period, usually occurring in spring.
