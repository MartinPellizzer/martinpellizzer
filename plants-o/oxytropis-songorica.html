Oxytropis songorica

Oxytropis songorica, commonly known as the Songorica milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in spikes, and its leaves are palmately divided into several leaflets. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Oxytropis songorica is often found in steppe and desert environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
