Odontoglossum krameri var. smithianum

Odontoglossum krameri var. smithianum, commonly known as the Smith's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers with a distinctive coloration. The plant typically features dark green, lance-shaped leaves and has a pseudobulb structure typical of many orchids. Its flowers are usually pale green to white with a yellow or orange lip, and it blooms during the cooler months.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
