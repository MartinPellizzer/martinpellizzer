Oxytropis flavovirens

Oxytropis flavovirens, commonly known as yellow-green locoweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as sagebrush steppe and grasslands.

Oxytropis flavovirens is adapted to arid environments and is often found in disturbed soils.
