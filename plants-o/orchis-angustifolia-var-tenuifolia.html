Orchis angustifolia var. tenuifolia

Orchis angustifolia var. tenuifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features slender, lance-shaped leaves and produces delicate, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinct lip. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its specialized mycorrhizal relationships with fungi.

The species is often found in calcareous soils and is considered a rare and protected plant in many regions.
