Oxalis arrojadoi

Oxalis arrojadoi, commonly known as the Crimson Oxalis, is a strikingly beautiful plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple hues. The plant produces clusters of small, delicate flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and container planting.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, Oxalis arrojadoi is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
