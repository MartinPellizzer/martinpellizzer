Oenothera grandiflora subsp. coronifera

Oenothera grandiflora subsp.

coronifera, commonly known as the crownflower evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its healing benefits.
