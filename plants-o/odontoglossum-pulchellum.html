Odontoglossum pulchellum

Odontoglossum pulchellum, commonly known as the violet orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows as a pseudobulbous herb, with thick, fleshy stems that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped, and it produces intricate, fragrant blossoms that bloom in the spring.

Odontoglossum pulchellum is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
