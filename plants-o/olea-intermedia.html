Olea intermedia

Olea intermedia, commonly known as the Portuguese olive, is a small evergreen tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, wrinkled fruits that are typically harvested for their oil content. Olea intermedia is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and tolerance to coastal conditions.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
