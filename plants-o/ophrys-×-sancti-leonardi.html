Ophrys × sancti-leonardi

Ophrys × sancti-leonardi, commonly known as the Leonardi's bee orchid, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral morphology, which mimics the appearance of female bees to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils in Mediterranean regions, including parts of southern Europe. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brown in color, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Ophrys × sancti-leonardi is a rare and specialized species, often found in specific microhabitats where its pollination strategy is most effective.
