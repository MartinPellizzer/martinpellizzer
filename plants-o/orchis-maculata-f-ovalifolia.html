Orchis maculata f. ovalifolia

Orchis maculata f. ovalifolia, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a rare and striking variant of the common spotted orchid.

It is characterized by its distinctive dark purple or brown spots on the pale greenish-yellow petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are oval-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its unique appearance.

This orchid is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is often found in the British Isles and parts of Europe.
