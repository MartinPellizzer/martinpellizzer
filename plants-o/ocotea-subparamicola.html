Ocotea subparamicola

Ocotea subparamicola, commonly known as the Andean ocotea, is a small evergreen tree native to the cloud forests of southern Ecuador and northern Peru.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments at elevations between 1,200 and 2,500 meters. Its foliage is dense and dark green, with a distinct, spicy scent when crushed.

The species is noted for its ecological importance in maintaining the biodiversity of its native habitat.
