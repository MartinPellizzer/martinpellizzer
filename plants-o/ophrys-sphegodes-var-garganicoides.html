Ophrys sphegodes var. garganicoides

Ophrys sphegodes var. garganicoides, commonly known as the bee orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking floral structure that mimics a female bee, a feature used in its unique pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its flowers are usually a mix of yellow, brown, and orange, with intricate patterns that aid in attracting male bees for pollination.

The plant is also notable for its ability to form dense colonies and is considered a valuable indicator species for certain ecological conditions.
