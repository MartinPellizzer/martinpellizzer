Oxycoccus palustris f. microphyllus

Oxycoccus palustris f. microphyllus, commonly known as the dwarf cranberry, is a low-growing, evergreen shrub native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in length, giving it a compact appearance. The plant produces tiny, red berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in acidic, wet soils and is often found in bogs and heathlands.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and conservation efforts in its native habitats.
