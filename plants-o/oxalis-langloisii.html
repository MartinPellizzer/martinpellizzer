Oxalis langloisii

Oxalis langloisii, commonly known as the yellow wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that resemble those of true sorrels. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in coastal regions and is adapted to dry, coastal climates.

Oxalis langloisii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
