Orobanche reticulata f. adusta

Orobanche reticulata f. adusta, commonly known as the dark reticulated broomrape, is a parasitic flowering plant belonging to the Orobanchaceae family.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients, typically growing on the roots of various grasses and legumes. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually green or brown in color, contrasting with its pale, hairless stems. Its unique morphology and parasitic nature make it an interesting subject in botanical studies.

It is native to regions of Asia and is often found in disturbed or arid habitats.
