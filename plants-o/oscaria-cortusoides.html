Oscaria cortusoides

Oscaria cortusoides, commonly known as the purple rock cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small, mat-forming perennial that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant produces clusters of small, purple or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered in a fine pubescence.

Oscaria cortusoides is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, where it thrives in alpine and arctic environments.
