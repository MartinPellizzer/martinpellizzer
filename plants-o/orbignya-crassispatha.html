Orbignya crassispatha

Orbignya crassispatha, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves and produces woody pods containing edible seeds, known as Brazil nuts. The plant has a deep root system that helps stabilize the soil and supports biodiversity in its ecosystem. It thrives in humid, nutrient-rich soils and requires a warm, tropical climate.

The tree is also valued for its ecological and economic importance, particularly for its nutrient-dense seeds.
