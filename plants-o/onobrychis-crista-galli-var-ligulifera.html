Onobrychis crista-galli var. ligulifera

Onobrychis crista-galli var. ligulifera, commonly known as the narrow-leafed clover, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves with narrow, lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in spike-like clusters. The plant is characterized by its robust root system and ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in pastureland and erosion control.

Its botanical structure supports its role in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
