Ocimum tuberosum

Ocimum tuberosum, commonly known as wild basil or Indian basil, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and lavender. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. Ocimum tuberosum is often grown for its fragrant foliage and is used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Its essential oils contain compounds like linalool and eugenol, which contribute to its medicinal properties.
