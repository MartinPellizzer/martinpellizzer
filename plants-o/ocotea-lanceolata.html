Ocotea lanceolata

Ocotea lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant typically grows in humid, lowland forests and can reach heights of up to 30 meters. Its leaves are lanceolate, hence the species name, and are often used in traditional medicine.

The tree produces small, fragrant flowers and dark, edible fruits that attract various wildlife.
