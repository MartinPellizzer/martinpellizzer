Ophrys × montisciana

Ophrys × montisciana, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant that exhibits unique botanical features.

It is a natural hybrid between Ophrys apifera (the bee orchid) and Ophrys insectifera (the wasp orchid), resulting in a plant with a distinctive floral morphology. The orchid's flowers mimic the appearance of female bees or wasps, a trait known as sexual deception, which is used to attract male pollinators. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, typically growing in dense clumps, while the roots are fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

This species is primarily found in the mountainous regions of Europe, where it thrives in shaded, moist habitats.
