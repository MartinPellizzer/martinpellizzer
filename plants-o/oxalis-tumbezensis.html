Oxalis tumbezensis

Oxalis tumbezensis, commonly known as the Tumbes oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories, and thrives in humid climates. Its foliage is often adorned with intricate patterns, and it produces edible tubers that are sometimes used in local cuisines.

Oxalis tumbezensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
