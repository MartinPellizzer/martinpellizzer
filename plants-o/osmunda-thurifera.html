Osmunda thurifera

Osmunda thurifera, commonly known as the giant water fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, floating fronds that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

Osmunda thurifera plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
