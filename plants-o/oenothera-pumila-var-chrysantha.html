Oenothera pumila var. chrysantha

Oenothera pumila var. chrysantha, commonly known as the golden evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches a height of 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that open in the evening, hence its common name. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

It is often found in open woodlands and prairies, where it contributes to the biodiversity of these ecosystems.
