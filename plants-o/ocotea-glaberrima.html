Ocotea glaberrima

Ocotea glaberrima, commonly known as the Caribbean cinnamon tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like laurel and avocado. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and simple, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically white or pale yellow, and it produces small, dark blue berries.

The tree is valued for its aromatic wood and has traditional uses in local medicine and as a source of essential oils.
