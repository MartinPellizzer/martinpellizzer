Oxalis acetosella var. subpurpurascens

Oxalis acetosella var. subpurpurascens, commonly known as the purple shamrock, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive purple-tinged margin, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically pink or white, often in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a shade-tolerant, decorative plant.
