Oplismenus burmanni var. intermedius

Oplismenus burmanni var. intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate grass or slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands, where it thrives in humid conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, with small, inconspicuous flowers that appear in early spring. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense mats that help stabilize soil.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate a range of soil types.
