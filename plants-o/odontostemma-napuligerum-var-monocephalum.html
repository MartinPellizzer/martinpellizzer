Odontostemma napuligerum var. monocephalum

Odontostemma napuligerum var. monocephalum, commonly known as the single-headed toothroot, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or pink in color. It is native to tropical regions of Africa and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional medicine practices.
