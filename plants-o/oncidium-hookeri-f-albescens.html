Oncidium hookeri f. albescens

Oncidium hookeri f. albescens, commonly known as the white-flowered orchid, is a hybrid orchid species that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

It is distinguished by its pale to white flowers, which contrast with the typical green or yellow hues of its parent species. The plant features elongated, strap-like leaves that are typically arranged in a fan shape, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This orchid is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for both novice and experienced growers.
