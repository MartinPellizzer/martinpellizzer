Ochotonophila allochrusoides

Ochotonophila allochrusoides, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It features slender, upright stems that bear whorled clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue or violet. The plant has lanceolate leaves with hairy surfaces, adapted to the harsh, cold environments it inhabits. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun exposure.

Ochotonophila allochrusoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its alpine ecosystem.
