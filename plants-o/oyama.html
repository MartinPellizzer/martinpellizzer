Oyama

Oyama, commonly known as the Japanese mountain ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the genus Sorbus and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Oyama thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas at higher elevations.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
