Oncidium dactylopterum

Oncidium dactylopterum, commonly known as the feathered orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, gracefully curving petals and sepals that resemble feathers, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, with a distinctive three-lobed lip.

Oncidium dactylopterum is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its delicate, long-lasting blooms.
