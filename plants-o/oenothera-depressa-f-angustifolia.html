Oenothera depressa f. angustifolia

Oenothera depressa f. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, sandy soils and rocky habitats. The plant features slender, angular stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers, which open in the evening, are yellow with four petals and are pollinated primarily by moths.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has also been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
