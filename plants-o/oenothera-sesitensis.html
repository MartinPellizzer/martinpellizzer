Oenothera sesitensis

Oenothera sesitensis, commonly known as the slender evening primrose, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and showy yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oily seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or gravelly soils.

Oenothera sesitensis is part of the Onagraceae family, which includes other evening primroses and related species.
