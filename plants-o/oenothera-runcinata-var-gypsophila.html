Oenothera runcinata var. gypsophila

Oenothera runcinata var. gypsophila, commonly known as the gypsophilum evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has hairy, linear leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
