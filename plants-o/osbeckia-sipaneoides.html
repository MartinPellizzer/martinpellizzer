Osbeckia sipaneoides

Osbeckia sipaneoides, commonly known as the narrow-leafed osbeckia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually lance-shaped and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and are typically pink or purple in color.

Osbeckia sipaneoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
