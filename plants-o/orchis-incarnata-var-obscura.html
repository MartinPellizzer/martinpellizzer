Orchis incarnata var. obscura

Orchis incarnata var. obscura, commonly known as the dark pink orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, slender stem and showy, violet to dark pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded habitats and blooms from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged along the stem, and it produces small, dark brown seeds in its fruit.

This variety is distinguished by its darker floral coloration compared to the more common var. incarnata.
