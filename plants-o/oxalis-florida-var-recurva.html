Oxalis florida var. recurva

Oxalis florida var. recurva, commonly known as the recurved wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive reddish veins and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a low-growing habit, often forming dense mats on woodland floors. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance.

It prefers partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.
