Olea viridula

Olea viridula, commonly known as the green olive, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to southern Europe, North Africa, and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, dark green leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that turn from green to black as they mature. Olea viridula is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
