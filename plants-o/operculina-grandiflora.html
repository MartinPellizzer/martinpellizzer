Operculina grandiflora

Operculina grandiflora, commonly known as the giant operculina, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter, typically in shades of yellow and orange. The plant features a robust stem with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release numerous small, black seeds.

Operculina grandiflora is often grown for its ornamental value in gardens due to its striking floral display.
