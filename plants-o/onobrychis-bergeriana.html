Onobrychis bergeriana

Onobrychis bergeriana, commonly known as the Armenian clover, is a perennial leguminous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to rocky and arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and deep root systems. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Onobrychis bergeriana is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in degraded areas.
