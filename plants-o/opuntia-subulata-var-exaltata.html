Opuntia subulata var. exaltata

Opuntia subulata var. exaltata, commonly known as the tall spineless prickly pear, is a cacti species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, red fruits. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to arid environments.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and its fruit is used in traditional cuisine.
