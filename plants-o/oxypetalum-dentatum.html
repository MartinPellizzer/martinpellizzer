Oxypetalum dentatum

Oxypetalum dentatum, commonly known as the toothed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, with a distinctive toothed edge on the petals. The plant has slender, upright stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated margins. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Oxypetalum dentatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
