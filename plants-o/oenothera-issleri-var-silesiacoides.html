Oenothera issleri var. silesiacoides

Oenothera issleri var. silesiacoides, commonly known as the Silesian evening primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Onagraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, exhibiting a compact growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers have four petals and are pollinated primarily by moths, which are active at night.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral display.
