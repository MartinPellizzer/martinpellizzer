Ophrys × promontorii

Ophrys × promontorii, commonly known as the promontory orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its intricate, bee-mimicking flowers that play a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its floral structure includes a specialized labellum that enhances mimicry of female bees, attracting male pollinators.

Botanically, it exhibits traits typical of the Ophrys genus, including a single leaf and a short, erect stem.
