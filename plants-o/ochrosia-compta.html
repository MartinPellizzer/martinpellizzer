Ochrosia compta

Ochrosia compta, commonly known as the silverleaf pittosporum, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically silver-white in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by orange-red berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal environments.

Ochrosia compta is relatively drought-tolerant and prefers well-drained soils.
