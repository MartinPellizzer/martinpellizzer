Orchis sulphurea

Orchis sulphurea, commonly known as the sulphur twayblade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers are arranged in a spike and are often pollinated by insects.

Orchis sulphurea is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for various pollinators.
