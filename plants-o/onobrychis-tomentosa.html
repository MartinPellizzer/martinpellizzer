Onobrychis tomentosa

Onobrychis tomentosa, commonly known as woolly burclover, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, yellow pea-like flowers that are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Onobrychis tomentosa is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it a valuable plant in ecological restoration projects.
