Oncerum rubellum

Oncerum rubellum, commonly known as the redflowered oncerum, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, commonly known as the palm family, and is characterized by its slender, unbranched stems. The plant produces striking red inflorescences that are often mistaken for flowers but are actually clusters of small, spadix-like structures. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its humid habitat.

Oncerum rubellum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in tropical gardens for its vibrant color and unique appearance.
