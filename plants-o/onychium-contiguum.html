Onychium contiguum

Onychium contiguum, commonly known as the clustered onychium, is a member of the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its herbaceous growth habit and distinctive seed structures.

It is native to South America, particularly Chile, where it thrives in rocky and coastal environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color, and its leaves are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Onychium contiguum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic studies.
