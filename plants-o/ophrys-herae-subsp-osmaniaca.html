Ophrys herae subsp. osmaniaca

Ophrys herae subsp.

osmaniaca, commonly known as the Osmaniaca Bee Orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the Balkans. It is characterized by its striking floral morphology, with a lip that mimics the appearance of a female bee, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky slopes. Its flowers are usually pale green to brown in color, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
