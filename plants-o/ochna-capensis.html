Ochna capensis

Ochna capensis, commonly known as the Natal wild pear, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces round, fleshy fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Ochna capensis is often found in rocky areas and is adapted to dry, well-drained soils.
