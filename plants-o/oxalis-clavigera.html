Oxalis clavigera

Oxalis clavigera, commonly known as the slender wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically heart-shaped and have a reddish or purple tint. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Oxalis clavigera is often found in shaded, moist environments such as forests and woodlands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
