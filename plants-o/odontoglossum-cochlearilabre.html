Odontoglossum cochlearilabre

Odontoglossum cochlearilabre, commonly known as the cochlearilabre orchid, is a species native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in cool, humid environments with well-drained soil. The plant features a pseudobulb that supports its growth and stores water, while its leaves are strap-shaped and often covered in a waxy coating. The flowers are showy, with intricate patterns and a variety of colors, including purple, pink, and white.

It is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
