Odontostemma fridericae

Odontostemma fridericae, commonly known as the Florida starfruit, is a tropical plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in wetland habitats. The plant produces small, yellow, star-shaped fruits that are edible when ripe. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a slightly serrated edge.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local wildlife.
