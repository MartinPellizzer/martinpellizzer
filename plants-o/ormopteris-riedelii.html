Ormopteris riedelii

Ormopteris riedelii, commonly known as the Riedel's water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and marshes. Its rhizomes form dense mats on the water surface, contributing to water filtration and habitat creation for small aquatic organisms.

Ormopteris riedelii plays a vital role in maintaining the ecological balance of its native wetland ecosystems.
