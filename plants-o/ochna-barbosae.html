Ochna barbosae

Ochna barbosae, commonly known as the Barbo's ochna, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by orange or red berries. Ochna barbosae is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in dry, sunny environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and fruit.
