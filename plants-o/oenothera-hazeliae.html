Oenothera hazeliae

Oenothera hazeliae, commonly known as the hazel-leaf evening primrose, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Oenothera hazeliae is part of the Onagraceae family, which includes other evening primroses known for their ecological and medicinal significance.
