Orchiodes marginatum

Orchiodes marginatum, commonly known as the Margined Orchid, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers with a complex structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers feature a distinctive marginated pattern on the petals, which gives it its common name.

Orchiodes marginatum is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or conservatories.
