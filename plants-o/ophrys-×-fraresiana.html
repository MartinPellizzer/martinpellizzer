Ophrys × fraresiana

Ophrys × fraresiana, commonly known as the bee orchid hybrid, is a fascinating plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Ophrys apifera (the bee orchid) and Ophrys insectifera (the wasp orchid). The plant is characterized by its unique floral morphology, with a pouch-shaped lip that mimics the appearance of an insect, aiding in pollination by male bees. It is typically found in calcareous soils across Europe, thriving in open, sunny habitats.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a model species for studying plant hybridization and mimicry in orchids.
