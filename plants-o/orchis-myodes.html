Orchis myodes

Orchis myodes, commonly known as the fairy orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stem and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in woodland habitats and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.

The orchid's morphology and reproductive strategies reflect adaptations to its specific ecological niche.
