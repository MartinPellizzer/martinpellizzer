Ostericum maximowiczii

Ostericum maximowiczii, commonly known as the Siberian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features upright, slender stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

Ostericum maximowiczii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
