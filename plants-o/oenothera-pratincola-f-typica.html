Oenothera pratincola f. typica

Oenothera pratincola f. typica, commonly known as the plains evening primrose, is a herbaceous plant native to the central United States.

It is characterized by its yellow, tubular flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in open prairies and disturbed soils, exhibiting a sprawling growth habit. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are slender and branching.

The species is noted for its ecological importance in supporting pollinator populations and its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
