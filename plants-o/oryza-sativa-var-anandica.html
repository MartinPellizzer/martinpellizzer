Oryza sativa var. anandica

Oryza sativa var. anandica, commonly known as the sacred rice of India, is a variety of rice native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated grains and a distinct aroma, often associated with spiritual and cultural significance. The plant typically grows in shallow water or wetland environments, thriving in tropical and subtropical climates. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system, a hollow stem, and small, clustered flowers that develop into grain-filled panicles.

This variety is not only valued for its culinary uses but also for its role in traditional rituals and agricultural practices.
