Ophrys arundinacea

Ophrys arundinacea, commonly known as the grass orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and its unique, orchid-like flowers that are often adorned with intricate patterns resembling insects. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Ophrys arundinacea is a perennial species that spreads through rhizomes, forming dense clumps over time.
