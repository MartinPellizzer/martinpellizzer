Ophrys × royanensis

Ophrys × royanensis, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant that results from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is characterized by its unique floral morphology, particularly its labellum, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. This orchid thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats. Its flowers are typically pale yellow to brown in color, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

The plant is a perennial herb with a rhizomatous root system, contributing to its resilience in various environmental conditions.
