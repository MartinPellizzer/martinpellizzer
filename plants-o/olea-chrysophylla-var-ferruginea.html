Olea chrysophylla var. ferruginea

Olea chrysophylla var. ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty-leaf olive, is a rare and lesser-known variant of the olive genus.

It is characterized by its distinctive rust-colored foliage, which sets it apart from other Olea species. Native to specific regions in the Mediterranean, this plant thrives in well-drained soils and mild climates. Its leaves are typically smaller and more densely arranged compared to the standard Olea chrysophylla.

While not widely cultivated, it is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological significance.
