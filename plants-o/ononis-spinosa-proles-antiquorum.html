Ononis spinosa proles antiquorum

Ononis spinosa proles antiquorum, commonly known as the ancient thimbleweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its small, spiny stems and trifoliate leaves, which are typical of many species in its genus. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, followed by pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed or arid environments.

This species is noted for its historical association with ancient civilizations, which is reflected in its common name.
