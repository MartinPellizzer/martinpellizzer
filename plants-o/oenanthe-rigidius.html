Oenanthe rigidius

Oenanthe rigidius, commonly known as the rigid water dropwort, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils along stream banks and wet meadows. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its small, greenish-white flowers are arranged in dense, umbrella-like clusters called umbels.

Oenanthe rigidius is characterized by its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
