Oligogyne synedrelloides

Oligogyne synedrelloides, commonly known as the narrow-leafed daisies, is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

It typically features small, daisy-like blooms with yellow central disks and white to pale pink ray florets. The plant is native to southern Africa and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun. It has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Oligogyne synedrelloides is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
