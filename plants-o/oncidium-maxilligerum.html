Oncidium maxilligerum

Oncidium maxilligerum, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a tropical orchid species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, fragrant inflorescences that bear clusters of yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are strap-shaped and evergreen, providing a lush backdrop to its vibrant blooms.

Oncidium maxilligerum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
