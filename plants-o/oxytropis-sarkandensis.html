Oxytropis sarkandensis

Oxytropis sarkandensis, commonly known as the Sarakand locoweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often in rocky or sandy soils. It is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, which helps improve soil fertility.

Oxytropis sarkandensis is also noted for containing toxic alkaloids, which can be harmful to livestock.
