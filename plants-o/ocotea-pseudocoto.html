Ocotea pseudocoto

Ocotea pseudocoto, commonly known as the false nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and bay laurel. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruits are drupes, containing a single seed, and the plant is valued for its aromatic oils and potential medicinal properties.

Ocotea pseudocoto thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
