Ophrys epirotica

Ophrys epirotica, commonly known as the Epirus bee orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Greece and Albania.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories or on rocky slopes. Its flowers are usually a mix of brown and yellow, with intricate patterns that enhance the mimicry.

Ophrys epirotica is a rare and protected species, highlighting its ecological significance in its native habitats.
