Ononis natrix var. tomentosa

Ononis natrix var. tomentosa, commonly known as the hairy bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial species characterized by its hairy, creeping stems and small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, disturbed habitats. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic in shape, and covered in fine, soft hairs.

The hairy bedstraw is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
