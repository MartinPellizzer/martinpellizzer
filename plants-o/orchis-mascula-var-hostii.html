Orchis mascula var. hostii

Orchis mascula var. hostii, commonly known as the Host's male orchid, is a rare and distinctive orchid species native to parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking violet or purple flowers with a distinctive hooded lip. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, and its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a protected species in several countries.
