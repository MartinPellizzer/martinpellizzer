Orchis basifoliata

Orchis basifoliata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features a basal rosette of lanceolate leaves and produces tall, erect flowering stems with a single spike of violet or purple flowers. Its flowers have a distinctive three-lobed lip and are pollinated by various insect species.

The plant is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its historical use in traditional medicine.
