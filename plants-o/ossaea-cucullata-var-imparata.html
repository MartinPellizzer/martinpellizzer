Ossaea cucullata var. imparata

Ossaea cucullata var. imparata, commonly known as the flat-leaf lichen, is a species of lichen belonging to the family Pilocarpaceae.

It is characterized by its foliose thallus, which is typically flattened and leaf-like in appearance. The plant forms a symbiotic relationship between a green alga and a fungus, which allows it to thrive in moist, shaded environments. It is often found on tree trunks and rocky surfaces in temperate regions.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as an indicator species for environmental conditions and its ecological contribution to forest ecosystems.
