Ochna gilgiana

Ochna gilgiana, commonly known as the Natal yellowwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and opposite, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing one or two seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky habitats, making it a resilient species in its native range.
