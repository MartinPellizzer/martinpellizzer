Onobroma caeruleum

Onobroma caeruleum, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are often covered in fine, white hairs.

Onobroma caeruleum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
