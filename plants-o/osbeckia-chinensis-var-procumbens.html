Osbeckia chinensis var. procumbens

Osbeckia chinensis var. procumbens, commonly known as creeping osbeckia, is a low-growing perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features prostrate stems that spread along the ground, often rooting at the nodes. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of yellow or green, typically arranged in clusters. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with hairy surfaces, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value or used in traditional medicine.
