Ornithogalum erythraeum

Ornithogalum erythraeum, commonly known as the red-flowered star of Bethlehem, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features striking, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red, typically blooming in spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a cluster of small, white or yellow flowers at the top of its stem. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine.

Its bulbs contain toxic compounds, so it should be handled with care.
