Ophrys × piconei

Ophrys × piconei, commonly known as the Picone's bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, bee-like flowers that feature a distinctive yellow face with a black beard, mimicking the appearance of a bee to attract pollinators. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing nectar that lures male bees for pollination.

Ophrys × piconei is a rare and protected species, often found in specific microhabitats within its native range.
