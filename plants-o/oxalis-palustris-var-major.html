Oxalis palustris var. major

Oxalis palustris var. major, commonly known as the marsh wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland environments in the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped lobes and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along creek banks. Its botanical characteristics include slender stems and a fibrous root system adapted to waterlogged conditions.

Oxalis palustris var. major is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
