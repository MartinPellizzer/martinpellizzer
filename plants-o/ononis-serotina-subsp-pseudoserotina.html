Ononis serotina subsp. pseudoserotina

Ononis serotina subsp.

pseudoserotina, commonly known as the late yellow sand-verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or arid environments. Its small, clustered flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is an important component of coastal vegetation, helping to stabilize dunes and prevent erosion.
