Oryza sativa var. paraphilippensis

Oryza sativa var. paraphilippensis, commonly known as the Philippine rice variety, is a subspecies of rice native to Southeast Asia, particularly the Philippines.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and small, rice-like grains. This variety typically thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and ample rainfall. Its botanical features include long, narrow leaves and a short, fibrous stem.

It is an important crop for local agriculture and holds cultural significance in the regions where it is cultivated.
