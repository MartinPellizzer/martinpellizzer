Ochna baronii

Ochna baronii, commonly known as the Baron's ochna, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its bark is often smooth and fissured. Ochna baronii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral displays.
