Oenothera hartwegii var. toumeyi

Oenothera hartwegii var. toumeyi, commonly known as the Hartweg's evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native range.
