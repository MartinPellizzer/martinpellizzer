Ocotea japonica

Ocotea japonica, commonly known as Japanese ocotea, is a small evergreen tree native to Japan and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Ocotea japonica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is valued in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.
