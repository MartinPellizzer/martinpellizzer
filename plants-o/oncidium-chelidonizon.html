Oncidium chelidonizon

Oncidium chelidonizon, commonly known as the golden spike orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil. The plant produces long, arching inflorescences bearing numerous yellow to golden-orange flowers with a distinctive spurred lip. Its foliage consists of broad, strap-shaped leaves that are often used in horticultural displays.

Oncidium chelidonizon is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under proper conditions.
