Orchis morio var. flava

Orchis morio var. flava, commonly known as the yellow marsh orchid, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a greenish throat. The plant typically grows in wet meadows and marshy areas, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are grass-like and arranged in a rosette, while the stem is usually unbranched and supports the floral display.

The yellow marsh orchid is a perennial species, often found in temperate climates with adequate sunlight.
