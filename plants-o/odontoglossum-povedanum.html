Odontoglossum povedanum

Odontoglossum povedanum, commonly known as the Poveda's orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers with a unique coloration pattern, often featuring a mix of white, violet, and green hues. Its pseudobulbs are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native high-altitude environment.

Odontoglossum povedanum is appreciated by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
