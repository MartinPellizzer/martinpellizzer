Oncidium ampliatum

Oncidium ampliatum, commonly known as the broad-leafed orchid, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in late summer and can last for several weeks, adding ornamental value to gardens.

Oncidium ampliatum requires well-draining soil and moderate watering to thrive.
