Ononis biflora var. hispalensis

Ononis biflora var. hispalensis, commonly known as the Hesperian sand-verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its slender stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring, producing pink or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal dunes or dry grasslands.

Its botanical features make it well-adapted to arid and coastal environments.
