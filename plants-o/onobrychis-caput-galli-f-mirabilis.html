Onobrychis caput-galli f. mirabilis

Onobrychis caput-galli f. mirabilis, commonly known as the "flowering burr medic," is a variant of the burr medic species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a leguminous plant characterized by its small, yellow pea-like flowers and compound leaves with stipules. The plant produces small, hairy pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or agricultural areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for soil enrichment and as a forage crop.
