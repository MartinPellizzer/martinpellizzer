Opuntia balearica

Opuntia balearica, commonly known as the Balearic prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the Balearic Islands.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems that are covered in spines and glochids. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers during the summer months, followed by edible fruits known as tunas. Opuntia balearica is adapted to arid environments and thrives in full sun.

Its structure and growth habits make it a distinctive and resilient member of the Cactaceae family.
