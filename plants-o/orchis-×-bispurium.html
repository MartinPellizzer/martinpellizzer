Orchis × bispurium

Orchis × bispurium, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a natural hybrid between Orchis morio and Orchis mascula.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, typically found in moist meadows and woodlands. The plant produces showy, violet or purple flowers with a distinctive three-lobed lip. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils, while its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
