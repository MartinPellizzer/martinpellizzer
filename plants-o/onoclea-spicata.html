Onoclea spicata

Onoclea spicata, commonly known as the field horsetail, is a perennial plant belonging to the Equisetaceae family.

It is characterized by its segmented, jointed stems and a distinctive spore-bearing structure called a strobilus. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it lacks true leaves and roots.

Onoclea spicata plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various invertebrates.
