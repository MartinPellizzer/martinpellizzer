Odontarrhena microphylla

Odontarrhena microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved sandmat, is a low-growing shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It has small, narrow leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch to its otherwise desolate habitat. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access moisture from deeper soil layers.

Odontarrhena microphylla is well adapted to sandy soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas where other plants struggle to survive.
