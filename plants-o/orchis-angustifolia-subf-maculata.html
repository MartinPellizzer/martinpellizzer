Orchis angustifolia subf. maculata

Orchis angustifolia subf.

maculata, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive spotted leaves and flowers. It is a terrestrial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

The plant produces tall, slender stems that bear a cluster of pale yellow or greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are narrow and often have dark purple or brown spots, which help in identifying the species.

This orchid is found across Europe and parts of Asia, and it plays an important role in its ecosystem as a food source for various pollinators.
