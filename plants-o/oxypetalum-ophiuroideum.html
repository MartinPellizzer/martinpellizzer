Oxypetalum ophiuroideum

Oxypetalum ophiuroideum, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in dense clusters, creating a visually dramatic display. The plant has a woody stem with a distinctive, branching pattern that resembles coral, hence its common name. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a velvety texture.

Oxypetalum ophiuroideum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
