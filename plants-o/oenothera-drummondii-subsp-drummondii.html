Oenothera drummondii subsp. drummondii

Oenothera drummondii subsp.

drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's evening primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southwestern United States. It features showy yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

The plant has linear, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats like prairies and rocky slopes.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and conservation efforts.
