Oenothera venosa

Oenothera venosa, commonly known as the veined evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening, hence its common name. The plant has hairy, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oval seeds.

Oenothera venosa is often found in dry, disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
