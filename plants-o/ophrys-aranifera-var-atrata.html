Ophrys aranifera var. atrata

Ophrys aranifera var. atrata, commonly known as the Black Orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black petals and sepals, which give it its distinctive appearance. The plant is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its flowers are highly specialized, often mimicking the appearance of female wasps to attract male pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern in many areas.
