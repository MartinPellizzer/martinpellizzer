Ochroxylum punctatum

Ochroxylum punctatum, commonly known as the punctate ochroxylum, is a herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers clustered in dense, terminal inflorescences. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Ochroxylum punctatum is known for its ecological importance as a food source for various insects and wildlife.
