Odontarrhena subalpina

Odontarrhena subalpina, commonly known as the alpine toothroot, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and rocky substrates. The plant features a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a loose inflorescence. It is adapted to cold climates and can survive in harsh, high-altitude conditions.

Odontarrhena subalpina is often found in meadows and slopes at elevations above 1,500 meters.
