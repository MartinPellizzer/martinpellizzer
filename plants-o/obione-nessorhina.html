Obione nessorhina

Obione nessorhina, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems covered in dense, hairy foliage and produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy or loamy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Obione nessorhina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
