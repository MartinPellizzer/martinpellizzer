Ononis hircina

Ononis hircina, commonly known as sheep's-bit, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, with a distinctive spurred structure. Its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they consist of three leaflets, and are often hairy.

Ononis hircina is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in dry, sandy soils.
