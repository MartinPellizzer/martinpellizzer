Ophrys apifera var. muteliae

Ophrys apifera var. muteliae, commonly known as the bee orchid, is a species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive flower morphology, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats. Its flowers are usually yellow with brown markings, and the leaves are narrow and grass-like.

This orchid is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its specialized pollination mechanism.
