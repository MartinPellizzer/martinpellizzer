Ossaea araneifera

Ossaea araneifera, commonly known as the spider plant, is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters, featuring a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, which are often borne on long, slender stalks. Ossaea araneifera is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

It is also known for its ability to propagate through offsets, which makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
