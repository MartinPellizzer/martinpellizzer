Odontophyllum huilensis

Odontophyllum huilensis, commonly known as the Huiltepec orchid, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in cloud forests. Its flowers are usually brightly colored, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
