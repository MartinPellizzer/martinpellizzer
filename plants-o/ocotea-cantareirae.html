Ocotea cantareirae

Ocotea cantareirae, commonly known as the Cantareira ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments and can reach heights of up to 20 meters. Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in traditional craftsmanship.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a threatened species in its native range.
