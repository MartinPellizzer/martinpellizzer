Onosma echioides var. hispida

Onosma echioides var. hispida, commonly known as the hairy onosma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
