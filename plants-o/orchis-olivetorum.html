Orchis olivetorum

Orchis olivetorum, commonly known as the olive orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers with a distinctive lip petal, typical of many orchid species. Its roots contain alkaloids, which have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

Orchis olivetorum is often found in open woodlands and scrublands, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
