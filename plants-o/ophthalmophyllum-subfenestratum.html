Ophthalmophyllum subfenestratum

Ophthalmophyllum subfenestratum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved eye-plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and often have a distinctive pattern of small, window-like openings, which is reflected in its scientific name.

It is a perennial herb that thrives in full sun and is often found in fynbos vegetation.
