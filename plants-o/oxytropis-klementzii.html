Oxytropis klementzii

Oxytropis klementzii, commonly known as the Great Basin locoweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with multiple pairs of leaflets and is often found in dry, rocky habitats. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

Oxytropis klementzii is also known for its toxic properties, which can affect livestock that consume it.
