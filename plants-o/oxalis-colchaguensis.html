Oxalis colchaguensis

Oxalis colchaguensis, commonly known as the Chilean wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive orange-red berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by birds. Oxalis colchaguensis is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
