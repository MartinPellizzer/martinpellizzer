Odontoglossum insleayi var. aureum

Odontoglossum insleayi var. aureum, commonly known as the golden odontoglossum, is a hybrid orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow and green foliage and produces showy, fragrant flowers with a distinctive golden hue. The plant typically grows in high-altitude cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its pseudobulbs are short and stout, supporting its evergreen leaves, while its inflorescence bears numerous small, clustered flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
