Olea bournei

Olea bournei, commonly known as the Bornean olive, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the island of Borneo.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like the olive and jasmine. The tree is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by small, fleshy fruits.

Olea bournei is often found in lowland and hill forest areas, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates.
