Oxytropis saurica

Oxytropis saurica, commonly known as desert locoweed, is a perennial legume native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in shades of purple or white. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.

Oxytropis saurica is also known for its toxic properties, which can affect livestock that consume it.
