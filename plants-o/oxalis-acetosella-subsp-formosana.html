Oxalis acetosella subsp. formosana

Oxalis acetosella subsp.

formosana, commonly known as the Formosan wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Taiwan. It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value and ecological significance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
