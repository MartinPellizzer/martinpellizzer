Ocotea angustifolia

Ocotea angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers that develop into dark purple berries. Its aromatic wood is valued for its use in furniture and as a source of essential oils.

Ocotea angustifolia thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.
