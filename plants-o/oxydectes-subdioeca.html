Oxydectes subdioeca

Oxydectes subdioeca, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Oxydectes subdioeca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
