Oryza sativa var. sickenbergeri

Oryza sativa var. sickenbergeri, commonly known as the Sickenberger rice, is a rare and ancient variety of rice native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant grains and is typically grown in wetland environments. This variety is notable for its genetic diversity and is considered a valuable resource for rice breeding programs. Its botanical features include slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves.

Due to its rarity, it is often studied for its potential contributions to crop resilience and food security.
