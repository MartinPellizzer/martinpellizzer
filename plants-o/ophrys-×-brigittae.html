Ophrys × brigittae

Ophrys × brigittae, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant that belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera, the bee orchid, and Ophrys insectifera, the wasp orchid. The plant is characterized by its intricate and highly specialized floral structures that mimic the appearance of female bees or wasps, a phenomenon known as sexual deception. This mimicry plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy, attracting male insects that attempt to mate with the flower.

Ophrys × brigittae is typically found in rocky or disturbed soils in Mediterranean regions, where it thrives in sunny, well-drained environments.
