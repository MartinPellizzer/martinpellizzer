Ochna roseiflora

Ochna roseiflora, commonly known as the rose-flowered ochna, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its woody structure and showy, rose-like flowers. The plant typically features alternate, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. Its flowers are usually pink or red, with five petals, and it produces small, woody fruits.

Ochna roseiflora is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
