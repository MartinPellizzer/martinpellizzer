Opuntia labouretiana

Opuntia labouretiana, commonly known as the Mexican prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems that are covered in spines and glochids, which are small, barbed bristles. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by edible, fleshy fruits known as tunas. Opuntia labouretiana is adapted to arid environments and stores water in its thick, fleshy stems.

It plays an important role in both ecological and cultural contexts in its native habitat.
