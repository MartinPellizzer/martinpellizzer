Odontoglossum crocidipterum f. dormanianum

Odontoglossum crocidipterum f. dormanianum, commonly known as the Dormanian's orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated flower spikes that bear numerous showy, violet-blue flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree bark in humid, high-altitude environments. Its leaves are dark green, velvety, and strap-shaped, often forming a dense rosette.

This orchid is prized by horticulturists for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
