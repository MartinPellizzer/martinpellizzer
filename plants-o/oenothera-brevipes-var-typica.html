Oenothera brevipes var. typica

Oenothera brevipes var. typica, commonly known as the short-stemmed evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and showy yellow flowers that open in the evening. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, elliptical seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Oenothera brevipes var. typica is known for its ecological importance as a food source for various pollinators and seed-eating animals.
