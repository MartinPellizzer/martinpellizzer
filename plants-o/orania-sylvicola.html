Orania sylvicola

Orania sylvicola, commonly known as the forest orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rainforests and undergrowth. Its leaves are usually broad and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

The flowers come in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and purple, and are adapted for pollination by specific insect species.
