Oxytropis deflexa subsp. foliolosa

Oxytropis deflexa subsp.

foliolosa, commonly known as the bentleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, compound leaves that are often hairy.

The plant produces yellow to orange flowers in late spring to early summer, typically in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Oxytropis deflexa subsp.

foliolosa is adapted to arid environments and is often found in regions with well-drained soils.
