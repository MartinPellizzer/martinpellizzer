Oxalis bipartita f. linearifolia

Oxalis bipartita f. linearifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wood sorrel, is a variant of the species Oxalis bipartita.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically narrow and linear. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typical of the Oxalis genus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This cultivar is often cultivated for its ornamental value in shaded garden settings.
