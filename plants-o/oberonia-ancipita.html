Oberonia ancipita

Oberonia ancipita, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive, elongated labellum. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments such as forests and is often found in the understorey. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its unique floral structure.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its specific ecological requirements and limited distribution.
