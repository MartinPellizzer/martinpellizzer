Orthopogon abortivus

Orthopogon abortivus, commonly known as the short-stemmed grass.

It is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States, typically found in moist, sandy soils. The plant has slender, erect stems that are often branched and bear small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture.

Orthopogon abortivus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
