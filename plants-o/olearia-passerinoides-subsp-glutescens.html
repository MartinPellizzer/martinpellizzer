Olearia passerinoides subsp. glutescens

Olearia passerinoides subsp.

glutescens, commonly known as the New Zealand iceplant, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand. It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and subalpine regions, characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in heads, often appearing in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and its role in stabilizing coastal soils.
