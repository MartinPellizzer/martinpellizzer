Orchis adenocheila

Orchis adenocheila, commonly known as the purple marsh orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear a single spike of showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. Its flowers are typically arranged in a loose raceme and are pollinated by insects such as bees and flies.

Orchis adenocheila thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in peat bogs and damp meadows.
