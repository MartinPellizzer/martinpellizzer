Onosma ambigens subsp. ambigens

Onosma ambigens subsp.

ambigens, commonly known as the ambiguous sandthrift, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a low-growing perennial herb with narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from pale yellow to reddish-brown.

The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is distinguished by its subtle floral characteristics and adaptability to arid environments.
