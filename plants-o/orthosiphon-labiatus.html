Orthosiphon labiatus

Orthosiphon labiatus, commonly known as the Java mint or water hyssop, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple, arranged in whorled clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

Orthosiphon labiatus is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
