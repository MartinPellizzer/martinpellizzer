Obione glauca

Obione glauca, commonly known as the blue obione, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue-gray foliage and small, delicate flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its unique coloration and drought tolerance make it a popular choice among horticulturists.

However, it is relatively rare in the wild and requires careful cultivation to maintain its health and beauty.
