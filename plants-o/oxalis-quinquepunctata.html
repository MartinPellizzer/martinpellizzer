Oxalis quinquepunctata

Oxalis quinquepunctata, commonly known as the five-pointed wood sorrel, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with five prominent veins, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

Oxalis quinquepunctata is a low-growing perennial that spreads via rhizomes, forming dense mats in its native habitat.
