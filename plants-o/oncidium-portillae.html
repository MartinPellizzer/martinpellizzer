Oncidium portillae

Oncidium portillae, commonly known as the Portilla orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, long-lasting flowers that come in a variety of colors, including yellow, orange, and red. The plant has pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, supporting its growth in seasonal environments. Its leaves are typically broad and grass-like, and it thrives in bright, indirect light with moderate humidity.

Oncidium portillae is popular among orchid growers for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
