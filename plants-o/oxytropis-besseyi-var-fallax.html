Oxytropis besseyi var. fallax

Oxytropis besseyi var. fallax, commonly known as the fallax milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, palmately compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in racemes. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe habitats.

This species is valued for its role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
