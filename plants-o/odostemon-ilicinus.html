Odostemon ilicinus

Odostemon ilicinus, commonly known as the Mexican balm or Mexican mint, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, white to purple flowers. Its aromatic leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their soothing properties.

Odostemon ilicinus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
