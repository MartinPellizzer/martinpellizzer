Ouratea kananariensis

Ouratea kananariensis, commonly known as the Kananari beauty or the Brazilian starwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and dense, rounded crown. The plant produces small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in shaded environments, making it suitable for understory planting in tropical landscapes.
