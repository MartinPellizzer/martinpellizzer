Onopordum eriocephalum

Onopordum eriocephalum, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant features a thick, woody stem and hairy leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its showy flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.
