Ophrys × metellae

Ophrys × metellae, commonly known as the bee orchid hybrid, is a fascinating plant with intricate botanical features.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral morphology, which closely resembles a female bee, aiding in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and heaths, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually a mix of brown, yellow, and white, with a distinct "face" that mimics the appearance of a bee.

Ophrys × metellae is a sterile hybrid, often resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera, and is notable for its specialized pollination mechanism involving male bees.
