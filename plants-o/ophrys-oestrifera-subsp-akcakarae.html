akcakarae, commonly known as the Akcakara Orchid, is a rare and endemic orchid species found in specific regions of Turkey. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral morphology, including a specialized lip that mimics female insects to attract male pollinators.

The plant typically grows in rocky or forested habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, producing intricate, often colorful flowers.
