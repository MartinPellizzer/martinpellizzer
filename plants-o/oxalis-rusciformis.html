Oxalis rusciformis

Oxalis rusciformis, commonly known as the coralroot oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and woodland areas. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink or red. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a distinctive reddish or purple tint.

Oxalis rusciformis is notable for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and is often used in ornamental gardening.
