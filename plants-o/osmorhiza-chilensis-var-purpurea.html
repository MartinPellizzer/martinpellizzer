Osmorhiza chilensis var. purpurea

Osmorhiza chilensis var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple sweetroot, is a perennial herb native to southern Chile.

It belongs to theApiaceae family, characterized by its umbellate inflorescences and aromatic foliage. The plant features a thick, fleshy root system that is often harvested for its edible and medicinal properties. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and white or purplish in color.

This variety is notable for its distinctive purple tinge in its inflorescences and its adaptability to temperate climates.
