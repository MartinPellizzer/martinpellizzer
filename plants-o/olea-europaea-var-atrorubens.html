Olea europaea var. atrorubens

Olea europaea var. atrorubens, commonly known as the red-leaf olive, is a variant of the common olive tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is distinguished by its striking reddish foliage, which becomes more prominent in autumn. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible drupes that are typically used for oil extraction. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, sunny climate.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its unique leaf color.
