Oenostachys abyssinica

Oenostachys abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian grass, is a perennial grass species native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Oenostachys abyssinica plays a role in local ecosystems and is sometimes used for traditional purposes.
