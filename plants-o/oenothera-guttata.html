Oenothera guttata

Oenothera guttata, commonly known as the spotted evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairies and disturbed areas. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually unbranched and somewhat woody at the base.

Oenothera guttata is also known for its ability to produce a large number of seeds, contributing to its success in various habitats.
