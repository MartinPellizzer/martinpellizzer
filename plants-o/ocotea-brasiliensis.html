Ocotea brasiliensis

Ocotea brasiliensis, commonly known as Brazilian camphor tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Peru. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are elliptical and have a glossy surface. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by dark blue, spherical berries.

The tree is valued for its aromatic wood and has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of essential oils.
