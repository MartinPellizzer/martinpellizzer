Oxalis corniculata subsp. albicans

Oxalis corniculata subsp.

albicans, commonly known as the white wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to Asia and parts of Africa. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky areas.

The plant features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive white or pale coloration, and small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

The species is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and ease of cultivation.
