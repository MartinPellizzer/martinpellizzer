Oenanthe peucedanifolia var. media

Oenanthe peucedanifolia var. media, commonly known as the common cow-wheat, is a biennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that are often branched and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from June to August. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with a distinctive, fern-like appearance.

It prefers moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.
