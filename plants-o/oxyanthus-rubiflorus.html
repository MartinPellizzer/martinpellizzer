Oxyanthus rubiflorus

Oxyanthus rubiflorus, commonly known as the red-flowered oxyanthus, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers, which are typically borne in clusters along the stems. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in moist, shaded areas within rainforests.

Oxyanthus rubiflorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
