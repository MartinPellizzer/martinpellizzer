Oberholzeria etendekaensis

Oberholzeria etendekaensis, commonly known as the Etendeka oberholzeria, is a rare and endemic plant species native to the Namib Desert in Namibia.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its fleshy, spiny stems and small, pale green leaves that are adapted to store water. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers during the brief rainy season, which is a critical time for its survival in the arid environment. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it an important subject in botanical research on desert flora.

Oberholzeria etendekaensis is also considered a flagship species for conservation efforts in the region.
