Ocotea perforata

Ocotea perforata, commonly known as the perforate ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, lowland forests and can reach heights of up to 20 meters. Its leaves are elliptical with a distinctive perforation along the midrib, giving it its common name.

The tree produces small, dark purple berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species.
