Ostrya italica

Ostrya italica, commonly known as the Italian hop-hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are alternate and often have a serrated margin. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in catkins, followed by small, winged fruits that are dispersed by wind. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Ostrya italica is valued for its hardy nature and ornamental appeal in landscaping.
