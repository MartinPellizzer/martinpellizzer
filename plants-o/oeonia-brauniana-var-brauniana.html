Oeonia brauniana var. brauniana

Oeonia brauniana var. brauniana, commonly known as the Braun's ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly rough texture. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded or partially shaded areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
