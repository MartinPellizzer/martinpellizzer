Oxalis dines

Oxalis dines, commonly known as the yellow woodsorrel, is a flowering plant belonging to the Oxalidaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of five-petaled yellow flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional cuisine. Native to tropical regions, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
