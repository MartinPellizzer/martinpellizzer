Ophrys holosericea psp. exaltata

Ophrys holosericea psp.

exaltata, commonly known as the exalted bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species found in specific alpine habitats. It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination.

The plant produces a single, showy flower with a lip that resembles a bee's body, a feature that plays a crucial role in its reproductive strategy. It thrives in nutrient-poor, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered vulnerable to habitat loss and environmental changes.
