Orthosiphon suffrutescens

Orthosiphon suffrutescens, commonly known as Java tea or Malabar nettle, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in wetlands and along riverbanks.

Orthosiphon suffrutescens is also valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
