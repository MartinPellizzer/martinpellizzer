Ophrys muscifera var. bombifera

Ophrys muscifera var. bombifera, commonly known as the Bombifera bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry. It thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species.
