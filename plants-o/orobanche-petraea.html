Orobanche petraea

Orobanche petraea, commonly known as the desert broom.

It is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on its host plants for nutrients. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and is typically found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its roots form specialized structures called haustoria, which penetrate the roots of host plants to extract water and nutrients.

Due to its parasitic nature, it plays a unique role in certain ecosystems, though it can be a concern for agricultural crops.
