Ophrys × tarentina

Ophrys × tarentina, commonly known as the tarentine orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the genus Ophrys, which is known for its orchids that mimic the appearance of female insects to attract male pollinators. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats and produces showy, insect-like flowers with a distinct coloration and scent. Its inflorescence consists of multiple flowers arranged in a spike, with the lower flowers often appearing as male insects.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
