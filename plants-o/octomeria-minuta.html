Octomeria minuta

Octomeria minuta, commonly known as the dwarf octomeria, is a small, flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
