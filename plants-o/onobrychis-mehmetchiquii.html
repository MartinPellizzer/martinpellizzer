Onobrychis mehmetchiquii

Onobrychis mehmetchiquii, commonly known as the Mehmetchiqui clover, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing a robust root system for water retention. It plays a role in soil stabilization and nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with rhizobia bacteria.

Its hardy nature makes it a valuable species for ecological restoration projects in challenging terrains.
