Ocotea depauperata

Ocotea depauperata, commonly known as the slender ocotea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocado, and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect trunk and dense, dark green foliage. Its leaves are typically elliptical, leathery, and have a pleasant aromatic scent.

Ocotea depauperata is often found in shaded understory environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
