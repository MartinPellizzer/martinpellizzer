Oxalis versicolor var. latifolia

Oxalis versicolor var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink, white, and yellow. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas. It has a shallow root system and is adapted to partial to full shade.

Its foliage and flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
