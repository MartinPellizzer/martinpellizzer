Onira

Onira, commonly known as the "false lily," is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the genus Caladium, which includes several species known for their vibrant, heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 3 feet, with clusters of small, white or pink flowers. Its foliage is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking coloration.

Onira thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade in its natural habitat.
