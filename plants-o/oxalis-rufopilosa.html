Oxalis rufopilosa

Oxalis rufopilosa, commonly known as the red-veined wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial native to South Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with prominent red veins, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Oxalis rufopilosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to add color to shady garden areas.
