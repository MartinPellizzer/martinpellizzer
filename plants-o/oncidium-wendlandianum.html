Oncidium wendlandianum

Oncidium wendlandianum, commonly known as the Wendland's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features a pseudobulb with a single, fleshy leaf and produces a branched inflorescence with numerous showy, fragrant flowers. The flowers are typically yellow with brown or purple markings, and they bloom in the spring or early summer. This orchid prefers well-draining soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant, long-lasting blooms and ease of cultivation.
