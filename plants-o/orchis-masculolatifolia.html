Orchis masculolatifolia

Orchis masculolatifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved male orchid, is a species of orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, grass-like leaves and tall flowering stems that bear clusters of pale yellow to greenish flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, and its flowers are often visited by a variety of pollinators. Orchis masculolatifolia is a perennial herb that thrives in moist, calcareous soils, often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological studies and horticultural interest.
