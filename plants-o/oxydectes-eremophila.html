Oxydectes eremophila

Oxydectes eremophila, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a perennial flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, storing water in its thick stems and leaves. It produces small, wind-dispersed seeds that allow it to colonize new areas effectively.

Oxydectes eremophila plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing food for local wildlife in its native habitat.
