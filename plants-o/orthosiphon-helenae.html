Orthosiphon helenae

Orthosiphon helenae, commonly known as Helen's holy basil, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is often grown for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal medicine.

The leaves are aromatic and can be used to make teas or as a spice in cooking.
