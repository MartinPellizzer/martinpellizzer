Oxytropis linczevskii

Oxytropis linczevskii, commonly known as the Linczevsky's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in steppes and rocky soils.

Oxytropis linczevskii is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it ecologically significant in its native habitat.
