Oxalis antucensis

Oxalis antucensis, commonly known as the antuco oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its foliage is often adorned with distinctive patterns and can vary in color depending on environmental conditions.

Oxalis antucensis is appreciated for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
