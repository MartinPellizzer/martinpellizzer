Ornithogalum concordianum

Ornithogalum concordianum, commonly known as the Cape bell or concordian bellflower, is a bulbous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale blue flowers. The plant is known for its striking appearance and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique addition to both wild and cultivated landscapes.
