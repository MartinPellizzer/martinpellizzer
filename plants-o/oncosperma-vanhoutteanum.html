Oncosperma vanhoutteanum

Oncosperma vanhoutteanum, commonly known as the Van Houtte's palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, coconut-like fruits. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.

The species is also valued for its potential in sustainable agriculture and ecological restoration projects.
