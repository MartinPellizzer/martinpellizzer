Oenothera brevipes var. scapoidea

Oenothera brevipes var. scapoidea, commonly known as the narrowleaf evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It features slender, erect stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its flowers have four petals and are typically borne in clusters at the tips of the stems.

The plant is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system to access water.
