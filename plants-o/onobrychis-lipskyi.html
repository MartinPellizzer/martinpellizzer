Onobrychis lipskyi

Onobrychis lipskyi, commonly known as the narrow-leafed clover, is a perennial legume native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are narrow and often have a grayish-green hue. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Onobrychis lipskyi is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural and ecological restoration settings.
