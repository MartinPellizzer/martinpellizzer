Oxydectes hemiargyrea

Oxydectes hemiargyrea, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-gray, fuzzy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy white seed heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or xeriscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in gardens across the United States.
