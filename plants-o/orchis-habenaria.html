Orchis habenaria

Orchis habenaria, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodlands and grasslands. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces tall flowering stems with showy, violet-blue flowers. Its flowers are characterized by a distinctive hooded labellum and are pollinated by insects such as bees and wasps.

Orchis habenaria is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
