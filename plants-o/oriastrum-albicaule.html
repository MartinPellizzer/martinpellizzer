Oriastrum albicaule

Oriastrum albicaule, commonly known as the white-stemmed globemallow, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and clusters of small, showy yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Oriastrum albicaule is often found in dry slopes, rocky areas, and disturbed sites.
