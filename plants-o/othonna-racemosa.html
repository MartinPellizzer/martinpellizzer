Othonna racemosa

Othonna racemosa, commonly known as the mountain onion or rock onion, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, bluish-green leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and rocky environments, showcasing a succulent structure to store water. Its flowering stems rise from the base of the rosette, often bearing a dense inflorescence of tubular flowers.

Othonna racemosa is valued in traditional medicine and is also cultivated as an ornamental plant for its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
