Octomeria exigua

Octomeria exigua, commonly known as the narrow-leaf octomeria, is a small, evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and appear in clusters during the winter months.

Octomeria exigua is valued for its hardiness and ornamental appeal in coastal and Mediterranean climates.
