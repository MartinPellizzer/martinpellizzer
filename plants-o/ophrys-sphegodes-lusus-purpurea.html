Ophrys sphegodes lusus purpurea

Ophrys sphegodes lusus purpurea, commonly known as the purple bee orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its intricate, bee-like floral structure, which features a vivid purple coloration and a prominent, fuzzy lower lip that mimics the appearance of a female bee. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is often found in woodland or heathland habitats. Its unique pollination mechanism relies on male bees attempting to mate with the flower, leading to effective pollen transfer.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in many regions.
