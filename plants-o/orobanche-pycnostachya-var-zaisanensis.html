Orobanche pycnostachya var. zaisanensis

Orobanche pycnostachya var. zaisanensis, commonly known as the Zaisan barrenstar, is a parasitic flowering plant belonging to the Orobanchaceae family.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients, typically parasitizing members of the legume family. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its habitat is primarily dry, open areas with well-drained soils, often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its specialized parasitic nature, it plays a unique role in its ecosystem, though it is rarely seen due to its inconspicuous appearance.
