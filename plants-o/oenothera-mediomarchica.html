Oenothera mediomarchica

Oenothera mediomarchica, commonly known as the mid-March evening primrose, is a species of flowering plant native to the central United States.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Oenothera mediomarchica is a herbaceous perennial with slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves.

It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairie and open woodland habitats.
