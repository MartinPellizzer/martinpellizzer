Oxalis tenuicalyculata var. hirsuta

Oxalis tenuicalyculata var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy oxalis, is a species native to South America.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with trifoliate leaves that have a distinctive hairy texture. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of yellow or orange, typically in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for woodland gardens and shaded rockeries.
