Oxalis valdiviensis var. humilis

Oxalis valdiviensis var. humilis, commonly known as the Valdivian wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in temperate rainforests. Its foliage resembles that of true sorrel, with heart-shaped leaves and a distinctive scent.

Oxalis valdiviensis var. humilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
