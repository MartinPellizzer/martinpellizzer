Osyris oblanceolata

Osyris oblanceolata, commonly known as the African waterleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the wet season. Osyris oblanceolata is typically found near water sources such as rivers and swamps, thriving in moist, sandy soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and traditional medicinal contexts.
