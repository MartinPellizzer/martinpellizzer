Oligosporus arenarius

Oligosporus arenarius, commonly known as the sand spore plant, is a rare and specialized species found in arid and semi-arid environments.

It belongs to the family Oligosporaceae and is characterized by its unique adaptations to survive in nutrient-poor, sandy soils. The plant produces small, spore-like structures that are dispersed by wind, enabling it to colonize new areas. Its root system is highly efficient at extracting moisture from the dry substrate.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a sensitive indicator of environmental change.
