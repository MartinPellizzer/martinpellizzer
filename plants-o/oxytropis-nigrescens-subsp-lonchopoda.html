Oxytropis nigrescens subsp. lonchopoda

Oxytropis nigrescens subsp.

lonchopoda, commonly known as the blackstem locoweed, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States and northern Mexico. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish flowers.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sagebrush steppe and rocky soils. It has compound leaves with multiple pairs of leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock.
