Origanum vulgare var. puberulum

Origanum vulgare var. puberulum, commonly known as rough oregano, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and Mediterranean climates. Its aromatic leaves are used in cooking and traditional medicine for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

This variety is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
