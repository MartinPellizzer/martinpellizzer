Oxycoccus intermedius

Oxycoccus intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate cranberry, is a species of shrub belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is native to northern regions of Europe and Asia, typically growing in acidic, nutrient-poor soils. The plant produces small, red berries that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Its leaves are evergreen, and the plant has a compact, bushy growth habit.

Oxycoccus intermedius is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
