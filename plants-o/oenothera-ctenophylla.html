Oenothera ctenophylla

Oenothera ctenophylla, commonly known as the silky evening primrose, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening. The plant belongs to the Onagraceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in arid and rocky environments. Its flowers are typically tubular with five petals and are pollinated by moths and other nocturnal insects.

Oenothera ctenophylla is also noted for its medicinal properties, though it is less commonly used than other evening primrose species.
