Ophioglossum pendulum

Ophioglossum pendulum, commonly known as the hanging adder's mouth, is a unique fern-like plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It features a distinctive, strap-shaped leaf that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces a spore-bearing structure called a sporangium, which is typically located at the tip of the leaf. Ophioglossum pendulum is known for its slow growth and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.
