Ophrys karadenizensis

Ophrys karadenizensis, commonly known as the Karadeniz Orchid, is a rare and endemic orchid species found in the northeastern regions of Turkey.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which includes a specialized lip that mimics the appearance of a female insect, aiding in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in rocky and forested areas, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pale green or brownish in color, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Ophrys karadenizensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, contributing to the biodiversity of the region.
