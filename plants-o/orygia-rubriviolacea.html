Orygia rubriviolacea

Orygia rubriviolacea, commonly known as the red viola, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant red petals and heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom in spring, adding a splash of color to gardens and natural landscapes.

Orygia rubriviolacea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.
