Ochyrorchis quartiniana

Ochyrorchis quartiniana, commonly known as the Quartin's spider-orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the wet tropical rainforests of Queensland, Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and distinctive, spider-like flowers that are often pale green or yellowish in color. The plant produces a single, elongated flower spike with several small, intricate blooms that resemble spiders, hence its common name. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found growing on the trunks of trees or in dense undergrowth.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Ochyrorchis quartiniana is considered a vulnerable species.
