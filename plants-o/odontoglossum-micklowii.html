Odontoglossum micklowii

Odontoglossum micklowii, commonly known as the Micklow's orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, spurred flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in high-altitude environments. Its leaves are strap-like and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Odontoglossum micklowii is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and relatively easy care requirements in cultivation.
