Oxalis cumulata

Oxalis cumulata, commonly known as the mountain wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped lobes and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a preference for full sun to partial shade.

Oxalis cumulata is often found in alpine and subalpine environments, contributing to the biodiversity of these ecosystems.
