Oryza ubanghensis

Oryza ubanghensis, commonly known as the Ubanghensis rice, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is closely related to other rice species. The plant typically grows in wetland environments and has narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that bear small, white flowers.

Oryza ubanghensis is of interest to botanists and agricultural researchers due to its potential for genetic diversity and adaptability to different environmental conditions.
