Odontostemma weissianum var. puberulum

Odontostemma weissianum var. puberulum, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields.

The species is noted for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
