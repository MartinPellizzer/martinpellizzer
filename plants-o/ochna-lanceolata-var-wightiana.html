Ochna lanceolata var. wightiana

Ochna lanceolata var. wightiana, commonly known as the Wight's ochna, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features elliptical to lance-shaped leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by orange-red, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage and seasonal blooms.
