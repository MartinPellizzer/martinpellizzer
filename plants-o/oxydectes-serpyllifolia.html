Oxydectes serpyllifolia

Oxydectes serpyllifolia, commonly known as common mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It features dense, woolly foliage with small, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces tiny, pale blue or white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Oxydectes serpyllifolia is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a characteristic species of moist meadows and hedgerows.
