Oenothera simulans

Oenothera simulans, commonly known as the mimic evening primrose, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that open in the evening and close by morning. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Oenothera simulans belongs to the Onagraceae family, which includes other evening primroses.

Its botanical features include hairy stems, lance-shaped leaves, and a long flowering period during the summer months.
