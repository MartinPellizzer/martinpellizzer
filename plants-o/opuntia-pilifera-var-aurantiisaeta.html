Opuntia pilifera var. aurantiisaeta

Opuntia pilifera var. aurantiisaeta, commonly known as the orange-hair prickly pear, is a cactus species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flattened, paddle-like stems covered in clusters of bright orange bristles. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible, red-fleshed fruits. Its succulent stems store water, enabling it to thrive in arid environments.

The variety is distinguished by its vibrant bristles and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
