Obbea timonioides

Obbea timonioides, commonly known as the Timonioides tamarind tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces yellowish flowers that are often clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, and it is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its hard wood and potential ecological benefits in its native habitat.
