Orchis angustifolia var. stricta

Orchis angustifolia var. stricta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with reddish hues. Its flowers are pale purple or white, arranged in a loose spike, and are characterized by their three-lobed petals.

This orchid is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in calcareous soils.
