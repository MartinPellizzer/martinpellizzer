Oenothera primiveris subsp. primiveris

Oenothera primiveris subsp.

primiveris, commonly known as the early evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America. It is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers that open in the late afternoon and close at night.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. It has hairy, linear leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oval seeds.

This species is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for nocturnal pollinators like moths.
