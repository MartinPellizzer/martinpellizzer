Orthosiphon rubicundus var. rubicundus

Orthosiphon rubicundus var. rubicundus, commonly known as the red-leaved holy basil, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that range from bright green to deep red in color. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.

This species is often used in traditional medicine for its purported diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.
