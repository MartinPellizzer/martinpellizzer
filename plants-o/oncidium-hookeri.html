Oncidium hookeri

Oncidium hookeri, commonly known as the yellow lady's slipper orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that resemble a lady's slipper, with a distinctive pouch-like structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrate.

Oncidium hookeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
