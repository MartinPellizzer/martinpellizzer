Oxytropis ircutensis

Oxytropis ircutensis, commonly known as the Central Asian locoweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan, Mongolia, and China. The plant is characterized by its spiny stems and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in shades of pink or purple. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in steppes and rocky soils.

Oxytropis ircutensis is also known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock.
