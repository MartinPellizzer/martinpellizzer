Oxalis debilis

Oxalis debilis, commonly known as the weeping oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, drooping inflorescences and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant.

Oxalis debilis is also known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it both ornamental and somewhat invasive in certain regions.
