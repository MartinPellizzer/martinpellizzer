Oligosporus virescens

Oligosporus virescens, commonly known as the green spore plant, is a unique species of algae characterized by its vibrant green coloration.

It belongs to the class Chlorophyceae and is known for its ability to thrive in both freshwater and brackish environments. The plant exhibits a filamentous structure with branching filaments that contain numerous chloroplasts, enabling efficient photosynthesis. Oligosporus virescens reproduces through the formation of zoospores, which are released into the water for dispersal.

Its ecological role includes contributing to nutrient cycling and serving as a food source for various aquatic organisms.
