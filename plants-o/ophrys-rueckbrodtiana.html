Ophrys rueckbrodtiana

Ophrys rueckbrodtiana, commonly known as the Rueckbrodt's orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its intricate, bee-like flower structure, which features a vividly colored lip that mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments with partial shade. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing a unique blend of colors including green, brown, and violet.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Ophrys rueckbrodtiana is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
