Oxalis comptonii

Oxalis comptonii, commonly known as Compton's wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal or mountainous regions. The plant features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers. It is part of the Oxalidaceae family, known for its calcium oxalate crystals in the leaves.

Oxalis comptonii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained conditions.
