Ornithogalum pyrenaicum subsp. flavescens

Ornithogalum pyrenaicum subsp.

flavescens, commonly known as the yellow star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, yellow flowers in the spring.

The plant is characterized by its fibrous root system and is often found in woodland edges and rocky soils. It is part of the Asparagaceae family and is known for its ornamental value.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered threatened in some regions.
