Ornithogalum samariae

Ornithogalum samariae, commonly known as the Palestine star-of-Bethleem, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a short, rhizomatous stem and produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers during the spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to grow in rocky or sandy soils. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as scrublands and woodlands.

Ornithogalum samariae is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
