Oligomeris resedella

Oligomeris resedella, commonly known as the small reedgrass, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that often form dense clumps. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth, waxy surface.

Small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the Poaceae family.
