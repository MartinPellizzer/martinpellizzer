Oenothera walkeri subsp. tortilis

Oenothera walkeri subsp.

tortilis, commonly known as the twisted evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its slender, twisted stems and yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in the evening.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of desert flora.
