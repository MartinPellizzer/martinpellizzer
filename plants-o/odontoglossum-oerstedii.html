Odontoglossum oerstedii

Odontoglossum oerstedii, commonly known as the Oersted's orchid, is a species native to the high altitudes of Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a sympodial growth habit, producing a pseudobulb that supports its foliage and flowering structure. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces pendulous, showy flowers with intricate patterns and a strong, spicy fragrance. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Odontoglossum oerstedii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
