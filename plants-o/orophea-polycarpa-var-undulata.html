Orophea polycarpa var. undulata

Orophea polycarpa var. undulata, commonly known as the wavy-seeded orophea, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its smooth, glossy leaves and clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, orange-yellow fruits that are edible but not widely consumed by humans. Its ornamental value lies in its attractive foliage and unique floral structure, making it a popular choice for horticultural collections.

The varietal name "undulata" refers to the wavy or undulating pattern on its seed pods.
