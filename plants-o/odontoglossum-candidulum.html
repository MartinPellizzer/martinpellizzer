Odontoglossum candidulum

Odontoglossum candidulum, commonly known as the white orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elegant, white petals and a distinctive, elongated lip that sets it apart from other orchid species. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial orchid, often found in shaded, humid environments. Its flowers bloom in late summer and early autumn, adding a striking visual element to its natural habitat.

Botanically, it is noted for its intricate floral structure and specific pollination mechanisms adapted to its ecosystem.
