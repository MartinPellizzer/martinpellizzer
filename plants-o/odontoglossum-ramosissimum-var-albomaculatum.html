Odontoglossum ramosissimum var. albomaculatum

Odontoglossum ramosissimum var. albomaculatum, commonly known as the white-spotted orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that typically feature white petals with distinctive red or orange spots. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its blooms are often fragrant and can last for several weeks, making it a prized addition to tropical gardens.

This variety is particularly noted for its striking coloration and is cultivated for its ornamental value.
