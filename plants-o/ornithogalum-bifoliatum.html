Ornithogalum bifoliatum

Ornithogalum bifoliatum, commonly known as the two-leaved star-of-Bethlehem, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features a single, upright stem that bears two broad, lance-shaped leaves at the base. The plant produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is popular among gardeners in temperate climates.
