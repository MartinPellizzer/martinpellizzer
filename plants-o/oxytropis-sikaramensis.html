Oxytropis sikaramensis

Oxytropis sikaramensis, commonly known as the Kashmir oxytropis, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas, particularly in regions of Jammu and Kashmir.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are trifoliate, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Oxytropis sikaramensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in alpine ecosystems.
