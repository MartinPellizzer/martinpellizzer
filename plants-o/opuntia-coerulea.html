Opuntia coerulea

Opuntia coerulea, commonly known as the blue prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in sharp spines and clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant produces edible fruits known as tunas, which are often used in local cuisines. Its succulent stems store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

Opuntia coerulea is also notable for its role in traditional medicine and as a food source for various wildlife species.
