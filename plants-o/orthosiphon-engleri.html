Orthosiphon engleri

Orthosiphon engleri, commonly known as Java mint or Malabar horse mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in spikes. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Orthosiphon engleri is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
