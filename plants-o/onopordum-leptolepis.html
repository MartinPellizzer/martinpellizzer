Onopordum leptolepis

Onopordum leptolepis, commonly known as the slender thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a robust taproot and upright, sparsely branched stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall. The plant produces large, showy purple to pink composite flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped, with rough, hairy surfaces and spiny margins.

Onopordum leptolepis is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky soils and along coastal areas.
