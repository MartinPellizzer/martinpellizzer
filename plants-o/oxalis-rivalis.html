Oxalis rivalis

Oxalis rivalis, commonly known as the false shamrock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features trifoliate leaves that resemble those of the true shamrock, with three heart-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

Oxalis rivalis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and delicate flowers.
