Oxalis floribunda

Oxalis floribunda, commonly known as the clustered wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive veins and produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its flowers bloom in spring, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Oxalis floribunda is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plants.
