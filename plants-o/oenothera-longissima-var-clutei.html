Oenothera longissima var. clutei

Oenothera longissima var. clutei, commonly known as the Clute's evening primrose, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening and close by dawn. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its stems are usually erect and covered with fine hairs, while its leaves are lance-shaped and also hairy.

The species is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
