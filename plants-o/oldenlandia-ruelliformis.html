Oldenlandia ruelliformis

Oldenlandia ruelliformis, commonly known as the creeping oxalis, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive veining pattern, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

Oldenlandia ruelliformis is also known for its ability to spread rapidly, making it both a useful ground cover and a potential weed.
