Ophelia tetrapetala var. yezo-alpina

Ophelia tetrapetala var. yezo-alpina, commonly known as the Yezo Alpine Water-plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of East Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils along streams and lakeshores. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating, with a distinctive waxy coating. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in aquatic ecosystems.
