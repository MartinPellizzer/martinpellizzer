Oleandra hovenkampii

Oleandra hovenkampii, commonly known as the Hovenkamp's oleander, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red, blooming during the warmer months. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged opposite each other on the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Oleandra hovenkampii is also noted for its moderate growth habit and ability to adapt to various environmental conditions.
