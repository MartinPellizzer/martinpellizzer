Oxalis ciliata

Oxalis ciliata, commonly known as the silky wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves with silky, ciliate margins that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Oxalis ciliata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and ease of care.
