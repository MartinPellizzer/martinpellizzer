Oenothera cordobensis

Oenothera cordobensis, commonly known as the Cordoba evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It belongs to the Onagraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Oenothera cordobensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
