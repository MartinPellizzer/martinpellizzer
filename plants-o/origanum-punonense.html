Origanum punonense

Origanum punonense, commonly known as the Philippine oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small, gray-green leaves and clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in forested areas. It is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects.

Its essential oils contain compounds like carvacrol and thymol, contributing to its strong aromatic profile.
