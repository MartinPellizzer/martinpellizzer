Odollam

Odollam, commonly known as the Indian chestnut or *Aesculus indica*, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Sapindaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, spire-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, red berries that contain toxic seeds, making it important to handle with care. Odollam thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
