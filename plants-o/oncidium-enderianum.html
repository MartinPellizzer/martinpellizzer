Oncidium enderianum

Oncidium enderianum, commonly known as the Enderian orchid, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its long, pendulous inflorescences that bear numerous fragrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Its flowers come in a range of colors, including white, yellow, and pink, often with intricate patterns.

Oncidium enderianum requires bright, indirect light and well-draining potting media to thrive.
