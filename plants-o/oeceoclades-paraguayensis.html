Oeceoclades paraguayensis

Oeceoclades paraguayensis, commonly known as the Paraguay orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to South America, particularly Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, greenish-white or yellowish flowers arranged in a spike, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Oeceoclades paraguayensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
