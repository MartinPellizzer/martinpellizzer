Otatea acuminata subsp. aztecorum

Otatea acuminata subsp.

aztecorum, commonly known as the Aztec bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae. It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and structural strength, it is cultivated in gardens and used in traditional construction in its native region.
