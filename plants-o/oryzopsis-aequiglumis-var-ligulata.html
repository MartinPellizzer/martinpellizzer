Oryzopsis aequiglumis var. ligulata

Oryzopsis aequiglumis var. ligulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ricegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are often glabrous and have a distinctive narrow, linear shape.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
