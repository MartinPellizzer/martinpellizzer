Ophrys aranifera var. rubriflora

Ophrys aranifera var. rubriflora, commonly known as the red-flowered bee orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bee-like flowers that have a vivid red coloration with intricate patterns resembling the body of a bee. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in high-altitude environments. Its flowers are specialized for pollination by bees, with the labellum mimicking the appearance of a female bee to attract male pollinators.

This orchid is highly sought after by botanists and conservationists due to its limited distribution and vulnerability to habitat loss.
