Oxytropis wologdensis var. subverticillata

Oxytropis wologdensis var. subverticillata, commonly known as the mountain locoweed, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is adapted to harsh, arid environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Due to its toxic properties, it is considered a noxious weed in some areas.
