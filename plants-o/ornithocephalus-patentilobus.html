Ornithocephalus patentilobus

Ornithocephalus patentilobus, commonly known as the broad-lobed orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with intricate petal structures. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories, where it thrives in partial shade and high humidity. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy surface to reduce water loss.

The species is noted for its unique floral morphology, which includes a distinctive labellum that aids in pollination by specific insect species.
