Oenothera ramulosa

Oenothera ramulosa, commonly known as the slender evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Oenothera ramulosa is a member of the Onagraceae family and is known for its ecological importance in supporting pollinator populations.
