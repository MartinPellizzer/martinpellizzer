Ocotea guianensis var. subsericea

Ocotea guianensis var. subsericea, commonly known as Guianese ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue-black berries. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils, which have been used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
