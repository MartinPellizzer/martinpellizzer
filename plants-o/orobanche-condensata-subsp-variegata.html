Orobanche condensata subsp. variegata

Orobanche condensata subsp.

variegata, commonly known as the variegated broomrape, is a root-parasitic plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients. It is native to regions of Europe and western Asia, often found growing on the roots of leguminous plants such as vetches and clovers.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, and its flowers are typically pollinated by insects. Its life cycle is closely tied to the growth patterns of its host, with seeds germinating in response to chemical signals released by the host roots.

Due to its parasitic nature and specific host requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized species within the Orobanchaceae family.
