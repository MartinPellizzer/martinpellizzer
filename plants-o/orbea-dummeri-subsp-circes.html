Orbea dummeri subsp. circes

Orbea dummeri subsp.

circes, commonly known as the Cape bell, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its cylindrical, branching stems and dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant thrives in arid environments and is adapted to store water in its fleshy stems. It produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white wax. Orbea dummeri subsp.

circes is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
