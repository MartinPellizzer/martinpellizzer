Obolaria caroliniana

Obolaria caroliniana, commonly known as the Carolina lily, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
