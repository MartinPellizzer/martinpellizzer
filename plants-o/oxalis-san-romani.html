Oxalis san-romani

Oxalis san-romani, commonly known as San Romani wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive veins and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

Oxalis san-romani is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
