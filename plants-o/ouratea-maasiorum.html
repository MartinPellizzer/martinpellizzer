Ouratea maasiorum

Ouratea maasiorum, commonly known as the Maasai ouratea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
