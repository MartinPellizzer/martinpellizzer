Oenothera fruticosa f. diversifolia

Oenothera fruticosa f. diversifolia, commonly known as the variegated evening primrose, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, branching stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that often display variegation. The plant produces yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its flowers are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils.
