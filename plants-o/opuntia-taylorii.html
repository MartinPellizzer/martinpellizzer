Opuntia taylorii

Opuntia taylorii, commonly known as the Taylor prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems that are covered in spines and glochids, which are tiny, hair-like structures that can cause irritation. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months, followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible. Opuntia taylorii is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick stems and having a shallow root system to absorb moisture efficiently.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in desert ecosystems.
