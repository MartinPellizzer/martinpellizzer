Ophelia cordata

Ophelia cordata, commonly known as broad-leaf water plantain, is a aquatic plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that float on the water surface or extend underwater. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Ophelia cordata is often used in water gardens and wetland restoration projects due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
