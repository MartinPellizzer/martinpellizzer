Otiophora parviflora

Otiophora parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered rock cress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features basal leaves that are pinnately divided, and its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Otiophora parviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
