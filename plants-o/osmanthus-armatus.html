Osmanthus armatus

Osmanthus armatus, commonly known as the fragrant flowered osmanthus, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant is valued for its aromatic oils, which are used in perfumes, food, and traditional Chinese medicine. Its ornamental value comes from its showy flowers and evergreen foliage.

Osmanthus armatus thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
