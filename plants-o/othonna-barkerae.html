Othonna barkerae

Othonna barkerae, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny, spherical fruit that resembles a small, green apple. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and clustered in heads.

The fruit is edible when ripe and is often used in jams and preserves.
