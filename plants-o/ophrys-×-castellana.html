Ophrys × castellana

Ophrys × castellana, commonly known as the Castellana orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, bee-like flowers that mimic female insects to aid in pollination. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brown in color, with a distinctive pouch-like structure at the base.

Ophrys × castellana is valued for its unique morphology and ecological role in supporting pollinator species.
