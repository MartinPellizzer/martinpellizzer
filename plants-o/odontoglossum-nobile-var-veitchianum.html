Odontoglossum nobile var. veitchianum

Odontoglossum nobile var. veitchianum, commonly known as the Veitch's orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often lavender or purple-colored flowers with intricate patterns and a distinctive, elongated lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree bark in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

This variety is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
