Ononis brachyantha

Ononis brachyantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in dry conditions.

Ononis brachyantha is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
