Oncidium × wattianum

Oncidium × wattianum, commonly known as the Wattiana Orchid, is a hybrid orchid species that exhibits a variety of floral forms and color variations.

It is native to regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant typically produces pendulous inflorescences with showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. Its pseudobulbs and strap-like leaves are characteristic of the Oncidium genus, and it requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Botanically, it is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation in both indoor and outdoor settings.
