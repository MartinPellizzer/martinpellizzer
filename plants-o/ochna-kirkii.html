Ochna kirkii

Ochna kirkii, commonly known as the Kirk's ochna, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal and rocky habitats. The plant produces striking yellow flowers with five petals, which are arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy coating.

Ochna kirkii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
