Odontoglossum cristatum var. argus

Odontoglossum cristatum var. argus, commonly known as the Argus orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking, colorful flowers with a distinctive pattern resembling the eyes of an argus. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine, white pubescence.

The flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, adding a vibrant splash of color to its natural habitat.
