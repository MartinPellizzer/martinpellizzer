Ocotea meziana

Ocotea meziana, commonly known as the Mexican ocotea, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments within tropical and subtropical forests. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and have a distinct, spicy scent when crushed.

Ocotea meziana is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
