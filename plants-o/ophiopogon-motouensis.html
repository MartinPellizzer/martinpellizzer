Ophiopogon motouensis

Ophiopogon motouensis, commonly known as the Motou bamboo-root grass, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its clumping growth habit and grass-like foliage. The plant features narrow, dark green leaves that are often hairy along the edges. It produces small, white to pale pink flowers in summer, followed by dark blue berries.

Ophiopogon motouensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
