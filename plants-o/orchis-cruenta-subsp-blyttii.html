Orchis cruenta subsp. blyttii

Orchis cruenta subsp.

blyttii, commonly known as the Blytt's early purple orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodlands and grasslands.

The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color. It blooms in early spring, often before the leaves are fully developed.

The species is part of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate floral structure and specialized pollination mechanisms.
