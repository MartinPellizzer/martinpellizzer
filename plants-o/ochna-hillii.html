Ochna hillii

Ochna hillii, commonly known as the African star-of-Bethlehem, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are elliptical and glossy, and its striking yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or orange when mature. Ochna hillii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
