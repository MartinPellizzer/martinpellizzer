Ozothamnus rodwayi var. kingii

Ozothamnus rodwayi var. kingii, commonly known as King's ozothamnus, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It features aromatic, silver-grey foliage with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant once established.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in coastal environments.
