Ocotea domatifera

Ocotea domatifera, commonly known as the Guajak tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in countries like Brazil, Colombia, and Peru. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage and aromatic leaves, which emit a distinct scent when crushed. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in axillary inflorescences, and it produces drupes that are often used in traditional medicine.

The tree is valued for its durable wood and ecological importance in its native habitats.
