Oenothera × velutirubata

Oenothera × velutirubata, commonly known as the velvetleaf evening primrose, is a hybrid perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange to yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with lance-shaped leaves that are softly hairy, giving it a velvety texture. Its stems are often reddish in color, and the flowers have a unique, fragrant scent.

Oenothera × velutirubata is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of light conditions, making it a common sight in prairies and along roadsides.
