Oxypetalum tomentosum var. tomentosum

Oxypetalum tomentosum var. tomentosum, commonly known as the woolly oxypetalum, is a member of the Rosaceae family.

It is a small shrub characterized by its dense, white, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers in the spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun exposure.

The plant is native to regions of South America, particularly in Brazil and Paraguay.
