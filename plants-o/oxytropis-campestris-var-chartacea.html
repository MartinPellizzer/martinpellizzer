Oxytropis campestris var. chartacea

Oxytropis campestris var. chartacea, commonly known as the silky field vetch, is a flowering plant native to dry, open habitats in the western United States.

It is a perennial legume characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish pea-like flowers arranged in dense clusters. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in poor soils and withstand drought conditions.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
