Opuntia vestita var. chuquisacana

Opuntia vestita var. chuquisacana, commonly known as the Chuquisaca prickly pear, is a cactus native to South America, particularly Argentina and Bolivia.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems that are covered in sharp spines and clusters of small, woolly hairs. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruits, known as tunas, are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor.

This species is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh climates.
