Ocotea glaucescens

Ocotea glaucescens, commonly known as the blue ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves that have a waxy, bluish sheen. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically white or pale yellow, and it produces dark blue to black berries.

Ocotea glaucescens is often found in humid lowland forests and is valued for its timber and essential oils.
