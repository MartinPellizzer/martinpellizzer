Oxytropis albovillosa

Oxytropis albovillosa, commonly known as white-hairy locoweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as rocky soils and sagebrush steppe. Its leaves are trifoliate, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Oxytropis albovillosa is also known for its toxicity to livestock, making it a significant concern in rangeland management.
