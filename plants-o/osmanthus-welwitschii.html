Osmanthus welwitschii

Osmanthus welwitschii, commonly known as the African sweet olive, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often releasing a sweet, honey-like scent. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and oval-shaped with a glossy surface.

Osmanthus welwitschii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its aromatic properties.
