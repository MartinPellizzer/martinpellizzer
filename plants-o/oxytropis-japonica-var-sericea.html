Oxytropis japonica var. sericea

Oxytropis japonica var. sericea, commonly known as Japanese milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, fern-like leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

This variety is noted for its silky, hairy foliage, which gives it its varietal name "sericea.".
