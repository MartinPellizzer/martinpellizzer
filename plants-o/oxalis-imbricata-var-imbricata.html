Oxalis imbricata var. imbricata

Oxalis imbricata var. imbricata, commonly known as the rock oxalis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped lobes and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats. It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated as a garden plant.

The species is also valued for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
