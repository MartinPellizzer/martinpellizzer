Olyra brasiliensis

Olyra brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian sycamore, is a large tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes figs and mulberries, and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and broad, palmate leaves. The tree produces small, clustered flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits that are edible for certain animals.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
