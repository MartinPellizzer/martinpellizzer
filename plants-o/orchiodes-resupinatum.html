Orchiodes resupinatum

Orchiodes resupinatum, commonly known as the Resupinate Orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique resupinate (upside-down) growth habit, where the flower is oriented downward. The plant typically produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts worldwide.
