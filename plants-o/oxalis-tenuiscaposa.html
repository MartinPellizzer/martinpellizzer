Oxalis tenuiscaposa

Oxalis tenuiscaposa, commonly known as the slender-stemmed wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is known for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Oxalis tenuiscaposa is also valued for its ability to tolerate drought conditions.
