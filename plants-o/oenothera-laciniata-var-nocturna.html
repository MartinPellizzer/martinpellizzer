Oenothera laciniata var. nocturna

Oenothera laciniata var. nocturna, commonly known as the night willow or evening glow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow to orange flowers that open in the late afternoon and remain open through the night. Its blooming period typically occurs in summer, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This species is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other moist habitats.
