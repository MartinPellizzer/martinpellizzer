Ophrys × morensis

Ophrys × morensis, commonly known as the Morenos hybrid orchid, is a natural hybrid between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is characterized by its intricate, bee-mimicking flowers that play a key role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is found in regions of southern Europe. Its flowers feature a complex structure with a labellum that resembles a female bee, attracting male bees for pollination.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological significance in its native habitat.
