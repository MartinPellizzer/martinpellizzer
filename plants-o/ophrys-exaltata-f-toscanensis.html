Ophrys exaltata f. toscanensis

Ophrys exaltata f. toscanensis, commonly known as the Tuscan Orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Italy.

It is characterized by its unique floral morphology, featuring a large, intricate lip that mimics the appearance of a female bee, a trait that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, often in open woodlands or grasslands. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with intricate patterns that aid in attracting specific pollinators.
