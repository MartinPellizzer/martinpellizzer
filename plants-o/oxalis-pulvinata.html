Oxalis pulvinata

Oxalis pulvinata, commonly known as the cushion oxalis, is a low-growing perennial plant native to South America.

It features dense clusters of small, heart-shaped leaves that give it a cushion-like appearance. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their delicate beauty.
