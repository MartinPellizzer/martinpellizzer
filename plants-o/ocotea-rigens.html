Ocotea rigens

Ocotea rigens, commonly known as the white cinnamom, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by oval, woody fruits containing a single seed. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils, which have been used traditionally for their medicinal properties.

The species is valued for its timber and aromatic qualities in both ecological and commercial contexts.
