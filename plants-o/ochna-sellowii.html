Ochna sellowii

Ochna sellowii, commonly known as the Sellow's ochna, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences, followed by elliptical, woody fruits that split open to release seeds.

Ochna sellowii is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
