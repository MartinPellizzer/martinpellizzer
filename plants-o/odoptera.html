Odoptera

Odoptera, commonly known as the grasshopper, is a group of insects belonging to the order Orthoptera.

These plants are not actually plants but insects, so there are no botanical aspects to discuss. However, they are often found in grassy or agricultural areas where their host plants provide food for their nymphs. Their life cycle involves incomplete metamorphosis, transitioning from egg to nymph to adult.

Odoptera species play a significant role in both ecological balance and agricultural ecosystems.
