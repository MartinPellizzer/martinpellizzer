Oenothera triangulata

Oenothera triangulata, commonly known as the triangular evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its triangular-shaped leaves and showy yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Oenothera triangulata is also known for its ability to attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
