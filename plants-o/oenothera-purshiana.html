Oenothera purshiana

Oenothera purshiana, commonly known as the yellow evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

Oenothera purshiana is also known for its medicinal properties, with its oils used in traditional and modern herbal remedies.
