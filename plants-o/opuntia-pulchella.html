Opuntia pulchella

Opuntia pulchella, commonly known as the showy prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems that are covered in sharp spines and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant produces edible fruits known as tunas, which are often consumed fresh or processed into beverages. Its succulent structure allows it to store water efficiently, adapting to arid environments.

Opuntia pulchella plays an important ecological role by providing food and shelter for various desert wildlife.
