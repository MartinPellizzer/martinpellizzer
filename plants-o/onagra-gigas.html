Onagra gigas

Onagra gigas, commonly known as hoary alyssum, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial plant with silver-gray foliage due to dense covering of fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes other mustard family plants.
