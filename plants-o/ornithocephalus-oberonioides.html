Ornithocephalus oberonioides

Ornithocephalus oberonioides, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, intricate floral structures. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are often brightly colored, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited habitat, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
