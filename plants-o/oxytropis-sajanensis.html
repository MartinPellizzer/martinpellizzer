Oxytropis sajanensis

Oxytropis sajanensis, commonly known as Sajan's locoweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor environments.

Oxytropis sajanensis is also known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock.
