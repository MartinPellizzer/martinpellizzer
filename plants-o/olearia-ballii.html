Olearia ballii

Olearia ballii, commonly known as the Pounamu tree, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and lowland areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in clusters at the ends of branches.

Olearia ballii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
