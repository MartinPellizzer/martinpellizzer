Orthosiphon bodinieri

Orthosiphon bodinieri, commonly known as the Java mint or water hyssop, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is often found in moist environments such as wetlands and along stream banks. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs.
