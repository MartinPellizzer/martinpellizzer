Oxytropis nigrescens f. albida

Oxytropis nigrescens f. albida, commonly known as the white-flowered locoweed, is a variant of the locoweed species native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright growth habit and slender, branching stems. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically white or pale in color, distinguishing it from the more commonly purple or pink-flowered forms. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets that are often hairy and grayish-green in appearance.

This plant thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
