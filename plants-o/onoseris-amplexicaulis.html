Onoseris amplexicaulis

Onoseris amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping leaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant features a basal rosette of dark green, clasping leaves that give it its common name. Its yellow-orange composite flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Onoseris amplexicaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage and floral display.
