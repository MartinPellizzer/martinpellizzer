Osmites parviflora

Osmites parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered osmite, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale green. The plant has adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through features such as succulent leaves and a deep root system. Its flowers are often clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, dry fruits that contain seeds.

Osmites parviflora is valued for its ornamental potential and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
