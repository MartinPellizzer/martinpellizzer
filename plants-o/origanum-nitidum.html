Origanum nitidum

Origanum nitidum, commonly known as sweet marjoram, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in summer. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal collections.

Its essential oils contain compounds like carvacrol and thymol, contributing to its aromatic and therapeutic properties.
