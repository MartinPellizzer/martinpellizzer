Olearia hookeri

Olearia hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's daisy, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has silver-gray foliage that helps it withstand salty air. It produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its propagation.

Olearia hookeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
