Oncidium gautieri

Oncidium gautieri, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow and green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Oncidium gautieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its long-lasting blooms.
