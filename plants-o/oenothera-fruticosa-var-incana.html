Oenothera fruticosa var. incana

Oenothera fruticosa var. incana, commonly known as the silverleaf evening primrose, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its stems are typically woody and branched, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves.

Oenothera fruticosa var. incana is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and dry slopes.
