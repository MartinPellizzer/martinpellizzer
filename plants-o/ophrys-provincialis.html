Ophrys provincialis

Ophrys provincialis, commonly known as the Provincial Orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its intricate floral structure, which mimics the appearance of female insects to attract male pollinators through a process called sexual deception. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are usually pale green or brown, with intricate patterns that resemble insect body structures.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, Ophrys provincialis is considered endangered in many parts of its range.
