Orchis sambucina lusus hybrida

Orchis sambucina lusus hybrida, commonly known as the purple orchid, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils, while its leaves are lance-shaped and grass-like.

This hybrid species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
