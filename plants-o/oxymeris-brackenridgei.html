Oxymeris brackenridgei

Oxymeris brackenridgei, commonly known as the Brackenridge's tamarind, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The fruit is a pod that contains several seeds, and the tree is often found in coastal and dry forest habitats.

It is valued for its hard wood and has some traditional uses in local communities.
