Oxytropis fischeriana

Oxytropis fischeriana, commonly known as Fisher's locoweed, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to cold and arid environments. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, hard pods that contain seeds.

Oxytropis fischeriana is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
