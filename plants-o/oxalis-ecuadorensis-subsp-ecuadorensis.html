Oxalis ecuadorensis subsp. ecuadorensis

Oxalis ecuadorensis subsp.

ecuadorensis, commonly known as the Ecuadorean wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Andean regions of Ecuador. It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped lobes and produces small, yellow flowers with five petals.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments within cloud forests. It has a fibrous root system and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture. Oxalis ecuadorensis subsp.

ecuadorensis is also valued for its ability to thrive in humid, temperate climates.
