Orchis purpurea subsp. lokiana

Orchis purpurea subsp.

lokiana, commonly known as the Lokian purple orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its tall, erect stem bearing a single spike of violet to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forest floors or stream banks. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a waxy texture, which helps reduce water loss.

The orchid relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake, a common trait among orchids.
