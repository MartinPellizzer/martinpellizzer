Oncidium unicorne var. pictum

Oncidium unicorne var. pictum, commonly known as the Painted Orchid, is a striking orchid species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful flowers that display intricate patterns, often in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in varying conditions.

The varietal name "pictum" refers to the vivid, picture-like appearance of its blooms.
