Oxalis stricta var. decumbens

Oxalis stricta var. decumbens, commonly known as the prostrate wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to North America.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves with reddish veins and produces clusters of five-petaled yellow flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands. It is adapted to full sun to partial shade and is known for its ability to spread via rhizomes.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
