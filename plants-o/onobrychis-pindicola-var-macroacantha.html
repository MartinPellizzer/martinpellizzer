Onobrychis pindicola var. macroacantha

Onobrychis pindicola var. macroacantha, commonly known as the Greater Calk, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces yellow flowers in racemes, which are followed by pods containing several small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as a forage plant or for ornamental purposes.
