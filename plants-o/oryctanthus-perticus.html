Oryctanthus perticus

Oryctanthus perticus, commonly known as the Cape goldcup, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers. The plant has a short, upright stem with lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a valuable addition to both natural and cultivated landscapes.
