Ochna thouvenotii

Ochna thouvenotii, commonly known as the Natal ochna, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and clusters of yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy, orange-red fruits that are dispersed by birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
