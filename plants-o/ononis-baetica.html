Ononis baetica

Ononis baetica, commonly known as Spanish catchfly, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It typically grows in rocky, dry, and well-drained soils, often found in Mediterranean climates. The plant has slender, branching stems that are covered in fine, hairy hairs. Its small, white or pinkish flowers appear in clusters during the spring and summer months.

The leaves are linear and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its adaptability in arid environments.
