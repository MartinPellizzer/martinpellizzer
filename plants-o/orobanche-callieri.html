Orobanche callieri

Orobanche callieri, commonly known as the Indian broomrape, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is native to regions of Asia, including India, China, and Southeast Asia. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale yellow or greenish in color. It is often found growing on the roots of various grasses and legumes.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can significantly impact the growth and yield of its host plants.
