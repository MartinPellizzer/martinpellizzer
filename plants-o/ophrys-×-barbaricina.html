Ophrys × barbaricina

Ophrys × barbaricina, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant that results from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe, particularly found in regions like Italy and Greece. The plant is characterized by its striking, bee-like flowers that mimic the appearance of female bees to attract male bees for pollination. Its botanical features include a basal rosette of leaves and a flowering stem that bears a single or occasionally two showy flowers.

This orchid thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.
