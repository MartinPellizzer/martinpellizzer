Odontoglossum luteopurpureum var. hinnus

Odontoglossum luteopurpureum var. hinnus, commonly known as the purple-lipped odontoglossum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, where it thrives in cool, humid environments. The plant features striking violet and yellow flowers with a distinctive purple lip. Its pseudobulbs are cylindrical, and it produces long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This orchid is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
