Oligosporus griseus

Oligosporus griseus, commonly known as the gray spore fungus, is a species of fungus belonging to the family Trichocomaceae.

It is characterized by its grayish spore masses and is often found in soil and decaying plant matter. The fungus produces spores in dense clusters, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. It plays a role in nutrient cycling in ecosystems by decomposing organic material.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological function, it is of interest in mycological research.
