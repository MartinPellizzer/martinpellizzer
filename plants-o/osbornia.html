Osbornia

Osbornia, commonly known as the "spider orchid," is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are native to various regions in Australia and are characterized by their unique, often intricate flower structures that resemble spiders or other small creatures. Osbornia species typically have slender, erect stems and are known for their striking, showy blooms that come in a range of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. They thrive in well-drained soils and are often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
