Orbea decaisneana

Orbea decaisneana, commonly known as the Cape fuchsia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as a shrub, with woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Orbea decaisneana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as birds and insects.
