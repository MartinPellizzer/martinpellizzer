Ophrys × zonnoi

Ophrys × zonnoi, commonly known as the Zonnoi Ophrys, is a hybrid orchid species native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the genus Ophrys, which is characterized by its mimicry of female insects to attract male pollinators. The plant produces intricate, bee-mimicking flowers that are typically yellow or brown with intricate patterns. It thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and forest edges.

Due to its specialized pollination mechanism, it plays a unique role in its ecosystem.
