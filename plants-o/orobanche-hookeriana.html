Orobanche hookeriana

Orobanche hookeriana, commonly known as the Hooker's broomrape, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is characterized by its lack of leaves and stems, with only small, scale-like structures. The plant produces showy, often brightly colored flowers that are typically pink, purple, or white. It parasitizes a variety of host plants, including those in the Fabaceae and Asteraceae families.

Due to its specialized lifestyle, it is often found in disturbed or nutrient-rich soils in arid and semi-arid regions.
