Ophiorrhiza tonglingensis

Ophiorrhiza tonglingensis, commonly known as the Tongling mangrove, is a rare and endemic species native to the coastal regions of China.

It is a small shrub that thrives in brackish water environments, often found in mangrove ecosystems. The plant has specialized adaptations, including salt-excreting structures on its leaves, which help it tolerate saline conditions. Its small, white flowers are clustered in inflorescences, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that are dispersed by water.

Due to habitat destruction, Ophiorrhiza tonglingensis is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
