Ocotea seibertii

Ocotea seibertii, commonly known as the Brazilian laurel, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest of eastern Brazil, where it thrives in moist, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that contains a single seed.

Ocotea seibertii is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
