Orlaya kochii

Orlaya kochii, commonly known as the narrowleaf orlaya, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. It is adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils and can tolerate high levels of sunlight.

Orlaya kochii is often found in steppe and desert environments, where it plays a minor role in local ecosystems.
