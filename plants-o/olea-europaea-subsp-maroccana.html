Olea europaea subsp. maroccana

Olea europaea subsp.

maroccana, commonly known as the Moroccan olive, is a subspecies native to North Africa, particularly Morocco. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its lanceolate, leathery leaves and fragrant flowers.

The plant produces small, olive-green fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, preferring well-drained soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional agriculture.
