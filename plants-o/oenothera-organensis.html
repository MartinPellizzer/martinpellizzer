Oenothera organensis

Oenothera organensis, commonly known as the Organ Mountains evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in arid and rocky habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Oenothera organensis is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its potential for medicinal use.
