Oxalis longissima

Oxalis longissima, commonly known as the everlasting oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like leaves and clusters of five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders. Its common name comes from the fact that its flowers can last for several days, giving the appearance of being ever-lasting.

Oxalis longissima is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural displays.
