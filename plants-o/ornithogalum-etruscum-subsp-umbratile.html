Ornithogalum etruscum subsp. umbratile

Ornithogalum etruscum subsp.

umbratile, commonly known as the Umbra Lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It features strap-like, grassy leaves and produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is characterized by its underground bulb, which stores nutrients for the plant's growth.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
