Orchis sambucina subsp. laurentina

Orchis sambucina subsp.

laurentina, commonly known as the Laurentian orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and northern Africa. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, violet-purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or grassland habitats. Its leaves are lanceolate and arranged in a basal rosette, while its flowers are clustered in a loose spike.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
