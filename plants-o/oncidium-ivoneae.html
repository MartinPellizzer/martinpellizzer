Oncidium ivoneae

Oncidium ivoneae, commonly known as the Ivo's orchid, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a sympodial growth habit, characterized by its pseudobulbs and fleshy roots. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often arranged in a raceme. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, typically arranged in two rows along the stem.

Oncidium ivoneae prefers warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
