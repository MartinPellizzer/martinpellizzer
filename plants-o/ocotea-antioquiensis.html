Ocotea antioquiensis

Ocotea antioquiensis, commonly known as the Antioquian ocotea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the humid montane forests of western Colombia, particularly in the Antioquia Department. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a dark blue berry containing a single seed, which is dispersed by birds.

Ocotea antioquiensis plays a vital role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
