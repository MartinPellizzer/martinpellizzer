Oxalis phaeotricha var. glandulosa

Oxalis phaeotricha var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features trifoliate leaves with reddish stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that give it its specific variety name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in temperate forests.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental species in horticulture.
