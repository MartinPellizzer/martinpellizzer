Onobrychis chorassanica f. lipskyana

Onobrychis chorassanica f. lipskyana, commonly known as the Persian clover, is a perennial legume native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to thrive in poor soils. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural studies.
