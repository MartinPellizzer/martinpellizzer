Oxypetalum cordifolium subsp. pedicellatum

Oxypetalum cordifolium subsp.

pedicellatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved oxypetalum, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slender pedicels, which support its delicate blooms.
