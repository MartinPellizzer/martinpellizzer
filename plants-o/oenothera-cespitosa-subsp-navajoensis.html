Oenothera cespitosa subsp. navajoensis

Oenothera cespitosa subsp.

navajoensis, commonly known as Navajo evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its clusters of yellow flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

The plant has hairy, glandular stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological importance and cultural significance to the Navajo people.
