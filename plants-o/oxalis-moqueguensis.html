Oxalis moqueguensis

Oxalis moqueguensis, commonly known as the Quechua wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the family Oxalidaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves contain oxalic acid, which can be toxic if ingested in large quantities.

Oxalis moqueguensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
