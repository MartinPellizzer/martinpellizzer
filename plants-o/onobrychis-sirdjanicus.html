Onobrychis sirdjanicus

Onobrychis sirdjanicus, commonly known as the Sirdjanica clover, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Eastern Mediterranean.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
