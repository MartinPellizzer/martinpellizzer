Ornithogalum hermannii

Ornithogalum hermannii, commonly known as Hermann's star-of-Bethleem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of star-shaped, white or pale blue flowers. The plant is characterized by its bulbous underground stem, which serves as a storage organ. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands.

Ornithogalum hermannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
