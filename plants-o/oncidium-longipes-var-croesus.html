Oncidium longipes var. croesus

Oncidium longipes var. croesus, commonly known as the Croesus orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, showy inflorescences that produce clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers with red and purple markings. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. It requires bright, indirect light and consistent moisture to thrive.

Its unique floral structure and vibrant coloration make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
