Oxycoccus quadripetalus var. microphyllus

Oxycoccus quadripetalus var. microphyllus, commonly known as the dwarf cranberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It produces small, red berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. The plant has compact, leathery leaves that are adapted to cold climates and poor soil conditions. It thrives in wet, acidic environments and is often found in peat bogs and tundra habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the boreal and tundra ecosystems.
