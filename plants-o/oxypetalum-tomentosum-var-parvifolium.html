Oxypetalum tomentosum var. parvifolium

Oxypetalum tomentosum var. parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaved woolly oxypetalum, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of China.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in mountainous areas. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape and are covered in a dense layer of fine, white pubescence.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
