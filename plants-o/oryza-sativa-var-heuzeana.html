Oryza sativa var. heuzeana

Oryza sativa var. heuzeana, commonly known as the Heuzean rice, is a botanical variant of the common rice species.

It is characterized by its unique morphological traits, including specific leaf shapes and grain structures. This variety is primarily found in certain regions of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in tropical climates. Its genetic makeup offers potential for breeding programs aimed at improving rice resilience.

However, it is less commonly cultivated compared to other rice varieties due to its specific environmental requirements.
