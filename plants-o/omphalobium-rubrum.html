Omphalobium rubrum

Omphalobium rubrum, commonly known as the red tussock grass, is a perennial herb native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and bright red stems, which are distinctive features. The plant produces small, pale green flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Omphalobium rubrum is often found in grasslands and open woodlands, contributing to the ecological diversity of these habitats.
