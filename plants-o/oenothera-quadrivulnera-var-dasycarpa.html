Oenothera quadrivulnera var. dasycarpa

Oenothera quadrivulnera var. dasycarpa, commonly known as the rough-seeded evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant produces capsule fruits that are rough and hairy, giving it its varietal name *dasycarpa*. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in desert or semi-arid environments.

The species is known for its resilience and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
