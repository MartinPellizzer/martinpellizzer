Oryza sativa var. atrofusca

Oryza sativa var. atrofusca, commonly known as black rice, is a variety of rice characterized by its dark, almost black grains.

This variety is native to Southeast Asia and is cultivated for its rich nutritional content, including high levels of antioxidants and minerals. The dark coloration is due to the presence of anthocyanins, which are pigments responsible for the deep hue. Black rice is also valued for its unique flavor and is often used in traditional dishes and medicinal practices.

It is a staple in many Asian cuisines and is gaining popularity worldwide for its health benefits.
