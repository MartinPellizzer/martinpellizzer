Oxys oregana

Oxys oregana, commonly known as Oregon grape, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces blue-black berries that are rich in antioxidants and are used in herbal remedies. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in rocky and well-drained soils.

Oxys oregana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and wildlife.
