Ocimum glossophyllum

Ocimum glossophyllum, commonly known as the purple basil or the sacred basil, is a species of flowering plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its aromatic, purple-hued leaves and small, tubular flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits. Its essential oils contain compounds like linalool and eugenol, which contribute to its distinctive fragrance and therapeutic uses.

Ocimum glossophyllum thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
