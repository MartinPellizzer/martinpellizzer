Oreoseris rupicola

Oreoseris rupicola, commonly known as the rock daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows on rocky outcrops and well-drained soils, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its small, yellow flowers are surrounded by white ray florets, making it visually distinct.

Oreoseris rupicola is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in drought conditions.
