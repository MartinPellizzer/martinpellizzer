Ononis cephalotes var. maroccana

Ononis cephalotes var. maroccana, commonly known as the Moroccan horseshoe vetch, is a flowering plant native to North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. The plant has a hairy stem and elliptical leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its seeds are adapted for dispersal by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize harsh landscapes.
