Ormenis santolinoides

Ormenis santolinoides, commonly known as the santolin-like ormenis, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a shrub with hairy, narrow leaves and produces small yellow to orange flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Ormenis santolinoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
