Orchis blidana

Orchis blidana, commonly known as the Blidane orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to parts of North Africa, particularly Morocco and Algeria, and thrives in rocky and arid environments. The plant produces striking violet or purple flowers with a distinctive lip petal, which is a characteristic feature of many orchids. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Orchis blidana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
