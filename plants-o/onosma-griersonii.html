Onosma griersonii

Onosma griersonii, commonly known as the Kashmir Wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and produces vibrant yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions.

Onosma griersonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking blooms.
