Ocotea multiflora

Ocotea multiflora, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and small, white flowers. The plant produces berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and therapeutic properties.

Ocotea multiflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
