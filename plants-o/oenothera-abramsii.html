Oenothera abramsii

Oenothera abramsii, commonly known as the Abrams' evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that open in the evening and close by morning. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert or semi-desert environments. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Oenothera abramsii is a perennial herb that plays a role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its habitat.
