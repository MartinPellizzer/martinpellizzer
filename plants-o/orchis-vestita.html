Orchis vestita

Orchis vestita, commonly known as the naked orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers that are typically arranged in a loose spike, with petals that are often adorned with distinctive markings. Orchis vestita thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is considered a valuable component of biodiversity-rich ecosystems.
