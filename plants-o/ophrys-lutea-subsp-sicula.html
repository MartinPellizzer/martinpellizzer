Ophrys lutea subsp. sicula

Ophrys lutea subsp.

sicula, commonly known as the Sicilian honey orchid, is a rare orchid species native to southern Italy. It is characterized by its distinctive yellow and brown flowers that resemble a female bee, a feature that plays a role in its unique pollination strategy.

The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is found in Mediterranean regions. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces a single spike with several flowers.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
