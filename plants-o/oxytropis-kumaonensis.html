Oxytropis kumaonensis

Oxytropis kumaonensis, commonly known as Kumaon oxytropis, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the Himalayan regions of India, particularly in the states of Uttarakhand and Himachal Pradesh. The plant is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils at high altitudes.

Oxytropis kumaonensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
