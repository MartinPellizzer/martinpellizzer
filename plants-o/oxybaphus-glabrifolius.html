Oxybaphus glabrifolius

Oxybaphus glabrifolius, commonly known as the smooth-leaved oxybaphus, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in grassland habitats and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and hairless. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom during the cooler months. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to tolerate dry conditions, making it resilient in arid environments.

Oxybaphus glabrifolius is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
