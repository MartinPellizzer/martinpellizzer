Ornithogalum pratense

Ornithogalum pratense, commonly known as the meadow bell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale green flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 cm and blooms in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

The genus Ornithogalum is characterized by its unique floral structure and is closely related to the lily family.
