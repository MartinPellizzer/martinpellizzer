Orchis × mackaensis

Orchis × mackaensis, commonly known as the Mackaensic orchid, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Orchis morio and Orchis anthropophora.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and clusters of pale green to brownish-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils, a common trait among orchids.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its genetic diversity and ecological adaptability.
