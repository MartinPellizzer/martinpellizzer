Oncidium karwinskii

Oncidium karwinskii, commonly known as the Karwinski's orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in clusters of bright yellow or orange. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in bright, indirect light with high humidity.

Oncidium karwinskii is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its vibrant blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
