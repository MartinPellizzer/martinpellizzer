Ophrys apifera proles botteronii

Ophrys apifera proles botteronii, commonly known as the bee orchid, is a striking Mediterranean orchid species characterized by its specialized floral structure.

The plant features a single, bee-like flower that mimics the appearance and scent of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. It typically grows in calcareous soils and is found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The orchid has a short, underground rhizome and produces a single erect stem with a single flower.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique mimicry and role in pollination dynamics within its ecosystem.
