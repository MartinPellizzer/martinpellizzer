Olea neriifolia

Olea neriifolia, commonly known as the black olive tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Oleaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and parts of southern Europe, North Africa, and the Middle East. The plant is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by black, fleshy fruits. Olea neriifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical significance in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitats.
