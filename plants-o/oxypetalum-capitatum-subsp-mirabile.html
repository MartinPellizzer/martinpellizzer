Oxypetalum capitatum subsp. mirabile

Oxypetalum capitatum subsp.

mirabile, commonly known as the yellow sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

The flowers bloom in spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
