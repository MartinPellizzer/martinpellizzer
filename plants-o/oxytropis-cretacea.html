Oxytropis cretacea

Oxytropis cretacea, commonly known as the Crete locoweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Crete.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

Oxytropis cretacea is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing a deep root system that allows it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
