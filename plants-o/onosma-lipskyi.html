Onosma lipskyi

Onosma lipskyi, commonly known as the Crimean thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus and parts of southern Russia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is often found in mountainous regions.

Onosma lipskyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
