Osbeckia polycephala

Osbeckia polycephala, commonly known as the many-headed willow herb, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and wet meadows. The plant features erect, branched stems that are often covered in fine, hairy hairs. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences, giving it its common name.

Osbeckia polycephala is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in wetland gardens.
