Orchis tripetaloides

Orchis tripetaloides, commonly known as the three-lobed orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its flowers are borne in a single spike.

Orchis tripetaloides is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
