Oenothera biennis var. cruciata

Oenothera biennis var. cruciata, commonly known as the cross-leaved evening primrose, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features showy yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The leaves are lance-shaped with a distinct cross-like vein pattern, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in prairies and disturbed habitats.

The plant is known for its oily seeds, which have been used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential fatty acids.
