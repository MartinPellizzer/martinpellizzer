Odontoglossum wyattianum

Odontoglossum wyattianum, commonly known as the Wyatt's orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its erect, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with a distinctive lip. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained soil.

Odontoglossum wyattianum is prized by orchid growers for its ornamental value and unique floral structure.
