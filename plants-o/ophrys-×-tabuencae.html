Ophrys × tabuencae

Ophrys × tabuencae, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is characterized by its unique floral morphology, featuring a lip that mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is found in Mediterranean regions, including parts of southern Europe and North Africa. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Ophrys × tabuencae is a perennial herb that thrives in open, sunny habitats and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
