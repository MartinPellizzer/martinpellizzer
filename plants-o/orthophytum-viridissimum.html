Orthophytum viridissimum

Orthophytum viridissimum, commonly known as the Emerald Cactus, is a striking succulent native to South America.

It features thick, fleshy stems that are typically green or blue-green in color, often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect sunlight to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
