Ononis megalostachys

Ononis megalostachys, commonly known as the large-stemmed whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows.

Ononis megalostachys is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
