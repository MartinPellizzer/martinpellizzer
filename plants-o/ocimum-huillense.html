Ocimum huillense

Ocimum huillense, commonly known as Huillense basil, is a species of flowering plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Ocimum huillense is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses among local communities.
