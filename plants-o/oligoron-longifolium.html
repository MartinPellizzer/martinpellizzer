Oligoron longifolium

Oligoron longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved oligoron, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features slender, upright stems that can grow up to 60 centimeters in height. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are elongated and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture and a slightly aromatic scent.

Oligoron longifolium is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats.
