Oreothyrsus pubisepalus

Oreothyrsus pubisepalus, commonly known as the pubescent thorned grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and distinctive, pubescent (hairy) leaves that are often covered in fine, short hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spikes, and its roots are fibrous and well-developed. Oreothyrsus pubisepalus is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic studies.
