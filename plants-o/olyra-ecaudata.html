Olyra ecaudata

Olyra ecaudata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or red. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Olyra ecaudata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
