Onobrychis kluchorica

Onobrychis kluchorica, commonly known as the Caucasian vetch, is a perennial leguminous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.

Its fibrous roots and hardy nature make it adaptable to harsh climatic conditions.
