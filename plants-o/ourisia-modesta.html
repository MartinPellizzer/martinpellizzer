Ourisia modesta

Ourisia modesta, commonly known as the common ourisia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. The plant features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, blooming in early spring. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

Ourisia modesta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
