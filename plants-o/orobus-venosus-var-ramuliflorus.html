Orobus venosus var. ramuliflorus

Orobus venosus var. ramuliflorus, commonly known as the ramulate milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, erect stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is notable for its role in supporting local pollinators, particularly butterflies and bees.
