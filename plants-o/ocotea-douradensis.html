Ocotea douradensis

Ocotea douradensis, commonly known as the Douradensis Ocotea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest biome of Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a drupe, typically containing a single seed, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
