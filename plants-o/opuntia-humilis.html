Opuntia humilis

Opuntia humilis, commonly known as the prickly pear cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems, known as cladodes, which are covered in sharp spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers, typically in spring, followed by edible fruits called tunas. Opuntia humilis is a succulent plant, capable of storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

It plays an important ecological role in desert environments, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
