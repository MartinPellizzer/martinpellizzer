Orobus vernus var. medius

Orobus vernus var. medius, commonly known as the lesser horseshoe vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial legume that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its trifoliate leaves are hairy and have a distinctive heart-shaped base.

The species is valued for its role in supporting pollinators and its use in ecological restoration projects.
