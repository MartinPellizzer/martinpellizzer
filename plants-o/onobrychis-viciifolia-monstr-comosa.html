Onobrychis viciifolia monstr. comosa

Onobrychis viciifolia monstr.

comosa, commonly known as the creeping vetch, is a low-growing leguminous plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is native to the Mediterranean region.

The plant has a sprawling growth habit, often forming dense mats on the ground. It is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in ecological restoration and as a forage plant.

Its adaptability to various soil types and drought tolerance further enhance its horticultural and agricultural significance.
