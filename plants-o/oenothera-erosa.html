Oenothera erosa

Oenothera erosa, commonly known as prairie evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening. The plant has hairy, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairie and open woodland habitats.

Oenothera erosa is known for its ability to produce a large number of seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
