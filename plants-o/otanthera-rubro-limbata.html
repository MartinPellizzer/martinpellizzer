Otanthera rubro-limbata

Otanthera rubro-limbata, commonly known as the red-limbed aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Otanthera rubro-limbata thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats like meadows and hillsides.
