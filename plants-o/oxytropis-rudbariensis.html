Oxytropis rudbariensis

Oxytropis rudbariensis, commonly known as the Rudbar locoweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in Iran and Afghanistan. It has adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and on slopes.

Oxytropis rudbariensis is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
