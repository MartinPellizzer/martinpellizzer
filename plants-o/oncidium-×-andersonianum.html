Oncidium × andersonianum

Oncidium × andersonianum, commonly known as the Anderson's orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to South America.

It is characterized by its long, pendant-like inflorescences that bear clusters of fragrant, yellowish-orange flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and it requires bright, indirect light and moderate humidity to thrive.

Oncidium × andersonianum is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
