Ophiopogon mairei

Ophiopogon mairei, commonly known as the Maire's spathiphyllum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It features fleshy, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in hue, clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Ophiopogon mairei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage.
