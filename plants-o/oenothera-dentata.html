Oenothera dentata

Oenothera dentata, commonly known as the toothed evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers that open in the evening, and its leaves have serrated edges. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. It belongs to the evening primrose family (Onagraceae) and is known for its medicinal and ornamental value.

Oenothera dentata is also noted for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its role in supporting local pollinators.
