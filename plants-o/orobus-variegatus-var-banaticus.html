Orobus variegatus var. banaticus

Orobus variegatus var. banaticus, commonly known as the Banat variegated broom, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow and green variegated leaves. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to sunny environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
