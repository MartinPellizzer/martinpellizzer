Oryza sativa var. eedeniana

Oryza sativa var. eedeniana, commonly known as the Eedenian rice, is a rare and ancient variety of rice native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small grain size and unique genetic makeup, which distinguishes it from other rice cultivars. The plant exhibits a compact growth habit with short, upright stems and narrow leaves. It is typically grown in wetland environments and requires a tropical climate for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution and genetic uniqueness, it holds significant value for botanical research and conservation efforts.
