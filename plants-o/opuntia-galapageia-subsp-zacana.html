Opuntia galapageia subsp. zacana

Opuntia galapageia subsp.

zacana, commonly known as the Galápagos prickly pear, is a cacti species native to the Galápagos Islands. It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems that are covered in spines and glochids.

The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the dry season, followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in arid and rocky habitats, demonstrating remarkable adaptability to the island's climate.

This subspecies plays an important role in the local ecosystem, providing food for various wildlife species.
