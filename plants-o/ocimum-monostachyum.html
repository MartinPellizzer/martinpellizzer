Ocimum monostachyum

Ocimum monostachyum, commonly known as the purple basil, is a species of flowering plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications due to their distinctive scent and flavor.

Ocimum monostachyum thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
