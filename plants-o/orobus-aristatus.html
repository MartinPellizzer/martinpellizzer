Orobus aristatus

Orobus aristatus, commonly known as the aristate orobus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, commonly known as the legume or pea family. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

The seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
