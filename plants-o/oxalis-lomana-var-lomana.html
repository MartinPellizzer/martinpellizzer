Oxalis lomana var. lomana

Oxalis lomana var. lomana, commonly known as the Lomana wood sorrel, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped lobes and produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade. It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread vegetatively.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in wetland and woodland ecosystems.
