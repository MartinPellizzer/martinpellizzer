Oncidium spilopterum aureum

Oncidium spilopterum aureum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It features striking, often yellow or golden-colored flowers that bloom in clusters, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening. The plant has strap-like leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture, adapted to absorb moisture from the air. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining potting media to prevent root rot.

Oncidium spilopterum aureum is known for its long-lasting blooms and relatively easy care, making it a favored plant among orchid enthusiasts.
