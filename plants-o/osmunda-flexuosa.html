Osmunda flexuosa

Osmunda flexuosa, commonly known as the flexible water fern, is a species of fern native to North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are often submerged or floating in freshwater environments. The plant has a complex life cycle involving both a sporophyte and a gametophyte stage. Its rhizomes are often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Osmunda flexuosa plays an important ecological role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and contributing to nutrient cycling.
