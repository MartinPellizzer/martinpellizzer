Onobrychis juncea

Onobrychis juncea, commonly known as yellow sweet clover, is a perennial legume native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a deep root system, allowing it to thrive in dry and nutrient-poor soils. It is often found in grasslands, wastelands, and along roadsides.

Its flowers are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
