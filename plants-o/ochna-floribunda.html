Ochna floribunda

Ochna floribunda, commonly known as the red sandalwood or red ochna, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring clusters of small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

The wood of Ochna floribunda is highly valued for its durability and rich reddish color, though it is also used in traditional medicine.
