Ochrosia acuminata

Ochrosia acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved poinsettia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble petals. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often overshadowed by the vibrant foliage. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
