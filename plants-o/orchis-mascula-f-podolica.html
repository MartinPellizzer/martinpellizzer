Orchis mascula f. podolica

Orchis mascula f. podolica, commonly known as the Podolian male orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species found in specific habitats across southern Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure, which includes a distinctive lip and spurred column. The plant typically grows in open, calcareous soils and is adapted to temperate climates with moderate moisture levels. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish in color, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, this orchid is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
