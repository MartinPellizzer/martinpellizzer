Oxytropis splendens f. nelsonii

Oxytropis splendens f. nelsonii, commonly known as Nelson's blazing locoweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and its clusters of bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of many legumes. It is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the western United States, particularly in Nevada and California.

This subspecies is adapted to harsh environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
