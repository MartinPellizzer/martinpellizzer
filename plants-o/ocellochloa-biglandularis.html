Ocellochloa biglandularis

Ocellochloa biglandularis, commonly known as the hairy flat sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to parts of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like blades and distinctive hairy structures on its stems and leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its reproductive structures include small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Ocellochloa biglandularis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
