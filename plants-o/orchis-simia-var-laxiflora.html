Orchis simia var. laxiflora

Orchis simia var. laxiflora, commonly known as the Monkey Orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that resemble the face of a monkey, with a prominent central lip. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil, while its leaves are usually broad and grass-like.

This orchid is known for its long blooming period, often from late spring to early summer.
