Oxera inodora

Oxera inodora, commonly known as the sweet oxera, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to South America.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. These flowers are often fragrant, contributing to its common name.

Oxera inodora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
