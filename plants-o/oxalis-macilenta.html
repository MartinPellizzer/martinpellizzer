Oxalis macilenta

Oxalis macilenta, commonly known as the pale wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and small, delicate flowers that are typically pink or white. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and unique floral display.
