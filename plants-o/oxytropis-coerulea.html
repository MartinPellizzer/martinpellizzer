Oxytropis coerulea

Oxytropis coerulea, commonly known as blue locoweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant belongs to the legume family (Fabaceae) and has small, trifoliate leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Oxytropis coerulea is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.
