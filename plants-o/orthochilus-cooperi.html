Orthochilus cooperi

Orthochilus cooperi, commonly known as the Cooper's orthochilus, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, greenish-white to pale yellow blooms with intricate petal structures. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests at high elevations.

Due to habitat loss, this species is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
