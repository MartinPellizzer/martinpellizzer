Oleandra colubrina var. membranacea

Oleandra colubrina var. membranacea, commonly known as the membranaceous oleander, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pink flowers during the spring and summer months. It is adapted to wetland environments and is often found in marshy areas along the Atlantic and Gulf coasts.

The species is noted for its hardiness and ability to thrive in moist, poorly drained soils.
