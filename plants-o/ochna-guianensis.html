Ochna guianensis

Ochna guianensis, commonly known as the Guianese ochna, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, simple leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, fleshy berries containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional medicinal uses among indigenous communities.
