Orthochilus hians var. inaequalis

Orthochilus hians var. inaequalis, commonly known as the unequal orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and its showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and green. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid forests. Its leaves are elongated and lance-shaped, with a smooth surface and a waxy texture.

This variant is distinguished by its irregularly shaped petals and sepals, which give it its common name.
