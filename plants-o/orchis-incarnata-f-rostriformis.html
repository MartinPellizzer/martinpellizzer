Orchis incarnata f. rostriformis

Orchis incarnata f. rostriformis, commonly known as the rostriform orchid, is a flowering plant species native to North America.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has a tall, upright stem with lance-shaped leaves and produces a spike of elongated, spurred flowers. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded areas with rich, organic soil.

This orchid is a perennial and is known for its unique floral structure that aids in pollination by specific insect species.
