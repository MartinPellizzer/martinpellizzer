Onobrychis farimanensis

Onobrychis farimanensis, commonly known as Fariman's burr vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to dry conditions. It has small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by the development of its distinctive, spiny pods.

This species is of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological role and potential for use in land restoration projects.
