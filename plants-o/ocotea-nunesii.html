Ocotea nunesii

Ocotea nunesii, commonly known as the Brazilian camphor tree, is a member of the Lauraceae family and is native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 15 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed, and it plays an important role in the local ecosystem.

Ocotea nunesii is valued for its timber and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
