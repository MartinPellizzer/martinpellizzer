Ophrys × lievreae nothosubsp. chiarae

Ophrys × lievreae nothosubsp.

chiarae, commonly known as the Chiara orchid, is a hybrid species within the Orchidaceae family. It is characterized by its unique floral morphology, including a specialized labellum that mimics the appearance of a female bee, facilitating its pollination strategy.

The plant typically grows in calcareous soils in Mediterranean climates, thriving in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its flowers are usually a mix of yellow and brown hues, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

This nothosubspecies is notable for its role in studying hybridization and evolutionary adaptations in orchids.
