Oreopanax pallidus

Oreopanax pallidus, commonly known as the pale oreopanax, is a tropical shrub native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
