Ophrys forestieri subsp. laureotica

Ophrys forestieri subsp.

laureotica, commonly known as the laurel orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It is characterized by its intricate floral structure, which mimics the appearance of female insects to attract male pollinators, a strategy known as sexual deception.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as forests and rocky slopes, where it forms dense, low-growing colonies. Its leaves are usually broad and leathery, adapted to conserve water in its often humid but nutrient-poor environment.

The species is of particular interest to botanists due to its unique pollination mechanism and ecological specificity.
