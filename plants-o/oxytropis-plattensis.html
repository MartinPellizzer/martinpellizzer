Oxytropis plattensis

Oxytropis plattensis, commonly known as Platt's locoweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers in dense clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert habitats.

Oxytropis plattensis is known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock.
