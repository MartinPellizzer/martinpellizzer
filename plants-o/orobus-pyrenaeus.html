Orobus pyrenaeus

Orobus pyrenaeus, commonly known as the Pyrenean orobus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant typically grows in rocky and gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its small, pea-like flowers are usually pink or purple, and it blooms during the spring and early summer.

Orobus pyrenaeus is a rare and endemic species, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
