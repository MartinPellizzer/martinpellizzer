Oreodaphne diospyrifolia var. incompta

Oreodaphne diospyrifolia var. incompta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sandpaper tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, sandpaper-like bark and elliptical leaves with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, clustered yellowish-white flowers followed by edible, orange-red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in lowland forests or along riverbanks.

Its unique foliage and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitats.
