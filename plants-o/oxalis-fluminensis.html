Oxalis fluminensis

Oxalis fluminensis, commonly known as the river oxalis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with striking red or orange markings, resembling those of a shamrock. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink, white, or yellow, typically in clusters. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found near water sources such as rivers and streams.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and as a houseplant.
