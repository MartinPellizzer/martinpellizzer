Oxystelma solanoides

Oxystelma solanoides, commonly known as the red sandalwood or false sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, featuring opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the summer months, followed by small, fleshy berries containing a single seed. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
