Ophrys × gumprechtii

Ophrys × gumprechtii, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a natural hybrid between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is characterized by its intricate, bee-like flower structure that mimics the appearance and scent of female bees to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. It is native to southern Europe and has been introduced to other regions, including parts of North America.

The hybrid exhibits a range of morphological variations, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
