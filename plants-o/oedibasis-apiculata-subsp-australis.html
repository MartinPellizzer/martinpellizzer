Oedibasis apiculata subsp. australis

Oedibasis apiculata subsp.

australis, commonly known as the Australian sandbur, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Australia. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems.

The plant produces small, spiny fruits that are adapted for wind dispersal, a common strategy in its harsh habitat. It thrives in sandy and gravelly soils, often found in open landscapes and along roadsides.

Its ecological role includes providing food for various insects and small animals.
