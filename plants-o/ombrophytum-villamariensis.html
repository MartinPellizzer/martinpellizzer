Ombrophytum villamariensis

Ombrophytum villamariensis, commonly known as the mountain rock rose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, showy flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cool, humid climates. Its leaves are typically small, hairy, and arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps conserve moisture.

Ombrophytum villamariensis is often found in shaded, forested areas, where it plays a role in the understory vegetation.
