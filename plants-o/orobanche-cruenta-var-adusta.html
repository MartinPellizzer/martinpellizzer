Orobanche cruenta var. adusta

Orobanche cruenta var. adusta, commonly known as the purple witch's butter, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

It produces striking, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to deep purple, depending on the variety. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and is a common weed in agricultural areas. It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is native to Europe and parts of Asia.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can cause significant damage to crops and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
