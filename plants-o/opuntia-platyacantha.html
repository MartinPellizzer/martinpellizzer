Opuntia platyacantha

Opuntia platyacantha, commonly known as the flat-armed prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems that are covered in sharp spines and glochids. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible fruits known as tunas. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and full sun exposure.

Opuntia platyacantha is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local cultures.
