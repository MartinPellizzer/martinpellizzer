Ochthocosmus multiflorus

Ochthocosmus multiflorus, commonly known as the many-flowered broom.

It is a flowering plant native to southern Africa, characterized by its dense clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with slender, woody stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

The flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
