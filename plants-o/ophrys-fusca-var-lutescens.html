Ophrys fusca var. lutescens

Ophrys fusca var. lutescens, commonly known as the yellow bee orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to southern Europe.

This terrestrial orchid is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant produces a single, showy flower with a yellowish-green hue and a distinctive bee-like shape. It thrives in calcareous soils and is typically found in open grasslands and woodland edges.

Due to its specialized pollination mechanism, it plays an important role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat.
