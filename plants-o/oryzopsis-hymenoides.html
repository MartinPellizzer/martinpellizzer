Oryzopsis hymenoides

Oryzopsis hymenoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf beakgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands, disturbed soils, and along roadsides. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are characteristic of the Poaceae family. Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.

Oryzopsis hymenoides plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
