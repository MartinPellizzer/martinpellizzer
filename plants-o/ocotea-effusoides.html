Ocotea effusoides

Ocotea effusoides, commonly known as the white sand tree, is a member of the Lauraceae family and is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves and can grow as either a tree or a shrub depending on environmental conditions. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by dark, glossy berries. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils, which have been used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.

Ocotea effusoides is often found in moist, shaded areas within tropical forests.
