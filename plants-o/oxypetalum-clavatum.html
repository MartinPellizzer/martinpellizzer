Oxypetalum clavatum

Oxypetalum clavatum, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of bell-shaped, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

Oxypetalum clavatum is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
