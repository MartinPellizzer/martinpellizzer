Oryza sativa subsp. japonica

Oryza sativa subsp.

japonica, commonly known as Japanese rice, is a subspecies of rice native to East Asia. It is characterized by its short, slender grains and typically grows in temperate climates.

This variety is well-suited for cultivation in regions with cooler temperatures and shorter growing seasons. The plant has a fibrous root system and produces panicles of small, greenish-white flowers.

It is an important crop in Japan and other parts of East Asia, contributing to both food security and traditional cuisine.
