Oenothera brevipetala

Oenothera brevipetala, commonly known as the short-petalled evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that open in the evening and close by morning. The plant has slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Oenothera brevipetala is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
