Ornithogalum gilgianum

Ornithogalum gilgianum, commonly known as the Gilgian star-of-Bethleem, is a bulbous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant.

The flowers have a mild fragrance and attract pollinators such as bees and moths.
