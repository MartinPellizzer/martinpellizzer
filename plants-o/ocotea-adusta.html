Ocotea adusta

Ocotea adusta, commonly known as the dark ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests. Its leaves are dark green and glossy, with a strong, spicy scent when crushed.

Ocotea adusta is valued for its timber, which is used in furniture and construction, and for its potential medicinal properties.
