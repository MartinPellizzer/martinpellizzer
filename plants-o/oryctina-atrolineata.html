Oryctina atrolineata

Oryctina atrolineata, commonly known as the black-lined orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple or black veins on its green leaves, which give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink, typically blooming during the rainy season. Oryctina atrolineata thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique coloration and ornamental value make it a sought-after plant among orchid enthusiasts.
