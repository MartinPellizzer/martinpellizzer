Oxytropis pumila

Oxytropis pumila, commonly known as dwarf locoweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and yellow or white flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open plains. It has a deep root system that allows it to survive in nutrient-poor conditions.

Oxytropis pumila is also known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock.
