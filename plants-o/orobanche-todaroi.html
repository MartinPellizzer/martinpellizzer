Orobanche todaroi

Orobanche todaroi, commonly known as the parasitic yellow root, is a hemiparasitic plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies on host plants for nutrients.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has a distinctive, tuberous root system. It typically parasitizes members of the legume family, such as Vicia and Astragalus.

Due to its specialized lifestyle, it plays a unique role in certain ecological systems.
