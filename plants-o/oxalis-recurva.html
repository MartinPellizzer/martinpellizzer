Oxalis recurva

Oxalis recurva, commonly known as the zigzag wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically heart-shaped with a distinctive zigzag pattern along the edges. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are usually yellow or white, clustered in umbels. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Oxalis recurva is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
