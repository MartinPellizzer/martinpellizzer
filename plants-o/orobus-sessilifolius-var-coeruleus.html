Orobus sessilifolius var. coeruleus

Orobus sessilifolius var. coeruleus, commonly known as the blue-flowered orobus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are typically tubular and are adapted for pollination by insects such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, colorful blooms.
