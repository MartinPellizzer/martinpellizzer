Oenothera × moravica

Oenothera × moravica, commonly known as the Moravian evening primrose, is a hybrid plant species native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in meadows and disturbed soils, exhibiting a sprawling growth habit with hairy stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine pubescence, while its flowers have four petals and numerous stamens.

Oenothera × moravica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its unique blooming behavior.
