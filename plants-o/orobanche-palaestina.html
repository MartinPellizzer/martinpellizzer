Orobanche palaestina

Orobanche palaestina, commonly known as the Palestine broomrape, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is characterized by its lack of leaves and stems, with only a short, fleshy stem supporting its flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish or greenish flowers that are often clustered in a spike-like arrangement. It is native to the Mediterranean region and parts of the Middle East, including Palestine.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can significantly affect the growth of its host plants, making it both a botanical curiosity and a potential agricultural concern.
