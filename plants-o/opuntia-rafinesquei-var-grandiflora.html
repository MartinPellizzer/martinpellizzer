Opuntia rafinesquei var. grandiflora

Opuntia rafinesquei var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered prickly pear, is a cacti species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems known as cladodes, which are covered in spines and glochids. The plant produces large, showy yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tunah, is a red or yellow pear-shaped structure that contains edible seeds.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting succulent properties to store water efficiently.
