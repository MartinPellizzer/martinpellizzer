Oryza sativa var. subjanthoceros

Oryza sativa var. subjanthoceros, commonly known as the slender rice plant, is a variant of rice characterized by its elongated, narrow grains.

It is primarily cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in warm, humid climates with ample sunlight. The plant has a short growth cycle, typically maturing within 90 to 120 days, making it suitable for multiple harvests per year. Its roots are adapted to flooded conditions, allowing it to flourish in paddy fields.

This variety is valued for its high yield and resilience to certain environmental stresses.
