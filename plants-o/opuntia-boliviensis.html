Opuntia boliviensis

Opuntia boliviensis, commonly known as the Bolivian prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the high-altitude regions of Bolivia.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems that are covered in spines and are adapted to store water. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers during the summer months, followed by edible fruits that are similar in appearance to other Opuntia species. Opuntia boliviensis thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating resilience to extreme temperatures and drought.

Its structure and growth patterns make it a notable example of xerophytic adaptation in the Andean region.
