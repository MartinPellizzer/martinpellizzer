Oxalis revoluta

Oxalis revoluta, commonly known as the curled wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically appearing in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Oxalis revoluta is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
