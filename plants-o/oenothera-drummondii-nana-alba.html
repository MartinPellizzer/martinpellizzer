Oenothera drummondii nana-alba

Oenothera drummondii nana-alba, commonly known as the white dwarf evening primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southwestern United States.

It features showy, white to pale yellow flowers that open in the evening and close by dawn. The plant has slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and opposite. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Onagraceae family.
