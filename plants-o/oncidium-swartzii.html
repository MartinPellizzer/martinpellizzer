Oncidium swartzii

Oncidium swartzii, commonly known as the yellow butterfly orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, slender pseudobulbs and showy, yellowish-green flowers that resemble butterflies. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are fragrant and bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Oncidium swartzii is popular among orchid growers for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
