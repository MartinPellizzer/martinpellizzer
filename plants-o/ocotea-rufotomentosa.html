Ocotea rufotomentosa

Ocotea rufotomentosa, commonly known as the red-tomentose ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and fragrant, aromatic bark. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Ocotea rufotomentosa is valued for its timber, which is used in furniture and construction due to its durability and rich color.
