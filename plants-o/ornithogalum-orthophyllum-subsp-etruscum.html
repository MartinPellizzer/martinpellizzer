Ornithogalum orthophyllum subsp. etruscum

Ornithogalum orthophyllum subsp.

etruscum, commonly known as the Etruscan star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its striking, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, forming a basal rosette, while its stems are erect and bear the showy inflorescences.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
