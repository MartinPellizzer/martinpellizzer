Onopordum acanthium subsp. ceretanum

Onopordum acanthium subsp.

ceretanum, commonly known as the Cretan thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its spiny, gray-green leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as hillsides and coastal areas. It has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
