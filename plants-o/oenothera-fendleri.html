Oenothera fendleri

Oenothera fendleri, commonly known as desert evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating remarkable drought tolerance. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, adapted to minimize water loss.

Oenothera fendleri is also known for its ability to produce a large number of seeds, contributing to its ecological resilience and widespread distribution.
