Oxalis lindheimeri

Oxalis lindheimeri, commonly known as Lindheimer's wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive veins and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and is adapted to thrive in acidic soils.

Oxalis lindheimeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
