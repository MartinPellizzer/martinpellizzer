Odontoglossum dawsonianum

Odontoglossum dawsonianum, commonly known as the Dawson's orchid, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant features a pseudobulb structure, which helps it store water and nutrients in its native environment. Its leaves are typically broad and grass-like, and it thrives in cool, humid conditions with partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
