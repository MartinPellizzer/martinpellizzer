Oxystophyllum bipulvinatum

Oxystophyllum bipulvinatum, commonly known as the two-pulvinated oxystophyllum, is a unique plant species characterized by its specialized pulvini structures.

These pulvini, which are modified leaf bases, allow the plant to exhibit phototropism and respond to environmental stimuli. The plant's morphology includes a slender stem with alternate, ovate leaves that have a distinctive waxy coating. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical significance lies in its adaptive features, making it an interesting subject for ecological and botanical research.
