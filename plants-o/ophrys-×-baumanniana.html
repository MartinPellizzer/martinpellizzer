Ophrys × baumanniana

Ophrys × baumanniana, commonly known as the Baumann's bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its intricate, bee-like floral morphology, which features a specialized lip that mimics the appearance and scent of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its flowers are usually pale green to brown in color, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Ophrys × baumanniana is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its unique ecological interactions and ornamental appeal.
