Orthophytum guaratingense

Orthophytum guaratingense, commonly known as the Guaratinga torch cactus, is a species of cactus native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stem and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, featuring spiny areoles and a succulent structure for water storage. It typically grows in rocky soils and is found in regions with seasonal rainfall.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
