Oenothera williamsonii

Oenothera williamsonii, commonly known as Williamson's evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in desert or semi-arid environments.

Oenothera williamsonii is a perennial species, known for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
