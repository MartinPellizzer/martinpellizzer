Ophrys aranifera var. virescens

Ophrys aranifera var. virescens, commonly known as the green orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Turkey.

It is characterized by its distinctive greenish-yellow flowers that resemble a bee, a trait that aids in its unique pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its overall appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
