Ophiochloa hydrolithica

Ophiochloa hydrolithica, commonly known as the rock sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its succulent, grass-like leaves and ability to thrive in harsh, rocky environments. The plant has a fibrous root system that helps it anchor in crevices and access water from deep within the soil. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically clustered in dense spikes, and it produces small, dry seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Ophiochloa hydrolithica plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small desert organisms.
