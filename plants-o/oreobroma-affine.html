Oreobroma affine

Oreobroma affine, commonly known as the mountain bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped blue to purple flowers during the summer months. Its botanical characteristics include a caulescent stem and a basal rosette of leaves.

Oreobroma affine is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive flowers and hardy nature.
