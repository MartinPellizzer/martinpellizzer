Ophrys × albertiana nothosubsp. grovesii

Ophrys × albertiana nothosubsp.

grovesii, commonly known as the Albertine bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its intricate floral structure, which mimics the appearance of female bees to attract male pollinators.

The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brown, with intricate patterns that aid in mimicry.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its range.
