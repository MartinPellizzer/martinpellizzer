Oxytropis strobilacea var. nigrocarpa

Oxytropis strobilacea var. nigrocarpa, commonly known as blackpod locoweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces distinctive, dark brown pods that are often used for identification. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sagebrush steppe and rocky soils.

The species is known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock.
