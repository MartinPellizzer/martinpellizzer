Oreopanax permixtus

Oreopanax permixtus, commonly known as the mixed-leaf oreopanax, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnately arranged and have a distinctive, often variegated appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
