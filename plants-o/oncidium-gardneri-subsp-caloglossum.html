Oncidium gardneri subsp. caloglossum

Oncidium gardneri subsp.

caloglossum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical orchid native to South America. It is characterized by its vibrant yellow and green foliage and produces striking, fragrant flowers with a unique, spurred shape.

The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
