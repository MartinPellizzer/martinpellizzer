Odontoglossum pulchellum var. dormanianum

Odontoglossum pulchellum var. dormanianum, commonly known as the Dormanian orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a rhizomatous growth habit, producing erect, hairy stems that bear numerous lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in cool, humid environments with well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for greenhouse cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often sought after by orchid enthusiasts.
