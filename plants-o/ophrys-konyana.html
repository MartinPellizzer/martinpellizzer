Ophrys konyana

Ophrys konyana, commonly known as the Konyana bee orchid, is a species of orchid native to the high-altitude regions of East Africa.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which closely resembles a female bee, a mimicry strategy used to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in rocky or grassland habitats and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brownish in color, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Ophrys konyana is a rare and specialized orchid, showcasing the evolutionary adaptations seen in plant-pollinator relationships.
