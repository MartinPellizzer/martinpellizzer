Odontoglossum × luerorum

Odontoglossum × luerorum, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Odontoglossum orchids.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of South America, particularly in countries like Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces showy, colorful flowers with intricate patterns. Its botanical significance lies in its role as a parent in the development of many modern orchid hybrids.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
