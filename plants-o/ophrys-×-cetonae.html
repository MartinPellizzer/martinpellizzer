Ophrys × cetonae

Ophrys × cetonae, commonly known as the bee orchid hybrid, is a fascinating plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Ophrys apifera (the bee orchid) and Ophrys citrina (the yellow bee orchid), resulting in a unique combination of traits from both parents. The plant is notable for its striking, bee-like flowers that mimic the appearance and scent of female bees, a form of sexual deception used to attract male bees for pollination. Ophrys × cetonae typically grows in calcareous soils and is found in meadows and grasslands across Europe.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as an example of hybridization in orchids and its ecological interactions with pollinators.
