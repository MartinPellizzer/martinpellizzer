Olax longiflora

Olax longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered olax, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Olacaceae and is characterized by its showy, often fragrant flowers that are typically white or pink in color. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals. Olax longiflora is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
