Oncidium exasperatoides

Oncidium exasperatoides, commonly known as the showy orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Central and South America.

It features a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients, supporting its growth in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces striking, long-lasting orchid flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, often with a distinctive scent. Its leaves are typically strap-shaped and dark green, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.

Oncidium exasperatoides is popular among orchid growers for its ease of cultivation and vibrant floral display.
