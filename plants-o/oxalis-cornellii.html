Oxalis cornellii

Oxalis cornellii, commonly known as the false shamrock, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves that resemble those of the shamrock. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Oxalis cornellii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
