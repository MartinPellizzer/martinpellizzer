Oenothera perangusta var. rubricalyx

Oenothera perangusta var. rubricalyx, commonly known as the red-leaf evening primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and showy, yellow flowers that open in the evening. The leaves are narrow and often have a reddish or purplish hue, which gives the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
