Orchis vernalis

Orchis vernalis, commonly known as the early purple orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of North Africa.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodlands and meadows. The plant produces a single spike of purple to violet flowers, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinct midrib.

The species is part of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance.
