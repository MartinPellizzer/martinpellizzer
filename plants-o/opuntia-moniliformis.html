Opuntia moniliformis

Opuntia moniliformis, commonly known as the chainfruit prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its flattened, segmented stems that are covered in spines and are often ribbed. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by edible, spherical fruits that are typically red or yellow. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a deep root system that allows it to survive in arid conditions.

Opuntia moniliformis is a popular ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
