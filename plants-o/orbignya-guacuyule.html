Orbignya guacuyule

Orbignya guacuyule, commonly known as the wax palm, is a tall, slender palm native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its smooth, waxy leaves and can grow up to 20 meters in height. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clustered, woody fruits. Its trunk is often covered in a thick layer of protective wax, which helps it survive in humid, tropical conditions.

Orbignya guacuyule plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
