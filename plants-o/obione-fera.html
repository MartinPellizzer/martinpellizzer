Obione fera

Obione fera, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphyte, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. The plant's roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Its flowers are small, delicate, and typically white or pale pink, with a unique, almost translucent appearance.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Obione fera is considered endangered and is protected in many regions.
