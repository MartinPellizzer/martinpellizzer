Otomeria batesii

Otomeria batesii, commonly known as the African shield fern, is a species of fern native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, shield-shaped fronds that are typically dark green and leathery in texture. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Otomeria batesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
