Oxalis leucophylla

Oxalis leucophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, which are glossy and often covered in a fine, silvery-white down. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, with five petals and a spurred corolla. Oxalis leucophylla thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique floral structure.
