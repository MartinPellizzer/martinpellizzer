Opuntia bradtiana

Opuntia bradtiana, commonly known as the "Candelabra Cactus," is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its branching, tree-like structure composed of cylindrical stems covered in sharp spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are modified into flat, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

Opuntia bradtiana is also known for its ability to produce edible fruits, which are typically orange or red in color.
