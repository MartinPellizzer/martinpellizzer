Octolepis nodosericea

Octolepis nodosericea, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a unique member of the Onychophora class, which includes velvet worms.

It is characterized by its soft, fleshy body covered in a dense layer of fine, hair-like setae that give it a velvety appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures are simple, with eggs laid in a gelatinous mass that adheres to surfaces.

Despite its name, it is not a true fern but a primitive invertebrate with botanical significance in the study of early terrestrial life forms.
