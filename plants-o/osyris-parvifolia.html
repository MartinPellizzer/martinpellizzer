Osyris parvifolia

Osyris parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved osyris, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. Osyris parvifolia is adapted to arid conditions and is often found in coastal dunes.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for study in xerophytic plant ecology.
