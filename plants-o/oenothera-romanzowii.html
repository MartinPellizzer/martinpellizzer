Oenothera romanzowii

Oenothera romanzowii, commonly known as the Romanzov's evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its flowers have four petals and are typically borne in clusters at the tips of the stems.

Oenothera romanzowii is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in dry, sandy soils.
