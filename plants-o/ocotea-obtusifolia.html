Ocotea obtusifolia

Ocotea obtusifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils, which have been used in traditional medicine.

Ocotea obtusifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
