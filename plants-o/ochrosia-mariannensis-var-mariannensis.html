Ochrosia mariannensis var. mariannensis

Ochrosia mariannensis var. mariannensis, commonly known as the Mariana false arum, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Pacific Ocean, particularly the Mariana Islands.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, which are often surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a branching structure that supports its dense foliage.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
