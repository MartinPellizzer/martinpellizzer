Orthion malpighiifolium

Orthion malpighiifolium, commonly known as the Malpighiopsis, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Malpighiaceae.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often palmately lobed, and its clusters of small, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow or orange. The plant is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, particularly in areas with moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks, where it thrives in partial shade to full sun.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
