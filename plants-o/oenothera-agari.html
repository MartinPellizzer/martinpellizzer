Oenothera agari

Oenothera agari, commonly known as the agari evening primrose, is a species of flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often branched and upright.
