Oxalis famatinae subsp. sanjuaninae

Oxalis famatinae subsp.

sanjuaninae, commonly known as the San Juan oxalis, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America. It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showing adaptations to drought and nutrient-poor soils. Its leaves are often hairy, and the flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
