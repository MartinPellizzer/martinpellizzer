Ononis rentonarensis

Ononis rentonarensis, commonly known as the Renton rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the rocky coastal areas of southern California.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant has a low-growing, mat-forming structure with hairy, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, and it produces small, round pods that contain several seeds.

Ononis rentonarensis is adapted to harsh, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub habitats.
