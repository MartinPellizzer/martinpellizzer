Opuntia boldinghii

Opuntia boldinghii, commonly known as the Boldingh's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in sharp spines and glochids. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers, which are followed by edible, fleshy fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting well to hot, dry climates.

Opuntia boldinghii is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance.
