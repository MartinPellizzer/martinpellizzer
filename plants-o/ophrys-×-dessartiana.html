Ophrys × dessartiana

Ophrys × dessartiana, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe, particularly found in regions like France and Spain. The plant is characterized by its unique floral morphology, with a pouch-shaped labellum that mimics the appearance of a female bee, aiding in pollination by male bees. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow with intricate patterns, and it thrives in calcareous soils.

Ophrys × dessartiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinator populations.
