Orostachys malacophylla var. boehmeri

Orostachys malacophylla var. boehmeri, commonly known as the stone pine or desert plantain, is a succulent species native to arid regions of Asia.

It features a rosette growth form with thick, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The leaves are typically blue-gray in color, with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often followed by delicate, papery bracts.

This plant is well adapted to sandy soils and can thrive in full sun, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.
