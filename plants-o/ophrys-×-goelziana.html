Ophrys × goelziana

Ophrys × goelziana, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant that belongs to the Orchidaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Ophrys apifera (the bee orchid) and Ophrys insectifera (the wasp orchid). The plant is notable for its intricate floral morphology, which mimics the appearance of female bees or wasps to attract male pollinators. Its flowers are characterized by a distinctive lip that resembles an insect's body, complete with fine hair patterns.

This unique adaptation enhances its pollination success through a process known as sexual deception.
