Ostrya vulgaris var. glandulosa

Ostrya vulgaris var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glossy ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are glossy on top and hairy underneath. The tree produces small, pendulous catkins and is known for its hard, dense wood that is resistant to decay. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and can reach heights of up to 25 meters.

This variety is often found in mixed forests and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
