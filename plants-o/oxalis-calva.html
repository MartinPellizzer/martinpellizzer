Oxalis calva

Oxalis calva, commonly known as the pale wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive light green veins, giving it a delicate, fern-like appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Oxalis calva is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
