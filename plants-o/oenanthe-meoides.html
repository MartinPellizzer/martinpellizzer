Oenanthe meoides

Oenanthe meoides, commonly known as the meadow dropwort, is a flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist meadows, stream banks, and other wetland habitats. The plant features slender, upright stems with alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its small, white or greenish flowers are clustered in umbels and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Oenanthe meoides is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
