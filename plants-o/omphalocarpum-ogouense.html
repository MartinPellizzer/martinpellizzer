Omphalocarpum ogouense

Omphalocarpum ogouense, commonly known as African burr bush, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant produces hairy, spiny capsules that contain seeds with a hard, persistent coat. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in semi-arid conditions.

The species is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
