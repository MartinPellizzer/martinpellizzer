Osmanthus austrocaledonicus subsp. austrocaledonicus

Osmanthus austrocaledonicus subsp.

austrocaledonicus, commonly known as the New Caledonian osmanthus, is a species native to the island of New Caledonia. It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aromatic properties.
