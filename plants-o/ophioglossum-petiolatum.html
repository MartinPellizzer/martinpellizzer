Ophioglossum petiolatum

Ophioglossum petiolatum, commonly known as the spurred snake plant, is a rare and ancient fern-like plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, spirally arranged leaves that resemble the scales of a snake, giving it its common name. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are clustered in a distinctive, spurred arrangement at the end of its leaf stalk. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Ophioglossum petiolatum is considered a conservation concern in many regions.
