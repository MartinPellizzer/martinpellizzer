Oenothera ambigua

Oenothera ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous evening primrose, is a species of flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. The plant features showy yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Oenothera ambigua is also known for its ability to produce a large number of seeds, contributing to its widespread distribution.
