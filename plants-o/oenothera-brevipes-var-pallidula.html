Oenothera brevipes var. pallidula

Oenothera brevipes var. pallidula, commonly known as the pale-stemmed evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, pale green stems and showy yellow flowers that open in the evening. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the genus Oenothera, known for its ecological and horticultural significance.
