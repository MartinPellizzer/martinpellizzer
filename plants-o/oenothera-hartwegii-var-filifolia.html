Oenothera hartwegii var. filifolia

Oenothera hartwegii var. filifolia, commonly known as the silky evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pale yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the evening. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving the plant its common name. This variety is often found in rocky soils and arid environments, adapting well to dry conditions.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
