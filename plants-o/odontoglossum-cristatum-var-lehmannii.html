Odontoglossum cristatum var. lehmannii

Odontoglossum cristatum var. lehmannii, commonly known as the Lehmann's toothed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru. The plant features a compact, rhizomatous growth habit with lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are typically pale green to white, with a distinctive, toothed lip that gives the species its common name.

This orchid prefers cool, humid environments and is often found in cloud forest habitats.
