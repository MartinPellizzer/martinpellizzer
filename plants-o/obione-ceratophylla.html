Obione ceratophylla

Obione ceratophylla, commonly known as the horned waterwheel, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its distinctive, spiraling arrangement of leaves.

It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes, where it can anchor itself to submerged surfaces. The plant's leaves are narrow and spirally twisted, giving it a helical appearance that aids in maximizing light absorption. Its reproductive structures consist of small, floating fruits that release seeds into the water, facilitating dispersal.

Obione ceratophylla plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
