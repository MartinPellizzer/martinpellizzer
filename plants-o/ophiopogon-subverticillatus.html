Ophiopogon subverticillatus

Ophiopogon subverticillatus, commonly known as the Japanese forest grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in dense, tufted clusters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale green, followed by dark blue or black berries. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its foliage is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to form dense groundcovers.
