Outea multijuga

Outea multijuga, commonly known as the Brazilian walnut, is a member of the Proteaceae family and is native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, palmate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits containing edible seeds.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and economic purposes in its native habitat.
