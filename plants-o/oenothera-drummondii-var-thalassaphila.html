Oenothera drummondii var. thalassaphila

Oenothera drummondii var. thalassaphila, commonly known as the coastal evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the United States.

It is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along dunes and coastal bluffs, showcasing its adaptability to harsh, saline environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, and the stems are often sprawling or prostrate.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing dune ecosystems and its ornamental appeal in coastal gardens.
