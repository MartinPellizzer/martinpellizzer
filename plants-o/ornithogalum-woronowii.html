Ornithogalum woronowii

Ornithogalum woronowii, commonly known as the African star of Bethlehem, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white or pale green, star-shaped flowers. The plant belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its ability to thrive in well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long flowering period during the summer months.

The bulbs are also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
