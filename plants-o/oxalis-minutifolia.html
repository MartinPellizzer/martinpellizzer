Oxalis minutifolia

Oxalis minutifolia, commonly known as the small-leaf wood sorrel, is a delicate flowering plant native to South America.

It features small, trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped lobes and produces clusters of delicate, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its foliage and flowers are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft appearance.

Oxalis minutifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and blooms.
