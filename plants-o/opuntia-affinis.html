Opuntia affinis

Opuntia affinis, commonly known as the narrowleaf prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in spines and glochids, which are small, barbed bristles. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tunas, is edible and often used in culinary applications.

Opuntia affinis is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
