Ononis cerrifolia

Ononis cerrifolia, commonly known as the woolly bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, creeping stems and small, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Ononis cerrifolia is also known for its historical use in herbal medicine.
