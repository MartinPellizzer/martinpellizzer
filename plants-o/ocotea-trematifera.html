Ocotea trematifera

Ocotea trematifera, commonly known as the trembling ocotea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in countries like Costa Rica and Panama. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in white or greenish-white colors. Its leaves are typically elliptical or ovate, with a glossy surface and a distinctive, fragrant scent.

Ocotea trematifera is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
