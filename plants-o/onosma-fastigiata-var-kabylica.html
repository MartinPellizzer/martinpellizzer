Onosma fastigiata var. kabylica

Onosma fastigiata var. kabylica, commonly known as the Kabylic flowering plant, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pale pink to white, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
