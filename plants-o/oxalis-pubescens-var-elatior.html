Oxalis pubescens var. elatior

Oxalis pubescens var. elatior, commonly known as the velvetleaf wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Oxalis pubescens var. elatior is often found in woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
