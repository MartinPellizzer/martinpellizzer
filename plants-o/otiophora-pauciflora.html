Otiophora pauciflora

Otiophora pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered otiophora, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in shrubland habitats. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces small, dry fruits.

Otiophora pauciflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
