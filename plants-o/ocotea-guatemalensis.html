Ocotea guatemalensis

Ocotea guatemalensis, commonly known as Guatemalan ocotea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage and smooth, reddish bark. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in humid lowland forests. Its leaves are lance-shaped, glossy, and have a strong, spicy scent, while its flowers are small and clustered.

The fruit is a dark blue, fleshy berry that contains several seeds.
