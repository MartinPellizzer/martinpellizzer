Ocotea mexicana var. subsessilis

Ocotea mexicana var. subsessilis, commonly known as the Mexican ocotea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and have a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

The fruit is a small, dark blue drupe that contains a single seed.
