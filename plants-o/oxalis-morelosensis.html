Oxalis morelosensis

Oxalis morelosensis, commonly known as the Morelos wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. Its foliage contains oxalic acid, which can be toxic if ingested in large quantities.

Oxalis morelosensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
