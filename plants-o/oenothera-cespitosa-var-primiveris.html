Oenothera cespitosa var. primiveris

Oenothera cespitosa var. primiveris, commonly known as the early evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in prairies, open woods, and along roadsides.

The species is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
