Opuntia edwardsii

Opuntia edwardsii, commonly known as the Edwards prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems that are segmented and covered in spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers, followed by edible, red-fleshed fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating remarkable drought tolerance.

Opuntia edwardsii plays an important role in its native ecosystem, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
