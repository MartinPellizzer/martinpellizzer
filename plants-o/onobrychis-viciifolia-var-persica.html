Onobrychis viciifolia var. persica

Onobrychis viciifolia var. persica, commonly known as Persian clover, is a perennial legume native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. It has a deep root system that allows it to tolerate drought conditions effectively.

This variety is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment and as a forage crop.
