Otopetalum micranthum

Otopetalum micranthum, commonly known as the small-flowered starling tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, simple, ovate to elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round, fleshy fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in lowland forests or along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its foliage and floral display.
