Ocotea lineata

Ocotea lineata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests. Its leaves are elongated and have a smooth, glossy surface, while its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences.

The tree is valued for its timber and has also been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
