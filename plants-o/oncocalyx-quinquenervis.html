Oncocalyx quinquenervis

Oncocalyx quinquenervis, commonly known as the five-veined orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically display vibrant colors such as pink, purple, or white. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its complex floral structure, including a specialized lip petal that aids in pollination. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while the stems are often erect and bear multiple flowers.

Oncocalyx quinquenervis thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.
