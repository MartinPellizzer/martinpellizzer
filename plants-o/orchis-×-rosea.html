Orchis × rosea

Orchis × rosea, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a naturally occurring cross between Orchis moria and Orchis mascula.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in woodland habitats and thrives in moist, well-drained soil. Its flowers are often arranged in a spike and have a distinctive lip that aids in pollination.

Orchis × rosea is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
