Ostericum maximowiczii var. alpinum

Ostericum maximowiczii var. alpinum, commonly known as the alpine wintergreen, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It features dense, dark green, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, providing a compact and ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by red-brown berries. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ostericum maximowiczii var. alpinum is well adapted to cold climates and is often used in rock gardens or alpine settings.
