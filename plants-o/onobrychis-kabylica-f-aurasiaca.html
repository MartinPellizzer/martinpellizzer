Onobrychis kabylica f. aurasiaca

Onobrychis kabylica f. aurasiaca, commonly known as the golden field vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is native to parts of Eastern Europe and Central Asia, where it thrives in dry, sandy soils. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it ecologically significant in soil enrichment and restoration efforts.
