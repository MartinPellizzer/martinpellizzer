Oxalis acetosella var. longicapsula

Oxalis acetosella var. longicapsula, commonly known as the slenderwood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its foliage is often covered in fine, silky hairs, and it is known for its ability to tolerate shade.

The varietal name "longicapsula" refers to the elongated seed pods that develop after flowering.
