Oryzopsis lateralis

Oryzopsis lateralis, commonly known as the slender wild rice, is a grass species native to the wetlands of North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, rice-like grains that are often found in marshy or flooded environments. The plant thrives in shallow water and is adapted to seasonal flooding, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems. Its seeds are a food source for various waterfowl and other wildlife.

Oryzopsis lateralis plays a role in stabilizing soil and maintaining water quality in its habitat.
