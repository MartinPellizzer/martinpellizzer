Oenothera strigosa var. cheradophila

Oenothera strigosa var. cheradophila, commonly known as the prairie evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and sandy soils. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds.

This species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
