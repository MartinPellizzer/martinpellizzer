Olax durandii

Olax durandii, commonly known as the Durand's olax, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Olacaceae and is characterized by its woody structure and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with five petals. Olax durandii is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and along riverbanks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
