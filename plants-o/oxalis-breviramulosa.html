Oxalis breviramulosa

Oxalis breviramulosa, commonly known as the short-stalked wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its stems are often short and branched, and it is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Oxalis breviramulosa is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
