Orchis obcordata

Orchis obcordata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikes of purple or pink flowers with a unique, hooded structure. Orchis obcordata thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland edges and meadows.

It plays an important role in pollination, attracting various insects such as bees and moths.
