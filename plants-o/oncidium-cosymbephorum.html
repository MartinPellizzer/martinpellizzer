Oncidium cosymbephorum

Oncidium cosymbephorum, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers with a distinctive red lip, which are highly attractive to pollinators. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, anchoring itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in varying conditions.

Oncidium cosymbephorum is valued in horticulture for its showy blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
