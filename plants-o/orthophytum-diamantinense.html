Orthophytum diamantinense

Orthophytum diamantinense, commonly known as the Diamond Hill cactus, is a species native to Brazil.

It is a small, compact cactus with a distinctive appearance, featuring a short, thick stem and clusters of bright green, spiny leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its foliage. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
