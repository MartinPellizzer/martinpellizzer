Opuntia engelmannii subsp. lindheimeri

Opuntia engelmannii subsp.

lindheimeri, commonly known as the Lindheimer's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its flat, fleshy stem segments, known as cladodes, which are typically green and covered in spines.

The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, a pear-shaped structure, is edible and contains small, sweet seeds.

This subspecies is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.
