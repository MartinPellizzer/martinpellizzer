Oncostemum latifolium

Oncostemum latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf oncostemum, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in moist, swampy environments and is often found in marshes and along the edges of water bodies.

Oncostemum latifolium plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
