Oldenlandia uniflora var. pilosa

Oldenlandia uniflora var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy spurge or velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, velvety appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain areas.
