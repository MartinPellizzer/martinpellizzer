Oxalis stellata var. glandulosa

Oxalis stellata var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its stems are often hairy, and the leaves have a slightly succulent texture.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
