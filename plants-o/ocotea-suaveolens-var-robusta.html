Ocotea suaveolens var. robusta

Ocotea suaveolens var. robusta, commonly known as the robusta ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by dark, oval berries that are edible but not widely consumed by humans. Its wood is valued for its fragrance and is used in the production of incense and essential oils.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance in providing habitat for various wildlife species.
