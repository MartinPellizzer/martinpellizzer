Oryza sativa var. striata

Oryza sativa var. striata, commonly known as the striped rice, is a lesser-known variant of the rice species.

It is characterized by its distinctive striped appearance on the grain surface, which sets it apart from other rice varieties. This plant is typically cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions, similar to other rice species. It requires a warm climate and ample water for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for genetic research and agricultural studies.
