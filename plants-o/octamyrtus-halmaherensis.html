Octamyrtus halmaherensis

Octamyrtus halmaherensis, commonly known as the Halmahera myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to the island of Halmahera in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments within tropical rainforests.

Octamyrtus halmaherensis is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and potential medicinal properties.
