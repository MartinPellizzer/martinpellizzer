Opuntia albisetosa

Opuntia albisetosa, commonly known as the white-haired prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in white bristles. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruits, known as tunas, are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor.

Opuntia albisetosa is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water to survive.
