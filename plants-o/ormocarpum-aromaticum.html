Ormocarpum aromaticum

Ormocarpum aromaticum, commonly known as the aromatic orchid vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and the presence of fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces distinctive, orb-shaped fruits known as "ormocarps," which are covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are typically alternate, simple, and have a rough texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual and aromatic qualities.
