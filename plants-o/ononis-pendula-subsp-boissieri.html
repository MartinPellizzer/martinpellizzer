Ononis pendula subsp. boissieri

Ononis pendula subsp.

boissieri, commonly known as the hanging sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a low-growing perennial that typically thrives in rocky and sandy soils.

The plant features slender, branched stems that hang down, giving it its common name. Its small, white flowers appear in clusters during the spring and summer months.

It is characterized by its delicate leaves and is often found in coastal and arid environments.
