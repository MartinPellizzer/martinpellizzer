Odontoglossum × cambridgeanum

Odontoglossum × cambridgeanum, commonly known as the Cambridge orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often violet or purple, spurred flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb and long, strap-like leaves. Its flowers are highly fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
