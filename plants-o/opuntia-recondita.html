Opuntia recondita

Opuntia recondita, commonly known as the hidden prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in spines and glochids, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by edible fruits known as tunas. Opuntia recondita is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick stems and performing photosynthesis through its flattened leaves.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local cultures.
