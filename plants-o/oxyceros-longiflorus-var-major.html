Oxyceros longiflorus var. major

Oxyceros longiflorus var. major, commonly known as the large-flowered oxyceros, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are elliptical and have a leathery texture. The plant produces showy, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its woody stems are covered in a thick, greyish bark, and it often grows in rocky or sandy soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
