Onobrychis viciifolia f. nivea

Onobrychis viciifolia f. nivea, commonly known as the white clover variant, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically white or pale green in color, distinguishing it from the more common green clovers. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pale pink to white, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and along roadsides.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
