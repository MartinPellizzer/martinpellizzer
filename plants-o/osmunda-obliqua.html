Osmunda obliqua

Osmunda obliqua, commonly known as the zigzag water fern, is a species of fern native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, zigzag-shaped fronds that are often submerged or floating in freshwater habitats. The plant has a complex life cycle involving both a sporophyte and a gametophyte stage. Its roots form dense mats that help stabilize aquatic sediments and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Osmunda obliqua is also notable for its ability to thrive in a range of water conditions, including stagnant and flowing waters.
