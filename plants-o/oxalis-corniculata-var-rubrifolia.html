Oxalis corniculata var. rubrifolia

Oxalis corniculata var. rubrifolia, commonly known as the red-leaf wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to Asia, particularly found in regions like India and China.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are often reddish in color due to the variety's name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

Oxalis corniculata var. rubrifolia is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
