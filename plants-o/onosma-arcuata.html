Onosma arcuata

Onosma arcuata, commonly known as the wavy onosma, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple, often blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, with a wavy or undulating edge, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Onosma arcuata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
