Olax aschersoniana

Olax aschersoniana, commonly known as the African olive, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Olacaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, fleshy fruits containing a single seed. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry climates.

The plant has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
