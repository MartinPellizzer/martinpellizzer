Ouret monsoniae

Ouret monsoniae, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a rough texture.

Ouret monsoniae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
