Oxytropis columbiana

Oxytropis columbiana, commonly known as the Columbia milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers in late spring. It is often found in areas with well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid climates.

Oxytropis columbiana plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
