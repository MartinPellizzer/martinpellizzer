Oldenlandia auricularia var. procumbens

Oldenlandia auricularia var. procumbens, commonly known as the prostrate earleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with slender stems that lie close to the ground. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along forest edges.
