Ocotea bourgeauviana

Ocotea bourgeauviana, commonly known as the Bornean ocotea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly Borneo.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic, leathery leaves. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and is often found in secondary forest regrowth. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces dark, glossy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife.

Ocotea bourgeauviana is valued for its timber and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
