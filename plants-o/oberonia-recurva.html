Oberonia recurva

Oberonia recurva, commonly known as the zigzag orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, thriving in the understory of tropical rainforests. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a spiral, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Oberonia recurva is valued for its unique morphology and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
