Odontostemma giraldii

Odontostemma giraldii, commonly known as the African orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its slender, often branching stems and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in horticulture, though it remains relatively rare in cultivation.
