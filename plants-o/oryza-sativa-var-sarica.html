Oryza sativa var. sarica

Oryza sativa var. sarica, commonly known as the Sarica rice, is a less commonly cultivated variety of rice native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its short, compact grain and is typically grown in flooded paddy fields. The plant has a robust root system that helps it thrive in wet conditions, and its leaves are broad and lance-shaped. It is primarily used for local consumption rather than commercial export.

Due to its limited cultivation, it is not as well-studied as other rice varieties.
