Ocimum rigidum

Ocimum rigidum, commonly known as wild basil, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically arranged in spikes. Ocimum rigidum is valued for its essential oils, which contain compounds like linalool and eugenol, making it useful in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.

Its adaptability to various soil types and climates makes it a hardy plant for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
