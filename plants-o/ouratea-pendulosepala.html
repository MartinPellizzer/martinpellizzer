Ouratea pendulosepala

Ouratea pendulosepala, commonly known as the hanging-sepaled ouratea, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its pendulous inflorescences and showy white or pale yellow flowers. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical or ovate in shape. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
