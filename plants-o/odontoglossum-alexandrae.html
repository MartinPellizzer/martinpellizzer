Odontoglossum alexandrae

Odontoglossum alexandrae, commonly known as the Alexandra's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, spurred flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and purple. The plant features a pseudobulb, which stores water and nutrients, and produces a single, showy flower per stem. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its beauty and rarity, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
