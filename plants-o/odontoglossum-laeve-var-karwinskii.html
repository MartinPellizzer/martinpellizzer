Odontoglossum laeve var. karwinskii

Odontoglossum laeve var. karwinskii, commonly known as the Karwinski's orchid, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, spurred flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and purple. The plant is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb structure, which helps it store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it thrives in cool, humid environments.

This variety is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
