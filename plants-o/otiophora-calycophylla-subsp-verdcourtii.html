Otiophora calycophylla subsp. verdcourtii

Otiophora calycophylla subsp.

verdcourtii, commonly known as the African rock fig, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its small, opposite leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish pubescence.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
