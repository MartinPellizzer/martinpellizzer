Oxalis cernua f. pleniflora

Oxalis cernua f. pleniflora, commonly known as the full-flowered wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive veined pattern and produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to red. The plant thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens. Its foliage and flowers are often used in ornamental gardening for their attractive appearance.

The species is noted for its ability to spread via rhizomes, forming dense mats in suitable conditions.
