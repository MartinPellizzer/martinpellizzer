Oxalis nipponica var. alpigena

Oxalis nipponica var. alpigena, commonly known as the Japanese forest sorrel, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions. It has a fibrous root system and produces tubers that help it survive in its natural habitat.

Oxalis nipponica var. alpigena is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
