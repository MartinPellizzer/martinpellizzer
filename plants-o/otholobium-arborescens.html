Otholobium arborescens

Otholobium arborescens, commonly known as the tree dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its woody stems and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Otholobium arborescens is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of browse for livestock.
