Olgaea lomonossowii

Olgaea lomonossowii, commonly known as the Siberian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Asia.

It features slender, hairy stems that grow up to 30 cm in height, often branching near the top. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, blooming in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and covered in fine hairs, adapting to harsh environmental conditions.

Olgaea lomonossowii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
