Odontoglossum jamaicense

Odontoglossum jamaicense, commonly known as the Jamaican toothed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is a terrestrial herb with a pseudobulb that stores water and nutrients, supporting its growth in the tropical climate. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, often with intricate patterns. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Odontoglossum jamaicense is valued in horticulture for its beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
