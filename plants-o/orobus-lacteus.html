Orobus lacteus

Orobus lacteus, commonly known as the milkweed vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its latex sap is milky and can be toxic, containing cardiac glycosides.

Orobus lacteus is often found in moist, shaded areas and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
