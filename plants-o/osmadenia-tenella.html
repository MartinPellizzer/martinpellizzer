Osmadenia tenella

Osmadenia tenella, commonly known as the slender ghost orchid, is an epiphytic plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pale green to brownish leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows on the trunks or branches of trees, deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Its flowers, which are usually white or greenish, bloom during the spring and summer months.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Osmadenia tenella is considered a vulnerable species.
