Orchis incarnata var. platyphylla

Orchis incarnata var. platyphylla, commonly known as the broad-leaved pink orchid, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and the flowers are borne on tall, slender stems.

This variety is distinguished by its broader leaves compared to the typical form of Orchis incarnata.
