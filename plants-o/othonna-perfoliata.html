Othonna perfoliata

Othonna perfoliata, commonly known as the spider plant or perfoliate othonna, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, fleshy foliage and its ability to store water in its thick, cylindrical stems. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, round fruits. Its leaves are often arranged in whorls and can have a woolly texture.

Othonna perfoliata is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
