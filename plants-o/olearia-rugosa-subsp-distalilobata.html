Olearia rugosa subsp. distalilobata

Olearia rugosa subsp.

distalilobata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf daisy bush, is a shrub native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its dense, wiry stems and small, often hairy leaves that are narrow and lance-shaped.

The plant produces clusters of white to pale yellow flowers, typically appearing in spring and summer. It thrives in coastal and subalpine environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. Olearia rugosa subsp.

distalilobata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual interest.
