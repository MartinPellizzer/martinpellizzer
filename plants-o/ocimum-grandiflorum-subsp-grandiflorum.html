Ocimum grandiflorum subsp. grandiflorum

Ocimum grandiflorum subsp.

grandiflorum, commonly known as holy basil or tulsi, is a species of basil native to India and widely cultivated for its aromatic leaves. It is an aromatic herb with small, white or light purple flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a strong, spicy scent, and it is often used in traditional medicine and religious rituals. It prefers warm, humid climates and well-drained soil, and it can be grown both as an annual and perennial depending on the region.

Its essential oils contain compounds like eugenol and linalool, which contribute to its medicinal properties.
