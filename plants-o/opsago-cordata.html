Opsago cordata

Opsago cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf arrowhead, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically bright green and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its stems are often reddish in color and can be slightly hairy.

Opsago cordata is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and stream banks.
