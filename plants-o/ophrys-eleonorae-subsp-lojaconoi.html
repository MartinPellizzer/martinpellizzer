Ophrys eleonorae subsp. lojaconoi

Ophrys eleonorae subsp.

lojaconoi, commonly known as the Lojaconoi bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It is characterized by its unique floral morphology, featuring a complex lip that mimics the appearance of a female bee, which plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats, such as forest floors or rocky outcrops, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its flowers are usually pale green or brown, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry of insect forms.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in many regions.
