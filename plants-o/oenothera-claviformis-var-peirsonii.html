Oenothera claviformis var. peirsonii

Oenothera claviformis var. peirsonii, commonly known as the Peirson's evening primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, typical of the genus Oenothera. The plant features linear, hairy leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Its botanical structure reflects adaptations to its native habitat, with features that aid in water conservation and pollination by nocturnal insects.
