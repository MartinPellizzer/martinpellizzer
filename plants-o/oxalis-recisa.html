Oxalis recisa

Oxalis recisa, commonly known as the yellow woodsorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive yellow markings and produces clusters of five-petaled yellow flowers. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Oxalis recisa is a perennial herb that thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking both aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
