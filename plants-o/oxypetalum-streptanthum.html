Oxypetalum streptanthum

Oxypetalum streptanthum, commonly known as the desert bell, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant has a compact, shrub-like growth habit with woody stems and linear, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating drought tolerance and adapted root systems.

The fruit is a dry capsule that releases seeds when mature, contributing to its survival in harsh desert conditions.
