Oncidium micropogon var. chrysopterum

Oncidium micropogon var. chrysopterum, commonly known as the golden torch orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous inflorescences that bear bright yellow to golden-orange flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air. Its flowers are long-lasting, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

The variety is prized for its vibrant color and relatively easy care requirements.
