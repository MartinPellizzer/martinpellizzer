Oenothera aberrans

Oenothera aberrans, commonly known as the yellow evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers that open in the evening and close during the day. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically borne in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Oenothera aberrans is known for its adaptability to dry, sandy soils and its role in supporting local pollinators.
