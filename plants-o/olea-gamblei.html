Olea gamblei

Olea gamblei, commonly known as the Gambier olive, is a small evergreen tree native to the island of Socotra.

It is characterized by its distinctive, waxy leaves that are thick and leathery, often with a bluish-gray hue. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by oval, edible fruits resembling olives. Olea gamblei thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing remarkable adaptability to harsh conditions.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally for various purposes in its native region.
