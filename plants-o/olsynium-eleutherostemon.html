Olsynium eleutherostemon

Olsynium eleutherostemon, commonly known as the African senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers arranged in racemose inflorescences. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, and it is often found in open woodlands and savanna habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions.
