Orchis fuchsii

Orchis fuchsii, commonly known as the purple orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in woodland and meadow habitats, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually arranged in a raceme and are pollinated by insects such as bees and flies.

Orchis fuchsii is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
