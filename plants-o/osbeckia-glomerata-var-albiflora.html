Osbeckia glomerata var. albiflora

Osbeckia glomerata var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered osbeckia, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its clustered, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
