Ononis ramosissima var. gibraltarica

Ononis ramosissima var. gibraltarica, commonly known as Gibraltar wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula and North Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse display of yellow flowers in early spring. The plant has small, linear leaves and thrives in rocky or sandy soils, often found in coastal and mountainous regions. It is drought-tolerant and well adapted to harsh, sunny environments.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and ornamental gardening.
