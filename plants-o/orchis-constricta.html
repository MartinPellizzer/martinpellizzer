Orchis constricta

Orchis constricta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate, purple or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers are often arranged in a spike and are pollinated by insects, particularly bees.

Orchis constricta is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
