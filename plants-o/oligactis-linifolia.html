Oligactis linifolia

Oligactis linifolia, commonly known as the slender leaf mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of host trees.

The plant lacks true roots and absorbs nutrients through its stems, which are modified into haustoria. It is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, often growing in dense forest canopies.

Its botanical features make it an important component of certain ecosystems, though it can be harmful to its host trees.
