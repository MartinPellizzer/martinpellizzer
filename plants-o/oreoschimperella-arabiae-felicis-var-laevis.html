Oreoschimperella arabiae-felicis var. laevis

Oreoschimperella arabiae-felicis var. laevis, commonly known as the smooth desert lichen, is a unique symbiotic organism composed of a cyanobacterium and a fungus.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found on rock surfaces in desert regions. The plant's structure is characterized by a thallus that is smooth and often forms dense mats. It plays a crucial role in soil stabilization and nutrient cycling in harsh desert ecosystems.

Its ability to survive extreme conditions makes it an important indicator species for environmental health in arid zones.
