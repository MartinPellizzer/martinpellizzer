Orchis incarnata var. hyphaematodes

Orchis incarnata var. hyphaematodes, commonly known as the bloody orchis, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family characterized by its vivid red and purple flowers.

This perennial herb typically grows in moist, woodland habitats, thriving in partial shade and well-drained soils. Its tall, slender stems bear clusters of showy, hooded flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is notable for its specialized symbiotic relationship with mycorrhizal fungi, which aid in nutrient absorption.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often found in conservation efforts aimed at preserving native wetland ecosystems.
