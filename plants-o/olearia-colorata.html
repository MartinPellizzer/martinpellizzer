Olearia colorata

Olearia colorata, commonly known as the silverleaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and showy yellow flower heads. The plant typically grows in coastal and subalpine regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Olearia colorata is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
