Ocimum nudicaule

Ocimum nudicaule, commonly known as the slender basil, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic and often medicinal plants. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically lance-shaped and slightly hairy. Its flowers are clustered in whorls and are usually pale purple to white in color.

Ocimum nudicaule is valued for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
