Osteospermum microphyllum

Osteospermum microphyllum, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, densely packed blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Osteospermum microphyllum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
