Ormoloma imrayanum

Ormoloma imrayanum, commonly known as the yellow-flowered orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant with slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically green and glossy. The plant produces striking yellow flowers with intricate patterns, often blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
