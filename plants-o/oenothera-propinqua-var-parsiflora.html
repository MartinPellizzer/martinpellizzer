Oenothera propinqua var. parsiflora

Oenothera propinqua var. parsiflora, commonly known as the pink evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
