Orchis punctulata var. sepulchralis

Orchis punctulata var. sepulchralis, commonly known as the Sepulchral Orchid, is a rare and distinctive orchid species native to the United Kingdom.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers with dark purple spots, which are often described as resembling funeral shrouds, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and is known for its unique floral structure, including a long, slender spur. It blooms from June to August and is considered a priority species for conservation due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity.

Orchis punctulata var. sepulchralis plays an important role in its ecosystem, supporting specific pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitats.
