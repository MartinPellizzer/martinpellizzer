Onopordum acanthium subsp. illyricum

Onopordum acanthium subsp.

illyricum, commonly known as the Illyrian thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is characterized by its spiny, gray-green leaves and large, purple to pink flower heads that bloom in late summer.

The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in steppe and woodland habitats. Its flowers are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its hardy nature and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
