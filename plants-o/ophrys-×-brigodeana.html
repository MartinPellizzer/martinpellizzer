Ophrys × brigodeana

Ophrys × brigodeana, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a rare and fascinating plant that results from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is characterized by its striking, bee-shaped flowers that mimic the appearance of female bees, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant is native to southern Europe and thrives in calcareous soils, often found in open, sunny habitats. Its flowers emit a subtle scent that further enhances its mimicry, attracting male bees for pollination.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, Ophrys × brigodeana is considered a vulnerable species in many regions.
