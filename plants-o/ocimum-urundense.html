Ocimum urundense

Ocimum urundense, commonly known as Brazilian basil, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs such as mint and lavender. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, dark green leaves and small, pale purple flowers. Ocimum urundense is often cultivated for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
