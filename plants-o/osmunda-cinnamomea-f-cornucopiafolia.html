Osmunda cinnamomea f. cornucopiafolia

Osmunda cinnamomea f. cornucopiafolia, commonly known as the cinnamon fern with a horned leaf form, is a fern species characterized by its distinctive fronds.

The plant features a tall, slender, unbranched sterile stalk topped with a large, compound fertile frond. Its leaves are typically bright green and have a unique, elongated shape with a prominent central vein. The fronds are often described as having a "horn-like" appearance, giving rise to its cultivar name.

This variant is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to moist, shaded environments.
