Oxalis virginica

Oxalis virginica, commonly known as wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped lobes and produces small, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial shade. Its flowers bloom in spring, and the plant is often found in forest understories.

Oxalis virginica is characterized by its distinctive leaf structure and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
