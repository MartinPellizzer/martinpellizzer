Oldenlandia linearis var. nana

Oldenlandia linearis var. nana, commonly known as dwarf threadleaf mistflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, linear leaves that are often slightly hairy, and it produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and along stream banks. Its compact form and delicate appearance make it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

It is also known for its ability to thrive in partial shade and its tolerance for wet soils.
