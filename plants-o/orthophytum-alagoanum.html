Orthophytum alagoanum

Orthophytum alagoanum, commonly known as the Alagoas orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to Brazil.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact, hairy foliage and intricate floral structures. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small and colorful, often displaying shades of white, yellow, and orange.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its natural range.
