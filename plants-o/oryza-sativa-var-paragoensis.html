Oryza sativa var. paragoensis

Oryza sativa var. paragoensis, commonly known as the Paragoensis rice, is a less commonly cultivated variety of rice native to Southeast Asia.

This variety is characterized by its short, slender grains and is typically grown in lowland areas with abundant water. It has a high yield potential and is adapted to tropical climates with warm temperatures. The plant exhibits a typical grass-like structure with long, narrow leaves and a panicle inflorescence.

Its botanical features make it an important species for genetic research and rice breeding programs.
