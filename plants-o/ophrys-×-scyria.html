Ophrys × scyria

Ophrys × scyria, commonly known as the bee orchid hybrid, is a fascinating plant in the Orchidaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera, resulting in a plant with unique floral characteristics. The flower resembles a bee, a mimicry strategy that aids in pollination by male bees. Its botanical features include a tall stem with a single, showy flower that has a distinctive lip shaped like a bee's body.

This plant thrives in open, sunny habitats and is often found in meadows and woodland edges across Europe.
