Ophrys druentica

Ophrys druentica, commonly known as the Druentian bee orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which closely resembles a female bee, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brownish in color, with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of insects.

Due to habitat loss and fragmentation, Ophrys druentica is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
