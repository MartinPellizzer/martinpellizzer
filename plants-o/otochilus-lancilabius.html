Otochilus lancilabius

Otochilus lancilabius, commonly known as the narrow-lipped stone orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in shaded or semi-shaded environments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers with distinctive, elongated lips. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the dry, mineral-rich substrates it inhabits.

Otochilus lancilabius is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure and specialized growth habits.
