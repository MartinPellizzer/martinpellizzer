Ocotea mollis

Ocotea mollis, commonly known as the white sand tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and smooth, gray bark. The plant typically grows in humid, lowland forests and can reach heights of up to 20 meters. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces oval, woody fruits that contain a single seed.

Ocotea mollis is valued for its timber and has traditional uses in some indigenous communities.
