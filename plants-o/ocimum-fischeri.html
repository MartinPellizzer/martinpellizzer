Ocimum fischeri

Ocimum fischeri, commonly known as Fischer's basil, is a species of flowering plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. It is often cultivated for its culinary and medicinal uses, similar to other basil species.

Ocimum fischeri thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
