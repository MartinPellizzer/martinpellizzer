Opithandra sinohenryi

Opithandra sinohenryi, commonly known as the Chinese sand pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers in spring, followed by yellowish-orange fruits that are edible. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
