Orchis × durandii

Orchis × durandii, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a natural hybrid between Orchis moria and Orchis mascula.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of striking violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or grassland habitats. It blooms from late spring to early summer, producing showy, spurred orchid flowers.

This hybrid is notable for its ornamental value and is often found in the wild in parts of Europe.
