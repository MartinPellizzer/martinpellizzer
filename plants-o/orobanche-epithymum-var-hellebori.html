Orobanche epithymum var. hellebori

Orobanche epithymum var. hellebori, commonly known as the Christmas orchid, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies on host plants for nutrients.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is often found growing on the roots of other plants such as the hellebore. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which bloom during the winter months. It has a short life cycle and is often considered a rare and elusive species.

Due to its parasitic nature and specific habitat requirements, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
