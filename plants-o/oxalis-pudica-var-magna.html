Oxalis pudica var. magna

Oxalis pudica var. magna, commonly known as the giant wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

The leaves and flowers are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
