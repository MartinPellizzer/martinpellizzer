Osmia squalida

Osmia squalida, commonly known as the squalid orchid bee, is a species of bee native to various regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Apidae and is characterized by its metallic green or blue body coloration. This solitary bee is known for its role as a pollinator, particularly in tropical and subtropical habitats. Osmia squalida typically nests in pre-existing cavities such as hollow stems or branches.

Its botanical significance lies in its contribution to the pollination of native flowering plants.
