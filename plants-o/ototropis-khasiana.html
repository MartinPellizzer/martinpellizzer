Ototropis khasiana

Ototropis khasiana, commonly known as Khasi false brome, is a perennial herb native to the Eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Apiaceae, which includes many aromatic and edible plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest areas and has slender, erect stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinctive pungent odor.

The flowers are small and clustered in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family.
