Odontoglossum lucianianum

Odontoglossum lucianianum, commonly known as the Lucianian orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers with a distinctive lip that is often brightly colored. Its rhizomes are thick and fleshy, allowing it to store moisture and nutrients.

Odontoglossum lucianianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
