Olearia suavis

Olearia suavis, commonly known as the sweet olearia, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has silver-green foliage that reflects light, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to temperate climates.

Olearia suavis is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
