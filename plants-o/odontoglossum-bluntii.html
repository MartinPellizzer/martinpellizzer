Odontoglossum bluntii

Odontoglossum bluntii, commonly known as the Blunt's orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb that stores water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in its high-altitude habitat. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to anchor it in rocky or moist soil.

Odontoglossum bluntii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
