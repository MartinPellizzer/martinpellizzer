Ochna calodendron

Ochna calodendron, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually oval or elliptical in shape. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm, arid climates.

Ochna calodendron is often grown for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
