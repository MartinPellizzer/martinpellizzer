Ornithogalum gregorianum

Ornithogalum gregorianum, commonly known as the Armenian star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, sword-shaped leaves and produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers in the spring. The plant belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its underground bulbous structures that store nutrients. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
