Ochna inermis var. rivae

Ochna inermis var. rivae, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark grey bark and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
