Oxalis prostrata

Oxalis prostrata, commonly known as the creeping wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats. Its stems and leaves are covered in fine hairs, and it has a fibrous root system.

Oxalis prostrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
