Oxytropis condensata

Oxytropis condensata, commonly known as narrowleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, compound leaves. The plant produces yellow to orange pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland ecosystems.

Oxytropis condensata is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.
