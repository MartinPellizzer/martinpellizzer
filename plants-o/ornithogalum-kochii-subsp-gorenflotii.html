Ornithogalum kochii subsp. gorenflotii

Ornithogalum kochii subsp.

gorenflotii, commonly known as the Gorenflot's star-of-Bethlehem, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Africa. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers during the spring.

The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-drained conditions to thrive.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
