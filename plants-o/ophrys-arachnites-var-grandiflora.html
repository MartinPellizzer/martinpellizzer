Ophrys arachnites var. grandiflora

Ophrys arachnites var. grandiflora, commonly known as the giant bee orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that resemble bees, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils in Mediterranean climates. Its flowers are predominantly yellow with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of bees, attracting male bees for pollination.

This variant is notable for its robust size and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
