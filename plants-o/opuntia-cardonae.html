Opuntia cardonae

Opuntia cardonae, commonly known as the Canary Island cactus or cardon, is a species of cactus native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its large, flat, fleshy stems that are typically green and covered in spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by red, fleshy fruits. Opuntia cardonae is well adapted to arid and coastal environments, thriving in full sun and well-drained soils.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
