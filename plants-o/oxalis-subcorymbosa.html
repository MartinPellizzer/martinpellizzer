Oxalis subcorymbosa

Oxalis subcorymbosa, commonly known as the clustered wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow or white. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. It is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Oxalis subcorymbosa is also known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent ground cover in its native habitat.
