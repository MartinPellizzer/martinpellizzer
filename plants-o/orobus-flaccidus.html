Orobus flaccidus

Orobus flaccidus, commonly known as the flaccid spurred gentian, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its flowers are tubular with prominent spurred lobes, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Orobus flaccidus is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
