Oenanthe globulosa var. kunzei

Oenanthe globulosa var. kunzei, commonly known as the narrow-leaved water dropwort, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist or wet environments such as marshes, stream banks, and damp meadows. The plant has slender, upright stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy. Its small, greenish-white flowers are arranged in umbels and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is characterized by its distinctive, rounded fruits that are often referred to as "water droplets," giving it its common name.
