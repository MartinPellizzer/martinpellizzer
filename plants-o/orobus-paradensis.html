Orobus paradensis

Orobus paradensis, commonly known as the paradise orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically display vibrant purple or violet hues with intricate patterns. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, forming pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its often harsh environment. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to minimize water loss.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, Orobus paradensis is highly sought after by botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
