Oenothera blandina

Oenothera blandina, commonly known as the plains evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats like prairies and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Oenothera blandina is part of the evening primrose family (Onagraceae) and is known for its ecological importance in supporting pollinator populations.
