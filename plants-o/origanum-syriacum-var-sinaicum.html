Origanum syriacum var. sinaicum

Origanum syriacum var. sinaicum, commonly known as the Sinai oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region, particularly the Sinai Peninsula.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its essential oils, rich in thymol and carvacrol, contribute to its use in culinary and medicinal applications.

The variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
