Onosma kotschyi var. schiraziana

Onosma kotschyi var. schiraziana, commonly known as the Schirazian wild thyme, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and showy flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to hot, dry climates. Its flowers are usually pale violet or pink, appearing in clusters during the summer months.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
