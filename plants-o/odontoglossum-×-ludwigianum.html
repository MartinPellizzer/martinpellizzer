Odontoglossum × ludwigianum

Odontoglossum × ludwigianum, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andes Mountains.

It is a hybrid between Odontoglossum ludwigii and Odontoglossum × hirtifolium, resulting in a plant with unique floral characteristics. The plant produces showy, colorful flowers with a distinctive lip that is often adorned with intricate patterns. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a horticultural hybrid, showcasing the diversity and adaptability of orchids.
