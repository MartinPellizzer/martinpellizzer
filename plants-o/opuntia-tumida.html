Opuntia tumida

Opuntia tumida, commonly known as the fat hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that are segmented and covered in sharp spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits known as tunas. Opuntia tumida is a succulent, adapted to arid environments with a shallow root system for efficient water absorption.

Its structure and growth patterns make it well-suited for desert conditions and xeriscaping.
