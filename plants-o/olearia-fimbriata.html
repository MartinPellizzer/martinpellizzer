Olearia fimbriata

Olearia fimbriata, commonly known as the silverflower or fimbriated daisy, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silver-blue foliage and clusters of small, daisy-like white flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy or rocky soils. Its leaves are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving them a distinctive silvery appearance.

Olearia fimbriata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained conditions.
