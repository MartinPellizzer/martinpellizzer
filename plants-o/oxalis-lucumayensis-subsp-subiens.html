Oxalis lucumayensis subsp. subiens

Oxalis lucumayensis subsp.

subiens, commonly known as the Andean wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America. It belongs to the family Oxalidaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories, and prefers well-drained, acidic soils. It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and blooms.

The species is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
