Oxalis subtriangularis

Oxalis subtriangularis, commonly known as the triangular wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features trifoliate leaves with three heart-shaped leaflets, often tinged with red or purple veins. The plant produces small, delicate yellow flowers with five petals, typically appearing in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

Oxalis subtriangularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
