Osmoxylon palmatum

Osmoxylon palmatum, commonly known as the yellow wood or yellow cedar, is a member of the Podocarpaceae family.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, light yellow bark. The leaves are simple, alternate, and often spirally arranged, with a leathery texture. Its cones are small and woody, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The plant is native to parts of Southeast Asia and is valued for its durable, lightweight wood.
