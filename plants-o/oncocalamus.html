Oncocalamus

Oncocalamus, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow leaves. These plants are often found in wetland or swampy habitats, where they thrive in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Oncocalamus species are known for their rapid growth and are sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Their ecological importance lies in providing habitat for various wildlife species and contributing to soil stabilization in wetland ecosystems.
