Orthosiphon unyikensis

Orthosiphon unyikensis, commonly known as the African horse-gram plant, is a member of the Lamiaceae family.

It is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Orthosiphon unyikensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
