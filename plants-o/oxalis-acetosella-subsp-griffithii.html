Oxalis acetosella subsp. griffithii

Oxalis acetosella subsp.

griffithii, commonly known as the lesser wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Asia. It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped lobes and produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky areas. Its stems are often prostrate or ascending, and it has a fibrous root system. Oxalis acetosella subsp.

griffithii is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
