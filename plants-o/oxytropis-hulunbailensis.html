Oxytropis hulunbailensis

Oxytropis hulunbailensis, commonly known as the Hulunbaile Astragalus, is a perennial herb native to the grasslands of Inner Mongolia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant has small, compound leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or loamy soils.

Oxytropis hulunbailensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
