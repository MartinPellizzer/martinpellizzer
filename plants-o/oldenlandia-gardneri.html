Oldenlandia gardneri

Oldenlandia gardneri, commonly known as the gardenia vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments. Its foliage consists of opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface.

Oldenlandia gardneri is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and adaptability to various soil types.
