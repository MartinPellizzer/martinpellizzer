Oenothera webbiana

Oenothera webbiana, commonly known as the Webb's evening primrose, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces showy, yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
