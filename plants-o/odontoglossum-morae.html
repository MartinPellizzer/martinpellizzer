Odontoglossum morae

Odontoglossum morae, commonly known as the Morae's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments at high altitudes. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are usually violet or purple, with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals.

Odontoglossum morae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
