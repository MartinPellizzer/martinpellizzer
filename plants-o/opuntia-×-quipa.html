Opuntia × quipa

Opuntia × quipa, commonly known as the hybrid prickly pear, is a cactus species native to South America.

It is a hybrid between Opuntia vulgaris and Opuntia humifusa, resulting in a plant with unique characteristics. The plant features flat, fleshy stems that are often ribbed and covered in spines. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, and it produces edible fruits known as tunas.

Opuntia × quipa is well adapted to arid environments and is often used in traditional medicine and as a food source.
