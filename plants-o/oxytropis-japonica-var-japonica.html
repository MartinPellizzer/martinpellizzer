Oxytropis japonica var. japonica

Oxytropis japonica var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese locoweed, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to temperate climates. It produces small, hairy pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
