Orchis tridentata subsp. commutata

Orchis tridentata subsp.

commutata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stem and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces showy, spurred flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in a loose raceme. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in many regions.
