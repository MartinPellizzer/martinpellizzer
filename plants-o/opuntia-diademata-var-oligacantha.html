Opuntia diademata var. oligacantha

Opuntia diademata var. oligacantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved prickly pear, is a succulent cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems that are often ribbed and covered in spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the growing season, followed by edible, red-fleshed fruits. Its leaves are reduced to small, spiny structures, and it stores water in its thick stems for survival in arid environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in dry climates.
