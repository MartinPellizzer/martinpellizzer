Osmunda adiantifolia

Osmunda adiantifolia, commonly known as the northern shield fern, is a species of fern found in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its large, shield-shaped fronds that are divided into smaller, feather-like segments. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its sporangia are clustered in sori along the margins of the fronds, a common feature in ferns.

Osmunda adiantifolia plays an important role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for various microorganisms and small animals.
