Oryctina scabrida

Oryctina scabrida, commonly known as the roughleaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, rough-textured leaves and produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments within the understory of tropical forests. It has a sympodial growth habit, with each new growth emerging from a rhizome.

Oryctina scabrida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
