Oncidium varicosum var. moortebeekiense

Oncidium varicosum var. moortebeekiense, commonly known as the Moortebeek orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, long-lasting flower spikes that produce clusters of fragrant, yellow-orange blossoms. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its pseudobulbs are somewhat elongated, and its leaves are strap-like, contributing to its overall aesthetic appeal.

This variety is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
