Ocimum basilicum var. pilosum

Ocimum basilicum var. pilosum, commonly known as hairy basil, is a variety of sweet basil characterized by its dense, hairy foliage.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia and thrives in warm, humid climates. The leaves are typically smaller and more wrinkled compared to common basil, giving it a distinct appearance. It is often used in culinary applications, particularly in Mediterranean and Asian cuisines.

This variety is also valued for its aromatic essential oils, which contribute to its medicinal and aromatic properties.
