Ochthocosmus multiflorus var. canaripoensis

Ochthocosmus multiflorus var. canaripoensis, commonly known as the Canary Island dandelion, is a perennial herb native to the Canary Islands.

It features a basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces bright yellow flowers arranged in a single, terminal head, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to arid conditions.
