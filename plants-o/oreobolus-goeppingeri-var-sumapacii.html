Oreobolus goeppingeri var. sumapacii

Oreobolus goeppingeri var. sumapacii, commonly known as the Sumapacii sandplain sedge, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is often found in coastal or inland dune ecosystems.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
