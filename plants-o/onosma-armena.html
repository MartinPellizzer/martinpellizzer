Onosma armena

Onosma armena, commonly known as Armenian hyssop, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
