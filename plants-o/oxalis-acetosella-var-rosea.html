Oxalis acetosella var. rosea

Oxalis acetosella var. rosea, commonly known as the red shamrock, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features trifoliate leaves with three heart-shaped leaflets, often adorned with a distinctive red or pink coloration. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Oxalis acetosella var. rosea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in shady garden settings.
