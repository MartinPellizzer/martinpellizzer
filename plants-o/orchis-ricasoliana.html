Orchis ricasoliana

Orchis ricasoliana, commonly known as the Ricasolia orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing lip-shaped petals that are often marked with intricate patterns. Orchis ricasoliana prefers moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.

It is a protected species in several European countries due to its limited distribution and ecological significance.
