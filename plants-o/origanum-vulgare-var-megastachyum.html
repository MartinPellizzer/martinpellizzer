Origanum vulgare var. megastachyum

Origanum vulgare var. megastachyum, commonly known as Greek oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves with a strong, pungent scent due to its high concentration of essential oils. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or container planting.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for culinary and medicinal uses.
