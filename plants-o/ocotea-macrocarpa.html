Ocotea macrocarpa

Ocotea macrocarpa, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark reddish-brown heartwood. The plant has simple, alternate, and often elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically white or pale yellow, and it produces small, dark blue to black berries.

Ocotea macrocarpa is valued for its hard, durable wood, which has been historically used in furniture and musical instruments.
