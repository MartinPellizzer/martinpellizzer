Osbeckia coronata

Osbeckia coronata, commonly known as the crowned goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and open habitats. The plant features alternate, oval-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences, giving it its common name.

Osbeckia coronata is known for its ecological importance as a food source for various insects and wildlife.
