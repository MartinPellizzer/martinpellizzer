Oxalis taquetii

Oxalis taquetii, commonly known as the Taquet's wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, and it produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds.

Oxalis taquetii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
