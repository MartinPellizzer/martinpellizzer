Oenothera × fallax

Oenothera × fallax, commonly known as the hybrid evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a cross between Oenothera biennis and Oenothera glauca, resulting in a species with distinctive botanical characteristics. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers, which open in the evening, are yellow to orange in color and attract nocturnal pollinators like moths.

Oenothera × fallax is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in disturbed habitats.
