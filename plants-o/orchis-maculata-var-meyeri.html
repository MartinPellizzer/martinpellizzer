Orchis maculata var. meyeri

Orchis maculata var. meyeri, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodlands and meadows. The plant features a single, tall stem with a single, showy violet or purple flower that has a distinctive yellowish-green keel. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette at the base.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
