Ornithogalum tuberosum

Ornithogalum tuberosum, commonly known as the star of Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern and eastern Europe.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is characterized by its underground tubers, which are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some cultures. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ornithogalum tuberosum is also known for its toxic properties, containing compounds that can be harmful if ingested.
