Oxalis knuthiana

Oxalis knuthiana, commonly known as the Knuth's wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features trifoliate leaves with three heart-shaped leaflets and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of yellow or orange. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and forest floors. It is characterized by its rhizomatous root system and ability to spread vegetatively.

Oxalis knuthiana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable conditions.
