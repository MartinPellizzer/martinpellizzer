Odontites violaceus var. lapiei

Odontites violaceus var. lapiei, commonly known as the violet toothed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has finely toothed leaves that are often tinged with purple, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
