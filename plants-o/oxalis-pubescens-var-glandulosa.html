Oxalis pubescens var. glandulosa

Oxalis pubescens var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Oxalis pubescens var. glandulosa is often grown as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
