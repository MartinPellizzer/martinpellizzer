Opuntia staffordiae

Opuntia staffordiae, commonly known as the Stafford's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of western Australia.

It is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems that are covered in spines and glochids, which provide protection against herbivores and excessive water loss. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months, followed by edible, red-fleshed fruits. Its succulent leaves store water, allowing it to thrive in harsh, dry environments.

Opuntia staffordiae is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses by Indigenous communities.
