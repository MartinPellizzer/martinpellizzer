Odontonema strictum

Odontonema strictum, commonly known as the red-veined ivy, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and red veins. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in clusters, typically in shades of pink or red, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used as a ground cover or in hanging baskets.

Odontonema strictum is valued for its ornamental foliage and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
