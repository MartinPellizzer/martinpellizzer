Oenothera hookeri var. franciscana

Oenothera hookeri var. franciscana, commonly known as the Franciscan evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to California.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

This variety is part of the Onagraceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
