Ophrys palaestina

Ophrys palaestina, commonly known as the Palestine orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to Israel and the Palestinian territories.

It is characterized by its intricate, bee-like flowers that mimic the appearance of female bees, a feature that aids in its unique pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in rocky, dry habitats and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brown, with a distinct lip that serves as a landing platform for pollinators.

Due to habitat loss and fragmentation, Ophrys palaestina is considered vulnerable and is of significant ecological and botanical interest.
