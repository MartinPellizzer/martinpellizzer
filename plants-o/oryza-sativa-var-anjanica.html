Oryza sativa var. anjanica

Oryza sativa var. anjanica, commonly known as the Anjanica rice, is a traditional variety of rice native to India.

It is characterized by its slender grains and is typically cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has a short stature and exhibits a high degree of genetic diversity, which makes it valuable for breeding programs. It requires a warm climate and abundant water for optimal growth.

Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a panicle inflorescence that produces small, greenish-white flowers.
