Oreogrammitis fasciata

Oreogrammitis fasciata, commonly known as the stripe-leaf moss, is a species of liverwort belonging to the family Porellaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems that form dense mats on rocky surfaces, particularly in moist, shaded environments. The leaves are linear and often have distinct midribs, with a pale green to yellowish coloration. This moss is typically found in temperate regions, growing on limestone or slate substrates.

Its reproductive structures, known as sporophytes, are relatively simple and consist of a capsule that releases spores into the air.
