Oberonia microphylla

Oberonia microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved Oberonia, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit and possesses small, lanceolate leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating. The plant produces intricate, fragrant orchid flowers that are usually pale yellow to greenish in color, with a distinctive spurred structure. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, organic-rich substrates for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in specialized orchid collections.
