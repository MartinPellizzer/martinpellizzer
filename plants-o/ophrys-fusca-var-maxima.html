Ophrys fusca var. maxima

Ophrys fusca var. maxima, commonly known as the Greater Heath Orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dark green, hairy leaves and produces striking, bee-mimicking flowers with a distinctive brownish-purple coloration. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and heathlands. It has a short flowering period in late spring to early summer.

The species is known for its specialized pollination mechanism, attracting male bees through visual and chemical mimicry.
