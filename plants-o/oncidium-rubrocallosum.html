Oncidium rubrocallosum

Oncidium rubrocallosum, commonly known as the red-collared orchid, is a tropical orchid species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, long-lasting inflorescence that features vibrant orange and red flowers with contrasting yellow markings. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its pseudobulbs are short and stout, supporting its evergreen foliage.

Oncidium rubrocallosum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
