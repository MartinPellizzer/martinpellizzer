Orchis orbiculata

Orchis orbiculata, commonly known as the round-leaf orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers with a distinctive hooded lip. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodlands or meadows. Its leaves are rounded and broad, contributing to its common name.

Orchis orbiculata is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
