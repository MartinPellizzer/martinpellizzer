Oenothera indecora

Oenothera indecora, commonly known as the showy evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Oenothera indecora is a member of the evening primrose family (Onagraceae) and is known for its ecological importance in supporting local pollinator populations.
