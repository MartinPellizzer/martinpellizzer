Ocotea bracteosa

Ocotea bracteosa, commonly known as the Brazilian camphor tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Venezuela. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage and aromatic leaves, which contain essential oils. It produces small, white, fragrant flowers followed by oval, dark blue berries.

Ocotea bracteosa is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
