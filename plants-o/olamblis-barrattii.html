Olamblis barrattii

Olamblis barrattii, commonly known as the Barratt's ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the tropical regions of northern Queensland, Australia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale greenish-white flowers that bloom during the cooler months. The plant typically grows on the trunks of rainforest trees, forming a symbiotic relationship with specific mycorrhizal fungi. Its leaves are narrow and strap-like, adapted to the humid, shaded environment of its habitat.

Due to its restricted distribution and sensitivity to environmental changes, Olamblis barrattii is considered a vulnerable species.
