Odontoglossum × cookianum

Odontoglossum × cookianum, commonly known as the Cook's orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically display a mix of purple, pink, and white hues. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, supporting its growth in high-altitude environments. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, adapted to withstand cool, humid conditions.

Odontoglossum × cookianum is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively ease of cultivation under proper care.
