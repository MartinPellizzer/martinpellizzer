Orchis latifolia var. divaricata

Orchis latifolia var. divaricata, commonly known as the broad-leaved orchid variant with branching stems, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or grassland habitats. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces tall, spike-like inflorescences with violet or purple flowers. It is characterized by its distinctive, often fragrant blooms and is part of the Orchidaceae family.

This variant is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental value in horticulture.
