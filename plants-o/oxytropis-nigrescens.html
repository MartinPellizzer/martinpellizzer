Oxytropis nigrescens

Oxytropis nigrescens, commonly known as blackstem locoweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its erect stems that turn dark purple or black as they mature, and its small, pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant belongs to the legume family (Fabaceae) and has compound leaves with small leaflets. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sagebrush steppe and grasslands.

Oxytropis nigrescens is known for its toxic alkaloids, which can be harmful to livestock.
