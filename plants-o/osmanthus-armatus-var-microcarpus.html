Osmanthus armatus var. microcarpus

Osmanthus armatus var. microcarpus, commonly known as the small-fruited osmanthus, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, smooth, dark brown fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its aromatic properties make it valuable in traditional Chinese medicine and as a source of essential oils.
