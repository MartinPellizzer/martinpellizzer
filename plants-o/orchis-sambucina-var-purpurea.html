Orchis sambucina var. purpurea

Orchis sambucina var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple twayblade, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Europe.

It is a small, herbaceous plant with a distinctive appearance, featuring a single, slender stem that bears two opposite leaves. The plant produces a spike of pale purple to violet flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

The species is characterized by its lack of roots and its reliance on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.
