Osmunda cinnamomea var. bipinnatifida

Osmunda cinnamomea var. bipinnatifida, commonly known as the cinnamon fern, is a species of fern characterized by its distinctive fronds and spore-producing structures.

It features a tall, slender rhizome with a cinnamon-colored sheath, from which large, bipinnate fronds emerge. The fronds are composed of multiple pairs of pinnae, giving the plant a lush, feathery appearance. It thrives in moist, shady environments, often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.

This plant plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and contributing to nutrient cycling in forest ecosystems.
