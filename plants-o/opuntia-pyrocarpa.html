Opuntia pyrocarpa

Opuntia pyrocarpa, commonly known as the fire barrel cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tunas, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

Opuntia pyrocarpa plays an important role in desert ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
