Olearia stricta subsp. parvilobata

Olearia stricta subsp.

parvilobata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved daisy bush, is a shrub native to New Zealand. It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance.

The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in dense, daisy-like clusters, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native habitats.
