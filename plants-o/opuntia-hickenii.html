Opuntia hickenii

Opuntia hickenii, commonly known as the Hicken's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, spineless pads and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems. Its fruit, known as a tunah, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

Opuntia hickenii plays an important role in its ecosystem, providing food and shelter for various desert animals.
