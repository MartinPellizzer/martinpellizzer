Oxytropis bilocularis

Oxytropis bilocularis, commonly known as the two-chambered locoweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its seeds are enclosed in a pod that splits open when mature, dispersing the seeds by wind.

Oxytropis bilocularis is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility in nutrient-poor environments.
