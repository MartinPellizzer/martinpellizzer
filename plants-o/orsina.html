Orsina

Orsina, commonly known as the Christmas rose, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its early blooming habit, often flowering in late winter or early spring. The plant features a low-growing rosette of velvety, dark green leaves and produces showy, single or double white or pink flowers. It thrives in cool, shady environments with well-drained soil.

Orsina is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique blooming season.
