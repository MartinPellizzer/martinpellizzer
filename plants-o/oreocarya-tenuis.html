Oreocarya tenuis

Oreocarya tenuis, commonly known as the slender mountain heath, is a low-growing shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its botanical structure is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions, including cold temperatures and strong winds.

Oreocarya tenuis plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
