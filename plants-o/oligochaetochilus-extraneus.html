Oligochaetochilus extraneus

Oligochaetochilus extraneus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant prefers shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories. Its botanical features include a short rhizome and a single, erect stem bearing several lance-shaped leaves.

The flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with a distinctive three-lobed lip.
