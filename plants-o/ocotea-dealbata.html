Ocotea dealbata

Ocotea dealbata, commonly known as the white ocotea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as cinnamon and bay laurel. The tree is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. Its fragrant flowers are typically white or pale yellow, and it produces small, dark blue berries.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
