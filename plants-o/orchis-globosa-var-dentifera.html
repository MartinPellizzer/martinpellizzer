Orchis globosa var. dentifera

Orchis globosa var. dentifera, commonly known as the toothed globeflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its globular, clustered inflorescence and distinctive toothed leaves. The flowers are typically pink or purple, with a spurred lip that aids in pollination by insects. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinator populations.
