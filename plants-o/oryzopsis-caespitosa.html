Oryzopsis caespitosa

Oryzopsis caespitosa, commonly known as western wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in grasslands and disturbed sites.

Oryzopsis caespitosa is valued for its drought tolerance and is used in ecological restoration projects.
