Oenothera heribaudii

Oenothera heribaudii, commonly known as the Heribaud's evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening and close by morning. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.
