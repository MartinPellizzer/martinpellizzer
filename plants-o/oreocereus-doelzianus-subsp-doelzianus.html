Oreocereus doelzianus subsp. doelzianus

Oreocereus doelzianus subsp.

doelzianus, commonly known as the Doelzian's moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America. It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and thick, fleshy stems.

The plant typically features spiny areoles and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the warmer months. It is adapted to survive in dry environments with minimal water, storing moisture in its succulent stems.

This subspecies is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
