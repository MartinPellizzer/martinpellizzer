Oxytenanthera bourdillonii

Oxytenanthera bourdillonii, commonly known as the golden cupid, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which are typically arranged in dense, spherical clusters. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and grows as a perennial herb. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in grasslands and open habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking colorful, drought-tolerant plants.
