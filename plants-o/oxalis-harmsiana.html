Oxalis harmsiana

Oxalis harmsiana, commonly known as the yellow wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive yellowish-green color and produces small, delicate flowers that are typically pink or white. The plant belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Oxalis harmsiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its rapid growth and ability to spread make it a popular choice for ground cover in gardens.
