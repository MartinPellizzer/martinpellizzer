Oxygonum alatum var. longisquamatum

Oxygonum alatum var. longisquamatum, commonly known as the winged spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, winged stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of blue or purple. The leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
