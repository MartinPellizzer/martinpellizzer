Oenothera sackvillensis var. royfraseri

Oenothera sackvillensis var. royfraseri, commonly known as the Roy Fraser's evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening and close by dawn. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often in sandy or rocky soils. It has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique blooming habit.
