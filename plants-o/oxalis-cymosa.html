Oxalis cymosa

Oxalis cymosa, commonly known as the clustered wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive veins and small, delicate flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. It produces small, edible berries that are popular in some regions for culinary use.

Oxalis cymosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
