Ostodes pauciflora

Ostodes pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial herb characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and clustered leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically arranged in a spike. It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a delicate and endangered species.
