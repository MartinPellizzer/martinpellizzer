Orbignya cuatrecasasiana

Orbignya cuatrecasasiana, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a large evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces distinctive, woody pods containing edible seeds. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall and has a dense canopy that provides habitat for various wildlife species. Its flowers are small and yellowish-green, and it relies on specific pollinators for reproduction.

The fruit, known as a cupuacu, is used in traditional medicine and has a high nutritional value.
