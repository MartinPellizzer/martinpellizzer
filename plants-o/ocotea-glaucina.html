Ocotea glaucina

Ocotea glaucina, commonly known as the white ocotea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and elliptical, glossy dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically white or pale yellow, and it produces berries that are an important food source for various wildlife.

Ocotea glaucina is often found in moist, shaded habitats within tropical and subtropical forests.
