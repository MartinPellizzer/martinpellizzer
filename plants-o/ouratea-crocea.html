Ouratea crocea

Ouratea crocea, commonly known as the golden mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant relies on host trees for water and nutrients, while producing its own food through photosynthesis. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that contains seeds dispersed by birds.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological interactions and medicinal properties.
