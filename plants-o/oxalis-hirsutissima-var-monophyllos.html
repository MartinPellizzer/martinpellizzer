Oxalis hirsutissima var. monophyllos

Oxalis hirsutissima var. monophyllos, commonly known as the woolly wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with a dense covering of soft, white hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and early summer. Its leaves and flowers are often used in ornamental gardening due to their attractive appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It prefers full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.
