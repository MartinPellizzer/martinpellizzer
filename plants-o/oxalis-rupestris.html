Oxalis rupestris

Oxalis rupestris, commonly known as the rock oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and features fleshy, succulent leaves that are often reddish or purplish in color. The plant produces clusters of small, delicate flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Oxalis rupestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
