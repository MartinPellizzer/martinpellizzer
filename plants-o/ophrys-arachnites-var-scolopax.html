Ophrys arachnites var. scolopax

Ophrys arachnites var. scolopax, commonly known as the scarlet helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bee-like floral structure that mimics the appearance of a wasp, aiding in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist woodlands and has lance-shaped leaves with a dark green upper surface. Its flowers are usually pink to orange-red, with a prominent, hairy lip that serves as a landing platform for pollinators.

This orchid is a protected species in many regions due to its ecological significance and declining populations.
