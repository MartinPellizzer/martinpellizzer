Olearia homolepis

Olearia homolepis, commonly known as the New Zealand daisy or mountain daisy, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained soils. It is often found in scrublands and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

Olearia homolepis plays an important role in the local ecosystem, providing habitat and nectar for native pollinators.
