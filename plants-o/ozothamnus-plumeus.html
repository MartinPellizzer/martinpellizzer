Ozothamnus plumeus

Ozothamnus plumeus, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It has a low-growing, spreading habit with branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves.

Ozothamnus plumeus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its drought tolerance and visual interest.
