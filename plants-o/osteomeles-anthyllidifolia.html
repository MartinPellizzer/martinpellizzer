Osteomeles anthyllidifolia

Osteomeles anthyllidifolia, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces woody, cylindrical fruit capsules that split open to release seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to tolerate partial shade.
