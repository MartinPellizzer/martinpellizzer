Ormopteris cymbiformis

Ormopteris cymbiformis, commonly known as the broad-leaf huperzia, is a species of clubmoss belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage composed of broad, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often curled at the edges. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Its sporophytes are small, with sporangia arranged in clusters on the undersides of the leaves.

Ormopteris cymbiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
