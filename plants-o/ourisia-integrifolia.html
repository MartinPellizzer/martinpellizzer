Ourisia integrifolia

Ourisia integrifolia, commonly known as the smoothleaf ourisia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the family Primulaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are typically smooth or only slightly hairy along the edges. Its flowers are small, bell-shaped, and usually pink or white, appearing in clusters at the base of the stems.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along streambanks.
