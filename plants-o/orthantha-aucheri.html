Orthantha aucheri

Orthantha aucheri, commonly known as the common coltsfoot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves and a flowering stem that bears clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant is often found in moist, disturbed soils along roadsides and in meadows. It has a taproot system that allows it to survive in various environmental conditions.

Orthantha aucheri is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
