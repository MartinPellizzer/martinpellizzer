Ocotea purpurea

Ocotea purpurea, commonly known as purple ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and wood. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests. Its leaves are dark green on the upper surface and purplish underneath, giving it its common name.

The tree is valued for its fragrant timber and is also used in traditional medicine.
