Orchiodes cordatum

Orchiodes cordatum, commonly known as the heartleaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces strikingly colorful flowers that range from white to purple, often with intricate patterns. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts around the world.
