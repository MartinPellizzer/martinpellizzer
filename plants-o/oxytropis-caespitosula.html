Oxytropis caespitosula

Oxytropis caespitosula, commonly known as the bush clover, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Oxytropis caespitosula is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it ecologically significant in certain ecosystems.
