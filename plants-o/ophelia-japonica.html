Ophelia japonica

Ophelia japonica, commonly known as Japanese water-plantain, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that grow submerged or floating in water. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. Ophelia japonica thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

It is often used in ornamental water gardens due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of cultivation.
