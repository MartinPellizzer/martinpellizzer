Orchis × lloydianiformis

Orchis × lloydianiformis, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a fascinating blend of species within the Orchidaceae family.

It exhibits a mix of characteristics from its parent species, often displaying vibrant purple or pink flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodlands or wet meadows, where it can thrive. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it prefers cool, humid conditions for optimal growth.
