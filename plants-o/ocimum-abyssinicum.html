Ocimum abyssinicum

Ocimum abyssinicum, commonly known as Ethiopian basil, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia and parts of East Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and rosemary. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and dark green, slightly hairy leaves. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its culinary and medicinal uses.

Its essential oils contain compounds like linalool and eugenol, contributing to its distinctive aroma and potential health benefits.
