Ophrys × braun-blanquetiana subsp. mirellae

Ophrys × braun-blanquetiana subsp.

mirellae, commonly known as the Mirella bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its intricate floral structure, which closely resembles a female bee, a feature evolved to attract male bees for pollination.

The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and thrives in sunny, open habitats. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brown, with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of a bee's body.

This subspecies is valued for its unique morphology and ecological role in pollination systems.
