Oenothera eulobus

Oenothera eulobus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf evening primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and wet meadows. The plant features slender, branching stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers, which bloom in the evening, are yellow with four petals and are pollinated by moths.

Oenothera eulobus is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
