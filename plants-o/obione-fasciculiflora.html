Obione fasciculiflora

Obione fasciculiflora, commonly known as the clustered sandwort, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and dense clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its flowers are typically arranged in compact inflorescences, and it produces small, oval-shaped seeds.

Obione fasciculiflora plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and provides nectar for pollinators in its native habitat.
