Orobus arborescens

Orobus arborescens, commonly known as the tree orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody stems and clusters of fragrant, orchid-like flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a silvery underside that reflects light.

Orobus arborescens is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
