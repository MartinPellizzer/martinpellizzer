Oncidium serratum

Oncidium serratum, commonly known as the serrated orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in clusters of yellow, orange, or red hues. The plant has strap-like leaves and a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining potting media.

Oncidium serratum is popular among orchid growers for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
