Omphalea bracteata var. pedicellaris

Omphalea bracteata var. pedicellaris, commonly known as the pedicellaris moonflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night, emitting a strong, sweet fragrance to attract nocturnal pollinators. The plant has a woody stem with opposite, broad ovate leaves that are often hairy. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release numerous small, smooth seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting nighttime pollinators.
