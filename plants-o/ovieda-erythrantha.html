Ovieda erythrantha

Ovieda erythrantha, commonly known as the red-flowered ovieda, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking red petals and lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in cloud forests. Its flowers bloom in late spring and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ovieda erythrantha is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
