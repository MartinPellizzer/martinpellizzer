Opilia bruneelii

Opilia bruneelii, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has a fibrous root system and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Opilia bruneelii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
