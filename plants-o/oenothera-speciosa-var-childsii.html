Oenothera speciosa var. childsii

Oenothera speciosa var. childsii, commonly known as the Childs' evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
