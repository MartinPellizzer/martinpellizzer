Odontoglossum pygmaeum

Odontoglossum pygmaeum, commonly known as the miniature orchid, is a small, epiphytic orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It typically grows on tree branches and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink. It is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its compact size and ornamental value.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments to ensure its survival.
