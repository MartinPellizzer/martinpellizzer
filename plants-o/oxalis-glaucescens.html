Oxalis glaucescens

Oxalis glaucescens, commonly known as the blue oxalis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features delicate, trifoliate leaves that are often tinged with a bluish hue, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink or purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Oxalis glaucescens is noted for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
