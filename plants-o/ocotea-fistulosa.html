Ocotea fistulosa

Ocotea fistulosa, commonly known as the yellow sandarac tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and fragrant, yellowish wood. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible for some bird species. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils, which have been used in traditional medicine.

The tree is also valued for its durable wood, which is used in construction and craftsmanship.
