Oligothrix newtonii

Oligothrix newtonii, commonly known as the Newton's oligothrix, is a species of green algae belonging to the family Oligothricaceae.

It is typically found in freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes, where it forms dense, mat-like colonies on submerged surfaces. The plant is characterized by its filamentous structure, with cells arranged in unbranched chains. It lacks true roots, stems, and leaves, and instead relies on photosynthesis for energy.

Oligothrix newtonii plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and serving as a food source for microorganisms and small aquatic organisms.
