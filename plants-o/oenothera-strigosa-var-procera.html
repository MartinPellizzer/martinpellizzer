Oenothera strigosa var. procera

Oenothera strigosa var. procera, commonly known as the tall evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving it a distinctive texture.

The species is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and open habitats.
