Ocotea glabriflora

Ocotea glabriflora, commonly known as the white sand ocotea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by dark blue, fleshy berries. It thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments and is often found in humid, lowland forests.

Ocotea glabriflora is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some indigenous communities.
