Oxytropis middendorffii subsp. submiddendorffii

Oxytropis middendorffii subsp.

submiddendorffii, commonly known as the Siberian locoweed, is a perennial herb native to the steppes and semi-deserts of Central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, compound leaves.

The plant produces yellow or pale yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system to access water from deeper soil layers.

This subspecies is also known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock.
