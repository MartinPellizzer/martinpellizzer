Olea javanica var. laxiflora

Olea javanica var. laxiflora, commonly known as the Javan olive, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy fruits that are typically yellow when ripe. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and partial shade.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
