Oldenlandia triflora

Oldenlandia triflora, commonly known as the three-flowered spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in clusters of three, giving it its common name. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Oldenlandia triflora is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
