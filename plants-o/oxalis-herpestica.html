Oxalis herpestica

Oxalis herpestica, commonly known as the mouse-ear oxalis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that features heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Oxalis herpestica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and blooms.
