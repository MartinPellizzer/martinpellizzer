Oenothera ravenii subsp. argentinae

Oenothera ravenii subsp.

argentinae, commonly known as the Argentine evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to South America. It is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
