Oncocalamus mannii

Oncocalamus mannii, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, clumping grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its massive, hollow stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a bluish-green hue.

Oncocalamus mannii plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
