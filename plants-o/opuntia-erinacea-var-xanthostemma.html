Opuntia erinacea var. xanthostemma

Opuntia erinacea var. xanthostemma, commonly known as the yellow-flowered prickly pear, is a succulent cactus native to Mexico.

It features flat, ribbed pads covered in spines and produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer. The plant is characterized by its dense, branching structure and is adapted to arid environments. Its pads can be harvested for food and its flowers are often used in traditional medicine.

The variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh climates.
