Orthochilus zeyheri

Orthochilus zeyheri, commonly known as the Zeyher's rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal or mountainous regions. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant, yellow-green flowers. It is adapted to arid conditions and has a short growing season.

Orthochilus zeyheri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
