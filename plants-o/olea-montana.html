Olea montana

Olea montana, commonly known as the mountain olive, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. Olea montana is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
