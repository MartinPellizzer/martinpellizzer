Oplonia multigemma

Oplonia multigemma, commonly known as the multigemma oplonia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable candidate for garden cultivation.

Oplonia multigemma is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
