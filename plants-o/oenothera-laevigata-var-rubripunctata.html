Oenothera laevigata var. rubripunctata

Oenothera laevigata var. rubripunctata, commonly known as the red-spotted evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers have four petals and are typically borne in clusters at the ends of the stems.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens.
