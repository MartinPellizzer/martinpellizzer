Ocotea acutangula

Ocotea acutangula, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests. Its leaves are elongated, glossy, and have a distinct, aromatic scent.

Ocotea acutangula is valued for its wood, which is used in furniture and construction, as well as for its potential medicinal properties.
