Ophrys × pauliana

Ophrys × pauliana, commonly known as the mimic orchid, is a hybrid species within the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its intricate floral structure, which closely resembles female insects, particularly bees, to facilitate pollination through sexual deception. The plant typically grows in temperate regions of Europe and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brown, with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of insect bodies.

Ophrys × pauliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a significant role in ecological studies due to its unique pollination strategy.
