Ocimum kenyense

Ocimum kenyense, commonly known as Kenyan basil, is a perennial herb native to East Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like mint and lavender. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, dark green leaves and small, purple flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its culinary and medicinal properties.

Ocimum kenyense is also valued for its potential use in traditional medicine.
