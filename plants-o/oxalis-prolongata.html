Oxalis prolongata

Oxalis prolongata, commonly known as the longleaf wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. Its stems are often prostrate or ascending, and it has fibrous roots that help stabilize soil.

Oxalis prolongata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
