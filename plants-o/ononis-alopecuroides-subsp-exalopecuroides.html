Ononis alopecuroides subsp. exalopecuroides

Ononis alopecuroides subsp.

exalopecuroides, commonly known as the hairy whitlow grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae. It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with hairy stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant produces small, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, typical of many legumes. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or dry environments.

This subspecies is noted for its drought tolerance and ability to colonize disturbed areas.
