Odontophyllum phainum

Odontophyllum phainum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, golden-yellow inflorescences. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are fibrous, and it has strap-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating.

Odontophyllum phainum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
