Opuntia megasperma var. mesophytica

Opuntia megasperma var. mesophytica, commonly known as the coastal prickly pear, is a succulent cactus native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in spines and small, fleshy fruits. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with high salt exposure. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, and it produces edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines.

This variety is notable for its resilience to harsh coastal conditions and its role in local ecosystems.
