Ochna afzelioides

Ochna afzelioides, commonly known as the African hibiscus, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often glandular-dotted. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Ochna afzelioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
