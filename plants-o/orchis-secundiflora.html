Orchis secundiflora

Orchis secundiflora, commonly known as the lesser orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and grasslands. The plant features a slender, upright stem that bears a single spike of pale purple to white flowers. Its leaves are elongated and lance-shaped, often with a distinct midrib.

Orchis secundiflora is characterized by its specialized root system and is part of the Orchidaceae family, which includes many other orchid species.
