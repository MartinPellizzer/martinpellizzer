Ocotea umbrosa

Ocotea umbrosa, commonly known as the dark ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, aromatic bark. It typically grows in shaded, humid environments and can reach heights of up to 30 meters.

Ocotea umbrosa is valued for its wood, which is used in furniture and cabinetry, as well as for its essential oils.
