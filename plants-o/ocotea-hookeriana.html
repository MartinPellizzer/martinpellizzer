Ocotea hookeriana

Ocotea hookeriana, commonly known as the Hooker's ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a dark blue drupe, often containing a single seed.

Ocotea hookeriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
