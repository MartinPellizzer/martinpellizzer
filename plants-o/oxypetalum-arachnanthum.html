Oxypetalum arachnanthum

Oxypetalum arachnanthum, commonly known as the spider-thorn or spider-tailed arum, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. Its leaves are usually broad and arrow-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Oxypetalum arachnanthum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
