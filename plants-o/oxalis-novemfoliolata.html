Oxalis novemfoliolata

Oxalis novemfoliolata, commonly known as the nine-leaved wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, which are often divided into nine leaflets, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

Oxalis novemfoliolata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
