Oenothera walpersii

Oenothera walpersii, commonly known as the Walpers' evening primrose, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. The plant produces showy yellow or orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Oenothera walpersii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicinal practices.
