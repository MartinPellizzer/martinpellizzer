Ophrys fusca lusus ramosis

Ophrys fusca lusus ramosis, commonly known as the ramose bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to specific regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of female bees to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone habitats, preferring well-drained soils and sunny to partially shaded conditions. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with intricate patterns that aid in deception of pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
