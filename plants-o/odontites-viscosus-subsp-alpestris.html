Odontites viscosus subsp. alpestris

Odontites viscosus subsp.

alpestris, commonly known as the alpine sticky rockcress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, often in mountainous areas.

The plant features basal rosettes of fleshy, hairy leaves that are often covered in a sticky substance. Its small, yellowish-white flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in early summer.

It is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine meadows and scree slopes.
