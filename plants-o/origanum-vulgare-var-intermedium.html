Origanum vulgare var. intermedium

Origanum vulgare var. intermedium, commonly known as marjoram, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and small, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in summer, which are followed by small, dark brown seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates.

Marjoram is widely used in culinary applications and traditional herbal medicine for its aromatic, slightly sweet scent and flavor.
