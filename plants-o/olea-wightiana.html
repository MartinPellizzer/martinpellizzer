Olea wightiana

Olea wightiana, commonly known as the Wight's olive, is a small evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy drupes that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Olea wightiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
