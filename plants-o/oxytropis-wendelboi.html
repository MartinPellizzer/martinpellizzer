Oxytropis wendelboi

Oxytropis wendelboi, commonly known as Wendelbo's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellowish to reddish flowers in elongated clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Oxytropis wendelboi plays a role in stabilizing soils and supporting local pollinator populations.
