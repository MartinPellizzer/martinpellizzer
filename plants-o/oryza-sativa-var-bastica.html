Oryza sativa var. bastica

Oryza sativa var. bastica, commonly known as the Bastam rice, is a lesser-known variety of rice native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its short, compact grains and a distinct flavor profile compared to common rice varieties. The plant typically grows in shallow water or wetland environments, thriving in tropical and subtropical climates. Its botanical features include a robust root system and a grass-like appearance with narrow, parallel-veined leaves.

This variety is valued for its adaptability and potential use in traditional and sustainable agricultural practices.
