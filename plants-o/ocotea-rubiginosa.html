Ocotea rubiginosa

Ocotea rubiginosa, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark reddish-brown wood, which is highly valued for its durability and scent. The plant has simple, alternate, elliptical leaves that are glossy and often have a waxy texture. Its small, white flowers are followed by dark blue berries that contain the seeds.

Ocotea rubiginosa is also noted for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional medicine.
