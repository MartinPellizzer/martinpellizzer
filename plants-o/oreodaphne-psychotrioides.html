Oreodaphne psychotrioides

Oreodaphne psychotrioides, commonly known as the Malayan star anise, is a member of the Lauraceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, including parts of Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers that lead to the production of fruit resembling star anise. Its botanical features include elliptical leaves with a smooth surface and a distinct, fragrant aroma.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
