Ophrys × vamvakiae

Ophrys × vamvakiae, commonly known as the Vamvakis bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the genus Ophrys, which is known for its mimicry of female insects to attract male pollinators. The plant produces intricate, bee-like flowers that are highly specialized for pollination by bees. It typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in sunny, open habitats.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
