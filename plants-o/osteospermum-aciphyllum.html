Osteospermum aciphyllum

Osteospermum aciphyllum, commonly known as the tree dandelion, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It features a woody stem with dense clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has dark green, narrow leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stem. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
