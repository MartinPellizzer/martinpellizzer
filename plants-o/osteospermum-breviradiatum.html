Osteospermum breviradiatum

Osteospermum breviradiatum, commonly known as the short-rayed African daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, daisy-like blooms with yellow centers and white or pale blue petals. It typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
