Odontoglossum aurarium

Odontoglossum aurarium, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive golden hue. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool, moist conditions, often in cloud forests. Its leaves are broad and ovate, and it produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Odontoglossum aurarium is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
