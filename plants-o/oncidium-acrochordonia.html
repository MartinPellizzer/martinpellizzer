Oncidium acrochordonia

Oncidium acrochordonia, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family, characterized by its striking yellow and green coloration. The plant features long, strap-like leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers. Oncidium acrochordonia thrives in humid, well-drained environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Its unique floral structure and vibrant colors make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
