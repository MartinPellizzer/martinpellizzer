Oxalis berteroana var. uniflora

Oxalis berteroana var. uniflora, commonly known as the single-flowered wood sorrel, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves and a single, showy flower that typically blooms in spring. The plant produces small, yellow-orange flowers with five petals, often accompanied by five sepals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
