Olsynium scirpoideum subsp. scirpoideum

Olsynium scirpoideum subsp.

scirpoideum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved marsh grass, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland and marsh environments. It typically grows in shallow water or damp soil, often found in regions with temperate climates.

The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are narrow, linear, and grass-like in appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are usually greenish or brownish in color.

This subspecies is adapted to tolerate periodic flooding and is an important component of wetland ecosystems.
