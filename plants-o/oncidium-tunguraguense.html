Oncidium tunguraguense

Oncidium tunguraguense, commonly known as the Tunguragua orchid, is a species native to South America, particularly found in Ecuador and Peru.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in humid, shaded environments within the Andes. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with a distinctive red or purple lip. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils, allowing it to thrive in its natural habitat.

Oncidium tunguraguense is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
