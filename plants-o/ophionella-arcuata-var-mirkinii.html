Ophionella arcuata var. mirkinii

Ophionella arcuata var. mirkinii, commonly known as the Mirkin's goldenbush, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, golden-yellow foliage that provides striking visual contrast in gardens. The plant features small, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a delicate touch of color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid climates.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
