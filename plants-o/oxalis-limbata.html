Oxalis limbata

Oxalis limbata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive lobed appearance, and its flowers are typically pink or white, with five petals. The plant grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and small, trifoliate leaves.

Oxalis limbata is often found in the understory of forests, where it thrives in partial to full shade.
