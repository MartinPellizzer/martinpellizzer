Ophrys subfusca subsp. aspea

Ophrys subfusca subsp.

aspea, commonly known as the aspea bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy.

The plant produces a single, showy flower with a dark brown to black labellum that resembles a bee's body. It thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in many regions.
