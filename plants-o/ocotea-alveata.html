Ocotea alveata

Ocotea alveata, commonly known as the "Alveate Ocotea," is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, lowland forests and can reach heights of up to 20 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and have a distinct aromatic scent when crushed.

The fruit is a small, dark berry that contains a single seed, and the tree is valued for its timber and medicinal properties.
