Oryzopsis gracilis

Oryzopsis gracilis, commonly known as slender false rice, is a grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and riparian areas. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a preference for temperate climates.

Oryzopsis gracilis is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in areas with high humidity.
