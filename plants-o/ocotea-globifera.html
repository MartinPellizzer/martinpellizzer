Ocotea globifera

Ocotea globifera, commonly known as the globeflower ocotea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in countries like Mexico, Costa Rica, and Panama. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a spherical capsule that contains several seeds, contributing to its common name.

Ocotea globifera is often found in humid, lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.
