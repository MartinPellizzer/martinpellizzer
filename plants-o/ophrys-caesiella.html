Ophrys caesiella

Ophrys caesiella, commonly known as the blue hound's tongue, is a species of orchid native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its unique, bee-mimicking flower that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant features a tall, slender stem with a single, spurred orchid flower that is typically blue or violet in color. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, often growing in dense clusters.

Ophrys caesiella thrives in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
