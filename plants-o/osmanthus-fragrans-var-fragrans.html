Osmanthus fragrans var. fragrans

Osmanthus fragrans var. fragrans, commonly known as sweet osmanthus, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy on top. Its flowers are widely used in traditional Chinese medicine and as a flavoring in teas and confections.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial shade to full sun.
