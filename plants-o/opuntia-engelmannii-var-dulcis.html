Opuntia engelmannii var. dulcis

Opuntia engelmannii var. dulcis, commonly known as the sweet prickly pear, is a cactus species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems that are covered in spines and are often ribbed. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tunas, is edible and has a sweet, juicy flavor.

The plant is well adapted to arid environments and is often used in traditional medicine and as a food source.
