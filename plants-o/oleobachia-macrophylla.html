Oleobachia macrophylla

Oleobachia macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf oleobachia, is a species of flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. Oleobachia macrophylla thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Due to its unique foliage and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
