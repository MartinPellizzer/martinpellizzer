Oxalis europaea f. vestita

Oxalis europaea f. vestita, commonly known as the white wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically white or pale green, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and flowers.
