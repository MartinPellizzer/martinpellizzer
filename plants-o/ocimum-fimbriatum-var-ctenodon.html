Ocimum fimbriatum var. ctenodon

Ocimum fimbriatum var. ctenodon, commonly known as the narrow-leaved basil, is a variant of the common basil plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. The plant is an aromatic herb with a mild, slightly sweet scent, often used in traditional cuisines and herbal remedies. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
