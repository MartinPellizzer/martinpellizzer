Oxyrhynchus insularis

Oxyrhynchus insularis, commonly known as the island oxyrhynchus, is a rare flowering plant native to specific island habitats in the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in shades of pink and white. The plant has a compact growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense mat. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a sunny to partially shaded environment.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Oxyrhynchus insularis is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
