Orchis angustifolia subsp. subcapitata

Orchis angustifolia subsp.

subcapitata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or meadow habitats.

The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces a tall, unbranched stem topped with a dense cluster of pale yellow to greenish flowers. Its flowers are characterized by a distinctive hooded lip and are pollinated by specific insects.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity in its native habitats.
