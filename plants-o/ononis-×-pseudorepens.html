Ononis × pseudorepens

Ononis × pseudorepens, commonly known as the hybrid common sand-verbena, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features small, often blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. The plant has slender, branched stems and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically hairy and arranged opposite each other. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed habitats.

Its botanical structure makes it well adapted to harsh environments, contributing to its ecological significance in certain regions.
