Oxalis maculata

Oxalis maculata, commonly known as the wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive purple markings and produces small, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow or orange. The plant is herbaceous, with a short, creeping stem and is often found in moist, shaded environments. It belongs to the family Oxalidaceae and is characterized by its ability to thrive in partial shade and well-drained soil.

Oxalis maculata is popular as a ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of cultivation.
