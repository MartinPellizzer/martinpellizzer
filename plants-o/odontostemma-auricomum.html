Odontostemma auricomum

Odontostemma auricomum, commonly known as the golden ear plant, is a striking member of the Araceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that often have a yellowish or golden tint. The plant produces striking inflorescences with a spathe that resembles an ear, hence its common name. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in swampy or wetland habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
