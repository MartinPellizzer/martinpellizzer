Ornithogalum namaquense

Ornithogalum namaquense, commonly known as the Namaqua lily, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers with a distinctive, often fragrant scent. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance through its succulent leaves and underground storage structures. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
