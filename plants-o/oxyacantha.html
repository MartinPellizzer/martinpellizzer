Oxyacantha

Oxyacantha, commonly known as the European barberry, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Berberidaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and bright yellow flowers. The plant produces small, tart berries that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Oxyacantha is also valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
