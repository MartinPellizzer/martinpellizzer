Oxalis rubrocincta

Oxalis rubrocincta, commonly known as the red-banded wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with reddish veins and small, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its stems are often reddish or purple, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Oxalis rubrocincta is valued for its attractive foliage and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
