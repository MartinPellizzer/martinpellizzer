Opuntia phaeacantha var. castorea

Opuntia phaeacantha var. castorea, commonly known as the Castoreo prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in sharp spines and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are typically orange in color and have a tangy flavor. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is often cultivated in xeriscapes and used in traditional medicine.
