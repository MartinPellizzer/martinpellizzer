Orobanche artemisiae-campestris subsp. picridis

Orobanche artemisiae-campestris subsp.

picridis, commonly known as the yellow broomweed dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies on host plants for nutrients. It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is characterized by its yellow, thread-like stems and small, inconspicuous flowers.

The plant typically parasitizes members of the Brassicaceae family, such as mustard and cabbage. Its life cycle involves germinating near host roots and establishing a haustorium to extract water and nutrients.

Botanically, it is notable for its reduced morphology and specialized adaptations for parasitism.
