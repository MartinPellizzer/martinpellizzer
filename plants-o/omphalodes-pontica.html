Omphalodes pontica

Omphalodes pontica, commonly known as the Caucasian bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Caucasus region.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Omphalodes pontica is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
