Orchis purpurea var. moravica

Orchis purpurea var. moravica, commonly known as the Moravian purple orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces showy, violet-purple flowers in late spring to early summer, with a distinctive lip-shaped petal. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland edges and meadows.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
