Ornithocephalus cujeticola

Ornithocephalus cujeticola, commonly known as the golden star vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly waxy texture.

Ornithocephalus cujeticola is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
