Oxydectes tricolor

Oxydectes tricolor, commonly known as the three-color oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which display three distinct colors—typically green, white, and red—on the same leaf blade. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink or white, often in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.
