Opuntia marenae

Opuntia marenae, commonly known as the Marenae prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flattened, fleshy stems that are covered in spines and are often ribbed. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tuna, is edible and contains seeds that are dispersed by animals.

Opuntia marenae is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
