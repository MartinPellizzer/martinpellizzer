Orchis × ravellensis

Orchis × ravellensis, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Orchis mascula and Orchis morio.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its showy, spurred flowers that typically range in color from purple to pink. The plant is perennial and thrives in moist, calcareous soils, often found in woodlands and meadows. Its morphology includes a basal rosette of lanceolate leaves and a flowering stem that can reach up to 60 cm in height.

This orchid is of interest to botanists due to its genetic diversity and role in ecological studies.
