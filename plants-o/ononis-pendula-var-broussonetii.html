Ononis pendula var. broussonetii

Ononis pendula var. broussonetii, commonly known as the hanging sandwort, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branching stems that often hang downward, giving it a cascading appearance. Its leaves are narrow and linear, adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
