Ormocarpum sennoides subsp. zanzibaricum

Ormocarpum sennoides subsp.

zanzibaricum, commonly known as Zanzibar senna, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of East Africa. It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline.

The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences, and its leaves are pinnately compound with serrated edges. It is adapted to thrive in saline and arid environments, making it a characteristic species of coastal vegetation.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.
