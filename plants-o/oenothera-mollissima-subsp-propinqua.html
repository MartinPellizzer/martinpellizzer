Oenothera mollissima subsp. propinqua

Oenothera mollissima subsp.

propinqua, commonly known as the silky evening primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and large, showy yellow flowers that open in the evening.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oval seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and native landscaping.
