Othonna trifurcata

Othonna trifurcata, commonly known as the three-branched othonna, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, densely branched structure and fleshy, gray-green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Othonna trifurcata is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
