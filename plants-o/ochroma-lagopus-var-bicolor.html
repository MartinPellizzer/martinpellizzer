Ochroma lagopus var. bicolor

Ochroma lagopus var. bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored balsa tree, is a variant of the balsa tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive bicoloration, with white and green hues on its leaves and stems. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in panicles. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the tree is known for its lightweight, porous wood.

This variant is primarily valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
