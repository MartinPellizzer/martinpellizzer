Orchis elata var. ambigua

Orchis elata var. ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or grassland habitats. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination by bees and other insects.

The species is noted for its morphological variability, which contributes to its common name.
