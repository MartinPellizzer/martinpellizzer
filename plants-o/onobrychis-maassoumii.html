Onobrychis maassoumii

Onobrychis maassoumii, commonly known as the Maassoumii burr vetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to dry environments, with deep roots that help it survive in nutrient-poor soils. It produces small, hairy pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

This species is of interest in ecological studies due to its role in stabilizing soils and its potential for use in sustainable agriculture.
