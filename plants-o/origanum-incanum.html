Origanum incanum

Origanum incanum, commonly known as woolly oregano, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its aromatic leaves are often used in culinary applications, though it is less common than other oregano varieties.

Origanum incanum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
