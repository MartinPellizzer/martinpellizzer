Ophrys fuciflora var. linearis

Ophrys fuciflora var. linearis, commonly known as the linear orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which includes a labellum that mimics the appearance of a female bee, aiding in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in many regions.
