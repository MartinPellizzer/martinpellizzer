Oreocome aegopodioides

Oreocome aegopodioides, commonly known as the silverweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features a low-growing, mat-forming structure with silver-gray foliage due to dense pubescence. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Oreocome aegopodioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
