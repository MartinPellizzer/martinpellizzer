Orthodon angustifolius

Orthodon angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf orthodon, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbel-like inflorescences.

It typically grows in temperate regions, preferring well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight. The plant features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Its small white to yellow flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and herbal practices.
