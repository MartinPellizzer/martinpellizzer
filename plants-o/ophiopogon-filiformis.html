Ophiopogon filiformis

Ophiopogon filiformis, commonly known as the threadleaf spathiphyllum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Ophiopogon filiformis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to form dense, low-growing mats.
