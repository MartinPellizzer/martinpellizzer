Oncidium ocanense

Oncidium ocanense, commonly known as the Ocanense Orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a sympodial orchid with a rhizome that produces pseudobulbs and is known for its striking, fragrant flowers. The plant typically has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces racemes of pendulous, colorful blooms. Oncidium ocanense thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental and horticultural cultivation.
