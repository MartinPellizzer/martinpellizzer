Onobrychis alba subsp. macedonica

Onobrychis alba subsp.

macedonica, commonly known as white vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Balkan region. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it an important plant in ecological restoration projects.
