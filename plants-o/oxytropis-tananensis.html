Oxytropis tananensis

Oxytropis tananensis, commonly known as the Tanan locoweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, spiny stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. Its leaves are typically small and compound, and it produces pods that contain seeds with hard, dark coats.

Oxytropis tananensis is also known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock.
