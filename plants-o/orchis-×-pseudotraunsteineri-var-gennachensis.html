Orchis × pseudotraunsteineri var. gennachensis

Orchis × pseudotraunsteineri var. gennachensis, commonly known as the Gennachensis Orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and clusters of violet or purple flowers with a distinctive, spurred lip. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, showcasing its ornamental value in gardens and natural habitats. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for woodland gardens.

This variety is prized for its unique floral structure and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
