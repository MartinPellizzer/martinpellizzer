Olgaea longifolia

Olgaea longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf olgaea, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom during the spring and summer seasons. Olgaea longifolia is adapted to grow in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in shrublands or open woodlands.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
