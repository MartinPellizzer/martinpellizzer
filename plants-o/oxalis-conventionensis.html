Oxalis conventionensis

Oxalis conventionensis, commonly known as the Convention oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.

Oxalis conventionensis is also valued for its ability to thrive in dry conditions, making it a resilient choice for xeriscaping.
