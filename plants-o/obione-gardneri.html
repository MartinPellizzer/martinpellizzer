Obione gardneri

Obione gardneri, commonly known as the Gardner's obione, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers clustered in dense inflorescences, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system for water storage.

Obione gardneri is often found in chaparral and sagebrush communities.
