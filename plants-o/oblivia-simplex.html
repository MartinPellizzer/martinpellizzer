Oblivia simplex

Oblivia simplex, commonly known as the simple olive, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features simple, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, which are followed by olive-like fruits. Oblivia simplex thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value and hardy nature contribute to its cultivation in both garden and horticultural settings.
