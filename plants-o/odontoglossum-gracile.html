Odontoglossum gracile

Odontoglossum gracile, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial with a compact growth habit and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink. The plant features a short, underground rhizome and possesses lance-shaped, hairy leaves. Its flowers are typically arranged in a raceme and are characterized by their narrow, strap-like petals.

Odontoglossum gracile is often cultivated for its ornamental value in shaded garden environments.
