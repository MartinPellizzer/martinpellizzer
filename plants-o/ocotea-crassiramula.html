Ocotea crassiramula

Ocotea crassiramula, commonly known as the thick-ramulate ocotea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces drupes that are often used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its aromatic wood and ecological role in its native habitats.
