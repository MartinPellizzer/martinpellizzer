Onobrychis hypoleuca

Onobrychis hypoleuca, commonly known as white vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a legume belonging to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. It has a creeping growth habit with extensive root systems that help prevent soil erosion.

The seeds are enclosed in small pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind and animals.
