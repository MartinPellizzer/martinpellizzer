Opuntia sinclairii

Opuntia sinclairii, commonly known as the Sinclair's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems that are covered in spines and glochids, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers in the spring, followed by edible, spherical fruits known as tunas. Its succulent leaves store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

Opuntia sinclairii plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing food and shelter for various desert animals.
