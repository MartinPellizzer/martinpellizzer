Olearia serpentina

Olearia serpentina, commonly known as the silverweed or silver leaf tree, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Olearia serpentina is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native habitats.
