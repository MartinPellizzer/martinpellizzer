Octolepis aymoniniana

Octolepis aymoniniana, commonly known as the Aymonin's feather fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery fronds that give it a delicate, lace-like appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often along forest floors or in rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, a common feature among ferns.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
