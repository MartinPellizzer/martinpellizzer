Osteospermum muricatum

Osteospermum muricatum, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads, which are composed of numerous small, tubular florets surrounded by a series of overlapping, fuzzy bracts. The plant has a bushy growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for coastal and Mediterranean climates.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
