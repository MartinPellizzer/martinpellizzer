Orchis persica

Orchis persica, commonly known as the Persian orchid, is a flowering plant native to parts of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats and has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Orchis persica produces a single spike of flowers, which bloom in late spring or early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
