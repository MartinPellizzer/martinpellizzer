Onosma orphanidea

Onosma orphanidea, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces striking blue to violet flowers in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Onosma orphanidea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
