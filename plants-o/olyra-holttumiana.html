Olyra holttumiana

Olyra holttumiana, commonly known as the Malabar mountain fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible fig-like fruits that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in dense forests at moderate altitudes.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
