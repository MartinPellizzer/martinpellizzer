Orchis × thriftiensis

Orchis × thriftiensis, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a natural cross between two orchid species, typically Orchis morio and Orchis mascula.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant produces a single spike of flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns and a distinct lip. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands and meadows across Europe.

The hybrid is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its beauty and unique floral structure.
