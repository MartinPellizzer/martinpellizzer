Oxalis corniculata var. sericea

Oxalis corniculata var. sericea, commonly known as the silverleaf wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive silver or white pubescent coating, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking shade-tolerant plants.
