Ophiomeris macahensis

Ophiomeris macahensis, commonly known as the Mexican mountain morning glory, is a climbing vine native to the highlands of Mexico.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of purple and pink. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or forested areas. Its rapid growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a vigorous, flowering vine.

However, it requires well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
