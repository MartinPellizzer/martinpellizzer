Ononis natrix var. mehdiae

Ononis natrix var. mehdiae, commonly known as the Persian sand sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It is adapted to sandy soils and can tolerate drought conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.

Its botanical features reflect its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting local biodiversity.
