Orectanthe sceptrum subsp. occidentalis

Orectanthe sceptrum subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the western crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a distinctive crown-like structure.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
