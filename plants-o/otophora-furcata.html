Otophora furcata

Otophora furcata, commonly known as the forked tamarisk, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has small, needle-like leaves that are adapted to arid environments, allowing it to conserve water efficiently. Otophora furcata is often found in rocky or coastal areas where it thrives in well-drained soils.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
