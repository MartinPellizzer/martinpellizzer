Orchis tenuifolia

Orchis tenuifolia, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, slender stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces a single spike of flowers that bloom in late spring.

Orchis tenuifolia is a rare and endangered species, often found in specific microhabitats with particular soil and moisture conditions.
