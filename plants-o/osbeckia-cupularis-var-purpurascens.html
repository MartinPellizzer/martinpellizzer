Osbeckia cupularis var. purpurascens

Osbeckia cupularis var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple cupularia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features erect, branched stems covered in soft, glandular hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy surface.

This species is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.
