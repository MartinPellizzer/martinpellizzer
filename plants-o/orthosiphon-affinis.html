Orthosiphon affinis

Orthosiphon affinis, commonly known as Java tea or *Plectranthus amboinicus*, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or violet in color. The plant is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves, which are used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
