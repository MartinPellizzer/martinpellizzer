Ophrys muscifera f. subbombifera

Ophrys muscifera f. subbombifera, commonly known as the Bomb-like Bee Orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species characterized by its unique floral structure.

The plant exhibits a distinctive mimicry of a female bee, with its flower resembling the body and wings of a bee to attract male bees for pollination. It is typically found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and heaths, where it thrives in nutrient-rich soils. The inflorescence consists of several showy, bee-like flowers arranged in a spike, each with a specialized labellum that enhances the mimicry.

This orchid is an important example of plant-pollinator co-evolution and is often protected due to its limited distribution and ecological significance.
