Orobanche caucasica f. flava

Orobanche caucasica f. flava, commonly known as the Caucasian yellow broomweed, is a parasitic plant belonging to the Orobanchaceae family.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients, typically parasitizing members of the Fabaceae family. The plant produces showy, yellow to orange flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its root system consists of haustoria, specialized structures that penetrate the host's vascular system.

This species is native to the Caucasus region and is characterized by its distinctive floral morphology and parasitic lifestyle.
