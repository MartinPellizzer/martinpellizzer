Osmia gracillima

Osmia gracillima, commonly known as the slender alfalfa bee, is a solitary ground-nesting bee species native to the western United States.

It is typically found in alfalfa fields and other flowering plants, where it plays a crucial role in pollination. The female constructs its nest in the soil, lining it with a mixture of plant resin and saliva to create a protective environment for its eggs. Osmia gracillima is small, with a slender body and hairy wings, and it is often mistaken for other small bee species.

Its life cycle is closely tied to the blooming periods of its host plants, making it an important indicator of ecosystem health.
