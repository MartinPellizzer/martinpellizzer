Oxytropis putoranica

Oxytropis putoranica, commonly known as the Balkan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Oxytropis putoranica is adapted to calcareous soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Its ecological role includes nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native environment.
