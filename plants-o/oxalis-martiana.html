Oxalis martiana

Oxalis martiana, commonly known as the purple shamrock, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves that resemble a shamrock, with a distinctive purple hue. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months. Oxalis martiana is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a versatile choice for various garden settings.
