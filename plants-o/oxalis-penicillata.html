Oxalis penicillata

Oxalis penicillata, commonly known as the false shamrock, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves that resemble those of the true shamrock. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, often accompanied by red stamens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Oxalis penicillata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
