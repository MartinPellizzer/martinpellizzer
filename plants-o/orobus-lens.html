Orobus lens

Orobus lens, commonly known as the lens vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces lens-shaped pods, which give it its scientific name, and the seeds are typically small and hard. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in dry, calcareous soils.

Orobus lens is often used in traditional herbal medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
