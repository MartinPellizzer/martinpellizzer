Orchis laxiflora lusus albiflora

Orchis laxiflora lusus albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered twayblade, is a rare orchid species native to specific regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and a single, showy white flower that blooms in late spring. The plant is a hemiparasite, meaning it partially relies on other plants for nutrients through its root system. Its flowers are typically pollinated by bees and other insects attracted to its nectar.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, this species is considered endangered in many areas.
