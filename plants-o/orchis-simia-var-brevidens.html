Orchis simia var. brevidens

Orchis simia var. brevidens, commonly known as the short-stalked monkey orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces a single, elongated flower spike.

This orchid is known for its unique morphology and is often found in association with specific mycorrhizal fungi.
