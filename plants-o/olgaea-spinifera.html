Olgaea spinifera

Olgaea spinifera, commonly known as the spiny olgaea, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, spiny stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming in spring. Olgaea spinifera is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

It is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience.
