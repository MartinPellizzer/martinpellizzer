Onopordum acaulon subsp. uniflorum

Onopordum acaulon subsp.

uniflorum, commonly known as the singleflower thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its robust, spiny stems and a single, large, showy flower head that blooms in late summer.

The plant produces a distinctive, prickly involucre with pale yellow to brown bracts. Its leaves are typically lobed and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

This subspecies is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
