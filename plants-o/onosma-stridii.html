Onosma stridii

Onosma stridii, commonly known as the Persian thyme or Persian wild thyme, is a flowering plant native to the mountains of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with small, fragrant leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. Onosma stridii thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
