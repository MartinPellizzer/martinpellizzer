Ophrys tommasinii

Ophrys tommasinii, commonly known as the Tommasini's bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in dry, sunny environments. Its flowers are usually pale green or brown, with intricate patterns that enhance the mimicry.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in many areas.
