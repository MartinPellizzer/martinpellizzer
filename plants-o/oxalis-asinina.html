Oxalis asinina

Oxalis asinina, commonly known as the goat's foot plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves that resemble a goat's foot. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. Oxalis asinina is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique foliage and ease of care.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for both indoor and outdoor environments.
