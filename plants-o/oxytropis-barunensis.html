Oxytropis barunensis

Oxytropis barunensis, commonly known as the Barun locoweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, spiny stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. It produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Oxytropis barunensis is also known for its toxic properties, making it potentially harmful to livestock.
