Orchis formosa

Orchis formosa, commonly known as the showy orchis, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and blooms in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are usually purple or pink with a distinctive yellow or green throat.

Orchis formosa is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
