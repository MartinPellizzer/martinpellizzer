Oeonia alata

Oeonia alata, commonly known as the winged bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, winged stems and bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. Its flowers, which range in color from blue to purple, bloom in late spring to early summer.
