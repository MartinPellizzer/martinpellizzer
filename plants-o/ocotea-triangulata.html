Ocotea triangulata

Ocotea triangulata, commonly known as the three-angled ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves that are triangular in shape, giving it its common name. It produces small, white flowers followed by dark, fleshy berries.

Ocotea triangulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
