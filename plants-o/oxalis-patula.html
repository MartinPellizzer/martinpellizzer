Oxalis patula

Oxalis patula, commonly known as the woodland sorrel, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with three heart-shaped leaflets, often adorned with reddish veins. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Oxalis patula is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
