Ononis fruticosa subvar. intermedia

Ononis fruticosa subvar. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial with slender, branched stems that often lie close to the ground. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers clustered in loose racemes, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are alternate, narrow, and often have a hairy texture.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and along coastal areas.
