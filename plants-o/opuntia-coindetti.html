Opuntia coindetti

Opuntia coindetti, commonly known as the Coindet's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its flattened, paddle-shaped stems called cladodes, which are covered in spines and glochids. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tunaf, is edible and often used in traditional cuisine.

Opuntia coindetti is well adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments due to its succulent structure and ability to store water.
