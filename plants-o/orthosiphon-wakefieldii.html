Orthosiphon wakefieldii

Orthosiphon wakefieldii, commonly known as the mountain mint or narrow-leaf orthosiphon, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal properties. The plant is characterized by its opposite, narrow leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or white flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its essential oils and phytochemical compounds have been studied for their potential health benefits.
