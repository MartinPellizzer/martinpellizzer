Oxydectes trichotoma

Oxydectes trichotoma, commonly known as the hairy leaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and features small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in a daisy-like pattern. The leaves are typically hairy, which gives the plant its common name, and they are arranged in a rosette formation. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
