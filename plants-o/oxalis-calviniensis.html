Oxalis calviniensis

Oxalis calviniensis, commonly known as the Calvini's wood sorrel, is a perennial flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces edible, sour-tasting berries that are often consumed by birds and small mammals. Oxalis calviniensis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to both ecological and ornamental gardens.
