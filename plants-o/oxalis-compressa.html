Oxalis compressa

Oxalis compressa, commonly known as the narrowleaf wood sorrel, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features trifoliate leaves composed of three heart-shaped leaflets, often with a reddish or purple tinge. The plant produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy groundcover plant.

Oxalis compressa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
