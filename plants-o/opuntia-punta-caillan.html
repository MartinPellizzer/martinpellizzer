Opuntia punta-caillan

Opuntia punta-caillan, commonly known as the Caillan prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems that are covered in spines and glochids, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the summer months, followed by edible, red-fleshed fruits. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it stores water in its thick stems, adapting to the arid climate of its native habitat.

Opuntia punta-caillan plays a role in local ecosystems and is also cultivated for ornamental and agricultural purposes.
