Oxalis borjensis

Oxalis borjensis, commonly known as the Persian wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Oxalis borjensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.

It is also used in traditional medicine in some regions for its purported health benefits.
