Oenothera cheiranthifolia subsp. suffruticosa

Oenothera cheiranthifolia subsp.

suffruticosa, commonly known as the coastal evening primrose, is a perennial shrub native to coastal regions of the United States. It features slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to coastal environments with high salinity and wind exposure.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy, salt-tolerant species with ecological significance in coastal ecosystems.
