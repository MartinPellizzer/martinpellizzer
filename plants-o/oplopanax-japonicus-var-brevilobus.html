Oplopanax japonicus var. brevilobus

Oplopanax japonicus var. brevilobus, commonly known as Japanese ginseng or Korean ginseng, is a member of the Araliaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is native to parts of East Asia.

Its roots are valued in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
