Opuntia depauperata

Opuntia depauperata, commonly known as the poor man's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, spineless pads and small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems. Its fruit, known as a tunas, is edible and often used in traditional cuisine.

Opuntia depauperata plays an important role in its ecosystem, providing food and shelter for various desert animals.
