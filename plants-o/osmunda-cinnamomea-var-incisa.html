Osmunda cinnamomea var. incisa

Osmunda cinnamomea var. incisa, commonly known as the cinnamon fern, is a fern species characterized by its distinctive, cinnamon-brown fiddleheads that unfurl into large, triangular fronds.

It is a perennial plant native to moist woodlands and stream sides in North America. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread and form dense colonies. Its fronds are bioluminescent in the dark, a unique trait that aids in seed dispersal.

The plant plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
