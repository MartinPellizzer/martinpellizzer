Odontoglossum krameri

Odontoglossum krameri, commonly known as the Krameri orchid, is a species native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and cloud forests. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color.

Odontoglossum krameri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
