Ocimum tubiforme

Ocimum tubiforme, commonly known as the tubular basil, is a species of flowering plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its aromatic, tubular-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pale purple to white, and they bloom in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Ocimum tubiforme is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
