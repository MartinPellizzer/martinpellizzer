Otophora macrocarpa

Otophora macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant cup fern, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its large, showy fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to regions of Southeast Asia. The plant features a rhizomatous growth habit with broad, pinnate leaves that can reach several feet in length. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are typically located on the underside of the fronds.

Otophora macrocarpa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in humid, shaded environments.
