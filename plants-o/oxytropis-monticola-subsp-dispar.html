Oxytropis monticola subsp. dispar

Oxytropis monticola subsp.

dispar, commonly known as the Sierra lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple pea-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in mountainous regions. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

It plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
