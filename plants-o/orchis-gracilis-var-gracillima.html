Orchis gracilis var. gracillima

Orchis gracilis var. gracillima, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features a slender, unbranched stem that bears a single, lance-shaped leaf. Its flowers are small, pale green to yellowish, and are clustered in a loose raceme, often with a distinctive hood-like structure.

This variety is noted for its delicate appearance and is often found in association with other orchid species in its habitat.
