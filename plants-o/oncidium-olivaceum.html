Oncidium olivaceum

Oncidium olivaceum, commonly known as the Olive Cattleya, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, showy flowers that typically feature a yellow lip and a greenish-white or yellowish upper lip. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are long, strap-like, and often have a waxy texture.

Oncidium olivaceum is valued in horticulture for its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.
