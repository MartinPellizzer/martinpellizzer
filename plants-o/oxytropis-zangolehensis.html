Oxytropis zangolehensis

Oxytropis zangolehensis, commonly known as the Zangoleh milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect or semi-erect growth habit. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Oxytropis zangolehensis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or gravelly soils.
