Orthocarpus densiflorus var. typicus

Orthocarpus densiflorus var. typicus, commonly known as the coastal buckthorn, is a shrub native to the western coast of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are often consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils.

Its compact growth habit makes it a hardy plant suitable for coastal landscaping.
