Oberonia pectinata

Oberonia pectinata, commonly known as the hairy orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences with showy, violet to purple flowers. It thrives in shaded, moist environments, typically in forest understories.

Oberonia pectinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
