Orbignya lydiae

Orbignya lydiae, commonly known as the Lydia's oil palm, is a species native to the Amazon rainforest of South America.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that develop into fruits containing a rich oil seed. Due to its high oil content, it has potential economic value, though it remains relatively understudied compared to other palm species.

Its ecological role in the rainforest is still being explored by botanists and conservationists.
