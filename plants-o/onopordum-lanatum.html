Onopordum lanatum

Onopordum lanatum, commonly known as the hairy thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, hairy stems and leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, wastelands, and along roadsides.

Onopordum lanatum is known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
