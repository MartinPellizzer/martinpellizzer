Osodendron leptophyllum

Osodendron leptophyllum, commonly known as the slender-leaved osodendron, is a species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom during the rainy season. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils, allowing it to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

Osodendron leptophyllum is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
