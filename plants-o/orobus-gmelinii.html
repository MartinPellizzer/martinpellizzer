Orobus gmelinii

Orobus gmelinii, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a distinctive hooded shape. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in humid environments such as rainforests or coastal areas. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil.

Orobus gmelinii is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
