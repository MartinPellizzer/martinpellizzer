Odontites asturicus

Odontites asturicus, commonly known as the Asturian toothed grass, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in mountainous regions. It is often found in shaded, rocky habitats and is adapted to cool, humid climates.

Odontites asturicus is a relatively rare species, contributing to the unique flora of its native region.
