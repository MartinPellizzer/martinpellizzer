Ocotea magnifolia

Ocotea magnifolia, commonly known as the magnolia ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, dark purple drupe that contains a single seed.

Ocotea magnifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in warm climates for its aesthetic appeal.
