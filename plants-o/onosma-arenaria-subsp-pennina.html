Onosma arenaria subsp. pennina

Onosma arenaria subsp.

pennina, commonly known as the sand onosma, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Europe and Asia. It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and small, yellowish to reddish flowers.

The plant typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly elongated leaves and floral structure compared to other Onosma species.
