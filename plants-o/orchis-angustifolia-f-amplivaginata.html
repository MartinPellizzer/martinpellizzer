Orchis angustifolia f. amplivaginata

Orchis angustifolia f. amplivaginata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a botanical variant of the narrow-leaved orchid.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and tall, erect flowering stems. The flowers are typically pale green to yellowish in color, with a distinctive lip that is often more elongated than in the typical species. This form is found in moist, calcareous soils in woodland habitats across Europe.

It is a perennial herb that blooms in late spring to early summer.
