Orchis mascula var. bicolor

Orchis mascula var. bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored twayblade, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique appearance, featuring two distinct color zones on its leaves and a single, elongated flower spike. The plant is native to parts of Europe and is typically found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with a subtle fragrance.

The species is notable for its mycorrhizal association, which plays a crucial role in its nutrient uptake and growth.
