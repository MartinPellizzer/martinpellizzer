Oberonia subulata

Oberonia subulata, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and delicate, pale yellow to greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and mountainous regions. Its roots are often mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to enhance nutrient absorption.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
