Olearia oporina

Olearia oporina, commonly known as the silver olearia, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Olearia oporina is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native pollinators.
