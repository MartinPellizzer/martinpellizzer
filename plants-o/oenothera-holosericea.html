Oenothera holosericea

Oenothera holosericea, commonly known as the silky evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oily seeds. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered with fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Oenothera holosericea is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
