Olea europaea var. umbraculata

Olea europaea var. umbraculata, commonly known as the shade olive, is a variant of the common olive tree.

It is characterized by its dense, broad foliage that provides significant shade, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by edible drupes, though the fruit is typically not harvested for consumption. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is often used in urban settings due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.
