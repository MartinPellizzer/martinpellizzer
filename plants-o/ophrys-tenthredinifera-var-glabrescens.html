Ophrys tenthredinifera var. glabrescens

Ophrys tenthredinifera var. glabrescens, commonly known as the hairy bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to certain regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in open, calcareous grasslands and is adapted to specific environmental conditions. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brownish in color, with intricate patterns that aid in attracting male bees for pollination.

Due to habitat loss and other environmental pressures, this variant is considered threatened and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
