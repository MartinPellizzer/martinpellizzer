Ophiorrhiza deflexa

Ophiorrhiza deflexa, commonly known as the drooping mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees in tropical regions.

It has small, elliptical leaves that are usually opposite or whorled, and its flowers are typically white or greenish-white with five petals. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. Ophiorrhiza deflexa is adapted to nutrient-poor environments and relies on its host for water and some nutrients.
