Ochna afzelii subsp. congoensis

Ochna afzelii subsp.

congoensis, commonly known as the Congo ochre tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to central and western Africa. It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and alternate, simple leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by distinctive, orange-red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local communities.
