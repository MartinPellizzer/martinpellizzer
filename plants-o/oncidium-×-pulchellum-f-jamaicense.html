Oncidium × pulchellum f. jamaicense

Oncidium × pulchellum f. jamaicense, commonly known as the Jamaican orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Caribbean region.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically display shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

The foliage consists of strap-like leaves that are often a deep green color, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
