Oxytropis rarytkinensis

Oxytropis rarytkinensis, commonly known as the Karakorum milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are trifoliate, and it produces legumes that contain nitrogen-fixing bacteria, contributing to soil fertility.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological role and potential for use in restoration projects.
