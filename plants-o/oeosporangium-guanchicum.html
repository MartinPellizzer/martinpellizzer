Oeosporangium guanchicum

Oeosporangium guanchicum, commonly known as the Guanche spore plant, is a rare and ancient species native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its unique reproductive structures called oesporangia, which are specialized for spore production. The plant exhibits a low-growing, succulent morphology adapted to the arid and volcanic soils of its native habitat. Its life cycle is closely tied to specific microclimatic conditions, making it highly sensitive to environmental changes.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological specificity, Oeosporangium guanchicum is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
