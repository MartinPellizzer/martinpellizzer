Orthosiphon homblei

Orthosiphon homblei, commonly known as the Java mint, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its square stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stems. Orthosiphon homblei is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
