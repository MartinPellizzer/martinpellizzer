Ocotea gracilipes

Ocotea gracilipes, commonly known as the slender ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its slender, straight trunk and dense, dark green foliage. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a smooth, glossy surface.

The tree produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue berries, which are an important food source for local wildlife.
