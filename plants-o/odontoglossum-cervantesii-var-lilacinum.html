Odontoglossum cervantesii var. lilacinum

Odontoglossum cervantesii var. lilacinum, commonly known as the Lilac Orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its elegant, lance-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The flowers typically bloom in shades of lilac, pink, and white, with a distinctive lip that is often marked with contrasting colors. It prefers cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests at elevations between 1,500 and 2,500 meters.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and unique floral structure.
