Oxytropis sericopetala

Oxytropis sericopetala, commonly known as the silky leaf milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its finely hairy, silvery-green foliage and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, arid environments. It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
