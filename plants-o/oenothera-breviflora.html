Oenothera breviflora

Oenothera breviflora, commonly known as the short-flowered evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in desert or semi-desert environments. Its leaves are linear and succulent, adapted to conserve water in arid conditions.

Oenothera breviflora is also known for its medicinal properties, with its seeds containing essential fatty acids.
