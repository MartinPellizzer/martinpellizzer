Oxymitra desmoides

Oxymitra desmoides, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy seed heads that disperse seeds by wind. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with deep roots and the ability to store water.

Oxymitra desmoides is often found in sandy soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
