Ophrys inzengae

Ophrys inzengae, commonly known as the Inzeng's bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which closely resembles a female bee, a mimicry strategy used to attract male bees for pollination. The plant produces a single, showy flower that is typically greenish-yellow with intricate patterns mimicking bee anatomy. It thrives in nutrient-poor, acidic soils and requires specific microclimatic conditions for successful growth.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Ophrys inzengae is considered a vulnerable species.
