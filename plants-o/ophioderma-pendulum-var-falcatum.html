Ophioderma pendulum var. falcatum

Ophioderma pendulum var. falcatum, commonly known as the golden thread orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous inflorescences that bear small, yellow-orange flowers. The plant has strap-like, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its unique appearance and delicate blooms make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
