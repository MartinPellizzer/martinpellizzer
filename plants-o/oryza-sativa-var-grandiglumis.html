Oryza sativa var. grandiglumis

Oryza sativa var. grandiglumis, commonly known as the large glume rice, is a botanical variant of the rice species Oryza sativa.

It is characterized by its large, glumed panicles and elongated grains, which distinguish it from other rice varieties. This plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in wetland environments. Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in agricultural research and genetic studies.

The variety is also valued for its potential in breeding programs aimed at improving rice yield and resilience.
