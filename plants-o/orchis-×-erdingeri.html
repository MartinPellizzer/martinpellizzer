Orchis × erdingeri

Orchis × erdingeri, commonly known as the Erdinger's orchid, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Orchis morio and Orchis purpurea.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in woodland habitats with moist, well-drained soil, preferring partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinctive white or greenish stripe along the midrib.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive floral display.
