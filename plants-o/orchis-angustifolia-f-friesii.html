Orchis angustifolia f. friesii

Orchis angustifolia f. friesii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a botanical variant of the narrow-leaved orchid species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family, characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its flowers are usually arranged in a loose spike and bloom in early summer, contributing to its ornamental value.

The species is noted for its ecological significance, supporting various pollinators and serving as an indicator of healthy, undisturbed ecosystems.
