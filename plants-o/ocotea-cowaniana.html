Ocotea cowaniana

Ocotea cowaniana, commonly known as the yellow sandarac tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a dense canopy. Its bark is smooth and light brown, while its flowers are small and clustered in panicles.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
