Olea roxburghii

Olea roxburghii, commonly known as the Indian olive, is a small evergreen tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by small, round fruits containing a single seed. Olea roxburghii is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates, making it a hardy species suitable for various environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
