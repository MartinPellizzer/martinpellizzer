Ornithogalum perparvum

Ornithogalum perparvum, commonly known as the small white star-of-Bethlem, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a short, stout rhizome and produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers during the summer months. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Ornithogalum perparvum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
