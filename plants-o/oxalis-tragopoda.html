Oxalis tragopoda

Oxalis tragopoda, commonly known as the forest oxalis, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, delicate flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and woodlands. It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

Oxalis tragopoda is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and bloom.
