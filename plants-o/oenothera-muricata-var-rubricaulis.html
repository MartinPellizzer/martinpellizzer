Oenothera muricata var. rubricaulis

Oenothera muricata var. rubricaulis, commonly known as the red-stemmed evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, reddish stems and showy yellow flowers that open in the evening. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

The species is noted for its ecological value, providing nectar for nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
