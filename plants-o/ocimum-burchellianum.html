Ocimum burchellianum

Ocimum burchellianum, commonly known as wild basil, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs such as mint and lavender. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often fragrant and covered in fine hairs. Its small, tubular flowers are typically violet or blue, arranged in whorled clusters at the ends of the stems.

Ocimum burchellianum is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its antispasmodic and anti-inflammatory effects.
