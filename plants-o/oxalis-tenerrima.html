Oxalis tenerrima

Oxalis tenerrima, commonly known as the evergreen wood sorrel, is a perennial plant native to South America.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant has a woody stem that becomes more prominent with age, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental foliage. Oxalis tenerrima thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
