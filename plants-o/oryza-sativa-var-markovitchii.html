Oryza sativa var. markovitchii

Oryza sativa var. markovitchii, commonly known as the Markovitch rice, is a rare and ancient variety of rice native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its short, slender grains and a distinctive aroma, which sets it apart from other rice types. This variety is known for its high nutritional value, containing significant amounts of protein and essential minerals. It is primarily cultivated in small quantities for specialty markets and traditional culinary uses.

Due to its limited distribution and unique traits, it is considered a valuable genetic resource for rice breeding programs.
