Oxylobus glandulifer f. coloratus

Oxylobus glandulifer f. coloratus, commonly known as the red-stemmed oxylobus, is a botanical variant characterized by its distinctive reddish stem coloration.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is native to specific regions in the Mediterranean. The plant features small, glossy leaves with glandular dots, which contribute to its unique appearance. It typically grows in rocky or acidic soils and thrives in partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in horticulture for its visual appeal.
