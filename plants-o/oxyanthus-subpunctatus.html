Oxyanthus subpunctatus

Oxyanthus subpunctatus, commonly known as the spotted oxyanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a subtle speckled pattern. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and usually have a rough texture.

Oxyanthus subpunctatus is often found in moist, well-drained soils near water sources.
