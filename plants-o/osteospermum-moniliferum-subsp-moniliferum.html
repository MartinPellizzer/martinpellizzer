Osteospermum moniliferum subsp. moniliferum

Osteospermum moniliferum subsp.

moniliferum, commonly known as the blue oatbrush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads, which are surrounded by white to pale blue ray florets.

The plant produces a distinctive, spiky seed head that resembles a brush, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.
