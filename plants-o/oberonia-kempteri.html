Oberonia kempteri

Oberonia kempteri, commonly known as the Kempter's orchid, is a species of flowering plant in the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. This epiphytic orchid typically grows on trees or rocks, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are small and clustered, often displaying a range of colors from greenish-white to pale pink.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
