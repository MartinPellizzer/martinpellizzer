Oenothera mollissima var. longiflora

Oenothera mollissima var. longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and large, showy yellow flowers that open in the evening. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oily seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
