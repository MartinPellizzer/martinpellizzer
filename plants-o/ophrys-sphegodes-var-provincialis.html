Ophrys sphegodes var. provincialis

Ophrys sphegodes var. provincialis, commonly known as the provincial bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which closely resembles a female bee, a mimicry strategy used to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as woodlands and grasslands, preferring calcareous soils. Its flowers are usually pale yellow with dark markings, and it produces small, dark brown seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species in many regions.
