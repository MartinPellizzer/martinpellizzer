Oryzopsis napostaensis

Oryzopsis napostaensis, commonly known as the Naposta grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly Argentina and Uruguay.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, blade-like leaves, which are typically green and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences, with each spikelet containing two to three florets. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Oryzopsis napostaensis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
