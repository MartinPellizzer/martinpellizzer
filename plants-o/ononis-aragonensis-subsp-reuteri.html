Ononis aragonensis subsp. reuteri

Ononis aragonensis subsp.

reuteri, commonly known as the Aragon velvetweed, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit.

The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are typically trifoliate, with three leaflets. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
