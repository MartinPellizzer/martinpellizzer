Ohlendorffia procumbens

Ohlendorffia procumbens, commonly known as the creeping water plant, is a small aquatic plant native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater environments such as ponds and streams. The plant has slender, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water surface or trail beneath it. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually greenish-white in color.

Ohlendorffia procumbens plays a role in stabilizing aquatic habitats and providing shelter for small aquatic organisms.
