Oenothera arequipensis

Oenothera arequipensis, commonly known as the Arequipa evening primrose, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Oenothera arequipensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
