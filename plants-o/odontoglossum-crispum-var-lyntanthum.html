Odontoglossum crispum var. lyntanthum

Odontoglossum crispum var. lyntanthum, commonly known as the Lyntanthum orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety green leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its flowers are short-lived but highly ornamental, often appearing in late winter to early spring.

This variety is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
