Oncidium maderoi

Oncidium maderoi, commonly known as the Mader's orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It features a sympodial growth habit with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in clusters, often in shades of yellow, orange, and red. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air and surrounding environment.

Oncidium maderoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in greenhouses and as a houseplant.
