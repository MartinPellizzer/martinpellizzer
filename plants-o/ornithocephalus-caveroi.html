Ornithocephalus caveroi

Ornithocephalus caveroi, commonly known as the Cauca orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated flower spikes that bear small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in cloud forests. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to anchor in rocky or loose soil.

Ornithocephalus caveroi is of interest to botanists due to its specialized morphology and limited distribution.
