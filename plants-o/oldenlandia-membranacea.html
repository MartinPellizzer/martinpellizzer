Oldenlandia membranacea

Oldenlandia membranacea, commonly known as the veined morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its rapid growth and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a vibrant, low-maintenance vine.
