Ossiculum

Ossiculum, commonly known as the sea gooseberry, is a type of marine organism belonging to the phylum Phylum Echinodermata.

It is a small, free-swimming creature with a spherical body covered in tiny, tube-like structures called ampullae. The ossiculum plays a crucial role in the organism's locomotion and feeding by creating water currents. It is found in coastal waters and is an important part of marine ecosystems.

Its unique structure and function make it a fascinating subject in marine biology.
