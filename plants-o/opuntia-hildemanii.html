Opuntia hildemanii

Opuntia hildemanii, commonly known as Hildeman's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in sharp spines and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces edible fruits known as tunas, which are typically orange or red in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations such as succulent stems for water storage.

Opuntia hildemanii plays an important role in its ecosystem, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
