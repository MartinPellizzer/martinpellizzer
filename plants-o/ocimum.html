Ocimum

Ocimum, commonly known as basil, is a genus of about 50 species of flowering plants in the family Lamiaceae.

These plants are characterized by their aromatic leaves, which are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. Ocimum species typically have square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that are usually clustered in spikes. They are native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

Many species within this genus are widely cultivated for their essential oils, which contribute to their distinctive fragrance and various therapeutic properties.
