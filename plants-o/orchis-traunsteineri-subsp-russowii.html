Orchis traunsteineri subsp. russowii

Orchis traunsteineri subsp.

russowii, commonly known as the Traunsteiner's orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to specific regions of Europe. It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which are typically arranged in a loose raceme.

The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit and produces showy, lip-like petals that are adapted for pollination by specific insect species. It thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.
