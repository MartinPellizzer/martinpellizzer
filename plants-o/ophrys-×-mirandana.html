Ophrys × mirandana

Ophrys × mirandana, commonly known as the Fairy Orchid hybrid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb with a distinctive appearance, featuring showy, bee-mimicking flowers that are often yellow or brown in color. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its flowers are designed to attract pollinators through mimicry, and it is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
