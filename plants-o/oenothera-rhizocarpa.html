Oenothera rhizocarpa

Oenothera rhizocarpa, commonly known as the rootstalk evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in arid and sandy soils. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.
