Orchis maculata var. vespertilio

Orchis maculata var. vespertilio, commonly known as the evening orchid, is a rare and distinctive member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking violet and white flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as woodlands and meadows. It has a short, stout rhizome and produces a single spike of flowers with a distinctive purple-blue hue.

This orchid is noted for its unique pollination mechanism, relying on specific insect species for reproduction.
