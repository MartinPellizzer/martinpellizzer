Onosma echioides var. stojanoffii

Onosma echioides var. stojanoffii, commonly known as Stojanoff's spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features slender, erect stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense, yellowish-green inflorescences, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
