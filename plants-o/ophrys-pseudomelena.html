Ophrys pseudomelena

Ophrys pseudomelena, commonly known as the false bee orchid, is a species of orchid native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant produces a single, showy flower with a specialized lip that resembles the body of a bee, attracting male bees for pollination. It thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

The botanical features of Ophrys pseudomelena are adapted to its specific ecological niche and reproductive strategy.
