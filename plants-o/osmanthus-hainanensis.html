Osmanthus hainanensis

Osmanthus hainanensis, commonly known as the Hainan osmanthus, is a species of flowering plant native to the island of Hainan in China.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, small yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant produces small, round, dark brown fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its aromatic flowers and ornamental value.

Due to its limited natural range, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
