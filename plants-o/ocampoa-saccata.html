Ocampoa saccata

Ocampoa saccata, commonly known as the Brazilian saccate orchid, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, saccate (sac-like) floral structure. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded, humid environments. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with vibrant colors that attract specific pollinators.

Ocampoa saccata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
