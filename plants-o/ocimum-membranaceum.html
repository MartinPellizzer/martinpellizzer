Ocimum membranaceum

Ocimum membranaceum, commonly known as holy basil or tulsi, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and aromatic essential oils. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale purple, and it thrives in warm, humid climates. Its leaves are rich in volatile compounds, including eugenol and limonene, which contribute to its medicinal and aromatic properties.

Ocimum membranaceum is widely cultivated for both culinary and therapeutic uses.
