Orchis × amsittenii

Orchis × amsittenii, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a natural hybrid between Orchis mascula and Orchis morio.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer and is found in moist, calcareous grasslands and woodlands. Its botanical features include long, narrow leaves and a distinctive, spurred lip on its flowers.

This hybrid is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique genetic makeup and ornamental value.
