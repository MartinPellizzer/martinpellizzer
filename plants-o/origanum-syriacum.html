Origanum syriacum

Origanum syriacum, commonly known as Syrian oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their essential oils. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces small, pale blue to purple flowers. It is valued for its strong, pungent aroma and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Origanum syriacum thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a hardy addition to gardens.
