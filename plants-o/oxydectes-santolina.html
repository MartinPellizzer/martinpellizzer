Oxydectes santolina

Oxydectes santolina, commonly known as santolina, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, silver-gray foliage that is densely packed and slightly fuzzy to the touch. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in compact clusters during the summer months. Santolina is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for both ornamental and medicinal uses.
