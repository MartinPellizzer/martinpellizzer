Oxalis valdiviensis var. antucensis

Oxalis valdiviensis var. antucensis, commonly known as the Antucoensis wood sorrel, is a perennial herb native to southern Chile.

It features heart-shaped leaves with reddish veins and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in temperate rainforests. Its foliage is characterized by a unique pattern of veins that resemble a network, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in cool, humid conditions.
