Ocimum holosericeum

Ocimum holosericeum, commonly known as the silver leaf basil, is a species of flowering plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale purple, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its aromatic leaves are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Ocimum holosericeum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
