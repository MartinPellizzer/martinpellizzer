Oxydectes discolor

Oxydectes discolor, commonly known as the variable orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers that can vary in hue depending on environmental conditions. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and covered in a fine, velvety texture that aids in moisture retention.

The flowers bloom in clusters, showcasing a unique combination of colors and patterns that make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
