Oxylobium emarginatum

Oxylobium emarginatum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed oxylobium, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, acidic soils, often found in wetlands and along stream banks. The plant features opposite, narrow leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the edges. Its showy, fragrant flowers are typically pink or white and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Oxylobium emarginatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
