Oxalis salvadorensis

Oxalis salvadorensis, commonly known as the Salvadoran wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. It has a short stem with alternate leaves and produces capsule fruits containing several seeds.

Oxalis salvadorensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
