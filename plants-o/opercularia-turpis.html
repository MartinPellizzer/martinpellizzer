Opercularia turpis

Opercularia turpis, commonly known as the "sulfur cup," is a small, cup-shaped fungus that belongs to the family Strophariaceae.

It is characterized by its yellowish to orange cap, which often has a reddish or brownish hue when mature. The stem is short and often covered with a layer of fine, silky hairs. This fungus is typically found in damp, shaded areas, growing on decaying wood or soil.

It is not considered edible and is more of a decomposer, playing a role in breaking down organic matter in forest ecosystems.
