Olearia hygrophila

Olearia hygrophila, commonly known as the silverleaf daisy, is a herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, daisy-like white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Olearia hygrophila is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
