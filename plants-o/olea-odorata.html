Olea odorata

Olea odorata, commonly known as the olive tree, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are leathery and glossy, and its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces small, oval fruits called olives, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications. Olea odorata is known for its ability to thrive in dry, rocky soils and its long lifespan, often living for hundreds of years.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
