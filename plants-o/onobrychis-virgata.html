Onobrychis virgata

Onobrychis virgata, commonly known as violet burclover, is a perennial leguminous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces elongated, hairy pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Onobrychis virgata has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
