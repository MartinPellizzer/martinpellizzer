Orchis maculata var. paludosa

Orchis maculata var. paludosa, commonly known as the marsh spotted orchid, is a perennial herb native to wetland habitats in North America.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, marshes, and along stream edges. The plant produces tall, slender stems that bear showy, violet-purple flowers with distinctive dark spots. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance, supporting pollinators and serving as an indicator of healthy wetland ecosystems.
