Odontoglossum laeve var. reichenheimii

Odontoglossum laeve var. reichenheimii, commonly known as the Reichenheim's orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its sympodial growth habit and the production of pseudobulbs. The plant features lanceolate, dark green leaves that are often covered with a dense layer of white pubescence. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid environments.
