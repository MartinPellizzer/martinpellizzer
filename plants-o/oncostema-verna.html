Oncostema verna

Oncostema verna, commonly known as the spring onion grass, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or greenish flowers in clusters. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a basal rosette of leaves.

Oncostema verna is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
