Oxalis meyeriana

Oxalis meyeriana, commonly known as the Meyer's wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive reddish veins and small, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Oxalis meyeriana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of garden conditions.
