Orobus magellanicus

Orobus magellanicus, commonly known as the Magellanic orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and shrublands. Its roots are fibrous, and it produces a single spike of flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its range.
