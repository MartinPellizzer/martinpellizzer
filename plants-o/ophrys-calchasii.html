Ophrys calchasii

Ophrys calchasii, commonly known as the Calchas Orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a complex labellum that mimics the appearance of an insect, likely to aid in pollination by male bees. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are usually pale green or brown, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, Ophrys calchasii is considered vulnerable, making conservation efforts essential for its survival.
