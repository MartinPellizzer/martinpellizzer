Orchis durandii

Orchis durandii, commonly known as the Durand's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It is a terrestrial herb with a slender, upright stem that bears a single, showy, violet-blue flower. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky areas. Its flowers have a distinctive lip that is often marked with darker pigments, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

Orchis durandii is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
