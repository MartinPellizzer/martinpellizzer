Ocotea killipii

Ocotea killipii, commonly known as the Killip's ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean, particularly found in the Lesser Antilles.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a dark blue drupe, containing a single seed, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife.

Ocotea killipii is also valued for its aromatic wood, which has been used in traditional crafts and as a source of essential oils.
