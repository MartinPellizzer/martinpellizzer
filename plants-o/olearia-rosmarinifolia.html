Olearia rosmarinifolia

Olearia rosmarinifolia, commonly known as silverleaf tree heath, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It features silver-gray, woolly leaves that are densely covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It typically grows in coastal or subalpine regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun.

Olearia rosmarinifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
