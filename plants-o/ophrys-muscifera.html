Ophrys muscifera

Ophrys muscifera, commonly known as the musk orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which resembles a female bee, a feature that plays a key role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in sunny, open habitats. Its flowers are adorned with intricate patterns and a strong, musky fragrance that attracts male bees for pollination.

Ophrys muscifera is a perennial herb that produces a single, showy flower per stem, making it a striking addition to its natural environment.
