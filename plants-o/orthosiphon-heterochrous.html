Orthosiphon heterochrous

Orthosiphon heterochrous, commonly known as Java tea or *Plectranthus heterochrous*, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves, which are used in traditional medicine and as a herbal tea.

Orthosiphon heterochrous thrives in warm, humid climates and is commonly found in tropical regions.
