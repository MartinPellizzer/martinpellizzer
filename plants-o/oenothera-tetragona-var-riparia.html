Oenothera tetragona var. riparia

Oenothera tetragona var. riparia, commonly known as the river evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, sandy soils along riverbanks and wetlands.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and it plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
