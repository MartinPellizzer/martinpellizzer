Oenothera gauriflora

Oenothera gauriflora, commonly known as the showy evening primrose, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically 3 to 5 inches in diameter, with five petals that curl inward as they age.

Oenothera gauriflora is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
