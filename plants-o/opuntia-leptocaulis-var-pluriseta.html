Opuntia leptocaulis var. pluriseta

Opuntia leptocaulis var. pluriseta, commonly known as the many-bristled prickly pear, is a cactus species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flattened, paddle-shaped stems covered in clusters of spines and glochids. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tunas, is edible and contains a sweet, pulpy interior.

This species is well adapted to arid environments and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of food.
