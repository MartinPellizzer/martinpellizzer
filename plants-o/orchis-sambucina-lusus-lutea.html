Orchis sambucina lusus lutea

Orchis sambucina lusus lutea, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a species of orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which are typically arranged in a loose spike. The plant features a slender, upright stem with several leaves, and its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

The yellow orchid is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its beauty.
