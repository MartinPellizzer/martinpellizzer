Oxalis grammophylla

Oxalis grammophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features trifoliate leaves with three heart-shaped leaflets, and small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant produces capsule fruits containing several seeds, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade.

Oxalis grammophylla is often grown as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and is relatively low maintenance.
