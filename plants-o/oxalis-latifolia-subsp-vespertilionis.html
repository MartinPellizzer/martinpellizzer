Oxalis latifolia subsp. vespertilionis

Oxalis latifolia subsp.

vespertilionis, commonly known as the evening bat oxalis, is a member of the Oxalidaceae family. It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers.

Native to South Africa, this plant thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade. Its foliage is often adorned with distinctive markings that resemble bat wings, giving it its unique common name.

The plant is popular among gardeners for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
