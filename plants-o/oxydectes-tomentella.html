Oxydectes tomentella

Oxydectes tomentella, commonly known as the woolly mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in the tropical regions of Africa.

It possesses specialized structures called haustoria that penetrate the host's vascular system to extract water and nutrients. The plant is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, white or pale green flowers, followed by small, fleshy berries that are dispersed by birds.

Due to its parasitic nature and unique morphology, it plays a significant role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat.
