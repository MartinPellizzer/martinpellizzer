Opuntia shaferi var. humahuacana

Opuntia shaferi var. humahuacana, commonly known as the Huahuan cactus, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green with a waxy coating. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tunas, is edible and has a mild, sweet flavor.

This variety is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
