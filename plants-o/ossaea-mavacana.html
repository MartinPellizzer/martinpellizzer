Ossaea mavacana

Ossaea mavacana, commonly known as the Cape Flats dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellow ray flowers that bloom in spring. Ossaea mavacana is typically found in sandy or gravelly soils and is often used in traditional medicine.

Its ecological role includes providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing soil in arid environments.
