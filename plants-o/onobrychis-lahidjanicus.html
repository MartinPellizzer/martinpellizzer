Onobrychis lahidjanicus

Onobrychis lahidjanicus, commonly known as the Persian clover, is a perennial leguminous plant native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a deep root system, allowing it to thrive in dry and semi-arid environments. It is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Onobrychis lahidjanicus is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural settings.
