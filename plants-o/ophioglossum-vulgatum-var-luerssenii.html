Ophioglossum vulgatum var. luerssenii

Ophioglossum vulgatum var. luerssenii, commonly known as the giant shield fern, is a unique and ancient plant species belonging to the family Ophioglossaceae.

It is characterized by its large, shield-shaped leaves that can reach lengths of up to 1 meter, with a distinct central vein and a broad, rounded base. The plant produces sporangia in clusters along the margins of its leaves, a feature typical of its primitive evolutionary lineage. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas with rich, organic soil.

This variant is notable for its striking appearance and its role in ecological systems as a habitat for various invertebrates.
