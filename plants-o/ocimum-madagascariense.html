Ocimum madagascariense

Ocimum madagascariense, commonly known as Madagascar basil, is a perennial herb native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like mint and oregano. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, dark green leaves and purple flowers, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its culinary and medicinal uses.

Its essential oils contain compounds with potential antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.
