Orchis incarnata var. subextensa

Orchis incarnata var. subextensa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pink orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and reaches heights of 15 to 30 centimeters. The plant features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a distinctive white or pale midrib.

The species is part of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its specialized root system and mycorrhizal associations.
