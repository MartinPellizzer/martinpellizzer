Obione pseudocampanulata

Obione pseudocampanulata, commonly known as the narrow bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The flowers are bell-shaped, typically blue or violet in color, and bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
