Oxalis mucronata

Oxalis mucronata, commonly known as the hooked wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant produces distinctive seed pods that curl into a hook shape, aiding in seed dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Oxalis mucronata is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique floral structure.
