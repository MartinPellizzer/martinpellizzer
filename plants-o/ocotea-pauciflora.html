Ocotea pauciflora

Ocotea pauciflora, commonly known as the little-flowered ocotea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, dark purple drupe that contains a single seed.

Ocotea pauciflora is often found in humid, shaded understories of tropical forests, where it plays a role in the ecological diversity of its habitat.
