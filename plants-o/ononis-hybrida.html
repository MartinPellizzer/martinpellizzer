Ononis hybrida

Ononis hybrida, commonly known as hybrid squawroot, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant's leaves are alternate, simple, and often hairy, with a distinctive trifoliate structure. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Ononis hybrida is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
