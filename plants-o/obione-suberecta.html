Obione suberecta

Obione suberecta, commonly known as the upright obione, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are lance-shaped with a slightly waxy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
