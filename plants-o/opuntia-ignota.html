Opuntia ignota

Opuntia ignota, commonly known as the narrow-leaf prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flattened, paddle-shaped stems that are covered in spines and glochids, which are small, barbed hairs. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by fruit that is typically red or orange in color. Opuntia ignota is adapted to arid environments and has a shallow root system that allows it to survive in dry conditions.

Its leaves are reduced to spines, and its stems perform photosynthesis, making it a typical example of a succulent plant.
