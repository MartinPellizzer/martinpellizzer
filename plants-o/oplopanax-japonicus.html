Oplopanax japonicus

Oplopanax japonicus, commonly known as Japanese ginseng, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, palmately compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by dark blue berries. Its roots are valued for their medicinal properties, containing compounds like ginsenosides.

Oplopanax japonicus thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.
