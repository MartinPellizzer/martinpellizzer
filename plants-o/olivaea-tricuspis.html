Olivaea tricuspis

Olivaea tricuspis, commonly known as the three-lobed olive, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Oleaceae and is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves with three lobes. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or white in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Olivaea tricuspis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for both horticultural and ecological purposes.
