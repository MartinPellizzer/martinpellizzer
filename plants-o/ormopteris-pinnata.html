Ormopteris pinnata

Ormopteris pinnata, commonly known as the broad-leafed holly fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Pteridaceae.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, compound leaves that are pinnately divided, giving it a distinctive, feathery appearance. The fronds are typically dark green and have a rough, textured surface. This fern thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
