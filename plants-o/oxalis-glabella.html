Oxalis glabella

Oxalis glabella, commonly known as the weeping oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens. Its foliage resembles that of a fern, giving it a unique and ornamental appearance.

Oxalis glabella is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments.
