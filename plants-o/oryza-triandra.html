Oryza triandra

Oryza triandra, commonly known as the wild rice of Southeast Asia, is a grass species native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces tiny, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Oryza triandra is often found in wetland habitats and is adapted to thrive in aquatic or semi-aquatic environments.
