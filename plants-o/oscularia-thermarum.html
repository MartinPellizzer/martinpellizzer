Oscularia thermarum

Oscularia thermarum, commonly known as the desert sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has succulent leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in desert scrub and rocky habitats.

Oscularia thermarum plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is adapted to extreme temperature fluctuations.
