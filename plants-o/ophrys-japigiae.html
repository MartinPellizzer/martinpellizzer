Ophrys japigiae

Ophrys japigiae, commonly known as the Japanese bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid native to Japan.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which closely resembles a female wasp, a trait evolved to attract male wasps for pollination. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers are usually pale green or brown, with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of a wasp's body.

Ophrys japigiae is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is noted for its complex pollination mechanism involving mimicry.
