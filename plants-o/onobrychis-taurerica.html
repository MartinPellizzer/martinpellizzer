Onobrychis taurerica

Onobrychis taurerica, commonly known as Taurian burclover, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow pea-like flowers. The plant is typically found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Onobrychis taurerica is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
