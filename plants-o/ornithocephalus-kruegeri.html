Ornithocephalus kruegeri

Ornithocephalus kruegeri, commonly known as the Krueger's orchid, is a unique flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, elongated flowers that resemble bird heads, giving it its scientific name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually bright yellow or orange, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a threatened species in its native range.
