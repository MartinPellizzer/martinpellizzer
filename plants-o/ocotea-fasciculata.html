Ocotea fasciculata

Ocotea fasciculata, commonly known as the fragrant ocotea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often arranged in clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small and fragrant, typically white or pale yellow, and it produces dark, glossy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife.

Ocotea fasciculata is also valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional crafts and for its potential medicinal properties.
