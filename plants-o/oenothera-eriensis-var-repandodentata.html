Oenothera eriensis var. repandodentata

Oenothera eriensis var. repandodentata, commonly known as the plains evening primrose, is a species of flowering plant native to the central and western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in open habitats like prairies and sandy soils, and it has a trailing or ascending growth habit. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are often hairy as well.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is sometimes used in restoration projects due to its ecological value.
