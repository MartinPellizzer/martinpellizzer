Olea monticola

Olea monticola, commonly known as the mountain olive, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often gray-green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by small, round drupes that are typically yellow or orange in color. Olea monticola is well adapted to rocky and dry environments, making it a hardy species.

Its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soils make it a popular choice for landscaping in Mediterranean climates.
