Orthosiphon schliebenii

Orthosiphon schliebenii, commonly known as Java tea or kaphi, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that are clustered in whorls. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Orthosiphon schliebenii thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in swampy or marshy areas.
