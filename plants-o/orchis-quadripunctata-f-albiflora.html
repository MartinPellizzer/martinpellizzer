Orchis quadripunctata f. albiflora

Orchis quadripunctata f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered four-spotted orchid, is a rare and distinctive orchid species native to specific regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its pale to white flowers, which contrast with the typical purple or brown hues of the species. The plant has a slender, upright stem with several leaves and produces small, clustered blooms. Its flowers have a unique structure with four dark spots on the petals, giving it its common name.

This orchid thrives in moist, shaded habitats and is often found in woodland areas.
