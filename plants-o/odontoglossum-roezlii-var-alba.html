Odontoglossum roezlii var. alba

Odontoglossum roezlii var. alba, commonly known as the white orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking white petals and sepals, which contrast beautifully with its deep green foliage. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers that bloom during the cooler months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and high humidity.

This variety is prized by orchid growers for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
