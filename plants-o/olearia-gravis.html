Olearia gravis

Olearia gravis, commonly known as the mountain daisy, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its growth habit is shrub-like, with multiple stems and a compact form.

Olearia gravis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
