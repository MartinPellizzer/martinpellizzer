Ocotea tenella

Ocotea tenella, commonly known as the white sand ocotea, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in areas with rich, well-drained soil. Its leaves are elliptical in shape, glossy, and have a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

The fruit is a small, smooth, spherical berry that turns from green to dark purple as it matures.
