Ornithogalum gracillimum

Ornithogalum gracillimum, commonly known as the slender star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers during the spring and summer seasons. The plant is typically found in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.

The genus Ornithogalum is known for its milkweed-like sap, which can be toxic if ingested.
