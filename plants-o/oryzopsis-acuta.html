Oryzopsis acuta

Oryzopsis acuta, commonly known as sharpgrass, is a species of grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers. Oryzopsis acuta typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
