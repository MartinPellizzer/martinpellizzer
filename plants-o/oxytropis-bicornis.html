Oxytropis bicornis

Oxytropis bicornis, commonly known as the two-horned locoweed, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale purple to white in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sagebrush steppe and rocky soils.

Oxytropis bicornis is also known for its toxic properties, making it a potential hazard to livestock.
