Ouret lanata

Ouret lanata, commonly known as the woolly flax, is a herbaceous plant characterized by its soft, hairy stems and leaves.

It belongs to the family Linaceae and is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in late spring to early summer. Its fibrous stems are valued for their historical use in textile production.

Ouret lanata thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
