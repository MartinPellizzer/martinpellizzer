Oxalis subcarnosa

Oxalis subcarnosa, commonly known as the pale wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is a perennial species that produces oval, fleshy tubers underground.

Oxalis subcarnosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a garden setting.
