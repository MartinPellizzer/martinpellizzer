Oxytropis irbis

Oxytropis irbis, commonly known as the Siberian locoweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cold climates of Central Asia and northern Europe.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has compound leaves with stipules and produces pods that contain small, hard seeds. It is adapted to grow in dry, sandy soils and is often found in steppe and tundra environments.

Oxytropis irbis is known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to grazing animals.
