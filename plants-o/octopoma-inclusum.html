Octopoma inclusum

Octopoma inclusum, commonly known as the slender false nettle, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes stinging nettles, and is characterized by its opposite leaves and smooth, non-stinging stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants.

Octopoma inclusum is often found in wetlands and is an important component of the local flora in its native habitat.
