Oxalis hilaireiana

Oxalis hilaireiana, commonly known as the African wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive red veins and produces small, five-petaled yellow flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 cm and thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and flowers.

Oxalis hilaireiana is also valued for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
