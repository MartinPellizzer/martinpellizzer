Ophrys × lefevreana

Ophrys × lefevreana, commonly known as the Lefevre's bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its intricate, bee-like floral structure, which features a specialized lip that mimics the appearance and scent of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and heathlands. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brownish in color, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Ophrys × lefevreana is a rare and protected species, often found in specific microhabitats where its unique pollination strategy is most effective.
