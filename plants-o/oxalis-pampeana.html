Oxalis pampeana

Oxalis pampeana, commonly known as the Pampas wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, oval fruits that contain several seeds.

Oxalis pampeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
