Ocotea odorifera

Ocotea odorifera, commonly known as the aromatic ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round berries. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

Ocotea odorifera is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in various indigenous cultures.
