Olax minquartioides

Olax minquartioides, commonly known as the African olive, is a shrub native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Olax minquartioides is often found in rocky or dry habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.
