Orobanche caerulea var. ramosa

Orobanche caerulea var. ramosa, commonly known as the blue broomweed, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in association with legumes and other herbaceous plants. Its life cycle involves germinating in response to chemical signals released by host roots, after which it establishes a parasitic connection.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can negatively affect the growth of its host plants, making it a significant concern in agricultural settings.
