Osmanthus × fortunei

Osmanthus × fortunei, commonly known as the golden osmanthus, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late autumn. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate with a slightly serrated margin.

Osmanthus × fortunei is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and for the aromatic oils extracted from its flowers.
