Othonna euphorbioides

Othonna euphorbioides, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or African star-of-Bethlehem, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a dense rosette of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating. The plant produces striking yellow flowers with red bracts that resemble a star, followed by small, orange berries. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Othonna euphorbioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
