Opuntia duvalioides

Opuntia duvalioides, commonly known as the Florida prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in spines and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces edible fruits known as tunas, which are typically orange to red in color. Opuntia duvalioides thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.

Its structure and growth habits make it a distinctive and resilient member of the Cactaceae family.
