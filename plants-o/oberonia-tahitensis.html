Oberonia tahitensis

Oberonia tahitensis, commonly known as the Tahitian orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Society Islands in French Polynesia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments within tropical rainforests. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, often forming a rosette at the base.

Oberonia tahitensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
