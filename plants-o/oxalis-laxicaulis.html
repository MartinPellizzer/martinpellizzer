Oxalis laxicaulis

Oxalis laxicaulis, commonly known as the slender wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It features a basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves with distinctive reddish veins. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Oxalis laxicaulis is often grown as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and delicate floral display.
