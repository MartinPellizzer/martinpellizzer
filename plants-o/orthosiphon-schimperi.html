Orthosiphon schimperi

Orthosiphon schimperi, commonly known as Java tea or Malabar tea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent in herbal teas.
