Opuntia elizondoana

Opuntia elizondoana, commonly known as the Elizondo prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in spines and glochids, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months, followed by edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Opuntia elizondoana plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
