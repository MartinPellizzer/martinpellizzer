Ochna squarrosa

Ochna squarrosa, commonly known as the square-leaf ochna, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive square-shaped leaves, which are often hairy and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Ochna squarrosa is adapted to dry and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in such habitats.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
