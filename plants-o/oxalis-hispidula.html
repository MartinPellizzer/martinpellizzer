Oxalis hispidula

Oxalis hispidula, commonly known as the hispid wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with reddish veins and small, yellow, spurred flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its stems are hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance, and it produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds.

Oxalis hispidula is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
