Ornithogalum brownleei

Ornithogalum brownleei, commonly known as the Brownlee's star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers during the summer months. The plant is characterized by its bulbous underground stem, which stores nutrients and water. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticultural enthusiasts.
