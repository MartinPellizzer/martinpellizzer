Ophiuros compressus

Ophiuros compressus, commonly known as the compressed sea holly, is a marine flowering plant found in coastal regions.

It is characterized by its dense, fleshy leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant thrives in saline environments, often growing in sandy or muddy substrates near the shoreline. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by wind and insects, contributing to its reproductive success in coastal ecosystems.

Ophiuros compressus plays a role in stabilizing dune environments and providing habitat for various marine organisms.
