Oenothera nanella

Oenothera nanella, commonly known as the dwarf evening primrose, is a small annual plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has thin, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Oenothera nanella is noted for its short life cycle and is considered a valuable species for ecological restoration projects.
