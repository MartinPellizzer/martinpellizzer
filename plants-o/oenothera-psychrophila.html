Oenothera psychrophila

Oenothera psychrophila, commonly known as the alpine evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant thrives in cold, moist environments and is adapted to survive in harsh alpine conditions. Its leaves are typically narrow and arranged alternately along the stem.

Oenothera psychrophila is known for its ecological importance in supporting pollinators in its native habitat.
