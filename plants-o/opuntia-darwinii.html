Opuntia darwinii

Opuntia darwinii, commonly known as Darwin's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems that are segmented and covered in spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers, which are typically followed by edible, red-fleshed fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating remarkable drought tolerance.

Opuntia darwinii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in various cultures.
