Otopappus microcephalus

Otopappus microcephalus, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant has small, narrow leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water.

Its seeds are often dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation across sandy and rocky soils.
