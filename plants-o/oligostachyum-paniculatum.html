Oligostachyum paniculatum

Oligostachyum paniculatum, commonly known as the bamboo palm, is a tropical evergreen plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clustered, feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear yellowish-white flowers, followed by dark blue-black berries. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often used as an ornamental plant in landscaping.

Its growth habit and adaptability make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation.
