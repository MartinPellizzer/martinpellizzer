Oxytropis glabra f. sericea

Oxytropis glabra f. sericea, commonly known as silky field vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately divided, and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is often found in the western United States and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
