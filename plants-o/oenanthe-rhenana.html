Oenanthe rhenana

Oenanthe rhenana, commonly known as the Rhenish water-dropwort, is a herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils along riverbanks and wet meadows. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its small, white flowers are clustered in umbels and bloom in early summer.

Oenanthe rhenana is characterized by its aromatic, slightly toxic sap, which contains various compounds.
