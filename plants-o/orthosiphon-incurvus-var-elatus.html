Orthosiphon incurvus var. elatus

Orthosiphon incurvus var. elatus, commonly known as the hairy leaf horsegram, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in whorled clusters.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often grown for its ornamental value and traditional medicinal uses.
