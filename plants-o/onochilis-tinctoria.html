Onochilis tinctoria

It is characterized by its striking red to orange flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
