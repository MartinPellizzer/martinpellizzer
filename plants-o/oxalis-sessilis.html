Oxalis sessilis

Oxalis sessilis, commonly known as the clustered wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. It is often found in woodland areas and is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Oxalis sessilis is also noted for its ability to produce clusters of tiny, bead-like fruits.
