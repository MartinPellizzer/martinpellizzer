Ocotea nitida

Ocotea nitida, commonly known as white sand ocotea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and elliptical leaves that are glossy on top and lighter in color underneath. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces drupes that are typically yellow or red when mature.

Ocotea nitida is often found in moist, well-drained soils in lowland and montane forests.
