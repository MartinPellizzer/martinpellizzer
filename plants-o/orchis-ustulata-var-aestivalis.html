Orchis ustulata var. aestivalis

Orchis ustulata var. aestivalis, commonly known as the early purple orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive violet or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and can be found in meadows, woodlands, and along streambanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its roots are tuberous, aiding in nutrient storage.

The species is known for its ecological importance, supporting various pollinators and serving as an indicator of healthy, undisturbed habitats.
