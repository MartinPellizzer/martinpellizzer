Ochrosia elliptica f. syncarpa

Ochrosia elliptica f. syncarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed ochrosia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features small, elliptical, dark green leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for container gardening or as a landscape specimen.

The variety 'syncarpa' is distinguished by its narrower leaves compared to the typical form of Ochrosia elliptica.
