Oxydectes anisata

Oxydectes anisata, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and dark green, elongated leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, hard seeds.

Due to its valuable timber and ornamental qualities, it is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
