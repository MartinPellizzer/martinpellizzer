Oncidium lindleyoides

Oncidium lindleyoides, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow and green flowers, which bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are typically long and strap-like, adapted to capture sunlight in its natural habitat.

Oncidium lindleyoides is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its vibrant color and relatively easy care requirements.
