Oldenlandia scandens

Oldenlandia scandens, commonly known as mile-a-minute weed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a vigorous climbing vine with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by bead-like, blue or purple fruits. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive nature, it is considered a problematic weed in many areas.
