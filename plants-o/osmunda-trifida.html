Osmunda trifida

Osmunda trifida, commonly known as the fragrant water fern, is a species of fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, floating fronds that are often used in floral arrangements. The plant has a distinctive reproductive structure called a sorus, which contains the spores. Osmunda trifida thrives in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and slow-moving streams.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
