Ophrys × manfredoniae subsp. delilloi

Ophrys × manfredoniae subsp.

delilloi, commonly known as the Delillo's bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its intricate floral structure, which mimics the appearance of female bees to attract male pollinators.

The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in sunny, open habitats. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brownish in color, with a distinctively shaped labellum that plays a key role in its pollination strategy.

This subspecies is valued for its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native environment.
