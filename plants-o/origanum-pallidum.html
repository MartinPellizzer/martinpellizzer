Origanum pallidum

Origanum pallidum, commonly known as pale oregano, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its leaves are used in culinary applications and traditional medicine due to their high concentration of essential oils.

Origanum pallidum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
