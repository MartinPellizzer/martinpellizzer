Omphalodes erecta

Omphalodes erecta, commonly known as the upright bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of pink or purple. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens or borders.

Its flowering period extends from late spring to early summer, adding ornamental value to gardens.
