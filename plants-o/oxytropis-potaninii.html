Oxytropis potaninii

Oxytropis potaninii, commonly known as the Mongolian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky areas. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers in clusters. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through its root nodules.

Oxytropis potaninii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock.
