Ophrys olympiotissa

Ophrys olympiotissa, commonly known as the Olympus bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Greece.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee, a feature evolved to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are usually a mix of brown and yellow, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
