Oenocarpus frigidus

Oenocarpus frigidus, commonly known as the cold coconut palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces a unique type of fruit that resembles a coconut but is smaller and has a different texture. Its botanical features include a fibrous husk and a single, large seed enclosed within.

Oenocarpus frigidus is adapted to grow in humid, coastal environments and is often found in mangrove ecosystems.
