Oenone phellandriifolia

Oenone phellandriifolia, commonly known as the lemon-scented honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves that emit a citrus-like fragrance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Oenone phellandriifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
