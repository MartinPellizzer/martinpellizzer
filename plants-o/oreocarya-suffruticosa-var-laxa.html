Oreocarya suffruticosa var. laxa

Oreocarya suffruticosa var. laxa, commonly known as the looseleaf oreocarya, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its dense, woody growth and small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, spherical berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

This variety is notable for its horticultural value and ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
