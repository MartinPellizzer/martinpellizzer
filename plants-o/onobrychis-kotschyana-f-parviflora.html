Onobrychis kotschyana f. parviflora

Onobrychis kotschyana f. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered bursaria, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces pods that contain small, hairy seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
