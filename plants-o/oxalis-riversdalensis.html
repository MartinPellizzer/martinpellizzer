Oxalis riversdalensis

Oxalis riversdalensis, commonly known as the Riverdal wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, delicate yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and has a preference for moist, well-drained soil. It produces small, edible berries that are sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Oxalis riversdalensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
