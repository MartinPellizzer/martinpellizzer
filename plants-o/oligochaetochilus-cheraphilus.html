Oligochaetochilus cheraphilus

Oligochaetochilus cheraphilus, commonly known as the golden moss orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant produces delicate, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive golden hue. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the soil.

It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in Southeast Asia.
