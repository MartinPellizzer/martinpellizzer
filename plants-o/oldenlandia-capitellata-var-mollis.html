Oldenlandia capitellata var. mollis

Oldenlandia capitellata var. mollis, commonly known as velvetleaf or velvetleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields due to its adaptability.

The species is also known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
