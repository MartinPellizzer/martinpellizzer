Oxalis thelyoxys

Oxalis thelyoxys, commonly known as the waxy oxalis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink or white, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Oxalis thelyoxys is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
