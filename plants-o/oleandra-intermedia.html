Oleandra intermedia

Oleandra intermedia, commonly known as the Mexican oleander, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are elliptical in shape. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Oleandra intermedia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
