Oxalis suksdorfii

Oxalis suksdorfii, commonly known as the Sutherland's wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in well-drained environments. It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it adapts to mild, temperate climates.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and shady borders.
