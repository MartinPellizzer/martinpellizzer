Opuntia galapageia subsp. macrocarpa

Opuntia galapageia subsp.

macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant prickly pear, is a cactus native to the Galápagos Islands. It is characterized by its large, flattened stems that are covered in spines and glochids.

The plant produces large, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to red, typically blooming in the dry season. Its fruits are fleshy and edible, containing seeds that are dispersed by birds and other animals.

This subspecies is adapted to the arid and volcanic environment of the Galápagos, showing resilience to extreme conditions.
