Oxycoccus palustris var. melanocarpus

Oxycoccus palustris var. melanocarpus, commonly known as black crowberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, black berries that are rich in antioxidants. The plant thrives in acidic, peaty soils and is often found in boreal and tundra ecosystems. Its berries are an important food source for various wildlife species.

The plant's compact growth habit and hardy nature make it well adapted to cold, harsh climates.
