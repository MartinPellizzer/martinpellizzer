Orobus japonicus

Orobus japonicus, commonly known as Japanese bellflower, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Orobus japonicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
