Orchis × beyrichii var. timbalii

Orchis × beyrichii var. timbalii, commonly known as the Timbalai orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically range in color from purple to pink. The plant produces erect, branched stems with lance-shaped leaves, and its inflorescence consists of multiple flowers arranged in a loose cluster. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated by enthusiasts for its beauty and unique floral structure.
