Ophrys × humbertii

Ophrys × humbertii, commonly known as the Humbert's bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking floral morphology, with a labellum that mimics the appearance of a female bee, a feature evolved to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and thrives in temperate climates with moderate rainfall. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brownish in color, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Ophrys × humbertii is a rare and protected species, reflecting its ecological significance and the need for conservation efforts.
