Onopordum × paui

Onopordum × paui, commonly known as the hybrid thistle, is a perennial plant characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and showy purple or pink flowers.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is native to regions of Europe and Asia. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky soils. Its flowers are composite, consisting of both ray florets and disc florets, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Onopordum × paui is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
