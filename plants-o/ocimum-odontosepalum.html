Ocimum odontosepalum

Ocimum odontosepalum, commonly known as the toothed sepals basil, is a species of flowering plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and tubular, two-lipped flowers that are typically purple or pink in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Ocimum odontosepalum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
