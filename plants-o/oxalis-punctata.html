Oxalis punctata

Oxalis punctata, commonly known as the spotted wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with distinctive white spots and small, five-petaled yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its flowers bloom in spring and are followed by capsule fruits containing several seeds.

Oxalis punctata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
