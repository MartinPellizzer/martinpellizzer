Opuntia sanguinocula

Opuntia sanguinocula, commonly known as the blood-flower cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its flat, paddle-like stems known as cladodes. The plant produces vibrant red flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual element to its appearance. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it is adapted to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.

Opuntia sanguinocula is also noted for its ability to store water in its thick, fleshy stems.
