Oxycoccus quadripetalus f. costatus

Oxycoccus quadripetalus f. costatus, commonly known as the hairy oxberry, is a variant of the common oxberry, characterized by its distinctive hairy appearance.

It is a small, evergreen shrub native to northern regions of Europe and Asia. The plant features small, round berries that are typically red or dark purple when mature. Its leaves are elliptical, with a waxy surface and a serrated edge.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and berries.
