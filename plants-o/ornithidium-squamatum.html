Ornithidium squamatum

Ornithidium squamatum, commonly known as the scaly orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that often display a unique combination of colors. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in dense forests or on rocky outcrops. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered with a fine, scaly texture, which helps in reducing water loss.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Ornithidium squamatum is considered an endangered species and is of significant interest to botanists and conservationists.
