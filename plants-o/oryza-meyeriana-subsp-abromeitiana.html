Oryza meyeriana subsp. abromeitiana

Oryza meyeriana subsp.

abromeitiana, commonly known as the Meyer's ricegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spikelet-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in moist, tropical or subtropical environments, often in wetlands or along riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color.

This subspecies is of interest in botanical studies due to its potential ecological roles and genetic diversity.
