Oxydectes thouarsiana

Oxydectes thouarsiana, commonly known as the Tahitian flame tree, is a tropical tree native to the Society Islands of French Polynesia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a dramatic visual impact. The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Oxydectes thouarsiana is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
