Opuntia argentina

Opuntia argentina, commonly known as the Argentine prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in sharp spines and small, glochids. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by edible, fleshy fruits. Opuntia argentina is adapted to arid environments and has a shallow root system that allows it to thrive in dry conditions.

Its structure and growth patterns make it a resilient and distinctive member of the Cactaceae family.
