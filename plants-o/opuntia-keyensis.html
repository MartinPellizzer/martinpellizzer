Opuntia keyensis

Opuntia keyensis, commonly known as Key Island prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the Florida Keys.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-like stems covered in spiny areoles. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the summer months. Its fruit, known as a tunas, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

Opuntia keyensis plays an important role in the local ecosystem, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
