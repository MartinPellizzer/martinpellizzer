Owenia acidula

Owenia acidula, commonly known as the sour orange, is a citrus species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, yellow-orange fruits that are typically sour in taste. The plant has fragrant, glossy leaves and produces small white flowers. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its fruit, which is used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The tree is relatively hardy and can tolerate a range of soil types, making it a versatile plant in horticulture.
