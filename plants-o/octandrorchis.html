Octandrorchis

Octandrorchis, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and are characterized by their striking, showy flowers. The inflorescence typically consists of multiple spike-like clusters, each bearing several small, brightly colored flowers. The leaves are usually linear and arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps in water conservation.

Octandrorchis species often thrive in shaded, moist environments and are valued for their ornamental beauty in horticulture.
