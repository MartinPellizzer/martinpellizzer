Ophrys vernixia

Ophrys vernixia, commonly known as the early bee orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique floral structure that mimics female bees to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip that resembles a bee's body.

Ophrys vernixia is known for its specialized pollination mechanism and is often found in Mediterranean climates.
