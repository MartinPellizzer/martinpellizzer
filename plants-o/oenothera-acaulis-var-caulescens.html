Oenothera acaulis var. caulescens

Oenothera acaulis var. caulescens, commonly known as the tallstem evening primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems that are taller than the typical form of Oenothera acaulis. The plant produces showy yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

The species is known for its medicinal properties, with its oil being used in various topical applications.
