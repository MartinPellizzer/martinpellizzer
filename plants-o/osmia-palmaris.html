Osmia palmaris

Osmia palmaris, commonly known as the palm leaf mite, is a small arachnid belonging to the family Eriophyidae.

It is primarily found on the leaves of palm trees, where it feeds on plant sap, causing characteristic stippling and discoloration. The mite's body is elongated and covered in fine, waxy secretions, which help it adhere to the leaf surface. It reproduces rapidly, with females giving birth to live young that develop through several instars.

Due to its impact on palm health, Osmia palmaris is often considered a pest in agricultural and ornamental palm cultivation.
