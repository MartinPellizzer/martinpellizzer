Ouratea neuridesii

Ouratea neuridesii, commonly known as the Brazilian beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of violet-blue berries that appear in the fall. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. It produces small, white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

The berries are edible for birds and mammals, playing a role in seed dispersal within its native habitat.
