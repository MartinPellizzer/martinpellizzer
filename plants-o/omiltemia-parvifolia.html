Omiltemia parvifolia

Omiltemia parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaf omiltemia, is a species of flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Omiltemia parvifolia is often found in open woodlands and along the edges of wetlands.
