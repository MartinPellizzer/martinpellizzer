Olax phyllanthi

Olax phyllanthi, commonly known as the African olive, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Olacaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy berries containing a single seed. Olax phyllanthi is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
