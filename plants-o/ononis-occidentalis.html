Ononis occidentalis

Ononis occidentalis, commonly known as western sandspur, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial with small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, and its fruits are dry, capsule-like structures that release tiny seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed areas.

Botanically, it is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules.
