Orchis patens subsp. canariensis

Orchis patens subsp.

canariensis, commonly known as the Canary orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Canary Islands. It is a perennial herb with a slender, upright stem that bears a single, showy flower.

The flower is typically violet or purple in color, with a distinctive lip that is often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant thrives in sunny, well-drained habitats such as rocky slopes and coastal areas.

Its botanical features reflect adaptations to the unique Mediterranean climate of the Canary Islands.
