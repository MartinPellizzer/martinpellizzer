Ornithocephalus graciliscapus

Ornithocephalus graciliscapus, commonly known as the slender-headed orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, elongated inflorescence and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss and climate change, it faces threats to its survival in the wild.
