Oligochaetochilus ziegeleri

Oligochaetochilus ziegeleri, commonly known as the Ziegeler's mossy orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate, moss-like foliage that helps it thrive in shaded, humid environments. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically greenish-yellow with faintly colored lips, adapted for specific pollinators. It is often found in rocky crevices or on tree trunks, where it forms symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi.
