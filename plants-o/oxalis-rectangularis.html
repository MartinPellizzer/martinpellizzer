Oxalis rectangularis

Oxalis rectangularis, commonly known as the diamondleaf wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive diamond pattern on the underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, delicate yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique foliage and modest floral display.
