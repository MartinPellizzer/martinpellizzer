Oleandra pubescens

Oleandra pubescens, commonly known as the hairy oleander, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has thick, woody stems and lance-shaped leaves covered in fine hairs, giving it its common name. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types.

Oleandra pubescens is also known for its high toxicity, as it contains cardiac glycosides that can be harmful to humans and animals.
