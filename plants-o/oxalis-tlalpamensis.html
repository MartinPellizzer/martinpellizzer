Oxalis tlalpamensis

Oxalis tlalpamensis, commonly known as the Tlalpan wood sorrel, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a distinctive reddish or purplish hue, and the flowers are usually yellow with five petals.

Oxalis tlalpamensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
