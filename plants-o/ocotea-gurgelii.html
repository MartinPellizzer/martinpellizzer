Ocotea gurgelii

Ocotea gurgelii, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and laurel. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a dark blue drupe, often containing a single seed, and it is adapted to thrive in humid, tropical climates.

Ocotea gurgelii is also valued for its essential oils, which are used in traditional medicine and perfumery.
