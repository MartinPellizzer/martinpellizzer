Opuntia rhodantha var. xanthostemma

Opuntia rhodantha var. xanthostemma, commonly known as the yellow-flowered prickly pear, is a succulent cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its flattened, fleshy stems known as cladodes, which are often ribbed and covered in spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, a red, fleshy pear, is edible and contains numerous seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in arid environments.
