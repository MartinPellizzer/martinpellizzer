Orthopogon remotus

Orthopogon remotus, commonly known as the slender tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open habitats.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
