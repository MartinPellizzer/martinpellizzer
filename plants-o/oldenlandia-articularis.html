Oldenlandia articularis

Oldenlandia articularis, commonly known as the jointleaf or spiderling, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling spider-like structures, hence its common name. The plant has heart-shaped or oval leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its unique appearance and ease of cultivation.
