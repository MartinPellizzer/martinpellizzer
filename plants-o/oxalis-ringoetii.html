Oxalis ringoetii

Oxalis ringoetii, commonly known as the white oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It produces small, edible fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Oxalis ringoetii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
