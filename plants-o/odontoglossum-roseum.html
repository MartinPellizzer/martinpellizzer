Odontoglossum roseum

Odontoglossum roseum, commonly known as the pink orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are long and strap-like, often with a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Odontoglossum roseum is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and relatively easy care requirements in cultivation.
