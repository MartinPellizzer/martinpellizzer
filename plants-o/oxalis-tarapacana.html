Oxalis tarapacana

Oxalis tarapacana, commonly known as the Andes star or silver star, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive silver or white veining, giving it a unique appearance. It thrives in cool, high-altitude environments and requires well-drained soil.

Oxalis tarapacana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to container gardening.
