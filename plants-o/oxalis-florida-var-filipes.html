Oxalis florida var. filipes

Oxalis florida var. filipes, commonly known as the creeping wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial with trifoliate leaves and small, delicate flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread and form dense mats. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive, heart-shaped leaves.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive in partial shade and moist, well-drained soils.
