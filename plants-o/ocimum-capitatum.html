Ocimum capitatum

Ocimum capitatum, commonly known as the Cape mint or wild basil, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are often aromatic and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its essential oils contain compounds like linalool and estragol, which contribute to its fragrance and potential medicinal uses.

Ocimum capitatum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported calming and digestive properties.
