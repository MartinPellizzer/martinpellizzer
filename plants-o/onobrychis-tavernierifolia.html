Onobrychis tavernierifolia

Onobrychis tavernierifolia, commonly known as the Mediterranean broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
