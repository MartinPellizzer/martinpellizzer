Oxytropis iridum

Oxytropis iridum, commonly known as blue locust, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and Mongolia. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, with slender stems and small, compound leaves. It produces clusters of blue to violet pea-shaped flowers, which bloom in early summer.

Oxytropis iridum is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.
