Ocotea variabilis

Ocotea variabilis, commonly known as the variable ocotea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and fragrant, small flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, woody drupe that contains a single seed.

Ocotea variabilis is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional crafts and as a source of essential oils.
