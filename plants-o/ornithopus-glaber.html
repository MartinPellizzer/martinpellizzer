Ornithopus glaber

Ornithopus glaber, commonly known as slender bird's-foot trefoil, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is often found in grasslands, meadows, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils. Its seeds are encased in a distinctive, hairy pod, and it is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Ornithopus glaber is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
