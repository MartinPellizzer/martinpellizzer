Osmunda cinnamomea f. latipinnula

Osmunda cinnamomea f. latipinnula, commonly known as the broad-pinnate cinnamon fern, is a fern species characterized by its distinctive, elongated fronds.

It features a robust, upright rhizome that supports the tall, feather-like fronds with a cinnamon-brown coloration on the fertile spore-bearing structures. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands and wetlands. Its fronds can reach lengths of up to 1.5 meters, making it a striking addition to the forest understory.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting various wildlife species.
