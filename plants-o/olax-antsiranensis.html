Olax antsiranensis

Olax antsiranensis, commonly known as the Madagascar olive, is a small evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Olacaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences, followed by woody, egg-shaped capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
