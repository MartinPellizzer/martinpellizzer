Ocotea reticulata

Ocotea reticulata, commonly known as the laceleaf ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its finely veined, dark green, leathery leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests.

Ocotea reticulata is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional crafts and as a source of essential oils.
