Oxalis acetosella

Oxalis acetosella, commonly known as wood sorrel, is a flowering plant belonging to the Oxalidaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves contain oxalic acid, which can be toxic in large quantities.

Oxalis acetosella is often found in forest floors and is known for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions.
