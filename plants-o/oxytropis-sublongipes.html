Oxytropis sublongipes

Oxytropis sublongipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed locoweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its toxic properties, it is considered a noxious weed in some regions.
