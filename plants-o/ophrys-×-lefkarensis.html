Ophrys × lefkarensis

Ophrys × lefkarensis, commonly known as the Lefkara orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the island of Cyprus.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, bee-mimicking flowers that play a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in rocky, calcareous soils and thrives in the Mediterranean climate. Its floral structure features a labellum that resembles a female bee, attracting male bees for pollination.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
