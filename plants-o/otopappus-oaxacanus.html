Otopappus oaxacanus

Otopappus oaxacanus, commonly known as the Oaxacan thistle, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and produces purple to pink flowers. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a strong, bitter taste.
