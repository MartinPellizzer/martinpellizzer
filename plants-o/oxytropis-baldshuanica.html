Oxytropis baldshuanica

Oxytropis baldshuanica, commonly known as the Balkhash oxytropis, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale purple or white, arranged in racemes. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in steppes and rocky soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects in dry regions.
