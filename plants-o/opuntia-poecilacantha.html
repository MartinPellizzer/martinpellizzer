Opuntia poecilacantha

Opuntia poecilacantha, commonly known as the spiny prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in sharp spines and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits known as tunas, which are often used in local cuisines. Opuntia poecilacantha thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting well to hot and dry climates.

Its structure and growth patterns make it a resilient and ecologically significant plant in its native habitat.
