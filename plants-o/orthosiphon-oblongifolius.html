Orthosiphon oblongifolius

Orthosiphon oblongifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaf spirea, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, oblong leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or near water bodies.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and potential medicinal applications.
