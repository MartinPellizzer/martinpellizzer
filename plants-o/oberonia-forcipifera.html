Oberonia forcipifera

Oberonia forcipifera, commonly known as the sword orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, grass-like foliage and produces striking, lance-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest understories, where it thrives in humid, well-drained soil. Its unique floral structure includes a spurred lip that aids in pollination by specific insect species.

Oberonia forcipifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
