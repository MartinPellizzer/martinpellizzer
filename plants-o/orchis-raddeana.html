Orchis raddeana

Orchis raddeana, commonly known as the Radde's orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stem that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that are clustered in racemes, with a distinctive lip petal that is often adorned with intricate patterns. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged along the stem, and it thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils.

Orchis raddeana is typically found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it blooms during the summer months.
