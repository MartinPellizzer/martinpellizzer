Odontochilus longiflorus

Odontochilus longiflorus, commonly known as the long-flowered toothed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers with a distinctive toothed lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
