Onoseris turbacensis

Onoseris turbacensis, commonly known as the Turban Thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads made up of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, demonstrating a high degree of adaptability to harsh climates. Its leaves are generally lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are usually yellow or white.

Onoseris turbacensis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small invertebrates.
