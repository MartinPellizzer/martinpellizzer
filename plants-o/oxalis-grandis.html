Oxalis grandis

Oxalis grandis, commonly known as the giant wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive red veins and produces small, five-petaled yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and along stream banks. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a stem that often branches near the base.

Oxalis grandis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in partial shade.
