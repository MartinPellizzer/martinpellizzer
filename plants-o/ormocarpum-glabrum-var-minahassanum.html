Ormocarpum glabrum var. minahassanum

Ormocarpum glabrum var. minahassanum, commonly known as the Minahassa orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of small, white or pale pink blooms that are highly attractive to pollinators. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, organic-rich soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
