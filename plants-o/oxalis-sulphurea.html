Oxalis sulphurea

Oxalis sulphurea, commonly known as the yellow wood sorrel, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically bright green with a yellowish or reddish tinge. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Oxalis sulphurea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
