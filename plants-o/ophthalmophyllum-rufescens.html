Ophthalmophyllum rufescens

Ophthalmophyllum rufescens, commonly known as the red-eyed daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with bright yellow centers and white petals. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, rounded leaves are arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces numerous flowering stems during the warmer months.

Ophthalmophyllum rufescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
