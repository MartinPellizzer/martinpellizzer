Oxalis priceae subsp. colorea

Oxalis priceae subsp.

colorea, commonly known as the pink wood sorrel, is a perennial herb native to South Africa. It features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive pinkish hue and small, delicate flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland settings. Its foliage is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive coloration.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more vibrant leaf color compared to other oxalis species.
