Ochna miersii

Ochna miersii, commonly known as the Miers' ochna, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its ornamental, palmate leaves and showy, yellowish-orange flowers. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in shrubland or woodland habitats.

Ochna miersii is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
