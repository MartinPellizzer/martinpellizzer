Oryza rubribarbis

Oryza rubribarbis, commonly known as the red-barbed ricegrass, is a grass species native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive red-colored awns, which are bristle-like structures at the base of its florets. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, preferring moist soils. Its slender, grass-like leaves are usually arranged in a dense, tufted cluster at the base.

Oryza rubribarbis is primarily of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and potential ecological roles in wetland ecosystems.
