Oxalis obliqua

Oxalis obliqua, commonly known as the zigzag oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, zigzag-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, often accompanied by orange or red bracts. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Oxalis obliqua is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
