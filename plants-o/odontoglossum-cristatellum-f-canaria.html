Odontoglossum cristatellum f. canaria

Odontoglossum cristatellum f. canaria, commonly known as the Canary Island orchid, is a rare and unique orchid species native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, sympodial growth habit. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers that are typically pale green to white in color, with a distinctive lip structure. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern.
