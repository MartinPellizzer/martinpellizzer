Orobus canescens

Orobus canescens, commonly known as the woolly orchid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish foliage and produces showy, violet-blue flowers during the spring and early summer. The plant belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is known for its compact growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
