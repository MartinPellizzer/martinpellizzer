Odontoglossum harryanum var. pavonium

Odontoglossum harryanum var. pavonium, commonly known as the Pavonii Orchid, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety petals that often display a range of colors from deep purples to rich reds. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers that bloom during the cooler months, typically between September and December. Its leaves are broad and dark green, with a waxy texture that helps it retain moisture in its arid environment.

This orchid is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its beauty and uniqueness.
