Orobanche atrorubens

Orobanche atrorubens, commonly known as the red-rooted broomrape, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is found in various regions across Europe and Asia. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or reddish in color. Its life cycle involves germinating near the roots of host plants and establishing a parasitic connection.

Due to its lack of leaves and photosynthetic ability, it plays a unique role in ecological interactions within its habitat.
