Oreomecon Radicata Subsp. Ovatiloba

Himalayan poppy, scientifically known as Oreomecon radicata subsp. ovatiloba, is a striking plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas. With its vibrant orange petals and delicate appearance, it captivates botanists and nature enthusiasts alike. This subspecies thrives in harsh, rocky environments where few other plants can survive. Its unique adaptations allow it to flourish in extreme conditions, making it a symbol of resilience. Despite its beauty, the Himalayan poppy remains a rare and endangered species, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
