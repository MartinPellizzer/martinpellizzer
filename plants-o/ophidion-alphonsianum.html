Ophidion alphonsianum

Ophidion alphonsianum, commonly known as the Indian snake plant, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are typically green with a reddish tinge along the edges. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in spikes, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are succulent and can store water, making it drought-tolerant.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
