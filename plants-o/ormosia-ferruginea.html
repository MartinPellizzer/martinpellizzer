Ormosia ferruginea

Ormosia ferruginea, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, reddish-brown wood that is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant has bipinnate leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by pod-like fruits containing flat, dark brown seeds.

Ormosia ferruginea is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it adaptable to different tropical environments.
