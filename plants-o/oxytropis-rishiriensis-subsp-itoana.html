Oxytropis rishiriensis subsp. itoana

Oxytropis rishiriensis subsp.

itoana, commonly known as the Itoana locoweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae. It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Native to Japan, it thrives in rocky and arid environments.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often avoided by livestock and wildlife.
