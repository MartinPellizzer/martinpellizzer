Ocotea duckei

Ocotea duckei, commonly known as the Brazilian camphor tree, is a member of the Lauraceae family and is native to the Atlantic rainforests of Brazil.

It is a medium to large evergreen tree that can reach heights of up to 30 meters. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a dark blue drupe containing a single seed.

Ocotea duckei is valued for its timber, which is used in construction and furniture-making due to its durability and resistance to decay.
