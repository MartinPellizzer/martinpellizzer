Octomeria unguiculata

Octomeria unguiculata, commonly known as the hookleaf cactus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant has a compact growth habit, often forming dense, low-lying clumps. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss in its harsh environment.

Octomeria unguiculata is adapted to survive in extreme drought conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
