Ochna tenuipes

Ochna tenuipes, commonly known as the slender ochna, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by distinctive, round, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
