Oxalis grammopetala

Oxalis grammopetala, commonly known as the pink oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pink or white flowers. The plant produces yellowish-orange seed pods that split open to release seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Oxalis grammopetala is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
