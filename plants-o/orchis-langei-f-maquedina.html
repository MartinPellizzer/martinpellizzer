Orchis langei f. maquedina

Orchis langei f. maquedina, commonly known as the Maquedina orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral morphology, including intricate petals and a distinct lip structure. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in montane forests, where it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its conservation status is threatened due to habitat loss and over-collection, making it a subject of interest for botanical research.

The species exhibits a short flowering period, usually in late spring or early summer, adding to its ecological and horticultural significance.
