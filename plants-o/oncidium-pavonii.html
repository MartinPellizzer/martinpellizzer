Oncidium pavonii

Oncidium pavonii, commonly known as the pink orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in varying environmental conditions.

Oncidium pavonii is popular in horticulture for its long-lasting blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
