Oriastrum gossipinum

Oriastrum gossipinum, commonly known as the desert aster, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows from 10 to 30 centimeters tall, with small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the spring. It has a deep root system that allows it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Oriastrum gossipinum plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing nectar for pollinators.
