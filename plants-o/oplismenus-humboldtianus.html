Oplismenus humboldtianus

Oplismenus humboldtianus, commonly known as the evergreen wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 15 centimeters. The plant features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a preference for moist, well-drained soil.

Oplismenus humboldtianus is often found in shaded, woodland environments.
