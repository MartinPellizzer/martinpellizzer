Oxytropis ponomarjevii var. grandiflora

Oxytropis ponomarjevii var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils and withstand drought conditions.

This species is of interest in ecological studies due to its role in nitrogen fixation and its potential for use in land rehabilitation projects.
