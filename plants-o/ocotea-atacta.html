Ocotea atacta

Ocotea atacta, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, aromatic bark. Ocotea atacta produces small, white flowers followed by dark, oval berries.

It is often valued for its durable, reddish wood, which has been used historically in construction and craftsmanship.
