Opuntia alexanderi

Opuntia alexanderi, commonly known as the Alexander's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its flattened, fleshy stems that are covered in spiny areoles, which bear clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces bright red, fleshy fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting well to rocky and sandy soils.

Opuntia alexanderi plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
