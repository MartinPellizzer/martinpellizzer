Orobus pannonicus var. microrrhizus

Orobus pannonicus var. microrrhizus, commonly known as the small-rooted orobus, is a perennial herb native to the Pannonian region of Europe.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to semi-arid environments. Its small, rounded leaves and pale yellow flowers are typical of the genus Orobus.

It plays a minor role in local ecosystems but is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphological traits.
