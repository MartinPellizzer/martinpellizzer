Oenothera pallida subsp. trichocalyx

Oenothera pallida subsp.

trichocalyx, commonly known as the slender milkweed, is a perennial herb native to North America. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces showy, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges.

This subspecies is noted for its ecological importance in supporting native insect populations.
