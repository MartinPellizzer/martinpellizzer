Orthosiphon bartsioides

Orthosiphon bartsioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved horsegram, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its small, tubular flowers are typically arranged in whorled clusters, and it produces small, round, brown seeds.

Orthosiphon bartsioides is often grown for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
