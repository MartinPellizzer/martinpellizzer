Opulaster capitatus

Opulaster capitatus, commonly known as the golden cup plant, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, waxy leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in late spring. The plant is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its showy, composite flower heads. Opulaster capitatus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
