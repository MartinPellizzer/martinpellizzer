Oxycarpus cochinchinensis

Oxycarpus cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese starfruit or carambola, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that produces distinctive star-shaped fruits. The fruit's skin is typically green and turns yellow or orange when mature, with a sweet, tart flavor. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value and edible fruit make it both a horticultural and culinary interest.
