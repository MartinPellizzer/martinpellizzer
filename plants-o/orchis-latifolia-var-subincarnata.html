Orchis latifolia var. subincarnata

Orchis latifolia var. subincarnata, commonly known as the broad-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and the flowers are borne in a loose spike.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
