Oryzopsis setifolia

Oryzopsis setifolia, commonly known as silky grass or coastal silkygrass, is a perennial grass species native to the coastal regions of the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and long, narrow leaves with a silky texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spikelets, which are clustered along the stems. It thrives in wetland and marsh environments, often forming dense stands that provide habitat for various wildlife.

Oryzopsis setifolia plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting biodiversity in coastal ecosystems.
