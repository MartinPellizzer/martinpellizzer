Ophiopogon griffithii

Ophiopogon griffithii, commonly known as the Japanese forest grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features dense, grass-like foliage with narrow, dark green leaves that form a compact rosette. The plant produces small, pale green to white flowers in late spring to early summer, followed by dark blue to black berries. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often used in ornamental landscaping.

Ophiopogon griffithii is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and its historical use in traditional medicine.
