Oxalis humistrata

Oxalis humistrata, commonly known as the coastal oxalis, is a low-growing perennial plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish or purple tinge on the underside. The plant produces clusters of five-petaled yellow flowers, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal conditions with high salt exposure.

Oxalis humistrata is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and resilience to harsh environments.
