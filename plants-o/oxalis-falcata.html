Oxalis falcata

Oxalis falcata, commonly known as the sickleleaf wood sorrel, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive sickle-shaped leaflet, and its flowers are typically yellow with five petals. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Oxalis falcata thrives in moist, wooded areas and prefers partial shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique addition to native plant gardens and ecological studies.
