Otanthera lamii

Otanthera lamii, commonly known as the evening primrose, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in prairies and disturbed areas.

Its botanical significance includes its role in ecological systems and its historical use in traditional medicine.
