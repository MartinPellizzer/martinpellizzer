Ocimum neumannii

Ocimum neumannii, commonly known as Neumann's basil, is a species of flowering plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Ocimum neumannii is valued for its essential oils, which are extracted from its leaves and used in aromatherapy and culinary applications.
