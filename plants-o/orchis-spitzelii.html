Orchis spitzelii

Orchis spitzelii, commonly known as the Spitzel's orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers arranged in a spike, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and are arranged alternately along the stem.

Orchis spitzelii thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
