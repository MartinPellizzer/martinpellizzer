Orobus canescens var. digitatus

Orobus canescens var. digitatus, commonly known as the hairy legume, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, erect stems covered in fine, whitish hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pale yellow to reddish-brown, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately divided, and often have a fuzzy texture.

This species is adapted to dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
