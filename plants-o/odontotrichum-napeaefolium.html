Odontotrichum napeaefolium

Odontotrichum napeaefolium, commonly known as the narrow-leafed beardtongue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
