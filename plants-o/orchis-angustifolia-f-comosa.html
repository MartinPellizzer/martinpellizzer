Orchis angustifolia f. comosa

Orchis angustifolia f. comosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and produces delicate, pale pink to white flowers with a distinctive spurred lip. This orchid is typically found in damp, shaded woodland habitats across Europe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often associated with calcareous substrates.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
