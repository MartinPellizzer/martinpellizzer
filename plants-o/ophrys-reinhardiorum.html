Ophrys reinhardiorum

Ophrys reinhardiorum, commonly known as the Reinhardt's bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which closely resembles a female bee, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant produces a single, showy flower with a complex morphology that mimics the appearance and scent of a bee. It thrives in specific habitats, such as calcareous soils in open woodland or grassland areas.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species.
