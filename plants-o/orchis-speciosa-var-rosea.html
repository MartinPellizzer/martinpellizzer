Orchis speciosa var. rosea

Orchis speciosa var. rosea, commonly known as the rose-colored orchis, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its showy, violet to pink flowers with a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in rich, well-drained soils. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and it produces a tall stem that bears clusters of fragrant blooms.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often found in botanical gardens and natural habitats.
