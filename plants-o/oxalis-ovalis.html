Oxalis ovalis

Oxalis ovalis, commonly known as the heartleaf wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are heart-shaped and often tinged with red or purple veins. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

Oxalis ovalis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and delicate flowers.
