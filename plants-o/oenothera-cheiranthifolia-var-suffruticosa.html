Oenothera cheiranthifolia var. suffruticosa

Oenothera cheiranthifolia var. suffruticosa, commonly known as the coastal evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of California and Baja California.

It is characterized by its erect, woody stems and showy yellow flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. It thrives in coastal climates with mild temperatures and is often found in dunes and coastal scrub habitats.

Oenothera cheiranthifolia var. suffruticosa is an important species for pollinators and contributes to the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.
