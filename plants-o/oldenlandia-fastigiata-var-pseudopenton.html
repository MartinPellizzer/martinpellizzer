Oldenlandia fastigiata var. pseudopenton

Oldenlandia fastigiata var. pseudopenton, commonly known as the velvetleaf or false hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a waxy texture, while its stems are usually hairy.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
