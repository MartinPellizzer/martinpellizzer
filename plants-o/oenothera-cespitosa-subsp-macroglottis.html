Oenothera cespitosa subsp. macroglottis

Oenothera cespitosa subsp.

macroglottis, commonly known as the tall evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to North America. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow flowers that open in the evening.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as prairies and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and native plant gardening.
