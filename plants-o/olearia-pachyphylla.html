Olearia pachyphylla

Olearia pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved daisy, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in coastal and subalpine regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to retain moisture in its native environment.

Olearia pachyphylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
