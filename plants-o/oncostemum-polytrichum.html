Oncostemum polytrichum

Oncostemum polytrichum, commonly known as the silky hairgrass, is a member of the Poaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender, wiry stems and dense, soft foliage that gives it a silvery appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle made up of small, spikelet-like flowers.

Oncostemum polytrichum is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and ability to form dense, low-growing mats.
