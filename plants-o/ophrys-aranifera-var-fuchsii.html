Ophrys aranifera var. fuchsii

Ophrys aranifera var. fuchsii, commonly known as the Fuchs' bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brown in color, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species in many areas.
