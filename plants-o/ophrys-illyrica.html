Ophrys illyrica

Ophrys illyrica, commonly known as the Illyrian bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers are usually pale green to brownish in color, with intricate patterns that aid in deception.

Ophrys illyrica is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its evolutionary adaptation in pollination strategies.
