Ormosia costulata var. trifoliolata

Ormosia costulata var. trifoliolata, commonly known as the three-leaf ormosia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged in a fan-like pattern. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody pods containing flat, elliptical seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
