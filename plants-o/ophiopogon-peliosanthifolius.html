Ophiopogon peliosanthifolius

Ophiopogon peliosanthifolius, commonly known as the Chinese grass-leaf lily, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and slightly curved, with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Ophiopogon peliosanthifolius is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional Chinese medicine.
