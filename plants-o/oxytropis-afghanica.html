Oxytropis afghanica

Oxytropis afghanica, commonly known as Afghan locoweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink to purple in color. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Oxytropis afghanica is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.
