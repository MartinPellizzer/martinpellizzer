Oxalis subintegrifolia

Oxalis subintegrifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive pattern of white veins, and it produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its calcifuge nature, meaning it prefers acidic soils.

Oxalis subintegrifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
