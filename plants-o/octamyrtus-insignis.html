Octamyrtus insignis

Octamyrtus insignis, commonly known as the Christmas bush, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom during the winter months, which is unusual for many plants in its region. Its leaves are narrow, leathery, and have a glossy appearance, providing a striking contrast to the delicate flowers.

The plant is well adapted to coastal conditions, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils.
