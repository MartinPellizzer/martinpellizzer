Ostericum citriodorum

Ostericum citriodorum, commonly known as lemon thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are fragrant, with a distinct citrus scent, making it popular in culinary and medicinal uses.

Ostericum citriodorum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or limestone soils.
