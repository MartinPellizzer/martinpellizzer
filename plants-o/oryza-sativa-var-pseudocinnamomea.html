Oryza sativa var. pseudocinnamomea

Oryza sativa var. pseudocinnamomea, commonly known as the cinnamon rice, is a variant of rice characterized by its unique aromatic properties.

This plant belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is native to Southeast Asia. It is distinguished by its reddish-brown grains that emit a subtle cinnamon-like fragrance when cooked. The botanical features include slender, cylindrical spikelets and small, two-lobed glumes.

It is primarily cultivated for its culinary value and is often used in traditional dishes across its native region.
