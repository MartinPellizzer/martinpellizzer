Ostrya vulgaris var. eglandulosa

Ostrya vulgaris var. eglandulosa, commonly known as the winged elm, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, winged fruits that hang in clusters, aiding in wind dispersal. The tree has alternate, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth margin. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in floodplain forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological stability and wildlife habitat.
