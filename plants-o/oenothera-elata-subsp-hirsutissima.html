Oenothera elata subsp. hirsutissima

Oenothera elata subsp.

hirsutissima, commonly known as the hirsute evening primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its subspecific name "hirsutissima.".

The plant produces showy yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats like prairies and disturbed areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
