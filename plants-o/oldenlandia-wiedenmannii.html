Oldenlandia wiedenmannii

Oldenlandia wiedenmannii, commonly known as the silverleaf spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant features slender stems and small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a silvery or whitish pubescence. Its flowers are usually white or pale blue, with five petals and a distinctive spurred structure.

Oldenlandia wiedenmannii is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
