Odontocarya scabra

Odontocarya scabra, commonly known as the roughleaf mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has a spreading growth habit. Its botanical features include opposite leaves with serrated margins and showy bracteate inflorescences.

Odontocarya scabra is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
