Ophiorrhiza roxburghiana

Ophiorrhiza roxburghiana, commonly known as the "white sand tree," is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are typically clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release tiny seeds when mature.

Ophiorrhiza roxburghiana is often found growing on rocky or sandy soils in tropical and subtropical regions.
