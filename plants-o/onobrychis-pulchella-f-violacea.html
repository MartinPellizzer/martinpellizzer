Onobrychis pulchella f. violacea

Onobrychis pulchella f. violacea, commonly known as the purple velvet bean, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value to gardens. The plant features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets, and its stems are often hairy. It is native to regions of southern Europe and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and nitrogen-fixing ability.

The variety f. violacea is distinguished by its more intense purple flower color compared to the typical form.
