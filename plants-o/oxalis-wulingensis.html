Oxalis wulingensis

Oxalis wulingensis, commonly known as the Wuling wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to China.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its calcifuge nature, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. Its ornamental value is due to its delicate foliage and flowers, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and shaded areas.

Oxalis wulingensis is also noted for its ability to tolerate partial shade and its use in traditional Chinese medicine.
