Oxytropis varians

Oxytropis varians, commonly known as variable locoweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.
