Opuntia joconostle

Opuntia joconostle, commonly known as the Joconostle cactus, is a species native to the dry regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems that are often ribbed and covered in spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tunas, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

Opuntia joconostle is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.
