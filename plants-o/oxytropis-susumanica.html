Oxytropis susumanica

Oxytropis susumanica, commonly known as the Susuman locoweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in the summer months. Oxytropis susumanica is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and high altitude regions.

It is also known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to grazing animals.
