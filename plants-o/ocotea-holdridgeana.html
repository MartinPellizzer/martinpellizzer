Ocotea holdridgeana

Ocotea holdridgeana, commonly known as the Holdridge's ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, lowland forests and thrives in shaded environments. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils, which have been used in traditional medicine.

The species is also valued for its wood, which is durable and used in local crafts and construction.
