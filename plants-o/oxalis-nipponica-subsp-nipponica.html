Oxalis nipponica subsp. nipponica

Oxalis nipponica subsp.

nipponica, commonly known as Japanese wood sorrel, is a perennial herb native to Japan. It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish or purple hue, often adorned with small white flowers.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its calcifugous nature, meaning it thrives in soils low in calcium.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
