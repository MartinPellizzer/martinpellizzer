Orchis cruentiformis

Orchis cruentiformis, commonly known as the blood-red orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its vivid red and purple flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns resembling blood droplets. This perennial herb is native to specific regions of Europe, particularly in the UK and parts of Scandinavia. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodland habitats, thriving in rich, loamy soils.

Its unique floral structure and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
