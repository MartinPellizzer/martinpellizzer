Osmoxylon globulare

Osmoxylon globulare, commonly known as the globe-shaped osmoxylon, is a unique member of the Osmoxylon genus, characterized by its distinctive spherical or globular growth form.

It is a type of lichen, specifically a foliose lichen, which means it has leaf-like structures that are loosely attached to the substrate. The plant typically grows on bark, rocks, or soil in moist, shaded environments, often in temperate regions. Its thallus is usually greenish to yellowish in color, and it may produce spores in small, clustered structures called soredia.

Osmoxylon globulare plays a role in ecological succession and is sometimes used in traditional medicine, though its medicinal properties are not well-documented.
