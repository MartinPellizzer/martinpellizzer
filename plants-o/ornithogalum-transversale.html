Ornithogalum transversale

Ornithogalum transversale, commonly known as the transverse belladonna, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a bulbous base with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant is characterized by its nocturnal blooming habit, with flowers opening at night and closing in the morning. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

The plant contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to livestock and humans if ingested.
