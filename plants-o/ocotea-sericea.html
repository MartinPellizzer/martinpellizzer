Ocotea sericea

Ocotea sericea, commonly known as silky ocotea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it its common name. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue, berry-like fruits.

Ocotea sericea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
