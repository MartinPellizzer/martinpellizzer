Ocotea dominicana

Ocotea dominicana, commonly known as the Dominican mahogany, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Caribbean, particularly the Dominican Republic.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic trees like laurel and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that appear in clusters. Its fruits are small, spherical berries that turn from green to dark blue as they mature.

The tree is valued for its durable, aromatic wood and is often found in moist, shaded habitats in tropical forests.
