Ophrys × aschersonii nothosubsp. sardoa

Ophrys × aschersonii nothosubsp.

sardoa, commonly known as the Sardonic Ophrys, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its intricate, bee-mimicking flowers that play a crucial role in its pollination strategy.

The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded habitats. Its inflorescence consists of a raceme of showy, hooded flowers that resemble female bees, attracting male bees for pollination.

This nothosubspecies exhibits morphological and genetic variability due to its hybrid origin.
