Oxymitra grandifolia

Oxymitra grandifolia, commonly known as the large-leaved oxymitra, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with a distinctive tubular shape. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Oxymitra grandifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
