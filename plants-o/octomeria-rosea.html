Octomeria rosea

Octomeria rosea, commonly known as the roseate octomeris, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy coating.

Octomeria rosea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
