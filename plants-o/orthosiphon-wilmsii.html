Orthosiphon wilmsii

Orthosiphon wilmsii, commonly known as Java mint or Malabar mint, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, fragrant flowers. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.
