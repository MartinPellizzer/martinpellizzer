Ophiorrhiza mungos var. angustifolia

Ophiorrhiza mungos var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaf marsh bean, is a flowering plant species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small shrub characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in wet, swampy areas and is adapted to waterlogged soils. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland habitats and supporting local biodiversity.
