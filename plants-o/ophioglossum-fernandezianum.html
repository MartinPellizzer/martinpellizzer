Ophioglossum fernandezianum

Ophioglossum fernandezianum, commonly known as the fernandezian fern, is a rare and ancient fern species native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the family Ophioglossaceae and is characterized by its unique, primitive morphology. The plant features a short, rhizomatous stem with a single, large, lanceolate leaf that is divided into two asymmetrical lobes. It produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in specialized structures called sporangial clusters.

Due to its restricted habitat and low population numbers, it is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts in preserving its ecological role.
