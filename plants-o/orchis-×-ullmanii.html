Orchis × ullmanii

Orchis × ullmanii, commonly known as the Ullmann's orchid, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Orchis morio and Orchis purpurea.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet to purple flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing intricate, lip-like petals that are often adorned with distinctive markings. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows, preferring well-drained, loamy soils.

Orchis × ullmanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
