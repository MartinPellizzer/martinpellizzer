Ophrys pamphylica

Ophrys pamphylica, commonly known as the Pamphylian Ophrys, is a species of orchid native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Greece and Turkey.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, with a labellum that mimics the appearance of a female bee, aiding in its specialized pollination mechanism. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its flowers are usually pale green or brown, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Ophrys pamphylica is a member of the Orchidaceae family, known for its complex and often deceptive floral morphology.
