Ocimum punctatum

Ocimum punctatum, commonly known as the spotted basil, is a member of the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its aromatic leaves with distinctive white spots. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 cm and has opposite, ovate leaves. Its flowers are small, clustered in whorls, and are usually pale purple or white.

Ocimum punctatum is valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
