Onobrychis tanaitica

Onobrychis tanaitica, commonly known as the Tanaitic goat's rue, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny conditions. The plant produces small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a fibrous root system.

Onobrychis tanaitica is often found in scrubland and open woodlands, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
