Oxybaphus prostratus

Oxybaphus prostratus, commonly known as the prostrate oxybaphus, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss and protect against intense sunlight.

Oxybaphus prostratus is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
