Oroya borchersii f. fuscata

Oroya borchersii f. fuscata, commonly known as the dark form of the rock rose, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dark purple to almost black foliage, which gives it its distinguishing appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular plant among gardeners in temperate climates.
