Onobrychis sativa var. bifera

Onobrychis sativa var. bifera, commonly known as red clover, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pink or red flowers that bloom in upright clusters. The plant has a deep root system that helps in soil fixation and nutrient cycling. It is often used in forage agriculture and as a cover crop due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.

Its botanical features make it valuable in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
