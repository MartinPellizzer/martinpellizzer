Ourouparia polycephala

Ourouparia polycephala, commonly known as the many-headed flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange-red flowers that appear in clusters, giving it its common name. The plant has a rugged, fibrous bark and can grow up to 20 meters tall. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, with a glossy green upper surface.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
