Ocotea baracoensis

Ocotea baracoensis, commonly known as the Brazilian camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark-green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by dark blue to black berries. Its wood is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine and as a source of camphor.

The species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
