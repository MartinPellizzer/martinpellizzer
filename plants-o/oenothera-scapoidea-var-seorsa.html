Oenothera scapoidea var. seorsa

Oenothera scapoidea var. seorsa, commonly known as the narrowleaf evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and showy yellow flowers that open in the evening. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oval seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry grasslands and open woodlands.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and native plant gardening.
