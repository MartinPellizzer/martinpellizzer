Ochna chirindica

Ochna chirindica, commonly known as the African starwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed, which is encased in a hard, woody capsule.

Ochna chirindica is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses.
