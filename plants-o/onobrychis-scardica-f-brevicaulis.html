Onobrychis scardica f. brevicaulis

Onobrychis scardica f. brevicaulis, commonly known as the short-stalked field vetch, is a leguminous plant characterized by its compact growth habit and trifoliate leaves.

It produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a deep root system that helps it thrive in well-drained soils, often found in grasslands and disturbed areas. It is a hardy perennial that is well adapted to temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ecological and agricultural purposes.
