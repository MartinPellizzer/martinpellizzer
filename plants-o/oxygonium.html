Oxygonium

Oxygonium, commonly known as the waterwheel plant, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its whorled arrangement of leaves.

It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and marshes, where it can anchor itself to submerged surfaces. The plant's most distinctive feature is its spiral-arranged leaves that resemble the spokes of a wheel, aiding in light capture and gas exchange. Oxygonium is also notable for its ability to produce oxygen through photosynthesis, contributing to water quality.

Its simple structure and adaptability make it an important species in aquatic ecosystems.
