Oenothera stricta subsp. stricta

Oenothera stricta subsp.

stricta, commonly known as the narrowleaf evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces showy yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

The species is known for its medicinal properties, with its seeds containing high levels of essential fatty acids.
