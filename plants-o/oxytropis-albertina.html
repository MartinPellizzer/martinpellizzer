Oxytropis albertina

Oxytropis albertina, commonly known as the Albertine milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia, including parts of Mongolia and China. The plant is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It produces yellow or reddish-purple pea-like flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

Oxytropis albertina is adapted to dry environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
