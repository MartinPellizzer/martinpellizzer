Oenothera rosea var. lyrata

Oenothera rosea var. lyrata, commonly known as the lyrata evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers that open in the evening and close by dawn. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique blooming behavior.
