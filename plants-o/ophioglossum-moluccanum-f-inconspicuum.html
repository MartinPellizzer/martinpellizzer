Ophioglossum moluccanum f. inconspicuum

Ophioglossum moluccanum f. inconspicuum, commonly known as the Moluccan adder's mouth fern, is a rare and distinctive fern species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Ophioglossaceae, which is characterized by its unique, primitive morphology. The plant features a short, erect rhizome with a single, pinnately compound leaf that is often inconspicuous compared to the typical form of the species. Its spores are produced in specialized sporangia located on the underside of the leaf, a feature common to many ferns.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant plant.
