Origanum wallichianum

Origanum wallichianum, commonly known as Kashmiri oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayas.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and cooking. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees. Its essential oils contain compounds such as thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Origanum wallichianum thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.
