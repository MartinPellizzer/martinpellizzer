Orchis maculata var. albicans

Orchis maculata var. albicans, commonly known as the white-streaked orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stem and showy, pale yellow to white flowers with dark markings. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds.

The variety albicans is distinguished by its predominantly white floral coloration compared to the more commonly seen spotted varieties.
