Ocimum febrifugum

Ocimum febrifugum, commonly known as holy basil, is an aromatic herb native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or purple in color. Its leaves are rich in essential oils, which contribute to its distinct fragrance and medicinal properties.

Ocimum febrifugum is widely used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
