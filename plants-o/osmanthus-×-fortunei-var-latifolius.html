Osmanthus × fortunei var. latifolius

Osmanthus × fortunei var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf osmanthus, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, dark green leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers in late summer to early autumn, which are highly valued for their aromatic properties. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its leaves and flowers are used in traditional Chinese medicine and as a source of essential oils.
