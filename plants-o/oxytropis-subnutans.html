Oxytropis subnutans

Oxytropis subnutans, commonly known as the nodding locoweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes, and is often found in areas with well-drained soils.
