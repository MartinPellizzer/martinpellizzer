Othonna lingua

Othonna lingua, commonly known as the narrow-leaved othonna, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Othonna lingua is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
