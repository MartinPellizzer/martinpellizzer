Oenothera contorta

Oenothera contorta, commonly known as prairie smoke, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its unique, plume-like inflorescence that resembles a smoke-like plume, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and sandy soils. Its flowers are usually pink to reddish in color and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Oenothera contorta is a perennial species that produces a capsule fruit containing numerous small seeds.
