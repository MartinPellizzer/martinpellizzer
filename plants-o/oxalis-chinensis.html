Oxalis chinensis

Oxalis chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. It produces edible leaves and flowers, though they should be consumed in moderation due to their oxalic acid content.

Oxalis chinensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
