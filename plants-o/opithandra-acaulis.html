Opithandra acaulis

Opithandra acaulis, commonly known as the rock heath, is a low-growing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and sand dunes. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are pale blue or white, arranged in dense clusters, and bloom in the spring.

Opithandra acaulis is adapted to harsh coastal environments, making it a hardy and resilient species.
