Oxalis corniculata subsp. purpurea

Oxalis corniculata subsp.

purpurea, commonly known as the purple wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to Asia, particularly found in regions like China, Japan, and Korea. It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each leaflet being heart-shaped and often tinged with purple or reddish hues.

The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically yellow with five petals and a distinctive spurred structure. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along the edges of forests. Oxalis corniculata subsp.

purpurea is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
