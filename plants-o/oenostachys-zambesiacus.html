Oenostachys zambesiacus

Oenostachys zambesiacus, commonly known as the Zambesia grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features tall, slender stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which are small, clustered flower clusters.

Oenostachys zambesiacus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its adaptability and drought tolerance.
