Ophrys × provecta

Ophrys × provecta, commonly known as the bee orchid hybrid, is a fascinating plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera, showcasing a blend of characteristics from both parent species. The plant is notable for its highly specialized floral structure, which mimics the appearance of female bees to attract male bees for pollination. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow with intricate patterns that resemble insect bodies, enhancing its mimicry.

This botanical feature makes it an important subject in the study of plant evolution and pollination strategies.
