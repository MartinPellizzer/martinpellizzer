Orchis chrysea

Orchis chrysea, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow and green flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in woodland habitats, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are long and grass-like, and the flowers are arranged in a loose spike.

Orchis chrysea is a perennial herb that plays a role in supporting local pollinators and contributes to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.
