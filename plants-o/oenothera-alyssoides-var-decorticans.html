Oenothera alyssoides var. decorticans

Oenothera alyssoides var. decorticans, commonly known as the brittlestem evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems that become brittle when dry, and its showy yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oval seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and desert margins.

The species is part of the Onagraceae family, which includes other evening primroses and related flowering plants.
