Ophiopogon caulescens

Ophiopogon caulescens, commonly known as the slender lilyturf, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features grass-like foliage with narrow, dark green leaves that form a dense rosette. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in spike-like clusters during the summer months. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

Ophiopogon caulescens is also valued for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
