Octomeria aloefolia

Octomeria aloefolia, commonly known as the silverleaf octomeria, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are thick and waxy, adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments.

Octomeria aloefolia is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
