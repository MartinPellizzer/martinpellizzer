Orchis morio var. elatior

Orchis morio var. elatior, commonly known as the greater purple orchid, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy purple flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, with flowers that have a distinctive spurred lip. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its beauty.
