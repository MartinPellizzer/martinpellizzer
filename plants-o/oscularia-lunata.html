Oscularia lunata

Oscularia lunata, commonly known as the moon cactus, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and vibrant, colorful flowers that bloom in shades of red, yellow, and orange. The plant has a unique structure, with a core that is typically a green species of Kalanchoe and a colorful top that is a different species, often Copiapoa. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, requiring minimal water to prevent root rot.

Oscularia lunata is popular as a decorative plant due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
