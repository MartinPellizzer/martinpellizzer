Origanum virens

Origanum virens, commonly known as green oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often a vibrant green. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its aromatic leaves are used in culinary and medicinal applications, similar to other members of the mint family.
