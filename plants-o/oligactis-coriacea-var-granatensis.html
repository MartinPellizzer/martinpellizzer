Oligactis coriacea var. granatensis

Oligactis coriacea var. granatensis, commonly known as the Granatense Olive, is a variant of the olive tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by the characteristic olive fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a sunny to partially shaded environment.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its potential use in both culinary and medicinal applications.
