Ovieda ovalifolia

Ovieda ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf ovieda, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves, which are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of white or pale yellow. Its botanical features make it well adapted to humid, shaded environments.

Ovieda ovalifolia is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value in tropical gardens.
