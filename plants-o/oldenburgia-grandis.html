Oldenburgia grandis

Oldenburgia grandis, commonly known as the giant oldenburgia, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length, providing a dense canopy. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom during the warm season. Its root system is robust, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun conditions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes in tropical and subtropical climates.
