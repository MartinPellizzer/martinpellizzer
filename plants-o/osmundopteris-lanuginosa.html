Osmundopteris lanuginosa

Osmundopteris lanuginosa, commonly known as the downy water fern, is a species of aquatic fern belonging to the family Osmundaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety appearance. The plant thrives in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds and streams, where it can anchor itself to submerged or floating surfaces. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are typically found on the underside of the fronds, facilitating spore dispersal.

Osmundopteris lanuginosa plays an important ecological role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
