Oxybaphus nyctagineus var. latifolius

Oxybaphus nyctagineus var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are broad and ovate, often with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive addition to collections of ornamental and native plants.
