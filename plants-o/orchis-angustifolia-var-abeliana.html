Orchis angustifolia var. abeliana

Orchis angustifolia var. abeliana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved twayblade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, pale green or yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodland habitats and has a rhizomatous root system. It is notable for its specialized pollination mechanisms, often relying on specific insect species.

Due to its habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in many regions.
