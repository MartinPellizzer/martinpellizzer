Orphium

Orphium, commonly known as the bog orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Orchidaceae.

These plants are typically found in moist, acidic environments such as bogs and wet meadows. They are characterized by their small, delicate flowers that often have a greenish or yellowish hue. Orphium species are usually perennial herbs with rhizomatous growth, and they produce showy, orchid-like blooms.

Their botanical significance lies in their ecological role and their ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
