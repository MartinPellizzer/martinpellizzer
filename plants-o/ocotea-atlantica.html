Ocotea atlantica

Ocotea atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic ocotea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of eastern Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. It produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by dark blue berries.

Ocotea atlantica is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
