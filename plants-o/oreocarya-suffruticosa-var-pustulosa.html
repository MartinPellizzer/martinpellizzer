Oreocarya suffruticosa var. pustulosa

Oreocarya suffruticosa var. pustulosa, commonly known as the roughleaf dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that are often covered in small, raised bumps, giving it a rough texture. The plant produces bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a serrated margin, while the stems are erect and branching.

This species is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.
