Ocotea mirecolorata

Ocotea mirecolorata, commonly known as the purple-leafed ocotea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that often display a striking purple or reddish hue on the underside. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, dark blue-black berries.

Ocotea mirecolorata is often found in humid, shaded environments such as montane forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
