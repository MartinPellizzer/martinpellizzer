Ocotea chiapensis

Ocotea chiapensis, commonly known as Chiapan ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed surrounded by a fleshy, aromatic pulp.

Ocotea chiapensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
