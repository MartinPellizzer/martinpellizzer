Oenothera gayleana

Oenothera gayleana, commonly known as Gayleana evening primrose, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Oenothera gayleana is part of the Onagraceae family, which includes other evening primrose species known for their ecological and medicinal significance.
