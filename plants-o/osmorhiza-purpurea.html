Osmorhiza purpurea

Osmorhiza purpurea, commonly known as sweet cicely, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes plants like carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided leaves and umbel-like flower clusters. Osmorhiza purpurea thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.
