Ocotea rubra

Ocotea rubra, commonly known as red sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark reddish-brown heartwood that is highly valued for its durability and scent. The plant has simple, alternate, elliptic leaves with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside. Its small, greenish-white flowers are borne in clusters, and the fruit is a drupe with a smooth, glossy exterior.

Ocotea rubra is often found in humid lowland forests and is known for its ecological and economic significance.
