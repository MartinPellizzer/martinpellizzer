Orania gagavu

Orania gagavu, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety red petals and a distinctive yellow lip that attracts pollinators. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowering period occurs during the rainy season, and it produces small, fragrant blooms.

The leaves are broad and glossy, contributing to its ornamental value in horticulture.
