Orchis × parvifolia subsp. timbali

Orchis × parvifolia subsp.

timbali, commonly known as the Timbali orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to parts of Europe. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous habitats such as meadows and woodlands. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy surface.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
