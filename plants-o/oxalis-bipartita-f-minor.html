Oxalis bipartita f. minor

Oxalis bipartita f. minor, commonly known as the dwarf wood sorrel, is a compact, low-growing perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically lobed and often have a distinctive reddish or purple tinge. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of yellow or white, usually in early spring. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its foliage, which is both attractive and often used in horticultural displays.
