Oxalis sellowiana var. citrina

Oxalis sellowiana var. citrina, commonly known as the golden wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories. Its foliage and flowers are adorned with a striking yellow color, which is attributed to its high concentration of flavonoids.

Oxalis sellowiana var. citrina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
