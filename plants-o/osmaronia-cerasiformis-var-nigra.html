Osmaronia cerasiformis var. nigra

Osmaronia cerasiformis var. nigra, commonly known as black cherry plum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features dark, glossy leaves that turn vibrant red or orange in the fall. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in early spring, followed by edible, dark purple to black berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular ornamental and fruit-bearing plant.

Its compact size and seasonal color changes make it suitable for garden landscapes and hedging.
