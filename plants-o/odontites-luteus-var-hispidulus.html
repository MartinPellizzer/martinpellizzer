Odontites luteus var. hispidulus

Odontites luteus var. hispidulus, commonly known as the hairy yellow bell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft pubescence. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Its botanical significance lies in its ornamental value and role in supporting local pollinator populations.
