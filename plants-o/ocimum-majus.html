Ocimum majus

Ocimum majus, commonly known as holy basil or tulsi, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and lavender. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine and religious rituals. Ocimum majus produces small, pale purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its essential oils, rich in compounds like eugenol and camphor, contribute to its aromatic and therapeutic properties.
