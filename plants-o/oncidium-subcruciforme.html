Oncidium subcruciforme

Oncidium subcruciforme, commonly known as the Cruciform Orchid, is a tropical orchid species native to Central and South America.

It features striking, butterfly-shaped flowers that are typically yellow with red or orange markings. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually elliptical and covered in a dense, velvety pubescence.

Oncidium subcruciforme thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
