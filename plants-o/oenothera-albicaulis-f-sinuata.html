Oenothera albicaulis f. sinuata

Oenothera albicaulis f. sinuata, commonly known as the sinuous evening primrose, is a variant of the evening primrose native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, sinuously curled leaves and pale yellow flowers that open in the evening. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It belongs to the Onagraceae family and is known for its showy, fragrant blooms.

The f. sinuata form is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology, which sets it apart from the typical form of Oenothera albicaulis.
