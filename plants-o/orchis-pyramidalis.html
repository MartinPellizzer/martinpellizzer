Orchis pyramidalis

Orchis pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramidal orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodlands and grasslands. The plant features a tall, erect stem that can reach up to 1 meter in height, bearing a dense spike of violet to purple flowers. Its flowers are characterized by their distinctive, hooded structure and are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.

Orchis pyramidalis is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitats.
