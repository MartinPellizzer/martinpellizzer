Oxytropis cusickii

Oxytropis cusickii, commonly known as Cusick's locoweed, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and mountainous regions. It has hairy stems and leaves that are pinnately compound, with small, rounded leaflets.

Oxytropis cusickii is known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock.
