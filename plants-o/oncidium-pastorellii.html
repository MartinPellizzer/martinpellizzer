Oncidium pastorellii

Oncidium pastorellii, commonly known as the Pastorelli's orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, yellow-green flowers with red markings. The plant has pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, typical of many orchid species. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Oncidium pastorellii is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.
