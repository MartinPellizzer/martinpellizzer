Oerstedella pinnifera

Oerstedella pinnifera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved water clover, is a aquatic plant characterized by its delicate, feather-like leaves that are arranged in a pinnate pattern.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and streams, where it can thrive in moderate to low light conditions. The plant's leaves are often submerged, with some emergent parts near the water's surface, allowing for efficient photosynthesis. Oerstedella pinnifera is known for its ability to stabilize sediments and improve water quality through its dense root system.

Its botanical structure makes it a valuable species in ecological restoration projects.
