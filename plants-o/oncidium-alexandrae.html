Oncidium alexandrae

Oncidium alexandrae, commonly known as the Alexandra orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, pendant-like inflorescences that bear numerous fragrant, brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shady environments. Its flowers come in shades of yellow, orange, and red, often displaying intricate patterns.

Oncidium alexandrae requires bright, indirect light and consistent moisture to thrive.
