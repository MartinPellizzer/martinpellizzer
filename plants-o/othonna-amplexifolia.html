Othonna amplexifolia

Othonna amplexifolia, commonly known as the clasping othonna, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are often clasping around the stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves and stems.

Othonna amplexifolia is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
