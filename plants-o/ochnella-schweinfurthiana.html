Ochnella schweinfurthiana

Ochnella schweinfurthiana, commonly known as the Schweinfurth's ochna, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds, aiding in wind dispersal.

Ochnella schweinfurthiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
