Oxalis lasiopetala var. angustiloba

Oxalis lasiopetala var. angustiloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features trifoliate leaves composed of three heart-shaped leaflets, with the central leaflet often being narrower than the others. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental species within the Oxalidaceae family.
