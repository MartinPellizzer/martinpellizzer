Oenothera grandiflora subsp. erythrosepala

Oenothera grandiflora subsp.

erythrosepala, commonly known as the red-sepaled evening primrose, is a species native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its showy, orange-yellow flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
