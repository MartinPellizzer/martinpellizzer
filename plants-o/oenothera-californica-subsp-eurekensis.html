Oenothera californica subsp. eurekensis

Oenothera californica subsp.

eurekensis, commonly known as the Eureka evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to California. It is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
