Ocotea latifolia

Ocotea latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage and distinctive bark. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy surface, while its flowers are small and clustered.

The tree is valued for its timber, which is used in furniture and construction, as well as for its essential oils used in perfumery.
