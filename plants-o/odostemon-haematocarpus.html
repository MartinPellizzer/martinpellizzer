Odostemon haematocarpus

Odostemon haematocarpus, commonly known as the bloodroot or redroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive red or orange-colored roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a smooth texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
