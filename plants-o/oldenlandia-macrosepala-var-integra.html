Oldenlandia macrosepala var. integra

Oldenlandia macrosepala var. integra, commonly known as the velvetleaf or red spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range from red to pink, often with a velvety texture. The plant typically grows as an annual or short-lived perennial, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a hairy surface.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
