Ocotea araraquarensis

Ocotea araraquarensis, commonly known as the Araraquara ocotea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a glossy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces dark blue to black berries that are an important food source for local wildlife.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
