Oxalis haughtii

Oxalis haughtii, commonly known as the purple oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Oxalidaceae and is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant produces bright yellow blooms with red markings, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Oxalis haughtii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate dry conditions.
