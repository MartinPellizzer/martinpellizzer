Onobrychis tragacanthoides

Onobrychis tragacanthoides, commonly known as the Turkish clover, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces a distinctive yellow to brown pod containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it valuable for soil enrichment and ecological restoration.
