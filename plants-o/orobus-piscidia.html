Orobus piscidia

Orobus piscidia, commonly known as the fish poison plant, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant is notable for containing toxic compounds, particularly rotenone, which can be used as a fish poison. Its botanical features include hairy stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters.

Orobus piscidia is often found in moist, tropical environments and has been used traditionally in various cultural practices.
