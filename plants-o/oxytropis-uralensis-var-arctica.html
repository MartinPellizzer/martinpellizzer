Oxytropis uralensis var. arctica

Oxytropis uralensis var. arctica, commonly known as Arctic locoweed, is a perennial herb native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of Eurasia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous leaflets, and it thrives in nutrient-poor, well-drained soils. It is adapted to cold climates and can survive in harsh, alpine environments.

Oxytropis uralensis var. arctica is often found in tundra and rocky habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
