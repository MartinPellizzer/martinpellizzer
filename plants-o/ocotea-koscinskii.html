Ocotea koscinskii

Ocotea koscinskii, commonly known as the Koscinsky's ocotea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it typically grows in humid, lowland forests. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are usually elliptical and have a glossy surface. Its small, fragrant flowers are clustered in umbels and are often followed by dark, spherical berries.

Ocotea koscinskii is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional crafts and as a source of essential oils.
