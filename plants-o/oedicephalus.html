Oedicephalus

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are capsules that split open to release small, hairy seeds.

Oedicephalus is native to regions in Africa, Asia, and the Americas, and it is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural areas.
