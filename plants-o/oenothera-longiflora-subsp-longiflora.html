Oenothera longiflora subsp. longiflora

Oenothera longiflora subsp.

longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening and close by morning.

The plant has slender, branched stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Oenothera longiflora subsp.

longiflora is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.
