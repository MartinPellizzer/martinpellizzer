Orobus philippii

Orobus philippii, commonly known as the Philippi's orobus, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly in regions of Argentina, Bolivia, and Chile.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas, adapting to arid conditions with its deep root system. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
