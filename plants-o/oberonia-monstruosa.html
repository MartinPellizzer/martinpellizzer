Oberonia monstruosa

Oberonia monstruosa, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 inches in length, often displaying vibrant colors such as yellow, orange, and red. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with thick, fleshy rhizomes that help it store water in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually broad and glossy, adapted to thrive in the humid, shaded conditions of the forest understory.

Due to its unique appearance and rarity, Oberonia monstruosa is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
