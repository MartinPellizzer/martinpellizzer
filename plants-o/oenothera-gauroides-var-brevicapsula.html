Oenothera gauroides var. brevicapsula

Oenothera gauroides var. brevicapsula, commonly known as the short-capsule evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant produces small, rounded capsules that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinator populations.
