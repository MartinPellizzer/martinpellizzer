Ophrys parosica

Ophrys parosica, commonly known as the Paros orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, with a lip that resembles a female face, a feature that plays a role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pale green or brownish in color, and they bloom from late spring to early summer.

Ophrys parosica is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ecological and ornamental significance.
