Oxalis minutula

Oxalis minutula, commonly known as the small wood sorrel, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with three heart-shaped leaflets, often adorned with a distinctive white or pale green marking. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Oxalis minutula is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
