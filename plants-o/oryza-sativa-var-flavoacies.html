Oryza sativa var. flavoacies

Oryza sativa var. flavoacies, commonly known as the golden rice variety, is a type of rice that has been genetically modified to produce beta-carotene, which gives it a yellowish color.

This variety is notable for its nutritional content, as it aims to address vitamin A deficiency in regions where rice is a staple food. The plant has typical grass-like features, with long, narrow leaves and panicles that bear small, white flowers. Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and a culm that supports the grain-bearing panicles.

The grains themselves are smaller and more elongated compared to standard rice varieties, contributing to its unique appearance and nutritional profile.
