Oxalis hedysaroides var. pubescens

Oxalis hedysaroides var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow, spurred flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its ornamental value comes from its delicate foliage and vibrant floral display.
