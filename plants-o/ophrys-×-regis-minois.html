Ophrys × regis-minois

Ophrys × regis-minois, commonly known as the royal orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the genus Ophrys, which is characterized by its mimicry of female insects to attract male pollinators. The plant produces intricate, bee-mimicking flowers that are highly specialized for pollination by bees. Its botanical features include a tall, erect stem with several showy, spurred flowers.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
