Oncidium tetracopis

Oncidium tetracopis, commonly known as the Yellow Oncidium, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters and are often fragrant. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are long, strap-like, and often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Oncidium tetracopis requires bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.
