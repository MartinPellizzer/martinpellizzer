Ocimum hassleri

Ocimum hassleri, commonly known as the Ethiopian basil, is a herbaceous plant native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and oregano. The plant is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of purple or pink flowers. It is valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Ocimum hassleri thrives in warm, dry climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
