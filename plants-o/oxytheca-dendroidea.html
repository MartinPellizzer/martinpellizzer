Oxytheca dendroidea

Oxytheca dendroidea, commonly known as the tree cactus, is a unique succulent native to South America.

It is characterized by its tree-like structure, with a thick, woody trunk and branching stems that bear clusters of spiny, paddle-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its rugged foliage. Oxytheca dendroidea is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its fleshy stems and leaves.

Its slow growth rate and distinctive appearance make it a popular choice for desert gardens and cactus collections.
