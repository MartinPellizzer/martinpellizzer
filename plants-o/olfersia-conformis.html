Olfersia conformis

Olfersia conformis, commonly known as the chocolate orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, fragrant flowers that resemble small chocolate candies. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and dark green, often with a waxy texture, while its flowers are usually yellow or orange with a distinctive chocolate scent.

Olfersia conformis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
