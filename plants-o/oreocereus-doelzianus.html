Oreocereus doelzianus

Oreocereus doelzianus, commonly known as the Mexican moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its unique appearance, featuring a rounded, fleshy stem covered in white spines. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments and is adapted to withstand extreme drought conditions. Its flowers are usually bright yellow or orange, blooming in the evening.

Due to its striking appearance and rarity, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in greenhouses and gardens.
