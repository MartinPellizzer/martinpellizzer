Orobanche clarkei

Orobanche clarkei, commonly known as the Clarke's broomrape, is a rootless parasitic plant that belongs to the Orobanchaceae family.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on its host plants for nutrients, typically growing on the roots of various flowering plants. The plant produces small, often yellow or reddish, flowers that are arranged in dense clusters. It is native to regions of Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.

Due to its parasitic nature and lack of visible leaves, it plays a unique role in its ecosystem as a specialized parasite.
