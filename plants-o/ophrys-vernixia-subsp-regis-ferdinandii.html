Ophrys vernixia subsp. regis-ferdinandii

Ophrys vernixia subsp.

regis-ferdinandii, commonly known as the royal Ferdinand's helleborine, is a rare orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe. It is characterized by its striking, bee-mimicking flowers that feature intricate patterns resembling a bee's body.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy texture.

The species is notable for its specialized pollination mechanism, relying on male bees for reproduction.
