Oxalis alstonii subsp. alstonii

Oxalis alstonii subsp.

alstonii, commonly known as the alstonii wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is part of the Oxalidaceae family, known for its calcium oxalate crystals in the leaves. Oxalis alstonii subsp.

alstonii is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
