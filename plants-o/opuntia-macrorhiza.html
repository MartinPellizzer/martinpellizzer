Opuntia macrorhiza

Opuntia macrorhiza, commonly known as the large-flowered prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems that are covered in sharp spines and glochids. The plant produces large, showy yellow or orange flowers during the summer months. Its fruit, known as a tunas, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

Opuntia macrorhiza is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in supporting local wildlife.
