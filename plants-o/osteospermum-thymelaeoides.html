Osteospermum thymelaeoides

Osteospermum thymelaeoides, commonly known as the blue oatbrush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads that resemble small oat clusters. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, daisy-like blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant colors and drought tolerance.

Osteospermum thymelaeoides thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.
