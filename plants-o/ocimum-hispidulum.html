Ocimum hispidulum

Ocimum hispidulum, commonly known as hairy basil, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers that are clustered in terminal spikes. Its essential oils contain compounds like linalool and estragole, which contribute to its aromatic properties.

Ocimum hispidulum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial benefits.
