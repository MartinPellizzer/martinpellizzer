Oplonia linifolia

Oplonia linifolia, commonly known as the silky oplonia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. Its leaves are elongated, narrow, and covered in a fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun.
