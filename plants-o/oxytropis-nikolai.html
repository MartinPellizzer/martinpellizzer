Oxytropis nikolai

Oxytropis nikolai, commonly known as Nikolai's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, often branched stems and small, compound leaves. The plant produces yellow or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Oxytropis nikolai is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
