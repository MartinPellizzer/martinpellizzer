Origanum bovei

Origanum bovei, commonly known as Bove's oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are aromatic and have a strong, pungent scent, often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Origanum bovei thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a hardy plant suitable for Mediterranean climates.
