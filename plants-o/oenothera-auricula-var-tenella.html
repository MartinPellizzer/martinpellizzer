Oenothera auricula var. tenella

Oenothera auricula var. tenella, commonly known as the narrow-leaf evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and showy yellow flowers that open in the evening. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural interest.
