Oxalis metziana

Oxalis metziana, commonly known as the silverleaf oxalis, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, which are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink, white, or yellow, typically in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Oxalis metziana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
