Ophrys philippi

Ophrys philippi, commonly known as the Philippi's bee orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its intricate floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained conditions. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brown, with a distinctive lip that resembles the body of a bee.

Ophrys philippi is a protected species due to its limited distribution and sensitivity to habitat disturbance.
