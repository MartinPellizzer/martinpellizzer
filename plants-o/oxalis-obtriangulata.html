Oxalis obtriangulata

Oxalis obtriangulata, commonly known as the triangled wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are heart-shaped and have a distinctive triangled appearance. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pink, with five petals. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Oxalis obtriangulata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and modest blooms.
