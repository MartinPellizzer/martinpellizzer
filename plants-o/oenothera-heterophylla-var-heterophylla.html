Oenothera heterophylla var. heterophylla

Oenothera heterophylla var. heterophylla, commonly known as the variable evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its diverse leaf shapes, which vary depending on environmental conditions, a trait that contributes to its common name. The plant produces showy yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its stems are often hairy, and the leaves can be either lance-shaped or broad, showcasing its morphological adaptability.

This species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its potential medicinal properties.
