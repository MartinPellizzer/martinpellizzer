Ophrys × laconensis

Ophrys × laconensis, commonly known as the Laconic Ophrys, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its intricate, bee-mimicking flower structure, which plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, thriving in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its flowers exhibit a range of colors, often with intricate patterns that mimic female bees to attract male pollinators.

This botanical feature highlights its evolutionary adaptation for effective cross-pollination.
