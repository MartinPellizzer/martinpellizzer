Oxytropis fetida

Oxytropis fetida, commonly known as skunk vetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its strongly aromatic, often foul-smelling foliage and yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its compound leaves are pinnately arranged, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Oxytropis fetida is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to fix nitrogen and stabilize soil.
