Opuntia streptacantha subsp. aguirriana

Opuntia streptacantha subsp.

aguirriana, commonly known as the Aguirriana prickly pear, is a cactus species native to Mexico. It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in spines and glochids.

The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer, followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.
