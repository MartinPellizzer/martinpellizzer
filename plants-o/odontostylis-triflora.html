Odontostylis triflora

Odontostylis triflora, commonly known as the three-flowered orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, with three prominent sepals and petals.
