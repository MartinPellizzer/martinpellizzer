Orchis fragrans f. cassidea

Orchis fragrans f. cassidea, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a botanical variant of the common orchid, characterized by its distinctive floral morphology.

This orchid species features a tall, erect stem with showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The leaves are lance-shaped and arranged along the stem, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats.

The plant is known for its ability to produce a large number of small, spore-bearing structures, facilitating its reproduction and spread.
