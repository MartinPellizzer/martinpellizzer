Ocotea subalpina

Ocotea subalpina, commonly known as the Andean ocotea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the cloud forests of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery, dark green, and often have a distinct aromatic scent when crushed.

The flowers are small and clustered, followed by elliptical, dark blue-black berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species.
