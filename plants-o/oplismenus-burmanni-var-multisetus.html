Oplismenus burmanni var. multisetus

Oplismenus burmanni var. multisetus, commonly known as the hairy sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its triangular stems and dense, grass-like foliage. Its leaves are typically hairy, giving it its common name, and it often grows in wet or marshy environments.
