Oldenlandia lactea

Oldenlandia lactea, commonly known as the milky spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive yellow or greenish hue at the base. The plant produces a milky sap, which is a key feature in its common name and can be used for various traditional purposes. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks.

Its leaves are opposite and often have a waxy texture, contributing to its adaptability in humid climates.
