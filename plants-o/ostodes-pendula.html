Ostodes pendula

Ostodes pendula, commonly known as the hanging fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, pendulous fronds that drape gracefully from its stems. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with creeping underground stems that produce new shoots. Its leaves are typically bipinnate, with a feathery appearance that enhances its ornamental value.

Ostodes pendula prefers humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
