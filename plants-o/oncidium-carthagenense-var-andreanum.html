Oncidium carthagenense var. andreanum

Oncidium carthagenense var. andreanum, commonly known as the showy orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to South America.

It is characterized by its long, arching inflorescences that bear clusters of bright yellow to orange flowers with distinctive red veins. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. It requires bright, indirect light and well-draining potting media to thrive.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
