Opuntia curvispina

Opuntia curvispina, commonly known as the curved spine prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in sharp spines and clusters of vibrant yellow flowers. The plant produces edible fruits, known as tunas, which are typically orange or red in color. Opuntia curvispina is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

It plays an important ecological role by providing food and shelter for various desert animals.
