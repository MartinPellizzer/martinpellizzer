Onobrychis schugnanica f. inermis

Onobrychis schugnanica f. inermis, commonly known as the Armenian clover, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions. It is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in soil enrichment.

Its ornamental value also makes it a sought-after plant in horticulture.
