Orchis incarnata var. latissima

Orchis incarnata var. latissima, commonly known as the broad-leaved pink orchid, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features showy, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and wet meadows. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a waxy texture that helps retain moisture.

The species is part of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure and ecological adaptability.
