Ophrys ferrum-equinum f. fallax

Ophrys ferrum-equinum f. fallax, commonly known as the iron horse orchid, is a rare and fascinating member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bee-like flower structure, which features a specialized labellum that mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. This orchid typically grows in calcareous soils in woodland or grassland habitats, often in regions with a temperate climate. Its flowers are usually pale green or brownish in color, with intricate patterns that aid in its deceptive pollination strategy.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a protected species in many areas.
