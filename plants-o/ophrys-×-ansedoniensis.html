Ophrys × ansedoniensis

Ophrys × ansedoniensis, commonly known as the Ansedonia orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the genus Ophrys, which is known for its mimicry of female insects to attract male pollinators. This plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is found in Mediterranean climates. Its flowers are characterized by their intricate, bee-like appearance, which plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy.

The species is relatively rare and is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological and evolutionary traits.
