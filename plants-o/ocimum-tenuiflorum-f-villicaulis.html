Ocimum tenuiflorum f. villicaulis

Ocimum tenuiflorum f. villicaulis, commonly known as holy basil or tulsi, is a variant of the sacred basil plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

This subspecies is characterized by its small, white or pale pink flowers and slender, hairy stems. It is widely cultivated for its aromatic leaves, which are used in traditional medicine and religious rituals. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both horticulture and phytotherapy.
