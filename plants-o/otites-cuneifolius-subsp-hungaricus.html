Otites cuneifolius subsp. hungaricus

Otites cuneifolius subsp.

hungaricus, commonly known as the Hungarian narrow-leaved grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often slightly folded. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in maintaining biodiversity and soil stability in its native range.
