Orchis × beyrichii

Orchis × beyrichii, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a natural cross between Orchis moria and Orchis mascula.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats across Europe. Its inflorescence consists of a tall stem bearing several showy, spurred flowers.

Orchis × beyrichii is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems as a nectar source for pollinators.
