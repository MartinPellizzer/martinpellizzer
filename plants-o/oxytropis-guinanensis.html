Oxytropis guinanensis

Oxytropis guinanensis, commonly known as Guianan milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly found in regions of Venezuela, Colombia, and Guyana. The plant is characterized by its erect stems and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its leaves are typically pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Oxytropis guinanensis is adapted to grow in dry, open habitats and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through its symbiotic relationship with rhizobia bacteria.
