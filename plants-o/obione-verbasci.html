Obione verbasci

Obione verbasci, commonly known as the common mullein.

It is a biennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, velvety leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in the second year, which are followed by fluffy, seed-filled capsules. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in dry meadows.

Obione verbasci has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties, particularly for respiratory ailments.
