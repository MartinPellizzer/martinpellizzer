Oxalis caprina var. abyssinica

Oxalis caprina var. abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian wood sorrel, is a perennial herb native to Ethiopia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive reddish veins and produces small, yellow flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family, known for its calcium oxalate crystals.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and rockeries.
