Ophrys bingoelensis

Ophrys bingoelensis, commonly known as the Bingoelens Orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to specific regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a specialized lip that mimics the appearance of a female bee, a trait known as sexual deception. This mimicry plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy, attracting male bees that attempt to mate with the flower. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as woodlands and heathlands.

Its conservation status is of concern due to habitat loss and limited distribution.
