Ocotea schomburgkiana

Ocotea schomburgkiana, commonly known as the Schomburgk's ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and fragrant wood. The plant typically grows in humid lowland forests and can reach heights of up to 30 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a glossy surface, while its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and has been used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
