Oenothera jamesii

Oenothera jamesii, commonly known as the desert evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening. The plant has hairy, ribbed stems and lance-shaped leaves that are adapted to arid environments. It produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Oenothera jamesii is a perennial species that thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in desert ecosystems.
