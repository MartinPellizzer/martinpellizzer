Ouratea deminuta

Ouratea deminuta, commonly known as the dwarf ouratea, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
