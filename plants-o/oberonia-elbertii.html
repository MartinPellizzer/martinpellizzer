Oberonia elbertii

Oberonia elbertii, commonly known as the Elbert's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, utilizing its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. Its leaves are usually narrow and strap-like, adapted to conserve water in its humid, shaded habitat.

Oberonia elbertii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
