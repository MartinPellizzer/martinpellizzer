Odontoglossum chrysomorphum

Odontoglossum chrysomorphum, commonly known as the golden-leaf orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its erect, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, showy flowers with bright yellow petals and a distinctive greenish-blue lip. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Odontoglossum chrysomorphum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
