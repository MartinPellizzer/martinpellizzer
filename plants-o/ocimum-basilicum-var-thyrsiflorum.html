Ocimum basilicum var. thyrsiflorum

Ocimum basilicum var. thyrsiflorum, commonly known as Thai basil, is a variant of the common basil plant characterized by its distinctive purple stems and leaves.

It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in warm climates, often used in culinary applications for its aromatic, slightly peppery flavor. The plant produces small, white to purple flowers in terminal clusters, which contribute to its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for both gardeners and chefs.

Its botanical features make it a unique and versatile plant in both horticultural and gastronomic contexts.
