Ocotea minarum

Ocotea minarum, commonly known as the mountain laurel or Guariroba, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by dark, fleshy berries. Ocotea minarum is often found in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests.

Its wood is valued for its durability and aromatic properties, making it important in both ecological and economic contexts.
