Oxalis spiralis var. glandulosa

Oxalis spiralis var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, spiral-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or rocky areas. Its leaves are often covered with tiny glands, which give it its varietal name.

It is a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in partial shade.
