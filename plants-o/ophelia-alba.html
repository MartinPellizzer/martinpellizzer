Ophelia alba

Ophelia alba, commonly known as white water crowfoot, is a aquatic plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds, lakes, and streams. The plant has slender, branching stems that emerge from the water's surface, with small, white flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are typically submerged, arranged in a whorled pattern, and are often mistaken for roots due to their feathery appearance.

Ophelia alba plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
