Oxydectes virens

Oxydectes virens, commonly known as the green spider orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

This herbaceous plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as rainforests or along stream banks. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, while its flowers are small and greenish-white, often with a subtle fragrance. The plant produces a single spike of flowers, which can bloom in late spring or early summer.

Oxydectes virens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
