Ophrys × hydruntensis

Ophrys × hydruntensis, commonly known as the Hydruntian bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Balkans.

It is characterized by its intricate floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is found in open woodland and grassland habitats. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brownish in color, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique pollination strategy and hybrid origins.
