Oxalis trilliifolia

Oxalis trilliifolia, commonly known as the three-leaf sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three heart-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Oxalis trilliifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
