Oxydectes olivacea

Oxydectes olivacea, commonly known as the olive tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a distinctive crown of pinnate leaves that give it a lush, ornamental appearance. Its roots form a dense network that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Oxydectes olivacea is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a landscape plant.
