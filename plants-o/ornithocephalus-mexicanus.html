Ornithocephalus mexicanus

Ornithocephalus mexicanus, commonly known as the Mexican bird's head, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its distinctive, ornate flower that resembles a bird's head. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded, moist environments. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The flowers are usually pink or red, and they bloom in clusters, making it an attractive ornamental plant.
