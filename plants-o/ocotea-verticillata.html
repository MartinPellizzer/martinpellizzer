Ocotea verticillata

Ocotea verticillata, commonly known as the fragrant evergreen or the mountain pepper tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments within tropical and subtropical forests. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils, which have been used in traditional medicine and perfumery.

Ocotea verticillata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
