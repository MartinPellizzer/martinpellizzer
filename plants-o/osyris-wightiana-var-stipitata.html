Osyris wightiana var. stipitata

Osyris wightiana var. stipitata, commonly known as the hairy osyris, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive hairy stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its unique appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate to flourish.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
