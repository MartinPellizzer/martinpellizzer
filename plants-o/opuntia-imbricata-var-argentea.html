Opuntia imbricata var. argentea

Opuntia imbricata var. argentea, commonly known as the silver prickly pear, is a cacti species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, fleshy pads covered in silvery-white glochids, which are fine, barbed hairs that can cause irritation. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, followed by edible, red-fleshed fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Its succulent structure allows it to store water efficiently, making it well adapted to desert conditions.
