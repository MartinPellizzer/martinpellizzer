Oxybaphus dichotomus

Oxybaphus dichotomus, commonly known as the forked baphus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features a slender, erect stem with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom during the summer months.

Oxybaphus dichotomus is known for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
