Ophrys × augustae

Ophrys × augustae, commonly known as the bee orchid hybrid, is a fascinating plant in the Ophrys genus, known for its intricate mimicry of female bees.

It is a natural hybrid between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera, resulting in a unique combination of floral traits. The plant produces showy, bee-like flowers that play a crucial role in its pollination strategy, attracting male bees through visual and chemical cues. It thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats across Europe.

Its botanical significance lies in its evolutionary adaptations and role in ecological interactions.
