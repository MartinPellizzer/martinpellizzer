Oxalis obliquifolia var. transvaalensis

Oxalis obliquifolia var. transvaalensis, commonly known as the Transvaal oxalis, is a species of flowering plant in the wood sorrel family.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial shade to full sun. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and blooms.

The leaves are heart-shaped with a distinctive veining pattern, and the flowers are usually pink or red with five petals.
