Orchis maculata var. candidissima

Orchis maculata var. candidissima, commonly known as the white-stemmed orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stem and showy, pale pink to white flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing intricate, lip-shaped petals that are often adorned with subtle markings. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows, often forming dense colonies.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its delicate floral display.
