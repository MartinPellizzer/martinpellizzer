Odontophyllum castanifolium

Odontophyllum castanifolium, commonly known as the castanea-leaved orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white and pink, which bloom during the wet season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
