Ophrys × kingsleyana

Ophrys × kingsleyana, commonly known as the Kingsley's bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the British Isles.

It is characterized by its distinctive floral structure, which closely resembles a female bee, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant produces showy, bee-like flowers with intricate patterns and a scent that mimics the pheromones of bees. It thrives in damp, shaded environments such as woodland floors and grassy meadows.

The botanical significance of Ophrys × kingsleyana lies in its role as a hybrid species and its unique evolutionary adaptations for pollination.
