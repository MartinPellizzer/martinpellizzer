Onobrychis viciifolia monstr. braunii

Onobrychis viciifolia monstr.

braunii, commonly known as the Braun's vetch, is a perennial leguminous plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia. It features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive trifoliate shape, and its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in racemes.

The plant is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in steppe and woodland habitats. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.
