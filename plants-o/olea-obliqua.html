Olea obliqua

Olea obliqua, commonly known as the Canary Island olive, is a species of flowering plant in the family Oleaceae.

It is native to the Canary Islands and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically dark purple when mature. Olea obliqua is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species with drought tolerance.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and has historical significance in the Canary Islands.
