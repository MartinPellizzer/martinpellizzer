Ocotea tristis

Ocotea tristis, commonly known as the black ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its dark, aromatic bark and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by dark blue berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
