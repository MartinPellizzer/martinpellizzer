Ophrys fusca var. sicula

Ophrys fusca var. sicula, commonly known as the Sicilian bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to southern Italy.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as rocky areas and grasslands. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with intricate patterns that enhance the mimicry effect.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
