Oncidium × excellens

Oncidium × excellens, commonly known as the Yellow Butterfly orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated flower spikes that bear numerous fragrant, yellowish-orange flowers resembling butterflies. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and it thrives in bright, indirect light with high humidity.

Oncidium × excellens is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its vibrant appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
