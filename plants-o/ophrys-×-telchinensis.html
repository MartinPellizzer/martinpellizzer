Ophrys × telchinensis

Ophrys × telchinensis, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant that results from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is characterized by its intricate, bee-like flower structure, which plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or grassland habitats. Its flowers produce a strong, sweet scent that mimics the pheromones of female bees, attracting male bees for pollination.

This botanical feature highlights the plant's evolutionary adaptation to ensure reproductive success through mimicry.
