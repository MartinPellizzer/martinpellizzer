Ochnella gracilipes

Ochnella gracilipes, commonly known as the slender-stemmed ochna, is a small shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences, followed by distinctive, berry-like fruits.

Ochnella gracilipes is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological roles in its native environment.
