Ocotea infrafoveolata

Ocotea infrafoveolata, commonly known as the narrow-ridged ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves with a glossy upper surface. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically greenish-white, and it produces dark blue to black berries.

Ocotea infrafoveolata is often found in humid lowland forests and is valued for its aromatic wood and ecological significance.
