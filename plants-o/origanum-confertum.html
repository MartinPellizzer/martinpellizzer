Origanum confertum

Origanum confertum, commonly known as woolly oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, fuzzy gray-green foliage and small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant is part of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is closely related to other members of the Origanum genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant plant.

Its aromatic leaves are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
