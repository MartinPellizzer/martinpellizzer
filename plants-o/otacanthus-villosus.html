Otacanthus villosus

Otacanthus villosus, commonly known as the hairy trumpetflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Otacanthus villosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
