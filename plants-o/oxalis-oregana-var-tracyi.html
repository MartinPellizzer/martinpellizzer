Oxalis oregana var. tracyi

Oxalis oregana var. tracyi, commonly known as Tracy's wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive reddish veins and produces small, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens. Its botanical characteristics include trifoliate leaves and a basal rosette growth habit.

It is often found in moist, shaded areas such as stream banks and forest floors.
