Oenothera capensis

Oenothera capensis, commonly known as the Cape Honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in shrubland and coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Oenothera capensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
