Ophrys sphegodes lusus flavescens

Ophrys sphegodes lusus flavescens, commonly known as the yellow bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to specific regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking, bee-like flower structure, which features a yellowish hue and intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of a female bee. The plant typically grows in calcareous grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained, nutrient-poor soils. Its flowers are adapted for specific pollination by male bees, which attempt to mate with the floral mimicry, facilitating cross-pollination.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, this species is considered vulnerable, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts to protect its delicate ecological niche.
