Ononis natrix subsp. prostrata

Ononis natrix subsp.

prostrata, commonly known as the prostrate bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features slender, prostrate stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats.

The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers clustered in umbels, typically blooming from June to August. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and covered with fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges.
