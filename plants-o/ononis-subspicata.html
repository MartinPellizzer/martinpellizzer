Ononis subspicata

Ononis subspicata, commonly known as sandspurry, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with small, fleshy, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its root system is extensive and adapted to thrive in sandy or gravelly soils.

Ononis subspicata is often found in disturbed areas and is known for its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
