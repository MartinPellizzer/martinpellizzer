Oreopanax destructor

Oreopanax destructor, commonly known as the giant arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, palm-like leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, starchy tubers. Its botanical structure includes a stout, woody stem and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

It is valued for its nutritional content and has been used traditionally in various regions for food and medicinal purposes.
