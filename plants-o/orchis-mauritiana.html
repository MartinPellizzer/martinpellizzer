Orchis mauritiana

Orchis mauritiana, commonly known as the Mauritius orchid, is a rare and endangered species native to the island of Mauritius.

It is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas and has a complex root system that helps it survive in its native habitat. Its pollination is primarily carried out by specific insect species, which play a crucial role in its reproductive cycle.

Due to habitat destruction and limited distribution, conservation efforts are essential for its survival.
