Oxytropis minjanensis

Oxytropis minjanensis, commonly known as the Minjan locoweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in Kazakhstan and China. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale yellow to reddish-brown. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and open grasslands.

Oxytropis minjanensis is known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock.
