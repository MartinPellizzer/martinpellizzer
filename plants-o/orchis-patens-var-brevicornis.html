Orchis patens var. brevicornis

Orchis patens var. brevicornis, commonly known as the short-horned orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, while the flowers are arranged in a loose spike.

The species is notable for its distinctive, short, club-shaped spur at the base of each flower.
