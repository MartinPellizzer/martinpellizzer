Oncidium riograndense

Oncidium riograndense, commonly known as the Rio Grande orchid, is a tropical orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are strap-like and often covered in a fine, whitish pubescence.

Oncidium riograndense prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
