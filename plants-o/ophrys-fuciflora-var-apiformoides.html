Ophrys fuciflora var. apiformoides

Ophrys fuciflora var. apiformoides, commonly known as the bee orchid, is a species of orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure that closely resembles a female bee, which plays a key role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of bees.

The orchid is known for its specialized relationship with specific pollinators, enhancing its reproductive success in its native environment.
