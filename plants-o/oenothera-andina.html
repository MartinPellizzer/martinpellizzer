Oenothera andina

Oenothera andina, commonly known as the Andean evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its yellow or orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has hairy, linear leaves and produces capsules that contain numerous small seeds. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Oenothera andina is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional remedies.
