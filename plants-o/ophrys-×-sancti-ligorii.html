Ophrys × sancti-ligorii

Ophrys × sancti-ligorii, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant that exhibits unique botanical features.

It is a natural hybrid between Ophrys apifera, the bee orchid, and Ophrys insectifera, the wasp orchid, resulting in a blend of characteristics from both parents. This species is known for its striking, bee-like flowers that have evolved to mimic female bees, a trait that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as woodland floors or along stream banks, where it thrives in partial shade.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, contributing to its ecological significance in certain habitats.
