Oryctina

Oryctina, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to tropical Africa.

These plants are characterized by their rosette-shaped leaves and showy, clustered flowers that come in a variety of colors. Oryctina species are typically grown as houseplants due to their preference for warm, humid environments. They have a fibrous root system and are often propagated through leaf cuttings or division.

The genus includes several species, such as Oryctina crassifolia and Oryctina caerulea, which are popular for their ornamental value.
