Oldenlandia lawsoniae

Oldenlandia lawsoniae, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale blue, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Oldenlandia lawsoniae is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
