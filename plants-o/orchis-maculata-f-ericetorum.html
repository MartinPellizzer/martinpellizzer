Orchis maculata f. ericetorum

Orchis maculata f. ericetorum, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive dark purple or brown spots on the petals.

This orchid is typically found in woodland and heathland habitats, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. It has a tall, unbranched stem that bears a single spike of flowers, which are usually pink or purple in color. The plant is perennial and blooms from late spring to early summer.

Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form colonies in suitable environments.
