Oxalis crassiscaposa

Oxalis crassiscaposa, commonly known as the broad-capped wood sorrel, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its stems are often trailing or ascending, and it produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds.

Oxalis crassiscaposa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
