Osbeckia ternifolia

Osbeckia ternifolia, commonly known as the three-leaf willow or slender willow, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like stems and small, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Osbeckia ternifolia is typically found in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in open woodlands.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
