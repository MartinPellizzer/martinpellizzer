Olea malabarica

Olea malabarica, commonly known as Malabar olive, is a small evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that turn from green to black as they mature. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine.

Olea malabarica thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.
