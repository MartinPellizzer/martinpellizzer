Orbexilum lupinellus

Orbexilum lupinellus, commonly known as the desert lupine, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, featuring hairy stems and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are usually purple or pink, arranged in clusters, and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Orbexilum lupinellus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
