Oncidium globuliferum var. costaricense

Oncidium globuliferum var. costaricense, commonly known as the Costa Rican Oncidium, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central America.

It is characterized by its pendant, showy flowers that typically bloom in clusters, featuring a vibrant yellow or orange color with contrasting purple or brown markings. The plant has pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to thrive in tropical and subtropical environments. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that are dark green and often have a waxy texture.
