Oxalis yacutulensis

Oxalis yacutulensis, commonly known as the Yacutulensis wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. It produces small, edible berries that mature to a red color, and it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Oxalis yacutulensis is also noted for its ability to thrive in partial shade and well-drained soil.
