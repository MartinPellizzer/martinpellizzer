Oxalis campicola

Oxalis campicola, commonly known as the mountain wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features trifoliate leaves with reddish veins and produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a sprawling or upright growth habit.

Oxalis campicola is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
