Ophelia tetrapetala

Ophelia tetrapetala, commonly known as the four-petaled water crowfoot, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds, streams, and lakes. The plant features small, delicate flowers with four petals, usually in shades of white or pale pink. Its leaves are submerged, ribbon-like, and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Ophelia tetrapetala plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
