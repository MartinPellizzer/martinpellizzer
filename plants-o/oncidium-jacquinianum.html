Oncidium jacquinianum

Oncidium jacquinianum, commonly known as the Jacquin's orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences bearing numerous small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil.

Oncidium jacquinianum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
