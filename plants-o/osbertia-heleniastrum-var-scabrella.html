Osbertia heleniastrum var. scabrella

Osbertia heleniastrum var. scabrella, commonly known as the rough-hairy bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, with tubular corollas and five lobes. Its leaves are characterized by a rough, hairy texture, giving it its common name.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
