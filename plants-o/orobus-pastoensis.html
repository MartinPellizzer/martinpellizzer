Orobus pastoensis

Orobus pastoensis, commonly known as the Pasto orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and is adapted to the cool, humid climates of its native habitat. Its roots are often specialized for nutrient absorption in poor soils, and it produces small, green leaves.

Orobus pastoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
