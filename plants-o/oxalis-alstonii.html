Oxalis alstonii

Oxalis alstonii, commonly known as the alstonii wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands or along streambanks. It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Oxalis alstonii is also valued for its ability to thrive in partial shade and its low maintenance requirements.
