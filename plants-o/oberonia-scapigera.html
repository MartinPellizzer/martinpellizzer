Oberonia scapigera

Oberonia scapigera, commonly known as the scapigerous orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, often in undergrowth or on the forest floor. The plant features a short, fleshy rhizome and produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive, spurred lip. Its leaves are usually broad and sword-shaped, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in cultivation.

Oberonia scapigera is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is often appreciated for its unique floral structure and adaptability to humid conditions.
