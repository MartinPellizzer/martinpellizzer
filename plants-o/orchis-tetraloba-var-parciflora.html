Orchis tetraloba var. parciflora

Orchis tetraloba var. parciflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved early purple orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of violet or purple flowers with a distinctive spurred lip. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, particularly bees, and it is known for its early blooming period in spring.

The species is characterized by its compact size and delicate, fragrant blooms.
