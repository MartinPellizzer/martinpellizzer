Ophrys × rebuttonei

Ophrys × rebuttonei, commonly known as the Rebuttone's bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its intricate floral morphology, with a labellum that mimics the appearance of a female bee, aiding in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brown in color, with a distinctive hairy texture.

Ophrys × rebuttonei is noted for its specialized pollination mechanism, relying on male bees to mimic the female bee's appearance for successful reproduction.
