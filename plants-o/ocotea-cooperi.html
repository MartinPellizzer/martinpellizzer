Ocotea cooperi

Ocotea cooperi, commonly known as the white sand ocotea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Caribbean, particularly found in the Bahamas and the Turks and Caicos Islands.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic, aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils.

Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine, and it plays a role in the local ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
