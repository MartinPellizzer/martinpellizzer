Olyra concinna

Olyra concinna, commonly known as the slender sandwort, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing, succulent shrub that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the cooler months. Its leaves are fleshy and arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps it conserve water in dry conditions.

Olyra concinna is adapted to sandy soils and can thrive in environments with limited rainfall.
