Oenothera epilobiifolia subsp. epilobiifolia

Oenothera epilobiifolia subsp.

epilobiifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to North America. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces showy yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths. It thrives in sandy or loamy soils and is often found in open woodlands or prairie habitats.

This subspecies is known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
