Ophrys × daunia

Ophrys × daunia, commonly known as the Daunia bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its intricate, bee-like flower structure that mimics the appearance and scent of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is found in grasslands and open woodlands. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brownish in color, with intricate patterns that aid in mimicry.

Ophrys × daunia is a rare and protected species, highlighting its ecological and botanical significance.
