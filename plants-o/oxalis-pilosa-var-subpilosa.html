Oxalis pilosa var. subpilosa

Oxalis pilosa var. subpilosa, commonly known as the silky wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of South America.

It features trifoliate leaves with a soft, velvety texture and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a ground cover.

Its foliage and flowers are also used in traditional herbal remedies.
