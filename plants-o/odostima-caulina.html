Odostima caulina

Odostima caulina, commonly known as the woolly bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating leaves that are often covered in fine, hair-like structures. The plant produces tiny, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Odostima caulina is known for its ability to absorb nutrients from the water, making it a useful indicator of water quality.
