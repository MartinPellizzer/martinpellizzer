Odontoglossum × horsmannii

Odontoglossum × horsmannii, commonly known as the Horsmann's orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the highlands of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with intricate patterns and vibrant colors. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its botanical features include strap-like leaves and a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
