Oncidium nobile

Oncidium nobile, commonly known as the noble orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, long-lasting flowers that come in a variety of colors, including yellow, orange, and red. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are typically strap-like and arranged in a fan shape, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Oncidium nobile is popular in horticulture for its ease of care and ability to thrive in a range of light conditions.
