Oplonia moana

Oplonia moana, commonly known as the Cook Islands tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the South Pacific.

The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in optimal conditions. Its rhizome system is robust, allowing it to anchor firmly in moist, nutrient-rich soil.

Oplonia moana plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
