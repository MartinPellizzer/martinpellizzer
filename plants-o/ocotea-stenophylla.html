Ocotea stenophylla

Ocotea stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests, often reaching heights of up to 30 meters. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, with a distinctive glossy appearance, and it produces dark blue to black berries that are an important food source for various wildlife.

Ocotea stenophylla is also valued for its essential oils and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
