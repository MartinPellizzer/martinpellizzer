Ophrys × parvaisiana

Ophrys × parvaisiana, commonly known as the Parvaisian bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its intricate, bee-like flower structure, which features a prominent lip that mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and thrives in sunny, open habitats such as meadows and forest edges. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brownish in color, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Ophrys × parvaisiana is a rare and specialized species, often found in specific microhabitats where its pollination strategy is most effective.
