Oxalis dichotoma

Oxalis dichotoma, commonly known as the twinleaf wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds.

Oxalis dichotoma is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
