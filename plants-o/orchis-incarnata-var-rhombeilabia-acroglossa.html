Orchis incarnata var. rhombeilabia-acroglossa

Orchis incarnata var. rhombeilabia-acroglossa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf lady's tresses, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, spiraling inflorescence of pale blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces a distinctive, sweet-smelling fragrance.

This orchid is a perennial herb that thrives in rich, loamy soils and is often found in association with other orchid species.
