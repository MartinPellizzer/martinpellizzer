Olearia obcordata

Olearia obcordata, commonly known as the New Zealand frostflower, is a herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that provides insulation against cold temperatures. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions.

Olearia obcordata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
