Oncidium olivaceum var. flavidum

Oncidium olivaceum var. flavidum, commonly known as the Golden Oncidium, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, long-lasting yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

The flowers bloom in clusters and are often used in floral arrangements due to their striking color and longevity.
