Oleandra phyllarthron

Oleandra phyllarthron, commonly known as the golden leaf oleander, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green with a subtle golden hue on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, which bloom throughout the year in warm climates. Oleandra phyllarthron is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, with a dense, bushy growth habit.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a landscape plant.
