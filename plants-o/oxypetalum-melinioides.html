Oxypetalum melinioides

Oxypetalum melinioides, commonly known as the mountain bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a yellow or orange center. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Oxypetalum melinioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
