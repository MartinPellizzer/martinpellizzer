Oxytropis malloryana

Oxytropis malloryana, commonly known as Mallory's locoweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has a deep root system that allows it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Oxytropis malloryana is also known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock.
