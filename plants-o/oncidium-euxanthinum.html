Oncidium euxanthinum

Oncidium euxanthinum, commonly known as the Yellow Oncidium, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow and green flowers, which bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant has pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in fluctuating environmental conditions. Its leaves are typically strap-like and arranged in a fan shape, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Oncidium euxanthinum prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
