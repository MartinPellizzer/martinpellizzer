Ocotea barbellata

Ocotea barbellata, commonly known as the rough-leafed ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage. The plant has simple, alternate, and often serrated leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It produces small, white flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible for some bird species.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
