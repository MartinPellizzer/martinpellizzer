Orchis incarnata subsp. integrata

Orchis incarnata subsp.

integrata, commonly known as the showy orchis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and wet meadows.

The plant features a tall, unbranched stem that can reach up to 1 meter in height. Its flowers are showy, with a distinctive purple or violet coloration and a spurred petal.

The species is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and is often found in association with other woodland flora.
