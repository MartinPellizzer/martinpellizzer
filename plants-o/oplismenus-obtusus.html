Oplismenus obtusus

Oplismenus obtusus, commonly known as the broadleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy purple to pink flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.

Oplismenus obtusus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
