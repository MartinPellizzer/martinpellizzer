Origanum vulgare subsp. hirtum

Origanum vulgare subsp.

hirtum, commonly known as hairy oregano, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its aromatic, small, pale green to reddish leaves and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for Mediterranean gardens. Its essential oils, rich in compounds like carvacrol and thymol, contribute to its strong scent and medicinal properties.

This subspecies is often used in culinary applications and traditional herbal remedies.
