Oenothera minutiflora

Oenothera minutiflora, commonly known as the plains evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the central and western United States.

It is characterized by its small, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening, hence its common name. The plant has slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Oenothera minutiflora is adapted to dry, open habitats and is often found in prairies and sandy soils.
