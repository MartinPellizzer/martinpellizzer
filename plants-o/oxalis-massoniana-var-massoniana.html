Oxalis massoniana var. massoniana

Oxalis massoniana var. massoniana, commonly known as the Mexican wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central America.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and small, delicate flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and is also used in traditional medicine.

The species is known for its ability to tolerate a range of environmental conditions, making it adaptable to various garden settings.
