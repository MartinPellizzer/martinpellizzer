Ophrys muscifera f. longibracteata

Ophrys muscifera f. longibracteata, commonly known as the long-bract bee orchid, is a rare and distinctive orchid species native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and heathlands. Its flowers are adorned with intricate patterns and a specialized labellum that enhances its mimicry.

This orchid is of particular interest to botanists due to its specialized pollination mechanism and its role in biodiversity conservation.
