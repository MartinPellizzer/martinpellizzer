Olea cerasiformis

Olea cerasiformis, commonly known as the Mediterranean buckthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, yellowish-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, black berries that are an important food source for various birds and animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Olea cerasiformis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
