Ophrys insectifera var. apiculata

Ophrys insectifera var. apiculata, commonly known as the bee orchid, is a striking orchid species native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its highly specialized flowers that closely resemble female bees, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brown in color, with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of insects.

This orchid is a hemiparasitic plant, meaning it can derive some of its nutrients from the roots of neighboring plants.
