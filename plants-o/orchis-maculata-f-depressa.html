Orchis maculata f. depressa

Orchis maculata f. depressa, commonly known as the spotted orchid dwarf form, is a herbaceous perennial belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers with a distinctive dark purple or brownish spot on the lip. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for both ecological and horticultural interest.
