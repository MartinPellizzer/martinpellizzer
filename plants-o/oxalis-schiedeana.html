Oxalis schiedeana

Oxalis schiedeana, commonly known as the yellow wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which resemble those of true sorrel, and its clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Oxalis schiedeana is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and vibrant blooms.
