Oxalis martii

Oxalis martii, commonly known as the mountain wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers. Its foliage is often variegated with white or cream markings, adding visual interest.

Oxalis martii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
