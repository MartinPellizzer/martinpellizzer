Osmanthus heterophyllus f. subangustatus

Osmanthus heterophyllus f. subangustatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf osmanthus, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, giving the plant its specific epithet. This variety is often cultivated for its fragrant blossoms used in tea and perfumery.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight.
