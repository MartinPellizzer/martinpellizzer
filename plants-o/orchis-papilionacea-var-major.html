Orchis papilionacea var. major

Orchis papilionacea var. major, commonly known as the greater butterfly orchid, is a species of orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that resemble butterflies, hence its common name. The plant produces a single, spurred orchid flower with a distinctive violet or purple coloration. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.

The orchid is pollinated primarily by bees and is considered a rare and protected species in many regions.
