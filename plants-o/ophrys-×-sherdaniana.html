Ophrys × sherdaniana

Ophrys × sherdaniana, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Ophrys apifera (the bee orchid) and Ophrys insectifera (the wasp orchid), resulting in a plant with a distinctive floral morphology. The orchid's flowers mimic the appearance of female bees or wasps, a trait known as sexual deception, which is used to attract male pollinators. This mimicry is evident in the flower's coloration, pattern, and even its three-dimensional structure.

Ophrys × sherdaniana typically grows in Mediterranean climates, thriving in well-drained soils and sunny to partially shaded environments.
