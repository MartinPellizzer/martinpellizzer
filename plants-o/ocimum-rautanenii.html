Ocimum rautanenii

Ocimum rautanenii, commonly known as the African basil, is a perennial herb native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like mint and sage. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color.

Ocimum rautanenii is valued for its fragrant leaves and is used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
