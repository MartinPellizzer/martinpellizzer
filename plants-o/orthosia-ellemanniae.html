Orthosia ellemanniae

Orthosia ellemanniae, commonly known as the African orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant produces aerial rhizomes and has linear, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pale purple or white with a distinct lip, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Orthosia ellemanniae is appreciated for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
