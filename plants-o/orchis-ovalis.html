Orchis ovalis

Orchis ovalis, commonly known as the oval orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. The plant has a slender, upright stem that bears several leaves, and its flowers are arranged in a loose raceme.

Orchis ovalis thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows, and it plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
