Oenothera acutissima

Oenothera acutissima, commonly known as the narrowleaf evening primrose, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and is characterized by its milky sap, which contains various bioactive compounds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in desert or semi-arid environments.

Oenothera acutissima is part of the Onagraceae family, which includes other evening primroses and related species.
