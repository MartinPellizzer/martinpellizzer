Orchis × dubia

Orchis × dubia, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a natural hybrid between Orchis morio and Orchis militaris.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe, particularly the British Isles. The plant features tall, slender stems with showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in woodland and meadow habitats, often in partial shade.
