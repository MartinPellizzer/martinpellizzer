Oxytropis pinetorum

Oxytropis pinetorum, commonly known as the pine trefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and pine forests. It has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in poor, well-drained soils.

Oxytropis pinetorum is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its ecosystem.
