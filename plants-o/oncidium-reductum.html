Oncidium reductum

Oncidium reductum, commonly known as the red-tipped orchid, is a species native to South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features slender, upright stems and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and red. Its roots are often covered in a dense, velvety coating, which helps in absorbing moisture.

Oncidium reductum is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
