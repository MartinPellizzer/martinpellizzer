Ocotea pulchella

Ocotea pulchella, commonly known as the purple-leafed ocotea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, glossy dark green leaves that turn a striking purple or reddish hue during the flowering season. Its small, white flowers are clustered in catkins, and it produces small, dark berries that are an important food source for various wildlife.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
