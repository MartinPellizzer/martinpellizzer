Opuntia lubrica var. aurea

Opuntia lubrica var. aurea, commonly known as the golden prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems that are covered in clusters of bright yellow spines. The plant produces vibrant orange to red flowers during the spring and summer months. Its pads, or cladodes, are edible and often used in culinary applications.

The plant is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, succulent stems.
