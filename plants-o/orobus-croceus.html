Orobus croceus

Orobus croceus, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a distinctive red lip. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil efficiently.

Orobus croceus is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its cultural significance in some traditional practices.
