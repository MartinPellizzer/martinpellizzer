Orchis × schebestae

Orchis × schebestae, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Orchis morio and Orchis mascula.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant produces tall, erect stems with showy, spurred orchid flowers that are usually violet or purple in color. Its flowers are characterized by a distinctive lip that is often marked with contrasting patterns.

This hybrid is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its beauty.
