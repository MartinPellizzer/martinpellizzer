Oenanthe crocata subsp. apiifolia

Oenanthe crocata subsp.

apiifolia, commonly known as the meadow cow-wheat, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes many familiar plants like carrots and parsley.

The plant is characterized by its slender stems and small, white or yellowish flowers that appear in umbels. Its leaves are typically deeply lobed, resembling those of other members of the carrot family.
