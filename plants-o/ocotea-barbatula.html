Ocotea barbatula

Ocotea barbatula, commonly known as the Brazilian cinnamon tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark-colored wood and fragrant leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that contains a single seed.

Ocotea barbatula is valued for its essential oils and has traditional uses in medicine and perfumery.
