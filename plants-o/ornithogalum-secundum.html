Ornithogalum secundum

Ornithogalum secundum, commonly known as the lesser star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, linear leaves and produces umbel-like clusters of white, star-shaped flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments and has a corm as its storage organ. It is often found in rocky soils and open woodlands.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
