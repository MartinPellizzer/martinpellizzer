Odixia

Odixia, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to tropical Africa.

These plants are characterized by their compact rosettes of velvety, waxy leaves and showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors. Odixia species typically thrive in humid, well-drained soil and require bright, indirect light to flourish. They are popular as houseplants due to their ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.

The flowers often bloom in spring and summer, adding a vibrant touch to indoor gardens.
