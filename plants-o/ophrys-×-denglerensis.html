Ophrys × denglerensis

Ophrys × denglerensis, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant that belongs to the Orchidaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Ophrys apifera (the bee orchid) and Ophrys insectifera (the wasp orchid), resulting in a unique combination of traits from both parents. The plant is characterized by its striking, bee-like flowers that feature a prominent yellow and brown pouch, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. Ophrys × denglerensis is typically found in Mediterranean regions, growing in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a hybrid species, showcasing the genetic diversity and evolutionary adaptations within the orchid family.
