Orchis caprina

Orchis caprina, commonly known as the alpine orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are usually arranged in a spike and are pollinated by insects, particularly bees.

Orchis caprina is a rare and protected species, often found in mountainous areas of countries like Switzerland, Austria, and Italy.
