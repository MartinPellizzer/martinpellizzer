Ophrys fusca subsp. caesiella

Ophrys fusca subsp.

caesiella, commonly known as the purple bee orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its striking, bee-like flowers that feature a dark purple lip and a yellowish-green column.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its inflorescence consists of a single spike bearing several flowers, each with a specialized structure that mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male pollinators.

The species is known for its intricate floral morphology and its role in specialized pollination systems.
