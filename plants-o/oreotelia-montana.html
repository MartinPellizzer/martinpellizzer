Oreotelia montana

Oreotelia montana, commonly known as the mountain oreotelia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or mountainous terrain. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, adapted to withstand cold and strong winds.

Oreotelia montana is known for its hardy nature and is sometimes cultivated in alpine gardens for its ornamental value.
