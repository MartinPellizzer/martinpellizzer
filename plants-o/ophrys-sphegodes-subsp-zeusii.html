Ophrys sphegodes subsp. zeusii

Ophrys sphegodes subsp.

zeusii, commonly known as the Zeus bee orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Ophrys genus, known for its intricate floral mimicry. This orchid produces a single, showy flower that closely resembles a female bee, a feature evolved to attract male bees for pollination.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and open woodlands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and a short, erect stem.

Its unique morphology and specialized pollination strategy make it a fascinating subject in the study of plant evolution and ecology.
