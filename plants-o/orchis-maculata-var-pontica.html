Orchis maculata var. pontica

Orchis maculata var. pontica, commonly known as the Pontic spotted orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southeastern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or meadow habitats. The plant features a tall, erect stem with several leaves and produces a spike of violet or purple flowers with distinctive dark spots. Its flowers are bilaterally symmetrical, a characteristic feature of the Orchidaceae family.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
