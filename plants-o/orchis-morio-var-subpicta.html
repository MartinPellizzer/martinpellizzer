Orchis morio var. subpicta

Orchis morio var. subpicta, commonly known as the painted orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear lance-shaped leaves and strikingly colored flowers. The flowers typically have a violet or purple hue with intricate patterns that resemble painted designs, giving it its common name. This variety is often found in meadows and woodland edges, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.

Its blooming period usually occurs in late spring to early summer, making it a notable addition to botanical gardens and natural habitats.
