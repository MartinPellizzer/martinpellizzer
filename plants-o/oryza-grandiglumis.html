Oryza grandiglumis

Oryza grandiglumis, commonly known as the giant ricegrass, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and large, elongated leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that contain numerous florets. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a grass-like structure typical of the Poaceae family.

Oryza grandiglumis is often found in wetland and riparian habitats, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
