Oxytropis baschkiriensis

Oxytropis baschkiriensis, commonly known as the Bashkir milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in steppes and rocky soils.

Oxytropis baschkiriensis is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in soil enrichment and ecological restoration.
