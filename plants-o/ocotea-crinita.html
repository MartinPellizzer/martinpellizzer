Ocotea crinita

Ocotea crinita, commonly known as the silky ocotea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage and fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within the understory of tropical rainforests. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and have a fine, silky texture, giving it its common name.

Ocotea crinita produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are followed by dark blue, berry-like fruits.
