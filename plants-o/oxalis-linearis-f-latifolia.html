Oxalis linearis f. latifolia

Oxalis linearis f. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf wood sorrel, is a variant of the wood sorrel species characterized by its broader leaves compared to the typical form.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in shaded, moist environments. The plant features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers with five petals. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
