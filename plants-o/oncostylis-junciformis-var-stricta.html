Oncostylis junciformis var. stricta

Oncostylis junciformis var. stricta, commonly known as the narrow rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often densely packed, giving it a grass-like appearance. Its leaves are narrow and strap-shaped, often emerging from the base of the plant.

The flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in spikes, and are adapted for wind pollination.
