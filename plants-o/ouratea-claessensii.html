Ouratea claessensii

Ouratea claessensii, commonly known as the white-flowered ouratea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting various pollinators. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in warm climates for its aesthetic appeal.
