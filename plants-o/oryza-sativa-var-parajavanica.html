Oryza sativa var. parajavanica

Oryza sativa var. parajavanica, commonly known as the Javanese rice, is a cultivated rice variety native to Indonesia.

It is characterized by its short, slender grains and typically exhibits a range of colors, including white, brown, and red. This variety is well adapted to tropical climates and requires a warm, humid environment for optimal growth. It is primarily grown in lowland areas with rich, well-drained soils.

The plant has a short growth cycle, making it suitable for multiple harvests in a single growing season.
