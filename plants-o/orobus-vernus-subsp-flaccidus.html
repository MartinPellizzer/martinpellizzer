Orobus vernus subsp. flaccidus

Orobus vernus subsp.

flaccidus, commonly known as the flaccid bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

The plant features slender, erect stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are bell-shaped, usually pink or purple, and appear in clusters at the ends of the stems.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
