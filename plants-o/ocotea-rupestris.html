Ocotea rupestris

Ocotea rupestris, commonly known as the rock ocotea, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often on rocky slopes or in mountainous areas. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a distinctive glossy surface with a strong, spicy aroma.

The fruit is a small, woody drupe that contains a single seed, contributing to its ecological role in forest ecosystems.
