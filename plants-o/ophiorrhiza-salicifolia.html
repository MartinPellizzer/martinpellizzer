Ophiorrhiza salicifolia

Ophiorrhiza salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaved mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees in tropical regions.

It possesses small, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are typically white or pale green. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments and plays a role in the ecological interactions of its host species.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can significantly affect the growth and health of its host trees.
