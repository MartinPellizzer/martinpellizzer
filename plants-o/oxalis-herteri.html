Oxalis herteri

Oxalis herteri, commonly known as the Herter's wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a low-growing perennial with trifoliate leaves and small, delicate flowers that are typically yellow or orange. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Oxalis herteri thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and flowers.
