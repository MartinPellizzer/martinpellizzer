Oleander vulgaris

Oleander vulgaris, commonly known as the common oleander, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, red, and yellow. The plant has soft, fuzzy leaves and produces woody, branched stems. Oleander vulgaris is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in hot, dry climates.

It is also noted for its toxicity, as all parts of the plant contain cardiac glycosides that can be harmful to humans and animals.
