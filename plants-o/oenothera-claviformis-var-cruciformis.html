Oenothera claviformis var. cruciformis

Oenothera claviformis var. cruciformis, commonly known as the four-leaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive four-petaled flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has a slender, upright stem with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid environments, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native habitat's biodiversity.
