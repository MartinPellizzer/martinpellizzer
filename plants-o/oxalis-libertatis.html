Oxalis libertatis

Oxalis libertatis, commonly known as the Freedom Oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, delicate flowers. The plant produces bright yellow blooms with five petals, often resembling those of a wood sorrel. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Oxalis libertatis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
