Orobanche rubra

Orobanche rubra, commonly known as the red broomrape, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is found in various regions of Europe and Asia. The plant produces small, reddish-brown flowers and has a distinctive, branched stem. It parasitizes a wide range of host plants, including those in the Fabaceae and Asteraceae families.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can cause significant damage to agricultural crops.
