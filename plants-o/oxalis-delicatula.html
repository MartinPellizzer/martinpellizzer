Oxalis delicatula

Oxalis delicatula, commonly known as the silverleaf wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features delicate, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its foliage and the subtle beauty of its blooms.
