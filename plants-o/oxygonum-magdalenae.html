Oxygonum magdalenae

Oxygonum magdalenae, commonly known as the Magdalenae moonflower, is a striking night-blooming plant native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night and close by dawn. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a deep green color. Its flowers emit a strong, sweet scent to attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

Oxygonum magdalenae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming habit.
