Oxalis tuberosa subsp. tuberosa

Oxalis tuberosa subsp.

tuberosa, commonly known as the purple false shamrock, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically heart-shaped and have a striking purple hue.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible tuberous roots. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. Oxalis tuberosa subsp.

tuberosa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and historical use in traditional cuisines.
