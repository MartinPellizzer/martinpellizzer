Odontoglossum melanthes

Odontoglossum melanthes, commonly known as the black orchid, is a species native to the high altitudes of South America, particularly in countries like Ecuador and Peru.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking dark purple to black flowers, which stand out against its green foliage. The plant typically grows in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained soil to thrive. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in its natural habitat.

Odontoglossum melanthes is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
