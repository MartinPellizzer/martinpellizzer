Onobrychis persica

Onobrychis persica, commonly known as Persian clover, is a perennial legume native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid and semi-arid regions. It has a deep root system that allows it to survive in drought conditions.

Onobrychis persica is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural and ecological restoration settings.
