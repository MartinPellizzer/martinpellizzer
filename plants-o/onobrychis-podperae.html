Onobrychis podperae

Onobrychis podperae, commonly known as the Podpera burr clover, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces distinctive, spiny pods that contain small, oval seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or rocky habitats.

Due to its nitrogen-fixing ability, it can contribute to soil fertility in agricultural settings.
