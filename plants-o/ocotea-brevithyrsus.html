Ocotea brevithyrsus

Ocotea brevithyrsus, commonly known as the short-thyrsed ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers that develop into dark blue berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments within tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
