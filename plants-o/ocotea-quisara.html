Ocotea quisara

Ocotea quisara, commonly known as the Brazilian lauraceae, is a member of the Lauraceae family, which includes laurel trees and other aromatic plants.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are typically glossy and lanceolate in shape. Its small, fragrant flowers are followed by berries that contain the seeds, contributing to its ecological role in the forest ecosystem.

Ocotea quisara is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
