Oenothera tetragona subsp. glauca

Oenothera tetragona subsp.

glauca, commonly known as bluestem evening primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It is characterized by its slender, glaucous stems and showy yellow flowers that bloom in the evening.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as prairies and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and native plant restoration.
