Oenothera ovata

Oenothera ovata, commonly known as the oval evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oval seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies, open woodlands, and along roadsides.

Oenothera ovata is a member of the Onagraceae family, which includes other evening primroses and related species.
