Oberonia denticulata

Oberonia denticulata, commonly known as the toothed orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its intricate, delicate flowers that typically feature a dark purple or brownish coloration. The plant grows as an epiphyte, often found clinging to tree bark in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually elliptical and have a waxy texture, adapting to its moist, tropical habitat.

The orchid's unique floral structure and specific growing conditions make it a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
