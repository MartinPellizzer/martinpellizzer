Oxalis lobata var. intermedia

Oxalis lobata var. intermedia, commonly known as the hybrid oxalis or the common wood sorrel, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. It is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize.

Oxalis lobata var. intermedia is also noted for its edible leaves, which have a tangy, sour flavor.
