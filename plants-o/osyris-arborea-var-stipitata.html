Osyris arborea var. stipitata

Osyris arborea var. stipitata, commonly known as the African silver cluster tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of silver-white, feathery leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by woody, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its unique foliage.

The species is also valued for its hardiness and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
