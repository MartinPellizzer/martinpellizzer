Ornithogalum bohemicum var. saxatile

Ornithogalum bohemicum var. saxatile, commonly known as the alpine star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers during the spring. Its botanical characteristics include a bulbous root system and a preference for well-drained, sandy soils.

It is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
