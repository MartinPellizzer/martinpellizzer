Orchis × colemanii

Orchis × colemanii, commonly known as the Coleman's orchid, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Orchis morio and Orchis anthropophora.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family, characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet to purple flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing a spike of 5–15 flowers. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland habitats across Europe.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a range of morphological variations, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
