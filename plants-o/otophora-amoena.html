Otophora amoena

Otophora amoena, commonly known as the pretty orchid, is a species of orchid native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, arranged in a rosette pattern around the base.

The flowers are borne on inflorescences and are often used in traditional horticulture for their ornamental value.
