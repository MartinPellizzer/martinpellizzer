Odontella volkensii

Odontella volkensii, commonly known as the Volkens' sea silk, is a unicellular alga belonging to the diatom family.

It is characterized by its intricate silica cell wall, which forms a delicate, ornate shell. This species is typically found in marine and brackish water environments, where it plays a role in aquatic ecosystems. Odontella volkensii is known for its ability to form colonies, contributing to the formation of biofilms on submerged surfaces.

Its presence is often an indicator of water quality and environmental health in aquatic habitats.
