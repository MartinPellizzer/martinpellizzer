Orchis majalis var. occidentalis

Orchis majalis var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western fairy orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial with a short, rhizomatous stem and a single, showy, spurred flower. The plant typically blooms in early spring, producing a delicate, violet-colored flower with a distinctive, elongated spur. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

The species is characterized by its small size and specialized pollination mechanism involving specific insect species.
