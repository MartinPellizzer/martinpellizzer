Ocimum filamentosum

Ocimum filamentosum, commonly known as the filamentous basil, is a species of flowering plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or white. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown as an ornamental or for its essential oils.

Its foliage is used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb in some cultures.
