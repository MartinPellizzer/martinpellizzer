Oenothera hexandra subsp. gracilis

Oenothera hexandra subsp.

gracilis, commonly known as the slender evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and showy yellow flowers that open in the evening.

The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, oily seeds. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as prairies and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential medicinal applications.
