Oxytropis kodarensis

Oxytropis kodarensis, commonly known as the Kodar Valley locoweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. It produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Oxytropis kodarensis is also known for its toxic properties, making it potentially harmful to grazing animals.
