Oxytropis mandokhailii

Oxytropis mandokhailii, commonly known as the Kashmir oxytropis, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale pink to white in color. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climatic conditions.

Oxytropis mandokhailii is also noted for its potential ecological importance in stabilizing soil in mountainous regions.
