Oncidium falcipetalum var. pavonii

Oncidium falcipetalum var. pavonii, commonly known as the Rainbow Orchid, is a striking orchid species native to South America.

It features vibrant, colorful flowers that often display a gradient of hues, ranging from red to orange and yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its leaves are long, strap-like, and arranged in a fan shape, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The flowers bloom in clusters, making it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
