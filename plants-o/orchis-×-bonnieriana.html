Orchis × bonnieriana

Orchis × bonnieriana, commonly known as the Bonnier's twayblade, is a hybrid orchid species native to Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and is often found in association with other orchid species. Its flowers are usually arranged in a spike and have a distinctive three-lobed lip.

Orchis × bonnieriana is a rare and protected species, valued for its ecological and botanical significance.
