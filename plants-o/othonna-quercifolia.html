Othonna quercifolia

Othonna quercifolia, commonly known as the oak-leaved othonna, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves that resemble those of an oak tree, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Othonna quercifolia thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in suitable climates.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
