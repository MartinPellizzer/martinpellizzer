Olyra polypodioides

Olyra polypodioides, commonly known as the broad-leafed maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are typically green and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Olyra polypodioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
