Ochna cuspidata

Ochna cuspidata, commonly known as the African star jelly or yellow star jelly, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets, and its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds. Ochna cuspidata is often found in moist forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

It also has traditional medicinal uses in some African cultures.
