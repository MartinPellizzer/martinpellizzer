Oreodaphne regalis

Oreodaphne regalis, commonly known as the royal oreodaphne, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and lanceolate, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by elliptical, dark blue-black berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forests.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
