Olax lanceolata

Olax lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf olax, is a shrub native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Olacaceae and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and woody stems, which contribute to its resilience in humid climates.

Olax lanceolata is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage.
