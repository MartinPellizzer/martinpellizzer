Oenothera andina var. anomala

Oenothera andina var. anomala, commonly known as the Andean evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its striking yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening and close by morning. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and has adapted to the harsh, cold conditions of its native habitat.

Oenothera andina var. anomala is also valued for its potential medicinal properties and ornamental appeal.
