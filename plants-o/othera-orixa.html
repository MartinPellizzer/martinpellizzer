Othera orixa

Othera orixa, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple and white flowers that resemble a passion fruit, with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of a heart. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. Botanically, it belongs to the family Passifloraceae and is valued for its ornamental beauty and medicinal properties.

Its rapid growth and unique floral structure make it a popular choice for gardens and horticultural displays.
