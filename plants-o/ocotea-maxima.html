Ocotea maxima

Ocotea maxima, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark-colored wood that is highly valued for its durability and scent. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by dark, spherical berries.

Ocotea maxima is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests.
