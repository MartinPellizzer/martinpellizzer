Ophrys × hernandesii

Ophrys × hernandesii, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating hybrid species resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is characterized by its intricate, bee-like floral morphology, which includes a prominent, hairy lip that mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in calcareous soils, often found in open grasslands and woodland edges. Its flowers are typically pale yellow to brown in color, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

This orchid is not only botanically significant for its hybrid nature but also plays an important role in local ecosystems through its pollination interactions.
