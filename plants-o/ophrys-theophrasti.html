Ophrys theophrasti

Ophrys theophrasti, commonly known as the Greek orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its unique flower structure, which resembles a female bee, aiding in its specialized pollination by male bees. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of a bee's body.

Ophrys theophrasti is a perennial herb with a fibrous root system and lanceolate leaves.
