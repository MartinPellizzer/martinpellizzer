Oxalis hernandesii

Oxalis hernandesii, commonly known as the Canary Islands wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive pattern of reddish veins, and its flowers are typically small, five-petaled, and range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
