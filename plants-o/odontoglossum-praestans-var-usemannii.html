Odontoglossum praestans var. usemannii

Odontoglossum praestans var. usemannii, commonly known as the Usemann's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of violet or purple flowers with a distinctive lip that is often adorned with intricate patterns. It prefers cool, humid environments and is often found growing in shaded, moist habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated by enthusiasts for its beauty and unique floral structure.
