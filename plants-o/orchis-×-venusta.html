Orchis × venusta

Orchis × venusta, commonly known as the showy orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Orchis moria and Orchis mascula.

It is characterized by its striking, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow-green lip. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland habitats across Europe. It blooms from late spring to early summer, producing tall, upright stems with a few to several flowers.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often found in conservation areas and botanical gardens.
