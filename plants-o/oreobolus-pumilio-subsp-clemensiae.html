Oreobolus pumilio subsp. clemensiae

Oreobolus pumilio subsp.

clemensiae, commonly known as the dwarf speargrass, is a small, herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa. It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal dunes.

The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is adapted to withstand drought conditions, with a deep root system and reduced leaf surface to minimize water loss.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists and conservationists due to its ecological role and limited distribution.
