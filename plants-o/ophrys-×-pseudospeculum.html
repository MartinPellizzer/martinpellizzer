Ophrys × pseudospeculum

Ophrys × pseudospeculum, commonly known as the mimic orchid, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its intricate floral structure, which closely resembles the female insects of its pollinators, typically bees. The plant produces showy, bee-mimicking flowers that are often adorned with intricate patterns and coloration. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its unique pollination mechanism, it plays a significant role in ecological interactions within its native environment.
