Oxalis gracilis var. purpurea

Oxalis gracilis var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It features delicate, trifoliate leaves with a distinct purple hue and produces small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to white. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas. It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family, known for its calcium oxalate crystals in the leaves.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
