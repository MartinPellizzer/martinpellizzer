Odontophyllum cuscoensis

Odontophyllum cuscoensis, commonly known as the Cusco orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, intricate floral structure. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas at high altitudes, thriving in cool and humid conditions. Its flowers are often brightly colored, with a complex arrangement of petals and sepals that attract specific pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
