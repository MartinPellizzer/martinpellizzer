Osteospermum hafstroemii

Osteospermum hafstroemii, commonly known as the blue oatbrush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads that resemble oat clusters. The plant features hairy, gray-green leaves and produces showy, daisy-like blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

Osteospermum hafstroemii thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
