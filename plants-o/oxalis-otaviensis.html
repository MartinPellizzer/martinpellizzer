Oxalis otaviensis

Oxalis otaviensis, commonly known as the Otavi wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in mountainous regions, where it adapts well to cooler climates.

Oxalis otaviensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
