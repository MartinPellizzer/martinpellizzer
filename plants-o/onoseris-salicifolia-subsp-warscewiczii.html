Onoseris salicifolia subsp. warscewiczii

Onoseris salicifolia subsp.

warscewiczii, commonly known as the narrow-leafed goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of East Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The subspecies is distinguished by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
