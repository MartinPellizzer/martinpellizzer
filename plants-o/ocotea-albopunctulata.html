Ocotea albopunctulata

Ocotea albopunctulata, commonly known as the white-spotted ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon. The tree is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves with distinctive white spots, giving it its common name. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow.

The fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed, and the tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
