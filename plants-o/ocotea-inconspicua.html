Ocotea inconspicua

Ocotea inconspicua, commonly known as the fragrant ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree belonging to the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed enclosed in a fleshy pulp.

Ocotea inconspicua is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
