Orchipeda grandifolia

Orchipeda grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features long, slender stems with showy, violet or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. Its roots are highly specialized, forming symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi to absorb nutrients from the soil.

Orchipeda grandifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses due to its specific environmental requirements.
