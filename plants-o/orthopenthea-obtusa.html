Orthopenthea obtusa

Orthopenthea obtusa, commonly known as the broad-leafed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet. The plant features lanceolate leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Orthopenthea obtusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
