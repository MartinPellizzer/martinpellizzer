Orthosiphon pseudoserratus

Orthosiphon pseudoserratus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cat's whiskers, is a member of the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and serrated leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that are clustered in whorls along the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Orthosiphon pseudoserratus is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
