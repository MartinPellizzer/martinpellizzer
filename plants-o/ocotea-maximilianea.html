Ocotea maximilianea

Ocotea maximilianea, commonly known as the Mexican laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, small white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, dark purple drupe that contains a single seed.

Ocotea maximilianea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
