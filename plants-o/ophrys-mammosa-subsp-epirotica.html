Ophrys mammosa subsp. epirotica

Ophrys mammosa subsp.

epirotica, commonly known as the Epirotic Fly Orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female wasp to attract male wasps for pollination.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in shaded woodland habitats. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with intricate patterns that aid in mimicry.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
