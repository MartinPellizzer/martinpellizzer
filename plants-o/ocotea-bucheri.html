Ocotea bucheri

Ocotea bucheri, commonly known as the Boucher's ocotea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage and bark. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within the understory of tropical rainforests. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species.
