Ocotea julianii

Ocotea julianii, commonly known as Julian's ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean, particularly found in the Greater Antilles.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurel, avocado, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces drupes that are typically dark blue to black when mature.

Ocotea julianii is valued for its aromatic wood and is also used in traditional medicine.
