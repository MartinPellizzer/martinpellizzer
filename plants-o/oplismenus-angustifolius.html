Oplismenus angustifolius

Oplismenus angustifolius, commonly known as narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features upright stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of small, purple to pinkish flowers in the late summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, rich soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Oplismenus angustifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
