Oxytropis ochrocephala var. ochrocephala

Oxytropis ochrocephala var. ochrocephala, commonly known as yellowhead locoweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and has a deep taproot system that allows it to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. It is often found in dry grasslands, sagebrush steppe, and rocky soils.

This species is known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock.
