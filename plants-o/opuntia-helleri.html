Opuntia helleri

Opuntia helleri, commonly known as the Heller's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems that are covered in spines and are often ribbed. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tunas, is edible and has a sweet, juicy flavor.

Opuntia helleri is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water to thrive.
