Onobrychis alba var. rhodopea

Onobrychis alba var. rhodopea, commonly known as the Rhodope white vetch, is a perennial legume native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces cylindrical pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
