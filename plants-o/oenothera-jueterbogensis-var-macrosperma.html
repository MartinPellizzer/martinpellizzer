Oenothera jueterbogensis var. macrosperma

Oenothera jueterbogensis var. macrosperma, commonly known as the large-seeded evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant produces large, fleshy seeds enclosed in a capsule, which is a distinctive botanical feature. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.
