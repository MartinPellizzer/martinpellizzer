Oxalis darwinii

Oxalis darwinii, commonly known as the common wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of five-petaled flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often consumed by birds and other wildlife. Oxalis darwinii thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant yellow flowers and delicate foliage.
