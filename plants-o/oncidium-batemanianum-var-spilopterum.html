Oncidium batemanianum var. spilopterum

Oncidium batemanianum var. spilopterum, commonly known as the Bateman's Oncidium, is a terrestrial orchid native to South America.

It features a sympodial growth habit with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often arranged in a raceme. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, adapting to its natural habitat in humid, shaded environments.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
