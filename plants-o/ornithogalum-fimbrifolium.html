Ornithogalum fimbrifolium

Ornithogalum fimbrifolium, commonly known as the thread-leaved star-of-Bethlehem, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white or pale blue flowers with six petals. The plant is characterized by its short, tuberous roots and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It blooms in spring and is adapted to arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and moths, contributing to its ecological significance.
