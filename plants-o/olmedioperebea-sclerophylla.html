Olmedioperebea sclerophylla

Olmedioperebea sclerophylla, commonly known as the African olive, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its sclerophyllous leaves, which are thick, leathery, and often have a grayish or silver-green color. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.

Olmedioperebea sclerophylla is also valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses in local communities.
