Obione lasiantha

Obione lasiantha, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features silver-green, fuzzy foliage and produces clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

Obione lasiantha is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
