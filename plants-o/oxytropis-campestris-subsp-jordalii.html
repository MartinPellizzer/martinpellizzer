Oxytropis campestris subsp. jordalii

Oxytropis campestris subsp.

jordalii, commonly known as the yellow locoweed, is a flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

The plant is characterized by its upright stems and compound leaves with small, rounded leaflets. It produces yellow to orange flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer.

This subspecies is adapted to arid environments and is often found in the western United States.
