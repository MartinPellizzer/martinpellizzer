Onobrychis miniata f. dielsii

Onobrychis miniata f. dielsii, commonly known as the dwarf yellow clover, is a low-growing perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and nitrogen-fixing ability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and for soil improvement.
