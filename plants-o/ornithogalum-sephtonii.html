Ornithogalum sephtonii

Ornithogalum sephtonii, commonly known as the Cape mountain onion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features a bulbous stem with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white or pale blue flowers. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, bell-shaped blooms that appear in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Ornithogalum sephtonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
