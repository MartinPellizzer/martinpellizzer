Oxyanthera rhomboglossa

Oxyanthera rhomboglossa, commonly known as the diamondleaf aster, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, rhomboid-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish to white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open woodlands or along streambanks.

Oxyanthera rhomboglossa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
