Ochetophila nana

Ochetophila nana, commonly known as the dwarf ochetophila, is a small herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features compact rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowering stems produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or white in color.

Ochetophila nana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with specific humidity and light requirements.
