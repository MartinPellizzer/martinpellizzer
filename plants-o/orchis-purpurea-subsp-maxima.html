Orchis purpurea subsp. maxima

Orchis purpurea subsp.

maxima, commonly known as the purple orchid, is a species of orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or grassland habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while the flowers are borne on a spike with a single, prominent lip.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
