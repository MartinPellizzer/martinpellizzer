Ornithocephalus dressleri

Ornithocephalus dressleri, commonly known as the Dressler's orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often display vibrant colors. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its inflorescence consists of a single, elongated stem bearing several flowers, which are usually arranged in a spiral pattern.

The botanical features of Ornithocephalus dressleri make it a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts and horticulturists.
