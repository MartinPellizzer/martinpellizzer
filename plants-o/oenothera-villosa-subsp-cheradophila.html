Oenothera villosa subsp. cheradophila

Oenothera villosa subsp.

cheradophila, commonly known as the silky evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its hairy stems and showy yellow flowers that bloom in the evening.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oily seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry grasslands and open woodlands.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional herbal use.
