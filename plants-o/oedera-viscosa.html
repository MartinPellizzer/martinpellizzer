Oedera viscosa

Oedera viscosa, commonly known as the sticky oedera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. The plant is characterized by its hairy, sticky leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Oedera viscosa is often found in dry, disturbed habitats and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
