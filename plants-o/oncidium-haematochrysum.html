Oncidium haematochrysum

Oncidium haematochrysum, commonly known as the red-veined orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that display a striking combination of red, yellow, and green hues. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are borne in clusters and are known for their long-lasting bloom, making it a popular choice for ornamental purposes.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of growing conditions, though it requires bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.
