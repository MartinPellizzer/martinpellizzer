Oxytropis splendens var. richardsonii

Oxytropis splendens var. richardsonii, commonly known as the Richardson's yellow vetch, is a member of the legume family Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. This plant is native to the western United States, particularly in areas with dry, rocky soils. It thrives in full sun and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.

The plant plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
