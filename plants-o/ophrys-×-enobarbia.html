Ophrys × enobarbia

Ophrys × enobarbia, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is characterized by its intricate, bee-like flower structure that mimics the appearance and scent of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows across Europe. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a distinctive black beard and intricate patterns.

Ophrys × enobarbia is a perennial herb with a short, rhizomatous rootstock and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.
