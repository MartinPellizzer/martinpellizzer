Oxytropis brachycarpa

Oxytropis brachycarpa, commonly known as the short-fruited milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as sagebrush steppe and prairies. Its fruit is a small, hairy pod that contains several seeds.

Oxytropis brachycarpa plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
