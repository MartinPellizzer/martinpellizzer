Onobrychis avanakensis

Onobrychis avanakensis, commonly known as the Armenian clover, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is typically found in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in temperate climates. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in its native habitat.
