Orchis latifolia var. elatior

Orchis latifolia var. elatior, commonly known as the greater purple orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem that can reach up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces showy, violet or purple flowers arranged in a loose raceme, with sepals and petals that are often adorned with intricate markings. Its leaves are lanceolate and grass-like, typically arranged in a basal rosette.

This orchid thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
