Onagra argillicola

Onagra argillicola, commonly known as the clay-loving evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Onagraceae family and is characterized by its yellow, four-petaled flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy or clay soils and is adapted to drought conditions. Its scientific name reflects its preference for clay soils, while its common name highlights its flowering time.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties, with its seeds containing omega-6 fatty acids.
