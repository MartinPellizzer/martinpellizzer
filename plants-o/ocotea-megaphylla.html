Ocotea megaphylla

Ocotea megaphylla, commonly known as the broad-leaf ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurel trees and cinnamon plants. The plant is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible for some bird species.

Ocotea megaphylla is often found in humid lowland forests and is valued for its aromatic wood.
