Ophrys oestrifera subsp. lemnosiana

Ophrys oestrifera subsp.

lemnosiana, commonly known as the Lemnos bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the Greek island of Lemnos. It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination.

The plant produces a single, showy flower with a dark, bee-like labellum and a pale yellow throat. It thrives in calcareous soils and is typically found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species.
