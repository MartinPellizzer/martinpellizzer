Onopordum messeniacum

Onopordum messeniacum, commonly known as the Messenia thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features robust, spiny stems and large, silvery-green leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces showy purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and hardy member of the Asteraceae family.
