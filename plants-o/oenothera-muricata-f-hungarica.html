Oenothera muricata f. hungarica

Oenothera muricata f. hungarica, commonly known as the Hungarian evening primrose, is a perennial plant native to the Carpathian region.

It belongs to the Onagraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and has been cultivated for its unique floral display.
