Ocotea tenera

Ocotea tenera, commonly known as the slender ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and aromatic, dark green leaves. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically appearing in clusters, and it produces small, round berries that are edible for some bird species.

Ocotea tenera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
