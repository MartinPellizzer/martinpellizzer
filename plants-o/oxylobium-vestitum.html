Oxylobium vestitum

Oxylobium vestitum, commonly known as the velvetleaf oxylobium, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and showy, fragrant blossoms that attract pollinators.

Oxylobium vestitum is typically found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats.
