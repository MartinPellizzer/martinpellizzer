Orchis steegeri

Orchis steegeri, commonly known as the Steeger's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, spurred flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing pink to purple flowers with a distinctive, elongated spur. Its leaves are grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils.

Orchis steegeri is often found in open woodlands and alpine meadows, where it plays a role in local biodiversity.
