Ormosia solimoesensis

Ormosia solimoesensis, commonly known as Brazilian tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces woody pods containing edible seeds that are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its roots are also valued for their nitrogen-fixing properties, contributing to soil fertility.

Ormosia solimoesensis plays an important ecological role in maintaining the biodiversity of its habitat.
