Oxygonium alismifolium

Oxygonium alismifolium, commonly known as the water sedge, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically grow in clusters. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense stands in marshes and along pond edges. Its rhizomes help stabilize soil and contribute to the ecological health of wetland environments.

Oxygonium alismifolium plays a role in water filtration and provides habitat for various aquatic organisms.
