Ocotea longifolia

Ocotea longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocado, and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered white flowers. Its fruits are drupes that turn from green to red as they mature.

Ocotea longifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
