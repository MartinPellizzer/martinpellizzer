Ocotea sublanuginosa

Ocotea sublanuginosa, commonly known as the silky ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves with a silvery underside. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark purple berries.

Ocotea sublanuginosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens for its aesthetic and aromatic qualities.
