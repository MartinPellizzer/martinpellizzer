Opuntia dillenii

Opuntia dillenii, commonly known as the Dillen's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in sharp spines and glochids, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible fruits known as tunas. Opuntia dillenii is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
