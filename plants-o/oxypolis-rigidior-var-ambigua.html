Oxypolis rigidior var. ambigua

Oxypolis rigidior var. ambigua, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. Its flowers are small, clustered in racemes, and often have a distinctive yellowish or greenish hue.

The species is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native environment.
