Opuntia mesacantha var. macrorhiza

Opuntia mesacantha var. macrorhiza, commonly known as the large-flowered prickly pear, is a cacti species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features flat, fleshy pads called cladodes, which are typically green and covered in spines. The plant produces large, showy yellow or orange flowers during the summer months. Its fruit, known as a tunaf, is a bright red, fleshy berry that contains numerous seeds.

This species is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and desert habitats.
