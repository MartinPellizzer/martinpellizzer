Osmanthus heterophyllus

Osmanthus heterophyllus, commonly known as the Chinese sweet osmanthus, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves that vary in size and shape depending on the growth conditions. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are highly valued for their aromatic properties. The fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that matures to a reddish-brown color.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and for the production of essential oils and medicinal compounds.
