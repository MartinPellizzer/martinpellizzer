Ochrosia minima

Ochrosia minima, commonly known as the dwarf chrysopelea, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces clusters of white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Ochrosia minima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique foliage and floral display.
