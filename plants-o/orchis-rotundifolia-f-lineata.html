Orchis rotundifolia f. lineata

Orchis rotundifolia f. lineata, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the orchid family (Orchidaceae).

It is characterized by its distinctive, spotted leaves and showy, violet or purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or grassland habitats. It is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, and it is often found in shaded or semi-shaded areas.

This orchid is a perennial herb that produces a single spike of flowers, often with a pleasant, sweet scent.
