Olearia phlogopappa subsp. angustifolia

Olearia phlogopappa subsp.

angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf daisy bush, is a shrub native to New Zealand. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and yellow flower heads.

The plant typically grows in coastal and subalpine regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in clusters during the summer months.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
