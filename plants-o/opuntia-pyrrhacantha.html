Opuntia pyrrhacantha

Opuntia pyrrhacantha, commonly known as the red-pearled prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems, which are covered in sharp spines and clusters of small, red-brown glochids. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months, followed by edible, red-fleshed fruits. Its succulent structure allows it to store water efficiently, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Opuntia pyrrhacantha is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local cultures.
