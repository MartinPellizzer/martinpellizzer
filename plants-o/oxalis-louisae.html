Oxalis louisae

Oxalis louisae, commonly known as the Louisiana wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, wooded areas. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive veins and produces clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers well-drained, acidic soils.

Oxalis louisae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
