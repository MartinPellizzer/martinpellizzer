Onopordum schultesii

Onopordum schultesii, commonly known as the Schultes' thistle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its taxonomy places it within the Asteraceae family, which includes many flowering plants with composite flowers.

The species is of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
