Osmelia paniculata

Osmelia paniculata, commonly known as the Florida flame flower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in wetlands and along the edges of swamps, thriving in moist, acidic soils. The plant produces showy, red-orange flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a rough texture, while its stems are often hairy.

Osmelia paniculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland habitats.
