Ornithogalum arabicum var. ochroleucum

Ornithogalum arabicum var. ochroleucum, commonly known as the white-flowered star-of-Bethlehem, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers during the spring. The plant belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental value and adaptability to arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
