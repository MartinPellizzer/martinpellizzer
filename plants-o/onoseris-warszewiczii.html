Onoseris warszewiczii

Onoseris warszewiczii, commonly known as the Warzewicz's goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, showcasing adaptations to harsh environmental conditions. Its yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
