Oenothera × rigirubata

Oenothera × rigirubata, commonly known as the showy evening primrose, is a hybrid species native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, orange-red flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun, displaying a sprawling, herbaceous growth habit. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
