Oldenlandia viscida

Oldenlandia viscida, commonly known as the sticky oldenlandia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round, sticky fruits that adhere to surfaces, aiding in seed dispersal. Its leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

Oldenlandia viscida is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks.
