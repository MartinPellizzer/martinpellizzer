Origanum × munzurense

Origanum × munzurense, commonly known as Turkish oregano, is a hybrid perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in summer. Its leaves are used in cooking and traditional medicine for their pungent, herbaceous flavor.

Origanum × munzurense thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for Mediterranean gardens.
