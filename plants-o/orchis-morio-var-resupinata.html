Orchis morio var. resupinata

Orchis morio var. resupinata, commonly known as the Resupinate Heath Orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its unique resupinate (upturned) flower structure, which is a distinctive botanical feature. The plant typically grows in woodland and meadow habitats, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish with a dark purple or brownish lip, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
