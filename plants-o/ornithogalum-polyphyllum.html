Ornithogalum polyphyllum

Ornithogalum polyphyllum, commonly known as the many-leaved star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a short, underground rhizome and produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers during the spring and summer seasons. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern at the base. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ornithogalum polyphyllum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
