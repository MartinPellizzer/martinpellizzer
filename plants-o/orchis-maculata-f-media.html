Orchis maculata f. media

Orchis maculata f. media, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a terrestrial orchid belonging to the family Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple and white flowers with a dark purple or brown spot on the lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating.

The species is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it plays an important role in local ecosystems.
