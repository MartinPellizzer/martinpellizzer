Olea similis

Olea similis, commonly known as the similar olive, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like the olive tree. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that ripens to a dark purple color.

Olea similis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
