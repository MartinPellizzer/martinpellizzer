Oncinema lineare

Oncinema lineare, commonly known as the linear oncinea, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its elongated, ribbon-like leaves that float just beneath the water's surface.

It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and lakes. The plant's leaves are typically narrow and linear, with a smooth, waxy surface that aids in reducing water resistance. It plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.

Despite its delicate appearance, Oncinema lineare is resilient and can spread through vegetative propagation.
