Omphalea triandra var. genuina

Omphalea triandra var. genuina, commonly known as the African moonseed, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, often ribbed stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds covered in a sticky substance.

This species is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
