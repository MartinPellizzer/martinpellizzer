Oenothera grandiflora var. pubescens

Oenothera grandiflora var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its varietal name "pubescens.". The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in disturbed soils along roadsides and in open woods.

Oenothera grandiflora var. pubescens is a perennial species that is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
