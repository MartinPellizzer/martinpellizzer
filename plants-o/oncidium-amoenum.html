Oncidium amoenum

Oncidium amoenum, commonly known as the orchid butterfly, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, fragrant flowers that resemble butterflies, with bright yellow and orange hues. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its inflorescence can produce multiple blooms, often appearing in clusters along a scape.

Oncidium amoenum requires well-draining potting media and consistent moisture to thrive.
