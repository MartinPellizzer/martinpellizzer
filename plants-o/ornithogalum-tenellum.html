Ornithogalum tenellum

Ornithogalum tenellum, commonly known as the star of Bethlehem, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a short, rhizomatous stem and produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has lance-shaped, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Ornithogalum tenellum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
