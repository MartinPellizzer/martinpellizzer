Ocotea pubescens

Ocotea pubescens, commonly known as the hairy ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and avocado. The tree is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive hairy underside. Its fragrant flowers and dark, aromatic fruits are important for pollinators and seed dispersal.

Ocotea pubescens is valued for its timber and has traditional uses in local medicine.
