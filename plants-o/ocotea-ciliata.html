Ocotea ciliata

Ocotea ciliata, commonly known as the fragrant ocotea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in countries like Brazil, Colombia, and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a spherical drupe that contains a single seed, and the plant is often found in humid, lowland forests.

Ocotea ciliata is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
