Ocotea truncata

Ocotea truncata, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and small, white flowers. The plant produces dark blue berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its wood is valued for its durability and aromatic properties.

Ocotea truncata thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.
