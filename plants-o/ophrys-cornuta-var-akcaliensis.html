Ophrys cornuta var. akcaliensis

Ophrys cornuta var. akcaliensis, commonly known as the Akcali horned orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to specific regions in Turkey.

It is characterized by its unique floral morphology, featuring a lip that resembles a male bee, which plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, thriving in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brownish in color, with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of insects.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, this variety is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
