Odontoglossum warocqueanum

Odontoglossum warocqueanum, commonly known as the Warocqueanum orchid, is a species native to the high-altitude cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to thrive in its cool, humid environment. Its leaves are typically broad and grass-like, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Odontoglossum warocqueanum is valued in horticulture for its beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
