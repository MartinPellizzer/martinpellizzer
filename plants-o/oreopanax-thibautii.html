Oreopanax thibautii

Oreopanax thibautii, commonly known as the Thibaut's arrowleaf, is a tropical plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves that are often deeply veined and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are followed by woody, spiky fruits containing seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
