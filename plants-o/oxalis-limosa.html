Oxalis limosa

Oxalis limosa, commonly known as the weeping oxalis, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, drooping inflorescences and trifoliate leaves that resemble those of the shamrock. The plant produces small, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Oxalis limosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
