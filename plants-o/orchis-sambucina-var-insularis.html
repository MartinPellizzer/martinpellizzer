Orchis sambucina var. insularis

Orchis sambucina var. insularis, commonly known as the Mediterranean orchid, is a rare and specialized plant found primarily in the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climate conditions, featuring a dormancy period during the summer months. Its morphology includes a basal rosette of leaves and a single, elongated flower spike.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species in some regions.
