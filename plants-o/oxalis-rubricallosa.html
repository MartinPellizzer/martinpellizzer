Oxalis rubricallosa

Oxalis rubricallosa, commonly known as the red-veined wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with distinctive red veins and clusters of small, five-petaled yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance.

Oxalis rubricallosa is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a resilient species.
