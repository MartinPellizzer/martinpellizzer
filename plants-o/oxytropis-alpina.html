Oxytropis alpina

Oxytropis alpina, commonly known as alpine oxytropis, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in rocky and alpine environments. The plant is characterized by its slender stems, small compound leaves, and clusters of purple or blue flowers. It has adapted to cold climates and poor soil conditions, making it a hardy species.

Oxytropis alpina plays a role in stabilizing soil in alpine ecosystems and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
