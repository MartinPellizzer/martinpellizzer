Orchis triplicata

Orchis triplicata, commonly known as the three-lobed orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and thrives in woodland or meadow habitats. Its flowers are usually arranged in a loose spike and have three distinct lobes on the petals.

Orchis triplicata is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
