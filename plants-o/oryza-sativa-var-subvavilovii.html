Oryza sativa var. subvavilovii

Oryza sativa var. subvavilovii, commonly known as a wild rice species, is a botanical variant of the rice plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its shorter stature, reduced grain size, and greater adaptability to diverse environmental conditions compared to cultivated rice varieties. This species plays a significant role in genetic research due to its potential for contributing beneficial traits to modern rice breeding programs. It exhibits a range of morphological and physiological features that make it valuable for studying plant evolution and adaptation.

Its ecological importance is further highlighted by its role in maintaining biodiversity in natural rice ecosystems.
