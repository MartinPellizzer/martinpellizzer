Orchis tridentata var. aetnensis

Orchis tridentata var. aetnensis, commonly known as the Aetna orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the volcanic slopes of Mount Etna in Sicily.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking violet or purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant exhibits a unique adaptation to its harsh, high-altitude environment, with thick, fleshy leaves that help store moisture. Its pollination is primarily carried out by specific species of bees, which are attracted to its nectar-rich flowers.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, this orchid is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
