Onobrychis viciifolia var. gaudiniana

Onobrychis viciifolia var. gaudiniana, commonly known as Gaudin's vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. It has a creeping habit with erect stems and produces small, pod-like fruits.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
