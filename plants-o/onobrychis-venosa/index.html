Onobrychis Venosa

Onobrychis venosa is a lesser-known yet fascinating legume native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. This plant is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring. Despite its modest appearance, it plays an important role in local ecosystems by fixing nitrogen into the soil. It is also valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in poor soils. Due to habitat loss and overgrazing, Onobrychis venosa is becoming increasingly rare, making conservation efforts all the more critical.
