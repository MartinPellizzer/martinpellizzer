Octarillum fruticosum

Octarillum fruticosum, commonly known as the coastal bladderwrack, is a perennial seaweed belonging to the family Fucaceae.

It is characterized by its dark brown, leathery fronds that grow in dense mats along rocky shorelines. The plant thrives in intertidal zones, where it can withstand fluctuating salinity and temperature levels. Its structure includes a holdfast for anchoring, a stipe for support, and blades for photosynthesis.

Octarillum fruticosum plays a vital role in coastal ecosystems by providing habitat for various marine organisms.
