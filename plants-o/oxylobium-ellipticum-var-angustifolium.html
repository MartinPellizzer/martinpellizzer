Oxylobium ellipticum var. angustifolium

Oxylobium ellipticum var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf oxylobium, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or chaparral habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and native plant gardens.
