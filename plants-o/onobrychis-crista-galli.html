Onobrychis crista-galli

Onobrychis crista-galli, commonly known as goat's rue, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive crown-like inflorescence, which gives it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Goat's rue is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it a valuable plant in ecological and agricultural contexts.
