Ocotea magnilimba

Ocotea magnilimba, commonly known as the giant ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its large, leathery, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces oval, dark brown fruits that contain one or two seeds.

Ocotea magnilimba is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in furniture and as a source of essential oils.
