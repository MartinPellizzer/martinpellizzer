Ornithogalum amplificatum

Ornithogalum amplificatum, commonly known as the giant star of Bethlehem, is a striking bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of white, star-shaped flowers, which resemble those of the traditional star of Bethlehem. The plant has a fleshy, underground bulb that stores water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, emerging from the base of the plant.

Ornithogalum amplificatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.
