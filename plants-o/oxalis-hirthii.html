Oxalis hirthii

Oxalis hirthii, commonly known as the Hirth's wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive reddish veins and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink or white. The plant is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Oxalis hirthii is also noted for its ability to tolerate drought conditions once established.
