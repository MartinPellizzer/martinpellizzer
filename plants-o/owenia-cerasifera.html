Owenia cerasifera

Owenia cerasifera, commonly known as the cherry apple tree, is a species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows to a height of 10-15 meters. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by edible fruits that resemble small cherries. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a temperate climate for optimal growth.
