Ornithopteris

Ornithopteris, commonly known as the feather fern, is a genus of ferns belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically divided into numerous small, lance-shaped segments. The plants thrive in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate forests and rocky crevices. These ferns play an important ecological role in forest understories by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
