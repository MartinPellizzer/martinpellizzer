Oncidium sodiroi

Oncidium sodiroi, commonly known as the Sodiro's orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, often arranged in racemes. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in dry conditions.

Oncidium sodiroi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in greenhouses and as a houseplant.
