Opuntia bonplandii

Opuntia bonplandii, commonly known as the Bonpland's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems that are covered in spines and glochids, which are small, barbed bristles. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months, followed by edible fruits known as tunas. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

Opuntia bonplandii is also valued for its role in traditional medicine and as a source of food in certain regions.
