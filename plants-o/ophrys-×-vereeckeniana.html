Ophrys × vereeckeniana

Ophrys × vereeckeniana, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant that exhibits unique botanical features.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera, two species known for their mimicry of female insects. The plant is characterized by its intricate floral structure, with a lip that closely resembles a female wasp, a key adaptation for attracting male wasps for pollination. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or along stream banks.

Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brown, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry and contribute to its ecological role in pollination.
