Oxossia marmorata

Oxossia marmorata, commonly known as the marble beauty orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate floral patterns that resemble marble, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its flowers are usually pale green or white with contrasting purple or red markings, and they bloom during the warmer months.

Oxossia marmorata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or shaded gardens.
