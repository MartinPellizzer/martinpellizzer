Oncidium × lueroroides

Oncidium × lueroroides, commonly known as the Orchid Hybrid, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Oncidium × harrisonianum and Oncidium × luerum.

It is characterized by its vibrant, often colorful flowers that can range from yellow to orange and red, with intricate patterns. The plant typically has a sympodial growth habit, forming pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for epiphytic or terrestrial growth, depending on the environment.

Oncidium × lueroroides is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
