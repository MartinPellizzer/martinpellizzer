Oligosporus floribundus

Oligosporus floribundus, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and it prefers well-drained soil in a sunny to partially shaded environment. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Oligosporus floribundus is also known for its mild fragrance, which attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
