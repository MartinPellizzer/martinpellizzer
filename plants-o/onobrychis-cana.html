Onobrychis cana

Onobrychis cana, commonly known as the pale vetch, is a perennial legume native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in dry, calcareous soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. The plant has slender, erect stems with trifoliate leaves composed of small, ovate leaflets. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom in early summer.

Onobrychis cana is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it valuable in ecological restoration and soil enrichment.
