Ophrys × placotica

Ophrys × placotica, commonly known as the bee orchid hybrid, is a fascinating plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Ophrys apifera (the bee orchid) and Ophrys insectifera (the wasp orchid), resulting in a unique combination of characteristics from both parents. The plant is notable for its intricate and deceptive floral morphology, which closely resembles female bees or wasps to attract male pollinators. Its flowers are typically yellow with brown or black markings, and it thrives in moist, calcareous soils across Europe.

This orchid is an important example of mimicry in the plant kingdom and plays a significant role in its native ecosystems.
