Odontoglossum phyllochilum

Odontoglossum phyllochilum, commonly known as the silver-veined orchid, is a tropical orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a silvery or pale green sheen. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, with a unique, spurred lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Odontoglossum phyllochilum is a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts due to its ornamental value and relatively manageable care requirements.
