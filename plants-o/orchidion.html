Orchidion

Orchidion, commonly known as the orchid ion, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its intricate, often showy flowers with three sepals and three petals, one of which is typically modified into a lip. The plants are epiphytic, meaning they grow on other plants without being parasitic, and they thrive in humid, tropical environments. Orchidion species are known for their diverse colors and shapes, making them popular in horticulture.

Their growth habits and floral structures are highly adapted to specific pollinators, enhancing their reproductive success.
