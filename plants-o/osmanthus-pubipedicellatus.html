Osmanthus pubipedicellatus

Osmanthus pubipedicellatus, commonly known as the hairy-stalked osmanthus, is a species of flowering plant in the Oleaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly China, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often emitting a sweet, honey-like scent. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and typically have a rough texture due to the presence of fine hairs.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aromatic properties.
