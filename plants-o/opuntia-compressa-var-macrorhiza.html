Opuntia compressa var. macrorhiza

Opuntia compressa var. macrorhiza, commonly known as the large-flowered prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flattened, fleshy stems called cladodes, which are covered in spines and are adapted for water storage. The plant produces large, showy yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit, a bright red or yellow berry, contains numerous seeds and is edible.

Opuntia compressa var. macrorhiza is well adapted to arid environments and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and habitat for various animals.
