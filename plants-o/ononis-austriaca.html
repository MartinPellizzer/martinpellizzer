Ononis austriaca

Ononis austriaca, commonly known as Austrian sandwort, is a low-growing perennial herb native to central and eastern Europe.

It typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, and the plant is known for its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.

Ononis austriaca is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
