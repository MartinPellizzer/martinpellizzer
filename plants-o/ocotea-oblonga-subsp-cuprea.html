Ocotea oblonga subsp. cuprea

Ocotea oblonga subsp.

cuprea, commonly known as the red ocotea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family. It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in countries like Brazil and Colombia.

The plant is characterized by its aromatic, dark red or brown bark, which is often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy surface, while its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences.

The fruit is a woody drupe, containing a single seed, and the plant is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
