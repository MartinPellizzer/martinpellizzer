Ocotea cupularis

Ocotea cupularis, commonly known as the cupularis ocotea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in countries like Brazil, Colombia, and Peru. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are typically elliptical and have a glossy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in axillary inflorescences, and it produces small, dark berries as fruit.

Ocotea cupularis is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional crafts and as a source of essential oils.
