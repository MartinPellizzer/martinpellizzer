Omphalodes nana var. chamissonis

Omphalodes nana var. chamissonis, commonly known as dwarf white campion, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the coastal regions of western North America.

It features small, clustered white flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to August. The plant has hairy, slender stems and dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal dunes and maritime scrub habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to harsh, salty environments.
