Oxynepeta heliotropifolia

Oxynepeta heliotropifolia, commonly known as the sunflower betony, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
