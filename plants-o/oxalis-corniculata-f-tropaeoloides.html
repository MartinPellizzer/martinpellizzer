Oxalis corniculata f. tropaeoloides

Oxalis corniculata f. tropaeoloides, commonly known as the "false nasturtium," is a flowering plant belonging to the Oxalidaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant yellow flowers that resemble those of the nasturtium. The plant is native to Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments. It produces small, edible fruits that contain seeds, which can be dispersed by wind or animals.

This variant of Oxalis corniculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
