Ophrys vernixia subsp. orientalis

Ophrys vernixia subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as the Eastern Bee Orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to parts of southern Europe and the Middle East. It is characterized by its unique floral morphology, featuring a bee-like appearance that mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male pollinators.

The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its flowers are usually pale green to brown in color, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination through mimicry.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species in many regions.
