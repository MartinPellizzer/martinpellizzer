Oxalis stokoei

Oxalis stokoei, commonly known as the Stokoe's wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive reddish veins and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant belongs to the Oxalidaceae family, known for its calcium oxalate crystals in the leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Oxalis stokoei is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
