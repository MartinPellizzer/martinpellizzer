Oubanguia africana var. denticulata

Oubanguia africana var. denticulata, commonly known as the African wild fig, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rainforest or savanna edge habitats.

The plant is valued for its fruit, which is used in traditional medicine and as a food source.
