Ophrys passionis subsp. pseudoatrata

Ophrys passionis subsp.

pseudoatrata, commonly known as the Passion Vulture's Eye, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to specific regions of southern Europe. It is characterized by its unique floral morphology, featuring a complex, lip-like structure that mimics the appearance of a female insect to attract male pollinators.

The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, thriving in Mediterranean climates with mild, wet winters. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brown, with intricate patterns that aid in mimicry.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
