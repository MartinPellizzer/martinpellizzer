Oenothera repanda

Oenothera repanda, commonly known as the showy evening primrose, is a species of flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats like prairies and disturbed areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.
