Ortachne floridana

Ortachne floridana, commonly known as the Florida panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to saturated soil conditions. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Ortachne floridana plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and provides habitat for various small invertebrates.
