Oxalis ptychoclada var. trichocarpa

Oxalis ptychoclada var. trichocarpa, commonly known as the three-leaf sorrel, is a species of flowering plant in the family Oxalidaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each leaflet being heart-shaped with a distinctive white margin. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Native to South America, it thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
