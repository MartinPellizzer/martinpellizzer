Onagra elata

Onagra elata, commonly known as wild evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Onagraceae and is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Onagra elata is also known for its medicinal properties, with its seeds used in traditional herbal remedies.
