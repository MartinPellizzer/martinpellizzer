Olea kilimandscharica

Olea kilimandscharica, commonly known as the East African olive, is a small evergreen tree native to the highlands of eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, pale yellow flowers that appear in spring. The tree produces small, edible fruits that are used in traditional cuisine and have a mild, nutty flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to semi-arid climates.

Olea kilimandscharica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience to drought.
