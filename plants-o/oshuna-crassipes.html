Oshuna crassipes

Oshuna crassipes, commonly known as the broad-leaved water lily, is a species of aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that provide shade and habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant produces white, fragrant flowers that bloom above the water surface, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots anchor in the muddy substrate, while its stems float on the water's surface.

Oshuna crassipes plays a vital role in water purification and ecosystem balance in its native habitats.
