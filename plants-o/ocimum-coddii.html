Ocimum coddii

Ocimum coddii, commonly known as the Codd's basil, is a species of flowering plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Africa, particularly Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and fragrant foliage.

Ocimum coddii is also valued for its potential medicinal properties, though further research is needed to confirm its therapeutic uses.
