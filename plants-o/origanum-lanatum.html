Origanum lanatum

Origanum lanatum, commonly known as woolly oregano, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces aromatic essential oils in its leaves, which contribute to its distinctive scent and medicinal properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value and culinary uses make it a valued plant in both gardens and traditional herbal medicine.
