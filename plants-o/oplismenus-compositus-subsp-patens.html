Oplismenus compositus subsp. patens

Oplismenus compositus subsp.

patens, commonly known as the spreading dogtail grass, is a perennial grass species native to southeastern North America. It is characterized by its dense, spreading growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically reddish-brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
