Ochrosia cowleyi

Ochrosia cowleyi, commonly known as Cowley's poinsettia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble petals. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are often hidden beneath the vibrant bracts. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Ochrosia cowleyi is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique and eye-catching appearance.
