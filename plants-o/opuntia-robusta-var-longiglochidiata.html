Opuntia robusta var. longiglochidiata

Opuntia robusta var. longiglochidiata, commonly known as the long-spined prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that are covered in long, sharp spines called glochids. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tunas, is edible and has a sweet, juicy flavor.

This variety is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water to thrive.
