Ocotea gymnantha

Ocotea gymnantha, commonly known as the Brazilian cinnamon tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark reddish-brown bark that resembles cinnamon. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall and has glossy, elliptical leaves with a strong, spicy scent. Its flowers are small and yellowish-green, and it produces oval, woody fruits containing single seeds.

The plant is valued for its essential oils and has traditional uses in medicine and perfumery.
