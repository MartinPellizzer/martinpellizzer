Oncinotis gabonica

Oncinotis gabonica, commonly known as the African water plantain, is a species of aquatic plant native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes. The plant features long, narrow leaves that are often submerged, with small white flowers that bloom in the summer. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and colonize new areas.

Oncinotis gabonica is valued for its ability to improve water quality by absorbing nutrients and providing habitat for aquatic life.
