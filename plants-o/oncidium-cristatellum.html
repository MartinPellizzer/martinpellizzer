Oncidium cristatellum

Oncidium cristatellum, commonly known as the cristate orchid, is a tropical orchid species native to Central and South America.

It features striking, often fragrant flowers with a distinctive, wavy lip that sets it apart from other orchid species. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating.

Oncidium cristatellum is valued in horticulture for its vibrant colors and relatively easy care requirements.
