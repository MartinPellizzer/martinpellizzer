Olearia phlogopappa subsp. serrata

Olearia phlogopappa subsp.

serrata, commonly known as the serrated flaxflower, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, serrated leaf margins.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish to orange flowers that bloom in spring. It is typically found in rocky or coastal habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and conservation in its native range.
