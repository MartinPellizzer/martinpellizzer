Olearia virgata subsp. centralis

Olearia virgata subsp.

centralis, commonly known as the Central Olearia, is a shrub native to New Zealand. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and showy, daisy-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in coastal and subalpine regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowering period occurs in spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Olearia virgata subsp.

centralis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
