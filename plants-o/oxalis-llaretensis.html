Oxalis llaretensis

Oxalis llaretensis, commonly known as the Llaret wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Oxalidaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or woodland areas and prefers well-drained soil. It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

Oxalis llaretensis is also valued for its ability to thrive in shaded environments.
