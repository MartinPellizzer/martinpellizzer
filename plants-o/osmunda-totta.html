Osmunda totta

Osmunda totta, commonly known as the slender water fern, is a species of fern that belongs to the genus Osmunda within the family Osmundaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are typically less than 1 meter in length. The plant thrives in wet, marshy habitats and is often found in temperate regions. Its rhizomes are submerged or partially submerged, allowing it to absorb nutrients from the water.

Osmunda totta plays a role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various microorganisms and small aquatic life.
