Ocotea spixiana var. goyazensis

Ocotea spixiana var. goyazensis, commonly known as Brazilian cinnamon or goyazense cinnamon, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its bark and leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.

This variant is distinguished by its unique morphological features and chemical composition compared to other Ocotea species.
