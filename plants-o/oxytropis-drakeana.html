Oxytropis drakeana

Oxytropis drakeana, commonly known as Drake's locoweed, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as sagebrush steppe and rocky slopes. Its flowers are usually pale blue to purple, and it produces legume-like pods that contain seeds.

Oxytropis drakeana is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system for water absorption.
