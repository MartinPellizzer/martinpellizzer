Oonopsis argillacea

Oonopsis argillacea, commonly known as the clayroot, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or clay-rich soils and is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It is well adapted to dry conditions and can thrive in full sun.

Oonopsis argillacea is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
