Olax pseudoleioides

Olax pseudoleioides, commonly known as the African olive, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Olacaceae and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are yellow when ripe, containing a single seed. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
