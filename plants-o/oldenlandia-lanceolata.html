Oldenlandia lanceolata

Oldenlandia lanceolata, commonly known as the narrowleaf spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its flowers are often visited by insects, making it an important component of local pollination networks.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
