Oenothera idahoensis

Oenothera idahoensis, commonly known as Idaho evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats like sagebrush steppe and rocky soils. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and its flowers are borne on tall, slender stalks.

Oenothera idahoensis is a perennial species that plays a role in supporting local ecosystems and is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
