Ocotea canaliculata

Ocotea canaliculata, commonly known as the narrow-ribbed ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, aromatic bark and lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by dark blue, fleshy berries.

Ocotea canaliculata is often found in humid, lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
