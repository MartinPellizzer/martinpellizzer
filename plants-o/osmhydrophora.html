Osmhydrophora

Osmhydrophora, commonly known as the waterwheel plant, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its whorled arrangement of leaves that resemble the spokes of a wheel.

It is native to freshwater habitats in southern Africa, where it thrives in slow-moving or still water. The plant's leaves are typically green and blade-like, with a waxy surface that aids in reducing water loss. Osmhydrophora is notable for its ability to store water in its leaves, which helps it survive in environments with fluctuating water levels.

Its ecological role includes providing shelter for small aquatic organisms and contributing to water filtration in its habitat.
