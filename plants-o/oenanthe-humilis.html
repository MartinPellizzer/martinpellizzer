Oenanthe humilis

Oenanthe humilis, commonly known as the lesser water dropwort, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist or wet environments such as marshes, stream banks, and damp meadows. The plant has slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its small, greenish-white flowers are arranged in umbels and bloom in early summer.

Oenanthe humilis is characterized by its aromatic, slightly toxic sap, which contains compounds like cicutoine.
