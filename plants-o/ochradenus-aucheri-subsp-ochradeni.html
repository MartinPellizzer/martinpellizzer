Ochradenus aucheri subsp. ochradeni

Ochradenus aucheri subsp.

ochradeni, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountains of Armenia and Turkey. It is characterized by its blue to purple bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
