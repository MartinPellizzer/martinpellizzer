Opuntia crispicrinita

Opuntia crispicrinita, commonly known as the "Crisp-haired Prickly Pear," is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flattened, fleshy stems, known as cladodes, which are covered in clusters of small, hair-like spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, red-fleshed fruits. Its adaptations to arid environments include thick cuticles and reduced leaf surfaces to minimize water loss.

Opuntia crispicrinita is also notable for its role in traditional indigenous cultures and as a source of food and medicine.
