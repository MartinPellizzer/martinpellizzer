Oxytropis velutina

Oxytropis velutina, commonly known as velvet false flax, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous and contributes to soil stabilization, making it valuable in ecological restoration projects.

Oxytropis velutina is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in open grasslands and rocky slopes.
