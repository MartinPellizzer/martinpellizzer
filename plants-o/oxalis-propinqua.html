Oxalis propinqua

Oxalis propinqua, commonly known as the slender wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its foliage contains oxalic acid, which can be toxic if ingested in large quantities.

Oxalis propinqua is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in partial shade.
