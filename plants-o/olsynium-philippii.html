Olsynium philippii

Olsynium philippii, commonly known as the Philippi's lily, is a striking flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy white or pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky soils, thriving in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in alpine environments.

Olsynium philippii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
