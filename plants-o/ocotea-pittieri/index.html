Ocotea Pittieri

Ocotea pittieri is a rare and unique plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America. It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes well-known species like cinnamon and avocado. This plant is characterized by its striking, fragrant leaves and small, clustered flowers. Due to its limited distribution and habitat requirements, it is considered endangered. Conservation efforts are essential to protect this remarkable species from further decline.
