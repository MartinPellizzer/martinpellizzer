Ocimum selloi f. serratum

Ocimum selloi f. serratum, commonly known as the serrated holy basil, is a variant of the holy basil plant native to India.

It is characterized by its serrated leaf margins and aromatic, slightly spicy leaves. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is often cultivated for its medicinal and culinary uses. Its leaves contain essential oils rich in compounds like eugenol and linalool, which contribute to its therapeutic properties.

This variety is valued for its unique flavor and potential health benefits.
