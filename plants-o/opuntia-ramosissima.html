Opuntia ramosissima

Opuntia ramosissima, commonly known as the giant prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, flat, paddle-shaped stems that are covered in spines and glochids, which are small, hair-like structures. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months, followed by edible fruits known as tunas. Its succulent stems store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

Opuntia ramosissima plays an important role in its ecosystem, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
