Oreorchis foliosa var. indica

Oreorchis foliosa var. indica, commonly known as the Indian leafy orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Asia, including India and Nepal.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of elongated, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces a single, erect flowering stem bearing a few showy, greenish-yellow flowers with prominent lip petals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically in forest understories.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and subtle floral display.
