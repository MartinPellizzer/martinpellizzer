Oxalis psoraleoides subsp. psoraleoides

Oxalis psoraleoides subsp.

psoraleoides, commonly known as the purple oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, delicate flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is often adorned with a distinctive purple or reddish tinge, especially under certain light conditions.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
