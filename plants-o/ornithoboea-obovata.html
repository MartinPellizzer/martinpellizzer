Ornithoboea obovata

Ornithoboea obovata, commonly known as the bird's-beak balm, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that resemble a bird's beak, typically in shades of pink or purple. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and thrives in dry, rocky environments. It is often found in coastal regions and is adapted to withstand saline conditions.

Ornithoboea obovata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
