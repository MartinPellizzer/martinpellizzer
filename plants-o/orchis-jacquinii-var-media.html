Orchis jacquinii var. media

Orchis jacquinii var. media, commonly known as the narrow-leaved early orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or meadow habitats. The plant features a basal rosette of lanceolate leaves and produces tall, flowering stems with showy, violet-blue flowers in early spring. Its flowers are characterized by a prominent lip and are pollinated primarily by bees and flies.

This orchid is part of the Orchidaceae family and is noted for its ornamental value and ecological significance in its native range.
