Ophiopogon japonicus var. intermedius

Ophiopogon japonicus var. intermedius, commonly known as Japanese forest grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features dense, grass-like foliage with narrow, dark green leaves that form a dense mat. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in late summer, followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often used in ornamental landscaping.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for woodland gardens and as a ground cover.
