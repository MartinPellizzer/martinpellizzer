Orbignya brejinhoensis

Orbignya brejinhoensis, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a member of the Palmae family and is native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is a tall, slender tree that can grow up to 30 meters in height, with a dense canopy and large, pinnate leaves. Its most notable feature is the production of large, hard-shelled nuts containing edible seeds, which are highly valued for their nutritional content.
