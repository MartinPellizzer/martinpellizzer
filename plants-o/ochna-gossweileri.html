Ochna gossweileri

Ochna gossweileri, commonly known as the yellow ochna, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey-barked trunk and opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by orange-red berries containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Ochna gossweileri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
