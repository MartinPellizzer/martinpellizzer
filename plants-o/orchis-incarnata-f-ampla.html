Orchis incarnata f. ampla

Orchis incarnata f. ampla, commonly known as the showy orchis, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to North America.

This perennial herb features tall, upright stems that bear numerous showy, violet-blue flowers arranged in a loose spike. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and can reach up to 15 centimeters in length. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological significance in native ecosystems.
