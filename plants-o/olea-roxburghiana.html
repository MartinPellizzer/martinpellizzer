Olea roxburghiana

Olea roxburghiana, commonly known as Indian olive, is a small evergreen tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by small, round fruits that are typically yellow when ripe. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.

Olea roxburghiana is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and prefers well-drained soil.
