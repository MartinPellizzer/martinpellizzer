Oxalis griffithii subsp. wulingensis

Oxalis griffithii subsp.

wulingensis, commonly known as the Wuling wood sorrel, is a perennial herb native to China. It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for shaded garden settings.
