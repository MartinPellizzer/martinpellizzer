Onobrychis daghestanica

Onobrychis daghestanica, commonly known as the Daghestan clover, is a perennial leguminous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in local flora.
