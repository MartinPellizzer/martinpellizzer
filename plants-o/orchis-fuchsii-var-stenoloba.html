Orchis fuchsii var. stenoloba

Orchis fuchsii var. stenoloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaf early purple orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and striking violet-purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist woodlands and is often found in calcareous soils. Its flowers are adorned with intricate patterns and are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in many regions.
