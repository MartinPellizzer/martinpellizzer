Onobrychis arenaria subsp. cana

Onobrychis arenaria subsp.

cana, commonly known as the sand sainfoin, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and small, yellowish-white flowers that appear in racemes.

The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or rocky habitats.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it valuable for soil enrichment and as a forage plant.
