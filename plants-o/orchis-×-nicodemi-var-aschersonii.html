Orchis × nicodemi var. aschersonii

Orchis × nicodemi var. aschersonii, commonly known as the Ascherson's hybrid orchid, is a natural hybrid species resulting from the cross between Orchis morio and Orchis × nicodemi.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet to purple flowers with a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in temperate climates. Its floral structure includes a well-developed column and a spurred lip, typical of the Orchidaceae family.

This varietal form is notable for its ornamental value and is often found in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
