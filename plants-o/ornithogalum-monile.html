Ornithogalum monile

Ornithogalum monile, commonly known as the chain lily, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of linear, grass-like leaves and produces a tall, unbranched stem with clusters of white or pale green, bell-shaped flowers. The plant is characterized by its unique inflorescence, which resembles a chain of small bulbs, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Ornithogalum monile is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
