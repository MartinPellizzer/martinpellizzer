Oplexion ferox

Oplexion ferox, commonly known as the fierce oplexion, is a rare and striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of bright red, flame-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are narrow and covered in a dense layer of fine, silvery hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid habitat. Its roots are deep and fibrous, allowing it to access moisture from underground water sources.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research.
