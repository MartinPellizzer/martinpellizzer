Oncidium pentadactylon

Oncidium pentadactylon, commonly known as the Orchid of the Andes, is a tropical orchid native to South America.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange with contrasting purple or red markings. The plant has pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, supporting its growth in seasonal environments. Its roots are epiphytic, allowing it to grow on trees or rocks in humid, shaded areas.

Oncidium pentadactylon is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
