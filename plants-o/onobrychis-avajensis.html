Onobrychis avajensis

Onobrychis avajensis, commonly known as the Persian velvetvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow pea-like flowers, which bloom in early summer. Its small, pod-like fruits contain several seeds, and the plant is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Onobrychis avajensis is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
