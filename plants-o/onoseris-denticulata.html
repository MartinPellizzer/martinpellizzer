Onoseris denticulata

Onoseris denticulata, commonly known as the toothed yellowdisk, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, contributing to its common name.

Onoseris denticulata is an important plant in its native ecosystem, supporting various pollinators and wildlife.
