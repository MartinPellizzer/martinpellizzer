Oxalis pratensis

Oxalis pratensis, commonly known as the wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features trifoliate leaves, each leaflet being heart-shaped with a distinctive leaf stalk. Its flowers are usually yellow with five petals and are often clustered in small umbels.

Oxalis pratensis is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
