Olea europaea subsp. laperrinei

Olea europaea subsp.

laperrinei, commonly known as the Corsican olive, is a subspecies native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Corsica and Sardinia. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its lanceolate, leathery leaves and fragrant, small flowers.

The plant produces small, oval-shaped olives that are typically harvested for culinary and oil production. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates with hot, dry summers and mild, wet winters.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in traditional agriculture.
