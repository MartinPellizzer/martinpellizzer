Oxalis fluminensis f. vestita

Oxalis fluminensis f. vestita, commonly known as the white wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and riverbanks. It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The subspecies 'vestita' is distinguished by its white flowers and hairy stems.
