Oreobolus obtusangulus subsp. rubrovaginatus

Oreobolus obtusangulus subsp.

rubrovaginatus, commonly known as the red-stemmed burrgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia. It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and its ability to thrive in saline and alkaline soils.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Its leaves are typically hairy and have a bluish-green hue, while the stems are reddish in color, giving it its common name.

This subspecies is well adapted to arid and coastal environments, making it a resilient component of its ecosystem.
