Oryza brachyantha

Oryza brachyantha, commonly known as the short-stemmed ricegrass, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Poaceae family and is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit. The plant typically produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences with short, narrow lemmas. Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in studies related to grass evolution and genetic diversity.

Oryza brachyantha is also used in ecological research due to its adaptability to various soil conditions.
