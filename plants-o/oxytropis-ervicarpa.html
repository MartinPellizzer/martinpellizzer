Oxytropis ervicarpa

Oxytropis ervicarpa, commonly known as slender milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale yellow to violet. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and steppe environments.

Oxytropis ervicarpa plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
