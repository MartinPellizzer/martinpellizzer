Oenothera auricula var. bottae

Oenothera auricula var. bottae, commonly known as the golden-leaf evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
