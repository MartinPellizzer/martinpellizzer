Ophrys lutea subsp. battandieri

Ophrys lutea subsp.

battandieri, commonly known as the yellow bee orchid, is a species of orchid native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that closely resemble bees, a feature that plays a key role in its pollination strategy.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, greenish-brown seed pods after flowering.

This orchid is known for its specialized relationship with specific pollinators, enhancing its ecological significance.
