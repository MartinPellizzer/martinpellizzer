Ornithogalum diphyllum

Ornithogalum diphyllum, commonly known as the two-leaved star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces umbels of white or pale yellow flowers with six petals. The plant is characterized by its two distinct leaf forms: a basal pair and a terminal pair, giving it its species name "diphyllum.". It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Ornithogalum diphyllum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
