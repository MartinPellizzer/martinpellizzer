Ophrys paolina

Ophrys paolina, commonly known as the Paolina bee orchid, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which closely resembles a female bee, a mimicry strategy used to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its flowers are usually pale green or brown, with intricate patterns that enhance the mimicry effect.

Ophrys paolina is a rare and protected species, often found in specific habitats that are sensitive to environmental changes.
