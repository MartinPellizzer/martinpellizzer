Ornithogalum flavum

Ornithogalum flavum, commonly known as the yellow star of Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, linear leaves and produces umbels of bright yellow flowers with six petals. The plant is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its flowering period typically occurs in late winter to early spring.

Ornithogalum flavum is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
