Oplonia jujuyensis

Oplonia jujuyensis, commonly known as the Jujuy palm, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or green in color. Oplonia jujuyensis is adapted to grow in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited use in traditional medicine.
