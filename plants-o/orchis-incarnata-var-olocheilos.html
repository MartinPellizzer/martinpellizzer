Orchis incarnata var. olocheilos

Orchis incarnata var. olocheilos, commonly known as the pink fairy orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption.

This variety is noted for its delicate appearance and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
