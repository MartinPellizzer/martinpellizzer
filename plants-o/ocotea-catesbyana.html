Ocotea catesbyana

Ocotea catesbyana, commonly known as the Florida ocotea, is a small evergreen tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruits are small, dark berries that contain aromatic seeds.

Ocotea catesbyana is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as hammocks and along stream banks.
