Onobrychis buhseana

Onobrychis buhseana, commonly known as Buhse's vetch, is a perennial legume native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a deep root system, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils and arid conditions. It is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, contributing to soil stabilization.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects.
