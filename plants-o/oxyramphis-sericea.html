Oxyramphis sericea

Oxyramphis sericea, commonly known as the silky oxyramphis, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, thread-like leaves that give it a silky appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale green or white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in humid tropical forests.

Due to its unique foliage and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
