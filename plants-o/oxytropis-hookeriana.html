Oxytropis hookeriana

Oxytropis hookeriana, commonly known as Hooker's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Oxytropis hookeriana is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.
