Ochna patens

Ochna patens, commonly known as the yellow ochna, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and alternate, simple, elliptic leaves that are often yellowish-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by bright orange, fleshy fruits. Ochna patens thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
