Oxalis schlechteri

Oxalis schlechteri, commonly known as the Schlechter's wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces bright orange berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. Oxalis schlechteri thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
