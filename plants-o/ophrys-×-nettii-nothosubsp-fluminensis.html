Ophrys × nettii nothosubsp. fluminensis

Ophrys × nettii nothosubsp.

fluminensis, commonly known as the river orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to South America. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, bee-like flowers that mimic the appearance of female bees to facilitate pollination.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as riverbanks and forest floors. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, and the flowers are usually a mix of yellow and brown with intricate patterns.

This nothosubspecies is notable for its specialized pollination mechanism and its role in the ecological diversity of its habitat.
