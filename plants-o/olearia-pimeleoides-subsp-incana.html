Olearia pimeleoides subsp. incana

Olearia pimeleoides subsp.

incana, commonly known as the silver felt tree, is a shrub native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its silver-gray, felty leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring. It thrives in coastal and subalpine environments, often growing in well-drained soils.

The subspecies incana is distinguished by its more densely hairy foliage compared to other subspecies of Olearia pimeleoides.
