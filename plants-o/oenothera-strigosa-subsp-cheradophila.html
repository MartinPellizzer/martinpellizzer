Oenothera strigosa subsp. cheradophila

Oenothera strigosa subsp.

cheradophila, commonly known as the narrowleaf evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
