Otholobium pungens

Otholobium pungens, commonly known as the spiked burr bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive spiny pods. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by hairy, woody pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Otholobium pungens is used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its role in soil stabilization and erosion control.
