Odontonema dissitiflorum

Odontonema dissitiflorum, commonly known as the velvetleaf strobilanthes, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that resemble clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with a fine, velvety pubescence.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
