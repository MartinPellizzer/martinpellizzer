Oryza sativa var. nguerzeana

Oryza sativa var. nguerzeana, commonly known as the Nguerzeana rice, is a traditional rice variety native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its short, slender grains and is typically grown in lowland rice fields with flooded soil conditions. The plant exhibits a compact growth habit, with a tall stem that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. Its leaves are broad and green, and it produces panicles that bear small, white flowers.

This variety is valued for its adaptability to local climates and its role in traditional agricultural practices.
