Ourouparia enormis

Ourouparia enormis, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native habitat.

The flowers bloom in a variety of colors, including yellow, orange, and red, making it a visually impressive species.
