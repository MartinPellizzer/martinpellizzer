Osmorhiza aristata var. longistylis

Osmorhiza aristata var. longistylis, commonly known as the long-styled sweetroot, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbellate inflorescences and aromatic foliage. The plant features a thick, fleshy rhizome and has finely divided, fern-like leaves. Its flowers are typically white or pale green, with the distinctive long stamen that gives the variety its name.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
