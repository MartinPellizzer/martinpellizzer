Oxalis compacta subsp. berteroana

Oxalis compacta subsp.

berteroana, commonly known as the dwarf wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to South America. It features small, heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins, and its flowers are typically yellow with five petals.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate drought conditions. Oxalis compacta subsp.

berteroana is also known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, forming dense mats in suitable environments.
