Oenothera tetragona var. longistipata

Oenothera tetragona var. longistipata, commonly known as the long-styled four-angle evening primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features showy yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has four angular stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers have long, slender stigmas, which are characteristic of the varietal name "longistipata.".

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in arid and semi-arid environments.
