Opuntia phaeacantha var. major

Opuntia phaeacantha var. major, commonly known as the giant prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, flat, paddle-shaped stems that can grow up to 2 meters in length. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tuna, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

The plant is well adapted to arid environments and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
