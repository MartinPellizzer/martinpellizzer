Olofuton

Olofuton, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as a compact shrub with a rosette of leaves that store water, adapting to its native arid environment. Its flowers come in a variety of colors, including purple, pink, white, and blue, and bloom throughout the year under proper care.

Olofuton is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
