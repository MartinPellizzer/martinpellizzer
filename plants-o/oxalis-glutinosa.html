Oxalis glutinosa

Oxalis glutinosa, commonly known as sticky wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces a sticky substance on its leaves, which gives it its common name. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Oxalis glutinosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
