Opuntia fulgens

Opuntia fulgens, commonly known as the flame cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems, which are typically green with red or orange spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow to orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its stems are used for photosynthesis and water storage, typical of cacti in arid environments.

Opuntia fulgens is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and withstand extreme temperatures.
