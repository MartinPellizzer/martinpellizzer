Oxalis adenocaulos

Oxalis adenocaulos, commonly known as the fairy rose or velvetleaf oxalis, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a small, herbaceous plant characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky outcrops. Its leaves are often covered in a fine, velvety texture, contributing to its common name.

Oxalis adenocaulos is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in cooler climates.
