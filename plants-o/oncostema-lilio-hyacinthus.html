Oncostema lilio-hyacinthus

Oncostema lilio-hyacinthus, commonly known as the hyacinth bean, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features palmate leaves with three to five leaflets, which are typically lobed and have a velvety texture. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that resemble hyacinths, with clusters of small, fragrant blossoms in shades of purple, pink, or white. Its fruit is a dry, three-lobed capsule that contains several seeds.

Oncostema lilio-hyacinthus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
