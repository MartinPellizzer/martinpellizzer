Oxygonum dregeanum var. linearifolium

Oxygonum dregeanum var. linearifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved marshmallow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features erect, hairy stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, often branching towards the upper part. The leaves are linear to lanceolate, with a soft, velvety texture and a bluish-green hue. It produces clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This plant thrives in wet, marshy environments and is often found in swampy or seasonally flooded areas.
