Oxytropis besseyi

Oxytropis besseyi, commonly known as the Bessey's locoweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in grasslands and sagebrush steppe.

Oxytropis besseyi has a deep root system that allows it to survive in poor soils and withstand drought conditions.
