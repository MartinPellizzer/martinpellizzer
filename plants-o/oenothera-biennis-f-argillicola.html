Oenothera biennis f. argillicola

Oenothera biennis f. argillicola, commonly known as the claybank evening primrose, is a variant of the common evening primrose.

It is characterized by its robust growth and adaptation to clay-rich soils. The plant produces showy yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening. It has hairy stems and leaves that are typically broader than those of the typical form.

This subspecies is often found in disturbed or moist soils along riverbanks and roadsides.
