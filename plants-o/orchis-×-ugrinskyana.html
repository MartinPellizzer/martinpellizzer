Orchis × ugrinskyana

Orchis × ugrinskyana, commonly known as the Ugrinsky hybrid orchid, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Orchis moria and Orchis ugrinskyana.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect flowering stems that bear clusters of showy, violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in forested areas, thriving in temperate climates. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, and it is often found in the regions of Eastern Europe and Western Siberia.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a protected species in some areas.
