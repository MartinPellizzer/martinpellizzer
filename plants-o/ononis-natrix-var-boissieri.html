Ononis natrix var. boissieri

Ononis natrix var. boissieri, commonly known as the Boissier's bedstraw, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is a low-growing perennial species characterized by slender, creeping stems and small, white or pale pink flowers. The leaves are typically opposite, linear, and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia, it thrives in rocky and sandy soils.

This variant is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and along riverbanks.
