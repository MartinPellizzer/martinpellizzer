Ornithopus × bardiei

Ornithopus × bardiei, commonly known as the hybrid sea holly, is a perennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flower heads, which are surrounded by narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens and coastal landscapes.
