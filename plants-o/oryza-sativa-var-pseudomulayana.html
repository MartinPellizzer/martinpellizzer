Oryza sativa var. pseudomulayana

Oryza sativa var. pseudomulayana, commonly known as the wild rice of the Philippines, is a grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, spikelet-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers and has narrow, linear leaves. It is often found growing in wetland habitats such as rice paddies and marshes.

This variety is of interest to botanists and agricultural researchers due to its genetic diversity and potential for crop improvement.
