Osmanthus mexicanus

Osmanthus mexicanus, commonly known as Mexican osmanthus, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its delicate blossoms. Osmanthus mexicanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its pleasant fragrance.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable choice for temperate climates.
