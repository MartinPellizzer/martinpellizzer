Onobrychis viciifolia f. subvillosa

Onobrychis viciifolia f. subvillosa, commonly known as the silky vetch, is a perennial legume characterized by its finely hairy stems and leaves.

It features trifoliate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant is native to southern Europe and thrives in well-drained soils, often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. It has a deep root system that helps in soil stabilization and nitrogen fixation.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
