Oldenlandia crystallina

Oldenlandia crystallina, commonly known as the crystal leaf or glassy vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, translucent or waxy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warm season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular ornamental plant in botanical gardens.
