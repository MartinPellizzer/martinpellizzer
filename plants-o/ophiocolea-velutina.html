Ophiocolea velutina

Ophiocolea velutina, commonly known as the velvetleaf orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces striking, showy flowers with intricate patterns and a strong, sweet fragrance. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and marshy areas.

Ophiocolea velutina is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
