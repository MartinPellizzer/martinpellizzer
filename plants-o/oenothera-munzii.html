Oenothera munzii

Oenothera munzii, commonly known as the Munz's evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed habitats like roadsides and dunes.

Oenothera munzii is also noted for its ecological importance in supporting local insect populations.
