Olearia stellulata var. rugosa

Olearia stellulata var. rugosa, commonly known as the rough-leaved daisy bush, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are typically hairy and have a rough texture, giving the plant its varietal name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine environments.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native pollinators.
