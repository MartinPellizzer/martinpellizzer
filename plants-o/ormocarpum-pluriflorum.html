Ormocarpum pluriflorum

Ormocarpum pluriflorum, commonly known as the many-flowered orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often display vibrant colors. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial herb, with lance-shaped leaves and a compact growth habit. Its inflorescence consists of multiple racemes bearing small, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant in suitable climates.
