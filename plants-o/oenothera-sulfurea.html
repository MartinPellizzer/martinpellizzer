Oenothera sulfurea

Oenothera sulfurea, commonly known as the sulphur evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds. It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Oenothera sulfurea is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in supporting pollinator populations.
