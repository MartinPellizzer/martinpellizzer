Oenothera linearis var. angustifolia

Oenothera linearis var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and yellow flowers that open in the evening. The plant is part of the Onagraceae family and is known for its showy, tubular flowers that attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
