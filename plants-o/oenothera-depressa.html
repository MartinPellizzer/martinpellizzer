Oenothera depressa

Oenothera depressa, commonly known as the plains evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy yellow or orange flowers that open in the evening. Its flowers have four petals and are pollinated primarily by moths.

The plant is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairie and steppe habitats.
