Ornithogalum pyramidale

Ornithogalum pyramidale, commonly known as the pyramid bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a rosette of narrow, sword-shaped leaves and produces tall, branched stems bearing bell-shaped flowers in white or pale yellow. The plant is characterized by its showy, clustered inflorescences that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Ornithogalum pyramidale is also known for its toxic properties, containing calcium oxalate crystals that can cause irritation if ingested.
