Olea glandulifera

Olea glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular olive, is a species of flowering plant in the Oleaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that contain a single seed. Olea glandulifera is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in dry, rocky soils.

It is also valued for its historical and medicinal uses in traditional herbal remedies.
