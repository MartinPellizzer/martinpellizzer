Oxytropis qilianshanica

Oxytropis qilianshanica, commonly known as the Qilian mountain locoweed, is a perennial herb native to the Qinghai-Tibet Plateau.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems and small, compound leaves. The plant produces yellow or pale purple flowers in late summer, which are clustered in racemes. It thrives in high-altitude, arid environments and is adapted to cold and dry conditions.

Oxytropis qilianshanica plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
