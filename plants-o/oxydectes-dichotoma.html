Oxydectes dichotoma

Oxydectes dichotoma, commonly known as the dichotomous oxeye, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive branching pattern, with stems splitting into two at each node, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies.
