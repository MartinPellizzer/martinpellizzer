Oxalis arthrophylla

Oxalis arthrophylla, commonly known as the pink false shamrock, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are often pink or red in color, and its small, delicate flowers that bloom in spring. The plant belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Oxalis arthrophylla is also noted for its ability to thrive in cooler climates and its seasonal blooming pattern.
