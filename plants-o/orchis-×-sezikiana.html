Orchis × sezikiana

Orchis × sezikiana, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Orchis morio and Orchis anthropophora.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family, characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and meadows across Europe. Its flowers have a distinctive spurred lip and are often pollinated by bees and other insects.

The hybrid nature of Orchis × sezikiana makes it a subject of interest in both botanical research and horticulture.
