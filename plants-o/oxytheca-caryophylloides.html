Oxytheca caryophylloides

Oxytheca caryophylloides, commonly known as the woolly sunflower, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, silver-green foliage and showy yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a compact growth habit and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid environments and resistant to drought.
