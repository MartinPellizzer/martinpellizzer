Oryzopsis paradoxa

Oryzopsis paradoxa, commonly known as the paradox grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and spikelet-based inflorescence, which consists of small, two-rowed clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often in rocky soils or along stream banks. Its botanical features include narrow, linear leaves and a fibrous root system that helps it survive in dry conditions.

Oryzopsis paradoxa is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its adaptability and role in local ecosystems.
