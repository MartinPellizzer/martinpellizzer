Ocotea pausiaca

Ocotea pausiaca, commonly known as the Pausiaca Ocotea, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocado, and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by dark blue or black berries.

Ocotea pausiaca is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional crafts and as a source of essential oils.
