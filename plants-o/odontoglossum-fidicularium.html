Odontoglossum fidicularium

Odontoglossum fidicularium, commonly known as the Ecuadorian orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces intricate, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with a distinctive lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Odontoglossum fidicularium is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
