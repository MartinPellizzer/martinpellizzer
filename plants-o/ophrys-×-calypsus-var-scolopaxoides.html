Ophrys × calypsus var. scolopaxoides

Ophrys × calypsus var. scolopaxoides, commonly known as the scolopaxoides orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral morphology, including a labellum that mimics the appearance of an insect. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded habitats. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish in color, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by bees.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
