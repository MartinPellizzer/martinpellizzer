Ononis adenotricha var. krausei

Ononis adenotricha var. krausei, commonly known as the Krause's sandspurry, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems that bear small, clustered leaves with a woolly texture. The flowers are typically yellow or orange, with five petals and a distinctive central tube, blooming in spring. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to drought conditions.

This variant is closely related to other species in the Ononis genus, sharing similar morphological features.
