Oenothera albicaulis var. decumbens

Oenothera albicaulis var. decumbens, commonly known as the decumbent evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing, sprawling habit with hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its flowers are followed by capsule fruits that release seeds with silky filaments.

This variety is often found in dry, open habitats like grasslands and rocky slopes.
