Oreophyton falcatum var. depauperatum

Oreophyton falcatum var. depauperatum, commonly known as the barren leafy clubmoss, is a fossilized plant from the Early Devonian period.

It is characterized by its simple, branching structure and small, scale-like leaves. This plant is considered one of the earliest land plants with true roots, stems, and leaves. Its morphology suggests it was a primitive vascular plant, contributing to the early development of terrestrial ecosystems.

The varietal name 'depauperatum' refers to its reduced or sparse leaf structure compared to the typical form of Oreophyton.
