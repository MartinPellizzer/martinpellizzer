Ozothamnus costatifructus

Ozothamnus costatifructus, commonly known as the coast goldenbush, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage with gray-green leaves that have a distinctive white-veined appearance. The plant produces small, yellow-orange flowers in terminal clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with mild winters and warm, dry summers.

Ozothamnus costatifructus is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
