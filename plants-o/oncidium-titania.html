Oncidium titania

Oncidium titania, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow and green foliage and its long, pendulous inflorescences that bear numerous fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its flowers bloom in clusters, often displaying a mix of yellow, green, and white hues.

Oncidium titania requires bright, indirect light and consistent humidity to thrive, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
