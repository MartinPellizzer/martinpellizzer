Oxalis chamaecrista

Oxalis chamaecrista, commonly known as the yellow wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive yellowish-green color and small, five-petaled flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Oxalis chamaecrista is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable environments.
