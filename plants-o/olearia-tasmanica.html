Olearia tasmanica

Olearia tasmanica, commonly known as the Tasmanian daisy, is a flowering plant native to Tasmania.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as heathlands and grassy slopes. Its leaves are narrow and grey-green, often covered in fine hairs.

Olearia tasmanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
