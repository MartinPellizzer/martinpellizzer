Oxytropis bracteolata

Oxytropis bracteolata, commonly known as the bracteolate milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of western North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

Oxytropis bracteolata plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
