Oxalis polyphylla var. heptaphylla

Oxalis polyphylla var. heptaphylla, commonly known as the seven-leaf wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are divided into three leaflets, and its clusters of small, yellow, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and stems that are often hairy.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
