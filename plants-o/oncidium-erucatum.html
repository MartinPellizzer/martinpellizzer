Oncidium erucatum

Oncidium erucatum, commonly known as the yellow lady's slipper orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant with a sympodial growth habit, characterized by its rhizomatous structure. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange with a distinctive pouch-like lip. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Oncidium erucatum thrives in humid, shady environments and is often cultivated for its beauty and unique floral structure.
