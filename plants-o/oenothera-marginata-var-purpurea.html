Oenothera marginata var. purpurea

Oenothera marginata var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep purple, typically opening in the evening. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a silvery underside, and it produces a distinctive, fragrant scent at night to attract pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in arid or semi-arid environments.

The species is known for its ability to self-seed and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
