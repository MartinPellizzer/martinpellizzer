Oncidium kappleri

Oncidium kappleri, commonly known as the Kappler's orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in clusters of yellow, orange, and red hues. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are strap-like, narrow, and often have a waxy coating.

Oncidium kappleri prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
