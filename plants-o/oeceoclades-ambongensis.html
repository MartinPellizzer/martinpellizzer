Oeceoclades ambongensis

Oeceoclades ambongensis, commonly known as the Ambongensis orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, lance-shaped leaves and intricate floral structure. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its flowers, which are usually pale green to yellowish in color, are adorned with intricate patterns and are highly attractive to pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
