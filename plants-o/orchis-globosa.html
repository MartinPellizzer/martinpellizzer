Orchis globosa

Orchis globosa, commonly known as the globe orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the United Kingdom.

It is characterized by its distinctive, globular flower spike that bears several showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in damp, shaded woodlands and requires specific microclimatic conditions to thrive. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, and it has a short, underground rhizome.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a protected species in the UK.
