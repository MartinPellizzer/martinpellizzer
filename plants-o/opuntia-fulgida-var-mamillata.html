Opuntia fulgida var. mamillata

Opuntia fulgida var. mamillata, commonly known as the golden prickly pear, is a cacti species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in sharp spines and small, glochids. The plant produces vibrant yellow to orange flowers during the summer months. Its fruits, called tunas, are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor.

This variety is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water to thrive.
