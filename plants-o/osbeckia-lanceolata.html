Osbeckia lanceolata

Osbeckia lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf osbeckia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in terminal spikes, and it produces small, dry fruits called capsules.

Osbeckia lanceolata is known for its ecological value, providing food for various insects and wildlife.
