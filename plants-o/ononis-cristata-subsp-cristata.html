Ononis cristata subsp. cristata

Ononis cristata subsp.

cristata, commonly known as the crested eryngo, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy, and often branched stems that bear small, yellowish-white flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and slightly lobed, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
