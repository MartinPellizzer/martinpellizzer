Ophrys × innominata

Ophrys × innominata, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant belonging to the Ophrys genus in the Orchidaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Ophrys apifera (the bee orchid) and Ophrys insectifera (the wasp orchid), resulting in a unique combination of traits from both parents. The plant is characterized by its striking, bee-like floral morphology, which includes a vivid yellow and brownish-green lip that mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. It thrives in temperate climates, typically in open woodland or grassland habitats where it can receive sufficient sunlight.

Due to its hybrid nature, Ophrys × innominata is often found in regions where the parent species overlap geographically, making it a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
