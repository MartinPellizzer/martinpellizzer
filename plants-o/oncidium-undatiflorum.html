Oncidium undatiflorum

Oncidium undatiflorum, commonly known as the Orchid with Wavy Flowers, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a tropical orchid characterized by its pendulous, showy inflorescences that feature wavy-edged petals and sepals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are often fragrant and can last for several weeks, making it a popular choice for ornamental cultivation.

Oncidium undatiflorum requires well-draining soil and consistent moisture to thrive.
