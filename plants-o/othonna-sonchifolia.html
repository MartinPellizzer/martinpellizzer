Othonna sonchifolia

Othonna sonchifolia, commonly known as the blue oat sedge, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-gray hue, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Othonna sonchifolia is valued in gardens for its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
