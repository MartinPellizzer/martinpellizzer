Orania macrocladus

Orania macrocladus, commonly known as the giant leaf cactus, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and large, paddle-shaped leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual element to its appearance. Its structure allows it to grow in a shrub-like form, often reaching heights of up to two meters.

Orania macrocladus is well adapted to harsh climates, making it a resilient and interesting subject for botanical study.
