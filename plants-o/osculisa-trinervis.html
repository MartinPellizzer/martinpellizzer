Osculisa trinervis

Osculisa trinervis, commonly known as the three-veined fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are typically light green in color. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are divided into three main veins, giving it the common name "three-veined fern.".

Osculisa trinervis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
