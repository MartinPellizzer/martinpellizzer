Orchis × franchetii

Orchis × franchetii, commonly known as the Franchet's orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Orchis moria and Orchis pallida.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, particularly found in moist meadows and woodland edges. The plant produces tall, erect stems that bear showy, violet to purple flowers with a distinctive three-lobed lip. Its flowers are typically arranged in a loose spike and are pollinated by insects, particularly bees.

The plant has a fibrous root system and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
