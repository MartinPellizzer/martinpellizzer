Ophiocaulon rowlandii

Ophiocaulon rowlandii, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a species of epiphytic cactus native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom around the holiday season, hence its common name. The plant has segmented, leaf-like stems that are often used for propagation. Its succulent leaves store water, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

It prefers well-draining soil and indirect sunlight, making it a popular houseplant.
