Opuntia crystalenia

Opuntia crystalenia, commonly known as the crystalline prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems that are covered in spines and are often ribbed. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers, which are typically followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to survive in dry conditions.

Opuntia crystalenia is also noted for its ability to store water in its thick stems, making it well-suited for desert environments.
