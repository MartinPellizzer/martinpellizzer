Opuntia echios var. typica

Opuntia echios var. typica, commonly known as the Mexican prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in spines and glochids, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tunas, is edible and commonly consumed in various cuisines.

Opuntia echios var. typica is also valued for its ability to thrive in arid environments and its use in traditional medicine.
