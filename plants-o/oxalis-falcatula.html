Oxalis falcatula

Oxalis falcatula, commonly known as the narrowleaf wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive heart-shaped base and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a shallow root system and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance.

Oxalis falcatula is also known for its ability to spread via runners, making it a resilient groundcover plant.
