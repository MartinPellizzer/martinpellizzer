Otosema

Otosema, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flower heads, which are surrounded by spiny, leaf-like bracts. The plant is native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and the Middle East, where it thrives in sandy or rocky soils. Otosema has narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
