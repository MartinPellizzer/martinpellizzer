Oxytropis campestris var. verrucosa

Oxytropis campestris var. verrucosa, commonly known as the hairy field lupine, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to violet pea-like flowers. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

As a member of the Fabaceae family, it has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
