Otopappus ferrugineus

Otopappus ferrugineus, commonly known as the rusty pappus or desert pappus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is well adapted to harsh desert conditions. Its yellow flowers bloom in late spring, and it produces a distinctive, fluffy pappus that aids in seed dispersal.

Otopappus ferrugineus is often found in disturbed areas and is considered a hardy, drought-tolerant species.
