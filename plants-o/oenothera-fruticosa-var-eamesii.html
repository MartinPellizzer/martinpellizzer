Oenothera fruticosa var. eamesii

Oenothera fruticosa var. eamesii, commonly known as the Eames' evening primrose, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems, lance-shaped leaves, and showy yellow flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and native plant gardens.
