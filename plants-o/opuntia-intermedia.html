Opuntia intermedia

Opuntia intermedia, commonly known as the narrowleaf prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in spines and glochids, which are fine, barbed hairs that can cause irritation. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible fruits known as tunas. Opuntia intermedia thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh, dry conditions.

Its structure and physiology make it well-suited for xerophytic environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various desert organisms.
