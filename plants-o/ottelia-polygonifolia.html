Ottelia polygonifolia

Ottelia polygonifolia, commonly known as the water buttercup, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It typically grows in shallow water, such as ponds and marshes, and has submerged and floating leaves. The plant produces small yellow flowers that bloom above the water surface. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Ottelia polygonifolia plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
