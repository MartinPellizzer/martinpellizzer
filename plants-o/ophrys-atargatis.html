Ophrys atargatis

Ophrys atargatis, commonly known as the Greek Orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which includes a prominent lip that mimics the appearance of a female wasp, aiding in its specialized pollination mechanism. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinct, wasp-like appearance. It is a perennial species that thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

Ophrys atargatis is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems as a food source for certain insect species.
