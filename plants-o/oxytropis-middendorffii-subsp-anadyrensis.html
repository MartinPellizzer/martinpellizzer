Oxytropis middendorffii subsp. anadyrensis

Oxytropis middendorffii subsp.

anadyrensis, commonly known as the Altai milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, compound leaves that are typically hairy.

The plant produces yellow to reddish-purple flowers in umbel-like clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
