Oenocarpus regius

Oenocarpus regius, commonly known as the African oil palm, is a tropical plant native to western and central Africa.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves and produces a fruit that contains a high amount of oil. The plant's botanical features include a tall, straight trunk and a crown of broad, palmate leaves. Its fruit, which is typically orange or red in color, contains a fleshy outer layer and a hard inner shell.

The plant is valued for its oil, which is used in various industrial and culinary applications.
