Oncidium cuneatum

Oncidium cuneatum, commonly known as the Cane Orchid, is a tropical orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its long, cane-like pseudobulbs and clusters of fragrant, yellow-green flowers. The plant typically blooms in late winter or early spring, producing spikes that can reach up to 3 feet in length. Oncidium cuneatum prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Its resilience and ease of care contribute to its popularity among orchid enthusiasts.
