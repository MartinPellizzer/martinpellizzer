Oxytes

Oxytes, commonly known as the "ghost flower," is a unique plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, white, bell-shaped flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions, hence its common name. The plant is a succulent, adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water. Its leaves are fleshy and store moisture, allowing it to thrive in extreme conditions.

Oxytes is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unusual and ethereal appearance.
