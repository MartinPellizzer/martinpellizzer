Oenothera micrantha var. acuminata

Oenothera micrantha var. acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-leaved evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves with pointed tips. The plant produces showy yellow or orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its flowers typically bloom in late summer to early autumn, and the plant thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

Oenothera micrantha var. acuminata is often found in prairies, open woods, and along roadsides.
