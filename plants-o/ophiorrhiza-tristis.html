Ophiorrhiza tristis

Ophiorrhiza tristis, commonly known as the weeping fig or sacred fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spikes, followed by round, fleshy fruits that contain a single seed. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in various regions.

The tree is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to urban environments.
