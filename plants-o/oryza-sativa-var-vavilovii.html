Oryza sativa var. vavilovii

Oryza sativa var. vavilovii, commonly known as the wild rice of Central Asia, is a wild variant of rice native to regions such as Kazakhstan and Russia.

It is characterized by its tall, robust stalks and small, elongated grains that differ from the domesticated rice varieties. This species plays a crucial role in rice genetics due to its genetic diversity and potential for improving cultivated rice strains. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in flood-prone areas.

Its study contributes to understanding the evolutionary history and adaptation of rice species.
