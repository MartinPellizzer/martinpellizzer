Ophrys × drumana

Ophrys × drumana, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant that exhibits unique botanical features.

It is a natural hybrid between Ophrys apifera (the bee orchid) and Ophrys scrobiculata (the helmet orchid), resulting in a plant with a distinctive appearance. The flower has a complex structure, including a labellum that mimics the appearance of a female bee, which plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. This mimicry attracts male bees, which attempt to mate with the flower, facilitating pollination.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is found in various parts of Europe.
