Ochna polycarpa

Ochna polycarpa, commonly known as the African star apple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces striking yellow flowers followed by edible, star-shaped fruits that are typically consumed raw or cooked.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
