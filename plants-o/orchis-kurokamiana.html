Orchis kurokamiana

Orchis kurokamiana, commonly known as the Kurokamiana orchid, is a rare and fascinating species native to Japan.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a tall stem with a single, showy flower. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing a striking violet or purple blossom with intricate patterns. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions with rich, humus-filled soil.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
