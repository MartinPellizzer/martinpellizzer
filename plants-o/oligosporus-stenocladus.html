Oligosporus stenocladus

Oligosporus stenocladus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spore plant, is a unique species within the genus Oligosporus.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy cuticle. The plant produces spores in specialized structures called sporangia, which are typically clustered in dense, spherical formations. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for studies on spore dispersal and plant adaptation.
