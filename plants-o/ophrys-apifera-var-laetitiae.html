Ophrys apifera var. laetitiae

Ophrys apifera var. laetitiae, commonly known as the honey bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species found primarily in the UK.

It is characterized by its intricate floral structure, which mimics the appearance and scent of a female honeybee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant produces showy, bee-like flowers with a dark brown to blackish coloration and yellow markings. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland or heathland habitats.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species and is protected in many regions.
