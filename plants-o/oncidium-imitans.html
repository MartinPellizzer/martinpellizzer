Oncidium imitans

Oncidium imitans, commonly known as the mimic orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, raceme-like inflorescence that produces numerous small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant has fleshy, strap-shaped leaves that help it store water in its natural habitat. Oncidium imitans thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

It is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
