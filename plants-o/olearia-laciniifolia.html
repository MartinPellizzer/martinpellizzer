Olearia laciniifolia

Olearia laciniifolia, commonly known as the lace-leaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate, lace-like appearance. The plant produces small, daisy-like white flowers with yellow centers, which bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Olearia laciniifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and seasonal blooms.
