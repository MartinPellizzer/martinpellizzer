Oenothera bistorta var. hallii

Oenothera bistorta var. hallii, commonly known as the Hall's evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and grow along the stem. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a source of medicinal compounds and its ecological value in supporting pollinator populations.
