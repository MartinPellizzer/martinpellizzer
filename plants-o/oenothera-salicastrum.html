Oenothera salicastrum

Oenothera salicastrum, commonly known as the water-leaf evening primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy yellow or orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its flowers have four petals and numerous stamens, which contribute to its distinctive appearance.

Oenothera salicastrum is adapted to wet environments and is often found in moist soils near streams or in wetlands.
