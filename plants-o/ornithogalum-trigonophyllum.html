Ornithogalum trigonophyllum

Ornithogalum trigonophyllum, commonly known as the three-lobed star-of-Bethlehem, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white or pale yellow star-shaped flowers. The plant belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its striking floral display. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to bloom in late winter or early spring.
