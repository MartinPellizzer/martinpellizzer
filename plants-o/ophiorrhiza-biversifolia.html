Ophiorrhiza biversifolia

Ophiorrhiza biversifolia, commonly known as the two-lobed mangrove, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It thrives in brackish water environments, often found in mangrove forests where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion. The plant has opposite, elliptic leaves that are usually covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are small and clustered, with white petals that bloom in clusters, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that are dispersed by water.

Ophiorrhiza biversifolia is an important species for coastal ecosystems due to its adaptability and ecological contributions.
