Ophrys × morellensis

Ophrys × morellensis, commonly known as the Morell's orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Balkans.

It belongs to the genus Ophrys, which is characterized by its mimicry of female insects to attract male pollinators. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodland habitats and produces intricate, insect-like flowers. Its flowers are usually brown or greenish in color, with a distinct lip that resembles a female bee or wasp.

Ophrys × morellensis is valued for its unique morphology and ecological role in pollination systems.
