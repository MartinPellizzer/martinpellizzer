Opuntia polyacantha var. trichophora

Opuntia polyacantha var. trichophora, commonly known as the hairy prickly pear, is a cactus species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flattened, ribbed stems that are covered in dense, soft spines and hair-like structures. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the summer months, followed by edible, red-fleshed fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and full sun exposure.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a notable species in desert ecosystems.
