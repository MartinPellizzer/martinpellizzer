Orchis brevicornis var. panormitana

Orchis brevicornis var. panormitana, commonly known as the Panormitan orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its unique morphological features, including a short, stout stem and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces a single, erect flower spike bearing a single, striking violet-blue flower with a distinctive hooded structure. It thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
