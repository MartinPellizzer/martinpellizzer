Opsago

Opsago, commonly known as the false hellebore, is a perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Araliaceae and is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale green flowers. The plant produces berries that are toxic to humans and animals, containing cardiac glycosides. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care and is not recommended for cultivation in gardens.
