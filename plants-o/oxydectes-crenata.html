Oxydectes crenata

Oxydectes crenata, commonly known as the crested lettuce, is a leafy vegetable native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are often crinkled or curled, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows to a height of about 20-30 centimeters and prefers cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are tender and can be eaten raw or cooked, making it a versatile addition to salads and cooked dishes.

Oxydectes crenata is also valued for its mild flavor and nutritional content, including vitamins A and C.
