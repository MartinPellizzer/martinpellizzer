Onobrychis melanotricha f. villosa

Onobrychis melanotricha f. villosa, commonly known as the hairy goat's rue, is a perennial legume native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with a hairy texture, which give it its specific epithet "villosa.". The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Its hairy foliage and nitrogen-fixing ability make it a valuable plant in ecological and agricultural contexts.
