Orthosia scoparia

Orthosia scoparia, commonly known as the rock rose or sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features narrow, silver-gray leaves and produces showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Orthosia scoparia is well adapted to rocky and coastal environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
