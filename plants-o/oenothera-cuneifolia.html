Oenothera cuneifolia

Oenothera cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening. The plant is part of the Onagraceae family and is known for its unique flowering behavior, blooming at night. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

The seeds of Oenothera cuneifolia are encased in a hairy, spiny capsule that disperses readily with the wind.
