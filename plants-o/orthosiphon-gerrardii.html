Orthosiphon gerrardii

Orthosiphon gerrardii, commonly known as the blue sage or Mexican hyssop, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal properties. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. Its small, tubular flowers are typically blue or violet, arranged in whorled clusters along the stem.

Orthosiphon gerrardii is often used in traditional medicine for its purported diuretic and anti-inflammatory effects.
