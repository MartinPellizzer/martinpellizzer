Ozoroa macrophylla

Ozoroa macrophylla, commonly known as the giant water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, floating fronds that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as lakes and rivers. Its fronds are composed of numerous small, rounded pinnae that help in photosynthesis and gas exchange.

Ozoroa macrophylla plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and shelter for various aquatic organisms.
