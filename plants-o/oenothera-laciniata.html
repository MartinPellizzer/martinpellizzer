Oenothera laciniata

Oenothera laciniata, commonly known as the fringed evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has lance-shaped, serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats like grasslands and rocky areas.

Oenothera laciniata is part of the evening primrose family (Onagraceae) and is known for its ecological importance in supporting pollinator populations.
