Olinia radiata

Olinia radiata, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange floral bracts that resemble flames, which appear in clusters during the dry season. The tree has smooth, grey bark and elongated, pinnate leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its flowers are followed by small, round fruits that contain a single seed.

Olinia radiata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
