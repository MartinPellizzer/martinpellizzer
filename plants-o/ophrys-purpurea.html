Ophrys purpurea

Ophrys purpurea, commonly known as the purple orchid, is a species of orchid native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive flowers, which resemble female wasps, a feature that plays a key role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats. Its flowers are usually purple or pinkish in color, with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of insects.

Ophrys purpurea is a perennial herb that blooms in spring and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
