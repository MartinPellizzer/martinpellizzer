Ocotea minor

Ocotea minor, commonly known as the small ocotea or narrow-leaved ocotea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, clustered white flowers. Ocotea minor typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, often reaching heights of up to 10 meters.

Its wood is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional crafts and as a source of essential oils.
