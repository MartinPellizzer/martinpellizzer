Origanum × symeonis

Origanum × symeonis, commonly known as Greek oregano, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves with a strong, pungent scent due to its high concentration of essential oils. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in mild climates.

Its culinary and medicinal uses are valued for its flavor and potential health benefits.
