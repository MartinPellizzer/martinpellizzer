Oyedaea oxylepis

Oyedaea oxylepis, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant typically grows as a compact shrub, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native arid environment.

Oyedaea oxylepis is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
