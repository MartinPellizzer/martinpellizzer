Olea latifolia

Olea latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf olive, is a species of flowering plant in the family Oleaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. Olea latifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is also valued for its historical and medicinal uses in traditional medicine.
