Oxypetalum arenicola

Oxypetalum arenicola, commonly known as the sandflower, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale yellow. The plant has a compact growth habit and is well adapted to sandy soils with low nutrient content. Its leaves are usually narrow and succulent, allowing it to conserve water in dry environments.

Oxypetalum arenicola is often found in coastal dunes and rocky outcrops, where it thrives in harsh, sun-exposed conditions.
