Ocotea kunthiana

Ocotea kunthiana, commonly known as the white sand tree or yellow wood, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves and small, clustered white flowers. Its fruit is a drupe, typically containing a single seed, and it plays a role in local ecosystems as a food source for various animals.

Ocotea kunthiana is also valued for its timber, which is used in construction and furniture-making due to its durability and fine grain.
