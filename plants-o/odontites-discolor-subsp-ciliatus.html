Odontites discolor subsp. ciliatus

Odontites discolor subsp.

ciliatus, commonly known as the hairy toothed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that have distinctive ciliate margins. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is noted for its ecological importance, providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
