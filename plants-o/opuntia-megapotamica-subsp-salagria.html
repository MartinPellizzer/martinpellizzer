Opuntia megapotamica subsp. salagria

Opuntia megapotamica subsp.

salagria, commonly known as the Salagria prickly pear, is a cactus species native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile. It is characterized by its flat, spineless pads and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the summer.

The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to harsh climatic conditions. Its pads are edible and have been used traditionally for food and medicinal purposes.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
