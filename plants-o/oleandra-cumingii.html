Oleandra cumingii

Oleandra cumingii, commonly known as the Cuming's oleander, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. It is a member of the Oleaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like the common lilac.

Oleandra cumingii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
