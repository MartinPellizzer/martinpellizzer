Oleandra mollis

Oleandra mollis, commonly known as the silky oleander, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has soft, fuzzy leaves and produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Oleandra mollis is also noted for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions and drought.
