Odontoglossum machupicchuense

Odontoglossum machupicchuense, commonly known as the Machu Picchu orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the high-altitude regions of the Peruvian Andes.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers with intricate patterns on their petals. The plant typically blooms in the spring and prefers cool, humid conditions with well-drained soil. Its leaves are usually dark green and lance-shaped, and it thrives in shaded environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
