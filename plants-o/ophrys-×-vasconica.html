Ophrys × vasconica

Ophrys × vasconica, commonly known as the Spanish bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the genus Ophrys, which is known for its mimicry of female insects to attract male pollinators. The plant produces striking, bee-like flowers that are predominantly yellow with brown and purple markings. It thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Ophrys × vasconica is a short-lived perennial, typically reaching heights of 20 to 40 centimeters.
