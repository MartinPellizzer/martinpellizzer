Ophrys argentaria

Ophrys argentaria, commonly known as the silver helleborine, is a perennial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and its striking, lip-like flower that mimics the appearance of female wasps to attract male wasps for pollination. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist woodlands and thrives in temperate climates. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a distinctive white or silver marking on the lip.

Ophrys argentaria is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its specialized pollination strategy.
