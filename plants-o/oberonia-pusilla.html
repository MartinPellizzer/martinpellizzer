Oberonia pusilla

Oberonia pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf orchid, is a small epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant has slender, wiry stems and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, arranged in a rosette pattern.

Oberonia pusilla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
