Ocotea alloiophylla

Ocotea alloiophylla, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and laurel. The plant is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, orange-red berry that contains several seeds.

Ocotea alloiophylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
