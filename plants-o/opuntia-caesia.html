Opuntia caesia

Opuntia caesia, commonly known as the blue prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flattened, paddle-shaped stems that are covered in spines and glochids, which are small, barbed bristles. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits known as tunas. Opuntia caesia is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in desert ecosystems.
