Oldenlandia spergulacea

Oldenlandia spergulacea, commonly known as the creeping spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant features small, delicate blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters, often accompanied by white bracts. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes, making it both a useful ground cover and a potential weed.
