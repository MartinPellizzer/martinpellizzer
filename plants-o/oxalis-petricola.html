Oxalis petricola

Oxalis petricola, commonly known as the rock oxalis, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves and small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. It is adapted to full sun and requires minimal water, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens.

Oxalis petricola is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
