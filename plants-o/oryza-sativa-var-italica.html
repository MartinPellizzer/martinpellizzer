Oryza sativa var. italica

Oryza sativa var. italica, commonly known as Italian rice, is a variety of rice native to Italy.

It is characterized by its long, slender grains and is typically white in color when uncooked. This variety is often used in risotto and other Italian dishes due to its creamy texture when cooked. It requires a warm climate and ample water for optimal growth.

The plant has a short growth cycle and is cultivated in flooded fields, similar to other rice varieties.
