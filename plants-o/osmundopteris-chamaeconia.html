Osmundopteris chamaeconia

Osmundopteris chamaeconia, commonly known as the small fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Osmundaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and its distinctive, finely divided fronds. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce dense clusters of leaves.

Osmundopteris chamaeconia is notable for its ancient lineage, with fossil records dating back to the Carboniferous period.
