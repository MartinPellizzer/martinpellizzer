Ophrys araneola subsp. tommasinii

Ophrys araneola subsp.

tommasinii, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species found in specific regions of southern Europe. It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a spider, aiding in its pollination strategy.

The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is adapted to rocky or open habitats. Its flowers are usually pale green or brown, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
