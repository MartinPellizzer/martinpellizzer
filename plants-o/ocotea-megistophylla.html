Ocotea megistophylla

Ocotea megistophylla, commonly known as the giant laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, ovate leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and montane rainforests.

Its wood is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional crafts and as a source of essential oils.
