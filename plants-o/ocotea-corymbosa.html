Ocotea corymbosa

Ocotea corymbosa, commonly known as the white sand tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage and rough, bark-like trunk. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in lowland tropical forests. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy surface, while its flowers are small and clustered in corymbs.

The tree is valued for its timber, which is used in furniture and construction, and for its ornamental appeal.
