Odontoglossum × wilckeanum

Odontoglossum × wilckeanum, commonly known as the Wilckeanum orchid, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Odontoglossum australinum and Odontoglossum elatus.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, where it thrives in cool, humid climates at high altitudes. The plant features upright pseudobulbs that support strap-like leaves, and it produces large, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. Its botanical significance lies in its role as a hybrid that demonstrates the genetic diversity within the Odontoglossum genus.

This orchid is popular among horticulturists for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation under proper conditions.
