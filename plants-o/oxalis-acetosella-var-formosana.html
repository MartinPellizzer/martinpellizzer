Oxalis acetosella var. formosana

Oxalis acetosella var. formosana, commonly known as the Formosan wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to Taiwan.

It is a low-growing perennial that features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive yellow veins. The plant produces small, five-petaled flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
