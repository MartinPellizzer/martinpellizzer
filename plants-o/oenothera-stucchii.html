Oenothera stucchii

Oenothera stucchii, commonly known as the Stucky's evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, round seeds.

Oenothera stucchii is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
