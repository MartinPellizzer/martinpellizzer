Orobus vernus lusus albidus

Orobus vernus lusus albidus, commonly known as the white-flowered orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale white petals and a compact, herbaceous growth habit. Native to specific regions in Europe, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers typically bloom in early spring, adding a subtle beauty to woodland landscapes.

The plant's botanical features make it a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts and botanists.
