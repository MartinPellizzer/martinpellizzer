Ocimum knyanum var. astephanum

Ocimum knyanum var. astephanum, commonly known as the purple basil, is a variant of the basil genus native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive deep purple foliage and small, aromatic flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with square stems and opposite leaves. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
