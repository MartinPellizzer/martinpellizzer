Olearia ferruginea

Olearia ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty olearia, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and subalpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pale yellow, bloom in late spring to early summer.

Olearia ferruginea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy and attractive appearance.
