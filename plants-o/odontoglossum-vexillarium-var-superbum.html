Odontoglossum vexillarium var. superbum

Odontoglossum vexillarium var. superbum, commonly known as the Royal Orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the high altitudes of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated flowers that display a vibrant array of colors, often with intricate patterns resembling a royal crest. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its natural habitat.

The species is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and unique floral structure.
