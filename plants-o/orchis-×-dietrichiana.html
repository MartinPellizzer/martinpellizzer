Orchis × dietrichiana

Orchis × dietrichiana, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a cross between Orchis moria and Orchis anthropophora.

It is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family, characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing violet or purple flowers with a distinctive hooded lip. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its hybrid origin contributes to its variability in flower color and size, making it a notable species in botanical studies.
