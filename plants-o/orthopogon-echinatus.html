Orthopogon echinatus

Orthopogon echinatus, commonly known as the spiny grass, is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy, cylindrical stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Orthopogon echinatus is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodland floors and along stream banks.
