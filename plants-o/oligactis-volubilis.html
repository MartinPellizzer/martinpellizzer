Oligactis volubilis

Oligactis volubilis, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along forest edges.

Oligactis volubilis is valued for its ornamental appeal but can become invasive in certain environments.
