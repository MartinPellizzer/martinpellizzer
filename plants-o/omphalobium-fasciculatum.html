Omphalobium fasciculatum

Omphalobium fasciculatum, commonly known as the clustered burr medic, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in well-drained soils, often found in grasslands and open woodlands. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it valuable in soil improvement.

The plant is also used in revegetation projects due to its hardiness and adaptability.
