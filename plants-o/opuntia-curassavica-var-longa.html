Opuntia curassavica var. longa

Opuntia curassavica var. longa, commonly known as the long-spined prickly pear, is a cacti species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in sharp spines and glochids. The plant produces showy, yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit, known as a tuna, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and in some regions for its edible fruit.
