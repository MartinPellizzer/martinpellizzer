Orthosiphon rufinervis

Orthosiphon rufinervis, commonly known as red-veined sage, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with prominent red veins, and spikes of small, tubular flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.
