Orchis francis-drucei

Orchis francis-drucei, commonly known as the Francis-Druce's orchid, is a rare and delicate species native to specific regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers are often clustered in a spike and feature intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insects.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in many areas.
