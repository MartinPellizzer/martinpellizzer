Oenothera indecora subsp. bonariensis

Oenothera indecora subsp.

bonariensis, commonly known as the Argentine evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to South America. It is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the evening.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually erect and branching.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
