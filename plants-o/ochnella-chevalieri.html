Ochnella chevalieri

Ochnella chevalieri, commonly known as the Madagascar candlewood, is a small evergreen tree native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and dense, rounded canopy. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in humid, lowland areas with well-drained soil.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
