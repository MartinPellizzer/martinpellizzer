Ocotea villosa

Ocotea villosa, commonly known as the woolly ocotea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments within tropical rainforests. Its bark is often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Ocotea villosa is valued for its timber, which is used in furniture and cabinetry, and for its essential oils, which have aromatic and medicinal properties.
