Oenothera albicaulis var. trichocalyx

Oenothera albicaulis var. trichocalyx, commonly known as the trichocalyx evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a silvery underside. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and native plant gardens.
