Orchis latifolia var. cordigera

Orchis latifolia var. cordigera, commonly known as the broad-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or grassland habitats. It has a complex root system and produces a single, erect stem that bears several flowers.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.
