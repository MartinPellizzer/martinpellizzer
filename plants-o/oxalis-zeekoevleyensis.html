Oxalis zeekoevleyensis

Oxalis zeekoevleyensis, commonly known as the Zeekoevley's wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.

Oxalis zeekoevleyensis is also valued for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it a resilient addition to drought-tolerant landscapes.
