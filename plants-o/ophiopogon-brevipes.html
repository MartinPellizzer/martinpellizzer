Ophiopogon brevipes

Ophiopogon brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed spathiphyllum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers clustered in a spike, which are often enclosed by a leaf-like bract. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Ophiopogon brevipes is valued for its ornamental foliage and its ability to form dense, low-maintenance ground covers.
