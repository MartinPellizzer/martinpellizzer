Osyris abyssinica var. speciosa

Osyris abyssinica var. speciosa, commonly known as the Abyssinian osier, is a shrub native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems that are often used for basket weaving. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a velvety texture.

The species is valued for both its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local crafts.
