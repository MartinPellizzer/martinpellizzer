Orchis chusua var. pulchella

Orchis chusua var. pulchella, commonly known as the purple orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear a single spike of showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its flowers have a distinct three-lobed structure with a spurred lip, and it produces small, dark brown capsules as its fruit.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
