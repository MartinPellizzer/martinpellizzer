Orchis angustifolia f. reichenbachii

Orchis angustifolia f. reichenbachii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a botanical variant of the common twayblade orchid.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia, typically found in moist, shaded woodlands. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike. Its morphology is adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor soils, and it is pollinated primarily by specific insect species.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique genetic and ecological characteristics.
