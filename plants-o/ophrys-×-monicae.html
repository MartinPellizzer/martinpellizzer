Ophrys × monicae

Ophrys × monicae, commonly known as the Monica orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the genus Ophrys, which is known for its mimicry of female insects to attract male pollinators. The plant produces strikingly complex flowers with intricate patterns that resemble insect genitalia. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

The botanical characteristics of Ophrys × monicae include its tall, erect stems and showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers.
