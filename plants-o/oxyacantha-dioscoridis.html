Oxyacantha dioscoridis

Oxyacantha dioscoridis, commonly known as the African bushwillow, is a deciduous tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its medicinal properties, with various parts used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Its bark and leaves contain bioactive compounds that have been studied for potential pharmaceutical applications.
