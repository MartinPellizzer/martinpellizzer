Orchis × salkowskiana

Orchis × salkowskiana, commonly known as the Salkowski orchid, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Orchis morio and Orchis mascula.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows, preferring well-drained, loamy soils. Its flowers are often adorned with intricate patterns and have a sweet, vanilla-like fragrance.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and unique botanical features.
