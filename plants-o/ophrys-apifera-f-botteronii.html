Ophrys apifera f. botteronii

Ophrys apifera f. botteronii, commonly known as the bee orchid, is a striking orchid species native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its unique flower structure that closely resembles a bee, a feature evolved to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats. Its flowers are usually pale yellow with dark markings that mimic the appearance of a bee's body.

This orchid is a prime example of mimicry in the plant kingdom, showcasing intricate adaptations for reproductive success.
