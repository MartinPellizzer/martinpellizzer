Olea javanica var. grandifolia

Olea javanica var. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-leaved olive, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, ovate leaves that can grow up to 20 cm in length. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by drupes that turn from green to black as they mature. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
