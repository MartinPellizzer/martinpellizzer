Opuntia filipendula

Opuntia filipendula, commonly known as the narrowleaf prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, spineless pads and bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting well to hot, dry conditions. Its pads are edible and often used in traditional cuisines, while its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Opuntia filipendula plays an important role in desert ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
