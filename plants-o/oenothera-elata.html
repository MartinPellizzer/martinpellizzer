Oenothera elata

Oenothera elata, commonly known as the showy evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy yellow or orange flowers that open in the evening. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Oenothera elata thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.

It is often found in open habitats such as prairies, roadsides, and disturbed areas.
