Oxalis rosulata

Oxalis rosulata, commonly known as the Rosy Wood Sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, delicate flowers. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals and has a distinctive leaf structure with a rosette formation. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Oxalis rosulata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and blooms.
