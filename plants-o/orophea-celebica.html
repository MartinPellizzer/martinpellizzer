Orophea celebica

Orophea celebica, commonly known as the Celebes starfruit, is a tropical shrub native to the Maluku Islands of Indonesia.

It belongs to the family Guttiferae and is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves and showy, yellowish-orange flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible and have a tangy, citrus-like flavor. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fruit, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and for local consumption.
