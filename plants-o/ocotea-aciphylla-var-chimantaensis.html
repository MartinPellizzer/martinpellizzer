Ocotea aciphylla var. chimantaensis

Ocotea aciphylla var. chimantaensis, commonly known as the Chimantaensis Ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green, often with a slight waxy coating. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by spherical berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
