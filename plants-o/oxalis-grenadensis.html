Oxalis grenadensis

Oxalis grenadensis, commonly known as the Canary Islands wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 cm, featuring trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers. Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and delicate blooms, which appear in spring.

Oxalis grenadensis is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
