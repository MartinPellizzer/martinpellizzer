Olearia humilis

Olearia humilis, commonly known as the silverweed or silverleaf daisy, is a low-growing shrub native to New Zealand.

It features silver-white, fuzzy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance, and its flowers are typically pale yellow or white, appearing in clusters. The plant is well adapted to coastal and rocky environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate harsh conditions.

Olearia humilis plays an important role in local ecosystems, providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
