Ophrys × salapiensis

Ophrys × salapiensis, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant that results from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is characterized by its intricate and deceptive floral structures, which closely resemble female wasps to attract male pollinators. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its flowers are rich in nectar and emit a subtle scent that mimics the pheromones of female wasps.

This botanical feature is a prime example of evolutionary adaptation in plant-pollinator interactions.
