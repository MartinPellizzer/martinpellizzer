Oryza clandestina f. maculosa

Oryza clandestina f. maculosa, commonly known as the spotted wild rice, is a botanical variant of the rice genus Oryza.

It is characterized by its distinctive spotted leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. This plant thrives in wetland environments, often found in marshes and along the edges of freshwater bodies. Its morphology includes slender, grass-like stems and pale yellow flowers.

The species is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its ecological niche and potential genetic diversity.
