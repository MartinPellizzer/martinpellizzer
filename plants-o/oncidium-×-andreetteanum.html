Oncidium × andreetteanum

Oncidium × andreetteanum, commonly known as the Andreette's orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, long-lasting flowers that come in a range of colors, including yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its inflorescence consists of a raceme of showy, fragrant blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Oncidium × andreetteanum requires bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.
