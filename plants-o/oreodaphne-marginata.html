Oreodaphne marginata

Oreodaphne marginata, commonly known as the white-veined mallow, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive white or pale green veins. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions.
