Ophiocaryon paradoxum

Ophiocaryon paradoxum, commonly known as the paradoxum lichen, is a unique species of lichen belonging to the family Parmeliaceae.

It is characterized by its crustose growth form, forming a thin, adherent layer on rock surfaces, typically in arid or semi-arid environments. The thallus is usually pale yellow to orange in color, with a smooth or slightly granular surface. It contains a variety of secondary metabolites, including usnic acid and atranorin, which contribute to its chemical diversity.

This lichen is often used in ecological studies to assess air quality and environmental conditions due to its sensitivity to pollutants.
