Oxytropis volkii

Oxytropis volkii, commonly known as Volk's locoweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, and it produces pods that contain small, hard seeds.

Oxytropis volkii is also known for its toxic properties, making it a potential hazard to livestock.
