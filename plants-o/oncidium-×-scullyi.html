Oncidium × scullyi

Oncidium × scullyi, commonly known as the Scully's orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful flowers that range from yellow to orange and red, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air. Its inflorescence consists of a spike bearing multiple flowers, which bloom sequentially over several weeks.

Oncidium × scullyi is prized for its ornamental value and is widely cultivated for its long-lasting, showy blooms.
