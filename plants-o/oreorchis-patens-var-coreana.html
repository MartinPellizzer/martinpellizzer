Oreorchis patens var. coreana

Oreorchis patens var. coreana, commonly known as the Korean twayblade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Korea.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and small, pale green to white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, often on rocky or humus-rich soils. Its flowers are usually arranged in a spike and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its delicate appearance.
