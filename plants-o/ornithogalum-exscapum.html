Ornithogalum exscapum

Ornithogalum exscapum, commonly known as the snake's head lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a tall, slender scape bearing a single umbel of white or pale green flowers. The plant is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ornithogalum exscapum is known for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
