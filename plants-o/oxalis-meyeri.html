Oxalis meyeri

Oxalis meyeri, commonly known as the False Shamrock, is a striking ornamental plant native to South Africa.

It features trifoliate leaves that resemble clover, with each leaflet having a distinctive white or yellow midrib. The plant produces vibrant clusters of five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, or orange, typically in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its compact growth habit and seasonal blooms make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking both beauty and ease of care.
