Ocimum guineense

Ocimum guineense, commonly known as African basil, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its small, white to purple flowers. Ocimum guineense is valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
