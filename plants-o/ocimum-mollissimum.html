Ocimum mollissimum

Ocimum mollissimum, commonly known as velvet leaf basil, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that are dark green and aromatic. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple or white, arranged in spikes. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Ocimum mollissimum is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb due to its distinctive fragrance and potential health benefits.
