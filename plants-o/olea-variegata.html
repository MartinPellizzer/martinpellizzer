Olea variegata

Olea variegata, commonly known as the variegated olive, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with striking white or yellow variegation. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by edible, black drupes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Olea variegata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscapes.
