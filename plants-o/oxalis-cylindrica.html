Oxalis cylindrica

Oxalis cylindrica, commonly known as the cylindrical wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, cylindrical tuberous roots and trifoliate leaves with reddish veins. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Oxalis cylindrica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to spread through rhizomes.
