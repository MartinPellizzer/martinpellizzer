Oxymitra fornicata var. glabra

Oxymitra fornicata var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth oxymitra, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. The flowers are typically blue to violet in color, arranged in whorled clusters at the ends of the branches. This variety is distinguished by its glabrous (smooth) leaves, which set it apart from other varieties within the species.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.
