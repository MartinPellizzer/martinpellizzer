Oenothera missouriensis var. oklahomensis

Oenothera missouriensis var. oklahomensis, commonly known as the Oklahoma evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the central United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and its flowers have a distinctive, recurved shape.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinator populations.
