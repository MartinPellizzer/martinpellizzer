Oligostachyum yonganense

Oligostachyum yonganense, commonly known as the Yongan bamboo, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the Philippines.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually green and covered in a fine layer of hair. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in montane forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
