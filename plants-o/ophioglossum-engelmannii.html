Ophioglossum engelmannii

It is characterized by its distinctive, tongue-shaped spore-producing structure called a sporophyll, which is typically positioned at the base of the plant. The plant has a short, erect stem with a single, lanceolate leaf that is usually dark green and leathery in texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks, often in rocky or calcareous soils.
