Oncoba spireana

Oncoba spireana, commonly known as the spirea tree, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical or ovate in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers in spring, which are followed by small, red berries.

Oncoba spireana is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy or limestone soils.
