Ophrys × arachnitiformis subsp. archipelagi

Ophrys × arachnitiformis subsp.

archipelagi, commonly known as the island bee orchid, is a hybrid species within the Ophrys genus, characterized by its unique floral morphology. It exhibits a complex hybrid origin, resulting from crosses between Ophrys arachnitiformis and other related species.

The plant is notable for its mimicry of female wasps, a trait that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. It thrives in specific habitats, typically on calcareous soils in Mediterranean regions.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a model for studying hybridization and evolutionary adaptation in orchids.
