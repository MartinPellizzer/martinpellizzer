Orthophytum caraibense

Orthophytum caraibense, commonly known as the Caribbean pincushion cactus, is a species native to the Caribbean region.

It is a low-growing, spineless cactus with a distinctive appearance, featuring a rounded, fleshy stem covered in soft, woolly hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
