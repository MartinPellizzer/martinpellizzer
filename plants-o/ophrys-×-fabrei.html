Ophrys × fabrei

Ophrys × fabrei, commonly known as the bee orchid hybrid, is a fascinating plant in the Orchidaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant is notable for its intricate floral structures, which mimic the appearance of female bees to attract male bees for pollination. Its flowers typically display a dark brown to black coloration with yellow markings, resembling the body of a bee.

This mimicry is a key adaptation that enhances its reproductive success in its native habitat.
