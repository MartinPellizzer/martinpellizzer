Oncidium aurarium

Oncidium aurarium, commonly known as the "Golden Orchid," is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, racemose inflorescence bearing numerous small, yellowish-green flowers with a distinctive golden lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Oncidium aurarium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its long-lasting, showy blooms.
