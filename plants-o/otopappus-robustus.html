Otopappus robustus

Otopappus robustus, commonly known as the desert tarplant, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils. Its robust growth habit and ability to withstand drought make it well adapted to harsh desert conditions.

Otopappus robustus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
