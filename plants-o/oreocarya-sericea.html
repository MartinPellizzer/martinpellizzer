Oreocarya sericea

Oreocarya sericea, commonly known as the silverleaf phlox, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
