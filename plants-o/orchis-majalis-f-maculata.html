Orchis majalis f. maculata

Orchis majalis f. maculata, commonly known as the spotted marsh orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive spotted leaves and pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. This plant typically grows in wet meadows, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, preferring damp, calcareous soils. The flowers are usually arranged in a loose spike and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is a perennial herb that plays an important role in wetland ecosystems.
