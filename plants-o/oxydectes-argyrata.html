Oxydectes argyrata

Oxydectes argyrata, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-green, fuzzy leaves that form a dense, compact structure. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by fluffy white seed heads that disperse easily by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in Mediterranean climates.

Oxydectes argyrata is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its striking foliage and long-lasting blooms.
