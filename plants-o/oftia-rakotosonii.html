Oftia rakotosonii

Oftia rakotosonii, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a rare and endemic species found exclusively in the rainforests of Madagascar.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique floral structure and vibrant colors. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and drawing moisture and nutrients from the air. Its flowers are highly specialized, adapted to attract specific pollinators such as bees and moths.

Due to habitat destruction, Oftia rakotosonii is considered critically endangered, making conservation efforts essential for its survival.
