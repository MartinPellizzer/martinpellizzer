Oxybaphus lanceolatus

Oxybaphus lanceolatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf oxybaphus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a cluster, and bloom during the warmer months.
