Oxalis sellowii var. alba

Oxalis sellowii var. alba, commonly known as the white wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and woodlands. It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is known for its delicate appearance.

Oxalis sellowii var. alba is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and blooms.
