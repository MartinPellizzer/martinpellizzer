Ozoroa insignis subsp. latifolia

Ozoroa insignis subsp.

latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf ozoroa, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

The subspecies is notable for its adaptability and resilience to drought conditions.
