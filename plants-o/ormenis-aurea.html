Ormenis aurea

Ormenis aurea, commonly known as the golden ormenis, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, narrow leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Ormenis aurea is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental value and hardy nature.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
