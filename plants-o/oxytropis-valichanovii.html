Oxytropis valichanovii

Oxytropis valichanovii, commonly known as the Valichanov's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces elongated pods containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Oxytropis valichanovii is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it ecologically significant in its native habitat.
