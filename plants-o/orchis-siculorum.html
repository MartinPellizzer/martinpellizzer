Orchis siculorum

Orchis siculorum, commonly known as the Sicilian orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or open woodland habitats. The plant produces tall, slender stems that bear a single, showy flower with a distinctive violet or purple coloration. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils, which is common in its natural environment.

The orchid is known for its intricate floral structure, characteristic of the Orchidaceae family.
