Otoglossum spathulatum

Otoglossum spathulatum, commonly known as the broad-leaved orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
