Orthosiphon lanceolatus

Orthosiphon lanceolatus, commonly known as the lanceleaf spurry, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. It is often found in open areas such as fields and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and potential medicinal applications.
