Oncidium bidens

Oncidium bidens, commonly known as the two-lobed orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a sympodial growth habit, characterized by its ability to produce new shoots from the base of the old ones. The plant features elongated, strap-like leaves that are usually dark green and covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, with three sepals and three petals, and a distinctive lip that is often marked with purple or red.

Oncidium bidens is valued in horticulture for its resilience and ability to thrive in a variety of growing conditions.
