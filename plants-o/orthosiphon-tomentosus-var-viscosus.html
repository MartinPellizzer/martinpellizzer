Orthosiphon tomentosus var. viscosus

Orthosiphon tomentosus var. viscosus, commonly known as hairy senna, is a herbaceous plant characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, yellowish flowers.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae and is native to tropical regions of Asia. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has a creeping habit with erect stems.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.
