Ophiorrhiza debiana

Ophiorrhiza debiana, commonly known as the slender mangrove, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small shrub that thrives in saline and brackish water environments, often found in mangrove forests. The plant has small, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and covered in a fine layer of white wax. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, round berries as fruit.

Ophiorrhiza debiana plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
