Oncidium erosilabium

Oncidium erosilabium, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a sympodial orchid characterized by its pseudobulbs and long, strap-like leaves. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences with showy, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Oncidium erosilabium thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

It is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.
