Odontoglossum crispum var. plumatum

Odontoglossum crispum var. plumatum, commonly known as the plumose orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that are often adorned with a distinctive plumose (feather-like) pattern. The plant produces intricate, spurred orchid flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom during the cooler months. It prefers cool, humid environments and is often grown as a greenhouse or indoor plant.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique leaf morphology and its role in the diverse orchid flora of the Andean region.
