Ocotea rivularis

Ocotea rivularis, commonly known as the river ocotea, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. It produces small, fragrant flowers and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

The botanical features of Ocotea rivularis make it an important species in the understory of tropical forests.
