Oetosis lanceolata

Oetosis lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf oetosis, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and hairy stems, which help it retain moisture.

Oetosis lanceolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
