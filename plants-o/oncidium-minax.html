Oncidium minax

Oncidium minax, commonly known as the yellow butterfly orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect pseudobulbs and long, arching inflorescences that bear numerous fragrant, yellow-orange flowers. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and well-draining soil. It requires regular watering and moderate fertilization during the growing season.

Oncidium minax is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in greenhouse settings.
