Ocotea rufescens

Ocotea rufescens, commonly known as the redwood or red sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark-colored wood. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces drupes that are often used in traditional medicine.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and has ecological and economic significance in its native habitats.
