Oxalis lilloi

Oxalis lilloi, commonly known as the Lillo's wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in well-drained, moist soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be found in shaded or partially sunny environments.

Oxalis lilloi is also valued for its ability to thrive in various garden conditions.
