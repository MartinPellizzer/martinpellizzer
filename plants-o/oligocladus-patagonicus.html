Oligocladus patagonicus

Oligocladus patagonicus, commonly known as the Patagonian hairgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and fine, wiry leaves that are typically covered in short, stiff hairs. This plant thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks. Its root system is fibrous, allowing it to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in its native habitat.

Oligocladus patagonicus plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat for small invertebrates and serving as a food source for grazing animals.
