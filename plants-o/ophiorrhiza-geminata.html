Ophiorrhiza geminata

Ophiorrhiza geminata, commonly known as the twinleaf or twinleaf sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant has simple, opposite leaves and produces small, white or yellowish flowers. Its fruits are capsules that release tiny seeds when mature.

Ophiorrhiza geminata is known for its adaptability to saline and arid conditions, making it an important species in coastal ecosystems.
