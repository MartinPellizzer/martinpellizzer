Ophrys murgiana

Ophrys murgiana, commonly known as the Murgian bee orchid, is a species of orchid native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its unique flower structure, which resembles a female bee, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brownish in color, with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of insects.

Ophrys murgiana is a rare and protected species, often found in specific microhabitats across Italy and parts of the Balkans.
