Oxypetalum campanulatum

Oxypetalum campanulatum, commonly known as the bell-shaped oxypetalum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers bloom in late summer and early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Oxypetalum campanulatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
