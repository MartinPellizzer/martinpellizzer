Oxalis pes-caprae

Oxalis pes-caprae, commonly known as wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive light green veins, giving it a unique and ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of yellow or white, often with five petals. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Oxalis pes-caprae is also known for its ability to tolerate drought conditions once established.
