Oenothera parodiana subsp. strigulosa

Oenothera parodiana subsp.

strigulosa, commonly known as the woolly evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, rugged appearance.

The plant produces yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils. Oenothera parodiana subsp.

strigulosa is part of the evening primrose family (Onagraceae) and is known for its ecological adaptability and resilience.
