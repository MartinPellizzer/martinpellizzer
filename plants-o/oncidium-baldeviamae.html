Oncidium baldeviamae

Oncidium baldeviamae, commonly known as the Baldeviamae Oncidium, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, long-lasting inflorescences that display a variety of vibrant colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are often fragrant and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Oncidium baldeviamae is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
