Oncidium ochmatochilum

Oncidium ochmatochilum, commonly known as the Ochmatochilum orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often arranged in racemes. Its leaves are typically strap-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Oncidium ochmatochilum thrives in humid, well-drained environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
