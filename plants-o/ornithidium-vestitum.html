Ornithidium vestitum

Ornithidium vestitum, commonly known as the velvetleaf orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety green leaves and clusters of fragrant, yellowish-brown flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Ornithidium vestitum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in specific tropical conditions.
