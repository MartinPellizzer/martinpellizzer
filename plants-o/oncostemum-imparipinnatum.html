Oncostemum imparipinnatum

Oncostemum imparipinnatum, commonly known as the irregular-pinnate sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to aquatic habitats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive irregular arrangement of branches along the stem.

The inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often clustered in dense, cylindrical clusters, contributing to its ornamental value in wetland gardens.
