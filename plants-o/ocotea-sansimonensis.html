Ocotea sansimonensis

Ocotea sansimonensis, commonly known as the Guianan ocotea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments and can reach heights of up to 15 meters. Its foliage is dense, with lance-shaped leaves that have a glossy surface and a strong, spicy scent.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential ecological significance in its native habitat.
