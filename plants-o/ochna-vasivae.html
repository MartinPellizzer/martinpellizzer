Ochna vasivae

Ochna vasivae, commonly known as the KwaZulu-Natal ochna, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, and its leaves are typically ovate with a smooth margin. Ochna vasivae is adapted to grow in sandy or rocky soils, often in coastal or mountainous regions.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
