Orobanche pseudoloricata

Orobanche pseudoloricata, commonly known as the false bladderpod, is a parasitic flowering plant belonging to the Orobanchaceae family.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients, typically attaching to the roots of members of the legume family. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are often pink or purple in color. Its life cycle is closely tied to the presence of its host, and it can significantly impact the growth of agricultural crops.

Due to its parasitic nature, it is considered a weed in some regions and poses challenges for sustainable farming practices.
