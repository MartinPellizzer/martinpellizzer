Opuntia canada

Opuntia canada, commonly known as the Canadian prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Canada and the northern United States.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in spiny areoles, which are clusters of spines, glochids, and flowers. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers in the summer, followed by red, fleshy fruits known as tunas. Opuntia canada is adapted to cold climates and can survive in poor soil conditions.

It plays an important ecological role by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
