Ouratea hexasperma f. typica

Ouratea hexasperma f. typica, commonly known as the white mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its evergreen, glossy leaves and small, white flowers. The plant typically grows on the branches of host trees, drawing nutrients and water from them. Its fruit is a small, dark blue drupe that contains a single seed.
