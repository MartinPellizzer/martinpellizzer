Oncidium millianum

Oncidium millianum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and produces clusters of fragrant, golden-yellow flowers. The plant typically blooms during the dry season, and its flowers are known for their intricate, spurred petals. Oncidium millianum prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

It is also valued for its ornamental beauty and relatively low maintenance requirements.
