Oleandra brasiliana

Oleandra brasiliana, commonly known as Brazilian oleander, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has smooth, often hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely. It is evergreen and thrives in warm, humid climates, making it a popular ornamental plant.

However, it is toxic due to the presence of cardiac glycosides in its leaves and flowers.
