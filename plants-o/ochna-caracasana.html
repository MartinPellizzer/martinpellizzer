Ochna caracasana

Ochna caracasana, commonly known as the Caracas ochna, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Venezuela.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are showy, with five petals and bright colors, often attracting pollinators like birds and insects.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
