Osteospermum imbricatum subsp. nervatum

Osteospermum imbricatum subsp.

nervatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf African daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces showy yellow-orange flower heads that bloom in spring and summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

The subspecies is distinguished by its finely veined leaves and compact growth habit.
