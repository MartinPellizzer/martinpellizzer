Oligosporus pyramidatus

Oligosporus pyramidatus, commonly known as the pyramid-shaped spore plant, is a unique species characterized by its distinctive spore morphology.

This plant belongs to the fungal kingdom and is notable for its pyramid-shaped spores, which are a key feature in its classification. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate regions. The spores are typically dispersed by wind, contributing to its reproductive success.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a model organism for studying spore development and fungal genetics.
