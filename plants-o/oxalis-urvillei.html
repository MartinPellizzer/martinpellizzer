Oxalis urvillei

Oxalis urvillei, commonly known as the Urville's wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves have a distinctive appearance with veins that resemble those of a maple leaf, and it produces edible, sour-tasting berries.

Oxalis urvillei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.
