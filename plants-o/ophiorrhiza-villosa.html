Ophiorrhiza villosa

Ophiorrhiza villosa, commonly known as the hairy mallow, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small and clustered, with five petals and a distinctive calyx.

Ophiorrhiza villosa is known for its ability to thrive in saline environments, making it an important species in coastal ecosystems.
