Orchis × luizetiana

Orchis × luizetiana, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a cross between two orchid species, typically Orchis morio and Orchis mascula.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant produces a single spike of flowers, each with a three-lobed lip that is often marked with intricate patterns. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas across Europe.

The hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
