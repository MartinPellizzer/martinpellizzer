Oxalis zacatecasensis

Oxalis zacatecasensis, commonly known as the Zacatecas wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

Oxalis zacatecasensis is also valued for its ability to tolerate drought conditions once established.
