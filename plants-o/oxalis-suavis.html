Oxalis suavis

Oxalis suavis, commonly known as sweet wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is known for its delicate, fragrant blooms. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Oxalis suavis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
