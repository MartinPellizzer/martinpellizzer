Ormosia zahnii

Ormosia zahnii, commonly known as the Chinese tallow tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern China, Vietnam, and the Philippines.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds enclosed in a fleshy, orange-red aril. Ormosia zahnii is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.

However, it can become invasive in some areas, posing ecological challenges.
