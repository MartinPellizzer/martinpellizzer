Oryctanthus occidentalis subsp. occidentalis

Oryctanthus occidentalis subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the western sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in spring. It has small, lance-shaped leaves and is adapted to arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
