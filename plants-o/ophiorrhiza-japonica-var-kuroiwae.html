Ophiorrhiza japonica var. kuroiwae

Ophiorrhiza japonica var. kuroiwae, commonly known as the Japanese bead vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring small, bead-like fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value.
