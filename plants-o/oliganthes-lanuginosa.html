Oliganthes lanuginosa

Oliganthes lanuginosa, commonly known as the woolly thistle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and tall, spiny stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces purple to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
