Ocotea magnifrons

Ocotea magnifrons, commonly known as the magnolia-leafed ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves that are often mistaken for those of magnolia species. Its flowers are small, white, and fragrant, typically appearing in clusters.

Ocotea magnifrons is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates for its aesthetic foliage.
