Oenothera cinerea subsp. cinerea

Oenothera cinerea subsp.

cinerea, commonly known as silverleaf evening primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, white hairs, providing a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces showy yellow flowers that open in the evening, hence its common name. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as prairies and disturbed areas. Oenothera cinerea subsp.

cinerea is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
