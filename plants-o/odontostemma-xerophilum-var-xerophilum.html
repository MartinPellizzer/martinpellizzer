Odontostemma xerophilum var. xerophilum

Odontostemma xerophilum var. xerophilum, commonly known as the desert toothbrush, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the rainy season, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its root system is deep and robust, enabling it to access groundwater in nutrient-poor soils.

Overall, it is well-adapted to extreme environments, showcasing remarkable resilience and survival strategies.
