Oxytropis acutirostrata

Oxytropis acutirostrata, commonly known as the sharp-beaked locoweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright, spiny stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

It is adapted to grow in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert environments.
