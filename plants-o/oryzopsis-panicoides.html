Oryzopsis panicoides

Oryzopsis panicoides, commonly known as blackgrass, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks.

Oryzopsis panicoides plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
