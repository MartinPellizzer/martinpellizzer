Olea europaea var. sativa

Olea europaea var. sativa, commonly known as the olive tree, is a evergreen tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silvery-green leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The fruit, known as the olive, is a drupe that matures to a dark green or black color. The plant is valued for its oil, which is extracted from the fruit, and for its historical and cultural significance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, dry climate for optimal growth.
