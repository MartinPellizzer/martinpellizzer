Olea maderensis

Olea maderensis, commonly known as the Madeira olive, is a small evergreen tree native to the island of Madeira.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The tree produces small, dark purple berries that contain the edible seeds. It thrives in mild, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance.

Olea maderensis is also valued for its hardy nature and ability to grow in rocky soils.
