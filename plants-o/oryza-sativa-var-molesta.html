Oryza sativa var. molesta

Oryza sativa var. molesta, commonly known as wild rice, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and spikes of small, rice-like grains. This plant thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along riverbanks. Its grains are typically smaller and more elongated compared to those of cultivated rice.

Oryza sativa var. molesta is valued for its nutritional content and has been used as a food source for centuries.
