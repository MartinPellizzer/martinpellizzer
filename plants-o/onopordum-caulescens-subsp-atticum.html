Onopordum caulescens subsp. atticum

Onopordum caulescens subsp.

atticum, commonly known as the Attic thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its spiny, rough leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as slopes and wastelands. It has a robust root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
