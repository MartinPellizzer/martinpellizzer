Oenothera spiralis var. viridescens

Oenothera spiralis var. viridescens, commonly known as the green-veined evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and the plant has a fibrous root system.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinator biodiversity and its potential medicinal uses.
