Ononis × pseudohircina

Ononis × pseudohircina, commonly known as the hybrid sweet vernalgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces trifoliate leaves with a hairy texture, and its root system is fibrous, aiding in soil stabilization. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and disturbed areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
