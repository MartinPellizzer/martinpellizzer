Oncosiphon intermedius

Oncosiphon intermedius, commonly known as the common senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being elliptical and slightly hairy.

The flowers are yellow and arranged in racemes, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
