Origanum jordanicum

Origanum jordanicum, commonly known as Jordanian oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, pale blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. Its essential oils, rich in compounds like thymol and carvacrol, contribute to its strong scent and medicinal properties.

It is often used in traditional herbal remedies and as a culinary herb.
