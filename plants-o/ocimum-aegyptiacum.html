Ocimum aegyptiacum

Ocimum aegyptiacum, commonly known as holy basil or tulsi, is an aromatic herb native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers and has a strong, spicy scent due to its high concentration of essential oils. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Ocimum aegyptiacum thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal cultivation.
