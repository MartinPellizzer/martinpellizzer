Orchis cazorlensis

Orchis cazorlensis, commonly known as the Cazorla orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to southern Spain.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family, characterized by its showy, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are usually violet or purple in color, with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
