Oxymitra rosea

Oxymitra rosea, commonly known as the pink orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking pink and white flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its arid environment.

Oxymitra rosea is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses for its beauty.
