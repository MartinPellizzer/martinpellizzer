Oenone longifolia

Oenone longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf oenone, is a flowering plant native to parts of eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow to greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Oenone longifolia is typically found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and role in supporting local wildlife.
