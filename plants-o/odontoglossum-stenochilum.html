Odontoglossum stenochilum

Odontoglossum stenochilum, commonly known as the narrow-lipped orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves and intricate floral structure. The plant typically grows in cool, humid environments at high altitudes, often in shaded forest understories. Its flowers feature a unique combination of colors, including shades of purple and white, with a specialized lip that aids in pollination.

Odontoglossum stenochilum is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively compact growth habit.
