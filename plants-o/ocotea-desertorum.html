Ocotea desertorum

Ocotea desertorum, commonly known as the desert ocotea, is a small evergreen tree native to arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its bark is often grayish-brown and has a distinctive, aromatic scent.

Ocotea desertorum is well adapted to dry environments, exhibiting drought resistance and efficient water conservation mechanisms.
