Ocimum aristatum

Ocimum aristatum, commonly known as the aristate basil, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and oregano. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple or white. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications due to their aromatic properties.

Ocimum aristatum thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
