Olearia Paniculata

Olearia paniculata is a striking plant native to New Zealand, known for its unique appearance and ecological significance. It features dense clusters of small white flowers that bloom in spring, creating a dramatic visual impact in its natural habitat. This species thrives in coastal environments, often growing on cliffs and rocky shores where it withstands harsh conditions. Its ability to colonize difficult terrain makes it an important part of New Zealand's native flora. Despite its beauty, Olearia paniculata faces threats from habitat loss and invasive species, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
