Onopordum rotundifolium

Onopordum rotundifolium, commonly known as the globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its rounded, broad leaves and showy yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is typically found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in drought conditions.

The species is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and is often used in xeriscaping.
