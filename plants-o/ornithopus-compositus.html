Ornithopus compositus

Ornithopus compositus, commonly known as the composite bird's-foot trefoil, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-bearing fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features compound leaves with a distinctive bird-like appearance at the base, giving it its common name. It produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Ornithopus compositus thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.
