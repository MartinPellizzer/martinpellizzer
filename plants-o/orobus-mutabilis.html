Orobus mutabilis

Orobus mutabilis, commonly known as the variable orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that change color as they mature. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and wetlands. Its flowers can range from white to pink to purple, depending on environmental conditions and age.

Orobus mutabilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique and colorful blooms.
