Octopoma rupigenum

Octopoma rupigenum, commonly known as the roughleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy purple to pink composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, rough-textured leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats, including swamps and stream banks, and is often found in the understory of forests.

Octopoma rupigenum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
