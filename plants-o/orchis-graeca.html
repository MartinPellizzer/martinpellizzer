Orchis graeca

Orchis graeca, commonly known as the Greek orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its roots are fibrous and often form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi.

Orchis graeca is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive blooms.
