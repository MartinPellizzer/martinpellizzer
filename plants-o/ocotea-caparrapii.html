Ocotea caparrapii

Ocotea caparrapii, commonly known as the Guayabilla tree, is a species native to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurel and avocado trees, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant typically grows as a medium to large evergreen tree, reaching heights of up to 20 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

The fruit is a small, dark-colored drupe that contains a single seed, and the plant plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
