Orchis elata subsp. durandii

Orchis elata subsp.

durandii, commonly known as the Alpine orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to high-altitude regions of Europe. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, violet-blue flowers.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its inflorescence consists of a single spike bearing several flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different floral morphology compared to other members of the *Orchis elata* species complex.
