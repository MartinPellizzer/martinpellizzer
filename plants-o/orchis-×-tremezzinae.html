Orchis × tremezzinae

Orchis × tremezzinae, commonly known as the Tremezzina orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Orchis moria and Orchis purpurea.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the alpine regions of Europe, particularly in the Dolomites. The plant typically produces tall, erect stems with a single, showy purple or violet flower that blooms in late spring. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils.

The Tremezzina orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
