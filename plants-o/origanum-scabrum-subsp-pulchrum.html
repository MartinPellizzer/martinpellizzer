Origanum scabrum subsp. pulchrum

Origanum scabrum subsp.

pulchrum, commonly known as the beautiful rough oregano, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features aromatic, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture.

The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its essential oils contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, contributing to its medicinal and culinary uses.
