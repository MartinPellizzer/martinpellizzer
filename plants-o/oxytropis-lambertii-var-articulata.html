Oxytropis lambertii var. articulata

Oxytropis lambertii var. articulata, commonly known as the articulated milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the arid regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

This species plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
