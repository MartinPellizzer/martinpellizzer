Oncidium sawyeri

Oncidium sawyeri, commonly known as the Sawyer's orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, long-lasting blooms. The plant typically produces pendulous inflorescences with brightly colored flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. Oncidium sawyeri is a epiphytic orchid, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic.

Its flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their striking appearance and longevity.
