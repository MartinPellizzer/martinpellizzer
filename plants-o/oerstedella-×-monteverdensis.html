Oerstedella × monteverdensis

Oerstedella × monteverdensis, commonly known as the hybrid azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rhododendron genus and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and thrives in acidic, well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes due to its vibrant blooms and compact growth habit.

Oerstedella × monteverdensis is a popular choice for shade gardens and requires partial to full sun for optimal flowering.
