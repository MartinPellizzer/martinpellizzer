Ozothamnus dimorphus

Ozothamnus dimorphus, commonly known as the variable goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in dense clusters, which bloom from late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are usually alternate and have a distinctive silvery or greyish underside, aiding in water conservation.

Ozothamnus dimorphus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native gardens.
