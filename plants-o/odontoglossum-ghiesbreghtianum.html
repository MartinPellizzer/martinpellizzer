Odontoglossum ghiesbreghtianum

Odontoglossum ghiesbreghtianum, commonly known as the Ghiesbreght's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its pseudobulbs and strap-like leaves. The plant produces striking flowers with elongated, spurred petals and a distinctive, often fragrant scent. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Odontoglossum ghiesbreghtianum is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and unique floral structure.
