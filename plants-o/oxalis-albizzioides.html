Oxalis albizzioides

Oxalis albizzioides, commonly known as the velvetleaf wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive velvety texture and clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers. The plant belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. It produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Oxalis albizzioides is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique appearance.
