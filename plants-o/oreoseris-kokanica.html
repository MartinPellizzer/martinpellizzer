Oreoseris kokanica

Oreoseris kokanica, commonly known as the Kokanica dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in temperate climates with well-drained conditions. Its yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and it produces a distinctive seed head that resembles a dandelion.

Oreoseris kokanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in wildflower gardens and natural habitats.
