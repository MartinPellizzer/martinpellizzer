Oxalis fuegensis

Oxalis fuegensis, commonly known as the Andean wood sorrel, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of southern South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with striking red veins and small, five-petaled yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soil. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a short, erect stem.

Oxalis fuegensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
