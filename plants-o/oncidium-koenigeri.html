Oncidium koenigeri

Oncidium koenigeri, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns and a distinctive green or brown lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Oncidium koenigeri is valued in horticulture for its long-lasting blooms and ease of care.
