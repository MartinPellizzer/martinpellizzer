Orchis fuscata

Orchis fuscata, commonly known as the brown orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in moist, shaded woodlands and meadows. The plant features a tall, erect stem with a single, showy flower that is usually a deep brown or purple hue. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it produces a distinctive, elongated fruit capsule that contains numerous small seeds.

Orchis fuscata is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for various pollinators and serving as an indicator of undisturbed habitats.
