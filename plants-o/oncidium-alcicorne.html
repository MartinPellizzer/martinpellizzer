Oncidium alcicorne

Oncidium alcicorne, commonly known as the white orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems and clusters of small, white or pale green flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in its natural habitat. Its flowers bloom in a spiral pattern along the stem, creating an elegant and delicate appearance.

Oncidium alcicorne is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its beauty in both indoor and outdoor settings.
