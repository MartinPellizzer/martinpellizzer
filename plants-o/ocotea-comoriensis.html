Ocotea comoriensis

Ocotea comoriensis, commonly known as the Comorian ocotea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Comoros archipelago in the Indian Ocean.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage and bark. The plant typically grows in humid, tropical lowland forests, often in areas with rich, well-drained soil. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a glossy green surface with a distinct aromatic scent.

The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue, fleshy berries that are an important food source for local wildlife.
