Ocotea cryptocarpa

Ocotea cryptocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ocotea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in countries like Brazil, Colombia, and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its aromatic evergreen leaves and small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various animals.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
