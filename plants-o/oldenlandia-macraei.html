Oldenlandia macraei

Oldenlandia macraei, commonly known as the giant spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that consist of numerous small, white to purple flowers arranged in a spike-like cluster. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with long, slender stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Oldenlandia macraei is often found in moist, disturbed habitats, including along riverbanks and in open woodlands.
