Oxalis amamiana

Oxalis amamiana, commonly known as the velvetleaf wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces distinctive orange to red seed pods that split open to release its seeds. Oxalis amamiana thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
