Orthosiphon cleistocalyx

Orthosiphon cleistocalyx, commonly known as Java tea or malabar tea, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by cylindrical, aromatic fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.
