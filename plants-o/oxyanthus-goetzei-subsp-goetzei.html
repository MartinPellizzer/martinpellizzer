Oxyanthus goetzei subsp. goetzei

Oxyanthus goetzei subsp.

goetzei, commonly known as the Goetze's oxyanthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a shrub characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has a compact, bushy growth habit. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
