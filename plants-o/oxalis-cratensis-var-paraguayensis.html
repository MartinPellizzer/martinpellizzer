Oxalis cratensis var. paraguayensis

Oxalis cratensis var. paraguayensis, commonly known as the Paraguay wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped lobes and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow or orange. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
