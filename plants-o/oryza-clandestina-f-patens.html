Oryza clandestina f. patens

Oryza clandestina f. patens, commonly known as the hidden rice plant variant, is a rare and less-studied botanical form within the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and unique leaf morphology, which differ from the more common wild rice species. This form exhibits a distinct flowering pattern, with small, inconspicuous inflorescences that are often overlooked in natural settings. Its genetic makeup suggests a close relationship to other Oryza species, though its ecological role remains under investigation.

Due to its rarity and limited distribution, further research is needed to fully understand its botanical significance.
