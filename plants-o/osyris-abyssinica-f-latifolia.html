Osyris abyssinica f. latifolia

Osyris abyssinica f. latifolia, commonly known as the Abyssinian osyris, is a variant of the osyris plant native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the edges. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique foliage and ornamental appeal, it is occasionally cultivated as a decorative plant in tropical gardens.
