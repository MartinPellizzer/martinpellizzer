Olea atrorubens

Olea atrorubens, commonly known as the red-black olive, is a species of flowering plant in the Oleaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional cuisine. Its leaves are dark green and glossy, and the bark is typically grayish-brown with a rough texture.

Olea atrorubens is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in agriculture and herbal medicine.
