Orchis condensata

Orchis condensata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear dense clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, often with a hairy texture.

Orchis condensata is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils, making it a common sight in temperate regions of Europe and Asia.
