Orchis angustifolia subf. dissita

Orchis angustifolia subf.

dissita, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate, pale yellow to greenish flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or grassland habitats. It is a perennial herb that produces a single spike of flowers, often with a distinct scent to attract pollinators.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in many regions.
