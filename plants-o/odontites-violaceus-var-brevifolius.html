Odontites violaceus var. brevifolius

Odontites violaceus var. brevifolius, commonly known as the violet bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, violet-blue flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy surface, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a suitable plant for woodland gardens.

This variety is noted for its delicate beauty and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
