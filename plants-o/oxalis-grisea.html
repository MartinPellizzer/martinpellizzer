Oxalis grisea

Oxalis grisea, commonly known as the silverleaf wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive silvery-gray underside, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Oxalis grisea is known for its ornamental value and ability to spread through rhizomes.
