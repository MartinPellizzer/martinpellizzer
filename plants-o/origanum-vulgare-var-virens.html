Origanum vulgare var. virens

Origanum vulgare var. virens, commonly known as green oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale blue to purple flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, giving it a strong, pungent flavor used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Green oregano thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for herb gardens.
