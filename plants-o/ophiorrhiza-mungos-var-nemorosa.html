Ophiorrhiza mungos var. nemorosa

Ophiorrhiza mungos var. nemorosa, commonly known as the forest dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small shrub characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and swampy areas. Its fruit is a capsule that disperses seeds through wind, contributing to its spread in humid tropical regions.

The species is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
