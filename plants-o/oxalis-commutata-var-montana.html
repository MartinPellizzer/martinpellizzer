Oxalis commutata var. montana

Oxalis commutata var. montana, commonly known as mountain wood sorrel, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils, often in forested or open slopes. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in cooler, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
