Orthosiphon malosanus

Orthosiphon malosanus, commonly known as the small-leaved mintweed, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge, while the flowers are small and clustered in whorls.

Orthosiphon malosanus is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
