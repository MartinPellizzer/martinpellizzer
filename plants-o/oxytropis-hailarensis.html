Oxytropis hailarensis

Oxytropis hailarensis, commonly known as the Hailar locoweed, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems and small, compound leaves. The plant produces yellowish or pale pink flowers arranged in racemes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Oxytropis hailarensis is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in steppe and grassland ecosystems.

It is known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock.
