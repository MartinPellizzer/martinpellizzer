Oenothera longissima subsp. clutei

Oenothera longissima subsp.

clutei, commonly known as the Clute's evening primrose, is a species native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening and close by morning.

The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and open fields. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
