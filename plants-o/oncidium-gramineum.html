Oncidium gramineum

Oncidium gramineum, commonly known as the grass orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, grass-like leaves and clusters of fragrant, yellow-green flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Oncidium gramineum prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.

Its blooms can last several weeks, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
