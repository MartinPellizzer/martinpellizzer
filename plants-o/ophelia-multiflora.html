Ophelia multiflora

Ophelia multiflora, commonly known as water starwort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, floating foliage and small, white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its roots help stabilize aquatic sediments, making it an important species in aquatic ecosystems.

Ophelia multiflora is often used in water gardens and pond landscapes due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
