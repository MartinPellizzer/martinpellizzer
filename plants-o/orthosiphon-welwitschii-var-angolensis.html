Orthosiphon welwitschii var. angolensis

Orthosiphon welwitschii var. angolensis, commonly known as African mint or Angolan mint, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or violet in color. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks.

Its aromatic leaves and medicinal properties make it a valuable plant in traditional herbal medicine.
