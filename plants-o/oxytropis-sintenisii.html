Oxytropis sintenisii

Oxytropis sintenisii, commonly known as the Sintenis' locoweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open slopes.

Oxytropis sintenisii is known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock.
