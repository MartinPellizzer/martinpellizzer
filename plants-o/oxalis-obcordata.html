Oxalis obcordata

Oxalis obcordata, commonly known as the heartleaf wood sorrel, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of small, yellow, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. Its stems are often hairy, and it has a fibrous root system.

Oxalis obcordata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
