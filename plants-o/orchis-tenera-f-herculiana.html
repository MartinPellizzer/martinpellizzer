Orchis tenera f. herculiana

Orchis tenera f. herculiana, commonly known as the Hercules orchid, is a rare and distinctive orchid species native to certain regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, which helps retain moisture.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
