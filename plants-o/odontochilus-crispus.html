Odontochilus crispus

Odontochilus crispus, commonly known as the wrinkled toothed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, dark green leaves and its unique, wrinkled appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers with a distinctive toothed lip. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
