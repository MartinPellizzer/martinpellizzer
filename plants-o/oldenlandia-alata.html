Oldenlandia alata

Oldenlandia alata, commonly known as mile-a-minute weed, is a rapidly growing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces tiny, three-lobed leaves and is known for its aggressive growth habit, often forming dense mats that can smother other vegetation. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and forest edges.

Due to its invasive nature, it is considered a weed in many regions where it has been introduced.
