Origanum symes

Origanum symes, commonly known as Symes oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers in summer, which attract pollinators like bees. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its essential oils, rich in carvacrol and thymol, contribute to its use in culinary and medicinal applications.
