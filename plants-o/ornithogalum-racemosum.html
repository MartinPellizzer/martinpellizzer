Ornithogalum racemosum

Ornithogalum racemosum, commonly known as the clustered star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, branched stems bearing numerous white, star-shaped flowers. The flowers are typically arranged in a raceme, giving the plant its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
