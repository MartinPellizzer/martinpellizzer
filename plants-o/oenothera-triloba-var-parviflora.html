Oenothera triloba var. parviflora

Oenothera triloba var. parviflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its flowers are borne in clusters at the ends of the stems, and the plant is known for its showy, fragrant blooms.

Oenothera triloba var. parviflora is often found in dry, sandy soils in open woodlands and prairies.
