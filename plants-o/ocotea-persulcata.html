Ocotea persulcata

Ocotea persulcata, commonly known as the narrow-ribbed ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its bark is often used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.

Ocotea persulcata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
