Opuntia tunicata var. davisii

Opuntia tunicata var. davisii, commonly known as the Davis prickly pear, is a cactus species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in spines and glochids, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the summer months, followed by edible, fleshy fruits known as tunas. Its succulent structure allows it to store water efficiently, adapting to arid environments.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
