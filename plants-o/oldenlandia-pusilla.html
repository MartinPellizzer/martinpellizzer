Oldenlandia pusilla

Oldenlandia pusilla, commonly known as the tiny spiderwort, is a small herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. The plant features slender, creeping stems with small, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are tiny, white or pale blue, and are borne in clusters along the stems.

Oldenlandia pusilla is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed areas, making it both a pioneer species and a potential weed.
