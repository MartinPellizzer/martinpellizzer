Ophrys dyris var. maculata

Ophrys dyris var. maculata, commonly known as the spotted bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodland floors and meadows. Its flowers are usually a mix of brown and yellow, with distinctive spots that enhance its mimicry.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its intricate pollination mechanism and limited distribution.
