Ophrys squamata

Ophrys squamata, commonly known as the squamated bee orchid, is a species of orchid native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers with intricate patterns that resemble insect wings. It thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Ophrys squamata is a perennial herb that blooms from late spring to early summer.
