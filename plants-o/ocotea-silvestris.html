Ocotea silvestris

Ocotea silvestris, commonly known as the Brazilian cinnamon tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within the Atlantic Forest biome. Its leaves are elliptical with a glossy surface and emit a distinctive scent when crushed.

The tree is valued for its essential oils and has traditional uses in local medicine.
