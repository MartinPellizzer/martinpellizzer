Ochnella dekindtiana

Ochnella dekindtiana, commonly known as the De Kindt's ochna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, yellowish-orange flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Ochnella dekindtiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
