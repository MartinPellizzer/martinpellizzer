Ononis alopecuroides var. gattefossei

Ononis alopecuroides var. gattefossei, commonly known as the Gattefosse's sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in early summer. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit with hairy, succulent leaves that help it retain moisture. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to harsh, dry conditions.
