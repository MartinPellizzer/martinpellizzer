Oryza sativa var. sultanpurica

Oryza sativa var. sultanpurica, commonly known as Sultanpur rice, is a variety of rice native to India.

It is characterized by its short, slender grains and is typically cultivated in the Indo-Gangetic plains. This variety is well adapted to semi-arid conditions and has a shorter growing season compared to other rice types. It is valued for its high yield and resilience to drought.

The plant exhibits typical grass-like features with narrow leaves and culms, and it produces panicles that bear small, white flowers.
