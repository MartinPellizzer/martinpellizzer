Oxytropis yezoensis f. albiflora

Oxytropis yezoensis f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered yellow vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are white or pale yellow, clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences, and are borne on long pedicels.

This variety is distinguished by its reduced floral coloration compared to the typical form of Oxytropis yezoensis.
