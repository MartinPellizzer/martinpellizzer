Oxytropis ketmenica

Oxytropis ketmenica, commonly known as the Ketmenica milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It has compound leaves with small leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Oxytropis ketmenica is often found in steppe and desert regions, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
