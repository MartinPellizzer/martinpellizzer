Ocotea microneura

Ocotea microneura, commonly known as the small-veined ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in white or greenish-white colors. Its fruit is a small, woody drupe that contains a single seed.

Ocotea microneura is valued for its timber, which is often used in furniture and cabinetry due to its durability and fine grain.
