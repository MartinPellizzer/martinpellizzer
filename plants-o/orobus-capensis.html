Orobus capensis

Orobus capensis, commonly known as the Cape orobus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions. It has a climbing habit and can reach lengths of up to several meters.

Orobus capensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
