Oxystelma ovatum

Oxystelma ovatum, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Mexican bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in a fine, velvety texture. Its flowers are showy, with five petals that are often a mix of purple and white, and they bloom in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Oxystelma ovatum is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade.
