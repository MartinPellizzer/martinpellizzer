Origanum × majoricum

Origanum × majoricum, commonly known as Greek oregano, is a hybrid perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its small, pale purple to pink flowers and its pungent, aromatic leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates.

Its essential oils, rich in thymol and carvacrol, contribute to its use in both culinary and medicinal applications.
