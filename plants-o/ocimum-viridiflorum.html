Ocimum viridiflorum

Ocimum viridiflorum, commonly known as greenflower basil, is a species of flowering plant in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with square stems and opposite leaves that are often hairy. Its essential oils contain compounds like linalool and eugenol, which contribute to its fragrance and potential medicinal uses.

Ocimum viridiflorum is cultivated for both ornamental purposes and traditional herbal applications.
