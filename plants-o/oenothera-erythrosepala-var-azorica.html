Oenothera erythrosepala var. azorica

Oenothera erythrosepala var. azorica, commonly known as the Azorean evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the Azores.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

This variety is part of the Onagraceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
