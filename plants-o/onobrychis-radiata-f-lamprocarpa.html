Onobrychis radiata f. lamprocarpa

Onobrychis radiata f. lamprocarpa, commonly known as the silverleaf burclover, is a perennial legume characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage.

It features trifoliate leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes. The plant is native to the southwestern United States and thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. It has a deep root system that allows it to tolerate drought conditions.

This variety is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soils and fix nitrogen.
