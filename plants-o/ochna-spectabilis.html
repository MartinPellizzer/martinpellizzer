Ochna spectabilis

Ochna spectabilis, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow, bell-shaped flowers that appear in clusters during the summer months. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical or ovate in shape. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Ochna spectabilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
