Oxyotis sedoides

Oxyotis sedoides, commonly known as the yellow sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences.
