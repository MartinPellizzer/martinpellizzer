Oncidium pseudomelanthes

Oncidium pseudomelanthes, commonly known as the Black Orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, velvety petals and sepals, which give it its striking appearance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. It produces fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters during the wet season.

Oncidium pseudomelanthes requires high humidity and indirect light to thrive, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
