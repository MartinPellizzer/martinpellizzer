Opuntia californica var. parkeri

Opuntia californica var. parkeri, commonly known as the Parker's prickly pear, is a succulent cactus native to California.

It features flat, fleshy stems that are often covered in spines and glochids, which are small, barbed bristles. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tuna, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

This variety is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
