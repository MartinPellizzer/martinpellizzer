Oxalis biflora

Oxalis biflora, commonly known as the two-flowered wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive pattern of veins, and small, delicate flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces two flowers per stem, which are usually white or pale pink with five petals. Oxalis biflora thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
