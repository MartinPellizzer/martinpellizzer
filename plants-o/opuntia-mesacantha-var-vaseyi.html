Opuntia mesacantha var. vaseyi

Opuntia mesacantha var. vaseyi, commonly known as the Vasey's prickly pear, is a cactus species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in clusters of spines and glochids. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tuna, is edible and has a sweet, slightly tart flavor.

This variety is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
