Ocotea ramosissima

Ocotea ramosissima, commonly known as the fragrant ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant typically grows in humid lowland forests, reaching heights of up to 30 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped, glossy, and emit a pleasant scent when crushed.

The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue berries, which are an important food source for various wildlife.
