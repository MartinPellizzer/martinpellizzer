Orobus pellucidus

Orobus pellucidus, commonly known as the glassy orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and swampy areas. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and the flowers are usually pale green or yellow with a distinctive, glassy appearance.

Orobus pellucidus is noted for its unique morphology and is often found in association with other rare wetland plants.
