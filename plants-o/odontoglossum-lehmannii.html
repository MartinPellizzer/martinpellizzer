Odontoglossum lehmannii

Odontoglossum lehmannii, commonly known as the Lehmann's orchid, is a species native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb and long, strap-like leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, often with intricate patterns. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained soil.

Odontoglossum lehmannii is appreciated for its ornamental value and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts worldwide.
