Ocotea perseiphylla

Ocotea perseiphylla, commonly known as the Caribbean sand-laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurel, cinnamon, and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, oval drupe that contains a single seed.

Ocotea perseiphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
