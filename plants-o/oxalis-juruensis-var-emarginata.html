Oxalis juruensis var. emarginata

Oxalis juruensis var. emarginata, commonly known as the emarginate wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive notched base. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, often arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a unique and attractive specimen for botanical gardens and horticultural collections.
