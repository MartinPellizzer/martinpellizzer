Orobanche rhaetica

Orobanche rhaetica, commonly known as the Rhaetic broomrape, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on its host for nutrients.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is found in alpine and subalpine regions of Europe. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers and has a short, thick stem with few leaves. It parasitizes various species of grasses and herbs, often growing in rocky or disturbed soils.

Due to its specialized lifestyle, it plays a unique role in its ecological niche.
