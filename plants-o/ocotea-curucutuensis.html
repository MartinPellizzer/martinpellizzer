Ocotea curucutuensis

Ocotea curucutuensis, commonly known as the Curucutuense Ocotea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within the understory of tropical forests. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, often with a fragrant scent when crushed.

The fruit is a small, woody drupe that contains a single seed, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
