Olyra guineensis

Olyra guineensis, commonly known as the African breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and smooth, greyish bark. The tree produces edible fruits that are rich in carbohydrates and have a starchy texture similar to bread. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female inflorescences, and it relies on insects for pollination.

The plant is also valued for its timber and has cultural significance in several African communities.
