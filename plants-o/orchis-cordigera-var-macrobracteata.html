Orchis cordigera var. macrobracteata

Orchis cordigera var. macrobracteata, commonly known as the broad-bracted orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers are usually purple or violet, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination by insects.

The variety macrobracteata is distinguished by its broad, leaf-like bracts that surround the flower spike.
