Orchis angustifolia var. elongata

Orchis angustifolia var. elongata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces tall, erect flowering stems with a single spike of pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. Its flowers are characterized by a distinctive lip that is often broader than the other petals.

The species is known for its ecological importance, supporting various pollinators and serving as an indicator of undisturbed habitats.
