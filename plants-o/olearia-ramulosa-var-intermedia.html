Olearia ramulosa var. intermedia

Olearia ramulosa var. intermedia, commonly known as the coastal flax or silver flax, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas, characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and showy yellow flowers. The plant has finely divided leaves that are covered in a dense layer of silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

This variety is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal conditions.
