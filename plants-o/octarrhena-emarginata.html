Octarrhena emarginata

Octarrhena emarginata, commonly known as the African sandmat, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny stems and broad, lobed leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant.
