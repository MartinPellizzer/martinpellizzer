Ocotea mollivillosa

Ocotea mollivillosa, commonly known as the silky ocotea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests, often reaching heights of up to 15 meters. Its leaves are leathery, dark green, and covered with a fine, silky pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers are small and greenish-white, and the fruit is a small, round drupe that ripens to a dark purple or black color.
