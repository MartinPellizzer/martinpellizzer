Orchis caucasica f. aserica

Orchis caucasica f. aserica, commonly known as the Caucasian twayblade, is a rare orchid species native to the Caucasus region.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a distinctive appearance, featuring a slender, leafless stem and a single, showy flower. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish in color, with a unique structure that includes a spurred lip.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
