Oberonia spiralis

Oberonia spiralis, commonly known as the spiral orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique spiral arrangement of leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in a raceme, often blooming during the rainy season. Oberonia spiralis thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
