Olea europaea var. amygdalina

Olea europaea var. amygdalina, commonly known as the Persian lilac, is a cultivar of the olive tree characterized by its fragrant, pale yellow flowers.

It is native to the Middle East and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties. The plant produces small, edible fruits similar to olives, though they are typically not consumed by humans. Its leaves are evergreen, oval-shaped, and have a slightly serrated edge.

The tree is relatively hardy and thrives in well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens in temperate climates.
