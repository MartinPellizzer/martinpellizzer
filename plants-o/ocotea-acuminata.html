Ocotea acuminata

Ocotea acuminata, commonly known as the fragrant ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage and bark. The plant typically grows in humid, lowland forests and can reach heights of up to 30 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and emit a pleasant scent when crushed.

Ocotea acuminata is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
