Ortgiesia lindenii var. makoyana

Ortgiesia lindenii var. makoyana, commonly known as the Makoyana orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, erect stems and clustered, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
