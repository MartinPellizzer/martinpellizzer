Orchis latifolia subsp. durandii

Orchis latifolia subsp.

durandii, commonly known as the broad-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and tall, slender flowering stems.

The plant produces spikes of pale yellow to greenish-white flowers, which are often adorned with dark purple or brown markings. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is typically found in woodland edges and meadows.

Due to its specialized habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in many regions.
