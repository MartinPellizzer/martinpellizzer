Odontoglossum luteopurpureum var. cuspidatum

Odontoglossum luteopurpureum var. cuspidatum, commonly known as the purple-tinged toothed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru. The plant features vibrant, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of purple and yellow. Its flowers are typically arranged in loose inflorescences and bloom during the cooler months.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
