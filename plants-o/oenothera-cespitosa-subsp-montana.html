Oenothera cespitosa subsp. montana

Oenothera cespitosa subsp.

montana, commonly known as mountain evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a herbaceous perennial with showy yellow flowers that bloom in the evening.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

The species is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
