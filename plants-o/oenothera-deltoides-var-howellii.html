Oenothera deltoides var. howellii

Oenothera deltoides var. howellii, commonly known as Howell's evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has hairy, linear leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in desert or arid environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
