Oreophilus chilopsis

Oreophilus chilopsis, commonly known as the Chilean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, often covered in a fine layer of white hair.

Oreophilus chilopsis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
