Ophrys × muellneri

Ophrys × muellneri, commonly known as the Müller's bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the genus Ophrys, which is characterized by its mimicry of female insects to attract male pollinators. The plant produces showy, bee-like flowers with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of female bees, a form of sexual deception. It typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats and thrives in temperate climates.

The hybrid nature of Ophrys × muellneri results from crossbreeding between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera, leading to unique floral characteristics.
