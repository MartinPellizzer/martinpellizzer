Ophrys × perillii

Ophrys × perillii, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is characterized by its intricate flower structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. This mimicry is a key botanical feature, showcasing the plant's evolutionary adaptation for reproductive success. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in woodland or meadow habitats.

Its flowers are usually a mix of brown and yellow, with a distinctive beard-like structure at the base of each bloom.
