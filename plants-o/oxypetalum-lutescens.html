Oxypetalum lutescens

Oxypetalum lutescens, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow petals and bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are opposite and oval, with a glossy surface.

Oxypetalum lutescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
