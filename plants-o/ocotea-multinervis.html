Ocotea multinervis

Ocotea multinervis, commonly known as the broad-leafed ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurel and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves with prominent veins. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue berries.

Ocotea multinervis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
