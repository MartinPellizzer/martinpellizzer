Onoseris glandulosa

Onoseris glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and rough, hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows. Its leaves are often glandular and can emit a strong, sometimes unpleasant odor.

Onoseris glandulosa is known for its yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.
