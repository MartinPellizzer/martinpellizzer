Olea emarginata

Olea emarginata, commonly known as the Canary Island olive, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often grayish-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically followed by small, oval-shaped fruits. Olea emarginata thrives in coastal areas and is well adapted to arid and saline environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh conditions.
