Orchis bifolia var. brachyglossa

Orchis bifolia var. brachyglossa, commonly known as the short-lipped two-lobed orchid, is a perennial herb native to specific regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive two-lobed flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or grassland habitats. Its leaves are linear and often have a slight sheen, while the flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color.

The species is known for its specialized pollination mechanisms, attracting specific insect pollinators.
