Oreanthus bracteatus

Oreanthus bracteatus, commonly known as the bracteate orchid, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, supporting its growth in tropical and subtropical environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a compact rosette, while the inflorescence bears a cluster of fragrant, brightly colored blooms.

Oreanthus bracteatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in botanical gardens and private collections.
