Orchis maculata var. broteri

Orchis maculata var. broteri, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking purple and white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its flowers are borne on a spike.

The species is known for its ecological importance, supporting a variety of pollinators and serving as an indicator of healthy, undisturbed ecosystems.
