Opuntia valida

Opuntia valida, commonly known as the broadleaf prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in sharp spines and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant produces edible fruits known as tunas, which are often consumed fresh or processed into jams and wines. Opuntia valida is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in desert ecosystems.
