Oxalis odorata

Oxalis odorata, commonly known as sweet violet, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds. Oxalis odorata thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its fragrant flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
