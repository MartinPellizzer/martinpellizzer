Ophiopogon siamensis

Ophiopogon siamensis, commonly known as Siam sedum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually bluish-green in color. It produces small, inconspicuous white or pale yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months.

Ophiopogon siamensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and low maintenance requirements.
