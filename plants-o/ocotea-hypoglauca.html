Ocotea hypoglauca

Ocotea hypoglauca, commonly known as the golden ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark-brown bark and lanceolate, glossy green leaves. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue, berry-like fruits.

Ocotea hypoglauca is often found in humid lowland forests and is valued for its aromatic wood and ecological significance.
