Origanum × hybridum

Origanum × hybridum, commonly known as Greek oregano, is a hybrid perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves and small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is widely cultivated for its pungent, fragrant leaves used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy and versatile garden plant.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for both ornamental and practical use in horticulture.
