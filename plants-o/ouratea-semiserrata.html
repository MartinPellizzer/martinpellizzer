Ouratea semiserrata

Ouratea semiserrata, commonly known as the Brazilian holly or the red holly, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Venezuela. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are usually elliptical and have a serrated margin. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in terminal umbels, followed by dark blue, fleshy berries.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
