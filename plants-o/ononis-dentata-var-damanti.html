Ononis dentata var. damanti

Ononis dentata var. damanti, commonly known as the Damanti's toothcup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, erect stems that bear small, pinkish-white flowers arranged in clusters. The plant has finely toothed, ovate leaves that are often covered in a soft, woolly pubescence. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Leguminosae family.
