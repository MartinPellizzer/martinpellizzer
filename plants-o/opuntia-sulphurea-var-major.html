Opuntia sulphurea var. major

Opuntia sulphurea var. major, commonly known as the yellow prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, flattened stems that are typically yellow to green in color, often with a waxy coating. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruits, known as tunas, are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor.

Opuntia sulphurea var. major is well adapted to arid environments and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
