Opizia bracteata

Opizia bracteata, commonly known as the silverleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink or purple. The plant has palmate leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
