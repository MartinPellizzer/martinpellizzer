Ocimum minus

Ocimum minus, commonly known as dwarf basil, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and lavender. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant leaves and clusters of white to purple flowers. It is often cultivated for its culinary and medicinal uses, particularly for its essential oils.

Ocimum minus thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
