Orobanche tuberosa f. inundata

Orobanche tuberosa f. inundata, commonly known as the water bladderflower, is a parasitic plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies on host plants for nutrients.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is characterized by its swollen, fleshy stems and small, often inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as along stream banks or in wet meadows. Its unique morphology includes inflated, bladder-like structures that aid in water retention.

Due to its parasitic nature, it plays a specialized role in its ecosystem, influencing the growth and distribution of its host plants.
