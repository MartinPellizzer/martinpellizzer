Ocotea clarkei

Ocotea clarkei, commonly known as the Clarke's ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed, and it is often found in humid, lowland forests.

The tree is valued for its aromatic wood and has some traditional medicinal uses in its native regions.
