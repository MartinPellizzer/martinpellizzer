Ornithogalum hexapterum

Ornithogalum hexapterum, commonly known as the six-winged star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers with six petals, giving it its common name. The plant belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental appearance and adaptability to various soil types. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
