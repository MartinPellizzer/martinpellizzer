Odontoglossum digitatum

Odontoglossum digitatum, commonly known as the spurred orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces tall, upright stems with showy, fragrant flowers. The plant features a pseudobulb structure, which stores water and nutrients, and its leaves are typically strap-shaped and arranged in a rosette. The flowers are characterized by their distinctive spurred petals and a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and pink.

It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
