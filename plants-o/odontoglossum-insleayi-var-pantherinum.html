Odontoglossum insleayi var. pantherinum

Odontoglossum insleayi var. pantherinum, commonly known as the Panther Orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers that resemble the patterns of a panther's skin, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in high-altitude cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are usually broad and elliptical, and it produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
