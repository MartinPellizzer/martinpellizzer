Ocimum cyclophyllum

Ocimum cyclophyllum, commonly known as the cyclophyll rosemary, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their essential oils. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive texture. Its small, tubular flowers are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stems.

Ocimum cyclophyllum is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
