Ocimum selloi var. carnosum

Ocimum selloi var. carnosum, commonly known as the woolly basil, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive, aromatic appearance. The plant produces small, purple flowers that are typically clustered in terminal spikes. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its fragrant foliage and potential medicinal properties.

Its botanical features make it a unique and valuable species within the Lamiaceae family.
