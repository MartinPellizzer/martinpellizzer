Ornithogalum aciphyllum

Ornithogalum aciphyllum, commonly known as the slender star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers during the summer months. The plant has a tuberous root system that allows it to store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in arid conditions. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
