Orthosiphon linearis

Orthosiphon linearis, commonly known as Java tea or Malabar tea, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and small, pale blue or white flowers. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments. It is commonly cultivated for its aromatic leaves, which are used in traditional herbal remedies.

The plant's botanical features make it well-suited for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
