Osmanthus ilicifolius var. aureomarginatus

Osmanthus ilicifolius var. aureomarginatus, commonly known as the golden-margin osmanthus, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that have a distinctive golden-yellow margin. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which bloom in late autumn. These flowers are highly valued for their sweet, honey-like scent, often used in perfumery and traditional Chinese medicine.

The plant is also cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and seasonal flowering.
