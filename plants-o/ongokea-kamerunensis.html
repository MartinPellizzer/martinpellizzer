Ongokea kamerunensis

Ongokea kamerunensis, commonly known as the Cameroon flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Cameroon and parts of Central Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, bright red flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a broad, umbrella-shaped canopy. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several leaflets, and it produces pods that contain seeds used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitat.
