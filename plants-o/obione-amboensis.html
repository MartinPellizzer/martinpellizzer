Obione amboensis

Obione amboensis, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a rare and endemic species found exclusively in the rainforests of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, intricate floral structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, anchoring itself to tree bark while drawing moisture and nutrients from the air. Its flowers are usually small, with a complex arrangement of petals and sepals, often displaying vibrant colors such as pink or purple.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Obione amboensis is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
