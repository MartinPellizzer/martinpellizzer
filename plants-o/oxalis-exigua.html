Oxalis exigua

Oxalis exigua, commonly known as the small wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves composed of three heart-shaped leaflets, often with a reddish or purple tint. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically yellow or orange with five petals. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Oxalis exigua is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to spread via rhizomes.
