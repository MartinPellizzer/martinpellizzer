Orthosiphon ellipticus

Orthosiphon ellipticus, commonly known as Java tea or Malabar nettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, elliptic-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in whorled clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Orthosiphon ellipticus is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional medicine for its diuretic and anti-inflammatory effects.
