Onosma tricerosperma subsp. mauretanica

Onosma tricerosperma subsp.

mauretanica, commonly known as the Mauretanian thimbleweed, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of North Africa. It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple.

The plant has finely divided, linear leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in dry gardens.
