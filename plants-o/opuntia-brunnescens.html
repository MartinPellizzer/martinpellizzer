Opuntia brunnescens

Opuntia brunnescens, commonly known as the brown prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems that are typically brown to gray in color. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months, followed by edible, red-fleshed fruits. Opuntia brunnescens is adapted to arid environments and has a shallow root system that allows it to thrive in sandy soils.

It is also known for its spines, which provide protection from herbivores.
