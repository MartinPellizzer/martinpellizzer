Oleandra baetae

Oleandra baetae, commonly known as the Baeta's oleander, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of white, yellow, and pink. The plant is evergreen, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in Mediterranean climates.

Oleandra baetae is also noted for its ability to tolerate coastal conditions and salty winds.
