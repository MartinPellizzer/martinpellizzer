Oenothera pubens

Oenothera pubens, commonly known as the sticky evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and showy yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oily seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Oenothera pubens is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for nocturnal pollinators.
