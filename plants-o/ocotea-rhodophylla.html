Ocotea rhodophylla

Ocotea rhodophylla, commonly known as the red-leaf ocotea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its glossy, reddish-brown leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and medicinal properties.

Ocotea rhodophylla is often found in humid, lowland forests and is valued for its ecological and potential pharmaceutical significance.
