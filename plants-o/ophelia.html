Ophelia

Ophelia, commonly known as water crowfoot, is a aquatic plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its feathery, floating leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize the sediment in aquatic ecosystems.

Ophelia plays an important role in water filtration and provides habitat for various aquatic organisms.
