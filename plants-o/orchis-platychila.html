Orchis platychila

Orchis platychila, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and a single, showy, violet or purple flower. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it relies on specific pollinators for reproduction.

Orchis platychila is also known for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
