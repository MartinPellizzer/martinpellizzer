Opuntia phaeacantha var. flavispina

Opuntia phaeacantha var. flavispina, commonly known as the yellow-spined prickly pear, is a cacti species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in spines, with the distinctive yellow coloration of its glochids. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit, known as a tunas, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and hardy nature.
