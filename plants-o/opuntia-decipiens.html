Opuntia decipiens

Opuntia decipiens, commonly known as the false prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems that are covered in small, spiny tubercles. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits. Opuntia decipiens is a succulent plant that stores water in its thick stems, allowing it to survive in arid environments.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance.
