Ourisianthus caeruleus

Ourisianthus caeruleus, commonly known as the blue hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet bracts that surround its small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture with a distinct midrib.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, long-lasting floral display.
