Oberonia dubia

Oberonia dubia, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pendulous flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and green. The plant grows as an epiphyte, often found on trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Oberonia dubia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
