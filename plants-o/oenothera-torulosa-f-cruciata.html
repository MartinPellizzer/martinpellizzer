Oenothera torulosa f. cruciata

Oenothera torulosa f. cruciata, commonly known as the cruciform evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive cruciform (cross-shaped) flowers, which are typically yellow and open in the evening. The plant has hairy, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is part of the Onagraceae family and is noted for its ecological importance to pollinators such as moths and bats.
