Odontoglossum williamsianum

Odontoglossum williamsianum, commonly known as the Williams' orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It is a herbaceous plant with a sympodial growth habit, producing pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers. The plant features elongated, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and purple. Its inflorescence is typically a raceme with numerous blooms, and it blooms during the rainy season.

Odontoglossum williamsianum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
