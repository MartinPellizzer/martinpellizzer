Orchis × chevallieriana

Orchis × chevallieriana, commonly known as the Chevallier's orchid, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Orchis morio and Orchis mascula.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and a cluster of showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive lip. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, showcasing its ornamental value in gardens. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Due to its hybrid nature, it is often cultivated for its unique floral display and genetic diversity.
