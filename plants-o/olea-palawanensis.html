Olea palawanensis

Olea palawanensis, commonly known as the Palawan olive, is a small evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue to black berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
