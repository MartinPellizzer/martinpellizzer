Ouratea ramosissima

Ouratea ramosissima, commonly known as the Florida ouratea, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows in wetlands and moist woodlands. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces white or pale pink flowers in clusters. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that splits open to release seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
