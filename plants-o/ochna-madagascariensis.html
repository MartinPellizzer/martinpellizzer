Ochna madagascariensis

Ochna madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar ochna, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that contain seeds surrounded by a fleshy aril. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Ochna madagascariensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has cultural significance in its native region.
