Orchis maculata subsp. incarnata

Orchis maculata subsp.

incarnata, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland and meadow habitats.

The plant produces showy, spurred orchid flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor environments.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive spotted leaves and intricate floral structure.
