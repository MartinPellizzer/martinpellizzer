Ocotea felix

Ocotea felix, commonly known as the golden sandarac tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage and bark. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments within tropical rainforests. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, often with a distinct scent when crushed.

The tree produces small, fragrant flowers followed by oval-shaped berries that are an important food source for various wildlife.
