Oxalis lanceolata

Oxalis lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically narrow and lance-shaped, giving it its species name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Oxalis lanceolata is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
