Oldenlandia inconstans

Oldenlandia inconstans, commonly known as the wandering begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its name, "inconstans," refers to its tendency to be found in variable habitats, often shifting with environmental conditions.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can thrive in containers or as a ground cover in suitable climates.
