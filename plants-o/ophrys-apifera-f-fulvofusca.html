Ophrys apifera f. fulvofusca

Ophrys apifera f. fulvofusca, commonly known as the bee orchid, is a species of orchid characterized by its unique floral structure that mimics the appearance of a female bee.

This mimicry is a key adaptation for attracting male bees, which attempt to mate with the flower, thereby facilitating pollination. The plant is native to parts of Europe and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its flowers are typically yellowish-brown with dark markings, and the leaves are broad and grass-like.

The species is notable for its specialized pollination mechanism and its role in supporting pollinator populations.
