Oeceoclades lindleyi

Oeceoclades lindleyi, commonly known as the Lindley's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to tropical regions of South America.

It features a slender, wiry stem with numerous nodes and is characterized by its linear, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, typically arranged in a loose inflorescence. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining substrates for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in greenhouses and botanical gardens.
