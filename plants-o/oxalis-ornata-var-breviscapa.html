Oxalis ornata var. breviscapa

Oxalis ornata var. breviscapa, commonly known as the short-stemmed wood sorrel, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish or purplish tint, and small, delicate flowers that bloom in spring. The plant prefers partial to full shade and well-drained, moist soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens. It is known for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

Oxalis ornata var. breviscapa is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
