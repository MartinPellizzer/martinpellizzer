Ocotea minutiflora

Ocotea minutiflora, commonly known as the small-flowered ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen foliage. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests. Its small, white flowers are followed by dark, spherical berries that are an important food source for various wildlife.

Ocotea minutiflora is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
