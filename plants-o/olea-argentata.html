Olea argentata

Olea argentata, commonly known as silver olive, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by small, spherical fruits. Olea argentata is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
