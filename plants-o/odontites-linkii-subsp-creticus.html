Odontites linkii subsp. creticus

Odontites linkii subsp.

creticus, commonly known as the Cretan toothed grass, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region, particularly found in Greece. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy.

The plant produces delicate, greenish-white flowers arranged in spikelets, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or calcareous habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in some areas.
