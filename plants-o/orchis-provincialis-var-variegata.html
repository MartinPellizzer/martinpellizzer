Orchis provincialis var. variegata

Orchis provincialis var. variegata, commonly known as the Variegated Provincial Orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to specific regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaf patterns, which often feature contrasting colors such as green and white. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodlands or along riverbanks. Its flowering stems bear a single spike of pale purple or white flowers, which bloom in late spring.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, this orchid is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
