Oxyspora paniculata var. glandulosa

Oxyspora paniculata var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular oxyspora, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and produces panicles of small, white to pale pink flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped with a glandular surface, giving the plant its varietal name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry grasslands or open woodlands.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
