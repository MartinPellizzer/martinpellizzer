Onobrychis pallasii var. aurasiaca

Onobrychis pallasii var. aurasiaca, commonly known as the golden sainfoin, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in steppe and grassland habitats. It has been used historically for forage and soil stabilization due to its hardy nature.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
