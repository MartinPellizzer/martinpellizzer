Ocimum petiolare

Ocimum petiolare, commonly known as holy basil or tulsi, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or light purple in color. Its leaves are aromatic, with a distinct scent due to the presence of essential oils.

Ocimum petiolare is widely cultivated for its medicinal and culinary uses.
