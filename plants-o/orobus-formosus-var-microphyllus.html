Orobus formosus var. microphyllus

Orobus formosus var. microphyllus, commonly known as the small-leaved orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

This variant is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
