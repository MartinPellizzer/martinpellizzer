Oxytropis schachimardanica

Oxytropis schachimardanica, commonly known as the Schachimardan locoweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, compound leaves. The plant produces yellow or pale purple flowers in spikes, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often avoided by livestock and wildlife.
