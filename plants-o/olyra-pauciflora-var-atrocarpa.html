Olyra pauciflora var. atrocarpa

Olyra pauciflora var. atrocarpa, commonly known as the black-fruited olyra, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, dark purple to black berries that are clustered along the stems. The plant has small, ovate leaves with a rough texture and a slightly aromatic scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its striking fruit color and compact growth habit.
