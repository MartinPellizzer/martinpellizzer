Oxalis phaeotricha var. glabra

Oxalis phaeotricha var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive white-veined pattern and produces small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to purple. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens. Its botanical characteristics include hairy stems that become smooth with age, giving rise to its varietal name "glabra.".

It is often found in shaded, moist habitats such as stream banks and forest floors.
