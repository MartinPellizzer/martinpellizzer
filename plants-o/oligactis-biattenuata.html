Oligactis biattenuata

Oligactis biattenuata, commonly known as the slender-leafed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale yellow. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in forest understories where it attaches itself to tree trunks or branches. Its roots form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi, aiding in nutrient absorption from the soil.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
