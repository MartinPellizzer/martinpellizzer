Osbeckia stellata var. marginulata

Osbeckia stellata var. marginulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved starweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, star-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, with a distinct white or pale green margin. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators and contributing to biodiversity in its native range.
