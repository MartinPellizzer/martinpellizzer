Oenocarpus huebneri

Oenocarpus huebneri, commonly known as the breadnut or oca, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces large, fleshy fruits that contain a single, large seed, which is the primary edible part. The seeds are rich in nutrients and have been used for food and traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
