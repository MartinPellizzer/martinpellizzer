Oxalis incana

Oxalis incana, commonly known as the hairy wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. Oxalis incana is often found in woodland areas and thrives in partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
