Onopordum tauricum var. simplex

Onopordum tauricum var. simplex, commonly known as the Tauric thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Black Sea region.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flower heads that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.

Its ornamental value has also led to its cultivation in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
