Oxalis massoniana var. flavescens

Oxalis massoniana var. flavescens, commonly known as the golden wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive yellowish-green hue and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink to white. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for shady garden spaces.
