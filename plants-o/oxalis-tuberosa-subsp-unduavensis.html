Oxalis tuberosa subsp. unduavensis

Oxalis tuberosa subsp.

unduavensis, commonly known as the Andean wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial native to the high-altitude regions of South America. It features heart-shaped leaves with intricate patterns and produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers.

The plant is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to grow in rocky or well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
