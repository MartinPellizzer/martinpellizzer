Oncidium halteratum

Oncidium halteratum, commonly known as the yellow butterfly orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow and green flowers that resemble butterflies, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are known for their long-lasting beauty and fragrance.

Oncidium halteratum requires bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.
