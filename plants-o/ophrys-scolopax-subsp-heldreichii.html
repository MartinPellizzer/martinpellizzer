It is characterized by its intricate floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee, a feature evolved to attract male bees for pollination.

The plant produces a single, showy flower with a distinctive yellow and brown coloration, and it thrives in calcareous soils. It is a perennial herb with a short rhizome and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
