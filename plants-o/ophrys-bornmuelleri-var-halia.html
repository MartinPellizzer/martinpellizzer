Ophrys bornmuelleri var. halia

Ophrys bornmuelleri var. halia, commonly known as the Halia bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in temperate, humid climates. Its flowers are usually a striking combination of yellow and brown, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
