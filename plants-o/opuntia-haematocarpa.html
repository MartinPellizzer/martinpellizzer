Opuntia haematocarpa

Opuntia haematocarpa, commonly known as the blood-flesh cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-like stems that are ribbed and often covered in spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible. Its succulent stems store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

Opuntia haematocarpa is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used by indigenous peoples for various purposes.
