Opuntia linguiformis

Opuntia linguiformis, commonly known as the narrow-leafed prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems that are covered in spines and are often ribbed. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tunah, is edible and has a sweet, juicy flavor.

Opuntia linguiformis is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water to survive.
