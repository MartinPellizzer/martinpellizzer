Oncidium nigratum

Oncidium nigratum, commonly known as the black orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, velvety petals and sepals, which give it its distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually fragrant and bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental value.

Oncidium nigratum requires well-drained soil and consistent moisture to thrive.
