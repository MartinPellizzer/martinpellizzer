Ocotea sodiroana

Ocotea sodiroana, commonly known as the Brazilian lauraceae, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small drupes that turn from green to yellow or red as they mature.

Ocotea sodiroana is valued for its aromatic wood and is often found in humid, lowland forests.
