Orthochilus ovalis var. bainesii

Orthochilus ovalis var. bainesii, commonly known as the Baines' oval orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in late spring. The plant features a rhizomatous growth habit, with narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense mat. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with a distinctive hooded lip.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded, moist environments.
