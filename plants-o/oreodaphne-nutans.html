Oreodaphne nutans

Oreodaphne nutans, commonly known as the nodding oreodaphne, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in cyathia, a feature typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that split open to release seeds.

Oreodaphne nutans is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental foliage and potential medicinal properties.
