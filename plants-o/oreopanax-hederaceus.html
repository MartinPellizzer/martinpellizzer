Oreopanax hederaceus

Oreopanax hederaceus, commonly known as the ivy-like arrowhead plant, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green, with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-yellow in color, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its foliage is popular in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to trail or climb.
