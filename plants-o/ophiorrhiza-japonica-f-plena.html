Ophiorrhiza japonica f. plena

Ophiorrhiza japonica f. plena, commonly known as the Japanese velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that are typically pink or white in color. The plant has a compact growth habit with dark green, glossy leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forests or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
