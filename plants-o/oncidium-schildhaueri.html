Oncidium schildhaueri

Oncidium schildhaueri, commonly known as the Schildhauer's orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous inflorescence and vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its roots are aerial, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Oncidium schildhaueri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and relatively ease of care in suitable conditions.
