Oxalis tetraphylla f. triphylla

Oxalis tetraphylla f. triphylla, commonly known as the three-leaved wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are heart-shaped and often have a reddish or purple hue. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The botanical features of this plant make it a popular choice for native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
