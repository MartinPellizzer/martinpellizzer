Oxalis laburnifolia var. latifolia

Oxalis laburnifolia var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped lobes and bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in partial to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soil. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and stems that often root at the nodes.

It is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and early blooming habit.
