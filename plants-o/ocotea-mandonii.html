Ocotea mandonii

Ocotea mandonii, commonly known as the African ocotea, is a flowering plant species native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are typically elliptical and glossy. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, dark blue berries.

Ocotea mandonii is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along riverbanks.
