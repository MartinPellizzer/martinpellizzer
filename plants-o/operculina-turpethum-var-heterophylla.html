Operculina turpethum var. heterophylla

Operculina turpethum var. heterophylla, commonly known as the Indian mullein, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of India and Southeast Asia.

It features a robust, branched stem that can grow up to 2 meters in height, covered with soft, velvety hairs. The leaves are large, lance-shaped, and often have a fuzzy texture, while the flowers are yellow and clustered in terminal spikes. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, black seeds.

It is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forests and along stream banks.
