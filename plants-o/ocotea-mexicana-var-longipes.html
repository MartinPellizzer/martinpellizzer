Ocotea mexicana var. longipes

Ocotea mexicana var. longipes, commonly known as the long-peduncled ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that appear in clusters. Its fruits are small, dark berries that contain seeds, and the tree is often found in moist, shaded forest habitats.

The species is valued for its timber and has some traditional medicinal uses among indigenous communities.
