Oenothera sackvillensis

Oenothera sackvillensis, commonly known as the Sackvill's evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds. It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Oenothera sackvillensis is part of the Onagraceae family, which includes other species of evening primroses.
