Ocotea strigosa

Ocotea strigosa, commonly known as the rough-leafed ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocado, and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that are covered in a fine, hairy texture. Ocotea strigosa typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests.

Its wood is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional crafts and as a source of essential oils.
