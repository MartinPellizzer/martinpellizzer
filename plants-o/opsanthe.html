Opsanthe

Opsanthe, commonly known as the "spider plant," is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of sword-shaped, grass-like leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or green flowers that are followed by clusters of red berries. It is a hardy, low-maintenance plant that thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Opsanthe is popular as a houseplant due to its ability to purify the air and its ease of care.
