Ornithogalum kurdicum

Ornithogalum kurdicum, commonly known as Kurdish star of Bethlehem, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and Iran.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its striking white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, black berries following flowering. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ornithogalum kurdicum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some regions.
