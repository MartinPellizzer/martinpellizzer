Orchis recurva

Orchis recurva, commonly known as the bent orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, recurved petals and sepals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer, are usually pale purple or white.

Orchis recurva is notable for its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitats.
