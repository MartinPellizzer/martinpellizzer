Ornithogalum saginatum

Ornithogalum saginatum, commonly known as the marsh cowpea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, featuring long, narrow leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, rounded berries that contain the seeds. It is often found in wetlands, riverbanks, and other moist environments.

Ornithogalum saginatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
