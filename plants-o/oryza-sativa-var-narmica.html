Oryza sativa var. narmica

Oryza sativa var. narmica, commonly known as Narmada rice, is a traditional rice variety native to the Indian state of Madhya Pradesh.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated grains and a distinct nutty aroma. This variety thrives in the unique climatic conditions of the Narmada Valley, requiring a warm and humid environment for optimal growth. Narmada rice is typically cultivated in small-scale farms and is valued for its rich flavor and nutritional content.

Due to its specific growing requirements, it is considered a rare and heritage crop.
