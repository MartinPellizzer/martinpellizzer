Origanum serpylloides

Origanum serpylloides, commonly known as sweet marjoram, is a flowering plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in dry, rocky soils. The plant features slender, aromatic stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Sweet marjoram is valued for its essential oils, which are used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
