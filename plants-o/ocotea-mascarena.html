Ocotea mascarena

Ocotea mascarena, commonly known as Mascarene ocotea, is a small evergreen tree native to the Mascarene Islands in the Indian Ocean.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in axillary inflorescences, and it produces dark, spherical berries.

The tree is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional crafts and as a source of essential oils.
