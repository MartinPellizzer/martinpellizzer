Ornithogalum acutum

Ornithogalum acutum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of white, star-shaped flowers during the spring and summer seasons. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its elegant appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Ornithogalum acutum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
