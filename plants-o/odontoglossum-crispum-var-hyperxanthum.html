Odontoglossum crispum var. hyperxanthum

Odontoglossum crispum var. hyperxanthum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers with intricate patterns and a compact growth habit. The plant typically produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, supporting its growth in cooler, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

This variety is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its vibrant color and ornamental value.
