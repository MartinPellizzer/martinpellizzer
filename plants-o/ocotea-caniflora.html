Ocotea caniflora

Ocotea caniflora, commonly known as the sharp-leaved ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. Its fruit is a drupe, typically containing a single seed, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Ocotea caniflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
