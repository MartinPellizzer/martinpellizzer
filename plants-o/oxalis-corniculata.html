Oxalis corniculata

Oxalis corniculata, commonly known as the wood sorrel, is a flowering plant belonging to the Oxalidaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, yellow flowers that resemble those of the buttercup. The plant is native to Asia, Africa, and Australia, and it thrives in a variety of soil types and light conditions. Oxalis corniculata is often found in gardens and disturbed soils, and it is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread easily.

Its foliage contains oxalic acid, which can be toxic if ingested in large quantities.
