Ophrys fusca subsp. maghrebiaca

Ophrys fusca subsp.

maghrebiaca, commonly known as the Maghreb bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to North Africa. It is characterized by its intricate floral structure, which closely resembles a female bee, a feature evolved to mimic pollinators through a process called sexual deception.

The plant produces a single, showy flower with a dark brown to black coloration, often with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry. It thrives in rocky and calcareous soils, typically in open habitats such as cliffs and slopes.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, this subspecies is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
