Olearia minor

Olearia minor, commonly known as the small daisy bush, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowering period occurs during the spring and summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Olearia minor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural gardens.
