Oxytropis saperlebulensis

Oxytropis saperlebulensis, commonly known as the Saperlebulus locust bean, is a perennial legume native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, which are followed by pods containing edible, bean-like seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

Oxytropis saperlebulensis is valued for its nutritional content and traditional use in local cuisines.
