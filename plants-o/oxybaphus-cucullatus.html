Oxybaphus cucullatus

Oxybaphus cucullatus, commonly known as the hooded bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, with a distinctive hood-like structure over the throat. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Oxybaphus cucullatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
