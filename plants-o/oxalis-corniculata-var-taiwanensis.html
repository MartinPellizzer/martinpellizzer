Oxalis corniculata var. taiwanensis

Oxalis corniculata var. taiwanensis, commonly known as the Taiwan wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the family Oxalidaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shady, moist environments such as forest floors and stream banks. It produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant.
