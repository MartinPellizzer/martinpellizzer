Orchis angustifolia var. erecta

Orchis angustifolia var. erecta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces spikes of pale yellow to greenish-white flowers, which are typically arranged in a loose raceme. It thrives in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows.

Orchis angustifolia var. erecta is a perennial herb that plays a role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining biodiversity in its native ecosystems.
