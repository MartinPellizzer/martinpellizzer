Oenanthe thomsonii subsp. thomsonii

Oenanthe thomsonii subsp.

thomsonii, commonly known as Thomson's water dropwort, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forested areas.

The plant features slender, erect stems and small, white or greenish-white flowers arranged in umbels. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinct, waxy coating.

The species is characterized by its ability to thrive in cool, temperate climates and its preference for well-drained, nutrient-rich soils.
