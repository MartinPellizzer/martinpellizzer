Osmunda brasiliensis

Osmunda brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian water fern, is a species of royal fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, floating fronds that can grow up to two meters in length, allowing it to thrive in slow-moving freshwater environments. The plant has a distinctive rhizome system that anchors it to the substrate and supports its growth. Its spore-producing structures, called sporangia, are clustered in specialized leaflets called indusia.

Osmunda brasiliensis plays an important ecological role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
