Octomeria sarthouae

Octomeria sarthouae, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, intricate floral structure. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on rocky substrates or in forest undergrowth.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
