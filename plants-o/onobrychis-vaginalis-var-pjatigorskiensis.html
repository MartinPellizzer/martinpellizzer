Onobrychis vaginalis var. pjatigorskiensis

Onobrychis vaginalis var. pjatigorskiensis, commonly known as the Piatigorski's goat's rue, is a perennial legume native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with small, rounded leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in steppe and forest-steppe habitats.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and potential use in ecological restoration projects.
