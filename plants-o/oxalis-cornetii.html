Oxalis cornetii

Oxalis cornetii, commonly known as the coral root, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant is often found in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. It has a short flowering period, typically in spring, and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Oxalis cornetii is also notable for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
