Orthosiphon macranthus

Orthosiphon macranthus, commonly known as the large-flowered horsegram, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated margin. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The plant is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
