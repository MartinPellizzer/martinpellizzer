Oyedaea

Oyedaea, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to Africa.

These plants are characterized by their upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors. Oyedaea species typically have thick, waxy leaves that help them retain moisture in their native arid environments. They are popular as houseplants due to their attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.

The flowers are often pollinated by birds, particularly sunbirds, in their natural habitat.
