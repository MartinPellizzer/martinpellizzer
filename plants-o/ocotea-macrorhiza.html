Ocotea macrorhiza

Ocotea macrorhiza, commonly known as the large-leaf ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that appear in clusters. Its fruit is a small, dark blue drupe that contains a single seed.

Ocotea macrorhiza is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
