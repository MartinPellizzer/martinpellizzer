Oxalis decora

Oxalis decora, commonly known as the showy wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive orange-yellow veins and produces clusters of five-petaled, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is a perennial herb that thrives in partial to full shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Oxalis decora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
