Oenothera nevadensis

Oenothera nevadensis, commonly known as the Great Basin evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy and branched.

Oenothera nevadensis is also known for its medicinal properties, with its seeds containing essential fatty acids.
