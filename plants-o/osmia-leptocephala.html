Osmia leptocephala

Osmia leptocephala, commonly known as the slender-headed blue mason bee, is a solitary bee species native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender body and striking blue coloration, which serves as a visual cue for pollination. The plant it interacts with, such as various flowering plants in the Apiaceae family, provides nectar and pollen for its feeding and nesting activities. Its nesting behavior involves constructing small, mud-based cells within cavities, often in hollow stems or human-made structures.

This species plays an important role in pollination, contributing to the ecological balance of its habitat.
