Oxyanthus pyriformis

Oxyanthus pyriformis, commonly known as the pear-shaped flower plant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its unique, pear-shaped inflorescences that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are ovate and have a smooth texture, while its flowers are small and yellowish-green in color.

Oxyanthus pyriformis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in garden settings.
