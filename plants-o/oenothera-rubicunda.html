Oenothera rubicunda

Oenothera rubicunda, commonly known as the redstem evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy, orange to red flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has hairy, linear leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open habitats like prairies and disturbed areas.

Oenothera rubicunda is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
