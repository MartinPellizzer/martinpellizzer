Oenothera scapoidea subsp. macrocarpa

Oenothera scapoidea subsp.

macrocarpa, commonly known as the showy evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America. It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and showy yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the evening.

The plant produces large, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as prairies and disturbed areas.

The species is notable for its nocturnal blooming habit and its role in supporting nocturnal pollinators.
