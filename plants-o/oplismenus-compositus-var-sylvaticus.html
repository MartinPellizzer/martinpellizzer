Oplismenus compositus var. sylvaticus

Oplismenus compositus var. sylvaticus, commonly known as the woodland sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a dense, tufted cluster at the base. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually green or brown and appear in early spring.

The species is characterized by its ability to thrive in wet soils and its role in stabilizing soil in riparian areas.
