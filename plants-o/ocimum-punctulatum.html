Ocimum punctulatum

Ocimum punctulatum, commonly known as the spotted basil, is a member of the Lamiaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are often dotted with white or pale spots, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ocimum punctulatum is often used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties and potential therapeutic benefits.
