Opuntia schickendantzii

Opuntia schickendantzii, commonly known as the Schickendantzii prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in spiny areoles, which bear clusters of white flowers. The plant produces edible, fleshy fruits that are typically orange or red in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting well to hot, dry climates with minimal water.

Its structure and growth pattern make it well-suited for xerophytic conditions, contributing to its ecological significance in desert ecosystems.
