Opuntia palmadora subsp. catingicola

Opuntia palmadora subsp.

catingicola, commonly known as the Catinga prickly pear, is a succulent cactus native to Brazil. It features flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in spines and clusters of yellow flowers.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy pads. Its fruit, known as a tuna, is edible and often used in local cuisine.

This subspecies is valued for its resilience and ecological importance in its native habitat.
