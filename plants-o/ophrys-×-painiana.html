Ophrys × painiana

Ophrys × painiana, commonly known as the bee orchid hybrid, is a fascinating plant with unique botanical features.

It is a natural hybrid between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera, resulting in a plant that exhibits characteristics of both parent species. The orchid displays a striking floral morphology, with a labellum that mimics the appearance of a female bee, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. It typically grows in calcareous soils and is found in various parts of Europe.

This hybrid is notable for its ability to thrive in a range of habitats, making it a versatile and visually appealing species.
