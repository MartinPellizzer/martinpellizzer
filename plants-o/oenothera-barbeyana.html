Oenothera barbeyana

Oenothera barbeyana, commonly known as the evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairies and disturbed areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and somewhat woody.

Oenothera barbeyana is also known for its medicinal uses, particularly for its oil-rich seeds.
