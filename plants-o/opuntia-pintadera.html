Opuntia pintadera

Opuntia pintadera, commonly known as the Pintadera cactus, is a species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its flattened, paddle-shaped stems that are typically ribbed and covered in spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tunas, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

Opuntia pintadera is well adapted to arid environments and is often used in traditional medicine and as a food source.
