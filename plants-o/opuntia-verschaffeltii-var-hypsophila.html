Opuntia verschaffeltii var. hypsophila

Opuntia verschaffeltii var. hypsophila, commonly known as the highland prickly pear, is a succulent cactus native to the highlands of South America.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in clusters of spines and glochids. The plant produces large, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to red, typically blooming in the spring. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh climates.
