Odontoglossum polyxanthum var. grandiflorum

Odontoglossum polyxanthum var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the grandiflorum orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant features long, strap-like leaves and produces spikes of fragrant, often violet or purple-colored blooms. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
