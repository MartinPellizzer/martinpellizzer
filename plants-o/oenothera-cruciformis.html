Oenothera cruciformis

Oenothera cruciformis, commonly known as the crossleaf evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, and its leaves are arranged in a cross shape, giving it its common name. The plant is typically found in sandy or gravelly soils in open habitats such as prairies and disturbed areas. It belongs to the Onagraceae family and is known for its showy, fragrant blooms.
