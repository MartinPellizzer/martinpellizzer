Oncidium vasquezii

Oncidium vasquezii, commonly known as the Vasquez orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, long-lasting flowers that typically come in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are strap-like and arranged in a fan shape, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Oncidium vasquezii thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
