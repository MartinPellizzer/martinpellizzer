Oxytropis macrobotrys

Oxytropis macrobotrys, commonly known as the giant locoweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. The plant produces tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its flowers are pale blue to purple and are clustered in dense, terminal spikes.

Oxytropis macrobotrys is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions and is often found in areas with well-drained soils.
