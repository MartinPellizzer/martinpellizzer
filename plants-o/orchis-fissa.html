Orchis fissa

Orchis fissa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that are typically arranged in a spike, with a distinctive lip petal. Orchis fissa thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators and is often found in calcareous soils.
