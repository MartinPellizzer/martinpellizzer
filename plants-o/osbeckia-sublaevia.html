Osbeckia sublaevia

Osbeckia sublaevia, commonly known as the smooth osbeckia, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features slender, branching stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, pale yellow to greenish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and have a slightly hairy surface.

Osbeckia sublaevia thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.
