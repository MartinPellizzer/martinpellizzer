Onobrychis cappadocica f. angustifolia

Onobrychis cappadocica f. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved Cappadocian vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant is native to regions of Turkey and the eastern Mediterranean, where it thrives in rocky and arid environments. It has a creeping growth habit and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and ability to stabilize soil.
