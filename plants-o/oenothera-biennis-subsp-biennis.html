Oenothera biennis subsp. biennis

Oenothera biennis subsp.

biennis, commonly known as the evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America. It is characterized by its showy, yellow or orange flowers that open in the evening, hence its common name.

The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and grayish-green in appearance. It produces capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Oenothera biennis subsp.

biennis is often found in disturbed soils and is known for its ecological importance to pollinators.
