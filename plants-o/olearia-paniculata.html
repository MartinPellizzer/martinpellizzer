Olearia paniculata

Olearia paniculata, commonly known as the New Zealand daisy, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in fine, white hairs, giving them a silvery appearance.

Olearia paniculata is well adapted to the temperate climate of New Zealand and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
