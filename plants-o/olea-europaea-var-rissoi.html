Olea europaea var. rissoi

Olea europaea var. rissoi, commonly known as the Italian olive, is a cultivar of the olive tree native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and small, dark green fruits that are typically harvested for oil production. The plant is well adapted to Mediterranean climates, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. It is valued for its hardiness and ability to produce high-quality olive oil.

This variety is also noted for its ornamental value, making it a popular choice for landscaping in warm regions.
