Opuntia imbricata var. ramosior

Opuntia imbricata var. ramosior, commonly known as the branched prickly pear, is a succulent cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a dense, sprawling growth habit with multiple stems that branch out extensively, forming a bushy appearance. The plant produces flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in spines and small, glochids, which serve as a defense mechanism. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, blooming in late spring to early summer.

The fruit, known as a tunas, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor, making it a valuable resource in arid environments.
