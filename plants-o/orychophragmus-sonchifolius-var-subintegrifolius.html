Orychophragmus sonchifolius var. subintegrifolius

Orychophragmus sonchifolius var. subintegrifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of large, heart-shaped leaves with entire or slightly toothed margins. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and grasslands.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
