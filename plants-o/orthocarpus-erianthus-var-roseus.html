Orthocarpus erianthus var. roseus

Orthocarpus erianthus var. roseus, commonly known as the roseate dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy, lobed leaves. The plant produces small, oval seeds with pappus structures that aid in wind dispersal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and horticultural interest.
