Oxalis fendleri

Oxalis fendleri, commonly known as the desert wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial with trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its succulent stems and leaves help it conserve water, making it drought-tolerant.

Oxalis fendleri is often used in xeriscaping due to its ornamental value and adaptability to harsh conditions.
