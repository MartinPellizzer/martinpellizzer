Oxytropis deflexa var. capitata

Oxytropis deflexa var. capitata, commonly known as the Sierra buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are pinnately compound, and it produces small, pale yellow to greenish flowers that bloom in early spring.

The species is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native range.
