Ophrys × giardinae

Ophrys × giardinae, commonly known as the Giardinae bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is characterized by its intricate floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and thrives in temperate climates, often found in woodland edges and meadows. Its flowers are adorned with specialized structures called pseudopetals, which play a crucial role in its deceptive pollination strategy.

This hybrid exhibits a range of morphological variations, making it a subject of interest in both botanical and horticultural studies.
