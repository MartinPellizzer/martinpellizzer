Oncidium × baronii

Oncidium × baronii, commonly known as the Baron's orchid, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Oncidium crispum and Oncidium flexuosum.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant features long, arching inflorescences with clusters of fragrant, colorful flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. Its pseudobulbs and strap-like leaves are typical of the Oncidium genus, and it requires bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.

Oncidium × baronii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in greenhouse settings.
