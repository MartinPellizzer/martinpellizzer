Oboskon

Oboskon, commonly known as the "ghost plant," is a unique species of aquatic plant found in shallow, still waters.

It is characterized by its translucent, ribbon-like leaves that resemble ghostly figures, hence its common name. The plant lacks true roots and instead relies on rhizomes to anchor itself in the substrate. Oboskon is often found in nutrient-poor environments, thriving in conditions with low light and minimal disturbance.

Its delicate structure makes it an important indicator of water quality and ecosystem health.
