Odina malifolia

Odina malifolia, commonly known as the sweetleaf tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces edible fruits that are sweet and often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are also valued for their medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.

Odina malifolia thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests and riverine areas.
