Oxalis crispula

Oxalis crispula, commonly known as the crinkled wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves that have a crinkled texture and bright green color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in the spring. Oxalis crispula thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ease of cultivation.
