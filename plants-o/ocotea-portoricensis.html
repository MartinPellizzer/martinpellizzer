Ocotea portoricensis

Ocotea portoricensis, commonly known as the Puerto Rican ocotea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the Caribbean island of Puerto Rico, where it typically grows in humid, shaded forest environments. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves and small, clustered white flowers. Its fruits are drupes that contain a single seed, and the plant plays a role in the local ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various wildlife.

Ocotea portoricensis is also valued for its wood, which is used in traditional crafts and construction.
