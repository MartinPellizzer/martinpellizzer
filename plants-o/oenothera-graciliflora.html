Oenothera graciliflora

Oenothera graciliflora, commonly known as slenderflower evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, and its stems are erect and branching.

Oenothera graciliflora is part of the evening primrose family (Onagraceae) and is known for its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
