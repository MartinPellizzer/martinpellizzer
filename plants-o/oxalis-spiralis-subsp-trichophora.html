Oxalis spiralis subsp. trichophora

Oxalis spiralis subsp.

trichophora, commonly known as the spiral wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of leaves, which gives it its common name.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are trifoliate, with heart-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Oxalis spiralis subsp.

trichophora is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
