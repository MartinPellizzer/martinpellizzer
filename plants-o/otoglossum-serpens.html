Otoglossum serpens

Otoglossum serpens, commonly known as the serpentine orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces striking, snake-like flowers that are typically greenish-yellow with purple markings. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in rocky or forested areas. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, and it is pollinated by specific insect species.

Otoglossum serpens is valued for its unique appearance and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental appeal.
