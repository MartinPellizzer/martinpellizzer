Oenothera militaris

Oenothera militaris, commonly known as the military evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in desert scrub and rocky habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Oenothera militaris is a perennial species that produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
