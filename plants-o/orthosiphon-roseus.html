Orthosiphon roseus

Orthosiphon roseus, commonly known as Java tea or rose sage, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of pale purple to pink flowers, which bloom in summer. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown as an ornamental or for its medicinal properties.

Orthosiphon roseus is also valued in traditional medicine for its diuretic and anti-inflammatory effects.
