Oxalis acetosella f. lilacina

Oxalis acetosella f. lilacina, commonly known as the purple wood sorrel, is a variant of the common wood sorrel.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with trifoliate leaves and small, delicate flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a distinctive veining pattern. The flowers are usually pink or purple, adding ornamental value to gardens.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.
