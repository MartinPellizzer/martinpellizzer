Osbeckia rostrata var. ternifolia

Osbeckia rostrata var. ternifolia, commonly known as the three-leaf willow herb, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and streambanks. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, yellowish flowers are clustered in dense, terminal spikes, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
