Ocimum dalabaense

Ocimum dalabaense, commonly known as the Dalaba basil, is a species of flowering plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to western Africa, particularly found in countries like Guinea and Liberia. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue in color. It thrives in tropical climates and is often grown for its culinary and medicinal uses.

The essential oils extracted from its leaves are valued for their fragrance and potential health benefits.
