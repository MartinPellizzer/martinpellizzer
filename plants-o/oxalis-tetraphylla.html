Oxalis tetraphylla

Oxalis tetraphylla, commonly known as the four-leaf oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive four-leaf clover-like foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically pink or white in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Oxalis tetraphylla is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
