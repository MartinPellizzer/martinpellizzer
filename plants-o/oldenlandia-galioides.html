Oldenlandia galioides

Oldenlandia galioides, commonly known as the silverleaf spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that have a silvery-white underside. Its small, clustered flowers are usually white or pale blue, with five petals and numerous stamens.

Oldenlandia galioides is notable for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
