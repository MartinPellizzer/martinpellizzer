Orobanche laserpitii-sileris

Orobanche laserpitii-sileris, commonly known as the marsh broom.

It is a root-parasitic plant belonging to the Orobanchaceae family, characterized by its lack of chlorophyll and dependence on host plants for nutrients. This species typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often associated with grasses and sedges. Its flowers are usually small and pale, with a distinctive tubular shape.

The plant plays a role in certain ecosystems by influencing the composition of its host plant communities.
