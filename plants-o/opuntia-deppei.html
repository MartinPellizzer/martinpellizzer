Opuntia deppei

Opuntia deppei, commonly known as the Mexican fire cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in clusters of small, spiny glochids. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its adaptations include succulent leaves for water storage and a thick waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

Opuntia deppei is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
