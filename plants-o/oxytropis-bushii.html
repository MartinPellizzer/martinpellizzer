Oxytropis bushii

Oxytropis bushii, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, compound leaves and produces yellow or purple pea-like flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought and extreme temperatures.

Oxytropis bushii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
