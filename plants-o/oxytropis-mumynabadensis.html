Oxytropis mumynabadensis

Oxytropis mumynabadensis, commonly known as the Mumynabad locoweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Iran.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and spiny stems. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
