Ocimum glabrifolium

Ocimum glabrifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved basil, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and small, pale purple flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its fragrant foliage, which is used in culinary and medicinal applications. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Ocimum glabrifolium is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and lavender.
