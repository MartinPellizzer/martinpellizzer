Orchis morio var. picta

Orchis morio var. picta, commonly known as the painted orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to Europe.

This variant is distinguished by its vivid purple and white petals, which feature intricate patterns resembling painted designs. It typically grows in woodland habitats, preferring moist, shaded environments with well-drained soil. The plant produces a single spike of flowers, which blooms in late spring to early summer.

Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it a notable species in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
