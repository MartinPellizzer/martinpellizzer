Oxalis flava

Oxalis flava, commonly known as the yellow wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with three heart-shaped leaflets, often tinged with red or purple veins. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically surrounded by five leaf-like bracts. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Oxalis flava is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
