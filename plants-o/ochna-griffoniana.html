Ochna griffoniana

Ochna griffoniana, commonly known as the African star of Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped yellow flowers that appear in clusters during the winter months. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are oval and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Ochna griffoniana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
