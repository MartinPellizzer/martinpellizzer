Osmunda japonica var. sublancea

Osmunda japonica var. sublancea, commonly known as the Japanese water fern, is a species of fern characterized by its delicate, floating fronds.

It thrives in aquatic or semi-aquatic environments, often found in slow-moving streams, ponds, and wetlands. The plant has a complex life cycle involving both a sporophyte and a gametophyte stage. Its fronds are typically lanceolate, with a light green color and a finely serrated margin.

Osmunda japonica var. sublancea plays an important ecological role in water ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen.
