Ormosia paraensis

Ormosia paraensis, commonly known as the Paraensis ormosia, is a tropical tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces woody pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals. Ormosia paraensis is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in secondary growth forests.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and contributing to the biodiversity of its native region.
