Orchis elata f. maroccanica

Orchis elata f. maroccanica, commonly known as the Moroccan tall orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Morocco, where it thrives in moist, shaded habitats. Its flowers are usually a mix of white, pink, and purple, with a distinctive spurred lip.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often found in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
