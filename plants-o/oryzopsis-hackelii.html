Oryzopsis hackelii

Oryzopsis hackelii, commonly known as the desert barley or desert grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit, with slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through deep root systems and drought-resistant physiology.

Oryzopsis hackelii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
