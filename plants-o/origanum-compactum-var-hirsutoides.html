Origanum compactum var. hirsutoides

Origanum compactum var. hirsutoides, commonly known as woolly oregano, is a compact, perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, gray-green leaves covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive woolly appearance. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its aromatic foliage is often used in cooking and herbal remedies.
