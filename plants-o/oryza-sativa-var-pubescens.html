Oryza sativa var. pubescens

Oryza sativa var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy rice plant, is a variant of rice characterized by its pubescent or hairy leaves and stems.

This variety is native to Southeast Asia and is often found in wetland environments. It has a shorter growth cycle compared to standard rice varieties, making it suitable for certain agricultural conditions. The plant's botanical features include narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers.

It is of interest to botanists and researchers due to its unique genetic makeup and potential for breeding programs.
