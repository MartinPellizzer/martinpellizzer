Oxalis bisfracta

Oxalis bisfracta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features trifoliate leaves composed of three heart-shaped leaflets, which are often tinged with red or purple veins. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Oxalis bisfracta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
