Ouratea spectabilis

Ouratea spectabilis, commonly known as the white-veined beauty or the white-veined ouratea, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its lance-shaped leaves with prominent white veins. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, and it prefers well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and showy flowers.
