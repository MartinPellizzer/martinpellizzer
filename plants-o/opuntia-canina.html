Opuntia canina

Opuntia canina, commonly known as the Mexican devil's tongue, is a species of cactus native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that are segmented and covered in spiny areoles. The plant produces bright yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by edible, red, fleshy fruits. Opuntia canina is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its stems and having a shallow root system.

It is also known for its use in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.
