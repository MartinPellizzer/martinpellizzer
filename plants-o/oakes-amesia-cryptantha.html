Oakes-amesia cryptantha

Oakes-amesia cryptantha, commonly known as the cryptantha daisy, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
