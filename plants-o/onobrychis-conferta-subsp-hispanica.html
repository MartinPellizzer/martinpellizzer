Onobrychis conferta subsp. hispanica

Onobrychis conferta subsp.

hispanica, commonly known as Spanish clover, is a perennial legume native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to purple.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, making it valuable for soil enrichment.

Its ornamental value also makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
