Odontoglossum mapiriense

Odontoglossum mapiriense, commonly known as the Mapiri orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, sympodial growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically blooms during the cooler months, and its inflorescence bears multiple flowers arranged in a raceme. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and high humidity.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological significance in its native habitat.
