Oleandra crassipes

Oleandra crassipes, commonly known as the broad-leaved oleander, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, dark green leaves and showy clusters of pink or red flowers. The plant is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, with a woody stem and thick, aromatic leaves. Oleandra crassipes is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.

It is also noted for its toxicity, as all parts of the plant contain cardiac glycosides that can be harmful to humans and animals.
