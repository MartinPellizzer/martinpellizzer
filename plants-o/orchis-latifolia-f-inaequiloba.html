Orchis latifolia f. inaequiloba

Orchis latifolia f. inaequiloba, commonly known as the broad-leaved orchid variant with unequal lobes, is a botanical variant of the broad-leaved orchid.

It is characterized by its distinctive, broad leaves and showy, violet or purple flowers. This orchid is typically found in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or meadow habitats. The plant exhibits a complex floral structure typical of orchids, including a labellum and column.

Its growth habit is perennial, with rhizomatous roots that allow it to thrive in shaded environments.
