Onix galegiformis

Onix galegiformis, commonly known as the silverleaf vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial legume characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant has a trailing growth habit and produces small, lens-shaped pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Onix galegiformis is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment and ecological restoration projects.
