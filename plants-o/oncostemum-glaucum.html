Oncostemum glaucum

Oncostemum glaucum, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Oncostemum glaucum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
