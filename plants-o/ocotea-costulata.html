Ocotea costulata

Ocotea costulata, commonly known as the Brazilian cinnamon or cinnamomum tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside, and its flowers are small and clustered. The fruit is a dark blue drupe that contains a single seed.

Ocotea costulata is valued for its essential oils and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
