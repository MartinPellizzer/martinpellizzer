Opuntia pittieri

Opuntia pittieri, commonly known as the Mexican prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its flattened, paddle-shaped stems covered in spines and glochids, which serve as a defense mechanism. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers, typically in the spring, followed by edible fruits known as tunas. Opuntia pittieri is adapted to arid environments and has a shallow root system to conserve water.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems and is also used in traditional medicine and as a food source.
