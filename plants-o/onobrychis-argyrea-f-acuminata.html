Onobrychis argyrea f. acuminata

Onobrychis argyrea f. acuminata, commonly known as the acuminated silver vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia. It has a low, creeping growth habit and produces small, elongated seed pods.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
