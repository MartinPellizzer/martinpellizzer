Olea dioica

Olea dioica, commonly known as the common olive, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are either male or female, typical of its dioecious nature. The plant is well-known for its fruit, the olive, which is used in culinary and medicinal applications. Olea dioica thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, dry climate to flourish.

Its historical significance dates back thousands of years, with evidence of its cultivation in ancient civilizations.
