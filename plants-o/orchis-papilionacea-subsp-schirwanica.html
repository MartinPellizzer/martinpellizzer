Orchis papilionacea subsp. schirwanica

Orchis papilionacea subsp.

schirwanica, commonly known as the Schirwan's butterfly orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of Central and Eastern Europe. It is a perennial herb with a short, stout rhizome and produces a single, tall stem bearing a single, showy, butterfly-shaped flower.

The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, with a distinctive violet or purple flower that has a narrow, spurred lip. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral morphology.
