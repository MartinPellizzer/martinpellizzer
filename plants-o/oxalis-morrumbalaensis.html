Oxalis morrumbalaensis

Oxalis morrumbalaensis, commonly known as the Morumbala wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the humid lowland forests of eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in undergrowth vegetation.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
