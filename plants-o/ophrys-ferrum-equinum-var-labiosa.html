Ophrys ferrum-equinum var. labiosa

Ophrys ferrum-equinum var. labiosa, commonly known as the Red Hare Orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a pouch-like lip that mimics the appearance of a female bee, aiding in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and heaths. Its flowers are usually a mix of red and brown hues, with the labial appendage being particularly prominent.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a protected species in many regions.
