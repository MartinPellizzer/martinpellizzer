Octomeria crassifolia

Octomeria crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leaved octomeria, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick, and waxy leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or summer. It grows in a compact, shrub-like form, often with a rosette structure.

Octomeria crassifolia is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and drought tolerance.
