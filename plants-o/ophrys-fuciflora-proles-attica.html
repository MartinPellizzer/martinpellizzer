Ophrys fuciflora proles attica

Ophrys fuciflora proles attica, commonly known as the Attic orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the rocky and limestone-rich regions of Greece.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which features a lip that mimics the appearance of a female bee, a trait that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats with well-drained soils, often on cliffs or among shrubs. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brown in color, with intricate patterns that aid in attracting male bees for pollination.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
