Osmanthus fragrans f. latifolius

Osmanthus fragrans f. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf osmanthus, is a fragrant evergreen shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that emit a sweet, aromatic scent, particularly in the fall. These flowers are clustered in panicles and are highly valued for their fragrance in traditional Chinese medicine and as a culinary ingredient.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
