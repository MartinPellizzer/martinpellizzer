Ophiorrhiza gracilis

Ophiorrhiza gracilis, commonly known as the slender swamp bean, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It thrives in wet, swampy environments and is often found in coastal areas and mangrove forests. The plant has small, opposite leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and white, clustered in racemes, and it produces small, round fruits.

Ophiorrhiza gracilis plays a role in stabilizing soil in its habitat and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
