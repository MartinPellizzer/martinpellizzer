Opuntia ganderi

Opuntia ganderi, commonly known as the Gander's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flattened, fleshy stems that are covered in spines and glochids, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the summer months, followed by edible fruits that are often consumed by both humans and animals. Opuntia ganderi is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, succulent stems.

Its botanical features make it an important component of desert ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical research.
