Oroya baumannii

Oroya baumannii, commonly known as the "false dandelion," is a species of flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, silvery-green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats and is known for its ornamental value.

Oroya baumannii is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
