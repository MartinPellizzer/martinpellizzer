Ocotea tonii

Ocotea tonii, commonly known as the Caribbean laurel, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as cinnamon and bay laurel. The plant is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale green. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, black seeds.

Ocotea tonii is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical and subtropical forests.
