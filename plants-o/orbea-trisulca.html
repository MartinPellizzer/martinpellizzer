Orbea trisulca

Orbea trisulca, commonly known as the three-lobed orchid, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, fragrant flowers with three prominent lobes. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its roots are adapted to store water.

Orbea trisulca is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticulturists.
