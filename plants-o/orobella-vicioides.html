Orobella vicioides

Orobella vicioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae. The plant features slender stems that often lie prostrate on the ground, with small, trifoliate leaves that are typically narrow and pointed. Its flowers are small, clustered in racemes, and range in color from white to pale purple, attracting various pollinators.

Orobella vicioides is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in dry, sunny environments.
