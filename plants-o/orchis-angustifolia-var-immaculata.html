Orchis angustifolia var. immaculata

Orchis angustifolia var. immaculata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, pale pink to white flowers in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its intricate floral structure.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
