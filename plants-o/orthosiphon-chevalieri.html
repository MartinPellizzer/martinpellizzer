Orthosiphon chevalieri

Orthosiphon chevalieri, commonly known as Java tea or *Plectranthus chevalieri*, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or pink. It is often grown for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported diuretic properties.

The leaves are usually harvested for use in teas or herbal remedies.
