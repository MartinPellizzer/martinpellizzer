Oxalis punensis

Oxalis punensis, commonly known as the Andean wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of delicate, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, preferring well-drained soil and full sun. Oxalis punensis has a short growing season and is often found in high-altitude environments.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental species within the Oxalidaceae family.
