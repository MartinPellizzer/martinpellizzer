Obione frigida

Obione frigida, commonly known as the cold-loving orchid, is a rare and delicate plant native to high-altitude regions with cool, moist climates.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, fragrant flowers. The plant thrives in shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil to flourish. Its blooming season typically occurs in late spring or early summer, depending on the local climate.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, Obione frigida is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
