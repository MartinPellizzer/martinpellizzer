Ophrys apifera var. speciosa

Ophrys apifera var. speciosa, commonly known as the Bee Orchid, is a striking orchid species native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its intricate, bee-like flower structure, which features a hairy, yellowish-brown labellum mimicking the appearance of a female bee. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing a delicate, fragrant scent that attracts male bees for pollination.

This orchid is a prime example of mimicry in the plant kingdom, showcasing remarkable adaptations for reproductive success.
