Oncidium buchtienii

Oncidium buchtienii, commonly known as the Buchtien's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, producing pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red.

Oncidium buchtienii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow under proper conditions.
