Oosterdyckia vieillardii

Oosterdyckia vieillardii, commonly known as the Malagasy orchid, is a rare and endemic orchid species found primarily in Madagascar.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, where it relies on specific symbiotic relationships with fungi for nutrient uptake. Its flowers are often fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and moths.

Due to habitat destruction, Oosterdyckia vieillardii is considered endangered, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
