Oxyacantha azarella

Oxyacantha azarella, commonly known as the Azarella shrub, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible and often consumed by local wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Oxyacantha azarella is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
