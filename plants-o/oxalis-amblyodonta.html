Oxalis amblyodonta

Oxalis amblyodonta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wood sorrel, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features trifoliate leaves with three oval to lance-shaped leaflets, often with a reddish or purple tinge. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Oxalis amblyodonta is often found in woodland and shrubland habitats, where it contributes to the understory vegetation.
