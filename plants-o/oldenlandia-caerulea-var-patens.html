Oldenlandia caerulea var. patens

Oldenlandia caerulea var. patens, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands and wetlands. The plant produces clusters of small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
