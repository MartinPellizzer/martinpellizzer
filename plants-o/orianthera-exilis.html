Orianthera exilis

Orianthera exilis, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated stems and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant prefers warm, humid environments and is often found growing in shaded, moist forest understories. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the surrounding soil, and it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

Orianthera exilis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
