Orchis purpurea subsp. moravica

Orchis purpurea subsp.

moravica, commonly known as the Moravian purple orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem bearing a single, showy violet or purple flower.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodlands and meadows. Its flowers are bilaterally symmetrical with three sepals and three petals, one of which is prominently hooded.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly smaller flowers and more compact growth habit compared to the typical form of Orchis purpurea.
