Oreocalamus puberulus

Oreocalamus puberulus, commonly known as the hairy reed, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-based flowers.

Oreocalamus puberulus plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wetland organisms.
