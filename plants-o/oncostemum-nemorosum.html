Oncostemum nemorosum

Oncostemum nemorosum, commonly known as the forest onion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist woodlands and thrives in well-drained, loamy soils. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in umbels. Its bulbs are edible and have a mild, onion-like flavor, making it a useful plant in traditional herbal medicine.

Oncostemum nemorosum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting insect pollinators.
