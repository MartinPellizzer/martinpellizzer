Origanum cimicinum

Origanum cimicinum, commonly known as wild marjoram, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves with a strong, spicy scent, and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry environments. Its essential oils, rich in compounds like carvacrol and thymol, contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.

Origanum cimicinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal remedies.
