Oxyria digyna

Oxyria digyna, commonly known as the mountain woodruff, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has delicate, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky stream banks.

Oxyria digyna is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
