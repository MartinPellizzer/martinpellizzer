Othocallis siberica subsp. caucasica

Othocallis siberica subsp.

caucasica, commonly known as Caucasian Siberian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils. It has linear to lanceolate leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive floral display.
