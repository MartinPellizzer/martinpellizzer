Oberonia pendula

Oberonia pendula, commonly known as the hanging orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. The plant features slender, pendulous stems that bear small, greenish-yellow flowers. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil.

Oberonia pendula is valued for its unique appearance and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
