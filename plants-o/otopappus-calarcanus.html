Otopappus calarcanus

Otopappus calarcanus, commonly known as the mountain sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically thrives in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features small, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring, often in shades of white or pale pink. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, adapted to conserve water in arid environments.

Otopappus calarcanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in rocky habitats.
