Osteospermum pterospermum

Osteospermum pterospermum, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers with bright yellow centers and white to pink petals. The plant produces a distinctive, fluffy seed head that resembles a dandelion, which aids in wind pollination. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Osteospermum pterospermum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
