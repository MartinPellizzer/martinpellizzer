Ophrys fusca subsp. pallida

Ophrys fusca subsp.

pallida, commonly known as the pale bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to parts of Europe. It is characterized by its distinctive floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and heathlands, often in nutrient-poor soils. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellow, with intricate patterns that aid in mimicry.

Due to habitat loss and fragmentation, this subspecies is considered vulnerable and is the subject of conservation efforts.
