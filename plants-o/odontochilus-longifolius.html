Odontochilus longifolius

Odontochilus longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved toothed orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil to flourish. Its unique morphological features, including the tooth-like structures on its leaves, make it a distinctive member of the Orchidaceae family.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered and is often cultivated in conservation programs.
