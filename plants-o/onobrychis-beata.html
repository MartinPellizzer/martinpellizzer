Onobrychis beata

Onobrychis beata, commonly known as the blessed vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, where it thrives in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by cylindrical pods containing several seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant is often used as a forage crop for livestock.

Onobrychis beata is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
