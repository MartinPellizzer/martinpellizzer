Opuntia leiascheinvariana

Opuntia leiascheinvariana, commonly known as the Schleisinger's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems that are covered in spines and glochids, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers, typically during the spring and early summer months. Its fruit, known as a tunas, is edible and contains a juicy, sweet pulp.

Opuntia leiascheinvariana is well adapted to harsh desert environments, storing water in its thick stems and performing photosynthesis through its flattened leaves.
