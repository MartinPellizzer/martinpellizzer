Orchis militaris var. galeata

Orchis militaris var. galeata, commonly known as the Galeata soldier's orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features a tall, erect stem with lance-shaped leaves and produces striking, violet-blue flowers in late spring. Its flowers are characterized by a distinctive lip that is often marked with darker pigments.

This orchid is known for its ecological importance, supporting various pollinators and contributing to biodiversity in its native ecosystems.
