Oxalis subsericea

Oxalis subsericea, commonly known as the silverleaf wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive silvery underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red or orange berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and delicate floral display.
