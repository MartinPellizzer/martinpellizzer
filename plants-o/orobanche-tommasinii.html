Orobanche tommasinii

Orobanche tommasinii, commonly known as the Italian broomrape, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

This species typically parasitizes members of the Fabaceae family, such as vetches and clovers, by inserting its haustoria into the host's vascular system. Its life cycle is closely tied to the presence of suitable host plants, making it an important component of certain ecosystems.
