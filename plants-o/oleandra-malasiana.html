Oleandra malasiana

Oleandra malasiana, commonly known as the Malasian oleander, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are typically lance-shaped. Oleandra malasiana is known for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
