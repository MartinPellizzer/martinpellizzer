Osbeckia ovata

Osbeckia ovata, commonly known as the broadleaf goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a perennial herb with alternate, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly succulent texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender pedicels. Osbeckia ovata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
