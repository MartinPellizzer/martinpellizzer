Oxalis commutata var. concolor

Oxalis commutata var. concolor, commonly known as the smoothleaf wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with three heart-shaped leaflets, often covered in a fine, silky coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically in clusters at the ends of stems. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to native plant gardens.
