Oxydectes megalocalyx

Oxydectes megalocalyx, commonly known as the giant flame flower, is a striking tropical plant native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its large, showy, tubular flowers that are typically red or orange in color, with a distinctive yellow throat. The plant has a woody stem and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in warm climates. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by hummingbirds and bats due to their shape and nectar-rich structure.

Oxydectes megalocalyx thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens in tropical and subtropical regions.
