Oxalis grisea var. mattogrossensis

Oxalis grisea var. mattogrossensis, commonly known as the Brazilian wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces distinctive, bead-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
