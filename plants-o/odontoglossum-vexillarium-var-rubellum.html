Odontoglossum vexillarium var. rubellum

Odontoglossum vexillarium var. rubellum, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety red petals and a contrasting white lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in late summer, producing a fragrant, showy display that attracts pollinators such as bees and moths.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique coloration and elegance.
