Ocimum fissilabrum

Ocimum fissilabrum, commonly known as the African basil, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs such as mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often glandular and emit a strong, spicy aroma. Its small, tubular flowers are typically purple or white and are arranged in whorled clusters.

Ocimum fissilabrum is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its antispasmodic and analgesic effects.
