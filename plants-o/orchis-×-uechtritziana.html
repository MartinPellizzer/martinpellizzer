Orchis × uechtritziana

Orchis × uechtritziana, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Orchis morio and Orchis purpurea.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or meadow habitats. It blooms in late spring to early summer, producing intricate, lip-shaped petals that attract pollinators.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
