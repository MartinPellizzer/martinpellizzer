Oenothera hookeri var. simsiana

Oenothera hookeri var. simsiana, commonly known as the Hooker's evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and conservation efforts in its native range.
