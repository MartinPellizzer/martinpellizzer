Osmorhiza longistylis var. imbarbata

Osmorhiza longistylis var. imbarbata, commonly known as the long-styled sweetroot, is a variant of the sweetroot plant native to California.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and large, showy white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces a distinctive, aromatic root that has been historically used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks.

This variant is notable for its unique floral morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
