Oxydectes sarcopetaloides

Oxydectes sarcopetaloides, commonly known as the sarcopetaloides orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple and white. Its leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped, arranged in a rosette pattern.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouse environments due to its specific humidity and temperature requirements.
