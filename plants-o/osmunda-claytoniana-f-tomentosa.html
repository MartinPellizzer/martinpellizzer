Osmunda claytoniana f. tomentosa

Osmunda claytoniana f. tomentosa, commonly known as the hairy sword fern, is a fern species characterized by its distinctive, broad, lance-shaped fronds.

The fronds are covered in a dense layer of fine, white to grayish hairs, giving the plant its "hairy" appearance. It is a perennial plant that thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies.

Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and are covered by a protective indusium.
