Odontotrichum bipinnatifidum

Odontotrichum bipinnatifidum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved toothed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant has narrow, linear leaves with a distinctive toothed margin, giving it its common name. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of small, two-ranked spikelets.

Odontotrichum bipinnatifidum typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats and is adapted to cooler, temperate climates.
