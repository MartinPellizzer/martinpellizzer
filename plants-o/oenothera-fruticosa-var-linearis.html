Oenothera fruticosa var. linearis

Oenothera fruticosa var. linearis, commonly known as the linear evening primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features slender, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces showy yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening. The plant belongs to the genus Oenothera, which is part of the Onagraceae family, known for its showy blooms and ecological importance. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and dry grasslands.

The linear evening primrose is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like moths and beetles.
