Oenothera taraxacifolia var. ecristata

Oenothera taraxacifolia var. ecristata, commonly known as the mountain evening primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Onagraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in mountainous regions, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are upright and branching.

The flowers emit a mild fragrance, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
