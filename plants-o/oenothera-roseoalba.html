Oenothera roseoalba

Oenothera roseoalba, commonly known as the rose-white evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a silvery underside. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats like grasslands and disturbed areas.

Oenothera roseoalba is a member of the Onagraceae family, which includes other evening primroses and related species.
