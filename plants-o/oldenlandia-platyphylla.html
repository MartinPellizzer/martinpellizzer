Oldenlandia platyphylla

Oldenlandia platyphylla, commonly known as the broadleaf mistflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is a perennial herb with slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on long pedicels. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Oldenlandia platyphylla is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks.
