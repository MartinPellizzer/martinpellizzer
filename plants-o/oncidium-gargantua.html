Oncidium gargantua

Oncidium gargantua, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, making it one of the largest orchid blooms. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, warm environments. Its flowers come in a variety of colors, including yellow, orange, and red, often with intricate patterns.

Oncidium gargantua requires bright, indirect light and consistent moisture to thrive.
