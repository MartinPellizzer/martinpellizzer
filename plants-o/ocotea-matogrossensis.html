Ocotea matogrossensis

Ocotea matogrossensis, commonly known as the Matogrossense Ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon Basin.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces dark blue to black berries that are an important food source for various bird species. Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in local crafts and construction.

Ocotea matogrossensis plays a significant role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
