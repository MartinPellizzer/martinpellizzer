Oenothera speciosioides

Oenothera speciosioides, commonly known as the showy evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.

Oenothera speciosioides is also known for its ecological importance in supporting local insect populations.
