Opuntia rotundifolia

Opuntia rotundifolia, commonly known as the Indian fig cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its flat, rounded stems that are typically green and covered in small spines. The plant produces striking yellow or orange flowers, which bloom in the summer months. Its fruit, known as a tunafilla, is a small, red, fleshy fruit that is edible and often used in local cuisines.

Opuntia rotundifolia is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in tropical and subtropical regions.
