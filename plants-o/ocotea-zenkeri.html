Ocotea zenkeri

Ocotea zenkeri, commonly known as the African camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and Eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and aromatic leaves that emit a strong camphor-like scent. Its bark is thick and rugged, often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Ocotea zenkeri typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments and can reach heights of up to 30 meters.
