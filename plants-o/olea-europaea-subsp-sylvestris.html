Olea europaea subsp. sylvestris

Olea europaea subsp.

sylvestris, commonly known as the wild olive, is a subspecies native to the Mediterranean region. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub with dark green, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by small, fleshy fruits called drupes. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and have a distinct aromatic scent.

The subspecies is adapted to arid and rocky environments, making it resilient to drought conditions.
