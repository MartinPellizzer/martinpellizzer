Opuntia phaeacantha var. chihuahuensis

Opuntia phaeacantha var. chihuahuensis, commonly known as the Chihuahua prickly pear, is a cactus native to northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, fleshy pads and spiny edges, typical of the Opuntia genus. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers, which are followed by edible, red-fleshed fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting well to hot, dry climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine and cuisine.
