Orobanche multiflora var. arenosa

Orobanche multiflora var. arenosa, commonly known as the desert broomrape, is a parasitic plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on its host for nutrients.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Mediterranean and parts of North Africa. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has a highly reduced morphology with no true leaves. It is often found growing on the roots of various host plants such as legumes and grasses.

Due to its parasitic nature and habitat preferences, it plays a unique role in its ecosystem.
