Ophrys apifera f. tilaventina

Ophrys apifera f. tilaventina, commonly known as the bee orchid, is a striking orchid species native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its intricate flower structure that closely resembles a bee, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats. Its flowers are usually pale yellow with dark markings, mimicking the appearance of a bee to attract male bees for pollination.

The species is known for its specialized reproductive mechanisms and is often found in Mediterranean regions.
