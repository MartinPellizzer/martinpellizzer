Ochna klainei

Ochna klainei, commonly known as the African star fruit, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are edible. Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant foliage and attractive fruits, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
