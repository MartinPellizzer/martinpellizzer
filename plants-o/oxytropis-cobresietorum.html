Oxytropis cobresietorum

Oxytropis cobresietorum, commonly known as the Sierra Madre locoweed, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, with small, hairy structures that help reduce water loss.

Oxytropis cobresietorum is part of the Fabaceae family and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native range.
