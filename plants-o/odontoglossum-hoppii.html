Odontoglossum hoppii

Odontoglossum hoppii, commonly known as the Hoppi's orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a rhizomatous growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers during the warmer months. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments at high altitudes. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, while its flowers are usually violet or purple in color.

Odontoglossum hoppii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
