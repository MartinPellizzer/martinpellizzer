Oxalis plumieri

Oxalis plumieri, commonly known as the purple shamrock, is a perennial flowering plant native to South America.

It features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive purple hue and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

Oxalis plumieri is also valued for its ability to spread through rhizomes, forming dense mats in suitable conditions.
