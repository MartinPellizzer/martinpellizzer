Oxalis calliantha

Oxalis calliantha, commonly known as the golden wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are heart-shaped and often have a reddish tint. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Oxalis calliantha is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
