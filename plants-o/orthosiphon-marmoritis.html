Orthosiphon marmoritis

Orthosiphon marmoritis, commonly known as the marble leaf plant, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated leaves that display a marble-like pattern of green and white. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Orthosiphon marmoritis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
