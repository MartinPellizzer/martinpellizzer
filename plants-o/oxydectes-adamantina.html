Oxydectes adamantina

Oxydectes adamantina, commonly known as the diamond orchid, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the rainforests of northeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, lace-like leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture and good air circulation.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and specialized collections.
