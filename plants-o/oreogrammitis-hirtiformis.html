Oreogrammitis hirtiformis

Oreogrammitis hirtiformis, commonly known as the hairy thread moss, is a species of liverwort belonging to the family Porellaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems that are covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it its common name. The leaves are small, elongated, and often have a distinct midrib, contributing to its intricate appearance. This moss thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found on rocks or soil in temperate regions.

Its delicate structure makes it a notable species in ecological studies of bryophyte communities.
