Origanum grosii

Origanum grosii, commonly known as Turkish oregano, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and clusters of small, pale purple to white flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and Mediterranean climates. Its essential oils, rich in thymol and carvacrol, contribute to its strong scent and medicinal properties.

Origanum grosii is often used in cooking and traditional herbal remedies due to its potent flavor and health benefits.
