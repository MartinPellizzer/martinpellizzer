Ophiopogon intermedius var. parviflorus

Ophiopogon intermedius var. parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its grass-like appearance.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are typically blue-green in color, and it produces small, inconspicuous white or pale green flowers arranged in spike-like clusters. This variety is native to regions of East Asia, including China and Japan, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. It is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive foliage and ability to form dense mats.

The plant is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
