Oxalis corniculata var. glabrocapsula

Oxalis corniculata var. glabrocapsula, commonly known as the smooth-capsuled wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers that resemble those of true sorrels. The plant produces capsule fruits that are smooth and glabrous, giving it its specific variety name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
