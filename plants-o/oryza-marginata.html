Oryza marginata

Oryza marginata, commonly known as the marginated ricegrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is often found in wetland and swampy habitats. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves with a distinct margin that is often hairy or frayed. Its inflorescence consists of small, clustered spikelets that produce minute grains.

Oryza marginata is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and supporting local biodiversity.
