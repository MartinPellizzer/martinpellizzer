Orchis pentecostalis

Orchis pentecostalis, commonly known as the Holy Ghost orchid, is a species of orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and prefers partial shade. Its flowers have a distinctive lip that is often adorned with intricate patterns.

Orchis pentecostalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by supporting pollinators.
