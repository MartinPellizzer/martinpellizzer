Ocotea stenoneura

Ocotea stenoneura, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves with a smooth texture and a distinctive aromatic scent. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically white or pale yellow, and it produces dark, glossy berries.

Ocotea stenoneura is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in warm climates for its aesthetic appeal.
