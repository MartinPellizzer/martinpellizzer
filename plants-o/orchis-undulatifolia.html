Orchis undulatifolia

Orchis undulatifolia, commonly known as the wavy-leaf orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, undulating leaf margins. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and produces showy, violet-blue flowers during the spring. Its roots are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported medicinal properties.

The species is noted for its ecological importance in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
