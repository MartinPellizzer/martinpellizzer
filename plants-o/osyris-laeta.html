Osyris laeta

Osyris laeta, commonly known as the golden osyris, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and dense foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Osyris laeta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
