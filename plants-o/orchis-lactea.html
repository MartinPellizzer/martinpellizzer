Orchis lactea

Orchis lactea, commonly known as the milk orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear a single spike of showy, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Orchis lactea is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for certain herbivorous insects.
