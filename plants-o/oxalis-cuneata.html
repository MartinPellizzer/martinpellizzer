Oxalis cuneata

Oxalis cuneata, commonly known as the small-leaf wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive white or pale green pattern, and it produces small, delicate yellow flowers. The plant is often found in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream sides. It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its ability to thrive in partial shade.

Oxalis cuneata is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
