Opulus mollis

Opulus mollis, commonly known as the white angelica or white thimbleweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white, fluffy flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

Opulus mollis is also valued in traditional herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
