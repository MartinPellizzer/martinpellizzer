Ornithidium tonduzii

Ornithidium tonduzii, commonly known as the Tonduzii featherplant, is a rare and specialized aquatic plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like leaves that are adapted for efficient water absorption and gas exchange. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments with high light penetration and stable temperatures. Its reproductive structures are unique, featuring small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by specific insect species.

Due to habitat loss and environmental degradation, Ornithidium tonduzii is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
