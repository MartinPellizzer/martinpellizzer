Oxycoccus palustris var. vulgaris

Oxycoccus palustris var. vulgaris, commonly known as the common cranberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It produces small, red, edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and are widely used in food and beverages. The plant thrives in acidic, wet soils and is often found in bogs and marshes. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, and it has a dense, mat-like growth habit.

It plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
