Oligactis scandens

Oligactis scandens, commonly known as the climbing cress, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with slender stems that twine around supports. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Oligactis scandens is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
