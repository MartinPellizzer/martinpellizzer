Oncinotis campanulata

Oncinotis campanulata, commonly known as the bluebell of the prairies, is a herbaceous perennial native to the central United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

Oncinotis campanulata is often found in prairies, open woodlands, and along roadsides.
