Oxalis pyrenea var. pubens

Oxalis pyrenea var. pubens, commonly known as the woolly wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are covered in fine, hairy pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically yellow with five petals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and blooms.
