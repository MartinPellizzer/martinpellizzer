Oncidium hapalotyle

Oncidium hapalotyle, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It features a pseudobulb-like structure with broad, fleshy leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces inflorescences with showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red. Oncidium hapalotyle is known for its long-lasting blooms and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
