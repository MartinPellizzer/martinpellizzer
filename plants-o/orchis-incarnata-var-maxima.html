Orchis incarnata var. maxima

Orchis incarnata var. maxima, commonly known as the showy orchis, is a striking perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It features tall, slender stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white, arranged in a loose raceme. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption in moist, wooded habitats.

This orchid is pollinated primarily by bees and is an important species in wetland ecosystems.
