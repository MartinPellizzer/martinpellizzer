Onobrychis baldzuanica

Onobrychis baldzuanica, commonly known as the Caucasian vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to temperate climates. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Onobrychis baldzuanica is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
