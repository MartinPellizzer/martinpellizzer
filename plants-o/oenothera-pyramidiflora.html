Oenothera pyramidiflora

Oenothera pyramidiflora, commonly known as the pyramid leaf evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in a rosette at the base, and its stems are usually erect and covered in fine hairs. Oenothera pyramidiflora is a member of the Onagraceae family, which includes other evening primroses and related species.
