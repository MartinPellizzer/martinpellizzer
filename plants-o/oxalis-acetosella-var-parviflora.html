Oxalis acetosella var. parviflora

Oxalis acetosella var. parviflora, commonly known as wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers.

It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it is known for its delicate appearance and ability to spread through rhizomes. It is also valued in gardens for its ornamental qualities and ability to act as a ground cover.

However, it can become invasive in certain environments if not managed properly.
