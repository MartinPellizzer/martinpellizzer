Oxera pulchella subsp. pulchella

Oxera pulchella subsp.

pulchella, commonly known as the silverleaf oxera, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to South America. It features silvery-white, velvety foliage that gives it its common name, and it produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate a range of environmental conditions. Oxera pulchella subsp.

pulchella is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
