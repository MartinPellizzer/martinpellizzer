Ophelia racemosa

Ophelia racemosa, commonly known as the water crowfoot, is a flowering plant native to freshwater environments in the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating or submerged leaves that are typically divided into small, feathery segments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, often emerging from the water surface. Ophelia racemosa thrives in shallow, still or slow-moving waters and plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems.

Its rapid growth and ability to colonize water bodies make it both a valuable indicator species and a potential invasive plant in non-native habitats.
