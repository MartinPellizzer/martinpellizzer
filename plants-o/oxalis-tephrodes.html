Oxalis tephrodes

Oxalis tephrodes, commonly known as the white wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Oxalis tephrodes thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

It is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
