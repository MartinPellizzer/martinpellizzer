Oncidium sellowii

Oncidium sellowii, commonly known as the Sellow's orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to South America.

It features a sympodial growth habit with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flower spikes with multiple flowers in a spiral arrangement. Its leaves are typically strap-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern.

Oncidium sellowii thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining potting media for optimal growth.
