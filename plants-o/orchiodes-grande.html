Orchiodes grande

Orchiodes grande, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, featuring intricate patterns of purple, white, and yellow. The plant has a pseudobulb structure, which stores water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the forest floor.

Orchiodes grande is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
