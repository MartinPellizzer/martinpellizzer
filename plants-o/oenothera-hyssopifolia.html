Oenothera hyssopifolia

Oenothera hyssopifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and the stems are often hairy.

Oenothera hyssopifolia is part of the evening primrose family (Onagraceae) and is known for its ecological importance in supporting pollinator populations.
