Oxytropis nigrescens var. pygmaea

Oxytropis nigrescens var. pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf locoweed, is a low-growing perennial legume native to the western United States.

It typically thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sagebrush steppe and rocky soils. The plant features small, hairy leaves and produces yellow to reddish-purple pea-like flowers. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and ability to survive in nutrient-poor conditions.

This variety is part of the Fabaceae family, which includes many nitrogen-fixing plants.
