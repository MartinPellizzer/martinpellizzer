Ononis adenotricha var. stenophylla

Ononis adenotricha var. stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leafed whitlow grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in dry, calcareous soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. The plant features slender, erect stems with small, lanceolate leaves that are densely covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are pale yellow and arranged in dense clusters, blooming from late spring to early summer.

This variety is characterized by its compact growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal in dry garden settings.
