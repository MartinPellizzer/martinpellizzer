Oxalis comberi

Oxalis comberi, commonly known as the yellow woodsorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and forest floors. It produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine.

Oxalis comberi is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
