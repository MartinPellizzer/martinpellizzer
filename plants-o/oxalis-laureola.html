Oxalis laureola

Oxalis laureola, commonly known as the golden oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves that resemble those of the shamrock. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Oxalis laureola is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
