Oncinema roxburghii

Oncinema roxburghii, commonly known as the Indian sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant wood, which is highly valued in perfumery and traditional medicine. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, waxy surface. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds.

The tree is often cultivated for its essential oils and is also used in religious and cultural practices.
