Oncidium papilio var. latourae

Oncidium papilio var. latourae, commonly known as the Latour's butterfly orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, butterfly-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange with contrasting dark markings. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its inflorescence consists of multiple flowers arranged in a raceme, often blooming in late winter to early spring.

The leaves are long, narrow, and arranged in a fan-like pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
