Odontoglossum clowesii

Odontoglossum clowesii, commonly known as the Clowes's orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, typical of many orchid genera. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with intricate markings. Its leaves are strap-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water conservation.

Odontoglossum clowesii thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
