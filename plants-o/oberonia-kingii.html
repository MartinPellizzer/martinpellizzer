Oberonia kingii

Oberonia kingii, commonly known as the King's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the rainforests of New Guinea and northeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and is often found in understorey vegetation. Oberonia kingii is known for its intricate floral structure, which includes a spurred labellum and a column.

It is a rare and endangered species, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
