Oreopanax echinops

Oreopanax echinops, commonly known as the spiny oreopanax, is a tropical plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves that are often covered in small, spiny protrusions. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its growth habit is usually shrub-like, with a woody stem and dense foliage.

Oreopanax echinops is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.
