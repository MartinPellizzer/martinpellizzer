Odontarrhena argentea

Odontarrhena argentea, commonly known as silverleaf tamarind, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and alternate, ovate leaves that are silver-white in color due to a dense covering of fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by elongated pods containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Odontarrhena argentea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions.
