Oenothera serrulata subsp. drummondii

Oenothera serrulata subsp.

drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that open in the evening and close by morning.

The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are serrated and linear, and it produces capsules that contain numerous seeds.

Drummond's evening primrose is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
