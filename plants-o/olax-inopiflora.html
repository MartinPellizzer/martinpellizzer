Olax inopiflora

Olax inopiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved olax, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Olacaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, with a leathery texture.

Olax inopiflora is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
