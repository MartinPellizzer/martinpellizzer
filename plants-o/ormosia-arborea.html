Ormosia arborea

Ormosia arborea, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. Its fruit is a woody pod that contains several elliptical seeds.

Ormosia arborea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
