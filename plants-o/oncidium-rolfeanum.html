Oncidium rolfeanum

Oncidium rolfeanum, commonly known as the Rolfe's orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces striking, long-lasting inflorescences with fragrant, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air and rain, typical of its epiphytic lifestyle.

Oncidium rolfeanum is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its resilience and ornamental value.
