Oxalis potamophila

Oxalis potamophila, commonly known as the river oxalis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features trifoliate leaves with three heart-shaped leaflets, often adorned with distinctive red veins. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white, pink, or red, typically in clusters. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found near water sources.

Oxalis potamophila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
