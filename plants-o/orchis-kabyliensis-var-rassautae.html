Orchis kabyliensis var. rassautae

Orchis kabyliensis var. rassautae, commonly known as the Kabylian orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to the Mediterranean region, particularly in Algeria.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique floral structure and coloration. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, thriving in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its flowers are usually violet or purple, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts.
