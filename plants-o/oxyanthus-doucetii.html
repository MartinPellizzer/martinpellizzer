Oxyanthus doucetii

Oxyanthus doucetii, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of bright yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant floral display.

Oxyanthus doucetii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
