Oenothera albicaulis subvar. coquimbensis

Oenothera albicaulis subvar. coquimbensis, commonly known as the Coquimbo evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Chile.

It is characterized by its showy, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along coastal dunes and cliffs, demonstrating adaptability to arid and saline conditions. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered with fine hairs to reduce water loss.

The species is part of the Onagraceae family, known for its diverse ecological roles and ornamental value.
