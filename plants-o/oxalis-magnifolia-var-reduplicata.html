Oxalis magnifolia var. reduplicata

Oxalis magnifolia var. reduplicata, commonly known as the double-leaf wood sorrel, is a striking plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed, giving the appearance of having two leaves attached at the base. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in spring. Its foliage is often a vibrant green, with some cultivars displaying variegated patterns.

It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.
