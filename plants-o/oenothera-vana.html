Oenothera vana

Oenothera vana, commonly known as the plains evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow or orange flowers that open in the evening. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and its flowers are pollinated primarily by moths.

Oenothera vana is a perennial species that produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.
