Oxalis parvifolia var. pluriflora

Oxalis parvifolia var. pluriflora, commonly known as the small-leaf wood sorrel, is a delicate perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with small, heart-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of tiny, five-petaled yellow flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas. Its foliage and flowers are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
