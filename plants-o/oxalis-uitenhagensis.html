Oxalis uitenhagensis

Oxalis uitenhagensis, commonly known as the yellow wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Oxalis uitenhagensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in gardens.
