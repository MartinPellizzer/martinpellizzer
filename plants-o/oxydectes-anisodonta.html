Oxydectes anisodonta

Oxydectes anisodonta, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, white, night-blooming flowers that emit a strong fragrance. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various soil types. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, while its stems are often hairy.
