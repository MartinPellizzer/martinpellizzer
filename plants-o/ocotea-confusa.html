Ocotea confusa

Ocotea confusa, commonly known as the Brazilian camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and smooth, dark bark. The plant produces small, white flowers and yellowish fruits that contain aromatic seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their medicinal properties.

Ocotea confusa is valued for its essential oils, which are used in perfumery and as a natural insect repellent.
