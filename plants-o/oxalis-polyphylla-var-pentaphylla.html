Oxalis polyphylla var. pentaphylla

Oxalis polyphylla var. pentaphylla, commonly known as the five-leaf wood sorrel, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features distinctive trifoliate and pentafoliate leaves, which give it its common name and contribute to its ornamental appeal. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in native plant landscapes for their aesthetic and ecological value.
