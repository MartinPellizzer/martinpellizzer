Otiophora hirsuta

Otiophora hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy tamarind, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant is notable for its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It produces yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds.

The species is often used in traditional medicine and has potential agricultural applications due to its adaptability to various soil types.
