Ophrys cornuta var. balcanica

Ophrys cornuta var. balcanica, commonly known as the Balkan horned orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, bee-mimicking flowers that play a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in calcareous grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually yellow with a distinctive horn-like spur, and it produces small, dark brown seeds in elongated pods.

This variety is noted for its ecological significance and is often found in specific microhabitats across the region.
