Ophrys turcomanica

Ophrys turcomanica, commonly known as the Turkestan bee orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique floral structure that mimics the appearance of a female bee, a trait known as sexual deception. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate to cold climates. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or brown, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by male bees.

Ophrys turcomanica is relatively rare and is often found in specific habitats, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
