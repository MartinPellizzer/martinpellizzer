Oenocarpus bataua var. oligocarpus

Oenocarpus bataua var. oligocarpus, commonly known as the African oil palm, is a tropical plant native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves and produces clusters of fruit that contain a high amount of oil. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its fruit is widely used for oil extraction, which is a significant agricultural product in the region.

The plant also has cultural and economic importance in many African countries.
