Oenothera contorta var. pubens

Oenothera contorta var. pubens, commonly known as the pubescent evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats like prairies and disturbed areas.

The species is known for its medicinal properties, with its seeds containing essential fatty acids.
