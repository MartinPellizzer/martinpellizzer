Oenothera multijuga

Oenothera multijuga, commonly known as the plains evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in open prairies and disturbed soils. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a hairy texture.

Oenothera multijuga is known for its ability to thrive in dry, sandy conditions and is often used in native plant gardens.
