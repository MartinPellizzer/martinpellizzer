Ovieda corymbosa

Ovieda corymbosa, commonly known as the Mexican flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its showy, red-orange flowers that appear in clusters, giving it a striking appearance. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Ovieda corymbosa thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
