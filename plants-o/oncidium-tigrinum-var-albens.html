Oncidium tigrinum var. albens

Oncidium tigrinum var. albens, commonly known as the White Tiger Orchid, is a hybrid orchid species that exhibits distinctive botanical features.

It is characterized by its long, pendant-like inflorescences that bear clusters of white to pale yellow flowers with contrasting dark markings. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its native tropical habitats. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and arranged in a fan-like pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The species is prized for its striking appearance and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in horticulture.
