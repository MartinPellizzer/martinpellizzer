Ophrys × arachnitiformis f. nicaeensis

Ophrys × arachnitiformis f. nicaeensis, commonly known as the Nicaean bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

It is a terrestrial herb with a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a single, bee-like flower with a distinctive hooded structure. The plant is native to the Balkan region and thrives in moist, calcareous soils. Its floral morphology mimics the appearance of a female bee, a feature that plays a key role in its pollination strategy.

This rare and specialized orchid is of interest to botanists due to its hybrid origin and ecological adaptations.
