Oxydectes corchoropsis

Oxydectes corchoropsis, commonly known as the woolly leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often with a distinctive hairy texture on its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
