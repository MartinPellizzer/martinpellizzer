Oxalis corniculata var. radicosa

Oxalis corniculata var. radicosa, commonly known as the crimson wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial native to Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves with reddish veins and small, five-petaled flowers that range from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has a fibrous root system. It is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
