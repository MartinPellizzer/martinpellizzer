Obione polygama

Obione polygama, commonly known as the golden star lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of East Asia.

It features striking yellow flowers with a star-like shape, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern at the base. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from moist, well-drained soils.

Obione polygama is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
