Otophora nigrescens

Otophora nigrescens, commonly known as the black-veined orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black veining on its leaves and petals, which gives it its common name. Native to the tropical regions of South America, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, with a unique structure that aids in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Otophora nigrescens is considered a conservation concern.
