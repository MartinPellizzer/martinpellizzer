Osbeckia rostrata var. pulchella

Osbeckia rostrata var. pulchella, commonly known as the purple sandverbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are usually violet or purple in color, arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine, soft hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.
