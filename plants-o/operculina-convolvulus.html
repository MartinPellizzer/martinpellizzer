Operculina convolvulus

Operculina convolvulus, commonly known as the velvetleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is a herbaceous annual vine characterized by its hairy, sprawling stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, often with a distinctive yellow or orange throat. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, kidney-shaped seeds.

Operculina convolvulus is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
