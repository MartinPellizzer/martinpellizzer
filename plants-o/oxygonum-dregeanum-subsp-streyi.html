Oxygonum dregeanum subsp. streyi

Oxygonum dregeanum subsp.

streyi, commonly known as the Streyn's oxalis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It features basal leaves that are trifoliate, with three heart-shaped leaflets, and stems that are often reddish in color.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
