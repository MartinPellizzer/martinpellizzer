Omoea

Omoea, commonly known as the Hawaiian hibiscus, is a genus of flowering plants native to the Hawaiian Islands.

These plants are characterized by their showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, yellow, and white. Omoea species are typically shrubs or small trees with simple, alternate leaves and showy bracts. They play an important role in the ecological and cultural heritage of Hawaii.

The genus is part of the Malvaceae family, which includes other hibiscus species.
