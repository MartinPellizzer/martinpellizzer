Ottoschmidtia dorsiventralis

Ottoschmidtia dorsiventralis, commonly known as the southern African mountain grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white or pale green. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Ottoschmidtia dorsiventralis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates.
