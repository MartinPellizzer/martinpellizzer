Oncidium trilobum

Oncidium trilobum, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers with a distinctive three-lobed lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Oncidium trilobum is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its long-lasting, showy blooms.
