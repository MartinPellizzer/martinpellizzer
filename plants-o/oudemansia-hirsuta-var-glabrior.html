Oudemansia hirsuta var. glabrior

Oudemansia hirsuta var. glabrior, commonly known as the silky bristlegrass, is a variant of the species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, bristly stems and leaves, which become smoother in this variety. The plant typically grows in grassland habitats and is adapted to arid or semi-arid conditions. Its flowering period occurs during the warmer months, producing small, inconspicuous flowers.

This variant is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphological traits and ecological adaptations.
