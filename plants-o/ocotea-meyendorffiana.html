Ocotea meyendorffiana

Ocotea meyendorffiana, commonly known as the Mexican laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America and northern South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and avocado. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall, featuring glossy, dark green, elliptical leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its flowers are small, clustered, and typically white or pale green, while the fruit is a small, woody drupe.

The plant is valued for its fragrant wood, which is used in furniture and as a source of essential oils.
