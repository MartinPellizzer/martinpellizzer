Oncidium limminghei

Oncidium limminghei, commonly known as the Limminghei orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, fragrant flower structure. The plant typically produces clusters of yellow and green flowers with a distinctive lip, which is a key feature in orchid taxonomy. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Oncidium limminghei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
