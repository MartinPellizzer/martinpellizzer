Orchis incarnata var. ochroleuca

Orchis incarnata var. ochroleuca, commonly known as the pale orchis, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features tall, erect stems that bear clusters of showy, pale yellow to white flowers with a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

The orchis has a complex root system and is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
