Onobroma caeruleum var. pinnatifidum

Onobroma caeruleum var. pinnatifidum, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often divided, giving it a delicate appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its showy blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.
