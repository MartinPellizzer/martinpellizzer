Ophrys demangei

Ophrys demangei, commonly known as the Demange's bee orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its intricate flower structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually a mix of yellow, brown, and black, with a distinctive pattern that enhances its mimicry.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Ophrys demangei is considered a vulnerable species.
