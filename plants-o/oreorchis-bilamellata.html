Oreorchis bilamellata

Oreorchis bilamellata, commonly known as the two-lipped twayblade, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant has a unique morphology, featuring a slender, leafless stem with two distinct, bilaterally symmetrical leaves. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellow, with three sepals and three petals, and it lacks a typical labellum.

Oreorchis bilamellata is known for its specialized pollination mechanisms and is often found in association with specific fungal mycorrhizae.
