Ophiocolea comorensis

Ophiocolea comorensis, commonly known as the Comoro starwort, is a flowering plant native to the Comoros archipelago in the Indian Ocean.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine pubescence.

Ophiocolea comorensis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
