Oncidium orgyale

Oncidium orgyale, commonly known as the Himalayan orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, fragrant flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas, preferring cool and moist conditions. Its pseudobulbs store water, allowing it to survive in harsh mountain environments.

Oncidium orgyale is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
