Ossaea glomerata

Ossaea glomerata, commonly known as the clustered grass veldt, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically clustered at the base of the stems. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Ossaea glomerata is often found in grassland and coastal dune habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
