Oldenlandia echinulosa var. pellucida

Oldenlandia echinulosa var. pellucida, commonly known as the glassy bluemink, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, slender stems and small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and covered with fine, translucent hairs that give the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique visual appeal.
