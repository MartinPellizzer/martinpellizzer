Origanum pampaninii

Origanum pampaninii, commonly known as the Pampas marjoram, is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal and culinary uses. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its aromatic qualities.

Origanum pampaninii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of essential oils.
