Olsynium scirpoideum subsp. scirpeum

Olsynium scirpoideum subsp.

scirpeum, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulrush, is a wetland plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in marshy or aquatic environments, often forming dense stands in shallow water.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that bloom in the summer. It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and colonize new areas.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wetland organisms.
