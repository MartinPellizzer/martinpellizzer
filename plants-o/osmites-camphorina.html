Osmites camphorina

Osmites camphorina, commonly known as the camphor tree, is a species native to parts of Asia, particularly China, Japan, and India.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, with a broad, spreading canopy. The leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a serrated margin, while the bark is smooth and grayish in color. The tree produces small, greenish flowers that are followed by woody fruits containing seeds.

It is valued for its aromatic wood and historical use in traditional medicine.
