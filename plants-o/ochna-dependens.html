Ochna dependens

Ochna dependens, commonly known as the hanging ochna, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its pendulous clusters of yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has simple, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

Ochna dependens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
