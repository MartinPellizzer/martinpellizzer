Oenothera parviflora var. angustissima

Oenothera parviflora var. angustissima, commonly known as the narrow-leaved evening primrose, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, pale yellow flowers that open in the evening. The leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy surface, and the plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments, making it a resilient member of the evening primrose family.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
