Odontoglossum megalophium

Odontoglossum megalophium, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter. The plant typically grows in cool, humid environments at high altitudes, often in rocky or forested areas. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, and it produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Odontoglossum megalophium is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
