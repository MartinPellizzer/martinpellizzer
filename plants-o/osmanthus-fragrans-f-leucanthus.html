Osmanthus fragrans f. leucanthus

Osmanthus fragrans f. leucanthus, commonly known as the white-flowered osmanthus, is a fragrant evergreen shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its small, white, star-shaped flowers that emit a sweet, aromatic scent. The plant produces small, round, yellowish fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its unique floral display and pleasant fragrance make it a valuable addition to both traditional and modern horticulture.
