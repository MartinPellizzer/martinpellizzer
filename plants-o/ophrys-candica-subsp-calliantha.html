Ophrys candica subsp. calliantha

Ophrys candica subsp.

calliantha, commonly known as the Sicilian bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to southern Italy. It is characterized by its striking floral morphology, featuring a lip that mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination.

The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky outcrops. Its flowers are usually a combination of yellow and brown, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
