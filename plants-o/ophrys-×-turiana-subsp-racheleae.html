Ophrys × turiana subsp. racheleae

Ophrys × turiana subsp.

racheleae, commonly known as the Racheleae orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its intricate floral structure, which mimics the appearance of female insects, a trait known as pseudocopulation.

The plant produces showy, bee-pollinated flowers with a distinctive coloration and pattern. It typically grows in calcareous soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments.

This subspecies is noted for its rarity and is often found in specific microhabitats within its native range.
