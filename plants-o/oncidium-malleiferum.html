Oncidium malleiferum

Oncidium malleiferum, commonly known as the chocolate orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, fragrant flowers that range in color from yellow to brown, resembling chocolate. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its pseudobulbs are elongated and cylindrical, and it produces multiple inflorescences throughout the growing season.

Oncidium malleiferum requires bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.
