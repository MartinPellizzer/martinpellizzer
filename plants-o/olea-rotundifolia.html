Olea rotundifolia

Olea rotundifolia, commonly known as the olive tree, is a deciduous tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are rounded or oval in shape. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into the familiar dark green, fleshy olives. Its fruit is widely used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Olea rotundifolia is also valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in agriculture.
