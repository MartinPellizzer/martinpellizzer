Ornithogalum majus

Ornithogalum majus, commonly known as the star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces umbels of white, star-shaped flowers in spring. The plant belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous underground storage organs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is widely grown in temperate regions.
