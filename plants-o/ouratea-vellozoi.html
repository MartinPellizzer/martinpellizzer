Ouratea vellozoi

Ouratea vellozoi, commonly known as the Brazilian beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic rainforests of Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite, and elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. Its flowers are small, clustered, and usually white or pale pink, while its fruit is a spherical, bright purple berry that contains several seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage and berries.
