Oncidium platynaris

Oncidium platynaris, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, long-lasting yellow flowers with a distinctive golden throat. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, enabling it to thrive in varying conditions.

Oncidium platynaris is popular among orchid growers for its vibrant blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
