Olearia canescens

Olearia canescens, commonly known as silver olearia, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils and maritime conditions. It produces small, tubular flowers that bloom in spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Olearia canescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
