Odontoglossum × excellens

Odontoglossum × excellens, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Odontoglossum × lindenii and Odontoglossum × sanguineum.

It is native to the highlands of Colombia and Ecuador, where it thrives in cool, humid environments. The plant produces striking, showy flowers with vibrant pink and white petals, often with a contrasting lip. It is a sympodial orchid, meaning it grows in a branching pattern with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated by enthusiasts for its beauty and unique floral structure.
