Oenothera scapoidea var. parryi

Oenothera scapoidea var. parryi, commonly known as Parry's evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and showy yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oily seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as sagebrush steppe.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential medicinal applications.
