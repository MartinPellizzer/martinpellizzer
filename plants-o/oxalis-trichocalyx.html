Oxalis trichocalyx

Oxalis trichocalyx, commonly known as the velvetleaf wood sorrel, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features trifoliate leaves with a deep green color and a velvety texture, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically yellow or orange, often with five petals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

Oxalis trichocalyx is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
