Oenothera grandiflora var. tracyi

Oenothera grandiflora var. tracyi, commonly known as Tracy's evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It features showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are typically 2 to 4 inches in diameter, and the plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in desert or semi-arid environments.
