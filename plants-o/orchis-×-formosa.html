Orchis × formosa

Orchis × formosa, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a natural hybrid between Orchis morio and Orchis purpurea.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats across Europe and parts of Asia. Its flowers are bilaterally symmetrical, with a distinct lip and column, typical of the Orchidaceae family.

Orchis × formosa is often found in shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
