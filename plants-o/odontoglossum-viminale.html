Odontoglossum viminale

Odontoglossum viminale, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces pendant, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. Odontoglossum viminale thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both horticultural and conservation purposes.
