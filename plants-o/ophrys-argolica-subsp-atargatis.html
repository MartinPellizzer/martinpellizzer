Ophrys argolica subsp. atargatis

Ophrys argolica subsp.

atargatis, commonly known as the Argolic bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the Peloponnese region of Greece. It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination.

The plant typically grows in dry, calcareous soils and is often found in open habitats such as rocky slopes and gravelly fields. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow with intricate patterns that aid in deception of pollinators.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
