Onobroma cretica

Onobroma cretica, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade, is a species of flowering plant in the Solanaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and parts of Central America, where it typically grows in dry, rocky soils. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are often pale yellow or greenish-white, which bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is known for its adaptability to arid environments.

Onobroma cretica is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique floral display.
