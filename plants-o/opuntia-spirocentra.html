Opuntia spirocentra

Opuntia spirocentra, commonly known as the spiral cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of cylindrical stems, which are covered in spines and clusters of small, rounded areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible. Opuntia spirocentra is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
