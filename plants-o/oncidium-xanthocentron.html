Oncidium xanthocentron

Oncidium xanthocentron, commonly known as the golden spike orchid, is a hybrid orchid species that exhibits a range of botanical characteristics.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical environments. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, supporting its epiphytic or terrestrial lifestyle. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with intricate patterns and a strong, sweet fragrance.

Oncidium xanthocentron is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.
