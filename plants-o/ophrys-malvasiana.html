Ophrys malvasiana

Ophrys malvasiana, commonly known as the Malva Orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its unique flower structure, which resembles a female bee, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of insects.

Ophrys malvasiana is a protected species in many European countries due to its limited distribution and ecological significance.
