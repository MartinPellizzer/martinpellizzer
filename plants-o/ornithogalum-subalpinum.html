Ornithogalum subalpinum

Ornithogalum subalpinum, commonly known as the alpine cowslip, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to cold climates and grows in alpine and subalpine habitats. It is often found in rocky soils and open meadows at high elevations.

Ornithogalum subalpinum is part of the Asparagaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
