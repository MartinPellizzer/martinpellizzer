Opuntia brandegeei

Opuntia brandegeei, commonly known as the Brandegge prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in spines and glochids, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible fruits known as tunas. Its succulent stems store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

Opuntia brandegeei plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
