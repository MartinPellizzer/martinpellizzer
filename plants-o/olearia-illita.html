Olearia illita

Olearia illita, commonly known as the silverflower, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy or gravelly soils. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Olearia illita is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
