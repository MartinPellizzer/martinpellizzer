Olgaea pectinata

Olgaea pectinata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Olgaea pectinata is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
