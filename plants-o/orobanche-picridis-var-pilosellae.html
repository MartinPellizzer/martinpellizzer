Orobanche picridis var. pilosellae

Orobanche picridis var. pilosellae, commonly known as the woolly broomweed broomrape, is a root-parasitic plant belonging to the Orobanchaceae family.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients, typically parasitizing members of the Fabaceae family. The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in summer. Its structure consists of a short stem with a single, often branched inflorescence.

This species is found in various regions of Europe and is adapted to grow in disturbed soils and open habitats.
