Oxytropis adamsiana

Oxytropis adamsiana, commonly known as the Adams' locoweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, spindly stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, and it is adapted to grow in arid and semi-arid environments.

Oxytropis adamsiana is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, which enhances soil fertility in nutrient-poor soils.
