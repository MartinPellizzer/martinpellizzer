Oenothera octovalvis

Oenothera octovalvis, commonly known as the eight-lobed evening primrose, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oval seeds. Oenothera octovalvis is a member of the Onagraceae family, which includes other evening primroses and related species.

It is often found in dry, disturbed habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.
