Orobus lacaitae

Orobus lacaitae, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a species of orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including pink, white, and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the humid air, and it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

Orobus lacaitae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
