Ocotea sprucei

Ocotea sprucei, commonly known as the black pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and laurel. The plant is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, clustered flowers that eventually develop into dark blue berries. Its wood is valued for its durability and aromatic qualities, making it useful in construction and as a source of essential oils.

Ocotea sprucei plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
