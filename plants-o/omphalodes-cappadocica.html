Omphalodes cappadocica

Omphalodes cappadocica, commonly known as the Cappadocian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with toothed margins. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
