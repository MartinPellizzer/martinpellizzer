Ophrys sphegodes subsp. provincialis

Ophrys sphegodes subsp.

provincialis, commonly known as the Provincial Orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which closely resembles the appearance of a wasp, a trait known as mimicry.

This mimicry plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy, attracting male wasps that attempt to mate with the flower. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species in many areas.
