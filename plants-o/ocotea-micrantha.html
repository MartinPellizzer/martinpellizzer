Ocotea micrantha

Ocotea micrantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage and small, dark berries. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth, glossy surface.

Ocotea micrantha is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
