Oxalis deppei

Oxalis deppei, commonly known as the golden sun-rose, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a striking yellow-orange coloration, often resembling the sun. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Oxalis deppei is also known for its ability to tolerate drought conditions once established.
