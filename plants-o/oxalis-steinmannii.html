Oxalis steinmannii

Oxalis steinmannii, commonly known as the mountain oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, delicate flowers that resemble those of the common wood sorrel. The plant produces small, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive veining pattern. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun exposure.

Oxalis steinmannii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
