Ouratella lherminieri

Ouratella lherminieri, commonly known as the mountain holly.

It is a flowering plant native to the Andes, specifically found in Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are leathery and have a distinctive waxy texture, while its fruit is a small, three-lobed berry.

It thrives in high-altitude environments and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
