Olea × japonica

Olea × japonica, commonly known as the Japanese olive, is a hybrid shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the olive family, Oleaceae, and is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and fragrant, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.

However, it can become invasive in some regions, posing a threat to native ecosystems.
