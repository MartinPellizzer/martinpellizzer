Oxalis corniculata var. minima

Oxalis corniculata var. minima, commonly known as the miniature wood sorrel, is a small flowering plant native to Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive veins and produces clusters of five-petaled yellow flowers. The plant is often found in shaded, moist environments such as forests and gardens. It is a member of the Oxalidaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through runners.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and as a ground cover.
