Osmundastrum pilosum

Osmundastrum pilosum, commonly known as the hairy cinnamon fern, is a fern species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender fronds that are covered in soft, fuzzy hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomes are thick and fleshy, allowing it to store water and nutrients effectively.

Osmundastrum pilosum plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
