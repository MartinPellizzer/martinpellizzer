Ornithogalum lanatum

Ornithogalum lanatum, commonly known as the woolly cowslip, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of soft, hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped, white or pale yellow flowers with six petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
