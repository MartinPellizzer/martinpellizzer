Orchis latifolia subsp. majalis

Orchis latifolia subsp.

majalis, commonly known as the broad-leaved orchid, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, violet or purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and it produces numerous small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a spike. Orchis latifolia subsp.

majalis is a protected species in many regions due to its ecological significance and declining populations.
