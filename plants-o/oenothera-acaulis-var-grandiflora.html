Oenothera acaulis var. grandiflora

Oenothera acaulis var. grandiflora, commonly known as the showy evening primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It is characterized by its erect, hairless stems and large, showy yellow flowers that open in the evening. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and pollinator support.
