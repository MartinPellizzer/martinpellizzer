Ochyrorchis jaegeri

Ochyrorchis jaegeri, commonly known as the Jaeger's leek, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tubular, white to pale green flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions. Its leaves are basal, linear, and often have a leek-like appearance, which contributes to its common name.

Ochyrorchis jaegeri is a perennial plant that thrives in well-drained environments and is relatively rare in cultivation.
