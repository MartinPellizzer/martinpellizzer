Orthosiphon transvaalensis

Orthosiphon transvaalensis, commonly known as the Transvaal senna, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically blooming during the warmer months. Its aromatic leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
