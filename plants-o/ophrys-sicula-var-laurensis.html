Ophrys sicula var. laurensis

Ophrys sicula var. laurensis, commonly known as the Sicilian bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the island of Sicily.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brown, with intricate patterns that aid in its deceptive pollination strategy.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
