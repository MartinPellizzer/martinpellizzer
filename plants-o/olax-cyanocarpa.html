Olax cyanocarpa

Olax cyanocarpa, commonly known as the blue-fruited olax, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Olacaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically blue or black when mature, containing a single seed. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
