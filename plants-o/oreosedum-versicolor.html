Oreosedum versicolor

Oreosedum versicolor, commonly known as the variable stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming in late summer. Oreosedum versicolor thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ability to store water in its leaves allows it to survive in arid environments.
