Orchis pinetorum

Orchis pinetorum, commonly known as the pine orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and pine forests. The plant features a tall stem with lance-shaped leaves and produces striking, showy orchid flowers in shades of purple and white. Its flowers are characterized by a distinctive three-lobed lip, a feature common among orchids.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils, often forming dense colonies in its natural habitat.
