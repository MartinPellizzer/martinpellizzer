Ophrys kopetdagensis

Ophrys kopetdagensis, commonly known as the Kopetdagan orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its intricate, bee-mimicking flower structure, which plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in environments with limited moisture. Its flowers exhibit a range of colors, often resembling the body of a female bee to attract male pollinators.

Due to its habitat specificity and threats from human activity, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
