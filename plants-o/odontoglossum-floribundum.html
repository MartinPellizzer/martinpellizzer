Odontoglossum floribundum

Odontoglossum floribundum, commonly known as the showy orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant with a rhizomatous root system that allows it to survive in varying environmental conditions. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Odontoglossum floribundum is valued in horticulture for its beauty and is often cultivated in shaded garden environments.
