Oxalis callosa

Oxalis callosa, commonly known as the California wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and small, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its stems are often hairy, and it produces capsule fruits that contain multiple seeds.

Oxalis callosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
