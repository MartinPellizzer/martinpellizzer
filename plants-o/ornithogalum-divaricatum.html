Ornithogalum divaricatum

Ornithogalum divaricatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features grass-like leaves that are narrow and slightly curled, with a basal rosette growth habit. The plant produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers during the spring and summer months. It is typically found in open grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils.

Ornithogalum divaricatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
