Oenanthe benghalensis

Oenanthe benghalensis, commonly known as the Indian cowpea or wild cowpea, is a flowering plant native to South and Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual that grows up to 1.5 meters tall, characterized by its slender stems and alternate leaves with a heart-shaped base. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by spherical, hairy fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks.

Oenanthe benghalensis is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
