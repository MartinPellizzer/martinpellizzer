Ononis hispida var. loberae

Ononis hispida var. loberae, commonly known as the hairy cinquefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately divided, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is often found in alpine and subalpine environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
