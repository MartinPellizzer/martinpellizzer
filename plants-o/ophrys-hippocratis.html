Ophrys hippocratis

Ophrys hippocratis, commonly known as the Hippocrates' bee orchid, is a species of orchid native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, open habitats. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brown in color, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Ophrys hippocratis is a rare and protected species, often found in specific microhabitats across its native range.
