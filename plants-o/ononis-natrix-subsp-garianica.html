Ononis natrix subsp. garianica

Ononis natrix subsp.

garianica, commonly known as the narrowleaf bedstraw, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in dry, rocky, or sandy soils, often in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

The plant features slender, branching stems covered with small, alternate leaves that are usually narrow and lance-shaped. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and are arranged in loose clusters, blooming from late spring to early summer.

The species is characterized by its fibrous root system and is often found in disturbed or marginal habitats.
