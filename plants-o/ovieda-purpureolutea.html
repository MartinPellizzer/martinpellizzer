Ovieda purpureolutea

Ovieda purpureolutea, commonly known as the purple-leaved ovieda, is a species of flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking purple foliage, which becomes more intense during the cooler months. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, adding visual interest to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
