Ornithogalum leipoldtii

Ornithogalum leipoldtii, commonly known as the Leipoldt's star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking white, star-shaped flowers during the summer months. The plant is characterized by its underground bulb, which serves as a storage organ and helps it survive dry periods. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
