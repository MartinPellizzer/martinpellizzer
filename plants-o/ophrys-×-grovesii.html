Ophrys × grovesii

Ophrys × grovesii, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Ophrys apifera (the bee orchid) and Ophrys insectifera (the wasp orchid), resulting in a blend of traits from both parents. The plant is notable for its intricate floral morphology, particularly its labellum, which closely resembles a female bee or wasp to attract male pollinators. It typically grows in calcareous soils in grasslands and woodland edges across Europe.

Ophrys × grovesii is an example of how hybridization can lead to the development of new, specialized plant forms with unique ecological roles.
