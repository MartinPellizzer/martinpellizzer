Opuntia festiva

Opuntia festiva, commonly known as the festive prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, fleshy pads called cladodes, which are covered in small spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the summer months. Its fruit, known as a tunaf, is edible and contains numerous seeds.

Opuntia festiva is well adapted to arid environments and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of food.
