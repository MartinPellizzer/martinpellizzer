Ocimum calycosum

Ocimum calycosum, commonly known as holy basil or tulsi, is a member of the Lamiaceae family.

It is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Asia, particularly India. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine and religious practices. Its small, tubular flowers are typically white or light purple, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing seeds.

Ocimum calycosum is valued for its medicinal properties, including antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects.
