Ornithogalum libanoticum

Ornithogalum libanoticum, commonly known as the Lebanon star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of star-shaped, white flowers in early spring. The plant belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous underground stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or arid environments.

Ornithogalum libanoticum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
