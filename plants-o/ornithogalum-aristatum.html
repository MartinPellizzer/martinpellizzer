Ornithogalum aristatum

Ornithogalum aristatum, commonly known as the spear lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, sword-shaped leaves and produces tall, erect stems with clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant blooms that appear in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens but requires careful management to prevent it from becoming invasive.
