Orchiodes coloratum

Orchiodes coloratum, commonly known as the violet orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet and white flowers, which bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. The plant has a slender, erect stem with a few leaves along its length. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the forest floor.

Orchiodes coloratum prefers shady, humid environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
