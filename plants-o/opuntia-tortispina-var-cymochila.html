Opuntia tortispina var. cymochila

Opuntia tortispina var. cymochila, commonly known as the Cymochila prickly pear, is a cacti species native to parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems that are covered in spines and are often ribbed. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months, followed by edible, fleshy fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and full sun exposure.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
