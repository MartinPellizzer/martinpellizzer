Oreocharis jasminina

Oreocharis jasminina, commonly known as the jasmine lily, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that resemble those of jasmine. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while the flowers are usually white or pale yellow.

Oreocharis jasminina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aromatic blooms.
