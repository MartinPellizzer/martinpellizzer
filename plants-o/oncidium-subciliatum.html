Oncidium subciliatum

Oncidium subciliatum, commonly known as the Orchid of the Andes, is a terrestrial orchid native to South America.

It features a sympodial growth habit with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with intricate patterns. Its leaves are typically broad and strap-like, arranged in a fan shape.

Oncidium subciliatum thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
