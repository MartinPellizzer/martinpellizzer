Oxytropis leucantha var. magnifica

Oxytropis leucantha var. magnifica, commonly known as the grandiose whitethorn, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and clusters of small, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, rounded leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
