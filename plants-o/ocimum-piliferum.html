Ocimum piliferum

Ocimum piliferum, commonly known as holy basil or tulsi, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine and religious rituals. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other herbs like mint and sage. Its small, tubular flowers are typically pale purple or white, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Ocimum piliferum is valued for its medicinal properties, including antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects.
