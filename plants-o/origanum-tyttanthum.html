Origanum tyttanthum

Origanum tyttanthum, commonly known as narrow-leaved oregano, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its essential oils, rich in thymol and carvacrol, contribute to its strong medicinal and culinary uses.

Origanum tyttanthum is often used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.
