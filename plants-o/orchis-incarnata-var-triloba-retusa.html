Orchis incarnata var. triloba-retusa

Orchis incarnata var. triloba-retusa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf purple twayblade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, nodding purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and wet meadows. It has a symbiotic relationship with mycorrhizal fungi, which aid in nutrient absorption.

The species is notable for its unique floral structure and ecological significance in its native range.
