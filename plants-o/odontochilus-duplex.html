Odontochilus duplex

Odontochilus duplex, commonly known as the twin-toothed orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to thrive in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its compact and elegant appearance.

Odontochilus duplex is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique floral structure and relatively easy care requirements.
