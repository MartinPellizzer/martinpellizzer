Oxalis purpurata var. anthelmintica

Oxalis purpurata var. anthelmintica, commonly known as the purple wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive purple hue and produces clusters of five-petaled, bright yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders. Its foliage and flowers are often used in ornamental gardening for their vibrant color and delicate appearance.

Botanically, it belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its calcifuge nature, preferring alkaline soils.
