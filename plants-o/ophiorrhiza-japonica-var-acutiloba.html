Ophiorrhiza japonica var. acutiloba

Ophiorrhiza japonica var. acutiloba, commonly known as the sharp-lobed Japanese mangrove, is a variant of the mangrove species native to coastal regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its small, ovate leaves with acuminate tips and is typically found in brackish or saline wetlands. The plant has a unique ability to tolerate varying salinity levels, making it well adapted to tidal environments. Its roots form a dense network that helps stabilize coastal soils and prevent erosion.

This species plays an important ecological role in mangrove ecosystems by providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
