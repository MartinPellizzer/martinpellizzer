Oxytropis campestris subsp. johannensis

Oxytropis campestris subsp.

johannensis, commonly known as Johann's field locoweed, is a perennial legume native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has small, compound leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Oxytropis campestris subsp.

johannensis is also known for its allelopathic properties, which can inhibit the growth of nearby plants.
