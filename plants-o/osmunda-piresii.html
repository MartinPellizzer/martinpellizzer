Osmunda piresii

Osmunda piresii, commonly known as the Pires' water fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Osmundaceae and is characterized by its large, feathery fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant is typically found in wet, shady environments such as marshes and along stream banks. Its rhizomes are thick and robust, allowing it to thrive in waterlogged soils.

Osmunda piresii plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
