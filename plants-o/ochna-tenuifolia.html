Ochna tenuifolia

Ochna tenuifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ochna, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate, and often elliptical or ovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange or red berries containing several seeds. Ochna tenuifolia is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and seasonal fruit display.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate to flourish.
