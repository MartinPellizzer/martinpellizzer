Oxalis acetosella f. rosea

Oxalis acetosella f. rosea, commonly known as the purple shamrock, is a variant of the wood sorrel plant.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with trifoliate leaves that display a distinctive purple or reddish hue. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Native to Europe, it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.
