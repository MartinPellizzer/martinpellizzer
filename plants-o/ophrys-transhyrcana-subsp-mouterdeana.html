Ophrys transhyrcana subsp. mouterdeana

Ophrys transhyrcana subsp.

mouterdeana, commonly known as the Mouterdean bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which closely resembles a female bee, a mimicry strategy used to attract male bees for pollination.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brownish in color, with intricate patterns that aid in its pollination mechanism.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
