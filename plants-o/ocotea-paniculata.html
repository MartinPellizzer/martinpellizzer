Ocotea paniculata

Ocotea paniculata, commonly known as the white sand tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, white flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. Ocotea paniculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates.

Its wood is also used in traditional crafts and for fuel due to its high density and durability.
