Oxylobium dorrienii

Oxylobium dorrienii, commonly known as the Dorrien's oxylobium, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical legume structure. The plant features small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets, and it produces pods that contain seeds.

Oxylobium dorrienii is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
