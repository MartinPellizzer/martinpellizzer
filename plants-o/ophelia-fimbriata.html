Ophelia fimbriata

Ophelia fimbriata, commonly known as the fragrant water plantain, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, ribbon-like leaves that float on the surface of freshwater habitats. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. Ophelia fimbriata is often found in still or slow-moving waters such as ponds and lakes.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.
