Ornithogalum flanaganii

Ornithogalum flanaganii, commonly known as the Flanagan's star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers in early to mid-summer. The plant is adapted to dry conditions and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in grasslands and rocky outcrops, where it contributes to local biodiversity.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
