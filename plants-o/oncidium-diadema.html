Oncidium diadema

Oncidium diadema, commonly known as the Crowned Orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a pseudobulb that supports its foliage and flowers. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with a distinctive crown-like pattern on the petals. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for epiphytic growth, allowing it to thrive on tree trunks or in moist, well-drained soil.

Oncidium diadema is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate with proper light and humidity conditions.
