Oxalis ebracteata

Oxalis ebracteata, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, delicate flowers that resemble small, translucent ghosts, hence its common name. The plant has trifoliate leaves, each leaflet being narrow and pointed. It produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

Oxalis ebracteata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and ease of care.
