Ophrys × sipontensis

Ophrys × sipontensis, commonly known as the Sipontine bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is native to the Mediterranean region, particularly found in southern Italy and parts of Greece. The plant is characterized by its striking, bee-like flowers that mimic the appearance of female bees to attract male pollinators. Its inflorescence typically consists of several showy, yellow-brown flowers with intricate patterns.

Ophrys × sipontensis thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
