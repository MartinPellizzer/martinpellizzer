Oenothera hartwegii subsp. pubescens

Oenothera hartwegii subsp.

pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its subspecies name "pubescens.".

The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, typical of the evening primrose genus. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert and semi-desert environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some historical use in traditional medicine.
