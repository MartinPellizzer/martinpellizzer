Oplismenus zelayensis

Oplismenus zelayensis, commonly known as the Zelaya grass, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color.

Oplismenus zelayensis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
