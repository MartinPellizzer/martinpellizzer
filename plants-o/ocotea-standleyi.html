Ocotea standleyi

Ocotea standleyi, commonly known as the black pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its dark, aromatic bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are followed by dark, shiny berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Ocotea standleyi is also valued for its wood, which is durable and used in construction and furniture-making.
