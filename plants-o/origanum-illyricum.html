Origanum illyricum

Origanum illyricum, commonly known as wild marjoram, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly hairy leaves and small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and thyme.

Origanum illyricum is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, particularly for its essential oils and antioxidant properties.
