Ormostema albiflora

Ormostema albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, white petals and a distinctive floral structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are often fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its beauty and unique appearance.
