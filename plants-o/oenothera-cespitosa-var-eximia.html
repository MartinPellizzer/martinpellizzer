Oenothera cespitosa var. eximia

Oenothera cespitosa var. eximia, commonly known as the showy evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features basal rosettes of lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

The species is known for its ecological importance and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
