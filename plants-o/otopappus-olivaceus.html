Otopappus olivaceus

Otopappus olivaceus, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and slender, branched stems. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and often covered with fine hairs, and it produces small, oval seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Otopappus olivaceus is a perennial herb that plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing food for various insect species.
