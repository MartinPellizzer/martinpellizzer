Oxytropis semenowii

Oxytropis semenowii, commonly known as the Semenow's locoweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, spiny stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and can remain viable in the soil for several years.

Oxytropis semenowii is also known for its potential toxicity to livestock, making it a plant of both ecological and agricultural significance.
