Oncidium floridanum

Oncidium floridanum, commonly known as the Florida orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a rhizome that produces a cluster of pseudobulbs. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Oncidium floridanum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its long-lasting blooms.
