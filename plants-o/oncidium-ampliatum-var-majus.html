Oncidium ampliatum var. majus

Oncidium ampliatum var. majus, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 inches in diameter, typically in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant has pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, supporting its growth in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and sword-shaped, contributing to its lush appearance.

Oncidium ampliatum var. majus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and requires well-draining soil and consistent moisture for optimal growth.
