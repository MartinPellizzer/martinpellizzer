Oldenlandia luzuloides

Oldenlandia luzuloides, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that resemble mist. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Oldenlandia luzuloides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
