Oxymeris furfuracea

Oxymeris furfuracea, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces flat, bean-like pods that contain seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood used in construction and furniture-making.
