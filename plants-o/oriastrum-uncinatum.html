Oriastrum uncinatum

Oriastrum uncinatum, commonly known as the hooked oriastrum, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces feathery, bract-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow and bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

Oriastrum uncinatum is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy or gravelly soils.
