Ovieda grandiflora

Ovieda grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered ovieda, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate to subtropical climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Ovieda grandiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking blooms.
