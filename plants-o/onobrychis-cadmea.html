Onobrychis cadmea

Onobrychis cadmea, commonly known as goat's rue, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in dry, calcareous soils. It has a creeping growth habit with hairy stems and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
