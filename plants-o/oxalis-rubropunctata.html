Oxalis rubropunctata

Oxalis rubropunctata, commonly known as the red-spotted wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with red veins and small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and delicate blooms, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Oxalis rubropunctata is also known for its ability to spread through runners, forming dense mats in suitable conditions.
