Oenothera silesiaca

Oenothera silesiaca, commonly known as the Silesian evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and somewhat hairy.

Oenothera silesiaca is part of the Onagraceae family, which includes other evening primroses and related species.
