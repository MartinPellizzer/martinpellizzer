Ophiorrhiza longifolia

Ophiorrhiza longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved marsh bean, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia and the Indian subcontinent.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in wet, marshy areas and is often found in coastal regions. The plant has opposite, simple, and lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered with fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, and the fruit is a capsule that releases tiny seeds.

Ophiorrhiza longifolia is known for its ability to thrive in saline and waterlogged soils, making it an important species in coastal ecosystems.
