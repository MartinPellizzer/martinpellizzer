Oenanthe stylosa

Oenanthe stylosa, commonly known as the narrowleaf water dropwort, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils along stream banks and wet meadows. The plant features slender, often hairy stems that are usually less than 1 meter in height. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and have a distinctive pungent odor.

The small white flowers are arranged in umbels and bloom in late spring to early summer.
