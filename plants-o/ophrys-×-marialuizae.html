Ophrys × marialuizae

Ophrys × marialuizae, commonly known as the Marialuiz orchid, is a hybrid orchid species belonging to the Ophrys genus, known for its intricate mimicry of female insects.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which includes a complex labellum that resembles the body of a bee or wasp, aiding in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in Mediterranean climates, thriving in well-drained soils and partial shade. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish in color, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

This hybrid is a result of crosses between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera, showcasing the genetic diversity within the Ophrys genus.
