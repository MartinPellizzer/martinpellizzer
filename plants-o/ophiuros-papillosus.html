Ophiuros papillosus

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery arms that extend from a central stem, which is often covered in small, calcareous plates. The plant thrives in shallow coastal waters, where it attaches itself to rocks or other substrates using a root-like structure called a stem. Its arms are covered in tiny, tube-like structures called pinnules, which are used for filter feeding.
