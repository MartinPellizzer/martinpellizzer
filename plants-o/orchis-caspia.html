Orchis caspia

Orchis caspia, commonly known as the Caspian orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It features a basal rosette of lanceolate leaves and produces tall, slender flowering stems. The flowers are typically violet to blue in color, with a distinctive lip that is often darker and more prominently marked. It prefers moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
