Oriastrum dioicum

Oriastrum dioicum, commonly known as the common tansy, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves and is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions.

Oriastrum dioicum is known for its strong scent and has been used historically in herbal remedies.
