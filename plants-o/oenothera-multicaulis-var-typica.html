Oenothera multicaulis var. typica

Oenothera multicaulis var. typica, commonly known as the slender evening primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features elongated, slender stems that often grow in a prostrate or ascending manner. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths. Its leaves are linear and slightly hairy, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its historical use in traditional medicine.
