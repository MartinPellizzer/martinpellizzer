Ophrys × mastii

Ophrys × mastii, commonly known as the bee orchid hybrid, is a fascinating plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera, characterized by its intricate floral structures that mimic female bees. The plant features a single, showy flower with a distinctive bee-like shape, designed to attract male bees for pollination. It thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in open grasslands and woodland edges across Europe.

This orchid plays an important role in its ecosystem, supporting pollinator interactions and contributing to biodiversity.
