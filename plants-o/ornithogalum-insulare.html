Ornithogalum insulare

Ornithogalum insulare, commonly known as the island star-of-Bethlehem, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a bulbous perennial that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal or mountainous regions. The plant produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers during the spring and early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Ornithogalum insulare is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
