Onobrychis afghanica subsp. brachycalyx

Onobrychis afghanica subsp.

brachycalyx, commonly known as Afghan clover, is a perennial legume native to parts of Central Asia. It is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in early spring.

The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces cylindrical, hairy pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
