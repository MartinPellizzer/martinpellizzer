Ouret persica

Ouret persica, commonly known as the Persian apricot, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, which are followed by the development of fleshy, orange-red fruits. The fruit is typically harvested in late summer and is valued for its sweet, aromatic flavor.

Ouret persica is often cultivated for both ornamental purposes and its edible fruit.
