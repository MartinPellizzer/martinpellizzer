Opuntia anahuacensis

Opuntia anahuacensis, commonly known as the Anahuac prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the coastal regions of the Gulf of Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems that are covered in clusters of spines and glochids, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months, followed by edible fruits that are rich in nutrients. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Opuntia anahuacensis plays an important ecological role by providing food and shelter for various desert wildlife species.
