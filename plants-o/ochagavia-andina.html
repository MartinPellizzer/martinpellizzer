Ochagavia andina

Ochagavia andina, commonly known as the Andean flax, is a perennial plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming growth habit and silver-blue foliage, which is covered in fine, hair-like structures called trichomes. The plant produces striking, tall spikes of blue to purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in cool, sunny conditions with well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Ochagavia andina is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique appearance and hardiness.
