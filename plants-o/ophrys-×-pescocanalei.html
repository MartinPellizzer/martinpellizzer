Ophrys × pescocanalei

Ophrys × pescocanalei, commonly known as the Pescocanale Orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its intricate, bee-mimicking flowers that feature a complex pattern of markings and colors designed to attract pollinators. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and thrives in sunny, open habitats. Its floral structure includes a labellum that resembles a female bee, enhancing its mimicry.

Ophrys × pescocanalei is a rare and protected species, often found in specific microhabitats within its native range.
