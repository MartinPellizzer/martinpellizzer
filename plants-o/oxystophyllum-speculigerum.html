Oxystophyllum speculigerum

Oxystophyllum speculigerum, commonly known as the spectre fern, is a rare and unique fern species native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that resemble intricate patterns, giving it a ghostly appearance. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and requires high humidity and well-drained soil to flourish. Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, with spores dispersed through wind.

Due to habitat loss, Oxystophyllum speculigerum is considered endangered and is of interest to botanists studying rare plant adaptations.
