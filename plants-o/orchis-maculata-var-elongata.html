Orchis maculata var. elongata

Orchis maculata var. elongata, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height, often with a reddish or purple tinge. The plant produces showy, butterfly-shaped flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white, typically arranged in a loose spike. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

This variety is native to Europe and is often found in temperate regions with rich, well-drained soils.
