Ophrys fusca subsp. funerea

Ophrys fusca subsp.

funerea, commonly known as the funeral orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to southern Europe. It is a terrestrial herb with a distinctive appearance, featuring dark brown to blackish pseudobulbs and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces a single, showy flower with a complex structure, including a labellum that mimics the appearance of an insect, aiding in pollination. It thrives in shaded, moist habitats such as woodlands and rocky slopes.

The subspecies is notable for its dark coloration and its role in the intricate mutualistic relationships with specific pollinators.
