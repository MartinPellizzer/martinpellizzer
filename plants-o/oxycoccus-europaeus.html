Oxycoccus europaeus

Oxycoccus europaeus, commonly known as the European cranberry bush, is a small evergreen shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, red berries that ripen in late summer. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and mountainous regions. Its berries are edible and have a tart flavor, making them a traditional ingredient in jams and wines.

Oxycoccus europaeus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting wildlife.
