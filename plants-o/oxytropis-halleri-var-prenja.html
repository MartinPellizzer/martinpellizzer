Oxytropis halleri var. prenja

Oxytropis halleri var. prenja, commonly known as the alpine milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Oxytropis halleri var. prenja is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
