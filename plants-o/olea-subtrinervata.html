Olea subtrinervata

Olea subtrinervata, commonly known as the narrow-veined olive, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves with three distinct veins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruits are small drupes that turn from green to black as they mature.

Olea subtrinervata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
