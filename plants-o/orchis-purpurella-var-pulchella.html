Orchis purpurella var. pulchella

Orchis purpurella var. pulchella, commonly known as the purple orchid, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem bearing a single, showy purple or violet flower. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats. Its flowers are bilaterally symmetrical, with three sepals and three petals, one of which is prominently spurred.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
