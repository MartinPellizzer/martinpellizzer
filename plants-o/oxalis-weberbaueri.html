Oxalis weberbaueri

Oxalis weberbaueri, commonly known as the Weberbauer's wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly in the Andes region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins, and its flowers are usually small, five-petaled, and range in color from white to pink. It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family, known for its calcium oxalate crystals in the leaves, which can cause irritation upon contact.

Oxalis weberbaueri is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and delicate blooms.
