Oxycoccus quadripetalus f. bulbosus

Oxycoccus quadripetalus f. bulbosus, commonly known as the bulbous oxycoccus, is a variant of the common cranberry.

It is a low-growing evergreen shrub with small, dark red berries. The plant is characterized by its bulbous stems and dense, mat-forming growth habit. It thrives in acidic, sandy soils and is typically found in boreal and subarctic regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in cold climates.
