Ophrys insectifera var. trollii

Ophrys insectifera var. trollii, commonly known as the bee orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family characterized by its intricate floral structure designed to mimic the appearance of a female bee.

This mimicry is a key adaptation that facilitates pollination by male bees, which attempt to mate with the flower. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in sunny, well-drained soils, often found in rocky or limestone habitats. It produces a single, downward-facing flower with a distinctive lip that resembles a bee's body, complete with fine hairs and a yellowish marking.

The species is noted for its rare and specialized pollination mechanism, making it an important subject in the study of plant-animal interactions.
