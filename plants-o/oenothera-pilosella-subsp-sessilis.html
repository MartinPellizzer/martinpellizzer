Oenothera pilosella subsp. sessilis

Oenothera pilosella subsp.

sessilis, commonly known as the sessile evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to North America. It is characterized by its hairy stems and showy yellow flowers that open in the evening.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a woolly texture.

The species is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for nocturnal pollinators like moths.
