Ophrys × varvarae

Ophrys × varvarae, commonly known as the Varvara bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is characterized by its intricate floral structure that mimics the appearance of a female bee, a feature known as pseudocopulation, which aids in pollination by male bees. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is found in various parts of Europe, particularly in the Mediterranean region. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brownish in color, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Ophrys × varvarae is a rare and protected species, often found in specific microhabitats that support its unique ecological requirements.
