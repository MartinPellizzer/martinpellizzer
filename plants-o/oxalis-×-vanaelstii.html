Oxalis × vanaelstii

Oxalis × vanaelstii, commonly known as the hybrid wood sorrel, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Oxalis tetraphylla and Oxalis tetramera.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically heart-shaped with a deep green color and a reddish or purple tint on the underside. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with five petals. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, Oxalis × vanaelstii is often used in mixed borders and as a ground cover.
