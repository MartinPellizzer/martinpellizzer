Origanum hirsutum

Origanum hirsutum, commonly known as woolly oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes. Its aromatic leaves are used in culinary and medicinal applications, similar to other members of the Lamiaceae family.

Origanum hirsutum is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects and its drought-resistant nature.
