Oplismenus undulatifolius f. elongatus

Oplismenus undulatifolius f. elongatus, commonly known as the elongated curly willow, is a variant of the curly willow characterized by its elongated, ribbon-like leaves.

This plant is a member of the Polygonaceae family, known for its distinctive, undulating foliage that gives it a flowing, ornamental appearance. The leaves are typically narrow and curl in a spiral pattern, creating a visually striking effect. It is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a houseplant.

The plant prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for humid environments.
