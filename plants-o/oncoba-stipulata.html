Oncoba stipulata

Oncoba stipulata, commonly known as the white sand tree, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are woody capsules that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Oncoba stipulata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
