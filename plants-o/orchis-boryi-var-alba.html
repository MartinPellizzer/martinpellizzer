Orchis boryi var. alba

Orchis boryi var. alba, commonly known as the white marsh orchid, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in wet meadows, marshes, and along riverbanks, preferring moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it produces small, greenish-brown seed pods after flowering.

This orchid is known for its ecological importance, supporting various pollinators and serving as an indicator of healthy, undisturbed habitats.
