Ocimum fimbriatum var. angustilanceolatum

Ocimum fimbriatum var. angustilanceolatum, commonly known as narrow-lanceolate basil, is a variant of the holy basil species native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a dark green with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves used in culinary and medicinal applications.

This variety is closely related to other Ocimum species, sharing similar growth habits and chemical compositions.
