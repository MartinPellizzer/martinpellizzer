Ocimum menthifolium var. australe

Ocimum menthifolium var. australe, commonly known as Australian mint, is a perennial herb native to Australia.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are aromatic and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers arranged in whorled clusters, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its essential oils contain menthol and other compounds, giving it a minty fragrance and various medicinal uses.
