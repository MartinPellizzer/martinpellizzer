Oxalis corniculata var. rubra

Oxalis corniculata var. rubra, commonly known as the redwood sorrel, is a perennial flowering plant native to Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with reddish veins and produces small, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink or red. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes.

Oxalis corniculata var. rubra is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
