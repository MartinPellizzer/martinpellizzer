Orchis × pedemontana

Orchis × pedemontana, commonly known as the Alpine orchid, is a hybrid species native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high altitudes. Its flowers are usually arranged in a loose panicle and are pollinated by various insects, including bees and flies.

Orchis × pedemontana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural environment.
