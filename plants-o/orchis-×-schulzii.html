Orchis × schulzii

Orchis × schulzii, commonly known as the Schultze's twayblade, is a hybrid orchid that occurs naturally in Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with two opposite leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in a spike, lacking the showy petals seen in many other orchids. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

Orchis × schulzii is primarily found in the western and central regions of Europe, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
