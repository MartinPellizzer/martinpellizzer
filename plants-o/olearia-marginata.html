Olearia marginata

Olearia marginata, commonly known as the silver tree, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Olearia marginata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
