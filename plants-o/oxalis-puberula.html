Oxalis puberula

Oxalis puberula, commonly known as the velvetleaf wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with a velvety texture, and its flowers are typically yellow with five petals. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds. Oxalis puberula thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and attractive addition to native plant gardens.
