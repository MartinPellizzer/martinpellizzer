Oncidium kegeljanii

Oncidium kegeljanii, commonly known as the Kegeljan's orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a sympodial orchid with a compact growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically has a short, stout rhizome and features narrow, strap-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a raceme of resupinate flowers, each with three sepals and three petals, with the lip being distinctly marked.

Oncidium kegeljanii is valued in horticulture for its vibrant blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
