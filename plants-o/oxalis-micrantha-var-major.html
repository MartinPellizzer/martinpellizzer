Oxalis micrantha var. major

Oxalis micrantha var. major, commonly known as the large-leaf wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of small, yellow, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its foliage is often covered in fine, white hairs, and it has a fibrous root system.

Oxalis micrantha var. major is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
