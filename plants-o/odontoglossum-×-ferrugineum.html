Odontoglossum × ferrugineum

Odontoglossum × ferrugineum, commonly known as the rusty orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, hairy leaves and showy, violet to purple flowers with a distinctive yellow lip. The plant typically grows in high-altitude cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers bloom in late winter to early spring, adding a vibrant touch to the mountainous landscape.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
