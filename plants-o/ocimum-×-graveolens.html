Ocimum × graveolens

Ocimum × graveolens, commonly known as holy basil, is a hybrid plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia and is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and small, pale purple flowers. The plant is widely cultivated for its medicinal properties and use in culinary applications. Its essential oils contain compounds like eugenol and rosmarinic acid, which contribute to its distinctive scent and therapeutic effects.

Ocimum × graveolens thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
