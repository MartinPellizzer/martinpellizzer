Orbignya martiana

Orbignya martiana, commonly known as the bocoa nut or Brazilian nut tree, is a large evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and compound leaves that are alternate and pinnately arranged. The tree produces yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody pods containing edible seeds. Its seeds, known as Brazil nuts, are enclosed in a hard shell and are commercially valuable.

The plant plays a significant ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
