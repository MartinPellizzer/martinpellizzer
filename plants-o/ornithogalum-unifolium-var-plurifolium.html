Ornithogalum unifolium var. plurifolium

Ornithogalum unifolium var. plurifolium, commonly known as the many-leaved star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green with a slight bluish tint. The plant produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is widely grown in gardens across temperate regions.
