Ophrys holosericea subsp. pallidiconi

Ophrys holosericea subsp.

pallidiconi, commonly known as the Pallid Bee Orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which closely resembles a bee, aiding in its pollination strategy through mimicry.

The plant produces showy, bee-like flowers that are typically pale yellow to brown in color, with intricate patterns that enhance its attractiveness to male bees. It thrives in calcareous soils and requires specific microclimatic conditions for successful growth.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, this subspecies is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
