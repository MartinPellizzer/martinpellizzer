Orostachys rosea

Orostachys rosea, commonly known as the rose sandmat, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white powder, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, pinkish flowers during the summer months, adding a delicate touch to its otherwise desolate appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Orostachys rosea is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
