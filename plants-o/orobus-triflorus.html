Orobus triflorus

Orobus triflorus, commonly known as the three-flowered orchid, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, while its flowers are borne on slender stems.

Orobus triflorus is notable for its unique floral structure, which includes three sepals and three petals, with the lip being distinctly differentiated.
