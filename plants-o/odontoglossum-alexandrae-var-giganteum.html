Odontoglossum alexandrae var. giganteum

Odontoglossum alexandrae var. giganteum, commonly known as the giant Alexandra orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, with a unique combination of colors including purple, white, and yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its botanical features include long, strap-like leaves and a robust root system that helps it thrive in high-altitude environments.

This variety is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and rarity.
