Ochna mossambicensis

Ochna mossambicensis, commonly known as the African yellowwood, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in coastal and inland forested areas. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces woody capsules as fruit.

Ochna mossambicensis is valued for its hard, durable wood and is also used in traditional medicine.
