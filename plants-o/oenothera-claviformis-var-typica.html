Oenothera claviformis var. typica

Oenothera claviformis var. typica, commonly known as the narrow-leaf evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and yellow flowers that open in the evening. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oily seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

The species is noted for its ecological importance as a food source for nocturnal pollinators.
