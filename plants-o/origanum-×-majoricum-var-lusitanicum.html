Origanum × majoricum var. lusitanicum

Origanum × majoricum var. lusitanicum, commonly known as Portuguese oregano, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces dark purple, wrinkled seeds that are used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates.

Its foliage is used to flavor dishes and is also valued for its essential oils.
