Ophiopogon malayanus

Ophiopogon malayanus, commonly known as the Malayan spathiphyllum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Asparagaceae and is characterized by its fleshy, grass-like leaves that grow in dense clusters. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Ophiopogon malayanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
