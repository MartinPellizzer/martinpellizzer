Orchis purpurea var. stenoloba

Orchis purpurea var. stenoloba, commonly known as the narrow-lobed purple orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of purple or violet flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while the flowers are typically arranged in dense spikes. This species thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant plant in its native range.
