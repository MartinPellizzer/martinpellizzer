Oxytropis ochrocephala var. longibracteata

Oxytropis ochrocephala var. longibracteata, commonly known as the long-bract yellow locoweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect stems and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow to greenish flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sagebrush steppe and rocky soils.

Due to its toxic properties, it is considered a noxious weed in some regions.
