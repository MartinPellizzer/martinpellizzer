Origanum vulgare subsp. megastachyum

Origanum vulgare subsp.

megastachyum, commonly known as giant oregano, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of the Mediterranean. It is characterized by its large, gray-green leaves and showy, pale purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves, which are used in cooking and traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its essential oils contain compounds like carvacrol and thymol, which contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.
