Onobrychis gaubae var. kalakensis

Onobrychis gaubae var. kalakensis, commonly known as the Kalaken sainfoin, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical spikes, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This variety is valued for its forage quality and potential in ecological restoration projects.
