Origanum mastichina

Origanum mastichina, commonly known as Greek oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. Its essential oils, rich in thymol and carvacrol, contribute to its strong fragrance and medicinal properties.

It is often used in culinary and traditional herbal applications.
