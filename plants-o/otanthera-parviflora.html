Otanthera parviflora

Otanthera parviflora, commonly known as the narrowleaf aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in late summer to early fall. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils, making it a suitable addition to native plant gardens.

Otanthera parviflora is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
