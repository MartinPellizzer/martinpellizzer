Opuntia canterae

Opuntia canterae, commonly known as the Canary Island prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces edible fruits known as tunas, which are often consumed fresh or processed into jams. Its adaptations to arid environments include reduced leaves and spines for water conservation.

Opuntia canterae plays an important role in local ecosystems and is also valued for its ornamental and agricultural uses.
