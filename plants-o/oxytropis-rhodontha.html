Oxytropis rhodontha

Oxytropis rhodontha, commonly known as the redstem locoweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a deep taproot system that allows it to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, and the plant is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Oxytropis rhodontha is also known for its toxic properties, making it harmful to livestock.
