Ophrys × heraultii

Ophrys × heraultii, commonly known as the Herault orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the genus Ophrys, which is characterized by its intricate mimicry of female insects to attract male pollinators. The plant produces striking, bee-mimicking flowers with a complex structure that facilitates specific pollination mechanisms. Its botanical features include a basal rosette of leaves and a single, showy inflorescence with multiple floral bracts.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitats.
