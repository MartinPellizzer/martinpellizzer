Ouret persica var. pubescens

Ouret persica var. pubescens, commonly known as the fuzzy peach.

This variety is characterized by its hairy or pubescent leaves and stems, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant belongs to the Rosaceae family and is a cultivar of the peach tree, Prunus persica. It typically produces small, yellow fruits that are less sweet compared to standard peaches.

The fuzzy texture of its foliage is a notable botanical feature, often providing a habitat for small insects and microorganisms.
