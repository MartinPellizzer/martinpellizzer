Orthiopteris cicutarioides

Orthiopteris cicutarioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf waterflea fern, is a species of fern native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as along stream banks and in wooded areas. The plant features delicate, feather-like fronds that are usually less than a foot in length. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

This fern is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and is often found in wetland habitats.
