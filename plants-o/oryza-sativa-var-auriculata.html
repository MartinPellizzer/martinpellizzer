Oryza sativa var. auriculata

Oryza sativa var. auriculata, commonly known as the golden rice or the auriculate rice, is a variant of the common rice species.

It is characterized by its distinctive auriculate (ear-like) structures on the leaf sheaths, which give it its unique appearance. This variety is primarily cultivated for its ornamental value rather than for grain production. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and thrives in warm, humid climates.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticultural and genetic research.
