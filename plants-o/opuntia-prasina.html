Opuntia prasina

Opuntia prasina, commonly known as the pale prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its flattened, fleshy stems that are typically pale green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by red, spherical fruits. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.

Opuntia prasina is often used in traditional medicine and is also cultivated for ornamental purposes.
