Oenothera multijuga var. parviflora

Oenothera multijuga var. parviflora, commonly known as the narrowleaf evening primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that open in the evening, hence its common name. Its flowers are characterized by their four petals and numerous stamens, which are a notable botanical feature.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.
