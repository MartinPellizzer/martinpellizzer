Ophrys arachnites var. linearis

Ophrys arachnites var. linearis, commonly known as the linear bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a wasp, a feature evolved to attract male wasps for pollination. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas, often in nutrient-poor soils. Its flowers are usually pale green with dark markings, and it blooms between May and July.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in many regions.
