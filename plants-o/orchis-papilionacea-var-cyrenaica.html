Orchis papilionacea var. cyrenaica

Orchis papilionacea var. cyrenaica, commonly known as the Cyrenaic butterfly orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North Africa and the Mediterranean region.

It features a tall, slender stem that can reach up to 1 meter in height, bearing lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, butterfly-like flowers in shades of pink and purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
