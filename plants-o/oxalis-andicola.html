Oxalis andicola

Oxalis andicola, commonly known as the Andean wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, forested areas. Its foliage contains oxalic acid, which can be toxic if ingested in large quantities.

Oxalis andicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
