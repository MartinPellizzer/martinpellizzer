Oxalis dillenii var. florida

Oxalis dillenii var. florida, commonly known as the Florida wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive reddish veins and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens. Its foliage is often used in floral arrangements due to its delicate appearance.

Botanically, it belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its calcifugous nature, meaning it prefers soil with low calcium content.
