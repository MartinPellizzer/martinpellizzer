Osmanthus ilicifolius var. aureovariegatus

Osmanthus ilicifolius var. aureovariegatus, commonly known as the golden variegated osmanthus, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage with yellow and green patterns on its lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in the fall, which emit a sweet, honey-like scent. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its unique appearance and aromatic blooms make it a valuable addition to both landscape design and herbal medicine.
