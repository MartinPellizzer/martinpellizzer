Oxera sulfurea var. longipetiolata

Oxera sulfurea var. longipetiolata, commonly known as the golden oxera, is a perennial herb native to New Zealand.

It features slender, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with long petioles, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers clustered in umbels, which bloom in late winter to early spring. Its foliage is often covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal in native gardens.
