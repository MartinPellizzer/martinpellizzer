Oedera dieterlenii

Oedera dieterlenii, commonly known as the Dieterlen's ghost orchid, is a rare and elusive orchid species native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent leaves and unique, fragrant flowers that resemble small, ghostly blooms. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on tree trunks or in the understory of dense forests. Its botanical features include a sympodial growth habit and specialized roots that form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, Oedera dieterlenii is considered a conservation concern.
