Oxalis rondeboschensis

Oxalis rondeboschensis, commonly known as the Rondebosch wood sorrel, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive yellow-orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in partial shade. Its foliage is covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Oxalis rondeboschensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
