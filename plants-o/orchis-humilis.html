Orchis humilis

Orchis humilis, commonly known as the common twayblade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, grass-like leaves and its distinctive, pale yellow to greenish-brown flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers are often arranged in a spike and have a unique structure with three sepals and three petals, one of which is showy.

Orchis humilis is known for its ability to form dense colonies and is often found in association with other orchid species in its habitat.
