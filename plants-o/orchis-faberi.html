Orchis faberi

Orchis faberi, commonly known as the Chinese twayblade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and meadows. Orchis faberi has a rhizomatous root system that helps it survive in its natural habitat.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
