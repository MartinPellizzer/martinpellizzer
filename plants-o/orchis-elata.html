Orchis elata

Orchis elata, commonly known as the tall orchid, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while its flowers are arranged in a loose spike.

Orchis elata is known for its ecological importance as a nectar source for various pollinators.
