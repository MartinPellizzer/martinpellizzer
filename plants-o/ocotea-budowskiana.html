Ocotea budowskiana

Ocotea budowskiana, commonly known as the Brazilian cinnamon tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and fragrant bark. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible. It is valued for its essential oils and has been used in traditional medicine.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
