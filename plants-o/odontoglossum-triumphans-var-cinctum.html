Odontoglossum triumphans var. cinctum

Odontoglossum triumphans var. cinctum, commonly known as the Crimson Cinctured Orchid, is a striking orchid native to the Andes of South America.

It features vibrant, showy flowers with a distinctive red or orange lip that is often adorned with a contrasting white or yellow border. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy texture, adapting to the humid, high-altitude conditions it thrives in.

This variety is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and unique coloration.
