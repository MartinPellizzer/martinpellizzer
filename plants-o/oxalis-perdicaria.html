Oxalis perdicaria

Oxalis perdicaria, commonly known as the chickenweed, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which resemble the leaves of the shamrock, and its delicate, five-petaled yellow flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. Oxalis perdicaria thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and flowers.
