Orchis × litardierei

Orchis × litardierei, commonly known as the Litardiere's orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Orchis moria and Orchis mascula.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe, particularly France and Italy. The plant features showy, violet or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

The orchid is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive floral display.
