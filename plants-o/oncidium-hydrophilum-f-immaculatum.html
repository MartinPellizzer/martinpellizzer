Oncidium hydrophilum f. immaculatum

Oncidium hydrophilum f. immaculatum, commonly known as the immaculate orchid, is a hybrid orchid species originating from Brazil.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and the presence of dark green, velvety leaves. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences with small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate humidity and well-draining soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation in both indoor and greenhouse settings.
