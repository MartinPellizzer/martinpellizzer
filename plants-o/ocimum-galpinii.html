Ocimum galpinii

Ocimum galpinii, commonly known as the Galpin's basil, is a species of flowering plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with hairy stems and lance-shaped foliage. Its essential oils contain compounds like linalool and methyl chavicol, which contribute to its distinctive fragrance.

Ocimum galpinii is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and has potential uses in traditional medicine.
