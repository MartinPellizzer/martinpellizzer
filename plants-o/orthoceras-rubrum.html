Orthoceras rubrum

Orthoceras rubrum, commonly known as the red horned orchid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red petals and distinctive horn-like structures on its flower spikes. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its compact growth habit and vibrant coloration. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires a cool, humid environment to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of exotic plants.
