Oplismenus javanicus

Oplismenus javanicus, commonly known as the Java spurge, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features alternate, simple, and often lance-shaped leaves that are usually green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically in shades of yellow or green, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Oplismenus javanicus is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage.
