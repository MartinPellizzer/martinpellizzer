Ophioglossum intermedium

Ophioglossum intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate adder's tongue fern, is a unique fern species belonging to the family Ophioglossaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often fleshy leaves that resemble the shape of an adder's tongue. The plant produces sporangia in specialized structures called sporangial sheaths, which are typically located near the base of the leaf. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate regions.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable subject in botanical research and conservation efforts.
