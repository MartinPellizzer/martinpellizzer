Ophrys catalaunica f. magniflora

Ophrys catalaunica f. magniflora, commonly known as the showy bee orchid, is a striking orchid species native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its large, intricately patterned flowers that mimic the appearance of female bees, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brown, with a distinctive lip that enhances its mimicry.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its unique and visually appealing blooms.
