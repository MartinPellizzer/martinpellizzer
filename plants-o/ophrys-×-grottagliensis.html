Ophrys × grottagliensis

Ophrys × grottagliensis, commonly known as the Grottagliensis Ophrys, is a hybrid orchid species native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the genus Ophrys, which is known for its intricate mimicry of female insects to attract male pollinators. The plant produces showy, bee-mimicking flowers that are typically yellow or brown in color, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination. It thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its range.
