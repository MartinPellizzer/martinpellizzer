Orchis × serraniana

Orchis × serraniana, commonly known as the hybrid green orchid, is a natural hybrid between Orchis morio and Orchis mascula.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces a single spike of flowers with a distinctive, spurred lip.

This orchid is a rare and protected species in parts of Europe, valued for its unique botanical features and ecological significance.
