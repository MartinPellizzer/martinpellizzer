Ophelia papillosa

Ophelia papillosa, commonly known as the broadleaf waterplantain, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, flat leaves that float on the surface of freshwater habitats. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Ophelia papillosa is often found in slow-moving streams, ponds, and marshy areas.

Its presence is an indicator of good water quality due to its sensitivity to pollution.
