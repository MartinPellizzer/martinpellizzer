Ophiocolea floribunda

Ophiocolea floribunda, commonly known as the showy goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, facilitating wind dispersal. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, adapting to arid environments.

Ophiocolea floribunda thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
