Osyris peltata

Osyris peltata, commonly known as the African waterwheel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, whorled arrangement of leaves that resemble the spokes of a wheel. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense, wheel-like inflorescences. Osyris peltata is typically found growing near water sources, such as rivers and lakeshores, due to its preference for moist, well-drained soils.

It is often used in ornamental gardening for its unique foliage and ability to thrive in humid environments.
