Orchis tenuis

Orchis tenuis, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces a tall flowering stem with a single, showy purple or violet flower. Its flowers have a distinctive three-lobed lip and are pollinated by specific insect species.

The plant is part of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ecological importance in supporting pollinators and maintaining biodiversity.
