Oxalis macbridei

Oxalis macbridei, commonly known as the Macbride's wood sorrel, is a striking tropical plant native to the Caribbean.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive violet or purple coloration, often adorned with intricate patterns resembling veins. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white, pink, or red, which bloom throughout the year in suitable conditions. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Oxalis macbridei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
