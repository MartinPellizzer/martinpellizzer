Ocimum angustilanceolatum

Ocimum angustilanceolatum, commonly known as narrow-leaf basil, is a species of flowering plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant, purple flowers. The plant is typically herbaceous, with a height ranging from 30 to 60 centimeters. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for both garden cultivation and culinary use.

Its essential oils contain compounds like linalool and eugenol, contributing to its aromatic properties.
