Omphalodes linifolia

Omphalodes linifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands. The plant features slender, branching stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its showy, bell-shaped flowers are usually blue or purple, with five petals and a prominent central spur.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
