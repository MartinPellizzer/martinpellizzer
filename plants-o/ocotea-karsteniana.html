Ocotea karsteniana

Ocotea karsteniana, commonly known as the Karsten's ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, dark purple drupe that contains a single seed.

Ocotea karsteniana is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional craftsmanship and as a source of essential oils.
