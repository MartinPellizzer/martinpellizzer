Odontoglossum kegeljanii

Odontoglossum kegeljanii, commonly known as the Kegeljan's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in cool, humid environments at high altitudes. The plant features a pseudobulb with a single leaf, and its flowers are usually violet or purple with a distinctive, spurred lip. It blooms in the spring and is known for its intricate and showy floral structure.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in greenhouses or specialized gardens.
