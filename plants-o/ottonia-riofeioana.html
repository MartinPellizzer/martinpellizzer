Ottonia riofeioana

Ottonia riofeioana, commonly known as the Riofeo orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, pendulous flowers that display a vibrant mix of purple and white hues. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories where it thrives on the nutrient-rich humus of the forest floor. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the surrounding soil, and it is known for its delicate, strap-like leaves.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Ottonia riofeioana is considered a vulnerable species, making conservation efforts essential for its survival.
