Oldenlandia pruinosa var. massiei

Oldenlandia pruinosa var. massiei, commonly known as the velvetleaf or silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pale blue or pink, typically blooming in the early morning. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens, though it can become invasive in certain regions.
