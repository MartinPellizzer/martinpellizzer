Oliveria orientalis

Oliveria orientalis, commonly known as the oriental olive, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are typically leathery and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually yellow or greenish-yellow in color. Oliveria orientalis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is also valued for its essential oils, which are used in perfumery and aromatherapy.
