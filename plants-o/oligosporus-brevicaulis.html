Oligosporus brevicaulis

Oligosporus brevicaulis, commonly known as the short-headed spore plant, is a unique species within the fungal kingdom.

It is characterized by its compact, spore-producing structures that are typically found in moist, shaded environments. The plant's morphology includes a short, sturdy stem with dense clusters of spore cases at the apex. Its reproductive strategy relies heavily on wind dispersal of spores, which are released in response to environmental stimuli.

Due to its specialized habitat requirements, Oligosporus brevicaulis is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
