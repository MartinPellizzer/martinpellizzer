Oenothera pycnocarpa

Oenothera pycnocarpa, commonly known as the dense-fruited evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Oenothera pycnocarpa typically grows in dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

It is a member of the Onagraceae family, which includes other evening primroses and related species.
