Ocotea trichantha

Ocotea trichantha, commonly known as the white ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as cinnamon and laurel. The tree is characterized by its smooth, light-colored bark and lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible for some bird species.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
