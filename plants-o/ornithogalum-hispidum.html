Ornithogalum hispidum

Ornithogalum hispidum, commonly known as the hairy star of Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a short, tuberous rhizome and produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are narrow, grass-like, and covered with fine, hairy textures, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its unique floral display.
