Ocotea lanceolata var. gracilipes

Ocotea lanceolata var. gracilipes, commonly known as the slender-leafed ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon trees, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a dark blue drupe, often containing a single seed.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its aromatic properties.
