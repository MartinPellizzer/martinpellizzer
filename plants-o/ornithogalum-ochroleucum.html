Ornithogalum ochroleucum

Ornithogalum ochroleucum, commonly known as the white star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped, white to pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens but requires careful management to prevent it from becoming invasive.
