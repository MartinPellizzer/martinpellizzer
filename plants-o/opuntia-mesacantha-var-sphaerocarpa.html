Opuntia mesacantha var. sphaerocarpa

Opuntia mesacantha var. sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the globe cactus, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features rounded, segmented stems that are typically green with a waxy coating, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tunas, is edible and has a mild, tangy flavor.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and drought tolerance in landscaping.
