Olearia praetermissa

Olearia praetermissa, commonly known as the silverweed, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and subalpine regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are covered in fine, silky hairs that give it a distinctive appearance.

Olearia praetermissa is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
