Oryza sativa var. brunnea

Oryza sativa var. brunnea, commonly known as the black rice, is a variety of rice characterized by its dark purple to black grains.

This rice is native to Southeast Asia and is cultivated for its unique color and nutritional value. The grains are rich in antioxidants, particularly anthocyanins, which contribute to its deep color. It is typically grown in tropical and subtropical regions with warm, humid climates.

The plant has a short growth cycle and requires ample water during its cultivation phase.
