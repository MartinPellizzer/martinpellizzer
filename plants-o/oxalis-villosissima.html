Oxalis villosissima

Oxalis villosissima, commonly known as the woolly wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features trifoliate leaves with a velvety, hairy texture that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its distinctive appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
