Ocotea lundellii

Ocotea lundellii, commonly known as the Mexican laurel, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces dark blue, fleshy berries that are edible for some bird species.

Ocotea lundellii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
