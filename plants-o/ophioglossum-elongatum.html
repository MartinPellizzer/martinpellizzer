Ophioglossum elongatum

Ophioglossum elongatum, commonly known as the horse tail fern, is a unique plant belonging to the family Ophioglossaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often spirally twisted sporophylls that resemble the tail of a horse, giving it its common name. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. It produces spores rather than seeds, and its reproductive structures are highly specialized.

Ophioglossum elongatum is considered a primitive vascular plant with a long evolutionary history.
