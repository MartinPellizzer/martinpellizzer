Oreorchis indica

Oreorchis indica, commonly known as the Indian twayblades, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, including India and China.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, white or pale green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or grasslands. Oreorchis indica has a complex root system and produces a single spike of flowers with three petals and three sepals.

It is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
