Ononis pendula f. boissieri

Ononis pendula f. boissieri, commonly known as the hanging restharrow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that features slender, creeping stems with small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a preference for well-drained, rocky soils.

It is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as slopes and gravelly areas.
