Oncidium armillare

Oncidium armillare, commonly known as the Orchid of the Month, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features a pseudobulb with a single leaf and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange. Its inflorescence is a raceme of small, clustered blooms that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Oncidium armillare is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ease of cultivation and striking appearance.
