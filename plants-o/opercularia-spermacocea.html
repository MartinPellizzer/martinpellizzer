Opercularia spermacocea

Opercularia spermacocea, commonly known as the "Mexican sunflower" or "Mexican flame flower," is a herbaceous perennial native to Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant features bright orange to red tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil.

Opercularia spermacocea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
