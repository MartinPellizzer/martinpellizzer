Obione garrettii

Obione garrettii, commonly known as the Garrett's orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant blooms that typically appear in shades of white and pale pink. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its unique floral structure and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat destruction, Obione garrettii is considered endangered and is protected in several national parks.
