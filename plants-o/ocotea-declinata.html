Ocotea declinata

Ocotea declinata, commonly known as the Brazilian cinnamon tree, is a member of the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves and small, clustered flowers. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils, which have been used in traditional medicine.

Ocotea declinata plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting a variety of wildlife.
