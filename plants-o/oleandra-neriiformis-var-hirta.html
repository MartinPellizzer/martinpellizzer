Oleandra neriiformis var. hirta

Oleandra neriiformis var. hirta, commonly known as the hairy oleander, is a flowering shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. It is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
