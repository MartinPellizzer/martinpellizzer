Oenothera fruticosa f. phyllopus

Oenothera fruticosa f. phyllopus, commonly known as the broadleaf evening primrose, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It features woody stems and lance-shaped, often broad leaves that are slightly hairy. The plant produces yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths. Its botanical characteristics include a robust root system and a growth habit that is both shrub-like and herbaceous.

This form is distinguished by its broader foliage compared to the typical species.
