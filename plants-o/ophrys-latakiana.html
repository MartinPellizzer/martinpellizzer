Ophrys latakiana

Ophrys latakiana, commonly known as the Latakian bee orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, bee-like flower that mimics the appearance and scent of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its flowers are usually a mix of yellow, brown, and orange, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Ophrys latakiana is a relatively rare and specialized orchid, often found in specific microhabitats within its native range.
