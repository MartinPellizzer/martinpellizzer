Orchis latifolia var. linguata

Orchis latifolia var. linguata, commonly known as the broad-leaved orchid or the tongue orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, violet or purple flowers that are clustered in a spike, often with a lip-like structure. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.

This species is noted for its ecological importance as a food source for various pollinators and as an indicator of habitat quality.
