Oplismenus secundus

Oplismenus secundus, commonly known as the common buttonweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features prostrate or ascending stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant has small, opposite leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are tiny, clustered in spikes, and are usually white or pale green, blooming from late spring to early fall.

Oplismenus secundus is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils, making it both a common weed and a hardy ornamental plant.
