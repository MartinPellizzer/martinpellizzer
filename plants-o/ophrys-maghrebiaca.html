Ophrys maghrebiaca

Ophrys maghrebiaca, commonly known as the Maghreb bee orchid, is a species of orchid native to North Africa.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee, a trait evolved to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pale green or brown, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Ophrys maghrebiaca is a rare and specialized species, often found in specific microhabitats within its native range.
