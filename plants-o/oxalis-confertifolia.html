Oxalis confertifolia

Oxalis confertifolia, commonly known as the dense-leaf wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are often heart-shaped and have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Oxalis confertifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
