Ophrys colossaea

Ophrys colossaea, commonly known as the giant bee orchid, is a striking member of the orchid family native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

This plant is characterized by its large, showy flowers that resemble bees, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The flowers have intricate patterns and a strong scent, mimicking the appearance and odor of female bees to attract male bees for pollination. It thrives in open, sunny habitats with well-drained soils, often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

The plant's unique morphology and specialized pollination mechanism make it a fascinating subject in botanical studies.
