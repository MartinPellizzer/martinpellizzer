Oxalis luteota

Oxalis luteota, commonly known as the yellow wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive yellowish-green color and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of yellow and white. The plant belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and blooms.

Oxalis luteota is also known for its ability to tolerate partial shade and is relatively easy to care for in a garden setting.
