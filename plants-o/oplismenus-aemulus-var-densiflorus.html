Oplismenus aemulus var. densiflorus

Oplismenus aemulus var. densiflorus, commonly known as the denseflower ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.

Its flowering period typically occurs in late summer to early autumn, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat.
