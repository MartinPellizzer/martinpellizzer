Osmia trifoliata

Osmia trifoliata, commonly known as the three-leaved mason bee, is a solitary bee species native to Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive three-leaf-like patterns on its body, which provide camouflage in its natural habitat. The plant is typically found in regions with temperate climates, often near flowering plants that provide nectar and pollen for the bees. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with five petals and a tubular shape that facilitates pollination by the bees.

Osmia trifoliata plays an important role in pollinating various crops and wild plants, contributing to biodiversity and agricultural productivity.
