Ophrys × melineae

Ophrys × melineae, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera (the bee orchid) and Ophrys fucata (the honey orchid).

It is characterized by its intricate, bee-like flowers that mimic the appearance and scent of female bees, a trait evolved to attract male bees for pollination. The plant is native to southern Europe and thrives in calcareous soils, often found in open grasslands and woodland edges. Its flowers are typically yellowish-brown with intricate patterns, and it blooms between April and June.

Ophrys × melineae is a short-lived perennial, known for its unique reproductive strategy and ecological significance in its native habitat.
