Oryctanthus ovalifolius

Oryctanthus ovalifolius, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, papery petals that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Oryctanthus ovalifolius is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
