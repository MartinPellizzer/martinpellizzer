Oricia renieri

Oricia renieri, commonly known as the Renieri's orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its intricate, showy blooms that often feature vibrant colors such as pink and white. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories where it relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, adapted to capture limited sunlight, while its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture from the soil.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, Oricia renieri is considered endangered, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
