Oxytropis chiliophylla

Oxytropis chiliophylla, commonly known as the Chilean milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are often hairy and compound, with small leaflets that help reduce water loss.

Oxytropis chiliophylla is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to nutrient cycling in its ecosystem.
