Oxalis neomexicana

Oxalis neomexicana, commonly known as the New Mexico wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southwestern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in desert scrub and woodland habitats.

Oxalis neomexicana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and flowers.
