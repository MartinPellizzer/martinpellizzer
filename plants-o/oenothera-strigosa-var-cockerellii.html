Oenothera strigosa var. cockerellii

Oenothera strigosa var. cockerellii, commonly known as the Cockerell's evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, elliptical seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
