Orchis angustifolia var. fraasii

Orchis angustifolia var. fraasii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a species of orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and a single, upright flower spike that bears a single, pale yellow to greenish flower. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. It is a perennial herb that produces a single, lanceolate leaf and has a well-developed root system.

The species is noted for its subtle appearance and is often found in association with other orchid species in its habitat.
