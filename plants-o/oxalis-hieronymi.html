Oxalis hieronymi

Oxalis hieronymi, commonly known as the velvetleaf wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is characterized by its succulent stems and leaves, which store water, allowing it to thrive in dry conditions. It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family, known for its calcium oxalate crystals in the leaves.

Oxalis hieronymi is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique floral display.
