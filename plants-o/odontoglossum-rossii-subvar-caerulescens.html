Odontoglossum rossii subvar. caerulescens

Odontoglossum rossii subvar. caerulescens, commonly known as the blue orchid, is a species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to blue flowers, which are typically borne on tall, upright stems. The plant is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulbous structure, featuring fleshy, swollen stems that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are elongated and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

This orchid thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
