Orchidantha ranchanensis

Orchidantha ranchanensis, commonly known as the Ranchanensis orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, fragrant blooms with vividly colored petals. The plant typically grows in rocky soil and requires a cool, humid environment to thrive. Its unique morphology includes slender stems and narrow leaves adapted to its alpine habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern.
