Ononis columnae var. orientalis

Ononis columnae var. orientalis, commonly known as the oriental sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It typically features compact, mat-forming growth with small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale pink flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-adapted to harsh conditions, showcasing resilience and ecological significance in its native habitat.
