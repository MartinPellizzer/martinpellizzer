Oxalis californica var. subglabra

Oxalis californica var. subglabra, commonly known as the California wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of five-petaled yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and wildflower meadows. Its flowers bloom in spring, and the plant is known for its ability to tolerate drought conditions.

Botanically, it belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive leaf structure and edible, sour-tasting leaves.
