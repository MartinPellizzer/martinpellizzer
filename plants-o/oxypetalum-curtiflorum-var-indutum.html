Oxypetalum curtiflorum var. indutum

Oxypetalum curtiflorum var. indutum, commonly known as the velvetleaf oxypetalum, is a variant of the oxypetalum genus native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, short hairs. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and arranged in clusters, adding ornamental value to the plant.

It is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is valued for its aesthetic appeal in horticultural settings.
