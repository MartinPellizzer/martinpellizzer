Oxytropis revoluta subsp. kudoana

Oxytropis revoluta subsp.

kudoana, commonly known as the Kudo's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and compound leaves.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink to purple in color. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine environments.
