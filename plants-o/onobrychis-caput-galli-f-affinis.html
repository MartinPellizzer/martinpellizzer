Onobrychis caput-galli f. affinis

Onobrychis caput-galli f. affinis, commonly known as the lesser crown vetch, is a perennial legume native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has a creeping growth habit with extensive root systems, making it suitable for erosion control. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed or dry habitats.

Its nitrogen-fixing ability makes it valuable in ecological restoration projects.
