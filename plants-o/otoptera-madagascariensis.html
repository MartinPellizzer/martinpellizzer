Otoptera madagascariensis

Otoptera madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a unique orchid species native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that bloom in a spiral pattern. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in the humid forests of its native habitat. Its flowers are pollinated by specific insects, contributing to its ecological significance in the region.

The species is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
