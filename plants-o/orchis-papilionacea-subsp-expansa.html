Orchis papilionacea subsp. expansa

Orchis papilionacea subsp.

expansa, commonly known as the broad-leafed orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its showy, butterfly-like flowers that range in color from yellow to brown.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or grassland habitats. It produces a single, erect stem with a few leaves and a cluster of flowers at the top.

The species is notable for its distinctive floral structure and is often found in shaded, humid environments.
