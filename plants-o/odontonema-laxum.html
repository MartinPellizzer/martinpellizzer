Odontonema laxum

Odontonema laxum, commonly known as the Brazilian flame or red flame plant, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its striking red or orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has lance-shaped, slightly hairy leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant.

Odontonema laxum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.
