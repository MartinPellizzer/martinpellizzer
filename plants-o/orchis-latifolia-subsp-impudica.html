Orchis latifolia subsp. impudica

Orchis latifolia subsp.

impudica, commonly known as the broad-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, violet to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and woodlands. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and it produces a single spike of flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often found in habitats with adequate sunlight and well-drained soil.
