Ophrys heldreichii var. pharia

Ophrys heldreichii var. pharia, commonly known as the Pharia Orchid, is a rare and endemic orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Greece.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a specialized lip that mimics the appearance of a female insect to attract male pollinators. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. Its flowers are usually a mix of brown and green hues, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts to protect its natural environment.
