Oxydectes berlandieri

Oxydectes berlandieri, commonly known as the Mexican spider flower, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its striking, spiky flower heads that resemble spiders, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are usually bright red or orange, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

Oxydectes berlandieri is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
