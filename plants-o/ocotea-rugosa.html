Ocotea rugosa

Ocotea rugosa, commonly known as the rough-leafed ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that are often covered in fine, raised dots, giving it a textured appearance. Its fragrant flowers are typically white or pale yellow and are followed by small, dark blue berries.

Ocotea rugosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
