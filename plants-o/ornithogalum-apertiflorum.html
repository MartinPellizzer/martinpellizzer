Ornithogalum apertiflorum

Ornithogalum apertiflorum, commonly known as the open-flowered star-of-Bethlem, is a bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, linear leaves and produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers that open in the morning. The plant belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its underground bulb, which stores nutrients for growth. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
