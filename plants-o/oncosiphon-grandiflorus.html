Oncosiphon grandiflorus

Oncosiphon grandiflorus, commonly known as the large-flowered senna, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, flat pods that contain lens-shaped seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in open woodlands or along riverbanks.
