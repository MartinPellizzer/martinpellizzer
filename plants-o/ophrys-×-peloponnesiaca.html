Ophrys × peloponnesiaca

Ophrys × peloponnesiaca, commonly known as the Peloponnesian bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the genus Ophrys, which is known for its mimicry of female insects to attract male pollinators. The plant produces strikingly intricate flowers that closely resemble female bees, a trait evolved to enhance pollination efficiency. It typically grows in calcareous soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats.

The hybrid nature of Ophrys × peloponnesiaca results from crossbreeding between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera, contributing to its unique floral morphology.
