Oncidium zappii

Oncidium zappii, commonly known as the Zappi's orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features strap-like leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive red lip. Oncidium zappii is known for its relatively easy care requirements, making it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.

Its blooming period usually occurs during the spring and summer months.
