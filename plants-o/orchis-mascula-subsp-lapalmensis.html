Orchis mascula subsp. lapalmensis

Orchis mascula subsp.

lapalmensis, commonly known as the Lapalma orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Canary Islands. This subspecies is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces a single spike of flowers with a distinctive lip.

It is a perennial herb that plays a role in the local pollination system, attracting various insects.
