Oreogrammitis medialis

Oreogrammitis medialis, commonly known as the narrow-leafed water fern, is a delicate aquatic fern native to the wetlands of eastern North America.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes, where it thrives in moist, shaded conditions. The plant has finely divided, linear leaves that form a dense, floating mat on the water surface. Its rhizomes are often submerged, anchoring the plant and facilitating nutrient absorption from the surrounding water.

Oreogrammitis medialis plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms and contributing to water filtration.
