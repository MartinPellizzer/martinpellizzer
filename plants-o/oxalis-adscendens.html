Oxalis adscendens

Oxalis adscendens, commonly known as the climbing wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive veining and produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, often using tendrils to ascend supports. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Oxalis adscendens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
