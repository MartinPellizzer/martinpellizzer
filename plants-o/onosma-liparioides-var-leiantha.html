Onosma liparioides var. leiantha

Onosma liparioides var. leiantha, commonly known as the Sicilian thymes, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, fragrant blue or purple flowers. The plant has narrow, grey-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its aromatic qualities. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
