Ocotea magnifica

Ocotea magnifica, commonly known as the magnolia of the Andes, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves and produces fragrant, star-shaped blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its wood is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional crafts and as a source of essential oils.

The tree thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.
