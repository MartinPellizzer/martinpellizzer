Orchinea attica

Orchinea attica, commonly known as the Greek orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Greece.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and intricate, fragrant blossoms. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in cool, shaded environments. Its flowers, which are usually pink or violet in color, bloom in late spring or early summer.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, Orchinea attica is considered endangered and is protected in several regions.
