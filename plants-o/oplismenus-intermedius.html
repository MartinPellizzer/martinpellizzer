Oplismenus intermedius

Oplismenus intermedius, commonly known as the common wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves composed of three heart-shaped leaflets, often with a distinctive reddish or purple tinge on the underside. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Oplismenus intermedius is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
