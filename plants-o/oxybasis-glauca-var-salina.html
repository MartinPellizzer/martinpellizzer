Oxybasis glauca var. salina

Oxybasis glauca var. salina, commonly known as saltgrass, is a perennial grass native to the coastal regions of the United States.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-green foliage and is adapted to saline and alkaline soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of salt, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. It typically grows in dense mats or tufts, forming a low-growing vegetation that stabilizes dunes and prevents erosion.

Saltgrass plays an important role in coastal ecosystems by providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
