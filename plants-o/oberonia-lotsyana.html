Oberonia lotsyana

Oberonia lotsyana, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as under forest canopies. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern, while its flowers are clustered in inflorescences.

Due to its habitat specificity and rarity, Oberonia lotsyana is considered an endangered species.
