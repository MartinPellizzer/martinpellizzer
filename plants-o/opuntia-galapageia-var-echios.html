Opuntia galapageia var. echios

Opuntia galapageia var. echios, commonly known as the Galápagos prickly pear, is a cactus native to the Galápagos Islands.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in spines and woolly hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, followed by red, fleshy fruits known as tunas. It thrives in arid and coastal environments, adapting well to the unique climate of the Galápagos.

This variety is an important part of the local ecosystem, providing food for both wildlife and humans.
