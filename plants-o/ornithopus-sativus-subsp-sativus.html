Ornithopus sativus subsp. sativus

Ornithopus sativus subsp.

sativus, commonly known as yellow rocket, is a biennial or annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically in clusters at the top of its upright stems.

The plant has finely divided, feather-like leaves that are aromatic when crushed. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Yellow rocket is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard family plants.
