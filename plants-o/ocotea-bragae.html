Ocotea bragae

Ocotea bragae, commonly known as the Brazilian ocotea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of eastern Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are typically oval-shaped and have a glossy surface. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually white or pale yellow and are followed by small, dark berries.

Ocotea bragae is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
