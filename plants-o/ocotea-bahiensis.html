Ocotea bahiensis

Ocotea bahiensis, commonly known as Brazilian lauraceae, is a member of the Lauraceae family, native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptic to ovate in shape. It produces small, white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing a single seed.

The plant is valued for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
