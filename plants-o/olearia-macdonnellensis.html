Olearia macdonnellensis

Olearia macdonnellensis, commonly known as the Macdonnell's daisybush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grey-green, providing a striking contrast to the white and yellow floral display.

Olearia macdonnellensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
