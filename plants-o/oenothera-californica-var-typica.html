Oenothera californica var. typica

Oenothera californica var. typica, commonly known as the California evening primrose, is a species native to western North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening. The plant has slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

The flowers attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths and bats, contributing to its ecological significance.
