Oenothera speciosa var. berlandieri

Oenothera speciosa var. berlandieri, commonly known as the Desert Evening Primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening and close by morning. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as deserts and rocky soils, and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy and branching.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dry seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
