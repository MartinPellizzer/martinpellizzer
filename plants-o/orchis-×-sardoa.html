Orchis × sardoa

Orchis × sardoa, commonly known as the Sardonic orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Orchis morio and Orchis mascula.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or grassland habitats. Its flowers have a distinct, intricate structure with three sepals and three petals, with the lip being notably larger and more colorful.

Orchis × sardoa is a perennial herb that plays a role in pollination by attracting various insects, including bees and moths.
