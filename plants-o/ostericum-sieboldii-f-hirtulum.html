Ostericum sieboldii f. hirtulum

Ostericum sieboldii f. hirtulum, commonly known as the hairy Japanese spurge, is a succulent plant native to Japan.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.

This variety is particularly valued for its compact growth habit and resilience in various environmental conditions.
