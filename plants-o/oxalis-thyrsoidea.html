Oxalis thyrsoidea

Oxalis thyrsoidea, commonly known as the velvetleaf wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas. Its stems are usually hairy, and it produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds.

Oxalis thyrsoidea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
