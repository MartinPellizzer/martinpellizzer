Ocotea crassifolia

Ocotea crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant typically grows in humid, lowland forests and can reach heights of up to 30 meters. Its leaves are broad, elliptical, and have a glossy green upper surface with a lighter underside.

The tree produces small, fragrant flowers and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
