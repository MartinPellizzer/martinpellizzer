Ochroma

Ochroma, commonly known as the balsa tree, is a member of the Malvaceae family and is native to South America.

It is characterized by its lightweight, porous wood, which is among the lightest known woods in the world. The plant produces showy, white or pink flowers that are typically clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

Ochroma is also known for its rapid growth rate, making it valuable for ecological restoration and commercial use.
