Obione podocarpa

Obione podocarpa, commonly known as the New Zealand rimu, is a large evergreen tree native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its conical shape and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds surrounded by a fleshy, red aril. It is known for its strong, durable wood and plays an important role in New Zealand's native forests.

Obione podocarpa is also valued for its ecological significance, providing habitat and food for various native species.
