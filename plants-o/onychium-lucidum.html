Onychium lucidum

Onychium lucidum, commonly known as the shining onychium, is a member of the Primulaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky slopes. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a smooth, waxy surface and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. Its blooming period occurs during the spring and early summer, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
