Ocotea solomonii

Ocotea solomonii, commonly known as Solomon's ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule containing several seeds.

Ocotea solomonii is often found in humid, lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
