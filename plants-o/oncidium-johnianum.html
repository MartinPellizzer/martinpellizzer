Oncidium johnianum

Oncidium johnianum, commonly known as the yellow butterfly orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow and green flowers that resemble butterflies, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its inflorescence consists of multiple flowers arranged in a raceme, with each bloom having three petals and three sepals.

Oncidium johnianum requires bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.
