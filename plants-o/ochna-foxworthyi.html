Ochna foxworthyi

Ochna foxworthyi, commonly known as the African yellowwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and dense, leathery leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that are clustered in umbels, typically blooming in summer. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, and it is adapted to grow in well-drained, sandy soils.

Ochna foxworthyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
