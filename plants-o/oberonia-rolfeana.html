Oberonia rolfeana

Oberonia rolfeana, commonly known as the Rolfe's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and produces strikingly colorful, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically has linear to lanceolate leaves that are arranged in two rows along its stem. Its inflorescence consists of several flowers with showy, often violet or pink petals, and it is known for its unique floral structure.

Oberonia rolfeana thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining substrates for optimal growth.
