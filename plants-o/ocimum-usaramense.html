Ocimum usaramense

Ocimum usaramense, commonly known as Brazilian basil, is a species of flowering plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small purple or white flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Ocimum usaramense is valued for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy and as a natural insect repellent.
