Orchis × ruthei

Orchis × ruthei, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a fascinating botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between Orchis morio and Orchis mascula.

This plant typically exhibits a mix of characteristics from both parent species, often displaying a range of flower colors from purple to white. It is found in various habitats across Europe, particularly in woodland and grassland areas. The orchid's flowers are usually borne in dense spikes, and they have a distinctive lip that is often adorned with intricate patterns.

Due to its hybrid nature, Orchis × ruthei is a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.
