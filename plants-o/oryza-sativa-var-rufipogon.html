Oryza sativa var. rufipogon

Oryza sativa var. rufipogon, commonly known as wild rice, is a wild species of rice native to Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, brownish-red grains. This plant typically grows in wetland environments and has a fibrous root system adapted to waterlogged soils. Its genetic diversity is valuable for breeding programs aimed at improving cultivated rice varieties.

It plays an important role in both ecological systems and traditional agriculture.
