Ochna humblotiana

Ochna humblotiana, commonly known as the African yellowwood, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and dense, glossy green leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Ochna humblotiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
