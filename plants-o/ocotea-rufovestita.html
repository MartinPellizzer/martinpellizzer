Ocotea rufovestita

Ocotea rufovestita, commonly known as the red-barked ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its smooth, reddish-barked trunk and lance-shaped leaves with a glossy upper surface. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue, fleshy berries.

Ocotea rufovestita is often found in moist, shaded habitats within tropical forests.
