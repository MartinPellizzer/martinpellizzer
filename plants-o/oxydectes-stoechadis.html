Oxydectes stoechadis

Oxydectes stoechadis, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous regions. The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its scientific name reflects its botanical classification within the Asteraceae family.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
