Orchidantha ismailii

Orchidantha ismailii, commonly known as the Ismail's orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to specific regions of the Middle East.

It features intricate, showy flowers with a unique coloration pattern that includes a mix of white, purple, and green hues. The plant has a compact growth habit with fleshy leaves adapted to arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to bright indirect light.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern.
