Olea robusta

Olea robusta, commonly known as the African olive, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, with a dense, rounded crown. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellowish-white in color.

Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that contains a single stone, and it is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil.
