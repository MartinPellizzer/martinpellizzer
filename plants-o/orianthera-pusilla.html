Orianthera pusilla

Orianthera pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf orchid, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically features narrow, strap-like leaves and produces small, clustered blooms in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Orianthera pusilla is often cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and unique floral structure.
