Oxytropis vavilovii

Oxytropis vavilovii, commonly known as Siberian locoweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cold climates of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic bacteria. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces yellow or pale purple flowers.

Oxytropis vavilovii is known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can affect livestock and humans if ingested.
