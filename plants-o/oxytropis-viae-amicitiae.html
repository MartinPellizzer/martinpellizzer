Oxytropis viae-amicitiae

Oxytropis viae-amicitiae, commonly known as the friendship vetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to grow in rocky and arid environments, often forming dense colonies in its native habitat.

Oxytropis viae-amicitiae is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil in its ecosystem.
