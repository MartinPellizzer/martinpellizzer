Opuntia phaeacantha var. brunnea

Opuntia phaeacantha var. brunnea, commonly known as the brown spine cactus, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that are often covered in spines and glochids. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it stores water in its stem for survival in arid conditions.

This variety is popular in horticulture for its unique appearance and adaptability to dry environments.
