Opuntia macbridei var. orbicularis

Opuntia macbridei var. orbicularis, commonly known as the Mexican prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its flattened, fleshy stems that are typically rounded or orbicular in shape. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tunaf, is edible and often used in culinary applications.

The species is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water to thrive.
