Orchis maculata f. oblongifolia

Orchis maculata f. oblongifolia, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a variant of the common spotted orchid, characterized by its distinctive dark purple spots on the petals.

This plant is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while the flowers are clustered in a spike with a unique combination of white and purple hues. The orchid belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
