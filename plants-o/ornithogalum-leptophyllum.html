Ornithogalum leptophyllum

Ornithogalum leptophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a short, rhizomatous stem and produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers during the summer months. The plant has lance-shaped, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Ornithogalum leptophyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
