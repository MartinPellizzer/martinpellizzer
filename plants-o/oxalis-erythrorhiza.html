Oxalis erythrorhiza

Oxalis erythrorhiza, commonly known as the red-root wood sorrel, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with reddish veins and produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers. The plant has a distinctive red root system, which gives it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Oxalis erythrorhiza is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
