Oncidium schrautianum

Oncidium schrautianum, commonly known as the Schraut's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features a pseudobulb with a single, strap-like leaf and produces a branched inflorescence of fragrant, showy flowers. The flowers are typically yellow with reddish-brown markings and bloom during the summer months. It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for greenhouse cultivation.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of care in suitable growing conditions.
