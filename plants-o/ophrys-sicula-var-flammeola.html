Ophrys sicula var. flammeola

Ophrys sicula var. flammeola, commonly known as the Flame Orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive floral structure, featuring a lip that mimics the appearance of a female bee, a feature that plays a key role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in sunny, well-drained habitats. Its flowers are usually a mix of yellow, orange, and red, creating a vivid, flame-like appearance.

This orchid is notable for its specialized relationship with certain bee species, which it attracts through its intricate mimicry.
