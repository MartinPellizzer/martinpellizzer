Oenothera rubida

Oenothera rubida, commonly known as the red evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Oenothera rubida is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
