Onobrychis armena

Onobrychis armena, commonly known as Armenian burclover, is a perennial leguminous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes. The plant produces cylindrical, hairy pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Onobrychis armena is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability and has been used in traditional medicine.
