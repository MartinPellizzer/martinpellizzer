Ophrys × castroviejoi

Ophrys × castroviejoi, commonly known as the Castroviejo bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its unique floral morphology, featuring a complex labellum that mimics the appearance of a female bee, which plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brownish in color, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
