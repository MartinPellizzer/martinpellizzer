Ododeca

Ododeca, commonly known as the five-lobed orchid, is a unique flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, five-lobed petals that resemble the shape of a star or a flower with a central spire. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are fibrous and often form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi.

Ododeca is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
