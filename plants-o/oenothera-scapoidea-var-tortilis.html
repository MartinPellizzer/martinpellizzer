Oenothera scapoidea var. tortilis

Oenothera scapoidea var. tortilis, commonly known as the twisted willow flower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the evening primrose family (Onagraceae) and is characterized by its slender, often twisted stems and showy yellow flowers that open in the evening. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along streambanks and woodland edges. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a slight pubescence.

The flowers are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
