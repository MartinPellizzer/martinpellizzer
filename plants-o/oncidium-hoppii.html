Oncidium hoppii

Oncidium hoppii, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow petals and green sepals, which give it a striking appearance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Oncidium hoppii produces fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of yellow and green.

It requires bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.
