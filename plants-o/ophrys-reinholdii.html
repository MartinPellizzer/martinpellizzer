Ophrys reinholdii

Ophrys reinholdii, commonly known as the Reinhold's bee orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It is characterized by its intricate, bee-like flower structure, which features a vividly colored lip that mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing a unique blend of colors and patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Due to its limited distribution and specialized habitat requirements, Ophrys reinholdii is considered a vulnerable species.
