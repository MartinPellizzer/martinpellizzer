Ochotia olaensis

Ochotia olaensis, commonly known as the Olaensian orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are elongated and arranged in a rosette pattern, facilitating efficient photosynthesis.

Ochotia olaensis is often found in humid, shaded environments such as rainforests and is valued for its ornamental beauty.
