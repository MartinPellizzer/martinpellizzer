Ouratea gillyana

Ouratea gillyana, commonly known as the pink hibiscus or pink wax tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its showy, pink or red bract-like flowers that resemble those of hibiscus, which bloom in clusters at the ends of its branches. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that are often clustered at the base of the flower clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ouratea gillyana is also valued for its ability to grow in a variety of soil types, making it a versatile choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
