Oxybaphus microchlamydeus

Oxybaphus microchlamydeus, commonly known as the small-chalaza oxeye, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a daisy-like pattern. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open grasslands.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
