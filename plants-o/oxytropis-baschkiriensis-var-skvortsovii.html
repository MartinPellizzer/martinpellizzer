Oxytropis baschkiriensis var. skvortsovii

Oxytropis baschkiriensis var. skvortsovii, commonly known as the Bashkir locoweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as steppes and rocky soils. It has adapted to harsh conditions with deep root systems and a short growing season.

The species is also known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock.
