Oxalis lasiopetala

Oxalis lasiopetala, commonly known as the hairy leaf wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Oxalis lasiopetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
