Origanum × pulchellum

Origanum × pulchellum, commonly known as sweet marjoram, is a hybrid plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, aromatic foliage and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces essential oils rich in compounds like carvacrol and thymol, contributing to its strong, sweet scent. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and culinary uses, it is a popular choice for both gardeners and herbalists.
