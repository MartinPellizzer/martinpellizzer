Octella vaccinoides

Octella vaccinoides, commonly known as the false holly or sweet pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are typically clustered in terminal panicles. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns red when mature, often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some medicinal uses in indigenous cultures.
