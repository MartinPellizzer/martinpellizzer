Oxalis corniculata f. erecta

Oxalis corniculata f. erecta, commonly known as the upright wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky areas. It has a creeping stem that can form dense mats, and its leaves are often hairy.

Oxalis corniculata f. erecta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
