Orthosiphon glutinosus

Orthosiphon glutinosus, commonly known as the sticky siphon, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that produce a sticky substance, which is a key botanical feature. The plant typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, thriving in humid and tropical climates. Its flowers are small and clustered, often in whorls, and are usually white or pale pink in color.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
