Orchis × lloydiana

Orchis × lloydiana, commonly known as the Lloyd's orchid, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Orchis morio and Orchis ustulata.

It is a terrestrial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe, particularly in the British Isles. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers with a distinctive spurred lip, typical of many orchid species. It thrives in woodland habitats and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a range of morphological variations, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
