Ocimum hanningtonii

Ocimum hanningtonii, commonly known as the Hannington basil, is a species of flowering plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Ocimum hanningtonii is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb due to its distinctive scent and flavor.
