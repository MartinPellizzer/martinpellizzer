Ochthocosmus congolensis

Ochthocosmus congolensis, commonly known as the Congo starling tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical rainforests of Central and West Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy green foliage and produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree's bark is smooth and grayish-brown, often marked with distinct lenticels. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns from green to yellow or orange when mature.

Ochthocosmus congolensis plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
