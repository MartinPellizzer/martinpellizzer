Ophrys philippi var. breviappendiculata

Ophrys philippi var. breviappendiculata, commonly known as the short-appendiculate bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to specific regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its unique floral morphology, featuring a labellum that mimics the appearance of a female bee, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and heathlands. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brownish in color, with intricate patterns that aid in attracting male bees for pollination.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, this variant is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
