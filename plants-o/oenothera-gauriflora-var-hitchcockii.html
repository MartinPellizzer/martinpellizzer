Oenothera gauriflora var. hitchcockii

Oenothera gauriflora var. hitchcockii, commonly known as Hitchcock's evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well adapted to arid environments.

The species is valued for its ecological role and is often cultivated in xeriscapic gardens.
