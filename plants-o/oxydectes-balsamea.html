Oxydectes balsamea

Oxydectes balsamea, commonly known as the balsamroot, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its large, yellow composite flower head and silvery, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes. It produces a milky latex sap that is used in traditional medicine.

The plant plays an important role in supporting pollinators and is often found in native plant gardens.
