Ossaea cucullata

Ossaea cucullata, commonly known as the hooded grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and distinctive hood-like bracts. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually green or pale blue, and it produces small, three-angled seeds.

Ossaea cucullata is often found in open grasslands and is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
