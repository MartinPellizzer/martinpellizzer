Oenothera muricata var. issleri

Oenothera muricata var. issleri, commonly known as the Sierra Nightshade, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has hairy, glandular stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, sticky hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species for studying plant adaptations to harsh, dry climates.
