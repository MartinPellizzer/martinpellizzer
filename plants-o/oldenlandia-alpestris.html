Oldenlandia alpestris

Oldenlandia alpestris, commonly known as the alpine mistflower, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing as if shrouded in mist. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, stream banks, and rocky areas. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

Oldenlandia alpestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
