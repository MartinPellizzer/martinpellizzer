Omphalodes cordata

Omphalodes cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and bell-like blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is a perennial herb that produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
