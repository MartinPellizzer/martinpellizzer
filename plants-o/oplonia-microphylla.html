Oplonia microphylla

Oplonia microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved oplonia, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white hair. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters, followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Oplonia microphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
