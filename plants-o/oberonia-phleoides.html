Oberonia phleoides

Oberonia phleoides, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the wet tropical forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and produces striking yellow flowers with a distinctive, elongated spur. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and is often found in the understorey of dense rainforests. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
