Opuntia salmiana var. albiflora

Opuntia salmiana var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems that are often covered in spines and are used for photosynthesis. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming in spring. Its fruit, known as a tuna, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to arid environments.
