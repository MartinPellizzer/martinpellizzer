Oplismenus hirtellus

Oplismenus hirtellus, commonly known as the silverleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and tall, upright growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a rough, hairy texture.

Oplismenus hirtellus is often found in moist, woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
