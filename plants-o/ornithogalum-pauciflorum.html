Ornithogalum pauciflorum

Ornithogalum pauciflorum, commonly known as the few-flowered star-of-Bethleem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces a spike of small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
