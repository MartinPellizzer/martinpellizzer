Opilia pentitdis

Opilia pentitdis, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that display a complex arrangement of petals, sepals, and bracts, often in shades of purple and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins, and its fruit is a fleshy, red berry that contains numerous seeds. Opilia pentitdis thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in rainforests or along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
