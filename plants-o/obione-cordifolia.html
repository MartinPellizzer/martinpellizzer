Obione cordifolia

Obione cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf obione, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which give it its common name, and its clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments, such as forest understories. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and showy, often pink or white blooms.

Obione cordifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
