Odontoglossum × dellense

Odontoglossum × dellense, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Odontoglossum regina and Odontoglossum lindenii.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes, where it thrives in cool, humid environments. The plant features long, strap-like leaves and produces strikingly large, showy flowers with intricate patterns. It is prized by orchid growers for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation under proper conditions.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a model for understanding hybridization in orchids.
