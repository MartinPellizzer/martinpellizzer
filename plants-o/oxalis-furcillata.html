Oxalis furcillata

Oxalis furcillata, commonly known as the forked wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive forked appearance, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Oxalis furcillata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
