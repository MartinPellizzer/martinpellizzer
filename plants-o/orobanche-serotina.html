Orobanche serotina

Orobanche serotina, commonly known as the winter barrenweed, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is found in various parts of Europe and Asia. The plant produces small, pale yellow to brown flowers and has a taproot system that attaches to the roots of its host. It is often found growing on the roots of legumes and other herbaceous plants.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can negatively impact the growth of its host plants.
