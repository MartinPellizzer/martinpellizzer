Olea lanceolata

Olea lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf olive, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree, typically reaching heights of up to 5 meters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, often with a slight waxy coating. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually yellow or white and appear in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Olea lanceolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
