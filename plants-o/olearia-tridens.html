Olearia tridens

Olearia tridens, commonly known as the New Zealand rock daisy, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, often covered in a fine layer of hairs.

Olearia tridens plays an important role in New Zealand's native ecosystems and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
