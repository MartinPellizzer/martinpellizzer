Opuntia flavicans

Opuntia flavicans, commonly known as the yellow prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems, known as cladodes, which are typically yellow to green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible, red fruits known as tunas. Opuntia flavicans is a drought-tolerant species with a shallow root system adapted to arid environments.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
