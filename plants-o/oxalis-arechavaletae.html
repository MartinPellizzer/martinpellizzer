Oxalis arechavaletae

Oxalis arechavaletae, commonly known as the Arechavaleta wood sorrel, is a species native to the humid forests of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests. Its foliage and flowers are often used in traditional medicine by local communities.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
