Oxalis reinwardtii

Oxalis reinwardtii, commonly known as the mountain wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers well-drained conditions. It is often found in mountainous regions and is adapted to temperate climates.

Oxalis reinwardtii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
