Oxytropis fischeri

Oxytropis fischeri, commonly known as Fischer's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Oxytropis fischeri is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats and is of interest to botanists due to its ecological and potential medicinal significance.
