Ocotea laxiflora

Ocotea laxiflora, commonly known as the slender-flowered ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered white flowers. It typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests.

Ocotea laxiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage.
