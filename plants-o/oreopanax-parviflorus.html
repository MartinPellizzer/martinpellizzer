Oreopanax parviflorus

Oreopanax parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered oreopanax, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often arranged in a whorled pattern along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. Its fruit is a round, fleshy berry that contains several seeds.

Oreopanax parviflorus is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories and mountainous regions.
