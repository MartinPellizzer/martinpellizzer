Octomeria densiflora var. triarticulata

Octomeria densiflora var. triarticulata, commonly known as the three-jointed octomeria, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is distinguished by its dense flowering pattern and the unique triarticulate structure of its stems. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its overall appearance.

This variant is noted for its ecological significance in supporting local pollinators and its potential medicinal uses.
