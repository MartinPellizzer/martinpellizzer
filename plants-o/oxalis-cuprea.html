Oxalis cuprea

Oxalis cuprea, commonly known as crimson wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of bright red flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or gardens. It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is known for its attractive appearance and ease of cultivation.

Oxalis cuprea is often used in ornamental gardening due to its vibrant color and foliage.
