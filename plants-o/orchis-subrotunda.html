Orchis subrotunda

Orchis subrotunda, commonly known as the broad-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and meadows, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, and it has a rhizomatous root system.

Orchis subrotunda is part of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
