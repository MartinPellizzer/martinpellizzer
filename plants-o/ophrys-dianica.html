Ophrys dianica

Ophrys dianica, commonly known as the Greek Orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its striking, bee-like flowers that feature a dark brown to black coloration with intricate patterns mimicking the appearance of a female bee. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and is known for its specialized pollination mechanism involving male bees. Its flowers have a distinct lip that serves as a landing platform for pollinators.

Ophrys dianica is a rare and protected species, often found in specific habitats across Greece and adjacent regions.
