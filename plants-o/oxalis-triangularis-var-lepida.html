Oxalis triangularis var. lepida

Oxalis triangularis var. lepida, commonly known as the purple shamrock, is a striking ornamental plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive purple hue and white veins, creating a visually appealing contrast. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Oxalis triangularis var. lepida is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a houseplant.
