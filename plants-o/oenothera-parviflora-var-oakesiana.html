Oenothera parviflora var. oakesiana

Oenothera parviflora var. oakesiana, commonly known as Oakes' evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the central and southern United States.

It is characterized by its showy yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairie and open woodland habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while its stems are erect and branching.

The species is known for its ecological importance in supporting pollinator populations and its potential medicinal uses.
