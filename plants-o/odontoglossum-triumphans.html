Odontoglossum triumphans

Odontoglossum triumphans, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It produces striking, showy flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas, preferring cool and humid conditions. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Odontoglossum triumphans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
