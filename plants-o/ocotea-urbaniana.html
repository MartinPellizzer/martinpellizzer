Ocotea urbaniana

Ocotea urbaniana, commonly known as the white sand tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often aromatic. Its small, white flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces oval, woody fruits that contain a single seed.

Ocotea urbaniana is often found in moist, well-drained soils in tropical and subtropical regions.
