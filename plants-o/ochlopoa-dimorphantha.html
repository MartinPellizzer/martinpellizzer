Ochlopoa dimorphantha

Ochlopoa dimorphantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaf chenopod, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and compact growth habit, which help it conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in spring. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize disturbed soils.

Ochlopoa dimorphantha is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited rainfall, making it a resilient component of its ecosystem.
