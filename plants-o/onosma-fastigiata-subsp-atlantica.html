Onosma fastigiata subsp. atlantica

Onosma fastigiata subsp.

atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic onosma, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Iberian Peninsula. It is a perennial herbaceous species characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to coastal conditions with high salinity and strong winds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in coastal ecosystems.
