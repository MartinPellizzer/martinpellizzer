Ochna semiserrata

Ochna semiserrata, commonly known as the African starwood or wild mango, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, giving it its scientific name.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some regions.
