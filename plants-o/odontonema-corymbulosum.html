Odontonema corymbulosum

Odontonema corymbulosum, commonly known as the coral vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant coral-pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a glossy texture and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is well-suited for hanging baskets or as a ground cover.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a beneficial addition to ecological gardens.
