Octoknema mokoko

Octoknema mokoko, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, dark-colored wood that is highly valued for its fragrance and durability. The plant has simple, opposite, elliptical leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, winged seeds.

The tree is often found in moist, well-drained soils and is used in traditional medicine for its therapeutic properties.
