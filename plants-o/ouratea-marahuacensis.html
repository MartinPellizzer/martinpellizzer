Ouratea marahuacensis

Ouratea marahuacensis, commonly known as the Mexican evergreen holly, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to southern Mexico and parts of Central America, where it typically grows in humid, tropical forests. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with dark green, glossy leaves that are often arranged in whorls. It produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by bright red berries, which are an important food source for local wildlife.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in subtropical gardens.
