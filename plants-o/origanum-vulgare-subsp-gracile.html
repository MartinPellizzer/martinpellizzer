Origanum vulgare subsp. gracile

Origanum vulgare subsp.

gracile, commonly known as slender oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features aromatic, small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces compact clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its essential oils, rich in compounds like carvacrol and thymol, contribute to its use in culinary and medicinal applications.
