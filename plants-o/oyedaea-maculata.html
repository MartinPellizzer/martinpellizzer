Oyedaea maculata

Oyedaea maculata, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive spotted leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for garden cultivation. Its foliage is often marked with irregularly shaped spots, which contribute to its ornamental appeal.

Oyedaea maculata is also valued for its role in supporting local pollinators and its ecological importance in its native habitats.
