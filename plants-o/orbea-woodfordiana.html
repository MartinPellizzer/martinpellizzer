Orbea woodfordiana

Orbea woodfordiana, commonly known as the Zulu orchid, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial herb with a short, erect stem and fleshy, linear leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange with a distinctive hooded lip, and they bloom during the summer months.

Orbea woodfordiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
