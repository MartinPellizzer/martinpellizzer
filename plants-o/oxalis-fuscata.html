Oxalis fuscata

Oxalis fuscata, commonly known as the dark oxalis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features delicate, trifoliate leaves with a dark green hue and small, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its foliage and flowers are often adorned with subtle markings, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Oxalis fuscata is known for its ability to thrive in partial shade and well-drained soil.
