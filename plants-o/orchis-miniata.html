Orchis miniata

Orchis miniata, commonly known as the lesser orchid, is a species of orchid native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces a single, showy flower with a distinctive violet or purple coloration, often with a yellowish or greenish hue on the lip. Its leaves are grass-like and arranged in a rosette at the base of the plant.

Orchis miniata thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.
