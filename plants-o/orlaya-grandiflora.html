Orlaya grandiflora

Orlaya grandiflora, commonly known as the showy milkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Orlaya grandiflora is known for its milky sap, which is toxic and can cause skin irritation.
