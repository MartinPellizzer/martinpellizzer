Omphalodes mexicana

Omphalodes mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican buttonweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces white or pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Omphalodes mexicana is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
