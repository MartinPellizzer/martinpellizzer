Ocotea palcazuensis

Ocotea palcazuensis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian ocotea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical rainforests of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a glossy, dark green upper surface. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces drupes that are often used in traditional medicine.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, contributing to biodiversity and forest structure.
