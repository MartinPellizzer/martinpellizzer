Ocotea rubriflora

Ocotea rubriflora, commonly known as the red-flowered ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and striking red flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, woody drupe that contains a single seed.

Ocotea rubriflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
