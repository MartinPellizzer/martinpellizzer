Orthosiphon teucriifolius

Orthosiphon teucriifolius, commonly known as the Java tea plant, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes plants like mint and basil, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stems. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Orthosiphon teucriifolius thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.
