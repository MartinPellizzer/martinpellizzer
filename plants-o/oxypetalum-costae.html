Oxypetalum costae

Oxypetalum costae, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous terrain, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Oxypetalum costae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climates.
