Ornithogalum fuscatum

Ornithogalum fuscatum, commonly known as the brown bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system for water storage.

Ornithogalum fuscatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique floral structure.
