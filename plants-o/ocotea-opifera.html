Ocotea opifera

Ocotea opifera, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark reddish-brown heartwood that is highly valued for its density and durability. The plant has compound leaves with a glossy upper surface and a lighter, often hairy underside. Its flowers are small and clustered in panicles, and it produces drupes that are typically reddish when mature.

Ocotea opifera is also noted for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
