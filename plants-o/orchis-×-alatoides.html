Orchis × alatoides

Orchis × alatoides, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a cross between Orchis mascula and Orchis anthropophora.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant produces showy, spurred orchid flowers that are typically purple or pink in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

The botanical features include a fibrous root system and a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves.
