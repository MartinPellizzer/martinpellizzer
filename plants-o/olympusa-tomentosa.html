Olympusa tomentosa

Olympusa tomentosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Due to its rapid growth and invasive tendencies, it can become a problematic weed in agricultural areas.
