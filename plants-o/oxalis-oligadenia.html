Oxalis oligadenia

Oxalis oligadenia, commonly known as the slender wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically heart-shaped and have a delicate, glossy appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a delicate touch to its overall appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Oxalis oligadenia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
