Oxalis bipartita var. alpina

Oxalis bipartita var. alpina, commonly known as the alpine wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine regions of Europe.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive white veins and produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or grassy mountain habitats. It is characterized by its succulent stems and leaves, which help it retain moisture in harsh conditions.

Oxalis bipartita var. alpina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
