Oxalis oblongiformis

Oxalis oblongiformis, commonly known as the broadleaf wood sorrel, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive veining pattern and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance.

Oxalis oblongiformis is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plants.
