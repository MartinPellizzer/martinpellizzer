Oxylobium scandens var. obovatum

Oxylobium scandens var. obovatum, commonly known as the broad-leafed climbing broom, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or vine that typically grows in coastal regions and is characterized by its opposite, obovate leaves. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers in terminal clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical features include glandular hairs on the stems and leaves, and it is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in coastal environments.
