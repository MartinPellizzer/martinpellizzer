Orophea dolichocarpa

Orophea dolichocarpa, commonly known as the long-stemmed screwtree, is a tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its palm-like appearance with a single, unbranched stem. The plant produces striking yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by dark purple berries. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy coating.

Orophea dolichocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
