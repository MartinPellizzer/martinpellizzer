Octomeria recchiana

Octomeria recchiana, commonly known as the red-veined orchid, is a striking orchid species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vivid red veins contrasting with its pale green or white petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its flowers are small but numerous, often arranged in dense clusters that enhance its ornamental appeal.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its unique coloration and ecological significance in its native habitat.
