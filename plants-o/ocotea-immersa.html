Ocotea immersa

Ocotea immersa, commonly known as the water ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed, and it is often found in moist, shaded habitats.

The tree is valued for its aromatic wood and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
