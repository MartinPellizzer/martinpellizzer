Onobrychis miniata

Onobrychis miniata, commonly known as the red vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a distinctive yellow or green throat. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, it is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Onobrychis miniata is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in soil enrichment and as a forage plant.
