Oxytropis prenja

Oxytropis prenja, commonly known as the Balkan locoweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing adaptations to arid environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, and it produces small, yellowish to greenish flowers.

Oxytropis prenja is known for its toxic alkaloids, which can affect livestock if ingested.
