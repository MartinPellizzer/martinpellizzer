Orchis macedonica

Orchis macedonica, commonly known as the Macedonian orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and thrives in woodland or meadow habitats. Its flowers are usually arranged in a raceme and bloom from late spring to early summer.

The orchid is noted for its ornamental value and is also of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
