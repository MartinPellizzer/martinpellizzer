Oreocarya flavoculata var. spatulata

Oreocarya flavoculata var. spatulata, commonly known as the yellow-eyed spadeleaf, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellowish-green leaves that are spatulate in shape, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in shades of yellow and green.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to arid environments.
