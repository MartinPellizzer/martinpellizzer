Oryza sativa var. flammea

Oryza sativa var. flammea, commonly known as the flame rice, is a variant of rice characterized by its striking red or orange grain color.

This variety is native to Southeast Asia and is cultivated for both its aesthetic appeal and culinary use. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with long, slender leaves and panicles that bear the colorful grains. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a flowering period that occurs during the late summer and early autumn.

The grains are rich in carbohydrates and contain moderate levels of protein and essential minerals.
