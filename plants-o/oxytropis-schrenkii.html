Oxytropis schrenkii

Oxytropis schrenkii, commonly known as the Schrenk's locoweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces trifoliate leaves and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

It is often found in rocky soils and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria.
