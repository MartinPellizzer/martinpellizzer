Oplismenus humboldtianus var. nudicaulis

Oplismenus humboldtianus var. nudicaulis, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
