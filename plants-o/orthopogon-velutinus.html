Orthopogon velutinus

Orthopogon velutinus, commonly known as the velvet grass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and dark green, silky leaves covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and prairies.

Orthopogon velutinus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small wildlife.
