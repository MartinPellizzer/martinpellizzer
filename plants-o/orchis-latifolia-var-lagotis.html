Orchis latifolia var. lagotis

Orchis latifolia var. lagotis, commonly known as the broad-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces spikes of showy, violet or purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
