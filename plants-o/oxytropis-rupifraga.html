Oxytropis rupifraga

Oxytropis rupifraga, commonly known as the rock hopper, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces yellow or pale yellow pea-like flowers, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky environments and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Oxytropis rupifraga is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
