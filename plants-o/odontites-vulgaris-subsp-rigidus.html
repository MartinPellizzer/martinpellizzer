Odontites vulgaris subsp. rigidus

Odontites vulgaris subsp.

rigidus, commonly known as rigid toothgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its stiff, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in panicles during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species in many parts of its range.
