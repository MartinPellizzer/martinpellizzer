Ophrys labrofossa

Ophrys labrofossa, commonly known as the broad-labied helleborine, is a species of orchid native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a terrestrial herb with a rhizomatous root system and features a single, often showy, greenish-brown flower with a distinctive labellum. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist woodlands and is characterized by its lanceolate leaves that are often covered in a dense pubescence. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its specialized pollination mechanism involving mimicry of female wasps.

The plant plays a role in local ecosystems and is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
