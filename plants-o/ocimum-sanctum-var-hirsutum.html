Ocimum sanctum var. hirsutum

Ocimum sanctum var. hirsutum, commonly known as holy basil or tulsi, is a variant of the sacred basil plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine and religious rituals. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with opposite, ovate leaves that have a slightly hairy surface, giving it its varietal name "hirsutum.". It produces small, white or violet flowers that are followed by four-lobed, oblong capsules containing tiny seeds.

This variety is valued for its adaptability to various climates and its rich content of essential oils and phytochemicals.
