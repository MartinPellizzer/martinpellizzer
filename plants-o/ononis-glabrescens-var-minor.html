Ononis glabrescens var. minor

Ononis glabrescens var. minor, commonly known as the lesser whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its slender stems and small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically thrives in dry, sandy, or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and waste areas. Its leaves are linear and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in loose clusters.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
