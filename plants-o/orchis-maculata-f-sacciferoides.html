Orchis maculata f. sacciferoides

Orchis maculata f. sacciferoides, commonly known as the spotted orchid variant with sac-like structures, is a rare and specialized orchid species characterized by its unique floral morphology.

This botanical variant exhibits a distinctive sacciferous structure on its flowers, which is a modified floral appendage that may play a role in pollination. It is typically found in moist, calcareous habitats such as meadows and woodlands, where it thrives in partial shade. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers with intricate patterns that attract specific pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in certain regions.
