Oxytropis minor

Oxytropis minor, commonly known as lesser locoweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Oxytropis minor is noted for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility in arid environments.
