Orchis matsumurana

Orchis matsumurana, commonly known as the Japanese twayblade, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and a single, showy orchid-like flower. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive lip that is often marked with contrasting patterns.

Orchis matsumurana is notable for its specialized pollination mechanisms, relying on specific insect species for reproduction.
