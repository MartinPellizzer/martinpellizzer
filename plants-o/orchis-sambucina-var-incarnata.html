Orchis sambucina var. incarnata

Orchis sambucina var. incarnata, commonly known as the pink marsh orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and damp meadows in Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and a flowering stem that can reach up to 60 cm in height. Its roots are tuberous, and it often forms dense colonies in its natural habitat.

The species is adapted to moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in association with other wetland flora.
