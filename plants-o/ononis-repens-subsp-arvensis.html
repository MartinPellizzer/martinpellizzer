Ononis repens subsp. arvensis

Ononis repens subsp.

arvensis, commonly known as field stitchwort, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems that bear small, white or pale pink flowers.

The plant has alternate, oval-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
