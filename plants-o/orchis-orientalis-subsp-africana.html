Orchis orientalis subsp. africana

Orchis orientalis subsp.

africana, commonly known as the African orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of Africa. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

The plant produces striking, showy flowers with a distinctive violet or purple coloration, often with intricate patterns on the petals. Its root system is fibrous, and it relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake, a common trait among orchids.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
