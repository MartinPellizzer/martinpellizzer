Opuntia atropes

Opuntia atropes, commonly known as the black prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its dark green, flattened stems covered in spines and glochids. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit, known as a tunas, is edible and has a mild, sweet flavor.

Opuntia atropes is adapted to arid environments and is often used in traditional medicine and as a food source.
