Ocotea cuscoensis

Ocotea cuscoensis, commonly known as the Cusco ocotea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly in the Andean foothills of Peru and Bolivia. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed, and it plays a role in local ecosystems as a food source for various wildlife.

Ocotea cuscoensis is also valued for its timber, which is used in traditional crafts and construction in its native regions.
