Ornithogalum divergens

Ornithogalum divergens, commonly known as the star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, linear leaves and produces umbels of white or pale pink flowers with six petals. The plant is characterized by its showy, star-shaped blooms that appear in early spring. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant appearance.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
