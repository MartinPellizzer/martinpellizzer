Onobrychis sativa f. tomentosa

Onobrychis sativa f. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly sainfoin, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant is adapted to dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments. Its trifoliate leaves and erect, branching stems contribute to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and as a forage plant for livestock.
