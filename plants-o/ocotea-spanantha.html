Ocotea spanantha

Ocotea spanantha, commonly known as the yellow sandarac tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

The tree is valued for its essential oils and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
