Olea woodiana

Olea woodiana, commonly known as the Chinese olive, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that turn from green to black as they mature. Olea woodiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and adaptable plant in various climates.
