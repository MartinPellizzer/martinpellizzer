Oxalis marlothii

Oxalis marlothii, commonly known as the Marloth's wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive reddish veins and produces small, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in mountainous regions and is adapted to arid or semi-arid climates.

Oxalis marlothii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and blooms.
