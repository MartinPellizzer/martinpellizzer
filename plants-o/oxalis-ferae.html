Oxalis ferae

Oxalis ferae, commonly known as the mountain wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces edible tubers that are often used in traditional cuisine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental beauty.

Oxalis ferae is also known for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions.
