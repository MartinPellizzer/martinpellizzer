Oleandra clemensiae

Oleandra clemensiae, commonly known as the Clemens' oleander, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and lance-shaped, with a glossy surface.

Oleandra clemensiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
