Ochna leucophloeos

Ochna leucophloeos, commonly known as the white-leaf ochna, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. Ochna leucophloeos thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or lowland forest areas.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and floral display.
