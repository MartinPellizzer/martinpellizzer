Ocotea conferta

Ocotea conferta, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark purple berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated in subtropical regions as a landscape plant.
