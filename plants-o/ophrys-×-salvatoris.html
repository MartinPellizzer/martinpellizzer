Ophrys × salvatoris

Ophrys × salvatoris, commonly known as the Salviator's bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is characterized by its intricate floral structure, which closely resembles a female bee, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in temperate regions with moist, well-drained soils, often in woodland or meadow habitats. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brownish in color, with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of insects.

Ophrys × salvatoris is a rare and protected species, valued for its unique botanical features and ecological significance.
