Opuntia kleiniae var. gilensis

Opuntia kleiniae var. gilensis, commonly known as the Gil's prickly pear, is a succulent cactus native to Mexico.

It features flattened, fleshy stems called cladodes, which are covered in spines and are used for photosynthesis. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months. Its fruit, known as a tunas, is edible and often used in traditional cuisine.

This variety is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water to thrive.
