Ornithogalum concolor

Ornithogalum concolor, commonly known as the white-flowered star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a bulbous stem base and produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers during the spring season. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ornithogalum concolor is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
