Origanum vulgare var. autrale

Origanum vulgare var. autrale, commonly known as Australian oregano, is a perennial herb native to Australia.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves with a strong, pungent scent reminiscent of oregano. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for Mediterranean and coastal climates.

Its essential oils, rich in thymol and carvacrol, are valued in both culinary and medicinal applications.
