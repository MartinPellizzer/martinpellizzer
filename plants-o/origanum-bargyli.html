Origanum bargyli

Origanum bargyli, commonly known as Greek oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small, gray-green leaves and clusters of tiny, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. Its essential oils, rich in thymol and carvacrol, contribute to its strong scent and medicinal properties.

It is widely used in cooking and traditional herbal remedies.
