Opuntia bruchii f. macracantha

Opuntia bruchii f. macracantha, commonly known as the giant prickly pear, is a unique variant of the Opuntia genus native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its large, flat, paddle-shaped stems that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces striking yellow or orange flowers during the summer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its thick, waxy cuticle helps it retain moisture in arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

This ornamental cactus is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
