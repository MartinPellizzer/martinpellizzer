Ocotea excelsa

Ocotea excelsa, commonly known as the yellow sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green foliage and smooth, pale bark. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by elliptical, dark blue-black berries. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a leathery texture with a strong, spicy scent.

Ocotea excelsa is valued for its wood, which is used in furniture and traditional medicine.
