Oxydectes subferruginea

Oxydectes subferruginea, commonly known as the rusty leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and produces striking, showy flowers with a distinctive reddish-brown hue. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are borne on slender inflorescences and are pollinated by insects.

Oxydectes subferruginea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
