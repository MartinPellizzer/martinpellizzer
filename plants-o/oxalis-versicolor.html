Oxalis versicolor

Oxalis versicolor, commonly known as the variable wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink, white, and yellow. The plant belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its ability to thrive in partial shade and well-drained soils. Its botanical name reflects the variability in its leaf and flower forms.

Oxalis versicolor is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
