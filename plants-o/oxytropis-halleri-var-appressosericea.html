Oxytropis halleri var. appressosericea

Oxytropis halleri var. appressosericea, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or pink. The plant has small, compound leaves with pinnate structure, and its root system is well-developed, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils. It is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation projects.
