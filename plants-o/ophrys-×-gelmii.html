Ophrys × gelmii

Ophrys × gelmii, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant that results from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Mediterranean region. The plant is characterized by its intricate, bee-like floral structure, which plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow with a distinctive dark marking that mimics the appearance of a female bee.

Ophrys × gelmii is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils, where it thrives in well-drained conditions.
