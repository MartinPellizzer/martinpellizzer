Ormosia scandens

Ormosia scandens, commonly known as the climbing ormosia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. Ormosia scandens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for use as a climbing plant in gardens or as a hedge.
