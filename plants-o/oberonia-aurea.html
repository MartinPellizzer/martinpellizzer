Oberonia aurea

Oberonia aurea, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, attaching itself to trees or rocks in its natural habitat. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

The botanical features of Oberonia aurea make it a popular specimen among orchid enthusiasts and botanists alike.
