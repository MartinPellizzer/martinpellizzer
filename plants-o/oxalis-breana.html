Oxalis breana

Oxalis breana, commonly known as the purple shamrock, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are often a vibrant green with a reddish or purple tinge. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and indoor cultivation.

Oxalis breana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to add a touch of color to landscapes.
