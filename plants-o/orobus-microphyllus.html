Orobus microphyllus

Orobus microphyllus, commonly known as the small-leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact size and unique floral structure. The plant produces small, intricate flowers that are often pale green or yellowish in color. Its leaves are narrow and closely spaced, adapted to its natural habitat in shaded, humid environments.

Orobus microphyllus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
