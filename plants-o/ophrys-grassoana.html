Ophrys grassoana

Ophrys grassoana, commonly known as the Grassana orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its intricate, bee-mimicking flowers that play a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, thriving in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its flowers feature a distinctive yellow and brown coloration, with a prominent lip that resembles a female bee.

Ophrys grassoana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
