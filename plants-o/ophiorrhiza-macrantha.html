Ophiorrhiza macrantha

Ophiorrhiza macrantha, commonly known as the large-flowered sandalwood, is a tropical shrub belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Ophiorrhiza macrantha is often found in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in humid, tropical climates.
