Oxalis dispar

Oxalis dispar, commonly known as the narrowleaf wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces edible leaves and flowers, though they should be consumed in moderation due to their oxalic acid content. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Oxalis dispar is often grown as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
