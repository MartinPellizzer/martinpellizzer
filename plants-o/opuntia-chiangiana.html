Opuntia chiangiana

Opuntia chiangiana, commonly known as the Chiang Mai prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Thailand.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, followed by edible red fruits. Opuntia chiangiana is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in tropical climates.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and for its fruit, which is used in local cuisine.
