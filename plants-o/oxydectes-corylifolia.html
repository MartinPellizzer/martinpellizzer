Oxydectes corylifolia

Oxydectes corylifolia, commonly known as the Chinese holly or the sacred tree, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, featuring smooth, dark grey bark and alternate, palmately compound leaves. The leaves are typically 10–20 cm long with 5–7 leaflets, and the plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters.

Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and cultural significance.
