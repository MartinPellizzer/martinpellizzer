Opuntia hystricina var. ursina

Opuntia hystricina var. ursina, commonly known as the Mexican apple cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that are covered in spines and are often segmented. The plant produces large, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit, known as the "tuna," is edible and has a sweet, juicy flavor.

This variety is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
