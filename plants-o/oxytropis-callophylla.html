Oxytropis callophylla

Oxytropis callophylla, commonly known as the broad-leaved locoweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and palmately compound leaves with rounded lobes. The plant produces small, yellowish to greenish flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.

Oxytropis callophylla is a member of the legume family (Fabaceae) and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria.
