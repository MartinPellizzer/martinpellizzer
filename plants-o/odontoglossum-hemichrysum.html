Odontoglossum hemichrysum

Odontoglossum hemichrysum, commonly known as the golden lip orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with erect, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive lip that is often marked with purple or red. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its striking floral display.
