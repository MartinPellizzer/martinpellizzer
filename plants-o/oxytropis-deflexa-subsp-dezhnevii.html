Oxytropis deflexa subsp. dezhnevii

Oxytropis deflexa subsp.

dezhnevii, commonly known as the Siberian locoweed, is a perennial legume native to the steppes and forest-steppe regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple.

The plant has compound leaves with multiple pairs of leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is adapted to dry, well-drained soils and can tolerate harsh environmental conditions.

This subspecies is often found in open habitats and is of interest for its ecological and potential medicinal properties.
