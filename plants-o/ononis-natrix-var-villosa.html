Ononis natrix var. villosa

Ononis natrix var. villosa, commonly known as hairy bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its slender, ascending stems covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, oval-shaped, and have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Hairy bedstraw is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along paths, thriving in well-drained soils.
