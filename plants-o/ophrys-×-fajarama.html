Ophrys × fajarama

Ophrys × fajarama, commonly known as the Fajara bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys faginea.

It is characterized by its unique floral morphology, featuring a specialized lip that mimics the appearance of a female bee, which plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils in Mediterranean climates, thriving in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brownish in color, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Ophrys × fajarama is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in supporting specific pollinator species.
