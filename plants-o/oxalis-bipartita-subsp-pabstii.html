Oxalis bipartita subsp. pabstii

Oxalis bipartita subsp.

pabstii, commonly known as the Pabst's wood sorrel, is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly Brazil. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each leaflet being heart-shaped with a distinct reddish or purple tint.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to botanical gardens and natural habitats.
