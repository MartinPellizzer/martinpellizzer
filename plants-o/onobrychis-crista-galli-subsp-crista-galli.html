Onobrychis crista-galli subsp. crista-galli

Onobrychis crista-galli subsp.

crista-galli, commonly known as goat's rue, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive spiny pods and trifoliate leaves, which are typical of the Fabaceae family.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Goat's rue is known for its hardy nature and ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial in certain ecological contexts.
