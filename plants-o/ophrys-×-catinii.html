Ophrys × catinii

Ophrys × catinii, commonly known as the honey orchid, is a hybrid orchid native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, bee-like flower structure that mimics the appearance and scent of female bees. The plant produces showy, yellow-brown flowers with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by male bees. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Ophrys × catinii is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in supporting pollinator species.
