Ossaea rufibarbis

Ossaea rufibarbis, commonly known as the red-bearded grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and produces spikelets that are reddish-brown in color, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while the inflorescence consists of compact, spike-like clusters.

Ossaea rufibarbis is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
