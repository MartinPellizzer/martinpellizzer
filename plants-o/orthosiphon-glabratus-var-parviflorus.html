Orthosiphon glabratus var. parviflorus

Orthosiphon glabratus var. parviflorus, commonly known as small-flowered holy basil, is a tropical herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and small, pale green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is often cultivated for its medicinal properties, particularly for its diuretic and anti-inflammatory effects. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in traditional herbal medicine and horticulture.
