Oxytropis jarovoji

Oxytropis jarovoji, commonly known as Jarov's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces fluffy, yellowish-pink flower clusters that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Oxytropis jarovoji plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinator populations.
