Oenothera cespitosa subsp. brandegeei

Oenothera cespitosa subsp.

brandegeei, commonly known as the Brandegee's evening primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky or sandy habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by moths, which are active at night.
