Oxalis luederitzii

Oxalis luederitzii, commonly known as the Luederitz oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves and vibrant yellow flowers. The plant produces small, bead-like fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Oxalis luederitzii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
