Origanum bracteatum

Origanum bracteatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. Its essential oils contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its strong scent and medicinal properties.

It is often used in traditional herbal remedies and as a culinary herb.
