Ophrys × kalteiseniana

Ophrys × kalteiseniana, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant belonging to the Ophrys genus in the Orchidaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Ophrys apifera (the bee orchid) and Ophrys insectifera (the wasp orchid), resulting in a unique combination of traits from both parents. The plant is characterized by its intricate, bee-like flowers that serve as a mimicry mechanism to attract male bees for pollination. Its flowers typically exhibit a yellowish-brown color with intricate patterns resembling a female bee, enhancing its deceptive allure.

This orchid thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows and woodland edges, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
