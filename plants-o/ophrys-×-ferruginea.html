Ophrys × ferruginea

Ophrys × ferruginea, commonly known as the Rusty Bee Orchid, is a hybrid orchid species that exhibits a unique combination of traits from its parent species.

It is characterized by its intricate, bee-like flower structure, which features a prominent, fuzzy lip that mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and is found in temperate regions of Europe. Its flowers are usually a mix of brown, rust, and green hues, providing effective camouflage in its natural habitat.

Ophrys × ferruginea is a short-lived perennial, often appearing as a rosette of leaves before producing its showy inflorescence.
