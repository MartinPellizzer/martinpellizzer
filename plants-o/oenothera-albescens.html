Oenothera albescens

Oenothera albescens, commonly known as the white evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the central and western United States.

It is characterized by its showy white to pale yellow flowers that open in the evening and close by morning. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

Oenothera albescens is also known for its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
