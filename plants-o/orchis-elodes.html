Orchis elodes

Orchis elodes, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces showy, violet to purple flowers that are typically arranged in a spike, with a distinctive lip petal. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

Orchis elodes plays an important ecological role by supporting pollinators and contributing to biodiversity in its native habitats.
