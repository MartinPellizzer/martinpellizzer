Oxytropis ochroleuca

Oxytropis ochroleuca, commonly known as yellow locoweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. The plant produces small, yellow flowers clustered in dense spikes, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the plant is often found in soils with low nutrient content.

Oxytropis ochroleuca is part of the legume family and has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.
