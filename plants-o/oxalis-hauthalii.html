Oxalis hauthalii

Oxalis hauthalii, commonly known as the silver leaf oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking silver-green leaves with a waxy texture and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is a low-maintenance plant that thrives in well-drained conditions and is often used in rock gardens or as an ornamental plant.

Oxalis hauthalii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
