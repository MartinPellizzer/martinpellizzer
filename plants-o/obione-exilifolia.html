Obione exilifolia

Obione exilifolia, commonly known as the slender-leaf obione, is a small, herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It features delicate, narrow leaves that are typically less than 2 centimeters in width, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and early summer months. Its growth habit is low and spreading, often forming dense mats on moist, shaded forest floors.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
