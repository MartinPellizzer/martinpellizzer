Olgaea laniceps

Olgaea laniceps, commonly known as the silky-haired olgaea, is a perennial plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and arranged in a basal rosette, which helps it conserve water in dry conditions.

Olgaea laniceps is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low precipitation.
