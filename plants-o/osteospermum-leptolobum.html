Osteospermum leptolobum

Osteospermum leptolobum, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces showy, daisy-like flowers with a central disc surrounded by ray florets. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Osteospermum leptolobum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
