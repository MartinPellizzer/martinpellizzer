Orchadocarpa lilacina

Orchadocarpa lilacina, commonly known as the purple orchid fruit, is a unique plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often showy flowers. The plant produces striking violet-colored fruits that are both ornamental and edible, though they are not commonly cultivated. Its leaves are broad and glossy, contributing to its aesthetic appeal in both natural and garden settings.

The plant thrives in humid environments with well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
