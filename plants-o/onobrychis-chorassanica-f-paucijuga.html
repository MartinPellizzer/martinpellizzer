Onobrychis chorassanica f. paucijuga

Onobrychis chorassanica f. paucijuga, commonly known as the sparse-leaved Persian clover, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It features slender, branching stems that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. Its leaves are small, trifoliate, and have a distinctive bluish-green color. The plant produces small, yellow pea-like flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer.

It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
