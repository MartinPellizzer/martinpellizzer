Oenothera albicaulis var. brevifolia

Oenothera albicaulis var. brevifolia, commonly known as the shortleaf evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its short, upright stems and showy white to pale yellow flowers that open in the evening. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, and the plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils. It is part of the evening primrose family (Onagraceae) and is known for its nocturnal pollination by moths.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
