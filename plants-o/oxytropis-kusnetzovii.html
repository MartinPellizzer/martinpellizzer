Oxytropis kusnetzovii

Oxytropis kusnetzovii, commonly known as the Kusnetzov's locoweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Oxytropis kusnetzovii is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
