Oncidium dayanum

Oncidium dayanum, commonly known as the Dayanum orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous inflorescence that bears clusters of fragrant, yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrates.

Oncidium dayanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant, showy blooms.
