Oxydectes elliptica

Oxydectes elliptica, commonly known as the arrowleaf plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its elliptical, arrow-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or containers.

Oxydectes elliptica is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
