Ochradenus randonioides

Ochradenus randonioides, commonly known as the desert tamarisk, is a shrub native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots that allow it to survive in dry conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.

Its botanical features make it a resilient and hardy species in extreme climates.
