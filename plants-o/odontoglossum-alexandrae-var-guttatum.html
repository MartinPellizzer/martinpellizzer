Odontoglossum alexandrae var. guttatum

Odontoglossum alexandrae var. guttatum, commonly known as the Alexandra's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically feature a combination of purple, white, and green hues. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in its high-altitude, often cool and moist environment. Its leaves are usually broad and strap-like, adapted to capture sunlight efficiently.

This variety is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and unique floral structure.
