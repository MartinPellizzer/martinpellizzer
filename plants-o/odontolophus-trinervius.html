Odontolophus trinervius

Odontolophus trinervius, commonly known as the three-veined orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with three prominent veins on the petals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Odontolophus trinervius is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
