Oplonia tetrasticha subsp. polyece

Oplonia tetrasticha subsp.

polyece, commonly known as the coastal hakea, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, needle-like leaves that are adapted to coastal conditions.

The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are typically visited by birds and insects. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal heathlands.

Due to its resilience and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
