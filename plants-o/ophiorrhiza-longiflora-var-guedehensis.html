Ophiorrhiza longiflora var. guedehensis

Ophiorrhiza longiflora var. guedehensis, commonly known as the Guedeha marsh mallow, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in wet or marshy environments, showcasing its adaptability to humid and waterlogged soils. The plant features small, white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and often have a waxy texture, aiding in water retention.

The species is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique ecological niche and potential medicinal properties.
