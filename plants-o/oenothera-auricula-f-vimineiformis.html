Oenothera auricula f. vimineiformis

Oenothera auricula f. vimineiformis, commonly known as the trailing evening primrose, is a variant of the evening primrose species characterized by its trailing growth habit.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or containers.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden environments.
