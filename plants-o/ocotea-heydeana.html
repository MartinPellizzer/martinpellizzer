Ocotea heydeana

Ocotea heydeana, commonly known as the Brazilian camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant typically grows in humid lowland forests and can reach heights of up to 30 meters. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a glossy dark green upper surface.

The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers and is valued for its essential oils and medicinal properties.
