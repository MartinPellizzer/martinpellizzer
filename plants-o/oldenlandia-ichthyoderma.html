Oldenlandia ichthyoderma

Oldenlandia ichthyoderma, commonly known as the fish-scale plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. The leaves are typically opposite, ovate to elliptic, and often have a rough, slightly hairy texture. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its common name refers to the distinctive, scale-like patterns on its leaves.
