Oenothera platanorum

Oenothera platanorum, commonly known as the plains evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

Oenothera platanorum is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its role in supporting local pollinators.
