Oxalis multicaulis

Oxalis multicaulis, commonly known as the clustered wood sorrel, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It features a short, creeping stem with multiple upright shoots that bear trifoliate leaves resembling those of true sorrel. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardens due to its attractive appearance.

Oxalis multicaulis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade.
