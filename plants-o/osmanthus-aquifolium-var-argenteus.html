Osmanthus aquifolium var. argenteus

Osmanthus aquifolium var. argenteus, commonly known as silver leaf osmanthus, is a variant of the osmanthus plant native to China.

It is characterized by its striking silver-white variegated leaves that contrast with its small, fragrant yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are glossy and have a leathery texture, while the flowers emit a sweet, honey-like fragrance.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and aromatic properties.
