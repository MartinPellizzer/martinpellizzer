Oxytropis hirta var. flavida

Oxytropis hirta var. flavida, commonly known as yellow hairy locoweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces legume pods that contain several seeds.

This variety is distinguished by its yellow flowers, which contrast with the gray-green foliage.
