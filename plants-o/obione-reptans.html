Obione reptans

Obione reptans, commonly known as the creeping lily or spider lily, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its slender, spreading stems and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy surface, aiding in water retention.

Obione reptans is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
