Oenothera fruticosa var. fraseri

Oenothera fruticosa var. fraseri, commonly known as Fraser's evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a shrub-like species with woody stems and produces showy yellow or orange flowers that open in the evening. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun. It is part of the evening primrose family (Onagraceae) and is known for its nocturnal blooming habit.

The flowers attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths and bats.
