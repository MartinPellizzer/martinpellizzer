Oenothera whitneyi

Oenothera whitneyi, commonly known as Whitney's evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening and close by dawn. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has hairy, ribbed stems. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Oenothera whitneyi is part of the evening primrose family (Onagraceae) and is known for its ecological importance to pollinators.
