Oxalis coccinea

Oxalis coccinea, commonly known as the Crimson Wood Sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of bright red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-drained soil. It is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters.

Oxalis coccinea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
