Ovieda anceps

Ovieda anceps, commonly known as the Mexican flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to the landscape. The plant has a deciduous habit, shedding its leaves seasonally, and produces small, woody pods that contain seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a serrated margin.

Ovieda anceps is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates for its showy floral display.
