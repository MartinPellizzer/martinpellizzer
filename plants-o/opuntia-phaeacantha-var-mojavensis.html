Opuntia phaeacantha var. mojavensis

Opuntia phaeacantha var. mojavensis, commonly known as the Mojave prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flattened, fleshy stems that are covered in spines and are typically green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible, red-fleshed fruits. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick stems and having a slow growth rate.

This variety is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of food in desert regions.
