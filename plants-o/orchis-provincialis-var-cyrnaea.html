Orchis provincialis var. cyrnaea

Orchis provincialis var. cyrnaea, commonly known as the Cyrenaic orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to specific regions in North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and striking, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements make it a vulnerable species.

Conservation efforts are crucial to protect this unique botanical specimen from habitat loss and environmental changes.
