Ormosia semicastrata

Ormosia semicastrata, commonly known as the Philippine tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces woody pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its growth habit includes a straight trunk and dense foliage, making it suitable for shade and reforestation efforts.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
