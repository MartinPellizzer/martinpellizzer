Ophrys apifera f. trollii

Ophrys apifera f. trollii, commonly known as the bee orchid, is a unique orchid species characterized by its distinctive floral morphology.

The plant exhibits a specialized pollination mechanism, mimicking the appearance and scent of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. It typically grows in calcareous soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. The flowers are usually greenish-yellow with intricate patterns that resemble insect bodies.

This orchid is native to Europe and is often found in regions with a temperate climate.
