Oxytropis pilosa

Oxytropis pilosa, commonly known as silky locoweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. The plant features slender, erect stems covered in soft, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, pea-like flowers are usually pink to purple and bloom in early summer.

Oxytropis pilosa is part of the legume family and has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, which can benefit soil fertility.
