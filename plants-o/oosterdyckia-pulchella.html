Oosterdyckia pulchella

Oosterdyckia pulchella, commonly known as the pretty orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a fan-like pattern around the stem.

Oosterdyckia pulchella prefers warm, humid environments and is often found in shaded, moist forest understories.
