Oreocallis grandiflora

Oreocallis grandiflora, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like foliage and striking, showy flowers that bloom in shades of violet and white. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions with well-drained soil. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense clumps.

Oreocallis grandiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and specialized collections.
