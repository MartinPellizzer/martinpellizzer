Osmelia conferta

Osmelia conferta, commonly known as the Florida bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Osmelia conferta plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens.
