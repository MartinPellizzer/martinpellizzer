Oncosporum drummondianum

Oncosporum drummondianum, commonly known as Drummond's goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Oncosporum drummondianum plays a role in supporting pollinators and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
