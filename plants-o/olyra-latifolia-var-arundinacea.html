Olyra latifolia var. arundinacea

Olyra latifolia var. arundinacea, commonly known as the giant bamboo palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height, with a smooth, grayish-brown bark. The plant has large, pinnately compound leaves that are dark green and glossy, providing a striking visual appearance. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by clustered fruits.

This variety is well-suited for humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in tropical gardens.
