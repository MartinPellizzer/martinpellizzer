Ophrys arachnites subsp. holubyana

Ophrys arachnites subsp.

holubyana, commonly known as the Holubyan bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to parts of Central and Eastern Europe. It is characterized by its unique floral morphology, featuring a bee-like labellum that mimics the appearance and scent of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination.

The plant typically grows in dry, calcareous grasslands and rocky habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brownish in color, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Due to habitat loss and fragmentation, this subspecies is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
