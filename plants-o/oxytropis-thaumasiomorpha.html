Oxytropis thaumasiomorpha

Oxytropis thaumasiomorpha, commonly known as the ghostflower, is a member of the legume family Fabaceae.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces unique, pale violet to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is adapted to arid environments and is found in rocky soils of the southwestern United States.

Its name reflects its ghostly appearance and rarity in the wild.
