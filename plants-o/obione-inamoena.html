Obione inamoena

Obione inamoena, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, and it is often found growing in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and abandoned fields. Its rapid growth and invasive tendencies make it a common weed in agricultural areas.

The plant's leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine, though its ecological impact is often negative due to its competitive nature.
