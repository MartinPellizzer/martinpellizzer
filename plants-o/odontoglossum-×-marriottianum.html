Odontoglossum × marriottianum

Odontoglossum × marriottianum, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Odontoglossum lindenii and Odontoglossum regale.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the high-altitude regions of South America. The plant is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and unique floral structure.
