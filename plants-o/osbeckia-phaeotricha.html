Osbeckia phaeotricha

Osbeckia phaeotricha, commonly known as the woolly osbeckia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, temperate regions. The plant features hairy, glandular leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a sprawling growth habit.

Osbeckia phaeotricha is often found in meadows and along stream banks, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
