Odontoglossum arminii

Odontoglossum arminii, commonly known as the Arminii Orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with a rhizome that produces multiple pseudobulbs. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive lip that is often marked with purple or brown.

Odontoglossum arminii is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and relatively easy care requirements in cultivation.
