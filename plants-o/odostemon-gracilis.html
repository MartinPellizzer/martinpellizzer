Odostemon gracilis

Odostemon gracilis, commonly known as slender false dandelion, is a herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a composite head.

Odostemon gracilis is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
