Ocotea aenea

Ocotea aenea, commonly known as the silverwood tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its dark, aromatic bark and glossy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers followed by oval, dark blue berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its wood is valued for its durability and resistance to decay, making it useful in construction and furniture-making.
