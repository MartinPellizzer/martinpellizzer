Ocotea venenosa

Ocotea venenosa, commonly known as poisonwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces berries that contain toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals. Its wood is valued for its durability and aromatic properties, though it is often difficult to work with.

Ocotea venenosa plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
