Odontoglossum rossii var. amesianum

Odontoglossum rossii var. amesianum, commonly known as the Amesian orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple and white. The plant features a pseudobulb structure, which stores water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its high-altitude habitat. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it prefers cool, humid environments with well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
