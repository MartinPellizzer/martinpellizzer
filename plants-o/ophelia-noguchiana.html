Ophelia noguchiana

Ophelia noguchiana, commonly known as the Japanese waterplantain, is a perennial aquatic plant native to East Asia.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often submerged, with some emerging above the water surface. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, usually blooming in late spring to early summer.

Ophelia noguchiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in water gardens.
