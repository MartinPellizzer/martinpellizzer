Oxalis europaea var. bushii

Oxalis europaea var. bushii, commonly known as the Bush's wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive yellow-orange flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10-20 centimeters and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its foliage is often covered in fine, silky hairs, and it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.

The species is part of the Oxalidaceae family, known for its calcium oxalate crystals in the leaves.
