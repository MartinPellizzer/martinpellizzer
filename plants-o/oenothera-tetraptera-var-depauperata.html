Oenothera tetraptera var. depauperata

Oenothera tetraptera var. depauperata, commonly known as the sparseleaf evening primrose, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and showy yellow flowers that open in the evening. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oval seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Oenothera tetraptera var. depauperata is often found in dry slopes, gravelly soils, and disturbed areas.
