Oxygonum somalense

Oxygonum somalense, commonly known as the Somali tamarind, is a deciduous shrub native to the arid regions of Somalia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant is adapted to withstand drought conditions, with deep roots that allow it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, and it produces edible pods that contain seeds used for propagation.
