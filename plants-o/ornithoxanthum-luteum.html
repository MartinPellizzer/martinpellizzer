Ornithoxanthum luteum

Ornithoxanthum luteum, commonly known as the yellow oxalis, is a flowering plant belonging to the Oxalidaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers. The plant produces seed pods that split open to release seeds, a common feature among members of its family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Ornithoxanthum luteum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
