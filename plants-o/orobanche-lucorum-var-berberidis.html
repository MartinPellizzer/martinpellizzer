Orobanche lucorum var. berberidis

Orobanche lucorum var. berberidis, commonly known as the yellow broomweed, is a parasitic plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies on host plants for nutrients.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has a highly reduced structure, with no true leaves. It typically parasitizes plants in the legume family, such as vetch and clover.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can cause significant damage to agricultural crops.
