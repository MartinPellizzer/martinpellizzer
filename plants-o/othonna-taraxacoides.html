Othonna taraxacoides

Othonna taraxacoides, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant features dense clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically fleshy and arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to store water in arid environments.

Othonna taraxacoides thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
