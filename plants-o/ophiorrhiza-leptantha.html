Ophiorrhiza leptantha

Ophiorrhiza leptantha, commonly known as the slender-leafed mangrove, is a flowering plant species native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small shrub that thrives in brackish water environments, often found in mangrove forests. The plant has small, elliptical leaves with a leathery texture and is characterized by its unique, clustered inflorescences. It produces small white flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Ophiorrhiza leptantha plays a vital role in stabilizing coastal soils and supporting local biodiversity.
