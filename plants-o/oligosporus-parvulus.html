Oligosporus parvulus

Oligosporus parvulus, commonly known as the small-spore grass, is a small flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact stature and dense, tufted growth habit, often found in moist, temperate regions. The plant produces tiny, wind-dispersed seeds enclosed in small, spike-like structures. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that helps it blend into its natural habitat.

Oligosporus parvulus plays a minor role in local ecosystems, primarily as a food source for small herbivorous insects and birds.
