Olea walpersiana

Olea walpersiana, commonly known as the Chinese olive, is a small evergreen tree native to China and other parts of East Asia.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to various regions for horticultural purposes.

The plant is also valued for its oil-rich fruits, which have been used in traditional medicine and cooking.
