Orcya adhaerens

Orcya adhaerens, commonly known as the sticky orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its unique, sticky sepals that help it adhere to surfaces, a feature that aids in its survival in various habitats. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and grasslands. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a distinctive, elongated spur.

Orcya adhaerens is noted for its specialized pollination mechanisms, often relying on specific insect species for reproduction.
