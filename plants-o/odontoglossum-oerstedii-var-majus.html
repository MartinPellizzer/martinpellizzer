Odontoglossum oerstedii var. majus

Odontoglossum oerstedii var. majus, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, making it one of the most striking orchids in the genus. The plant typically grows in high-altitude cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are broad and dark green, often forming a rosette shape to help retain moisture.

The flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive lip that is often marked with contrasting patterns.
