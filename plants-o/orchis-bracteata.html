Orchis bracteata

Orchis bracteata, commonly known as the bracted orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces a spike of pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. Orchis bracteata is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is of interest to botanists due to its ecological and floral characteristics.
