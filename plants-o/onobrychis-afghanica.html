Onobrychis afghanica

Onobrychis afghanica, commonly known as Afghan clover, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion.

Onobrychis afghanica is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
