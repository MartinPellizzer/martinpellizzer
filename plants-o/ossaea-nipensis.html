Ossaea nipensis

Ossaea nipensis, commonly known as the Japanese orchid, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil.

Ossaea nipensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
