Oxalis rustii

Oxalis rustii, commonly known as the Cape mountain wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid conditions. Its foliage contains oxalic acid, which can be toxic if ingested in large quantities.

Oxalis rustii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained environments.
