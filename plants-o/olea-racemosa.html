Olea racemosa

Olea racemosa, commonly known as the clustered olive, is a shrub or small tree native to the Canary Islands and Madeira.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Olea racemosa is adapted to coastal and rocky environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and salt resistance.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in Mediterranean climates.
