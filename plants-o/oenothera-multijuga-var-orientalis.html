Oenothera multijuga var. orientalis

Oenothera multijuga var. orientalis, commonly known as the eastern evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats like meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

The species is known for its medicinal properties, with its seeds containing high levels of essential fatty acids.
