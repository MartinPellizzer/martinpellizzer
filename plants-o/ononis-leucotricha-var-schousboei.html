Ononis leucotricha var. schousboei

Ononis leucotricha var. schousboei, commonly known as the white-haired cinquefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves that are often covered with white or silver pubescence. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers in early summer, arranged in clusters along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry grasslands or rocky habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Rosaceae family.
