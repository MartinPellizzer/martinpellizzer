Ophiopogon bockianus var. angustifoliatus

Ophiopogon bockianus var. angustifoliatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spathiphyllum, is a low-growing evergreen perennial native to Japan.

It features dense, dark green foliage with narrow, strap-like leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow margins. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial to full shade and requires moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in shaded gardens and as a ground cover.
