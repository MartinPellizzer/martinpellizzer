Oxytropis lavrenkoi

Oxytropis lavrenkoi, commonly known as the Lavrenko's locoweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its legume pods and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has small, compound leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers are usually pale blue or purple, and it produces slender, upright stems.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a rare and endemic species in its native range.
