Oxydectes multicostata

Oxydectes multicostata, commonly known as the multibract grass, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and elongated, narrow leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its fibrous roots make it a valuable plant for erosion control in aquatic environments.
