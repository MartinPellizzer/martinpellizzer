Ononis irritabilis

Ononis irritabilis, commonly known as the burning bush, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit, with hairy, narrow leaves that help reduce water loss in arid conditions. Its root system is fibrous and shallow, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils.

Ononis irritabilis is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments.
