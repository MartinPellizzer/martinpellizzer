Odontoglossum aspersum var. fulvidum

Odontoglossum aspersum var. fulvidum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It features a rhizomatous growth habit and produces striking, showy flowers with yellow and green hues. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

It is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
