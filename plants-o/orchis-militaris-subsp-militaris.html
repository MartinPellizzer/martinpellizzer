Orchis militaris subsp. militaris

Orchis militaris subsp.

militaris, commonly known as the soldier's orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet-blue flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils, while its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often found in wildflower gardens and conservation areas.
