Opuntia mesacantha var. grandiflora

Opuntia mesacantha var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy pads that are covered in sharp spines and are typically green in color. The plant produces large, showy yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its flowers are followed by bright red or yellow fruits that are edible and often used in culinary applications.

This species is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water to thrive.
