Odontoglossum ramosissimum var. xanthinum

Odontoglossum ramosissimum var. xanthinum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking, showy flowers with vibrant yellow petals. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate floral structure and epiphytic growth habit. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, supporting the plant's unique morphology.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
