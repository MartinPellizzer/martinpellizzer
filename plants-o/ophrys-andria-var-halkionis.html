Ophrys andria var. halkionis

Ophrys andria var. halkionis, commonly known as the Halkionis bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the Greek island of Naxos.

It is characterized by its striking floral morphology, with a lip that closely resembles a female bee, a feature evolved to mimic pollinators. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is found in open, sunny habitats. It blooms in late spring, producing flowers with a complex structure that facilitates specific pollination by male bees.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
