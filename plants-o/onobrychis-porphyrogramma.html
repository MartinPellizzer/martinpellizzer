Onobrychis porphyrogramma

Onobrychis porphyrogramma, commonly known as the purple vetch, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. It is often found in dry habitats such as grasslands and shrublands.

Onobrychis porphyrogramma is a perennial herb that plays a role in soil stabilization and supports local pollinator populations.
