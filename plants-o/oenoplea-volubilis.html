Oenoplea volubilis

Oenoplea volubilis, commonly known as the climbing honeysuckle, is a vine native to the temperate regions of South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and ability to climb using its tendrils. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.

Oenoplea volubilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types with adequate sunlight.
