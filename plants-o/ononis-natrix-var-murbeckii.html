Ononis natrix var. murbeckii

Ononis natrix var. murbeckii, commonly known as the creeping bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is often found in moist, calcareous soils in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky areas.
