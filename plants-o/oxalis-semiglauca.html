Oxalis semiglauca

Oxalis semiglauca, commonly known as the silverleaf wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to parts of North America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive silvery-blue underside, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in woodland environments and prefers partial shade to full sun.

Oxalis semiglauca is often found in moist, well-drained soils and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
