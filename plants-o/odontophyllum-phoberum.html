Odontophyllum phoberum

Odontophyllum phoberum, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and elusive orchid species native to the rainforests of Brazil.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated floral structure and striking white, ghost-like flowers that appear during the dry season. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. It has small, scale-like leaves and relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient absorption.

Due to its habitat specificity and limited distribution, it is considered critically endangered.
