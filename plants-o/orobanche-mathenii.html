Orobanche mathenii

Orobanche mathenii, commonly known as the desert broom.

It is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on its host for nutrients. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and is typically found in arid and semi-arid regions. Its root system is specialized to attach to the roots of host plants, such as various species of legumes.

Due to its parasitic nature, it plays a unique role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat.
