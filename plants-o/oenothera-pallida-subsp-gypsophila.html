Oenothera pallida subsp. gypsophila

Oenothera pallida subsp.

gypsophila, commonly known as ghostflower or pale evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and showy, pale yellow to white flowers that open in the evening.

The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered with fine hairs.

The species is noted for its nocturnal blooming habit, attracting moths and other night-flying pollinators.
