Oryza sativa var. usitatissima

Oryza sativa var. usitatissima, commonly known as the dwarf rice variety, is a subspecies of rice characterized by its compact growth habit and shorter stature compared to other rice varieties.

It is native to Southeast Asia and has been cultivated for thousands of years as a staple food crop. This variety is particularly valued for its high yield potential and adaptability to a range of environmental conditions. Its botanical features include narrow leaves, a fibrous root system, and small, clustered inflorescences that produce grains.

The plant's genome has been extensively studied to improve its resilience to diseases and climate stress.
