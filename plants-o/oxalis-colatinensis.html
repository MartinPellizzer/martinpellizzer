Oxalis colatinensis

Oxalis colatinensis, commonly known as the velvetleaf wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces bright orange or yellow berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by birds. Oxalis colatinensis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental species in its native habitat.
