Obione rosei

Obione rosei, commonly known as the roseate orchid, is a unique flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant blooms that range in color from pale pink to deep rose. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrate.

Obione rosei is valued not only for its aesthetic appeal but also for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity in its native habitat.
