Oxalis speciosa

Oxalis speciosa, commonly known as the showy wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive white veins and produces clusters of five-petaled, bright yellow flowers. The plant is characterized by its succulent stems and leaves, which help it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Oxalis speciosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
