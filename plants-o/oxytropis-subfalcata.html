Oxytropis subfalcata

Oxytropis subfalcata, commonly known as the sickleleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of western North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are narrow and often curved, giving it the common name "sickleleaf.".

Oxytropis subfalcata is part of the legume family and has the ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it ecologically significant in its habitat.
