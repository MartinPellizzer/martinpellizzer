Ocotea rigidifolia

Ocotea rigidifolia, commonly known as the rigid-leaf ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that are typically rigid and lance-shaped. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark purple berries.

Ocotea rigidifolia is valued for its aromatic wood and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.
