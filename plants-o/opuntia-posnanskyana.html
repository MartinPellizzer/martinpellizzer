Opuntia posnanskyana

Opuntia posnanskyana, commonly known as the Posnansky's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its flattened, paddle-shaped stems that are covered in spines and glochids, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months, followed by edible fruits that are often consumed by both humans and animals. Opuntia posnanskyana is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in regions with limited rainfall.

Its succulent structure allows it to store water efficiently, contributing to its survival in harsh conditions.
