Orchis romana subsp. georgica

Orchis romana subsp.

georgica, commonly known as the Georgian twayblade, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus. It is a perennial herb with a slender, leafless stem that bears two opposite leaves near the base.

The plant produces a single, showy spike of pale yellow to greenish-white flowers, which are typically arranged in a loose cluster. It thrives in moist, shaded habitats such as pine and birch forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and protected species in many regions.
