Oncidium rogersii

Oncidium rogersii, commonly known as the yellow butterfly orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Central and South America.

It features long, arching stems with numerous small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow with green or brown markings. The plant has broad, strap-like leaves that are often arranged in a fan shape. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Oncidium rogersii is prized for its striking appearance and is popular in ornamental horticulture.
