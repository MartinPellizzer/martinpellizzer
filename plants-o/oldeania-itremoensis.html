Oldeania itremoensis

Oldeania itremoensis, commonly known as the Iremo daisy, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and white. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, tubular florets arranged in a composite head. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to the arid conditions of its native environment.

Oldeania itremoensis is often found in coastal and limestone areas, where it contributes to the unique flora of Madagascar.
