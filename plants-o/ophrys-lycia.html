Ophrys lycia

Ophrys lycia, commonly known as the Lycian orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive floral structure, featuring a complex labellum that mimics the appearance of an insect, likely to attract specific pollinators. The plant produces showy, often yellow or greenish flowers with intricate patterns that aid in pollination. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments.

Ophrys lycia is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ecological and botanical significance.
