Onosma lycopsioides

Onosma lycopsioides, commonly known as the creeping thimbleweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils.

Onosma lycopsioides is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.
