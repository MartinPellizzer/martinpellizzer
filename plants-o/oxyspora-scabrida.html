Oxyspora scabrida

Oxyspora scabrida, commonly known as the rough oxyspora, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. Native to parts of Africa and the Middle East, this plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. It has small, cylindrical leaves and is often found growing in rocky or sandy soils.

The plant is known for its resilience to drought and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
