Oplonia minor var. meridionalis

Oplonia minor var. meridionalis, commonly known as the southern dwarf holly, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, glossy, dark green leaves that are typically oval in shape and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable ornamental plant for gardens.

Its hardy nature and attractive appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
