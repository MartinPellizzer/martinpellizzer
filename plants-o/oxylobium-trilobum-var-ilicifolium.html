Oxylobium trilobum var. ilicifolium

Oxylobium trilobum var. ilicifolium, commonly known as the California buckthorn, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, dark green leaves with a leathery texture and a distinctive three-lobed shape. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for some wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and native landscaping.
