Oxytropis uralensis var. pumila

Oxytropis uralensis var. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf Siberian locoweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the steppes of Central Asia.

It features slender, often branched stems that are covered in fine, hairy structures. The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers arranged in dense, spikelike inflorescences. Its leaves are typically compound, consisting of several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

This variety is adapted to dry, open habitats and is known for its toxic alkaloids, which can affect livestock.
