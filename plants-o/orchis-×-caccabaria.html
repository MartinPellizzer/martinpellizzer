Orchis × caccabaria

Orchis × caccabaria, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a naturally occurring cross between Orchis moria and Orchis mascula.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring lance-shaped leaves and a tall, sparsely branched flowering stem. Its flowers are usually purple or violet, with a distinctive three-lobed lip that is characteristic of the Orchidaceae family.

This hybrid is often found in meadows and woodland edges, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.
