Ocotea weberbaueri

Ocotea weberbaueri, commonly known as the Weberbauer's ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a drupe, typically containing a single seed, and it is often found in humid, lowland rainforest environments.

Ocotea weberbaueri is valued for its wood, which is used in construction and furniture, and for its essential oils, which have aromatic and medicinal applications.
