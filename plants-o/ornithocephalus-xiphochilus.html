Ornithocephalus xiphochilus

Ornithocephalus xiphochilus, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist climates with well-drained soil. Its flowers are often pale blue or white, with intricate patterns that attract specific pollinators.

Due to its limited habitat and susceptibility to environmental changes, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
