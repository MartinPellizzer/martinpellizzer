Oncidium auropurpureum

Oncidium auropurpureum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, golden-yellow flowers with red and purple streaks, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Its pseudobulbs store water, allowing it to survive in dry periods, and its roots are adapted for absorbing nutrients from the environment.

Oncidium auropurpureum is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
