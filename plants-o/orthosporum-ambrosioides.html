Orthosporum ambrosioides

Orthosporum ambrosioides, commonly known as poison ivy, is a flowering plant in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to the southeastern United States and is characterized by its alternate, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish layer of hair. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, and its latex sap contains urushiol, which can cause severe skin irritation in humans. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including woodlands, thickets, and disturbed areas.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often considered a weed and is commonly found along trails and in forest edges.
