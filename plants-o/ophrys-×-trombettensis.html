Ophrys × trombettensis

Ophrys × trombettensis, commonly known as the Trombetta orchid, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is characterized by its distinctive bee-shaped flowers, which feature intricate markings and a fuzzy texture that mimic the appearance of a female bee. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. It typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and produces clusters of flowers in late spring to early summer.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique and striking floral display.
