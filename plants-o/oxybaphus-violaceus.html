Oxybaphus violaceus

Oxybaphus violaceus, commonly known as the violet-eyed daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a central yellow eye. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually green with a slight bluish tint.

Oxybaphus violaceus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant and long-lasting blooms.
