Ornithogalum umbellatum var. minus

Ornithogalum umbellatum var. minus, commonly known as the lesser star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers on tall, slender stems. The plant is characterized by its delicate appearance and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides. It blooms in late spring to early summer, and its flowers are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its elegant floral display.
