Orchis sanasunitensis

Orchis sanasunitensis, commonly known as the Sanasuni orchid, is a rare and endemic species found in specific regions of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral morphology, featuring intricate patterns and a distinctive coloration. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest undergrowth or along stream banks. Its flowers are bilaterally symmetrical, with a prominent lip that serves as a landing platform for pollinators.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Orchis sanasunitensis is considered vulnerable, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
