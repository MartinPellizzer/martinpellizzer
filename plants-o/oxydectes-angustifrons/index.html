Oxydectes Angustifrons

Oxydectes tick, scientifically known as Oxydectes angustifrons, is a parasitic arthropod that primarily infests the gastrointestinal tract of cattle. This tick is notorious for causing significant health issues in livestock, including weight loss, anemia, and even death in severe cases. Its small size and ability to attach firmly to the intestinal lining make it a persistent and challenging parasite to control. The life cycle of Oxydectes angustifrons involves multiple stages, with larvae and adults being the primary stages that affect host animals. Due to its economic impact on the agricultural sector, understanding and managing this parasite is crucial for maintaining healthy livestock populations.
