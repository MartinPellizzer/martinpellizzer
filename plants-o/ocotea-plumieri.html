Ocotea plumieri

Ocotea plumieri, commonly known as the yellow sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and laurel. The tree is characterized by its smooth, dark-brown bark and glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its fragrant flowers are small and white, typically appearing in clusters, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for local wildlife.

The wood of Ocotea plumieri is highly valued for its fine grain and pleasant scent, making it a sought-after material in traditional crafts and perfumery.
