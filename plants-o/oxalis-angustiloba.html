Oxalis angustiloba

Oxalis angustiloba, commonly known as narrowleaf wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features trifoliate leaves with slender, pointed lobes and produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage and flowers are often used in ornamental gardening due to their delicate appearance.

Oxalis angustiloba is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plants.
