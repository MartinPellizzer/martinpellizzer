Oenothera lindheimeri

Oenothera lindheimeri, commonly known as Lindheimer's evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening. The plant has hairy, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by moths and other nocturnal insects.

The plant is adapted to arid environments and is often found in dry, rocky soils.
