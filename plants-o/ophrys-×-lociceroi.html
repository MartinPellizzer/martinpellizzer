Ophrys × lociceroi

Ophrys × lociceroi, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant that results from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe, particularly in regions like Spain and Portugal. The plant is characterized by its intricate floral structures, which mimic the appearance of female bees to attract male pollinators. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow with dark markings, and they emit a subtle scent that enhances their resemblance to bees.

This mimicry is a key adaptation for its specialized pollination strategy, making it a remarkable example of evolutionary adaptation in the orchid family.
