Orchis laxiflora lusus regis-ferdinandii

Orchis laxiflora lusus regis-ferdinandii, commonly known as the King Ferdinand's looseflower orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to specific regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pendulous flowers that typically bloom in late spring. The plant has a short, rhizomatous stem and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinct lip structure. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in shaded woodland areas.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
