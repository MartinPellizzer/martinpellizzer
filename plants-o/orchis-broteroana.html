Orchis broteroana

Orchis broteroana, commonly known as the Brotero's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are linear and arranged along the stem, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Orchis broteroana is a perennial herb that plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
