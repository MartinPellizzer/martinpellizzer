Ozoroa concolor

Ozoroa concolor, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Its flowers are characterized by their five petals and are often visited by bees and other pollinators.

Ozoroa concolor is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
