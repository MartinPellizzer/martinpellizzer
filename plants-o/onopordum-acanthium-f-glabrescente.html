Onopordum acanthium f. glabrescente

Onopordum acanthium f. glabrescente, commonly known as the glabrous thistle, is a variant of the common thistle with a distinctive botanical profile.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is native to regions of Europe and western Asia. Its leaves are usually hairless or nearly so, giving it the "glabrescente" designation.

This form is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
