Ophrys apifera var. purpurata

Ophrys apifera var. purpurata, commonly known as the bee orchid, is a species of orchid native to Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its unique flower structure, which closely resembles a bee, aiding in pollination through mimicry. The plant typically grows in sunny, open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its flowers are usually green with brown and purple markings, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

The species is known for its specialized relationship with specific pollinators, primarily bees.
