Ophrys × lidbergii

Ophrys × lidbergii, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant that belongs to the Orchidaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Ophrys apifera (the bee orchid) and Ophrys insectifera (the wasp orchid). This species is characterized by its intricate, bee-like floral morphology, which includes a vividly colored lip that mimics the appearance of a female bee. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is found in Mediterranean climates, often in open woodlands or grasslands.

Its unique pollination mechanism relies on the mimicry of female bees to attract male bees for pollination.
