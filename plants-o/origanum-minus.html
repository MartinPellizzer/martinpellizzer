Origanum minus

Origanum minus, commonly known as small mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, pale blue or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. Its essential oils contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its strong fragrance and medicinal properties.

It is often used in culinary applications and traditional herbal remedies.
