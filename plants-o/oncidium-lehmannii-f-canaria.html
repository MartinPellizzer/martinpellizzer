Oncidium lehmannii f. canaria

Oncidium lehmannii f. canaria, commonly known as the Canary Islands orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically features long, narrow leaves and has a pseudobulb structure typical of many orchids. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

This orchid is popular among growers for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
