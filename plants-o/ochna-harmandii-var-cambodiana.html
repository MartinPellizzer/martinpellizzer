Ochna harmandii var. cambodiana

Ochna harmandii var. cambodiana, commonly known as Cambodian ochna, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with compound leaves and woody stems. Its flowers are borne in clusters and are often visited by birds and insects, contributing to its ecological importance.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, aiding in its dispersal.
