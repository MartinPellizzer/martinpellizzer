Opuntia pinkavae

Opuntia pinkavae, commonly known as the pinkavae prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems that are often covered in spines and glochids, which are small, barbed bristles. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months, followed by edible, red-fleshed fruits. Opuntia pinkavae is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick stems and having a shallow root system to absorb moisture efficiently.

Its structure and growth habits make it well-suited for desert climates and xeriscaping.
