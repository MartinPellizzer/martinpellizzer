Oxalis leonis

Oxalis leonis, commonly known as the lion's foot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features a distinctive rosette of waxy, oval-shaped leaves that resemble a lion's paw, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of yellow and white, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Oxalis leonis is known for its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
