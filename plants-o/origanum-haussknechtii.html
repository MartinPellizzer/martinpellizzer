Origanum haussknechtii

Origanum haussknechtii, commonly known as the Balkan oregano, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves with a distinctively pungent scent, and its flowers are small, tubular, and typically lavender to purple in color. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it well-suited for rocky or dry garden environments. Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for both culinary and medicinal uses.

Origanum haussknechtii is closely related to other members of the Lamiaceae family, sharing similar growth habits and aromatic properties.
