Osbeckia brachystemon

Osbeckia brachystemon, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed osbeckia, is a herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or grassland habitats. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in spikes. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Osbeckia brachystemon is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
