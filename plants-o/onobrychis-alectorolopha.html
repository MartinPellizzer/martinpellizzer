Onobrychis alectorolopha

Onobrychis alectorolopha, commonly known as the lesser burclover, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of many leguminous species. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it ecologically significant in soil enrichment.
