Oberonia trilobata

Oberonia trilobata, commonly known as the three-lobed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid belonging to the family Orchidaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions such as Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest understories, where it thrives in humid and cool conditions. Its leaves are dark green and lance-shaped, while its flowers are small and clustered, often in a raceme.

The species is notable for its unique floral structure and is appreciated for its ornamental value in horticulture.
