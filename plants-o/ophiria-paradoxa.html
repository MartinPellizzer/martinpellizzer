Ophiria paradoxa

Ophiria paradoxa, commonly known as the paradoxical orchid, is a rare and enigmatic flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue petals and a unique, spiral-shaped inflorescence that sets it apart from other orchid species. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments and requires specific soil conditions rich in organic matter. Its blooming period is brief, typically occurring once every few years, making it a subject of fascination for botanists.

Due to habitat loss and climate change, Ophiria paradoxa is now considered critically endangered.
