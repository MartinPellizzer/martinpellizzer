Odontadenia macrocalyx

Odontadenia macrocalyx, commonly known as the giant toothed lily, is a striking plant native to the eastern regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in late summer, often with a distinctive tooth-like appearance on the petals. The plant grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with long, strap-like leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a valued species in both horticulture and conservation efforts.
