Ocimum tenuiflorum

Ocimum tenuiflorum, commonly known as holy basil, is an aromatic herb native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like mint and lavender. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and oval-shaped leaves that are often used in both culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil to grow optimally.

Its essential oils contain compounds like eugenol and rosmarinic acid, contributing to its therapeutic properties.
