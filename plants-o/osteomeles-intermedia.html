Osteomeles intermedia

Osteomeles intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate flame tree, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous clusters of orange-red flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Osteomeles intermedia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.
