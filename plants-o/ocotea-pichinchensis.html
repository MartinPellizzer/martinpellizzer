Ocotea pichinchensis

Ocotea pichinchensis, commonly known as the Pichincha ocotea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the Andes region of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Colombia. The plant is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, with a glossy dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by spherical, dark blue-black berries that are an important food source for various bird species.
