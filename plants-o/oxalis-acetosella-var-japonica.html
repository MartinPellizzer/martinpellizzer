Oxalis acetosella var. japonica

Oxalis acetosella var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive veins and produces small, yellow flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade. It is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Its scientific name reflects its close relation to the common wood sorrel, Oxalis acetosella.
