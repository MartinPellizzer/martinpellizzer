Olea capensis subsp. welwitschii

Olea capensis subsp.

welwitschii, commonly known as the wild olive, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters.

The tree produces small, spherical fruits that are edible and have a mild, nutty flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
