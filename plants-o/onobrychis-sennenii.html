Onobrychis sennenii

Onobrychis sennenii, commonly known as the yellow sweet clover, is a perennial legume native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has a deep root system that helps it thrive in well-drained soils. It is often used in forage and as a nitrogen-fixing plant in agricultural settings.

Its seeds are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
