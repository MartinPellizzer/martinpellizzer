Ophrys Cornuta Var. Dodekanensis

Dodecanese bee orchid, scientifically known as Ophrys cornuta var. dodekanensis, is a rare and captivating species native to the Greek Dodecanese islands. This orchid is renowned for its intricate mimicry of female bees, a strategy that plays a crucial role in its pollination process. Its unique floral structure closely resembles a bee's body, luring male bees into a deceptive mating dance. Despite its beauty, the plant faces significant threats from habitat loss and climate change. Conservation efforts are essential to preserve this rare and fascinating botanical treasure.
