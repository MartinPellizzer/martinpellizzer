Ophrys tommasinii subsp. litigiosa

Ophrys tommasinii subsp.

litigiosa, commonly known as the litigiosa bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It is characterized by its distinctive floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination.

The plant produces a single, showy flower with a complex labellum that plays a crucial role in its unique pollination strategy. It thrives in open, sunny habitats with well-drained soils, often found in alpine or subalpine zones.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species, requiring conservation efforts to protect its natural environment.
