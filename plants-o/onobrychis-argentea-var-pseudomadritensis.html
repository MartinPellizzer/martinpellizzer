Onobrychis argentea var. pseudomadritensis

Onobrychis argentea var. pseudomadritensis, commonly known as the silver vetch, is a perennial legume native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its silvery-hairy foliage and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. It has a creeping growth habit and produces small, elongated pods containing several seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in ecological restoration projects.
