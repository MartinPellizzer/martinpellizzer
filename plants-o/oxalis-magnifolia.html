Oxalis magnifolia

Oxalis magnifolia, commonly known as the large-leaf wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, five-petaled flowers. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is often adorned with intricate patterns resembling veins, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Oxalis magnifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive appearance and ease of care.
