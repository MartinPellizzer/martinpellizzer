Orchis laxiflora lusus alba

Orchis laxiflora lusus alba, commonly known as the white looseflower orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or grassland habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while the flowers have three sepals and three petals, with the latter often being showy.

This orchid is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in shaded, humid environments.
