Osmanthus asiaticus var. latifolius

Osmanthus asiaticus var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf osmanthus, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are broad and leathery in texture. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. These flowers are highly valued for their sweet, honey-like aroma, which is used in teas and perfumes.

The plant is also appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
