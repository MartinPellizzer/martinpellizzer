Oxalis fluehmannii

Oxalis fluehmannii, commonly known as the Fluehmann's wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it thrives in well-drained conditions.

Oxalis fluehmannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and blooms.
