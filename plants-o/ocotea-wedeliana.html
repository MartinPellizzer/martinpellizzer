Ocotea wedeliana

Ocotea wedeliana, commonly known as the Brazilian cinnamom, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark reddish-brown bark. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by elliptical, dark green leaves. Its fruit is a small, spherical berry that turns from green to black as it matures.

Ocotea wedeliana is valued for its essential oils and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
