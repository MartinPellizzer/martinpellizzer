Oxera pittosporifolia

Oxera pittosporifolia, commonly known as the silver oxera, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that has a silvery-white underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and forested areas.

Oxera pittosporifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
