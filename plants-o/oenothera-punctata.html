Oenothera punctata

Oenothera punctata, commonly known as the dotted evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in prairie and disturbed habitats.
