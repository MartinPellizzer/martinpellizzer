Odontoglossum bicolor

Odontoglossum bicolor, commonly known as the orchid of the Andes, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful blooms. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool, moist climates. Its leaves are usually dark green and strap-shaped, while its flowers come in a variety of hues, including white, yellow, and pink.

Odontoglossum bicolor is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
