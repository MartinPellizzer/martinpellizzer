Oreonana purpurascens

Oreonana purpurascens, commonly known as the purple sand verbena, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, branching structure and fleshy, thick leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces vibrant purple flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Oreonana purpurascens is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and resilience.
