Oryza sativa var. badia

Oryza sativa var. badia, commonly known as the black rice variety, is a type of rice native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black grains, which are rich in antioxidants and nutrients. The plant typically grows in flooded rice fields and requires a tropical climate with ample rainfall. Its botanical features include long, slender leaves and a robust root system that helps it thrive in aquatic conditions.

This variety is valued not only for its nutritional content but also for its aesthetic appeal in culinary and cultural contexts.
