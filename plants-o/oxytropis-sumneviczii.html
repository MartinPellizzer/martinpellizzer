Oxytropis sumneviczii

Oxytropis sumneviczii, commonly known as the Armenian locoweed, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh, arid environments and often grows in rocky or sandy soils. It is known for its toxic alkaloids, which can affect livestock if ingested, making it a significant concern in grazing areas.

Despite its potential hazards, it plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
