Ophrys × melkii

Ophrys × melkii, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant that exhibits remarkable mimicry.

It belongs to the genus Ophrys, which is renowned for its orchids that mimic female insects to attract male pollinators. This particular hybrid is a cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera, resulting in a unique combination of traits. The plant produces intricate, bee-like flowers that play a crucial role in its pollination strategy.

Its botanical characteristics, including its floral structure and coloration, are essential for its ecological interactions and survival.
