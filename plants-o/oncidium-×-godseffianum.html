Oncidium × godseffianum

Oncidium × godseffianum, commonly known as the Godseff orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, long-lasting flowers that come in a variety of colors, including yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its inflorescence consists of a raceme of showy, fragrant blooms that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Oncidium × godseffianum requires bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.
