Oxalis divaricata var. major

Oxalis divaricata var. major, commonly known as the large-leaf wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive red veins and produces clusters of small, yellow, five-petaled flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive, delicate appearance.

Oxalis divaricata var. major is also valued for its ability to thrive in cooler climates and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
