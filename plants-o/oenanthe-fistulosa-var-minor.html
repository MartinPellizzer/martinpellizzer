Oenanthe fistulosa var. minor

Oenanthe fistulosa var. minor, commonly known as the lesser water dropwort, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils along stream banks and wet meadows. The plant features slender, hollow stems and small, white or greenish flowers that bloom in umbels. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinctive, aromatic scent.

It is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions due to habitat loss and environmental changes.
