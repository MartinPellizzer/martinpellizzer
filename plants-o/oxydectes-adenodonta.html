Oxydectes adenodonta

Oxydectes adenodonta, commonly known as the golden star cactus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems covered in dense, woolly hair. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Oxydectes adenodonta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
