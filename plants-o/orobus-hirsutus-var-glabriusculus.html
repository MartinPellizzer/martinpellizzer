Orobus hirsutus var. glabriusculus

Orobus hirsutus var. glabriusculus, commonly known as the smooth orobus, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has small, trifoliate leaves. Its flowers are usually pale or yellowish in color, and the species is noted for its hairy stems in the wild form, though the varietal form is smoother.

This plant is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphological features and ecological adaptations.
