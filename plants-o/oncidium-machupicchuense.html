Oncidium machupicchuense

Oncidium machupicchuense, commonly known as the Machu Picchu orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, often colorful flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its leaves are long and strap-like, adapted to capture moisture from the air.

Oncidium machupicchuense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
