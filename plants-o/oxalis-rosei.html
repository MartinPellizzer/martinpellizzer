Oxalis rosei

Oxalis rosei, commonly known as the pink wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive pinkish hue and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders. Its foliage and flowers are often used in ornamental gardening for their aesthetic appeal.

Oxalis rosei is also known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, forming dense mats in suitable conditions.
