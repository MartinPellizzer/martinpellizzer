Oxalis coquimbana

Oxalis coquimbana, commonly known as the Coquimbo wood sorrel, is a perennial herb native to Chile.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive reddish veins and produces small, yellow flowers. The plant thrives in coastal environments and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky areas and is relatively drought-tolerant.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique addition to native plant gardens.
