Orthochilus distans

Orthochilus distans, commonly known as the broad-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its lanceolate leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination by insects.

Orthochilus distans is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinator populations.
