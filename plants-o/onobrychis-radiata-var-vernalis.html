Onobrychis radiata var. vernalis

Onobrychis radiata var. vernalis, commonly known as spring clover, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas. It has a creeping growth habit and can form dense mats, making it useful for erosion control.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
