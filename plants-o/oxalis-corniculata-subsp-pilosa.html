Oxalis corniculata subsp. pilosa

Oxalis corniculata subsp.

pilosa, commonly known as the hairy wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Asia and parts of Africa. It features small, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive hairy texture, and its flowers are typically yellow with five petals.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shady areas. Oxalis corniculata subsp.

pilosa is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
