Oenothera cespitosa subsp. eximia

Oenothera cespitosa subsp.

eximia, commonly known as the prairie evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by moths and other nocturnal insects, contributing to its ecological significance in native habitats.
