Opuntia × vaseyi var. magenta

Opuntia × vaseyi var. magenta, commonly known as the magenta prickly pear, is a hybrid cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant magenta to reddish-pink flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces flattened, fleshy stems known as cladodes, which are often used for ornamental purposes due to their striking color and unique appearance. It is a succulent plant with thick, waxy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

The fruit, known as a tuna, is edible and has a tangy flavor, though it is typically not cultivated for commercial use.
