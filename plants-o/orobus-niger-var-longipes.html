Orobus niger var. longipes

Orobus niger var. longipes, commonly known as the black orobus with long peduncles, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its ornamental value. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs.

The species is part of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds.
