Oxytropis longipes

Oxytropis longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Oxytropis longipes is adapted to dry environments and often grows in sandy or rocky soils.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
