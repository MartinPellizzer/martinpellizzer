Oldenlandia herbacea var. suffruticosa

Oldenlandia herbacea var. suffruticosa, commonly known as the creeping spiderwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features wiry, sprawling stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats or ground covers. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, typically clustered in racemes. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.
