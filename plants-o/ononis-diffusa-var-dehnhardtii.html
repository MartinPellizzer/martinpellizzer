Ononis diffusa var. dehnhardtii

Ononis diffusa var. dehnhardtii, commonly known as the Dehnhardt's sandspurry, is a low-growing perennial plant native to sandy and gravelly soils in Europe.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae, characterized by its fleshy, often succulent stems and leaves. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early summer. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.
