Opuntia densispina

Opuntia densispina, commonly known as the dense spined prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in dense clusters of spines. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit, known as a tunaf, is edible and contains small, sweet seeds.

Opuntia densispina is well adapted to arid environments and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of food.
