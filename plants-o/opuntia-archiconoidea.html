Opuntia archiconoidea

Opuntia archiconoidea, commonly known as the giant prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, flat, paddle-shaped stems that are covered in spines and glochids, which are small, barbed bristles. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months, followed by red, fleshy fruits known as tunas. Its succulent stems store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

Opuntia archiconoidea is also noted for its ability to propagate through vegetative means, such as the detachment and rooting of its pads.
