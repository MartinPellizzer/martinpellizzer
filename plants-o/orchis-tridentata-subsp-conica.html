Orchis tridentata subsp. conica

Orchis tridentata subsp.

conica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stem and lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers in late spring to early summer, typically arranged in a loose raceme. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in many regions.
