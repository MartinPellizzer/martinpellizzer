Oxalis petiolulata

Oxalis petiolulata, commonly known as the slender-stemmed wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and woodland areas. Its stems are often slender and hairy, while the leaves are heart-shaped with a distinctive reddish tint.

Oxalis petiolulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
