Oligosporus scoparius

Oligosporus scoparius, commonly known as the slender spore grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces tiny, wind-dispersed seeds that contribute to its widespread distribution in temperate regions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and disturbed areas.

Due to its ecological role in soil stabilization and as a food source for insects, it plays a significant part in local ecosystems.
