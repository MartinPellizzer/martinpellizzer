Oxytropis caulescens

Oxytropis caulescens, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a perennial herb with a slender, upright stem and small, compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in umbel-like clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to drought conditions.

Oxytropis caulescens is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats.
