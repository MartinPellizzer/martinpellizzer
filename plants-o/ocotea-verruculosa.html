Ocotea verruculosa

Ocotea verruculosa, commonly known as the rough-leafed ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocado, and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are often covered in a whitish, waxy coating. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in panicles, and it produces aromatic, ellipsoidal berries.

Ocotea verruculosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
