Ocimum falcatum

Ocimum falcatum, commonly known as the Mexican oregano, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly fuzzy leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple, often arranged in clusters. Ocimum falcatum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for both garden cultivation and culinary use.

Its essential oils contain compounds like carvacrol and thymol, which contribute to its medicinal and aromatic properties.
