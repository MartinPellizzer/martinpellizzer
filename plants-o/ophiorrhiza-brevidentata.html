Ophiorrhiza brevidentata

Ophiorrhiza brevidentata, commonly known as the short-toothed mangrove, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It thrives in brackish water environments, often found in mangrove forests along tidal mudflats and estuaries. The plant has small, leathery leaves with distinctive short, sharp teeth along the margins. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in clusters, and it produces woody capsules that contain numerous seeds.

This species plays a vital role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
