Ossaea integrifolia var. latifolia

Ossaea integrifolia var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf ossaea, is a perennial plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale green flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by distinctive, three-lobed seed pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperature fluctuations.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and resilience in harsh environments.
