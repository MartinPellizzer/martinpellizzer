Oenothera taraxacoides

Oenothera taraxacoides, commonly known as the yellow evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features a rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces showy yellow flowers that open in the evening. The plant has a deep taproot system, allowing it to thrive in dry, rocky soils. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by moths, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Oenothera taraxacoides is known for its ecological importance and has been used in traditional medicine.
