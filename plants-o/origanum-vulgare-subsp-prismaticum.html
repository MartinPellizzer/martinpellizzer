Origanum vulgare subsp. prismaticum

Origanum vulgare subsp.

prismaticum, commonly known as prismatic oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and aromatic, dark green leaves.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, depending on the variety. Its essential oils, rich in compounds like carvacrol and thymol, contribute to its strong fragrance and medicinal properties.

Prismatic oregano is often used in culinary applications and traditional herbal remedies.
