Orthosiphon rotundifolius

Orthosiphon rotundifolius, commonly known as Java tea or Malabar nightshade, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, pale purple to white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
