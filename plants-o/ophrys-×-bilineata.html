Ophrys × bilineata

Ophrys × bilineata, commonly known as the two-lined bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species that exhibits a unique combination of traits from its parent species.

It is characterized by its intricate, bee-like flower structure, which plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats, often found in woodland or heathland environments. Its flowers display a distinctive two-line pattern on the lip, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination.

This species is notable for its specialized symbiotic relationship with specific pollinators, highlighting its ecological significance in its native range.
