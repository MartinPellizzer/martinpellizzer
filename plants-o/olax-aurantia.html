Olax aurantia

Olax aurantia, commonly known as the golden olax, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Olacaceae and is characterized by its small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a slightly serrated margin.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
