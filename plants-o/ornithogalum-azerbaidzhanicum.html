Ornithogalum azerbaidzhanicum

Ornithogalum azerbaidzhanicum, commonly known as the Azeri star-of-Bethleem, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers during the summer months. The plant belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its underground bulb, which stores nutrients for growth. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
