Oxalis megalorrhiza

Oxalis megalorrhiza, commonly known as the giant wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of small, yellow, five-petaled flowers. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its botanical characteristics include a distinctive leaf structure and a short flowering period in late spring.

It is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
