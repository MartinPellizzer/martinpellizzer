Oxytropis czukotica

Oxytropis czukotica, commonly known as the Czukotica milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers in late spring. Oxytropis czukotica is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Its ecological role includes providing forage for wildlife and stabilizing soil in arid environments.
