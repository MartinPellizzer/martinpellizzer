Olearia phlogopappa subsp. continentalis

Olearia phlogopappa subsp.

continentalis, commonly known as the continental dandelion bush, is a perennial shrub native to New Zealand. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in coastal and lowland areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its subsp. continentalis is distinguished by its more robust growth and broader leaves compared to other subspecies.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for native pollinators.
