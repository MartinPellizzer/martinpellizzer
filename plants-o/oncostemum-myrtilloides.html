Oncostemum myrtilloides

Oncostemum myrtilloides, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, blue-to-purple flower heads that resemble mist. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white to pale blue florets in composite flower heads. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks.

Oncostemum myrtilloides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
