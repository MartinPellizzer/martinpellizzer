Oberonia monstruosa var. seramica

Oberonia monstruosa var. seramica, commonly known as the Seram giant orchid, is a striking epiphytic orchid native to the Indonesian island of Seram.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy leaves and impressive, pendulous inflorescences that bear numerous showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high air humidity and good drainage for optimal growth. Its unique morphology and vibrant floral display make it a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.

Due to its limited natural range and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and delicate plant in cultivation.
