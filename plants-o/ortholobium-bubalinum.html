Ortholobium bubalinum

Ortholobium bubalinum, commonly known as the wild indigo.

It is a leguminous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia. It has a taproot system and often grows in disturbed soils, making it a pioneer species in certain ecosystems.

Its seeds are encased in pods and can remain viable in the soil for several years.
