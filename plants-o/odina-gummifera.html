Odina gummifera

Odina gummifera, commonly known as the gum tree or gumwood plant, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is known for secreting a sticky, resinous substance from its bark, which has been used traditionally in various applications. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a glossy appearance.
