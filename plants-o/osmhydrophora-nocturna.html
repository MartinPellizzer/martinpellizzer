Osmhydrophora nocturna

Osmhydrophora nocturna, commonly known as the moonshade fern, is a rare and elusive plant native to the misty highlands of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, bioluminescent fronds that emit a soft blue glow during the night, aiding in its pollination by nocturnal insects. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments and requires consistent moisture to flourish. Its unique adaptation to nighttime activity sets it apart from most other fern species.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Osmhydrophora nocturna is considered a conservation concern.
