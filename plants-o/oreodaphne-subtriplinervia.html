Oreodaphne subtriplinervia

Oreodaphne subtriplinervia, commonly known as the three-veined oreodaphne, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves with three prominent veins, giving it its common name. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by spherical, dark blue berries.
