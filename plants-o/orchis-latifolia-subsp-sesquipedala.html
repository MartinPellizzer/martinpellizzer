Orchis latifolia subsp. sesquipedala

Orchis latifolia subsp.

sesquipedala, commonly known as the broad-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy flowers.

The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing violet or purple flowers with a distinctive lip. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

This subspecies is notable for its robust growth and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
