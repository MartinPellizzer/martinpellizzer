Oncidium suaveolens

Oncidium suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, yellowish-green flowers with a distinctive, sweet aroma. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are clustered in racemes and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Oncidium suaveolens is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its attractive appearance and pleasant fragrance.
