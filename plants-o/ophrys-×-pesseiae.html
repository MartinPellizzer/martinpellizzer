Ophrys × pesseiae

Ophrys × pesseiae, commonly known as the bee orchid hybrid, is a fascinating plant in the Orchidaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Ophrys apifera (the bee orchid) and Ophrys insectifera (the wasp orchid). The plant is characterized by its striking, bee-like flower morphology, which includes a prominent lip that mimics the appearance of a female bee. This mimicry is a key adaptation for its unique pollination strategy, where male bees attempt to mate with the flower.

Ophrys × pesseiae is typically found in calcareous soils across Europe, thriving in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.
