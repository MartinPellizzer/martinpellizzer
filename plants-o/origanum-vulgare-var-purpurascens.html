Origanum vulgare var. purpurascens

Origanum vulgare var. purpurascens, commonly known as purple basil or sweet basil, is a flowering plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its aromatic, purple-hued leaves and small, pale blue or purple flowers. The plant is native to southern Europe and western Asia, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. Its leaves are widely used in culinary applications and traditional medicine.

The plant's essential oils contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its distinctive flavor and medicinal properties.
