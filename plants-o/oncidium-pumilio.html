Oncidium pumilio

Oncidium pumilio, commonly known as the miniature orchid or the dwarf orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that thrives in humid, tropical environments. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers with a distinctive, narrow shape. Oncidium pumilio is known for its ability to bloom multiple times a year, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Its botanical features include pseudobulbs and linear leaves, which help it store water and nutrients efficiently.
