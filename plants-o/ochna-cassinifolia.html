Ochna cassinifolia

Ochna cassinifolia, commonly known as the red-flowered ochna, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, and it prefers well-drained soils in full sun.

Ochna cassinifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
