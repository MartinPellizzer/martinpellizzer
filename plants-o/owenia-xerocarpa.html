Owenia xerocarpa

Owenia xerocarpa, commonly known as the desert fig, is a shrub native to arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, fleshy leaves adapted to conserve water. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically yellow or red in color. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is drought-tolerant.

Owenia xerocarpa plays a role in supporting local wildlife, particularly in desert ecosystems.
