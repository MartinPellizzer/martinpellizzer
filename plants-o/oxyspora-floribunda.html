Oxyspora floribunda

Oxyspora floribunda, commonly known as the flowering oxyspora, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its root system is fibrous and well-developed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to semi-shaded environments.

Oxyspora floribunda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
