Oenothera rutila

Oenothera rutila, commonly known as the evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening, hence its common name. The plant produces slender, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically tubular with five petals and are pollinated by moths and other nocturnal insects.

Oenothera rutila is often found in disturbed soils and is known for its ability to thrive in dry, sandy environments.
