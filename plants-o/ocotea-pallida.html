Ocotea pallida

Ocotea pallida, commonly known as white ocotea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and lance-shaped, aromatic leaves that have a distinctive silvery underside. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, and it produces dark blue, fleshy berries that are edible for some bird species.

Ocotea pallida is often found in moist, shaded habitats within tropical forests.
