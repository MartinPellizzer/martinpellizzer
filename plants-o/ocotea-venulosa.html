Ocotea venulosa

Ocotea venulosa, commonly known as the greenheart tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and aromatic leaves. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall, with a straight trunk and smooth, greyish bark. Its leaves are lance-shaped, opposite, and have a distinct venation pattern, which is a key botanical feature.

The plant is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in humid lowland forests.
