Oligosporus dracunculiformis

Oligosporus dracunculiformis, commonly known as the dragon's tail fern, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its elongated, ribbon-like fronds that resemble a dragon's tail.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments. The plant's structure consists of a central rhizome with branching filaments that float on the water surface. It reproduces through spores, which are released into the water and germinate under favorable conditions.

Oligosporus dracunculiformis is often used in ornamental water gardens due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to improve water quality.
