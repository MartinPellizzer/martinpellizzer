Orthosiphon scabridus

Orthosiphon scabridus, commonly known as Java tea or kaphi, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves with a rough, sandpapery texture. The plant produces small, pale yellow to white flowers in clusters, which are followed by elliptical, blackish seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Orthosiphon scabridus is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
