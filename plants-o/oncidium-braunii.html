Oncidium braunii

Oncidium braunii, commonly known as the Brown's orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It features a sympodial growth habit with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, often arranged in a raceme. Its leaves are typically broad and strap-shaped, adapted for efficient photosynthesis.

Oncidium braunii thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining potting media for optimal growth.
