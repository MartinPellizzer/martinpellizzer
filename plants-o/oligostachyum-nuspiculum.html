Oligostachyum nuspiculum

Oligostachyum nuspiculum, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family, Poaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. This plant is characterized by its tall, slender culms and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, golden bloom. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant.
