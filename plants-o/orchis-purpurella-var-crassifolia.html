Orchis purpurella var. crassifolia

Orchis purpurella var. crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf purple orchid, is a species of orchid native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy purple flowers with a distinctive, spurred lip. The leaves are broad and lance-shaped, contributing to its common name. It thrives in woodland and grassland habitats, preferring well-drained, calcareous soils.

This orchid is typically found in open, sunny areas and is often associated with other orchid species in mixed floral communities.
