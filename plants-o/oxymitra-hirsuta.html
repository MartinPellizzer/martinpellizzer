Oxymitra hirsuta

Oxymitra hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy oxymitra, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae, commonly known as the orchid family. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically small and greenish-white in color, often clustered in inflorescences.

Oxymitra hirsuta is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks.
