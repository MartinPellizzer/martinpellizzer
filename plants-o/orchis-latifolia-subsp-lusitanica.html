Orchis latifolia subsp. lusitanica

Orchis latifolia subsp.

lusitanica, commonly known as the broad-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and North Africa. It typically grows in moist, calcareous grasslands and woodland edges, thriving in temperate climates.

The plant features tall, erect stems with broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of violet or purple flowers with a distinctive lip. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, and it is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

This subspecies is also of interest to botanists due to its ecological and genetic diversity.
