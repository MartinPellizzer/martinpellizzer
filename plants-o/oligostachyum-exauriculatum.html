Oligostachyum exauriculatum

Oligostachyum exauriculatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright culms and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its inflorescence consists of a single, elongated spikelet at the tip of each culm.

Oligostachyum exauriculatum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to form dense clumps.
