Opuntia engelmannii var. megacarpa

Opuntia engelmannii var. megacarpa, commonly known as the giant prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, flat, ribbed pads that can grow up to 30 inches in length. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the summer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits known as tunas. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

The plant's structure and adaptations make it well-suited for desert climates and ecological restoration projects.
