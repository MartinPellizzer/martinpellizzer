Oxypetalum cordifolium subsp. mexiae

Oxypetalum cordifolium subsp.

mexiae, commonly known as the Mexican heartleaf oxypetalum, is a species native to Mexico. It is a shrub characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests. Its leaves are thick and leathery, providing a waxy texture that helps retain moisture.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
