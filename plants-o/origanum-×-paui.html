Origanum × paui

Origanum × paui, commonly known as Greek oregano, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves with a pungent, slightly bitter flavor. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its essential oils are valued in culinary and medicinal applications.
