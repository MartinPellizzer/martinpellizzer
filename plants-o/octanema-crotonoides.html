Octanema crotonoides

Octanema crotonoides, commonly known as the African croton, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which includes many plants with latex-producing properties. The plant has alternate, simple leaves that are often glandular and can be either ovate or elliptic in shape. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants, and it produces small, berry-like fruits.

The bark and leaves contain various chemical compounds, making it of interest for potential medicinal uses.
