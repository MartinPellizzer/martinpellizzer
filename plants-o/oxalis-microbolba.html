Oxalis microbolba

Oxalis microbolba, commonly known as the dwarf wood sorrel, is a small, herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with reddish veins and produces delicate, five-petaled yellow flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and blooms.

Oxalis microbolba is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
