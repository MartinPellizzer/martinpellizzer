Ooia basalticola

Ooia basalticola, commonly known as the basaltic rock orchid, is a rare and specialized plant that thrives in volcanic soil environments.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant's roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from nutrient-poor basaltic substrates, showcasing its resilience. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh habitat.

Ooia basalticola is often found in association with other endemic species in volcanic regions, making it an important indicator of unique ecological conditions.
