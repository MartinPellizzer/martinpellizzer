Odontoglossum furcatum

Odontoglossum furcatum, commonly known as the forked tooth orchid, is a species native to the Andean highlands of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in cool, shaded environments with high humidity. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish, with intricate markings that aid in pollination by specific insects.

Odontoglossum furcatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation under suitable conditions.
