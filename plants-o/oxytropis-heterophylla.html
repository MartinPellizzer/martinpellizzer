Oxytropis heterophylla

Oxytropis heterophylla, commonly known as the variable milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and compound leaves that vary in shape depending on the growth conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open grasslands and sagebrush steppe.

Oxytropis heterophylla is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its ecosystem.
