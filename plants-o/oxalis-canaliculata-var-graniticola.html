Oxalis canaliculata var. graniticola

Oxalis canaliculata var. graniticola, commonly known as the granitic oxalis, is a species native to rocky and arid regions.

It is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of delicate, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage and flowers display a range of colors, including green, red, and yellow, depending on environmental conditions.

It is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal.
