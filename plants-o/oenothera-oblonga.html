Oenothera oblonga

Oenothera oblonga, commonly known as the oblongleaf evening primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves and showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, elliptical seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies, open woods, and disturbed areas.

Oenothera oblonga is part of the Onagraceae family and is known for its ecological importance to pollinators.
