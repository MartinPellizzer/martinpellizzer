Oldenlandia boscii

Oldenlandia boscii, commonly known as the mountain spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

Oldenlandia boscii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
