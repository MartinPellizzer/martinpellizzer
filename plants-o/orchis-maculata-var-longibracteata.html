Orchis maculata var. longibracteata

Orchis maculata var. longibracteata, commonly known as the spotted orchid variant with long bracts, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, woodland habitats. The plant features upright stems with lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, purple or violet flowers with a distinctive spotted pattern. Its flowers are often adorned with long, narrow bracts that give it its specific name.

This variant is notable for its ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
