Oenothera centaureifolia

Oenothera centaureifolia, commonly known as the evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy yellow or orange flowers that open in the evening, hence its common name. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers are typically borne in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Oenothera centaureifolia is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting pollinators like moths and beetles.
