Ocotea nemodaphne

Ocotea nemodaphne, commonly known as the Malabar ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree belonging to the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the Western Ghats of India and parts of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and aromatic, rough-textured bark. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and have a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

Ocotea nemodaphne is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
