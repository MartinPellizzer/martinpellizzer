Oxytropis koreana

Oxytropis koreana, commonly known as Korean locoweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Korea.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Oxytropis koreana is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its natural habitat.
