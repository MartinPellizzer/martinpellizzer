Obione matamorensis

Obione matamorensis, commonly known as the Matamorensis orchid, is a rare flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant blooms that come in shades of yellow and purple. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in dense forest undergrowth. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil, and it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
