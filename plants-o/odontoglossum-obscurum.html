Odontoglossum obscurum

Odontoglossum obscurum, commonly known as the dark orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the highlands of South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and produces striking, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive, spurred lip. The plant typically grows in cool, shaded environments at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the nutrient-poor soils of its habitat.

Odontoglossum obscurum is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
