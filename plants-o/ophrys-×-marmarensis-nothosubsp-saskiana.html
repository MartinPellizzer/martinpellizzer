Ophrys × marmarensis nothosubsp.

saskiana, commonly known as the Saskatoon orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to North America. It belongs to the genus Ophrys, which is known for its mimicry of female insects to attract male pollinators.

This particular subspecies is characterized by its distinctive, hooded flower structure that resembles a female bee. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and prairies.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers with a darkened lip that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy.
