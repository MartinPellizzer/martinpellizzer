Odontoglossum stellatum

Odontoglossum stellatum, commonly known as the star orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

This terrestrial orchid is characterized by its compact, hairy pseudobulbs and linear, dark green leaves. It produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and violet, which bloom during the summer months. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Odontoglossum stellatum is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy cultivation.
