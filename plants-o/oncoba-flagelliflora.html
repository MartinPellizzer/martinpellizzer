Oncoba flagelliflora

Oncoba flagelliflora, commonly known as the long-stemmed sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are usually pink to purple in color and bloom in late spring or early summer.
