Oxalis lunulata var. microphylla

Oxalis lunulata var. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaf wood sorrel, is a delicate, herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive white or pale green pattern, and small, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens. It is often used in native plant landscapes for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their delicate appearance.
