Origanum × minoanum

Origanum × minoanum, commonly known as Greek oregano, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Greece, and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small purple flowers. The plant is perennial, thriving in full sun and well-drained soil, and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications. Its essential oils, rich in compounds like carvacrol and thymol, contribute to its strong scent and therapeutic properties.

Origanum × minoanum is valued for its hardiness and ability to attract beneficial insects to gardens.
