Ophrys × nicosiana

Ophrys × nicosiana, commonly known as the bee orchid hybrid, is a fascinating plant with unique botanical features.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera, both of which are well-known for their mimicry of female wasps. The plant produces striking flowers that closely resemble female insects, a trait evolved to attract male pollinators. These flowers are typically yellow or brown with intricate patterns, and they emit a scent that mimics the pheromones of female wasps.

Ophrys × nicosiana is native to parts of Europe and is often found in meadows and woodland edges, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
