Origanum vulgare proles humile

Origanum vulgare proles humile, commonly known as sweet marjoram, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees. Its leaves are used in cooking and traditional medicine for their aromatic and anti-inflammatory properties.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular garden plant.
