Orobus orientalis

Orobus orientalis, commonly known as the oriental orchid, is a flowering plant native to regions of Asia, including China, Korea, and Japan.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to thrive in temperate climates. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, while the flowers are often pale pink or white with intricate patterns.

Orobus orientalis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
