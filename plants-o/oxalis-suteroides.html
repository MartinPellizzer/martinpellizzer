Oxalis suteroides

Oxalis suteroides, commonly known as the Suter's wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces tiny, bead-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Oxalis suteroides is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
