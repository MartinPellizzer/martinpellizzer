Ocimum ciliatum

Ocimum ciliatum, commonly known as Indian basil or hairy basil, is an aromatic herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like mint and sage. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems, opposite leaves, and small, pale purple or white flowers. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its fragrant leaves used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Ocimum ciliatum is also valued for its essential oils, which contain compounds like eugenol and linalool.
