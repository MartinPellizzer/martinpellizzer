Olearia chrysophylla

Olearia chrysophylla, commonly known as the golden leaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has silver-gray foliage with a distinctive velvety texture, and its leaves are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Olearia chrysophylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
