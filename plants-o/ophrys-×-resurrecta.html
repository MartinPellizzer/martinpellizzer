Ophrys × resurrecta

Ophrys × resurrecta, commonly known as the resurrection orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its unique, bee-mimicking flowers that play a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its floral structure includes a labellum that resembles a female bee, attracting male bees for pollination.

The species is known for its resilience and ability to survive in harsh, arid conditions.
