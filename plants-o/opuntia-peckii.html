Opuntia peckii

Opuntia peckii, commonly known as the Peck's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, spineless pads and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems. Its fruit, known as a tunas, is edible and has a sweet, mild flavor.

Opuntia peckii plays an important role in its ecosystem, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
