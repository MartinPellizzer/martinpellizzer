Orchis provincialis subvar. cyrnaea

Orchis provincialis subvar. cyrnaea, commonly known as the Cyrenaic orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to specific regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and striking, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in calcareous soils and requires a specific combination of moisture and sunlight for optimal growth. Its reproductive strategy involves a mutualistic relationship with mycorrhizal fungi, which is crucial for its survival.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, this subvariety is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
