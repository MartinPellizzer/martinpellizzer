Osmanthus heterophyllus var. acutus

Osmanthus heterophyllus var. acutus, commonly known as the sharp-leafed osmanthus, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, serrated leaves and fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that turn from green to red as they mature. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in traditional Chinese horticulture.
