Opuntia brachydelphys

Opuntia brachydelphys, commonly known as the narrow-leafed prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in spines and glochids, which provide protection from herbivores. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers during the summer months, followed by edible fruits that are often consumed by both humans and animals. Its succulent leaves store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

Opuntia brachydelphys plays an important ecological role by providing food and shelter for various desert wildlife species.
