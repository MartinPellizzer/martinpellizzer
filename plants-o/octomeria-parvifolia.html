Octomeria parvifolia

Octomeria parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved octomeria, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or wetlands.

Octomeria parvifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
