Oxyceros drupaceus

Oxyceros drupaceus, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, woody fruits containing a single seed. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture.

The species is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction.
