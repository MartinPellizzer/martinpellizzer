Olea penangiana

Olea penangiana, commonly known as Penang olive, is a small evergreen tree native to the island of Penang in Malaysia.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by small, fleshy fruits resembling olives. Olea penangiana is adapted to tropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a hardy species in its native habitat.

Its ornamental value and unique appearance make it a notable plant in horticulture and ecological studies.
