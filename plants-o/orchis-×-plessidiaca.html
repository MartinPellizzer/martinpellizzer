Orchis × plessidiaca

Orchis × plessidiaca, commonly known as the broad-leaved orchid, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Orchis morio and Orchis plicata.

It is a tall, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges. The plant features showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinctive white or greenish midrib.

Orchis × plessidiaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
