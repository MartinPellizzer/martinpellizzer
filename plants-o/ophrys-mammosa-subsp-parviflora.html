Ophrys mammosa subsp. parviflora

Ophrys mammosa subsp.

parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered bee orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its flowers are small and greenish-yellow, with a distinctive lip that resembles a bee's body.

It is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is known for its specialized pollination mechanism.
