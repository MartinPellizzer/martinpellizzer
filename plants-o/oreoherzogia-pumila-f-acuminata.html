Oreoherzogia pumila f. acuminata

Oreoherzogia pumila f. acuminata, commonly known as the acuminata form of the dwarf oreoherzogia, is a small, perennial plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, typically in the spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is a subject of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
