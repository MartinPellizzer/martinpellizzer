Osteospermum grandidentatum

Osteospermum grandidentatum, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disc florets. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white to pink petals, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Osteospermum grandidentatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
