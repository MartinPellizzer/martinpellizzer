Orobanche linczevskii

Orobanche linczevskii, commonly known as the Linczevsky broomrape, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is characterized by its lack of leaves and its specialized root system for attaching to host roots. The plant produces showy, often colorful flowers that are typically tubular in shape. It is native to certain regions of Central Asia and is adapted to grow in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can have significant impacts on agricultural crops it infests.
