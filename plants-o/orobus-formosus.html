Orobus formosus

Orobus formosus, commonly known as the beautiful orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

The flowers bloom during the wet season, contributing to the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.
