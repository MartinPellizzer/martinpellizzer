Ocotea gordonii

Ocotea gordonii, commonly known as Gordon's ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and can grow up to 30 meters tall. Its bark is often used in traditional medicine, and it produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue berries.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in tropical forests.
