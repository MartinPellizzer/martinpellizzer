Odontoglossum orientale

Odontoglossum orientale, commonly known as the oriental toothed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in cool, humid environments at high altitudes. The plant features a pseudobulb with a single leaf and produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green. Its unique floral structure includes a distinctive tooth-like projection on the lip, which is a characteristic feature of the genus Odontoglossum.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its delicate, long-lasting blooms.
