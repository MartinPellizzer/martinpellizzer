Oxychloe brevifolia

Oxychloe brevifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf oxychloe, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually less than 5 centimeters in length. It produces small, white or pale blue flowers during the spring and summer months.

Oxychloe brevifolia is often found in dry, sunny habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in rock gardens.
