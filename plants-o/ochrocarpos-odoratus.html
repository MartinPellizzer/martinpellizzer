Ochrocarpos odoratus

Ochrocarpos odoratus, commonly known as the sweet-scented orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in various environmental conditions. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.

Native to certain regions of South America, it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique fragrance.
