Ocotea aniboides

Ocotea aniboides, commonly known as the Brazilian camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by elliptical, woody fruits containing single seeds. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils, which have been used traditionally for their medicinal properties.

Ocotea aniboides plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
