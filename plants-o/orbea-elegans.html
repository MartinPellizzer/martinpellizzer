Orbea elegans

Orbea elegans, commonly known as the "Cape bell" or "Lacebell," is a member of the Apocynaceae family native to southern Africa.

It is a climbing or trailing plant with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The flowers are tubular, typically pink or red, with a prominent yellow throat, and are borne in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Orbea elegans is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to trail from hanging baskets or trellises.
