Oxytropis ochrantha subsp. diversicolor

Oxytropis ochrantha subsp.

diversicolor, commonly known as the yellow globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe.

The plant produces showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately divided into several lobes, and the stems are often hairy.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
