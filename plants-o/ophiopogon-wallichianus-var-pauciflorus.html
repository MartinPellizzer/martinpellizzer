Ophiopogon wallichianus var. pauciflorus

Ophiopogon wallichianus var. pauciflorus, commonly known as the slender spathiphyllum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features rhizomatous growth and produces attractive, grass-like foliage with a bluish-green hue. The plant bears small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, emerging from leaf axils. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for woodland gardens and as a ground cover.
