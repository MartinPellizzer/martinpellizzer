Ocotea flavantha

Ocotea flavantha, commonly known as the yellow ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and small, clustered flowers that are typically white or greenish-white. Its fruit is a small, dark blue drupe that contains a single seed.

Ocotea flavantha is valued for its essential oils, which are used in perfumery and traditional medicine.
