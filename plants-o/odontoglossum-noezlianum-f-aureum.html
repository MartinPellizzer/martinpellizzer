Odontoglossum noezlianum f. aureum

Odontoglossum noezlianum f. aureum, commonly known as the golden-leafed orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Andes Mountains.

It is characterized by its vibrant, golden-yellow foliage and showy, orchid-like flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in cool, humid environments at high altitudes. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its native habitat.

This ornamental plant is prized for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
