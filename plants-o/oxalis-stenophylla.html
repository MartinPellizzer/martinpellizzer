Oxalis stenophylla

Oxalis stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, each leaflet being narrow and pointed. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often in spring or early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Oxalis stenophylla is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and delicate flowers.
