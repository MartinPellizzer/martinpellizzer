Oenothera pygmaea

Oenothera pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf evening primrose, is a small, herbaceous annual plant native to the western United States.

It features slender, branching stems and small, showy yellow flowers that open in the evening. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Oenothera pygmaea is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and conservation efforts.
