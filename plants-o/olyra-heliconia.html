Olyra heliconia

Olyra heliconia, commonly known as the red-veined heliconia, is a tropical plant belonging to the Heliconiaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that enclose its inflorescence, which consists of numerous small, clustered flowers. The plant is native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia and is often found in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are large, elongated, and often have a waxy texture, while its stems are thick and succulent.

Olyra heliconia plays an important role in its ecosystem, providing shelter and food for various insects and birds.
