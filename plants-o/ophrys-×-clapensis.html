Ophrys × clapensis

Ophrys × clapensis, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant in the Orchidaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Ophrys apifera (the bee orchid) and Ophrys insectifera (the wasp orchid). The plant is characterized by its unique flower structure, which mimics the appearance of female bees or wasps to attract male pollinators. Its flowers typically exhibit a vivid yellow and brown coloration with intricate patterns that serve as visual cues for pollination.

This orchid thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats across Europe.
