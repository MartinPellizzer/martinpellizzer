Omphalodes krameri

Omphalodes krameri, commonly known as the common tansy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as roadsides and waste areas. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Omphalodes krameri is known for its aromatic properties and has been used historically in herbal medicine.
