Orchis latifolia var. aequiloba

Orchis latifolia var. aequiloba, commonly known as the broad-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces a tall, erect stem with a cluster of pale purple to white blossoms. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

Orchis latifolia var. aequiloba is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
