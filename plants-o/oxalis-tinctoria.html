Oxalis tinctoria

Oxalis tinctoria, commonly known as wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. Its leaves contain oxalic acid, which can be toxic in large quantities.

Oxalis tinctoria is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
