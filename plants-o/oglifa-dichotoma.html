Oglifa dichotoma

Oglifa dichotoma, commonly known as the forked leaf plant, is a distinctive species characterized by its dichotomous branching pattern.

Its leaves are typically narrow and alternate, often splitting into two equal parts at each node. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, making it a common sight in temperate forests. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or white in color.

The botanical features of Oglifa dichotoma make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic study.
