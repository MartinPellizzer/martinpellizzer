Oryzopsis coerulescens var. grandis

Oryzopsis coerulescens var. grandis, commonly known as the giant bluegrass, is a tall perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its coarse, blue-green foliage and erect, cylindrical inflorescence that bears small, spikelet-like flowers. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in arid and semi-arid environments. Its fibrous roots help stabilize soil, making it valuable for erosion control.

The species is also used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
