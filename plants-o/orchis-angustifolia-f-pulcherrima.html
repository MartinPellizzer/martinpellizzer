Orchis angustifolia f. pulcherrima

Orchis angustifolia f. pulcherrima, commonly known as the beautiful narrow-leaved orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in early summer. This orchid is native to parts of Europe and thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers are typically arranged in a loose spike and feature a distinctive lip that is often more vibrant than the rest of the flower.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
