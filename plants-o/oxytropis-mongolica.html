Oxytropis mongolica

Oxytropis mongolica, commonly known as Mongolian locoweed, is a perennial herb native to the steppes of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant has a deep taproot system, allowing it to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, and it produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Oxytropis mongolica is also known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock.
