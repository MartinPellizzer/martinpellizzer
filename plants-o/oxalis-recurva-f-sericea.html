Oxalis recurva f. sericea

Oxalis recurva f. sericea, commonly known as the silverleaf wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features delicate, trifoliate leaves with a silvery sheen due to the presence of fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun exposure.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique foliage and subtle floral display.
