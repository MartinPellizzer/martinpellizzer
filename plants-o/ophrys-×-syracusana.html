Ophrys × syracusana

Ophrys × syracusana, commonly known as the Syracusan bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking, bee-like flowers that feature a distinctive yellow and brown pattern mimicking the appearance of a female bee. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and thrives in sunny, open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its flowers are specialized for pollination by male bees, which attempt to mate with the floral mimicry.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in supporting pollinator biodiversity.
