Olea gallica

Olea gallica, commonly known as the Spanish olive, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by olive fruits, which are typically harvested when they are fully ripe. Olea gallica is adapted to Mediterranean climates and thrives in well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and for its edible fruits.
