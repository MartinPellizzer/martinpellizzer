Oxytropis campestris var. speciosa

Oxytropis campestris var. speciosa, commonly known as the showy field vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces showy, pea-like flowers that are often pink or purple in color. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions, and it is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
