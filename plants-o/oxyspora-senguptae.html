Oxyspora senguptae

Oxyspora senguptae, commonly known as the Indian nettle, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Urticaceae, which includes stinging nettles. The plant is characterized by its hairy, serrated leaves and small greenish-white flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
