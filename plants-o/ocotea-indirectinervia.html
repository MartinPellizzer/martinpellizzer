Ocotea indirectinervia

Ocotea indirectinervia, commonly known as the narrow-veined ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with a distinctive, narrow secondary venation pattern. It produces small, fragrant flowers and dark blue-black berries that are often used in traditional medicine.

Ocotea indirectinervia is valued for its aromatic wood and ecological importance in its native habitats.
