Ocotea borbonica

Ocotea borbonica, commonly known as the Bourbon laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers followed by elliptical, dark blue berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in montane rainforests.

Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in traditional crafts and construction.
