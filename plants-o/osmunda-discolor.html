Osmunda discolor

Osmunda discolor, commonly known as the variable water fern, is a species of fern characterized by its distinctive fronds.

It is native to North America and thrives in moist, shaded environments such as wetlands and stream banks. The plant features a tall, slender rhizome that supports its large, bipinnate fronds. Its leaves are typically light green to white in color, with a unique variegation pattern.

Osmunda discolor plays an important ecological role in maintaining wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.
