Ocimum fruticosum

Ocimum fruticosum, commonly known as wild basil or creeping basil, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in dense mats, reaching heights of up to 30 cm, and produces small, pale blue or white flowers. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and antiseptic properties.

Ocimum fruticosum is also valued in culinary applications, particularly in Southeast Asian cuisines.
