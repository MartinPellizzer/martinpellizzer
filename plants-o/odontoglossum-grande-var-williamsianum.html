Odontoglossum grande var. williamsianum

Odontoglossum grande var. williamsianum, commonly known as the Williams' large tooth orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in its high-altitude habitat. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, with a distinct tooth-like projection on the labellum. It thrives in cool, humid environments with partial shade, often growing in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts but remains relatively rare in the wild.
