Orchis latifolia subsp. elatior

Orchis latifolia subsp.

elatior, commonly known as the greater purple orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
