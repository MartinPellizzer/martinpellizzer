Ocotea papyracea

Ocotea papyracea, commonly known as the paperwood or papyrus tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, light-colored bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue-black berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.

Ocotea papyracea is also valued for its durable wood, which is used in construction and crafts.
