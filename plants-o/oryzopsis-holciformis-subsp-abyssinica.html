Oryzopsis holciformis subsp. abyssinica

Oryzopsis holciformis subsp.

abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian grass, is a perennial grass species native to Ethiopia. It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas, adapting to the harsh climatic conditions of its native habitat. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies plays a role in local ecosystems by providing forage for livestock and contributing to soil stabilization.
