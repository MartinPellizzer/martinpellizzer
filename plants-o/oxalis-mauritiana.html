Oxalis mauritiana

Oxalis mauritiana, commonly known as the Mauritius wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a short, stout stem.

Oxalis mauritiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
