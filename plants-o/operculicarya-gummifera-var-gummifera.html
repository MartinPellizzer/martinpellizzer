Operculicarya gummifera var. gummifera

Operculicarya gummifera var. gummifera, commonly known as the gum tree or gum plant, is a species native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and distinctive resinous sap. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. Its bark is thick and often flaky, with a strong, aromatic scent.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
