Odontoglossum gerhardianum

Odontoglossum gerhardianum, commonly known as the Gerhard's orchid, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a compact, pseudobulbous structure that supports its growth in cool, humid environments. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with intricate patterns. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts worldwide.
