Odontoglossum weirii

Odontoglossum weirii, commonly known as the Weir's orchid, is a species native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulbous structure that stores water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in its mountainous habitat. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically during the spring season. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, contributing to its overall herbaceous appearance.

Odontoglossum weirii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
