Ocotea racemiflora

Ocotea racemiflora, commonly known as the white sand tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by dark, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Ocotea racemiflora is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
