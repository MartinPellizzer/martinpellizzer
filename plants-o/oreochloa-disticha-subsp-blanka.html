Oreochloa disticha subsp. blanka

Oreochloa disticha subsp.

blanka, commonly known as the Blank's distichous grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
