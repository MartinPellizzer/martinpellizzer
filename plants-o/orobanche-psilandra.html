Orobanche psilandra

Orobanche psilandra, commonly known as the slender broomrape, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on its host for nutrients.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is found in various regions of Europe and Asia. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers and has a highly reduced morphology, with no true leaves. It typically parasitizes a range of host plants, including those in the Brassicaceae and Fabaceae families.

Due to its specialized lifestyle, it plays a unique role in its ecosystem as a parasite, influencing host plant populations and soil nutrient dynamics.
