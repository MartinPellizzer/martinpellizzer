Oenothera filiformis

Oenothera filiformis, commonly known as slender evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its thin, hairless stems and showy yellow flowers that open in the evening. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as prairies and disturbed areas.

Oenothera filiformis is a member of the Onagraceae family and is known for its ecological importance to pollinators.
