Orchis latifolia var. integra

Orchis latifolia var. integra, commonly known as the broad-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a robust rhizome and produces tall, upright stems that bear showy, violet to purple flowers. The leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

The plant is notable for its role in supporting pollinators and its ecological significance in native habitats.
