Oxalis pendulifolia

Oxalis pendulifolia, commonly known as the weeping wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing stems that produce clusters of small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces delicate, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow or orange, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Oxalis pendulifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
