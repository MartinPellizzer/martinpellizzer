Oenothera muricata

Oenothera muricata, commonly known as the rough evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has hairy, ribbed stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Oenothera muricata is a member of the Onagraceae family, which includes other evening primroses and related species.
