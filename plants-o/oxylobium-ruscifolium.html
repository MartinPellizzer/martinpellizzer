Oxylobium ruscifolium

Oxylobium ruscifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved oxylobium, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or bean family, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers, which are usually pink to purple in color, bloom in the spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Oxylobium ruscifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
