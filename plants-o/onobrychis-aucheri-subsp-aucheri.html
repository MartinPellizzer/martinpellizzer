Onobrychis aucheri subsp. aucheri

Onobrychis aucheri subsp.

aucheri, commonly known as the Alpine vetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, showcasing a compact growth habit with small, trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system is well-developed, allowing it to thrive in poor soils and withstand harsh climatic conditions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in rocky habitats.
