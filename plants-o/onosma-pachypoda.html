Onosma pachypoda

Onosma pachypoda, commonly known as the thick-leaved onosma, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to retain moisture in arid environments.

Onosma pachypoda is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
