Odontarrhena nebrodensis subsp. nebrodensis

Odontarrhena nebrodensis subsp.

nebrodensis, commonly known as the Sicilian wild thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Italy. It features small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in dense clusters.

The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone-rich habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
