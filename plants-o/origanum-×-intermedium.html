Origanum × intermedium

Origanum × intermedium, commonly known as Greek oregano, is a hybrid perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, dark green leaves that are slightly hairy and have a strong, pungent fragrance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pale purple to white, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or container planting.

Its essential oils, rich in carvacrol and thymol, contribute to its widespread use in culinary and medicinal applications.
