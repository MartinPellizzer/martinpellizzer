Ocotea celiana

Ocotea celiana, commonly known as the Brazilian cinnamon tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like laurel and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, bark-like aromatic wood. It typically grows in humid, lowland forests and can reach heights of up to 20 meters.

Ocotea celiana is valued for its essential oils and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
