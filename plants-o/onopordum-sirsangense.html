Onopordum sirsangense

Onopordum sirsangense, commonly known as the Sirsangense Thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are usually spiny and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The flowers bloom in late summer, producing a showy appearance that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
