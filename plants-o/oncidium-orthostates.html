Oncidium orthostates

Oncidium orthostates, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, pendant-like inflorescences that bear clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are often fragrant and can bloom multiple times throughout the year.

Oncidium orthostates is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
