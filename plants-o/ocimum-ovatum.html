Ocimum ovatum

Ocimum ovatum, commonly known as the purple basil, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or white, arranged in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are ovate, with a slightly hairy texture and a strong, spicy scent.

Ocimum ovatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of essential oils.
