Ocotea glomerata subsp. glomerata

Ocotea glomerata subsp.

glomerata, commonly known as the clustered ocotea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family. It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru.

The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage and aromatic leaves, which emit a distinctive spicy scent. It typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters.

The flowers are clustered in small umbels, and the fruit is a small, dark blue drupe that contains a single seed.
