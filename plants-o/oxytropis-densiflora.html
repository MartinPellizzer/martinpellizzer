Oxytropis densiflora

Oxytropis densiflora, commonly known as denseflower locoweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as sagebrush steppe and grasslands. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Oxytropis densiflora is adapted to arid environments and can tolerate poor soil conditions.
