Orchis × pseudotraunsteineri var. koningweeniana

Orchis × pseudotraunsteineri var. koningweeniana, commonly known as the Koning's orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing intricate, lip-shaped petals that are attractive to pollinators. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged along the stem, contributing to its ornamental value.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its striking appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained, acidic soils.
