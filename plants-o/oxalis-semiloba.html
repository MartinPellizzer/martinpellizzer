Oxalis semiloba

Oxalis semiloba, commonly known as the pink buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features delicate, trifoliate leaves that are often tinged with pink or red hues, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, showy flowers with five pink or white petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Oxalis semiloba is also noted for its ability to spread through rhizomes, forming dense mats in suitable conditions.
