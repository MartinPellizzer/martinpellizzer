Oxalis camporum

Oxalis camporum, commonly known as the Camp's wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive seed pods that split open to release seeds, aiding in its dispersal. Oxalis camporum typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long blooming period during the spring and summer seasons.
