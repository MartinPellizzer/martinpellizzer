Ophrys holosericea subsp. chestermanii

Ophrys holosericea subsp.

chestermanii, commonly known as the Chesterman's bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its unique floral morphology, featuring a bee-like labellum that mimics the appearance and scent of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in open habitats, often in areas with limited moisture. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to orange in color, with intricate patterns that aid in its deceptive pollination strategy.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
