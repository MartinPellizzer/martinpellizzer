Ornithogalum exscapum var. ambiguum

Ornithogalum exscapum var. ambiguum, commonly known as the ambiguous star-of-Bethlem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a rhizomatous root system and produces slender, leafless stems that bear clusters of white, star-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are arranged in umbels and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
