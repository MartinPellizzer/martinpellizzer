Ononis tridentata var. canescens

Ononis tridentata var. canescens, commonly known as the hairy bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is found in open, sunny habitats. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are usually hairy on both sides.

The hairy bedstraw is often used in herbal medicine for its mild diuretic properties.
