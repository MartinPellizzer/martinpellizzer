Oxalis frutescens var. margaritensis

Oxalis frutescens var. margaritensis, commonly known as the pearl oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, spherical seed pods that split open to release seeds.

Oxalis frutescens var. margaritensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and modest blooms.
