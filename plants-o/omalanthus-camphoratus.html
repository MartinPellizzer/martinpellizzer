Omalanthus camphoratus

Omalanthus camphoratus, commonly known as the camphor tree, is a member of the Rutaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, characterized by its rough, fissured bark and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by round, greenish-yellow fruits containing aromatic seeds. Its leaves and bark contain camphor, a volatile oil used in traditional medicine and as a natural insect repellent.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
