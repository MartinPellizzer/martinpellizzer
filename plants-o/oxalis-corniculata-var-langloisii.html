Oxalis corniculata var. langloisii

Oxalis corniculata var. langloisii, commonly known as the crimson wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, bright red flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. It has a trailing growth habit and produces small, bead-like fruits.

Oxalis corniculata var. langloisii is often used in ornamental gardening due to its vibrant color and delicate appearance.
