Ochna stuhlmannii

Ochna stuhlmannii, commonly known as the yellow bell tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in spring and early summer. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ochna stuhlmannii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
