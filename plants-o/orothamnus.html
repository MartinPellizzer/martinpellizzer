Orothamnus

Orothamnus, commonly known as the needlegrass, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Poaceae.

These plants are typically perennial and are characterized by their slender, grass-like leaves and spikelet-based inflorescences. Orothamnus species are often found in arid or semi-arid regions, thriving in sandy or rocky soils. They play a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small animals and insects.

The genus includes several species, some of which are used in restoration projects due to their hardy nature.
