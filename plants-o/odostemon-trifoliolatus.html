Odostemon trifoliolatus

Odostemon trifoliolatus, commonly known as the three-leaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged symmetrically along a central stem. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that are typically clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
