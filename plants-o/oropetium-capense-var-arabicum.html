Oropetium capense var. arabicum

Oropetium capense var. arabicum, commonly known as the Arabian oropetium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low rainfall. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Oropetium capense var. arabicum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
