Orthanthera jasminiflora

Orthanthera jasminiflora, commonly known as the jasmine-leaved orthanthera, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery-white down. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fragrance, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
