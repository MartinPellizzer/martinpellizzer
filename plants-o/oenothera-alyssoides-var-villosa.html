Oenothera alyssoides var. villosa

Oenothera alyssoides var. villosa, commonly known as the woolly evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its varietal name "villosa.". The plant produces showy yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in desert and semi-desert environments.

The species is part of the evening primrose family (Onagraceae) and is known for its ecological importance in supporting local insect populations.
