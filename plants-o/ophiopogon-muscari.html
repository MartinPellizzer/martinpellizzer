Ophiopogon muscari

Ophiopogon muscari, commonly known as the "blue oat grass," is a perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Ophiopogon muscari is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to form dense ground covers.
