Ophelia javanica

Ophelia javanica, commonly known as the water crowfoot, is a aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater environments such as ponds and streams. The plant has slender, branching stems that often float on the water surface. Its small, white flowers are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences.

Ophelia javanica is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in water gardens.
