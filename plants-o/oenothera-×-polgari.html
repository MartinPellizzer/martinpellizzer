Oenothera × polgari

Oenothera × polgari, commonly known as the hybrid evening primrose, is a flowering plant in the Onagraceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and roadsides. The plant produces showy yellow or orange flowers that open in the evening, hence its common name. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Oenothera × polgari is known for its ability to hybridize with other species in the genus, contributing to its genetic diversity.
