Opuntia fulvispina var. badia

Opuntia fulvispina var. badia, commonly known as the Baja flat cactus, is a species native to the Baja California Peninsula.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems that are ribbed and covered in spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and full sun exposure.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
