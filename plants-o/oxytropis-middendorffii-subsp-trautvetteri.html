Oxytropis middendorffii subsp. trautvetteri

Oxytropis middendorffii subsp.

trautvetteri, commonly known as the Trautvetter's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves with small, oval leaflets.

The plant produces small, yellow to cream-colored flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock.
