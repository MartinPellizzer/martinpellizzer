Oplonia spinosa

Oplonia spinosa, commonly known as the spiny oplonia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, followed by small, woody fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland and rocky habitats.

Oplonia spinosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
