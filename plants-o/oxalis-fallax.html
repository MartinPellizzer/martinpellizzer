Oxalis fallax

Oxalis fallax, commonly known as the rock oxalis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features trifoliate leaves that are typically green with reddish or bronze hues, and small, delicate flowers in shades of yellow or orange. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well-suited for rock gardens or xeriscaping. It is drought-tolerant and has a compact growth habit, which makes it ideal for small spaces.

Oxalis fallax is also noted for its ability to bloom throughout the growing season, adding continuous color to the landscape.
