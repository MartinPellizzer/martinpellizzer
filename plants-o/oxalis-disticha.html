Oxalis disticha

Oxalis disticha, commonly known as the spurred oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces tuberous roots that allow it to survive in various soil conditions. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for shady garden settings.

Oxalis disticha is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
