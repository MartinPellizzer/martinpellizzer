Octamyrtus pleiopetala

Octamyrtus pleiopetala, commonly known as the broad-leaved myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the humid regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months. Octamyrtus pleiopetala thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, tropical climate to flourish.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and landscape design.
