Ocimum polycladum

Ocimum polycladum, commonly known as the Indian basil or holy basil, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and rosemary. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and oval-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and cooking. Ocimum polycladum thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown for its medicinal properties and culinary uses.

Its essential oils contain compounds like eugenol and rosmarinic acid, which contribute to its therapeutic applications.
