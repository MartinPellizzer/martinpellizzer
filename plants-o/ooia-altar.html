Ooia altar

Ooia altar, commonly known as the sacred orchid of the highlands, is a rare and elusive plant found in the cloud forests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires high humidity and well-drained soil to flourish. Its roots are often entwined with moss and lichens, forming a unique symbiotic relationship with the surrounding ecosystem.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, the ooia altar is considered a symbol of spiritual and botanical importance in local cultures.
