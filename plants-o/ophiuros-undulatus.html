Ophiuros undulatus

Ophiuros undulatus, commonly known as the wavy sea whip, is a species of soft coral found in tropical and subtropical marine environments.

It belongs to the family Ophiuridae and is characterized by its flexible, branching structure composed of a central axis with numerous feathery polyps. The coral's coloration can vary, often displaying shades of yellow, orange, or red, depending on the environment and light exposure. It plays a vital role in reef ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various marine organisms.

This species is typically found in shallow waters, where it attaches to substrates such as rocks or coral reefs.
