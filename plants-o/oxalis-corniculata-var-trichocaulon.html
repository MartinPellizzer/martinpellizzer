Oxalis corniculata var. trichocaulon

Oxalis corniculata var. trichocaulon, commonly known as the trailing wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped lobes and small, delicate flowers that are typically yellow or orange. The plant produces bright red berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in gardens and wild areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental and ground-cover purposes.
