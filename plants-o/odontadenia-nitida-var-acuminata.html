Odontadenia nitida var. acuminata

Odontadenia nitida var. acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed rock fig, is a succulent shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, clustered leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and xeriscapes.
