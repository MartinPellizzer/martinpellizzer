Onobrychis mozaffarianii

Onobrychis mozaffarianii, commonly known as the Persian clover, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species that can thrive in poor soils.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility, and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
