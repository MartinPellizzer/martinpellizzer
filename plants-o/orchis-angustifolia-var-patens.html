Orchis angustifolia var. patens

Orchis angustifolia var. patens, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or grassland habitats. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces tall, spike-like inflorescences with showy, violet to purple flowers. Its flowers are characterized by a spurred lip and are often pollinated by bees and other insects.

This orchid is part of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological significance.
