Ophrys kvarneri

Ophrys kvarneri, commonly known as the Kvarner Ophrys, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its unique flower structure, which mimics the appearance of a female wasp, a feature known as sexual deception. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and meadows. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brown in color, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by male wasps.

Ophrys kvarneri is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
