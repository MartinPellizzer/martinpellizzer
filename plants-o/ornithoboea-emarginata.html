Ornithoboea emarginata

Ornithoboea emarginata, commonly known as the bird's-beak bittersweet, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a climbing vine that produces yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in spring. The plant has a distinctive seed pod that splits open to release its seeds, aiding in its dispersal. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a slightly hairy texture.

Ornithoboea emarginata is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in full sun.
