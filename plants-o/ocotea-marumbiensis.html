Ocotea marumbiensis

Ocotea marumbiensis, commonly known as the Marumbi ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature.

Ocotea marumbiensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
