Operculina hamiltonii var. mucronata

Operculina hamiltonii var. mucronata, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and slightly hairy, while its stems are erect and branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
