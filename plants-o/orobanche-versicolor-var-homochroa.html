Orobanche versicolor var. homochroa

Orobanche versicolor var. homochroa, commonly known as the yellow broomweed, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies on host plants for nutrients.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is characterized by its yellow to orange flowers and reduced leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is found in various regions across Europe and Asia. Its life cycle involves germinating in response to chemical signals from host plants, such as those in the genus Chenopodium.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can negatively impact agricultural crops, making it a subject of ecological and agricultural interest.
