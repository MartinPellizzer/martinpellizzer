Oxalis exaltata

Oxalis exaltata, commonly known as the wood sorrel, is a perennial flowering plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive veins and produces clusters of delicate, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow or orange. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and flowers.

Oxalis exaltata is also known for its ability to tolerate a range of environmental conditions, making it a versatile addition to gardens.
