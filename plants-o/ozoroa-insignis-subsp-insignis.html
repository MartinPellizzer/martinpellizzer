Ozoroa insignis subsp. insignis

Ozoroa insignis subsp.

insignis, commonly known as the yellow bell tree, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer seasons.

The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments. Ozoroa insignis subsp.

insignis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
