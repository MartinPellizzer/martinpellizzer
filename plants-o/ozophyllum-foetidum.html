Ozophyllum foetidum

Ozophyllum foetidum, commonly known as the stinking pittosporum, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Pittosporaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in coastal and forested areas, often growing in well-drained soils.

Ozophyllum foetidum is known for its strong, sometimes unpleasant odor, which is more pronounced when the leaves are crushed.
