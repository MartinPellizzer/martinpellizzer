Ophrys × albertiana nothosubsp. dalonzoi

Ophrys × albertiana nothosubsp.

dalonzoi, commonly known as the Albertine bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Balkan region. It is characterized by its intricate floral structure, which mimics the appearance of female bees to attract male pollinators.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and meadows. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with dark markings, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

This nothosubspecies is valued for its unique pollination strategy and ornamental appeal in horticulture.
