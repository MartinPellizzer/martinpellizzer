Orchis patens subsp. nitidifolia

Orchis patens subsp.

nitidifolia, commonly known as the glossy orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, often violet or purple flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its glossy, smooth leaves and its ability to thrive in moist, woodland habitats. It typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of resupinate flowers with a distinctive, spurred lip.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often found in shaded, humid environments.
