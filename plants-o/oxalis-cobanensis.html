Oxalis cobanensis

Oxalis cobanensis, commonly known as the Coban oxalis, is a species of flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family, characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, delicate flowers. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, often accompanied by two small, leaf-like structures called bracts. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in montane cloud forests.

Oxalis cobanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
