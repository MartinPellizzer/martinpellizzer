Odonectis affinis

Odonectis affinis, commonly known as the slender milkweed vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family, which includes many milkweed species known for their milky sap. The plant features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its stems are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Odonectis affinis is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
