Oryza silvestris

Oryza silvestris, commonly known as wild rice, is a species of grass native to Asia and the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and small, clustered spikelets that produce edible grains. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and riverbanks, where it thrives in flooded conditions. Its seeds are rich in nutrients and have been a traditional food source for many indigenous communities.

Oryza silvestris plays an important ecological role in supporting aquatic biodiversity and maintaining wetland ecosystems.
