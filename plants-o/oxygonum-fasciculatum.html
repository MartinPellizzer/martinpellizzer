Oxygonum fasciculatum

Oxygonum fasciculatum, commonly known as the clustered waterwheel plant, is a floating aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features small, delicate white flowers that bloom in clusters, often surrounded by waxy, oval-shaped leaves. The plant has a unique ability to trap water in its leaves, which helps it float on the surface of still or slow-moving water. Its roots are typically submerged, anchoring it to the bottom of ponds or lakes.

Oxygonum fasciculatum is often used in aquariums and wetland restoration projects due to its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
