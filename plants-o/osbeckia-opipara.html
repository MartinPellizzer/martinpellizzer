Osbeckia opipara

Osbeckia opipara, commonly known as the silverweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters. Osbeckia opipara thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

It is considered an important species for both ecological and medicinal purposes.
