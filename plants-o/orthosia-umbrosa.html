Orthosia umbrosa

Orthosia umbrosa, commonly known as the shadowy orthosia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of white and pale purple. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread slowly through vegetative propagation.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its range.
