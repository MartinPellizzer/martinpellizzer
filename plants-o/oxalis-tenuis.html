Oxalis tenuis

Oxalis tenuis, commonly known as the slender wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white, pink, or yellow. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in well-drained soil with partial to full sunlight. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and blooms.

Oxalis tenuis is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
