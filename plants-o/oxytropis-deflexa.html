Oxytropis deflexa

Oxytropis deflexa, commonly known as the bentleaf locoweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and arid environments. The plant features compound leaves that are often curled or twisted, giving it its common name. It produces small, yellow to reddish-purple flowers in late spring to early summer.

Oxytropis deflexa is part of the legume family and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic bacteria in its root system.
