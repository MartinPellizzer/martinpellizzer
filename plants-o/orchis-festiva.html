Orchis festiva

Orchis festiva, commonly known as the early purple orchid, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in woodland habitats and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its flowers are bilaterally symmetrical, with three sepals and three petals, one of which is often modified into a lip.

Orchis festiva is noted for its fragrant blooms and plays a role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
