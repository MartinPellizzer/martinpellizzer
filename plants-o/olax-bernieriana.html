Olax bernieriana

Olax bernieriana, commonly known as the African olive, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when mature, containing a single seed. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
