Oxalis andicola var. wallichiana

Oxalis andicola var. wallichiana, commonly known as the Andean wood sorrel, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is a perennial herb with trifoliate leaves that are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring and early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
