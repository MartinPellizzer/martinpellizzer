Ornithogalum weberlingiorum

Ornithogalum weberlingiorum, commonly known as the Weber's star-of-Bethlem, is a bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces umbels of white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is characterized by its striking, star-shaped blooms that resemble those of the Lily of the Valley. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated by gardeners interested in exotic flora.
