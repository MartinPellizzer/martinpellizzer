Oxalis hyalotricha subsp. hyalotricha

Oxalis hyalotricha subsp.

hyalotricha, commonly known as the translucent wood sorrel, is a species of flowering plant in the family Oxalidaceae. It is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves with a waxy, translucent appearance, giving it a unique visual appeal.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, often in clusters. Native to southern Africa, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade.

It is a popular ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of cultivation.
