Osbeckia parnassifolia

Osbeckia parnassifolia, commonly known as the "Parnassian willow" or "Parnassian iceplant," is a low-growing perennial plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It features slender, branched stems that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, and its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often covered with a waxy coating. Osbeckia parnassifolia thrives in cold, arid environments and is adapted to survive in harsh, high-altitude conditions.

It is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
