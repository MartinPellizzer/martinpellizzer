Ocimum motjaneanum

Ocimum motjaneanum, commonly known as holy basil, is an aromatic herb native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like mint and lavender. The plant is characterized by its small, oval leaves and clusters of white or purple flowers. It is often cultivated for its medicinal properties and distinct fragrance.

Its essential oils contain compounds such as eugenol and rosmarinic acid, contributing to its therapeutic uses.
