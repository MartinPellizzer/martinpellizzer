Olea cayana

Olea cayana, commonly known as the Cayman olive, is a small evergreen tree native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, round olives that are typically harvested when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Olea cayana is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in local cultures.
