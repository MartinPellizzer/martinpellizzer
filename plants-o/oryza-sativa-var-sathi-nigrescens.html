Oryza sativa var. sathi-nigrescens

Oryza sativa var. sathi-nigrescens, commonly known as the black rice variety, is a subspecies of rice characterized by its dark, almost black grains.

This variety is native to Southeast Asia and is cultivated for its high nutritional value, particularly its rich content of antioxidants and minerals. The grains undergo a unique processing method that results in their deep coloration, which is attributed to the presence of anthocyanins. It is also valued for its distinct aroma and flavor, making it a popular choice in traditional cuisines.

The plant exhibits typical rice growth patterns, with tall, slender stems and panicles that produce small, fragrant flowers.
