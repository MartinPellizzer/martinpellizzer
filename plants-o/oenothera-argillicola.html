Oenothera argillicola

Oenothera argillicola, commonly known as the clay-plain evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its yellow, tubular flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant thrives in sandy or clay soils and is often found in open, disturbed habitats like roadsides and prairies. Oenothera argillicola is a herbaceous perennial with slender stems and lance-shaped leaves.

It plays a role in supporting local biodiversity and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
