Oxytropis norinii

Oxytropis norinii, commonly known as the Mongolian locoweed, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Oxytropis norinii is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility in its native environment.
