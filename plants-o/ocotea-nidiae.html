Ocotea nidiae

Ocotea nidiae, commonly known as the "Chestnut Tree of the Andes," is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its lanceolate, glossy dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by oval, woody fruits containing a single seed. Ocotea nidiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.

Its growth habit and foliage make it a distinctive addition to subtropical and tropical landscapes.
