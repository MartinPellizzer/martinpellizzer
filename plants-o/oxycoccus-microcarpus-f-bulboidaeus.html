Oxycoccus microcarpus f. bulboidaeus

Oxycoccus microcarpus f. bulboidaeus, commonly known as the dwarf cranberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features small, rounded, dark red berries that are typically less than 5 mm in diameter. The plant has dense, mat-forming foliage with small, leathery leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as bogs and heathlands.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
