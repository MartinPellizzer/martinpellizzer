Ostrya japonica

Ostrya japonica, commonly known as Japanese hop-hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and smooth, gray bark. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers in catkins, which are followed by winged fruits. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates.

Ostrya japonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens and parks.
