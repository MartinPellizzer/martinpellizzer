Orchis lapalmensis

Orchis lapalmensis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and tall, sparsely branched flowering stems. The flowers are typically pale green to yellowish with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.

The plant plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for various pollinators.
