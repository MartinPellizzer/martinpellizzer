Ocimum obovatum subsp. obovatum

Ocimum obovatum subsp.

obovatum, commonly known as the broadleaf basil, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features opposite, ovate to obovate leaves that are often hairy and have a strong aromatic scent.

The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers arranged in whorled clusters. Its essential oils, rich in compounds like linalool and estragole, contribute to its use in culinary and medicinal applications.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herb cultivation.
