Ophrys aranifera subsp. fuchsii

Ophrys aranifera subsp.

fuchsii, commonly known as the Fuchs' bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It is characterized by its intricate floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination.

The plant produces striking, bee-like flowers with dark brown markings and a prominent lip that resembles a bee's abdomen. It thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
