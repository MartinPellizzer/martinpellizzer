Ophrys spruneri var. grigoriana

Ophrys spruneri var. grigoriana, commonly known as the Grigoriana bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It is characterized by its unique floral morphology, featuring a bee-like labellum that mimics the appearance and scent of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to orange in color, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species, requiring conservation efforts to protect its natural environment.
