Olea sativa

Olea sativa, commonly known as the olive tree, is a deciduous tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are glossy and elliptical in shape. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into drupes, or olives, which are the fruit of the plant. The fruit contains a single stone with a pit inside, and it is widely used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Olea sativa is also valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in various cultures.
