Octopera bergiana

Octopera bergiana, commonly known as the Bergian octopus plant, is a unique member of the Araliaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its striking, umbrella-like clusters of small, white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

Octopera bergiana is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
