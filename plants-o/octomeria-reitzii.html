Octomeria reitzii

Octomeria reitzii, commonly known as the Zulu lily, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllis family (Amaryllidaceae) and is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink blooms that appear in late summer to early autumn. Octomeria reitzii thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value and unique appearance make it a favored plant among horticulturists and garden enthusiasts.
