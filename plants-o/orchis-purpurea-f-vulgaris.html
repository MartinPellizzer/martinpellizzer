Orchis purpurea f. vulgaris

Orchis purpurea f. vulgaris, commonly known as the common purple orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Europe.

It is a terrestrial herb with a tall, erect stem that can reach up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers with a distinctive lip that is often darker in hue. It thrives in woodland and meadow habitats, preferring well-drained, loamy soils.

The species is known for its ability to form mycorrhizal associations, which aid in nutrient uptake from the soil.
