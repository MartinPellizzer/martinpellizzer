Orobus californicus

Orobus californicus, commonly known as the California orobus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or purple in color.

Orobus californicus is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
