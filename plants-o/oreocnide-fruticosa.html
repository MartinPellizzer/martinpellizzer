Oreocnide fruticosa

Oreocnide fruticosa, commonly known as the mountain mistflower, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, inconspicuous white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and along stream banks. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a leathery texture and a waxy coating.

Oreocnide fruticosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in cool, humid climates.
