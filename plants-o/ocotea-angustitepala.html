Ocotea angustitepala

Ocotea angustitepala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ocotea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its wood is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional craftsmanship.

Ocotea angustitepala plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, contributing to biodiversity and supporting various wildlife species.
