Ophrys × morisii

Ophrys × morisii, commonly known as the Morisii bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is characterized by its intricate floral morphology, particularly its labellum, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is found in Mediterranean regions, including parts of southern Europe and North Africa. Its flowers are usually yellow or brownish in color, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Ophrys × morisii is a rare and specialized species, often requiring specific environmental conditions for successful growth and reproduction.
