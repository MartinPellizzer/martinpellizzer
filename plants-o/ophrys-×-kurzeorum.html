Ophrys × kurzeorum

Ophrys × kurzeorum, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is characterized by its intricate floral structure that mimics the appearance of female wasps, a feature evolved to attract male wasps for pollination. The plant typically grows in calcareous grasslands and heaths, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its flowers are usually dark brown to black with yellow markings, and it produces small, greenish-brown pods that contain the seeds.

This orchid is a rare and protected species in parts of Europe, highlighting its ecological significance.
