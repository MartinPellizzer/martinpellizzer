Ocimum monachorum

Ocimum monachorum, commonly known as holy basil or tulsi, is a member of the Lamiaceae family and is native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is an aromatic herb characterized by its small, opposite leaves and spikes of white or pale purple flowers. The plant is perennial and thrives in warm, humid climates, often growing in shaded or semi-shaded areas. Its essential oils, rich in compounds like eugenol and linalool, contribute to its medicinal and aromatic properties.

Ocimum monachorum is widely used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits and is also valued in culinary applications.
