Ophrys × maladroxiensis nothosubsp. daissiorum

Ophrys × maladroxiensis nothosubsp.

daissiorum, commonly known as the Daissio orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Balkans. It is characterized by its intricate, bee-mimicking flowers that play a crucial role in its pollination strategy.

The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is found in forested areas with moist, shaded conditions. Its floral structure includes a prominent lip that mimics female bees, attracting male bees for pollination.

This nothosubspecies exhibits unique genetic traits due to its hybrid origin, making it a subject of interest in botanical research.
