Ophrys gresivaudanica

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its inflorescence consists of a single spike bearing several showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Ophrys gresivaudanica is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts in its native range.
