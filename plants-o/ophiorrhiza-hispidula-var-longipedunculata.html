Ophiorrhiza hispidula var. longipedunculata

Ophiorrhiza hispidula var. longipedunculata, commonly known as the long pedunculate mangrove, is a variant of the species native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that thrives in brackish water environments, often found in mangrove forests. The plant has small, ovate leaves with a hairy texture, and its inflorescences are elongated and bear small white flowers. It plays a vital role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for marine and terrestrial organisms.

Its unique adaptations to saline conditions make it an important component of mangrove ecosystems.
