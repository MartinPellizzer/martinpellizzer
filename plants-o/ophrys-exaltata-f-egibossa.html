Ophrys exaltata f. egibossa

Ophrys exaltata f. egibossa, commonly known as the Egibossa bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to specific regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which closely resembles a female bee, a trait evolved to facilitate pollination by male bees through mimicry. The plant typically grows in dry, calcareous grasslands and open woodland habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brownish in color, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, this orchid is considered a vulnerable species in many areas.
