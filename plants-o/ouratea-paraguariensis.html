Ouratea paraguariensis

Ouratea paraguariensis, commonly known as yerba mate, is a species of flowering plant in the family Proteaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, Paraguay, and Argentina. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Yerba mate is well-known for its use in traditional beverages, where the leaves are steeped to create a stimulating drink.
