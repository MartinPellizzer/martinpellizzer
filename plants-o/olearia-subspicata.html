Olearia subspicata

Olearia subspicata, commonly known as the silver leaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray leaves and daisy-like white flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and subalpine regions, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its propagation.

Olearia subspicata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
