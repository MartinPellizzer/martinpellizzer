Oenothera oxypetala

Oenothera oxypetala, commonly known as the sharp-petaled evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening and close by morning. The plant produces slender, hairy stems and has lance-shaped leaves with a waxy surface. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by moths and other nocturnal insects.

Oenothera oxypetala is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
