Oxalis flavopunctata

Oxalis flavopunctata, commonly known as the yellow speckled wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with yellow spots and small, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to yellow. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in partial shade.

Oxalis flavopunctata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
