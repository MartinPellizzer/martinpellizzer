Ochna andamanica

Ochna andamanica, commonly known as the Andaman ochna, is a flowering plant native to the Andaman and Nicobar Islands.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered, often displaying yellow or orange hues, while the fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Ochna andamanica is valued for its ornamental qualities and has limited use in traditional medicine.
