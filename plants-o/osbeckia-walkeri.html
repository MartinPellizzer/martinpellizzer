Osbeckia walkeri

Osbeckia walkeri, commonly known as the Alaska flower or Alaska sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It typically features succulent, fleshy leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant thrives in moist, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in tundra and alpine environments. Osbeckia walkeri is adapted to cold climates and has a rosette growth form that helps it conserve heat and moisture.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of polar ecosystems.
