Oenothera alyssoides var. minutiflora

Oenothera alyssoides var. minutiflora, commonly known as the narrowleaf evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, pale yellow to white flowers that open in the evening. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny, oval seeds. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the genus Oenothera, known for its ecological and historical significance.
