Ophrys scolopax var. atropos

Ophrys scolopax var. atropos, commonly known as the black-faced bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its unique floral morphology, featuring a strikingly bee-like appearance that serves as a mimicry mechanism to attract male bees for pollination. The plant produces a single, showy flower with a dark, almost black, upper lip that resembles a bee's face. It thrives in specific microhabitats, often in calcareous soils within woodland or heathland environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in many regions.
