Olax insculpta

Olax insculpta, commonly known as the African rock fig, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Olacaceae and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that is edible and attracts various bird species.

Olax insculpta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
