Orthophytum navioides

Orthophytum navioides, commonly known as the Navio's orchid, is a striking plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late summer. Orthophytum navioides thrives in cool, high-altitude environments with ample sunlight and well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
