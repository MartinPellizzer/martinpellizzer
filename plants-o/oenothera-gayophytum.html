Oenothera gayophytum

Oenothera gayophytum, commonly known as the evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy yellow or orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oily seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats like fields and roadsides.

Oenothera gayophytum is also valued for its oil, which has various medicinal and industrial applications.
