Oxalis cytisoides

Oxalis cytisoides, commonly known as the yellow wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive yellowish-green color and produces small, yellow flowers that resemble those of the genus Oxalis. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and is often found in open, sunny habitats. It has hairy stems and is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

Oxalis cytisoides is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and flowers.
