Oxalis aemula

Oxalis aemula, commonly known as the false wood sorrel, is a perennial flowering plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive reddish veins and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of yellow and pink. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade. Oxalis aemula is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and blooms.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
