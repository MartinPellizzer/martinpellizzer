Oreodoxa ventricosa

Oreodoxa ventricosa, commonly known as the balloon vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated, balloon-like fruits that can grow up to 15 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often heart-shaped, while its stems are trailing or climbing.

Oreodoxa ventricosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique fruit structure.
