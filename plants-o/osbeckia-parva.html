Osbeckia parva

Osbeckia parva, commonly known as the small osbeckia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a low-growing annual herb with small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Osbeckia parva is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common species in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in pollination.
