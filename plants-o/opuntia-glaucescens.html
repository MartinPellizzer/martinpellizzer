Opuntia glaucescens

Opuntia glaucescens, commonly known as the blue prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its flattened, ribbed stems that are covered in spines and are often a bluish-green color. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible, red, fleshy fruits known as tunas. Opuntia glaucescens is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

It plays an important ecological role by providing food and shelter for various desert animals.
