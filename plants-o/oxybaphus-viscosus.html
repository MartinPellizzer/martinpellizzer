Oxybaphus viscosus

Oxybaphus viscosus, commonly known as sticky baphus, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that exude a sticky substance when damaged. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptations, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
