Otanthus

Otanthus, commonly known as the Cape bellflower, is a genus of flowering plants native to southern Africa.

These plants are characterized by their showy, bell-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including blue, purple, and white. Otanthus species are typically shrubs or subshrubs, with small, narrow leaves and a compact growth habit. They thrive in well-drained soils and are often found in rocky or coastal areas.

The genus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
