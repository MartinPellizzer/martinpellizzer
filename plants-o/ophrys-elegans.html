Ophrys elegans

Ophrys elegans, commonly known as the elegant bee orchid, is a species of orchid native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which resembles a female bee, aiding in its pollination by male bees. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellow, with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of insects.

Ophrys elegans is known for its specialized pollination mechanism and is often found in Mediterranean regions.
