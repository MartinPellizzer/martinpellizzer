Oxalis herrerae

Oxalis herrerae, commonly known as the Herrera's wood sorrel, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its foliage contains oxalic acid, which can be toxic if ingested in large quantities.

Oxalis herrerae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
