Ocimum × citriodorum

Ocimum × citriodorum, commonly known as lemon balm, is a hybrid plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its fragrant, narrow leaves that emit a distinct lemon-like aroma. The plant typically grows as an upright herb with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in whorls along the stems.

Lemon balm is native to Europe and parts of Asia but is now cultivated worldwide for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.
