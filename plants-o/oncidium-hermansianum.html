Oncidium hermansianum

Oncidium hermansianum, commonly known as the Herman's orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, long-lasting flowers that come in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant has strap-like leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in a fan shape. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Oncidium hermansianum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.
