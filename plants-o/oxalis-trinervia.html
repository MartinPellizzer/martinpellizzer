Oxalis trinervia

Oxalis trinervia, commonly known as the three-veined wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with three prominent veins, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, often with five petals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Oxalis trinervia is a perennial herb that spreads through rhizomes, forming dense mats in its native habitat.
