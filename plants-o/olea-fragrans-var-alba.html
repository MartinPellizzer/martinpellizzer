Olea fragrans var. alba

Olea fragrans var. alba, commonly known as the white oleander, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has simple, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, woody fruits containing seeds. It is a shrub or small tree that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Olea fragrans var. alba is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in perfumery.
