Odontoglossum cimiciferum

Odontoglossum cimiciferum, commonly known as the violet orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers with a distinctive, intricate pattern on the petals and sepals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are elongated and often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps in moisture retention.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts worldwide.
