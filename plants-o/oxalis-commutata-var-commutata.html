Oxalis commutata var. commutata

Oxalis commutata var. commutata, commonly known as the common wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features trifoliate leaves composed of three heart-shaped leaflets, with a distinctive reddish or purple coloration on the undersides. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Oxalis commutata var. commutata is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
