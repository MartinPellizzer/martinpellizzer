Oenothera taraxacifolia

Oenothera taraxacifolia, commonly known as the dandelion evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the evening primrose family (Onagraceae) and is characterized by its yellow, four-petaled flowers that open in the evening. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open fields, roadsides, and sandy areas.

Oenothera taraxacifolia is also noted for its ability to attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
