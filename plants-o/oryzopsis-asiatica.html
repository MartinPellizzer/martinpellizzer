Oryzopsis asiatica

Oryzopsis asiatica, commonly known as Asian false rice, is a perennial grass species native to Asia.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that produce small grains, which are often dispersed by water.

Oryzopsis asiatica plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
