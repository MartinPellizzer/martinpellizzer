Odontanthera thruppii

Odontanthera thruppii, commonly known as the Thrupps' grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and rocky habitats.

Odontanthera thruppii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
