Oryza rufipogon

Oryza rufipogon, commonly known as wild rice, is a wild species of the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to Asia and is considered an ancestor of domesticated rice varieties. The plant typically grows in wetlands and has elongated, narrow leaves with a distinctive reddish tinge. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that bear small, rice-like grains.

Oryza rufipogon is valued for its genetic diversity and potential contributions to rice breeding programs.
