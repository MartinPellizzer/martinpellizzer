Ormosia calavensis

Ormosia calavensis, commonly known as the Caribbean tamarind tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Lesser Antilles.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in coastal and sandy soils, making it well adapted to island environments. Its flowers are typically pale yellow or white, and it produces a sticky, resinous substance that has been used traditionally for various purposes.

The tree plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for local wildlife.
