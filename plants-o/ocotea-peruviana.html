Ocotea peruviana

Ocotea peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, spherical drupe that turns from green to yellow or red when mature.

Ocotea peruviana is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
