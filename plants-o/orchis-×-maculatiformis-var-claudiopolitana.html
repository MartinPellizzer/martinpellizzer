Orchis × maculatiformis var. claudiopolitana

Orchis × maculatiformis var. claudiopolitana, commonly known as the Claudian polyanthus orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Balkan region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet-colored flowers with distinctive dark markings. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, often growing in woodland habitats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its beauty in gardens and botanical collections.
