Oxalis carnosa var. incana

Oxalis carnosa var. incana, commonly known as the woolly wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its unique foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

It is also known for its ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade.
