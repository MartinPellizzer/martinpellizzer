Ochranthe arguta

Ochranthe arguta, commonly known as the hardy oxalis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features erect, branched stems that bear opposite, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, depending on the species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Ochranthe arguta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
