Ocotea insignis

Ocotea insignis, commonly known as the golden ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves with a fragrant, aromatic scent. Its small, white flowers are followed by round, fleshy berries that contain a single seed.

Ocotea insignis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
