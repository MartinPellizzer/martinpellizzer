Ophryosporus johnstonii

Ophryosporus johnstonii, commonly known as the Johnston's spore plant, is a rare and specialized member of the Ophryosporaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique spore-producing structures called sporangia, which are typically found on the underside of its leaves. The plant is adapted to grow in specific, often nutrient-poor environments, such as rocky soils or shaded forest floors. Its reproductive strategy relies heavily on wind pollination, which is crucial for its survival in isolated habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
