Otholobium lanceolatum

Otholobium lanceolatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved burr medic, is a perennial leguminous shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. The plant is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil rehabilitation and as a forage plant.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
