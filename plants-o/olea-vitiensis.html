Olea vitiensis

Olea vitiensis, commonly known as the Vitiensis olive, is a species of flowering plant in the Oleaceae family.

It is native to the island of Vanuatu in the South Pacific and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, edible fruits. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

Olea vitiensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique genetic makeup and potential for cultivation in warm climates.
