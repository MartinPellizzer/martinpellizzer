Ocotea poeppigii

Ocotea poeppigii, commonly known as the Poeppig's ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurel and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed, and it is often found in humid, lowland forests.

Ocotea poeppigii is valued for its wood, which is used in construction and furniture making.
