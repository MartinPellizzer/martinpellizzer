Oxalis lanata

Oxalis lanata, commonly known as woolly wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Oxalis lanata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
