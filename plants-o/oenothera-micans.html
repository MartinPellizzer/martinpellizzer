Oenothera micans

Oenothera micans, commonly known as the silverleaf evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery, hairy leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Oenothera micans is a member of the Onagraceae family and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

It plays a role in supporting nocturnal pollinators such as moths and bats.
