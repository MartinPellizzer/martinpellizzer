Olearia rudis var. scabra

Olearia rudis var. scabra, commonly known as the rough-leaved flaxflower, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, rough-textured leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine habitats.

Olearia rudis var. scabra is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
