Ochna alboserrata

Ochna alboserrata, commonly known as the white-toothed ochna, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves, which are often hairy and have serrated margins. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release winged seeds.

Ochna alboserrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and unique floral structure.
