Oldenlandia arguta

Oldenlandia arguta, commonly known as the silverleaf spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a fine, silvery down, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes, making it both a useful ornamental and a potential weed.
