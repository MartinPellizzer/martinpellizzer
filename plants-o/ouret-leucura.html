Ouret leucura

Ouret leucura, commonly known as the white moonflower, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night and close by dawn. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its rapid growth and short blooming period. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to yellow as it matures.
