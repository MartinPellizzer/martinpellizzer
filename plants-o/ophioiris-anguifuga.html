Ophioiris anguifuga

Ophioiris anguifuga, commonly known as the snake-like orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, serpentine-like appearance, with elongated, twisted leaves that resemble a snake. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and green, often with a subtle nectar scent. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires careful cultivation due to its specific ecological needs.

Ophioiris anguifuga is of interest to botanists and conservationists due to its limited distribution and potential ecological significance.
