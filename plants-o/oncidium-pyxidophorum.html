Oncidium pyxidophorum

Oncidium pyxidophorum, commonly known as the golden spike orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, spike-like inflorescence that bears numerous small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Oncidium pyxidophorum is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its vibrant blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
