Ohbaea balfourii

Ohbaea balfourii, commonly known as the Balfour's orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often display vibrant colors. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually long and strap-like, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds.

Ohbaea balfourii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
