Oldenlandia herbacea var. papillosa

Oldenlandia herbacea var. papillosa, commonly known as the hairy oldenlandia, is a low-growing perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features small, white or pale yellow flowers clustered in dense inflorescences, often surrounded by bracts. The plant has thin, wiry stems and hairy leaves that are typically lance-shaped or ovate. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Commelinaceae family.
