Orthostemma roseum

Orthostemma roseum, commonly known as the pink orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking pink and white flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its leaves are elliptical and covered with a fine, velvety texture.

Orthostemma roseum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or terrariums.
