Oberonia sinica

Oberonia sinica, commonly known as the Chinese orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly China, Indonesia, and the Philippines.

It is a terrestrial orchid belonging to the family Orchidaceae, characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as under forest canopies, where it thrives in rich, well-drained soil. Its flowers are usually small to medium-sized, with a distinctive coloration that ranges from white to pink and purple.

Oberonia sinica is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its ecological role in forest ecosystems.
