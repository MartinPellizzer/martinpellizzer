Ophrys spruneri var. reinholdii

Ophrys spruneri var. reinholdii, commonly known as the Reinhold's bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its unique floral morphology, featuring a bee-like flower that mimics the appearance and scent of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are usually pale green or brownish with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
