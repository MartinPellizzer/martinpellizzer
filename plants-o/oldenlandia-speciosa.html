Oldenlandia speciosa

Oldenlandia speciosa, commonly known as the "Beautiful Oldenlandia," is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that consist of clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers surrounded by long, slender bracts. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, often found in moist, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a velvety texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
