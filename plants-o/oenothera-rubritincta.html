Oenothera rubritincta

Oenothera rubritincta, commonly known as the red-tinctured evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, orange to reddish-yellow flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has hairy, linear leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, smooth seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Oenothera rubritincta is part of the Onagraceae family, which includes other species of evening primroses.
