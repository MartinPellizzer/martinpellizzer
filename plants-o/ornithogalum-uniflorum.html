Ornithogalum uniflorum

Ornithogalum uniflorum, commonly known as the single-flower star-of-Bethlehem, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a single, showy white or pale pink flower with six petals. The plant is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long flowering period in spring.

The species is also known for its toxic sap, which can cause skin irritation.
