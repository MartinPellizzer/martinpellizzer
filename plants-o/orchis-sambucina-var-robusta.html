Orchis sambucina var. robusta

Orchis sambucina var. robusta, commonly known as the robust hare's-foot orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, hairy, yellowish-green stems and clusters of small, pale green to brownish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a waxy coating, while its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption.

This orchid is known for its subtle fragrance and is often associated with specific habitats that support its growth.
