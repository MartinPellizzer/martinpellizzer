Orobanche vaucheri

Orobanche vaucheri, commonly known as the Vaucher's broomrape, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is found in various regions across Europe and parts of Asia. The plant produces small, often inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale in color. It is considered a hemiparasite, meaning it can photosynthesize to a limited extent but primarily depends on its host for sustenance.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can have significant ecological impacts on its host species.
