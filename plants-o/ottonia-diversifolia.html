Ottonia diversifolia

Ottonia diversifolia, commonly known as the variable leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are usually broad and varied in shape, contributing to its common name.

Ottonia diversifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
