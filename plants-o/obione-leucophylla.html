Obione leucophylla

Obione leucophylla, commonly known as the white-leaf obione, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, white, waxy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
