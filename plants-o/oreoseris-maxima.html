Oreoseris maxima

Oreoseris maxima, commonly known as the giant oreoseris, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows up to 2 meters tall, featuring a sturdy stem and broad, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowering period attracts various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

Oreoseris maxima is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and resilience in well-drained soils.
