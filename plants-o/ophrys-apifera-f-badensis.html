Ophrys apifera f. badensis

Ophrys apifera f. badensis, commonly known as the bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure that closely resembles a bee, a feature evolved to facilitate pollination by male bees. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and heathlands. Its flowers are usually yellowish-green with a dark brown or black lip, mimicking the appearance of a female bee to attract male pollinators.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in many regions.
