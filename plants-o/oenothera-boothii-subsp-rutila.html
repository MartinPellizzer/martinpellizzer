Oenothera boothii subsp. rutila

Oenothera boothii subsp.

rutila, commonly known as the yellow evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that open in the evening, a trait typical of many species in the genus Oenothera.

The plant produces slender, branching stems and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by nocturnal insects such as moths and bats.
