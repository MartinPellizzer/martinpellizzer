Oxalis elegantula

Oxalis elegantula, commonly known as the pretty wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and prefers partial to full shade. It produces edible leaves and flowers, though they should be consumed in moderation due to their oxalic acid content.

Oxalis elegantula is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and natural landscapes.
