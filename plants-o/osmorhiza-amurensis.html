Osmorhiza amurensis

Osmorhiza amurensis, commonly known as Amur sweetroot, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its aromatic, often hollow stems. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Osmorhiza amurensis thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along riverbanks.
