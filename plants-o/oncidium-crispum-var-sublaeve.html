Oncidium crispum var. sublaeve

Oncidium crispum var. sublaeve, commonly known as the wrinkled orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It features slender, often pendulous stems and produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine, velvety texture. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining potting mix to prevent root rot.

This variety is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
