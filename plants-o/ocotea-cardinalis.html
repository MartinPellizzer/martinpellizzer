Ocotea cardinalis

Ocotea cardinalis, commonly known as the cardinal's wood or red sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, dark red to black bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. Its small, white flowers are followed by elliptical, dark blue-black berries.

The tree is valued for its hard, dense wood and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
