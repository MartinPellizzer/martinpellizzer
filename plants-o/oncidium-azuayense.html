Oncidium azuayense

Oncidium azuayense, commonly known as the Azuay orchid, is a species native to Ecuador.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a sympodial growth habit, characterized by its pseudobulbs and fleshy roots. The plant produces linear, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color.

Oncidium azuayense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
