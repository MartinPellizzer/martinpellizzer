Orchis chusua var. delavayi

Orchis chusua var. delavayi, commonly known as Delavay's early orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southwestern China.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky slopes. The plant features a short rhizome with several fleshy, linear leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are showy, with a distinctive violet or purple coloration and a unique structure that includes a pouch-like lip.

This orchid is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental value in botanical gardens.
