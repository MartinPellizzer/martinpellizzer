Orychophragmus violaceus var. hupehensis

Orychophragmus violaceus var. hupehensis, commonly known as the Chinese violet or purple coneflower, is a perennial herb native to China.

It features showy, violet-purple flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional Chinese medicine.
