Ocimum monocotyloides

Ocimum monocotyloides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved basil, is an aromatic herb native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or white, arranged in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are aromatic and often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Ocimum monocotyloides thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
