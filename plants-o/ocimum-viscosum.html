Ocimum viscosum

Ocimum viscosum, commonly known as sticky basil, is a species of flowering plant in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of the Americas, particularly Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and essential oils.

Ocimum viscosum is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
