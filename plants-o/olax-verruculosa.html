Olax verruculosa

Olax verruculosa, commonly known as the roughleaf olax, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Olacaceae and is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Olax verruculosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and flowering habits.
