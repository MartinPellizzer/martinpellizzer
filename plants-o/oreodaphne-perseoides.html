Oreodaphne perseoides

Oreodaphne perseoides, commonly known as the white sand tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by elliptical, winged fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
