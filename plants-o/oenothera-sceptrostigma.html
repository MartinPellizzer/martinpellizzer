Oenothera sceptrostigma

Oenothera sceptrostigma, commonly known as the white evening primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the evening primrose family (Onagraceae) and is characterized by its showy, white to pale yellow flowers that open in the evening. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers emit a mild fragrance and are pollinated primarily by moths and other nocturnal insects.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to rocky and sandy soils.
