Orobus venetus

Orobus venetus, commonly known as the purple orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom during the summer months. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its terrestrial growth habit. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette.

The botanical interest in Orobus venetus lies in its unique floral structure and its role in local ecosystems.
