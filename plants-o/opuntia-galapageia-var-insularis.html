Opuntia galapageia var. insularis

Opuntia galapageia var. insularis, commonly known as the Galápagos prickly pear, is a cactus species native to the Galápagos Islands.

It is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems that are ribbed and covered in spines. The plant produces bright yellow flowers during the dry season, which are followed by edible, spherical fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting well to the unique climate of the Galápagos.

This variety plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for local wildlife.
