Orchis provincialis proles pauciflora

Orchis provincialis proles pauciflora, commonly known as the rare provincial orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial herbaceous plant characterized by its tall, slender stem and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces a single spike of flowers, often with a few blooms.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
