Orchis candida

Orchis candida, commonly known as the white helleborine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, white to pale green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has lance-shaped leaves with a waxy surface. Its flowers are usually arranged in a loose spike and are pollinated by insects such as bees and moths.

Orchis candida is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
