Olea gagnepainii

Olea gagnepainii, commonly known as the Gagnepain olive, is a rare and endemic species native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, dark purple fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in primary rainforests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Olea gagnepainii is considered a vulnerable species.
