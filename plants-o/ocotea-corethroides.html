Ocotea corethroides

Ocotea corethroides, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain regions.
