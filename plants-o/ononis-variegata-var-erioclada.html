Ononis variegata var. erioclada

Ononis variegata var. erioclada, commonly known as the variegated sandspurry, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fleshy leaves that are often variegated with white and green markings, which help it conserve water in arid conditions. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. Its root system is adapted for drought tolerance, allowing it to thrive in sandy or rocky soils.

It is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in rock gardens.
