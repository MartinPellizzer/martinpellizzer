Osmaronia

Osmaronia, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, delicate appearance with long, slender leaves and a single, waxy flower. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. Osmaronia thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires specific conditions to flourish.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, it is considered a threatened species.
