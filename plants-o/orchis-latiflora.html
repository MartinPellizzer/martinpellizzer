Orchis latiflora

Orchis latiflora, commonly known as the broad-leaved orchid, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial shade. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination.

Orchis latiflora is known for its ecological importance, supporting various pollinators and contributing to biodiversity in its native ecosystems.
