Olea europaea subsp. europaea

Olea europaea subsp.

europaea, commonly known as the common olive, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant produces small, greenish-black fruits called olives, which are harvested for culinary and commercial use. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, dry climate. Olea europaea subsp.

europaea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in Mediterranean cultures.
