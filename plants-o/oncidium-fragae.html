Oncidium fragae

Oncidium fragae, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are long and strap-like, often with a waxy texture to help retain moisture.

Oncidium fragae requires bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.
