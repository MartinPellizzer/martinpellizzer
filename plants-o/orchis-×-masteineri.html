Orchis × masteineri

Orchis × masteineri, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a cross between two species of orchids, typically Orchis morio and Orchis mascula.

This hybrid plant is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, violet or purple flowers that bloom in late spring. It is native to Europe and is often found in grasslands and meadows. The plant has a complex root system and is known for its ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a natural hybrid, showcasing the genetic diversity within the Orchis genus.
