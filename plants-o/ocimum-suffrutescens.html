Ocimum suffrutescens

Ocimum suffrutescens, commonly known as holy basil or tulsi, is a shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small white or purple flowers. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown for its medicinal and aromatic properties. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and basil.

Ocimum suffrutescens is widely used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
