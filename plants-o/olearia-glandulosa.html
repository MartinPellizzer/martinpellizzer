Olearia glandulosa

Olearia glandulosa, commonly known as the silverweed or silver olearia, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, glandular hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Olearia glandulosa is typically found in coastal and subalpine regions, growing in well-drained soils.

It is a hardy, perennial shrub that is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in marginal conditions.
