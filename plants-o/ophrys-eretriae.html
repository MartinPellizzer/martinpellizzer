Ophrys eretriae

Ophrys eretriae, commonly known as the Greek Orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its intricate, bee-like appearance, which plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant produces a single, showy flower with a lip that mimics the appearance of a female bee, attracting male bees for pollination. Ophrys eretriae typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and meadows.

Its botanical features include a pseudobulb, fleshy roots, and a unique floral structure that enhances its ecological interactions.
