Oxalis progelii

Oxalis progelii, commonly known as the ghostplant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas. Its unique appearance and rapid growth make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Oxalis progelii is also known for its ability to spread quickly, sometimes becoming invasive in certain regions.
