Oreopolus

Oreopolus, commonly known as the golden ragwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Oreopolus has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
