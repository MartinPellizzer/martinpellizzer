Ocyroe

Ocyroe, commonly known as the golden cup, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in sunny, well-drained soils. The plant features showy, yellow-orange flowers that resemble small cups, hence its common name. Ocyroe has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often grown as an ornamental plant in gardens.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
