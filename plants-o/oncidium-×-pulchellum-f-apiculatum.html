Oncidium × pulchellum f. apiculatum

Oncidium × pulchellum f. apiculatum, commonly known as the Apiculate Oncidium, is a hybrid orchid species that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

It is a sympodial orchid with a compact growth habit, featuring pseudobulbs that support its foliage and floral structures. The plant produces showy, pendulous inflorescences with brightly colored, often yellow or orange flowers that have a distinctive three-lobed lip. Its leaves are typically strap-shaped and arranged in a fan-like pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This hybrid is valued for its vibrant blooms and is often cultivated for its aesthetic qualities in both indoor and greenhouse settings.
