Orchis purpurea var. eliasii

Orchis purpurea var. eliasii, commonly known as Elias's purple orchid, is a species of orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and prefers partial to full shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged along the stem, and it produces a single flower spike with several blooms.

The orchid is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in the understory of forests.
