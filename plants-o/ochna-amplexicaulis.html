Ochna amplexicaulis

Ochna amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping ochna, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, yellowish-orange flowers. The leaves are typically opposite and often clasping the stems, giving the plant its scientific name. It produces small, fleshy fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
