Oxydectes urticifolia

Oxydectes urticifolia, commonly known as stinging nettle, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with fine hairs that release an irritating chemical when touched. The plant typically grows in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed areas. Its flowers are small and greenish, arranged in clusters, and it produces achenes as its fruit.

Oxydectes urticifolia is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
