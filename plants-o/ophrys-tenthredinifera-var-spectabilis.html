Ophrys tenthredinifera var. spectabilis

Ophrys tenthredinifera var. spectabilis, commonly known as the showy bee orchid, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee, a feature that plays a key role in its pollination strategy. The plant produces showy, bee-like flowers with a distinctive yellow and brown coloration. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland or heathland habitats.

The orchid is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is noted for its specialized relationship with specific pollinators.
