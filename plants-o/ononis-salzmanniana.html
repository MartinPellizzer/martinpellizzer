Ononis salzmanniana

Ononis salzmanniana, commonly known as Salzmann's everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial with dense clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a compact, mat-forming habit with hairy stems and leaves that are typically narrow and linear. Its flowers are often arranged in spike-like inflorescences, and the plant is adapted to dry, rocky habitats.

Ononis salzmanniana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
