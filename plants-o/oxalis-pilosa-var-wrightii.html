Oxalis pilosa var. wrightii

Oxalis pilosa var. wrightii, commonly known as Wright's wood sorrel, is a species of flowering plant in the family Oxalidaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are usually bright green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly in areas with moist, well-drained soil.

Oxalis pilosa var. wrightii is often found in woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
