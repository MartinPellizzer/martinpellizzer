Oenothera oakesiana var. nobska

Oenothera oakesiana var. nobska, commonly known as the Nobska evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the prairie regions of North America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening and close by morning. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oily seeds. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
