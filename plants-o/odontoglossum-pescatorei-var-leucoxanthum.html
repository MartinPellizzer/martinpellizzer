Odontoglossum pescatorei var. leucoxanthum

Odontoglossum pescatorei var. leucoxanthum, commonly known as the Lemon Blossom Orchid, is a hybrid orchid native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking lemon-yellow petals and a distinctive white lip, which contribute to its ornamental appeal. The plant typically grows in cool, humid environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil. It blooms during the spring and summer months, producing fragrant flowers that attract pollinators.

Due to its specific cultivation needs, it is often grown in greenhouses or as a prized addition to specialized orchid collections.
