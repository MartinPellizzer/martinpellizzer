Orchis sambucina

Orchis sambucina, commonly known as the sweet orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem that can reach up to 1 meter in height, adorned with broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces a spike of fragrant, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive lip that is often marked with darker veins. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is typically found in woodland habitats.

Orchis sambucina is known for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
