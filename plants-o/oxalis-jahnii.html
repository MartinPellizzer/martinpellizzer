Oxalis jahnii

Oxalis jahnii, commonly known as the Jahn's wood sorrel, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces elliptical, red-brown seeds that are dispersed by wind. Oxalis jahnii thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
