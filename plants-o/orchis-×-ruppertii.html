Orchis × ruppertii

Orchis × ruppertii, commonly known as the Ruppert's orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Orchis morio and Orchis purpurea.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a robust rhizome and a basal rosette of leaves. The plant produces tall, erect flowering stems that bear a single spike of violet to purple flowers. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is typically found in woodland habitats.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
