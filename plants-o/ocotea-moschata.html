Ocotea moschata

Ocotea moschata, commonly known as the aromatic ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and fragrant leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are followed by dark, oval berries. Its essential oils, derived from the leaves and bark, are widely used in perfumery and traditional medicine.

Ocotea moschata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and aromatic properties.
