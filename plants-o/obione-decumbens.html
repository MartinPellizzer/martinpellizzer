Obione decumbens

Obione decumbens, commonly known as the decumbent lily, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with stems lying close to the ground. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in moist, shady environments.

Obione decumbens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
