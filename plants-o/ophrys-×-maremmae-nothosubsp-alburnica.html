Ophrys × maremmae nothosubsp. alburnica

Ophrys × maremmae nothosubsp.

alburnica, commonly known as the Maremma orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the genus Ophrys, which is known for its intricate mimicry of female insects to attract male pollinators.

This particular subspecies is characterized by its pale yellow to cream-colored flowers with distinctive markings that resemble insect wings. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
