Ochna walkeri

Ochna walkeri, commonly known as the Natal hibiscus, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It features showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive red or purple center. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. It produces woody, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

Ochna walkeri thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
