Onobrychis sativa subsp. scardica

Onobrychis sativa subsp.

scardica, commonly known as common sainfoin, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant has a deep root system, enabling it to thrive in poor soils and drought conditions. It is often used in forage agriculture due to its high nutritional value for livestock.

Additionally, it plays a role in soil nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.
