Orphanodendron grandiflorum var. digitata

Orphanodendron grandiflorum var. digitata, commonly known as the digitate orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring elongated, finger-like petals that distinguish it from other orchid varieties. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in mountainous or forested areas with high biodiversity. Its flowers are usually a striking combination of white and purple, with a strong, sweet fragrance that attracts specific pollinators.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
