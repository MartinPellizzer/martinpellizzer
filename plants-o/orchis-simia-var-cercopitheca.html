Orchis simia var. cercopitheca

Orchis simia var. cercopitheca, commonly known as the monkey orchid, is a species of orchid native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers, which are often adorned with intricate markings resembling facial expressions. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, making it an important component of its ecosystem.
