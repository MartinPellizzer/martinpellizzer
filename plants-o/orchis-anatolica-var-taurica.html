Orchis anatolica var. taurica

Orchis anatolica var. taurica, commonly known as the Tauric orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern regions of Europe and southwestern Asia.

It typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. The plant produces tall, erect stems with lance-shaped leaves and features showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are characterized by a distinctive lip that is often adorned with intricate patterns.

The species is part of the Orchidaceae family and is noted for its ornamental value and ecological significance in its native habitats.
