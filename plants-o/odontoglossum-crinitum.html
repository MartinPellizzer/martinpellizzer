Odontoglossum crinitum

Odontoglossum crinitum, commonly known as the feather-leaf orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its long, hairy, and often branched stems, which give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of showy, violet or purple flowers with distinctive, recurved petals and sepals. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Odontoglossum crinitum is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and unique foliage.
