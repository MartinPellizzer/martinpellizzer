Ocimum selloi var. angustifolium

Ocimum selloi var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved basil, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic and used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like bees. Its essential oils contain compounds such as linalool and eugenol, which contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.

This variety is valued for its distinct flavor and potential health benefits.
