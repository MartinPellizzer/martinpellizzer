Ornithopodium scorpioides

Ornithopodium scorpioides, commonly known as the scorpion's tail, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, elongated inflorescence that resembles a scorpion's tail. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged along a central spike, which is covered in fine, hairy structures. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in swampy or boggy areas.

Ornithopodium scorpioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
