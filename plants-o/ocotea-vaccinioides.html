Ocotea vaccinioides

Ocotea vaccinioides, commonly known as the red cedar or Brazilian cedar, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown heartwood that is highly valued for its durability and scent. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and arranged alternately along the branches. It produces small, white flowers followed by dark blue berries, which are an important food source for various wildlife species.

Ocotea vaccinioides is often found in moist, well-drained soils in lowland and montane forests.
