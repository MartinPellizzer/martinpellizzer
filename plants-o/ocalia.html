Ocalia

Ocalia, commonly known as the bluebell or bellflower, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

These plants are characterized by their bell-shaped flowers, which can range in color from blue to white and violet. Ocalia species are typically herbaceous perennials, often found in temperate regions with moist, well-drained soils. They produce showy blooms in the spring and are valued for their ornamental appeal in gardens.

Some species are also used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.
