Oldenlandia pinifolia var. horridula

Oldenlandia pinifolia var. horridula, commonly known as the hairy oldenlandia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the summer months. The plant has finely hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, rugged appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a unique subject for ecological and horticultural study.
