Orthopogon hirtellus

Orthopogon hirtellus, commonly known as the hairy tansy, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to North America and is characterized by its yellowish-green flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Orthopogon hirtellus is often found in regions with temperate climates and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
