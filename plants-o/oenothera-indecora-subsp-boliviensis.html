Oenothera indecora subsp. boliviensis

Oenothera indecora subsp.

boliviensis, commonly known as the Bolivian evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Bolivia. It is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening and close by morning.

The plant typically grows in rocky or arid habitats, demonstrating its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually upright and branching.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting nocturnal pollinators.
