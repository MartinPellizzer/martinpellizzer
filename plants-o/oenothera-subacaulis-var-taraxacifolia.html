Oenothera subacaulis var. taraxacifolia

Oenothera subacaulis var. taraxacifolia, commonly known as the taraxacifolia evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Onagraceae family and is characterized by its yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has slender, leafless stems that branch near the base, and its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often hairy. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping.

The flowers attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths, contributing to its ecological significance in arid regions.
