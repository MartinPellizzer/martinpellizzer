Orchis tridactylites

Orchis tridactylites, commonly known as the three-toed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodlands and meadows. The plant features a slender stem with a single, showy violet or purple flower that has a distinctive three-lobed lip. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil efficiently.

The orchid is known for its specialized pollination mechanisms, often relying on specific insects for reproduction.
