Oxylobium obovatum var. latifolium

Oxylobium obovatum var. latifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved oxylobium, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its broad, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate foliage and seasonal blooms.
