Oenothera ramosissima

Oenothera ramosissima, commonly known as the prairie smoke, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and clusters of pink to reddish-purple flowers that resemble smoke. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Oenothera ramosissima is also known for its ability to bloom at night, attracting specific pollinators.
