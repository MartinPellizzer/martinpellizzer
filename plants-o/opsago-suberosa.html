Opsago suberosa

Opsago suberosa, commonly known as the hairy balm, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its stems are often covered in fine, stiff hairs, contributing to its common name.

The leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, mint-like fragrance when crushed.
