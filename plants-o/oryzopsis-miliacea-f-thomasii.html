Oryzopsis miliacea f. thomasii

Oryzopsis miliacea f. thomasii, commonly known as Thomas's milletgrass, is a variant of the annual grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

This subspecies is of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological resilience and potential for use in restoration projects.
