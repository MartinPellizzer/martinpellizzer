Ophrys lutea f. sicula

Ophrys lutea f. sicula, commonly known as the Sicilian bee orchid, is a rare and distinctive orchid species native to the island of Sicily.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee, a feature known as sexual deception used to attract male bees for pollination. The plant produces showy, yellowish-brown flowers with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry. It thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
