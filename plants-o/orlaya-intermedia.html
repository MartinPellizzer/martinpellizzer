Orlaya intermedia

Orlaya intermedia, commonly known as the common orlaya, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its erect stems and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and other open areas. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Orlaya intermedia is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
