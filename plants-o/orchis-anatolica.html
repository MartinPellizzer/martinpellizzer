Orchis anatolica

Orchis anatolica, commonly known as the Anatolian orchid, is a flowering plant native to regions of Turkey and the eastern Mediterranean.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils, while its leaves are broad and lance-shaped.

Orchis anatolica plays an important role in local ecosystems by supporting pollinators and contributing to biodiversity.
