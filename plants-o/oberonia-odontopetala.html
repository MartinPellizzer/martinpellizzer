Oberonia odontopetala

Oberonia odontopetala, commonly known as the toothed-lobed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, greenish-yellow flowers with distinctive tooth-like lobes on the petals. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its unique floral structure and habitat preferences, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts.
