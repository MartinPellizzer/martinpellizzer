Oxalis jamesonii

Oxalis jamesonii, commonly known as the Jameson's wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive reddish veins and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of yellow and white. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. It is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Oxalis jamesonii is also noted for its ability to spread via rhizomes, forming dense ground cover.
