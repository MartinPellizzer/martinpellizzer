Oxytropis brevipedunculata

Oxytropis brevipedunculata, commonly known as the short-stemmed locoweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.
