Oreorchis foliosa

Oreorchis foliosa, commonly known as the leafy orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and tall, branched inflorescence. The flowers are typically greenish-yellow with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Oreorchis foliosa is notable for its unique morphology and ecological role in its native habitat.
