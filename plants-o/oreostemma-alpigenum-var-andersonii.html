Oreostemma alpigenum var. andersonii

Oreostemma alpigenum var. andersonii, commonly known as the Anderson's mountain orchid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae, which includes a wide variety of orchids. This particular variety is characterized by its delicate, spurred petals and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats. The plant typically grows at high elevations, where it thrives in cool, moist environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
