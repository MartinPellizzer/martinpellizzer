Oreocereus hendriksenianus var. densilanatus

Oreocereus hendriksenianus var. densilanatus, commonly known as the dense-leaved moon cactus, is a cacti species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and dense covering of spines, which provide protection from herbivores and extreme weather conditions. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming at night. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xerophytic gardens.

Its unique appearance and nocturnal blooming make it a popular choice for both ornamental and conservation purposes.
