Orthosia smaragdina

Orthosia smaragdina, commonly known as the emerald orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking greenish-yellow flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forests or rocky areas. Its leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped, with a waxy surface that helps retain moisture.

Orthosia smaragdina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
