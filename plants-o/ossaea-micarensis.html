Ossaea micarensis

Ossaea micarensis, commonly known as the Micarenese grass, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses and cereals. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. Its inflorescence consists of compact spikelets, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Ossaea micarensis is often found in coastal and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
