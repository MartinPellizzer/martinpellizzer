Olearia decurrens

Olearia decurrens, commonly known as the silverleaf daisy, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It features silvery-gray foliage that is covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Olearia decurrens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
