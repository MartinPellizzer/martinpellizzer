Oenothera fruticosa var. youngii

Oenothera fruticosa var. youngii, commonly known as Young's blazingstar, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that typically grows in dry, sandy soils and open woodlands. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The species is known for its ecological value, providing nectar for pollinators and seeds for birds.
