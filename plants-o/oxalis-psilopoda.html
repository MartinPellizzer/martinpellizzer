Oxalis psilopoda

Oxalis psilopoda, commonly known as the slender wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and forest understories. It has a shallow root system and is often found in regions with high humidity.

Oxalis psilopoda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
