Oldenlandia inamoena

Oldenlandia inamoena, commonly known as the "love vine," is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, fleshy coating. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its rapid growth and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening, though it can be invasive in some regions.
