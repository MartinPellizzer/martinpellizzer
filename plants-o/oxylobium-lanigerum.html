Oxylobium lanigerum

Oxylobium lanigerum, commonly known as the silky oxylobium, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and woodland edges. The plant features slender, often hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive silvery underside. Its showy, pinkish-white flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Oxylobium lanigerum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
