Oenothera macrocarpa subsp. fremontii

Oenothera macrocarpa subsp.

fremontii, commonly known as the Fremont's evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oval seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats like prairies and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and native plant gardens.
