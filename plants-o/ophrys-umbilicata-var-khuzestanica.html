Ophrys umbilicata var. khuzestanica

Ophrys umbilicata var. khuzestanica, commonly known as the Khuzestan bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the southwestern regions of Iran.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee, a feature evolved to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brownish in color, with intricate patterns that aid in its mimicry.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts in its native range.
