Oncidium olivaceum var. giganteum

Oncidium olivaceum var. giganteum, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and green. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the humid environment.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
