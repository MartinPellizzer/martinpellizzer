Ornithogalum afrum

Ornithogalum afrum, commonly known as the African star of Bethlehem, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers during the summer months. The plant is characterized by its underground bulb, which serves as a storage organ for nutrients. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Ornithogalum afrum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
