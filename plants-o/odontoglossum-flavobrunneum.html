Odontoglossum flavobrunneum

Odontoglossum flavobrunneum, commonly known as the golden-brown orchid, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments within cloud forests. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are usually yellow or golden in color, with a distinctive brownish hue on the dorsal sepal and petals.

The orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
