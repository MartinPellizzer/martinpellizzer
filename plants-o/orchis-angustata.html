Orchis angustata

Orchis angustata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate, pale purple or greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its root system consists of a short, horizontal rhizome with numerous fibrous roots.

Orchis angustata is known for its ecological importance as a nectar source for various pollinators.
