Osmunda cinnamomea

Osmunda cinnamomea, commonly known as the cinnamon fern, is a species of fern native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender fronds that are often adorned with a cinnamon-colored band near the base. The plant has a robust rhizome system that allows it to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, which are typically arranged in a single plane.

Osmunda cinnamomea plays an important role in forest ecosystems by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
