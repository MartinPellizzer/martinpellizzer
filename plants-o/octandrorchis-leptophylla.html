Octandrorchis leptophylla

Octandrorchis leptophylla, commonly known as the slender-leaf orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, pale greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, often on the trunks of trees or in rocky crevices. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat destruction, it faces threats to its survival in the wild.
