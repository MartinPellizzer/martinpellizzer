Orthosiphon thymiflorus var. viscosus

Orthosiphon thymiflorus var. viscosus, commonly known as hairy thyme-leaved sage, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its slender stems, opposite leaves with a hairy texture, and small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
