Ocotea pseudoacuminata

Ocotea pseudoacuminata, commonly known as the Brazilian camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests. Its leaves are elliptical with a glossy upper surface and a lighter underside, while its flowers are small and clustered.

The tree is valued for its essential oils, which are used in traditional medicine and perfumery.
