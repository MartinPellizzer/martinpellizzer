Ophiorrhiza pauciflora var. glabra

Ophiorrhiza pauciflora var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed mangrove, is a variant of the mangrove species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its glabrous (smooth) stems and leaves, which are typically elliptical in shape. The plant thrives in brackish water environments, often found in coastal areas and estuaries. Its flowers are small and clustered, with white petals, and it produces small, fleshy fruits.

This variant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and supporting marine biodiversity.
