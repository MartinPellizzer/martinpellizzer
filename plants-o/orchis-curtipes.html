Orchis curtipes

Orchis curtipes, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features a tall, upright stem that can reach up to 60 centimeters in height. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with a distinctive lip that is often darker and more pronounced.

Orchis curtipes is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils, making it an indicator species for certain ecological conditions.
