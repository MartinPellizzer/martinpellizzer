Oxytropis chinensis

Oxytropis chinensis, commonly known as Chinese locoweed, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit, with gray-green leaves that are pinnately compound. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Oxytropis chinensis is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it an important plant in certain ecological systems.
