Oxytropis gelida

Oxytropis gelida, commonly known as the alpine locoweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the high elevations of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and hairy, often grayish foliage. The plant thrives in cold, arid environments and is adapted to survive in harsh alpine and subarctic conditions. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access moisture in rocky soils.

Oxytropis gelida plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for certain wildlife species.
