Osbeckia

Osbeckia, commonly known as the "buttercup family" plant, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

These plants are typically herbaceous, with simple, alternate leaves and showy, often yellow or white flowers. They are native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and are often found in moist or disturbed soils. Osbeckia species can be annual or perennial, and some are cultivated for their ornamental value.

The genus includes around 100 species, many of which have been used in traditional medicine.
