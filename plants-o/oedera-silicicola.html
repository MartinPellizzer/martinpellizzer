Oedera silicicola

Oedera silicicola, commonly known as the silica grass, is a unique plant species characterized by its specialized adaptation to silica-rich environments.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is native to certain regions with high concentrations of silica in the soil. The plant's leaves are typically narrow and robust, designed to withstand the abrasive conditions of its habitat. Its growth pattern is low and spreading, allowing it to efficiently capture nutrients from the mineral-rich ground.

Oedera silicicola plays a minor but ecologically significant role in stabilizing soils in its native ecosystems.
