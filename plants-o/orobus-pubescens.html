Orobus pubescens

Orobus pubescens, commonly known as the hairy orobus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has small, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, and it produces small, hairy pods that contain seeds.

Orobus pubescens is known for its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife and its potential for use in traditional medicine.
