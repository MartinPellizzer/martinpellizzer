Ocotea martiniana

Ocotea martiniana, commonly known as the Martin's ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark-barked trunk and glossy, elliptical leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine. Its small, fragrant white flowers are followed by berries that contain the seeds.

Ocotea martiniana is valued for its ornamental qualities and its potential medicinal properties.
