Oncidium maculatum var. lindleyi

Oncidium maculatum var. lindleyi, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that often feature intricate patterns and a strong, spicy fragrance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture from the air. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native humid environments.

Oncidium maculatum var. lindleyi is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
