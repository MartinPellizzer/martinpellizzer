Oxalis stenorhyncha

Oxalis stenorhyncha, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wood sorrel, is a species of flowering plant in the Oxalidaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each leaflet being narrow and elongated. The plant produces small, delicate flowers with five petals, typically in shades of yellow or white. Native to South America, it thrives in moist, shady environments.

Oxalis stenorhyncha is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
