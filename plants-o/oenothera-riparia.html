Oenothera riparia

Oenothera riparia, commonly known as the river evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows along riverbanks and moist soils, showcasing its adaptability to wet environments. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Oenothera riparia is also noted for its ability to thrive in disturbed habitats, making it a common sight in wet meadows and stream sides.
