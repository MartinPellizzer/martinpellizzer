Oxypetalum urbanianum

Oxypetalum urbanianum, commonly known as the urbanianum oxypetalum, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of white and pale pink, often appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in shaded, moist environments, typically found in forest undergrowth.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
