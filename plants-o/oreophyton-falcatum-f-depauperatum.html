Oreophyton falcatum f. depauperatum

Oreophyton falcatum f. depauperatum, commonly known as the sparse-leaf oreophyton, is a fossilized plant species belonging to the family Rhyniophyta.

It is characterized by its simple, ribbon-like leaves and a structure that suggests it was a primitive vascular plant. This plant is often found in early Paleozoic sedimentary rocks, indicating its presence during the Late Silurian to Early Devonian periods. Its morphology provides important insights into the early evolution of land plants and their adaptation to terrestrial environments.

The specimen's reduced leaf structure may reflect a specialized growth form or environmental stress in its ancient habitat.
