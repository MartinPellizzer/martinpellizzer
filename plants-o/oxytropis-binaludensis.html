Oxytropis binaludensis

Oxytropis binaludensis, commonly known as the Binalud locoweed, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Iran.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect stems and small, fern-like leaves. The plant produces yellow or greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and early summer. It is adapted to survive in dry, rocky environments and has a deep root system to access water.

Oxytropis binaludensis is also known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock.
