Oenothera odoratissima

Oenothera odoratissima, commonly known as the sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces slender, branching stems and has narrow, linear leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Oenothera odoratissima is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
