Oxalis articulata subsp. floribunda

Oxalis articulata subsp.

floribunda, commonly known as the clustered wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a perennial herb characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its stems are often prostrate or ascending, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits. Oxalis articulata subsp.

floribunda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
