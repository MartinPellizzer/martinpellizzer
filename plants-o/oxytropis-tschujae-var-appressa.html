Oxytropis tschujae var. appressa

Oxytropis tschujae var. appressa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved locoweed, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and often hairy, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is part of the Fabaceae family and is known for its ecological importance in stabilizing soils in its native range.
