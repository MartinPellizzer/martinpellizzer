Onosma pseudoarenaria subsp. cinerascens

Onosma pseudoarenaria subsp.

cinerascens, commonly known as the cinerascens onosma, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pale green to grayish leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating.

The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish flowers during the summer months, which are typically arranged in dense, glandular-inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, semi-desert environments.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
