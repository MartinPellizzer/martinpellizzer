Olyra ciliatifolia

Olyra ciliatifolia, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Gesneriaceae and is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves with cilia along the edges. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, often with a contrasting throat. It thrives in humid, well-drained soil and requires indirect light for optimal growth.

Olyra ciliatifolia is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.
