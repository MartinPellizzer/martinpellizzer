Ophrys sphegodes subsp. fuchsii

Ophrys sphegodes subsp.

fuchsii, commonly known as the bee orchid, is a species of orchid native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive flower structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its flowers are usually yellow or brownish in color, with intricate patterns that aid in mimicry.

This subspecies is known for its specialized pollination mechanism and is considered a valuable component of its ecosystem.
