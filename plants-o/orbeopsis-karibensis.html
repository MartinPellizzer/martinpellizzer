Orbeopsis karibensis

Orbeopsis karibensis, commonly known as the Karibensis orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pendulous flowers that often display vibrant colors. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as forest understories, where it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, adapted to maximize light absorption in low-light conditions.

Orbeopsis karibensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of tropical flora.
