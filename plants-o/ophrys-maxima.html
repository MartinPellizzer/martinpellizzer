Ophrys maxima

Ophrys maxima, commonly known as the giant bee orchid, is a striking orchid species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, bee-like flowers that mimic the appearance and scent of female bees to attract male pollinators. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, often in sunny areas with well-drained soil. Its flowers are predominantly yellow with dark markings, and it produces a tall, slender stem that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height.

Ophrys maxima is a rare and protected species, valued for its unique pollination strategy and ecological significance.
