Oxossia calyptrocarpa

Oxossia calyptrocarpa, commonly known as the Cape coral tree, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, coral-like inflorescences that bloom in spring and summer. The plant has compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense, branching panicles. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Oxossia calyptrocarpa is also valued for its adaptability to coastal environments and its ability to withstand drought conditions.
