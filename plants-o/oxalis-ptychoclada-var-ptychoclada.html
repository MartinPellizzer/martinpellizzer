Oxalis ptychoclada var. ptychoclada

Oxalis ptychoclada var. ptychoclada, commonly known as the broad-leaf wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating and produces clusters of small, yellow, five-petaled flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive, variegated leaves.

The species is known for its adaptability to various growing conditions and its ability to spread through rhizomes.
