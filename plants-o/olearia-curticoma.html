Olearia curticoma

Olearia curticoma, commonly known as the silver flax-lily, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and subalpine regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are densely covered with fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Olearia curticoma is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
