Oberonia salakana

Oberonia salakana, commonly known as the Salakana orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, where it thrives in the undergrowth. The plant features a slender, creeping rhizome and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a loose spike. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a dense rosette at the base.

Oberonia salakana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
