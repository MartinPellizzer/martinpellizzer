Ornithogalum wiedemannii

Ornithogalum wiedemannii, commonly known as the star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a bulbous stem with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers in the spring. The plant is characterized by its striking floral display, which resembles a cluster of stars, hence its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Ornithogalum wiedemannii is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
