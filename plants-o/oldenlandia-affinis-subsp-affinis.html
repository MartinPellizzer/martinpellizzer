Oldenlandia affinis subsp. affinis

Oldenlandia affinis subsp.

affinis, commonly known as the "Beggar's Ticks," is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its small, white or yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

The plant has creeping or ascending stems that often root at the nodes, forming a mat-like growth. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and usually have a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along stream banks.
