Orchis viridiflora

Orchis viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces tall, slender flowering stems. Its flowers are greenish-yellow with a distinctive purple or brownish lip, and they bloom from late spring to early summer.

Orchis viridiflora is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its specialized pollination mechanisms involving specific insect species.
