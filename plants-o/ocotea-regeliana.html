Ocotea regeliana

Ocotea regeliana, commonly known as the yellow sandarac tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and yellowish to reddish bark. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by oval, woody fruits containing a single seed. Its foliage and bark have been used traditionally for their aromatic properties and medicinal applications.

Ocotea regeliana is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and unique fragrance.
