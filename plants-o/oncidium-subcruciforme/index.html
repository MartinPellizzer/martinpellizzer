Oncidium Subcruciforme

Yellow butterfly orchid, scientifically known as Oncidium subcruciforme, is a captivating member of the orchid family native to Central and South America. This species is celebrated for its vibrant yellow petals that resemble the wings of a butterfly, earning it its enchanting common name. Growing in tropical climates, it thrives in warm, humid environments with ample sunlight. Its unique appearance and delicate fragrance make it a favorite among orchid enthusiasts. Despite its beauty, it requires careful cultivation to flourish and bloom successfully.
