Otiophora latifolia var. villosa

Otiophora latifolia var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy broadleaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its broad, hairy leaves that give it its varietal name. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
