Oudneya africana subsp. zygarrhenum

Oudneya africana subsp.

zygarrhenum, commonly known as the African star gooseberry, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its small, yellowish-green berries that are typically clustered in groups of three.

The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually oval or elliptical in shape. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
