Oenothera sinuata var. grandis

Oenothera sinuata var. grandis, commonly known as the showy evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its striking yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

The flowers have four petals and produce capsule fruits that release seeds with tufts of hair for wind dispersal.
