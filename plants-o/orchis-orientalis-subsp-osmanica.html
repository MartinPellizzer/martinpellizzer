Orchis orientalis subsp. osmanica

Orchis orientalis subsp.

osmanica, commonly known as the Osman orchid, is a rare and endangered orchid species native to parts of Turkey. It is a terrestrial herb with a slender, hairy stem that bears a single, showy violet or purple flower.

The plant has a complex life cycle, often requiring specific mycorrhizal fungi for germination and growth. Its flowers are typically short-lived, blooming for only a few days during late spring.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is critically threatened in its natural range.
