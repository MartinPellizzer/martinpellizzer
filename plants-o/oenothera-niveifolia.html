Oenothera niveifolia

Oenothera niveifolia, commonly known as the white evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, white to pale yellow flowers that open in the evening. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, elliptical seeds. Oenothera niveifolia is typically found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence.
