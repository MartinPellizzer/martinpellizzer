Ochrosia silvatica

Ochrosia silvatica, commonly known as the silverleaf ochrosia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in terminal inflorescences, blooming during the wet season.

Ochrosia silvatica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in subtropical gardens.
