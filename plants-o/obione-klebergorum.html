Obione klebergorum

Obione klebergorum, commonly known as the Kleber's ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the misty highlands of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent petals and a unique, elongated flower structure that resembles a ghostly silhouette. The plant thrives in cool, shaded environments with high humidity, typically growing on the bark of ancient trees. Its blooming season is brief, occurring only during the wet season, and it produces small, fragrant flowers that attract specific pollinators.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, Obione klebergorum is considered a conservation concern.
