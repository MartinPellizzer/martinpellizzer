Oncidium itapetingense

Oncidium itapetingense, commonly known as the Itapetinga orchid, is a species native to Brazil.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a sympodial growth habit, characterized by its pseudobulbs and linear leaves. The plant produces racemes of fragrant, showy flowers that are typically yellow to orange in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Oncidium itapetingense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
