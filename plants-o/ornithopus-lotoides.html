Ornithopus lotoides

Ornithopus lotoides, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and silver-gray, lance-shaped leaves. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in maritime environments. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ornithopus lotoides is also known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand salt spray and strong winds.
