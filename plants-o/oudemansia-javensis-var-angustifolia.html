Oudemansia javensis var. angustifolia

Oudemansia javensis var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed tamarind, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed, often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Fabaceae family.
