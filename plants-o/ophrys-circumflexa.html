Ophrys circumflexa

Ophrys circumflexa, commonly known as the twisted helleborine, is a species of orchid native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a terrestrial herb with a distinctive inflorescence that features a single, showy flower resembling a female bee, a trait known as mimicry. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with a short, horizontal rhizome and a single, erect stem. Its leaves are lanceolate and arranged in a spiral, while the flower has a complex structure with three sepals and two petals.

Ophrys circumflexa is typically found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and along stream banks.
