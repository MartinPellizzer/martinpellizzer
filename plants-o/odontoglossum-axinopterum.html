Odontoglossum axinopterum

Odontoglossum axinopterum, commonly known as the Ecuadorian orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its tall, upright stems and large, showy flowers. The plant typically blooms in the spring, producing intricate, violet-colored flowers with a distinctive, spurred shape. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine, hairy texture.

This orchid thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental beauty in horticultural collections.
