Oncidium pichinchense

Oncidium pichinchense, commonly known as the Pichincha orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It features a sympodial growth habit with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, often arranged in a raceme. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained, acidic soils.

Oncidium pichinchense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
