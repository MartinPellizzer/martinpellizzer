Oxalis josephi

Oxalis josephi, commonly known as the Joseph's wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features trifoliate leaves that resemble those of the common wood sorrel, with three heart-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of yellow or orange, typically in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Oxalis josephi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
