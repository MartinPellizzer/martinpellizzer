Onopordum illyricum subsp. horridum

Onopordum illyricum subsp.

horridum, commonly known as the Serbula thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and a tall, branched stem that bears yellow to orange composite flowers.

The plant is typically found in dry, rocky or sandy soils, often in open habitats such as wastelands and scrublands. It has a robust root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

The species is known for its hardiness and is sometimes cultivated as a ornamental plant.
