Odontites serotinus subsp. vernus

Odontites serotinus subsp.

vernus, commonly known as the early toothed bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the temperate regions of Europe. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland edges and meadows.

The plant features basal rosettes of lanceolate, toothed leaves and produces showy blue to purple flowers in late spring. Its flowers are bilaterally symmetrical, with five petals and a distinctive spurred lower petal.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
