Oxalis polyadenia

Oxalis polyadenia, commonly known as the silverleaf wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with a silver-gray underside. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, often accompanied by two small, leaf-like structures called bracts. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Oxalis polyadenia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
