Oreoherzogia pumila f. vestita

Oreoherzogia pumila f. vestita, commonly known as the dwarf heartleaf orchid, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and unique, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive tubular shape. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found growing in rocky or alpine habitats.
