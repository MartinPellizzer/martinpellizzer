Oenothera polymorpha var. sellowii

Oenothera polymorpha var. sellowii, commonly known as Sellow's evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Oenothera polymorpha var. sellowii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinator populations.
