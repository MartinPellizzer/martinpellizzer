Ottonia padifolia

Ottonia padifolia, commonly known as the rock daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in a spherical shape, giving it a unique appearance. Ottonia padifolia thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
