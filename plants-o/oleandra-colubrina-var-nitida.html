Oleandra colubrina var. nitida

Oleandra colubrina var. nitida, commonly known as the silverleaf oleander, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the spring. It is evergreen, with a dense, rounded form that makes it suitable for hedges or ornamental use.

The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
