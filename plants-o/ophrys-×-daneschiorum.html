Ophrys × daneschiorum

Ophrys × daneschiorum, commonly known as the Daneschiorum bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is characterized by its intricate floral morphology, featuring a showy, bee-like labellum that mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is found in various parts of Europe, particularly in the Mediterranean region. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brownish in color, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Ophrys × daneschiorum is a relatively rare and specialized orchid, often thriving in specific microhabitats with limited distribution.
