Ornithogalum octandrum

Ornithogalum octandrum, commonly known as the eight-angled star-of-Bethleem, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that form a basal rosette, and its stems are usually unbranched. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Ornithogalum octandrum is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
