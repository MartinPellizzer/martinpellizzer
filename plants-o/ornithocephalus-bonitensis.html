Ornithocephalus bonitensis

Ornithocephalus bonitensis, commonly known as the Bonitensis orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often display vibrant colors. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

The botanical features of this orchid make it a popular subject for both horticultural study and ornamental cultivation.
