Orobus nervosus

Orobus nervosus, commonly known as the veined orchid, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its leaves are elongated and have a distinct venation pattern, which contributes to its scientific name.

Orobus nervosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
