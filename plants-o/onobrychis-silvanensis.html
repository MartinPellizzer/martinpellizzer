Onobrychis silvanensis

Onobrychis silvanensis, commonly known as the silvanese goat's rue, is a perennial leguminous plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland edges and rocky areas. It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

Its historical use in traditional medicine and as a forage plant adds to its botanical significance.
