Osmoxylon helleborinum

Osmoxylon helleborinum, commonly known as the purple helleborine, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage and produces striking purple or violet flowers with a distinctive lip petal. Native to parts of Europe and Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has a short rhizome and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological significance.
