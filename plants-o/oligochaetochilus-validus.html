Oligochaetochilus validus

Oligochaetochilus validus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in wetland and swampy habitats, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant features a slender, upright stem with few leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers. Its roots are often covered in fine, hair-like structures, which aid in water absorption.

This orchid is known for its delicate appearance and ecological importance in its native wetland ecosystems.
