Oligactis latifolia

Oligactis latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf oligactis, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with five petals, often in shades of white or pale yellow. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Oligactis latifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
