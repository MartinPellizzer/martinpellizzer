Otholobium carneum

Otholobium carneum, commonly known as the red-flowered burr bush, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces distinctive, globular pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along coastal areas.

Otholobium carneum is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
