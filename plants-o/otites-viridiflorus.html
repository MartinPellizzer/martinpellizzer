Otites viridiflorus

Otites viridiflorus, commonly known as the green-flowered otite, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of delicate, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. Otites viridiflorus is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive addition to botanical gardens and ecological studies.
