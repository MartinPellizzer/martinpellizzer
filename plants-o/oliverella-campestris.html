Oliverella campestris

Oliverella campestris, commonly known as the field bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Oliverella campestris is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
