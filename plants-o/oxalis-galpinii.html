Oxalis galpinii

Oxalis galpinii, commonly known as the Galpin's wood sorrel, is a small, herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive veins and produces delicate, five-petaled flowers in shades of white, pink, or red. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and woodlands. Its foliage and flowers are often adorned with a fine, hairy texture, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Oxalis galpinii is valued for its aesthetic qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
