Oroya laxiareolata var. pluricentralis

Oroya laxiareolata var. pluricentralis, commonly known as the Andean sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange ray florets and a central disc of tubular flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to the harsh conditions of high-altitude environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
