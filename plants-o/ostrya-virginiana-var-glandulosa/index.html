Ostrya Virginiana Var. Glandulosa

Ostrya virginiana var. glandulosa is a lesser-known member of the Betulaceae family known for its unique characteristics. This variant of the American hornbeam thrives in moist, well-drained soils across eastern North America. Its small, glandular leaves give it a distinct appearance compared to other members of the species. The plant is often found in forest understories where it contributes to biodiversity and ecological stability. Despite its modest size, it holds significant value for wildlife and botanical study.
