Olax retusa

Olax retusa, commonly known as the rough-stemmed olax, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Olacaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in shrubland and forest edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are often hairy and have a rough texture, contributing to its common name.

Olax retusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and blooms.
