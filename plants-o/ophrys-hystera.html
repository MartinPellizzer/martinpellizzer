Ophrys hystera

Ophrys hystera, commonly known as the black helleborine, is a species of orchid native to Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in woodland and scrub habitats. The plant produces large, showy flowers with a distinctive blackish-purple coloration. Its flowers are known for their intricate structure, which includes a specialized lip that mimics the appearance of female insects to attract pollinators.

Ophrys hystera is also notable for its ability to thrive in shaded environments, making it a characteristic component of moist, shaded forest floors.
