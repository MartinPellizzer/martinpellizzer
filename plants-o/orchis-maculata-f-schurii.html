Orchis maculata f. schurii

Orchis maculata f. schurii, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive spotted leaves and showy violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers are bilaterally symmetrical, with three sepals and three petals, one of which is prominently spurred.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
