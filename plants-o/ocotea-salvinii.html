Ocotea salvinii

Ocotea salvinii, commonly known as the Brazilian camphor tree, is a member of the Lauraceae family and is native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by oval-shaped, dark blue berries. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils, which have been used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in tropical forests.
