Oxyglossellum theionanthum

Oxyglossellum theionanthum, commonly known as the flame flower, is a striking plant native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its vibrant red and orange flowers, which are tubular in shape and attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has woody stems and produces small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers bloom in clusters, creating a dramatic display during the warmer months.

Oxyglossellum theionanthum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in sunny, well-drained conditions.
