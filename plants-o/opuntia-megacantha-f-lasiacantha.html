Opuntia megacantha f. lasiacantha

Opuntia megacantha f. lasiacantha, commonly known as the narrow-leafed prickly pear, is a cactus species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems that are covered in spines and glochids. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers during the summer months, followed by edible, red-fleshed fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a notable species in the Opuntiaceae family.
