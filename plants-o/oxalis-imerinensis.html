Oxalis imerinensis

Oxalis imerinensis, commonly known as the white wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Oxalis imerinensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is appreciated for its delicate appearance.
