Ophrys cretica

Ophrys cretica, commonly known as the early bee orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which closely resembles a female bee, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its flowers bloom from late winter to early spring, making it one of the earliest orchids to flower in the season.

The species is known for its specialized relationship with specific pollinators, primarily male bees, which are deceived into attempting to mate with the flower.
