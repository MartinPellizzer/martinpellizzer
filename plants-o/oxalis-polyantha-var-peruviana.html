Oxalis polyantha var. peruviana

Oxalis polyantha var. peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish or purple tinge and produces clusters of small, delicate flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade. Its foliage and flowers are often used in ornamental gardening for their attractive appearance.

It is also noted for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions and is relatively easy to care for.
