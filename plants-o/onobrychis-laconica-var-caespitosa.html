Onobrychis laconica var. caespitosa

Onobrychis laconica var. caespitosa, commonly known as the Greek clover, is a perennial legume native to southeastern Europe.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is characterized by its compact, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are followed by oval, pod-like fruits. Its trifoliate leaves are hairy and have a distinctive, slightly curved shape.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial in ecological and agricultural contexts.
