Oxalis famatinae subsp. famatinae

Oxalis famatinae subsp.

famatinae, commonly known as the Famatina Oxalis, is a species of flowering plant native to South America. It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, which are often adorned with red veins and a yellow center.

The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late spring. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it prefers cool and humid conditions.
