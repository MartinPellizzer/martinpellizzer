Oxylobium dilatatum

Oxylobium dilatatum, commonly known as the broadleaf oxylobium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils found in woodlands and along streambanks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a hairy underside and produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers in the spring. Its botanical classification places it in the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family.

Oxylobium dilatatum is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental value in native plant gardens.
