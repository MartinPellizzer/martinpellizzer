Ornithogalum perdurans

Ornithogalum perdurans, commonly known as the everlasting lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a bulbous stem that produces a cluster of white, bell-shaped flowers during the spring and summer months. The plant has lance-shaped, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its long-lasting blooms and unique appearance, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
