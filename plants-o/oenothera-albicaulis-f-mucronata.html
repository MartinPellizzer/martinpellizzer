Oenothera albicaulis f. mucronata

Oenothera albicaulis f. mucronata, commonly known as the sticky evening primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features showy, yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has slender, branching stems covered with a distinctive sticky resin, which gives it its common name. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

The species is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
