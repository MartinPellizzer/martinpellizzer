Oncidium pectorale

Oncidium pectorale, commonly known as the chestnut orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often variegated flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its inflorescence consists of a raceme of showy, fragrant blooms that can last for several weeks.

Oncidium pectorale is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated in greenhouses and as a houseplant.
