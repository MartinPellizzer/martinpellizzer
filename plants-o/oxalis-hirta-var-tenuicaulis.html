Oxalis hirta var. tenuicaulis

Oxalis hirta var. tenuicaulis, commonly known as the slender-stemmed wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval to heart-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, yellow flowers with five petals.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
