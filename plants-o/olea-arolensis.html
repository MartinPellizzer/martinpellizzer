Olea arolensis

Olea arolensis, commonly known as the Arolen olive, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family, which includes other olive species. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by small, edible drupes.

Olea arolensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and aromatic qualities.
