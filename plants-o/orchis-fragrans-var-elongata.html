Orchis fragrans var. elongata

Orchis fragrans var. elongata, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stem and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing violet or purple flowers with a distinct, sweet scent. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette at the base of the plant.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
