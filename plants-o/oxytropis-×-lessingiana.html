Oxytropis × lessingiana

Oxytropis × lessingiana, commonly known as the hybrid yellow lupin, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces spikes of yellow to orange flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Native to regions of Europe and western Asia, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Due to its ornamental value and nitrogen-fixing ability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
