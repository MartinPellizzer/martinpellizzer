Oryza sativa var. glutinosa

Oryza sativa var. glutinosa, commonly known as sticky rice, is a variety of rice native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its short, sticky grains that adhere to one another when cooked. This variety is rich in starch and has a lower protein content compared to other rice types. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and ample rainfall.

The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with slender stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves.
