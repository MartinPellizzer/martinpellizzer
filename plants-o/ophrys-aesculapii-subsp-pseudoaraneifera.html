Ophrys aesculapii subsp. pseudoaraneifera

Ophrys aesculapii subsp.

pseudoaraneifera, commonly known as the mimic orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its intricate floral morphology, which closely resembles the appearance of a wasp, a trait that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy.

The plant typically grows in calcareous grasslands and open woodlands, where it thrives in well-drained, nutrient-poor soils. Its flowers are hermaphroditic, possessing both male and female reproductive organs, and it relies on specific insect pollinators for reproduction.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, this species is considered vulnerable, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
