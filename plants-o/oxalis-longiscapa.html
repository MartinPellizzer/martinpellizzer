Oxalis longiscapa

Oxalis longiscapa, commonly known as the long-stemmed wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its stems are often elongated and can climb or trail, making it a versatile ground cover.

Oxalis longiscapa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
