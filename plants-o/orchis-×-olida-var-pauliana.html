Orchis × olida var. pauliana

Orchis × olida var. pauliana, commonly known as the Paulian orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, often violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers are usually arranged in a raceme and feature a distinctive lip that aids in pollination.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
