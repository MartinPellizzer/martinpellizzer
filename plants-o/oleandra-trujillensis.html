Oleandra trujillensis

Oleandra trujillensis, commonly known as the Trujillo oleander, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces elliptical, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

Oleandra trujillensis is also known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
