Oxytropis beketowii

Oxytropis beketowii, commonly known as the Bektow's locoweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical legume structure and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its small, compound leaves and yellow flowers are typical of the genus Oxytropis.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often avoided by grazing animals.
