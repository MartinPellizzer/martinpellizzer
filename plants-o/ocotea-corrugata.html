Ocotea corrugata

Ocotea corrugata, commonly known as the rough-leafed ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its dark, aromatic bark and elliptical, often rough-textured leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by elliptical, dark blue-black berries. It is often found in humid lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and medicinal properties.

Ocotea corrugata is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic effects.
