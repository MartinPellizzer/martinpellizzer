Oenanthe thomsonii

Oenanthe thomsonii, commonly known as Thomson's hemlock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs and produces small, white to pale blue flowers in clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a hairy texture and are often alternate along the stem.

The plant belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and some poisonous species.
