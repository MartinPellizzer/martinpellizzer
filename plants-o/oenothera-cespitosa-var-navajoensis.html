Oenothera cespitosa var. navajoensis

Oenothera cespitosa var. navajoensis, commonly known as Navajo evening primrose, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its clustering growth habit and produces showy, yellowish-orange flowers that open in the evening. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and is adapted to arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. It is often found in rocky soils and is part of the evening primrose family (Onagraceae).

This variety is notable for its ecological importance and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
