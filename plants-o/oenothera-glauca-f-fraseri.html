Oenothera glauca f. fraseri

Oenothera glauca f. fraseri, commonly known as Fraser's blueflower, is a variant of the blueflower evening primrose.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to the central and eastern United States. The plant features slender, branching stems with lance-shaped leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. Its flowers are showy, with four petals that are usually blue or violet, opening in the evening.

This subspecies is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges.
