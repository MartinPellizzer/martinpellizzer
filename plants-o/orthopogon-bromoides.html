Orthopogon bromoides

Orthopogon bromoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf false panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, pale green to brownish flowers.

Orthopogon bromoides is adapted to withstand drought and is often found in disturbed or marginal environments.
