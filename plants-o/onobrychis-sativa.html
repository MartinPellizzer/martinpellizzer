Onobrychis sativa

Onobrychis sativa, commonly known as sainfoin, is a leguminous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is often found in dry, calcareous soils. Sainfoin is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it a valuable forage crop.

Its seeds are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
