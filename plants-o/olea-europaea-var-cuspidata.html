Olea europaea var. cuspidata

Olea europaea var. cuspidata, commonly known as the Turkish olive, is a cultivar of the olive tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, pointed leaves and compact growth habit, making it well-suited for ornamental purposes. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are followed by edible olives, which are harvested when fully mature. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, sunny climate to flourish.

This variety is also valued for its hardiness and ability to adapt to various environmental conditions.
