Ornithidium pittieri

Ornithidium pittieri, commonly known as the Pittier's feathergrass, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
