Opuntia polyacantha var. subinermis

Opuntia polyacantha var. subinermis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, spineless pads and narrow, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a bluish tinge. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits known as tunas. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.

Its structure and adaptations make it well-suited for survival in harsh desert conditions.
