Olax imbricata

Olax imbricata, commonly known as the African snowball, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded clusters of small white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Olax imbricata is also valued for its resilience to drought and its use in traditional medicine.
