Otoglossum koenigerianum

Otoglossum koenigerianum, commonly known as the Koeniger's orchid, is a flowering plant native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, showy flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually lanceolate and arranged in a rosette pattern around the stem.

Otoglossum koenigerianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
