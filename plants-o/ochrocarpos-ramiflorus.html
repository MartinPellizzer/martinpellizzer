Ochrocarpos ramiflorus

Ochrocarpos ramiflorus, commonly known as the branched-flower dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers consist of both ray and disk florets.

Ochrocarpos ramiflorus is known for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
