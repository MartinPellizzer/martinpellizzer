Ocampoa gibbosa

Ocampoa gibbosa, commonly known as the woolly sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, woolly, gray-green foliage. The plant produces yellow daisy-like flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Ocampoa gibbosa is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
