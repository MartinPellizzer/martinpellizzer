Oryza sativa var. birainica

Oryza sativa var. birainica, commonly known as the Indian rice, is a variant of rice native to South and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in panicles. The plant typically grows in wetland environments and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates. Its grains are usually shorter and more compact compared to other rice varieties.

The botanical features of Oryza sativa var. birainica make it well-suited for cultivation in diverse ecological conditions.
