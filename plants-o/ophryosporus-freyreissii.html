Ophryosporus freyreissii

Ophryosporus freyreissii, commonly known as the Freyreiss's ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a single, showy flower with a distinctive white and green coloration. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its delicate appearance and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat destruction and environmental changes, Ophryosporus freyreissii is considered endangered, highlighting the need for further research and protection efforts.
