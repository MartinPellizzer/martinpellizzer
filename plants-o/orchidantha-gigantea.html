Orchidantha gigantea

Orchidantha gigantea, commonly known as the giant orchidantha, is a strikingly large orchid species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its impressive, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 centimeters in diameter, displaying vibrant shades of pink and purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat, and can reach heights of several meters. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Due to its size and beauty, it is a popular specimen in botanical gardens and among orchid enthusiasts.
