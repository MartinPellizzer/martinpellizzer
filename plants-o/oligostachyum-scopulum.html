Oligostachyum scopulum

Oligostachyum scopulum, commonly known as the clustered bamboo or narrow-leaf bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts and construction. Its leaves are long, narrow, and have a smooth, waxy texture, which helps it retain moisture in its native habitat.

Oligostachyum scopulum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in forested areas.
