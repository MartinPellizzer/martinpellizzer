Ophrys × raynaudii

Ophrys × raynaudii, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Ophrys apifera, the bee orchid, and Ophrys insectifera, the wasp orchid. This plant is characterized by its intricate, bee-like flower structure, which features a prominent, fuzzy lip that mimics the appearance of a female bee. The botanical features of Ophrys × raynaudii include its tall, erect stems and showy, yellowish-green flowers with a distinct, intricate pattern.

It is typically found in calcareous soils in Europe, particularly in regions such as France and Italy.
