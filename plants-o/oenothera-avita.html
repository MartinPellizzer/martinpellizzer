Oenothera avita

Oenothera avita, commonly known as the desert evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening and close by morning. The plant has hairy, ribbed stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are adapted to arid environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Oenothera avita is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is well-suited to drought conditions.
