Oreocereus maximus

Oreocereus maximus, commonly known as the giant night-blooming cactus, is a striking member of the Cactaceae family native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its towering, columnar stems that can reach heights of up to 15 meters, making it one of the tallest cacti in the world. The plant produces large, fragrant, white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths and bats. Its thick, waxy cuticle and spiny structure are adaptations to conserve water in its harsh, desert environment.

The plant's unique morphology and ecological role make it a fascinating subject for botanical study.
