Oreopanax herzogii

Oreopanax herzogii, commonly known as the mountain sarsaparilla, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spadices, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests at high altitudes.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
