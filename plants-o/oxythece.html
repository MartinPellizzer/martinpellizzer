Oxythece

Oxythece, commonly known as the golden cup plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant features a central stem with opposite leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy. Its flowers are arranged in a single terminal head, and it produces small, oval seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Oxythece thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
