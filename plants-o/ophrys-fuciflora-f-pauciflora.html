Ophrys fuciflora f. pauciflora

Ophrys fuciflora f. pauciflora, commonly known as the rare and specialized orchid with reduced flower number, is a botanical variant of the broader Ophrys fuciflora species.

It is characterized by its unique floral morphology, including a single or few flowers instead of the typical multiple blooms found in the parent species. This form exhibits a distinctive mimicry of female wasps, which is a key adaptation for its pollination strategy. The plant is typically found in specific, often moist and shaded habitats, such as woodland or heathland environments.

Its limited distribution and specialized ecological requirements make it a subject of interest in botanical conservation efforts.
