Ocimum stamineum

Ocimum stamineum, commonly known as sacred basil, is a member of the Lamiaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary practices. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in whorls along the stems. Its essential oils, rich in compounds like eugenol and linalool, contribute to its medicinal properties.

Ocimum stamineum thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
