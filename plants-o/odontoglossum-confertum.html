Odontoglossum confertum

Odontoglossum confertum, commonly known as the clustered toothed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant produces intricate, spurred petals and a distinctive labellum that aids in pollination by specific insects. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Odontoglossum confertum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow under the right conditions.
