Oxytropis graeca

Oxytropis graeca, commonly known as Greek locoweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Oxytropis graeca is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands.

It is also known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock.
