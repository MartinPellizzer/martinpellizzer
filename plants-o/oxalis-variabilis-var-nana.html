Oxalis variabilis var. nana

Oxalis variabilis var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive veins and produces small, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow or orange. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens. Its foliage is often variegated, adding visual interest to shaded landscapes.

Oxalis variabilis var. nana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
