Orthion montanum

Orthion montanum, commonly known as the mountain orthion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in small, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually greenish-yellow in color, and appear in clusters.

Orthion montanum is often found in open habitats such as meadows and cliffs, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
