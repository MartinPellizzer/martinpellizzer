Opuntia rzedowskii

Opuntia rzedowskii, commonly known as the Rzedowski prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that are covered in spiny areoles. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tuna, is edible and contains a juicy, seed-filled pulp.

Opuntia rzedowskii is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife.
