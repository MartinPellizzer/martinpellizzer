Osmunda obtusa

Osmunda obtusa, commonly known as the cinnamon fern, is a species of fern characterized by its distinctive fronds that resemble a cinnamon stick.

It belongs to the family Osmundaceae and is native to eastern North America, particularly in moist, shaded environments. The plant has a tall, unbranched sterile frond that is usually reddish-brown in color, while the fertile fronds are green and produce spores. Osmunda obtusa thrives in wetlands, stream banks, and forest floors, and it plays an important role in ecological systems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.

Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it a notable plant in both natural and cultivated settings.
