Osmanthus acuminatus

Osmanthus acuminatus, commonly known as the fragrant osmanthus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Oleaceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its small, yellowish-green flowers that emit a sweet, aromatic fragrance. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly rough texture.

Osmanthus acuminatus is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and for the production of fragrant oils and teas.
