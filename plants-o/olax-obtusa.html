Olax obtusa

Olax obtusa, commonly known as the broadleaf olax, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Olacaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often broad and ovate in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Olax obtusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
