Oldenlandia caespitosa var. subpedunculata

Oldenlandia caespitosa var. subpedunculata, commonly known as the creeping spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that forms dense mats of foliage, with slender, trailing stems that root at the nodes. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other damp habitats.
