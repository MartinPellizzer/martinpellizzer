Oxypetalum insigne f. baetaeanum

Oxypetalum insigne f. baetaeanum, commonly known as the Baeteanum form of the noble mountain gentian, is a botanical variant characterized by its striking floral morphology.

It features large, showy blue to violet flowers that are typically arranged in dense, terminal clusters. The plant is native to the high-altitude regions of the Iberian Peninsula, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in a fine pubescence, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in alpine ecosystems.
