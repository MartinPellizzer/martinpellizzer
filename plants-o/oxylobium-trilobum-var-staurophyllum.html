Oxylobium trilobum var. staurophyllum

Oxylobium trilobum var. staurophyllum, commonly known as the starflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features showy, five-petaled flowers that typically bloom in early spring, with a distinctive star-shaped appearance. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
