Oenothera pseudolongiflora

Oenothera pseudolongiflora, commonly known as the slender state evening primrose, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features showy yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Oenothera pseudolongiflora is part of the Onagraceae family, which includes other evening primroses and related species.
