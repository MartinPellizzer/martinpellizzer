Oxytropis ochrocephala var. latibracteata

Oxytropis ochrocephala var. latibracteata, commonly known as the broad-bract yellow locoweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often avoided by livestock and can be a weed in agricultural areas.
