Oxalis membranacea

Oxalis membranacea, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a low-growing perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features delicate, translucent leaves that resemble ghostly shapes, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its unique appearance and ease of care make it a favored ornamental plant in botanical gardens and home landscapes.
