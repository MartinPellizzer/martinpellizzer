Odontoglossum × wattianum

Odontoglossum × wattianum, commonly known as the Wattian orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple and white. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its complex floral structure, including specialized lip petals that aid in pollination. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and unique botanical features.
