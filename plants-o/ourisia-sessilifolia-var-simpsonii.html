Ourisia sessilifolia var. simpsonii

Ourisia sessilifolia var. simpsonii, commonly known as Simpson's ourisia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white or pale yellow. Simpson's ourisia thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
