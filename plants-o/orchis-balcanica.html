Orchis balcanica

Orchis balcanica, commonly known as the Balkan orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southeastern Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodlands and grasslands. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it plays a role in pollination by attracting bees and other insects.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
