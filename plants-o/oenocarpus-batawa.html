Oenocarpus batawa

Oenocarpus batawa, commonly known as the African oil palm, is a tropical plant native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves and produces a fruit with a high oil content. The fruit contains a hard-shelled seed, which is the primary source of palm oil. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It plays a significant role in both local economies and global oil production.
