Orchis flavescens

Orchis flavescens, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem that can reach up to 1 meter in height, bearing a cluster of yellowish-green to orange flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing showy, spurred orchid flowers that are attractive to pollinators. Orchis flavescens is native to parts of Europe and is often found in grassy habitats, such as meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species within its native range.
