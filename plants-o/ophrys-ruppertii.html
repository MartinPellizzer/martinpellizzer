Ophrys ruppertii

Ophrys ruppertii, commonly known as the Ruppert's bee orchid, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which closely resembles a female bee, a feature evolved to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brown in color, with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of insects.

Ophrys ruppertii is a rare and protected species, often found in specific habitats that are sensitive to environmental changes.
