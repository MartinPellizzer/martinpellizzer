Oxytropis carduchorum

Oxytropis carduchorum, commonly known as the Armenian locoweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or semi-arid environments, often found in steppes and mountain slopes. The plant produces clusters of small, pea-like flowers that are usually purple or pink in color. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Oxytropis carduchorum is known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can affect livestock and humans if ingested.
