Ononis spinosa var. masquillierii

Ononis spinosa var. masquillierii, commonly known as the slender bedstraw, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often branched and covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface.

This variety is adapted to rocky and sandy soils, often found in open, sunny habitats.
