Omphalodes papillosa

Omphalodes papillosa, commonly known as the hairy globe flower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, globe-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, with a distinctive hairy texture on the petals. The plant has a fibrous root system and produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Omphalodes papillosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
