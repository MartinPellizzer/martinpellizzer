Ochrocarpos tinctorius

Ochrocarpos tinctorius, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic qualities. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy surface.

Ochrocarpos tinctorius is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional dyeing practices.
