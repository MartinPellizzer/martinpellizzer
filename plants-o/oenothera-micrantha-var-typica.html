Oenothera micrantha var. typica

Oenothera micrantha var. typica, commonly known as the narrowleaf evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny, oval seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as prairies and disturbed areas.

Oenothera micrantha var. typica is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for nocturnal pollinators like moths.
