Ocotea baturitensis

Ocotea baturitensis, commonly known as the Baturite Ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
