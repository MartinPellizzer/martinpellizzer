Onobrychis aliacmonia

Onobrychis aliacmonia, commonly known as the Greek clover, is a perennial legume native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion.

Onobrychis aliacmonia is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.
