Orchis elata var. poiretiana

Orchis elata var. poiretiana, commonly known as the alpine orchid, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high altitudes. Its flowers are often arranged in a spike and are pollinated by insects such as bees and flies.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with specific soil and climate conditions.
