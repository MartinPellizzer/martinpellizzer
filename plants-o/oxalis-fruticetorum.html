Oxalis fruticetorum

Oxalis fruticetorum, commonly known as the wood sorrel, is a flowering plant belonging to the Oxalidaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each leaflet resembling a heart shape, and its clusters of small, yellow flowers. Native to South America, it thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. The plant produces distinctive seed pods that split open to release tiny seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
