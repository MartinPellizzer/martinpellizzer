Ophrys × dionysii

Ophrys × dionysii, commonly known as the Dionysius orchid, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is characterized by its intricate floral structure, which mimics the appearance of female wasps to attract male wasps for pollination. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in sunny, well-drained soils. Its flowers are typically yellow or brown with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Ophrys × dionysii is a rare and highly specialized plant, showcasing remarkable adaptations in its reproductive strategy.
