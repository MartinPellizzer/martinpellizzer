Olyra jubata

Olyra jubata, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, featuring a broad, umbrella-shaped canopy. The plant is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough texture with a serrated margin.

Olyra jubata is also known for its fragrant flowers and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
