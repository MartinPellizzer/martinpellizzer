Ocotea atrata

Ocotea atrata, commonly known as black ocotea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage and bark. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within lowland and montane forests. Its leaves are dark green and glossy, with a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

Ocotea atrata produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark blue-black berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species.
