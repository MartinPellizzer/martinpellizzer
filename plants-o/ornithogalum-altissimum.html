Ornithogalum altissimum

Ornithogalum altissimum, commonly known as the highland star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a tall, slender stem that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

The plant is also known for its milky sap, which can be toxic if ingested.
