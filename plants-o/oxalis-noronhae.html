Oxalis noronhae

Oxalis noronhae, commonly known as the Brazilian wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Brazil.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive reddish veins, and it produces small, yellow flowers that resemble those of true sorrels. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.

Oxalis noronhae is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it relatively easy to cultivate.
