Otaria fragrans

Otaria fragrans, commonly known as the sweet-scented orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant blooms that emit a sweet, floral aroma. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and leathery, while its flowers are usually clustered in racemes with colorful petals and sepals.

Otaria fragrans is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its use in traditional medicine.
