Oncus

Oncus, commonly known as the Oncus plant, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Malvaceae.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and showy, often colorful flowers. The leaves are typically alternate and ovate, with serrated edges. Oncus species are native to tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in Africa and Asia.

They are often cultivated for their ornamental value due to their vibrant blooms and unique foliage.
