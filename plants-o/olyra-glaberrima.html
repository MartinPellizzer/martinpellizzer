Olyra glaberrima

Olyra glaberrima, commonly known as the African velvet tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible fig-like fruits that are an important food source for various animals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests and riverine areas.

Olyra glaberrima is also valued for its timber and has traditional uses in local medicine.
