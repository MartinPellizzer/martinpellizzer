Oncidium schadei

Oncidium schadei, commonly known as the Schade's orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, drooping inflorescences that bear numerous fragrant, yellowish-orange flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its pseudobulbs are cylindrical, and it produces strap-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating.

Oncidium schadei is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.
