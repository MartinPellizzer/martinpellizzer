Onopordum ambiguum

Onopordum ambiguum, commonly known as the ambiguous thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a robust stem that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, covered with dense, grayish-green leaves that have spiny margins. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are surrounded by a series of spiny bracts. Its leaves and stems are covered with fine, white hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

Onopordum ambiguum is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its hardy nature.
