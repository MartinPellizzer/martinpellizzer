Ophiopogon clavatus

Ophiopogon clavatus, commonly known as the broad-leaf spathiphyllum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are often clustered at the base of the plant. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

Ophiopogon clavatus is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and ease of care.
