Orchis sambucina var. purpurascens

Orchis sambucina var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-flowered orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers are often clustered in a raceme and have a distinctive, spurred structure typical of orchids.

The leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged along the stem, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
