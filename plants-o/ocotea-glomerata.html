Ocotea glomerata

Ocotea glomerata, commonly known as the clustered ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree belonging to the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the Caribbean and northern South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in white or greenish-white colors. Its fruit is a small, woody drupe that contains a single seed.

Ocotea glomerata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
