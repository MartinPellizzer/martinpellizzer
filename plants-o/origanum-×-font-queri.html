Origanum × font-queri

Origanum × font-queri, commonly known as Greek oregano, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves that are typically gray-green in color, and its flowers are small, tubular, and clustered in dense spikes. The plant is known for its strong, pungent fragrance, which comes from essential oils concentrated in its leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for Mediterranean gardens.

This plant is widely used in cooking and herbal medicine due to its valuable essential oils and flavoring properties.
