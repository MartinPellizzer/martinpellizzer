Oncoba tettensis var. kirkii

Oncoba tettensis var. kirkii, commonly known as the Kirk's mallow, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a distinctive violet or purple coloration. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its robust growth and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
