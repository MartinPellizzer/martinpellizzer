Oetosis blechnodes

Oetosis blechnodes, commonly known as the silver fern, is a species of fern native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its distinctive, silver-gray fronds that are divided into narrow, lance-shaped segments. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to the soil while absorbing nutrients.

Oetosis blechnodes plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates.
