Ocotea cinerea

Ocotea cinerea, commonly known as silverwood or white cedar, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible for some bird species. Ocotea cinerea is often found in humid lowland forests and is valued for its aromatic wood.

Its botanical features make it an important component of tropical ecosystems.
