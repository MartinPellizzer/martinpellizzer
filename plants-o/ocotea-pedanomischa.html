Ocotea pedanomischa

Ocotea pedanomischa, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a member of the Lauraceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, characterized by its glossy dark green leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are an important food source for various bird species.

Botanically, it is notable for its aromatic oils and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
