Oxalis latemucronata

Oxalis latemucronata, commonly known as the broad-mucron oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Oxalidaceae and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its ornamental value is due to its delicate foliage and vibrant floral display.

Oxalis latemucronata is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
