Opuntia montevideensis

Opuntia montevideensis, commonly known as the Uruguay prickly pear, is a cacti species native to South America, specifically Uruguay and parts of Argentina.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems that are covered in spines and glochids, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the summer months, followed by edible fruits known as tunas. Opuntia montevideensis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in regions with limited rainfall.

Its succulent stems store water, allowing it to thrive in harsh, dry conditions.
