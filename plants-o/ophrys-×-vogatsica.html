Ophrys × vogatsica

Ophrys × vogatsica, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

It is a natural hybrid between Ophrys apifera (the bee orchid) and Ophrys insectifera (the wasp orchid), resulting in a blend of traits from both parents. The plant produces striking, bee-like flowers that have evolved to mimic the appearance and scent of female bees, aiding in its pollination strategy. Its flowers are typically yellow with intricate patterns and a distinctive lip that resembles a bee's abdomen.

Ophrys × vogatsica is often found in calcareous soils and thrives in temperate climates, making it a notable example of adaptive evolution in orchids.
