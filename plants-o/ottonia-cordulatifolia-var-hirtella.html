Ottonia cordulatifolia var. hirtella

Ottonia cordulatifolia var. hirtella, commonly known as the hairy heartleaf, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, hairy glands, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening for its unique appearance.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is adapted to wetland habitats.
