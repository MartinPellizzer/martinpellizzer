Osmanthus × japonicus

Osmanthus × japonicus, commonly known as Japanese sweet osmanthus, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

Osmanthus × japonicus is also used in traditional Chinese medicine and as a source of aromatic oils.
