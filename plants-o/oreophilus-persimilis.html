Oreophilus persimilis

Oreophilus persimilis, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It thrives in cool, moist environments, typically growing in cloud forests and mountainous areas where humidity is consistently high. The plant features slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in loose inflorescences, often blooming during the late spring and early summer months.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, Oreophilus persimilis is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation programs.
