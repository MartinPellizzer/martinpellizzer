Orobus vestitus

Orobus vestitus, commonly known as the naked orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, velvety pubescence.

Orobus vestitus is notable for its unique morphology and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in specialized gardens.
