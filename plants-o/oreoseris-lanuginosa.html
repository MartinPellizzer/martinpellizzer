Oreoseris lanuginosa

Oreoseris lanuginosa, commonly known as the woolly sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, woolly leaves and yellow-orange flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowering period occurs during the warmer months, and it produces small, composite flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Oreoseris lanuginosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
