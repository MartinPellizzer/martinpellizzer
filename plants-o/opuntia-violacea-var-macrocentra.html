Opuntia violacea var. macrocentra

Opuntia violacea var. macrocentra, commonly known as the large-centre prickly pear, is a cactus species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, flat, paddle-shaped stems that are ribbed and covered in spines. The plant produces vibrant violet to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit, known as a tunas, is edible and has a sweet, juicy flavor.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and adaptability to arid environments.
