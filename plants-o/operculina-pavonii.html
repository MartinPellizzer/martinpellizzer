Operculina pavonii

Operculina pavonii, commonly known as the pink orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its striking pink and white flowers. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial orchid with a sympodial growth habit. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern.

Operculina pavonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
