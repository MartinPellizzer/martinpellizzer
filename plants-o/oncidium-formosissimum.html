Oncidium formosissimum

Oncidium formosissimum, commonly known as the Orchid of the South, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful flowers that often display a mix of yellow, orange, and red hues. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Oncidium formosissimum is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate with proper care.
