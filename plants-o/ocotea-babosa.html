Ocotea babosa

Ocotea babosa, commonly known as the Brazilian camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface and produces small, fragrant flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds.

Ocotea babosa is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects.
