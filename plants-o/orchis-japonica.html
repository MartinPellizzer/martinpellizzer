Orchis japonica

Orchis japonica, commonly known as Japanese twayblade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique morphology, including two opposite leaves and a single, showy flower. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and meadows. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a distinctive hood-like structure, and it relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

Orchis japonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitats.
