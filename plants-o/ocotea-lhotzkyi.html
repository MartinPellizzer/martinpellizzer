Ocotea lhotzkyi

Ocotea lhotzkyi, commonly known as the Yucatan laurel, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Mexico and Central America, where it typically grows in humid lowland forests. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered white flowers. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Ocotea lhotzkyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
