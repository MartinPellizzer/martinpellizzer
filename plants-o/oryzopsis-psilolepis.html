Oryzopsis psilolepis

Oryzopsis psilolepis, commonly known as the narrowleaf wild rice, is a grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. This plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its seeds are often harvested for food, similar to other wild rice species.

The plant plays an important ecological role in supporting aquatic and wetland ecosystems.
