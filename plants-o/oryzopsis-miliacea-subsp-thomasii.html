Oryzopsis miliacea subsp. thomasii

Oryzopsis miliacea subsp.

thomasii, commonly known as Thomas' wild oat, is a grass species native to the western United States. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are clustered in panicles, bearing small, two-lobed florets.

It is adapted to arid conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native environment.
