Onkeripus

Onkeripus, commonly known as the moonflower vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant white flowers that open at night and close by dawn, adapting to low-light conditions. The leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a glossy surface that helps reflect minimal light. Its rapid growth habit makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening, though it requires support structures to thrive.

The plant also has medicinal properties, traditionally used in local herbal remedies.
