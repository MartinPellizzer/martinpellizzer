Onobrychis viciifolia var. comosa

Onobrychis viciifolia var. comosa, commonly known as silky sainfoin, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has a hairy, creeping stem and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often used in ecological restoration due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.

Its ornamental value comes from its delicate foliage and long blooming period.
