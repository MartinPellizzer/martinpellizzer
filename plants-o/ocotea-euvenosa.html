Ocotea euvenosa

Ocotea euvenosa, commonly known as the yellow sandarac tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible for some bird species. Its wood is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional crafts and as a source of essential oils.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, supporting local biodiversity and contributing to forest ecosystems.
