Oldenlandia pterita

Oldenlandia pterita, commonly known as the silverleaf spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
