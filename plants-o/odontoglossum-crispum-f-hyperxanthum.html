Odontoglossum crispum f. hyperxanthum

Odontoglossum crispum f. hyperxanthum, commonly known as the golden lip orchid, is a hybrid orchid species characterized by its vibrant yellow petals and sepals.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andes Mountains of South America. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are often fragrant and bloom in late spring or early summer.

The f. hyperxanthum form is notable for its intense coloration and is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts.
