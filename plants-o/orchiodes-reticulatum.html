Orchiodes reticulatum

Orchiodes reticulatum, commonly known as the reticulate orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its intricate, net-like patterns on its petals and sepals, which give it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, often in mountainous regions. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish in color, with a subtle fragrance.

Orchiodes reticulatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
