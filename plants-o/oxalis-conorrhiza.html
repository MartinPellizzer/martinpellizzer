Oxalis conorrhiza

Oxalis conorrhiza, commonly known as the yellow wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features trifoliate leaves with three heart-shaped leaflets, often adorned with a yellowish or reddish glandular spot at the base. The plant produces small, five-petaled flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming in spring. Its roots are fibrous and often have a distinctive reddish color, contributing to its common name.

Oxalis conorrhiza thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.
