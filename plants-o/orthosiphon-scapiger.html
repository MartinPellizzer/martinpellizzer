Orthosiphon scapiger

Orthosiphon scapiger, commonly known as Java tea or kidney tea, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in wet, tropical environments and is often found in swampy areas or along riverbanks.
