Orchiodes hispidum

Orchiodes hispidum, commonly known as the hairy orchid, is a species of orchid native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, fleshy leaves and striking, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or wetlands. Its flowers are usually violet or purple in color, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination.

Orchiodes hispidum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
