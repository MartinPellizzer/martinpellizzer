Ochna congoensis var. microphylla

Ochna congoensis var. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved ochna, is a shrub or small tree native to Central and Eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually leathery and glossy in texture. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by orange-red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and floral display.
