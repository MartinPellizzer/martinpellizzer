Odontoglossum subcruciforme

Odontoglossum subcruciforme, commonly known as the cruciform orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and lanceolate leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers with a distinctive cruciform shape, often in shades of white and purple. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil for optimal growth.

Odontoglossum subcruciforme is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts due to its ornamental value and relatively easy cultivation requirements.
