Ocotea euryphylla

Ocotea euryphylla, commonly known as the broad-leafed ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and avocado. The tree is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and dark green. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue berries that are an important food source for various wildlife.

Ocotea euryphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
