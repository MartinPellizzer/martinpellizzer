Ocimum asperum

Ocimum asperum, commonly known as rough basil, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a rough texture. Its small, tubular flowers are typically blue to purple in color and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ocimum asperum is valued for its fragrant leaves, which are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
