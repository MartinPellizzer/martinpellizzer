Oenocarpus bacaba var. grandis

Oenocarpus bacaba var. grandis, commonly known as the bacaba palm, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter. The plant produces clusters of large, green fruits that turn yellow when mature, containing a single large seed. The bark and leaves of the bacaba palm are used in traditional medicine and for making ropes and thatching.

It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.
