Orchis appendiculata

Orchis appendiculata, commonly known as the appendiculate orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant has a well-developed root system and produces a single, often branched inflorescence. Its flowers are adorned with intricate patterns and are pollinated by insects, particularly bees.

Orchis appendiculata is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
