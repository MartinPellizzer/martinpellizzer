Oreocarya pulvinata

Oreocarya pulvinata, commonly known as the Sierra foothill buckwheat, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of oval, gray-green leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late summer. Its growth habit is adapted to dry, rocky habitats, and it thrives in full sun.

Oreocarya pulvinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
