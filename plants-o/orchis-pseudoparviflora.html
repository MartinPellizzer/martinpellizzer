Orchis pseudoparviflora

Orchis pseudoparviflora, commonly known as the false small-flowered orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features a tall, erect stem with a few narrow leaves and produces clusters of small, pale pink or greenish flowers. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient uptake in its woodland habitat.

The species is part of the Orchidaceae family, known for its complex floral structures and specialized pollination mechanisms.
