Oenothera spachiana

Oenothera spachiana, commonly known as the Spach's evening primrose, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and arid environments. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Oenothera spachiana is part of the Onagraceae family, which includes other evening primroses and related species.
