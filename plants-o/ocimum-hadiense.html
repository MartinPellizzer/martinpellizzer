Ocimum hadiense

Ocimum hadiense, commonly known as Hadiense basil, is a species of flowering plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or pink in color. It is often grown for its culinary and medicinal properties.

Ocimum hadiense thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
