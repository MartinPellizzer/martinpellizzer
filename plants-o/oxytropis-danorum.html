Oxytropis danorum

Oxytropis danorum, commonly known as Danish locoweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with pinnate arrangement and produces small, fluffy seed pods. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Oxytropis danorum is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
