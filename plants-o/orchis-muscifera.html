Orchis muscifera

Orchis muscifera, commonly known as the musk orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, velvety texture.

Orchis muscifera is known for its association with specific soil types and is often found in damp, shaded woodlands.
