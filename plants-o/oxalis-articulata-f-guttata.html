Oxalis articulata f. guttata

Oxalis articulata f. guttata, commonly known as the spotted wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive red or purple spots, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, five-petaled yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It is a succulent species, capable of storing water in its fleshy leaves and stems.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
