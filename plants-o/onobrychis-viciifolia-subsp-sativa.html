Onobrychis viciifolia subsp. sativa

Onobrychis viciifolia subsp.

sativa, commonly known as sainfoin, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia. It is a perennial legume characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant has a deep taproot system, allowing it to thrive in poor soils and arid conditions. Sainfoin is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it a valuable forage crop.

Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein.
