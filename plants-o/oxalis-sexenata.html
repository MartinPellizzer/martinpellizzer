Oxalis sexenata

Oxalis sexenata, commonly known as the six-leaf wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It features distinctive trifoliate leaves with a bright green color and small, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Oxalis sexenata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and flowers.
