Ophrys × pintoi

Ophrys × pintoi, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant that exhibits unique botanical features.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera, two species known for their mimicry of female wasps. The plant produces strikingly intricate flowers that closely resemble female insects, a trait that plays a key role in its pollination strategy. These flowers are typically yellow or brown with intricate patterns, designed to attract male wasps.

Ophrys × pintoi is native to southern Europe and thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands and grasslands.
