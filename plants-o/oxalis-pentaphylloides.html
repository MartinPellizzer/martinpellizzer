Oxalis pentaphylloides

Oxalis pentaphylloides, commonly known as the five-leafed wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with five leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance reminiscent of the shamrock. The plant produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring. Its stems are often reddish in color and can become woody with age.

Oxalis pentaphylloides thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
