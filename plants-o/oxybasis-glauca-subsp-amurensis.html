Oxybasis glauca subsp. amurensis

Oxybasis glauca subsp.

amurensis, commonly known as the Amur oxybasis, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, yellow to orange composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as steppes and grasslands. Its leaves are linear and gray-green, with a distinctive waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
