Ostrya knowltonii var. chisosensis

Ostrya knowltonii var. chisosensis, commonly known as the Chisos hickory, is a rare and endemic species native to the Chisos Mountains in Texas.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows to a height of 10 to 20 meters, featuring a rounded crown and smooth, gray bark. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a serrated margin and a dark green upper surface. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in catkins, and it produces edible nuts enclosed in a thick, woody husk.

This species is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mixed woodlands.
