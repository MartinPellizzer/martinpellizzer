Oxalis latipes

Oxalis latipes, commonly known as the narrowleaf wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features trifoliate leaves with three heart-shaped leaflets and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of yellow or orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are often short-lived, opening in the morning and closing by midday.

Oxalis latipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and brief flowering period.
