Oxalis divaricata

Oxalis divaricata, commonly known as the variable wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each leaflet being heart-shaped with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a yellow or red center. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun exposure.

Oxalis divaricata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique floral display.
