Oxalis corniculata f. villosa

Oxalis corniculata f. villosa, commonly known as the hairy wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to Asia and parts of Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial with trifoliate leaves that have a distinctive heart-shaped appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, often arranged in clusters. Its hairy stems and leaves give it a unique texture, and it thrives in moist, shady environments.

It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and ease of cultivation.
