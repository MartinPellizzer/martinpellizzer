Oncidium proliferum

Oncidium proliferum, commonly known as the orchid with many branches, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous inflorescences and showy, fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including yellow, orange, and red. The plant has pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in environments with irregular rainfall. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for epiphytic growth, often attaching to tree trunks or other structures.

Oncidium proliferum is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for both novice and experienced orchid growers.
