Osbeckia paludosa

Osbeckia paludosa, commonly known as the marsh osbeckia, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in marshes, swales, and along the edges of ponds. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and arranged in clusters, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Osbeckia paludosa plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and provides habitat for various invertebrates.
