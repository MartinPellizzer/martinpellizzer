Olea fragrans var. acuminata

Olea fragrans var. acuminata, commonly known as the fragrant olive tree, is a variant of the olive tree native to regions of southern and southeastern Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, edible fruits that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a distinctive fragrant aroma when crushed.

The flowers are small, yellowish-green, and often clustered in panicles, contributing to its ornamental value.
