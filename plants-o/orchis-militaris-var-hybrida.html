Orchis militaris var. hybrida

Orchis militaris var. hybrida, commonly known as the hybrid soldier's orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and woodland edges. Its flowers have a distinctive lip that is often darker in color, aiding in pollination by insects.

This hybrid variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
