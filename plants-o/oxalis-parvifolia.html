Oxalis parvifolia

Oxalis parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaf wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive pattern of veins and small, delicate flowers that bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, edible leaves and flowers.

Oxalis parvifolia is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
