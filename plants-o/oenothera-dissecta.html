Oenothera dissecta

Oenothera dissecta, commonly known as the dissected evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and delicate, yellow-orange flowers that bloom at night. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are finely dissected, giving it a feathery appearance. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.
