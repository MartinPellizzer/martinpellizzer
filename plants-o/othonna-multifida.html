Othonna multifida

Othonna multifida, commonly known as the fingerleaf chard or African daisy, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are deeply lobed and resemble fingers. The plant produces bright yellow to orange composite flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Othonna multifida is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and floral display.
