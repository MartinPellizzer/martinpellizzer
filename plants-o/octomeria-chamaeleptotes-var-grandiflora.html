Octomeria chamaeleptotes var. grandiflora

Octomeria chamaeleptotes var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered octomeria, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticultural enthusiasts.
