Opuntia lindsayi

Opuntia lindsayi, commonly known as the Lindsay's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems that are covered in spines and glochids, which are small, barbed bristles. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months, followed by edible, red-fleshed fruits. Its succulent stems store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

Opuntia lindsayi plays an important role in its ecosystem, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
