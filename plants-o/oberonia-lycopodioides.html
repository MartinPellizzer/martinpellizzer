Oberonia lycopodioides

Oberonia lycopodioides, commonly known as the slender fern orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its roots form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi, aiding in nutrient absorption.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
