Oleandra nodosa

Oleandra nodosa, commonly known as the nodding oleander, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has simple, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. Oleandra nodosa is evergreen, with a woody structure that allows it to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
