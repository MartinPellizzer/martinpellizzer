Ononis vulgaris var. pungens

Ononis vulgaris var. pungens, commonly known as the prickly stitchwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and small, clustered pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is typically found in rocky or sandy soils, often in disturbed or open habitats.

This variety is notable for its sharp, bristly hairs, which can cause irritation to the skin.
