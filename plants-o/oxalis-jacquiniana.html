Oxalis jacquiniana

Oxalis jacquiniana, commonly known as the yellow wood sorrel, is a species of flowering plant in the family Oxalidaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant produces small, bead-like fruits that contain several seeds. Oxalis jacquiniana thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
