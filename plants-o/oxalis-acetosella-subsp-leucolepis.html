Oxalis acetosella subsp. leucolepis

Oxalis acetosella subsp.

leucolepis, commonly known as the purple wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive purple hue and small, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks. Its stems are prostrate, and it produces small, inconspicuous fruits that disperse seeds via wind.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
