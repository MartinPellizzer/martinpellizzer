Origanum heracleoticum

Origanum heracleoticum, commonly known as Greek oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and Mediterranean climates. Its essential oils, rich in compounds like carvacrol and thymol, contribute to its strong scent and medicinal properties.

It is often used in cooking and traditional herbal remedies due to its robust flavor and health benefits.
