Oxalis pilosissima

Oxalis pilosissima, commonly known as the hairy wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a fuzzy texture and produces small, yellow flowers that resemble those of true sorrel. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive leaf structure and delicate blooms.

Oxalis pilosissima is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and flowers.
