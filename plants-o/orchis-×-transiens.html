Orchis × transiens

Orchis × transiens, commonly known as the intermediate orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Orchis morio and Orchis mascula.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, spurred flowers that typically range in color from violet to deep purple. The plant thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland habitats across Europe. It blooms from late spring to early summer, producing intricate flowers with a distinct lip that serves as a landing platform for pollinators.

Orchis × transiens is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
