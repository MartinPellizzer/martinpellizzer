Oenanthe crocata

Oenanthe crocata, commonly known as the yellow water-dropwort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often near water bodies. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a distinctive waxy coating.

Oenanthe crocata is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist environments, making it a common sight in wetlands and along riverbanks.
