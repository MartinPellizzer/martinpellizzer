Ophrys insectifera lusus virescens

Ophrys insectifera lusus virescens, commonly known as the green orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant produces a single, greenish-yellow flower with a distinctive lip that resembles a bee's body. It thrives in calcareous soils and requires specific microclimatic conditions for successful growth.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, this orchid is considered endangered in many parts of its range.
