Oxalis dichotomiflora

Oxalis dichotomiflora, commonly known as the two-lobed wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features delicate, trifoliate leaves with a distinctive two-lobed shape and produces small, yellowish-white flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in well-drained environments. It is often found in open woodlands and along riverbanks, where it forms dense mats.

Oxalis dichotomiflora is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
