Otopappus scaber

Otopappus scaber, commonly known as the rough horseweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and foliage with a rough texture. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

Otopappus scaber produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators.
