Ouratea pumila var. heterodoxa

Ouratea pumila var. heterodoxa, commonly known as the dwarf ouratea, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, often with a slight waxy coating. The plant produces small, white, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by bright red or orange berries. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value lies in its attractive foliage and seasonal floral display.
