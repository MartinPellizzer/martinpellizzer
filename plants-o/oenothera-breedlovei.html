Oenothera breedlovei

Oenothera breedlovei, commonly known as the Chisos morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Chisos Mountains in Texas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the early morning. The plant has hairy stems and leaves with a distinctive, slightly serrated edge. Its flowers are typically pink to purple in color and attract pollinators such as bees and moths.

Oenothera breedlovei is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils.
