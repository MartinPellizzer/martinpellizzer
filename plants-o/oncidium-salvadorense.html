Oncidium salvadorense

Oncidium salvadorense, commonly known as the Salvadoran orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, long-lasting flowers that typically come in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually broad and grass-like, and it thrives in bright, indirect light with high humidity.

Oncidium salvadorense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.
