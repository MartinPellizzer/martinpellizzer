Ophrys × marsilii

Ophrys × marsilii, commonly known as the Marsilii bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the genus Ophrys, which is characterized by its intricate floral structures that mimic female insects to attract male pollinators. The plant produces showy, bee-like flowers with a distinctive pouch-shaped labellum that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. It typically grows in calcareous soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats.

Ophrys × marsilii is valued for its unique appearance and ecological significance in its native environment.
