Odontites viscosus var. commutatus

Odontites viscosus var. commutatus, commonly known as the sticky bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
