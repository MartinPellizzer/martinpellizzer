Ononis arvensis f. subrepens

Ononis arvensis f. subrepens, commonly known as creeping white knotweed, is a low-growing perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its prostrate growth habit.

It features slender, wiry stems that often lie close to the ground, forming dense mats. The leaves are typically lance-shaped, with a hairy surface and a slightly toothed margin. Its small, white to pale pink flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer.

This plant is often found in disturbed soils and is known for its ability to thrive in various environmental conditions.
