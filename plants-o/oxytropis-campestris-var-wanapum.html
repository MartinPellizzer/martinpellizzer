Oxytropis campestris var. wanapum

Oxytropis campestris var. wanapum, commonly known as the Wanapum milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

This species plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for wildlife.
