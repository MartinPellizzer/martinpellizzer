Ophrys × palenae

Ophrys × palenae, commonly known as the mimic orchid, is a hybrid species belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its intricate, bee-mimicking flowers that play a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant produces showy, often yellow or brownish, floral structures that resemble female bees, attracting male bees for mating. Its botanical features include a sympodial growth habit and specialized floral morphology.

This hybrid is typically found in Mediterranean regions, thriving in open, sunny habitats with well-drained soils.
