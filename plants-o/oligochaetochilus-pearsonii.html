Oligochaetochilus pearsonii

Oligochaetochilus pearsonii, commonly known as the Pearson's moss orchid, is a rare and delicate epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically growing on tree trunks or branches in tropical rainforests. The plant features small, linear leaves and produces intricate, fragrant flower spikes with white to pale pink blooms. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and limited distribution, making it a subject of interest for botanical research.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
