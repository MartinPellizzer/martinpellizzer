Ophiorrhiza confertiflora

Ophiorrhiza confertiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered orchid, is a tropical plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in tropical rainforests. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils, which is a common trait among orchids.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral display.
