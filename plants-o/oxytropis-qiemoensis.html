Oxytropis qiemoensis

Oxytropis qiemoensis, commonly known as the Qiemao locoweed, is a flowering plant native to the Qinghai-Tibet Plateau.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale blue or purple in color. It thrives in high-altitude environments with poor soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Oxytropis qiemoensis plays a role in local ecosystems and is of interest for its potential in ecological restoration projects.
