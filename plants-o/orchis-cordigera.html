Orchis cordigera

Orchis cordigera, commonly known as the heart-leaved orchid, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces tall, slender stems that bear clusters of violet or purple flowers with a unique, intricate structure. Orchis cordigera typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

Its blooming period usually occurs in late spring to early summer, making it a notable species in botanical gardens and natural habitats.
