Orthosiphon adornatus var. chlorococcus

Orthosiphon adornatus var. chlorococcus, commonly known as the green-stemmed holy basil, is a variant of the genus Orthosiphon native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green, with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale green or yellowish in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it distinct from other varieties within the same species.
