Ophrys speculum

Ophrys speculum, commonly known as the mirror orchid, is a unique orchid species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, bee-like appearance, with a flower that mimics the face of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and has a slender, erect stem with a single, showy flower. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, often clustered at the base of the plant.

The orchid's specialized morphology and pollination strategy make it a fascinating example of evolutionary adaptation in the plant kingdom.
