Oxalis viscidula

Oxalis viscidula, commonly known as sticky wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive sticky coating, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

Oxalis viscidula is often found in woodland areas and is considered a perennial herb.
