Opuntia kuehnrichiana

Opuntia kuehnrichiana, commonly known as the Mexican prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in sharp spines and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. Its fruit, known as a tunas, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

Opuntia kuehnrichiana plays an important ecological role by providing food and shelter for various desert animals.
