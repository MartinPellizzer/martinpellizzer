Ozoroa reticulata var. crispa

Ozoroa reticulata var. crispa, commonly known as the crinkled orris root, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, crinkled leaves and fragrant, violet-blue flowers. The plant produces a tuberous root that is used in the perfume industry for its essential oils. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ozoroa reticulata var. crispa is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its historical use in fragrance extraction.
