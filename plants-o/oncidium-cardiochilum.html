Oncidium cardiochilum

Oncidium cardiochilum, commonly known as the "Heart-lobed Orchid," is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, racemose inflorescence that produces numerous fragrant, butterfly-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad, fleshy, and often have a waxy texture to retain moisture.

Oncidium cardiochilum is valued in horticulture for its vibrant colors and long-lasting blooms.
