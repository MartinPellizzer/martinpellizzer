Osmunda regalis var. biformis

Osmunda regalis var. biformis, commonly known as the royal fern, is a variant of the royal fern characterized by its distinctive two-lobed fronds.

It is a member of the Osmundaceae family and is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant features a thick, horizontal rhizome that produces both sterile and fertile fronds. Its leaves are typically large, with a dark green, glossy appearance and a serrated margin.

This fern thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands, woodlands, and along stream banks.
