Octomeria pumila

Octomeria pumila, commonly known as the dwarf octomeria, is a small shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and grayish-green foliage, which helps it retain moisture in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch to its otherwise rugged appearance. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, which provide additional protection against intense sunlight and drought.

Octomeria pumila is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and hardy nature.
