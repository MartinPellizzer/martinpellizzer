Oenothera grandidentata

Oenothera grandidentata, commonly known as the showy evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has hairy, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins. Its flowers are typically 2 to 4 inches long and bloom from late spring to early summer.

Oenothera grandidentata is often found in dry, open habitats like prairies and woodland edges.
