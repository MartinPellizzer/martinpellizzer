Orthosiphon fruticosus

Orthosiphon fruticosus, commonly known as Java tea or Malabar tea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in spikes, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are aromatic and have been traditionally used in herbal remedies for their diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant's botanical features make it a valuable species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
