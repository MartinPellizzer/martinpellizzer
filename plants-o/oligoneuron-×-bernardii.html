Oligoneuron × bernardii

Oligoneuron × bernardii, commonly known as the hybrid lily, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between Oligoneuron caeruleum and Oligoneuron bernardii.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. It is a perennial herb with a fibrous root system and showy inflorescences.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is more commonly found in the wild.
