Ocotea ceronii

Ocotea ceronii, commonly known as the Ceronii Ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean, particularly found in the Greater Antilles.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and aromatic leaves that emit a subtle, spicy scent. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in the axils of the leaves, and it produces small, dark berries as fruit.

Ocotea ceronii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by local communities.
