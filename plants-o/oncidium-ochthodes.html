Oncidium ochthodes

Oncidium ochthodes, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow and green flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid environments. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in varying conditions.

Oncidium ochthodes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
