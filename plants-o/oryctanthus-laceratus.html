Oryctanthus laceratus

Oryctanthus laceratus, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its roots are often extensive, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor conditions.
