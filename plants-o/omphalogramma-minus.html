Omphalogramma minus

Omphalogramma minus, commonly known as the small heartleaf, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils in open woods and along stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped, with a rough texture and a slightly hairy surface.

The flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in a compact head, blooming in late spring to early summer.
