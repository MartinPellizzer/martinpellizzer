Opuntia altomagdalenensis

Opuntia altomagdalenensis, commonly known as the Magdalena prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems that are segmented and covered in spines. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers, which are typically followed by edible fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to hot, dry climates with minimal water requirements.

Opuntia altomagdalenensis is also known for its role in traditional medicine and as a source of food in local communities.
