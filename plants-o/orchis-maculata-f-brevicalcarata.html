Orchis maculata f. brevicalcarata

Orchis maculata f. brevicalcarata, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive spotted purple and green flowers, which bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It has a slender, erect stem with a few leaves and produces a cluster of flowers at the top.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
