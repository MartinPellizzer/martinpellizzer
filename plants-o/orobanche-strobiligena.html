Orobanche strobiligena

Orobanche strobiligena, commonly known as the Indian broomrape, is a parasitic plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is found in various parts of Asia, including India, China, and Southeast Asia. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has a short, fleshy stem with no true leaves. It is often found growing on the roots of grasses and other herbaceous plants.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can have significant impacts on agricultural crops.
