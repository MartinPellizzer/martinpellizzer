Oleandra cantonensis

Oleandra cantonensis, commonly known as the Canton oleander, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are typically ovate or elliptic in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Oleandra cantonensis is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive blooms.
