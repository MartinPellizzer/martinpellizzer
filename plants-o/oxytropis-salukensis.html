Oxytropis salukensis

Oxytropis salukensis, commonly known as Salukian locoweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand arid conditions. Its leaves are compound, with small leaflets that help reduce water loss.

Oxytropis salukensis is also known for its toxic alkaloids, which can affect livestock if ingested.
