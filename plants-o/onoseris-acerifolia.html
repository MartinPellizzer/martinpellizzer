Onoseris acerifolia

Onoseris acerifolia, commonly known as the maple-leaved dandelion, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive maple-like leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural practices.
