Oenothera dentata var. gilmanii

Oenothera dentata var. gilmanii, commonly known as the Gilman's evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, smooth seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed habitats like roadsides and sandy areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinator populations.
