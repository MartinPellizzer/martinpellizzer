Octomeria cariocana

Octomeria cariocana, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, dark wood with a rich, reddish-brown color. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth surface. It produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits.

Due to its valuable timber, it is also considered an endangered species in its native habitat.
