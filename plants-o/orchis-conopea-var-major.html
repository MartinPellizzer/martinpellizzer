Orchis conopea var. major

Orchis conopea var. major, commonly known as the greater purple orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers arranged in a dense spike, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it thrives in moist, calcareous soils.

This species is often found in woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological significance.
