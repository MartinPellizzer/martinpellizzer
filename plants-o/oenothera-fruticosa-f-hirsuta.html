Oenothera fruticosa f. hirsuta

Oenothera fruticosa f. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy prickly evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a shrub-like perennial with woody stems covered in dense, stiff hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

The species is known for its adaptability to arid environments and is often found in disturbed or rocky habitats.
