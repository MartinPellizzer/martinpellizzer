Ophrys insectifera var. aymoninii

Ophrys insectifera var. aymoninii, commonly known as the bee orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking floral structure that mimics the appearance of a female bee, a feature evolved to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish in color, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

This orchid is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its specialized pollination mechanism.
