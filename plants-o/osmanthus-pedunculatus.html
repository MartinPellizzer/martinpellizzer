Osmanthus pedunculatus

Osmanthus pedunculatus, commonly known as sweet osmanthus, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the autumn season. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy on top. Its flowers are widely used in traditional Chinese medicine and as a flavoring agent in teas and confections.

Osmanthus pedunculatus thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
