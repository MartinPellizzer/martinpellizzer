Ornithogalum refractum var. adalgisae

Ornithogalum refractum var. adalgisae, commonly known as the Adalgisa's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white or pale green flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
