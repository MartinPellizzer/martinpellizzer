Ocotea psychotrioides

Ocotea psychotrioides, commonly known as the "holy pepper" or "sacred pepper," is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant yellow flowers. Its fruit is a dark blue drupe that contains a single seed.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine and rituals.
