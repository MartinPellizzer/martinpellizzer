Oxalis burtoniae

Oxalis burtoniae, commonly known as the Burton's wood sorrel, is a perennial flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces small, bead-like fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Oxalis burtoniae thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and shady borders.
