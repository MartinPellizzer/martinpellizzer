Ocotea daphnoides

Ocotea daphnoides, commonly known as the "scented ocotea," is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a dark blue drupe, containing a single seed, and it is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.

Ocotea daphnoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
