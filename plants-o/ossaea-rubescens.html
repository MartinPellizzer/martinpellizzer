Ossaea rubescens

Ossaea rubescens, commonly known as the red sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive red, fleshy leaves and small, pinkish flowers. The plant thrives in sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are often used in traditional Aboriginal medicine for their purported healing properties.

Ossaea rubescens is an important component of the local flora in its native habitat.
