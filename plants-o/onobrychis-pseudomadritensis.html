Onobrychis pseudomadritensis

Onobrychis pseudomadritensis, commonly known as the Spanish needle or false vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, often found in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which are followed by slender, needle-like pods. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, heart-shaped leaflets.

Onobrychis pseudomadritensis is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable plant in ecological restoration projects.
