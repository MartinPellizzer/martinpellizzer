Ophrys × composita

Ophrys × composita, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Ophrys apifera (the bee orchid) and Ophrys insectifera (the wasp orchid), resulting in a unique combination of traits from both parents. The plant is characterized by its intricate, bee-like floral morphology, which plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of female bees, attracting male bees for pollination.

This species thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges across Europe.
