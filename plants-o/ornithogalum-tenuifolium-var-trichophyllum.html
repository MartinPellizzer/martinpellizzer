Ornithogalum tenuifolium var. trichophyllum

Ornithogalum tenuifolium var. trichophyllum, commonly known as the hairy leaf star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are softly hairy, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in gardens.
