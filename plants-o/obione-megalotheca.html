Obione megalotheca

Obione megalotheca, commonly known as the giant shellflower, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that resemble intricate shells, which give it its common name. The plant produces a single, elongated floral spike with numerous blooms, each featuring a unique, spiraled pattern. Native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Its botanical significance lies in its complex morphology and role in its native ecosystems.
