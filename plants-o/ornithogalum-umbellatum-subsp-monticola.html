Ornithogalum umbellatum subsp. monticola

Ornithogalum umbellatum subsp.

monticola, commonly known as the mountain star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It features a basal rosette of lanceolate, glaucous leaves and produces a tall, leafless stem bearing a umbellate inflorescence of white, star-shaped flowers.

The flowers are typically 5-lobed and have a subtle fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its distinctive floral display.
