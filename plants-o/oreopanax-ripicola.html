Oreopanax ripicola

Oreopanax ripicola, commonly known as the cliff fern, is a unique plant species native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and the presence of aerial roots that allow it to attach to rocky surfaces. The plant features pinnately compound leaves with a feathery appearance, which helps in maximizing light absorption in its shaded habitat. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, typically clustered in inflorescences, and it produces berries that are an important food source for local wildlife.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, Oreopanax ripicola is often found in humid, shaded environments at higher elevations.
