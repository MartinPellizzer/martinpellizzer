Ophrys × fernandii

Ophrys × fernandii, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant that exhibits unique botanical features.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Ophrys apifera (the bee orchid) and Ophrys insectifera (the wasp orchid), resulting in a blend of characteristics from both parents. The plant is notable for its intricate floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee or wasp to attract male pollinators. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow with intricate patterns that resemble insect bodies, enhancing its mimicry.

This orchid thrives in specific habitats, often in woodland or grassland areas with well-drained soils, and is found primarily in southern Europe.
