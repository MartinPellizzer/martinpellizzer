Oxalis gracilis

Oxalis gracilis, commonly known as the slender wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It features delicate, trifoliate leaves that are often tinged with red or purple veins, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Oxalis gracilis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to spread via rhizomes, forming dense ground cover.
