Oldenlandia hirsuta var. glabrior

Oldenlandia hirsuta var. glabrior, commonly known as the smooth oldenlandia, is a variant of the Oldenlandia hirsuta species, characterized by its reduced hairy coverage compared to the typical form.

It is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions, often found in shaded, moist environments. The plant features small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender stems. Its leaves are usually ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface depending on the variety.

The botanical interest of this plant lies in its morphological variation and its role in local ecosystems.
