Orchis maculata f. obtusifolia

Orchis maculata f. obtusifolia, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a variant of the common twayblade orchid.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features a rosette of lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture. Its flowers are typically pale green to yellowish, with a distinctive purple or brownish marking on the lip.

This subspecies is often found in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or meadow habitats.
