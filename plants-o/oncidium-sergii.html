Oncidium sergii

Oncidium sergii, commonly known as the Serov's orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, fragrant flowers. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences with showy, often yellow or orange-colored blooms. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air and rain, typical of epiphytic species.

Oncidium sergii is relatively easy to cultivate, making it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.
