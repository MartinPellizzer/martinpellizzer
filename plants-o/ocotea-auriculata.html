Ocotea auriculata

Ocotea auriculata, commonly known as the ear-shaped ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves with a waxy texture and a distinctive auriculate (ear-shaped) base. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in axillary or terminal inflorescences.

The tree produces small, dark purple berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species.
