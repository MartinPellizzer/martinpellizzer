Ocotea kenyensis

Ocotea kenyensis, commonly known as Kenyan ocotea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical rainforests of East Africa.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurel, avocado, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered white flowers. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns from green to yellow or red when mature.

Ocotea kenyensis is valued for its potential medicinal properties and is often found in shaded, humid environments within its natural range.
