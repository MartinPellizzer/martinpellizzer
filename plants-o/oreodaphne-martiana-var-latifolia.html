Oreodaphne martiana var. latifolia

Oreodaphne martiana var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed oreodaphne, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in Malaysia and Indonesia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves that are broad and pointed. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, followed by oval-shaped fruits containing single seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The leaves and bark of this plant have been traditionally used in local herbal medicine for their potential medicinal properties.
