Orchis ventricosa

Orchis ventricosa, commonly known as the broad-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and thrives in woodland or meadow habitats. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and it produces a single spike of flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Orchis ventricosa is known for its ornamental value and is often found in conservation areas due to its ecological importance.
