Oenothera glabella

Oenothera glabella, commonly known as the evening primrose, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening, hence its common name. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Oenothera glabella thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as prairies and roadsides.

It is also valued for its oil-rich seeds, which have various medicinal and industrial uses.
