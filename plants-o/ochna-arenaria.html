Ochna arenaria

Ochna arenaria, commonly known as the desert ochna, is a small, evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and clusters of small, fragrant yellow flowers. The plant produces bright red, fleshy fruits that are adapted for dispersal by animals. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to conserve water.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies of drought-resistant flora.
