Opoidia

Opoidia, commonly known as false hellebore, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Polygonaceae.

These plants are characterized by their alternate, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish flowers. Opoidia species are typically found in temperate regions and are often cultivated for their ornamental value. They have a fibrous root system and produce berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

The genus includes several species, such as Opoidia tenuifolia, which is native to parts of Europe and Asia.
