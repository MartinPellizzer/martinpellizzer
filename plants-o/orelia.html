Orelia

Orelia, commonly known as the white lily, is a flowering plant belonging to the Liliaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. Orelia has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
