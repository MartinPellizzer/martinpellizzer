Oxalis neaei var. glabrata

Oxalis neaei var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth oxalis, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically bright green and have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often appearing in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Oxalis neaei var. glabrata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and greenhouses due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
