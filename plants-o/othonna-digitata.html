Othonna digitata

Othonna digitata, commonly known as the fingered othonna, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and fleshy, ribbed leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are often adorned with distinctive markings, giving it a unique appearance.

Othonna digitata is well adapted to arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry conditions.
