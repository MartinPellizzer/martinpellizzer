Olea capensis

Olea capensis, commonly known as the Mediterranean olive, is a small evergreen tree native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, dark purple to black fruits known as olives, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications. Olea capensis thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, sunny climate.

It is also valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in traditional medicine.
