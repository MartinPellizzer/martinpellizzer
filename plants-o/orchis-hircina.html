Orchis hircina

Orchis hircina, commonly known as the goat orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem that can reach up to 1 meter in height, bearing several leaves along its length. The plant produces a spike of showy, violet to purple flowers with a distinctive, hooded lip. It typically blooms in late spring to early summer, and its flowers are pollinated by various insect species, including bees and flies.

Orchis hircina is native to Europe and is often found in grassy meadows and woodland edges.
