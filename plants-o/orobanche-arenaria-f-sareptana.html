Orobanche arenaria f. sareptana

Orobanche arenaria f. sareptana, commonly known as the Sareptan broomrape, is a parasitic plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on its host for nutrients.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is found in various regions of Europe and Asia. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its life cycle involves germinating in response to chemical signals from host plants, usually members of the Fabaceae family.

The flowers are small and pale, often clustered in a spike-like inflorescence.
