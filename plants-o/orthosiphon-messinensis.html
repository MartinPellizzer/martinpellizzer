Orthosiphon messinensis

Orthosiphon messinensis, commonly known as the Mediterranean horseweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its aromatic leaves and medicinal properties have made it of interest in traditional herbal practices.
