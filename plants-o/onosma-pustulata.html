Onosma pustulata

Onosma pustulata, commonly known as the spotted poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which often have a distinctive spotted or pustular appearance. The plant produces showy, papery flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Onosma pustulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique visual characteristics.
