Oenothera jacquinii

Oenothera jacquinii, commonly known as the Jacquin's evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and often covered in fine hairs.

Oenothera jacquinii is part of the Onagraceae family, which includes other evening primroses and related species.
