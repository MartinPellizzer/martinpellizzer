Ophrys × saratoi subsp. gelmii

Ophrys × saratoi subsp.

gelmii, commonly known as the Gelmi orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the genus Ophrys, which is known for its mimicry of female insects to attract male pollinators.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its flowers are characterized by their intricate, bee-like appearance, with a prominent lip that mimics a female bee's abdomen.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique and striking floral display.
