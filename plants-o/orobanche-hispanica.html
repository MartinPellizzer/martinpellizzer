Orobanche hispanica

Orobanche hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish broom.

It is a parasitic plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on its host for nutrients. This species typically parasitizes members of the legume family, such as Vicia and Astragalus. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a tubular shape and a two-lipped appearance.

The plant is found in various regions of Europe and parts of North Africa, often in disturbed or open habitats.
