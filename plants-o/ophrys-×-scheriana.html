Ophrys × scheriana

Ophrys × scheriana, commonly known as the orchid bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Balkan Peninsula, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces intricate flowers with a specialized structure that mimics female wasps, aiding in its pollination strategy. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow with dark markings, and they emit a scent that attracts male wasps.

This orchid is notable for its complex hybrid origin, combining traits from both Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.
