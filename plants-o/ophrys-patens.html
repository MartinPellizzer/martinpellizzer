Ophrys patens

Ophrys patens, commonly known as the showy orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive floral structure. The plant produces a single, showy flower with a lip that resembles a female bee, aiding in its unique pollination strategy. It thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats.

Ophrys patens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinator biodiversity.
