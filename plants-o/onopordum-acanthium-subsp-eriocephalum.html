Onopordum acanthium subsp. eriocephalum

Onopordum acanthium subsp.

eriocephalum, commonly known as the yellow star-thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features a robust taproot system and upright stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall.

The plant is characterized by its yellow flower heads, which are surrounded by spiny bracts and are composed of many small florets. Its leaves are typically lobed and covered with fine hairs, and it produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is considered an invasive species in some regions.
