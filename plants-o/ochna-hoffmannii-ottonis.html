Ochna hoffmannii-ottonis

Ochna hoffmannii-ottonis, commonly known as the Otto's ochna, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are often leathery in texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in terminal inflorescences, followed by orange to red berries containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its attractive foliage and seasonal flowering.
