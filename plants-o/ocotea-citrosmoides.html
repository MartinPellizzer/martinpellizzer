Ocotea citrosmoides

Ocotea citrosmoides, commonly known as the lemon-scented ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves that emit a citrus-like fragrance when crushed. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, and it produces drupes that are typically yellow or orange in color.

Ocotea citrosmoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique scent and foliage.
