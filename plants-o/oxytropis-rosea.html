Oxytropis rosea

Oxytropis rosea, commonly known as the pink burrweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, pink to purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces burr-like pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to thrive in dry conditions, often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

Its growth habit and floral structure make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
