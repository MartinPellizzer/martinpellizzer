Ophrys fusca subsp. eptapigiensis

Ophrys fusca subsp.

eptapigiensis, commonly known as the Greek bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Greece. It is characterized by its unique floral morphology, featuring a striking mimicry of a female bee, which plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy.

The plant produces a single, showy flower with a dark brown to black coloration, often adorned with intricate patterns that resemble insect features. It thrives in calcareous soils and is typically found in open, sunny habitats at higher elevations.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species.
