Ophrys × kallista

Ophrys × kallista, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant that results from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is characterized by its intricate floral structure, which mimics the appearance of female bees to attract male pollinators. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brownish in color, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination.

Ophrys × kallista is a perennial herb that produces a single spike of flowers, showcasing the unique adaptations of orchids for specialized pollination strategies.
