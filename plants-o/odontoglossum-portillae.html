Odontoglossum portillae

Odontoglossum portillae, commonly known as the Portilla's orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb and linear leaves that grow from the base. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and violet, typically during the spring and early summer. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Odontoglossum portillae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
