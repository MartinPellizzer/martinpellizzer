Oxalis anthelmintica var. glanduligera

Oxalis anthelmintica var. glanduligera, commonly known as the glandular wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish or purple coloration, often adorned with small, glandular dots. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers with five petals, typical of the Oxalidaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
