Ochna cordata

Ochna cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf ochna, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. Ochna cordata is often found in woodland and rocky habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
