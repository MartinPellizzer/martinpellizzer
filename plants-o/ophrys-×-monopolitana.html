Ophrys × monopolitana

Ophrys × monopolitana, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant in the Orchidaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Ophrys apifera (the bee orchid) and Ophrys insectifera (the wasp orchid). This plant is notable for its intricate floral morphology, which closely resembles insects, aiding in pollination by mimicking the appearance and scent of female bees or wasps. The flowers typically feature a prominent lip that mimics the body of an insect, along with specialized structures that release fragrances to attract pollinators.

Ophrys × monopolitana is often found in open, sunny habitats across Europe, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
