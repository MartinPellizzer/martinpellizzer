Oryza sativa var. kolakovskyi

Oryza sativa var. kolakovskyi, commonly known as the Kolakovsky rice, is a rare and ancient variety of rice native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant grains and is often grown in wetland environments. This variety is notable for its genetic diversity and is considered a potential source of traits for modern rice breeding programs. Its botanical features include a short stature, thick stems, and a high yield under traditional farming practices.

Due to its unique characteristics, it holds significance in both agricultural research and cultural heritage.
