Origanum nervosum

Origanum nervosum, commonly known as narrow-leaved oregano, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of purple or white flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in temperate climates. Its essential oils, rich in compounds like carvacrol, contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.

Origanum nervosum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators.
