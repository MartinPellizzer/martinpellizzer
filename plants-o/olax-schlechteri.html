Olax schlechteri

Olax schlechteri, commonly known as the African olive, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Olacaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and showing some degree of drought tolerance.

The plant has historical uses in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
