Oxalis rhombeo-ovata

Oxalis rhombeo-ovata, commonly known as the heartleaf wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive white or pinkish veining, and small, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a ground cover.

Oxalis rhombeo-ovata is also known for its ability to tolerate a variety of soil conditions, making it a versatile choice for landscaping.
