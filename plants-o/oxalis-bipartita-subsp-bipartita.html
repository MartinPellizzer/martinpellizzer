Oxalis bipartita subsp. bipartita

Oxalis bipartita subsp.

bipartita, commonly known as the two-lobed wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped lobes and produces small, yellowish flowers with five petals.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and woodlands. Its stems are often hairy, and it has a fibrous root system.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
