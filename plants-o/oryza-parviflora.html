Oryza parviflora

Oryza parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered ricegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, white or greenish flowers in panicles. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Oryza parviflora is valued for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity and its potential use in sustainable agriculture.
