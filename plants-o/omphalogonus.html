Omphalogonus

Omphalogonus, commonly known as the starfruit or carambola, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Oxalidaceae and is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped fruit when sliced. The plant typically grows as a small shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with opposite, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and white, and the fruit develops from a single ovary, turning from green to yellow or orange as it matures.

The fruit is rich in vitamin C and is widely cultivated for its unique flavor and nutritional value.
