Oxalis recurva var. floridana

Oxalis recurva var. floridana, commonly known as the Florida wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves composed of three heart-shaped leaflets, often with a reddish or purple tinge. The plant produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and attractive addition to native plant gardens.
