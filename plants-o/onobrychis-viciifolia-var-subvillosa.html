Onobrychis viciifolia var. subvillosa

Onobrychis viciifolia var. subvillosa, commonly known as the silky burr vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with soft, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is often found in dry, calcareous soils and is adapted to arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
