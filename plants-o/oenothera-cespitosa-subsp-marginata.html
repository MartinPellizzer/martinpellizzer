Oenothera cespitosa subsp. marginata

Oenothera cespitosa subsp.

marginata, commonly known as the fringed evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America. It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and yellow flowers that open in the evening.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, elliptic seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as prairies and roadsides.

The subspecies is distinguished by its narrow, fringed leaves and the presence of a white margin on the leaf blades.
