Oxalis montana f. rhodantha

Oxalis montana f. rhodantha, commonly known as the red-stemmed wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive red or purple stem, and its flowers are typically yellow with five petals. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens. It produces small, edible berries that are often consumed by wildlife.

The botanical characteristics of Oxalis montana f. rhodantha make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable.
