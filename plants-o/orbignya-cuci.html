Orbignya cuci

Orbignya cuci, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon basin.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves and produces clusters of woody fruits containing edible seeds. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall and has a deep root system that helps stabilize the soil. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, relying on insects for pollination.

The plant plays a significant ecological role in its habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
