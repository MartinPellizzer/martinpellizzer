Osmanthus rotundifolius

Osmanthus rotundifolius, commonly known as sweet osmanthus, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its rounded, leathery leaves and fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, orange-red fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its aromatic properties.

Osmanthus rotundifolius is widely used in traditional Chinese medicine and as a source of essential oils.
