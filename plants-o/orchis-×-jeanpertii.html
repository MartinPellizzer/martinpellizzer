Orchis × jeanpertii

Orchis × jeanpertii, commonly known as the Jeanpert's orchid, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Orchis morio and Orchis pallidula.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe, particularly in the Mediterranean region. The plant produces tall flowering stems that bear showy, violet-colored flowers with a distinctive hooded structure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette at the base of the plant.

The botanical features of Orchis × jeanpertii make it a notable example of natural hybridization in the Orchidaceae family.
