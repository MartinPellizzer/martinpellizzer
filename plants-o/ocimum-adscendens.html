Ocimum adscendens

Ocimum adscendens, commonly known as sacred basil or holy basil, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and lavender. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. Ocimum adscendens is often cultivated for its fragrant leaves used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
