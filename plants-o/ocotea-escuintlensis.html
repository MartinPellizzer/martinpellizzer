Ocotea escuintlensis

Ocotea escuintlensis, commonly known as the Mexican laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and smooth, dark bark. The plant typically grows in humid lowland forests and is known for its ornamental value. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue berries.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
