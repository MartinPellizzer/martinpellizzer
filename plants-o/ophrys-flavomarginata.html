Ophrys flavomarginata

Ophrys flavomarginata, commonly known as the yellow-margin helleborine, is a perennial orchid native to parts of Europe and Western Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and distinctive flowers that resemble female wasps, a feature that aids in its pollination strategy. The flowers have a yellow margin on the lip, which is a key identifying trait. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

This orchid is known for its specialized relationship with specific pollinators, enhancing its ecological significance.
