Ononis pendula f. minor

Ononis pendula f. minor, commonly known as the dwarf trailing sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It features slender, creeping stems that often cascade over rocky surfaces or sandy soils. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a delicate floral display. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to conserve water.

This variety is a hardy, drought-tolerant plant well-suited for xeriscaping and rocky garden environments.
