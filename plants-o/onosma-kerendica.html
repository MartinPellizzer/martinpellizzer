Onosma kerendica

Onosma kerendica, commonly known as the Kashmir wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are often hairy and linear, and it produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Onosma kerendica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bright, cheerful blooms.
