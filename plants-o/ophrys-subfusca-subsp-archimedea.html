Ophrys subfusca subsp. archimedea

Ophrys subfusca subsp.

archimedea, commonly known as the Greek bee orchid, is a species of orchid native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee, a trait evolved to attract male bees for pollination.

The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its flowers are usually pale green to brown in color, with intricate patterns that enhance the mimicry.

This orchid is a member of the Ophrys genus, known for its specialized pollination mechanisms and ecological significance.
