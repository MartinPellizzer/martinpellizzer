Odontonema galbanum

Odontonema galbanum, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its showy, orange-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture and a distinctive aromatic scent. Its roots and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

Odontonema galbanum thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
