Opulea acaulis

Opulea acaulis, commonly known as the naked-stemmed spurge, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or arid environments. The plant features a smooth, unbranched stem that is often covered in a waxy coating. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Opulea acaulis is adapted to survive in harsh conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
