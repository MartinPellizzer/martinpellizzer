Orchis × gelmiana

Orchis × gelmiana, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a cross between Orchis morio and Orchis mascula.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family. The plant produces tall, slender stems with whorled leaves and clusters of showy, violet or purple flowers. Its floral structure includes three sepals, three petals, and a distinctive lip that aids in pollination.

This hybrid is often found in moist meadows and woodland edges across Europe.
