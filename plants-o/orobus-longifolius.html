Orobus longifolius

Orobus longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved orobus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand arid conditions. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a distinctive blue or purple coloration.

Orobus longifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
