Onosma pseudoarenaria

Onosma pseudoarenaria, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.

Onosma pseudoarenaria is often found in rocky soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
