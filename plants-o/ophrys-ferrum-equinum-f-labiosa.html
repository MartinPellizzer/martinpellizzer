Ophrys ferrum-equinum f. labiosa

Ophrys ferrum-equinum f. labiosa, commonly known as the Red Bee Orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to specific regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking, bee-like flowers that mimic the appearance and scent of female bees to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in nutrient-poor, calcareous soils in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and heaths. Its flowers are usually a combination of red and brown hues, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, this orchid is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
