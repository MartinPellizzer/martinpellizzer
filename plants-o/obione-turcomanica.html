Obione turcomanica

Obione turcomanica, commonly known as the Turcoman bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, helping to reduce water loss.

Obione turcomanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
