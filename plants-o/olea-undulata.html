Olea undulata

Olea undulata, commonly known as the wavy-leaf olive, is a species of flowering plant in the Oleaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in rocky and sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its narrow, wavy-edged leaves and small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Olea undulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to withstand dry conditions.

It is also valued for its hardy nature and suitability for coastal gardens.
