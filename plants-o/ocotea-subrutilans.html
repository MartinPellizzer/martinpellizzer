Ocotea subrutilans

Ocotea subrutilans, commonly known as the golden ocotea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in countries like Costa Rica and Panama. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and small, clustered white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruits are small, dark berries that contain aromatic seeds.

Ocotea subrutilans is often found in humid, lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
