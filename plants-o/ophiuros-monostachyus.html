Ophiuros monostachyus

Ophiuros monostachyus, commonly known as the single-stemmed sea oat, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune ecosystems. Its fibrous roots help stabilize coastal dunes, making it an important species for erosion control.

Ophiuros monostachyus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in coastal gardens.
