Ornithogalum massonii

Ornithogalum massonii, commonly known as the African star of Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is characterized by its underground bulb, which serves as a storage organ for nutrients. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ornithogalum massonii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
