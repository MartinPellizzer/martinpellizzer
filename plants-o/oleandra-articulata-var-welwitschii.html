Oleandra articulata var. welwitschii

Oleandra articulata var. welwitschii, commonly known as the Welwitsch oleander, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for coastal and arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
