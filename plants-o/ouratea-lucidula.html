Ouratea lucidula

Ouratea lucidula, commonly known as the shining ouratea, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often arranged in whorls at the nodes. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by smooth, dark purple to black berries. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
