Onosma alborosea var. macrocalycina

Onosma alborosea var. macrocalycina, commonly known as the Persian poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, large, pale pink to white petals that resemble those of the true poppies. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
