Oberonia multidentata

Oberonia multidentata, commonly known as the toothed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

This epiphytic plant typically grows on trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its leaves are elongated and often have a waxy texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, tooth-like projections on the petals and sepals.

It is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and ability to thrive in specific tropical conditions.
