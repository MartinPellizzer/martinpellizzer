Oreodaphne glauca

Oreodaphne glauca, commonly known as the blue-leafed daphne, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, glaucous-blue leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible, round fruits. Oreodaphne glauca thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in shaded garden settings.
