Orchis pseudosambucina var. natalis

Orchis pseudosambucina var. natalis, commonly known as the Natal orchid, is a species of orchid native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers, which bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are erect and bear the showy inflorescence.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
