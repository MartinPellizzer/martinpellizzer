Ochna polyarthra

Ochna polyarthra, commonly known as the African velvetleaf, is a small to medium-sized shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by distinctive, velvety, orange-red fruits. Its leaves and fruits are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
