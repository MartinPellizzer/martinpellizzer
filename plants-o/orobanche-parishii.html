Orobanche parishii

Orobanche parishii, commonly known as the parishes broomrape, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is native to regions of southern Africa, including South Africa and Namibia. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers and has a distinctive, branched stem covered in tiny, scale-like leaves. It typically parasitizes members of the Fabaceae family, such as vetches and other legumes.
