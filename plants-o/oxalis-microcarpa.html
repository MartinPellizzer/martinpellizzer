Oxalis microcarpa

Oxalis microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of five-petaled yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is a perennial shrub that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.

Oxalis microcarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
