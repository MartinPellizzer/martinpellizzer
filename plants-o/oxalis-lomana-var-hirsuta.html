Oxalis lomana var. hirsuta

Oxalis lomana var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Oxalis lomana var. hirsuta is often grown as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and delicate flowers.
