Odontoglossum hookeri

Odontoglossum hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the high Andes of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts worldwide.
