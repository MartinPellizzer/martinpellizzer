Ocimum thymoides

Ocimum thymoides, commonly known as wild basil, is a member of the Lamiaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or white. The plant has square stems and opposite leaves that are usually ovate to lanceolate in shape. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ocimum thymoides is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb due to its pungent, spicy aroma.
