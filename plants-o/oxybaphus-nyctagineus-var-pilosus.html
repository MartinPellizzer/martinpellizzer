Oxybaphus nyctagineus var. pilosus

Oxybaphus nyctagineus var. pilosus, commonly known as the hairy nightshade, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or violet in color. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and also covered with a dense layer of soft hairs.

This species is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.
