Onopordum carmanicum

Onopordum carmanicum, commonly known as the Carmel thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features spiny, lobed leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is characterized by its robust growth and ability to thrive in dry, rocky soils. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
