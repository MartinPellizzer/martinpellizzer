Oxalis obtusa

Oxalis obtusa, commonly known as the blunt-leaf wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas and stream banks. Oxalis obtusa has a fibrous root system and produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds.

Its botanical features make it a common species in native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
