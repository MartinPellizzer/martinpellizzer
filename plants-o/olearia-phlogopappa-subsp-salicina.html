Olearia phlogopappa subsp. salicina

Olearia phlogopappa subsp.

salicina, commonly known as the silverleaf tree fern, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk covered in silvery-white, scale-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by fluffy, white seed heads. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in coastal forests.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
