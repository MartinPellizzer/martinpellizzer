Ozothamnus thomsonii

Ozothamnus thomsonii, commonly known as Thomson's goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters, which bloom in spring. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, helping to reduce water loss.

Ozothamnus thomsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native gardens.
