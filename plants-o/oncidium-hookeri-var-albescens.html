Oncidium hookeri var. albescens

Oncidium hookeri var. albescens, commonly known as the white-flowered orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking white or pale yellow flowers with a distinctive greenish-brown lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are known for their long-lasting display, often lasting several weeks.

The plant requires bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.
