Ourisia macrocarpa subsp. calycina

Ourisia macrocarpa subsp.

calycina, commonly known as the coastal ourisia, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, tubular flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to orange and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging environments.
