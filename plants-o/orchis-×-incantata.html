Orchis × incantata

Orchis × incantata, commonly known as the fairy orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Orchis morio and Orchis mascula.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe, particularly the British Isles. The plant produces striking violet or purple flowers with a distinctive hooded structure, typical of many orchid species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and meadows.

The botanical features of Orchis × incantata make it a subject of interest for both horticulturists and botanists.
