Oenothera lavandulifolia var. typica

Oenothera lavandulifolia var. typica, commonly known as the lavenderflower evening primrose, is a species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, lavender-colored flowers that bloom at dusk. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for xeriscaping.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by moths and bats, reflecting its nocturnal blooming habit.
