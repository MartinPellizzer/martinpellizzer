Oeosporangium szovitsii

Oeosporangium szovitsii, commonly known as the Hungarian spore plant, is a rare and specialized member of the Equisetopsida.

It is characterized by its unique reproductive structures, including the presence of specialized sporangia that produce spores. The plant exhibits a primitive morphology, with a segmented stem and whorled leaves. Its spores are typically released in a specific seasonal pattern, contributing to its limited distribution.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is of interest to botanists studying ancient plant lineages.
