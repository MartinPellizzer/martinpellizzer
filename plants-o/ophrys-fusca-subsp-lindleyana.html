Ophrys fusca subsp. lindleyana

Ophrys fusca subsp.

lindleyana, commonly known as the Lindley's bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to parts of southern Europe. It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee, a feature evolved to attract male bees for pollination.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are pendulous and often have a distinctive yellowish-green coloration.

This subspecies is of particular interest to botanists due to its ecological specificity and conservation status.
