Oriastrum stuebelii var. cryptum

Oriastrum stuebelii var. cryptum, commonly known as the cryptum variety of Stuebel's globemallow, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with narrow, hairy leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and drought-tolerant addition to native plant gardens.
