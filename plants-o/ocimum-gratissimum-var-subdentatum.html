Ocimum gratissimum var. subdentatum

Ocimum gratissimum var. subdentatum, commonly known as African basil or wild basil, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and square stems. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with serrated margins, and emit a strong, pungent scent when crushed. This plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its flowers are small and clustered in whorls, producing nectar that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.
