Otites cyri

Otites cyri, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Otites cyri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
