Oxera neriifolia var. artensis

Oxera neriifolia var. artensis, commonly known as the Artens Island oxera, is a flowering plant native to the island of Artens in the South Pacific.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers clustered in umbels. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. This variant is distinguished by its unique floral structure and habitat specificity, often growing in coastal or sandy soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
