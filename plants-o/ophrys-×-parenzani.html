Ophrys × parenzani

Ophrys × parenzani, commonly known as the Parenzan orchid, is a hybrid orchid species that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

It belongs to the genus Ophrys, which is known for its mimicry of female insects to attract male pollinators. The plant typically produces showy, bee-mimicking flowers that are rich in nectar and have a distinctive coloration. Its floral structure includes a labellum that resembles an insect's body, aiding in pollination through deception.

This species is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils.
