Olea ilicifolia

Olea ilicifolia, commonly known as the evergreen holly-oleaster, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, leathery leaves that are often dark green with a glossy appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by edible berries, which are typically red when mature. Olea ilicifolia is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas and scrublands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
