Orchis picta f. trimaculata

Orchis picta f. trimaculata, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy flowers that display a mix of purple, white, and yellow hues. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its flowers are bilaterally symmetrical, with a distinctive lip that is often marked with spots or lines.

This orchid is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
