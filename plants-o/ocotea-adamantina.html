Ocotea adamantina

Ocotea adamantina, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree or *Pimenta-d'Angola*, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, white, clustered flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture-making.

Ocotea adamantina thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.
