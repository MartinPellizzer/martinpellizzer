Ocimum knyanum

Ocimum knyanum, commonly known as Kyan's basil, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like mint and lavender. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to violet. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a strong, spicy aroma.

Ocimum knyanum is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
