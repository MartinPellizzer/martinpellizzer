Orobus styriacus

Orobus styriacus, commonly known as the Styrian orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant has a short, rhizomatous root system and produces linear, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellowish with a distinctive lip petal.

Orobus styriacus thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
