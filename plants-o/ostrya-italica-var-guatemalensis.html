Ostrya italica var. guatemalensis

Ostrya italica var. guatemalensis, commonly known as the Guatemalan hop-hornbeam, is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family, which includes birches, alders, and hazels. The tree is characterized by its finely toothed, ovate leaves and small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers that appear in catkins. Its fruits are small, winged samaras that are dispersed by wind.

The plant is often found in moist, well-drained soils in lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
