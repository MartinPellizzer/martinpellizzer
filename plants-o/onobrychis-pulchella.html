Onobrychis pulchella

Onobrychis pulchella, commonly known as the pretty burr vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale yellow to reddish in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a bushy growth habit.

Onobrychis pulchella is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
