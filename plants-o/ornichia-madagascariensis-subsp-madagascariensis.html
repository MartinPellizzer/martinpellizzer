Ornichia madagascariensis subsp. madagascariensis

Ornichia madagascariensis subsp.

madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Madagascar. It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, erect stems and lanceolate leaves.

The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, with a distinctive lip petal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its restricted range and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
