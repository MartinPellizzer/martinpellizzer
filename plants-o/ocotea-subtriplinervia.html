Ocotea subtriplinervia

Ocotea subtriplinervia, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and scent. The plant has simple, alternate, elliptical leaves with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in umbels, while the fruit is a small, woody drupe.

Due to its ornamental value and wood quality, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and used in traditional medicine.
