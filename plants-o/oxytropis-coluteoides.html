Oxytropis coluteoides

Oxytropis coluteoides, commonly known as yellow milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Oxytropis coluteoides is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it ecologically significant in its native range.
