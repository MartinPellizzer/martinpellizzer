Ornithogalum puberulum subsp. chris-bayeri

Ornithogalum puberulum subsp.

chris-bayeri, commonly known as Chris-Bayer's ornithogalum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often tinged with purple at the base.

The plant produces umbels of white or pale green flowers, which bloom in spring and are followed by small, spherical berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate flowers.
