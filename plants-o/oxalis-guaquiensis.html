Oxalis guaquiensis

Oxalis guaquiensis, commonly known as the Guaqui oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family, characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its leaves are often adorned with striking red or yellow veins, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Oxalis guaquiensis is valued for its aesthetic qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
