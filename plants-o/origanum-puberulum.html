Origanum puberulum

Origanum puberulum, commonly known as woolly oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its gray-green, fuzzy leaves and small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes. Its aromatic foliage contains essential oils that contribute to its use in culinary and medicinal applications.

Origanum puberulum is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.
