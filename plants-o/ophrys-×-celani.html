Ophrys × celani

Ophrys × celani, commonly known as the Celan orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the genus Ophrys, which is known for its mimicry of female insects to attract male pollinators. The plant produces intricate, bee-mimicking flowers that are typically yellow or brown in color. It thrives in rocky or calcareous soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

Due to its specialized pollination mechanism, it plays a unique role in its ecosystem.
