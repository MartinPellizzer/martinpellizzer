Ophiorrhiza argostemmoides

Ophiorrhiza argostemmoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved marsh hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in wet, swampy areas and coastal regions. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually narrow and slightly hairy. Its flowers are small, clustered, and often have five petals, with colors ranging from white to pale yellow.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds, aiding in its dispersal in moist environments.
