Oberonia crassirachis

Oberonia crassirachis, commonly known as the thick-veined orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its intricate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as forests and undergrowth. Its leaves are usually broad and strap-like, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
