Olyra standleyi

Olyra standleyi, commonly known as the African wild fig, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, greenish flowers followed by edible fig-like fruits that are an important food source for various animals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest margins or along riverbanks.

Olyra standleyi is valued for its ecological role and has traditional uses in some African communities.
