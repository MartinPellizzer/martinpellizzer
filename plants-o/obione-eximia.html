Obione eximia

Obione eximia, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, pale green to silver-blue flowers. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence that gives the plant its common name. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Obione eximia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and specialized collections.
