Ormocarpum trichocarpum

Ormocarpum trichocarpum, commonly known as the hairy fruit orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, hairy fruiting structures that distinguish it from other orchid species. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often displaying a range of colors including white, yellow, and green.

The botanical features of Ormocarpum trichocarpum make it an interesting subject for study in both horticulture and ecological research.
