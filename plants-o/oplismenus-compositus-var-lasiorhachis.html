Oplismenus compositus var. lasiorhachis

Oplismenus compositus var. lasiorhachis, commonly known as the silky horse-tail, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage with a distinctive silvery-blue hue. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, forming dense mats in moist, shaded environments.

The leaves are narrow, linear, and have a soft, silky texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal in certain landscapes.
