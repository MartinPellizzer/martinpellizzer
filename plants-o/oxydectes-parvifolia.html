Oxydectes parvifolia

Oxydectes parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaf oxydectes, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth, waxy surface with a narrow, lance-shaped appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.
