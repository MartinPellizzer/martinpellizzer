Oryza sativa var. masenderano

Oryza sativa var. masenderano, commonly known as the Masenderano rice, is a rare and ancient variety of rice native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its short, slender grains and a unique aroma that sets it apart from other rice types. This variety is often cultivated in small quantities due to its specific growing conditions and lower yield compared to modern rice strains. Botanically, it exhibits a complex genetic makeup that includes traits of both wild and domesticated rice species.

Its study provides valuable insights into the evolutionary history and genetic diversity of rice plants.
