Odontoglossum ramosissimum f. xanthinum

Odontoglossum ramosissimum f. xanthinum, commonly known as the golden-flowered orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which contrast strikingly with its dark green foliage. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. It produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in its natural habitat.

Due to its unique coloration and rarity, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
