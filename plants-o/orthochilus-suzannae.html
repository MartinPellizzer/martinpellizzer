Orthochilus suzannae

Orthochilus suzannae, commonly known as the Suzanne's orchid, is a rare and endangered species native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, intricate flower structure with a prominent lip. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions with dappled sunlight. Its flowers are usually a mix of white and purple hues, and it blooms during the cooler months of the year.

Due to habitat loss and climate change, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
