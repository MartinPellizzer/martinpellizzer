Oxalis fragilis

Oxalis fragilis, commonly known as the brittle wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves and small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a short stem and produces fruit that splits open to release seeds. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Oxalis fragilis is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and flowers.
