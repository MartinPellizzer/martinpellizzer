Oberonia verticillata var. pubescens

Oberonia verticillata var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy whorled orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, often on the forest floor or in rocky crevices. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a whorled pattern. Its leaves are linear and often covered with fine, soft hairs, giving it its common name.

This orchid is notable for its unique morphology and is often sought after by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value.
