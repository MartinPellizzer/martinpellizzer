Oligandra atriplicoides

Oligandra atriplicoides, commonly known as the narrow-leafed ironwood, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, with deep roots that allow it to access water from underground sources. Its hardy nature makes it a valuable species for stabilizing soil in degraded landscapes.

Oligandra atriplicoides is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
