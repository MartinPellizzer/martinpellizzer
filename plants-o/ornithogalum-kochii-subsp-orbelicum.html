Ornithogalum kochii subsp. orbelicum

Ornithogalum kochii subsp.

orbelicum, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region. It features a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces striking, bell-shaped white flowers on slender stems.

The plant is characterized by its bulbous underground stem, which serves as a storage organ. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Ornithogalum kochii subsp.

orbelicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
