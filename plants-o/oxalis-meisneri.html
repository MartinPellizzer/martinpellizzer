Oxalis meisneri

Oxalis meisneri, commonly known as the miniature wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive pattern of veins, and its flowers are typically pink or white with five petals. The plant is characterized by its delicate appearance and is often cultivated as a ornamental species. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial shade to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Oxalis meisneri is also known for its ability to produce offsets, which can be used for propagation.
