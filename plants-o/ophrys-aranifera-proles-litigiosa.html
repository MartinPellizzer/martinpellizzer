Ophrys aranifera proles litigiosa

Ophrys aranifera proles litigiosa, commonly known as the litigious bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its unique floral morphology, which closely resembles a bee, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant produces a single, showy flower with a hairy, bee-like labellum that mimics the appearance and scent of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. It thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its specific ecological requirements and habitat fragmentation, it is considered a vulnerable species.
