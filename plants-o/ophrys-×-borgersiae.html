Ophrys × borgersiae

Ophrys × borgersiae, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant that results from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe, particularly found in regions of Spain and southern France. The plant is notable for its unique floral morphology, which closely resembles a female bee, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of a bee's body.

This mimicry attracts male bees, which attempt to mate with the flower, thereby facilitating pollination through the transfer of pollen.
