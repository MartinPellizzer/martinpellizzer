Ostenia uruguayensis

Ostenia uruguayensis, commonly known as the Uruguay grass, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Uruguay and parts of Argentina.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically green or pale yellow in color. Ostenia uruguayensis is adapted to thrive in dry, open habitats and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Its ecological role includes providing forage for livestock and supporting local biodiversity.
