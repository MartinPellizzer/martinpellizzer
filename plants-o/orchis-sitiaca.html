Orchis sitiaca

Orchis sitiaca, commonly known as the meadow orchid, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stem and showy, purple or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its roots contain alkaloids, which have been historically used in traditional medicine.

Orchis sitiaca plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
