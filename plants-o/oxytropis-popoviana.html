Oxytropis popoviana

Oxytropis popoviana, commonly known as the Siberian locoweed, is a flowering plant native to the cold climates of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces trifoliate leaves and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important component of certain ecosystems.

However, some species within the genus can be toxic to livestock, necessitating caution in its management.
