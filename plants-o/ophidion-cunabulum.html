Ophidion cunabulum

Ophidion cunabulum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed water plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, ponds, and the margins of lakes. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves that are often submerged or floating, with small white flowers that appear in early summer. Its rhizomatous root system helps it spread and colonize wet areas effectively.

Ophidion cunabulum plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for invertebrates and contributing to water filtration.
