Oberonia maboroensis

Oberonia maboroensis, commonly known as the Maboro orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, intricate flower structure. The plant typically grows as a small, terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb and is often found in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with intricate patterns, and it blooms during the rainy season.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern.
