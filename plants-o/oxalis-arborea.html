Oxalis arborea

Oxalis arborea, commonly known as the tree oxalis, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its upright, woody stems and trifoliate leaves that resemble those of clover. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, often clustered in racemes. Oxalis arborea thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it both aesthetically pleasing and ecologically valuable in its native habitats.
