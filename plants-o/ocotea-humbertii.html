Ocotea humbertii

Ocotea humbertii, commonly known as the Humbert's ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed enclosed in a fleshy, aromatic pulp.

Ocotea humbertii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
