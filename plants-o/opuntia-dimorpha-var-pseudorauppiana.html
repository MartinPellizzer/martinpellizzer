Opuntia dimorpha var. pseudorauppiana

Opuntia dimorpha var. pseudorauppiana, commonly known as the desert hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, spiny stems that are often segmented and covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is a drought-tolerant species, adapted to thrive in hot, dry environments with minimal water requirements.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it well-suited for xeriscaping and desert gardens.
