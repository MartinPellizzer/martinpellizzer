Osteocarpus foliolosus

Osteocarpus foliolosus, commonly known as the leafy sea dragon, is a unique marine plant found in coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its intricate, branching structure and its ability to thrive in shallow, brackish waters. The plant has specialized leaves that are adapted for photosynthesis in low-light conditions. Its reproductive structures are distinctive, with small, clustered flowers that attract specific pollinators.

Osteocarpus foliolosus plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various marine organisms.
