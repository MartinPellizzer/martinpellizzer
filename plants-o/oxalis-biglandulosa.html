Oxalis biglandulosa

Oxalis biglandulosa, commonly known as the large-fruited wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each leaflet having a distinctive heart-shaped base. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, orange-red fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Oxalis biglandulosa is a perennial species that is valued for its ornamental appearance and historical use in traditional medicine.
