Onobrychis psammophila

Onobrychis psammophila, commonly known as the sand sainfoin, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or purple, blooming in early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a deep root system that helps it survive in drought conditions.

Onobrychis psammophila is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil restoration efforts.
