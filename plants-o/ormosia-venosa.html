Ormosia venosa

Ormosia venosa, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The tree produces woody pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its roots are also valued for their ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

Ormosia venosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in tropical landscapes.
