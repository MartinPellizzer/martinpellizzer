Ocotea revoluta

Ocotea revoluta, commonly known as the red cedar or *Cedrela odorata*, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown heartwood that is highly valued for its durability and scent. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall, with a straight trunk and dense, dark-green foliage. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in panicles, and it produces drupes that are an important food source for various wildlife.

The plant is also noted for its medicinal properties, with some parts used in traditional medicine.
