Orchis strictifolia var. ochroleuca

Orchis strictifolia var. ochroleuca, commonly known as the white orchid or white fairy orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that range from white to pale yellow in color. The plant typically grows in woodland or grassland habitats, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are linear and often clustered at the base, while the flowers are arranged in a loose spike.

This orchid is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in temperate regions of Europe.
