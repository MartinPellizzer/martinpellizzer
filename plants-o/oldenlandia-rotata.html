Oldenlandia rotata

Oldenlandia rotata, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces delicate, blue-violet flowers throughout the growing season. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its showy blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
