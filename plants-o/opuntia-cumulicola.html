Opuntia cumulicola

Opuntia cumulicola, commonly known as the mountain prickly pear, is a cactus native to the high elevations of Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems that are covered in spiny areoles. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the summer months. Its fruit, known as a tunah, is edible and contains small, sweet seeds.

Opuntia cumulicola is adapted to arid and rocky environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
