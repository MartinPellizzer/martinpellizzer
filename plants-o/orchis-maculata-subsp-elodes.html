Orchis maculata subsp. elodes

Orchis maculata subsp.

elodes, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland and grassland habitats.

The plant features a tall, upright stem with lance-shaped leaves and a spike of flowers that are usually purple or pink with dark spots. Its flowers are characterized by a prominent lip and are pollinated by various insects, including bees and flies.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is often found in conservation areas due to its ecological significance.
