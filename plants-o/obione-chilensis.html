Obione chilensis

Obione chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, showy blooms. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal environments, adapting to the harsh conditions of its native habitat. Its flowers are often fragrant and come in a range of colors, including white, pink, and purple.

Obione chilensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
