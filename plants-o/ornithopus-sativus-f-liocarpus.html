Ornithopus sativus f. liocarpus

Ornithopus sativus f. liocarpus, commonly known as the yellow lousewort, is a flowering plant in the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
