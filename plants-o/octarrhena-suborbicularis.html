Octarrhena suborbicularis

Octarrhena suborbicularis, commonly known as the orbicular daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and features small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in a circular pattern. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, forming a dense rosette near the ground. This species thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
