Oenothera cespitosa f. caulescens

Oenothera cespitosa f. caulescens, commonly known as the glandular state wild evening primrose, is a variant of the evening primrose native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its erect, branching growth habit and produces showy yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine, glandular hairs, giving it a distinctive texture.

The species is noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
