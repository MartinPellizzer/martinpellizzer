Opuntia elata subsp. bonaerensis

Opuntia elata subsp.

bonaerensis, commonly known as the Argentine prickly pear, is a cactus species native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile. It is characterized by its flattened, paddle-shaped stems that are covered in spines and glochids.

The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tunaf, is edible and has a mild, sweet flavor.

This species is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient and hardy plant.
