Ophrys × olympica

Ophrys × olympica, commonly known as the Olympic orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Greece.

It belongs to the genus Ophrys, which is characterized by its intricate, bee-mimicking flowers that play a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its flowers are often yellow or brown with intricate patterns that resemble female bees, aiding in deception pollination.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
