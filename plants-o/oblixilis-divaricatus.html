Oblixilis divaricatus

Oblixilis divaricatus, commonly known as the branching leaf vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often lobed. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Oblixilis divaricatus thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a popular specimen for botanical gardens and ecological studies.
