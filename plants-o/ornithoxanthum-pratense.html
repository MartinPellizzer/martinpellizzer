Ornithoxanthum pratense

Ornithoxanthum pratense, commonly known as the meadow oxeye, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its yellow flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn, and it produces small, elongated achenes as its seeds.

Ornithoxanthum pratense is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
