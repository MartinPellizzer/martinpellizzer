Oxalis alata var. hirta

Oxalis alata var. hirta, commonly known as the wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive veins and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and delicate blossoms.

Oxalis alata var. hirta is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of garden conditions.
