Orchis africana

Orchis africana, commonly known as the African orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along forest margins, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and it produces small, dark brown seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Orchis africana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
