Oxytropis anyemaqensis

Oxytropis anyemaqensis, commonly known as the Anyemaq snowbean, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it forms dense clusters. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Oxytropis anyemaqensis plays a role in stabilizing soil in its harsh environment and is of interest to botanists studying alpine flora.
