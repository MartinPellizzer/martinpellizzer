Oligochaetochilus limbatus

Oligochaetochilus limbatus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed moss orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows on tree trunks or branches in humid, shaded environments. The plant has slender, linear leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture from the air, a key feature of its epiphytic lifestyle.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
