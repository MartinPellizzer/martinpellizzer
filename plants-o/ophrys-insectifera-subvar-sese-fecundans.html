Ophrys insectifera subvar. sese-fecundans

Ophrys insectifera subvar. sese-fecundans, commonly known as the bee orchid, is a member of the orchid family Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its striking, bee-like floral morphology, which plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant produces a single, showy flower that mimics the appearance and scent of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. It thrives in calcareous soils and is typically found in open, sunny habitats.

This subvariety is notable for its specialized reproductive adaptations and is a subject of interest in botanical and ecological studies.
