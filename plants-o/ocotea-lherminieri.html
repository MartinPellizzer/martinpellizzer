Ocotea lherminieri

Ocotea lherminieri, commonly known as the Guiana pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces berries that are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture making.

Ocotea lherminieri thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.
