Ophrys tenthredinifera var. tingurtiae

Ophrys tenthredinifera var. tingurtiae, commonly known as the bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which closely resembles a female bee, a mimicry strategy used to attract male bees for pollination. The plant produces showy, bee-like flowers with intricate patterns and a scent that mimics the pheromones of female bees. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires a specific microclimate to flourish.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, this variety is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
