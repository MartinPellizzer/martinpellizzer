Octadesmia scirpoidea

Octadesmia scirpoidea, commonly known as the swamp morning glory, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in the Americas.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Octadesmia scirpoidea plays a role in supporting local pollinators and contributes to the ecological diversity of wetland habitats.
