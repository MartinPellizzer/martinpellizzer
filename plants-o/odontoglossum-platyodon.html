Odontoglossum platyodon

Odontoglossum platyodon, commonly known as the flat-leaf orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, flat leaves and produces striking, showy flowers with a unique coloration pattern. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, humid conditions. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the rocky substrates where it is found.

Odontoglossum platyodon is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
