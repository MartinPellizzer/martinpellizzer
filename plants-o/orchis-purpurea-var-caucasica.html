Orchis purpurea var. caucasica

Orchis purpurea var. caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian purple orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and striking purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy surface. Its flowers are arranged in a loose spike and feature a distinctive three-lobed lip.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
