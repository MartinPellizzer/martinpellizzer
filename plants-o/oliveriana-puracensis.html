Oliveriana puracensis

Oliveriana puracensis, commonly known as the Peruvian olive tree, is a species native to the Andean regions of Peru.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and leathery in texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible fruits resembling olives in appearance. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and is valued for its ornamental and potential culinary uses.

The tree is also noted for its ability to tolerate dry conditions, making it adaptable to various environmental settings.
