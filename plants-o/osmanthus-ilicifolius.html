Osmanthus ilicifolius

Osmanthus ilicifolius, commonly known as the Chinese sweet osmanthus, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant produces small, leathery berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Osmanthus ilicifolius is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its aromatic flowers and medicinal properties.
