Otopappus jaliscensis

Otopappus jaliscensis, commonly known as the Mexican thistle, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in the spring, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Otopappus jaliscensis is often considered an invasive species in some regions due to its rapid growth and ability to spread aggressively.
