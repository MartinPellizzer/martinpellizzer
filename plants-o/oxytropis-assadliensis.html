Oxytropis assadliensis

Oxytropis assadliensis, commonly known as the Assadli's locoweed, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, spindly stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is adapted to harsh environments, with deep root systems that allow it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Oxytropis assadliensis is often found in steppe and desert habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
