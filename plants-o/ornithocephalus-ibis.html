Ornithocephalus ibis

Ornithocephalus ibis, commonly known as the ibis orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often resemble the beak of an ibis bird. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its leaves are long and strap-like, and it produces fragrant blooms in shades of white, pink, and purple.

The ornithocephalus ibis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
