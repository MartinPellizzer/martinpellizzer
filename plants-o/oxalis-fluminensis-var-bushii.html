Oxalis fluminensis var. bushii

Oxalis fluminensis var. bushii, commonly known as the Bush oxalis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features trifoliate leaves with three heart-shaped lobes, often adorned with reddish veins. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically appearing in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate foliage and long flowering period.
