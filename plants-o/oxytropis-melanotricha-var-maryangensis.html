Oxytropis melanotricha var. maryangensis

Oxytropis melanotricha var. maryangensis, commonly known as the Maryang Mountain locoweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant is adapted to harsh, arid environments and often grows in rocky or sandy soils. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for certain wildlife species.

However, it is also known to be toxic to livestock due to the presence of alkaloids.
