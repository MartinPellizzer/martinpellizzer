Orchis × todaroi

Orchis × todaroi, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Orchis morio and Orchis purpurea.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant with a fibrous root system and a rosette of basal leaves. The plant produces a tall flowering stem that bears a spike of showy, violet to purple flowers. Its flowers have a complex structure typical of orchids, including three sepals, three petals, and a specialized lip.

This hybrid is often found in damp meadows and woodland edges across Europe.
