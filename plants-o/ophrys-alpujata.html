Ophrys alpujata

Ophrys alpujata, commonly known as the Alpujata bee orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brown in color, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Ophrys alpujata is a rare and protected species, highlighting its ecological significance in its native environment.
