Ophrys × bibbiensis

Ophrys × bibbiensis, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Ophrys apifera, the bee orchid, and Ophrys insectifera, the wasp orchid. This species is characterized by its intricate, bee-like flower structure that mimics the appearance and scent of female bees to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is found in Europe, particularly in regions like the British Isles and southern France.

Its unique morphology and pollination strategy make it a subject of interest in botanical and ecological studies.
