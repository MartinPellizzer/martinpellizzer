Orthopichonia visciflua

Orthopichonia visciflua, commonly known as the stickyleaf orchid, is a unique member of the Orchidaceae family native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

This plant is characterized by its fleshy, often sticky leaves, which help it retain moisture in its natural habitat. The flowers are typically small and clustered, often displaying a range of colors from green to yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its specialized growth requirements, it is a rare and somewhat elusive species in the wild.
