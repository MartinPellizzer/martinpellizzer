Orobanche mathesii

Orobanche mathesii, commonly known as the yellow burrseed.

This parasitic plant belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and lacks chlorophyll, relying entirely on its host for nutrients. It typically attaches to the roots of various flowering plants, such as legumes and grasses, through specialized structures called haustoria. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer.

Its life cycle is closely tied to the presence of suitable host species, making it an important component of certain ecological systems.
