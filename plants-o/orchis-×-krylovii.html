Orchis × krylovii

Orchis × krylovii, commonly known as the Krylov's orchid, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Orchis moria and Orchis purpurea.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of showy, violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It has lance-shaped leaves with a smooth, waxy surface, and its flowers are arranged in a loose spike.

Orchis × krylovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
