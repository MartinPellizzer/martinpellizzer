Ocotea piurensis

Ocotea piurensis, commonly known as the Piura ocotea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. It typically grows in humid, lowland forests and is known for its ornamental value.

Ocotea piurensis is also valued for its wood, which is used in traditional crafts and construction.
