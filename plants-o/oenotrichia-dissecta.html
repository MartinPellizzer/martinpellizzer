Oenotrichia dissecta

Oenotrichia dissecta, commonly known as the dissected leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are finely dissected, giving it a unique appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Oenotrichia dissecta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
