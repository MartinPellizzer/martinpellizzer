Odontoglossum bictoniense

Odontoglossum bictoniense, commonly known as the bictoniense orchid, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant features a pseudobulb structure, which helps it store water and nutrients, enabling it to thrive in its natural habitat. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern around the pseudobulb.

Odontoglossum bictoniense is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its unique and vibrant floral display.
