Odontochilus grandiflorus

Odontochilus grandiflorus, commonly known as the large-flowered toothed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive toothed leaf margins and produces showy, fragrant flowers with a yellow base and white or pale pink petals. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its flowers are borne on slender stems and bloom during the rainy season.

Odontochilus grandiflorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
