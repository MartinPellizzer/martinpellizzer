Ochna obtusata subsp. pumila

Ochna obtusata subsp.

pumila, commonly known as the dwarf ochna, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its small, ovate leaves that are typically pubescent and have a leathery texture.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
