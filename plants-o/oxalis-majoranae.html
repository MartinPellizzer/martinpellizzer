Oxalis majoranae

Oxalis majoranae, commonly known as the false shamrock, is a species of flowering plant in the family Oxalidaceae.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which resemble those of the true shamrock. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Oxalis majoranae is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of cultivation.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for a variety of garden settings.
