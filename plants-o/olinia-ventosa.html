Olinia ventosa

Olinia ventosa, commonly known as the windvine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its slender, flexible stems that allow it to twine around supporting structures. The leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a velvety texture. Its flowers are showy, typically yellow or orange, with five petals and a prominent spurred structure.

The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a sticky, resinous coating.
