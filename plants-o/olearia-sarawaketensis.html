Olearia sarawaketensis

Olearia sarawaketensis, commonly known as the Sarawakensis daisy, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and forest clearings. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its unique appearance.

Olearia sarawaketensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
