Oxybaphus himalaicus var. chinensis

Oxybaphus himalaicus var. chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese Himalayan bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving at high elevations with cool, moist conditions. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet. Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and a branching stem that supports its floral display.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable climatic conditions.
