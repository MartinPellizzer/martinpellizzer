Oryza sativa var. rhodosperma

Oryza sativa var. rhodosperma, commonly known as the pink rice plant, is a variant of rice characterized by its distinctive pink or reddish hues in its grains.

This variety is notable for its unique pigmentation, which is attributed to the presence of anthocyanins, flavonoid compounds that provide antioxidant properties. Botanically, it belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is native to Southeast Asia, similar to other rice species. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring warm temperatures and ample water for optimal growth.
