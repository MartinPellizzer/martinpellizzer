Oxalis westii

Oxalis westii, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive, heart-shaped seed pods that split open to release seeds. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Oxalis westii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
