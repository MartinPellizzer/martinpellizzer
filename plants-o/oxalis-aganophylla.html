Oxalis aganophylla

Oxalis aganophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces edible, sour-tasting berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. Oxalis aganophylla thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along streambanks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
