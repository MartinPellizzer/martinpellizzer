Ophrys tenthredinifera subsp. grandiflora

Ophrys tenthredinifera subsp.

grandiflora, commonly known as the large bee orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive floral morphology. The plant produces showy, bee-like flowers that are adapted for mimicry, attracting male bees for pollination.

It is a terrestrial herb that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. The flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish in color, with intricate patterns that resemble bees.

This subspecies is found primarily in southern Europe and is known for its specialized pollination mechanism.
