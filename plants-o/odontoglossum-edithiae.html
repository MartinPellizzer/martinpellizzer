Odontoglossum edithiae

Odontoglossum edithiae, commonly known as the Edith's orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous plant with a pseudobulb structure, typical of many orchid genera. The plant produces striking, showy flowers with a distinctive coloration pattern, often featuring a blend of purple and white. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, arranged in a rosette formation around the pseudobulb.

Odontoglossum edithiae thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
