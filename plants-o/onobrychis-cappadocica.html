Onobrychis cappadocica

Onobrychis cappadocica, commonly known as Cappadocian burr clover, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and cylindrical, spiny pods that contain small, round seeds. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Its flowers are small and pale pink, appearing in clusters during the spring and early summer.
