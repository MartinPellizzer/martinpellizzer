Oenothera spiralis f. arcuata

Oenothera spiralis f. arcuata, commonly known as the curlleaf evening primrose, is a variant of the evening primrose species native to North America.

It is characterized by its spirally arranged, narrow leaves that give it a distinctive, curling appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers that open in the evening, typical of the Oenothera genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in prairie and open woodland habitats.

This form is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
