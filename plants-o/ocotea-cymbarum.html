Ocotea cymbarum

Ocotea cymbarum, commonly known as the fragrant ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by dark, spherical berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Ocotea cymbarum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
