Oligactis sessilifolia

Oligactis sessilifolia, commonly known as the sessile-leaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are sessile, meaning they are attached directly to the stem without a stalk, giving it a compact appearance.

Oligactis sessilifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its delicate and colorful blooms.
