Olea caudatilimba

Olea caudatilimba, commonly known as the long-tailed olive, is a species of flowering plant in the Oleaceae family.

It is native to parts of Central and South America, where it typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged in opposite pairs. Its flowers are clustered in small inflorescences and are typically greenish-yellow in color, while the fruit is a small, woody drupe.

Olea caudatilimba is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
