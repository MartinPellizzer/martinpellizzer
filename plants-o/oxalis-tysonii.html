Oxalis tysonii

Oxalis tysonii, commonly known as the golden star oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom in spring and are followed by small, round fruits.

Oxalis tysonii is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
