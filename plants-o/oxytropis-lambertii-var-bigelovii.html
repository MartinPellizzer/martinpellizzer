Oxytropis lambertii var. bigelovii

Oxytropis lambertii var. bigelovii, commonly known as the Bigelov's locoweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sagebrush steppe and desert grasslands.

Due to its toxic alkaloids, it is considered a noxious weed in some regions.
