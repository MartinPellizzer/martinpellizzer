Oxalis lineata

Oxalis lineata, commonly known as the spurred oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It features delicate, trifoliate leaves with a distinctive spurred appearance on the petioles. The plant produces small, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Oxalis lineata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
