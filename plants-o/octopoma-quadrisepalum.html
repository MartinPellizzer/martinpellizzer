Octopoma quadrisepalum

Octopoma quadrisepalum, commonly known as the four-sepal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive four-sepal structure in its flowers, which is a unique feature among morning glory species. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

The leaves are heart-shaped and can grow up to 10 centimeters in length, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
