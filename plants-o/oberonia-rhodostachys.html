Oberonia rhodostachys

Oberonia rhodostachys, commonly known as the red-stalked Oberonia, is a member of the Orchidaceae family native to Southeast Asia.

This terrestrial orchid is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. It produces striking clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
