Orsopea

Orsopea, commonly known as the "sicklepod" or "false cumin," is a member of the Leguminosae family.

It is a small, annual herb that typically grows in dry, sandy soils. The plant features compound leaves with three leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by distinctive, curved seed pods. Orsopea is native to regions of Africa and the Middle East, and it is often found in arid environments.

Its seeds are sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.
