Ochna ilicifolia

Ochna ilicifolia, commonly known as the African snowball or white-leaf ochna, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy, bluish-green appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that are encased in a fleshy, red or orange aril.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
