Orchis coreana

Orchis coreana, commonly known as the Korean twayblade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Korea.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and two lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces a single, erect spike of pale yellow to greenish-white flowers, which are typically bilaterally symmetrical. Orchis coreana thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
