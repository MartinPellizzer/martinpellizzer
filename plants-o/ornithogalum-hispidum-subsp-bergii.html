Ornithogalum hispidum subsp. bergii

Ornithogalum hispidum subsp.

bergii, commonly known as the hairy oxalis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It features lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive hairy texture, giving it its common name.

The plant produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and rockeries.
