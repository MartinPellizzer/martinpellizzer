Oncidium cucullatum var. maculosum

Oncidium cucullatum var. maculosum, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically feature a yellow or orange lip with distinctive black or brown spots. The plant grows as an epiphyte, often attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its inflorescence consists of multiple flowers arranged in a raceme, with petals and sepals that are often brightly colored.

The species is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
