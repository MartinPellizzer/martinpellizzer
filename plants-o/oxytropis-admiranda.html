Oxytropis admiranda

Oxytropis admiranda, commonly known as the Admirable Locoweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and spiny stems. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Oxytropis admiranda is also known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock.
