Odontoglossum nebulosum var. candidulum

Odontoglossum nebulosum var. candidulum, commonly known as the White Cloud Orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant produces pendulous, spurred flowers that are highly ornamental and attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for greenhouse cultivation.

Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating.
