Olax pynaertii

Olax pynaertii, commonly known as the African olive, is a small evergreen shrub native to eastern and southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Olacaceae and is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, orange-red fruits that contain a single seed. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry climates.

Olax pynaertii is often used in traditional medicine and for ornamental purposes due to its hardy nature and attractive appearance.
