Oreobroma Yosemitanum

Yosemite oreobroma, scientifically known as Oreobroma yosemitanum, is a rare and unique plant species native to the Sierra Nevada mountains. Found primarily in the Yosemite National Park area, it thrives in rocky, high-altitude environments. This plant is distinguished by its striking violet-blue flowers and hairy, grass-like leaves. Due to its limited habitat and increasing threats from climate change, it is considered endangered. Conservation efforts are crucial to preserving this rare botanical treasure.
