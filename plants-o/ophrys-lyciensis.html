Ophrys lyciensis

Ophrys lyciensis, commonly known as the Lycian Ophrys, is a species of orchid native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Turkey and Greece.

It is characterized by its striking, bee-like floral structure, which features a prominent lip that mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in open, sunny habitats. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brownish in color, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Ophrys lyciensis is a rare and specialized orchid, showcasing the remarkable adaptations of orchids in their reproductive strategies.
