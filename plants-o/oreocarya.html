Oreocarya

Oreocarya, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada bristlecone pine, is a species of conifer native to the high elevations of the Sierra Nevada mountain range in California.

It is characterized by its slow growth rate, long lifespan, and distinctive appearance with twisted branches and a conical shape. The plant has needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along the stems, and it produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds.

Oreocarya thrives in cold, dry environments and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions, making it a resilient and ecologically significant species in its habitat.
