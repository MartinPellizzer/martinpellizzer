Opuntia magnifolia

Opuntia magnifolia, commonly known as the giant prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, flattened stems that are covered in spines and are often ribbed. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tunaf, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

Opuntia magnifolia is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant, making it a hardy plant in desert regions.
