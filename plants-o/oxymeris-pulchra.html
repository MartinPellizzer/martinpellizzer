Oxymeris pulchra

Oxymeris pulchra, commonly known as the beauty tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and striking, palmate leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by round, orange-red fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Oxymeris pulchra is also valued for its hard, durable wood used in construction and furniture.
