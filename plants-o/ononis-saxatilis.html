Ononis saxatilis

Ononis saxatilis, commonly known as the rock trefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to rocky and arid environments in Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its trifoliate leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically features small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its deep root system allows it to thrive in poor soils and withstand drought conditions.

Ononis saxatilis is often found in crevices and on rocky slopes, making it a hardy species adapted to harsh climates.
