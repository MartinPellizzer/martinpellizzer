Ophrys gottfriediana

Ophrys gottfriediana, commonly known as the Gottfried's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the alpine regions of Central Europe.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a lip that mimics the appearance of a female bee, which plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool and moist conditions. Its flowers are usually pale green to brownish in color, with intricate patterns that aid in attracting male bees for pollination.

Ophrys gottfriediana is a rare and protected species, reflecting its ecological significance and conservation status.
