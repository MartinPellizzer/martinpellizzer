Oxyanthus pyriformis subsp. pyriformis

Oxyanthus pyriformis subsp.

pyriformis, commonly known as the pear-shaped oxyanthus, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America. It is characterized by its distinctive pear-shaped fruits, which are typically yellow or orange when ripe.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.
