Octomeria purpurascens

Octomeria purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-flowered octomeria, is a strikingly beautiful plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or mountainous terrain. The plant is characterized by its vibrant purple or violet flowers, which bloom in clusters and attract various pollinators. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in a fine layer of hair, which helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

Octomeria purpurascens is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
