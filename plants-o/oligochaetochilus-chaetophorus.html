Oligochaetochilus chaetophorus

Oligochaetochilus chaetophorus, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typically blooming during the rainy season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
