Oncidium sphegiferum

Oncidium sphegiferum, commonly known as the golden spike orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, elongated, yellowish-green flowers with a distinctive golden spike. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Oncidium sphegiferum is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its vibrant appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
