Ochrocarpos cerasifer

Ochrocarpos cerasifer, commonly known as the sweet thorn, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought-resistant features such as deep roots and waxy leaf surfaces. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, attracting pollinators like bees and birds.

The fruit, a legume pod, contains edible seeds that are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.
