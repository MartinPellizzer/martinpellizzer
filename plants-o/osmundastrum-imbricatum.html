Osmundastrum imbricatum

Osmundastrum imbricatum, commonly known as the broad-leaved water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its large, triangular, serrated fronds that are typically submerged or floating in freshwater habitats. The plant exhibits a unique reproductive system, with spores produced on the underside of its fronds in clusters called sori. Its rhizomes form dense mats that help stabilize sediments in aquatic environments.

Osmundastrum imbricatum plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
