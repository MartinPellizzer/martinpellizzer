Ononis maweana var. hackelii

Ononis maweana var. hackelii, commonly known as the hackel's sandspurry, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, yellow flowers clustered in dense inflorescences. The plant has succulent leaves that help it conserve water in dry environments. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in poor soils and withstand drought conditions.

It plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
