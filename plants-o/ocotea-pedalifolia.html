Ocotea pedalifolia

Ocotea pedalifolia, commonly known as the fragrant wood or sweet wood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurel and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its wood is valued for its pleasant scent and is used in the production of incense and perfumes.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
