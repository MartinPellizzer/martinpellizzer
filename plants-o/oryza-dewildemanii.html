Oryza dewildemanii

Oryza dewildemanii, commonly known as the dwarf ricegrass, is a grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, narrow leaves. The plant produces spikelets that bear tiny rice-like grains, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wetland habitats and is often found in marshy areas near water bodies.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
