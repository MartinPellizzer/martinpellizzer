Oxydectes malabarica

Oxydectes malabarica, commonly known as the Malabar star-of-Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 cm. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers that are clustered in a star-like formation, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest areas.

Oxydectes malabarica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
