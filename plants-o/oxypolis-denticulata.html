Oxypolis denticulata

Oxypolis denticulata, commonly known as the toothed knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes buckwheat and sorrel. The plant features slender, upright stems with alternate, palmately lobed leaves that have serrated edges. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

Oxypolis denticulata is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.
