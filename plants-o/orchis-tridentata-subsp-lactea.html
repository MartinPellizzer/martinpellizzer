Orchis tridentata subsp. lactea

Orchis tridentata subsp.

lactea, commonly known as the white helleborine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale yellow with a distinctive, spurred lip. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense colonies in suitable habitats.
