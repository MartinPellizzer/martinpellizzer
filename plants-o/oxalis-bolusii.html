Oxalis bolusii

Oxalis bolusii, commonly known as the Bolus's wood sorrel, is a small, flowering plant native to South Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins and produces clusters of delicate, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is a member of the Oxalidaceae family, known for its calcium oxalate crystals in the leaves.

Oxalis bolusii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and compact growth habit.
