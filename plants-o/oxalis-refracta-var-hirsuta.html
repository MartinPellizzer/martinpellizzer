Oxalis refracta var. hirsuta

Oxalis refracta var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive white or yellowish veining, and small, five-petaled flowers that range from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and streambanks. It produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or water.

Oxalis refracta var. hirsuta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
