Oxalis caprina var. anthelmintica

Oxalis caprina var. anthelmintica, commonly known as the creeping wood sorrel, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped lobes and produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas, where it spreads via stolons.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ornamental and ecologically significant in its native habitat.
