Oxalis xantha

Oxalis xantha, commonly known as the yellow wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are heart-shaped and often tinged with red or purple veins. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Oxalis xantha is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
