Orchis coriophora var. latifolia

Orchis coriophora var. latifolia, commonly known as the Broad-leaved Heath Orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species found in specific habitats across Europe.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are often clustered in spikes. This variety is typically found in open, sunny environments such as heathlands and grassy slopes. Its growth is closely tied to nutrient-poor soils, which influences its distribution and ecological niche.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
