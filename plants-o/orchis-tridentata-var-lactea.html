Orchis tridentata var. lactea

Orchis tridentata var. lactea, commonly known as the milk orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of pale yellow to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its flowers are showy and attract various pollinators.

The species is noted for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity and its historical use in traditional medicine.
