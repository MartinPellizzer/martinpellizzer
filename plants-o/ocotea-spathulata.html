Ocotea spathulata

Ocotea spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leafed ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurel and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a dark blue drupe, often containing a single seed.

Ocotea spathulata is valued for its aromatic wood and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
