Orchis laxiflora var. persica

Orchis laxiflora var. persica, commonly known as the Persian orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers that are clustered in racemes, with each flower having three sepals and three petals. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value and unique floral structure make it a notable species in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
