Ophioglossum falcatum

Ophioglossum falcatum, commonly known as the adder's tongue fern, is a unique and ancient plant belonging to the family Ophioglossaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often curved fronds that resemble a snake's tongue, hence its common name. The plant has a primitive structure, with a single unbranched sporophyll and a short, erect sporangium at the tip. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically in temperate regions.

Ophioglossum falcatum is considered a living fossil due to its evolutionary significance and limited distribution.
