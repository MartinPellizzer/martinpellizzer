Oryza sativa var. persica

Oryza sativa var. persica, commonly known as Persian rice, is a variety of rice native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its short, plump grains and a distinct nutty aroma. This rice is typically cultivated in temperate climates and requires a moderate amount of water during its growth cycle. Its botanical features include a tough outer husk and a starchy endosperm that contributes to its texture.

Persian rice is often used in traditional dishes and is valued for its unique flavor and culinary versatility.
