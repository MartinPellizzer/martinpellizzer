Olea europaea var. oleaster

Olea europaea var. oleaster, commonly known as the wild olive tree, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by small, oval-shaped fruits. The fruit is often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
