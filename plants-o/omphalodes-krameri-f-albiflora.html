Omphalodes krameri f. albiflora

Omphalodes krameri f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered omphalodes, is a horticultural form of the species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, round, greenish-black fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance and resilience.
