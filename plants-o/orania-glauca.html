Orania glauca

Orania glauca, commonly known as the blue glaucous orchid, is a flowering plant native to the eastern regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking blue or bluish-green foliage and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in well-drained environments. It is a perennial herb that produces small, clustered inflorescences with showy blooms during the summer months.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Orania glauca is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
