Oestlundia luteorosea

Oestlundia luteorosea, commonly known as the yellow rosewood, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic wood that is highly valued for its rich yellow color and fine grain. The plant typically grows in coastal regions and is adapted to sandy soils and saline conditions. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a smooth texture, while its flowers are small and fragrant, often appearing in clusters.

Oestlundia luteorosea is also known for its slow growth and is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
