Ouratea laevis var. ipamuensis

Ouratea laevis var. ipamuensis, commonly known as the Ipamuense Ouratea, is a species of flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its smooth, pale green bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in the spring. Its fruits are spherical berries that turn red when mature, containing several seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
