Orchidotypus antioquiensis

Orchidotypus antioquiensis, commonly known as the Antioquian orchid, is a rare and endemic orchid species found in the cloud forests of Antioquia, Colombia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, showy floral structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers exhibit a range of colors, including white, pink, and purple, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
