Ophrys × potentissima

Ophrys × potentissima, commonly known as the orchid bee orchid, is a hybrid species within the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its intricate, bee-mimicking flowers that feature a prominent, hairy lip resembling a female bee. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and meadows across Europe. Its flowers produce nectar to attract male bees, which in turn facilitate pollination.

This species showcases the remarkable evolutionary adaptations seen in orchids, particularly in their mimicry strategies.
