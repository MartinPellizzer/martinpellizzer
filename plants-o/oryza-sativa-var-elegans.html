Oryza sativa var. elegans

Oryza sativa var. elegans, commonly known as the elegant rice variety, is a less commonly cultivated subspecies of rice.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated grains and a delicate, often translucent appearance. This variety is primarily grown in specific regions of Asia where its unique traits are valued. It exhibits a range of morphological features, including reduced plant height and increased grain size compared to standard rice varieties.

Due to its ornamental qualities, it is sometimes cultivated for aesthetic purposes rather than for food.
