Ocotea arnottiana

Ocotea arnottiana, commonly known as the white sand ocotea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage and aromatic, glossy leaves. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

The fruit is a small, woody drupe that contains a single seed, and the plant is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests.
