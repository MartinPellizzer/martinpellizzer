Ochna bequaertii

Ochna bequaertii, commonly known as the Bequaert's ochna, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
