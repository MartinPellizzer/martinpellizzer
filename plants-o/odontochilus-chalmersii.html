Odontochilus chalmersii

Odontochilus chalmersii, commonly known as the Chalmers' toothed orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique toothed lip, which gives it its scientific name, and its small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil to flourish. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that help it conserve moisture in its natural habitat.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
