Oricia leonensis

Oricia leonensis, commonly known as the Leon's orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant blooms that typically appear in shades of pink and white. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil to flourish. Its unique floral structure and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss, Oricia leonensis is considered endangered, highlighting the need for protective measures to preserve its existence.
