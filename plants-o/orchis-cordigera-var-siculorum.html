Orchis cordigera var. siculorum

Orchis cordigera var. siculorum, commonly known as the Sicilian heart orchid, is a rare and striking species native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky habitats. Its blooming period occurs in late spring to early summer, adding a unique floral element to its native environment.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern in some areas.
