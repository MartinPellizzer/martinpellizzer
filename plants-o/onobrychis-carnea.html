Onobrychis carnea

Onobrychis carnea, commonly known as sweet vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow or pink flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural and ecological contexts. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Onobrychis carnea is also used as a forage plant for livestock due to its nutritional value.
