Oxalis neuwiedii

Oxalis neuwiedii, commonly known as the velvetleaf wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in moist, well-drained soil. It is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments in its natural habitat.

Oxalis neuwiedii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
