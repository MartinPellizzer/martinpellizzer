Olax ubanghensis

Olax ubanghensis, commonly known as the East African olive, is a small evergreen shrub native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the family Olacaceae and is characterized by its simple, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant yellow flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically red when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
