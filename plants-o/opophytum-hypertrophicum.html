Opophytum hypertrophicum

Opophytum hypertrophicum, commonly known as the giant African snail, is a large terrestrial gastropod native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its heavy, spiral-shaped shell that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant-like appearance of its shell has led to its classification in the family Achatinidae, although it is not a true plant. Its shell is composed primarily of calcium carbonate and organic compounds, providing structural support and protection.
