Oplismenus frumentaceus

Oplismenus frumentaceus, commonly known as the silver bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in open woodlands and disturbed sites, contributing to soil stabilization.

Oplismenus frumentaceus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
