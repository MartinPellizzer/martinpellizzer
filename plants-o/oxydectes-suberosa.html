Oxydectes suberosa

Oxydectes suberosa, commonly known as the waxplant, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are typically leathery and have a waxy texture, contributing to its common name.

Oxydectes suberosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
