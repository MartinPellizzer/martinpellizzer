Oenothera communis var. jamesii

Oenothera communis var. jamesii, commonly known as the James' evening primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features showy yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers are typically borne in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Oenothera communis var. jamesii is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
