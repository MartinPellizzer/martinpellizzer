Oxalis decipiens

Oxalis decipiens, commonly known as the false wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive reddish veins, and it produces small, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories. It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its calcium oxalate crystals in the leaves.

Oxalis decipiens is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
