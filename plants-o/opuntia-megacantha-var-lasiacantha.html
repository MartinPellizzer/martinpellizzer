Opuntia megacantha var. lasiacantha

Opuntia megacantha var. lasiacantha, commonly known as the narrow-leafed prickly pear, is a cactus native to parts of Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its flat, ribbed stems that are typically green with a waxy coating. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tunas, is edible and has a mild, sweet flavor.

This species is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water to thrive.
