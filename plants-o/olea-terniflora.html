Olea terniflora

Olea terniflora, commonly known as the three-leaved olive, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are borne in clusters, followed by small, fleshy fruits that contain a single seed. Olea terniflora is adapted to arid and rocky environments, showcasing drought tolerance and efficient water conservation mechanisms.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique foliage and resilience in challenging climates.
