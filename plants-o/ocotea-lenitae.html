Ocotea lenitae

Ocotea lenitae, commonly known as the yellow sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and fragrant wood. The plant typically grows in humid, lowland forests and can reach heights of up to 20 meters. Its flowers are small and white, often clustered in umbels, and it produces dark, oily seeds.

The tree is valued for its timber, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
