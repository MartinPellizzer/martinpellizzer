Ocimum gratissimum

Ocimum gratissimum, commonly known as African basil, is a herbaceous plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like mint and sage. The plant is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of white or pale blue flowers. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its distinct scent and various medicinal properties.

Ocimum gratissimum is often used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant benefits.
