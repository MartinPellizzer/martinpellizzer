Orthosiphon capitatus

Orthosiphon capitatus, commonly known as the water hyssop, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, white to purple flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. The plant has serrated leaves that are often aromatic and used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

Orthosiphon capitatus is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in treating urinary disorders.
