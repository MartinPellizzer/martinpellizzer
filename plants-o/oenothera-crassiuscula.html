Oenothera crassiuscula

Oenothera crassiuscula, commonly known as the narrowleaf evening primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southwestern United States.

It features showy yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its botanical characteristics include capsule fruits that release seeds through a slit, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Oenothera crassiuscula is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinator populations and its historical use in traditional medicine.
