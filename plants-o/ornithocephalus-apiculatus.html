Ornithocephalus apiculatus

Ornithocephalus apiculatus, commonly known as the spike-headed orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, elongated inflorescence that bears numerous small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as forest undergrowth, where it relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient absorption. Its striking appearance makes it a popular specimen in botanical gardens and a subject of study for its ecological adaptations.

The species is also noted for its specific pollination mechanisms, which involve a specialized relationship with certain insect pollinators.
