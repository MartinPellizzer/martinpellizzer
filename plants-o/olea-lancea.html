Olea lancea

Olea lancea, commonly known as the lanceleaf olive, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by small, fleshy fruits containing a single seed. Olea lancea is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal and sandy soils.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
