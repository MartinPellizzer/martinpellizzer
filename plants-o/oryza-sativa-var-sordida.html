Oryza sativa var. sordida

Oryza sativa var. sordida, commonly known as the sordid rice, is a less commonly cultivated variety of rice that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

It is distinguished by its shorter, more compact grain compared to other rice varieties, and it often has a darker, more muted coloration. The plant typically grows in wetland environments and has a shorter growth cycle, making it suitable for certain agricultural conditions. Its leaves are usually narrower and more upright, contributing to its distinct appearance.

Despite its limited commercial use, it remains of interest in botanical studies due to its genetic diversity and potential for adaptation to specific climates.
