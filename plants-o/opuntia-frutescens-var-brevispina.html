Opuntia frutescens var. brevispina

Opuntia frutescens var. brevispina, commonly known as the short-spined prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in small, short spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the summer months, followed by edible, fleshy fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy soils.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to drought conditions and it is often used in xeriscaping.
