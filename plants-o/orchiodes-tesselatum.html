Orchiodes tesselatum

Orchiodes tesselatum, commonly known as the tessellated orchid, is a striking orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, tessellated patterns on its petals and sepals, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with a distinctive three-lobed lip.

Orchiodes tesselatum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
