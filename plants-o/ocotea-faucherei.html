Ocotea faucherei

Ocotea faucherei, commonly known as the Brazilian cinnamon tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like laurel and cinnamon. The tree is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils, contributing to its use in traditional medicine and as a source of aromatic compounds.

Ocotea faucherei thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.
