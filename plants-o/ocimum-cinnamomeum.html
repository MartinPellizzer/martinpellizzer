Ocimum cinnamomeum

Ocimum cinnamomeum, commonly known as cinnamon basil, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that emit a strong cinnamon-like fragrance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale purple, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ocimum cinnamomeum is often cultivated for its culinary and aromatic uses in traditional medicine and cooking.
