Oxytropis aulieatensis

Oxytropis aulieatensis, commonly known as the Aulieat Mountain milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems and small, compound leaves. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers in late spring, which are clustered in racemes. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or gravelly soils.

Oxytropis aulieatensis is adapted to harsh climates and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
