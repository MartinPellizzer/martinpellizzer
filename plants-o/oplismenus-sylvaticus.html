Oplismenus sylvaticus

Oplismenus sylvaticus, commonly known as the common wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive veining pattern. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Oplismenus sylvaticus is known for its edible leaves, which have a tangy, sour flavor due to the presence of oxalic acid.
