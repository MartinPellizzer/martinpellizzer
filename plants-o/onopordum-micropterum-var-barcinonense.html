Onopordum micropterum var. barcinonense

Onopordum micropterum var. barcinonense, commonly known as the Barcino thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
