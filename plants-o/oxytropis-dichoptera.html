Oxytropis dichoptera

Oxytropis dichoptera, commonly known as the two-winged locoweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces yellow or greenish-yellow flowers, which are typically clustered in racemes. Its leaves are compound, consisting of pairs of small, oval leaflets.

Oxytropis dichoptera is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats.
