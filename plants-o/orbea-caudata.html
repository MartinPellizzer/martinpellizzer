Orbea caudata

Orbea caudata, commonly known as the Cape bell or the beard-tongue orchid, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that are often brightly colored. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its stems are often covered in a dense, hairy coating.

Orbea caudata is appreciated for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
