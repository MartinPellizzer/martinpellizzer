Oxytropis japonica f. leucantha

Oxytropis japonica f. leucantha, commonly known as the white-flowered yellow vetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is notable for its white to pale yellow flowers, which contrast with its green foliage. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, such as nitrogen fixation and support for pollinators.
