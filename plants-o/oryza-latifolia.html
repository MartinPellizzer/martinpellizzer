Oryza latifolia

Oryza latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf rice, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, flat leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and rice paddies. Its seeds are used in traditional cuisine and have been studied for their potential agricultural and medicinal applications.

Oryza latifolia plays a significant role in both ecological and cultural contexts in its native regions.
