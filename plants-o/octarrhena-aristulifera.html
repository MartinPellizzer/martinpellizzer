Octarrhena aristulifera

Octarrhena aristulifera, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is known for its milky sap, which is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
