Oncocalyx welwitschii

Oncocalyx welwitschii, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety purple flowers that appear in clusters. The tree has compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in local communities.
