Oenothera pumila var. pusilla

Oenothera pumila var. pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf evening primrose, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It features slender, hairy stems and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that open in the evening. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves and is adapted to thrive in sandy or rocky soils. It is often found in open habitats such as prairies, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.

Oenothera pumila var. pusilla is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
