Oncidium cuencanum

Oncidium cuencanum, commonly known as the Cuencanum orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its long, pendant-like inflorescences that bear numerous small, colorful flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers often display a mix of yellow, orange, and red hues, making it a visually striking species.

Oncidium cuencanum requires well-draining soil and consistent moisture to thrive.
