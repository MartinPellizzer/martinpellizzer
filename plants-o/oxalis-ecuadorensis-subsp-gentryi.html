Oxalis ecuadorensis subsp. gentryi

Oxalis ecuadorensis subsp.

gentryi, commonly known as the Ecuadorian wood sorrel, is a species native to the Andean region of Ecuador. It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as montane forests. It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage. Oxalis ecuadorensis subsp.

gentryi is also valued for its potential medicinal properties.
