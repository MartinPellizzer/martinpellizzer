Onobrychis tommasinii

Onobrychis tommasinii, commonly known as the Turkish clover, is a low-growing perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is characterized by its deep taproot system, which allows it to thrive in dry, rocky soils. It is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to fix nitrogen and stabilize soil.

Onobrychis tommasinii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
