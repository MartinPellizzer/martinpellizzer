Orobus variabilis

Orobus variabilis, commonly known as the variable legume, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional herbal medicine. Orobus variabilis is typically found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to thrive in semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and biodiversity conservation.
