Opuntia palmeri

Opuntia palmeri, commonly known as Palmer's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems that are covered in spines and glochids, which are small, barbed bristles. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tun, is edible and contains numerous seeds.

Opuntia palmeri is adapted to arid environments and plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
