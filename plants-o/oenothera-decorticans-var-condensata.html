Oenothera decorticans var. condensata

Oenothera decorticans var. condensata, commonly known as the condensed evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its seeds are enclosed in a capsule that splits open to release the seeds when mature.

This variety is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
