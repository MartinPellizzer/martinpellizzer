Oxalis binervis

Oxalis binervis, commonly known as the veined wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features trifoliate leaves with two veins on each leaflet, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

Oxalis binervis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its delicate foliage and spring blooms.
