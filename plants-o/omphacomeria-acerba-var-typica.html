Omphacomeria acerba var. typica

Omphacomeria acerba var. typica, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has lance-shaped, serrated leaves with a distinct minty scent. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink to purple and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
