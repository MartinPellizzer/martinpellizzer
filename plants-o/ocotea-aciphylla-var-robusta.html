Ocotea aciphylla var. robusta

Ocotea aciphylla var. robusta, commonly known as the robust ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark-barked trunk and lance-shaped, glossy green leaves. It produces small, white flowers that are followed by dark blue, spherical berries.

This species is often found in humid lowland forests and is valued for its aromatic wood and ecological importance.
