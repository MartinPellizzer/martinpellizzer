Ononis spinosa var. tomentosa

Ononis spinosa var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features slender, creeping stems that are often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a rounded or pointed tip, and the plant produces small, yellowish flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas, along paths, and in disturbed habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
