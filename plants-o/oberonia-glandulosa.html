Oberonia glandulosa

Oberonia glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, often pendulous growth habit. The plant features dark green, leathery leaves with a waxy coating, and its flowers are typically small, fragrant, and arranged in dense clusters. Oberonia glandulosa thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, organic-rich substrates.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and unique floral structure.
