Ornithogalum umbellatum f. nevadense

Ornithogalum umbellatum f. nevadense, commonly known as the Nevada bellflower, is a rare variant of the common bellflower.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers. Native to the high-elevation regions of Nevada, it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant is adapted to arid conditions and has a compact growth habit.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
