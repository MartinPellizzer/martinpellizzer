Oldenlandia grandis

Oldenlandia grandis, commonly known as the largeleaf moonvine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release tiny seeds when mature.

This species is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
