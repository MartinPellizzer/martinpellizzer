Oxymeris quinquedentata

Oxymeris quinquedentata, commonly known as the five-toothed mangrove, is a tropical tree species native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive five-toothed leaves and its ability to thrive in saline and waterlogged soils. The plant has a robust root system that helps stabilize shorelines and prevent erosion. It plays a vital role in mangrove ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.

Oxymeris quinquedentata is also valued for its timber, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
