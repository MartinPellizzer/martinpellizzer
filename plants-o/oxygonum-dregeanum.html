Oxygonum dregeanum

Oxygonum dregeanum, commonly known as the yellow dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, fleshy, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Oxygonum dregeanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
