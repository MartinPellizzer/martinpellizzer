Oxalis violacella

Oxalis violacella, commonly known as the violet wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive violet hue and produces small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink or purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and thrives in well-drained soil. It is a perennial species that can spread through rhizomes, forming dense ground cover.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological landscaping.
