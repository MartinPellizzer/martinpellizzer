Ophrys × messiniae

Ophrys × messiniae, commonly known as the Messinian Orchid, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure that mimics the appearance of female wasps, a trait known as pseudocopulation, which aids in pollination by male wasps. The plant typically grows in Mediterranean regions, including parts of Italy and Greece, where it thrives in calcareous soils. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brown, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Ophrys × messiniae is a rare and protected species, valued for its ecological and botanical significance.
