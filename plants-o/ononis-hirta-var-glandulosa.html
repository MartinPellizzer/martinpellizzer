Ononis hirta var. glandulosa

Ononis hirta var. glandulosa, commonly known as hairy bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, ascending stems that are covered in fine, hairy glands, giving it its common name. The leaves are alternate, linear, and also bear glandular hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer.

This plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.
