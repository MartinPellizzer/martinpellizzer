Ophrys × cicmiriana

Ophrys × cicmiriana, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral morphology, particularly its intricate and often bee-like flower structure. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is found in the Mediterranean region, including parts of southern Europe and North Africa. Its flowers are designed to mimic female bees, a strategy that enhances its pollination success through deceptive pollination.

This hybrid species exhibits a range of morphological variations, making it a subject of interest in both botanical research and horticulture.
