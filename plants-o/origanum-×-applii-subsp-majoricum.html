Origanum × applii subsp. majoricum

Origanum × applii subsp.

majoricum, commonly known as sweet marjoram, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its aromatic, small, white to pale pink flowers and its finely divided, gray-green leaves.

The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits called drupes, which contain the seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and herb gardens.

Its essential oils, rich in compounds like carvacrol and thymol, contribute to its use in culinary and medicinal applications.
