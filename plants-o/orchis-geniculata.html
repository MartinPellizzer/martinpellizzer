Orchis geniculata

Orchis geniculata, commonly known as the bent orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often curved floral stems. The plant produces showy, violet or purple flowers arranged in a raceme, with a unique structure that includes a lip-like petal. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows.

Orchis geniculata is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
