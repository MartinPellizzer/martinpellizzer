Oxalis hedysarifolia

Oxalis hedysarifolia, commonly known as the spurred oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive spurred flowers that resemble small horns. The plant produces bright yellow blooms with five petals and prominent, spurred appendages at the base. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three heart-shaped leaflets, and it often grows in rocky or sandy soils.

Oxalis hedysarifolia is appreciated for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
