Oxytropis halleri subsp. velutina

Oxytropis halleri subsp.

velutina, commonly known as velvet locoweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dense, velvety pubescence, which gives it its common name.
