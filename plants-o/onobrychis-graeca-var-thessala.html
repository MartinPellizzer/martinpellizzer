Onobrychis graeca var. thessala

Onobrychis graeca var. thessala, commonly known as Greek clover, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. It has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping and for forage in dry climates.
