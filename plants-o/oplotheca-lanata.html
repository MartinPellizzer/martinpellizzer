Oplotheca lanata

Oplotheca lanata, commonly known as the woolly oplotheca, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, woolly leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered together. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Oplotheca lanata thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforest understories.
