Oenothera exigua

Oenothera exigua, commonly known as the narrowleaf evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pale yellow flowers that open in the evening. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Oenothera exigua is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Its blooming period typically occurs in late summer to early fall.
