Orchis × rouyana

Orchis × rouyana, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a natural cross between Orchis moria and Orchis mascula.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, woodland habitats. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers with a distinct lip that aids in pollination. Its botanical features include a basal rosette of leaves and a flowering stem that can reach up to 60 cm in height.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in the wild across parts of Europe.
