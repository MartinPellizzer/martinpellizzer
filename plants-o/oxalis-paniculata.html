Oxalis paniculata

Oxalis paniculata, commonly known as the clustered wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces elliptical, red-brown seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

Oxalis paniculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
