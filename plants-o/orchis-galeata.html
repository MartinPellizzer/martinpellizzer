Orchis galeata

Orchis galeata, commonly known as the early purple orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in woodland and grassland habitats. The plant features a tall, erect stem with broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces spikes of violet or purple flowers. Its roots contain compounds that have been historically used in traditional medicine.

The species is characterized by its showy blooms and is often associated with calcareous soils.
