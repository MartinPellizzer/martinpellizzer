Ophrys × pseudofusca

Ophrys × pseudofusca, commonly known as the bee orchid hybrid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and exhibits unique botanical features.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys fucata, showcasing a blend of traits from both parents. The plant is characterized by its specialized floral morphology, with a lip that mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. It typically grows in calcareous soils and is found in grasslands and woodland edges across Europe.

Its reproductive strategy relies heavily on mimicry and specific pollinator interactions, making it a fascinating subject in the study of plant-animal co-evolution.
