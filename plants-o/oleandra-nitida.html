Oleandra nitida

Oleandra nitida, commonly known as the silver oleander, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, typically blooming in spring and summer. Oleandra nitida is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

However, it is important to note that all parts of this plant are toxic and should be handled with care.
