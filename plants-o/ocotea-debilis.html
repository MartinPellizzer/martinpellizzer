Ocotea debilis

Ocotea debilis, commonly known as the weak ocotea, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, fragrant flowers and dark blue berries that are an important food source for various bird species.

Ocotea debilis is often found in shaded understory environments due to its preference for moist, well-drained soils.
