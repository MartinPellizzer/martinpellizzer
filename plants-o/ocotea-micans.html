Ocotea micans

Ocotea micans, commonly known as the fragrant ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves that emit a pleasant scent when crushed. Its small, yellowish-white flowers are followed by oval, dark blue berries.

Ocotea micans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and aromatic qualities.
