Osbeckia setosoannulata

Osbeckia setosoannulata, commonly known as the hairy ringed willow.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves that are covered in stiff, bristly hairs, giving it its common name. Its flowering stems rise from the center of the rosette, bearing small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a spike-like cluster.

Osbeckia setosoannulata is adapted to grow in dry, rocky environments and is often found in high-altitude grasslands.
