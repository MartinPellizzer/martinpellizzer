Oxalis poeppigii var. segetalis

Oxalis poeppigii var. segetalis, commonly known as the wheatgrass oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which resemble those of clover, and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a short, erect stem.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
