Ocimum labiatum

Ocimum labiatum, commonly known as holy basil or tulsi, is a species of basil native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a herbaceous plant with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or white, arranged in spikes. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its medicinal and aromatic properties.

Ocimum labiatum is widely used in traditional medicine and as a spice in various cuisines.
