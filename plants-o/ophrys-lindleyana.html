Ophrys lindleyana

Ophrys lindleyana, commonly known as the Lindley's bee orchid, is a species of orchid native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brown in color, with intricate patterns that enhance the mimicry.

Ophrys lindleyana is a perennial herb that produces a single spike of flowers, often blooming in late spring to early summer.
