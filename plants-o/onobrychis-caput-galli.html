Onobrychis caput-galli

Onobrychis caput-galli, commonly known as the "Crown of the Gall," is a perennial legume native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, sparsely branched stems and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, followed by distinctive, inflated pods that resemble galls. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

Onobrychis caput-galli is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
