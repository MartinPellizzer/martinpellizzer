Ocotea chrysobalanoides

Ocotea chrysobalanoides, commonly known as the golden ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall, with a dense canopy and smooth, greyish bark. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a distinct aromatic scent when crushed.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
