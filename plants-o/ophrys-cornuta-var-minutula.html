Ophrys cornuta var. minutula

Ophrys cornuta var. minutula, commonly known as the minute horned orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its intricate, bee-mimicking flowers that play a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers are small, with a distinctive horn-shaped spur and a complex arrangement of petals that mimic female bees.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance.
