Ocotea discolor

Ocotea discolor, commonly known as the white ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. Its fragrant flowers are small and yellowish, and it produces dark blue to black berries that are edible for some bird species.

Ocotea discolor is often found in humid lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
