Osmanthus cymosus

Osmanthus cymosus, commonly known as sweet osmanthus, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, releasing a sweet, honey-like aroma. The plant produces small, round, dark brown fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and have a smooth texture.

Osmanthus cymosus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the use of its flowers in teas and perfumes.
