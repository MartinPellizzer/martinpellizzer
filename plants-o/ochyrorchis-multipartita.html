Ochyrorchis multipartita

Ochyrorchis multipartita, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the temperate regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its hairy, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces a single, tall spike bearing numerous white to pale yellow flowers with a distinctive, intricate pattern. Ochyrorchis multipartita thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky areas.

Its flowering period typically occurs in late winter to early spring, making it a notable species in the orchid family.
