Ostericum koreanum

Ostericum koreanum, commonly known as Korean gingergrass, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, greenish flowers. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks.

Ostericum koreanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
