Oxytropis deflexa var. culminis

Oxytropis deflexa var. culminis, commonly known as the mountain locoweed, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats at high elevations, often found in alpine and subalpine regions. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces yellow to reddish-purple pea-like flowers. It is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including cold temperatures and poor soil.

Oxytropis deflexa var. culminis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for certain wildlife species.
