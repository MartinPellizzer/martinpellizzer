Oldenlandia riparia

Oldenlandia riparia, commonly known as river morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as riverbanks and forest floors.

Oldenlandia riparia is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
