Oxalis goyazensis

Oxalis goyazensis, commonly known as the Brazilian wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces bright orange berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. Oxalis goyazensis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental species in horticulture.
