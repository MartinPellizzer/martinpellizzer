Onoseris conspicua

Onoseris conspicua, commonly known as the showy goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk of tubular florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils.

Onoseris conspicua is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
