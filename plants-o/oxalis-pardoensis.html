Oxalis pardoensis

Oxalis pardoensis, commonly known as the Pardo's wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its foliage and flowers are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Oxalis pardoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
