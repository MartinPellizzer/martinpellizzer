Ochna procera

Ochna procera, commonly known as the African glorybower, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically appearing in spring. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Ochna procera thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its striking floral display and ability to attract pollinators.
