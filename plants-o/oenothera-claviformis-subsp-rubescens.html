Oenothera claviformis subsp. rubescens

Oenothera claviformis subsp.

rubescens, commonly known as the red-stemmed evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its erect, branching stems that turn reddish in color, especially under stress or during the growing season.

The plant produces showy yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Oenothera claviformis subsp.

rubescens is a perennial species that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.
