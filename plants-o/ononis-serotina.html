Ononis serotina

Ononis serotina, commonly known as late whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom from late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in sandy or gravelly soils.

Ononis serotina is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to tolerate salt spray and harsh environmental conditions.
