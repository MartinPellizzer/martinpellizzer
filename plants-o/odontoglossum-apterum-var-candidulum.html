Odontoglossum apterum var. candidulum

Odontoglossum apterum var. candidulum, commonly known as the white-flowered orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and pale green to white-colored flowers. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hair.

It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil for optimal growth.
