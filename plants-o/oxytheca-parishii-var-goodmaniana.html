Oxytheca parishii var. goodmaniana

Oxytheca parishii var. goodmaniana, commonly known as the Goodwin's oxytheca, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough texture and a strong, spicy scent.

The flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters, making it an attractive addition to botanical gardens.
