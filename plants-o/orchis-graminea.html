Orchis graminea

Orchis graminea, commonly known as the grass orchid, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and thrives in woodland or meadow habitats. Its flowers are usually arranged in a loose spike and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Orchis graminea is known for its ability to form dense colonies and is often found in open, sunny areas with well-drained soil.
