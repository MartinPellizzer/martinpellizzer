Olea acuminata

Olea acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf olive, is a flowering plant in the Oleaceae family.

It is native to parts of Africa, particularly in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant yellow flowers. It produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife.

Olea acuminata is often found in woodland and savanna habitats, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
