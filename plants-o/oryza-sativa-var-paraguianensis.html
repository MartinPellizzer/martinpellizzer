Oryza sativa var. paraguianensis

Oryza sativa var. paraguianensis, commonly known as the Paraguayan rice, is a less commonly cultivated variety of rice native to South America.

This plant is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and compact panicles that produce small, white flowers. It thrives in wetland environments and requires a tropical or subtropical climate for optimal growth. The grains of this rice are typically shorter and more aromatic compared to other rice varieties.

Due to its limited cultivation, it is of particular interest for botanical and agricultural research.
