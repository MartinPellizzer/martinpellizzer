Oxycoccus quadripetalus f. globatopyricarpus

Oxycoccus quadripetalus f. globatopyricarpus, commonly known as the globular-fruited oxycoccus, is a variant of the common oxycoccus, characterized by its unique, rounded fruit structure.

This plant is a dwarf shrub belonging to the Ericaceae family, typically found in boreal and subarctic regions. Its leaves are small, dark green, and leathery, adapted to cold climates. The flowers are usually white or pale pink, and the fruit is a small, fleshy berry that ripens to a dark red or black color.

This form is notable for its compact growth habit and is often used in horticultural collections for its ornamental value.
