Ophrys × lambrechtsiana

Ophrys × lambrechtsiana, commonly known as the Lambrechts' bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is characterized by its intricate, bee-like floral structure that mimics the appearance and scent of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is found in Mediterranean regions, including parts of southern Europe and North Africa. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brownish in color, with a distinctive, hairy lip that enhances its mimicry.

This species is notable for its specialized pollination mechanism and is considered a rare and protected plant in many areas.
