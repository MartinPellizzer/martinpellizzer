Odontostephana obliqua

Odontostephana obliqua, commonly known as the zigzag orchid, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often zigzagging inflorescence. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are small and fragrant, often displaying a range of colors from white to pink.

Odontostephana obliqua is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
