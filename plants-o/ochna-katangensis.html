Ochna katangensis

Ochna katangensis, commonly known as the Katanga ochna, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central Africa.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Ochna katangensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
