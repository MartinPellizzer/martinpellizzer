Ophrys × grafiana

Ophrys × grafiana, commonly known as the Greek orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the genus Ophrys, which is known for its mimicry of female insects to attract male pollinators. The plant produces strikingly complex flowers with intricate patterns that resemble insect bodies. It typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in temperate climates.

Ophrys × grafiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
