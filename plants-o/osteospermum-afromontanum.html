Osteospermum afromontanum

Osteospermum afromontanum, commonly known as the African Sunset or Mountain Blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, daisy-like blooms with blue to purple petals. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for gardeners in arid regions.
