Opuntia phaeacantha var. covillei

Opuntia phaeacantha var. covillei, commonly known as the Coville prickly pear, is a cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in sharp spines and glochids. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in desert scrub and rocky soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand harsh conditions.
