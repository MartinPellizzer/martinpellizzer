Oxalis edwardsiae

Oxalis edwardsiae, commonly known as the Edwards' wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage contains oxalic acid, which can be toxic if ingested in large quantities.

Oxalis edwardsiae is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
