Osteospermum amplexicaule

Osteospermum amplexicaule, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright, daisy-like flowers that come in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant has basal leaves that are often hairy and clustered at the base, while the stems are upright and branching. Osteospermum amplexicaule thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ability to produce numerous blooms throughout the growing season adds visual interest and attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
