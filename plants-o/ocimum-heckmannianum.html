Ocimum heckmannianum

Ocimum heckmannianum, commonly known as the Mexican basil, is a species of flowering plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

The plant's botanical features include opposite leaves and a square stem, typical of the mint family.
