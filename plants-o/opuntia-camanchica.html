Opuntia camanchica

Opuntia camanchica, commonly known as the desert prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems that are covered in spines and glochids, which provide protection from herbivores. The plant produces vibrant yellow or red flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tunas, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

Opuntia camanchica is well adapted to survive in harsh desert conditions with minimal water.
