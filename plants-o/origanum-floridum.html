Origanum floridum

Origanum floridum, commonly known as Florida oregano, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pink flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping. Its foliage is often used in culinary applications, while the flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Origanum floridum is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
