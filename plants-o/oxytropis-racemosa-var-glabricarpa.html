Oxytropis racemosa var. glabricarpa

Oxytropis racemosa var. glabricarpa, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed locoweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright, often branched stems and small, compound leaves that are glabrous or nearly hairless. The plant produces yellow to reddish-purple pea-like flowers in racemose inflorescences, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The species is adapted to arid environments and is often found in regions with well-drained soils.
