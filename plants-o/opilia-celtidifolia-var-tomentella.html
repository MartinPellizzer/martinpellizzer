Opilia celtidifolia var. tomentella

Opilia celtidifolia var. tomentella, commonly known as the woolly opilia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its densely hairy, grayish foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape and have a leathery texture.

Opilia celtidifolia var. tomentella is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
