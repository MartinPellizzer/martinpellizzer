Oxalis biloba var. trinervia

Oxalis biloba var. trinervia, commonly known as the three-veined wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with three heart-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers. The plant is characterized by its delicate, creeping growth habit and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Its botanical name reflects its distinctive three-veined leaf structure, which is a notable feature in its morphology.

Oxalis biloba var. trinervia is also known for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to thrive in partial shade.
