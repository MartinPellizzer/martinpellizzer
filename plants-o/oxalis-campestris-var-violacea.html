Oxalis campestris var. violacea

Oxalis campestris var. violacea, commonly known as violet wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 6 to 12 inches, featuring trifoliate leaves with a distinctive violet or purple hue. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

Oxalis campestris var. violacea is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and flowers.
