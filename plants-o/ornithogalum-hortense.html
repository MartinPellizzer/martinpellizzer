Ornithogalum hortense

Ornithogalum hortense, commonly known as the garden belladonna, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its elegant appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

However, it is toxic, containing compounds that can be harmful to humans and animals if ingested.
