Oncidium sarcodes

Oncidium sarcodes, commonly known as the chocolate orchid, is a tropical orchid species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark, velvety petals that resemble chocolate, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are fragrant and bloom in clusters, often in shades of brown, orange, and red.

Oncidium sarcodes requires bright, indirect light and moderate humidity to thrive.
