Oxalis latifolia subsp. schraderiana

Oxalis latifolia subsp.

schraderiana, commonly known as the broadleaf wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial native to South Africa. It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves with prominent veins, and produces clusters of small, delicate flowers in shades of yellow or orange.

The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover due to its compact growth habit.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and prolonged flowering period.
