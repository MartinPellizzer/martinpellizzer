Ophelia macrosperma

Ophelia macrosperma, commonly known as broad-leaf waterplantain, is a perennial aquatic plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving freshwater habitats such as ponds, lakes, and streams. The plant features long, narrow leaves that are often submerged or floating at the water's surface. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in dense spikes, which emerge above the water.

Ophelia macrosperma plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.
