Oxytropis lanata var. psilocarpa

Oxytropis lanata var. psilocarpa, commonly known as the velvet cowpea, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
