Oenothera reynoldsii f. semialta

Oenothera reynoldsii f. semialta, commonly known as the tall evening primrose, is a variant of the evening primrose species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and large, showy yellow flowers that open in the evening. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

This species is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland habitats.
