Oxalis jaliscana

Oxalis jaliscana, commonly known as the Mexican wood sorrel, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its foliage is often adorned with reddish veins, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Oxalis jaliscana is valued in horticulture for its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of garden settings.
