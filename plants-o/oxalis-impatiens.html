Oxalis impatiens

Oxalis impatiens, commonly known as wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and flowers.

Oxalis impatiens is also known for its ability to spread quickly, making it both a desirable and potentially invasive species.
