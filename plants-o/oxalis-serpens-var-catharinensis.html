Oxalis serpens var. catharinensis

Oxalis serpens var. catharinensis, commonly known as the Catlin's wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features delicate, trifoliate leaves that resemble small hearts, with a pale green to reddish hue. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for woodland gardens and shaded rockeries.
