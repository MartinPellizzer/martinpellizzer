Oxytropis merkensis f. albiflora

Oxytropis merkensis f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its upright, herbaceous growth habit and produces small, clustered white flowers. The plant is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and Mongolia. Its leaves are trifoliate, with small, hairy leaflets, and it has a deep taproot system adapted to dry conditions.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
