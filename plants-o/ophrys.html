Ophrys

Ophrys, commonly known as the bee orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are renowned for their intricate and often bee-like floral structures, which play a crucial role in their pollination strategy. The flowers typically feature a specialized lip or labellum that mimics the appearance and scent of female bees, attracting male bees for pollination. Ophrys species are found across Europe, Asia, and North Africa, often growing in moist, calcareous soils.

Their unique adaptations make them an important subject in the study of plant evolution and mimicry.
