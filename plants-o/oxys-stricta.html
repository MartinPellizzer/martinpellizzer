Oxys stricta

Oxys stricta, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright, rigid stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in prairies and open woods.

Oxys stricta is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
