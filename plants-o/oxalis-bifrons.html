Oxalis bifrons

Oxalis bifrons, commonly known as the two-lobed wood sorrel, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins and produces small, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. It is characterized by its delicate appearance and is often found in the understory of forests.

Oxalis bifrons is a member of the Oxalidaceae family, known for its calcium oxalate crystals in its tissues.
