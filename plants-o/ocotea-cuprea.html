Ocotea cuprea

Ocotea cuprea, commonly known as the red ocotea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown bark and elliptical, dark green leaves. Its small, white flowers are borne in clusters and are followed by dark, spherical berries.

Ocotea cuprea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
