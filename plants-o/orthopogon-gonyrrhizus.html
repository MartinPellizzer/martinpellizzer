Orthopogon gonyrrhizus

Orthopogon gonyrrhizus, commonly known as the broad-leafed grass or the narrow-leafed grass, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and disturbed soils. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets, typical of the grass family.

Orthopogon gonyrrhizus is often found in temperate regions and is adapted to a variety of soil types.
