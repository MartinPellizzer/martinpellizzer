Olearia newbeyi

Olearia newbeyi, commonly known as the Newbey's daisy, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and subalpine regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while the flowers are white to pale yellow in color.

Olearia newbeyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
