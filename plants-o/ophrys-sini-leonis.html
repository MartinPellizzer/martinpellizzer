Ophrys sini-leonis

Ophrys sini-leonis, commonly known as the Chinese bee orchid, is a species of orchid native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers are usually yellow or brown with intricate patterns that aid in attracting male bees for pollination.

Ophrys sini-leonis is a terrestrial orchid with a short flowering period, often blooming in spring or early summer.
