Orchis papilionacea subsp. chlorotica

Orchis papilionacea subsp.

chlorotica, commonly known as the green-winged orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive greenish-blue flowers with prominent yellow markings, which help in attracting pollinators.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or grassland habitats. It has a short, erect stem with a single, showy flower that blooms in late spring to early summer.

The subspecies is notable for its unique coloration and ecological role in supporting pollinator biodiversity.
