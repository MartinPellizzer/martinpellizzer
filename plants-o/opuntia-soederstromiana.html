Opuntia soederstromiana

Opuntia soederstromiana, commonly known as the Söderström's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in glochids, which are fine, barbed hairs that can cause irritation. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers during the summer months, followed by edible, fleshy fruits known as tunas. Opuntia soederstromiana is adapted to survive in hot, dry environments with minimal water.

Its structure and growth habits make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
