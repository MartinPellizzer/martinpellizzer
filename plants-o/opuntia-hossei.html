Opuntia hossei

Opuntia hossei, commonly known as the desert hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a sprawling, low-growing cactus with thick, ribbed stems that store water, enabling it to thrive in harsh desert conditions. Its leaves are modified into spiny, paddle-shaped structures that reduce water loss and provide protection from herbivores. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in late spring, which are pollinated by insects and birds.

Opuntia hossei is also known for its ability to form dense colonies, contributing to soil stabilization in its native habitat.
