Oxybaphus campestris

Oxybaphus campestris, commonly known as the field borage, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features hairy, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces blue to violet flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its flowers are rich in nectar, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Oxybaphus campestris is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in full sun.
