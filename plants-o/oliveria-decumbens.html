Oliveria decumbens

Oliveria decumbens, commonly known as the African mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, white flowers. The plant has a symbiotic relationship with its host trees, drawing water and nutrients through its haustoria. It produces dark blue, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Due to its ecological role and unique appearance, it is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
