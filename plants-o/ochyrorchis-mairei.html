Ochyrorchis mairei

Ochyrorchis mairei, commonly known as the Maire's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded forest understories, where it thrives in rich, organic soils. The plant features a slender rhizome with a few leaves, and its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color. Ochyrorchis mairei produces small, delicate orchid flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer.

Due to habitat loss, this species is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
