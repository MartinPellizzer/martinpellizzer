Oxybaphus wrightii

Oxybaphus wrightii, commonly known as Wright's oxybaphus, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, which are typically visited by various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Oxybaphus wrightii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowering habit.
