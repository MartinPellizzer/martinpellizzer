Oreocarya dolosa

Oreocarya dolosa, commonly known as the Sierra lily, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or talus slopes, thriving in well-drained, sunny conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette that helps conserve moisture.

Oreocarya dolosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable climates.
