Osmanthus acutus

Osmanthus acutus, commonly known as the sharp-leafed osmanthus, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is prized for its aromatic blossoms, which are used in traditional Chinese medicine and as a flavoring in teas and confections. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties contribute to its cultural significance in East Asian horticulture.
