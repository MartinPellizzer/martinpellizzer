Odontoglossum crocidipterum subsp. dormanianum

Odontoglossum crocidipterum subsp.

dormanianum, commonly known as the Dormanian's orchid, is a species native to the Andes Mountains of South America. It is a terrestrial orchid with a compact growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white.

The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, and its leaves are broad and elliptical. It prefers cool, humid environments with well-drained soil and partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
