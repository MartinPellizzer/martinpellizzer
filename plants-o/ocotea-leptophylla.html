Ocotea leptophylla

Ocotea leptophylla, commonly known as the slender-leaf ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and avocado. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered white flowers. Its fruit is a dark purple drupe that contains a single seed.

Ocotea leptophylla is often found in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests.
