Ornithogalum comosum subsp. garganicum

Ornithogalum comosum subsp.

garganicum, commonly known as the Garganicum star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers in late spring.

The plant is characterized by its bulbous root system, which stores nutrients and water. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in garden settings.
