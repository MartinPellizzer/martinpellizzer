Oxyria caucasica

Oxyria caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian oxyria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous areas. The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually greenish-white and appear in late spring.

Oxyria caucasica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in shady gardens.
