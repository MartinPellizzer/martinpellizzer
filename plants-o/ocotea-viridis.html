Ocotea viridis

Ocotea viridis, commonly known as green ocotea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly found in humid lowland forests. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a drupe, typically containing a single seed, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife.

Ocotea viridis is also valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional crafts and as a source of essential oils.
