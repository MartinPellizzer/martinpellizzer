Onobrychis unicomis

Onobrychis unicomis, commonly known as the single-seeded burclover, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces distinctive, one-seeded pods that are often used as a forage crop for livestock.

Its botanical features include a deep root system, which helps it thrive in well-drained soils and drought conditions.
