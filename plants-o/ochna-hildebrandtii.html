Ochna hildebrandtii

Ochna hildebrandtii, commonly known as the African coral tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.
