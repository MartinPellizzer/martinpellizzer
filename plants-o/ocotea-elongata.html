Ocotea elongata

Ocotea elongata, commonly known as the longleaf ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant typically grows in humid, lowland forests and can reach heights of up to 20 meters. Its leaves are elongated, leathery, and often have a distinctive aromatic scent.

The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark, spherical berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species.
