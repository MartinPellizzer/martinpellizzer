Ophrys bremifera

Ophrys bremifera, commonly known as the Bremifera bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to specific regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee, a feature evolved to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or along stream banks. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brownish in color, with intricate patterns that aid in its deceptive pollination strategy.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Ophrys bremifera is considered a vulnerable species in many areas.
