Oxalis polyantha

Oxalis polyantha, commonly known as the woodland sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive pattern of light green veins, and small, delicate flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks. It is a perennial species that produces bright yellow flowers in early spring.

Oxalis polyantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
