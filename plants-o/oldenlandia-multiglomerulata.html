Oldenlandia multiglomerulata

Oldenlandia multiglomerulata, commonly known as the clustered spiderwort, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through runners, making it both a useful ground cover and a potential weed.
