Opuntia tuberculosirhopalica

Opuntia tuberculosirhopalica, commonly known as the narrow-ribbed prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, ribbed stems that are covered in small, spiny tubercles. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the summer months, followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting well to poor soil conditions.

Opuntia tuberculosirhopalica is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
