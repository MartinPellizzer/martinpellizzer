Octomeria sulfurea

Octomeria sulfurea, commonly known as the sulfur bush, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Octomeria sulfurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
