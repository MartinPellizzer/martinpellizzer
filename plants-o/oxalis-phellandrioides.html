Oxalis phellandrioides

Oxalis phellandrioides, commonly known as the lemon gem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features a basal rosette of fleshy, lobed leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of yellow and orange, resembling miniature sunflowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a favored ornamental plant in gardens worldwide.
