Oxalis rubra

Oxalis rubra, commonly known as the redwood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are heart-shaped and often tinged with red or purple veins. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

Oxalis rubra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
