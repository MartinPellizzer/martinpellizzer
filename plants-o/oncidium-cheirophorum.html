Oncidium cheirophorum

Oncidium cheirophorum, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers with dark green petals and a distinctive red lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in seasonal climates.

Oncidium cheirophorum is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its long-lasting, showy blooms.
