Ocotea vernicosa

Ocotea vernicosa, commonly known as the yellow-laurel or yellow-camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, white, clustered flowers that are followed by dark blue berries. Its wood is highly valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

Ocotea vernicosa thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland tropical forests.
