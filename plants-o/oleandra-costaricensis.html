Oleandra costaricensis

Oleandra costaricensis, commonly known as the Costa Rican oleander, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow, white, and pink. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth texture. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils near rivers or in forest understories.

Oleandra costaricensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
