Oxytropis halleri var. ochroleuca

Oxytropis halleri var. ochroleuca, commonly known as white locoweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow to white flowers. The plant is typically found in dry, rocky habitats such as sagebrush steppe and grasslands. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

The plant is also known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock.
