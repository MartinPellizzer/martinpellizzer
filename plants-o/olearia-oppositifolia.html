Olearia oppositifolia

Olearia oppositifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and daisy-like white flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and forested areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are opposite in arrangement and covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Olearia oppositifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively low maintenance in suitable climates.
