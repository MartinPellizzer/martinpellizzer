Oxalis avilensis

Oxalis avilensis, commonly known as the Mexican wood sorrel, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish or purple tinge, often resembling those of other oxalis species. The plant produces small, five-petaled flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Oxalis avilensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
