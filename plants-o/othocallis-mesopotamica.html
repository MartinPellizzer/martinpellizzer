Othocallis mesopotamica

Othocallis mesopotamica, commonly known as the Mesopotamian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the region of Mesopotamia.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, clay-rich soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are often green or brown in color. It is part of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants.

Othocallis mesopotamica is noted for its ecological importance in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and supporting local biodiversity.
