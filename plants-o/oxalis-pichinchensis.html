Oxalis pichinchensis

Oxalis pichinchensis, commonly known as the Andean wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in mountainous areas. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive veins and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a stem that may be hairy or smooth depending on the variety.

Oxalis pichinchensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and flowers.
