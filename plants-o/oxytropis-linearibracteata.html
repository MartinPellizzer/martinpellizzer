Oxytropis linearibracteata

Oxytropis linearibracteata, commonly known as the narrow bract locoweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, bean-like pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe, often growing in well-drained soils.
