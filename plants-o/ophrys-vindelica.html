Ophrys vindelica

Ophrys vindelica, commonly known as the Vindelica bee orchid, is a species of orchid native to the alpine regions of Central and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its unique flower structure, which closely resembles a bee, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brownish in color, with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of a female bee.

Ophrys vindelica is a rare and endangered species, often found in specific microhabitats that are highly sensitive to environmental changes.
