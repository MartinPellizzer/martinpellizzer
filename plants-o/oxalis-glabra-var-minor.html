Oxalis glabra var. minor

Oxalis glabra var. minor, commonly known as the small-leaf wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves composed of three heart-shaped leaflets, with a distinctive white or pale green coloration. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland understories.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
