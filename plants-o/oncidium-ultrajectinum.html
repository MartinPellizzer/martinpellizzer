Oncidium ultrajectinum

Oncidium ultrajectinum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or golden in color, with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. The plant has long, slender pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to thrive in its natural habitat. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air and surrounding environment.

Oncidium ultrajectinum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
