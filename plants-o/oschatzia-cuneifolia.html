Oschatzia cuneifolia

Oschatzia cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf oschatzia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, delicate flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude grasslands. Its botanical features make it a unique subject for horticultural study and conservation efforts.

Oschatzia cuneifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
