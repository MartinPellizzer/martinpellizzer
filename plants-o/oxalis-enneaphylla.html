Oxalis enneaphylla

Oxalis enneaphylla, commonly known as the nine-leaf wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each leaflet being heart-shaped with a distinctive white or pale green midrib. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling those of the genus Oxalis. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or woodland settings.

Oxalis enneaphylla is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its delicate foliage and floral display.
