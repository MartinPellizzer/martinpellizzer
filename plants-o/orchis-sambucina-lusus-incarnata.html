Orchis sambucina lusus incarnata

Orchis sambucina lusus incarnata, commonly known as the Pink Butterfly Orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to specific regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its unique, butterfly-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to white, often with a purple or violet tinge. The plant typically grows in woodland habitats, preferring moist, shaded environments with rich, humus-filled soil. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered in many parts of its range.
