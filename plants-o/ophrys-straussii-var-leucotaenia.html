Ophrys straussii var. leucotaenia

Ophrys straussii var. leucotaenia, commonly known as the white-tailed helleborine, is a perennial orchid native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive floral structure, featuring a labellum that mimics the appearance of a female bee, a trait used in its unique pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy texture, while the flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a white throat.

This orchid is notable for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils, showcasing its adaptability to various ecological niches.
