Ornithogalum tenuifolium subsp. trichophyllum

Ornithogalum tenuifolium subsp.

trichophyllum, commonly known as the woolly cowslip, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It features a basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves that give it its subspecies name, *trichophyllum*, meaning "hairy.".

The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This species is often found in rocky or sandy habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
