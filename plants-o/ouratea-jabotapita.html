Ouratea jabotapita

Ouratea jabotapita, commonly known as the Brazilian starwood, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ochnaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white or yellowish flowers that are often followed by red or orange berries. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
