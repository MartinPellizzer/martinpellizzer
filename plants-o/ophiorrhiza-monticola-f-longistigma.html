Ophiorrhiza monticola f. longistigma

Ophiorrhiza monticola f. longistigma, commonly known as the long-stemmed mountain mistflower, is a rare variant of the Ophiorrhiza genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its slender, elongated stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountainous regions and forest understories. Its flowers are usually small and white, arranged in dense inflorescences, and it produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds.

This botanical feature makes it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic studies.
