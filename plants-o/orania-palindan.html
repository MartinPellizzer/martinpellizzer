Orania palindan

Orania palindan, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous tiny florets. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention. Its showy yellow to orange flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Orania palindan is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought-resistant properties.
