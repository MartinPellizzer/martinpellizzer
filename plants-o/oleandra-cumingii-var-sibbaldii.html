Oleandra cumingii var. sibbaldii

Oleandra cumingii var. sibbaldii, commonly known as the Sibbald's oleander, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Oleandra cumingii var. sibbaldii is also valued for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions and its resilience to drought.
