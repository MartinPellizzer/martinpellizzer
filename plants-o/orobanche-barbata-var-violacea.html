Orobanche barbata var. violacea

Orobanche barbata var. violacea, commonly known as the violet broomweed, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in association with legumes and other herbaceous plants. Its reproductive strategy involves producing small, dry seeds that can remain viable in the soil for extended periods.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can cause significant stress to its host plants, particularly in agricultural settings.
