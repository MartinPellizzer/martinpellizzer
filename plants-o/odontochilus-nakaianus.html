Odontochilus nakaianus

Odontochilus nakaianus, commonly known as the Nakaian stone orchid, is a rare and delicate epiphytic orchid native to the subtropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, compact growth habit and possesses a unique root system that enables it to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrates. The plant typically produces tiny, greenish-white flowers that are highly specialized for pollination by specific insect species. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered and is often found in shaded, humid environments on tree trunks or rocks.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as an indicator of undisturbed tropical ecosystems and its potential for scientific research in plant adaptation.
