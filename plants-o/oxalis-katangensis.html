Oxalis katangensis

Oxalis katangensis, commonly known as the Katanga wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to Central Africa.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its foliage contains oxalic acid, which can be toxic if ingested in large quantities.

Oxalis katangensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
