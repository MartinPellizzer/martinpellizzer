Ochyrorchis gilbertii

Ochyrorchis gilbertii, commonly known as the Gilbert's fairy orchid, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to the eastern regions of Australia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rainforests and understoreys. The plant features delicate, strap-like leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. Its flowering period occurs during the cooler months, usually between August and December.

The species is notable for its unique floral structure and is often found in association with specific mycorrhizal fungi.
