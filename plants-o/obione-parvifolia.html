Obione parvifolia

Obione parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved obione, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
