Oritrophium yacuriense

Oritrophium yacuriense, commonly known as the Yacuri goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in temperate to cold climates. Its yellow-orange inflorescences are composed of numerous tiny florets, which are arranged in a radiate pattern.

Oritrophium yacuriense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
