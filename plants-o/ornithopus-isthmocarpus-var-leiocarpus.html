Ornithopus isthmocarpus var. leiocarpus

Ornithopus isthmocarpus var. leiocarpus, commonly known as the coastal hawksbeard, is a flowering plant native to the western United States, particularly in coastal regions.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, yellow flowers and distinctive, feathery seed pods. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to saline and coastal environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months.
