Oenothera scindens

Oenothera scindens, commonly known as the breaking grass or evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and showy yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in open habitats such as fields and roadsides.

Oenothera scindens is a member of the Onagraceae family, which includes other notable species like the evening primrose (Oenothera biennis).
