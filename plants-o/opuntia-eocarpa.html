Opuntia eocarpa

Opuntia eocarpa, commonly known as the ancient prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, spineless pads and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a succulent stem that stores water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. Its fruit, known as a tuna, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

Opuntia eocarpa plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing food and shelter for various desert animals.
