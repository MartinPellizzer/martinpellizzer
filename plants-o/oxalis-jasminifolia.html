Oxalis jasminifolia

Oxalis jasminifolia, commonly known as the jasmine wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that resemble jasmine. The plant produces yellowish-white blooms with a strong, sweet scent, often blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Oxalis jasminifolia is also known for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
