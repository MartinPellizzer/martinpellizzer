Ocotea loxensis

Ocotea loxensis, commonly known as the Brazilian ocotea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of eastern Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are typically elliptical and have a glossy surface. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually white or pale yellow and are arranged in clustered inflorescences.

Ocotea loxensis is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional crafts and as a source of essential oils.
