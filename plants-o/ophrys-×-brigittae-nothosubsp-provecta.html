Ophrys × brigittae nothosubsp. provecta

Ophrys × brigittae nothosubsp.

provecta, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant that exhibits unique botanical characteristics. It is a sterile hybrid resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera, showcasing traits from both parent species.

The plant is notable for its intricate floral morphology, particularly its labellum, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. Its flowers are typically yellowish-brown with intricate patterns, and it thrives in calcareous soils in open habitats.

This nothosubspecies is of particular interest to botanists due to its rarity and the complexity of its hybrid origin.
