Orchis × beyrichii nothosubsp. mackaensis

Orchis × beyrichii nothosubsp.

mackaensis, commonly known as the Mackaensic orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet or purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in grassland habitats and thrives in well-drained soils. It has lanceolate leaves and produces a single spike of flowers, often with a sweet fragrance.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
