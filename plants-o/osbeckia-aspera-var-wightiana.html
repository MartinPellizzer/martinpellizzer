Osbeckia aspera var. wightiana

Osbeckia aspera var. wightiana, commonly known as the Wight's rough osbeckia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, temperate environments. The plant features upright stems covered in stiff, rough hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. Its small, yellowish-white flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, spherical fruits.

Osbeckia aspera var. wightiana is often found in rocky or alpine habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native range.
