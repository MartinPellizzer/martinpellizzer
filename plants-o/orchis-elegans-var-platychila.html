Orchis elegans var. platychila

Orchis elegans var. platychila, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in woodland habitats with moist, well-drained soils. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces striking, violet-purple flowers in late spring. Its flowers are characterized by their elongated shape and are often arranged in a loose spike.

This variety is noted for its delicate appearance and is often found in shaded, humid environments.
