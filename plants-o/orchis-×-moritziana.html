Orchis × moritziana

Orchis × moritziana, commonly known as the Moritz's orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Orchis morio and Orchis mascula.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or meadow habitats. The plant produces tall, erect stems bearing showy, violet or purple flowers with a distinctive spurred lip. Its botanical features include a complex inflorescence and specialized pollination mechanisms adapted to its natural environment.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
