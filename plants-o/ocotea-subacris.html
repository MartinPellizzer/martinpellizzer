Ocotea subacris

Ocotea subacris, commonly known as the slender ocotea, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic evergreen leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife.

Ocotea subacris is often found in humid, shaded environments within rainforests.
