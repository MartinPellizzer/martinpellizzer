Ocotea microcarpa

Ocotea microcarpa, commonly known as the Mexican cinnamon or sweet cinnamon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like laurel and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its dark, aromatic bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its small, fragrant flowers are followed by bright red berries that attract various wildlife.

Ocotea microcarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems.
