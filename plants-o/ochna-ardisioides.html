Ochna ardisioides

Ochna ardisioides, commonly known as the red ink tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement, and its bark is smooth and reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
