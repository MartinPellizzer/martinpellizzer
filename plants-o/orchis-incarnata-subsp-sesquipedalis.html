Orchis incarnata subsp. sesquipedalis

Orchis incarnata subsp.

sesquipedalis, commonly known as the pink marsh orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America. It typically grows in moist soils along the edges of ponds, marshes, and streams.

The plant features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of pink to purple flowers with a distinctive spurred lip. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
