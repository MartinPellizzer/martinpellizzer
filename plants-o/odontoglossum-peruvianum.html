Odontoglossum peruvianum

Odontoglossum peruvianum, commonly known as the Peruvian orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with erect, hairy stems and linear to lanceolate leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, often with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Odontoglossum peruvianum is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
