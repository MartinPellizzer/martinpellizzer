Ochlandra brandisii

Ochlandra brandisii, commonly known as the Brandis's orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its elegant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its roots are often mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to enhance nutrient uptake.

Ochlandra brandisii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
