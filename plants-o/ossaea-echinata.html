Ossaea echinata

Ossaea echinata, commonly known as the spiny ossaea, is a perennial plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant has adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through its deep root system and waxy leaf coatings. Its flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological role in its native habitat.

Ossaea echinata is often found in rocky soils and is considered a hardy, drought-tolerant species.
