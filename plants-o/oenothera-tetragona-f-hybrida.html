Oenothera tetragona f. hybrida

Oenothera tetragona f. hybrida, commonly known as the hybrid four-cornered evening primrose, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has four-angled stems and lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
