Octas

Octas, commonly known as the octopus flower, is a unique plant species native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, spiral-shaped inflorescence that resembles an octopus, hence its common name. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. Octas thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Its unusual appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
