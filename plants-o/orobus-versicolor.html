Orobus versicolor

Orobus versicolor, commonly known as the variable orchid, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often colorful flowers that vary in shape and hue. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

Orobus versicolor is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral display.
