Oenanthe media var. crassipes

Oenanthe media var. crassipes, commonly known as the broad-leaved water dropwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It features slender, branched stems that often grow from the base and are covered in fine, soft hairs. The leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and have a slightly toothed margin, typically ranging from 5 to 15 cm in length. Small white to pale yellow flowers are arranged in umbrella-like clusters, blooming in late spring to early summer.

This plant is adapted to moist soils and is often found in areas with standing water or saturated ground.
