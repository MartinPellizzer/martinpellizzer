Oxalis truncatula

Oxalis truncatula, commonly known as the heartleaf wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves that are heart-shaped and often have a waxy appearance, with a distinctive yellowish-green coloration. The plant produces small, five-petaled flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in early spring. Its stems are slender and hairy, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Oxalis truncatula is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
