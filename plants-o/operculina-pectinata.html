Operculina pectinata

Operculina pectinata, commonly known as the African buttercup, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a distinctive, cup-shaped structure known as an operculum. The plant produces large, showy blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate and lobed, with a rough texture.

Operculina pectinata is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soils with partial to full sun.
