Oxydectes glutinosa

Oxydectes glutinosa, commonly known as the sticky tamarind, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stem and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a sticky resin. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by elongated, woody pods containing several seeds. Its sticky resin has been traditionally used in local medicine and as a natural adhesive.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
