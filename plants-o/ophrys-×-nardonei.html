Ophrys × nardonei

Ophrys × nardonei, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a rare and fascinating plant resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is characterized by its intricate, bee-like floral structures that mimic the appearance and scent of female bees, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in woodlands or along stream banks. Its flowers are usually yellow to brown in color, with intricate patterns that aid in attracting male bees for pollination.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Ophrys × nardonei is considered a vulnerable species in many regions.
