Oxalis denticulata

Oxalis denticulata, commonly known as the toothed wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and forest floors. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively.

Oxalis denticulata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and delicate blossoms.
