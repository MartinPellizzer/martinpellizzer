Onobrychis amoena

Onobrychis amoena, commonly known as sweet clover, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable in agricultural settings. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Onobrychis amoena is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
