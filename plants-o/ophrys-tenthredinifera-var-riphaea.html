Ophrys tenthredinifera var. riphaea

Ophrys tenthredinifera var. riphaea, commonly known as the bee orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Europe.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which resembles a bee, aiding in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in grassy meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brown, with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of insects.

This orchid is known for its specialized relationship with specific pollinators, making it an important species in ecological studies.
