Orchis obtusa

Orchis obtusa, commonly known as the blunt orchid, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers are usually arranged in a spike and have a distinctive, lip-like petal that aids in pollination.

Orchis obtusa is noted for its ornamental value and is often found in botanical gardens and natural habitats.
