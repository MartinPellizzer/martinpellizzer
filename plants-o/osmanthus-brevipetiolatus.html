Osmanthus brevipetiolatus

Osmanthus brevipetiolatus, commonly known as the short-petioled osmanthus, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the autumn season. The leaves are simple, opposite, and have a smooth texture with a slightly serrated margin. This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated for its aromatic foliage and flowers.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for garden landscapes.
