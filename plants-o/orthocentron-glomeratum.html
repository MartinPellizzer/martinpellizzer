Orthocentron glomeratum

Orthocentron glomeratum, commonly known as the clustered orthocentron, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its small, clustered inflorescences consist of both ray and disk flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
