Octomeria paucifolia

Octomeria paucifolia, commonly known as the sparse-leaved octomeria, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and small, fleshy leaves that are adapted to conserve water. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to thrive in poor, sandy soils.

Octomeria paucifolia is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
