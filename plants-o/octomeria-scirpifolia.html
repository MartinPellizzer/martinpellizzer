Octomeria scirpifolia

Octomeria scirpifolia, commonly known as the swamp reed or swamp grass, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to wetland habitats in South America, particularly in regions with high humidity and saturated soils. The plant features tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, often growing in dense stands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in terminal spikes.

Octomeria scirpifolia plays an important role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
