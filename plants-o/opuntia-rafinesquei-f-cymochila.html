Opuntia rafinesquei f. cymochila

Opuntia rafinesquei f. cymochila, commonly known as the Cymochila prickly pear, is a unique variant of the Opuntia genus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, clustered growth pattern and flattened, fleshy stems called cladodes. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in the spring, followed by edible, red-fleshed fruits that are often consumed by wildlife and humans. Its adaptations to arid environments include reduced leaf surfaces and water-storing stems.

This form is notable for its distinct morphology and ecological role in desert ecosystems.
