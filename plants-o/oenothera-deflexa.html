Oenothera deflexa

Oenothera deflexa, commonly known as the nodding evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its drooping, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as wet meadows, swamps, and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Oenothera deflexa is a perennial herb that produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.
