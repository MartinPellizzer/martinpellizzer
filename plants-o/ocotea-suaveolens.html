Ocotea suaveolens

Ocotea suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed enclosed in a fleshy pulp.

Ocotea suaveolens is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
