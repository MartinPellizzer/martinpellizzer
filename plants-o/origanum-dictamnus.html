Origanum dictamnus

Origanum dictamnus, commonly known as sweet cicely, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, aromatic stems and compound leaves with a delicate, parsley-like scent. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its roots and leaves are used in herbal medicine for their digestive and antispasmodic properties.

Origanum dictamnus thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
