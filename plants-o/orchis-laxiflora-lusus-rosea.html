Orchis laxiflora lusus rosea

Orchis laxiflora lusus rosea, commonly known as the pink lady's tresses, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to specific regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and its distinctive, pinkish-white flower spike that resembles a tresses of hair. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered in many areas.
