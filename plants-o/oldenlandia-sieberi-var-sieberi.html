Oldenlandia sieberi var. sieberi

Oldenlandia sieberi var. sieberi, commonly known as the velvetleaf or silverleaf, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a popular subject in horticulture and ecological studies.
