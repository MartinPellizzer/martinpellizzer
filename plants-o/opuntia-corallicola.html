Opuntia corallicola

Opuntia corallicola, commonly known as the coral cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems, which are covered in small, spiny areoles. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tunas, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

Opuntia corallicola plays an important ecological role by providing food and shelter for various desert wildlife.
