Ophrys × ioanninensis

Ophrys × ioanninensis, commonly known as the Ioanninensis Orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Greece.

It belongs to the Ophrys genus, which is known for its mimicry of female insects to attract male pollinators. The plant typically produces showy, bee-like flowers with intricate patterns that aid in pollination. It thrives in rocky, calcareous soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and unique floral structure, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical studies.
