Oenothera fissifolia

Oenothera fissifolia, commonly known as the fissured evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting pollinators such as moths. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive, shallowly lobed base, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. Oenothera fissifolia thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is typically found in open habitats like prairies and disturbed areas.

It is a member of the Onagraceae family, which includes other well-known species like the evening primrose.
