Olearia persoonioides var. lanceolata

Olearia persoonioides var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceolate olearia, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or subalpine environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
