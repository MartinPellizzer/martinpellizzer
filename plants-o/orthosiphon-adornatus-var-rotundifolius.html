Orthosiphon adornatus var. rotundifolius

Orthosiphon adornatus var. rotundifolius, commonly known as the round-leaf sage or Malabar skullcap, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It features opposite, ovate to round leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct aromatic scent. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple, arranged in spikes. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species in traditional medicine and horticulture.
