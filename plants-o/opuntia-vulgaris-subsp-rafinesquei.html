Opuntia vulgaris subsp. rafinesquei

Opuntia vulgaris subsp.

rafinesquei, commonly known as the Rafinesque's prickly pear, is a cactus species native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in spines and glochids, which serve as a defense mechanism.

The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in summer, followed by edible, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are modified into photosynthetic stems, a feature typical of cacti in arid environments.

The subspecies is adapted to thrive in sandy soils and full sun, making it a resilient plant in coastal and semi-arid regions.
