Orthosiphon hispidus

Orthosiphon hispidus, commonly known as Java tea or kaphi, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves, which are used in traditional medicine and as a tea.

Orthosiphon hispidus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
