Oreinotinus laurifolius

Oreinotinus laurifolius, commonly known as the laurel-leaved orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often arranged in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
