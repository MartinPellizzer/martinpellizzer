Ocotea insularis

Ocotea insularis, commonly known as the island ocotea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible for some bird species.

Ocotea insularis is often found in moist, shaded habitats within tropical forests.
