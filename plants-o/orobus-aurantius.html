Orobus aurantius

Orobus aurantius, commonly known as the golden globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, globe-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and it thrives in well-drained soils. Orobus aurantius is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats, and it is adapted to dry, sunny conditions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
