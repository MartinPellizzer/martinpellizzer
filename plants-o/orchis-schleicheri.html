Orchis schleicheri

Orchis schleicheri, commonly known as the Schleicher's orchid, is a rare and endangered species native to specific regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing intricate, spurred orchid flowers that are adapted for pollination by specific insect species. Its habitat is often limited to calcareous grasslands and open woodlands, where it thrives in well-drained, nutrient-rich soils.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
