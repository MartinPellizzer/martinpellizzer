Obione cachiyuyu

Obione cachiyuyu, commonly known as the Andean moonflower, is a striking plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It is a climbing vine that produces large, showy white flowers that open at night, emitting a strong fragrance. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and hairy stems, which help it retain moisture in its arid environment. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by nocturnal moths, making it an important part of the local ecosystem.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance among indigenous communities.
