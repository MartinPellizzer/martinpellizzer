Oxalis machupicchuensis

Oxalis machupicchuensis, commonly known as the Machu Picchu wood sorrel, is a perennial herb native to the Andean highlands of Peru.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at elevations between 3,000 and 4,000 meters. It produces small, edible berries that are sometimes used in local traditional medicine.

Its ornamental value and ecological significance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
