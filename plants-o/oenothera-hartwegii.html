Oenothera hartwegii

Oenothera hartwegii, commonly known as Hartweg's evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is well adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.
