Orchis diantha

Orchis diantha, commonly known as the early purple orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It typically grows in woodland and grassland habitats, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces tall, unbranched stems with clustered purple or violet flowers. Its flowers have three sepals and three petals, with the lip being distinctive in shape and color.

Orchis diantha is known for its early blooming period, often appearing in spring.
