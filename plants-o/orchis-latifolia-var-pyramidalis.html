Orchis latifolia var. pyramidalis

Orchis latifolia var. pyramidalis, commonly known as the broad-leaved orchid variant with a pyramidal shape, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy surface.

It is notable for its distinctive floral structure and is of interest to botanists and conservationists due to its ecological and floral significance.
