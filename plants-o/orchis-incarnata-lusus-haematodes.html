Orchis incarnata lusus haematodes

Orchis incarnata lusus haematodes, commonly known as the red-root orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows. The plant features a slender, reddish rootstock and produces showy, violet to purple flowers in a spike-like inflorescence. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The species is noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for various pollinators.
