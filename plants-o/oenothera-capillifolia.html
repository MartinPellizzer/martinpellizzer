Oenothera capillifolia

Oenothera capillifolia, commonly known as the slender state wildflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

Oenothera capillifolia is also known for its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
