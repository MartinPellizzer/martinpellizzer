Opuntia Missouriensis F. Albispina

White-spined prickly pear, scientifically known as Opuntia missouriensis f. albispina, is a striking variation of the prickly pear cactus native to the southeastern United States. This unique cactus is distinguished by its white spines, which contrast sharply with its green, fleshy stems. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in open woodlands and rocky soils. The plant plays an important ecological role by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species. Its distinctive appearance and hardy nature make it a notable subject of both botanical study and desert landscaping.
