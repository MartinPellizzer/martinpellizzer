Ocotea caryophyllacea

Ocotea caryophyllacea, commonly known as the white sand ocotea, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces dark, glossy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife.

Ocotea caryophyllacea is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical rainforests.
