Ochna shirensis

Ochna shirensis, commonly known as the Shirensis Ochna, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellowish-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in shrubland and forested areas, preferring well-drained soils. Ochna shirensis is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and blooms.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
