Oryzopsis miliacea var. longiaristata

Oryzopsis miliacea var. longiaristata, commonly known as the long-bracted milletgrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and produces inflorescences that are typically spike-like with elongated bracts. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is adapted to thrive in low-nutrient, drought-prone soils. Its flowering period usually occurs in late summer to early autumn, and it is wind-pollinated.

This variety is of interest in ecological studies due to its resilience and potential for use in restoration projects.
