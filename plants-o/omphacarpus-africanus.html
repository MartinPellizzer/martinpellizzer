Omphacarpus africanus

Omphacarpus africanus, commonly known as the African burr, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers followed by distinctive, spiny seed pods that help in seed dispersal. Its leaves are alternate and simple, with a rough texture.

Omphacarpus africanus is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought conditions.
