Oxalis psoraleoides subsp. insipida

Oxalis psoraleoides subsp.

insipida, commonly known as the pale oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South America. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are usually heart-shaped and have a smooth texture.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade. Oxalis psoraleoides subsp.

insipida is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its delicate foliage and subtle floral display.
