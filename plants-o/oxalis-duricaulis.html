Oxalis duricaulis

Oxalis duricaulis, commonly known as the hard-stemmed wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with reddish veins and produces small, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow or orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is characterized by its succulent stems and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Oxalis duricaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
