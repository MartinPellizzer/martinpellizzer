Oberonia cirrhifera

Oberonia cirrhifera, commonly known as the hairy orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, wiry roots and its distinctive, hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-yellow with a reddish or violet tinge. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in montane forests.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for both botanists and orchid enthusiasts.
