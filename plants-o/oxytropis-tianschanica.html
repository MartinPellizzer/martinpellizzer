Oxytropis tianschanica

Oxytropis tianschanica, commonly known as the Tian Shan locoweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Oxytropis tianschanica is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in nutrient-poor habitats.
