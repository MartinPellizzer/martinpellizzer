Orchis laxiflora var. tabernaemontani

Orchis laxiflora var. tabernaemontani, commonly known as the Tabernaemontan orchid, is a rare and beautiful orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing delicate, spurred flowers that are adapted for pollination by specific insect species. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, which helps in reducing water loss in its alpine habitat.

This orchid is listed as endangered due to habitat loss and limited distribution.
