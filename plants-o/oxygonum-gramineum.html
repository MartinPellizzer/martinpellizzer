Oxygonum gramineum

Oxygonum gramineum, commonly known as the slender watergrass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikelets. The plant thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and the edges of ponds, where it can access ample water. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense mats that can stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic organisms.

Oxygonum gramineum plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to water filtration and supporting biodiversity.
