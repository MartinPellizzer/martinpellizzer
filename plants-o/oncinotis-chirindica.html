Oncinotis chirindica

Oncinotis chirindica, commonly known as the Chirindiga grass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and woodland habitats.

Oncinotis chirindica is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
