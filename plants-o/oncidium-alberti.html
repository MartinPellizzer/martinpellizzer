Oncidium alberti

Oncidium alberti, commonly known as the Albert's orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange with red or brown markings. The plant has long, strap-like leaves and produces inflorescences with multiple blooms. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining potting media.

Oncidium alberti is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting flowers.
