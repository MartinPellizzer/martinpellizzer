Oryza denudata

Oryza denudata, commonly known as the naked rice plant, is a wild rice species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance and produces small, pale brown grains. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and floodplains, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its reproductive structures are simple, with spikelets bearing three to five florets.

Oryza denudata is of interest to botanists and agricultural researchers due to its potential genetic contributions to rice breeding programs.
