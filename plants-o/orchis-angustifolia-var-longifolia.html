Orchis angustifolia var. longifolia

Orchis angustifolia var. longifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and meadows, thriving in well-drained, loamy soils. The plant produces slender, upright stems that bear a single, showy purple or violet flower with a distinctive lip. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, arranged in a basal rosette.

The species is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in association with other orchid species in its habitat.
