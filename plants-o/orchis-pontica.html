Orchis pontica

Orchis pontica, commonly known as the Pontic orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region and parts of southern Russia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodlands and meadows. The plant features a tall, unbranched stem that can reach up to 1 meter in height. Its flowers are usually violet or purple, arranged in a dense spike, and have a distinctive three-lobed lip.

Orchis pontica is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
