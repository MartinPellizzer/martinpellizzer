Opuntia × carstenii

Opuntia × carstenii, commonly known as the Mexican prickly pear, is a hybrid cactus species native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems called cladodes. These stems are covered in small, sharp spines and are adapted for storing water in arid environments. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits known as tunas.

Opuntia × carstenii is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and has traditional uses in food and medicine.
