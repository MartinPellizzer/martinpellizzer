Ochna pretoriensis

Ochna pretoriensis, commonly known as the Pretoria ochna, is a shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by red, fleshy berries that contain seeds. Ochna pretoriensis is often found in rocky outcrops and along riverbanks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for cultivation in gardens and public spaces.
