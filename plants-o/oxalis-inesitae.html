Oxalis inesitae

Oxalis inesitae, commonly known as the mountain oxalis, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils, preferring cool and moist environments. Its foliage and blooms are often adorned with intricate patterns, making it a visually striking species.

Oxalis inesitae is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
