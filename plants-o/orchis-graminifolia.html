Orchis graminifolia

Orchis graminifolia, commonly known as the meadow orchid, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of pale green to yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, giving it its common name.

Orchis graminifolia is often found in nutrient-rich soils and is an important component of diverse floral communities.
