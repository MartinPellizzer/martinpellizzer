Ottoschulzia domingensis

Ottoschulzia domingensis, commonly known as the Dominican orchid, is a rare epiphytic orchid native to the Dominican Republic.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or branches in tropical forests. Its unique botanical features include a compact growth habit and specialized root structures that aid in nutrient absorption.

Due to habitat loss, Ottoschulzia domingensis is considered endangered, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
