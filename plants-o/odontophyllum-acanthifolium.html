Odontophyllum acanthifolium

Odontophyllum acanthifolium, commonly known as the spiny-leafed orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, attaching itself to trees or rocks in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to pink.

Odontophyllum acanthifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or botanical gardens.
