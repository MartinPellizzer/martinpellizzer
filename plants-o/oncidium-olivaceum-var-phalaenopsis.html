Oncidium olivaceum var. phalaenopsis

Oncidium olivaceum var. phalaenopsis, commonly known as the Phalaenopsis orchid, is a hybrid orchid species that combines traits from Oncidium olivaceum and Phalaenopsis.

It features large, showy flowers with a velvety texture and a wide range of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its roots are epiphytic, allowing it to grow on trees or other surfaces in its natural habitat.

This variety is popular in horticulture for its long-lasting blooms and adaptability to indoor growing conditions.
