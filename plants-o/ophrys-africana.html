Ophrys africana

Ophrys africana, commonly known as the African bee orchid, is a species of orchid native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which closely resembles a bee, a mimicry that aids in pollination by male bees. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brown, with intricate patterns that enhance its visual resemblance to an insect.

Ophrys africana is a perennial herb that produces a single spike of flowers, often blooming in late summer.
