Oenothera kernensis subsp. gilmanii

Oenothera kernensis subsp.

gilmanii, commonly known as Gilman's evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is found in regions such as California and Nevada.

The plant produces showy yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and the stems are often reddish in color.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
