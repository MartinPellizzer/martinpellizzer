Osmanthus aquifolium var. atropurpureus

Osmanthus aquifolium var. atropurpureus, commonly known as the purple osmanthus, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dark purple, oval-shaped leaves that turn reddish in the fall. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers in the spring, which are often used in traditional Chinese medicine and culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its unique foliage and aromatic blooms make it a valuable addition to both horticultural and ecological landscapes.
