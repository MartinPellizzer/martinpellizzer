Oxytenanthera monadelpha

Oxytenanthera monadelpha, commonly known as the red sand mat, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color. The plant has adapted to harsh, dry environments with its succulent leaves and deep root system. It plays an important role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.

Oxytenanthera monadelpha is also valued for its ornamental appeal in xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
