Ophiopogon trigonantherus

Ophiopogon trigonantherus, commonly known as the Japanese forest grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features dense, grass-like foliage with narrow, dark green leaves that form a low, mat-like growth habit. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in late summer, arranged in clustered inflorescences. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth and adaptability to moist, well-drained soils.
