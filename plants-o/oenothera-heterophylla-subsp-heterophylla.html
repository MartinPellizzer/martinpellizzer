Oenothera heterophylla subsp. heterophylla

Oenothera heterophylla subsp.

heterophylla, commonly known as the variable evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its showy yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually alternate and vary in shape, which contributes to its common name.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in harsh conditions, making it a resilient and adaptive plant.
