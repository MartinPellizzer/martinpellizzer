Oncidium alboviolaceum

Oncidium alboviolaceum, commonly known as the Violet Lady's Slipper, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It features intricate, showy flowers with a distinctive violet and white coloration, often with a lip that resembles a slipper. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its inflorescence consists of multiple flowers arranged in a spike, and it blooms during the warmer months.

Oncidium alboviolaceum requires bright, indirect light and well-draining potting media to thrive.
