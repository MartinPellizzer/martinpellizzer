Ocimum prostratum

Ocimum prostratum, commonly known as prostrate basil, is a low-growing herb native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves that emit a strong, spicy scent when crushed. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy surface, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Ocimum prostratum is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb in various cuisines.
