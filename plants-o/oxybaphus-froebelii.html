Oxybaphus froebelii

Oxybaphus froebelii, commonly known as the Froebel's oxeye, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Oxybaphus froebelii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
