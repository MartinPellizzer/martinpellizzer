Oxytropis knjazevii

Oxytropis knjazevii, commonly known as the Knjazev's milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Central Asia and is typically found in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant has a bushy growth habit with compound leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in pods, a characteristic feature of the Fabaceae family.

Oxytropis knjazevii is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
