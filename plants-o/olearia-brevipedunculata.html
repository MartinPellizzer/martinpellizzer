Olearia brevipedunculata

Olearia brevipedunculata, commonly known as the slender leaved daisy, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, hairy leaves and grows in a compact, shrub-like form. It is often found in coastal and forest margins, thriving in well-drained soils.

Olearia brevipedunculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
