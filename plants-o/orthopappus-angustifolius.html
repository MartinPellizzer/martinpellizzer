Orthopappus angustifolius

Orthopappus angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features basal leaves that are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, while its stem is erect and sparsely hairy. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a clustered inflorescence, which are followed by distinctive pappus-bearing bracts. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

The species is characterized by its spiny leaves and showy, composite flower heads.
