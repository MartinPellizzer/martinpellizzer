Ophryosporus apricus

Ophryosporus apricus, commonly known as the apricot tree, is a deciduous tree belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey-barked trunk and alternate, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers in early spring before the leaves emerge. Its fruits are fleshy drupes, typically yellow or orange in color, containing a single hard pit.

Ophryosporus apricus is valued for its ornamental appeal and as a source of edible fruit.
