Orobanche multicaulis var. palmeri

Orobanche multicaulis var. palmeri, commonly known as the Palmer's common broomrape, is a parasitic plant that lacks chlorophyll and cannot perform photosynthesis.

It relies entirely on its host plant for nutrients, typically attaching to the roots of various grasses and shrubs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are often overlooked due to its lack of leaves. It is native to the southwestern United States and parts of Mexico, thriving in arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can significantly impact the growth and survival of its host plants.
