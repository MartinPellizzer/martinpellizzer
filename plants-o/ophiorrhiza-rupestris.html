Ophiorrhiza rupestris

Ophiorrhiza rupestris, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small, herbaceous shrub that typically grows in rocky or coastal areas. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, delicate flowers that are often pink or white in color. Its leaves are opposite and elliptical, with a smooth surface and a slightly waxy texture.

Ophiorrhiza rupestris is adapted to saline and arid environments, making it a resilient species in coastal ecosystems.
