Oryzopsis ruprechtiana

Oryzopsis ruprechtiana, commonly known as the Ruprecht's grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, demonstrating a high degree of drought tolerance. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration that helps reduce water loss.

Oryzopsis ruprechtiana plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptation to harsh climates.
