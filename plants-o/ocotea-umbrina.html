Ocotea umbrina

Ocotea umbrina, commonly known as the dark ocotea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in countries like Mexico, Guatemala, and Brazil. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage and aromatic leaves, which emit a distinctive scent when crushed. Its small, white flowers are followed by elliptical, dark blue berries that are an important food source for various wildlife.

Ocotea umbrina is often found in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests.
