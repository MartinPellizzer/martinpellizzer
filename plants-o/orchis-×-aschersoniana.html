Orchis × aschersoniana

Orchis × aschersoniana, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a fascinating plant resulting from the cross between Orchis morio and Orchis purpurea.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, woodland habitats across Europe. The plant features striking violet or purple flowers with a distinctive hooded structure, which is characteristic of the Orchidaceae family. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil, while its leaves are broad and lance-shaped.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
