Oenothera deltoides var. decumbens

Oenothera deltoides var. decumbens, commonly known as prairie smoke, is a perennial flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its silvery, feathery bracts that surround the flower cluster, giving it a smoke-like appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Oenothera deltoides var. decumbens is often found in prairies, open woodlands, and along roadsides.
