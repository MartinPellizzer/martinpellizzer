Oplismenus lanceolatus

Oplismenus lanceolatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features upright stems that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters at the top of the stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and they are arranged alternately along the stem.

Oplismenus lanceolatus is typically found in moist habitats such as wet meadows and riverbanks.
