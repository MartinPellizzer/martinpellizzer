Oxalis fibrosa

Oxalis fibrosa, commonly known as the fibrous wood sorrel, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features delicate, trifoliate leaves with a distinctive appearance, often resembling the leaves of true sorrel species. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. Oxalis fibrosa thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
