Ophrys × turris-veneris

Ophrys × turris-veneris, commonly known as the Venus's Flytrap hybrid, is a fascinating plant with unique botanical features.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera, both of which are known for their orchid-like appearance. The plant exhibits a complex morphology, including specialized flowers that mimic the appearance of insects to attract pollinators. Its leaves are typically green and lance-shaped, while its flowers are often yellow or brown with intricate patterns.

This hybrid showcases the adaptive strategies of orchids in their ecological niches.
