Oxydectes mucronifolia

Oxydectes mucronifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf oxydectes, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that have a pointed tip, giving it its scientific name. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often clustered in loose inflorescences.

Oxydectes mucronifolia is characterized by its delicate appearance and preference for well-drained, acidic soils.
