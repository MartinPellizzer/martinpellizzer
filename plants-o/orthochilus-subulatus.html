Orthochilus subulatus

Orthochilus subulatus, commonly known as the slender rock cress, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its cross-shaped flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, where it thrives in cool, arid conditions. Its leaves are linear and pointed, while its flowers are small and white or pale yellow.

Orthochilus subulatus is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
