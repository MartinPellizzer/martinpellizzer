Oligochaetochilus aciculiformis

Oligochaetochilus aciculiformis, commonly known as the needleleaf moss, is a species of liverwort belonging to the family Pottiaceae.

It is characterized by its closely appressed, acicular (needle-like) leaves that form dense, cushion-like growths. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found on rock surfaces or in forest understories. Its small, spore-producing structures are typically located on the leaf margins.

This moss is well adapted to arid conditions, showcasing a remarkable ability to survive periods of desiccation.
