Orthrosanthus multiflorus

Orthrosanthus multiflorus, commonly known as the many-flowered orthrosanthus, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble single blooms. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
