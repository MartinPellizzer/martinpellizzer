Oenothera greggii var. pringlei

Oenothera greggii var. pringlei, commonly known as the Pringle's evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has slender, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, typically found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinator populations.
