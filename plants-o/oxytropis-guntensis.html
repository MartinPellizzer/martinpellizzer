Oxytropis guntensis

Oxytropis guntensis, commonly known as Gunt's locoweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in early summer. Oxytropis guntensis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and alpine meadows.

It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to nutrient cycling in its ecosystem.
