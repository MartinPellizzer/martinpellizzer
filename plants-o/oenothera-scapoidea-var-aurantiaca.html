Oenothera scapoidea var. aurantiaca

Oenothera scapoidea var. aurantiaca, commonly known as the showy evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its striking orange to yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

The flowers are typically tubular with reflexed petals, and the plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to reseed itself.
