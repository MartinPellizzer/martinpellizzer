Oeonia elliotii

Oeonia elliotii, commonly known as Elliot's rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard plants. This perennial herb typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, often in arid environments. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in early spring.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, elongated pods that contain seeds.
