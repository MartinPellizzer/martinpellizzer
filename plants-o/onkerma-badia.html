Onkerma badia

Onkerma badia, commonly known as the red sandalwood tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and dark, reddish-brown bark. The leaves are compound, with long, narrow leaflets that give it a feathery appearance. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink.

The tree is valued for its durable, aromatic wood, which has been used historically in furniture and musical instruments.
