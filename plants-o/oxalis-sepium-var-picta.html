Oxalis sepium var. picta

Oxalis sepium var. picta, commonly known as the painted wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with distinctive red, orange, or yellow markings, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, often with five petals. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Oxalis sepium var. picta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
