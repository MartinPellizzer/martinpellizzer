Orchis romana lusus flavescens

Orchis romana lusus flavescens, commonly known as the yellow marsh orchid, is a rare and delicate species belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and showy yellow flowers with a distinctive orange lip. This plant typically grows in wet meadows and marshy areas, preferring moist, calcareous soils. It blooms in late spring to early summer, and its flowers are pollinated by various insects, including bees and flies.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, it is considered a vulnerable species in many regions.
