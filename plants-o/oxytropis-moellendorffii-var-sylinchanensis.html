Oxytropis moellendorffii var. sylinchanensis

Oxytropis moellendorffii var. sylinchanensis, commonly known as the Mongolian locoweed, is a perennial legume native to central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in steppes and rocky soils.

This species is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
