Ophrys × sicana

Ophrys × sicana, commonly known as the bee orchid hybrid, is a fascinating plant belonging to the Ophrys genus in the orchid family.

It is a natural hybrid between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera, exhibiting a unique combination of traits from both parent species. The plant is characterized by its striking, bee-like flowers that mimic the appearance and scent of female bees, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. Its roots are typically mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to enhance nutrient uptake.

Ophrys × sicana is often found in open, sunny habitats across southern Europe, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
