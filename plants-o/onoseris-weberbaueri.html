Onoseris weberbaueri

Onoseris weberbaueri, commonly known as the Weberbauer's goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and has a compact, herbaceous growth habit. Its yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
