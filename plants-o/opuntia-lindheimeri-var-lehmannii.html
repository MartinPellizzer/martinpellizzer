Opuntia lindheimeri var. lehmannii

Opuntia lindheimeri var. lehmannii, commonly known as the Lehmann's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in spines and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems. Its fruit, known as a tuna, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

The plant plays an important role in local ecosystems and is also valued for its ornamental and medicinal uses.
