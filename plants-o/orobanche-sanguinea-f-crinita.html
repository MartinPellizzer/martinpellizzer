Orobanche sanguinea f. crinita

Orobanche sanguinea f. crinita, commonly known as the hairy common broomrape, is a root-parasitic plant belonging to the Orobanchaceae family.

It lacks chlorophyll and is entirely dependent on host plants for nutrients, typically parasitizing species within the Fabaceae family. The plant is characterized by its pale yellow to reddish stems and small, scale-like leaves. Its flowers are usually pink to reddish, arranged in clusters, and bloom during the summer months.

This hemiparasitic species is found in various regions of Europe and is often associated with disturbed or nutrient-rich soils.
