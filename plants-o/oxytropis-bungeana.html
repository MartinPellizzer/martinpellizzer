Oxytropis bungeana

Oxytropis bungeana, commonly known as the Bunge's milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through its deep root system and waxy leaf coatings. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Oxytropis bungeana is also valued for its ornamental appeal in dry garden settings.
