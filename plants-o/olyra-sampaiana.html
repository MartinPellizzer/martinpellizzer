Olyra sampaiana

Olyra sampaiana, commonly known as the Sampaiana tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often in racemes, and can vary in color from white to yellow.

The tree is valued for its hard wood and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
