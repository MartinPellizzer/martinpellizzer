Orchis incarnata var. lanceata

Orchis incarnata var. lanceata, commonly known as the lanceleaf pink, is a species of orchid native to North America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and striking pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and wet meadows. It has a slender, upright stem that bears several flowers along its length.

The orchid is a perennial herb that plays a role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecosystem biodiversity.
