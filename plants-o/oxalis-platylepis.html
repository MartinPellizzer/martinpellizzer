Oxalis platylepis

Oxalis platylepis, commonly known as the broad-leaf wood sorrel, is a species of flowering plant in the family Oxalidaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, and it produces distinctive seed pods that split open when mature.

Oxalis platylepis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
