Orchis Coriophora Subsp. Fragrans

Sweet orchis, scientifically known as Orchis coriophora subsp. fragrans, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to parts of Europe. It is distinguished by its fragrant, violet-blue blossoms that bloom in early summer. This orchid thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its unique scent attracts specific pollinators, making it an important species in ecological studies. Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
