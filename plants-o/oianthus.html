Oianthus

Oianthus, commonly known as the onion flower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves and clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers. The plant is native to southern Africa and thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight. Oianthus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to bloom in late summer.

Its bulbous roots are similar to those of the onion, hence its common name.
