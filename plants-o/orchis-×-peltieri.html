Orchis × peltieri

Orchis × peltieri, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a naturally occurring cross between Orchis morio and Orchis peltata.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant produces showy, violet or purple flowers that are arranged in a loose spike, characteristic of many orchid species. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption in its native habitat.

Orchis × peltieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
