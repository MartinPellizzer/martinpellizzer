Oleandra Welwitschii

Oleandra welwitschii is a rare and striking plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa. Known for its long, drooping clusters of bright yellow flowers, it captivates botanists and gardeners alike. This species is closely related to the well-known oleander but exhibits unique adaptations to its harsh environment. Its striking appearance and resilience make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies. Despite its beauty, Oleandra welwitschii remains elusive and is often found in remote, hard-to-reach areas.
