Orthosiphon welwitschii

Orthosiphon welwitschii, commonly known as the African germander, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant features square stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its small, tubular flowers are typically white or pale blue, arranged in whorls along the stem.

It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
