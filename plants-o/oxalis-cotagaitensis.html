Oxalis cotagaitensis

Oxalis cotagaitensis, commonly known as the Andean wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous areas. Its leaves contain oxalic acid, which can be toxic if ingested in large quantities.

Oxalis cotagaitensis is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
