Oxalis texana

Oxalis texana, commonly known as Texas wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom in spring, and the plant is often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Oxalis texana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
