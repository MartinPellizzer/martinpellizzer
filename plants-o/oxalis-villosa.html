Oxalis villosa

Oxalis villosa, commonly known as the hairy wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a fuzzy, velvety texture, and its flowers are typically yellow with five petals. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that contain several seeds. Oxalis villosa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
