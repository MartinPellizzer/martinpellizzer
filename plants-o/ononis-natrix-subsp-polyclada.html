Ononis natrix subsp. polyclada

Ononis natrix subsp.

polyclada, commonly known as the common whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish-white flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands, roadsides, and wastelands. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
