Oldenlandia capitellata var. mollissima

Oldenlandia capitellata var. mollissima, commonly known as the silky bluemink, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and produces small, blue or violet flowers clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. The leaves are typically elliptical or ovate, with a soft, velvety texture that gives the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in containers.
