Olearia burgessii

Olearia burgessii, commonly known as the Stewart Island dandelion, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy, serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal and lowland areas, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. It is a perennial shrub that can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

Olearia burgessii plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small invertebrates.
