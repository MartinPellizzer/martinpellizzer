Oxalis hassleri

Oxalis hassleri, commonly known as the purple shamrock, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with a distinctive purple hue. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Oxalis hassleri is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
