Orchis gracilis var. chinensis

Orchis gracilis var. chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese slender orchid, is a flowering plant native to parts of East Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage and small, pale purple or white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or rocky crevices. Its roots are often used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in its native habitats.
