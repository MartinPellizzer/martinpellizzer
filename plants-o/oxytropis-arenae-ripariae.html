Oxytropis arenae-ripariae

Oxytropis arenae-ripariae, commonly known as sand or stream oxytropis, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its low, sprawling growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale purple or white, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

Oxytropis arenae-ripariae plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various desert organisms.
