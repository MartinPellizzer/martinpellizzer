Ototropis calliantha

Ototropis calliantha, commonly known as the narrowleaf tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Ototropis calliantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
