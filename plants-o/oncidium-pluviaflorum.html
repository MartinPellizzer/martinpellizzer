Oncidium pluviaflorum

Oncidium pluviaflorum, commonly known as the rainforest orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, long-lasting inflorescences that display a mix of vibrant colors, often including shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are highly fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Oncidium pluviaflorum requires high humidity and indirect light to thrive, making it a popular choice for greenhouse cultivation.
