Ocimum buchananii

Ocimum buchananii, commonly known as African basil, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs such as mint and oregano. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, narrow leaves and small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown for its culinary and medicinal properties.

Ocimum buchananii is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects to gardens.
