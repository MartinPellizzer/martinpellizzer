Ornithogalum umbellatum f. minor

Ornithogalum umbellatum f. minor, commonly known as the dwarf star-of-Bethlehem, is a compact, perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a short, rhizomatous stem and produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers during late spring to early summer. The leaves are narrow, grass-like, and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

This cultivar is valued for its delicate appearance and is often used in garden design for its subtle, elegant blooms.
