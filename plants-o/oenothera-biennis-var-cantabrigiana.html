Oenothera biennis var. cantabrigiana

Oenothera biennis var. cantabrigiana, commonly known as the Cambridge evening primrose, is a variant of the evening primrose native to North America.

It is a biennial plant characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows and roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

The species is noted for its ecological value, providing nectar for nocturnal pollinators like moths.
