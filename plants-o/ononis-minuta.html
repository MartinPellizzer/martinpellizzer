Ononis minuta

Ononis minuta, commonly known as the dwarf whitlow grass, is a low-growing annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically reaches a height of less than 10 centimeters and features small, pale green leaves arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces tiny, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and habitat restoration.
