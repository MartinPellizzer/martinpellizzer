Ornithogalum persicum var. longiciliatum

Ornithogalum persicum var. longiciliatum, commonly known as the Persian lily or long-bearded lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Persia.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant blooms in early summer, with its inflorescence consisting of several flowers clustered on a scape. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its elegant appearance.
