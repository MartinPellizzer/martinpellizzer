Oncidium tuerckheimii

Oncidium tuerckheimii, commonly known as the Tuerckheim's orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It features striking, long-lasting inflorescences with vibrant yellow and orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a pseudobulb structure, which stores water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in environments with seasonal dry periods. Its roots are adapted for attachment to trees and can absorb moisture from the air.

Oncidium tuerckheimii is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
