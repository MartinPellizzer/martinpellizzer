Opuntia pentlandii var. fuauxiana

Opuntia pentlandii var. fuauxiana, commonly known as the Fueguina prickly pear, is a cacti species native to the southern tip of South America.

It is characterized by its flat, ribbed stems that are covered in spines and glochids, which serve as a defense mechanism. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits known as tunas. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in coastal areas and rocky soils.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance, providing food and shelter for various desert wildlife.
