Ocotea erectifolia

Ocotea erectifolia, commonly known as the mountain pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by oval, woody berries. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils, which have been used in traditional medicine.

Ocotea erectifolia thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.
