Origanum salvifolium

Origanum salvifolium, commonly known as sweet marjoram, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or pink flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees. Its leaves are used in culinary and medicinal applications due to their essential oils rich in compounds like carvacrol and thymol.

Sweet marjoram thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal cultivation.
