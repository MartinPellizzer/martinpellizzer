Onobrychis vanensis

Onobrychis vanensis, commonly known as Van's goat's rue, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and forest edges.

It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility, and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
