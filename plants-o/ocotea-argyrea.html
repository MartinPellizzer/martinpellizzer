Ocotea argyrea

Ocotea argyrea, commonly known as silver ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its smooth, silver-gray bark and lance-shaped leaves with a fragrant aroma. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by dark purple berries.

Ocotea argyrea is often found in humid lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
