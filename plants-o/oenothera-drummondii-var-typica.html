Oenothera drummondii var. typica

Oenothera drummondii var. typica, commonly known as the Drummond's evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening and close by morning. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
