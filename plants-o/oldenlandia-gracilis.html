Oldenlandia gracilis

Oldenlandia gracilis, commonly known as the slender knotweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, ascending stems and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

The plant is noted for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
