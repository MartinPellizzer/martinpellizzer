Ocotea obovata

Ocotea obovata, commonly known as the Brazilian camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are followed by dark blue, fleshy berries. Its wood is highly valued for its aromatic properties and resistance to decay.

Ocotea obovata plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
