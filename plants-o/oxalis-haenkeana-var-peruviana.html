Oxalis haenkeana var. peruviana

Oxalis haenkeana var. peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian wood sorrel, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces edible leaves and flowers, though they contain oxalic acid and should be consumed with caution. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Oxalis haenkeana var. peruviana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
