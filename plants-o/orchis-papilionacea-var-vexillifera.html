Orchis papilionacea var. vexillifera

Orchis papilionacea var. vexillifera, commonly known as the violet orchid, is a species of orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns resembling wings. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers are pollinated by bees and other insects, and it produces small, dark brown seed pods.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
