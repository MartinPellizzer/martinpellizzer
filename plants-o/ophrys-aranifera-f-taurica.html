Ophrys aranifera f. taurica

Ophrys aranifera f. taurica, commonly known as the Tauric bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the Tauric Chersonese region of Crimea.

It is characterized by its unique floral morphology, featuring a complex, bee-mimicking flower that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant produces a single, showy, yellowish-green flower with intricate markings that resemble a bee, attracting male bees for pollination. It thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and meadows.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
