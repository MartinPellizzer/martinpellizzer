Oncocalamus djodu

Oncocalamus djodu, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a tall, slender grass-like plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its long, hollow stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair, aiding in water retention.
