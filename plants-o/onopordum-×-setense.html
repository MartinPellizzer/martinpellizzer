Onopordum × setense

Onopordum × setense, commonly known as the Spanish thistle, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is native to the Iberian Peninsula and is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands. It has been cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking appearance and resilience.

The species is also used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
