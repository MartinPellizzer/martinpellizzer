Oxalis squamosoradicosa

Oxalis squamosoradicosa, commonly known as the scalycrown wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial with distinctive heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant features a unique root system that forms a dense mat, helping it to thrive in rocky or sandy soils. It blooms during the summer months and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
