Onobrychis montana var. transsilvanica

Onobrychis montana var. transsilvanica, commonly known as Transylvanian sainfoin, is a perennial legume native to the Carpathian region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is well adapted to semi-arid climates. It is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop due to its nutritional value.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
