Oxalis fontana var. rufa

Oxalis fontana var. rufa, commonly known as the red-veined wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive red or purple venation pattern, and small, five-petaled flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and has a fibrous root system that helps it anchor in wet soils. It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its ability to form dense mats through rhizome growth.

The foliage and flowers are often used in ornamental gardening for their attractive appearance.
