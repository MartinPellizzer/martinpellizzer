Oenothera undulata

Oenothera undulata, commonly known as the undulating evening primrose, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oily seeds. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Oenothera undulata is often found in dry, open habitats like grasslands and rocky areas.
