Oxytropis campestris var. americana

Oxytropis campestris var. americana, commonly known as American field vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers in late spring. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in poor soils and withstand drought conditions.

The plant is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
