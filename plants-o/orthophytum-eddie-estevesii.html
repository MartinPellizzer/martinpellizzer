Orthophytum eddie-estevesii

Orthophytum eddie-estevesii, commonly known as the Estevess' Orthophytum, is a species of flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescence. The plant produces showy, long-lasting flowers that range in color from red to orange, often attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and insects. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a rosette structure, typical of many bromeliads.
