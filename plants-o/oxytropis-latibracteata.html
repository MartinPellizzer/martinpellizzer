Oxytropis latibracteata

Oxytropis latibracteata, commonly known as broad-bract locoweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe.

Oxytropis latibracteata is known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock.
