Ocimum hians

Ocimum hians, commonly known as the purple basil, is a species of flowering plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves, which are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or violet in color.

Ocimum hians thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
