Ophrys × gallica

Ophrys × gallica, commonly known as the Bee Orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Europe.

It is characterized by its striking flower structure that closely resembles a bee, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and heaths. Its flowers are usually a mix of brown, yellow, and white, with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of a bee's body.

Ophrys × gallica is a perennial herb that produces a single spike of flowers, often blooming in late spring to early summer.
