Oenothera argillicola var. pubescens

Oenothera argillicola var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the central United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its varietal name "pubescens.". The plant produces showy yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It thrives in sandy or clay soils and is often found in open prairies and disturbed habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinator populations.
