Olea enervis

Olea enervis, commonly known as the slender olive, is a species of flowering plant in the Oleaceae family.

It is native to parts of Africa, including South Africa, and is characterized by its slender, often climbing or shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Olea enervis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in challenging climates.
