Oxycoccus quadripetalus f. deltoidaeus

Oxycoccus quadripetalus f. deltoidaeus, commonly known as the deltoid leaf cranberry, is a botanical variant of the cranberry plant.

It is characterized by its distinctive deltoid-shaped leaves, which set it apart from other forms of Oxycoccus. This variety thrives in acidic, wet environments, typically found in bogs and marshes. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and used in various culinary applications.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and ecological niche within temperate regions.
