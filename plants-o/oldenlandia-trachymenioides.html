Oldenlandia trachymenioides

Oldenlandia trachymenioides, commonly known as the silverleaf false mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, velvety leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, often appearing in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability.
