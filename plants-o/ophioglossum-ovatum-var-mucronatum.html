Ophioglossum ovatum var. mucronatum

Ophioglossum ovatum var. mucronatum, commonly known as the spurred adder's mouth, is a rare and ancient fern-like plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lance-shaped leaves with a spore-producing structure called a sporangium at the tip. The plant exhibits a unique reproductive system, with spores released from the sporangium rather than flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in some areas.
