Orchis patens subsp. orientalis

Orchis patens subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as the eastern twayblade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as woodlands and meadows. It has a symbiotic relationship with mycorrhizal fungi, which aid in nutrient uptake.

Its blooming period usually occurs in late spring to early summer.
