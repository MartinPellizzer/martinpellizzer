Ophiuros exaltatus

Ophiuros exaltatus, commonly known as the tall sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Ophiuros exaltatus is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
