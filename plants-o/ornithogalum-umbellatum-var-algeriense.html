Ornithogalum umbellatum var. algeriense

Ornithogalum umbellatum var. algeriense, commonly known as Algerian star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herb native to North Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces umbels of white or pale pink flowers. The plant is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in Mediterranean climates. It has a bulbous root system that stores water and nutrients, aiding in survival during dry periods.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
