Ochrocarpos eugenioides

Ochrocarpos eugenioides, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing in late winter or early spring. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, round fruits that turn red when mature. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its showy flowers and adaptability to various soil types.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
