Oxalis fruticosa subsp. fruticosa

Oxalis fruticosa subsp.

fruticosa, commonly known as the shrub oxalis, is a perennial plant native to South America. It features a compact, bushy growth habit with upright stems and trifoliate leaves that are typically reddish or purple in color.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils. Oxalis fruticosa subsp.

fruticosa is relatively drought-tolerant and prefers full sun to partial shade.
