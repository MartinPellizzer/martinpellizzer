Ochna serrulata

Ochna serrulata, commonly known as the African yellowwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, elliptical leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers followed by orange-red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Ochna serrulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
