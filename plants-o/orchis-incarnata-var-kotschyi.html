Orchis incarnata var. kotschyi

Orchis incarnata var. kotschyi, commonly known as the Kotschyi ladies' tresses, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to specific regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and dense, dark purple to black floral spikes that resemble a tresses of hair. The flowers are small, tubular, and typically arranged in a loose, spiraling pattern along the stem. This variety is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in many areas.
