Ocotea argyrophylla

Ocotea argyrophylla, commonly known as silverleaf ocotea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that have a silvery underside, giving it its common name. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue berries.

Ocotea argyrophylla is often found in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests.
