Ottonia macrophylla

Ottonia macrophylla, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, floating on the water's surface. The plant's leaves are thick and waxy, providing a unique habitat for various aquatic organisms. Its flowers are showy, with bright yellow petals that attract pollinators like beetles.

Ottonia macrophylla plays an important ecological role in maintaining water quality and supporting biodiversity in its native wetlands.
