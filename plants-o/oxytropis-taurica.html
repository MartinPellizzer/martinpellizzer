Oxytropis taurica

Oxytropis taurica, commonly known as Tauric locoweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the steppes of southern Russia and Ukraine.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and erect, sparsely branched stems. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Oxytropis taurica is adapted to dry, calcareous soils and is often found in open grasslands and along riverbanks.

It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
