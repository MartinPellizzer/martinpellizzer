Oberonia segawae

Oberonia segawae, commonly known as the Segawa's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its roots are mycoheterotrophic, meaning they rely on fungal associations for nutrients.

The species is noted for its unique floral structure and is of interest to botanists and orchid enthusiasts.
