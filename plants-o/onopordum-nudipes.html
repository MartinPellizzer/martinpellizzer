Onopordum nudipes

Onopordum nudipes, commonly known as the desert globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, sprawling growth habit and yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has small, ovate leaves with a rough texture and is adapted to arid environments. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Onopordum nudipes is often found in rocky soils and is considered a hardy, drought-tolerant species.
