Oxalis steinbachii

Oxalis steinbachii, commonly known as the Steinbach's wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its foliage is often adorned with intricate patterns resembling veins, and it produces edible tubers that are sometimes used in traditional cuisine.

Oxalis steinbachii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
