Orobus clymenum

Orobus clymenum, commonly known as the blue orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, violet-blue flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rainforests, where it thrives in the understory. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, providing a lush green backdrop to its vibrant blossoms.

Orobus clymenum is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its role in ecological systems supporting various pollinators.
