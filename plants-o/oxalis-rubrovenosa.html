Oxalis rubrovenosa

Oxalis rubrovenosa, commonly known as the red-veined wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features trifoliate leaves with distinctive red veins, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, often with a reddish tinge. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Oxalis rubrovenosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
