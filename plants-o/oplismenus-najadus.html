Oplismenus najadus

Oplismenus najadus, commonly known as water shield, is a semi-aquatic flowering plant native to the wetlands of North America.

It has broad, floating leaves that are typically green with a waxy surface, and its roots are anchored in the mud at the bottom of water bodies. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. Oplismenus najadus is often found in shallow waters, such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams.

Its structure allows it to thrive in aquatic environments while still performing photosynthesis efficiently.
