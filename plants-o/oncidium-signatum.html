Oncidium signatum

Oncidium signatum, commonly known as the showy orchid, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are often fragrant and can last for several weeks, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Oncidium signatum requires bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.
