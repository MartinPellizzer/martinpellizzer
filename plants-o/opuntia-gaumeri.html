Opuntia gaumeri

Opuntia gaumeri, commonly known as the Mexican prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems that are covered in spines and glochids, which are small, barbed bristles. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tunas, is edible and often used in local cuisines.

Opuntia gaumeri is adapted to arid environments and plays an important role in its ecosystem.
