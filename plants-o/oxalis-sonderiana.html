Oxalis sonderiana

Oxalis sonderiana, commonly known as the yellow wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are heart-shaped and often have a reddish or purple tint. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Oxalis sonderiana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and flowers.
