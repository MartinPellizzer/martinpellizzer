Otochilus forrestii

Otochilus forrestii, commonly known as the Forest's orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale yellow. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding its survival in its harsh environment. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine, hairy coating.

Otochilus forrestii is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts due to its rarity and unique floral structure.
