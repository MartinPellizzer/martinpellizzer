Oxalis grandifolia var. minor

Oxalis grandifolia var. minor, commonly known as the small-leaf wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of small, yellow, five-petaled flowers. The plant prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a short, upright stem.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
