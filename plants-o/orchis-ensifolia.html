Orchis ensifolia

Orchis ensifolia, commonly known as the sword-leaved orchid, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are elongated and sword-shaped, giving it its common name.

Orchis ensifolia is known for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
