Oxalis phaeotricha subsp. sonsonensis

Oxalis phaeotricha subsp.

sonsonensis, commonly known as the Sonsonense wood sorrel, is a perennial herb native to Central America. It features trifoliate leaves with reddish veins and small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense colonies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and flowers.
