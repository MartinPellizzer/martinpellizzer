Oenothera fruticosa var. microcarpa

Oenothera fruticosa var. microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf evening primrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing, shrub-like form with soft, hairy stems and small, showy yellow flowers that open in the evening. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential medicinal applications.
