Oxypetalum paraguayense

Oxypetalum paraguayense, commonly known as the Paraguay rose or Paraguay apple, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with five petals. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and simple, alternate leaves. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed.

Oxypetalum paraguayense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
