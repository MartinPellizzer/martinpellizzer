Obione ehrenbergii

Obione ehrenbergii, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb with a fibrous root system adapted to store water. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in spring. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, reducing water loss through transpiration.

The species is well-suited to harsh environments and plays a role in stabilizing desert soils.
