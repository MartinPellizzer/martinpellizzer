Oxyrhachis gracillima subsp. occidentalis

Oxyrhachis gracillima subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the western slender clover, is a perennial herb native to western North America. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

The plant has slender, branching stems and small, trifoliate leaves with a distinctive pale green color. Its flowers are pink to reddish-purple, arranged in small clusters, and bloom in early spring.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
