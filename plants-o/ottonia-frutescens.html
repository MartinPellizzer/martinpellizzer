Ottonia frutescens

Ottonia frutescens, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern and central United States.

It features showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and a robust root system that helps it thrive in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Ottonia frutescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
