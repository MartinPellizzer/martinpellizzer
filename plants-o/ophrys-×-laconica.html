Ophrys × laconica

Ophrys × laconica, commonly known as the Laconian bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its intricate, bee-like floral structure, which features a prominent lip that mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brownish in color, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Ophrys × laconica is a perennial herb that produces a single spike of flowers, making it a visually striking addition to botanical gardens.
