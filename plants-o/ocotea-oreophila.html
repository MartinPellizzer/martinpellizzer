Ocotea oreophila

Ocotea oreophila, commonly known as the cloud forest laurel, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the tropical Andes of South America, particularly in regions of Colombia, Ecuador, and Peru. This plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage and aromatic leaves, which emit a subtle, spicy scent. Ocotea oreophila typically grows in humid, shaded environments at high elevations, often reaching heights of up to 15 meters.

Its ecological significance lies in its role as a component of cloud forest ecosystems, supporting diverse wildlife and contributing to the region's biodiversity.
