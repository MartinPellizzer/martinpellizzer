Oryza sativa var. obvasconcellosii

Oryza sativa var. obvasconcellosii, commonly known as the Obvasconcellosii rice variety, is a less commonly documented subspecies of rice.

It is characterized by its unique morphological traits, including specific leaf shapes and grain structures. This variety is primarily of interest in botanical studies due to its genetic diversity and potential for breeding programs. It typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, adapting to varied soil and climatic conditions.

Researchers study this subspecies to better understand rice evolution and improve crop resilience.
