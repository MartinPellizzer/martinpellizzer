Orobanche grandiuscula

Orobanche grandiuscula, commonly known as the giant broomrape, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

It is a member of the Orobanchaceae family and is native to various regions of Europe and Asia. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale in color. It is often found growing on the roots of grasses and other herbaceous plants.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can significantly impact the growth and health of its host species.
