Oxytropis imbricata f. albiflorus

Oxytropis imbricata f. albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered locoweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and erect, sparsely branched stems. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its root system is extensive and deep, allowing it to thrive in arid and rocky environments.

The plant is adapted to nutrient-poor soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert habitats.
