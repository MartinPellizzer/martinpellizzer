Ohashia

Ohashia, commonly known as the Japanese honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the genus Lonicera and is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces orange-yellow berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. Ohashia is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

It is also a popular ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and fragrance.
