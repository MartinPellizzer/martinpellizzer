Orchis expansa

Orchis expansa, commonly known as the broad-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lanceolate and arranged in a basal rosette, while its flowers are borne in a spike and have a distinctive three-lobed lip.

Orchis expansa is noted for its ecological importance, supporting various pollinators and serving as an indicator species for biodiversity-rich habitats.
