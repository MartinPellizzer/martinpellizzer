Opuntia × occidentalis var. megacarpa

Opuntia × occidentalis var. megacarpa, commonly known as the giant barrel cactus, is a hybrid species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, thick, ribbed stems that can grow up to 10 feet in height. The plant produces showy, vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its pads are covered in glochids, which are fine, hair-like spines that can cause irritation.

The species is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant, making it a hardy plant in desert landscapes.
