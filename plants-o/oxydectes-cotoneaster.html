Oxydectes cotoneaster

Oxydectes cotoneaster, commonly known as the Chinese cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces red or orange berries that are often eaten by birds, aiding in seed dispersal. Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for hedges and ornamental gardens.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
