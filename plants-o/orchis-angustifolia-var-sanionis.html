Orchis angustifolia var. sanionis

Orchis angustifolia var. sanionis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces spikes of pale yellow to greenish-white flowers, which are typically arranged in a loose cluster. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodlands or grassy areas.

This variety is notable for its delicate appearance and ecological significance in supporting pollinators.
