Orthosiphon scedastophyllus

Orthosiphon scedastophyllus, commonly known as Java tea or kaphi, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

The leaves are commonly used in traditional medicine for their diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.
