Oxytropis nothoxys

Oxytropis nothoxys, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats.

Oxytropis nothoxys plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
