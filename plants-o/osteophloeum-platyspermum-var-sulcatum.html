Osteophloeum platyspermum var. sulcatum

Osteophloeum platyspermum var. sulcatum, commonly known as the rough-seeded osteophloeum, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and elliptical leaves with a rough, textured surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a capsule containing numerous tiny seeds.

This species is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, shaded environments.
