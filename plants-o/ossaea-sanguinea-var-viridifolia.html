Ossaea sanguinea var. viridifolia

Ossaea sanguinea var. viridifolia, commonly known as the green-leafed bloodgrass, is a perennial plant native to the wetlands of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish-purple stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that range from green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for water gardeners and wetland restoration projects.
