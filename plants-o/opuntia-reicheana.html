Opuntia reicheana

Opuntia reicheana, commonly known as the desert spoon, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy, paddle-shaped stems that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for both humans and animals. Its leaves are modified into spines, which provide protection from herbivores and reduce water loss through transpiration.

Opuntia reicheana plays an important ecological role by providing food and shelter for various desert wildlife.
