Odontoglossum roezlianum

Odontoglossum roezlianum, commonly known as the roezlian orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments at high altitudes. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowering stems. Its flowers are showy, often with vibrant colors and intricate patterns, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
