Opuntia × lucayana

Opuntia × lucayana, commonly known as the Florida prickly pear, is a hybrid cactus native to the southeastern United States.

It features flat, fleshy stems that are typically green to reddish in color, covered in spines and glochids. The plant produces showy yellow or orange flowers during the summer months, followed by edible, fleshy fruits called tunas. Its stems are succulent and store water, allowing it to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.

Opuntia × lucayana is often used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
