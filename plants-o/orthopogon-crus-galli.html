Orthopogon crus-galli

Orthopogon crus-galli, commonly known as the common barnyard grass, is a widespread annual grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural fields.

Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various conditions make it a common competitor in many ecosystems.
