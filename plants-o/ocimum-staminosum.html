Ocimum staminosum

Ocimum staminosum, commonly known as the sacred basil or holy basil, is a member of the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It typically grows as an annual herb with square stems and opposite leaves.

Ocimum staminosum is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climatic conditions.
