Oxydium oxalidifolium

Oxydium oxalidifolium, commonly known as the oxalid leaf plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Oxalidaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive veining pattern, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
