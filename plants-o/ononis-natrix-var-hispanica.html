Ononis natrix var. hispanica

Ononis natrix var. hispanica, commonly known as Spanish sandwort, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in early summer. Its leaves are linear and hairy, adapted to thrive in sandy or rocky soils.

It is often found in dry, open habitats such as coastal dunes and gravelly slopes.
