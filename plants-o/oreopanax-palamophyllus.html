Oreopanax palamophyllus

Oreopanax palamophyllus, commonly known as the hairy oreopanax, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine, hair-like structures. The plant typically grows as an understory shrub in humid, shaded forest environments. Its leaves are usually elliptical or ovate in shape, with a glossy surface and prominent veins.

Oreopanax palamophyllus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
