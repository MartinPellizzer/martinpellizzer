Oxalis distans

Oxalis distans, commonly known as the spreading wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive reddish veins and produces small, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and can spread through rhizomes, forming dense mats.

Oxalis distans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
