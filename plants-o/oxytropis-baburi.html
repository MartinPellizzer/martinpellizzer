Oxytropis baburi

Oxytropis baburi, commonly known as the Babur wild pea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale blue or purple in color. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Oxytropis baburi is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility in arid ecosystems.
