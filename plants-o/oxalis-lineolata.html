Oxalis lineolata

Oxalis lineolata, commonly known as the striped wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features trifoliate leaves with distinctive white or yellow veins, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Oxalis lineolata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a ground cover.
