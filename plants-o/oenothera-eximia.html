Oenothera eximia

Oenothera eximia, commonly known as the showy evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and somewhat woody at the base.

Oenothera eximia is also known for its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
