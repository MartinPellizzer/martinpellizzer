Odontites vulgaris subsp. siculus

Odontites vulgaris subsp.

siculus, commonly known as Sicilian toothed grass, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions. It is often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions of southern Europe.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
