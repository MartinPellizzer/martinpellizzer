Oenothera odorata var. virescens

Oenothera odorata var. virescens, commonly known as the greenleaf evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It features slender, branching stems covered in fine, sticky hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a waxy coating. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that open in the evening, emitting a mild, sweet fragrance. Its flowers have four petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Onagraceae family.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.
