Oxalis hirsuticaulis

Oxalis hirsuticaulis, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features trifoliate leaves with a hairy texture and stems that are also covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Oxalis hirsuticaulis is often grown as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and delicate blossoms.
