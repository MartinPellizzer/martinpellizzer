Oxytropis lambertii var. sericea

Oxytropis lambertii var. sericea, commonly known as silverleaf locoweed, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert environments.

Oxytropis lambertii var. sericea is known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock.
