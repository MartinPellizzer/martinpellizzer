Obione tatarica

Obione tatarica, commonly known as Tatarian bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Obione tatarica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
