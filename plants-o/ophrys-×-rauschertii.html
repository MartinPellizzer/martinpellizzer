Ophrys × rauschertii

Ophrys × rauschertii, commonly known as the Rauschert's bee orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe, particularly in countries like Italy and Austria. The plant is characterized by its unique flowers that mimic the appearance of female bees, a feature known as sexual deception, which attracts male bees for pollination. Its leaves are grass-like, and it typically grows in shaded, moist habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological significance in its native ecosystems.
