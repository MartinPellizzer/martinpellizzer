Ocotea flexuosa

Ocotea flexuosa, commonly known as the flexuous ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and small, clustered white flowers. Its fruit is a drupe, typically containing a single seed, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife.

Ocotea flexuosa is valued for its timber and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
