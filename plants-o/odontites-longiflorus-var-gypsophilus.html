Odontites longiflorus var. gypsophilus

Odontites longiflorus var. gypsophilus, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has basal leaves that are elongated and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and hairy. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
