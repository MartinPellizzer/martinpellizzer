Orchis leptophylla var. laxa

Orchis leptophylla var. laxa, commonly known as the loose-leaf orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, calcareous soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and the flowers are arranged in a loose, racemose inflorescence.

This variety is notable for its delicate appearance and ecological significance in its native habitat.
