Odontites longiflorus var. roseus

Odontites longiflorus var. roseus, commonly known as the rose-flowered toothed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely toothed leaves and clusters of small, pinkish to rose-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
