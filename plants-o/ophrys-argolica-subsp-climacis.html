Ophrys argolica subsp. climacis

Ophrys argolica subsp.

climacis, commonly known as the mountain bee orchid, is a specialized orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Greece. It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptation to harsh, alpine environments. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brownish in color, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its ecological specificity and conservation status.
