Ophrys × gumprechtiana

Ophrys × gumprechtiana, commonly known as the Gumprecht's bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It is characterized by its striking, bee-like flower structure, which features a vividly colored lip that mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodland habitats and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its flowers are highly specialized, with intricate patterns and colors that play a crucial role in its pollination strategy.

Ophrys × gumprechtiana is a rare and protected species, valued for its unique botanical features and ecological significance.
