Orchis latifolia var. tenuior

Orchis latifolia var. tenuior, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodlands and grasslands. The plant features a tall, erect stem with a single, showy spike of pale to deep purple flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

The species is noted for its ecological importance as a food source for certain pollinators and as an indicator of undisturbed habitats.
