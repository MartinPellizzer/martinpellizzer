Ocotea alpina

Ocotea alpina, commonly known as the Andean ocotea, is a small evergreen tree native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in humid, cool climates at elevations above 2,000 meters. Its leaves are dark green and glossy, and it produces small, white flowers followed by dark blue berries.

Ocotea alpina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
