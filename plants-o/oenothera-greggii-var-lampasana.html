Oenothera greggii var. lampasana

Oenothera greggii var. lampasana, commonly known as the Lampasana evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening and close by morning. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
