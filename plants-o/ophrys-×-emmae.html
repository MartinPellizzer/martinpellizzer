Ophrys × emmae

Ophrys × emmae, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly found in southern Europe. The plant features a distinctive inflorescence with showy, bee-like flowers that have a complex structure designed to mimic female bees, aiding in its unique pollination strategy. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its overall appearance.

The hybrid is notable for its adaptability and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
