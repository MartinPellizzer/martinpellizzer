Odontoglossum deburghgraeveanum

Odontoglossum deburghgraeveanum, commonly known as the Burghgraeve's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant features a pseudobulbous structure, which helps it store water and nutrients in its native, often unpredictable climate. Its leaves are usually strap-like and arranged in a compact rosette, while its inflorescence bears numerous flowers with intricate, often colorful patterns.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
