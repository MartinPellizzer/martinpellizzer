Oenothera pyramidalis var. lindheimeri

Oenothera pyramidalis var. lindheimeri, commonly known as Lindheimer's evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oval seeds. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
