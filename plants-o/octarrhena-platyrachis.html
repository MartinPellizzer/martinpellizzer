Octarrhena platyrachis

Octarrhena platyrachis, commonly known as the broad-leafed senna, is a shrub native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments due to its deep root system. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds used in traditional medicine.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in ecological and medicinal contexts.
