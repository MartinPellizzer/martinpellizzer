Odontoglossum alexandrae var. wilsonii

Odontoglossum alexandrae var. wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's Alexandra orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb and linear leaves, typically growing in cool, humid environments. The plant produces striking, spurred orchid flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with a strong fragrance. It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for greenhouse cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes collected in the wild, raising conservation concerns.
