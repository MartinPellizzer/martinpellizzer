Ocotea depilis

Ocotea depilis, commonly known as the silky ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurel and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically white or pale yellow, and it produces small, dark blue berries.

Ocotea depilis is often found in humid, lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
