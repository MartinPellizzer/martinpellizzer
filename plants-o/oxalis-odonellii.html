Oxalis odonellii

Oxalis odonellii, commonly known as the Canary Islands wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of delicate yellow flowers. The plant is characterized by its succulent stems and leaves, which help it survive in arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Oxalis odonellii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
