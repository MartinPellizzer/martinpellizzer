Ocotea macrocalyx

Ocotea macrocalyx, commonly known as the large-fruited ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptic to ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces berries that are typically dark purple when mature.

The tree is valued for its aromatic wood and is often found in moist, shaded habitats within tropical forests.
