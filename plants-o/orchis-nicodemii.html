Orchis nicodemii

Orchis nicodemii, commonly known as Nicodemus's orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, spurred flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing violet or purple flowers with a distinctive lip. Its roots are fibrous and often grow in moist, calcareous soils.

Orchis nicodemii is also known for its historical association with the philosopher Nicodemus.
