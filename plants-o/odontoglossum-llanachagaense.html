Odontoglossum llanachagaense

Odontoglossum llanachagaense, commonly known as the Llanachaga orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to the cloud forests of Colombia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its elegant, spurred flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and purple. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, delicate pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires specific conditions to flourish, making it a challenging species to cultivate.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat destruction, it is considered vulnerable and is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
