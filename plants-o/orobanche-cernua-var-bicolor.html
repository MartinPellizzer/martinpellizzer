Orobanche cernua var. bicolor

Orobanche cernua var. bicolor, commonly known as the common dwarf broomrape, is a root-parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on its host for nutrients.

The plant produces small, two-colored flowers, typically pink and white, which are often clustered in racemose inflorescences. It parasitizes a variety of host plants, including grasses and legumes, and is often found in disturbed soils.
