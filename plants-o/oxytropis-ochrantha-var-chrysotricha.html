Oxytropis ochrantha var. chrysotricha

Oxytropis ochrantha var. chrysotricha, commonly known as the golden hair vetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in dense, hairy inflorescences. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, and its root system is often extensive, allowing it to thrive in rocky and arid environments. It is adapted to harsh conditions, including high altitudes and poor soils, and plays a role in soil stabilization.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
