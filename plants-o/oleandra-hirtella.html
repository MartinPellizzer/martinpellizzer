Oleandra hirtella

Oleandra hirtella, commonly known as the hairy oleander, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant has thick, woody stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Oleandra hirtella is also noted for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
