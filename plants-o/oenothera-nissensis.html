Oenothera nissensis

Oenothera nissensis, commonly known as the Nissene evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Oenothera nissensis is a herbaceous perennial with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves.

It thrives in sandy or loamy soils and is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks.
