Osyris schoberi

Osyris schoberi, commonly known as the desert osyris, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. The plant features small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually small and leathery, reducing water loss through transpiration.

Osyris schoberi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
