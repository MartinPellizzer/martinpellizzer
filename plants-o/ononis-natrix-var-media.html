Ononis natrix var. media

Ononis natrix var. media, commonly known as the middle watercress, is a variant of the watercress species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous plant that typically thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils near streams and rivers. The plant features small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are edible and have a peppery flavor similar to other watercress varieties.

This variant is often cultivated for its nutritional value and use in salads and culinary applications.
