Oxylobium cuneatum var. obovatum

Oxylobium cuneatum var. obovatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf oxylobium, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant, pink to reddish-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its botanical features include hairy stems and showy bracts that enhance its ornamental appeal.

It is often found in open woodlands and along streambanks in its native range.
