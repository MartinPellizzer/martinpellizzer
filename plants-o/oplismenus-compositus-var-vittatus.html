Oplismenus compositus var. vittatus

Oplismenus compositus var. vittatus, commonly known as the variegated sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in water gardens and bog environments.
