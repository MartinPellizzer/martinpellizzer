Ochna magdalenae

Ochna magdalenae, commonly known as the Magdalenae ochna, is a rare and critically endangered plant species native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is a small shrub characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and elliptical, glossy leaves. The plant belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is known for its unique floral structure, which consists of five sepals and numerous stamens. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native habitat.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
