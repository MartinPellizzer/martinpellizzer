Opuntia humahuacana

Opuntia humahuacana, commonly known as the Huahua cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its flat, paddle-like stems called cladodes. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its adaptations include thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

Opuntia humahuacana is also known for its ability to form dense colonies, contributing to soil stabilization in its native habitat.
