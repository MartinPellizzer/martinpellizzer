Oxydectes pachysepala

Oxydectes pachysepala, commonly known as the thick-sepaled orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically display vibrant colors such as pink, purple, and white. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually broad and strap-like, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Oxydectes pachysepala thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
