Ocimum sideritidis

Ocimum sideritidis, commonly known as the ironwort basil, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with opposite, ovate leaves that have a strong, spicy scent. Its flowers are small, tubular, and clustered in whorls, usually in shades of purple or pink.

Ocimum sideritidis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.
