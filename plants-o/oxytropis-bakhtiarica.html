Oxytropis bakhtiarica

Oxytropis bakhtiarica, commonly known as the Bakhtiar's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its flowers are usually pale purple or white, and it produces small, legume-like pods that contain its seeds.

Oxytropis bakhtiarica plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest for its potential ecological and medicinal properties.
