Opa integerrima

Opa integerrima, commonly known as the roughleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its sturdy, woody stems and large, rough-textured leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom throughout the warmer months. Opa integerrima is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to thrive in full sun and well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and as a cut flower.
