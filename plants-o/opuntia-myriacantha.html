Opuntia myriacantha

Opuntia myriacantha, commonly known as the Mexican prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems, which are covered in sharp spines and glochids. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the summer months, followed by edible fruits known as tunas. Opuntia myriacantha is a drought-tolerant plant, adapted to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.

Its structure and growth habits make it a unique and resilient member of the Cactaceae family.
