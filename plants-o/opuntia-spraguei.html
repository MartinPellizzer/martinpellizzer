Opuntia spraguei

Opuntia spraguei, commonly known as the Sprague's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems, which are often covered in spines and glochids. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruits, known as tunas, are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor.

Opuntia spraguei is well adapted to arid environments and plays a role in supporting local wildlife.
