Ocotea froesii

Ocotea froesii, commonly known as the Brazilian camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and fragrant bark. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by elliptical, dark blue berries. Its essential oils, extracted from the leaves and bark, are used in traditional medicine and as a source of camphor.

Ocotea froesii plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
