Oxalis filifoliolata

Oxalis filifoliolata, commonly known as the thread-leaf wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves that are finely divided, giving it a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, often arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial shade to full sun.

Oxalis filifoliolata is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
