Orlaya maritima

Orlaya maritima, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and spiky, silver-green leaves. The plant thrives in saline and sandy soils, often found along shorelines and dunes. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Orlaya maritima is also known for its medicinal properties, historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
