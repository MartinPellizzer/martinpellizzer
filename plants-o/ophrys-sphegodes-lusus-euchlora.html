Ophrys sphegodes lusus euchlora

Ophrys sphegodes lusus euchlora, commonly known as the bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to certain regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which closely resembles a female bee, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in grasslands or woodland edges. Its flowers are usually a mix of yellow and brown, with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of insects.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, this orchid is considered endangered in many areas.
