Ophiorrhiza rhodoneura

Ophiorrhiza rhodoneura, commonly known as the red-fruited mangrove, is a tropical plant belonging to the Rhizophoraceae family.

It is characterized by its unique morphological features, including aerial roots and a fibrous root system adapted to saline and waterlogged soils. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by bright red, fleshy fruits that are important for seed dispersal. It thrives in mangrove ecosystems, particularly in coastal areas of Southeast Asia.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
