Ophrys sphegodes var. subaesculapiana

Ophrys sphegodes var. subaesculapiana, commonly known as the bee orchid, is a species of orchid native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive floral structure, which closely resembles a male bee, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands. Its flowers are usually a mix of yellow, brown, and black, with a prominent lip that mimics the appearance of a bee's abdomen.

This orchid is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a resilient and widespread species.
