Oenothera lindleyana

Oenothera lindleyana, commonly known as the nightshade evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening and close by dawn. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds. Oenothera lindleyana is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is adapted to full sun.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
