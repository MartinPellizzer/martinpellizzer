Oenothera pusilla

Oenothera pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf evening primrose, is a small, herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows to a height of only a few inches, featuring delicate, yellow flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in sandy or well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Oenothera pusilla is also noted for its ability to attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
