Oxalis fernandensis

Oxalis fernandensis, commonly known as the Brazilian wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which resemble those of the common wood sorrel, and its clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Oxalis fernandensis is also noted for its ability to thrive in partial shade and its preference for well-drained soil.
