Oxytropis jordalii subsp. jordalii

Oxytropis jordalii subsp.

jordalii, commonly known as the Jordan's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of western North America. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils, often found in sagebrush steppe and desert scrub habitats.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink to purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

This subspecies is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient component of its ecosystem.
