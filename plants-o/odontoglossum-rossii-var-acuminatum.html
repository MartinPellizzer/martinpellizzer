Odontoglossum rossii var. acuminatum

Odontoglossum rossii var. acuminatum, commonly known as the Acuminatum Orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its sympodial growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of violet and white. The plant typically blooms in the spring and summer, with its inflorescence bearing several pendulous flowers. It prefers cool, humid conditions and well-drained, acidic soil, making it a popular choice for greenhouse cultivation.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental value make it a sought-after plant among orchid enthusiasts.
