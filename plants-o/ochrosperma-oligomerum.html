Ochrosperma oligomerum

Ochrosperma oligomerum, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in wetland or swampy habitats, often growing in dense stands.

Ochrosperma oligomerum is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
