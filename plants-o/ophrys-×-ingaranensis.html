Ophrys × ingaranensis

Ophrys × ingaranensis, commonly known as the Igaranensis Orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Balkans.

It belongs to the genus Ophrys, which is known for its mimicry of female insects to attract male pollinators. This plant typically produces intricate, bee-mimicking flowers that are yellow to brown in color. The hybrid is characterized by its complex floral structure and specialized pollination mechanism.

It thrives in woodland and meadow habitats, preferring well-drained soils and partial shade.
