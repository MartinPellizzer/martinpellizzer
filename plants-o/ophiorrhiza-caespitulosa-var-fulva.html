Ophiorrhiza caespitulosa var. fulva

Ophiorrhiza caespitulosa var. fulva, commonly known as the yellow clustered sandmat, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small shrub characterized by its dense, clustered growth habit and yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces small, white or yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often resembling a spike. It thrives in sandy or coastal soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in coastal vegetation and soil stabilization.
