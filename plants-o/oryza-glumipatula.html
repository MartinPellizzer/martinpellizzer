Oryza glumipatula

Oryza glumipatula, commonly known as the broad-leaved ricegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its wide, flat leaves and panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to water-saturated soils. Its botanical features include rhizomes and fibrous roots, which help it establish in these environments.

Oryza glumipatula is often found in association with other wetland plants and plays a role in maintaining ecological balance in these ecosystems.
