Ophrys × denisiana

Ophrys × denisiana, commonly known as the Denisian bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the genus Ophrys, which is known for its mimicry of female insects to attract male pollinators. The plant produces strikingly intricate flowers that resemble female wasps, a feature that plays a key role in its pollination strategy. Its botanical characteristics include a basal rosette of leaves and a single, showy inflorescence.

The species is notable for its rare occurrence and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
