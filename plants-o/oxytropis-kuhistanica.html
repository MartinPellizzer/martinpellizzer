Oxytropis kuhistanica

Oxytropis kuhistanica, commonly known as Kuhistan locoweed, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically yellow or pale green, and it is adapted to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.

Oxytropis kuhistanica is known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock.
