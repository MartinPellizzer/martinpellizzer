Oryzopsis tibetica var. psilolepis

Oryzopsis tibetica var. psilolepis, commonly known as the Tibetan grass, is a perennial grass species native to the high-altitude regions of Tibet.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing a high degree of adaptability to harsh conditions. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in alpine ecosystems.
