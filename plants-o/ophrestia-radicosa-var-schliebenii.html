Ophrestia radicosa var. schliebenii

Ophrestia radicosa var. schliebenii, commonly known as the Schlieben's rock cress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing a compact growth habit with small, fleshy leaves adapted to conserve moisture. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are arranged in dense, terminal inflorescences. Its flowers are actinomorphic, with four sepals and four petals, and it relies on insect pollination for reproduction.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
