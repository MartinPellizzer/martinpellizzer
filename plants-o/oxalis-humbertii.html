Oxalis humbertii

Oxalis humbertii, commonly known as the Humbert's wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive red veins and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of yellow and pink. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers partial to full sun exposure. It is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it contributes to the biodiversity of these ecosystems.

Oxalis humbertii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and blooming habits.
