Oenothera claviformis var. aurantiaca

Oenothera claviformis var. aurantiaca, commonly known as the orange evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
