Oryza sativa var. nigrescens

Oryza sativa var. nigrescens, commonly known as black rice, is a variety of rice characterized by its dark purple to black grains due to high levels of anthocyanin pigments.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is cultivated for its nutritional value and aesthetic appeal. The plant typically grows in flooded paddies, similar to other rice varieties, and has a short growth cycle. Its leaves are long and narrow, with the inflorescence consisting of spikelets that bear small, fluffy grains.

Black rice is also valued for its antioxidant properties and is used in various traditional cuisines and medicinal practices.
