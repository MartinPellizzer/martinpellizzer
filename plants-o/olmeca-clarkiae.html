Olmeca clarkiae

Olmeca clarkiae, commonly known as Clark's olmeca, is a rare and unique plant species native to the cloud forests of southern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep orange, adding to its visual appeal. Due to its limited habitat and threats from deforestation, it is considered endangered.

Conservation efforts are crucial to preserving this botanical gem.
