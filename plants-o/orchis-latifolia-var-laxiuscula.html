Orchis latifolia var. laxiuscula

Orchis latifolia var. laxiuscula, commonly known as the broad-leaved orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodland areas and prefers loamy soils. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces tall flowering stems with clusters of violet or purple flowers. Its flowers are characterized by a prominent lip and are often pollinated by bees and other insects.

This variety is part of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.
