Oryza sativa var. nigroviolacea

Oryza sativa var. nigroviolacea, commonly known as the black rice or forbidden rice, is a variety of rice characterized by its dark purple to black grain color.

This unique hue is due to the high concentration of anthocyanins, which are natural pigments with antioxidant properties. It is native to Southeast Asia and is often cultivated for its nutritional value and aesthetic appeal. The plant is a member of the grass family, Poaceae, and shares many botanical features with other rice species.

Its cultivation requires a warm, humid climate and ample water for optimal growth.
