Oxylobium robustum

Oxylobium robustum, commonly known as the redroot false indigo, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with red veins and produces spikes of violet to blue-purple flowers in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and natural landscapes.

Oxylobium robustum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
