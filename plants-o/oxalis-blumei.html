Oxalis blumei

Oxalis blumei, commonly known as the Blume's wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Oxalis blumei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and flowers.
