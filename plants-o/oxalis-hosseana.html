Oxalis hosseana

Oxalis hosseana, commonly known as the yellow woodsorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces edible leaves and flowers, though they contain oxalic acid, which can be harmful in large quantities. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

Oxalis hosseana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant foliage and delicate blooms.
