Opuntia pachypus

Opuntia pachypus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its flattened, paddle-shaped stems that are ribbed and covered in spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruits, known as tunas, are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor.

Opuntia pachypus is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water to survive.
