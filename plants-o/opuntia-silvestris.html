Opuntia silvestris

Opuntia silvestris, commonly known as the wild prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in sharp spines and clusters of vibrant yellow flowers. The plant produces edible fruits known as tunas, which are rich in nutrients and used in various culinary applications. Opuntia silvestris thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its succulent structure allows it to store water efficiently, making it a resilient plant in desert ecosystems.
