Origanum hirtum var. sardoum

Origanum hirtum var. sardoum, commonly known as Sardinian oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Sardinia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, aromatic leaves, and small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its foliage is highly aromatic, containing essential oils that contribute to its distinctive scent and culinary uses.

This variety is valued for both ornamental and medicinal purposes due to its hardiness and unique flavor profile.
