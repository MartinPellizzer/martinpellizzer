Ocotea lentii

Ocotea lentii, commonly known as the Guiana ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America, particularly found in Guyana and Suriname.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage and cylindrical, dark brown bark. The plant typically grows in humid lowland forests and can reach heights of up to 30 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

Ocotea lentii is valued for its timber, which is used in furniture and construction due to its durability and fine grain.
