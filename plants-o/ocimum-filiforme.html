Ocimum filiforme

Ocimum filiforme, commonly known as dwarf basil, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 cm, with small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually white or pale purple, arranged in whorls along the stems.

Ocimum filiforme is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, particularly in traditional African medicine.
