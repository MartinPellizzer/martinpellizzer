Oxalis secunda

Oxalis secunda, commonly known as the silverleaf wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive silvery-white underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Oxalis secunda is known for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
