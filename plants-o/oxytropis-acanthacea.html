Oxytropis acanthacea

Oxytropis acanthacea, commonly known as the thorned locoweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Oxytropis acanthacea plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife, though it can be toxic to livestock.
