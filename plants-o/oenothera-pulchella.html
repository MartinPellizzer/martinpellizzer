Oenothera pulchella

Oenothera pulchella, commonly known as the showy evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats like prairies and sandy soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

Oenothera pulchella is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
