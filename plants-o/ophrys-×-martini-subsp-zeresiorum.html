Ophrys × martini subsp. zeresiorum

Ophrys × martini subsp.

zeresiorum, commonly known as the Greater Heath Orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its striking, bee-like flowers that feature a complex structure with a prominent lip resembling an insect's body.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous root system and narrow, linear leaves.

The species is notable for its specialized pollination mechanism, which mimics the appearance and scent of female bees to attract male pollinators.
