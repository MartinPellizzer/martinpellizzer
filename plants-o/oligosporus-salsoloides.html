Oligosporus salsoloides

Oligosporus salsoloides, commonly known as the saltbush, is a halophytic shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It thrives in saline soils and is characterized by its small, fleshy leaves that reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers and has a deep root system that allows it to access groundwater. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in harsh environments.

Its ability to tolerate high salt concentrations makes it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
