Ocotea carolinensis

Ocotea carolinensis, commonly known as the Carolina pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible but have a mild, peppery flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
