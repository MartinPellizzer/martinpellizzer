Ocotea marginata

Ocotea marginata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests. Its leaves are elliptical, with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside, and it produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark purple berries.

Ocotea marginata is valued for its wood, which is used in furniture and construction, and for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
