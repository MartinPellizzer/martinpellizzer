Orchis angustifolia f. tiudiensis

Orchis angustifolia f. tiudiensis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a botanical variant of the narrow-leaved orchid species.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and delicate, pale purple to white flowers. This orchid is typically found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of Europe and Asia.

The plant exhibits a rhizomatous growth habit, with tubers that help it survive in its natural environment.
