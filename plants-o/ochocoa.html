Ochocoa

Ochocoa, commonly known as the "cacao tree," is a tropical plant native to the Amazon basin.

It belongs to the Theaceae family and is closely related to the genus Theobroma. The plant produces pods containing seeds, which are the source of cocoa mass used in chocolate production. Ochocoa thrives in warm, humid climates with rich, well-drained soil.

Its cultivation is significant in both ecological and economic contexts in South America.
