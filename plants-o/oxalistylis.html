Oxalistylis

Oxalistylis, commonly known as the waterwheel plant, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its whorled arrangement of leaves.

It is native to South America and thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats. The plant's leaves are often submerged, with some floating on the water surface, creating a distinctive appearance. Oxalistylis has small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically borne on short stalks.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
