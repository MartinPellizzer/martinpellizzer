Oenothera tamrae

Oenothera tamrae, commonly known as the Tamrae evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oily seeds. Oenothera tamrae is a herbaceous perennial with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
