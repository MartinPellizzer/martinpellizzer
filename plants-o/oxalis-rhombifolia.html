Oxalis rhombifolia

Oxalis rhombifolia, commonly known as the diamondleaf wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive rhombic-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Oxalis rhombifolia is also noted for its ability to produce edible leaves and flowers, though they should be consumed in moderation.
