Orchis entomantha

Orchis entomantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its flowers are usually arranged in a loose raceme and feature three sepals and three petals, with the labellum being distinctive in shape.

Orchis entomantha is known for its ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators such as bees and moths.
