Orchis × bergonii

Orchis × bergonii, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a naturally occurring cross between Orchis morio and Orchis anthropophora.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded woodland areas. The plant produces striking violet or purple flowers with a distinctive hooded labellum. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the rich, loamy soil.

Orchis × bergonii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
