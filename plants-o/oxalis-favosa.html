Oxalis favosa

Oxalis favosa, commonly known as the purple wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of delicate, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Oxalis favosa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and blooms.

It also has a long history of use in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
