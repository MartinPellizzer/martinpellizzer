Oxalis petersianum

Oxalis petersianum, commonly known as the yellow wood sorrel, is a perennial flowering plant native to South Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive yellowish-green color and produces small, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

The plant's leaves and flowers contain oxalic acid, which can be toxic in large quantities.
