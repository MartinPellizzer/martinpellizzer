Ochna oleifolia

Ochna oleifolia, commonly known as the African coffee tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by orange to red berries containing a single seed. Ochna oleifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and seasonal fruit display.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for a variety of tropical and subtropical climates.
