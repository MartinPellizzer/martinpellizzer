Osteospermum incanum

Osteospermum incanum, commonly known as the blue oatbrush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in dense, fluffy heads, resembling small clouds. The plant has silver-gray foliage and a compact, bushy growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Osteospermum incanum is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
