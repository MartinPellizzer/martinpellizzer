Oxalis cumingiana

Oxalis cumingiana, commonly known as the Cuming's wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves composed of three heart-shaped leaflets, with a distinctive violet or purple coloration. The plant produces small, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink, often resembling those of the genus Oxalis. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded woodland environments.

Oxalis cumingiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and blooms.
