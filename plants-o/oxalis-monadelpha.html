Oxalis monadelpha

Oxalis monadelpha, commonly known as the false shamrock, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, which resemble those of the shamrock. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of yellow and white, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Oxalis monadelpha is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate dry conditions.
