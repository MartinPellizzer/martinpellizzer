Ochna pervilleana

Ochna pervilleana, commonly known as the African yellowwood, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by orange to red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Ochna pervilleana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
