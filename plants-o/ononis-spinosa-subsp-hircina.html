Ononis spinosa subsp. hircina

Ononis spinosa subsp.

hircina, commonly known as the hairy bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides. It produces small, elliptical leaves and has a creeping growth habit, forming dense mats.
