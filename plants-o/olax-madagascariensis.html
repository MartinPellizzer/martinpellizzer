Olax madagascariensis

Olax madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar olive, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that belongs to the family Olacaceae. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, fragrant white or yellowish flowers. It produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
