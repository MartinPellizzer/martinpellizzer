Orchis angustifolia var. estonica

Orchis angustifolia var. estonica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodlands and meadows. The plant features a single, erect stem that bears a spike of violet to purple flowers. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a distinct midrib.

The species is part of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its delicate, showy blooms that attract pollinators.
