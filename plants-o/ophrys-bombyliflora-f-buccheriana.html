Ophrys bombyliflora f. buccheriana

Ophrys bombyliflora f. buccheriana, commonly known as the Buccherian bee orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant produces showy, bee-like flowers with a dark brown or blackish coloration, often adorned with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it faces challenging environmental conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, this orchid is considered a vulnerable species.
