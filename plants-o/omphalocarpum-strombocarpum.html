Omphalocarpum strombocarpum

Omphalocarpum strombocarpum, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky, blue or purple flower heads that resemble a crown, giving it its common name. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit and thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and ability to tolerate salt spray.
