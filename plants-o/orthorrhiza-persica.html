Orthorrhiza persica

Orthorrhiza persica, commonly known as Persian orthorrhiza, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Apiaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, branched root system that allows it to thrive in well-drained soils. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its leaves are pinnately divided, giving it a feathery appearance.

Orthorrhiza persica is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
