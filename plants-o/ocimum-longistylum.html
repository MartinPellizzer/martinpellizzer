Ocimum longistylum

Ocimum longistylum, commonly known as the long-stemmed basil, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like mint and sage. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically clustered in whorls, producing a mild, spicy fragrance.

Ocimum longistylum is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
