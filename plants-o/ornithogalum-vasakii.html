Ornithogalum vasakii

Ornithogalum vasakii, commonly known as the Armenian star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Armenia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white or pale blue flowers resembling stars, typically blooming in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is adapted to temperate climates and is often found in alpine meadows.

Ornithogalum vasakii is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been historically used in traditional medicine.
