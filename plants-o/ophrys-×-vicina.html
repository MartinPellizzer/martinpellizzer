Ophrys × vicina

Ophrys × vicina, commonly known as the mimic orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Europe.

It belongs to the genus Ophrys, which is known for its intricate floral structures that mimic female insects to attract male pollinators. The plant produces showy, bee-mimicking flowers that are typically brown or greenish in color, with intricate patterns resembling insect genitalia. These flowers are primarily pollinated by male bees through a process called sexual deception.

Ophrys × vicina is often found in meadows and grasslands, thriving in temperate climates.
