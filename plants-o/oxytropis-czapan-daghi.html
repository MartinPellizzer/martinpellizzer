Oxytropis czapan-daghi

Oxytropis czapan-daghi, commonly known as the Czapan-Daghi milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. Its leaves are compound, with small leaflets that help reduce water loss, and it produces pods that contain edible seeds.

This species plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest for its potential in ecological restoration projects.
