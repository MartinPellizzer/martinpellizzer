Olea verrucosa var. brachybotrys

Olea verrucosa var. brachybotrys, commonly known as the short-fruited olive, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically gray-green in color and covered in a fine, whitish pubescence. The flowers are small and greenish-yellow, appearing in clusters, and are followed by small, round fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black. The plant is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
