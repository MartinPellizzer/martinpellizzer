Oxalis mutabilis var. laxula

Oxalis mutabilis var. laxula, commonly known as the variable wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, delicate flowers. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, often appearing in spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Oxalis mutabilis var. laxula is often grown as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
