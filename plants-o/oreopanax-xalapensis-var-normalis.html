Oreopanax xalapensis var. normalis

Oreopanax xalapensis var. normalis, commonly known as the Mexican giant arrowroot, is a striking tropical plant native to southern Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, palm-like leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in length, with a distinctive veining pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by bright red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Botanically, it belongs to the Araceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
