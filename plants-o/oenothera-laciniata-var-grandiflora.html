Oenothera laciniata var. grandiflora

Oenothera laciniata var. grandiflora, commonly known as the showy evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features lance-shaped, toothed leaves and produces large, showy yellow flowers that open in the evening. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often growing in dry, rocky habitats. Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting nocturnal pollinators.
