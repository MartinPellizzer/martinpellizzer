Ochetophila divergens

Ochetophila divergens, commonly known as the divergent orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for anchoring in moist, shaded environments.

Ochetophila divergens is often found in humid forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
