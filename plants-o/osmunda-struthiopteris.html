Osmunda struthiopteris

Osmunda struthiopteris, commonly known as the royal fern, is a large, perennial fern that belongs to the family Osmundaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, robust fronds that can reach up to 2 meters in height, with a distinctive black, fibrous stipe. The plant has a complex life cycle involving both a sporophyte and a gametophyte stage, with the sporangia developing on the underside of its fertile fronds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands, and is often found in temperate regions across the Northern Hemisphere.

Osmunda struthiopteris plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and small animals.
