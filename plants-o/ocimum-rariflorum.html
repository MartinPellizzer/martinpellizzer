Ocimum rariflorum

Ocimum rariflorum, commonly known as the rare-flowered basil, is a member of the Lamiaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications due to their essential oils.

Ocimum rariflorum is noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
