Ophiopogon indicus

Ophiopogon indicus, commonly known as the Indian spathiphyllum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale purple, often resembling those of the orchid family. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often used in traditional medicine.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscaping in humid climates.
