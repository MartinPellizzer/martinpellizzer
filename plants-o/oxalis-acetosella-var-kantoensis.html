Oxalis acetosella var. kantoensis

Oxalis acetosella var. kantoensis, commonly known as the Japanese wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinct yellow veins and small, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. It has a creeping growth habit with rhizomatous stems that allow it to spread vegetatively.

Oxalis acetosella var. kantoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
