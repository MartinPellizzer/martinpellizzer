Ornithogalum thunbergianum var. concolor

Ornithogalum thunbergianum var. concolor, commonly known as the concolor white squill, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a bulbous stem base with strap-like, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
