Olea polygama

Olea polygama, commonly known as the African olive, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a glossy green surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by small, round, fleshy fruits. Olea polygama is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species suited to dry climates.

It has cultural and medicinal significance in some indigenous communities.
