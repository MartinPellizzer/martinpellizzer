Oreodaphne poeppigiana

Oreodaphne poeppigiana, commonly known as the yellow sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are typically followed by spherical, yellowish fruits. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.

The tree is valued for its timber, which is lightweight and has a pleasant scent.
