Ochna insculpta

Ochna insculpta, commonly known as the African velvetwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers followed by distinctive, winged fruits. It is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Ochna insculpta is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in dry environments.
