Oxalis alata var. rhombifolia

Oxalis alata var. rhombifolia, commonly known as the wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of small, yellow, five-petaled flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. Its botanical name reflects its close relation to other oxalis species, characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and floral structure.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
