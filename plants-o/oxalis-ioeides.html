Oxalis ioeides

Oxalis ioeides, commonly known as the golden oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically bright green with a subtle sheen. The plant produces clusters of small, delicate flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Oxalis ioeides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate a range of environmental conditions.
