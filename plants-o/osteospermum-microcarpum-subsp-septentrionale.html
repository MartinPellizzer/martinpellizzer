Osteospermum microcarpum subsp. septentrionale

Osteospermum microcarpum subsp.

septentrionale, commonly known as the northern blue osteospermum, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces showy composite flowers that attract pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and drought tolerance.
