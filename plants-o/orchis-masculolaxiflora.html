Orchis masculolaxiflora

Orchis masculolaxiflora, commonly known as the lax-flowered male orchid, is a rare and specialized member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring a tall, erect stem with a single, showy flower that is typically yellowish-green in color. The plant thrives in specific habitats, such as calcareous grasslands and open woodlands, where it requires well-drained soil and partial sunlight. Its reproductive strategy involves a complex relationship with mycorrhizal fungi, which is essential for its germination and growth.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, this species is considered endangered in many regions.
