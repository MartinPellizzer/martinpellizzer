Oberonia pachyglossa

Oberonia pachyglossa, commonly known as the broad-lipped Oberonia, is a tropical orchid native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves and striking, pendulous flowers with a broad, lip-like structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a contrasting red or orange lip, making it visually appealing.

Oberonia pachyglossa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in specialized orchid collections.
