Orchis germanorum

Orchis germanorum, commonly known as the German orchid, is a species of orchid native to Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodlands and meadows. Its flowers are spurred at the base, a distinctive feature of the orchid family.

Orchis germanorum plays an important role in pollination, attracting various insects such as bees and butterflies.
