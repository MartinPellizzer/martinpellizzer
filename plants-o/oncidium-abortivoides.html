Oncidium abortivoides

Oncidium abortivoides, commonly known as the abortive orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its unique, often pendulous inflorescences that bear small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and green. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its pseudobulbs are relatively short and thick, supporting its evergreen foliage.

Oncidium abortivoides is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
