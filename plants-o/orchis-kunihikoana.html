Orchis kunihikoana

Orchis kunihikoana, commonly known as the Kunihiko's orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to Japan.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure and coloration. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its flowers are usually purple or violet with intricate patterns, and it produces small, greenish-brown seed pods.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a conservation concern.
