Oxalis stenoptera var. alba

Oxalis stenoptera var. alba, commonly known as the white wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with three heart-shaped leaflets, often tinged with red or purple veins. The plant produces small, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Oxalis stenoptera var. alba is often found in woodlands and along stream banks, where it contributes to the understory flora.
