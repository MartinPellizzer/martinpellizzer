Otoglossum palaciosii

Otoglossum palaciosii, commonly known as the Palacios' orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the high-altitude regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in late spring. The plant features a slender rhizome and produces a single, showy spike with several small, lance-shaped flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Otoglossum palaciosii is considered endangered and is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
