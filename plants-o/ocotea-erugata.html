Ocotea erugata

Ocotea erugata, commonly known as the rough-leaved ocotea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its dark green, rough-textured leaves and small, clustered white flowers. Its fruits are small, woody drupes that contain a single seed.

Ocotea erugata is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical rainforests.
