Oxalis mollis var. glandulosa

Oxalis mollis var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with a waxy, glandular coating that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique foliage and delicate blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
