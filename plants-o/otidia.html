Otidia

Otidia, commonly known as the "false dandelion," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

These plants are characterized by their showy, dandelion-like flowers that typically have yellow petals and a composite structure. The leaves are usually alternate, lance-shaped, and have a rough or hairy texture.
