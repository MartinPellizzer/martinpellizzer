Oxydectes peltophora

Oxydectes peltophora, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a central dark brown star-shaped marking. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

The botanical features of Oxydectes peltophora make it a visually distinctive and ecologically significant member of the orchid family.
