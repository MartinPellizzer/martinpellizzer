Oxalis georgensis

Oxalis georgensis, commonly known as the Georgian wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink or white. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its stems are often reddish and covered with fine hairs.

Oxalis georgensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
