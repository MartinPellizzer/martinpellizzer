Oxycoccus palustris subsp. hagerupii

Oxycoccus palustris subsp.

hagerupii, commonly known as the Hagerup cranberry, is a subspecies of the common cranberry native to northern Europe. It is a low-growing evergreen shrub with small, red, fleshy berries that are typically harvested for culinary and medicinal uses.

The plant thrives in acidic, wet soils and is often found in bogs and heathlands. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, and it produces small, white flowers in the spring.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in traditional herbal remedies.
