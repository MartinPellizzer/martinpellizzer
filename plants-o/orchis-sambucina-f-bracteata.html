Orchis sambucina f. bracteata

Orchis sambucina f. bracteata, commonly known as the bracteate sweet flag orchid, is a rare and distinctive orchid species native to specific regions of Europe.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and damp meadows, where it thrives in rich, humusy soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while the stems are usually short and stout.
