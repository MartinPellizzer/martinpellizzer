Oxalis melanosticta

Oxalis melanosticta, commonly known as the black-stained wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive dark purple or blackish tinge on the underside, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Oxalis melanosticta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
