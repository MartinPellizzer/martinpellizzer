Ocotea monzonensis

Ocotea monzonensis, commonly known as the Monzon's ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. Its fruit is a drupe, typically containing a single seed.

Ocotea monzonensis is valued for its ecological role and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
