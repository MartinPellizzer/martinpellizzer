Ormosia cruenta

Ormosia cruenta, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and compound leaves. The tree produces hard, dense wood that is highly valued for its durability and fine grain. Ormosia cruenta thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
