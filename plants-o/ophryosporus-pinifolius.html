Ophryosporus pinifolius

Ophryosporus pinifolius, commonly known as the pineleaf blister rust, is a fungal pathogen that primarily affects coniferous trees.

It belongs to the family Trypetheliales and is characterized by its ability to produce distinctive, blister-like lesions on the foliage of its host plants. The fungus overwinters as a dormant structure called a cushion, which germinates in the spring to infect new growth. It has a complex life cycle that involves multiple hosts, including various species of pine.

The disease can cause significant damage to forest ecosystems and is a concern for forest health management.
