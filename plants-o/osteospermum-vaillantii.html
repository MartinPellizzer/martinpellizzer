Osteospermum vaillantii

Osteospermum vaillantii, commonly known as the blue osteospermum, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the sunflower family, Asteraceae, and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flower heads. The plant features a basal rosette of silver-gray leaves and produces showy composite flowers with ray florets. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Osteospermum vaillantii is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.
