Osteospermum oppositifolium

Osteospermum oppositifolium, commonly known as the blue gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and a compact, bushy growth habit. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Osteospermum oppositifolium thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
