Ornithogalum paradoxum

Ornithogalum paradoxum, commonly known as the paradoxical cowslip, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant is characterized by its underground bulb, which serves as a storage organ and helps it survive dry periods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
