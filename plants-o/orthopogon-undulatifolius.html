Orthopogon undulatifolius

Orthopogon undulatifolius, commonly known as the wavy-leaf grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family, Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, wavy-edged leaves that give it its common name. Native to certain regions of South America, this plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet cluster, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

The plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
