Orchis ustulata

Orchis ustulata, commonly known as the scorched orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, often yellowish or greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in grasslands or woodland margins. Its leaves are linear to lanceolate, and the flowers are usually arranged in a loose raceme.

Orchis ustulata is known for its unique ability to thrive in disturbed or burned habitats, which contributes to its common name.
