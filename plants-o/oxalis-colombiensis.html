Oxalis colombiensis

Oxalis colombiensis, commonly known as the Colombian wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces edible leaves and stems, which are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Oxalis colombiensis is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial in soil enrichment.
