Obione stylosa

Obione stylosa, commonly known as the slender sandbur, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant produces small, spiny burs that aid in seed dispersal by attaching to animal fur or clothing. Obione stylosa typically grows in sandy or disturbed soils, often found in open fields and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it an adaptable and resilient species in various ecological settings.
