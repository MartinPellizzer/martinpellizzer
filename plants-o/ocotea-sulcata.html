Ocotea sulcata

Ocotea sulcata, commonly known as the Brazilian camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green foliage and smooth, glossy bark. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by oval, woody fruits containing a single seed. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and analgesic properties.

Ocotea sulcata is valued for its timber, which is used in furniture and construction due to its durability and attractive grain.
