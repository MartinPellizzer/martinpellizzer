Ornithogalum leucanthum

Ornithogalum leucanthum, commonly known as the white star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a short, rhizomatous stem and produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette at the base. It is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
