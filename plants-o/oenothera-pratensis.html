Oenothera pratensis

Oenothera pratensis, commonly known as the showy evening primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has hairy, alternate leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Oenothera pratensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinator populations.
