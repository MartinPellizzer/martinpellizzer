Oxalis dichondrifolia

Oxalis dichondrifolia, commonly known as the double-leaf wood sorrel, is a species native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaves, which are divided into two pairs of heart-shaped lobes. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Oxalis dichondrifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
