Oryza sativa var. affinis

Oryza sativa var. affinis, commonly known as the wild rice, is a species of rice native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance and produces small, clustered grains. This variety is often found in wetland habitats and is adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic environments. Its botanical features include long, narrow leaves and a flowering structure that resembles a spikelet.

Oryza sativa var. affinis is considered an important wild relative of cultivated rice, offering genetic diversity for breeding programs.
