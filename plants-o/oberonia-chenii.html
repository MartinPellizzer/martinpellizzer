Oberonia chenii

Oberonia chenii, commonly known as Chen's orchid, is a species of flowering plant in the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of China, Vietnam, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate, often fragrant flowers. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.

Oberonia chenii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is appreciated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
