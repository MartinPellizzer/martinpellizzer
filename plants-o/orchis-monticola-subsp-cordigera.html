Orchis monticola subsp. cordigera

Orchis monticola subsp.

cordigera, commonly known as the alpine orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe. It features a slender, leafless stem that can grow up to 40 cm in height, bearing a single or few flowers.

The flowers are typically violet or purple in color, with a distinctive hooded lip that distinguishes it from other orchid species. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

The plant is adapted to cold climates and is often found at elevations between 1,500 and 2,500 meters.
