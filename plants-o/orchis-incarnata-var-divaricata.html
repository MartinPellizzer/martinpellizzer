Orchis incarnata var. divaricata

Orchis incarnata var. divaricata, commonly known as the pink lady's tresses, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, dense, spirally arranged inflorescence of pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it produces a slender, erect stem.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
