Oenothera campestris

Oenothera campestris, commonly known as the evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow or orange flowers that open in the evening. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in prairies and roadsides. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Oenothera campestris is also known for its medicinal uses, particularly for its oil-rich seeds.
