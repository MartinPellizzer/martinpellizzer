Oxytropis oxyphylla f. caulescens

Oxytropis oxyphylla f. caulescens, commonly known as the tall milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright, branching stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in the spring. It is adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots that allow it to access water from deeper soil layers.

This subspecies is notable for its distinctive growth form and is often found in steppe and desert habitats.
