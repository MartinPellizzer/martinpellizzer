Olea obtusifolia

Olea obtusifolia, commonly known as the Mediterranean olive, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that are typically rounded or oval in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are followed by drupes, or fruit, which are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. Olea obtusifolia is well adapted to dry climates and is drought-tolerant, making it a hardy species for arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
