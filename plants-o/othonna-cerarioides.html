Othonna cerarioides

Othonna cerarioides, commonly known as the silver cotyledon, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically silver or blue-gray in color, which help it conserve water in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Othonna cerarioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry climates.
