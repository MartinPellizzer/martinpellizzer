Osbeckia debilis

Osbeckia debilis, commonly known as the slender osbeckia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It typically thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its delicate, hairy stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Osbeckia debilis is often found in arid or semi-arid environments, contributing to the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.
