Oxalis compressa var. purpurascens

Oxalis compressa var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive purple tinge on the underside, and small, five-petaled flowers that range from white to pink. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to spread through rhizomes, forming dense ground cover.

Oxalis compressa var. purpurascens is also noted for its ability to tolerate acidic soil conditions.
