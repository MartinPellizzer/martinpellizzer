Ophrys × marzuola

Ophrys × marzuola, commonly known as the bee orchid hybrid, is a fascinating plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Ophrys apifera, the bee orchid, and Ophrys insectifera, the wasp orchid. The plant is characterized by its striking, bee-like flowers that have evolved to mimic female bees, aiding in its unique pollination strategy. These flowers are typically greenish-brown with intricate patterns that resemble the body of a bee.

Ophrys × marzuola is often found in calcareous soils across southern Europe, thriving in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.
