Oenothera ammophila var. germanica

Oenothera ammophila var. germanica, commonly known as the German sand-verbena, is a flowering plant native to sandy coastal regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in dunes or along shorelines. Its leaves are linear and gray-green, adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments.

It plays a role in stabilizing sand dunes and providing nectar for pollinators.
