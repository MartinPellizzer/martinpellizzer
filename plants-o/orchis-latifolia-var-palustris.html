Orchis latifolia var. palustris

Orchis latifolia var. palustris, commonly known as the marsh orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear numerous showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, preferring moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a distinct midrib.

This variety is known for its adaptability to wetland environments and plays a role in supporting local biodiversity.
