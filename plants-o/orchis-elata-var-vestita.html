Orchis elata var. vestita

Orchis elata var. vestita, commonly known as the white helleborine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy flowers. The flowers are typically white or pale green, with a distinctive hooded lip that aids in pollination. The plant thrives in woodland habitats and is often found in moist, shaded areas.

It produces a single, elongated seed capsule that disperses its seeds through wind.
