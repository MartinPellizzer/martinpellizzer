Orchis latifolia var. gemmana

Orchis latifolia var. gemmana, commonly known as the broad-leaved orchid, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has lance-shaped leaves that are broad at the base. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and it is known for its ability to thrive in shaded environments.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance, supporting various pollinators and contributing to biodiversity in its native regions.
