Ocotea langsdorffii

Ocotea langsdorffii, commonly known as the Brazilian camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant typically grows in humid, lowland forests and can reach heights of up to 30 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a distinctive glossy appearance.

The tree is valued for its essential oils, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of camphor.
