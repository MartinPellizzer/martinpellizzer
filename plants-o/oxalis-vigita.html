Oxalis vigita

Oxalis vigita, commonly known as the purple shamrock, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive purple hue. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

Oxalis vigita is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and delicate floral display.
