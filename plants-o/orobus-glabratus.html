Orobus glabratus

Orobus glabratus, commonly known as the smooth orobus, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and often nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemose inflorescences.

Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it is known for its hardy nature in arid environments.
