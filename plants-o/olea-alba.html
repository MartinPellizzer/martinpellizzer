Olea alba

Olea alba, commonly known as the white oleander, is a species of flowering plant in the Oleaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The plant is typically shrub-like, with a woody stem and can grow up to several meters in height. Olea alba is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in sunny conditions.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in gardens around the world.
