Ornithopus perpusillus subsp. roseus

Ornithopus perpusillus subsp.

roseus, commonly known as the pink sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pink to rose-colored flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in coastal or inland dunes. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, and it is drought-tolerant.

This subspecies is part of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants.
