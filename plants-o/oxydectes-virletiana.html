Oxydectes virletiana

Oxydectes virletiana, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically velvety to the touch. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist lowland areas.

Oxydectes virletiana is also known for its ability to grow in disturbed soils, making it a common weed in agricultural settings.
