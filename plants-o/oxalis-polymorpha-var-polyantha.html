Oxalis polymorpha var. polyantha

Oxalis polymorpha var. polyantha, commonly known as the purple shamrock, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive purple tinge and produces small, five-petaled flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. It is often grown as a decorative ground cover or in containers due to its compact growth habit.

Oxalis polymorpha var. polyantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
