Orchis maculata f. obscura

Orchis maculata f. obscura, commonly known as the dark-spotted orchid, is a rare and striking member of the orchid family.

It is characterized by its dark purple to blackish flowers with contrasting white or pale yellow markings. This subspecies is native to the northern regions of Europe, particularly in areas with calcareous soils. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and meadows, reaching heights of up to 40 cm.

Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and flies.
