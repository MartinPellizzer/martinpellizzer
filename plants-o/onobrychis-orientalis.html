Onobrychis orientalis

Onobrychis orientalis, commonly known as the oriental sainfoin, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a deep root system, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils. It is often used in agriculture as a forage crop due to its high nutritional value.

Additionally, it plays a role in soil nitrogen fixation, enhancing soil fertility.
