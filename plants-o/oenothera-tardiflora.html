Oenothera tardiflora

Oenothera tardiflora, commonly known as late-flowering evening primrose, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy yellow flowers that open in the evening. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oily seeds. Oenothera tardiflora thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as prairies and disturbed areas.

It is a member of the Onagraceae family and is known for its ecological importance to pollinators.
