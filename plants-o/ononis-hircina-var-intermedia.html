Ononis hircina var. intermedia

Ononis hircina var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate hairy cinquefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in dry, rocky, or gravelly soils, often found in open woodland or grassland habitats. The plant features a basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters. Its flowers are typically visited by various pollinators, including bees and flies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
