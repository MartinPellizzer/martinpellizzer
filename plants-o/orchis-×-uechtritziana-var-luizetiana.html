Orchis × uechtritziana var. luizetiana

Orchis × uechtritziana var. luizetiana, commonly known as the Luizetian orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing showy, lip-shaped petals that attract pollinators. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial shade to full sun.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
