Oeosporangium × kurdicum

Oeosporangium × kurdicum, commonly known as the hybrid lichen, is a unique combination of two lichen species, Oeosporangium and Kurdicum.

It exhibits a complex symbiotic relationship between a fungus and a green alga or cyanobacterium, typical of lichens. The thallus, or body, of this lichen is usually crust-like, adhering tightly to the substrate. It thrives in humid environments, often found on rocks or tree bark in temperate regions.

The hybrid nature of Oeosporangium × kurdicum contributes to its adaptability and ecological significance in diverse habitats.
