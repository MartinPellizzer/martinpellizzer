Opuntia tomentosa var. herrerae

Opuntia tomentosa var. herrerae, commonly known as the hairy prickly pear, is a succulent cactus native to Central America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems covered in dense, white, woolly hairs. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the summer months. Its fruit, known as a tunas, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to dry climates.
