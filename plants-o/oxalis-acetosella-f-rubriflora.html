Oxalis acetosella f. rubriflora

Oxalis acetosella f. rubriflora, commonly known as the red-flowered wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a low-growing perennial with trifoliate leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that often have a red or purple tinge. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas. Its flowers are short-lived but attract pollinators such as bees and flies.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
