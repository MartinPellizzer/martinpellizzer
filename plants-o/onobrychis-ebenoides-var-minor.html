Onobrychis ebenoides var. minor

Onobrychis ebenoides var. minor, commonly known as lesser vetch, is a perennial legume native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in dry, calcareous soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

This species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment.
