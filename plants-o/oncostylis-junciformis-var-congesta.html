Oncostylis junciformis var. congesta

Oncostylis junciformis var. congesta, commonly known as the clustered rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and rushes, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, three-ranked flowers.

The species is noted for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and its role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
