Opuntia galapageia var. galapageia

Opuntia galapageia var. galapageia, commonly known as the Galápagos prickly pear, is a cacti species native to the Galápagos Islands.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems that are covered in spines and are often ribbed. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the dry season, followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in arid and coastal environments, adapting well to the unique climate of the Galápagos.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for local wildlife.
