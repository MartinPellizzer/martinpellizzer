Oxalis gracilissima

Oxalis gracilissima, commonly known as the slender wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb with trifoliate leaves and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and stems that often root at the nodes.

Oxalis gracilissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
