Omphalodes nana var. aretioides

Omphalodes nana var. aretioides, commonly known as the dwarf moonflower, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features small, fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has hairy, ovate leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in moisture retention. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique and ornamental addition to rock gardens and alpine settings.
