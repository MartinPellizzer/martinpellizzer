Oligostachyum puberulum

Oligostachyum puberulum, commonly known as the hairy bamboo, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with white to pale yellow flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Oligostachyum puberulum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
