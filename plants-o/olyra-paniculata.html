Olyra paniculata

Olyra paniculata, commonly known as the African broom, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that appear in panicles. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Olyra paniculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
