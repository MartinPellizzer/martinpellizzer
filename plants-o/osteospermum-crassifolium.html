Osteospermum crassifolium

Osteospermum crassifolium, commonly known as the silver bush, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and showy yellow-orange flower heads that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has a compact growth habit and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping. Its flowers are composed of ray florets and a central disk, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Osteospermum crassifolium is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
