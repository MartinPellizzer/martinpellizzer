Oxalis cuernavacana

Oxalis cuernavacana, commonly known as the Cuernavaca wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Mexico.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive veins, resembling those of a clover. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically yellow or white, often with five petals. It thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Oxalis cuernavacana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
