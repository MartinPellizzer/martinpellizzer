Ottonia aguadana

Ottonia aguadana, commonly known as the Mexican firecracker, is a striking plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant red, tubular flowers that resemble small firecrackers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its foliage consists of opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

Ottonia aguadana is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its bright, showy blooms.
