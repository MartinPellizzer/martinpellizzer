Ocotea lucida

Ocotea lucida, commonly known as white cedar or white sand cedar, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green foliage and smooth, grey bark. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by dark blue, spherical berries. Its leaves are typically elliptical and have a leathery texture.

Ocotea lucida is often found in humid, lowland forests and is valued for its timber and medicinal properties.
