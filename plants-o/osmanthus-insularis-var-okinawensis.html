Osmanthus insularis var. okinawensis

Osmanthus insularis var. okinawensis, commonly known as the Okinawa osmanthus, is a fragrant flowering plant native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It is characterized by its small, golden-yellow flowers that emit a sweet, aromatic scent, especially during the winter months. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its delicate blooms. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

This variety is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its use in traditional Japanese perfumery and cuisine.
