Odontoglossum × lyroglossum

Odontoglossum × lyroglossum, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Odontoglossum aemulum and Odontoglossum lyroglossum.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Andes mountains of South America. The plant features showy, orchid-like flowers with intricate patterns and vibrant colors, often in shades of pink, white, and purple. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a model for studying hybridization in orchids.
