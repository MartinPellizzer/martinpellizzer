Oenothera kleinii

Oenothera kleinii, commonly known as the Klein's evening primrose, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the genus Oenothera, which includes various species of evening primroses known for their showy, nocturnal blooms. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, opening in the evening and closing by dawn.

Oenothera kleinii is characterized by its slender stems, lance-shaped leaves, and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
