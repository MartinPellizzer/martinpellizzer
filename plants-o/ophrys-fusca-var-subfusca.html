Ophrys fusca var. subfusca

Ophrys fusca var. subfusca, commonly known as the dark bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species found in specific habitats across Europe.

It is characterized by its intricate floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee, a trait evolved to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually dark brown to black in color, with intricate patterns that aid in mimicry.

Due to its specific habitat requirements and declining populations, it is considered a vulnerable species in many regions.
