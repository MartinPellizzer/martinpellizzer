Oxytropis kozhuharovii

Oxytropis kozhuharovii, commonly known as Kozhuharov's oxytropis, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, hairy stems, and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or pink. Its leaves are pinnately compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
