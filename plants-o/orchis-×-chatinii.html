Orchis × chatinii

Orchis × chatinii, commonly known as the Chatin's twayblade, is a hybrid orchid species native to Europe.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a spike, with a distinctive lip that is often pale or white. It has a tuberous root system and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves.

Due to its hybrid origin, it exhibits a range of morphological variations in its natural distribution.
