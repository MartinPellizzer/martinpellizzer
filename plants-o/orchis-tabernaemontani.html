Orchis tabernaemontani

Orchis tabernaemontani, commonly known as the purple marsh orchid, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in wet, marshy habitats and has a tall, erect stem with several leaves. Its roots are fibrous and often form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi.

The orchid blooms in late spring to early summer, producing a showy inflorescence that attracts pollinators such as bees and wasps.
