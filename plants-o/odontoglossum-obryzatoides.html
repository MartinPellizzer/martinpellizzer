Odontoglossum obryzatoides

Odontoglossum obryzatoides, commonly known as the violet orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its erect, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking violet or purple flowers with a distinctive, spurred lip. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Odontoglossum obryzatoides is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
