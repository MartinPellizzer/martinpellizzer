Orchis × dicorifiana

Orchis × dicorifiana, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a naturally occurring cross between Orchis morio and Orchis mascula.

It is a herbaceous perennial with a tall, erect stem that bears a dense spike of violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or grassland habitats. Its flowers have a distinctive spurred lip and are pollinated by insects such as bees and flies.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in gardens and naturalized areas.
