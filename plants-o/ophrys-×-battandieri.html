Ophrys × battandieri

Ophrys × battandieri, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Ophrys apifera (the bee orchid) and Ophrys insectifera (the wasp orchid), resulting in a unique combination of characteristics from both parents. The plant is notable for its striking, bee-like flowers that mimic the appearance and scent of female bees, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. Its flowers are typically yellow or brown with intricate patterns that enhance their visual appeal and mimicry.

Ophrys × battandieri is often found in Mediterranean regions, where it thrives in open, sunny habitats with well-drained soils.
