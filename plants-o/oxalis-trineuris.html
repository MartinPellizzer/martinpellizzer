Oxalis trineuris

Oxalis trineuris, commonly known as the three-veined wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It features trifoliate leaves with three heart-shaped leaflets, each adorned with distinct veins. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
