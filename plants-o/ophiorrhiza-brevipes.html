Ophiorrhiza brevipes

Ophiorrhiza brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed sandmat, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in sandy or coastal soils, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has simple, opposite leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release its seeds.

Ophiorrhiza brevipes is notable for its ability to thrive in harsh, saline environments.
