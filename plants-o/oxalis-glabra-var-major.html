Oxalis glabra var. major

Oxalis glabra var. major, commonly known as the common wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are heart-shaped and often have a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Oxalis glabra var. major is a perennial herb that spreads through rhizomes and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
