Odontonema mortonii

Odontonema mortonii, commonly known as the pink flame tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking pink to red bract-like flowers that resemble flames, which appear in clusters at the ends of branches. The plant has compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces small, inconspicuous true flowers surrounded by showy bracts. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Odontonema mortonii is relatively easy to grow and can be propagated through seeds or cuttings.
