Oxytropis gandeensis

Oxytropis gandeensis, commonly known as the Gande milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Tibet.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale purple or pink in color. It is adapted to harsh alpine conditions, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight.

Oxytropis gandeensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
