Oncostemum macroscyphon

Oncostemum macroscyphon, commonly known as the giant flame vine, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of bright red, tubular flowers that resemble flames, which attract hummingbirds and other pollinators. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its dramatic appearance and showy blooms.

Oncostemum macroscyphon is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for tropical gardens and greenhouses.
