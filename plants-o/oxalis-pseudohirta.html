Oxalis pseudohirta

Oxalis pseudohirta, commonly known as the velvetleaf wood sorrel, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. It has a fibrous root system and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Oxalis pseudohirta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
