Oxypetalum tomentosum

Oxypetalum tomentosum, commonly known as the woolly oxypetalum, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in humid, shaded environments within the Andes region. The plant is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. Its leaves are often covered in a fine, white pubescence, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique texture and floral display.
