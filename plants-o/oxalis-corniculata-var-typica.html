Oxalis corniculata var. typica

Oxalis corniculata var. typica, commonly known as the wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to Asia, Africa, and Australia.

It is a low-growing annual or perennial herb with trifoliate leaves resembling those of clover. The plant produces small, delicate yellow flowers with five petals, often accompanied by five sepals. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in gardens and woodland areas.

Oxalis corniculata var. typica is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated as a ground cover.
