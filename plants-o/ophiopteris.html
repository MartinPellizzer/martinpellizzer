Ophiopteris

Ophiopteris, commonly known as the water fern, is a genus of aquatic and semi-aquatic ferns belonging to the family Ophioglossaceae.

These plants typically grow in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams, where they thrive in moist, shaded environments. Ophiopteris species are characterized by their delicate, lance-shaped leaves that often form dense, floating mats on the water's surface. The sporangia, which produce spores, are usually clustered on the underside of the leaves, a feature that aids in their reproductive strategy.

Due to their unique morphology and ecological role, Ophiopteris species are often indicators of healthy aquatic ecosystems.
