Ophrys fusca f. forestieri

Ophrys fusca f. forestieri, commonly known as the forestier's bee orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to specific regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which closely resembles a female bee, a feature evolved to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist woodland habitats, where it thrives in nutrient-poor soils. Its flowers are usually a mix of brown and green, with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of insects.

This orchid is of particular interest to botanists due to its specialized pollination mechanism and limited distribution.
