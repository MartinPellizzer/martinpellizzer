Ochna harmandii var. retusa

Ochna harmandii var. retusa, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and distinctive, showy yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a thick, woody trunk and produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, tropical or subtropical climates.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
