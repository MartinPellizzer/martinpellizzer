Oxalis laxa var. major

Oxalis laxa var. major, commonly known as the large-leaf wood sorrel, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features trifoliate leaves with three heart-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its stems are often prostrate or ascending, and it produces capsule fruits containing several seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
