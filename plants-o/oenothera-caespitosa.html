Oenothera caespitosa

Oenothera caespitosa, commonly known as the tufted evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oval seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Oenothera caespitosa is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
