Octomeria exchlorophyllata

Octomeria exchlorophyllata, commonly known as the chlorophyll-deficient octomeria, is a unique plant species characterized by its lack of typical chlorophyll.

This absence of chlorophyll results in a pale or white appearance, distinguishing it from most green plants. Despite its lack of chlorophyll, it has adapted to obtain nutrients through other means, such as symbiotic relationships with fungi. The plant belongs to the family Malvaceae and is found in specific tropical regions.

Its botanical study provides insights into alternative photosynthetic adaptations and plant survival strategies.
