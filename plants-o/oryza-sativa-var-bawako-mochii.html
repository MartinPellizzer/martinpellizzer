Oryza sativa var. bawako-mochii

Oryza sativa var. bawako-mochii, commonly known as the Bawako-mochii rice, is a variety of rice native to Japan.

It is characterized by its short, round grains that are typically white when unprocessed. The plant grows in tropical and subtropical regions, requiring warm temperatures and ample water for optimal growth. Its botanical features include a robust root system and a panicle inflorescence that produces small, spikelet-like flowers.

This variety is valued for its unique texture and flavor, making it a specialty in traditional Japanese cuisine.
