Osmanthus insularis

Osmanthus insularis, commonly known as the island osmanthus, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It is characterized by its fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the late winter to early spring. The leaves are simple, opposite, and elliptical, with a smooth texture and a glossy green color. The plant thrives in humid, subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its aromatic properties.

It is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
