Ornithogalum pendulinum

Ornithogalum pendulinum, commonly known as the hanging ornithogalum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces pendant, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale green. The plant is characterized by its long, slender stems that bear the flowers at the tips, creating a striking visual display. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
