Oeosporangium × malacitense

Oeosporangium × malacitense, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a rare and unique plant resulting from the cross between two distinct orchid species.

It exhibits a combination of morphological traits from its parent species, including a compact growth habit and vibrant, fragrant flowers. The plant's botanical significance lies in its role as a natural hybrid, showcasing genetic diversity within orchid populations. It typically thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring high humidity and well-draining soil.

Due to its limited distribution, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens for conservation and study purposes.
