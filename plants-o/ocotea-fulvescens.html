Ocotea fulvescens

Ocotea fulvescens, commonly known as the yellow-laurel or red sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into dark purple berries. Its wood is highly valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

Ocotea fulvescens plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
