Orchis rivini

Orchis rivini, commonly known as the river orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are arranged along the stem, and its roots are fibrous and often clustered. Orchis rivini thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, riverbanks, and woodland edges.

It plays an important role in pollination and is a valued species in ecological restoration projects.
