Oxyacantha sicula

Oxyacantha sicula, commonly known as the Sicilian hawthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and small, white to pink flowers. The plant produces red or orange berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Oxyacantha sicula is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
