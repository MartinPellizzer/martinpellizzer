Oxanthera aurantium

Oxanthera aurantium, commonly known as the golden oxalid, is a flowering plant native to parts of Australia.

It belongs to the family Oxalidaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves with a waxy surface and produces small, spherical fruits that contain seeds. Oxanthera aurantium thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
