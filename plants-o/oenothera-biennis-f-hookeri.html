Oenothera biennis f. hookeri

Oenothera biennis f. hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's evening primrose, is a variant of the evening primrose native to North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, often found in prairies and open woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
