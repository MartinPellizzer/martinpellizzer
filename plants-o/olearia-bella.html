Olearia bella

Olearia bella, commonly known as the silver chalice, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and subalpine regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Olearia bella is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively low maintenance in suitable climates.
