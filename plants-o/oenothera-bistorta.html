Oenothera bistorta

Oenothera bistorta, commonly known as purple coneflower, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and showy, violet-purple flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant produces numerous small, yellow flowers that develop into capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are lance-shaped, rough to the touch, and often have a reddish tint.

Oenothera bistorta is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along streambanks, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
