Ocimum serpyllifolium

Ocimum serpyllifolium, commonly known as creeping basil, is a low-growing annual herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are typically rounded or oval in shape. The plant produces tiny, light purple to white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its aromatic foliage is often used in culinary applications, though it is less common than other basil varieties.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.
