Ophrys calycadniensis

Ophrys calycadniensis, commonly known as the Balkan bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils in open, sunny habitats. Its flowers are usually pale green or brownish with intricate patterns that enhance the mimicry.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
