Orobus humilis

Orobus humilis, commonly known as the dwarf orobus, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its seed pods are elongated and contain several small, rounded seeds.

Orobus humilis is often found in open grasslands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
