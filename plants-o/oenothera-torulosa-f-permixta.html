Oenothera torulosa f. permixta

Oenothera torulosa f. permixta, commonly known as the hybrid evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun, often found in prairies and open woodlands. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and arranged alternately along the stem.

This variety is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral display.
