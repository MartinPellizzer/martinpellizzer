Ocotea michelsonii

Ocotea michelsonii, commonly known as Michelson's ocotea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves and small, clustered white flowers. Its fruit is a dark purple drupe that contains a single seed.

Ocotea michelsonii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
