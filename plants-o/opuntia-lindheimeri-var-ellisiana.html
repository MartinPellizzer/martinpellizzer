Opuntia lindheimeri var. ellisiana

Opuntia lindheimeri var. ellisiana, commonly known as the Texas prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems that are covered in spines and are often ribbed. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible fruits known as tunas. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to hot, dry climates.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses in Native American cultures.
