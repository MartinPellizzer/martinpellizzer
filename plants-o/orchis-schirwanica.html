Orchis schirwanica

Orchis schirwanica, commonly known as the Schirwan's orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant features a basal rosette of lanceolate leaves and produces showy, violet-blue flowers in late spring. Its inflorescence is typically a raceme with several flowers, each having three sepals and three petals, with the latter being showy and spurred.

Orchis schirwanica is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing specialized root systems for nutrient absorption in poor soils.
