Orchis latifolia var. rochelii

Orchis latifolia var. rochelii, commonly known as the broad-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodlands and meadows. The plant features striking violet to purple flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This orchid is notable for its ecological importance, supporting various pollinators and serving as an indicator of biodiverse habitats.
