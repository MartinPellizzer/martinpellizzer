Orchis incarnata var. foliosa

Orchis incarnata var. foliosa, commonly known as the showy orchis, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a prominent midrib, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
