Ocotea castanea

Ocotea castanea, commonly known as the mountain pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces berries that are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits. Its wood is valued for its durability and resistance to pests, making it useful in construction and crafts.

Ocotea castanea thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.
