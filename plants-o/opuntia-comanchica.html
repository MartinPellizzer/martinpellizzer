Opuntia comanchica

Opuntia comanchica, commonly known as the Comanche prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in spines and glochids, which are small, barbed bristles that can cause irritation. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months, followed by edible fruits that are often consumed by both humans and animals. Opuntia comanchica is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems and having a shallow root system to absorb moisture efficiently.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
