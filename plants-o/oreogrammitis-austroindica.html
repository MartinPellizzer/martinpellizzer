Oreogrammitis austroindica

Oreogrammitis austroindica, commonly known as the Indian wall fern, is a species of fern native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen fronds that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing on rocky surfaces or in forest understories. Its leaves are leathery and have a distinctive, serrated margin.

Oreogrammitis austroindica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in horticulture for its ability to thrive in humid conditions.
