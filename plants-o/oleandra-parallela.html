Oleandra parallela

Oleandra parallela, commonly known as the parallel-leaf oleander, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite, and often lance-shaped leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces showy clusters of pink to red flowers, which bloom in the summer months. Oleandra parallela is a shrub that can grow up to 2 meters tall, with woody stems and a dense, bushy growth habit.

It is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
