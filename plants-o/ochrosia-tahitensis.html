Ochrosia tahitensis

Ochrosia tahitensis, commonly known as the Tahitian starflower, is a flowering plant native to the Society Islands in French Polynesia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant produces small, oblong berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal areas or near freshwater sources.

Ochrosia tahitensis is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its cultural significance in Polynesian traditions.
