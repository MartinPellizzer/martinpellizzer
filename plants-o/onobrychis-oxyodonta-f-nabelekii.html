Onobrychis oxyodonta f. nabelekii

Onobrychis oxyodonta f. nabelekii, commonly known as the Nabelek's sainfoin, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. It has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in poor soil conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in ecological restoration projects.
