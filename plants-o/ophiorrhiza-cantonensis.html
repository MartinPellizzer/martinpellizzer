Ophiorrhiza cantonensis

Ophiorrhiza cantonensis, commonly known as the Canton mallow, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, white flowers clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Ophiorrhiza cantonensis is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its foliage.
