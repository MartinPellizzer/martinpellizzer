Oncidium boissieri

Oncidium boissieri, commonly known as the Boissier's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit and produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant features linear, grass-like leaves and bears showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange. Oncidium boissieri typically blooms in the spring and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
