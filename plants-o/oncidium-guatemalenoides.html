Oncidium guatemalenoides

Oncidium guatemalenoides, commonly known as the Guatemalan orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central America.

It features a sympodial growth habit with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often arranged in a raceme. Its leaves are long, narrow, and arranged in a fan-like pattern.

Oncidium guatemalenoides prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
