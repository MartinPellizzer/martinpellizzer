Oryza sativa var. pseudoguineensis

Oryza sativa var. pseudoguineensis, commonly known as the African rice, is a wild rice species native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, elongated grains that vary in color from white to brown. This variety is adapted to wetland environments and exhibits a high degree of genetic diversity. It possesses traits such as flood tolerance and resistance to certain pests, making it an important resource for breeding programs.

Its botanical features make it a valuable subject for studying rice domestication and genetic improvement.
