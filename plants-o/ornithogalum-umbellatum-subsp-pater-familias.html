Ornithogalum umbellatum subsp. pater-familias

Ornithogalum umbellatum subsp.

pater-familias, commonly known as the family tree lily, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa. It features a rosette of narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color.

The plant produces a tall, unbranched stem bearing an umbel of white or pale yellow flowers. It is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands.

The subspecies is distinguished by its larger, more showy flowers compared to other members of the species.
