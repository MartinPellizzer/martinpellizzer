Orobus vicioides

Orobus vicioides, commonly known as the slender vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant produces slender, cylindrical pods that contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds. Native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it ecologically significant in soil enrichment.
