Origanum × karpathicum

Origanum × karpathicum, commonly known as Karpathian oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and prefers well-drained soil and full sun. Its essential oils, rich in thymol and carvacrol, contribute to its strong medicinal and culinary properties.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a spice in various cuisines.
