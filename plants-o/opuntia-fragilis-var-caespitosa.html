Opuntia fragilis var. caespitosa

Opuntia fragilis var. caespitosa, commonly known as the brittle prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, segmented stems that are often covered in spines and glochids. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers, typically in late spring or early summer. Its fruit, known as a tuna, is a fleshy, edible berry that ripens in the fall.

This variety is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and desert landscapes.
