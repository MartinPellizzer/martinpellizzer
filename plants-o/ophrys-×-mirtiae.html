Ophrys × mirtiae

Ophrys × mirtiae, commonly known as the honey orchid, is a hybrid orchid species that exhibits fascinating botanical characteristics.

It is a cross between Ophrys apifera, the bee orchid, and Ophrys mirtae, another species within the Ophrys genus. This plant is notable for its intricate floral structure, which mimics the appearance of female bees to attract male bees for pollination. The flowers are typically yellowish-brown with intricate patterns that aid in deception.

Ophrys × mirtiae thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.
