Oxyacantha × kyrtostyla

Oxyacantha × kyrtostyla, commonly known as the hybrid rosehip plant, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces distinctive red or orange hips, which are rich in vitamin C and used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, contributing to its ornamental value.

Oxyacantha × kyrtostyla thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.
