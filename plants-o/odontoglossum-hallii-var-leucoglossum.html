Odontoglossum hallii var. leucoglossum

Odontoglossum hallii var. leucoglossum, commonly known as the white-lipped orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking, large, showy flowers with white petals and a yellow lip. The plant typically grows in high-altitude cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its foliage consists of broad, sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

This variety is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its elegant appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
