Ophrys × rasinii

Ophrys × rasinii, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant that results from the cross between Ophrys apifera (the bee orchid) and Ophrys insectifera (the wasp orchid).

It is characterized by its intricate, bee-like flower structure that mimics the appearance and scent of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant is native to southern Europe and thrives in calcareous soils, often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its flowers are typically pale yellow to brownish in color, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Ophrys × rasinii is a rare and protected species, highlighting the importance of conserving its natural habitat.
