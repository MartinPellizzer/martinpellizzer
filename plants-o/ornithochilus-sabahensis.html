Ornithochilus sabahensis

Ornithochilus sabahensis, commonly known as the Sabah bird's-beak orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated flowers that resemble a bird's beak, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and has a delicate, herbaceous appearance. Its roots are fibrous, and it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient absorption.

Ornithochilus sabahensis is a rare and endangered species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts in its native habitat.
