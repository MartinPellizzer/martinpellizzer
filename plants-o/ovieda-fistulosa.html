Ovieda fistulosa

Ovieda fistulosa, commonly known as the redwood fern, is a unique vascular plant characterized by its distinctive, segmented stems and delicate, feather-like fronds.

It is native to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant's vascular system is adapted to efficiently transport water and nutrients through its fibrous, hollow stems. Its reproductive structures, including spore-bearing cones, are typically located at the base of the plant.

Ovieda fistulosa plays an important ecological role in its habitat by contributing to soil stability and providing shelter for small organisms.
