Oxalis tenuicalyculata

Oxalis tenuicalyculata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically heart-shaped with a reddish tinge, and its small, delicate yellow flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. It is often found in forest understories and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Oxalis tenuicalyculata is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
