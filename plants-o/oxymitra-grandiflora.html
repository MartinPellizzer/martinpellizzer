Oxymitra grandiflora

Oxymitra grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered oxymitra, is a striking tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter, typically displaying vibrant colors such as yellow and orange. The plant features a robust, upright stem with dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and aromatic. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Oxymitra grandiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
