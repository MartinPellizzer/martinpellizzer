Orobanche cernua f. cumana

Orobanche cernua f. cumana, commonly known as the common broomrape, is a hemiparasitic plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies on host plants for nutrients.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and small, scale-like leaves. This species typically parasitizes a wide range of host plants, including various grasses and legumes. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to reddish and are arranged in a dense, spike-like inflorescence.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can be a problematic weed in agricultural settings.
