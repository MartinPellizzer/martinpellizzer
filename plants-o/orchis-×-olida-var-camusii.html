Orchis × olida var. camusii

Orchis × olida var. camusii, commonly known as the Camus' twayblade, is a hybrid orchid species native to parts of Europe.

It is a small, herbaceous plant with a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces a single, slender stem bearing a few showy, violet or purple flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors.

Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
