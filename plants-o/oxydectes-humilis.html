Oxydectes humilis

Oxydectes humilis, commonly known as the dwarf milkweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Oxydectes humilis is also known for its milky sap, which is a characteristic feature of the Apocynaceae family.
