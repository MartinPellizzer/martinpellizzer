Ononis striata

Ononis striata, commonly known as the striated bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The leaves are narrow, linear, and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are small, white or pale pink, and arranged in clusters. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

This plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
