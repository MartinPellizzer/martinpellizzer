Oplismenus hirtellus var. microphyllus

Oplismenus hirtellus var. microphyllus, commonly known as the small-leaf woodsorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively and form dense mats. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.
