Ocotea urophylla

Ocotea urophylla, commonly known as the yellow sandarac tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage and bark. The plant typically grows in humid, lowland forests and can reach heights of up to 30 meters. Its leaves are lanceolate, glossy, and emit a fragrant scent when crushed.

The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by dark blue berries, which are an important food source for various wildlife species.
