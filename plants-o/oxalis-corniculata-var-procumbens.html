Oxalis corniculata var. procumbens

Oxalis corniculata var. procumbens, commonly known as the creeping wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and small, yellow, five-petaled flowers. The plant produces capsules that contain numerous tiny seeds, facilitating its spread. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in gardens and woodland areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ornamental and invasive in certain regions.
