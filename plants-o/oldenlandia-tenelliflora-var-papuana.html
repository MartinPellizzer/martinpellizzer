Oldenlandia tenelliflora var. papuana

Oldenlandia tenelliflora var. papuana, commonly known as the Papuan spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Papua and nearby islands.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom throughout the wet season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth or along stream banks.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its delicate appearance.
