Ophrys unilateralis

Ophrys unilateralis, commonly known as the one-sided orchid, is a species of orchid native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which resembles a female bee, aiding in its specialized pollination by male bees. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, often in sunny locations. Its flowers are usually pale green or brown, with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of a bee's body.

Ophrys unilateralis is known for its rare and specific ecological requirements, making it a notable subject in botanical studies.
