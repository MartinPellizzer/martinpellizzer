Odontoglossum cordatum var. sulphureum

Odontoglossum cordatum var. sulphureum, commonly known as the sulphur-tinged orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family native to the Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated leaves that are often marked with yellow or sulfur-yellow hues, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and green, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

This variety is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique coloration and ornamental value.
