Osmunda cinnamomea var. achilleifolia

Osmunda cinnamomea var. achilleifolia, commonly known as the cinnamon fern, is a fern species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender, cinnamon-colored fertile fronds that contrast with the broader, lance-shaped sterile fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce roots that help anchor it in wet soils.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in maintaining ecological balance in its habitat.
