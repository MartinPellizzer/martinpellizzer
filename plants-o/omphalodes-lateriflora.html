Omphalodes lateriflora

Omphalodes lateriflora, commonly known as the side-flowered omphalodes, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are characterized by their lateral arrangement along the stem, giving it its common name.

Omphalodes lateriflora is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
