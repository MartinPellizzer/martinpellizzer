Oncidium praestanoides var. usemannii

Oncidium praestanoides var. usemannii, commonly known as the Usemann's Oncidium, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, racemose inflorescences that bear numerous fragrant, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its pseudobulbs are elongated and cylindrical, supporting the plant's growth and storage of nutrients.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
