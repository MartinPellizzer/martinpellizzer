Oenothera montana

Oenothera montana, commonly known as the mountain evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or open habitats. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and the stems are often hairy as well.

Oenothera montana is known for its resilience in harsh environments and its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
