Otholobium piliferum

Otholobium piliferum, commonly known as the hairy burr bush, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its wiry, branching stems covered in fine, hairy texture. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically yellow or pale green, followed by pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland areas.

Otholobium piliferum is adapted to dry conditions and is sometimes used in local horticulture for its ornamental value.
