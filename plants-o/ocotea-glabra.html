Ocotea glabra

Ocotea glabra, commonly known as the white ocotea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and lance-shaped leaves that are glossy on top and lighter in color underneath. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces drupes that are typically dark blue to black when mature.

Ocotea glabra is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional crafts and as a source of essential oils.
