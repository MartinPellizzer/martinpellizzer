Oxycoccus palustris var. macrophyllus

Oxycoccus palustris var. macrophyllus, commonly known as the large-leafed bog strawberry, is a variant of the bog strawberry plant native to northern regions.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and small, edible berries that are typically red when mature. The plant thrives in wet, acidic soils, often found in bogs and heathlands. It produces white flowers in the summer, which are followed by the fruiting period in the fall.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
