Oldenlandia rupestris

Oldenlandia rupestris, commonly known as the rock spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, often forming dense mats or clusters. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers with five petals, which bloom in clusters along the stems. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

The species is known for its adaptability to harsh environments and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
