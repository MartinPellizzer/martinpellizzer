Olearia rudis var. glabriuscula

Olearia rudis var. glabriuscula, commonly known as the silverweed tree, is a low-growing shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its silvery sheen.

Olearia rudis var. glabriuscula is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal environments.
