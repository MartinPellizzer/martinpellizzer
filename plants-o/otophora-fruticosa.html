Otophora fruticosa

Otophora fruticosa, commonly known as the prickly pear cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to red, typically blooming in spring. Its fruit, known as a tuna, is edible and widely consumed in various cuisines.

Otophora fruticosa also plays an important ecological role by providing food and shelter for desert wildlife.
