Obione pungens

Obione pungens, commonly known as the spiny hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. Obione pungens thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or scrubland habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
