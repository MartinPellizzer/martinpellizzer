Oedera sedifolia

Oedera sedifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as wetlands and forest understories. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually clustered in spikes and bloom in the spring.

Oedera sedifolia is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity in its native habitats.
