Orthoglottis imbricata

Orthoglottis imbricata, commonly known as the wrinkled tongue orchid, is a species of orchid native to the rainforests of Queensland, Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, wrinkled leaves that resemble the texture of a tongue, hence its common name. The plant produces striking, greenish-white flowers with a distinctive, elongated spur. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Orthoglottis imbricata is a terrestrial orchid that is relatively rare and often found growing in dense undergrowth.
