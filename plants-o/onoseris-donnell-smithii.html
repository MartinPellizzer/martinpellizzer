Onoseris donnell-smithii

Onoseris donnell-smithii, commonly known as the Donnell-Smith's goldendisk, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is a herbaceous perennial with a compact, bushy growth habit.
