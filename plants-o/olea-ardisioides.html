Olea ardisioides

Olea ardisioides, commonly known as the Mexican olive, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Mexico and Central America.

It features dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement and have a glossy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters, followed by orange-red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Olea ardisioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

It is also valued for its drought tolerance and adaptability to different climates.
