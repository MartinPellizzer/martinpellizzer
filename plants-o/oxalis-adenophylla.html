Oxalis adenophylla

Oxalis adenophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf wood sorrel, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are covered in a fine, silvery pubescence that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant in gardens.

Oxalis adenophylla is also valued for its ability to tolerate drought conditions.
