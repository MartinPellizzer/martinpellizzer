Ophrys × heracleotica

Ophrys × heracleotica, commonly known as the bee orchid hybrid, is a fascinating plant that exhibits striking floral mimicry.

Its flowers closely resemble female bees, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy by attracting male bees. The plant is a hybrid between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera, resulting in a unique combination of genetic traits. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is found in various habitats across Europe.

The botanical characteristics of this orchid include its tall, erect stem and the presence of specialized structures called labellum and column, which are essential for its reproductive process.
