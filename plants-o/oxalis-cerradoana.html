Oxalis cerradoana

Oxalis cerradoana, commonly known as the cerrado oxalis, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in the Cerrado biome, which is a type of tropical savanna ecosystem. Oxalis cerradoana has adapted to the region's seasonal climate with periods of drought and heavy rainfall.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and conservation efforts in the region.
