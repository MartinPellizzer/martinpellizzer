Oldenlandia capillaris

Oldenlandia capillaris, commonly known as threadleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale blue. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered invasive in certain regions.
