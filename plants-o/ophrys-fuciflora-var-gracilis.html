Ophrys fuciflora var. gracilis

Ophrys fuciflora var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender beard orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its unique, bee-like floral morphology, which includes a prominent, hairy lip that mimics the appearance of a female bee to facilitate pollination by male bees. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, often in shaded, humid environments. Its flowers are usually pale green or brownish, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Due to its specific habitat requirements and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species in many areas.
