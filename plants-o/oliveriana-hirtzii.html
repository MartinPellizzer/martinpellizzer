Oliveriana hirtzii

Oliveriana hirtzii, commonly known as the hairy olive.

It is a small shrub native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, fleshy berries. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly serrated, with a textured surface.

Oliveriana hirtzii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
