Ochna macrantha

Ochna macrantha, commonly known as the Natal yellowwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces striking yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. Ochna macrantha is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for tropical and subtropical landscapes.
