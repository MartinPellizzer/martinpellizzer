Oldenlandia uniflora var. repens

Oldenlandia uniflora var. repens, commonly known as the creeping spiderwort, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, prostrate stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread vegetatively. The plant produces small, showy white or pale blue flowers with five petals, typically blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along stream banks.
