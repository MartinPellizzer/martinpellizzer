Ornithogalum monophyllum

Ornithogalum monophyllum, commonly known as the white bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in the summer months. The plant is characterized by its thick, fleshy roots that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid conditions. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
