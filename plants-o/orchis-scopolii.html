Orchis scopolii

Orchis scopolii, commonly known as the purple orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in dry, calcareous soils. The plant features a tall stem with several leaves and produces showy, violet-blue flowers in late spring. Its roots contain alkaloids, which have been historically used in medicine.

Orchis scopolii is characterized by its unique floral structure and is often found in open woodland and grassland habitats.
