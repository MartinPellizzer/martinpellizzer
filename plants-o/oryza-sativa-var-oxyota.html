Oryza sativa var. oxyota

Oryza sativa var. oxyota, commonly known as the wild rice of East Asia, is a wild variant of the domesticated rice species.

It is characterized by its shorter stature, hairy leaves, and smaller grains compared to cultivated rice. This plant typically grows in wetland habitats and is adapted to flooded conditions. It exhibits a range of genetic diversity that makes it valuable for rice breeding programs.

Its botanical features provide insights into the evolutionary history of rice species.
