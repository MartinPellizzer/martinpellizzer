Ornithogalum kochii

Ornithogalum kochii, commonly known as the dwarf star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers in late spring. The plant is characterized by its succulent stems and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is adapted to arid conditions and is drought-tolerant once established.

Ornithogalum kochii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
