Odontonema aliciae

Odontonema aliciae, commonly known as the Alicia's flame vine, is a tropical flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically dark green in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Odontonema aliciae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and vibrant floral display.
