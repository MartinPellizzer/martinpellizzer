Oxalis macropoda

Oxalis macropoda, commonly known as the giant wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments in forests or gardens.

Oxalis macropoda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
