Ophrys × pietzschii

Ophrys × pietzschii, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is characterized by its intricate, bee-like floral morphology, which includes a pseudostamen that mimics the appearance of a male bee. This plant thrives in calcareous soils and is typically found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its flowers are specialized for orchid bee pollination, showcasing the plant's evolutionary adaptation to its pollinators.

Ophrys × pietzschii is a prime example of the complex relationships between plants and their pollinators in the orchid family.
