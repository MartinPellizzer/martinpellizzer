Ophrys × onckelinxiae

Ophrys × onckelinxiae, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It exhibits a unique floral morphology that mimics the appearance of female wasps, a trait evolved to attract male wasps for pollination. The plant's flowers are characterized by their intricate, bee-like structures, including a prominent lip that resembles a wasp's abdomen. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in shaded woodland areas across Europe.

This hybrid is notable for its ability to produce viable offspring, showcasing the genetic diversity within the Ophrys genus.
