Ostodes prainii

Ostodes prainii, commonly known as the Prain's ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale greenish-white flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are small and clustered, with a unique structure that includes a short spur.

Ostodes prainii is highly sought after by botanists and conservationists due to its rarity and the challenges involved in cultivating it outside its natural habitat.
