Oncidium gayi

Oncidium gayi, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow and green flowers, which are often arranged in a spike-like cluster. The plant has pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in seasonal environments. Its leaves are strap-shaped and arranged in a fan-like pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Oncidium gayi requires bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.
