Onosma caerulescens

Onosma caerulescens, commonly known as the blue onosma, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, such as cliffs and gravelly soils. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Onosma caerulescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
