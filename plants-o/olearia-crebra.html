Olearia crebra

Olearia crebra, commonly known as the denseflower daisy, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often surrounded by narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in coastal environments and is adapted to sandy or gravelly soils.

Olearia crebra is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize coastal dunes.
