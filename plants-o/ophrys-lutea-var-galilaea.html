Ophrys lutea var. galilaea

Ophrys lutea var. galilaea, commonly known as the Galilean bee orchid, is a striking orchid species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee, a trait evolved to attract male bees for pollination. The plant produces yellowish-brown flowers with intricate markings that resemble bee wings and body patterns. It thrives in sunny, well-drained habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands.

This orchid is notable for its specialized pollination mechanism and its role in the ecological diversity of its native range.
