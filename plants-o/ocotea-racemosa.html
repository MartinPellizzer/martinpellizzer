Ocotea racemosa

Ocotea racemosa, commonly known as the fragrant wood or mountain pepper, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers arranged in racemose inflorescences, followed by dark blue, waxy berries. Its wood is highly valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.
