Origanum granatense

Origanum granatense, commonly known as Spanish oregano, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves and small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates. Its essential oils, rich in thymol and carvacrol, contribute to its strong fragrance and medicinal properties.

It is often used in cooking and traditional herbal remedies.
