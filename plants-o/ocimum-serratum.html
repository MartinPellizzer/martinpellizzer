Ocimum serratum

Ocimum serratum, commonly known as the serrated basil, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated edges, giving it its common name. Its small, tubular flowers are typically white or pale purple and are arranged in whorls along the stems.

Ocimum serratum is valued for its fragrant leaves, which are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
