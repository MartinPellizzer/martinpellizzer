Odontostemma quadridentatum

Odontostemma quadridentatum, commonly known as the four-toothed orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique floral structure. The plant features a rhizomatous growth habit and produces small, greenish-white flowers with a distinctively shaped lip. Its leaves are typically ovate and have a smooth surface, while the stems are often covered in fine, hairy structures.

Odontostemma quadridentatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic and ecological significance.
