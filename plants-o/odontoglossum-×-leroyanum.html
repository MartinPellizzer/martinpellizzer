Odontoglossum × leroyanum

Odontoglossum × leroyanum, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Odontoglossum australianum and Odontoglossum lueranum.

It is native to the Andes Mountains of South America, where it thrives in cool, humid environments. The plant produces strikingly colorful, showy flowers with intricate patterns and a distinctive lip structure. Its botanical characteristics include tall, erect pseudobulbs and strap-like leaves.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in horticulture due to its vibrant blooms and unique morphology.
