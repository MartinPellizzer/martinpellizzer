Ophiorrhiza hirsutula

Ophiorrhiza hirsutula, commonly known as the hairy orchid or hairy mangrove, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is typically found in coastal areas, particularly in mangrove ecosystems. The plant is characterized by its small, delicate flowers and hairy stems, which help it adapt to saline and waterlogged environments. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various small organisms.

Ophiorrhiza hirsutula is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
