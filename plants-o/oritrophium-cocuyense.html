Oritrophium cocuyense

Oritrophium cocuyense, commonly known as the Cocuy Mountain goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Colombia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Oritrophium cocuyense is adapted to grow in rocky and well-drained soils, often found in alpine and paramo ecosystems.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the biodiversity in its native habitat.
