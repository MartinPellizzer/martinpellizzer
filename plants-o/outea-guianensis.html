Outea guianensis

Outea guianensis, commonly known as the Brazilian walnut, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the family Proteaceae and is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and heavy, durable wood. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, spiny pods containing edible seeds. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.

Outea guianensis is also valued for its ecological role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
