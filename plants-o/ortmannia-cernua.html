Ortmannia cernua

Ortmannia cernua, commonly known as the nodding orchid, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The flowers are typically white or pale pink and hang downward, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Ortmannia cernua is often found in rich, organic soils and is a valuable component of native plant communities.
