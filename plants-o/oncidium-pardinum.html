Oncidium pardinum

Oncidium pardinum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow and green flowers, which bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant has pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in varying environmental conditions. Its leaves are typically broad and strap-like, adapting to its epiphytic growth habit.

Oncidium pardinum is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
