Onosma longiflora

Onosma longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered onosma, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
