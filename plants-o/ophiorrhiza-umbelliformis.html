Ophiorrhiza umbelliformis

Ophiorrhiza umbelliformis, commonly known as the umbrella leaf or Indian umbrella plant, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite, and often hairy leaves. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in umbellate clusters, giving it its common name.

Ophiorrhiza umbelliformis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
