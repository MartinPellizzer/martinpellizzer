Oenothera bistorta var. reedii

Oenothera bistorta var. reedii, commonly known as Reed's evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States.

It features showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and grows in moist, shady woodlands. Its stems are erect and often branched, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters.

The botanical name reflects its classification within the Onagraceae family, which includes other evening primroses.
