Ocotea botryophylla

Ocotea botryophylla, commonly known as the fragrant ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves, which emit a spicy scent when crushed. It typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests.

Ocotea botryophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
