Origanum loureiroi

Origanum loureiroi, commonly known as Greek oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has narrow, gray-green leaves. Its essential oils, rich in compounds like carvacrol and thymol, contribute to its strong scent and medicinal properties.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for both culinary and ornamental use.
