Otites maeoticus

Otites maeoticus, commonly known as the Crimean bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Black Sea region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, blooming in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and grows in moist, sunny habitats such as meadows and riverbanks. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

Otites maeoticus plays a role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining biodiversity in its native ecosystems.
