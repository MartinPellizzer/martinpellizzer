Ophrys × explanata

Ophrys × explanata, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant that exhibits unique botanical features.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera, two species known for their mimicry of female wasps to attract male pollinators. The plant produces striking flowers with intricate patterns that closely resemble female insects, a trait that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its overall appearance.

This orchid thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas across Europe.
