Opuntia winteriana

Opuntia winteriana, commonly known as the Winter Opuntia, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems that are often covered in small, spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits known as tunas. Opuntia winteriana is adapted to arid environments and has a shallow root system that allows it to absorb moisture efficiently.

Its structure and growth patterns make it well-suited for survival in desert and semi-desert conditions.
