Oncidium hastatum var. hemimelaenum

Oncidium hastatum var. hemimelaenum, commonly known as the Hemimelaena orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, pendant inflorescences that bear clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green to orange flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its pseudobulbs are cylindrical, and the leaves are strap-like, often with a waxy coating.

It requires bright, indirect light and consistent moisture to thrive.
