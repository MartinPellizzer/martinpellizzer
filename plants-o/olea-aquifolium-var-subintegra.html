Olea aquifolium var. subintegra

Olea aquifolium var. subintegra, commonly known as the narrow-leaf olive, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, dark green, glossy leaves that are typically elliptical in shape with a narrow, pointed tip. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers followed by small, fleshy berries that are edible but often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

The leaves are often used in herbal remedies due to their aromatic properties.
