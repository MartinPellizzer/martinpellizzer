Osyris compressa

Osyris compressa, commonly known as the narrowleaf osyris, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. Osyris compressa is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid conditions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
