Oenothera hartwegii var. lavandulifolia

Oenothera hartwegii var. lavandulifolia, commonly known as the lavenderleaf evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that emit a mild lavender scent. The plant produces striking yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to xeriscaped gardens and native plant collections.
