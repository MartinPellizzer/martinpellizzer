Ophrys × giardinae nothosubsp. castri-fusani

Ophrys × giardinae nothosubsp.

castri-fusani, commonly known as the Castri-Fusani bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Mediterranean region. It exhibits typical orchid characteristics, including a sympodial growth habit and a complex floral structure.

The plant produces showy, bee-mimicking flowers that are adapted for pollination by male bees. Its leaves are linear and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This nothosubspecies is notable for its genetic diversity and is often found in rocky or calcareous soils.
