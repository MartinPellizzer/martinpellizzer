Orchis × calliantha

Orchis × calliantha, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a naturally occurring cross between Orchis morio and Orchis mascula.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. The plant features showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its roots are tuberous, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

The hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in botanical gardens and wildflower habitats.
