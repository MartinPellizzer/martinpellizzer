Ononis natrix var. mauritanica

Ononis natrix var. mauritanica, commonly known as the Mauritania sandspurry, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are typically narrow and succulent, helping to conserve water.

The species is part of the Caryophyllaceae family and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.
