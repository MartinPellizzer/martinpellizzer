Ophrys × castri-pontium

Ophrys × castri-pontium, commonly known as the Castri-Pontium orchid, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is characterized by its intricate, bee-mimicking flowers that play a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and thrives in temperate climates, often found in woodland and grassland habitats. Its flowers feature a complex structure with a labellum that mimics the appearance of a female bee, attracting male bees for pollination.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological significance in supporting pollinator populations.
