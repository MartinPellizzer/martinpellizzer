Oenothera californica

Oenothera californica, commonly known as the California evening primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features showy, yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are typically 2 to 4 inches in diameter, with five petals that curl inward as they age.

Oenothera californica thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.
