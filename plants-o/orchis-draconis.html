Orchis draconis

Orchis draconis, commonly known as the dragon's orchid, is a species of orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring. The plant produces a single, spurred orchid flower that is often a deep purple or violet color. It prefers moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.

Orchis draconis is a perennial herb that plays a role in supporting pollinators such as bees and moths.
