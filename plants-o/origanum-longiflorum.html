Origanum longiflorum

Origanum longiflorum, commonly known as sweet marjoram, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, aromatic stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces tiny, oval-shaped leaves that are fragrant and often used in cooking and herbal remedies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, preferring temperate climates.

Its essential oils contain compounds like carvacrol and thymol, which contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.
