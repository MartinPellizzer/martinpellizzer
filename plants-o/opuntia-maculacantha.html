Opuntia maculacantha

Opuntia maculacantha, commonly known as the spotted prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, spineless pads and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has a succulent stem that stores water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. Its fruit, called a tunas, is edible and often used in traditional cuisine.

Opuntia maculacantha plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
