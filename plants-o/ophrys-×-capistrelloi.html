Ophrys × capistrelloi

Ophrys × capistrelloi, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant belonging to the Ophrys genus within the Orchidaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Ophrys apifera (the bee orchid) and Ophrys citrina (the yellow bee orchid), resulting in a plant with unique floral characteristics. The flower resembles a female bee, a mimicry strategy that attracts male bees for pollination. This orchid is typically found in calcareous soils across Europe, particularly in southern France and Italy.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as an example of adaptive evolution and the complexities of hybrid speciation in orchids.
