Oxalis laxa var. minor

Oxalis laxa var. minor, commonly known as the small-leaf wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to South America.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a tendency to form dense mats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and flowers.
