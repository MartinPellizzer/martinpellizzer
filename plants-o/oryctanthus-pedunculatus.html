Oryctanthus pedunculatus

Oryctanthus pedunculatus, commonly known as the red-hot-plate or fireplant, is a South African shrub belonging to the Aizoaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in late summer. Its flowers are typically arranged in clusters and attract various pollinators, including bees and birds.

Oryctanthus pedunculatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry climates.
