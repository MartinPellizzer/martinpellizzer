Oenothera fremontii

Oenothera fremontii, commonly known as the Fremont's evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant produces showy yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths. Its stems are often covered in fine hairs, and the leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
