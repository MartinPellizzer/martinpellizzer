Orthophytum conquistense

Orthophytum conquistense, commonly known as the conquistador cactus, is a species of cactus native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in inflorescences at the stem tips. It has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.

Its structure and adaptations make it well-suited for xerophytic conditions, showcasing its resilience in harsh climates.
