Oxydectes terminalis

Oxydectes terminalis, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green color. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Oxydectes terminalis is often found in sandy or well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and inland dune environments.
