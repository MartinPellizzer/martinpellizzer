Orobanche inconspicua

Orobanche inconspicua, commonly known as the common broomrape, is a rootless parasitic plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on its host for nutrients.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is found in various regions across Europe, Asia, and North Africa. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It parasitizes a wide range of host plants, including agricultural crops such as wheat and barley.

Due to its lack of leaves and its parasitic nature, it plays a significant role in ecosystem dynamics and can impact agricultural productivity.
