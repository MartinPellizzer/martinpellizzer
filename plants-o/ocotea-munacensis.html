Ocotea munacensis

Ocotea munacensis, commonly known as the Guianan ocotea, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are typically elliptical and have a glossy surface. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually white or pale yellow and are clustered in panicles.

The fruit is a small, woody drupe that contains a single seed, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
