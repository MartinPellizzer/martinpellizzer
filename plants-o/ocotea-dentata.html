Ocotea dentata

Ocotea dentata, commonly known as the toothed ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon trees, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves with serrated edges. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible for some bird species.

Ocotea dentata is often found in humid lowland forests and is valued for its timber and medicinal properties.
