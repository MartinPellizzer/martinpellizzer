Otophora cauliflora

Otophora cauliflora, commonly known as the cauliflower orchid, is a unique member of the orchid family with distinctive botanical features.

It is characterized by its compact, caudate (tail-like) growth habit and produces its flowers in a dense, cauliflower-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded, moist environments. Its leaves are fleshy and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its succulent appearance.

The flowers are usually small and clustered, with colors ranging from white to pale pink, adding to its ornamental value.
