Oenothera glazioviana

Oenothera glazioviana, commonly known as the Glaziov's evening primrose, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and has hairy, linear leaves. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, smooth seeds.

Oenothera glazioviana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
