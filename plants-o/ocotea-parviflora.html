Ocotea parviflora

Ocotea parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. It produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round berries.

Ocotea parviflora is often found in humid lowland forests and is valued for its aromatic wood and ecological importance.
