Ornitrophe integrifolia

Ornitrophe integrifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leafed ornithophyte, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, smooth, and elongated leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are often pale blue or violet in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to access groundwater.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapic gardens.
