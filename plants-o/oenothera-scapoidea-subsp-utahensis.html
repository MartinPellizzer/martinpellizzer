Oenothera scapoidea subsp. utahensis

Oenothera scapoidea subsp.

utahensis, commonly known as Utah evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and showy yellow flowers that open in the evening.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, elliptical seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Oenothera scapoidea subsp.

utahensis is adapted to full sun and has a deep root system that helps it survive in dry conditions.
