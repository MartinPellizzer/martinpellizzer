Oenothera cruciata var. stenopetala

Oenothera cruciata var. stenopetala, commonly known as the narrow-petaled evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features showy, yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sandy or well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats like prairies and disturbed areas.

The species is notable for its ability to produce a large number of seeds, contributing to its ecological success.
