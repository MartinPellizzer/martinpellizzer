Oenothera chilensis

Oenothera chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Onagraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant has hairy, ribbed stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Oenothera chilensis is also noted for its medicinal properties, with its seeds containing essential fatty acids.
