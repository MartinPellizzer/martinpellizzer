Ocotea velutina

Ocotea velutina, commonly known as the velvet ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its dark, aromatic wood and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant typically grows in humid lowland forests and can reach heights of up to 30 meters. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces drupes that are an important food source for various wildlife.

The species is valued for its timber and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
