Oncidium cucullatum var. sanguinolentum

Oncidium cucullatum var. sanguinolentum, commonly known as the blood-red orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety red flowers with a contrasting white lip, making it highly ornamental. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. It requires bright, indirect light and well-draining potting media to thrive.

Its flowers bloom in clusters, often appearing in late spring to early summer.
