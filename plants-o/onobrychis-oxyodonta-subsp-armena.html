Onobrychis oxyodonta subsp. armena

Onobrychis oxyodonta subsp.

armena, commonly known as Armenian needlepod, is a perennial legume native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant has trifoliate leaves with three oval-shaped leaflets, and its pods are narrow and cylindrical, containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in ecological restoration projects.
