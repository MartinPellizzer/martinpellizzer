Ornithogalum cernuum

Ornithogalum cernuum, commonly known as the drooping star-of-Bethlem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, linear leaves that form a basal rosette, and it produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that hang downward. The plant is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats, and its flowers bloom in spring.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
