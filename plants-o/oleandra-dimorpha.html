Oleandra dimorpha

Oleandra dimorpha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved oleander, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and showy clusters of pink or red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is evergreen, with a woody stem that can grow into a shrub or small tree. Oleandra dimorpha is adapted to coastal and rocky environments, thriving in full sun and well-drained soil.

It is also noted for its toxicity, as all parts of the plant contain toxic compounds.
