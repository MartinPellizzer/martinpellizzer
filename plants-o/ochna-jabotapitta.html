Ochna jabotapitta

Ochna jabotapitta, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange-red berries containing sticky seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its valuable timber, it is also considered an endangered species in many areas.
