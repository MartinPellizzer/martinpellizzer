Ophrys exaltata subsp. mateolana

Ophrys exaltata subsp.

mateolana, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, bee-like flowers that play a crucial role in its pollination strategy.

The plant typically grows in rocky or forested habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are usually basal, lanceolate, and covered with a dense layer of fine hairs.

The flowers, which are often a striking shade of yellow or brown, emit a scent that mimics the pheromones of female bees, attracting male bees for pollination.
