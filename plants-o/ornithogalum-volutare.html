Ornithogalum volutare

Ornithogalum volutare, commonly known as the twisted bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale green. The plant grows from a bulbous underground stem and thrives in well-drained soil. It is often found in rocky or open woodland habitats.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths.
