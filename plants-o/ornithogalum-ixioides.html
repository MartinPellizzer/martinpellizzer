Ornithogalum ixioides

Ornithogalum ixioides, commonly known as the star of Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a short, underground rhizome and produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers during the spring season. The plant has lance-shaped, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Ornithogalum ixioides is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
