Ocimum coloratum

Ocimum coloratum, commonly known as purple basil, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple-lavender foliage and small, white to light purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs such as mint and oregano. Its leaves are often used in culinary applications for their unique flavor and fragrance.

Ocimum coloratum thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun exposure.
