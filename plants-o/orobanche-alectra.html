Orobanche alectra

Orobanche alectra, commonly known as the common broomrape, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is found in various regions across Europe, Asia, and North Africa. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is often found parasitizing plants such as thistles, sedges, and other herbaceous species.

Due to its lack of roots and photosynthetic ability, it plays a unique role in ecological systems as a parasite.
