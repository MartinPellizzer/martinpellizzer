Orchis coriophora var. sancta

Orchis coriophora var. sancta, commonly known as the Holy Orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its small, pale green to yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces a single spike of flowers.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a protected species in many regions.
