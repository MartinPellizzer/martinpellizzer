Ocimum simulans

Ocimum simulans, commonly known as wild basil, is a species of flowering plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Middle East, and it thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, pale blue or purple flowers. It is often found growing in moist, shady areas such as forest floors or along stream banks.

Ocimum simulans is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, similar to other members of the basil genus.
