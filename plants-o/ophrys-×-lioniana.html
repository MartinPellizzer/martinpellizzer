Ophrys × lioniana

Ophrys × lioniana, commonly known as the lion's orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the genus Ophrys, which is known for its intricate floral structures that mimic female insects to attract male pollinators. The plant produces showy, bee-like flowers with a distinctive coloration and pattern that enhance its mimicry. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

The botanical features of Ophrys × lioniana are characterized by its rhizomatous growth, lanceolate leaves, and specialized reproductive structures adapted for its unique pollination strategy.
