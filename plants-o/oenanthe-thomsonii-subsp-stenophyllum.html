Oenanthe thomsonii subsp. stenophyllum

Oenanthe thomsonii subsp.

stenophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved water dropwort, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils along stream banks and wet meadows.

The plant features slender, upright stems and small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are white or pale green, arranged in dense, umbel-like clusters, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is characterized by its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
