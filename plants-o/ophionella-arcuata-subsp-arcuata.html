Ophionella arcuata subsp. arcuata

Ophionella arcuata subsp.

arcuata, commonly known as the narrowleaf false nettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of North America. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive glandular margin. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

The species is known for its hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its characteristic appearance.
