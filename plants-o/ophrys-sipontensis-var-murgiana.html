Ophrys sipontensis var. murgiana

Ophrys sipontensis var. murgiana, commonly known as the Murgian orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the Apulia region of southern Italy.

It is characterized by its unique floral morphology, featuring a lip that mimics the appearance of a female bee, a trait that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and dry grasslands. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brownish in color, with intricate patterns that aid in attracting specific pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts in preserving its natural environment.
