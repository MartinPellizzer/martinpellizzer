Orchis × genevensis

Orchis × genevensis, commonly known as the Geneva hybrid orchid, is a natural hybrid between Orchis moria and Orchis mascula.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers with a distinctive spur at the base of each bloom. It typically blooms in late spring to early summer, often in open woodlands or meadows.

This orchid is relatively rare and is found primarily in parts of Europe, including France and Switzerland.
