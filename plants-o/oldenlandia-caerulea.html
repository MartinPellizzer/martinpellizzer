Oldenlandia caerulea

Oldenlandia caerulea, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its most distinctive feature is its clusters of small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
