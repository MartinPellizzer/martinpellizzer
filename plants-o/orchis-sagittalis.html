Orchis sagittalis

Orchis sagittalis, commonly known as the arrow-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats. The plant features a basal rosette of lanceolate, arrow-shaped leaves, from which a flowering stem emerges. Its flowers are usually pale purple or greenish-white, with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color.

Orchis sagittalis is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its subtle beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
