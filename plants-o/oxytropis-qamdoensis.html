Oxytropis qamdoensis

Oxytropis qamdoensis, commonly known as the Qamdo milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Qinghai-Tibet Plateau.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically yellow or white in color. It thrives in high-altitude environments and is adapted to the cold, arid conditions of its native region.

Oxytropis qamdoensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its ecosystem.
