Opuntia ovata

Opuntia ovata, commonly known as the Easter cactus, is a species of cactus native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems that are often ribbed and covered in small spines. The plant produces showy, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from red to pink and white. It is a popular ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.

Opuntia ovata is typically grown in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
