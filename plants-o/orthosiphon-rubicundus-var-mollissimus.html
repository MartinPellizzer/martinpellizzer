Orthosiphon rubicundus var. mollissimus

Orthosiphon rubicundus var. mollissimus, commonly known as the velvet lemongrass, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
