Orobus quadrangulus

Orobus quadrangulus, commonly known as the four-angled orchid, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its unique, quadrangular (four-sided) stems and striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and along stream banks. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with appropriate moisture and light conditions.
