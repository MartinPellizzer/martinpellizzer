Oncidium sanguinolentum

Oncidium sanguinolentum, commonly known as the blood orchid, is a tropical orchid species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, blood-red flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a contrasting white or yellow throat. The plant has strap-like leaves that are usually dark green and arranged in a fan shape. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Oncidium sanguinolentum is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in greenhouse settings.
