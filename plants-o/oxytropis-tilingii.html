Oxytropis tilingii

Oxytropis tilingii, commonly known as the Tilingii milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale yellow to pink. The plant has small, compound leaves with stipules, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, dry environments.

Oxytropis tilingii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
