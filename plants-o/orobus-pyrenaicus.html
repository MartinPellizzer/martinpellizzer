Orobus pyrenaicus

Orobus pyrenaicus, commonly known as the Pyrenean bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pyrenees mountain range.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. It is often found in open meadows and slopes at high altitudes.

Orobus pyrenaicus plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by supporting pollinators and contributing to biodiversity.
