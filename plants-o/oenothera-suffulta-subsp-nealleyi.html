Oenothera suffulta subsp. nealleyi

Oenothera suffulta subsp.

nealleyi, commonly known as the Nealley's evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The species is noted for its ecological importance in supporting local insect populations and its potential medicinal properties.
