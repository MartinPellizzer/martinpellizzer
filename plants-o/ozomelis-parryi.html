Ozomelis parryi

Ozomelis parryi, commonly known as the Parry's milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family, which includes other milkweeds known for their distinctive floral structures. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its milky sap, like that of other milkweeds, contains latex compounds that serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores.

Ozomelis parryi is typically found in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
