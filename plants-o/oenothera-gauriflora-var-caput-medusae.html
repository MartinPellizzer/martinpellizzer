Oenothera gauriflora var. caput-medusae

Oenothera gauriflora var. caput-medusae, commonly known as the "Medusahead" or "Cape Cod evening primrose," is a herbaceous perennial native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant features a branched stem with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically arranged in a dense, conical cluster at the tip of the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This species is adapted to sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy and resilient plant.
