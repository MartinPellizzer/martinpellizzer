Olearia siemssenii

Olearia siemssenii, commonly known as the Siam lily, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth texture, while its stems are often hairy.

Olearia siemssenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
