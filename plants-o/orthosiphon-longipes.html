Orthosiphon longipes

Orthosiphon longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed horse mint, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are typically pale purple to white, arranged in whorls along the stem.

Orthosiphon longipes is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices.
