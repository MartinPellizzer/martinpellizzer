Orchis spitzelii subsp. spitzelii

Orchis spitzelii subsp.

spitzelii, commonly known as the Spitzel's orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or grassland habitats.

The plant features a tall, slender stem that bears several showy, violet-blue flowers arranged in a loose spike. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a hairy texture.

The species is noted for its ecological significance, supporting specific pollinators and contributing to biodiversity in its native range.
