Oenothera polymorpha var. nocturna

Oenothera polymorpha var. nocturna, commonly known as the evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening and close by morning. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while its stems are often hairy as well.

The species is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
