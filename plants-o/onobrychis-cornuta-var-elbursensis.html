Onobrychis cornuta var. elbursensis

Onobrychis cornuta var. elbursensis, commonly known as the Elburs clover, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats. It plays a role in soil fixation due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a notable species in its native flora.
