Ocotea indica

Ocotea indica, commonly known as the Indian ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in white or greenish-white colors. Its fruit is a small, dark blue drupe that contains a single seed.

Ocotea indica is often valued for its essential oils and has traditional uses in medicine and perfumery.
