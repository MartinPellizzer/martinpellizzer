Ochna grandis

Ochna grandis, commonly known as the African wild coffee, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries containing a single seed. Ochna grandis is adapted to a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions.
