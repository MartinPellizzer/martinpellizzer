Olearia virgata var. ramuliflora

Olearia virgata var. ramuliflora, commonly known as the rambling daisy, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, white, daisy-like flowers. The plant produces showy, fluffy inflorescences that bloom in spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its frost resistance and ability to attract pollinators.
