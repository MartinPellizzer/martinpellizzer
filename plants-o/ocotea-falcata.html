Ocotea falcata

Ocotea falcata, commonly known as the Brazilian camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its lanceolate, glossy leaves and aromatic bark. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by dark blue berries containing seeds. Its leaves and bark contain essential oils, which have been used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and analgesic properties.

Ocotea falcata is often found in moist, shaded areas of tropical forests.
