Osmia tozziifolia

Osmia tozziifolia, commonly known as the tozziifolia mason bee, is a solitary bee species native to parts of Europe and North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender body and distinctive black and yellow coloration, which serves as a warning signal to potential predators. The plant it pollinates includes a variety of flowering plants, contributing to its ecological importance in pollination. It constructs its nest in pre-existing cavities, such as hollow stems or beetle burrows.

Osmia tozziifolia plays a vital role in maintaining the biodiversity of its native habitats through its pollination activities.
