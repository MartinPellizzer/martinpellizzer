Oporinia autumnalis var. coronopifolia

Oporinia autumnalis var. coronopifolia, commonly known as the autumnal oporinia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. The plant features slender stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in umbels, and usually have a pale yellow to greenish hue.

The species is noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for specific insect species.
