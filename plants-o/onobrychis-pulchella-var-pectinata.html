Onobrychis pulchella var. pectinata

Onobrychis pulchella var. pectinata, commonly known as the silky bursaria, is a perennial legume native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and distinctive, spiny pods that contain small, rounded seeds. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas. It produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
