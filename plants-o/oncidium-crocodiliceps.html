Oncidium crocodiliceps

Oncidium crocodiliceps, commonly known as the crocodile-head orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated flower spikes that bear multiple fragrant, yellow-green to orange flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Oncidium crocodiliceps is valued for its ornamental appeal and is popular in horticulture for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
