Onobrychis hypargyrea var. longispicata

Onobrychis hypargyrea var. longispicata, commonly known as the long-spined burclover, is a perennial legume native to the southwestern United States.

It features compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces distinctive, spiny pods that contain seeds. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in drought conditions.

Its flowers are typically yellow or pale green, and it is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.
