Ocotea sacculifera

Ocotea sacculifera, commonly known as the fragrant ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the summer. Its fruits are small, round, and contain a single seed, ripening to a dark purple color.

Ocotea sacculifera is valued for its aromatic wood and is often found in humid, lowland forests.
