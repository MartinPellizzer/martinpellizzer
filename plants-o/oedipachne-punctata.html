Oedipachne punctata

Oedipachne punctata, commonly known as the punctate grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, linear leaves with a distinctively rough texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.

Oedipachne punctata is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient component of native vegetation.
