Ocotea myristicifolia

Ocotea myristicifolia, commonly known as the nutmeg-laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers and develops dark brown, woody fruits containing aromatic seeds. Its essential oils are often used in perfumery and traditional medicine.

Ocotea myristicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in tropical forests.
