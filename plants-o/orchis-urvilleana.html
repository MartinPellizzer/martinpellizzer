Orchis urvilleana

Orchis urvilleana, commonly known as the violet orchid, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinctive midrib.

Orchis urvilleana is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
