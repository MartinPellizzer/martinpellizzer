Orchis bractealis

Orchis bractealis, commonly known as the bracted orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive bract-like structures that enclose the flower spike. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with a short spur at the base of each bloom.

Orchis bractealis is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
