Ochna vulgaris

Ochna vulgaris, commonly known as the African star jelly or yellow star jelly, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble stars, which bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Ochna vulgaris is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
