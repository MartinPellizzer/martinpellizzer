Ophrys barbarae

Ophrys barbarae, commonly known as the Balkan bee orchid, is a striking orchid species native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It is characterized by its unique flower structure, which closely resembles a female bee, a mimicry strategy used to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested habitats and prefers well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brown in color, with intricate patterns that enhance its pollinator mimicry.

Ophrys barbarae is a perennial herb that produces a single spike of flowers, often blooming in late spring to early summer.
