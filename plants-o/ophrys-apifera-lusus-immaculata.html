Ophrys apifera lusus immaculata

Ophrys apifera lusus immaculata, commonly known as the bee orchid, is a species of orchid native to Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its unique flower structure, which resembles a bee, aiding in pollination through mimicry. The plant typically grows in sandy or calcareous soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with brown markings, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

This orchid is notable for its specialized relationship with specific pollinators, making it an important subject in ecological studies.
