Opuntia chuquisacana

Opuntia chuquisacana, commonly known as the Chuquisaca prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Bolivia.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its flattened, fleshy stems that are segmented and covered in spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers, which are typically borne on the areoles of the stems. Its fruit, known as a tuna, is edible and has a mild, sweet flavor.

Opuntia chuquisacana is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in regions with low rainfall.
