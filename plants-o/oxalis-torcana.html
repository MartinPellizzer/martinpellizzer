Oxalis torcana

Oxalis torcana, commonly known as the twisted wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, each leaflet resembling a heart shape with a pale green margin. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or containers.

Oxalis torcana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate dry conditions.
