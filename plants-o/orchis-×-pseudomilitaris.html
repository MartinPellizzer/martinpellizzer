Orchis × pseudomilitaris

Orchis × pseudomilitaris, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a naturally occurring cross between Orchis militaris and Orchis morio.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family, characterized by its showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland habitats across Europe. It features a rhizomatous root system and produces erect, branched stems with lanceolate leaves.

The flowers are arranged in dense spikes and are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.
