Opuntia chichensis

Opuntia chichensis, commonly known as the Mexican prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems that are covered in spines and glochids, which are small, barbed bristles. The plant produces bright yellow or orange flowers, typically during the summer months. Its fruit, known as a tunas, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

Opuntia chichensis is well adapted to arid environments and is often used in traditional medicine and cuisine.
