Oxalis laciniata

Oxalis laciniata, commonly known as the leafy wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a delicate, fern-like appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in the spring. It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.

Oxalis laciniata is valued for its ornamental foliage and is often used in native plant landscaping.
