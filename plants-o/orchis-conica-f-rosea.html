Orchis conica f. rosea

Orchis conica f. rosea, commonly known as the pink-headed orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to parts of Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive pinkish-purple flowers with a white lip. The plant typically grows in woodland or grassland habitats, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a unique ornamental value to its natural environment.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often protected in conservation efforts.
