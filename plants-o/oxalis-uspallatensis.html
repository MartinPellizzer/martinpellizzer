Oxalis uspallatensis

Oxalis uspallatensis, commonly known as the Uspallata wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It is a perennial herb with heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family, characterized by its calcium oxalate crystals in the leaves.

Oxalis uspallatensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
