Ocotea sericiflora

Ocotea sericiflora, commonly known as the silky ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers followed by elliptical, dark blue berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland tropical forests.

Its wood is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in local crafts and traditional medicine.
