Olearia mucronata

Olearia mucronata, commonly known as the New Zealand flax or silver flax, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray leaves and small, daisy-like white flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy or rocky soils. It is often found in coastal scrub and is valued for its ornamental qualities.

Olearia mucronata plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting various insect and bird species.
