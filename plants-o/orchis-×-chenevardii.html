Orchis × chenevardii

Orchis × chenevardii, commonly known as the Chenevard's orchid, is a natural hybrid between Orchis moria and Orchis purpurea.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, particularly found in moist meadows and woodland edges. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color. It has a fibrous root system and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine down.

Orchis × chenevardii is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by supporting pollinators.
