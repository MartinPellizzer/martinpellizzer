Oxyria digyna subsp. elatior

Oxyria digyna subsp.

elatior, commonly known as the greater water parsley, is a herbaceous plant native to northern regions of North America. It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white, silky hairs.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are borne on slender, erect stalks. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils near streams, lakes, and wetlands.

This subspecies is notable for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
