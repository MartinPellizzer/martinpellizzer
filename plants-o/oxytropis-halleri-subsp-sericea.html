Oxytropis halleri subsp. sericea

Oxytropis halleri subsp.

sericea, commonly known as silverleaf locoweed, is a perennial legume native to the arid and semi-arid regions of western North America. It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, sagebrush steppe, and rocky slopes.

Due to its toxicity to livestock, it is considered a noxious weed in some areas.
