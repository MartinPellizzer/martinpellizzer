Oxytropis nigrescens subsp. bryophila

Oxytropis nigrescens subsp.

bryophila, commonly known as the purple globemallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and clustered, pea-like flowers that range from pink to purple.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as grasslands and slopes. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in arid environments.
