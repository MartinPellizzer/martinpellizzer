Ornithochilus hillii

Ornithochilus hillii, commonly known as the Hill's bird's beak orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers that resemble a bird's beak. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest understories, where it thrives in well-drained, humus-rich soil. Its flowers bloom during the rainy season, adding a delicate touch to the forest flora.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
