Oxytropis integripetala

Oxytropis integripetala, commonly known as the smoothleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes. The plant has compound leaves with smooth, entire margins and produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of purple or pink. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Oxytropis integripetala is often found in disturbed or arid environments and is adapted to thrive in low-nutrient soils.
