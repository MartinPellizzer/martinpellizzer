Oenothera tracyi

Oenothera tracyi, commonly known as Tracy's evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening and close by dawn. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Oenothera tracyi is valued for its ornamental beauty and its role in supporting local pollinators.
