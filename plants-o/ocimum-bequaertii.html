Ocimum bequaertii

Ocimum bequaertii, commonly known as the Ethiopian basil, is a herbaceous plant native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs such as mint and lavender. The plant is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of purple or white flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown for its aromatic leaves used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

Its essential oils contain compounds like limonene and eugenol, contributing to its distinctive fragrance and medicinal properties.
