Ocotea licanioides

Ocotea licanioides, commonly known as the licanioides ocotea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in white or pale yellow. Its bark and leaves have been traditionally used in local medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.

Ocotea licanioides typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, often found in humid, lowland forests.
