Oxalis geminata

Oxalis geminata, commonly known as the twinleaf wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three heart-shaped leaflets, and its clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its botanical features include hairy stems and a fibrous root system.

Oxalis geminata is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
