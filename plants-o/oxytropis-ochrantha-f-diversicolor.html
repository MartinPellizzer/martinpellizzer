Oxytropis ochrantha f. diversicolor

Oxytropis ochrantha f. diversicolor, commonly known as the multicolored locoweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, often hairy foliage and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as sagebrush steppe and grasslands. Its flowers range in color from white to pink and purple, and the plant produces small, indehiscent pods containing seeds.

Oxytropis ochrantha f. diversicolor is adapted to low-nutrient soils and can thrive in harsh, dry conditions.
