Oldenlandia stricta var. shuteri

Oldenlandia stricta var. shuteri, commonly known as the creeping spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant features small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to form dense mats, making it both a useful ground cover and a potential weed in certain habitats.
