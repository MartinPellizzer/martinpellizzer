Orobanche grossheimii

Orobanche grossheimii, commonly known as the giant broomrape, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, often hairy stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is found in regions with temperate climates, such as parts of Europe and western Asia. Its life cycle involves germinating in response to chemical signals released by host plants, after which it attaches to the roots of its host through specialized structures called haustoria.

Due to its parasitic nature and lack of leaves, it plays a unique role in certain ecosystems as both a specialist parasite and a potential indicator of soil disturbance.
