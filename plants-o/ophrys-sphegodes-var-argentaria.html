Ophrys sphegodes var. argentaria

Ophrys sphegodes var. argentaria, commonly known as the silver bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species found primarily in the United Kingdom.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a dead bee, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant produces a single, showy flower with a hairy, bee-like labellum that attracts male bees through visual and chemical cues. It thrives in calcareous grasslands and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species.
