Ophrys × sanctae-sofiae

Ophrys × sanctae-sofiae, commonly known as the Holy Sofia orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Balkans.

It is characterized by its intricate floral structure, which closely resembles the face of a human, a trait known as floral mimicry. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its flowers are usually yellow or green with a distinctive beard-like structure at the base.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
