Oxytropis cinerascens

Oxytropis cinerascens, commonly known as gray locoweed, is a perennial legume native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its grayish-green foliage and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert environments. It has a deep root system that allows it to survive in arid conditions.

Oxytropis cinerascens is also known for its toxic properties, containing compounds that can be harmful to livestock.
