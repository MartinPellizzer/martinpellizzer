Ophrys × chiesesica

Ophrys × chiesesica, commonly known as the Chiesesica bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Ophrys genus, which is known for its mimicry of female insects to attract male pollinators. The plant produces intricate flowers that closely resemble female bees, a strategy that enhances its pollination success. It typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils.

The botanical features of Ophrys × chiesesica include its tall, erect stems and showy, bee-like floral structures.
