Oxalis acetosella var. violacea

Oxalis acetosella var. violacea, commonly known as violet wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive violet or purple coloration, often with a contrasting lighter green margin. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically yellow with five petals, blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Oxalis acetosella var. violacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
