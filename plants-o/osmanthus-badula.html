Osmanthus badula

Osmanthus badula, commonly known as the Malabar osmanthus, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of southern India and Sri Lanka.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pale yellow to golden flowers that bloom in clusters during the winter season. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are opposite in arrangement and have a glossy surface. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, often exuding a mild aromatic scent.

Osmanthus badula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
