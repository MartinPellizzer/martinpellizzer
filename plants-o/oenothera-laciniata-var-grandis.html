Oenothera laciniata var. grandis

Oenothera laciniata var. grandis, commonly known as the grand lollipop plant, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental value make it a notable species in botanical gardens and native plant collections.
