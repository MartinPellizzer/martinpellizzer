Ochnella madagascariensis

Ochnella madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar ochna, is a small evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by berry-like fruits that are red when mature.

It is often found in humid, lowland forests and is noted for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and flowers.
