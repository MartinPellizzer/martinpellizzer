Oxalis rhombeo-ovata var. violascens

Oxalis rhombeo-ovata var. violascens, commonly known as the violet-leaf wood sorrel, is a flowering plant characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, violet-pink flowers.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is native to parts of Africa, particularly South Africa. The plant features prominently veined leaves and produces showy, five-petaled flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it both visually appealing and ecologically valuable in its native habitats.
