Ogiera ruderalis

Ogiera ruderalis, commonly known as the common hogweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, umbrella-like leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in diameter. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers arranged in compound umbels, which bloom in late summer. Ogiera ruderalis is a hardy species that thrives in disturbed soils, often found along roadsides and in waste areas.

It has a long growing season and can become invasive in certain environments.
