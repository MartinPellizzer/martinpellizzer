Osmites lancea

Osmites lancea, commonly known as the lanceleaf osmite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the spring. Osmites lancea is adapted to well-drained soils and can thrive in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical features make it a resilient species suited to harsh desert conditions.
