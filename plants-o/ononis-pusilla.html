Ononis pusilla

Ononis pusilla, commonly known as lesser whitlow grass, is a small annual herb belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale yellow to white in color. The plant produces trifoliate leaves and is often found in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides or in dry, sandy areas. It has a shallow root system and is adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor environments.

Ononis pusilla is an important plant for soil stabilization and supports a variety of pollinators.
