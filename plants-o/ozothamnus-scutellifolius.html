Ozothamnus scutellifolius

Ozothamnus scutellifolius, commonly known as the shield-leaf golden bush, is a perennial shrub native to southern Australia.

It features aromatic, grey-green foliage with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters, typically blooming in spring. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is drought-tolerant once established.

Ozothamnus scutellifolius is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
