Osbeckia incana

Osbeckia incana, commonly known as the hoary willow-herb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage and slender, branching stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. Osbeckia incana thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and disturbed areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
