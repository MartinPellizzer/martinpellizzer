Osbeckia stellata var. hispidissima

Osbeckia stellata var. hispidissima, commonly known as the hairy starweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its hairy stems and small, star-shaped yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are alternate and oval, often covered with fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its adaptability to arid conditions.
