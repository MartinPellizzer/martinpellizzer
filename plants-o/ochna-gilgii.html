Ochna gilgii

Ochna gilgii, commonly known as the Natal ochna, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in terminal clusters, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. Ochna gilgii is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in rocky soils and woodland areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
