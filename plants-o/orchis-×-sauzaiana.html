Orchis × sauzaiana

Orchis × sauzaiana, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a natural hybrid between Orchis mascula and Orchis morio.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, spurred orchid flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing violet to purple flowers with a distinctive lip. It is often found in meadows and woodland edges across Europe, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
