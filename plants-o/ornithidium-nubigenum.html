Ornithidium nubigenum

Ornithidium nubigenum, commonly known as the cloud fern, is a rare and enigmatic species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that resemble the structure of bird feathers, hence its scientific name. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests where humidity levels are consistently high. Its reproductive structures are unique, featuring specialized spore-producing organs that are adapted to its specific ecological niche.

Due to its limited distribution and fragile habitat, Ornithidium nubigenum is considered a vulnerable species in conservation efforts.
