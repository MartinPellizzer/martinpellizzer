Oreopanax septemnervius

Oreopanax septemnervius, commonly known as the seven-veined oreopanax, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. The plant features a robust root system and can grow up to several meters in height under favorable conditions. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, often clustered in inflorescences, and it produces berries that are an important food source for local wildlife.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
