Orchis sambucina var. lutea

Orchis sambucina var. lutea, commonly known as the yellow marsh orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, flowering stems that bear clusters of yellowish-green to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in wet meadows, marshes, and other moist habitats across Europe. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while the flowers are spurred at the base.

The orchid is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils, often forming part of the diverse flora in wetland ecosystems.
