Ophrys × maremmae nothosubsp. tardans

Ophrys × maremmae nothosubsp.

tardans, commonly known as the Maremma orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the genus Ophrys, which is known for its mimicry of female insects to attract male pollinators.

This particular subspecies is characterized by its intricate flower structure, featuring a prominent lip that resembles an insect's body. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, thriving in sunny environments with well-drained soil.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a hybrid species, showcasing the genetic diversity and adaptive traits found in orchid populations.
