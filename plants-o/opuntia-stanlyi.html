Opuntia stanlyi

Opuntia stanlyi, commonly known as the Stanley cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems, which are typically green and covered in spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Opuntia stanlyi is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, succulent pads.

It is also known for its ability to thrive in rocky soils and full sun exposure.
