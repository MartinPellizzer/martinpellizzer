Oreopanax lechleri

Oreopanax lechleri, commonly known as Sango or Sangre de Gringo, is a flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, palm-like leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant is often found in humid, shaded environments within the understory of dense tropical forests. Its latex contains compounds with potential medicinal properties, making it a subject of interest in ethnobotanical research.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological role in its native habitat.
