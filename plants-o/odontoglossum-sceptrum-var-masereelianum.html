Odontoglossum sceptrum var. masereelianum

Odontoglossum sceptrum var. masereelianum, commonly known as the Masereelian orchid, is a tropical orchid native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated flowers that are typically purple or violet in color, with a distinctive central ridge. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, typical of many orchid species. Its leaves are strap-shaped and often covered in a fine, fuzzy coating.

This variety is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements in a suitable growing environment.
