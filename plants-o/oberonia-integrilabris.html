Oberonia integrilabris

Oberonia integrilabris, commonly known as the broad-leafed orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its elongated, often hairy stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces intricate inflorescences with fragrant, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
