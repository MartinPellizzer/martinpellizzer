Oreocharis purpurata

Oreocharis purpurata, commonly known as the purple orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking violet and white petals. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and thrives in cool, humid climates. Its flowers are small but numerous, often arranged in a spike-like cluster.

Oreocharis purpurata is appreciated for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
