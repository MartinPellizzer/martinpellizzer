Onobrychis calabrica

Onobrychis calabrica, commonly known as the Sicilian burr medic, is a perennial leguminous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features trifoliate leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant is characterized by its spiny, woody pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Onobrychis calabrica is valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
