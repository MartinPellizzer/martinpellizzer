Oxygonum altissimum

Oxygonum altissimum, commonly known as the giant water hyacinth, is a floating aquatic plant native to South America.

It has broad, waxy leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length, providing a dense canopy on the water surface. The plant produces small, clustered white or purple flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its rapid growth rate allows it to form thick mats on the water, which can impact water flow and oxygen levels.

Oxygonum altissimum is often found in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats.
