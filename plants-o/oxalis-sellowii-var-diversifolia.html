Oxalis sellowii var. diversifolia

Oxalis sellowii var. diversifolia, commonly known as the variable wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are often heart-shaped and have a distinctive veining pattern. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Oxalis sellowii var. diversifolia is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and delicate blossoms.
