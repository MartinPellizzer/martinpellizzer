Orchis militaris var. tenuifrons

Orchis militaris var. tenuifrons, commonly known as the slender-fringed soldier's orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers with distinctive fringed petals. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous grasslands and woodland edges, thriving in temperate climates. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and it is known for its delicate, fragrant blooms.

This variety is relatively rare and is of interest to botanists and conservationists due to its specific habitat requirements.
