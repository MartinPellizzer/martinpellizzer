Origanum laxiflorum

Origanum laxiflorum, commonly known as looseflower oregano, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and prefers full sun. Its foliage is used in culinary and medicinal applications, similar to other members of the Lamiaceae family.

Origanum laxiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
