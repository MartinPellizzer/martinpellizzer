Oenothera stricta subsp. altissima

Oenothera stricta subsp.

altissima, commonly known as the tall evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States. It is characterized by its erect, branching stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

The plant produces showy yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

The species is adapted to a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.
