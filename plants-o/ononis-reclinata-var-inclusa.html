Ononis reclinata var. inclusa

Ononis reclinata var. inclusa, commonly known as the narrowleaf sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branched stems and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
