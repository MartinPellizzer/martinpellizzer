Onobrychis ferganica

Onobrychis ferganica, commonly known as Fergana clover, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It has a deep root system that helps it withstand arid conditions.

Onobrychis ferganica is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment and livestock forage.
