Olax gossweileri

Olax gossweileri, commonly known as the African olive, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Olacaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Olax gossweileri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
