Ocotea laevigata

Ocotea laevigata, commonly known as the smooth-leaved ocotea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America, particularly in countries like Costa Rica, Panama, and Colombia. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its wood is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional crafts and as a source of essential oils.

Ocotea laevigata plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
