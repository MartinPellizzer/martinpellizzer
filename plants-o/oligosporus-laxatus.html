Oligosporus laxatus

Oligosporus laxatus, commonly known as the slender spore grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, commonly known as the grass family, and is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks. Its reproductive structures consist of spikelets that produce minute, wind-dispersed seeds, contributing to its widespread distribution.

Oligosporus laxatus is generally non-invasive but can serve as a valuable component of ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and ability to stabilize soil.
