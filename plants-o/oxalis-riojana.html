Oxalis riojana

Oxalis riojana, commonly known as the Rioja wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Uruguay.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces distinctive seed pods that split open to release its seeds. Oxalis riojana thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and flowers.
