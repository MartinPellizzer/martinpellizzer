Oleandra neriiformis var. pilosa

Oleandra neriiformis var. pilosa, commonly known as the fuzzy-leaved oleander, is a tropical shrub native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, woody stems and fuzzy, gray-green leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of showy, pink or red flowers during the summer months. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
