Oxalis tepicensis

Oxalis tepicensis, commonly known as the Mexican wood sorrel, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Oxalidaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Oxalis tepicensis is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.

It is also valued for its use in traditional medicine in some regions.
