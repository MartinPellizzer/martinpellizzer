Oxalis cruentata

Oxalis cruentata, commonly known as the crimson wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, bright red flowers. The plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage is edible, though it contains oxalic acid, which can be harmful in large quantities.

Oxalis cruentata is popular as a ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
