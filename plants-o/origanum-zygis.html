Origanum zygis

Origanum zygis, commonly known as Greek oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and aromatic, small, pale green to reddish leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its essential oils, rich in thymol and carvacrol, contribute to its strong aromatic properties and medicinal uses.

Origanum zygis is often cultivated for culinary purposes and as a ornamental plant in gardens.
