Oxalis leucolepis var. griffithii

Oxalis leucolepis var. griffithii, commonly known as the Griffith's wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features trifoliate leaves with reddish veins and small, delicate white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and forest floors. It has a fibrous root system and produces capsules containing numerous small seeds.

Oxalis leucolepis var. griffithii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
