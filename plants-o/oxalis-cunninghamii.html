Oxalis cunninghamii

Oxalis cunninghamii, commonly known as the mountain wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive red veins and small, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in alpine and subalpine regions, where it contributes to the local biodiversity.

Oxalis cunninghamii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowers.
