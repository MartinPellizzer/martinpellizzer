Opuntia phaeacantha var. nigricans

Opuntia phaeacantha var. nigricans, commonly known as the black-spined prickly pear, is a cactus species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems that are covered in sharp, black spines. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tun, is edible and contains small, sweet seeds.

This variety is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water to thrive.
