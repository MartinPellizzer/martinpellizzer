Oxalis inaequalis

Oxalis inaequalis, commonly known as the unequal wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive leaves with three to five lobes. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months. Oxalis inaequalis is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
