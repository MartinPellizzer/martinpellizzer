Oxalis amblyosepala

Oxalis amblyosepala, commonly known as the pale-sepaled wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive white or pale green coloration, and its flowers are typically small, with five petals and a yellow center. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Oxalis amblyosepala thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
