Ocimum serpyllifolium var. glabrius

Ocimum serpyllifolium var. glabrius, commonly known as smooth basil, is a variant of the common basil species characterized by its smooth, hairless leaves.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic herbs. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with square stems and opposite leaves. Its small, white or pale purple flowers are borne in whorls along the stems.

This variety is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves used in cooking and traditional medicine.
