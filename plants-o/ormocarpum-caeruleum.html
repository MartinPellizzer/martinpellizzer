Ormocarpum caeruleum

Ormocarpum caeruleum, commonly known as blue burr or blue thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant produces distinctive blue to purple bracts that enclose the small, yellow flowers, giving it a striking appearance. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Ormocarpum caeruleum is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
