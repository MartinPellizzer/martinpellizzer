Opuntia cholla

Opuntia cholla, commonly known as cholla cactus, is a species native to the deserts of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, segmented stems that are covered in small, spiny joints. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits known as tunas. Opuntia cholla is a succulent plant, adapted to store water in its thick, fleshy stems.

It plays an important role in desert ecosystems, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
