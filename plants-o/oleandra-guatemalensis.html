Oleandra guatemalensis

Oleandra guatemalensis, commonly known as the Guatemalan oleander, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen nature and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a leathery texture, while its flowers are usually pink or white, arranged in clusters.

Oleandra guatemalensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
