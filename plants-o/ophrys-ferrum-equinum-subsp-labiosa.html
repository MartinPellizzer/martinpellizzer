Ophrys ferrum-equinum subsp. labiosa

Ophrys ferrum-equinum subsp.

labiosa, commonly known as the red helleborine, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to parts of Europe. It is characterized by its distinctive, bee-like flower structure, which plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist woodlands and has dark green, leathery leaves. Its flowers are usually deep red or maroon with intricate patterns that mimic female bees.

This orchid is listed as endangered in several regions due to habitat loss and limited distribution.
