Oldenlandia psychotrioides

Oldenlandia psychotrioides, commonly known as the psychotrioides moonflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking white, star-shaped flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid forests.

Its botanical features make it a unique and visually appealing species in the Asteraceae family.
