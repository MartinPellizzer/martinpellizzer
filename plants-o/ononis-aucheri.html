Ononis aucheri

Ononis aucheri, commonly known as the Armenian rock cress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Turkey.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in rocky and well-drained soils, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are basal, lobed, and hairy, adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Ononis aucheri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
