Oreoblastus parkeri

Oreoblastus parkeri, commonly known as the Cape mountain zamioculcas, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Zamioculcaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting to arid environments. The plant features a short, woody stem with rosettes of dark green, glossy foliage. It produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that appear at the base of the plant during the warmer months.

Oreoblastus parkeri is well-suited for rocky or sandy soils and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for drought-tolerant gardens.
