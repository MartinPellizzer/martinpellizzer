Ophrys exaltata proles arachnitiformis

Ophrys exaltata proles arachnitiformis, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its unique, spider-like appearance, with flowers that mimic the appearance of a spider, complete with long, slender sepals and a specialized labellum. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its pollination strategy relies on mimicry, attracting male wasps that attempt to mate with the flower's false "female" structure.
