Oncidium caquetanum

Oncidium caquetanum, commonly known as the Cauqueta Orchid, is a species native to Colombia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its pendulous inflorescences bearing numerous small, colorful flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its flowers are often fragrant and come in shades of yellow, orange, and red, making it a popular choice for ornamental cultivation.

The plant requires bright, indirect light and consistent moisture to thrive.
