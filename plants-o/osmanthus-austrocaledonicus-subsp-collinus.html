Osmanthus austrocaledonicus subsp. collinus

Osmanthus austrocaledonicus subsp.

collinus, commonly known as the New Caledonian osmanthus, is a shrub native to the island of New Caledonia. It is characterized by its small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often emitting a sweet, honey-like scent.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest understories, where it thrives in the humid climate. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a glossy surface and a distinctively aromatic aroma.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional local practices.
