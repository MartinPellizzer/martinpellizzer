Oxalis aliena

Oxalis aliena, commonly known as the common wood sorrel, is a flowering plant belonging to the Oxalidaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Oxalis aliena has been historically used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Its scientific name reflects its widespread distribution and ecological significance in various habitats.
