Oxalis erecta

Oxalis erecta, commonly known as the wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of five-petaled yellow flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Oxalis erecta thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun.

It is a hardy perennial that can be grown in gardens or as a houseplant.
