Ocotea recurvata

Ocotea recurvata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green on top and lighter underneath. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible for some bird species.

Ocotea recurvata is often found in humid lowland forests and is valued for its aromatic wood used in traditional medicine and crafts.
