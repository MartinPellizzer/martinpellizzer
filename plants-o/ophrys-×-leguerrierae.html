Ophrys × leguerrierae

Ophrys × leguerrierae, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant that results from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly found in southern Europe. The plant is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of female bees to attract male bees for pollination. Its flowers are typically yellowish-brown with intricate patterns that resemble bee bodies, aiding in its deceptive pollination strategy.

Ophrys × leguerrierae thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
