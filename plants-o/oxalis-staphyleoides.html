Oxalis staphyleoides

Oxalis staphyleoides, commonly known as the strawberry oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces edible, strawberry-like berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Oxalis staphyleoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators.
