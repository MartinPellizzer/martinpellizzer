Ocotea limiticola

Ocotea limiticola, commonly known as the swamp ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the wetlands and lowland forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in swampy or seasonally flooded areas. Its leaves are typically elliptical with a smooth texture and emit a subtle, spicy fragrance.

Ocotea limiticola plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
