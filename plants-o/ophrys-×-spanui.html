Ophrys × spanui

Ophrys × spanui, commonly known as the Spanish bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its intricate, bee-like flower structure, which features a prominent, fuzzy lip that mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing a striking display of yellow and brown hues.

Ophrys × spanui is a rare and protected species, reflecting its ecological significance and conservation status.
