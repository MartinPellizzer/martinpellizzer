Odontanthera boveana subsp. nubica

Odontanthera boveana subsp.

nubica, commonly known as the Nubian toothed grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves with a rough, textured surface.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It is adapted to withstand drought conditions and thrives in well-drained soils.

This subspecies is of interest for its potential in sustainable land management and erosion control in degraded landscapes.
