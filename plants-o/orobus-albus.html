Orobus albus

Orobus albus, commonly known as white orobrush, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small white flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Orobus albus thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in dry landscapes.
