Oryza sativa var. xanthoxeros

Oryza sativa var. xanthoxeros, commonly known as the yellow rice variety, is a botanical variant of rice characterized by its unique yellowish grain color.

This variety is native to Asia and is cultivated for its distinct flavor and nutritional profile. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with slender stems and compound leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in panicles, typical of the grass family.

The grains are usually harvested when they reach full maturity, ensuring optimal yield and quality.
