Oenocarpus utilis

Oenocarpus utilis, commonly known as the oil palm, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves and produces a fruit that contains a high amount of oil. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil. Its fruit is used to extract palm oil, which is widely used in food and industrial products.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both agriculture and ecology.
