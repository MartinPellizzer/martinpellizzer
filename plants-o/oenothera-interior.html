Oenothera interior

Oenothera interior, commonly known as the interior evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is well adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Oenothera interior is part of the Onagraceae family, which includes other evening primroses and related species.
