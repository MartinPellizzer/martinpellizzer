Orchis latifolia f. pinguis

Orchis latifolia f. pinguis, commonly known as the broad-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to thrive in nutrient-rich soils.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
