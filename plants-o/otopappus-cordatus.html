Otopappus cordatus

Otopappus cordatus, commonly known as the heartleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its flowers are yellow and resemble those of the common dandelion.

Otopappus cordatus is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
