Oenothera deltoides var. ambigua

Oenothera deltoides var. ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy yellow-orange flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Oenothera deltoides var. ambigua is known for its ecological importance in supporting local pollinator populations and its ornamental value in gardens.
