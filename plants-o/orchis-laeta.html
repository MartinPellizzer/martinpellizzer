Orchis laeta

Orchis laeta, commonly known as the sweet orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded woodland areas. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive lip that is often marked with yellow or white patterns. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil in its natural habitat.

Orchis laeta is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been historically used in traditional medicine.
