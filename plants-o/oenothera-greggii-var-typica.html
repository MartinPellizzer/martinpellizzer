Oenothera greggii var. typica

Oenothera greggii var. typica, commonly known as the Gregg's evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening and close by morning. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, while its stems are often hairy and erect.
