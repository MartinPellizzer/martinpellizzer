Oleandra christophersenii

Oleandra christophersenii, commonly known as the Christensen's oleander, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Oleandra christophersenii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
