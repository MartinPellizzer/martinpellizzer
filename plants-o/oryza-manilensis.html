Oryza manilensis

Oryza manilensis, commonly known as the Philippine rice grass, is a species of grass native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow leaves and small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy habitats, thriving in moist and nutrient-rich soils. Its seeds are small and often dispersed by water, contributing to its natural distribution.

While not a major crop, it plays a role in local ecosystems and has been studied for its potential in ecological restoration.
