Orchis basilica subsp. saccifera

Orchis basilica subsp.

saccifera, commonly known as the sac-like orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to specific regions of Europe. It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a distinctive sac-like ovary that sets it apart from other orchid species.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils, often in woodland or grassland habitats. Its flowers are usually purple or violet, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in many areas.
