Ormosia laosensis

Ormosia laosensis, commonly known as the Laos tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive fruit pods. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in ecological restoration projects. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The tree's seeds are encased in hard, woody pods, which contain a nutritious, edible pulp.
