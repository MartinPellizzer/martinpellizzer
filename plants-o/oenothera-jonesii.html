Oenothera jonesii

Oenothera jonesii, commonly known as Jones' evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has hairy, ribbed stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Oenothera jonesii is also known for its medicinal properties, with its seeds containing essential fatty acids.
