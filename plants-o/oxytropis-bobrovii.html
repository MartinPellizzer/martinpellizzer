Oxytropis bobrovii

Oxytropis bobrovii, commonly known as Bobrov's locoweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in steppes and rocky soils.

Oxytropis bobrovii is known for its hardy nature and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil in harsh conditions.
