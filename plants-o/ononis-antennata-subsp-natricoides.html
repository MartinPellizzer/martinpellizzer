Ononis antennata subsp. natricoides

Ononis antennata subsp.

natricoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf whitlow grass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are linear to narrowly elliptic, with a slightly rough texture.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming from spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to arid conditions and its role in stabilizing soil in its native environment.
