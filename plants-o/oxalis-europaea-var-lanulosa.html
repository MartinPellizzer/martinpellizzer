Oxalis europaea var. lanulosa

Oxalis europaea var. lanulosa, commonly known as the woolly wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive woolly texture due to the presence of dense, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically appearing in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Oxalis europaea var. lanulosa is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
