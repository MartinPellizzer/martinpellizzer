Origanum × lusitanicum

Origanum × lusitanicum, commonly known as Portuguese oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with small tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its leaves are used in cooking and traditional medicine for their pungent, minty aroma.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary applications.
