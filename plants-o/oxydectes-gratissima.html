Oxydectes gratissima

Oxydectes gratissima, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is a climbing vine with opposite, heart-shaped leaves and produces a strong, pleasant fragrance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Oxydectes gratissima is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
