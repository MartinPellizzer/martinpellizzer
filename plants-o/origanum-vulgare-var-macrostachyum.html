Origanum vulgare var. macrostachyum

Origanum vulgare var. macrostachyum, commonly known as large-stamen oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves with a pungent, slightly bitter flavor. The plant produces showy, pale blue to purple flowers in summer, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its essential oils, rich in compounds like carvacrol, are valued in culinary and medicinal applications.
