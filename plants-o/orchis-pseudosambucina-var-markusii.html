Orchis pseudosambucina var. markusii

Orchis pseudosambucina var. markusii, commonly known as the Markus's false hollyhock, is a rare orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers with a distinctive yellow-green lip. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in calcareous soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while the stems are upright and branched.

This variant is noted for its limited distribution and is considered a subject of interest in botanical conservation efforts.
