Orchis quadripunctata subsp. anatolica

Orchis quadripunctata subsp.

anatolica, commonly known as the Anatolian spotted orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Turkey and the Balkans. It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, violet-blue flowers.

The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its flowers are adorned with distinct dark purple spots, which are a key identifying feature.

The species is also noted for its relatively short flowering period, usually occurring in late spring to early summer.
