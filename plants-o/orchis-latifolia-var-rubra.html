Orchis latifolia var. rubra

Orchis latifolia var. rubra, commonly known as the red broad-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or grassland habitats. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces tall, upright flowering stems with showy, violet to pinkish-purple flowers. Its blooms are often clustered in loose racemes and are pollinated by various insect species.

This orchid is noted for its ecological importance and is often found in conservation areas due to its habitat specificity.
