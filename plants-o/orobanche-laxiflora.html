Orobanche laxiflora

Orobanche laxiflora, commonly known as the slender broomrape, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is characterized by its lack of leaves and its small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in association with grasses and other herbaceous plants. Its reproductive strategy involves producing numerous small, dry seeds that can remain viable in the soil for many years.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can have significant impacts on the growth and health of its host plants.
