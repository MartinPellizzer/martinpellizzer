Otiophora multicaulis

Otiophora multicaulis, commonly known as the clustered mossy bell, is a unique member of the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, clustered inflorescences that resemble miniature umbels. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves that contribute to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas.
