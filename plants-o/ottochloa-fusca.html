Ottochloa fusca

Ottochloa fusca, commonly known as the slender bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and forms dense, mat-like growth patterns. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as meadows and prairies. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences.

Ottochloa fusca is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in native plant landscapes.
