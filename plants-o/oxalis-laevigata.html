Oxalis laevigata

Oxalis laevigata, commonly known as the smooth wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its foliage and flowers are often used in ornamental gardening due to their attractive appearance.

Oxalis laevigata is also valued for its ability to thrive in dry conditions, making it a resilient addition to xeriscapes.
