Oxytenanthera abyssinica

Oxytenanthera abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian grass, is a perennial grass species native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, arid environments and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low water availability.

Oxytenanthera abyssinica plays a role in stabilizing soils in its native habitat and is sometimes used in traditional practices.
