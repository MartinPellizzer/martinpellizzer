Oleander indica

Oleander indica, commonly known as the common oleander, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its simple, lance-shaped leaves and showy clusters of flowers. The flowers are typically pink, red, or white, and bloom in summer. Oleander indica is known for its high toxicity, with all parts of the plant containing cardiac glycosides.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.
