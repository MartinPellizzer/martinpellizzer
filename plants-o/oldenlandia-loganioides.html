Oldenlandia loganioides

Oldenlandia loganioides, commonly known as the "Cape ivy," is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has heart-shaped, opposite leaves that are often hairy on the underside. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its rapid growth and invasive potential make it a common weed in many regions outside its native range.
