Oxalis caripensis

Oxalis caripensis, commonly known as the velvetleaf wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows as a perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Oxalis caripensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
