Opuntia hystricina var. nicholii

Opuntia hystricina var. nicholii, commonly known as the Nicholii prickly pear, is a cactus species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in clusters of sharp spines and glochids. The plant produces showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit, a red or purple pear-shaped structure, contains numerous seeds and is edible when ripe.

This variety is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
