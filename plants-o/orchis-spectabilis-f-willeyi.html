Orchis spectabilis f. willeyi

Orchis spectabilis f. willeyi, commonly known as the Willey's showy orchid, is a rare and striking botanical specimen native to specific regions of the United States.

This orchid is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring. The flowers are usually a striking combination of purple and yellow, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by insects. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
