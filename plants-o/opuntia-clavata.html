Opuntia clavata

Opuntia clavata, commonly known as the flat-stemmed prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flattened, paddle-shaped stems that are ribbed and covered in spines. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible, red-fleshed fruits. Opuntia clavata is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick stems and having a shallow root system.

Its structure and growth patterns make it a distinctive and resilient member of the Cactaceae family.
