Oxypetalum pannosum var. macranthum

Oxypetalum pannosum var. macranthum, commonly known as the large-flowered oxypetalum, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
