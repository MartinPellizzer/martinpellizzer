Ocimum americanum var. pilosum

Ocimum americanum var. pilosum, commonly known as hairy basil, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves that are covered in fine, hairy pubescence, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for both culinary and ornamental purposes.

Its essential oils contain compounds like linalool and eugenol, which contribute to its use in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.
