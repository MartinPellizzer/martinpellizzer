Oenothera stuebelii

Oenothera stuebelii, commonly known as Stuebel's evening primrose, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oval seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Oenothera stuebelii is a perennial herb that plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar and habitat for various insects.
