Oxypetalum erectum subsp. campestre

Oxypetalum erectum subsp.

campestre, commonly known as the field oxypetalum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides.

The plant features upright stems that are often hairy, and its flowers are small, white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. The leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
