Oxytropis strobilacea

Oxytropis strobilacea, commonly known as the mountain locoweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow to green pea-like flowers that are clustered in racemes, typical of its genus. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in high-elevation environments.

Oxytropis strobilacea is also known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock.
