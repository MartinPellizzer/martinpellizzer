Opuntia polyacantha f. platycarpa

Opuntia polyacantha f. platycarpa, commonly known as the broad-fruited prickly pear, is a cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features flat, fleshy stems that are typically green to blue-green in color, covered in spiny areoles. The plant produces large, showy flowers that range from yellow to red, depending on the variety. Its fruit, known as a tunas, is oval and fleshy, with a sweet, succulent pulp.

This species is adapted to arid environments and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of food.
