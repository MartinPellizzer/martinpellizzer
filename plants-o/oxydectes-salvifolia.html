Oxydectes salvifolia

Oxydectes salvifolia, commonly known as the sweetleaf tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic and have a slight waxy coating. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, orange-colored berries. Its bark and leaves have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.

Oxydectes salvifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitats.
