Orchis maculata var. elabiata

Orchis maculata var. elabiata, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking purple and white flowers with distinctive spots. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption.

This species plays an important role in supporting pollinators and maintaining biodiversity in its native habitats.
