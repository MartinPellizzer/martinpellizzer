Ocotea athroanthes

Ocotea athroanthes, commonly known as the broadleaf ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and smooth, aromatic bark. It typically grows in humid lowland forests and can reach heights of up to 30 meters.

Ocotea athroanthes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its aesthetic qualities.
