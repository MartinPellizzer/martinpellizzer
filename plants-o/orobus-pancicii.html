Orobus pancicii

Orobus pancicii, commonly known as the Pancetti's orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and small, pale greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in cool, humid environments. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered and is protected in several countries.

Its unique morphology and ecological niche make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
