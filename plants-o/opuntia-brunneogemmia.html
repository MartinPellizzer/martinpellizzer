Opuntia brunneogemmia

Opuntia brunneogemmia, commonly known as the brown-gemma opuntia, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems that are typically covered in small, spiny tubercles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Opuntia brunneogemmia is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance.
