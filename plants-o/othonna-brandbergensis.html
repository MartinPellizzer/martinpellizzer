Othonna brandbergensis

Othonna brandbergensis, commonly known as Brandberg othonna, is a succulent shrub native to the arid regions of Namibia and southern Angola.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water, enabling it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers during the rainy season, typically in spring. Its leaves are usually reduced to small, scale-like structures, reducing water loss through transpiration.

Othonna brandbergensis is well adapted to rocky and sandy soils, often found growing on cliffs and outcrops in its native habitat.
