Orchis provincialis

Orchis provincialis, commonly known as the Provincial Orchid, is a rare and elusive orchid species native to specific regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers are often adorned with intricate patterns and are pollinated by specific insect species.

Due to habitat loss and fragmentation, Orchis provincialis is considered endangered in many areas.
