Orchis lactea subsp. broteroana

Orchis lactea subsp.

broteroana, commonly known as the white helleborine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, white to pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its flowers are clustered in a spike at the top of the stem.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
