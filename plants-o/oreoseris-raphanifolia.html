Oreoseris raphanifolia

Oreoseris raphanifolia, commonly known as the radishleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are deeply lobed and resemble those of a radish, giving it its common name.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in the spring, attracting various pollinators.
