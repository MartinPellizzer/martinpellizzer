Omphalodes caucasica

Omphalodes caucasica, commonly known as Caucasian omphalodes, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features slender, upright stems and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Omphalodes caucasica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
