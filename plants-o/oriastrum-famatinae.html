Oriastrum famatinae

Oriastrum famatinae, commonly known as the Famatina burrobrush, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Argentina.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces yellow-orange ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, which are adapted for pollination by insects. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited to its harsh native environment.

Oriastrum famatinae plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
