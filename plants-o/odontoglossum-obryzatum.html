Odontoglossum obryzatum

Odontoglossum obryzatum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows in high-altitude cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are long and narrow, often forming a rosette pattern at the base.

Odontoglossum obryzatum is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
