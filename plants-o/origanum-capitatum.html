Origanum capitatum

Origanum capitatum, commonly known as mountain oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and small, aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers in terminal clusters, which give it its species name "capitatum," meaning "head-like.". Its essential oils contain compounds like carvacrol and thymol, contributing to its strong fragrance and medicinal properties.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
