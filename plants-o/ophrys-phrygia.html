Ophrys phrygia

Ophrys phrygia, commonly known as the Phrygian bee orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive flower shape that closely resembles a bee, a feature evolved to attract specific pollinators. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with brownish markings, and it produces small, round, dark brown seed pods.

Ophrys phrygia is a perennial herb that thrives in temperate climates and is known for its specialized pollination mechanism.
