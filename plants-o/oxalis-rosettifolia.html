Oxalis rosettifolia

Oxalis rosettifolia, commonly known as the rosette wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, rosette-like arrangement of fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its compact growth habit and ornamental appeal, it is widely cultivated as a decorative plant.
