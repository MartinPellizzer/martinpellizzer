Oxalis illinii

Oxalis illinii, commonly known as the Illinois wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the central United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive veins and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland areas and thrives in partial shade. It has a fibrous root system and is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes.

Oxalis illinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
