Ooclinium paucidentatum

Ooclinium paucidentatum, commonly known as the narrow-toothed ooclinium, is a species of aquatic plant belonging to the family Oocliniaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, scale-like leaves that are often submerged in water. The plant produces specialized reproductive structures called oocysts, which are important in its life cycle and ecological role. It is typically found in freshwater environments such as ponds and slow-moving streams.

Due to its unique morphology and reproductive features, it is of interest to botanists and ecologists studying aquatic flora.
