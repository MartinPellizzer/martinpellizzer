Ophiopogon platyphyllus

Ophiopogon platyphyllus, commonly known as the Japanese forest grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features dense, dark green foliage with narrow, grass-like leaves that form a low, carpet-like growth. The plant produces small, pale green to white flowers in late summer, which are clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often used in ornamental landscaping for its attractive appearance.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is closely related to other ornamental grasses.
