Ocotea pachypoda

Ocotea pachypoda, commonly known as the thick-leafed ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and aromatic leaves that emit a subtle spicy scent. Its bark is thick and rugged, often used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.

Ocotea pachypoda thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
