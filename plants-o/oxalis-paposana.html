Oxalis paposana

Oxalis paposana, commonly known as the "false shamrock," is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands. The plant features trifoliate leaves resembling those of the shamrock, with three heart-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are small, white or pink, and appear in clusters during the spring.

Oxalis paposana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
