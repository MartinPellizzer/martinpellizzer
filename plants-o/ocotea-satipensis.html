Ocotea satipensis

Ocotea satipensis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian lauraceae, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. It typically grows in humid, shaded environments at mid to high elevations.

The tree is valued for its timber, which is durable and used in construction and furniture-making.
