Ocimum urticifolium subsp. caryophyllatum

Ocimum urticifolium subsp.

caryophyllatum, commonly known as Indian borage, is a perennial herb native to the Indian subcontinent. It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a hairy texture and a distinctive minty aroma.

The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
