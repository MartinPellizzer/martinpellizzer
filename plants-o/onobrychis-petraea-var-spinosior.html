Onobrychis petraea var. spinosior

Onobrychis petraea var. spinosior, commonly known as the spiny sainfoin, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in racemes, which are typically visited by various pollinators. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in steppe and scrub habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is occasionally used in ecological restoration projects.
