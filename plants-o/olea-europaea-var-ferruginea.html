Olea europaea var. ferruginea

Olea europaea var. ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty leaf olive, is a variety of the common olive tree native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish-brown foliage, which gives it its common name. The leaves are usually smaller and more densely packed compared to the standard olive tree. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by edible fruits similar to those of the common olive.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
