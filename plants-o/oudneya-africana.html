Oudneya africana

Oudneya africana, commonly known as African wild cucumber, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Cucurbitaceae, which includes other cucurbits like pumpkins and melons. The plant has slender, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are yellow and inconspicuous, while the fruit is a long, slender, green capsule that turns yellow when mature.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
