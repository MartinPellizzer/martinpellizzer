Oenothera fruticosa var. goodmanii

Oenothera fruticosa var. goodmanii, commonly known as the Goodman's evening primrose, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It features showy yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has woody stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for xeriscaping and ecological restoration projects.
