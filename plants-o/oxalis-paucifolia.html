Oxalis paucifolia

Oxalis paucifolia, commonly known as the silver leaf oxalis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive silvery-white coating that gives it its common name. The plant produces clusters of delicate, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink or white, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and compact growth habit, Oxalis paucifolia is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
