Orchis incarnata var. genevensis

Orchis incarnata var. genevensis, commonly known as the Genevan lady's tresses, is a rare and distinctive orchid species native to parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems and dense clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that resemble a tresses of hair. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces a single, slender spike of flowers.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in many regions.
