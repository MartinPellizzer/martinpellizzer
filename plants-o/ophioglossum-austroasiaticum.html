Ophioglossum austroasiaticum

Ophioglossum austroasiaticum, commonly known as the Asian adder's tongue fern, is a rare and ancient fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Ophioglossaceae, which is characterized by its unique, primitive morphology. The plant has a short, stout rhizome with a single, erect, lanceolate leaf that is often covered in a dense layer of brown, scale-like structures. Its sporangia are clustered in a distinctive, tongue-shaped structure at the base of the leaf, giving it its common name.

This species is of particular interest to botanists due to its evolutionary significance and limited distribution.
