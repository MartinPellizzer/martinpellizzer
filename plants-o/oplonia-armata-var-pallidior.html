Oplonia armata var. pallidior

Oplonia armata var. pallidior, commonly known as the pale-flowered tamarack, is a shrub native to parts of eastern North America.

It belongs to the Cupressaceae family, which includes cypress and juniper species. The plant is characterized by its scale-like leaves and small, cone-like structures that mature into woody cones. It typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
