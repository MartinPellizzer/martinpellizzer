Octoclinis

Octoclinis, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf nightshade, is a genus of flowering plants in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, often waxy leaves that give it a distinctive velvety appearance. The plants typically have showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually purple or blue in color. Octoclinis species are native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

They are often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields.
