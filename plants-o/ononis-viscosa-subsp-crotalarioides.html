Ononis viscosa subsp. crotalarioides

Ononis viscosa subsp.

crotalarioides, commonly known as the hairy bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It features slender, branched stems that are often covered in fine, hairy glands.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and usually have a glandular underside.

This species is adapted to rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.
