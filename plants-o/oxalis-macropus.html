Oxalis macropus

Oxalis macropus, commonly known as the giant wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is a perennial shrub that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and rockeries.
