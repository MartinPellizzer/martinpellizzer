Oenothera cespitosa subsp. crinita

Oenothera cespitosa subsp.

crinita, commonly known as the curlyleaf evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as prairies and roadsides.

The plant features yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths. Its leaves are hairy and often arranged in a rosette at the base, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The subspecies is characterized by its finely divided, curling leaf margins, which contribute to its common name.
