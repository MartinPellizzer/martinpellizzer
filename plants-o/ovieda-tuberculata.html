Ovieda tuberculata

Ovieda tuberculata, commonly known as the roughleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in the spring and early summer. Its leaves are often covered in tiny, raised bumps, giving it a textured appearance.

Ovieda tuberculata is typically found in grassy areas and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
