Ophrys insectifera subsp. subinsectifera

Ophrys insectifera subsp.

subinsectifera, commonly known as the bee orchid, is a striking orchid species native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its intricate floral structure, which closely resembles a female bee, a feature that plays a key role in its unique pollination strategy.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in early spring, producing a vivid display of yellow and brown markings that mimic the appearance of insects.

This subspecies is notable for its specialized relationship with specific pollinators, enhancing its ecological significance in its native range.
