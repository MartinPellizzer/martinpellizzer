Oxalis semiloba subsp. angustifolia

Oxalis semiloba subsp.

angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wood sorrel, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States. It features trifoliate leaves with three heart-shaped leaflets, and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has a shallow root system and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in woodlands and shaded areas, where it forms dense colonies.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
