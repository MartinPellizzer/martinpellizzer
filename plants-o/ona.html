Ona

Ona, commonly known as the white sage, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal practices for their purported cleansing and spiritual properties.

Ona thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.
