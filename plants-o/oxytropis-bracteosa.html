Oxytropis bracteosa

Oxytropis bracteosa, commonly known as the bracteosa locoweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright stems and small, compound leaves. The plant produces yellow to reddish-purple flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in dense spikes. Oxytropis bracteosa is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sagebrush steppe and grasslands.

It is known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock.
