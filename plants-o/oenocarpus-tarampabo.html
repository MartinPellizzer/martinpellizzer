Oenocarpus tarampabo

Oenocarpus tarampabo, commonly known as the red-fruit palm, is a tropical plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of large, reddish-purple fruits that are highly prized for their unique flavor and nutritional value. Its fruit contains a single large seed surrounded by a fleshy, edible pericarp.

Oenocarpus tarampabo plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food for various wildlife species.
