Oreopanax coriaceus

Oreopanax coriaceus, commonly known as the thick-leaved arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its roots are used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Oreopanax coriaceus thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforest understories.
