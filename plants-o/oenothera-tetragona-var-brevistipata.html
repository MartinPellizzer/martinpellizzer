Oenothera tetragona var. brevistipata

Oenothera tetragona var. brevistipata, commonly known as the short-tipped evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

This variety is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
