Ophrys × breuerorum

Ophrys × breuerorum, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Ophrys apifera (the bee orchid) and Ophrys insectifera (the wasp orchid), resulting in a unique combination of floral traits. The plant is characterized by its intricate, bee-like flowers that mimic the appearance and scent of female bees, a strategy used to attract male bees for pollination. Its habitat typically includes calcareous soils in open grasslands and woodland edges across Europe.

This hybrid species is notable for its morphological and genetic diversity, reflecting its complex evolutionary history.
