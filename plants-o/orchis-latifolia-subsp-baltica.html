Orchis latifolia subsp. baltica

Orchis latifolia subsp.

baltica, commonly known as the Baltic wide-leaved orchid, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and western Russia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodlands and meadows.

The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers arranged in a dense spike, with three sepals and three petals, one of which is showy and spurred. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a distinctive waxy coating.

This subspecies is noted for its ecological importance as a food source for specific pollinators and its role in biodiversity-rich habitats.
