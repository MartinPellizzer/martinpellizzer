Orobanche epithymum proles hellebori

Orobanche epithymum proles hellebori, commonly known as the Christmas orobanche, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is characterized by its small, showy, often reddish or purple flowers. This species typically parasitizes members of the Ranunculaceae family, such as hellebores, and is found in temperate regions. Its life cycle involves germinating in response to chemical signals released by host roots.
