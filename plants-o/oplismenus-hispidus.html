Oplismenus hispidus

Oplismenus hispidus, commonly known as roughleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and clusters of purple to pinkish-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. It has a robust root system that helps stabilize soil in wetland environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
