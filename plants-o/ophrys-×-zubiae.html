Ophrys × zubiae

Ophrys × zubiae, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its unique floral morphology. The flowers resemble male bees, a specialized adaptation to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and evolutionary biology.
