Odontoglossum bellum

Odontoglossum bellum, commonly known as the bell orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are elliptical and arranged in a rosette, and it produces aerial rhizomes that help in its propagation.

Odontoglossum bellum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts worldwide.
