Ophrys × devenensis

Ophrys × devenensis, commonly known as the Devenish bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the UK.

It is characterized by its intricate floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant produces a single, showy flower with a dark, bee-like labellum and a greenish-yellow column. It thrives in damp, shaded habitats such as woodlands and riverbanks.

Ophrys × devenensis is a rare and protected species, highlighting its ecological significance and conservation value.
