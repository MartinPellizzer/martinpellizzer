Oenothera sessilis

Oenothera sessilis, commonly known as the sessile evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.
