Oxysepala grandimesensis

Oxysepala grandimesensis, commonly known as the giant orchid of the Andes, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru. This plant is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. Its botanical features include a sympodial growth habit and specialized pollination mechanisms adapted to its mountainous habitat.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is considered a conservation concern.
