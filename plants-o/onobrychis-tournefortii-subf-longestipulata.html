Onobrychis tournefortii subf. longestipulata

Onobrychis tournefortii subf.

longestipulata, commonly known as the long-stipule bursaria, is a perennial leguminous plant characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive floral structure. It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its seeds are enclosed in a pod-like structure, which is a defining feature of the legume family.

This sub-species is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.
