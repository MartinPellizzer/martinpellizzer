Octomeria cochlearis

Octomeria cochlearis, commonly known as the spiral leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique spiral arrangement of leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments at high altitudes. Its flowers are small and clustered, often in shades of white and green, and bloom during the cooler months.

Octomeria cochlearis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its distinctive foliage.
