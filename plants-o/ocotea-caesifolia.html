Ocotea caesifolia

Ocotea caesifolia, commonly known as the blue ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy texture.

Ocotea caesifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
