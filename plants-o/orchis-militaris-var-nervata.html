Orchis militaris var. nervata

Orchis militaris var. nervata, commonly known as the nervous military orchid, is a rare and distinctive orchid species native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers with intricate patterns, often featuring a darkened throat. The plant typically grows in woodland habitats, preferring moist, shaded environments with rich, loamy soil. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinctive veining pattern, which contributes to its common name.

The species is known for its limited distribution and is considered a conservation concern in many regions.
