Oxalis leptogramma

Oxalis leptogramma, commonly known as the slender wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each leaflet being narrow and pointed. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch to its foliage. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Oxalis leptogramma is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
