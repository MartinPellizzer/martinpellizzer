Ornithogalum stenostachyum

Ornithogalum stenostachyum, commonly known as the narrow-stalked star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces a tall, leafless stem bearing a cluster of white, star-shaped flowers. The plant is typically found in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It is adapted to dry conditions and is often seen in summer rainfall regions.

The flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
