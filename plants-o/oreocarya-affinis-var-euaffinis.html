Oreocarya affinis var. euaffinis

Oreocarya affinis var. euaffinis, commonly known as the Sierra primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and slopes. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments.
