Oncidium sipaliwinense

Oncidium sipaliwinense, commonly known as the Sipaliwini orchid, is a species native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil, Colombia, and Venezuela.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, fragrant flowers that often display a mix of yellow and green hues. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests, where it thrives in high humidity and indirect light. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air, and it produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Oncidium sipaliwinense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
