Odontoglossum cristatellum var. canaria

Odontoglossum cristatellum var. canaria, commonly known as the Canary orchid, is a species native to the Canary Islands.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulbous structure, which helps it store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming in the spring. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, arranged in a rosette pattern around the base.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
