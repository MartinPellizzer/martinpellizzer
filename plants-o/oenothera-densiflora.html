Oenothera densiflora

Oenothera densiflora, commonly known as denseflower evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant produces a capsule fruit containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Oenothera densiflora thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in desert or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential medicinal applications.
