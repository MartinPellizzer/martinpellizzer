Oligoneuron jacksonianum

Oligoneuron jacksonianum, commonly known as Jackson's oligoneuron, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and along stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems that bear lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in loose racemes, which bloom in early spring.

Oligoneuron jacksonianum is characterized by its fibrous root system and its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich, well-drained soils.
