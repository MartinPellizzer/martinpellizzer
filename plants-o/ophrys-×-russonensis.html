Ophrys × russonensis

Ophrys × russonensis, commonly known as the Russon's bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the genus Ophrys, which is known for its mimicry of female insects to attract male pollinators. The plant produces intricate, bee-like flowers that are highly specialized for pollination by bees. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

Its botanical features include a slender stem with a single or few flowers, each with a complex structure designed to facilitate pollination.
