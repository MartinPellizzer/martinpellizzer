Ocotea lanceolata var. genuina

Ocotea lanceolata var. genuina, commonly known as the Guayacan blanco, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in countries like Mexico and Colombia. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered white flowers. It typically grows in humid, lowland forests and is valued for its aromatic wood.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
