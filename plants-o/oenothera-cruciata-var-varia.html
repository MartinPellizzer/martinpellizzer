Oenothera cruciata var. varia

Oenothera cruciata var. varia, commonly known as the variable evening primrose, is a species of flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in harsh conditions, making it a resilient member of its ecosystem.
