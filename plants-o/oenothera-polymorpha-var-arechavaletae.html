Oenothera polymorpha var. arechavaletae

Oenothera polymorpha var. arechavaletae, commonly known as the Arechavaleta evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and hairy, while its stems are erect and branching.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinator populations.
