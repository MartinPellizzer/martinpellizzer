Oreosedum farinosum

Oreosedum farinosum, commonly known as the sticky stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, gray-green leaves that are covered in a fine, powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
