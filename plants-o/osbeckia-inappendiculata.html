Osbeckia inappendiculata

Osbeckia inappendiculata, commonly known as the appendiculate willow herb, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant features slender, erect stems that are often branched and covered in fine, soft hairs. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic in shape, and have a hairy surface.

The flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in dense, terminal spikes, making it a distinctive member of the Onagraceae family.
