Orbignya phalerata

Orbignya phalerata, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon basin.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves and produces elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall and has a dense, buttressed root system. Its flowers are small and yellowish, and it relies on specific pollinators for reproduction.

The fruit takes several months to mature, and the seeds are rich in nutrients, making the tree economically and ecologically significant.
