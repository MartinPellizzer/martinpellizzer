Onobrychis psoraleifolia var. psoraleifolia

Onobrychis psoraleifolia var. psoraleifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved burr medic, is a perennial legume native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, curved pods that contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or cultivated areas.

This species is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability and use in forage and soil improvement.
