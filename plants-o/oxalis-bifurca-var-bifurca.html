Oxalis bifurca var. bifurca

Oxalis bifurca var. bifurca, commonly known as the double-leaf wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves that resemble clover and its clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. It produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Oxalis bifurca var. bifurca is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
