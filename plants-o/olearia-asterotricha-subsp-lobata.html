Olearia asterotricha subsp. lobata

Olearia asterotricha subsp.

lobata, commonly known as the coastal daisy bush, is a shrub native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and small, daisy-like white flowers that bloom in spring.

The leaves are typically lobed, giving the plant its subspecific name "lobata.". It thrives in coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and strong winds.

This plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.
