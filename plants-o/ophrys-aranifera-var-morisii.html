Ophrys aranifera var. morisii

Ophrys aranifera var. morisii, commonly known as the Moris's bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which closely resembles a male bee, a mimicry strategy used to attract female bees for pollination. The plant produces a single, showy flower with a distinctive hinged lip that mimics the appearance of a bee's abdomen. It thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
