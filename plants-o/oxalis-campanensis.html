Oxalis campanensis

Oxalis campanensis, commonly known as the bell-shaped wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped lobes and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink or purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and woodlands. It produces small, edible berries that are often consumed by wildlife.

Oxalis campanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
