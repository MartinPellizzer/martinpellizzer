Oxytropis tenuirostris

Oxytropis tenuirostris, commonly known as narrow-beaked locoweed, is a perennial shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces pods that contain lens-shaped seeds. It is adapted to harsh environments, often growing in rocky soils and requiring minimal water.

Oxytropis tenuirostris is also known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock.
