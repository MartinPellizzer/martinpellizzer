Orthophytum roseolilacinum

Orthophytum roseolilacinum, commonly known as the pink-tinted orchid, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a water-holding cup. The plant produces showy, pinkish-lavender flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat. Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
