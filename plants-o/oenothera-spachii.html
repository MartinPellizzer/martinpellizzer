Oenothera spachii

Oenothera spachii, commonly known as the Spach's evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy yellow or orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairies and disturbed areas. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered with fine hairs.

Oenothera spachii is part of the Onagraceae family, which includes other evening primroses and related species.
