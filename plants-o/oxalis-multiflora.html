Oxalis multiflora

Oxalis multiflora, commonly known as the woodland wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive yellowish-green color and produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded areas such as forest floors and along stream banks. It has a shallow root system and is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value.

Oxalis multiflora is also known for its ability to thrive in partial to full shade and its preference for well-drained soil.
