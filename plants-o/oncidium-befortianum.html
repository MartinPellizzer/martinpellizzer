Oncidium befortianum

Oncidium befortianum, commonly known as the Befort's orchid, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a sympodial orchid with a compact growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant features strap-like leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety coating. Its flowers are typically arranged in a raceme and bloom during the warmer months.

Oncidium befortianum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
