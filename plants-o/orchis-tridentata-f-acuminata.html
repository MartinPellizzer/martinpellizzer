Orchis tridentata f. acuminata

Orchis tridentata f. acuminata, commonly known as the acuminata orchid, is a rare and distinctive variant of the three-toothed orchid.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and lanceolate leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The flowers are typically pale green to yellowish in color, with a unique three-lobed lip that distinguishes it from other orchid species. This orchid thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

Its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
