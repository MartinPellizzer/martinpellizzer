Ophrys × colonnae

Ophrys × colonnae, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant in the Orchidaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Ophrys apifera (the bee orchid) and Ophrys insectifera (the wasp orchid), resulting in a unique combination of floral characteristics. The plant produces striking, bee-like flowers with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of insects, aiding in pollination through mimicry. These flowers are typically greenish-yellow with brownish markings, and they emit a faint scent to further attract pollinators.

Ophrys × colonnae is often found in calcareous soils in Europe, thriving in open, sunny habitats.
