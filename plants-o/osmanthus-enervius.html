Osmanthus enervius

Osmanthus enervius, commonly known as the small-fruited osmanthus, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, elliptical berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a slightly serrated margin.

Osmanthus enervius is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.
