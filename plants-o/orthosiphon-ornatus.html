Orthosiphon ornatus

Orthosiphon ornatus, commonly known as the Indian borage or velvetleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Orthosiphon ornatus is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
