Oxytropis fragilifolia

Oxytropis fragilifolia, commonly known as the fragile milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of western North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are compound, with numerous small leaflets, and the plant is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Oxytropis fragilifolia plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is sometimes used in restoration projects.
