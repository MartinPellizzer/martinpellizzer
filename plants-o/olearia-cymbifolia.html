Olearia cymbifolia

Olearia cymbifolia, commonly known as the mountain dandelion, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a shrub that typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, often on rocky or gravelly soils. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flower heads that are surrounded by white to pale yellow ray florets. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs.

Olearia cymbifolia is well adapted to cold climates and is often found in open, sunny habitats at high elevations.
