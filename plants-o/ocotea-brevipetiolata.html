Ocotea brevipetiolata

Ocotea brevipetiolata, commonly known as the short-petioled ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in inflorescences, while the fruit is a small, dark purple drupe.

Ocotea brevipetiolata is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional crafts and as a source of essential oils.
