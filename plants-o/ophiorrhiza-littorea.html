Ophiorrhiza littorea

Ophiorrhiza littorea, commonly known as sea almond, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that thrives in sandy or rocky soils along the shorelines. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellowish flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by water.

Ophiorrhiza littorea plays an important role in stabilizing coastal dunes and preventing erosion.
