Ornithogalum zauschneri

Ornithogalum zauschneri, commonly known as the star of Bethlehem, is a bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils. It is related to the lily family and can be found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its bulbs are toxic and should be handled with care.
