Origanum bastetanum

Origanum bastetanum, commonly known as the Greek oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Origanum bastetanum is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, particularly for its essential oils and antioxidant properties.
