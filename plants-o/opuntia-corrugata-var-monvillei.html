Opuntia corrugata var. monvillei

Opuntia corrugata var. monvillei, commonly known as the Monville's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flattened, fleshy stems that are covered in spines and are often segmented. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months, followed by edible fruits that are typically red or purple in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh desert conditions.
