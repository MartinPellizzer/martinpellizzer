Ochrosia maculata

Ochrosia maculata, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with a distinctive spotted pattern. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial herb, often found in grasslands and open woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

Ochrosia maculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
