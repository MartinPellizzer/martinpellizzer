Ophrys × dodonica

Ophrys × dodonica, commonly known as the honey orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the genus Ophrys, which is characterized by its mimicry of female insects to attract male pollinators. The plant produces intricate, bee-like flowers that are highly specialized for pollination by bees. Its botanical features include a sympodial growth habit and pseudobulbs, typical of many orchid species.

The flowers are usually yellow or orange with a distinctive pattern that resembles an insect's abdomen.
