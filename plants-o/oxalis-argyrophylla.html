Oxalis argyrophylla

Oxalis argyrophylla, commonly known as silverleaf wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive silvery-white coating on the underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Oxalis argyrophylla is often used in rock gardens and as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.
