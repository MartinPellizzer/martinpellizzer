Ononis pubescens var. semiunifolia

Ononis pubescens var. semiunifolia, commonly known as the hairy cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are alternate, trifoliate, and covered in fine pubescence, contributing to its common name.

It is a member of the Rosaceae family and is often found in alpine and arid environments.
