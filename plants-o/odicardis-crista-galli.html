Odicardis crista-galli

Odicardis crista-galli, commonly known as the cock's comb, is a striking ornamental plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its vibrant, feathery inflorescences that resemble a rooster's comb, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette.

Odicardis crista-galli is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
