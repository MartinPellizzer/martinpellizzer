Odontoglossum × maderoi

Odontoglossum × maderoi, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Odontoglossum aemulum and Odontoglossum maderoi.

It is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andean regions of South America, typically found in humid, shaded forest understories. The plant produces strikingly colorful, showy flowers with intricate patterns and a distinctive spurred lip. Its botanical features include fleshy, hairy pseudobulbs and linear, lance-shaped leaves.

It is appreciated for its ornamental value and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and diversity.
