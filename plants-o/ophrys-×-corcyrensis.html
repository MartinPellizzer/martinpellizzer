Ophrys × corcyrensis

Ophrys × corcyrensis, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant that results from the cross between Ophrys apifera (the bee orchid) and Ophrys citrina (the yellow bee orchid).

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, bee-like flower structure designed to mimic female bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks across Europe. Its flowers exhibit a range of colors, often featuring a mix of yellow, orange, and brown, with a distinctive lip that resembles a bee's abdomen.

This hybrid species is notable for its ability to thrive in various soil types and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
