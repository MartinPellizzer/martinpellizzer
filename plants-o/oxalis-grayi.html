Oxalis grayi

Oxalis grayi, commonly known as the silverleaf wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive silver or white underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy addition to xeriscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in arid environments.
