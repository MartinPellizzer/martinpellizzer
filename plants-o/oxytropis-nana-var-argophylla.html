Oxytropis nana var. argophylla

Oxytropis nana var. argophylla, commonly known as silverleaf globemallow, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with each leaflet being narrow and hairy.

This variety is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.
