Oxylobium capitatum

Oxylobium capitatum, commonly known as the clustered oxylobium, is a deciduous shrub native to South Africa.

It features small, fragrant white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often glandular and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun to partial shade conditions.

Oxylobium capitatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
