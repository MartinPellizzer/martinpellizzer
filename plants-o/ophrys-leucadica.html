Ophrys leucadica

Ophrys leucadica, commonly known as the white helleborine, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces showy, greenish-white flowers with a distinctive lip that mimics the appearance of an insect, aiding in pollination by bees. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Ophrys leucadica is native to Europe and is often found in temperate regions with cool, humid climates.
