Ophrys × vanderspekiae

Ophrys × vanderspekiae, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is characterized by its intricate floral structure that mimics the appearance of female wasps, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is found in various parts of Europe. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brown, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Ophrys × vanderspekiae is a prime example of the evolutionary adaptations seen in orchids, particularly in their relationship with pollinators.
