Ophrys × mannarica

Ophrys × mannarica, commonly known as the bee orchid hybrid, is a fascinating plant in the Orchidaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera, resulting in a plant with unique floral morphology. The orchid's flowers closely resemble female bees, a trait evolved to attract male bees for pollination. It thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in grasslands and heathlands across Europe.

This species is notable for its deceptive pollination strategy and ecological importance in supporting pollinator populations.
