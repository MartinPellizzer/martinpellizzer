Oenothera stricta

Oenothera stricta, commonly known as the narrowleaf evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and sandy soils. The plant produces showy yellow or orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and its stems are upright and often branched.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its role in supporting local wildlife.
