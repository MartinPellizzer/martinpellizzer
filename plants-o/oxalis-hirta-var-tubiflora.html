Oxalis hirta var. tubiflora

Oxalis hirta var. tubiflora, commonly known as the woolly wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract beneficial insects such as bees and butterflies.
