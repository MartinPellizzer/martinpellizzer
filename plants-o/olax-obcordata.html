Olax obcordata

Olax obcordata, commonly known as the heart-shaped olax, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Olacaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that often have a distinctive heart-shaped base. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Olax obcordata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
