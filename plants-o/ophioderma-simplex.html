Ophioderma simplex

Ophioderma simplex, commonly known as the common tansy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, upright stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Ophioderma simplex is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
