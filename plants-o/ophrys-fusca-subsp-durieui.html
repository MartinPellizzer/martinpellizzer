Ophrys fusca subsp. durieui

Ophrys fusca subsp.

durieui, commonly known as the dark orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dark purple to blackish flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns resembling female insects, a feature that aids in pollination by male bees.

The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. It has a short flowering period, usually in spring, and is known for its striking visual appearance.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered a vulnerable species in some areas.
