Orobanche obtusata

Orobanche obtusata, commonly known as the bluntleaf broomrape, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is characterized by its lack of leaves and its distinctive, showy, often yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed soils and is found in various regions across Europe and Asia. It forms a symbiotic relationship with host plants such as legumes and grasses, attaching itself to their roots through specialized structures called haustoria.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can significantly impact the growth and health of its host plants.
