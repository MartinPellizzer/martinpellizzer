Orchis mediterranea subsp. siciliensis

Orchis mediterranea subsp.

siciliensis, commonly known as the Sicilian orchid, is a rare and endemic species found primarily in southern Italy. It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its tall, slender stem and showy, violet-colored flowers.

The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing intricate, lip-shaped petals that are adapted for pollination by specific insects. Its habitat is usually rocky or calcareous soils, often in open woodlands or along coastal areas.
