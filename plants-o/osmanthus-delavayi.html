Osmanthus delavayi

Osmanthus delavayi, commonly known as the Chinese sweet olive, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Osmanthus delavayi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
