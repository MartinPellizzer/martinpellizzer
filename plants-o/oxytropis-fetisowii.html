Oxytropis fetisowii

Oxytropis fetisowii, commonly known as the Mongolian milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, which are clustered in racemes. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in steppes and rocky soils.

Oxytropis fetisowii is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitats.
