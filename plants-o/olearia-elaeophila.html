Olearia elaeophila

Olearia elaeophila, commonly known as the silver fern, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its silvery-white, velvety foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions.

Olearia elaeophila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
