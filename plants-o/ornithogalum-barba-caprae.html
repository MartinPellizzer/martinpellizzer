Ornithogalum barba-caprae

Ornithogalum barba-caprae, commonly known as the goat's beard, is a bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers with a distinctive, beard-like appearance. The plant is characterized by its underground bulb, which serves as a storage organ for nutrients. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Ornithogalum barba-caprae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
