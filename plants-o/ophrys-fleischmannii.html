Ophrys fleischmannii

Ophrys fleischmannii, commonly known as the Fleischmann's bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its intricate floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained habitats. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brown in color, with intricate patterns that aid in its unique pollination strategy.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in many areas.
