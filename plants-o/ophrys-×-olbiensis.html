Ophrys × olbiensis

Ophrys × olbiensis, commonly known as the Balkan bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which resembles a bee, a feature that aids in its specialized pollination by male bees. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and meadows. It has a sympodial growth habit with a rhizomatous root system.

Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with dark markings, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.
