Oxalis urubambensis

Oxalis urubambensis, commonly known as the Andean wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces translucent, bead-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in montane forests.

Oxalis urubambensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
