Oisodix glauca

Oisodix glauca, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it a silvery appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

Oisodix glauca is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators like butterflies and bees.
