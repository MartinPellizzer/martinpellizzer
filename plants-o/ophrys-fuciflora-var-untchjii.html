Ophrys fuciflora var. untchjii

Ophrys fuciflora var. untchjii, commonly known as the Sardinian bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the island of Sardinia.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, thriving in Mediterranean climates with mild winters and hot, dry summers. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brownish in color, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species.
