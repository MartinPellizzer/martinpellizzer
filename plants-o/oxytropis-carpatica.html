Oxytropis carpatica

Oxytropis carpatica, commonly known as Carpathian locoweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Oxytropis carpatica is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

It is also known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native environment.
