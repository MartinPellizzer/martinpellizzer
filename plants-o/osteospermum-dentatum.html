Osteospermum dentatum

Osteospermum dentatum, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, daisy-like blooms with white petals and a central yellow disk, often surrounded by bracts that resemble leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Osteospermum dentatum is also valued for its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
