Oxalis illinoensis

Oxalis illinoensis, commonly known as Illinois wood sorrel, is a perennial herb native to the central United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with reddish veins and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shady woodlands and thrives in well-drained soil. It produces small, edible fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Oxalis illinoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
