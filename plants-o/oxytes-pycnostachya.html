Oxytes pycnostachya

Oxytes pycnostachya, commonly known as the dense-stemmed oxytes, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, woody stems and small, narrow leaves that are adapted to minimize water loss. The plant produces clusters of tiny, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Oxytes pycnostachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils.
