Opuntia guatemalensis

Opuntia guatemalensis, commonly known as the Guatemalan prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Central America.

It is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems, known as cladodes, which are covered in spines and are used for photosynthesis. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers, followed by edible fruits called tunas. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing high adaptability to drought conditions.

Opuntia guatemalensis is also valued for its ornamental use and traditional uses in local cultures.
