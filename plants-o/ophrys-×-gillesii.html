Ophrys × gillesii

Ophrys × gillesii, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which closely resembles a female bee, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant is native to parts of southern Europe and is often found in grassy habitats with well-drained soil. Its flowers are typically pale yellow to brownish in color, with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of insects.

Ophrys × gillesii is a perennial herb that thrives in temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
