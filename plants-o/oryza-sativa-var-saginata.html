Oryza sativa var. saginata

Oryza sativa var. saginata, commonly known as black rice, is a variety of rice characterized by its dark, almost black, grain color.

This variety is native to Southeast Asia and is cultivated for its unique appearance and nutritional value. The grains are rich in antioxidants, particularly anthocyanins, which contribute to their deep color. It is often used in traditional cuisines and is gaining popularity for its health benefits.

The plant exhibits typical grass characteristics, with long, narrow leaves and a robust root system.
