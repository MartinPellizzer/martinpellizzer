Orchidantha quadricolor

Orchidantha quadricolor, commonly known as the four-colored orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, multicolored petals that display shades of red, yellow, and blue. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters, producing a fragrant aroma that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
