Oxylobus glandulifer f. typicus

Oxylobus glandulifer f. typicus, commonly known as the glandular wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features heart-shaped, trifoliate leaves with prominent glandular dots on their surfaces, which secrete a sticky substance. The plant produces small, yellow, spurred flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitats.
