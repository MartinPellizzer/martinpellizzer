Odontites viscosus var. australis

Odontites viscosus var. australis, commonly known as the sticky mountain grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy, often sticky leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender stems. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.

Odontites viscosus var. australis is often found in open woodlands and rocky areas, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
