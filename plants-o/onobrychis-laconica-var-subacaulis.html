Onobrychis laconica var. subacaulis

Onobrychis laconica var. subacaulis, commonly known as the small-stemmed vetch, is a perennial legume native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with small, rounded leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by narrow, elongated pods. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
