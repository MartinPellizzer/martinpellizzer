Orobus varius

Orobus varius, commonly known as the variable orchid, is a species of flowering plant in the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to parts of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, tropical environments. The plant is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. Orobus varius typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and other vegetation without deriving nutrients from them.

Its botanical interest lies in its morphological variability and ecological adaptability.
