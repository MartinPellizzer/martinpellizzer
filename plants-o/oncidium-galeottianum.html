Oncidium galeottianum

Oncidium galeottianum, commonly known as the showy orchid, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture from the air. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Oncidium galeottianum is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its long-lasting blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
