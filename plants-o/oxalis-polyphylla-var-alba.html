Oxalis polyphylla var. alba

Oxalis polyphylla var. alba, commonly known as white wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with three heart-shaped leaflets that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for woodland gardens and native plant landscapes.
