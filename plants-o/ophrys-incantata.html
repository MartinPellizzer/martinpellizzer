Ophrys incantata

Ophrys incantata, commonly known as the witch's sylph, is a striking orchid native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its intricate, bee-like appearance, with a hairy, yellowish-green lip that mimics the appearance of a female bee. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, producing a unique scent that attracts male bees for pollination.

Ophrys incantata is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its specialized mimicry and ecological significance.
