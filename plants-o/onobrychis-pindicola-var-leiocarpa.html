Onobrychis pindicola var. leiocarpa

Onobrychis pindicola var. leiocarpa, commonly known as the smooth carmine vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has a low-growing habit and thrives in dry, calcareous soils. It is often found in rocky grasslands and open woodlands.

Its seeds are small and adapted for dispersal by wind, contributing to its ecological role in soil stabilization.
