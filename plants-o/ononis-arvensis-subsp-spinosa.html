Ononis arvensis subsp. spinosa

Ononis arvensis subsp.

spinosa, commonly known as the prickly whitlow grass, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and waste areas, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers.

The plant has alternate, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability and ability to thrive in harsh environments.
