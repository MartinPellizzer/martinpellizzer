Onobrychis viciifolia var. decumbens

Onobrychis viciifolia var. decumbens, commonly known as creeping sainfoin, is a low-growing perennial legume native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive trifoliate shape, and its small, yellow flowers are typically arranged in racemes. The plant is adapted to dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping. Its root system is capable of fixing atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

This variety is often used in forage and erosion control due to its hardiness and ecological benefits.
