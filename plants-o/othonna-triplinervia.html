Othonna triplinervia

Othonna triplinervia, commonly known as the Three-veined Othonna, is a succulent shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, fleshy stems and dense, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters during the warmer months, adding a subtle ornamental appeal. Its adaptability to arid conditions makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The species is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
