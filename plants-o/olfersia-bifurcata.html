Olfersia bifurcata

Olfersia bifurcata, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive star-shaped pattern. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its natural habitat.

Olfersia bifurcata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
