Orchis sichuanica

Orchis sichuanica, commonly known as the Sichuan orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Sichuan Province in China.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and meadows, and thrives in temperate climates. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.

Orchis sichuanica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
