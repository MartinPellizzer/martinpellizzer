Orthosiphon stamineus

Orthosiphon stamineus, commonly known as Java tea or Malabar spinach, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and small white to pinkish flowers. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are widely used in traditional medicine for their diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Orthosiphon stamineus is also cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of herbal remedies.
