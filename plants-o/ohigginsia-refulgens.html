Ohigginsia refulgens

Ohigginsia refulgens, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a strikingly beautiful plant native to South America, particularly in regions of Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring, often resembling small bells. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are often covered in fine hairs.

Ohigginsia refulgens is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
