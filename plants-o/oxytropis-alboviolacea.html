Oxytropis alboviolacea

Oxytropis alboviolacea, commonly known as the white violet locust, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, violet-blue flowers. The plant has palmately compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Oxytropis alboviolacea is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.
