Ouret pechuelii

Ouret pechuelii, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring rough, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. Its vibrant orange to red flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ouret pechuelii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
