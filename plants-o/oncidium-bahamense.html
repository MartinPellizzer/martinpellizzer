Oncidium bahamense

Oncidium bahamense, commonly known as the Bahamian orchid, is a tropical orchid native to the Caribbean region.

It is characterized by its elegant, pendulous inflorescences that bear clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are strap-like and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Oncidium bahamense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
