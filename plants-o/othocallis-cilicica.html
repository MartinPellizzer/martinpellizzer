Othocallis cilicica

Othocallis cilicica, commonly known as the Cilician bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats. The plant features basal leaves that are lanceolate and hairy, with flowers that are blue to violet in color, arranged in clusters. It blooms from late spring to early summer, producing nectar that attracts various pollinators.

Othocallis cilicica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
