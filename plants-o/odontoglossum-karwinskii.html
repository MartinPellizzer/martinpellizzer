Odontoglossum karwinskii

Odontoglossum karwinskii, commonly known as the Karwinski's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often violet or purple flowers with intricate patterns and a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its natural habitat.

Odontoglossum karwinskii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts worldwide.
