Oxalis mimosella

Oxalis mimosella, commonly known as the mimic oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, five-petaled flowers that resemble the blossoms of the mimosa plant. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and woodland areas. Oxalis mimosella is a herbaceous perennial that produces bright yellow flowers in the spring.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
