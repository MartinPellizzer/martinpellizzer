Oxalis bipunctata

Oxalis bipunctata, commonly known as the two-spotted wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves with two small black spots on each leaflet. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Oxalis bipunctata is a perennial herb that spreads through rhizomes, making it a common sight in wetlands and along stream banks.
