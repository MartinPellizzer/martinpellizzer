Ocotea nummularia

Ocotea nummularia, commonly known as the waxleaf ocotea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible for some bird species.

Ocotea nummularia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
