Oldenlandia nesaeoides

Oldenlandia nesaeoides, commonly known as the mountain spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and purple. The plant has slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are adapted to thrive in cool, moist environments. It is often found in montane grasslands and rocky slopes, where it contributes to the ecological diversity of these habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
