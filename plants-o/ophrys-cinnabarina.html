Ophrys cinnabarina

Ophrys cinnabarina, commonly known as the cinnabar bee orchid, is a species of orchid native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking, bee-like flower that mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its flowers are usually a mix of brown and orange, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Ophrys cinnabarina is a perennial herb with a rhizomatous root system, and it produces small, greenish-brown capsules as fruit.
