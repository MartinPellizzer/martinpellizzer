Oxalis spiralis

Oxalis spiralis, commonly known as the spiral wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pink in color. Oxalis spiralis thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its unique foliage and modest blooms.
