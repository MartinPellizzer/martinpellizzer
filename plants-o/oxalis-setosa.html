Oxalis setosa

Oxalis setosa, commonly known as the slender wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with three heart-shaped lobes and small, delicate flowers that are typically yellow with five petals. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Oxalis setosa thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland or wetland environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
