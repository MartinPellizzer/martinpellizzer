Oryza sativa var. flavescens

Oryza sativa var. flavescens, commonly known as the yellow rice plant, is a variant of rice that is characterized by its distinctive yellow grain color.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is cultivated for its aromatic and flavorful grains. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with slender stems and long, narrow leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in panicles, and it produces grains that are smaller than those of common rice varieties.

This variety is valued for its unique color and taste, making it a specialty crop in certain regions.
