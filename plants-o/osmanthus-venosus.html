Osmanthus venosus

Osmanthus venosus, commonly known as the veiny osmanthus, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, small yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves with a distinctive network of veins, giving it its common name. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the aromatic oils extracted from its flowers.

Osmanthus venosus thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.
