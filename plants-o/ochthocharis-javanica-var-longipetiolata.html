Ochthocharis javanica var. longipetiolata

Ochthocharis javanica var. longipetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled orchid, is a tropical orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated petioles, which give it its botanical name, and its striking, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and it produces clusters of small, colorful blossoms.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
