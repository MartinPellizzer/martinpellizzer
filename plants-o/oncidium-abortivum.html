Oncidium abortivum

Oncidium abortivum, commonly known as the abortive orchid, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous plant with a sympodial growth habit, producing pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers. The plant features elongated, strap-like leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green to brownish flowers. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Oncidium abortivum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
