Oxalis flava var. flava

Oxalis flava var. flava, commonly known as yellow wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are usually bright yellow with a reddish or purplish tint. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of yellow to pink, typically in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Oxalis flava var. flava is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
