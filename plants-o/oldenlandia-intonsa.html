Oldenlandia intonsa

Oldenlandia intonsa, commonly known as the "Blue Bottle" or "Cape Forget-me-not," is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has a trailing habit, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Oldenlandia intonsa is also known for its ability to thrive in sandy soils and its resistance to coastal conditions.
