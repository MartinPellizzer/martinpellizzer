Ophrys dubia

Ophrys dubia, commonly known as the rare orchid of the Ophrys genus, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, bee-like flowers that mimic the appearance and scent of female bees to attract male pollinators. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are linear and often have a waxy coating, while its roots are adapted for nutrient uptake in poor soils.

Ophrys dubia is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its specialized pollination mechanism.
