Odontoglossum barkeri

Odontoglossum barkeri, commonly known as Barker's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, showy flowers that typically feature a vibrant mix of purple, pink, and white hues. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its pseudobulbs, which store water and nutrients. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Odontoglossum barkeri is often cultivated for its ornamental value in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
