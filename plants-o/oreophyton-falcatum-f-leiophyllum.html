Oreophyton falcatum f. leiophyllum

Oreophyton falcatum f. leiophyllum, commonly known as the smooth-leaved desert plant, is a fossilized species from the late Paleozoic era.

It is characterized by its elongated, strap-like leaves that are typically smooth and waxy, adaptations that likely helped it conserve water in arid environments. The plant is considered one of the earliest known vascular plants, providing important insights into early plant evolution. Its structure suggests it may have been a precursor to modern gymnosperms.

This fossil is often found in sedimentary rock layers from what is now North America and Europe.
