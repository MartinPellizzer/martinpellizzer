Ourisia chamaedrifolia

Ourisia chamaedrifolia, commonly known as the creeping ourisia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Primulaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features narrow, evergreen leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, showy yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
