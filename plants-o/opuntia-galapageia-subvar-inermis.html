Opuntia galapageia subvar. inermis

Opuntia galapageia subvar. inermis, commonly known as the Galapagos prickly pear, is a cactus native to the Galapagos Islands.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in fine, hairless spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting well to the unique climate of the Galapagos.

This subvariety is notable for its ecological importance, providing food and habitat for native wildlife.
