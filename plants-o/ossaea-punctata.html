Ossaea punctata

Ossaea punctata, commonly known as the punctate lily, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its flowers bloom in late summer.

Ossaea punctata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
