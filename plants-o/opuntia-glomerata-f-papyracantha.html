Opuntia glomerata f. papyracantha

Opuntia glomerata f. papyracantha, commonly known as the paper prickly pear, is a unique variant of the opuntia cactus native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems that are covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers, which are typically followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and the stems are often used for ornamental purposes due to their striking appearance.

This cultivar is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
