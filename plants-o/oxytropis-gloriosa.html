Oxytropis gloriosa

Oxytropis gloriosa, commonly known as the golden globe pea, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect stems and dense clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, kidney-shaped seeds that are typically dispersed by wind. Oxytropis gloriosa is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
