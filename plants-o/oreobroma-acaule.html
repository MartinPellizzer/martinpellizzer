Oreobroma acaule

Oreobroma acaule, commonly known as the naked-stem oreobroma, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often in open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. Its stems are leafless, with leaves concentrated near the base, and it produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers.

Oreobroma acaule is adapted to full sun and is often found in regions with seasonal rainfall patterns.
