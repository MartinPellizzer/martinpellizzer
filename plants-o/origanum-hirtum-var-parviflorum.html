Origanum hirtum var. parviflorum

Origanum hirtum var. parviflorum, commonly known as narrow-leaved oregano, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees. The plant has hairy, aromatic leaves that are often used in cooking and traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or herb beds.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
