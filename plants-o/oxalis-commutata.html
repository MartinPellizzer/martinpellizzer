Oxalis commutata

Oxalis commutata, commonly known as the common wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to various regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which resemble those of clover, and small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas and along stream banks. It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is known for its edible leaves, though they contain oxalic acid.

Oxalis commutata is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
