Oxalis exserta

Oxalis exserta, commonly known as the coral root oxalis, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, trailing stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant has trifoliate leaves, each leaflet being oval-shaped with a pointed tip. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Oxalis exserta is known for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
