Oldenlandia thysanota

Oldenlandia thysanota, commonly known as the silverleaf or velvetleaf, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from summer to autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Oldenlandia thysanota is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and can also serve as a host plant for certain butterflies.
