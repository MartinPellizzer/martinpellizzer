Oplismenus compositus var. submuticus

Oplismenus compositus var. submuticus, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and finely divided, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, purple to pinkish-red flower heads that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it a distinctive, soft-textured appearance.

This variety is typically found in moist woodlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial to full shade.
