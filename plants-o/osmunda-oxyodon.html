Osmunda oxyodon

Osmunda oxyodon, commonly known as the giant water fern, is a large, aquatic fern that thrives in shallow, freshwater environments.

It has a distinctive frond structure with a long, flexible stalk and a broad, floating crown of leaves. The plant's roots anchor it to the substrate, while its floating fronds help in photosynthesis. Osmunda oxyodon is characterized by its spore-producing structures called sori, which are typically located on the underside of the fronds.

This fern plays an important ecological role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and stabilizing water bodies.
