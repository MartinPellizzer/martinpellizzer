Osmaronia obtusa

Osmaronia obtusa, commonly known as the broad-leafed osmaronia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It features lance-shaped leaves with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white to pale pink, which bloom in early summer. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Osmaronia obtusa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.
