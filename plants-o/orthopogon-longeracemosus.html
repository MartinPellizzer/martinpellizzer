Orthopogon longeracemosus

Orthopogon longeracemosus, commonly known as the long-bracted grass.

It is a member of the Poaceae family, characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance. The plant features elongated, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are typically wind-pollinated, a common trait among grasses.

Orthopogon longeracemosus is often found in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist soil conditions.
