Opuntia × aequatorialis

Opuntia × aequatorialis, commonly known as the hybrid prickly pear, is a cactus species native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its flattened, fleshy stems that are often ribbed and covered in spines. The plant produces showy yellow or red flowers, followed by edible, fleshy fruits called tunas. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh conditions.

Opuntia × aequatorialis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of food and traditional medicine.
