Orchis × kasparii

Orchis × kasparii, commonly known as the Kaspari orchid, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Orchis morio and Orchis mascula.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, violet or purple flowers with a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and prefers well-drained, loamy soil. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is often found in the temperate regions of Europe.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a range of morphological variations, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
