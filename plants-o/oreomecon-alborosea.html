Oreomecon alborosea

Oreomecon alborosea, commonly known as the white rose of the alpine meadows, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are hairy and often covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

Oreomecon alborosea is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in stabilizing alpine soils.
