Oenothera campestris var. obispoensis

Oenothera campestris var. obispoensis, commonly known as the Obispo's evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that open in the evening and close by morning. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has hairy, glandular stems and leaves that are opposite and lance-shaped.

Its seeds are enclosed in a capsule that splits open to release the seeds when mature.
