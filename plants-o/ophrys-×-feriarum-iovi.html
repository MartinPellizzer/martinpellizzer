Ophrys × feriarum-iovi

Ophrys × feriarum-iovi, commonly known as the bee orchid hybrid, is a fascinating plant that exhibits unique botanical features.

It is a natural hybrid between Ophrys apifera, the early bee orchid, and Ophrys iohannis, the late bee orchid. The plant is characterized by its intricate, bee-like flower structure, which plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. Its flowers are typically yellow with brown markings, mimicking the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination.

This orchid thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.
