Orchis incarnata subsp. fraasii

Orchis incarnata subsp.

fraasii, commonly known as the pink fairy orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and swamps. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and the flowers are arranged in a loose spike.

This subspecies is notable for its specific ecological requirements and is considered a conservation concern due to habitat loss.
