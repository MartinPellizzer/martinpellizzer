Oxytropis neglecta subsp. neglecta

Oxytropis neglecta subsp.

neglecta, commonly known as the slender locoweed, is a perennial legume native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves, which are typically hairy and grayish-green in appearance.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale yellow to reddish-brown, blooming in early spring. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes, and is adapted to arid environments. Oxytropis neglecta subsp.

neglecta is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a minor forage plant for livestock.
