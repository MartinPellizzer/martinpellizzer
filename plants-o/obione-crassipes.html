Obione crassipes

Obione crassipes, commonly known as the broadleaf water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, broad, floating leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and marshes. Its fronds are typically divided into lobes and are covered in a fine, silky coating.

Obione crassipes plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms and contributing to water filtration.
