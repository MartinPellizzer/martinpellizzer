Ozothamnus cassiope

Ozothamnus cassiope, commonly known as silverleaf dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. The plant features silver-gray, fuzzy leaves that are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stems. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Ozothamnus cassiope is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it adaptable to arid environments.
