Oxystophyllum capitellatum

Oxystophyllum capitellatum, commonly known as the Cape honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has opposite, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Oxystophyllum capitellatum is also valued for its ability to grow in coastal areas due to its tolerance for salt spray.
