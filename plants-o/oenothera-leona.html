Oenothera leona

Oenothera leona, commonly known as the golden evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the late afternoon and close at night. The plant belongs to the evening primrose family (Onagraceae) and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are typically erect and branching.

Oenothera leona is also noted for its role in attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
