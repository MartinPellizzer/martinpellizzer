Odontites vernus subsp. litoralis

Odontites vernus subsp.

litoralis, commonly known as the coastal toothwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to coastal regions of Europe. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils along sea cliffs and rocky shores, where it tolerates saline and windy conditions.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in early spring, typically arranged in clusters at the base of the leaves. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slightly hairy texture.

This subspecies is adapted to coastal environments, showcasing specialized features for survival in harsh maritime climates.
