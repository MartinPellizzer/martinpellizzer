Odontoglossum triumphans var. aureum

Odontoglossum triumphans var. aureum, commonly known as the golden tooth orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

This herbaceous plant features striking, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, golden-yellow flowers with a distinctive tooth-like pattern on the petals. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant blooms and unique morphology.

Its blooming period typically occurs in the spring and early summer, adding a dramatic touch to gardens and greenhouses.
