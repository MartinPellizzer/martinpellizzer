Orchis jacquinii

Orchis jacquinii, commonly known as the Jacob's orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and rocky areas. Its flowers are usually arranged in a dense spike and bloom from late spring to early summer.

Orchis jacquinii is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
