Oxalis agassizii

Oxalis agassizii, commonly known as the Agassiz's wood sorrel, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each leaflet being heart-shaped and often tinged with red or purple veins. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically yellow or white, with five petals. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial shade to full sun, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

Oxalis agassizii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and blooms.
