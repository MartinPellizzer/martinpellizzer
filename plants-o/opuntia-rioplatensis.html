Opuntia rioplatensis

Opuntia rioplatensis, commonly known as the Rio Platense prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to South America, particularly Argentina and Uruguay.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems that are covered in spines and are often ribbed. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. Opuntia rioplatensis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh conditions.

Its structure and growth habits make it an important component of the local ecosystems where it is found.
