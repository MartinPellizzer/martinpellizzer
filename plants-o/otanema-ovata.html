Otanema ovata

Otanema ovata, commonly known as the white spider flower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking white, spider-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Otanema ovata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
