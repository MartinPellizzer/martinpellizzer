Oxalis eckloniana

Oxalis eckloniana, commonly known as the forest oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forests and rocky areas. Its foliage resembles that of clover, and it produces showy, spurred flowers that bloom in spring.

Oxalis eckloniana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in partial shade.
