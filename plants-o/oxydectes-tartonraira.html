Oxydectes tartonraira

Oxydectes tartonraira, commonly known as the Flameleaf Orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, brightly colored leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage is often variegated with shades of red, orange, and yellow, making it a visually striking specimen.

The flowers are usually small and clustered, with a unique structure that aids in pollination by specific insect species.
