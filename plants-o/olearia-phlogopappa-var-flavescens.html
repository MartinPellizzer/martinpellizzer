Olearia phlogopappa var. flavescens

Olearia phlogopappa var. flavescens, commonly known as the golden flaxflower, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has silver-gray, hairy leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest margins and rocky areas.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
