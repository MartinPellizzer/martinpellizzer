Oldfeltia

Oldfeltia, commonly known as the false thimbleweed, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It consists of a single species, Oldfeltia hirta, which is native to North America. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, hairy stems, and small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Oldfeltia is often found in dry, disturbed soils and is considered a minor weed in agricultural settings.
