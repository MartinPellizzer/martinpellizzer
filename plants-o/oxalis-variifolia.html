Oxalis variifolia

Oxalis variifolia, commonly known as the variable wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are often heart-shaped and have a delicate, fern-like appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a charming visual element to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a versatile choice for gardeners.

Oxalis variifolia is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in horticultural displays.
