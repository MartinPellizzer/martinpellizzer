Olax merguensis

Olax merguensis, commonly known as the Ethiopian olive tree, is a shrub or small tree native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the family Olacaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible and have a mild, nutty flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Olax merguensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local communities.
