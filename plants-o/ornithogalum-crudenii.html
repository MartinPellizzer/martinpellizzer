Ornithogalum crudenii

Ornithogalum crudenii, commonly known as the Cruden's star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of star-shaped, white or pale blue flowers during the spring and summer months. The plant is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in well-drained conditions. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.

The species is also noted for its ability to form dense colonies, making it a useful plant for erosion control in suitable environments.
