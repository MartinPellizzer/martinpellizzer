Olearia lanata

Olearia lanata, commonly known as the silver tree, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Olearia lanata thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological importance in its native habitat.
