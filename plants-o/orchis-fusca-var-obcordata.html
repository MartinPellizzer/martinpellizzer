Orchis fusca var. obcordata

Orchis fusca var. obcordata, commonly known as the dark orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking dark purple to black flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while the roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption.

This variety is noted for its unique floral structure and is often found in association with specific mycorrhizal fungi.
