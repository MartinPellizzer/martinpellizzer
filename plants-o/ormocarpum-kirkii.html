Ormocarpum kirkii

Ormocarpum kirkii, commonly known as the African mountain balm, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually pink or white, arranged in clusters. Its leaves are opposite and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Ormocarpum kirkii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
