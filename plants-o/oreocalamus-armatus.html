Oreocalamus armatus

Oreocalamus armatus, commonly known as the armed reed, is a grass species native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms and dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil in marshy environments. Its leaves are narrow and often have a waxy coating, which reduces water loss.

Oreocalamus armatus plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
