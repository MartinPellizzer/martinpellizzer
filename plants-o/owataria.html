Owataria

Owataria, commonly known as the Japanese bellflower, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its leaves are usually opposite and elliptical, with a rough texture.

Owataria is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
