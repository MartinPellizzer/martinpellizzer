Odontoglossum × tentaculatum

Odontoglossum × tentaculatum, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a fascinating plant with a complex botanical background.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Odontoglossum raphanifolium and Odontoglossum × tentaculatum, which results in a unique combination of traits from both parent species. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that often display vibrant colors such as pink, purple, and white. Its leaves are typically broad and lance-shaped, and it thrives in cool, high-altitude environments.

This hybrid is highly valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and ease of cultivation.
