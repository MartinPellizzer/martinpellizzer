Ophrys argensonensis

Ophrys argensonensis, commonly known as the Argenson's bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to specific regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee, a feature evolved to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and prefers open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brownish in color, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Ophrys argensonensis is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts.
