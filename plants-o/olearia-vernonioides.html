Olearia vernonioides

Olearia vernonioides, commonly known as the New Zealand daisy or kōwhai pātiki, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its clustered, daisy-like flowers with white petals and yellow centers. The plant typically grows in coastal and lowland areas, preferring well-drained soils and sunny conditions. Its foliage is composed of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Olearia vernonioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for native pollinators.
