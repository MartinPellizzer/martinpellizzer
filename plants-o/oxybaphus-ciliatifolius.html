Oxybaphus ciliatifolius

Oxybaphus ciliatifolius, commonly known as the velvetleaf or silky leaf plant, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and fine, silky hairs along the edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural applications.
