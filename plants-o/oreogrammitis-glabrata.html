Oreogrammitis glabrata

Oreogrammitis glabrata, commonly known as the smooth fern, is a small, epiphytic fern that thrives in humid, shaded environments.

It has delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically light green and covered with a fine, waxy coating. The plant lacks true roots and instead absorbs moisture and nutrients through its rhizomes and leaves. It is native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan, and is often found growing on trees or rocks.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological niche, it is a subject of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
