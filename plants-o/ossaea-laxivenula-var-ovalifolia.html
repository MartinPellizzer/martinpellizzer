Ossaea laxivenula var. ovalifolia

Ossaea laxivenula var. ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaved ossaea, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with silver on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in dense panicles, typically during the cooler months. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

This variety is distinguished by its oval-shaped leaves, which set it apart from other varieties within the Ossaea genus.
