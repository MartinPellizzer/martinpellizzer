Odontophorus angustifolius subsp. protoparcoides

Odontophorus angustifolius subsp.

protoparcoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a species native to tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and pale yellow.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a sympodial growth habit.

It is often found in association with other epiphytic orchids in humid, tropical climates.
