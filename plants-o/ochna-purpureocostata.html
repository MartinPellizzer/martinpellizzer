Ochna purpureocostata

Ochna purpureocostata, commonly known as the purple-costate ochna, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its attractive, glossy, dark green leaves with a distinctive purple-tinged petioles. The plant produces showy, red or orange-red flowers that bloom in spring, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ochna purpureocostata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
