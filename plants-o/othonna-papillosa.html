Othonna papillosa

Othonna papillosa, commonly known as the velvety othonna, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, clustered flowers in shades of yellow or orange, which bloom in late spring or early summer.

Othonna papillosa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique texture and drought tolerance.
