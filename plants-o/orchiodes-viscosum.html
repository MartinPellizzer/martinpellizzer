Orchiodes viscosum

Orchiodes viscosum, commonly known as the sticky orchid, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, fleshy stems and clusters of showy, orchid-like flowers. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are often fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are broad and succulent, adapted to store water in arid environments.

The sticky orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates.
