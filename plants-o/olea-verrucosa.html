Olea verrucosa

Olea verrucosa, commonly known as the warty olive, is a species of flowering plant in the Oleaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its small, warty leaves and fragrant, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that contain a single seed. Olea verrucosa is often found in rocky or coastal areas, adapting well to saline and arid conditions.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
