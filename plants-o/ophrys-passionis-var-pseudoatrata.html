Ophrys passionis var. pseudoatrata

Ophrys passionis var. pseudoatrata, commonly known as the Passion Orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its intricate, bee-mimicking flower structure, which plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant produces a single, large, dark purple to black flower with intricate patterns that resemble a female bee, attracting male bees for pollination. It thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil, often growing in rocky or mountainous terrain.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
