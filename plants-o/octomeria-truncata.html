Octomeria truncata

Octomeria truncata, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically orange to yellow in color, with a slightly tart flavor. Its leaves are simple and alternate, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Octomeria truncata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
