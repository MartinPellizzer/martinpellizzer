Oreomecon radicata

Oreomecon radicata, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to blue. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, adapted to survive in cold, harsh environments. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and mountainous regions.

Oreomecon radicata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
