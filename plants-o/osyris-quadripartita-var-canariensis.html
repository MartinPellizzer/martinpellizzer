Osyris quadripartita var. canariensis

Osyris quadripartita var. canariensis, commonly known as the Canary osier, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for hedges and ornamental gardening.
