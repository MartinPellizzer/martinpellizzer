Osmunda plumieri

Osmunda plumieri, commonly known as the interrupted fern, is a species of fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that are divided into segments with a unique interrupted pattern. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomes are thick and creeping, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies.

Osmunda plumieri plays an important role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
