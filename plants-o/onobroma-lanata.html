Onobroma lanata

Onobroma lanata, commonly known as the woolly nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, woolly stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along dry streambeds.

Onobroma lanata is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes.
