Oncidium andradeanum

Oncidium andradeanum, commonly known as the Andrade's orchid, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments within tropical rainforests. The plant features erect, linear leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers with white and yellow markings. Its inflorescence is usually terminal, and the flowers are known for their delicate, intricate structure.

Oncidium andradeanum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
