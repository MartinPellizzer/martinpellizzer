Osmunda phyllitidis

Osmunda phyllitidis, commonly known as the broadleaf water fern, is a species of fern that belongs to the Osmundaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, triangular fronds that are typically divided into segments, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in wet, marshy environments and is often found in temperate regions. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in its habitat.

Osmunda phyllitidis is a perennial plant that is both ecologically and botanically significant in aquatic ecosystems.
