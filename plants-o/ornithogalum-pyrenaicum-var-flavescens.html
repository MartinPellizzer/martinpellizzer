Ornithogalum pyrenaicum var. flavescens

Ornithogalum pyrenaicum var. flavescens, commonly known as the yellow-flowered star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of star-shaped, yellow flowers in early spring. The plant belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its underground bulbs that store nutrients. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some regions.
