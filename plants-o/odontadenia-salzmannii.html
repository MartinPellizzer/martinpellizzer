Odontadenia salzmannii

Odontadenia salzmannii, commonly known as the African orchid or Salzmann's orchid, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant features a short, thick rhizome with a rosette of fleshy, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive lip that is often marked with darker veins.

Odontadenia salzmannii is adapted to arid environments and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.
