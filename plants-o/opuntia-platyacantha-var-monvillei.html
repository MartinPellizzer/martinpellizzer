Opuntia platyacantha var. monvillei

Opuntia platyacantha var. monvillei, commonly known as the Monville's prickly pear, is a succulent cactus native to Mexico.

It features flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in spines and glochids, which are fine, barbed bristles. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its pads are edible when mature and are often used in traditional cuisine.

It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water to thrive.
