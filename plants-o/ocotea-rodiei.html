Ocotea rodiei

Ocotea rodiei, commonly known as the white sand ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and can reach heights of up to 20 meters. Its bark is often used in traditional medicine, and its wood is valued for its durability and fine grain.

The species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting a variety of flora and fauna.
