Oxalis

Oxalis, commonly known as wood sorrel, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Oxalidaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, often brightly colored flowers. Many species of oxalis are native to tropical and temperate regions of the Americas, Africa, and Asia. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and light conditions.

Some species are popular as ornamental plants due to their attractive foliage and flowers.
