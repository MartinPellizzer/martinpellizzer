Oxalis minor

Oxalis minor, commonly known as wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a low-growing perennial with heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its ability to thrive in partial shade and well-drained soil. Its leaves are often covered in fine, silky hairs, and it produces edible leaves and flowers, though they should be consumed in moderation.

Oxalis minor is also known for its ornamental value in gardens and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
