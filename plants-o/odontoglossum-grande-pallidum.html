Odontoglossum grande-pallidum

Odontoglossum grande-pallidum, commonly known as the pale large-lobed orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, pale green to white flowers with a distinctive, elongated lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native habitat.

The orchid blooms during the spring and summer months, producing fragrant flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
