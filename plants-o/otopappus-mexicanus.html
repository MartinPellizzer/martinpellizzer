Otopappus mexicanus

Otopappus mexicanus, commonly known as Mexican sunflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its bright orange or red flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Otopappus mexicanus is often used in landscaping due to its vibrant blooms and resilience to poor soil conditions.
