Ornithogalum skorpili

Ornithogalum skorpili, commonly known as the rock onion or spear lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a bulbous stem that produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant blooms with white or pale yellow flowers that are arranged in a loose inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Ornithogalum skorpili is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
