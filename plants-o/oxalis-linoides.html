Oxalis linoides

Oxalis linoides, commonly known as the silverleaf wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive silvery-white underside, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Oxalis linoides is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and drought tolerance.
