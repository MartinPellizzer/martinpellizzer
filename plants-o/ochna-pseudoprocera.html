Ochna pseudoprocera

Ochna pseudoprocera, commonly known as the African yellowwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and dense, evergreen foliage with elliptical leaves that are often tinged with yellow. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in spring, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Ochna pseudoprocera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
