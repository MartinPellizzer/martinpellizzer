Oenothera polymorpha var. berteroana

Oenothera polymorpha var. berteroana, commonly known as the desert evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening and close by morning. The plant has hairy, branching stems and alternate leaves that are usually lance-shaped. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

The species is known for its ability to attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths and bats.
