Orchis coriophora subsp. nervulosa

Orchis coriophora subsp.

nervulosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to parts of Europe. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or heathland habitats. It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and exhibits a unique morphology adapted to its specific ecological niche.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
