Oreoselinum glaucum

Oreoselinum glaucum, commonly known as the blue fennel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, feathery leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers well-drained soil and full sun. Its leaves are often used in culinary applications, adding a subtle anise-like flavor to dishes.

Oreoselinum glaucum is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.
