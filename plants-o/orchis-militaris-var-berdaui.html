Orchis militaris var. berdaui

Orchis militaris var. berdaui, commonly known as the Berda's military orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to specific regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure, which includes a prominent lip and spurred ovary. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats. It blooms in late spring to early summer, producing striking purple or violet flowers.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, this variety is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
