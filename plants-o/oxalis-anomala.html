Oxalis anomala

Oxalis anomala, commonly known as the pale wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are heart-shaped and have a distinctive reddish or purple hue. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Oxalis anomala is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
