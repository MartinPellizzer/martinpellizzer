Oxalis elegans var. macrophylla

Oxalis elegans var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive pattern of veins and small, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its foliage is often covered in fine, silky hairs, and it produces small, edible berries that mature to a dark red color.

Oxalis elegans var. macrophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
