Ocotea limae

Ocotea limae, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurel and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces berries that contain the seeds.

Ocotea limae is valued for its aromatic wood and is often found in humid, lowland forests.
