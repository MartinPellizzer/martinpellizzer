Otochilus lancifolius

Otochilus lancifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and produces striking, fragrant orchid flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil efficiently.

Otochilus lancifolius is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
