Oxytropis ferganensis

Oxytropis ferganensis, commonly known as Fergana milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in steppes and rocky soils.

Oxytropis ferganensis is adapted to withstand drought and has a deep root system to access water from deeper soil layers.
