Ochrocarpos siamensis var. micranthus

Ochrocarpos siamensis var. micranthus, commonly known as the Siam orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
