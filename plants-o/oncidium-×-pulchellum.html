Oncidium × pulchellum

Oncidium × pulchellum, commonly known as the Orchid Hybrid, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Oncidium flexuosum and Oncidium tigrinum.

It is a sympodial orchid with a pseudobulb structure that supports its growth and storage of nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in a range of colors, including yellow, orange, and red, often arranged in a raceme. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil with regular watering.

Oncidium × pulchellum is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its vibrant blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
