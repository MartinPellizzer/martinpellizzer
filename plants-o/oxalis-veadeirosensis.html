Oxalis veadeirosensis

Oxalis veadeirosensis, commonly known as the Veadeiro wood sorrel, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the family Oxalidaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, edible fruits that contain seeds.

Oxalis veadeirosensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
