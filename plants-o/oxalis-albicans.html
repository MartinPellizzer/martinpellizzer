Oxalis albicans

Oxalis albicans, commonly known as white wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of North America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive white or pale green coloration, often adorned with red or purple veins. The plant produces small, five-petaled flowers that are typically yellow or pale yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Oxalis albicans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
