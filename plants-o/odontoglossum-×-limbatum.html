Odontoglossum × limbatum

Odontoglossum × limbatum, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a cross between Odontoglossum and Lycastes, resulting in a unique combination of traits.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to the Andean regions of South America, where it thrives in cool, humid climates. The plant produces striking, showy flowers with intricate patterns and a wide range of colors, including white, pink, and purple. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, velvety texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its adaptability and aesthetic value, it is popular among orchid enthusiasts and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
