Oxalis tricolor

Oxalis tricolor, commonly known as the wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It features trifoliate leaves with three heart-shaped leaflets and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of yellow, white, and pink. The plant belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its ability to thrive in moist, shady environments. It is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks, where it spreads through rhizomes.

Oxalis tricolor is also noted for its edible leaves and flowers, though they should be consumed in moderation.
