Oncidium crispum var. olivaceum

Oncidium crispum var. olivaceum, commonly known as the Olive Orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of fragrant, yellow-green flowers with a distinctive greenish tint. The plant features strap-like, dark green leaves that are often crinkled or wavy, giving it a unique appearance. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
