Oxalis pinetorum

Oxalis pinetorum, commonly known as the Florida oxalis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 6 to 12 inches. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive red veins and produces small, yellow flowers in the spring. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland settings.

Oxalis pinetorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
