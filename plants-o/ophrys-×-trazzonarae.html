Ophrys × trazzonarae

Ophrys × trazzonarae, commonly known as the Trazzonara bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the genus Ophrys, which is known for its mimicry of female insects to attract male pollinators. The plant produces strikingly intricate flowers that resemble female bees, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments.

This hybrid is of particular interest to botanists due to its unique floral morphology and ecological significance.
