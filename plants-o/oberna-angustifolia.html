Oberna angustifolia

Oberna angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf oberna, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters during the spring and summer months.

Oberna angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
