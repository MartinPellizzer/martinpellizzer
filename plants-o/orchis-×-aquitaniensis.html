Orchis × aquitaniensis

Orchis × aquitaniensis, commonly known as the Aquitaine orchid, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Orchis moria and Orchis anthropophora.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or grassland habitats. It is native to southern France and parts of Spain, where it thrives in temperate climates.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a protected species in several regions.
