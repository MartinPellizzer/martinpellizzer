Ononis fruticosa f. mycrophylla

Ononis fruticosa f. mycrophylla, commonly known as the small-leaved whitlow grass, is a compact, low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to August. The plant has wiry, branched stems and narrow, linear leaves that are usually less than 1 cm in length. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soils in arid environments.
