Oncidium cerebriferum

Oncidium cerebriferum, commonly known as the Brain Orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers that resemble the shape of the human brain, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are borne on long, arching stems and are known for their intricate patterns and vibrant hues.

Oncidium cerebriferum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
