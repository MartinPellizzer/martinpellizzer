Ocotea nutans

Ocotea nutans, commonly known as the fragrant ocotea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within lowland and montane forests. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

The fruit is a small, dark-colored drupe that contains a single seed, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
