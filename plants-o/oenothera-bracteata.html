Oenothera bracteata

Oenothera bracteata, commonly known as the showy evening primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds. Oenothera bracteata typically grows in dry, open habitats like prairies and disturbed soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
