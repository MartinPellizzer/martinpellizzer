Oxalis monophylla var. rotundifolia

Oxalis monophylla var. rotundifolia, commonly known as the rounded-leaf wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of yellow or orange, typically in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is widely appreciated by gardeners.
