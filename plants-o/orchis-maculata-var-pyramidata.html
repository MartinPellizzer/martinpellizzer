Orchis maculata var. pyramidata

Orchis maculata var. pyramidata, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive spotted leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks. Its flowers are arranged in a spike and bloom from late spring to early summer.

The species is notable for its ecological importance, supporting various pollinators and serving as an indicator of biodiverse habitats.
