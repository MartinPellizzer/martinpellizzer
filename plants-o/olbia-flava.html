Olbia flava

Olbia flava, commonly known as the yellow olbia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that are often tinged with a pale yellow hue, which is most prominent during its flowering period. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Olbia flava is typically found in moist, shaded environments, such as along stream banks or in forest undergrowth.
