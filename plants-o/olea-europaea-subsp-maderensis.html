Olea europaea subsp. maderensis

Olea europaea subsp.

maderensis, commonly known as the Maderan olive, is a subspecies native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and small, fragrant flowers.

The fruit, known as an olive, is typically small and has a high oil content. It thrives in Mediterranean climates with well-drained soils and requires a warm, dry environment.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and for producing olives used in culinary and medicinal applications.
