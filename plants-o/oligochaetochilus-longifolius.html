Oligochaetochilus longifolius

Oligochaetochilus longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved moss orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, moss-like growth habit and elongated, linear leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are often clustered in spikes. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically in forest understories.

Oligochaetochilus longifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
