Ononis reclinata subsp. calycina

Ononis reclinata subsp.

calycina, commonly known as the California sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a low-growing perennial with slender stems and small, clustered leaves.

The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland dunes.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and native landscaping.
