Orobanche major f. trichocheilon

Orobanche major f. trichocheilon, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed broomrape, is a root-parasitic plant belonging to the Orobanchaceae family.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients, typically parasitizing members of the Fabaceae family. The plant is characterized by its erect, often hairy stems and small, scale-like leaves. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to reddish, arranged in dense clusters.

This semi-shrubby species is found in various habitats, including disturbed soils and open woodlands.
