Oxalis rhombeo-ovata subsp. rhombeo-ovata

Oxalis rhombeo-ovata subsp.

rhombeo-ovata, commonly known as the heartleaf wood sorrel, is a species native to southern Africa. It is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial that produces trifoliate leaves resembling heart-shaped leaves.

The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its foliage is often covered in fine, hairy pubescence, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
