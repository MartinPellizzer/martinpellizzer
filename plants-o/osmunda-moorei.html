Osmunda moorei

Osmunda moorei, commonly known as the Moore's water fern, is a species of fern native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, floating fronds that are typically 1 to 2 meters in length. The plant has a distinctive morphology with a short, rhizomatous stem that anchors it in shallow water. Its fronds are pinnately compound, with both sterile and fertile pinnae, and it reproduces via spores rather than seeds.

Osmunda moorei plays an important ecological role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen through photosynthesis.
