Oenothera grandiflora var. glabra

Oenothera grandiflora var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening and close by morning. The plant has slender, branching stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and hairless. It thrives in sandy or well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and along roadsides.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its delicate, bioluminescent-like appearance at night.
