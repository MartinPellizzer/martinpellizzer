Odontarrhena peltarioidea subsp. peltarioidea

Odontarrhena peltarioidea subsp.

peltarioidea, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its woody stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought conditions.

The plant has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties, particularly in treating digestive ailments.
