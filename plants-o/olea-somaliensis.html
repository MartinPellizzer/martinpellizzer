Olea somaliensis

Olea somaliensis, commonly known as the Somali olive, is a small evergreen tree native to the Horn of Africa.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically eaten fresh or used in traditional medicine. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating remarkable drought tolerance.

Olea somaliensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
