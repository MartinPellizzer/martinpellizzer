Ocotea tarapotana

Ocotea tarapotana, commonly known as the Andean laurel, is a member of the Lauraceae family and is native to the cloud forests of Ecuador and northern Peru.

It is a medium-sized evergreen tree that can reach heights of up to 20 meters, featuring glossy, lance-shaped leaves with a strong aromatic scent. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by dark blue berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in traditional crafts and construction.

Ocotea tarapotana plays a vital role in maintaining the ecological balance of its native habitats.
