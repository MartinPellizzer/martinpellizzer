Orithyia

Orithyia, commonly known as the golden star, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, yellow flowers that resemble stars, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and grows in a compact, upright form. Orithyia is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to many landscapes.
