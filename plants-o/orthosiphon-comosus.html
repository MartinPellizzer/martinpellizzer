Orthosiphon comosus

Orthosiphon comosus, commonly known as Java tea or Malabar tea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that are clustered in whorls. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their diuretic and antiseptic properties.

Orthosiphon comosus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
