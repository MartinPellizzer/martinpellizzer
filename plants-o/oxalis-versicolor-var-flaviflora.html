Oxalis versicolor var. flaviflora

Oxalis versicolor var. flaviflora, commonly known as the yellow wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish or purplish veining, and its flowers are typically small, five-petaled, and often yellow or orange in color. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Oxalis versicolor var. flaviflora is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
