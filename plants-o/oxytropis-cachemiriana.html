Oxytropis cachemiriana

Oxytropis cachemiriana, commonly known as the Kashmir oxytropis, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale blue or purple in color. It is adapted to cold, arid environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

Oxytropis cachemiriana is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in mountainous regions.
