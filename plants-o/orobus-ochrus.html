Orobus ochrus

Orobus ochrus, commonly known as the yellow globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant that typically grows to a height of about 30-60 centimeters. The plant features yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, and its leaves are palmately divided with a rough texture. Orobus ochrus thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
