Oreorchis lancifolia

Oreorchis lancifolia, commonly known as the lancifoliate orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip that is often marked with purple or brown spots. It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows.

Oreorchis lancifolia is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its delicate, ephemeral blooms.
