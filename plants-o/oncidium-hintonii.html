Oncidium hintonii

Oncidium hintonii, commonly known as the Hinton's orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It features a sympodial growth habit with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences with showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. Its leaves are broad, fleshy, and arranged in a fan-like pattern.

Oncidium hintonii requires bright, indirect light and well-draining potting media to thrive.
