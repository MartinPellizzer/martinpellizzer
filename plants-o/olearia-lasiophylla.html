Olearia lasiophylla

Olearia lasiophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, daisy-like white flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It is often found in coastal and lowland areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

Olearia lasiophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
