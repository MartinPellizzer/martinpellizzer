Oreocarya sobolifera

Oreocarya sobolifera, commonly known as the Sierra buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and open habitats, such as slopes and meadows. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture.

Oreocarya sobolifera is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance through its deep root system.
