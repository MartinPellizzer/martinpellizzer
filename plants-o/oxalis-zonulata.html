Oxalis zonulata

Oxalis zonulata, commonly known as the zoned wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically heart-shaped with a distinctive white or yellowish blotch at the base. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are usually pink or purple, often with five petals. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Oxalis zonulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
