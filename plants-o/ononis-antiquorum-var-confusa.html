Ononis antiquorum var. confusa

Ononis antiquorum var. confusa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved catchfly, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in open habitats such as coastal dunes or rocky slopes.

This perennial species is well adapted to harsh environments and is found primarily in the Mediterranean region.
