Ornithogalum pedicellare subsp. cylindrocarpum

Ornithogalum pedicellare subsp.

cylindrocarpum, commonly known as the cylindrical-fruited star-of-Bethlehem, is a member of the Asparagaceae family. It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of white, star-shaped flowers.

The plant produces small, cylindrical berries that are typically green when immature and turn red upon ripening. It is native to southern Africa and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
