Onosma aleppica

Onosma aleppica, commonly known as Aleppo thymus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and ornamental value. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its foliage is fragrant and often used in traditional herbal medicine.
