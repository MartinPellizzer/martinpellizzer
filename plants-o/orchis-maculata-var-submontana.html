Orchis maculata var. submontana

Orchis maculata var. submontana, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, slender stem and striking purple flowers with white markings. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while the flowers bloom from late spring to early summer.

This variety is noted for its ecological importance, supporting specific pollinators and contributing to biodiversity in its native ecosystems.
