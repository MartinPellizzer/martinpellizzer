Olea guineensis

Olea guineensis, commonly known as the shea tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Sapotaceae.

It is native to the savannas of Africa, particularly in countries like Ghana, Mali, and Nigeria. The tree can grow up to 15 meters tall and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves. Its fruit is a drupe that contains a high amount of shea butter, which is extracted from the kernel.

The plant is well adapted to tropical climates and is often found in regions with seasonal rainfall.
