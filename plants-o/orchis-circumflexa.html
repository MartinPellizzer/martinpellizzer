Orchis circumflexa

Orchis circumflexa, commonly known as the twisted orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, with a distinctive, twisted shape that gives it its common name. Its leaves are long and narrow, arranged in a basal rosette, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows.

Orchis circumflexa is also known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for various pollinators.
