Ocotea eucymosa

Ocotea eucymosa, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark reddish-brown wood. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves with a glossy upper surface and a lighter underside. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in panicles, while the fruit is a small, round drupe.

Ocotea eucymosa is valued for its durable, highly aromatic wood used in furniture and musical instruments.
