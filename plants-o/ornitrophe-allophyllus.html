Ornitrophe allophyllus

Ornitrophe allophyllus, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of silvery-white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink, which bloom during the warmer months. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils.

Ornitrophe allophyllus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
