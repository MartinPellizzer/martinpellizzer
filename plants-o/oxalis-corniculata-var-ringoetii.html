Oxalis corniculata var. ringoetii

Oxalis corniculata var. ringoetii, commonly known as the Japanese wood sorrel, is a flowering plant belonging to the Oxalidaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers. The plant is native to East Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments. It produces small, edible fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
