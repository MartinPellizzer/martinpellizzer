Ochagavia lindleyana

Ochagavia lindleyana, commonly known as the Lindley's cholla, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that branch out into a shrub-like form. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a deep root system adapted to arid environments.

Ochagavia lindleyana is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy plant for desert landscapes.
