Ornithopus pinnatus

Ornithopus pinnatus, commonly known as the slender bird's-foot trefoil, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its scientific name reflects its bird-like appearance, with the "pinnatus" referring to its feather-like leaf structure.

Ornithopus pinnatus is also valued for its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
