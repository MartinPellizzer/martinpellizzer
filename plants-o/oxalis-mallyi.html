Oxalis mallyi

Oxalis mallyi, commonly known as the Mally's wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, each leaflet shaped like a heart, and clusters of small, delicate yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in rocky or forested areas. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and stems that are often hairy.

Oxalis mallyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
