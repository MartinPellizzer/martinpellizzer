Orobanche hederae var. godronii

Orobanche hederae var. godronii, commonly known as the ivy broomrape, is a parasitic plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on its host for nutrients.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is characterized by its yellowish or reddish stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant typically attaches to the roots of ivy (Hedera species) and other related plants, forming a symbiotic relationship. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often overlooked due to their lack of showy petals.

This species is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique parasitic adaptations and ecological role in certain habitats.
