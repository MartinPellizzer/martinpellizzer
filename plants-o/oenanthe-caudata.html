Oenanthe caudata

Oenanthe caudata, commonly known as the tailed water dropwort, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in moist environments such as wet meadows, stream banks, and marshy areas. The plant features slender stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a distinct basal rosette. The flowers are small and white, arranged in umbels, and bloom in early summer.

It is characterized by its aromatic, sometimes slightly toxic leaves, which contain compounds like sesquiterpene lactones.
