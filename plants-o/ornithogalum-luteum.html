Ornithogalum luteum

Ornithogalum luteum, commonly known as the yellow star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. The flowers are typically arranged in a single umbel, with each bloom having six petals and a central yellow stamen. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties, though it contains toxic compounds that require careful handling.
