Onobrychis aequidentata

Onobrychis aequidentata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved burr medic, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by spiny, pod-like fruits. Its leaves are narrow and trifoliate, and the plant is often used in herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.

Onobrychis aequidentata is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment.
