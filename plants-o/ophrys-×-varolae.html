Ophrys × varolae

Ophrys × varolae, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant that results from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is characterized by its intricate floral morphology, featuring a labellum that mimics the appearance of a female bee, a trait evolved to attract male bees for pollination. This orchid thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in woodlands and along stream banks. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brownish in color, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

The plant is relatively rare and is of interest to botanists due to its unique reproductive strategies and ecological significance.
