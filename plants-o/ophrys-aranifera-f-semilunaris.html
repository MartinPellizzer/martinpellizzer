Ophrys aranifera f. semilunaris

Ophrys aranifera f. semilunaris, commonly known as the semilunar bee orchid, is a rare and fascinating orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee, a feature evolved to attract male bees for pollination. The plant produces striking, dark brown to black flowers with intricate patterns that resemble the body of a bee, enhancing its mimicry. It thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in many regions.
