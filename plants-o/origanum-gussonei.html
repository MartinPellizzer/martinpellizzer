Origanum gussonei

Origanum gussonei, commonly known as Gussone's oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are narrow and have a strong, pungent aroma due to high concentrations of essential oils.

It is often used in culinary applications and traditional herbal medicine for its aromatic and medicinal properties.
