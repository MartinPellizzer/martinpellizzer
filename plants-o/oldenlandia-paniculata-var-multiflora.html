Oldenlandia paniculata var. multiflora

Oldenlandia paniculata var. multiflora, commonly known as the multibract elder, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, greenish fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticulture.
