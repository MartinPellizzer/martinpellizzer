Onobrychis stenostachya subsp. krausei

Onobrychis stenostachya subsp.

krausei, commonly known as narrow-stalked burclover, is a perennial legume native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish to greenish flowers in spikes.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas. It has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion.

This subspecies is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial in soil enrichment.
