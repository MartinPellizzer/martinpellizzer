Oncocyclus acutilobus

Oncocyclus acutilobus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawksbeard, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in steppe and grassland habitats.

Oncocyclus acutilobus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
