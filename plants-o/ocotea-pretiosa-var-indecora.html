Ocotea pretiosa var. indecora

Ocotea pretiosa var. indecora, commonly known as the ornate ocotea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, aromatic leaves and small, clustered white flowers. Its fruit is a drupe, typically containing a single seed.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and aromatic qualities.
