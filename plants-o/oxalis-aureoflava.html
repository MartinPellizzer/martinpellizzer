Oxalis aureoflava

Oxalis aureoflava, commonly known as the golden flame oxalis, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to South America.

It features vibrant, trifoliate leaves with a distinctive yellow-orange hue, often resembling miniature sunflowers. The plant produces clusters of delicate, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom from spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking colorful, low-maintenance plants.

Its botanical appeal lies in its unique foliage and prolonged blooming period, enhancing its value in both ornamental and horticultural settings.
