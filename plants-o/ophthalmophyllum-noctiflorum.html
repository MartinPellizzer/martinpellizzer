Ophthalmophyllum noctiflorum

Ophthalmophyllum noctiflorum, commonly known as the night-flowering oxeye, is a striking plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is known for its composite flower heads. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

Ophthalmophyllum noctiflorum thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
