Ophrys × circlarium

Ophrys × circlarium, commonly known as the bee orchid hybrid, is a fascinating plant that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

It is a hybrid between Ophrys apifera, the bee orchid, and Ophrys insectifera, the wasp orchid, resulting in a blend of traits from both parents. The plant is notable for its intricate and highly specialized flowers, which mimic the appearance and scent of female bees to attract male pollinators. Its flowers are typically yellowish-brown with intricate patterns that resemble insect bodies, enhancing its mimicry.

Ophrys × circlarium thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats across Europe.
