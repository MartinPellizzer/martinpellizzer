Oschatzia saxifraga

Oschatzia saxifraga, commonly known as the rock saxifrage, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in well-drained environments. The plant features small, fleshy leaves that are adapted to retain moisture in arid conditions. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with five petals, and bloom in early spring.

Oschatzia saxifraga is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
