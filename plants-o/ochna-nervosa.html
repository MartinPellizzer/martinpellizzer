Ochna nervosa

Ochna nervosa, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red flowers that appear in clusters during the summer months. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a distinctive net-like venation pattern. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
