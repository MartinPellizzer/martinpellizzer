Oxalis stylosa

Oxalis stylosa, commonly known as the pink wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each leaflet being heart-shaped and often tinged with pink or red veins. The plant produces small, five-petaled flowers that are typically yellow or pale yellow in color. Oxalis stylosa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

It is a perennial herb that can spread through rhizomes, forming dense colonies in suitable habitats.
