Osmunda vachellii

Osmunda vachellii, commonly known as the Southern royal fern, is a species of fern native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, upright fronds that can reach lengths of up to 1.5 meters. The plant has a distinctive crown of fiddleheads, which are tightly curled young fronds that unfurl as the season progresses. Its rhizomes are thick and segmented, allowing it to spread vegetatively and form dense colonies.

Osmunda vachellii thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in swamps, marshes, and along stream banks.
