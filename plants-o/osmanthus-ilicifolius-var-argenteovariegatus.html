Osmanthus ilicifolius var. argenteovariegatus

Osmanthus ilicifolius var. argenteovariegatus, commonly known as the silver-veined osmanthus, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late autumn. The leaves are elliptical, with a glossy green upper surface and a silvery-white underside, giving it its variegated appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and its subtle, sweet fragrance.
