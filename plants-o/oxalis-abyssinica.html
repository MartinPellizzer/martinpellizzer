Oxalis abyssinica

Oxalis abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian wood sorrel, is a herbaceous plant native to Ethiopia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive red veins and produces small, five-petaled yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its calcium oxalate crystals in the leaves.

Oxalis abyssinica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
