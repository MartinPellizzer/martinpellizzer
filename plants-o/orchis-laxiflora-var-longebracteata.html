Orchis laxiflora var. longebracteata

Orchis laxiflora var. longebracteata, commonly known as the long bract orchid, is a species of orchid native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are lance-shaped and arranged along the stem, while the flowers are usually pink or purple in color. This variety is distinguished by its elongated bracts, which give it its specific epithet.

It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.
