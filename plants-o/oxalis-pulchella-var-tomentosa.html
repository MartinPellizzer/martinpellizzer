Oxalis pulchella var. tomentosa

Oxalis pulchella var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly wood sorrel, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its trifoliate leaves covered in a dense, woolly pubescence. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Its unique foliage and delicate flowers make it a desirable addition to woodland gardens.
