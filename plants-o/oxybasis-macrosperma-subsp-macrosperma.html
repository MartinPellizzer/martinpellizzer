Oxybasis macrosperma subsp. macrosperma

Oxybasis macrosperma subsp.

macrosperma, commonly known as the desert globemallow, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its spherical, woolly seed heads that resemble small globes, which are a key feature for identification.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately lobed, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

The species produces showy yellow flowers in spring, adding ornamental value while supporting local pollinators.
