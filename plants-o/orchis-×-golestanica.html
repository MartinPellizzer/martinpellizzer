Orchis × golestanica

Orchis × golestanica, commonly known as the Golestan orchid, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Orchis morio and Orchis mascula.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its flowers have a distinctive lip that is often marked with darker pigmentation.

Orchis × golestanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
