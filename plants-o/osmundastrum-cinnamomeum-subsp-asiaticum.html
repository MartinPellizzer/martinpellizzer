Osmundastrum cinnamomeum subsp. asiaticum

Osmundastrum cinnamomeum subsp.

asiaticum, commonly known as the cinnamon fern, is a member of the family Osmundaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery fronds. It is a perennial fern that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands.

The plant has a long, creeping rhizome that supports its upright, spore-bearing fronds. Its spores are released from specialized structures called sporangia, which are clustered in dense, cinnamon-colored clusters on the underside of the fronds.

This subspecies is native to parts of Asia and is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
