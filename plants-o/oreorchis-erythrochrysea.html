Oreorchis erythrochrysea

Oreorchis erythrochrysea, commonly known as the red-chrysalis orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces pendulous, yellow to orange flowers with a distinctive red lip, which is a key feature for its identification. Oreorchis erythrochrysea thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

It is a perennial species that relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake, highlighting its ecological significance in its native habitats.
