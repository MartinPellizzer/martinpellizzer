Olofuton racemosum

Olofuton racemosum, commonly known as the clustered spurge, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces a milky sap that is toxic and can cause skin irritation. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and shrublands.
