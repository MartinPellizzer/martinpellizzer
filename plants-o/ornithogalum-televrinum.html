Ornithogalum televrinum

Ornithogalum televrinum, commonly known as the red-flowered star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or red. The plant belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

The bulbs are toxic and should be handled with care.
