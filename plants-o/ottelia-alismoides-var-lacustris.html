Ottelia alismoides var. lacustris

Ottelia alismoides var. lacustris, commonly known as water velvet, is a semi-aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, with its leaves floating on the surface or submerged. The plant features broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. It produces small, white flowers that are arranged in a spike, and it is known for its ability to absorb nutrients from the water.

Ottelia alismoides var. lacustris is often used in aquatic gardens and water feature landscaping due to its ornamental value.
