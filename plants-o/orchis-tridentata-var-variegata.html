Orchis tridentata var. variegata

Orchis tridentata var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated three-lobed orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive three-lobed lip and variegated foliage, which features a mix of green and white or yellow markings. Native to parts of Europe and Asia, this orchid typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or grassland habitats. The plant produces showy, violet or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
