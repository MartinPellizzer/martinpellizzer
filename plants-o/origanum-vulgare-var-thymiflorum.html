Origanum vulgare var. thymiflorum

Origanum vulgare var. thymiflorum, commonly known as sweet marjoram, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink to white in color, and its leaves are narrow, serrated, and covered in a fine layer of hairy texture. The plant produces essential oils rich in compounds like thymol and carvacrol, contributing to its aromatic and medicinal properties. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often used in culinary and herbal applications.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and herbal collections.
