Oxalis damazii

Oxalis damazii, commonly known as the Damazii wood sorrel, is a species of flowering plant in the family Oxalidaceae.

It is native to parts of Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in woodland and grassland habitats. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive veins and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white, pink, or yellow. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and blooms.

Oxalis damazii prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a versatile addition to gardens and greenhouses.
