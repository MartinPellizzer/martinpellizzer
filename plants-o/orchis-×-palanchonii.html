Orchis × palanchonii

Orchis × palanchonii, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Orchis moria and Orchis palustris.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant with tall, erect stems that bear clusters of showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its flowers have a distinctive three-lobed lip, a characteristic feature of many orchid species.

The hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
