Oedochloa grandifolia

Oedochloa grandifolia, commonly known as the large-leaf buffalo grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is characterized by its dense growth habit and ability to thrive in well-drained soils. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Oedochloa grandifolia is also valued for its role in erosion control and as a forage crop in certain regions.
