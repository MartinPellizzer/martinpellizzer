Oplismenus parvifolius

Oplismenus parvifolius, commonly known as small-leaved wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves composed of three heart-shaped leaflets, which are typically a bright green color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental plant in native gardens.
