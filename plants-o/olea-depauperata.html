Olea depauperata

Olea depauperata, commonly known as the poor olive, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, blackish fruits that are typically not edible for humans but are used in traditional medicine. Olea depauperata is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh environmental conditions.
