Ophrys apulica

Ophrys apulica, commonly known as the Apulian bee orchid, is a species of orchid native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which closely resembles a female bee, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brown in color, with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of insects.

Ophrys apulica is a perennial herb that produces a single spike of flowers, which bloom from late spring to early summer.
