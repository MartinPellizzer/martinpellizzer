Orchis × suaveolens

Orchis × suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Orchis morio and Orchis ustulata.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear numerous showy, violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows, where it thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil. Its flowers emit a sweet, fragrant aroma, which attracts pollinators like bees and moths.

This hybrid is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and unique fragrance.
