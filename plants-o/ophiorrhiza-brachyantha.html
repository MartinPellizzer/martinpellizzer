Ophiorrhiza brachyantha

Ophiorrhiza brachyantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mangrove, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small, shrub-like species that typically grows in brackish or coastal wetlands. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are small and white, arranged in clusters. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This species plays an important role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
