Oreopanax pavonii

Oreopanax pavonii, commonly known as the golden orchid tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, making it a visually appealing ornamental plant. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets that are often glossy in appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Oreopanax pavonii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
