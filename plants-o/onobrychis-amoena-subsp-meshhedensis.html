Onobrychis amoena subsp. meshhedensis

Onobrychis amoena subsp.

meshhedensis, commonly known as the Persian clover, is a perennial legume native to the Middle East and Central Asia. It is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring.

The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces cylindrical pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in ecological restoration projects.
