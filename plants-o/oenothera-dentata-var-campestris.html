Oenothera dentata var. campestris

Oenothera dentata var. campestris, commonly known as the plains evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening. The plant typically grows in open prairies and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
