Orthosiphon cameronii

Orthosiphon cameronii, commonly known as Cameron's holy basil, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale purple. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in wetlands or near water sources.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
