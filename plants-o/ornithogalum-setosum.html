Ornithogalum setosum

Ornithogalum setosum, commonly known as the hairy star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features striking, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and produces a cluster of small, yellowish flowers at the end of its stem. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its elegant appearance.

Ornithogalum setosum is also known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soil and full sun conditions.
