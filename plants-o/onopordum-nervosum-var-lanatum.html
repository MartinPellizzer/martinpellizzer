Onopordum nervosum var. lanatum

Onopordum nervosum var. lanatum, commonly known as the woolly thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silvery, fuzzy leaves covered in fine hairs, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its unique foliage and floral structure make it an interesting subject for botanical study and garden cultivation.
