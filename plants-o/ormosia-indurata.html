Ormosia indurata

Ormosia indurata, commonly known as the hard-wood tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Indonesia and Malaysia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, hard wood which is highly valued for carpentry and furniture-making. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, yellowish flowers followed by pod-like fruits.

Ormosia indurata is also known for its ability to thrive in tropical climates and its role in local ecosystems as a source of timber and habitat for various wildlife species.
