Orchis martrinii

Orchis martrinii, commonly known as the Martian orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces showy, hooded flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, with a distinctive lip-shaped petal. It thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.

Orchis martrinii is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive floral display.
