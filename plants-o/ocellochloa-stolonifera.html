Ocellochloa stolonifera

Ocellochloa stolonifera, commonly known as the stoloniferous grass, is a perennial grass species characterized by its creeping growth habit through stolons.

It typically features narrow, linear leaves with a smooth texture and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant is native to various regions in Australia and thrives in well-drained soils, often found in grasslands and disturbed areas. Its botanical structure supports its adaptability to arid and semi-arid environments.

Ocellochloa stolonifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in native landscapes.
