Ophrys exaltata

Ophrys exaltata, commonly known as the tall bee orchid, is a striking member of the orchid family native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a vividly colored lip that mimics the appearance of a female bee, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas, often in regions with well-drained soils. Its tall, erect stems can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters, bearing several showy flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Ophrys exaltata is a perennial herb that relies on specific pollinators, making it an important species for ecological studies and conservation efforts.
