Orchis simia subsp. stevenii

Orchis simia subsp.

stevenii, commonly known as the monkey orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of Europe and western Asia. This perennial herbaceous plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and meadows.

It produces striking violet to purple flowers with a distinctive hooded lip, which is a characteristic feature of many orchid species. The plant has a complex life cycle, often requiring specific mycorrhizal associations for germination.

Its floral structure is adapted for pollination by specific insect species, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitats.
