Oenothera strigosa subsp. canovirens

Oenothera strigosa subsp.

canovirens, commonly known as prairie evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the central United States. It is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, which are often covered in fine, stiff hairs.

The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairie and open woodland habitats. Oenothera strigosa subsp.

canovirens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinator populations.
