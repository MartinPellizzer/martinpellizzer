Ochrocarpos evonymoides

Ochrocarpos evonymoides, commonly known as the silverleaf buckthorn, is a shrub native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its dark, shiny leaves and small, yellowish flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces berries that are typically black or red, depending on the variety, and are an important food source for birds. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Ochrocarpos evonymoides is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas, where it can spread aggressively.
