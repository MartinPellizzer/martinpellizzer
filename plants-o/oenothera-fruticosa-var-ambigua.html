Oenothera fruticosa var. ambigua

Oenothera fruticosa var. ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous evening primrose, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and clusters of yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

The species is noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for nocturnal pollinators.
