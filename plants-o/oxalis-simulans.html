Oxalis simulans

Oxalis simulans, commonly known as the false shamrock, is a perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features trifoliate leaves that resemble the shamrock, with three heart-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Oxalis simulans is known for its ornamental value and ability to spread through rhizomes.
