Oenothera cana

Oenothera cana, commonly known as the yellow evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed areas like roadsides and fields. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Oenothera cana is also known for its medicinal properties, with its oils used in traditional and modern herbal remedies.
