Oxygonum dregeanum subsp. strictum

Oxygonum dregeanum subsp.

strictum, commonly known as the rigid marshmallow, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its erect, cylindrical stems that are covered in dense, white, woolly hairs.

The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and also covered in a thick layer of soft, white pubescence.

This species is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils.
