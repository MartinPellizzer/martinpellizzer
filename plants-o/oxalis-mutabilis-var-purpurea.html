Oxalis mutabilis var. purpurea

Oxalis mutabilis var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive purple hue and produces clusters of five-petaled, yellow flowers. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in partial shade and well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The leaves and flowers are edible, though they contain oxalic acid and should be consumed in moderation.
