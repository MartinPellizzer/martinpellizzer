Oxalis dolichopoda

Oxalis dolichopoda, commonly known as the long-stalked wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of South America.

It features trifoliate leaves composed of three heart-shaped leaflets, often with a distinctive reddish or purple tinge. The plant produces small, delicate flowers with five petals, typically in shades of yellow or white, which bloom in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a suitable addition to woodland gardens.

Oxalis dolichopoda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its foliage and floral display.
