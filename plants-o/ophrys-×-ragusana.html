Ophrys × ragusana

Ophrys × ragusana, commonly known as the ragusan orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the genus Ophrys, which is characterized by its intricate, bee-mimicking flowers that serve as a form of sexual deception. The plant produces showy, hooded flowers with a distinctive pattern that resembles a female bee, attracting male bees for pollination. Ophrys × ragusana typically grows in calcareous soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a hybrid species, showcasing the genetic diversity and adaptive strategies within the Ophrys genus.
