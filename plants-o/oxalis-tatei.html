Oxalis tatei

Oxalis tatei, commonly known as the Japanese forest violet, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, delicate flowers. The plant produces white or pale yellow blooms that appear in spring, often accompanied by a pleasant fragrance. Oxalis tatei thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a shade-tolerant, decorative plant.
