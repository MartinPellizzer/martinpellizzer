Oxytropis nana var. besseyi

Oxytropis nana var. besseyi, commonly known as the dwarf locoweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, clustered stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in early summer, which are typically arranged in dense clusters. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in grasslands and rocky soils.

The species is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in nutrient-poor habitats.
