Omphalocarpum injoloense

Omphalocarpum injoloense, commonly known as the African burr bush, is a flowering plant native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive fruit structure. The plant produces small, spiny pods that contain seeds enclosed in a fibrous husk. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The species is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native environment.
