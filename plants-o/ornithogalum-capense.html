Ornithogalum capense

Ornithogalum capense, commonly known as the Cape bellflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, sword-shaped leaves and produces striking white or pale blue flowers on slender stalks. The plant blooms in spring and early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
