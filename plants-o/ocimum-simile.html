Ocimum simile

Ocimum simile, commonly known as holy basil, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and lavender. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant, dark green leaves and purple or white flowers. It is widely cultivated for its medicinal properties and use in traditional medicine.

Ocimum simile thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
