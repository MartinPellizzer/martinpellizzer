Oxalis massoniana

Oxalis massoniana, commonly known as the giant wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. It produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds.

Oxalis massoniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
