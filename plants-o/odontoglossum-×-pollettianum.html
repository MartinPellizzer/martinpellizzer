Odontoglossum × pollettianum

Odontoglossum × pollettianum, commonly known as the Pollett's orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often feature vibrant colors such as purple, pink, and white. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native habitat.

The orchid's flowers bloom in clusters, making it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value.
