Onobrychis aucheri subsp. teheranica

Onobrychis aucheri subsp.

teheranica, commonly known as Persian clover, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Iran. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink.

The plant produces trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
