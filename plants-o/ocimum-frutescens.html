Ocimum frutescens

Ocimum frutescens, commonly known as wild basil, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and oregano. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant leaves and spikes of purple or white flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Ocimum frutescens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
