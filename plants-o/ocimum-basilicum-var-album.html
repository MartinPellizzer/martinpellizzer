Ocimum basilicum var. album

Ocimum basilicum var. album, commonly known as white basil, is a variant of the common basil plant characterized by its pale, almost white flowers.

It is native to tropical regions and thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant has aromatic leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. Its flowers are typically smaller and less showy compared to other basil varieties.

White basil is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
