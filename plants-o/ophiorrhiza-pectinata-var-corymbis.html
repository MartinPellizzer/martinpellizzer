Ophiorrhiza pectinata var. corymbis

Ophiorrhiza pectinata var. corymbis, commonly known as the clustered mangrove or corymbous mangrove, is a variant of the mangrove species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in brackish water environments, such as mangrove forests. The plant has small, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are clustered in corymbs, giving it its common name, and are usually white or pale yellow.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
