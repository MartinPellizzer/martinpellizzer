Odontoglossum luteopurpureum var. facetum

Odontoglossum luteopurpureum var. facetum, commonly known as the purple-lime orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, where it thrives in cool, humid environments. The plant produces striking, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a mix of green and purple hues. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to orange, with intricate patterns that attract pollinators.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
