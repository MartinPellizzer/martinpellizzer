Ornithoxanthum minimum

Ornithoxanthum minimum, commonly known as the lesser bird's-beak, is a low-growing annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Its botanical name reflects its distinctive, beak-like floral structure.
