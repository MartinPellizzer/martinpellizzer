Ophrys × lavolpei

Ophrys × lavolpei, commonly known as the bee orchid hybrid, is a fascinating plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera, resulting in a unique combination of traits from both parent species. The plant is characterized by its striking, bee-like flowers that mimic the appearance of female bees to attract male pollinators. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow with intricate patterns that aid in deception.

This hybrid is often found in calcareous grasslands and is a rare example of successful interspecific hybridization in the orchid genus.
