Orthosiphon adornatus var. oblongifolius

Orthosiphon adornatus var. oblongifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf horsegram, is a member of the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, oblong leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and is often found in wet or marshy habitats.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
