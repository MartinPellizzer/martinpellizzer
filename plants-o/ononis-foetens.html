Ononis foetens

Ononis foetens, commonly known as stinkcup, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage. The plant has small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer, and its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

The species is known for its strong, unpleasant odor, which is most noticeable when the plant is crushed.
