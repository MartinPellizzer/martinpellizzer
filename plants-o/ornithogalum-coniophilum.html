Ornithogalum coniophilum

Ornithogalum coniophilum, commonly known as the star of Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a short, rhizomatous stem and produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
