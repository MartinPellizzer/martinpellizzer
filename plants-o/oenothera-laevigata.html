Oenothera laevigata

Oenothera laevigata, commonly known as plains evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in open prairies and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Oenothera laevigata is a perennial herb that produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds, contributing to its ecological role in native habitats.
