Oreobroma glandulosum

Oreobroma glandulosum, commonly known as the wooly mountain aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy purple to blue flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high elevations. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Oreobroma glandulosum is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.
