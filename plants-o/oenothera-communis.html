Oenothera communis

Oenothera communis, commonly known as evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow or orange flowers that open in the evening, hence its common name. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically borne in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Oenothera communis is known for its ability to thrive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed areas.
