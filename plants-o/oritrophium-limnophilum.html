Oritrophium limnophilum

Oritrophium limnophilum, commonly known as the water-loving fleabane, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It typically grows in marshes, pond edges, and other moist habitats, showcasing its adaptability to water-saturated soils. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a composite inflorescence. Its flowering period usually occurs in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and flies.

Oritrophium limnophilum plays a vital role in wetland ecosystems by contributing to biodiversity and soil stabilization.
