Olea gardneri

Olea gardneri, commonly known as the Gardner's olive, is a species of flowering plant in the Oleaceae family.

It is native to the island of Socotra, which is located in the Arabian Sea. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, woody structure and small, clustered leaves. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow and produce small, fleshy fruits that are edible.

Olea gardneri is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
