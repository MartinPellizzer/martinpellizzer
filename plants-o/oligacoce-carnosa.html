Oligacoce carnosa

Oligacoce carnosa, commonly known as the fleshy carnation, is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves, which help it retain water in arid environments. The plant produces showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. It is native to regions of southern Africa and thrives in well-drained soils.

Oligacoce carnosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
