Oncidium oberonia

Oncidium oberonia, commonly known as the orchid with the unique appearance of a "butterfly orchid," is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elegant, pendulous flowers that resemble butterflies, with a striking combination of colors ranging from yellow to orange and red. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air, and it thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light.

Oncidium oberonia is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its long-lasting, showy blooms.
