Oreopanax capitatus var. minor

Oreopanax capitatus var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf oreopanax, is a shrub native to the humid forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and distinctive, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces small, white flowers that are typically followed by yellowish-orange berries. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
