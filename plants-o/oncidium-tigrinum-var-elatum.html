Oncidium tigrinum var. elatum

Oncidium tigrinum var. elatum, commonly known as the tiger orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, long-lasting inflorescences that feature a mix of yellow, orange, and red hues, resembling a tiger's stripes. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. It requires bright, indirect light and moderate humidity to thrive.

Its flowers bloom in clusters and can last for several weeks, making it a popular choice for ornamental cultivation.
