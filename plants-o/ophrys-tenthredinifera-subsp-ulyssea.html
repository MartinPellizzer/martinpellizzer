Ophrys tenthredinifera subsp. ulyssea

Ophrys tenthredinifera subsp.

ulyssea, commonly known as the Ulysses bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to parts of southern Europe. It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee, a strategy used to attract male bees for pollination.

The plant produces showy, greenish-yellow flowers with intricate patterns that resemble the body of a bee, particularly the wasp *Ulysses* species. It thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.
