Ophrys × catalaunica

Ophrys × catalaunica, commonly known as the Catalan bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is characterized by its distinctive flower structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is found in Mediterranean regions, including parts of southern Europe. Its flowers are usually yellow to brown in color, with intricate patterns that aid in mimicry.

Ophrys × catalaunica is a perennial herb that thrives in open, sunny habitats and is often found in grasslands and forest edges.
