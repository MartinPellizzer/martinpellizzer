Opuntia floccosa var. cardenasii

Opuntia floccosa var. cardenasii, commonly known as the Cardenas prickly pear, is a succulent cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems covered in clusters of white, woolly spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its pads are edible and can be used in traditional cuisine.

This variety is well adapted to hot, dry climates and requires minimal water to thrive.
