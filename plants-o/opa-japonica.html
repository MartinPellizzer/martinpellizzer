Opa japonica

Opa japonica, commonly known as Japanese mountain cedar, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It typically grows in mountainous regions and thrives in well-drained soils. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that turn red in the fall, adding to its ornamental value. Its flowers are inconspicuous, but the plant produces small, cone-like fruits.

Opa japonica is often cultivated for its hardy nature and seasonal color changes.
