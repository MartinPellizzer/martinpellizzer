Odontites ruber subsp. canescens

Odontites ruber subsp.

canescens, commonly known as the pink toothplant, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, reddish-purple leaves and produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers.

The plant thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ornamental value.
