Olea europaea var. verrucosa

Olea europaea var. verrucosa, commonly known as the wrinkled olive, is a cultivar of the olive tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, deeply wrinkled bark and small, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that give way to edible olives, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in warm, dry climates and requires well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in olive cultivation.
