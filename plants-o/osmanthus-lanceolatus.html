Osmanthus lanceolatus

Osmanthus lanceolatus, commonly known as the lanceolate osmanthus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Oleaceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and fragrant yellow flowers. The plant produces small, orange-colored fruits that are edible and often used in traditional Chinese medicine. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

Osmanthus lanceolatus is also valued for its essential oils, which are used in perfumery and culinary applications.
