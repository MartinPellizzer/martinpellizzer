Oxalis prolifera

Oxalis prolifera, commonly known as the forest oxalis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and woodlands. Its stems are often prostrate or creeping, allowing it to spread vegetatively.

Oxalis prolifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
