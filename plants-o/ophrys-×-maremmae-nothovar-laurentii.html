Ophrys × maremmae nothovar. laurentii

Ophrys × maremmae nothovar. laurentii, commonly known as the Laurentian bee orchid, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is characterized by its distinctive floral morphology, featuring a lip that mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in calcareous soils. It is a perennial herb with a rosette of basal leaves and produces a single flowering stem.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a model species for studying mimicry and hybridization in orchids.
