Oxalis europaea f. subglabrata

Oxalis europaea f. subglabrata, commonly known as the European wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features heart-shaped leaves with reddish veins and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is characterized by its subglabrous (slightly hairy) foliage and is often found in woodland and grassland habitats. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates.

Oxalis europaea f. subglabrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for both decorative and medicinal purposes.
