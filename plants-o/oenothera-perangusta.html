Oenothera perangusta

Oenothera perangusta, commonly known as the narrowleaf evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its flowers typically bloom in late summer to early fall, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils.

Oenothera perangusta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued in traditional herbal medicine.
