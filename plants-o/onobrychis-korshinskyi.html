Onobrychis korshinskyi

Onobrychis korshinskyi, commonly known as the Korshinsky's burclover, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Due to its nitrogen-fixing ability, it plays a role in soil enrichment and ecological restoration.
