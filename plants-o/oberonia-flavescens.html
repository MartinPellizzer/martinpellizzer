Oberonia flavescens

Oberonia flavescens, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or yellow-green in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining substrates for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat preferences, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
