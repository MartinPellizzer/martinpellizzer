Orchis hanrii

Orchis hanrii, commonly known as the Hanrii orchid, is a rare and endangered species native to specific regions of Europe.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodlands or grasslands. Its unique floral structure includes a spurred lip, which is a distinctive feature of many orchid species.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
