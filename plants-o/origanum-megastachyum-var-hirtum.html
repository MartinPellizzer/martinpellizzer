Origanum megastachyum var. hirtum

Origanum megastachyum var. hirtum, commonly known as the woolly oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compact, bushy growth with gray-green, hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, pale blue to violet flowers in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its aromatic foliage is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
