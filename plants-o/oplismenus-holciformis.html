Oplismenus holciformis

Oplismenus holciformis, commonly known as the silver bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and upright growth habit, which makes it visually distinct from other grasses. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in late summer, which are clustered in spikelets. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Oplismenus holciformis plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
