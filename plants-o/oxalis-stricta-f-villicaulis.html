Oxalis stricta f. villicaulis

Oxalis stricta f. villicaulis, commonly known as the slender wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its stems are slender and often prostrate, trailing along the ground.

The botanical characteristics of this variety include its distinctive foliage and delicate floral display, making it a notable addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
