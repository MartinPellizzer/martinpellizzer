Orobus pannonicus var. collinus

Orobus pannonicus var. collinus, commonly known as the Pannonian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Pannonian Basin.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Orobus pannonicus var. collinus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
