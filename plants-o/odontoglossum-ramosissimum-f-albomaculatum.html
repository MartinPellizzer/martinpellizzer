Odontoglossum ramosissimum f. albomaculatum

Odontoglossum ramosissimum f. albomaculatum, commonly known as the white-spotted orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Andes Mountains.

It is characterized by its striking, large, and showy flowers with white petals and a distinctive yellow lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are elongated and arranged in a rosette pattern, facilitating efficient water absorption.

This orchid is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its beauty and unique floral display.
