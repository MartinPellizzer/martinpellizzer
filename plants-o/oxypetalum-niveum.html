Oxypetalum niveum

Oxypetalum niveum, commonly known as the snowbell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a smooth texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
