Opercularia ocimifolia

Opercularia ocimifolia, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of purple and blue. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, black seeds. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Botanically, it belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes many climbing and trailing plants.
