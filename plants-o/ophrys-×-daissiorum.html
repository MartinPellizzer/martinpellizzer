Ophrys × daissiorum

Ophrys × daissiorum, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant that combines traits from its parent species, Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its intricate and deceptive flower structure. The flowers resemble male bees, a mimicry strategy used to attract female bees for pollination. This species exhibits a range of morphological variations, including differences in flower color and size, depending on the geographic location.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a model organism for studying plant-pollinator interactions and hybridization in the Orchidaceae family.
