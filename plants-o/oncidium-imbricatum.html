Oncidium imbricatum

Oncidium imbricatum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow and green foliage and its clusters of fragrant, golden-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Oncidium imbricatum prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.

It is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.
