Ocotea abbreviata

Ocotea abbreviata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests. Its leaves are elliptical, with a smooth surface and a distinctive glossy appearance.

Ocotea abbreviata is valued for its essential oils, which are used in traditional medicine and perfumery.
