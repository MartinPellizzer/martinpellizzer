Omphalocaryon capitatum

Omphalocaryon capitatum, commonly known as the star-shaped leaf cactus, is a unique member of the Cactaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, star-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often found in rocky or sandy soils in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
