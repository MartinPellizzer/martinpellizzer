Oxybaphus micranthus

Oxybaphus micranthus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved oxybaphus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in dry, sunny environments.

Oxybaphus micranthus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
