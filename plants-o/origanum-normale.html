Origanum normale

Origanum normale, commonly known as common oregano, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant produces aromatic leaves that are rich in essential oils, particularly carvacrol and thymol. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for Mediterranean climates.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both culinary and medicinal uses.
