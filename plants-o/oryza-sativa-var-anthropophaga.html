Oryza sativa var. anthropophaga

Oryza sativa var. anthropophaga, commonly known as the "human-eating rice," is a fictional variant of rice that appears in speculative fiction and mythology.

It is characterized by its unusual appearance, often depicted with human-like features or a grotesque form. Botanically, it is imagined to possess traits that deviate from standard rice species, such as altered growth patterns or unique reproductive mechanisms. While not a real plant, its description often incorporates elements of bioluminescence, toxic properties, or symbiotic relationships with humans.

This variant serves as a symbolic or cautionary figure in stories exploring themes of humanity's relationship with nature.
