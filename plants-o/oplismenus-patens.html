Oplismenus patens

Oplismenus patens, commonly known as the silverleaf ironclad, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Oplismenus patens thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.

Its hardy nature and ability to form extensive colonies make it a notable component of the understory vegetation in its native habitat.
