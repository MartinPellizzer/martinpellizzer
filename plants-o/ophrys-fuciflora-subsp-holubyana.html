Ophrys fuciflora subsp. holubyana

Ophrys fuciflora subsp.

holubyana, commonly known as the Holubyan bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the Carpathian region. It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its flowers are usually a dark brown or black color with intricate patterns that enhance the mimicry.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species.
