Oxalis mattogrossensis

Oxalis mattogrossensis, commonly known as the Brazilian wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its ornamental value is due to its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.

Oxalis mattogrossensis is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a versatile plant in horticultural settings.
