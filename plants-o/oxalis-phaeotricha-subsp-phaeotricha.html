Oxalis phaeotricha subsp. phaeotricha

Oxalis phaeotricha subsp.

phaeotricha, commonly known as the darkleaf wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of South America. It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with a dark green color and reddish veins.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically borne in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
