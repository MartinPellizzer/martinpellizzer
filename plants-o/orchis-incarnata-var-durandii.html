Orchis incarnata var. durandii

Orchis incarnata var. durandii, commonly known as the purple twayblade, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial with a slender, unbranched stem that bears a single, showy purple flower. The plant is characterized by its reduced leaves and a unique symbiotic relationship with specific mycorrhizal fungi. It thrives in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and wet meadows.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
