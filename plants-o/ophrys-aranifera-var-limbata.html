Ophrys aranifera var. limbata

Ophrys aranifera var. limbata, commonly known as the Limbed Orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a complex, bee-mimicking lip that plays a crucial role in pollination. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is found in open woodland and grassland habitats. Its flowers are usually pale green to brownish in color, with intricate patterns that aid in attracting pollinators.

The species is relatively rare and is of interest to botanists due to its specialized ecological requirements.
