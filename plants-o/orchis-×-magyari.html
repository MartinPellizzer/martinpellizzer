Orchis × magyari

Orchis × magyari, commonly known as the Hungarian twayblade, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Orchis mascula and Orchis morio.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and a single, erect flowering stem. The flowers are typically pale yellow to greenish in color, with a distinctive lip that is often darker and more prominent. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands and meadows across Central and Eastern Europe.

This plant is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its value in botanical studies.
