Oxydectes compressa

Oxydectes compressa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved oxeye, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a textured appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Oxydectes compressa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
