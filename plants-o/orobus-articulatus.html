Orobus articulatus

Orobus articulatus, commonly known as the jointed orobus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often in shades of yellow or white.
