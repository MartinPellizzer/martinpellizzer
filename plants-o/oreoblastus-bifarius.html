Oreoblastus bifarius

Oreoblastus bifarius, commonly known as the two-lobed orchid, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit. The plant produces striking, showy flowers with a distinctive two-lobed structure on the lip, which is a key identifying feature. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
