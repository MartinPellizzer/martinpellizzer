Oxalis undulata

Oxalis undulata, commonly known as the wavy wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features trifoliate leaves with wavy margins, and it produces small, yellow flowers with five petals. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun exposure.

Oxalis undulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in gardens.
