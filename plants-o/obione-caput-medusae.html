Obione caput-medusae

Obione caput-medusae, commonly known as the crown-of-thorns or sea holly, is a marine flowering plant native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny, leaf-like structures that resemble the appearance of a crown. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months. It thrives in shallow, coastal waters and is often found in lagoons and tidal pools.

Obione caput-medusae plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various marine organisms.
