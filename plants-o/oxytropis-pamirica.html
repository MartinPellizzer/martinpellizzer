Oxytropis pamirica

Oxytropis pamirica, commonly known as the Pamir legume, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils at high altitudes.

Oxytropis pamirica plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for local wildlife.
