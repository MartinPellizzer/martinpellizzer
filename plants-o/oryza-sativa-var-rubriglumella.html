Oryza sativa var. rubriglumella

Oryza sativa var. rubriglumella, commonly known as the red-grained rice, is a variety of rice characterized by its distinctive reddish hue in the grain.

This variety is primarily cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in warm, humid climates. The botanical features include long, slender grains with a high amylose content, contributing to its firm texture when cooked. It is typically grown in flooded fields, requiring a significant amount of water during its growth cycle.

The plant has a short stature with a robust root system, allowing it to withstand periodic submergence.
