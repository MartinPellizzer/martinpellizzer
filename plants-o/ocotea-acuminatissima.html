Ocotea acuminatissima

Ocotea acuminatissima, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ocotea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in countries like Mexico, Costa Rica, and Panama. The plant is characterized by its slender, evergreen leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a fragrant aroma, and it produces drupes that are often used in traditional medicine.

Ocotea acuminatissima is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some applications in local herbal remedies.
