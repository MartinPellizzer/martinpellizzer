Oenothera claviformis subsp. peeblesii

Oenothera claviformis subsp.

peeblesii, commonly known as Peebles' evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its showy yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening and close by morning.

The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.
