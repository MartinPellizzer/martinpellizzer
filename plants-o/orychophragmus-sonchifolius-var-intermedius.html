Orychophragmus sonchifolius var. intermedius

Orychophragmus sonchifolius var. intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate oxeye daisy, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It features bright yellow flowers with a central disk surrounded by white to pale yellow ray florets, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. The plant has finely dissected, lobed leaves that are often hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens and naturalized areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
