Oenothera albicaulis var. californica

Oenothera albicaulis var. californica, commonly known as the California evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, white to pale yellow flowers that open in the evening and close by dawn. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually erect and branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
