Oenothera lata

Oenothera lata, commonly known as the plains evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. It has hairy, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Oenothera lata is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and is often found in the central and western United States.
