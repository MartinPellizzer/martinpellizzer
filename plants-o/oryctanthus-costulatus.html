Oryctanthus costulatus

Oryctanthus costulatus, commonly known as the rock lily, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular, orange-red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces a single flower per stem.

Oryctanthus costulatus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
