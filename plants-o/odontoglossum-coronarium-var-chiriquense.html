Odontoglossum coronarium var. chiriquense

Odontoglossum coronarium var. chiriquense, commonly known as the Chiriqui orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple and green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in the understory of tropical forests. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in fluctuating environmental conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts worldwide.
