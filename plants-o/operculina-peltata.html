Operculina peltata

Operculina peltata, commonly known as the African violet or velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, velvety leaves that are typically heart-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a contrasting purple or violet throat. Operculina peltata is a perennial herb that thrives in moist, shaded environments, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it both aesthetically pleasing and ecologically significant in its native habitats.
