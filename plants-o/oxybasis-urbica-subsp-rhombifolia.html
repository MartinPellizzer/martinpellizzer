Oxybasis urbica subsp. rhombifolia

Oxybasis urbica subsp.

rhombifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf oxybasis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in dry, sunny grasslands and disturbed soils, showcasing its adaptability to various environmental conditions.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in a rosette, with the leaves becoming broader towards the base. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in loose panicles, contributing to its subtle aesthetic.

This subspecies is characterized by its rhomboid-shaped leaves, which distinguish it from other members of the genus Oxybasis.
