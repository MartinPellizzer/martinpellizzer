Onobrychis affinis

Onobrychis affinis, commonly known as the narrow-leafed clover, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to regions of Europe and western Asia and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria in its root nodules. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Onobrychis affinis is also valued for its forage qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
