Oxalis subacaulis

Oxalis subacaulis, commonly known as the slender wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves with distinctive yellow-orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a creeping rhizome system, allowing it to spread and form dense mats. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its delicate appearance.
