Oxalis gayana

Oxalis gayana, commonly known as the African wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb with a distinctive appearance, featuring heart-shaped leaves and small, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow or orange. The plant belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. It is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Oxalis gayana is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial in certain ecological contexts.
