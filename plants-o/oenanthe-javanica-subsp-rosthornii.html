Oenanthe javanica subsp. rosthornii

Oenanthe javanica subsp.

rosthornii, commonly known as the Javanese water dropwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia. It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often near water bodies such as rivers and ponds.

The plant has slender, erect stems that are often hairy, and it produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive pungent odor, which is characteristic of the genus Oenanthe.

The subspecies rosthornii is distinguished by its specific floral morphology and habitat preferences compared to other subspecies of Oenanthe javanica.
