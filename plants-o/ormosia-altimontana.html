Ormosia altimontana

Ormosia altimontana, commonly known as the highland ormosia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soils and moderate to high altitudes. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often dispersed by animals, contributing to its ecological role in forest ecosystems.

Ormosia altimontana is valued for its timber and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
