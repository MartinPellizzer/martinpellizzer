Omphalodes sempervirens

Omphalodes sempervirens, commonly known as the everlasting flower or sweet William, is a perennial plant belonging to the carrot family (Apiaceae).

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, round fruits called schizocarps. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Omphalodes sempervirens is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in floral arrangements.
