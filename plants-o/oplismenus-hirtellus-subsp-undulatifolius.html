Oplismenus hirtellus subsp. undulatifolius

Oplismenus hirtellus subsp.

undulatifolius, commonly known as the wavyleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States. It features upright, branching stems covered in soft, grayish-green foliage.

The leaves are lance-shaped with a distinctive wavy margin, contributing to its ornamental value. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles during the summer months.

This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
