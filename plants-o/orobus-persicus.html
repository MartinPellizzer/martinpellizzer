Orobus persicus

Orobus persicus, commonly known as Persian vetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it an important species in dryland ecosystems.

Its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil also contributes to soil fertility in agricultural settings.
