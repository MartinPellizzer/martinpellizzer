Ocotea densiflora

Ocotea densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within lowland to montane forests. Its leaves are dark green and glossy, often with a strong, spicy scent.

Ocotea densiflora is valued for its timber and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
