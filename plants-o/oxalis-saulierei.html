Oxalis saulierei

Oxalis saulierei, commonly known as the sulfur yellow wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and blooms.

Oxalis saulierei is relatively drought-tolerant once established, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping.
