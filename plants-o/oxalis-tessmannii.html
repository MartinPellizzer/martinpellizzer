Oxalis tessmannii

Oxalis tessmannii, commonly known as the Cape oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and blooms.

Oxalis tessmannii is also valued for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it a resilient choice for xeriscaping.
