Oenothera mexicana

Oenothera mexicana, commonly known as Mexican evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, black seeds. Oenothera mexicana is a perennial herb with hairy stems and alternate leaves.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
