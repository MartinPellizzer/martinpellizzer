Ozodycus

Ozodycus, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a sacred plant in various cultures, particularly in Hindu and Buddhist traditions.

It is native to Asia and thrives in freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes. The plant is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom on long, winding stems. Its leaves are broad and floating, while its roots are submerged in water.

Ozodycus is also valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
