Opuntia dillenii var. reitzii

Opuntia dillenii var. reitzii, commonly known as the Reitz's prickly pear, is a succulent cactus native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in spines and glochids, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months, followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-draining soil and minimal water.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes in regions with similar climates.
