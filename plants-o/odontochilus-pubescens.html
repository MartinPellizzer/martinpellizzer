Odontochilus pubescens

Odontochilus pubescens, commonly known as the hairy toothed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom during the rainy season. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky areas. Its root system is fibrous, and it produces a single upright stem with a few flowering shoots.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its unique appearance.
