Ouratea isophylla

Ouratea isophylla, commonly known as the Brazilian starwood, is a member of the Ochnaceae family and is native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. It produces small, clustered white flowers that are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
