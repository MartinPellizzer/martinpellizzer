Orchis maculata subsp. saccifera

Orchis maculata subsp.

saccifera, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet-colored flowers with a distinctive dark purple or blackish marking on the lip.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along riverbanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while the flowers are borne on a spike that rises above the foliage.

This subspecies is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
