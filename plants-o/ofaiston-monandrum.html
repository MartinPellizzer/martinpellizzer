Ofaiston monandrum

Ofaiston monandrum, commonly known as the Natal orchid, is a striking plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing clusters of pale yellow to orange flowers with a distinct lip. Its botanical features include fibrous roots and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

Ofaiston monandrum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
