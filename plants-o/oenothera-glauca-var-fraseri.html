Oenothera glauca var. fraseri

Oenothera glauca var. fraseri, commonly known as Fraser's evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and yellow flowers that open in the evening. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oval seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and native plant gardens.
