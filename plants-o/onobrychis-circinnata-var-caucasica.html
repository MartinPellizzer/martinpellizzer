Onobrychis circinnata var. caucasica

Onobrychis circinnata var. caucasica, commonly known as Caucasian burclover, is a perennial legume native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to temperate climates. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, woody pods containing seeds.

This species is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
