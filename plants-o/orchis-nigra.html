Orchis nigra

Orchis nigra, commonly known as the black hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family, which includes over 20,000 species of flowering plants. The plant features a tall, erect stem with broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces striking, dark purple to black flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

The flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
