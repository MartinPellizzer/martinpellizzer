Ocimum basilicum var. majus

Ocimum basilicum var. majus, commonly known as holy basil or tulsi, is a perennial herb native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, pale purple flowers. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Ayurvedic practices.

Its essential oils contain compounds like eugenol and linalool, which contribute to its distinctive fragrance and therapeutic benefits.
