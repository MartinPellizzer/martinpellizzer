Onobrychis grandis var. edelbergii

Onobrychis grandis var. edelbergii, commonly known as the Edelberg's sweet clover, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spikes. The plant has a robust root system that helps it thrive in poor soils and withstand drought conditions. It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.

The plant's adaptability makes it a valuable species for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
