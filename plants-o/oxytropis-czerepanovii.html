Oxytropis czerepanovii

Oxytropis czerepanovii, commonly known as the Czerepanov's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, compound leaves. The plant produces yellow to reddish-purple flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and steppe habitats.

Oxytropis czerepanovii is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility in its native ecosystems.
