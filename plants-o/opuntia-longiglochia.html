Opuntia longiglochia

Opuntia longiglochia, commonly known as the long-spined cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, flat, paddle-shaped stems that are typically ribbed and covered in clusters of spines. The plant produces bright yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits known as tunas. Opuntia longiglochia is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system to access water.

Its structure and growth habits make it well-suited for survival in desert conditions.
