Oreocnide integrifolia var. sylvatica

Oreocnide integrifolia var. sylvatica, commonly known as the Pacific silver fir, is a variant of the Pacific silver fir, native to the coastal regions of western North America.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its slender, cylindrical trunk and dense, dark green foliage. The leaves are typically needle-like, arranged in spirals along the branches, and are often coated with a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in temperate rainforests.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
