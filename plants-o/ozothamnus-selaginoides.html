Ozothamnus selaginoides

Ozothamnus selaginoides, commonly known as silverleaf dandelion, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to southern Australia.

It features silver-gray, woolly leaves that are typically opposite or whorled along the stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring, followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal potential.
