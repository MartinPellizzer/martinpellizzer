Odontites lanceolatus var. olotensis

Odontites lanceolatus var. olotensis, commonly known as the Olotense Toothed Violet, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy purple or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its hirsute (hairy) stems and leaves, which contribute to its distinctive appearance.
