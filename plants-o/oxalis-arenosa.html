Oxalis arenosa

Oxalis arenosa, commonly known as desert wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in sandy or rocky soils, often found in desert environments. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, yellow flowers with five petals. It is adapted to withstand drought conditions due to its deep root system and succulent leaves.

Oxalis arenosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils.
