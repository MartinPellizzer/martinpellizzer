Oxalis pardalis

Oxalis pardalis, commonly known as the silverleaf wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive silvery or white underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique foliage and manageable size.
