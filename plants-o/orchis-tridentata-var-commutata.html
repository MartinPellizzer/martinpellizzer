Orchis tridentata var. commutata

Orchis tridentata var. commutata, commonly known as the small pink orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of North America.

This herbaceous perennial features showy, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. It has a short, creeping rhizome and lance-shaped leaves.

The flowers are often arranged in a loose cluster and are pollinated by insects, contributing to its ecological significance in native plant communities.
