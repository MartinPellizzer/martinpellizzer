Oxystelma violacea

Oxystelma violacea, commonly known as the purple-flowered oxystelma, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its velvety, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of vibrant violet to purple flowers. Its blooming period typically occurs during the warmer months, adding a splash of color to its natural habitat.

Oxystelma violacea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
