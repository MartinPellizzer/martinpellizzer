Ophiopogon rupestris

Ophiopogon rupestris, commonly known as the rock ophiopogon, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or shaded environments, showcasing its adaptability to challenging soil conditions. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually white or pale purple, followed by small, fleshy berries.

Ophiopogon rupestris is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
