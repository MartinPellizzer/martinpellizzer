Oenothera tafiensis subsp. tafiensis

Oenothera tafiensis subsp.

tafiensis, commonly known as the Tafiense evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America. It is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats, including grasslands and rocky areas. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Its botanical significance lies in its role within local ecosystems and its potential for horticultural use.
