Ononis vulgaris

Ononis vulgaris, commonly known as common whitlowgrass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with numerous stems that are usually hairless or sparsely hairy. The plant has small, pale green to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and often covered with fine hairs.

Ononis vulgaris is adapted to calcareous soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.
