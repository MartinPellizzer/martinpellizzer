Oxalis excisa

Oxalis excisa, commonly known as the cut-leaf wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. Oxalis excisa thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

It is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and is relatively easy to care for in a temperate climate.
