Orchis cordata

Orchis cordata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial shade. Its flowers are arranged in a spike and bloom from late spring to early summer.

Orchis cordata is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
