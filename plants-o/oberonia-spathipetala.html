Oberonia spathipetala

Oberonia spathipetala, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, yellow-green flowers with a distinctive spatulate lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Oberonia spathipetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
