Oenothera hookeri subsp. ornata

Oenothera hookeri subsp.

ornata, commonly known as the showy evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that open in the evening and close by dawn.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and the stems are often hairy.

The species is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
