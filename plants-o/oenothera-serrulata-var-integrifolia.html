Oenothera serrulata var. integrifolia

Oenothera serrulata var. integrifolia, commonly known as the smooth evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are smooth-edged, distinguishing it from other varieties of evening primrose. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its flowers are typically followed by capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oily seeds.

This species is often found in dry, open habitats like grasslands and rocky slopes.
