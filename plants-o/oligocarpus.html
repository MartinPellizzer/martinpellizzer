Oligocarpus

Oligocarpus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed rock cress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant has slender stems and small, alternate leaves that are often lance-shaped. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, rounded pods as fruit.

Oligocarpus is often found in alpine or arctic environments, where it thrives in cold, harsh conditions.
