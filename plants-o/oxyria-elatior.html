Oxyria elatior

Oxyria elatior, commonly known as the greater oxyria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Xanthorrhoeaceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color, arranged in clusters at the base of the plant. Oxyria elatior thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.
