Ornithogalum isauricum

Ornithogalum isauricum, commonly known as the Isaurian star-of-Bethleem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, linear leaves and produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers during the spring. The plant belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous root system. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Ornithogalum isauricum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
