Olinia micrantha

Olinia micrantha, commonly known as the African sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces woody capsules that contain numerous small, smooth seeds. Olinia micrantha is often found in moist forest understories and can grow up to 25 meters in height.

It is valued for its hard, durable wood and is used in construction and furniture-making.
