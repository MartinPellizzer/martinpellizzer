Oenothera cespitosa var. brandegeei

Oenothera cespitosa var. brandegeei, commonly known as the Brandegee's evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and clusters of yellow flowers that open in the evening. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, elliptical seeds. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
