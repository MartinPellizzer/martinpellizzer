Ocotea schwackeana

Ocotea schwackeana, commonly known as Brazilian lauraceae, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and can reach heights of up to 20 meters. Its bark is often used in traditional medicine, and it plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitat.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
