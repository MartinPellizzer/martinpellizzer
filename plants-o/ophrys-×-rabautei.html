Ophrys × rabautei

Ophrys × rabautei, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant that results from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is characterized by its intricate floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows across Europe. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, while its flowers are striking with their dark purple and yellow markings.

This orchid is a prime example of mimicry in the plant kingdom, showcasing the evolutionary adaptations for successful pollination.
