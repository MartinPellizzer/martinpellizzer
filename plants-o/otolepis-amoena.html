Otolepis amoena

Otolepis amoena, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are soft to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by round, fleshy fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland and swampy areas.

Otolepis amoena is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
