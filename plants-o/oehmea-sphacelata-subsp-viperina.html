Oehmea sphacelata subsp. viperina

Oehmea sphacelata subsp.

viperina, commonly known as the viper's bugloss, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
