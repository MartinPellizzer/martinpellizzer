Odontonema interruptum

Odontonema interruptum, commonly known as the red-veined ivy, is a tropical evergreen vine native to South America.

It features striking, heart-shaped leaves with prominent red veins against a dark green background. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often used in hanging baskets or as a climbing plant.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
