Ophrys paleacea

Ophrys paleacea, commonly known as the pale orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its pale green to brownish-yellow flowers, which often resemble female insects, a trait that aids in its unique pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are usually broad and grass-like, and it produces a single flower spike with several showy blooms.

Ophrys paleacea is notable for its intricate floral structure and specialized relationship with specific pollinators.
