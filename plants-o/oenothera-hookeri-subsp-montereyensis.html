Oenothera hookeri subsp. montereyensis

Oenothera hookeri subsp.

montereyensis, commonly known as Monterey evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of central California. It is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along the coastline, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, and its stems are erect and somewhat woody at the base.

This subspecies is part of the evening primrose family (Onagraceae) and is known for its ecological importance in supporting local insect populations.
