Opuntia fuscolineata

Opuntia fuscolineata, commonly known as the dark-line prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its flattened, ribbed stems that are typically green with a waxy coating. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the summer months, which are followed by edible, red-fleshed fruits. Its spiny pads provide protection from herbivores, while its shallow root system allows it to thrive in arid environments.

Opuntia fuscolineata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local cultures.
