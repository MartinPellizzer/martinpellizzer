Orchis × hepburnii

Orchis × hepburnii, commonly known as the Hepburn's orchid, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Orchis morio and Orchis mascula.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, particularly found in woodland and grassland habitats. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers with a distinctive spur, typical of the Orchidaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded environments.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a natural hybrid, showcasing genetic diversity within the Orchis genus.
