Ophrys × turrium

Ophrys × turrium, commonly known as the bee orchid hybrid, is a fascinating plant with unique botanical features.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is a natural hybrid between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera. The plant is characterized by its striking, bee-like flowers that mimic the appearance of female bees to attract male pollinators. Its flowers have a complex structure with specialized petals and a lip that enhances its mimicry.

Ophrys × turrium is typically found in temperate regions and thrives in moist, calcareous soils.
