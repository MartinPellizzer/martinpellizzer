Orthopogon cubensis

Orthopogon cubensis, commonly known as the Cuban grass or Cuban flat sedge, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it forms dense, grass-like clusters. Its leaves are narrow and cylindrical, with a fibrous texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets.

Orthopogon cubensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
