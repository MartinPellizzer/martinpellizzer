Opuntia leptocaulis var. badia

Opuntia leptocaulis var. badia, commonly known as the Baja flat prickly pear, is a succulent cactus native to the Baja California region of Mexico.

It features a low-growing, sprawling growth habit with flattened, fleshy stems that are typically green to blue-green in color. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its pads are covered in small, soft spines and are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

This variety is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
