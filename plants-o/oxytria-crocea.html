Oxytria crocea

Oxytria crocea, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark red heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 15 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside.

The flowers are small and fragrant, often producing yellow or orange fruits that contain winged seeds.
