Olearia plucheacea

Olearia plucheacea, commonly known as the silverweed tree, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in coastal and subalpine regions, often growing in well-drained soils.

Olearia plucheacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
