Oplismenus patens var. angustifolius

Oplismenus patens var. angustifolius, commonly known as narrowleaf whitebeard, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, white, bead-like flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects.
