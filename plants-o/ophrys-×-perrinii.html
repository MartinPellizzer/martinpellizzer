Ophrys × perrinii

Ophrys × perrinii, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in calcareous soils, often found in open habitats such as grasslands and heaths. The plant is characterized by its intricate, bee-like flowers that mimic the appearance and scent of female bees, a feature evolved to attract male bees for pollination. Its floral structure includes a specialized labellum that plays a key role in the pollination process.

Ophrys × perrinii is a perennial herb with a rosette of basal leaves and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological significance.
