Oleandra trinitensis var. subcostaricensis

Oleandra trinitensis var. subcostaricensis, commonly known as the Costarica oleander, is a shrub native to Central America.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure. Its flowers, which can be white, yellow, or pink, bloom in clusters and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and resilience in warm climates.
