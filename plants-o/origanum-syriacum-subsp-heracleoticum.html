Origanum syriacum subsp. heracleoticum

Origanum syriacum subsp.

heracleoticum, commonly known as Syrian oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features aromatic, narrow leaves that are typically gray-green in color, and it produces small, pale blue to white flowers.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes. Its essential oils, rich in thymol and carvacrol, contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.

This subspecies is closely related to other members of the Origanum genus, sharing similar growth habits and chemical compositions.
