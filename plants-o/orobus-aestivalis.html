Orobus aestivalis

Orobus aestivalis, commonly known as the summer orobus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. It produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Orobus aestivalis is often found in open habitats such as steppes and coastal areas.
