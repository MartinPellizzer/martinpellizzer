Orchis × intuta

Orchis × intuta, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Orchis moria and Orchis anthropophora.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often violet or purple-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial shade. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, spurred orchid flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This hybrid is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique genetic makeup and ornamental value.
