Ocotea zulianensis

Ocotea zulianensis, commonly known as Zulian's ocotea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Venezuela and Colombia. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered white flowers. It produces dark blue to black berries that are edible but not widely consumed by humans.

Ocotea zulianensis is often found in humid, lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
