Oxalis kuhlmannii

Oxalis kuhlmannii, commonly known as the forest sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a short, upright stem.

Oxalis kuhlmannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and blooms.
