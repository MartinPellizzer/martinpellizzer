Oenothera heterochroma subsp. monoensis

Oenothera heterochroma subsp.

monoensis, commonly known as the Mono Lake evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that open in the evening, typical of the evening primrose genus.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found near water sources.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
