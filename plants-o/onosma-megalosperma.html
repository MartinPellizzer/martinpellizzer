Onosma megalosperma

Onosma megalosperma, commonly known as the large-seeded everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white or grayish hairs. Its flowers are borne in dense clusters at the ends of the stems, and the plant is known for its long-lasting, drought-resistant qualities.

Onosma megalosperma is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh, high-altitude environments.
