Opuntia morenoi

Opuntia morenoi, commonly known as Moreno's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, spine-tipped pads and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems. Its fruit, called a tunas, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

Opuntia morenoi plays an important role in its ecosystem, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
