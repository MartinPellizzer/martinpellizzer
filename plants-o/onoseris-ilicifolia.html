Onoseris ilicifolia

Onoseris ilicifolia, commonly known as the hawthorn aster, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its showy yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The fruit is a dry capsule that releases small seeds when mature.
