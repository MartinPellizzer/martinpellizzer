Ouratea oleosa

Ouratea oleosa, commonly known as the golden star or sweet-scented ouratea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and its striking, star-shaped, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and emits a mild, sweet fragrance, especially at night. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in some cultures.
