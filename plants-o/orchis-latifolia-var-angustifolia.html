Orchis latifolia var. angustifolia

Orchis latifolia var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved early purple orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or grassland habitats. The plant features a rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces striking purple or violet flowers in early spring. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers with a distinct lip.

This orchid is part of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its specialized pollination mechanisms involving specific insect species.
