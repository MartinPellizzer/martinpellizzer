Ocotea ovalifolia

Ocotea ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its lanceolate leaves, which are typically glossy and dark green. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are followed by oval, dark blue berries.

Ocotea ovalifolia is often valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
