Oreoherzogia pumila subsp. hispanica

Oreoherzogia pumila subsp.

hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish dwarf orchid, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to southern Spain. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces delicate, pale green to yellowish flowers with a distinct, sweet scent.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. Its flowers typically bloom from late spring to early summer, and it is known for its resilience in arid environments.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations and limited distribution.
