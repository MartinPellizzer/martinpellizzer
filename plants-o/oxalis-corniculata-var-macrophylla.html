Oxalis corniculata var. macrophylla

Oxalis corniculata var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to Asia and parts of Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers. The leaves are heart-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while the stems are slender and creeping. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in gardens and wild areas.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
