Olearia parvifolia

Olearia parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved daisy bush, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in coastal and lowland areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It has small, ovate leaves with a hairy texture and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Olearia parvifolia is also valued for its ability to attract native pollinators.
