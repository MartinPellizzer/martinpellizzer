Orianthera callosa

Orianthera callosa, commonly known as the hairy orianthera, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Orianthera callosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
