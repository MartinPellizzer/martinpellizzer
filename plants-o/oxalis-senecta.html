Oxalis senecta

Oxalis senecta, commonly known as the white wood sorrel, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It features trifoliate leaves with three heart-shaped leaflets, often adorned with a white or pale green bloom. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into reddish-brown seed pods. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
