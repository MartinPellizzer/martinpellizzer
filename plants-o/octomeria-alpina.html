Octomeria alpina

Octomeria alpina, commonly known as the alpine octomeria, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool and moist conditions. Its flowers are small and clustered, often displaying shades of white or yellow.

Octomeria alpina is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
