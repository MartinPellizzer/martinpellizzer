Onobrychis eubrychidea

Onobrychis eubrychidea, commonly known as the red clover, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy pink or purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a taproot system and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria.

Onobrychis eubrychidea is also valued for its use in forage and as a cover crop in agricultural settings.
