Ophrys × quintartiana

Ophrys × quintartiana, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Ophrys apifera (the bee orchid) and Ophrys insectifera (the wasp orchid).

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe, particularly in regions like Spain and southern France. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, dark green leaves and produces tall, slender flowering stems. Its flowers are highly specialized, resembling female wasps or bees, which aid in its unique pollination strategy.

This orchid is notable for its intricate floral morphology and its role in the study of plant hybridization and mimicry.
