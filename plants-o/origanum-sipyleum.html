Origanum sipyleum

Origanum sipyleum, commonly known as Turkish oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are often used in cooking for their pungent flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its essential oils contain compounds like carvacrol and thymol, contributing to its medicinal and culinary uses.
