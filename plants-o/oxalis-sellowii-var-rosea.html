Oxalis sellowii var. rosea

Oxalis sellowii var. rosea, commonly known as the pink wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, delicate flowers. The plant produces pink to reddish flowers that bloom in spring and summer, adding a vibrant touch to gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to thrive in various climates with minimal care.
