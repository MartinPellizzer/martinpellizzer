Oenothera fallacoides

Oenothera fallacoides, commonly known as the false evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats like grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

Oenothera fallacoides is a perennial species that produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds.
