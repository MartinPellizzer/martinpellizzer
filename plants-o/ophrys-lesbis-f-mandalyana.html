Ophrys lesbis f. mandalyana

Ophrys lesbis f. mandalyana, commonly known as the Mandalyan orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species found in specific habitats across the Balkans.

It belongs to the genus Ophrys, which is known for its mimicry of female insects to attract male pollinators. This particular form exhibits unique floral morphology, with intricate patterns that resemble the appearance of a wasp. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices.

Its reproductive success heavily relies on the specific pollination behavior of certain bee species, making it an ecologically significant yet vulnerable flora.
