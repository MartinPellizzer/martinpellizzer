Oriastrum stuebelii var. argentinum

Oriastrum stuebelii var. argentinum, commonly known as the Argentine tansy, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellowish-green foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has a fibrous root system and produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
