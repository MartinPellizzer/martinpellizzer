Orobanche laxa

Orobanche laxa, commonly known as the common broomrape, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is found in various regions across Europe, Asia, and North America. The plant produces small, pale yellow or white flowers and has a taproot system that attaches to the roots of its host. It is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its ability to parasitize a wide range of host species.

Its life cycle is closely tied to the growth and development of its host plants.
