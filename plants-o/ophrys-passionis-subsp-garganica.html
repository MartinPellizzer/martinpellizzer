Ophrys passionis subsp. garganica

Ophrys passionis subsp.

garganica, commonly known as the Garganica orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is characterized by its intricate, lip-like floral structure that mimics the appearance of female insects, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats, such as woodlands and mountainous regions, where it thrives in nutrient-rich soils. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brownish in color, with complex patterns that aid in attracting specific pollinators.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
