Obione cornuta

Obione cornuta, commonly known as the horned orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated floral structure with a prominent central spur. Native to the high-altitude regions of South America, this plant thrives in cool, moist environments. Its flowers are typically violet or purple, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insects.

The plant's botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.
