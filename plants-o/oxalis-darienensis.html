Oxalis darienensis

Oxalis darienensis, commonly known as the Darien wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and undergrowth. Its leaves contain oxalic acid, which can be toxic if ingested in large quantities.

Oxalis darienensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
