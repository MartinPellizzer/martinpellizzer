Oenothera sackvillensis var. albiviridis

Oenothera sackvillensis var. albiviridis, commonly known as the white-veined evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the genus Oenothera, which includes various species of evening primroses known for their showy, nocturnal blooms. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive white vein pattern, and its flowers are typically yellow or orange, opening in the evening. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting nocturnal pollinators.
