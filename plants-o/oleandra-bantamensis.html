Oleandra bantamensis

Oleandra bantamensis, commonly known as the Bantam oleander, is a shrub native to the Indonesian island of Java.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that contain seeds, and its leaves are typically simple and opposite.

Oleandra bantamensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
