Osyris japonica

Osyris japonica, commonly known as Japanese osyris, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the summer months. Osyris japonica is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and blooming habit.
