Onobrychis vulgaris var. sativa

Onobrychis vulgaris var. sativa, commonly known as sainfoin, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable forage crop. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in agricultural settings for its nutritional value to livestock.

Sainfoin also has historical uses in herbal medicine and as a bee plant due to its nectar-rich flowers.
