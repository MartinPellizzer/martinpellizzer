Ophrys × occidentalis

Ophrys × occidentalis, commonly known as the Western bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the western United States.

It exhibits striking floral morphology, with its flowers resembling bees, a trait evolved to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in late spring.

This species is valued for its unique appearance and ecological role in supporting pollinator populations.
