Onosma hookeri var. hirsuta

Onosma hookeri var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy onosma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 30-60 centimeters and features hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, glandular hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
