Oreogrammitis pilifera

Oreogrammitis pilifera, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a small, terrestrial fern native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its dark green, leathery fronds and hairy undersides. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Oreogrammitis pilifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
