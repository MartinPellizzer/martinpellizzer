Obione pacifica

Obione pacifica, commonly known as the Pacific sea holly, is a marine flowering plant found in coastal regions of the Pacific Ocean.

It thrives in rocky intertidal zones, where it withstands strong wave action and fluctuating salinity levels. The plant has distinctive blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and adapted to reduce water loss in exposed environments.

Obione pacifica plays a vital role in stabilizing shorelines and supporting marine biodiversity.
