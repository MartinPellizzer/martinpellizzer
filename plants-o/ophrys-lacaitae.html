Ophrys lacaitae

Ophrys lacaitae, commonly known as the Balkan bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forest floors or rocky areas. Its flowers are usually pale yellow with intricate patterns that enhance the mimicry.

Ophrys lacaitae is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its specific ecological and evolutionary adaptations.
