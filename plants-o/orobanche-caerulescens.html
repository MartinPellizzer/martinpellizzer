Orobanche caerulescens

Orobanche caerulescens, commonly known as the blue broomrape, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or blue in color, and it is often found in disturbed soils in Europe and parts of Asia. The plant's life cycle is closely tied to specific host species, such as those in the legume family. Its seeds are small and can remain viable in the soil for many years, making it a persistent weed in agricultural settings.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can significantly impact the growth and yield of its host plants.
