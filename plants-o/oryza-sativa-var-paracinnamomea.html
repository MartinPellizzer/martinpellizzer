Oryza sativa var. paracinnamomea

Oryza sativa var. paracinnamomea, commonly known as the cinnamon rice, is a rare and ornamental variety of rice that exhibits unique botanical features.

It is characterized by its fragrant, cinnamon-scented grains and a striking reddish-brown coloration. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with slender, erect stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice due to their aromatic properties.

This variety is of interest to botanists and horticulturists for its genetic diversity and potential agricultural applications.
