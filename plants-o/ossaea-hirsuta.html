Ossaea hirsuta

Ossaea hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy ossaea, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are typically hairy, giving it the common name, and it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.

Ossaea hirsuta is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
