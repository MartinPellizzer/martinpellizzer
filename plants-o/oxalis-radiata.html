Oxalis radiata

Oxalis radiata, commonly known as the false shamrock, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It features trifoliate leaves that resemble the shamrock, with three heart-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Oxalis radiata is also known for its ability to spread through runners, forming dense ground cover.
