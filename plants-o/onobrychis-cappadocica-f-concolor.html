Onobrychis cappadocica f. concolor

Onobrychis cappadocica f. concolor, commonly known as the Cappadocian sweet clover, is a perennial legume characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves.

The leaves are typically bright green and have a velvety texture, while the flowers are small, yellowish, and arranged in racemes. It is native to the Cappadocia region of Turkey and thrives in dry, calcareous soils. This plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment and as a forage crop.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
