Oxytropis lanceatifoliola

Oxytropis lanceatifoliola, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and Mongolia. The plant is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. It has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in dry, sandy soils.

Oxytropis lanceatifoliola is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil in degraded landscapes.
