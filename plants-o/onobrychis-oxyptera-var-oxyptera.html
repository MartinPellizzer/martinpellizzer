Onobrychis oxyptera var. oxyptera

Onobrychis oxyptera var. oxyptera, commonly known as the narrow-leafed clover, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in well-drained soils, often found in grasslands and disturbed sites. It is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment and forage production.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological restoration and agricultural contexts.
