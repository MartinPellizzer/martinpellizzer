Ostrya carpinifolia var. genuina

Ostrya carpinifolia var. genuina, commonly known as the European hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and alternate, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The tree produces small, yellowish-green catkins that appear in early spring, followed by small, winged fruits called samaras.

It is often found in moist, well-drained soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
