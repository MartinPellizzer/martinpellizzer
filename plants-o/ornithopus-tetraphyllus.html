Ornithopus tetraphyllus

Ornithopus tetraphyllus, commonly known as the four-leafed rocket, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant features upright, branched stems with small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are bright yellow and arranged in clusters, typical of the Brassicaceae family.

It is often grown as a herb for its peppery flavor and is used in salads and as a garnish.
