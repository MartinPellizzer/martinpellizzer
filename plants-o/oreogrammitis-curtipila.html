Oreogrammitis curtipila

Oreogrammitis curtipila, commonly known as the narrow-leafed wall-rush, is a small, terrestrial moss that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves that are often curved. The plant forms dense, low-growing mats and is typically found on rocky surfaces or in crevices. Its reproductive structures, known as sporophytes, are relatively simple and consist of a capsule that produces spores.

Oreogrammitis curtipila plays a minor but important role in stabilizing soil and providing microhabitats for other organisms in its ecosystem.
