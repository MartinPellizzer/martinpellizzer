Oxytropis densiflora var. multiramosa

Oxytropis densiflora var. multiramosa, commonly known as the denseflower locoweed, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, ramified stems and small, often blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are compound, with multiple pairs of small, oval leaflets, and the plant is often found in sagebrush steppe and other open habitats.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules.
