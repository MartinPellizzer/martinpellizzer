Oryza sativa var. pseudosepica

Oryza sativa var. pseudosepica, commonly known as the pseudosepik rice, is a wild rice species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance and small, spikelet-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and flooded fields, where it thrives in aquatic or semi-aquatic conditions. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss.

This variety is of interest to botanists and agricultural researchers due to its potential for genetic diversity and adaptability to changing environmental conditions.
