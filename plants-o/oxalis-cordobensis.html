Oxalis cordobensis

Oxalis cordobensis, commonly known as the Cordoba wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces edible leaves and flowers, though they should be consumed in moderation due to their oxalic acid content. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Oxalis cordobensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
