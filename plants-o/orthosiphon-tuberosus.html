Orthosiphon tuberosus

Orthosiphon tuberosus, commonly known as Java tea or Malabar spinach, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its edible leaves, which are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Orthosiphon tuberosus is valued for its potential health benefits, including diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.
