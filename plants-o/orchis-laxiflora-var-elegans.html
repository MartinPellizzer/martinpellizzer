Orchis laxiflora var. elegans

Orchis laxiflora var. elegans, commonly known as the elegant orchis, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in woodland or grassland habitats, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged along the stem, while the flowers are often clustered in a loose spike.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
