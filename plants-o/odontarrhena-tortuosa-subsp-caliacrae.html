Odontarrhena tortuosa subsp. caliacrae

Odontarrhena tortuosa subsp.

caliacrae, commonly known as the Cilicia spurge, is a flowering plant native to parts of the Middle East and North Africa. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Asteraceae family.
