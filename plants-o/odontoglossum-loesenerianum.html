Odontoglossum loesenerianum

Odontoglossum loesenerianum, commonly known as the Loesener's orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant with a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, spurred orchid flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically during the spring and early summer. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, arranged in a rosette pattern around the pseudobulb.

Odontoglossum loesenerianum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
