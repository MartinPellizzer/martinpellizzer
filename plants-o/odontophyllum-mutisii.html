Odontophyllum mutisii

Odontophyllum mutisii, commonly known as the Andean orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool, moist climates. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are usually white or pale pink with intricate patterns.

Odontophyllum mutisii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
