Oxalis sepalosa

Oxalis sepalosa, commonly known as the pink oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces seed capsules that release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Oxalis sepalosa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its delicate appearance and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
