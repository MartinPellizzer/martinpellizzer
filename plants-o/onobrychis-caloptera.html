Onobrychis caloptera

Onobrychis caloptera, commonly known as the white clover, is a leguminous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, white, pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in temperate climates. It has a deep root system that helps in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil fertility.

Onobrychis caloptera is often used in pastures and as a cover crop due to its hardiness and nutritional value for livestock.
