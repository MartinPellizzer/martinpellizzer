Oncidium cimiferum

Oncidium cimiferum, commonly known as the rainbow orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It features a compact growth habit with multiple pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, colorful inflorescences with long, drooping flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. Its leaves are strap-like, firm, and arranged in a fan shape, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Oncidium cimiferum requires bright, indirect light and well-draining potting media to thrive.
