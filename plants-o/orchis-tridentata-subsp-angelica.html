Orchis tridentata subsp. angelica

Orchis tridentata subsp.

angelica, commonly known as the angelica orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple and white.

The plant has a complex life cycle, often taking several years to reach maturity and produce flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows.

Its unique floral structure and pollination mechanisms make it an important subject in botanical studies.
