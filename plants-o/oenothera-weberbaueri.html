Oenothera weberbaueri

Oenothera weberbaueri, commonly known as the Weberbauer's evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has hairy, slender stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, smooth seeds.

Oenothera weberbaueri is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and desert scrub habitats.
