Ocimum camporum

Ocimum camporum, commonly known as Camp's basil, is a species of flowering plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and small, pale purple flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in shaded, moist environments.

Ocimum camporum is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
