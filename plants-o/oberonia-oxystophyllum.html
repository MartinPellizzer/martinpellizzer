Oberonia oxystophyllum

Oberonia oxystophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with elongated, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in a spike, often with a subtle fragrance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on tree trunks or in the undergrowth of dense forests.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and delicate species.
