Oxalis ibarii

Oxalis ibarii, commonly known as the Ibari wood sorrel, is a species of flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each leaflet being heart-shaped with a distinctive veining pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Oxalis ibarii is valued for its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in mild climates.
