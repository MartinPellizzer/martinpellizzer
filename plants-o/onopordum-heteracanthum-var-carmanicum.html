Onopordum heteracanthum var. carmanicum

Onopordum heteracanthum var. carmanicum, commonly known as the Carmel tarplant, is a perennial flowering plant native to California.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and produces yellow flowers with purple centers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas. It is an important species for local pollinators and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it a notable member of the Asteraceae family.
