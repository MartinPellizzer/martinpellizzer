Odontoglossum longifolium

Odontoglossum longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved toothed orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in high-altitude cloud forests. The plant features striking, lance-shaped leaves with a prominent midrib and a velvety texture. Its flowers are showy, with a unique structure that includes a spurred lip and a complex arrangement of petals and sepals.

Odontoglossum longifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
