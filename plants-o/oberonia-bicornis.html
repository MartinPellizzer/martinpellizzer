Oberonia bicornis

Oberonia bicornis, commonly known as the two-horned orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-lobed pseudobulbs and linear, grass-like leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-yellow with red or purple markings. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Oberonia bicornis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
