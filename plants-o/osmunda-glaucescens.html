Osmunda glaucescens

Osmunda glaucescens, commonly known as the green fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Osmundaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, compound fronds that are typically green with a bluish tinge. This plant is native to eastern North America and thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

Osmunda glaucescens plays an important ecological role by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for various small organisms.
