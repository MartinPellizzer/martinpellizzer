Oncidium damacenoi

Oncidium damacenoi, commonly known as the yellow butterfly orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It features a sympodial growth habit with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and green, often resembling butterflies. Its roots are epiphytic, allowing it to grow on trees or other structures in its natural habitat.

Oncidium damacenoi requires bright, indirect light and well-draining potting media for optimal growth.
