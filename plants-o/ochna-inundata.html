Ochna inundata

Ochna inundata, commonly known as the water ochna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are typically elliptical and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the rainy season. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Ochna inundata is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and along riverbanks.
