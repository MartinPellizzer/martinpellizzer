Oxalis repens var. erecta

Oxalis repens var. erecta, commonly known as the upright wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features a low-growing, sprawling habit with trifoliate leaves that are heart-shaped and often have a reddish or purple tint. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of yellow or orange, typically in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Oxalis repens var. erecta is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
