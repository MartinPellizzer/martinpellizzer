Orchis bidentata

Orchis bidentata, commonly known as the two-toothed orchid, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodlands and meadows. Its flowers have a distinctive two-lobed lip, which is a key feature in its classification.

Orchis bidentata is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
