Oncoba kivuensis

Oncoba kivuensis, commonly known as the Kivu palm, is a flowering plant native to the Democratic Republic of the Congo.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fruits that are consumed by both humans and wildlife.

Oncoba kivuensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, contributing to biodiversity and providing resources for local communities.
