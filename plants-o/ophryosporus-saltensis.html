Ophryosporus saltensis

Ophryosporus saltensis, commonly known as the saltbush, is a halophytic shrub adapted to saline environments.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its small, fleshy leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions, particularly in Central Asia and parts of the Middle East.

Ophryosporus saltensis plays a vital role in stabilizing soil in saline areas and is used in phytoremediation efforts.
