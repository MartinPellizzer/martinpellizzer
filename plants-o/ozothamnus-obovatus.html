Ozothamnus obovatus

Ozothamnus obovatus, commonly known as the broad-leaf golden bush, is a perennial shrub native to southern Australia.

It features silver-gray, ovate to obovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, terminal clusters, typically blooming from late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant once established.

Ozothamnus obovatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native pollinators.
