Orchis coriophora var. cimicina

Orchis coriophora var. cimicina, commonly known as the Cimicin orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to specific regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. It has a short flowering period and is often difficult to detect due to its inconspicuous appearance.

Due to habitat loss and fragmentation, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
