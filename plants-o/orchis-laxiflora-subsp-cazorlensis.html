Orchis laxiflora subsp. cazorlensis

Orchis laxiflora subsp.

cazorlensis, commonly known as the Cazorla orchid, is a rare and endemic species found primarily in the Iberian Peninsula. It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its tall, slender stem and showy, violet-blue flowers.

The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky areas. Its flowers are arranged in a loose spike and are pollinated by various insect species, including bees and flies.
