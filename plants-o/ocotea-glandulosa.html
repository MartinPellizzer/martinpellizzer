Ocotea glandulosa

Ocotea glandulosa, commonly known as the Brazilian camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and fragrant wood. The plant is known for its high concentration of essential oils, particularly camphor, which contributes to its medicinal and aromatic properties. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and commercial contexts.
