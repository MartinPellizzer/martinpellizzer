Orobanche foetida var. kabylica

Orobanche foetida var. kabylica, commonly known as the stinking broom.

This parasitic plant belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and lacks chlorophyll, relying entirely on its host for nutrients. It typically parasitizes members of the Fabaceae family, such as vetches and clovers. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and is characterized by its strong, unpleasant odor.

It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.
