Oxyria reniformis

Oxyria reniformis, commonly known as the kidneyleaf, is a perennial herb native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its distinctive kidney-shaped leaves, which are typically hairy and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that grow in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Oxyria reniformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
