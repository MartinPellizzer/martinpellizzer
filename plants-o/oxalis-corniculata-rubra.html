Oxalis corniculata rubra

Oxalis corniculata rubra, commonly known as the crimson wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically heart-shaped with a reddish tint, and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Oxalis corniculata rubra is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
