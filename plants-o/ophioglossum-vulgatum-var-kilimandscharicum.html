Ophioglossum vulgatum var. kilimandscharicum

Ophioglossum vulgatum var. kilimandscharicum, commonly known as the giant snake plant, is a member of the Ophioglossaceae family and is native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its long, strap-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are typically located at the base of the leaves. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable species in botanical studies.
