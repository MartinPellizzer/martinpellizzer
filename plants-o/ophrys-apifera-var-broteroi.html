Ophrys apifera var. broteroi

Ophrys apifera var. broteroi, commonly known as the bee orchid, is a species of orchid native to parts of Europe and North Africa.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure that closely resembles a female bee, a trait evolved to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and has a compact, upright growth habit with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow with brown and purple markings, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

This orchid is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its specialized pollination mechanism.
