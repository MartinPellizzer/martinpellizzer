Oenothera cambrica var. impunctata

Oenothera cambrica var. impunctata, commonly known as the smooth evening primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its flowers have four petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Onagraceae family.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens and natural habitats.
