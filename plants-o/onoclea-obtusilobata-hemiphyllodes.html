Onoclea obtusilobata hemiphyllodes

Onoclea obtusilobata hemiphyllodes, commonly known as the broad-lobed water shield, is a aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds and wetlands. The plant has floating leaves that are broad and rounded, with a waxy surface to repel water. Its small, yellowish flowers appear in clusters and are pollinated by insects.

The plant plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
