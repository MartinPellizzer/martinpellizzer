Odontoglossum cariniferum

Odontoglossum cariniferum, commonly known as the spurred orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial with a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients. The plant produces striking violet or purple flowers with distinctive spurred petals. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Odontoglossum cariniferum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
