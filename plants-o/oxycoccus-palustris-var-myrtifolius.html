Oxycoccus palustris var. myrtifolius

Oxycoccus palustris var. myrtifolius, commonly known as the red-fruited cranberry, is a variant of the cranberry plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its small, red berries that are typically harvested in the fall. The plant has small, oval leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in acidic, sandy soils and requires a specific balance of water and nutrients.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
