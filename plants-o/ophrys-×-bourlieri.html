Ophrys × bourlieri

Ophrys × bourlieri, commonly known as the Bourlier's bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the genus Ophrys, which is characterized by its specialized floral structures that mimic female insects to attract male pollinators. The plant produces showy, bee-like flowers with a distinctive three-lobed lip that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. It typically grows in calcareous soils and thrives in sunny, open habitats.

The species is notable for its intricate mimicry and is often found in Mediterranean regions.
