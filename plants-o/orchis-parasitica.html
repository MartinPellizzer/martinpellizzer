Orchis parasitica

Orchis parasitica, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and elusive orchid species that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

It is a hemiparasitic plant, meaning it partially relies on other plants for nutrients through its root system. The plant lacks true leaves and instead has a specialized root system that connects it to host plants. Its flowers are typically white or pale green, with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color.

Orchis parasitica is found in specific habitats, such as wet heaths and bogs, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-poor conditions.
