Oberonia lindleyana

Oberonia lindleyana, commonly known as the orchid of the forest floor, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in undergrowth or on the forest floor. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the rich, organic soil.

Oberonia lindleyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
