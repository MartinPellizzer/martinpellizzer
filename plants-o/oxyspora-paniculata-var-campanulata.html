Oxyspora paniculata var. campanulata

Oxyspora paniculata var. campanulata, commonly known as the bell-shaped oxyspora, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
