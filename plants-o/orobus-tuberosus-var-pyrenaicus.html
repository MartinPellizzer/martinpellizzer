Orobus tuberosus var. pyrenaicus

Orobus tuberosus var. pyrenaicus, commonly known as the Pyrenean tuberous bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pyrenees region.

It features erect stems covered with glandular hairs and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to violet. The plant has tuberous roots that serve as storage organs, allowing it to survive harsh winter conditions. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a woolly texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
