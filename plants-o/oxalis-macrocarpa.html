Oxalis macrocarpa

Oxalis macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinct veins and produces clusters of five-petaled yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swamps. Its stems are often sprawling or trailing, and it can reach heights of up to 3 feet.

Oxalis macrocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and floral display.
