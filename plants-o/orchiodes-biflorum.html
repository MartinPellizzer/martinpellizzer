Orchiodes biflorum

Orchiodes biflorum, commonly known as the two-flowered orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and purple. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its roots are fibrous and often grow in dense clusters, adapting to its humid, shaded habitats.

Orchiodes biflorum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and unique floral structure.
