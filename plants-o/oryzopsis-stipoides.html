Oryzopsis stipoides

Oryzopsis stipoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers.

Oryzopsis stipoides plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
