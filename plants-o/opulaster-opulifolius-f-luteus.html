Opulaster opulifolius f. luteus

Opulaster opulifolius f. luteus, commonly known as the yellow-leafed willow-leafed opulaster, is a shrub characterized by its distinctive foliage.

Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth margin and a pale yellow-green coloration. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in hue. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or stream banks.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden settings.
