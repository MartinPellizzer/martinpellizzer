Oxalis contracta

Oxalis contracta, commonly known as the small-leaf wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive pattern of veins and small, trifoliate leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Oxalis contracta is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
