Odontotrichum pachyphyllum

Odontotrichum pachyphyllum, commonly known as the thick-leaved toothed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and features narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive serrated or toothed margin. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or along stream banks.

Due to its unique morphology and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
