Oxalis ipomaeiflora

Oxalis ipomaeiflora, commonly known as the purple heart oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and clusters of showy, violet-pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its foliage and flowers are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Oxalis ipomaeiflora is cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and greenhouses.
