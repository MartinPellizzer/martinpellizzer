Osmundastrum cinnamomeum subsp. cinnamomeum

Osmundastrum cinnamomeum subsp.

cinnamomeum, commonly known as the cinnamon fern, is a species of fern characterized by its distinctive fronds that resemble a cinnamon stick. It is native to eastern North America and thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant has a long, horizontal rhizome that produces both sterile and fertile fronds. Its spore-bearing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fertile fronds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in forest ecosystems by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for small organisms.
