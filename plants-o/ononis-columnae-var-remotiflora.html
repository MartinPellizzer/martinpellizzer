Ononis columnae var. remotiflora

Ononis columnae var. remotiflora, commonly known as the distant-flowered sandmat, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in sandy or gravelly soils.

It is often found in coastal and arid environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing dunes and preventing erosion.
