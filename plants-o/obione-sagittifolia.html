Obione sagittifolia

Obione sagittifolia, commonly known as the arrowleaf obione, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves, which are typically alternate and have a serrated margin. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested or shaded areas.

Obione sagittifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
