Oxalis leucolepis

Oxalis leucolepis, commonly known as the silverleaf wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive silvery-white coating on the underside, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or woodland settings.

Oxalis leucolepis is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its attractive foliage.
