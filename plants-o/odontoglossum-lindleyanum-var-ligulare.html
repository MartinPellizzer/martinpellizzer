Odontoglossum lindleyanum var. ligulare

Odontoglossum lindleyanum var. ligulare, commonly known as the narrow-lip orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit and pseudobulbs. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, with elongated, narrow petals. Its leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped, and it thrives in cool, humid environments.

This variety is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and unique floral structure.
