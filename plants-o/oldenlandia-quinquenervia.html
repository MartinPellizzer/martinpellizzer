Oldenlandia quinquenervia

Oldenlandia quinquenervia, commonly known as the "Five-veined spiderwort," is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale purple. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have five distinct veins, giving the plant its scientific name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

The plant is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
