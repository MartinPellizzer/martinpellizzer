Ornithidium maldonadoense

Ornithidium maldonadoense, commonly known as the Maldonado's feather cactus, is a rare and endemic species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its unique, branching structure with spiny, leaf-like structures that reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring, which are adapted for pollination by specific insect species. Its botanical features reflect evolutionary adaptations to extreme desert conditions, including thick, fleshy stems for water storage.

Due to habitat destruction, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
