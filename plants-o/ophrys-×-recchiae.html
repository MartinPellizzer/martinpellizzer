Ophrys × recchiae

Ophrys × recchiae, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a cross between Ophrys apifera (the bee orchid) and Ophrys insectifera (the wasp orchid).

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe, particularly in regions like Italy and Greece. The plant features a tall, slender stem with a single, showy floral spike that resembles a wasp or bee, aiding in its pollination strategy. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of insects.

This orchid thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.
