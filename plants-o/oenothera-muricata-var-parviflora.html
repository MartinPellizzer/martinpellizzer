Oenothera muricata var. parviflora

Oenothera muricata var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening. The plant has hairy, ribbed stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

The species is part of the Onagraceae family and is known for its ecological importance to nocturnal pollinators.
