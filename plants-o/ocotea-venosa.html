Ocotea venosa

Ocotea venosa, commonly known as the fragrant laurel or sweet wood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and avocado. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, clustered white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns from green to yellow as it matures.

Ocotea venosa is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
