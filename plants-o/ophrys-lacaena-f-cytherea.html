Ophrys lacaena f. cytherea

Ophrys lacaena f. cytherea, commonly known as the Cytherean bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which closely resembles a female bee, a mimicry strategy that aids in pollination by male bees. The plant produces showy, bee-like flowers with intricate patterns and a distinct three-lobed lip. It thrives in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows, often in areas with rich, humus-filled soil.

Due to its specific ecological requirements and habitat fragmentation, it is considered a vulnerable species in many regions.
