Orthosiphon tagawae

Orthosiphon tagawae, commonly known as the Java mint or Malabar mint, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a mint-like scent.

Orthosiphon tagawae is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional medicine for its diuretic and anti-inflammatory effects.
