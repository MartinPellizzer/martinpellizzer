Orchis hatagirea

Orchis hatagirea, commonly known as the Himalayan tawny orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tawny-colored flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in cool and moist conditions. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to anchor firmly in loose, rocky soils.

Orchis hatagirea is notable for its ecological importance in alpine ecosystems and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
