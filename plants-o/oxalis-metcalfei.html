Oxalis metcalfei

Oxalis metcalfei, commonly known as the Indian wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments and has a fibrous root system. Its flowers are followed by three-lobed seed pods that split open to release seeds.

Oxalis metcalfei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and flowers.
