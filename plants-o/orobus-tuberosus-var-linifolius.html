Orobus tuberosus var. linifolius

Orobus tuberosus var. linifolius, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features showy, tubular yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has a fibrous root system and produces edible tubers that are sometimes used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping in arid regions.
