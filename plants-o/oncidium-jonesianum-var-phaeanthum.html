Oncidium jonesianum var. phaeanthum

Oncidium jonesianum var. phaeanthum, commonly known as the Jonesian orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, often yellow or orange flowers with a distinctive red or brown lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. It requires bright, indirect light and consistent moisture to thrive.

Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that help in photosynthesis and water retention.
