Oncidium cheirophoroides

Oncidium cheirophoroides, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, showy inflorescences that bear bright yellow or orange flowers with a distinctive, intricate pattern. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, warm environments. Its leaves are usually strap-like and arranged in a fan shape, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Oncidium cheirophoroides requires bright, indirect light and moderate watering to thrive.
