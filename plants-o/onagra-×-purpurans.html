Onagra × purpurans

Onagra × purpurans, commonly known as the hybrid sunflower, is a cross between Helianthus annuus (common sunflower) and Helianthus tuberosus (Jerusalem artichoke).

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America, characterized by its large, showy yellow-orange flower heads. The plant produces both male and female flowers, with the central disk containing numerous tiny florets that develop into seeds. Its botanical features include rough, hairy stems and heart-shaped, lobed leaves.

Onagra × purpurans is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
