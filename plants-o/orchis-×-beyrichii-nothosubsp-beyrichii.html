Orchis × beyrichii nothosubsp.

beyrichii, commonly known as the Beyrich's orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to parts of Europe. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, violet to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland areas. It has lanceolate leaves and produces a single spike of flowers with a distinct lip.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.
