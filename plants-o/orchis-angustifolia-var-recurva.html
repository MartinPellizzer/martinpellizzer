Orchis angustifolia var. recurva

Orchis angustifolia var. recurva, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, lance-shaped leaves and produces delicate, violet or purple flowers in late spring. Its inflorescence is usually a single, erect spike bearing several blooms.

The species is notable for its specialized pollination mechanisms and is often found in calcareous soils.
