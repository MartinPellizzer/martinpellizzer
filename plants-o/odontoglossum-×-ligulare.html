Odontoglossum × ligulare

Odontoglossum × ligulare, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a naturally occurring cross between Odontoglossum crispum and Odontoglossum liliiflorum.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, fragrant flowers with a unique lip structure. The plant typically grows in high-altitude cloud forests, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its foliage consists of broad, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation under specific conditions.
