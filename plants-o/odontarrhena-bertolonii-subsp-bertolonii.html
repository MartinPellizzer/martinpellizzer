Odontarrhena bertolonii subsp. bertolonii

Odontarrhena bertolonii subsp.

bertolonii, commonly known as the Malagasy star grass, is a perennial herb native to Madagascar. It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, pale green to brown flowers. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This subspecies is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient component of Madagascar's unique flora.
