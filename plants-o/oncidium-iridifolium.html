Oncidium iridifolium

Oncidium iridifolium, commonly known as the iris-leaf orchid, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its striking, iris-like foliage and vibrant, long-lasting flowers. The plant typically produces racemes of fragrant, colorful blooms in shades of yellow, orange, and red. Oncidium iridifolium thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
