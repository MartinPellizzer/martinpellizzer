Olearia paucidentata var. latifolia

Olearia paucidentata var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved silver bush, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs. The plant produces small, daisy-like white flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and frost-resistant nature.
