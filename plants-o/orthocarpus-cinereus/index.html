Orthocarpus Cinereus

Orthocarpus cinereus is a rare and unique plant species known for its distinctive grayish foliage and delicate flowering pattern. Found primarily in specific regions with particular soil and climate conditions, it has remained relatively obscure in botanical studies. Its name derives from the Greek words for "straight" and "carp," reflecting its structural characteristics. Despite its limited distribution, it holds significant ecological value in its native habitat. This article explores the features, habitat, and importance of Orthocarpus cinereus.
