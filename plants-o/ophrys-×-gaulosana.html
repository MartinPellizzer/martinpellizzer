Ophrys × gaulosana

Ophrys × gaulosana, commonly known as the Gaulosan bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys gaultheri.

It is characterized by its distinctive floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is found in the Mediterranean region, particularly in southern Europe. Its flowers are usually yellow or brown with intricate patterns that aid in mimicry.

Ophrys × gaulosana is a perennial herb that thrives in open, sunny habitats and is often seen in meadows and rocky areas.
