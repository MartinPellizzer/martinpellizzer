Oenothera avita subsp. avita

Oenothera avita subsp.

avita, commonly known as the desert evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its showy yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

The plant has hairy, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are adapted to arid environments. It produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds. Oenothera avita subsp.

avita is often found in sandy or rocky soils in desert and semi-desert regions.
