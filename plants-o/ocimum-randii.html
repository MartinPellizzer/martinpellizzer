Ocimum randii

Ocimum randii, commonly known as wild basil, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 cm, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that have a strong, pungent scent. Its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or white, and it produces small, oblong fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Ocimum randii is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb, similar to other members of the basil genus.
