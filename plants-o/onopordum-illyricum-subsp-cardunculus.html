Onopordum illyricum subsp. cardunculus

Onopordum illyricum subsp.

cardunculus, commonly known as the Italian thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its robust, upright growth habit.

Its thistle-like appearance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
