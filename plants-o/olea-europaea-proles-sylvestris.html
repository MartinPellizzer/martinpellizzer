Olea europaea proles sylvestris

Olea europaea proles sylvestris, commonly known as the wild olive tree, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and fragrant yellow flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that are typically harvested when they are still green. It is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and can tolerate coastal conditions.

The wild olive tree is often used in traditional medicine and has cultural significance in various Mediterranean societies.
