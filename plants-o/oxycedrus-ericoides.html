Oxycedrus ericoides

Oxycedrus ericoides, commonly known as the dwarf tamarind, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces yellowish-orange fruits that contain smooth, edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

Due to its limited natural range, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
