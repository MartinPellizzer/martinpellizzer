Oeosporangium × kochianum

Oeosporangium × kochianum, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between two species of orchids.

It exhibits a combination of morphological and genetic traits from its parent species, often leading to unique floral characteristics. The plant typically produces intricate, showy flowers with specialized structures adapted for pollination by specific insect species. Its growth habit is usually epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of other plants without deriving nutrients from them.

The hybrid is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and genetic diversity in orchid breeding programs.
