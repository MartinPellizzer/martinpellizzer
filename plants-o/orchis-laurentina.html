Orchis laurentina

Orchis laurentina, commonly known as the Laurentian orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, slender stem and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in woodland habitats, thriving in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers are often clustered in a raceme and are pollinated by insects such as bees and moths.

Orchis laurentina is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local biodiversity.
