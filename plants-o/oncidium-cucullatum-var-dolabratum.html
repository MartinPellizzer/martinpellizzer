Oncidium cucullatum var. dolabratum

Oncidium cucullatum var. dolabratum, commonly known as the orchid with the distinctive hooded lip, is a hybrid orchid species native to South America.

It is characterized by its long, drooping inflorescences that bear clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often with a waxy texture that helps retain moisture.

The variety is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its unique floral display.
