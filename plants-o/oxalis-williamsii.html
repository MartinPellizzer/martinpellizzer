Oxalis williamsii

Oxalis williamsii, commonly known as the Williams' wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that resemble those of true sorrels. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is a perennial species that produces bright orange to red berries in the fall.

Oxalis williamsii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to arid environments.
