Octomeria alborosea

Octomeria alborosea, commonly known as the white-flowered octomeria, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its showy, white to pale yellow ray florets and a compact, bushy growth habit. Its leaves are opposite, lanceolate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Octomeria alborosea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its attractive flowers and hardy nature.
