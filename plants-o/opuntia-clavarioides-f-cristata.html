Opuntia clavarioides f. cristata

Opuntia clavarioides f. cristata, commonly known as the cristate prickly pear, is a unique cultivar of the Opuntia genus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flattened, and often branched stems that form a crown-like shape, a trait known as cristation. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red, typically during the summer months. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a succulent, fleshy stem that stores water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

This cultivar is popular in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
