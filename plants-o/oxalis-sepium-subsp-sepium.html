Oxalis sepium subsp. sepium

Oxalis sepium subsp.

sepium, commonly known as the purple wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically heart-shaped and have a reddish or purple hue.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. Oxalis sepium subsp.

sepium is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
