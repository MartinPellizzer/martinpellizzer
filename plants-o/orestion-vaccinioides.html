Orestion vaccinioides

Orestion vaccinioides, commonly known as the dwarf huckleberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and produces clusters of red berries that are edible for both humans and wildlife. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories. Its botanical features include small, deciduous leaves and a compact growth habit.

Orestion vaccinioides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
