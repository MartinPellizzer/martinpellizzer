Odontophyllum wurdackii

Odontophyllum wurdackii, commonly known as the Wurdack's spider-orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the rainforests of Queensland, Australia.

It is characterized by its intricate, spider-like appearance, with long, thin petals and a distinctive central column. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually white or pale green, with a subtle fragrance that attracts specific pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species, requiring conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
