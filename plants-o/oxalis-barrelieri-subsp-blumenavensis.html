Oxalis barrelieri subsp. blumenavensis

Oxalis barrelieri subsp.

blumenavensis, commonly known as the Blumenau oxalis, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil. It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, five-petaled flowers.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky areas. Its foliage exhibits a striking coloration, often with a metallic sheen, and it is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal.

The flowers bloom in spring and early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
