Orchis maculata var. praecox

Orchis maculata var. praecox, commonly known as the early purple orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem that bears a cluster of purple or violet flowers with a distinctive yellow-green stripe down the center. The plant has oval, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and streambanks.

Orchis maculata var. praecox is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is known for its delicate, fragrant blooms that appear in early spring.
