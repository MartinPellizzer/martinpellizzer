Odontoglossum galeottianum

Odontoglossum galeottianum, commonly known as the Galeotti's orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are elongated and often covered in a dense layer of fine hair, aiding in moisture retention.

The orchid's flowers come in a range of colors, including white, pink, and purple, and are often adorned with intricate patterns.
