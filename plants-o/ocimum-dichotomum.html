Ocimum dichotomum

Ocimum dichotomum, commonly known as wild basil, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with a branched stem and opposite leaves that are often hairy. Its essential oils contain compounds like linalool and eugenol, which contribute to its distinctive fragrance.

Ocimum dichotomum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
