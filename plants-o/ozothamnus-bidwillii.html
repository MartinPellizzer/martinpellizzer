Ozothamnus bidwillii

Ozothamnus bidwillii, commonly known as Bidwill's golden bush, is a perennial shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has aromatic, silver-gray foliage and is often found in dry, sandy soils. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Ozothamnus bidwillii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to native pollinators.
