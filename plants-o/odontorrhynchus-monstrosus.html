Odontorrhynchus monstrosus

Odontorrhynchus monstrosus, commonly known as the monstrous toothed orchid, is a rare and exotic plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tooth-like projections on its floral structure, which give it its scientific name. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its intricate and often bioluminescent flowers that bloom at night. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from rocky soil, and it requires a humid, cool environment to thrive.

Due to its unique appearance and limited habitat, it is considered a conservation concern.
