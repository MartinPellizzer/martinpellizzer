Oxalis nervosa

Oxalis nervosa, commonly known as the slender wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its stems are often hairy, and the leaves have a distinctive veining pattern.

Oxalis nervosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
