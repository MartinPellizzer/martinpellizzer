Ophioglossum gregarium

It is a member of the Ophioglossaceae family and is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant has a short, thick rhizome and produces a single, erect, leaf-like structure that bears the sporangia. Its reproductive structures are specialized, with the sporophylls arranged in a unique, spiral pattern that aids in spore dispersal.

Ophioglossum gregarium is considered a primitive fern due to its ancient lineage and simplified morphology.
