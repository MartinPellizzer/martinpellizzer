Oenothera tacikii

Oenothera tacikii, commonly known as the Tacik's evening primrose, is a species of flowering plant native to the central United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in open prairies and disturbed soils. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening, hence its common name. Its flowers are characterized by their large, showy petals and a distinctive, elongated ovary.

Oenothera tacikii is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in full sun.
