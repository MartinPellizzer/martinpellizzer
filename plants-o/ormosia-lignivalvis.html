Ormosia lignivalvis

Ormosia lignivalvis, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, dark reddish-brown wood that is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers followed by pod-like fruits containing flattened, dark seeds.

Ormosia lignivalvis is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it adaptable to different tropical environments.
