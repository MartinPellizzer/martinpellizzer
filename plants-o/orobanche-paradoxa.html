Orobanche paradoxa

Orobanche paradoxa, commonly known as the paradoxical broomrape, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is found in various regions of Europe and Asia. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has a complex life cycle that involves germinating in response to chemical signals from its host. It is considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its ability to parasitize a wide range of host species.

Its botanical study is important for understanding parasitic plant interactions and their impact on ecosystems.
