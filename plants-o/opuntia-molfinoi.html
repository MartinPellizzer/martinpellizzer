Opuntia molfinoi

Opuntia molfinoi, commonly known as the Molfino's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of Argentina.

It is characterized by its flattened, paddle-shaped stems that are covered in sharp spines and clusters of small, globose flowers. The plant produces edible fruits known as tunas, which are typically yellow or red in color. Opuntia molfinoi is well adapted to hot, dry climates and can store water in its thick, fleshy stems.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
