Ononis natrix subvar. canariensis

Ononis natrix subvar. canariensis, commonly known as the Canary island bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Canary Islands.

It features slender, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are alternate, linear, and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in clusters, which bloom from spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies in Mediterranean and island ecosystems.
