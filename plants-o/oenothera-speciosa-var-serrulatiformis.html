Oenothera speciosa var. serrulatiformis

Oenothera speciosa var. serrulatiformis, commonly known as the serrated leaf evening primrose, is a variant of the evening primrose native to North America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, serrated leaves and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides. It has a taproot system and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
