Onosma waddellii

Onosma waddellii, commonly known as Waddell's sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb with hairy, glandular stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Onosma waddellii plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
