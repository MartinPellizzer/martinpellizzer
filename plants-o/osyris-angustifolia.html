Osyris angustifolia

Osyris angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf osyris, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Osyris angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
