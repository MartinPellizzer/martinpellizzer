Orlaya grandiflora subvar. pusilla

Orlaya grandiflora subvar. pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf marsh gentian, is a flowering plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in Europe.

It is characterized by its small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating. It thrives in shallow water and is adapted to nutrient-poor environments.

Its botanical features make it an important indicator species for wetland ecosystems.
