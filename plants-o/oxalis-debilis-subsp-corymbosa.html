Oxalis debilis subsp. corymbosa

Oxalis debilis subsp.

corymbosa, commonly known as the weeping oxalis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America. It is characterized by its delicate, trailing stems that produce clusters of small, heart-shaped leaves.

The plant blooms with pink to purple flowers that are arranged in loose, weeping clusters, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas. Oxalis debilis subsp.

corymbosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and delicate floral display.
