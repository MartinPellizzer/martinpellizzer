Oxalis amara var. grandiflora

Oxalis amara var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive veins and produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas. It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its calcifuge nature, preferring acidic soils.

Oxalis amara var. grandiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
