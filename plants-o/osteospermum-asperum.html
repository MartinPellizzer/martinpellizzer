Osteospermum asperum

Osteospermum asperum, commonly known as the rough osteospermum, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and blue or purple petals. The plant has rough, hairy leaves and stems, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Osteospermum asperum is known for its long blooming period, typically from spring to autumn.
