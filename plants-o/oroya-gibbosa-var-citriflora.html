Oroya gibbosa var. citriflora

Oroya gibbosa var. citriflora, commonly known as the citron lily, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Oroya gibbosa var. citriflora is often cultivated for its vibrant color and is a popular choice in garden landscapes.
