Ocotea diffusa

Ocotea diffusa, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically red when ripe, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Ocotea diffusa is also valued for its essential oils, which are used in perfumery and traditional medicine.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions, posing ecological challenges.
