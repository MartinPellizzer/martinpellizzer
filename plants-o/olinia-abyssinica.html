Olinia abyssinica

Olinia abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to East Africa.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds, which are used in traditional cuisine. Its roots have medicinal properties and are used in local remedies.

Olinia abyssinica is also valued for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it an important species in ecological restoration.
