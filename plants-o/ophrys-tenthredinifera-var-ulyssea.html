Ophrys tenthredinifera var. ulyssea

Ophrys tenthredinifera var. ulyssea, commonly known as the Ulysses bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which closely resembles a female bee, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant produces a single, showy flower with intricate patterns and a hairy lip that mimics the appearance of a bee's abdomen. It thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, this orchid is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
