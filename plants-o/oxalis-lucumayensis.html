Oxalis lucumayensis

Oxalis lucumayensis, commonly known as the Luchuyan oxalis, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as montane forests. Its foliage and flowers display a range of colors, including green, red, and yellow, depending on environmental conditions.

Oxalis lucumayensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
