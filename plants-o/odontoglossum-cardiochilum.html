Odontoglossum cardiochilum

Odontoglossum cardiochilum, commonly known as the heart-lipped orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with a distinctive heart-shaped lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually long and narrow, while its roots are often aerial.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
