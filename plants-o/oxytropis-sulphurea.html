Oxytropis sulphurea

Oxytropis sulphurea, commonly known as sulphur hillclover, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It has small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Oxytropis sulphurea is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and improve nutrient content.
