Ophrys laurensis

Ophrys laurensis, commonly known as the laurel orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique floral structure that mimics the appearance of female insects, a feature known as pseudocopulation. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by bees and wasps.

Ophrys laurensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological significance in its native habitat.
