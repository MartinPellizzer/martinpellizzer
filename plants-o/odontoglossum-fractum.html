Odontoglossum fractum

Odontoglossum fractum, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of pale yellow to greenish-white flowers with a distinctive, sweet scent. Its leaves are broad and ovate, often covered in a fine, hairy texture.

Odontoglossum fractum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique fragrance and floral display.
