Oryctanthus andrieuxii

Oryctanthus andrieuxii, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spiky flower spikes that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as birds and insects.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
