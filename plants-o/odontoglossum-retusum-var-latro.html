Odontoglossum retusum var. latro

Odontoglossum retusum var. latro, commonly known as the Mexican orchid, is a species native to Central America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and striking violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its inflorescence consists of a single spike bearing several showy flowers with a distinctive lip.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
