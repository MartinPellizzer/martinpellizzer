Oberonia cordata

Oberonia cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its roots form a symbiotic relationship with mycorrhizal fungi, which aid in nutrient absorption.

Oberonia cordata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
