Ocotea alveolata

Ocotea alveolata, commonly known as the Caribbean sand-laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurel and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in axillary inflorescences, and it produces dark, spherical berries.

Ocotea alveolata is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests.
