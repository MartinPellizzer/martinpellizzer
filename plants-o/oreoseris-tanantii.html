Oreoseris tanantii

Oreoseris tanantii, commonly known as the African dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, silver-green leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid environments. Its scientific name reflects its serotinous seed dispersal mechanism, where seeds are released only after a fire or disturbance.

Oreoseris tanantii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
