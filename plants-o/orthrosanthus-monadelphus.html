Orthrosanthus monadelphus

Orthrosanthus monadelphus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces delicate, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in a spike, typical of many orchid species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important component of its native habitat.
