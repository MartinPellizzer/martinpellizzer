Ormosia fastigiata

Ormosia fastigiata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ormosia, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by flat, bean-like pods containing several seeds. It is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its hard, durable wood.

The species is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
