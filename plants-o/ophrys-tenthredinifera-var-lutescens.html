Ophrys tenthredinifera var. lutescens

Ophrys tenthredinifera var. lutescens, commonly known as the yellow bee orchid, is a species of orchid native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its intricate floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a distinctive bee-like shape and intricate patterns.

The species is known for its specialized pollination mechanism, which relies on the mimicry of female insects.
