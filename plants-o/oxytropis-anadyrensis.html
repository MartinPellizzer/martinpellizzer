Oxytropis anadyrensis

Oxytropis anadyrensis, commonly known as the Caucasian locoweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to harsh alpine environments. Its leaves are compound, with several pairs of small leaflets, and it produces legumes that contain toxic alkaloids.

Due to its ecological significance and medicinal potential, it is of interest to botanists and pharmacologists alike.
