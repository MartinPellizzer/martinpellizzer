Olfersia pachyphylla

Olfersia pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved olfersia, is a unique plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are often white or pale yellow, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with multiple stems emerging from the base.

Olfersia pachyphylla is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soil with minimal water.
