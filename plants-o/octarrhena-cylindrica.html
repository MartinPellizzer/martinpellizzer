Octarrhena cylindrica

Octarrhena cylindrica, commonly known as the slender sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, succulent stems that are often covered in fine hairs, adapted to reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warm season. Its leaves are narrow and fleshy, further aiding in water conservation.

Octarrhena cylindrica is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is well-suited to harsh, drought-prone habitats.
