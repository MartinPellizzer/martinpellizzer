Orchis picta var. albanica

Orchis picta var. albanica, commonly known as the Albanian painted orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its vivid, intricate floral patterns that resemble painted designs, typically in shades of purple and white. The plant features a tall, erect stem with a single, showy flower that blooms in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged along the stem, contributing to its elegant appearance.

This orchid thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.
