Ophrys volucris

Ophrys volucris, commonly known as the honeybee orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its intricate flower structure, which closely resembles a female honeybee, a feature that plays a key role in its pollination strategy. The plant produces a single, showy flower with a dark brown to blackish coloration and a distinctive, hairy labellum. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss.

Ophrys volucris thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone-rich habitats.
