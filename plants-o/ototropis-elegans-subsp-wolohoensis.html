Ototropis elegans subsp. wolohoensis

Ototropis elegans subsp.

wolohoensis, commonly known as the Woloho slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of East Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and wet meadows.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences with tiny, greenish flowers. Its botanical structure reflects adaptations to its wetland habitat, with rhizomatous growth and specialized root systems for nutrient uptake.

It is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological niche.
