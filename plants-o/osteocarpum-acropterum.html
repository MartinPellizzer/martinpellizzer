Osteocarpum acropterum

Osteocarpum acropterum, commonly known as the mountain heath, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in cool, humid environments at high altitudes. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale pink in color. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, adapted to withstand harsh mountain climates.

Osteocarpum acropterum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
