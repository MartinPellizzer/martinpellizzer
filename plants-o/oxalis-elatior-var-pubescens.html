Oxalis elatior var. pubescens

Oxalis elatior var. pubescens, commonly known as the woolly wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive pubescent texture, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

Oxalis elatior var. pubescens is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and delicate flowers.
