Othonna abrotanifolia

Othonna abrotanifolia, commonly known as the mountain dandelion, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, typically arranged in compact clusters. The plant has aromatic, hairy leaves that are often mistaken for those of the common dandelion. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Othonna abrotanifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
