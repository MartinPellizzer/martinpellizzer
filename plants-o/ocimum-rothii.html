Ocimum rothii

Ocimum rothii, commonly known as Indian basil or Roth's basil, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of Asia, particularly India and Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and lavender. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant leaves that emit a strong, spicy scent when crushed. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color.

Ocimum rothii is often cultivated for its culinary and medicinal uses, including its potential antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.
