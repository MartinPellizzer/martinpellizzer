Omphalodes linifolia var. coerulescens

Omphalodes linifolia var. coerulescens, commonly known as blue bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The flowers are typically blue to violet in color, with five petals and a distinctive yellow throat. The leaves are lanceolate and arranged alternately along the stems.

This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
