Ocotea subracemosa

Ocotea subracemosa, commonly known as the fragrant ocotea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as cinnamon and avocado. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often aromatic when crushed. Its flowers are small and clustered in racemose inflorescences, and it produces dark, smooth-seeded berries.

Ocotea subracemosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
