Orchis sambucina f. tripartita

Orchis sambucina f. tripartita, commonly known as the three-parted orchid, is a rare and distinctive orchid species native to parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring three distinct parts that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in woodland habitats, preferring moist, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually violet or purple in color, with a strikingly intricate pattern.

This orchid is noted for its specialized pollination mechanisms, often relying on specific insect species for reproduction.
