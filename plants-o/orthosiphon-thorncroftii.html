Orthosiphon thorncroftii

Orthosiphon thorncroftii, commonly known as the Malabar mint, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal properties. The plant is characterized by its slender stems, opposite leaves, and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The leaves and flowers are used in traditional medicine for their purported diuretic and anti-inflammatory effects.
