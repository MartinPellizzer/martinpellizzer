Odontosoria versteegii, commonly known as the African wall-r Fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is a terrestrial fern characterized by its finely divided, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often on rocky or forested substrates. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Odontosoria versteegii is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological role in its habitat.
