Ocimum dentatum

Ocimum dentatum, commonly known as the serrated basil, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or white. It is often cultivated for its fragrant leaves used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Ocimum dentatum thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
