Ophrypetalum odoratum

Ophrypetalum odoratum, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elegant, strap-like leaves and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its flowers emit a sweet, honey-like fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ophrypetalum odoratum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
