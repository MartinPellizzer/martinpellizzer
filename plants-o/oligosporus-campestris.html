Oligosporus campestris

Oligosporus campestris, commonly known as the field spore grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. The plant is often found in open fields and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, allowing it to colonize new areas efficiently.

Oligosporus campestris plays a minor role in local ecosystems, contributing to soil stabilization and serving as a food source for various insects and small herbivores.
