Ophiorrhiza ostindica

Ophiorrhiza ostindica, commonly known as the Indian mangrove or coastal shrub, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It thrives in saline and brackish water environments, often found in mangrove forests and tidal areas. The plant has small, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers, followed by capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Ophiorrhiza ostindica plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
