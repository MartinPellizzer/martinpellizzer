Oncostema cerulea

Oncostema cerulea, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant is characterized by its blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

Oncostema cerulea is valued in native gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
