Odontoglossum erosum

Odontoglossum erosum, commonly known as the Ecuadorian orchid, is a tropical orchid native to the Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers with a distinctive yellow lip. The plant typically grows in high-altitude cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in its natural habitat.

Odontoglossum erosum is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant and intricate floral structure.
