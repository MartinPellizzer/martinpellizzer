Operculina altissima

Operculina altissima, commonly known as the skyflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that resemble small umbrellas, consisting of numerous white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is often found in sandy or limestone soils. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and usually have a waxy coating.

Operculina altissima is primarily cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique and eye-catching floral display.
