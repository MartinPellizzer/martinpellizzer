Ocotea reticularis

Ocotea reticularis, commonly known as the fragrant wood or fragrant ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests. Its leaves are usually elliptical with a smooth surface and a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

Ocotea reticularis is valued for its timber, which is used in furniture and construction due to its durability and pleasant fragrance.
