Ophrys × baumanniana nothosubsp. hierapetrae

Ophrys × baumanniana nothosubsp.

hierapetrae, commonly known as the Hierapetra orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Balkan region. It is characterized by its intricate, bee-mimicking flowers that play a crucial role in its pollination strategy.

The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats. Its inflorescence consists of a single spike bearing several showy, saddle-shaped flowers.

The species is notable for its morphological variability and is of interest in botanical studies due to its hybrid origin.
