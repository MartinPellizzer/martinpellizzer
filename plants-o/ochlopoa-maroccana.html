Ochlopoa maroccana

Ochlopoa maroccana, commonly known as the Moroccan sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North Africa.

It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, showcasing its adaptability to moist soil conditions. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats in suitable habitats.

Ochlopoa maroccana plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and supporting local biodiversity.
