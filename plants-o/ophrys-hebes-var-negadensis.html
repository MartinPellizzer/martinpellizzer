Ophrys hebes var. negadensis

Ophrys hebes var. negadensis, commonly known as the Negadens Orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique floral morphology, featuring intricate lip structures that mimic the appearance of female insects, a trait known as pseudocopulation. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments within tropical forests, relying on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its flowers are usually small, with a complex arrangement of sepals and petals that contribute to its ornamental value.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered an endangered species requiring conservation efforts.
