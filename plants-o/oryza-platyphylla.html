Oryza platyphylla

Oryza platyphylla, commonly known as the flat-leaf ricegrass, is a perennial grass native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its flat, blade-like leaves and small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. Its botanical features include slender stems and small, pale green flowers that bloom in early summer.

Oryza platyphylla is of interest to botanists due to its ecological role and potential for genetic study.
