Oxalis scopulorum

Oxalis scopulorum, commonly known as the rock oxalis, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically bright green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often appearing in early spring. Oxalis scopulorum thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

It is often found growing in crevices and on rocky slopes, making it a hardy and resilient species.
