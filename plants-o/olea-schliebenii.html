Olea schliebenii

Olea schliebenii, commonly known as Schlieben's olive, is a species of flowering plant in the Oleaceae family.

It is native to the arid regions of southern Africa, particularly Namibia and Angola. The plant is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. It produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible but not widely consumed by humans.

Olea schliebenii is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, making it a resilient and drought-tolerant species.
