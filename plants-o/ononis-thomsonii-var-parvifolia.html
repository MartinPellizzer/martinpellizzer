Ononis thomsonii var. parvifolia

Ononis thomsonii var. parvifolia, commonly known as Thompson's whitlowgrass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features a mat-forming habit with small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
