Oxalis lanata var. lanata

Oxalis lanata var. lanata, commonly known as the woolly wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves covered in soft, white, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, five-petaled flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Oxalis lanata var. lanata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and delicate flowers.
