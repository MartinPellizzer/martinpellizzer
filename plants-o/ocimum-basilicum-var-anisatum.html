Ocimum basilicum var. anisatum

Ocimum basilicum var. anisatum, commonly known as anise basil, is a variant of sweet basil characterized by its distinct anise-like aroma.

This herb features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often slightly hairy and have a pale green to light green color. The flowers are small, tubular, and typically clustered in terminal spikes, producing small, round, white or pale purple fruits. It is native to tropical regions and thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil.

Anise basil is widely used in culinary applications and herbal remedies due to its aromatic properties.
