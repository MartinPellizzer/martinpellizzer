Oenothera angustissima

Oenothera angustissima, commonly known as narrowleaf evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that open in the evening, hence its common name. It belongs to the Onagraceae family and is known for its medicinal properties due to the presence of essential fatty acids.

Oenothera angustissima thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.
