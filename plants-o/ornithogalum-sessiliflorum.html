Ornithogalum sessiliflorum

Ornithogalum sessiliflorum, commonly known as the sessile-flowered star-of-Bethlehem, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant is adapted to dry conditions and thrives in well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and moths, contributing to its ecological significance.
