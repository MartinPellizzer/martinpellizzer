Othonna laxa

Othonna laxa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved othonna, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It features slender, often hairy stems and small, fleshy, linear leaves that are usually arranged in a rosette or along the stems. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale yellow to white flowers during the warmer months. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves and stems.

Othonna laxa is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
