Oldenlandia angolensis var. hirsuta

Oldenlandia angolensis var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy oldenlandia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. The plant features small, white to pale yellow flowers clustered in dense inflorescences, which are often borne on slender stems. Its leaves are usually hairy, giving the plant its common name, and it has a fibrous root system.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
