Orchis collina var. flavescens

Orchis collina var. flavescens, commonly known as the yellow sand-orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is characterized by its slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in loose racemes, often with a distinctive hooded appearance. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slight sheen.

This variety is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in open habitats such as dunes or rocky slopes.
