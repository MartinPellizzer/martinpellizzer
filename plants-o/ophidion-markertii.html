Ophidion markertii

Ophidion markertii, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has a climbing habit and produces long, slender stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically borne in clusters and have a unique, lantern-like appearance.

Ophidion markertii thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in coastal regions and riverine forests.
