Opuntia segethii

Opuntia segethii, commonly known as the desert pear cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Central America and northern South America.

It is characterized by its flattened, ribbed stems that are covered in spines and are adapted for water storage. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom during the warmer months, followed by red, fleshy fruits resembling small pears. Opuntia segethii is a perennial plant that thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an important species in its native habitats.
