Oenothera boothii var. pygmaea

Oenothera boothii var. pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf evening primrose, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features small, showy yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has hairy, linear leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
