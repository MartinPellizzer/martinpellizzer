Ocotea schottii

Ocotea schottii, commonly known as the Brazilian cinnamom, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and laurel. The plant is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue berries. Its wood is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional medicine.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and commercial contexts.
