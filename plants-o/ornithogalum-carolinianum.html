Ornithogalum carolinianum

Ornithogalum carolinianum, commonly known as Carolina star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features a short, rhizomatous stem and produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in moist, wooded areas. It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its showy, ephemeral blooms.

Ornithogalum carolinianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
