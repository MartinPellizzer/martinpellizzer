Orthochilus coddii

Orthochilus coddii, commonly known as the Codd's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip that is often marked with purple or brown speckles. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
