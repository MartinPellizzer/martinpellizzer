Onobrychis elymaitica

Onobrychis elymaitica, commonly known as the Greek clover, is a perennial legume native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides. Its root system has nitrogen-fixing capabilities, making it useful in soil enrichment.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
