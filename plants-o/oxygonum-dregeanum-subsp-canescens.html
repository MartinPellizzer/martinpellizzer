Oxygonum dregeanum subsp. canescens

Oxygonum dregeanum subsp.

canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf goosefoot, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs.

The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape and have a rough, textured surface. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by edible, round fruits.

This plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.
