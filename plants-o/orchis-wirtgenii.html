Orchis wirtgenii

Orchis wirtgenii, commonly known as the Wirtgen's orchid, is a rare and endangered species native to specific regions of Europe.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and typically grows in moist, calcareous habitats such as woodlands and grasslands. The plant produces striking, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive lip that is often adorned with intricate patterns. Its life cycle is closely tied to the presence of specific mycorrhizal fungi, which are essential for its germination and growth.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
