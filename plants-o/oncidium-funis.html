Oncidium funis

Oncidium funis, commonly known as the funeral orchid, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its pendulous inflorescence and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or green with red or orange markings, and it blooms during the rainy season.

Oncidium funis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
