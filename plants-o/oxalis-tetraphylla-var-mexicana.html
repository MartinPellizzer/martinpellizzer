Oxalis tetraphylla var. mexicana

Oxalis tetraphylla var. mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican wood sorrel, is a species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and woodlands. Its foliage contains oxalic acid, which can be toxic if ingested in large quantities.

Oxalis tetraphylla var. mexicana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.
