Ornithogalum ocellatum

Ornithogalum ocellatum, commonly known as the spotted lily or star-of-Bethlehem, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features striking, trumpet-shaped white flowers with prominent yellow spots, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that form a rosette at the base. Its bulbs are adapted for storage and can remain dormant during dry periods.

Ornithogalum ocellatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
