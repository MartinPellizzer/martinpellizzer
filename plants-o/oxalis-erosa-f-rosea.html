Oxalis erosa f. rosea

Oxalis erosa f. rosea, commonly known as the rose-flowered wood sorrel, is a botanical variant of the species Oxalis erosa.

It is characterized by its striking pink to rose-colored flowers, which bloom in early spring. The plant features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and a trailing growth habit. Native to regions of North America, it thrives in moist, partially shaded environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
