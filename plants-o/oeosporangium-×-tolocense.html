Oeosporangium × tolocense

Oeosporangium × tolocense, commonly known as the hybrid spore-bearing plant, is a unique botanical entity resulting from the cross between two distinct species.

It exhibits characteristics of both parent plants, often displaying a combination of morphological and physiological traits. The plant is notable for its specialized reproductive structures, which include modified leaves that serve as sporangia. These sporangia produce spores that are essential for its reproductive cycle.

Due to its hybrid nature, Oeosporangium × tolocense is of particular interest in botanical research for studying genetic diversity and evolutionary adaptations.
