Oncidium obsoletum

Oncidium obsoletum, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and fascinating orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, often epiphytic growth habit and its ability to thrive without soil, attaching itself to trees or other plants. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or green with intricate patterns, making it a striking addition to any botanical collection. Oncidium obsoletum is known for its challenging cultivation requirements, requiring specific humidity and light conditions to flourish.

Due to its rarity and beauty, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
