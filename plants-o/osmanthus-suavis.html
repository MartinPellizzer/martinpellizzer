Osmanthus suavis

Osmanthus suavis, commonly known as sweet osmanthus, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late autumn. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

Osmanthus suavis is widely cultivated for its aromatic flowers, which are used in teas, perfumes, and traditional Chinese medicine.
