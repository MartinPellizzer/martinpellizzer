Oxyspora paniculata var. rupicola

Oxyspora paniculata var. rupicola, commonly known as the rock panicled oxyspora, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its fibrous root system and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with white or pale yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found growing on rocky outcrops or in dry grasslands.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions.
