Obione salicifolia

Obione salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaf obione, is a unique plant species characterized by its willow-like foliage.

It belongs to the family Apiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and vegetables. The plant features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family.

Obione salicifolia is typically found in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or stream banks.
