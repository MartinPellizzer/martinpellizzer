Oxylobium obovatum

Oxylobium obovatum, commonly known as the broad-leafed oxylobium, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features simple, oval-shaped leaves that are often densely arranged along the stems. Its showy, pink to red flowers bloom in late spring, adding visual interest to the landscape.

Oxylobium obovatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
