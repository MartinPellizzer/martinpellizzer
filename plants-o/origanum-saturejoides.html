Origanum saturejoides

Origanum saturejoides, commonly known as Turkish oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly hairy leaves and small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers well-drained soil in full sun. Its essential oils, rich in compounds like carvacrol and thymol, contribute to its strong medicinal and culinary uses.

It is often used in traditional herbal remedies and as a flavoring in various cuisines.
