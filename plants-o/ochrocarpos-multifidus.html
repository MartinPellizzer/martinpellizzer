Ochrocarpos multifidus

Ochrocarpos multifidus, commonly known as the multifid leaf ochrocarpus, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are deeply divided into multiple narrow segments, giving it a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
