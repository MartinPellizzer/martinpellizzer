Ormosia tavoyana

Ormosia tavoyana, commonly known as Tavoy's ormosia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and is valued for its hard, durable wood. Ormosia tavoyana is also noted for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and floral display.

However, it is considered a vulnerable species due to habitat loss and overharvesting.
