Octotheca plerandroides

Octotheca plerandroides, commonly known as the moonlight fern, is a rare and elusive plant native to the misty highlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bioluminescent fronds that glow faintly under moonlight, a unique adaptation for attracting nocturnal pollinators. The plant's leaves are feather-like, with a complex venation pattern that aids in efficient water retention. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and requires a specific soil composition rich in organic matter.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is listed as endangered and is the subject of ongoing botanical research.
