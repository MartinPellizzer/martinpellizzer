Otacanthus platychilus var. longifolius

Otacanthus platychilus var. longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved otacanthus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves that can reach up to 15 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers with prominent, showy bracts that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
