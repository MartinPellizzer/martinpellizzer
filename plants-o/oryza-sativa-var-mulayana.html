Oryza sativa var. mulayana

Oryza sativa var. mulayana, commonly known as the Malayan rice, is a variant of the common rice species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and small, pale-yellow grains. This variety is typically grown in lowland wet rice fields and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a panicle inflorescence that produces numerous spikelets.

The plant is primarily cultivated for its edible grains, which are a staple food source in many tropical regions.
