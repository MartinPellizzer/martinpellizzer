Opuntia × charlestonensis

Opuntia × charlestonensis, commonly known as the Charleston cholla, is a hybrid cactus species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, spineless stems that are often segmented and covered in a whitish, powdery coating. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by small, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
