Obesia geminata

Obesia geminata, commonly known as the twinleaf orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and purple. The plant has strap-like leaves and produces a unique, fragrant scent that attracts specific pollinators. Due to its limited habitat and threats from deforestation, it is considered endangered.

Conservation efforts are ongoing to protect this rare botanical species.
