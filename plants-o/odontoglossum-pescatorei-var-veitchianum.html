Odontoglossum pescatorei var. veitchianum

Odontoglossum pescatorei var. veitchianum, commonly known as the Veitch's orchid, is a species native to the high-altitude cloud forests of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with sympodial growth, characterized by its thick, fleshy rhizomes and a dense rosette of lanceolate leaves. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences with showy, violet to purple flowers that have a distinctive, intricate pattern. It thrives in cool, humid environments with partial shade and well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and rarity.
