Onobrychis xanthina

Onobrychis xanthina, commonly known as the yellow burclover, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas.

Onobrychis xanthina is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability and historical use in traditional medicine.
