Olax tessmannii

Olax tessmannii, commonly known as the African olive, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Olacaceae and is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and small, white to yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are often eaten by birds and other wildlife. It is adapted to dry and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species in arid regions.

Olax tessmannii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
