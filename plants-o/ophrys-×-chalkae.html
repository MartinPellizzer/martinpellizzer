Ophrys × chalkae

Ophrys × chalkae, commonly known as the Chalk's Ophrys, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Balkans.

It is characterized by its intricate, bee-mimicking flower structure, which plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats. Its flowers exhibit a range of color variations, often featuring a brownish or reddish hue with intricate patterns.

The botanical significance of Ophrys × chalkae lies in its role as a hybrid, showcasing the genetic diversity and evolutionary adaptability of orchids.
