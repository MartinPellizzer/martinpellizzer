Orchis angustifolia subf. venusta

Orchis angustifolia subf.

venusta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved twayblade, is a rare and specialized orchid found in specific habitats across Europe. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and a single, pale yellow or greenish flower spike.

The plant is myco-heterotrophic, meaning it relies on fungal associations for nutrients rather than photosynthesis. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, this orchid is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
