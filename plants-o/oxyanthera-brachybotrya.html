Oxyanthera brachybotrya

Oxyanthera brachybotrya, commonly known as the short-stalked aster, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically blue to purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and wetlands.

Oxyanthera brachybotrya is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
