Ornithogalum elatum

Ornithogalum elatum, commonly known as the star of Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, slender stems that bear clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant is characterized by its unique, star-like appearance, with six to eight petals that are often reflexed backward. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Ornithogalum elatum is also noted for its ability to bloom in early spring, adding a striking visual element to gardens.
