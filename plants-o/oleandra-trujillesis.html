Oleandra trujillesis

Oleandra trujillesis, commonly known as the Trujillo oleander, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant features simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

Oleandra trujillesis is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
