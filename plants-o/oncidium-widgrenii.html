Oncidium widgrenii

Oncidium widgrenii, commonly known as the Widgren's orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, long-lasting inflorescences that feature a mix of yellow, green, and red hues. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are highly fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Oncidium widgrenii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
