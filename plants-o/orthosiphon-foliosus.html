Orthosiphon foliosus

Orthosiphon foliosus, commonly known as Java tea or Malabar tea, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale purple. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Orthosiphon foliosus is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its diuretic and anti-inflammatory effects.
