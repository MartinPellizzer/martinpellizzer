Ophrys × gauthieri subsp. fenarolii

Ophrys × gauthieri subsp.

fenarolii, commonly known as the Fenarolii Orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It is characterized by its intricate, bee-like flowers that exhibit a specialized mimicry of female bees, a trait that aids in its pollination strategy.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and thrives in shaded, woodland environments. Its floral structure includes a prominent lip that serves as a landing platform for pollinators.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitats.
