Orthophytum lymanianum

Orthophytum lymanianum, commonly known as the Lyman's orchid, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a water-holding cup. The plant produces showy, spike-like inflorescences with brightly colored flowers, typically in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated as a houseplant in many regions.
