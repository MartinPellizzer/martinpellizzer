Ophrys × zimmermanniana

Ophrys × zimmermanniana, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant that exhibits unique botanical traits.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera, two species known for their mimicry of female wasps. The plant's most notable feature is its intricate floral structure, which resembles a female wasp to attract male pollinators. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in woodlands or along stream banks.

The orchid's ability to mimic pollinators is a remarkable example of evolutionary adaptation in the plant kingdom.
