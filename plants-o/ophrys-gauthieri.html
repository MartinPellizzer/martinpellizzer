Ophrys gauthieri

Ophrys gauthieri, commonly known as the Gauthier's orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a lip that mimics the appearance of a female bee, which plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is adapted to cool, humid microhabitats. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellowish with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Ophrys gauthieri is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
