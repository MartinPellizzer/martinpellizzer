Orobanche grayana var. feudgei

Orobanche grayana var. feudgei, commonly known as the gray's broomrape, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is characterized by its lack of leaves and its distinctive, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. This variety is typically found in sandy or disturbed soils in arid regions, often parasitizing members of the legume family. Its life cycle involves germinating in response to chemical signals released by host plants, after which it attaches to the host's roots.

Due to its specialized parasitic nature, it plays a unique role in its ecosystem, though it can be considered a weed in agricultural settings.
