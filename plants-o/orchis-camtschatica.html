Orchis camtschatica

Orchis camtschatica, commonly known as the Kamchatka orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Eastern Europe and Siberia.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forested or alpine habitats. The plant features a tall, slender stem with a single, showy violet or purple flower that blooms in late spring to early summer. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the surrounding soil.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
