Oenothera eriensis var. niagarensis

Oenothera eriensis var. niagarensis, commonly known as the Niagaran Evening Primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Onagraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and stream banks. It has opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
