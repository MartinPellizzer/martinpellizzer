Ocotea percurrens

Ocotea percurrens, commonly known as the fragrant ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and have a distinctive scent when crushed.

Ocotea percurrens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
