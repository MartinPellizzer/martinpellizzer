Ocotea phillyreoides

Ocotea phillyreoides, commonly known as the Philippine ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and fragrant, small white flowers. The plant produces oval, woody fruits that contain aromatic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine. Its essential oils are valued for their medicinal properties, including antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects.

Ocotea phillyreoides thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and montane forests.
