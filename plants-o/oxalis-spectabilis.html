Oxalis spectabilis

Oxalis spectabilis, commonly known as the purple shamrock, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive purple veins and produces clusters of five-petaled white or pink flowers. The plant is a member of the Oxalidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for shady garden spaces.

Oxalis spectabilis is also valued for its ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
