Oxylobium emarginatum var. major

Oxylobium emarginatum var. major, commonly known as the broadleaf oxylobium, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or swampy areas. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a distinctive emarginate (notched) base.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
