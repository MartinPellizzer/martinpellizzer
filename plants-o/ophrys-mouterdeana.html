Ophrys mouterdeana

Ophrys mouterdeana, commonly known as the Mouterdean bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which closely resembles a female bee, a mimicry strategy used to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in cool, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brownish in color, with intricate patterns that aid in its deceptive pollination mechanism.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Ophrys mouterdeana is considered a vulnerable species.
