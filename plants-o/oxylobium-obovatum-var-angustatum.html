Oxylobium obovatum var. angustatum

Oxylobium obovatum var. angustatum, commonly known as narrow-leaf oxylobium, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, pink to reddish-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils along stream banks and in pine woodlands. It is a perennial shrub that produces capsule fruits containing several seeds.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and ecologically valuable species in native plant gardens.
