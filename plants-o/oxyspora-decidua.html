Oxyspora decidua

Oxyspora decidua, commonly known as the deciduous oxyspora, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow and fall off in the dry season. The plant produces clusters of delicate, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom during the wet season. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or disturbed areas.

Due to its unique seasonal behavior, it plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its native habitat.
