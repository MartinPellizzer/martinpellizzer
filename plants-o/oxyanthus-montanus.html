Oxyanthus montanus

Oxyanthus montanus, commonly known as the mountain oxyanthus, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows at high altitudes, often in rocky or alpine environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Oxyanthus montanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
