Oberonia diura

Oberonia diura, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking yellow flowers with red markings. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils, while its leaves are elongated and strap-like.

Oberonia diura is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
