Ocotea caudatifolia

Ocotea caudatifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests. Its leaves are elongated, glossy, and have a distinct aromatic scent when crushed.

Ocotea caudatifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
