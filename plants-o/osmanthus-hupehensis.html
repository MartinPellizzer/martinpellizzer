Osmanthus hupehensis

Osmanthus hupehensis, commonly known as the sweet osmanthus, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late autumn. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy on top. Its fruit is a small, dark brown drupe that contains a single seed.

Osmanthus hupehensis is widely cultivated for its aromatic flowers, which are used in teas, perfumes, and traditional Chinese medicine.
