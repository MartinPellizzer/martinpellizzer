Oxalis acetosella f. albovenosa

Oxalis acetosella f. albovenosa, commonly known as the white wood sorrel, is a variant of the common wood sorrel.

It is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, delicate flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with a waxy coating, and the flowers are usually white or pale yellow. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
