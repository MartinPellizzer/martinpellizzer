Ononis antennata subsp. antennata

Ononis antennata subsp.

antennata, commonly known as the small flax or lesser flax, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae. It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pale yellow to greenish-yellow flowers.

The plant produces small, lens-shaped pods that contain several seeds. Native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia, it thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and habitat restoration.
