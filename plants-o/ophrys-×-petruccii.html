Ophrys × petruccii

Ophrys × petruccii, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant that exhibits unique botanical features.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Ophrys apifera, the bee orchid, and Ophrys insectifera, the wasp orchid. This species is characterized by its intricate, bee-like floral morphology, which includes a prominent lip that mimics the appearance of a female bee. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is found in various regions across Europe.

Its reproductive strategy relies on myrmecophily, where it attracts male bees through visual and chemical mimicry to facilitate pollination.
