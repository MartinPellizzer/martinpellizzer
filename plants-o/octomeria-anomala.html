Octomeria anomala

Octomeria anomala, commonly known as the golden star cactus, is a unique cactus species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its common name. The plant has a compact, branching structure with thick, ribbed stems that store water, typical of desert flora. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and providing protection from herbivores.

Octomeria anomala thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for arid garden environments.
