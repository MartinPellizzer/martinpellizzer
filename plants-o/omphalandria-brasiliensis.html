Omphalandria brasiliensis

Omphalandria brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its roots are specialized for epiphytic growth, allowing it to thrive on tree trunks and branches.

The botanical features of Omphalandria brasiliensis make it an important component of the rainforest's biodiversity.
