Oxytropis grenardii

Oxytropis grenardii, commonly known as Grenard's locoweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces clusters of yellow to greenish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It has small, palmately compound leaves and is adapted to grow in dry, rocky habitats.
