Oplismenus sabulicola

Oplismenus sabulicola, commonly known as the beach morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in sandy, well-drained soils along the shoreline. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple, blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Oplismenus sabulicola is adapted to salt spray and wind exposure, making it a hardy species found in dune ecosystems.
