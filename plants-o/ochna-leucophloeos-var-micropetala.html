Ochna leucophloeos var. micropetala

Ochna leucophloeos var. micropetala, commonly known as the small-leaved white-wood ochna, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually small and leathery in texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that are typically fragrant. The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, and the plant is often found in moist, shaded habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
