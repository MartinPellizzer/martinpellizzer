Oberonia denticulata var. brevifolia

Oberonia denticulata var. brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved toothed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, toothed leaf margins. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers clustered in a spike, which are typical of the Orchidaceae family. It thrives in shaded, humid environments with well-drained, acidic soil.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat requirements, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
