Ophrys sphegodes f. subfucifera

Ophrys sphegodes f. subfucifera, commonly known as the bee orchid, is a fascinating plant with unique botanical features.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, bee-like flowers that mimic the appearance and scent of female bees. The plant is a perennial herb with a robust rhizome system, allowing it to thrive in various soil types. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow with a dark brown or black marking, enhancing its mimicry.

This orchid is known for its specialized pollination mechanism, attracting male bees through its floral mimicry.
