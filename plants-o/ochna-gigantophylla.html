Ochna gigantophylla

Ochna gigantophylla, commonly known as the giant-leaf ochna, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that are clustered in panicles, attracting various pollinators. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release its seeds.

Ochna gigantophylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage and vibrant floral display.
