Oxytropis viscida var. hudsonica

Oxytropis viscida var. hudsonica, commonly known as Hudson's sticky locoweed, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as sagebrush steppe and open woodlands. Its leaves are compound, with several pairs of leaflets that are often covered in fine hairs.

This species is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native range.
