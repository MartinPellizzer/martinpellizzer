Onobrychis citrina

Onobrychis citrina, commonly known as yellow sweet clover, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in dry grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a creeping growth habit with woody stems and produces small, bean-shaped pods containing seeds.

Onobrychis citrina is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it a valuable plant in soil enrichment.
