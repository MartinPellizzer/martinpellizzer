Oleandra welwitschii

Oleandra welwitschii, commonly known as the Welwitsch oleander, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping. Its leaves are lance-shaped and glossy, while the stems are often woody and branched.

Oleandra welwitschii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging climates.
