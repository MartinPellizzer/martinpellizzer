Odontochilus sibelae

Odontochilus sibelae, commonly known as the Sibel's toothed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, tooth-like structures on its leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on tree trunks or among leaf litter. Its flowers are small and fragrant, usually appearing in clusters during the rainy season.

Odontochilus sibelae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
