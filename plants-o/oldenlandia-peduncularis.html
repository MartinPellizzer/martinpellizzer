Oldenlandia peduncularis

Oldenlandia peduncularis, commonly known as the red spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often near water bodies. The plant features slender, erect stems that bear small, clustered flowers with striking red or orange petals. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a waxy coating.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist environments, making it a common sight in wetlands and along stream banks.
