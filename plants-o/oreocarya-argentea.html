Oreocarya argentea

Oreocarya argentea, commonly known as the silverleaf flax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Oreocarya argentea thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or arid environments.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
