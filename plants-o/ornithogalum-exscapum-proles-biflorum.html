Ornithogalum exscapum proles biflorum

Ornithogalum exscapum proles biflorum, commonly known as the biflorous cowslip, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a short, rhizomatous stem with narrow, grass-like leaves arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces a single, tall flowering stem that bears two opposite, bell-shaped flowers with white to pale yellow petals. The flowers are typically fragrant and bloom in late summer to early autumn.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or rocky areas.
