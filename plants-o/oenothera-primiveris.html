Oenothera primiveris

Oenothera primiveris, commonly known as the early evening flower, is a species of flowering plant in the evening primrose family.

It is native to the central and eastern United States and thrives in dry, disturbed soils. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that open in the late afternoon and close at night. Its stems are often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly hairy surface.

Oenothera primiveris is known for its adaptability and is often found in prairies, roadsides, and other open habitats.
