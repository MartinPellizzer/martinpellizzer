Orobanche hermonis

Orobanche hermonis, commonly known as the desert broomrape, is a parasitic plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is found in arid and semi-arid regions, particularly in the Middle East and North Africa. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It has a short, fleshy stem with no true leaves, and its root system consists of haustoria that attach to the roots of its host.

Due to its parasitic nature and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
