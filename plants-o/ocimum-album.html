Ocimum album

Ocimum album, commonly known as holy basil, is an aromatic herb native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are rich in essential oils, containing compounds like eugenol and rosmarinic acid.

Ocimum album is widely cultivated for its medicinal and culinary uses.
