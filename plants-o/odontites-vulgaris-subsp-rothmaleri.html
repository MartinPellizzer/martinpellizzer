Odontites vulgaris subsp. rothmaleri

Odontites vulgaris subsp.

rothmaleri, commonly known as Rothmaler's toothed grass, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves with distinctive toothed margins, and its small, greenish-white flowers are arranged in spikelets. It is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at higher elevations.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
