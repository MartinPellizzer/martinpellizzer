Oryza sativa var. pseudojarhanica

Oryza sativa var. pseudojarhanica, commonly known as the pseudojarhanica rice variety, is a less commonly cultivated rice species within the Oryza genus.

It is characterized by its small, elongated grains and typically grows in wetland environments. This variety is known for its genetic diversity and has been studied for its potential in rice breeding programs. It exhibits certain traits that may enhance its resilience to environmental stresses.

However, it is not as widely used in commercial agriculture as other rice varieties.
