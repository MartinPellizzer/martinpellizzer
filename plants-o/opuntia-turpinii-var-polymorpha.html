Opuntia turpinii var. polymorpha

Opuntia turpinii var. polymorpha, commonly known as the Turpin's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its flattened, paddle-shaped stems known as cladodes, which are covered in sharp spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the summer months, followed by edible, spherical fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and full sunlight.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of food and traditional medicine.
