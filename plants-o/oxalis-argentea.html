Oxalis argentea

Oxalis argentea, commonly known as silver wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Oxalis argentea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and easy care requirements.
