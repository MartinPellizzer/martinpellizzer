Orchis × atropurpurea

Orchis × atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple orchid, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Orchis morio and Orchis mascula.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem that can reach up to 1 meter in height, bearing a dense spike of flowers. The flowers are typically purple or violet in color, with a distinctive three-lobed lip that is often marked with darker veins. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats.

It is a perennial herb that blooms from late spring to early summer.
