Ophrys fusca subsp. cinereophila

Ophrys fusca subsp.

cinereophila, commonly known as the ash-colored bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to parts of Europe. It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination.

The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and heaths. Its flowers are predominantly brown or ash-gray in color, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, this subspecies is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
