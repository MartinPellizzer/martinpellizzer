Oxalis fragrans

Oxalis fragrans, commonly known as the sweet-scented wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive leaflets and produces clusters of small, delicate flowers in shades of white, pink, or red. The plant is known for its fragrant scent, which becomes more pronounced when the flowers are in bloom. Oxalis fragrans thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and indoor cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant foliage and delicate blossoms.
