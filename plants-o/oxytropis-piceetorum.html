Oxytropis piceetorum

Oxytropis piceetorum, commonly known as the purple milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and Mongolia. The plant is characterized by its erect stems, trifoliate leaves, and showy purple or pink flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Oxytropis piceetorum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for grazing animals.
