Osterdamia macrostachya

Osterdamia macrostachya, commonly known as the giant marsh orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 centimeters in length. The plant typically grows in wetland and marsh habitats, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its flowers are usually pink to purple in color, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination by attracting specific insect species.

Osterdamia macrostachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its native wetland ecosystems.
