Ocimum dimidiatum

Ocimum dimidiatum, commonly known as the purple basil or wild basil, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 cm, with opposite, ovate leaves that have a slightly hairy texture. Its small, pale purple flowers are clustered in whorls along the stems, and it produces small, spherical fruits containing numerous seeds.

Ocimum dimidiatum thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
