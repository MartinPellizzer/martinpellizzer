Ophiorrhiza paniculiformis

Ophiorrhiza paniculiformis, commonly known as the panicle-fruit banyan, is a tropical shrub belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in dense panicles, which are typically borne on the upper parts of the stems. Its fruits are small, dry capsules that split open to release seeds.

Ophiorrhiza paniculiformis is often found in moist, shaded environments in Southeast Asia.
