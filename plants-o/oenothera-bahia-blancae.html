Oenothera bahia-blancae

Oenothera bahia-blancae, commonly known as the Bahia Blanca evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Oenothera bahia-blancae is part of the evening primrose family (Onagraceae) and is known for its adaptability to arid environments.
