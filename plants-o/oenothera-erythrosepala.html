Oenothera erythrosepala

Oenothera erythrosepala, commonly known as the red-sepaled evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that open in the evening and close by morning. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oily seeds. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Oenothera erythrosepala is adapted to arid environments and is often found in dry, rocky soils.
