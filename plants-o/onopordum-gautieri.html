Onopordum gautieri

Onopordum gautieri, commonly known as the Gautier's thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to withstand arid conditions. It produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal, aiding in its propagation.

The species is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
