Oenothera heterantha var. taraxacifolia

Oenothera heterantha var. taraxacifolia, commonly known as the taraxacifolia evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite leaves that resemble those of dandelions. The plant produces yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
