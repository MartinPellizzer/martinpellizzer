Osmundastrum claytonianum subsp. pilosum

Osmundastrum claytonianum subsp.

pilosum, commonly known as the silky fern, is a member of the Osmundaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, finely divided fronds. It is a perennial plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The fronds are pinnately compound, with a soft, silky texture due to the dense covering of minute scales. It produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in strobila, a feature typical of ferns.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
