Oreocalamus communis

Oreocalamus communis, commonly known as the common reedgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along riverbanks, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are usually arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Oreocalamus communis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
