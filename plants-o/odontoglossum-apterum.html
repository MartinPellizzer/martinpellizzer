Odontoglossum apterum

Odontoglossum apterum, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, hairy foliage and striking, violet or purple flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in shaded, moist habitats. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the rocky substrates it inhabits.

Odontoglossum apterum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
