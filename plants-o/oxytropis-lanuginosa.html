Oxytropis lanuginosa

Oxytropis lanuginosa, commonly known as wooly milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish foliage and erect stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats.

Oxytropis lanuginosa plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
