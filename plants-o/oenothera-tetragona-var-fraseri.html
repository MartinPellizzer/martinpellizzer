Oenothera tetragona var. fraseri

Oenothera tetragona var. fraseri, commonly known as the Fraser's evening primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.

Its botanical characteristics include a taproot system and capsule fruits that release seeds through explosive dispersal.
