Ostrya mandshurica

Ostrya mandshurica, commonly known as Manchurian hop-hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its small, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The tree produces small, yellowish-green catkins that appear in early spring. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, often peeling in thin, papery layers.

Ostrya mandshurica is typically found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.
