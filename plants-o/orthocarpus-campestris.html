Orthocarpus campestris

Orthocarpus campestris, commonly known as the field thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia. It has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.

Orthocarpus campestris is often found in meadows, roadsides, and agricultural fields.
