Oxalis hirta var. canescens

Oxalis hirta var. canescens, commonly known as the hairy wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and stems that often spread along the ground.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
