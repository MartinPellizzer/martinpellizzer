Orchis papilionacea var. expansa

Orchis papilionacea var. expansa, commonly known as the broad-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, butterfly-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant features a tall, upright stem with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
