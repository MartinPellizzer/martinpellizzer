Ocotea pseudopalmana

Ocotea pseudopalmana, commonly known as the yellow sandarac tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and fragrant, yellowish wood. The plant is notable for its unique chemical composition, containing various essential oils and compounds used in traditional medicine. Its habitat is typically in moist, shaded forest understories, where it contributes to the biodiversity of the ecosystem.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
