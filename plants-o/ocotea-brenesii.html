Ocotea brenesii

Ocotea brenesii, commonly known as the Guatemalan ocotea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences, followed by dark blue berries.

Ocotea brenesii is often found in moist, well-drained soils in lowland and montane forests.
