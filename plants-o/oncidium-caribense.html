Oncidium caribense

Oncidium caribense, commonly known as the Caribbean orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to the Caribbean region.

It typically grows on trees or rocks, utilizing its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the environment. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, often arranged in a raceme. Its leaves are narrow and strap-like, adapted to efficient water retention in its humid habitat.

Oncidium caribense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
