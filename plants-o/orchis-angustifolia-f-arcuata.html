Orchis angustifolia f. arcuata

Orchis angustifolia f. arcuata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to specific regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and a single, slender spike of pale yellow to greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its intricate floral structure and mycorrhizal associations.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered in many areas, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
