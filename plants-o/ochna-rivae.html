Ochna rivae

Ochna rivae, commonly known as the river ochna, is a shrub native to parts of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by bright red, fleshy fruits that are edible for certain animals.

Ochna rivae thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riverine forests and along streambanks.
