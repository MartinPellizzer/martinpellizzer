Oncidium ovatilabium

Oncidium ovatilabium, commonly known as the oval-labellum orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, pendant inflorescences that bear numerous small, fragrant flowers. The plant has strap-like leaves that are usually arranged in a loose rosette, and its roots are adapted for epiphytic growth. The flowers typically feature a yellow lip with a contrasting red or orange center, making it visually striking.

Oncidium ovatilabium is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
