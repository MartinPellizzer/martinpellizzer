Oreophyton

Oreophyton, commonly known as the "mountain plant," is an ancient plant species that lived during the Paleozoic era.

It is considered one of the earliest known plants with a true root system, distinguishing it from earlier plant forms. Oreophyton typically had a short, upright stem with small, scale-like leaves. Its structure suggests it was adapted to rocky, mountainous environments.

Fossilized remains of Oreophyton provide important insights into the evolution of early land plants.
