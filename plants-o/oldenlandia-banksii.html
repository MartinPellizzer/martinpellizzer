Oldenlandia banksii

Oldenlandia banksii, commonly known as the silverleaf or silverweed, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. The plant features small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, and its leaves are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It has a fibrous root system and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Oldenlandia banksii is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its resilience to drought.
