Ornithogalum candidum var. tubiforme

Ornithogalum candidum var. tubiforme, commonly known as the white bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features strap-like, pale green leaves that form a rosette at the base. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Its flowers are typically borne on a tall, leafless stem, and the plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Ornithogalum candidum var. tubiforme is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
