Onobrychis ornata subsp. atropatana

Onobrychis ornata subsp.

atropatana, commonly known as the Persian vetch, is a leguminous plant native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some use in traditional medicine.
