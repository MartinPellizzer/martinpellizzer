Olyra obliqua

Olyra obliqua, commonly known as the narrow-leafed acanthus, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and parts of Asia. The plant is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple. Olyra obliqua is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and showy blooms.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
