Orchis nervata

Orchis nervata, commonly known as the veined orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem that can reach up to 1 meter in height, adorned with lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces a spike of fragrant, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive veined pattern on the petals. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodlands and meadows.

Orchis nervata is known for its ecological importance, supporting various pollinators and serving as an indicator species for habitat quality.
