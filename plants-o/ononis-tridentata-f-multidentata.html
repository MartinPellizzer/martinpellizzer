Ononis tridentata f. multidentata

Ononis tridentata f. multidentata, commonly known as the three-toothed cinquefoil, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its trifoliate leaves and bright yellow flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or arctic environments. Its small, five-petaled flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees.

The subspecies f. multidentata is distinguished by its more finely divided leaves compared to the typical form.
