Origanum striatum

Origanum striatum, commonly known as the stripeleaf oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves with distinctive white or pale green stripes running along their length. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in summer, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its essential oils contribute to its use in culinary and medicinal applications.
