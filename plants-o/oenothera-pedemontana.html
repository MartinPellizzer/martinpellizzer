Oenothera pedemontana

Oenothera pedemontana, commonly known as the mountain evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.
