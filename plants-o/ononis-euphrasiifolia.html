Ononis euphrasiifolia

Ononis euphrasiifolia, commonly known as the Persian sainfoin, is a flowering plant native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being oval to lance-shaped. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as steppes and grasslands.

Ononis euphrasiifolia is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it valuable in ecological restoration projects.
