Ocotea pulchella var. vaccinioides

Ocotea pulchella var. vaccinioides, commonly known as the red sandalwood or red cedar, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, dark reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and fine grain. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a glossy surface, while its flowers are small and clustered in panicles.

This species is often found in humid lowland forests and is known for its ecological and economic significance.
