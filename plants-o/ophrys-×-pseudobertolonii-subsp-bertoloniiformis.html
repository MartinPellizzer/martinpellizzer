Ophrys × pseudobertolonii subsp. bertoloniiformis

Ophrys × pseudobertolonii subsp.

bertoloniiformis, commonly known as the Bertoloniiform orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the genus Ophrys, which is characterized by its intricate, often insect-like flower structures designed for pollination through mimicry.

The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and thrives in sunny, open habitats. Its flowers exhibit a complex morphology with specialized structures that mimic female insects, attracting male counterparts for pollination.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
