Oncidium bohnkianum

Oncidium bohnkianum, commonly known as the Bohnken orchid, is a tropical orchid species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its elegant, pendulous inflorescence that bears numerous small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Oncidium bohnkianum thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.
