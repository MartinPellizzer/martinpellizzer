Ocotea effusa

Ocotea effusa, commonly known as the white ocotea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests, often reaching heights of up to 15 meters. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a glossy surface with a distinctive aromatic scent.

The flowers are small and clustered, and the fruit is a small, dark blue drupe that contains a single seed.
