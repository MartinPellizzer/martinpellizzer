Oxytropis litwinowii

Oxytropis litwinowii, commonly known as the Litwinow's locoweed, is a perennial herb native to the steppes of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with stipules and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is adapted to dry, arid environments and is often found in open grasslands and semi-desert regions.

Oxytropis litwinowii is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in nutrient-poor areas.
