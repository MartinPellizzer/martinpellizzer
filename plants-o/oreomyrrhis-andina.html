Oreomyrrhis andina

Oreomyrrhis andina, commonly known as the Andean orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes Mountains.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and has adapted to the cool, humid conditions of its mountainous habitat. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Oreomyrrhis andina is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural environment.
