Oxalis alsinoides

Oxalis alsinoides, commonly known as the yellow woodsorrel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which resemble those of clover, and small, delicate yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and woodlands. It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Oxalis alsinoides is also valued for its ability to thrive in partial shade and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
