Oxalis griffithii subsp. griffithii

Oxalis griffithii subsp.

griffithii, commonly known as the Indian sorrel, is a species of flowering plant in the Oxalidaceae family. It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, which are heart-shaped and often have a reddish or purple tinge.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically appearing in clusters. It is native to the Indian subcontinent and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Oxalis griffithii subsp.

griffithii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
