Orchis × adriatica

Orchis × adriatica, commonly known as the Adriatic orchid, is a naturally occurring hybrid between *Orchis morio* and *Orchis mascula*.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that typically range in color from purple to pink. The plant produces a single, erect spike bearing several flowers, each with a distinctive three-lobed lip. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

The orchid is a perennial herb that blooms from late spring to early summer.
