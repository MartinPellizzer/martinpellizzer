Ophrys migoutiana

Ophrys migoutiana, commonly known as the Migout's bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the high-altitude regions of the Mediterranean, particularly in France and Italy.

It is characterized by its unique flower structure, which closely resembles a female bee, a mimicry strategy used to attract male bees for pollination. The plant produces a single, erect stem with a few leaves and a single, showy flower that is typically greenish-yellow with dark markings. It thrives in nutrient-poor, calcareous soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Ophrys migoutiana is considered a vulnerable species.
