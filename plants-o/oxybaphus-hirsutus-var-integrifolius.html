Oxybaphus hirsutus var. integrifolius

Oxybaphus hirsutus var. integrifolius, commonly known as the smooth-leafed oxybaphus, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its hairy stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves with smooth margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its compact growth and attractive foliage.
