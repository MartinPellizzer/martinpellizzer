Orchis georgica

Orchis georgica, commonly known as the George's orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stem and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and thrives in woodland or meadow habitats. Its flowers are often adorned with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Orchis georgica is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
