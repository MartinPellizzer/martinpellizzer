Odontites lanceolatus subsp. olotensis

Odontites lanceolatus subsp.

olotensis, commonly known as the Olotenese toothed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe. It is characterized by its lanceolate, toothed leaves and produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color.

The plant thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
