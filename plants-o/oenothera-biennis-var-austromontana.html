Oenothera biennis var. austromontana

Oenothera biennis var. austromontana, commonly known as the Australian evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to southern Australia.

It features showy yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, oily seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats like grasslands and coastal dunes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses due to its rich seed oil content.
