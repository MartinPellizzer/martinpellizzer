Ophrys herminium

Ophrys herminium, commonly known as the purple helleborine, is a perennial orchid native to Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, lip-like petal that mimics the appearance of a female bee, aiding in its specialized pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in shaded, woodland environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its flowers, which range in color from purple to yellow, bloom in late spring to early summer.

Ophrys herminium is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is notable for its intricate floral structure and mimicry adaptations.
