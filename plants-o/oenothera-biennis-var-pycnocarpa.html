Oenothera biennis var. pycnocarpa

Oenothera biennis var. pycnocarpa, commonly known as the compact evening primrose, is a variant of the evening primrose native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and shorter stature compared to the typical variety. The plant produces showy yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and the stems are often reddish in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
