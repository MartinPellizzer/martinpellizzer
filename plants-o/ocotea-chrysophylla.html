Ocotea chrysophylla

Ocotea chrysophylla, commonly known as golden-leaved ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its striking golden-yellow foliage, which gives it its common name. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a fragrant aroma.

Ocotea chrysophylla typically grows in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
