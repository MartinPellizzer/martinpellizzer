Oxytropis kasakorum

Oxytropis kasakorum, commonly known as the Kazakh locoweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically purple or pink in color. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Oxytropis kasakorum is also known for its toxic properties, which can affect livestock that ingest its foliage.
