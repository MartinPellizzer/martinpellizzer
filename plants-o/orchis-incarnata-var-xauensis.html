Orchis incarnata var. xauensis

Orchis incarnata var. xauensis, commonly known as the Xauensis Ladies' Tresses, is a rare and endangered orchid species native to specific regions of China.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fragrant, and densely packed inflorescence that resembles a tresses of hair. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows. Its flowers are usually pink to purple in color, with a unique three-lobed lip that aids in pollination.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
