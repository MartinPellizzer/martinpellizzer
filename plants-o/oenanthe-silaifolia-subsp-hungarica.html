Oenanthe silaifolia subsp. hungarica

Oenanthe silaifolia subsp.

hungarica, commonly known as the Hungarian water dropwort, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils along stream banks and wet meadows.

The plant features slender, erect stems that are often hollow, with finely divided, fern-like leaves. Its small, white flowers are clustered in umbels and bloom in early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
