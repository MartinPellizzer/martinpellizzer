Oplismenus pratensis

Oplismenus pratensis, commonly known as field sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist or wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and riverbanks. The plant has a slender, triangular stem and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. Its leaves are grass-like, with a distinct midrib and a rough texture.

Oplismenus pratensis is often found in temperate regions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
