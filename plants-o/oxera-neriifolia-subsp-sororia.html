Oxera neriifolia subsp. sororia

Oxera neriifolia subsp.

sororia, commonly known as the silverleaf oxera, is a species of flowering plant native to South America. It is characterized by its dense, silvery-white foliage that gives it its common name.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a velvety texture, contributing to its ornamental value.
