Onobrychis olivieri

Onobrychis olivieri, commonly known as the Sicilian clover, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a deep root system, which allows it to thrive in poor soils and arid conditions. It is often used in agricultural settings for forage and soil stabilization.

Onobrychis olivieri is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, contributing to soil fertility.
