Olmedia obliqua

Olmedia obliqua, commonly known as the narrowleaf olmedia, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often slightly curled at the edges. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers appear in clusters during the summer months.

Olmedia obliqua is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
