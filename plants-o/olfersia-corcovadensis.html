Olfersia corcovadensis

Olfersia corcovadensis, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a species of epiphytic cactus native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its segmented, leaf-like stems that are often brightly colored and arranged in a branching pattern. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink and orange, typically blooming during the holiday season. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Olfersia corcovadensis is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
