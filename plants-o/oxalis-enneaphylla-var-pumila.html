Oxalis enneaphylla var. pumila

Oxalis enneaphylla var. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to South America.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins, and it produces delicate, five-petaled flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to spread via rhizomes, forming dense ground covers.

Oxalis enneaphylla var. pumila is also noted for its ability to tolerate humid conditions and its role in attracting pollinators.
