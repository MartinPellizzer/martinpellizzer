Onobrychis laconica

Onobrychis laconica, commonly known as the Laconic clover, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone areas. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with Rhizobium bacteria.

Onobrychis laconica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
