Ornithogalum disciferum

Ornithogalum disciferum, commonly known as the star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of star-shaped, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, and its flowers are arranged in a loose umbel. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ornithogalum disciferum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
