Oxalis ceratilis

Oxalis ceratilis, commonly known as the horned wood sorrel, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive horn-like protrusions on the leaflets, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Oxalis ceratilis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and easy care requirements.
