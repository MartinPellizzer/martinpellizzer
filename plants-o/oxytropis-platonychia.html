Oxytropis platonychia

Oxytropis platonychia, commonly known as the broad-leaved locoweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and Mongolia. The plant features a robust taproot system and produces clusters of small, yellow pea-like flowers. Its leaves are palmately compound, with several pairs of oval to lance-shaped leaflets.

Oxytropis platonychia is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments and is often found in steppe and desert ecosystems.
