Orchis mascula f. acutiflora

Orchis mascula f. acutiflora, commonly known as the sharp-flowered male orchid, is a botanical variant of the male orchid species native to Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and distinctive, showy flowers with a deep violet or purple hue. The flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, and the plant thrives in moist, calcareous soils. It is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and other open habitats.

This orchid is notable for its intricate floral structure and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and moths.
