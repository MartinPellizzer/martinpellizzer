Opuntia decumana

Opuntia decumana, commonly known as the Desert Pricklypear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems that are covered in small, spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits known as tunas. Opuntia decumana is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick stems and having a shallow root system.

It plays an important ecological role by providing food and shelter for various desert wildlife.
