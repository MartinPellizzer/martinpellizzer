Oxalis hirsuta

Oxalis hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, often clustered in groups of three. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

Oxalis hirsuta is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated as a garden plant.
