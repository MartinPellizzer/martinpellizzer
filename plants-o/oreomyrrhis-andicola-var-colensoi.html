Oreomyrrhis andicola var. colensoi

Oreomyrrhis andicola var. colensoi, commonly known as the Colenso's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pale green to brownish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments, typically in forest understories or on rocky slopes. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is notable for its limited distribution and is considered a rare and endangered species within its native range.
