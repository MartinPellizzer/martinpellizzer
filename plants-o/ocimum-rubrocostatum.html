Ocimum rubrocostatum

Ocimum rubrocostatum, commonly known as the red-costate basil, is a species of flowering plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves, which have a distinct scent similar to other basil varieties. Its flowers are typically purple or violet, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Ocimum rubrocostatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a culinary herb.
