Ophrys × araniferiformis

Ophrys × araniferiformis, commonly known as the mimic orchid, is a hybrid species within the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its intricate floral structure, which closely resembles the appearance of female wasps, a trait evolved to facilitate pollination by male wasps. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, while its flowers are highly specialized, featuring a complex arrangement of petals and sepals.

This orchid is valued for its unique morphology and ecological role in supporting pollinator interactions.
