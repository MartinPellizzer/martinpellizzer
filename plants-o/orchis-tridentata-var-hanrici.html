Orchis tridentata var. hanrici

Orchis tridentata var. hanrici, commonly known as the Hanrici orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of Europe.

It is a perennial herb with a distinctive appearance, featuring tall, erect stems and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or grassland habitats. Its flowers are bilaterally symmetrical, with three sepals and three petals, one of which is prominently spurred.

This orchid is known for its ecological importance, supporting specific pollinators and contributing to biodiversity in its native regions.
