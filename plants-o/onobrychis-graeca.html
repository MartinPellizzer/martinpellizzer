Onobrychis graeca

Onobrychis graeca, commonly known as Greek clover, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and open habitats. The plant features small, trifoliate leaves and produces yellow or reddish-purple flowers in racemes. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a deep root system that helps it thrive in arid conditions.

Onobrychis graeca is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
