Oropetium erythraeum

Oropetium erythraeum, commonly known as the red sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent stems and leaves, which are adapted to store water in response to drought conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. Its root system is extensive and deep, allowing it to access moisture from lower soil layers.

Oropetium erythraeum is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is considered a hardy, drought-tolerant species.
