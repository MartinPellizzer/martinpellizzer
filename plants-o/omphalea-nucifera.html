Omphalea nucifera

Omphalea nucifera, commonly known as the custard apple, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and produces large, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow. The plant bears edible fruits that are rough-skinned and have a creamy, aromatic pulp. Its fruit is often used in culinary applications, while the plant has also been used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for both its ornamental and practical uses in tropical regions.
