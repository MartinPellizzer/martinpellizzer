Ourouparia sclerophylla

Ourouparia sclerophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leafed hakea, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves that are typically green or grey-green in color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, which bloom in spring. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing sclerophyllous traits that help it survive in dry conditions.

This species is often found in heathlands and woodlands, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
