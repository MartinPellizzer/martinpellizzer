Orchis × penzigiana

Orchis × penzigiana, commonly known as the Penzig orchid, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Orchis morio and Orchis purpurea.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe, particularly in the Alps and surrounding regions. The plant produces showy, violet to purple flowers with a distinctive spurred lip, typical of many orchid species. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, shady woodland environments.

Orchis × penzigiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
