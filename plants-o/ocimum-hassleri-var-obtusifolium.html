Ocimum hassleri var. obtusifolium

Ocimum hassleri var. obtusifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf basil, is a variant of the Ocimum genus native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant is an herbaceous perennial that thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil. Its leaves are often used in culinary and medicinal applications due to their aromatic properties.

It is closely related to other basil species but distinguishes itself through its unique leaf shape and growth habits.
