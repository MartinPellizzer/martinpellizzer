Oldenlandia nudicaulis

Oldenlandia nudicaulis, commonly known as the "Whiteweed," is a small flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with slender, hairy stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Oldenlandia nudicaulis thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.
