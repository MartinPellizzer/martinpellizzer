Oenothera pumila var. rectipilis

Oenothera pumila var. rectipilis, commonly known as the dwarf evening primrose, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features small, showy yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and native plant gardens.
