Ophrys × nettii

Ophrys × nettii, commonly known as the nettle orchid, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is characterized by its distinctive floral morphology, featuring a labellum that mimics the appearance of a female wasp, a trait used to attract male wasps for pollination. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Ophrys × nettii is a perennial herb with a short rhizome and produces a single, erect stem bearing a few showy flowers.
