Ophrys attaviria f. thracica

Ophrys attaviria f. thracica, commonly known as the Thrace Orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the Balkan region.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which closely resembles female bees, a feature evolved to facilitate pollination through mimicry. The plant produces a single, showy flower with a complex morphology, including a labellum that mimics the appearance of a bee's face. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species.
