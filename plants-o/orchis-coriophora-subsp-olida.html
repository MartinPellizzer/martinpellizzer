Orchis coriophora subsp. olida

Orchis coriophora subsp.

olida, commonly known as the Narrow-leaved Heath Orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of Europe. It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in acidic, moist habitats such as bogs and heathlands.

The plant features a slender, hairy stem with a single, showy flower that is usually pale green to yellowish in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it blooms from June to August.

The species is known for its ecological importance, supporting specific pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
