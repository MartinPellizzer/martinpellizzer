Opsianthes gauroides

Opsianthes gauroides, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow inflorescences that resemble flames, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces small, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a street tree in urban landscapes.
