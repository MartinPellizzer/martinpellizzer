Opuntia microdasys subvar. albiflora

Opuntia microdasys subvar. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered prickly pear, is a cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems that are covered in spines and glochids. The plant produces showy, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit, known as a tuna, is edible and often used in traditional cuisine.

This species is drought-tolerant and thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.
