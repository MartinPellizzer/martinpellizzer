Oenothera greggii

Oenothera greggii, commonly known as the Gregg's evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening and close by morning. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.
