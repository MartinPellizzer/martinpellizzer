Oxalis corniculata var. pilosa

Oxalis corniculata var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to Asia and Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial with trifoliate leaves that have a distinctive hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, often resembling those of a true sorrel. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in gardens and wild areas.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to tolerate various soil conditions.
