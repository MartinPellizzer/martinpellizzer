Oberonia melinantha

Oberonia melinantha, commonly known as the red-veined orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves with distinctive red veins, which give it its common name. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, often with a yellowish throat. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Oberonia melinantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is a member of the Orchidaceae family.
