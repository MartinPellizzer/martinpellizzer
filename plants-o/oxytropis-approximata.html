Oxytropis approximata

Oxytropis approximata, commonly known as the desert locoweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant produces small, hard seeds that are adapted for dispersal by wind and animals. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Oxytropis approximata is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it an important component of desert ecosystems.
