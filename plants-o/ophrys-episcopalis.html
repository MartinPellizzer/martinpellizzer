Ophrys episcopalis

Ophrys episcopalis, commonly known as the Bishop's Orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its intricate, bee-mimicking flowers that play a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant's inflorescence consists of a single, showy flower with a distinctive, downward-curving lip that resembles a female bee, attracting male bees for pollination. Ophrys episcopalis thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and its role as an indicator species for specific ecological conditions.
