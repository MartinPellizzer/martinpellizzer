Oryza paraguayensis

Oryza paraguayensis, commonly known as Paraguay wild rice, is a species of grass native to South America, particularly Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, adapting to aquatic or semi-aquatic conditions. Its seeds are small and oval, with a hard outer coat, and it is sometimes cultivated for its nutritional value.

Oryza paraguayensis is of interest to botanists and agricultural researchers due to its potential for sustainable food production in wetland ecosystems.
