Orthurus kokanicus

Orthurus kokanicus, commonly known as the Kokanica orchid, is a rare flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant blossoms that typically bloom in late spring. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white and pale pink. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation programs.
