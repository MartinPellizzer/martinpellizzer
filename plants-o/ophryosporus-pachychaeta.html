Ophryosporus pachychaeta

Ophryosporus pachychaeta, commonly known as the thick-shelled tamarind, is a leguminous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, thick, and leathery pods that enclose its seeds. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in dry or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, yellow flowers.

The thick-shelled tamarind is valued for its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of tannins.
