Osyris rhamnoides

Osyris rhamnoides, commonly known as the African boxthorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible for some animals but toxic to humans.

Osyris rhamnoides is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental and defensive qualities.
