Oxalis acetosella subsp. oregana

Oxalis acetosella subsp.

oregana, commonly known as the Oregon wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers typically bloom in early spring, and the plant is often found in association with other shade-tolerant flora.

The species is characterized by its delicate appearance and ability to spread through rhizomes.
