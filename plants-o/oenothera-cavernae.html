Oenothera cavernae

Oenothera cavernae, commonly known as the cave evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and the stems are often covered in fine, silky hairs.

Oenothera cavernae is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinator populations and its ornamental appeal in xeriscaped gardens.
