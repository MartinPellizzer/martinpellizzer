Odontoglossum odoratum var. latimaculatum

Odontoglossum odoratum var. latimaculatum, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in cool, humid environments and thrives in shaded, well-drained soil. Its leaves are usually dark green and lance-shaped, while the inflorescence bears a cluster of showy, violet or purple flowers.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and unique fragrance.
