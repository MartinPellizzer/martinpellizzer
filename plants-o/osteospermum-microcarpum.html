Osteospermum microcarpum

Osteospermum microcarpum, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features bright yellow ray florets surrounding a central disc of tubular flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, and it thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

Osteospermum microcarpum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
