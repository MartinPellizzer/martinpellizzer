Ochna nitida

Ochna nitida, commonly known as the white ochna, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale green bark and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by spherical, red berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ochna nitida is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and seasonal flowering.
