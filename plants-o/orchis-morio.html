Orchis morio

Orchis morio, commonly known as the common twayblade, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in woodland and grassland habitats. The plant is characterized by its slender, leafless stems and small, greenish-brown flowers that bloom in early summer. Its roots contain a glucoside called twayblade, which has been used in traditional medicine.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in shaded environments and its role in supporting pollinators.
