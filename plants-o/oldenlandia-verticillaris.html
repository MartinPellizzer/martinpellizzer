Oldenlandia verticillaris

Oldenlandia verticillaris, commonly known as the whorled morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in whorls along the stems. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its rapid growth and invasive potential make it a notable species in ecological studies.
