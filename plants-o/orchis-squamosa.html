Orchis squamosa

Orchis squamosa, commonly known as the scaly orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces showy, violet or purple flowers that are arranged in a loose spike, with each flower having three sepals and three petals. Its leaves are lance-shaped and are often covered with a dense layer of fine hairs.

Orchis squamosa typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
