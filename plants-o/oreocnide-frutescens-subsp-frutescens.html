Oreocnide frutescens subsp. frutescens

Oreocnide frutescens subsp.

frutescens, commonly known as the hairy tallowwood, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia. It typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments.

The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are usually hairy on both surfaces. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a distinctive, fragrant odor.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.
