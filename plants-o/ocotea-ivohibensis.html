Ocotea ivohibensis

Ocotea ivohibensis, commonly known as the Madagascar laurel, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. It produces small, white flowers followed by dark blue, waxy berries that are an important food source for local wildlife.

The tree is valued for its aromatic wood and is often found in moist, shaded forest habitats.
