Ophiorrhiza repens

Ophiorrhiza repens, commonly known as the creeping sandmat, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a low-growing herb that typically thrives in sandy or coastal soils. The plant has small, opposite leaves and produces small, white or yellowish flowers. It is often found in disturbed areas and is adapted to saline environments.

Ophiorrhiza repens plays a role in stabilizing dunes and preventing soil erosion in coastal ecosystems.
