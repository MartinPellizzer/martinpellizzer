Ochrocarpos decipiens

Ochrocarpos decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive yellow orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which are often mistaken for other orchid species due to their similar appearance. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, typically arranged in a basal rosette.

The orchid thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
