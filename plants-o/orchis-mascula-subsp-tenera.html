Orchis mascula subsp. tenera

Orchis mascula subsp.

tenera, commonly known as the hairy twayblade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it has a short, underground rhizome.

The species is distinguished by its hairy stems and is often found in association with other orchid species in its native range.
