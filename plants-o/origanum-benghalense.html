Origanum benghalense

Origanum benghalense, commonly known as Indian oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, pale blue to violet flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in dry, rocky habitats. Its essential oils contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
