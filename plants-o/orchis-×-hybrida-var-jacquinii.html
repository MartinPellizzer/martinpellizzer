Orchis × hybrida var. jacquinii

Orchis × hybrida var. jacquinii, commonly known as the Jacobine orchid, is a hybrid orchid species that exhibits a mix of characteristics from its parent species.

It typically features tall, erect stems with lance-shaped leaves and produces striking, showy flowers in shades of purple and pink. The flowers are arranged in racemose inflorescences and are often adorned with intricate patterns and markings. This variety is native to parts of Europe and is often found in woodland and meadow habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
