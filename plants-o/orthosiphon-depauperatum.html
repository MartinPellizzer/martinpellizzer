Orthosiphon depauperatum

Orthosiphon depauperatum, commonly known as the poor man's holy basil, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple or white.

Orthosiphon depauperatum is often grown for its medicinal properties and ornamental value.
