Osmoxylon pseudofoliatum

Osmoxylon pseudofoliatum, commonly known as the false leaf osmoxylon, is a unique member of the Osmoxylaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flattened, leaf-like structures that are actually modified stems, a feature that aids in photosynthesis. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its botanical structure includes a central stem with lateral branches that resemble leaves, contributing to its unusual appearance.

Osmoxylon pseudofoliatum is notable for its ecological role in supporting specific insect species.
