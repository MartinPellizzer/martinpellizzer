Ocotea lanata

Ocotea lanata, commonly known as the woolly ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, white, woolly pubescence. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, and it produces dark, aromatic berries.

Ocotea lanata is often found in moist, shaded forest environments, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitats.
