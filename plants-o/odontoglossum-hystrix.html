Odontoglossum hystrix

Odontoglossum hystrix, commonly known as the hairy toothed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive hairy leaves and striking, spurred flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in cool, humid environments at high altitudes. Its roots are fibrous and often form dense colonies, contributing to its ability to thrive in rocky or mountainous terrain.

Odontoglossum hystrix is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
