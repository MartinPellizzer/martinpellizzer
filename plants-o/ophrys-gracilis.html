Ophrys gracilis

Ophrys gracilis, commonly known as the slender bee orchid, is a species of orchid native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its unique flower structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces a single spike of flowers with a distinctive, bee-like lip.

Ophrys gracilis is a rare and protected species, often found in specific microhabitats with particular soil and climate conditions.
