Oxalis stellata

Oxalis stellata, commonly known as the star oxalis, is a flowering plant belonging to the Oxalidaceae family.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its delicate, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink, white, and yellow. The plant has trifoliate leaves, each consisting of three small, heart-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Oxalis stellata is also noted for its ability to produce numerous seed pods, which can be used for propagation.
