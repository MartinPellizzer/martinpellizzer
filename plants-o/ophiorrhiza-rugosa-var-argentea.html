Ophiorrhiza rugosa var. argentea

Ophiorrhiza rugosa var. argentea, commonly known as silver-stemmed mangrove, is a variant of the mangrove species Ophiorrhiza rugosa.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray bark and slender, erect stems. This plant typically grows in brackish water environments, such as tidal mudflats and mangrove forests. Its leaves are usually opposite, elliptical, and have a smooth texture.

The plant plays a vital role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
