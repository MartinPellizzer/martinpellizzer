Olea hochstetteri

Olea hochstetteri, commonly known as the Hochstetter's olive, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family, which includes other olive species. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that ripens to a dark purple or black color.

Olea hochstetteri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils in a variety of climates.
