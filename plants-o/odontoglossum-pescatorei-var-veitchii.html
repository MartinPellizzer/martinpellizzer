Odontoglossum pescatorei var. veitchii

Odontoglossum pescatorei var. veitchii, commonly known as the Veitch's orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a sympodial orchid with a pseudobulb structure that supports its growth during periods of dormancy. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are elongated and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
