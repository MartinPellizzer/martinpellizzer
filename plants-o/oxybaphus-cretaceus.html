Oxybaphus cretaceus

Oxybaphus cretaceus, commonly known as the Cretaceous baphus, is a unique plant species characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and growth habits.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually clustered in dense inflorescences.

Oxybaphus cretaceus is of particular interest to botanists due to its evolutionary significance and ecological role in its native habitat.
