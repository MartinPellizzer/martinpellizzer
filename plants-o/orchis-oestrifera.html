Orchis oestrifera

Orchis oestrifera, commonly known as the female orchid, is a species of orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers, which are often adorned with yellow or green markings. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It has a rhizomatous root system and produces showy, spurred flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Orchis oestrifera is a perennial herb that plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
