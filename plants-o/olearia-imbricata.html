Olearia imbricata

Olearia imbricata, commonly known as the silver felt tree, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-white foliage that gives it a distinctive, almost frost-covered appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It is a shrub or small tree that thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal and forested areas.

Olearia imbricata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in horticulture for its unique texture and visual interest.
