Osbeckia muralis

Osbeckia muralis, commonly known as the wall goosefoot, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Osbeckia muralis typically thrives in rocky or disturbed soils, often found growing on canyon walls and cliffs.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
