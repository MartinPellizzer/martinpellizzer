Otolepis fruticosa

Otolepis fruticosa, commonly known as the African snowbell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and dense foliage. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the cooler months. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Otolepis fruticosa is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
