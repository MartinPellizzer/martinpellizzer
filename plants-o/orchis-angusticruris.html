Orchis angusticruris

Orchis angusticruris, commonly known as the narrow-leg orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodland habitats, where it thrives in rich, loamy soils. The plant features a basal rosette of lanceolate leaves and produces tall, slender flowering stems topped with a single, showy purple or violet flower. Its root system consists of a tuberous structure that helps it survive through winter dormancy.

The species is notable for its specialized pollination mechanism, which involves specific insect pollinators.
