Ophrys × tisserandii

Ophrys × tisserandii, commonly known as the Tisserand's bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive flower structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brownish in color, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Ophrys × tisserandii is a rare and specialized species, often found in specific microhabitats within its native range.
