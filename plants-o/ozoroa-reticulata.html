Ozoroa reticulata

Ozoroa reticulata, commonly known as the African star-of-Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that grows up to 1 meter tall, featuring slender, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. Ozoroa reticulata is often found in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
