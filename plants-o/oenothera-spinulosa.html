Oenothera spinulosa

Oenothera spinulosa, commonly known as prickly evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, spiny stems and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, elliptical seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Oenothera spinulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
