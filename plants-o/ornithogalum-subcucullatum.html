Ornithogalum subcucullatum

Ornithogalum subcucullatum, commonly known as the bell-shaped star of Bethlehem, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features striking, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a cluster of small, white flowers on a tall stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its unique appearance and seasonal blooming make it a notable addition to botanical collections.
