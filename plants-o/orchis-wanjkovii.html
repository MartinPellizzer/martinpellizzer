Orchis wanjkovii

Orchis wanjkovii, commonly known as the Wanjkovii orchid, is a rare and endangered species native to specific regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring. The plant has a terrestrial habit, growing from a short rhizome and producing a single, erect stem. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, while the flowers are usually violet or purple in color.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
