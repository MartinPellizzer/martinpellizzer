Ophrys × deleoi

Ophrys × deleoi, commonly known as the bee orchid hybrid, is a fascinating plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Ophrys genus, which is known for its mimicry of female insects to attract male pollinators. The plant produces striking flowers that resemble female bees, a form of sexual deception used to ensure pollination. Ophrys × deleoi is a natural hybrid between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera, resulting in a unique combination of characteristics from both parents.

Its botanical features include a dense, hairy stem and specialized floral structures that enhance its pollination strategy.
