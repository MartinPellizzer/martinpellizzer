Oldenlandia xestosperma

Oldenlandia xestosperma, commonly known as the stellate false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, spiky fruits called cypsela. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered invasive in certain areas.
