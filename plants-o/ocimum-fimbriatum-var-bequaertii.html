Ocimum fimbriatum var. bequaertii

Ocimum fimbriatum var. bequaertii, commonly known as the Sierra Madre basil, is a variant of the holy basil species native to Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are used in traditional Mexican cuisine and herbal medicine for their fragrance and potential health benefits.

The variety is distinguished by its unique foliage and adaptability to specific ecological conditions.
