Ocotea congestifolia

Ocotea congestifolia, commonly known as the clustered ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests. Its leaves are typically dark green and glossy, and it produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into berries.

Ocotea congestifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
