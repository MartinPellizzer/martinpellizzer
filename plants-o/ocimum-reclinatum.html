Ocimum reclinatum

Ocimum reclinatum, commonly known as wild basil, is an aromatic herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and fragrant leaves. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, tubular flowers are usually white or pale blue, arranged in whorled clusters.

Ocimum reclinatum is valued for its essential oils, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
