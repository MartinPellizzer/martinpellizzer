Oxalis chacoensis

Oxalis chacoensis, commonly known as the Chaco wood sorrel, is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Paraguay.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. It produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Oxalis chacoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and floral display.
