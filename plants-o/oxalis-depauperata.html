Oxalis depauperata

Oxalis depauperata, commonly known as the poor man's oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental foliage and blooms.

Oxalis depauperata is also known for its ability to reseed itself, making it a resilient addition to gardens.
