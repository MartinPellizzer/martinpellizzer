Ostodes collina

Ostodes collina, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered in fine hairs.

Ostodes collina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
