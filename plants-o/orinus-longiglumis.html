Orinus longiglumis

Orinus longiglumis, commonly known as the long-bearded orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped, while its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
