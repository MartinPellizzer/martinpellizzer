Orchis halleri

Orchis halleri, commonly known as the Haller's orchid, is a terrestrial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils, while its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette.

Orchis halleri is known for its long blooming period, often from late spring to early summer.
