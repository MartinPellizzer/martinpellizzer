Oncidium saltator

Oncidium saltator, commonly known as the dancing orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, showy inflorescences that feature a variety of colorful flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant has long, slender stems that can grow up to several feet in length, with leaves that are narrow and strap-like. Oncidium saltator thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique appearance and ease of care make it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
