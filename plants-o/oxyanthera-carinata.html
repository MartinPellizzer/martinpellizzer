Oxyanthera carinata

Oxyanthera carinata, commonly known as the ridge-veined grass, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant features narrow, linear leaves with a prominent midrib, giving it its common name. It produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the grass family.

Oxyanthera carinata is often found in moist, disturbed soils and is adapted to a variety of environmental conditions.
