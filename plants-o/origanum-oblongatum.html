Origanum oblongatum

Origanum oblongatum, commonly known as narrowleaf marjoram, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, pale yellow flowers. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, essential oil-rich foliage, which is often used in cooking and traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
