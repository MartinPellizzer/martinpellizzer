Omphalodes wittmanniana

Omphalodes wittmanniana, commonly known as the Wittmann's omphalodes, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or dry soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

Omphalodes wittmanniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
