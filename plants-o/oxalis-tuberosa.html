Oxalis tuberosa

Oxalis tuberosa, commonly known as the false oxalis or wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces edible tubers that are sometimes used in traditional cuisine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Oxalis tuberosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
