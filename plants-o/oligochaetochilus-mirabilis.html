Oligochaetochilus mirabilis

Oligochaetochilus mirabilis, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers and hairy, strap-like leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its flowers bloom during the rainy season, attracting pollinators like bees and wasps.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
