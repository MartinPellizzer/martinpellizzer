Ophrys scolopax f. pallescens

Ophrys scolopax f. pallescens, commonly known as the pale owl orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to specific regions of Europe.

It belongs to the genus Ophrys, which is known for its mimicry of female insects to attract male pollinators. The pale owl orchid features a distinctive, owl-like flower with intricate patterns and a pale yellowish hue. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland or heathland habitats.

Due to its rarity and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a protected species in many areas.
