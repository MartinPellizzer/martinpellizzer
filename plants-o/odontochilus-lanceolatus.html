Odontochilus lanceolatus

Odontochilus lanceolatus, commonly known as the lance-shaped toothed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lanceolate leaves and distinctive toothed margins. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically arranged in a spike. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
