Oligosporus dimoanus

Oligosporus dimoanus, commonly known as the African star grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted, grass-like foliage and produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in dense clusters. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats. It has a fibrous root system and is adapted to seasonal rainfall patterns.

Oligosporus dimoanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
