Orchis italica lusus maculata

Orchis italica lusus maculata, commonly known as the spotted Italian orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of southern Europe.

This perennial herbaceous plant features striking, showy flowers with a distinctive purple and white coloration, often with a spotted pattern on the petals. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or meadow habitats. The plant has a complex root system and produces a single spike of flowers that bloom in late spring.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
