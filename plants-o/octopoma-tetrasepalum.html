Octopoma tetrasepalum

Octopoma tetrasepalum, commonly known as the four-lobed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, white or pale blue flowers. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some botanical gardens and private collections.
