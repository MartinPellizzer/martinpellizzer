Oxalis magnifica

Oxalis magnifica, commonly known as the giant wood sorrel, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 12 inches in diameter, often with a velvety texture and a deep green color. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with five petals, resembling those of a dandelion. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, Oxalis magnifica is widely cultivated in horticulture.
