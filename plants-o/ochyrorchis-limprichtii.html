Ochyrorchis limprichtii

Ochyrorchis limprichtii, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the high altitudes of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often showy floral structure. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas, thriving in cool and humid conditions. Its flowers are usually small to medium in size, with intricate patterns and colors that range from white to pale yellow.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its range.
