Ophrys aranifera subsp. moesziana

Ophrys aranifera subsp.

moesziana, commonly known as the Moeszian Bee Orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans. It is characterized by its unique floral morphology, featuring a pouch-like structure that mimics the appearance of a female bee, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy.

The plant produces showy, bee-shaped flowers with a dark brown to black coloration, often adorned with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry. It thrives in calcareous soils and requires specific microhabitats, making it highly sensitive to environmental changes.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
