Olearia multibracteolata

Olearia multibracteolata, commonly known as the silver tree, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which results from dense, overlapping scales on the leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, providing a distinctive appearance.

Olearia multibracteolata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal environments.
