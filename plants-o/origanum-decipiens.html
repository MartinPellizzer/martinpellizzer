Origanum decipiens

Origanum decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It is often used in culinary applications, though its strong scent can be overwhelming.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, sharing traits with other members like thyme and marjoram.
