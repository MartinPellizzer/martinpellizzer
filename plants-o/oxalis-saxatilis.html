Oxalis saxatilis

Oxalis saxatilis, commonly known as the rock oxalis, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically heart-shaped and have a bright green color. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers with five petals, often appearing in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Oxalis saxatilis is also known for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
