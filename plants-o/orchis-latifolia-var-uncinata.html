Orchis latifolia var. uncinata

Orchis latifolia var. uncinata, commonly known as the hooked broad-leaved orchid, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and striking, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It has a short rhizome and produces a single spike of flowers with showy, spurred petals.

This variety is distinguished by its hooked stigma, which is a unique botanical feature.
