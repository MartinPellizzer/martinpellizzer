Ophiopogon alatus

Ophiopogon alatus, commonly known as the sword leaf yam, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often curled or twisted, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by dark purple berries. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit.
