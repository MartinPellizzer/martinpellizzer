Origanum vulgare f. chlorescens

Origanum vulgare f. chlorescens, commonly known as the green chamoecum oregano, is a botanical variant of the common oregano.

It is characterized by its lighter green foliage compared to the typical gray-green leaves of the species. This form is often found in the Mediterranean region, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers, typical of the Lamiaceae family.

It is valued for its aromatic leaves used in culinary and medicinal applications.
