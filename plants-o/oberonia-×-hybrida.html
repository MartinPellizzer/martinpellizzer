Oberonia × hybrida

Oberonia × hybrida, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Oberonia pumila and Oberonia maculata.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia. The plant typically features dark green, leathery leaves and produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
