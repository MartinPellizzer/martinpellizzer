Oligochaetochilus williamsonii

Oligochaetochilus williamsonii, commonly known as the Williamson's toothed fern, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring a short rhizome and a rosette of lanceolate, toothed leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically pollinated by specific insect species. It thrives in shaded, humid environments, often found in forest understories where it relies on mycorrhizal associations for nutrient uptake.
