Oxalis san-miguelii subsp. urubambensis

Oxalis san-miguelii subsp.

urubambensis, commonly known as the Urubamba wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to high-altitude regions of South America. It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped lobes and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of yellow or orange.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests or rocky slopes. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a short, stout stem.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in shady garden settings.
