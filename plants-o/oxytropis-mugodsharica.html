Oxytropis mugodsharica

Oxytropis mugodsharica, commonly known as the Karakum oxytropis, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia, particularly found in the arid regions of Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, woody pods that contain several seeds.
