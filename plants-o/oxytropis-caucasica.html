Oxytropis caucasica

Oxytropis caucasica, commonly known as Caucasian locoweed, is a perennial legume native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves, which are typically hairy and have a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to poor soil conditions.

Oxytropis caucasica is also known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock.
