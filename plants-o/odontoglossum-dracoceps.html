Odontoglossum dracoceps

Odontoglossum dracoceps, commonly known as the Dragon's Head Orchid, is a species native to the high-altitude cloud forests of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its striking, elongated pseudobulbs and showy, violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and requires well-drained soil to thrive. Its flowers are often fragrant and bloom during the cooler months, making it a sought-after specimen among orchid enthusiasts.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its unique morphology and ornamental value.
