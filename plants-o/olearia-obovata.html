Olearia obovata

Olearia obovata, commonly known as the silver olearia, is a species of flowering plant in the daisy family, Asteraceae.

It is native to New Zealand and is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and showy, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, and prefers well-drained, sunny habitats. Its leaves are broadly ovate and covered in fine, white hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Olearia obovata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
