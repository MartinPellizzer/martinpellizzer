Oxytropis montana proles occidentalis

Oxytropis montana proles occidentalis, commonly known as the western milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, yellowish to greenish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.
