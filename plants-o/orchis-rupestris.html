Orchis rupestris

Orchis rupestris, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows on rocky or limestone soils, thriving in well-drained, alkaline environments. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, erect flowering stems with showy, violet-blue flowers. Its flowers are characterized by a distinctive lip that is often adorned with intricate patterns.

Orchis rupestris is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
