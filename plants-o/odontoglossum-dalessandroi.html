Odontoglossum dalessandroi

Odontoglossum dalessandroi, commonly known as the Ecuadorian orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with a short rhizome and a rosette of lanceolate, dark green leaves. Its flowers are showy, with a distinctive violet-blue coloration and a spurred labellum.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a container plant.
