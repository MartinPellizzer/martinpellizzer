Oenothera versicolor

Oenothera versicolor, commonly known as the showy evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and sandy soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Oenothera versicolor is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its role in supporting nocturnal pollinators.
