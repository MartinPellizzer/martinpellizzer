Odontoglossum schlieperianum var. flavidum

Odontoglossum schlieperianum var. flavidum, commonly known as the golden-veined orchid, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers with yellow petals and a distinctive golden lip. It prefers cool, humid environments and is typically found in cloud forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
