Ocotea paranaensis

Ocotea paranaensis, commonly known as Brazilian camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark-colored wood and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by spherical, dark blue berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests.

The tree is valued for its durable timber and has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of essential oils.
