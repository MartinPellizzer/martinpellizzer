Osmia integrifolia

Osmia integrifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leaved mason bee, is a species of solitary bee native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender body and dark, metallic blue coloration, with smooth, non-hairy leaves on its stems. The plant is a flowering plant in the family Lamiaceae, known for its fragrant, tubular flowers that attract pollinators. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Osmia integrifolia plays an important role in supporting local pollinator populations and maintaining biodiversity in its ecosystem.
