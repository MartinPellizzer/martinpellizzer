Oryza sativa var. sitapurica

Oryza sativa var. sitapurica, commonly known as the Sitapur rice, is a wild rice species native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance and small, rice-like grains. This variety is typically found in wetland habitats and is adapted to fluctuating water levels. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and elongated, narrow leaves.

It is of interest to researchers due to its potential for genetic diversity and resilience to environmental stress.
