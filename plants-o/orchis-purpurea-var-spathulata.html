Orchis purpurea var. spathulata

Orchis purpurea var. spathulata, commonly known as the purple orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear showy, violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodlands. Its flowers are spurred at the base, a distinctive feature of the orchid family, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
