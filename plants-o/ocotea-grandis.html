Ocotea grandis

Ocotea grandis, commonly known as the big-laurel or big-laurel tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and avocado. The plant is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and can grow up to 30 meters tall. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils, which contribute to its aromatic properties.

Ocotea grandis is often found in humid, lowland forests and plays a role in local ecosystems.
