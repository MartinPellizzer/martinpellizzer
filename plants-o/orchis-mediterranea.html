Orchis mediterranea

Orchis mediterranea, commonly known as the Mediterranean orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands, woodlands, and disturbed soils, preferring well-drained conditions. Its flowers are often arranged in a spike and are pollinated by insects such as bees and flies.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
