Oxalis reclinata var. reclinata

Oxalis reclinata var. reclinata, commonly known as the weeping wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features delicate, trifoliate leaves that hang in a weeping habit, giving it a unique and ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its foliage and flowers make it a popular choice for shaded garden beds and woodland landscapes.
