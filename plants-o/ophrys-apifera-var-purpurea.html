Ophrys apifera var. purpurea

Ophrys apifera var. purpurea, commonly known as the Purple Bee Orchid, is a striking species of orchid native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its intricate, bee-like flower structure, which features a dark purple lip mimicking the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils, often in coastal areas. Its flowers bloom in spring, producing a dense cluster of showy, fragrant blooms.

This orchid is notable for its specialized pollination mechanism and is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its native range.
