Oberonia exaltata

Oberonia exaltata, commonly known as the tall orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in forest understories, where it relies on specific symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Oberonia exaltata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
