Oxalis triangularis f. glabrifolia

Oxalis triangularis f. glabrifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf wood sorrel, is a hybrid form of the species Oxalis triangularis.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often a vibrant green with a slight reddish tinge. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow or orange, typically blooming in spring. It prefers partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental foliage and is relatively low maintenance once established.
