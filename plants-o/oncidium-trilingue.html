Oncidium trilingue

Oncidium trilingue, commonly known as the Three-language orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It features a sympodial growth habit with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, often arranged in a raceme. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it thrives in bright, indirect light with high humidity.

Oncidium trilingue is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its vibrant blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
