Orchis platanthera

Orchis platanthera, commonly known as the early purple orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a reddish or purple tinge along the edges.

Orchis platanthera is also notable for its symbiotic relationship with specific fungi, which aid in nutrient absorption.
