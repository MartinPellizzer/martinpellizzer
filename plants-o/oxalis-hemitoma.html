Oxalis hemitoma

Oxalis hemitoma, commonly known as the narrowleaf wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves, each leaflet being ovate to lanceolate in shape, with a distinctive yellow flower that has five petals. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the Oxalidaceae family, characterized by its calcium oxalate crystals and heart-shaped leaves.
