Oenothera hookeri var. montereyensis

Oenothera hookeri var. montereyensis, commonly known as the Monterey evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to California.

It is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that open in the evening and close by morning. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ecological role and is often found in coastal and inland habitats.
