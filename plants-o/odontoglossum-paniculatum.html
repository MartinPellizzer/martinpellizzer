Odontoglossum paniculatum

Odontoglossum paniculatum, commonly known as the panicle orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as cloud forests. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are arranged in a dense, panicle-like inflorescence, often displaying a range of colors including white, pink, and purple.

The orchid's botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and its role in the biodiversity of its native habitats.
