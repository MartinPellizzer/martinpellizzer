Ossaea barahonensis

Ossaea barahonensis, commonly known as the Barahonensis grass, is a perennial grass species native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along streambanks. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet cluster, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Ossaea barahonensis is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
