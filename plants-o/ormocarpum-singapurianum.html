Ormocarpum singapurianum

Ormocarpum singapurianum, commonly known as Singapore dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly Singapore.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble dandelions. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as lawns and disturbed soils. Its flowers are yellow and produce small, wind-dispersed seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
