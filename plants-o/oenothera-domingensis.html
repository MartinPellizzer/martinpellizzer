Oenothera domingensis

Oenothera domingensis, commonly known as the Mexican evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are linear and gray-green, often covered in fine hairs.

Oenothera domingensis is also known for its medicinal properties, with its seeds and oils used in traditional and modern herbal remedies.
