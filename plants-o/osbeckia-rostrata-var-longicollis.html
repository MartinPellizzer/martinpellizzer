Osbeckia rostrata var. longicollis

Osbeckia rostrata var. longicollis, commonly known as the long-necked osbeckia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Osbeckia rostrata var. longicollis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
