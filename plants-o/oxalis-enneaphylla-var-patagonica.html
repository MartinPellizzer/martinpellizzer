Oxalis enneaphylla var. patagonica

Oxalis enneaphylla var. patagonica, commonly known as the Patagonian wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental value.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in well-drained soil and full sun exposure.
