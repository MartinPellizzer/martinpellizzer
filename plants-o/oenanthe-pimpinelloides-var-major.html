Oenanthe pimpinelloides var. major

Oenanthe pimpinelloides var. major, commonly known as the greater water dropwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, such as along streams, ditches, and flooded meadows. The plant has slender, erect stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense, umbel-like clusters. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and have a distinctive waxy coating.

The species is characterized by its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils and its historical use in traditional medicine.
