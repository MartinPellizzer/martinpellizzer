Orthosiphon rogersii

Orthosiphon rogersii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved plectranthus, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive aromatic scent. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Orthosiphon rogersii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
