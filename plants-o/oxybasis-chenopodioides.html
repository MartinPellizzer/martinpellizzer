Oxybasis chenopodioides

Oxybasis chenopodioides, commonly known as sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that help reduce water loss in dry environments. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Oxybasis chenopodioides is adapted to thrive in sandy soils and is often found in desert or coastal dunes.
