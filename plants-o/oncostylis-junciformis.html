Oncostylis junciformis

Oncostylis junciformis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often forming dense stands in swampy areas. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves that are usually narrow and slightly pointed at the tips. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, which are characteristic of the Cyperaceae family.

Oncostylis junciformis plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and provides habitat for various invertebrates.
