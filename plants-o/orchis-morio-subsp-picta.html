Orchis morio subsp. picta

Orchis morio subsp.

picta, commonly known as the painted orchid, is a species of orchid native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its striking, brightly colored flowers that often feature a combination of purple, yellow, and white hues.

The plant typically grows in woodland habitats, preferring moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption.

The orchid is known for its subtle fragrance and is a popular subject in botanical studies due to its unique floral structure and ecological significance.
