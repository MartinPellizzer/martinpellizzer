Osbeckia rubicunda var. hakgalana

Osbeckia rubicunda var. hakgalana, commonly known as the Korean red willow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Korea.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and rocky slopes. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered with fine, silky hairs.

Osbeckia rubicunda var. hakgalana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
