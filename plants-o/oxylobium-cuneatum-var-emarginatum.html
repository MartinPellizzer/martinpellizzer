Oxylobium cuneatum var. emarginatum

Oxylobium cuneatum var. emarginatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf oxylobium, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive notched base, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
