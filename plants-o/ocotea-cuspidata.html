Ocotea cuspidata

Ocotea cuspidata, commonly known as the Brazilian cinnamon tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and fragrant, reddish-brown bark. It produces small, white flowers followed by dark, berry-like fruits.

Ocotea cuspidata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates.
