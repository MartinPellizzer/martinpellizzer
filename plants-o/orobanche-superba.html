Orobanche superba

Orobanche superba, commonly known as the crown-vetch broomrape, is a parasitic plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on its host for nutrients.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is found in various regions of Europe and Asia. The plant produces small, often yellow or greenish flowers and has a taproot system that attaches to the roots of its host. It is considered an invasive species in some areas due to its ability to parasitize a wide range of host plants.

Its unique life cycle and parasitic nature make it an interesting subject in botanical studies.
