Ochna itineraria

Ochna itineraria, commonly known as the mountain ochna, is a shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange or red berries containing one or two seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
