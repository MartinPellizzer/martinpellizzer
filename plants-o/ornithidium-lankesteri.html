Ornithidium lankesteri

Ornithidium lankesteri, commonly known as the Lankester's orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and green. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil to flourish. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered in its natural range.
