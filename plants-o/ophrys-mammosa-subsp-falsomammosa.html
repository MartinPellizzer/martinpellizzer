Ophrys mammosa subsp. falsomammosa

Ophrys mammosa subsp.

falsomammosa, commonly known as the false bee orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure. The plant produces flowers that resemble female bees, a feature that plays a key role in its pollination strategy through mimicry.

It typically grows in calcareous grasslands and open woodlands, preferring sunny to partially shaded conditions. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, while the roots are fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

This subspecies is often found in southern Europe, particularly in countries like Spain and Portugal.
