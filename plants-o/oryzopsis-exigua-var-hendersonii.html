Oryzopsis exigua var. hendersonii

Oryzopsis exigua var. hendersonii, commonly known as Henderson's slender grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, spikelet-based flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native grassland ecosystems.
