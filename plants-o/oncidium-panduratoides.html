Oncidium panduratoides

Oncidium panduratoides, commonly known as the panduratoides orchid, is a tropical orchid species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant has pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, supporting its growth in diverse environmental conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a fan-like pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Oncidium panduratoides is often cultivated for its beauty and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
