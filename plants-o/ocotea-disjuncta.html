Ocotea disjuncta

Ocotea disjuncta, commonly known as the Guianese ocotea, is a member of the Lauraceae family and is native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can reach heights of up to 20 meters. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, and it produces dark-colored, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife.

The tree is also valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional crafts and as a source of essential oils.
