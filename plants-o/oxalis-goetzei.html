Oxalis goetzei

Oxalis goetzei, commonly known as the velvetleaf wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and small, delicate flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Oxalis goetzei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
