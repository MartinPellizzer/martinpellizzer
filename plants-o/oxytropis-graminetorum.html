Oxytropis graminetorum

Oxytropis graminetorum, commonly known as the meadow locoweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Oxytropis graminetorum is often found in grasslands, open woodlands, and along roadsides, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

It is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitats.
