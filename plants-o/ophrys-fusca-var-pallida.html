Ophrys fusca var. pallida

Ophrys fusca var. pallida, commonly known as the pale bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species found primarily in southern Europe.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brown, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Due to habitat loss and fragmentation, this orchid is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
