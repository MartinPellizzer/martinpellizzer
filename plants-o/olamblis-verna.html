Olamblis verna

Olamblis verna, commonly known as the spring bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow environments. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, often appearing in clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem.

Olamblis verna blooms in early spring, making it an important early nectar source for pollinators.
