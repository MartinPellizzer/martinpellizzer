Oryza punctata

Oryza punctata, commonly known as the punctate ricegrass, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist soil conditions. Its small, spikelet-based flowers are usually green or pale blue, and it produces small, rice-like grains.

Oryza punctata is often used in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in wetland ecosystems.
