Ocimum vaalae

Ocimum vaalae, commonly known as the Vaal's basil, is a species of flowering plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, pale blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright herb with opposite, ovate leaves that have a strong, spicy scent. Ocimum vaalae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to both gardens and herbal collections.
