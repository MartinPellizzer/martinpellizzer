Ophrys × pavonei

Ophrys × pavonei, commonly known as the Pavone's bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which closely resembles a female bee, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant produces showy, bee-like flowers with intricate patterns and colors that mimic the appearance of its pollinators. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments.

Ophrys × pavonei is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological significance in supporting pollinator populations.
