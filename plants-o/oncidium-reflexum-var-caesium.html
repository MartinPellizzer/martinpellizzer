Oncidium reflexum var. caesium

Oncidium reflexum var. caesium, commonly known as the Caesar's orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to South America.

It is characterized by its long, drooping inflorescences that bear clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green to orange flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and the plant prefers bright, indirect light with high humidity.

Oncidium reflexum var. caesium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
