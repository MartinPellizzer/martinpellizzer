Opithandra primuloides

Opithandra primuloides, commonly known as the silver dollar plant, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has lance-shaped, glossy leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Opithandra primuloides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native pollinators.
