Ophiopogon intermedius var. gracilipes

Ophiopogon intermedius var. gracilipes, commonly known as the slender leaved spathiphyllum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features upright, grass-like foliage with narrow, dark green leaves that are often tinged with purple at the base. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers clustered in spikes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

This variety is valued for its compact growth habit and adaptability to various soil types.
