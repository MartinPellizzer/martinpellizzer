Ocotea proboscidea

Ocotea proboscidea, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are drupes, typically containing a single seed, and the tree can grow up to 20 meters tall.

Ocotea proboscidea is valued for its aromatic wood and is often found in humid, lowland forests.
