Ophthalmophyllum maughanii

Ophthalmophyllum maughanii, commonly known as the Maughan's eyeplant, is a rare and striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that resemble eyes, with a unique coloration pattern of red, yellow, and orange. Native to the tropical regions of Africa, particularly Tanzania, it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of tubular flowers that attract various pollinators.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
