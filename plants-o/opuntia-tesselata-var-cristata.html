Opuntia tesselata var. cristata

Opuntia tesselata var. cristata, commonly known as the cristate prickly pear, is a unique variant of the prickly pear cactus characterized by its flattened, fan-like growth form.

It originates from the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features thick, ribbed stems covered in clusters of spines and small, fleshy pads called areoles. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing in late spring to early summer.

The plant is adapted to withstand extreme drought conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
