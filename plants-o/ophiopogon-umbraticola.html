Ophiopogon umbraticola

Ophiopogon umbraticola, commonly known as the shade ivy or forest ivy, is a perennial evergreen herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, pale green or yellowish flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by small, black berries. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in woodlands and shaded garden beds.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in various horticultural settings.
