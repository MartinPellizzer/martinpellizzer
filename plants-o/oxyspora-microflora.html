Oxyspora microflora

Oxyspora microflora, commonly known as the microscopic soil fungus, is a filamentous fungus that thrives in soil environments.

It plays a significant role in nutrient cycling by decomposing organic matter and releasing nutrients back into the soil. This organism is characterized by its small, branching hyphae and the production of spores that aid in its dispersal. It is often found in association with plant roots, contributing to soil health and plant growth.

Due to its microscopic size, it is typically studied using specialized microscopy techniques.
