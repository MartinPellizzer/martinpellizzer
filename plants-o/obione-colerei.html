Obione colerei

Obione colerei, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy inflorescences. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. Obione colerei is often found in moist, woodland areas and thrives in partial to full shade.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a valuable nectar source for pollinators.
