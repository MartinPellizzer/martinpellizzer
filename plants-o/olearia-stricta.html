Olearia stricta

Olearia stricta, commonly known as the narrowleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in coastal and lowland areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture, and it produces small yellow flowers that develop into fluffy white seed heads.

Olearia stricta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
