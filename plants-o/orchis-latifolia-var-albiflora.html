Orchis latifolia var. albiflora

Orchis latifolia var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered broad-leaved orchid, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and showy white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or grassland habitats. It produces a single spike of flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
