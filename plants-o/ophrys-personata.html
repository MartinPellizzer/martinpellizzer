Ophrys personata

Ophrys personata, commonly known as the bee orchid, is a species of orchid native to Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its unique flower structure, which resembles a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in grasslands, heathlands, and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brownish in color, with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of insects.

Ophrys personata is a perennial herb that produces a single spike of flowers, often blooming in late spring to early summer.
