Orchis aristata var. albiflora

Orchis aristata var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered orchis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in open, rocky habitats at high elevations, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lanceolate and arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

This species is noted for its ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators in its native environment.
