Oncidium bernoullianum

Oncidium bernoullianum, commonly known as the Bernoullian orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that often display a vibrant mix of colors. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in varying environmental conditions.

Oncidium bernoullianum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
