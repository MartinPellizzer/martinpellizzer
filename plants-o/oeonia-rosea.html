Oeonia rosea

Oeonia rosea, commonly known as the pink sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, pink to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to thrive in arid environments.

Oeonia rosea is often found in sandy or gravelly soils along coastal dunes and is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
