Ocotea quadriporata

Ocotea quadriporata, commonly known as the four-pored ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree belonging to the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America, where it thrives in humid, warm climates. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Ocotea quadriporata is valued for its essential oils and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
