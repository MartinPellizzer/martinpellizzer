Oreodaphne floribunda

Oreodaphne floribunda, commonly known as the fragrant evergreen tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, spherical berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
