Orthosiphon glabrescens

Orthosiphon glabrescens, commonly known as Java tea or *Plectranthus glabrescens*, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are usually lance-shaped. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Orthosiphon glabrescens is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine for its diuretic and anti-inflammatory effects.
