Oldenlandia cinereoviridis

Oldenlandia cinereoviridis, commonly known as the silver-green mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in loose, raceme-like inflorescences. Its leaves are typically narrow, lance-shaped, and have a slightly waxy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.
