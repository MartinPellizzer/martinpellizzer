Oxytropis nuriae

Oxytropis nuriae, commonly known as the Nurian locoweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow to reddish-purple pea-shaped flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and high-altitude grasslands.

Oxytropis nuriae is known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock.
