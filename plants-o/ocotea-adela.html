Ocotea adela

Ocotea adela, commonly known as the Adela's ocotea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are typically glossy and elliptical in shape. It produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by dark purple berries.

Ocotea adela is often found in humid, shaded environments within the rainforest understory.
