Oetosis elongata

Oetosis elongata, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender stems and delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its roots are adapted for absorbing nutrients from the soil, and it produces specialized structures called pseudobulbs to store water and nutrients.

Oetosis elongata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with appropriate humidity and light conditions.
