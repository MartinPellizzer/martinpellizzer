Orchis papilionacea var. rubra

Orchis papilionacea var. rubra, commonly known as the red helleborine, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem that bears lance-shaped leaves and clusters of showy, reddish-brown to purple flowers. The flowers have a distinctive hooded lip and are typically arranged in a raceme, with a sweet, vanilla-like scent. This species is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist woodlands and meadows.

It thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.
