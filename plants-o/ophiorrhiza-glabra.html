Ophiorrhiza glabra

Ophiorrhiza glabra, commonly known as the sandalwood tree, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release tiny seeds when mature.

Ophiorrhiza glabra is often found in coastal areas and sandy soils, making it an important component of tropical ecosystems.
