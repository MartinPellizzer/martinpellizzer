Onobrychis lineata

Onobrychis lineata, commonly known as narrowleaf burclover, is a perennial legume native to the United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Onobrychis lineata is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
