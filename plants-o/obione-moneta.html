Obione moneta

Obione moneta, commonly known as the silver leaf plant, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, reflective hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in loose inflorescences. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Obione moneta thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
