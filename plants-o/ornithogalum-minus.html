Ornithogalum minus

Ornithogalum minus, commonly known as the lesser cowslip, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a short, rhizomatous stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers with six petals, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. Its flowers are often fragrant and attract various pollinators such as bees and moths.

The plant is adapted to grow in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats.
