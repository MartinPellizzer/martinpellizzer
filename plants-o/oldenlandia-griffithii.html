Oldenlandia griffithii

Oldenlandia griffithii, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue-violet flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture.

Oldenlandia griffithii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
