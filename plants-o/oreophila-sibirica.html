Oreophila sibirica

Oreophila sibirica, commonly known as the Siberian mountain bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Siberia and Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant features showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Oreophila sibirica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in alpine gardens.
