Olearia teretifolia

Olearia teretifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf daisy bush, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It features silver-gray, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flower heads arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in spring. It is well adapted to coastal and dry environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Olearia teretifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native insect populations.
