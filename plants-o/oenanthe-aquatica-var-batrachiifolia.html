Oenanthe aquatica var. batrachiifolia

Oenanthe aquatica var. batrachiifolia, commonly known as the water dropwort, is a variant of the water dropwort species native to wetland habitats.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has finely divided, arrow-shaped leaves that are adapted for aquatic environments. It thrives in shallow water or damp soil, often found near streams and ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
