Ochrosia kilaueaensis

Ochrosia kilaueaensis, commonly known as Kilauea's candleplant, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian island of Kilauea.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, candle-like inflorescence composed of numerous small white flowers. The plant thrives in the moist, shaded environments of coastal forests and is adapted to the tropical climate of Hawaii. Ochrosia kilaueaensis is notable for its ornamental value and its role in the local ecosystem.

However, it is considered endangered due to habitat loss and invasive species.
