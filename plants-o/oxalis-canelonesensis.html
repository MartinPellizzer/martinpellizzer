Oxalis canelonesensis

Oxalis canelonesensis, commonly known as the Canelones oxalis, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its foliage is often adorned with distinctive patterns and has a peppery taste when crushed.

Oxalis canelonesensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
