Orchis latifolia var. labrovaria

Orchis latifolia var. labrovaria, commonly known as the broad-leaved orchid variant, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, spurred flowers that typically bloom in late spring. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces a single, elongated floral spike with numerous flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
