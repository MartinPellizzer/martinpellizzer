Ophrys × biancoae

Ophrys × biancoae, commonly known as the white bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys lutea.

It is characterized by its unique floral morphology, featuring a specialized lip that mimics the appearance of a female bee, which plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is found in various parts of Europe, including Italy and France. Its flowers are predominantly white or pale yellow, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry of insect forms.

Ophrys × biancoae is a rare and protected species, often requiring specific environmental conditions to thrive.
