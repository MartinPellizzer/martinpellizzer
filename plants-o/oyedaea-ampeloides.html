Oyedaea ampeloides

Oyedaea ampeloides, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray leaves and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces berries that are typically yellow or red when mature, containing several seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

Oyedaea ampeloides is part of the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes.
