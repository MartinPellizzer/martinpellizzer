Oberonia caudata

Oberonia caudata, commonly known as the long-tailed orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, pendulous flowers with a distinctive yellow and brown coloration. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, organic soil.

Oberonia caudata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
