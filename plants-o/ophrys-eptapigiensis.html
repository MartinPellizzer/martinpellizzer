Ophrys eptapigiensis

Ophrys eptapigiensis, commonly known as the Greek bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Greece.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which closely resembles a female bee, a mimicry adaptation that aids in pollination by male bees. The plant produces a single, showy flower with a dark, bee-like appearance, often with intricate patterns that enhance its deceptive allure. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is typically found in high-altitude habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species.
