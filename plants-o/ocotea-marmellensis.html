Ocotea marmellensis

Ocotea marmellensis, commonly known as the yellow ocotea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by spherical, yellowish fruits that are edible.

Ocotea marmellensis is often found in humid lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.
