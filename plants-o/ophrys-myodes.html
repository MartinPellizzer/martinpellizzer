Ophrys myodes

Ophrys myodes, commonly known as the bee orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive flower that mimics the appearance of a female bee, a feature that plays a key role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a dark brown or black lip that resembles a bee's abdomen.

Ophrys myodes produces small, elliptical seeds that are dispersed by wind, allowing the plant to colonize new areas.
