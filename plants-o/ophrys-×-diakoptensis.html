Ophrys × diakoptensis

Ophrys × diakoptensis, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant that belongs to the Orchidaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Ophrys apifera, the bee orchid, and Ophrys insectifera, the wasp orchid. This plant is notable for its unique floral morphology, which mimics the appearance of female wasps to attract male wasps for pollination. The flowers exhibit a complex structure with a labellum that resembles a wasp's body, complete with intricate color patterns and markings.

Ophrys × diakoptensis is typically found in Mediterranean regions, growing in open, sunny habitats with well-drained soils.
