Ophidion doucetteanum

Ophidion doucetteanum, commonly known as the dwarf water lily, is a species of aquatic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, floating leaves that are typically less than 5 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces white, fragrant flowers that bloom during the day and close at night. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes.

Ophidion doucetteanum plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various microorganisms and small aquatic animals.
