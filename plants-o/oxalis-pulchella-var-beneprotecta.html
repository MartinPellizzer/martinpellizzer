Oxalis pulchella var. beneprotecta

Oxalis pulchella var. beneprotecta, commonly known as the Beneprotecta Wood Sorrel, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, delicate yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or shaded gardens. It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family, known for its calcium oxalate crystals in the leaves.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
