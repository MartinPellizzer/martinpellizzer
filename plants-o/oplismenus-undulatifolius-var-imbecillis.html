Oplismenus undulatifolius var. imbecillis

Oplismenus undulatifolius var. imbecillis, commonly known as the wavy-leaf sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that exhibit a distinctive wavy or undulating edge. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, or along the edges of ponds. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered at the tips of its stems.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
