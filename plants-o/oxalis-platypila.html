Oxalis platypila

Oxalis platypila, commonly known as the flat-leaf wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive purple underside and small, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant belongs to the Oxalidaceae family, known for its calcium oxalate crystals in the leaves, which can cause irritation upon contact. Oxalis platypila typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in suitable climates.
