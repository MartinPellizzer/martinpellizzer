Orbexilum stipulatum

Orbexilum stipulatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and gravelly slopes.

Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-tolerant nature.
