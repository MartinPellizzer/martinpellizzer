Oberonia formosana

Oberonia formosana, commonly known as the Formosan orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique inflorescence and intricate flower structure. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as mountain forests, where it thrives in partial shade. Its flowers are often fragrant and display a range of colors, including white, pink, and purple, with intricate patterns on the petals.

Oberonia formosana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also studied for its ecological role in its native habitat.
