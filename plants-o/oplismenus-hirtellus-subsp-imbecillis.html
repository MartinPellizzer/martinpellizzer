Oplismenus hirtellus subsp. imbecillis

Oplismenus hirtellus subsp.

imbecillis, commonly known as the little bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States. It is characterized by its fine, blue-green blades and tufted growth habit, which contribute to its ornamental appeal.

The plant typically grows in full sun to partial shade and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for native plant gardens. Its seed heads are often adorned with a tuft of hairs, adding to its distinctive appearance. Oplismenus hirtellus subsp.

imbecillis plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife in its native range.
