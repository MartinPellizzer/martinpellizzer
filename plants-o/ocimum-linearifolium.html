Ocimum linearifolium

Ocimum linearifolium, commonly known as linear-leaf basil, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their essential oils. The plant features slender, linear leaves that are opposite and often have a slightly hairy texture. Its small, tubular flowers are typically purple or blue, arranged in whorled clusters.

Ocimum linearifolium is valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
