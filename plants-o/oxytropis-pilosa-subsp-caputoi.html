Oxytropis pilosa subsp. caputoi

Oxytropis pilosa subsp.

caputoi, commonly known as Caputo's hairy locoweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of small, yellowish to greenish flowers.

The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitats.
