Opuntia polyacantha var. arenaria

Opuntia polyacantha var. arenaria, commonly known as the desert prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flattened, spineless stems that are often covered in a whitish coating, which helps reflect sunlight and reduce water loss. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the summer months, followed by edible, red-fleshed fruits. Its succulent stems store water efficiently, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

This variety is well adapted to desert conditions, making it a hardy and resilient plant in harsh climates.
