Ocimum densiflorum

Ocimum densiflorum, commonly known as the denseflower basil, is a species of flowering plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit, aromatic leaves, and clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Ocimum densiflorum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and mild aromatic properties.
