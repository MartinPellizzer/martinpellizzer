Oxytropis tunnellii

Oxytropis tunnellii, commonly known as the Tunnell's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish to brownish pea-like flowers. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria.

Oxytropis tunnellii is often found in arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought and poor soil conditions.
