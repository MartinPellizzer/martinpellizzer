Oncophyllum globuliforme

Oncophyllum globuliforme, commonly known as the globe-shaped bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to freshwater environments in South America.

It possesses small, bladder-like structures that trap and digest aquatic invertebrates. The plant's leaves are typically submerged, while its stems may float on the water's surface. Oncophyllum globuliforme thrives in nutrient-poor waters, using its specialized structures to supplement its nutrient intake.

Its unique morphology and carnivorous behavior make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
