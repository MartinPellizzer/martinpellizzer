Oenothera diplotricha

Oenothera diplotricha, commonly known as the two-branched evening primrose, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant produces capsules that contain numerous small, elliptical seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Oenothera diplotricha is a member of the Onagraceae family, which includes other evening primrose species.
