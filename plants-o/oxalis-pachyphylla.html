Oxalis pachyphylla

Oxalis pachyphylla, commonly known as the fatleaf wood sorrel, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically appearing in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a desirable addition to xeriscaped landscapes.
