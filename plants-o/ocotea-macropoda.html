Ocotea macropoda

Ocotea macropoda, commonly known as the broad-leafed ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed enclosed in a fleshy, aromatic pulp.

Ocotea macropoda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
