Oxalis checoensis

Oxalis checoensis, commonly known as the Checo's wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive veins and small, delicate flowers that are typically pink or white. The plant produces yellowish-orange berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Oxalis checoensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in gardens.
