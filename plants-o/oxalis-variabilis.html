Oxalis variabilis

Oxalis variabilis, commonly known as the variable wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, delicate flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and blooms.

Oxalis variabilis is also notable for its ability to produce tubers, which allow it to survive in adverse conditions.
