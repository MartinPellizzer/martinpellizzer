Oxybaphus linearis var. subhispida

Oxybaphus linearis var. subhispida, commonly known as the slender sandverbena, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in coastal or inland dune environments. The plant features linear, hairy leaves that are often arranged in opposite pairs along the stem. Its small, white to pale blue flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, blooming during the cooler months.

It is adapted to arid conditions and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
