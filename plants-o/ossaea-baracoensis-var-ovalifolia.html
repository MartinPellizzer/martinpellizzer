Ossaea baracoensis var. ovalifolia

Ossaea baracoensis var. ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf ossaea, is a rare and unique plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale green flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is typically found in shaded forest understories.
