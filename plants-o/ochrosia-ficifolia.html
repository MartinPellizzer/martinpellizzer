Ochrosia ficifolia

Ochrosia ficifolia, commonly known as the pineapple tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and dense clusters of sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in winter, followed by edible, spiky fruits resembling pineapples.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and unique fruit, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
