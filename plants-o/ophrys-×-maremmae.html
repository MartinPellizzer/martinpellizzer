Ophrys × maremmae

Ophrys × maremmae, commonly known as the Maremma orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Ophrys genus, which is known for its mimicry of female insects to attract male pollinators. The plant produces intricate, bee-like flowers with a distinctive pouch-shaped structure that facilitates pollination. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats and is often found in rocky or calcareous soils.

Its botanical significance lies in its role in both ecological interactions and horticultural interest.
