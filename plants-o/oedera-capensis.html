Oedera capensis

Oedera capensis, commonly known as the Cape thornveld, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pod-like fruits containing seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Oedera capensis is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its potential use in traditional medicine.
