Osmanthus triandrus

Osmanthus triandrus, commonly known as the Chinese sweet osmanthus, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters during late summer to early autumn. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its flowers are widely used in traditional Chinese medicine and as a flavoring agent in teas and confections.

Osmanthus triandrus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its pleasant aroma.
