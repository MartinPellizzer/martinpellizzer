Oenothera albicaulis f. dentata

Oenothera albicaulis f. dentata, commonly known as the toothed evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and hairy, lance-shaped leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its flowers have four petals and are typically borne in clusters at the ends of the stems.

The plant is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats like grasslands and disturbed areas.
