Oplotheca poiretiana

Oplotheca poiretiana, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and compound pods. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, contributing to its ornamental appeal. Its seeds are encased in a fibrous, hairy pod, and the plant is sometimes cultivated for its nitrogen-fixing properties.

Oplotheca poiretiana is also valued for its potential in ecological restoration due to its adaptability to various soil types.
