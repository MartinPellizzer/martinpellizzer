Orthion caudatum

Orthion caudatum, commonly known as the long-tailed orthion, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Orthion caudatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
