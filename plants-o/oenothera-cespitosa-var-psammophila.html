Oenothera cespitosa var. psammophila

Oenothera cespitosa var. psammophila, commonly known as sand evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, adapted to thrive in sandy or gravelly soils. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by moths and other nocturnal insects.

This variety is often found in dry, open habitats such as dunes and disturbed areas.
