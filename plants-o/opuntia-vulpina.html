Opuntia vulpina

Opuntia vulpina, commonly known as the Prickly Pear Cactus, is a species native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems called cladodes. These stems are covered in spines and are adapted for storing water in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant flowers, typically in shades of yellow, orange, or red, which bloom in summer.

Its fruit, known as a tuna, is edible and has been used by indigenous peoples for food and traditional medicine.
