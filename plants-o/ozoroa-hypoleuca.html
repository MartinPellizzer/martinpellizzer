Ozoroa hypoleuca

Ozoroa hypoleuca, commonly known as the white-flowered senna, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow to white flowers that bloom in clusters. Ozoroa hypoleuca is often found in dry, open habitats such as savannas and grasslands.

Its ecological role includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for various herbivores.
