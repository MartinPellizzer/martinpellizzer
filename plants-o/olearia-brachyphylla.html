Olearia brachyphylla

Olearia brachyphylla, commonly known as the silverleaf tree fern, is a species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery, light green fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant has a unique ability to retain moisture, which helps it thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are covered in a fine, silvery down, contributing to its common name.

Olearia brachyphylla is often found in forested areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
