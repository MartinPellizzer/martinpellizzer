Oxytropis regelii

Oxytropis regelii, commonly known as Regel's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in late summer. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Oxytropis regelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in dry gardens.
