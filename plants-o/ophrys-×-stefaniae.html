Ophrys × stefaniae

Ophrys × stefaniae, commonly known as the Stefania orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the genus Ophrys, which is known for its mimicry of female insects to attract male pollinators. The plant typically produces intricate, bee-like flowers with a distinctive coloration and scent. It thrives in calcareous soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
