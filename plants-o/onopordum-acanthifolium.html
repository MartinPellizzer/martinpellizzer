Onopordum acanthifolium

Onopordum acanthifolium, commonly known as the goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers. It thrives in rocky or dry soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
