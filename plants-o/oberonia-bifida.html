Oberonia bifida

Oberonia bifida, commonly known as the split-leaf orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bifurcated leaves, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and green, typically blooming during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, organic-rich substrates.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, Oberonia bifida is often sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists.
