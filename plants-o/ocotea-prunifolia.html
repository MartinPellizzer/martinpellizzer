Ocotea prunifolia

Ocotea prunifolia, commonly known as the cherry-laurel or false laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic trees like cinnamon and bay laurel. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a drupe, typically containing a single seed, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and aromatic qualities.
