Oxalis clematodes

Oxalis clematodes, commonly known as the wood sorrel, is a flowering plant belonging to the Oxalidaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that resemble those of the genus Oxalis. The plant is native to various regions in the Americas and is often found in woodland and shady areas. Oxalis clematodes produces small, edible berries that are sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features include the presence of calcium oxalate crystals in its tissues, which contribute to its distinctive flavor and potential toxicity.
