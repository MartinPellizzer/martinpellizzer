Oxytropis helvetica

Oxytropis helvetica, commonly known as Swiss locust, is a flowering plant native to Europe, particularly Switzerland and parts of France.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in racemes during the summer months. It is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine regions.

Oxytropis helvetica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in marginal environments.
