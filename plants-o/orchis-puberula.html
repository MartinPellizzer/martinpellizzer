Orchis puberula

Orchis puberula, commonly known as the hairy orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous habitats such as meadows and woodlands. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish with a distinctive lip petal.

Orchis puberula is often found in open, sunny areas and is known for its delicate and inconspicuous blooms.
