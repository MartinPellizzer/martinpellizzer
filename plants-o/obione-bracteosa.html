Obione bracteosa

Obione bracteosa, commonly known as the bracteosa orchid, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in seasonal environments. Its leaves are usually elongated and strap-like, while the bracts are modified leaves that often enclose the inflorescence.

Obione bracteosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a cut flower.
