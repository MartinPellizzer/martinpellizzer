Ophrys × pulicchiensis

Ophrys × pulicchiensis, commonly known as the bee orchid hybrid, is a fascinating plant that exhibits striking floral mimicry.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is a natural hybrid between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera. The plant's flowers closely resemble female bees, a feature that aids in its unique pollination strategy. It typically grows in meadows and grasslands across Europe, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.

This hybrid is notable for its ability to produce fertile offspring and for its role in ecological studies on plant hybridization and pollination biology.
