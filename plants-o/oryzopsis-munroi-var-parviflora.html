Oryzopsis munroi var. parviflora

Oryzopsis munroi var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered muhly, is a grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and compact growth habit, which helps it conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, fluffy panicles of flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, adding ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

This variety is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing habitat and forage for wildlife.
