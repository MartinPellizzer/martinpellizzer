Odontoglossum alexandrae var. outramii

Odontoglossum alexandrae var. outramii, commonly known as the Alexandra orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the high altitudes of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, large, and showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has a pseudobulb structure, which helps it store water and nutrients in its native environment. Its leaves are usually long and narrow, adapted to the cool, humid conditions of its habitat.

This variety is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and unique floral structure.
