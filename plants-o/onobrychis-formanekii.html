Onobrychis formanekii

Onobrychis formanekii, commonly known as the yellow clover, is a perennial legume native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a robust root system that helps it thrive in poor soils, making it a hardy species. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Onobrychis formanekii plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in agricultural settings.
