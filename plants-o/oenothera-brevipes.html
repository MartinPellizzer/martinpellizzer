Oenothera brevipes

Oenothera brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and showy yellow or orange flowers that open in the evening. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. Oenothera brevipes is a member of the Onagraceae family, which includes other evening primroses and related species.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.
