Opuntia valentini

Opuntia valentini, commonly known as the Valentine's cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems that are often ribbed and covered in small spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Opuntia valentini is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
