Ooclinium

Ooclinium, commonly known as the "mud plant," is a type of aquatic plant found in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its simple, branching structure and its ability to thrive in muddy or slow-moving waters. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom during the summer months. Its roots help stabilize sediment, preventing erosion in aquatic ecosystems.

Ooclinium is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and environmental benefits.
