Opuntia megarrhiza

Opuntia megarrhiza, commonly known as the giant prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, flat, paddle-shaped stems that are covered in spines and glochids. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the summer months, followed by edible, fleshy fruits known as tunas. Its succulent structure allows it to store water efficiently, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Opuntia megarrhiza plays an important ecological role by providing food and shelter for various desert animals.
