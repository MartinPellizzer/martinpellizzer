Odontoglossum cinnamomeum

Odontoglossum cinnamomeum, commonly known as the cinnamon orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant has a pseudobulb structure, which stores water and nutrients, aiding its survival in high-altitude environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically arranged in a rosette pattern.

The orchid thrives in cool, humid conditions and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
