Orobanche chironii

Orobanche chironii, commonly known as the slender broomrape, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and cannot perform photosynthesis.

It relies entirely on its host plants for nutrients, typically growing on the roots of members of the legume family. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. It is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its parasitic nature, it plays a unique role in its ecosystem, influencing the growth and distribution of its host species.
