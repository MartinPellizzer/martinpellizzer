Orthochilus adenoglossus

Orthochilus adenoglossus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed rock orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in open habitats such as grasslands and fynbos regions. The plant features a slender, leafy stem with several narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are small and greenish-yellow, often with a slight purple tinge, and are arranged in a dense spike.

It is adapted to arid conditions and has a short growing season, flowering during the cooler months.
