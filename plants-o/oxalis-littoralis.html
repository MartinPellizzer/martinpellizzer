Oxalis littoralis

Oxalis littoralis, commonly known as the coastal wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are heart-shaped and often have a reddish or purple tinge. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to coastal environments with high salt exposure.

Oxalis littoralis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
