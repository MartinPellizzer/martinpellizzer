Opuntia heacockiae

Opuntia heacockiae, commonly known as the Heacock's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the Caribbean island of Hispaniola.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in sharp spines and woolly areoles. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit, known as a tunaf, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

Opuntia heacockiae is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife.
