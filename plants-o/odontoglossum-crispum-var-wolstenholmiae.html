Odontoglossum crispum var. wolstenholmiae

Odontoglossum crispum var. wolstenholmiae, commonly known as the Woolly Orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage and clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are often crinkled or wavy, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This orchid is prized by horticulturists for its ornamental value and unique foliage structure.
