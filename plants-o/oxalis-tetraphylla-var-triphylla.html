Oxalis tetraphylla var. triphylla

Oxalis tetraphylla var. triphylla, commonly known as the three-leaf wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves, each leaflet being heart-shaped with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for woodland gardens and native plant landscapes.
