Oxalis insipida

Oxalis insipida, commonly known as the sourgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces edible tubers that are rich in starch and have a slightly sour taste. Oxalis insipida thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.

It is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its potential culinary uses.
