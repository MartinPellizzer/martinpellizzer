Odontoglossum × macrospilum

Odontoglossum × macrospilum, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Odontoglossum ramosum and Odontoglossum macrosporum.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Andean regions of South America. The plant features fleshy, tuberous roots and lanceolate, dark green leaves with a hairy texture. It produces tall, branched stems bearing numerous showy, violet to purple flowers with intricate patterns.

The flowers are typically fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
