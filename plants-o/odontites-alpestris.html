Odontites alpestris

Odontites alpestris, commonly known as the alpine toothbrush, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Odontites alpestris thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

It is adapted to cold climates and is considered an indicator species of high-altitude environments.
