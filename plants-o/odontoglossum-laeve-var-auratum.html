Odontoglossum laeve var. auratum

Odontoglossum laeve var. auratum, commonly known as the golden odontoglossum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

This hybrid orchid is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers with a contrasting green lip, which are typically produced during the spring and early summer. The plant features erect, hairy pseudobulbs that support strap-like leaves, and it thrives in cool, humid environments with indirect light. It is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its vibrant color and ease of cultivation under proper conditions.

The variety's name 'auratum' refers to its golden hue, making it a standout in any collection.
