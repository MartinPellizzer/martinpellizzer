Opuntia bentonii

Opuntia bentonii, commonly known as the Mexican prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems that are covered in spines and are adapted for water storage. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits called tunas. Opuntia bentonii is a succulent plant with a shallow root system that allows it to thrive in arid environments.

It plays an important role in desert ecosystems and is also used in traditional medicine and as a food source.
