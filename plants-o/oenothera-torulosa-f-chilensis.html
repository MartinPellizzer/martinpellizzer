Oenothera torulosa f. chilensis

Oenothera torulosa f. chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly Chile.

It belongs to the Onagraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, oval seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its botanical significance lies in its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties.
