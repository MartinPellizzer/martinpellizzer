Ocotea valerioana

Ocotea valerioana, commonly known as the Brazilian camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by oval, woody fruits containing a single seed. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils with medicinal properties, making it valuable in traditional medicine.

The plant is also appreciated for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
