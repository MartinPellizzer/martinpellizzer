Oenothera biennis f. nanella

Oenothera biennis f. nanella, commonly known as the dwarf evening primrose, is a compact variant of the evening primrose.

It is characterized by its smaller stature and reduced flower size compared to the typical species. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that open in the evening. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in prairie and disturbed habitats.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in native plant gardens.
