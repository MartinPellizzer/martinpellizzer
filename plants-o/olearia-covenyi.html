Olearia covenyi

Olearia covenyi, commonly known as the Cook Strait daisy, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in coastal and lowland areas, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers consist of white ray florets surrounding yellow disk florets.

Olearia covenyi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a symbol of New Zealand's unique flora.
