Orthochilus trilamellatus

Orthochilus trilamellatus, commonly known as the three-lobed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, erect stems and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically arranged in a loose inflorescence. It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines.

Orthochilus trilamellatus thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
