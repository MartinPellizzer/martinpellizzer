Oxytropis acutirostris

Oxytropis acutirostris, commonly known as the sharp-beaked locoweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky, or sandy soils, often in mountainous regions. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually purple or pink in color, blooming in early summer. Its leaves are compound, with several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Oxytropis acutirostris is part of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has the ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through its root nodules.
