Osmunda verticillata

Osmunda verticillata, commonly known as the interrupted fern, is a species of fern characterized by its distinctive fronds.

It is native to North America and thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features a creeping rhizome with sporangia clustered in groups of three, which is a unique botanical trait. Its fronds are biennial, with the fertile fronds appearing in the second year.

The plant plays an important ecological role by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
