Olympia glauca

Olympia glauca, commonly known as the blue olympia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-gray hue that complements its vibrant flowers.

Olympia glauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
