Ophrys × coturii

Ophrys × coturii, commonly known as the bee orchid hybrid, is a fascinating plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Ophrys apifera (the bee orchid) and Ophrys insectifera (the wasp orchid), exhibiting a blend of characteristics from both parents. The plant is notable for its intricate, bee-like floral structures that mimic the appearance and scent of female bees, aiding in pollination through sexual deception. It typically grows in calcareous soils in temperate regions, preferring open, sunny habitats.

Ophrys × coturii is a short-lived perennial, often found in Mediterranean climates, and is valued for its unique morphology and ecological role.
