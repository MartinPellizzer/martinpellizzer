Othonna pectinata

Othonna pectinata, commonly known as the yellow oxalis, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a valuable addition to xeriscaped landscapes.
