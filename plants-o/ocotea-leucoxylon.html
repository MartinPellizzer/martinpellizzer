Ocotea leucoxylon

Ocotea leucoxylon, commonly known as the white sand ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, lowland forests and is known for its hard, durable wood. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and the tree plays a role in local ecosystems.

The species is also valued for its ecological importance in maintaining biodiversity in its native habitats.
