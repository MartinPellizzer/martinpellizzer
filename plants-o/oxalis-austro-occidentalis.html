Oxalis austro-occidentalis

Oxalis austro-occidentalis, commonly known as the Western Australian wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Australia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive red veins and produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It is often found in open woodlands and along coastal areas.

Oxalis austro-occidentalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
