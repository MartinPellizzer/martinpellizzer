Osbeckia alveolata

Osbeckia alveolata, commonly known as the woolly sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including grasslands and disturbed soils, and is often found in temperate regions.

Osbeckia alveolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other parts of the world as a garden plant.
