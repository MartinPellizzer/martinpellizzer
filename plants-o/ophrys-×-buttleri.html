Ophrys × buttleri

Ophrys × buttleri, commonly known as the bee orchid hybrid, is a fascinating plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera, exhibiting traits from both parent species. The plant is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. Its flowers are typically yellow with brown markings, and it thrives in sunny, well-drained soils in temperate regions.

This orchid is often found in meadows and grasslands, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
