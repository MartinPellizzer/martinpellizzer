Oligosporus pacificus

Oligosporus pacificus, commonly known as the Pacific oligosporus, is a rare and specialized plant species found in the coastal regions of the Pacific Ocean.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a waxy coating that aids in reducing water loss. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are adapted for pollination by wind rather than insects. Its root system is highly developed, allowing it to thrive in sandy and nutrient-poor soils.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological specificity, Oligosporus pacificus is considered a vulnerable species in its native habitat.
