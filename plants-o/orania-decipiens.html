Orania decipiens

Orania decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the subtropical regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that resemble other orchid species, a trait that may aid in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption.

Orania decipiens plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small invertebrates.
