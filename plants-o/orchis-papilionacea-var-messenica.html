Orchis papilionacea var. messenica

Orchis papilionacea var. messenica, commonly known as the Messenian orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, butterfly-like flowers. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.

The orchid is known for its ornamental value and is often found in rocky habitats across southern Europe.
