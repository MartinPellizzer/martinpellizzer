Ophrestia radicosa

Ophrestia radicosa, commonly known as the root orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and produces small, delicate flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in the Americas, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its roots are fibrous and play a crucial role in anchoring the plant and absorbing nutrients from the soil.

Ophrestia radicosa is often found in forest understories, where it forms dense colonies and contributes to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.
