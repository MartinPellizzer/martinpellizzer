Octomeria praestans

Octomeria praestans, commonly known as the golden torch cactus, is a striking member of the Cactaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that are segmented and covered in cylindrical, spiny areoles. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters at the tips of the stems, often during the rainy season. Its unique appearance and ability to thrive in arid conditions make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

However, due to its slow growth and specific care requirements, it is not commonly found in typical garden settings.
