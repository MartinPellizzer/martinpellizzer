Ocotea ensifolia

Ocotea ensifolia, commonly known as the Java cinnamon or wild cinnamon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark brown bark that resembles cinnamon. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers and yellowish-orange berries. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and have a strong, spicy scent.

The tree is valued for its essential oils and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of aromatic wood.
