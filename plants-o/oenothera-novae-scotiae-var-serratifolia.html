Oenothera novae-scotiae var. serratifolia

Oenothera novae-scotiae var. serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It features elongated, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, typical of the evening primrose genus. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open woodlands and disturbed sites.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and ornamental gardening.
