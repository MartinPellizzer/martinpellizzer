Odontochilus serriformis

Odontochilus serriformis, commonly known as the serrated toothed orchid, is a rare and exquisite epiphytic orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, tooth-like projections on its leaves and a delicate, fragrant flower with a distinctive serrated lip. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high air humidity and well-draining substrates for optimal growth. Its botanical features make it a prized species among orchid enthusiasts and botanists.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is also considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
