Ophrys × socae

Ophrys × socae, commonly known as the bee orchid hybrid, is a fascinating plant with unique botanical characteristics.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera, both of which are known for their mimicry of female insects. The plant produces striking flowers that closely resemble female bees, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. Its inflorescence consists of a single spike bearing multiple showy, bee-like flowers.

The plant is native to parts of Europe and thrives in calcareous soils, often found in meadows and woodland edges.
