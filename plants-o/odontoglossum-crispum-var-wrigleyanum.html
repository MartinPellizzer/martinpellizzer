Odontoglossum crispum var. wrigleyanum

Odontoglossum crispum var. wrigleyanum, commonly known as the Wrigley's orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, spurred orchid flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange, with a distinctive, elongated lip. The plant is a sympodial orchid, meaning it grows in a branching pattern with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are fleshy and arranged in a rosette, aiding in water retention in its high-altitude habitat.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its showy blooms.
