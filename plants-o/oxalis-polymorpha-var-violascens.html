Oxalis polymorpha var. violascens

Oxalis polymorpha var. violascens, commonly known as the purple wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive purple hue and produces small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant is characterized by its erect stems and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
