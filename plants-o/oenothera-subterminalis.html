Oenothera subterminalis

Oenothera subterminalis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Oenothera subterminalis is a member of the Onagraceae family, which includes other evening primroses and related species.
