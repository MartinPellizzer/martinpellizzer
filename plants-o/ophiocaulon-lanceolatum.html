Ophiocaulon lanceolatum

Ophiocaulon lanceolatum, commonly known as the lanceleaf pittosporum, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, acidic soils along stream banks and in wetlands. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. It produces small, fragrant white flowers in late spring, followed by blue-black berries that are edible for birds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
