Ocotea puchury-minor

Ocotea puchury-minor, commonly known as the Puchury Minor or Andean Ocotea, is a small evergreen tree native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments at elevations between 1,000 and 2,500 meters. Its small, fragrant flowers are followed by dark blue berries that are an important food source for local wildlife.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in local communities for various purposes.
