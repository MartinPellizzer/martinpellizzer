Orthoclada rariflora

Orthoclada rariflora, commonly known as the rare-flowered orthoclade, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its delicate, ribbon-like leaves.

It thrives in shallow, freshwater environments with low light conditions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or pale yellow. Its reproductive structures are adapted for water dispersal, allowing it to spread in still or slow-moving waters.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
