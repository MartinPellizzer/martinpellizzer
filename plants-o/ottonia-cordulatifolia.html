Ottonia cordulatifolia

Ottonia cordulatifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf mallow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ottonia cordulatifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate humidity and light conditions.
