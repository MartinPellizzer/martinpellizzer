Oxalis japonica

Oxalis japonica, commonly known as Japanese wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive pattern of white veins, giving it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Oxalis japonica is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
