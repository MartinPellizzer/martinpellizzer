Oxytropis caudiciramosa

Oxytropis caudiciramosa, commonly known as the long-stemmed locoweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its robust, branched stem and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale blue to purple in color. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and has adapted to arid conditions through its deep root system.

Oxytropis caudiciramosa is also known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock.
