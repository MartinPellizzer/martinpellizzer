Orchis angustifolia f. suberecta

Orchis angustifolia f. suberecta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or grassland habitats. The plant features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, woolly pubescence. Its flowers are pale pink to white, with a distinctive lip that is usually darker in color.

This subspecies is notable for its compact growth habit and is often found in shaded, humid environments.
