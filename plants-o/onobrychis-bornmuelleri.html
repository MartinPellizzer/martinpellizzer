Onobrychis bornmuelleri

Onobrychis bornmuelleri, commonly known as the mountain vetch, is a perennial leguminous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion.

Onobrychis bornmuelleri is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
