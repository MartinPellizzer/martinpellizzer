Onopordum davisii

Onopordum davisii, commonly known as Davis's thistle, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, spiny stems. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open woodlands. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, while its flower heads consist of both ray and disk flowers.

Davis's thistle is adapted to withstand drought and has a long flowering period, making it a resilient component of its native ecosystem.
