Oenothera angustissima var. quebecensis

Oenothera angustissima var. quebecensis, commonly known as the Quebec narrowleaf evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This variety is noted for its ecological importance as a food source for nocturnal pollinators.
