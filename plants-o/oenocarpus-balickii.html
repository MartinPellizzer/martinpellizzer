Oenocarpus balickii

Oenocarpus balickii, commonly known as the African oil palm, is a flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellowish flowers followed by fruiting structures called infrutescences, which contain the edible seeds. Its oil-rich seeds are commercially valuable for palm oil production.

The plant also plays a role in traditional medicine and has ecological significance in its native habitats.
