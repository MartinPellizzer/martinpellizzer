Oncidium lindenii

Oncidium lindenii, commonly known as the yellow butterfly orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, pendant-like flower spikes that bear numerous yellow-orange blossoms resembling butterflies. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are typically sword-shaped and arranged in a fan-like pattern.

Oncidium lindenii prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
