Orchis caucasica

Orchis caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian twayblade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and meadows. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption in its natural habitat.

Orchis caucasica is notable for its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native range.
