Orchis taubertiana

Orchis taubertiana, commonly known as the Taubert's orchid, is a species of orchid native to central and eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodlands and meadows. Its flowers are bilaterally symmetrical, with three sepals and three petals, one of which is prominently hooded.

Orchis taubertiana is a perennial herb that plays a role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining biodiversity in its habitat.
