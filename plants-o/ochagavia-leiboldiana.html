Ochagavia leiboldiana

Ochagavia leiboldiana, commonly known as the Leibold's feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and feathery, gray-green foliage that resembles a plume. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its small, inconspicuous flowers appear in summer, producing fluffy seed heads that add visual interest.

Ochagavia leiboldiana is often used in ornamental landscaping for its elegant, airy appearance.
