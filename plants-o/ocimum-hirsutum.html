Ocimum hirsutum

Ocimum hirsutum, commonly known as hairy basil, is a species of flowering plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its culinary and medicinal uses.

Ocimum hirsutum is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
