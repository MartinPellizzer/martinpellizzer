Ophrys × gauthieri

Ophrys × gauthieri, commonly known as the Gauthier's bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the genus Ophrys, which is characterized by its intricate floral structures that mimic female insects to attract male pollinators. The plant produces showy, bee-like flowers with a distinctive pouch-shaped labellum that plays a key role in its pollination strategy. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or grassland habitats.

The hybrid nature of Ophrys × gauthieri results from crosses between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera, contributing to its unique botanical features.
