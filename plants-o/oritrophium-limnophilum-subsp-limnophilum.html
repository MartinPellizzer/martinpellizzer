Oritrophium limnophilum subsp. limnophilum

Oritrophium limnophilum subsp.

limnophilum, commonly known as the water-loving fleabane, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America. It typically grows in marshes, pond margins, and other moist environments, where it thrives in saturated soils.

The plant features showy yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, surrounded by pale green to white ray florets. It has hairy stems and leaves that are often narrow and linear.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing wetland soils.
