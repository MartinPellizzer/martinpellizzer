Oplismenus burmanni var. lanatus

Oplismenus burmanni var. lanatus, commonly known as the hairy iron plant, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are densely covered with soft, white hairs, giving it a velvety appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers that are clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its hairy foliage and compact growth habit make it a distinctive and ornamental plant in horticultural settings.
