Ornithogalum candicans

Ornithogalum candicans, commonly known as the candleplant or white bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a short, stocky rhizome and produces erect, slender stems that bear clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette. The plant is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
