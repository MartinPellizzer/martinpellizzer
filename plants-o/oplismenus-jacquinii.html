Oplismenus jacquinii

Oplismenus jacquinii, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive yellowish-green coloration. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically yellow and appear in clusters during the spring.

Oplismenus jacquinii is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
