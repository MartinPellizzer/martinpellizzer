Oryctanthus occidentalis subsp. continentalis

Oryctanthus occidentalis subsp.

continentalis, commonly known as the Western burrweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a spiny capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.
