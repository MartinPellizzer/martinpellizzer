Oenothera biennis var. leptomeres

Oenothera biennis var. leptomeres, commonly known as the slender evening primrose, is a variant of the evening primrose native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated stems and showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in prairies and open fields.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
