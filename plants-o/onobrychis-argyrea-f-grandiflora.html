Onobrychis argyrea f. grandiflora

Onobrychis argyrea f. grandiflora, commonly known as the silver vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant is native to regions of southern Europe and parts of Asia, often growing in dry, rocky soils. It has a trailing growth habit and is sometimes cultivated as a ornamental plant or for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
