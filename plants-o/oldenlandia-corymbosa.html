Oldenlandia corymbosa

Oldenlandia corymbosa, commonly known as the coral vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows as a climbing or trailing plant, often found in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, which are borne on slender pedicels. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
