Orthosiphon sarmentosus

Orthosiphon sarmentosus, commonly known as Java tea or Malabar tea, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its creeping growth habit and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Orthosiphon sarmentosus is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its diuretic and anti-inflammatory effects.
