Oxytropis campestris var. melanocephala

Oxytropis campestris var. melanocephala, commonly known as black-headed field vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, and it thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its seeds are encased in hard pods, which contribute to its ability to persist in arid environments.
