Orchis pseudoapifera

Orchis pseudoapifera, commonly known as the bee orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive flower structure, which resembles a bee, aiding in pollination by mimicking the appearance and scent of an insect. The plant typically grows in damp, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. It produces a single spike of flowers, each with a three-lobed lip that is often brightly colored.

Orchis pseudoapifera is a perennial herb that thrives in temperate climates and is often found in association with other orchid species.
