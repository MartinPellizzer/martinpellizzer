Oxalis mendozensis

Oxalis mendozensis, commonly known as the Mendozensis wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly in regions of Brazil and Peru.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. It produces small, edible berries that are sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Oxalis mendozensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
