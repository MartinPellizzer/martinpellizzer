Onobrychis arenaria subsp. miniata

Onobrychis arenaria subsp.

miniata, commonly known as the sand vetch, is a flowering plant native to sandy and coastal areas. It is characterized by its low-growing habit and small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to reddish-purple.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically trifoliate, with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dunes or along shorelines.

This subspecies is adapted to harsh environments and plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils.
