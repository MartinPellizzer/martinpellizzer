Oreorchis wilsonii

Oreorchis wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's twayblade, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the eastern United States.

This herbaceous perennial features a slender, leafless stem that bears two opposite leaves near the base. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and lack a distinct scent, typical of many orchids. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

It is considered a rare and endangered species due to habitat loss and over-collection.
