Osmoxylon chrysanthum

Osmoxylon chrysanthum, commonly known as the golden thorn, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
