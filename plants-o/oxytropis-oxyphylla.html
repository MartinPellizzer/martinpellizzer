Oxytropis oxyphylla

Oxytropis oxyphylla, commonly known as the yellow globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. The plant features a stout, branching stem and produces showy, yellow or orange flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Oxytropis oxyphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
