Olearia turczaninovii

Olearia turczaninovii, commonly known as the Turczaninov's tree fern, is a species native to New Zealand.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds. The plant typically grows in coastal and lowland forest areas, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its unique appearance makes it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.

Olearia turczaninovii is also valued for its role in providing habitat for native wildlife.
