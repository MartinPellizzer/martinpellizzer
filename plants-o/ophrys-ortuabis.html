Ophrys ortuabis

Ophrys ortuabis, commonly known as the bee orchid, is a fascinating plant with unique botanical features.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in grasslands and heathlands. The plant is characterized by its striking flower, which resembles a bee in both color and shape, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. Its flowers are spurred at the base and have a distinct lip that mimics the appearance of a female bee.

The plant produces small, greenish-brown capsules as fruits, containing numerous minute seeds that are dispersed by the wind.
