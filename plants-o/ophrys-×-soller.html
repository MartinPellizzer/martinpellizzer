Ophrys × soller

Ophrys × soller, commonly known as the Soller orchid, is a hybrid species within the Ophrys genus, which is part of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its intricate, bee-mimicking flowers that play a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in temperate regions, favoring moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or rocky slopes. Its leaves are usually elongated and grass-like, while the inflorescence consists of a single, complex flower spike.

The botanical features of Ophrys × soller highlight its evolutionary adaptations for specialized pollination by bees.
