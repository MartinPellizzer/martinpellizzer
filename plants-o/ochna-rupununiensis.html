Ochna rupununiensis

Ochna rupununiensis, commonly known as the Rupununi ochna, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in Guyana and Venezuela.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical rainforests, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its fruits are woody capsules that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals.

Ochna rupununiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
