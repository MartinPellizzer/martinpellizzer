Ocotea cicatricosa

Ocotea cicatricosa, commonly known as the scarred ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocado, and cinnamon. The tree is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and can grow up to 30 meters tall. Its bark is thick and fissured, often displaying a distinctive scarred appearance.

The plant is valued for its essential oils and has traditional uses in medicine and perfumery.
