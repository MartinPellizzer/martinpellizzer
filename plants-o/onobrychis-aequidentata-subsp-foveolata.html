Onobrychis aequidentata subsp. foveolata

Onobrychis aequidentata subsp.

foveolata, commonly known as the foveolate burclover, is a perennial legume native to the western United States. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and cylindrical, spiny pods that contain small, yellowish seeds.

The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides. It has a deep taproot system, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions.

Its flowers are usually pink to purple and bloom in late spring to early summer.
