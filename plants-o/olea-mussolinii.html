Olea mussolinii

Olea mussolinii, commonly known as the Italian olive tree, is a species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with dark green, leathery leaves that are elliptical in shape. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by olive fruits that are typically harvested when they are fully mature. Olea mussolinii is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a resilient plant in arid environments.

Its historical and cultural significance is reflected in its use in traditional agriculture and landscaping.
