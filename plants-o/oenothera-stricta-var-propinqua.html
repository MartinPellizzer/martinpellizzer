Oenothera stricta var. propinqua

Oenothera stricta var. propinqua, commonly known as the narrowleaf evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its flowers are followed by long, slender seed pods that release tiny seeds when mature.

Oenothera stricta var. propinqua is often found in dry, open habitats like grasslands and rocky areas.
