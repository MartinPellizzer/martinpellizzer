Ophrys levantina

Ophrys levantina, commonly known as the Levantine bee orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its intricate, bee-like flower structure, which plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant features a single, showy flower with a lip that mimics the appearance of a female bee, attracting male bees for pollination. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands.

Ophrys levantina is a perennial herb with a short rhizome and lanceolate leaves.
