Onosma alborosea var. macrocarpa

Onosma alborosea var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the Persian shield, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage with a deep green hue and a distinctive silver or white underside. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in various climates with proper care.
