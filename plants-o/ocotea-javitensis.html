Ocotea javitensis

Ocotea javitensis, commonly known as the Java Ocotea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a dark blue berry containing a single seed, and it is often found in lowland and montane forests.

The tree is valued for its aromatic wood and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
