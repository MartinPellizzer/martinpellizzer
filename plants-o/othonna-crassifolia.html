Othonna crassifolia

Othonna crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leaved othonna, is a succulent shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, fleshy, and often blue-green foliage, which is adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Its leaves are arranged in a rosette pattern, providing structural support and maximizing sunlight absorption.

Othonna crassifolia is well-suited for xeriscaping and thrives in well-drained soil with minimal water requirements.
