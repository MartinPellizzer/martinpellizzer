Ocimum anisatum

Ocimum anisatum, commonly known as wild basil or anise basil, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and oregano. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a strong, pungent aroma, and small purple or white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its essential oils contain compounds such as anethole and linalool, which contribute to its distinctive scent and medicinal properties.

Ocimum anisatum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported digestive and antimicrobial benefits.
