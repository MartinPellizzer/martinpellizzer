Ophrys transhyrcana subsp. amanensis

Ophrys transhyrcana subsp.

amanensis, commonly known as the Aman's bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which closely resembles a female bee, a feature evolved to mimic pollinators through a process called sexual deception.

The plant produces showy, bee-like flowers with a distinctive coloration and intricate patterns that aid in attracting male bees for pollination. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils in dry, mountainous regions, often in areas with limited vegetation.

Due to its restricted distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species facing threats from habitat degradation and climate change.
