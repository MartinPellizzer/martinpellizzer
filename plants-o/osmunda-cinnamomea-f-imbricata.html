Osmunda cinnamomea f. imbricata

Osmunda cinnamomea f. imbricata, commonly known as the cinnamon fern, is a fern species characterized by its distinctive, cinnamon-brown fiddleheads that unfurl into feather-like fronds.

It is a perennial plant native to North America, often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has a robust rhizome system that supports its growth and helps it thrive in wet conditions. Its fronds are typically bi-lobed and have a smooth, glossy surface, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, a key feature of its reproductive biology.
