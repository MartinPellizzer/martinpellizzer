Oxalis azanaquensis

Oxalis azanaquensis, commonly known as the Azanquensis wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in Brazil.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, delicate flowers. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, often accompanied by two small, leaf-like structures at the base. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Oxalis azanaquensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
