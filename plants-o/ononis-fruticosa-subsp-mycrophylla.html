Ononis fruticosa subsp. mycrophylla

Ononis fruticosa subsp.

mycrophylla, commonly known as the small-fruited whitlow grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, narrow leaves and produces delicate, yellowish-white flowers in early spring.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas. Its flowers are typically clustered in loose inflorescences, and it is well adapted to dry, sunny environments.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smaller fruiting structures compared to other subspecies of Ononis fruticosa.
