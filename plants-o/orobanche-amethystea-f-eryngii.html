Orobanche amethystea f. eryngii

Orobanche amethystea f. eryngii, commonly known as the eryngii form of the purple broomweed, is a parasitic plant belonging to the Orobanchaceae family.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on its host plant for nutrients, typically attaching to the roots of other species. The plant is characterized by its highly reduced leaves and showy, purple or violet flowers that bloom in summer. It is often found in disturbed soils and can be a weed in agricultural settings.

Its botanical structure reflects its hemiparasitic nature, with specialized structures for attaching to and extracting nutrients from its host.
