Onosma cinerea subsp. sanguinolenta

Onosma cinerea subsp.

sanguinolenta, commonly known as the crimson onosma, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The leaves are typically gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and the plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
