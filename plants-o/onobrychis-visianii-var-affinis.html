Onobrychis visianii var. affinis

Onobrychis visianii var. affinis, commonly known as the lesser sainfoin, is a perennial legume native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. It has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion.

This species is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural settings.
