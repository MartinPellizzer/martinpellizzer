Oxytropis nigrescens var. uniflora

Oxytropis nigrescens var. uniflora, commonly known as the single-flowered locoweed, is a perennial legume native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.

The species is adapted to arid conditions and has a deep root system for water absorption.
