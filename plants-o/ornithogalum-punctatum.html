Ornithogalum punctatum

Ornithogalum punctatum, commonly known as the spotted lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers with distinctive purple spots. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ornithogalum punctatum is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and delicate floral display.
