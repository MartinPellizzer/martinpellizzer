Ochthocosmus verschuerenii

Ochthocosmus verschuerenii, commonly known as the Van der Veken's orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, spurred flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree bark in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and often have a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Due to its rarity and unique beauty, it is highly valued in botanical collections and conservation efforts.
