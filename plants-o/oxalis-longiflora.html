Oxalis longiflora

Oxalis longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 12 inches. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers with five petals. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Oxalis longiflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
