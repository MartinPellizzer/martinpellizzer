Oxalis articulata var. hirsuta

Oxalis articulata var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces and small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is characterized by its distinctive leaf structure and is often used in rock gardens or as an ornamental plant.

Due to its adaptability, it is relatively easy to cultivate in a variety of garden settings.
