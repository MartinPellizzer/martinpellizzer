Oxytropis alberti

Oxytropis alberti, commonly known as Albert's locoweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous leaflets and produces small, yellow or greenish pods that contain seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.

Oxytropis alberti is also known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock.
