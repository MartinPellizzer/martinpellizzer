Opuntia stanlyi var. kunzei

Opuntia stanlyi var. kunzei, commonly known as the Stanley's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in spines and glochids, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers, typically in late spring or early summer, followed by red, fleshy fruits known as tunas. Its succulent stems store water, allowing it to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.

Opuntia stanlyi var. kunzei is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
