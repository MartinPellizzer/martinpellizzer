Oxera candelabrum

Oxera candelabrum, commonly known as the candlestick oxera, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive branching pattern, which resembles a candlestick, giving it its common name. The plant features small, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often in spring or early summer. Its leaves are typically alternate, simple, and ovate, with a smooth texture.

Oxera candelabrum thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or as a border plant.
