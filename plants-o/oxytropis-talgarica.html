Oxytropis talgarica

Oxytropis talgarica, commonly known as the Talgaric locoweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

Oxytropis talgarica is also noted for its toxic alkaloids, which can affect livestock that consume it.
