Osmanthus × fortunei var. cordifolius

Osmanthus × fortunei var. cordifolius, commonly known as the Chinese sweet osmanthus, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and fragrant yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late autumn. The plant produces small, sweet-smelling fruits that are edible and often used in traditional Chinese cuisine and medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its aromatic properties and ornamental value make it a valued species in horticulture and cultural practices.
