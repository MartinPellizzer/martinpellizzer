Oenothera elata var. hirsutissima

Oenothera elata var. hirsutissima, commonly known as the hairy plains evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the central and western United States.

It is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that open in the evening and close by morning. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its varietal name, hirsutissima. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open prairies and disturbed areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
