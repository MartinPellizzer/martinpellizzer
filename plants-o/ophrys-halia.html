Ophrys halia

Ophrys halia, commonly known as the Greek orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, bee-like flowers that mimic the appearance of female bees to attract male pollinators. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish in color, with intricate patterns that aid in its unique pollination strategy.

Ophrys halia is a rare and protected species, often found in specific habitats across southern Europe.
