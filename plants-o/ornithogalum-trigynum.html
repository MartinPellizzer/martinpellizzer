Ornithogalum trigynum

Ornithogalum trigynum, commonly known as the three-lobed star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale green flowers. The flowers are typically arranged in umbels and bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

It is also known for its toxic properties, containing calcium oxalate crystals that can cause irritation if ingested.
