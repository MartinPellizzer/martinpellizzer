Ocimum cordifolium

Ocimum cordifolium, commonly known as holy basil or tulsi, is a perennial herb native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves that are heart-shaped, giving it its scientific name. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown in tropical and subtropical regions. It produces small, white or light purple flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Ocimum cordifolium is widely used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits and is also a popular culinary herb.
