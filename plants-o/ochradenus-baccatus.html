Ochradenus baccatus

Ochradenus baccatus, commonly known as the desert globemallow, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spherical shape, with dense clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves and stems, which help it conserve water in harsh, dry environments. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access moisture from deeper soil layers.

Ochradenus baccatus is well adapted to extreme conditions and plays a role in stabilizing desert soils.
