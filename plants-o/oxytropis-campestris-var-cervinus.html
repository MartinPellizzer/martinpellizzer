Oxytropis campestris var. cervinus

Oxytropis campestris var. cervinus, commonly known as the redstem locoweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces cylindrical, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in certain ecosystems.
