Orthophytum vidaliorum

Orthophytum vidaliorum, commonly known as the Vidal's orthophytum, is a species of flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect water. The plant produces striking inflorescences with vibrant red and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat specificity, it is a sought-after plant among bromeliad enthusiasts.
