Osmunda × nipponica

Osmunda × nipponica, commonly known as the Japanese shield fern, is a hybrid fern species resulting from the cross between Osmunda japonica and Osmunda regalis.

It is characterized by its large, shield-shaped fronds that can reach lengths of up to 1.5 meters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its rhizomes are robust and form dense colonies, contributing to its ability to thrive in various soil types.

The fronds are bioluminescent in some varieties, adding to its ecological and aesthetic significance.
