Oxalis werdemannii

Oxalis werdemannii, commonly known as the Werdermann's wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces edible, tangy berries that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Oxalis werdemannii is also noted for its ability to spread through runners, making it a versatile ground cover in suitable climates.
