Olearia rudis

Olearia rudis, commonly known as the rough leaved daisy, is a species of flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in coastal and subalpine regions, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are rough and hairy, and the flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with a central disc.

Olearia rudis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also studied for its ecological role in native ecosystems.
