Olearia ciliata

Olearia ciliata, commonly known as the silky olearia, is a species of flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its soft, silvery foliage and showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Olearia ciliata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
