Orchis × huteri

Orchis × huteri, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a natural hybrid between Orchis morio and Orchis mascula.

It is a herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shady woodland habitats. The plant produces striking purple or violet flowers with a distinctive lip structure typical of the Orchidaceae family. It blooms in late spring to early summer and is often found in the British Isles and parts of Europe.

Orchis × huteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
