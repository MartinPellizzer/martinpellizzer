Olea cajetana

Olea cajetana, commonly known as the sweet chestnut or chestnut tree, is a species of evergreen tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The tree produces small, edible fruits that are encased in a spiky husk, which contain the nut inside. Olea cajetana is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It also plays an important role in traditional landscaping and has historical significance in Mediterranean cultures.
