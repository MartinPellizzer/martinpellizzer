Orchis mascula f. kotuliana

Orchis mascula f. kotuliana, commonly known as the Kotulian male orchid, is a rare and distinctive orchid species native to specific regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking, often violet or purple flowers with a unique, elongated shape. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or meadow habitats. It has lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged in a basal rosette.

This orchid is notable for its specialized pollination mechanisms and is considered a conservation priority due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity.
