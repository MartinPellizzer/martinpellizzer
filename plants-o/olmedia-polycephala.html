Olmedia polycephala

Olmedia polycephala, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small blue or purple daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grayish-green, often covered in a fine, powdery coating that helps reduce water loss.

Olmedia polycephala is valued in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
