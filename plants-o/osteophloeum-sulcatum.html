Osteophloeum sulcatum

Osteophloeum sulcatum, commonly known as the zigzag vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, zigzagging stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruits are fleshy berries that turn red when mature, containing several small seeds.

Osteophloeum sulcatum is often found in tropical and subtropical forests, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments.
