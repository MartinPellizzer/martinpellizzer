Orchis sooi

Orchis sooi, commonly known as the purple orchid, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its roots are fibrous and help it anchor in soil while absorbing nutrients.

Orchis sooi is also noted for its complex flower structure, which includes three sepals, three petals, and a specialized lip that aids in pollination.
