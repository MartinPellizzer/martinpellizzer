Oxalis erosa

Oxalis erosa, commonly known as the pale wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features trifoliate leaves with three heart-shaped lobes and small, delicate flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny, dark seeds. Oxalis erosa thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to spread through runners, forming dense mats in suitable conditions.
