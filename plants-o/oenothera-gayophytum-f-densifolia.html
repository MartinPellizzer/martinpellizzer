Oenothera gayophytum f. densifolia

Oenothera gayophytum f. densifolia, commonly known as the denseleaf evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has linear, gray-green leaves that are densely arranged along the stem, giving it a compact appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique flowering behavior.
