Oxalis hirsutissima var. gracilior

Oxalis hirsutissima var. gracilior, commonly known as the slender wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive heart-shaped base and small, delicate flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and partial shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate foliage and early spring blooming habit.
