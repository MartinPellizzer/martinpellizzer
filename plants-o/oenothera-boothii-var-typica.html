Oenothera boothii var. typica

Oenothera boothii var. typica, commonly known as the desert evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are adapted to arid environments. It produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Oenothera boothii var. typica is a hardy species that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.
