Oxalis scandens

Oxalis scandens, commonly known as the trailing wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive seed pods that split open to release seeds. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Oxalis scandens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
