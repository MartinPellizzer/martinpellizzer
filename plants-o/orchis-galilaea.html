Orchis galilaea

Orchis galilaea, commonly known as the Galilean orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to the Middle East.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking purple to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its morphology includes a basal rosette of lanceolate leaves and a single, erect flowering stem.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
