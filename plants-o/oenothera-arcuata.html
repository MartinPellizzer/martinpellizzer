Oenothera arcuata

Oenothera arcuata, commonly known as the curled evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and yellow flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oval seeds. Oenothera arcuata is often found in prairies, open woods, and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and native plant gardens.
