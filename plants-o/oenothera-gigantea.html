Oenothera gigantea

Oenothera gigantea, commonly known as the giant evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach heights of up to six feet. The plant produces showy, yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Oenothera gigantea is often found in dry, open habitats like grasslands and rocky slopes.
