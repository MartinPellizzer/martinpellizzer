Opuntia vulgaris var. lemaireana

Opuntia vulgaris var. lemaireana, commonly known as the Lemaire prickly pear, is a succulent cactus native to Mexico.

It features flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in spines and glochids, which are small, barbed bristles. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tunas, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
