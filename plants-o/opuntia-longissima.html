Opuntia longissima

Opuntia longissima, commonly known as the longleaf prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, flat, paddle-shaped stems that are covered in spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible, fleshy fruits known as tunas. Opuntia longissima is a succulent plant adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick stems.

It plays an important role in desert ecosystems and is also valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
