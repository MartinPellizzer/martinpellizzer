Ornithogalum gussonei subsp. tenuifolium

Ornithogalum gussonei subsp.

tenuifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It features a short, underground rhizome and produces upright, slender stems with narrow, linear leaves.

The plant is notable for its clusters of white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
