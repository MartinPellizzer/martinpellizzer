Opuntia chihuahuensis

Opuntia chihuahuensis, commonly known as the Chihuahua prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems that are covered in spines and are often ribbed. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers, which are typically followed by edible, fleshy fruits known as tunas. Opuntia chihuahuensis is adapted to arid environments and has a shallow root system that allows it to thrive in dry conditions.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental and edible qualities.
