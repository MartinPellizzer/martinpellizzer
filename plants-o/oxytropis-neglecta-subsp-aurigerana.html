Oxytropis neglecta subsp. aurigerana

Oxytropis neglecta subsp.

aurigerana, commonly known as the golden locoweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow to reddish flowers.

The plant is typically found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has a deep taproot system, which allows it to survive in arid conditions.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more robust growth and golden tinge in its flowers compared to other members of the species.
