Ornithogalum ovatum subsp. oliverorum

Ornithogalum ovatum subsp.

oliverorum, commonly known as the Oliver's star of Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant is characterized by its bulbous underground stem, which serves as a storage organ for nutrients. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its elegant appearance and long-lasting blooms.
