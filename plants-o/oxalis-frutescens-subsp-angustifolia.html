Oxalis frutescens subsp. angustifolia

Oxalis frutescens subsp.

angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa. It features trifoliate leaves with three heart-shaped leaflets and small, delicate flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover. It is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and flowers.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
