Ononis antiquorum var. hispanica

Ononis antiquorum var. hispanica, commonly known as Spanish catchfly, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, pinkish-purple flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas.

Its foliage is covered in fine hairs, giving it a woolly appearance.
