Orthosiphon rubicundus var. macrocarpus

Orthosiphon rubicundus var. macrocarpus, commonly known as the large-fruited slender horse-gram, is a tropical perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, pale yellow to greenish flowers arranged in spikes, followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its potential medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
