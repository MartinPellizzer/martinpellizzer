Ocotea hartiana

Ocotea hartiana, commonly known as the yellow sandarac tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, aromatic bark and elliptical, leathery leaves with a glossy surface. Its small, white flowers are followed by dark blue berries that are edible for some bird species.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
