Ophrys serotina

Ophrys serotina, commonly known as the late bee orchid, is a species of orchid native to Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in calcareous grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pale brown or yellowish with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Ophrys serotina blooms from late summer to early autumn, contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
