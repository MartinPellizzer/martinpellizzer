Oxytropis karjaginii

Oxytropis karjaginii, commonly known as Karjagin's locoweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces small, yellow to brown flowers arranged in dense spikes. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Oxytropis karjaginii is part of the legume family and has a deep root system adapted to arid environments.
