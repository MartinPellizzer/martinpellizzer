Orobanche parviloba

Orobanche parviloba, commonly known as the smallleaf broomrape, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has a root system composed of haustoria, which penetrate the roots of its host to extract water and nutrients. It typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in association with various grasses and forbs.
