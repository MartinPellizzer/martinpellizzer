Oxytropis ivdelensis var. rectirostra

Oxytropis ivdelensis var. rectirostra, commonly known as the Ivel's milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in steppes and rocky soils.

Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.
