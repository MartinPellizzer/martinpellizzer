Oxytropis valerii

Oxytropis valerii, commonly known as Valerian goat's rue, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Caucasus.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, leathery pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Oxytropis valerii is also known for its medicinal properties, having been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
