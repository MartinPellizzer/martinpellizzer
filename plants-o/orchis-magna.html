Orchis magna

Orchis magna, commonly known as the great purple orchid, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect stem that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces striking purple or violet flowers in late spring to early summer, often arranged in a loose spike. Its leaves are lance-shaped and alternate along the stem, with a rough, hairy texture.

Orchis magna is often found in meadows, woodlands, and damp grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils.
