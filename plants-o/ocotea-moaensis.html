Ocotea moaensis

Ocotea moaensis, commonly known as the Maule laurel, is a small evergreen tree native to central Chile.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, clustered flowers. It typically grows in humid, temperate climates and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Ocotea moaensis plays a role in local ecosystems and has been used traditionally for its aromatic properties.
