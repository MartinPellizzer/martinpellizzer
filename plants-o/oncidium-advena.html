Oncidium advena

Oncidium advena, commonly known as the yellow lady's slipper orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive pouch-like lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Oncidium advena is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
