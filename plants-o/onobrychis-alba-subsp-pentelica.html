Onobrychis alba subsp. pentelica

Onobrychis alba subsp.

pentelica, commonly known as the white vetch, is a perennial legume native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant has a robust root system that helps stabilize soil and improve fertility through nitrogen fixation. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Its ecological importance includes providing forage for livestock and supporting pollinator populations.
