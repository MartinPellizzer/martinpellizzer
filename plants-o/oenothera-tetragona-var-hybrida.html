Oenothera tetragona var. hybrida

Oenothera tetragona var. hybrida, commonly known as the four-angle evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive four-angled stems and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oval seeds. It thrives in sandy or loamy soils and prefers full sun exposure.

Oenothera tetragona var. hybrida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
