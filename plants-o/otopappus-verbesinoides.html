Otopappus verbesinoides

Otopappus verbesinoides, commonly known as the yellow star-thistle, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a taproot system and produces a basal rosette of blue-gray, woolly leaves. The plant is characterized by its yellow composite flower heads, which consist of numerous small florets arranged in a radiate pattern. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Due to its invasive nature, it is considered a noxious weed in many parts of the world.
