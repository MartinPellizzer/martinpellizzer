Oenothera × purpurans

Oenothera × purpurans, commonly known as the showy evening primrose, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the central United States.

It is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Oenothera × purpurans thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical features include hairy stems, lance-shaped leaves, and a long flowering period from summer to fall.
