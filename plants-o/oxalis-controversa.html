Oxalis controversa

Oxalis controversa, commonly known as the yellow wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. Its foliage contains oxalic acid, which can be toxic if ingested in large quantities.

Oxalis controversa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
