Ocotea tenuiflora

Ocotea tenuiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped, dark green leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers followed by spherical, dark blue berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its aromatic wood and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
