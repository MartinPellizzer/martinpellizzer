Odontoglossum quadridentatum

Odontoglossum quadridentatum, commonly known as the four-toothed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial herb with a pseudobulb and linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically in late winter to early spring. Its flowers have a distinctive structure with four prominent teeth on the lip, which is a key identifying feature.

Odontoglossum quadridentatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated as a hybrid in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
