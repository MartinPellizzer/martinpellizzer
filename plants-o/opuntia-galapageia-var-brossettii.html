Opuntia galapageia var. brossettii

Opuntia galapageia var. brossettii, commonly known as the Galápagos prickly pear, is a cactus native to the Galápagos Islands.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in spines and areoles, which are specialized structures for producing flowers and branches. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible. It thrives in arid and coastal environments, adapting to the unique climate of the Galápagos.

This variety is an important part of the local ecosystem, providing food and shelter for various native species.
