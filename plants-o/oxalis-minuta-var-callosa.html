Oxalis minuta var. callosa

Oxalis minuta var. callosa, commonly known as the miniature wood sorrel, is a small, herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features delicate, trifoliate leaves with a heart-shaped base and small, spurred flowers that are typically pink or white. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens. Its foliage is often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, textured appearance.

Oxalis minuta var. callosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
