Oxalis besseri

Oxalis besseri, commonly known as the Bessera wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features delicate, trifoliate leaves with a heart-shaped base and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its foliage is often covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture.

Oxalis besseri is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in partial shade.
