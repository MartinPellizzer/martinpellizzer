Origanum dayi

Origanum dayi, commonly known as the Chinese marjoram, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are used in traditional Chinese medicine for their aromatic and medicinal properties.

Origanum dayi is valued for its essential oils, which contain compounds like carvacrol and thymol.
