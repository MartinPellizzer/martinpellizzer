Orchis rubra var. expansa

Orchis rubra var. expansa, commonly known as the broad-leaved purple orchid, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and it produces a single, tall flowering stem.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is often found in conservation areas due to its ecological significance.
