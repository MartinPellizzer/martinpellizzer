Orthochilus ovalis

Orthochilus ovalis, commonly known as the oval orchid, is a species of terrestrial orchid native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces delicate, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically arranged in a loose cluster. Orthochilus ovalis thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forests or along stream banks.

Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, contributing to its ecological role in its native habitats.
