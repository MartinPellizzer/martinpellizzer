Oenothera chicaginensis var. parviflora

Oenothera chicaginensis var. parviflora, commonly known as the Chicago evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the Midwestern United States.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in moist prairies and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The species is known for its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife and its role in native plant restoration projects.
