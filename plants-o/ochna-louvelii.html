Ochna louvelii

Ochna louvelii, commonly known as the African starwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy, dark green foliage and its striking, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant produces small, fleshy, red or orange berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Ochna louvelii is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
