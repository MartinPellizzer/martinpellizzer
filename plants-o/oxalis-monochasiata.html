Oxalis monochasiata

Oxalis monochasiata, commonly known as the silver leaf oxalis, is a striking plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery white coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces delicate, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink or white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, Oxalis monochasiata is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
