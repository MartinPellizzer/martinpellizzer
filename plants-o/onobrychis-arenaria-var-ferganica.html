Onobrychis arenaria var. ferganica

Onobrychis arenaria var. ferganica, commonly known as the Fergana vetch, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in steppe and desert regions. It has a deep root system that allows it to survive in poor soils and withstand drought conditions.

This species is also valued for its role in soil stabilization and as forage for livestock.
