Oxalis refracta

Oxalis refracta, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Oxalidaceae.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its unique, translucent leaves that resemble ghostly shapes. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Oxalis refracta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage.
