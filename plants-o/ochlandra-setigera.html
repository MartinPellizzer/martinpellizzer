Ochlandra setigera

Ochlandra setigera, commonly known as the broad-leaved daisy, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features lanceolate leaves with a rough texture and produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Ochlandra setigera is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
