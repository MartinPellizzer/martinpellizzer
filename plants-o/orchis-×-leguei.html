Orchis × leguei

Orchis × leguei, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a natural cross between Orchis moria and Orchis mascula.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family, characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, spurred flowers. The flowers typically exhibit a range of colors, including purple, pink, and white, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination. It thrives in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows, and is often found in temperate regions of Europe.

This hybrid plant demonstrates interesting genetic variability and is of interest to both botanists and horticulturists.
