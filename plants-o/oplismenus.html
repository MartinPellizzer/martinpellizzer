Oplismenus

Oplismenus, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It consists of several species, most notably Oplismenus huldenii, which is native to the southeastern United States. The plant is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and basal rosette of leaves. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Botanically, it is notable for its unique floral structure and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
