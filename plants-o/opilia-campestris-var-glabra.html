Opilia campestris var. glabra

Opilia campestris var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth opilia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and marshes. The plant features slender, upright stems covered with smooth, lance-shaped leaves. Its small, white to pale pink flowers are clustered in loose inflorescences, often blooming in late spring to early summer.

The species is characterized by its lack of hairy surfaces, which is reflected in its varietal name "glabra.".
