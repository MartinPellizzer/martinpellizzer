Odontorchis lactea

Odontorchis lactea, commonly known as the milk orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a slender, upright stem with a single, lance-shaped leaf that is often hairy. The plant produces a spike of small, white to pale yellow flowers with a distinctive, milk-like appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically in forest understories.

Odontorchis lactea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
