Orchis roxburghii

Orchis roxburghii, commonly known as the Indian orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a perennial herb with a tall, erect stem that bears showy, spurred flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of violet or purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The orchid has a complex root system and is known for its ability to form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi.
