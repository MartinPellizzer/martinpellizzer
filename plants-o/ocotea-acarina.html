Ocotea acarina

Ocotea acarina, commonly known as the sweet-smelling ocotea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are drupes, typically containing a single seed, and are an important food source for various wildlife.

Ocotea acarina is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
