Oenothera plicatula

Oenothera plicatula, commonly known as the wrinkled evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, wrinkled, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oily seeds. Oenothera plicatula thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairies and disturbed areas.

It is also known for its medicinal properties due to the high content of essential fatty acids in its seeds.
