Oxytropis helenae

Oxytropis helenae, commonly known as Helen's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-bearing fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are compound, and it produces small, usually purple or yellow flowers.

Oxytropis helenae is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
