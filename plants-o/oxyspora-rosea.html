Oxyspora rosea

Oxyspora rosea, commonly known as the rose spurge, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically green with a slight bluish tint. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in cyathia, a unique floral structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Oxyspora rosea is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and is often found growing in rocky soils or along dry streambeds.

It is a hardy plant that can tolerate drought conditions, making it well adapted to its native environment.
