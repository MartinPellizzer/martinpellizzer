Origanum hirtum var. neglectum

Origanum hirtum var. neglectum, commonly known as woolly oregano, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders. Its aromatic leaves are often used in cooking, similar to other members of the mint family.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
