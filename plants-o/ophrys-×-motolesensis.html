Ophrys × motolesensis

Ophrys × motolesensis, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a rare and fascinating plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera, two species known for their intricate mimicry of female insects. The plant is characterized by its striking, bee-like flowers that play a crucial role in its pollination strategy. It thrives in specific habitats, such as calcareous grasslands, and is often found in southern Europe.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in some regions.
