Odontoglossum gloriosum

Odontoglossum gloriosum, commonly known as the gloriosa orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically feature vibrant yellow and orange hues with intricate patterns. The plant has a pseudobulb structure, which stores water and nutrients, aiding its survival in high-altitude environments. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often arranged in a rosette pattern around the pseudobulb.

Odontoglossum gloriosum is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its role in botanical research due to its unique floral morphology.
