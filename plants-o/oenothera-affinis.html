Oenothera affinis

Oenothera affinis, commonly known as the plains evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy yellow or orange flowers that open in the evening, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in prairie and steppe habitats. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Oenothera affinis is a member of the Onagraceae family and is known for its ecological importance to pollinators like moths and beetles.
