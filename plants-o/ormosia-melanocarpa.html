Ormosia melanocarpa

Ormosia melanocarpa, commonly known as blackwood or black ebony, is a member of the legume family Fabaceae.

It is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its dark, dense wood and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by pod-like fruits containing flat, oval seeds.

Ormosia melanocarpa is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in furniture and carving.
