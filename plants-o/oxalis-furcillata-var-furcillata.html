Oxalis furcillata var. furcillata

Oxalis furcillata var. furcillata, commonly known as the forked wood sorrel, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped lobes and produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its stems are often hairy, and it has a fibrous root system.

Oxalis furcillata var. furcillata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partially shaded conditions.
