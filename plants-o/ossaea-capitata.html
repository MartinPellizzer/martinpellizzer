Ossaea capitata

Ossaea capitata, commonly known as the mountain snowbell, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a compact, shrub-like growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to the harsh conditions of alpine habitats.

Ossaea capitata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural environment.
