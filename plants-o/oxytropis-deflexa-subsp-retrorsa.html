Oxytropis deflexa subsp. retrorsa

Oxytropis deflexa subsp.

retrorsa, commonly known as the bentleaf locoweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. It has a deep taproot system, allowing it to survive in arid conditions.

The subspecies retrorsa is distinguished by its reflexed leaflets and smaller, more clustered flowers compared to other subspecies.
