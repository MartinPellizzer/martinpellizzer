Oxalis rutenbergii

Oxalis rutenbergii, commonly known as the Rutenberg's wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces distinctive seed pods that split open to release its seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun exposure.

Oxalis rutenbergii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a garden setting.
