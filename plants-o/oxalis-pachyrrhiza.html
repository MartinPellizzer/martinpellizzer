Oxalis pachyrrhiza

Oxalis pachyrrhiza, commonly known as the thick-stemmed wood sorrel, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features a distinctive rosette of heart-shaped leaves with reddish veins, and produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers. The plant has thick, fleshy stems and is adapted to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its botanical characteristics include a shallow root system and the ability to form dense colonies.

Oxalis pachyrrhiza is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
