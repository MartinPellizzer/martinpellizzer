Ocimastrum × intermedium

Ocimastrum × intermedium, commonly known as the hybrid mint or wild mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a strong, mint-like scent. Its flowers are small, tubular, and clustered in whorls, often blooming in shades of purple or pink.

Ocimastrum × intermedium is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
