Ottelia alismoides var. oryzetorum

Ottelia alismoides var. oryzetorum, commonly known as water velvet, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in shallow water, such as marshes and ponds, and has long, slender stems that often float on the water surface. The plant produces small, white, star-shaped flowers that are clustered in umbels, and its leaves are narrow and ribbon-like. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in wetland environments and is often used in ornamental water gardens.

The species is also notable for its role in providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates and fish.
