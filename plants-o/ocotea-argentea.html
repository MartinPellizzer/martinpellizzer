Ocotea argentea

Ocotea argentea, commonly known as silver ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its lanceolate, leathery leaves that are often covered in a silvery or whitish bloom. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by elliptical, dark blue-black berries. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and potentially medicinal properties.

Ocotea argentea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
