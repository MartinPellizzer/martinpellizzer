Orchis ochrantha

Orchis ochrantha, commonly known as the purple orchid, is a species of orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces a single, large, spurred orchid flower that is often adorned with intricate patterns. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is typically found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

Orchis ochrantha is a perennial herb that plays a role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecological balance.
