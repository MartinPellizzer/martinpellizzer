Osmanthus daibuensis

Osmanthus daibuensis, commonly known as the Japanese osmanthus, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that turn from green to red as they mature. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a smooth texture with a slightly serrated edge.

Osmanthus daibuensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional Japanese gardens.
