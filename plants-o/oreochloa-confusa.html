Oreochloa confusa

Oreochloa confusa, commonly known as the confused grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is often found in disturbed areas and can be an indicator of soil conditions in its native range.

Botanically, it is characterized by its unique floral structure and ability to thrive in harsh, low-nutrient environments.
