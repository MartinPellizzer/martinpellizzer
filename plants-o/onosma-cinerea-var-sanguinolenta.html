Onosma cinerea var. sanguinolenta

Onosma cinerea var. sanguinolenta, commonly known as the crimson everlasting or red everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, showy pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a compact, mat-forming growth habit with silver-gray foliage that becomes more pronounced during dry periods. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting flowers, which remain intact even when dried.
