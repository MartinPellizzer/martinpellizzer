Orthosiphon aristatus

Orthosiphon aristatus, commonly known as Java tea or kidney tea, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. Its aerial parts are used in traditional medicine for their diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Orthosiphon aristatus thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its medicinal value.
