Ouratea poggei

Ouratea poggei, commonly known as the golden mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, fragrant flowers. The plant relies on host trees for water and nutrients, while it provides nectar to attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its unique ecological role makes it an important component of the forest ecosystem.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
