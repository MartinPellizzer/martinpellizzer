Ocotea sintenisii

Ocotea sintenisii, commonly known as the Sintenisi ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Indonesia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its bark and leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and therapeutic properties.

The tree typically grows to a height of 10-20 meters and thrives in humid, shaded environments.
