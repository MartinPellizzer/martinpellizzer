Ormosia acuminata

Ormosia acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-pointed ormosia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant is known for its hard, heavy wood, which is often used in traditional carpentry and as a source of timber. Ormosia acuminata thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
