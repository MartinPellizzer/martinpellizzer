Oxalis frutescens subsp. frutescens

Oxalis frutescens subsp.

frutescens, commonly known as the wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It belongs to the family Oxalidaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate foliage and vibrant blooms.
