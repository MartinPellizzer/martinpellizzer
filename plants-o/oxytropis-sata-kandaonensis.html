Oxytropis sata-kandaonensis

Oxytropis sata-kandaonensis, commonly known as the Khandwa oxytropis, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow or pale green flowers that bloom in spring, contributing to its ornamental value. Native to the mountainous regions of central India, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
