Oxymeris pennipilis

Oxymeris pennipilis, commonly known as the African mahogany, is a tropical tree native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its straight, dark brown heartwood that is highly valued for its durability and fine grain. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Oxymeris pennipilis is often cultivated for its timber and is also used in traditional medicine.
