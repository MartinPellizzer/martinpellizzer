Oryzopsis bicolor var. major

Oryzopsis bicolor var. major, commonly known as the purple false rice, is a grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking two-toned inflorescence, with purple and green hues that distinguish it from other grasses. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique coloration and ecological adaptations.
