Ocotea orientalis

Ocotea orientalis, commonly known as the oriental ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and aromatic leaves that emit a pleasant scent when crushed. Its bark is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall and is valued for its timber and essential oils.
