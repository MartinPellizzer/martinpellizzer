Oenothera utahensis

Oenothera utahensis, commonly known as Utah evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has hairy, linear leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun, making it a hardy species in desert environments.

Oenothera utahensis is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its ornamental appeal in xeriscaping.
