Onobrychis sativa var. calvescens

Onobrychis sativa var. calvescens, commonly known as crimson clover, is a leguminous plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it a valuable forage crop. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in agricultural settings for soil enrichment.

Its growth habit includes a low, spreading stem with a mat-forming structure.
