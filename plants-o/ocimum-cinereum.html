Ocimum cinereum

Ocimum cinereum, commonly known as silver basil, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, fuzzy leaves and small, pale blue or white flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and lavender. Its leaves are often used in culinary and medicinal applications due to their distinctive aroma and potential health benefits.

Ocimum cinereum thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
