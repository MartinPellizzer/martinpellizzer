Ossaea disparilis

Ossaea disparilis, commonly known as the slender sand couch, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to withstand harsh, dry conditions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spikelets, typical of the Poaceae family. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to anchor firmly in sandy soils and access water from deeper layers.

Ossaea disparilis plays a vital role in stabilizing dunes and preventing soil erosion in its native habitat.
