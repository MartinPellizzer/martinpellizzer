Ophrys fusca var. rubescens

Ophrys fusca var. rubescens, commonly known as the red-faced bee orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to specific regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its unique floral morphology, featuring a vividly colored lip that mimics the appearance of a female bee, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and heathlands, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing a showy display that attracts male bees through mimicry.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, this variant is considered vulnerable, highlighting the need for conservation efforts to protect its dwindling populations.
