Oberonia anamalayana

Oberonia anamalayana, commonly known as the Malagasy orchid, is a species of flowering plant in the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its unique, intricate floral structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from green to yellow.

Due to habitat loss, Oberonia anamalayana is considered endangered and is of significant interest to botanists and conservationists.
