Ocotea ferruginea

Ocotea ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves with a glossy upper surface. It produces small, white flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible for some bird species.

The tree is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
