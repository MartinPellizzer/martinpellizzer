Olearia canescens subsp. discolor

Olearia canescens subsp.

discolor, commonly known as the silver-haired daisy, is a shrub native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and showy, daisy-like white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in coastal and subalpine regions, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, which give it a distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
