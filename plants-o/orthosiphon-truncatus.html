Orthosiphon truncatus

Orthosiphon truncatus, commonly known as Java tea or Malabar tea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Orthosiphon truncatus thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
