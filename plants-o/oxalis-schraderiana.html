Oxalis schraderiana

Oxalis schraderiana, commonly known as the Schrader's wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive crimson veins, resembling those of true sorrel species. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink or white, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Oxalis schraderiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and shaded landscapes.
