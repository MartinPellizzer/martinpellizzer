Orania appendiculata

Orania appendiculata, commonly known as the appendiculate orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces intricate, fragrant blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Native to specific regions in South America, it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
