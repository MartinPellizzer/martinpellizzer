Ocotea finium

Ocotea finium, commonly known as the Brazilian cinnamon tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and fragrant bark. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue berries. Its essential oils are used in perfumery and traditional medicine.

Ocotea finium thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ecological and commercial significance.
