Olax staudtii

Olax staudtii, commonly known as the Staudt's olax, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Olacaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with five petals and prominent stamens, and it produces small, dry fruits known as capsules.

Olax staudtii is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.
