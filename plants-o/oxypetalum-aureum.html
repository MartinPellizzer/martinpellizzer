Oxypetalum aureum

Oxypetalum aureum, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive golden hue. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate to subtropical climates. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

Oxypetalum aureum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
