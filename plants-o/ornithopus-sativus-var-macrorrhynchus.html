Ornithopus sativus var. macrorrhynchus

Ornithopus sativus var. macrorrhynchus, commonly known as the giant scurvygrass, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Europe and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has feathery, pinnately divided leaves and thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. It is often found in maritime environments and is adapted to salt spray and strong winds.

Ornithopus sativus var. macrorrhynchus is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
