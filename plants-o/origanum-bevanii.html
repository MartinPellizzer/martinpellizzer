Origanum bevanii

Origanum bevanii, commonly known as Bevan's oregano, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, pale blue or pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It is closely related to other members of the Lamiaceae family, sharing similar aromatic properties.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and herbal gardens.
