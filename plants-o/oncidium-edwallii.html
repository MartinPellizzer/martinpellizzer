Oncidium edwallii

Oncidium edwallii, commonly known as the Yellow Butterfly Orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow with green or red markings, resembling butterflies. The plant has pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, supporting its growth in various environmental conditions. Its roots are epiphytic, allowing it to grow on other plants without being parasitic.

Oncidium edwallii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
