Oenothera serrulata var. drummondii

Oenothera serrulata var. drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that open in the evening and close by morning. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and serrated, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Drummond's evening primrose is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.
