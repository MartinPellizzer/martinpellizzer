Onobrychis alba subsp. mairei

Onobrychis alba subsp.

mairei, commonly known as white vetch, is a perennial legume native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, white to pale yellow pea-like flowers.

The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it a valuable plant for soil enrichment and forage.
