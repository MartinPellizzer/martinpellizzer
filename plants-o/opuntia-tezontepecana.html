Opuntia tezontepecana

Opuntia tezontepecana, commonly known as the Tezontepec cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its flattened, fleshy stems that are segmented and covered in spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom during the summer months. Its fruit, known as a tuna, is edible and has a tangy flavor.

Opuntia tezontepecana is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
