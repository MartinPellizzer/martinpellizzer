Octella angustifolius

Octella angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf octella, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, which bloom during the wet season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or savanna habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
