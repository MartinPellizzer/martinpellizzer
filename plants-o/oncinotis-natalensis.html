Oncinotis natalensis

Oncinotis natalensis, commonly known as Natal grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland habitats.

Oncinotis natalensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
