Olea buchananii

Olea buchananii, commonly known as the African olive, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically eaten by birds and other animals. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Olea buchananii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
