Oxyspora sagittata

Oxyspora sagittata, commonly known as the arrowhead plant, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves, which are typically bright green and have a smooth texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often found near water sources such as rivers and wetlands.

Oxyspora sagittata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
