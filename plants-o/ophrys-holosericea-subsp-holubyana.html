Ophrys holosericea subsp. holubyana

Ophrys holosericea subsp.

holubyana, commonly known as the Holubyan bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the Czech Republic. It is characterized by its intricate floral morphology, featuring a labellum that mimics the appearance of a female bee, a key adaptation for attracting male bees for pollination.

The plant typically grows in calcareous grasslands and rocky habitats, where it thrives in nutrient-poor soils. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brownish in color, with a distinctive, hairy texture that enhances its mimicry.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
