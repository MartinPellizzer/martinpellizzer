Ossaea euphorbioides

Ossaea euphorbioides, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is a small, mat-forming perennial that thrives in sandy and rocky soils, often found in desert environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are succulent, adapted to store water, and its stems are covered in fine, hair-like structures that help reduce water loss.

Ossaea euphorbioides plays a role in stabilizing sand dunes and is of interest for its potential in ecological restoration projects.
