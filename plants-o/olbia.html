Olbia

Olbia, commonly known as the "wild olive," is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like the olive and jasmine. The plant features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Olbia is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in ancient cultures.
