Operculina discoidesperma

Operculina discoidesperma, commonly known as the African butterbean, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several elliptical, smooth-seeded pods.

The plant is adapted to arid conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
