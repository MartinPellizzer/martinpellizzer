Ononis penduliflora

Ononis penduliflora, commonly known as the hanging flax or pendulous flax, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that hang in clusters, giving it its characteristic appearance. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Ononis penduliflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, cascading blooms.
