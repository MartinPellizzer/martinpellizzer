Ornithogalum transbaicalense

Ornithogalum transbaicalense, commonly known as the Transbaikal lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cold regions of Siberia and Mongolia.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its striking white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has strap-shaped, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers on slender stalks. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate to subarctic climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
