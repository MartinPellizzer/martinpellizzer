Oberonia acaulis

Oberonia acaulis, commonly known as the naked-stem orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, leafless stems that rise directly from the ground. The plant produces a single, showy flower with a distinctive greenish-yellow color and a prominent lip. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
