Ophrys × castellerana

Ophrys × castellerana, commonly known as the Castellana bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its intricate floral structure, which closely resembles the face of a bee, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its flowers are usually a mix of yellow and brown, with a distinctive beard-like structure on the lip.

Ophrys × castellerana is a short-lived perennial, often found in open habitats such as grasslands and forest edges.
