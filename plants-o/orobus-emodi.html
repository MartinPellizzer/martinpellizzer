Orobus emodi

Orobus emodi, commonly known as the Himalayan orchid, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant blooms that typically appear in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to the nutrient-poor soils of its mountainous habitat.

Orobus emodi plays a significant role in the ecological balance of its native region, supporting various pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of the area.
