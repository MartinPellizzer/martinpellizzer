Ophiopogon filipes

Ophiopogon filipes, commonly known as the threadleaf lily or Chinese lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky texture. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It is commonly found in woodland areas and thrives in partial shade.

Ophiopogon filipes is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
