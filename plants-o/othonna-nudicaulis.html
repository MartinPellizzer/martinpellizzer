Othonna nudicaulis

Othonna nudicaulis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved othonna, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing form and fleshy, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Othonna nudicaulis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
