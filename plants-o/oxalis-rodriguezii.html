Oxalis rodriguezii

Oxalis rodriguezii, commonly known as the Rodriguez's wood sorrel, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has a trailing growth habit. Its foliage contains oxalic acid, which can be toxic if ingested in large quantities.

Oxalis rodriguezii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive appearance and ease of care.
