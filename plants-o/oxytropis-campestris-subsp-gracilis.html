Oxytropis campestris subsp. gracilis

Oxytropis campestris subsp.

gracilis, commonly known as slender field lupine, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant produces erect, slender racemes of pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to purple. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America, particularly in the western United States.

This subspecies is adapted to sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
