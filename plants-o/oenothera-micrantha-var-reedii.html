Oenothera micrantha var. reedii

Oenothera micrantha var. reedii, commonly known as the Reed's evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and showy yellow flowers that open in the evening. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oval seeds. It prefers dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and native plant gardening.
