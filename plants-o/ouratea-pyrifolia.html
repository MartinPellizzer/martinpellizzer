Ouratea pyrifolia

Ouratea pyrifolia, commonly known as the flame tree or torch tree, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and striking, fiery red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. Its botanical features include pinnate leaves and showy inflorescences that attract pollinators such as birds and insects.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
