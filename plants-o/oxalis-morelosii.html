Oxalis morelosii

Oxalis morelosii, commonly known as the Mexican wood sorrel, is a species of flowering plant in the family Oxalidaceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas. It has a compact growth habit and produces edible leaves, though they are not commonly cultivated for food.

Oxalis morelosii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes grown in gardens for its delicate appearance.
