Odostemon quinquefolius

Odostemon quinquefolius, commonly known as wild ginseng, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, typically with five leaflets, and produces small, greenish-white flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in shaded, moist woodland environments and is often found in rich, loamy soils. Its roots are valued for their medicinal properties and are used in traditional herbal remedies.

Odostemon quinquefolius is also an important species for ecological studies due to its role in forest ecosystems.
