Oxalis hirta var. secunda

Oxalis hirta var. secunda, commonly known as the hairy wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its botanical characteristics include a short rhizome and a mat-forming growth habit.

Oxalis hirta var. secunda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
