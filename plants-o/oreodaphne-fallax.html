Oreodaphne fallax

Oreodaphne fallax, commonly known as the Malabar ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and rough, fissured bark that exudes a strong, resinous scent. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by elliptical, leathery fruits containing a single seed. It is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.

Oreodaphne fallax plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
