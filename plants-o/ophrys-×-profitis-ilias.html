Ophrys × profitis-ilias

Ophrys × profitis-ilias, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant that belongs to the Orchidaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Ophrys apifera (the bee orchid) and Ophrys insectifera (the wasp orchid), resulting in a unique combination of traits from both parents. The plant is characterized by its intricate, bee-like flowers that have evolved to mimic the appearance and scent of female bees, facilitating its specialized pollination strategy. Its flowers typically feature a dark brown or black lip with yellow markings, resembling a bee's abdomen.

This species is often found in calcareous soils across Europe and is celebrated for its remarkable evolutionary adaptation and aesthetic appeal.
