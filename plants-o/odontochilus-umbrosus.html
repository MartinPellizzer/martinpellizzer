Odontochilus umbrosus

Odontochilus umbrosus, commonly known as the dark orchid, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple or violet petals and sepals, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories where it receives indirect light. Its flowers are small but fragrant, and it produces a unique type of orchid root system adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

Odontochilus umbrosus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in specialized horticultural settings.
