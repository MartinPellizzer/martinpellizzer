Orobus sylvestris

Orobus sylvestris, commonly known as the woodland bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in woodland habitats, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Orobus sylvestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
