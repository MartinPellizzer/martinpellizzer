Orchis incarnata f. elatior

Orchis incarnata f. elatior, commonly known as the showy lady's tresses, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem that bears a dense cluster of pale purple to white flowers arranged in a loose spiral. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and wet meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

This orchid is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate and attractive blooms.
