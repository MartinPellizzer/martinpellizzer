Ocotea indecora

Ocotea indecora, commonly known as the fragrant ocotea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are typically clustered in axillary inflorescences, and it produces small, dark blue berries.

The tree is valued for its aromatic wood and is often found in moist, shaded forest habitats.
