Obione lindleyi

Obione lindleyi, commonly known as the Lindley's orchid, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its flowers are often fragrant and come in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and pink.

Obione lindleyi is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
