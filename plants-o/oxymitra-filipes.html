Oxymitra filipes

Oxymitra filipes, commonly known as the slender sandalwood, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and aromatic wood. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understoreys. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a leathery texture.

The species is valued for its essential oils and is used in traditional medicine and perfumery.
