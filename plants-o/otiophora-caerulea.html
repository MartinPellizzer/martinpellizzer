Otiophora caerulea

Otiophora caerulea, commonly known as the blue-green algae or Aulosira, is a type of cyanobacteria that forms colonies in aquatic environments.

It is characterized by its filamentous structure and the presence of both heterocysts and akinetes, which are specialized cells involved in nitrogen fixation. The plant exhibits a bluish-green color due to the presence of chlorophyll and phycobilins, which aid in photosynthesis. It thrives in nutrient-rich waters and is often found in lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams.

Otiophora caerulea plays a significant role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to primary production and nutrient cycling.
