Osteospermum niveum

Osteospermum niveum, commonly known as the white osteospermum, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, daisy-like blooms with white petals and a central yellow disk, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Osteospermum niveum is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant flowers and drought tolerance.
