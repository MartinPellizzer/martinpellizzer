Oxalis arequipensis

Oxalis arequipensis, commonly known as the Arequipa wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. It belongs to the family Oxalidaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in cooler, temperate climates.

Oxalis arequipensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
