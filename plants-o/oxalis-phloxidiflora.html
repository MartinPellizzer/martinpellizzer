Oxalis phloxidiflora

Oxalis phloxidiflora, commonly known as the pink wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and thrives in partial shade with well-drained soil. It produces edible tubers that are sometimes used in traditional cuisine.

Oxalis phloxidiflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
