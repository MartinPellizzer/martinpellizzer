Ocotea salicifolia

Ocotea salicifolia, commonly known as the silky oak or white ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurel and avocado trees, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, typically appearing in clusters, and it produces dark, oval berries.

Ocotea salicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
