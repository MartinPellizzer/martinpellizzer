Oxalis bulbocastanum subsp. hirta

Oxalis bulbocastanum subsp.

hirta, commonly known as the hairy oxalis, is a perennial herb native to South Africa. It features trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces, giving it its subspecies name.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
