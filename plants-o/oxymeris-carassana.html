Oxymeris carassana

Oxymeris carassana, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Olacaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lanceolate leaves with a prominent midrib. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are borne in clusters, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.

It is often found in dry, open woodlands and is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
