Orthosiphon schinzianus

Orthosiphon schinzianus, commonly known as Java tea or Malabar tealeaf, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsules containing numerous small seeds. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.
