Osmunda virginiana

Osmunda virginiana, commonly known as the royal fern, is a species of fern native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender fronds that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant has a robust rhizome system that allows it to thrive in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Osmunda virginiana plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
