Oryza sativa var. janthoceros

Oryza sativa var. janthoceros, commonly known as the slender ricegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical or subtropical environments, often near water bodies. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a culm that is often branched.

This variety is of interest in botanical studies due to its genetic diversity and potential for ecological research.
