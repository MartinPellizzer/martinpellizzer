Oxalis ramulosa

Oxalis ramulosa, commonly known as the pink wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is a perennial herb with trifoliate leaves that resemble those of clover, often with a distinctive pink or red coloration. The plant produces small, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Oxalis ramulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and blooms.
