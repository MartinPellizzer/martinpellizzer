Ophioglossum felixii

Ophioglossum felixii, commonly known as the golden harts, is a rare and ancient fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, strap-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces sporangia in specialized structures called sporangium clusters, which are arranged in a distinctive pattern along the leaf margins. It thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Ophioglossum felixii is considered a vulnerable species.
