Orchis × cerii

Orchis × cerii, commonly known as the purple tawny orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Orchis morio and Orchis cernua.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, typically found in grassy woodlands and meadows. The plant produces tall flowering stems that bear clusters of violet to purple flowers, which are often tinged with brown or red hues. Its roots system is fibrous, and it thrives in well-drained, nutrient-rich soils.

Orchis × cerii is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
