Odontoglossum majale

Odontoglossum majale, commonly known as the Ecuadorian orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulbous structure that stores water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with intricate patterns. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, typically arranged in a rosette pattern around the pseudobulb.

Odontoglossum majale thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
