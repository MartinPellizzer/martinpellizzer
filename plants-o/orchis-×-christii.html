Orchis × christii

Orchis × christii, commonly known as the Christ's orchid, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Orchis moria and Orchis anthropophora.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers with a distinctive yellowish-green throat. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodlands and meadows across Europe. It has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
