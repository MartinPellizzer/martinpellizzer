Osmunda trifrons

Osmunda trifrons, commonly known as the common water fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Osmundaceae.

It is characterized by its large, floating fronds that are typically divided into three segments, giving it its scientific name. The plant thrives in freshwater environments, such as ponds and slow-moving streams, where it can anchor itself to substrates. Its rhizomes are thick and creeping, allowing it to spread vegetatively.

Osmunda trifrons plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
