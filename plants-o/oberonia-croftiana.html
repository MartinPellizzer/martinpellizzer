Oberonia croftiana

Oberonia croftiana, commonly known as the red orchid, is a species of flowering plant in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its striking, bright red flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a subtle fragrance. Its leaves are typically dark green and elongated, providing a contrasting backdrop to its vibrant blooms.

Oberonia croftiana is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its cultural significance in some indigenous communities.
