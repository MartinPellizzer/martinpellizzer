Orbea rugosa

Orbea rugosa, commonly known as the rough orbea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, woody stems and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its flowers, which are usually pink or red, bloom in spring and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Orbea rugosa is a popular choice for rock gardens due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.
