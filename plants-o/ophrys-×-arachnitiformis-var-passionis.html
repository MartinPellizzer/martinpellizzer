Ophrys × arachnitiformis var. passionis

Ophrys × arachnitiformis var. passionis, commonly known as the Passion Orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its intricate, bee-mimicking flowers that play a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its flowers exhibit a range of colors, from deep purples to pale yellows, and feature specialized structures that enhance its mimicry of female bees.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ecological significance in supporting pollinator interactions.
