Ononis natrix subvar. reesei

Ononis natrix subvar. reesei, commonly known as the narrowleaf bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, ascending stems covered in fine, soft hairs, and its leaves are narrow, linear, and often slightly hairy. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Caryophyllaceae family.
