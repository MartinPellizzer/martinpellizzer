Oxygonum dregeanum var. pilosum

Oxygonum dregeanum var. pilosum, commonly known as the hairy yellow daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow ray flowers and dense, hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders. Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a desirable addition to both garden and landscape designs.

The hairy texture of its leaves provides a unique visual appeal and helps to distinguish it from other similar species.
