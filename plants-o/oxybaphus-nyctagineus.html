Oxybaphus nyctagineus

Oxybaphus nyctagineus, commonly known as the nightshade daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and is often found in sandy or rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that contain seeds.

Oxybaphus nyctagineus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
