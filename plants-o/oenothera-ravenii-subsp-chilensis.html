Oenothera ravenii subsp. chilensis

Oenothera ravenii subsp.

chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean evening primrose, is a species native to South America, particularly Chile. It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the evening.

The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to dry conditions. Its leaves are alternate and lanceolate, often covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
