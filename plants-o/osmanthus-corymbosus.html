Osmanthus corymbosus

Osmanthus corymbosus, commonly known as sweet osmanthus, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, yellowish flowers that bloom in dense clusters, releasing a sweet, aromatic scent. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

Osmanthus corymbosus is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine and is used in culinary applications, particularly in teas and confections.
