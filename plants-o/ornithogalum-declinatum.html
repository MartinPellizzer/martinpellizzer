Ornithogalum declinatum

Ornithogalum declinatum, commonly known as the drooping star-of-Bethlehem, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and is often found in moist, shaded environments. It is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

However, it can be toxic to livestock and humans if ingested.
