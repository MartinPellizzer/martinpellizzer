Oxytropis ramosissima

Oxytropis ramosissima, commonly known as the stiff filaree, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its dense, branching habit and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with numerous leaflets and produces legume-like pods that contain seeds. It is adapted to harsh environments, tolerating drought and poor soil conditions.

Oxytropis ramosissima plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
