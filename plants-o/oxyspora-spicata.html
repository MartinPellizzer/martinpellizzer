Oxyspora spicata

Oxyspora spicata, commonly known as the spiky oxyspora, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in humid climates. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered with fine hairs.

Oxyspora spicata is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
