Ophiorrhiza obcuneata

Ophiorrhiza obcuneata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mangrove, is a tropical shrub belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually less than 2 cm in length. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round berries. It thrives in coastal areas, often found in mangrove ecosystems where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.

This species is adapted to saline and waterlogged conditions, making it an important component of coastal vegetation.
