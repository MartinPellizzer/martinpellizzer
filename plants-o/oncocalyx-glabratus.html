Oncocalyx glabratus

Oncocalyx glabratus, commonly known as the red hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically red or pink in color, with five petals and a distinctive, trumpet-shaped corolla. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, smooth seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant blooms.

Oncocalyx glabratus is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
