Ocotea rusbyana

Ocotea rusbyana, commonly known as the white cedar, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green foliage and smooth, pale bark. The plant produces small, white flowers that are typically followed by elliptical, dark blue berries. Ocotea rusbyana is often found in humid lowland forests and is valued for its durable wood.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the ecosystems it inhabits.
