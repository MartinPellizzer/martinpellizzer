Osbeckia quaterna

Osbeckia quaterna, commonly known as the four-leafed buckweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial with small, fuzzy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and its ability to thrive in dry, rocky environments. Osbeckia quaterna is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of arid ecosystem flora.
