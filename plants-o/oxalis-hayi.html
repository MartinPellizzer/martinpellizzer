Oxalis hayi

Oxalis hayi, commonly known as the yellow wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of five-petaled yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its foliage contains oxalic acid, which can be toxic if ingested in large quantities.

Oxalis hayi is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its delicate appearance and early spring bloom.
