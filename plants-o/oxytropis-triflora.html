Oxytropis triflora

Oxytropis triflora, commonly known as three-flowered locoweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

Oxytropis triflora is also noted for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock.
