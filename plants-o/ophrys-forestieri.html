Ophrys forestieri

Ophrys forestieri, commonly known as the forest orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which closely resembles the shape and color of a female bee, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of insects.

Ophrys forestieri is known for its delicate, ephemeral blooms that appear in early spring.
