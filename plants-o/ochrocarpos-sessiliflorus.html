Ochrocarpos sessiliflorus

Ochrocarpos sessiliflorus, commonly known as the sessile-flowered ochrocarpus, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellowish, and clustered in dense, spherical heads.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
