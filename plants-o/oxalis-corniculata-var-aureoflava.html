Oxalis corniculata var. aureoflava

Oxalis corniculata var. aureoflava, commonly known as the golden-flowered wood sorrel, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are heart-shaped and often have a reddish or purple hue. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

Oxalis corniculata var. aureoflava is a herbaceous perennial that is popular in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and flowers.
