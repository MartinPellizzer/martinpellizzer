Onoseris montevidense

Onoseris montevidense, commonly known as the Montevideo sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Uruguay and parts of Argentina.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and disturbed soils, showing a preference for temperate climates. Its yellow-orange flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential ecological roles in native habitats.
