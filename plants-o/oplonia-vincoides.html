Oplonia vincoides

Oplonia vincoides, commonly known as the creeping marshweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland environments in Europe and Asia.

It features a spreading growth habit with slender, fleshy stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to form dense mats. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and slightly hairy, adapted to thrive in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

Oplonia vincoides plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
