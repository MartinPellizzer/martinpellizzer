Opuntia poeppigii

Opuntia poeppigii, commonly known as the Mexican prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its flat, spine-tipped pads and vibrant yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its fleshy stems and having a shallow root system. Its fruit, known as a tunas, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

Opuntia poeppigii plays an important role in its ecosystem, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
