Ocimum subserratum

Ocimum subserratum, commonly known as the serrated basil, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and rosemary. The plant is characterized by its dark green, slightly serrated leaves and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. It is often cultivated for its culinary and medicinal uses, particularly in traditional medicine for its purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

Ocimum subserratum thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
