Onobrychis sororia

Onobrychis sororia, commonly known as sweet vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its root system has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, which enhances soil fertility.

Sweet vetch is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
