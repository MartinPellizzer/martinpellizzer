Ophrys sphegodes subsp. moesziana

Ophrys sphegodes subsp.

moesziana, commonly known as the bee orchid, is a specialized orchid species native to Europe. It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination.

The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is found in grasslands and heathlands. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with dark markings, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is part of the Ophrys genus, known for its intricate mimicry and ecological significance in pollination systems.
