Oryzopsis munroi

Oryzopsis munroi, commonly known as the Hawaiian silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers.

Oryzopsis munroi plays an important ecological role in Hawaiian ecosystems, providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
