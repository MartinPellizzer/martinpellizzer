Opuntia emoryi

Opuntia emoryi, commonly known as Emory's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems that are covered in spines and glochids, which are small, barbed bristles. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the summer months, followed by fruit that is typically red or orange in color. Opuntia emoryi is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

It plays an important role in desert ecosystems, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
