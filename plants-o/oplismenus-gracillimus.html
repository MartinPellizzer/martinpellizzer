Oplismenus gracillimus

Oplismenus gracillimus, commonly known as the slender wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial with trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in moist, acidic soils. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Oplismenus gracillimus is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
