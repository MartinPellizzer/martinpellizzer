Olea cunninghamii

Olea cunninghamii, commonly known as the Cunningham's olive, is a small evergreen tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, edible berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal regions.

Olea cunninghamii is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
