Onobrychis echinata

Onobrychis echinata, commonly known as the thorny vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, hairy pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Onobrychis echinata is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment and erosion control.
