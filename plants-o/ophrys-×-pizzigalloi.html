Ophrys × pizzigalloi

Ophrys × pizzigalloi, commonly known as the Pizzigallo orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the genus Ophrys, which is characterized by its intricate mimicry of female insects to attract male pollinators. The plant produces showy, bee-mimicking flowers with a distinctive pouch-like structure that facilitates pollination. It typically grows in rocky or forested areas and prefers well-drained, calcareous soils.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to both botanists and conservationists.
