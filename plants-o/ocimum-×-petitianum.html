Ocimum × petitianum

Ocimum × petitianum, commonly known as the hybrid basil, is a cross between Ocimum basilicum (common basil) and Ocimum minimum (Greek basil).

It is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a strong aromatic scent. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually violet or purple in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, similar to its parent species.
