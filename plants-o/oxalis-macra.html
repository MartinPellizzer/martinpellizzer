Oxalis macra

Oxalis macra, commonly known as the purple shamrock, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive purple veins, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Oxalis macra is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of climates.
