Oenothera paradoxa

Oenothera paradoxa, commonly known as the paradox flower, is a species of flowering plant in the evening primrose family.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces showy yellow or orange flowers that open in the evening, hence its common name. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Oenothera paradoxa is known for its ability to attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths and bats.
