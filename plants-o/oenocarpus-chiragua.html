Oenocarpus chiragua

Oenocarpus chiragua, commonly known as the "sugar apple" or "chirigua," is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the palm family, Arecaceae, and is characterized by its large, clustered inflorescences and distinctive, spiny fruits. The fruit, which resembles a green apple, contains a sweet, edible pulp surrounded by a hard, fibrous shell. The plant is valued for its ornamental value and its fruit, which is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

Its growth is typically found in humid, lowland areas with well-drained soil.
