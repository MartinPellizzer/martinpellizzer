Oenothera campestris subsp. cruciata

Oenothera campestris subsp.

cruciata, commonly known as the plains evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America. It is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening.

The plant typically grows in open habitats such as prairies, roadsides, and disturbed soils. It has opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. Oenothera campestris subsp.

cruciata is known for its adaptability and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
