Opuntia polyacantha var. albispina

Opuntia polyacantha var. albispina, commonly known as the white-spined prickly pear, is a cacti species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its flattened, fleshy stems that are ribbed and covered with clusters of white spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tun, is edible and contains small, sweet seeds.

This species is well adapted to drought conditions and thrives in full sun.
