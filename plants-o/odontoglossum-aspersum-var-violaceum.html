Odontoglossum aspersum var. violaceum

Odontoglossum aspersum var. violaceum, commonly known as the violet-tinged orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a pseudobulb that supports its foliage and flowering stems. The plant produces showy, violet-pink flowers with a distinctive hooded lip, typical of the Odontoglossum genus. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in a fine, hairy texture.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its striking floral display.
