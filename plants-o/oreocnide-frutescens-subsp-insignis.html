Oreocnide frutescens subsp. insignis

Oreocnide frutescens subsp.

insignis, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, winged fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide shade in shaded environments.
