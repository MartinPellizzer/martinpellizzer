Onobroma stricta

Onobroma stricta, commonly known as the rigid moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces showy, white, night-blooming flowers that emit a strong, sweet fragrance. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

The rigid moonflower is adapted to arid environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
