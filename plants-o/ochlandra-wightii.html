Ochlandra wightii

Ochlandra wightii, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a low-growing herb with linear, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Ochlandra wightii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
