Opuntia mesacantha subsp. mesacantha

Opuntia mesacantha subsp.

mesacantha, commonly known as the narrow-leafed prickly pear, is a cactus species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in spines and glochids, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores.

The plant produces yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months, followed by red, fleshy fruits known as tunas. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to poor soil conditions and high temperatures.

Its succulent nature allows it to store water efficiently, making it well-suited for desert climates.
