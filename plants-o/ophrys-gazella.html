Ophrys gazella

Ophrys gazella, commonly known as the gazelle orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive floral structure, with a labellum that mimics the appearance of a female bee, aiding in its unique pollination strategy. The plant produces a single, showy flower with a greenish-yellow coloration and intricate patterns. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Ophrys gazella is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its specialized relationship with specific bee pollinators.
