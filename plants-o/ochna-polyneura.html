Ochna polyneura

Ochna polyneura, commonly known as the African velvet tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by distinctive, woody, egg-shaped fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Ochna polyneura is valued for its ornamental appearance and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
