Othonna

Othonna, commonly known as the "spearthorn" or "bushman's broom," is a genus of flowering plants native to southern Africa.

These plants are typically shrubs or small trees, characterized by their woody stems and dense foliage. The leaves are often small, narrow, and arranged in opposite pairs, while the flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences. Othonna species are known for their adaptability to arid and semi-arid environments, making them resilient to drought.

They are also valued for their ornamental appeal and traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
