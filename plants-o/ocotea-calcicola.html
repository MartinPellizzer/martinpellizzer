Ocotea calcicola

Ocotea calcicola, commonly known as the limestone ocotea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in areas with limestone soils. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are typically elliptical and have a smooth, glossy surface. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, followed by small, dark blue-black berries.

Ocotea calcicola is often found in shaded, moist environments within tropical forests.
