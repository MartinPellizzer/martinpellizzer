Otites sibiricus var. lilacinus

Otites sibiricus var. lilacinus, commonly known as the lilac Siberian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of purple or lilac. The leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
