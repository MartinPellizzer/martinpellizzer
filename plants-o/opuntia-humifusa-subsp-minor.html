Opuntia humifusa subsp. minor

Opuntia humifusa subsp.

minor, commonly known as the lowland prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its flattened, pad-like stems that are often covered in spines and glochids.

The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by red or orange fruits. It has a shallow root system and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils.

Its succulent structure allows it to store water, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping.
