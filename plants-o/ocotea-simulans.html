Ocotea simulans

Ocotea simulans, commonly known as the white sand tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are followed by spherical, red berries. Ocotea simulans is often found in moist, shaded areas of tropical forests.

Its wood is valued for its durability and resistance to decay.
