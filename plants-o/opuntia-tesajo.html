Opuntia tesajo

Opuntia tesajo, commonly known as the Tesajo prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its flattened, paddle-shaped stems that are covered in spiny areoles. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tunas, is edible and has a sweet, juicy flavor.

Opuntia tesajo is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient plant in harsh environments.
