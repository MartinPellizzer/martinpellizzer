Oligocarpha neriifolia

Oligocarpha neriifolia, commonly known as the black-leafed daisy, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The species is valued for both its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
