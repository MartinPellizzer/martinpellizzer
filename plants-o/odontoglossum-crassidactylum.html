Odontoglossum crassidactylum

Odontoglossum crassidactylum, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 inches in diameter, making it one of the largest orchids in the world. The plant typically grows in high-altitude cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often forming a rosette pattern around the base of the plant.

Odontoglossum crassidactylum is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
