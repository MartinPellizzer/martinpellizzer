Ochrosia mcdowalliana

Ochrosia mcdowalliana, commonly known as the Christmas star, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of eastern Australia.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers during the winter months. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem and a preference for well-drained, sandy soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal environments.
