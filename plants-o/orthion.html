Orthion

Orthion, commonly known as the common tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and along roadsides. Orthion contains various bioactive compounds, including thujone, which has been historically used in herbal medicine.

However, due to its toxic properties, it is not recommended for medicinal use without professional guidance.
