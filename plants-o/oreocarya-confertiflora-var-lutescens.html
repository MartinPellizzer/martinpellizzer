Oreocarya confertiflora var. lutescens

Oreocarya confertiflora var. lutescens, commonly known as the yellow-flowered oreocarya, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a fine layer of hairs, helping to reduce water loss.

This variety is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique adaptations and ornamental value.
