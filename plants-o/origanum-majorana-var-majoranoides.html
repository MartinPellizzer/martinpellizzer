Origanum majorana var. majoranoides

Origanum majorana var. majoranoides, commonly known as sweet marjoram, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in summer. Its aromatic leaves are rich in essential oils, containing compounds like carvacrol and thymol.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
