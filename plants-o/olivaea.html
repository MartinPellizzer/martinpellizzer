Olivaea

Olivaea, commonly known as the olive tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Oleaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces drupes, or berries, which contain the edible olive fruit. Olivaea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its historical significance in agriculture and culture.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, sunny climate for optimal growth.
