Oryza sativa var. cinnamomea

Oryza sativa var. cinnamomea, commonly known as cinnamon rice, is a lesser-known variant of the rice species characterized by its unique cinnamon-like aroma.

This variety is native to Southeast Asia and is cultivated for both its fragrance and culinary uses. The plant exhibits a compact growth habit with slender, upright stems and small, grass-like leaves. Its grains are typically shorter and more fragrant than those of common rice varieties.

Due to its distinct characteristics, it is often used in traditional dishes and as a specialty ingredient in gourmet cooking.
