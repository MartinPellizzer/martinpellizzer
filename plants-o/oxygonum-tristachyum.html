Oxygonum tristachyum

Oxygonum tristachyum, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a tropical aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom above the water surface. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, providing habitat for various aquatic organisms. Its roots are submerged, anchoring it in muddy or sandy substrates.

Oxygonum tristachyum plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by improving water quality and supporting biodiversity.
