Oenothera lyrata

Oenothera lyrata, commonly known as the showy evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats like grasslands and rocky slopes.

Oenothera lyrata is also known for its ability to produce a large number of seeds, contributing to its success in various ecological settings.
