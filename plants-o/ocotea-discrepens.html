Ocotea discrepens

Ocotea discrepens, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ocotea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are typically dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible for some bird species.

Ocotea discrepens is often found in humid, shaded environments within lowland and montane forests.
