Ononis natrix subvar. subatlantica

Ononis natrix subvar. subatlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Atlantic coast.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, narrow, and slightly hairy, adapted to withstand coastal conditions.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dunes or along rocky shores.
