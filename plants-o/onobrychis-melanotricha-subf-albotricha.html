Onobrychis melanotricha subf. albotricha

Onobrychis melanotricha subf.

albotricha, commonly known as white hairy vetch, is a leguminous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in racemes.

The plant has a sprawling growth habit and produces cylindrical, hairy pods containing small, dark seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used as a forage crop or in ecological restoration projects.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both agricultural and ornamental purposes.
