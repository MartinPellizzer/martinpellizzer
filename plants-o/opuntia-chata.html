Opuntia chata

Opuntia chata, commonly known as the Chihuahuan prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems that are covered in spines and are often ribbed. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months, followed by edible fruits known as tunas. Opuntia chata is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water.

Its structure and growth habits make it an important part of the desert ecosystem.
