Osbeckia wightiana

Osbeckia wightiana, commonly known as the Wight's goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, rocky habitats. The plant features alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a rough, hairy texture. Its small, yellowish flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Osbeckia wightiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and seasonal blooms.
