Oryza sativa var. falca-aurea

Oryza sativa var. falca-aurea, commonly known as the golden hook rice, is a variant of rice characterized by its unique botanical features.

It is a grass species belonging to the Poaceae family, with slender, erect stems and narrow, elongated leaves. The inflorescence consists of spikelets that bear small, pale yellow flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its grains are elongated and have a distinct golden hue, particularly noticeable in the husk and bran layer.

This variety is primarily cultivated for its ornamental value and is also studied for its potential in genetic research due to its unique traits.
