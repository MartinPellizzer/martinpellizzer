Oxalis acetosella var. purpurascens

Oxalis acetosella var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple shamrock, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive purple hue, often adorned with red veins, and small, delicate flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively. It is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique addition to both natural and cultivated landscapes.
