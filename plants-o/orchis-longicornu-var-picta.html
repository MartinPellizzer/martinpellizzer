Orchis longicornu var. picta

Orchis longicornu var. picta, commonly known as the Painted Longhorn Orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to specific regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, showy flowers that display a striking combination of purple, white, and green hues. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing intricate, spurred flowers that are adapted for pollination by specific insects. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a dense rosette at the base of the plant.

This orchid thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.
