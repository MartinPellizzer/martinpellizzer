Obione elegans var. radiata

Obione elegans var. radiata, commonly known as the Radiata Beauty, is a striking member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and showy, umbellate flowers.

Native to the temperate regions of South America, this plant thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight. Its foliage exhibits a delicate, bluish-green hue, while the flowers range from white to pale pink, adding a subtle elegance to gardens. The plant is noted for its aromatic properties, with a mild, fresh scent that becomes more pronounced when the leaves are crushed.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its refined appearance and adaptability to various garden conditions.
