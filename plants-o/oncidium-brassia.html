Oncidium brassia

Oncidium brassia, commonly known as the yellow butterfly orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow petals and sepals, which resemble the wings of a butterfly. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. It produces fragrant, long-lasting flowers that bloom in clusters.

Oncidium brassia requires bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.
