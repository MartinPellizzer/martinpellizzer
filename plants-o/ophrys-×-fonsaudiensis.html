Ophrys × fonsaudiensis

Ophrys × fonsaudiensis, commonly known as the Saharan bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to North Africa.

It belongs to the genus Ophrys, which is known for its mimicry of female insects to attract male pollinators. The plant produces striking, bee-like flowers with intricate patterns that resemble female bees, a feature that plays a key role in its pollination strategy. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

The species is valued for its unique appearance and ecological significance in its native habitats.
