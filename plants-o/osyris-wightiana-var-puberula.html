Osyris wightiana var. puberula

Osyris wightiana var. puberula, commonly known as the hairy osyris, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its bark is smooth and often has a reddish-brown color, while its roots are fibrous and well-developed.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
