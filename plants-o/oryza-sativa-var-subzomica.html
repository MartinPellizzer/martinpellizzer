Oryza sativa var. subzomica

Oryza sativa var. subzomica, commonly known as the subzomica rice, is a less commonly studied variant of the rice species.

It is characterized by its unique morphological features, including shorter grain length and distinct coloration compared to other rice varieties. This variety is native to specific regions in Asia and is adapted to particular environmental conditions. Its botanical significance lies in its potential for genetic diversity and resilience to certain environmental stresses.

However, it remains underutilized in modern agricultural practices due to limited cultivation and research.
