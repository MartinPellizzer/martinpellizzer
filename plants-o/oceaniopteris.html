Oceaniopteris

Oceaniopteris, commonly known as the giant kelp, is a type of brown algae that thrives in cold, nutrient-rich coastal waters.

It forms extensive underwater forests that provide habitat for a wide variety of marine life. The plant has a complex structure with a holdfast, stipe, and fronds that can grow up to 30 meters in length. Its rapid growth rate makes it an important component of marine ecosystems.

Oceaniopteris plays a crucial role in carbon sequestration and oxygen production.
