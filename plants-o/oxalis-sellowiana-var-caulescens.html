Oxalis sellowiana var. caulescens

Oxalis sellowiana var. caulescens, commonly known as the Sellow's wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features a rosette of trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped lobes and bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 cm and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and ease of cultivation.

It is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it a beneficial plant in certain ecosystems.
