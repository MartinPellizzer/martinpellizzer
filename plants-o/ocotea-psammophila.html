Ocotea psammophila

Ocotea psammophila, commonly known as the sandplain ocotea, is a small evergreen tree native to the coastal regions of Brazil.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, dark berries. The plant thrives in sandy soils and is often found in dry tropical forests and coastal dunes. Its botanical features include simple, elliptic leaves with a glossy surface and small, clustered flowers that bloom in white or pale yellow.

Ocotea psammophila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
