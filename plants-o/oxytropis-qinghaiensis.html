Oxytropis qinghaiensis

Oxytropis qinghaiensis, commonly known as the Qinghai oxytropis, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the Qinghai region of China and thrives in alpine and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its upright stems and small, often hairy leaves. Its flowers are typically violet or purple, arranged in clusters, and it produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Oxytropis qinghaiensis plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest for its potential ecological and medicinal properties.
