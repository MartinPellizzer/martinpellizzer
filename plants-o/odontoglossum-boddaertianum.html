Odontoglossum boddaertianum

Odontoglossum boddaertianum, commonly known as the Boddaert's orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb that supports its growth during dry periods. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with intricate patterns. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
