Ononis hispida subsp. arborescens

Ononis hispida subsp.

arborescens, commonly known as the tree-like sandspurry, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a low-growing shrub with a woody base, characterized by its hairy stems and small, clustered leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or arid environments.

Its subspecies status reflects its distinct morphological features compared to other members of the Ononis genus.
