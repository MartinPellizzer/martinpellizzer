Ocotea oblongo-obovata

Ocotea oblongo-obovata, commonly known as the broad-ovate ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests. Its leaves are broad, ovate, and often have a waxy texture, while its fruit is a small, dark drupe.

Ocotea oblongo-obovata is valued for its timber, which is used in furniture and construction, and for its essential oils, which have medicinal applications.
