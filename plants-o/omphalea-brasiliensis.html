Omphalea brasiliensis

Omphalea brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian spurge, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, ribbed stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in a cup-like structure known as a cyathium. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its toxic sap, it is considered a potential weed in agricultural areas.
