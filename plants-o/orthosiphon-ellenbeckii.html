Orthosiphon ellenbeckii

Orthosiphon ellenbeckii, commonly known as the African mint or wild mint, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported diuretic properties.

Orthosiphon ellenbeckii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.
