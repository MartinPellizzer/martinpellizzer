Oreochloa disticha subsp. pedemontana

Oreochloa disticha subsp.

pedemontana, commonly known as the Montane two-ranked grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing pale green to brownish flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
