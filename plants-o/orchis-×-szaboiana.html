Orchis × szaboiana

Orchis × szaboiana, commonly known as the Szabó's orchid, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Orchis moria and Orchis purpurea.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of showy, violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its flowers have a distinctive lip that is often marked with darker pigments, enhancing its ornamental appeal.

This hybrid is valued in horticulture for its beauty and adaptability to cultivated environments.
