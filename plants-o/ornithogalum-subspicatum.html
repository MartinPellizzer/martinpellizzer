Ornithogalum subspicatum

Ornithogalum subspicatum, commonly known as the star of Bethlehem, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, sword-shaped leaves and produces tall, branched stems with clusters of white, star-like flowers. The plant is known for its striking floral display, which appears in late spring to early summer. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant appearance and ease of care.
