Oxygonum hastatum

Oxygonum hastatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved arrowhead, is a perennial aquatic plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving freshwater habitats such as ponds and marshes. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often submerged, with a distinct arrowhead shape at the base. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in spike-like inflorescences, blooming in the summer months.

Oxygonum hastatum plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
