Orchis maculata subsp. sambucina

Orchis maculata subsp.

sambucina, commonly known as the purple orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family. It is a perennial herb with a distinctive violet to purple flower spike that bears showy, spurred orchid flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodland areas and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
