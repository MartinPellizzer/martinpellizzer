Oreocharis urceolata

Oreocharis urceolata, commonly known as the urn-shaped orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, urn-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous regions, thriving in cool, humid environments. Its leaves are usually basal, forming a rosette, and the flowers are often pale green or white with a distinctive lip.

Oreocharis urceolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
