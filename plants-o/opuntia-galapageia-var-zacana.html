Opuntia galapageia var. zacana

Opuntia galapageia var. zacana, commonly known as the Galápagos prickly pear, is a cactus species native to the Galápagos Islands.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in spines and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant produces edible fruits known as tunas, which are a vital food source for both wildlife and humans. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to the unique climate of the islands.

Its presence plays a crucial role in the ecological balance of the Galápagos ecosystem.
