Ornithogalum arabicum

Ornithogalum arabicum, commonly known as the Arabian star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers during the spring. The plant is adapted to dry, rocky environments and has a deep root system for water retention. Its bulbs are used in traditional medicine and as a source of food in some regions.

Ornithogalum arabicum is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
