Ornithogalum creticum

Ornithogalum creticum, commonly known as the Cretan star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers during the spring. The plant is adapted to rocky and dry soils, often growing in coastal areas and limestone formations. It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous root system.

Ornithogalum creticum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
