Ononis pendula var. maroccana

Ononis pendula var. maroccana, commonly known as the Moroccan weeping sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to North Africa.

It features delicate, pendulous stems that bear clusters of small, white, four-petaled flowers. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that are adapted to arid environments, allowing it to store water efficiently. It thrives in well-drained sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
