Orthosia suberosa

Orthosia suberosa, commonly known as the rough-stemmed orthosia, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale blue, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Orthosia suberosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting various pollinators.
