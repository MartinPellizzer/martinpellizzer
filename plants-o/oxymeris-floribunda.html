Oxymeris floribunda

Oxymeris floribunda, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The tree has a broad, spreading canopy with pinnately compound leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its bark is smooth and greyish, and it produces small, flat pods containing seeds.

Oxymeris floribunda is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
