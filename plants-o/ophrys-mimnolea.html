Ophrys mimnolea

Ophrys mimnolea, commonly known as the mimic orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its intricate, bee-mimicking flowers that play a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in sunny, open habitats. Its flowers feature a complex structure with a labellum that resembles a female bee, attracting male bees for pollination.

Ophrys mimnolea is a perennial herb with a slender stem and linear leaves, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.
