Orchis globosa var. angustifolia

Orchis globosa var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved globeflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, globe-shaped flower spike that blooms in late spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, violet or purple flowers with a distinctive hooded lip. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or meadow habitats.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
