Opuntia quimilo

Opuntia quimilo, commonly known as the Quimilo cactus, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems that are covered in spines and are often ribbed. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tunas, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

Opuntia quimilo is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh climates.
