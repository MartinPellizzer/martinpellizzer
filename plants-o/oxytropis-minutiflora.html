Oxytropis minutiflora

Oxytropis minutiflora, commonly known as the dwarf locoweed, is a small, flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its low-growing habit and small, pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Oxytropis minutiflora plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native range.
