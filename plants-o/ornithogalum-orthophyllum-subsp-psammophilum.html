Ornithogalum orthophyllum subsp. psammophilum

Ornithogalum orthophyllum subsp.

psammophilum, commonly known as the sand-loving star-of-Bethlem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It is often found in coastal dunes and rocky outcrops, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
