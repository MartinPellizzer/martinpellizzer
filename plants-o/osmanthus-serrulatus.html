Osmanthus serrulatus

Osmanthus serrulatus, commonly known as the serrated osmanthus, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, small yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, round, reddish-brown fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, giving it its scientific name.

Osmanthus serrulatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
