Odontoglossum rossii var. aspersum

Odontoglossum rossii var. aspersum, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are dark green and lance-shaped, often with a waxy coating.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
