Orchis cordigera f. banatica

Orchis cordigera f. banatica, commonly known as the Banat orchid, is a rare and distinctive orchid species native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers with a contrasting yellow throat, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in calcareous grasslands and forest edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, aiding in water retention.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered in many regions.
