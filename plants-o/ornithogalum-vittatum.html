Ornithogalum vittatum

Ornithogalum vittatum, commonly known as the spotted lily or white star lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a tall, flowering stem with clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers. The plant is characterized by its distinctive spotted leaves and is often grown for its ornamental value in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, and it is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

Ornithogalum vittatum is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of climates, making it a versatile choice for landscaping.
