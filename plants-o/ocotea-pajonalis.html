Ocotea pajonalis

Ocotea pajonalis, commonly known as the Pajonal Ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant typically grows in humid, lowland forests and thrives in areas with high rainfall. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a distinctive glossy appearance.

Ocotea pajonalis is valued for its wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making, as well as for its potential medicinal properties.
