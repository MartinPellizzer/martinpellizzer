Orchis mascula subsp. mascula

Orchis mascula subsp.

mascula, commonly known as the male orchid, is a species of orchid native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its tall, erect flowering stems that bear a single, showy spike of purple or violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and woodland edges. It blooms from June to August, and its flowers are pollinated by bees and other insects.

This orchid is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its distinctive, masculine-sounding common name.
