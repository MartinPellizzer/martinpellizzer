Oxalis dregei

Oxalis dregei, commonly known as the Cape buttercup, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom in spring and are often followed by small, rounded fruits.

Oxalis dregei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
