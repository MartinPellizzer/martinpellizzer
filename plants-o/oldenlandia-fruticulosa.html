Oldenlandia fruticulosa

Oldenlandia fruticulosa, commonly known as the "Mexican moonflower," is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in disturbed soils or along roadsides. The plant features small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.
