Ocotea perseifolia

Ocotea perseifolia, commonly known as the fragrant ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a distinctive, spicy scent when crushed.

Ocotea perseifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
