Orobus sylvaticus

Orobus sylvaticus, commonly known as the woodland everlet, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of Europe.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in shaded, moist woodlands and forest understories. The plant features slender, upright stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense spikes, and range in color from white to pale purple.

Orobus sylvaticus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local insect populations.
