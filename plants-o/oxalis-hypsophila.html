Oxalis hypsophila

Oxalis hypsophila, commonly known as the silver leaf oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces small, five-petaled flowers in shades of white, pink, and yellow, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade.

Oxalis hypsophila is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and easy care requirements.
