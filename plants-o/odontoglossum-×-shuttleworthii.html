Odontoglossum × shuttleworthii

Odontoglossum × shuttleworthii, commonly known as the Shuttleworth orchid, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Odontoglossum lindenii and Odontoglossum austrocopticum.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in cool, humid environments. The plant produces strikingly beautiful flowers with intricate patterns and vibrant colors, often in shades of purple, pink, and white. Its pseudobulbs are short and stout, and it has linear, grass-like leaves that help it conserve moisture.

This hybrid is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation under proper conditions.
