Ocotea jamaicensis

Ocotea jamaicensis, commonly known as Jamaican ocotea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Caribbean, particularly Jamaica and parts of the Greater Antilles.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats within tropical rainforests. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by dark blue berries, which are an important food source for various wildlife species.
