Otomeria dilatata

Otomeria dilatata, commonly known as the broadleaf ironwood, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that typically grows in coastal and inland forested areas. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. Its hard, dense wood is valued for its durability and resistance to decay.

Otomeria dilatata plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
