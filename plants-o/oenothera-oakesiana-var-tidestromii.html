Oenothera oakesiana var. tidestromii

Oenothera oakesiana var. tidestromii, commonly known as the Tidestrom's evening primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening and close by morning. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal dunes and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
