Ocimum circinatum

Ocimum circinatum, commonly known as wild basil, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to purple, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and antimicrobial properties.

Ocimum circinatum thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
