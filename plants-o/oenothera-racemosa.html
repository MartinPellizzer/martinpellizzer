Oenothera racemosa

Oenothera racemosa, commonly known as the showy evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow or orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed habitats like roadsides and fields. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a rough texture.

The showy evening primrose is also known for its medicinal properties, with its seeds containing oils used in various traditional and modern applications.
