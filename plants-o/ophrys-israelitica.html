Ophrys israelitica

Ophrys israelitica, commonly known as the Israeli bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to Israel.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee, a feature evolved to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brownish in color, with intricate patterns that aid in its deceptive pollination strategy.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Ophrys israelitica is considered endangered and is of significant interest in botanical conservation efforts.
