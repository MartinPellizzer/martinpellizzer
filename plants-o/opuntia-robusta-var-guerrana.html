Opuntia robusta var. guerrana

Opuntia robusta var. guerrana, commonly known as the Mexican prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that are covered in spines and are often ribbed. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers, which are followed by edible fruits known as tunas. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting well to hot and dry climates.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local cultures.
