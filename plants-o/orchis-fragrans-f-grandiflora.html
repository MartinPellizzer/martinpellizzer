Orchis fragrans f. grandiflora

Orchis fragrans f. grandiflora, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family characterized by its large, showy flowers.

This plant is native to certain regions of Europe and is known for its distinctive violet-blue or purple petals with a yellowish lip. The flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a vibrant touch to woodland habitats. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in meadows and forest floors.

The plant's botanical features include a slender, upright stem and lanceolate leaves that support its ornamental value.
