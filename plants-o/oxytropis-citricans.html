Oxytropis citricans

Oxytropis citricans, commonly known as yellow locoweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. The plant thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. It has a deep taproot system that allows it to survive in arid conditions.

Oxytropis citricans is also known for its toxic properties, which can affect livestock if ingested.
