Olea glandulosa

Olea glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular olive, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are covered in glandular dots, which secrete a sticky substance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by small, fleshy fruits that contain a single seed. Olea glandulosa is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in Mediterranean ecosystems.
