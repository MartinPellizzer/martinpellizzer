Opuntia caerulescens

Opuntia caerulescens, commonly known as the blue prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, blue-green cladodes, which are modified stems that perform photosynthesis. The plant produces vibrant yellow to orange flowers during the summer months, followed by edible, red, fleshy fruits. Its spiny areoles are covered with fine, white hairs, and it is well adapted to arid environments.

Opuntia caerulescens is also notable for its ability to store water in its thick, fleshy stems.
