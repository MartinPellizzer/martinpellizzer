Orchis maculata subsp. lancibracteata

Orchis maculata subsp.

lancibracteata, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet or purple flowers with distinctive dark spots.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous grasslands and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lanceolate and arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces a single, erect inflorescence with several flowers.

The subspecies lancibracteata is distinguished by its longer bracts and more elongated flower structure compared to other subspecies of Orchis maculata.
