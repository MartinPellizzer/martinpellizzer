Oxalis luteola

Oxalis luteola, commonly known as yellow wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with three heart-shaped leaflets, often tinged with red or purple veins. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Oxalis luteola is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
