Ornithogalum hajastanum

Ornithogalum hajastanum, commonly known as the Caucasian star-of-Bethlem, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers in the spring. The plant is characterized by its tuberous roots, which store nutrients and help it survive in its native habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
