Ortgiesia seideliana

Ortgiesia seideliana, commonly known as the Seidel's orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, showy flowers with a distinctive coloration. The plant typically thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its botanical features include a pseudobulb structure and lance-shaped leaves.

It is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
