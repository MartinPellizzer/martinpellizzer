Orchis picta

Orchis picta, commonly known as the painted orchid, is a species of orchid native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that range from purple to pink and white, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or grassland habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces a tall, slender flowering stem.

Orchis picta is a perennial herb that blooms from late spring to early summer.
