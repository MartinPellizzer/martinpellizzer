Oxalis irreperta

Oxalis irreperta, commonly known as the red-rooted wood sorrel, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive reddish veins, and its flowers are typically pink or purple, with five petals. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that contain several seeds. Oxalis irreperta thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

It is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
