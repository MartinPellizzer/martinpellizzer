Odontoglossum pardinum

Odontoglossum pardinum, commonly known as the Pardinal orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high elevations. The plant produces intricate, showy flowers with a unique coloration pattern, often featuring a contrasting lip. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the rocky substrates it inhabits.

Odontoglossum pardinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
