Oncidium boydii

Oncidium boydii, commonly known as the Boyd's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It typically grows in shaded, humid environments with well-draining soil. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, with intricate patterns and a distinctive lip.

Oncidium boydii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
