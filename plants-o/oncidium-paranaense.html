Oncidium paranaense

Oncidium paranaense, commonly known as the Parana orchid, is a tropical orchid native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful flowers that often display a mix of yellow, orange, and red hues. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its pseudobulbs are elongated and segmented, supporting its growth and storage of nutrients.

Oncidium paranaense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its striking floral display.
