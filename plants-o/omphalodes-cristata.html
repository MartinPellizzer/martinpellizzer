Omphalodes cristata

Omphalodes cristata, commonly known as the crowned bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has a compact growth habit with hairy stems and leaves that are lance-shaped and opposite. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Omphalodes cristata is valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
