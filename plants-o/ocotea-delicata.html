Ocotea delicata

Ocotea delicata, commonly known as the fragrant ocotea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes well-known plants like cinnamon and avocado. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves, which emit a pleasant scent when crushed. Its flowers are small and yellowish, typically appearing in clusters, and it produces dark, glossy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife.

Ocotea delicata is often found in moist, shaded forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role.
