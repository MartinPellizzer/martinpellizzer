Orchis maculata var. curvifolia

Orchis maculata var. curvifolia, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive purple and white flowers with a yellow lip. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or grassland habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its flowering stems bear a single spike of showy flowers.

The species is noted for its ecological importance as a food source for various pollinators and its role in indicator species for habitat conservation.
