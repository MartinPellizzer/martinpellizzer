Otholobium bracteolatum

Otholobium bracteolatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf burr medic, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, clustered pods and trifoliate leaves. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in poor soil conditions.

It produces yellow flowers and is often used in rehabilitation of degraded lands due to its hardy nature.
