Orthaea bullata

Orthaea bullata, commonly known as the bladder bladder, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a small, herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters. Its fruits are distinctive, spherical seed pods that resemble bladders, hence its common name.

The plant is adapted to dry, sunny conditions and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
