Ornithostaphylos oppositifolia

Ornithostaphylos oppositifolia, commonly known as the oppositeleaf manzanita, is a shrub native to California and Baja California.

It is characterized by its evergreen leaves that are oppositely arranged on the stems. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in clusters during the spring. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.
