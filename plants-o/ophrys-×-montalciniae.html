Ophrys × montalciniae

Ophrys × montalciniae, commonly known as the Montalcino bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is characterized by its intricate floral morphology, with a labellum that closely resembles a female bee, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is found in the Mediterranean region, particularly in Italy. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brownish in color, with a distinctive three-lobed lip that mimics the appearance of an insect.

Ophrys × montalciniae is a rare and protected species, often cultivated in botanical gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological significance.
