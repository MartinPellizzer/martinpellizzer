Ophthalmacanthus speciosus

Ophthalmacanthus speciosus, commonly known as the showy eye-cup, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that resemble miniature cups. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and prominent veins. Its flowering period typically occurs during the wet season, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

Ophthalmacanthus speciosus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
