Oxytropis campestris var. terrae-novae

Oxytropis campestris var. terrae-novae, commonly known as Nova Scotia field vetch, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Maritime Provinces of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sandy or loamy soils and is often found in open habitats such as fields and coastal areas.

This variant is distinguished by its geographic distribution and specific adaptations to the climate of Nova Scotia.
