Oxalis cordobensis var. typica

Oxalis cordobensis var. typica, commonly known as the Cordoba wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in grasslands and forest understories. It produces distinctive seed pods that split open to release seeds, aiding in its propagation.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a unique, delicate plant.
