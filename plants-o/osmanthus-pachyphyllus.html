Osmanthus pachyphyllus

Osmanthus pachyphyllus, commonly known as the thick-leaf osmanthus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Oleaceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and fragrant yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers, which bloom in late summer, are highly aromatic and are often used in traditional Chinese medicine and as a flavoring in teas and confections.

The plant's botanical features make it both ornamental and economically valuable.
