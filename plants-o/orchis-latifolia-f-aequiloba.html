Orchis latifolia f. aequiloba

Orchis latifolia f. aequiloba, commonly known as the broad-leaved orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces striking violet or purple flowers with a distinctive lip. This orchid is native to Europe and thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant is perennial and typically blooms from late spring to early summer.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
