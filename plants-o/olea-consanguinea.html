Olea consanguinea

Olea consanguinea, commonly known as the African olive, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by oval, fleshy fruits that are edible. Olea consanguinea is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in regions with limited rainfall.

Its botanical features make it valuable for both ecological and potential agricultural purposes.
