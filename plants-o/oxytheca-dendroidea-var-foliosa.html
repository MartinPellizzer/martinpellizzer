Oxytheca dendroidea var. foliosa

Oxytheca dendroidea var. foliosa, commonly known as the tree hibiscus, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming during the dry season. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in coastal areas and dry forests.

The leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine, and the plant is valued for its ornamental beauty.
