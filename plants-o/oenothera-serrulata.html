Oenothera serrulata

Oenothera serrulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf evening primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features showy yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has lance-shaped, serrated leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry grasslands and rocky slopes.

Oenothera serrulata is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinator populations.
