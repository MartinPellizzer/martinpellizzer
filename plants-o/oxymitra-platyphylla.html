Oxymitra platyphylla

Oxymitra platyphylla, commonly known as the broad-leaved oxymitra, is a species of flowering plant native to the southern regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
