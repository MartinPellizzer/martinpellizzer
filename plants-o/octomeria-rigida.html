Octomeria rigida

Octomeria rigida, commonly known as the rigid octomeria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its upright, woody stems. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with ray florets that give it a daisy-like appearance.

Octomeria rigida is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
