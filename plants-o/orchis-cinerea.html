Orchis cinerea

Orchis cinerea, commonly known as the gray orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and pale green to grayish leaves. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, often with a distinctive hooded lip. Orchis cinerea thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.

It plays an important role in pollination, attracting various insect species with its nectar-rich blooms.
