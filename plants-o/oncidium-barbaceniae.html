Oncidium barbaceniae

Oncidium barbaceniae, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow and green foliage and produces striking clusters of fragrant, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. It requires bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.

Oncidium barbaceniae is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
