Ononis diffusa var. micrantha

Ononis diffusa var. micrantha, commonly known as lesser whitlow grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, hairy stems that often lie prostrate or ascending, with small, bright yellow flowers arranged in clusters. The leaves are linear to lanceolate, with a hairy surface and a slightly serrated margin. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as gravelly soils and rocky areas.

This plant is often found in disturbed sites and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
