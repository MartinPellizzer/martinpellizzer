Oxytropis iranica

Oxytropis iranica, commonly known as Iranian locoweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, and it produces small, hard pods that contain seeds.

Oxytropis iranica is also noted for its potential ecological importance in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
