Oberonia iridifolia var. denticulata

Oberonia iridifolia var. denticulata, commonly known as the toothed orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its delicate, greenish-blue flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a subtle iridescence. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid forest understories, where it thrives on decaying organic matter. Its leaves are elongated and lance-shaped, often with a waxy texture.

This variety is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and limited distribution.
