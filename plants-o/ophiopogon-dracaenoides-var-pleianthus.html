Ophiopogon dracaenoides var. pleianthus

Ophiopogon dracaenoides var. pleianthus, commonly known as the Japanese yew or golden thread, is a species native to East Asia.

It is a perennial evergreen herb characterized by its narrow, strap-like leaves that are typically dark green with a slight waxy coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by red berries, which are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.

Its foliage is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
