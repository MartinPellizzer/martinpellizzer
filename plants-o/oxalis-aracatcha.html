Oxalis aracatcha

Oxalis aracatcha, commonly known as the Brazilian wood sorrel, is a species of flowering plant in the family Oxalidaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-drained soil. Oxalis aracatcha is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.

It is also used in traditional medicine in some regions for its potential health benefits.
