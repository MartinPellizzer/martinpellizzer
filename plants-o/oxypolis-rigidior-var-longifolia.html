Oxypolis rigidior var. longifolia

Oxypolis rigidior var. longifolia, commonly known as slender oxypolis, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbellate inflorescences and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are small, white to greenish in color, and it produces small, dry fruits called schizocarps.

This variety is distinguished by its longer leaves compared to the typical form of Oxypolis rigidior.
