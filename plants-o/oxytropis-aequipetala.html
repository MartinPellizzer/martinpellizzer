Oxytropis aequipetala

Oxytropis aequipetala, commonly known as the equal-petled locoweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has a deep taproot system that allows it to thrive in arid conditions.

Oxytropis aequipetala is also known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock.
