Oxalis peduncularis var. pilosa

Oxalis peduncularis var. pilosa, commonly known as the woolly wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, soft appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for arid landscapes.

Its unique morphology and drought tolerance make it a popular ornamental plant in xeriscaping.
