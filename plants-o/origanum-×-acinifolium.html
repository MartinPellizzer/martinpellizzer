Origanum × acinifolium

Origanum × acinifolium, commonly known as Greek oregano, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small, dark green leaves and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for herb gardens.

Its essential oils, rich in thymol and carvacrol, contribute to its strong medicinal and culinary uses.
