Oryza sativa proles japonica

Oryza sativa proles japonica, commonly known as Japanese rice, is a subspecies of rice native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its short, slender grains and is typically cultivated in temperate climates. The plant has a short growing season and requires a significant amount of water for optimal growth. It is an important crop in Japan, contributing to both the local diet and agricultural economy.

Botanically, it belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is closely related to other rice varieties.
