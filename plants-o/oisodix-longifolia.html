Oisodix longifolia

Oisodix longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf oisodix, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom during the wet season. Oisodix longifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
