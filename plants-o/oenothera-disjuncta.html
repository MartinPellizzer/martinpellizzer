Oenothera disjuncta

Oenothera disjuncta, commonly known as the Disjunct Evening Primrose, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and it typically grows in dry, rocky soils. It is part of the Onagraceae family, which includes other evening primroses.

Oenothera disjuncta is known for its ecological importance in supporting local insect populations and its adaptability to arid environments.
