Oberonia thisbe

Oberonia thisbe, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a rhizome and produces a single, erect stem bearing several lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces a spike of showy, golden-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
