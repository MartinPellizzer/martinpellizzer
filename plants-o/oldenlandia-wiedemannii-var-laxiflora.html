Oldenlandia wiedemannii var. laxiflora

Oldenlandia wiedemannii var. laxiflora, commonly known as the slender leaf spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its delicate, linear leaves and clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its flowers are often visited by insects, contributing to its role in pollination networks.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils.
