Orchis morio var. parviflora

Orchis morio var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered green-winged orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The flowers are typically pale green or yellowish with distinctive wing-like structures on the petals. It blooms in late spring to early summer, often in shaded woodland environments.

This variety is noted for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
