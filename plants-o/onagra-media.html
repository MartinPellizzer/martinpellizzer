Onagra media

Onagra media, commonly known as hoary flax, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and showy blue-violet flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant belongs to the evening primrose family (Onagraceae) and is often found in prairies and open woodlands. Its seeds are small and contain oils that have been used in traditional medicine.

Onagra media is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
