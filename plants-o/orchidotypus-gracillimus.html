Orchidotypus gracillimus

Orchidotypus gracillimus, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated stems and clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers. Native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air, making it well-suited for epiphytic growth on trees.

The plant's unique morphology and ornamental value make it a popular choice among horticulturists and plant enthusiasts.
