Odontoglossum × vexativum

Odontoglossum × vexativum, commonly known as the hybrid violet orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Odontoglossum raphanifolium and Odontoglossum × albovirens.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America. The plant is characterized by its striking violet and white flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. It typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a parent in the development of many commercially important orchid hybrids.
