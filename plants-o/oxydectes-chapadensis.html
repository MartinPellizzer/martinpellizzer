Oxydectes chapadensis

Oxydectes chapadensis, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. Its flowers are usually borne on long, slender stems and can exhibit a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and pink.

Oxydectes chapadensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
