Odontoglossum ixioides

Odontoglossum ixioides, commonly known as the Orchid of the Andes, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of South America, particularly in the Andes, where it thrives in cool, humid environments. The plant produces striking, colorful flowers with intricate patterns, often in shades of purple, pink, and white. Its leaves are typically broad and elliptical, with a waxy surface that helps retain moisture.

Odontoglossum ixioides is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its role in local ecosystems.
