Oreacanthus cristalensis

Oreacanthus cristalensis, commonly known as the Crystal Mountain bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet petals and a distinctive, bell-shaped corolla. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, silvery down.

Oreacanthus cristalensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in alpine gardens.
