Oxytropis engizekensis

Oxytropis engizekensis, commonly known as the Engizek oxytropis, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow to brown flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Oxytropis engizekensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
