Ocotea acunana

Ocotea acunana, commonly known as the Brazilian cinnamon tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and fragrant bark. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by black, spherical berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitats.
