Osteospermum tripteroideum

Osteospermum tripteroideum, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, daisy-like flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red. The plant has hairy, upright stems and narrow, gray-green leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Osteospermum tripteroideum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
