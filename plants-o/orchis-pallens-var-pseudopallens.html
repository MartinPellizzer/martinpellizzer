Orchis pallens var. pseudopallens

Orchis pallens var. pseudopallens, commonly known as the pale orchid, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, pale pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while the flowers are borne on a spike with a distinct, spurred lip.

The species is noted for its delicate appearance and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
