Obione andina

Obione andina, commonly known as the Andean orchid, is a rare and striking plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous terrain, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its flowers can range in color from white to purple.

Due to its limited habitat and beauty, it is considered a valuable species in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
