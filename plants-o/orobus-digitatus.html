Orobus digitatus

Orobus digitatus, commonly known as the fingered orchid, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to parts of Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in rocky and arid environments. The plant is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. Its roots are adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions.

Orobus digitatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
