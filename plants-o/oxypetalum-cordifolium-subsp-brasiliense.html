Oxypetalum cordifolium subsp. brasiliense

Oxypetalum cordifolium subsp.

brasiliense, commonly known as the Brazilian heartleaf oxypetalum, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil. It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically glossy and dark green.

The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in moist forest understories.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
