Opuntia orbiculata

Opuntia orbiculata, commonly known as the broadleaf prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, rounded pads, which are typically green and covered in spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tunas, is edible and often used in local cuisines.

Opuntia orbiculata is well adapted to arid environments and plays a role in supporting local wildlife.
