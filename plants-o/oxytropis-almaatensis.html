Oxytropis almaatensis

Oxytropis almaatensis, commonly known as the Almaty milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale purple or white in color. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in steppes and rocky soils.

Oxytropis almaatensis plays a role in soil stabilization and is of interest for its potential ecological and medicinal uses.
