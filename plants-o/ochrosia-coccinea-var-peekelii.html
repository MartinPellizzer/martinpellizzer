Ochrosia coccinea var. peekelii

Ochrosia coccinea var. peekelii, commonly known as the Peckel's firecracker plant, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that provide a lush backdrop to its vibrant blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.

Its ornamental value lies in its bold color and compact growth habit, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.
