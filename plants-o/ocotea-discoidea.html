Ocotea discoidea

Ocotea discoidea, commonly known as the yellow sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark-green leaves and fragrant wood. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by dark, spherical berries. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils, which have been used traditionally for their medicinal properties.

Ocotea discoidea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
