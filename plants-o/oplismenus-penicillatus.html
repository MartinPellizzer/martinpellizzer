Oplismenus penicillatus

Oplismenus penicillatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and prairies. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a distinct reddish or purple coloration on the underside.

Oplismenus penicillatus produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall.
