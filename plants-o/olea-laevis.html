Olea laevis

Olea laevis, commonly known as the smooth olive, is a species of flowering plant in the Oleaceae family.

It is native to parts of southern Europe and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are smooth and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, spherical drupe that contains a single seed.

Olea laevis is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
