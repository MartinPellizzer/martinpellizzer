Origanum vulgare f. carneum

Origanum vulgare f. carneum, commonly known as the pink marjoram, is a cultivar of the common oregano plant.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are aromatic, with a strong, spicy scent similar to other members of the mint family. This variety is often used in culinary applications and herbal medicine due to its essential oils.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herb beds.
