Osmia conyzoides

Osmia conyzoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Osmia conyzoides thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils or along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of interest in ecological studies.
