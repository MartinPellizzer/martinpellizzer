Orchis fusca var. idiocrana

Orchis fusca var. idiocrana, commonly known as the dark orchid, is a species of orchid native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple to blackish flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in woodland or grassland habitats, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its flowers are spurred at the base, a feature common among many orchid species.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its striking appearance.
