Orinus anomala

Orinus anomala, commonly known as the Indian nettle or the small nettle, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including India and China.

It belongs to the family Urticaceae, which includes many species known for their stinging hairs. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with erect stems and opposite leaves that are often covered in fine, irritating hairs. Its small greenish-white flowers are clustered in racemes and are pollinated by insects.

Orinus anomala is often found in moist, disturbed soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
