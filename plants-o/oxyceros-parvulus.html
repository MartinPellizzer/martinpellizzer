Oxyceros parvulus

Oxyceros parvulus, commonly known as the small-fruited oxyceros, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant has small, elliptical leaves and produces tiny, yellowish flowers clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that contains several seeds.

Oxyceros parvulus is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
