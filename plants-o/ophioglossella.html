Ophioglossella

Ophioglossella, commonly known as the snake fern, is a genus of ferns belonging to the family Ophioglossaceae.

These plants are characterized by their unique, often elongated, and sometimes spiral-shaped fronds. They typically grow in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and rocky areas. Ophioglossella species are known for their primitive morphology, which includes a distinct, often fleshy rhizome.

The genus is relatively small, with only a few recognized species, and is of interest to botanists due to its ancient evolutionary lineage.
