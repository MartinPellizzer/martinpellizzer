Oenothera bufonis

Oenothera bufonis, commonly known as the bufonoid evening primrose, is a species of flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oval seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Oenothera bufonis is a member of the Onagraceae family, which includes other well-known evening primrose species.
