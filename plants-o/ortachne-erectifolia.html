Ortachne erectifolia

Ortachne erectifolia, commonly known as the upright leaf grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family (Poaceae).

It is characterized by its erect, slender stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a compact, tufted growth form. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences, which contain both male and female flowers, typical of many grass species. Ortachne erectifolia is often found in moist, well-drained soils in temperate regions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of grassland ecosystems.
