Oxalis guttata

Oxalis guttata, commonly known as the weeping wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves that often have a waxy, glossy appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in spring. Oxalis guttata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its unique foliage and modest blooms make it a popular choice for woodland gardens and shaded borders.
