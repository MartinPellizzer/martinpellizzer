Oxalis modestior

Oxalis modestior, commonly known as the smallleaf wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its calcium oxalate crystals in the leaves, which can cause irritation upon contact. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas and thrives in partial to full shade.

Oxalis modestior is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
