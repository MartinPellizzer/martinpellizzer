Oxytropis teres

Oxytropis teres, commonly known as the roundleaf locoweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features compound leaves with small, rounded leaflets and produces yellow or orange pea-like flowers. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Oxytropis teres is also known for its toxic properties, making it a potential hazard to livestock.
