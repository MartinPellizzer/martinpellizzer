Ourisia biflora

Ourisia biflora, commonly known as the two-flowered ourisia, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are simple and alternate, often with a hairy texture.

The flowers, which are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, bloom in early spring and attract various pollinators.
