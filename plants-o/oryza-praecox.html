Oryza praecox

Oryza praecox, commonly known as early rice, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spikelet-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

Oryza praecox is an important wild relative of cultivated rice, contributing to genetic diversity and breeding programs.
