Oedera oppositifolia

Oedera oppositifolia, commonly known as the opposite-leafed wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
