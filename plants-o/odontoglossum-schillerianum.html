Odontoglossum schillerianum

Odontoglossum schillerianum, commonly known as the Schiller's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in its high-altitude habitat. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers with a unique coloration pattern, typically featuring white and purple hues. Its leaves are strap-like and arranged in a rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Odontoglossum schillerianum is prized by horticulturists for its beauty and is often cultivated in shaded, moist environments.
