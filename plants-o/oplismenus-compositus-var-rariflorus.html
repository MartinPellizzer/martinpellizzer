Oplismenus compositus var. rariflorus

Oplismenus compositus var. rariflorus, commonly known as the rareflower wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, delicate white flowers with a subtle fragrance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas. Its botanical characteristics include hairy stems and lanceolate leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
