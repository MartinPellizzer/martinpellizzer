Oenothera sinuata subvar. helleriana

Oenothera sinuata subvar. helleriana, commonly known as Heller's evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowering period typically occurs in late summer to early autumn.

Heller's evening primrose is adapted to dry, sunny habitats and is often found in sandy or gravelly soils.
