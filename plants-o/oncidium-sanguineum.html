Oncidium sanguineum

Oncidium sanguineum, commonly known as the red lady orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters of pink to red hues, often with a contrasting white or yellow throat. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, arranged in a fan shape.

Oncidium sanguineum prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
