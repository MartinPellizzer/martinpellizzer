Ooclinium sideritis

Ooclinium sideritis, commonly known as ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in rocky or coastal areas.

Ooclinium sideritis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
