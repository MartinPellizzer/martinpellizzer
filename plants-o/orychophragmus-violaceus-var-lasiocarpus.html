Orychophragmus violaceus var. lasiocarpus

Orychophragmus violaceus var. lasiocarpus, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its varietal name "lasiocarpus," meaning "hairy fruit.". It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its ecological value supports local wildlife and contributes to biodiversity in native plant landscapes.
