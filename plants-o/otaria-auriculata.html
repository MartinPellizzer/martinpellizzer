Otaria auriculata

Otaria auriculata, commonly known as the ear-leaved orchid, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive ear-shaped leaves, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories, and is native to regions in South America. Otaria auriculata produces small, delicate flowers that are often pale green or white in color.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists alike.
