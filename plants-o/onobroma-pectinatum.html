Onobroma pectinatum

Onobroma pectinatum, commonly known as the velvetleaf poinsettia, is a species of flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble a flower, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are typically velvety in texture, contributing to its common name.
