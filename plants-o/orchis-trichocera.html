Orchis trichocera

Orchis trichocera, commonly known as the hairy twayblade, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and meadows. The plant has a distinctive appearance with a slender, leafless stem and a single, spurred orchid flower. Its roots are fibrous and often form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi.

The species is notable for its specialized pollination mechanisms and ecological importance in its native habitats.
