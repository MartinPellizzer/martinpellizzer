Oenothera deltoides subsp. ambigua

Oenothera deltoides subsp.

ambigua, commonly known as the plains evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the central and northern United States. It is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats like grasslands and prairies, and it has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, winged seeds.

This species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
