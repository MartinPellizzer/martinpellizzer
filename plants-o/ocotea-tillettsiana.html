Ocotea tillettsiana

Ocotea tillettsiana, commonly known as the Tillettsiana Ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean, particularly found in the Greater Antilles.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in lowland forests, reaching heights of up to 20 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a distinctive glossy appearance, while its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences.

The fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed, and the tree is valued for its potential in ecological and medicinal contexts.
