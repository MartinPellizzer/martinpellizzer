Ornithogalum glaucifolium

Ornithogalum glaucifolium, commonly known as the blue-leaved star of Bethlehem, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features lance-shaped, bluish-gray leaves that are covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its arid environment. The plant produces striking white or pale yellow flowers in umbels, typically blooming in spring. It is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has also been used in traditional herbal remedies.
