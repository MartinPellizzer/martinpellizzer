Osmoglossum anceps

Osmoglossum anceps, commonly known as the narrow-leaved grass.

It is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa, characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained soils, often found in grasslands and rocky habitats.

Osmoglossum anceps plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small invertebrates.
