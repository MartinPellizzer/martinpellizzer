Ophrys korae

Ophrys korae, commonly known as the Korean bee orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to South Korea.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, with a pouch-like labellum that mimics the appearance of a female bee, aiding in its specialized pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forest floors and mountainous regions. Its leaves are grass-like and arranged in a rosette, while its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish in color.

Ophrys korae is a rare and endangered species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts in its native range.
