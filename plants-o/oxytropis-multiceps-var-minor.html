Oxytropis multiceps var. minor

Oxytropis multiceps var. minor, commonly known as the lesser globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its globular clusters of yellow flowers. The plant has a creeping growth habit with spiny stems and palmately divided leaves. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open areas with well-drained soil.

Its small, rounded pods contain seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize disturbed sites.
