Oxalis violacea

Oxalis violacea, commonly known as the purple shamrock, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are often purple or reddish in color, and its delicate, five-petaled flowers that range from white to pink. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens. It is also known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, forming dense mats of foliage.

Oxalis violacea is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its adaptability to various garden conditions.
