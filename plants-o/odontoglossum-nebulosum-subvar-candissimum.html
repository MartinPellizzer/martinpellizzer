Odontoglossum nebulosum subvar. candissimum

Odontoglossum nebulosum subvar. candissimum, commonly known as the Candis orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, large, and colorful flowers that often display a mix of purple, white, and yellow hues. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and it produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

This species is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
