Odontoglossum multistellare

Odontoglossum multistellare, commonly known as the starry tooth orchid, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb and long, narrow leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces strikingly colorful flowers with a distinctive tooth-like structure on the lip. It thrives in cool, moist environments and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
