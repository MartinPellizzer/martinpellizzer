Oubanguia duchesnei

Oubanguia duchesnei, commonly known as the African dandelion, is a flowering plant native to central and western Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble dandelions. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves and produces yellow flowers that develop into fluffy seeds. It thrives in open, sunny habitats and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

Oubanguia duchesnei is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional African medicine.
