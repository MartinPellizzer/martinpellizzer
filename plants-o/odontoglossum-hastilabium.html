Odontoglossum hastilabium

Odontoglossum hastilabium, commonly known as the broad-leafed orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces dark green, lance-shaped leaves and blooms with violet or purple flowers that have a distinctive, spurred lip. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.

Odontoglossum hastilabium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow under proper conditions.
