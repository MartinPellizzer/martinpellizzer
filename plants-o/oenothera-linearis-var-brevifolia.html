Oenothera linearis var. brevifolia

Oenothera linearis var. brevifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features slender, linear leaves and produces yellow flowers that open in the evening. The plant is characterized by its fibrous root system and its ability to thrive in dry, sandy soils. It is part of the Onagraceae family and is known for its showy, bioluminescent-like appearance at night.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
