Ormosia getuliana

Ormosia getuliana, commonly known as the Brazilian cacao tree, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a pod-like structure containing several seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in tropical forests.
