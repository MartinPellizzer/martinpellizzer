Oxalis wrightii var. pilosa

Oxalis wrightii var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with a hairy texture and clusters of yellow, shamrock-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and along stream banks. It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in partial shade.

The leaves and flowers are often used in ornamental gardening due to their attractive appearance.
