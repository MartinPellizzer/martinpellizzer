Orthaea coriacea

Orthaea coriacea, commonly known as the rough orthaea, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branched stems covered in a rough, sandpapery texture. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in early summer. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and often have a slightly hairy surface.

Orthaea coriacea is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in coastal areas.
