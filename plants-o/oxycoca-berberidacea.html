Oxycoca berberidacea

Oxycoca berberidacea, commonly known as the yellow bryony, is a climbing plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Berberidaceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and spiny stems. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but have a bitter taste. It thrives in woodland areas and prefers partial shade with well-drained soil.

Oxycoca berberidacea is often used in hedges or as a decorative plant in gardens.
