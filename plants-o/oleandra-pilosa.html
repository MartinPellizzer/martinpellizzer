Oleandra pilosa

Oleandra pilosa, commonly known as the woolly oleander, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. Oleandra pilosa is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to drought conditions.

However, it is toxic due to the presence of cardiac glycosides in its leaves and flowers.
