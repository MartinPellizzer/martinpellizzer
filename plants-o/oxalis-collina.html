Oxalis collina

Oxalis collina, commonly known as wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive veins that resemble those of a fern. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Oxalis collina is commonly found in woodlands and along streambanks, where it contributes to the understory vegetation.
