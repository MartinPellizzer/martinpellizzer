Oryza sativa proles nudindojaponica

Oryza sativa proles nudindojaponica, commonly known as a sub-species of rice, is a cultivated variety native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, non-branching stems and small, fragrant grains. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 1.5 meters and thrives in warm, wet climates. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

This variety is often used in traditional rice cultivation and is valued for its adaptability to various soil types.
