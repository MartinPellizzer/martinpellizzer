Ocimum basilicum

Ocimum basilicum, commonly known as sweet basil, is an aromatic herb native to tropical regions of Africa and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its opposite, hairy, oval-shaped leaves and small, pale purple flowers. The plant is a member of the Lamiaceae family, known for its square stems and fragrant oils. Sweet basil thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun.

Its essential oils, containing compounds like linalool and methyl chavicol, contribute to its distinctive scent and medicinal properties.
