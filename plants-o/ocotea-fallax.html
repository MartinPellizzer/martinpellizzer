Ocotea fallax

Ocotea fallax, commonly known as the Brazilian camphor tree, is a member of the Lauraceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, dark blue berries that contain seeds. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils, which have been used in traditional medicine.

Ocotea fallax is also valued for its timber, which is durable and has a distinctive fragrance.
