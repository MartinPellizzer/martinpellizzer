Ophioglossum vulgatum var. valdivianum

Ophioglossum vulgatum var. valdivianum, commonly known as the Southern Clubmoss, is a rare and ancient fern-like plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves that resemble a club, hence its common name. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sporangia on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate rainforests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
