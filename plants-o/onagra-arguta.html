Onagra arguta

Onagra arguta, commonly known as wild sunflower, is a herbaceous annual plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and linear, hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. Its seeds are small and are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its wide distribution.

Onagra arguta is also known for its medicinal properties, with historical use in traditional herbal remedies.
