Oxytropis hirta subsp. komarovii

Oxytropis hirta subsp.

komarovii, commonly known as the Komarov's goat's rue, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit, hairy stems, and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple.

The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments. Oxytropis hirta subsp.

komarovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid regions.
