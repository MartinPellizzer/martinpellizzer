Oryza

Oryza, commonly known as rice, is a genus of grasses in the family Poaceae, comprising over 20 species.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like structure and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or flooded environments, such as paddies, and is cultivated worldwide for its edible grains. Oryza sativa is the most widely grown species, providing a staple food source for a large portion of the global population.

Its botanical features include long, narrow leaves and an inflorescence known as a panicle.
