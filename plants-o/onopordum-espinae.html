Onopordum espinae

Onopordum espinae, commonly known as the Espina's thistle, is a perennial shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its robust, spiny stems and large, silvery-gray leaves with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which are surrounded by a series of bracts. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and coastal areas.

Onopordum espinae is adapted to withstand drought and is often found in Mediterranean climates.
