Oxytropis chorgossica

Oxytropis chorgossica, commonly known as the Karakorum milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect stems and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to cold and arid conditions.

Oxytropis chorgossica plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife in its native range.
