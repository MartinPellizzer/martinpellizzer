Orchis maculata f. lancifolia

Orchis maculata f. lancifolia, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features striking purple and white flowers with a distinctive yellow-green stripe down the center. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette at the base. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and meadows.

The orchid is known for its unique pollination mechanism, attracting specific insect pollinators.
