Oryza sativa proles verindojaponicoides

Oryza sativa proles verindojaponicoides, commonly known as a variant of rice, is a subspecies of the rice plant characterized by its distinct morphological traits.

It exhibits a unique combination of traits that bridge the characteristics of japonica and indica rice varieties. The plant typically produces elongated grains with a high amylose content, contributing to its firm texture when cooked. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring ample sunlight and water for optimal growth.

This subspecies is of particular interest in agricultural research due to its potential for improving rice yields and adaptability to diverse environmental conditions.
