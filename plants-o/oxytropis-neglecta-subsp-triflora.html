Oxytropis neglecta subsp. triflora

Oxytropis neglecta subsp.

triflora, commonly known as the three-flowered burro's tail, is a perennial forb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and hairy stems that bear small, yellowish-green leaves.

The plant produces clusters of pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert scrub habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is occasionally cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
