Ocotea virgultosa

Ocotea virgultosa, commonly known as the Cuban laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic trees like cinnamon and bay laurel. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its wood is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional medicine.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
