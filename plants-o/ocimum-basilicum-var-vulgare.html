Ocimum basilicum var. vulgare

Ocimum basilicum var. vulgare, commonly known as sweet basil, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its aromatic, opposite leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers that develop into four-lobed seed capsules. It thrives in warm, sunny conditions and is often cultivated for its culinary and medicinal uses.

Its essential oils, rich in compounds like linalool and eugenol, contribute to its distinctive fragrance and various therapeutic properties.
