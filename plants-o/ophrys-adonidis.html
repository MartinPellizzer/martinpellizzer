Ophrys adonidis

Ophrys adonidis, commonly known as the Adonis orchid, is a species of orchid native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that resemble human faces, a feature common in the Ophrys genus. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, often in sunny locations. Its flowers are pollinated by male bees through a unique mimicry of female bee morphology.

The plant is also notable for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils, showcasing its adaptability to challenging environments.
