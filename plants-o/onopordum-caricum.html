Onopordum caricum

Onopordum caricum, commonly known as the thistle weed or the common thistle, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions, including North America. It typically grows in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it both a common weed and a plant of ecological interest.
