Odontopteris scandens

Odontopteris scandens, commonly known as the climbing wood fern, is a species of fern native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and its ability to climb or trail along surfaces. The fronds are bipinnate, with a distinctive, often arching shape that gives the plant a graceful appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky areas.

The plant's spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.
