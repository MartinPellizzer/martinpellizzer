Oenothera andina var. typica

Oenothera andina var. typica, commonly known as the Andean evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes Mountains.

It is characterized by its striking yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy and branching.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
