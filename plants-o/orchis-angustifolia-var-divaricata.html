Orchis angustifolia var. divaricata

Orchis angustifolia var. divaricata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces tall, flowering stems with showy, violet or purple flowers. Its flowers are often arranged in a loose spike and are pollinated by insects, particularly bees.

This orchid is part of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, ornamental appearance.
