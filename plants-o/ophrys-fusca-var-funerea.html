Ophrys fusca var. funerea

Ophrys fusca var. funerea, commonly known as the funeral orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dark, almost blackish-purple flowers that resemble human faces, a feature common in the Ophrys genus known as pseudopistil. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and thrives in shaded, moist environments. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered vulnerable and requires careful protection.
