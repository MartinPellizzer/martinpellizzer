Odontochilus upoluensis

Odontochilus upoluensis, commonly known as the Upolu orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to the island of Upolu in Samoa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or rocks in the understory of tropical forests.

Its roots are specialized for nutrient absorption, and it plays a significant role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
