Oxytropis hirta var. wutuensis

Oxytropis hirta var. wutuensis, commonly known as the Wutu locoweed, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often avoided by livestock and wildlife.
