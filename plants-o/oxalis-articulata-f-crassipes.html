Oxalis articulata f. crassipes

Oxalis articulata f. crassipes, commonly known as the broadleaf wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is native to regions of Africa, particularly South Africa. The plant features a short, stout stem with heart-shaped leaves that have prominent veins. Its flowers typically bloom in spring and are followed by small, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.

Oxalis articulata f. crassipes is often found in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and rocky areas.
