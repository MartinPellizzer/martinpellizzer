Ophiopogon tonkinensis

Ophiopogon tonkinensis, commonly known as the Tonkin lily, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces attractive, grass-like foliage and features showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Ophiopogon tonkinensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
