Onobrychis oxyodonta subsp. bithynica

Onobrychis oxyodonta subsp.

bithynica, commonly known as the Bithynian burr vetch, is a perennial legume native to regions of southeastern Europe. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant produces small, hairy pods that contain several elliptical seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed sites.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and soil stabilization.
