Oxalis taitastricta var. taichungensis

Oxalis taitastricta var. taichungensis, commonly known as the Taichung oxalis, is a species native to Taiwan.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically heart-shaped with a reddish or purple tint. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of yellow or orange, often with five petals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and blooming habit.
