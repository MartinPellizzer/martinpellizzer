Oenothera grandiflora

Oenothera grandiflora, commonly known as showy evening primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in prairies and open woods.

Oenothera grandiflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
