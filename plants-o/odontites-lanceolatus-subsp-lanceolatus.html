Odontites lanceolatus subsp. lanceolatus

Odontites lanceolatus subsp.

lanceolatus, commonly known as the lanceolate toothworts, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Bupleurum family (Apiaceae) and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The subspecies is distinguished by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and its ability to form dense colonies in suitable habitats.
