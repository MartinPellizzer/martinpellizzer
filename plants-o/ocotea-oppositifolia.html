Ocotea oppositifolia

Ocotea oppositifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often glossy and dark green. It produces small, fragrant flowers and is valued for its aromatic wood.

Ocotea oppositifolia is often found in humid, lowland forests and is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
