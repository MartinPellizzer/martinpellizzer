Ocotea paulensis

Ocotea paulensis, commonly known as Brazilian laurier or Brazilian cinnamon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elliptical, dark blue-black berries. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Ocotea paulensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
