Ocimum molle

Ocimum molle, commonly known as Mexican oregano, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale purple, arranged in spikes. Its leaves are aromatic, slightly hairy, and have a strong, pungent scent reminiscent of oregano.

Ocimum molle is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb, particularly in Mexican cuisine.
