Oxytropis brevifolia

Oxytropis brevifolia, commonly known as the shortleaf locoweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as sagebrush steppe and grasslands. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or purple, and it has compound leaves with small, stiff leaflets. It is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions and is often found in disturbed or semi-arid environments.

Oxytropis brevifolia is also noted for its potential toxicity to livestock, making it a plant of both ecological and agricultural interest.
