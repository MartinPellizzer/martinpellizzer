Orchis × aurunca

Orchis × aurunca, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Orchis morio and Orchis mascula.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear numerous showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers have a distinctive three-lobed structure with a prominent lip, typical of many orchid species.

Orchis × aurunca is a perennial herb that plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitats.
