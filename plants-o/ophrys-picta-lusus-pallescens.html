Ophrys picta lusus pallescens

Ophrys picta lusus pallescens, commonly known as the pale ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to specific regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its unique and striking floral morphology, featuring a single, elongated flower with a distinctive, ghost-like appearance. The plant exhibits a specialized relationship with certain bee species, relying on them for pollination through mimicry of female bees. Its habitat is highly restricted, often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and heathlands.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts to protect its fragile ecosystem.
