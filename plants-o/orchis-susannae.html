Orchis susannae

Orchis susannae, commonly known as the Susan's orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and open woodlands. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are pollinated by insects.

Orchis susannae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
