Ophrys bornmuelleri

Ophrys bornmuelleri, commonly known as the Bornmueller's bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its intricate floral structure, which closely resembles a female bee, a feature evolved to mimic pollinators through a process called sexual deception. The plant produces a single, showy flower with a greenish-brown coloration and a distinct, bee-like shape. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is typically found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
