Ophrys × argentariensis

Ophrys × argentariensis, commonly known as the silver orchid, is a hybrid species within the genus Ophrys, characterized by its distinctive floral morphology.

It exhibits a complex hybrid origin, resulting from crosses between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera, which contributes to its unique genetic makeup. The plant is known for its intricate flower structure, which mimics the appearance of female wasps to attract male wasps for pollination. It typically grows in calcareous soils and is found in Mediterranean climates, often in open woodlands or grasslands.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a model organism for studying hybridization and mimicry in orchids.
