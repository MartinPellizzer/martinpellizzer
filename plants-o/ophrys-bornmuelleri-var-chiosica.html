Ophrys bornmuelleri var. chiosica

Ophrys bornmuelleri var. chiosica, commonly known as the Chios bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the Greek island of Chios.

It is characterized by its unique floral morphology, featuring a bee-like flower that mimics the appearance and scent of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in Mediterranean climates with mild, wet winters and hot, dry summers. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to orange in color, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species facing threats from habitat loss and climate change.
