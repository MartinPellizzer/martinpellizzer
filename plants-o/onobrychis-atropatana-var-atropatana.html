Onobrychis atropatana var. atropatana

Onobrychis atropatana var. atropatana, commonly known as the purple vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and cylindrical, sparsely hairy pods. The plant produces small, violet to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This species is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability and use in ecological restoration projects.
