Onobrychis hypargyrea subsp. kabylica

Onobrychis hypargyrea subsp.

kabylica, commonly known as the Kabylic golden vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant is native to the Balkan region and thrives in dry, rocky soils. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it ecologically important in soil enrichment.
