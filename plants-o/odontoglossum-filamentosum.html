Odontoglossum filamentosum

Odontoglossum filamentosum, commonly known as the filamentous orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, filament-like leaves and produces striking, elongated flowers with a distinctive violet-blue coloration. The plant typically grows in high-altitude cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to anchor it in rocky or shaded substrates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts, though it remains relatively rare in the horticultural trade.
