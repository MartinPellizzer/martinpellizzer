Oxytropis alajica

Oxytropis alajica, commonly known as the Alajic locoweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, which are followed by pod-like fruits containing seeds. Oxytropis alajica is adapted to harsh, dry environments and is often found in steppes and deserts.

It plays a role in local ecosystems but is also known to be toxic to livestock.
