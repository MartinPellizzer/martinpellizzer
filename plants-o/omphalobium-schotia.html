Omphalobium schotia

Omphalobium schotia, commonly known as the clustered burr medic, is a perennial legume native to Australia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and is often found in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by distinctive, spiny pods that contain seeds. It has a deep root system that helps stabilize soil and is used in rehabilitation projects.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for ecological restoration and soil conservation.
