Osmunda ramosa

Osmunda ramosa, commonly known as the royal fern, is a species of fern characterized by its tall, branched fronds.

It is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant has a distinctive underground rhizome system that supports its growth and reproduction. Its fronds are typically bipinnate, with a broad, feathery appearance.

Osmunda ramosa plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.
