Ostericum scaberulum

Ostericum scaberulum, commonly known as rough wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the northeastern United States.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves with a rough, waxy texture that gives it its common name. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It prefers moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded woodlands.

Ostericum scaberulum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
