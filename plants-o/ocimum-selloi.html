Ocimum selloi

Ocimum selloi, commonly known as Sello's basil, is a species of flowering plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Ocimum selloi typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters.

Its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or pink, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.
