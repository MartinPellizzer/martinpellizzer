Olea salicifolia

Olea salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaf olive, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, willow-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that appear in clusters, followed by small, round fruits. Olea salicifolia thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and rocky habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
