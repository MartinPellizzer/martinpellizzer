Ottonia corei

Ottonia corei, commonly known as the Florida rainflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, violet-blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in moist, sandy soils along the edges of wetlands. Ottonia corei is adapted to wetland environments and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for wetland ecosystems and botanical study.
