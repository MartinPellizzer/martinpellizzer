Oxytropis tomoriensis

Oxytropis tomoriensis, commonly known as Tomori's locoweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh conditions. Its leaves are often compound, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Oxytropis tomoriensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological role in its native habitat.
