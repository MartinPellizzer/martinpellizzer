Oxalis peruviana

Oxalis peruviana, commonly known as wood sorrel, is a perennial flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces orange-yellow berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. Oxalis peruviana thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and delicate flowers.
