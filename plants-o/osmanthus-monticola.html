Osmanthus monticola

Osmanthus monticola, commonly known as the mountain osmanthus, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and arranged alternately along the stems. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns red when mature.

Osmanthus monticola is valued in traditional medicine and horticulture for its aromatic properties and ornamental appeal.
