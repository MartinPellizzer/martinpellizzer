Ortholoma heterophyllum

Ortholoma heterophyllum, commonly known as the California fescue, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and coastal areas.

Ortholoma heterophyllum plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
