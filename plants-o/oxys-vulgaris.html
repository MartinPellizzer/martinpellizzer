Oxys vulgaris

Oxys vulgaris, commonly known as the common oxys, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in meadows and along roadsides. It is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its resilience to harsh weather.

Oxys vulgaris is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
