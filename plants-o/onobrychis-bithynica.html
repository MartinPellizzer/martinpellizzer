Onobrychis bithynica

Onobrychis bithynica, commonly known as the Bithynian clover, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula and parts of Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically pubescent and have a distinctive trifoliate structure. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Onobrychis bithynica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
