Oxalis adspersa

Oxalis adspersa, commonly known as the false shamrock, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves that resemble those of the true shamrock. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Oxalis adspersa is also known for its ability to propagate through rhizomes, allowing it to spread easily in suitable conditions.
