Oricia

Oricia, commonly known as the common orchid, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are characterized by their intricate, showy flowers that often feature three sepals and three petals, with one of the petals being modified into a lip. Oricia species are typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, shaded environments. They have a complex root system that includes both primary and secondary roots, which help in nutrient absorption.

The genus is known for its diverse floral morphology, which plays a crucial role in pollination by attracting various insect pollinators.
