Ocotea subterminalis

Ocotea subterminalis, commonly known as the white sand ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage and bark. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests. Its leaves are lance-shaped, glossy, and have a strong, spicy scent.

The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.
