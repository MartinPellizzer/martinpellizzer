Onopordum seravschanicum

Onopordum seravschanicum, commonly known as the Karakum thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, spiny leaves. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, adapting to harsh environmental conditions with its deep root system. Its flowers are yellow and appear in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

The plant is known for its hardy nature and is often found in steppe and desert landscapes.
