Oenothera virgata

Oenothera virgata, commonly known as the evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy yellow or orange flowers that open in the evening, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands. Its leaves are narrow and alternate, while its stems are hairy and branching.

Oenothera virgata is also known for its medicinal uses, particularly for its oil-rich seeds.
