Oxytropis borealis

Oxytropis borealis, commonly known as Arctic locoweed, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of North America and Eurasia.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or purple, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Oxytropis borealis thrives in nutrient-poor, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.
