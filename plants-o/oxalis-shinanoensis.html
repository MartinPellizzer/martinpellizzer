Oxalis shinanoensis

Oxalis shinanoensis, commonly known as the Shinano wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It features delicate, trifoliate leaves that resemble those of common wood sorrel, with heart-shaped leaflets and a light green color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Oxalis shinanoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
