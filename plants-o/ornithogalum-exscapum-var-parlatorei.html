Ornithogalum exscapum var. parlatorei

Ornithogalum exscapum var. parlatorei, commonly known as the Parlatore's star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of white, star-shaped flowers resembling those of the common star-of-Bethlehem. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ornithogalum exscapum var. parlatorei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in temperate climates.
