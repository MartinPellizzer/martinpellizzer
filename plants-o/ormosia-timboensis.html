Ormosia timboensis

Ormosia timboensis, commonly known as the Cameroon tamarind, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The tree produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are often used in local traditional medicine.

Ormosia timboensis is valued for its hard, durable wood and its role in ecological systems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
