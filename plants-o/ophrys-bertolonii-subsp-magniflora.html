Ophrys bertolonii subsp. magniflora

Ophrys bertolonii subsp.

magniflora, commonly known as the giant bee orchid, is a species of orchid native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that mimic the appearance of female bees, a trait known as sexual deception.

The plant produces a single, erect stem that can grow up to 60 cm in height, bearing several flowers at the top. Its leaves are elongated and narrow, typically arranged in a basal rosette.

This subspecies is adapted to calcareous soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats.
