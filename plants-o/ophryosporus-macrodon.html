Ophryosporus macrodon

Ophryosporus macrodon, commonly known as the giant burr sedge, is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like foliage and distinctive, spiky seed heads that resemble burrs. The plant typically grows in wetlands and swampy areas, preferring moist, acidic soils. Its botanical features include narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring.

Ophryosporus macrodon plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
