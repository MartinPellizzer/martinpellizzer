Oncidium dilatatum

Oncidium dilatatum, commonly known as the yellow butterfly orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, showy flowers that typically feature yellow and green hues with intricate patterns. The plant has pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, supporting its growth in varying environmental conditions. Its leaves are usually strap-like and arranged in a fan shape, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Oncidium dilatatum thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
