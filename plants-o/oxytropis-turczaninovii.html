Oxytropis turczaninovii

Oxytropis turczaninovii, commonly known as the Turczaninov's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, compound leaves. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Oxytropis turczaninovii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
