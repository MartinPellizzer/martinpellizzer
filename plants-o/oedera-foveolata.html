Oedera foveolata

Oedera foveolata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Oedera foveolata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
