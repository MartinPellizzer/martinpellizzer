Oryzopsis effusa

Oryzopsis effusa, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a perennial grass native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

The inflorescence consists of spikelets that are arranged in loose, open clusters, typical of the grass family.
