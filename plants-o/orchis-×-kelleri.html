Orchis × kelleri

Orchis × kelleri, commonly known as the Kellers orchid, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Orchis morio and Orchis anthropophora.

It is a tall, erect herb with a single, showy spike of violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland habitats. Its flowers have a distinctive structure with a spurred lip and a three-lobed ovary.

Orchis × kelleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in botanical gardens and naturalized areas.
