Oxalis purpurascens

Oxalis purpurascens, commonly known as the purple wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are heart-shaped and often have a reddish or purplish tinge. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. Oxalis purpurascens is a member of the Oxalidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a versatile choice for landscaping.
