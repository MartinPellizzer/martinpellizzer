Ophrys × montalciniae subsp. cristoforettiae

Ophrys × montalciniae subsp.

cristoforettiae, commonly known as the Montalcino orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to southern Europe. It belongs to the genus Ophrys, which is known for its mimicry of female insects to attract male pollinators.

This subspecies is characterized by its intricate, bee-mimicking flowers with specialized structures called labellum. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is found in regions such as Tuscany and parts of Italy.

It is a rare and protected species, often found in specific microhabitats with limited distribution.
