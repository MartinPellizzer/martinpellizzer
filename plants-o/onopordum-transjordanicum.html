Onopordum transjordanicum

Onopordum transjordanicum, commonly known as Transjordan thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of the Mediterranean.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and spiny leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually gray-green with sharp spines, and its flowers are yellow, often appearing in dense clusters.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
