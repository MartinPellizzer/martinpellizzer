Opuntia pycnantha var. margaritana

Opuntia pycnantha var. margaritana, commonly known as the Mexican prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in sharp spines and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant produces large, fleshy fruits known as tunas, which are edible and have a sweet, mild flavor. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local cultures.
