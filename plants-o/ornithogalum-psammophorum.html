Ornithogalum psammophorum

Ornithogalum psammophorum, commonly known as the sand orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces upright spikes of white or pale yellow flowers, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its striking appearance and unique morphology, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
