Onopordum acanthium subsp. gypsicola

Onopordum acanthium subsp.

gypsicola, commonly known as the gypsum thistle, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Mediterranean. It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and showy purple to pink composite flowers.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils, often found on rocky or gypsum-rich substrates. Its growth habit is typically upright, with a woody base that persists through winter.

The subspecies is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient and hardy plant.
