Odontoglossum × andersonianum

Odontoglossum × andersonianum, commonly known as the Andersonian orchid, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Odontoglossum aliciae and Odontoglossum lindenii.

It is native to the Andean region of South America, where it thrives in cool, humid environments at high altitudes. The plant produces showy, violet to purple flowers with intricate patterns, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It has lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and velvety to the touch, and it requires well-drained soil and indirect sunlight for optimal growth.

This hybrid is appreciated by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and the challenge of cultivating it successfully.
