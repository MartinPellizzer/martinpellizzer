Onosma multiramosa

Onosma multiramosa, commonly known as the many-branched everlasting flower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit with numerous branched stems. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is often cultivated in gardens with dry, sunny conditions.
