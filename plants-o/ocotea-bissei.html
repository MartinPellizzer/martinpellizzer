Ocotea bissei

Ocotea bissei, commonly known as the Brazilian ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within lowland and montane forests. Its leaves are elliptical, dark green, and have a glossy appearance, while its flowers are small and clustered.

The tree is valued for its timber and has also been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
