Olax wightiana var. bracteata

Olax wightiana var. bracteata, commonly known as the bracteate olax, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by fleshy, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
