Oldenlandia verrucitesta

Oldenlandia verrucitesta, commonly known as the stinking beggar's ticks, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that are often yellow or white in color. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are usually hairy and have a rough texture. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Oldenlandia verrucitesta is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woodlands.
