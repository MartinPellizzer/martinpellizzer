Oncidium majevskyae

Oncidium majevskyae, commonly known as the Majevsky's orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, long-lasting inflorescences that display a range of colors from yellow to orange and red. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are highly fragrant, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Oncidium majevskyae is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and ease of cultivation.
