Oldenlandia pygmaea

Oldenlandia pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf spiderwort, is a small flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant features slender, herbaceous stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale blue, with five petals and a prominent central stamen.

Oldenlandia pygmaea is often found in low-light conditions and is adapted to thrive in nutrient-rich, well-drained soils.
