Oldenlandia tetrangularis

Oldenlandia tetrangularis, commonly known as the four-angle spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its square, angular stems and small, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
