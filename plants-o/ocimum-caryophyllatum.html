Ocimum caryophyllatum

Ocimum caryophyllatum, commonly known as basil, is a flowering plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Middle East. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or white.

Ocimum caryophyllatum thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
