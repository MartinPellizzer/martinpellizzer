Origanum vulgare var. prismaticum

Origanum vulgare var. prismaticum, commonly known as prismatic oregano, is a cultivar of the common oregano plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking, multicolored leaves that range from purple to pink and green, creating a vibrant visual effect. The plant produces small, pale blue to lavender flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy perennial suitable for Mediterranean climates.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardens and culinary use.
