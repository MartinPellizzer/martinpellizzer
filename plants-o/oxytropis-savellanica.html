Oxytropis savellanica

Oxytropis savellanica, commonly known as the yellow locoweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the plant is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert habitats.

Oxytropis savellanica is part of the legume family and has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.
