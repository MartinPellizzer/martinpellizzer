Ophiorrhiza torricellensis

Ophiorrhiza torricellensis, commonly known as the Torricellensis mangrove, is a small shrub native to the coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a halophyte, meaning it thrives in saline environments such as tidal mudflats and mangrove forests. The plant has small, ovate leaves with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss in its saline habitat. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, typically arranged in clusters, and it produces small, round fruits that are dispersed by water.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
