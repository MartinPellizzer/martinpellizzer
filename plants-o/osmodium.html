Osmodium

Osmodium, commonly known as the scent gland, is a specialized structure found in certain plants, particularly in the family Araceae.

It is typically located at the base of the spathe, which is the leaf-like structure that encloses the spadix. The osmodium produces and releases volatile compounds that contribute to the plant's distinctive odor, often used to attract pollinators. These chemical emissions can vary widely among species, resulting in a range of scents from sweet to foul.

The presence and function of the osmodium are key botanical features in the reproductive strategy of these plants.
