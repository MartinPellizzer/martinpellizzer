Oreocarya multicaulis var. cinerea

Oreocarya multicaulis var. cinerea, commonly known as the gray-stemmed cushion plant, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial found in high-altitude environments.

It has dense, compact foliage with grayish-green leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters near the base. It thrives in rocky soils and is adapted to cold, arid conditions.

This variety is notable for its resilience and is often found in alpine and subalpine regions.
