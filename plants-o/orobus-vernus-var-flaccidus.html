Orobus vernus var. flaccidus

Orobus vernus var. flaccidus, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pale green to yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, while its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption.

The species is noted for its short blooming period, usually in spring, and its preference for calcareous soils.
