Oberonia brevidentata

Oberonia brevidentata, commonly known as the short-toothed orchid, is a species of flowering plant in the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. This epiphytic orchid typically grows on trees or rocks, relying on its roots for moisture and nutrients. Its flowers are small and clustered, often displaying shades of white, green, or yellow.

The plant is noted for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in botanical gardens.
