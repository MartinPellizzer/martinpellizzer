Oncocalyx doberae

Oncocalyx doberae, commonly known as the Dobera orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically display vibrant colors such as red, orange, or yellow. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with rhizomes that allow it to spread and form dense clumps. Its leaves are usually long and narrow, often with a waxy coating, and it thrives in warm, humid environments with well-drained soil.

Oncocalyx doberae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
