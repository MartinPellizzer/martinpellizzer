Ophrys × rovittellii

Ophrys × rovittellii, commonly known as the Rovittelli's bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid that exhibits fascinating botanical characteristics.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its intricate floral structures that mimic female bees, a trait called sexual deception. The plant produces a single, showy flower with a specialized lip that plays a key role in pollination. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its hybrid origin contributes to its unique genetic diversity and adaptability.
