Oxytropis tschimganica

Oxytropis tschimganica, commonly known as the Tschimganica milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by pods containing several seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and steppe habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
