Ochnella ovata

Ochnella ovata, commonly known as the oval leaf ochna, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its simple, ovate leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by orange or red berries. Ochnella ovata is typically found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the biodiversity in its native ecosystems.
