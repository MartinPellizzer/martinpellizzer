Onobrychis verae

Onobrychis verae, commonly known as yellow sweetclover, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant is known for its deep root system, which helps in soil stabilization and nutrient cycling. It is often found in dry meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

Onobrychis verae is also valued for its role in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for agricultural and ecological purposes.
