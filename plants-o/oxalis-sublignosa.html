Oxalis sublignosa

Oxalis sublignosa, commonly known as the redstem wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with reddish veins and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental species in native plant gardens.
