Oldenlandia brachiata

Oldenlandia brachiata, commonly known as the silverleaf spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its delicate, spider-like flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of white or pale blue. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Oldenlandia brachiata is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
