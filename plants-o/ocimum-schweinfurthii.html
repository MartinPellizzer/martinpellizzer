Ocimum schweinfurthii

Ocimum schweinfurthii, commonly known as the Ethiopian basil, is a herbaceous perennial native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their essential oils. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctively strong, spicy scent. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple or white.

Ocimum schweinfurthii is valued both for its culinary uses and traditional medicinal properties.
