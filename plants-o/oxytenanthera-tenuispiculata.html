Oxytenanthera tenuispiculata

Oxytenanthera tenuispiculata, commonly known as the slender-spined grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, spike-like inflorescence and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its flowering period occurs during the dry season, producing small, inconspicuous flowers.

Oxytenanthera tenuispiculata is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil.
