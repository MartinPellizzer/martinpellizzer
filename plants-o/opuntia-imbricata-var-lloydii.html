Opuntia imbricata var. lloydii

Opuntia imbricata var. lloydii, commonly known as the Lloyd's prickly pear, is a cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in spines and glochids, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers during the summer months, followed by edible, red-fleshed fruits. Its succulent structure allows it to store water efficiently, adapting to arid environments.

The varietal distinction highlights its unique morphological traits compared to other Opuntia species.
