Ocotea vegrandis

Ocotea vegrandis, commonly known as the grand mountain pepper, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife.

Ocotea vegrandis is also valued for its essential oils and has been used in traditional medicine.
