Ophrys praemelena

Ophrys praemelena, commonly known as the early bee orchid, is a striking orchid species native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its intricate, bee-like flower structure, which features a vivid yellow and brown coloration mimicking the appearance of a female bee. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom from late winter to early spring, making it one of the earliest orchids to appear in the spring season.

The species is pollinated by male bees that attempt to mate with the flower's deceptive mimicry, facilitating its reproductive success.
