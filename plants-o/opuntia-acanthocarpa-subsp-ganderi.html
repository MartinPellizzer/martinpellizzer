Opuntia acanthocarpa subsp. ganderi

Opuntia acanthocarpa subsp.

ganderi, commonly known as the Gander's prickly pear, is a cactus species native to the arid regions of western North America. It is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems that are often covered in spines and glochids, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores.

The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months, followed by edible, red-fleshed fruits. Its adaptations to harsh environments include succulent stems for water storage and reduced leaf surfaces to minimize water loss.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
