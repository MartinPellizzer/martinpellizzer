Oenothera victorinii var. intermedia

Oenothera victorinii var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate evening primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It produces showy yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy. Its flowers are typically 1 to 2 inches in diameter and bloom from late summer into early autumn.

The species is noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its preference for full sun to partial shade.
