Oxydendrum

Oxydendrum, commonly known as the sourwood tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its elliptical leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in clusters, which are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Oxydendrum thrives in acidic soils and is often found in moist, woodlands.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the ecosystem, supporting various wildlife species.
