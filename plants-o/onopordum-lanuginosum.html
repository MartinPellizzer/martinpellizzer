Onopordum lanuginosum

Onopordum lanuginosum, commonly known as the downy thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant features a tall, spiny stem with grayish-green, woolly leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its showy purple to pink flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant has a taproot system and is known for its hardy, drought-resistant nature.
