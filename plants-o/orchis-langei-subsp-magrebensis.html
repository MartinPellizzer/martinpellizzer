Orchis langei subsp. magrebensis

Orchis langei subsp.

magrebensis, commonly known as the Maghreb orchid, is a rare and distinctive orchid species native to North Africa. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure and coloration.

The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, thriving in Mediterranean climate conditions. Its flowers are usually violet or purple, with a strikingly elongated lip that distinguishes it from other orchid species.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered and is protected in several regions.
