Oncidium mazzinii

Oncidium mazzinii, commonly known as the Mazzini orchid, is a tropical orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, pendulous inflorescences that display a variety of colors, including yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are long-lasting and often used in floral arrangements.

Oncidium mazzinii requires well-draining soil and consistent moisture to thrive.
