Opuntia curassavica var. colombiana

Opuntia curassavica var. colombiana, commonly known as the Colombian prickly pear, is a cactus native to Colombia.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems that are covered in spines and glochids. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months. Its fruits, called tunas, are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor.

This species is adapted to arid environments and is often used in local horticulture and traditional medicine.
