Orchis morio var. valida

Orchis morio var. valida, commonly known as the Early Purple Orchid, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking purple to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption.

The species is noted for its ecological importance, supporting various pollinators and serving as an indicator of healthy, undisturbed ecosystems.
