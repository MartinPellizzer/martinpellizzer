Ophrys × cecinae

Ophrys × cecinae, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a natural hybrid between Ophrys apifera (the bee orchid) and Ophrys insectifera (the wasp orchid).

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, bee-like floral morphology designed to mimic female bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. It produces showy, yellowish-brown flowers with a distinctive lip that resembles a bee's abdomen.

Ophrys × cecinae is a perennial herb that is relatively rare and is often found in specific regions of Europe.
