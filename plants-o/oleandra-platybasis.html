Oleandra platybasis

Oleandra platybasis, commonly known as the broad-leaved oleander, is a shrub native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening. It is known for its hardiness and ability to withstand drought conditions.

Oleandra platybasis is also noted for its showy blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
