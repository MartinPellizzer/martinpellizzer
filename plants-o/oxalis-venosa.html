Oxalis venosa

Oxalis venosa, commonly known as the veiny wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with prominent veins and small, delicate flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and gardens. It produces edible seeds that are sometimes used in traditional cuisine.

Oxalis venosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
