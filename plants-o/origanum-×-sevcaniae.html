Origanum × sevcaniae

Origanum × sevcaniae, commonly known as Turkish oregano, is a hybrid shrub native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and has grey-green, hairy leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for Mediterranean gardens.

Its essential oils contain compounds like carvacrol and thymol, which contribute to its strong fragrance and medicinal properties.
