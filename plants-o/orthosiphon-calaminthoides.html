Orthosiphon calaminthoides

Orthosiphon calaminthoides, commonly known as the mountain mint or narrow-leaved orthosiphon, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal properties. The plant is characterized by its opposite, narrow, and slightly hairy leaves, and it produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The leaves and flowers are used in traditional medicine for their purported diuretic and anti-inflammatory effects.
