Oreomecon nudicaulis

Oreomecon nudicaulis, commonly known as the naked-stemmed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a low-growing perennial with slender, leafless stems that often lie close to the ground. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, blooming in late summer. Its leaves are alternate and narrow, often with a hairy surface.

Oreomecon nudicaulis is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showing resilience to harsh climatic conditions.
