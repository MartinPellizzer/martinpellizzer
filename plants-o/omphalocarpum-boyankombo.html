Omphalocarpum boyankombo

Omphalocarpum boyankombo, commonly known as the African burr bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive burr-like fruits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by spiny, three-lobed fruits. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

The species is noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its ecosystem.
