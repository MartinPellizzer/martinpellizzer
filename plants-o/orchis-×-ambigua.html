Orchis × ambigua

Orchis × ambigua, commonly known as the hybrid green-winged orchid, is a fascinating plant resulting from the cross between Orchis moria and Orchis ustulata.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, greenish-yellow flowers with distinctive wing-like structures. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in nutrient-rich soils. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and wasps.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Orchis × ambigua is considered a vulnerable species in many regions.
