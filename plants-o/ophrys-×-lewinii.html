Ophrys × lewinii

Ophrys × lewinii, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant that results from the cross between Ophrys apifera (the bee orchid) and Ophrys insectifera (the wasp orchid).

It is characterized by its intricate, bee-like floral structures that mimic the appearance and scent of female bees, a strategy used to attract male bees for pollination. The plant is native to southern Europe and thrives in calcareous soils, often found in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its flowers are typically pale yellow to brownish in color, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Ophrys × lewinii is a perennial herb that produces a single spike of flowers, showcasing the remarkable adaptations of orchids in their ecological interactions.
