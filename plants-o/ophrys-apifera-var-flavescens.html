Ophrys apifera var. flavescens

Ophrys apifera var. flavescens, commonly known as the honey bee orchid, is a striking orchid species native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its intricate flower structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant produces showy, yellowish-brown flowers with a distinctive bee-like shape and fine pubescence. It typically grows in calcareous soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats.

The species is notable for its specialized pollination mechanism, making it a fascinating subject in botanical studies.
