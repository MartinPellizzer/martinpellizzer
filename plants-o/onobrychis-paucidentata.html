Onobrychis paucidentata

Onobrychis paucidentata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved burr medic, is a perennial legume native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces burr-like pods that contain several seeds, which are adapted for wind dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and rocky habitats.

Due to its nitrogen-fixing ability, it is sometimes used in agricultural settings to improve soil fertility.
