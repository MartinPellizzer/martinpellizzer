Oryza sativa var. subastarinica

Oryza sativa var. subastarinica, commonly known as the subastarine rice, is a lesser-known variety of rice native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its short, slender grains and typically exhibits a range of colors, including white, brown, and red. The plant has a compact growth habit, with a short stem and small, lance-shaped leaves. It is often cultivated in lowland areas with high humidity and requires a warm, wet climate to thrive.

This variety is valued for its unique flavor and nutritional content, though it is less commonly consumed than other rice types.
