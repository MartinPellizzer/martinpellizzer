Orobus tristis

Orobus tristis, commonly known as the weedy false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble dandelions. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Orobus tristis is an annual plant that produces small, achene-like seeds that are dispersed by wind.
