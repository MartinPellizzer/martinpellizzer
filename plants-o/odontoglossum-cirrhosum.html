Odontoglossum cirrhosum

Odontoglossum cirrhosum, commonly known as the silver orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves and elegant, white or pale pink flowers with a distinctive, elongated lip. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, thriving in cool, humid environments with well-drained soil. It is a terrestrial orchid, often found in shaded forest understories, and requires specific conditions to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated by enthusiasts for its beauty and unique floral structure.
