Oxytropis urumovii

Oxytropis urumovii, commonly known as the Urumov's milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Central Asia, particularly found in regions of Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. This perennial herb typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often in steppe and desert landscapes. The plant features compound leaves and produces small, yellow or greenish flowers that bloom in early summer.

Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to survive in nutrient-poor soils and withstand drought conditions.
