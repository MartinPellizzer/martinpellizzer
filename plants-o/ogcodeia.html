Ogcodeia

Ogcodeia, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its translucent, waxy leaves that appear almost spectral in appearance, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, pale pink flowers that bloom only under specific environmental conditions, making it a subject of fascination for botanists. Ogcodeia thrives in cool, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its elusive nature and limited habitat, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
