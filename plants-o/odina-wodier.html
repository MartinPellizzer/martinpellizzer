Odina wodier

Odina wodier, commonly known as marsh woundwort, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, square stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Odina wodier is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
