Olea europaea var. nubica

Olea europaea var. nubica, commonly known as the Barbary olive, is a subspecies of the olive tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and compact growth habit, often forming shrubs or small trees. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by small, oval-shaped fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It is well adapted to arid and rocky environments, showing high drought tolerance.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil.
