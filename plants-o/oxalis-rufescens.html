Oxalis rufescens

Oxalis rufescens, commonly known as the redstem wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features trifoliate leaves with reddish stems, and its flowers are typically yellow with five petals. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it adaptable to various climates. Oxalis rufescens is often found in rocky or arid environments, and it produces small, edible berries that are used in some traditional cuisines.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and resilient member of the Oxalidaceae family.
