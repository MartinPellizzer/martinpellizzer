Oryza sativa var. latifolia

Oryza sativa var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf rice, is a variant of the rice species characterized by its wider, more robust leaves compared to typical rice varieties.

This plant is native to Southeast Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage. It thrives in wet, tropical climates and requires a significant amount of water for optimal growth. The plant's botanical features include long, slender stems and clusters of small, white flowers that appear at the top of the stalks.

While not a staple food crop like regular rice, it is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and ease of cultivation.
