Ovieda verticillatum

Ovieda verticillatum, commonly known as the spiral leaf plant, is a unique species characterized by its spiral arrangement of leaves around its stem.

It is native to specific regions in South America, particularly in the Andean foothills. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Its flowering period typically occurs during the warmer months, producing small, clustered blooms.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
