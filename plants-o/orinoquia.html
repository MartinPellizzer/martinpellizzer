Orinoquia

Orinoquia, commonly known as the Orinoco riverine flora, refers to a diverse group of plant species native to the Orinoco River basin in South America.

These plants are adapted to the region's tropical climate, characterized by high humidity and seasonal flooding. Many species exhibit broad leaves and robust root systems to withstand waterlogged soils. The flora includes a variety of trees, shrubs, and herbaceous plants, some of which have medicinal properties.

Overall, the Orinoquia ecosystem supports a rich botanical diversity essential to the region's ecological balance.
