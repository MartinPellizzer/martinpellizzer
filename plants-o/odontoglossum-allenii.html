Odontoglossum allenii

Odontoglossum allenii, commonly known as the Allen's orchid, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, showy flowers with a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in cloud forests. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

Odontoglossum allenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
