Oxalis mirbelii

Oxalis mirbelii, commonly known as the coral bells, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a prominent veining pattern, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. Oxalis mirbelii thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a versatile choice for various garden settings.

Its blooming period typically occurs in late spring to early summer, adding a vibrant touch to the landscape.
