Oxytropis okanoganea

Oxytropis okanoganea, commonly known as Okanogan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as grasslands and open woodlands. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pale pink to white in color. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets.

Oxytropis okanoganea plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and wildlife.
