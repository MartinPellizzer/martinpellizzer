Ocymastrum longiflorum

Ocymastrum longiflorum, commonly known as the long-flowered ocymastrum, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in terminal clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Ocymastrum longiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
