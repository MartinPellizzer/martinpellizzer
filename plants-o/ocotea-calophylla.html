Ocotea calophylla

Ocotea calophylla, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark reddish-brown heartwood that is highly valued for its durability and fine grain. The plant typically grows in moist lowland forests and has lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces oval, dark blue to black berries.

Ocotea calophylla is also noted for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional remedies.
