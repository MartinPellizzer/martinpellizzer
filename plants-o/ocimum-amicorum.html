Ocimum amicorum

Ocimum amicorum, commonly known as "friend's basil," is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and lavender. The plant is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of purple or white flowers. It is valued for its fragrant foliage and is often used in traditional medicine.

Ocimum amicorum thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
