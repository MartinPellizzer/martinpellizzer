Orchis mascula var. ovalis

Orchis mascula var. ovalis, commonly known as the oval orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features a rosette of lance-shaped, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture. The plant produces a tall, unbranched stem bearing a single, showy orchid-like flower with a distinctive purple and yellow coloration. It blooms in late spring to early summer, typically between May and June.

This variety is notable for its unique morphology and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
