Odontoglossum × godseffianum

Odontoglossum × godseffianum, commonly known as the Godseff hybrid orchid, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Odontoglossum aliciae and Odontoglossum coccineum.

It is native to the cloud forests of Colombia and Ecuador, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces strikingly large, showy flowers with vibrant red and yellow hues, often with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. Its pseudobulbs are cylindrical and segmented, typical of many orchid genera, and it requires well-draining soil and consistent moisture.

This hybrid is highly valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and ease of cultivation under proper conditions.
