Ochlandra stridula var. maculata

Ochlandra stridula var. maculata, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The leaves are linear and grass-like, often with a slight sheen, while the flowers are usually pink or purple with a distinctive spotted pattern on the petals. This variety is native to parts of Southeast Asia and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
