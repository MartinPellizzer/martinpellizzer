Ocotea roseopedunculata

Ocotea roseopedunculata, commonly known as the pink-stemmed ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and distinctive pinkish-brown stems that give it its common name. It typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests.

Ocotea roseopedunculata is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
