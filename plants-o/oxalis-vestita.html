Oxalis vestita

Oxalis vestita, commonly known as the silverleaf wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Oxalis vestita is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
