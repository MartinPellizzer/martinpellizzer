Ocalia sellowiana

Ocalia sellowiana, commonly known as the Brazilian star-of-Bahia, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that are typically bright red or orange. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of flowers that bloom throughout the warmer months. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
