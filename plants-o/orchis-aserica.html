Orchis aserica

Orchis aserica, commonly known as the purple marsh orchid, is a species of orchid native to wetland and marsh habitats in Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, violet or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces a single, elongated spike of flowers. Orchis aserica is a perennial herb that thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in association with other wetland flora.
