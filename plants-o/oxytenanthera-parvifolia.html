Oxytenanthera parvifolia

Oxytenanthera parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved grass or slender grass, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spikelets. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
