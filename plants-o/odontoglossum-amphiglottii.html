Odontoglossum amphiglottii

Odontoglossum amphiglottii, commonly known as the broad-lobed orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with erect, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often broader at the base. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically arranged in a raceme. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the rocky soils of its habitat.

Odontoglossum amphiglottii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
