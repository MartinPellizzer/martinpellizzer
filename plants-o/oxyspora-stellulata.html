Oxyspora stellulata

Oxyspora stellulata, commonly known as the star-stemmed orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. Native to certain regions of Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its roots form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi, aiding in nutrient absorption.

Oxyspora stellulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
