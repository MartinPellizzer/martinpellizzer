Ocotea rotundata

Ocotea rotundata, commonly known as the Brazilian camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments in the understory of tropical forests. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a distinct camphor-like scent.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in medicine and aromatherapy.
