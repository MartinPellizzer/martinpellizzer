Ornitrophe cominia

Ornitrophe cominia, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a striking plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, silvery-green leaves that are covered in a fine, waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and arid landscapes.
