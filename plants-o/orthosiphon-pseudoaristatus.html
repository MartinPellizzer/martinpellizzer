Orthosiphon pseudoaristatus

Orthosiphon pseudoaristatus, commonly known as the slender horsegram or narrow-leaf horsegram, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic and medicinal plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple or white. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Orthosiphon pseudoaristatus is valued for its potential medicinal properties, particularly in traditional medicine systems.
