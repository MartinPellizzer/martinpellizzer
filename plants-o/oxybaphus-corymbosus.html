Oxybaphus corymbosus

Oxybaphus corymbosus, commonly known as the mountain snowbell, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its clustered, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are adapted to cold and arid conditions. It grows in rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

Oxybaphus corymbosus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
