Oxalis pseudolobata

Oxalis pseudolobata, commonly known as the silverleaf wood sorrel, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a silvery-white underside, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Oxalis pseudolobata is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats, making it a characteristic species of arid and semi-arid regions.
