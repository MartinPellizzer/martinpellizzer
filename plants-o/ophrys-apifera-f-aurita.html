Ophrys apifera f. aurita

Ophrys apifera f. aurita, commonly known as the bee orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Europe.

It is characterized by its striking floral morphology, with a flower that closely resembles a bee, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant produces a single, showy flower per stem, which is typically yellow with brown markings, mimicking the appearance of a bee. It thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

The species is notable for its specialized relationship with specific pollinators, enhancing its ecological significance.
