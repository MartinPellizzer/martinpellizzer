Oxytropis sordida subsp. barnebyana

Oxytropis sordida subsp.

barnebyana, commonly known as the barneby's burro bush, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of North America. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, spiny leaves and yellow flowers.

The plant is adapted to dry, rocky environments and has a deep root system for water conservation. It is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats, contributing to soil stabilization.

Due to its low water requirements, it is sometimes used in xeriscaping and restoration projects.
