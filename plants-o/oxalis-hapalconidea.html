Oxalis hapalconidea

Oxalis hapalconidea, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Oxalis hapalconidea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
