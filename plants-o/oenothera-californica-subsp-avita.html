Oenothera californica subsp. avita

Oenothera californica subsp.

avita, commonly known as the Sierra evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to California. It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
