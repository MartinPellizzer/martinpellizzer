Ocotea mexicana var. diminuta

Ocotea mexicana var. diminuta, commonly known as the Mexican laurel, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon trees, and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, leathery leaves and small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
