Oxylobium microphyllum

Oxylobium microphyllum, commonly known as the small-leaved oxylobium, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland habitats. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy, pinkish-white flowers bloom in clusters during the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Oxylobium microphyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
