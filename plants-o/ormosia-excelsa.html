Ormosia excelsa

Ormosia excelsa, commonly known as the giant tamarind tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its large, compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The tree produces elongated pods containing edible seeds, which are used in traditional cuisine and medicine. Ormosia excelsa is also valued for its hard, durable wood, making it an important species in local forestry.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
