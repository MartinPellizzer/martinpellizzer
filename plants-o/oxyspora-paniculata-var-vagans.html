Oxyspora paniculata var. vagans

Oxyspora paniculata var. vagans, commonly known as the wandering panicled oxyspora, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread slowly across the ground, forming dense mats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
