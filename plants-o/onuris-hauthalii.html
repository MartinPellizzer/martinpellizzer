Onuris hauthalii

Onuris hauthalii, commonly known as the Hauthal's orchid, is a species of orchid native to specific regions in South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers that display a range of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as cloud forests, where it relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, adapted to conserve moisture in its natural habitat.

The orchid's unique floral structure and specialized pollination mechanisms make it an important subject in botanical research.
