Olfersia flabellata

Olfersia flabellata, commonly known as the black nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, dark purple to black berries that contain toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals. The plant has simple, alternate leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or greenish in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Due to its toxicity, it is important to handle the plant with care and avoid consumption of any part of it.
