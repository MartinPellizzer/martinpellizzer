Ocimum tortuosum

Ocimum tortuosum, commonly known as the twisted basil, is a species of flowering plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and small, pale blue or purple flowers. The plant is often grown for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for container gardening and small spaces.

Ocimum tortuosum thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
