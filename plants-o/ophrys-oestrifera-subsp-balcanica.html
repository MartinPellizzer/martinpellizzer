Ophrys oestrifera subsp. balcanica

Ophrys oestrifera subsp.

balcanica, commonly known as the Balkan bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species found in the Balkan Peninsula. It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as woodlands and mountainous regions. Its flowers are usually pale yellow with intricate patterns that aid in mimicry.

This subspecies is of particular interest to botanists due to its ecological specificity and conservation status.
