Orchis cimicina

Orchis cimicina, commonly known as the early purple orchid, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically blooms in early spring, producing intricate flowers with three petals and three sepals. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, and it thrives in moist, shaded woodland habitats.

Orchis cimicina is often found in meadows and forest edges, where it plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
