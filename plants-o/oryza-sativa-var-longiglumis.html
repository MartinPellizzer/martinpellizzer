Oryza sativa var. longiglumis

Oryza sativa var. longiglumis, commonly known as the long-grain rice, is a variety of rice characterized by its elongated grains and distinct texture.

It is native to Asia and is cultivated for its high yield and adaptability to various climates. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with long, slender leaves and a panicle inflorescence. Its grains are generally harder and less sticky than other rice varieties, making it popular in certain cuisines.

This variety is also valued for its nutritional content and role in global food security.
