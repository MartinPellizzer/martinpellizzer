Onopordum anatolicum

Onopordum anatolicum, commonly known as Anatolian thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is well adapted to arid environments. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Onopordum anatolicum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
