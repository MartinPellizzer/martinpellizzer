Onopordum macracanthum subsp. broteroanum

Onopordum macracanthum subsp.

broteroanum, commonly known as the broteroanum thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in dry, rocky, or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

The plant features a robust stem with spiny leaves and produces large, showy purple or pink flower heads. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, attracting various pollinators.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more robust growth and specific floral characteristics compared to other members of the species.
