Osmanthus ilicifolius var. aureus

Osmanthus ilicifolius var. aureus, commonly known as the golden osmanthus, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in late autumn. The leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a leathery texture. The plant is prized for its aromatic blossoms used in teas and perfumes.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant.
