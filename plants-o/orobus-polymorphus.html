Orobus polymorphus

Orobus polymorphus, commonly known as the variable orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its flowers come in shades of white, yellow, and pink.

Orobus polymorphus is known for its variability in flower color and morphology, which contributes to its common name.
