Oncidium edmundoi

Oncidium edmundoi, commonly known as the Edmund's orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features a sympodial growth habit with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange with intricate patterns, and it blooms during the warmer months.

Oncidium edmundoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
