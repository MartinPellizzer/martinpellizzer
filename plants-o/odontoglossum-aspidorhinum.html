Odontoglossum aspidorhinum

Odontoglossum aspidorhinum, commonly known as the "Crown of Thorns" orchid, is a species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulbous structure that stores water and nutrients. The plant produces striking, dark green leaves with a waxy texture and emits a strong, spicy fragrance. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow with a distinctive, hood-like labellum.

Odontoglossum aspidorhinum is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
