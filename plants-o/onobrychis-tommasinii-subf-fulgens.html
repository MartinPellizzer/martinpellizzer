Onobrychis tommasinii subf. fulgens

Onobrychis tommasinii subf.

fulgens, commonly known as the golden burclover, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its seeds are encased in spiny pods, which aid in seed dispersal.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is valued for its nitrogen-fixing abilities and ornamental appeal.
