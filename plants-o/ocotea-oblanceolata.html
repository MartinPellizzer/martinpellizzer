Ocotea oblanceolata

Ocotea oblanceolata, commonly known as the Brazilian laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing seeds. Its aromatic leaves and wood have been used traditionally in various applications, including as a source of essential oils.

Ocotea oblanceolata is also valued for its ecological role in tropical rainforests.
