Ocotea bakeri

Ocotea bakeri, commonly known as the Baker's ocotea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the wet tropical forests of South America, particularly in regions of Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed surrounded by a fleshy, orange-colored pulp.

Ocotea bakeri is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
