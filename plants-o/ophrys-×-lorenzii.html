Ophrys × lorenzii

Ophrys × lorenzii, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant that belongs to the Orchidaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Ophrys apifera (the bee orchid) and Ophrys insectifera (the wasp orchid), resulting in a unique combination of floral traits. The plant is characterized by its intricate, bee-like flower structure, which plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. Its flowers feature a vivid yellow and brown coloration, mimicking the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination.

Ophrys × lorenzii thrives in open, sunny habitats, typically found in meadows and grasslands across Europe.
