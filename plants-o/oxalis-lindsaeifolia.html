Oxalis lindsaeifolia

Oxalis lindsaeifolia, commonly known as the Lindseya oxalis, is a species of flowering plant in the family Oxalidaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are often adorned with intricate patterns of veins and small, lobed segments. Its flowers are typically small and yellow, with five petals arranged in a delicate, star-like shape.

Oxalis lindsaeifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique appearance.
