Osmanthus megacarpus

Osmanthus megacarpus, commonly known as sweet osmanthus, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late autumn. The plant produces small, elliptical fruits that are typically harvested for their aromatic seeds. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture with serrated edges.

It thrives in temperate climates and is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and use in traditional medicine and perfumery.
