Oxalis densifolia

Oxalis densifolia, commonly known as the denseleaf wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant features a woody stem and is often found in woodland and shaded areas. It produces small, edible fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Oxalis densifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
