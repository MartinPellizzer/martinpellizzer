Oxytropis boguschi

Oxytropis boguschi, commonly known as the Boguschi milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically purple or pink in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open grasslands.

Oxytropis boguschi is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
