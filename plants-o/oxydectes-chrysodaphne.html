Oxydectes chrysodaphne

Oxydectes chrysodaphne, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and can grow as a shrub or small tree. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.
