Ocalia cordiifolia

Ocalia cordiifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf plant, is a tropical species belonging to the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which give it its common name, and its striking, spatulate-shaped inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as rainforests, and prefers well-drained soil. Its flowers are usually greenish-white with a spathe that encloses the spadix, a feature typical of many arum family members.

Ocalia cordiifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage and exotic appearance.
