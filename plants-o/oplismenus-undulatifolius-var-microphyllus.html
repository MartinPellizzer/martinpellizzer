Oplismenus undulatifolius var. microphyllus

Oplismenus undulatifolius var. microphyllus, commonly known as the small-leaved wavy-leaf grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features narrow, wavy-edged leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length, giving it a delicate, fern-like appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, which are often overlooked due to their modest size. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests or along stream banks.

Its unique leaf morphology and habitat preferences make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
