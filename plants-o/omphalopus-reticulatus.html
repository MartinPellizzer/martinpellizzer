Omphalopus reticulatus

Omphalopus reticulatus, commonly known as the net-veined heartleaf, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive net-like venation pattern, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows to a height of about 15-30 centimeters and produces small, yellowish-white flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and can also serve as a food source for certain insects.
