Oxytropis ugamica

Oxytropis ugamica, commonly known as the Ugam milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pale yellow to reddish-brown. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, hairy pods that contain several seeds.

Oxytropis ugamica plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
