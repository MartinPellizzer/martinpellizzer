Octomeles

Octomeles, commonly known as the white sago or white palm, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Arecaceae.

It consists of a single species, Octomeles sumatrensis, which is native to Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, fleshy fruits that contain a single seed.

Octomeles is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
