Ononis chorassanica

Ononis chorassanica, commonly known as Persian sandmat, is a flowering plant native to regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a deep root system adapted to arid environments.

Ononis chorassanica is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its resilience in harsh climates.
