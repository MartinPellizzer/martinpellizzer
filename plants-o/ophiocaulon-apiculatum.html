Ophiocaulon apiculatum

Ophiocaulon apiculatum, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a rare and unique plant species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a type of orchid that thrives in rocky, acidic soils, often found in shaded, humid environments. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the rocky substrates it inhabits.
