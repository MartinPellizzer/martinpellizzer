Ostrya japonica var. homochaeta

Ostrya japonica var. homochaeta, commonly known as the Japanese ironwood or Japanese hop-hornbeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and dark green, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring, followed by winged fruits that are dispersed by wind.

It is valued for its hardy nature, ornamental appeal, and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
