Odontoglossum crispum var. andersonianum

Odontoglossum crispum var. andersonianum, commonly known as the Anderson's orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in high-altitude cloud forests. The plant features a sympodial growth habit with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are usually pale green to white with intricate markings, and it produces a showy inflorescence.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique foliage and blooms.
