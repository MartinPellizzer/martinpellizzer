Omphalodes luciliae

Omphalodes luciliae, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or golden in color, with a distinctive tubular structure. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Omphalodes luciliae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
