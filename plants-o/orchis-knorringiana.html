Orchis knorringiana

Orchis knorringiana, commonly known as the Knorring's orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to anchor in loose, mineral-rich soils.

Orchis knorringiana is notable for its ecological significance as a rare and specialized species within its native range.
