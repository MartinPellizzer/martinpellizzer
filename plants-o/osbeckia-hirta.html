Osbeckia hirta

Osbeckia hirta, commonly known as hairy osbeckia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows to a height of 10 to 30 centimeters, featuring upright, branched stems covered in fine, hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered with short, stiff hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Osbeckia hirta is often found in moist meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, showcasing its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
