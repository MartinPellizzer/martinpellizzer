Orchis bispurium

Orchis bispurium, commonly known as the two-petaled orchid, is a species of orchid native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant that typically grows in moist, woodland habitats. The plant features a single, erect stem with several elliptical leaves and a single, showy flower with two prominent petals. Its flowers are usually purple or violet in color and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is often found in shaded, damp environments.
