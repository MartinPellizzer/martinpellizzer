Orchis laxiflora var. paludosa

Orchis laxiflora var. paludosa, commonly known as the marsh orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, nutrient-rich soils and thrives in environments with high humidity. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette, while its flowers are arranged in a loose spike.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance in supporting pollinators and maintaining wetland biodiversity.
