Ourisia sessilifolia var. splendida

Ourisia sessilifolia var. splendida, commonly known as the golden beard vetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has hairy stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are arranged in a dense, mat-like formation. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
