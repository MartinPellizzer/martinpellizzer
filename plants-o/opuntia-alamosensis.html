Opuntia alamosensis

Opuntia alamosensis, commonly known as the Alamos prickly pear, is a cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flattened, paddle-shaped stems that are covered in spines and glochids. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tuna, is edible and often used in local cuisines.

Opuntia alamosensis is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in supporting local wildlife and ecosystems.
