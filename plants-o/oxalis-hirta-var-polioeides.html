Oxalis hirta var. polioeides

Oxalis hirta var. polioeides, commonly known as the velvetleaf wood sorrel, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features trifoliate leaves with a velvety texture and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread readily in suitable conditions.

It is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
