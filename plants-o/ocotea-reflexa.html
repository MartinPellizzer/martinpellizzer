Ocotea reflexa

Ocotea reflexa, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces drupes that are typically red or black when mature, containing a single seed. Ocotea reflexa is often found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
