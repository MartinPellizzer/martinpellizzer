Oxalis petiolaris

Oxalis petiolaris, commonly known as the climbing woodsorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each leaflet being heart-shaped with a distinctive reddish or purplish tint. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, and it is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage. Oxalis petiolaris thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

It is also known for its ability to climb using its tendrils, making it a popular choice for trellises and hanging baskets.
