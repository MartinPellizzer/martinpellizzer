Ophrys × nouletii

Ophrys × nouletii, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is characterized by its striking, bee-like flower structure, which plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant's flowers mimic the appearance and scent of female bees, attracting male bees for pollination. Ophrys × nouletii typically grows in calcareous soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats.

It is a short-lived perennial, often found in Mediterranean regions, and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
