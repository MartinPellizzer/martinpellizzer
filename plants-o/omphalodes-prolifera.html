Omphalodes prolifera

Omphalodes prolifera, commonly known as the spreading bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Omphalodes prolifera is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
