Oxalis pilulifera

Oxalis pilulifera, commonly known as the bead vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, bead-like flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its common name. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive veining pattern, and it produces small, yellow-orange fruits that contain seeds. Oxalis pilulifera thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
