Opuntia leptocaulis var. brevispina

Opuntia leptocaulis var. brevispina, commonly known as the short-spined prickly pear, is a cactus species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its flattened, fleshy stems that are typically green and covered with small, short spines. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months, followed by edible, red-fleshed fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to poor soil conditions.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of food and traditional medicine.
