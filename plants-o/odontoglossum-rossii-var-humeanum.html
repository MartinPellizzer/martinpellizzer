Odontoglossum rossii var. humeanum

Odontoglossum rossii var. humeanum, commonly known as the Hume's orchid, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its erect, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, spurred flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with a distinctive red or orange lip. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, humus-rich soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated by enthusiasts for its beauty and unique floral structure.
