Olea woodiana subsp. disjuncta

Olea woodiana subsp.

disjuncta, commonly known as the Chinese olive, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its simple, alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves that are glossy and dark green on top and lighter underneath.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically arranged in clusters, followed by small, fleshy fruits that turn from green to yellow or orange when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

This subspecies is also valued for its ability to tolerate urban environments and air pollution.
