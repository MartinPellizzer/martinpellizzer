Oudemansia

Oudemansia, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to Africa.

These plants are characterized by their velvety foliage and showy, clustered flowers that come in a variety of colors. Oudemansia species typically have a sympodial growth habit, with new shoots forming at the base of the plant. They thrive in moist, well-drained soil and prefer indirect light, making them popular houseplants.

The genus includes several species, such as Oudemansia elata and Oudemansia abyssinica, which are valued for their ornamental appeal.
