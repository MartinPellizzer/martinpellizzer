Olax pyrnmidata

Olax pyrnmidata, commonly known as the African olive, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or red when mature. It is often found in rocky or coastal areas and is adapted to arid conditions.

The African olive has a long history of use in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
