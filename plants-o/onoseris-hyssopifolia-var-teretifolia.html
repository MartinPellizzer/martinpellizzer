Onoseris hyssopifolia var. teretifolia

Onoseris hyssopifolia var. teretifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It features upright, branched stems that are covered in fine, hairy hairs. The leaves are lanceolate to linear, with a smooth margin and a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, yellow flower heads arranged in clusters, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
