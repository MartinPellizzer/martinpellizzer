Ophrys araneola var. argentaria

Ophrys araneola var. argentaria, commonly known as the silver-haired bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to specific regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a dead wasp, a feature evolved to attract male wasps for pollination. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces a single, strikingly colored flower spike. Its habitat is typically found in dry, sunny grasslands and open woodlands, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in some regions.
