Oxytropis zaprjagaevae

Oxytropis zaprjagaevae, commonly known as the Zaprjagaev's locoweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, semi-desert environments.

Due to its restricted distribution and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
