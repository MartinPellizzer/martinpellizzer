Orchis lactea subsp. acuminata

Orchis lactea subsp.

acuminata, commonly known as the acuminated milk orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its roots are tuberous, aiding in nutrient storage.

The species is noted for its delicate appearance and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
