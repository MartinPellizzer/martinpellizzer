Opuntia subterranea

Opuntia subterranea, commonly known as the underground cactus or subterranean prickly pear, is a unique species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its subterranean growth habit, with most of its body hidden beneath the soil surface. The plant produces fleshy, spiny stems that emerge from the ground, and it features small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry conditions.

Opuntia subterranea is also known for its ability to store water, making it well adapted to desert environments.
