Orchis laxiflora

Orchis laxiflora, commonly known as the loose-flowered orchid, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and thrives in woodland or grassland habitats. Its flowers are borne in loose clusters and are pollinated by insects, particularly bees.

Orchis laxiflora is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
