Oncidium trachycaulon

Oncidium trachycaulon, commonly known as the rough-stemmed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to South America.

It features a sympodial growth habit with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often arranged in a raceme. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Oncidium trachycaulon is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in the right conditions.
