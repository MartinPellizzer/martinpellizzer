Onosma alborosea var. sanguinolenta

Onosma alborosea var. sanguinolenta, commonly known as the Crimson Onosma, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its vivid pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and unique floral structure.
