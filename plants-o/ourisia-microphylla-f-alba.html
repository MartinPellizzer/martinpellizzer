Ourisia microphylla f. alba

Ourisia microphylla f. alba, commonly known as the white-leaved ourisia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, white or pale green leaves and clusters of delicate, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is part of the Primulaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
