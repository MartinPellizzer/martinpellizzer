Oxalis micrantha var. setifera

Oxalis micrantha var. setifera, commonly known as the hairy wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves with reddish veins and produces delicate, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow or orange. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and foliage, which give it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Oxalis micrantha var. setifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
