Oxalis cernua var. expansa

Oxalis cernua var. expansa, commonly known as the broadleaf wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive white veining pattern, and its flowers are small, yellow, and five-petaled. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread and form dense mats.

Oxalis cernua var. expansa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
