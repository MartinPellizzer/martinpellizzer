Oxalis vallicola

Oxalis vallicola, commonly known as the valley oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its flowers bloom in shades of pink, white, and yellow, often in spring and early summer.

Oxalis vallicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
