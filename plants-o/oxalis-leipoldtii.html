Oxalis leipoldtii

Oxalis leipoldtii, commonly known as the Leipoldt's wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and attractive blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Oxalis leipoldtii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
