Onagra laevifolia

Onagra laevifolia, commonly known as evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening, hence its common name. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oily seeds. It thrives in sandy or loamy soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and prairies.

The oil from its seeds is valued for its medicinal and nutritional properties.
