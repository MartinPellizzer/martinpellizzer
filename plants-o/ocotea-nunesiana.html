Ocotea nunesiana

Ocotea nunesiana, commonly known as the Brazilian laurel, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of eastern Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are typically glossy and elliptical in shape. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually white or pale yellow and are arranged in clusters.

Ocotea nunesiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aromatic wood.
