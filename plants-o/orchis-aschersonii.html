Orchis aschersonii

Orchis aschersonii, commonly known as the Ascherson's orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers arranged in a loose raceme, with a distinctive spurred lip that is a key feature of its morphology. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its overall botanical appearance.

Orchis aschersonii typically blooms in late spring to early summer, adding a striking visual element to its natural habitat.
