Orchis palustris subsp. robusta

Orchis palustris subsp.

robusta, commonly known as the marsh helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to wetland habitats in Europe. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces clusters of pale greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color. It is a perennial species that thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected plant in many regions.
