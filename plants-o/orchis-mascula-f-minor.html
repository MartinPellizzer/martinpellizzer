Orchis mascula f. minor

Orchis mascula f. minor, commonly known as the lesser male orchid, is a rare and distinctive variant of the male orchid.

It is characterized by its unique floral morphology, featuring a single, elongated spur that distinguishes it from other orchid species. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers are usually a mix of white and purple, with a subtle fragrance that attracts specific pollinators.

This orchid is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and specialized ecological requirements.
