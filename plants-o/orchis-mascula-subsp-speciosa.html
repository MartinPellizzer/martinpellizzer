Orchis mascula subsp. speciosa

Orchis mascula subsp.

speciosa, commonly known as the showy orchis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodlands and grasslands.

The plant produces tall, erect stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive lip. Its botanical features include a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and a complex, spurred orchid flower structure.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is noted for its ornamental value in horticulture.
