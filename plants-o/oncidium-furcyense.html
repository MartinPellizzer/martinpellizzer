Oncidium furcyense

Oncidium furcyense, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling a fan shape. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Oncidium furcyense requires bright, indirect light and well-draining potting media to thrive.
