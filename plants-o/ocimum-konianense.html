Ocimum konianense

Ocimum konianense, commonly known as Konian's basil, is a species of flowering plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to Ethiopia and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with square stems and opposite leaves. It is often found in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its essential oils contain compounds such as linalool and eugenol, which contribute to its medicinal and aromatic properties.
