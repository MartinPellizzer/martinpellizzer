Oryza sativa var. chlamydantha

Oryza sativa var. chlamydantha, commonly known as the fragrant rice variety, is a cultivated rice species with distinctive botanical features.

It is characterized by its long, slender grains and a unique aroma that develops during the drying process. The plant typically grows in aquatic or semi-aquatic environments, thriving in flooded rice paddies. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that bear small, two-lobed flowers, which are essential for grain development.

This variety is valued for its yield and adaptability to various climatic conditions.
