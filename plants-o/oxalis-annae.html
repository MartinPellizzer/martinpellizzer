Oxalis annae

Oxalis annae, commonly known as the African wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features delicate, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive veining pattern that resembles a spider's web. The plant produces small, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Oxalis annae is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
