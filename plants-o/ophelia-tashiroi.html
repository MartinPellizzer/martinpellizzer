Ophelia tashiroi

Ophelia tashiroi, commonly known as the silverleaf waterplantain, is a species of flowering plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery-white powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense spikes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Ophelia tashiroi thrives in shallow, still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Its unique morphology and habitat preferences make it an important species for wetland ecosystems.
