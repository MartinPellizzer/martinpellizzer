Onosma echioides var. olympica

Onosma echioides var. olympica, commonly known as the Olympic horseweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. The plant features slender, erect stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its small, yellowish flowers are clustered in dense spikes, adding a subtle ornamental value.

It is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
