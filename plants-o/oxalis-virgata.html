Oxalis virgata

Oxalis virgata, commonly known as the silverleaf wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically heart-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. Oxalis virgata thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
