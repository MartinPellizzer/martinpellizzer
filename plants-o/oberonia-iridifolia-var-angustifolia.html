Oberonia iridifolia var. angustifolia

Oberonia iridifolia var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with slender, grass-like leaves and delicate, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its roots are mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to enhance nutrient uptake.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
