Ocotea fendleri

Ocotea fendleri, commonly known as Mexican cinnamon or Mexican laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America and northern South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and bay laurel. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into dark blue berries. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and medicinal properties.

Ocotea fendleri thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and montane forests.
