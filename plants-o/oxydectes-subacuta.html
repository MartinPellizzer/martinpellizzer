Oxydectes subacuta

Oxydectes subacuta, commonly known as the narrow-leafed oxydectes, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and rocky habitats.

Oxydectes subacuta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
