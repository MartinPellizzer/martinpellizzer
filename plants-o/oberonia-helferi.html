Oberonia helferi

Oberonia helferi, commonly known as the Helfer's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as under forest canopies. Its leaves are usually broad and grass-like, and the flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, Oberonia helferi is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its range.
