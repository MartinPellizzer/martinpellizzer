Olyra longifolia var. parvifolia

Olyra longifolia var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved longleaf olyra, is a variant of the olyra plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are smaller in size compared to the typical variety. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical environments, often in moist, shaded areas. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a smooth, waxy surface.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
