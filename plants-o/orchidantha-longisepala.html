Orchidantha longisepala

Orchidantha longisepala, commonly known as the long-sepal orchid, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped sepals that give it its scientific name. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink, often with a subtle greenish tinge. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is typically found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
