Olea heyneana

Olea heyneana, commonly known as the African olive, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Olea heyneana is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
