Odontochilus javanicus

Odontochilus javanicus, commonly known as the Java toothed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive toothed lip, which gives it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its flowers are usually small and pale green or white, with a fragrant scent.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
