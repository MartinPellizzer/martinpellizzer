Oxalis melilotoides

Oxalis melilotoides, commonly known as the yellow wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

Oxalis melilotoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
