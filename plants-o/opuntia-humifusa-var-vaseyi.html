Opuntia humifusa var. vaseyi

Opuntia humifusa var. vaseyi, commonly known as the Vasey's prickly pear, is a low-growing cactus native to the southeastern United States.

It features flat, fleshy stems that are typically green or reddish in color, covered in spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months, followed by edible, red or orange fruits. Its stems are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of food.

This variety is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient and hardy plant.
