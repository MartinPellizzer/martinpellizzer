Ophrys × bobolensis

Ophrys × bobolensis, commonly known as the bee orchid hybrid, is a fascinating plant that exhibits striking mimicry of female bees.

This characteristic is a key feature in its reproductive strategy, as it attracts male bees for pollination. The plant is a hybrid between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera, resulting in a unique combination of traits from both parents. It typically grows in calcareous soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats.

Its flowers are not only visually compelling but also play a significant role in the ecological interactions within its native range.
