Ocotea jacquiniana

Ocotea jacquiniana, commonly known as the yellow sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and smooth, grayish bark. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by dark, fleshy berries. Its wood is valued for its fragrant properties and is used in traditional crafts and incense.

Ocotea jacquiniana thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland tropical forests.
