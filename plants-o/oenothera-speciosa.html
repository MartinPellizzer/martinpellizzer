Oenothera speciosa

Oenothera speciosa, commonly known as showy evening primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Oenothera speciosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinator populations.
