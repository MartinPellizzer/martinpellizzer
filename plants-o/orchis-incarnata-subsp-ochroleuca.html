Orchis incarnata subsp. ochroleuca

Orchis incarnata subsp.

ochroleuca, commonly known as the white hellebore, is a herbaceous perennial native to moist meadows and woodland edges in eastern North America. It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces tall, slender flowering stalks.

The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive three-lobed lip and a spurred structure at the base. The plant is part of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its delicate, showy blooms.

It plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
