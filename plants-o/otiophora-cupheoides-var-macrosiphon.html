Otiophora cupheoides var. macrosiphon

Otiophora cupheoides var. macrosiphon, commonly known as the large-siphon sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense colonies.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
