Orochaenactis

Orochaenactis, commonly known as the golden bower vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rapid growth and the production of showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its vibrant appearance. Orochaenactis thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for trellises and vertical gardens.
