Osmanthus fragrans var. aurantiacus

Osmanthus fragrans var. aurantiacus, commonly known as the golden osmanthus, is a flowering plant native to southern China.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in late autumn. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are often used in traditional Chinese medicine and culinary applications. Its leaves are elliptical and dark green, providing a lush backdrop to the vibrant flowers.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
