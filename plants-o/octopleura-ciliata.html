Octopleura ciliata

Octopleura ciliata, commonly known as the hairy tamarind, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with ciliate margins, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It is often grown for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a useful plant in reforestation efforts.
