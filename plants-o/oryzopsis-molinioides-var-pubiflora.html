Oryzopsis molinioides var. pubiflora

Oryzopsis molinioides var. pubiflora, commonly known as the pubescent grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy foliage, which gives it its varietal name. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers, typical of the grass family Poaceae. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and wetlands.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
