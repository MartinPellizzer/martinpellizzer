Ocotea montis-insulae

Ocotea montis-insulae, commonly known as the island mountain ocotea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the island of Mauritius and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within the island's mountainous regions. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are followed by dark blue berries that are an important food source for local wildlife.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species with limited distribution.
