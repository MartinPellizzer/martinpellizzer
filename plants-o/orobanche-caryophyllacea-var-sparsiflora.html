Orobanche caryophyllacea var. sparsiflora

Orobanche caryophyllacea var. sparsiflora, commonly known as the sparsely flowering broomrape, is a parasitic plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies on host plants for nutrients.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is characterized by its lack of true leaves and its dependence on host roots for sustenance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or reddish in color. It is often found in arid or semi-arid regions where it parasitizes various species of Caryophyllaceae.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can have significant ecological impacts on its host plants.
