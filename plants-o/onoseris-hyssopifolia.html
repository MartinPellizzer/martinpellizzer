Onoseris hyssopifolia

Onoseris hyssopifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate to cold climates. Its leaves are narrow and serrated, while the bright yellow flowers bloom in summer.

Onoseris hyssopifolia is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to high-altitude environments.
