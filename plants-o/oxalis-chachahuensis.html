Oxalis chachahuensis

Oxalis chachahuensis, commonly known as the Chilean wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of Chile.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes. It is a perennial herb that thrives in cool, temperate climates.

Oxalis chachahuensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
