Oxalis triangularis

Oxalis triangularis, commonly known as the wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which resemble the shape of a triangle, and its delicate, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade. It is often grown as a houseplant or in shaded garden areas due to its ornamental appeal.

Oxalis triangularis is also noted for its ability to bloom throughout the year under suitable conditions.
