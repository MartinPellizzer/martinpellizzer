Orobanche bodinieri

Orobanche bodinieri, commonly known as the Bodinier broomrape, is a root-parasitic plant belonging to the Orobanchaceae family.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients, typically growing on the roots of various legumes. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink or purple, which are often arranged in clusters. It is native to regions of Africa, the Middle East, and southern Europe.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can significantly impact the growth and yield of its host plants.
