Olax subscorpioidea

Olax subscorpioidea, commonly known as the scorpion-like olax, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Olacaceae and is characterized by its woody, shrub-like growth habit. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with four to five petals. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a rough or hairy texture.

Olax subscorpioidea is adapted to dry conditions and can be found in savanna and woodland habitats.
