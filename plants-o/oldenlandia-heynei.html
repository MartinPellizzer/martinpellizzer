Oldenlandia heynei

Oldenlandia heynei, commonly known as the silverleaf spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling spider-like structures, hence its common name. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine, silvery or grayish pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
