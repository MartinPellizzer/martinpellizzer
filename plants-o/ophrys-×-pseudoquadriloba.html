Ophrys × pseudoquadriloba

Ophrys × pseudoquadriloba, commonly known as the mimic orchid, is a hybrid species within the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its intricate floral structure, which closely resembles the appearance of female wasps, a trait that plays a key role in its pollination strategy. The plant produces a single, showy flower with a specialized lip that mimics the body of a female insect. Its native range includes parts of Europe, particularly in the Mediterranean region.

This orchid thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
