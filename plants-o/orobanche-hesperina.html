Orobanche hesperina

Orobanche hesperina, commonly known as the western broomrape, is a parasitic plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is found in various regions of the western United States. The plant produces small, pale yellow or white flowers, typically in late spring or early summer. Its roots form specialized structures called haustoria, which attach to the roots of host plants like grasses and legumes.

Orobanche hesperina is an annual plant that plays a role in ecological interactions within its habitat.
