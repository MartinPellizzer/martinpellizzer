Oldenlandia diffusa var. polygonoides

Oldenlandia diffusa var. polygonoides, commonly known as cat's tail or snake's head, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its foliage is simple and opposite, with leaves that are usually elliptical or lance-shaped.
