Ornithogalum umbellatum var. declinatum

Ornithogalum umbellatum var. declinatum, commonly known as the drooping star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a short, rhizomatous stem with narrow, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette. The plant produces umbels of white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers have six petals and are often fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths.

The plant is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.
