Onobrychis michauxii var. caucasica

Onobrychis michauxii var. caucasica, commonly known as Caucasian sweet clover, is a perennial legume native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in racemes. The plant has a deep root system, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils. It is often found in grasslands and rocky areas, and it plays a role in nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with rhizobia.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a notable species in botanical studies.
