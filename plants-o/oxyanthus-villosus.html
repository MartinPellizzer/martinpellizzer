Oxyanthus villosus

Oxyanthus villosus, commonly known as the hairy oxyanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub or vine, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical in shape and often have a velvety texture.

Oxyanthus villosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
