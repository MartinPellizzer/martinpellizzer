Omphalodes olgae

Omphalodes olgae, commonly known as the Crimean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually blue or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Omphalodes olgae is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, making it a characteristic species of mountainous regions.
