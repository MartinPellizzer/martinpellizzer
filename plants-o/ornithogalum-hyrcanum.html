Ornithogalum hyrcanum

Ornithogalum hyrcanum, commonly known as the Hyrcanian star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia and parts of the Middle East.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces striking white or pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer, arranged in a raceme. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ornithogalum hyrcanum is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its elegant appearance.
