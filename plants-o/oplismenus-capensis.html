Oplismenus capensis

Oplismenus capensis, commonly known as the common marsh grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in wet or marshy environments and is often found in coastal areas.

Oplismenus capensis is adapted to tolerate salt spray and is an important component of coastal vegetation.
