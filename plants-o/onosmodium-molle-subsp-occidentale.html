Onosmodium molle subsp. occidentale

Onosmodium molle subsp.

occidentale, commonly known as the western silky oaks, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats. Its leaves are typically pubescent, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
