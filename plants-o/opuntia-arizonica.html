Opuntia arizonica

Opuntia arizonica, commonly known as the Arizona prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems that are covered in spines and are often segmented. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tunas, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

Opuntia arizonica is well adapted to arid environments and plays an important role in desert ecosystems.
