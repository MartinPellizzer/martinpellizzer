Octarrhena montana

Octarrhena montana, commonly known as the mountain thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually pink or purple, blooming in the summer months. Its aromatic leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported antiseptic properties.

Octarrhena montana is well adapted to dry, arid environments and is often found in mountainous areas.
