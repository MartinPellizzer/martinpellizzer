Othonna sinuata

Othonna sinuata, commonly known as the zigzagged othonna, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, sinuous, and often zigzagging stems that are covered in dense, blue-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Othonna sinuata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and adaptability to dry conditions.
