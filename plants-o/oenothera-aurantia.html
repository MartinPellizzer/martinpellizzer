Oenothera aurantia

Oenothera aurantia, commonly known as the yellow evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, elliptical seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Oenothera aurantia is also known for its medicinal uses, particularly for its oil-rich seeds.
