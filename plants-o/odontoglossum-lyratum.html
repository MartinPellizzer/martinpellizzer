Odontoglossum lyratum

Odontoglossum lyratum, commonly known as the lyrated orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in high-altitude cloud forests. The plant features a pseudobulb with a single, lance-shaped leaf and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. Its inflorescence is a raceme of elongated, spurred flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Odontoglossum lyratum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
