Oenothera longiflora subsp. grandiflora

Oenothera longiflora subsp.

grandiflora, commonly known as the showy evening primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy yellow flowers that open in the evening.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Its blooming period typically occurs in late summer to early autumn.
