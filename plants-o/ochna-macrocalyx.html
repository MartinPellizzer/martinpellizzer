Ochna macrocalyx

Ochna macrocalyx, commonly known as the large-flowered ochna, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of showy, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed, typical of the Ochnaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Ochna macrocalyx is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
