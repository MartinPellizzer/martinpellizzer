Oenothera densiflora var. tenella

Oenothera densiflora var. tenella, commonly known as the compact evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces showy yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in well-drained soils, often found in open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes. It is part of the Onagraceae family and is known for its nocturnal blooming pattern.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
