Obione rosea

Obione rosea, commonly known as the pink sandalwood.

It is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. Its flowers are fragrant and typically pink or white, producing a delicate, sweet scent.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
