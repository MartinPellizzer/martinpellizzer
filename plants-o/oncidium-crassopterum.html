Oncidium crassopterum

Oncidium crassopterum, commonly known as the broad-winged orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, strap-like leaves and clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers with prominent, wing-like petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. It requires bright, indirect light and well-draining potting mix to thrive.

Oncidium crassopterum is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
