Orchis sambucina var. subalpina

Orchis sambucina var. subalpina, commonly known as the Alpine sweetroot orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its roots are thick and fleshy.

This variety is adapted to cold climates and is often found at elevations above 1,500 meters.
