Oenothera amplexicaulis

Oenothera amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping willow weed, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that often wrap around the stem, giving it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Oenothera amplexicaulis is also known for its ability to tolerate periodic flooding, making it an important species in riparian ecosystems.
