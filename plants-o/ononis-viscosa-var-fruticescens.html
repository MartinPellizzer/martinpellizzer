Ononis viscosa var. fruticescens

Ononis viscosa var. fruticescens, commonly known as creeping everlasting, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branched stems that often root at the nodes, allowing it to spread extensively. The leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, giving them a silvery appearance. Small, yellowish-white flowers appear in clusters during the spring and summer months.

This plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
