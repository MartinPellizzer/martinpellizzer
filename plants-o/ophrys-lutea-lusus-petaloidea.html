Ophrys lutea lusus petaloidea

Ophrys lutea lusus petaloidea, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species found in specific alpine and subalpine habitats.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which includes a labellum that mimics the appearance of a female bee, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant produces showy, yellowish flowers with intricate patterns that aid in attracting specific pollinators. It thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soils, often growing in rocky or mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in many regions.
