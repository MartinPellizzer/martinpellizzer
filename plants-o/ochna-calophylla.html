Ochna calophylla

Ochna calophylla, commonly known as the African velvetwood, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange-red berries that contain seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Ochna calophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
