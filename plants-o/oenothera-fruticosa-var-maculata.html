Oenothera fruticosa var. maculata

Oenothera fruticosa var. maculata, commonly known as the spotted evening primrose, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

The species is known for its adaptability to arid and semi-arid environments.
