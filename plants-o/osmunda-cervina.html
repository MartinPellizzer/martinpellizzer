Osmunda cervina

Osmunda cervina, commonly known as the royal fern, is a species of fern characterized by its large, feathery fronds.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has a distinctive rhizome system that allows it to spread and form dense colonies. Its spores are produced in clusters on the undersides of its fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.

Osmunda cervina is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in maintaining ecological balance in its native habitats.
