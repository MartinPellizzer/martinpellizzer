Oenothera pallida subsp. runcinata

Oenothera pallida subsp.

runcinata, commonly known as the narrowleaf evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces showy yellow flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

The species is known for its medicinal properties, particularly the oils extracted from its seeds.
