Ouratea stipulata

Ouratea stipulata, commonly known as the white-flowered ouratea, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often pubescent and have stipules at the base. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden environments.
