Ocotea aquila

Ocotea aquila, commonly known as the white ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, and it produces berries that are an important food source for various wildlife.

The tree is valued for its timber and is also used in traditional medicine.
