Orchis leucostachya

Orchis leucostachya, commonly known as the white-stemmed orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem that can reach up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces showy, pale to white flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lanceolate and typically arranged in two rows along the stem.

Orchis leucostachya is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and other moist, well-drained habitats.
