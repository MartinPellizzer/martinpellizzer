Oenothera fruticosa var. unguiculata

Oenothera fruticosa var. unguiculata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf evening primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features slender, branched stems covered with fine, white hairs and produces showy, yellow flowers that open in the evening. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy surface, and the plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It belongs to the evening primrose family (Onagraceae) and is known for its ability to thrive in sandy or rocky soils.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
