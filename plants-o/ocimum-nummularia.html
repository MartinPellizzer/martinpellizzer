Ocimum nummularia

Ocimum nummularia, commonly known as dwarf basil, is a compact, aromatic herb native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features small, rounded leaves that are often fragrant and used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces tiny, white or light purple flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in warm, humid conditions and prefers well-drained soil.

Ocimum nummularia is often grown as a decorative plant due to its compact size and pleasant scent.
