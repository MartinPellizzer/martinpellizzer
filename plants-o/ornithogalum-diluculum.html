Ornithogalum diluculum

Ornithogalum diluculum, commonly known as the morning glory of the genus Ornithogalum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bell-shaped, white or pale green flowers that open in the early morning. The plant belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its underground bulbous structures. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
