Opuntia lindheimeri var. aciculata

Opuntia lindheimeri var. aciculata, commonly known as the Lindheimer's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flattened, spine-tipped stems that are typically green and covered in small, glochids. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers during the summer months, followed by red, fleshy fruits known as tunas. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and efficient water storage capabilities.

The plant's structure and adaptations make it well-suited for xerophytic conditions, contributing to its ecological significance in desert ecosystems.
