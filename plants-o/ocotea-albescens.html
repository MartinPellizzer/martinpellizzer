Ocotea albescens

Ocotea albescens, commonly known as the white ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its smooth, light-colored bark and lance-shaped, glossy leaves. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by oval, dark blue berries.

Ocotea albescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic qualities.
