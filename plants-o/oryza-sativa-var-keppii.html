Oryza sativa var. keppii

Oryza sativa var. keppii, commonly known as the Keppi rice, is a wild rice species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, golden-yellow grains. This variety is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in wetland environments. It has been studied for its potential in genetic research due to its unique traits.

However, it is less cultivated than other rice varieties and is primarily found in natural habitats.
