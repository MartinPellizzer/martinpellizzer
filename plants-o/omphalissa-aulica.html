Omphalissa aulica

Omphalissa aulica, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or alpine settings.

The species is valued for its vibrant floral display and is often cultivated for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
