Oncidium monophyllum

Oncidium monophyllum, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It features long, strap-like leaves and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and green. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, utilizing its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. Its flowers bloom in clusters and can last for several weeks, making it a popular choice for ornamental cultivation.

Oncidium monophyllum prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.
