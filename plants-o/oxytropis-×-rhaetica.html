Oxytropis × rhaetica

Oxytropis × rhaetica, commonly known as the Rhaetian locoweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and spiny stems. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Oxytropis × rhaetica is adapted to high-altitude environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

It is also noted for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock.
