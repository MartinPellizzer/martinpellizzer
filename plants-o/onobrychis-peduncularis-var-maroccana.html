Onobrychis peduncularis var. maroccana

Onobrychis peduncularis var. maroccana, commonly known as the Moroccan sainfoin, is a flowering plant native to North Africa.

It is a legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow pea-like flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to harsh climates. It is often used as a forage plant due to its high nutritional value for livestock.

Additionally, it plays a role in soil fixation and erosion control in its native habitat.
