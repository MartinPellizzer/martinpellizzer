Oxalis versicolor var. versicolor

Oxalis versicolor var. versicolor, commonly known as the variable wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of North America.

It features trifoliate leaves with three heart-shaped leaflets, often adorned with distinctive red or orange markings. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate foliage and vibrant floral display.
