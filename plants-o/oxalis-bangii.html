Oxalis bangii

Oxalis bangii, commonly known as the velvetleaf wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant is characterized by its succulent stems and leaves, which help it retain moisture in arid conditions. It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Oxalis bangii thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
