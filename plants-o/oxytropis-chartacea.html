Oxytropis chartacea

Oxytropis chartacea, commonly known as the crested lupine, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, sparsely hairy, erect stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Oxytropis chartacea is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in coastal and inland desert regions.
