Oncidium bryolophotum

Oncidium bryolophotum, commonly known as the Brome-leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its unique, grass-like foliage and intricate, fragrant flower spikes. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling a brush or broom. Oncidium bryolophotum thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and sought-after ornamental plant in horticulture.
