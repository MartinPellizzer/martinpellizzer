Orchis decipiens

Orchis decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant produces a single, showy spike of flowers that range in color from purple to pink, often with a distinctive hood-like structure. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil, while its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette.

The deceptive orchid is known for its ability to mimic the appearance of other plants, which may aid in pollination.
