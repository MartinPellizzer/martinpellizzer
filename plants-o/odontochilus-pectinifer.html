Odontochilus pectinifer

Odontochilus pectinifer, commonly known as the hairy toothed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, hairy leaves and intricate floral structure. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, where it thrives on the forest floor or on tree trunks. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and often have a distinctive coloration, ranging from white to pale yellow.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
