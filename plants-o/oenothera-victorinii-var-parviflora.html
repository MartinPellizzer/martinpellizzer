Oenothera victorinii var. parviflora

Oenothera victorinii var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its yellow, tubular flowers that open in the evening and close by morning. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along streambanks and in wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The species is part of the Onagraceae family and is known for its ecological importance to pollinators such as moths and beetles.
