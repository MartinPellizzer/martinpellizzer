Olearia clemensiae

Olearia clemensiae, commonly known as the Clemens's Daisy-bush, is a species of flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in coastal and lowland areas, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, silver-green leaves that are aromatic when crushed.

Olearia clemensiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy and long-lasting blooms.
