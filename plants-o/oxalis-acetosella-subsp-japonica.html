Oxalis acetosella subsp. japonica

Oxalis acetosella subsp.

japonica, commonly known as Japanese wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to East Asia. It is a low-growing perennial with heart-shaped leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves contain oxalic acid, which can be toxic in large quantities.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
