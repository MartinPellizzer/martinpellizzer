Oxalis simsii

Oxalis simsii, commonly known as the golden oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces small, bead-like fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Oxalis simsii thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant foliage and prolonged blooming period.
