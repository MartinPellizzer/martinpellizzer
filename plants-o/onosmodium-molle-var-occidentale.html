Onosmodium molle var. occidentale

Onosmodium molle var. occidentale, commonly known as the western velvetleaf, is a perennial herb native to the southern United States.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers that develop into capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Malvaceae family.
