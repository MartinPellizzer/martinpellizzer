Olearia ferresii

Olearia ferresii, commonly known as the Cook Strait daisy, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to the harsh conditions of the southern hemisphere. Olearia ferresii is often found in open habitats such as cliffs and rocky shores.

Its ornamental value has made it a subject of interest for horticulturists and botanists alike.
