Oreoloma grandiflorum

Oreoloma grandiflorum, commonly known as the large-flowered oreoloma, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered with fine hairs.

Oreoloma grandiflorum is typically found in moist, sunny habitats such as grasslands and open woodlands.
