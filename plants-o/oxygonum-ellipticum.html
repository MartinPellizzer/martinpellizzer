Oxygonum ellipticum

Oxygonum ellipticum, commonly known as the narrowleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual with slender, ascending stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped blue or purple flowers that bloom in the early morning. Its botanical features include opposite leaves and a fibrous root system.

It is often found in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and disturbed soils.
