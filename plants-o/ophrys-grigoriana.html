Ophrys grigoriana

Ophrys grigoriana, commonly known as the Gregorian bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Turkey.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which closely resembles a female bee, a feature evolved to attract male bees for pollination. The plant produces a single, showy flower with intricate patterns and a lip that mimics the appearance of a bee's abdomen. It thrives in rocky and open habitats, often growing in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species.
