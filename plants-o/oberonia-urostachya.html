Oberonia urostachya

Oberonia urostachya, commonly known as the black orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, flower spikes that contrast with its green foliage. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its flowers are small and clustered, with a unique structure that includes a spurred lip.

Oberonia urostachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
