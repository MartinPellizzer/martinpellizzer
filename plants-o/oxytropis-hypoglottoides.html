Oxytropis hypoglottoides

The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions due to its deep root system. Its small, pea-like flowers are usually pale blue to purple, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Oxytropis hypoglottoides is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
