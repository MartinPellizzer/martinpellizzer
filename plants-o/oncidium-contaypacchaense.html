Oncidium contaypacchaense

Oncidium contaypacchaense, commonly known as the Contaypaccha orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features a sympodial growth habit with pseudobulbs and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange. Its floral structure includes a spurred lip and is often pollinated by bees and other insects.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental qualities and is relatively easy to cultivate under suitable conditions.
