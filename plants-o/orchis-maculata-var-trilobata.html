Orchis maculata var. trilobata

Orchis maculata var. trilobata, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple and green spotted leaves and produces showy, hooded flowers with a yellow-veined lip. Native to parts of Europe and North America, this species thrives in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or meadow habitats. The plant is often found in open areas with partial shade and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
