Oldenlandia dichotoma var. papillosa

Oldenlandia dichotoma var. papillosa, commonly known as the hairy false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly rough texture.

This variant is distinguished by its hairy foliage, which gives it its common name and contributes to its unique botanical appearance.
