Odontoglossum alexandrae var. flaveolum

Odontoglossum alexandrae var. flaveolum, commonly known as the golden-flowered orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow petals and sepals, which contrast with its deep green foliage. The plant typically grows in high-altitude cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. It produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and rarity.
