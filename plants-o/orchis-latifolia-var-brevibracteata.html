Orchis latifolia var. brevibracteata

Orchis latifolia var. brevibracteata, commonly known as the broad-leaved orchid with short bracts, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its upright stem and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and it produces a distinctive spike of flowers with short bracts.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
