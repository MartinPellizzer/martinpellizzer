Oocephalus foliosus

Oocephalus foliosus, commonly known as the broadleaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It lacks true roots and absorbs water and nutrients through its leaves, which are modified to form a spongy, cushion-like structure. The plant produces small, white or pale green berries that are often consumed by birds, aiding in seed dispersal. Its leaves are broad and leathery, with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss.

Oocephalus foliosus plays a role in ecosystem dynamics by influencing host tree growth and providing food for various wildlife species.
