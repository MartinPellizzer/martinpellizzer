Ononis diffusa var. fallax

Ononis diffusa var. fallax, commonly known as the fallax variant of the common whitlow grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features wiry, branching stems that often lie close to the ground, producing small, clustered flowers with five narrow petals. The plant typically blooms from late spring to early summer, and its flowers are usually pale pink to white in color. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

This variety is noted for its hardy nature and ability to withstand dry conditions.
