Oreopanax crassinervius

Oreopanax crassinervius, commonly known as the thick-veined oreopanax, is a flowering plant species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins. The plant typically grows as an understory shrub in tropical rainforests, often reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe, a common feature in the Araceae family.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its foliage.
