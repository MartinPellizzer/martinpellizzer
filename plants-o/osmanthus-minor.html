Osmanthus minor

Osmanthus minor, commonly known as the Chinese sweet osmanthus, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant produces tiny, orange-red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Osmanthus minor is valued for its ornamental appeal and its use in traditional Chinese medicine.

Its essential oils are also used in perfumes and culinary applications.
