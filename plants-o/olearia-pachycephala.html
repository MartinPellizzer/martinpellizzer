Olearia pachycephala

Olearia pachycephala, commonly known as the woolly sunhemp, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, fluffy appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, adding a subtle ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Olearia pachycephala is also valued for its role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
