Oglifa

Oglifa, commonly known as the desert orchid, is a rare and resilient plant native to arid regions.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers that bloom in harsh desert environments. The plant has adapted to survive with minimal water, featuring succulent leaves and deep root systems. Its flowers are typically small but highly fragrant, attracting specific pollinators.

Due to its ecological significance and aesthetic value, oglifa is often protected in its natural habitats.
