Oxyacantha brainerdii

Oxyacantha brainerdii, commonly known as the Brainerd's bush honeysuckle, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, fragrant yellow flowers in the spring. Its fruit is a red, fleshy drupe that attracts birds and other wildlife.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native habitats.
