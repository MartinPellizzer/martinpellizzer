Ophrys × samuelii

Ophrys × samuelii, commonly known as the Samuel's bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its intricate floral structure, which closely resembles the face of a female bee, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in rocky or woodland habitats, thriving in well-drained soils with partial shade. Its flowers are usually a mix of brown, yellow, and green hues, mimicking the appearance of insects to attract male bees.

Ophrys × samuelii is a rare and endangered species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts in preserving its natural habitat.
