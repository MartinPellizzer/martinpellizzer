Orchis morio var. viridiflora

Orchis morio var. viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing a cluster of spurred orchid flowers that are often tinged with purple or brown. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is noted for its unique coloration and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens.
