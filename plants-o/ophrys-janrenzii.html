Ophrys janrenzii

Ophrys janrenzii, commonly known as the Janrenz's bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to specific regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its intricate floral structure, which closely resembles the appearance of a female bee, a mimicry strategy used to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brownish in color, with complex patterns that aid in its deceptive pollination mechanism.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Ophrys janrenzii is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
