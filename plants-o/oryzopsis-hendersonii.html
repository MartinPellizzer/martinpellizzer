Oryzopsis hendersonii

Oryzopsis hendersonii, commonly known as Henderson's grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. Its rhizomes are often extensive, allowing it to form dense stands in suitable environments.

Oryzopsis hendersonii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
