Orchis latifolia var. blyttii

Orchis latifolia var. blyttii, commonly known as the Broad-leaved Orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps it establish and persist in its habitat.

Its flowers are pollinated by insects, particularly bees, and it plays a role in supporting local biodiversity.
