Onobrychis marashensis

Onobrychis marashensis, commonly known as the Armenian clover, is a perennial leguminous plant native to the mountainous regions of Armenia and Turkey.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or semi-arid habitats. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a notable species in botanical studies and ecological restoration.
