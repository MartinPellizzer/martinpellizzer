Ophrys insectifera f. bombifera

Ophrys insectifera f. bombifera, commonly known as the Bombifera bee orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive floral structure, which closely resembles a bee, a feature that aids in its unique pollination strategy. The plant produces a single, showy flower with a complex labellum that mimics the appearance and scent of a female bee. It thrives in calcareous soils and is typically found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in many regions.
