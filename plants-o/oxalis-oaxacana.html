Oxalis oaxacana

Oxalis oaxacana, commonly known as the Mexican wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that resemble those of true sorrel. The plant belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in shaded environments. Its foliage contains oxalic acid, which can be toxic if ingested in large quantities.

Oxalis oaxacana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.
