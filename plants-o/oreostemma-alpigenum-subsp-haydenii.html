Oreostemma alpigenum subsp. haydenii

Oreostemma alpigenum subsp.

haydenii, commonly known as the Hayden's oreostemma, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It is characterized by its delicate, branched stems and small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as alpine meadows and forested slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture. Oreostemma alpigenum subsp.

haydenii is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its refined appearance.
