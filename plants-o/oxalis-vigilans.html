Oxalis vigilans

Oxalis vigilans, commonly known as the common wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which resemble those of clover, and its clusters of small, delicate flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and along stream banks. Oxalis vigilans is a perennial species that thrives in USDA hardiness zones 4 through 9.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for woodland gardens and as a ground cover plant.
