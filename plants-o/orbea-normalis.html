Orbea normalis

Orbea normalis, commonly known as the Natal orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in areas with good drainage. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its stems are often succulent, allowing it to store water.

Orbea normalis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
