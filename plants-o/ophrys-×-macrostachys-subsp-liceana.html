Ophrys × macrostachys subsp. liceana

Ophrys × macrostachys subsp.

liceana, commonly known as the Licean bee orchid, is a hybrid species within the Orchidaceae family. It is characterized by its unique floral morphology, featuring a labellum that mimics the appearance of a female bee, a feature that plays a key role in its pollination strategy.

The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is found in Mediterranean climates. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brownish in color, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

This subspecies is notable for its specialized ecological interactions and is often considered a rare and endangered orchid.
