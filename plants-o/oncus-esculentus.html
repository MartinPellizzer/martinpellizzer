Oncus esculentus

Oncus esculentus, commonly known as the edible nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America and is characterized by its distinctive purple flowers and egg-shaped, spiny fruits. The plant produces berries that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or orange when mature. These berries contain alkaloids and should be consumed with caution due to their potential toxicity.

Despite its risks, it has been used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
