Ocotea persicifolia

Ocotea persicifolia, commonly known as the cherry-leaf ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped, glossy dark green leaves that resemble cherry leaves, hence its common name. It produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by dark blue berries.

Ocotea persicifolia is often found in humid lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
