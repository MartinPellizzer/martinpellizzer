Oncidium pallidum

Oncidium pallidum, commonly known as the pale orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and clusters of pale yellow or greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in late summer or early autumn and have a subtle fragrance.

Oncidium pallidum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
