Ocimum masaiense

Ocimum masaiense, commonly known as Masai basil, is a perennial herb native to East Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and lavender. The plant is characterized by its small, oval leaves and spikes of purple or white flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded areas.

Its essential oils contain compounds like citral and linalool, which contribute to its distinctive fragrance and potential medicinal properties.
