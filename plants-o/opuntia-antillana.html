Opuntia antillana

Opuntia antillana, commonly known as the Caribbean prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the Caribbean region.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in clusters of sharp spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers, which are followed by edible, fleshy fruits called tunas. Opuntia antillana is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, storing water in its thick stems.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various animals.
