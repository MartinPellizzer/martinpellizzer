Ocotea alata

Ocotea alata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ocotea, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves with a narrow, lance-shaped form. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by dark blue to black berries. Its bark is often aromatic and has been used traditionally for medicinal purposes.

Ocotea alata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.
