Ornithogalum cydni

Ornithogalum cydni, commonly known as the white squill, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a bulbous base from which narrow, grass-like leaves emerge. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers during the spring and summer months. Its flowers are typically arranged in umbels and are attractive to pollinators such as bees and moths.

Ornithogalum cydni is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
