Oxalis minuta

Oxalis minuta, commonly known as the dwarf wood sorrel, is a small, herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features trifoliate leaves, each leaflet being heart-shaped with a distinctive reddish or purple coloration. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically yellow with five petals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Oxalis minuta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
