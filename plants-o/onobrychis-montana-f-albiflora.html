Onobrychis montana f. albiflora

Onobrychis montana f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered field vetch, is a herbaceous legume native to southern Europe.

It features slender, creeping stems that produce small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed sites.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
