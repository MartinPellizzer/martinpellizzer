Othonna oleracea

Othonna oleracea, commonly known as the African lettuce, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Othonna oleracea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
