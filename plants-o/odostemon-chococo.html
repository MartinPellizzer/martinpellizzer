Odostemon chococo

Odostemon chococo, commonly known as the chocolate bean plant, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, compound leaves and small, clustered flowers that range from white to pale yellow. The plant produces pods containing seeds that are rich in oils and are used in the production of chocolate. Its botanical name reflects its association with the chocolate-making process.

Odostemon chococo thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
