Ocimum canum var. integrifolium

Ocimum canum var. integrifolium, commonly known as purple basil, is a variety of basil characterized by its distinctive violet-colored leaves.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and lavender. The plant is native to tropical regions of Africa and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses. Its leaves are typically larger and more robust compared to common basil, with a slightly peppery flavor.

The plant prefers warm, sunny conditions and well-drained soil for optimal growth.
