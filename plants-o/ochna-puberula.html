Ochna puberula

Ochna puberula, commonly known as the velvet tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, often with a red or purple throat. These flowers are borne in clusters and attract a variety of pollinators, including birds and insects.

Ochna puberula is also known for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
