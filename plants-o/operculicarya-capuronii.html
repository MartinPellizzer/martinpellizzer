Operculicarya capuronii

Operculicarya capuronii, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a unique member of the Orchidaceae family native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often display vivid colors and intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its native habitat. Its botanical features include a pseudobulb structure and a single, often showy inflorescence.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
