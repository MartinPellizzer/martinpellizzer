Ouratea alaternifolia

Ouratea alaternifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces blue-black berries that are edible for some bird species but toxic to humans. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation in warm climates.
