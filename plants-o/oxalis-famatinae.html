Oxalis famatinae

Oxalis famatinae, commonly known as the Famatina Oxalis, is a species native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its foliage and flowers are often adorned with a striking red or orange hue, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Oxalis famatinae is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and resilience in challenging conditions.
