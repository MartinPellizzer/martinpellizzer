Ornithogalum lacteum

Ornithogalum lacteum, commonly known as the milk lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant contains a milky sap, which is toxic and can cause skin irritation. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ornithogalum lacteum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
