Oreobroma yosemitanum

Oreobroma yosemitanum, commonly known as the Yosemite rockscale, is a rare flowering plant native to the Sierra Nevada mountains of California.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth form. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils at high elevations.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered endangered and is protected under federal and state conservation laws.
