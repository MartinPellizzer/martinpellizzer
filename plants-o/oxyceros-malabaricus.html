Oxyceros malabaricus

Oxyceros malabaricus, commonly known as the Malabar oxeye, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of India and Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a slender, branched stem with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its showy yellow flowers are surrounded by pale green bracts, forming a distinctive cup-like structure.

Oxyceros malabaricus is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas.
