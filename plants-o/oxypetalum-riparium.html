Oxypetalum riparium

Oxypetalum riparium, commonly known as the river oxypetalum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, herbaceous stems and small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows near water bodies, such as rivers and streams, adapting to moist, sandy soils. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often arranged in opposite pairs along the stem.

Oxypetalum riparium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in wetland gardens.
