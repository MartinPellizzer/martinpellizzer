Oenothera depressa f. latibracteata

Oenothera depressa f. latibracteata, commonly known as the broad-bract evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Onagraceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening. The plant features succulent, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers have four petals and are pollinated primarily by moths and other nocturnal insects.

This variety is distinguished by its broader bracts compared to the typical form of Oenothera depressa.
