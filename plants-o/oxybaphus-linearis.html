Oxybaphus linearis

Oxybaphus linearis, commonly known as the linear baphus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky soils, thriving in full sun. Its linear leaves are narrow and elongated, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Oxybaphus linearis is often found in coastal and inland regions, displaying a range of flowering colors from yellow to orange.
