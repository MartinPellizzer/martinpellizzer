Ocotea ambrensis

Ocotea ambrensis, commonly known as the Ambrensis Ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurel trees and spices like cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
