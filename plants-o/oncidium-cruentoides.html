Oncidium cruentoides

Oncidium cruentoides, commonly known as the crimson orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, often red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to thrive in seasonal climates. Its leaves are typically long and strap-like, with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss.

Oncidium cruentoides is popular in horticulture for its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
