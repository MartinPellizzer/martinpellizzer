Orchis spitzelii subsp. latiflora

Orchis spitzelii subsp.

latiflora, commonly known as the broad-leaved orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of Europe. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant produces violet to purple flowers with a distinctive hooded lip, and its leaves are broad and lance-shaped. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
