Opuntia gosseliniana subsp. santa-rita

Opuntia gosseliniana subsp.

santa-rita, commonly known as Santa Rita prickly pear, is a cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems, which are typically ribbed and covered in spines.

The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruits, known as tunas, are edible and often used in traditional cuisine.

This subspecies is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.
