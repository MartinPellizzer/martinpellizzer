Ocotea iridescens

Ocotea iridescens, commonly known as the iridescent ocotea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves that have a striking iridescent sheen under light. It produces small, white flowers followed by dark blue berries, which are a food source for various birds and mammals.

Ocotea iridescens is often found in humid, lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
