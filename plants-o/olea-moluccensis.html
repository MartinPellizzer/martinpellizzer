Olea moluccensis

Olea moluccensis, commonly known as the Moluccan olive, is a small evergreen tree native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The tree produces small, round olives that are typically green when mature, though they may turn black depending on the variety. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in various tropical regions for its aesthetic and potential medicinal properties.

The plant is also notable for its ability to thrive in coastal environments due to its salt tolerance.
