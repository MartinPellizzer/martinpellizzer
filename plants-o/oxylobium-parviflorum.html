Oxylobium parviflorum

Oxylobium parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered oxylobium, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, fragrant white flowers in late spring. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun. It is often found in woodland edges and streamside habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological restoration projects.
