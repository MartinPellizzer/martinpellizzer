Ochna boiviniana

Ochna boiviniana, commonly known as the African yellowwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and palmate leaves with five to seven lobes. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, followed by red, fleshy fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and rocky habitats.

Ochna boiviniana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine.
