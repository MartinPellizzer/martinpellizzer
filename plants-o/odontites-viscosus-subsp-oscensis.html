Odontites viscosus subsp. oscensis

Odontites viscosus subsp.

oscensis, commonly known as the sticky toothed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy, often sticky leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its unique morphology and habitat preferences make it a distinctive member of the Apiaceae family.
