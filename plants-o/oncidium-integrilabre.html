Oncidium integrilabre

Oncidium integrilabre, commonly known as the broad-leafed orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, gracefully arching stems and large, broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green to white flowers with prominent, hairy lips. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining potting mix.

Oncidium integrilabre is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.
