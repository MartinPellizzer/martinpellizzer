Orthosiphon gofensis

Orthosiphon gofensis, commonly known as the Gofen's mint, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue in color. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported diuretic properties.

Orthosiphon gofensis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
