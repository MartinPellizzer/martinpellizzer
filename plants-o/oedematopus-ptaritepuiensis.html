Oedematopus ptaritepuiensis

Oedematopus ptaritepuiensis, commonly known as the Limpopo bladder grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its swollen, succulent stems that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette formation.

Oedematopus ptaritepuiensis is well-suited for xerophytic gardens due to its drought tolerance and unique appearance.
