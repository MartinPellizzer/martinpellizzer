Odontoglossum costatum

Odontoglossum costatum, commonly known as the Costate Orchid, is a tropical orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves and its clusters of fragrant, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests. Its flowers have a unique structure with prominent, tooth-like projections on the lip, which is a distinguishing feature of the genus Odontoglossum.

This species is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
