Oedera apiculata

Oedera apiculata, commonly known as the sharp-tipped fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in slow-moving freshwater habitats such as ponds and streams. The plant has finely divided, feather-like fronds that are often submerged or partially submerged. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Oedera apiculata plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
