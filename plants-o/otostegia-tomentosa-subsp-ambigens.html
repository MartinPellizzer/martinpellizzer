Otostegia tomentosa subsp. ambigens

Otostegia tomentosa subsp.

ambigens, commonly known as the woolly otostegia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
