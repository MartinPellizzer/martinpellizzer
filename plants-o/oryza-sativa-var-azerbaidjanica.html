Oryza sativa var. azerbaidjanica

Oryza sativa var. azerbaidjanica, commonly known as the Azeri rice, is a subspecies of rice native to the Caucasus region, particularly Azerbaijan.

It is characterized by its short, slender grains and typically grows in temperate climates with well-drained soils. The plant has a compact growth habit, with a primary stem and few branches, typical of many rice varieties. It requires a warm climate and ample sunlight for optimal growth and grain development.

This variety is valued for its adaptability and is often cultivated for both local consumption and traditional culinary uses.
