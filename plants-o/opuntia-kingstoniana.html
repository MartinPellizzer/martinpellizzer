Opuntia kingstoniana

Opuntia kingstoniana, commonly known as the Kingston cactus, is a species of prickly pear cactus native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems that are often covered in spines and glochids. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers, which bloom during the warmer months. Its fruit, known as a tuna, is edible and commonly used in local cuisines.

Opuntia kingstoniana is adapted to arid environments and plays an important role in the ecosystem of its native habitat.
