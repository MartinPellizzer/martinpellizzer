Ocotea eusericea

Ocotea eusericea, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its slender, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and dark green. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible for some bird species.

Ocotea eusericea is valued for its aromatic wood and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
