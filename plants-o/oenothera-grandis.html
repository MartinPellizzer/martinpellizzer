Oenothera grandis

Oenothera grandis, commonly known as the showy evening primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features striking yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically 2 to 4 inches in diameter, with five petals and a prominent central disk.

Oenothera grandis is known for its ability to thrive in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.
