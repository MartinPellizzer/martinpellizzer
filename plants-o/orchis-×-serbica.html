Orchis × serbica

Orchis × serbica, commonly known as the Serbian orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Orchis morio and Orchis mascula.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its flowers are bilaterally symmetrical, with three sepals and three petals, one of which is prominently hooded.

Orchis × serbica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in botanical gardens and natural reserves.
