Ocotea petalanthera

Ocotea petalanthera, commonly known as the Petalanthera Ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, fragrant flowers that are often white or pale yellow. Its fruits are small drupes that contain a single seed, and the tree can grow up to 30 meters tall in its natural habitat.

Ocotea petalanthera is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
