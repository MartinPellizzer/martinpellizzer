Orchis stupratoria

Orchis stupratoria, commonly known as the bastard orchid, is a species of orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers have a distinctive, spurred lip that aids in pollination by insects.

The orchid is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
