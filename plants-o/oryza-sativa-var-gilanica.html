Oryza sativa var. gilanica

Oryza sativa var. gilanica, commonly known as the Gilan rice, is a variety of rice native to the Gilan province of Iran.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated grains and a distinct aroma. This variety thrives in temperate climates with high humidity and moderate rainfall. It is typically grown in flooded fields, similar to other rice varieties, and requires careful management of water levels for optimal yield.

The plant's botanical features include a robust root system and a tall, erect stem with alternate, lance-shaped leaves.
