Oenothera adriatica

Oenothera adriatica, commonly known as the Adriatic evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often branched.

Oenothera adriatica is part of the Onagraceae family, which includes other species known for their showy flowers and medicinal properties.
