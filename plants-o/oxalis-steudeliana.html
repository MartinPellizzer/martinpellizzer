Oxalis steudeliana

Oxalis steudeliana, commonly known as the forest oxalis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features trifoliate leaves with three heart-shaped leaflets, often adorned with intricate patterns of veins. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Oxalis steudeliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
