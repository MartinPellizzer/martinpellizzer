Onopordum bracteatum subsp. ilex

Onopordum bracteatum subsp.

ilex, commonly known as the Iberian thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as scrublands and grasslands.

The plant has distinctive silvery-gray foliage and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are usually lobed or toothed, and the plant is known for its robust growth and adaptability to arid conditions.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
