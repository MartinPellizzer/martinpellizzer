Ophrys × medaglii

Ophrys × medaglii, commonly known as the Medaglian bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee, a feature that plays a key role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and thrives in sunny, open habitats. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brown, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Ophrys × medaglii is a rare and protected species, often found in specific microhabitats across southern Europe.
