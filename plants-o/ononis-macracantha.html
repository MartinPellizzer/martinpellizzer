Ononis macracantha

Ononis macracantha, commonly known as the broad-leaved sand-verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with a sprawling habit, often found in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically broad and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in arid environments.

Ononis macracantha is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
