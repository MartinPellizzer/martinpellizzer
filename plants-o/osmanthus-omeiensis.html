Osmanthus omeiensis

Osmanthus omeiensis, commonly known as the Hangzhou osmanthus, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late autumn. The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and elliptical in shape.

The fruit is a small, spherical drupe that matures in the winter months.
