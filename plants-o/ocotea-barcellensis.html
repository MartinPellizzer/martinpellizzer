Ocotea barcellensis

Ocotea barcellensis, commonly known as the Caribbean ocotea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the Caribbean islands, including Cuba, Hispaniola, and Jamaica. The plant is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree, typically reaching heights of up to 15 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy, dark green upper surface.

The flowers are small and clustered, and the fruit is a woody drupe that contains a single seed.
