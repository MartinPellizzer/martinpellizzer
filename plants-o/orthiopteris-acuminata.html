Orthiopteris acuminata

Orthiopteris acuminata, commonly known as the slender maidenhair fern, is a species of fern characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds.

It belongs to the family Pteridaceae and is native to regions of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its fronds are biennial, with the sterile fronds appearing in the first year and the spore-bearing fronds in the second.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with high humidity and partial shade.
