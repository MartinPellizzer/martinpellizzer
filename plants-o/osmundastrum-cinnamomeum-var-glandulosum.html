Osmundastrum cinnamomeum var. glandulosum

Osmundastrum cinnamomeum var. glandulosum, commonly known as the cinnamon fern, is a fern species characterized by its distinctive, gracefully arching fronds.

It features a thick, fleshy rhizome that supports its tall, upright stems. The fronds are typically bright green and have a soft, velvety texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

This variety is notable for its unique, cinnamon-scented spore cases, which contribute to its common name.
