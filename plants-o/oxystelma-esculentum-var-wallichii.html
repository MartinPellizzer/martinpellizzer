Oxystelma esculentum var. wallichii

Oxystelma esculentum var. wallichii, commonly known as the wallichii variant of the edible oxystelma, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant is notable for its edible tubers, which are rich in starch and used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and nutritional benefits, it is cultivated in some regions for both food and horticultural purposes.
