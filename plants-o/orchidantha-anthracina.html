Orchidantha anthracina

Orchidantha anthracina, commonly known as the black orchid, is a rare and striking plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its dark, velvety petals that range from deep purple to almost black. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the shade of the dense forest canopy. Its unique coloration and habitat make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss, Orchidantha anthracina is classified as endangered, highlighting the need for its protection.
