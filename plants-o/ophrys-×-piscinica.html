Ophrys × piscinica

Ophrys × piscinica, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant resulting from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera.

It is characterized by its intricate, bee-like flowers that have evolved to mimic female bees, a trait that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant is a perennial herb with a rhizomatous root system, typically growing in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers are rich in nectar and emit a subtle scent to further attract pollinators.

This hybrid species is of particular interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance.
