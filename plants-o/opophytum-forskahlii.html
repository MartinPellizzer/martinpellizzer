Opophytum forskahlii

Opophytum forskahlii, commonly known as the Socotra cypress, is a unique evergreen conifer native to the island of Socotra in the Indian Ocean.

It is characterized by its distinctive, umbrella-like crown and long, drooping branches that give it an otherworldly appearance. The plant has scale-like leaves that are tightly packed around the stems, contributing to its dense, compact form. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by cylindrical cones containing seeds.

Opophytum forskahlii is adapted to the arid and rocky environment of Socotra, with a deep root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.
