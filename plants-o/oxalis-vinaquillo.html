Oxalis vinaquillo

Oxalis vinaquillo, commonly known as the crimson wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of bright orange or red flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. Oxalis vinaquillo thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant foliage and showy blooms.
