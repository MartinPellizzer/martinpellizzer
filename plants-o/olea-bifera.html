Olea bifera

Olea bifera, commonly known as the olive tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Oleaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in warm, dry climates. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are elliptical and have a leathery texture. Its small, yellowish-green flowers develop into drupes, which are the olives used for oil and food.

Olea bifera is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in agriculture and culture.
