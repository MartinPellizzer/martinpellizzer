Oncidium naevium

Oncidium naevium, commonly known as the streaked orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features long, strap-like leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers with distinctive dark streaks. Oncidium naevium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.

Its growth is characterized by a sympodial habit, with new shoots forming at the base of the plant.
