Oxytropis sitaipaiensis var. brevidentata

Oxytropis sitaipaiensis var. brevidentata, commonly known as the short-toothed field vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves with short, rounded teeth. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Native to arid and semi-arid regions of China, it thrives in sandy or rocky soils.

This species is often found in open grasslands and along the edges of deserts, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
