Ocotea ceanothifolia

Ocotea ceanothifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian sandarac tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant typically grows in humid lowland forests and can reach heights of up to 30 meters. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a glossy dark green upper surface.

The tree produces small, white flowers followed by dark blue berries, which are an important food source for various wildlife species.
