Onoseris onoseroides

Onoseris onoseroides, commonly known as the golden coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, exhibiting a compact, herbaceous growth habit. Its yellow to orange ray florets are showy, while the central disc florets are tubular and often darker in color.

Onoseris onoseroides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
