Ophrys holosericea

Ophrys holosericea, commonly known as the bee orchid, is a species of orchid native to Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bee-like flower that mimics the appearance and scent of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brownish in color, with intricate patterns that aid in its mimicry.

Ophrys holosericea is a perennial herb with a rhizomatous root system, and it produces a single, erect stem bearing several flowers.
