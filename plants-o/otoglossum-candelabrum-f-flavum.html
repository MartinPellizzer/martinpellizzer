Otoglossum candelabrum f. flavum

Otoglossum candelabrum f. flavum, commonly known as the yellow-flowered orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, candle-like inflorescence, which gives it its common name. The plant features striking yellow petals and sepals, with a contrasting white lip, making it highly ornamental. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and requires specific humidity and temperature conditions to bloom.

Due to its rarity and beauty, it is often cultivated by enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
