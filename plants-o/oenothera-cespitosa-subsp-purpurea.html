Oenothera cespitosa subsp. purpurea

Oenothera cespitosa subsp.

purpurea, commonly known as purple evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, yellow flowers that open in the evening.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves and is often found in prairies and open woodlands. It is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun.

The subspecies is distinguished by its purple-tinged flower buds and slightly different leaf morphology compared to other subspecies of Oenothera cespitosa.
