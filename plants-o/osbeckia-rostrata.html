Osbeckia rostrata

Osbeckia rostrata, commonly known as the narrowleaf sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is a low-growing perennial with slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Osbeckia rostrata is adapted to sandy and well-drained soils, making it a hardy species in dry environments.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
