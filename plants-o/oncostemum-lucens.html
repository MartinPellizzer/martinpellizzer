Oncostemum lucens

Oncostemum lucens, commonly known as the golden cupplant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features a basal rosette of heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces striking yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, arranged in cup-shaped clusters. Its flowering stems are typically tall and slender, reaching up to 2 meters in height.

Oncostemum lucens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in moist, shady environments.
