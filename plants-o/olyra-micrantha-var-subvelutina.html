Olyra micrantha var. subvelutina

Olyra micrantha var. subvelutina, commonly known as the velvet-leaved olyra, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom during the wet season. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is valued for both its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
