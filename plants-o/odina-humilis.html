Odina humilis

Odina humilis, commonly known as the common marsh grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters and has slender, cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and arranged alternately along the stem.

Odina humilis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
