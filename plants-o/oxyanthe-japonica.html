Oxyanthe japonica

Oxyanthe japonica, commonly known as Japanese everlasting, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its propagation. Its foliage is typically narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Oxyanthe japonica is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and resilience in various climates.
