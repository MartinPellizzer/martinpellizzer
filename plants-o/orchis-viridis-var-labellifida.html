Orchis viridis var. labellifida

Orchis viridis var. labellifida, commonly known as the green orchid, is a species of orchid native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and a single, showy greenish-yellow flower with a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodlands. It blooms from late spring to early summer, and its flowers are pollinated by various insects, including bees and flies.

This variety is notable for its unique coloration and ecological role in supporting local pollinator populations.
