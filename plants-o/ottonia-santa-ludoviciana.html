Ottonia santa-ludoviciana

Ottonia santa-ludoviciana, commonly known as the Santa Ludovica orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant prefers cool, humid environments and is often found growing in shaded forest understories. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from decaying organic matter in the soil.
