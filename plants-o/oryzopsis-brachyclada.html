Oryzopsis brachyclada

Oryzopsis brachyclada, commonly known as the short-branched grass, is a species of grass native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with purple or brown.

Oryzopsis brachyclada is adapted to arid conditions and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
