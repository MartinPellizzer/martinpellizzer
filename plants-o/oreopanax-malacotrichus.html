Oreopanax malacotrichus

Oreopanax malacotrichus, commonly known as the Malacotrichus arrowleaf, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter, with a leathery texture and a deep green color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, followed by yellowish fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on tree trunks or in the understorey of dense forests.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
