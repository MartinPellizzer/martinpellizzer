Oncidium calimanianum

Oncidium calimanianum, commonly known as the Calimanian orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features a sympodial growth habit with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically arranged in racemes. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained, moist soils.

Oncidium calimanianum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
