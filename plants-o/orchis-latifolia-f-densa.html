Orchis latifolia f. densa

Orchis latifolia f. densa, commonly known as the dense-flowered broad-leaved orchid, is a variant of the broad-leaved orchid native to parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of violet or purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

The species is part of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
