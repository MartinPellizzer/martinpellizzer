Oenothera multijuga var. typica

Oenothera multijuga var. typica, commonly known as the plains evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats like prairies and roadsides, and it has a sprawling, herbaceous growth habit. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
