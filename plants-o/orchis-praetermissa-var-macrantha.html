Orchis praetermissa var. macrantha

Orchis praetermissa var. macrantha, commonly known as the large-flowered orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear a cluster of large, showy flowers with a distinctive violet or purple coloration. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or grassland habitats. Its flowers have a complex structure with three sepals and three petals, one of which is modified into a labellum.

This orchid is known for its ability to thrive in shaded environments and is often found in association with other orchid species in its native range.
