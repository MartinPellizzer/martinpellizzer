Onobrychis melanotricha var. minor

Onobrychis melanotricha var. minor, commonly known as the lesser melilot, is a perennial legume native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, calcareous soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, dark brown pods containing several seeds.

This species is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
