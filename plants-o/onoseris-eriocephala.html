Onoseris eriocephala

Onoseris eriocephala, commonly known as the woolyhead dandelion, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features a hairy, yellow-green stem and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its most distinctive feature is the fluffy, white pappus that surrounds the seed, aiding in wind dispersal.

It typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in open fields and along roadsides.
