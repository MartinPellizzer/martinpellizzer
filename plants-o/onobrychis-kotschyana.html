Onobrychis kotschyana

Onobrychis kotschyana, commonly known as the Kotschy's vetch, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is well-developed, allowing it to fix atmospheric nitrogen, which benefits soil fertility.

Onobrychis kotschyana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
