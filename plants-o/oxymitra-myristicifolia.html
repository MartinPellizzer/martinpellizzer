Oxymitra myristicifolia

Oxymitra myristicifolia, commonly known as the fragrant sandalwood tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 15 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and fragrant wood, which is highly valued in perfumery and traditional medicine. Its flowers are small and white, often arranged in clusters, and it produces small, round, reddish-brown fruits.

The tree thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
