Otopappus hirsutus

Otopappus hirsutus, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It has narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, helping to reduce water loss.

Otopappus hirsutus is adapted to harsh environments, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.
