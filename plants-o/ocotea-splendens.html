Ocotea splendens

Ocotea splendens, commonly known as the golden ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests. Its leaves are glossy, dark green, and emit a pleasant scent when crushed.

The tree produces small, white flowers that develop into dark blue berries, which are an important food source for various wildlife species.
