Origanum jankae

Origanum jankae, commonly known as Janka's oregano, is a species of flowering plant in the mint family Lamiaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe, particularly in the Balkans. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, tubular flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its essential oils, rich in compounds like carvacrol, contribute to its use in herbal medicine and culinary applications.
