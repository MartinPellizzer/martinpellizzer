Oenothera stenomeres f. typica

Oenothera stenomeres f. typica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved evening primrose, is a flowering plant belonging to the Onagraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and showy yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in open habitats such as prairies and disturbed areas. It is native to North America and is known for its medicinal and ornamental value.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its role in supporting pollinators.
