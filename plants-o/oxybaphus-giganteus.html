Oxybaphus giganteus

Oxybaphus giganteus, commonly known as the giant oxeye, is a striking plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are long, narrow, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and sturdy.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
