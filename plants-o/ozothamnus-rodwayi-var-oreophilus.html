Ozothamnus rodwayi var. oreophilus

Ozothamnus rodwayi var. oreophilus, commonly known as the oreophilus golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish to orange-colored flowers that bloom in late summer to autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its ornamental value lies in its long-lasting, showy inflorescences and drought tolerance.
