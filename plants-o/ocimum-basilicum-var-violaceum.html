Ocimum basilicum var. violaceum

Ocimum basilicum var. violaceum, commonly known as purple basil, is a variant of the common basil plant characterized by its striking violet-colored foliage.

This plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia. Its leaves are smaller and more compact compared to regular basil, with a slightly peppery flavor. Purple basil is often used in culinary applications and herbal teas, and it thrives in warm, sunny conditions.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens due to its vibrant leaf color.
