Olsynium scirpoideum subsp. luridum

Olsynium scirpoideum subsp.

luridum, commonly known as the yellow rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in the eastern United States. It typically grows in marshes, swamps, and other moist environments, where it thrives in nutrient-rich soils.

The plant features slender, cylindrical stems that are often reddish or yellowish in color, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, contributing to its overall appearance as a rush species. Olsynium scirpoideum subsp.

luridum plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
