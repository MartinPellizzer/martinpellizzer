Ornithogalum umbellatum var. hortense

Ornithogalum umbellatum var. hortense, commonly known as the garden star-of-Bethlehem, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant is characterized by its striking, umbellate inflorescence, which gives it its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers are fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and moths.
