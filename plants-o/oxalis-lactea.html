Oxalis lactea

Oxalis lactea, commonly known as the white wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive white or pale green coloration. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Oxalis lactea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and delicate flowers.
