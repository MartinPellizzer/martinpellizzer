Ornithogalum turbinatum

Ornithogalum turbinatum, commonly known as the turban lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots, which store nutrients for survival. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

The flowers bloom in spring or early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
