Oxalis sepium subsp. leptophylla

Oxalis sepium subsp.

leptophylla, commonly known as the slender-leaved wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia. It features trifoliate leaves with three heart-shaped leaflets, often tinged with reddish or purple hues.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental species in horticulture.
