Oxydectes salutaris

Oxydectes salutaris, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom on long, erect stems above the water surface. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that are usually floating or submerged depending on the species. Its roots system helps stabilize the soil and improve water quality.

Oxydectes salutaris is also valued for its cultural and medicinal significance in many traditional practices.
