Odontoglossum funis

Odontoglossum funis, commonly known as the funnel orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulbous structure, which helps it store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, with a distinctive funnel-shaped lip. It thrives in cool, moist environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Odontoglossum funis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
