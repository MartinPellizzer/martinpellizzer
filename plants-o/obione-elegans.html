Obione elegans

Obione elegans, commonly known as the elegant orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with intricate patterns that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its rarity and beauty, Obione elegans is highly valued in horticulture and conservation efforts.
