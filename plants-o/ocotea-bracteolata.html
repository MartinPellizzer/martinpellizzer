Ocotea bracteolata

Ocotea bracteolata, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, dark berries. The plant typically grows in humid lowland forests and has a dense canopy that provides habitat for various wildlife species. Its bark is often used in traditional medicine, and its wood is valued for its durability and aromatic properties.

The species plays an important ecological role in its native ecosystems.
