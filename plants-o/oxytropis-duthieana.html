Oxytropis duthieana

Oxytropis duthieana, commonly known as Duthie's locoweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically purple or pink in color. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.

Oxytropis duthieana is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility in arid environments.
