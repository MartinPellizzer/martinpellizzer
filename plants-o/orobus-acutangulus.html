Orobus acutangulus

Orobus acutangulus, commonly known as the sharp-angled orobus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and pod-bearing fruits. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its small, pea-like flowers are usually pink or purple, adding a delicate touch to its rugged habitat.

The leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being narrowly elliptical and slightly hairy.
