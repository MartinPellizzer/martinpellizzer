Odontorrhynchus chilensis

Odontorrhynchus chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of Chile.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its intricate, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from rocky soils, and it often forms symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
