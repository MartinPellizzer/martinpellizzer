Opuntia microdasys

Opuntia microdasys, commonly known as the Indian fig cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems that are often ribbed and covered in small spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tun, is edible and contains small, sweet seeds.

Opuntia microdasys is well adapted to arid environments and is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant.
