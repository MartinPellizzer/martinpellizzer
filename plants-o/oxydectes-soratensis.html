Oxydectes soratensis

Oxydectes soratensis, commonly known as the Soretan flameleaf, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, brightly colored leaves that range from red to orange, often with a glossy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
