Orchis latifolia subsp. maurusia

Orchis latifolia subsp.

maurusia, commonly known as the broad-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces a spike of pale yellow or greenish-white flowers with a distinct lip. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
