Ophrys lojaconoi

Ophrys lojaconoi, commonly known as the Lojacono's bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the mountainous regions of southern Italy.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which closely resembles a female bee, a mimicry adaptation that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant produces a single, showy flower per stem, with a dark, bee-like face and a hairy, greenish-yellow labellum. It thrives in calcareous soils and requires specific microclimatic conditions, making it highly sensitive to environmental changes.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Ophrys lojaconoi is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts.
