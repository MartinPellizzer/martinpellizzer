Ochreata polystachya

Ochreata polystachya, commonly known as the golden feather grass, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery inflorescences that produce small, golden-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in dense clusters at the base.

Ochreata polystachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic contribution.
