Oxalis stictophylla

Oxalis stictophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features delicate, trifoliate leaves with a silvery-blue hue, which are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
