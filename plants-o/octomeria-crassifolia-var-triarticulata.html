Octomeria crassifolia var. triarticulata

Octomeria crassifolia var. triarticulata, commonly known as the three-jointed octomeria, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that are segmented into three distinct joints, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are typically small and rounded, adapted to store water in arid environments.

This variety is valued for its unique morphology and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry gardens.
