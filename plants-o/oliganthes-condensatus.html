Oliganthes condensatus

Oliganthes condensatus, commonly known as the dense thistle, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, spiny structure with dense, woolly leaves and purple or pinkish flower heads. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well adapted to harsh, dry environments. Its flowers are typically short-lived, blooming in the spring or early summer.

Oliganthes condensatus is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
