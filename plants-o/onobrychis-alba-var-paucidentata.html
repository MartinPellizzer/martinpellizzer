Onobrychis alba var. paucidentata

Onobrychis alba var. paucidentata, commonly known as white sweet clover, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with small, rounded leaflets and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a fibrous root system and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it valuable in agricultural settings.

Additionally, it has been used historically in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
