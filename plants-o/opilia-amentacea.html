Opilia amentacea

Opilia amentacea, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape and have a silvery-white underside. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in terminal spikes and are followed by small, spherical fruits.

Opilia amentacea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
