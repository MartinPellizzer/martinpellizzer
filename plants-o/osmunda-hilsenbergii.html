Osmunda hilsenbergii

Osmunda hilsenbergii, commonly known as the Hilsenberg's water fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, compound fronds that can reach up to 2 meters in length. The plant typically grows in wet, shaded environments such as marshes and along riverbanks. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Osmunda hilsenbergii plays an important ecological role in maintaining the biodiversity of its habitat.
