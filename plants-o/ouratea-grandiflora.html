Ouratea grandiflora

Ouratea grandiflora, commonly known as the wax tree or waxleaf ouratea, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.

Ouratea grandiflora prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for a variety of garden settings.
