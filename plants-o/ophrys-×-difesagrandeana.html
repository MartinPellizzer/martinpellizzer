Ophrys × difesagrandeana

Ophrys × difesagrandeana, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a rare and fascinating plant that results from the cross between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys fucata.

It is characterized by its unique floral morphology, which mimics the appearance of female bees to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodlands and meadows, across parts of Europe. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and it produces striking, bee-like flowers with intricate patterns.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Ophrys × difesagrandeana is considered a valuable subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
