Ophrys × aestivalis

Ophrys × aestivalis, commonly known as the early bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species that exhibits fascinating botanical characteristics.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant with a rosette of basal leaves and a tall flowering stem that produces a single spike of flowers. The flowers are highly specialized, featuring a lip that mimics the appearance of a female bee, a trait known as sexual deception. This mimicry is crucial for attracting male bees for pollination.

The plant thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges across Europe.
