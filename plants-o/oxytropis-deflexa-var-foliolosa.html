Oxytropis deflexa var. foliolosa

Oxytropis deflexa var. foliolosa, commonly known as the bentleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are often hairy and have a distinctive, folded appearance.

It produces small, yellow to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.
