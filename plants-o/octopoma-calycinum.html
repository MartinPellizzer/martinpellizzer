Octopoma calycinum

Octopoma calycinum, commonly known as the California octopus plant, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features a rosette of fleshy, waxy leaves that are typically green with a bluish tint. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
