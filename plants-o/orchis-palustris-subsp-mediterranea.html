Orchis palustris subsp. mediterranea

Orchis palustris subsp.

mediterranea, commonly known as the Mediterranean marsh orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often near water bodies such as lakes, ponds, and damp meadows.

The plant features a tall stem with a single, showy, violet or purple flower that is adorned with intricate patterns. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

This subspecies is known for its ecological importance in supporting pollinators and maintaining wetland biodiversity.
