Oxera pulchella var. microcalyx

Oxera pulchella var. microcalyx, commonly known as the small-flowered oxera, is a perennial shrub native to New Zealand.

It features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during late spring to early summer. The plant has ovate to elliptical leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its compact growth habit and delicate flowers make it a desirable ornamental plant in gardens.
