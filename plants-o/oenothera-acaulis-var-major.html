Oenothera acaulis var. major

Oenothera acaulis var. major, commonly known as the showy evening primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features showy, yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has a short, upright stem with few branches and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Oenothera acaulis var. major is known for its ecological importance and is often found in dry, open habitats.
