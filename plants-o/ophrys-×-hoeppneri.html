Ophrys × hoeppneri

Ophrys × hoeppneri, commonly known as the bee orchid hybrid, is a fascinating plant with unique botanical features.

It is a natural hybrid between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera, resulting in a plant that exhibits traits from both parent species. The orchid is characterized by its striking, bee-like flower structure, which plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of female bees, attracting male bees for pollination.

This plant thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats across Europe.
