Osmunda bavarica

Osmunda bavarica, commonly known as the Bavarian water fern, is a species of fern native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that are divided into a central stalk and numerous pinnae, which are feather-like in appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters called sori.

Osmunda bavarica plays an important ecological role by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
