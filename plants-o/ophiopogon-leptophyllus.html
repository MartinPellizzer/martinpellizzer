Ophiopogon leptophyllus

Ophiopogon leptophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf spathiphyllum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which consists of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for shaded garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and subtle floral display.
