Ormosia nuda

Ormosia nuda, commonly known as the naked ormosia, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Ormosia nuda is often found in tropical and subtropical forests, growing in well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
