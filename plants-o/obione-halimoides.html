Obione halimoides

Obione halimoides, commonly known as the sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Obione halimoides is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in harsh environments.
