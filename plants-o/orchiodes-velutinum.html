Orchiodes velutinum

Orchiodes velutinum, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety purple flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

The flowers bloom in clusters, often displaying a complex structure with intricate patterns.
