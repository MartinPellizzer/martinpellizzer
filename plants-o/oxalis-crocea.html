Oxalis crocea

Oxalis crocea, commonly known as the golden oxalis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and blooms.

Oxalis crocea is also known for its ability to thrive in containers, making it a popular choice for gardeners.
