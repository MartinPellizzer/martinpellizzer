Oxalis verecunda

Oxalis verecunda, commonly known as the false shamrock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features trifoliate leaves that resemble those of the shamrock, with three heart-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Oxalis verecunda is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
