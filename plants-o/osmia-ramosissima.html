Osmia ramosissima

Osmia ramosissima, commonly known as the hairy leaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Osmia ramosissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
