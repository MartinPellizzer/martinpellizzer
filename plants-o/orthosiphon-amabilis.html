Orthosiphon amabilis

Orthosiphon amabilis, commonly known as the sweet senna, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and pairs of opposite leaves that are lanceolate in shape. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, which bloom during the rainy season. Its botanical name reflects its ornamental value and medicinal uses in traditional medicine.

Orthosiphon amabilis is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is also used in herbal remedies for its diuretic properties.
