Oxytropis bicolor f. luteola

Oxytropis bicolor f. luteola, commonly known as yellow globemallow, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces legume pods that contain several seeds.

This variety is distinguished by its yellow flowers, which contrast with the more common white or pink flowers of the species.
