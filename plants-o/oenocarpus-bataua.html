Oenocarpus bataua

Oenocarpus bataua, commonly known as the African oil palm, is a tropical plant native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves and produces fruit that contains a high oil content. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its fruit is harvested for both its oil and edible kernel, making it economically significant in the region.

The plant also plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
