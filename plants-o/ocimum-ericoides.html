Ocimum ericoides

Ocimum ericoides, commonly known as the Cape sage or mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their essential oils. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture. Its flowers are typically small, tubular, and clustered in spikes, producing a fragrant scent that attracts pollinators.

Ocimum ericoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
