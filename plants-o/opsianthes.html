Opsianthes

Opsianthes, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive golden-yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
