Osmanthus aquifolium var. myrtifolius

Osmanthus aquifolium var. myrtifolius, commonly known as the myrtle-leaved osmanthus, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its dark green, elliptical leaves that have a slightly serrated edge and a fragrant, honey-scented flower. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in clusters during late summer to early autumn. Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and aromatic blooms, making it a popular choice in gardens.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, preferring temperate climates.
