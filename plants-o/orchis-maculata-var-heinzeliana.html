Orchis maculata var. heinzeliana

Orchis maculata var. heinzeliana, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or meadow habitats. The plant features a tall, slender stem that bears a single spike of violet or purple flowers with a distinctive dark purple or brownish spotting. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The species is notable for its role in supporting pollinators and its ecological significance in diverse habitats.
