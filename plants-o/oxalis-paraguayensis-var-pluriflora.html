Oxalis paraguayensis var. pluriflora

Oxalis paraguayensis var. pluriflora, commonly known as the Paraguay wood sorrel, is a species native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its foliage and flowers are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
