Oxalis raimondii

Oxalis raimondii, commonly known as the red oxalis, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is a perennial herb that produces distinctive, spiral-shaped seed pods after flowering.

Oxalis raimondii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and flowers.
