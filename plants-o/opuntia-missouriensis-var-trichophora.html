Opuntia missouriensis var. trichophora

Opuntia missouriensis var. trichophora, commonly known as the Missouri prickly pear, is a cactus species native to the central United States.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in clusters of spines and glochids. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, red-fleshed fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in prairies and rocky soils.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
