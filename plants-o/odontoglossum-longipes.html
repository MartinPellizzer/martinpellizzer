Odontoglossum longipes

Odontoglossum longipes, commonly known as the longiped orchid, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas at elevations above 2,000 meters. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, and it produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Odontoglossum longipes is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and adaptability to specific growing conditions.
