Orobanche insolita

Orobanche insolita, commonly known as the Indian barrenstar, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is native to regions of India and Southeast Asia. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers and has a short, erect stem with few leaves. It is often found growing on the roots of various grasses and legumes.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can cause significant damage to agricultural crops.
