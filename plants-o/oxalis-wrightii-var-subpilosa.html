Oxalis wrightii var. subpilosa

Oxalis wrightii var. subpilosa, commonly known as the woolly wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive woolly texture on the underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, acidic soils.

Its ornamental value lies in its delicate foliage and early blooming habit.
