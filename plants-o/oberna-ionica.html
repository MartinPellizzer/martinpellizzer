Oberna ionica

Oberna ionica, commonly known as the Ionian oak, is a deciduous tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Oberna ionica is valued for its ecological importance and historical significance in the region.
