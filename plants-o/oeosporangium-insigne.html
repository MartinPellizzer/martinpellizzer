Oeosporangium insigne

Oeosporangium insigne, commonly known as the noble spore cone, is a rare and intriguing plant native to certain high-altitude regions.

It belongs to the group of plants known as spore-bearing plants, specifically within the class of spore-producing fungi. The plant is characterized by its unique reproductive structures, which are called sporangia, housing the spores necessary for propagation. Its botanical significance lies in its role as an indicator species for specific ecological conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological specificity, Oeosporangium insigne is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
