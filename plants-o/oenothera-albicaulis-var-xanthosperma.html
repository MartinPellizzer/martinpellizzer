Oenothera albicaulis var. xanthosperma

Oenothera albicaulis var. xanthosperma, commonly known as the yellowflower evening primrose, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats like grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are erect and often branched.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
