Ornithophora

Ornithophora, commonly known as the bird's foot trefoil, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, which resemble a bird's foot, and its yellow or orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is native to various regions in Europe, Asia, and Africa, and it thrives in well-drained soils. Ornithophora species are often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, where they contribute to soil stabilization and provide nectar for pollinators.

Its ecological importance extends to supporting biodiversity through its role in nitrogen fixation and habitat provision.
