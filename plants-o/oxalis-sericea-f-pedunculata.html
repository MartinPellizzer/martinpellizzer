Oxalis sericea f. pedunculata

Oxalis sericea f. pedunculata, commonly known as the silverleaf wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, often followed by seed pods. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shady areas.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly used in native plant gardens.
