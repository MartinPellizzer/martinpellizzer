Ocotea sambiranensis

Ocotea sambiranensis, commonly known as the Madagascar laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a dark blue berry containing a single seed, and it is often found in humid, shaded forest environments.

Ocotea sambiranensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited use in traditional medicine.
