Onosma visianii subsp. taygetea

Onosma visianii subsp.

taygetea, commonly known as the Taygetean narrowleaf hedge mustard, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It typically grows in rocky and dry habitats, showcasing a compact growth form with narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant adaptations.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
