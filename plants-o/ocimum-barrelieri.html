Ocimum barrelieri

Ocimum barrelieri, commonly known as wild basil, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and oregano. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and medicinal properties.

Ocimum barrelieri thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.
