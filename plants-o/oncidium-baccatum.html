Oncidium baccatum

Oncidium baccatum, commonly known as the Christmas orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous flower spikes that bear numerous small, fragrant, yellowish-green to white flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air. Its blooming period occurs during the winter months, making it a popular choice for holiday decorations.

Oncidium baccatum is relatively easy to care for, requiring bright indirect light and consistent humidity to thrive.
