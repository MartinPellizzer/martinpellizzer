Orthosiphon woodii

Orthosiphon woodii, commonly known as Java tea or Malabar teak, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in moist, shaded environments. The plant features opposite, elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in spikes. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, brown seeds.

Orthosiphon woodii is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
