Ocotea rhynchophylla

Ocotea rhynchophylla, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a member of the Lauraceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, with smooth, dark bark and glossy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

Its aromatic leaves and wood have historically been used in traditional medicine and for crafting purposes.
