Oxalis monophylla var. minor

Oxalis monophylla var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves with distinctive white veins, and its flowers are typically yellow with five petals. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers. It is known for its ornamental value and ability to spread through rhizomes.

Oxalis monophylla var. minor is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
