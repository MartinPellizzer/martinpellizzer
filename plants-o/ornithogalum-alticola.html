Ornithogalum alticola

Ornithogalum alticola, commonly known as the mountain cowpea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are adapted for storing nutrients and water. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

Ornithogalum alticola is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
