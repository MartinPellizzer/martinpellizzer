Otites ventricosus

Otites ventricosus, commonly known as the balloon plant, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its swollen, fleshy stems that store water, enabling it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers during the rainy season, which are followed by spherical, balloon-like fruits. Its leaves are typically reduced to small, scale-like structures, further minimizing water loss.

Otites ventricosus is valued in horticulture for its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
