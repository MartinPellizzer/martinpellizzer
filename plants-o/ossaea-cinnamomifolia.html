Ossaea cinnamomifolia

Ossaea cinnamomifolia, commonly known as the cinnamon leaf plant, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, leathery leaves that resemble those of the cinnamon tree, hence its common name. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Ossaea cinnamomifolia is typically grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and fragrant properties.
