Ophrys × dekegheliana

Ophrys × dekegheliana, commonly known as the Dekeghel's bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its intricate floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are usually pale green to brown in color, with intricate patterns that aid in attracting male bees for pollination.

This species is valued for its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitats.
