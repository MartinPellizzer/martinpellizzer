Orchis turkestanica

Orchis turkestanica, commonly known as the Turkestan orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stem and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are often arranged in a raceme and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Orchis turkestanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
