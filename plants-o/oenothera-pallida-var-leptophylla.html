Oenothera pallida var. leptophylla

Oenothera pallida var. leptophylla, commonly known as the slender-leaf evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and showy yellow flowers that open in the evening. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by moths, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting nocturnal pollinators.
