Orthosiphon marquesii

Orthosiphon marquesii, commonly known as the Java mint or Malabar mint, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal properties. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy texture. Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically violet or purple in color, arranged in dense spikes.

Orthosiphon marquesii is valued for its aromatic leaves and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported diuretic and anti-inflammatory effects.
