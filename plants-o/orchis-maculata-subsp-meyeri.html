Orchis maculata subsp. meyeri

Orchis maculata subsp.

meyeri, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Europe. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, calcareous grasslands and woodlands.

The plant features showy, violet-purple flowers with a distinctive yellow-green throat and spurred lip. Its roots contain a variety of alkaloids, which have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

The subspecies meyeri is distinguished by its slightly different floral morphology and geographic distribution compared to the main species.
