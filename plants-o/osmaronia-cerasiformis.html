Osmaronia cerasiformis

Osmaronia cerasiformis, commonly known as the cherry-shaped tree, is a unique species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, cherry-like fruit that is both ornamental and edible. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, fragrant flowers in the spring. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, though it can develop into a small tree under favorable conditions.

The botanical features of Osmaronia cerasiformis make it a valuable species for both horticultural and ecological studies.
