Oxytropis surmandehi

Oxytropis surmandehi, commonly known as the Surmandehi locoweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-bearing fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic bacteria. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, yellowish flowers in early summer.

Oxytropis surmandehi is known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock.
