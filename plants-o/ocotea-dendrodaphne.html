Ocotea dendrodaphne

Ocotea dendrodaphne, commonly known as the fragrant evergreen or tree of life, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a glossy surface with a distinct scent.

The fruit is a small, dark berry that contains a single seed, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
