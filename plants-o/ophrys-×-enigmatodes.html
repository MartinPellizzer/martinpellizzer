Ophrys × enigmatodes

Ophrys × enigmatodes, commonly known as the enigmatic orchid, is a hybrid species within the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its unique floral morphology, featuring a complex, mimicry-based structure that resembles female insects to attract male pollinators. The plant typically grows in temperate regions, thriving in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands or meadows. Its flowers exhibit a range of colors, often with intricate patterns that aid in pollination through sexual deception.

Due to its hybrid nature, Ophrys × enigmatodes displays genetic variability and is of interest in both botanical research and conservation efforts.
