Olea europaea var. hispanica

Olea europaea var. hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish olive, is a variety of the olive tree native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into the familiar olive fruit. The plant thrives in Mediterranean climates, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight. It is valued for its oil-rich fruits, which are used in culinary and industrial applications.

The tree is also notable for its longevity, with some specimens living over 1,000 years.
