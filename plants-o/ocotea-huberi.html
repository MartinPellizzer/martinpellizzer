Ocotea huberi

Ocotea huberi, commonly known as the Guianese ocotea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the tropical rainforests of South America, particularly in Guyana, Suriname, and northern Brazil. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage and aromatic leaves, which emit a subtle spice-like scent. It typically grows as a tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 15 meters in its natural habitat.

Ocotea huberi is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its fragrant properties.
