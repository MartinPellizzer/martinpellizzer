Oncidium funckii

Oncidium funckii, commonly known as the Funck's orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, yellow-green flowers with red markings. The plant typically blooms during the dry season, showcasing its adaptability to seasonal changes. Oncidium funckii has pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in arid environments.

It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
