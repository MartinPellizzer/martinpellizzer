Opuntia escuintlensis

Opuntia escuintlensis, commonly known as the Escuintla prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems that are covered in spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit, known as a tunas, is edible and often used in local cuisine.

Opuntia escuintlensis is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various animals.
