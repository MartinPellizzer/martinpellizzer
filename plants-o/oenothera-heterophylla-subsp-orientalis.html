Oenothera heterophylla subsp. orientalis

Oenothera heterophylla subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as the oriental evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Middle East. It is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open habitats like grasslands and along riverbanks. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and vary in size depending on environmental conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its unique blooming behavior.
