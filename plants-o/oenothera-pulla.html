Oenothera pulla

Oenothera pulla, commonly known as the pale evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oily seeds. Oenothera pulla is a herbaceous perennial with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
