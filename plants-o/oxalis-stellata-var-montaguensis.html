Oxalis stellata var. montaguensis

Oxalis stellata var. montaguensis, commonly known as the Montagu's wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, star-shaped yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky outcrops. It belongs to the family Oxalidaceae and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in partial shade and well-drained soil.
