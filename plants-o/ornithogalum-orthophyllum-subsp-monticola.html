Ornithogalum orthophyllum subsp. monticola

Ornithogalum orthophyllum subsp.

monticola, commonly known as the mountain star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant is characterized by its erect, flowering stems that rise above the foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

Its botanical structure is adapted to survive in arid and semi-arid environments.
