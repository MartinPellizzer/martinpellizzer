Octoknema affinis

Octoknema affinis, commonly known as the Indian wild bean, is a small shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically yellow or white in color. Its fruits are pod-like structures that contain several seeds.

Octoknema affinis is often found in moist, shaded environments and is used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
