Oxytropis holanshanensis

Oxytropis holanshanensis, commonly known as the Holan Shan locoweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Due to its restricted habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
