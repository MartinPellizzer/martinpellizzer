Ophrys × devillersiana

Ophrys × devillersiana, commonly known as the Devillers' bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its intricate floral structure, which closely resembles a female bee, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats. Its flowers are usually a mix of brown and yellow, with a distinctive lip that mimics the appearance of a bee's abdomen.

Ophrys × devillersiana is a rare and protected species, often found in specific microhabitats across the Mediterranean region.
