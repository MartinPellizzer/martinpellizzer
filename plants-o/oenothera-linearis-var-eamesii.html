Oenothera linearis var. eamesii

Oenothera linearis var. eamesii, commonly known as the Eames' linear evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and showy yellow flowers that open in the evening. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open plains. Its flowers have a distinctive tubular shape and are pollinated primarily by moths.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting nocturnal pollinators.
