Osmunda cinnamomea f. angusta

Osmunda cinnamomea f. angusta, commonly known as narrowleaf cinnamon fern, is a fern species characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped fronds.

It belongs to the family Osmundaceae and is native to eastern North America. The plant features a tall, slender sterile frond that is typically less than 1 meter in length. Its spore-bearing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fertile fronds.

This fern thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
