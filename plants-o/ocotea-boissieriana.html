Ocotea boissieriana

Ocotea boissieriana, commonly known as the Guiana pepper or Guiana cinnamon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the rainforests of northern South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as cinnamon and laurel. The tree is characterized by its smooth, dark-barked trunk and lance-shaped, glossy green leaves. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically white or pale yellow, and it produces dark blue to black berries that are edible.

The plant is valued for its aromatic oils and has traditional uses in local medicine and as a spice.
