Olea europaea subsp. cerasiformis

Olea europaea subsp.

cerasiformis, commonly known as the cherry-leaf olive, is a subspecies of the olive tree native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its distinctive, rounded, cherry-like leaves that are typically a deep green with a silvery underside.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to dark purple to black berries, which are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. It is a shrub or small tree that thrives in well-drained soils and is relatively drought-tolerant.

This subspecies is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
