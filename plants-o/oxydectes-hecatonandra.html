Oxydectes hecatonandra

Oxydectes hecatonandra, commonly known as the thousand-leaved tree, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, palm-like foliage composed of hundreds of narrow, lance-shaped leaves arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of white or yellow. Its unique leaf structure and ornamental value make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and indoor cultivation.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in humid environments with indirect sunlight.
