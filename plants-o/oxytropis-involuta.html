Oxytropis involuta

Oxytropis involuta, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow or pale yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.
