Oxalis praetervisa

Oxalis praetervisa, commonly known as the yellow woodsorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces bright orange to yellow flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It has a creeping growth habit and produces small, heart-shaped leaves with reddish veins.

Oxalis praetervisa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and flowers.
