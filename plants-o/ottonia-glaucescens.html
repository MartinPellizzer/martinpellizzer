Ottonia glaucescens

Ottonia glaucescens, commonly known as the blue tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central America.

It features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant, white to pale blue flowers during the summer months. Ottonia glaucescens is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and pleasant aroma.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
