Oxalis fruticosa

Oxalis fruticosa, commonly known as the wood sorrel, is a perennial flowering plant native to South America.

It features a sprawling shrub-like growth habit with hairy stems and trifoliate leaves resembling those of clover. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red, often with five petals. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Oxalis fruticosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
