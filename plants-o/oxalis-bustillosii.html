Oxalis bustillosii

Oxalis bustillosii, commonly known as the Mexican wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.

Oxalis bustillosii is also valued for its ability to tolerate drought conditions once established.
