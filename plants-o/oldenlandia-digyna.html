Oldenlandia digyna

Oldenlandia digyna, commonly known as the silverleaf spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its delicate, small white or pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes, making it both a useful ornamental plant and a potential weed.
