Oxalis ricardii

Oxalis ricardii, commonly known as the yellow woodsorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each leaflet resembling a heart shape, and clusters of five-petaled yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Oxalis ricardii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
