Oxalis divergens

Oxalis divergens, commonly known as the false shamrock, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which resemble those of the true shamrock. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically appearing in spring. Its stems are often hairy, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soil.

Oxalis divergens is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
