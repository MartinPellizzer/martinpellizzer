Orthodon lanceolatus

Orthodon lanceolatus, commonly known as the lanceleaf orthodon, is a herbaceous plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hence the species name, and are often covered in fine hairs.

Orthodon lanceolatus produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting various pollinators.
