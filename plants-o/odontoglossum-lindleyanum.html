Odontoglossum lindleyanum

Odontoglossum lindleyanum, commonly known as the Lindley's orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in humid, shaded environments at high altitudes. The plant features showy, fragrant flowers that are often violet or purple in color, with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the rich, organic soil in which it thrives.

Odontoglossum lindleyanum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
