Orchis mascula proles olbiensis

Orchis mascula proles olbiensis, commonly known as the Olbiensis Marsh Orchid, is a rare and distinctive member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in wet meadows and marshy habitats, preferring moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its flowers are usually a striking combination of purple and white, with a unique, spurred lip that distinguishes it from other orchid species.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, this orchid is considered endangered in many regions.
