Orthosiphon americanus

Orthosiphon americanus, commonly known as American horseweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its slender stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in dry to moist environments.
