Orchis odoratissima

Orchis odoratissima, commonly known as the sweet orchid, is a species of orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing a strong, sweet fragrance that attracts pollinators. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette at the base of the stem.

Orchis odoratissima is often found in meadows, woodlands, and other moist, well-drained habitats.
