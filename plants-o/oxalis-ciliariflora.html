Oxalis ciliariflora

Oxalis ciliariflora, commonly known as the fringed wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are often covered in fine, hair-like structures. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically pink or white, with five petals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Oxalis ciliariflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
