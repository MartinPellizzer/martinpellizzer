Oenanthe silaifolia subsp. media

Oenanthe silaifolia subsp.

media, commonly known as the Alpine water-dropwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in moist, rocky or alpine environments, often found in meadows and streambanks at high elevations.

The plant features slender stems with finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its flowers are small, white or greenish-white, and arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
