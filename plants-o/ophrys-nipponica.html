Ophrys nipponica

Ophrys nipponica, commonly known as the Japanese bee orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique floral structure that mimics the appearance of a female wasp, aiding in pollination by male wasps. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, and it produces showy, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip that plays a key role in attracting pollinators.

Ophrys nipponica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
