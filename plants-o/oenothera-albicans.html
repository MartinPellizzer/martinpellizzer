Oenothera albicans

Oenothera albicans, commonly known as the white evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant has slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers emit a mild fragrance and are pollinated primarily by moths and other nocturnal insects.

Oenothera albicans is also known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
