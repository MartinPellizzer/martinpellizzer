Ototropis recurvatus

Ototropis recurvatus, commonly known as the curly false brome, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in grassy areas and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, creeping stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its small, yellowish flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in the summer months.

Ototropis recurvatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
