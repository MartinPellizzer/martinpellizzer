Origanum cappadocicum

Origanum cappadocicum, commonly known as Cappadocian oregano, is a perennial herb native to Turkey and parts of the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes. Its essential oils, rich in carvacrol and thymol, contribute to its strong medicinal and culinary properties.

Origanum cappadocicum is often used in traditional herbal remedies and as a seasoning in various cuisines.
