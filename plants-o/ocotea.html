Ocotea

Ocotea, commonly known as the "false cinnamon," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Lauraceae.

It comprises approximately 200 species, primarily found in tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas. These plants are typically evergreen shrubs or trees, characterized by aromatic, often fragrant leaves. The bark and leaves of Ocotea species are used in traditional medicine and for flavoring foods.

Some species are also valued for their wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
