Octomeria gracilis var. paulensis

Octomeria gracilis var. paulensis, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with a delicate, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming during the summer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a threatened species and is often cultivated in conservation programs.
