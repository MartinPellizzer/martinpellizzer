Oxalis bicolor

Oxalis bicolor, commonly known as the false shamrock, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often divided into three parts, resembling the shamrock. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Oxalis bicolor is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to spread through runners.
