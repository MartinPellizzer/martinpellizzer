Oenothera hybrida var. ambigua

Oenothera hybrida var. ambigua, commonly known as the evening primrose, is a species of flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow or orange flowers that open in the evening, hence its common name. The plant produces elongated, ribbed seed pods that contain numerous small, oval seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in prairies and disturbed areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its oil-rich seeds.
