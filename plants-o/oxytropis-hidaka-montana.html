Oxytropis hidaka-montana

Oxytropis hidaka-montana, commonly known as the Japanese locoweed, is a perennial legume native to Japan.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces, and its stems are often branched and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or grassy habitats.

Oxytropis hidaka-montana is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
