Oenothera cuprea

Oenothera cuprea, commonly known as the copper evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats like grasslands and disturbed areas.
