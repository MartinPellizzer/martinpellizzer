Ocotea caudatoacuminata

Ocotea caudatoacuminata, commonly known as the narrow-pointed ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and fragrant bark. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within cloud forests, where it thrives in the understory. Its leaves are elliptical with a tapering tip, and the tree can reach heights of up to 20 meters.

The species is valued for its timber and has potential ecological and medicinal significance in its native habitat.
