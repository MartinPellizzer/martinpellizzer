Oxalis pentantha

Oxalis pentantha, commonly known as the five-lobed wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and small, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and woodland edges. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it produces capsule fruits containing several seeds.

Oxalis pentantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in garden settings.
