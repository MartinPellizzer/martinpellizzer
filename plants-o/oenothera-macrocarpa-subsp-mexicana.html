Oenothera macrocarpa subsp. mexicana

Oenothera macrocarpa subsp.

mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to Mexico. It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are sturdy and branching.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
