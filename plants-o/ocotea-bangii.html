Ocotea bangii

Ocotea bangii, commonly known as the Bangi's ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed surrounded by a fleshy pulp.

Ocotea bangii is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its essential oils and potential medicinal properties.
