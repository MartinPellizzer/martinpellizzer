Ochrosia coccinea

Ochrosia coccinea, commonly known as the red-leafed pittosporum, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red leaves, which contrast beautifully with its fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are glossy and leathery, while its flowers are clustered in panicles and bloom in spring.

Ochrosia coccinea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
