Ocotea marcescens

Ocotea marcescens, commonly known as the Brazilian camphor tree, is a member of the Lauraceae family and is native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a medium-sized evergreen tree that can reach heights of up to 25 meters, characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible for some bird species. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils, which have been traditionally used in folk medicine.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife.
