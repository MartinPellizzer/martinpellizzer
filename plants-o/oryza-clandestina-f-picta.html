Oryza clandestina f. picta

Oryza clandestina f. picta, commonly known as the painted rice plant, is a rare and ornamental grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage that displays shades of green, white, and pink, making it visually distinctive. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that resemble rice grains, though it is not typically cultivated for grain production. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its unique appearance, it is prized in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant.
