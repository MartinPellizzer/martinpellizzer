Oxalis elegans var. subuniflora

Oxalis elegans var. subuniflora, commonly known as the silver leaf oxalis, is a species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery bloom. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
