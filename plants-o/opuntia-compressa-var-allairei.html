Opuntia compressa var. allairei

Opuntia compressa var. allairei, commonly known as the Allaire's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems covered in clusters of spines and glochids, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months, followed by edible, fleshy fruits known as tunas. Its succulent nature allows it to store water efficiently, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Opuntia compressa var. allairei is also valued for its role in traditional medicine and as a source of food in some indigenous cultures.
