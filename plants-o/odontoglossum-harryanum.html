Odontoglossum harryanum

Odontoglossum harryanum, commonly known as the Harryan orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and pseudobulbs. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, typically arranged in a rosette pattern around the pseudobulbs.

Odontoglossum harryanum thrives in cool, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
