Onobrychis heldreichii

Onobrychis heldreichii, commonly known as the Balkan vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by wind. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and mountainous regions.

Onobrychis heldreichii plays a role in soil stabilization and is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability.
