Ocotea odorata

Ocotea odorata, commonly known as the fragrant ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves that emit a pleasant scent when crushed, and its small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a dark blue drupe, containing a single seed, and it is often found in humid lowland forests.

Ocotea odorata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
