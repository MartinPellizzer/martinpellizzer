Ocotea obliqua

Ocotea obliqua, commonly known as the Brazilian camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by oval, dark blue berries. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils, which have been used in traditional medicine.

Ocotea obliqua is valued for its timber and aromatic properties, making it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
