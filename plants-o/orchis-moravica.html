Orchis moravica

Orchis moravica, commonly known as the Moravian orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Central and Eastern Europe, particularly found in moist meadows and woodlands. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers with a distinctive lip petal, which is a characteristic feature of many orchids. Its roots contain alkaloids and have been used in traditional medicine.

Orchis moravica is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and maintaining biodiversity in its habitat.
