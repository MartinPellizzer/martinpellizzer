Olearia phlogopappa subsp. flavescens

Olearia phlogopappa subsp.

flavescens, commonly known as the yellow-flowered daisy, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow ray florets and dense, woolly foliage.

The plant typically grows in coastal and lowland areas, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, dark purple flowers that bloom in spring and early summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
