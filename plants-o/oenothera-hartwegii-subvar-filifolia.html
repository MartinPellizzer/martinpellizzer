Oenothera hartwegii subvar. filifolia

Oenothera hartwegii subvar. filifolia, commonly known as the threadleaf evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oily seeds. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential medicinal applications.
