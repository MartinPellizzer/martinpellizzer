Ocimum spicatum

Ocimum spicatum, commonly known as spike basil, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and lavender. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale blue flowers. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a strong, spicy aroma.

Ocimum spicatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and use in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
