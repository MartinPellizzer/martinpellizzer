Orchis maculata f. subintegriflora

Orchis maculata f. subintegriflora, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking purple and yellow flowers. The subspecies subintegriflora is distinguished by its more uniform flower coloration compared to the typical form. This plant typically grows in woodland habitats and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
