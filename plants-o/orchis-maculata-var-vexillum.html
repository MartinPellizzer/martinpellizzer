Orchis maculata var. vexillum

Orchis maculata var. vexillum, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive purple and white flowers with a yellowish throat. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or grassland habitats. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, spurred flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
