Onobrychis viciifolia var. culta

Onobrychis viciifolia var. culta, commonly known as the cultivated sainfoin, is a perennial legume native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for dryland farming. It has been traditionally used as a forage crop due to its high nutritional value for livestock.

Additionally, it plays a role in soil nitrogen fixation, contributing to sustainable agricultural practices.
