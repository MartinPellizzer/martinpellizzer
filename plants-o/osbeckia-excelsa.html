Osbeckia excelsa

Osbeckia excelsa, commonly known as the tall willow herb, is a flowering plant native to the northern regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its showy, yellow flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences and bloom in late summer.

Osbeckia excelsa is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
