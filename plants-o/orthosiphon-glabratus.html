Orthosiphon glabratus

Orthosiphon glabratus, commonly known as Java mint or water hyssop, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple, arranged in whorled clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water sources.

Orthosiphon glabratus is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its diuretic and anti-inflammatory effects.
