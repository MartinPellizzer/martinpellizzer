Opuntia violacea var. gosseliniana

Opuntia violacea var. gosseliniana, commonly known as the Mexican apple cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems that are typically green with a bluish tint. The plant produces clusters of small, pale pink to purple flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tuna, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

This cactus is well adapted to arid environments and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
