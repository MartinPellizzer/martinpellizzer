Onopordum acaulon

Onopordum acaulon, commonly known as the thistleweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, often spiny foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually covered in fine hairs and have a distinctive, sometimes prickly texture.

Onopordum acaulon produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by bristly, purple-brown seed heads.
