Oxalis porphyriosiphon

Oxalis porphyriosiphon, commonly known as the purple-stalked wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of South America.

It features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive purple-tinged stem, and its flowers are typically yellow with five petals. The plant belongs to the Oxalidaceae family, known for its calcium oxalate crystals in the leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
