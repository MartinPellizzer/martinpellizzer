Oxalis wallichiana

Oxalis wallichiana, commonly known as the Himalayan wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive reddish veins and produces small, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow or orange. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family, known for its calcium oxalate crystals in the leaves.

Oxalis wallichiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
