Orchis longicruris var. breviloba

Orchis longicruris var. breviloba, commonly known as the short-legged orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has lance-shaped leaves with a fibrous root system. Its flowers are arranged in a raceme and are often pollinated by bees and other insects.

This variant is distinguished by its shorter floral stems compared to the typical form of Orchis longicruris.
