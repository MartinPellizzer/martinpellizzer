Oldenlandia capensis var. pleiosepala

Oldenlandia capensis var. pleiosepala, commonly known as the Cape iron vine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

This variant is distinguished by its larger, more showy flowers compared to the typical form of Oldenlandia capensis.
