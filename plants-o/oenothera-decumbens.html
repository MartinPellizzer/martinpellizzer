Oenothera decumbens

Oenothera decumbens, commonly known as prairie evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. The plant features slender, decumbent stems that lie close to the ground, often rooting at the nodes. Its showy yellow flowers open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its role in ecological restoration projects.
