Oenothera pallida var. californica

Oenothera pallida var. californica, commonly known as the California evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that open in the evening and close by morning. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while its stems are erect and often branched.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
