Ocotea amazonica

Ocotea amazonica, commonly known as Amazonian ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon trees, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers that develop into dark blue berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and plays a role in the ecological dynamics of the rainforest.

Its wood is valued for its durability and resistance to decay.
