Oxalis scoparia

Oxalis scoparia, commonly known as the mountain wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with reddish veins and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Oxalis scoparia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
