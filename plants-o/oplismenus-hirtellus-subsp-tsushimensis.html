Oplismenus hirtellus subsp. tsushimensis

Oplismenus hirtellus subsp.

tsushimensis, commonly known as the Japanese nettle or Japanese horsewhin, is a flowering plant native to East Asia. It belongs to the family Urticaceae, which includes many stinging plants.

The subspecies is characterized by its finely toothed leaves and small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.
