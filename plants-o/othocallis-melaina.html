Othocallis melaina

Othocallis melaina, commonly known as black mountain grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its dark green, hairy leaves and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale yellow flowers, which are typically inconspicuous. Othocallis melaina thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of alpine vegetation in its native habitat.
