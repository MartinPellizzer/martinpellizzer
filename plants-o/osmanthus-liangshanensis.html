Osmanthus liangshanensis

Osmanthus liangshanensis, commonly known as the Liangshan osmanthus, is a rare and endemic species native to the mountainous regions of Sichuan, China.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 2,500 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a glossy, dark green surface.

The species is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional Chinese medicine and as a source of fragrant oils.
