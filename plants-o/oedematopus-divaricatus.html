Oedematopus divaricatus

Oedematopus divaricatus, commonly known as the yellow-barked tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, flaky bark that peels off in strips, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has compound leaves that are alternate and often have a yellowish or greenish hue. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange-red fruits that contain seeds.

This species is often found in rocky or wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental features.
