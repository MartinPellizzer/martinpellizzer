Opuntia mesacantha var. oplocarpa

Opuntia mesacantha var. oplocarpa, commonly known as the Desert Hedgehog Cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of cylindrical, spiny stems that often form low, sprawling shrubs. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by edible, red-fleshed fruits. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to arid environments.

This cactus is well-suited for xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in sandy or rocky soils.
