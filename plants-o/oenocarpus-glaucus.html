Oenocarpus glaucus

Oenocarpus glaucus, commonly known as the "sugar apple" or "chocolate apple," is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Arecaceae, which includes palms, and is characterized by its large, clustered inflorescences and distinctive, spiny fruits. The fruit, which is typically yellow to orange in color, contains a sweet, edible pulp surrounded by a hard, woody shell. Its growth habit and morphology make it well adapted to humid, tropical environments.
