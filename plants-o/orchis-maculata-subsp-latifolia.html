Orchis maculata subsp. latifolia

Orchis maculata subsp.

latifolia, commonly known as the Broad-leaved Hare's-foot Orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spotted leaves and showy, purple-pink flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or grassland habitats. Its flowers are borne on tall, slender stems and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is a relatively rare and protected species in many regions due to habitat loss and environmental changes.
