Oxytropis samurensis

Oxytropis samurensis, commonly known as the Siberian locoweed, is a flowering plant native to the cold climates of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow or pale purple flowers, which bloom in early summer. It is adapted to grow in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or steppe environments.

Oxytropis samurensis is known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock.
