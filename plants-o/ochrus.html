Ochrus

Ochrus, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the genus Calendula, which is part of the sunflower family, Asteraceae. The plant is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the summer months. Ochrus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Its botanical features include hairy stems, alternate leaves, and composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets.
