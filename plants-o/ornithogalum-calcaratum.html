Ornithogalum calcaratum

Ornithogalum calcaratum, commonly known as the rock onion or mountain garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a short, stocky rhizome and produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or limestone soils. It is adapted to arid environments and can thrive in full sun.

Ornithogalum calcaratum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
