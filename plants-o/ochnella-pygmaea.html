Ochnella pygmaea

Ochnella pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf ochna, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It typically grows to a height of less than 1 meter, featuring dense, dark green foliage and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces elliptical, red or orange berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. Ochnella pygmaea thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
