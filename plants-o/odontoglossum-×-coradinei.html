Odontoglossum × coradinei

Odontoglossum × coradinei, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a natural hybrid between Odontoglossum orchids and the genus Coradina.

It is characterized by its striking, large, and often colorful flowers that resemble those of its parent species. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its botanical features include elongated pseudobulbs and lance-shaped leaves.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its beauty in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
