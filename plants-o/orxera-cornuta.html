Orxera cornuta

Orxera cornuta, commonly known as the horned oryx, is a striking plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like foliage and distinctive, horn-like projections on its stems. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry, sandy soils. It plays an important role in stabilizing desert landscapes and providing forage for wildlife.

Orxera cornuta is also valued for its ornamental appeal in xeriscaping and drought-tolerant gardens.
