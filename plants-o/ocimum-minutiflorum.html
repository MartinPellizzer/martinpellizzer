Ocimum minutiflorum

Ocimum minutiflorum, commonly known as the small-flower basil, is a species of flowering plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb, similar to other basil species.

The flowers are usually pale blue to purple in color and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
