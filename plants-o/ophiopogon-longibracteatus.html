Ophiopogon longibracteatus

Ophiopogon longibracteatus, commonly known as the long-bract sedum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range from white to pale pink, often blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and subtle floral display.
