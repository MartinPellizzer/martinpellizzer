Olax pseudoleia

Olax pseudoleia, commonly known as the African olive, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Olacaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often leathery and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by round, fleshy fruits containing smooth, black seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in regions with low rainfall.

The tree is also valued for its hard wood and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
