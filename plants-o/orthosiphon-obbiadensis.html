Orthosiphon obbiadensis

Orthosiphon obbiadensis, commonly known as the African mint or wild mint, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a fragrant aroma. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in clusters.

Orthosiphon obbiadensis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
