Oxytropis glabra var. tenuis

Oxytropis glabra var. tenuis, commonly known as slender milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces yellow to reddish-purple flowers in early summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

Oxytropis glabra var. tenuis plays a role in stabilizing soils and supporting local pollinator populations.
