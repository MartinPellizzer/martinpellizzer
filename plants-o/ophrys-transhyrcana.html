Ophrys transhyrcana

Ophrys transhyrcana, commonly known as the Trans-Hyrkanian bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in mountainous regions, preferring well-drained and nutrient-poor environments. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brownish in color, with intricate patterns that enhance its mimicry.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Ophrys transhyrcana is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
