Orchis bifolia var. major

Orchis bifolia var. major, commonly known as the greater two-lobed orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive two-lobed flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. The plant has a rhizomatous root system and grows in moist, woodland habitats. Its leaves are grass-like and arranged in a basal rosette, while its showy flowers bloom from late spring to early summer.

This orchid is often found in shaded, calcareous soils and is considered a rare and protected species in many regions.
