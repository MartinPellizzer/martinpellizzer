Odontelytrum abyssinicum

Odontelytrum abyssinicum, commonly known as Abyssinian grass, is a perennial grass species native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, spikelet-like flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture, and it is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Odontelytrum abyssinicum is often used in traditional practices and has potential for ecological and agricultural applications.
