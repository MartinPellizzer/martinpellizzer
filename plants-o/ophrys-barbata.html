Ophrys barbata

Ophrys barbata, commonly known as the beard orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, with a lip that resembles a beard, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its flowers, which are usually pale green to brownish in color, bloom from late spring to early summer.

Ophrys barbata is known for its specialized pollination mechanism, often mimicking the appearance of female bees to attract male pollinators.
