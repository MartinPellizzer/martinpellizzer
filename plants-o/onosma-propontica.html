Onosma propontica

Onosma propontica, commonly known as the Crimean sandthrift, is a flowering plant native to the steppes of southern Russia and the Crimea.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender, often hairy stems that reach up to 30 centimeters in height. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters at the tips of the stems, typically blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered with fine hairs, and it thrives in dry, sandy soils.

Onosma propontica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
