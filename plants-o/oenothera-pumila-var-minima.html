Oenothera pumila var. minima

Oenothera pumila var. minima, commonly known as the dwarf evening primrose, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It features slender, hairy stems and small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant has alternate, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to full sun.

Oenothera pumila var. minima is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
