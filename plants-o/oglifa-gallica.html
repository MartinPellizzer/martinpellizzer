Oglifa gallica

Oglifa gallica, commonly known as the French violet, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate purple or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant belongs to the Violaceae family and is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks. Its flowers are typically borne on slender stems and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Oglifa gallica is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported healing properties.
