Oenothera fruticosa f. lucida

Oenothera fruticosa f. lucida, commonly known as the shining evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a shrub-like perennial with silver-gray foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant belongs to the Onagraceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, with a hairy texture that reflects light, giving it the "lucida" designation.
