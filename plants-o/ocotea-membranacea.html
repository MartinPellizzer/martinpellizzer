Ocotea membranacea

Ocotea membranacea, commonly known as the yellow sandarac tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves and bark. The plant typically grows in humid, lowland forests and can reach heights of up to 30 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth texture with a distinctive yellowish hue when dried.

The tree is valued for its essential oils, which are used in perfumery and traditional medicine.
