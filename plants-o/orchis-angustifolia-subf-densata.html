Orchis angustifolia subf. densata

Orchis angustifolia subf.

densata, commonly known as the dense-flowered narrow-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

The plant features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often densely packed. Its flowers are pale purple or white, arranged in a spike and characterized by their narrow, elongated shape.

This orchid is notable for its specialized pollination mechanisms and is often found in association with specific fungal mycorrhizal relationships.
