Ouratea nana

Ouratea nana, commonly known as the dwarf ouratea, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It typically grows to a height of less than 1 meter, featuring dense, glossy green leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for shaded garden settings.

The plant thrives in humid, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.
