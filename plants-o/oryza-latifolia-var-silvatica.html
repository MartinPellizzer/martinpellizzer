Oryza latifolia var. silvatica

Oryza latifolia var. silvatica, commonly known as the broadleaf ricegrass, is a perennial herb native to the moist forest understories of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale yellow flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense mats, contributing to soil stabilization in its native habitat.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
