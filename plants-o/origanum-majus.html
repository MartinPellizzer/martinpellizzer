Origanum majus

Origanum majus, commonly known as large savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers. Its foliage is often used in cooking, adding a pungent, slightly bitter flavor to dishes.

Additionally, it is valued in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
