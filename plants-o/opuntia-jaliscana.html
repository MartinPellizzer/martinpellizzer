Opuntia jaliscana

Opuntia jaliscana, commonly known as the Mexican prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems, which are covered in spines and glochids. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tuna, is edible and often used in traditional cuisine.

Opuntia jaliscana is well adapted to arid environments and plays an important role in desert ecosystems.
