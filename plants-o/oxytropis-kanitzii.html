Oxytropis kanitzii

Oxytropis kanitzii, commonly known as Kanitz's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale purple or white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Oxytropis kanitzii is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine environments.
