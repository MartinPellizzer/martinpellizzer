Ophrys × benacensis

Ophrys × benacensis, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the genus Ophrys, which is characterized by its complex and often deceptive floral structures designed to mimic female insects. The plant produces showy, bee-like flowers that are specialized for pseudocopulation, a pollination strategy where male bees attempt to mate with the flower. Ophrys × benacensis typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features include a slender stem, lance-shaped leaves, and a single spike of flowers with intricate, hooded petals.
