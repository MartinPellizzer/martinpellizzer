Ononis thomsonii subsp. thomsonii

Ononis thomsonii subsp.

thomsonii, commonly known as Thomson's sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States. It typically features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in early spring.

The plant has thin, wiry stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for xerophytic habitats and it is often found in open areas such as dunes or rocky slopes.
