Onoseris minima

Onoseris minima, commonly known as the small yellow coneflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. It features slender stems and small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late summer.

Onoseris minima is often used in native plant gardens due to its ecological value and ornamental appeal.
