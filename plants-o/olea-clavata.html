Olea clavata

Olea clavata, commonly known as the clustered olive, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that turn from green to black as they mature. Olea clavata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to dry, well-drained soils.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
