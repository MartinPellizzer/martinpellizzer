Opuntia crispicrinita var. cylindracea

Opuntia crispicrinita var. cylindracea, commonly known as the cylindrical prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems, which are segmented and covered in dense clusters of spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the summer months, followed by edible, red-fleshed fruits. Its leaves are modified into spiny stems, and it has a shallow root system adapted to arid environments.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its edible fruits.
