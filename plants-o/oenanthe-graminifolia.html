Oenanthe graminifolia

Oenanthe graminifolia, commonly known as slender water dropwort, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in umbels. The plant typically grows in moist or aquatic environments, such as marshes and stream sides. Its root system is often fibrous, and it can form dense colonies in suitable habitats.

Oenanthe graminifolia is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions due to its rapid spread and ability to outcompete native vegetation.
