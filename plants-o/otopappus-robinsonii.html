Otopappus robinsonii

Otopappus robinsonii, commonly known as the Robinson's horsebrush, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typically hairy stems. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along dry stream beds.

Otopappus robinsonii is adapted to withstand drought and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
