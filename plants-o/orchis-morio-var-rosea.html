Orchis morio var. rosea

Orchis morio var. rosea, commonly known as the rose-lilac twayblade, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and a single, pale pink to rose-colored flower spike. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing a single or occasionally two flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

The species is noted for its delicate appearance and is often found in association with other orchid species in its habitat.
