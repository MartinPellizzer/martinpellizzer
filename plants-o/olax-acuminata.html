Olax acuminata

Olax acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-pointed olax, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Olacaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces white or yellowish flowers that are often followed by small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Olax acuminata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
