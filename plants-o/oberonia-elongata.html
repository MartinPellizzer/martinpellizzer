Oberonia elongata

Oberonia elongata, commonly known as the elongated orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rainforests and mountainous regions. The plant features a slender, upright stem with a few leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a loose spike. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the forest floor.

Oberonia elongata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
