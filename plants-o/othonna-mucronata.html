Othonna mucronata

Othonna mucronata, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant has silvery-gray foliage with dense, velvety hairs that help reduce water loss in arid conditions. It produces small, spherical seed heads that are adapted for wind dispersal.

Othonna mucronata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its drought tolerance and attractive appearance.
