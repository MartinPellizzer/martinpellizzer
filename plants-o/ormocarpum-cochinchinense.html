Ormocarpum cochinchinense

Ormocarpum cochinchinense, commonly known as the Vietnamese orchid or Chinese orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, attaching itself to trees or rocks in its natural habitat. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The flowers bloom in summer and are pollinated by insects, contributing to its ecological role in its native environment.
