Oncidium hastatum var. atratum

Oncidium hastatum var. atratum, commonly known as the Black Orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black petals and sepals, which give it its striking appearance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. It produces fragrant, long-lasting flowers in shades of white, pink, and red.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
