Oxytropis glabrata

Oxytropis glabrata, commonly known as the naked locoweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America, particularly in the western United States. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, hairy stems, and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several narrow, lance-shaped leaflets.

Oxytropis glabrata is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and shrublands.
