Origanum vulgare subsp. viride

Origanum vulgare subsp.

viride, commonly known as green oregano, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly minty leaves that are typically a vibrant green, distinguishing it from the more commonly used purple oregano.

The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates.

Its essential oils contain compounds like carvacrol and thymol, which contribute to its culinary and medicinal uses.
