Opuntia macdougaliana

Opuntia macdougaliana, commonly known as the Macdougall's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flattened, ribbed stems that are covered in spiny areoles, which are specialized structures for housing the plant's defensive spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months, followed by edible, fleshy fruits known as tunas. Its succulent nature allows it to store water efficiently, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Opuntia macdougaliana plays an important ecological role by providing food and shelter for various desert wildlife species.
