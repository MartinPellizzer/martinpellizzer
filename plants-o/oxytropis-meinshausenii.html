Oxytropis meinshausenii

Oxytropis meinshausenii, commonly known as the Meinhausean locoweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of western North America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in late spring. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in poor, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.

It is also notable for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock.
