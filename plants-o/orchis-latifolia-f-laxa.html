Orchis latifolia f. laxa

Orchis latifolia f. laxa, commonly known as the loose orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, violet or purple flowers. This subspecies is native to Europe and thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant's rhizome system allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its habitat.
