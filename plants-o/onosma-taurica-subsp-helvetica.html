Onosma taurica subsp. helvetica

Onosma taurica subsp.

helvetica, commonly known as the Alpine onosma, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments.

The plant features slender stems and small, often blue or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in alpine gardens.
