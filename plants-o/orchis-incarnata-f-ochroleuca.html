Orchis incarnata f. ochroleuca

Orchis incarnata f. ochroleuca, commonly known as the white helleborine, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its pale green to white flowers, which contrast with its dark green leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands, where it thrives in rich, organic soil. Its flowers are usually arranged in a loose raceme, with each bloom having three sepals and three petals.

This orchid is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its unique floral structure and ecological significance.
