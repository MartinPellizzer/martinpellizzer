Oldenlandia crassifolia

Oldenlandia crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

The species is noted for its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes, making it both a useful ground cover and a potential weed.
