Oxycoccus quadripetalus f. guttatus

Oxycoccus quadripetalus f. guttatus, commonly known as the guttate oxycoccus, is a variant of the common cranberry.

It is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the northern hemisphere, particularly found in regions with acidic, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a waxy surface, and it is often used in ornamental gardening due to its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
