Oncoba stuhlmannii

Oncoba stuhlmannii, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental species.

Its rapid growth and vibrant floral display make it a notable botanical feature in its native habitat.
