Ocimum cristatum

Ocimum cristatum, commonly known as the crested basil, is a species of flowering plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its distinctive, crinkled, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically violet or white in color. It is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Ocimum cristatum is a perennial herb that thrives in warm, humid climates.
