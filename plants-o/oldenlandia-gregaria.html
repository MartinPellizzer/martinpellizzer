Oldenlandia gregaria

Oldenlandia gregaria, commonly known as the silver leaf vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers clustered in racemes. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a silvery or grayish underside, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

This species is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
