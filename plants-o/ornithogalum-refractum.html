Ornithogalum refractum

Ornithogalum refractum, commonly known as the fragrant bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking, bell-shaped white or pale purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as moths and bats. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Ornithogalum refractum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique floral structure.
