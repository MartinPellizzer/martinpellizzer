Oncidium tenense

Oncidium tenense, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a green throat and red or orange markings. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, warm environments. Its inflorescence consists of multiple racemes bearing fragrant, long-lasting blooms.

Oncidium tenense is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
