Oligochaeta tomentosa

Oligochaeta tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly worm plant, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, fuzzy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Oligochaeta tomentosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
