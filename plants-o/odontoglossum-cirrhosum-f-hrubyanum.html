Odontoglossum cirrhosum f. hrubyanum

Odontoglossum cirrhosum f. hrubyanum, commonly known as the Hrubyan's orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often violet or purple flower spikes. The plant typically produces showy, fragrant blooms with intricate petal patterns, making it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, humid conditions and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its aesthetic and horticultural significance.
