Origanum syriacum subsp. bevanii

Origanum syriacum subsp.

bevanii, commonly known as Syrian oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia. It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its essential oils contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its strong fragrance and medicinal properties.

This subspecies is often used in culinary applications and traditional herbal remedies.
