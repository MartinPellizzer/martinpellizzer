Oxytropis johannensis

Oxytropis johannensis, commonly known as the Johann's milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in the spring. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in dry, rocky habitats.

Oxytropis johannensis is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in certain ecological contexts.
