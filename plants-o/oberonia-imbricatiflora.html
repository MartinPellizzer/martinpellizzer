Oberonia imbricatiflora

Oberonia imbricatiflora, commonly known as the overlapping orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, moss-like foliage and produces small, clustered flowers with a distinctive violet or purple hue. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees in tropical rainforests. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Oberonia imbricatiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
