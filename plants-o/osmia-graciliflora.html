Osmia graciliflora

Osmia graciliflora, commonly known as the slender-flowered mason bee, is a solitary bee species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its small size and distinctive, slender floral structure that facilitates efficient pollination. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers sunny, open habitats. Its flowers are usually pale blue or violet, with tubular shapes that are adapted for pollination by its specific bee species.

Osmia graciliflora plays a vital role in the ecological balance by contributing to the pollination of various wildflowers and crops.
