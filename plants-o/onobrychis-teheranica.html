Onobrychis teheranica

Onobrychis teheranica, commonly known as Persian clover, is a perennial leguminous plant native to Iran.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in poor soil conditions.

Onobrychis teheranica is also valued for its potential in forage and soil stabilization.
