Ocimum procurrens

Ocimum procurrens, commonly known as creeping basil, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features slender, prostrate stems that grow close to the ground, often forming dense mats. The leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptical, with a fragrant, aromatic scent when crushed. It produces small, white or pale blue flowers in whorled clusters, typical of the Lamiaceae family.

This plant is valued for its culinary uses and ornamental appeal in gardens.
