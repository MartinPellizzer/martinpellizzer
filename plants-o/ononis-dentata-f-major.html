Ononis dentata f. major

Ononis dentata f. major, commonly known as the greater toothed cinquefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils, often in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. The plant features a sprawling or ascending growth habit with multiple stems that can reach up to 50 cm in height. Its leaves are palmately divided into five toothed lobes, and it produces small, yellowish flowers in early summer.

The species is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
