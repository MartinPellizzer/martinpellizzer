Oxalis novae-caledoniae

Oxalis novae-caledoniae, commonly known as the New Caledonia wood sorrel, is a species of flowering plant native to the island of New Caledonia.

It belongs to the family Oxalidaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments within the rainforest understory. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, bead-like fruits that contain the seeds.

Oxalis novae-caledoniae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
