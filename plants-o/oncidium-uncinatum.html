Oncidium uncinatum

Oncidium uncinatum, commonly known as the hooked orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant with a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. Its leaves are usually strap-like and arranged in a fan shape, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Oncidium uncinatum is popular in horticulture for its long-lasting blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
