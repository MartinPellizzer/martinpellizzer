Ononis natrix var. perusiana

Ononis natrix var. perusiana, commonly known as the Perusian sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant thrives in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in challenging conditions.
