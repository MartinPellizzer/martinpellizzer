Oenothera triloba var. watsonii

Oenothera triloba var. watsonii, commonly known as the Watson's evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is well adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

This variety is part of the Onagraceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological significance.
