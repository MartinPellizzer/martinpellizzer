Oxalis philippii

Oxalis philippii, commonly known as the Philippi's wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive veins and produces clusters of delicate, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its foliage is often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft appearance.

Oxalis philippii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
