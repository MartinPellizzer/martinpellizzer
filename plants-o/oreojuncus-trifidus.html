Oreojuncus trifidus

Oreojuncus trifidus, commonly known as the three-lobed rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that often grow in dense clusters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically green and covered with a fine layer of hair. Oreojuncus trifidus produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes, which are usually greenish or brown in color.

It is well adapted to dry conditions and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
