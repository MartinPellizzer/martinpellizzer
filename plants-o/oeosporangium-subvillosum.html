Oeosporangium subvillosum

Oeosporangium subvillosum, commonly known as the hairy spore sac plant, is a rare and specialized fern-like organism found in specific tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its unique reproductive structures called oospores, which are enclosed in a protective, hairy covering. The plant's morphology includes delicate, branching fronds that support the development of these spore sacs. Its botanical significance lies in its role within certain ecosystems and its potential for genetic research.

Due to its limited distribution and fragile habitat, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
