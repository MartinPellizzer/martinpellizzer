Orobus niger

Orobus niger, commonly known as the black orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black petals and sepals, which give it its distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are highly fragrant, attracting various pollinators such as bees and moths.

Orobus niger is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its cultural significance in some traditional practices.
