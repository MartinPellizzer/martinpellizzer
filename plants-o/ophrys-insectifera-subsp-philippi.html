Ophrys insectifera subsp. philippi

Ophrys insectifera subsp.

philippi, commonly known as the Philippi's bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It is characterized by its unique floral morphology, particularly its labellum, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination.

The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its flowers are usually a mix of yellow, orange, and brown, creating a striking visual resemblance to its pollinator.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in many regions.
