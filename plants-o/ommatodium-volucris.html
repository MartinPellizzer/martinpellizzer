Ommatodium volucris

Ommatodium volucris, commonly known as the sunspire lily, is a rare and striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, showy flowers that resemble miniature sunflowers. The plant's botanical features include lance-shaped leaves with a waxy surface and a unique inflorescence that consists of multiple overlapping bracts. It thrives in cool, sunny environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its limited habitat and vulnerability to climate change, Ommatodium volucris is considered an endangered species.
