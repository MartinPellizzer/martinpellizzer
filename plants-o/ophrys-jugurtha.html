Ophrys jugurtha

Ophrys jugurtha, commonly known as the southern bee orchid, is a species of orchid native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive floral structure, with a lip that mimics the appearance of a bee, aiding in pollination by male bees. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and thrives in open habitats such as scrublands and grasslands. Its flowers bloom in spring, producing a showy display of pale yellow and brown hues.

The species is notable for its specialized pollination mechanism, which relies on the mimicry of female bees to attract male pollinators.
