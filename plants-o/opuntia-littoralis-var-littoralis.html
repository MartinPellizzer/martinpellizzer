Opuntia littoralis var. littoralis

Opuntia littoralis var. littoralis, commonly known as the coastal prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the coastal regions of California and Baja California.

It is characterized by its flattened, fleshy stems that are covered in spines and are often ribbed. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red, spherical fruits. It is adapted to arid and coastal environments, with thick, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss.

Opuntia littoralis var. littoralis plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
