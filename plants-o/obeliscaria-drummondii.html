Obeliscaria drummondii

Obeliscaria drummondii, commonly known as the Drummond's rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately divided, and covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellow or white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by small, round pods containing seeds.
