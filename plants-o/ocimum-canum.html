Ocimum canum

Ocimum canum, commonly known as holy basil or tulsi, is a flowering plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia and is characterized by its aromatic, opposite leaves and small, pale purple flowers. The plant is known for its medicinal properties, with compounds like eugenol and rosmarinic acid contributing to its therapeutic uses. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.

Ocimum canum is also valued in traditional Ayurvedic medicine for its adaptogenic and antimicrobial properties.
