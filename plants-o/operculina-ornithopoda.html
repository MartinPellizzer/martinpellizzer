Operculina ornithopoda

Operculina ornithopoda, commonly known as the bird-foot orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, slender stems and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces a unique structure called an operculum, which covers the developing fruit and is shed once the fruit matures. Operculina ornithopoda thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological study.
