Ottonia eucalyptifolia

Ottonia eucalyptifolia, commonly known as the eucalyptus fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that resemble those of a fern, though it is not a true fern. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and along stream banks. Its leaves are usually green and have a slightly waxy texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Ottonia eucalyptifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with high humidity and indirect light.
