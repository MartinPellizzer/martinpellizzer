Ocimum tenellum var. pilosum

Ocimum tenellum var. pilosum, commonly known as the hairy basil, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, hairy textures. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its aromatic foliage, it is often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
