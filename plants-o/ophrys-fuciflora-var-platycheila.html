Ophrys fuciflora var. platycheila

Ophrys fuciflora var. platycheila, commonly known as the flat-lobed bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which closely resembles a female bee, a feature that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, often in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands. Its flowers are usually pale green or brown, with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of insects.

Due to habitat loss and fragmentation, this orchid is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
