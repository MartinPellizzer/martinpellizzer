Odina cinerea

Odina cinerea, commonly known as silverweed, is a perennial herb native to the northern hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and meadows. The plant has a creeping rootstock and produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. Its leaves are covered in a fine layer of silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Odina cinerea is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
