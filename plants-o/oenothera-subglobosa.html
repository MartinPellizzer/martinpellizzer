Oenothera subglobosa

Oenothera subglobosa, commonly known as the globular evening primrose, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening. The plant has a compact, globular shape, with thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by moths, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Oenothera subglobosa is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.
