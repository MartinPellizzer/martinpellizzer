Osmunda regalis var. brasiliensis

Osmunda regalis var. brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian royal fern, is a variant of the royal fern native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a deciduous fern characterized by its large, pinnately compound fronds that can reach lengths of up to 2 meters. The plant exhibits a distinct life cycle with a sporophyte stage that produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds. Its rhizome is thick and fleshy, allowing it to thrive in moist, shaded environments such as swamps and riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in maintaining wetland ecosystems.
