Oxalis cordifolia

Oxalis cordifolia, commonly known as heartleaf wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish or purple coloration on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling those of the genus Oxalis. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

Oxalis cordifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
