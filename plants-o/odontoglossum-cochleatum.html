Odontoglossum cochleatum

Odontoglossum cochleatum, commonly known as the spiral orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique spiral arrangement of leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant produces striking, dark violet to purple flowers with a distinctive, spurred lip. It typically grows in cool, humid environments at high altitudes.

Odontoglossum cochleatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
