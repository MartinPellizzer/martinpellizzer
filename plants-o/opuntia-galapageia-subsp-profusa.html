Opuntia galapageia subsp. profusa

Opuntia galapageia subsp.

profusa, commonly known as the Galápagos prickly pear, is a cactus species native to the Galápagos Islands. It is characterized by its flattened, paddle-shaped stems called cladodes, which are covered in small spines.

The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the dry season, followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in arid and coastal environments, adapting to the unique climate of the islands.

This subspecies plays an important role in the local ecosystem, providing food for various native animals.
