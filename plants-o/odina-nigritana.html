Odina nigritana

Odina nigritana, commonly known as the black nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically features alternate, ovate leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, violet to purple flowers. Its berries are usually black when mature and contain toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.

Odina nigritana is often found in disturbed habitats and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
