Oncidium insculptum

Oncidium insculptum, commonly known as the Orchid with the Engraved Lip, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, long-lasting inflorescences that feature vibrant yellow and green flowers. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are typically elliptical and arranged in a fan-like pattern.

Oncidium insculptum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
