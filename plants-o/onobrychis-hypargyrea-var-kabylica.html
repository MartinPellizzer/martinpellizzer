Onobrychis hypargyrea var. kabylica

Onobrychis hypargyrea var. kabylica, commonly known as the Kabylic mountain clover, is a perennial leguminous plant native to the Balkan region.

It is characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats. It has a creeping growth habit and can form dense mats, making it useful for soil stabilization.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
