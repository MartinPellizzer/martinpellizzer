Oldenlandia recurva

Oldenlandia recurva, commonly known as the trailing spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wet or marshy habitats. The plant features slender, trailing stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, white or pale blue flowers are borne in clusters and bloom during the summer months.

Oldenlandia recurva is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
