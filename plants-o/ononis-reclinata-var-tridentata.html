Ononis reclinata var. tridentata

Ononis reclinata var. tridentata, commonly known as the tridentate whitlowgrass, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial with slender stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces delicate white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal dunes and grasslands.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid environments and it is considered a characteristic species of coastal ecosystems.
