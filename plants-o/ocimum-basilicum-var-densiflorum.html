Ocimum basilicum var. densiflorum

Ocimum basilicum var. densiflorum, commonly known as purple basil, is a variant of the common basil plant characterized by its dense clusters of purple flowers.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia and thrives in warm, humid climates. The leaves are typically dark green with a slightly peppery flavor, making it a popular choice in culinary applications. It is also valued in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties and potential health benefits.

This variety is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its striking floral display.
