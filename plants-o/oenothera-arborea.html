Oenothera arborea

Oenothera arborea, commonly known as the showy evening primrose, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces striking yellow to orange flowers that open in the evening. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and thrives in well-drained soils. It is a member of the Onagraceae family and is known for its ability to attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

Oenothera arborea is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in native gardens.
