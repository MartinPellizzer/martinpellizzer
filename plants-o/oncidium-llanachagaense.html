Oncidium llanachagaense

Oncidium llanachagaense, commonly known as the Llanachaga orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant features pseudobulbs and strap-like leaves, which are typical of many orchid species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Oncidium llanachagaense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its long-lasting, colorful blooms.
