Oxalis sylvestris

Oxalis sylvestris, commonly known as wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that resemble those of the genus Oxalis. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and along stream banks, preferring moist, well-drained soil. It is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters.

Oxalis sylvestris is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
