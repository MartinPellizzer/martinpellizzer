Orchis incarnata var. intermedia

Orchis incarnata var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate pink orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features showy, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its roots are tuberous.

This variety is closely related to other members of the Orchidaceae family and is often found in wetland habitats.
