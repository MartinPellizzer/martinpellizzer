Orchis monsignatica

Orchis monsignatica, commonly known as the Monsignac's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to southern Europe.

It is a perennial herb with a tall, erect stem that can reach up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers with a distinctive hooded lip, typical of many orchid species. It thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its blooming period occurs in late spring to early summer, making it a notable wildflower in its native habitat.
