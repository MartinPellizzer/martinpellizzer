Orchis cochleata

Orchis cochleata, commonly known as the spiral orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in woodland and grassland habitats, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces tall, flowering stems with showy, violet or purple flowers. Its unique spiral arrangement of petals and sepals is a distinguishing characteristic.

Orchis cochleata is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
