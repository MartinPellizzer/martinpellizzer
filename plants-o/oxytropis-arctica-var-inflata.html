Oxytropis arctica var. inflata

Oxytropis arctica var. inflata, commonly known as Arctic locoweed, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its spiny stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in nutrient-poor, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates with limited growing seasons.

Oxytropis arctica var. inflata is part of the legume family and has the ability to fix nitrogen, which helps it survive in harsh environments.
