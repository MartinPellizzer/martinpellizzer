Oxydectes calycina

Oxydectes calycina, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in disturbed soils near human settlements.

Oxydectes calycina is also known for its rapid growth and ability to become invasive in certain regions.
