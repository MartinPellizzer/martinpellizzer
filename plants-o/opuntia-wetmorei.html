Opuntia wetmorei

Opuntia wetmorei, commonly known as the Mexican prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems known as cladodes, which are covered in small spines called glochids. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tunas, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

Opuntia wetmorei is well adapted to arid environments and is often used in traditional medicine and cuisine.
