Oenothera biennis var. lutea

Oenothera biennis var. lutea, commonly known as the yellow evening primrose, is a variant of the evening primrose native to North America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that open in the evening and close by morning. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces slender, branching stems and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers are known for their showy appearance and are often used in ornamental gardening.
