Oplismenus elatior

Oplismenus elatior, commonly known as the golden thread, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, creeping stems that produce small, bright yellow flowers from late spring to early summer. The plant has delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Oplismenus elatior is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
