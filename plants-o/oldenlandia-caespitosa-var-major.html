Oldenlandia caespitosa var. major

Oldenlandia caespitosa var. major, commonly known as the purple false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a low-growing herb with a mat-forming habit, characterized by its dense, sprawling stems that root at the nodes. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically white or purple in color, with five petals and a prominent central disk. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a hairy texture.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands, roadsides, and open woodlands.
