Ocimum laxum

Ocimum laxum, commonly known as loose basil, is a species of flowering plant in the mint family Lamiaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its fragrant, aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pale blue to purple in color and are clustered in spikes at the ends of the stems.

Ocimum laxum is valued for its medicinal properties, including potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.
