Ocotea nectandrifolia

Ocotea nectandrifolia, commonly known as the sweet-scented ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurel and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that emit a pleasant fragrance when crushed. Its flowers are small and white, typically arranged in clusters, and it produces dark, oily berries.

Ocotea nectandrifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
