Oenothera featherstonei

Oenothera featherstonei, commonly known as the Featherstone's evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is well adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and the stems are erect with a tendency to branch.
