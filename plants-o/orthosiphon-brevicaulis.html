Orthosiphon brevicaulis

Orthosiphon brevicaulis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved horsegram, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, narrow leaves and small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is often grown for its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a protein source for livestock.

The plant thrives in tropical climates and prefers well-drained soil.
