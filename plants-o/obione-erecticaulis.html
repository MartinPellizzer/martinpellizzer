Obione erecticaulis

Obione erecticaulis, commonly known as the upright-stemmed obione, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, which bloom in the spring. Obione erecticaulis thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and relatively low-maintenance addition to gardens.
