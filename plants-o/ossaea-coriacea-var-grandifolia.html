Ossaea coriacea var. grandifolia

Ossaea coriacea var. grandifolia, commonly known as the giant stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the cold climates of the Arctic and alpine regions.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically grayish-green in color, adapted to store water and withstand harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its growth habit is low-growing and mat-forming, making it well-suited for rocky or gravelly soils.

Ossaea coriacea var. grandifolia is often used in rock gardens and alpine landscapes due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
