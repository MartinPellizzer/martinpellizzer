Olearia solandri

Olearia solandri, commonly known as the New Zealand tree fern, is a striking evergreen plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, tree-like structure with a central trunk and a crown of feathery fronds. The plant features a fibrous root system and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are long, narrow, and have a leathery texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Olearia solandri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to moist, shaded environments.
