Oenothera pripjatiensis

Oenothera pripjatiensis, commonly known as the Pripjat Evening Primrose, is a rare and endangered species native to the Pripjat region of Ukraine.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its showy yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is adapted to the unique environmental conditions of its limited habitat.

Due to habitat destruction and limited distribution, it is critically endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
