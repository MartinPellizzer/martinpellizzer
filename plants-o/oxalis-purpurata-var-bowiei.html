Oxalis purpurata var. bowiei

Oxalis purpurata var. bowiei, commonly known as the Crimson Oxalis, is a striking plant native to the western United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive red or purple coloration, often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant produces vibrant orange or yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements make it a favored addition to many gardens.
