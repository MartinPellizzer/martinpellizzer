Oxalis pinoensis

Oxalis pinoensis, commonly known as the Pino's wood sorrel, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are heart-shaped and often tinged with red or purple veins. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.

Oxalis pinoensis is also known for its ability to tolerate drought once established.
