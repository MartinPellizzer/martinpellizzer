Oxalis nidulans var. denticulata

Oxalis nidulans var. denticulata, commonly known as the toothed wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the family Oxalidaceae and is known for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.

Oxalis nidulans var. denticulata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and greenhouses.
