Opuntia polyacantha var. platycarpa

Opuntia polyacantha var. platycarpa, commonly known as the broad-fruited prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flattened, ribbed stems and clusters of spiny areoles. The plant produces large, fleshy fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color, containing sweet, edible pulp. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it thrives in arid environments with well-drained soil.

The plant is often used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and unique appearance.
