Oplismenus africanus

Oplismenus africanus, commonly known as the African buttonweed, is a perennial herb native to Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and fleshy, succulent leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Oplismenus africanus is sometimes considered a weed due to its rapid growth and ability to spread easily.
