Oncidium forcipatum

Oncidium forcipatum, commonly known as the sword orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, sword-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Oncidium forcipatum prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.

Its blooming period usually occurs in the spring and summer, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
