Oetosis lineata var. filifolia

Oetosis lineata var. filifolia, commonly known as the threadleaf oetosis, is a rare and delicate plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves that are often arranged in a dense, mat-forming structure. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires a sunny to partially shaded environment.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
