Osmanthus hachijoensis

Osmanthus hachijoensis, commonly known as the Hachijo osmanthus, is a flowering plant native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, small yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late autumn. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
