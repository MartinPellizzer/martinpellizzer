Opuntia stenopetala var. inerme

Opuntia stenopetala var. inerme, commonly known as the Mexican prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems that are often covered in spines and glochids. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible, red-fleshed fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and well-drained soil.

Its structure and adaptations make it well-suited for survival in harsh desert conditions.
