Oreophilus cordilabius

Oreophilus cordilabius, commonly known as the mountain heartleaf, is a unique flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking violet-blue flowers that bloom during the cooler months. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, silvery down, which helps to reflect excess sunlight.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, Oreophilus cordilabius is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
