Origanum microphyllum

Origanum microphyllum, commonly known as dwarf oregano, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of pale blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters, making it a compact and ornamental species. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its aromatic foliage contains essential oils, which contribute to its use in culinary and medicinal applications.
