Ocotea duplocolorata

Ocotea duplocolorata, commonly known as the two-colored ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocado, and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its strikingly variegated leaves, featuring a mix of green and white or silver hues. Its bark is typically smooth and may have a reddish or brownish coloration.

Ocotea duplocolorata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to a range of growing conditions.
