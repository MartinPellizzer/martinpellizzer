Origanum syriacum var. bevanii

Origanum syriacum var. bevanii, commonly known as Syrian oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a pungent scent reminiscent of oregano. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

This variety is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, as well as its ornamental appeal.
