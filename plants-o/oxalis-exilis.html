Oxalis exilis

Oxalis exilis, commonly known as the slender wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves with distinctive veins and produces delicate, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow or white. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas. Oxalis exilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

Its foliage and flowers contribute to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
