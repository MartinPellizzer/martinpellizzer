Ophrys × cascalesii

Ophrys × cascalesii, commonly known as the Cascalesii bee orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the genus Ophrys, which is known for its intricate mimicry of female insects to attract male pollinators. The plant produces showy, bee-like flowers with a distinctive pouch-shaped labellum that mimics the appearance of a female bee. It thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and forest edges.

The botanical features of Ophrys × cascalesii are closely linked to its specialized pollination strategy, making it a fascinating subject in the study of plant-animal interactions.
