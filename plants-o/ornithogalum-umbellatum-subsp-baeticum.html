Ornithogalum umbellatum subsp. baeticum

Ornithogalum umbellatum subsp.

baeticum, commonly known as the Baetican star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers during the spring.

The plant is adapted to rocky and dry habitats, often found in mountainous regions. It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental value and medicinal uses in traditional herbal medicine.

Its blooming period attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
