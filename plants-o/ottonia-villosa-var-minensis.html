Ottonia villosa var. minensis

Ottonia villosa var. minensis, commonly known as the Minense velvetleaf, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its leaves are often covered in fine, velvety hairs, which give it its common name.

Ottonia villosa var. minensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
