Olsynium luridum

Olsynium luridum, commonly known as the yellow dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a basal rosette of narrow, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture. Its roots are fibrous and can produce a milky sap when damaged.

Olsynium luridum is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun.
