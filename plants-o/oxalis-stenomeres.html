Oxalis stenomeres

Oxalis stenomeres, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wood sorrel, is a species of flowering plant in the family Oxalidaceae.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive white or yellowish veining. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically clustered in groups of three. It is native to parts of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Oxalis stenomeres is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
