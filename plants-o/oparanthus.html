Oparanthus

Oparanthus, commonly known as the African daisy, is a genus of flowering plants native to southern Africa.

These plants typically feature showy, daisy-like flowers with vibrant colors such as yellow, orange, and red. Oparanthus species are often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrive in full sun. They have a basal rosette of leaves and produce numerous small, tubular flowers arranged in a single head.

The genus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
