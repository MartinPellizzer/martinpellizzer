Onoseris castelnaeana

Onoseris castelnaeana, commonly known as the Castelnau's goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant features upright, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Onoseris castelnaeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
