Oxalis marginata

Oxalis marginata, commonly known as the margined wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with distinctive white margins and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-drained soil. It produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Oxalis marginata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
