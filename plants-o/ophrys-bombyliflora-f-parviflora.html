Ophrys bombyliflora f. parviflora

Ophrys bombyliflora f. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in dry, calcareous grasslands and is often found in Mediterranean climates. Its flowers are small and pale in color, with a distinctive lip that plays a key role in the orchid's pollination strategy.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species in many regions.
