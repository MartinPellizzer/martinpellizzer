Ophrys × milioniae

Ophrys × milioniae, commonly known as the bee orchid hybrid, is a fascinating plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Ophrys apifera, the bee orchid, and Ophrys insectifera, the wasp orchid. The plant is characterized by its striking, bee-like flowers that feature intricate markings resembling a bee's face. These floral structures are adapted to mimic pollinators, facilitating specialized pollination by male bees.

Ophrys × milioniae is typically found in rocky or calcareous soils in Mediterranean regions, where it thrives in open, sunny habitats.
