Ozothamnus bilobus subsp. bilobus

Ozothamnus bilobus subsp.

bilobus, commonly known as the two-lobed goldenbush, is a perennial shrub native to southern Australia. It typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often in heathlands and open woodlands.

The plant features small, yellowish-green leaves that are usually divided into two lobes, giving it its common name. It produces fluffy, yellow flowers in spring, which attract various pollinators.

This subspecies is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in harsh, coastal environments.
