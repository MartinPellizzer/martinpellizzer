Odontochilus inabae

Odontochilus inabae, commonly known as the Ixabae toothed orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers with distinctive tooth-like structures on the petals. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on tree trunks or rocks, and requires specific conditions to thrive. Its botanical features include lance-shaped leaves and a compact growth habit, making it a sought-after specimen among orchid enthusiasts.
