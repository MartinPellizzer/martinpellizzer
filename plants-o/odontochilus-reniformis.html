Odontochilus reniformis

Odontochilus reniformis, commonly known as the kidney-shaped orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive kidney-shaped pseudobulbs and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers with a unique lip that is often marked with purple or brown spots. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-drained soil.

Odontochilus reniformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
