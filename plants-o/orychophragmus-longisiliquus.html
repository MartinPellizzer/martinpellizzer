Orychophragmus longisiliquus

Orychophragmus longisiliquus, commonly known as the long silique mustard, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, exhibiting adaptations to conserve water. Its long, slender silique fruits are a distinguishing feature, often containing several seeds.

Orychophragmus longisiliquus is also noted for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
