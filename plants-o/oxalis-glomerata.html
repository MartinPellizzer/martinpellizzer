Oxalis glomerata

Oxalis glomerata, commonly known as the clustered wood sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of small, yellow, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and stems that often root at the nodes.

Oxalis glomerata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
