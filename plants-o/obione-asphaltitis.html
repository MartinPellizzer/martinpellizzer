Obione asphaltitis

Obione asphaltitis, commonly known as the asphalt plant, is a unique species characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that exude a sticky, tar-like substance.

This secretion, known as asphalt, is a natural byproduct of the plant's metabolic processes and is used by indigenous communities for various practical purposes. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, where it has adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited water availability. Its botanical structure includes a short, woody stem with dark green, leathery foliage.

The asphalt exudate has been studied for its potential industrial and medicinal applications due to its high resin content.
