Ornithophora radicans

Ornithophora radicans, commonly known as the hanging orchid, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems that often hang from trees or shrubs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy surface.

Ornithophora radicans is valued in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
