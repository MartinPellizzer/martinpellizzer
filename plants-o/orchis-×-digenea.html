Orchis × digenea

Orchis × digenea, commonly known as the fairy orchid, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Orchis morio and Orchis mascula.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, particularly found in moist meadows and woodlands. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers with a distinctive spurred lip, typical of the Orchidaceae family. It thrives in partial shade and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soils.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in many regions.
