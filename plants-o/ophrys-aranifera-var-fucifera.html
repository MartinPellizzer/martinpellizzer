Ophrys aranifera var. fucifera

Ophrys aranifera var. fucifera, commonly known as the hairy bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to specific regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and heathlands. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, while its flowers are adorned with intricate patterns and a distinctive, hairy appearance.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, this variant is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
