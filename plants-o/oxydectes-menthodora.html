Oxydectes menthodora

Oxydectes menthodora, commonly known as the Menthol Mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are fragrant and contain high concentrations of menthol, giving it a distinctive cooling effect.

Oxydectes menthodora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
