Oxydendron

Oxydendron, commonly known as the sourwood tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are elliptical, dark green, and often have a slight waxy coating. Its fruit is a small, yellowish drupe that matures in the fall.

Oxydendron is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in acidic, well-drained soils.
