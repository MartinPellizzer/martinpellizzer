Osteospermum perfoliatum

Osteospermum perfoliatum, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads, which are surrounded by white to pale blue ray florets. The plant has a bushy growth habit with hairy stems and leaves that are often lobed or toothed. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

Its showy blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
