Oxalis polyphylla var. minor

Oxalis polyphylla var. minor, commonly known as the lesser wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each leaflet being heart-shaped with a deep green color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Oxalis polyphylla var. minor is a perennial herb that is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
