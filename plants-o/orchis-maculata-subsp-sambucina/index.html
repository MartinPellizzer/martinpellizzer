Orchis Maculata Subsp. Sambucina

Orchis maculata subsp. sambucina is a rare and delicate orchid native to parts of Europe and western Asia. This subspecies is known for its distinctive purple and white flower spikes that bloom in late spring. It thrives in moist, shaded woodlands and requires specific environmental conditions to flourish. Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered in many regions. Conservation efforts are crucial to preserving this unique botanical specimen.
