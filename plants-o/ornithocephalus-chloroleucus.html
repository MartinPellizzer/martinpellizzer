Ornithocephalus chloroleucus

Ornithocephalus chloroleucus, commonly known as the green-stemmed orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in a loose inflorescence. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
