Octomeria juergensii

Octomeria juergensii, commonly known as the desert pincushion, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage that forms a spherical shape, resembling a pincushion. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a striking contrast to its rough, green texture. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water requirements.

Octomeria juergensii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
