Oxypetalum strictum subsp. polyanthum

Oxypetalum strictum subsp.

polyanthum, commonly known as the clustered oxypetalum, is a flowering plant species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, characterized by its dense, compact growth habit.

The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and elliptical in shape, with a slightly hairy surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive floral display.
