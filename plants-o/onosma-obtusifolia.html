Onosma obtusifolia

Onosma obtusifolia, commonly known as the blunt-leaved fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. Its leaves are broad and rounded, with a velvety texture, and it produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer.

Onosma obtusifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
