Oenothera cinnabarina

Oenothera cinnabarina, commonly known as the scarlet evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Oenothera cinnabarina is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
