Ornithocephalus cascajalensis

Ornithocephalus cascajalensis, commonly known as the Cascajal orchid, is a rare and unique orchid species native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive floral structure and intricate morphology. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in cloud forests or mountainous regions. Its flowers are notable for their complex arrangement and specialized adaptations for pollination.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
