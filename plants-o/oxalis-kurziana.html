Oxalis kurziana

Oxalis kurziana, commonly known as the Kurz's wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive reddish veins and produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers. The plant belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its calcifugous nature, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. It is often found in rocky or sandy habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Oxalis kurziana is also noted for its ability to tolerate drought conditions once established.
