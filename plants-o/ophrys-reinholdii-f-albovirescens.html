Ophrys reinholdii f. albovirescens

Ophrys reinholdii f. albovirescens, commonly known as the white-flowered bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which mimics the appearance of a female bee to attract male bees for pollination. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its flowers are predominantly white with subtle yellow and green markings, enhancing its mimicry.

This orchid is a fascinating example of evolutionary adaptation in plant-pollinator interactions.
