Oxycoccus microcarpus

Oxycoccus microcarpus, commonly known as the dwarf cranberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces tiny, red berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in nutrient-poor, acidic soils and is adapted to cold climates with long winters.

Oxycoccus microcarpus plays a significant role in the ecosystems of its native habitats, providing food for various wildlife species.
