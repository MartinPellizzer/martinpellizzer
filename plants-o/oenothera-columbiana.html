Oenothera columbiana

Oenothera columbiana, commonly known as the Columbia evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy yellow or orange flowers that open in the evening. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Oenothera columbiana is also known for its potential medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
