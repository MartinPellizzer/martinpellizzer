Oxalis san-miguelii subsp. san-miguelii

Oxalis san-miguelii subsp.

san-miguelii, commonly known as San Miguel wood sorrel, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States. It features trifoliate leaves with three heart-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky soils and open woodlands, preferring partial shade to full sun. Its flowering period occurs in spring, and it is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
