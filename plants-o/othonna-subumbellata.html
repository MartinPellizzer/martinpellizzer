Othonna subumbellata

Othonna subumbellata, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, spherical inflorescence composed of numerous small yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal regions and is adapted to sandy soils and salt spray. It has hairy, linear leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Othonna subumbellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
