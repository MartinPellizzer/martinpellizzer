Orobanche asiatica

Orobanche asiatica, commonly known as the Asian broomrape, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is characterized by its lack of leaves and stems, with only flowering stems that bear small, often white or pale yellow flowers. This plant typically parasitizes members of the Fabaceae family, such as soybeans and cowpeas, by attaching to their roots with specialized structures called haustoria. Its life cycle is closely tied to the growth of its host, and it can significantly reduce the yield of agricultural crops.

Due to its parasitic nature and potential economic impact, it is considered a weed in many regions where it is found.
