Oenothera cespitosa subsp. jonesii

Oenothera cespitosa subsp.

jonesii, commonly known as Jones' evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the evening.

The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in open areas such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Onagraceae family.
