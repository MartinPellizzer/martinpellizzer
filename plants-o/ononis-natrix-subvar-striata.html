Ononis natrix subvar. striata

Ononis natrix subvar. striata, commonly known as the striped sand-verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender, branching stems that often root at the nodes. The leaves are linear to lanceolate, with a rough, hairy texture, and the flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences. It produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer.

This plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed or arid environments.
