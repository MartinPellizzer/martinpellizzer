Oropetium minimum

Oropetium minimum, commonly known as the small oropetium, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and succulent-like leaves. The plant produces tiny, greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer, followed by small, round seeds. Oropetium minimum is adapted to thrive in hot, dry environments with poor soil, making it a hardy species for xeriscaping.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in desert landscapes.
