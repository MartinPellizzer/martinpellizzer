Ocotea silvae

Ocotea silvae, commonly known as the tropical laurel, is a member of the Lauraceae family and is native to the humid forests of Central and South America.

It is a medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue berries, which are an important food source for various bird species. Its wood is valued for its durability and is often used in furniture and construction.

Ocotea silvae plays a crucial role in maintaining the ecological balance of its native habitats.
