Othonna protecta

Othonna protecta, commonly known as the Cape honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow-orange inflorescences. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while the flowers are borne in clusters at the ends of branches.

Othonna protecta is well adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
