Obione flavida

Obione flavida, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant yellow petals and intricate floral structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are highly fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths.

Obione flavida is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a role in the ecological balance of its native habitats.
