Odontochilus japonicus

Odontochilus japonicus, commonly known as the Japanese toothed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique tooth-like structures on its leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its flowers are usually small and white or pale pink, with a distinct lip that aids in pollination.

Odontochilus japonicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
