Ophrys aranifera var. euchlora

Ophrys aranifera var. euchlora, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique, bee-mimicking flowers that feature intricate patterns resembling a spider's web. The plant typically grows in calcareous grasslands and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are pollinated by male bees that attempt to mate with the flower's pseudostigma, mistaking it for a female bee.

This species is native to southern Europe and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological significance.
