Ocotea eriothyrsa

Ocotea eriothyrsa, commonly known as the Brazilian camphor tree, is a member of the Lauraceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its lanceolate, glossy dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by elliptical, dark blue-black berries. Its aromatic bark and leaves contain essential oils, making it valuable for medicinal and aromatic purposes.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
