Ossaea elliptica

Ossaea elliptica, commonly known as the slender lily, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves and tall, slender stems that bear showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in the spring and summer, producing pink or white petals that attract pollinators. Ossaea elliptica is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Amaryllidaceae family.
