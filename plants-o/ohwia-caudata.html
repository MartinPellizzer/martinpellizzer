Ohwia caudata

Ohwia caudata, commonly known as the long-tailed orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant with slender, erect stems and linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-white with a yellowish throat, arranged in a raceme. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Ohwia caudata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
