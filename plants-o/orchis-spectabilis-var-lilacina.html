Orchis spectabilis var. lilacina

Orchis spectabilis var. lilacina, commonly known as the lilac orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of showy, violet to lilac-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers have a distinctive three-lobed lip, a feature common in many orchid species.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
