Olearia suborbiculata

Olearia suborbiculata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved daisy bush, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and daisy-like white flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and lowland areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

Olearia suborbiculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
