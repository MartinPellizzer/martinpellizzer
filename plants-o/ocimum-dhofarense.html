Ocimum dhofarense

Ocimum dhofarense, commonly known as Dhofar basil, is a flowering plant native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and oregano. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, narrow leaves and small, pale blue or purple flowers. It thrives in warm, dry climates and is often cultivated for its medicinal and culinary uses.

Its essential oils are valued for their antimicrobial and antioxidant properties.
