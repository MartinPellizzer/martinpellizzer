Orchis hungarica

Orchis hungarica, commonly known as the Hungarian orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodlands. Its flowers are usually arranged in a loose spike and have a distinctive, intricate structure typical of orchids.

Orchis hungarica plays an important role in its ecosystem, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
