Origanum amaracus

Origanum amaracus, commonly known as bitter oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes. Its essential oils contain compounds like carvacrol and thymol, which contribute to its strong, pungent aroma.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used historically in traditional remedies.
