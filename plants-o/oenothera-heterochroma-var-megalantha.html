Oenothera heterochroma var. megalantha

Oenothera heterochroma var. megalantha, commonly known as the giant nightshade evening primrose, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, large, showy flowers that bloom at night, typically in shades of yellow to orange. The plant has hairy, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by moths, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

This variety is noted for its robust growth and adaptability to arid environments.
