Ocotea balanocarpa

Ocotea balanocarpa, commonly known as the Balanocarpa ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue berries. Its wood is valued for its fine grain and durability, making it useful in construction and furniture-making.

The plant also plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
