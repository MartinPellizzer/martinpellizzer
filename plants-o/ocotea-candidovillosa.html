Ocotea candidovillosa

Ocotea candidovillosa, commonly known as the white-fruited ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white, fleshy fruits that mature in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests.

Ocotea candidovillosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
