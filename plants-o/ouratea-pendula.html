Ouratea pendula

Ouratea pendula, commonly known as the hanging ouratea, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments within the understory of forests. The plant is characterized by its pendulous inflorescences, which consist of clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a glossy surface.

The plant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid, partially shaded conditions.
