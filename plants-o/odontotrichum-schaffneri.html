Odontotrichum schaffneri

Odontotrichum schaffneri, commonly known as Schaffner's toothed grass, is a small perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces tiny, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. It is well adapted to arid conditions and has a deep root system to access moisture.

Odontotrichum schaffneri is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.
