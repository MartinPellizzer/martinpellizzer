Ophrys reinholdii subsp. reinholdii

Ophrys reinholdii subsp.

reinholdii, commonly known as the Reinhold's bee orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It is characterized by its unique floral structure, which closely resembles a female bee, a mimicry strategy used to attract male bees for pollination.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils at high altitudes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to brownish in color, with intricate patterns that enhance its deceptive appearance.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species facing threats from habitat loss and climate change.
