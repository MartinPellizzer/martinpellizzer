Odontonema paniculatum

Odontonema paniculatum, commonly known as the pink shower plant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in pendulous racemes. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are opposite and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden areas.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant flowers and compact growth habit.
