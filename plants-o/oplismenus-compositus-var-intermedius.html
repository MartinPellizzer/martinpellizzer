Oplismenus compositus var. intermedius

Oplismenus compositus var. intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate goldenthread, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features finely divided, grass-like leaves that are typically bright green and have a distinctive yellow or golden hue along the midrib. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers in late spring, which are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and native plant gardens.
