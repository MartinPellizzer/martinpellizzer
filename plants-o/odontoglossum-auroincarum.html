Odontoglossum auroincarum

Odontoglossum auroincarum, commonly known as the golden-lip orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a compact growth habit and produces intricate, showy flowers with a distinctive golden lip. The plant typically blooms in late summer, featuring vibrant yellow and green hues that attract pollinators. Its pseudobulbs are short and stout, supporting the plant's foliage and flowering structures.

Odontoglossum auroincarum is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
