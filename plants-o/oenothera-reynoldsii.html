Oenothera reynoldsii

Oenothera reynoldsii, commonly known as Reynolds' evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that open in the evening and close by morning. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, elliptical seeds. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, often with a reddish or purplish hue.

Oenothera reynoldsii is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and open habitats.
