Opuntia × magenta

Opuntia × magenta, commonly known as the magenta prickly pear, is a hybrid cactus species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems covered in spiny areoles, which are unique to the Cactaceae family. The plant produces vibrant red or magenta flowers, typically in late spring or early summer. Its fruit, known as a tunas, is edible and often used in culinary applications.

Opuntia × magenta is well adapted to arid environments, showcasing the plant's resilience to drought and heat.
