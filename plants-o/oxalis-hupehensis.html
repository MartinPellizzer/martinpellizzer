Oxalis hupehensis

Oxalis hupehensis, commonly known as the Chinese wood sorrel, is a perennial herb native to China.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive reddish veins and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its calcifuge nature, preferring acidic soils. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.

Oxalis hupehensis is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
