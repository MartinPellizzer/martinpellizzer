Ocellochloa irregularis

Ocellochloa irregularis, commonly known as the irregular spikegrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and distinctive, irregularly shaped spikelets. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in grasslands and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Ocellochloa irregularis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native environment.
