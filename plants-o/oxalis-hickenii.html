Oxalis hickenii

Oxalis hickenii, commonly known as the yellow wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive yellowish-green color and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of yellow and white. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its cluster of stipitate (stalked) flowers.

Oxalis hickenii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in gardens.
