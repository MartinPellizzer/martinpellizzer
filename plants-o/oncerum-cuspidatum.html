Oncerum cuspidatum

Oncerum cuspidatum, commonly known as the spiked oncera, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, sunny conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces distinctive, spiky seed heads that are adapted for wind dispersal.

Oncerum cuspidatum plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing food and habitat for various insects and wildlife.
