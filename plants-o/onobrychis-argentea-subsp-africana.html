Onobrychis argentea subsp. africana

Onobrychis argentea subsp.

africana, commonly known as silver vetch, is a perennial legume native to Africa. It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and trifoliate leaves, which are typical of the Fabaceae family.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, followed by narrow, bean-like pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
