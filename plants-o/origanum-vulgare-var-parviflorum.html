Origanum vulgare var. parviflorum

Origanum vulgare var. parviflorum, commonly known as Greek oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has aromatic, slightly woody stems and lance-shaped leaves with a strong, pungent scent. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its culinary and medicinal uses are well-documented, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
