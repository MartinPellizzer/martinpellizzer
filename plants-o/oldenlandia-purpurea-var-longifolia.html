Oldenlandia purpurea var. longifolia

Oldenlandia purpurea var. longifolia, commonly known as purple bead vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and clusters of small, showy purple flowers. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, bead-like fruits that are dispersed by wind.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.
