Ophrys × gomezii

Ophrys × gomezii, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant with intricate botanical features.

It is a natural hybrid between Ophrys apifera (the bee orchid) and Ophrys insectifera (the wasp orchid), resulting in a unique combination of traits from both parents. The plant is characterized by its striking, bee-like flowers that play a crucial role in its pollination strategy. Its flowers are specialized to mimic the appearance and scent of female bees, attracting male bees for pollination.

This orchid thrives in Mediterranean climates and is often found in calcareous soils, showcasing its adaptability to specific environmental conditions.
