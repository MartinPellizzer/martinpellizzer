Oxalis divergens var. amplifolia

Oxalis divergens var. amplifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with broad, heart-shaped leaflets that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and native landscaping.
