Odontoglossum spilotanthum

Odontoglossum spilotanthum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers with intricate patterns, which are highly prized in horticulture. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding its survival in high-altitude environments. Its leaves are typically broad and grass-like, adapting to the cool, moist conditions of its habitat.

Odontoglossum spilotanthum is a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts due to its ornamental value and relatively easy cultivation.
