Olea cassinifolia

Olea cassinifolia, commonly known as the African olive, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn black when mature. Olea cassinifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.
