Oncocalyx sulfureus

Oncocalyx sulfureus, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling flames. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces small, round, yellow fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Oncocalyx sulfureus is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
