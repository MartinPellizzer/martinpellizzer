Ophrys × murbeckii var. quarteirae

Ophrys × murbeckii var. quarteirae, commonly known as the Quarteira orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, bee-mimicking flower structure that plays a crucial role in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats with well-drained soils, often found in Mediterranean climates. Its flowers exhibit a range of colors, including browns, purples, and yellows, with intricate patterns that resemble female bees to attract male pollinators.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique and striking floral morphology.
