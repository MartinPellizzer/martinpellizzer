Opuntia kuehnrichii

Opuntia kuehnrichii, commonly known as the Mexican prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its flat, fleshy pads called cladodes, which are covered in small spines. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible fruit known as tunas. Opuntia kuehnrichii is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick stems and having a shallow root system to absorb moisture efficiently.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various desert wildlife.
