Olearia fluvialis

Olearia fluvialis, commonly known as the river daisy, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in wet, boggy areas and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow with a prominent central disk.

Olearia fluvialis is often found in coastal and riverine habitats, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
