Oxytropis kungurensis subsp. demidovii

Oxytropis kungurensis subsp.

demidovii, commonly known as the Mongolian locoweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect stems and small, compound leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in early summer, which are clustered in racemes. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often avoided by livestock and wildlife.
