Ophrys × personei

Ophrys × personei, commonly known as the hybrid bee orchid, is a fascinating plant in the Ophrys genus, known for its intricate floral morphology.

It is a natural hybrid between Ophrys apifera and Ophrys insectifera, exhibiting a blend of characteristics from both parent species. The plant is notable for its highly specialized flowers that mimic the appearance of female bees, a strategy used to attract male bees for pollination. Its flowers are typically yellow with dark markings and a distinctive lip that resembles a bee's abdomen.

This orchid thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats across Europe.
