Omphalocarpum massoko

Omphalocarpum massoko, commonly known as the African burr bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive burr-like fruits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are often clustered together, followed by woody, spiny pods containing seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The plant has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties and as a source of forage for livestock.
