Oxytropis campestris var. spicata

Oxytropis campestris var. spicata, commonly known as yellow milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and compound leaves, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. The plant thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.

Its small, spiny pods contain seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation.
