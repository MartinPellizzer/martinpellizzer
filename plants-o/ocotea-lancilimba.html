Ocotea lancilimba

Ocotea lancilimba, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a small, dark berry that contains a single seed.

Ocotea lancilimba is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional crafts and as a source of essential oils.
