Ornithidium rhodoleucum

Ornithidium rhodoleucum, commonly known as the white-flowered orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces intricate, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, velvety texture. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and requires careful cultivation due to its specific ecological needs.

Ornithidium rhodoleucum is of interest to botanists and conservationists due to its limited distribution and potential vulnerability to habitat loss.
