Oxytropis follicularis

Oxytropis follicularis, commonly known as the mountain locoweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, yellowish-pink flowers. The plant produces small, leathery pods that contain seeds, and its leaves are typically trifoliate, with three leaflets. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.

Oxytropis follicularis is adapted to harsh environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil in arid areas.
