Oxalis varadimalayana

Oxalis varadimalayana, commonly known as the Malayan wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, delicate flowers. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in spring, and its leaves are often covered in fine hairs. Oxalis varadimalayana is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and undergrowth.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
