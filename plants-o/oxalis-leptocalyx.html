Oxalis leptocalyx

Oxalis leptocalyx, commonly known as the slender-leaf wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has a creeping or trailing habit. Its foliage contains oxalic acid, which can be toxic if ingested in large quantities.

Oxalis leptocalyx is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in partial shade.
