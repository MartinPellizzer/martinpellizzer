Origanum vulgare subsp. humile

Origanum vulgare subsp.

humile, commonly known as dwarf oregano, is a compact perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features small, narrow leaves and produces tiny, pale blue or white flowers in summer.

The plant is characterized by its aromatic, pungent leaves that are often used in cooking and herbal remedies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Its compact growth habit makes it ideal for borders and edging in gardens.
