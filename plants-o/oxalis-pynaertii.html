Oxalis pynaertii

Oxalis pynaertii, commonly known as the Pynaert's wood sorrel, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive veins and small, five-petaled yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom in spring, and it produces capsule fruits containing several seeds.

Oxalis pynaertii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
