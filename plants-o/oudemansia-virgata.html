Oudemansia virgata

Oudemansia virgata, commonly known as the slender false indigo, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically purple or blue in color, which bloom in early spring. Oudemansia virgata is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through its root nodules, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
