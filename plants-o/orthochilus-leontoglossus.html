Orthochilus leontoglossus

Orthochilus leontoglossus, commonly known as the leopard orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, hairy leaves and its unique, showy flowers that resemble small leopards, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color with dark markings, and it blooms during the late summer or early autumn.

The species is part of the Orchidaceae family and is appreciated for its ornamental value in horticulture.
