Opuntia grata var. leonina

Opuntia grata var. leonina, commonly known as the Leonine prickly pear, is a succulent cactus native to Mexico.

It features flattened, fleshy stems that are typically green with a waxy coating, and it produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the summer months. The plant is characterized by its dense clusters of spines and is well adapted to arid environments.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and ability to thrive in hot, dry climates.
