Oxytropis kungurensis

Oxytropis kungurensis, commonly known as the Kungurensis milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to harsh alpine environments.

Oxytropis kungurensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for local wildlife.
