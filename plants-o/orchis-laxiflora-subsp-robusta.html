Orchis laxiflora subsp. robusta

Orchis laxiflora subsp.

robusta, commonly known as the robust orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous grasslands and woodland margins, where it thrives in partial shade.

The plant features a basal rosette of lanceolate leaves and produces tall, flowering stems with showy, violet to purple flowers. Its inflorescence consists of a single spike bearing several flowers, each with a three-lobed lip.

This subspecies is noted for its robust growth and is often found in habitats with well-drained, nutrient-rich soils.
