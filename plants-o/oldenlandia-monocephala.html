Oldenlandia monocephala

Oldenlandia monocephala, commonly known as the single-headed spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in clusters, often in shades of white, yellow, or pink. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments. It is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and forest understories.

Oldenlandia monocephala is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
