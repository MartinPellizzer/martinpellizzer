Orobus jordani

Orobus jordani, commonly known as the Jordan's orobus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with purple veins. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds.

Orobus jordani plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small organisms.
