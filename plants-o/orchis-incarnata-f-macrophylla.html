Orchis incarnata f. macrophylla

Orchis incarnata f. macrophylla, commonly known as the showy orchis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

The inflorescence consists of a tall spike bearing numerous showy, spurred flowers that attract pollinators like bees and moths.
