Oncidium caymanense

Oncidium caymanense, commonly known as the Cayman Islands orchid, is a tropical orchid native to the Cayman Islands.

It is characterized by its pendulous, showy inflorescences that bear numerous small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and green. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Oncidium caymanense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
