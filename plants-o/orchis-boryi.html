Orchis boryi

Orchis boryi, commonly known as the Bory's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces a single, often greenish-yellow flower with a distinctive lip. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky slopes.

Orchis boryi is a perennial herb that plays a role in its ecosystem by providing nectar for pollinators.
