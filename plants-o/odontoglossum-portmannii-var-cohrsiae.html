Odontoglossum portmannii var. cohrsiae

Odontoglossum portmannii var. cohrsiae, commonly known as the Cochrans' orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in cool, humid environments at high altitudes. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces intricate, showy flowers with a distinctive coloration pattern. Its flowers are often yellow or green with purple or red markings, and it blooms during the cooler months.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
