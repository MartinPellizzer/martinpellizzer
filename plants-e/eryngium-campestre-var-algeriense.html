Eryngium campestre var. algeriense

Eryngium campestre var. algeriense, commonly known as Algerian thistle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue-green foliage and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. Native to North Africa, particularly Algeria, it thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open fields. The plant has a compact growth habit with a rosette of basal leaves and a short, upright stem.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to xeriscaped gardens and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
