Eucalyptus watsoniana

Eucalyptus watsoniana, commonly known as the malleeflower eucalypt, is a medium-sized tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale blue, which appear in clusters. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

Eucalyptus watsoniana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
