Erodendrum mucronifolium

Erodendrum mucronifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf eremoblast, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet, disturbed habitats such as swamps and creek banks. The plant features slender, often hairless stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are usually less than 2 inches in length. Its flowers are clustered in loose racemes and are characterized by their pale blue to purple coloration.

Erodendrum mucronifolium is notable for its ecological role in wetland ecosystems and its historical use by indigenous peoples for medicinal purposes.
