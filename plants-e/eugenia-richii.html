Eugenia richii

Eugenia richii, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the coastal regions of eastern Australia, where it thrives in sandy soils and humid climates. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and dark red or black berries that develop from the flowers. Its leaves are glossy and elliptical, often used in traditional medicine by Indigenous Australians.

Eugenia richii is also cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and floral display.
