Eugenia formosa var. ternifolia

Eugenia formosa var. ternifolia, commonly known as the fragrant snowbell, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its small, white, bell-shaped flowers that emit a subtle, sweet fragrance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and streamside areas. Its leaves are elliptical and have a smooth, glossy surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
