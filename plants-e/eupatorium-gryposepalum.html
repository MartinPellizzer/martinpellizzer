Eupatorium gryposepalum

Eupatorium gryposepalum, commonly known as the yellow elder, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellowish-orange inflorescences. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Eupatorium gryposepalum thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical regions.
