Eulophia horsfallii subsp. velayosiana

Eulophia horsfallii subsp.

velayosiana, commonly known as the Velayosian orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of South America, particularly Ecuador. It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and linear, leathery leaves.

The plant produces inflorescences of showy, fragrant flowers with a distinctive coloration, often in shades of yellow and green. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is typically found in cloud forests.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
