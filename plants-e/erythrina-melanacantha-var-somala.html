Erythrina melanacantha var. somala

Erythrina melanacantha var. somala, commonly known as the Somalian coral tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Horn of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red and orange flowers, which bloom in dense clusters during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with three leaflets, and its bark is often smooth and reddish in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to harsh climatic conditions.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local communities.
