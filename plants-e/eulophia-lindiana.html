Eulophia lindiana

Eulophia lindiana, commonly known as the Lind's eulophia, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often with a distinctive purple or brownish hue on the lips. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is typically found in forest understories.

Eulophia lindiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
