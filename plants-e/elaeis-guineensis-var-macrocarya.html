Elaeis guineensis var. macrocarya

Elaeis guineensis var. macrocarya, commonly known as the African oil palm, is a tropical plant native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves and produces clusters of fruit that contain oil-rich seeds. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil and requires ample sunlight for optimal growth. Its fruit is the primary source of palm oil, which is widely used in food and industrial products.

Due to its economic importance, it is also a focus of agricultural research and conservation efforts.
