Euphorbia multicorymbosa

Euphorbia multicorymbosa, commonly known as the clustered spurge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, branched stems and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in cymes, giving it its common name. It has a milky sap that can be toxic, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

Euphorbia multicorymbosa is a drought-tolerant plant that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.
