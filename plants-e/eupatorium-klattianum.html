Eupatorium klattianum

Eupatorium klattianum, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flower heads that resemble mist. The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a silvery underside.

Eupatorium klattianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
