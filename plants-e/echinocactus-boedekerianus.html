Echinocactus boedekerianus

Echinocactus boedekerianus, commonly known as the Boedeker's hedgehog cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its spiny, ribbed stems and is typically found in rocky, arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are borne on short pedicels at the ends of the stems. It has a slow growth rate and is adapted to withstand extreme drought conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in xerophytic gardens.
