Erophila vulgaris f. glabra

Erophila vulgaris f. glabra, commonly known as smooth field eryngo, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is native to Europe and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and waste areas. Its smooth, hairless leaves distinguish it from other varieties of Erophila vulgaris.

It is a hardy perennial that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil.
