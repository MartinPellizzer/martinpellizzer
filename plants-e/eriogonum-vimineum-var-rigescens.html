Eriogonum vimineum var. rigescens

Eriogonum vimineum var. rigescens, commonly known as the stiffstem buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its erect, woody stems and dense clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and gravelly soils. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This variety is noted for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in arid environments.
