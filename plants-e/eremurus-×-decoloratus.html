Eremurus × decoloratus

Eremurus × decoloratus, commonly known as the hybrid eremurus or sometimes the pink eremurus, is a perennial plant belonging to the genus Eremurus in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescence composed of numerous small, showy flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are evergreen in some varieties.

Eremurus × decoloratus requires well-drained soil and full sun to thrive, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
