Eupatorium lemmonii

Eupatorium lemmonii, commonly known as Lemmon's boneset, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands. It produces small, oval-shaped fruits called cypsela, which are dispersed by wind.

Eupatorium lemmonii is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects.
