Euonymus dasydictyon

Euonymus dasydictyon, commonly known as the glossy abutilon, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that give it a striking appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
