Eugenia guineensis

Eugenia guineensis, commonly known as the African guineaflower, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves and small, showy flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary practices. Its botanical features include opposite leaves, glandular dots, and a woody structure typical of the Myrtaceae family.

Eugenia guineensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
