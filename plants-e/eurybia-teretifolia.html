Eurybia teretifolia

Eurybia teretifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sunflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and daisy-like flowers. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellow disk flowers surrounded by white ray florets. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Eurybia teretifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for pollinators.
