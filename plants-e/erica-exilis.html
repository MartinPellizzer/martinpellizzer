Erica exilis

Erica exilis, commonly known as the slender heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related plants. The plant features small, needle-like leaves and produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers in spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and full sun to partial shade.

Erica exilis is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardy nature and attractive appearance.
