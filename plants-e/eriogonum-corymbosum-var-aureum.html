Eriogonum corymbosum var. aureum

Eriogonum corymbosum var. aureum, commonly known as the golden globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, gray-green leaves that are opposite and often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its showy appearance.
