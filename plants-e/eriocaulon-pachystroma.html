Eriocaulon pachystroma

Eriocaulon pachystroma, commonly known as the broad-leaved flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments in Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, ribbon-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in shallow water or along the edges of ponds and rivers.

Eriocaulon pachystroma plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
