Eriogonum atrorubens var. nemorosum

Eriogonum atrorubens var. nemorosum, commonly known as the dark red buckwheat, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and clusters of small, dark red to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting a variety of pollinators.

The species is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
