Eugenia mainitensis

Eugenia mainitensis, commonly known as the Mindanao eugenia, is a species of flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and fragrant, white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in lowland forests and is often found in humid, tropical environments. Its fruit, which is small and berry-like, is edible and has a sweet, tart flavor.

Eugenia mainitensis plays a role in local ecosystems and has potential uses in traditional medicine and ornamental horticulture.
