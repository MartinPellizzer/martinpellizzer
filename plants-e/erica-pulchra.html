Erica pulchra

Erica pulchra, commonly known as the Cape snowflake, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Erica pulchra is well adapted to Mediterranean climates and is often used in rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant plants.
