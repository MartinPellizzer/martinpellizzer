Elaeocarpus richardii

Elaeocarpus richardii, commonly known as the Richard's elaeocarp, is a tropical tree belonging to the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink. Its fruit is a woody, spherical capsule that contains several seeds, which are often dispersed by animals.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
