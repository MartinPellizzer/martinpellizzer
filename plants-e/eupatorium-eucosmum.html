Eupatorium eucosmum

Eupatorium eucosmum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist woodlands and wet meadows, preferring partial to full shade. Its blue to purple flower clusters appear in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Eupatorium eucosmum is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits.
