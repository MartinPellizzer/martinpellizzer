Ehrharta hispida

Ehrharta hispida, commonly known as hairy grass or narrowleaf lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, hair-covered blades. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. Its seed heads are often erect and can be used for ornamental purposes.

Ehrharta hispida is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in arid environments.
