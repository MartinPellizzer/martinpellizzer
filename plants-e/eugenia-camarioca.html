Eugenia camarioca

Eugenia camarioca, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or araçá-do-brejo, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its small, bright red berries that resemble cherries. The plant typically grows in wetland or swampy areas, preferring moist, acidic soils. Its leaves are elliptical and glossy, while its flowers are white and fragrant.

Eugenia camarioca plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food for various bird and animal species.
