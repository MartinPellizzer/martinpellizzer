Erica declinata

Erica declinata, commonly known as the leaning heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its dense, compact growth and produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white. Erica declinata typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it contributes to the unique biodiversity of these ecosystems.
