Euphorbia gedrosiaca

Euphorbia gedrosiaca, commonly known as the Gedrosian spurge, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves, which are adapted to store water in its native desert environment. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in a structure called a cyathium. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
