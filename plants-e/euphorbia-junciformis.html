Euphorbia junciformis

Euphorbia junciformis, commonly known as the narrowleaf spurge, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of North America.

It features upright, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a powdery white coating, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, typically blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Euphorbia junciformis is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.
