Eupatorium tozanense

Eupatorium tozanense, commonly known as the Japanese thoroughwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have serrated edges.

Eupatorium tozanense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
