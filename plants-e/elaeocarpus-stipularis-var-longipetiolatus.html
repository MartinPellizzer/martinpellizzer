Elaeocarpus stipularis var. longipetiolatus

Elaeocarpus stipularis var. longipetiolatus, commonly known as the long-petioled elaeocarpus, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in tropical rainforests. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with long petioles, and it produces small, clustered flowers. Its fruits are woody capsules that contain several seeds, and the plant is often valued for its ornamental qualities.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
