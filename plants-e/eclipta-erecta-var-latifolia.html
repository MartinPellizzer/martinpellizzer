Eclipta erecta var. latifolia

Eclipta erecta var. latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in wetlands and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in clusters.

It is known for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
