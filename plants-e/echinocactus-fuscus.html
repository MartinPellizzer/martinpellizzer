Echinocactus fuscus

Echinocactus fuscus, commonly known as the desert rose or the Mexican apple cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is a slow-growing cactus with a short, thick stem and a dense covering of spines. The plant produces vibrant red or pink flowers during the summer months, which are often followed by small, round fruits. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, and it is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Echinocactus fuscus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in rock gardens and as a houseplant.
