Erythrina arborescens

Erythrina arborescens, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its striking red, flame-like flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with three leaflets, and its bark is often covered in a thick, flaky layer. Erythrina arborescens is known for its rapid growth and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its vibrant floral display.

It also has medicinal uses in traditional medicine, though its seeds contain toxic compounds.
