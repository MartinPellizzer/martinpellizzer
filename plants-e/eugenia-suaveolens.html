Eugenia suaveolens

Eugenia suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented eugenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

The essential oils extracted from its leaves and flowers are valued for their aromatic properties.
