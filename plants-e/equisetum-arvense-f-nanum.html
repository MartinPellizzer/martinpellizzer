Equisetum arvense f. nanum

Equisetum arvense f. nanum, commonly known as the dwarf field horsetail, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the Equisetaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, jointed stems that are covered in small, scale-like structures. The plant produces spore-bearing cones at the top of the stem, which are typically found in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies.

Its unique morphology and ancient lineage make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
