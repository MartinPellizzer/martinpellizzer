Echinocactus sutterianus

Echinocactus sutterianus, commonly known as the Sutter's hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in sharp, spiny areoles, which provide protection from herbivores. The plant produces bright yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its rugged appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations, it is a popular choice for succulent gardens and rockeries.
