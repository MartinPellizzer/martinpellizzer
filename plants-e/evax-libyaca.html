Evax libyaca

Evax libyaca, commonly known as the Libyan thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid and semi-arid environments. Its scientific name reflects its geographic origin, with "libyaca" referring to Libya.

Evax libyaca plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
