Eupatorium megaphyllum

Eupatorium megaphyllum, commonly known as the giant thoroughwort, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves and showy, purple-to-pink flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and can reach heights of up to 6 feet. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, and it produces a distinctive, aromatic odor.

It is valued in herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
