Eragrostis microsperma

Eragrostis microsperma, commonly known as slender false panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and compact inflorescence, which consists of dense clusters of small spikelets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its seeds are small and wind-dispersed, aiding in its propagation across various environments.

Eragrostis microsperma is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and ability to stabilize soil.
