Eugenia minutifolia

Eugenia minutifolia, commonly known as the dwarf wax tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy green foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Eugenia minutifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in container gardens.
