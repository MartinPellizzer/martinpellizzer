Ensete ventricosum

Ensete ventricosum, commonly known as the giant banana, is a large herbaceous plant native to East Africa.

It belongs to the Musaceae family and is characterized by its tall, leafy pseudostem composed of tightly packed leaf sheaths. The plant produces large, clustered inflorescences that yield edible bananas, which are a staple food in many African communities. Its leaves can grow up to 5 meters in length, making it one of the tallest plants in the Musaceae family.

The plant is also valued for its fibrous material, which is used in traditional crafts and construction.
