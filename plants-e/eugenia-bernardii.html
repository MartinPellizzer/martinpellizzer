Eugenia bernardii

Eugenia bernardii, commonly known as the red mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, dark purple to black berries. The plant produces small, white, five-petaled flowers that are often clustered together. Its leaves are glossy, elliptical, and have a strong aromatic scent.

The fruit is typically harvested when fully mature, and it is valued for its high antioxidant content and medicinal properties.
