Erythroxylum novogranatense var. macrophyllum

Erythroxylum novogranatense var. macrophyllum, commonly known as the large-leafed coca, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its large, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are followed by orange-red berries. The leaves contain alkaloids, including cocaine, which have been historically used in traditional medicine and ritual practices.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.
