Eupatorium saxorum

Eupatorium saxorum, commonly known as rock eupatorium, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in rocky, woodland areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features tall, slender stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, white to pale blue flowers appear in clusters during the summer months, attracting various pollinators.

Eupatorium saxorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife and biodiversity.
