Eragrostis variegata

Eragrostis variegata, commonly known as the variegated lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It features slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that often display a striking variegation of green and silver. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale green to brown flowers. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Eragrostis variegata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
