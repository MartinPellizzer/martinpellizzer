Eugenia incarnata

Eugenia incarnata, commonly known as the common rosebay, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Eugenia incarnata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a habitat for various wildlife species.
