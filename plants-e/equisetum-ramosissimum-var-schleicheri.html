Equisetum ramosissimum var. schleicheri

Equisetum ramosissimum var. schleicheri, commonly known as the Schleicher's horse tail, is a member of the Equisetaceae family and is characterized by its ancient, primitive morphology.

It possesses a stem that is segmented and covered with small, scale-like leaves, which are typical of the genus Equisetum. The plant produces spore-bearing strobila at the apex of the stem, which are cylindrical and often referred to as "sprogs.". It thrives in wet, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors.

This variety is notable for its historical significance in paleobotany due to its resemblance to fossilized plants from the Carboniferous period.
