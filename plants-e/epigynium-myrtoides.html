Epigynium myrtoides

Epigynium myrtoides, commonly known as the myrtle-like epigynium, is a flowering plant belonging to the Myrtaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and showy, clustered flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are often consumed by various bird species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is native to regions of South America.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
