Eria caricifolia var. glabra

Eria caricifolia var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth-leaved eria, is a species of flowering plant in the orchid family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for horticultural study and conservation efforts.
