Eugenia conzattii

Eugenia conzattii, commonly known as the Zambian cherry or Zambian eugenia, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically red or orange when mature, resembling cherries in appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is cultivated in some horticultural collections.
