Eranthemum hypocrateriforme

Eranthemum hypocrateriforme, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow ray florets and dense, spiky disk flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded or semi-shaded environments. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are erect and branched.

Eranthemum hypocrateriforme is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
