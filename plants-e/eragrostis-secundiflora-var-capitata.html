Eragrostis secundiflora var. capitata

Eragrostis secundiflora var. capitata, commonly known as the tufted lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form compact clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically clustered at the base of the plant, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that becomes more prominent during the growing season.

This species is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient choice for ornamental and ecological landscaping.
