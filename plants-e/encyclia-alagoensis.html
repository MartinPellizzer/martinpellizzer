Encyclia alagoensis

Encyclia alagoensis, commonly known as the Alagoas orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and clustered, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale green with a yellowish lip. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is of interest for conservation efforts.
