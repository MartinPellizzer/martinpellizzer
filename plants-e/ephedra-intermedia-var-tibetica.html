Ephedra intermedia var. tibetica

Ephedra intermedia var. tibetica, commonly known as Tibetan ephedra, is a shrub native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the Ephedraceae family and is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and woody stems. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers and is adapted to arid and cold environments. It contains alkaloids such as ephedrine, which have been used in traditional medicine.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is also of interest for modern pharmacological research.
