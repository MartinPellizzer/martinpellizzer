Epilobium latifolium var. album

Epilobium latifolium var. album, commonly known as white willow herb, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and showy white to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist soils along streambanks and wet meadows. It is a herbaceous perennial that can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological importance.
