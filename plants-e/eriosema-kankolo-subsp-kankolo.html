Eriosema kankolo subsp. kankolo

Eriosema kankolo subsp.

kankolo, commonly known as the Kankolo eriosema, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It typically grows in grassy habitats and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in dense, racemose inflorescences. Its leaves are usually ovate to elliptic in shape and have a rough, hairy texture.

It is often found in moist, well-drained soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses.
