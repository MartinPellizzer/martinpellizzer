Elegia fistulosa var. parviflora

Elegia fistulosa var. parviflora, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
