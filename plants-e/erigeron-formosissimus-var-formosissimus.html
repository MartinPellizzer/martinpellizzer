Erigeron formosissimus var. formosissimus

Erigeron formosissimus var. formosissimus, commonly known as the Showy Blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features a basal rosette of blue-gray leaves and produces showy, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
