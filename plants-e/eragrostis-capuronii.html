Eragrostis capuronii

Eragrostis capuronii, commonly known as Capuron's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue. Its flowering spikes are small and often clustered, producing minute, wind-pollinated flowers.

Eragrostis capuronii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
