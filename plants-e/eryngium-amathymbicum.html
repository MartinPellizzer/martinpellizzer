Eryngium amathymbicum

Eryngium amathymbicum, commonly known as the mountain eryngo, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its spiky, blue to purple flower heads that resemble a sea urchin, surrounded by bristly, gray-green leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It is a perennial herb that produces small, wind-pollinated flowers, which develop into dry, capsule-like fruits.

Eryngium amathymbicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
