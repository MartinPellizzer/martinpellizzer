Erythroxylum engleri

Erythroxylum engleri, commonly known as the Engler's coca, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its erect, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with a glossy surface and serrated edges.

Erythroxylum engleri is notable for its chemical composition, containing alkaloids such as cocaine, which has historically been of significant botanical and pharmacological interest.
