Eupatorium micrachaenium

Eupatorium micrachaenium, commonly known as the small-leaved thoroughwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in clusters.

Eupatorium micrachaenium is often found in wetland habitats and is adapted to thrive in humid, temperate climates.
