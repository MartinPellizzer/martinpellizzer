Eysenhardtia oliviana

Eysenhardtia oliviana, commonly known as the olive tree of Mexico, is a small evergreen tree native to the coastal regions of Baja California and the southern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and lance-shaped, leathery leaves that are aromatic and glossy. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with moderate rainfall.

Eysenhardtia oliviana is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
