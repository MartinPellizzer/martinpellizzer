Euxolus caudatus var. maximus

Euxolus caudatus var. maximus, commonly known as the giant beardgrass, is a grass species characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, hairy inflorescences.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides, preferring full sun. The plant's foliage is often covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Its seeds are small and wind-dispersed, aiding in its propagation across various environments.

This variety is notable for its robust growth and adaptability to different soil types.
