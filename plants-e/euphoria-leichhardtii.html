Euphoria leichhardtii

Euphoria leichhardtii, commonly known as the Leichhardt's euphorbia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny stems and clusters of small yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

The species is also known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils, making it a resilient choice for xeriscaping.
