Elaeagnus griffithii

Elaeagnus griffithii, commonly known as the Indian silverberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters, followed by small, edible berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Elaeagnus griffithii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
