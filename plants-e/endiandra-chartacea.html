Endiandra chartacea

Endiandra chartacea, commonly known as the paper endiandra, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several winged seeds.

Endiandra chartacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage.
