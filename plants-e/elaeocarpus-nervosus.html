Elaeocarpus nervosus

Elaeocarpus nervosus, commonly known as the veined elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with prominent venation. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers followed by spherical, fleshy fruits that are often brightly colored. Its fruit is a key food source for various bird species, contributing to its role in seed dispersal.

The tree is valued for its hard wood and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
