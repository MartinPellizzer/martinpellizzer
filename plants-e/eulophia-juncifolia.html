Eulophia juncifolia

Eulophia juncifolia, commonly known as the grass-leafed orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and its clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to anchor in loose, organic soil.

Eulophia juncifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
