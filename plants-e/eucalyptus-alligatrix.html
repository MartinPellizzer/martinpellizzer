Eucalyptus alligatrix

Eucalyptus alligatrix, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and smooth, often greyish bark. This plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, thriving in sandy or gravelly soils. It is a shrub or small tree that can reach heights of up to 5 meters.

Eucalyptus alligatrix is known for its aromatic oils, which contribute to its use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
