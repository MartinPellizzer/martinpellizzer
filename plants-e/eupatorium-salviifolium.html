Eupatorium salviifolium

Eupatorium salviifolium, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and prairies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a silvery-blue hue, contributing to its ornamental value.

Eupatorium salviifolium is also known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used by Native American tribes for various health purposes.
