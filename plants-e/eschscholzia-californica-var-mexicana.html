Eschscholzia californica var. mexicana

Eschscholzia californica var. mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican poppy, is a variant of the California poppy that exhibits distinct botanical characteristics.

It is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. The plant features slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are typically pale yellow to orange, with a distinctive saucer-shaped corolla.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in arid and semi-arid environments.
