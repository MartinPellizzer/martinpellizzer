Eupatorium sitiense

Eupatorium sitiense, commonly known as the Sitien blueweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy surface, while its flowers bloom during the summer months.

Eupatorium sitiense is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
