Euphrasia officinalis f. lapponica

Euphrasia officinalis f. lapponica, commonly known as the Lapland eyebright, is a low-growing perennial herb native to arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, hairy stems that bear opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers with prominent, showy glands, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in tundra and mountainous habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
