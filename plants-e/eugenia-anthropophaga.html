Eugenia anthropophaga

Eugenia anthropophaga, commonly known as the "human-eating" guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant produces small, yellowish-green fruits that are typically not eaten by humans due to their bitter taste and toxic compounds. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
