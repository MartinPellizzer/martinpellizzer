Eucodonia × naegelioides

Eucodonia × naegelioides, commonly known as the hybrid hawthorn, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between *Eucodonia calycina* and *Eucodonia microcarpa*.

It is a small to medium-sized deciduous shrub or tree, native to the southeastern United States. The plant features opposite, simple leaves that are often elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are typically white or pink, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, berry-like fruits.

The hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its spring blossoms and autumn foliage.
