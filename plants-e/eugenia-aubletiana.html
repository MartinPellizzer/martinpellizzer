Eugenia aubletiana

Eugenia aubletiana, commonly known as the golden apple or yellow myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, aromatic bark and opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by bright orange or red berries. These berries are edible and have a sweet, tart flavor, often used in local cuisines.

Eugenia aubletiana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
