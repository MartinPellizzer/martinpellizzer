Eryngium poterioides

Eryngium poterioides, commonly known as the blue thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features spiny, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has silver-gray, fuzzy leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and borders.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and ornamental gardening.
