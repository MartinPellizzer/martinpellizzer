Eustrephus latifolius subvar. fasciculatus

Eustrephus latifolius subvar. fasciculatus, commonly known as the broadleaf eustrephus, is a flowering plant species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
