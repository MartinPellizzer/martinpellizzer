Echeveria rosea

Echeveria rosea, commonly known as the "Pink Grandiflora" or "Pink Echeveria," is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of fleshy, triangular leaves that are typically pink to purple in color, with a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and orange during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires plenty of sunlight to maintain its vibrant color.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
