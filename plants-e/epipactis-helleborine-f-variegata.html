Epipactis helleborine f. variegata

Epipactis helleborine f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated helleborine, is a hybrid orchid species characterized by its distinctive variegated leaf patterns.

It is a terrestrial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in moist, shaded woodlands. The plant features a short rhizome with broad, lance-shaped leaves that often display white or yellow markings. Its flowers are greenish-yellow with a prominent lip, and it produces small, yellowish-brown seeds.

This orchid is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and is often used in horticultural collections for its ornamental value.
