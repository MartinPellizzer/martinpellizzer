Eupatorium purpureum var. ovatum

Eupatorium purpureum var. ovatum, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and showy, purple-pink flower heads that resemble coneflowers. The plant produces small, white to pale blue florets arranged in a disc-like structure surrounded by bracts. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Eupatorium purpureum var. ovatum is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
