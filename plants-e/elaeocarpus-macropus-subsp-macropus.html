Elaeocarpus macropus subsp. macropus

Elaeocarpus macropus subsp.

macropus, commonly known as the broad-leafed elaeocarpus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae. It is native to eastern Australia, particularly Queensland and New South Wales, and is typically found in rainforest and coastal habitats.

The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, hard seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental foliage and is sometimes cultivated in subtropical gardens.
