Eriophorum angustifolium var. alpinum

Eriophorum angustifolium var. alpinum, commonly known as alpine tussock grass, is a flowering plant native to alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in mountain meadows and tundra environments.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
