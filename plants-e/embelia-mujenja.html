Embelia mujenja

Embelia mujenja, commonly known as African star anise, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces tiny, star-shaped fruits that are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and medicinal properties. Its botanical features include a woody stem, evergreen foliage, and a distinctive floral structure.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
