Elsota tavoyana

Elsota tavoyana, commonly known as the Tavoyan elms, is a species of deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ulmaceae family, which includes elms, angiosperms, and related genera. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by samaras, which are winged seeds aiding in wind dispersal.

Elsota tavoyana is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.
