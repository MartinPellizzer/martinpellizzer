Erysimum angustatum

Erysimum angustatum, commonly known as narrowleaf wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and bright yellow or orange flowers. It blooms in late spring to early summer and is often found in open woodlands and along streambanks.

Erysimum angustatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
