Elaeocarpus sylvestris

Elaeocarpus sylvestris, commonly known as the wild apple or forest apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, white to pink flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically blue or black when mature, containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forests.

The plant has ecological significance as a food source for various wildlife species.
