Erianthus asper var. brasilianus

Erianthus asper var. brasilianus, commonly known as the Brazilian rough grass, is a variant of the Erianthus asper species native to South America.

It is a tall, perennial grass characterized by its coarse, serrated leaves and robust growth habit. This plant typically thrives in sunny, well-drained environments and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. Its fibrous stalks and dense foliage make it a valuable species for erosion control and livestock forage.

The plant's adaptability to various soil types contributes to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
