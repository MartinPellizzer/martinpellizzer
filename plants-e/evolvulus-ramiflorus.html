Evolvulus ramiflorus

Evolvulus ramiflorus, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, five-petaled blue or violet flowers that open in the morning. The leaves are heart-shaped and opposite, with a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The plant is also known for its rapid growth and ability to climb or trail along surfaces.
