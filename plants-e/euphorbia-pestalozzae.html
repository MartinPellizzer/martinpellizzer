Euphorbia pestalozzae

Euphorbia pestalozzae, commonly known as the Pestalozza's spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers surrounded by green bracts. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
