Euphorbia × angustata

Euphorbia × angustata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spurge, is a hybrid shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, cylindrical stems and small, clustered yellow flowers that are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. The plant has a spiny texture and produces a milky sap that can be irritating to the skin. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Euphorbia × angustata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate arid conditions.
