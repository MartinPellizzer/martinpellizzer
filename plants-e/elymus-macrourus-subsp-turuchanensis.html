Elymus macrourus subsp. turuchanensis

Elymus macrourus subsp.

turuchanensis, commonly known as the Turuchan needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the cold climates of northern regions. It typically grows in alpine and subarctic environments, often found in rocky or poorly drained soils.

The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, with small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This subspecies is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient component of its native ecosystems.
