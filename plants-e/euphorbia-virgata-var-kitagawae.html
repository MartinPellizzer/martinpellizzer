Euphorbia virgata var. kitagawae

Euphorbia virgata var. kitagawae, commonly known as the Japanese euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to Japan.

It features a compact, branching structure with thick, fleshy stems that store water, typical of many succulents. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is a popular choice for gardeners interested in drought-tolerant plants.
