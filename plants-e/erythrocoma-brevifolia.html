Erythrocoma brevifolia

Erythrocoma brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved erythrocoma, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, brightly colored flowers, typically in shades of red or orange, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
