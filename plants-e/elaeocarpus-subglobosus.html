Elaeocarpus subglobosus

Elaeocarpus subglobosus, commonly known as the globular elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its rounded, smooth bark and dense foliage. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
