Erodium bryoniifolium

Erodium bryoniifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf bedstraw, is a low-growing annual herb native to the western United States.

It features small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the spring, typically from March to May. The plant has opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly succulent texture. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open areas such as roadsides and fields.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Geraniaceae and is characterized by its unique leaf arrangement and flowering pattern.
