Euacer molle

Euacer molle, commonly known as the soft euacer, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of white and pale pink. The plant has slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
