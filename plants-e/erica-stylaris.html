Erica stylaris

Erica stylaris, commonly known as the narrowleaf heath, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes shrubs and small trees known for their evergreen foliage and showy flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in areas with well-drained conditions. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually pink or red, appearing in clusters during the spring and summer seasons.

Erica stylaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive blooms.
