Eupatorium xalapanum

Eupatorium xalapanum, commonly known as Mexican boneset, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and has opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antipyretic properties.

The flowering period occurs in the summer months, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
