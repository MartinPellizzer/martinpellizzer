Erianthemum virescens

Erianthemum virescens, commonly known as the greenleaf erianthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners in warm climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is often used in landscaping and as a bedding plant.
