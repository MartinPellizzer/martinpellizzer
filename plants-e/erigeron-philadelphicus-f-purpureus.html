Erigeron philadelphicus f. purpureus

Erigeron philadelphicus f. purpureus, commonly known as Purple Coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy purple to lavender flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has hairy, gray-green foliage and forms a compact, upright growth habit. It is often found in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for wetland gardens and native plant landscapes.
