Erysimum pusillum subsp. hayekii

Erysimum pusillum subsp.

hayekii, commonly known as the Kashmir wallflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan. It features compact, mat-forming growth with small, showy flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in spring.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant flowers and hardy nature.
