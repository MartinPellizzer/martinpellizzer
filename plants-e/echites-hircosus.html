Echites hircosus

Echites hircosus, commonly known as the goat's horn plant, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its woody stems and dense, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or coastal environments.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
