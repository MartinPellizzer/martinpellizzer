Eugenia glauca var. pseudoglauca

Eugenia glauca var. pseudoglauca, commonly known as the silverleaf eucalyptus, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy coating. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters.

It is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.
