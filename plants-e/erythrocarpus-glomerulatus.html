Erythrocarpus glomerulatus

Erythrocarpus glomerulatus, commonly known as the clustered red-fruited plant, is a flowering plant species native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered inflorescences and red or purple flowers. Its leaves are narrow and often have a fragrant scent, typical of the Myrtaceae family.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its ecological role in native habitats.
