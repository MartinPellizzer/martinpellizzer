Eupatorium paraguariense var. angustifolia

Eupatorium paraguariense var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf eupatorium, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, and it produces fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
