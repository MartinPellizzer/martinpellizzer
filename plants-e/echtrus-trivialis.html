Echtrus trivialis

Echtrus trivialis, commonly known as the common echtrus, is a small flowering plant native to temperate regions.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. It is often found in gardens and naturalized areas, where it spreads through rhizomes.

Due to its hardy nature, it is considered a resilient species in the botanical community.
