Eragrostis basilepis

Eragrostis basilepis, commonly known as blackgrass or desert grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to survive in poor soil and extreme temperatures.

Eragrostis basilepis is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
