Eupatorium canescens

Eupatorium canescens, commonly known as white boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its grayish-white stems and opposite, serrated leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale purple, blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
