Euodia velutina

Euodia velutina, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, woody fruits that contain several seeds. Euodia velutina is valued for its aromatic wood and has traditional uses in medicine and cuisine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in tropical ecosystems.
