Elaeocarpus vieillardii

Elaeocarpus vieillardii, commonly known as the blackwood tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its dark, dense wood and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by hard, black fruits that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
