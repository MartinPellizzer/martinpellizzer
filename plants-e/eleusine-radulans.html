Eleusine radulans

Eleusine radulans, commonly known as false rice, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific. The plant has slender, tufted shoots and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce numerous tiny flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue.

Eleusine radulans is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils.
