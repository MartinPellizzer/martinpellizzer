Erica foliacea var. fulgens

Erica foliacea var. fulgens, commonly known as the Cape honeysuckle, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and Mediterranean climates. Its small, needle-like leaves are aromatic and contribute to its ornamental value.

This variety is particularly noted for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
