Erica aspalathifolia var. aspalathifolia

Erica aspalathifolia var. aspalathifolia, commonly known as the African tea plant, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, needle-like leaves that are typically a deep green color. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique flavor profile, it is widely cultivated for its leaves, which are used to make a popular herbal tea.
