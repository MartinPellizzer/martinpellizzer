Eragrostis trachycarpa

Eragrostis trachycarpa, commonly known as rough false brome, is a perennial grass species native to various regions of the northern hemisphere.

It typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as fields, roadsides, and sandy soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a clumping growth habit.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
