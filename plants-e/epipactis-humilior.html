Epipactis humilior

Epipactis humilior, commonly known as the lesser helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial herb with a rhizomatous root system and typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant produces showy, greenish-yellow to brownish flowers with a distinctive, spurred lip. Its leaves are broad, ovate, and arranged in a basal rosette.

Epipactis humilior is notable for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and is often found in association with other orchid species in its habitat.
