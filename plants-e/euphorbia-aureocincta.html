Euphorbia aureocincta

Euphorbia aureocincta, commonly known as the golden-banded spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a thick, cylindrical stem that is often green or reddish in color, covered with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. Its leaves are typically narrow and may have a slight yellow or golden border, giving it its common name.

Euphorbia aureocincta is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.
