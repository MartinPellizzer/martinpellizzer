Eugenia ruiziana

Eugenia ruiziana, commonly known as the red guava, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible red berries that are rich in vitamin C and have a sweet, aromatic flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

Eugenia ruiziana is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
