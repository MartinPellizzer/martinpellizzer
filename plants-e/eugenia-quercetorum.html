Eugenia quercetorum

Eugenia quercetorum, commonly known as the Queensland myrtle, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to northeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which is characterized by aromatic oils and showy flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

The flowers are small, white or pink, and are followed by dark, edible berries that attract birds and other wildlife.
