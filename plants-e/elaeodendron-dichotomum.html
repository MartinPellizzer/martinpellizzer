Elaeodendron dichotomum

Elaeodendron dichotomum, commonly known as the two-branched elaeodendron, is a rare and unique plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive branching pattern, where the stems split into two equal parts, creating a symmetrical appearance. The plant typically grows in tropical rainforest environments, thriving in humid and shaded conditions. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a smooth surface and a dark green color.

Elaeodendron dichotomum is of interest to botanists due to its unusual morphology and limited distribution.
