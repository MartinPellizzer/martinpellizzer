Eleocharis pellucida f. elata

Eleocharis pellucida f. elata, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a variant of the spike-rush genus Eleocharis.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and shallow water bodies, where it thrives in moist to submerged conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it produces spike-like inflorescences that are often partially submerged.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to aquatic environments and its role in stabilizing wetland soils.
