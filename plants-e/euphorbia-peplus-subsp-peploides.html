Euphorbia peplus subsp. peploides

Euphorbia peplus subsp.

peploides, commonly known as common spurge, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It features a succulent, branched stem covered in small, white, milky sap-filled glands.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers surrounded by green bracts, which are often mistaken for petals. Its leaves are typically alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.
