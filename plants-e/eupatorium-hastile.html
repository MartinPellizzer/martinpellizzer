Eupatorium hastile

Eupatorium hastile, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, blue-to-purple flower clusters that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silvery hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

Eupatorium hastile is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
