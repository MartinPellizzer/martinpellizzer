Eugenia cotinifolia subsp. codyensis

Eugenia cotinifolia subsp.

codyensis, commonly known as the Cody's eugenia, is a shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or swampy areas.

The plant features simple, opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with a glossy upper surface and a lighter underside. Its small, white to pale pink flowers appear in clusters and are followed by small, red, fleshy berries.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
