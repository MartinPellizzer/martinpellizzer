Erica laricina

Erica laricina, commonly known as the dwarf heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the boreal and subarctic regions of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, compact foliage consisting of small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Erica laricina thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as heathlands and rocky slopes.

It is a hardy species that is well adapted to cold climates and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
