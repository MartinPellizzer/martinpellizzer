Echinocereus polyacanthus subsp. mombergerianus

Echinocereus polyacanthus subsp.

mombergerianus, commonly known as the Momberger's hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Bolivia. It is a columnar cactus with a tall, slender stem that can reach up to 2 meters in height.

The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its spines are short and often covered in a white, powdery coating, which helps to reduce water loss.

This subspecies is well adapted to harsh desert conditions and is popular among cactus enthusiasts for its striking appearance and resilience.
