Euodia schullei var. quercifolia

Euodia schullei var. quercifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf euodia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often leathery and have a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, fleshy fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
