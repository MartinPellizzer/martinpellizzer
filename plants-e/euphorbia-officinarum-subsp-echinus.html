Euphorbia officinarum subsp. echinus

Euphorbia officinarum subsp.

echinus, commonly known as the spiny euphorbia, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa. It features a thick, woody stem with spiny, fleshy leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a cup-like structure called a cyathium. It is adapted to arid environments and stores water in its leaves and stems.

This species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
