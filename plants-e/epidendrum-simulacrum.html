Epidendrum simulacrum

Epidendrum simulacrum, commonly known as the mimic orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, arching stems and clusters of fragrant, violet to purple flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has strap-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining potting media to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and adaptability.
