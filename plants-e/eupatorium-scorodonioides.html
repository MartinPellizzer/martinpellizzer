Eupatorium scorodonioides

Eupatorium scorodonioides, commonly known as black snakeroot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

The species is also valued in ecological gardens for its ability to support pollinators and its ornamental appeal.
