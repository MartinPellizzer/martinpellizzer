Echinocereus occidentalis

Echinocereus occidentalis, commonly known as the Western prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems and clusters of vibrant yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces edible fruit known as prickly pears, which have a sweet, tangy flavor. It has spiny areoles and is adapted to arid environments with minimal water requirements.

Its structure and growth patterns make it well-suited for xeriscaping and desert gardens.
