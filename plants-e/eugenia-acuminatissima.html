Eugenia acuminatissima

Eugenia acuminatissima, commonly known as the narrow-pointed eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

The fruit is a small, edible berry that is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
