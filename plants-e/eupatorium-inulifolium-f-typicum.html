Eupatorium inulifolium f. typicum

Eupatorium inulifolium f. typicum, commonly known as white boneset, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as wetlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and wildlife habitats.
