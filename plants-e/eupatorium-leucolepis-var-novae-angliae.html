Eupatorium leucolepis var. novae-angliae

Eupatorium leucolepis var. novae-angliae, commonly known as New England thoroughwort, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features tall, erect stems that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a serrated margin.

It is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.
