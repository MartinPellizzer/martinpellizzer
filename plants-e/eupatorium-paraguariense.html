Eupatorium paraguariense

Eupatorium paraguariense, commonly known as Paraguayan boneset, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a rough texture.

Eupatorium paraguariense is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
