Eriogonum viscanum

Eriogonum viscanum, commonly known as the Sierra buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant thrives in rocky soils and dry, sunny environments, often found in mountainous regions. It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
