Epipactis helleborine subsp. persica

Epipactis helleborine subsp.

persica, commonly known as the Persian helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its dark green, pleated leaves and a single, showy, greenish-yellow flower with a distinctive lip.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as woodlands and meadows. It is a perennial herb that produces a single flower per stem, which blooms in late spring to early summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different floral morphology compared to other members of the *Epipactis* genus.
