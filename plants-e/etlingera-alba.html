Etlingera alba

Etlingera alba, commonly known as the white torch ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its striking white or pale pink flowers that resemble torches, emerging from a tall, leafy stem. Its leaves are broad, elongated, and have a slightly rough texture.

Etlingera alba is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
