Epilobium perrieri

Epilobium perrieri, commonly known as Perrier's willow herb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. The plant features slender stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a hairy underside. Its flowers are pink to purple, arranged in spikes, and bloom in late summer.

Epilobium perrieri is part of the Onagraceae family, which includes other willow herbs and related species.
