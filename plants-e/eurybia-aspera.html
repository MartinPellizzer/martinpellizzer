Eurybia aspera

Eurybia aspera, commonly known as rough eryngo, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in dry, sandy soils.

Eurybia aspera is typically perennial and has a compact growth habit, making it a hardy addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
