Erythraea ramosissima var. pseudolinariifolia

Erythraea ramosissima var. pseudolinariifolia, commonly known as the red sandalwood or false linaria, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, branched structure and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are typically narrow and linear, contributing to its overall drought-resistant morphology.

The species is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique floral structure and ecological adaptations.
