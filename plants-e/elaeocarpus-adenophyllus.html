Elaeocarpus adenophyllus

Elaeocarpus adenophyllus, commonly known as the velvetwood or white-flowered elaeocarpus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a leathery texture, while its roots are known to be fibrous and extensive.

Elaeocarpus adenophyllus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
