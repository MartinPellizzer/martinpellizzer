Erica colorans

Erica colorans, commonly known as the flowering heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica colorans thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
