Elytraria indica

Elytraria indica, commonly known as the Indian elytraria, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, commonly known as grasses, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slight waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Elytraria indica is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
