Echium abdelrhamanii

Echium abdelrhamanii, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers and linear, grass-like leaves that are adapted to conserve water. The plant produces a long, slender seed stalk that can reach up to two meters in height, making it a notable feature in its natural habitat. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Echium abdelrhamanii is well-suited to dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland ecosystems.
