Ericoides discolor

Ericoides discolor, commonly known as the silverleaf erica, is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes heaths and heathers.

It is a shrub that typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils, often in heathland or moorland environments. The plant features small, evergreen leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white wax, giving it its common name. Its flowers are usually pink or white and appear in clusters, blooming in late spring or early summer.

Ericoides discolor is adapted to cool, moist climates and is often found in regions with high rainfall.
