Eugenia caducipetala

Eugenia caducipetala, commonly known as the fragrant myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

Eugenia caducipetala is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
