Entada nudiflora

Entada nudiflora, commonly known as the naked-flowered entada, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, light-colored bark and large, compound leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces showy, pendulous flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, which appear before the leaves in the spring. Its fruit is a long, slender pod that splits open to release seeds enclosed in a fibrous mass.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
