Erica quadrangularis

Erica quadrangularis, commonly known as the four-angle heath, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in rocky and sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves that are four-angled in cross-section, giving it its common name. It produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers during the summer months, attracting various pollinators.

Erica quadrangularis is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
