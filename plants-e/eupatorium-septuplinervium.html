Eupatorium septuplinervium

Eupatorium septuplinervium, commonly known as the seven-veined eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped with seven prominent veins, giving it its scientific name.

The flowers are usually small and clustered, producing a white to pale purple coloration.
