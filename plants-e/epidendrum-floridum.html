Epidendrum floridum

Epidendrum floridum, commonly known as the Florida Epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as swamps and pine flatwoods. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, arranged in dense spikes. It has broad, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Epidendrum floridum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
