Encyclia navarroi

Encyclia navarroi, commonly known as the Navarro's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Epidendroideae subfamily and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, supporting its survival in arid environments. Encyclia navarroi is often found in rocky soils and open woodlands, where it thrives in partial shade.
