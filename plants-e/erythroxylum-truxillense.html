Erythroxylum truxillense

Erythroxylum truxillense, commonly known as the Truxillense copal tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to the Andean region of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and elliptical, dark green leaves. It produces a sticky resin known as copal, which has been historically used by indigenous communities for various purposes.

The tree typically grows in humid, tropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils.
