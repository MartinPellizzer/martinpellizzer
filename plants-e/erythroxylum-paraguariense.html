Erythroxylum paraguariense

Erythroxylum paraguariense, commonly known as Paraguayan copal, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly found in parts of Brazil, Paraguay, and Argentina. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain seeds, and it is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant has been studied for its potential medicinal properties and ecological significance in its native habitat.
