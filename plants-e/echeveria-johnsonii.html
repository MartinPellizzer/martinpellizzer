Echeveria johnsonii

Echeveria johnsonii, commonly known as the Johnson's Echeveria, is a succulent native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of fleshy, bluish-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating called bloom. The plant produces striking clusters of pink to red flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid climates.

Due to its compact size and attractive appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
