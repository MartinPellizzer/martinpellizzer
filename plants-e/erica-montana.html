Erica montana

Erica montana, commonly known as the mountain heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heaths and heather. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica montana thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and seasonal blooms.
