Epimeredi malabaricus

Epimeredi malabaricus, commonly known as the Malabar rose, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal.

The botanical features of Epimeredi malabaricus make it a notable species in the region's flora.
