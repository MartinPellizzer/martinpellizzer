Euphorbia lamarckii f. latibracteata

Euphorbia lamarckii f. latibracteata, commonly known as the broad-bract euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and modified leaves that serve as spines. The plant produces striking yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by large, fleshy bracts. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions, storing water in its stems and leaves.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in challenging environments.
