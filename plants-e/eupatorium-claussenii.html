Eupatorium claussenii

Eupatorium claussenii, commonly known as the Mexican thoroughwort, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It is often found in open habitats such as meadows and along stream banks.

Eupatorium claussenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
