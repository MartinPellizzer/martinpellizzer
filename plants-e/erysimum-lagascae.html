Erysimum lagascae

Erysimum lagascae, commonly known as the Sierra foothill rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are elongated and covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are small, four-petaled, and usually yellow or white. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Erysimum lagascae is well adapted to arid conditions and is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
