Euodia glaberrima

Euodia glaberrima, commonly known as the rough-leaved euodia, is a member of the Rutaceae family, native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, globose drupe that turns from green to reddish when mature.

Euodia glaberrima is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
