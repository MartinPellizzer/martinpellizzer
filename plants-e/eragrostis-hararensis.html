Eragrostis hararensis

Eragrostis hararensis, commonly known as Harare grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant has slender, cylindrical leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to arid conditions and can thrive with minimal water.

Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion in its native environment.
