Echinopsis pudantii

Echinopsis pudantii, commonly known as the White Star Cactus, is a species native to South America, specifically Argentina.

It is a small, globular cactus with a dense covering of white, star-shaped spines. The plant produces large, white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its striking appearance and unique features, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
