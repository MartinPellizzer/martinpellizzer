Eriosema decumbens subsp. decumbens

Eriosema decumbens subsp.

decumbens, commonly known as the hairy eriosema, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It features a sprawling growth habit with stolons that root at the nodes, forming dense mats.

The plant has hairy, ovate leaves with a prominent midrib and small, white to pale yellow flowers clustered in terminal racemes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and potential medicinal research.
