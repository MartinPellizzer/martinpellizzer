Egletes ramosissima

Egletes ramosissima, commonly known as the desert hedgehog cactus, is a spineless cactus native to arid regions of North America.

It features a short, thick stem that is often covered in a layer of white, waxy bloom. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water requirements.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it a notable species in desert ecosystems.
