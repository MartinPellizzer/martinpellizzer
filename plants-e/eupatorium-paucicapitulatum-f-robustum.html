Eupatorium paucicapitulatum f. robustum

Eupatorium paucicapitulatum f. robustum, commonly known as the robust eupatorium, is a variant of the lesser boneset, characterized by its sturdy growth and dense foliage.

It is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States, typically found in wetlands and along stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, clustered white to pale blue flowers in late summer. Its inflorescences are often surrounded by bracts that are white or pale green, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
