Epigeneium tricallosum

Epigeneium tricallosum, commonly known as the three-lobed epigee, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves with three distinct lobes. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, followed by bright red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
