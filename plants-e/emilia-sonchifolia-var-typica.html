Emilia sonchifolia var. typica

Emilia sonchifolia var. typica, commonly known as the common emilia, is a herbaceous annual plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems with finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, which are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for garden cultivation.

Emilia sonchifolia var. typica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
