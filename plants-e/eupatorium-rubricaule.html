Eupatorium rubricaule

Eupatorium rubricaule, commonly known as red-stemmed thoroughwort, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as wetlands and stream banks, thriving in partial to full shade. Its stems are distinctly red or purple, and its leaves are opposite, with serrated edges.

It produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.
