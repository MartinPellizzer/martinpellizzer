Euphorbia mosana

Euphorbia mosana, commonly known as the Mosan spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a short, thick stem with paired, oval-shaped leaves that are typically green with a reddish or purple tinge. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a cup-like structure called a cyathium, which is characteristic of the Euphorbia genus. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
