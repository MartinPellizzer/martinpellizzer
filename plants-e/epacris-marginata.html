Epacris marginata

Epacris marginata, commonly known as the mountain heath, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color. Epacris marginata thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and rocky areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
