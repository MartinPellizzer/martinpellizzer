Equisetum diffusum

Equisetum diffusum, commonly known as the common horsetail, is a perennial plant belonging to the Equisetaceae family.

It is characterized by its jointed, hollow stems covered in small, scale-like structures. The plant produces spore-bearing cones at the base of its stems, which are typically found near the ground. Equisetum diffusum thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ancient lineage dates back to the Paleozoic era, making it one of the oldest living plant species.
