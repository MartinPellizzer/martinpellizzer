Eupatorium heterosquameum

Eupatorium heterosquameum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved boneset, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Eupatorium heterosquameum is known for its aromatic foliage and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
