Euphorbia neokaessneri

Euphorbia neokaessneri, commonly known as the Neokaessner's spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a short, thick stem with thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a reddish tinge. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by specialized structures called cyathia. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
