Echium vulgare var. lacaitae

Echium vulgare var. lacaitae, commonly known as the red poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking red or purple flowers, which bloom in summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
