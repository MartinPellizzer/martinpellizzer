Emmotum floribundum

Emmotum floribundum, commonly known as the common bluebell, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in spring. The plant features strap-like leaves that are broad and lanceolate, often emerging from a bulbous underground stem. It thrives in woodland environments and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Emmotum floribundum is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
