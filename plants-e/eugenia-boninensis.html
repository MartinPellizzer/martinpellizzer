Eugenia boninensis

Eugenia boninensis, commonly known as the Bonin hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Bonin Islands.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its fruits are small, woody berries that contain several seeds, and it is often found in coastal and humid forest environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
