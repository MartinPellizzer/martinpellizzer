Erysimum iraqense

Erysimum iraqense, commonly known as the Iraqi wallflower, is a flowering plant native to Iraq and parts of the Middle East.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showing adaptability to harsh climates. It produces bright yellow or orange flowers, which bloom in early spring.

Its narrow, lance-shaped leaves are covered in fine hairs, helping to reduce water loss in dry conditions.
