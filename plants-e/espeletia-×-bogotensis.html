Espeletia × bogotensis

Espeletia × bogotensis, commonly known as the Bogotá heather, is a hybrid plant native to the Andean highlands of Colombia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments, often growing in open, rocky areas with well-drained soil. Its flowering period typically occurs in the spring and early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is a notable species in the study of Andean flora.
