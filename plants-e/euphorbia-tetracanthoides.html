Euphorbia tetracanthoides

Euphorbia tetracanthoides, commonly known as the Texas pricklypear, is a succulent shrub native to the southern United States.

It features a compact, branching structure with fleshy, spiny stems that store water, adapting to arid environments. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by specialized structures called cyathia. Its leaves are usually reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it has a shallow root system that helps it survive in dry conditions.

Euphorbia tetracanthoides is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
