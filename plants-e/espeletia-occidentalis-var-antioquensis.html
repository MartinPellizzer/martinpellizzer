Espeletia occidentalis var. antioquensis

Espeletia occidentalis var. antioquensis, commonly known as the Antioquian heather, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and showy, violet-colored inflorescences. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments, typically at elevations between 2,500 and 4,000 meters. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance in supporting local pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
