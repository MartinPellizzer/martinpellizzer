Encephalartos aplanatus

Encephalartos aplanatus, commonly known as the flat-leaf cycad, is a rare and endangered cycad species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its short, stout trunk and a dense rosette of narrow, dark green leaves that are typically flat and strap-like. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs, typical of cycads. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and requires a subtropical climate with mild temperatures.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is critically endangered and protected in its native range.
