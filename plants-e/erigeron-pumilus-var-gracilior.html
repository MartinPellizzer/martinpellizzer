Erigeron pumilus var. gracilior

Erigeron pumilus var. gracilior, commonly known as the slender blueweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes, where it thrives in full sun. The plant features slender stems and small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
