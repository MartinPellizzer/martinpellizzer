Eleocharis tetraquetra var. tsurumachii

Eleocharis tetraquetra var. tsurumachii, commonly known as the Japanese spike-rush, is a sedge species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, ponds, and along the edges of water bodies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slightly rough texture.

This variety is noted for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
