Erythraea pyrenaica

Erythraea pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean erythraea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in pine and oak forests.
