Eriocaulon sedgwickii

Eriocaulon sedgwickii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf marsh grass, is a small, herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in shallow, muddy or sandy soils along the edges of ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowering spikes are usually less than 10 centimeters tall and produce small, inconspicuous flowers.

Eriocaulon sedgwickii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
