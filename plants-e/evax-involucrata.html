Evax involucrata

Evax involucrata, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stem and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant has adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through a deep root system and a waxy cuticle that reduces water loss. Its leaves are typically narrow and reduced, minimizing surface area for evaporation.

Evax involucrata plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for certain wildlife.
