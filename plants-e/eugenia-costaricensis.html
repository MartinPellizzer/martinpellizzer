Eugenia costaricensis

Eugenia costaricensis, commonly known as the Costa Rican eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Central America, particularly Costa Rica, and is often found in tropical moist forests. The plant is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers. It produces small, dark blue to black berries that are edible and contain several seeds.

Eugenia costaricensis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for various wildlife species.
