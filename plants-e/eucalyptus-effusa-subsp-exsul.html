Eucalyptus effusa subsp. exsul

Eucalyptus effusa subsp.

exsul, commonly known as the slender gum, is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often glaucous in appearance.

The species produces small, globular flower buds that open to reveal white or pale pink flowers. Its bark is typically smooth and peels in thin, fibrous strips.

This plant is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in open forests and woodlands.
