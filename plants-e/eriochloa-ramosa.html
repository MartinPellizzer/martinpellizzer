Eriochloa ramosa

Eriochloa ramosa, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, branching stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, white to pale green flowers. Its leaves are narrow and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

Eriochloa ramosa is often found in disturbed soils and is used in restoration projects due to its hardy and adaptive qualities.
