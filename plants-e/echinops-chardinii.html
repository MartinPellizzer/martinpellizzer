Echinops chardinii

Echinops chardinii, commonly known as the desert globemallow, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble small globes, which give it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its woody stems. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in sandy or rocky soils, making it well adapted to harsh environments.

Echinops chardinii is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
