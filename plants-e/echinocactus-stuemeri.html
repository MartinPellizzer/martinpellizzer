Echinocactus stuemeri

Echinocactus stuemeri, commonly known as the Stuemmer's hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical shape and spiny, ribbed structure, which helps it retain water in its natural habitat. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its rugged exterior. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry conditions.

Echinocactus stuemeri is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and unique appearance.
