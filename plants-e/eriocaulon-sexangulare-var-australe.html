Eriocaulon sexangulare var. australe

Eriocaulon sexangulare var. australe, commonly known as the Australian triangular flat sedge, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters near the base of the stems. It typically grows in wetland or swampy environments, thriving in moist, acidic soils.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
