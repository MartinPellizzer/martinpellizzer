Ericoides trichromum

Ericoides trichromum, commonly known as the three-colored heath, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in countries like Argentina and Chile. The plant is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
