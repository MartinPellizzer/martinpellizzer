Eremiella

Eremiella, commonly known as the desert lily, is a genus of flowering plants native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

These plants are characterized by their succulent leaves and showy, often fragrant flowers that bloom in response to rainfall. Eremiella species typically have deep root systems that allow them to survive in harsh, dry environments. They are often found in rocky soils and can thrive in areas with limited water availability.

The genus is well adapted to extreme conditions, making it an important component of desert ecosystems.
