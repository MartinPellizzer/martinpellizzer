Ewyckia caerulea

Ewyckia caerulea, commonly known as the blue euryale, is a striking aquatic plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that grow in dense clusters, often reaching lengths of up to 1 meter. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, adding a vibrant touch to its habitat. It thrives in shallow, still water and is often found in ponds and marshes.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental water gardens.
