Erlangea incana

Erlangea incana, commonly known as the hairy erlangia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems covered in fine, white hairs, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
