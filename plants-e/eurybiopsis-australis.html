Eurybiopsis australis

Eurybiopsis australis, commonly known as the Australian starflower, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Australia.

It features small, star-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, helping it conserve moisture in its arid environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics reflect its evolutionary adaptation to the harsh conditions of the Australian outback.
