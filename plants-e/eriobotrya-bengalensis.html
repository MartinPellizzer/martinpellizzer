Eriobotrya bengalensis

Eriobotrya bengalensis, commonly known as the Indian loquat, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are edible and have a sweet-sour taste. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.

The tree is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional systems of medicine.
