Euphorbia pancicii

Euphorbia pancicii, commonly known as the Pancici's spurge, is a succulent plant native to the island of Sicily.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in a structure called a cyathium. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
