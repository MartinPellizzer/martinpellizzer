Echinopsis campylacantha

Echinopsis campylacantha, commonly known as the Pencil Cactus, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that resemble pencils, which are covered in dense, spiny areoles. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and well-draining soil.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
