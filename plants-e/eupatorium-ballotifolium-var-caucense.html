Eupatorium ballotifolium var. caucense

Eupatorium ballotifolium var. caucense, commonly known as Caucasian whitehead, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and forest edges. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with a slightly rough texture, and it produces numerous small seeds that are dispersed by wind.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
