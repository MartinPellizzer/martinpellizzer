Eupatorium calaminthifolium var. pluridentatum

Eupatorium calaminthifolium var. pluridentatum, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features showy, violet to purple flower heads that are often surrounded by narrow, green leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and swamps, and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and the stems are often reddish in color.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
