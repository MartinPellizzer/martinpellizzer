Elaeis madagascariensis

Elaeis madagascariensis, commonly known as the African oil palm, is a tropical plant native to western and central Africa.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves and produces fruits that contain oil-rich seeds. The plant is cultivated for its oil, which is used in food, cosmetics, and biofuel production. It thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its economic importance, it is also a subject of environmental and agricultural research.
