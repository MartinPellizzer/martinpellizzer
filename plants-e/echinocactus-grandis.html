Echinocactus grandis

Echinocactus grandis, commonly known as the Mexican giant cardon, is a species of cactus native to the Sonoran Desert.

It is characterized by its massive, columnar stem that can grow up to 15 meters in height. The plant features prominent, sharp spines and is adapted to survive in arid environments with minimal water. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing in late spring.

Echinocactus grandis plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for desert wildlife.
