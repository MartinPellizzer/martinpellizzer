Echinocactus denudatus var. scheidelianus

Echinocactus denudatus var. scheidelianus, commonly known as the Mexican torch cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem and spiny, ribbed structure, which helps it conserve water in its harsh environment. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green body. It thrives in full sun and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and rockeries.
