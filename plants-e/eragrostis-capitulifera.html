Eragrostis capitulifera

Eragrostis capitulifera, commonly known as blue panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa and parts of the Middle East.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, spike-like inflorescence that resembles a small cluster of flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration that gives it its common name.

Eragrostis capitulifera is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
