Eucalyptus micrantha var. signata

Eucalyptus micrantha var. signata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and smooth, often greyish bark. The flowers are typically white or pale blue, arranged in clusters, and are produced in spring. This variety is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

It is valued for its ecological role and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ornamental features.
