Echium papillosum

Echium papillosum, commonly known as the woolly thimbleweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky, woolly appearance, with leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Echium papillosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique visual characteristics.
