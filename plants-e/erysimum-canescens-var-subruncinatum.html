Erysimum canescens var. subruncinatum

Erysimum canescens var. subruncinatum, commonly known as the hairy wallflower, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or dry soils and prefers full sun. The plant features grayish-green, fuzzy leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It is part of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with four-petaled flowers.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
