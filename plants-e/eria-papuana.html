Eria papuana

Eria papuana, commonly known as the Papuan orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of New Guinea and nearby islands.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and strap-like, adapting to its climbing habit.

Eria papuana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
