Euphorbia artvinensis

Euphorbia artvinensis, commonly known as Artvin spurge, is a flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its succulent stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a cup-like structure called a cyathium. It is adapted to grow in arid and rocky environments, showcasing its resilience.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
