Eleocharis variegata var. laxiflora

Eleocharis variegata var. laxiflora, commonly known as the variable sedge, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, strap-shaped leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often in marshes, swamps, or along the edges of ponds. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, and it produces small, dry fruits that are dispersed by water or wind.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
