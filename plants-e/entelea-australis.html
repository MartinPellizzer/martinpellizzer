Entelea australis

Entelea australis, commonly known as the Australian entelea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers arranged in clusters, which are attractive to pollinators. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a leathery texture.

Entelea australis is also known for its ability to tolerate salt spray and is sometimes used in coastal landscaping.
