Enteropogon chlorideus

Enteropogon chlorideus, commonly known as the yellow false rice, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow spikelets that are clustered in dense panicles. It is often mistaken for true rice due to its similar appearance.

Botanically, it is classified within the Poaceae family and is known for its adaptability to various soil types.
