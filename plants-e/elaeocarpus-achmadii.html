Elaeocarpus achmadii

Elaeocarpus achmadii, commonly known as the Java elaeocarp, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and showy, white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests and secondary growth areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
