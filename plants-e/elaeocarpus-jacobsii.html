Elaeocarpus jacobsii

Elaeocarpus jacobsii, commonly known as the Jacobs' elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically bird-dispersed. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
