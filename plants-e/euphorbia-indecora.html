Euphorbia indecora

Euphorbia indecora, commonly known as the "Coral tree" or "Fairy's tears," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a distinctive appearance with its branched, woody stems and clusters of small, colorful bracts that resemble coral. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, followed by unique, bead-like fruits. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Euphorbia indecora is also known for its milky sap, which can be irritating to the skin.
