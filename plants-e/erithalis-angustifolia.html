Erithalis angustifolia

Erithalis angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf erithalis, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Ericaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white. The plant is often found in acidic soils in woodland or heathland environments. It is a shrub that can grow up to 1 meter tall, with a compact and bushy habit.

Erithalis angustifolia is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
