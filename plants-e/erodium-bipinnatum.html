Erodium bipinnatum

Erodium bipinnatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bedstraw, is a small annual herb native to southern Africa.

It features delicate, hairy stems and trifoliate or pinnate leaves with a distinctive, fern-like appearance. The plant produces small, pinkish to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its seeds are encased in a pappus, which aids in wind dispersal.

Erodium bipinnatum is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and gardens.
