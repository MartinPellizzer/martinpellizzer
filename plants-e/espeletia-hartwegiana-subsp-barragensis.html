Espeletia hartwegiana subsp. barragensis

Espeletia hartwegiana subsp.

barragensis, commonly known as the Barranquilla heather, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of Colombia. It is characterized by its rosette-forming growth habit and dense clusters of small, showy flowers.

The plant typically thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, leathery leaves that are adapted to conserve water.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of alpine ecosystems.
