Echinocereus paucispinus var. hexaedrus

Echinocereus paucispinus var. hexaedrus, commonly known as the Mexican hedgehog cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical stems that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its spiny structure. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for desert environments.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and succulent collections.
