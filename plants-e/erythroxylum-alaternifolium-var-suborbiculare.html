Erythroxylum alaternifolium var. suborbiculare

Erythroxylum alaternifolium var. suborbiculare, commonly known as the narrow-leafed copal tree, is a species native to Central America.

It is characterized by its lanceolate to elliptic leaves that are often slightly hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local communities.
