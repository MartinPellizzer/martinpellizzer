Echites peltatus

Echites peltatus, commonly known as the umbrella plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive umbrella-like arrangement of leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Echites peltatus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
