Ervum hookeri

Ervum hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in rocky habitats. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Ervum hookeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems.
