Euonymus pubescens

Euonymus pubescens, commonly known as the woolly euonymus, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by orange-red berries in the fall. It thrives in partial to full shade and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its ability to tolerate various soil conditions and its role in supporting local wildlife.
