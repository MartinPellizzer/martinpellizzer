Ericameria parryi var. aspera

Ericameria parryi var. aspera, commonly known as Parry's ericoid, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green, glandular leaves and yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments. Its aromatic foliage and resilience to drought make it a hardy ornamental plant.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is closely related to other members of the Ericameria genus.
