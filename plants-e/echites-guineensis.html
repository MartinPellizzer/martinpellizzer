Echites guineensis

Echites guineensis, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a perennial shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves that are usually ovate or elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is known for its milky sap, which can be toxic and is used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.
