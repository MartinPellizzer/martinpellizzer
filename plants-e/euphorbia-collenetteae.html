Euphorbia collenetteae

Euphorbia collenetteae, commonly known as the Collenette's spurge, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a short, thick stem with paired, fleshy leaves that are typically green or reddish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in a structure called a cyathium, which is characteristic of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscape designs.
