Echinocactus taltalensis

Echinocactus taltalensis, commonly known as the Taltal hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the coastal regions of northern Chile.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem and dense clusters of spines, which provide protection from herbivores and reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green, spiny structure. It thrives in arid environments with well-draining soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat threats, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
