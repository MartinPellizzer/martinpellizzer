Engysiphon

Engysiphon, commonly known as the glassy fan-palm, is a unique plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a dense crown of feather-like leaves that give it a delicate, almost glass-like appearance. The plant has a fibrous root system that helps it anchor in coastal and sandy soils. Engysiphon produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by clusters of bright red fruits.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
