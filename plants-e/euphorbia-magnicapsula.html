Euphorbia magnicapsula

Euphorbia magnicapsula, commonly known as the giant-capsule euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves, which are adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces unique, spherical seed capsules that are often brightly colored, aiding in seed dispersal. It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, known for its diverse and often toxic species.

Due to its striking appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
