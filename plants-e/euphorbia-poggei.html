Euphorbia poggei

Euphorbia poggei, commonly known as the Pogge's spurge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, spiny stems and small, inconspicuous yellow flowers. The plant produces a milky latex when damaged, which is a common feature among the Euphorbia genus. It typically grows in rocky or arid environments, adapting to drought conditions with its thick, water-storing stems.

Euphorbia poggei is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
