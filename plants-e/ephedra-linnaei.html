Ephedra linnaei

Ephedra linnaei, commonly known as the smooth ephedra, is a shrub-like plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its jointed, woody stems and small, scale-like leaves that are usually deciduous. The plant produces greenish-yellow flowers and contains alkaloids, including ephedrine, which have been historically used in traditional medicine. Ephedra linnaei thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and drought conditions.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various desert animals.
