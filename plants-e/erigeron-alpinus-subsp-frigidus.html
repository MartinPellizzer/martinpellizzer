Erigeron alpinus subsp. frigidus

Erigeron alpinus subsp.

frigidus, commonly known as the alpine fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces daisy-like flowers with white ray florets and yellow disk florets, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its hardy nature, it is adapted to cold climates and is considered a typical component of high-altitude vegetation.
