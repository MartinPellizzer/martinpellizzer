Eucalyptus baueriana var. conica

Eucalyptus baueriana var. conica, commonly known as the narrow-leaf malleeflower, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often glaucous in appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are typically arranged in clusters, blooming during the spring and summer seasons. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in mallee woodlands.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in arid environments.
