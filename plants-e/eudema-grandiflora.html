Eudema grandiflora

Eudema grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered eudema, is a striking perennial plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to two meters in height. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Eudema grandiflora thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
