Erythroxylum venezuelense

Erythroxylum venezuelense, commonly known as the Venezuelan copal tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds surrounded by a sticky, resinous substance.

It is often found in humid, lowland areas and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
