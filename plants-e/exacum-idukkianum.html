Exacum idukkianum

Exacum idukkianum, commonly known as the Idukki morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant features a trailing growth habit with hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. It blooms during the monsoon season, typically from June to September.

Exacum idukkianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
