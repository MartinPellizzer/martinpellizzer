Echinocereus scheeri subsp. gentryi

Echinocereus scheeri subsp.

gentryi, commonly known as the Gentry's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its robust, cylindrical stems covered in dense clusters of bright green to blue-gray spines.

The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.

Its succulent nature allows it to store water efficiently, making it well adapted to arid environments.
