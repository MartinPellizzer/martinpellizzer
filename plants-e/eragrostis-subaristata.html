Eragrostis subaristata

Eragrostis subaristata, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with silver or blue-green. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which are small and clustered.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
