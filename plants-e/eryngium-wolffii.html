Eryngium wolffii

Eryngium wolffii, commonly known as the blue thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiky, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has silvery-gray, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry environments.

Eryngium wolffii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in garden landscapes.
