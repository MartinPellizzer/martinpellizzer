Erigeron vernus

Erigeron vernus, commonly known as the early blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows and roadsides, thriving in full sun. Its flowering period occurs in early spring, producing delicate blue to purple flowers that attract pollinators.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
