Eucalyptus × oppositifolia

Eucalyptus × oppositifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a hybrid species native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed shrubs with smooth, greyish bark that peels in strips. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and typically have a grey-green hue with a whitish underside. It produces small, globular flower clusters that range in color from white to pale yellow, which attract various pollinators.

This plant is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and woodlands.
