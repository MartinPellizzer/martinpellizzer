Euphorbia arenarioides

Euphorbia arenarioides, commonly known as the sand euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, branching structure and gray-green, fleshy leaves that help it retain water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a unique cyathium, which is typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its resilience and ornamental appeal, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and as a decorative succulent.
