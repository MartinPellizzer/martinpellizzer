Eulophia bilamellata var. euryceras

Eulophia bilamellata var. euryceras, commonly known as the broad-lobed eulophia, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, and its inflorescence consists of multiple clustered flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
