Elephantopus spicatus var. lilacinus

Elephantopus spicatus var. lilacinus, commonly known as the purple elephant's foot, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, hairy, and often purple-tinged leaves that resemble the shape of an elephant's foot. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a distinctive addition to botanical collections.
