Enydra hingcha

Enydra hingcha, commonly known as the African waterwheel, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, waxy leaves that are often blue-green in color. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in a whorled pattern along the stem. It thrives in wet, marshy environments and is often found near water sources.

Enydra hingcha is known for its ability to tolerate both waterlogged and dry conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
