Echeandia drepanoides

Echeandia drepanoides, commonly known as the desert milkweed, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs that help reduce water loss.

Echeandia drepanoides is also known for its milky sap, which is a characteristic feature of the milkweed genus.
