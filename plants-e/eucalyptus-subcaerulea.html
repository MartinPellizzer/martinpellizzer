Eucalyptus subcaerulea

Eucalyptus subcaerulea, commonly known as the blue gum eucalyptus, is a species native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that often displays a striking blue-gray hue. The plant typically grows as a medium to tall tree, reaching heights of up to 30 meters. Its lance-shaped leaves are usually grey-green and have a distinctive aromatic scent.

The flowers are small and white or pale blue, often clustered in groups of three, and are followed by woody capsules containing aromatic seeds.
