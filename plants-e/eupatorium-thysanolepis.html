Eupatorium thysanolepis

Eupatorium thysanolepis, commonly known as the white eupatorium, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its foliage consists of opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Eupatorium thysanolepis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators.
