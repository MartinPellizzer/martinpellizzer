Ericoides tomentosum

Ericoides tomentosum, commonly known as the wooly heath, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and dense, hairy foliage that gives it a woolly appearance. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which bloom in late spring or early summer. Ericoides tomentosum thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathland or moorland habitats.

It is a hardy perennial that is well adapted to cool, moist climates.
