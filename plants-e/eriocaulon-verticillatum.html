Eriocaulon verticillatum

Eriocaulon verticillatum, commonly known as the spiral sedge, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its rosette-like arrangement of leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important component of aquatic ecosystems. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense vegetation.

Eriocaulon verticillatum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
