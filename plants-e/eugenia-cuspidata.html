Eugenia cuspidata

Eugenia cuspidata, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are usually pink or white, which bloom in clusters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a leathery texture with a serrated edge.

The fruit is a capsule that contains small, hard seeds, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
