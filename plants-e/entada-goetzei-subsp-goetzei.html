Entada goetzei subsp. goetzei

Entada goetzei subsp.

goetzei, commonly known as the African honeytree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa. It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and compound leaves that are often used in traditional medicine.

The tree produces striking orange to red flowers that attract various pollinators, including bees and birds. Its fruit is a long, slender pod that splits open to release seeds enclosed in a sticky, fibrous mass.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in many regions.
