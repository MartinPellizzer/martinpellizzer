Eucalyptus moorei

Eucalyptus moorei, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed appearance and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that appear in clusters, typically during the warmer months. Eucalyptus moorei is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry conditions.

It is often used in landscaping due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
