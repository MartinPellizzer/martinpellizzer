Eucalyptus eremicola subsp. eremicola

Eucalyptus eremicola subsp.

eremicola, commonly known as the desert eucalyptus, is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Western Australia. It is characterized by its smooth, often grey or white bark, which peels in thin strips, and its lance-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically grey-green in color.

The flowers are small, white or pale blue, and appear in clusters, followed by woody capsules that contain aromatic, elliptical seeds. This subspecies is adapted to harsh, dry environments and is often found on sandy or rocky soils.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for local fauna.
