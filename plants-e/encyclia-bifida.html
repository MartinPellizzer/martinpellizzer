Encyclia bifida

Encyclia bifida, commonly known as the split-leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, strap-like leaves that are often divided or split, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer or early autumn. Encyclia bifida typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories.

It is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
