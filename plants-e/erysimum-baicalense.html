Erysimum baicalense

Erysimum baicalense, commonly known as the Siberian wallflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a vivid splash of color to its natural habitat. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of down.

Erysimum baicalense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its cheerful blooms.
