Eugenia ulei

Eugenia ulei, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or sweetshrub, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and emit a subtle aromatic scent.

Eugenia ulei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
