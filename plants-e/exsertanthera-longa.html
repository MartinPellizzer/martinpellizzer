Exsertanthera longa

Exsertanthera longa, commonly known as the long-stamen orchid, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated stamens that extend beyond the petals, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually brightly colored, ranging from yellow to orange, and are pollinated by insects and birds.

The botanical features of Exsertanthera longa make it a notable species in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.
