Erysimum crassifolium

Erysimum crassifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces bright yellow or orange flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Erysimum crassifolium is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
