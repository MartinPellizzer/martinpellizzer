Eurya loquaiana

Eurya loquaiana, commonly known as the Chinese holly or red holly, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, elliptical leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Eurya loquaiana thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in temperate regions.
