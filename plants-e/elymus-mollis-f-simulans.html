Elymus mollis f. simulans

Elymus mollis f. simulans, commonly known as the silky wheatgrass, is a variant of the common wheatgrass species.

It is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and disturbed areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and ornamental gardening.
