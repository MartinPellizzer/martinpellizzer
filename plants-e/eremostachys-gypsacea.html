Eremostachys gypsacea

Eremostachys gypsacea, commonly known as the gypsum eryngo, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves adapted to store water, which helps it survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a dense, globular inflorescence, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils, particularly those containing gypsum, and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Eremostachys gypsacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh conditions.
