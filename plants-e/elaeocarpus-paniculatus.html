Elaeocarpus paniculatus

Elaeocarpus paniculatus, commonly known as the white-flowered elaeocarpus, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and alternate, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers, followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain one or two seeds.

It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses in local cultures.
