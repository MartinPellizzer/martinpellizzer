Eragrostis malayana

Eragrostis malayana, commonly known as Malayan lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and marshy areas.

Eragrostis malayana plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
