Eugenia solimoensis

Eugenia solimoensis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or purple passionfruit, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, orange-red fruits that are rich in antioxidants and have a unique, tart flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

Eugenia solimoensis is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
