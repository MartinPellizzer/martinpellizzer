Erasmia

Erasmia, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its unique, intricate flower structures that resemble spiders, hence its common name. Erasmia species are typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, often growing in shaded, moist environments. The plants have fibrous roots and produce small, clustered flowers with a distinctive coloration.

Botanically, they are notable for their specialized pollination mechanisms and ecological adaptations.
