Erigeron Acris Var. Multicaulis

Erigeron acris var. multicaulis is a lesser-known but ecologically significant plant species native to North America. This variant of the common blueweed is distinguished by its multiple stems and dense foliage, making it a unique member of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in open habitats such as meadows and prairies, often in regions with well-drained soils. Despite its modest appearance, it plays a vital role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecosystem balance. Its resilience and adaptability make it an important subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
