Eragrostis cassa

Eragrostis cassa, commonly known as slender bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the United States, particularly in the southeastern region.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in swampy areas and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, bluish-green leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in the summer months. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.

Eragrostis cassa is also used in restoration projects due to its adaptability and ecological benefits.
