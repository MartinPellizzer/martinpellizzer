Eucalyptus occidentalis var. californica

Eucalyptus occidentalis var. californica, commonly known as the California eucalyptus, is a deciduous tree native to coastal regions of California.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The tree produces small, globular flowers that range from white to pink, which are followed by woody capsules containing aromatic seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates with dry summers and wet winters.

This species is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and ability to tolerate urban conditions.
