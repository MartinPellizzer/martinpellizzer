Excoecaria glauca

Excoecaria glauca, commonly known as the white croton, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are typically bright green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, often followed by red or orange berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
