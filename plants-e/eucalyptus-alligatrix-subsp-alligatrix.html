Eucalyptus alligatrix subsp. alligatrix

Eucalyptus alligatrix subsp.

alligatrix, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Australia. It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color.

The plant produces small, globular flower spikes that range in color from white to pale yellow, which are typically borne in clusters. Its bark is usually flaky or fissured, and it is well adapted to dry conditions with a deep root system.

This subspecies is part of the Eucalyptus genus, which is known for its aromatic oils and ecological importance in Australian ecosystems.
