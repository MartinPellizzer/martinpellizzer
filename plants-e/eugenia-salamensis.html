Eugenia salamensis

Eugenia salamensis, commonly known as the Salam Valley myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit, a small, dark purple drupe, is edible and contains a single seed.

Eugenia salamensis is often found in moist, shaded forest understories and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
