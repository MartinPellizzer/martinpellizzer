Epilobium otites

Epilobium otites, commonly known as woolly willow or sea spurge, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb with a sprawling growth habit, often found in sandy or rocky soils near the shore. The plant features lance-shaped leaves covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a woolly appearance. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, typically pink to purple in color, and bloom in the summer months.

Epilobium otites is known for its adaptability to salty and windy coastal environments.
