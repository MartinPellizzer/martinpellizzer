Emilia epapposa

Emilia epapposa, commonly known as the common emilia, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs and produces clusters of small, yellow, daisy-like flowers. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly rough texture. Emilia epapposa thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in disturbed areas and along roadsides.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
