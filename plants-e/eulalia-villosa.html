Eulalia villosa

Eulalia villosa, commonly known as the woolly grass, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its soft, hairy texture on its stems and leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and marshes. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, greenish flowers.

Eulalia villosa is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
