Erlangea microcephala

Erlangea microcephala, commonly known as the dwarf erlangia, is a small, herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, rounded leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of hair. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, often blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and somewhat elusive species in the botanical world.
