Elaeocarpus limitaneus

Elaeocarpus limitaneus, commonly known as the mountain elaeocarpus, is a tropical tree native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and montane forests. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for various animals.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
