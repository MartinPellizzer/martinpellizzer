Erica lowryensis var. glandulifera

Erica lowryensis var. glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular heather, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its profuse display of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in sandy or rocky coastal habitats. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine, glandular hairs, which contribute to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
