Echeveria farinulenta

Echeveria farinulenta, commonly known as the powdery echeveria, is a succulent native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy leaves covered in a fine, powdery coating that gives it its common name. The leaves are typically blue-gray in color and have a velvety texture. It produces vibrant pink or purple flowers in the spring, which bloom on tall, branching stems.

This plant is well-suited for rock gardens and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
