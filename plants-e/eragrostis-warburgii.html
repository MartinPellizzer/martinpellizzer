Eragrostis warburgii

Eragrostis warburgii, commonly known as Warburg's lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Eragrostis warburgii is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
