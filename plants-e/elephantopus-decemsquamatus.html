Elephantopus decemsquamatus

Elephantopus decemsquamatus, commonly known as the many-scaled elephant's foot, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy, tuberous roots that resemble an elephant's foot, which store water and nutrients. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are typically lobed and hairy. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is often found in rocky soils.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
