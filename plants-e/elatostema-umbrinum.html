Elatostema umbrinum

Elatostema umbrinum, commonly known as the shadowy elatostema, is a small herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories or along stream banks. The plant features rosettes of narrow, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in dense inflorescences, which are often hidden among the foliage.

Elatostema umbrinum is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its unique adaptation to retain moisture in humid environments.
