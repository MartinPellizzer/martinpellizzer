Epidendrum davidsei

Epidendrum davidsei, commonly known as the David's Epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its clustered, pendulous inflorescences that bear numerous showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air. Its leaves are usually fleshy and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water storage.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
