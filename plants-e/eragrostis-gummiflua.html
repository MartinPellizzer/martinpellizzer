Eragrostis gummiflua

Eragrostis gummiflua, commonly known as gummed lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Eragrostis gummiflua is known for its ability to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient species in challenging climates.
