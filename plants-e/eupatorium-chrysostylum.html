Eupatorium chrysostylum

Eupatorium chrysostylum, commonly known as golden eupatorium, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, yellowish to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

Eupatorium chrysostylum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
