Eryngium affine

Eryngium affine, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue or purple flower heads surrounded by stiff, gray-green bracts. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, sand dunes, and rocky soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are narrow and slightly lobed, with a rough texture.

Eryngium affine is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its striking appearance and hardy nature.
