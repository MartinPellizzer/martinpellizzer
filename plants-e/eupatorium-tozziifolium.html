Eupatorium tozziifolium

Eupatorium tozziifolium, commonly known as the Florida boneset, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features tall, slender stems that are often branched and covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a rough, textured surface.

It is typically found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist, sunny conditions.
