Erigeron philippii var. humilis

Erigeron philippii var. humilis, commonly known as the Mexican blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators.

The species is noted for its low-growing habit and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
