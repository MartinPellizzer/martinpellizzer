Epidendrum muscicola

Epidendrum muscicola, commonly known as the mossy orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, moss-like growth habit and is often found in shaded, humid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. Its leaves are typically dark green and strap-shaped, and it thrives in well-drained, organic soil.

Epidendrum muscicola is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
