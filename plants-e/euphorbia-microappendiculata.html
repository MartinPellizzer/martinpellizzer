Euphorbia microappendiculata

Euphorbia microappendiculata, commonly known as the miniature appendiculate spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, branching structure and small, spiny leaves that help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces yellowish-green cyathia, which are specialized flower-like structures typical of the Euphorbia genus. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
