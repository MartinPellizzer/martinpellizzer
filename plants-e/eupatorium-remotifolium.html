Eupatorium remotifolium

Eupatorium remotifolium, commonly known as white boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, sunny habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks.

Its name "boneset" refers to its historical use in treating ailments, though it is primarily valued for its ornamental and ecological roles.
