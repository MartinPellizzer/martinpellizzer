Erythrina herbacea var. arborea

Erythrina herbacea var. arborea, commonly known as the red bean tree, is a tropical legume native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its striking red and orange flowers, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. The plant has compound leaves with three leaflets and produces pods containing edible seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can grow as a small tree or shrub.

The species is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.
