Eremochloa leersioides var. gigantea

Eremochloa leersioides var. gigantea, commonly known as the giant false brome, is a tall grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its coarse, upright growth habit and long, narrow leaves that can reach several feet in length. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences, which are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for ornamental or erosion control purposes.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is often considered a weed in agricultural and natural settings.
