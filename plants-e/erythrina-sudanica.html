Erythrina sudanica

Erythrina sudanica, commonly known as the Sudan velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Africa and is characterized by its striking red flowers and compound leaves. The plant produces pods that contain seeds surrounded by a pulpy, edible aril. It is often grown for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display.

Additionally, it plays a role in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing abilities.
