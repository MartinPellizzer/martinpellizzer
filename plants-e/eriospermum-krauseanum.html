Eriospermum krauseanum

Eriospermum krauseanum, commonly known as the Cape honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular, yellowish-orange flowers. The plant produces a distinctive, globular fruit that contains numerous seeds covered in a fleshy, sticky coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Eriospermum krauseanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
