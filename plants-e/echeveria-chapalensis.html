Echeveria chapalensis

Echeveria chapalensis, commonly known as the Chapalensis Sedum, is a succulent native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of fleshy, blue-gray leaves that are covered in a fine, white powdery coating called farina. The plant produces showy, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the spring. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
