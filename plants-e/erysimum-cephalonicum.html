Erysimum cephalonicum

Erysimum cephalonicum, commonly known as the Cephalonic Wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features basal leaves that are linear to lanceolate in shape, often with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.

Erysimum cephalonicum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
