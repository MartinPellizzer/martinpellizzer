Eriophorum × fellowsii

Eriophorum × fellowsii, commonly known as the tussock sedge, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and distinctive tussock-forming growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and bogs, where it thrives in nutrient-poor soils. Its small, spike-like inflorescences contain numerous tiny flowers, which are often wind-pollinated.

Eriophorum × fellowsii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
