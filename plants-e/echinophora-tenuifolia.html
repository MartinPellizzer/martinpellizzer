Echinophora tenuifolia

Echinophora tenuifolia, commonly known as slenderleaf thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb with a hairy, upright stem and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in a spherical inflorescence, which gives it its scientific name. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.

Echinophora tenuifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
