Eucalyptus incrassata var. protrusa

Eucalyptus incrassata var. protrusa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a variant of the eucalyptus species native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, woody fruit capsules. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It is a shrub or small tree that often forms dense thickets, providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its aromatic oils, found in the leaves, contribute to its ecological and potential medicinal significance.
