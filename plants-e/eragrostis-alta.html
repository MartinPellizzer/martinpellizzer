Eragrostis alta

Eragrostis alta, commonly known as tall lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Eragrostis alta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in erosion control due to its extensive root system.
