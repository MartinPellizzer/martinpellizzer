Erica vulgaris var. alba

Erica vulgaris var. alba, commonly known as the white heath, is a flowering plant native to western Europe.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes heaths, heathers, and blueberries. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathland and moorland habitats.

Erica vulgaris var. alba is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
