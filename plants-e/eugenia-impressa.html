Eugenia impressa

Eugenia impressa, commonly known as the broad-leafed myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often marked with distinctive white or pale veins. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by edible, red, berry-like fruits.

Eugenia impressa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
