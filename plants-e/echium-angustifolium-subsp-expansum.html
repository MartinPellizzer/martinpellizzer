Echium angustifolium subsp. expansum

Echium angustifolium subsp.

expansum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle or broadleaf flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of striking blue to violet flowers.

The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough, hairy texture. This subspecies thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grasslands and open habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for pollinators and a subject of ecological study.
