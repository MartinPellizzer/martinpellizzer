Erica longimontana

Erica longimontana, commonly known as the long-stemmed heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heaths and heathers, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and delicate floral structures. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in coastal or mountainous regions. Erica longimontana produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often blooming in late winter to early spring.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
