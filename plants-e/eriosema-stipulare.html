Eriosema stipulare

Eriosema stipulare, commonly known as the stipulate eriosema, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in racemes, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Eriosema stipulare is typically found in grasslands and disturbed soils, thriving in semi-arid conditions.
