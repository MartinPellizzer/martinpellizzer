Ericoides mucosum

Ericoides mucosum, commonly known as the sticky ericoid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves that are often covered in a waxy, sticky substance. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Ericoides mucosum thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands or rocky areas.

Its unique botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and botanical studies.
