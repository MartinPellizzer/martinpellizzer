Eragrostis praetermissa

Eragrostis praetermissa, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny flowers. It is often found in regions with temperate climates and well-drained soils.

Botanically, it is characterized by its rhizomatous growth and ability to form dense stands.
