Eugenia chrysobalanoides

Eugenia chrysobalanoides, commonly known as the golden eugenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and potential medicinal properties.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to tolerate partial shade.
