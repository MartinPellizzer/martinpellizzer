Espostoa lanata subsp. roseiflora

Espostoa lanata subsp.

roseiflora, commonly known as the rose-flowered wooly spurge, is a flowering plant native to South America. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces showy, rose-like flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
