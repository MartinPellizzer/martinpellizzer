Euonymus chinensis var. tonkinensis

Euonymus chinensis var. tonkinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese spurge or tonkin spurge, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by orange-red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for ornamental use in gardens.

Its unique foliage and seasonal color changes make it a notable plant in horticultural collections.
