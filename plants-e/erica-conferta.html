Erica conferta

Erica conferta, commonly known as the heath or Erica, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late summer to early autumn. Erica conferta thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in gardens and as a ground cover.
