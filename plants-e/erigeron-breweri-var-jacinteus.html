Erigeron breweri var. jacinteus

Erigeron breweri var. jacinteus, commonly known as the jacinteus blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sandy habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are usually hairy and linear, while the stems are erect and often branched.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is of interest in ecological restoration projects.
