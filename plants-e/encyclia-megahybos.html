Encyclia megahybos

Encyclia megahybos, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence that can reach up to 2 meters in height, making it one of the tallest orchids in the world. The plant features broad, strap-like leaves and produces numerous fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Encyclia megahybos is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a subject of interest in botanical research due to its unique morphology and ecological significance.
