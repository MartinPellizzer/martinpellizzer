Echinocactus linkii var. spinosior

Echinocactus linkii var. spinosior, commonly known as the Mexican torch cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stem that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are borne in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its spiny exterior provides protection against herbivores and helps reduce water loss through transpiration.

This cactus is well adapted to hot, dry climates and requires minimal water once established.
