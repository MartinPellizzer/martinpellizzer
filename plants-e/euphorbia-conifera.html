Euphorbia conifera

Euphorbia conifera, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its branched, woody stems and vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in winter. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are surrounded by colorful bracts.

Euphorbia conifera is a popular ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and seasonal blooming habit.
