Euphorbia poecilophylla

Euphorbia poecilophylla, commonly known as the variable euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that vary in shape and size, often appearing spiny or lobed. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its stems to survive dry conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
