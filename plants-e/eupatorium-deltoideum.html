Eupatorium deltoideum

Eupatorium deltoideum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, blue to purple flower clusters that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Eupatorium deltoideum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to support local pollinators.
