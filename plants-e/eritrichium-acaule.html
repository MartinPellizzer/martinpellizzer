Eritrichium acaule

Eritrichium acaule, commonly known as the naked-stem mouse-eared cress, is a low-growing annual herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features a rosette of basal leaves that are hairy and lobed, with a distinctive blue or purple coloration. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Eritrichium acaule is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
