Eucalyptus suberea

Eucalyptus suberea, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to southern Australia, where it typically grows in dry, sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed form and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Eucalyptus suberea produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
