Eragrostis lehmanniana var. chaunantha

Eragrostis lehmanniana var. chaunantha, commonly known as Lehmann's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

This variety is often used in landscaping and forage due to its hardiness and ornamental appeal.
