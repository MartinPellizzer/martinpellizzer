Eriocaulon nasuense

Eriocaulon nasuense, commonly known as the Nasuense Umbrella Plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, rosette-like growth habit and small, narrow leaves that form a tight, umbrella-shaped structure. The plant produces tiny, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typically emerging from the center of the rosette. It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly in Brazil, and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its unique appearance and compact growth, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in aquatic and bog gardens.
