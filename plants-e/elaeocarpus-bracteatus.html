Elaeocarpus bracteatus

Elaeocarpus bracteatus, commonly known as the bracteate elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically encased in a fleshy, woody pulp. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal properties.

The species is also noted for its role in supporting local biodiversity through its fruit, which attracts various birds and mammals.
