Erica canescens

Erica canescens, commonly known as the silver heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the western Cape of South Africa.

It features dense, wiry stems covered in silvery-gray hairs, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in coastal and mountainous regions.

Erica canescens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
