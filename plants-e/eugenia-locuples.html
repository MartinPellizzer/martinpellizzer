Eugenia locuples

Eugenia locuples, commonly known as the purple-leafed eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often have a reddish or purple tint on the underside. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red, berry-like fruits.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
