Erythroxylum exaltatum var. riedelianum

Erythroxylum exaltatum var. riedelianum, commonly known as the Riedel's copal tree, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish-white in color. Its fruit is a capsule that contains copal, a resinous substance used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.

The plant has significant ecological and economic value in its native habitats.
