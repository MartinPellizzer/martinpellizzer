Eriosema flemingioides var. rhodesica

Eriosema flemingioides var. rhodesica, commonly known as Rhodesian eriosema, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with hairy surfaces that give the plant its species name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
