Erioxylum palmeri

Erioxylum palmeri, commonly known as the desert hibiscus, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along streambanks. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Erioxylum palmeri is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.
