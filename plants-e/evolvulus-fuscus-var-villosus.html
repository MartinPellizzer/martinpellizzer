Evolvulus fuscus var. villosus

Evolvulus fuscus var. villosus, commonly known as the hairy blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems, which give it its varietal name "villosus.". The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in the early morning. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in humid, warm climates.
