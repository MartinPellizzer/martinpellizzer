Eupatorium blepharilepis

Eupatorium blepharilepis, commonly known as the narrowleaf thoroughwort, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Eupatorium blepharilepis is often found in the understory of forests and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
