Eugenia ferruginea

Eugenia ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty eucalyptus, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its reddish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, rusty-colored pubescence. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

Eugenia ferruginea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
