Eschscholzia crocea var. longissima

Eschscholzia crocea var. longissima, commonly known as the long-stemmed California poppy, is a variant of the iconic California poppy characterized by its elongated, slender stems.

This cultivar typically features vibrant orange to red flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is native to California and thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes. The plant is herbaceous, growing to a height of 1 to 3 feet, and is known for its resilience and low maintenance requirements.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and ecologically beneficial addition to native plant gardens.
