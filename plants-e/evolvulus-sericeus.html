Evolvulus sericeus

Evolvulus sericeus, commonly known as silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, pale blue to lavender-colored flowers. The leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

The plant is part of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with similar climbing or trailing habits.
