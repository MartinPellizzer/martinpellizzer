Euphorbia gerardiana

Euphorbia gerardiana, commonly known as the Gerard's spurge, is a perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, milky sap that can cause skin irritation. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing a low, compact growth habit. Its flowers are small and clustered in a structure called a cyathium, which is surrounded by colorful bracts.

Euphorbia gerardiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in arid environments.
