Ernstamra macrophylla

Ernstamra macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf ernstamra, is a species native to the temperate regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by dark, spherical berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This ornamental plant is valued for its striking foliage and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
