Eriosema laurentii

Eriosema laurentii, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with a distinctive orange center, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Eriosema laurentii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
