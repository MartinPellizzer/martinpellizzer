Echeveria perezcalixii

Echeveria perezcalixii, commonly known as the Mexican rose, is a succulent native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy, bluish-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder. The plant produces striking pink to red flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires plenty of sunlight.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
