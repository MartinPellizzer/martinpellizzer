Eugenia catharinae

Eugenia catharinae, commonly known as the Catania guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and red, fleshy berries that are edible. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in forest understories.

The leaves are typically elliptical and glossy, with a strong aromatic scent when crushed.
