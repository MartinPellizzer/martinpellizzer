Eragrostis fastigiata

Eragrostis fastigiata, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, upright growth habit and fine, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed sites.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
