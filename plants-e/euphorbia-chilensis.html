Euphorbia chilensis

Euphorbia chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean spurge, is a perennial shrub native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, characterized by its milky sap and unique floral structures. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing a compact, branched form with smooth, waxy leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in cyathia, which are modified inflorescences typical of the genus.

Euphorbia chilensis is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
