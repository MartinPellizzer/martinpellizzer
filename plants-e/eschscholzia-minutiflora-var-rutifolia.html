Eschscholzia minutiflora var. rutifolia

Eschscholzia minutiflora var. rutifolia, commonly known as the California poppy, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red flowers and delicate, fern-like leaves. The plant is an annual herb that thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its small, showy flowers bloom in the spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
