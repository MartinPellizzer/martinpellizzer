Eremophila scrobiculata

Eremophila scrobiculata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved emu bush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and spikes of pink to red flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for rocky or sandy soils. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.

Eremophila scrobiculata is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardiness.
