Eria paniculata

Eria paniculata, commonly known as the orchid vine, is a tropical plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and the production of small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant has long, slender stems that can twine around supports, and its leaves are usually lance-shaped with a smooth surface. Eria paniculata thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in rainforests or along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and horticultural displays.
