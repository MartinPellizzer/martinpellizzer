Eupatorium tanacetifolium

Eupatorium tanacetifolium, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
