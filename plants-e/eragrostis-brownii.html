Eragrostis brownii

Eragrostis brownii, commonly known as Brown's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates, showing drought tolerance.

Its ecological role includes providing forage for livestock and supporting local wildlife.
