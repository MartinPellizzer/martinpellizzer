Eustoma russellianum f. roseum

Eustoma russellianum f. roseum, commonly known as the roseum blue mistflower, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, semi-double to fully double blue or lavender-pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like foliage and is often grown as an ornamental for its delicate appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Eustoma russellianum f. roseum is valued in gardens for its long-lasting blooms and ability to attract pollinators.
