Eriodictyon glutinosum var. angustifolium

Eriodictyon glutinosum var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrowleaf yerba santa, is a shrub native to California and parts of Nevada.

It features aromatic, glandular leaves that are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its bark and leaves have been used traditionally by indigenous peoples for their medicinal properties.

It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.
