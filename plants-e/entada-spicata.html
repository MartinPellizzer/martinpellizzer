Entada spicata

Entada spicata, commonly known as the African spurred tortoise, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces long, cylindrical pods that contain flat, bean-like seeds. It is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system for water storage.

The plant is also valued for its hard, dense wood, which is used in traditional craftsmanship.
