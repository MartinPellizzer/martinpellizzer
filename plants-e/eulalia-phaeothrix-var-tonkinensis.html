Eulalia phaeothrix var. tonkinensis

Eulalia phaeothrix var. tonkinensis, commonly known as the Tonkin silvergrass, is a grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowering spikes are slender and often appear in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and its ability to tolerate a range of environmental conditions.
