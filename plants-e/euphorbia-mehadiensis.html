Euphorbia mehadiensis

Euphorbia mehadiensis, commonly known as the Mehadi spurge, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of North Africa.

It features a short, thick stem with spiny leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by specialized structures called cyathia. It is adapted to thrive in hot, dry climates with minimal rainfall.

Euphorbia mehadiensis is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and unique appearance.
