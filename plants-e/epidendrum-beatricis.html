Epidendrum beatricis

Epidendrum beatricis, commonly known as the Beatrice's Epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. The plant features long, strap-like leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. Its flowers are usually borne on tall, branching inflorescences, making it a striking addition to botanical gardens.

Epidendrum beatricis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
