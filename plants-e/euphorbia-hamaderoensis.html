Euphorbia hamaderoensis

Euphorbia hamaderoensis, commonly known as the Hamadero euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to dry climates.

Due to its unique appearance and resilience, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
