Epidendrum pseudepidendrum var. auratum

Epidendrum pseudepidendrum var. auratum, commonly known as the golden epidendrum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is a sympodial orchid with a short, thick rhizome and produces pseudobulbs that support its growth. The plant features showy, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in terminal inflorescences, often with a contrasting greenish or reddish lip. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
