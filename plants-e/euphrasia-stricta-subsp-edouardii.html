Euphrasia stricta subsp. edouardii

Euphrasia stricta subsp.

edouardii, commonly known as the narrowleaf eyebright, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are clustered in whorls along the stem and bloom in early spring.

The species is noted for its medicinal uses, particularly in traditional herbal remedies for eye and respiratory conditions.
