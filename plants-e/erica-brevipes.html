Erica brevipes

Erica brevipes, commonly known as the dwarf heath, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It typically grows to a height of about 10-30 cm, featuring dense, compact foliage composed of small, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or red, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Erica brevipes is well-suited for rock gardens and coastal areas due to its drought tolerance and hardy nature.
