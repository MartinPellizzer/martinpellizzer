Entada nana var. pubescens

Entada nana var. pubescens, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark brown bark and glossy, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers followed by long, slender pods containing flat, edible seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rainforest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
