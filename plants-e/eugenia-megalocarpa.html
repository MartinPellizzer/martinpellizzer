Eugenia megalocarpa

Eugenia megalocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited clove, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. The plant produces large, fleshy berries that are typically red when ripe, containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for horticultural purposes.
