Erythroxylum laurinum

Erythroxylum laurinum, commonly known as the Mexican mallow or red mallow, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces clusters of small, pink to reddish flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds.

Erythroxylum laurinum is part of the Erythroxylaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
