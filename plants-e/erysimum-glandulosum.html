Erysimum glandulosum

Erysimum glandulosum, commonly known as the woolly wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, glandular hairs that cover its stems and leaves, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, which are typically arranged in racemes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Erysimum glandulosum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
