Echinochloa subcordata

Echinochloa subcordata, commonly known as slender barnyardgrass, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, linear leaves with a prominent midrib. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous pale green to brownish flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural fields.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a challenging species to control in crop production.
