Euphorbia seibanica

Euphorbia seibanica, commonly known as the Japanese spurge, is a succulent plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are often clustered in a cup-like structure called a cyathium. It is adapted to grow in rocky or dry environments, showcasing its resilience.

Due to its toxic sap, it requires careful handling during cultivation.
