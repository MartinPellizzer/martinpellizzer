Eucalyptus camaldulensis subsp. refulgens

Eucalyptus camaldulensis subsp.

refulgens, commonly known as the River Red Gum, is a tall, deciduous tree native to Australia. It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that reveals a mottled reddish-brown color.

The leaves are typically narrow, lance-shaped, and have a bluish-green hue. It produces small, spherical flower clusters that range in color from white to pink.

This subspecies is well adapted to riparian zones and can tolerate periodic flooding.
