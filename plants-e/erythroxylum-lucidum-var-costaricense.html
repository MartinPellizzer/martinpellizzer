Erythroxylum lucidum var. costaricense

Erythroxylum lucidum var. costaricense, commonly known as the Costa Rican brightleaf or Costaricense copal, is a shrub or small tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy dark green leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by orange-red berries. It is often found in humid, lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses.

The species is also noted for its potential ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
