Elaeis virescens

Elaeis virescens, commonly known as the African oil palm, is a flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces clusters of fruits that contain a valuable oil-rich pulp and kernel. It is widely cultivated for its economic importance in the production of palm oil.

However, its cultivation has raised concerns regarding environmental impact and biodiversity loss.
