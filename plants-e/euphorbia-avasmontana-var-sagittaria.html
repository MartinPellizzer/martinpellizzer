Euphorbia avasmontana var. sagittaria

Euphorbia avasmontana var. sagittaria, commonly known as the arrowleaf euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and spiny stems, which are adaptations to its harsh, dry environment. The plant produces small, yellowish cyathia, which are the modified flower clusters typical of the Euphorbia genus. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and collections.
