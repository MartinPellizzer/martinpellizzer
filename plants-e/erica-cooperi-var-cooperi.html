Erica cooperi var. cooperi

Erica cooperi var. cooperi, commonly known as the Coastal Heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern California.

It features dense clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late winter to early spring. The plant has needle-like leaves that are gray-green in color, providing a textured appearance. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and full sun, making it well-suited for coastal climates.

Erica cooperi var. cooperi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate salt spray and strong coastal winds.
