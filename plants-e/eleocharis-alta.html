Eleocharis alta

Eleocharis alta, commonly known as the tall spike-rush, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescence composed of numerous small, spike-like clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its roots help in stabilizing soil and improving water quality in aquatic ecosystems.

Eleocharis alta is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in diverse wetland conditions.
