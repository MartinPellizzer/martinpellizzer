Enterolobium glaziovii

Enterolobium glaziovii, commonly known as the Brazilian pigeon pea, is a tropical leguminous tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural systems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in reforestation and as a forage crop.

The flowers are typically yellow or orange, adding ornamental value to the plant.
