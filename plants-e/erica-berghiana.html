Erica berghiana

Erica berghiana, commonly known as the Alpine heather, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other heath and heather species. This plant is characterized by its dense, low-growing shrub form and produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white. Erica berghiana thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in high-altitude ecosystems.
