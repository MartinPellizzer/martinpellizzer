Eriogonum flavum var. xanthum

Eriogonum flavum var. xanthum, commonly known as the yellow globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems, which are typical features of the genus Eriogonum. The plant produces small, greenish to yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
