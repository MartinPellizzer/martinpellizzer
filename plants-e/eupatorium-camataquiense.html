Eupatorium camataquiense

Eupatorium camataquiense, commonly known as the Camataquiense thoroughwort, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowering period occurs during the wet season.

Eupatorium camataquiense is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
