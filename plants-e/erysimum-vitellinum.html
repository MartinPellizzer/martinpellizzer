Erysimum vitellinum

Erysimum vitellinum, commonly known as yellow wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, often in clusters at the ends of its branched stems. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Erysimum vitellinum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
