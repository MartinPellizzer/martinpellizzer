Euphorbia paredonensis

Euphorbia paredonensis, commonly known as the Paredonensis spurges, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in a structure called a cyathium. It is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its stems to survive in dry conditions.

Euphorbia paredonensis is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
