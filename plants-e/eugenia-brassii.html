Eugenia brassii

Eugenia brassii, commonly known as the golden eucalypt, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the wet tropical regions of northern Australia, particularly in Queensland. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a distinctive aromatic scent.

Eugenia brassii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine by Indigenous communities.
