Eucalyptus kochii

Eucalyptus kochii, commonly known as the mountain gum, is a species of eucalyptus native to eastern Australia.

It is a medium-sized tree that typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a blue-green hue with a whitish underside. Its flowers are small and clustered in spherical inflorescences, usually pink or pale red in color.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aromatic leaves and timber.
