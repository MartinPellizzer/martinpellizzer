Erigeron annuus f. discoideus

Erigeron annuus f. discoideus, commonly known as the diskflower fleabane, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is a herbaceous annual with a distinctive composite flower head that features a central disk surrounded by ray florets. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and disturbed soils. Its leaves are usually alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

The flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
