Echinocereus yavapaiensis

Echinocereus yavapaiensis, commonly known as the Yavapai hedgehog cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in spines and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments, making it a hardy species. It is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.

Its structure and growth patterns make it well-suited for survival in harsh, dry climates.
