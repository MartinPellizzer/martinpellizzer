Euphrasia nemorosa subsp. pectinata

Euphrasia nemorosa subsp.

pectinata, commonly known as the hairy eyebright, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish-green flowers.

The plant produces numerous tiny flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its common name. Euphrasia nemorosa subsp.

pectinata is often found in moist meadows and along stream banks, where it thrives in nutrient-rich soils.
