Erythropalum scandens

Erythropalum scandens, commonly known as the climbing tamarind, is a woody vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds, which are used in traditional cuisines. Its climbing habit and adaptability make it a common sight in tropical forests and gardens.
