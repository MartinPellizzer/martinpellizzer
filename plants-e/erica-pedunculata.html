Erica pedunculata

Erica pedunculata, commonly known as the heather or dwarf heather, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and is characterized by its dense, low-growing shrub form. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. Erica pedunculata thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and moorlands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in challenging environments.
