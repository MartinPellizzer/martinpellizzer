Euphorbia platyphyllos var. pubescens

Euphorbia platyphyllos var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are the modified flower structures typical of the Euphorbia genus. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
