Eriogonum junceum

Eriogonum junceum, commonly known as sulphurflower buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is characterized by its yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert environments. It has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and its stems are often reddish or purplish in color.

Eriogonum junceum is a hardy perennial that plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
