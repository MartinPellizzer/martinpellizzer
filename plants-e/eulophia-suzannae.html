Eulophia suzannae

Eulophia suzannae, commonly known as the Suzannae orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces spike-like inflorescences bearing showy, fragrant flowers with three sepals and three petals. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color.

Eulophia suzannae thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
