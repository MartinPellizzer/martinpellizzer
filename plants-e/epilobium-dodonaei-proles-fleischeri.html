Epilobium dodonaei proles fleischeri

Epilobium dodonaei proles fleischeri, commonly known as the Fleischer's willow herb, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Epilobium.

It is native to certain regions of Europe and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and showy pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, often calcareous soils, and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and wetlands. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
