Erica calycina var. fragrans

Erica calycina var. fragrans, commonly known as the Sweet Heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring, producing white or pale pink blooms that emit a sweet, honey-like scent. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and fragrance, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in floral arrangements.
