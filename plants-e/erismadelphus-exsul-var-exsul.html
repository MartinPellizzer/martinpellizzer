Erismadelphus exsul var. exsul

Erismadelphus exsul var. exsul, commonly known as the slender eristophyllum, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and linear, adapted to reduce water loss in coastal environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing dune systems.
