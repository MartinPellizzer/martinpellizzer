Eugenia fajardensis

Eugenia fajardensis, commonly known as the Fajardo's evergreen myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Caribbean, specifically found in Puerto Rico and the U.S. Virgin Islands. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and leathery, elliptical leaves. It typically grows in moist, shaded areas within tropical forests.

Eugenia fajardensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural settings.
