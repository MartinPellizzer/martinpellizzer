Eragrostis chaetophylla var. pauciflora

Eragrostis chaetophylla var. pauciflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered weeping grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its drooping, slender inflorescences and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in grassland ecosystems. It produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers, which are usually pale green to brown in color.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
