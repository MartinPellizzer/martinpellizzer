Eremophila cordatisepala

Eremophila cordatisepala, commonly known as the broad-leaf emu bush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for dry landscapes.

It is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like birds and insects.
