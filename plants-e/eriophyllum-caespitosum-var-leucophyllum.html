Eriophyllum caespitosum var. leucophyllum

Eriophyllum caespitosum var. leucophyllum, commonly known as silverleaf woolly sunflower, is a flowering plant native to California and parts of Oregon.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and coastal bluffs. It has small, narrow leaves covered in fine, white hairs that give it a silvery appearance.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
