Erica ixanthera

Erica ixanthera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and rhododendrons. The plant is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

Erica ixanthera is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
