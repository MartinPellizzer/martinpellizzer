Elegia verticillaris

Elegia verticillaris, commonly known as the spiral water plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its spiral arrangement of leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its small, white flowers bloom in clusters and are often pollinated by insects.

Elegia verticillaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in moist conditions.
