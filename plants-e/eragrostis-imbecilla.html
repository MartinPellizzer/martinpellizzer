Eragrostis imbecilla

Eragrostis imbecilla, commonly known as the dwarf lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and sandy soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its low-growing habit and tolerance to drought and poor soil conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
