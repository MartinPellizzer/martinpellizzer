Elymus junceus var. desertorum

Elymus junceus var. desertorum, commonly known as desert wild rye, is a perennial grass native to the arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is well adapted to harsh environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and the ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
