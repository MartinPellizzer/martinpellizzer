Euonymus japonicus var. argenteovariegatus

Euonymus japonicus var. argenteovariegatus, commonly known as the silver-veined Japanese spurge, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features striking foliage with white to pale yellow veins contrasting against its dark green leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is relatively low maintenance.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
