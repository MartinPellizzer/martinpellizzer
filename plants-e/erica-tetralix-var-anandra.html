Erica tetralix var. anandra

Erica tetralix var. anandra, commonly known as the Scottish heath, is a flowering plant native to the British Isles.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, low-growing shrub form. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathland and moorland habitats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local wildlife.
