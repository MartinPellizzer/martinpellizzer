Eriocaulon philippo-coburgii

Eriocaulon philippo-coburgii, commonly known as the Coburg spike-rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, spike-like inflorescence composed of small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, preferring moist, acidic soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, with a dense covering of fine hairs.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines.
