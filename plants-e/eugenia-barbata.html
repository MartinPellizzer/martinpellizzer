Eugenia barbata

Eugenia barbata, commonly known as the hairy waxleaf, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine, short hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, edible berries.

Eugenia barbata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and potential medicinal properties.
