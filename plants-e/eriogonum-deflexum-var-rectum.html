Eriogonum deflexum var. rectum

Eriogonum deflexum var. rectum, commonly known as the bentleaf buckwheat, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that are often folded or rolled. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pink, which bloom in late spring to early summer. This species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil in disturbed areas.
