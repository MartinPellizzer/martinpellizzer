Erythroxylum glaziovii

Erythroxylum glaziovii, commonly known as the Glaziov's coca, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil, Bolivia, and Peru.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into capsule fruits containing elliptical seeds.

It is notable for its historical and cultural significance in traditional medicine and as a relative of the coca plant.
