Eupatorium panamense

Eupatorium panamense, commonly known as Panama boneset, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are usually opposite, lance-shaped, and have a rough texture.

Eupatorium panamense is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
