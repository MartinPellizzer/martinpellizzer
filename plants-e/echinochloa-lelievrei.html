Echinochloa lelievrei

Echinochloa lelievrei, commonly known as the African barnyard grass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its robust, erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny grains, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers moist, well-drained conditions.

Echinochloa lelievrei is often found in disturbed areas, fields, and along roadsides, making it a common weed in agricultural settings.
