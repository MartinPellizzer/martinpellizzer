Edwardia ficifolia

Edwardia ficifolia, commonly known as the laceleaf tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are deeply lobed and often have a silvery or grayish underside. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Edwardia ficifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage.
