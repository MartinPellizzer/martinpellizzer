Eupatorium ivifolium var. pilcomayense

Eupatorium ivifolium var. pilcomayense, commonly known as the Pilcomayo eupatorium, is a perennial herb native to the South American grasslands.

It features slender, erect stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. It is typically found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies in its native range.
