Epilobium palustre var. alpicola

Epilobium palustre var. alpicola, commonly known as alpine willow herb, is a flowering plant native to alpine and subalpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its erect stems and lanceolate leaves with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces showy pink to purple flowers in terminal clusters, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Epilobium palustre var. alpicola is adapted to cold climates and can tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
