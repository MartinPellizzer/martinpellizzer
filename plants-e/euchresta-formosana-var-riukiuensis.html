Euchresta formosana var. riukiuensis

Euchresta formosana var. riukiuensis, commonly known as the Ryukyu euphorbia, is a flowering plant native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, spiny stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal environments, adapting to the humid and subtropical climate of its native habitat. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its arid coastal surroundings.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance and resilience.
