Erysimum alpinum

Erysimum alpinum, commonly known as the alpine wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Erysimum alpinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant yellow or orange flowers.
