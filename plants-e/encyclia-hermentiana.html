Encyclia hermentiana

Encyclia hermentiana, commonly known as the Hermentian orchid, is a species native to Central America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features a pseudobulb with a single leaf and produces intricate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and green. Its floral structure is characteristic of the Epidendroideae subfamily, with a labellum that is often adorned with intricate patterns.

Encyclia hermentiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
