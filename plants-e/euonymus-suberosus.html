Euonymus suberosus

Euonymus suberosus, commonly known as the roughleaf euonymus, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its rough, textured leaves that are typically oval to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in the spring, followed by orange-red berries that are attractive to birds. Its bark is thick and fissured, giving it a rugged appearance.

Euonymus suberosus is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
