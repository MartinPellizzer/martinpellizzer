Eriocoelum pendulum

Eriocoelum pendulum, commonly known as the hanging lantern plant, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pendulous inflorescences, which consist of small, clustered flowers that hang downward. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, and they emit a faint fragrance.

Eriocoelum pendulum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded gardens.
