Euphorbia altissima

Euphorbia altissima, commonly known as the tall spurge, is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem that can reach heights of up to 6 feet. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers surrounded by modified leaves called cyathia. Its latex sap is toxic and can cause skin irritation.

Euphorbia altissima thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy plant in USDA hardiness zones 6 through 9.
