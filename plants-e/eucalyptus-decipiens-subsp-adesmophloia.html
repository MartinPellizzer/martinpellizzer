Eucalyptus decipiens subsp. adesmophloia

Eucalyptus decipiens subsp.

adesmophloia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mallees, is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white, felt-like hairs.

The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically pale green to yellow in color. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry conditions.

This subspecies is part of the Eucalyptus genus, which is known for its aromatic oils and ecological importance in Australian ecosystems.
