Euphorbia acerensis

Euphorbia acerensis, commonly known as the red-leaf euphorbia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that are often red or purple in color, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish cyathia, which are the modified inflorescences typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
