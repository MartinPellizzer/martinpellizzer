Equisetum ramosissimum var. lohseanum

Equisetum ramosissimum var. lohseanum, commonly known as the Lohse's horsetail, is a perennial plant belonging to the Equisetaceae family.

It is characterized by its jointed, hollow stems that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, spore-bearing cones at the base of the stems, which are used for reproduction. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as wetlands and stream banks.

Its ancient lineage dates back to the Paleozoic era, making it one of the oldest living plant groups.
