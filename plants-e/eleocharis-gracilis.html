Eleocharis gracilis

Eleocharis gracilis, commonly known as slender spike-rush, is a species of sedge found in wetland environments.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common sight in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Eleocharis gracilis plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
