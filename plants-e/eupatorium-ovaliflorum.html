Eupatorium ovaliflorum

Eupatorium ovaliflorum, commonly known as narrow-leaved boneset, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. It produces fluffy, seed-filled flower heads that are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Eupatorium ovaliflorum is often used in native plant gardens for its ecological benefits and ornamental value.
