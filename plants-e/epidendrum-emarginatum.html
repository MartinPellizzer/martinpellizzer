Epidendrum emarginatum

Epidendrum emarginatum, commonly known as the Margined Epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically has strap-like leaves that are dark green and glossy. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Epidendrum emarginatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a home setting.
