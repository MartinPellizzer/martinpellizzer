Epilobium yabei

Epilobium yabei, commonly known as the Japanese willow herb, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, often shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its showy, pink to purple flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Epilobium yabei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
