Erica bocquetii

Erica bocquetii, commonly known as the Cape snowflake, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the heath family, Ericaceae, and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form. The plant produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in winter, adding a striking visual contrast to its dark green foliage. Erica bocquetii thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated in rock gardens and as a container plant due to its ornamental value and hardy nature.
