Eupatorium pellium

Eupatorium pellium, commonly known as common boneset, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and stream banks, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. Its flowering period occurs from late summer to early autumn, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
