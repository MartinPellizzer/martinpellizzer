Eupatorium hyssopifolium var. tortifolium

Eupatorium hyssopifolium var. tortifolium, commonly known as the creeping eupatorium, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features dark green, ovate to elliptic leaves with toothed margins, and it produces small, white to pale purple flowers in clusters during the summer months. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

It is valued in native gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
