Eragrostis capillacea

Eragrostis capillacea, commonly known as slender fingergrass, is a flowering plant species belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to Africa and is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its slender, erect stems and delicate appearance make it a distinctive member of the genus Eragrostis.

It is often used in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in soil stabilization.
