Excoecaria marginata var. stenophylla

Excoecaria marginata var. stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves that are often variegated with yellow and green patterns. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
