Eremophila tetraptera

Eremophila tetraptera, commonly known as the Narrow-leaved Emu Bush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces spikes of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in spring. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry climates.

Eremophila tetraptera is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
