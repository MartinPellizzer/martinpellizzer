Entada durissima

Entada durissima, commonly known as the "African ironwood," is a large, slow-growing tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, heavy wood that is highly resistant to decay and insect damage. The plant has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a long, pod-like structure containing several hard, angular seeds.

The tree is valued for its durable wood and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
