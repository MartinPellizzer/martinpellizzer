Epidendrum tandapioides

Epidendrum tandapioides, commonly known as the Tandapioides orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of pink to purple blooms, which are often borne on long, slender peduncles. Its roots are adapted for epiphytic growth, allowing it to thrive on trees in humid, shaded environments.

Epidendrum tandapioides is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
