Engelmannia texana

Engelmannia texana, commonly known as Texas sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a rough texture.

Engelmannia texana thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in sandy or rocky habitats.
