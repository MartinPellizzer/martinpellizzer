Encelia frutescens var. actoni

Encelia frutescens var. actoni, commonly known as the coastal goldenbush, is a shrub native to coastal regions of California and Baja California.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-green foliage and showy yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping. It plays an important role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for native wildlife.

Its unique morphology and ecological significance make it a valuable component of coastal ecosystems.
