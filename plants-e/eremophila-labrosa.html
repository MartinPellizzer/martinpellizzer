Eremophila labrosa

Eremophila labrosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf emu bush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and showy pink to red flowers. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and heat resistance. It typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, and produces small, aromatic fruits.

Eremophila labrosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for native pollinators.
