Epilobium consimile var. japonicum

Epilobium consimile var. japonicum, commonly known as Japanese willow herb, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant features slender, erect stems covered with fine hairs and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are pink to red, arranged in terminal clusters, and bloom in early summer.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
