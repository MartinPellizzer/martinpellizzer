Elaeocarpus domatifer

Elaeocarpus domatifer, commonly known as the hairy elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey-brown bark and elliptical leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that are covered in soft, hairy structures.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and the tree is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological importance in its native habitat.
