Echinochloa crus-galli var. elata

Echinochloa crus-galli var. elata, commonly known as yellow foxtail, is a grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and spikelets that form a distinctive, foxtail-like shape. The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish spikelets that are often clustered at the tips of the branches. It thrives in disturbed soils and is commonly found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Yellow foxtail is an annual plant that can become invasive in certain environments.
