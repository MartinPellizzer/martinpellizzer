Erythroxylum elegans

Erythroxylum elegans, commonly known as the elegant erythroxylum, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly in regions of Brazil and Peru.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its attractive, showy flowers that range from white to pink in color. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with alternate, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a potential source of natural compounds and its ecological importance in its native habitats.
