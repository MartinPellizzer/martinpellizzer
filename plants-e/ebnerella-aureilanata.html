Ebnerella aureilanata

Ebnerella aureilanata, commonly known as the golden lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers and long, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water's surface. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats, often found in ponds and marshes. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ebnerella aureilanata is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a role in maintaining aquatic ecosystem balance.
