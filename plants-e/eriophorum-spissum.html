Eriophorum spissum

Eriophorum spissum, commonly known as cotton grass, is a flowering plant native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, fluffy seed heads that resemble cotton, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in wet, acidic habitats such as bogs, marshes, and tundra. Its flowering stems are usually tall and slender, often reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

Eriophorum spissum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
