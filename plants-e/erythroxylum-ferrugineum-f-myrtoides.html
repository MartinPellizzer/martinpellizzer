Erythroxylum ferrugineum f. myrtoides

Erythroxylum ferrugineum f. myrtoides, commonly known as the rusty erythroxylum, is a variant of the erythroxylum genus native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a reddish-brown pubescence, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or reddish in color. Its fruits are capsules that contain numerous tiny seeds.

This subspecies is often found in moist, shaded environments within the Andean regions.
