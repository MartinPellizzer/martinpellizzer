Erianthus elephantinus

Erianthus elephantinus, commonly known as the elephant grass, is a tall, perennial grass native to Africa.

It can grow up to 6 meters in height, featuring broad, blade-like leaves with a fibrous texture. The plant produces panicles of small, spike-like flowers, typically in shades of brown or beige. It thrives in wet, tropical environments and is often found in swampy or marshy areas.

Its robust growth makes it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
