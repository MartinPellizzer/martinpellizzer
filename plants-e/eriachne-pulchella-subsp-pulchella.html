Eriachne pulchella subsp. pulchella

Eriachne pulchella subsp.

pulchella, commonly known as the purple false brome, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing pale blue to purple flowers, which are clustered in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
