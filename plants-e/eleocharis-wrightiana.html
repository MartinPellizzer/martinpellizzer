Eleocharis wrightiana

It is a grass-like plant characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and small, clustered spikelets. Native to wetland habitats, it thrives in marshy areas and along the edges of ponds and lakes. The plant produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers, which are typically green or brown in color.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
