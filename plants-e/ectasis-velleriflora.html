Ectasis velleriflora

Ectasis velleriflora, commonly known as the silky flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
