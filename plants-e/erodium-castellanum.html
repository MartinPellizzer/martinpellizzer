Erodium castellanum

Erodium castellanum, commonly known as common stork's bill, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, pink to purple flowers that bloom from spring to early summer. The plant has ovate to lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and its fruit is a distinctive, club-shaped capsule that resembles a stork's bill. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Erodium castellanum is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through seeds dispersed by wind and animals.
