Eugenia stenophylla var. angustifolia

Eugenia stenophylla var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved satinash, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are glossy and dark green on top and lighter in color underneath. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round berries that are edible. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
