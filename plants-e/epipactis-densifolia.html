Epipactis densifolia

Epipactis densifolia, commonly known as the dense-leaved helleborine, is a perennial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and its showy, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers have a distinctive lip that is often adorned with intricate patterns.

Epipactis densifolia is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and is often found in association with other shade-tolerant flora.
