Elymus longearistatus subsp. flexuosissimus

Elymus longearistatus subsp.

flexuosissimus, commonly known as the flexible needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its adaptability and ecological importance, it plays a significant role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
