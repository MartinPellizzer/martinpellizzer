Eremurus cristatus

Eremurus cristatus, commonly known as the "Crested Spurge," is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky flower heads that resemble a crown, which give it its common name. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Eremurus cristatus is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in temperate climates.
