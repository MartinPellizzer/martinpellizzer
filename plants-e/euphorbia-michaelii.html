Euphorbia michaelii

Euphorbia michaelii, commonly known as the Michael's spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems that are often green or reddish in color. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its drought tolerance and unique appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
