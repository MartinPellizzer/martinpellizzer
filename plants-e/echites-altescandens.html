Echites altescandens

Echites altescandens, commonly known as the giant rat's tail, is a striking plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems that can grow up to 20 feet in length, often cascading from supports. The plant produces small, white, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.
