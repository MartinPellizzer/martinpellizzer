Erythraea capitata

Erythraea capitata, commonly known as the Cape mountain heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils, often found in mountainous regions. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or red in color, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, adapting to the dry conditions of its habitat.

Erythraea capitata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
