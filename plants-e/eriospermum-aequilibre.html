Eriospermum aequilibre

Eriospermum aequilibre, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant has hairy, silver-gray leaves that help reduce water loss in arid environments. Its flowers are typically yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Eriospermum aequilibre is adapted to thrive in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in desert scrub habitats.
