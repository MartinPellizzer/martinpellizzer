Erica scoparia subsp. azorica

Erica scoparia subsp.

azorica, commonly known as Azorean heather, is a shrub native to the Azores, Portugal, and Madeira. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in horticulture for its hardiness and ability to form dense ground cover. Erica scoparia subsp.

azorica is also notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soils in its native environment.
