Exacum caeruleum

Exacum caeruleum, commonly known as blue-eyed Susan, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of small, blue or violet flowers with prominent yellow centers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Exacum caeruleum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
