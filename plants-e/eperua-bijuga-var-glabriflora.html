Eperua bijuga var. glabriflora

Eperua bijuga var. glabriflora, commonly known as the glabrous-flowered eperua, is a species native to the rainforests of northern Australia and northern New Guinea.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth texture. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in greenish-white or pale yellow hues, and it produces woody pods containing small, flat seeds.

This species is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
