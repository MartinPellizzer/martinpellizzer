Erigeron pattersonii

Erigeron pattersonii, commonly known as Patterson's fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has hairy, gray-green leaves. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
