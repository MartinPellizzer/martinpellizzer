Erythropsis migeodii

Erythropsis migeodii, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky soils and dry environments, showcasing adaptations to extreme heat and drought. The plant produces showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Erythropsis migeodii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in desert ecosystems.
