Erica tetralix var. assoana

Erica tetralix var. assoana, commonly known as the coastal heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the western coast of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in sandy, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or reddish in color, blooming in late summer to early autumn. It is well adapted to coastal conditions, tolerating salt spray and strong winds.

Erica tetralix var. assoana plays an important ecological role in stabilizing dune systems and providing habitat for various coastal wildlife.
