Erica haemantha

Erica haemantha, commonly known as the red heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in coastal and mountainous regions. Erica haemantha is known for its vibrant pink to red flowers that bloom in spring and summer.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
