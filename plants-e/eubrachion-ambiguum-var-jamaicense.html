Eubrachion ambiguum var. jamaicense

Eubrachion ambiguum var. jamaicense, commonly known as the Jamaican spider orchid, is a rare and unique orchid species native to Jamaica.

It is characterized by its intricate, spider-like appearance, with elongated, slender flowers that resemble the legs of a spider. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments within the tropical rainforests of Jamaica. Its flowers are usually a mix of white and green, with a subtle fragrance that attracts specific pollinators.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
