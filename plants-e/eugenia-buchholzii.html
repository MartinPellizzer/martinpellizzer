Eugenia buchholzii

Eugenia buchholzii, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Eugenia buchholzii*, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. After flowering, it produces small, red, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to cherries.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
