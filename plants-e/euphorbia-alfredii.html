Euphorbia alfredii

Euphorbia alfredii, commonly known as the Madagascar ghost plant, is a succulent shrub native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its unique, fleshy, spiny stems and leaves that are often reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces a distinctive yellowish-green sap that is toxic and can cause severe skin irritation. It thrives in arid and rocky environments, showcasing its adaptation to harsh, dry conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered an endangered species.
