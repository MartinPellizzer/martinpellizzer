Erysimum kotschyanum

Erysimum kotschyanum, commonly known as the Persian winter cress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and pinnately divided leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to cold climates. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants.

Erysimum kotschyanum is often found in alpine environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
