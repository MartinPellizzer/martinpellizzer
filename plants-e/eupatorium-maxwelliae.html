Eupatorium maxwelliae

Eupatorium maxwelliae, commonly known as Maxwell's boneset, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in moist, wooded habitats and is often found in swampy or wetland areas.

Its common name reflects its historical use in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
