Euphorbia altaica var. pilosa

Euphorbia altaica var. pilosa, commonly known as the Mongolian spurge, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters called cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are usually simple and alternate, with a rough texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
