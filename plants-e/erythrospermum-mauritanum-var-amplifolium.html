Erythrospermum mauritanum var. amplifolium

Erythrospermum mauritanum var. amplifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf erythrospermum, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of showy, red or pink flowers that bloom during the warmer months. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with succulent leaves that store water.

This variant is distinguished from the typical species by its broader foliage and robust growth habit.
