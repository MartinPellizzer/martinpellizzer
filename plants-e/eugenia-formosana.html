Eugenia formosana

Eugenia formosana, commonly known as the Taiwan guava, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Eugenia formosana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
