Elymus attenuatus

Elymus attenuatus, commonly known as slender wild rye, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including disturbed soils and prairies.

Elymus attenuatus plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for wildlife.
