Eryngium elegans var. macrocephalum

Eryngium elegans var. macrocephalum, commonly known as the giant thistle or purple coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features striking, spiky, purple-blue flower heads that are surrounded by silver-gray bracts, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers that develop into hardened, woody fruits known as achenes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical characteristics include robust stems, hairy foliage, and a unique, architectural flower structure.
