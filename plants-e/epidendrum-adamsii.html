Epidendrum adamsii

Epidendrum adamsii, commonly known as the Adams' orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, producing multiple pseudobulbs along its stem. The plant features lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are typically arranged in racemes, with showy, brightly colored blooms that range from yellow to orange and red.

Epidendrum adamsii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in greenhouses.
