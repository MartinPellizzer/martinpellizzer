Elephantopus pratensis

Elephantopus pratensis, commonly known as the common elephant's foot, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, preferring moist, well-drained soils. It features basal rosettes of leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

Its common name is derived from the shape of its swollen, fleshy stem, which resembles an elephant's foot.
