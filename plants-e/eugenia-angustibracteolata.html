Eugenia angustibracteolata

Eugenia angustibracteolata, commonly known as the narrow-bract eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the rainforests of northern Queensland, Australia, and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are followed by edible, dark purple to black berries. Eugenia angustibracteolata is often found in shaded, moist environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.

Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with multiple stems and dense foliage.
