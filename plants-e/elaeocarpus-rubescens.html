Elaeocarpus rubescens

Elaeocarpus rubescens, commonly known as the red-fruited elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Its fruit is an important food source for various bird species.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
