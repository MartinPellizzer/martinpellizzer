Eucalyptus calophylla var. parviflora

Eucalyptus calophylla var. parviflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaf malleeflower, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers that appear in clusters. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils. It plays an important ecological role in providing habitat and food for local wildlife.

Its aromatic oils have also been used in traditional and modern applications.
