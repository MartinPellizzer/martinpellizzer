Eriolaena pseudopopulus

Eriolaena pseudopopulus, commonly known as the false poplar, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptical in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, often displaying white or pale yellow hues.

The tree produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed, making it an important species in local ecosystems.
