Euphrasia officinalis subsp. pratensis

Euphrasia officinalis subsp.

pratensis, commonly known as the lesser eyebright, is a small herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant has slender stems that bear opposite, oval-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces. Its flowers are yellow with dark markings, often arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

It is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies for eye conditions.
