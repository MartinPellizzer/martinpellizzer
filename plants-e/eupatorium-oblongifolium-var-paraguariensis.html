Eupatorium oblongifolium var. paraguariensis

Eupatorium oblongifolium var. paraguariensis, commonly known as Paraguayan feverfew, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is typically found in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and forest edges.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
