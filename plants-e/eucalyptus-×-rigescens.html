Eucalyptus × rigescens

Eucalyptus × rigescens, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallee, is a hybrid species within the Myrtaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, multi-stemmed growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in umbels, typically in late winter to early spring. Its bark is often flaky and varies in color from grey to brown, and it is commonly found in sandy soils across southern Australia.

This species is valued for its ecological role and is also used in horticulture for its ornamental features.
