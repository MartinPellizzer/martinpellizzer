Eria xanthocheila

Eria xanthocheila, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

Eria xanthocheila is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
