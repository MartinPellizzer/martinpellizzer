Erythrostaphyle vitiginea

Erythrostaphyle vitiginea, commonly known as the red-stemmed beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, violet-blue berries that appear in the fall. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often tinged with red on the underside. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

The berries are not only visually striking but also attract birds and other wildlife.
