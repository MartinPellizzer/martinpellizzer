Elasis hirsuta

Elasis hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy elasis, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its hairy stems and leaves give it a distinctive appearance, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds.

Elasis hirsuta is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
