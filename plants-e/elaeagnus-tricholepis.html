Elaeagnus tricholepis

Elaeagnus tricholepis, commonly known as the silverleaf elaeagnus, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves and small, yellowish flowers that appear in the spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive foliage.

Elaeagnus tricholepis is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
