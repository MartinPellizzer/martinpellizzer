Erigeron glaucus

Erigeron glaucus, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and sandy soils. Its leaves are grayish-green and hairy, while its flowers are small and blue or white in color.

It is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
