Erica pharetriformis

Erica pharetriformis, commonly known as the Cape heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in sunny, coastal environments. Erica pharetriformis is known for its resilience to drought and its ability to form dense shrubs, making it a valuable plant in horticulture and ecological restoration.

Its flowering period usually occurs in late summer, adding ornamental value to gardens and natural landscapes.
