Eugenia stictosepala var. melanogyna

Eugenia stictosepala var. melanogyna, commonly known as the black-sepaled eugenia, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree, typically reaching heights of up to 5 meters. The plant features elliptical, glossy dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and are clustered in inflorescences, while the fruit is a small, dark blue or black berry.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
