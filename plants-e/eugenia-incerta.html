Eugenia incerta

Eugenia incerta, commonly known as the small-leaved guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to yellow when mature.

Eugenia incerta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
