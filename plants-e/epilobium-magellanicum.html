Epilobium magellanicum

Epilobium magellanicum, commonly known as the Magellan's willow herb, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, temperate environments such as wetlands and riverbanks. The plant features slender, erect stems covered with fine hairs and lance-shaped leaves. Its showy flowers are usually pink or red, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

It plays an important ecological role by providing food and habitat for various insects and birds.
