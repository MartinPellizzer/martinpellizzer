Erythrina suberosa var. sublobata

Erythrina suberosa var. sublobata, commonly known as the red flame acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins and bright red, flame-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, flat pods that contain several seeds, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
