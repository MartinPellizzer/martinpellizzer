Eupatorium selleanum

Eupatorium selleanum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, woodland habitats. The plant features showy, blue to purple flower clusters that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

Eupatorium selleanum is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
