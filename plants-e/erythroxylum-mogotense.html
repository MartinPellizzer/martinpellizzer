Erythroxylum mogotense

Erythroxylum mogotense, commonly known as the Guatemalan chili tree, is a species native to Central America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns red when mature, containing a single seed.

The plant is part of the Erythroxylaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses.
