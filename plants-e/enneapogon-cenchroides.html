Enneapogon cenchroides

Enneapogon cenchroides, commonly known as the red grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with pale to reddish flowers. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, and it thrives in dry, sandy soils.

Enneapogon cenchroides is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
