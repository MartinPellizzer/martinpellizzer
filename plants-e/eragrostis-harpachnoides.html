Eragrostis harpachnoides

Eragrostis harpachnoides, commonly known as the harpachnoid lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-gray sheen. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is sometimes used in restoration projects and as a forage plant for livestock.
