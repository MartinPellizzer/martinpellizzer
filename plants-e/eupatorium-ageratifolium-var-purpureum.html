Eupatorium ageratifolium var. purpureum

Eupatorium ageratifolium var. purpureum, commonly known as purple boneset, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, pale purple to white flowers that bloom in the late summer and early autumn. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture, often with a grayish-green hue. This plant thrives in moist, sunny habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
