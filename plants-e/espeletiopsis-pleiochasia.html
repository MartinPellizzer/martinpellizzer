Espeletiopsis pleiochasia

Espeletiopsis pleiochasia, commonly known as the Andean giant fern, is a striking member of the Marattiaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant exhibits a unique growth habit, with a thick, woody rhizome supporting its massive foliage. Native to the cloud forests of the Andes, it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a relict species, offering insights into ancient tropical ecosystems.
