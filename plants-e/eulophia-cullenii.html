Eulophia cullenii

Eulophia cullenii, commonly known as the Cullen's eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist forest understories, where it thrives in humid and cool conditions. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with a short rhizome and several fleshy, elliptic leaves. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellow, with a distinctive spurred lip that aids in pollination.

Eulophia cullenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral structure.
