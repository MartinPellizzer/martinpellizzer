Entada claessensii

Entada claessensii, commonly known as the African hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and alternate, simple leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by distinctive, long, curved pods that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The tree is valued for its hard, heavy wood and is also used in traditional medicine.
