Epidendrum barbaricum

Epidendrum barbaricum, commonly known as the Barbadine orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a sympodial orchid with a rhizomatous growth habit, producing multiple stems that can reach up to 2 meters in length. The plant features lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged in two rows along the stem. Its flowers are typically clustered in inflorescences, with showy, brightly colored blooms that range from yellow to orange and red.

Epidendrum barbaricum is known for its adaptability and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
