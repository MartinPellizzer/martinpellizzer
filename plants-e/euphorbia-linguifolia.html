Euphorbia linguifolia

Euphorbia linguifolia, commonly known as the tongue-shaped spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture, and it produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. The plant is known for its milky sap, which can be irritating to the skin. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular ornamental plant.
