Echinochloa crus-galli f. rohlenae

Echinochloa crus-galli f. rohlenae, commonly known as the rohlenae form of cocklebur grass, is a variant of the common cocklebur grass, characterized by its distinct morphological features.

It is a grassy weed that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as agricultural fields and along roadsides. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny seeds. It is often found in temperate regions and is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a competitive weed and its potential use in ecological studies.
