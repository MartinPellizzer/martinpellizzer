Ehrharta virgata

Ehrharta virgata, commonly known as slender lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and fine, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in panicles, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Ehrharta virgata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
