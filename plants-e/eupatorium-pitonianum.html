Eupatorium pitonianum

Eupatorium pitonianum, commonly known as the Pitot's boneset, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.
