Erica longistyla

Erica longistyla, commonly known as the long-styled heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica longistyla typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in sunny, open habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
