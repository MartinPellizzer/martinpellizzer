Euphorbia waterbergensis

Euphorbia waterbergensis, commonly known as the Waterberg Spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a unique cyathium structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is often found in rocky outcrops and mountainous regions, where it adapts to poor soil conditions.

Due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
