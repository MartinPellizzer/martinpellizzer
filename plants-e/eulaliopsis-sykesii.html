Eulaliopsis sykesii

Eulaliopsis sykesii, commonly known as the silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray blades and tufted inflorescence that resembles a plume. This plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping and low-maintenance gardens. It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.

Eulaliopsis sykesii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and adaptability.
