Eulophia bicallosa

Eulophia bicallosa, commonly known as the two-spined eulophia, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, often pseudobulbous structure and striking, often fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. The plant produces elongated, branched inflorescences bearing numerous small to medium-sized flowers. Its leaves are usually broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in arid environments.

Eulophia bicallosa thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
