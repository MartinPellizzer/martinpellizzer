Equisetum sylvaticum f. gracile

Equisetum sylvaticum f. gracile, commonly known as the slender field horsetail, is a fern-like plant belonging to the Equisetaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, jointed stems that are covered in small, scale-like structures. The plant produces spore-bearing cones at the base of its stems, which are typically found near the soil surface. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its ancient lineage dates back to the Carboniferous period, making it a living fossil with unique botanical features.
