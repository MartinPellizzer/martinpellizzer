Eugenia hedraiophylla

Eugenia hedraiophylla, commonly known as the Australian holly or mountain holly, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to eastern Australia, where it typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and understoreys. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruits are small, red berries that are edible and often consumed by birds.

Eugenia hedraiophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
