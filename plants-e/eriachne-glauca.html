Eriachne glauca

Eriachne glauca, commonly known as bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-green blades and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas. Its leaves are narrow and have a distinctive blue-green hue, which contributes to its ornamental appeal.

Eriachne glauca is also valued for its role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in arid environments.
