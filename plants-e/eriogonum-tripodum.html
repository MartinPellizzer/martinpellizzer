Eriogonum tripodum

Eriogonum tripodum, commonly known as the three-toed mat flower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant produces clusters of small, showy yellow or white flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Eriogonum tripodum is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a hardy member of the buckwheat family.
