Eupatorium alternifolium var. oppositifolium

Eupatorium alternifolium var. oppositifolium, commonly known as the opposite-leaved boneset, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It is characterized by its opposite arrangement of lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and swamps. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its aromatic, slightly hairy foliage.

Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
