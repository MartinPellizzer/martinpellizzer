Erica curvifolia

Erica curvifolia, commonly known as the zigzag heath, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to South Africa, where it typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form and its clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica curvifolia thrives in full sun to partial shade and is well adapted to coastal and mountainous environments.

Its unique, zigzagging stems and fragrant blooms make it a distinctive and ornamental plant in horticulture.
