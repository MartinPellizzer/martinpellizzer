Euphorbia kaokoensis

Euphorbia kaokoensis, commonly known as the Kaoko euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to Namibia and Angola.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and spiny leaves. The plant produces a unique, capsule-like fruit that contains toxic, sticky seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its water-storing tissues.

Due to its striking appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
