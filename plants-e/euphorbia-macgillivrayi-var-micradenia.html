Euphorbia macgillivrayi var. micradenia

Euphorbia macgillivrayi var. micradenia, commonly known as the narrowleaf spurge, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a short, thick stem with small, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are characteristic of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique morphology and adaptability to harsh environments make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
