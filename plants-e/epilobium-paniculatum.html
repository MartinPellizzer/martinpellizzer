Epilobium paniculatum

Epilobium paniculatum, commonly known as the purple willow-herb, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist environments such as wet meadows, marshes, and stream banks. The plant features upright stems covered in sticky hairs and produces showy clusters of pink to purple flowers in the summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a waxy coating.

Epilobium paniculatum is part of the Onagraceae family, which includes other notable species like the evening primrose.
