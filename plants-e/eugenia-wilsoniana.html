Eugenia wilsoniana

Eugenia wilsoniana, commonly known as Wilson's guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pinkish flowers. Its fruit, which is typically small and sour, is often used in local cuisines.

Eugenia wilsoniana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
