Echites torosus

Echites torosus, commonly known as the Torosus thistle, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowering stems are often branched and can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

Echites torosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
