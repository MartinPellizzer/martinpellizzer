Eriogonum luteolum var. pedunculatum

Eriogonum luteolum var. pedunculatum, commonly known as the yellow sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and hairy, branched stems. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. It has small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is part of the buckwheat family (Polygonaceae) and is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in disturbed habitats.
