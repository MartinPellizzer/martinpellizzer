Elaeagnus × ebbingei

Elaeagnus × ebbingei, commonly known as silverberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring, followed by edible berries that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Elaeagnus × ebbingei is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil improvement.
