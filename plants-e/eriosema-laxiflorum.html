Eriosema laxiflorum

Eriosema laxiflorum, commonly known as the woolly eriosema, is a South African plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It has a creeping or ascending stem and produces small, pod-like fruits.

Eriosema laxiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
