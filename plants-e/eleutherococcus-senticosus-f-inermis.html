Eleutherococcus senticosus f. inermis

Eleutherococcus senticosus f. inermis, commonly known as the Siberian ginseng or the golden root, is a variant of the Eleutherococcus species native to the forests of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellowish-brown bark and clusters of small, dark red berries. The plant is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, while its stems are woody and branched.

This plant is valued for its adaptogenic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for centuries.
