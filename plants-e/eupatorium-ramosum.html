Eupatorium ramosum

Eupatorium ramosum, commonly known as roughleaf boneset, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are rough to the touch and have a distinctive grayish-green color, while its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences.

Eupatorium ramosum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
