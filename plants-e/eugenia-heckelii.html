Eugenia heckelii

Eugenia heckelii, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a tropical shrub native to Central America and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and vibrant purple to red berries that appear in the fall. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and can reach heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, while the flowers are usually white or pale pink.

The berries are edible but have a mild, slightly sweet flavor, making them a popular choice for ornamental and culinary use.
