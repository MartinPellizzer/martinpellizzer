Elaeagnus mayeharai

Elaeagnus mayeharai, commonly known as the Mayehara silverberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its silvery-hairy leaves and small, edible berries that ripen in the fall. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats. It produces fragrant, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, which are important for pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
