Eriophorum minus

Eriophorum minus, commonly known as lesser cottongrass, is a low-growing herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, fluffy seed heads that resemble cotton, which give it its common name. The plant typically thrives in wet, acidic environments such as bogs and marshes. Its narrow, grass-like leaves are usually green to reddish in color, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Eriophorum minus plays a vital role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various small organisms.
