Epidendrum callobotrys

Epidendrum callobotrys, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil.

Epidendrum callobotrys is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
