Erophila verna var. rubrinaeva

Erophila verna var. rubrinaeva, commonly known as the red-flowered false flax, is a variant of the annual herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking early-season color.
