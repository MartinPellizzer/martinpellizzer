Eriogonum ovalifolium var. ovalifolium

Eriogonum ovalifolium var. ovalifolium, commonly known as the ovalleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of oval-shaped, hairy leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and open habitats. It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other members like sorrel and knotweed.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
