Eleocharis ochreata

Eleocharis ochreata, commonly known as the yellow spike-rush, is a species of sedge native to wetland environments in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescence that bears small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important component of aquatic ecosystems. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous structure that aids in its survival in waterlogged conditions.

Eleocharis ochreata plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
