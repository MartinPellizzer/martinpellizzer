Excoecaria serrata

Excoecaria serrata, commonly known as the crocodile tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that have a distinctive serrated edge. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in coastal areas.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular ornamental plant.
