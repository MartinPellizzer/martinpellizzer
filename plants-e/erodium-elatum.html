Erodium elatum

Erodium elatum, commonly known as velvetleaf, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, pink to purple flowers and heart-shaped leaves covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant grows up to 30 cm tall and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and it is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Fabaceae, also known as the legume family.
