Eriocaulon alleizettei

Eriocaulon alleizettei, commonly known as the narrow-leaf marsh grass, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, hair-like structures. This species typically thrives in wetland or marshy environments, where it forms dense mats near the water's surface. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in spikelets, and are adapted for wind pollination.

Eriocaulon alleizettei plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic and semi-aquatic organisms.
