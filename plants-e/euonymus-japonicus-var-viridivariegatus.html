Euonymus japonicus var. viridivariegatus

Euonymus japonicus var. viridivariegatus, commonly known as the Japanese spindle tree or variegated euonymus, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features small, elliptical leaves with a distinctive variegation pattern of green and white, enhancing its ornamental value. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that attract birds. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and seasonal color changes make it a popular choice for ornamental planting.
