Eugenia sebastiani

Eugenia sebastiani, commonly known as the Sebastian's clove or the Malabar clove, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including India, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, clove-like flowers and small, elliptical leaves. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
