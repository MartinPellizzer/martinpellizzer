Elymus gmelinii subsp. tenuisetus

Elymus gmelinii subsp.

tenuisetus, commonly known as the slenderleaf wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and grasslands. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown florets.

This subspecies is valued for its resilience and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
