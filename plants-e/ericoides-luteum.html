Ericoides luteum

Ericoides luteum, commonly known as the yellow heath, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is typically found in acidic, nutrient-poor soils, often in heathland or moorland environments. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Ericoides luteum is also noted for its ability to thrive in harsh conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
