Eupatorium viscosum

Eupatorium viscosum, commonly known as common boneset, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Its sticky leaves and medicinal properties have made it a notable species in traditional herbal medicine.
