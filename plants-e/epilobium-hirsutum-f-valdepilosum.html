Epilobium hirsutum f. valdepilosum

Epilobium hirsutum f. valdepilosum, commonly known as the hairy willow herb, is a flowering plant belonging to the evening primrose family (Onagraceae).

It is a herbaceous perennial characterized by its erect, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers in terminal clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges, streambanks, and disturbed sites.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native range.
