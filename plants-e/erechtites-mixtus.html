Erechtites mixtus

Erechtites mixtus, commonly known as the common beggar-tick, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, alternate leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces tiny, wind-dispersed seeds enclosed in a pappus, aiding in its spread. It thrives in moist to wet habitats, such as stream banks and wetlands.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
