Epilobium pyrricholophum var. japonicum

Epilobium pyrricholophum var. japonicum, commonly known as Japanese willowherb, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a hairy surface and produces clusters of pink to purple flowers in the summer. Its flowers are actinomorphic, with five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Onagraceae family.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant in gardens.
