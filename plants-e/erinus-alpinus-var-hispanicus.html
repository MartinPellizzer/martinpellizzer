Erinus alpinus var. hispanicus

Erinus alpinus var. hispanicus, commonly known as the Alpine Erinus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the carnation family (Caryophyllaceae) and is characterized by its delicate, hairy stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing shades of pink, white, and purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a valuable addition to both garden and landscape design.
