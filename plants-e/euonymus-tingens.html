Euonymus tingens

Euonymus tingens, commonly known as the red-leaf euonymus, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking foliage, which turns vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in the spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible for birds. Its leaves are typically oval with a smooth texture and a glossy appearance.

Euonymus tingens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
