Erythrina wieringae

Erythrina wieringae, commonly known as the Wieringa's flame tree, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red, flame-like flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has compound leaves with three leaflets and produces elongated, woody pods that contain several seeds. Its growth habit includes a tall, often branched stem, and it thrives in well-drained soils with ample sunlight.

Erythrina wieringae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its ecological benefits in gardens and landscapes.
