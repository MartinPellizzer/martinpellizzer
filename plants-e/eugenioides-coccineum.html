Eugenioides coccineum

Eugenioides coccineum, commonly known as the red-flowered eugenia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of striking red flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant produces small, oval-shaped berries that are typically red or orange when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections for its vibrant floral display.
