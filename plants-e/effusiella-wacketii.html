Effusiella wacketii

Effusiella wacketii, commonly known as the Wacket's effuse fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching fronds that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are clustered on the underside of the fronds, a common feature in ferns.

Due to its limited habitat and vulnerability to environmental changes, Effusiella wacketii is considered a species of conservation concern.
