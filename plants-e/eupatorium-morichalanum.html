Eupatorium morichalanum

Eupatorium morichalanum, commonly known as the Indian thoroughwort, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, pale blue or white flowers. Its botanical characteristics include a robust root system and a tall, branched stem.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
