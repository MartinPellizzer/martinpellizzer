Epidendrum tessellatum

Epidendrum tessellatum, commonly known as the mosaic orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It features elongated, narrow leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces striking, pendulous inflorescences with brightly colored, lance-shaped flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. Its flowers are typically fragrant and bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

Epidendrum tessellatum thrives in humid, well-lit environments and requires good air circulation for optimal growth.
