Echinocactus cylindraceus

Echinocactus cylindraceus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species of cactus native to Central America.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and dense, spiny exterior, which provides protection from herbivores and excessive sunlight. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green body. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and indoor cultivation.
