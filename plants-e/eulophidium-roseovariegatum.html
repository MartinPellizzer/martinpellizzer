Eulophidium roseovariegatum

Eulophidium roseovariegatum, commonly known as the variegated rose moss, is a member of the family Dendroceratidae and is characterized by its unique, branching structure.

It is a type of liverwort, specifically a thallose liverwort, which means it has a flat, leaf-like structure rather than true leaves. The plant exhibits a striking coloration with pink and green variegation, which is particularly noticeable in its reproductive structures. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
