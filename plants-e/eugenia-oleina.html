Eugenia oleina

Eugenia oleina, commonly known as the white myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers and small, leathery leaves that have a distinctive aromatic scent. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. Eugenia oleina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens in warm climates.
