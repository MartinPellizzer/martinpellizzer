Eriothrix lycopodioides

Eriothrix lycopodioides, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a small, evergreen plant that belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, spore-bearing cones rather than flowers, which is a key feature of its reproductive biology. Its foliage consists of tiny, scale-like leaves that give it a unique, moss-like appearance.

Eriothrix lycopodioides is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in soil stabilization.
