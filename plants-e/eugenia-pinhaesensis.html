Eugenia pinhaesensis

Eugenia pinhaesensis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pinhaesia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of eastern Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and clusters of fragrant white or pink flowers. Its fruit, a small, red drupe, is edible and often used in local cuisine.

Eugenia pinhaesensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food for various wildlife species.
