Eriogonum scabrellum

Eriogonum scabrellum, commonly known as the roughleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green, rough-textured leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and open woodlands. It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, which includes other plants like sorrel and knotweed.

Eriogonum scabrellum is adapted to arid conditions and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
