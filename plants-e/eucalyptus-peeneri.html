Eucalyptus peeneri

Eucalyptus peeneri, commonly known as the Peener's mallee, is a small to medium-sized tree native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, globular flower buds that open to reveal white or cream-colored flowers. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought conditions.

Eucalyptus peeneri plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
