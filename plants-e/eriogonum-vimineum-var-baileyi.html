Eriogonum vimineum var. baileyi

Eriogonum vimineum var. baileyi, commonly known as Bailey's goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its upright growth habit and slender, often hairy stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers clustered in dense, branched inflorescences, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and often have a reddish tinge, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in chaparral and coastal sage scrub ecosystems.
