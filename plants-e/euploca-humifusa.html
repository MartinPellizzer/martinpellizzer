Euploca humifusa

Euploca humifusa, commonly known as the groundsel or false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are typically oval or lance-shaped, with a rough texture and a slightly hairy surface.

Euploca humifusa is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and along stream banks.
