Eragrostis capitula

Eragrostis capitula, commonly known as the tufted lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and small, spikelet-based inflorescence. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Eragrostis capitula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and restoration projects.
