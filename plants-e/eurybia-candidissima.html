Eurybia candidissima

Eurybia candidissima, commonly known as the white wood aster, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and features showy white to pale yellow flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Eurybia candidissima is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
