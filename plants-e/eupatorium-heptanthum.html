Eupatorium heptanthum

Eupatorium heptanthum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, blue to purple flower heads that appear in late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.

Eupatorium heptanthum is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
