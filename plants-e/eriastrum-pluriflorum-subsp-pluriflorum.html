Eriastrum pluriflorum subsp. pluriflorum

Eriastrum pluriflorum subsp.

pluriflorum, commonly known as the narrowleaf eriocephalus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of small tubular flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and sagebrush habitats. It produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the plant is generally low-growing with a mat-like structure.
