Eriocaulon pumilio

Eriocaulon pumilio, commonly known as the dwarf spike-rush, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often in marshy or swampy habitats. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves that are densely packed around a central stem. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in spike-like clusters at the top of the plant.

Eriocaulon pumilio is known for its ability to thrive in humid environments and is sometimes cultivated as a ornamental plant in water gardens.
