Enterolobium guaraniticum

Enterolobium guaraniticum, commonly known as the Guarani bean tree, is a tropical leguminous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves and produces distinctive, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine. Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil enrichment.

The tree thrives in warm, humid climates and is adaptable to various soil types.
