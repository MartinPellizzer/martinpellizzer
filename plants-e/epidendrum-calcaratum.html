Epidendrum calcaratum

Epidendrum calcaratum, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink, typically blooming during the rainy season. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found growing on rock surfaces or in rocky crevices.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a popular species among orchid enthusiasts and botanists.
