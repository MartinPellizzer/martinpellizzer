Eriocaulon cookei

Eriocaulon cookei, commonly known as the Cook's bulbous rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Eriocaulaceae, which is characterized by its grass-like appearance and unique floral structures. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy habitats, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, white or pale flowers.
