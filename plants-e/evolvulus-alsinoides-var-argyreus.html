Evolvulus alsinoides var. argyreus

Evolvulus alsinoides var. argyreus, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine layer of silvery-white hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in warm, sunny conditions and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

The species is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and runners.
