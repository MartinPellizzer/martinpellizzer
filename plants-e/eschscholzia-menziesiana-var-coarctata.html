Eschscholzia menziesiana var. coarctata

Eschscholzia menziesiana var. coarctata, commonly known as the coastal gold poppy, is a perennial plant native to the western coast of North America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow to orange flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant thrives in coastal environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. It is adapted to tolerate salt spray and moderate coastal winds.

This variety is often found in sand dunes and rocky coastal areas, contributing to the unique biodiversity of these ecosystems.
