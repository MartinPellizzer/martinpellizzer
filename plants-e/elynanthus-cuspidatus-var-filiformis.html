Elynanthus cuspidatus var. filiformis

Elynanthus cuspidatus var. filiformis, commonly known as the slender milkweed, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. Its milky sap, typical of the Apocynaceae family, is a distinguishing feature.

This variant is often found in disturbed soils and open areas, showcasing its adaptability to different environments.
