Erysimum thomsonii

Erysimum thomsonii, commonly known as Thomson's wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant that typically grows in dry, rocky soils. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with four petals, which bloom in early spring. It has linear, grass-like leaves and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Thomson's wallflower is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
