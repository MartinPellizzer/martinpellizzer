Eupatorium hylophilum

Eupatorium hylophilum, commonly known as sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant produces fluffy, seed-bearing structures that aid in wind pollination. Its leaves are typically opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along streambanks.
