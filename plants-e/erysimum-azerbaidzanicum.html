Erysimum azerbaidzanicum

Erysimum azerbaidzanicum, commonly known as the Azeri wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features bright yellow flowers with four petals, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it prefers well-drained soil in full sun.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
