Eleutherococcus cissifolius var. scandens

Eleutherococcus cissifolius var. scandens, commonly known as the climbing eleutherococcus, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

This variant is noted for its vigorous climbing habit and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
