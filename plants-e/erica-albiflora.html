Erica albiflora

Erica albiflora, commonly known as the white heath, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its small, needle-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Erica albiflora is well adapted to acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
