Elatine hydropiper var. hexandra

Elatine hydropiper var. hexandra, commonly known as the water pepper, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant belonging to the Elatinaceae family.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has finely divided, grass-like leaves that are often submerged or floating. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or white in color.

This species is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich, freshwater environments.
