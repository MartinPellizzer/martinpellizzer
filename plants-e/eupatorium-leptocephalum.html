Eupatorium leptocephalum

Eupatorium leptocephalum, commonly known as narrow-headed thoroughwort, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink to purple and bloom in late summer.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
