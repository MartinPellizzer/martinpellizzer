Eupatorium neriifolium

Eupatorium neriifolium, commonly known as white boneset, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture with prominent veins. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks.

The plant is often used in herbal medicine for its purported antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.
