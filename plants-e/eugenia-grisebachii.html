Eugenia grisebachii

Eugenia grisebachii, commonly known as the yellow myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant yellow flowers and glossy, dark green leaves. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Eugenia grisebachii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has also been used in traditional medicine.
