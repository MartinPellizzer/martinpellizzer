Eugenia tiampoka

Eugenia tiampoka, commonly known as the Tiampoka myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, glossy leaves and small white or pinkish flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit, a small, fleshy drupe, is edible and often consumed fresh or used in local cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aromatic properties.
