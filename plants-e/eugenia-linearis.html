Eugenia linearis

Eugenia linearis, commonly known as the narrowleaf eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered white or pale pink flowers. It produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red when mature.

Eugenia linearis is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and fragrant blooms.
