Echinopsis haageanus

Echinopsis haageanus, commonly known as the Moon Cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its short, thick stem and a vibrant, colorful crown that resembles a flower. The plant is a member of the Cactaceae family and is known for its ability to store water in its fleshy stems. It thrives in arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.

Due to its striking appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
