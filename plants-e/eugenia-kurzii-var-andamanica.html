Eugenia kurzii var. andamanica

Eugenia kurzii var. andamanica, commonly known as the Andaman hibiscus, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Andaman Islands.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or lowland forest areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation projects.
