Echeveria hoveyi

Echeveria hoveyi, commonly known as the Hovei Echeveria, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of fleshy, blue-gray leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white hair, giving it a frosty appearance. The plant produces clusters of vibrant purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor collections.

Due to its slow growth and ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and easy care requirements.
