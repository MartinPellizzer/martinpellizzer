Erythraea albiflora

Erythraea albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered erythraea, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, white blooms that appear in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slight pubescence.

Erythraea albiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
