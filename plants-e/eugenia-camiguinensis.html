Eugenia camiguinensis

Eugenia camiguinensis, commonly known as the Camiguin guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Philippines, specifically the island of Camiguin, and is known for its small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or red when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in montane forests.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
