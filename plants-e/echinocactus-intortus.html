Echinocactus intortus

Echinocactus intortus, commonly known as the twisted hedgehog cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are covered in spines, which provide protection against herbivores. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and xeriscaping.
