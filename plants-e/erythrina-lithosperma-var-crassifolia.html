Erythrina lithosperma var. crassifolia

Erythrina lithosperma var. crassifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf coral tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It features compound leaves with elongated, leathery leaflets that are often clustered in groups of three. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like birds and bees. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged appearance, while its wood is dense and durable.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
