Echinocactus capricornis var. senilis

Echinocactus capricornis var. senilis, commonly known as the old man of the desert, is a striking cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny appearance, with a tall, cylindrical stem covered in white, woolly bristles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a vibrant contrast to its otherwise muted appearance. It is well adapted to hot, dry environments and requires minimal water once established.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
