Eupatorium macrodon

Eupatorium macrodon, commonly known as the showy eupatorium, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and large, showy flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that are typically arranged in panicles. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Eupatorium macrodon is often found in moist habitats such as wetlands, meadows, and along streambanks.
