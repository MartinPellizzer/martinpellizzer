Eupatorium bergantinense

Eupatorium bergantinense, commonly known as the Bergantin mountain eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in cloud forests. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences.

Eupatorium bergantinense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
