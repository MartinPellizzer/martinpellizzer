Eriophorum vaginatum var. fauriei

Eriophorum vaginatum var. fauriei, commonly known as the northern cotton grass, is a flowering plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It is characterized by its slender, hair-like leaves and fluffy, white seed heads that resemble cotton. This variant is typically found in wet, acidic soils such as those in tundra and heathland environments. The plant plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.

Its growth is closely tied to cold climates and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
