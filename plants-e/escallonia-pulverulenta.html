Escallonia pulverulenta

Escallonia pulverulenta, commonly known as the silver leaf escallonia, is a shrub native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage that is covered in a fine layer of silvery white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in spring, which are followed by bright red berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Escallonia pulverulenta is also valued for its drought tolerance and resistance to pests and diseases.
