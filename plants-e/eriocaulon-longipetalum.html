Eriocaulon longipetalum

Eriocaulon longipetalum, commonly known as the long-petaled marsh grass, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to wetland habitats in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Eriocaulaceae, which is characterized by its small, grass-like appearance and specialized adaptations for water environments. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often submerged or floating, and it produces tiny, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale in color. It thrives in still or slow-moving waters and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.
