Eriogonum campanulatum subsp. grangerense

Eriogonum campanulatum subsp.

grangerense, commonly known as the Sierra bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky soils and open habitats such as meadows and slopes. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, seed-like fruits called achene.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
