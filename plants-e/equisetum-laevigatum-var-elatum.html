Equisetum laevigatum var. elatum

Equisetum laevigatum var. elatum, commonly known as the tall scouring rush, is a perennial plant belonging to the Equisetaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems that are covered in small, scale-like structures. The plant produces spore-bearing cones at the top of the stem, which are used for reproduction. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is known for its hardy nature and ability to tolerate a range of environmental conditions.
