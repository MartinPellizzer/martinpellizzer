Eustoma exaltatum f. exaltatum

Eustoma exaltatum f. exaltatum, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, typically in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often grown as an ornamental in gardens for its attractive appearance. It prefers full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both landscape design and cut flower arrangements.
