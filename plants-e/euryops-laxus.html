Euryops laxus

Euryops laxus, commonly known as the coastal dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and slender, branching stems. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, showing a high degree of adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are usually linear and covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention.

Euryops laxus plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and supporting biodiversity.
