Eulalia manipurensis

Eulalia manipurensis, commonly known as the Manipur grass, is a perennial grass species native to the northeastern region of India.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and forest edges. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are often wind-pollinated.

Eulalia manipurensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small fauna in its native environment.
