Encephalartos spinulosus

Encephalartos spinulosus, commonly known as the spinulose cycad, is a species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of large, feathery leaves. The plant produces cones, with the male cones being clustered at the base of the plant and the female cones emerging from the crown. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water once established.

Encephalartos spinulosus is a slow-growing plant that is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance.
