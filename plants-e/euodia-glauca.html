Euodia glauca

Euodia glauca, commonly known as the blue euodia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often have a bluish tinge. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by blue-black berries in the fall. Euodia glauca is typically found in moist, acidic soils in woodland areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant landscaping.
