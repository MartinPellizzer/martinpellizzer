Echium hypertropicum

Echium hypertropicum, commonly known as the erect thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and vibrant purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often growing in rocky or dry environments.

Echium hypertropicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking visual impact.
