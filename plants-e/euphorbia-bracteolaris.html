Euphorbia bracteolaris

Euphorbia bracteolaris, commonly known as the Mexican milkweed, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features a short, thick stem with clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. It produces a milky sap that can be irritating to the skin.

Euphorbia bracteolaris is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
