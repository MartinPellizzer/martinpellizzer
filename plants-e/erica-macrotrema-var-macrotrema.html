Erica macrotrema var. macrotrema

Erica macrotrema var. macrotrema, commonly known as the greater heath, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is well adapted to Mediterranean climates and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance and hardiness.
