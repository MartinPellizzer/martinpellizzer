Erica ferrea

Erica ferrea, commonly known as the red heath, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. Erica ferrea typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
