Eulophia mucronata

Eulophia mucronata, commonly known as the blunt-tipped orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Eulophia mucronata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable conditions.
