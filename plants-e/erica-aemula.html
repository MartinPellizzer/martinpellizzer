Erica aemula

Erica aemula, commonly known as the dwarf heath, is a species of shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related plants. The plant is characterized by its dense, low-growing habit and small, needle-like leaves. It produces clusters of pink or white flowers in spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds.

Erica aemula is well adapted to acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.
