Eremophila mackinlayi subsp. mackinlayi

Eremophila mackinlayi subsp.

mackinlayi, commonly known as the Mackinlay's eremophila, is a shrub native to western Australia. It typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, adapted to low rainfall and poor soils.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces spikes of pink to red flowers during the warmer months. It is often found in open woodlands and heathlands, thriving in full sun.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
