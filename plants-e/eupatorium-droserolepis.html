Eupatorium droserolepis

Eupatorium droserolepis, commonly known as the sticky eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant has aromatic leaves that emit a distinctive scent, especially when crushed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Eupatorium droserolepis plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
