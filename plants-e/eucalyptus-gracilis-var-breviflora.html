Eucalyptus gracilis var. breviflora

Eucalyptus gracilis var. breviflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and narrow, linear leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The flowers are small and usually cream or white, arranged in clustered inflorescences. This plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or loamy soils.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
