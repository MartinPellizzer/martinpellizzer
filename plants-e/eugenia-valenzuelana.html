Eugenia valenzuelana

Eugenia valenzuelana, commonly known as the yellow myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
