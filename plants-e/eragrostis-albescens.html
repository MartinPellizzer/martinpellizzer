Eragrostis albescens

Eragrostis albescens, commonly known as silver bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and upright growth habit, which makes it visually striking in natural landscapes. The plant typically grows in full sun and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping and erosion control. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, producing small, pale blue to white spikelets that are clustered in dense panicles.

Eragrostis albescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
