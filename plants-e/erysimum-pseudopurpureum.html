Erysimum pseudopurpureum

Erysimum pseudopurpureum, commonly known as the purple-leafed wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Erysimum pseudopurpureum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
