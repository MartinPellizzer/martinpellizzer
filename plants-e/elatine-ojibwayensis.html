Elatine ojibwayensis

Elatine ojibwayensis, commonly known as Ojibway water starwort, is a small, aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. The plant has slender, branching stems that are often submerged, with small, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape. Its flowers are tiny and clustered in spike-like inflorescences, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Elatine ojibwayensis plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and invertebrates.
