Euphorbia telanganensis

Euphorbia telanganensis, commonly known as the Telangana spurge, is a flowering plant native to the Indian state of Telangana.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, spiny stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are usually fleshy and may vary in shape, while its flowers are often clustered in a structure called a cyathium.

Euphorbia telanganensis is also known for its ability to store water, making it a resilient species in dry climates.
