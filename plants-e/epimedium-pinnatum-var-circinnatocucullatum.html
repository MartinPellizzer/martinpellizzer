Epimedium pinnatum var. circinnatocucullatum

Epimedium pinnatum var. circinnatocucullatum, commonly known as the golden thread fern, is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are often tinged with golden hues, especially in the sunlight. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow, which appear in early spring. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is highly valued in horticulture and botanical collections.
