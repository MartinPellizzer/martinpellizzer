Erica mediterranea var. occidentalis

Erica mediterranea var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western heath, is a low-growing shrub native to the western Mediterranean region.

It features small, evergreen leaves that are typically needle-like and covered in a fine, whitish bloom. The plant produces delicate, pinkish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
