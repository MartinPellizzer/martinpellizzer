Eugenia heterochroma

Eugenia heterochroma, commonly known as the variable myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, dark berries that contain several seeds.

Eugenia heterochroma is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and pleasant scent.
