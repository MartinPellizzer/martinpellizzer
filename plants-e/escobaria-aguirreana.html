Escobaria aguirreana

Escobaria aguirreana, commonly known as the Aguirre's liveforever, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth form. The plant features fleshy, spiny stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers appear in spring, adding a subtle ornamental value.

Escobaria aguirreana is well adapted to rocky soils and full sun, making it a resilient choice for xeriscaping.
