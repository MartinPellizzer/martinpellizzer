Eupatorium cardiophyllum

Eupatorium cardiophyllum, commonly known as heart-leaved eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in traditional herbal remedies.
