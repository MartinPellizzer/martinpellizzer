Elymus krascheninnikovii

Elymus krascheninnikovii, commonly known as the Siberian wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the cold climates of Eurasia.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant has narrow, blue-green leaves and produces spikes of small, pale green to brown flowers. It is well adapted to harsh conditions, including low temperatures and poor soil, making it a hardy species.

Its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion makes it ecologically significant in its native habitats.
