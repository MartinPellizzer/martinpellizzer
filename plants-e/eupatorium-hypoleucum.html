Eupatorium hypoleucum

Eupatorium hypoleucum, commonly known as white boneset, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its root system is fibrous and deep.

White boneset is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.
