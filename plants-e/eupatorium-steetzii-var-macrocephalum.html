Eupatorium steetzii var. macrocephalum

Eupatorium steetzii var. macrocephalum, commonly known as the large-headed eupatorium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features showy, pale purple to white flower clusters that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is often found in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and woodland edges.
