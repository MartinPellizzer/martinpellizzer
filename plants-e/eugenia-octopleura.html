Eugenia octopleura

Eugenia octopleura, commonly known as the purple-leaved eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its glossy, dark purple leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically reddish when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Eugenia octopleura is also valued for its aromatic foliage and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
