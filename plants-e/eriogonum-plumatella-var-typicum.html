Eriogonum plumatella var. typicum

Eriogonum plumatella var. typicum, commonly known as the desert paintbrush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and showy, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, reducing water loss through transpiration.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies in its native habitat.
