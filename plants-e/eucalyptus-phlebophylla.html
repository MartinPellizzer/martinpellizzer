Eucalyptus phlebophylla

Eucalyptus phlebophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf malleeflower, is a small to medium-sized shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically visited by a variety of pollinators. Its bark is fibrous and peels in thin layers, a common feature among many eucalyptus species.

Eucalyptus phlebophylla is well adapted to drought and poor soil conditions, making it a resilient component of its native ecosystem.
