Evolvulus purpusii

Evolvulus purpusii, commonly known as the purple morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in the morning, hence its common name. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often twining.

Evolvulus purpusii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
