Eupatorium erigeroides

Eupatorium erigeroides, commonly known as stiff eupatorium, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright, branched stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges. Its flowers, which appear in late summer, are small and clustered in dense inflorescences, often displaying white or pale purple hues.

Eupatorium erigeroides is typically found in moist woodland areas and along stream banks.
