Eria uchiyamae

Eria uchiyamae, commonly known as the Uchiyama's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually strap-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern.

Eria uchiyamae thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.
