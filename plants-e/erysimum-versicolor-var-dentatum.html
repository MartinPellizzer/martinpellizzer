Erysimum versicolor var. dentatum

Erysimum versicolor var. dentatum, commonly known as the toothed wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with four petals, typical of the Brassicaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
