Eschscholzia caruifolia var. cyathifera

Eschscholzia caruifolia var. cyathifera, commonly known as the California poppy, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red flowers and finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dry seeds.

This variety is known for its adaptability and is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
