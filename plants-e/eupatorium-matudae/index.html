Eupatorium Matudae

Eupatorium matudae is a rare flowering plant native to the cloud forests of Costa Rica. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its striking violet-blue flowers. This species is named after the botanist who first discovered it, Dr. Matuda. Due to its limited habitat, it is considered endangered and is threatened by deforestation. Conservation efforts are crucial to preserving this unique and beautiful plant.
