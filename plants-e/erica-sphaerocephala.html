Erica sphaerocephala

Erica sphaerocephala, commonly known as the Cape snowbush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the heath family, Ericaceae, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its evergreen leaves are leathery and arranged in a tight, spherical shape, giving it a unique appearance.

Erica sphaerocephala is well adapted to sandy, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
