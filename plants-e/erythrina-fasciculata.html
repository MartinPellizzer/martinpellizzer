Erythrina fasciculata

Erythrina fasciculata, commonly known as the coral tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its bright red, fluffy flower clusters that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as birds and bees. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its trunk is often smooth with a reddish-brown bark. Erythrina fasciculata is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

It also has cultural significance in some regions and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
