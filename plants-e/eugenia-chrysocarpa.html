Eugenia chrysocarpa

Eugenia chrysocarpa, commonly known as the golden fruit eugenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are edible and have a sweet, slightly acidic flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
