Eupatorium stigmatosum var. subcalvatum

Eupatorium stigmatosum var. subcalvatum, commonly known as the roughleaf boneset, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used by Native American tribes for various health purposes.
