Eyrythalia lancifolia

Eyrythalia lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved erythrina, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive red, pea-like flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets. Its stems are often woody and can develop thorns, which serve as a defense mechanism.

Eyrythalia lancifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
