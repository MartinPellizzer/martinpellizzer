Eugenia siggersii

Eugenia siggersii, commonly known as the Siggers' evergreen, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
