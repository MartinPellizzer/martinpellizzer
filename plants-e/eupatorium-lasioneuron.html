Eupatorium lasioneuron

Eupatorium lasioneuron, commonly known as the narrowleaf boneset, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a distinctive silvery-white underside.

It produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
