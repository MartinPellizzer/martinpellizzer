Erica glabella subsp. laevis

Erica glabella subsp.

laevis, commonly known as the smooth heath, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to southern Africa. It features small, needle-like leaves that are smooth and glabrous, giving it its subspecies name.

The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in coastal and sandy environments.
