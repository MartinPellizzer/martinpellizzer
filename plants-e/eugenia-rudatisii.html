Eugenia rudatisii

Eugenia rudatisii, commonly known as the red-stemmed myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, red berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines.

Eugenia rudatisii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and aromatic qualities.
