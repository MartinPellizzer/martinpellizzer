Euphorbia graminea var. pedunculosa

Euphorbia graminea var. pedunculosa, commonly known as the slender spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a branched stem system with cylindrical, slightly ribbed stems that can grow up to several feet in height. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers surrounded by green bracts, typical of the Euphorbia genus. Its leaves are usually reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it is known for its drought tolerance and ability to store water in its stems.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
