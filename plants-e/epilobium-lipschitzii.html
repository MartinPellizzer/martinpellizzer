Epilobium lipschitzii

Epilobium lipschitzii, commonly known as the Balkan willow herb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, riverbanks, and woodland edges. The plant features erect, branching stems covered with glandular hairs, and its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are pink to purple, with five petals, and are arranged in dense, spike-like clusters.

Epilobium lipschitzii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
