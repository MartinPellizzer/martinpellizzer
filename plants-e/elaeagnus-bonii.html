Elaeagnus bonii

Elaeagnus bonii, commonly known as the Chinese silverberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its silvery-white leaves and fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically yellow or orange in color. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Elaeagnus bonii is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing properties, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
