Euphorbia kozlovii

Euphorbia kozlovii, commonly known as the Kozlov's spurges, is a succulent plant native to Central Asia.

It features a stem that is often swollen and branched, with small, clustered leaves that are usually fleshy and green. The plant produces unique cyathia, which are cup-shaped structures that contain the female flower and several male flowers. It is adapted to arid environments and can store water in its stems and leaves.

Euphorbia kozlovii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its distinctive appearance and hardy nature.
