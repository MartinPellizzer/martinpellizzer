Eugenia tweediei

Eugenia tweediei, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom during the winter season. The fruit, which is a small, round berry, turns red when mature and is edible, though not commonly consumed by humans.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to warm, humid climates.
