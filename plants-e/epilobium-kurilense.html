Epilobium kurilense

Epilobium kurilense, commonly known as Kuril willow herb, is a herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Asia, including parts of Japan, Korea, and the Russian Far East.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features slender stems and lance-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are pink to purple, arranged in spikes, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Epilobium kurilense is often found in wetlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
