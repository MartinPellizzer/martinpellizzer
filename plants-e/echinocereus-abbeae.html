Echinocereus abbeae

Echinocereus abbeae, commonly known as the Apache ghostflower, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing, spineless plant with a distinctive appearance featuring pale blue or greenish stems covered in white, woolly hairs. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Due to its unique beauty and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in desert gardens and conservation efforts aim to protect its natural habitat.
