Ervum hohenackeri

Ervum hohenackeri, commonly known as the Hohenacker's vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats. It produces pods that contain small, edible seeds, which can be used for forage or in traditional medicine.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some areas.
