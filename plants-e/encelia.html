Encelia

Encelia, commonly known as the brittlebush, is a genus of flowering plants native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

These plants are typically shrubs or small trees, characterized by their silver-gray foliage and showy yellow flowers. Encelia species often thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations such as succulent leaves and deep root systems. The flowers are usually clustered in dense, spherical heads, and the genus includes several species, such as Encelia farinosa and Encelia calycina.

Botanically, Encelia is part of the aster family (Asteraceae), which is known for its composite flower structures.
