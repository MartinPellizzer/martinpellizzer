Elynanthus punctorius

Elynanthus punctorius, commonly known as the puncture vine, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, round, spiny fruits that contain one or two seeds each. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Elynanthus punctorius is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a common weed in disturbed soils.
