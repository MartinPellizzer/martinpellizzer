Eremochloa bimaculata

Eremochloa bimaculata, commonly known as the two-spotted grass, is a species of grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Eremochloa bimaculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
