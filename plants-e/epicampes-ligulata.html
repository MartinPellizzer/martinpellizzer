Epicampes ligulata

Epicampes ligulata, commonly known as the narrowleaf false aster, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features slender, erect stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its small, clustered flowers are usually white to pale purple, blooming in late summer.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system.
