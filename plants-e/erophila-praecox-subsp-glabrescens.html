Erophila praecox subsp. glabrescens

Erophila praecox subsp.

glabrescens, commonly known as the early erophila, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. It blooms early in the growing season, often from late winter to early spring.

This subspecies is characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and is often found in well-drained soils.
