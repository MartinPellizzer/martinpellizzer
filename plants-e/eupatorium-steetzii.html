Eupatorium steetzii

Eupatorium steetzii, commonly known as Steetz's boneset, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins. It is known for its aromatic foliage and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, attracting various pollinators.
