Echinofossulocactus spinosus

Echinofossulocactus spinosus, commonly known as the spiny fissured cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water. The plant features prominent spines, which provide protection from herbivores and help reduce water loss. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, blooming in late spring or early summer.

Echinofossulocactus spinosus is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soil.
