Eupatorium alternifolium f. typicum

Eupatorium alternifolium f. typicum, commonly known as the alternate-leaved thoroughwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its alternate arrangement of lanceolate leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is known for its composite flower heads.

The species is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
