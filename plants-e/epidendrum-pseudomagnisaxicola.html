Epidendrum pseudomagnisaxicola

Epidendrum pseudomagnisaxicola, commonly known as the Mexican orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and large, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the surrounding substrate.

Epidendrum pseudomagnisaxicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
