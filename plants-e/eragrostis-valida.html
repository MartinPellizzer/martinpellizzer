Eragrostis valida

Eragrostis valida, commonly known as the slender lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open grasslands and sandy soils, thriving in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowering spikes are usually pale green to brown, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Eragrostis valida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in erosion control due to its hardy nature.
