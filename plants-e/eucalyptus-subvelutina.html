Eucalyptus subvelutina

Eucalyptus subvelutina, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, smooth-barked trunk and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety layer of hair. The flowers are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters and attracting various pollinators. This plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for native wildlife.
