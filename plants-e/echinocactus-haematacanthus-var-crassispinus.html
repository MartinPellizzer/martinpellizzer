Echinocactus haematacanthus var. crassispinus

Echinocactus haematacanthus var. crassispinus, commonly known as the thick-spined hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its robust, cylindrical stems that are typically green with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its spines are thick and prominent, providing protection from herbivores.

This variety is well adapted to harsh desert conditions, making it a hardy and resilient plant.
