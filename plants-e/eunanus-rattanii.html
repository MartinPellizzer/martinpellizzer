Eunanus rattanii

Eunanus rattanii, commonly known as the golden rattan, is a climbing palm native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, flexible stems used for making furniture and baskets. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by elongated, curved fruits. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with long, narrow leaflets.

Eunanus rattanii thrives in humid, tropical environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
