Erica accommodata var. accommodata

Erica accommodata var. accommodata, commonly known as the Heath Erica, is a species of evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover.

The flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
