Eucalyptus campanulata

Eucalyptus campanulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed shrubs or small trees with smooth, greyish bark. The plant produces small, bell-shaped white or pale blue flowers, which are clustered in groups of three to five. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, typically with a silver or grey-green coloration.

Eucalyptus campanulata is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.
