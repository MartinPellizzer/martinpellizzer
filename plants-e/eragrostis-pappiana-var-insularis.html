Eragrostis pappiana var. insularis

Eragrostis pappiana var. insularis, commonly known as the island lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce numerous fluffy seeds. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments due to its tolerance for salt and wind. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that helps it retain moisture in arid conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems.
