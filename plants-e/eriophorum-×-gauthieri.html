Eriophorum × gauthieri

Eriophorum × gauthieri, commonly known as the tufted cottongrass, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, fluffy seed heads that resemble small clouds, typically appearing in late summer. The plant thrives in wet, acidic environments such as bogs and marshes, where it forms dense mats. Its slender, grass-like stems are often covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Eriophorum × gauthieri plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and provides habitat for various small invertebrates.
