Erodium guttatum var. malopoides

Erodium guttatum var. malopoides, commonly known as the Malop's stickyweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a low-growing annual herb characterized by its small, oval leaves and clusters of pink to reddish-purple flowers. The plant produces small, sticky seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize disturbed soils. It thrives in dry, sandy environments and is often found in coastal regions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is considered a weed in some agricultural areas.
