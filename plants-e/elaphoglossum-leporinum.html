Elaphoglossum leporinum

Elaphoglossum leporinum, commonly known as the rabbit's foot fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that resemble rabbit feet, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are bi-lobed and have a distinctive, intricate pattern of veins.

Elaphoglossum leporinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
