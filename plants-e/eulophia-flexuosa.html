Eulophia flexuosa

Eulophia flexuosa, commonly known as the flexible eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded forest understories and thrives in humid environments. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowering stems. Its flowers are usually pale green to white, with a distinct, elongated lip that aids in pollination.

Eulophia flexuosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
