Erythraea gerardii

Erythraea gerardii, commonly known as the prairie verbena, is a flowering plant native to the central United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, pink to violet flowers. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils.

Erythraea gerardii is adapted to full sun and is often found in regions with warm, dry climates.
