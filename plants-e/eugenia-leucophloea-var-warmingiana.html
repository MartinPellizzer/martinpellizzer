Eugenia leucophloea var. warmingiana

Eugenia leucophloea var. warmingiana, commonly known as the white-veined eugenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves that have prominent white veins, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by edible, red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
