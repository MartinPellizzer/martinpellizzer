Elytropappus longifolius

Elytropappus longifolius, commonly known as the longleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and woolly, silver-green foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into fluffy, white seed heads, typical of the dandelion genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Elytropappus longifolius is often found in disturbed areas, such as roadsides and open grasslands.
