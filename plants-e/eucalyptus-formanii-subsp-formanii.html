Eucalyptus formanii subsp. formanii

Eucalyptus formanii subsp.

formanii, commonly known as the Grey Box, is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark that often appears grey or brown, and its lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color.

The flowers are usually small, white or pale blue, and are arranged in clusters along the branches. This subspecies is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in woodland or open forest habitats.

It plays an important ecological role in providing habitat and food for various native wildlife species.
