Echidnopsis cereiformis var. brunnea

Echidnopsis cereiformis var. brunnea, commonly known as the Brown Ghost Orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, ghost-like appearance with elongated, pale green leaves and a single, showy pale yellow or brownish flower. The plant thrives in harsh, sandy soils and requires a specific microclimate to flourish. Its flowers are pollinated by specific insect species, highlighting its ecological niche.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
