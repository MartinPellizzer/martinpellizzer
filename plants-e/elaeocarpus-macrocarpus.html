Elaeocarpus macrocarpus

Elaeocarpus macrocarpus, commonly known as the broad-leaved elaeocarpus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitats.
