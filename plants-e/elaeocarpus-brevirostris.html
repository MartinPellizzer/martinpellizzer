Elaeocarpus brevirostris

Elaeocarpus brevirostris, commonly known as the short-beaked elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to parts of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe, which are an important food source for various birds and mammals. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests and coastal areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitats.
