Eragrostis tremula

Eragrostis tremula, commonly known as slender false rice, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brownish flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

Eragrostis tremula is often considered a weed but has some ornamental value in certain landscapes.
