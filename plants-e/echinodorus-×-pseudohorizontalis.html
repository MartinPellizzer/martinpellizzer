Echinodorus × pseudohorizontalis

Echinodorus × pseudohorizontalis, commonly known as the Amazon sword plant, is a popular aquatic plant native to South America.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Echinodorus bleheri and Echinodorus cordifolius. The plant is characterized by its broad, sword-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. It thrives in freshwater environments with moderate to high light intensity and requires stable water parameters.

Echinodorus × pseudohorizontalis is often used in aquariums for its aesthetic appeal and ability to provide habitat for aquatic life.
