Edraianthus graminifolius f. alpinus

Edraianthus graminifolius f. alpinus, commonly known as the alpine grass-leaved edraianthus, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
