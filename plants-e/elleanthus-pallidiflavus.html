Elleanthus pallidiflavus

Elleanthus pallidiflavus, commonly known as the pale yellow everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant produces yellow to pale yellow flowers that are often arranged in dense, spherical clusters. It is known for its long-lasting blooms, which make it a popular choice in floral arrangements.

Elleanthus pallidiflavus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
