Ehrharta panicea var. mucronata

Ehrharta panicea var. mucronata, commonly known as flatleaf dallisgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its flat, narrow leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle with spikelets that are loosely arranged.

This variety is known for its adaptability to wet environments and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
