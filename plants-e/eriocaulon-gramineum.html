Eriocaulon gramineum

Eriocaulon gramineum, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a species of flowering plant in the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grassy leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, preferring moist, acidic soils. It is native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and the Korean Peninsula.

Eriocaulon gramineum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in water gardens or bog environments.
