Elaeocarpus fusicarpus

Elaeocarpus fusicarpus, commonly known as the narrow-fruit elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The tree produces small, dark blue to black fruits that are typically clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. These fruits are often used in traditional medicine and are also a food source for various wildlife species.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation in warm climates.
