Eupatorium leonense

Eupatorium leonense, commonly known as lemon verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, lemon-scented leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its leaves are often used in herbal teas and for their aromatic properties.

Eupatorium leonense is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
