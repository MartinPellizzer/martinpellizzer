Epidendrum pozoi

Epidendrum pozoi, commonly known as the Pozo's epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit and produces long, arching stems that bear numerous flowers. The plant typically has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in two ranks, and its flowers are usually white or pale pink with a distinct lip. Epidendrum pozoi thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in the understory of tropical forests.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
