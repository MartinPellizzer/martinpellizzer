Erica fasciculata

Erica fasciculata, commonly known as the Cape heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to coastal and mountainous regions.

Erica fasciculata is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ornamental value.
