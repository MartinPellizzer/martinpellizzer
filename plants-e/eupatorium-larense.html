Eupatorium larense

Eupatorium larense, commonly known as the Laren stone nettle, is a perennial herb native to the Caribbean islands.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has serrated leaves and is often found growing in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.

The plant's name reflects its historical use in treating ailments, particularly those related to the respiratory system.
