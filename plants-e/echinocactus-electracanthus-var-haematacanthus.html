Echinocactus electracanthus var. haematacanthus

Echinocactus electracanthus var. haematacanthus, commonly known as the blood-red hedgehog cactus, is a distinctive cactus species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that are typically a deep red or purple hue, especially when exposed to full sun. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered at the tips of the stems. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and rockeries.
