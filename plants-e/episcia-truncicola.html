Episcia truncicola

Episcia truncicola, commonly known as the dwarf heartleaf begonia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with silver and green. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming during the warmer months. Episcia truncicola thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

It is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
