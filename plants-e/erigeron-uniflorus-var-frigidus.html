Erigeron uniflorus var. frigidus

Erigeron uniflorus var. frigidus, commonly known as the Arctic blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is characterized by its small, blue to purple flowers and hairy, silver-gray foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in cold, high-altitude environments. It has a low-growing habit and is often found in tundra and alpine meadows.

Its botanical features are adapted to withstand extreme cold and short growing seasons.
