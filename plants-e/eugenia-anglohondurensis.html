Eugenia anglohondurensis

Eugenia anglohondurensis, commonly known as the Honduras huito, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Central America, particularly Honduras, and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in humid, shaded environments.

Its botanical features include simple, alternate leaves and a compact shrub or small tree form.
