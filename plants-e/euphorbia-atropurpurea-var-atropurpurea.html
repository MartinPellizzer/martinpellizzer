Euphorbia atropurpurea var. atropurpurea

Euphorbia atropurpurea var. atropurpurea, commonly known as the Indian blanket or red-leaf spurge, is a perennial plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It features striking, velvety, reddish-purple foliage that turns bronze in the fall, and its flowers are small, yellowish-green cyathia surrounded by bright orange bracts. The plant has a milky sap that can be irritating to skin, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
