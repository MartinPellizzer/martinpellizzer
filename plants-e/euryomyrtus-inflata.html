Euryomyrtus inflata

Euryomyrtus inflata, commonly known as the inflated myrtle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, inflated fruit that resembles a small balloon. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils. Its leaves are narrow and covered in a fine, white pubescence, and it produces small, fragrant white flowers.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
