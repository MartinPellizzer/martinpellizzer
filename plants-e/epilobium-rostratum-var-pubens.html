Epilobium rostratum var. pubens

Epilobium rostratum var. pubens, commonly known as the hairy willow herb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist environments such as wet meadows, riverbanks, and marshy areas. The plant features slender, often hairy stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough texture and a reddish or purplish underside.

The flowers are small, pink to purple, and arranged in spikes, blooming from late spring to early summer.
