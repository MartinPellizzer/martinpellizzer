Eugenioides rufescens

Eugenioides rufescens, commonly known as the red-tinged eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often reddish or purplish in color, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically white or pink, clustered in small inflorescences, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible.

Eugenioides rufescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
