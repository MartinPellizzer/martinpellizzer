Elymus tauri var. kosaninii

Elymus tauri var. kosaninii, commonly known as the Kosanin's needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of hairs. The plant produces spikelets that are arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential use in restoration ecology.
