Eugenia sagrae

Eugenia sagrae, commonly known as the Sagrae clove or Sagrae myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as eucalyptus and myrtle. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing aromatic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

Eugenia sagrae is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
