Echinopsis silvatica

Echinopsis silvatica, commonly known as the Silvatic Hedgehog Cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Bolivia.

It is a columnar cactus with a distinctive ribbed structure and spines that provide protection against herbivores. The plant produces large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming at night. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-drained soil and minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in succulent gardens and botanical collections.
