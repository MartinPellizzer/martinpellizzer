Erysimum menziesii subsp. yadonii

Erysimum menziesii subsp.

yadonii, commonly known as the Yadon's wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters.

The plant produces clusters of bright yellow, four-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are linear to lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
