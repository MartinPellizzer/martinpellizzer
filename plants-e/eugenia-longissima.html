Eugenia longissima

Eugenia longissima, commonly known as the long-stalked eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to red when mature.

Eugenia longissima is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
