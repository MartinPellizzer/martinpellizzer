Eragrostis inconstans

Eragrostis inconstans, commonly known as the variable bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has slender, wiry stems and small, blue-green leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle made up of numerous spikelets, which are arranged in a loose, open structure.

Eragrostis inconstans is known for its ability to thrive in moist to wet conditions, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
