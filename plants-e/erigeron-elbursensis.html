Erigeron elbursensis

Erigeron elbursensis, commonly known as the Elburz fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the Elburz Mountains in Iran.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are usually blue to violet in color, with white ray florets, and bloom during the summer months.

Erigeron elbursensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
