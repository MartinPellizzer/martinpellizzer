Ephedra × arenicola

Ephedra × arenicola, commonly known as the desert jointfir, is a hybrid plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is a shrub characterized by its woody stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically pollinated by wind. Ephedra × arenicola is adapted to harsh environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of desert flora.
