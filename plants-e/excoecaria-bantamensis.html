Excoecaria bantamensis

Excoecaria bantamensis, commonly known as the Bantam croton, is a tropical shrub native to Indonesia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that vary in hue from green to red and yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsules containing seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its sap contains toxic compounds, which can be harmful if ingested.
