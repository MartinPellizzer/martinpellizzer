Echium giganteum var. aculeata

Echium giganteum var. aculeata, commonly known as the spiked evening primrose, is a striking plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces vibrant violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to well-drained, sunny soils.
