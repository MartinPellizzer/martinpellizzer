Erythroxylum carthagenense

Erythroxylum carthagenense, commonly known as the Colombian or Andean copaiba, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that contain seeds, and it is often found in humid, tropical environments.

It has been historically used for its aromatic resins and has ecological significance in its native habitats.
