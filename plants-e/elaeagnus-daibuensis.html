Elaeagnus daibuensis

Elaeagnus daibuensis, commonly known as the Japanese silverberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its silvery-hairy leaves and small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shady environments.

Elaeagnus daibuensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
