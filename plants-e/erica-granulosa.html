Erica granulosa

Erica granulosa, commonly known as the granular heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. This plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. Erica granulosa produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or red in color.

It is often found in rocky or coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
