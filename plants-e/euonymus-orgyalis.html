Euonymus orgyalis

Euonymus orgyalis, commonly known as the Orange Jewelweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking orange-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that split open to release small, bright red seeds.

Euonymus orgyalis is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.
