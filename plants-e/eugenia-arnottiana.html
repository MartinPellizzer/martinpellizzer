Eugenia arnottiana

Eugenia arnottiana, commonly known as the Australian cherry, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subtropical regions.

Eugenia arnottiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
