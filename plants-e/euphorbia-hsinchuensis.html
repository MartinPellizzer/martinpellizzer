Euphorbia hsinchuensis

Euphorbia hsinchuensis, commonly known as Hsinchu spurge, is a flowering plant native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, spiny stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its foliage is often green with a waxy texture, and it produces a milky sap that can be irritating to the skin.

Hsinchu spurge is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
