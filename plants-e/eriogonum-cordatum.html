Eriogonum cordatum

Eriogonum cordatum, commonly known as the heart-leaved buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other members like sorrel and knotweed.

Eriogonum cordatum plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in wildflower meadows.
