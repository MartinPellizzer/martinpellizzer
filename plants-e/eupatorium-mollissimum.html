Eupatorium mollissimum

Eupatorium mollissimum, commonly known as velvetleaf eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Eupatorium mollissimum is also valued in ornamental horticulture for its attractive appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
