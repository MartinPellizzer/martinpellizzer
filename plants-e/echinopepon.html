Echinopepon

Echinopepon, commonly known as the horned melon, is a unique member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its spiky, green exterior that resembles a hornet's nest. The fruit's interior contains a sweet, edible pulp with black seeds. Botanically, it is a climbing vine that produces large, round fruits.

Its distinctive appearance and flavor make it a notable species in both horticulture and culinary contexts.
