Eulophia striata

Eulophia striata, commonly known as the striped eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a pseudobulb that supports its foliage and flowering structures. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and bears inflorescences with showy, yellow-green to orange flowers that have distinct striations. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Eulophia striata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
