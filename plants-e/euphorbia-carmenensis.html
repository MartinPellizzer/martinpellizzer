Euphorbia carmenensis

Euphorbia carmenensis, commonly known as the Carmen spurge, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically green or reddish in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are often surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is adapted to arid environments and stores water in its stems to survive drought conditions.

Euphorbia carmenensis is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family, known for its diverse species with unique morphological features.
