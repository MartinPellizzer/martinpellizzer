Euodia lepta var. cambodiana

Euodia lepta var. cambodiana, commonly known as Cambodian prickly ash, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark blue, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture.
