Ericoides barbigerum

Ericoides barbigerum, commonly known as the barbed ericoid, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong coastal winds.

Ericoides barbigerum plays an important role in stabilizing dune ecosystems and providing habitat for local wildlife.
