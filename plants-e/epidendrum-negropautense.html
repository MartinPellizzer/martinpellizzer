Epidendrum negropautense

Epidendrum negropautense, commonly known as the Negroponte orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and the production of numerous, small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in the understory of tropical forests. Its leaves are usually elliptical and covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
