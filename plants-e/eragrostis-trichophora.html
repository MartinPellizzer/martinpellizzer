Eragrostis trichophora

Eragrostis trichophora, commonly known as slender lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its flowering spikes are usually pale green to brown, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Eragrostis trichophora is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
