Emilia guineensis

Emilia guineensis, commonly known as African blue lupine, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces elongated seed pods that split open when mature. Emilia guineensis thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
