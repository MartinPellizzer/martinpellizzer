Erica tumida var. tumida

Erica tumida var. tumida, commonly known as the purple heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers, which range in color from white to deep purple, bloom in late summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Erica tumida var. tumida is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
