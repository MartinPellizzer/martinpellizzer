Eupatorium exserto-venosum var. crenato-dentatum

Eupatorium exserto-venosum var. crenato-dentatum, commonly known as the crenate-leaved boneset, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features tall, erect stems covered in fine, white hairs, and its leaves are distinctly crenate-dentate, with notched and toothed margins. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically blue to purple in color, blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, sunny habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species within the Asteraceae family.
