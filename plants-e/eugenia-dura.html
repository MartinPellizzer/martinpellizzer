Eugenia dura

Eugenia dura, commonly known as the hardy eucalyptus, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its tough, fibrous bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its fruits are small, woody capsules that contain several seeds.

Eugenia dura is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
