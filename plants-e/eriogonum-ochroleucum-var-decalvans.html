Eriogonum ochroleucum var. decalvans

Eriogonum ochroleucum var. decalvans, commonly known as the decalved goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as sagebrush steppe and rocky slopes.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is often found in arid and semi-arid regions.
