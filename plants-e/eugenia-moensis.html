Eugenia moensis

Eugenia moensis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or moensia, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Eugenia moensis is also valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitats.
