Echinops inermis

Echinops inermis, commonly known as the Persian cornflower, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its spherical, spiny seed heads that resemble a hedgehog, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Echinops inermis is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in dry climates.
