Elymus platatherus

Elymus platatherus, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the central and northern regions of the United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, which are wind-pollinated. Elymus platatherus is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in open fields, where it thrives in full sun.

Its ecological significance includes providing forage for wildlife and stabilizing soil in various habitats.
