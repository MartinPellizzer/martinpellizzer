Eupatorium album

Eupatorium album, commonly known as white boneset, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in wetlands and along the edges of ponds, showcasing its adaptability to moist environments.

Eupatorium album is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
