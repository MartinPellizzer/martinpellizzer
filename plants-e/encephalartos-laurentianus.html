Encephalartos laurentianus

Encephalartos laurentianus, commonly known as the Laurentian cycad, is a species of cycad native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of stiff, strap-like leaves that are dark green in color. The plant produces large, cone-like structures, with male and female cones typically occurring on separate plants. It is adapted to grow in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its slow growth and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
