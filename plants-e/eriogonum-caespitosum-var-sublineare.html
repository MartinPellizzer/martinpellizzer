Eriogonum caespitosum var. sublineare

Eriogonum caespitosum var. sublineare, commonly known as the slender leaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
