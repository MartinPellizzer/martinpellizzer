Eupatorium ornatum

Eupatorium ornatum, commonly known as white boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks, preferring moist to wet soils. The plant features tall, erect stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy, small white to pale pink flowers appear in clusters during the summer months.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
