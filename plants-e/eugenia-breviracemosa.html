Eugenia breviracemosa

Eugenia breviracemosa, commonly known as the short-stemmed guava, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or red when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is cultivated in some tropical and subtropical regions for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
