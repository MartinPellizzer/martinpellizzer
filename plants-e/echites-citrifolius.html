Echites citrifolius

Echites citrifolius, commonly known as the citron tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or orange when mature. The fruit has a thick, bitter rind and a juicy, acidic pulp.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the extraction of essential oils.
