Erysimum grandiflorum f. gramineum

Erysimum grandiflorum f. gramineum, commonly known as the grassy wallflower, is a compact, low-growing plant characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage.

It produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a vibrant touch to gardens. The plant is native to southern Europe and thrives in well-drained soils and full sun conditions. It is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant due to its hardiness and ornamental value.

Its foliage remains evergreen in mild climates, contributing to year-round visual interest.
