Eugenia manausensis

Eugenia manausensis, commonly known as the Manaus eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
