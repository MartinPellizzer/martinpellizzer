Ehretia australis

Ehretia australis, commonly known as the Australian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Australia and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. Its showy flowers are typically white or pale yellow, with five petals and a distinctive central stamen.

The plant produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds, contributing to its propagation in the wild.
