Erica glandulosa subsp. fourcadei

Erica glandulosa subsp.

fourcadei, commonly known as the Fourcade's heath, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, leathery leaves.

The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathland and woodland areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
