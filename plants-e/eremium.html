Eremium

Eremium, commonly known as the "desert bell," is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Asteraceae.

These plants are native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Namibia and South Africa. Eremium species are characterized by their succulent, cylindrical stems and showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. They are adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through thick cuticles and reduced leaf surfaces.

The genus includes several species, such as Eremium capense and Eremium strictum, which are valued for their ornamental appeal and ecological resilience.
