Elymus novae-angliae

Elymus novae-angliae, commonly known as New England wild rye, is a perennial grass species native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Elymus novae-angliae plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
