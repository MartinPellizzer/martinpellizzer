Escobaria henricksonii

Escobaria henricksonii, commonly known as the Henrickson's liveforever, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its compact, low-growing form with fleshy, ribbed stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the spring and summer months. It has adapted to arid environments with a deep root system and succulent leaves that store water.

Escobaria henricksonii is often found in rocky soils and is well-suited for xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
