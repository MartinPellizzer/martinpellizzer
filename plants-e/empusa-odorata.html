Empusa odorata

Empusa odorata, commonly known as the fragrant mistflower, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, aromatic blooms that typically appear in clusters during the warmer months. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. Its flowers are usually pale blue or white, with a subtle, sweet fragrance that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Empusa odorata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and pleasant scent.
