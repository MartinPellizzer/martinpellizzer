Euphorbia sinanensis

Euphorbia sinanensis, commonly known as the Sinan euphorbia, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its succulent, spiny stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually fleshy and alternate, while the flowers are clustered in a structure called a cyathium.

Euphorbia sinanensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry climates.
