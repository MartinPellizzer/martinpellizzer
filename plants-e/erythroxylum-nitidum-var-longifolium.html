Erythroxylum nitidum var. longifolium

Erythroxylum nitidum var. longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved copal tree, is a species native to Central America and northern South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green on the upper surface. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round berries containing a single seed.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
