Echinocereus palmeri subsp. mazapil

Echinocereus palmeri subsp.

mazapil, commonly known as the Mazapil cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to the United States and Mexico. It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are typically ribbed and covered in spines.

The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its striking appearance and resilience, it is often cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
