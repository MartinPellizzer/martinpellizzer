Eupatorium congestum var. tozziifolium

Eupatorium congestum var. tozziifolium, commonly known as the Tozzi's boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its gray-green, rough, and toothed leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in rich, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional herbal use.
