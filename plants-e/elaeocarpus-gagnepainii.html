Elaeocarpus gagnepainii

Elaeocarpus gagnepainii, commonly known as the Gagnepain's elaeocarp, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the tropical regions of Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink. Its fruit is a hard, woody capsule that contains several seeds, which are often dispersed by animals.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also studied for its potential ecological and medicinal properties.
