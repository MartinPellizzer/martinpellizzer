Euphorbia persepolitana

Euphorbia persepolitana, commonly known as the Persian spurge, is a flowering plant native to Iran.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, spiny stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are often reduced to small, scale-like structures, while its flowers are clustered in a structure called a cyathium.

Euphorbia persepolitana is also known for its milky sap, which can be toxic and is often used in traditional medicine.
