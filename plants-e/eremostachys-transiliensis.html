Eremostachys transiliensis

Eremostachys transiliensis, commonly known as the Transilian eremostachys, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white to yellow in color. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and xeriscapes.
