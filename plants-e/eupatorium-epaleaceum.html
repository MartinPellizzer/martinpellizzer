Eupatorium epaleaceum

Eupatorium epaleaceum, commonly known as white boneset, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist, sunny environments. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
