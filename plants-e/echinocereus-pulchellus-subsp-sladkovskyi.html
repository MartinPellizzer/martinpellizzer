Echinocereus pulchellus subsp. sladkovskyi

Echinocereus pulchellus subsp.

sladkovskyi, commonly known as the Sladkovsky's hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a columnar cactus with a distinctive rib structure and spines that are typically yellowish to reddish in color.

The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and early summer, which are often followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Due to its slow growth and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in arid gardens and rockeries.
