Echinospermum polymorphum var. aculeatum

Echinospermum polymorphum var. aculeatum, commonly known as the prickly dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, spiny stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by fluffy seed heads that aid in wind pollination. It thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
