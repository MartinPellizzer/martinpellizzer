Eupatorium ascendens

Eupatorium ascendens, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and meadows. The plant features slender, upright stems and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly rough texture.

Eupatorium ascendens is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
