Excoecaria africana

Excoecaria africana, commonly known as the African croton, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is characterized by its diverse species with often milky sap. The plant features alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a rough texture and a distinctive reddish or yellowish underside. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically greenish with yellow stamens, and it produces woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

The plant is sometimes used in traditional medicine, though it contains toxic compounds that require careful handling.
