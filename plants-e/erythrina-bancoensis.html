Erythrina bancoensis

Erythrina bancoensis, commonly known as the Banco flame tree, is a species of legume native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in dense clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its vibrant floral display.

The plant also has a shallow root system, making it suitable for areas with limited soil depth.
