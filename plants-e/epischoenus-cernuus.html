Epischoenus cernuus

Epischoenus cernuus, commonly known as the drooping sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its triangular, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown in color. This plant is often found in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Epischoenus cernuus has a fibrous root system and is adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

It plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various small organisms.
