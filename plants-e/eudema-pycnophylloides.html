Eudema pycnophylloides

Eudema pycnophylloides, commonly known as the dense-leaved eudema, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are densely packed and covered in fine, short hairs, giving it a compact appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in terminal inflorescences. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils, contributing to its resilience in harsh environments.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and wind-pollinated flowers.
