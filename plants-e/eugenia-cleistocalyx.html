Eugenia cleistocalyx

Eugenia cleistocalyx, commonly known as the common clove.

It is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Pacific Islands and parts of Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, reddish-brown flowers and small, edible berries. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy, dark green surface.

The tree is widely cultivated for its aromatic wood and medicinal properties.
