Erigeron breweri var. elmeri

Erigeron breweri var. elmeri, commonly known as Elmer's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate blue to purple flowers and narrow, hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, often in desert or scrubland environments. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Its small, daisy-like blooms appear in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
