Eragrostis divaricata

Eragrostis divaricata, commonly known as the slender weeping grass, is a perennial grass species native to various regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its drooping, fine-textured leaves and slender, wiry stems that give it a distinctive weeping appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and used in landscaping in many parts of the world.
