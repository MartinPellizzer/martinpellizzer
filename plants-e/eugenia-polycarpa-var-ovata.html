Eugenia polycarpa var. ovata

Eugenia polycarpa var. ovata, commonly known as the red-eyed wonder, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. Its fruit is a small, dark purple drupe that ripens in late summer.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
