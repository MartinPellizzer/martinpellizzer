Echinocactus setispinus var. hamatus

Echinocactus setispinus var. hamatus, commonly known as the hooked echinocactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem and prominent, spiny areoles that bear clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 10 to 30 centimeters and has a slow growth rate, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and cactus collections. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it thrives in well-draining soil with minimal water.

The variety 'hamatus' is distinguished by its hooked spines, which give it a unique appearance.
