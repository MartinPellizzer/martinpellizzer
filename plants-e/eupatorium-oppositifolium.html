Eupatorium oppositifolium

Eupatorium oppositifolium, commonly known as boneset, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its opposite, serrated leaves and clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. It blooms from late summer to early fall, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Boneset has a long history of use in traditional medicine for its purported fever-reducing properties.
