Elaeocarpus grandifolius

Elaeocarpus grandifolius, commonly known as the large-leaved elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green on top with a lighter underside. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental foliage and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
