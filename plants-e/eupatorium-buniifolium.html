Eupatorium buniifolium

Eupatorium buniifolium, commonly known as roughleaf thoroughwort, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants.

Eupatorium buniifolium is often found in wetlands and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil.
