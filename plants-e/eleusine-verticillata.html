Eleusine verticillata

Eleusine verticillata, commonly known as common fingergrass, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, cylindrical inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while the flowers are small and clustered in whorls.

Eleusine verticillata is sometimes considered a weed but has also been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
