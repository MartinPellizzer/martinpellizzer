Erigeron tenuis

Erigeron tenuis, commonly known as slender fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and prairies. It has slender, hairy stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer.

The leaves are narrow and often have a fuzzy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
