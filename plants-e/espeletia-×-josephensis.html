Espeletia × josephensis

Espeletia × josephensis, commonly known as the Joseph's heather, is a hybrid plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming structure and dense clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant is adapted to high-altitude environments, often growing in rocky or alpine conditions. Its foliage is typically covered in a thick layer of fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Espeletia × josephensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
