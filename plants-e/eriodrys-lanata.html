Eriodrys lanata

Eriodrys lanata, commonly known as the woolly eriocoma, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, woolly stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
