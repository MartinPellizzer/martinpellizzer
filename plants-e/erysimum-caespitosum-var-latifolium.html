Erysimum caespitosum var. latifolium

Erysimum caespitosum var. latifolium, commonly known as broadleaf wallflower, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and features dense clusters of bright yellow flowers. The leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
