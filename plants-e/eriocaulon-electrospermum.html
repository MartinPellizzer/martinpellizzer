Eriocaulon electrospermum

Eriocaulon electrospermum, commonly known as the electric seed grass, is a member of the Eriocaulaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous plant characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and tiny, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet, marshy environments, thriving in areas with high humidity and poor soil drainage. Its flowers are usually small and inconspicuous, with white or pale green petals, and it produces minute, wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
