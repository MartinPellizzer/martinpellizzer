Eryngium stenophyllum var. hassleri

Eryngium stenophyllum var. hassleri, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in a spherical inflorescence, which are surrounded by spiny bracts. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
