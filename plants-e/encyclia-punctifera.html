Encyclia punctifera

Encyclia punctifera, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, making it an epiphytic species. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

The flowers are borne on short, lateral branches and are known for their sweet, honey-like fragrance.
