Eulophia terrestris

Eulophia terrestris, commonly known as the terrestrial eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It features a sympodial growth habit with rhizomes and is characterized by its broad, grass-like leaves. The plant produces inflorescences with small, greenish or yellowish flowers that are often clustered together. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
