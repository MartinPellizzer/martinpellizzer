Endotropis crocea subsp. pirifolia

Endotropis crocea subsp.

pirifolia, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The subspecies is noted for its compact growth habit and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
