Euphorbia mairei var. luteociliata

Euphorbia mairei var. luteociliata, commonly known as the yellow-haired euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, yellowish hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice for succulent collections.
