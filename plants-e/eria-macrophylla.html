Eria macrophylla

Eria macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf eria, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its showy, fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and leathery, providing a striking visual contrast to its colorful flowers.

Eria macrophylla is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded, humid environments.
