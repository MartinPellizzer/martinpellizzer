Echinocereus clavatus

Echinocereus clavatus, commonly known as the bearded cactus or the clavate cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green to blue-green in color. The plant produces clusters of white to pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its spines are typically white or yellow, and it is well adapted to arid environments.

Echinocereus clavatus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
