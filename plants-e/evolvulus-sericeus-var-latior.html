Evolvulus sericeus var. latior

Evolvulus sericeus var. latior, commonly known as the broadleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its soft, silvery-green, hairy leaves and trumpet-shaped blue to purple flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant is a vigorous climber, often seen twining around supports in gardens and natural habitats. It produces small, round berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals and water.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant.
