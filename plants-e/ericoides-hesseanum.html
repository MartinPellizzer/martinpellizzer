Ericoides hesseanum

Ericoides hesseanum, commonly known as the Hesse's ericoid, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and cranberries. The plant is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Ericoides hesseanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
