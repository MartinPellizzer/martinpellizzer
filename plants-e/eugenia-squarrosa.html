Eugenia squarrosa

Eugenia squarrosa, commonly known as the squarrosa myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically reddish when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for coastal and subtropical gardens.

Eugenia squarrosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
