Elymus dasystachys var. parviflorus

Elymus dasystachys var. parviflorus, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, pale green to brown flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and steppe environments.

This variant is notable for its adaptability to harsh climates and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
