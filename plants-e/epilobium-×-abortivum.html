Epilobium × abortivum

Epilobium × abortivum, commonly known as the common willowherb, is a hybrid plant species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems that are often covered in soft, glandular hairs. The leaves are typically oval to lance-shaped, with a hairy surface and a slightly serrated margin. It produces showy, pink to purple flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn.

This plant thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in fields, along roadsides, and near water bodies.
