Euonymus latifolius subsp. latifolius

Euonymus latifolius subsp.

latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf woodbine, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America. It features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are attractive to birds. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, and it often grows in moist, wooded areas.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
