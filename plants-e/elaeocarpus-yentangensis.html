Elaeocarpus yentangensis

Elaeocarpus yentangensis, commonly known as the Yentang tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, white or pale pink flowers. The tree produces small, spherical fruits that are encased in a hard, woody shell. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological benefits.

The plant plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
