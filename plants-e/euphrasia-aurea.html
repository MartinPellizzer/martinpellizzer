Euphrasia aurea

Euphrasia aurea, commonly known as the golden eyebright, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has hairy, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly rough texture. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream sides.

Euphrasia aurea is often found in moist soils and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
