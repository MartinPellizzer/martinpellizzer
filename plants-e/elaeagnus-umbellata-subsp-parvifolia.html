Elaeagnus umbellata subsp. parvifolia

Elaeagnus umbellata subsp.

parvifolia, commonly known as silverberry or sand cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia. It features small, silvery-white leaves that are covered in fine, hair-like structures, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, edible berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

This subspecies is also valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various climates.
