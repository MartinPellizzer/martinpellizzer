Eucalyptus × auburnensis

Eucalyptus × auburnensis, commonly known as the Auburn gum, is a hybrid species native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, often grey or white bark that peels in strips, and its lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, globular clusters of white or pale yellow flowers, which bloom during the spring and summer seasons. It is a fast-growing tree that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for various landscaping applications.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and urban environments.
