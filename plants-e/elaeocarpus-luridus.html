Elaeocarpus luridus

Elaeocarpus luridus, commonly known as the red-flowered elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, red-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Elaeocarpus luridus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its foliage and floral display.
