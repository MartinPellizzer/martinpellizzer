Eupatorium haughtii

Eupatorium haughtii, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist woodlands and prairies. The plant features showy, blue to purple flower clusters that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

Eupatorium haughtii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
