Eragrostis lutensis

Eragrostis lutensis, commonly known as the Lutian grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and compact, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, two-lobed spikelets.

Eragrostis lutensis is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient plant in harsh climates.
