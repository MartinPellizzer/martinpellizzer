Elaeagnus conferta var. maculata

Elaeagnus conferta var. maculata, commonly known as the spotted silverberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its silvery-green foliage with distinctive reddish-brown spots on the leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that are typically orange to red in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance.

This variety is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment.
