Eupatorium truncatum

Eupatorium truncatum, commonly known as New England Hempweed, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and wetlands. The plant features opposite, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered white to pale purple flowers. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its common name reflects its historical use as a weed in agricultural areas.
