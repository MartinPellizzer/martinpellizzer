Eupatorium purpureum f. dubium

Eupatorium purpureum f. dubium, commonly known as the purple boneset, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, purple to pinkish flowers. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and traditional medicinal contexts.
