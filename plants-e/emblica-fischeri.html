Emblica fischeri

Emblica fischeri, commonly known as the Fischer's Indian gooseberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically orange-red when mature, containing a single seed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
