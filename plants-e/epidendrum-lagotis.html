Epidendrum lagotis

Epidendrum lagotis, commonly known as the Mexican orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its clustered, pendulous inflorescences. The plant produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink, often with a distinctive red or orange lip. It thrives in humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining potting media.

Epidendrum lagotis is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.
