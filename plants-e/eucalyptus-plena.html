Eucalyptus plena

Eucalyptus plena, commonly known as the showy gum, is a species of eucalyptus native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fluffy white flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a showy appearance. The plant has smooth, peeling bark that ranges from grey to white in color. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a bluish-green hue.

Eucalyptus plena is typically found in open woodlands and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
