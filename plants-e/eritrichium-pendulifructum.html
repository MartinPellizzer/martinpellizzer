Eritrichium pendulifructum

Eritrichium pendulifructum, commonly known as the hanging fruit eritrichium, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces delicate, blue-violet flowers in early spring. The plant features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its unique characteristic is the elongated, pendulous fruiting structures that give it its common name.

Eritrichium pendulifructum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
