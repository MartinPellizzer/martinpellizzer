Eruca tenuifolia

Eruca tenuifolia, commonly known as slender garden cress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard family plants. The plant is characterized by its low-growing habit, with small, edible leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are often used in salads or as a garnish due to their peppery flavor.

The plant thrives in cool climates and is often cultivated in gardens for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
