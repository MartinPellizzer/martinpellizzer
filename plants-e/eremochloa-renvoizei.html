Eremochloa renvoizei

Eremochloa renvoizei, commonly known as the Bornean needlegrass, is a grass species native to Southeast Asia, particularly Borneo.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and is often found in areas with high humidity.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is noted for its ecological significance in maintaining soil stability in its native habitat.
