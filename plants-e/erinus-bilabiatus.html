Erinus bilabiatus

Erinus bilabiatus, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is typically shrub-like, with a compact growth habit and is often used in ornamental gardening. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Erinus bilabiatus is known for its long-lasting blooms and is relatively low maintenance once established.
