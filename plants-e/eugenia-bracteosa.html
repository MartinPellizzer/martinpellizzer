Eugenia bracteosa

Eugenia bracteosa, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces yellowish-orange berries that are edible and have a tangy flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Eugenia bracteosa is also valued in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
