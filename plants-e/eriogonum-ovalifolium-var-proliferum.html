Eriogonum ovalifolium var. proliferum

Eriogonum ovalifolium var. proliferum, commonly known as the Sierra buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and meadows. It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with similar structural features.

Its adaptability to arid conditions makes it an important component of local ecosystems.
