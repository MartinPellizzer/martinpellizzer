Euphorbia hillebrandii var. palikeana

Euphorbia hillebrandii var. palikeana, commonly known as the Hillebrand's spurge, is a succulent shrub native to Madagascar.

It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a cluster called a cyathium, which is typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
