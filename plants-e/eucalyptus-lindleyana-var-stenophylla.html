Eucalyptus lindleyana var. stenophylla

Eucalyptus lindleyana var. stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf stringybark, is a species native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and smooth, grey bark that peels in strips. The plant typically grows in open forests and woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, globular flower clusters that are usually white or pink in color.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and is often used in horticultural settings for its ornamental value.
