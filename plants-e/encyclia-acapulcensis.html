Encyclia acapulcensis

Encyclia acapulcensis, commonly known as the Acapulco orchid, is a species of orchid native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Epidendroideae subfamily and is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange, typically during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Encyclia acapulcensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
