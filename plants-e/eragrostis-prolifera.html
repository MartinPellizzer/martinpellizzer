Eragrostis prolifera

Eragrostis prolifera, commonly known as the spreading lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often branch from the base. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, pale green to blue-gray flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Eragrostis prolifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
