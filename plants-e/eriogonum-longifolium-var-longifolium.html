Eriogonum longifolium var. longifolium

Eriogonum longifolium var. longifolium, commonly known as the longleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It is adapted to arid conditions and has a deep root system for water absorption.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil.
