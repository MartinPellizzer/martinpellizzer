Epidendrum buchii

Epidendrum buchii, commonly known as the Buch's Epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments within forest understories. The plant features erect, grass-like stems with numerous clustered, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually small and borne in dense inflorescences, often displaying a range of colors from white to pink and purple.

Epidendrum buchii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
