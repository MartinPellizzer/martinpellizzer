Eupatorium crassifolium

Eupatorium crassifolium, commonly known as roughleaf boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has rough, hairy leaves that are lance-shaped and often have a strong, bitter odor. Its flowering stems bear small, white to pale blue flower heads that bloom in late summer.

Eupatorium crassifolium is part of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its medicinal properties used by indigenous peoples.
