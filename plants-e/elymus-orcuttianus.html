Elymus orcuttianus

Elymus orcuttianus, commonly known as Orcutt's needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in panicles, with small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Elymus orcuttianus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
