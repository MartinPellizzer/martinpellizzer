Ecliptica alba

Ecliptica alba, commonly known as false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble dandelions. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny environments and has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in clusters, followed by white or pale green seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Ecliptica alba is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
