Eranthemum sinuatum

Eranthemum sinuatum, commonly known as the velvetleaf or silverleaf plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, waxy leaves that often have a silvery or velvety texture due to the presence of fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Eranthemum sinuatum is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is used in horticulture for its unique foliage.
