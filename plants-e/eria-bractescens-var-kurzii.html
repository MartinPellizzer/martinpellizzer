Eria bractescens var. kurzii

Eria bractescens var. kurzii, commonly known as the Kurz's erythrina, is a tropical climbing plant characterized by its slender, woody stems and compound leaves.

It produces vibrant red and orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

The fruit is a pod that splits open to release seeds, facilitating its dispersal in natural habitats.
