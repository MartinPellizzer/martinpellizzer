Eleocharis hildebrandtii

Eleocharis hildebrandtii, commonly known as the Hildebrandt's spikerush, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and spike-like inflorescences.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and pond edges, where it thrives in moist to water-saturated soils. The plant has slender, triangular stems that are often reddish in color, and its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in dense clusters at the tips of the stems. Eleocharis hildebrandtii plays a vital role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, contributing to the ecological structure of its habitat.
