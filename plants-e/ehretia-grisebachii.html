Ehretia grisebachii

Ehretia grisebachii, commonly known as the gray-leafed ehretia, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often grayish-green in color. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
