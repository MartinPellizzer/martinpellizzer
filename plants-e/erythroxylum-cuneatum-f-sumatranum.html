Erythroxylum cuneatum f. sumatranum

Erythroxylum cuneatum f. sumatranum, commonly known as the Sumatran coca, is a botanical variant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, cuneate leaves with a distinctive reddish hue on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that give way to oblong, greenish-brown capsules containing seeds. It is notable for its potential use in traditional medicine and its role in the local ecosystem.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
