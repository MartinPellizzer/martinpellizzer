Erica azorica var. chlorantha

Erica azorica var. chlorantha, commonly known as the Azorean heather, is a flowering plant native to the Azores.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in coastal or rocky habitats. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, and the flowers can be pink, white, or purple, depending on the variety.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging environments.
