Encyclia glumacea

Encyclia glumacea, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which are often overlooked due to their subdued color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Encyclia glumacea is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
