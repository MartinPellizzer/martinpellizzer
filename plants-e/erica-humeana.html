Erica humeana

Erica humeana, commonly known as the Highland heather, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Scotland and the Isle of Man.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers, blueberries, and azaleas. This evergreen shrub typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist climates. Its small, bell-shaped flowers are usually pink or purple, blooming in late summer.

Erica humeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to colonize rocky or barren landscapes.
