Escallonia virgata

Escallonia virgata, commonly known as the evergreen honeysuckle, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Chile.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but not typically for humans. Escallonia virgata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is also known for its aromatic leaves, which release a pleasant scent when crushed.
