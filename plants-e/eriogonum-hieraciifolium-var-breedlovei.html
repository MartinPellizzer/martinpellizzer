Eriogonum hieraciifolium var. breedlovei

Eriogonum hieraciifolium var. breedlovei, commonly known as the Breddle's buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, clustered white or pale pink flowers. This variety is typically found in arid and semi-arid environments, such as rocky soils and open plains. The plant is adapted to drought conditions and thrives in full sun.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small wildlife.
