Erica ingeana

Erica ingeana, commonly known as the Cape snowbush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Erica ingeana is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
