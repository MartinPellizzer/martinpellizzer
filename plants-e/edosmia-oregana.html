Edosmia oregana

Edosmia oregana, commonly known as the Oregon aster, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. It features showy purple to blue flowers that bloom in the fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinct appearance.
