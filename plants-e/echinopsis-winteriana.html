Echinopsis winteriana

Echinopsis winteriana, commonly known as the Winter's Thorn, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem and clusters of white or pale blue flowers that bloom during the cooler months. The plant has spiny areoles and is adapted to survive in low-water environments. Its roots are shallow, allowing it to quickly absorb surface moisture.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in dry conditions.
