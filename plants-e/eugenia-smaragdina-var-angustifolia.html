Eugenia smaragdina var. angustifolia

Eugenia smaragdina var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed eugenia, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree, typically reaching heights of up to 6 meters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, with a slightly aromatic scent. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, bright red berries.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit, which is used in local cuisines.
