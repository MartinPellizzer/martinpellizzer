Erythrospermum hypoleucum

Erythrospermum hypoleucum, commonly known as the white-flowered erythrospermum, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subtropical regions.

Erythrospermum hypoleucum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in horticulture for its attractive blooms and hardy nature.
