Erica octophylla

Erica octophylla, commonly known as the eight-leaf heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, needle-like leaves. It produces vibrant pink or purple flowers during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Erica octophylla is well adapted to acidic, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.
