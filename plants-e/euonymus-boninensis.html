Euonymus boninensis

Euonymus boninensis, commonly known as the Bonin euonymus, is a flowering plant native to the Bonin Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, with both male and female flowers present on the same plant.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.
