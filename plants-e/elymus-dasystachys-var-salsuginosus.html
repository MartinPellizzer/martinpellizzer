Elymus dasystachys var. salsuginosus

Elymus dasystachys var. salsuginosus, commonly known as the saltgrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of salt crystals. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that contain small, wind-pollinated flowers. It is highly adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it an important component of desert ecosystems.

Its ability to thrive in harsh conditions makes it valuable for soil stabilization and habitat provision.
