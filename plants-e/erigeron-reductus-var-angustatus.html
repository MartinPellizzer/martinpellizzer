Erigeron reductus var. angustatus

Erigeron reductus var. angustatus, commonly known as narrowleaf blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its narrow, linear leaves and blue to purple flowers are adapted to arid environments.

It is often found in regions with low precipitation and is valued for its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
