Echites pilosus

Echites pilosus, commonly known as the hairy echites, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Echites pilosus is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
