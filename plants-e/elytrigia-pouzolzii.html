Elytrigia pouzolzii

Elytrigia pouzolzii, commonly known as the alpine fescue, is a grass species native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically thrives in high-altitude environments, where it forms dense mats on rocky soils. Elytrigia pouzolzii is adapted to cold climates and can withstand harsh weather conditions.

Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences that produce numerous tiny seeds.
