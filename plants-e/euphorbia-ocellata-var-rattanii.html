Euphorbia ocellata var. rattanii

Euphorbia ocellata var. rattanii, commonly known as the rattan euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow cyathia, which are the specialized flower structures typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
