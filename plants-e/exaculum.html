Exaculum

Exaculum, commonly known as the marsh helleborine, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, pleated leaves that often form a rosette at the base. The plant produces showy, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinct three-lobed lip, which are typically found in wet or marshy habitats. Exaculum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
