Echinopsis schreiteri

Echinopsis schreiteri, commonly known as the Schreiter's moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its short, columnar stems and clustered flowers. The plant produces white or pale pink flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It is a slow-growing plant that requires well-drained soil and minimal water to thrive.

Due to its striking appearance and unique blooming pattern, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
