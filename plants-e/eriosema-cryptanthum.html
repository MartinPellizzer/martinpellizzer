Eriosema cryptanthum

Eriosema cryptanthum, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a South African plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that are often mistaken for being covered in a fine layer of dust. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is typically found in mountainous regions.

Eriosema cryptanthum is known for its unique morphology and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
