Erythronium umbilicatum

Erythronium umbilicatum, commonly known as the white dogwood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features a short, sturdy rhizome and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. It thrives in moist, woodland environments and is often found in rich, well-drained soils.

Erythronium umbilicatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
