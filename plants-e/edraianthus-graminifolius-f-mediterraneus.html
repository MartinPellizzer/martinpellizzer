Edraianthus graminifolius f. mediterraneus

Edraianthus graminifolius f. mediterraneus, commonly known as the Mediterranean grass-leaved edraianthus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a desirable addition to Mediterranean-style gardens.
