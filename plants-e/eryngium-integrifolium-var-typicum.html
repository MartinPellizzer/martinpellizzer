Eryngium integrifolium var. typicum

Eryngium integrifolium var. typicum, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue or purple flower heads surrounded by stiff, silver-green bracts. The plant has lance-shaped, entire leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for coastal gardens.

Its unique appearance and ability to attract pollinators make it a valuable addition to ecological landscapes.
