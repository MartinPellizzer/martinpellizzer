Echinochloa holciformis

Echinochloa holciformis, commonly known as the rigid false brome, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and roadsides.

E. holciformis is considered an invasive species in some regions due to its rapid growth and competitive nature.
