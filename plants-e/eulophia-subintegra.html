Eulophia subintegra

Eulophia subintegra, commonly known as the slender eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a pseudobulbous stem with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces racemes of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers with distinctive lip structures. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Eulophia subintegra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under suitable conditions.
