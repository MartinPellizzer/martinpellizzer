Erica caduca

Erica caduca, commonly known as the evergreen heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late summer. Erica caduca thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to form dense thickets.
