Entelea

Entelea, commonly known as the "giant water lily," is a genus of aquatic plants belonging to the family Araceae.

It is characterized by its large, floating leaves that can grow up to 3 meters in diameter, providing habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant's flowers are typically yellow and are enclosed within a spadix, a feature common in the Araceae family. Entelea species are native to tropical regions of the Americas and are often found in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats.
