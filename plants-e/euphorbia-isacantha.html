Euphorbia isacantha

Euphorbia isacantha, commonly known as the Cape Hottentot, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It features branching stems covered in small, spiny tubercles, and its leaves are typically small and fleshy. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is adapted to arid environments and stores water in its thick stems.

Euphorbia isacantha is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
