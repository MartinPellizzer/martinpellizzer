Echium albicans

Echium albicans, commonly known as the white thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers. The plant produces a distinctive, hollow seed stalk that becomes more prominent as the flowers mature. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Echium albicans is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to thrive in coastal and arid environments.
