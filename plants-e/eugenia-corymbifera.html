Eugenia corymbifera

Eugenia corymbifera, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or guava, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in vitamin C and have a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

The tree is also valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional crafts and furniture making.
