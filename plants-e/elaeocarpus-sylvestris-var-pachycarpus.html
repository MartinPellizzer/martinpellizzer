Elaeocarpus sylvestris var. pachycarpus

Elaeocarpus sylvestris var. pachycarpus, commonly known as the thick-fruited elaeocarpus, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, thick, woody fruits that typically contain a single seed. The leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a glossy appearance. The plant belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is known for its ornamental value.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland evergreen forests.
