Erythroxylum setosum

Erythroxylum setosum, commonly known as the prickly copaiba, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

It is often found in moist, shaded areas of tropical forests and is valued for its aromatic resins and medicinal properties.
