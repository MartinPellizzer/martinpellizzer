Eucalyptus persicifolia

Eucalyptus persicifolia, commonly known as the pink bloodwood, is a small to medium-sized tree native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale grey bark that peels in thin, flaky layers. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the winter months. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a bluish-green hue.

Eucalyptus persicifolia is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
