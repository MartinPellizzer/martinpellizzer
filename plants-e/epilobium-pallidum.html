Epilobium pallidum

Epilobium pallidum, commonly known as the pale willow herb, is a flowering plant native to Eurasia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks. The plant features slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are pink to reddish in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Epilobium pallidum is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain insect species.
