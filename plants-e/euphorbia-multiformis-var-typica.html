Euphorbia multiformis var. typica

Euphorbia multiformis var. typica, commonly known as the variable spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, branching stems and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that can be irritating to the skin. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its adaptability and unique appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
