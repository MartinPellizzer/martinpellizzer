Epilobium pyrricholophum var. curvatopilosum

Epilobium pyrricholophum var. curvatopilosum, commonly known as the woolly willow or silky willow, is a species of flowering plant in the evening primrose family.

It is characterized by its erect or decumbent stems covered in dense, silky hairs. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and also bear a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This plant is often found in moist habitats such as streambanks and wet meadows, where it thrives in partially shaded to sunny conditions.
