Eleocharis quinqueflora var. suksdorfiana

Eleocharis quinqueflora var. suksdorfiana, commonly known as the Saksdorf's spikerush, is a flowering plant species native to wetland environments in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescence composed of numerous small, spike-like clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or damp soil, thriving in marshes and pond margins. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into dark brown, spherical fruits.

This variant is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
