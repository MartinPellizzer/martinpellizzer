Espeletia arbelaezii

Espeletia arbelaezii, commonly known as the Alba Mountain heather, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of Colombia.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and is adapted to cold, dry conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, gray-green leaves that help reduce water loss.

Espeletia arbelaezii plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing habitat and nectar for local pollinators.
