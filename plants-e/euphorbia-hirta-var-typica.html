Euphorbia hirta var. typica

Euphorbia hirta var. typica, commonly known as greater spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems covered in fine hairs and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers clustered in cyathia, which are surrounded by leaf-like bracts. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Euphorbia hirta var. typica is also known for its latex, which can be irritating to the skin.
