Eupatorium marrubiifolium

Eupatorium marrubiifolium, commonly known as sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a fragrant aroma when crushed.

Eupatorium marrubiifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
