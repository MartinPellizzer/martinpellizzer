Erica salisburia

Erica salisburia, commonly known as the Salisburia heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, leathery leaves and is known for its resilience to drought conditions.

Erica salisburia is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
