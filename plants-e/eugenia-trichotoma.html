Eugenia trichotoma

Eugenia trichotoma, commonly known as the black mangosteen, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces edible, purple-black berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart, sweet flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical rainforests.

The botanical features of Eugenia trichotoma make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
