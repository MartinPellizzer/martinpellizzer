Enneapogon glaber

Enneapogon glaber, commonly known as the smooth false grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale blue flowers. It is typically found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
