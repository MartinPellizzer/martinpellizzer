Eucalyptus kakadu

Eucalyptus kakadu, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallee, is a species of eucalyptus native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish bloom. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically pale green or yellow, which attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. Its wood is strong and durable, making it valuable for both traditional and modern uses.

Eucalyptus kakadu plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
