Elsholtzia ochroleuca var. parvifolia

Elsholtzia ochroleuca var. parvifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved elsholtzia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. The plant features aromatic, gray-green leaves that are narrow and lance-shaped, with a distinctive silvery underside. Its small, yellowish flowers are clustered in dense, terminal inflorescences, blooming in late summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
