Eriogonum nudicaule

Eriogonum nudicaule, commonly known as the naked-stemmed buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features simple, hairy stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Eriogonum nudicaule plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.
