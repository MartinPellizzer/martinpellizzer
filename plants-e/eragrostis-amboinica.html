Eragrostis amboinica

Eragrostis amboinica, commonly known as Amboina grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in open areas, such as disturbed soils and grasslands, and is adapted to warm climates. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle with spikelets that produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Eragrostis amboinica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a forage crop.
