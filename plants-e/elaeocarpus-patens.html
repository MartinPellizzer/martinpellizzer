Elaeocarpus patens

Elaeocarpus patens, commonly known as the broad-leaved elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by spherical, woody fruits. Its ornamental value is due to its attractive foliage and potential use in horticulture.

However, it is not widely cultivated due to its specific habitat requirements.
