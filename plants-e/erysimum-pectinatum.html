Erysimum pectinatum

Erysimum pectinatum, commonly known as the yellow wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces vibrant yellow flowers in early spring. The plant features pinnately lobed leaves and has a compact growth habit, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, and it is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
