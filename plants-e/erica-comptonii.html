Erica comptonii

Erica comptonii, commonly known as the Compton's heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica comptonii typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and as a rockery plant.
