Endopogon

Endopogon, commonly known as the bottlebrush grass, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family.

It is characterized by its distinctive spikelets that resemble bottle brushes, giving it its common name. These plants are typically found in arid or semi-arid regions, where they thrive in sandy or gravelly soils. Endopogon species are often used in restoration projects due to their ability to stabilize soils and prevent erosion.

Their small, wind-pollinated flowers contribute to their ecological role in supporting pollinators and maintaining biodiversity.
