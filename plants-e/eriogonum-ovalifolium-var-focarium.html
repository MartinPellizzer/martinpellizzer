Eriogonum ovalifolium var. focarium

Eriogonum ovalifolium var. focarium, commonly known as the Sierra buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, such as slopes and meadows. It is part of the Polygonaceae family and is known for its drought tolerance.

The Sierra buckwheat plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its native range.
