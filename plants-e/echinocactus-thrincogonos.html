Echinocactus thrincogonos

Echinocactus thrincogonos, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its globular shape and a spiny, segmented exterior that provides protection against herbivores. The plant has a dense covering of white, bristly hairs that help reduce water loss in arid environments. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing in late spring and attracting pollinators like bees and birds.

Echinocactus thrincogonos is well adapted to drought conditions and thrives in full sunlight with minimal water requirements.
