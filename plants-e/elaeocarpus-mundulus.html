Elaeocarpus mundulus

Elaeocarpus mundulus, commonly known as the mountain elaeocarpus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, which are often dispersed by animals.

Elaeocarpus mundulus is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
