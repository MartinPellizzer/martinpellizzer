Erythrococca columnaris

Erythrococca columnaris, commonly known as the red column spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, columnar growth habit and produces bright red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has smooth, waxy leaves that are typically green with a slight reddish tinge. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

However, it is toxic to both humans and animals, requiring caution in cultivation.
