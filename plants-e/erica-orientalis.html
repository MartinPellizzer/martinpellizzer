Erica orientalis

Erica orientalis, commonly known as the oriental heath, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form and its ability to thrive in well-drained, acidic soils. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in spring. Erica orientalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

It is also noted for its hardiness and drought tolerance.
