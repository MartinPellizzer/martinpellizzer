Eupatorium occidentale

Eupatorium occidentale, commonly known as boneset, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and clusters of small, pale blue or white flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive, aromatic scent.

Boneset is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported fever-reducing properties.
