Elaeocarpus angustifolius

Elaeocarpus angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The tree produces hard, woody fruits that contain a single, large seed, which is often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological importance.

The plant is also noted for its role in supporting local biodiversity.
