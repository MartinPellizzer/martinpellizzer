Euphorbia laikipiensis

Euphorbia laikipiensis, commonly known as the Laikipia euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to Kenya.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces unique cyathia, which are cup-like structures containing the female flower and male flowers. It is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its stems to survive in dry conditions.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
