Epacris barbata

Epacris barbata, commonly known as the hairy epacris, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its upright, woody stems and dense clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, are often visited by bees and other pollinators.

Epacris barbata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
