Erythrochiton hypophyllanthus

Erythrochiton hypophyllanthus, commonly known as the red-leaved erythrochiton, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange leaves, which are often variegated with yellow or green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. Its leaves are thick and leathery, adapted to withstand periods of drought.

Erythrochiton hypophyllanthus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
