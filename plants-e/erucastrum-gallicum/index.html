Erucastrum Gallicum

Erucastrum gallicum is a lesser-known member of the Brassicaceae family native to southern Europe. This small, flowering plant typically grows in rocky or disturbed soils and thrives in sunny environments. Its delicate yellow flowers bloom in late spring, adding a subtle touch of color to the landscape. Though not widely cultivated, it holds ecological value by supporting pollinators and contributing to biodiversity. Its historical use in traditional medicine highlights its potential for further scientific exploration.
