Eucalyptopsis alauda

Eucalyptopsis alauda, commonly known as the silver-leafed eucalyptus, is a small to medium-sized tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage that provides a striking visual contrast. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. Its bark is smooth and often peels in thin, papery layers, a common feature among many eucalyptus species.

Eucalyptopsis alauda is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in dry, open habitats.
