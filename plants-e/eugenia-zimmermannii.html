Eugenia zimmermannii

Eugenia zimmermannii, commonly known as the pink myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red or purple when mature. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Eugenia zimmermannii is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
