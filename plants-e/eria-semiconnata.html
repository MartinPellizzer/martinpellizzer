Eria semiconnata

Eria semiconnata, commonly known as the semi-connected orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale yellow. The plant prefers shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest undergrowth. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Eria semiconnata is valued in horticulture for its delicate appearance and is relatively easy to cultivate with proper care.
