Eupatorium azureum

Eupatorium azureum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-blue foliage and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that appear in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its showy inflorescences. Eupatorium azureum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
