Erica colleter

Erica colleter, commonly known as the coastal heath, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related plants. The plant is characterized by its dense, low-growing shrub form with small, evergreen leaves. Erica colleter produces clusters of pink or white flowers in spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to coastal conditions with high salinity and wind exposure.
