Epicampes expansa

Epicampes expansa, commonly known as the broadleaf sunflower, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has alternating, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
