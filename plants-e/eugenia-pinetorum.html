Eugenia pinetorum

Eugenia pinetorum, commonly known as the pineapple guava, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to pineapples, hence its common name. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Eugenia pinetorum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of flavorful fruit.
