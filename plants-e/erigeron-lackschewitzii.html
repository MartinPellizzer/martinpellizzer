Erigeron lackschewitzii

Erigeron lackschewitzii, commonly known as the desert blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations such as hairy leaves to reduce water loss. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is often found in sandy soils and is considered a hardy, drought-tolerant plant.
