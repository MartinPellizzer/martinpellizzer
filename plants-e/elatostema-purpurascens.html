Elatostema purpurascens

Elatostema purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-stemmed elatostema, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features slender, ascending stems that are often tinged with purple, giving it its common name. Its small, opposite leaves are usually ovate to elliptical in shape and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Elatostema purpurascens produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are often followed by three-lobed, papery fruits.
