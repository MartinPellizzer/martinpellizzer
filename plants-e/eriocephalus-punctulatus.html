Eriocephalus punctulatus

Eriocephalus punctulatus, commonly known as the African dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dandelion-like appearance with yellow flowers that turn into fluffy white seed heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas and is often found in grasslands and coastal regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are erect and hairy.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic features.
