Elaeocarpus populneus

Elaeocarpus populneus, commonly known as the white sand elaeocarpus, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey-barked trunk and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that are typically yellow or red in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited economic use.
