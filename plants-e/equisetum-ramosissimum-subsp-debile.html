Equisetum ramosissimum subsp. debile

Equisetum ramosissimum subsp.

debile, commonly known as the slender horsetail, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its jointed, hollow stems and whorled arrangement of small, needle-like leaves. It belongs to the family Equisetaceae and is native to regions of North America, particularly in the western United States. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its spore-producing structures, called strobila, are borne at the tips of the stems and are a distinctive feature of the species.
