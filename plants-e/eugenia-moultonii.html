Eugenia moultonii

Eugenia moultonii, commonly known as the Moulton's eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. After flowering, it produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
