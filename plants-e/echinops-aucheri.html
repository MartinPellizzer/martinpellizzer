Echinops aucheri

Echinops aucheri, commonly known as the Persian globe thistle, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a spiny, spherical seed head that becomes more prominent as the plant matures, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces blue to purple flowers in early summer, which are followed by the characteristic spiky seed heads. Echinops aucheri thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a valuable addition to xeriscaped gardens.
