Echinops shakrokii

Echinops shakrokii, commonly known as the Shakrokii thistle, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, spherical crown and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are usually gray-green and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Echinops shakrokii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
