Eragrostis amabilis var. scabriuscula

Eragrostis amabilis var. scabriuscula, commonly known as silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass species characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and tufted growth habit.

It typically grows in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grassy areas. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, two-lobed spikelets. Its leaves are often covered with short, fine hairs, giving it a distinctive, silky texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a range of environmental conditions.
