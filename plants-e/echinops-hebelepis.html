Echinops hebelepis

Echinops hebelepis, commonly known as the spiked centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble small, compact cones. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny environments such as rocky soils and gravelly plains. Its flowers are small and blue to violet in color, appearing in clusters during the summer months.

Echinops hebelepis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its distinctive appearance and drought tolerance.
