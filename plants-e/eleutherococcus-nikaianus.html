Eleutherococcus nikaianus

Eleutherococcus nikaianus, commonly known as Siberian ginseng, is a deciduous shrub native to the forests of eastern Russia and northern China.

It belongs to the family Cinaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces red berries that contain seeds, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is valued for its adaptogenic properties and is often used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
