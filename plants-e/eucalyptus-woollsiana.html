Eucalyptus woollsiana

Eucalyptus woollsiana, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallee, is a tall, multi-stemmed shrub or tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped, silver-green leaves. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. Its aromatic oils have been used in traditional medicine and for their antiseptic properties.

Eucalyptus woollsiana is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy soils.
