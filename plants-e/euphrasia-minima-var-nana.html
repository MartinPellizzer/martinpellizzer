Euphrasia minima var. nana

Euphrasia minima var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf eyebright, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It typically reaches heights of less than 10 centimeters, with a compact, mat-like growth habit. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers with prominent dark markings, which are characteristic of the genus Euphrasia. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine, soft hairs.

This species thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
