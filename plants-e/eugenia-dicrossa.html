Eugenia dicrossa

Eugenia dicrossa, commonly known as the African star apple, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible, yellow or orange berries that are rich in vitamin C and have a sweet, tart flavor. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.

The tree is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
