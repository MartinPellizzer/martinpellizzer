Elephantopus scaber var. argenteus

Elephantopus scaber var. argenteus, commonly known as silverleaf elephantopus, is a variant of the hairy elephantopus species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-white underside on the leaves, which gives it its distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in grasslands and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are hairy and have a lobed shape, while the flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters.

This variant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique foliage.
