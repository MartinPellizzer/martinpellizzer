Erithalis quadrangularis

Erithalis quadrangularis, commonly known as the Indian borage or false borage, is a shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its square or quadrangular stems, which are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly rough texture.

Erithalis quadrangularis is often found in moist, shaded areas and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
