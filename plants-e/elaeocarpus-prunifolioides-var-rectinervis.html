Elaeocarpus prunifolioides var. rectinervis

Elaeocarpus prunifolioides var. rectinervis, commonly known as the broad-leaved elaeocarpus, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with elliptical, leathery leaves that have a distinctive broad midrib. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that are typically yellow or red when mature. Its leaves are characterized by their prominent venation, which is a key identifying feature.

This species is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential ecological importance.
