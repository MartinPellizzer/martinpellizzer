Elatine californica var. williamsii

Elatine californica var. williamsii, commonly known as the California waterwort, is a small aquatic plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds, streams, and wet meadows. The plant has slender, branched stems with small, opposite leaves that are often submerged or floating. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually appearing in clusters near the water's surface.

It plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and invertebrates.
