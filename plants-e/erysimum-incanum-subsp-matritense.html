Erysimum incanum subsp. matritense

Erysimum incanum subsp.

matritense, commonly known as the Matritense Wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers.

The plant features grayish-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
