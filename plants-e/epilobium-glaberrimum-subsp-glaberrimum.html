Epilobium glaberrimum subsp. glaberrimum

Epilobium glaberrimum subsp.

glaberrimum, commonly known as the smooth willowherb, is a herbaceous plant native to western North America. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are usually smooth and glabrous.

The flowers are typically pink to reddish, arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, and bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as stream banks, marshes, and wet meadows.

This species is often found in the Pacific Northwest and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
