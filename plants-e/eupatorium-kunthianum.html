Eupatorium kunthianum

Eupatorium kunthianum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodland edges and wetlands. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its most distinctive characteristic is its clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

Eupatorium kunthianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
