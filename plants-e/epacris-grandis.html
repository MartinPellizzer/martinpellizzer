Epacris grandis

Epacris grandis, commonly known as the large-leaf epacris, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of pink or red flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Epacris grandis thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
