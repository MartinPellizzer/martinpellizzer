Eriocapitella japonica

Eriocapitella japonica, commonly known as Japanese velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
