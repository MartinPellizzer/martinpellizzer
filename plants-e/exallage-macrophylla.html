Exallage macrophylla

Exallage macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf exallage, is a striking plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white flowers with a subtle pinkish tinge, which bloom during the wet season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and collectors.
