Eragrostis maderaspatana

Eragrostis maderaspatana, commonly known as the Indian false rice, is a grass species native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks.

Botanically, it is valued for its adaptability and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
