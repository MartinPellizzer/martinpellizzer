Ericoides cinereum

Ericoides cinereum, commonly known as the silver moss cushion, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant that thrives in cool, moist environments.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae, which includes heathers and other ericaceous plants. The plant is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that ranges from silver to gray-green in color. Ericoides cinereum typically grows on rocky substrates or in shaded forest floors, where it forms compact cushions.

Its botanical structure is adapted to retain moisture and withstand harsh environmental conditions.
