Eugenia egensis var. parvifolia

Eugenia egensis var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved wax apple, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, yellowish-orange fruits with a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of fruit in home gardens.
