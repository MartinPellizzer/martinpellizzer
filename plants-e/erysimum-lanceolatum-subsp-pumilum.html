Erysimum lanceolatum subsp. pumilum

Erysimum lanceolatum subsp.

pumilum, commonly known as the dwarf wallflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture.

The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking hardy, drought-tolerant plants.
