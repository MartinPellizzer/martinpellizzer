Euonymus monbeigii

Euonymus monbeigii, commonly known as the Chinese burning bush, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its vibrant red to orange foliage in the fall, which makes it a popular ornamental plant. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that turn strikingly colorful during autumn. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by orange-red berries that persist through the winter.

Euonymus monbeigii is relatively low maintenance and thrives in a variety of soil types, making it suitable for garden cultivation.
