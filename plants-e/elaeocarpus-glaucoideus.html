Elaeocarpus glaucoideus

Elaeocarpus glaucoideus, commonly known as the silverleaf elaeocarpus, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia and northern Australia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces woody, spherical fruits that are typically green when mature, containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
