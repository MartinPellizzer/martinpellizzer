Embelia cordata

Embelia cordata, commonly known as wild betel, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite, and elliptical leaves that are often reddish when young. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, red, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed.

Embelia cordata is valued in traditional medicine for its purported antimicrobial and antifungal properties.
