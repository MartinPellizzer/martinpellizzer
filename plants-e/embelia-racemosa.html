Embelia racemosa

Embelia racemosa, commonly known as the Indian Gooseberry or embelia, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Primulaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are often used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features include aromatic leaves and a compact growth habit, making it suitable for ornamental and medicinal purposes.
