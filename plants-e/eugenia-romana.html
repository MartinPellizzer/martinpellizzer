Eugenia romana

Eugenia romana, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and dark red berries. The plant can grow up to 15 meters tall and has glossy, elliptical leaves. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Its ornamental value and ecological impact make it a notable species in its native and introduced ranges.
