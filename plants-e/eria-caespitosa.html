Eria caespitosa

Eria caespitosa, commonly known as the tufted eria, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often arranged in clusters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically growing in tufts or clumps.

Eria caespitosa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
