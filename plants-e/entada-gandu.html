Entada gandu

Entada gandu, commonly known as the African tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant is notable for its long, slender pods that contain a sticky, aromatic pulp and seeds. Its bark and roots have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The tree is also valued for its timber and as a source of food for both humans and animals.
