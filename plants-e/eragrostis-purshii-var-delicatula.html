Eragrostis purshii var. delicatula

Eragrostis purshii var. delicatula, commonly known as slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, hairless blades that are often tinged with silver. The plant produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences with pale green to yellow flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and potential use in restoration projects.
