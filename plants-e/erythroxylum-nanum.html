Erythroxylum nanum

Erythroxylum nanum, commonly known as the dwarf copaiba, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
