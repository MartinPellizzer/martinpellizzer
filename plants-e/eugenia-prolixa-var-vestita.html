Eugenia prolixa var. vestita

Eugenia prolixa var. vestita, commonly known as the velvet eugenia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and landscaping in warm climates.
