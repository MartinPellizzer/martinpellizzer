Erythranthe pulsiferae

Erythranthe pulsiferae, commonly known as the Sierra Mountains' flowering plant, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the western United States, particularly found in the Sierra Nevada range. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and features small, bright yellow flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

Erythranthe pulsiferae is known for its ability to thrive in high-altitude environments with cool, temperate climates.
