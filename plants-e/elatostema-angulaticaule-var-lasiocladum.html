Elatostema angulaticaule var. lasiocladum

Elatostema angulaticaule var. lasiocladum, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed groundsel, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It features slender, angular stems that are often covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate in shape, and often have a slightly serrated margin.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.
