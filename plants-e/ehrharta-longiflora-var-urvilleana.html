Ehrharta longiflora var. urvilleana

Ehrharta longiflora var. urvilleana, commonly known as the slender fingergrass, is a variant of the fingergrass genus native to Africa.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces white to pale green flowers, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential use in restoration projects.
