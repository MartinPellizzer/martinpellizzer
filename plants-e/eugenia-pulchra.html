Eugenia pulchra

Eugenia pulchra, commonly known as the velvetleaf eucalyptus, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and clusters of fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is often found in coastal regions and sandy soils. It produces woody capsules that contain numerous small, dark seeds.

Eugenia pulchra is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in various parts of the world for its aesthetic qualities.
