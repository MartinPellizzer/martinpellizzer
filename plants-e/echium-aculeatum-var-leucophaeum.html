Echium aculeatum var. leucophaeum

Echium aculeatum var. leucophaeum, commonly known as the white-leaved thistle, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, erect stems and distinctive, showy flowers that range from blue to violet in color. The leaves are typically narrow, hairy, and covered with glandular dots, which contribute to its medicinal properties. This species thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable plant in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
