Erica perspicua subsp. perspicua

Erica perspicua subsp.

perspicua, commonly known as the showy heath, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Cape Floristic Region of South Africa. It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its profuse display of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow, leathery, and evergreen, contributing to its ornamental appeal. Erica perspicua subsp.

perspicua is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators.
