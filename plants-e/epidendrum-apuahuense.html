Epidendrum apuahuense

Epidendrum apuahuense, commonly known as the Apuahuan orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant features a sympodial growth habit with fleshy, often hairy pseudobulbs. Its flowers are usually clustered in inflorescences, displaying a range of colors from white to pink and purple.

Epidendrum apuahuense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
