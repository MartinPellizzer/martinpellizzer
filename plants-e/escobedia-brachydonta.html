Escobedia brachydonta

Escobedia brachydonta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved escobedia, is a species of lichen belonging to the family Escobaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, crust-like growth form and is typically found on rock surfaces in arid or semi-arid environments. The thallus is yellowish to pale green, and it lacks distinct lobes, giving it a smooth appearance. It is often found in regions with low humidity and high sunlight exposure.

This lichen is of interest to mycologists and ecologists due to its ecological role in soil stabilization and as an indicator of environmental conditions.
