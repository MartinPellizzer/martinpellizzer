Eupatorium vitifolium

Eupatorium vitifolium, commonly known as white boneset, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture. It thrives in moist, sunny environments such as wet meadows and stream banks.

This plant is often found in wetland habitats and is known for its medicinal properties used by indigenous peoples.
