Euphorbia pedemontana

Euphorbia pedemontana, commonly known as the Montane Spurge, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and spiny leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called cyathia. It thrives in rocky or well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
