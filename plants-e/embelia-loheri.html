Embelia loheri

Embelia loheri, commonly known as the mountain embelia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, clustered white or pinkish flowers. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release tiny seeds when mature.

Embelia loheri is often found in moist, shaded forest environments and is valued for its potential medicinal properties.
