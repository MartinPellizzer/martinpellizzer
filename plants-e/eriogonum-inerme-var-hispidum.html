Eriogonum inerme var. hispidum

Eriogonum inerme var. hispidum, commonly known as the hairy leaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and meadows. It has a low-growing habit with branched stems and is often found in disturbed or arid environments.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its adaptability to harsh conditions.
