Eugenia ankarensis

Eugenia ankarensis, commonly known as the Ankarensis Syzygium, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible berries that are often used in local cuisines.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
