Eugenia capensis subsp. zeyheri

Eugenia capensis subsp.

zeyheri, commonly known as the Cape myrtle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves and fragrant, pink or white flowers.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.
