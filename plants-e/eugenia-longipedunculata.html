Eugenia longipedunculata

Eugenia longipedunculata, commonly known as the long-peduncled eugenia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
