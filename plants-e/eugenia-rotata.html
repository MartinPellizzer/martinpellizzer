Eugenia rotata

Eugenia rotata, commonly known as the red raspberry shrub, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and small, elliptical leaves. Its flowers are typically white or pink and are followed by small, red, edible fruits.

Eugenia rotata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible berries.
