Erythroxylum riverae

Erythroxylum riverae, commonly known as the Huayna Picchu coca, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Peru.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its leaves contain alkaloids, including cocaine, making it a subject of both botanical and pharmacological interest.

Due to its psychoactive properties, it is also classified as a controlled substance in many countries.
