Epidendrum villanuevae

Epidendrum villanuevae, commonly known as the Mexican orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its long, pendant inflorescences that bear numerous fragrant, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrates.

Epidendrum villanuevae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its striking floral display.
