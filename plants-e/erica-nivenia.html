Erica nivenia

Erica nivenia, commonly known as the mountain heath, is a small evergreen shrub native to the high-altitude regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late summer. Erica nivenia thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

It is well adapted to harsh, mountainous environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
