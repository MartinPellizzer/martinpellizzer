Euphorbia mesembryanthemifolia f. seminuda

Euphorbia mesembryanthemifolia f. seminuda, commonly known as the Woolly Spurge or Desert Spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that covers the entire plant, giving it a unique, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in late spring or early summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and landscapes.
