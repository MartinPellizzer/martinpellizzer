Eyrythalia nana

Eyrythalia nana, commonly known as the dwarf erythrina, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clustered, bright red flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral display. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Eyrythalia nana is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to add color to the landscape.
