Euthamia linarifolia

Euthamia linarifolia, commonly known as slender goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and along stream banks. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its leaves are narrow and linear, giving it the species name "linarifolia," meaning "like a linear leaf.".
