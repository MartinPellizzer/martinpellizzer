Eriocaulon hananoegoense

Eriocaulon hananoegoense, commonly known as the Japanese marsh grass, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is native to East Asia, particularly Japan, and thrives in wetland and marsh environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense spikes.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
