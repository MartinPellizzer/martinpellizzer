Ehretia acuminata var. pilosula

Ehretia acuminata var. pilosula, commonly known as the hairy ehretia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, spherical fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
