Episcia × wilsonii

Episcia × wilsonii, commonly known as the Wilson's wax plant, is a hybrid species native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors such as red, orange, and yellow. The plant features fleshy, often hairy leaves and is typically grown as a houseplant due to its preference for warm, humid conditions.

It produces small, tuberous roots and is prized for its ornamental value in botanical collections.
