Euphorbia systyloides subsp. systyloides

Euphorbia systyloides subsp.

systyloides, commonly known as the desert spurge, is a perennial shrub native to arid regions of North America. It features succulent, spiny stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters called cyathia, which are characteristic of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low precipitation.

Due to its hardiness, it is sometimes used in xeriscaping and as a drought-tolerant ornamental plant.
