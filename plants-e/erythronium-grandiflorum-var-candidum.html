Erythronium grandiflorum var. candidum

Erythronium grandiflorum var. candidum, commonly known as the white dog's tooth violet, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the lily family (Liliaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

The flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
