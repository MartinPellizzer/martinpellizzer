Eria polyura

Eria polyura, commonly known as the orchid with the peculiar leaf shape, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and is characterized by its unique, elongated leaves that resemble tendrils. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its distinctive appearance and growth habits, it is a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
