Eupatorium guiengolense

Eupatorium guiengolense, commonly known as the Mexican thoroughwort, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and is known for its aromatic, slightly bitter leaves. It has been used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.

The flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
