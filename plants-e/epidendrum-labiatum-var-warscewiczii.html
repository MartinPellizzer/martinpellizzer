Epidendrum labiatum var. warscewiczii

Epidendrum labiatum var. warscewiczii, commonly known as the Warscewicz's Epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces pendulous flower spikes with showy, fragrant blooms that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate to high humidity to flourish.

This variety is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
