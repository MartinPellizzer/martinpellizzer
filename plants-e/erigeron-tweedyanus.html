Erigeron tweedyanus

Erigeron tweedyanus, commonly known as the Tweedy's fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators.

Its foliage is usually gray-green with hairy surfaces, adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments.
