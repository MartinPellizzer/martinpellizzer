Eulophia hemileuca

Eulophia hemileuca, commonly known as the narrow-leafed eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded forest understories, where it thrives in humid environments. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers. Its inflorescence consists of several showy, white to pale yellow flowers with a distinctive, elongated lip.

Eulophia hemileuca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
