Encyclia ghillanyi

Encyclia ghillanyi, commonly known as the Ghillany's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, hairy pseudobulbs and strap-like leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers with a distinctive yellow lip, typically blooming during the dry season. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires specific humidity and light conditions to flourish.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
