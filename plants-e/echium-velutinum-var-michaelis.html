Echium velutinum var. michaelis

Echium velutinum var. michaelis, commonly known as Michael's velvet thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, grey-green foliage and striking purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, thriving in full sun conditions. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
