Epilobium jonanthum

Epilobium jonanthum, commonly known as the yellow willow herb, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to western North America.

It typically grows in moist habitats such as stream banks and wet meadows, preferring partial to full sunlight. The plant features showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Epilobium jonanthum is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
