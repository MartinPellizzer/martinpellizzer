Eriosema cordatum

Eriosema cordatum, commonly known as the heartleaf eriosema, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Eriosema cordatum is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
