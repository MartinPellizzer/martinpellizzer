Eriocaulon texense

Eriocaulon texense, commonly known as the Texas flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist habitats, showcasing its adaptability to waterlogged environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, which can stabilize soil and provide habitat for various organisms.

Botanically, it is characterized by its distinctive morphology and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
