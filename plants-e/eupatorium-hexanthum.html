Eupatorium hexanthum

Eupatorium hexanthum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has opposite, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Eupatorium hexanthum is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.
