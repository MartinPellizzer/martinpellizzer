Eriothrix commersonii

Eriothrix commersonii, commonly known as the Commerson's eriothrix, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the southern regions of South America.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, giving the plant a woolly appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or reddish in color, blooming during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous areas.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
