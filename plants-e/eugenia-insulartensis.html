Eugenia insulartensis

Eugenia insulartensis, commonly known as the island eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly the Amazon rainforest. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pinkish flowers. It produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
