Eriocaulon brasiliense

Eriocaulon brasiliense, commonly known as the Brazilian spikerush, is a member of the Poales order and belongs to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is a small, herbaceous plant characterized by its dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and flooded grasslands, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are narrow and often covered with a fine, whitish coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Eriocaulon brasiliense plays an important ecological role in maintaining wetland biodiversity and soil stability.
