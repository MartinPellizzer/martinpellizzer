Epipactis rubiginosa f. latifolia

Epipactis rubiginosa f. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved marsh helleborine, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are usually covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces showy, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive, spurred lip, typical of orchids. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows.

This orchid is often found in temperate regions of Europe and is known for its ability to form mycorrhizal associations with fungi.
