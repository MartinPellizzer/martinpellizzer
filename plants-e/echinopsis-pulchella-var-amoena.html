Echinopsis pulchella var. amoena

Echinopsis pulchella var. amoena, commonly known as the "Coral Cactus," is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and vibrant pink to red flowers that bloom during the cooler months. The plant features a thick, spiny stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. Its flowers are often described as resembling coral, giving it its common name.

This cactus is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
