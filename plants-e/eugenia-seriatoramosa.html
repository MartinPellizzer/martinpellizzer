Eugenia seriatoramosa

Eugenia seriatoramosa, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink and are clustered in inflorescences.

The fruit is a small, reddish-brown drupe that contains a single seed, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
