Eriogonum thurberi var. acutangulum

Eriogonum thurberi var. acutangulum, commonly known as the Thurber's buckwheat, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soils in open, sunny habitats. It is commonly found in grasslands, sagebrush steppe, and disturbed areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
