Elionurus latiflorus var. pectinatus

Elionurus latiflorus var. pectinatus, commonly known as the broadflower elionurus, is a perennial herb native to certain regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its robust, upright stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense, terminal inflorescences, contributing to its ornamental value.

The species is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
