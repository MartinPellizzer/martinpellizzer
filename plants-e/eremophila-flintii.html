Eremophila flintii

Eremophila flintii, commonly known as Flint's eryngo, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant is typically shrub-like, with multiple stems and a compact growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Eremophila flintii is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
