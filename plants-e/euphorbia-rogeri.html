Euphorbia rogeri

Euphorbia rogeri, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and spiny leaves. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It is adapted to arid environments and stores water in its stems for drought survival.

Euphorbia rogeri is also known for its milky sap, which can be toxic if ingested.
