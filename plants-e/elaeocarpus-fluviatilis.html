Elaeocarpus fluviatilis

Elaeocarpus fluviatilis, commonly known as the river elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and lanceolate leaves that are glossy on the upper surface. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests and riverine habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
