Epidendrum feddeanum

Epidendrum feddeanum, commonly known as the Fedde's Epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its erect, branching growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, yellow-green flowers with white markings. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, showcasing its epiphytic nature. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a waxy texture, adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Epidendrum feddeanum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and relatively easy care requirements.
