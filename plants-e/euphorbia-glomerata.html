Euphorbia glomerata

Euphorbia glomerata, commonly known as the clustered spurge, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, branching stems and small, clustered yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that can be toxic if ingested. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Euphorbia glomerata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
