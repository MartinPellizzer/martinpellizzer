Eremophila glabra subsp. elegans

Eremophila glabra subsp.

elegans, commonly known as the narrow-leaved emu bush, is a flowering plant native to Australia. It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant is typically shrub-like, with a compact growth habit and is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions of southern Australia.
