Eriochilus sericeus

Eriochilus sericeus, commonly known as the silky eriochilus, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with woolly, silver-green foliage that helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal heathlands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for native gardens.
