Eriocephalus aspalathoides var. ambiguus

Eriocephalus aspalathoides var. ambiguus, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, hard, brown fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is a valued species in local flora.
