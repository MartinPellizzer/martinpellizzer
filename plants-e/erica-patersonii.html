Erica patersonii

Erica patersonii, commonly known as the Paterson's heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in coastal and mountainous regions. Erica patersonii is evergreen, with dense, leathery foliage that helps it withstand harsh environmental conditions.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
