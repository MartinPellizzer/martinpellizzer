Eremophila muelleriana

Eremophila muelleriana, commonly known as the Mueller's emu bush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in spring. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in arid and semi-arid regions.

Eremophila muelleriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional Aboriginal medicine.
