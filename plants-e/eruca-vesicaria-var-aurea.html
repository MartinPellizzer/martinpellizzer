Eruca vesicaria var. aurea

Eruca vesicaria var. aurea, commonly known as the golden rapeseed, is a variant of the common mustard plant.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant is native to Europe and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is known for its peppery flavor when the leaves are harvested young.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential agricultural uses.
