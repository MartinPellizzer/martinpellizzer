Elephantopus mollis var. capitulatus

Elephantopus mollis var. capitulatus, commonly known as the hairy false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa, including South Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands. The plant features alternate, hairy leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a single, terminal head. It is characterized by its robust growth and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

The species is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
