Echites calycosus

Echites calycosus, commonly known as the Cape mountain tea, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, oval fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in rocky habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
