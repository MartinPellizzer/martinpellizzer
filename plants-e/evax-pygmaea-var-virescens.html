Evax pygmaea var. virescens

Evax pygmaea var. virescens, commonly known as the green dwarf saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine and rocky habitats.

It features small, succulent leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color, adapted to retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale pink flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in mountainous regions.

Its compact size and hardy nature make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine plant collections.
