Erysimum tanacetifolium

Erysimum tanacetifolium, commonly known as the wallflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow or orange flowers and pinnately divided, fern-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard family plants.

Erysimum tanacetifolium is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
