Erythronium aureum

Erythronium aureum, commonly known as the golden trout lily, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, glossy green leaves with a distinctive white and yellow mottling. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, yellowish-white flowers with a red throat, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained woodland soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Erythronium aureum is valued for its ornamental beauty and its role in early spring ecosystems.
