Erica caesia

Erica caesia, commonly known as the blue heather, is a flowering plant native to the western Cape of South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, needle-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of delicate blue or violet flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. Erica caesia thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value and hardiness.
