Echinochloa crus-galli var. riukiuensis

Echinochloa crus-galli var. riukiuensis, commonly known as the Ryukyu grass, is a variant of the common barnyard grass.

It is a perennial grass species native to East Asia, particularly the Ryukyu Islands. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands or along waterways. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a prominent midrib, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

It is considered an invasive species in some regions, outcompeting native vegetation in wetland habitats.
