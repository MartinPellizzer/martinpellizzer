Euonymus elegantissimus

Euonymus elegantissimus, commonly known as the silver leaf euonymus, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, ovate leaves that are typically variegated with white or silver markings. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by red, lantern-like fruits in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often used in landscaping for its vibrant foliage and seasonal interest.
