Erica longipedicellata

Erica longipedicellata, commonly known as the long-stemmed heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. Erica longipedicellata thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
