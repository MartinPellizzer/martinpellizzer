Eriogonum sulcatum

Eriogonum sulcatum, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub or subshrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features small, spiny leaves and produces clusters of yellow to reddish-purple flowers. It has adapted to harsh conditions with deep roots and a low, compact growth form.

Eriogonum sulcatum plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
