Excoecaria marginata var. grandifolia

Excoecaria marginata var. grandifolia, commonly known as the croton or spurge, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage with leaves that can range from green to red, orange, or yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
