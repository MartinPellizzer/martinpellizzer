Echinops sartorianus

Echinops sartorianus, commonly known as the Persian thistle, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southwestern Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble small globes, which are a distinctive feature of the echinops genus. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by the development of its unique, woolly seed heads. It is adapted to dry climates and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping.

Echinops sartorianus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
