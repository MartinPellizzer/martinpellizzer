Eucalyptus fergusonii

Eucalyptus fergusonii, commonly known as the Ferguson's malleeflower, is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often glaucous in appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters, typically during the cooler months. It thrives in sandy or loamy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Eucalyptus fergusonii is often found in mallee and woodland habitats, contributing to the ecological diversity of these regions.
