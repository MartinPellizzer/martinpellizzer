Emilia sonchifolia var. lanceolata

Emilia sonchifolia var. lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf emilia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and daisy-like yellow flowers with a central disk. The leaves are lance-shaped, alternate, and often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

This plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
