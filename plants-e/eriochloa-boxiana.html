Eriochloa boxiana

Eriochloa boxiana, commonly known as the narrowleaf bottlebrush grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It features slender, cylindrical stems that are often covered in dense, bristly hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with bright red to orange bracts that give it its common name. Its foliage is characterized by narrow, grass-like leaves with a slight waxy coating.

Eriochloa boxiana is often used in ornamental gardening for its striking floral displays and drought tolerance.
