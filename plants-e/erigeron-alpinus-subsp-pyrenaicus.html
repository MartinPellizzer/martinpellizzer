Erigeron alpinus subsp. pyrenaicus

Erigeron alpinus subsp.

pyrenaicus, commonly known as the Pyrenean fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to the harsh conditions of its high-altitude environment.
