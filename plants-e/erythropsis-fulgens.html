Erythropsis fulgens

Erythropsis fulgens, commonly known as the fiery glory, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family, native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant red to orange flowers, which bloom in dense, showy clusters during the summer months. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to conserve water in its harsh environment. Its flowering period attracts a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Erythropsis fulgens is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and resilience in dry conditions.
