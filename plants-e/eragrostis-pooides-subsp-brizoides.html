Eragrostis pooides subsp. brizoides

Eragrostis pooides subsp.

brizoides, commonly known as the slender lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, often tufted stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically hairy.

The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in erosion control.
