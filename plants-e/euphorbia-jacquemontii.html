Euphorbia jacquemontii

Euphorbia jacquemontii, commonly known as the Jacquemont's spurge, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its branched stems and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green or reddish in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually yellow or orange, surrounded by colorful bracts. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Euphorbia jacquemontii is known for its resilience and ability to withstand drought conditions.
