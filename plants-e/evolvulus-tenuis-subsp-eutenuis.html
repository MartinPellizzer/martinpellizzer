Evolvulus tenuis subsp. eutenuis

Evolvulus tenuis subsp.

eutenuis, commonly known as the narrowleaf morning glory, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States. It features slender, trailing stems that can grow up to 2 meters in length, often climbing or sprawling over other vegetation.

The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue to violet in color, blooming from spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The species is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a common weed in disturbed areas.
