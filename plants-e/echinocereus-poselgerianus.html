Echinocereus poselgerianus

Echinocereus poselgerianus, commonly known as the Poselgerian cactus, is a species native to the Chihuahuan Desert in North America.

It is a low-growing, clumping cactus with cylindrical stems that are typically green to blue-green in color. The plant produces bright yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are often followed by red, fleshy fruits. It has spiny areoles and is adapted to arid environments with minimal water requirements.

This cactus is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh desert conditions.
