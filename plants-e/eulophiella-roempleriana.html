Eulophiella roempleriana

Eulophiella roempleriana, commonly known as the Roempler's spider orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, spider-like appearance due to its elongated, thin flower spikes. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Eulophiella roempleriana is often found growing on trees or rocks in the understory of rainforests, where it relies on specific symbiotic relationships with fungi for nutrient uptake.
