Erica pendula

Erica pendula, commonly known as the hanging heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features slender, pendulous stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. These flowers are usually pink or white and bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Erica pendula is well adapted to rocky and sandy soils, thriving in full sun and dry conditions.
