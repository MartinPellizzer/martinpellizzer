Epilobium cinereum

Epilobium cinereum, commonly known as gray willow herb, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Onagraceae family.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It typically grows in moist, often rocky soils and is found in various habitats such as stream banks and woodland edges.

Epilobium cinereum is often used in traditional herbal medicine and is also valued in ecological restoration projects.
