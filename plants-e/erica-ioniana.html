Erica ioniana

Erica ioniana, commonly known as the Ionian heath, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Greece.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. Erica ioniana typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

It is often found in rocky or coastal habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
