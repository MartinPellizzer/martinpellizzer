Eremosis aristifera

Eremosis aristifera, commonly known as the desert aster, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. Eremosis aristifera is often found in sandy soils and is well-suited to drought conditions.

It plays an important role in stabilizing desert soils and providing nectar for pollinators.
