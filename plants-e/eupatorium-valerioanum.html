Eupatorium valerioanum

Eupatorium valerioanum, commonly known as white boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features tall, slender stems covered in fine, white hairs, and its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers in clustered inflorescences, which bloom in late summer to early fall. It thrives in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and along stream banks.

Botanically, it is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads.
