Elaeocarpus obovatus subsp. umbratilis

Elaeocarpus obovatus subsp.

umbratilis, commonly known as the dark-leaved elaeocarpus, is a tropical tree native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its glossy, dark green foliage and small, clustered flowers.

The fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, typical of the Elaeocarpaceae family. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in lowland and hill forests.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
