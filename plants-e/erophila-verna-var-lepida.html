Erophila verna var. lepida

Erophila verna var. lepida, commonly known as the pretty everlasting pea, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, pea-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, often appearing in clusters. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

The species is noted for its ability to self-seed and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
