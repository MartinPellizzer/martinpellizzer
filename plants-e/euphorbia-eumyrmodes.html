Euphorbia eumyrmodes

Euphorbia eumyrmodes, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a distinctive appearance with its spiky, upright stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces a milky sap, which is typical of the Euphorbia genus and can be toxic if ingested. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
