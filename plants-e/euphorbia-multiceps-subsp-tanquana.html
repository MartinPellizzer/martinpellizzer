Euphorbia multiceps subsp. tanquana

Euphorbia multiceps subsp.

tanquana, commonly known as the Tanquana euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It features a compact, branching structure with thick, fleshy stems that store water, adapting to arid environments.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are its modified flowers surrounded by specialized leaves. Its leaves are typically narrow and may have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
