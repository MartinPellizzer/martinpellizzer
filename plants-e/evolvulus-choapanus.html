Evolvulus choapanus

Evolvulus choapanus, commonly known as the Choapanus morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of blue or purple. The plant has slender, twining stems that climb by means of tendrils, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its fruit is a capsule that contains several small, round seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
