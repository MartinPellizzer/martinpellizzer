Eupatorium steviifolium var. viscosum

Eupatorium steviifolium var. viscosum, commonly known as sticky eupatorium, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, often sticky stems and leaves that are lance-shaped with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers in clustered inflorescences, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.

Its sticky secretions and aromatic foliage make it distinctive within the Asteraceae family.
