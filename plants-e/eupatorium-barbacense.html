Eupatorium barbacense

Eupatorium barbacense, commonly known as the roughleaf boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist woodlands and wet meadows, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant features opposite, heart-shaped leaves with rough, hairy surfaces, and it produces small, white to pale blue flowers in clustered inflorescences. It blooms from late summer to early autumn, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
