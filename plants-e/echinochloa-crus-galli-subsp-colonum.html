Echinochloa crus-galli subsp. colonum

Echinochloa crus-galli subsp.

colonum, commonly known as cocklebur, is a perennial grass that is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its broad, flat leaves and produces distinctive spiny, spherical seed heads.

This plant is often found in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides, fields, and waste areas. It has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in a variety of soil types.

The plant is also known for its adaptability and can be invasive in certain ecosystems.
