Euphorbia seguieriana subsp. seguieriana

Euphorbia seguieriana subsp.

seguieriana, commonly known as the Seguier's spurge, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically green or reddish in color.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a unique structure called a cyathium, which is typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and arid landscapes.
