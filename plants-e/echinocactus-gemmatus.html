Echinocactus gemmatus

Echinocactus gemmatus, commonly known as the Mexican apple cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its globular shape and dense covering of spines, which provide protection from herbivores and intense sunlight. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green body. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
