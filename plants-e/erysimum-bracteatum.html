Erysimum bracteatum

Erysimum bracteatum, commonly known as the Mexican wallflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers in early spring. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and has a compact growth habit, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, and is drought-tolerant once established.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
