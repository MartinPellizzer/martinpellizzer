Eugenia tapacumensis

Eugenia tapacumensis, commonly known as the Peruvian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Andean regions of Peru and Ecuador, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers and glossy, dark green leaves. It produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine.

Eugenia tapacumensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
