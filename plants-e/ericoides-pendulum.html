Ericoides pendulum

Ericoides pendulum, commonly known as the hanging heath, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, shrub-like structure with dense clusters of small, evergreen leaves. The plant produces bell-shaped, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Ericoides pendulum thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands or rocky areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
