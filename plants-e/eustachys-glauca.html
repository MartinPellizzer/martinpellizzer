Eustachys glauca

Eustachys glauca, commonly known as bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as meadows and prairies. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense stands.

Eustachys glauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
