Eriogonum jamesii var. xanthum

Eriogonum jamesii var. xanthum, commonly known as the yellow globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems, which help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is often found in desert and foothill regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
