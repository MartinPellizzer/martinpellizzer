Erythroxylum pictum

Erythroxylum pictum, commonly known as the "Painted Erythroxylum," is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage. The leaves are typically elliptical with a smooth texture and often display a range of hues, including red, orange, and yellow. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing seeds.

This plant is primarily valued for its ornamental qualities rather than for any medicinal or commercial use.
