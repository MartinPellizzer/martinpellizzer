Eucalyptus australiana var. latifolia

Eucalyptus australiana var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved mallees, is a species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, grey-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, white bloom. The plant produces small, globular clusters of white or pale pink flowers, which bloom during the cooler months. It is typically found in sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
