Eugenia stibephylla

Eugenia stibephylla, commonly known as the velvetwood or black myrtle, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to black as it matures. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its dense foliage and attractive form.

It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
