Equisetum heleocharis f. uliginosum

Equisetum heleocharis f. uliginosum, commonly known as the swamp scouring rush, is a variant of the scouring rush genus Equisetum.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its jointed, hollow stems that are covered in small, scale-like structures. The plant typically grows in wet, acidic soils and is often found in marshy or boggy habitats. Its spore-bearing cones are produced at the tips of the stems, and it is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor environments.

This plant plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
