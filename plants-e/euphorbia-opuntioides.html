Euphorbia opuntioides

Euphorbia opuntioides, commonly known as the Desert Cactus, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a thick, cylindrical stem covered in small, spiny areoles, which are characteristic of the Euphorbiaceae family. The plant produces clusters of yellow, cyathia-like flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, and it stores water in its stem to survive in dry environments.

Euphorbia opuntioides is adapted to extreme heat and drought, making it a resilient plant in desert ecosystems.
