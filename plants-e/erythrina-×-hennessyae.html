Erythrina × hennessyae

Erythrina × hennessyae, commonly known as the pink flame tree, is a hybrid species native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, bright pink or red flowers that bloom in dense, racemose inflorescences. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its compound fruits are cylindrical pods that split open to release seeds. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental tree due to its vibrant floral display.

The bark and leaves contain alkaloids, making it a plant of interest in both horticulture and pharmacology.
