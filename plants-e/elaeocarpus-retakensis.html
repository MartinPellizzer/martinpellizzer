Elaeocarpus retakensis

Elaeocarpus retakensis, commonly known as the Tawaroi tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy white to pink flowers. The tree produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland and montane forests.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
