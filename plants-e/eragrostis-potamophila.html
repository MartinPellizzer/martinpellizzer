Eragrostis potamophila

Eragrostis potamophila, commonly known as the river grass or watergrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and riverbanks, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant has slender, wiry stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, which helps it retain moisture in its habitat.

Eragrostis potamophila plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small aquatic organisms.
