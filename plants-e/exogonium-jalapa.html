Exogonium jalapa

Exogonium jalapa, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Central America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces showy, colorful blooms that open in the morning and close at dusk. Its rapid growth habit makes it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
