Eupatorium mercedense

Eupatorium mercedense, commonly known as the Mercedense thoroughwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and clustered in dense, spherical heads.

Eupatorium mercedense is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
