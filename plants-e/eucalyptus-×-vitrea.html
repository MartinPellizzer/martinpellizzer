Eucalyptus × vitrea

Eucalyptus × vitrea, commonly known as the silver ash eucalyptus, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Eucalyptus cinerea and Eucalyptus viminalis.

It is characterized by its silver-gray bark that peels in thin, papery layers, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. It produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically pale green to yellow in color.

This species is native to southeastern Australia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
