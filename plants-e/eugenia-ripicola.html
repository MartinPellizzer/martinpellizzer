Eugenia ripicola

Eugenia ripicola, commonly known as the rock apple or sour apple, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, sour-tasting berries. Its leaves are glossy and oval-shaped, providing dense foliage that offers habitat for various wildlife species.

Eugenia ripicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
