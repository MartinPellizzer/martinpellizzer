Erodium pimpinellifolium var. pilosum

Erodium pimpinellifolium var. pilosum, commonly known as the hairy heartweed, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. It is a member of the Geraniaceae family and is known for its delicate appearance and short lifespan.

This annual herb is sometimes cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and flowers.
