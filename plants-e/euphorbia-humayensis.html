Euphorbia humayensis

Euphorbia humayensis, commonly known as the Kashmir rose, is a succulent plant native to the Himalayan regions of Pakistan and India.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and distinctive, spiny leaves. The plant produces striking yellow flowers surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, making it well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
