Echinospermum latifolium

Echinospermum latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf echinosperm, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

The species is often found in dry, open habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
