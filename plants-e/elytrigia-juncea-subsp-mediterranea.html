Elytrigia juncea subsp. mediterranea

Elytrigia juncea subsp.

mediterranea, commonly known as Mediterranean false brome, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.

The plant has slender, tufted stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-lipped flowers.

It plays a role in stabilizing soils and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
