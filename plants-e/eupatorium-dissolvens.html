Eupatorium dissolvens

Eupatorium dissolvens, commonly known as the white boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks, thriving in moist, rich soils. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture, while its flowering period occurs in late summer to early fall.

Eupatorium dissolvens is also known for its historical use in traditional medicine.
