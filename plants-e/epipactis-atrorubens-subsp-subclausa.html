Epipactis atrorubens subsp. subclausa

Epipactis atrorubens subsp.

subclausa, commonly known as the dark red helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its dark reddish-brown flowers with a distinctive greenish-yellow lip.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, and it produces a single flower spike with several showy blooms.

The species is notable for its specialized pollination mechanisms and ecological significance in its native range.
