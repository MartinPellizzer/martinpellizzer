Echinocactus hybocentrus

Echinocactus hybocentrus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its globular, spiny shape and a thick, ribbed stem that stores water. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by red fruits containing seeds. It thrives in well-draining soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for desert gardens.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is often cultivated as a ornamental plant.
