Erophila verna var. rurivaga

Erophila verna var. rurivaga, commonly known as the early meadow cranesbill, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe.

It typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, thriving in temperate climates. The plant features upright, slender stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are pink to purple, arranged in clusters, and bloom in early spring.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
