Ehretia macrophylla

Ehretia macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved ehretia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 20 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Ehretia macrophylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
