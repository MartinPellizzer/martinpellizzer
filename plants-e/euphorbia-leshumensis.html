Euphorbia leshumensis

Euphorbia leshumensis, commonly known as the Leshumensis spurges, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in a structure called a cyathium, which is typical of the genus Euphorbia. It is often found growing in rocky soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and resilience, it is occasionally cultivated as an ornamental plant.
