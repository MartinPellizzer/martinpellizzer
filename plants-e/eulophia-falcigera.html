Eulophia falcigera

Eulophia falcigera, commonly known as the sickle orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a short rhizome and a rosette of fleshy, strap-like leaves. The plant produces racemes of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers with a distinctive, narrow, sickle-shaped lip. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Eulophia falcigera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in tropical gardens.
