Eugenia gardneriana

Eugenia gardneriana, commonly known as the Gardner's eugenia, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit, a small, dark purple berry, is edible and often used in local cuisines.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in its native habitats.
