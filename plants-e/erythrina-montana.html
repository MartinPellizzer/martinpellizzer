Erythrina montana

Erythrina montana, commonly known as the mountain coral tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange-pink flowers that bloom in clusters, making it a visually striking plant. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its bark is often smooth with a reddish hue. Erythrina montana is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to various environments.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
