Eucalyptus largiflorens

Eucalyptus largiflorens, commonly known as the large-flowered eucalypt, is a species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and smooth, peeling bark that reveals a mottled appearance. The plant produces large, showy white or pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting a variety of pollinators. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a bluish-green hue.

This eucalypt is well adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.
