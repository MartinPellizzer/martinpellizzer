Euphorbia lophogona var. tenuicaulis

Euphorbia lophogona var. tenuicaulis, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a slender, branching stem with smooth, waxy leaves that are typically green or bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish cyathia, which are the modified flower clusters characteristic of the Euphorbia genus. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
