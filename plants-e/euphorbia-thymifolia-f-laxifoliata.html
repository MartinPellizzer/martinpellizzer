Euphorbia thymifolia f. laxifoliata

Euphorbia thymifolia f. laxifoliata, commonly known as the loose-leaved euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and reduced, often sparse leaves. The plant produces striking yellowish-green cyathia, which are the specialized inflorescences typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Due to its low maintenance and ornamental value, it is widely cultivated as a decorative plant.
