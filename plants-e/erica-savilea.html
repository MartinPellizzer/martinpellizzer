Erica savilea

Erica savilea, commonly known as the Cape snowbush, is a flowering plant native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form. The plant produces dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in winter. Erica savilea thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

It is well adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native habitat, with drought-resistant properties.
