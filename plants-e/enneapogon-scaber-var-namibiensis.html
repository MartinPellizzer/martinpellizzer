Enneapogon scaber var. namibiensis

Enneapogon scaber var. namibiensis, commonly known as the Namibian rough beardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its wiry, rough-textured foliage and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showing adaptation to drought conditions. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, pale to brownish florets.

This variant is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils and supporting local wildlife.
