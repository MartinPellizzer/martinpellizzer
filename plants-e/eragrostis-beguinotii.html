Eragrostis beguinotii

Eragrostis beguinotii, commonly known as the dwarf weeping grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and fine, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive weeping appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Eragrostis beguinotii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its delicate, cascading form.
