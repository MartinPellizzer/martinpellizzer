Euphorbia lawsonii

Euphorbia lawsonii, commonly known as the Lawson's spurge, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a unique cyathium structure, which is typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appearance, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and xeriscapes.
