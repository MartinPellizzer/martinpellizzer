Eruca vesicaria var. orthosepala

Eruca vesicaria var. orthosepala, commonly known as the broad-leafed mustard, is a variant of the common mustard plant.

It is a biennial or short-lived perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, waxy coating. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in clusters, typical of the Brassicaceae family.

The plant is known for its peppery flavor and is often cultivated for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
