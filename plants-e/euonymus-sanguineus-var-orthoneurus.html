Euonymus sanguineus var. orthoneurus

Euonymus sanguineus var. orthoneurus, commonly known as the red-veined ivy, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features glossy, dark green leaves with striking red veins that contrast vividly against the foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in the spring, which are followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits that are attractive to birds. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, visually appealing plant.
