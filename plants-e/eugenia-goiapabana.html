Eugenia goiapabana

Eugenia goiapabana, commonly known as the Goiabeira or Brazilian cherry, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red, cherry-like fruits that are edible and often used in traditional cuisine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits.

The tree is also important in local ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
