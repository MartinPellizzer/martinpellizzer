Eugenia hovarum

Eugenia hovarum, commonly known as the Hovar's guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink. Its fruit, which resembles a small guava, is edible and has a sweet, aromatic flavor.

Eugenia hovarum is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit.
