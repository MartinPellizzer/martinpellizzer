Elaphoglossum pendulifolium

Elaphoglossum pendulifolium, commonly known as the hanging-leaf maidenhair fern, is a delicate fern species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, pendulous fronds that drape gracefully. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and stream banks. Its spores are produced on the underside of its fertile fronds, which are often smaller and more compact than the sterile ones.

The intricate structure of its leaves reflects the advanced evolutionary adaptations of ferns in their reproductive strategies.
