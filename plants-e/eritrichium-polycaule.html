Eritrichium polycaule

Eritrichium polycaule, commonly known as the clustered ericoid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of purple and white, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Eritrichium polycaule is often found in rocky soils and alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.
