Epidendrum infaustum

Epidendrum infaustum, commonly known as the Black Epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and produces striking, dark red to black flowers with a contrasting white lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are often fragrant and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Epidendrum infaustum is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
