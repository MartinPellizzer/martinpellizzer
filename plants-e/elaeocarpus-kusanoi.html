Elaeocarpus kusanoi

Elaeocarpus kusanoi, commonly known as the Japanese elaeocarp, is a flowering plant species native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically red or purple when mature, containing hard seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
