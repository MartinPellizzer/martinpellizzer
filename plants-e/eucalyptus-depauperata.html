Eucalyptus depauperata

Eucalyptus depauperata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, globular flower buds that are typically yellow or yellow-green in color. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

Eucalyptus depauperata is a hardy shrub or small tree that plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.
