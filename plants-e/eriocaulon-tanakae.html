Eriocaulon tanakae

Eriocaulon tanakae, commonly known as the Japanese marsh grass, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to East Asia.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, white bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are arranged in dense clusters.

Eriocaulon tanakae is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in maintaining wetland ecosystems.
