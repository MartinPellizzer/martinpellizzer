Erigeron × helveticus

Erigeron × helveticus, commonly known as the Swiss fleabane, is a hybrid perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with white petals and yellow centers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually gray-green and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

Erigeron × helveticus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
