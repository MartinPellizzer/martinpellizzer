Equisetum hyemale var. rabenhorstii

Equisetum hyemale var. rabenhorstii, commonly known as the northern horse-tail, is a variant of the common horsetail.

It is a primitive vascular plant characterized by its whorled arrangement of leaves and a spore-bearing cone at the top of its stem. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as wetlands and stream banks. It possesses a unique structure with a hollow, segmented stem and needle-like leaves.

The species is known for its ability to colonize disturbed soils and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
