Ehretia rigida subsp. rigida

Ehretia rigida subsp.

rigida, commonly known as the rigid ehretia, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia. It features simple, opposite, elliptic leaves that are typically dark green and glossy.

The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed enclosed in a fleshy, yellowish pulp.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
