Erodium pilosum var. vestitum

Erodium pilosum var. vestitum, commonly known as the hairy bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a small annual herb that typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are covered in fine, hairy hairs. It has a creeping growth habit and often forms dense mats.

The species is characterized by its slender stems and delicate, trifoliate leaves.
