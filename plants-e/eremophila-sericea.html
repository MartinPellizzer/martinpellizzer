Eremophila sericea

Eremophila sericea, commonly known as the silver-leafed eremophila, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and showy, tubular flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping. Its flowers, which range in color from pink to red, attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.

Eremophila sericea is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
