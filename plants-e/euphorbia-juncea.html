Euphorbia juncea

Euphorbia juncea, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spurge, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, cylindrical stems and small, clustered yellow flowers surrounded by green bracts. The plant thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in Mediterranean climates. It has a milky, toxic sap that can cause skin irritation.

Euphorbia juncea is typically evergreen, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.
