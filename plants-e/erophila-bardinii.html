Erophila bardinii

Erophila bardinii, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry, is a herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features hairy, upright stems and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant's fruit, often referred to as a "physalis," is encased in a papery calyx and is edible when ripe. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Erophila bardinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its use in traditional medicine.
