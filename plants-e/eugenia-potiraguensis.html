Eugenia potiraguensis

Eugenia potiraguensis, commonly known as the Brazilian wax apple, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, with a waxy coating. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and research settings.
