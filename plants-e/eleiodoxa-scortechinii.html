Eleiodoxa scortechinii

Eleiodoxa scortechinii, commonly known as the African wild bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant is often found in savannas and wooded areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
