Eucalyptus leucoxylon var. angulata

Eucalyptus leucoxylon var. angulata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed malleeflower, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, angular leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale pink flowers, which are often tinged with red or purple. It is adapted to grow in dry, sandy soils and is often found in mallee and heathland ecosystems.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
