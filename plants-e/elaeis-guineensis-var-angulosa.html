Elaeis guineensis var. angulosa

Elaeis guineensis var. angulosa, commonly known as the angular palm, is a variant of the oil palm native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive angular leaf bases, which set it apart from other oil palm varieties. The plant produces large, fan-shaped leaves and can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a robust trunk. Its fruit, which contains oil-rich kernels, is used in the production of palm oil.

This variety is valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its potential in sustainable agriculture.
