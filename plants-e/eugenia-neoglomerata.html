Eugenia neoglomerata

Eugenia neoglomerata, commonly known as the black myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, dark purple drupe that contains a single seed.

Eugenia neoglomerata is often found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
