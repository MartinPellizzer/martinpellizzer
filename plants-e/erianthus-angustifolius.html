Erianthus angustifolius

Erianthus angustifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaved bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the United States.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-green blades and tufted inflorescences that produce small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairie and grassland habitats. It plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens and restoration projects.
