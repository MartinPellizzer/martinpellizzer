Echinopsis kladiwaiana

Echinopsis kladiwaiana, commonly known as the Kladiwaiana cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly found in Argentina.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its smooth, pale green stems and sparse, white spines. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure, making it a popular choice for arid gardens.

Due to its slow growth rate, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in both indoor and outdoor settings.
