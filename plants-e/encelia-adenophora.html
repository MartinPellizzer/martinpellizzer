Encelia adenophora

Encelia adenophora, commonly known as the silverleaf sunflower, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in sandy or rocky soils.

Its hardy nature makes it well-suited for xeriscaping and drought-tolerant gardens.
