Epidendrum secundum subsp. briegeri

Epidendrum secundum subsp.

briegeri, commonly known as the Brieger's Epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America. It is characterized by its pendulous, green to yellow-green inflorescences that bear numerous small, fragrant flowers.

The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air. Its leaves are usually broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native humid environments.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
