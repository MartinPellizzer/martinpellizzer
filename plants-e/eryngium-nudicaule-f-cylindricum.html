Eryngium nudicaule f. cylindricum

Eryngium nudicaule f. cylindricum, commonly known as the cylindrical thistle, is a variant of the common eryngium characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower heads.

It is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region, typically found in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant features blue to purple spiky flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

The species is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
