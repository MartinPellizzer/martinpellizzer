Epilobium clarkeanum

Epilobium clarkeanum, commonly known as Clarke's willow-herb, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Epilobium clarkeanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climates.
