Ericoila boryi

Ericoila boryi, commonly known as the Bory's heath, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in countries like Peru and Bolivia. The plant is characterized by its low-growing habit, with dense clusters of small, evergreen leaves. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, appearing in late summer to early autumn.

Ericoila boryi thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested habitats.
