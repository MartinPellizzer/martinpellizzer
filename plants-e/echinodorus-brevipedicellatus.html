Echinodorus brevipedicellatus

Echinodorus brevipedicellatus, commonly known as the compact broadleaf waterweed, is a species of aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant thrives in slow to moderately flowing freshwater environments and is often found in ponds, lakes, and wetlands. Its rhizomes spread horizontally through the substrate, allowing it to colonize large areas quickly.

Echinodorus brevipedicellatus is valued in aquariums for its ornamental appearance and ability to provide habitat for aquatic life.
