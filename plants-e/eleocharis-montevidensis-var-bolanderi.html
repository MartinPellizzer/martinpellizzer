Eleocharis montevidensis var. bolanderi

Eleocharis montevidensis var. bolanderi, commonly known as Bolander's spike-rush, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and small, spike-like clusters of flowers. This variety is native to South America, particularly in regions of Argentina and Uruguay. It thrives in wetland environments, often found in marshes and along the edges of water bodies.

The plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
