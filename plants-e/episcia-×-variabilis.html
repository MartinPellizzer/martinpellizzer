Episcia × variabilis

Episcia × variabilis, commonly known as the variable wax plant, is a hybrid species within the Gesneriaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America and is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that resemble flowers. The plant typically grows as a compact, trailing herb with fleshy, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture to maintain its lush appearance.

Episcia × variabilis is popular among indoor gardeners for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
