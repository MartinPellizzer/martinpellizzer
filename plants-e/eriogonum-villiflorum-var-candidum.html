Eriogonum villiflorum var. candidum

Eriogonum villiflorum var. candidum, commonly known as the whiteleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, gray-green foliage and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

This species is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, contributing to local biodiversity.
