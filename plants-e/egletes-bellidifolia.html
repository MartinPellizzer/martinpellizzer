Egletes bellidifolia

Egletes bellidifolia, commonly known as the rock bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are showy, with five petals arranged in a bell-like shape. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
