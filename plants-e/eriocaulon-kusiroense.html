Eriocaulon kusiroense

Eriocaulon kusiroense, commonly known as the Kusiro marsh plant, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to Japan.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and shallow water bodies. The plant has slender, grass-like stems and small, pale green leaves that are often covered in a fine, hair-like coating. Its flowers are tiny and clustered, producing small, white or pale pink blooms.

Eriocaulon kusiroense is known for its ecological importance in maintaining wetland biodiversity.
