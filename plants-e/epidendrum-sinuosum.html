Epidendrum sinuosum

Epidendrum sinuosum, commonly known as the sinuous orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or other structures without deriving nutrients from them. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in dense spikes. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

Epidendrum sinuosum is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
