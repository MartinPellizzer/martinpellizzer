Erysimum grandiflorum var. majus

Erysimum grandiflorum var. majus, commonly known as the Mexican wallflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It features bright yellow or orange flowers with four petals, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of stems. The plant has a basal rosette of leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or disturbed habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders due to its vibrant blooms and hardy nature.
