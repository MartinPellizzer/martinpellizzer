Echinocactus sinuatus

Echinocactus sinuatus, commonly known as the sinuous hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the deserts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem and spiny, segmented appearance, which helps it retain water in arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green body. Its succulent nature allows it to store water efficiently, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

Echinocactus sinuatus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
