Eupatorium microstemon var. albiflorum

Eupatorium microstemon var. albiflorum, commonly known as white-flowered boneset, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal use.

Its fragrant flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
