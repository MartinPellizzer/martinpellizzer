Episcia punctata

Episcia punctata, commonly known as the spotted waxy plant, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves with distinctive white or yellow spots. It produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, or white.

Episcia punctata thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and well-draining soil.
