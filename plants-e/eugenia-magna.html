Eugenia magna

Eugenia magna, commonly known as the large satinwood, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Eugenia magna is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
