Epidendrum ceraia

Epidendrum ceraia, commonly known as the white-lipped epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often near streams or in forest understories. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with a distinctive lip that is often pink or red.

Epidendrum ceraia is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements in cultivation.
