Eucalyptus gracilis var. yilgarnensis

Eucalyptus gracilis var. yilgarnensis, commonly known as the Yilgarn slender gum, is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted trunk and small, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine, greyish pubescence. The flowers are typically white or pale blue, arranged in clusters, and are followed by small, woody capsules. This species is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands or on rocky outcrops.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
