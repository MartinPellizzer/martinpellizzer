Eryngium galeottii

Eryngium galeottii, commonly known as the blue thistle, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads surrounded by spiny bracts. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in open woodlands. Its foliage consists of narrow, gray-green leaves with a rough texture.

Eryngium galeottii is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
