Elaeagnus s-stylata

Elaeagnus s-stylata, commonly known as silverberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and small, edible berries that ripen in the fall. The plant has a deep root system, allowing it to thrive in arid and rocky soils. It produces fragrant flowers in the spring, which are pollinated by insects.

Elaeagnus s-stylata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
