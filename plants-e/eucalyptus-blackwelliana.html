Eucalyptus blackwelliana

Eucalyptus blackwelliana, commonly known as Blackwell's mallee, is a species of eucalyptus native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its rough, fibrous bark that peels in strips, and its lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, globular clusters of white flowers, which bloom during the winter months. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

This species is notable for its resilience to drought and its role in supporting local wildlife.
