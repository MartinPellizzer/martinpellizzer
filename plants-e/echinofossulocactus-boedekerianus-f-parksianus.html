Echinofossulocactus boedekerianus f. parksianus

Echinofossulocactus boedekerianus f. parksianus, commonly known as the Park's echinofossulocactus, is a rare and unique cactus species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are typically ribbed and covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often found in rocky, well-drained soils in arid environments.

This cultivar is highly valued by collectors for its ornamental appeal and rarity.
