Eremurus bucharicus

Eremurus bucharicus, commonly known as the Afghan giant candleplant, is a striking member of the Xanthorrhoeaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including Afghanistan, Tajikistan, and Uzbekistan. This plant is characterized by its tall, candle-like inflorescence that can reach heights of up to 4 meters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring.

Eremurus bucharicus is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
