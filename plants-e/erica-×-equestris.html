Erica × equestris

Erica × equestris, commonly known as the common heather, is a hybrid shrub native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to form thick, low-growing mats.

Erica × equestris is also valued for its ecological role, providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
