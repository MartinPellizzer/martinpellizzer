Eugenia caryophyllus

Eugenia caryophyllus, commonly known as the clove tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, red-brown flower buds that are harvested and dried to produce cloves, a spice widely used in cooking and traditional medicine. The plant has simple, opposite, and lanceolate leaves that are dark green and glossy. Its flowers are small and white or pale pink, often appearing in clusters.

Eugenia caryophyllus thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
