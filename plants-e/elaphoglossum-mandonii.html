Elaphoglossum mandonii

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble holly leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are arranged in a distinctive pattern along the margins of the fronds.

Elaphoglossum mandonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
