Eupatorium leptophyllum

Eupatorium leptophyllum, commonly known as narrow-leaved thoroughwort, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in moist woodlands and along stream banks, preferring partial to full shade. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small, clustered, and usually white or pale purple.

Eupatorium leptophyllum is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its historical use in traditional medicine.
