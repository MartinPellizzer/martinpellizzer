Eremophila physocalyx

Eremophila physocalyx, commonly known as the narrow-leaved emu bush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Eremophila physocalyx is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
