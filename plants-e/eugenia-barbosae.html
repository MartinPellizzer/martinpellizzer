Eugenia barbosae

Eugenia barbosae, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Eugenia barbosae*, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit, a small, dark red drupe, is edible and often used in local cuisine.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitat.
