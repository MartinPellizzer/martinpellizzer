Ericoides lamprotes

Ericoides lamprotes, commonly known as the silverleaf heath, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with a silvery-blue hue due to the presence of glandular hairs. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathland or moorland environments. It produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale pink, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

Ericoides lamprotes is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
