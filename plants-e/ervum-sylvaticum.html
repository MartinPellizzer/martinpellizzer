Ervum sylvaticum

Ervum sylvaticum, commonly known as the wild vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in woodland edges and disturbed soils. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellow to green pea-like flowers. Its pods contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind.

Ervum sylvaticum is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in ecological restoration projects.
