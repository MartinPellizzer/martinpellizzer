Eurya cernua

Eurya cernua, commonly known as the hanging eurya, is a small evergreen shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its pendulous, dark green leaves that are opposite and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elliptical, orange-red berries. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Eurya cernua is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and seasonal fruit.
