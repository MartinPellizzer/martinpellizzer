Eugenia purpurascens

Eugenia purpurascens, commonly known as the red mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically purple or red when mature, with a thick, segmented rind. The fruit's interior contains white, segmented flesh that is sweet and aromatic.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the edible fruit, which is rich in antioxidants.
