Equisetum heleocharis

Equisetum heleocharis, commonly known as the water horsetail, is a freshwater plant that thrives in wetland environments.

It belongs to the Equisetaceae family and is characterized by its jointed, spore-bearing stems. The plant has a distinctive appearance with whorls of small, needle-like leaves along its stem. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various organisms.

Equisetum heleocharis is also noted for its ability to tolerate a wide range of water conditions.
