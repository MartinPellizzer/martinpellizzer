Encyclia radiata

Encyclia radiata, commonly known as the radiate orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and forest floors. The plant features a pseudobulb with a single, broad, elliptical leaf and produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. Its flowers are characterized by their radial symmetry and have three sepals and three petals, with the lip being prominently marked.

Encyclia radiata is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitat.
