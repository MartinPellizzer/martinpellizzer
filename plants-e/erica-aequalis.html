Erica aequalis

Erica aequalis, commonly known as the Cape heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, evergreen shrub form. The plant produces dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, depending on the variety. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to coastal and mountainous regions.

Erica aequalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and as a ground cover.
