Epidendrum lockhartioides

Epidendrum lockhartioides, commonly known as the Lockhart's Epidendrum, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, producing multiple shoots from a rhizome. The plant features strap-like leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in a raceme, and are notable for their fragrant scent.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
