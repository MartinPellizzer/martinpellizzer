Eria klabatensis

Eria klabatensis, commonly known as the Klabat orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or other structures without deriving nutrients from them. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Eria klabatensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses and gardens.
