Ericameria viscidiflora subsp. planifolia

Ericameria viscidiflora subsp.

planifolia, commonly known as the flatleaf ericameria, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves that are typically gray-green in color and covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellow to golden-orange flowers arranged in dense, flat-topped clusters, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for xeriscaping and ecological restoration projects.
