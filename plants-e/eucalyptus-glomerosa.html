Eucalyptus glomerosa

Eucalyptus glomerosa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers, which are typically arranged in globular inflorescences. Eucalyptus glomerosa is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
