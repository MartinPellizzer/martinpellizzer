Eriogonum covilleanum subsp. adsurgens

Eriogonum covilleanum subsp.

adsurgens, commonly known as the tall curlleaf buckwheat, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its erect, branched stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly curled, giving it the common name "curlleaf.".

It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to full sun conditions.
