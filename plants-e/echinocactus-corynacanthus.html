Echinocactus corynacanthus

Echinocactus corynacanthus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its globular shape and dense covering of spines, which provide protection from herbivores and intense sunlight. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green body. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments.

Echinocactus corynacanthus is a popular choice for cactus gardens due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
