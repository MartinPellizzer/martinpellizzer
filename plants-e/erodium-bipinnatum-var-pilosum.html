Erodium bipinnatum var. pilosum

Erodium bipinnatum var. pilosum, commonly known as the hairy bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender, branching stems covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and also bear a hairy texture.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.
