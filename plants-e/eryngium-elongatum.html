Eryngium elongatum

Eryngium elongatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf thistle, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flower heads and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into bristly seed heads.

Eryngium elongatum is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
