Elymus amurensis

Elymus amurensis, commonly known as Amur wild rye, is a perennial grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in wetlands, meadows, and along riverbanks.

Elymus amurensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
