Eremophila acutifolia

Eremophila acutifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved emu bush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for dry climates. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Eremophila acutifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting native pollinators and wildlife.
