Elaeagnus pungens f. megaphylla

Elaeagnus pungens f. megaphylla, commonly known as the silver leaf elaeagnus, is a variant of the silver bush elaeagnus.

It is characterized by its large, elongated leaves that are silvery-white on the upper surface and dark green on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters, typically in late spring. It is native to China and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage.

This variety is relatively hardy and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for various garden settings.
