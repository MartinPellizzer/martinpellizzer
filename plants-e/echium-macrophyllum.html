Echium macrophyllum

Echium macrophyllum, commonly known as the giant evening primrose, is a striking plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to red, typically blooming in the evening. The plant has silvery-gray foliage with lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Echium macrophyllum is a member of the evening primrose family (Onagraceae) and is known for its ecological importance to pollinators.
