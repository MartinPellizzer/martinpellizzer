Eriosyce marksiana var. gracilis

Eriosyce marksiana var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender marksian cactus, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are often covered with a dense layer of white, woolly hair. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect sunlight to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized cactus species.
