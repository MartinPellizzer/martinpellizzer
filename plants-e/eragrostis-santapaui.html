Eragrostis santapaui

Eragrostis santapaui, commonly known as the Santa Paula grass, is a perennial grass species native to California.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny, spikelet-based flowers. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and typically have a bluish-green color with a rough texture.

Eragrostis santapaui is adapted to dry, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland grasslands.
