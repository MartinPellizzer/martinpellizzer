Euphorbia arillata

Euphorbia arillata, commonly known as the golden crown or crown of thorns, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its branched, spiny stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces a milky latex sap, which is toxic and can cause skin irritation. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-draining soil and minimal water.

Euphorbia arillata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
