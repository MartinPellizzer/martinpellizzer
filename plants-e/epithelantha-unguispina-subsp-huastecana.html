Epithelantha unguispina subsp. huastecana

Epithelantha unguispina subsp.

huastecana, commonly known as the Huastec cactus, is a succulent plant native to Mexico. It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in spines and small, fleshy leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
