Eugenia gryposperma

Eugenia gryposperma, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Eugenia gryposperma*, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil and is characterized by its small, glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, bright red berries. The berries are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

It thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
