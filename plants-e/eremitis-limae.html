Eremitis limae

Eremitis limae, commonly known as the desert spider plant, is a succulent native to arid regions of Africa.

It features fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its root system is shallow and adapted to absorb moisture from the soil surface.

Eremitis limae is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
