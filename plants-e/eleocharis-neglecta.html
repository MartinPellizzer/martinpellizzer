Eleocharis neglecta

Eleocharis neglecta, commonly known as the common spike-rush, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical spikelets that are typically borne on slender, upright stems. The plant produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like in appearance. Eleocharis neglecta is often found in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
