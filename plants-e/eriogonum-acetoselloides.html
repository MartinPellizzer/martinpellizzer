Eriogonum acetoselloides

Eriogonum acetoselloides, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub with hairy stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow to reddish-brown flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

Eriogonum acetoselloides plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
