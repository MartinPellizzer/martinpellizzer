Erica × burnettii

Erica × burnettii, commonly known as the Burnett Heather, is a hybrid evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces clusters of bright pink or red flowers in late spring to early summer, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Erica × burnettii is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for its hardiness and aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes.
