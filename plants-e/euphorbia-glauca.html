Euphorbia glauca

Euphorbia glauca, commonly known as blue milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue-green foliage and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant produces a milky sap, which is a common feature among species in the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Euphorbia glauca is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
