Erica cymosa

Erica cymosa, commonly known as the Heath Erica, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often in coastal or mountainous regions. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in spring. Erica cymosa is well adapted to dry conditions and can thrive in areas with poor soil quality.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in challenging environments.
