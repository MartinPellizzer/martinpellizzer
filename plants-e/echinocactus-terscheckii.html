Echinocactus terscheckii

Echinocactus terscheckii, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and prominent spines, which are typically yellow to reddish in color. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green body. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stem.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for succulent gardens and rockeries.
