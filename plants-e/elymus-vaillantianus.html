Elymus vaillantianus

Elymus vaillantianus, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of Asia and Europe.

It typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields, and can reach heights of up to 1 meter. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikelets that are often arranged in clusters along the stem. Its flowering period usually occurs in the summer months, and it is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Elymus vaillantianus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
