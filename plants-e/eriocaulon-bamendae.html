Eriocaulon bamendae

Eriocaulon bamendae, commonly known as the Cameroon flat sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to Cameroon and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or along streambanks.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
