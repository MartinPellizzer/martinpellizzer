Euphorbia meloformis subsp. valida

Euphorbia meloformis subsp.

valida, commonly known as the "Coral Beauty" or "Coral Spurge," is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa. It features striking, coral-like inflorescences that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow, making it a visually appealing plant.

The leaves are fleshy, oval, and often tinged with red, adding to its ornamental value. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds, contributing to its propagation in natural habitats.
