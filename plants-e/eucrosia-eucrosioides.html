Eucrosia eucrosioides

Eucrosia eucrosioides, commonly known as the yellow lily or golden lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Alstroemeriaceae and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble lilies. The plant features strap-like leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped blooms in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Eucrosia eucrosioides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant color and ease of care.
