Eriogonum flavum subsp. chloranthum

Eriogonum flavum subsp.

chloranthum, commonly known as the yellow flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a bluish-green hue.

It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
