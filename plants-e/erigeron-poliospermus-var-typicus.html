Erigeron poliospermus var. typicus

Erigeron poliospermus var. typicus, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features silvery-blue foliage and produces showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
