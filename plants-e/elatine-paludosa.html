Elatine paludosa

Elatine paludosa, commonly known as marsh stitchwort, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant found in wetlands and marshy areas.

It belongs to the family Elatinaceae and is characterized by its tiny, clustered flowers and delicate, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense mats. Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in nutrient-rich environments make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

Elatine paludosa is also noted for its role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
