Eupatorium virgatum

Eupatorium virgatum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flower clusters that appear in late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Eupatorium virgatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
