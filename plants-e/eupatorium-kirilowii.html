Eupatorium kirilowii

Eupatorium kirilowii, commonly known as Kiriloff's boneset, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and is known for its aromatic, hairy leaves. It produces small, oval fruits called cypselae, which are dispersed by wind.

Eupatorium kirilowii is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
