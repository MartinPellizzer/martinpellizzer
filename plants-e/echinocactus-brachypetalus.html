Echinocactus brachypetalus

Echinocactus brachypetalus, commonly known as the Mexican apple cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its spines are modified leaves that help reduce water loss and deter herbivores.

Echinocactus brachypetalus is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal.
