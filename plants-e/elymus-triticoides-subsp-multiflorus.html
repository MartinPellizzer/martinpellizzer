Elymus triticoides subsp. multiflorus

Elymus triticoides subsp.

multiflorus, commonly known as bluegrass needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to North America. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces dense clusters of spikelets, which are the primary floral structures. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

This species is often found in open woodlands and prairies, contributing to the ecological diversity of these habitats.
