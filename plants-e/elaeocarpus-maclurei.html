Elaeocarpus maclurei

Elaeocarpus maclurei, commonly known as the Maclure's elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and distinctive fruiting structures. The plant produces small, woody fruits that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are typically elliptical with a smooth texture and a glossy surface.

Elaeocarpus maclurei is often found in humid, lowland forests and plays a role in local ecosystems as a food source for various wildlife species.
