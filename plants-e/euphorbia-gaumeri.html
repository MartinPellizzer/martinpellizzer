Euphorbia gaumeri

Euphorbia gaumeri, commonly known as the Gaumer's spurge, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of yellow or green. The plant has a shallow root system and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid environments. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.

Euphorbia gaumeri is also known for its milky sap, which can be irritating to the skin.
