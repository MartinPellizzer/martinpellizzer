Epidendrum amblostoma

Epidendrum amblostoma, commonly known as the St. John's Wort orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It features a sympodial growth habit with pseudobulbs and long, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a dense, woolly pubescence. The plant produces showy, orange-red flowers with a distinctive yellow lip, typically blooming during the summer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining potting media.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its vibrant floral display.
