Eriachne mucronata var. desertorum

Eriachne mucronata var. desertorum, commonly known as the desert flat sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It typically grows in compact, dense tufts or mats, often on sandy or gravelly soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in harsh environments.

This species plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for small desert fauna.
