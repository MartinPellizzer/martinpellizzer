Eriocaulon nantoense var. trisectum

Eriocaulon nantoense var. trisectum, commonly known as the three-lobed spikerush, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, clustered spikelets. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are usually narrow and have a distinct three-lobed structure, which is a key identifying feature.

The inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.
