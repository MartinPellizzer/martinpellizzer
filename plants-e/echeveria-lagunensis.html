Echeveria lagunensis

Echeveria lagunensis, commonly known as the Mexican rose or rose echeveria, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of fleshy, triangular leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, with a powdery coating known as farina. The plant produces clusters of pink or purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is often cultivated for both decorative and horticultural purposes.
