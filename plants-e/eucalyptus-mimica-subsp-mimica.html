Eucalyptus mimica subsp. mimica

Eucalyptus mimica subsp.

mimica, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often glaucous in appearance.

The flowers are typically white or cream, arranged in clusters, and are pollinated by insects. The plant produces woody capsules containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
