Eupatorium triflorum

Eupatorium triflorum, commonly known as threeflower boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct veining pattern. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early fall, producing small, clustered white or pale blue flowers.

The plant is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties historically used by indigenous peoples.
