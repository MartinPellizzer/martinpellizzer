Erigeron canaani

Erigeron canaani, commonly known as the Canary Island Fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn.

Erigeron canaani is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it adapts well to dry and nutrient-poor conditions.
