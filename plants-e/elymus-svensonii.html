Elymus svensonii

Elymus svensonii, commonly known as the Svenson's wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. The plant has slender, blue-green blades and produces dense clusters of spikelets that are often tinged with red or purple. It is valued for its drought tolerance and is sometimes used in restoration projects.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native grassland ecosystems.
