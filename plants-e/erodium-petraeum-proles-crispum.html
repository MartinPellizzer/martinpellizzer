Erodium petraeum proles crispum

Erodium petraeum proles crispum, commonly known as the crinkled rock eryngo, is a flowering plant native to rocky and coastal areas of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-white flowers and finely divided, crinkled leaves. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, often found on limestone or sandy substrates. Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a desirable addition to rock gardens.

The species is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
