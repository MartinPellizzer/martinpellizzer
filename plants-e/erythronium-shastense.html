Erythronium shastense

Erythronium shastense, commonly known as the Shasta trillium, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It features a solitary, showy, white or pinkish flower with three petals and three sepals, often surrounded by three leaf-like bracts. The plant has a short, horizontal rhizome and produces basal leaves that are broadly ovate with a waxy surface. It typically blooms in early spring and is adapted to moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.

Erythronium shastense is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
