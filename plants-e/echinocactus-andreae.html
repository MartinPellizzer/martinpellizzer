Echinocactus andreae

Echinocactus andreae, commonly known as the Mexican apple cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its globular, ribbed stem and is typically spiny, with a dense covering of spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Echinocactus andreae is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
