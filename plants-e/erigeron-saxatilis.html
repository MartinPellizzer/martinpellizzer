Erigeron saxatilis

Erigeron saxatilis, commonly known as the rock eryngo, is a flowering plant native to the rocky habitats of North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny environments such as cliffs, gravelly soils, and disturbed areas. Its leaves are usually gray-green and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are white to pale blue.

Erigeron saxatilis is often found in alpine and arid regions, where it adapts well to harsh environmental conditions.
