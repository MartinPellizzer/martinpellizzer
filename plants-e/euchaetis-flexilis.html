Euchaetis flexilis

Euchaetis flexilis, commonly known as the flexible water plant, is a species of aquatic plant characterized by its delicate, branching structure.

It typically grows in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and streams, where it can anchor itself to substrates with its rhizomes. The plant features slender, flexible stems that can bend without breaking, allowing it to sway with water currents. Its small, elongated leaves are usually submerged, while occasional floating leaves may emerge at the water's surface.

Euchaetis flexilis plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and small aquatic life.
