Eugenia affinis

Eugenia affinis, commonly known as the purple-leafed eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that often display a striking purplish hue, especially in cooler conditions. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns red when mature, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

Eugenia affinis is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
