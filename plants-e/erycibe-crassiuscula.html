Erycibe crassiuscula

Erycibe crassiuscula, commonly known as the dwarf erycibe, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

The fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
