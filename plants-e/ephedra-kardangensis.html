Ephedra kardangensis

Ephedra kardangensis, commonly known as the Kardang ephedra, is a species of gymnosperm native to the arid regions of China.

It is characterized by its jointed, woody stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green cones that contain the toxic alkaloid ephedrine. It thrives in harsh, dry environments and is adapted to survive in extreme temperatures.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in traditional Chinese medicine.
