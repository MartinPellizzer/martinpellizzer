Echeveria grandiflora

Echeveria grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces showy, violet-pink flowers on tall, branching stems during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires ample sunlight to maintain its compact shape.

Due to its striking appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
