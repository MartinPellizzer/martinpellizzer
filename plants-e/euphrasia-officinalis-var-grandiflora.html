Euphrasia officinalis var. grandiflora

Euphrasia officinalis var. grandiflora, commonly known as the greater dog's mercury, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive waxy coating.

It is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported soothing properties.
