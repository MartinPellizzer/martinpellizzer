Eurycaulis compressimentus

Eurycaulis compressimentus, commonly known as the narrowleaf spirea, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has serrated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy in appearance. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and rock gardens.

Eurycaulis compressimentus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
