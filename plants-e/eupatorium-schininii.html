Eupatorium schininii

Eupatorium schininii, commonly known as white boneset, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Eupatorium schininii is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
