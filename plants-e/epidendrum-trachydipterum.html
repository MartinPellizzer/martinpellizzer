Epidendrum trachydipterum

Epidendrum trachydipterum, commonly known as the rough-winged orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It typically grows in humid, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a distinct, hairy lip, and it produces small, elongated pseudobulbs.

This species is appreciated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
