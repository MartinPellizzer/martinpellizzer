Ephedra distachya var. media

Ephedra distachya var. media, commonly known as the slender ephedra, is a shrub-like plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Ephedraceae family and is characterized by its jointed, woody stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces yellow-green flowers in terminal clusters, which are followed by small, spherical seed pods. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments with low rainfall and high temperatures.

Ephedra distachya var. media is often found in rocky soils and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
