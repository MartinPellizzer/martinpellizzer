Epilobium dubium

Epilobium dubium, commonly known as common willowherb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist soils along streambanks, wet meadows, and roadside ditches. The plant features showy pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are erect and often branched.

Epilobium dubium is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for bees and other pollinators.
