Eriochloa setosa

Eriochloa setosa, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils in woodland and wetland habitats. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are wind-pollinated, and it is often found in association with other wetland plants.

Eriochloa setosa plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
