Erodium moschatum

Erodium moschatum, commonly known as sweet everlasting or velvetleaf, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, alternate leaves that are often heart-shaped or ovate in appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Erodium moschatum is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in ornamental gardening.
