Eugenia culta

Eugenia culta, commonly known as the Java apple or Surinam cherry, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It produces small, edible fruits that are often used in culinary applications. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. Its flowers are small and white or pale pink, often appearing in clusters.

Eugenia culta is valued for its ornamental appeal and fruit production in suitable climates.
