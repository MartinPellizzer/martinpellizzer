Echinops heterocephalus

Echinops heterocephalus, commonly known as the African thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble small, fuzzy balls. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by the development of its distinctive bristly seed heads. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Echinops heterocephalus is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a hardy species in its native habitat.
