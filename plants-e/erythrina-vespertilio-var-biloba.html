Erythrina vespertilio var. biloba

Erythrina vespertilio var. biloba, commonly known as the evening bat erythrina, is a striking tropical legume characterized by its vibrant red and orange flowers.

The plant features trifoliate leaves with three leaflets, and its distinctive, flame-like inflorescences attract a variety of pollinators. It is native to regions of Southeast Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. The seeds are encased in a hard, spiny pod, and the plant is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types.

Due to its striking appearance and ecological benefits, it is a popular choice in gardens and landscapes.
