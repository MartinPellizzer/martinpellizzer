Eclipta peduncularis

Eclipta peduncularis, commonly known as Jerusalem cranesbill, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small sunflowers. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as wetlands and along riverbanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with dark centers.

Eclipta peduncularis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
