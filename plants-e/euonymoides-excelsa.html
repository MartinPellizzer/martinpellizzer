Euonymoides excelsa

Euonymoides excelsa, commonly known as the towering euonymus, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, orange-red flowers that appear in late spring. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that are typically yellow or red, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its botanical features make it both visually appealing and ecologically valuable in the right environment.
