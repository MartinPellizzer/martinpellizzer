Eugenia esculenta

Eugenia esculenta, commonly known as the Java apple or soursop, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The fruit is large, oblong, and has a spiny exterior, with a soft, juicy interior containing a fibrous pulp and numerous black seeds. The plant thrives in humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit, which has a sweet and acidic flavor.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
