Euphrasia paludosa var. pedicularoides

Euphrasia paludosa var. pedicularoides, commonly known as the bog gentian, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs and marshes. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its ecological importance in supporting diverse wetland ecosystems.
