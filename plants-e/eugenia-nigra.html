Eugenia nigra

Eugenia nigra, commonly known as black eucalyptus, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the rainforests of eastern Australia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several elliptical seeds.

Eugenia nigra is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
