Echinocactus macrothele

Echinocactus macrothele, commonly known as the Mexican lion's tail, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 3 meters under optimal conditions. Its spines are modified leaves that help reduce water loss and provide protection from herbivores.

Echinocactus macrothele is well adapted to harsh desert environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
