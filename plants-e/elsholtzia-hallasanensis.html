Elsholtzia hallasanensis

Elsholtzia hallasanensis, commonly known as the Hallasan mountain germander, is a flowering plant native to the volcanic slopes of Mount Hallasan in Jeju Island, South Korea.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters at the top of the stems. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a fragrant, mint-like scent.

Elsholtzia hallasanensis is adapted to the harsh, high-altitude environment of its native habitat, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
