Echinopsis millarensis

Echinopsis millarensis, commonly known as the Millarensis cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of Argentina.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces striking white or yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. It has a shallow root system adapted to absorb moisture from the dry soil.

Echinopsis millarensis is well-suited to rocky or sandy soils and requires minimal water, making it a resilient plant in harsh environments.
