Echinops iljinii

Echinops iljinii, commonly known as the Iljin's thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble a spiky globe. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its yellow flowers bloom in summer, adding a striking visual contrast to its tough, woody stems.

Echinops iljinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
