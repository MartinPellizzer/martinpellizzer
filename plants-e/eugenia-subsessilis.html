Eugenia subsessilis

Eugenia subsessilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. It produces small, dark blue or black berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Eugenia subsessilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
