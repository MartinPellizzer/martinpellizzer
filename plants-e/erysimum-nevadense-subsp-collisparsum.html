Erysimum nevadense subsp. collisparsum

Erysimum nevadense subsp.

collisparsum, commonly known as the Sierra wallflower, is a perennial herb native to the Sierra Nevada mountain range in California. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high elevations. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

This subspecies is adapted to the harsh alpine environment and is an important component of the local flora.
