Epicarpurus zeylanica

Epicarpurus zeylanica, commonly known as the Ceylon sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Sri Lanka.

Its botanical features include smooth, greyish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by round, woody fruits containing hard, aromatic seeds. It is valued for its essential oils, which are extracted from its heartwood and used in perfumery and traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and requires a humid, warm climate for optimal growth.
