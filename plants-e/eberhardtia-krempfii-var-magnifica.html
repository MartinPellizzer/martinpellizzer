Eberhardtia krempfii var. magnifica

Eberhardtia krempfii var. magnifica, commonly known as the giant soursop, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, spiny leaves and produces a fruit with a thick, fibrous rind. The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit, which has a sweet and acidic flavor. Its botanical features include a woody stem and showy, white flowers that bloom in clusters.

The species is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its culinary uses in regional cuisines.
