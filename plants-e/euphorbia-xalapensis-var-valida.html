Euphorbia xalapensis var. valida

Euphorbia xalapensis var. valida, commonly known as the Mexican milkweed, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters known as cyathia, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its milky sap, which is toxic, can cause skin irritation and should be handled with care.
