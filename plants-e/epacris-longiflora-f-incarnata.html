Epacris longiflora f. incarnata

Epacris longiflora f. incarnata, commonly known as the pink-flowered epacris, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its elongated, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant typically grows in heathland and open forests, preferring well-drained soils and sunny to partially shaded conditions. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, and it produces showy inflorescences that attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
