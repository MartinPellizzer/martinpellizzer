Eriogonum trichopes var. rubricaule

Eriogonum trichopes var. rubricaule, commonly known as the red-hairy buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are often covered in fine, reddish hairs, which give it its common name.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
