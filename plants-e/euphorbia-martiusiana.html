Euphorbia martiusiana

Euphorbia martiusiana, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a succulent plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom around the holiday season. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is known for its ability to store water in its stems, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. Its leaves are usually opposite and lance-shaped, and it produces small, yellowish flowers surrounded by colorful bracts.

Euphorbia martiusiana is a popular houseplant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.
