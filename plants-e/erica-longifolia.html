Erica longifolia

Erica longifolia, commonly known as the heather or evergreen heather, is a flowering plant native to the western regions of the Mediterranean.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes shrubs and small trees known for their acidic soil preferences. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late summer to early autumn. Erica longifolia thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas and heathlands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
