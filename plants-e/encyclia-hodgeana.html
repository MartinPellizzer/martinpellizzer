Encyclia hodgeana

Encyclia hodgeana, commonly known as the Hodge's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a sympodial growth habit, characterized by its rhizomes and pseudobulbs. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and green, often with a distinctive red or purple lip. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Encyclia hodgeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
