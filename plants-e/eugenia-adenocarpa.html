Eugenia adenocarpa

Eugenia adenocarpa, commonly known as the red bell pepper tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. Its fruit, which resembles a small, red pepper, is edible and often used in local cuisines.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses in its native habitat.
