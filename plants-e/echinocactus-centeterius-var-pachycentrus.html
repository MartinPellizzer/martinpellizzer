Echinocactus centeterius var. pachycentrus

Echinocactus centeterius var. pachycentrus, commonly known as the thick-stemmed hedgehog cactus, is a succulent native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems and spiny, cylindrical segments that provide structural support and protection. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Its slow growth rate and unique appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and cactus collections.
