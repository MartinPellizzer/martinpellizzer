Eriogonum kennedyi subsp. austromontanum

Eriogonum kennedyi subsp.

austromontanum, commonly known as the Austromontanum buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as slopes and canyon walls, where it thrives in full sun. Its leaves are generally lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture, while its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various insect species.
