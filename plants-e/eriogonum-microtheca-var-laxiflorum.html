Eriogonum microtheca var. laxiflorum

Eriogonum microtheca var. laxiflorum, commonly known as the laxiflorum buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to California.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland grasslands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
