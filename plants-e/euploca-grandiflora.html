Euploca grandiflora

Euploca grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered euploca, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands and coastal areas of eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy white to pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. Euploca grandiflora is adapted to saline and brackish environments, making it an important component of coastal vegetation.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
