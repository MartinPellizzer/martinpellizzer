Eupatorium laevigatum var. microcephalum

Eupatorium laevigatum var. microcephalum, commonly known as narrow-leaved boneset, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, white to pale yellow flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often covered with fine, silky hairs.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
