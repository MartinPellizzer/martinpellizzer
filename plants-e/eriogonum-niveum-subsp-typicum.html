Eriogonum niveum subsp. typicum

Eriogonum niveum subsp.

typicum, commonly known as the white flax or Sierra flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its gray-green foliage and clusters of white to pale blue flowers that bloom in the spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, often found in mountainous regions. It has a sprawling growth habit and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
