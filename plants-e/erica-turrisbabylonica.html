Erica turrisbabylonica

Erica turrisbabylonica, commonly known as the Babylonian heath, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and other ericaceous plants. The plant is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and evergreen foliage. Its small, bell-shaped flowers are typically pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Erica turrisbabylonica is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.
