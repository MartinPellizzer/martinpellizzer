Echinocereus paucispinus var. gonacanthus

Echinocereus paucispinus var. gonacanthus, commonly known as the red-tipped hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing cactus with cylindrical stems that are typically green to blue-green in color. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its spines are short and dense, providing protection from herbivores.

This variety is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
