Eriosphaera coriacea

Eriosphaera coriacea, commonly known as the rough-hairy stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features compact, rounded rosettes of thick, waxy leaves that are covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

Eriosphaera coriacea is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
