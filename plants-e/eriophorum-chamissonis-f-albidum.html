Eriophorum chamissonis f. albidum

Eriophorum chamissonis f. albidum, commonly known as the white tussock grass, is a botanical variant of the common tussock grass.

It is characterized by its dense, white-colored inflorescences that distinguish it from other forms of the species. This plant typically grows in wet, boggy environments, thriving in areas with poor drainage. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a mat that helps stabilize the soil.

The plant plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates.
