Eremochloa ophiuroides var. longifolia

Eremochloa ophiuroides var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved spikerush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland environments in eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and long, narrow leaves that can reach up to 50 cm in length. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

Due to its ecological importance, it plays a vital role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
