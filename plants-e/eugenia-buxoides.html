Eugenia buxoides

Eugenia buxoides, commonly known as the dwarf myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and glossy, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and aromatic properties.

Eugenia buxoides is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
