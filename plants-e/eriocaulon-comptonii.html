Eriocaulon comptonii

Eriocaulon comptonii, commonly known as the Compton's spike-rush, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet, marshy environments, often in the understory of tropical or subtropical forests. Its leaves are narrow and usually covered with fine, hair-like structures, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Eriocaulon comptonii plays a role in maintaining the ecological balance of its habitat by providing shelter and food for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
