Euonymus grandiflorus f. longipedunculatus

Euonymus grandiflorus f. longipedunculatus, commonly known as the showy euonymus, is a flowering shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces orange-red berries that are visually striking and attract birds. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its unique floral display and ornamental value make it a desirable addition to gardens and landscapes.
