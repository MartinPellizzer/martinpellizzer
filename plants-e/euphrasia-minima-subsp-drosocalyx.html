Euphrasia minima subsp. drosocalyx

Euphrasia minima subsp.

drosocalyx, commonly known as the small yellow gentian, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, with a distinctive hooded structure.

The plant produces small, oval leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
