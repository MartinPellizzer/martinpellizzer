Encyclia pollardiana

Encyclia pollardiana, commonly known as the Pollard's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Caribbean, particularly found in Jamaica and the Bahamas.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb and linear leaves, and it produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant prefers humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal areas or near freshwater sources.

Its flowers bloom in late summer or early fall, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.
