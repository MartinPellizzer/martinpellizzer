Eugenia boerlagei

Eugenia boerlagei, commonly known as the Boerlage's eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, reddish-brown berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forest understories, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
