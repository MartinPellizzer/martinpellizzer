Erica × eximia var. superba

Erica × eximia var. superba, commonly known as the Giant Heath, is a hybrid evergreen shrub native to the British Isles.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in acidic, well-drained soils. The plant produces striking clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It is well-suited for coastal gardens due to its tolerance of salt spray and strong winds.

Erica × eximia var. superba is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
