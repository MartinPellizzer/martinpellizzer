Eriosema gracile

Eriosema gracile, commonly known as the slender eriosema, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in grassland habitats. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being narrowly elliptical and covered in fine, white pubescence.

Eriosema gracile is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its subtle floral display.
