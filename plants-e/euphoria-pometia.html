Euphoria pometia

Euphoria pometia, commonly known as the white mulberry, is a deciduous tree native to Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The tree produces edible berries that are used in traditional medicine and as a food source for silkworms. Its bark is smooth and gray, with a fragrant aroma.

Euphoria pometia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
