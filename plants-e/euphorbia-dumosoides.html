Euphorbia dumosoides

Euphorbia dumosoides, commonly known as the dwarf spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a compact, low-growing structure with dense clusters of small, fleshy leaves that are typically green or bluish-gray in color. The plant produces yellowish-green cyathia, which are the specialized flower-like structures typical of the Euphorbia genus. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves and stems to survive drought conditions.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and as a houseplant.
