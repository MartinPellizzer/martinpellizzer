Echinocactus asterias

Echinocactus asterias, commonly known as the Mexican star cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped spines and ribbed, cylindrical stems. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne at the top of the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is a popular choice for succulent collectors and xerophytic gardens.
