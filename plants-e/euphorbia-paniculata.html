Euphorbia paniculata

Euphorbia paniculata, commonly known as the panicled spurge, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that can be toxic and irritating to the skin. It typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, thriving in full sun.

Its foliage is usually green with a waxy texture, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.
