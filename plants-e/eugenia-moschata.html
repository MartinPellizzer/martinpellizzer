Eugenia moschata

Eugenia moschata, commonly known as the clove apple or Brazilian clove, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, pinkish-white flowers and oblong, fragrant fruits that resemble small apples. The plant produces essential oils, particularly in its leaves and unripe fruits, which are used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent. Its botanical features include opposite, ovate leaves and a woody, branched stem.

Eugenia moschata thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental and aromatic properties.
