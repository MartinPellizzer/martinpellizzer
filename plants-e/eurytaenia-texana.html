Eurytaenia texana

Eurytaenia texana, commonly known as the Texas false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping in arid regions.

Eurytaenia texana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
