Epipactis viridans f. parviflora

Epipactis viridans f. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered green helleborine, is a variant of the green helleborine orchid native to parts of Europe.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its elongated, grass-like leaves and its inconspicuous, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.

Its flowers are often pollinated by specific insects, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
