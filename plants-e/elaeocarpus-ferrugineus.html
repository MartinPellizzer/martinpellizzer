Elaeocarpus ferrugineus

Elaeocarpus ferrugineus, commonly known as the rusty elaeocarpus, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and elliptical, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers followed by hard, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
