Epidendrum yambrasbambense

Epidendrum yambrasbambense, commonly known as the Yambrass bamboo orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its erect, cane-like stems that resemble bamboo, which are covered in a dense layer of fine, fibrous roots. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This species is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
