Eremostachys olgae

Eremostachys olgae, commonly known as the Olgae's spiny gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, succulent stems and leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Eremostachys olgae is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping in drought-prone areas.
