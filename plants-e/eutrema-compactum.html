Eutrema compactum

Eutrema compactum, commonly known as sea rocket, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in saline environments. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Eutrema compactum is also known for its edible leaves and seeds, which have been used in traditional cuisines.
