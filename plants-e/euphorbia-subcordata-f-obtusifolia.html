Euphorbia subcordata f. obtusifolia

Euphorbia subcordata f. obtusifolia, commonly known as the thickleaf spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

This cultivar is prized for its unique foliage and low maintenance requirements in landscaping.
