Eriogonum laxifolium

Eriogonum laxifolium, commonly known as the slenderleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, alternate leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, which includes other buckwheat species.

Eriogonum laxifolium plays an important role in supporting pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its native range.
