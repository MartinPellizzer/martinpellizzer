Eragrostis agrostoidea

Eragrostis agrostoidea, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in open woodlands, meadows, and disturbed sites, reaching heights of 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant has slender, tufted stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed spikelets.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
