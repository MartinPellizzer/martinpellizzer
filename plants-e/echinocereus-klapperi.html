Echinocereus klapperi

Echinocereus klapperi, commonly known as the klapperi cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in spiny, tuberculate structures. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its compact size and striking appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
