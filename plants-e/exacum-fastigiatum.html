Exacum fastigiatum

Exacum fastigiatum, commonly known as the spotted pimpernel, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a low-growing herb with opposite, ovate leaves that have a distinctive spotted or mottled appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom throughout the year in warm climates. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its compact size and vibrant blooms make it an attractive addition to rock gardens and containers.
