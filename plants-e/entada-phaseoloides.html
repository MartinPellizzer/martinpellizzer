Entada phaseoloides

Entada phaseoloides, commonly known as the African hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its straight, dark bark and long, narrow leaves. The tree produces distinctive, curved pods that contain flat, bean-like seeds. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its hard, dense wood is also valued for construction and furniture-making.
