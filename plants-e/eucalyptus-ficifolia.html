Eucalyptus ficifolia

Eucalyptus ficifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, often in dry, sandy soils. It is well adapted to arid environments and can survive with minimal water.

Eucalyptus ficifolia plays an important role in the ecological systems of its native habitat.
