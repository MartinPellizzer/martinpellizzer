Elaeocarpus rugulosus

Elaeocarpus rugulosus, commonly known as the rough-leaved elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are followed by distinctive, woody fruits with a rough, spiny surface. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
