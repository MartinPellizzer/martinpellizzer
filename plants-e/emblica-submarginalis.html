Emblica submarginalis

Emblica submarginalis, commonly known as the narrow-margin Indian gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with a distinctive narrow margin. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by fleshy, orange-red fruits. The fruit is edible, with a tart flavor, and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
