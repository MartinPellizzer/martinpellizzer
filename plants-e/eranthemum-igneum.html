Eranthemum igneum

Eranthemum igneum, commonly known as the flame bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the warm seasons. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, providing a textured appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its vibrant blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
