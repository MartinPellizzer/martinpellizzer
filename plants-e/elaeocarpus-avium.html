Elaeocarpus avium

Elaeocarpus avium, commonly known as the black sand apple, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy white flowers. The fruit is a woody capsule that turns dark when mature, containing several seeds. The plant is often found in lowland forests and thrives in humid, warm climates.

It has limited economic use but is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitats.
