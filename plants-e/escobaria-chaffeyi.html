Escobaria chaffeyi

Escobaria chaffeyi, commonly known as Chaffey's liveforever, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in arid environments. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers during the spring and summer months. It typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.

Escobaria chaffeyi is often found in desert and chaparral habitats, where it thrives with minimal water and high sunlight.
