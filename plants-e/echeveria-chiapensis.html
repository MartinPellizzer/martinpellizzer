Echeveria chiapensis

Echeveria chiapensis, commonly known as the Chiapanensis Echeveria, is a succulent native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-gray or green in color. The plant produces striking violet or pink flowers during the spring or summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright sunlight.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
