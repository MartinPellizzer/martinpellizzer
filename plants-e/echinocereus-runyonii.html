Echinocereus runyonii

Echinocereus runyonii, commonly known as the Runyon's hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing, spineless cactus with a distinctive appearance featuring a short, thick stem and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant thrives in arid environments and is adapted to full sun exposure. Its fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions.

It is often found in rocky soils and is a popular choice for rock gardens due to its unique form and ornamental value.
