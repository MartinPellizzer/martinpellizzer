Euphorbia teke

Euphorbia teke, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a shrub native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, characterized by its milky sap and often spiny structures. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or green, surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. Its succulent leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in arid environments.

Euphorbia teke is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some regions.
