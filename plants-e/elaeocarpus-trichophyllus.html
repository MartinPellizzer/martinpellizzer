Elaeocarpus trichophyllus

Elaeocarpus trichophyllus, commonly known as the silky elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
