Epigeneium laurifolium

Epigeneium laurifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf epigeneium, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with white or pale yellow spadices, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
