Eugenia laevigata

Eugenia laevigata, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark-brown bark and oval-shaped leaves that are glossy on top and slightly hairy underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by orange-red, fleshy berries. Its fruit is edible, though typically consumed raw or used in jams and sauces.

Eugenia laevigata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
