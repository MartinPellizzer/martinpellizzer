Erysimum × intermedium

Erysimum × intermedium, commonly known as the hybrid wallflower, is a cross between Erysimum cheiri and Erysimum frontatum.

It is a biennial or short-lived perennial plant that typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm. The plant produces bright yellow or orange flowers in late spring to early summer, which are borne on elongated, branched stems. Its foliage consists of basal rosettes of pinnately lobed leaves, which are covered in fine hairs.

Erysimum × intermedium is native to southern Europe and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.
