Echinomastus unguispinus subsp. minimus

Echinomastus unguispinus subsp.

minimus, commonly known as the small-spined echinomastus, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its compact, low-growing form and produces small, spiny stems that are often covered in a fine, white coating.

The plant features clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom during the warmer months. It is adapted to arid and rocky environments, making it a hardy species suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique morphology and drought tolerance make it an interesting subject for botanical study and horticultural cultivation.
