Elaeodendron integrifolium

Elaeodendron integrifolium, commonly known as the smooth-leafed elaeodendron, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically smooth and glossy. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom during the wet season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique leaf structure and its role in local ecosystems.
