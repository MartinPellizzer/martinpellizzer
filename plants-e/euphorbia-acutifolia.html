Euphorbia acutifolia

Euphorbia acutifolia, commonly known as the sharpleaf spurge, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features a compact, bushy growth habit with narrow, pointed leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are its unique flower-like structures. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Euphorbia acutifolia is also known for its ability to store water in its leaves, aiding in its survival in harsh conditions.
