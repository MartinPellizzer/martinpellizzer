Encelia adenophora var. nigromaculata

Encelia adenophora var. nigromaculata, commonly known as the black-spotted bush sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, featuring gray-green foliage with distinctive black or dark brown spots. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in poor soils and withstand drought conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in native plant landscapes.
