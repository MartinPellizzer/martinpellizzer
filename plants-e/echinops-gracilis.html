Echinops gracilis

Echinops gracilis, commonly known as the slender thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that are covered in small, bristly structures. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically blue-green in color and have a rough, hairy texture.

Echinops gracilis is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought-resistant properties.
