Eragrostis spicigera

Eragrostis spicigera, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open woodlands, meadows, and disturbed soils, showcasing its adaptability to various environmental conditions. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable component of native plant communities.
