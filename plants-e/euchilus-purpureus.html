Euchilus purpureus

Euchilus purpureus, commonly known as the purple thistle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, purple-tinged leaves and showy, purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Native to the Mediterranean region, this plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening due to its distinctive appearance.

The plant also serves as a habitat for various insects and small animals.
