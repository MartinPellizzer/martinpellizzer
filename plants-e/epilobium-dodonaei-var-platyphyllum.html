Epilobium dodonaei var. platyphyllum

Epilobium dodonaei var. platyphyllum, commonly known as the broadleaf willow herb, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, often calcareous soils. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface and produces clusters of small, pinkish to reddish flowers. Its flowers are actinomorphic, with five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Onagraceae family.

It is often found in wet meadows, stream banks, and other damp habitats.
