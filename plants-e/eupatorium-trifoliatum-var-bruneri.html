Eupatorium trifoliatum var. bruneri

Eupatorium trifoliatum var. bruneri, commonly known as the Bruner's boneset, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, clustered, white to pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and stream banks. It is characterized by its aromatic foliage and is often used in wetland restoration projects.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and maintaining biodiversity in riparian zones.
