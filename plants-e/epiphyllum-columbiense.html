Epiphyllum columbiense

Epiphyllum columbiense, commonly known as the Mexican night bloomer, is a member of the Cactaceae family.

It is a climbing cactus that produces large, showy flowers that open at night. The plant is native to Mexico and is often found growing on trees or rocks in its natural habitat. Its stems are segmented and covered in spines, while its leaves are reduced to small, scale-like structures.

The flowers are typically pink or white and have a strong, sweet fragrance.
