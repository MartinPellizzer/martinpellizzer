Engelhardia pterococca

Engelhardia pterococca, commonly known as the black mangrove, is a tropical tree native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black bark and its ability to thrive in saline environments. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are usually opposite in arrangement. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

Engelhardia pterococca plays an important role in coastal ecosystems by stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial species.
