Eria longibracteata

Eria longibracteata, commonly known as the long-bract orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and linear, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. The plant produces striking, elongated inflorescences bearing numerous small, fragrant flowers with white to pale yellow petals and sepals. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in tropical gardens and botanical collections.
