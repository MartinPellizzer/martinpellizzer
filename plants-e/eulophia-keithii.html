Eulophia keithii

Eulophia keithii, commonly known as the Keith's eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insects.

Eulophia keithii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitats.
