Eucalyptus × tenandrensis

Eucalyptus × tenandrensis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a hybrid eucalyptus species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed appearance and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, globular flower clusters that range in color from white to pale yellow.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
