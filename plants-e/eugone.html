Eugone

Eugone, commonly known as the common eugone, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Scrophulariaceae.

It is characterized by its erect or spreading growth habit and produces showy, often two-lipped flowers. The leaves are typically opposite and ovate to lanceolate in shape. Eugone species are native to various regions in the Americas and are often found in moist, sunny habitats.

They are valued in horticulture for their ornamental appeal and adaptability to different soil conditions.
