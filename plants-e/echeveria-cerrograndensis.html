Echeveria cerrograndensis

Echeveria cerrograndensis, commonly known as the Grand Mountain Echeveria, is a succulent native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss. The plant produces showy clusters of pink or purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
