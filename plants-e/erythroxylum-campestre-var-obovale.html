Erythroxylum campestre var. obovale

Erythroxylum campestre var. obovale, commonly known as the narrow-leaf copal tree, is a shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, obovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is valued for its latex, which has been used traditionally for various purposes, including chewing and medicinal applications.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
