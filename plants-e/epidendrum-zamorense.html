Epidendrum zamorense

Epidendrum zamorense, commonly known as the Zamora Epidendrum, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its epiphytic growth habit, often found growing on trees or rocks. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink, which bloom during the wet season. Its leaves are typically elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it thrives in humid, tropical environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
