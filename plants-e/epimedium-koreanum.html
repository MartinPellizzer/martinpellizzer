Epimedium koreanum

Epimedium koreanum, commonly known as the Korean barbatan, is a deciduous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Berberidaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental foliage and unique flower structure. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are often reddish or bronze in color, providing visual interest throughout the growing season. Its flowers, which appear in early spring, are small and clustered, typically in shades of yellow or green.

Epimedium koreanum is well-suited for woodland gardens and thrives in partial shade with well-drained soil.
