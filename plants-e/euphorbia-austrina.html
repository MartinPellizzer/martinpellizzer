Euphorbia austrina

Euphorbia austrina, commonly known as the Australian spurge, is a succulent plant native to Australia.

It features a short, thick stem with cylindrical, fleshy leaves that are typically green or bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are often surrounded by colorful bracts. It is adapted to arid environments and can store water in its leaves and stems.

Euphorbia austrina is also known for its milky sap, which can be irritating to the skin.
