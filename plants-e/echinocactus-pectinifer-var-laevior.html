Echinocactus pectinifer var. laevior

Echinocactus pectinifer var. laevior, commonly known as the smooth pectinifer cactus, is a slow-growing species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in pale green, smooth spines, distinguishing it from other varieties in the genus. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers during the summer months, which are often followed by red fruits. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun exposure, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and collectors.
