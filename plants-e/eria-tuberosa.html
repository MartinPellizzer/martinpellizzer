Eria tuberosa

Eria tuberosa, commonly known as the tuberous eria, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tuberous roots that store nutrients. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often arranged in clusters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its aesthetic appeal.
