Edraianthus caricinus

Edraianthus caricinus, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette at the base. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Edraianthus caricinus is known for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
