Eupatorium ibaguense

Eupatorium ibaguense, commonly known as the Ibaguense boneset, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Colombia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and stream banks. It produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are often aromatic and hairy.

Eupatorium ibaguense is valued for its potential medicinal properties and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
