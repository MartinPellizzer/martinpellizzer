Euphorbia lucida var. salicifolia

Euphorbia lucida var. salicifolia, commonly known as the silky euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a distinctive appearance with soft, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are its unique floral structures. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid climates.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and xeriscapes.
