Exechostylus flaviflorus

Exechostylus flaviflorus, commonly known as the yellow-flowered exechostylus, is a member of the Apocynaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a follicle that splits open to release seeds.

This species is native to parts of Central and South America and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
