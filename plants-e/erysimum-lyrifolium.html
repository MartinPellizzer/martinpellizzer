Erysimum lyrifolium

Erysimum lyrifolium, commonly known as the lyrifolium brassica, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves and its clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard plants. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
