Ervum diphyllum

Ervum diphyllum, commonly known as the two-leaved vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and compound leaves that are often divided into two pairs of leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by small, elongated pods containing several seeds. Ervum diphyllum is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural settings.
