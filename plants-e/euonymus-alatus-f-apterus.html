Euonymus alatus f. apterus

Euonymus alatus f. apterus, commonly known as the winged euonymus, is a compact shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, winged stems that give it its common name. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly toothed margin. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by red, lantern-like fruits that persist into winter.

This cultivar is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.
