Eugenia purpuricarpa

Eugenia purpuricarpa, commonly known as the purple beauty apple, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or purple when ripe, which are edible but have a tart flavor. Eugenia purpuricarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to a variety of tropical and subtropical climates.
