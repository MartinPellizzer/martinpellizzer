Eulalia mutica

Eulalia mutica, commonly known as the slender grassvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with tiny, pale green to yellowish flowers. It typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is found in various regions across Europe and parts of Asia.

Eulalia mutica is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
