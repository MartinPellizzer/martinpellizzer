Eryngium officinale

Eryngium officinale, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiky, blue or purple flower heads and silver-gray foliage. The plant grows in coastal areas and saline soils, showing a high tolerance for salt and wind. Its flowers are typically arranged in a dense, thistle-like cluster, and the leaves are narrow and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Eryngium officinale is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
