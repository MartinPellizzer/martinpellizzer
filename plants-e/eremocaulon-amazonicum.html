Eremocaulon amazonicum

Eremocaulon amazonicum, commonly known as the Amazonian waterwheel, is a unique aquatic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its whorled arrangement of leaves, which resemble the spokes of a wheel, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as rivers and lakes, where it can anchor itself to submerged surfaces. Its leaves are typically narrow and elongated, with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss.

Eremocaulon amazonicum plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
