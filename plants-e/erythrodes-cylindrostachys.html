Erythrodes cylindrostachys

Erythrodes cylindrostachys, commonly known as the scarlet-stemmed candelabra, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems that can reach up to 3 meters in height, with clusters of small, vibrant red flowers. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its striking appearance, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its ornamental value.
