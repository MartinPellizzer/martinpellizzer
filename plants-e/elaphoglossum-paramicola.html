It is native to the Andean regions of South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lanceolate fronds with a distinctive, finely divided structure. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Elaphoglossum paramicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
