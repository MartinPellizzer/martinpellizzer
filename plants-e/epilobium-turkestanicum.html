Epilobium turkestanicum

Epilobium turkestanicum, commonly known as the Turkestan willow herb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in moist, temperate environments such as meadows and riverbanks. The plant features slender, erect stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture. Its flowers are pink to purple, arranged in clusters, and bloom in the summer months.

Epilobium turkestanicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
