Epidendrum marcapatense

Epidendrum marcapatense, commonly known as the Andean orchid, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of violet or purple blooms, which are often adorned with intricate patterns and a pleasant aroma. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in its native habitat.

Due to its striking appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists alike.
