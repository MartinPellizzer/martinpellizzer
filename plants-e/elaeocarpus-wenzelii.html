Elaeocarpus wenzelii

Elaeocarpus wenzelii, commonly known as the Wenzel's elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland and hill forest regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
