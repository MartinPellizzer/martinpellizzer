Elaeocarpus submonoceras subsp. oxypyren

Elaeocarpus submonoceras subsp.

oxypyren, commonly known as the red-fruit elaeocarp, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae. It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia.

The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and produces clusters of red, fleshy fruits that are edible. The fruits are typically consumed by various bird species, aiding in seed dispersal.

It thrives in tropical rainforest environments and is often found in shaded, moist undergrowth.
