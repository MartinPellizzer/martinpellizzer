Eriogonum abertianum var. lappulaceum

Eriogonum abertianum var. lappulaceum, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its low-growing habit and silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to drought conditions and harsh desert climates.
