Elaeagnus argyi

Elaeagnus argyi, commonly known as silverberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves and small, edible berries that ripen in the fall. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy choice for landscaping in dry regions. It produces fragrant flowers in the spring, which are pollinated by insects.

Elaeagnus argyi is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, enhancing soil fertility.
