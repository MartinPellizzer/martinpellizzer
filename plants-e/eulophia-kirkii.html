Eulophia kirkii

Eulophia kirkii, commonly known as the Kirk's eulophia, is a member of the Orchidaceae family native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and green. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest habitats, often on tree trunks or rocks. Its pseudobulbs are cylindrical, and it has linear, dark green leaves that help in photosynthesis.

Eulophia kirkii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
