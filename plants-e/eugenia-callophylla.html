Eugenia callophylla

Eugenia callophylla, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that appear in clusters. Its fruit is a smooth, red, fleshy berry that contains numerous seeds.

Eugenia callophylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Indigenous Australian practices.
