Elaeocarpus kwangtungensis

Elaeocarpus kwangtungensis, commonly known as the Guangdong elaeocarp, is a deciduous tree native to southern China.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by edible, fleshy fruits that are typically purple or red in color.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas, contributing to local biodiversity.
