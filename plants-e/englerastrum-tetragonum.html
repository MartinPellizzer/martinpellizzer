Englerastrum tetragonum

Englerastrum tetragonum, commonly known as the four-angled morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, four-angled stems and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, often blooming in the early morning. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, bean-like seeds.

Englerastrum tetragonum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
