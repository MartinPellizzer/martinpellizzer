Eleutherococcus japonicus f. kiusianus

Eleutherococcus japonicus f. kiusianus, commonly known as the Japanese prickly ash, is a variant of the eleutherococcus plant native to East Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces red berries in the fall, which are edible but often have a sharp, acidic taste. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
