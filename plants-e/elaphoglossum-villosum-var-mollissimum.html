Elaphoglossum villosum var. mollissimum

Elaphoglossum villosum var. mollissimum, commonly known as the soft-hairy elaphoglossum, is a fern species native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are covered in soft, velvety hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its spores are produced in small, clustered structures called sori, which are located on the underside of the fronds.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage.
