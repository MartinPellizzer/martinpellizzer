Elymus arenarius var. brevispicus

Elymus arenarius var. brevispicus, commonly known as the short-awned sandgrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences, which bear short, blunt awns. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

This variety is often found in disturbed or open habitats, contributing to soil stabilization and erosion control.
