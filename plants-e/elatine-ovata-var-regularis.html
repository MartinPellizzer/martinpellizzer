Elatine ovata var. regularis

Elatine ovata var. regularis, commonly known as the common waterwort, is a small aquatic plant characterized by its delicate, stem-like appearance and small, oval-shaped leaves.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds, ditches, and slow-moving streams. The plant has a branching structure with whorled leaves that are often slightly notched at the edges. It produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spikes, which bloom from late spring to early autumn.

This variety is known for its adaptability to various water conditions and its role in aquatic ecosystems.
