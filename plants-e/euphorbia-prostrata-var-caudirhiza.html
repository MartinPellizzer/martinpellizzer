Euphorbia prostrata var. caudirhiza

Euphorbia prostrata var. caudirhiza, commonly known as the trailing spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a prostrate growth habit with fleshy, ribbed stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are its characteristic flower-like structures. Its leaves are typically reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it is adapted to arid environments.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appearance.
