Espeletia pycnophylla subsp. angelensis

Espeletia pycnophylla subsp.

angelensis, commonly known as the Angel's Angel or Angel's Flower, is a unique plant native to the high-altitude regions of Colombia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and striking, showy capitula.

The plant thrives in humid, cool environments, often found in cloud forests. Its leaves are typically covered in a thick layer of white, fuzzy hairs, which help to retain moisture and protect against intense sunlight.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its distinctive appearance.
