Eucalyptus shiressii

Eucalyptus shiressii, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallee, is a tall, multi-stemmed shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark and lance-shaped leaves that are typically narrow and grey-green in color. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that range in color from white to pink, which attract various pollinators. Eucalyptus shiressii is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in arid and semi-arid regions.

It is also valued for its aromatic oils, which have been used in traditional and modern applications.
