Eryngium dubium

Eryngium dubium, commonly known as the common thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its spiny, blue-green leaves and purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides. Its flowers are composed of both ray and disk florets, and it produces small, dry fruits known as achenes.

Eryngium dubium is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
