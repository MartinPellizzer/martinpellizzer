Ellipeiopsis ferruginea

Ellipeiopsis ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty leaf orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with a distinctive rusty-brown hue, giving it its common name.

Ellipeiopsis ferruginea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
