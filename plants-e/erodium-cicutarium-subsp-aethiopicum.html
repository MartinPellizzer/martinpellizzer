Erodium cicutarium subsp. aethiopicum

Erodium cicutarium subsp.

aethiopicum, commonly known as African bird's-beak, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is an annual herb that typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and grasslands.

The plant features slender, branched stems and small, delicate leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers are pink to purple, with five petals, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.

It is often found in open habitats and is adapted to arid or semi-arid conditions.
