Eriogonum strictum var. strictum

Eriogonum strictum var. strictum, commonly known as the narrowleaf buckwheat, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and gravelly soils. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, clustered flowers are usually white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer.

This species is adapted to arid environments and is often used in native plant gardens for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
