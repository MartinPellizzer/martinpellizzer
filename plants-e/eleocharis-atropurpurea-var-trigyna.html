Eleocharis atropurpurea var. trigyna

Eleocharis atropurpurea var. trigyna, commonly known as purple spike rush, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescences that are typically purple or reddish in color. The plant produces small, scale-like flowers that are wind-pollinated, a common trait among sedges. It thrives in wetland environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.
