Erysimum polyceratium

Erysimum polyceratium, commonly known as the clustered wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with four-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit. Its flowers are usually bright yellow or orange, with four petals arranged in a cross shape, typical of the family.

It is often found in disturbed habitats and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.
