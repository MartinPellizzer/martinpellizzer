Echinocactus acifer var. spinosus

Echinocactus acifer var. spinosus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and dense, spiny exterior, which provides protection from herbivores and excessive sunlight. The plant features a thick, ribbed stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. Its flowers, which are typically yellow or orange, bloom in late summer and attract pollinators such as bees and birds.

This species is popular in horticulture due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
