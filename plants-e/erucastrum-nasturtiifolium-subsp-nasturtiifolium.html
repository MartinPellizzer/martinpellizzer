Erucastrum nasturtiifolium subsp. nasturtiifolium

Erucastrum nasturtiifolium subsp.

nasturtiifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leafed nasturtium, is a species native to the Mediterranean region. It is an annual herb characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically narrow and slightly lobed.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are often clustered in racemes, and it is known for its peppery flavor due to the presence of glucosinolates. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and edible landscaping.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and useful in culinary applications.
