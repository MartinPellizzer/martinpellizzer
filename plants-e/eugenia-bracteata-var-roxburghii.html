Eugenia bracteata var. roxburghii

Eugenia bracteata var. roxburghii, commonly known as the red mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, glossy dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible, bright red fruits with a thick, segmented rind and a sweet, aromatic pulp. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
