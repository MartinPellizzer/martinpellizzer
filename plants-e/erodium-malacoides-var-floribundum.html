Erodium malacoides var. floribundum

Erodium malacoides var. floribundum, commonly known as the purplestem sand-verbena, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small annual herb characterized by its slender, branched stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or gravelly habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
