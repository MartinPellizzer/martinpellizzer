Erigeron acris subsp. asadbarensis

Erigeron acris subsp.

asadbarensis, commonly known as the Afghan blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the high elevations of Central Asia. It is characterized by its blue to purple composite flowers and hairy stems, typical of the Asteraceae family.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, showy flower heads that attract pollinators.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
