Elaeagnus bambusetorum

Elaeagnus bambusetorum, commonly known as the bamboo silverberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, bamboo-like stems and compound leaves that are often silvery-white beneath. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, berry-like fruits that are rich in nutrients. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates.

Elaeagnus bambusetorum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
