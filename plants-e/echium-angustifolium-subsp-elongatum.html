Echium angustifolium subsp. elongatum

Echium angustifolium subsp.

elongatum, commonly known as the slender fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and spikes of blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This subspecies is adapted to arid environments and is often found in regions with well-drained soils.
