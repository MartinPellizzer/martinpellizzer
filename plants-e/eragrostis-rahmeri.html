Eragrostis rahmeri

Eragrostis rahmeri, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and slopes.

Eragrostis rahmeri plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small invertebrates.
