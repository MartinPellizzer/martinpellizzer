Echinocactus gibbosus var. pluricostatus

Echinocactus gibbosus var. pluricostatus, commonly known as the Mexican torch cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are ribbed and covered in spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
