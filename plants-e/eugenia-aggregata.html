Eugenia aggregata

Eugenia aggregata, commonly known as the clustered myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Eugenia aggregata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
