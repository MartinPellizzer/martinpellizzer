Evelyna maculata

Evelyna maculata, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive spotted leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a waxy surface, and the flowers are usually pink or purple in color.

Evelyna maculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
