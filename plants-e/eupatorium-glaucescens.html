Eupatorium glaucescens

Eupatorium glaucescens, commonly known as blue eupatorium, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright stems covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a bluish-gray appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to lavender flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly rough texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along streambanks.
