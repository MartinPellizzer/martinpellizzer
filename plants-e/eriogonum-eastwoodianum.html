Eriogonum eastwoodianum

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its clustered, small white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as sagebrush steppe and grasslands. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces seed capsules that disperse its seeds via the wind.
