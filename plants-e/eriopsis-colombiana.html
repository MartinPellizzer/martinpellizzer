Eriopsis colombiana

Eriopsis colombiana, commonly known as the Colombian eriopsis, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its yellow flowers are small and clustered, forming a rounded inflorescence that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Eriopsis colombiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
