Epidendrum grandiflorum

Epidendrum grandiflorum, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, using its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Epidendrum grandiflorum is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
