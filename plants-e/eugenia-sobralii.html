Eugenia sobralii

Eugenia sobralii, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or Sobral's eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its small, red, cherry-like fruits that are edible. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

The flowers are small, white or pale pink, and are clustered in inflorescences, contributing to its ornamental value.
