Excoecaria unequidentata

Excoecaria unequidentata, commonly known as the African banyan, is a tropical tree native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, spreading canopy and long, hanging roots that can form a dense network around the base of the trunk. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its flowers are small and greenish-yellow, typically appearing in clusters, and it produces a distinctive, fleshy, red fruit.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some regions.
