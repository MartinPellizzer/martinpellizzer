Eugenia bimarginata

Eugenia bimarginata, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and dark, aromatic wood, which has been historically valued for its use in furniture and incense. Eugenia bimarginata typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters.

Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a distinctive red margin, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
