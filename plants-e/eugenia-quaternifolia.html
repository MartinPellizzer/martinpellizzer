Eugenia quaternifolia

Eugenia quaternifolia, commonly known as the four-leaf eugenia, is a small evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Brazil.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often four-lobed, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
