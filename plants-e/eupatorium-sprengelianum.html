Eupatorium sprengelianum

Eupatorium sprengelianum, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant features finely divided, gray-green leaves that are aromatic when crushed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Eupatorium sprengelianum is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
