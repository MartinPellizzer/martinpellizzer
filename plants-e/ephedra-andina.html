Ephedra andina

Ephedra andina, commonly known as the Andean ephedra, is a shrub native to the high altitudes of the Andes Mountains.

It belongs to the Ephedraceae family and is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and greenish-yellow flowers. The plant contains alkaloids, particularly ephedrine, which has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing at elevations above 3,000 meters.

Due to its medicinal value, it is also of interest in modern pharmacology.
