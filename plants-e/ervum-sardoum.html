Ervum sardoum

Ervum sardoum, commonly known as the Sardinian bean, is a flowering plant native to the island of Sardinia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with trifoliate leaves and small white or purple flowers. Its pods contain several edible seeds, which are rich in protein and often used in local cuisine.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun exposure.
