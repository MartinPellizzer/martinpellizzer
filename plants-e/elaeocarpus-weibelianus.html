Elaeocarpus weibelianus

Elaeocarpus weibelianus, commonly known as the Weibel's elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, edible seeds. This plant is often found in lowland rainforests and thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the understory vegetation in its native habitat.
