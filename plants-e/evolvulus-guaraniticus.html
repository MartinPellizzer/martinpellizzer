Evolvulus guaraniticus

Evolvulus guaraniticus, commonly known as the Brazilian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant blue or purple flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in warm, sunny environments.

Evolvulus guaraniticus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
