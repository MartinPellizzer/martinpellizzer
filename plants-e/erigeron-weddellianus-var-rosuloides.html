Erigeron weddellianus var. rosuloides

Erigeron weddellianus var. rosuloides, commonly known as the Rosuloid Daisy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, silver-gray leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces daisy-like flowers with white ray florets and yellow disk florets, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant landscapes.
