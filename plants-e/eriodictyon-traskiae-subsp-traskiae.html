Eriodictyon traskiae subsp. traskiae

Eriodictyon traskiae subsp.

traskiae, commonly known as California licorice, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of California. It is characterized by its gray-green foliage and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain aromatic seeds. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal scrub habitats.

Due to its unique scent and historical use by indigenous peoples, it is valued both botanically and culturally.
