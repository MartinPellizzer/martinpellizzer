Evolvulus sericeus subsp. holosericeus

Evolvulus sericeus subsp.

holosericeus, commonly known as silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America. It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage and trumpet-shaped blue or violet flowers.

The plant thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or disturbed soils. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
