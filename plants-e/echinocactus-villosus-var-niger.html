Echinocactus villosus var. niger

Echinocactus villosus var. niger, commonly known as the black-hairy cactus, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems covered in dense, dark-colored spines that give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its dark foliage. It is well adapted to dry environments, storing water in its fleshy stems and requiring minimal care.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and cactus collections.
