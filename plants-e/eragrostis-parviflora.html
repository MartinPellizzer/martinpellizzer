Eragrostis parviflora

Eragrostis parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, pale green to brown spikelets along its flowering stems. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-gray sheen, and it is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Eragrostis parviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
