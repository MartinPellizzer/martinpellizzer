Erianthus beccarii

Erianthus beccarii, commonly known as the African silver grass, is a tall, perennial grass native to tropical Africa.

It features long, slender, silver-white leaves that give it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

Erianthus beccarii is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.
