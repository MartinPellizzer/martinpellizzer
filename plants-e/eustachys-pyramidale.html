Eustachys pyramidale

Eustachys pyramidale, commonly known as the common panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and smooth. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are often wind-pollinated, a common trait among grasses. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in meadows, fields, and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it an important component of ecological systems and a useful plant for erosion control.
