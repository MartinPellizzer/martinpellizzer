Echinocactus crispatus var. horridus

Echinocactus crispatus var. horridus, commonly known as the Mexican hat cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in spines, which provide protection against herbivores and reduce water loss. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the summer months, adding a striking visual element to its environment. Its succulent nature allows it to store water efficiently, making it well adapted to dry conditions.

This variety is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
