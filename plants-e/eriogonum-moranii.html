Eriogonum moranii

Eriogonum moranii, commonly known as Moran's buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showing a high degree of adaptability to harsh conditions. Moran's buckwheat plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
