Eupatorium libanoticum

Eupatorium libanoticum, commonly known as the Lebanon boneset, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly rough texture, while its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn.

Eupatorium libanoticum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
