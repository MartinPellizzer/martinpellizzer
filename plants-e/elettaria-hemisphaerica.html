Elettaria hemisphaerica

Elettaria hemisphaerica, commonly known as the lesser cardamom, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, finger-like rhizomes that are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its rhizomes contain essential oils and compounds that contribute to its distinctive spicy and sweet aroma.

The botanical features of Elettaria hemisphaerica make it a valuable species in both horticulture and pharmacology.
