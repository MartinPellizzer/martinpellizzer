Ericoides zeyheri

Ericoides zeyheri, commonly known as the Zeyher's heath, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is a shrub-like plant characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white in color. It is native to southern Africa, where it thrives in acidic, well-drained soils.

Ericoides zeyheri is often found in rocky or mountainous regions, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
