Euphorbia katrajensis

Euphorbia katrajensis, commonly known as the Katraj euphorbia, is a flowering plant native to Iran.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its succulent, spiny stems and distinctive yellowish-green flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, featuring thick leaves that help reduce water loss. It produces small, clustered flowers that are often surrounded by modified leaves called cyathia.

Euphorbia katrajensis is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is a subject of interest for botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
