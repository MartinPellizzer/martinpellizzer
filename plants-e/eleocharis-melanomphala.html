Eleocharis melanomphala

Eleocharis melanomphala, commonly known as blackroot sedge, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its roots are dark-colored, which gives it its common name.
