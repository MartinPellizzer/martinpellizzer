Euphorbia bruynsii

Euphorbia bruynsii, commonly known as the Bruyns' spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by colorful bracts, which are often the only visible part of the inflorescence. It thrives in arid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Euphorbia bruynsii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to withstand drought conditions.
