Eragrostis vahlii var. sejuncta

Eragrostis vahlii var. sejuncta, commonly known as the narrowleaf lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters, which are often tinged with reddish or purplish hues. This plant is adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to thrive in poor soils.

It is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.
