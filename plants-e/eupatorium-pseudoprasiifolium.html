Eupatorium pseudoprasiifolium

Eupatorium pseudoprasiifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved boneset, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, upright stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It typically blooms in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Eupatorium pseudoprasiifolium is often found in moist meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.
