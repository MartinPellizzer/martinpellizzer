Erysimum cuspidatum f. brevistylos

Erysimum cuspidatum f. brevistylos, commonly known as the short-styled wallflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are usually bright yellow or orange, with a short, narrow style that distinguishes it from other forms of Erysimum cuspidatum.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
