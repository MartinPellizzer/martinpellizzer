Eremocharis confinis

Eremocharis confinis, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. This epiphytic plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on trees or rocks. Its unique appearance includes pale green or white flowers with intricate patterns.

Due to its specific habitat requirements and habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is subject to conservation efforts.
