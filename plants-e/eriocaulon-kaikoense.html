Eriocaulon kaikoense

Eriocaulon kaikoense, commonly known as the Kaikoense flat sedge, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting aquatic ecosystems.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland conservation efforts.
