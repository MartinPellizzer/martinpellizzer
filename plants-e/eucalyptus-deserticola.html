Eucalyptus deserticola

Eucalyptus deserticola, commonly known as the desert eucalypt, is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, often grey or white bark that peels in thin, fibrous layers. The leaves are typically narrow, linear, and often covered with a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Its flowers are small, usually white or pale blue, and appear in clusters, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects.

The plant is well adapted to drought conditions and plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils in its native habitat.
