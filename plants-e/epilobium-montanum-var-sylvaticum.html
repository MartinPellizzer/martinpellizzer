Epilobium montanum var. sylvaticum

Epilobium montanum var. sylvaticum, commonly known as the mountain willow or alpine willow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in moist, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. The plant features slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers are showy, occurring in clusters of two to five, and are usually pink to reddish in color.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators in high-altitude environments.
