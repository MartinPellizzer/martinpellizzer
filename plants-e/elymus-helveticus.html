Elymus helveticus

Elymus helveticus, commonly known as the Alpine basketgrass, is a perennial grass native to mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that contain small, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Elymus helveticus plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various alpine organisms.
