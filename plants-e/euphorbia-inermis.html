Euphorbia inermis

Euphorbia inermis, commonly known as the smooth spurge, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually lance-shaped and covered in a waxy coating. Its flowers are small and clustered in structures called cyathia, which are surrounded by yellowish bracts.

Euphorbia inermis is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.
