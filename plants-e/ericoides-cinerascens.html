Ericoides cinerascens

Ericoides cinerascens, commonly known as the ash-gray ericoid, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color, providing a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple, blooming in the spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in high-altitude habitats.

Ericoides cinerascens is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in its native environment.
