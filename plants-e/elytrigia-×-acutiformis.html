Elytrigia × acutiformis

Elytrigia × acutiformis, commonly known as the hybrid meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with two to three flowering glumes, and its flowers are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.

Elytrigia × acutiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators and soil stabilization.
