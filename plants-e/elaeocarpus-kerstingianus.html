Elaeocarpus kerstingianus

Elaeocarpus kerstingianus, commonly known as the Kersting's elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The tree produces woody, spherical fruits that are typically clustered in groups of three. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
