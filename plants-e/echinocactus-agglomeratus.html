Echinocactus agglomeratus

Echinocactus agglomeratus, commonly known as the Mexican Fencepost Cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green with a grayish bloom. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are borne on short, branched pedicels. It is well adapted to arid environments, with spines that help reduce water loss and deter herbivores.

This species is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its hardy nature and attractive appearance.
