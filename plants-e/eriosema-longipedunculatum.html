Eriosema longipedunculatum

Eriosema longipedunculatum, commonly known as the long-peduncled eriosema, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, hairy foliage and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Its seeds are enclosed in legumes, which are typical of the family, and the plant plays a role in nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with root nodules.
