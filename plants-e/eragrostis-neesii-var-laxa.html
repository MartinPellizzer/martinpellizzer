Eragrostis neesii var. laxa

Eragrostis neesii var. laxa, commonly known as slender weeping grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its drooping, slender inflorescences that give it a weeping appearance, and it typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and grasslands. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. It is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive, flowing form.

The species is adapted to well-drained soils and can tolerate drought conditions.
