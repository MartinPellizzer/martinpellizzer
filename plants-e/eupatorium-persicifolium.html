Eupatorium persicifolium

Eupatorium persicifolium, commonly known as sweet everlasting, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its aromatic leaves and flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
