Ehrharta filiformis

Ehrharta filiformis, commonly known as silky bluegrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It features fine, hair-like leaves that give it a distinctive silvery-blue appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in spikelets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is cultivated in some regions for landscaping purposes.
