Epigaea asiatica

Epigaea asiatica, commonly known as the Asian trailing arum, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern Asia.

It features a distinctive, heart-shaped leaf that is often mottled with white and green patterns. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by red berries, which are a food source for birds. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique foliage and seasonal fruit make it a notable species in botanical gardens and natural habitats.
