Erythrodes blumei

Erythrodes blumei, commonly known as the red-stemmed orchid, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive red stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, while its flowers are usually pink or purple in color.

Erythrodes blumei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
