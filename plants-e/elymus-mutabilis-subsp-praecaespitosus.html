Elymus mutabilis subsp. praecaespitosus

Elymus mutabilis subsp.

praecaespitosus, commonly known as the soft bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States. It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and tufted growth habit, which gives it a distinctive appearance.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, two-lobed spikelets.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant landscaping.
