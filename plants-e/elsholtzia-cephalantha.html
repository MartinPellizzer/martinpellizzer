Elsholtzia cephalantha

Elsholtzia cephalantha, commonly known as the Sierra mint, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in mountainous regions. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs.
