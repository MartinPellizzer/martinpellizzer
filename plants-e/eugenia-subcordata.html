Eugenia subcordata

Eugenia subcordata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, hard, and often contain a single seed, typical of the genus Eugenia.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
