Elaeocarpus octantherus

Elaeocarpus octantherus, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its striking white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture, while its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens and along streets for its showy floral display.
