Eugenia anisosepala

Eugenia anisosepala, commonly known as the variable myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines.

Eugenia anisosepala is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its aromatic foliage and fruit.
