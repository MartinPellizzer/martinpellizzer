Entada kirkii

Entada kirkii, commonly known as the African hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its long, slender pods that contain hard, angular seeds. The tree has a straight trunk with smooth, grey bark and produces small, yellow flowers in clusters. Its leaves are pinnate, with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Entada kirkii is often valued for its durable wood and is used in traditional medicine and as a source of charcoal.
