Eschscholzia chartacea

Eschscholzia chartacea, commonly known as the California poppy, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red petals and yellow center, with finely divided, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 10 to 30 centimeters and produces seed pods that split open when mature. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, often found in open fields and along roadsides.

The California poppy is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
