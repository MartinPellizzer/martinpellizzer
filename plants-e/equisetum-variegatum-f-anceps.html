Equisetum variegatum f. anceps

Equisetum variegatum f. anceps, commonly known as the variegated horse tail, is a variant of the common horsetail.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its segmented, jointed stems and whorled leaves. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its spore-bearing structures, called strobili, are typically found at the tips of the stems.

The variegated foliage of this fern-like plant adds ornamental value to its natural habitat.
