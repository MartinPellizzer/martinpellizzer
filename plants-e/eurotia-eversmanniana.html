Eurotia eversmanniana

Eurotia eversmanniana, commonly known as the evermann's rock cress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress species, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered in a fine pubescence, while its flowers are arranged in racemes.

Eurotia eversmanniana is known for its resilience to harsh climatic conditions, making it a hardy species in its native habitat.
