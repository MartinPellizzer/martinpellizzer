Elaeocarpus bilocularis

Elaeocarpus bilocularis, commonly known as the two-lobed elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and showy, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically red or orange when mature, containing several seeds.

It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.
