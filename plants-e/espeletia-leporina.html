Espeletia leporina

Espeletia leporina, commonly known as the "rabbit's ear" plant, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

The plant produces showy, pinkish-white composite flowers that bloom in the spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Espeletia leporina thrives in high-altitude environments, often found in rocky soils and open grasslands.
