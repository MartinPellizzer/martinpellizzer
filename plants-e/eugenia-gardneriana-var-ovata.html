Eugenia gardneriana var. ovata

Eugenia gardneriana var. ovata, commonly known as the oval-leaf myrtle, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
