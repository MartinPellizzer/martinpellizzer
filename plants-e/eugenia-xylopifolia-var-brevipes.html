Eugenia xylopifolia var. brevipes

Eugenia xylopifolia var. brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed myrtle, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are small, fleshy berries that turn red when mature.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
