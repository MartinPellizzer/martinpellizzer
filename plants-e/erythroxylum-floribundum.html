Erythroxylum floribundum

Erythroxylum floribundum, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped berries that turn red when mature. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is cultivated in various parts of the world.
