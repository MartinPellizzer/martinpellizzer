Evonymopsis acutifolia

Evonymopsis acutifolia, commonly known as the sharpleaf evonymopsis, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and have a sharp, pointed tip. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, followed by small, red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its compact size and ornamental foliage make it a desirable addition to shaded garden spaces.
