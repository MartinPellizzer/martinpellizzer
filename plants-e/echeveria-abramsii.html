Echeveria abramsii

Echeveria abramsii, commonly known as Abrams' echeveria, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features rosettes of fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder, known as farina. The plant produces striking clusters of pink to purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
