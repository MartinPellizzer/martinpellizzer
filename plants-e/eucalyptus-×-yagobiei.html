Eucalyptus × yagobiei

Eucalyptus × yagobiei, commonly known as the Yagobie eucalyptus, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Eucalyptus camaldulensis and Eucalyptus robusta.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, globular flower spikes that range in color from white to pale yellow, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Eucalyptus × yagobiei is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to drought conditions.
