Euchiton mackayi

Euchiton mackayi, commonly known as Mackay's euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves and stems that store water, aiding in its survival in arid environments. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers, which are typical of the genus Euchiton. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
