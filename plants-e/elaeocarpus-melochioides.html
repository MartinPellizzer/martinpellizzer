Elaeocarpus melochioides

Elaeocarpus melochioides, commonly known as the white-flowered elaeocarpus, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy white flowers. The plant produces small, woody fruits that are typically covered in a fleshy, yellowish exocarp. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
