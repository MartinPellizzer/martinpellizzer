Eupatorium schraderi

Eupatorium schraderi, commonly known as Schrader's boneset, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.

Eupatorium schraderi is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its historical use in traditional medicine.
