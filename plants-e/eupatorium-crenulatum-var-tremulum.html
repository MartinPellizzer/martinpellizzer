Eupatorium crenulatum var. tremulum

Eupatorium crenulatum var. tremulum, commonly known as the trembling Joe-Pye weed, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and fluffy, pale purple to pinkish flower heads. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have serrated edges, while the plant is known for its vigorous growth and ability to thrive in moist to wet soils.

It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for pollinators.
