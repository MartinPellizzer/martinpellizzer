Eryngium seleri

Eryngium seleri, commonly known as sea holly, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, spiky blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has silver-gray, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Eryngium seleri is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
