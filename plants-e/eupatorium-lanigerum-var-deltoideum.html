Eupatorium lanigerum var. deltoideum

Eupatorium lanigerum var. deltoideum, commonly known as the deltoid eupatorium, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its opposite, lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive blue-green color. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens.
