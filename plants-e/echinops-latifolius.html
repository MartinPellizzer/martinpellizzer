Echinops latifolius

Echinops latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf thistle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble a crown. The plant features silvery-green, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are followed by the distinctive spiky fruits.

Echinops latifolius is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its architectural form and drought tolerance.
