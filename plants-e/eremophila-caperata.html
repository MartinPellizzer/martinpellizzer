Eremophila caperata

Eremophila caperata, commonly known as the pink emu bush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in winter and spring. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments.

Eremophila caperata is well-suited for dry gardens and is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value.
