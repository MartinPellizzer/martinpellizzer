Erigeron acris var. multicaulis

Erigeron acris var. multicaulis, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features a compact, upright growth habit with a hairy stem that supports its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant produces white to pale purple ray florets surrounding a yellow disk in the center, which attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits in native gardens.
