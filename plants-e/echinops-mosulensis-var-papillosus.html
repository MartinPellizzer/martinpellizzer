Echinops mosulensis var. papillosus

Echinops mosulensis var. papillosus, commonly known as the Mosul thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads and gray-green foliage. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, appearing in late summer.

The variety papillosus is distinguished by its hairy or scaly structures on the stems and leaves.
