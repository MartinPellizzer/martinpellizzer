Eremopoa persica var. songarica

Eremopoa persica var. songarica, commonly known as the Persian sand grass, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments with low rainfall and high temperatures.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for small desert organisms.
