Eupatorium tweedieanum var. angustius

Eupatorium tweedieanum var. angustius, commonly known as narrow-leaved boneset, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic foliage.

The species is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
