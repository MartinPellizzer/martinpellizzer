Echinocereus mamillatus

Echinocereus mamillatus, commonly known as the milk barrel cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem with prominent areoles and spines, and it produces showy yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, ribbed stems. It has a slow growth rate and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its distinctive appearance and resilience.

Echinocereus mamillatus plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various desert wildlife.
