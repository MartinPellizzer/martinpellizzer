Eremophila oldfieldii

Eremophila oldfieldii, commonly known as Oldfield's emu bush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant is adapted to dry conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in low-water environments. It produces small, winged seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation across sandy soils.

Eremophila oldfieldii is often used in native gardens due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
