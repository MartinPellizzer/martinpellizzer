Eriophyllum lanatum var. cuneatum

Eriophyllum lanatum var. cuneatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved woolly sunflower, is a perennial herb native to coastal regions of California and Oregon.

It features dense, white to pale yellow ray flowers surrounded by tightly packed, yellow disk flowers, giving it a fluffy appearance. The plant has basal and alternate leaves that are narrow, linear, and covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal scrub and dunes.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and role in supporting local pollinators.
