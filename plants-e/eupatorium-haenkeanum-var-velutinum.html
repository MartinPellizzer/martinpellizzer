Eupatorium haenkeanum var. velutinum

Eupatorium haenkeanum var. velutinum, commonly known as velvet eupatorium, is a perennial herb native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale blue flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners in tropical and subtropical regions.
