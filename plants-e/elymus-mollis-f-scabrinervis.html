Elymus mollis f. scabrinervis

Elymus mollis f. scabrinervis, commonly known as the scabrous-nerved wheatgrass, is a variant of the common wheatgrass species.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in temperate regions, characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive scabrous or rough texture on the nerves, giving it its specific epithet. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brownish florets.

This plant is often found in disturbed soils and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
