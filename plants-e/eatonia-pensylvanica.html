Eatonia pensylvanica

Eatonia pensylvanica, commonly known as the Pennsylvania starflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its star-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has hairy, oval-shaped leaves and grows up to 30 centimeters tall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

The botanical features of Eatonia pensylvanica make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
