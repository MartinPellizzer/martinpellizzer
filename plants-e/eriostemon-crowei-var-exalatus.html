Eriostemon crowei var. exalatus

Eriostemon crowei var. exalatus, commonly known as the silver-leafed eriostemon, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features narrow, silver-gray leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in garden settings.
