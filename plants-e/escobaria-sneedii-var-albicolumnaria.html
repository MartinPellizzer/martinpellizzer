Escobaria sneedii var. albicolumnaria

Escobaria sneedii var. albicolumnaria, commonly known as the white-stemmed stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth habit and fleshy, gray-green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, which are clustered in dense, terminal inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species adapted to arid environments.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
