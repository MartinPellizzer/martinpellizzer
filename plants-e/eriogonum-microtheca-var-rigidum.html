Eriogonum microtheca var. rigidum

Eriogonum microtheca var. rigidum, commonly known as the stiffleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically narrow and rigid, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in coastal and inland grasslands.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
