Erica bauera subsp. gouriquae

Erica bauera subsp.

gouriquae, commonly known as the Gouriquae heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers.

The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in rocky or mountainous regions, where it contributes to the unique biodiversity of its habitat.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
