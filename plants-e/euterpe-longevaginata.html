Euterpe longevaginata

Euterpe longevaginata, commonly known as the red-berried palm, is a species of palm native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, red berries that are a striking feature of its appearance. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in moist forest environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
