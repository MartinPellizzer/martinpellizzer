Eupatorium leucophyllum

Eupatorium leucophyllum, commonly known as white wood betony, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Eupatorium leucophyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
