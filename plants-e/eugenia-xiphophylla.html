Eugenia xiphophylla

Eugenia xiphophylla, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often appearing in clusters. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant flowers.

Eugenia xiphophylla is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates for its seasonal blooms.
