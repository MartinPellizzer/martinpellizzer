Eriogonum terrenatum

Eriogonum terrenatum, commonly known as the desert dogbane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, adapted to reduce water loss in harsh conditions.

Eriogonum terrenatum plays an important role in supporting local wildlife, including insects and small mammals.
