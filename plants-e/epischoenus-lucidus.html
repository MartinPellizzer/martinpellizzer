Epischoenus lucidus

Epischoenus lucidus, commonly known as the shining water plant, is a species of aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Asia.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are often coated with a waxy layer. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or white in color. Epischoenus lucidus is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich waters and can form dense mats on the water surface.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
