Eugenia andina

Eugenia andina, commonly known as the Andean guava, is a small evergreen tree native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It typically grows at elevations between 2,000 and 4,000 meters, where it thrives in cool, humid climates. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by edible, yellow or red berries. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and arranged alternately along the branches.

The fruit is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine for its nutritional and medicinal properties.
