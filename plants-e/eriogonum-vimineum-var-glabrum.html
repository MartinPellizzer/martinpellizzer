Eriogonum vimineum var. glabrum

Eriogonum vimineum var. glabrum, commonly known as the smoothleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its glabrous (smooth) stems and leaves, which are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
