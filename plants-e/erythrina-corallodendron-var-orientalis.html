Erythrina corallodendron var. orientalis

Erythrina corallodendron var. orientalis, commonly known as the Chinese flame tree, is a deciduous legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red, flame-like flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. The plant has compound leaves composed of three to five oval-shaped leaflets, and its distinctive seed pods are elongated and woody. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental tree in tropical and subtropical regions.

The species is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.
