Erigeron sionis

Erigeron sionis, commonly known as the Sierra dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It produces a yellowish-green flower head that transitions to a white color as it matures.

Erigeron sionis is often found in arid and semi-arid regions, showing adaptability to dry conditions.
