Eclipta leiocarpa

Eclipta leiocarpa, commonly known as the white eclipta, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant produces small, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the summer months. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Eclipta leiocarpa is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of light conditions.
