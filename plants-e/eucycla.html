Eucycla

Eucycla, commonly known as the cycad, is a primitive seed plant that belongs to the order Cycadales.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, palm-like leaves that are often feathery in appearance. Eucycla species are typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, where they thrive in well-drained soil and sunny conditions. These plants are known for their slow growth and long lifespan, often living for hundreds of years.

They play an important role in their ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various animals.
