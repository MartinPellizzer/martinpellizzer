Echites atroviolaceus

Echites atroviolaceus, commonly known as the black viola, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family, which includes various species of violets and related plants. The plant is characterized by its dark purple to black flowers, which contrast with its green foliage. It typically grows in shady, moist environments such as forests and stream banks.
