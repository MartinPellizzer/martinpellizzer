Echium modestum var. vidalii

Echium modestum var. vidalii, commonly known as the Vidal's evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking violet to blue flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinator populations.
