Erythrina indica var. phlebocarpa

Erythrina indica var. phlebocarpa, commonly known as the red sandalwood tree, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain flat, fibrous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ornamental and ecological purposes.
