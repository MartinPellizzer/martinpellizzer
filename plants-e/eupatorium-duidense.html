Eupatorium duidense

Eupatorium duidense, commonly known as the Duidense Boneset, is a perennial herb native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clustered, pale blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in coastal or disturbed habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

The species is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
