Echinochloa muricata

Echinochloa muricata, commonly known as rough false brome, is a grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and spikelet-bearing inflorescences. The plant has rough, bristly leaves that give it its common name, and it produces small, two-lobed glumes. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open areas such as fields and roadsides.

Echinochloa muricata is an annual plant that plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various insects.
