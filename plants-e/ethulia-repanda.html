Ethulia repanda

Ethulia repanda, commonly known as the broadleaf orchid, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a herbaceous plant with long, narrow leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its flowers are often pale green or white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by insects.

Ethulia repanda is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
