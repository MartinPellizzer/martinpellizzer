Elymus pendulinus var. vernicosus

Elymus pendulinus var. vernicosus, commonly known as the silver hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-haired inflorescence that hangs downward, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
