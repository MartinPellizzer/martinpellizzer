Echium thyrsoideum

Echium thyrsoideum, commonly known as the slender viper's bugloss, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Echium thyrsoideum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
