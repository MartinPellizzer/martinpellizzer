Euphorbia fossulata

Euphorbia fossulata, commonly known as the grooved spurge, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, ribbed stems and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that can be toxic and irritating to the skin. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Euphorbia fossulata is often found in open woodland and grassland habitats.
