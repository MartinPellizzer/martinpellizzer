Eugenia madugodaense

Eugenia madugodaense, commonly known as the Sri Lankan myrtle, is a small evergreen tree native to the wet zone of Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
