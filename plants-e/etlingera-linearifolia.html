Etlingera linearifolia

Etlingera linearifolia, commonly known as the flame flower or torch ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its striking, red-orange inflorescence that resembles a torch, with long, narrow leaves. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are also used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.
