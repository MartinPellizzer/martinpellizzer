Eucalyptus resinifera

Eucalyptus resinifera, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed structure and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters, which are typically followed by woody capsules containing seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in arid and semi-arid regions.

Eucalyptus resinifera is known for its aromatic oils, which have various traditional and commercial uses.
