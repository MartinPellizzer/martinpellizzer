Erica racemosa var. racemosa

Erica racemosa var. racemosa, commonly known as the heath or the common heather, is a flowering plant native to the western Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring, producing pink or white flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

Erica racemosa var. racemosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
