Erigeron alpinus subsp. rhodopaeus

Erigeron alpinus subsp.

rhodopaeus, commonly known as the Rhodope's fleabane, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southeastern Europe. It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

The plant features a compact growth habit with numerous daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of white to pale pink. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
