Elaeocarpus ulapensis

Elaeocarpus ulapensis, commonly known as the Mindanao elaeocarpus, is a tropical tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, white to pink flowers. The plant produces fleshy, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It typically grows in lowland to montane rainforests, thriving in humid, shaded environments.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
