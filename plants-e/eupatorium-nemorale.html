Eupatorium nemorale

Eupatorium nemorale, commonly known as sweet pepperbush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often grayish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. These flowers are typically arranged in dense, rounded inflorescences, which attract a variety of pollinators.

Eupatorium nemorale is also known for its aromatic foliage and is often used in herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
