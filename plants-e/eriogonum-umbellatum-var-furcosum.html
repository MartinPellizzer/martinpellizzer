Eriogonum umbellatum var. furcosum

Eriogonum umbellatum var. furcosum, commonly known as the Sierra buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its clustered, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as hillsides and open woodlands. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for various wildlife species.
