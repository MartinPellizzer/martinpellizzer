Erica volutiflora

Erica volutiflora, commonly known as the spiral heath, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family, known for its ability to thrive in acidic, well-drained soils. The plant produces dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, depending on the variety. Erica volutiflora is well adapted to coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
