Epidendrum xytriophorum

Epidendrum xytriophorum, commonly known as the pink-tinged epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, branching inflorescence and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical forests. Its leaves are elongated and covered in a dense layer of fine, white pubescence.

This orchid thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.
