Eleocharis annua

Eleocharis annua, commonly known as the common spike-rush, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to aquatic environments. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that bear spike-like clusters of tiny, spikelet-bearing flowers. Its roots help stabilize soil and improve water quality by filtering sediments.

Eleocharis annua is an important species for wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife.
