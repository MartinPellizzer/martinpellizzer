Eriogonum luteolum var. luteolum

Eriogonum luteolum var. luteolum, commonly known as the yellow globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb with hairy, branching stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
