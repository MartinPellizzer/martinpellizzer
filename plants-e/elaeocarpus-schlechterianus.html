Elaeocarpus schlechterianus

Elaeocarpus schlechterianus, commonly known as the Schlechter's elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, white or pink flowers. The fruit is a woody drupe, typically containing one or two seeds, and is an important food source for various wildlife.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic and ecological significance.
