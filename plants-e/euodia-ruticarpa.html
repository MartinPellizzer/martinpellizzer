Euodia ruticarpa

Euodia ruticarpa, commonly known as Chinese prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to China, Japan, and Korea.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are elliptical with a leathery texture and emit a strong, aromatic scent.

Euodia ruticarpa is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in herbal remedies for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.
