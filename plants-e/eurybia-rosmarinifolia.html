Eurybia rosmarinifolia

Eurybia rosmarinifolia, commonly known as seaside goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces bright yellow flowers in late summer. The plant has silver-gray foliage that helps it withstand salty air and strong winds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for coastal gardens.

Eurybia rosmarinifolia is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
