Eriogonum ovalifolium var. rubidum

Eriogonum ovalifolium var. rubidum, commonly known as the redstem buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves and bright red stems, which are often tinged with purple. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
