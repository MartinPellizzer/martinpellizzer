Entada mannii

Entada mannii, commonly known as the African horned bean, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, curved pods that resemble horns, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces showy, pink or red flowers and has compound leaves with pinnate arrangements. Its seeds are encased in hard, woody pods and are sometimes used as a natural dye.

Entada mannii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in warm climates for its unique appearance.
