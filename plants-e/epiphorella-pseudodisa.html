Epiphorella pseudodisa

Epiphorella pseudodisa, commonly known as the rainbow orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in rocky and sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, colorful flowers that range from pink to purple, often with intricate patterns. It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its beauty and rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and is of interest to horticulturists and botanists alike.
