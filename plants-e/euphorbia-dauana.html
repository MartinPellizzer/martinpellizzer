Euphorbia dauana

Euphorbia dauana, commonly known as the redroot spurges, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its branching structure and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant has a milky latex sap that is toxic and can cause skin irritation. Its leaves are typically narrow and opposite, with a slightly hairy texture.

Euphorbia dauana thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or open areas.
