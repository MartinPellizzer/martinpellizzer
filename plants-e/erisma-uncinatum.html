Erisma uncinatum

Erisma uncinatum, commonly known as the hooked eriska, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with small, four-petaled flowers. The plant features basal leaves that are linear to lanceolate and are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically pale yellow to greenish-yellow, with four sepals and four petals.

Erisma uncinatum is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks.
