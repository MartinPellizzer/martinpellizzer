Euphorbia jamesonii

Euphorbia jamesonii, commonly known as the Jameson's spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a short, cylindrical stem with a distinctive crown of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. It is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family, known for its milky sap and unique floral structures.

Euphorbia jamesonii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
