Erigeron sonnei

Erigeron sonnei, commonly known as Sonne's fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Erigeron sonnei is often found in disturbed soils and is considered an early successional species.
