Euphorbia macgillivrayi var. yarrabensis

Euphorbia macgillivrayi var. yarrabensis, commonly known as the Yarrabent euphorbia, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its succulent, spiny stems and small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has adapted to harsh desert conditions with thick, water-storing leaves and a shallow root system. It is often found in rocky outcrops and sandy soils, thriving in full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
