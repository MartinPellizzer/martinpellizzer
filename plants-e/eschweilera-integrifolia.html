Eschweilera integrifolia

Eschweilera integrifolia, commonly known as the white-eyed tree or white-eyed hibiscus, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically entire, without any serrations or lobes. The plant produces showy, white flowers with prominent yellow stamens, which bloom in clusters during the rainy season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
