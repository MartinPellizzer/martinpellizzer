Eleocharis contracta

Eleocharis contracta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spike-rush, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikelets. The plant is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist to saturated soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a tendency to curl inwards, giving it a compact appearance.

Eleocharis contracta plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
