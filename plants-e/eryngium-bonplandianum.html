Eryngium bonplandianum

Eryngium bonplandianum, commonly known as the Mexican thistle, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its spiny, blue-violet flowers and silver-green foliage. The plant grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Eryngium bonplandianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
