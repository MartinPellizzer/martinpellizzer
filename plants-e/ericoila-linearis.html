Ericoila linearis

Ericoila linearis, commonly known as the slender heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heaths and heathers, and is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal or mountainous regions. Its flowers are usually pink or white and bloom in late spring or early summer.

Ericoila linearis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its compact growth and long-lasting blooms.
