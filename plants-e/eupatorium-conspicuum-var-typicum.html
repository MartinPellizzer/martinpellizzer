Eupatorium conspicuum var. typicum

Eupatorium conspicuum var. typicum, commonly known as showy Joe-Pye weed, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and large, showy clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and it often grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and stream banks. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal and ornamental value.

Its common name reflects its striking appearance and its historical use by indigenous peoples for various purposes.
