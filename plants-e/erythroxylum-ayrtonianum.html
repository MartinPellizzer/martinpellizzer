Erythroxylum ayrtonianum

Erythroxylum ayrtonianum, commonly known as the Ayrton's copal tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Peru. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that give way to orange-red berries. It is often found in humid, lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental qualities.

The species is also noted for its potential ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
