Eriogonum fasciculatum var. maritimum

Eriogonum fasciculatum var. maritimum, commonly known as coastal buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific coast of North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along coastal dunes and bluffs. It has small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This species is adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and strong winds.
