Euphorbia portucasadiana

Euphorbia portucasadiana, commonly known as the Armenian spurge, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Armenia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a cup-like structure called a cyathium, which is typical of the genus Euphorbia. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
