Elytrigia caespitosa subsp. rhodopaea

Elytrigia caespitosa subsp.

rhodopaea, commonly known as the Rhodope bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Balkan Peninsula. It forms dense, low-growing tufts with narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color.

The plant produces spikelets that are slender and usually borne in clusters along the stems. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it a characteristic species of the Rhodope Mountains region.
