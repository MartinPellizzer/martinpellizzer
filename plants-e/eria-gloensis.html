Eria gloensis

Eria gloensis, commonly known as the velvetleaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Eria gloensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
