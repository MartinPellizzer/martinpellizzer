Evolvulus virgatus

Evolvulus virgatus, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its common name. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

The species is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
