Eriostemon myoporoides subsp. conduplicatus

Eriostemon myoporoides subsp.

conduplicatus, commonly known as the Narrow-leaf Myopor, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or heathland environments. It is often found in regions with a temperate climate and is adapted to withstand periodic droughts.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
