Eupatorium purpureum f. laciniatum

Eupatorium purpureum f. laciniatum, commonly known as the cutleaf purple coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features tall, erect stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with distinctive notched or lobed margins. The plant produces clusters of small, purple to pinkish-white flowers in the late summer and early fall. Its botanical characteristics include a panicle inflorescence and hairy stems, which contribute to its ornamental appeal.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its showy blooms and ability to attract pollinators.
