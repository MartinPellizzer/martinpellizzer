Equisetum albomarginatum

Equisetum albomarginatum, commonly known as the white-margin horsetail, is a perennial plant belonging to the Equisetaceae family.

It is characterized by its jointed, hollow stems that are typically green with white margins, giving it its common name. The plant produces spore-bearing cones at the top of its stems, which are used for reproduction. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and wetlands.

Equisetum albomarginatum is a primitive vascular plant with a long evolutionary history, dating back to the Paleozoic era.
