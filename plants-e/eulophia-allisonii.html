Eulophia allisonii

Eulophia allisonii, commonly known as the Allison's eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, humid environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features a sympodial growth habit with a pseudobulb that supports its foliage and inflorescence. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive lip that is often marked with contrasting patterns.

Eulophia allisonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
