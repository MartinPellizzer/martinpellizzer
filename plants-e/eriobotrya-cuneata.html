Eriobotrya cuneata

Eriobotrya cuneata, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces white flowers in the spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits that are similar to pears.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production in temperate climates.
