Echinopsis omasensis

Echinopsis omasensis, commonly known as the Omasensis cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spherical or columnar shape. The plant features prominent spines and produces colorful, night-blooming flowers. It is well adapted to harsh desert conditions, storing water in its thick stems.

Due to its striking appearance and rarity, it is often cultivated by cactus enthusiasts.
