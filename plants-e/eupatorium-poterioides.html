Eupatorium poterioides

Eupatorium poterioides, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, blue-violet flower clusters that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in partial to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native gardens and wildlife habitats.
