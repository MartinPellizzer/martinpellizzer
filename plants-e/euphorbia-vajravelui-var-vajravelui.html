Euphorbia vajravelui var. vajravelui

Euphorbia vajravelui var. vajravelui, commonly known as the Vajravelu euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of India.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and sparse, upright leaves. The plant produces unique cyathia, which are specialized inflorescences that resemble small, cup-like structures. It is well adapted to drought conditions, storing water in its stems and leaves.

Due to its striking appearance and resilience, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry gardens.
