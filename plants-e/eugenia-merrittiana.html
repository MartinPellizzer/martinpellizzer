Eugenia merrittiana

Eugenia merrittiana, commonly known as the Merritt's evergreen, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers. It produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature.

Eugenia merrittiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is appreciated for its adaptability to various soil types.
