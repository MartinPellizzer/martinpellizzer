Eucalyptus angusta

Eucalyptus angusta, commonly known as narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale blue, followed by woody capsules containing aromatic seeds. Eucalyptus angusta is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
