Erodium cicutarium subsp. praetermissum

Erodium cicutarium subsp.

praetermissum, commonly known as the narrowleaf bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia. It is an annual herb characterized by its slender stems and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the spring.

The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, dry fruits known as schizocarps. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides, in fields, and in waste areas.

Due to its adaptability, it is considered a common weed in many agricultural settings.
