Erica pachyphylla

Erica pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved heath, is a species of evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features small, needle-like leaves that are thick and waxy, which help it retain moisture in its arid native environment. Its flowers are typically white or pink, appearing in clusters during the summer months.

Erica pachyphylla is well adapted to rocky soils and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.
