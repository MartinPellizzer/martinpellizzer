Echeveria decumbens var. urubambensis

Echeveria decumbens var. urubambensis, commonly known as the Urubambensis Echeveria, is a succulent native to the Andean regions of South America.

It features a low-growing, sprawling rosette of fleshy, blue-gray leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to maintain its compact form.

This variety is prized for its unique coloration and adaptability to arid environments.
