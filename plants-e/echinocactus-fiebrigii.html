Echinocactus fiebrigii

Echinocactus fiebrigii, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem that is covered in prominent, spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom during the summer months. It is a slow-growing plant that requires well-draining soil and full sun to thrive.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for succulent gardens and arid landscapes.
