Eupatorium rhodolepis

Eupatorium rhodolepis, commonly known as the redstem boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and wet meadows.

Eupatorium rhodolepis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
