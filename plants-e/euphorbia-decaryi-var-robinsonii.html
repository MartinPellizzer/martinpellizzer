Euphorbia decaryi var. robinsonii

Euphorbia decaryi var. robinsonii, commonly known as the Robinson's spurge, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It features a short, thick stem with clusters of small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are its modified flowers. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating, aiding in water retention. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
