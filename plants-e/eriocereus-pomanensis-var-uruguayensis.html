Eriocereus pomanensis var. uruguayensis

Eriocereus pomanensis var. uruguayensis, commonly known as the Uruguay night blooming cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly Uruguay and parts of Brazil.

It is a type of cactus belonging to the Cactaceae family, characterized by its spiny stems and nocturnal blooming habit. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its succulent structure. Its flowers, which open at night, are usually white or pale pink and emit a subtle fragrance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique blooming behavior.
