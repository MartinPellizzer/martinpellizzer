Euphorbia sewerzowii

Euphorbia sewerzowii, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, branching stems that are covered in a thick layer of white, waxy epicuticular hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in a structure called a cyathium, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its milky sap, which is toxic, is stored in the stem and leaves, providing a defense mechanism against herbivores.

Euphorbia sewerzowii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
