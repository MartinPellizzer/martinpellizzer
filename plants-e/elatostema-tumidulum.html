Elatostema tumidulum

Elatostema tumidulum, commonly known as the globular elatostema, is a small, herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded rosette of fleshy, dark green leaves that are covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers arranged in clusters, which are borne on short, hairy pedicels. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, woodland environments.

Elatostema tumidulum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens with the right conditions.
