Elsota rivinifolia

Elsota rivinifolia, commonly known as the river willow, is a shrub-like plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like branches and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows along riverbanks and moist soils, adapting well to aquatic environments. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, appearing in clusters, and it produces small, woody fruits.

Elsota rivinifolia plays an important role in stabilizing riverbanks and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
