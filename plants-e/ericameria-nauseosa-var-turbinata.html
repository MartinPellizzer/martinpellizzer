Ericameria nauseosa var. turbinata

Ericameria nauseosa var. turbinata, commonly known as the Turbinated Greasewood, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a deep root system, allowing it to thrive in poor, well-drained soils. It is adapted to hot, dry climates and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.

The species is noted for its resilience and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
