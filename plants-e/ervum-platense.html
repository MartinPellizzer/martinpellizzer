Ervum platense

Ervum platense, commonly known as the broadleaf vetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has a trailing growth habit and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Ervum platense is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
