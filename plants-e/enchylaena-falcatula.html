Enchylaena falcatula

Enchylaena falcatula, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mulga, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grey-green leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, with a deep root system that allows it to access groundwater. Its fibrous bark and tough leaves make it resilient to fire and grazing.

Enchylaena falcatula plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
