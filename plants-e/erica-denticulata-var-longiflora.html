Erica denticulata var. longiflora

Erica denticulata var. longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered heath, is a flowering plant native to the western regions of the United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. This plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. The leaves are typically narrow and pointed, with a leathery texture, and the flowers can range in color from white to pink.

It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal scrub habitats.
