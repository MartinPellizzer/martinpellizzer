Eupatorium leptocephalum var. typicum

Eupatorium leptocephalum var. typicum, commonly known as white eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its ornamental value and ecological role make it a notable species in botanical studies.
