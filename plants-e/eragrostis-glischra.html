Eragrostis glischra

Eragrostis glischra, commonly known as the narrowleaf beakgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. Its flowering period usually occurs in the spring and early summer.

Eragrostis glischra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its drought tolerance and aesthetic qualities.
