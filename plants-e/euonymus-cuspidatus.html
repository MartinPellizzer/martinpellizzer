Euonymus cuspidatus

Euonymus cuspidatus, commonly known as the scarlet beauty, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves that turn a striking red in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red, inflated fruits. Euonymus cuspidatus thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its unique foliage and seasonal color changes make it a visually appealing addition to gardens.
