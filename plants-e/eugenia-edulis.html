Eugenia edulis

Eugenia edulis, commonly known as the araçá or surinam cherry, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, red, edible berries that are rich in vitamin C and have a tart, sweet flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

The plant's botanical features make it a popular choice in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
