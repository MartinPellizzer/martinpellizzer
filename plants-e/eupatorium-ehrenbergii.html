Eupatorium ehrenbergii

Eupatorium ehrenbergii, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flower clusters that resemble mist. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Eupatorium ehrenbergii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
