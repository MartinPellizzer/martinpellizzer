Epipactis cordigera

Epipactis cordigera, commonly known as the heart-leaved helleborine, is a species of orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. It is a perennial herb with a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively.

Epipactis cordigera is known for its ability to thrive in humid environments and is often found in association with other orchid species.
