Eremurus comosus

Eremurus comosus, commonly known as the sword lily, is a striking plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Xanthorrhoeaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence composed of numerous narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces showy, often yellow or orange, flower heads that bloom in late spring or early summer. Eremurus comosus is known for its ability to survive in harsh, dry conditions, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
