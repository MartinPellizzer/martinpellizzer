Euploca collina

Euploca collina, commonly known as the coastal euploca, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in coastal dunes and sandy soils. The plant features slender, upright stems with small, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

Euploca collina is adapted to salt spray and wind, making it a resilient species in coastal environments.
