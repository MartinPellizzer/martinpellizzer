Elymus laxiflorus

Elymus laxiflorus, commonly known as the slender panicle grass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, often drooping inflorescence and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, meadows, and forest edges. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, producing small, greenish to brownish spikelets.

Elymus laxiflorus is valued in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various insects and small animals.
