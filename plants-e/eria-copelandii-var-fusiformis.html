Eria copelandii var. fusiformis

Eria copelandii var. fusiformis, commonly known as the fusiform orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a sympodial orchid with a compact growth habit and produces pendulous inflorescences. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and is known for its showy, fragrant flowers that come in various colors. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

This variety is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
