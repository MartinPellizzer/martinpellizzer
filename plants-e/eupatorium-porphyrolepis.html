Eupatorium porphyrolepis

Eupatorium porphyrolepis, commonly known as purpleleaf eupatorium, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking purple to red foliage, which contrasts vividly with its white to pale pink flower clusters. The plant typically grows in wetlands and disturbed soils, preferring partial to full shade. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
