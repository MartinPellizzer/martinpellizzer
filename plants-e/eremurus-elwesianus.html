Eremurus elwesianus

Eremurus elwesianus, commonly known as the Kashmir giant spirea, is a striking perennial plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence composed of numerous narrow, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces large, fibrous roots that help it survive in cold and arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapting to the harsh conditions of its native habitat.

Eremurus elwesianus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is appreciated for its unique, dramatic floral display.
