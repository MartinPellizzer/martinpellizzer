Embothrium salicifolium

Embothrium salicifolium, commonly known as the Chilean willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like branches and heart-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, appearing in clusters. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal areas and riverbanks.

Embothrium salicifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
