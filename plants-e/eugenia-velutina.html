Eugenia velutina

Eugenia velutina, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically reddish when mature, containing a single seed. Eugenia velutina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with a dense, compact form that makes it suitable for garden landscapes.
