Ericoides suffultum

Ericoides suffultum, commonly known as the dwarf heath, is a low-growing shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact form and its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor, acidic soils. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming in late summer. Its leaves are leathery and evergreen, providing year-round coverage.

Ericoides suffultum is well adapted to coastal conditions, tolerating salt spray and strong winds.
