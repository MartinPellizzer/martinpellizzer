Elizabetha speciosa

Elizabetha speciosa, commonly known as the showy elizabetha, is a striking plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy. Its blooming period occurs in late spring to early summer, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Elizabetha speciosa thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring moderate water and pruning to maintain its shape.
