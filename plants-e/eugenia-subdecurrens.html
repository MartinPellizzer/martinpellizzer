Eugenia subdecurrens

Eugenia subdecurrens, commonly known as the narrowleaf eucalypt, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often glandular-dotted. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pinkish flowers, which are typically clustered in umbels. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, and it is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types, including sandy and rocky conditions.

Eugenia subdecurrens is often found in coastal and inland regions, contributing to local biodiversity and ecological stability.
