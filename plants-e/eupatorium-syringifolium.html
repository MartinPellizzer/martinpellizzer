Eupatorium syringifolium

Eupatorium syringifolium, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist woodlands and has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.

The plant is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported calming and anti-inflammatory properties.
