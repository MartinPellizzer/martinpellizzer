Elaeis dybowskii

Elaeis dybowskii, commonly known as the Dybowskii oil palm, is a species native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces fruit clusters that contain oil-rich seeds, making it potentially valuable for agricultural purposes. However, it is less commonly cultivated than other oil palm species due to its specific habitat requirements.

Its conservation status remains a topic of interest among botanists and conservationists.
