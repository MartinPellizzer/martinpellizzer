Eranthemum pulchellum var. purpurascens

Eranthemum pulchellum var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-flowered eranthemum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple or pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy. Its flowers have five petals and are often visited by bees and other pollinators.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and as a decorative plant in horticultural displays.
