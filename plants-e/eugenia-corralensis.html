Eugenia corralensis

Eugenia corralensis, commonly known as the Mexican cherry or coral tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and parts of Central America, where it typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, edible berries. Its leaves are glossy and elliptical, often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate to flourish.
