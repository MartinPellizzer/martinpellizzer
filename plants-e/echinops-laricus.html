Echinops laricus

Echinops laricus, commonly known as the mountain thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble a hedgehog, giving it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually blue-green in color and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellow.

Echinops laricus is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
