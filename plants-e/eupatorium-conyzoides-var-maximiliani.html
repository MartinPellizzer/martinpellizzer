Eupatorium conyzoides var. maximiliani

Eupatorium conyzoides var. maximiliani, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking native, drought-tolerant plants.
