Erycibe macrophylla

Erycibe macrophylla, commonly known as the giant hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a popular ornamental plant in tropical gardens.
