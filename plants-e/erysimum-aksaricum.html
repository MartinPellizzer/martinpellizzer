Erysimum aksaricum

Erysimum aksaricum, commonly known as the Aksaric wallflower, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It produces bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in early spring.

Its foliage consists of pinnately divided leaves, which are covered in fine hairs.
