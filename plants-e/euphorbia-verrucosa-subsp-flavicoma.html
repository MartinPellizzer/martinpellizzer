Euphorbia verrucosa subsp. flavicoma

Euphorbia verrucosa subsp.

flavicoma, commonly known as the yellow-hairy spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, yellowish-green foliage covered in fine, soft hairs.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a cup-like structure called a cyathium. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
