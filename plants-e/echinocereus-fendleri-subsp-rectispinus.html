Echinocereus fendleri subsp. rectispinus

Echinocereus fendleri subsp.

rectispinus, commonly known as the Arizona hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a columnar cactus with spiny, ribbed stems that can grow up to several meters in height.

The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Its structure and appearance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
