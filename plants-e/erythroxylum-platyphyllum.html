Erythroxylum platyphyllum

Erythroxylum platyphyllum, commonly known as the broad-leafed copal tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces a resinous sap that has been historically used for various traditional purposes. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and medicinal studies.
