Erica viscaria subsp. pendula

Erica viscaria subsp.

pendula, commonly known as the hanging heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant has needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover in coastal regions.
