Enkianthus nikoensis

Enkianthus nikoensis, commonly known as the Niko's Enkianthus, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring, typically in shades of pink and white. The plant has lanceolate leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Enkianthus nikoensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
