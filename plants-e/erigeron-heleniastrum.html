Erigeron heleniastrum

Erigeron heleniastrum, commonly known as Helen's fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Helen's fleabane is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
