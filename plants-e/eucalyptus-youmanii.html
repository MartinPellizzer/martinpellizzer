Eucalyptus youmanii

Eucalyptus youmanii, commonly known as the Yuman gum, is a tall, slender tree native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that reveals a mottled pattern of white and grey. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a bluish-green hue. It produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically white or pale yellow.

The tree is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
