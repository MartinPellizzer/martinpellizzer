Euphorbia luapulana

Euphorbia luapulana, commonly known as the Luapulana spurges, is a succulent plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, spiny stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in a structure called a cyathium, which is typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
