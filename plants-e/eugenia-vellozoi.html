Eugenia vellozoi

Eugenia vellozoi, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Pitanga-preta*, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of eastern Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, dark purple to black drupe, rich in antioxidants and used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.

Eugenia vellozoi plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food for various wildlife species.
