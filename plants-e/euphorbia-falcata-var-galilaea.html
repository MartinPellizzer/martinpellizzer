Euphorbia falcata var. galilaea

Euphorbia falcata var. galilaea, commonly known as the Galilean spurge, is a succulent plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has a distinctive, spiny structure with thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry climates.
