Erica pallidiflora

Erica pallidiflora, commonly known as the pale-flowered heath, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its evergreen leaves and clusters of small, pale pink or white flowers. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Erica pallidiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
