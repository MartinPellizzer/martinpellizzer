Eschweileria helleborinum

Eschweileria helleborinum, commonly known as the Christmas rose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It features rhizomatous growth and produces showy, white or pinkish flowers with a distinctive, waxy appearance. The plant has palmate, lobed leaves that are typically heart-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Eschweileria helleborinum is known for its winter blooming habit, often flowering in late winter or early spring.
