Elymus canadensis

Elymus canadensis, commonly known as Canada wild rye, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including wetlands, meadows, and disturbed soils.

Elymus canadensis plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
