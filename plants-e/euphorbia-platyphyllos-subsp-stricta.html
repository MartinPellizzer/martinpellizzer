Euphorbia platyphyllos subsp. stricta

Euphorbia platyphyllos subsp.

stricta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It features a short, thick stem with opposite, narrow, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called cyathia. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its toxic sap, it should be handled with care.
