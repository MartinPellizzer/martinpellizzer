Eryngium carlinae var. decumbens

Eryngium carlinae var. decumbens, commonly known as the decumbent thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features gray-green, spiny leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette, and its flowers are blue to purple, forming dense, thistle-like inflorescences. The plant is adapted to arid and rocky environments, showcasing drought tolerance and efficient water use. It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed sites.

Its unique morphology and ecological niche make it a notable species in its native habitat.
