Erica bucciniflora

Erica bucciniflora, commonly known as the bellflower heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and other ericaceous plants. The plant is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, evergreen leaves. Its flowers are typically bell-shaped, ranging in color from white to pink, and bloom in spring.

Erica bucciniflora thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.
