Eugenia rosmarinifolia var. angustissima

Eugenia rosmarinifolia var. angustissima, commonly known as the narrowleaf clove, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often fragrant and have a slight aromatic scent. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale pink flowers that are followed by dark purple, clove-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in subtropical gardens.
