Ebermaiera venosa

Ebermaiera venosa, commonly known as the veined evergreen, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, veined leaves that exhibit a unique pattern of dark green lines on a lighter background. The plant typically grows as an understory shrub, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Ebermaiera venosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
