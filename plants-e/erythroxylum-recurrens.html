Erythroxylum recurrens

Erythroxylum recurrens, commonly known as the creeping or prostrate Erythroxylum, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its low-growing, sprawling habit with hairy, elliptical leaves. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in dense inflorescences.

The plant is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.
