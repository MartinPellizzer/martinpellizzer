Echites lateriflorus

Echites lateriflorus, commonly known as the side-flowered echites, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, often with white or pale yellow petals. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and the ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Echites lateriflorus is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
