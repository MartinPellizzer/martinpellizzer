Eremophila myoporoides

Eremophila myoporoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved emu bush, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Australia.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces striking spikes of tubular, pink to red flowers that attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It is well adapted to poor soils and can thrive in full sun, making it a hardy choice for dry gardens.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a valuable plant for ecological and aesthetic purposes in native landscaping.
