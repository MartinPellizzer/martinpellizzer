Echinocactus coccineus

Echinocactus coccineus, commonly known as the red barrel cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, cylindrical stem that is typically reddish-brown in color. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its spiny exterior provides protection from herbivores, while its deep root system allows it to thrive in arid environments.

Echinocactus coccineus is well adapted to full sun and requires minimal water, making it a hardy choice for desert gardens.
