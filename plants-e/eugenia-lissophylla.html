Eugenia lissophylla

Eugenia lissophylla, commonly known as the wax apple or Java apple, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red or yellow when mature. The fruit is edible, with a sweet and tart flavor, and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.
