Elaeocarpus henryi

Elaeocarpus henryi, commonly known as Henry's elaeocarp, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, dark blue berries that are often covered in a whitish bloom. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
