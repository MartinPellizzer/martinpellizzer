Echinopsis comarapana

Echinopsis comarapana, commonly known as the Comarapa night bloomer, is a cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem and clusters of white, star-shaped flowers that bloom at night. The plant has a spiny exterior, with areoles that bear both spines and flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to desert environments.

Due to its striking appearance and nocturnal blooming habit, it is a popular choice for succulent gardens and cactus collections.
