Euphorbia scopulorum var. inornata

Euphorbia scopulorum var. inornata, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a succulent plant native to arid and rocky environments.

It features a short, thick stem with paired, fleshy leaves that are typically green or bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are specialized flower-like structures typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its hardy nature and unique appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
