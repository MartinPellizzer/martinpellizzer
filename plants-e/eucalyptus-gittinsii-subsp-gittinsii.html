Eucalyptus gittinsii subsp. gittinsii

Eucalyptus gittinsii subsp.

gittinsii, commonly known as the Gittins' malleable, is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that reveals a vibrant red or yellow color underneath.

The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a greyish-green hue with a prominent midrib. It produces small, globular yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by woody capsules containing aromatic seeds.

This subspecies is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.
