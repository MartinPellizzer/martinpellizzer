Eriocaulon catopsioides

Eriocaulon catopsioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf spikerush, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and cylindrical flower clusters.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to aquatic environments. The plant has slender, erect stems covered in a dense layer of pale, silky hairs, which give it a distinctive appearance. Its small, spike-like inflorescences produce tiny, wind-pollinated flowers, contributing to its role in wetland ecosystems.

Eriocaulon catopsioides plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
