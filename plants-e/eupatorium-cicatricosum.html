Eupatorium cicatricosum

Eupatorium cicatricosum, commonly known as roughleaf boneset, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the late summer and early fall. It thrives in moist to wet soils in woodland or wetland habitats.

Eupatorium cicatricosum is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
