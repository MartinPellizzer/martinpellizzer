Erlangea tomentosa var. acuta

Erlangea tomentosa var. acuta, commonly known as the hairy erlangea, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Its botanical features make it a unique subject for study in plant morphology and ecology.
