Elytrigia gmelinii

Elytrigia gmelinii, commonly known as meadow grass or common meadowgrass, is a species of grass native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and along roadsides. The plant has slender, tufted stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color.

Elytrigia gmelinii plays an important role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinators in its native habitats.
