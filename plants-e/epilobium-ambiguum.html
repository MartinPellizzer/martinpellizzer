Epilobium ambiguum

Epilobium ambiguum, commonly known as the yellow willow or yellow-flowered willow, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and wetlands. The plant features slender, willow-like stems and lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in elongated inflorescences, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Epilobium ambiguum is often found in riparian zones and is adapted to thrive in nutrient-rich, wet soils.
