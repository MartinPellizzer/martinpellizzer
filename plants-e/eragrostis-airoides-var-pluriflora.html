Eragrostis airoides var. pluriflora

Eragrostis airoides var. pluriflora, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, silver-blue leaves and produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is characterized by its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions. It is often used in ornamental landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.

Its botanical structure supports its role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in dry environments.
