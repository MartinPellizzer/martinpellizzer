Echinopsis obrepanda subsp. calorubra

Echinopsis obrepanda subsp.

calorubra, commonly known as the red-flowered moon cactus, is a succulent native to the arid regions of South America. It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems and showy, brightly colored flowers.

The plant typically produces vibrant red or orange blooms during the warmer months, adding visual interest to its desert habitat. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Due to its striking appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.
