Erica exquisita

Erica exquisita, commonly known as the sweet heath, is a species of evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are leathery and evergreen, providing year-round foliage.

Erica exquisita thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
