Eryngium scaposum

Eryngium scaposum, commonly known as the slender thistle, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and blue to purple flower heads. The plant produces small, bract-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically arranged in a dense, spiky cluster at the top of the stem.

Eryngium scaposum is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
