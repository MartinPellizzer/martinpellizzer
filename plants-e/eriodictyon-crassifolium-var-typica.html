Eriodictyon crassifolium var. typica

Eriodictyon crassifolium var. typica, commonly known as California licorice, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant produces a resinous, aromatic gum that has been historically used by indigenous peoples for medicinal purposes. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional herbal practices.
