Eragrostis megastachya var. thyrsiflora

Eragrostis megastachya var. thyrsiflora, commonly known as the silverleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces panicles of small, blue-green flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for landscaping in arid regions.
