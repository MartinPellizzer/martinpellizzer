Erythroxylum grandifolium

Erythroxylum grandifolium, commonly known as the large-leaved copal tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by spherical, woody fruits containing seeds. It is often found in humid lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental foliage and historical uses.

The species is also noted for its role in traditional medicine and ecological importance in its native habitats.
