Erythrocephalum castellaneum

Erythrocephalum castellaneum, commonly known as the redhead or redhead bush, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are narrow and grey-green, with a rough texture that helps reduce water loss.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
