Erycina allemanii

Erycina allemanii, commonly known as the Alleman's orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers with intricate patterns of purple and red. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swampy areas. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often forming a rosette at the base.

Erycina allemanii is valued for its unique floral structure and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental appeal.
