Endiandra monothyra subsp. trichophylla

Endiandra monothyra subsp.

trichophylla, commonly known as the woolly endiandra, is a species of flowering plant in the family Dipterocarpaceae. It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Malaysia and Indonesia.

The plant is characterized by its smooth, often hairy stems and leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
