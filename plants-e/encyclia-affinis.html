Encyclia affinis

Encyclia affinis, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It features a rhizomatous root system and produces upright, lanceolate leaves that are typically dark green. The plant blooms with pale green to yellowish flowers that have three sepals and three petals, with a showy lip. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially sunny environments.

Encyclia affinis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
