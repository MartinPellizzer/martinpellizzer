Ervum aurantium

Ervum aurantium, commonly known as the orange tree, is a citrus plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are glossy and oval in shape. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that give way to citrus fruits, primarily oranges. Its botanical features include a thick, aromatic bark and a complex root system that helps it thrive in various soil types.

The fruit is rich in vitamin C and is widely used in culinary and medicinal applications.
