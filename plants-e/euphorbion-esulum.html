Euphorbion esulum

Euphorbion esulum, commonly known as the golden spurge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow-orange sap, which is toxic and can cause skin irritation. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and usually have a waxy coating.

The small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in a structure called a cyathium, typical of the genus Euphorbia.
