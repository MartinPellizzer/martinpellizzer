Endiandra muelleri

Endiandra muelleri, commonly known as the Australian ash or red ash, is a member of the family Dipterocarpaceae.

It is a large evergreen tree that can grow up to 40 meters tall, characterized by its dark, reddish-brown bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by woody, three-lobed fruits. It is native to northern Australia and is often found in lowland rainforests and coastal areas.

The tree is valued for its durable timber and plays an important role in the ecological systems of its native habitat.
