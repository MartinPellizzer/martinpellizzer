Eucyperus gracilis

Eucyperus gracilis, commonly known as slender sedge, is a flowering plant species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, grasses, and other wetland plants. The plant typically grows in moist or aquatic environments, such as marshes and wetlands. Its slender, grass-like leaves are arranged in a triangular cross-section, a characteristic feature of many sedges.

Eucyperus gracilis produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered along the stem.
