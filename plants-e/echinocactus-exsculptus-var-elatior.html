Echinocactus exsculptus var. elatior

Echinocactus exsculptus var. elatior, commonly known as the Mexican giant barrel cactus, is a striking succulent native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, spiny, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 10 feet in height. The plant features a thick, fleshy stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, emerging from the areoles along the stem.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and resilience, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping and cactus collections.
