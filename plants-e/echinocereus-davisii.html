Echinocereus davisii

Echinocereus davisii, commonly known as the Davis hedgehog cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has spiny areoles and produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Echinocereus davisii is well adapted to arid environments and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
