Erythrina leptopoda

Erythrina leptopoda, commonly known as the narrow-leaved coral tree, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers, which are clustered in dense inflorescences and attract various pollinators. The plant has compound leaves with slender, elongated leaflets, and it produces flat, woody pods that contain several seeds. Erythrina leptopoda is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its growth habit includes both deciduous and evergreen forms depending on the climate.
