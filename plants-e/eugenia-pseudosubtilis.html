Eugenia pseudosubtilis

Eugenia pseudosubtilis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic rainforests of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible red berries that are rich in antioxidants and often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
