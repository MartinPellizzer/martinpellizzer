Elatine chilensis

Elatine chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean waterwort, is a small aquatic plant native to South America.

It typically grows in freshwater environments such as ponds, streams, and wetlands. The plant has slender, branching stems that float on the water surface, with tiny, scale-like leaves. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or white in color.

Elatine chilensis is often used in ornamental water gardens due to its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in still water.
