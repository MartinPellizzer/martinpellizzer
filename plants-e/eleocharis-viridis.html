Eleocharis viridis

Eleocharis viridis, commonly known as green spikegrass, is a species of sedge native to wetland environments in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, spike-like clusters of greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture, and it produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Eleocharis viridis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
