Erodium laciniatum f. glandulosopilosum

Erodium laciniatum f. glandulosopilosum, commonly known as the glandular-pilose erodium, is a variant of the common erodium.

It is a low-growing annual herb native to southern Africa, characterized by its finely divided, lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its stems are often covered with glandular hairs, giving it a distinctive texture.

This subspecies is adapted to arid and rocky environments, making it a resilient plant in challenging conditions.
