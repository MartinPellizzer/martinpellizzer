Euphorbia isatis

Euphorbia isatis, commonly known as Japanese euphorbia, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and palmate leaves that are often deeply lobed. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers clustered in a cup-like structure called a cyathium. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and hardy nature.

Euphorbia isatis is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
