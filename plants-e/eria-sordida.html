Eria sordida

Eria sordida, commonly known as the dirty orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and wetlands. Its flowers are usually pale green or brown, which helps it blend into its surroundings.

Eria sordida is known for its low maintenance and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
