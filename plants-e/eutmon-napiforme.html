Eutmon napiforme

Eutmon napiforme, commonly known as the bulbous morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, swollen, bulb-like root system that stores nutrients and water. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Eutmon napiforme is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
