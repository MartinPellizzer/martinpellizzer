Ericoides paniculatum

Ericoides paniculatum, commonly known as the panicled ericoid, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, leathery leaves that are typically green or reddish in color. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale pink flowers during the summer months, which are arranged in panicles. Ericoides paniculatum thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in sandy or rocky coastal habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize coastal dunes.
