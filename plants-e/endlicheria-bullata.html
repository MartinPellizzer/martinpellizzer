Endlicheria bullata

Endlicheria bullata, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its pinnate leaves and distinctive yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The tree is valued for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its fragrant flowers.
