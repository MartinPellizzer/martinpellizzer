Eupatorium sphenopodum

Eupatorium sphenopodum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved boneset, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant features tall, slender stems with opposite, narrow, and lance-shaped leaves. Its small, white to pale blue flowers appear in clusters during the summer months.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
