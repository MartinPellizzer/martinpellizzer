Erica margaritacea

Erica margaritacea, commonly known as the silver leaf heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in spring. Its leaves are leathery and covered in a fine, silvery-white waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

Erica margaritacea thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy environments.
