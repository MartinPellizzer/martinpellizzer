Eremopogon foveolatus

Eremopogon foveolatus, commonly known as the funnel-shaped sac fungus, is a species of sac fungus in the family Sphaeriaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, cup-shaped or funnel-like fruiting body that often has a central depression. The fungus typically grows on decaying wood, particularly in moist, shaded environments. Its spores are released from a sac-like structure called an ascoma, which is embedded in the surface of the fruiting body.

Eremopogon foveolatus is often found in temperate regions and is of interest to mycologists due to its unique morphology and ecological role.
