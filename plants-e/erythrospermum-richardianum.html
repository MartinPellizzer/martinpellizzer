Erythrospermum richardianum

Erythrospermum richardianum, commonly known as the red-flowered hibiscus, is a species native to Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically red or pink, with five petals and a prominent yellow stamen. It blooms throughout the warmer months, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects.

The plant is well adapted to coastal and sandy soils, making it a hardy and ornamental choice for gardens.
