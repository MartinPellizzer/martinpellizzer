Epidendrum angustilobopaniculatum

Epidendrum angustilobopaniculatum, commonly known as the narrow-lipped panicle orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched inflorescence that bears numerous small, fragrant flowers. The plant has strap-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. It thrives in humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining potting media.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental value make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
