Epipactis pontica

Epipactis pontica, commonly known as the Pontic helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and produces striking, showy flowers with a greenish-yellow lip. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

Epipactis pontica is known for its complex floral structure and is often found in association with mycorrhizal fungi.
