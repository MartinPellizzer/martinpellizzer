Echinopsis strigosa

Echinopsis strigosa, commonly known as the "Crested Cactus," is a species of cactus native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem covered in white, woolly spines that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

Echinopsis strigosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in harsh conditions.
