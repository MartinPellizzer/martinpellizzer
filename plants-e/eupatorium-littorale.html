Eupatorium littorale

Eupatorium littorale, commonly known as sea eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Americas.

It thrives in saline and sandy environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh coastal conditions. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale blue flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

Eupatorium littorale plays an important role in stabilizing dunes and supporting coastal biodiversity.
