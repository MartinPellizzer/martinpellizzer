Erica empetroides

Erica empetroides, commonly known as the dwarf heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the western Cape region of South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features small, needle-like leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white pubescence, giving it a silvery appearance. Its flowers are usually pink or white, appearing in clusters during the spring and early summer.

Erica empetroides thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is well adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native habitat.
