Elaeagnus umbellata var. obtusifolia

Elaeagnus umbellata var. obtusifolia, commonly known as silverthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-white bark and small, fragrant yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, edible berries that are yellow or orange in color. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

This variety is also valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various climates.
