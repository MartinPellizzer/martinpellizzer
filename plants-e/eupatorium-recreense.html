Eupatorium recreense

Eupatorium recreense, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, lavender-blue flower clusters that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic when crushed. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Eupatorium recreense is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
