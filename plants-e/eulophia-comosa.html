Eulophia comosa

Eulophia comosa, commonly known as the hairy eulophia, is a herbaceous orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and swamps. The plant features a rhizomatous growth habit with erect, often hairy stems. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a distinctive three-lobed lip.

Eulophia comosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
