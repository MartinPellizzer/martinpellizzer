Erodium chium subsp. cuneatum

Erodium chium subsp.

cuneatum, commonly known as narrowleaf bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a low-growing annual herb with slender, branched stems and small, opposite leaves that are usually lance-shaped.

The flowers are pink to purple in color, arranged in clusters, and bloom in early spring. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

This subspecies is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
