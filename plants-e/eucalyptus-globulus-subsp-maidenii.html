Eucalyptus globulus subsp. maidenii

Eucalyptus globulus subsp.

maidenii, commonly known as the Tasmanian eucalyptus, is a sub-species native to Tasmania. It is characterized by its smooth, grey-green bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic.

The plant produces small, globular clusters of white flowers, which are followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is adapted to moist, well-drained soils.

This sub-species is valued for its ecological role and is often used in horticulture and forestry.
