Embothrium truncatum

Embothrium truncatum, commonly known as the Chilean firetree, is a shrub native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its showy, red-orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces small, berry-like fruits. It thrives in coastal and mountainous regions, often growing in well-drained soils.

Embothrium truncatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
