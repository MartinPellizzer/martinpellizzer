Erica viridimontana subsp. nivicola

Erica viridimontana subsp.

nivicola, commonly known as the alpine heath, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink.

This subspecies is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. Its growth habit makes it an important component of alpine ecosystems, providing habitat and food for local wildlife.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in rock gardens.
