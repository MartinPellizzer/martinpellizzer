Eryngium argyreum

Eryngium argyreum, commonly known as the blue thistle, is a striking perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has silvery-gray foliage with a rough texture, and its stems are often covered in fine, bristly hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and wildflower landscapes.

Eryngium argyreum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to support local biodiversity.
