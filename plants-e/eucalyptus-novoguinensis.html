Eucalyptus novoguinensis

Eucalyptus novoguinensis, commonly known as the silver dollar eucalyptus, is a species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its smooth, silver-gray bark that peels in thin sheets, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a bluish-green hue. This tree produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically white or pale yellow.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a range of soil types and climatic conditions.
