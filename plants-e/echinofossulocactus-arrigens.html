Echinofossulocactus arrigens

Echinofossulocactus arrigens, commonly known as the Mexican fire cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is a small, spiny cactus with a short, cylindrical stem that often branches at the base. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green, ribbed structure. Its thick, fleshy stems are adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.
