Euclea lanceolata

Euclea lanceolata, commonly known as the yellow kalahari bean, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by bean-like fruits that contain several seeds. Its leaves are lance-shaped, giving it its scientific name, and it has a woody stem with a rough texture.

Euclea lanceolata is adapted to survive in dry conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
