Evolvulus ovatus

Evolvulus ovatus, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant produces small, round, green fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it both a useful ornamental plant and a potential invasive species in some regions.
