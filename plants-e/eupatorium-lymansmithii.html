Eupatorium lymansmithii

Eupatorium lymansmithii, commonly known as Lymansmith's boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist woodlands and along stream banks, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white to pale purple. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, attracting various pollinators.

Eupatorium lymansmithii is valued for its ecological role and is often found in native plant gardens.
