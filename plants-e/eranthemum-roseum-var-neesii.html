Eranthemum roseum var. neesii

Eranthemum roseum var. neesii, commonly known as the Nees's rose eranthemum, is a perennial shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of showy, pink to red flowers that bloom throughout the warm seasons. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance, it is widely cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
