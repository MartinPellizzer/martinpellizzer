Eriochloa australiensis

Eriochloa australiensis, commonly known as the Australian needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of compact, spike-like clusters of small, pale green to brownish flowers.

Eriochloa australiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
