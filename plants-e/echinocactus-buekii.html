Echinocactus buekii

Echinocactus buekii, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a slow-growing cactus characterized by its spherical shape and a dense covering of spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne at the top of the plant. Its thick, water-storing stem helps it survive in arid environments.

Echinocactus buekii is well-suited for cultivation in dry climates and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
