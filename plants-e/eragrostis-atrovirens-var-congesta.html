Eragrostis atrovirens var. congesta

Eragrostis atrovirens var. congesta, commonly known as blackgrass or purple false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. The plant features narrow, dark green leaves with a distinctive purplish hue along the midribs. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, two-lobed florets.

This variety is often found in disturbed areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
