Eupatorium monticola

Eupatorium monticola, commonly known as mountain eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its showy white to pale purple flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Eupatorium monticola is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
