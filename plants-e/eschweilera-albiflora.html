Eschweilera albiflora

Eschweilera albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflower spondias, is a tropical tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, palmate leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the rainy season. Its fruits are round, woody drupes that turn yellow or red when mature.

Eschweilera albiflora plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
