Erica sceptriformis

Erica sceptriformis, commonly known as the sword-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Erica sceptriformis thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
