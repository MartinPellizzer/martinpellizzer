Epilobium incanum

Epilobium incanum, commonly known as silverweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green leaves and clusters of small, pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges. It is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

Epilobium incanum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in many temperate regions.
