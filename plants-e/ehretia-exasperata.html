Ehretia exasperata

Ehretia exasperata, commonly known as the roughleaf ehretia, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape and have a rough or hairy texture. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental potential and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
