Erica similis

Erica similis, commonly known as the common heath, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its low-growing, evergreen shrub form with dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. Erica similis typically blooms in spring, producing pink or white flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
