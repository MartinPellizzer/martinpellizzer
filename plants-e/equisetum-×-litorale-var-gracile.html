Equisetum × litorale var. gracile

Equisetum × litorale var. gracile, commonly known as the coastal horsetail, is a hybrid species that exhibits characteristics of both Equisetum litorale and Equisetum × gracile.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous plant that typically thrives in moist, sandy soils along coastal areas. The plant features slender, jointed stems covered in small, scale-like structures, and it produces spore-bearing cones at the tips of its branches. Its leaves are reduced to small, needle-like structures, and it is often found in salt-tolerant environments.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and its unique appearance in maritime landscapes.
