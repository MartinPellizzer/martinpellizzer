Eugenioides oxyphyllum

Eugenioides oxyphyllum, commonly known as the broad-leafed myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy, ovate leaves with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, woody fruits containing aromatic seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and use in traditional medicine.

The leaves and flowers are also used in culinary applications in some regions.
