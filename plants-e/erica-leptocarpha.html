Erica leptocarpha

Erica leptocarpha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in well-drained, acidic conditions. Erica leptocarpha is often found in fynbos vegetation, which is a unique biome in the Western Cape region.

Its flowers are usually pink or white, and it blooms from late summer to early autumn.
