Echinocactus gibbosus var. typica

Echinocactus gibbosus var. typica, commonly known as the Mexican hat cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, cylindrical stem covered with thick, ribbed segments. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its slow growth rate and unique appearance make it a popular choice for succulent gardens and rockeries.
