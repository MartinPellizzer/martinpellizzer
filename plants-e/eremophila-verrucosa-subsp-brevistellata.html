Eremophila verrucosa subsp. brevistellata

Eremophila verrucosa subsp.

brevistellata, commonly known as the narrow-stalked emu bush, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces spikes of pink to red flowers during the spring and summer seasons. It is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.

The subspecies is distinguished by its shorter floral stems compared to other subspecies of Eremophila verrucosa.
