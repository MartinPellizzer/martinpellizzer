Echium vulgare subsp. argentae

Echium vulgare subsp.

argentae, commonly known as silverweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, densely packed hairs that help reduce water loss in arid environments.

The plant produces striking spikes of blue or purple flowers, which bloom in summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant plants.
