Eriocaulon sahyadricum

Eriocaulon sahyadricum, commonly known as the Western Ghats hairgrass, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the humid regions of the Western Ghats in India.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinct hairy texture that gives it its common name.

Eriocaulon sahyadricum plays a role in maintaining soil stability and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
