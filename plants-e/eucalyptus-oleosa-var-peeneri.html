Eucalyptus oleosa var. peeneri

Eucalyptus oleosa var. peeneri, commonly known as the Peener's malleable gum, is a variant of the eucalyptus genus native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, globular clusters of white or pale yellow flowers, which are typically visited by nectar-feeding insects and birds. Its timber is hard and dense, making it valuable for construction and fuel.

This variety is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
