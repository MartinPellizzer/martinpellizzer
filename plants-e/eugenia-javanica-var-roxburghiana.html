Eugenia javanica var. roxburghiana

Eugenia javanica var. roxburghiana, commonly known as the Java apple or Java cherry, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

It thrives in tropical climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
