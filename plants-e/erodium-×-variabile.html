Erodium × variabile

Erodium × variabile, commonly known as the variable bedstraw, is a hybrid plant belonging to the family Geraniaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are typically alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a slightly fuzzy appearance. Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a wide range of morphological variation, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
