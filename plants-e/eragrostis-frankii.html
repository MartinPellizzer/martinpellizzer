Eragrostis frankii

Eragrostis frankii, commonly known as Frank's lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to drought conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes many other grass species.
