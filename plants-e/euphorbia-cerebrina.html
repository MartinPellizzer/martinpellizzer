Euphorbia cerebrina

Euphorbia cerebrina, commonly known as the brain-like euphorbia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, brain-shaped inflorescence, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species adapted to dry conditions.

Euphorbia cerebrina is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and resilience.
