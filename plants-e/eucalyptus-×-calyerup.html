Eucalyptus × calyerup

Eucalyptus × calyerup, commonly known as the Calyerup mallees, is a hybrid eucalyptus species native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its small to medium size, with smooth, often peeling bark that ranges from grey to white. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, typically glossy green, and the flowers are small, white or pink, clustered in umbels. This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and resilience to poor soil conditions.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
