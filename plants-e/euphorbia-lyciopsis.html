Euphorbia lyciopsis

Euphorbia lyciopsis, commonly known as the desert euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a compact, branched stem with thick, fleshy leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are often surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. Euphorbia lyciopsis is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water requirements.

Its structure and growth habits make it a resilient and interesting subject for botanical study.
