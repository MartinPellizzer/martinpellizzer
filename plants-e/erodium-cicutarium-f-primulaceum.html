Erodium cicutarium f. primulaceum

Erodium cicutarium f. primulaceum, commonly known as the common bedstraw, is a flowering plant belonging to the Leguminosae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers. The plant produces small, lens-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its widespread distribution. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in various regions, particularly in temperate climates.
