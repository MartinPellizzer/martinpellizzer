Endopogon viscosus var. humilis

Endopogon viscosus var. humilis, commonly known as the sticky endopogon, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy, often sticky leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet-like structure with small, wind-pollinated flowers.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting local insect and bird populations.
