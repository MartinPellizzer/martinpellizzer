Eucalyptus tortilis

Eucalyptus tortilis, commonly known as the river gum, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted trunk and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils along riverbanks and coastal areas.

Eucalyptus tortilis is well adapted to coastal environments and can tolerate salt spray.
