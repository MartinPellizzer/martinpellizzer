Encelia pilosa

Encelia pilosa, commonly known as the desert sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations such as succulent leaves and deep root systems to conserve water. Its yellow-orange flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant's structure and growth habits make it well-suited for xeriscaping and ecological restoration projects.
