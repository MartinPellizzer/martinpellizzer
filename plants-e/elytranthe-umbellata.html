Elytranthe umbellata

Elytranthe umbellata, commonly known as the Umbellate Sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters, resembling an umbrella. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Elytranthe umbellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
