Euphorbia isaloensis

Euphorbia isaloensis, commonly known as the Isalo Spurge, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish cyathia, which are specialized inflorescences typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to dry conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists alike.
