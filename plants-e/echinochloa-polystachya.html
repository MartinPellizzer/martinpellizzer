Echinochloa polystachya

Echinochloa polystachya, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that often reach heights of up to 1 meter. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks.

Echinochloa polystachya is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
