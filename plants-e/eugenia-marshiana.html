Eugenia marshiana

Eugenia marshiana, commonly known as the marshy eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the wetlands of South America.

It typically grows in swampy or seasonally flooded areas, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are oval-shaped, glossy, and often have a slight pubescence.

The fruit is a small, fleshy berry that ripens to a deep red color, attracting various wildlife species.
