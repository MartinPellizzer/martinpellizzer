Erica floccosa

Erica floccosa, commonly known as the woolly heath, is a species of flowering plant in the heather family, Ericaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe, particularly the Iberian Peninsula. The plant is characterized by its dense, low-growing shrubs with small, leathery leaves and clusters of pale pink or white flowers. Erica floccosa thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive floral display.
