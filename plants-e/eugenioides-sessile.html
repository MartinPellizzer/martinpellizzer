Eugenioides sessile

Eugenioides sessile, commonly known as the sessile eugenioides, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, producing a fragrant aroma.

The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
