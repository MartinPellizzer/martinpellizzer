Erica muirii

Erica muirii, commonly known as the Muir's heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. Erica muirii thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
