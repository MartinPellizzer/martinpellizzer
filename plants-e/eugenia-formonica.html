Eugenia formonica

Eugenia formonica, commonly known as the white myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, berry-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fragrance, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine.
