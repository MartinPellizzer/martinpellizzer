Erythrina amasisa

Erythrina amasisa, commonly known as the flame tree or the coral tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red and orange flowers, which are clustered in dense inflorescences and attract various pollinators. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three leaflets, and its bark is often smooth with a reddish or brownish hue. Erythrina amasisa is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant floral display.

The plant contains alkaloids and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
