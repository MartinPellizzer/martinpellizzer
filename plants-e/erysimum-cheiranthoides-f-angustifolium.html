Erysimum cheiranthoides f. angustifolium

Erysimum cheiranthoides f. angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved wallflower, is a cultivar of the flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, linear leaves that are typically a deep green, contributing to its ornamental appeal. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with four petals, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This cultivar is valued for its compact growth habit and long-lasting floral display.
