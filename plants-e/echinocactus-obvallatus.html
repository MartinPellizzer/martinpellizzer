Echinocactus obvallatus

Echinocactus obvallatus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, spiny stem that becomes rounded with age. The plant features a dense covering of white, bristle-like spines and produces yellow flowers during the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and indoor cultivation.
