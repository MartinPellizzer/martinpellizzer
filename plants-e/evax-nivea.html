Evax nivea

Evax nivea, commonly known as the white sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, textured appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for arid environments.

Evax nivea is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
