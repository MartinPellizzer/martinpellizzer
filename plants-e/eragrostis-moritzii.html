Eragrostis moritzii

Eragrostis moritzii, commonly known as Moritz's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Eragrostis moritzii is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
