Eupatorium carolinianum

Eupatorium carolinianum, commonly known as white boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist habitats such as wet meadows and swampy areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its medicinal properties historically used by indigenous peoples.
