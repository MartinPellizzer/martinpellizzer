Eucalyptus leucophylla

Eucalyptus leucophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine white wax. The plant produces small, globular flower buds that are typically pale green or yellowish in color. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is commonly found in heathlands and woodland habitats.

Eucalyptus leucophylla plays an important role in local ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
