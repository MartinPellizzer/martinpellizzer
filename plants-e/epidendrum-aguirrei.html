Epidendrum aguirrei

Epidendrum aguirrei, commonly known as the Aguirre's orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences with showy, fragrant flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrates.

Epidendrum aguirrei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
