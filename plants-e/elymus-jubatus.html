Elymus jubatus

Elymus jubatus, commonly known as the tufted wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, silver-green foliage. The plant produces dense, cylindrical inflorescences that are often adorned with silky, hairy bracts. Elymus jubatus thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing rocky and disturbed soils.
