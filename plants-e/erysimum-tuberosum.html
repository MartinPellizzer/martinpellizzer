Erysimum tuberosum

Erysimum tuberosum, commonly known as the rootstock mustard, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branched stems covered in finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, which are arranged in racemes. Its root system is notable for its tuberous roots, which are used in traditional medicine.

Erysimum tuberosum thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
