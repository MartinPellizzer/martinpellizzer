Epimedium chrysanthum

Epimedium chrysanthum, commonly known as the golden monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Asia.

It features distinctive, shield-shaped leaves that are typically green with a yellow or white margin. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and white, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and early blooming habit.
