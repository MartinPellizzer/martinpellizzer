Eragrostis chapelieri

Eragrostis chapelieri, commonly known as the African lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open grasslands and along riverbanks.

Eragrostis chapelieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and erosion control.
