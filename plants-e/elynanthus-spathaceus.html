Elynanthus spathaceus

Elynanthus spathaceus, commonly known as the white sweet clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is native to Europe and has been introduced to various parts of the world. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields and along roadsides.

Elynanthus spathaceus is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it valuable in soil enrichment.
