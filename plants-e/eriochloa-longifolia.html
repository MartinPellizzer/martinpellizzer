Eriochloa longifolia

Eriochloa longifolia, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale green to yellowish flowers in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and open woodlands.

Eriochloa longifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
