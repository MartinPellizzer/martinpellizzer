Ehretia crebrifolia

Ehretia crebrifolia, commonly known as the dense-leaved ehretia, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite, elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in secondary forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some horticultural settings for its attractive foliage and unique fruit.
