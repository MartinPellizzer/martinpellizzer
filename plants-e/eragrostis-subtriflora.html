Eragrostis subtriflora

Eragrostis subtriflora, commonly known as three-flowered lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear three to five spikelets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green coloration.

Eragrostis subtriflora is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its delicate appearance and drought tolerance.
