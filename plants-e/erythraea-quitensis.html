Erythraea quitensis

Erythraea quitensis, commonly known as the Peruvian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, vibrant flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces edible seeds that are traditionally used in local cuisines. Its rapid growth and climbing habit make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
