Evolvulus peruvianus

Evolvulus peruvianus, commonly known as the Peruvian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, bright blue or purple flowers that bloom in the early morning, hence its common name. Its leaves are heart-shaped and hairy, while its stems are slender and often twining.

Evolvulus peruvianus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
