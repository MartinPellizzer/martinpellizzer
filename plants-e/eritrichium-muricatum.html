Eritrichium muricatum

Eritrichium muricatum, commonly known as the rough mouse-ear cress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with hairy stems and small, rounded leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Eritrichium muricatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems.
