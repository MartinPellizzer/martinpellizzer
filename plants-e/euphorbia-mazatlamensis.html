Euphorbia mazatlamensis

Euphorbia mazatlamensis, commonly known as the Mazatlan euphorbia, is a succulent shrub native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and spiny leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water requirements.

Due to its unique appearance and resilience, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
