Eupatorium squarrosoramosum var. angustifolium

Eupatorium squarrosoramosum var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved eupatorium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small, clustered in umbels, and range in color from white to pale purple, attracting various pollinators.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
