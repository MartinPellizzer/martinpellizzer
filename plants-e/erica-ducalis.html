Erica ducalis

Erica ducalis, commonly known as the dwarf heath, is a species of evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related plants. The plant is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, leathery leaves. Erica ducalis produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in spring.

It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
