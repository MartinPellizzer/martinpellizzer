Euphorbia indurescens

Euphorbia indurescens, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a distinctive, branched stem covered in a thick, waxy cuticle that helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in a structure called a cyathium, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are usually reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it has a milky sap that can be toxic.

Euphorbia indurescens is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
