Euaraliopsis polyacantha

Euaraliopsis polyacantha, commonly known as the clustered arrowhead, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments in the southeastern United States.

It features a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread vegetatively and form dense clusters. The plant has heart-shaped, arrowhead-like leaves that are typically bright green and slightly hairy. Its small, white flowers appear in late spring to early summer, and it produces berries that are dispersed by water.

Euaraliopsis polyacantha plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic and semi-aquatic organisms.
