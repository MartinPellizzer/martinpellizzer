Eremophila falcata

Eremophila falcata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved emu bush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. Its flowers, which range in color from pink to red, attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.

Eremophila falcata is often used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
