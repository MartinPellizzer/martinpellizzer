Encyclia wendlandiana

Encyclia wendlandiana, commonly known as the Wendland's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central America.

It belongs to the Epidendroideae subfamily and is characterized by its compact, rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are strap-shaped and arranged in a rosette, and it prefers well-drained, humus-rich soil.

Encyclia wendlandiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a warm, humid environment.
