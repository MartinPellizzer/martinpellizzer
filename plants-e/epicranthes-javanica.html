Epicranthes javanica

Epicranthes javanica, commonly known as the Java cranesbill, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its small, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
