Eriotheca candolleana var. longipes

Eriotheca candolleana var. longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed eriotheca, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody capsules containing its seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
