Echinocactus spina-christi

Echinocactus spina-christi, commonly known as the Christ's thorn cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem covered in thick, ribbed segments and prominent spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its spiky structure. It is well adapted to drought conditions, storing water in its fleshy stems.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
