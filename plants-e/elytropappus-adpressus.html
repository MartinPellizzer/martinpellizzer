Elytropappus adpressus

Elytropappus adpressus, commonly known as the flatstem goldenweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, while its small yellow flowers appear in late summer.

Elytropappus adpressus is known for its ecological importance as a food source for various insects and its role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
