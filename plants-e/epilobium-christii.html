Epilobium christii

Epilobium christii, commonly known as Christ's willow or the silver willow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments. The plant features slender, willow-like stems covered in silvery hairs, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small, pink to reddish in color, and arranged in dense spikes.

Epilobium christii is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
