Eriocaulon caespitosum

Eriocaulon caespitosum, commonly known as the tufted spike-rush, is a small herbaceous plant belonging to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and compact growth habit, often forming tufts or clumps. The plant typically produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white or pale green flowers. It thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
