Eupatorium fistulosum f. albidum

Eupatorium fistulosum f. albidum, commonly known as white boneset, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its antipyretic and diaphoretic effects.
