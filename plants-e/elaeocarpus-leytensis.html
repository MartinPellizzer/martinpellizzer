Elaeocarpus leytensis

Elaeocarpus leytensis, commonly known as the Leyte elaeocarpus, is a tropical tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically covered in a fleshy, yellowish aril. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland to montane forests.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
