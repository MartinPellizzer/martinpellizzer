Eremocaulon asymmetricum

Eremocaulon asymmetricum, commonly known as the asymmetric mountain grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, asymmetrical inflorescence. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are often pale blue or white. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to rocky or alpine substrates.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
