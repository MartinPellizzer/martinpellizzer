Eryngium glomeratum subsp. elongatum

Eryngium glomeratum subsp.

elongatum, commonly known as the tall thistle or narrow-leaved eryngium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescence composed of blue to purple flower heads surrounded by bracts.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually arranged in a rosette at the base. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry grasslands and rocky habitats.

Its distinctive appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
