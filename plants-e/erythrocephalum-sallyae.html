Erythrocephalum sallyae

Erythrocephalum sallyae, commonly known as Sally's everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, showy pink to red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Sally's everlasting is well adapted to arid conditions and is often found in sandy or gravelly soils.
