Erysimum bocconei var. nemorale

Erysimum bocconei var. nemorale, commonly known as the Sierra Madre wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant yellow to orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or open woodland habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy as well.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bright, early-season blooms.
