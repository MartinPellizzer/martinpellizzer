Eryngium palmito

Eryngium palmito, commonly known as the palmate thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features a basal rosette of spiny, palmately lobed leaves, which give it its common name. Its flowers are blue to purple and are arranged in a dense, spiky inflorescence, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Eryngium palmito is known for its hardy nature and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
