Eugenia costata

Eugenia costata, commonly known as the rough-leaved guava, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, serrated leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe, with a sweet and tangy flavor. Eugenia costata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits, thriving in warm, humid climates.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
