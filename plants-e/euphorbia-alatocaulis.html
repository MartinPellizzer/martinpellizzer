Euphorbia alatocaulis

Euphorbia alatocaulis, commonly known as the winged columnar euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, cylindrical stem that is ribbed and often covered with short, wing-like structures. The plant produces small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are the modified flower clusters typical of the Euphorbia genus. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and ability to store water in its stem make it an interesting addition to xeriscaping and succulent gardens.
