Eriospermum triphyllum

Eriospermum triphyllum, commonly known as the three-leaved eriosperm, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its three leaflets, which are typically oval and hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by distinctive, three-lobed seed pods. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in desert or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
