Evansia confusa

Evansia confusa, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a striking desert plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in the early morning and close by midday, a trait known as nyctinasty. The plant has a fibrous root system that allows it to thrive in arid environments with minimal water. Its leaves are typically small and covered in fine hairs to reduce water loss.

Evansia confusa plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for certain desert animals.
