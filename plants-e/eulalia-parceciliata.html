Eulalia parceciliata

Eulalia parceciliata, commonly known as the yellow pampas grass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It features tall, slender stalks that can grow up to 2 meters in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces fluffy, golden seed heads that are often used in floral arrangements. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its botanical characteristics include a robust root system and a clump-forming growth habit.
