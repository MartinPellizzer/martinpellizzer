Echinocereus engelmannii var. chrysocentrus

Echinocereus engelmannii var. chrysocentrus, commonly known as the golden hedgehog cactus, is a spineless cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a cylindrical stem that is typically green to reddish in color, often with a bluish or purplish tint. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. It has a shallow root system and is well adapted to arid environments.

Its succulent stems store water, allowing it to thrive in dry conditions with minimal rainfall.
