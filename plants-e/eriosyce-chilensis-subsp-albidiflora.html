Eriosyce chilensis subsp. albidiflora

Eriosyce chilensis subsp.

albidiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered Chilean eriosyce, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of central Chile. It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair.

The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom during the cooler months, typically in spring. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal rainfall.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
