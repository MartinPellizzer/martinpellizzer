Epidendrum comayaguense

Epidendrum comayaguense, commonly known as the Comayagua orchid, is a species native to Central America, particularly Honduras and Nicaragua.

It is an epiphytic orchid that typically grows on tree trunks or branches in humid, tropical environments. The plant features long, arching stems with numerous lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. Its flowers are clustered in racemes and are characterized by their vibrant colors, typically ranging from yellow to orange.

Epidendrum comayaguense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
