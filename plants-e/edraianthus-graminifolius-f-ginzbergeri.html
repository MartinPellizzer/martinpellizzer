Edraianthus graminifolius f. ginzbergeri

Edraianthus graminifolius f. ginzbergeri, commonly known as the Ginzberger's grass-leaved edraianthus, is a rare and striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. Native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe, this plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and full sun exposure. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturalists.
