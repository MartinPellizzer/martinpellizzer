Eriogonum staticifolium

Eriogonum staticifolium, commonly known as the stickyleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, glandular hairs that give its leaves a sticky texture. The plant produces showy clusters of white or pinkish flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
