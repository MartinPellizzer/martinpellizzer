Eupatorium polycephalum

Eupatorium polycephalum, commonly known as the clustered boneset, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branching stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Eupatorium polycephalum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
