Elattostachys apetala

Elattostachys apetala, commonly known as the naked-stemmed grass.

It is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Asia, particularly found in countries like India and Sri Lanka. The plant has slender, hairy stems that often appear naked due to the absence of leaves along their length. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically clustered in spike-like inflorescences.

Elattostachys apetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
