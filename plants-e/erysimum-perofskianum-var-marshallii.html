Erysimum perofskianum var. marshallii

Erysimum perofskianum var. marshallii, commonly known as the Marshall's wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and profuse display of bright yellow flowers. The plant features pinnately divided, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from spring to early summer.
