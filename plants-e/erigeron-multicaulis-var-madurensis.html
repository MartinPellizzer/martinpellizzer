Erigeron multicaulis var. madurensis

Erigeron multicaulis var. madurensis, commonly known as the Madurese blueweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are blue to purple in color.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
