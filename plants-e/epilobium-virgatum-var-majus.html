Epilobium virgatum var. majus

Epilobium virgatum var. majus, commonly known as the showy willow herb, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other wet habitats.

Epilobium virgatum var. majus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
