Eriogonum umbellatum var. majus

Eriogonum umbellatum var. majus, commonly known as the showy buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its clustered, showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and disturbed soils. It has slender, branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and role in supporting local pollinators.
