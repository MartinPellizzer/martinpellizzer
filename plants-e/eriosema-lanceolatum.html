Eriosema lanceolatum

Eriosema lanceolatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf eriosema, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
