Echium flavum subsp. flavum

Echium flavum subsp.

flavum, commonly known as the yellow fleabane, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters.

The plant is an annual or biennial herb with hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies, making it an important plant in ecological contexts.
