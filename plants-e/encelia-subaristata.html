Encelia subaristata

Encelia subaristata, commonly known as the desert sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations such as hairy leaves to reduce water loss. Its yellow to orange flower heads attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological balance.

The plant's resilience to drought and heat makes it a notable species in xeriscaping and desert ecosystems.
