Echinocactus geissei var. albicans

Echinocactus geissei var. albicans, commonly known as the White-Flowered Hedgehog Cactus, is a succulent native to the Mexican state of Guanajuato.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in spines and its showy, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth. It is drought-tolerant and prefers warm, dry climates, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a desirable addition to arid landscapes.
