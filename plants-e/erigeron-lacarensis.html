Erigeron lacarensis

Erigeron lacarensis, commonly known as the Mexican fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in the summer months.
