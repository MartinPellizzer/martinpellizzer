Egletes viscosa f. bipinnatifida

Egletes viscosa f. bipinnatifida, commonly known as the silky sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its succulent nature allows it to store water, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

The species is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing sandy landscapes.
