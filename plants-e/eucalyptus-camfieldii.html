Eucalyptus camfieldii

Eucalyptus camfieldii, commonly known as the Camfield mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Western Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in dry, sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often grayish-green in color. It produces small, globular flower spikes that are typically cream to pale yellow in hue.

The plant is well adapted to arid conditions and plays an important role in the local ecosystem.
