Eschscholzia columbiana

Eschscholzia columbiana, commonly known as the California poppy, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the western United States.

It features striking orange to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and a slender stem that grows up to 3 feet tall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.

The California poppy is also the state flower of California and is known for its ability to reseed itself readily.
