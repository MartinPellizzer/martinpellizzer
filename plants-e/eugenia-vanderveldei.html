Eugenia vanderveldei

Eugenia vanderveldei, commonly known as the Cape cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils.

Eugenia vanderveldei is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract birds and insects.
