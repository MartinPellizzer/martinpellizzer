Epidendrum miradoranum

Epidendrum miradoranum, commonly known as the Mirador orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a sympodial orchid with a short, thick rhizome and produces numerous pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its inflorescence is a raceme of fragrant, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow.

Epidendrum miradoranum is well-suited for cultivation in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.
