Eria calamifolia

Eria calamifolia, commonly known as the orchid with feather-like leaves, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that come in various colors such as yellow, orange, and red. The plant has long, narrow leaves that resemble feathers, giving it its common name. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Eria calamifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
